\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou're expected to know how logic gates work and how to do boolean algebra, which is fine. It's cool how you get to build a Turing complete computer. But you lost me at programming.

You're supposed to write in assembly, and not just that, you're supposed to make your own assembly from binary values. At this point, even if you've been able to breeze though the rest, it becomes stupidly difficult, to the point that there's no challenge, and it's just pure frustration. The game teaches you a bit about logic, but it throws you into assembly with minimum to no explanation.

Learning how to use the robot was really confusing. How was I supposed to know I had to just type out the number in the editor and send it to the output to make the robot move? I never figured out how to make labels work. Sometimes loops work, most of the times they don't.

Some hints assume you're stupid, telling you some basic math concept, and then they expect you to make a robot solve a maze. I loved all the previous levels, but I really think the programming part has to be taken more slowly and with more explanation and examples.

I don't recommend it unless you know about digital logic AND assembly.\t\t\t\t\t
