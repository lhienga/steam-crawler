\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you have even a passing interest in electronics or computing, you might like this; it's hands-down one of the most engaging experiences I've had in years — I count about 4-5 nights where it was suddenly 3am and I was still poking away at the CPU that I'd built in-game.

... Did I mention that you start off with gate puzzles, but end up building multiple CPUs in the game?

The UI can take a little bit to get used to, and moving stuff and running wires isn't a perfectly intuitive process, but a few hours in, you'll have gotten used to it. Also, eventually you will swear at the limited assembler (cannot generate multiple bytes from a single op). Generally, though, the level of abstraction and tools provided are "just enough".

This game has monopolised my time for the past few weeks, every moment I'm not playing the game (and not at work), I'm thinking about how I could refactor my CPU architecture or my assembly solution to shave off the last four bytes needed for the achievement.

Buy it. If you're not convinced, buy it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
