\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have completed this and gotten all the achievements, so I can safely say I have enough experience with the game to rate it.

It's pretty good in the 'computer programming game' genre, and will take you from basic logic to a functional computer. The presentation is nice, the ingame story is small but fun, and the build up is good.

Here are the cavets:
- It really could do with a few quality of life improvements, such as being able to copy/paste multiple basic logic gates rather than having to copy them one by one.
- Some of the features aren't explained/tutorialed well - I didn't know how to get to the component factory for awhile after I'd made my computer, for example. Also, I didn't know the components could be rotated with the space bar.
- The tests for some of the more advanced levels aren't very through and can be cheesed by accident; in this genre, that can cheapen the experience.
- With components that contain RAM (such as the stack), the contents of the RAM can't be examined for debugging.

So, overall a recommendation, with cavets. There are better games in the programming/computer genre, but this is a decent entry.\t\t\t\t\t
