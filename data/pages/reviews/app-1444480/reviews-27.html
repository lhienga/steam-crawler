\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis Game is the most interesting game i have ever played. I am a big fan of Factorio, Satisfactory and Dyson Sphere Program, which are all games that focus on factory building. Now these games all have one thing in common, problem solving, which can be incredibly fun and incredibly frustrating. Turing complete is an amazing example of a problem solving game in my eyes, however, there is one thing i must say. It is far from perfect: the controls are quite buggy and some levels are unclear on what you must do. This and the fact that the game has not been updated in quite a while are my only problems with it. This being said, the game is still in early access so these things may be fixed or changed or the game may never get updated again, i truly do not know. This is why i will update my review if the game goes out of early access or gets updated with changes to my problems.

At the time of this review, i have 187 hours which i think is quite a lot so that shows how much i enjoy this game and how much i think it is a great game. I would recommend this game to anyone who is studying computer science because i found it really interesting and it gave me a lot of knowledge on how a computer works. It is worth Â£15 i believe.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tgreat game but you need more than a bit of prior experience with digital circuits and instruction sets. other than that this is simple and enjoyable. all your imaginary fantasy machines and projects postponed because your reluctance on buying new pieces of hardware can now be brought into reality: just build your own thing and run it here. Too bad semi-conductor manufacturing and design is still proprietary most of the time, otherwise there should be a button to print your circuit on a chip.
Stuff you should know buying this game:
1. basic logic gates. how to get one logic gate from another. what are the universal gates?
2. basic programming. human resource machine and 7 billion humans are great introductions to imperative programming with instructions.
3. basic math skills. mainly binary calculations, having an intuitive grasp on binary numbers is very helpful.

Designing a game based on digital circuit is not a new idea, however Turing Complete might be the most straightforward and accessible implementation I have seen. Well done.\t\t\t\t\t
