\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Video games are a complete waste of time and teach nothing of value *grumble grumble*" --Your Dad

I've never really bothered to learn much about logic gates and how to apply them. To me, it's a concept that is best learned through practice. Unless you have a pile of logic gates, decoders/encoders, shift registers, mux'es, 8-bit memory, etc laying around (or if you're just too lazy to breadboard everything like I am), or if your approach to learning is with brute force trial and error -- this game is amazing. Heck, even if you never even heard of digital logic gates, but you like computers, learning, and developing your critical thinking skills -- this is worth trying out. However, if you have zero background or education in computer science, electronics, or engineering, you'll likely have to do some research on some concepts or even (*gasp*) critically think!

PROS:
- It teaches the very foundation of digital logic, architecture, digital circuits, etc. The stuff lower-level than your object-oriented programming.
- It'll also get you 'critically thinking', which is the most useful skill of all. Once you get the gyst of things, it becomes very addicting.
- EXTREMELY rewarding!

POTENTIAL CONS:
- This game doesn't have a lot of hand-holding, and at times can be unclear in it's directions.

SOME MAY NOT LIKE:
- Each level will likely take some research, thinking, and experimenting.
- Not a game you can 'turn your brain off' to

If you are new to digital logic/circuits -- the good news is, the internet has all the information you need to figure out the needed concepts to solve these, it just takes some time and effort. There's also some guides and walkthroughs, but I really recommend trying to crack these yourself and build up those problem solving skills.

That being said, not everyone is into using their brain, especially when playing video games. Some just like to play Call of Battlefield 13, run around on a map, aim a cross-hair at enemies and brainlessly shoot -- while being endlessly insulted by prepubescent kids telling you to 'git gud f!@#$%' . And that's totally okay! Just know this game may not be for you.

I hope the developer makes more games like this! I would love a PCB design/puzzle game, where it teaches you more about electricity with building common circuits like rectifiers, voltage dividers, ADC voltage ladders, filters, etc. And also build with common ICs like 555 timers, microcontrollers, op-amps. power supplies, 74.. logic chips, ADCs DACs, use digital/analog signals, and buses like i2c, spi, 1-wire, serial. Then again, that's a big ask, since you would essentially be making a full blown circuit designer like eagle mixed with a full blown simulator like spice. A man can dream though.\t\t\t\t\t
