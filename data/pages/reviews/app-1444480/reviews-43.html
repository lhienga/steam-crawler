\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has taken courses on computer architecture and system design, I have to say I'm unimpressed. I've reached the point where I'm a few challenges shy of a full 8-bit computer and I definitely wouldn't have reached this point without my university knowledge of circuitry, so this is not a good teaching tool.

If you want to learn to make your own 8-bit computer, I recommend looking up some guides online, as there are tons of interactive tools and tutorials which will teach you the basics far better than this "game," and they won't cost you 20$. The key phrases to look for are things like "8-bit computer", "DIY", and "online tool."

If you just want a fun puzzle game with the slim veneer of a story, go pick up literally any Zachtronics game. They're far better at teaching, better at building on previous knowledge, and have significantly better stories.

Finally, if you're interested in learning assembly code... then just learn assembly code! My favorite way to learn it is to just boot up Cheat Engine and try to build scripts for various (single-player!) games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game. I heard it taught you computer basics while you play. But it doesn't actually teach anything. As a novice with some basic understanding of OR, AND, SR Latch, etc, but knowing nothing of how those work inside a bigger system I was excited and ready to go.

The early levels are a bit of trial and error which is fine. But once it gets into more complex systems like registers you can't just trial and error it anymore, you need to understand what you're doing. It doesn't explain what ticks are or how a clock works in the early levels. When testing a component it doesn't let you keep testing if it hits a wrong result. So if tick 1/128 is wrong I can't see if ticks 2-128 are right. They might give me a clue why the incorrect ticks are incorrect.

Also the instructions for some levels are vague and the levels often skip in terms of logical progression. It introduces negative numbers by having you take a quiz on which bits add up to the desired number. Then the next level goes straight to turn all values negative. It never explained fully how negative numbers work.


Edit: While I still stand by everything I have stated above. I have come to find that it is still a very good computer simulation program. Once I looked into information outside the game I was able to solve levels and utilize the sandbox feature. If they could fix the learning curve issue then this would be an excellent game.\t\t\t\t\t
