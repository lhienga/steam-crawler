\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely amazing experience. The music, the steadily increasing complexity, the humor, the technical accuracy, and rock-solid UI make this a must-play game for anyone interested in digital logic and computers.

I especially appreciate the tradeoff of slowly introducing new concepts as puzzles, and then letting you use your previous solutions as new components. The game also avoids tedium where appropriate, while still making you appreciate the principles involved in scaling something up. The puzzle involving four bytes of register memory is a good example of this.

I'll admit that I'm an Electrical Engineer, and already have much of the background in digital electronics, computer architecture, assembly programming, and software abstraction that this game teaches. But I think that just makes me appreciate it all the more!

If you're a fan of Zachtronics games, I think you'll find this is easily as good as the best of those, and maybe even better in many ways.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat i liked:
* Educational, but also fun. Even if you know theory about computers this can show how everything works together or allow you to put the pieces together in a more practical way.
* Good pacing, you don't have to build things twice as the game will give you a finished component after building something once (or build the components later yourself). It's easy to breeze through the levels you already know like bitwise logic without getting bored, but I imagine for somebody who is newer to these topics the game also covers enough basics and hints.
* The game isn't short. Depending on how much you know, you can easily get 20h out of it. Personally I'm on the functions levels after 20h, with some afk time and playing around included, which is the second last level block, after building two basic CPU Architectures. And with some more functions, assembly and sandbox content to come I believe I'm far from done playing the game.
* UI is mostly good, the way bits are displayed and toggle-able by mouse (including the instructions "decoder") make it easy to play around and is intuitive.

What I didn't like:
* I'm not sure if that was my mistake but the game didn't introduce assembly labels before completing the minigame where you need to shoot space rats with a robot, which was annoying.
* Sometimes you need to reopen the level dialoge to get some info, and some abbreviations might be unfamiliar. Tbf this probably also promotes carefully reading the instructions/docs and writing stuff down… which I didn't do… but is somewhat reminiscent of the real thing.

Overall: recommended for everyone interested in computers or/and programming
