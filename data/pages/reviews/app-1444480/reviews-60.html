\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving completed a "Turing complete" computer of my own, I have proved myself to be just as intelligent and accomplished as Alan Turing himself. And I'm only halfway through the game!

Seriously, though: this game is amazing. There really is nothing quite like making your own computer completely out of components you designed yourself.

The difficulty curve can be a little rough sometimes, as you are building a real virtual computer from logic gates up. Some levels are a lot more time consuming than others, just because some components are a lot more complicated than others, and that's just how computers work. Still, the dev has tried to make things as smooth as possible, and although you certainly can turn to Wikipedia and Youtube for help, the game does provide all the information you need to figure everything out yourself.

When I was a kid i easily beat Rocky's Boots, because Rocky's Boots was supposed to be easy. I never beat Robot Odyssey when I was a kid, because faulty copy protection prevented me from playing the main game until decades later. Robot Odyssey 2 was never actually made and thus no one got to play it. But Turing Complete is a lot like what I imagined Robot Odyssey 2 might have been.

There are a lot of other good engineering games (and engineering themed games) on Steam. But Turing Complete is the only one where the Discord has players discussing implementing real world CPU architectures in the game. For fun.

And if that's not enough to convince you, consider this: Every other video game you ever have played, will play, or could play can be implemented inside Turing Complete with enough effort. So buying this game is kind of like buying ALL POSSIBLE games. You just need to put them together yourself.\t\t\t\t\t
