\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love it. It's like doing high school math homework (honors level, maybe). It's not all that hard. But it is challenging. The attraction is just that sort of a challenge done for its own sake. Well, then again, you might be able to look at it as educational on topics like assembly programming or chip design. I'm not qualified to tell you if this is close enough to the real world to give you usable experience in any such areas. I just play it for the challenge. (No story or adventure or such as far as I can can see so far. The funny narrator is pretty peripheral.) People who aren't math-inclined may find it too difficult or too unappealing. But I think it may be a favorite of mine (having finished arithmetic and memory so far). The promotional video implies that I can look forward to making some pretty sophisticated software!

Edit: Some feedback for the developers is that I'd like to get more feedback in the game on my solutions. How can it be improved? What are different approaches? And can my solution compete against others'. I suspect some of these things may be coming after Early Access.

Also, an obvious and interesting comparison is Factorio, particularly Factorio's circuits. They are significantly different in that Factorio's switches are always discrete, but so far only one of Turing Complete's is. And TC's short circuit conditions are very different. I'm making things in both. But I don't feel a full understanding of the differences. Perhaps related, I can make SR Latches in Factorio easily, but I don't yet understand the latches in TC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed the game for a while. Let me tell you what you get.

TL;DR: If you have even the slightest interest in logic, computer architecure or assembler then give it a try. It's worth every penny

The Campaign in detail

Early Game: Logic Gates
You start with a single gate. You can play around with the inputs and oututs and see how the gate reacts in different configurations. Using this gate, you build circuits with different behaviour, resulting in new gates. Storywise, the whole thing is accompanied by an alien whose people have abducted you and now doing tests on you.

Pros
Cons
  • Nice UI with intuitive functionality
  • Early levels are easy and not too hard, even without prior knowledge
  • The difficulty curve is decent, with a spike at the XOR gate
  • Story is a little weird, but okay
  • The game fails to create a link between logic gates and their literal representation in a spoken sentence
  • It also fails to explain, that you only have to worry about the ON-signals

My opinion: The early game is decent and not too hard if you have a little bit of background knowledge. If this is your very first time that you hear about logic gates, then the early game is a bit confusing for you, but you will manage.

Early Components
At this point the level map splits up and you can choose, which path you want to tackle first. You can either go for the arithmetic part ot the memory part and freely choose where you want to progress. This is good and bad, because now a level highly depends on the things you learned in its previous level. If you do the levels horizonally (progressing equaly in each path) you forget the things that you learned before. It is expected that you remember those things. So for the best experience, its smart to progress vertically as far as you can, before you go to a different path.

Contentwise, you now learn about (and build) the basic components of computers like adder, register, counter etc. The explanation is good for the most part, but there are ofc some places where it could be better. This section is noteable harder than the early game with a spike at the level Saving Gracefully.

Pros
Cons
  • The riddles are really fun to solve
  • You understand how a computer do things on the most basic level
  • Difficulty is decent, and you can do a different level, if you get stuck
  • If you have no prior knowledge, you may encounter difficulty spikes that can leave you frustrated
  • Taking long breakes between sessions is bad, because you have to remember what you learned before

My opinion: This part is fun. Its hard, but the riddles are simple enough that you can help yourself with trying around, reading the manual (or ingame hints), asking other people, or even googeling real life circuits.

Architecture
You now unlock the ability to create your own custom components. Additionally you start creating your very own computer using all the parts you build before. Its all about creating new components and wiring them up in a smart way, so your computer can follow simple instructions. The explanations have little text, but are surprisingly accurate. This section goes on until you have a turing complete computer.

Pros
Cons
  • You learn a lot about architecture
  • You can build your own components now and use them in your computer
  • Due to regular game updates, this part encountered several savegame wipes
  • You need more time to finish a level, because you have to go back again to understand everything that happens

My opinion: Challenging part - but fun. Your first computer will look messy, but if you ever replay it again (after you finished the campaign) it will look so much better, because of the things you learned on the way.

Programming
Once your computer is completed, you start programming it. This is difficult at first, and gets easier the further you go. You might need to lookup an example on how to do it, because the ingame manual is very limited. The riddles are very interesting, but can also be frustrating when you have the right idea, but struggle with the syntax.

Pros
Cons
  • You learn how programming is translated to hardware
  • The programming tool looks nice and is easy to use
  • You use your very own hardware to run your program on. So you can also solve every programing level solely in hardware, which is a nice challenge.
  • The assembler tool could use better explanations and examples
  • You can not move on to more hardware levels, when you have no interest in coding

My opinion: I had struggles with this part at the beginning, mainly because i had no idea what i could do with the tool i was given. It got better over time, but on my first playthrough, i was very happy when this part was done.

More Components and Architecture
Once you completed your first computer and solved the first programming puzzles, the game continues with the introduction of more complex components. You build a new architecture which is way more sophisticated than the one you build before, so you can do more things. Basically you repeat the concepts from before in a more condenced way, until you complete another computer (which is way better to use). But notice that the game is not handholding you anymore. You have way more freedom in terms of detail-decisions (which is a good and a bad thing).

Pros
Cons
  • You learn more about complex components like RAM, Stack etc.
  • The new computer is better than the last
  • The hardware riddles are challenging
  • You have to make many descisions on your own, so you might feel lost
  • The levels take longer to complete, because you have to do many things as once

My opinion: It is nice to build a new computer step by step, using the concepts that you learned before. It might get messy and maybe you have to start over, but it feels really nice when it works at the end.

Final Programming
The programming puzzles for the new architecture are hard, but really nice. Your computer can do things better that the last, and you get the hang on using assembler. If you want a real challenge, try to solve the riddles without programming.

Pros
Cons
  • The riddles are hard and presented nicely
  • Every riddle can be solved using programming or hardware
  • You have to think about expanding your hardware to solve riddles easier
  • You might get stuck, because the riddles are hard
  • Programming background is very very helpful

My opinion: This time i had way more fun with the programming part. The riddles are challenging on its own - the programming and hardware part adds to that. It feels veeery satisfying when you finaly got a working solution.

What else
  • There is a Sandbox mode, which contains many components like a sprite console, network adapter, soundbox etc.
  • You unlock a scoring system with an online leaderboard for the best solutions
  • Custom level sharing is an upcomming feature
  • German and French localization

Final thoughts
I had and still have a ton of fun playing this game. The dev is really active and bugs are fixed withing hours. If you are interested just give it a try - you won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t
