\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh, boy....

I know this is early access, but this feels more like "don't access".
Let's not talk about the gameplay, it's awesome. Love the concept, love the game.
But it's so unplayable. This badboy crashes at every single move. (Exceptions flying wild I suppose)

Want prove? Open up Level 3, select the "In" Node and press CTRL+C.

Now, you might wonder: Why did I get the idea to copy the "In" Node in the first place?
The reason: One level crashed and the "In" Node DISAPPEARED. I had a full solution there and now I can't complete it because I have NO IN NODE.
It also crashes when you press CTRL+Z too often.

I played for two hours and had about 10 crashes. This is not fun.

Apart from that, the wiring mechanic feels pretty bad. It often feels like you have to wire it in weird ways so you have enough room. And by that I mean that the paths the wires try to create are bulky and useless often. Making them have nice paths is pretty annoying.

You also seemingly can't copy structures, which is VERY necessary in my opinion.

I really like the idea of this game and the puzzles are fun, but it's so unstable that right now I cannot recommend it. Hopefully this will be fixed soon. If it is, I will change this review. I want to love it but it doesn't let me right now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHuge potential, but definitely an early access title.

I recommend this depending on what you want to get out of the game, but with a HUGE caveat that it doesn't do anything to teach you new concepts beyond giving you a puzzle to play with and test your guesses. As such, I would recommend this as a supplement to a course like the famous "NAND 2 Tetris" course or the accompanying "Elements of Computing Systems" book to learn the theory then apply/test it in Turing Complete.

Another big caveat is that in addition to not explaining the computing concepts, the interface to the game isn't explained very well either. This lead me to about 15 minutes of blindly clicking random UI elements to figure out what the game wanted of me. If you're used to looking at truth tables, you'll probably have an easier time figuring it out, but the interface is still very awkward, with the signal emitter toggles in the far left of the screen and the truth tables on the bottom (and some of the tables are interactive, and some aren't, and it's not telegraphed well at all) and the components in the far right. I also had some dialogue boxes overlay on top of each other, making them impossible to read. I think this is exacerbated since I'm playing on a linux computer with a hidpi display, which makes everything super tiny and probably out of place.

One last thing is that as far as I can tell, when you move a component, you can't move any of the signal routing "wires" with it, so you have to delete the wires and re-connect them if you need to move components to organize your circuit better. I'm sure that if there's not already a way to preserve connections, there will be one soon enough.

Overall, I recommend it to people who are aware of the issues with the current early access build and still want to play it or support the project, otherwise I'd say wait until it's a bit more polished. Regardless, I think it has a ton of potential and am really excited to see this game evolve.\t\t\t\t\t
