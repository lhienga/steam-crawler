\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWonderful game (tool?).
If you have any interest in logic gates absolutely give this a go, for fun _or_ for learning.

1. The progression from base concepts to more complex interactions is natural, so every level is an application of knowledge learned previously.
2. The UI is nice, but I would highly recommend getting to know the hotkeys, as they will make your life dramatically easier.
3. The "lore" for why you're doing what you're doing is great, and adds a lighthearted undertone to the game.

There are a few smaller improvements that I think would benefit, however.
1. Let me invert/flip objects. Objects with 3 inputs (switch, for example) are a pain to rotate and I often find myself wrapping around them. (This may be part of the puzzle, I guess, but to me the main focus is solving the logic, not positioning the objects).
2. On the earlier "missions" where you're building the core components, maybe after a successful run the game could show a comparison of how it _could_ be done most efficiently, to compare.
3. More colours! Maybe the ability to choose your own colour palette?\t\t\t\t\t
