\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR Absolutely love this game and recommend to any fellow geeks!

I am a total geek. Have been into programming for 40 years and a "professional" programmer for 36 of those and computers have been my life for all of that time. I have always been fascinated by how they work i.e. more than just being able to code, actually understanding how the guts of computers work in relation to the code you write.

I didn't get the chance to go to university and so have only my 'A' level computer science and what I've picked up over the years in regards to the foundations of computing i.e. logic gates etc

So, I approached this game as someone who understands the principles of logic gates and circuits but who has not had any serious education in relation to them. I had high hopes for it. I mean, my two favourite past-times : programming and gaming!

I have not been disappointed.

At it's heart this game is a puzzler. The main aim of every level is to build a small part of a computer beginning at the smallest components and working your way up, via a "tech tree", to larger and larger circuits to a working computer.

The challenges become progressively greater and you work through the tech tree. The learning curve is very well designed and each step is a logical enough progression that work previously done makes sense as you move up the tree.

The interface is particularly good, at least to me. The way that circuits are built and connected is done via simple drag and drop and I actually find it quite relaxing when creating some of the larger circuits.

The only real negative I can think of is the lack of "copy and paste" functionality i.e. you cannot copy a group of gates and connections and paste them around the circuit - you have to re-create them again and again. Time consuming but not the end of the world.

There is a story of sorts but, although not terrible, I did find it a little grating!

As a geek I would totally recommend this game to any fellow geeks. It is challenging enough to feel a sense of achievement on completion of a circuit but never - at least for me - enough to become annoying. Obviously if you're not geeky or logically minded then this probably isn't for you but if you have any serious interest in programming and/or computer hardware then you should enjoy this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you enjoy electronic logic or wish to hone up your skills, but find university courses on the topic mind numbing - this will definitely enhance your experience.
There are a lot of logic simulators out there (including professional ones used to engineer new hardware), but few will offer a learning curve as intuitive as this one, for new comers and experienced engineers alike.

It is still early in development and still needs work in stability and user experience.
The game sometimes crashes (but so far, thankfully, it saved my progress) and the levels could use labels to let the user know which level includes which type of logic gate design. This would be especially useful when you need to go back and review one of the designs for reference.

In the end though, I definitely think it's a worthwhile purchase if you're into this type of 'games'.

It starts easy, but the difficulty ramps up pretty quickly (which is a good thing in my opinion), since it forces you to really get into what you are doing.
