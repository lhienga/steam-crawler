\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe circuit design game you didn't know you wanted. If you're a computer enthusiast and want to understand how it *really* works, this game is an excellent way to learn.

The game does a decent job of introducing all of the standard computer logic gates (AND, NAND, OR, etc.) but it is up to you to puzzle out how to use them efficiently. This can be a positive if you like figuring things out for yourself, but on harder puzzles you may need to consult outside sources or look up guides. Fortunately - it takes a long time to reach this stage.

The game really opens up once you have a functioning "computer" which operates on 8-bit instructions and has 6 bytes of memory - you finally have something that you can properly "program" and "run" by feeding it in information. At this stage you can design modular components for your machine - dedicated subcomponents for math, conditional statements - and the game does a great job helping you understand the power of this idea (allowing your designs to be easier to look at, and letting you isolate unrelated bits of logic from each other). This modularization and puzzle-based optimization scratches a similar itch to automation games like Factorio.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm reviewing a pre-release of the game. The issues seem minor and typical of games still in development.

I'm just getting to the part where the actual computer construction starts. I managed to figure out the earlier steps, but it really helped that I knew Karnaugh maps and could simplify some of the logic. I sometimes wished I could step through the test data even past an error. Sometimes I was not sure what the instructions meant and it would have been helpful to see more examples of correct answers.

I ran into two issues:
- The Mac version of the game from Jan 29, 2022 did not run on Apple silicon macs. I gather from seeing other comments that earlier versions were working.
- When I went from the step with an ALU doing and/or/nand/nor to one doing and/or/nand/not/plus/minus, I was unable to build on the previous step and had to start with a new schematic. It told me the "save was from an earlier version"

The game is fun. It turns circuit design into a series of interesting puzzles to solve.\t\t\t\t\t
