\r\n\t\t\t\t\tContains a pretty cool logic simulator. This is something you would normally have to pay thousands of dollars to get. And even then, they're usually buggy and broken, whereas this one actually works correctly without bugs.

There are a few caveats that I hope will change when it gets out of early access:

1. The assembly language is clunky. You can't use differing syntax to choose between an immediate, a memory address, and a register -- you have to use a different opcode. Also, you can't define multi-byte opcodes, even when some operations should obviously have fewer parameters, with dummy bytes as part of the operation. I think they did this to keep the architecture open ended.

2. The number of puzzles is a little low. The game contains complete 64-bit architecture support, but over the course of the game you will only develop 2 different 8-bit architectures. Any 64-bit work has to be in the sandbox.

3. The game has a story (purely as flavor text) where you have been abducted by aliens and enslaved into working on their computers. Some of the levels simply lack the alien stuff, and just give you instructions instead. It makes the game look unpolished, but isn't really a problem for an early access game.

Took me 36 hours to complete all the puzzle content (as of 2022-03), but I do embedded software for a living.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has promise with a number of rough edges (early release phase). At least it tries to guide people in the general direction of solutions. This is better then other similar games.

Problems:
- The only way to get a fresh schematic is to delete all other schematics to reset. This deletes all the other schematics for the level. I highly recommend to create a new and not change the default schematic on levels where there are mandatory parts.

- Copy / Paste from component to schematic, all mandatory parts must be removed before copying it from the component. There's no explanation for why pasting is disabled when mandatory parts are left in.

- In the Component editor, it isn't apparent that the "switch schematics" button changed to "switch components". Maybe just split it out into a second button and disable whichever is active at that moment?

- In levels with mandatory parts, components can't contain those parts yet there's a need to de-clutter and organize. Components are how to do that.

- The importance of chip layout isn't stressed enough in the intro levels. When those levels are made into components, then the PCB profile is shown. This creates unnecessary work later to re-organize the previous level into something compact and useful as a component.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI almost never leave reviews but felt obliged to do so here. This game is absolutely incredible. I'll preface this by saying that I work as a software engineer and went to school for computer science, so I really enjoy logic and problem solving puzzles. If you don't personally, then this game might not be for you.

However, if you're in any way interested in computers, electrical engineering, or even just enjoy solving cool puzzles or logic riddles, you'll likely enjoy this game.

It starts off super simply having you build super basic pieces, and before you know it, you're combining all the pieces you've built into a working computer and programming it. I can't get over how much I enjoy this game and I haven't fully finished it yet (as of 17 hours in I've solved the maze and am working on the 'extra' levels). It easily makes it's way into one of my top 10 all time games, and the developers should be proud of the masterpiece they've built.\t\t\t\t\t
