\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt doesn't really feel like a 'puzzle' game, it's just straight up learning computer architecture. With that being said, it's very good as a learning tool and simulation tool. As far as puzzles go, it's missing a lot of what makes a puzzle game satisfying.

Concepts pass by very quickly and I never feel like I develop a solid knowledge of anything since we move on to the next subject immediately. Often when I finish a level I'm thinking "Is that really the solution? That can't be the solution" and it actually is. The 'tips' are pretty useless - "This level is hard" is not a good tip.
There's no hints - it's either the whole solution or nothing. Some solutions are missing so you gotta go on the internet to find them, hopefully this gets addressed during early access.
The ability to test individual solutions when you start moving into massive projects doesn't exist. Like when you start building with memory modules and you know the latter 2/3 of a solution, but just want to test it, you can't do it intuitively the first few predetermined inputs only test the first 1/3 of the problem.
I particularly loathed the small box project. It's like 20 levels in, and the game finally decides to officially tell you about rotating pieces. Even knowing the solution, I struggled for so long to get it to actually fit in the box and got really frustrated and really stopped having fun at that point (though I kept going a little while longer), especially since being confined to a small space was never something you had to worry about.

The UI works really well in my opinion, it's pretty intuitive and is very impressively put together, especially when things start to get messy. The general concept of the game is extremely cool and I would hope that they would use it as a learning tool in universities. So with that, I can't in good faith give it a thumbs down - it's very impressive, it's just not really a puzzle game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's extremely engaging, surprisingly so for such a simple premise. The slow build-up to a very simple computer is satisfying and mostly gradual enough to not be frustrating.

The OVERTURE computer stays around just about long enough for its initial novelty to wear off and motivate a better machine - and that's where the training wheels start coming off. The result is a decent learning curve in terms of what must be implemented. I do think that before the hand-holding stops, some of the decisions affecting this replacement architecture, LEG are weird (Two immediate operands on every instruction plus a whole byte for the result destination? Branches are taken by interpreting the result byte as an immediate address in program ROM?), but perhaps even that is just a motivation for the player to design something better ASAP.

There are a few areas that could be improved: The interface is somewhat quirky, though consistent, and would benefit from a few more tips spread throughout the initial levels. Latches are limited to the basics and occasionally are not recognized as latches, breaking the simulation. More flexibility in component I/O design would be nice, and the same goes for a program component that's RAM.
My biggest gripe, however, is with the Lab's testing capabilities, which are annoying to write tests for - having to account for every detail of the intermediate steps does not translate well beyond the simplest tests. Something like a level editor to define inputs and expected outputs would be very appreciated.

Overall, very fun and a strong recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat puzzle game about digital computers.

Pros:
- Clean, clear, nice graphics
- Decent amount of content and levels
- Progression is fun
- Great tutorials at the start
- Impressive range of things covered

Cons:
- No progression to any sort of compiler or basically any assembler. Only allows you to add keywords for byte sized constants, makes the programming a lot more tedious when using wide instructions or any sort of complex instruction setup.
- Very little explanation / direction for some of the later and harder levels. I enjoyed them with some background in this area, but I imagine they would be quite the challenge if you're not familiar with the content going in.

Would definitely recommend if this seems up your alley!
If you like programming or logic puzzle games, get this as well!\t\t\t\t\t
