\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm about finished with all the levels in the game. The difficulty depends a lot on how much you already know about computer architecture and assembly programming methodology. I have a lot of experience in both, so the majority of the levels I found pretty trivial. The most difficulty I had was re-arranging the architecture when the game called for a new feature to be added and the recursive algorithm puzzles.

It's hard to say how this would be on it's own as an educational tool. But, I think it could be great alongside another resource, like the book Code by Charles Petzold. The game itself is all digital logic, but some of the mechanics aren't especially well explained such as logic level precedence. The simulation also doesn't cover any kind of internal component characteristics, such as gate propagation delays, hold times, etc. So, microcoding/instruction sequencing is never really necessary as gates are programmed to instantly activate and avoid the race conditions you'd find in real hardware. That does take away from the realism, but I think that's probably a good thing for a game (computer engineering is hard, yo).

It doesn't scratch the Zachtronics itch as much as I'd hoped. But, it's still a very interesting game. I'd recommend it if you're interested in learning about digital logic or basic computer design theory.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are two phases to this game:
1) solving the levels and eventually building your own computer. While it sounds hard, it is actually very manageable and if you get stuck, there's a great discord community that could help you.
2) doing stuff after all the levels are solved, which could be either building a better and bigger computer, or competing on the leaderboard. Both could take an absolutely enormous amount of time, but are also very fun if you are into that sort of stuff.
I would recommend everyone to try this game. Not only is it very rewarding, but also, even if you just complete the normal levels you will get at least a semester worth of learning within a few days.\t\t\t\t\t
