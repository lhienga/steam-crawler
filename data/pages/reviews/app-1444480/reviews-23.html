\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe concept is cool and I've been dreaming of a game like this. I understand making a whole computer is daunting, but that aside I think didactically the game still isn't good at teaching you. At some point, big leaps are made without making sure you thoroughly understand a component and its functions. You get one level to make the component and one level at some point to use it. I think maybe the subject matter is just too complex to teach in the few levels that the game offers.

It quickly turned from something enjoyable into clicking view solution because what they were asking became confusing. You gotta make sure people didn't just read what you said but have come to their own thorough understanding, or they will hit a roadblock later on and it becomes frustrating. One prime example is the DEC component, which makes things so much easier in a later level, but by the time it becomes very useful, there is no thorough understanding of the component and its uses from the player's part. And so the player instead starts using AND components (and I'm not the only one, considering the solution video had multiple people attempting this same thing, which proves the game failed to impart understanding of the usefulness of DEC at some point.)

So yeah, I had high hopes for this, but I feel like it's more suited to people who are into hardcore puzzles like this. I have a background in compsci btw, I'm no stranger to binary and I've made a virtual machine myself and yet this game was still hard as hell.\t\t\t\t\t
