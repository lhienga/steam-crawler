\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very effective at demonstrating low-level computer logic, but don't get your hopes up with the promise of building a turing-complete CPU. The higher-level stages of logic aren't viable with the game's current UI features, and the instructions for each puzzle are vague and even self-contradictory at times, meaning you will spend hours working on something only to find out that you misinterpreted the instructions, forcing you to delete the solution that you've built. It's by no means as finished and user-friendly as Zachtronics games.

As the game is, I would possibly recommend it as a tutorial in low-level logic in the stages that actually work well, except that I wouldn't pay $20 for something that limited in scope. It shows promise and it was both fun and educational for the initial stages, but it needs some work before it becomes a useful tool for demonstrating the functions of a full CPU.\t\t\t\t\t
