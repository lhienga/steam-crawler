\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts a good game that teaches you how to build a CPU. However, the UX needs some serious work. The instructions are often very confusing and unclear. Information that would be important to have in a level is only accessible if you go back to earlier levels (for example opcodes for a CPU architecture). There is a manual but important info is not added there.

You cannot go back in dialogue, or the same dialogue keeps popping up, for example when you go from the components factory back to the main level. When you have a bug in your computer often that bug is in a sub or subsubcomponent. However, the settings don't carry over when you go from one schematic to another meaning you have to manually recreate them all the time.

The binary switches in a byte are represented differently in the side pane and in the schematic, making it complete guesswork to figure out which bit is where.

As such, the game is interesting and very educational, but also endlessly frustrating sometimes. I gave up due to that frustration.

The difficulty curve might also be a bit steep, it was ok for me but I did design CPUs by hand before.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I can recommend this game but that would be dishonest of me.

My only issue with the game is with the UI/UX of the game. It gets in the way and just result in a rather uncomfortable experience where I'm unable to "be in the zone" and iterate quickly to solve each of the puzzle.

It lacks niceties that I consider essential such as wires being tied to their connections as you move the components around, adjusting the slack of the wires, grouping components, etc.

As it is hard to put these kind of things into words, I recommend trying out the following similar software: Logisim Evolution, Blender's Node Editor, Unreal's Blueprint Editor, Diagrams.net, Lucidchart, Microsoft Visio.

It has gone to the point where I wish I can just use Verilog instead.

Other than that, this seems like a decent game but I couldn't bring myself to go further as it simply became too frustrating for me to continue. So I'll never know for sure how good this game is at teaching the elements of computers.\t\t\t\t\t
