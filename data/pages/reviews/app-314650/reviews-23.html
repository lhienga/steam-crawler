A CHECKLIST

A peaceful experience overall. It features some mellow tunes while you navigate.
Visually impressive, but only from a distance. Once you fly closer, textures go back in time to 20 years ago (incredibly low res).
The scale available to see is truly impressive! It's an ambitious project, that's for sure!
Performance-wise it was doing pretty okay! The game ran smooth overall.
No greedy DLC strategy. There are only a few high-res texture packs and a right-to-use-commercially addon (which is fine).
In my opinion, there's a shocking lack of creativity on the controls part. Flying around and navigating in space is very unintuitive, but after 10 minutes you'll start to get the hang of it.
The UI design is bad all over the place. My guess is that they were planning to 'outsource' this part to modders? While I can understand, there should be a better minimally functional UI in place for those who don't want to add mods.
The game actually crashed a few times while I was playing. The crahes seemed random. One crash was in the menu screen.
NOT playable on GeForceNOW.
I feel like the price is way to high for this product, even on sale.
Aparently playable on VR. Haven't tried it though (since I don't own a headset), but these types of games/programs are an almost must be compatible with VR, these days.
The game/program looks better in the marketing material/presentation than it actually is. It has either changed since the marketing material was taken, or the marketing material is a fine tuned version. I would compare it with the Ubisoft titles in this regard. Ubisoft titles look great in their presentation, but the actual games are less impressive.

MENTIONS

Release date: Jun, 2019
I've played it in: Dec, 2023
Downloaded it last night and spent nearly an hour with it trying to figure out what I can and can't do in this game/program. I want to mention that I am a passionate 'space enjoyer' and simply navigating to check cosmic items is not boring to me at all. That being said, I don't feel like I would get my money's worth for this product, therefore I refunded it.

GOOD TO KNOW

The development of this project seems very slow. It may take a long time until it's finished.
It appears that there is some drama between the community and the developers. I have no idea what's it all about, but my point of view is for the product itself and my experience with it. It is unrelated to the respective 'drama'.
I don't think the price is justified (even on sale). There are many comparable titles on Steam that you can get for a lot cheaper. Additionally, if we were to consider the slow development along with the previously mentioned drama, maybe morale for this project could kill it soon, therefore I would not recommend a purchase of this title, at least not until it's out of early access.
Community Rating at time of review: Overwhelmingly Positive

EXTRA THOUGHTS

I feel like the controls can be greatly improved with a bit of fine tunning. This is the first thing that 'turned me off'. For controls, you can take reference from Google Earth tbh (or similar projects). It's much more intuitive, and people expect navigation to be simple.
There is absolutely minimal effort put on the UI side. All icons are the same size, but not all are equaly important and should be differentiated. Aside from that, the functionality of the frames is also sub-par. Filtering when searching something, for instance: e-commerce websites have solved the issue of filtering a long time ago with check marks for item types. I don't want to feel like I'm wasting time looking for something. I want to relax when playing the game/program. This experience is frustrating.
