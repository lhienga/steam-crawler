\r\n\t\t\t\t\tdefinitely the best universe simulator I've ever played and heard of. just the sheer scale of the universe is incredible, because every planet, asteroid, and moon are realistic sizes ( massive ) and fully explore-able, and in every galaxy, there's probably between a few hundred thousand, to a few billion planets. there are as many galaxies ( if not more ) as there are star systems in a single, large galaxy. the space in between galaxies and stars is realistic ( so there's lots of massive empty space in between objects ).the universe is absolutely massive, and completely explore-able. there are even real celestial bodies, like real galaxies, planets, and stars, and that library of real objects in this game is ever-growing.

the graphics look great, except some of the lighting when standing on rocky celestial objects ( bad shadows mostly ), but i think the developers will update the graphics soon. the developers are actively improving this simulator/game after all the years

the downsides i think of about the current state of the game are 1: not much variety in the textures and models of nebulas and galaxies. 2: the terrain generation on rocky bodies doesn't have much variety. it is randomly and procedurally generated, but the types of mountains, valleys etc are currently quite limited. everything else about planets like atmospheres, skies, rings, colour of textures etc do have lots of variety though.

as much as some randomly generated things have limitations, they are reasonable limitations ( hardware goals and stuff ), and there are plenty of things that keep this game fresh even for me with 114 hours in it.

i do recommend this game for people who love the beauty of space, for people who love astronomy, space physics, and just most people interested in most aspects of space\t\t\t\t\t
