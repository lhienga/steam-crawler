\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe universe is a totally BONKERS place and this app helps me to de-BONKER it a little bit so my limited human brain can slightly wrap itself around it.

Want to land on S0-2 and bear witness how it orbits Sagittarius A* at 7000 km/s at 120 AU from it? This app can do it. Want to sit on earth, pick your favorite star and fly to it while reading about its properties? This app can do it. Want to look at our Solar System from 1 billion light years away and zoom into it? This app can do it. In other words: this app is INSANE, and an extremely helpful tool in getting to grips with distances and time scales that are otherwise un-graspable.

This is not a game, this is a science-driven universe engine, and a gem at that.


The biggest limitation in my opinion is that galaxies such as our Milky Way do not move internally, that is: you cannot see the Solar System make its orbit around Sag A* in 250 million years. I guess that would be impossible to simulate on regular computers given the scale of simulation.

SpaceEngine will make your mind go supernova. Highly recommended if you are even slightly interested in astronomy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've waited my entire life to experience a game where I can freely observe and explore the entire universe. While most of it is generated with what is likely to exist, you're able to visit every, planet, moon, asteroid, solar system, star, galaxy, nebula and black holes that have either been discovered or theorised. The developers nailed the sense of scale and depth to really demonstrate just how big the universe really is. A feature I enjoy is while in free mode, you're able to set how fast you want to travel through space in units, The highest being 100mly/sec which if I'm not mistaken is 100 million light years per second. Comparing the distance between different objects is and realising that even at light speed it takes thousands of years to reach your destination.

I've gone slightly off track with the review as it has no structure, I hope you take that as a good thing as I am simply mind blown the more I explore and read about space within the game. If you are even remotely curious about space and want to learn about planets or just itch the part of your brain that wants to travel throughout the universe, please buy this game.

I hope to see a lot of other people get lost in this simulator for hours learning about the great unknown and possibly experience an existential crisis.\t\t\t\t\t
