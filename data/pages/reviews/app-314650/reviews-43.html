Developed by the brilliant mind of Russian astronomer and programmer Vladimir Romanyuk, this interactive 3D planetarium is a testament to the power of technology and human curiosity.

At its core, SpaceEngine is a meticulously crafted 1:1 scale representation of the observable universe, blending real astronomical data with scientifically precise procedural generation algorithms. The result is a breathtakingly realistic cosmos that you can explore in any direction, at any speed, and even travel back and forth in time. It's a sandbox for the universe, limited only by your imagination.

One of the most remarkable aspects of SpaceEngine is its commitment to scientific accuracy. As you navigate the cosmos, vital information about celestial objects, such as temperature, mass, radius, and spectrum, is readily available on the Heads-Up Display (HUD) and in an accessible information window. This dedication to detail makes SpaceEngine an invaluable tool for both astronomy enthusiasts and professionals alike.

SpaceEngine's vast celestial playground includes a staggering collection of over 130,000 real objects. It features stars from the Hipparcos catalog, galaxies from the NGC and IC catalogs, iconic nebulae, and a comprehensive database of known exoplanets and their host stars. Whether you're gazing at a distant star, exploring the intricacies of a galaxy cluster, or visiting an exoplanet light-years away, SpaceEngine offers an immersive experience that is second to none.

The evolution of SpaceEngine from its freeware origins to a paid edition on Steam has only enriched its capabilities and support for enthusiasts. The addition of VR headset compatibility takes the experience to new heights, allowing you to become truly immersed in the vastness of space.
