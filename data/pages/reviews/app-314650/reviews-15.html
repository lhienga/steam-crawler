\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short? Yes, 100%; this "game" is absolutely amazing. Yet, I will be truthful here for the sake of others mental stability.


IF YOU HAVE SERIOUS DEPRESSION, DREAD, ANXIETY, OR EXISTENTIAL CRISES THEN I DO NOT SUGGEST!!!

I know about mental health problems VERY well. I have PTSD from my time in the military and some of the stuff I had to go through for months on end. I battle my mind everyday, but I have been able to deal with it. I also work for the mentally disabled. With guys that have severe schizophrenia, depression, anxiety, autism, ADHD, ADD, and loads more. I'm not a doctor, but I know quite a bit when it comes to this stuff. It's like asking a truck driver to explain how to build a good truck. He doesn't know exactly how it's done, but he knows a lot more than most and is a good starting point.

If you are depressed, speak to someone. I beg you. However, what does mentality mean when it comes to this game? Well, the human condition itself is the main problem. We just CANNOT fathom how big the universe is. Some points on my review. I don't think spoilers are possible for this game but I will tag it just in case.

By the time you speed up time a couple units? You will find the years add on. Hitting year 54,295 can make your mind go to a place of existential dread and meaninglessness. Being able to select ANYTHING you see on screen is overwhelming. When clicking on a small speck of light to go towards it becomes another millions of specs of light, to then zoom in and see a solar system that could have 1000s of celestial bodies? It makes you think about being alone in all of it. However! There is a good thing about all of it. Finding planets that are perfect for harboring life makes you pretty happy to know it's possible and most likely probable life already exists somewhere else. I remember a random planet I found about 10 minutes into playing. It was essentially Earth, but 3 times bigger with extremely light blue, almost white, oceans.

Galaxies, Stars, singularities, planets and other things can be absolutely MASSIVE. If you have astrophobia? This is a horror game through and through. The size of some stuff makes you think about how insignificant you are. You're not insignificant! The greatest things come from the smallest packages. Remember that. Please make sure you understand you're own mental psyche when exploring around. Play some music, give it a shot, but if it's getting you into a place of being down? Stop playing for a bit and come back.

After all, the universe isn't going anywhere anytime soon.
