\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe amount of detail and real data in this simulator is amazing. Though this game is pretty resource intensive, I am shocked at how well it runs, as it is extremely beautiful and relatively detailed. Some of the planet surfaces can be a little plain when flat, but things with rougher surfaces (often moons and asteroids) can appear very detailed and convincing, especially when the light shines at the right angles to emphasize the shadows. There are so many camera options, and the ability to edit stellar objects is rudimentary but very effective for creating really cool looking shots. The virtual photos you can take are perfect desktop backgrounds.

There are some occasional graphical glitches, particularly on asteroids, but they aren't too common. My game has crashed a few times when I have pushed it to its limits, or when I have too many other programs open at the same time on my PC; Due to this, almost every crash I have had so far has been somewhat predictable and understandable, but you have to be careful to not overdo certain actions such as zooming the camera in too far or you will overload the game (Keep in mind, I am running it on max settings, which is pushing it a little bit for my computer).
In my 5 hours of playtime so far I have not gotten bored whatsoever, but I can see how over time things could get repetitive.

This sim is WELL worth it at full price, let alone if it goes on sale. I highly recommend to anyone who likes space.\t\t\t\t\t
