\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a wonderful program for those interested in astronomy or related fields. I come back to it every few nights before bed, and I'm still exploring the 'neighborhood'. I started with the moon, which provoked interest in the moon's geographical features (rendered in exquisite detail); I'm now reading all about those and how they formed in DK's 'Universe' book. I've also begun to explore Mars and read about its features. Of course, I couldn't help jumping to nearby stars and exoplanets, observing Sagittarius A* (the black hole at the galactic center!), and flying hundreds of thousands of light years to nearby galaxies. SpaceEngine is staggering in extent; you can 'zoom out' I-don't-know-how-many billions of light years to view, in three dimensions, the cosmic filamentous structure of the universe. You can pilot spaceships with Alcubierre 'warp' drives (I read about those, too) to beautiful procedurally-generated planets with exotic multicellular life. My favorite thing about the program, though, is that, played with Wikipedia and a few books on hand (note that there is an in-game wiki, too), it has become a fruitful educational experience for me, a layman eager to know what actually and possibly lies beyond our 'pale blue dot'.\t\t\t\t\t
