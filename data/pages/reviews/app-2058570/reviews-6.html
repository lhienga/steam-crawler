\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRift Wizard 2 is a genius piece of traditional roguelike design. At the time of this review I've only tried it out for an hour but I have seen enough to know that this is a special game.

The basic premise is simple. You teleport into a level. To progress, you must clear all of the enemies in the level. You find skillpoints in the levels with which you can purchase new spells or passives (there are also other mechanics to gain new abilities). There is an incredible amount of original spells which all have three upgrades each, so the variety of builds is incalculable. Besides the basic fantasy spells you might expect such as icicles, summoning familiars or fireballs, there's also fun original contributions. Become an elephant, summon 10 more elephants and stampede through the level!

There are a lot of traditional roguelikes which are built upon elaborate, interlocking simulations, generating sprawling worlds, procedural histories and so on - such as Caves of Qud or Dwarf Fortress. These games are great, but Rift Wizard 2 is a very accessible roguelike which has you making non-trivial choices from the very first moment you press play. There's no larger world to explore, just turn to turn gameplay.

The presentation also does not leave anything to be asked for. Readable and pleasing visuals, impressive music.

P.S. One issue I would like to see fixed is that if you have the equipment which starts you with a friendly spawner in a level, the spawner can block you in. This can be avoided by taking care when you enter a level, but it can be a softlock in some situations.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhich do I recommend, Rift Wizard or Rift Wizard 2? I honestly can't say, because they are both sufficiently different to be hard to compare. I can understand why the dev chose to make this a sequel instead of an update or a DLC. I prefer RW2.

In RW1 the meta is to have a handful of extremely upgraded spells with multiple damage types. Once you discover a strategy that (sometimes) works, you can reliably execute that strategy most of the time. Because of how daunting and large the spell list is, there's very little incentive to branch out to new strategies or to invest in niche spells.

In RW2 the meta is to have a wide variety of spells that synergize with your gear. You're heavily encouraged to invest lightly in a wide variety of niche spells. Because much of the power of spells comes from RNG items, not player chosen upgrades, it's very much up to RNG which strategies will work best (many strategies still work well enough even without items).

Both games are very difficult. In both games the difficulty feels mostly fair - most of my deaths are due to misreading tooltips, being stingy on consumables, or losing track of how much damage I'm taking per turn. Between the helpful tooltips and the battle log, the only things I can blame are my inexperience and complacency.

I got 100+ hours out of RW1, and I suspect I'll get another 100+ hours if not more out of RW2. That said, this is a game best played in SMALL doses.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 20 hours I cannot recommend this when RW1 exists. The changes to spells and character builds is just very strange and it leads to you being very very weak in later levels to where you absolutely have to rely on pets and summons. The Spell upgrades are extremely gimmicky and rarely feel like they have useful synergies (although I do love ricochet on magic missile). It has really opened my eyes when thinking about my issues with the first game, as they don't seem nearly as bad as this. Although RNG could ♥♥♥♥ things up as with any amazing RL, I believe shrines have a much better implementation compared to the equipment or new skills. Nothing really feels like an improvement so for now I would wait until more updates.

RW1 has some challenging achievements and if you're in the RL mood just play that instead of RW2 and try to get them since this game doesn't currently have any. Despite my many problems I still think this could outshine the original if updated and worked on.\t\t\t\t\t
