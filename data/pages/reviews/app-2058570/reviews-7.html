\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm gonna beat this game on my first ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ try.
I'm gonna beat this game on my second ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ try.

Oh, I actually did. All hail ice mage.


Those who've played RW1 will recognize much of the content currently available in RW2 Early Access, as the majority of RW1's spells, skills, and enemies were carried over. In my estimation, new spells and skills represent about a fifth of RW2's spellbook.

The upgrade choices for many existing spells were also reworked, changing synergy possibilities. Spell upgrades in RW2 are generally more individually impactful, but each spell can only be upgraded once. Goodbye, super Death Bolt. You served me well.

Discount circles and shrines have been replaced by scrolls and equipment chests. Scrolls allow the wizard to learn one of 3-4 spells or skills for free. Chests offer a choice between 3-4 equipment items, grouped into staffs, robes, hats, shoes, pets, or trinkets. Trinkets can stack, but the other slots get replaced by new selections (unsure about pets). Whereas RW1's shrines were spell-specific, RW2's equipment benefits usually apply across a particular school of magic. They're like skills, but weaker and simpler. You can see scroll and chest options when previewing rifts. (It's possible for a chest to offer two of the same piece of equipment, which is probably unintended.)

Potions are much rarer, but the wizard is now refreshed when stepping through a rift. That means full heal, full recharge, and all statuses cleared - sorry, Eye pre-casters.

Memory orb rewards now scale with progress, from 2 orbs on early stages to 5 near the end.

There are new large and massive enemies that occupy multiple tiles. This gives them a greater visual impact, though gameplay-wise there's not much difference, except that it appears they're super-vulnerable to effects that hit multiple tiles.

The UI is largely unchanged, and unfortunately still lacks many QoL features that could mitigate late-game information overload. Actual gameplay challenge aside, later stages are difficult to even look at. Rift previews don't provide enemy counts or resistance/immunity summaries, so you'll again be checking enemy stat pages one-by-one.

Some spell upgrade detail pages now include stat pages for summoned minions, but this isn't available for all spells.

"Threat view" (hover tile & hold T) is just as laggy as before, namely for units with breath-type patterns. I guess the code is checking the breath spread for every tile within breath range? It'll take ~5 seconds to check threat range for a single breathing unit, and far longer if you dared to try and check threats from all enemies.

The soundtrack has improved, but the animation quality hasn't changed.

All in all, this feels more like a remix than a full sequel. New mechanics are limited: there are no big changes like alternative stage clear conditions, run modifiers, optional rewarding challenges, or risky shortcuts. There's obviously no meta-progression. If there are any secrets, they're too secret for me. Even the desktop menu is exactly the same, confusingly enough.

I'm personally content with my purchase and the promise of further development, just be aware that RW2 EA = RW1.5.


If you enjoyed RW1 even half as much as I did, then RW2 is an easy recommendation.
If you felt so-so on the original, then you might want to wait and see.
And if you hated RW1, then why are you here? Get lost.\t\t\t\t\t
