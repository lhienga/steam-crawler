\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a fun time with lots of spells, upgrades, passives, gear, and monsters.
It may seem intimidating at first, but the game is focused enough such that you will quickly learn what you need in order to make meaningful decisions and have fun.

The controls are also very approachable when compared to many traditional roguelikes. You do not need to memorise dozens of keybinds just to play the game. You can get by with only knowing the movement and character sheet key (C) -- everything else is either clearly labelled or accessible via mouse.

Also do not let "Early Access" dissuade you! This feels like a complete and polished game already.

Edit: I just beat the game for the first time, and the final battle felt so epic and full of strategy. It was exactly how a wizard battle should be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery similar to Rift Wizard 1, so don't expect a very different experience. There are some changes that change the feel of the game a bit however. Overall I would say RW2 is a bit more difficult than RW1 but the fundamentals of how to build character are the same.

First big change is Realms now have more mob variety, which encourages the player to have more versatility in their build. This is achieved through randomized modifiers such as 'icy' or 'metallic' which give resistances and abilities to enemies. This is mostly felt in the early realms, where relying on a single strong spell to carry you is a lot harder now.

Second, no more shrines and instead we collect equipment. This lets you piece together your build more gradually rather than coming across a single shrine that suddenly and radically changes your build. Overall, you can achieve similar power-levels as RW1, but progression is smoother. Many abilities from shrines in RW1 have been reworked into spells/skills in RW2.

Third, most spells now have their own unique upgrades that modify the behavior of the spell rather than just stat bonus upgrades.

Exotic Pet Shop is a fun addition. Multi-tile enemies are also fun. The music is quite annoying.

If you liked the first Rift Wizard, there's no reason why you wouldn't like Rift Wizard 2, just don't expect a radically new experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tshe rift on my wizard till i chaos lamasu

Rift Wizard 1 was a very interesting game, to me. On one hand, the theoretical huge amount of build variety and unique synergies was super cool. On the other hand, it felt very difficult to ever make use of those options, due to a variety of reasons including but not limited to:
Fixed 3 SP per rift means a 7SP skill requires leaving your build as-is for 3 straight rifts
Spells only recharging with mana potions causes your strongest (and thus frequently most fun) options to be very infrequently available due to either saving for an emergency or their low amount of charges
Variety between runs is limited to rift items, which are either max health, circles which give a 1SP discount on everything for a specific spell school (which were also annoying since you could end up saving SP for several rifts only to not find a relevant circle), or shrines which come in a variety of very specific flavors (and are rare enough to frequently not be applicable for your build).
Since all recovery of HP and spells is tied to limited-use items that have no realistically-attainable cap, you constantly need to manage your resources. Paired with the difficulty of the game, this results in being forced to construct a plan to deal with everything in a rift before you even go in.

However, RW2 fixes all of these issues handily (full restore on rift clear, SP/rift scales from 2 to 5, new equipment system with both stackable and slotted items, additional support for builds that were lacking, etc) leaving the best parts to shine through, and tacks on a very solid handful of extra content along with a balance pass on everything in the game, and as a result I have done nothing but play Rift Wizard 2 for the last three days. Send help.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first, I was skeptical when I learned that the developer was working on a sequel to Rift Wizard (RW). It seemed to me at the time, that the best path forward would be to keep working on improving the original, but the developer explained that he wanted to do drastic changes that would heavily affect how the game plays and he did not want to alienate players that liked the way the original game works.

I really like the original game, but its determinism always led me to try the same builds and after finishing a run I would usually end up trying something similar on the next one. This kind of led me to play the game only occasionally, and it never really pulled me in like some other roguelikes had in the pass. I am glad to say that the changes in RW2 dramatically improves the game. Among other things, the addition of items and limiting the number of spell upgrades to one really changes how the game plays, there is now a lot more run to run diversity and it is very hard to stop playing because each time it is something slightly different and you just want to try one more time.

Overall, although the game is still in early access, I can definitely say that I enjoy RW2 a lot more than RW and hopefully it will only get better. In that sense, I think that the changes from the first one already show that the developer knows what he is doing and that the game is heading in the right direction.

This game is basically (IMHO) a masterpiece already.

I just want to finish this review with a heartfelt thank you to all indie developers for keeping on innovating, exploring new things, working on passion projects and ultimately for keeping the essence of gaming alive. In the time of live service games, microtransactions and other harmful practices you show the world that there is still hope.\t\t\t\t\t
