\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start by saying that I have several hundred hours in the original Goat Simulator. While my Steam profile may say I have little over 100 hours, it was a game I regularly played in offline mode and introduced to many people.

I fully expected (and was willing) to be paying for another Goat Simulator DLC.

This is not that.

This is a fully-fledged game, online multiplayer (frequently teased about being missing in the previous installment) is present. The map is expanded and built out to be a full game, in a way that you would not expect from an otherwise joke series. I LOVED goat simulator and all of its DLC. This is a game clearly made as a love letter to the original, with a map that is inspired by the locations in the original game and its DLC content, but built out to an extreme degree.

THE PROS
  • Objectives are clearly marked
  • Gameplay and movement feel smooth and polished
  • Lots of different body and equipment options for your goat
  • Driving vehicles feels smooth and intuitive
  • Map feels vast and complete
  • Map traversal is greatly improved (to compensate for much bigger map)
  • Objectives and trophies feel intuitive
  • Locations clearly reference previous game, and often feels like a high-budget version of the original in many places
  • Maintains the sense of whimsy and fun of the original

THE CONS
  • Map is almost too big, making it disappointing when something is not
  • Some buildings or rooms feel like they should be interact-able and are not
  • Sometimes set pieces and objects will reset when you get farther away, limiting the amount of chaos you can create
  • Game is almost... too polished? Some of the fun of the original was that it was a nonsense game, that would behave erratically or even crash

I highly recommend this game as a fun way to kill time, especially to play and enjoy as a party game until other people show up to an event or game night. If you loved the first game, you'll love this one. If you thought that the DLC was a bit much in the original, this is a great way middle ground, with less complication than the later DLC content.

My only real complaint is I would have preferred to have the map still be separated into different areas, if it meant that we could have more chaos from intractable objects at a time, even if it meant that the game was less stable.\t\t\t\t\t
