\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't think you'd be able to improve peak simulator, but alas Peak Simulator 3 does just that! With three times the peak of the first entry, it's sure to get your peakometers overflowing.

Coming off of the disappointment of Peak Simulator 2, often considered a "dud", Peak Simulator 3 brings itself back to tradition. Gone are the days of saving up your star tokens to afford a quazi-nuclear lithogram, now your QNLs are unlimited, and available right from the start. The Magicka system has also been overhauled, ensuring zorbles no longer impede your journey to excellency.

Peak Simulator 2 was also very controversial for its often frustrating boss fights. While famous bosses from Peak Simulator returned, such as the Fallen Foreskin or the Glizzy Gobbler, they failed to live up to many people's expectations, and new bosses such as the Giant Evil Prolapsed Anus just didn't feel like they belonged in the game. While the Anus boss returns in Peak Sim 3, it has also been reworked, and now feels right at home alongside its eleventy-seventh fraternal great-granduncle, the Anal Ass-ass-in.

New additions to Peak Sim only help improve its simulatorlike gameplay further. The Gussy Grappler, while seeming inconsequential on first look, is actually one of the best mechanics I've ever seen in a video game, allowing you to grapple that gussy with reckless abandon. Life just got a whole lot gussier.

In totality, Peak Sim 3 marks the grand return of the Peak Sim franchise to its former peakiness. With statues marked to be erected to the developers in Fecal Frontiers, Montana, it's undeniable that this game will in fact have a significant impact on the United States election this year. Godspeed, Peak Simulator, and thank you for your service. I salute you.\t\t\t\t\t

Another great game that suffered due to PC exclusivity with the Epic Games Store. The game is brimming with comedy centered play, and has a significant amount of single player content to enjoy while exploring the larger world. The multiplayer is insanely hectic, and makes for some seriously funny moments when playing with friends.

Pros:
- Lots of different goats
- A huge amount of equipment to unlock
- In-game currency generated by game completion to buy equipment / skins
- Lots of funny quests and side missions
- Everyone on the map has a detailed life

Cons:
- Epic Game Store exclusivity killed the release value of this game

Overall:
This game is genuinely fun, packed full of quality and stupid laughs. If you have friends willing to deal with the stupidity of goats, has the potential for some great multiplayer game play.

Buy it on sale.
