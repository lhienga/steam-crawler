\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis Survivor-like is a gem. For one, it has what I can only describe as..production value? The sound mixing is sound excellent. From the combat sounds to the level up sound, the all just sound professional. It feels closer to a niche game by a major company than an indie title.

The special effects around your abilities and leveling up are shiny, flashy and crisp. And add a lot of pop to the various special moves you acquire.

I know it's weird to bring those things up first, but I find those elements are too often overlooked in indie titles, especially a Vampire Survivors clone. And frankly it makes it stand out from the rest of the bunch.

Now, as far as the game itself, it has an awesome Castlevania design. In fact, tbh, you could retitle this Castlevania: Scarlet Tower and it would fit in the series. The music could come from the next Castlevania. The characters all have a Castlevania vibe, and your slaying monsters like werewolves. It doesn't just feel like they copied Castlevania either.

It feels like fans of the games decided to make a survivors-like game. And its glorious.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnlike Vampire Survivors not having vampires, Scarlet Tower does indeed have a Tower in it, So lets just get that out of the way lol. Joking aside, is this a good game though? Yes, but ill also explain what I DON'T like about it further down.

The pixel art is amazing. They really nailed the gothic aesthetic. There's currently 7 characters and 5 maps. I won't go into a lot of detail with the menus and upgrades but its your typical go on a run, obtain currencies, then upgrade your characters and stats. If you played these types of games before, you know the process. There's a fair amount of weapons for an "early access" game. Couple of them being pretty useless.
Controller support functions really well in case anyone was wondering.

NOW, Things I DON'T LIKE:

First off the weapon fusions. To accomplish this, you have to obtain 2 specific weapons and level them both to 5. Then open any chest. Once you do, there's just nothing spectacular about it. Boring actually. This method also eats up slots quickly. You only get 6 weapon slots. Accessories isn't a factor in this process like in V.S.

Another issue is the maps don't really have destinations or secret areas. Once you obtain the compass, you'll figure out quickly that the map is just an endless loop with the random events/quests just spawning somewhere near the character at set times on the clock.

Last but not least there are a lot of repeated content. You can choose a different map and yet your quests are always the same. Bosses are repeated over and over again with no "secret" bosses anywhere.

At nearly 15 hours in, I have unlocked all weapons and upgraded almost everything, unlocked a fair amount of the achievements and I'm starting to hit fatigue with the game. I still play a run or two, but not hours at a time like I did when I first purchased. If your a fan of this genre, you'll get several hours of enjoyment out of this one and at such a low cost I'm recommending it even though I feel it needs a little more time.

Let's get this out of the way.

I am extremely sceptical of this game as this seems to be the same developer as Mists of Noyah. Under the same name just minus the LLC at the end. They have the same artstyle, assets and so on. I followed Mists of Noyah for maybe a year or two?, the game ended up being mixed in reviews. So when a developer changes their name, or in this case creates a new dev studio, basically "erasing" a past game, it just seems shady as hell.

Most Vampire Survivor enthusiasts will probably like this game. I have played a fair share of bullet-hell games, all the way from the original Raiden series in the arcade cabinet and Gradius on NES. I am both old and picky as to what I like and don't like.

Onto the first impressions:
  • This game is quite good. It's fluid.
  • It has a strong design to their brand (due to their past game)
  • Controls are snappy and responsive
  • I commend the hitboxes, they are pretty much perfect, nice and tight to the character's silhouette.
  • Interesting game mechanics that I still need to spend more time into before I can comment more on it.

Here are the things I don't like:
  • Sound design, it's not terrible but it's not great either. There seems to be a lack of crunch, it sounds a bit soft and hollow. Personally I feel the crunch sound design will work well with the UI menus. So transitioning between menus or selecting power-ups.
  • The menus are a bit unresponsive. This almost contradicts the point I mentioned above. It happens when transitioning between pages or selecting/levelling-up upgrades. The power-up menu seems to be bugged or might have some translation issues, or lack of instructions.
  • Tutorial-wise, it's very basic but possibly not written in the best way. Definitely some confusion.

Small disclaimer: I do work in the UX and UI industry, so I'm not pulling strings out my butt.

Bottom line
I do recommend this game but with an asterisk.

Aside from the shadiness in the intro, this feels like a good game. Maybe the developers of the old were reformed or project management got better. I think it could be a learning experience that they had. Still doesn't excuse the name change/new studio reason. It's an odd decision.

At the time of this impression, it's only been one hour of gameplay. Considering it's an early access title, I cannot give a full review. If VS turned you off due to its graphics (to each their own), this might help scratch that itch of dopamine that you're looking for.\t\t\t\t\t
