\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDidn't work for me, felt like a worse version of Vampire Survivors (even though the visuals are infinitely more pleasing).

Gameplay-wise, I think that the weapons and characters are too imbalanced. Like, why is the dual katana, which doesn't even have an evolution, dealing WAY more damage than my 2 max leveled evolved items? And why is there a character that starts with said katana, being able to even stand immobile, while characters such as Ninn have a terrible time all around simply existing because her starting weapon pales so much in comparison?

Also, while we're at it with all these questions, why in God's name is the Elite spawn rate so low? Honestly, getting 2~3 chests per run isn't fun, specially when the weapon combination system in this game is based on weapon + weapon instead of weapon + item - kinda like Vandalier and Phieraggi, except all of them (that I know of).

These factors already make the game really underwhelming and aggravating, but the moment that specially feels like it's flipping you off intentionally is when it throws a Reaper with a massive health bar and speed which increases over time (I'm pretty sure it does, but don't quote me on that one) on the last two and a half minutes at you. This reaper doesn't instakill you as in Vampire Survivors, but get this: if it kills you, you failed the level and just wasted about 21 minutes. Are you serious at all? I mean, I didn't even make it through the first of eight difficulties of the first map in the game in two hours of playtime, and I'm a regular at this genre.

So yeah, the game looks astounding all around and derives a lot from other similar games, but the gameplay really lacked in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNice entry into the bullet heaven/survivor type games. I like the art style, character variation, and the number of stuff to buy. Unfortunately, it needs some more time in the oven to cook. It has some annoying and very glaring bugs. The most annoying being tool tips not disappearing when you take your cursor away. Others range from damage formulas being in raw code, numerous miss-numbered % per talent point errors, boss/elite enemies just freezing up, and my favorite...buggy chests. I don't know if a bug or a feature, but chests seems to spawn when they feel like it. Half the time I don't get chests from elites or even bosses. Other times 5 will drop in 2 minutes from regular enemies. If it's tied to the luck stat, which it might be, then that's just obviously bad design.

Other than the glaring bugs, balance is the only real game play issue. Characters, abilities, items, talents, etc. aren't even close to being even near equal. Oh, and that pathetic side step called a dash...it's not useless but just barely so. The games got potential and I want to see what it's like in about 6 months or so. As it stands now though, it's a 5/10.\t\t\t\t\t
