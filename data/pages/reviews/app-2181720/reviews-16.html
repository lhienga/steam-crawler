- Gorgeous pixel art with beautiful animations
- Incredible varied roguelite progression - there are offensive and defensive talents, passives, power ups, racial traits, and other additional unlocks to obtain, along with many different unlockable characters
- The runs, so far, feel quite satisfying and progression feels like its moving forward at an appropriate speed

- Unique day / night cycle with associated changes in enemy groups, additional currency drops (and like more I haven't discovered as yet)

Cons:
- Some descriptions in the upgrade menu could be better fleshed out - for example, power ups do not explain how much of a boon you will obtain until you actually upgrade the selected item. Minor detail at this time that I'm certain will be corrected.
- I honestly can't think of anything else - I've had a lot of fun so far!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA strong horde survival game that feels very similar to Vampire Survivors. Bold to price above it. However there is a lot of content here, some gaps & 'coming soon's that are a bit disappointing but the developer seems to update the game frequently. Meta-progression seems pretty good & you do start off pretty weak in the initial runs but not to the degree that you are immediately flattened, definitely compels you to return to the game particularly to progress through the 'talent' tree to discover new buffs.

As it stands of all the survival-esque games I've cluttered my library with as the genre has taken off this is currently the one I am most keeping my eye on along with Rogue: Genesia. I've found that with a lot of these games after a handful of runs you can feel pretty 'done' & not particularly interested in returning to play further. So far this one has me wanting to keep going.

Will be interesting to see where the dev takes this one, some more weapon/upgrade variety would be nice, and it could do with differentiating itself more from VS. At times it feels a little too much like a clone.\t\t\t\t\t
