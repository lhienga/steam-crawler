\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had this on my wishlist for a while but decided today that I would give it a try seeing as it was on sale a bit.
Looking at a lot of the negative reviews people seemed upset that it was grindy, and that's what I expect from this genre and I am totally ok with that, so I should love it!

So, 5/10 so far, and I have only just started but I think everything I will dislike I've already found.. If Steam had neutral I would give it that because there are already some things I see that I like, I just really dislike what I dislike about it.

In this game you play a character that walks around in an expansive map while endless hordes of monsters come at you from all sides and you kill them for experience to level up and every time you level up you get to choose an ability. You have six active slots and six passive slots.
These abilities include fireballs, icy projectiles, a holy bible that orbits you, a knife you throw that pierces enemies, holy (oops I mean sacred) water and, lightning strikes, etc.
Shortly after you start the first level you reach a point where a circle of encroaching plant monsters spawn all around you and... Wait, haven't I done this before?

This game takes wayyyyy too much from Vampire Survivors. I get that vampire survivors started an entire genre and I play just about every one of the new horde/bullet heaven games that I see, because I really like them, and I get that they will always have a lot of overlap with eachother, but this just immediately felt absurd. The abilities are virtually EXACTLY THE SAME. They almost even have the same names. The lack of creativity is staggering. There are so many ways they could have lessened this, with little to no difference in work while initially making the game.
-Instead of circling books make them swords that circle you
-Instead of fireballs make them blood projectiles or dark orbs
-Instead of holy (oops did it again) water make it sacred fire or burning oil
-instead of knives make it a pistol
Of course these are all really just cosmetic changes but at least it could lessen the effect.
I truly can not fathom why they would have so directly copied the core ability pool of vampire survivors to this degree.
On other notes...
-gathering range is very uncomfortable short, even with the power up stat maxed and the maxed upgrade passive.
-a lot of the upgrades are for very puny amounts - 3% cdr per level being the worst offender.
-There is an attack speed passive and a cooldown passive, the attack speed passive only affecting your primary weapon - why? Why not just have cooldown and have it affect everything.

Now on to the good - the reason the previous stuff bothers me so much is because I want to see more games like this but I think they should all be innovating and improving, and this game already appears to do that in several ways

-multiple screens where there are ways to power up over time available, including racial passives, stat boosts, glyphs that give bonuses, etc. Long term progression is awesome and the more different kinds are available the more gameplay people should get out of this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tScarlet Tower is an entertaining experience, a fun survivors-like game where you take control of a character and you have 24 minutes at your disposal over a day/night cycle spread over 12 minutes each, to survive the swarm of enemies coming your way and defeat the boss at the end of the wave.
Although this game brings fresh and interesting mechanics to the genre, there are several flaws that can impact your game experience.

But first let's start with the pros:
- fun maps to explore, each of them are memorable in their own right, especially Leviathan's Lair due to the presence of the oxygen stat, which encourages you to wander the map in search for oxygen bubbles, rather than stay in a single place
- interesting weapon combinations
- corruption system, it offers new and challenging ways to take on the game, progressively increasing enemy stats (however the enemy speed increase is not shown at all)
- visually pleasing, the character designs arouse your interest
- dash mechanic, a personal favourite of mine
- winter mode
- day/night cycle
- the World Eater, it's a welcome change of pace from the usual end game boss, you have to kite this boss from the beginning, careful not to run into him ;) (learnt that lesson at my own expense, although you can avoid this outcome by using Allure's Rising Charge)
- racial traits, it brings variety to the game and impacts your character choice
- runes and the enchanting system offer you more ways to be better prepared to tackle higher corruption stages
- familiars, they provide valuable buffs and aid to the player

Now it's time for the cons:
- you can only have 4 weapon fusions at max, because the starting weapon occupies a slot and you can't even fusion it with another weapon, a good solution would either be separating the main weapon from the slots meant for regular weapons, or creating possible fusions which include the starting weapon
- the lack of a speed up mechanic turns the game from a fun challenge to a grindfest, especially when you need to progress through 1-10 corruption stages as well as simply hoarding enough resources for the much valuable upgrades
- although I previously praised runes, enchanting system and the racial traits (I still do, it's a fresh and welcome feature of the game), I find the cost a bit too steep and it only adds to the grinding process
- the dice rolls have a chance to despawn right as you're about to pick them up
- if you purchase any buff/weapon from a shop, all nearby shops instantly despawn as well, which can be annoying if you tend to hoard your souls before deciding to spend them

I'm very eager to change the rating to a positive one, since I believe this game has a lot of potential and a capable dev team. I sure hope this game will become an ever more worthwhile experience in the future, I'm looking forward to the future patches, cheers!\t\t\t\t\t
