Maybe a neutral rating would be fairer but the fact that you basically have to grind the first level 8-10 times (increasing corruption levels just means more 'special' enemies cluttering up the screen ) just to unlock the next one really kills the appeal for me.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGet Vampire Survivors, instead. No, seriously; this game is the sort of game that's so into getting closer to its idol that to a disinterested outside observer it looks like it's trying to be its idol. And not in that cute, healthy, kids will be kids way. This game is trying to be Vampire Survivors to the point that not even Vampire Survivors' wife will notice when it bloodily murders Vampire Survivors, disposes of its corpse, moves into its house, and starts sleeping in its bed.

Mechanically, this is mostly just Vampire Survivors. The things that are different seem to only be different so that the claim can be made that it is different. For example, chests exist in this game, too! One of the few improvements on the formula, actually: they're not animated, which is pretty cool. But there's not actually a guarantee that you'll see even one in a game, because them dropping at all is influenced by a stat that only functions if you have the trinket that boosts it.

Another issue is the difficulty. Ignore the steam stats, I alt-tabbed out of the game to look something up at about the eighty minute mark. Which means that in eighty minutes of play I beat the boss-that-summons-death once, died to the boss-that-summons-death once, and quit at the start of night due to sheer, bloody-minded boredom (after having tabbed out for 20 minutes) once. And that netted me 71% of the game's achievements. On top of that, the game's the worst kind of tedious; the gameplay's bland and uninspired, as though we've seen it a dozen or more times before, but if you give in to the urge to yawn, something will kill you before you've recovered. Further, Elite enemies aren't visually distinct in the least, hiding in the crush of enemies and dropping AoE fields under your feet that will only land if you've decided to stop moving for some fool reason (did you forget this isn't Vampire Survivors? I don't blame you, but don't stop moving). The only enemy that is visually distinct is the boss that spawns with eight minutes left on the clock, takes ten minutes or more to kill, and fires random AoE/DoTs at you that either miss and do nothing or land and guarantee you'll take a chunk of damage. The problem is, this is the only attack the boss has, it fires rarely, hits maybe one shot in 10, and is only going to kill you if you're unlucky. So the boss taking ten minutes to kill? Is entirely because it's got more health than the combined total of all of the enemies you kill between the start of the game and death spawning and killing you; that's not difficulty, dammit. It's not difficult to knock down a house with a hammer; just boring, time-consuming, and tedious.

Finally, the game touts a 'unique' day/night cycle. ......Which has no influence on the game at all, something it accidentally lets slip if you take too long to kill the boss mentioned above. Well, that's not entirely true. See, there are two meta-currencies in this one: gold, which upgrades all the stuff worth bothering with; and 'night crystals', which are used for upgrading various passives that have effect values so infinitesimal, particularly for how expensive they are, that there's no point in bothering. So during the day, enemies occasionally drop gold and xp, and at night enemies occasionally drop night crystals and xp. Oh and the start of night triggers a spawn of enemies that fit the night aesthetic, but since they're not replaced with day aesthetic enemies when the night ends it's pretty clear this is just standard progression timing pretending to be something it isn't.

Also, that 'occasionally' up there is worth reiterating: Enemies don't have a guaranteed drop of xp gems. There's no reliable source of gold or 'night crystals'. If the RNG doesn't feel like awarding those things to you, you'll just fail to kill the boss due to xp starvation. Between that and there being more slots for equipment (six weapons and six trinkets) than it's possible to collect the XP to actually upgrade, the game feels unfair in the weirdest way. 'Behold all these things you could have, if only I felt like letting you reach them!' Except they're not things you need to beat the game.

Look, if you already have every other bleeding game in the genre and the five bucks and you wouldn't be better off just buying a couple cans of Monster, sure: pick it up and revel in finally completing your collection. But this game doesn't do hardly anything that the innumerable other games in the genre haven't already done, and most of the things it does have that they don't, there's a reason for it. If you're desperate for more of VS' gameplay and you've 100%'d it and have so many eggs bought that Poe's starting Garlic can melt every boss up to death without moving, there's a second set of a squillion of these games over on the mobile market, starting with Magic Survival (the game VS is loosely based on).

But personally I'd tend to say to avoid this game. It brings nothing new to the table, it's boring, it's tedious, and its efforts to add difficulty just double-down on the tedium.

EDIT: Also, I am well aware I didn't explicitly mention the 'racial bonus' thing or familiars: since none of the characters mention explicitly what race they are and all of the bonuses are piddling to meh and very expensive to upgrade, I didn't see the point. Familiars are worse: They've also got piddling to meh bonuses and are even more expensive to upgrade.\t\t\t\t\t
