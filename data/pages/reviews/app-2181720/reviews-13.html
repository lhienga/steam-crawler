\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVampire Survivors this is not. While it shares many obvious similarities with it, including some abilities that are basically the exact same thing with the same power, it is nowhere near the level of "Become a God and sit still" that VS is.

This is one of the harder games in this genre to be sure, and you'll need to farm out some upgrades to properly stand a chance at beating a stage. But with the ability to toggle auto-aim off and on at will, as well as a peculiar yet interesting take on enemy pathfinding, there is a satisfaction to letting enemies amass behind you and then melt the mob.

Currently there isn't heaps of content in this game compared to its peers in the genre, but it's looking very positive so far. With the prices of these games being what it is I would recommend to pick this one up if you're a fan of Vampire Survivors or other such games like it. That is, so long as you don't mind being challenged by this kind of game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tScarlet Tower may come off as one in the number of many rogue-lites but there is much to like about this game. The biggest comparison for most, would be, how well it plays versus more popular games in the genre like the insanely popular Vampire Survivors. I think overall, they feel pretty similar, if not for the slower pace of your avatar in this game. Aesthetically, the game is phenomenal, everything seems to have a glow to it and it reminds me of the eye catching aesthetics of games like Geometry Wars back in the day. I look at this game and I think that this is what a game would look like on a 16-bit system from yesteryear versus the NES look of Vampire Survivors. There are a few hang ups with this title. I wasn't completely in love with the weapons of each of the characters and the sub weapons that are available. When you max out certain power ups, they do unlock new sub weapons but nothing ever feels devastating or helpful. I was hoping to have an experience similar to Vampire Survivors, where certain power ups could pair off and cause a lot of havoc, but through several playthroughs, I just haven't found anything that I like sticking to. I also was a bit frustrated with the lack of explanations in regards to power ups, character unlock prerequisites, and in game currencies which still I have no idea how to obtain. Overall, I think the game is a great time waster, but if you already have Vampire Survivors, I'd recommend sticking with that unless you want something that looks prettier but is also half baked and a bit frustrating. 7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is kinda fun, but has some easily noticeable issues, which ruins the overall experience.
Some game breaking bugs:
-Being able to drop 2 new weapons when you only have 1 slot available, and if you try to keep both the game will only give you one, so one drop gets wasted.
- Some items still get received even if you refuse them in a dropped chest, ruining your current build in the process.
- The fusion system is both poorly explained and feels erratic. Like, the holy book (totally copied from Vampire Survivors BTW), can be fused with multiple items, and if you have 2 possible fusions when you drop a chest, you can get both fusions at once and only lose the book once. I'm not even sure that's intentional.

The mechanics are very poorly explained, there is no way to know which fusions are possible, or which item will allow for a fusion, tooltips are very often useless (e.g. ice queen summon, you buy levels but have no idea what it does), or wrong (some talents say you get a 0.3% increase per point for example, then you spend your point and understand that's not the case at all).

Overall, IMO the game feels still too early in the development to be available in early access.\t\t\t\t\t
