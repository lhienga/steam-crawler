\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReader's Note: play the Evrima beta channel. That's where 95% of the game's player-base is. Plenty of full servers. The game's actually in active development, despite what Steam is telling you; just the main channel (now called 'legacy') is being completely rebuilt from the ground up in the Evrima beta channel.

It's fun! But it's a semi-realistic, PVP-ish, survival sandbox; so it's not for everyone. You will starve, get eaten and die. A lot. If you're easily frustrated by losing a good run's built-up progress, this isn't the game for you. There isn't much character permanence. You dying is actually a core mechanic, so that other players can continue to survive off the nutrients your body provides. Talkin proteins, lipids and carbs. No, really. You get buffs based on your diet health.

And fun is what you make it, so don't expect anybody to tell you what you should do or where to go. There is no 'win' condition. You're a baby dinosaur, you have no idea where you are, you hear weird sounds, try not to get eaten.

Like gang-stalking big herbivores? They've got a dino for that. Like grazin like a cow or just headbuttin stuff? They've got a dino for that. Like being literally as tall as a blade of grass and spitting venom? They've got a dino for that. Like flyin around, eatin fish by the sea? They got a dino for that. Like just lounging in a tree and watchin all that busy nonsense from above? Got a dino for that, too.

The game's very pretty, and if you've got even a half-decent setup you'll hit ~100 FPS most of the time. The game's got all your typical alpha bugs and idiosyncrasies, servers will crash, patches will break things, but they don't outweigh the vibes the game offers. If you're looking for a specific dinosaur-related sandbox vibe, they've either got you covered, or it's on the roadmap.

The game's 10x better with friends, especially if you can start as an egg in a pack, but a solo experience is also a good time. And some dinos are built for the solo experience specifically.\t\t\t\t\t
