\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs you can tell, I love this game.

My hours are at 717.1 hours at this time of review.

However, that being said, the game has barely changed since I started playing years ago. I am only giving this game a negative review due to the protesting going on by the community to get the developers to better connect with their community.

The developers are absolutely horrible at understanding the needs and wants of the players. They keep coming up with all these ideas that no one even wants and focus on them - to just trash them. Very little real progress is made to the game over quite a large period of time. The developers have been very arrogant towards their community as well.

I always come back to the isle, but it is disappointing to see all these ideas being worked on and then just thrown away. It's a waste of their time and the player's time as it just keeps disappointing people who are actually excited for new features.

I play the isle to play as a dinosaur and to do nesting with my friends. They released a "group system" where you couldn't even find other players besides making noises. That alone made me quit playing this game because I just wanted to be able to group up with my friends without having to try to find them by sound every few seconds - if we could even find each other at all. However, i'm not sure if this is still the case or not as I haven't played since then. But they used to have a feature where you could find others by their steam profiles if you added them to a group. Basically, they took away that major feature from the players. They keep twiddling their thumbs on things that shouldn't be focused on right now.

Besides that, I do like the simulation of living the life of a wild animal. It is going to be simple and boring at times, because realistically, that is how animals live in the wild. They find mates, group up, have babies, travel, and inevitably die. That is one of the things that appeals me to the game is because of the realistic aspect of that.

The Isle fears their game isn't fun, which is not the case. People play because they like what I just mentioned above. Personally that is why I played too. If they are so scared of their game not being fun, then add more interactions between dinosaurs and their babies. Make it so people have more to do that isn't like a chore. Because of a lot of the entertainment does come from the players roleplaying. Being able to do actions such as grooming another dinosaur, adding "seasons" to where dinosaurs start mating/having eggs to add to the realism would create migrations every season and a goal for players to reproduce during a specific period, playing with another dinosaur, nuzzling your babies, carrying your babies, and such forth, will add so much to the game. Just things like that that players can use to interact with each other will give so much diversity on gameplay and story. Not to mention, more diversity in species. Even adding things like weather events, natural disasters, unexpected events (like trees falling) would also add a lot to the game, because you would never know what happens in that dinosaur's lifetime. I think the isle needs to focus on adding more dinosaurs and adding more interactions between players and natural events.\t\t\t\t\t
