\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had my moneys more than worth with The Isle over the years, and to give credit where it is due: Today it looks objectively better in terms of visuals, amazing in fact, and it is in some regards noticably more fleshed out than back in the day.

But you'd expect some more serious progress in 2024. Instead this game is at its core still what it has been since its initial release in 2015: a dinosaur showroom that is shaky at best at its very foundation in game performance, server stability, cheat protection and game balance.

The current version is - after almost 5 whopping years of development - still a seperate beta branch in an early access alpha and it is to this day dwarfed by its predecessor from 2018 in volume of content, which in itself wasn't even worthy of being called beta let alone finished. Half the dinosaurs that have been teased in the release trailer for Evrima are still nowhere near playable. Not to mention playable humans. They are teased at best once a year for a brief moment and it appears that it only serves to garner attention and sow controversy.

Content releases are mindboggingly slow nevertheless b/c devs change their creative vision over and over, throwing tried and true ideas and concepts straight out the window.

What's worse is that community feedback is disregarded and ridiculed, they mass ban community members from their official discord voicing legitimate criticism. Developers push through with changes that are objectively horrible, only to ever so slowly revert to more proven concepts, wasting even more prescious development time.

With their announcement of a redeveloping of the game way back in 2018 they promised a better game with higher quality content at a significantly faster pace, better visiuals, better performance and better communication, yet little of that came to fruition.

And let me repeat: The game is an early access alpha, and within that the main game is a seperate beta branch which you manually have to opt into. And within that branch they even run test servers. You might say: "Its a work in progress, be glad there is testing". I'm glad there is. But testing's a given in an early access alpha. Everyone is a stress tester the moment they install the game. Surrounding yourself with all those extra wip-walls is just a shield from criticism and responsibilities at this point. "It's a work in progress" or "quality over quantity" can be a legitimate excuse for only so long. If you are running in circles for almost a decade without any real progress you look like amateurs.

Hence at this current point in early 2024 I would strongly suggest to steer clear of purchasing this game. It is still lightyears away from reaching its full potential and offering you all that was promised for years upon years. It is well past its prime and if theres ever a chance of reaching a new one it is certainly not now or anywhere in the foreseeable future.\t\t\t\t\t

I have fond memories of playing the Legacy version prior to Evrima being released for play, and although I've tried it simply feels lacking despite the time they've allegedly been working on it.

So to answer the question, would I recommend this game to other players? Perhaps once upon a time I would've happily despite the issues I was aware of surrounding the development team behind the game. The concept of separating art from the artist is impossible in this situation, when part of the reason this game has any competition as a funny little dinosaur survival game is because there is no real concept of being professional or understanding how to conduct oneself when it comes to interacting with a community. I comprehend respecting the developer's intent with the product they're making. I do. But I don't have to like or enjoy the direction aspects of the game-play have been taken in and nor do I have to continue to pretend that aspects of the developers simply do not exist.

If anky is a bad animal, The Isle is being developed by a bad team that acts like a pseudo-Blizzard Entertainment under Bobby Koteck. If this is the direction they wish to take their game in, then I wish them all the best in achieving what they are setting out to do.\t\t\t\t\t
