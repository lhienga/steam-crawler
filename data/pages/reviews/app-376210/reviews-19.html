\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts difficult for me to decide if I want to recommend The Isle or not... I am going to recommend it because it is one of my favorite games and I love a lot of things about it. However, lets say my real recommendation is "I recommend this game if you're willing to deal with how hard this game is on new players and have a high tolerance to it's unforgiving nature and myriad of issues." I would not say this game is for everyone, but I wouldn't say its only for a very small specific group of people either... its more of a game for people who love dinosaurs, can handle disappointment and frustration, people who are patient, and people who do not quit easily. Lets get into it...

Starting with the bad stuff first: There is a massive learning curve and it can be very unforgiving... it is not new player friendly at all. There is no tutorial or anything to tell you how to play, and you also have to be able to take a lot of that gets thrown at you.
People in this game will not care that you are new and they do not care that you are minding your own business and not looking to fight, people will kill you just to kill you... and sometimes this means you just wasted several hours of your life growing a dinosaur to adulthood (if you manage to get that far). Sometimes it means losing your pack of friends you've met along the way, or that you now have to waste x amount of minutes trying to A) get food as a baby dino (often very difficult) and B) get back to where you were before you died, which may be a big walk if your dino's closest spawn point still isn't close enough (there are only some spawn points available depending on the dino you decide to play, so if you want to get somewhere that you cannot spawn in then you're going to have to travel there manually). Sometimes it means you just got killed by a member of your own species while your guard was down for no other reason than they think it'd be funny to kill you. I love this game and know how it works, sometimes I still become upset and have to log off for awhile. You have to really want to play this game to get anywhere. Other players have strong opinions over how you should play the game and can become quite mean if you play the game in a way that they think isn't very fun or ruins the game, even if its virtually harmless. There are also issues with hackers and cheaters, and oftentimes nothing is done about them. While the map is very big, players tend to hang around "hot spots" which end up being densely populated, leaving the rest of the map sometimes scarce of other players.
The development team also don't seem to respond well to criticism or backlash and have made it clear that they are making the game they want and not the game their userbase wants. Because of this, sometimes highly disliked changes are made despite userbase backlash. This may be a dealbreaker for some people. There are also still a lot of bugs. Sometimes you fall through the map or get stuck somewhere (and it is unlikely that a moderator will help you out before you die, regardless of if you're playing on an official server or an unofficial server). I have to relog often to fix bugs that prevent my dinosaur from eating and drinking. The game is still in early access... after a very very long time, but still early access nonetheless I guess... Progress on the game moves slowly. The dev team also seems to focus on adding new features rather than improve or fix old features. It feels like they'll stop focusing on things that are "good enough" but not necessarily good or perfect.
Some dinosaurs have really poor night vision, which makes it very difficult and often impossible to play during the night cycle which lasts for a bit too long in my opinion on official servers. You can only speak in-game with players of your same species, if you want to communicate with other dinos you have to use dino calls and behavior to get a point across to them (I personally like and prefer only having chat for members of your species, but I'm including it here because a lot of people dislike this change they made), however they do have plans to reintroduce global chat as an optional thing for server owners. There are also some decently bad performance issues. It did get a little better with a recent update/bug fix but sometimes you still drop frames like crazy or start rubberbanding for no reason (and more frustratingly, this sometimes makes it hard to fight and may end in your dino dying). I think it could be optimized better, but I honestly don't know a lot about that kinda thing. The health bar could be improved too, at the moment its pretty ambiguous and sometimes I'm closer to death than I realize (which ends up with me thinking I can take another hit when I actually can't).

Onto the good stuff: The graphics are wonderful, I absolutely adore them and take loads of screenshots. It is a very pretty game. I also like the sound design, the dinosaurs all sound so cool and the ambient noise is very immersive (excluding auditory bugs). The dinosaurs have really cool animations and attacks as well, they have awesome models. Also if you like customization, my friends and I love playing what we call "dinosaur dress up" where you can create and save multiple different skins for each dino. The dino creator has lots of fun colors without going overboard or breaking immersion with really bright unnatural colors (some dinosaurs do have access to these colors if it suits their species though). The camera is 3rd person and reminiscent of WolfQuest, which I love. I really enjoy seeing my dinosaur as I'm playing. The map is also HUGE and very pretty in my opinion, there is lots to see and explore (and lots of places to hide).
I've met lots of friends and fun people while playing too. Being able to socialize or just chill with people who are my same species, or even who aren't my same species (because you can't talk to them in chat), is fun and relaxing. I enjoy using this game as a medium to meet and talk to people. I love how unique the dinosaurs are from each other, I love the decent range of play styles you can learn and try out by playing a dino you haven't played before. The nesting function is also incredibly fun. If you manage to find another adult of your species who is the opposite sex, you can lay and maintain eggs until they hatch! And the babies (other players who nest in) look like both of you! It mixes your genetics, which is so cool! It also can be insanely fun and rewarding (or heartbreaking if your dino ends up dying) to hunt other players, especially as a pack, and extra especially if you and your pack are in a Discord call together.
I love the variety of food you can eat (although I do think it could be tweaked for some carnivores) and you can even eat AI creatures like boar and deer if you can't or don't want to to kill other players and your dino doesn't eat plants. I think having to worry about your food, water, health, and stamina is a good amount of things to worry about without being overwhelming or taxing to deal with (some games, especially survival sims, give you way too many things to manage in my opinion).

Sorry if this review is a wreck and all over the place. In short, there are a lot of issues with this game and a lot of things that could be improved upon, but theres also a lot about it that I find charming and that I personally enjoy. Theres a lot of stuff I'm looking forward to, but cautiously. Sometimes some of the devs' ideas never come to fruition or they add things that I would have personally preferred they hadn't (although a lot of changes they make tend to be controversial within the community). My recommendation is purchase at your own risk.\t\t\t\t\t
