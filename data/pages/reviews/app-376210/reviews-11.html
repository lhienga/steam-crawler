\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Isle is low-key MMO that exists in the niche genre of Openworld Survival MMO. And while it is harassed with mixed reviews and slow updates. I can say I prefer The Isle over its relatives such as Beasts of Bermuda or Day of Dragons. I would say this is indeed the apex out of its genre. With the community having some relaxed crowds and a particular emphasis on patience. This game remains delightfully low key and delightful. And with the high ambitions this game has and the exciting changes in updates I can say this game keeps me waiting and wanting for more! Here is why:

The Gameplay
__________________________________________________________________________________
The game throws you into the harsh world of kill or be killed. The primal threat of other players is a real thing. The hunt for food, mates (which I will cover in the community section) and the right places to migrate to makes this game a challenge of patience and awareness. In true MMO fashion the game does allow some room for manipulation and strategy. What you eat becomes your diet, a good diet offers many rewards for continuing the diet such as speed, energy, and growth speed. The progression in the game is how much you cultivate your cute little dino into an adult. Certain species have different times. But the long the growth time typically is the stronger it tends to be as a FG or 'Full Grown'. Although death remains permanent but so is the circle of life in The Isle. Hunt, kill, survive, roll down a cliff, eat the wrong thing, mate, and die! The gameplay is difficult but simple allowing you the player to take things easy or go all in depending on the area you wish to make your stomping grounds. So if your someone like me that wants to wind down after a long day at work where you just want to roam and take it slow this is a great lite-mmo. So long as you know what your doing!

Difficulty
__________________________________________________________________________________

The biggest challenge in this game is knowing where food is. The game will NEVER hold your hand in any regard and its up for you to learn how to forage and hunt, where is the water, where to hide, when to rest, what to eat and when is it SAFE to drink!

I HIGHLY recommend playing the game with a map to know where the food is best to be found. Typically though it is plains that often have the highest yield for players and food! This game is feast or famine and it can require third party knowledge to help fresh players to find their way. Or even hatch into a family or meet another dino to show you the ropes. THis game is alll about the growth.

Social
__________________________________________________________________________________

While this game demands patience and challenges you all the time the one nicer quality of this game is the community. You can meet other friendly herbivores or players of your OWN species and talk to them. And while not all players are friendly and pvp is a very REAL and deadly aspect. It is wholesome to find the good players in the community. If you choose to mate, it only consists of a cute little dance and then you lay eggs! These eggs allow new players to be born under a team. Essentially allowing you to make your own 'faction' or 'clan' as it were. This aspect establishes the fun and charm in the community fostering good spirited content!

Versions
__________________________________________________________________________________

The game consists of two different settings. The Classic (which is default) or the updated beta called Envrima ( which can be accessed by adjusting the game in your steam library!) I myself am an envrima player. Enjoying the physics and updated identity for the game. Both are good!

Summary
__________________________________________________________________________________
This game is an olllld one and still in the process of trying to become a dream! But dont let the mixed reviews fool you. There is still alot of love and memories to be made in this one. and if you looking for an MMO thats LITE on the grind and dedication this one is a good choice to kick your feet up on! This game HAS some rough spots but its truly the casual charm, rough fights, and the awesome people that you find in the community that give this game a BIG YES from me!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has potential despite having such a small team. Out of all of the dinosaur games available, this one is by far the best if you're able to run it. It has the best combat compared to others, ( As in other survival dino games you could be a rex and get assridden by 12 raptors and die without being able to hit them. ), whereas in the isle you are actually able to put up a fight no matter what you're playing as.
When it comes to visuals, the game exceeds most. The models are well done, well animated, and aren't stocky. They move with energy and it makes the game feel alive.

The Isle however has a long way to go before it should be considered a full game. It is lacking dinosaurs as the devs take their time making each creature appealing rather than just making the model and sending it in with no niche. There aren't many things to do other than grow and survive the world.
The community is however extremely toxic & one of the worst communities ive seen. They constantly talk everything and everyone, constantly complain over the most mundane things, and think the game should revolve around them and everything they want. If you're looking for a visually appealing and combat driven game, play the isle. If you want realism and a ok community, play Path of Titans. If you want to have fun with a.. community but bad graphics, play Path of- I mean Beasts of titans-- I mean beasts of burmuda.\t\t\t\t\t
