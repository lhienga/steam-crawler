\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Evrima player here, so im only reviewing the newest version, not Legacy.)
Yes, the game needs alot of development, thats totally right. There are still major bugs around and especially the performance issues are making it hard to enjoy at some moments. In general, it works fine for me but my Pc is a rather good one. Still, if i am at a crowded place, it will lag to the point that fights are not winneable.
Some friends that i would've loved to play with, couldn't play the game and therefore wasted their money on it because of the performance issues, even tho their computers are decent ones too (they usually ended up with 4 fps).

But
Most of the time I enjoy this game alot and it is addicting. The graphics, thoughts behind mechanics, the really nice big open world map, all the dinosaur choices. It is really fun and i personally never get bored of the game.
You can play it alone, with one or several friends or with strangers you meet. This and the fact that every playable creature is so unique in mechanics, theres big differences in playstyles you can choose from. I think everyone will find something they like to play and do in The Isle.
Trying to survive in this realistic game can be a challenge, but thats what its about and thats what makes it so interesting.

Overall, i would say it's a typical early acess situation. Great game but unfinished content and bugs. It's all part of the journey. Some people are patient and accept the few issues and some people are frustrated by it. But honestly i dont think theres THAT many issues as long as your Pc can handle the performance.
Definitely watch some videos on Youtube before buying this game tho.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game after Evrima had come out and have recently enough passed 1000 hours. I went back and tried Legacy later but I don't have nostalgia goggles for it, so I can see it for the weird pond sitting chat simulator it is. Legacy is just tail riding with what feels like the same dino, in a different skin.

Evrima had a lot more going for it. I had to join the discord because of the lack of global chat, but it's barely moderated by volunteers and so many people get banned unjustly. I literally saw someone get banned for 'propagating misinformation' by letting a player know they would probably die vs receive an unstuck by an admin. (which is true) There are cheathackers galore and no real support from devs. The feedback channel is decorative at best and a banning channel at worst. If you fall under the map, get stuck in a hole, lose your dino to a hacker; you will not receive support from anyone. If you're new and dislike what might seem like rulebreaking behavior in game and speak about it in the discord you'll get bullied. (there are no rules, probably because someone would have to do the work of enforcing them)

The game also doesn't teach you in itself. You HAVE to learn from people that know. There is literally nowhere, not a wiki, NOWHERE that tells you how to play certain dinos, what abilities they have or what keys activate them. The game now sets players on automatic alt attacks which many don't even realize. So unless you're lucky enough to find someone nice to explain to you in the discord or even rarer in the game, you're basically SOL.

I play this game despite it's many flaws for these wonderful glimmers I see sometimes while playing or interacting with friends, but I can't in good conscience recommend it. If I didn't already have a friend group in this game, I don't know how I'd manage anything in it.\t\t\t\t\t
