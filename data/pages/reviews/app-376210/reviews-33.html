\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHowdy! So some of you that see this may know me and know my community. I own Isla Antigua, a realism server on Evrima. I have had a realism server on this game for a little over 3 years now (This past November/December was our 3 year anniversary), Antigua has been on Evrima for 2 years and before that we were on Legacy for about a year. However, I personally have owned this game since June of 2019. I played on multiple servers for a year or so before I decided to make a server and community where a lot of us could come together to get away from the two extremes that are 1. the people that love Dondi and love the game, and 2. the people that hate Dondi and hate the game. This game was the first game I bought when I got my PC after playing on Xbox, and I had my eye on it because of content creators like Anthomnia and TheGamingBeaver.

The Isle: Legacy and Evrima
So, anyone that has been affiliated with the game for a fair amount of time knows that there are two branches on this game: Legacy and Evrima. Legacy is what you see on the store page, a game with the functioning core mechanics that you would expect to see from a game such as this. Water and food can always be found, your stamina and combat mechanics are generally the same across multiple species, your growth is pretty linear, etc. Then, you have Evrima which is HEAVILY in development with things like food, combat, and growth all being dependent on different factors such as diet and that diet being affected by what location you're in. There are two separate branches you can play on for Evrima - Spiro or Gateway. Spiro was the first version of "Evrima" and Gateway is supposed to be what the developers want the game to be. Spiro has our officially released species and then your "functioning" mechanics from Legacy. Gateway is essentially a "test branch" where you're getting the newly released mechanics, features, and dinosaurs.

The Positives for this game
  • Evrima is visually STUNNING. There isn't a game as visually pleasing as this game is in its genre right now.
  • Evrima has some pretty accurate or semi-accurate combat mechanics and collision. One of my biggest gripes with games like Path of Titans and Beasts of Bermuda is their combat and how it feels almost "cartoony".
  • The vision and goal for this game is ambitious, with the developers shooting for mechanics and features that haven't been added into a game of this genre before. That in and of itself peaks interest and curiosity within the player/consumer, and makes you want to investigate if not go ahead and buy the game to support its ongoing development.
  • The atmosphere when you are in game and playing is immersive. Evrima specifically lives up to the categorization of this game as a "Survival Horror Game" with lighting and environment features that really put you in the moment when you're hunting or being hunted.

The negatives for this game
  • Evrima is NOT optimized in the slightest. Like, at all. I'm sure we've all bumped into some games that were graphically demanding and whatnot, but this game out of everything I have played takes the cake. I play this game at about 60-80 FPS with my graphics on Medium-High, with the highest setting being Cinematic. When Avatar: Frontiers of Pandora released, I was able to run the game on max graphics and still touch 100 FPS on average.
  • Evrima will crash your computer consistently. Whether it's an issue with the anti-cheat or with the connection to official servers or even community servers, it is almost a guarantee that you will crash at least once per play session regardless of what you do.
  • None of the branches receive "active" development/updates. To start on this one, I define "active development/updates" by making announcements to your community about ACTUAL development, releasing patches, and then obviously releasing content additions or updates. Ideally, patches/announcements would happen monthly if not bi-monthly, and content additions would take something longer like 3-4 months. You will be lucky to see this game add significant content in a timely fashion and you'll be even more lucky to witness the development team issue a patch that doesn't break the rest of the features of the game.
  • Developers do not care about your opinions, they do not care about what you believe should be added to the game, and they have 0 intentions of adding anything we suggest to them unless by the grace of some God or cosmic force you convince them that your idea is a good idea. To quote KissenKitten - the main voice for the development team, essentially the person you see interacting with and dealing with the public most often - "By all means let us know if you don't like what we cook but the kitchen and the stove is for the dev team to use. You can't go to a restaurant and demand they serve the type of food you want just because you came in with 50 people that agree with you. If they don't serve BBQ, they don't serve BBQ." But I've personally never been to a restaurant where the kitchen staff refused to cook, only gave you a quarter of the promised portion of food, and then never obliged by anyone's food allergies.
  • This game genuinely isn't fun to play anymore. As you can see, I have almost 2,500 hours in this game. I am a stress tester for the game, and when Gateway was releasing/set to be released, I was excited. The main thought was that they were really going to be emphasizing survival and taking care of your dinosaur so that you don't mess up your playtime, and as someone who owns a realism server, that is the best thing I can hope for to come from the game. However, Gateway being as new as it is with as many issues as it has, it is nearly impossible to travel and keep your dinosaur fed. The last time I played this game was 2 months ago, and I spent 5 hours starving and running around walking to different migration zones - since migration zones currently do not function as intended - attempting to eat and fill up. Ultimately I died, as I had lost all of my diet hexes and received an INSANE debuff from being "malnourished" that the moment I was attacked, I died.
  • Developers - specifically Dondi - will demean and degrade you for your opinions, tell you your opinion is unimportant or isn't valued, and based off of the response to the community push back about the camera changes, they will ban you for a differing opinion if you aren't super careful about your tone. And even after all of the recent community push back, KissenKitten has confirmed that camera changes are coming to the game regardless of what people say or want.

Conclusion
Here's where I'll get honest and opinionated: I've uninstalled The Isle and do not plan to reinstall it, if I could refund it I would. Over the last 6 months to a year, people that play video games have received some of the BEST gaming titles we've seen in years. Baldur's Gate 3, Helldivers 2, Palworld, Tears of the Kingdom, Alan Wake, and more. The games that came from "small indie companies" have had some of the most kind and open developers that have changed my standards on gaming and gaming companies. The Larian Studio developers being one of them. Baldur's Gate 3 was in early access from October of 2020 until August of 2023. It was in development for 6 years in total. The Isle has been in "early access" since 2015. The game has been out for almost TEN YEARS and we still don't have anything, really. The game is no further now than it was in 2015, with incomplete dinosaurs, maps, and developers that don't care what you think. I would not recommend purchasing this game.

Edit 3/3/2024: Shortened some things so I can say this, there are 71,506 reviews on steam. If we assume each person has bought one copy of the game, the minimum amount of money Dondi could have made is $1,430,120 and we still don't have a finished game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is 9 years into development and there's very little to show for it.

It runs terribly. Hard stop.

It's littered with pitfalls (both literally and figuratively) that you as a player will invariably become a victim and lose access to play the game for up to months at a time. Literally, as in actual holes in the map where you will get stuck and cannot do anything (there is no !unstuck, there is no suicide button. you just have to sit there for 55 minutes waiting to starve out so you can die and start again). Figuratively, as in the game is constantly breaking such that entire groups of players can't get it to launch for months at a time.

There is less than 30% of the advertised roster of playable dinosaurs actually available. There are no humans. The development pace is a joke. The devs constantly talk about how the next big milestone will be the one that will open the door to an expedited development pace, but then after months/years they finally arrive at that milestone and the goalposts just shift to whatever the next thing is.

The entire dev team is constantly getting distracted from forward progress to make overhauls to things nobody's asked for (stamina system, diet system, growth system, the camera, etc...). Moreover the "overhauls" they come up every time are unanimously ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ every time and leave the game in a worse place than had they just left it alone and focused on making forward progress instead.

The game is content-averse. You spawn, fill up your stomach, afk in a bush for 20-35 minutes, then eat and afk some more until you're full grown (which, mind you, takes anywhere from 1-6hrs). If you're caught out by another player while you're little, you just die. Once you're full grown, you meander around aimlessly because there's nothing to do once you're an adult other than just maintain your hunger.

It's bad fellas. Try Path of Titans instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wish I could recommend this game. I'm an absolute dinosaur aficionado, and between this account and others, I have well over 1000 hours in this game. I have countless memories and many friends I've made through this game, and there is genuinely something special about this game. Gameplay can be tense, exhilarating, and terrifying, whether you're a juvenile scurrying amongst the grasses to avoid predators, a daring carnivore darting between the defenders of a herd to target their young, or a massive apex herbivore standing your ground against a pack.

No competitor NAILS dinosaur survival like The Isle does. PoT is far more of a casual and MMOesque experience, and any other rival is hardly worth mentioning. Sound design, animations, mechanics, The Isle *should* be head and shoulders above anything else in the genre. But it just isn't.

Say what you will about Early Access games, but I don't think that tag holds much meaning if a game has been out for nearly a decade. The Isle is fraught with development hang-ups, hostile communication with its playerbase, and is rife with drama at every turn. Development of the original game ceased and necessitated a recoding of the game after the lead programmer came into conflict with the creator of the game, resulting in nearly 2 years without updates. And when the update finally came out, it was a buggy, unoptimized mess, going from a roster of dozens down to just 2 playable dinosaurs. (Although admittedly with far more mechanics and features, to give credit.)

It's been a slow slog back up to feature parity in Evrima (the internal name of the recode), and for what it's worth, it's even better than the original! The game has tremendously more to do, with revamped and all new systems such as nesting/skins/weather/migrations/diets and many more. But all the while, those same issues keep cropping up. The lead developer, Dondi, is well known for abusive language and attacking members of the community, and even took a step back from being the public face of the game for a few years due to continuous and overwhelming negative feedback. When content creators have made critiques of the game, the devs have gone on livestream and insulted them while watching their videos. There is a constant sense that most (with some exceptions) of the developers have a distaste for the community at large, something best documented in the project lead, producer, and lead animator. This is all without mentioning a former developer being revealed to have engaged in misconduct with a minor, which was defended by the dev team for weeks until more evidence came to light and the developer in question left themselves.

The impetus for this negative review - despite how much I *ADORE* what this game is and can be - is the latest in a longstanding tradition of The Isle developers attacking their own community. As of recently, the devs began work on a new camera system, going from an open, 3rd person wide orbit of your character, to a pseudo-1st person view, essentially giving the player a POV that is intended to replicate what the animal itself would be able to see. This got a large amount of negative feedback - that this devalues the skin system because you can't see your character as well, that this would make shorter playables unfun due to their cameras constantly being obscured by vegetation - and the developer's response was not to assure their community that they were looking into these issues. Instead, any dissent on the public Discord was met with bans for 'misinformation'. Dozens if not hundreds of users were cut off from development updates and a sizable portion of the community without warning, with many reporting that they were instantly banned after sending a message with the words "first person" in it.

This level of toxicity and abuse cannot be made up for with cool mechanics and new dinosaurs. For all that The Isle should be the pinnacle of dinosaur survival games, I cannot with a good conscience say that it's worth investing in.\t\t\t\t\t
