\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[This is for Evrima version, the new version of the game that the developers are focused on]
The isle is the most polished and (in my opinion) the harshest of the main Dinosaur simulator games. AI prey such as goats, boars,fish, etc. can sometimes be hard to come by and there will be situations where you're just unlucky right from the start and eventually starve to death or get eaten by something due to breaking a leg or running out of stamina. It's brutal.

However, if you're able to get past this, once you've mastered your diet and found your footing, The isle can be extremely fun- with a (admittedly limited) roster of dinosaurs that all have their unique niches and abilities that add replay ability and caters to a lot of different play styles. Want to fly around the map and fish? play ptera. Want to work with a large pack and use the night to apply venom to your prey. Play troodon. Or maybe you want to be a hulking mass of spikes and join a herd? Be a stego. The point is that even though there are a limited amount of dinosaurs to play- they are all different enough fundamentally to offer a wide range of ways to play and this can not only be addicting but quite fun.

Of course this game is no way near perfect- with common glitches, bugs and crashes- and you'll be needing an impressive PC to run all this, as well as problems with AI. For beginners, this game will be quite hard to understand as well, Why did the other croc eat me? How do i find other people? with a lot of abilities being unexplained. But I feel this adds to the brutality and the sense of achievement once you've mastered these- such as flying, fishing, climbing a tree or knowing where you are on the map without looking at a map.

Speaking of which, the map. Gateway (the newest map at this time of writing) is filled with swamps, man made structures, rivers, jungles, large lakes, etc. It's quite fun just exploring for a little bit if you're starting out- and there's enough obvious structures (or natural formations) to quickly learn where about in the map you are once you've played a dozen hours.

As for the future of the game- I'm not sure. The development has always been a bit rocky (to say the least) with The isle, but it has never been dead, with bustling servers and an active community making the wait for slow updates not as painful. Regardless, with the current state of the game, If you are into dinosaur survival games- or even survival games that are brutal (and you are prepared to do trial and error or AFK grow ( Sitting in a bush away from everything and slowly growing), I reccomend The isle and investing some time into it. Who knows, you may become a leader of a stego herd or a carnotosaurs that ravages the plains. Try it out.

P.S- I couldn't talk about other aspects such as combat, nesting, night vision, etc. For the most part, combat is one of the most engaging things in The Isle, although sometimes clunky, it offers an edge when you're fighting to save up to 4 hours of progress. Night vision as a herbivore ( or even a small carnivore) turns The isle into a horror game, with noises and glowing eyes out in the darkness and nesting is okay.. I guess- there is no inheritance system or real incentive but it's there and it offers some entertainment which is a start.\t\t\t\t\t
