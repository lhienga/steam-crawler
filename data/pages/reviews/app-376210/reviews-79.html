\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would like to emphasize that I am not someone who just buys a game at first glance or because it looks good or neat. I normally wait to look at the reviews and watch the in-game footage and playthroughs. So when I first heard about the Isle, I was excited to see what this game had to offer and have been looking forward to playing it ever since the first trailer. After a few years of waiting, and one Steam Sale later, I finally bought the game and installed it.

This game turned out to be Half Baked at best. A number of features were non-functional and the presentation was extremely confusing. Creating or joining an existing game Lobby was extremely frustrating. I too suffered from the "black-screen-of-audible-noise". This was before I discovered the version that I was presented with was not the playable version. Why would the developers even make this "test" version available?

After switching it to the correct version, I ended up playing three separate game sessions. All three had lobbies that were considered relative full with players. For playthrough 1 and 2 I played as a carnivore. Both sessions ended up with me dying due to starvation for being unable to find any prey to eat. That being either Bot-Dinos or in-game players. My third playthrough I explored a good chunk of the map and still was unable to find any players, only encountering a single wild boar. It was only towards the end of this run when I decided to log off was I finally attacked by (and survived) a carnivore I am uncertain if it was a player or a bot-dino.

I still have high hopes for this fascinating game in the foreseeable future, however it desperately needs to properly finish cooking. As of this time of writing this critical review, I cannot in good conscious earnestly recommend The Isle in its current state. I sincerely hope this independent review humbly offers a little insight into the dreadful troubles that I unfortunately had.\t\t\t\t\t
