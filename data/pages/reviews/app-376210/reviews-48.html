\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHello!
I would strongly recommend this game for anyone who likes dinosaurs but also survival games. To describe how much I like this game and how good I think it is I will give you the first long playthrough that I had as a female Hypsilophodon (a bird like herbivor dinosaur).

I started of as an adult (compare to most other dinos the Hypsi start as an adult and can only be played as a young dino from hatching) and roamed around the middle of the map occasionally calling out for other Hypsis that might be in the area. Hypsis are rather rare since most players prefer the bigger dinos or the carnosaurs and I did not expect to find another Hypsi on this playthrough. Usually I just roam around following the migrations of herbivors and try to find good potential nesting spots/safe spots for the future.

To my amazement I suddenly heard another Hypsi call and we immediately began searching for eachother on the hillside. I finally found him (a male Hypsi) named Xero and we both got super excited. In Isle Evrima you can only write chat to other members of the same species so as a hypsi, usually being alone, you do not have anyone else to really talk to. Because of that we both were superhappy to find eachother and we decided to travel to the next migration zone together. After the nights travel we came to a big swamp and we talked about what to do since dangerous crocodile dinos are usually in the bigger water areas. We decided to go around and after a bit of swimming/running we safely got to the other side of the swamp and also found our prefered food (all dinos have different food preferences and finding the right ones are key to good survival). I talked about my plans to set up a nest at a good location and asked Xero (the name of the male Hypsi player) if he would be interested in making a little family. He said yes and I was overjoyed since most players are not interested in nesting (it is hard and you have to protect the young ones when they are born). I lead the way to a good big rock a bit from the swamp where it was easy for us as hypsis to access but harder for other dinos (hypsis can jump very high). After watching the view in a glorious dawn (this game is truly stunning in the environmental setting and scenery) we courted eachother and made a nest. The nests have to be built so we had to collect sticks to create it and once it was done it was truly magnificient.

As the female I laid the eggs and then we decided how many we wanted to hatch, all communication was done in written chat in form of bird sounds from our dinos making the experience very immersive. We basically roleplayed a dinosaur family making ready for little babies, and decided on two eggs to hatch to start with. After warming the eggs by incubating them (laying on them) it was finally time. One male and one female was born and our days revolved around first feeding them (regurgitating food to them) in the nest and later escorting/guarding them from predators while we as a family searched for food and water around the nestcliff. Both of the other players really seemed to enjoy the roleplaying aspect and the feeling of being a pack/herd/family was growing on us all.

But great disaster struck. One morning after me and Xero returned from our feeding round the female hypsi, our daughter, had by accident fallen of the cliff and died in the fall. We could all see her dead body and as a family we mourned. Me and Xero had watched these two young hypsis go from newborn chicks to adult hypsis, beautiful and strong, and then by a mere bad luck one of them was now dead. Since we now had 3 adults in our family we decided to hatch another egg and a new chick was born. The youngest one was more needy and had a hard time finding us and would take some risks in swimming in the swamp and going of on small adventures by himself but he always came back to the nest.

After some time we realized that the food at the migration zone started to run out (herbivors need to migrate according to specific dynamic zones in order to find their prefered food). We had a familymeeting and decided to migrate from the old nest towards the new migration zone. I´m not gonna lie, it was a bit painful leaving the nest that me and Xero had spent so much time building and in one way it felt a bit like leaving our home. But we all knew that if we as a group were gonna survive we would have to find more and better food for all of us otherwise we would start to starve sooner or later.

The journey would be long and we traveled along the coast for a while without any encounters with predators or dangers. Suddenly the youngest one started behaving weird and maybe he did not want to play with us anymore since he, after some mean words, left and swam out into the ocean to drown in the salty water. It was sad to lose one of the family and now we were down to 3 again.

Traveling through the forest and jungle up the hill we came upon the spot where me and Xero first met and this was really a full circle moment for me. It was truly amazing how we together had created this epic story of survival in this world of dinosaurs and I have to say that I it takes a lot for a game to really move me to the core. It wa something about the dangerous landscape and predators, the will and determination of the family and us all working together to just have a good time gaming, even though we could only make birdsounds in chat to eachother. In the end we finally reached the new migration zone and our last surviving son started calling out for a potential mate in the junglegrowth.

All I can say is that The Isle Evrima has clearly touched my heart by the storytelling that it has the potential to create and I cannot recommend this enough.\t\t\t\t\t
