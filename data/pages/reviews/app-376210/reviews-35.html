\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as I'd like to love this game, I can't endorse it.

It is the most beautiful Dino game I've played, with great animations.
Combat is also great compared to other games of the genre, which mostly revolve around tail riding.
Dinosaurs are unique, with everyone having their own special playstyle.

But for all the great visuals and potential for a great game, the fact remains, it's been in beta since 2015.
Changes keep getting implemented with complete disregard for player opinions.

First off, there's no tutorial or basic guidelines on how the game works, what abilities do. How much dmg you can do or take. Or even how to navigate and find other players. New players are forced to look these things up on youtube or die and lose all progress a 100 times over trying to figure it all out.

The map is way too big for 100 players. If you're a carnivore, you need to eat other players or random, but unreliable npc spawns. But most players will be centered at one or two hot spots on the map, so if you wander off too far from these, you're likely to starve to death.

Night time is very long during which, you have to activate night vision to be able to see anything. Some dinos like Pteranodon have very poor night vision and are basically forced to land on the ground somewhere safe and wait the night out (roughly 40 min), during which they are likely to starve or thirst to death if not already on a full stomach. So it's better to just log out of the game during night time if you're playing a Dino with bad night vision.

Apart from night time, there's thick fog, random storms which occur far too often and turn daytime into night literally, so again, you can't see a thing during much of the day.

The server menu is the worst I've ever seen. On a rare occasion it will show you what dinosaur you have on a server, but most of the time it wont, so better keep a pen and paper next to your keyboard if you play on a lot of servers, because you can only have one Dino per server. And good luck connecting.
The servers may read 90/100 players, but when you click on it it will say "server is full" and you'll be booted to the main menu, forced to repeat this for 5 or even 40 minutes, depending on if they just released a new dinosaur.

Then there are random crashes or disconnects. I can't count how often I've started to eat a corpse and crash to desktop. Keep in mind, that your dino stays in game for several minutes, 15 some say, unless you safelog. So when you eventually manage to get back in game, someone might have killed you or like when I started eating, my dino kept eating until I managed to get back, during which it had gotten vomit sickness and I lost all of my diet.

Also keep in mind that some Dinos can take 6 hours to grow and when you're dead, you're dead, start over.

And there are random hackers running around killing everyone.

And finally, the nail in the coffin, are the devs who's vision of the game is not on the same planet as the player base.
If you dare to criticise them even on the slightest game changes, they will ban you on discord and claim it's for misinformation.

All in all it is a beautiful game with great potential, but it is being held back by the implementation of more and more frustrating and unwanted game mechanics which each are filled with bugs that just keep piling up.\t\t\t\t\t
