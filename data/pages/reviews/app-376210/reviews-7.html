►\tmechanics ( flying, water ambushing, charge ambushing, hallucinations, bile mechanic, pouncing and latching, bleed severity, diets, migration )
►\tmap visuals ( interesting biomes )
►\taudio ( the world feels alive and active listening is a must )

Cons:
►\tdistribution of visibly playable day/stormless times ( game is unplayable for most of its day cycle )
►\tmap use ( illogical spawn points causing unnecessary difficulty, hotspot development due to force water scarcity, lack of migration use, lack of environmental food distribution )
►\tgrowth consideration (illogical spawn points resulting in easy camping of fresh spawns, disrespecting time sink for some actor types' growth times )
►\tAI ( detection of 'stealthed' actors at unreasonable distances, over-tuned aggression tables )
►\tbalance for the good mechanics for actors ( cannot see while flying because of visibly playable time being so limited, water ambushing depends on player interaction as environmental interaction is impossible due to bad AI, over tuned hallucination specs, pouncing and latching frustrations due to low success chance )
►\tbalance of environmental mechanic for diets ( there is one 'meta' diet setup, encourages and rewards negative play styles in some dino communities who can eat their own kind )
►\tdistribution of good mechanics ( the amount of dinos that are uninspired in their gameplay loops are more than double the dinos that have engaging gameplay mechanics )
►\tmap playability ( many places where a player may lose all their progress from glitches resulting in their death )
►\taudio problems ( consistent audio failure in a game where audio cues are paramount? priority please?? )


The spirit of The Isle is one that is absolutely worth getting excited about, but the current and past execution of that vision more often than not results in frustration. The Isle is a fun game for a short while, but the longtime retention of players will depend on the aggravation from poorly-fulfilled mechanics and long moments where the game itself is actively countering your ability to play it somehow overshadowing a player's desire to continue engaging with it.\t\t\t\t\t
