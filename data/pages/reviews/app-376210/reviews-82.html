\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI may not have crazy hours in the game, but I've played enough to make an honest review of it.

So there's 3 branches of this game that you can play. Legacy (the default branch), EVRIMA, and Spiro (the other two can be selected in the 'betas' tab). The legacy version is good, but there seem to be a great number of Chinese servers and few English servers, so not the best for my fellow English speakers. The gameplay is good enough, and as expected for its version. Spiro I have not played yet, and do not intend to as I am very content with EVRIMA. Yes, this version is still in Alpha stage and yes, it has numerous bugs, including one that (at the time of review) causes the servers to crash and kick everyone every now and then. Some regions of servers do it more than others. There was a recent update that may have fixed this but I have not checked.

EVRIMA has noticeably better gameplay than legacy in my opinion, however sometimes the game can feel a bit like a walking simulator, especially when playing as a herbivore that takes MANY HOURS to grow fully. Omnivores can be a bit confusing to feed, but fun to play once you get the hang of it. They seem to be the ones you have to always keep a sharp eye out for other players that, for the most part, could probably one-hit you if they wished. Just yesterday I was playing as a small, bird-like omnivore, minding my own business and taking a drink from a lake, when a crocodile much bigger than myself jumped out of the water to slaughter me on the spot. A genuine jumpscare and a memorable moment from the game.

Carnivores are tough to play as sometimes. The AI animals in the game include deer, chicken, turtle, goat, and probably others I know nothing about. They are VERY fast and hard to catch as a small carnivore, unless you happen to be a speedy Omniraptor. Finding other players can be tough as well. Playing as a carnivore as opposed to herbivore or omnivore can be a much more enjoyable and rewarding experience if the game decides it should be.

One last thing to add, if you join their Discord and find other players to play with, the game is MUCH more enjoyable than playing it by yourself.

If you love dinosaurs, and can handle sometimes walking alone in the forest or across grassy plains with nothing to do but starve while you search for your next meal, then this game is definitely worth it. I personally love The Isle, and can't wait to see what it becomes in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
