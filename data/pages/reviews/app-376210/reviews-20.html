\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn case you haven't heard of the recent updates coming to Evrima, I suggest you check that out with the new camera system. Can't tell if it'll be as bad as it looks, but the limiting vision will probably make PVP even worse of a nightmare.

I haven't played Evrima in a while, since they continued to update the graphics my computer couldn't handle them but the animations, environment, and details we're nice when started off. So, handle this review as a grain of salt.

There wasn't much fun in playing the early builds, everything was (and still is) a large jungle. You couldn't see or know where you were going because there were zero landmarks or scent cues, and still now there's not that many landmarks as far as I know. I do know there was a new map coming out, not sure if it already is but haven't seen people talking about it so I am unsure.

The main reason why I heavily do not suggest this game is because within recent years the developers have gotten more and more deaf with listening to their community. They don't care for their opinions and if they do, they just write off bad reviews as people being upset with the game itself when its actually the people involved. They repeatedly add unbalanced dinosaur gameplay, overall making it hard to grow or compete with packs, herds, or apexes, and there still isn't much to do. The diet gameplay, new skin system, and scenting was interesting but didn't really add much.

I loved Legacy and it was great. The bugs made the game fun and there was many memories and people I met. When I started going on Evrima that all disappeared. Nobody would get on servers, and if they did you couldn't tell because after they removed global chat its hard to communicate or meet other people unless you find someone not willing to cannibalize you because they're starving. PVP is an interesting experience. Like I said before, not sure if it's unbalanced still, but I know that large groups of herbivores and packs can just tackle each other in combat wars without any negative consequences.

That being said, if you just want a pretty dinosaur game and don't care about the community or the toxic people in it. This is the game for you. In case you do care, play Path of Titans. Most of the servers and people you meet are fair depending on what you find. I don't recommend getting it. If by some miracle it does, I'll edit this review. I apologize if I get or had gotten any information wrong as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really, really, REALLY want to like this game but it is the definition of the Steam early-access experience.
Unfinished & never will be. They've had great ideas for more dinos & gameplay and seemingly scrapped them, and as a result the current state (as 2024 Envrima) is incredibly boring. This game promises so much yet delivers so little and what it does deliver is very minimal & buggy.

Getting (and even spawning) stuck is a common occurrence. The game offers little to no information through smelling, and wikis aren't much help either due to the randomness and scarcity of food. My herbivore experience can be summed up in two words: guaranteed starvation. You CAN graze to keep a minimal amount of food, but the way the game wants you to vary your diet doesn't allow for that to be viable (poor diet will nerf your character substantially). I go to places where food is supposed to spawn more abundantly but end up finding JACK.

I want to like this game but the lack of quality developments make it difficult.\t\t\t\t\t

Me and many other friends have struggled to play the game with its constant bugs. From getting stuck inside trees and rocks, to having certain abilities or in game function fail regularly. It is even hard to join servers. Sometimes our dinos are thrown up into the air and then fall down and die, this has happened many times to my friends while I was playing with them. Even though I can run RDR2 with 0 issues, this game continues to be a struggle simply due to poor development.

One patch it will work, another it mountains teleport, rivers are glitching, and trees are pixelated. And yes, this is every patch. Every patch there is a new bug, a new pug in the land, the dinos, or joining servers. Nothing is fixed, it is supposed to be fixed and then never fixed.

Dondi calls people babies, and bans people from the discord server (the only place to even get updates and or information) and does not listen to the community.

If your community is missing the old, toaster version of your game even after you revamp the entire map, you have messed up somewhere---and the fact people continue to act as if people missing legacy isn't the product of years of disappointment for old players and a rude awakening for new players, are not in the shoes of those who love this game and try their best to enjoy it but time and time again get fooled by thinking "Okay, its good now, just a little bug, just some glitches that will be fixed" and then log back on the next patch after months of waiting to find that a whole new set of issues are there along with the old ones.

Otherwise, if you want to play a dino game. Give it a try, just don't expect it will be worth your money at this point in time. I would wait until they figure their ♥♥♥♥ out, but that won't happen any time soon.

Anyways, I will always love this game. It is one of the only survivals on the market that is so far into its development (will not count POT as it is an arcade, battle royale style) but because of this, people don't have many options.
