\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was a pretty awesome game once, but sadly this is no longer the case.

Your first warning is the simple fact that the game has been in Early Access for almost a decade now. That's a very long time for a game to be in the 'demo' stage. The Isle developers make grandiose promises about how amazing the game 'will' be, then shift the goalposts and blame the playerbase when they do not deliver on their own promises.

Perhaps the most egregious example of this would be Evrima, which for two years was known as the "recode". Dondi (the lead developer and owner of the game) is known to be extremely defensive and hostile when encountering constructive criticism, and fired his own head coder for daring to question him. This, of course, led to the game's code being something the rest of the dev team did not understand, (especially since Deathly was not only the the lead programmer, but the ONLY programmer) which forced Dondi and co to rewrite much of the game's code from the ground up.

Called the "Recode", Dondi and Co promised the world. Every single dinosaur would have a distinctive playstyle and set of animations. Balance would be fixed. The game would be ready for release. There were trailers and dev streams released that spruiked the Spinosaurus and Tyrannosaurus Rex as almost ready to go. During the two years that the 'recode' was developed, there were almost no updates to the main game. When the Recode finally came out, now dubbed 'Evrima'...

...There were only two dinosaurs. Utahraptor and Tenontosaurus. No Spino, No T-Rex. In fact, neither of those two dinosaurs are in Evrima at all, playable or otherwise. Of course, anyone who questioned what happened to the game they were told would come with the recode, and that they had paid for, were silenced. Bans from the Isle discord are pretty much constant. At the end of the day, the Isle is no closer to being a finished game now than it was five years ago.

I cannot in good conscious recommend this game to prospective new players. The game is filled with bugs, balance issues and broken promises, and the lead developer is actively antagonistic to the people who pay for his glorified demo.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're considering buying this game, I advise you to rethink your choices. This game has been in development for almost a decade now and it's still nowhere near complete. Legacy has a sizable roster, but the dinosaurs are poorly balanced and lack variation. Evrima has a much smaller and unique roster, but it's still kinda bare bones. Both versions are a buggy mess with poorly optimized graphics.

Development on this game is super slow, and we get one or two big updates a year. Keep in mind the game has over 60 prehistoric creatures planned for this game (which is unrealistic and a nightmare to balance), with the inclusion of humans, mutants, tribesmen, and other playable creatures.

Apparently, they are also exploring the idea of a zoomed in camera similar to that of a first-person camera, but not quite. I think this decision is quite absurd considering it can affect dinosaurs like Stegosaurus and Tenontosaurus that rely heavily on rear attacks. There are also people who have motion sickness that may be affected by this change.

I originally bought the game after seeing YouTubers cover it, and it piqued my interest as one of the only games I can play as a dinosaur; however, after playing, I began to realize the game's flaws. Sadly, any criticism expressed to the developers is either met with a ban or a insultingly condescending rebuttal as they treat their community very poorly. I wish I could get a refund, but sadly, that's no longer an option for me.

To any potential buyers, there are other dinosaur games that are more fleshed-out with faster development like Path of Titans and Beasts of Bermuda. I highly recommend you check out those games. They are much more likely to satiate your dreams of being able to play as a dinosaur.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game once upon a time was both fun, impressive, and while somewhat clunky was quite enjoyable in terms of game play. A very well done dinosaur PvP Survival style game. Unfortunately in recent years the development team has (in their attempts to "improve" what already existed) countless times missed the mark on what their have attempted and are actively alienating their own player base. They are currently a juggernaut of tone deaf development slowly polluting and killing their own game while simultaneously punishing their own players for having the audacity to enjoy it in it's previous state. They openly unleash backlash for suggested improvements or opinions from their own community in a very egotistical manner coming off as a "We know best and you just shut up and play our game" type way.

As this is a review I will say this. Is the game fun? Yes. The Legacy version that can be accessed via the "Beta" properties is infact fun and what the game once was. The current live build however is quite the opposite and seems to get less impressive with each new update pushed onto the playerbase. For a game about dinosaurs the live version is very lacking. More over for a game to have been in early access for as long as this one has been is probably the biggest red flag.

If you like dinosaur games. Find something else and give this one a pass. The drama laden dev team and their lack of consideration for the people whom have been unfortunate enough to waste money on their ongoing project are sure to kill any level of fun you'd get out of this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a long time, I loved this game. I loved playing this game, I loved being a dinosaur- I have been here sense Legacy, which was the version before Evirma. Evirma itself, shockingly, isn't even the cause of my change of opinion for the game- rather, what they decided to do within Evirma.

Let me lead with the positives: I love the graphics of this game. While not exactly easy on a computer, and the graphics that are recommended are out of date if that is a worry for you, as the Steam suggested ones are for legacy. Seeing the dinosaurs is amazing (shame we wont be able to anymore if they keep pushing through...) and I loved running around as a little critter throughout the game. It was fun to play with friends. It is a beautiful concept, and with better execution it would be an easy recommend.

The negatives are much more expansive. To focus on game play once more, there isn't anything to do. You grow a dinosaur, then either fall off a cliff due to piss-poor night-vision(that the devs refuse to change), starve due to the large map(so you can't find anyone due to the officials player cap), or get stuck in a hole(once more than the devs refuse to fix.) All of the issues are frequently mentioned in the official discord, though most the users who do mention them are banned shortly after. You live, sit in a bush, and then a series of unfortunate events happen and you have wasted at least an hour of your life growing a dinosaur. Congratulations.

The most prominent issue is one that can not, and will never be, mended. The people in charge. Do not get this game if you want a competent dev team that can listen to it's player base. There are tons of scandals within it, the one coming to my mind first being the pedophile that the lead dev defended. Time and time again these people have basically spit on our already dead bodies as we plead for some kind of relief. They will sooner die then admit to their prideful and egotistical selves that hey, maybe we messed up, and maybe we should listen to our player base, who actually play the game- because there is no way in hell that they themselves do. As of now, they're trying to push through a change to the camera- which, funny enough, removes one of the positives I mentioned. I have never seen this, admittedly toxic, community so united in saying that they should not push this. In response, we have gotten essentially told that it is a skill issue(just like they say about any change that anyone pushes back on) and banned if you complain about it too much due to the mods not wanting people to "spread misinformation". This is a permanent ban, if you are curious. This is also the change that forced me into writing this review after years of tolerating the dev team. They do this for every change that the community doesn't like. They push through, and tell the entire community to get good because they aren't changing it.


Edit: After a flood of bad reviews, the devs are claiming that the camera system was "just a prototype" and that we would "be surprised how many ideas never reach public access." They have yet to address the mass banning, and they seemed to have had every intention to push through with it until this backlash. I emphasize again: Do your research on this dev team. Do not look on any 'official' place run by them, as they ban those who give valid criticism in these spaces. Also be sure that, if you watch a YouTube video, that it is not one of the YouTubers known to suck up to the devs. This does not change much of what was previously stated in my review.

TL;DR: Great graphics, Great concept, Absolutely Dev Team, Bad Replayability, Iffy Map.\t\t\t\t\t
