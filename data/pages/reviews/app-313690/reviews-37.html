\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving just finished the second LEGO Batman game I was eager and curious to check out what this might have in store. I had a great time playing through it, it took elements from 2 and really polished them up while doing it's own thing. For example LEGO Batman one was your typical old school hub with level entries, 2 was an open hub world in the form of Gotham city for you to explore, this game manages to combine those two elements nicely in a way that makes it feel fresh and like a progression in the series.
some pros, the game is very fun and easy to pick up and get into, it improved on some issues I had with the previous game such as glitches and loading times. The levels are noticeably longer but I never felt they dragged. Also they are broken into two or three sections each with a checkpoint at which you may save and turn off the game, returning to that point in the level when you return. It's huge character selection is pure fan service, everyone and anyone is playable and what I liked about that was I've learnt about a whole bunch of new characters I wasn't familiar with. I loved the nods to and appreciation of the 60's Batman series and it was cool to see all the classic versions of the characters appear as well as Adam West who is your "citizen in peril" throughout the game.
There was some abilities in 2 that they've done a good job reworking such as not having to go find ability suits to use it on one area to progress, now most characters have a few skills so you're not relying on Batman & Robin all the time for free play.
That does bring me onto my cons which is that you will find yourself switching characters to use abilities in free play constantly, you could face an obstacle that involves blasting the silver metal to reveal the magnetic cover that you open to laser cut a shape out of gold to reveal a panel that the technology ability can activate. With such a huge roster of characters you're sure to be able to play as your favourite but it's very unlikely you'll get through a whole area without having to quickly swap over to someone else to progress. The characters I found I used the most for free play personally were Cyborg, Batman '66, Green Lantern and Batgirl, the first three possess mostly every skill you need and the last just because she's my favourite DC character.

Overall this game and it's predecessors got me out of a slump of not being able to stick with a game all the way to the end but I've managed to 100% all three of these LEGO Batman games so far and I look forward to eventually trying out the fourth installment.

You can play as Detective Chimp and drive a banana truck 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
