\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me say that if there is a perfect trilogy in gaming, this is one of them. After playing the first two games, I expected LEGO Batman 3 to impress me, and it did it. It's for me one of the best LEGO games ever done, without exagerating.

The story just takes place after LEGO Batman 2: just after the Justice Leages defeats both Joker and Lex Luthor, Brainiac arrives to the Earth. After chasing Killer Croc and stoping it, Batman turns out to become a puppet of Brainiac, with Robin freeing his mentor. And after defeating once again the Evil League (Lex Luthor, Joker, Cheetah, etc...), the Justice League teams up with the villains to stop the evil Brainiac. I loved how TT Games managed to create a good story that, jokes asides, I would like to see in a TV show or movie.

Playing this game feels like an improved version of the previous game. Now the characters flying feel way more fluid, the heroes and villains receive more suits with more powerups, there are more puzzles, etc... But the two most important things I consider in this game are the new characters' roster and the new open world. About the first detail, now you can play not just with Batman's allies and enemies or the Justice League, but with all (or almost all) the characters in DC Universe, including many movies and shows. About the second thing, this time you don't play in Gotham, but in the Batcave and planets such as Oa, Ysmault, Nok, Qward... Hence here the name of the game "Beyond Gotham". I kinda loved this new improvements, but the open world feels kind of repetitive and maybe too abusive, with no map like in the previous game (which was really useful, by the way).

Now the graphics are improved too. Knowing this game released back in 2014, when the PS4 and Xbox One just appeared, TT Games made it one of the best looking LEGO games. The only thing I didn't like was, once again, with the planets. Yeah, I get it that every planet is about each Lantern Corp, but sometimes the abusive colors (pink, blue, red...) harmed my eyes. Still, the graphics are an amazing job in the game.

And guess what? Voice acting and music are way better too. Now it's just not the Tim Burton trilogy's OST, but also from movies and shows, just like the Superman theme that plays while flying with him, or the Wonder Woman's theme when flying with her. And I know many people prefer the babbling rather than voice acting, but still, at least they dubbed the game well.

OK, so the full price for this game is 30€, which is not that bad considering the amount of hours you can play it. But remember this game has DLCs, and in case you want to get every achievement in the game, you need the DLCs that come in the season pass for 15€ more. I think if you can get the full version for the price that you can buy the base game for, I would recommend it at full price. In case you are not sure, on sales it costs 5€, and the season pass 3€ more. I would recommend the second way instead: buying it on sales.

We all know that almost every LEGO game made it means perfection, and LEGO Batman 3 is no exception of it. I guarantee you will have fun with it if you love DC Comics' universe\t\t\t\t\t
