\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLEGO Batman 3 isn't bad. It's not broken or buggy. It's not actively aggravating the way that LEGO Hobbit was. There's no horrible crafting mechanic. It's just that it's not as good as LEGO Batman 2.

The first problem is that it wants to be bigger than LEGO Batman 2, and the only way it can is by having even more characters, even more locations, and it just makes it tedious.

There are so many hub worlds. There's one for each of the Lantern colours. There's the Batcave. There's the Justice League HQ. There's the Watchtower. And there's only one world on which you can drive any of the vehicles. What's the point of that?

And the Lantern hub worlds are tiny, so they have that funny curve to them that makes the camera zoom out. Fair play, the missions are nice and varied, but there are so many of them.

And at least you can do all everything with a small cast of characters, thanks to the suit-changes being so much simpler. There are a lot of quality-of-life improvements in the game, it's just that there's a point at which it's past "padding" and into "please stop" territory.

Mildly recommended. There are better LEGO Batman games (even the first one), but this isn't a complete waste of money.\t\t\t\t\t
