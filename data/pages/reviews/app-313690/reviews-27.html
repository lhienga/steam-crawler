\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a shame..

This game is 'better' than the first two. It starts a little slow, then gets really great with the peak being the outer space level on the rotating ship. It's a spectacular level that looks and sounds GREAT. Then things take a sharp fall REALLY QUICK. Some basic fundamentals are missing in this game - sometimes you're not sure how to proceed, or whether you should fast tap a button or hold it - these are basic flaws that revolve around bad UI/UX. The player MUST KNOW how to proceed at all times. When things get too mysterious, the game grinds to a halt- and that's the biggest shame.

It overwhelms the player with a slew of characters, all with different powers and again, it's so much, you're forced to go through a process of elimination - WHICH ISN'T FUN; IT'S FRUSTRATING. Not only that, but the celebrity tie-ends are just....bizarre. That's all they can be described as. They don't add value whatsoever.

I've gotten kickbacks for my reviews on the other Lego Batman games saying they're "kids games." The bottom line is, a game HAS TO BE FUN and when it's done properly, both kids and adults enjoy it. It's clear the developer not once used third party QA to review the game and get feedback. If they did, that QA team had to be family/friends that told them what they wanted to hear, and not objectivity.

I'll avoid any future titles from this developer because it's clear they just don't know how to make fun, bug-free games. They used the exact same engine, sound effects, etc. on nearly all three of these Lego Batman games so there's a bit of laziness here also. The soundtrack, for the most part, Danny Elfman's original score from the 1989 Batman film - it's rehashed in all three of these games. Too lazy to even bother to write/hire someone to do original music.

The Lego Batman series is a lazy, disappointing, poorly designed claptrap of games rehashing their own material. The same issues/bugs found in the first one will be found in this one. It's unfortunate.\t\t\t\t\t
