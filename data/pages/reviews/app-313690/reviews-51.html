\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played the Xbox 360 and Xbox One versions of this and many LEGO games, I am very happy with this version. Playing at 60fps smoothly instead of 30fps (since the console versions cap it there) is a nice change, and the performance is stellar. It does stutter when there's a lot of studs on-screen or when a new area is loading in, but I'm fairly sure that's because of my PC (being a low-to-mid tier gaming laptop). I'd definitely recommend if you enjoyed other LEGO games, or played this on a console. The gameplay is fun and easy to pick up, with the ability to customize your controls (which isn't available on the console versions either), and the story is fun and easy to grasp for both casual and hardcore DC fans alike.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLego Batman 3 is... The black sheep of the Batman series in my opinion. From the nostalgic and fun to play first game to the sequel which shook up what all future Lego games would become, trend setting in every positive way, Batman 3 just winds the Batman series back to something best said as "In between Batman 1 by advancing the gameplay and the sequel by having what feels like less of a game". I'll get into all of this in a bit. It's hard for me to recommend or even not recommend it since it brings back some good features and directly continues the story of Batman 2, but at the same time i feel i just criticise this game more.

Story wise, as said, continues on from Batman 2's Brainiac tease. Capturing majority of the different Lantern wielders (White and Black are not featured in story, however they are playable characters) except for Green Lantern. The first level brings Batman and Robin to chasing Killer Croc through Gotham's sewers in what feels a same looking color palette from the previous entry, real familiar and sort of welcoming back before reintroducing the suit system in which the main heroes (As well as some other characters later on which was a pleasant idea) swap outfits to complete puzzles. After the chase, Brainiac invades Earth and after capturing the Green Lantern, has the power to set his city capturing hobby aside and go after his new goal of capturing whole planets. It's now up to the combined forces of the Justice League and some of it's villains to defeat Brainiac and fix the damage that was caused.

The levels do tend to drag on for a little too long, maybe a few of them could've been 5-10 minutes shorter than they were in story and to a degree, freeplay considering the lack of level skipping in certain parts. Big trouble in little Gotham is a prime example of my point here. Want to get the minikits in the last section of part 1? Well too bad, you have to complete the first and second areas before you're able to get to where you want to be. Tedious. Boring.

Once some of the main cast gets zapped by various Lantern ring powers, i feel like they just get stupidly annoying to listen to in cutscenes and in levels. Cyborg especially since he gets zapped by Sinestro's fear powers and turns into this absolute clingy, sheepish, not in any realm of funny character.

The Hub worlds are laughably poor. All the non-Green Lantern planets are small spheres that you can zoom around on as a flying character and (to Nok and Odym's discredit) look practically bland and same-ish to go around. Two quests in particular (one on each planet) get even more tedious since the quests (on every non-GL hub) are all repetitive to no end. Ones where you have to go around and track down certain things with no real starting off point just annoy me. Ambush Bug's final quest on Nok and the banana quest for Gorilla Grodd on Odym can burn.

All the vehicles you collect? Pretty much unusable anywhere except for races on Oa, the Green Lantern hub. And even then all of the flying vehicles are useless since you can do flying races with FLYING CHARACTERS. But at least i could do the ground vehicle races with the 1989 movie Batmobile. That was cool.

My personal pet peeve is how padded out the character roster is with... Celebrities or DC writers. Adam West (and i say this while still holding respect for the man in giving us his portrayal of the camp 60s Batman) is... Annoying in levels. Unlike Stan Lee in the Marvel games (who might give out the very spaced out cry for help), he just doesn't shut up when you're close by to him in levels. We get it, you need a rescue but I'M IN STORY MODE, NO FREE PLAY TO HELP YOU. Plus we already get a 60s Batman that has abilities which is leagues and bounds more useful. Geoff Johns, Jim Lee and Kevin Smith round out my head scratching additions to the game. I'm sure we could've gotten 3 extra DC heroes or villains to fill in... Or just not have the slots at all.

The more i write this, the more i'm obviously leaning towards a negative review of the game. Which is a shame. It's still got some fun in places... If you can find it.\t\t\t\t\t
