Lego Batman 3: Beyond Gotham Review
_________________________________

(SPOILERS IF YOU HAVEN'T COMPLETED THE GAME)

Recently I have been coming back to all my childhood lego games and getting 100%. I decided that I am going to do this with all the Lego games I haven't played as well. I have already finished Lego LOTR, The Hobbit, and Lego Batman 1-2 ( I've left reviews as well if you would like to check those out). I just finished Lego Batman 3 today and let me just say, this game was just so awesome to me. It was such a huge step up from Lego Batman 2. I couldn't believe it. There was so many improvements from the second game and so much other things that just excelled. The reason I am mentioning the second game so much was because it wasn't that good to me at all (Read my previous review for the reasons for that). Anyways here are the reasons why I liked this game so much. BTW, this will be a pretty long review just because of all the EPICNESS I will mention, so maybe grab a bag of popcorn or something

(Pros and Cons are purely based on my opinion)

Pros:

- Classic funny and interesting Lego storytelling
- 217 unlockable characters to play and unlock (including DLC)
- 250 Gold Bricks to collect (You can walk and jump when collecting Gold Bricks)
- 20 Red Bricks to collect and find (Ordered by mission level)
- 30 Different times to save the legendary ADAM WEST
- 28 Ground and Air vehicles to drive and unlock
- 9 Bonus DLC packs (Packed with extra levels, characters, and achievements)
- 70 Achievements! (Including DLC)
- 11 Different hub locations to explore
- Side quests and VR missions to unlock characters and Gold Bricks
- Great soundtrack featuring songs from different DC shows, movies, and Comics
- Unique sounds and animations for most characters (Wonderwomen/Superman theme when flying, Throwing/fighting animations
- I ran into hardly any glitches but this is different for everybody

- Awesome Storytelling and Voice Acting: The cast in this game is just incredible and so is their voice acting (Actors such as ADAM WEST!!, Conan O'Brien, Geoff Johns, and Stephen Amell) . It really helped me get immersed in the story and actually made me laugh. Even though I didn't like the previous game that much, it continued the story from it and brought elements from Batman 1 and 2 in the mix. The story was filled with tons of cool, and retro references from shows, movies, and previous games. Even though I did not know much about DC, the game made me want to know more. I even spent a lot of time just researching what characters I had after I would unlock or buy one.

- Level and Hub Design: The level design and hub design was just so unique and colorful. The levels that interested me most were the different planets that you have to go in order to get the rings. I am not a huge fan of DC so I didn't know what any of these places were. Even so, the design of these levels made me feel like I was in a totally different world. It almost made me feel like I was playing Lego Star Wars just because of all the new creatures and Biomes.

Cons:
- Vehicles only playable on 1 Hub Planet (Which is incredibly tiny compared to Lego Batman 2's open world)

- This game can be quite difficult for a normal kid to complete. The constant switching of suits and puzzles could be very confusing. There also isn't always clear indicators on what needs to be done to progress in the level. Another stab towards kids enjoyment is that unlocking all achievements is locked behind a paywall. In order to be able to get all achievements, you have to buy the DLC. This is extremely scummy, especially for all the kids not having the money to be able to buy the ALL THE DLC.

- More of a DC game then a strictly BATMAN game: If you are looking for a game just focusing on Batman and Batman alone, this isn't the game for you. This game spends way more time on the DC universe rather then the story of Batman. Although this is the case, there is tons of Love towards Batman, specifically in the Bonus Level and DLC.

Overall I did enjoy this game a lot, better then the second one. My complaints are mainly with how this game was advertised and made for. The prices for the DLC are not good enough just to be able to get all achievements. The levels and exploration can be very tricky for kids and they won't get most of the references and jokes in the story. The game is also more of a DC Universe game, rather than a Batman game. Other than that, if we are reviewing based on the game itself and not the production side of things, I think it is worth the buy (On sale)

Rating 9/10
