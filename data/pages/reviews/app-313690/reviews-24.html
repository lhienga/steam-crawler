\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Glitches and solutions at bottom of review.*
This lego game is somewhere between the old-school lego games and the modern ones.
The levels are small and simple, and there are only a few quests/races/collectibles in the hubworlds.
There are far more glitches in this game than any other lego game I've played, but none were game breaking.

The game was enjoyable, and took 40hrs to get 100% (including the DLC) and I enjoyed the DLC mini levels quite a lot.

Please note, this game requires you to buy the DLC in order to unlock all of the achievements. Usually this annoys me, especially in a lego game, but the DLC for this game are cheap, and there are 6 DLC levels. Each DLC level is very short though.

The biggest glitches/issues I had were as follows:
- Issues using a controller for the game. Partway through playing my controller disconnected and the game wouldn't respond to it when I reconnected it. I had to disconnect my cordless controller from my laptop, re-connect it to my laptop, and then start the game again.
- Some planets were extremely difficult to use flying characters on. They ran into invisible walls, or just dropped out of the sky for no reason.
- Glitch with the quest "Greedy Loontern". Details below.
- Glitch with the quest "Welcome to Nok!". Details below.

Details for "Greedy Loontern" glitch and solution: The quest would randomly cancel and I'd have to go back and start it again. Luckily this one was solved by just repeatedly doing the quest, beating up the Green Loontern to knock studs out of him. It seems that progress in knocking him out is saved even if you have to restart the quest. Eventually around the 4th time I managed to knock the studs out of him, it finished the quest.

Details for "Welcome to Nok!" glitch and solution: In this quest you follow Daffy (Green Loontern) around, and fight two waves of bad guys. For many people, after the second fight Daffy just stands there and doesn't keep moving. You must leave the planet and return in order to restart this quest.
I managed to get past this glitch. I played as robin (no special outfit), only used melee attack (no ranged weapons, or ground slams), and I made sure that Green Loontern didn't die during the second attack (although he had died many times in the first one). This time, immediately after the last bad guy was defeated, Daffy ran on ahead as he was supposed to, instead of standing still and doing nothing.\t\t\t\t\t
