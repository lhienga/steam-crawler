\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLEGO Batman 1 was a classic story about the dynamic duo and the Arkham goons and Batman 2 expanded upon that by bringing in the Justice League. So when the time comes for the inevitable third entry, where do you go from there? LEGO Batman 3 goes well beyond Gotham City, strangely focusing on the Lanterns and their whole situation instead.

The story summed up is that Brainiac (as teased at the end of Batman 2) has kidnapped the different colored lanterns and shrunk the planet, resulting in the Justice League going to each of the lantern planets in order to undo the damage caused. The levels usually range from being alright to waaaay too long. To my knowledge, this is the first game to include the shorter DLC levels featured in the latter half of TT's work, mostly ranging from films such as The Dark Knight and Man of Steel, they're worth picking up if you want an extra 10 minutes of fun.

2014 was also Batman's 75th anniversary, so it's to be expected Batman 3 would have something to celebrate the occasion, and while there is a DLC pack for it, the base game still has a thing or two up its sleeve in the form of beloved Quahog mayor Adam West as well as a bonus level based on the 1966 TV series. That's about it though. You also get over 100+ characters to unlock across the DC universe, including Kevin Smith & Conan O'Brien for whatever reason I simply cannot understand.

In my opinion, I think that Batman 3 is definitely the weakest game in the trilogy, and I would hope that should Batman 4 happen (which has been heavily rumored to be TT's next title at the time of writing this review), it would improve on 3's shortcomings. Nevertheless, I think it's still a pretty decent LEGO game that's worth picking up, especially for any fans of the Lantern Corps in particular.\t\t\t\t\t
