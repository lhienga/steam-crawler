\r\n\t\t\t\t\t A breath of fresh air after playing the second Lego Batman game. The levels and the character variety in them kept the levels feeling fresh, unlike 2 where you only play as Batman, Superman, and Robin until the last two levels where you actually get to play as different characters.
The dialogue and voice acting in this one didn't take away from the game for a change, probably because I was used to it by this point but also because some of the line deliveries were good on the actor's part.
The free roam missions and activities were fun enough to do, though they started to become repetitive after a time, and some gold bricks looked like they would've been a nightmare to find had I not gotten the gold brick detector extra.
As for the character customization, was good enough to where I could make my own characters and use them in Free Play without having to switch to a different character too often, however, I still found this game's character creator a tad limiting, mostly in terms of abilities though still not as bad as Lego Batman 2's customizer.
Would recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo after hours of playing, I changed my mind.
This game is NOT BAD. I didn't Hated it. Im entertained, but only for several hours.

The first level is SEWER LEVEL. Wow, what a great introduction guys.
And then Batman Boss fights with...probably the most overlong setpieces to reach Batman, and then Batman become Giant which is...meh.

Oh, you expect its gonna be action packed after this right?
Nope, you gotta fix the Rocket and traverse Batcave....as if this cave wasn't belong to Batman. What's with the hindrance, unecessary and overlong puzzle just to refuel a Rocket? After that, you go to Space, Star wars style in Watch Tower. But at that point, Im all Burnt out...and bored. The story feels Forced because how its constantly on edge, and then you switch characters later, had a boss fights and then switch again. Its disorganized.
Should have been called the game as "LEGO Justice League" because Batman had nothing to do with the story anymore, he's just...there.
And what's with the paid DLC? I can take the Free DLC for extra characters and I did bought The Squad.
I felt Robbed.
The whole DLC mission ends in less than 6 minutes, with so little action and puzzles.
All that for an Extra Characters who played pretty much the same as other characters already.
And how could this game Forced you to play ONLINE all the time? Why this game is unplayable while Offline for a Game who doesn't had any Online multiplayer? (Maybe someone had answer for this).

Its a Good Game, but its so lackluster Im actually bored to continue. Atleast I can call myself lucky to get this on Trilogy Bundle, because atleast I can play Lego Batman 1 for Nostalgia and 2 which I never played before(and I had a Blast!) than this one.

Kids, Don't Buy The DLC's they ain't worth it.

PREVIOUS REVIEW


I don't know where to start with this game. Lego Batman 3 is alright for a Lego game. If you've played the previous 2 games then, this is practically the same concept. However unlike the other 2, I despise this game for some reason. Something about it just makes it sooooo much worse compared to the others (more specifically Lego Batman 2). Maybe it's the nostalgia I hit when I played 2 and only having played this one years and years later for the first time. I've only played through about 8 working on 9 levels, and so far, its underwhelming to say the least. Levels feel slow and repetitive, which to be fair is most Lego games, yet this ones even worse than that. The story got boring after the first 2 levels and I've found more bugs within the little side transitional missions than I have played the game. Unfortunately I have sworn to 100% every Lego game made and this one has me dreading even looking at. I don't recommend if you're going for what I'm doing but it is still an alright game.
