\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a game following the much-loved Lego Marvel Superheroes, I expected better. I would give it 2/5 stars.

There isn't any one specific thing that makes it less than its predecessors, but rather a host of little annoyances with the gameplay. For example there are many times during a level where the camera will move of its own accord, clearly in an attempt to give you a hint towards the main path, but in doing so makes it difficult to search for Minikits or studs. The multitude of suits for characters like Batman, while a cool gimmick, quickly get tiring to constantly swap between. The side-challenges are minimal, the plot bland, and the controls often freezing. The companion characters are useless and the villains respawn at such a rapid rate you have zero time to actually complete the quest--and that's assuming you don't get stuck in one of the dozen glitches apparent in most levels.

All-in-all this is a game I would recommond only to avid LEGO fans or to DC fanatics. If you want a truly great game go for LEGO Marvel Superheroes, which has all the features and blind fun that this one does not--ironic since it was the predecessor.\t\t\t\t\t
