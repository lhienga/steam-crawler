\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I got all three LEGO Batman games in a bundle on sale (discounted to the point where buying just this game, the only one I hadn't yet played at the time, would've cost the same as buying the bundle with all three; I also have copies of this game's two predecessors for Wii), this was the only one that was new to me.

Back in LEGO Batman 2, the sequel hook that became this was Brainiac finding the Earth either right before the credits or as a post-credits scene (and since it doesn't have a place to re-watch cutscenes that aren't part of Story Mode levels, you can only see that cutscene once per saved game). But I think he had a different voice actor there than in this.

PROS:
Like its predecessors, despite using licensed characters, it has an original story (or maybe it's an adaptation of an event from the comics like LEGO DC Super-Villains could be, though at least that game is a looser adaptation than usual for TT Games' LEGO video game formula, and the formula's movie adaptations are already pretty loose)
You can change suits whenever you want, something I would've wanted in LEGO Batman 2 (there's one puzzle in Level 9 of 15 that works better in Story mode because the suit swappers remember the suit you wore into them and designed a puzzle around it; to get past it in Free Play, where switching characters out of Batman or Robin's suits causes you to lose those suits, I just had both players as Batman)
Unlike LEGO Batman 2, Killer Croc is actually sane (in that game, he only had one voiced line, that being "Croc hungry!". LEGO Batman 1 didn't have any intelligible voiced lines, for the record)
The characters that can switch suits tend to have at least one suit they can't use and the others can, meaning that none of them outclass another

CONS:
There's no open world like there was in LEGO Batman 2 (and since this came after that, it's a more valid complaint than if you were to say that LEGO Batman 1 is bad for not having an open world)
I found some of the music that plays when you're flying as certain characters to be annoying, though I didn't grow up with the 1966 Batman show, something this game loves calling back to (the one time I saw it on cable TV, I thought it was a parody)

I'm probably not the best judge for if this game is worth getting... because again, I got it on sale in a bundle to the point where buying all three LEGO Batman games was $5 and so would've buying just this.\t\t\t\t\t
