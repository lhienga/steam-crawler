\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort version, while still a somewhat entertaining LEGO game, it is absolutely the worst one I have played. Even comparing it to their earliest titles, and it still falls short, which is saying a lot. It is very easy and rather short, so good for younger players or those just getting into the LEGO games. For more experienced players or those that have played other LEGO titles, it is notably lacking for such a late production.

Pick it up on sale, but that is about it. Not a bad game, but not good either, just VERY... meh...

The Hub area is small and similar to the first games, just a few rooms to show off mini kits, buy extras, build your character. Not the sprawling cities or large open maps of many of the later games.

There are no wandering NPCs or other characters you have bought wandering around, so even though it has a small Hub area, it still manages to feel empty and lifeless.

There is a decent selection of vehicles, with the DLC, otherwise there aren't that many. There is only one place in the entire game you can use them, and you can't use them for much. Not a single vehicle level, other than a couple arcade shooter style levels in the story.

Character selection is good, a good amount without being overwhelming.

Studs are extremely easy to come by, and you will never lack for them, even without the stud bonuses.

The game itself is also very easy, with a short story of only 16 levels, and another 5 or 6 with all the DLC, though they don't have any red bricks.

The VR missions are very strange, and totally break the pacing of the game. It was a nice break from the same old same old, but it just felt off and not well integrated. They don't require a VR headset or anything, it is just what the game calls their minigames.\t\t\t\t\t
