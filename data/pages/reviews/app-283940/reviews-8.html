\r\n\t\t\t\t\t100% worth the nostalgia trip. I remember playing this over and over (with my brother, I think?) in our school library when I was . . . maybe 7 years old? We'd figure out bits and pieces but might have started over every time until we could beat it. I have the sense that then, like now, I had no idea how to save it (apparently, press "s"). Took me just over an hour this time, poking about. It turns out the game presents different puzzles each time, so you won't have to use every item each time you play--all the more reason to re-play it! I did look up a walk-through at the very end; I couldn't figure out where to go.

I would highly recommend this for young kids, and even as an adult, it's a delightful, complex game. I practiced some "advanced" addition and subtraction! But more than that, the game encourages point-and-click exploring with so many fun bonus animations on top of the objects you need to discover, and the puzzles are challenging enough that, well, that I still needed help on the one I didn't instinctively remember. There are clues in the dialogue that a patient, helping adult might pick up on more than a kid. The voice acting really brings the story to life. For just a few bucks, replaying this was such a delightful and fun experience, and I'd certainly recommend it for kids.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnique things about Freddi Fish and the Case of the Missing Kelp Seeds:
•\tIt seems like the shortest Freddi Fish game
•\tFun things to do on the side include a theater and minigames where you feed animals and solve math problems (every kid’s favorite, right?)
•\tThere’s at least a couple different potential paths for the game (which one you get is randomized each time you play)
•\tSometimes Freddi and Luther are drawn awkwardly (luckily, this is the only entry where this is the case)

Like all Freddi Fish games, it:
•\tIncludes high quality, hand-drawn art and animation
•\tHas great music. Seriously, I often find myself sitting in an area just to listen to the soundtrack
•\tContains lots of things in the background and foreground to click on for fun
•\tHas great, memorable characters, especially with Freddi and Luther (even though Luther’s voice is rather unfortunate)
•\tIs super nostalgic for anyone who grew up with it
•\tIs a phenomenal game for children
•\tHas some awkward pauses in dialogue, but maybe that’s common for kid’s games
•\tTakes about 30-60 minutes to complete (for me, anyway)
•\tFrequently goes on sale on Steam

Freddi Fish 1 is an okay game. Not bad, but not one of my favorites either. Regardless, it’s an excellent game for kids (and for adults who are looking for a dose of nostalgia).
