\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLemme confess and say I wasn't alive when this game was released (I was born in the year 2000). However, when I heard about this game through a youtube video, I was curious about it and was surprised to see this on Steam. I was even more pleased to find it on sale so I got the whole package containing the five main games and the two spin-off titles (I wrote a bad review on Water Worries). So, going into this game first time, I have to say the graphics are very nice for the time this was released and I have to say, even for a kids game, it's also a good point-and-click adventure game that takes place in the good old underwater setting. I will say that it's surprisingly short but I had to remind myself that back then, no PC game CD could hold that much storage back in the early 90's so it was natural for games like this to be short. However, the length of the game (as well as the other four game) is compensated by the amount of silly things you can click on which is nice as far as detail goes in video games. All in all, this is a point-and-click adventure game with a simplistic story and good graphics for its time.I say get this game and its whole franchise.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs an adult with money and a Steam account, I'm bought this game for nostalgia's sake. While I easily beat Putt Putt Joins the Parade (another Humongous game from my childhood) in 20 minutes (since I still have the entire quest memorized), it still took me over an hour to finish Freddi's in depth, varied-each time quest, (and only the main quest, without exploring everywhere like I did in Putt Putt).

It was great to reexperience the friendly, brave Freddi, the greedy, goofy Luther, kind Grandma Grouper, the stuck-under-the-same-freaking-rock-for-20-years Gabby, the junkyard Dogfish, Fiddler Crab, Ray and his SuperDuperSomethingSomethingPolyGizmo, King Crab, Eddy the Electric Eel, Mr. Starfish the math teacher, the Pirate fish with the AARRRGH song, and of course, (the ones who make the game totally worth it) Boss, Spongehead, and the Squidfather.

With friendly (and affably evil) characters, a compelling story with high, established stakes (all of the fish will die) in a kids' game, an interesting quest, and funny animations with every click, this game is definitely worth $7 for all ages.
