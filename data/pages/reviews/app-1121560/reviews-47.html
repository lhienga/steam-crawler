it's a lovely game you can sink a looooot of time in - if you have the controller.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst up: This is my first Atelier game and i didnt even know that there were more so i cant say anything of how it fares against other atelier games.

Honestly tho? I adore this game with all my heart. Especially the way they implemented crafting (Alchemy). of the 43 hours i have in this game, ive easily spent 30 of those just in the alchemy menu, trying to create the best items i could think of, which is also an amazing thing, that, although you gain more powerful recipes and ingredients after progressing the story, nothing stops you from spending literally 10 hours on one item that can one-shot the next 2 bosses!!!

Its amazing that the game lets you just do stuff like that!!!

Even IF you dont like the Alchemy the shops still sell items and gear "premade" for you to equip, but they dont come CLOSE to the gear you can make yourself. It also gives the game a nice way to make the combat more difficult/easier. Although there are difficulty settings, at almost any point you can just say: "This is getting to hard, so im going to make gear that can 2 shot anything and reduce all damage i receive to 0" or: "This game is to easy, lets give all my consumables debuff traits so that when i want to heal for example, that character will get a debuff in exchange!"

The only complaint i have for alchemy is that ryza has 2 lines that she repeats like every 3 seconds while in the alchemy menu, and if you have like a 2 hour session of nothing but alchemy it WILL drive you insane (tho you can just turn down the volume and listen to music in the background)

I realise now that i only talked about the alchemy, but honestly? The system is too good to NOT have an entire review dedicated to it)\t\t\t\t\t

The battle and crafting system feels good to me now. I always see myself wanting to craft everything. The story is decent, just your typical feel good JRPG. The characters are lovable.

As for the port, they can do better to be honest, I average around 95-100fps on 1080p everything maxed on my 5700 XT, but they can do better for the graphics of this game. To give reference, I can achieve 100-110fps 1080p on Monster Hunter World, everything high. This game needs improvement. However, that doesn't take away my enjoyment of the game. Freesync works good, no crashes. As long as I can finish the game, it's fine.

I'll definitely play Atelier Ryza 2 next year. I just hope the PC port will be better.\t\t\t\t\t
