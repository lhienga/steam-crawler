\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEnjoyable game, although series veterans will probably find the crafting a bit underwhelming. Reforge makes it laughably easy to get the right traits/effects {of which there are fewer of to begin with} on armor with max quality and little need for planning or preparation since you can address input quality, traits, and output effects one by one instead of all at once. Crafting depth is also less interesting trait-wise {doubly so on consumables} due to the lesser variety. The same systems make it a good entry point into the series for beginners, however. I would say I recommend this game for a first time Atelier player before tackling the older, and tougher games.

The strength of the postgame enemies is very underwhelming in comparison to the items we can make. Hopefully we get some actually challenging postgame content as DLC, because as it stands there is basically zero need to make any type of items, or even min/max equips since a half assed one is good enough to reduce bosses damage to nothing.

I enjoyed the battle system, it had some interesting depth to it and would definitely like to see a mix of turn base/RT games in the future. My main issue here was simply the difficulty being too low. Battles were far too easy even on HARD, and I was able to go through entire game in a shoddy half made set of 2nd tier armor until building my postgame armor. Since there is no time limit, I feel there would be no harm in pushing us into fighting more difficult enemies and bringing careful synthesizing to the foreground of the game for its entirety instead of only at the end.

The story was enjoyable enough, but the small roster and much lower number of character events was a bit of a letdown. Still, the game made up by having relatively more story scenes in exchange. No real complaints here, aside from the few random unvoiced scenes.

No complaints from the technical side, I did not encounter any crashes, freezes, or performance slowdowns. Early reviews had issues with a lack of gfx settings and other system issues which have been resolved.

Overall, I recommend. I've been playing Atelier games since the PS2 era and continue to look forward to playing far into the future.\t\t\t\t\t

*added review information*

I originally put a short review because obviously i was being lazy but also I wanted to make sure to be able to give it a thumbs up as soon as I knew this game was as fun as it is.

Things that I really like about this version from the Atelier series are:

The active combat system using action points rather than just straight up turns is very effective and fun to use. It was hard at first to get used to the controls and how things work - just a warning - at least for me it is very different from any combat system in a jrpg that I've played. In a good way. I won't go into details here because frankly I'm sure there are others that will explain better anyway.

It's not entirely "open" 'world but has a somewhat open world feel to it, more so than the first atelier game - sophie, that I played. I did love sophie and didn't mind the way the map worked too much but this is definitely an improvement. Each area has sections and load screens between them but within the areas the exploring is relatively open.

The crafting/synthesis system has changed for the better and like combat took some getting used to and I''m still figuring out how it works as I go but I really like it and this is one area that I really think it shines in.

I've seen some people say this is the best Atelier game of the series and though I've only played one other installment, I can understand why. Its very engrossing. Every part of it for me at least is exciting and fun, discovering new materials, new areas keeps you pushing further into the new areas, trying different tools for gathering, etc. finding new recipes as you do synthesis, and new ways to unlock better, more powerful tools and weapons. It all just keeps me motivated to keep trying new things and just literally experiment a lot.

If you're the type of person that is into the features I described in the last paragraph, this is a no brainer imo, and you should definitely get the game.

Hope this review was useful!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is an amazing entry point for those wanting to get in the Atelier series or RPG's. Gameplay is energetic and fun using the battle system of action points. Synthesis and gathering are simple enough not to become annoying but also feature deep replayability. Trying to craft the best version of items and gear. The Atelier games relies alot on synthesis and crafting items, You will be spending most of your time gathering items to craft with alchemy.

The world is very big with alot to explore. Crafting new items and weapons yourslef just feels soo good to do. The Characters are really likeable, What I like the most is how the characters all have their own backgrounds with different perspectives on things and how the world works which shows in their motives, strengths and weaknesses, struggles and conflicts . Each of them are working real hard to chase their dreams while it puts them at odds sometimes they still have to work together to work things out.


In Conclusion Atelier Ryza is a solid RPG game. The Story is really enjoyable ride. The choices made during encounters carry into making synthesis and gathering which are the core aspects of the game. Combat is fun and unleashing skills is really rewarding especially when you have a very long combo going. Worlds are full of life, and the environments are beautiful. If you want to get into the RPG genre or the Atelier series, this game is for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has a slow start, but when you get pass it (~1 hour) it's when the game's unique core mechanic - Alchemy, sucks you in. Graphic is amazing, both in high and low end pc, i managed to pull through with 30-40fps on my potato laptop (i5-E7200U, 4GB Ram, HD620) with all setting to low, no shadow and the game still look impressive. For the story, even though up to the point i write this review (about 7 hours), it's seem like i still at the beginning of the game lol, maybe because most of the time i spent in this game was for hunting ingredients and experimented with Alchemy.

In conlusion, a good buy for all you JRPG fan out there, even if you're new to Atelier franchise.

However, be advised that if you want to enjoys this game to the fullest, an Xbox controller is a must. Even though one of the game's latest update added mouse support which is a big thumb up for Koei Tecmo for listening to the fan, control still feel...awkward.\t\t\t\t\t
