\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCasual and laid-back Atelier experience. The story itself is engaging enough to keep me going through the end, but it's over far too quickly and you're not given all that much opportunity to engage with the characters. That being said, the game removes a lot of the pain points of prior Atelier games, and the core game can be easily completed without hunting down all the side areas or even upgrading to all the best items.
The alchemy itself is a lot more predictable and rewarding while taking far less work than the earlier tetris-style. The game offers many different systems for gaining materials and crafting gear, and while they may be required on higher difficulties many can be ignored on standard.
The main pain point the game introduces involves long lists: some can be scrolled with left and right d-pad, some with the right control stick, but the longest lists can only be scrolled one item or line at a time.
The art and animation is really attractive, the music is a little catchy, but the partial voice acting feels more partial than I'm used to. Am I the only one who felt weird not hearing Pamila's voice?

Atelier Ryza has high production values and should keep the player interested and engaged long enough to beat it, but its short length makes its retail price seem a bit high. That said, the game is an easy recommend if you want something that's fun and won't challenge you too much.

Full Disclosure, my playtime reflects that I often fell asleep while playing the game because I was very tired, and does not reflect the actual content length.\t\t\t\t\t
