\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a good RPG game. Great Arts. Great Designs.. I think i will give a point around 90-94 / 100 for this game.

This game's RPG game but it's not story about fierce battle, Heavy drama or anything. It's slice of life with some little drama.
This game focus on craft something to do other thing. and you have to collect item for craft. That mean you have to farm a lot.
Craft system is really great you can make a lot of thing. One item have a lot of possible effect. And when you combine with core item system it will just like you can create skill for yourself.
Battle system really interesting but it not that fun when you compare to other rpg game duo balance. (For me battle system of Mana khemia is the best for atelier series)
Art CG Design is really great animation also really good but still lacking. for example Ryza ran or walk is really good animation but when she turn around she don't have animation for that she just turn to new direction. Also when you can't jump over something freely like other open world game She's just hit hidden wall it the air.
Song/Music of this game is really good. You can listen without bore.

By the way this game have slow start. You may think it's bore at first. you need to learn with Ryza and you will meet a lot of interesting about alchemist. And then you will feel love her atelier.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtelier is a beloved series with a long and storied history. I tried getting into it previously with Atelier Rorona on the PS3, and although I loved the concept of alchemy, the laid back theme and the gorgeous music and art, I was pushed away by the stress the deadline system put on me. I had the same issue with Reccetear, a cute and colourful shop-themed RPG that punished you harshly for missing a deadline. Essentially you had to min-max your time spent ingame and it was simply impossible to do everything in one playthrough. Personally, I did not like this approach and it's why I dropped Rorona before really giving it a proper try.

When I heard Atelier Ryza had the same focus on alchemy, cute and likable characters and a laid-back theme, but ditched the dead-line mechanic I felt it was finally time to give the series another try, and this time I loved it!

Ryza may not be for everyone, apparently it dumbs down some systems the series has become known for; the alchemy isn't a complex as in previous entries from what I've heard and there aren't that many character events outside of the main story, but I had a great time with it. Collecting different materials, getting to know the characters, mastering the crafting system and making the best equipment and items you can to battle overwhelming foes, for me it had everything I could ask for from and RPG of this nature.

Sure, it has a bit of a slow start but that's a common theme among RPGs in general and it makes a lot of sense here considering the characters are a bunch of teenagers who just want to go on adventures outside their island. Hardly the heroes of your typical RPG. But eventually things pick up in the story and you are faced with a real threat. This is apparently going against series tradition, but I felt it added to the experience as a whole. There is a "bad guy", but you never feel pressured to go fight them, you can take your time and enjoy gathering and crafting at your leisure. There are also tons of sidequests and treasures to find, both on your island and in the wild. The maps aren't huge or big on exploration, but every map is worth going through multiple times since different tools yield different results when gathering. It rarely felt repetitive either since the enviroment looks vastly different depending on time of day and weather.

Without spoiling too much, the characters do grow throughout the story and I can't wait to see where their stories pick up in Ryza 2. You start off with just three characters, but eventually your party grows to a total of six characters and you can customize each to fit different roles from attacker, defender and support. Their skills are pre-set but generally every character can fit any role although they are bound to front or backline so you may want your backline to have a support and your frontline to have a defender. Items range from damaging or healing to adding buffs or debuffs, and with the extensive alchemy system you can min-max and customize items to fit a ton of situations.

The game also has a photo mode for people who like taking pictures, I know I spent a lot of time in it. It's a gorgeous looking game, and the photo mode makes you take all that in even more. The music is generally nothing to write home about, but some tracks stand out and had a great impact on me. My favourites were the exploration tracks for the Volcano and the Underworld. You can even change what music tracks are played and a free DLC lets you pick music from older Atelier games. Personally I put Cyclone, the battle theme from Rorona for the battle music as it's an outstanding track. Sadly the volume mixing seems a bit off on some tracks and from what I found there was no way to manually change it, so it was often drowned out by sound effects, still it's not a huge issue just a minor annoyance.

If you have heard good things about the Atelier series but just haven't gotten around to trying it, I think Ryza is a good place to start. It was a great introduction for me, and I believe it should be for others too!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - Cute little crafting JRPG but really easy

Pros:
- Art direction is great, nails the anime look with everything being pretty vibrant without overdoing it.
- Soundtrack really sets the mood well. Has the feel of a Studio Ghibli soundtrack at times.
- Crafting system is robust and offers a lot of things to unlock
- The game does a good job of giving you a variety of things to do and rewards you for doing them
- Story is decent and characters likable enough

Cons:
- Crafting is maybe a little too complicated and will take a while to understand even with tutorials
- It's super easy to become massively OP after the slightest bit of crafting making combat non-existent.
- No English voice acting, only Japanese. They assume you're a dirty weeb.
- While there's some nice locales and vistas, with everything occurring on or near the same home area makes things lack a typical sense of adventure you might expect in an RPG.

If you really like JRPGS and crafting and don't mind being OP, this is a great game. If you need challenging combat though, you'd be much better off passing this one up.\t\t\t\t\t
