\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary: in my personal opinion, no doubt to be the most amazing atelier game within the franchise, the one and only; also happen to be the best one for the new comers to be introduced to this series.
I’ve played previous atelier game recently and own every atelier game on steam( hadn’t got chance to play them all due to lack of free time), I will compare Ryza to Lydie & Suelle I’ve played recently to prove my statements why Ryza is a true enhanced atelier version and more like an ultimate experience among its ancestors. My perspective will from a new comers’ view since this review will likely to service my friends who put Ryza on wishlists but haven’t played any atelier games at all. Hopefully help them out in a way or two.
Tutorial: first thing first, Atelier games actually have their own original and unique system from the very start of their 1st entry and each last piece added something new to the system a bit, eventually it formed the core mechanics of the gameplay now we’ve seen, a result of complexity & depth alchemy system; it takes quite some time to learn and lots of experiments until you slowly master it. The battle level would go from fairly easy to extremely challenge based on how much you understand the alchemy system and the items you make to suit the situation when you can take the best advantage of it, well, it is where the fun is in my opinion. In addition, different from Lydie & Suelle, Ryza recorded every tip appearing in the game along your journey in the ‘help’ section, in case if you didn’t fully understand the explanation at that point and later decided to check them out again or simply forgot one or two in the later game and needed a review at some time. It is vital to someone new who haven’t played any atelier games before, I mean, this is the whole point of this ‘section’ of my review. For example, like Lydie & Suelle, not every tutorial reappear in help section, some of them only appear once and no way to be found again. I had hard time playing that game as a newbie since some of game tips I didn’t understand when it first explained and later I can only recalled it with my blur memory. Till the very end of the game I realized I missed some very import things that led me missing the true ending, what’s worse? The game never gives a hint or two the entire time, of course it was a very bad example of game tutorial. However Atelier Ryza made sure this doesn’t happen again. Personally, I think it is significant on top of any other experience to talk about first. Atelier games are lengthy, you don’t want to miss something that force you a second play through, not based on your own will.

Alchemy System: This time Gust changed the entire layout of the alchemy receipts: A. now the diagram is more easily understand and clear to you, ex. what you can make next and what materials you haven’t discovered yet in order to make your target items etc. B. A more interesting view than the previous bookmark diagram. C. Even more enhanced system: now the quality of the items show by the numbers of materials you add (compare to L&S), before you add materials, the chat pattern shows you what kinds of materials you need (animal; plant; ore; etc); added element value as a bonus when making things (not gonna go deep with this this part, it is something you should go and explore yourself in game, totally fun to play with. Being honest, so many potentials, so many possibilities to do it your own way, and then battle monsters to test your understandings of them out)

Battle: Battles in Ryza features new elements based on atelier games’ traditional ‘half-real time’ turn-based system: you now focus controlling one character during the battle and can switch your focus character at any time if battle tide changes; fire orders doesn’t pause the enemy’s timeline gives a more intense environment to challenge; new introduced Tactic level gives diverse strategy to fight on monsters from something weak to a powerful boss creature, and save time when farming materials that only monster drops repeatedly. ‘Battle section’ in my opinion is a pure upgraded system. Gust has changed previous boring battles into individual interesting situation via this.

Difficulty: better to say than never, why Ryza is considering the best entry atelier game is: the difficulty is enjoyable. Even with the hardest difficulty, for a person like me knowing what I am doing, it’s not hard at all, but it comes from someone who had spent 150+ hours into previous atelier games lol. When I 1st played Lydie & Suelle, I had headache on normal difficulty, the monsters all looked very cute, however they hit hard like devils have no mercy. I remember I struggled a lot on the fields until I was good at making powerful gears. Pretty sure this doesn’t happen in Ryza, you can play normal difficulty and be confident to bring your first no experienced making gear to test your strength without thinking might flee at any minute.

Story: actually this is the 2nd important thing in this review. Even though Atelier franchise is 20+ years old, have published more than 10 different games. However, I still love Blue Reflection more than any other Gust’s games because its memorable story. Atelier games may win popularity because of its original gameplay, but to me, it never really had some real touching stories that hit my heart. Ryza happens to be the first one that gives this whole ‘alchemy things’ some meanings and more precisely, a ‘heart’ that connect me to Ryza’ s little island village and her friends lives there, there are touching moments for the first time making me think in front of the screen, and I see these story reflect matters that I’ve encountered in real life. Surely they did some impact to my heart, I no longer didn’t care about these story characters, instead truly felt happiness; sorrow; anger; hate for them. Regardless the result, I see this is a successful story is about, producer successfully pulled audiences towards it, not drove them away ...

Flaws
Since I don’t have issues about running this game on PC, my machines run very smooth with Atelier Ryza (a medium build PC with 1050ti, and a high end PC with 2080ti), the performance are both excellent with amazing high quality graphics, I won’t say Ryza did bad on optimization. But since it charges an AAA title’s price, I can understand people demanding proper M&K support, and wanted more advanced graphic settings, I can only say Ryza might be overpriced if you are not a turn-based on JRPG HC fan, and might consider waiting some time for a discount.

I pre-ordered it with £54.99 without regret, it’s a KT standard price tag; in my region, it always £49.99 or £54.99 for the DE version, KT doesn’t specifically rise price or low it down for Ryza. However Atelier Ryza definitely is the first one truly worth this price tag among the entire franchise if you ask me. As a long time fan of Gust’s games, it is hard to find any flaw in this game proves where my praise comes from.

Scarlet’s Score: 9.0/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10+ hours in, I'd say it's a pretty nice game.It has a smaller scale story following a group of friends going on a fun adventure and learning about the world; An enjoyable change of pace compared to some other overly grandiose JRPGs. The combat makes you think without getting overly bogged down in complexity, and the crafting is surprisingly fun and enjoyable, especially if you're into min-maxing gear.

The graphics aren't the greatest, the game isn't super well optimized, and it could definitely stand to be a bit harder (The combat seems balanced around a player not making use of alchemy, even on hard mode.) but overall I'd say it's a pretty solid game so far.

Another bonus is it's actually getting some post launch PC support, with KT adding in new features and options requested by PC players, so it's worth getting just to show support for Japanese devs giving the PC port a little more care.\t\t\t\t\t
