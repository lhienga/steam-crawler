Story
9/10
Solid story with nice character growth
Graphics
8/10
Anime-style clean graphics, a bit too heavy on the hardware given what's show on the screen...
Audio
10/10
Japanese voice acting on anime styled games is a must have, soundtrack is very chill and enjoyable
Battle gameplay
7/10
Fast paced "turn based" gameplay, broken by some op mechanics and very easy overall
Gathering & alchemy gameplay
9/10
Progressive gathering growing with tools quality and super fun alchemy item creation: rarely grindy, mostly at the endgame
Character Design
10/10
Ryza alone is 11/10, might argue about Lent's outfit being too "warrior-like" even when he isn't a warrior yet, but everyone is well designed

PROS:
  • Relaxing
  • Easy to understand alchemy system, yet time consuming for minmaxing
  • New features are unlocked slowly during the entire story, so the player is not overwhelmed with information
  • There is always something to do, either by creating stuff or completing sub quests
  • Cultured girl characters design
  • Steam achievements are not toxic at all and just demand the player to play the game in full

CONS:
  • Maybe too relaxing, it's almost impossible to lose a battle (Normal difficulty) with proper gear progression and without the use of items
  • Gathering might result a bit grindy when gem reduction is unlocked as the player will need a lot of materials to convert into gems
  • Battle items are broken, and they completely break the battle system at the point where spamming them is the easiest way to make enemies permanently stunned in place and win battles

Long version WITH SPOILERS

STORY:
The story round around Reisalin Stout, Ryza in short, a countrygirl daughter of two farmers who hates manual labor and refuses to help in the farm wandering around the village in search of adventure. She has two childhood friends, Lent and Tao, who are a strong tall teenager with combat and brute force in mind, and the other one is just the opposite, a short guy with a nerdy look who likes books and knowledge. They all meet Empel and Lila, two adults who happened to be in Kurken Island for reasons the player will find out during the storyline: Empel is an alchemist and Ryza is instantly charmed by alchemy, so she insists she wants to learn more about it and asks Empel to teach her, while Lent asks Lila, an all-round fighter, to teach him the art of blade. Tao also has a new objective in mind, which is studying an old language, ability that will become a key element later in the story as he will be able to decipher ancient writings. The three protagonists later meet Klaudia, daughter of a travelling merchant and classy girl who wants to try the thrill of adventure, who joins the group with her father's approval after he tests Ryza's alchemy skills. Starting from here, the story is all about the four "adventurers" improving their skills while also discovering some mysteries surrounding Kurken Island and a dark secret about some known legends of the past...

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're not familiar with using a controller, do not bother picking up the game. It's not a good experience learning to use a controller for the first time with this game, but it gets worse if you happen to not have one:

- The game's default keyboard controls make no particular sense whatsoever. I could throw darts at a wall with paper with random alphabet letters on them and it'd make more sense. It also asks you to have three hands since WASD, IJKL -and- mouse buttons are all parts of the default control scheme.
- The control scheme can be remapped... but not from within game. Owing to heavily obviously being the result of someone not really doing any more work than testing if the console version of the game can physically run on a PC and adding a quick emulation for control adjustments, there's no feature anywhere from within the game to change what button on your keyboard represents what button on a controller. You have to play with the nonsense controls and keep a list of what button you'd prefer to use for a particular interaction, then close the game, remap, and retry. Expect to spend a fair while on this if you're determined.
- Speaking of control emulation, the joystick's movement is also emulated. This means even if you've rebound movement and menu control to WASD as would be sane, hitting the appropriate button on your keyboard might not actually result in the movement taking place at all in a menu because the virtual joystick hasn't gotten out of the dead zone yet. You have to hold the button down to move around in menus, and worse still is that once is IS out of the virtual deadzone, it's quite happy to zip around on the menus. Shouldn't have to play a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ rhythm game just to change the volume a little.
- Also, since it's just a 1:1 control emulation that pretends some buttons are others, you can't have more than one button for the same key. I'd like to jump with space, but the key for that is also the back button. I settled on Q (since I was using E for confirm and it's on the other side of W), and had to jump around with Q because I couldn't -also- have space. That's far too complicated for a 2019 AAA release.
- On top of the awful controls, I found myself spamming the confirm key very frequently just to get past all the "thing available!" screens that seemingly every new load space wanted to give me. This got all the more grating with how often I was restarting the game since I couldn't find a save point to load from and skip those pop-ups.

The Yakuza franchise has a bit of a running joke within its community that many people try playing it on KB/M and either suffer a little more for doing so, or wind up switching to controller. "Real Yakuza use a controller", right? But at least it's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ playable. At least those releases, some of which are noticeably janky ports that feel like outsourced work pushed onto a small team, still manage to maintain the basic semblance of pretending that the PC port is viable. In-game remappable controls, allowing multiple inputs and - get this - are even context sensitive!

If you aren't already familiar with using a controller, this is a hard pass. I can use a controller, though I dislike doing so, and I'm still not okay with the time frame of release, or price, of a game that fails to provide considerably basic parts of a PC build of the game. You either support keyboard/mouse, or you don't. The store page is a lie. I was looking forward to working through the franchise since this release was meant to draw in a larger audience, but I'm just not going to bother with 2 or 3 at all.\t\t\t\t\t
