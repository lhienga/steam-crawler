This is my introduction to the Atelier series. The best aspect about this game is the Synthesis system, as standard for the Atelier series. You can get absorbed for hours upon hours in it creating new stuffs or optimising your builds.

My favourite moment has got to be when I created my first 999 quality (max quality) item by accident. I knew that Quality++ and Best Quality works together, I just never bothered to calculate the exact breakpoint of base quality. From that moment, I spent like 20-30 hours just making EVERYTHING else 999 quality as well. The result is me going into the final boss with 999 quality weapon and armor on Lila, absolutely humiliated the final boss. In fact, I am glad that it has 2 phases: the first phase died before I got to unleash Lila's fatal drive (+4 element).

As for the story, I think it has a nice story. Nothing too heavy, but the story does have some interesting revelations later on. For the characters, I thought that everyone likes Lila because of her huge badonkers, but the moment I got her into my party, I knew. I knew that she is going to be main character of my party from that point on. Her fluid moments, fast skills, and being the only character to deal damage in all 4 elements makes her one of the best character in the game. She uses twin claws and her Fatal Drive is tied for first place in my heart (tied with Ryza and Claudia (wait, girls get beautiful Fatal Drives?!?)). All in all, best character Lila.

10/10 would recommend if you like to create and optimize everything, from equipment, to items, to accessory.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: 8.5-9/10 game. Definitely one of my new favorites. I'm a fan, and I can't wait to play more of Atelier. If you're a newcomer to the series, I think even vets can agree that this is the game to play first. Can't wait to experience Ryza 2!

Warning: This is my FIRST Atelier game, and so I'm practically coming from a blank slate. I have NOT beaten the post-game content, but I doubt it will change my thoughts below.

Presentation:
This game is BEAUTIFUL. Visuals look great and the music is gorgeous. Definitely some of my most favorite pieces of music I have heard from a video game in a while.

Gameplay:
The alchemy and material gathering is the bread and butter in this game, and it is downright addicting and enjoyable. There's a certain joy to be had in crafting items and then trying to get the traits and attributes you want. There is a lot of depth to be had in alchemy, and you can practically clear the game by synthesizing the correct items despite your party being somewhat under leveled. It's far from perfect though, the gathering can sometimes feel pace-breaking (since Ryza has to stop and do her animation to gather materials, this can get a bit tedious, but nothing too tiresome). The game also doesn't really give you a guide or hint to do certain side quests (this is neither a good or bad thing depending on you). I found that you practically soft lock yourself near the end if you manage to go about the game without doing the side quests (like I did).

The combat is.... honestly something a bit rough. The real-time component is super enjoyable and engaging. There is a lot of tactical depth to be explored in it, but the way the Core Charge system and changing which party member you control is somewhat baffling. It's stuck in this weird limbo where Item use feels "pasted on" since the only way to gain Core Charges efficiently is to do Quick Actions (which eats Action Points). party member switching is also somewhat awkward at first, as you're encouraged to switch control between party members to trigger your other members' abilities, but it wants you to stay on your current selection until they finish their animation and the hit connects before said abilities trigger, which discourages you then from micro-managing your other party members. It;s frustrating at first to be quite honest but something you adapt and plan around as the game progresses.

I think this is an aspect that's hard to describe in deeper detail and critique succinctly, so all I can say is find out for yourself. It's nothing that's game breaking or would kill interest, but it DOES take time to get used to.

Difficulty wise this isn't a hard game, just some difficulty spikes that are very manageable if you regularly do alchemy and explore for more materials.

Story:
The plot is engaging and is paced really well. My only complaint is that one character feels particularly out of place and did not get as much exposition as the other characters despite being presented as what I feel like someone major I'm talking about Kilo \t\t\t\t\t
