\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing Atelier games since the release of the Arland trilogy. I can confidently say that this is my favorite Atelier game in the series. I love the combat improvements, the characters are amazing like always, the music is top notch even for a series that is full of great music, and the world is stunning.

With all of this being said I do have some negatives. The PC port could be a bit better. The developers have done a good job at fixing a lot of the glaring issues that plagued the game at launch, but some issues still stand. This game is hard to run. I have a 1080ti and have grown accustomed to being able to do 4k60 with most of the games I play, but Ryza refused to run at 60fps even at the lowest settings. I was forced to drop down to 1440p in order to lock the game to 60fps. With a slightly faster gpu I have no doubts that I'll be able to run this game at 4k, but do keep this in mind if you are running weaker hardware at higher resolutions.

With all of that out of the way I still give this game my highest recommendation for any JRPG fan or Atelier fan. This is a must play JRPG and one of the best JRPG's of the past year.\t\t\t\t\t

On your turn, you need to act fast because it will not be on standby mode. Traditionally, on your turn, you'll take your sweet time accessing items, skills, reading descriptions etc and then executes action after, (For example, Persona or Neptunia) but not in this game. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wouldn't recommend this as an introduction to the series. Synthesis is dumbed down heavily, no longer do you have to plan everything out, you can just glue on what's missing after making it with the rebuild system. You can't combine traits anymore, money is useless and the animations for the battle-use items are just lazy and awful.

The ATB-ish battle system is new and refreshing, but as you play the game you'll start to see that it doesn't allow as much creativity as it first seemed. Every battle plays out the same. You build up the tactics level, you spam items (you'll never run out) and you use quick action when the time comes. Buffs and debuffs do not feel like they do anything. On the unlikely event that something survives by the time you get to tactics level 5, you turn on aggressive mode and fatal drive when you run out of gas.

Then there's the difficulty. I played the game on hard and not once did I get a real challenge. It'll be harder if you don't synthesize but that's like ignoring half the game. Also, you have to beat the game to unlock the higher difficulties. If you're somehow not bored out of your mind by the time you get there at least.

The game's alright. Definitely not the "best" Atelier game though. Not by a long shot. If you're new to Atelier and want to check it out, play any game other than this and Nelke. If you're not new, you're really not missing out on much by not picking this up. If you're just plain horny then by all means go ahead. The game will put Ryza's breasts and thighs in front of your face the entire time.\t\t\t\t\t
