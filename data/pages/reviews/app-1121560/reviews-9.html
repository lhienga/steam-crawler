\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis may be one of the best JRPGs out there *IF* you have a heavy interest in item collection, crafting, and management. If none of that interests you, this may just be a mediocre experience of an RPG with a charming and a well crafted story and characters.

I appreciated the layout and design of the island, and how the game makes every area and enemy heavily replayable as you level up and enhance your gear throughout the game. As you progress you'll be able to collect and craft different items using different tools to enhance your gear and craftsmanship. It's such a well thought out game, with every enemy and area having several purposes, even if not accessible early on. It does at times show its age, especially with so many invisible walls where there's no reason for it.

The story really grows on you with the development of characters and friendships, making every character interesting and the way they interact with one another really keeps you invested. I have no major hangups with the game but if you hate crafting and continuously collecting/battling for items, this game would likely be a major pain for you. I did find the first few hours to be frustrating for an overwhelming amount of things the game doesn't tell you or explain, yet it also doesn't stop feeling like the tutorial ever ends as you still unlock important features throughout the entire game. You'll quickly find out all the answers are within the pause menu somewhere, just requires extra work as it's not a game to hold your hand for everything. Normally that'd be good, but there's too much information to start with in this type of game to not inform you of at least some things upfront.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was split between Ryza and Sophie as my first Atelier game. I ended up going with Ryza since it's a more recent title and reviewers said this has the latest QoL features.

The alchemy system seemed overwhelming at first, but ended up making sense as I continued through the story. I ended up getting hooked on the alchemy system, and I ended up becoming overpowered because of it (which is what I like since I'm a filthy casual). This helps compensate for the combat since it's an active turn-based system that doesn't auto-switch characters like older Final Fantasy titles.

Some things I didn't like is it doesn't have the option for a borderless windowed full screen, and you're stuck with 1920x1080 on full screen. For some reason, windowed mode gives you better resolution which is weird. Another thing is on the alchemy screen, the amount of nodes gets your FPS down like crazy. There are times where I go as low as 20 FPS when everything else is a constant 60.

Overall, you can get a good 30+ hrs on this. Story is nothing to write home about though, and it didn't spark any interest to do a replay. This is definitely a good game to get on sale. I wouldn't recommend it at its full price though.\t\t\t\t\t
