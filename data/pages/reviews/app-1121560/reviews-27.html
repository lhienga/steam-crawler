Like any franchise, you have to look at it both from outside and inside the franchise itself.

AS A JRPG

As a JRPG, it's pretty alright. It starts as a slice of life, which has a ton of potential. When every adventure was just The Gang trying to get a secret hideout going, and all the side-quest were getting me acquainted with villagers, I was really enjoying myself. It almost had a Harvest Moon/Stardew Valley feel.

Then the plot felt the urge to do something bigger, and we ended up facing off against bug armies and underworlds and all that. It felt like it lost itself. Too much of an EPIC FANTASY storyline butting into the SLICE OF LIFE storyline and leaving both whimpering.

The crafting is super fun, but a little too messy for your average JRPG. This is the focal point of the game, though. The battles take the back seat. And it feels like it. So if you want a fun battle grinder JRPG, this isn't a great pick.

AS AN ATELIER GAME

As far as Atelier games, Ryza is both more accessible and also not accessible enough. It's trying to bring in new players, but also it has forgotten what information and what ideas make it hard to understand. There were aspects that I was still discovering near the end of the game that had just been sitting in front of me, unused, the whole time.

I can imagine a true simplification where all these ideas are leaked very slowly throughout the whole experience. Traits, for example, which are a integral piece of hardcore item crafting in the series, aren't extremely useful until mid game or later. Still, you see them there and juggle all this info or just plain ignore it because, let's face it, in the heat of battle a 1% increase to Critical Hit Chance is imperceptible.

But then when you master the system, get the right ingredients, or just reach the end of the game, the system is super easy and too exploitable. It's a fun system for sure, but in the grand scheme of Atelier games, not the finest.

So, like the story, we're sitting in a best-of-both-worlds situation where the system is not truly accessible to a newcomer because of the sheer amount of information you have to encounter, but also not as good as the other entries in the series because they simplified it at the point where the nuance should dwell.

The battle system is one I didn't much care for at all. So much is happening all the time that it may as well be that nothing is happening. And I truly dislike a battle system where I am just mashing the attack button so that when my turn comes there's no downtime. I put it in Hard Mode about 15 hours in because I was trying to grind some quests and the enemies kept dying before I could achieve my goal, in part because your teammates will attack non-stop no matter what and if you're even a hair stronger than you ought to be you're going to clean the enemy clocks. Even crazier is that at one point I removed all of my team's good equipment just so that the battle would last long enough for me to reach LV.5 Tactics to try out the supermoves. It is nice they have higher difficulties just in case, though. In the end I beat the superboss on the second-hardest difficulty because why not.

AS A RECOMMENDATION

Yeah, it's good and fun. It is probably the best choice for newcomers, but also some of the stuff it shows off is not the best in show which could push a newcomer away. The day-night system no longer has any weight and so it feels unfinished. The battles are hectic messes so you can't get a true feel of the little pieces of progress you made in the crafting. And the crafting itself is still a wall of info that shows you more than you can even use, creating a potential roadblock if you aren't used to it. I could be better, but it could be worse.

Pro
- Loved the characters, love the way they give each other (particularly Ryza) a bit of banter. Kinda cute and charming
- Combat is fun, big and flashy and not too hard
- Not too grindy, but to be honest the gear synthesis grind was at the end of my patience. Luckily the game is forgiving enough that you don't have to sacrifice your life to make the BEST GEAR EVAH. I went some way, 999 quality all round but never went beyond mid level gear and I finished the game pretty easily. This is good planning on their part.
- Synthesis is very deep and absorbing
- Music is 7/10 but high quality
- Boobs

Con
- Shonky animation outside combat. Just jump and you see what I mean
- Minimal explanation of synthesis tricks, expect to spend a lot of time Googling. I also found it a bit hard to find quality guides that weren't video (shudder)
- Ridiculours boobs
