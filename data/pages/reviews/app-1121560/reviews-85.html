\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't be intimidated by the keyboard mapping my friend! Here is what to do:

Buy an X1 joystick and plug it in.

Oh of course I'm joking. Sorry. Here is what you really should do if you don't have a joystick and found the key mapping to be confusing:

**Write the key mappings down on a piece of paper.**

Or if you have a printer just print the screenshot of the key mapping configuration tool. Displaying the screenshot with your cellphone is also good. You then play the game and every time a key tip in things like ABXY pops up and you don't know which key you should press, read that piece of paper and go on. In tens of minutes you'll get used to it and soon muscle memory will develop and everything now feels natural.

I did this with Atelier Rorona and bought a joystick after around 70 hours of playing to see if it will feel better. I threw it away after some 10 minutes because the keyboard now feels more natural for me.


BTW, it looks like that Atelier games use the same default key mapping, so go through the process once, and you are good to go with other titles. Ryza's key mapping is super comfortable for me because of my experience with Rorona.

And of course, one don't need a mouse to become an alchemist :)

Some other tips:

- The game currently defaults to run with Intel integrated graphics and you must manually configure your graphic driver to run it with your proper GPU.
- Screen width and height can be changed with the ini file which should be in your document folder, under path like C:\\Users\\<user_name>\\Documents\\KoeiTecmo\\Atelier Ryza . Go for some 4K if you have a good monitor.
- If you didn't find the key mapping configuration tool then you probably are playing the game windowed@1280*720. Now is a good time to find that configuration tool.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent: Visuals, Character Design, Battles, Crafting
The character designs and personalities of the main cast can seem a bit cliche, but the game is able to express their personalities and quirks quite well within the confines of the plot.

Fighting has switched to a real time system, which makes battles more dynamic and engaging.

Alchemy and crafting in this game is much more nuanced and developed than previous entries.

----
Passable: Plot, Map Design, UI
The plot itself is passable. It's actually quite good for an Atelier game. However, the writing is standard at best and a bit bland at worst.
The map itself sure is pretty, but very little of note actually happens in any of the stages. The main towns don't really feel alive, as a great number of NPCs can't be interacted with at all.
The UI is clean and uncluttered, passable.
----------
Poor: Controls, Traveling

As many others will have pointed out, the game does not have mouse + keyboard support. All gameplay buttons will happen on the keyboard exclusively. The default keybinds are atrocious, as they attempt to simulate the right analog stick on the controller using the "RTYG" keyset on the keyboard. In addition, the game's key prompts will all be exclusively for the controller. This extreme inconvenience almost makes the game unplayable as you're forced to remember which key on your keyboard corresponds to each button on the controller. This is absolutely horrendous as battles are real time, meaning that you'll spend quite a few fights crippled until you figure out how your controls work.

Moving from stage to stage is the most uneventful thing you can do in this game. You simply walk up to a signboard and instantly teleport. While this is awfully convenient, it leaves something to be desired.
-------
Overall, Ryza is a great game with great design. I highly suggest you play it with a controller to avoid the horrendous mess that is their keyboard controls.
Is the game worth the $60 price tag? No. You should wait for a sale and pick it up for $40 or less.\t\t\t\t\t

100% recommended.

The port it's better than in previous titles, but it still suffers from the limited settings you're able to tweak, still you can tweak all the KB bindings and adapt it to your taste.

I'm not going to discourage people from getting it though, I bought a controller after trying to play it for 40 mins.

Here is what I think the keyboard mapping is:

WASD - for strafing and menuing
Arrow Keys - Also for menuing but also the up arrow is log and down arrow is map
RYTG - Rotate camera left right up down
K - A button
L - B button
i - Y Button
j - X button
Enter - the menu button thing on the controller
H - center camera forward
OU - Zoom in and out

----

As for the actual game, its pretty good.

Atelier games all fill the specific niche of being an alchemist, so you gather and craft to support your party in combat.

The combat being made pseudo-real time speeds up encounters considerably but makes difficult fights a bit harder.

After playing for over 10 hours, I'm still getting more features/mechanics introduced that make the alchemy and gearing deeper.

So if you really like min/maxing in JRPGs this is pretty appealing.\t\t\t\t\t
