\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to enjoy the game but after the wait and install, I have to say I am deeply unimpressed. Started the game and before I even got through the intro, I noticed something was amiss. I had the game running at 60fps, but the graphics looked weird. I turned the game off and started troubleshooting and came across numerous posts stating the game needs to stay locked at 30fps for some things (like the clothing physics) to work. Capped the game at 30 and like magic, the clothes started billowing properly.

Not a huge issue, I guess. I have played plenty of games in my life at 30FPS. It isn't the end of the world. I start my adventure but quickly realize the buttons need remapping. Open the menu to discover the options... or rather lack thereof. Very few options, abysmal remapping options, no sound options outside a few sliders... Ok, I guess they might have had a budget. Not every game needs triple A game level options.

Start the main quest and.... game is tearing... I have .2 hours in the game and have already had to quit out three times to fix graphical issues; fullscreen is not in the game it must be entered in outside menu, downgrade and lock FPS at 30, and finally it starts to tear? No thank you. I might come back after a few patches and change my review, but honestly I can't remember the last time I have had so many issues in such a short amount of time. Feels like the game wasn't optimized for PC at all, just ported.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very easily the best-looking and most polished Atelier to date, and an excellent entry point for beginners. If you've been hesitant on giving the series a shot, this is a good chance!
The OST is top-notch, and the map designs are fantastic. Combined with the large visual overhaul, the game as a whole looks gorgeous.
For me, the game's biggest strength compared to its predecessors is probably its alchemy system. Not only is the act of actually crafting more satisfying than ever before, there'se also tools added to remove the tediousness previous games suffered from and make alchemy really accessible to anyone. The depth is still there, and it's possible to spend hours working on that one single item, but never in this game did I ever feel like crafting was a chore.
The biggest talking point in this game is certainly its combat system. When a series that has stuck with very traditional turn-based combat for over 20 years goes real-time, it's a big gamble but I'd say Gust definitely pulled it off. While it intially confused me, the combat system in Ryza felt really good as soon as I got used to it, to the point where late in the game I would be repeatedly fighting the optional bosses just for fun - and that's what's most important to me, that the combat is FUN, which it certainly is.
Most veterans would argue that the game is overall on the easier side, but there is still plenty to do and systems to explore for the more dedicated players. Even if you're not one of them, there's still an excellent game to be played.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot recommend this game in it's current state.

Let's face it, there are people who prefer to play their games with keyboard and mouse over controller. I personally prefer the controller side of the table, but I can understand the frustrations with keyboard mapping. They are nonsensical and require a fair amount of configuration before beginning the game.

Secondly, The lack of graphical options on this game baffles me when previous entries allowed you to change a few more options. This is a grave oversight and locks several modifications that can make the game run better away from the user. Koei Tecmo Games was not fair with this port and it seems borderline lazy. I know developing is a hard gig, but anyone can copy and paste. If you are going to do it, at least put effort into the options because QA had to test this game on several different machines.

Finally, I come to my biggest issue of all:

THIS GAME CRASHES A LOT.
...I mean, really, a lot.

Want to go into Old Town? Crash. Needing to go into Fairystone Forest to gather Nameless Grass? Looks like you will be gathering from your desktop instead. I even had a crash just changing into the deluxe edition outfits! Unfortunately, these issues are probably stemming from the "Poor Optimization" everyone is referring to.

However, I can say that running on a laptop RTX2060, The game is beautiful and smooth as butter. From what I have seen with little combat and mostly tutorial running around the town, I haven't seen any issues...aside from the anti-aliasing for the true graphics connoisseurs. :)

I'm not going to lie, initially, I had the thought to refund it. I have been loving the Atelier Series since Marie and have been trying to play through each one of them. When I saw the trailer for Ryza, I was sold. While the system configuration options are technically minor gripes, the crashes made me want to use my money elsewhere. However, hope for a patch or two combined with an admittedly biased love for the series is compelling me to hold out for a little longer.

All of that being said, No one will fault you for buying it. The game, when it works, is incredible. If you are set on it, hold out for patches. By all means. That is why I wrote my review. I wanted everyone, including Koei Tecmo Games, to be informed of various grievances in terms of the game's performance and omissions. If you still buy it despite all of the issues mentioned here, I can only assume that you are optimistic and you really want this game.

As an aside, I have not really noticed many people, if any at all mention having crashes like myself. It has me wondering if it is a "Localized Aurora Borealis" within my machine. (Kudos to you if you got the reference!)

Thanks for reading my review! I hope it gets patched and we can all enjoy it the way that we want! Happy Gaming!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGraphics improved in comparsion to other games of the series.
After 10+ hours of playing my thoughts about the game:
-New combat system is hectic at first but after getting used to it it's been nice. The battles are a lot faster than they were in previous games, which is pretty nice when you just want to farm mobs. Going through all the motions of a turn based combat system just to kill a slime felt like a waste of time previously. On the other hand support type characters feel a lot less useful, since you share action points across the party you'd rather use it on attack abilities than healing. Which leaves the support character without much of a purpose, one attack caster and two auto attacker tanks seem like a better composition. Not that you really need to minmax in this game but still the role support characters had in the turn based combat system seem obsolete here.
-New synthesis system is pretty nice, it is also a lot faster than the previous iterations. You can still go into depth when making items but the auto synthesis saves a lot of time. When you have to make 5 planks for a quest or something then pressing it once is a lot nicer than going through the whole synthesis for 5 items that you'll just hand in. The high quality auto synthesis also makes pretty decent items so if you don't feel like bothering to make 3 pieces of armor for everyone in your party then it's not awful. The core charge system is also pretty nice. Replenishing items again goes into the category of faffing around for a long time, especially if you don't have Cornelia in the game. A lot of changes seem like just cut down the faffing around you would otherwise do in this game. One annoying part of it though is that you can recharge your core charges in battle by disabling another item temporarily, but it takes a few seconds to do that and if someone does a special attack it plays a cutscene and that cancels it, I wish it was just a click and not a long bar.
-On that topic there's no Cornelia shop in this game, or so far I haven't found it. For people who haven't played the Mysterious series Cornelia was a character who would replicate simple items for you for a fee. So if you made an ingot, cloth, bomb, potion etc that you like then you didn't have to make it from scratch after using it. It's just a bit of a quality of life thing but I do prefer having a shop like hers.
- The main characters are all fleshed out and well done, even the ones I didn't expect to like I ended liking. Their stories can be unexpectedly heavy sometimes but the game still manages to maintain a laid back atmosphere. On the other hand the side characters are really forgettable. In the Mysterious games I really liked doing quests and watching events for Fritz, Drossel, Cornelia, Tess, Leon, Ilmeria, Lucia, the returning characters like Hagel and Pamela but in Ryza I don't care about Daniel, Peter, Pat, Cynthia, Gina or any of the other villagers. I suppose it does give you the feeling of living in a village on a secluded island but still I would prefer if the side characters were a bit more colorful.
-The story and the world so far feels like on the same level as the Mysterious games, it's there, it's an interesting and unique enough fantasy world but not as engaging as the Dusk series was.
Overall many aspects have been streamlined and improved, I would personally like some quality of life additions and more engaging side content but those are just my subjective feelings.\t\t\t\t\t
