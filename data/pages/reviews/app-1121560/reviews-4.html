\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI overall enjoyed myself!

If you are an interested fan of JRPGs, but don't know anything about Atelier Ryza, few caveats:
- You will spend 80% of your time either gathering resources or crafting
- The rest is split between story and fighting

This is not an action/battle heavy JRPG, but the story was good and it was fun! Indeed, I usually don't like crafting and grinding for materials, but this one kept me entertained for the full 60 hours I spent on it.

Also, normal difficulty is pretty easy. I only crafted upper-mid gear for the main story boss (quality around 300-400) and had no problem taking her down. Luckily, you can change difficulty on the fly so if you want more or less challenge you can get that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, i knew this game from the collaboration with Azur Lane. I have all six of them at level 125 and used them frequently in operation siren and events. They are great ships. I thought why not give this game a try? So I did. Overall this game is really fun. I got all the achievements on steams. The story is interesting. The story starts slow in the beginning and proceeds to be okay in the middle but the ending is quite rushed. There is some plot twists here and there. All I can say is that it is an amazing game.

There are some issues I have with this game.

1. There is a bug that prevents the quest of the Panicking Boy: 5 from starting. I spent one week trying to figure out how to solve this and in the end, I gave up and restarted the game from the very beginning. I heard another person have this issue but there is no solution to it. I lost 12 hours of progress because of this. Quite painful actually.

2. The storage limit in the base is ridiculous. 3000 item limit? Bruh. Give us more lmao. I almost maxed out those limits and have to frequently clean it up. Kinda annoying.

3. The discard-all and item filters need improvement. Manually selecting all the items for discarding is a waste of time.

4. U need a controller for this game since it doesn't support a mouse.

Well, that is all from me. Good game. Give it a try. I will go and try the Atelier Ryza 2: Lost Legends & the Secret Fairy. See u there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is much better than I expected. The effectiveness of the characters is governed by equipment and items rather than equipment and levels, so you can get fairly high in level and still be considered weak if you haven't crafted better items or equipment: Abilities are strengthened more through equipment than skill improvements, and combative items are more powerful than abilities when those items are crafted well.

It is a prissy variant of an old-school jrpg style game but the gameplay is up there, especially if you stick with it through some of the early plot. Mid-Late game makes you consider what you need to craft a lot more than the early game because the enemies become much more difficult, so patience is rewarded. The game scales very well as you progress the story. The story is somewhat lackluster but that also improved later at around the mid-point. I'm only saying this because I assume I reached the mid-point at the time of this review.

The reviews undersell the crafting system in this game: The best of all games I've experienced in that regard but it's hard to get behind because of the constant squealing and talking in the process. If you mute the vocals it may be worth 5 stars because of the crafting and combat system being as interesting as they are. The flexibility and control that you have over the combat system is pretty impressive. I find it hard to explain exactly why the combat system is better than most turn based games . . . In terms of maximizing efficiency, this is one of the hardest as a turn-based game: I mean that fully utilizing the capability of the combat system is extremely difficult, even though the actual fights are nowhere near as difficult. I think most players don't appreciate it because you generally end up winning accidentally, rather than through deliberate effort; however, when you figure out how to fully control the combat system, it is one of the best I've seen as well, with respect to turn-based games: An enemy can be stun locked to inaction until death, but that is ridiculously difficult to pull off and I've only done that to non-boss enemies.

It is just significantly better than I expected, but also extremely annoying. The recommendation has nothing to do with the story, the setting, graphics, cuteness, breasts, thighs, or whatever else: pure gameplay has me saying this may be worth 5 stars. I actually wish the setting, graphics, theme, and plot were all different; but that really doesn't matter, this is still a good game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish there was a "meh" option rather than binary like/dislike for Steam reviews for exactly this situation.

I did enjoy parts of the game and I liked it enough to play it for 40+ hours but I sure don't love it either. This is a very tepid recommendation.

What I like: Very colorful good looking JRPG that will run on modest hardware. The game really does look nice due to the art direction more than anything despite not being taxing to run. I played this mostly on the Steamdeck and it ran well. The game's tone also matched the visual style and the game in general is a cheerful slice of life adventure shared by friends. Good positive stuff and it was nice playing something that wasn't trying to be dark, brooding or in any way edgy. The alchemy system also has a lot of play to it and it is fun figuring out how you can stretch it to make equipment that you didn't think you could make at first glance. I had a lot of fun playing around with it. The soundtrack is also nice, upbeat and cheery and works well with the game. I dig it though it is pretty "anime" sounding which might be a turn off for some (not me).

What I neither liked nor disliked: the combat system. It is basically a more hyper version of the Final Fantasy active time system from years ago. It is really pretty fast paced which makes battles move but at the same time it leads to some awkward situations where things just quickly spiral out of control and make it hard to recover. The need to have a quick/responsive battle interface also limits somewhat the actions you have access to but this is mitigated a bit by building up to better and more interesting techniques through basic attacks. It mostly works, I didn't hate it, but I didn't like it either. Thankfully there are no random encounters so that is a big plus at least.

What I did not like: This game really really expects you to master the alchemy system and make some really killer equipment but it doesn't bother to push you in that direction (or warn you that you are doing it wrong) until you get to the last half of the last dungeon when the game takes a big difficulty spike. I would have had to grind quite a bit to finish it so I opted for watching the last boss fight and ending on YouTube.

The game world is also pretty limited. It seems like there should be more to explore and do but a lot of the game feels pretty artificially constrained as far as where you can go and what you can do. Fast travel was a nice option and appreciated but this game could have done with some more world building and interesting places to explore. Considering that ability gating is present (which I like) there wasn't nearly enough to go back and explore once you unlocked certain things.

Also there were more characters to use than I expected there to be and I didn't really feel like I got a great reason to use a lot of the ones that I got later in the game. It was nice to have them but felt a touch tacked on.

I also suspect that some will be turned off by the low stakes, slice of life feel of the game but I thought it works so I won't listed that as a con for me but it probably will be for some.

Overall this is a "fine" game, just not groundbreaking and not quite good enough for me to recommend it without reservations but it certainly isn't bad and it is worth a try if you want something with a very different and upbeat approach to its story. At some point I'll likely check out the sequels since they seem to address at least some of the issues I had with this one, especially in regards to world building.\t\t\t\t\t
