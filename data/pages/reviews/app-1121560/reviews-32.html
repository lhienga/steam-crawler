\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJRPG with a heavy focus on a fairly unique crafting system. Story is really nothing to write home about (pretty generic) put combat is pretty fun and I had good time crafting and figuring out how to make the best items. Dialog is Japanese only, which I think is a lot of the reason this game is under the radar. No biggie if you're used to subbed Anime.

Things I liked:
-Robust crafting system
-Decent combat system
-Casual friendly

Things I disliked:
-Story, it was pretty generic but the characters were likeable.
-Most of the time is spent crafting just because the crafting menu is missing some key features like jumping into a recipe from a different one. After you spent 10 mins chaining only to find out you need an item you didn't craft.

Overall, worth the $25 I spent on steam sell for a run-of-the-mill JRPG with a good crafting mechanic. Looking forward to playing the sequel if it goes lower in price.

Would recommend if you're a fan of JRPGs and like experimenting with crafting mechanics.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like grinding this game is for you

+The depth of the grind is very insane, alchemy gives such a huge amount of choices. Getting max quality weapons and items is just so satisfying. A different grind from normal JRPGs since the level of characters is nothing compared to their equipment here. The main focus of this game is the alchemy and it really delivers.

+The combat is very fun. I really enjoyed the tactics level ups and the item sacrifices and the micromanaging you really have to do to optimize your combat. It really felt like it rewarded mastery of the combat. Maybe if you really grind your heart out and get the best equipment possible (which I eventually tried for) then you can just brute force your way through the game but getting to that point you must actually think through the battles. They are very engaging as just like Final Fantasy 7, they don't wait for you to do your actions and it is an active form of turn-based combat.

+I wanted to just say the CC gauge management in this game is actually fairly difficult to get used to but very satisfying to use. I really enjoyed the way items are dealt with in this game. AP management as well in this game is actually so interesting. The combat was truly really deep and enjoyable.

+On this note, since the combat was deep and enjoyable, the dam grind of doing the same thing over and over again was just so dam satisfying. The loop was really fun and the dam fights with harder enemies were by no means a cakewalk even if I did grind. The secret bosses were not easy by any means and really felt rewarding once you have grinded enough to beat them.

+People who tend to hoard consumables will finally use items. I'm the type to hoard consumables and never use them but here you can just give consumable to everyone even if they won't use it at least you have fodder for your CC. Items finally really feel useful even if they are just in your back pocket. In fact items are the main draw of combat and the main focus of the game which felt different in a good way.

+The secret bosses are godlike. When you see them you will understand. Excellent design choices.

+The soundtrack is great. The anime intro you get to see when the game starts up is actually a banger. Normally would skip for other games but they did a great job in this game. No the most standout of soundtracks but does fit the vibe of the game very well. The tutorial text box sound that plays is way too much of a banger, I don't click out of tutorials immediately cause of it.

+None of the 6 characters are better than the other really and you get to build and use the team you really want without any repercussion. This is mostly because stats are really based on how well you grind for items for the characters and how well you use alchemy. This way stats are really dependent on gear and how well thought out the gear is in this game. I personally used the 3 girls for scientific reasons alone and no other reason.

+I already mentioned the grind but dam the numbers on your stats that you get to see truly makes the game. Nothing like farming to up your items for hours for those fancy numbers. Definitely one for all the people who are willing to grind doing the same mindless things over and over.

+I already mentioned this but dam the alchemy in this game. Such a deep system and can make the same item have completely different stats. The whole game actually revolved around this mechanic. The grind is worthless without a great understanding of the alchemy system. This may seem like a negative but dam it is really really fun to tinker around with. Anyone obsessed with getting ultimate gear will have a blast with this system.

+Ryza

-Small world and repetitive scenery. You will be in the same town forever.

-Story was boring. I don't want to spoil too much but honestly the island didn't feel so nice anyway to begin with.

Came for Ryza, stayed for the grind\t\t\t\t\t

Overview
At best I'm only about a third of the way through this game, but I'm really enjoying it so far. It has a cute main character and story, great music and pretty good graphics. The game revolves around alchemy and crafting, and from what I understand you can break the game fairly easily with a little effort. I'm unsure how necessary this is, as so far the game isn't too difficult and I'm swimming in raw materials. I can tell that the synthesis system really scratches my completionist itch though, I feel compelled to make the best stuff possible and the ease with which it is possible is simply joyful. The numerous ways of fighting, outrageous amount of equipment and the ability to make all of your items super strong (or weak) offers an incredible amount of replayability. If you don't mind constantly grinding for materials, which is also made easier via alchemy. Again, I haven't finished the game but I can say it is the kind of game that I will gladly replay multiple times. (Even just to listen to the music, which has some samples from other games from the publisher)

Story
I'll edit this (If I remember) once I beat the game, but at between 1/4 and 1/3 of the way through the game's story it seems to be a decent combination of light-hearted slice-of-life and personal growth/coming of age type of story. The game starts in a small town on an island that is fairly isolated from the rest of the world. The cliches of small town folks being distrustful of outsiders and life always being the same come into play, and naturally the main characters are the kinds of kids that want to escape that life. An early encounter leads to Ryza (the main character whose name is in the title and so not a spoiler) studying alchemy. Things snowball from there leading to numerous changes in her life and her friends and "enemies" as well.

Gameplay
The main game loop is basically wander around gathering materials and fighting monsters in RPG battles, go back to your atelier to drop off the materials, make stuff with alchemy so you can get stronger equipment/items and open new paths. This lets you gather more and better quality materials, which loops until you get bored or too strong and just steamroll through everything. If you didn't get the idea from the game description, let alone the previous sentences, crafting items with alchemy is a core component of the game. Healing, buffing and attacking items, weapons, armor and accessories can all be made and form the core of gameplay loop. You have to make certain items to progress in the game, either because the story won't progress or because you need access to another area to continue the story.

The main point of crafting though is to make good equipment that lets you blow the enemy away with little to no effort. Recipes can change during crafting, letting you make new items or better equipment. Your weapons, for instance, evolve from one form to another during crafting. You need certain materials to craft items, and better items need better materials. Weapons in particular need metal that is crafted/evolved from various ores. By the time you reach the first real boss you can evolve your weapons to their third tier if you take the time to craft gathering tools and forage for items.

That leads to the next bit though. This game is pretty easy so far. I made decently buffed tier two armor, but I only slightly boosted tier one weapons with some slightly boosted materials. Despite this, the first boss wasn't a challenge. Most random mobs are pretty easily handled with regular attacks and the occasional skill or attack item. I only lost one fight, which isn't a game over: You die, you just lose some random materials in your basket. No money lost, no equipment ruined, just some lost items which are easily replaced. You are free to experiment with how to fight, what to make and who to bring to a fight. From what I've heard, previous games in the Atelier franchise had time limits. That isn't the case at all for Ryza, you may have to make some stuff or wait a day or three of game time; but you are never rushed to accomplish anything by the game. I've read guides that mention that with strong enough armor the post-game superboss only does one point of damage to you. Making that kind of equipment takes some effort, but yeah.

Otherwise, there are plenty of side quests. Requests made by villagers or people you find out in the world will reward you with anything from materials to items and recipes to make items or equipment. Then there are party quests which give you passive abilities to boost your combat prowess. Requests range from simply watching a scene, fetch quests or making an item with alchemy. Party quests require performing certain actions, anything form making x number of items with alchemy to using certain techniques during combat.

Graphics and sound
Let me make this simple. The game came out on the PS4 back in 2019. Its a fairly recent game, so the graphics are up to par. Areas are fairly well detailed, there's lots of variation between locales and times of day. Exploring is a pleasure, and there's a photo mode to really let you look around. Unfortunately, there are next to no graphic settings to tweak on PC. It still looks good, great even; but you can't really refine it any. I've noticed some texture issues here and there. A "line" that moves with you through certain areas that seems to be related to draw distance(?), a few times where textures flicker either constantly or when you move (the castle that the first boss is in was where I noticed this constantly, there's an area with some rubble over a tile and the tile's texture flickers and transposes the opposite side of the tile) but overall they aren't too problematic.

The sound on the other hand, is fantastic. Some area's music reminded me of Ghibli music and a bit of Ni No Kuni, just to give an idea of the quality. The music obviously loops and as should be expected of a modern game, it does so seamlessly. Besides there being a good variety of songs from this game, you can change the BGM of any area to another's and even use music from previous games in the Atelier series and other games from Gust. It looks like you can even have multiple songs for a single area, so I guess it will cycle between your choices. I've just used the BGM selection screen as a jukebox though.

Tldr;
All in all, I can strongly recommend this game. It has lots to do and look at, and I've yet to get bored because of it. The graphics are good, and the sound is great. Combat is fairly standard real-time combat like various Final Fantasy games (no ATB bar, but scrolling icons with character/monster pictures) that's not too difficult. There's no real penalty for dying, and items and money are fairly easy to come by. There are a fair amount of side quests and the story is at least passable, its nice to see how people can grow and change and the characters are nice/relatable enough that I want to see how things end up. The fact that I can tell that just about anything can be overpowered makes me think that there's more than a few ways to play through the game and I know I'll be replaying this awhile after I beat it.\t\t\t\t\t
