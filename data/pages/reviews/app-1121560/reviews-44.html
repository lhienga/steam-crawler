\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst game of the Ryza series.

You play as Reisalin Stout, or Ryza for short, a girl who is eager to go on adventures, with her friends, Lent and Tao. She tries her best to leave the town on every occasions, even if it means running away from tasks asked by her parents. While adventuring outside of the island, she learns about alchemy from a wandering alchemist and instantly get interested about it. With their help, she ends up exploring the world of Alchemy, while doing her things and helping the town.

Atelier Ryza is probably the most user friendly Atelier game there is, but it also has its downsides. First of all, it is probably the shortest and easiest game of the bunch. It brings a completely new battle system, one that doesn't seem to be too well received by older fans. It also lacks english voices, which completely killed my excitement less than a minute into the game; but I can overlook that.

Regarding the combat system, it's kind of a mix bag of goods and bads. I think my favorite part was the item usage system. You can equip items to each character's "Core" and using them consumes "CC"(party wide), which can be renewed by temporarily disabling one of the items equipped in the party. I did not mind the active part of the fight. However, what I enjoyed less was having to build up AP and increase the Tactics Level. It made fighting normal monsters feel like a drag. The worst part is the initial first 3 levels of Tactics. At level 1, you can only normal attack once(1 AP/attack) and at level 2, twice. From 3 and beyond, you can normal attack three times, but getting there takes a decent amount of time, if you don't have items that generates AP. Once you're set past level 3, it isn't so bad. However, using a Fatal Drive or party members dieing reduces your Tactics Level.

That aside, the world was a lot of fun to explore, with no time limit! As you progress, you unlock new gathering tools, which lets you collect new materials from several gathering spots. Some gathering spots can be gathered by multiple tools, but will yield different materials based on the tool used. There is more features that get unlocked, but I don't feel like any of them deserve any attention; they were okay.

The alchemy system in this game has to be the best one I've seen in this brand. While the synthesis tree of each item was fun to play with, the best addition was the rebuild function, which made things so much easier. That feature lets you add into items that are already built by using gems and increasing the item level(which cannot exceed your alchemy level), whether it's to change the item's traits or finish unlocking all of the effects in its synethesis tree. Gems are obtained by destroying any items found or made. The dupe mechanic was tied with the gem currency, reducing the stress on the normal currency massively, to the point that.. I never used it outside of buying recipe books at the shops. Speaking of recipes, most of them are hidden within other recipes, which I personally enjoyed.

Overall, it is a great game, very loose and relaxing, but it is dragged down by its length, its combat system and its difficulty. However, nothing is perfect. I still consider Atelier Ryza my favorite Atelier among the ones I've played, by far.
