\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay: Real time RPG, you can swap around which character you control while the others are control by an AI. Its not complex but you can do various things like skills, items, simple attack, etc. Its feels nice and fast.

Graphics: They boosted the standard for atelier games quite a bit, its pretty and has some nice locations.

Music: Ateliers has always had a good BGM with plenty of wind instruments. For this entry I couldn't find a piece that blew my mind, but it was consistent.

Plot: This became the weakest point for ateliers since the Arland saga on PS3. This entry manages to steer the plot into something that isn't "some things happen while you do alchemy". For atelier standards is quite good, but from RPG in generals its suffer from bad pacing (slow at the beginning and way too fast in the final third of the game). The characters are likable and have some sort of dream/goal that is more or less followed through the game, so they don't end up like lifeless drones devoid of personality.

Length: Took me 40 hours to finish it, and it still has some replay value. You can finish it faster if you don't take your time. Some days I played it without advancing the plot at all, simply gathering and doing a lot of synthesis.

Summary: It was fun to play and I loved the characters (Ryza specially). I can see that a lot of love was put into this game, but I might be biased since I have played most atelier since Iris. By atelier standards I think this end up in quite high as one of the best entries, and by RPG in general, this is a solid 8. Take into account this is without taking into account the port issues, and there's quite a bit of critic about those, which is understandable, but I have played it anyway and I regret nothing.\t\t\t\t\t

Here's what you should know if you're new to the series. A level up gives you +1 to each of your stats (mostly). That's it. Getting a better version OF THE SAME WEAPON can easily be 15 of each stat (and maybe +25 in the stats you care about).

This game can be broken wide open because of this. But that's one of the strong things about it. In a game where you're concerned with being a better and better alchemist, it should be a matter of course that being a better alchemist is better than being a better fighter.

Break the game. It wants you to, and it's easier than other games in the series. Then realize you spent 6 hours crafting the perfect bomb instead of 6 hours grinding enemies and start committing what can only be described as serious war crimes against cute animals.\t\t\t\t\t
