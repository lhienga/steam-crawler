\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeriously overpriced for what it is. Get this on deep discount.

Pros:
Great crafting system, but very low difficulty game, especially if you farm a bit and craft well. Story is basic, slice of life, and very much kid age centric (even more than usual for jrpg). Technically it's well executed, I have no problems with the PC port (but controller is mendatory). The launcher not supporting the gamepad is annoying on steamlink. Finally, I'm not one to turn down fan service, but Lyla's breasts are so large they are grotesque and really detrimental to the immersion given the rest of the crew/world.

Cons:
Don't make the same mistake I did. Keep progressing the story until things are too hard, and then only craft as a response. Doing it the other way around (exhausing crafting option before advancing the story) results in a very lame and unchallenging experience. And you are basically wasting your time. I spent 80h on a 40-50h game. In addition none of the fights were fun, because everything died super fast and you didn't really have time use your skills and strategize. Building tactics 5 was impossible (pre apocalypse clock) on anything except the hardest fight, and only if you held back.

This game REALLY needs more difficulty settings during the first playthrough or less crafting tiers. I wasted so much time gathering materials and upgrading my gear because the crafting supported it. After basically one-shoting the end boss on 'hard' (which is the highest available), I loaded my save and decided to try it again with my previous weapons and armors. Defeating the boss on hard was easy with my tier 3 weapons, which means I probably spent a few dozens hours maxing tiers 4 and 5 for no reason. This is aggravating enough that I'm switching my review to negative.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWanted to wait until I finished the storyline before reviewing the game.

While I've played other games by KOEI TECMO for some reason I never came across the Atelier Ryza games until a good friend very enthusiastically pointed the series out and to start off with this one and I waited until it was on sale. Starting on a small island you play as Ryza, a girl seeking adventure alongside her two friends not knowing that their big adventure will take them to the mainland while discovering the hidden secrets of their home Island and mysterious kingdom long lost to a force ready to return to their realm. While discovering locations and doing quests you are busy learning you're new passion of alchemy.

You will be creating various recipies with the many resources you come across you're travels with many of these used to help villagers with their side quest's but to also arm and protect yourself and you're team. As you progress further you will be able to improve already made items as long as you have the resources and alchemy level. The resources collected or made will fall into several categories, elements and even rank so as you'd expect the higher the rank the better it is and once you can duplicate items it will save a lot of time spent gathering resources again to make that item. A alchemy level is also in place and the higher it is means you get access to build more powerful item's and bring already built items to that level so remember to keep creating.

As for battles you also level up there as well and the battle's aren't too hectic as long as you remember to keep you're equipment in line. A point system is on place which can be filled by normal attacks or doing requests called out by teammates which will result in them performing special attacks. I found the battles pace more relaxing and usually less hectic than other RPG's I've played in the past like Final Fantasy and Star Ocean.

Overall I really enjoyed the game as it was relaxing but also engaging when it needed to be and I look forward to playing the next one so a BIG thanks to friend-chan for pointing this one out. As for the thicc thighs people keep pointing out, YES I noticed and they are nice but I'm sure that's not the only thing that was being looked at ;)\t\t\t\t\t

I'm currently at 23 hours as of (4/16/2021) And I still haven't gotten the hammer or fought a 'real' boss. I think I'll still be getting them, even at the end. Not like it's a problem. Just wanted to point that out.

Port? It's a very janky/clunky PC port. I don't own a controller to play games that require it so I'm forced to use a mouse and keyboard. (You can enable the house in the settings before launching the game from the mini launcher.) I found this to be a problem for a while but I got used to it pretty quickly. Mainly because the game gives you a link to their official site. There is also a direct ‘manual’ link in the mini launcher. I’ve referenced this Control cheat sheet a lot. As for all the lag issues or other issues people are talking about… I’m thankful I haven’t ran into any so far. The only problem I would say I have is. I play the game in 1600x900 which I’m not used to but just like the controls it didn’t really bother me. The game doesn’t act well when I try to tab out from it, kinda like Genshin Impact where it minimizes when you tab out. Of course these aren’t the only two games I’ve played that didn’t like it when I tab out but I’ve had to make/do a lot of work around for these sort of things or just get used to the specified game not liking me tabbing out. (Yes, I did keep to the default key layout of the game but changed the run to shift which originally was RB.)


For a while I got annoyed that the ‘adult’ characters kept doubting me and my friend’s abilities. (besides our mentors.) But to my surprise they stopped doubting me and my friends as I was getting annoyed. I got the other cover girl (Klaudia) about 20 hours into the game and switched out the four eyed kid (Tao) for her.

This was my first Atelier game and I’m glad for it. I hear people talk about it being one of the best in the series because they removed the weekly/yearly limit thing? That’s kinda like Darkest Dungeon’s limited day cycle type thing? (Where you have to complete the game within a specified duration.)

You can change the in game music at any time to different sound tracks. There is a lot of little attention to detail in the game that is really nice.

I’ve played 82 hours (83 if you round the number up from 82.9.) + the DLC content that I did play through. I finished the Klaudia, Ryza mini story and stopped at Lila's story boss. (which is a rehashed boss from the end of the game.) I struggled to finish the Ryza one (island vacation) since it had a boss at the end as well. (I did Ryza mini story first then tried the ‘Secret Solitary Island’ and finished that by a hair’s breadth but didn’t feel like going through all that trouble of defeating yet another boss from Lila’s story so I went and did Klaudia’s story and found it to be very enjoyable. The only problem with her story was that it kinda felt like it got cut off since we only made one failed donut. By fail I mean it wasn’t the one that Empel remembered eating and instead it was sour? Why didn’t we try it like we did the other things first?


Overall I would recommend the game. It was a fun nice slice of life game. My only real complaint is that I didn’t like the combat.
