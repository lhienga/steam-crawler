\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother gem from Koei Tecmo. I've been real happy with their JRPGs. This one was particularly good because I felt like I could really relate to the main character even if she was in a world full of magic and monsters. The story was great and the characters were really well made (both in terms of their role in the game and in terms of their role in the story/world). The battle mechanics were solid and there weren't any major things that made battles annoying (there are stuns and things that take control of your characters away from you, but they are rare and don't last long).
There is plenty to do in the game. Don't feel like fighting? Wander around and gather stuff to make cool equipment. Want to feel useful? Do some quests. Just want to see what you are made of? Go fight some bosses out in the wild.
Fatal Drive is actually terrible. Not only do battles rarely last long enough to activate it, but it does only a little more damage than your skills while resetting your tactics level to zero. You know... that level that is really hard to build up and allows you to hit more and harder and you definitely want to keep up. It's supposed to be this Hail Mary mega attack, but if you actually look at the damage numbers, it's not worth it ever. They should have doubled the power or made it only take half your tactics level (probably not both though).
I will say this game has the least memorable soundtrack I've ever heard. If someone walked up to me on the street and offered me $100 if I could guess the song and they started playing the MAIN BATTLE THEME from this game, I don't think I'd recognize it (or maybe I'd get it with just HOW generic it is). They couldn't even hype up the final boss. THE LITERAL CLIMAX OF THE GAME! I guess Blue Reflection got all the music budget.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter a blind playthrough, I can say that Ryza was one of the best RPGs I have played in recent years. Coming from earlier games (From both Atelier and Mana Khemia), I shouldn't be this surprised, but things I learned back in the DS days while playing Mana Khemia or older Atelier Games still apply to this game after all those years. That consistency just makes me happy. Makes me feel like I have learned a lot from my previous experiences. Much like how I feel about Disgaea or the Persona series.

Personally, I try playing only games I truly enjoy and 100% the ones I truly truly enjoy or love, and this is one of them. And I was even happier to learn that the achievements in this game are all obtainable in a single playthrough instead of requiring two or multiple New Game+ runs.

Now for the negatives:

Gem consumption and synthesizing are a little tedious in the early and midgame. At least they were for me, who was trying to get good traits on every single piece of equipment and consumable. With the introduction of later areas and tools, the grind was much more enjoyable and manageable. This could have led to some burnout, but thankfully, those tools and areas helped a lot.

The game crashed frequently when I tried to alt+tab, which led to a very unfortunate situation where I lost a very good weapon that I spent hours crafting. I haven't saved the game after a good while, so there was no way to recover it after a long chain of synthesis and rebuilding materials. It's a little frustrating to lose everything for a crash caused solely by Alt Tab'n. BUT I am happy to announce that I created something even stronger after taking a break for a day.

That being said, Even with those crashes and hurdles, I still loved this game and will definitely be picking up Ryza 2 when I can.
