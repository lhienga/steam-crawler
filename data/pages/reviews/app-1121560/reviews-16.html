\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti remember playing older Atelier games on the DS (didn't remember the name specifically) but I was too young to understand the game back in those days. I'd consider Ryza as my first Atelier game and it is AMAZING.

The synthesizing system is a bit difficult to grasp early on in the game especially for new players like me but once you get the hang of it, it gets really compelling and fun. I'd recommend watching some YouTube videos or read the official manual instead of relying on the tutorial in-game.

The battle system implements a realtime turn-based mechanics similar to FFXII The Zodiac Age or Child Of Light and it might take some time to getting used to. Personally I think that Ryza's combat system is a little tame compared to FFXII and the game should get easier if you have the right equipments and items which you can get by Synthesizing them. IMO the combat mechanics in this game is much, much better than the two games I've previously mentioned, probably because it doesn't pressure you as much as those two games. Being able to chain attacks after gaining Tactics Level in combat feels so satisfying, and usable Synthesized items can also come in handy against certain enemies as well. These two factors allow for limitless experimentation in combat and I really liked that.

Overall a pretty chill game with very in-depth mechanics and I would definitely recommend playing this if you're new to Atelier as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t3 years ago I bought this game pre-pandemic and dropped it after 10 hours or so because I thought this sort of gameplay was boring, I was used to endless combat and grinding on my jrpgs. 3 years later and becoming more of a busy adult, I've learned to appreciate this sort of "Slice of Life" game.

Atelier Ryza is a cozy game. Its meant to be played slowly at your own pace. I had more fun playing this game on my breaks rather than trying to rush it like I did 3 years ago. It has a charming cast of characters that I've grown attached to as I played more of this game. If I were to look at this at an objective standpoint, I would say the story is pretty straightforward. Its nothing stellar and the same could be said with the combat of the game. The graphics isn't anything amazing even for jrpg standards. Heck even the physics is pretty jank. However, I am going to ignore all of that because this game resonated well with me. It did more for me than some of the recent AAA titles I played for the last few months,

I give this game 10 thicc thighs/10.

Now i'm off to playing the 2nd game to see where this great cast of characters go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter putting nearly 50 hours into this game, I've come to the conclusion that it is a mediocre game at best. The story is slow, plodding and there's no real conflict for a while. What conflict there is is so far in the background that it's forgettable until, near the end of the game, they realize they need to have some sort of finale and increase the stakes.

Gameplay is far, far too easy. Battles have no difficulty and for a JRPG to have a whopping total of THREE bosses is kinda pathetic. Especially when the only one that has any semblance of difficulty is the final one.

The alchemy is this game's saving grace with how interesting and complex it can be, but the gameplay is so pathetically easy that alchemy renders it even more easy. You can make super powerful weapons with a crazy amount of effort, but you can also put in some basic effort making lower tier weapons and armor and still breeze through the game, even on the Hard difficulty setting.

All in all, Atelier Ryza is just an "okay" game. I'd rate it 4/10 on a scaling system. Unfortunately, I can only choose Yes or No on Steam's reviews, so that sadly puts it as a "do no recommend" from me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRyza is a pretty significant paradigm shift for the Atelier series, and while I feel mixed on some of the changes, I think it's an overall improvement. Many modernized gameplay tweaks to allow for better pacing and less dull moments. Combat is also much more exciting and involved being Active Turn Based combat instead of the traditional turn-based.

In my personal opinion, I don't think the story or alchemy is as strong as it was in previous iterations, particularly the Mysterious trilogy. The characters themselves are great, but it felt like the plot glossed over a lot of things that could've been expanded upon; I found it strange no party members had their own questline like the other games, instead they're half-baked in the main story to present their arc. Synthesis is easier to understand than it's even been which is good, but I felt so railroaded on the things you can make, because it relies solely on item quality and simple numeric values instead of the multiplier-based mechanic used in others. You either can or can't create something to the best it's been. Not the biggest fan.


Overall though I wholeheartedly recommend Ryza for Atelier newcomers. Despite my complaints, experiencing this world and seeing it's characters are a real highlight like any other Atelier game.\t\t\t\t\t

This game rewards you heavily for going out of your way to explore every nook and cranny of it, and oftentimes trivializes combat to the point where you don't even have to look at your screen to win. The thing is, this game isn't about combat, and I don't think the entertainment this game provides stems from difficulty at all. I found that the plot is cute and compact, and that the cast of characters are visually appealing with some amount of thought put into them, Additionally, the soundtrack isn't half bad, and the graphics still hold up at present time. I'd recommend this game to anyone looking for a comfy coming-of-age story that leaves you with warm and fuzzy feelings.

- Switch to the Compatibility tab and click on Change high DPI settings, under "Settings".

- Select Application from the dropdown menu and confirm with OK.
- Click Apply, then OK on the Properties window.
- Launch the game.

Overall 7.75/10

For my first Atelier game I was surprised as to how fun this ended up being. It has a lot of things that makes me think of games I hold really dear; good world building, consequences, characters that doesn't insult your intelligence, some good twists, and of course a female protag (there's something Kino about female leads in games)

I was also surprised with how "safe" this game is, always saw Ryza's arts and thought the game was too lewd pandering to people that likes thighs, and besides 2 character designs (and some slight jiggly physics and butt camera), the game is actually very tame and does not use stupid generic trope jokes.

Game is quite easy (that depends mostly on the learning curve tho), but it's still very fun to play, the gameplay loop is fun and sometimes the game has some difficulty spikes that are well received, I was also amazed with how polished it is as there are certain things that makes things quite balanced, in this case, Item and Skill usage. Also, Small numbers goes brrrrrrrrrrrr.

Soundtrack is full of bangers as expected of GUST.

This game is hella beautiful holy cow

Now, there's some things that I wish were done a little better, I understand the idea behind not being able to carry a bunch of items in your explorations, but it's quite annoying having to go back to the Atelier to get rid of items to continue exploring, and you only get the bag expansion somewhere midgame, and the item scanner further into the game, which would have saved me a lot of trouble early, but it is what it is. Other than that, it was a solid experience.
