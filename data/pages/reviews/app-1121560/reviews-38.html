\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, I really enjoyed this. I really like JRPGs but this is my first Atelier game and it was surprisingly really fun. Although, you can tell that it's a low budget game with limited animations, static NPCs, you can only traverse in a way the devs want you to (As in, you're not able to jump knee high obstacles to get around, there are invisible walls everywhere), and the character models feel lacking. I wish they were dynamic in terms of at the very beginning they'd be wearing regular islander clothing with no proper weaponry but as they got through the game their outfits got more personal to the characters.

I feel like it's a more casual kind of JRPG even though the turn based combat is dynamic (In the sense of, it's sort of rhythmic rather than static like Dragon Quest) but the main aspect of the game is alchemy crafting and exploring so I'm not overly fussed on how the combat functions. Speaking of, it's a really cool, most games with alchemy are set recipes, Skyrim being combine up to 3 ingredients for a potion, DQ9 being a set 2 ingredients after you've read a crafting book, and while you do need crafting books for a lot of things in this game that's not the case for armour and weapons. So while not being a true open world, do as you want, I feel like it's a really good RPG with a pretty deep alchemy system.

The areas you explore all look really cool and are pretty big, not necessarily good since you're really slow until about 3/4 through the game where you can craft speed boots that let you explore locked off areas in certain areas of the map getting better materials and there was a few times where I felt a bit annoyed because of how little there was in an area to collect so there were times where I was just grinding materials, teleporting back to base, doing all this while listening to a podcast.

Monster design is good but not very varied, every mob has about 4 recolours indicating difficulty but that makes it feel like there isn't a wide variety of monsters. I get recolouring slimes, slimes are classic JRPG but weasels? Fairies? Wyverns? The phusian dogs? Not all recycled mobs are bad, like the knights armour feels different enough, the rock golems are fine for different ores you can get, the bettles were noticeably different and that's all the regular mobs I remember. There are the mini bosses (That I assume are a post game thing) but they are just women in floating chairs.

Sadly, the characters aren't really that deep, there's cutscenes for the party characters when you're walking around the island doing quests for the townspeople which is a nice touch but I wish there were something more. There are quests for every party member but it's just something that appears in your quest log with no unique voiceline afterwards . You do get abilities for doing them but it just feels like busy work. The development of the characters is solid but it's nothing to really write about; the further they get in the game, the stronger, more confident they are, the better they are. It's character writing 101.

Music good. Although I wouldn't listen to it outside of the game.

I can't understand Japanese so I'm gonna go ahead and just say that the voice acting is really good since they conveyed the tone really well but I could tell that there was some localisation changes happening to the subtitles which in my eyes is a negative 90% of the time but hey ho.

I didn't 100% this game and I don't think I will, while it's fun I can't really bring myself to grind to level 99 on the characters and alchemy AND then beat the 6 post game bosses, I tried one of them a few hours before I beat the game (Because those bosses just exist around the map) and I got floored. I'll probably start it back up for a few days and do the remaining side quests but I'm really happy with the ending and am really wanting to play the second game in the Ryza series. I don't know if I'll ever replay it but I'm quite tempted to do a harder difficulty.

I also like the extra menu you get after you beat the game, there's a section that's just the voice actors talking about their experience with the game and it's really nice to hear, I wish more games did this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDifficulty:
Easy if you get a good grasp of the alchemy system, items are as important as special moves. I got stuck early in the game because I didn't pay much attention to the importance of items, after that everything became much smoother.

Story:
Slice of life fantasy dimensional coming of age story.

Gameplay:
This is my first Atelier game and really enjoyed the alchemy system. Fetching materials wasn't a chore and building new items was nice. Something I liked a lot is the game keeps introducing new systems up until the endgame and they are not overwhelming, most of the latest ones are quality of life improvements.

Combat:
Very satisfying. Action and turn based at the same time, a mix of Dragon Quest/Persona turn based systems with active character choosing. No hard turns between party and enemies.

Graphics:
Beautiful.

Audio/Music:
Excellent. Cicadas, pianos, waves.

Price:
Worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
