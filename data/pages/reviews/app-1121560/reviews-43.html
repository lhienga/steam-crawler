\r\n\t\t\t\t\t - This is my first entry into the Atelier series, and oh boy, i have to say...

...ValkyrieAurora was right. (if anyone even remembers her now)
This series is SO underrated.

- Excellent customization in combat styles and builds, due to the variety of custom items you can synthesize in the alchemy cauldron. The same type of item can yield different results and builds to your team depends on how you crafted it, and i think i must have tried like a hundred combinations by now. The synthesizing mechanics are sooo addicting, i just want to keep creating stuff with it. It's like a minigame of its own.
- Combat also have realtime events and quicktime events in addition to traditional turn-based combat all RPGs have, and it makes you constantly on edge on what you have to do, what skills or items you have to use in order to fulfill a certain combat order or to achieve a quick time event. Really, really makes the whole combat stuff engaging and not repetitive for me at least.
- Stunning visuals and really decent graphics for a Koei Tecmo title. Which is saying a lot since we have stuff like FE Three Houses and Warriors series' infamous PNG textures. The character designs are so pretty and pleasing to look at. The sky, the scenery is stunning.
- Character development and impression are good, and the story is simple yet really charming. Sometimes I don't need my RPG to have complex multi-dimensional story that is really hard to swallow, just small stories and a simple plot to glue everything together like this is more than enough.

In conclusion: I kinda feel like i got this game for only 15$ was a rob, because this game can easily mark itself as a 60$ AAA title and i will still be okay with it. Seriously, if you are a fan of open-world fantasy tactical JRPGs with a charm, this game won't disappoint you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtelier Ryza is what you'd expect, a game centered around crafting and stats and trying to get the best gear possible. Going into this I knew it would be complicated, but I did become quite overwhelmed. Mind you, this is my first Atelier game as I had heard this was a great starting point for the franchise. I messed up badly enough that quite frankly I had to turn to easy mode to beat the last boss and didn't even touch all the optional bosses. Even as a entry point, a lot of the crafting can be overwhelming, but after working through things and reading guides, I'm much more prepared for the next entry of the series. I might play back through this game one day with the knowledge I have now.

As for the story.....well It was interesting somewhat, but not really my favorite. Felt like some of the plot points were cliche, but if you're looking for a game about childhood friends centered on a small town, then the story might be right for you. I wanted a bit more adventure, but again this is my first Atelier game so I don't know how the story compares to the other games, I'm just touching on my own tastes. I mostly went in for the crafting and was satisfied with what I experienced.

10/10 would kill my brain repeatedly trying to figure out what I did wrong craft wise again\t\t\t\t\t
