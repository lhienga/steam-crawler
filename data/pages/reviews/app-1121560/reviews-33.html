\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, it is a typical J-RPG narrative-wise with some altered gameplay designs. Of course the main course is the Alchemy system, where you collect materials via various means, and these materials all have different traits and stats, so you will have to mix them accordingly to yield the effects that suit you. And naturally these Alchemy products are used in combat or exploration to help you progress the story. A quite neat and logical combination of various gameplay elements. A little bit of cons: 1. sometimes it gets tedious when you repeat the process to craft a piece of equipment, cuz you have to farm materials and craft a lot of mid-product; 2. the overall visual presentation is great but sometimes it's just not on par with the standard of this generation. Some cutscenes are just hasty or even omitted. (Guess that's because of the budget typical for this franchise, not a AAA of course). Generally I think it's a worthy 40+ hours playing this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtelier Ryza is honestly one of comfiest JRPGs I’ve ever played, and it's not just the small town atmosphere, which I adore, the general pacing is so laid back that it doesn't make you want to rush to progress with the story. You are free to go around to gather materials, craft stuff, level up your alchemy or do side-quests. The timing based combat felt rather sloppy and it becomes boring outside of big enemies and bosses, it's probably the weakest part of Ryza, but the game is so crafting-heavy that you can consider the combat as a secondary mechanic that is mainly used to show off how good at crafting you are. The alchemy system is amazing in how it's executed, you get most recipes by modifying ones you already have which is a lot more interesting than just finding recipe books. The environments are nice to look at and the gathering system is also fairly deep since you can create different tools that will give you different materials from being used on the same resources, when going out to search for specific ingredients I couldn't help but remember when I would grind professions in WoW. Fun ♥♥♥♥, are all Atelier games like this?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComing from the perspective of someone who's never played an Atelier game before.

Gameplay
Exploration and Material gathering is the biggest part of the game, finding new locations as well as experimenting with Alchemy (crafting system) is the main bulk of the gameplay. The overall story leans towards a slice of life style anime in between major story beats.

Combat is similar to Final Fantasy's ATB system where there's a timeline and enemies can act while you're deciding on your next move which causes the game to be more fast paced. Characters that you aren't controlling can be set to either use skills or build AP.

AP is the most important thing in the combat system. You use it to execute skills and increase your Tactics level. Tactics is essential as it increases the damage and gives your abilities and items extra effects.

Items aren't consumed on use, but are equipped to each character instead. These items can be used limitlessly but at the cost of core charges. Items can also be converted during combat in order to regain core charges.

The Good:
  • Cute game, practically everything is cute, even the more "disturbing" backstory enemies
  • Super easy to pick up and drop due to the focus on slice of life than a main overarching plot
  • Simple but interactive turn based gameplay
  • In-depth crafting system (alchemy) that is just a joy to mess around with for min-maxers and casual players alike
  • Story is easy to digest and mostly light-hearted fun

The Bad:
  • Alchemy can look daunting and may turn new players away (it's like the main draw of the game so this can be bad)
  • Overarching plot kinda comes out of nowhere around halfway into the story till the end
  • Story isn't anything unique, typical coming-of-age story where the protagonists start of as kinda annoying and immature, then learning to stand on their own and mature.
  • Not very challenging, got through hard mode without breaking a sweat
  • Post game content is pretty limited to fighting like 2 secret bosses and gathering missing items/recipes

About the DLCs
✅ Secret Solitary Island: Good DLC, pretty challenging (until you make broken gear and level up). Basically an endgame gathering spot with tons of high level enemies and new items and expands the pretty short postgame.

✅ Lila and Empel's Episode: Brings more lore about the underworld and the Oren. Takes place a few years after the end of the story, making it the only DLC that brings relatively good rewards.

❌ Cosmetic DLCs: If you have to get them separately, they're honestly not worth it, but as these are purely cosmetic, if you like the outfits then go ahead and nab em, I won't judge

❌ Episodes (Excluding Lila and Empel's Episode): They're cute but are insanely short, the rewards you gain for finishing them are also pretty bad unless you do them at the proper times. They also have a few weird continuity issues and don't really add anything to the lore or overarching plot. They're basically fetch quests that let you control someone other than Ryza (minus the summer episode). Only nab these if you wanna see some extra side plots related to the group.

Timeline of Episode DLCs/Best Time to Do Them
Tao Episode: Before getting the hideout/after getting the hideout (takes place before getting it but it gives some decent early game synthesis material recipes)

Lent Episode: After Meteor Castle's Boss, unlocks an optional boss that has unique traits but by the time you can beat it you generally have better traits.

Klaudia Episode: can be done anytime since this one's the most disconnected continuity wise, any time after getting Lila playable works.

Summer Episode: After you solve the issue of the missing fish and get your sixth playable character: Empel


TL;DR
Good game, is it worth the 60USD base price tag? Oh heck no, that's way too pricey for a niche series. It's good but not THAT good. The story is good enough to keep interest but I wouldn't class it as a game that's worth that much.

If you want the DLCs too, get it on sale, this game can be insanely pricey depending on where you're from. The game's completely playable (and plat-able) with just the base game, but is far shorter without the DLCs.

The playtime also significantly shortens if you don't play around with the crafting system and only do the bare minimum needed to beat the game.

If you think you'll enjoy experimenting with crafting really strong items and enjoy slice of life content, this game is worth it, maybe not at full price unless you have money to spare though.\t\t\t\t\t
