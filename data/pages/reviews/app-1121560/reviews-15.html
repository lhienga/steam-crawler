\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtelier Ryza Ever - Darkness & the Secret Hideout


If you're thinking to yourself the same way I am "HEY I always see this game series on Steam, but it's so damn expensive... should I buy it anyway?"
Those were exactly my thoughts that enticed me to buy the ENTIRE Atelier game series after years. And I'll take the decision from you now.
One thing I really need to note before I write this review. Only buy this game if you are rich or if it has been reduced to at least 20€. Otherwise it's just not worth it. The biggest problem with the game is not that it isnt bad, it is just average. It's that it costs a whole 60€ even though it can't even begin to make up for the price with content. I paid 29,99€ and I still think that the game was overpriced. And I am someone who has almost 300 Steam games, this already shows my skepticism. I hope part 2 will be better.

Atelier Ryza 1 is a very colorful mostly crafting and quest based game, with a relatively refreshing combat system. Unfortunately, the game really has little that sets it apart and classifies it as special. And despite everything, I have to say that I did enjoy the game to some extent. I spent almost 70 hours here until I finished everything. Unfortunately, apart from promising game systems, there was nothing to captivate you. Especially the characters are a massive disappointment.

Positives
- Pleasant atmosphere
- Difficulty level is also suitable for casual players.
- Great crafting system with immense variety of items and details.
- A relatively new battle system which offers variety at the beginning.

Negatives
- Average story
- Poor dialogue, the main characters have NPC conversations at their best.
- Disappointing characters. It's almost like playing one of the worst fantasy anime as an RPG.
- Character animations are poor. Although the game has limited levels anyway with no real requirement. And yet there are 4-5 different animations, the walking animations are like from a PS1 game and it fills in so cheap.
- Game has poor explanations even though it has immense amount of systems.
- There are only 3 bosses in the game and all 3 are recycled repeatedly with different names. Which is immensely lazy.
- The game map does not update to progress. So characters from the beginning of the game stand in the city and are still your enemies, although you have befriended each other in the story for a long time.
- The side missions are just bad without mercy. Such bad quests are hard to find.
- The characters' personalities are barely there, as are their proportions. What the hell is going on with Lent and Lila? Lent looks like a bodybuilder who was given the arms and head of a child. And Lila carries around 40kg of tits.


If you're still skeptical, check out gameplays to see if the game appeals to you at least in terms of gameplay. Otherwise, I recommend playing Atelier Sophie, which had worse graphics, but much better characters.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a pretty good game that just seems unfinished in the UX department. The crafting system itself is wonderful, but a potential inventory of 5000 items calls for very good search and management systems. There are some basic filters and searches in some menus, but in some places they're absent. In some places you have to scroll through a list of about 50 categories of something and scrolling is extremely slow with no button to speed it up, go by page, or at least reorder the items alphabetically so you can find what you are looking for. There are more details that are annoying, but the as a whole this leads to your time being mostly spent in the crafting menu looking for things instead of actually doing the crafting.

As a small second wrinkle, the game felt to kind of end abruptly. I never really got to use the good gear I crafted in the story because the story was already over at that point. I know there are post-game bosses but spending a lot more time in the crafting menu just to beat bosses for no reason didn't seem appealing either.

All in all, I wouldn't buy this at full price. But if you're story focused and not that annoyed with bad menus and/or crafting suboptimally this is quite good.\t\t\t\t\t
