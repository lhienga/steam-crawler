\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a rather cute game, with anime-styled graphics. The focus is mostly on exploration and Alchemy, but the combat system progressively expands as you go through the story (Through the Tactic levels, Core items, Action Orders, Extra Orders and Fatal Drives, and roles later on). Speaking of which, the story itself is rather simple, as your band of young adults follow their goals and seek their way out into the world. As they go, they uncover a plot that threatens their home and decide to stop it.

There's quite a bit to do, most of it related to Alchemy. You get to craft your own armor, weapons, accessories, gathering tools (even fusing some of them to compound their utility) and materials. You can customize your hideout a little bit through unlockable stuff that grants you bonuses. There's even a little garden which you can use to grow different kinds of materials eventually. There's a little treasure hunt, a good amount of quests, some of which are personal (unlocks passives for your playable characters!) and other from non-playable characters.

I recommend using a game controller for this instead of the keyboard. I did quite enjoy this game, and I've only just completed the main storyline (took on average 60h in total on normal, without guides/walkthroughs). Though I enjoy RPGs and I had heard about the Atelier series before, this was the first game I've played from that franchise.

There's a difficulty level for everybody (Easy, normal, hard, very hard, charismatic), with a New Game+ function, and even some additional content after you complete the main game. Some extras are also unlocked after the main story, but they're the usual stuff (Gallery, movie viewer, music player as well as some words from the voice actors regarding their own character and the game in general).

There is a little growth character-wise for most of the cast, while some remain quite the same throughout the game. Once you've cleared the story, most of what you'll likely do is get the remaining achievements, min-maxing your gear for NG+ on Charismatic difficulty, but otherwise our options are limited at the moment.

All in all, this isn't such a bad game, and I found it quite entertaining. I hope you will enjoy it too!

TL;DR: I give this an 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was good, but a little average.
This was my first game in this series, and I can see how it's gained it's fans, but it might be a while before I play another game in the Atelier series.

The alchemy system was interesting. There's a lot you can do with it. However, that also means you can spend hours crafting, like I did. Later in the game, once you have a lot of reagents and recipes, the interface gets a bit cluttered, though. I wish they showed you the actually showed you the number count of how many items you have. Also, it would be nice if they added some detail to the item trait descriptions so you can tell at a glance with traits work with weapons, armor, useable items, reagents, etc...

The combat system is fast paced, but leaves room for strategy. The commands your party members give you make no sense, sometimes. If a character tells you to "Heal Yourself", they really mean "Heal Me", it seems, for example. Also, it can be hard to figure out what ability they want you to use when they say, "Reduce elemental resistances".

The characters follow some simple archetypes, but are a little bland. Not too bad, though. The story's above average.

Overall, I'd recommend it to a JRPG fan, but I wouldn't use it to introduce someone to JRPGs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely loved Atelier Ryza.

To be honest, I got the game initially because I was charmed by Ryza's character design like a lot of other people. It's a fantastic character design (for all the right reasons). The people who worked on her design should all get a raise.

Part of me was expecting the game itself to be trash, but I'm delighted to report that I was terribly wrong, and I had an absolutely charming. fun and wonderful time. I've only cleared the main story and still have lots of sidequests and more extra stuff to finish, but Atelier Ryza has to be one of my favorite games of this year. The game has a really intricate crafting system that sucks you in once you get the hang of it, really fun combat that's super challenging at times, super lovable characters, breathtaking gorgeous art, and great atmospheric soundtrack.

I heard that Ryza might appear in another Atelier game in the future, and I can't wait. They say that Atelier Ryza has been the best in the series for a while, but I'm excited to give the previous Atelier titles a shot sometime. It'll be super fun!

P.S. Atelier Ryza drinking game where you take a shot every time Ryza says "boken" or "renkinjutsu". You'd be guaranteed alcohol poisoning after two hours.\t\t\t\t\t
