\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll get straight to the point and say Atelier Ryza is an amazing game. I entered this series with Atelier Sophie, which I absolutely loved, and while I liked Atelier Firis, I didn't find it as enjoyable. I don't like Atelier Lydie and Suelle, and because I didn't play the Arland trilogy I haven't touched Lulua. So this is not my first Atelier game, and assuming they make more, it certainly won't be my last. They got rid of by far my least favourite part of the Mysterious trilogy, which is the "idea" system, Sophie and Firis did it well enough, but in Lydie and Suelle it wore out its welcome, and became a chore, now you unlock new recipes through old recipes by combining new ingredients in. The Combat stepped up to a Real Time-Turn Based Hybrid-esque System, it feels really weird at first, but it doesn't take long to get used to it, and you either have to fly by the seat of your pants and hope everything works out, or think about what you're going to do during everyone else's turn. Due to the new combat system, you can only control one character at a time, you can switch to any other character at any time, but the other two will continue taking actions until' you switch over to them, I don't mind having the AI do the majority of the fighting while I toss items and spells, so I didn't mind it. The Story is genuinely intriguing, and the characters are really likeable, and everyone gets a nice little character arc. Something I very much enjoyed is that the tone isn't as hunky dory as the Mysterious trilogy, right from the get-go there are themes of abuse, alcoholism, bullying, and the like, and the doom and gloom sticks around, which made the light, happy moments, and triumphant moments all the sweeter. I highly recommend this game to fans of the Atelier games, and fans of RPGs in general.\t\t\t\t\t

This is definitely the prettiest Atelier so far, and I think the combat is the best so far, but I'm just a sucker for ATB style fighting. The alchemy system is easy to understand and requires a little amount of thinking to master, but is very rewarding once you learn it properly. I do, however have some complaints about the story. There are parts in the game where there should've been cutscenes. Example, you're at your base, and a character wants to talk to you. You then need to have a talk to someone in a distant town. You then have to teleport (which granted, doesn't take long), but then the next obj is to wait at your home base. That could've all been a small cutscene or autoplayed through instead of wasting like 2-3 minutes of my time. But just a gripe.

With the only other Atelier I've played being Escha & Logy, I can definitely say I like the dropping of the deadline mechanic. Before, you would always need to collect an item or perform an action by a certain date. However, you as the player have the freedom to explore, gather, and fight at your leisure.

All in all, it's a good game. For a non fan, I'd justify this game at $40-50. However, do be wary with all of the graphical/crashing issues that others have reported.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs Ryza a good game? Yeah, with an updated combat and alchemy system, it's really fun to play, game is visually impressive, a new shader system to compliment the new art style.

Is Ryza a "fun game"? No.......

That's the point, Ryza is a bittersweet journey, a story about coming of age, growing up as a "person", Atelier is usually very sunshine and rainbow, i specifically play it for that since the market is kinda saturated with edgy "saving the world" teenagers titles. So Atelier is that one niche game that have you do something else other than saving the world, just mainly craft stuffs and hang out with friends. But this game..........deviated quite far from that, it's a "new direction"

This "new direction", can turn alot of older fans off, me included, i don't hate drama but sometime it can lead to nowhere, annoys you, and just straight up unnecessary. It dealt alot with social and family drama, it's not GUST first attempt at drama, previous Twilight Trilogy also dealt with a heavier story as a whole, but it's more of personal drama, with friendship, partners, lovers, sisters, family, Ryza have a more social aspect to it, as in "what if society think of you as a weirdo?" kind of view.

Being a game of "summer adventure" and heavier focus on exploring, while it's great to have all these new addtions to exploring, more varities, it doesn't feel as good as Firis - another game in the series also with a focus on exploring and adventuring, with map being available to you at the start, being able to go home at any moment,....

Even the clothing, which is probably the worst part to compare, Ryza literally have........nothing, it's just color alterations or a jacketless version, and.......Klau's outfit, she have one original outfit and that's the default outfit, i know there will be upcoming T-shirts, but seriously, T-shirts? Even Lulua have a whole new original dress, Firis have regional outfits, and so much varieties to it, comparing them to Ryza, it's a huge step backward.

Music is great, nice, comfy, summery, melancholy

I wish that this game sell well so GUST have the chance to improve upon the gameplay that they have, it's fun, and as a whole maybe a good starter entry for newcommer, but.......as someone who've been playing Atelier ever since Rorona, it feel...........lacking, so many things

I'd give it a mixed review, but since there's no such thing, and i don't hate it, yeah, i guess try it and see for yourself\t\t\t\t\t

Foreword
Majority of the hate this game is getting is because of the keyboard and mouse controls and other technicalities mostly relating to the PC optimization and port. Now I don't know about the previous Atelier titles on the PC but one can say that this could have been expected coming from Koei Tecmo at least. That of course doesn't make it right or any better but in some people's cases I find it questionable to complain about a dead-end road if you did notice the signs leading up to it beforehand. Not to necessarily downplay the concerns of a shoddy port as it's a valid complaint nevertheless, but I do feel it has to be said as there's multiple ways people will perceive or care for it.

In any case, the game plays just fine with a controller and personally with 13 hours in, I have yet to run in any technical issue or anything; running the game at a stable 130+ FPS. Yeah, it's a shame the clothing physics break at anything higher than 30/60, but if that's your absolute deciding factor for this game, I would recommend certain other titles. Really my only gripe with this game so far is some of the idle animations, usually in cutscenes look a bit stiff along the lines of almost t-posing. But I could easily tie this in with the other concerns which doesn't truly affect my enjoyment of the game.

Combat
For the game itself is very enjoyable to say the least, and that's coming from someone new to the crafty Atelier series. Combat has a unique if not interesting take on mixing real-time with some tactical orders and actions to control. It may take getting used to as there's quite a bit to keep track of with everything moving in real-time but it does feel good when you finally get the hang of it. With spells, attacks and action triggers going off, it can turn into a flashy spectacle to behold.

Crafting
As for the main charm of this game, the crafting: I find it to be very enjoyable being able to create your own gear and items with specific traits and qualities depending on the materials used from gathering. Now I don't have any previous Atelier title under my belt to compare it to as from my understanding this system is a bit of a new one. It's a seemingly simplistic concept but gradually gets more interesting and detailed as you go, and I haven't even gotten to the part where you can apparently rebuild and/or upgrade past created items. Not much more I can say about it as I think it's something one has to see and try out for themselves.

Conclusion
And with that said, those two factors (combat and crafting) already make this game worth the money to me. The story so far has been interesting enough to me to want to explore more, but it's nothing groundbreaking and I obviously haven't finished it yet to make a fair assessment; I might update this review when I do clear it. But really, I'm already fully enjoying just going around the maps, gathering materials, picking up side quests and crafting stuff to fulfill certain orders or to improve the gear and items of my own team. If I can enjoy 13 hours being this early in the game still on progress, I'm confident I'll enjoy the rest of it just as much if not more.

So like with most other games, if you have the right mindset for it and enjoy it's core mechanics then any technicality that doesn't personally affect you will easily fade away. Once more this doesn't mean that I don't acknowledge KT's fault in optimizing and porting the game for PCs, but this will always be a subjective thing that people have to weigh out for themselves. There's plenty of games on Steam that get 'mixed' reviews because of certain expectations or hype, but one should always consider taking a personal look into it before letting other people's opinions decide for them. So as a wise man once said, take all reviews with a grain of salt; which yes, includes mine as well.

Arbitrary # out of 10, I'm lovin' it.

100% Update
Just finished the story and beat the game in one run without going through new game plus. Had to get the last few achievements from new events that unlock post-completion but I got 100% completion now for the time being. Don't really have much to add except that the story was actually quite enjoyable. It was a lighthearted adventure with very wholesome moments.

But really, after having seen and done most if not everything in the game, I just want to reiterate that the crafting system is absolutely charming and I now see why it's the main attraction of the series. One other thing I'll add though is that albeit subjective, the music in this game was a heavenly experience from start to finish all the way to the credits. Combine it all in one package and it's definitely worth the time and money spent. For now I'm done but I had 69 hours of amazing fun.\t\t\t\t\t
