Very Recommended

This game is Top steam October 2019 Release games, as an Atelier fan I'm quite happy for the success of this series, as you all know Atelier games are niche title among the west and Atelier games are usually come with trilogy or quadrilogy, most people get discouraged when they want to play one of the newest games but they realize that the game is a continuation from one of the series. Now that Ryza is out, it’s very good starting point for new player to try out an atelier game.

The illustrator who worked for this series is Toridamono. he also drew artwork for RPG maker MV.

Most of Soundtrack are from Blue Reflection Composer Hayato Asano. It’s great and you should listen to his soundtracks. Daisuke Achiwa and Ken Nakagawa didn’t compose on this game unfortunately, this game has more Blue Reflection feel than Atelier game in my opinion.

Into Pros
- I didn’t have any issue while running the game, no crash whatsoever but it’s still not mature compared to Lulua, probably because of Dynamic lighting, different combat system, and stuff.
- The newest update patch has Game setting to adjust your graphic setting and mouse button configuration setting. I still recommend that you used gamepad though.
- The aspect of Atelier game still present to this game, It’s still has the comfy vibe we are known and loved.
- Great character Design not just Ryza but all character in this series including the side event one.
- Great soundtrack.
- Beautiful Artwork.
- For the first time the new mechanics of "synthesizing" are a little complicated but you can get used to it. It’s really fun and cheese able.
- It has really good lighting and immersive environment compared to previous Gust titles.
- AI NPC for running/walking along the street is really well code.
- Combat system is using ATB(active time battle) some people found that they don’t really like it, personally I don’t like it either. But for this game it’s really fun and different experience. Also quick action can be cheese able.
- There’s no consumable.
- No time limit
- Simple UI text for time
- Changing of camera angle when discovering special Landmarks.
- Smooth camera movement.
- EXP sharing for all character, no need to farm EXP for unused characters.
- Quest are tied with side events, saved a lot of time.

Cons
- Lack of voice act in the side events.
- Name Typo on recurring character, It’s not Localization fault but still they should rename it and cover up the dev mistake.
- Bad game difficulty design(Post game difficulty).
- They should add blinking eyes animation to all character in every cutscenes and while standing still or not doing anything.
- Lack of Artwork.
- So many words put into the tutorial.
- Hard to micro manage other character battle order status because ATB system is little bit too fast.

Overall
Overall of the game They really tried something different without changing the core value of Atelier game, It's really good improvement and I'd say That this is the best Atelier game I've played.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a nice chill game and a major step up from previous Atelier games. Most notable is the graphics. Lulua was a major improvement, but they really double down in the graphics department for this one. The background art actually fits with the character models. No more jarring difference in quality between the character models and the background like in Sophie.

Synthesis system has been greatly simplified. They do away with balancing the elemental value like in Lulua and instead introduce a synthesis tree system. No more worrying about conflicting elemental value, you can just put in any ingredient you want to get the effect you want. Which I must say is way more user-friendly for newcomers. Another aspect I like from the system is the introduction of Gem Reduction mechanics, which allows you to convert unused items into gems that you can then use as a sort of "currency" for improving your item. Granted if you're a hoarder like me, you'd still end up with a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ of items in your container. But at least now you can use some of them in another way instead of letting them wasting away in the far corner of your inventory.

Battle system is another big change. To be honest, I was not a big fan of it in the beginning. It takes some time to get used to compared to the previous turn-based combat. I think it took me about 8 hours in to finally grasp the basic fundamental of it and get in tune with it. Once I did tho, combat became fun. The fast paced combat and the satisfying feeling I get when I see my characters pull off crazy special skills keep pulling me back. I'm not sure if I could go back to the old turn-based system after getting a taste of it.

Regarding the story... Well, I think I could say it holds up. The whole arc about this mysterious threat and the mysterious fallen kingdom is really interesting and set up some interesting plot points that I'd really love to see expanded in the future. Though, some of the others are kinda hit and miss with me. Especially the part with the whole friendship kind of things, which tbh I find a bit stupid.

Overall, it's a nice game to chill to and I don't regret spending my money on it.\t\t\t\t\t
