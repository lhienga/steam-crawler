\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing a deluge of boring and uninspired JRPGs lately, this game was the first one in quite a while where I didn't feel like I was forcing myself to finish it.

I did not enjoy Caligula Effect Overdose or Ni No Kuni II.

Tokyo Mirage Sessions had good gameplay but terrible everything else.

FFXV seemed to be confused about what it wanted to be and its own plot.

Ryza is not a 10/10, but it's a succinct journey with enjoyable characters and mechanics to make the 20 - 30 hours not feel like they are dragging.

There are no annoying mascot characters, no overly complex plot points, the battle system isn't trying to reinvent the wheel, and best of all there wasn't really a point where I felt like the game was artificially slowing me down to pad for time.

The dialogue got a few chuckles out of me, the characters were all likable for once, and surprisingly for a game where everyone knows the character as 'thighmaster general' the fanservice is much more tame than something like Tokyo Mirage Sessions.

Combat is sufficient to keep your attention without mashing A to proceed, crafting and alchemy can almost pass as a mini puzzle each time, gathering is quick enough not to wear on you, the graphics are pleasant and the music/voice acting never detracts from the experience.

Overall, I enjoyed my time with Ryza greatly. It's not a redefinition for the JRPG genre, and it doesn't need to be.\t\t\t\t\t

Introduction
Heyo. I'm reviewing this game from the perspective of someone who's never played the rest of the Atelier series. I guess the main point that brings across is that I have no comparison to how it is when looking at the series as a whole, and instead am viewing this game by itself.
Art Style & Graphics
This has anime vibes all over it, and I love it. It looks like a modern anime game. Very visually pleasing to look at.
Story
The story starts as your typical "I wanna get out and see the world" type of deal and transitions to a "I wanna save my island", and I think as cliche as that sounds, it makes for a beautiful canvas to paint the picture of the stories of the people within very well. A few of the characters have interesting backgrounds that get revealed as the game progresses, and a lot of it has depth that surprised me in a good way. Some characters break out of the traditional character norms I tend to see just a little bit. Cliches are cliche for a reason, but it's nice to spice it up with details that make the character who they are, and this game does that well. Likewise, I enjoyed how the story progressed, and I really, really, really hope that they add sequels to this particular story. I feel that Ryza has more adventures in her future, and this may be the setup to something even better.
Alchemy
There's no two-ways about it: It's clear the devs spent a lot of time creating a world full of items that you can combine that all have important uses, traits and effects that will benefit you. It also has a very elaborate and detailed crafting system (Alchemy) that you'll be spending a lot of time on. Yes, you do need to use this function to stand a chance of completing this, but it's easy and honestly insanely fun to get into.
I've been looking for a game that had a crafting system of this depth for a long time, and I've finally found that in Atelier Ryza.
Combat
I really enjoy the combat system, and I feel that any explanation I give here would undersell it. It's turn-based, but realtime at the same time. There's a variety of things you can do, like use crafted items (think heals and bombs), skills, and normal attacks. Instead of MP, they use a system where as your party attacks the monsters, you gain AP, which you can spend to use skills. I like this better than MP, as it gets you considering other options during battle and not just spamming Y to nuke everything. Instead it's like "If I do this thing, I can set it up so I can do this when this happens", etc. Strategy planning is something I can dig and I'm glad to see the variety of actions you can take gives you a variety of options for combat. I'm limited in how much in-depth I can take this review, so suffice it to say I really enjoy the combat, and I think you will too.
Difficulty Levels
I haven't experienced the higher difficulties yet, but on normal difficulty, once I got a handle on making good gear with Alchemy, the enemies didn't have a satisfying challenge for me. Which makes sense, if you make broken gear you're basically turning down the difficulty for yourself. It's not a fault of the game, moreso a fault of my own curiosity. I can't say how the higher difficulties are in comparison as I have yet to play through it on them, but I look forward to the challenge.
Time Spent and Replay-Value
It took me about 50 hours to complete it my first time, and that's again coming from me having never played an Atelier game in depth. I came in blind, not knowing how the combat worked, and really focusing on having a chill time enjoying the story and theorycrafting in Alchemy to figure out what will get me over the wall I'm up against. After beating the game, I want to play it all through again from scratch on the hardest difficulty for the added challenge. There is a New Game+ feature that lets you take your equips and equipped consumable items with you, but I feel like that would detract from the challenge I'm looking for personally. Looking forward to a Charismatic difficulty run, which unlocks once you beat the game for the first time.

Edit: Since my initial review I actually did go back and discovered the highest difficulty is actually Legendary, and not Charismatic. It adds 1000% HP to monsters, you're permanently cursed (-10% DEF), and the monsters have higher speed stat. It's a challenge that takes OP gear into consideration. I only made OP weapons and armor, so this run I still have OP items and accessories to consider crafting.

Conclusion
Is this game worthwhile? Very yes. I truly enjoy this game to my heart's content and would recommend it to anyone and everyone I know. You don't need prior knowledge of the series to get into this one, so I'd consider this a great entry point to the Atelier series. That being said, I haven't played the other Atelier games, so if you're looking at the other titles, I'd check out reviews for those, as I hear those games are essentially their own entities that have their own flavor and experiences, like you'd expect from a Final Fantasy game. I think that's a good thing, and leaves plenty of room for variety.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA pretty chill adventure about friends/rivals, helping people, and growing up.

I only have Atelier Ayesha and about 10 hours of Sophie as reference. I was initially disappointed at how simple the alchemy/crafting system was, especially compared to Ayesha. But eventually, you're introduced to so many systems outside of alchemy that it can seem really overwhelming. Truthfully though, for a normal difficulty run, you probably won't need to touch most of them.

There are no time limits, of course. Though I did find myself kinda missing the sense of urgency having one gives you. The main story & side stories are spread across events which you will end up chasing all over (side events will be concentrated on the island though). But I guess that's pretty typical for JRPGs, right? Wish there was more development for your companion characters, but that's probably what the DLC is for...

Still, I'm looking forward to the next installment in Ryza's series.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate: I've managed to fix my issue and get the game to play consistently. I've had to drop all but the shadows, its set to Low, when it comes to the graphics but it did the trick. I'd like to be able to play the game at full graphics(I definitely have the rig for it), but the game wasn't optimized well for the PC and for me if I try to use my Geforce Exp's recommended graphics, maxed out, it chugs on all but the battles and even then it'll chug on a big attack, which every enemy after the first area has especially the boss monsters.

First off, I'm going to clarify something...I do not hate this game; far from it. I've enjoyed the time I've gotten to play Atelier Ryze but as I'm going to explain shortly, this game has a very big issue.

I'm a big RPG fan and have been playing them for over a decade yet I haven't even touched any of the Atelier series. I have heard lots of good things about this series but I hadn't taken the time to get one of them and try them out. Seeing that this game just came out and was getting raving reviews, I figured I'd buy it and give it a go. Immediately, once I got access to it, I was drawn in to the crafting system and, through that, the exploration of the world and the monsters you can fight. I'm a big fan of FFIV so the ATB system was right up my alley and if I didn't feel like fighting then I could just run around and collect materials to make any of the new items I had access to at the time. I'm already a fan of anime and the various tropes and such that comes with it so this game was exactly what I enjoy.

Story: This is one part that doesn't try to hard to be good. It is refreshing that there isn't some "chosen one/few" overarching story but thats all I can really say considering my situation but I'll explain it when I get to it.

Art Style: Due to this game being an anime game, the art style is exactly that which means it can run on weaker computers just fine.

Exploration: This is one of those "as much as I love it, there are flaws" kind of points. You can explore to you're hearts content but only in areas that you've already unlocked. Other areas are locked behind story progression which sucks. I get that those areas are significant to the story or may be too high a level for me but I wish it would let me make such mistakes.

Combat: Very enjoyable. Classic Active Time Battle system with its own unique mechanics. Four actions total: Attack, Spell/Ability, Item and Move/Run. Attacking is your basic auto attack and raises your Tactics meter with which you use for spells and abilities or max it out and raise your Tactics level to gain better autos, stronger spells/abilities with extra effects and a higher Tactics meter pool. You'll also get requests from your allies, theres two of them, to do a specific action and be rewarded with an extra powerful attack/emergency healing if needed.

Alchemy System: the bread and butter of this game and where much of the other mechanics of the game revolve around. You complete story/side quests to get new recipes, then you go exploring through the areas you have access to obtain materials via pick up or fight drops then bring it back to your atelier and turn them into materials for other recipes, consumables, and even armor and weapons with the quality of the materials determining how good the item is. Theres so much more to I can go into but I think you get the gist.

This is the part where I explain why I can't recommend this game on PC. Steam says I put 17h into this game and its true but its not continuous like it would be otherwise. This PC port has a serious problem and I can't fix it. I can get, at most, a few hours into the game before it straight up crashes. It doesn't matter what I'm doing in game: watching story bits, exploring, fighting monsters, crafting; anything at all and the game with just crash at random. I've lost at least 5 hours worth of progress because of the random crashes and I'm at my wits end. This game is very enjoyable and something that I would play for days straight but because I can't make too much progress before another crash, until the developers fix it, I won't be touching this game again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough it is without a doubt the "easiest" atelier game in the series it's still a very good game. The characters are likeable and the newly introduced active-time-battle (previous titels had the good old turn-based battle-system that most older RPGs use) is fun and makes the battles feel less grindy.

The only negativ point i could find was that the game doesn't have as much content as previous games had (i have 100% achivments and i did not rush at all except for the alchemy lvl 99 one where i used a fast method) so you can expect to have cleared the game by around 35-50hours depending on your playstyle. getting 100% achivments should be possible in 45-55hours depending on how optimized you play.

But as you can see on the thumps up, i would still recommend this game as it is a nice addition to the series (different from the nelke one which was a disappointment sadly) especially Ryza was a nice and refreshing surprise, as she is neither too childish nor too mature for her age and one of the most realistic and believeable characters in the whole series until now. (She is also...more grown up in other places than usual but thats not that important right *cough*)\t\t\t\t\t
