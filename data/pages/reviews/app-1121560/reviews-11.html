Introduction
As a fan of the series, I decided to write reviews for the Atelier games. I tried to make them in a way that newcomers and maybe even fellow fans who're looking for the next installment find out something about it.

About Atelier
This is a classic JRPG franchise, meaning it has turn based battles where you have to select from a list what will your characters do in their turn. Atelier's charm however lies in the alchemy system.
This system is about gathering materials in around the world, be it from monsters or harvesting material nodes. These materials have certain traits and effects, that you can transfer to the new synthetised item. Alchemy allows you to make consumables, QoL items and even equipment.

This is what this game's battle system is actually about, making the strongest possible gear and decimate foes under a few turns with cheatlike power. While these traits differ slightly in every title, there's usually a huge ammount of them so you can make whatever you fancy, be it health potion that automatically heals and revive allies or a bomb that can insta-kill trash enemies and deal a huge amount of damage to bosses.

Making these items however requires a lot of planning, you can't just create an item and check whatever you need. You have to craft synthesis materials rom the raw materials, combining traits until you get what you want. These items usually require 30+ steps to get.

You don't have to fret out, you can complete the games without relying these overpowered items. However most games have challange bosses that requires you to actually create these items.

Storywise these games fall into the Slice of Life category. The game is not about saving the world from an ancient evil, but enjoying the life of the characters, their discussions, personality. The characters also have relations to characters that appear in an another title, or they themselfes appear in it, which can be a pleasant surprise.

If you enjoy theorycrafting, searching for the optimal equipment then these games are definitely for you. Even then, I would recommend using a guide/wiki to check material drops and traits, cause the ingame codex for that is not very convinient and you can go hundreds of hours without seeing them all. If that's not your cup of tea, but you enjoy Slice of Life animes give it a chance, I think you won't be disappointed. If you want to be the hero of the story, saving princesses in faraway castles, this game is definitely not for you.

About Ryza
I'm gonna start with saying Ryza is the second worst Atelier game I played beaten only by Totori and only cause that has obnoxious Time Limit. If not for that, I would say Ryza is the worst atelier game. The game is extremely watered down to appeal for a mass audience. It's not bad game, but it's a really bad Atelier game. All of the characters are really bland and boring, including Ryza herself. They don't even have their own stories and endings like in other games. Also there are way too few characters, you don't interact with the villagers like in other games, there are no characters like Pamela or Hagel, this game has instead some generic NPCs that are only giving you fetch quests.

The story is awful, not Atelier-like at all. It tries to be all heroic that you're saving the world, while this was present in other games that was not the main storyline, you just casually saved the world while you were doing other things. Here THAT is the main storyline and it's extremely boring. Also while in other games the villagers depend on you and come to trust you more and more as you become a better alchemist, in this game they straight up hate you, for that you are untrustworthy kid who's just playing around. Also this game doesn't have any interesting events OR EVEN CGs, there are like 2 cgs in the game, what heck is up with that? The game is elso very streamlined, you do the main story and that's it, like I said there are no character endings or random events, you're only following the quest marker until you're finished. And if you think there might be some post-game events you're wrong, once you're done, you ARE done, unless you want to grind out alchemy level and whatsoever to do a few challange bosses that also doesn't do anthing at all.

The battle system in this game is realtime and turn-based at the same time, what I mean each character can only act when it's their turn, but the time will never stop, the enemies will attack you while you're thinking what you want to do. You only control 1 character so at least it's not a micromanagement nightmare. If you played Chrono Trigger or FFXIII you should know what I'm talking about. Personally I hate this system cause it takes out everything tactical from the battles.

In this game even the alchemy is watered down to ridiculous levels. There are no alchemy skills or anything, you're just dumping materials on a screen that's like an ability tree. The more materials you dump, the stronger the items get. Yea, that's it. You can get new recipes from recipe books, but mostly from getting certain nodes on an item's "ability tree", which transforms it into the new item and unlocks the recipe to craft it directly. It could be a fun system, but you don't know what you will unlock several layers down the line, only the recipe you can unlock directly from the recipe. This design made me completely miss a vital Exploration item cause I thought I would get it from a recipe book.

In this game you have to use exploration equipment to farm certain things, many nodes can drop different things depending on which item you use. While this is not bad, it makes the in-game codex even more unusable. It makes it even worse that some materials can be only farmed at very specific areas with certain equipment, cause of this it's really easy to miss them. There are parts where you have to use certain exploration items to get through, this is a really bad design in my opinion.

If you're a newcomer to the series I would say try out ANY other Atelier game. While this is stupidly easy, it's also very boring and missing many of the fun parts of the earlier atelier games. If for some reason you liked this game, DEFINITELY try out the earlier games, like I said this is not a bad game, but it's far the weakest in the franchize.
