\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere to revise my review since the game got patched:

The PC port finally has keyboard/mouse support, graphics options, and bugfixes (such as fixes for the physics related issues). Although mouse support in menus is missing, menus can be navigated using WSAD. Mouse support does require checking off a box in the launcher's options however. Scrolling changes the camera distance, which is nice. I'd recommend binding the left mouse button to A, and the right mouse button to X. I can't speak on the behalf of ultrawide users, and I haven't tested controller support to see if the eight-way directional movement bug with the analog sticks has been fixed yet, but my thoughts on this is looking more positive now that it got fixed. From my experience, there's still some framerate dips in town areas, mainly because of GPU usage (This could possibly be fixed by adding an option to change the density of non-interactive NPCs and grass density), but it's great that they patched the bigger issues to begin with. I've been holding off on playing more until these issues got resolved, but now that they have been, I can get myself to play more.

Congrats Koei Tecmo on patching these things in, even though they are pretty much standard on PC games these days. To the fanboys, this is what happens when people are vocal about issues. Positive change or visibility of said issues ensue.

Now onto the game itself:

It's really comfy. I like the combat system, it's like Final Fantasy XIII, but not horrible like Final Fantasy XIII. The atelier gameplay mechanics where you gather materials to craft items is really in-depth, and it's great. Reistalin is like Haruhi Suzumiya without the god-like status. If you like slice of life anime and JRPGs, you will really enjoy this game if you can get past the hurdles of finding where to gather materials. I've been wanting to get into the Atelier series, since around Atelier Sophie, but never really got the chance to play them, so this is my first Atelier game.

The developers did a good job, especially considering big AAA publishers like Rockstar are having launch issues that are even worse.\t\t\t\t\t

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eXb9SQ5BfiM&t=4963s

Okay so I've been seeing a lot of negative reviews about the game so far. Mostly because people have said that the performance of this PC is "very bad".

I'm going to say that in my own machine which is basically just a LITTLE BELOW the recommended specs this game is running PERFECTLY. It might just be me though but I'm having higher frames and smoother gameplay than what Atelier Lulua gave me the first time that came out.

So what is it about this game that is particularly good?

Well, for me it's the usual. The Characters.
The characters in this game are pretty fleshed out. I like their interactions and their full on voice acting too. You can actually some real stress in the relationship of some of the characters with each other pretty early in the game which is something I don't usually see in Atelier games.

Another thing I absolutely loved in Atelier Ryza is that the battle and synthesis gameplay has been changed. The battle sequences with the "timeline" is very awesome although it did need getting used to from the more traditional Atelier battle but it was an overall VERY GOOD change for me. I loved the fact that it feels more of an actual battle and that you have to decide faster what you need to do.

The Synthesis mechanic is also amazingly well done. It made alchemy. The bread and butter of this game more intuitive and fun to do. I really missed the puzzle style grids of the mysterious series when Atelier Lulua came out and that was one of my negatives about Lulua. However, THIS synthesis is quite easy to understand and gives more flair and fun to the mixing and matching of the alchemy in-game.

Graphics and performance are AMAZING. I'm running the game at a good 60 - 90 frames a second depending on the environment on a GTX 1060, I expect it to be even better once I upgrade fully to my 2070 super. The vibe of the place gives me a nostalgic feeling to Atelier Totori and that is a plus point.

Music is also very well done! The symphony of the many instruments in the background adds a very relaxing feel but at the same time epicness to the game.

The only negative criticism I can actually give to this is that the game has lost the ability to talk and interact with the townspeople. I just liked reading random comments from people in town. Also... if you've seen in the video I've posted above, the townspeople ghosted me when I walked towards them lol.


Overall, I can't recommend Atelier Ryza enough. I am loving every minute of the game so far!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've only played Iris 3, Mana Khemia, and recently Rorona DX and Totori DX as of this review so I will be comparing mainly to Rorona and Totori. This is my re-review after 100% achievements.

Atelier Ryza was definitely a new experience for me, and it did not disappoint. The new action based combat system is much more engaging than the older turn based games, and is less brain-dead spam basic attack to win. Fights require more micromanaging and I found myself using items much more than I did in the Arland games even up until the final boss. I cannot say how the alchemy system compares to the more recent Atelier games, but I very much enjoyed the system in this game over the RNG fest of Arland. However, the system still has issues which I will explain later. What stood out most to me about the graphically are the colors. I never felt bored exploring and revisiting areas due to the day/night cycle and the occasional rain that would switch the coloring of every area around. I hope Gust continues to implement this in their future games, with even more weather patterns.

Now, some of my complaints. While the Atelier series has never been extremely focused on the combat aspect of its game, I would've liked to have seen better balancing on the difficulty throughout the game. Hard difficulty should not allow me to reach the final boss using only Bronze/Staltium level gear I made near the beginning of the game and accessories from the merchants. The problem I have with the alchemy system, is that it allows you to chain 50+ items together to make ridiculously powerful gear midway through the game. I steamrolled the final boss after making just ONE new weapon for Ryza after not being able to clear it with the gear I mentioned earlier. The two harder difficulties and the extra bosses that unlock after you clear the game are almost immediately obsoleted by these godly gear as you'll take no damage at all.

The AI of enemies also needs be improved, either in the upcoming DLC or in a future game. Almost every enemy will almost always basic attack whoever is in the frontline center position. Once the enemy reaches about 60% hp, they'll switch over to spamming their rage attacks until they die. Only the two dragon fights were actually challenging (died multiple times to the second one) since they had attacks that varied from elemental to debuff to status ailment and did not heavily focus down one person.

Other than those complaints, Atelier Ryza is a great game for those looking for a fresh start into the Atelier series and I highly recommend it. Currently, the game has not received its announced optimization patch yet so those with interest but do not have a beefy pc (I am running a Vega 56 at 1440p and was dropping below 60fps frequently) should definitely wait until this patch releases.\t\t\t\t\t
