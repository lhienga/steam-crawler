\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright so let me start off by giving you a small important piece of info...I never write reviews ever UNTIL TODAY!!! This game is the first Jrpg that has straight up sucked me in. It made me stop playing Dota 2 and Monster Hunter, sure those two games are a thing of their own, however the crafting system here is straight up MURDER. I have had and still have fun gathering materials and making OP gear. Sure Ryza has them thicc thighs and Lilas has that distracting Side chesto, but out of the characters Lent was my favorite that boy has some serious will power with the besto dad in the world. This is by far one of the most fun games I have gladly given over 80 hours for the last two weeks staying up till 4 am crafting. Addicting highly reccomended for the system. I have not had this much fun with a Jrpg since Tales Of Symphonia has a similar style.

Careful with the DLC its best to look that stuff up as I beat the game and found out later that well some of those events are during the story campaign in between certain objectives.
A down side of the game is the campaign length its very short at least it felt like this to me since I am the player who loves to grind early resources and levels which TBH did mess with the experience. The post game is lacking be warned. The difficulty setting is a great way to fix your over powered heros at the start of the game if you grinded like a zombie with tunnel vision. The difficulty spike can be hard at first but once you make the right items its GG, increase difficulty again :D
Looking forward to the next chapter or Atelier Ryza I already put it in mah wish list.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have spent a lot of time playing this game, mostly to get my moneys worth since I took too long to try and refund it. I enjoy the crafting and combat system, but the story progression is like pulling teeth.

Way too often the story is only progressed by you having to fast travel to a certain location to watch a cutscene, fast travel back to your hideout to watch another cutscene where the character discussed what they just saw and how to deal with it, Ryza says she'll solve it with alchemy, you craft something, return to the last location, use your item and then move to the next location to repeat the process all over again.

At one point you discover a new location, and in a cutscene the party says let's return to the hideout to talk about this, you pull up your map to fast travel there, a cutscene triggers where you all talk about the new location, a party member asks to talk to you BACK at the location you just left, so you pull up your map to fast travel there to watch another cutscene to progress the story.

I suppose the idea is between all of these cutscenes the freedom is there for you to explore and gather items without being locked it, but the pacing is so poor and so many scenes could be shortened and combined to make the progression less painful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was stuck in Atelier Sophie and Atelier Lydie and Suelle due to some random exploration not triggering the necessary recipes needed for me to progress the story. I'd say Atelier Ryza fixes that and has the most layman-friendly synthesis system where you know exactly what's needed to upgrade an item or discover a new formula. I managed to finish Atelier Firis but the ending was lackluster, so Ryza feels like the most "proper JRPG" like of the series thus far. Of course I also liked Rorona and the time limits, but it gets annoying when you have, say 4 days to venture and kill 20 monsters and that sort of thing, these kinds of stupid quests are thankfully nonexistent here.

The only downside I think is that things seem too streamlined and linear, but I guess that's the price for the best production value Atelier thus far.\t\t\t\t\t
