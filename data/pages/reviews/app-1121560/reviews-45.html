\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game comes together very nicely. What really blows my mind is how well the different elements are balanced. There is almost no drudgery in this game, as quests, crafting, gathering and leveling all line up so nicely that you are busy and engaged all the time. Here's what I mean:
- Story missions offer you some direction, which will take you to areas with new resources for your alchemy.
- The enemies dotted around the place not only offer a nice challenge and distraction, but also contribute to character side quests which unlock additional skills and perks.
- A decently sized, but limited, gathering basket encourages careful resource management and frequent trips back to base, where you will often have cut scenes boosting character development and will get a chance to craft without slaving away for endless hours over a hot cauldron.
- The town is full and lively, but also offers a good supply of more common resources
- Buying resources is expensive, encouraging you to craft and sell your excess goods. This doesn't mean making 4000 daggers, looking at you, Skyrim, but rather means that you won't sit with useless armour and weapons in your inventory.

All of the above comes together to make the game feel full. You don;t feel rushed, which is nice for the tone, but you do feel like there is always a bunch of stuff to do. Add to this a combat system that encourages quick thinking and fast reactions, and some truly stellar voice acting throughout the ENTIRE game, and you have the king, or queen, of the Atelier series.

Oh (and this may or may not be a selling point for some of you) Ryza, and even the sub-character who is 90% boob, are surprisingly and impressively (for a JRPG) unsexualised. Sure you're spend a lot of time watching Ryza's thighs rub together as she runs between dungeons, but it's less erotic than it is a great advertisement for talcum powder. Seriously, that's got to burn.

TL;DR: If you're a fan of JRPG, or a fan of crafting sims, get it, you will not be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtelier Ryza is probably one of the best Atelier games to start on this amazing franchise. This game doesn't have a lot of content or ultra hard battles like the previous trilogies but has probably the best cast and a really good story.

The battle system is in real time but you can use a command to pause the time and use any item or skill that feels really good (and you will use a lot this mechanic for certain difficult fights).

The alchemy system is really fun, especially to find new recipes. I feel the alchemy system is easier than in past games, mostly because the items are infinite now and you have reason to use them a LOT even in normal fights.

Atelier Ryza has a more traditional story than other Atelier games and still finds time to develop the characters, there's no character endings but you can still find a really good conclusion to each one.

The music is OK, theres other Atelier games or JRPG with amazing OST out there, but is fine, theres a song with lyrics in the middle game that is actually pretty good, but I still find Atelier Firis having the best one.

I really hope this is an Atelier Ryza trilogy, I need more of this amazing girl.\t\t\t\t\t

While Sophie has a lower price point, the overall story of that one felt a bit more flat than the Ryza story. The alchemy mechanics in this game are more beginner friendly than with Sophie in my opinion as well. Both entries are pretty liberal or nonexistent with timelines, but the flow of Ryza helps to eliminate any perceived pressure of time or resources in Sophie.

Do yourself a favour and buy this if you're still new or absolutely new to the Atelier series.\t\t\t\t\t
