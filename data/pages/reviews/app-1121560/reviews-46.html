\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was hesitant at first having never played an Atelier title before but I'm very glad I decided to give it a try!! The story was fantastic and not overly serious which was nice - it was just overall pretty relaxing and enjoyable and didn't feel generic at all. Overall I had an amazing time with the game and can't wait for the second one!

A couple more things to highlight:
-I beat the game in about 32 hours going along at a good pace but also watching all of the cutscenes, there is probably another 10 or more hours of content I could explore if I wanted to. If you really took your time with crafting and side quests you could easily get over 50 hours of gameplay.
- The game, for a JRPG, is really not very story/cutscene heavy, maybe 20% of your time, but it's nothing like a Neptunia game or something. It follows a pretty distinct pattern of story, go collect/kill stuff, craft, more story, go collect/kill stuff, craft, more story - which I know sounds boring but the story itself and progression keeps it interesting.
- Crafting is confusing at first but before long gets easy and is actually a pretty cool /fun way to make stuff. As long as you are picking materials up as you go there is not a ton of back-tracking that you have to do in my experience.
- Other's said it and they're right, you basically need a controller. I usually prefer keyboard but I took one look at the keyboard controls and grabbed my xbox controller.
- Personally I would buy the game even at full price for the enjoyment I got out of it and after playing it I'm happy to pay full price for the second one.

Anyways, great game, I strongly recommend!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe art and music are good, but the story is super boring. At first I thought I was going to put in effort to learning the story and about the characters, but they're all just so bland and extremely generic. Just copy and paste any tomboy, strong guy, and weak nerdy guy and slap them on this game. And the story is just... so damn uninteresting. "Oh boy we get to go on an adventure!" but 20 hours later nothing happens. "Oh man a slime with a smiling emoji for a face... WHAT A TOUGH MONSTER HOLY JESUS TAKE IT EASY ON ME" kills monster in 2 hits after 8 min tutorial* "WHEW THAT SURE WAS A TOUGH BATTLE, GREAT JOB TEAM" *everyone high-fives*. I don't know why this has almost become standard for Japanese/Anime styled games. A simple "here's how you kill the thing" would be more then enough. If I were to put it into prospective of an anime, imagine if a show took 5 episodes just to get up and go to the store, Its unnecessary. And also the "bad guys" are pointless, you cant relate to them, its like there was no effort going into it at all. They're only there to drag out the already extremely dragged out story.
When the alchemy system got introduced I was already checked out from the lack of actual game-play after having played for awhile, even then there wasn't much to it and it was also overly explained in a poor way to leave me with questions about it while also somehow boring.

The game is just another real time turn based game, only hyped up because this time its got thicc thighs and big tiddies.
I would like to say this is a decent enough game... but not for the price. I would maybe pick it up for a third of its current price. It doesn't feel like a $60 game.\t\t\t\t\t
