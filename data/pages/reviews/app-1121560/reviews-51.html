\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I talk about anything negative, I want to make it clear. I like the game, and I do recommend it. However this "positive review" will contain mainly one thing I don't like.

I don't mind the game play changes like the battle system and alchemy system. The main complaint is about the story, or more specific... the way the story is told.

I played dusk series first back few years ago, and was fascinated by the world building. Yet even though the story took place in an apocalyptic world, it still got the light-hearted tone of Atelier series. This is probably the reason I like the Dusk series. The key word is "light-hearted but not mundane".

The problem start with mysterious series, but Atelier Ryza is the worst in this department. Simply saying, this game's story is light-hearted AND mundane. Also, too much human-related drama, Atelier series in my opinion shine in the idea of adventure, mystery, exploration.

There are always relationship aspects in the series, true. But when human drama takes the main spot, that is the issue. About two-third of this game's story focus on human drama, but not adventure. Only toward the end the story start to pick up the pace (which I started to enjoy).

Overall, this is a good game. But if the series' story going in the direction I mention above, I'm not sure if I will like it that much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat I liked:
+ Love the graphics and cutscenes since it is very similar to anime.
+ I enjoyed the free rein the game gives where you can easily go around to the different zones to either just walk around collecting alchemy ingredients or to simply just explore and watch the scenery.
+ My first Atelier game and I feel like, from my experience its a good entry point for newcomers and I liked the crafting system a lot.
+ The soundtrack is catchy and it took me 52 hours before I beat the campaign given I did spend a lot of time doing all the side quests and crafting.
+ No time limit. Heard other Atelier games have time limits. Played visual novels with time limits and I am just not a big fan being limited by time.

What I disliked:
- I dislike the fact that everything is in instances/maps. Makes it feel like the old monster hunter games where everything feels disconnected from the town.
- Will not post spoilers but I do feel the actual story wasn't very dramatic or long.
- While there were points that seemed like it was supposed to be climatic/dramatic it just didn't feel like that. I think for certain sections the pacing was to fast so it didn't have an impact on the player.
- If you didn't get as absorbed into the crafting as I did I feel you can finish the game in easily half the time. Maybe even a third if you didn't explore or revisit areas.

What I hope for Atelier Ryza 2:
+ I hope for it to be open world. With a game with a big focus on collecting ingredients and crafting through alchemy, a world fully connected will promote travelling and searching as opposed to teleporting to whatever instance you need to for specific ingredients/quests.
+ A longer story line. Even though I had a long play time in this game most of it was spent doing crafting and exploring. I feel they can at least double the length of the story or more.
+ Don't make content only available after the story unless its after story stuff, and maybe give us all the important and difficult crafting recipes a bit before the game. That way someone could do everything there is before finishing the story. I know some people may not like this but after I finished the story for Ryza I didn't feel a need to keep playing. Might have to do with the ending but without a story to drive you forward and other games on your list to play just doesn't feel worth it to continue to play just to make every crafting recipe and to do after game stuff.

+ When I first started Ryza I thought we were going to be able to craft and add more to the base to better personalize it, however all you really do it discover more skins for the already built house, and no option to customize the interior furniture. I hope Ryza 2 had a more involved base builder.

Overall:
+ I think Atelier Ryza is a great game and I will most likely buy or even preorder the second one that is coming this Winter. So far I have not looked at what changes will be made, but I hope they will address some of the changes I would like. Even if it doesn't I will probably still get it since I enjoyed this one so much.\t\t\t\t\t

One thing is a bit disappointing though for me, which was the battle system, i like the usual turn based more rather than real time battle like this one, since we can only control 1 character with this kind of system, while usually we would control 3 or more character at once.
The no time limit is nice too, with that i can gather anything, anytime. Felt a bit weird at first though, since this one was the first atelier series i played that have no time limit. I kind of looking for the next Ryza, but looks like it will keep the real time battle too, so i only looking forward for the story, and most likely only going to buy it when its on discount. Even in the future atelier series i would only buy it on discount, unless if its going back to the old turn based (especially if the gameplay would be like the Mana Khemia 2 gameplay, i like that the most).\t\t\t\t\t
