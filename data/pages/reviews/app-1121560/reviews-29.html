\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecided to pick this game up because I'd always been a bit curious about the Atelier series, and I wanted to start with Ryza because she's super cute! Plus, it'd been a while since I played an actual JRPG, and I figured this would be a good way to slip back into the groove of 'em.
The gameplay was nice and chill, and the loop wasn't tedious at all (if you did the crafting correctly, for which there's a ton a different methods in guides to help you along). I can see why people really enjoy the crafting in these games--it's a bit of a hurdle to grasp initially, but once you learn the ropes it's pretty easy to get sucked into even if you're not hardcore about min/max.
The combat was simple, but activating combos was what made it fun.
Story was decent. Nothing crazy, no plot twists or shock value, but that wouldn't fit in this game imo. This is the kind of game that ends on a good note and tugs a tiny bit at the heartstrings. This isn't a story that'll drastically move you, but it's still a wholesome story that leaves you satisfied about how the characters grew.
All in all, I'd give the game an 8.5/10. Definitely worth playing if you want a warm game that has forgiving mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t

A great game to play even on 2022

I started playing for the first time in September 2021 and ended on Feb 2022 hunting every achievement in game without guides or any other sort of help expect the game manual. This game is not big in terms of maps and locations, but quite extensive in content. I always liked Ryza character art , I even bought her figure when it came couple of years ago without even thinking of playing the game or anything. She just looks great. Now that I played this game I got more to add, Ryza and pals are a very fun crowd. They all have their unique personality which becomes more develop as the game progresses, even NPCs are well designed.

This game has a really relaxing atmosphere, everything adds up to make it relaxing, the picturesque landscapes, the music and the no rush gameplay. I had tried other Atelier games before but found them either too dumb, childish or boring to keep me interested. So this is the first atelier game that I like,

I won't add up much to whatever has been said, if you like rpgs, crafting games, good stories with great characters Atelier Ryza will deliver. I higly recommend it, The only negative thing I must add is that the difficulty is a bit easy, and the combat a bit simple, but besides that every mechanic is very well done and animations are exciting and flashy.

Verdict: 9/10

Give it a shot! great story, characters, and a good relaxed atmosphere.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, this was my first modern Atelier game, though I end up playing all of Ayesha, a good chunk of Escha & Logy, and some Sophie as well before beating it, owing in part to a lull around the 20 hour mark that left me a little unmotivated. But once it picked up from that lull, it was really engaging and the ending was really good. I struggled with the final boss and considered grinding (I generally avoided enemies for chunks of the game), but decided instead to work on alchemy, and got through it. It felt really satisfying to think I might be underleveled but still able to solve it by thinking about it more.

Of course, the question that begs to be answered is, Is this a good entry point for the series. And I think it very much is. It's not the best Atelier has to offer and if you're going to get deep into synthesis (one of the major highlights of the series), it's a little simplistic. But I think it gives you enough of the comfy setting and good mechanics that, if you're going to like the series, you're going to like Ryza, and probably want more. Plus, there's no pressure to get a bundle.

All in all, it was a great experience and I'm very happy to have played it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI promise to do this review without further mention of t̶̶h̶̶i̶̶g̶̶h̶̶s̶̶.

Atelier Ryza is a simple wholesome coming-of-age tale where three headstrong youths discover their paths in life. It has a laid-back and lighthearted flow where you watch the friendship of the main characters develop and grow. There isn't any main villian and the focus of the gameplay is on the amazingly deep alchemy system.

Through alchemy, with all the possible branching nodes overloading you with information, you can easily fall into the rabbit hole of chasing after the best of every type of equipment. No matter what difficulties you may encounter in combat, everything can be solved by delving deeper into the alchemy tree.

The downside to this is that game moves like peak hour traffic. At every point in the story, you'll have to head back to the atelier to craft something. Furthermore, with crafting and experimentation comes material gathering. Then back to monster hunting and rinse and repeat. This is not that huge of an issue with the intricately designed alchemy system making things interesting, but it feels rather stilted with the glacially slow pace.

I personally prefer JPRGs with higher stakes involved or with a more refined storyline, but with it's unique gameplay, Atelier Ryza provides an excellent change of pace. If you're looking for a relaxed and charming game with a sophisticated crafting system, then this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good game, if you like JRPGs and crafting, but some caveats before you go in:

The story is a very mellow sort of slice-of-life vibe and it really takes a while to get going and make you care about the characters. If you have no interest in that sort of thing, be aware.

The combat is somehow simultaneously way more complicated than it needs to be and mind-crushingly easy. While I am by no means a member of the idiotic "git gud" crowd who mistake poor encounter design for difficulty, this game *really* is too easy by default. And that hurts everything else - because crafting is the heart of the game and you really, really, really don't need to craft ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ for like 3/4 of the game (unless a quest forces you to) because the combat is so easy. I recommend tooling around on default difficulty for a few fights until you figure out the nonsense combat system and then jacking it up to "very difficult" otherwise the game is just... stupidly easy.

Pretty good, tho, all around. Fun game. Great crafting system once you have a reason to participate in it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpoiler Free.

First and foremost the core gameplay of Atelier Ryza is centered on an extremely deep collectible/crafting system. The majority of progress made by the player requires extensive harvesting of materials which allow new items to be crafted. This primary element of gameplay demands a slight learning curve from the player, and initially can feel intimidating. But once the player becomes proficient it is an absolute joy to hunt down specific materials to create more powerful weapons or specific items needed for progression. I'd even go so far as to say that Atelier Ryza arguably has the best crafting system ever developed. One potential downside to such a deep system is the necessity to revisit the same areas to collect all of the needed items. Some players will find this rewarding and others will find it frustrating. If you're impatient with farming items or constant use of a crafting system this game is not for you. If you do enjoy these elements then Atelier Ryza almost over-delivers in how vast the crafting system can feel.

The world Atelier Ryza takes place is in is sufficiently large and varied enough to maintain player interest. Don't expect super deep world building or lore. There's just enough exposition and fragments of history in the story to give the player a taste of the state of the world and your purpose in it. Early in the game the explorable areas might seem like they lack variety. However, by the midgame and into the lategame much larger and more interesting areas become available. Once new areas are unlocked they are easily accessible again via a straightforward fast travel system.

Combat is fairly simple and easy to learn. A unique hybrid of turn-based and real-time strategy is implemented. Quick decision making is mandatory, as taking too much time to take action can result in poor fights. There are no random encounters - all enemies can be visibly seen on the overworld and it's left up to the player's discretion if they wish to fight or avoid them. You're limited to a party of 3 characters. As you meet new potential party members you can sub them in or out of your combat party. Characters can be built into filling specific combat roles. Each character has predetermined traits that make them better in some roles and worse in others. Here the crafting system is also very relevant as getting newer and better gear demands that the player create it themselves. The monster and boss designs are eclectic - some are extremely cool in design and ability and others are simply cheesy. Despite this wide range in design every creature feels like it belongs in the world.

The story is passable. While definitely not a gem, story elements are just interesting enough to hold Atelier Ryza together over the finish line. Characters fit very cleanly into common anime tropes, and their individual motivations are mostly made immediately clear. As the story progress each character's back stories are explored to some degree, but not extraordinarily deeply either. While the story is most used as a tool to introduce the player to new explorable areas, there are some pieces of the story arc that are genuinely compelling.

Graphically Atelier Ryza is quite pretty. Each area, especially the later game areas, feel unique and well designed. Locations vary wildly in mood and appearance - open plains, mystical ruins, moody dungeons, a charming city, The character models are well designed. Some animation feels a bit rough.

Atelier Ryza has elements of fan service throughout the game. Blatant nudity is never featured. If these elements generally put you off from playing a game but the game itself sounds interesting, consider bracing yourself to give Atelier Ryza a try. The rest of you... Bonk - straighttohornyjail.

Ultimately, Atelier Ryza is a quality crafting and collectible game with JRPG elements.\t\t\t\t\t
