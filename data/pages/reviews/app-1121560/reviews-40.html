\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my first Atelier game, and honestly, I'm very impressed. Some of the things I've heard about the older games like there being a hard time limit, actually kinda turn me off just a tad but not severely, and I've heard things about how the older games have a more complicated crafting system, which considering this game has one of the most robust crafting systems I've ever SEEN, I'm now interested in trying them.

Considering basically the entire game format changed between games, it's understandable that some people might not like this game compared to the others, but as someone who's starting this series for the VERY first time, well, I'm very interested. Not only that, but I like this format, and different as it may be, I haven't played the others so I don't have context for them yet, from what I've heard, I can definitively say this game is still a great place to start, even if you are more interested in the older games.

Despite the grievances I've seen about the new format, I simply have to brush them off as irrelevant, because on it's own this game is STILL excellent, and while it's different, majorly changing a series doesn't mean the new entry is BAD, the old entries still exist and people still have the freedom to make more games inspired by the old ones, like how Ni No Kuni 2 is an entirely new format, but despite that, it's still a great ARPG dungeon crawler.

Although at the end of the day, I just really like a casual pace and super detailed crafting. I plan on playing the other games, but I'm going to judge them mostly separately to this one, considering I myself am biased towards liking one of the big changes, since I tend to hate time limits in almost any game. Considering this is literally the 23rd game in the series, the game reinventing itself is by no means a problem considering there are literally over a dozen games of the old format to enjoy.

So I've talked a lot about other people's complaints, but why is this game fun? It's very casual, encourages gathering and crafting above combat, and also has a lot of charming and well written dialogue. It's charming and yet also addictive, but casual enough that it can get me through work when I need to switch away from the game a lot, since I don't have to time manage or think about how I can advance the goals each in-game day.\t\t\t\t\t

Atelier Ryza: Ever Darkness & the Secret Hideout

Story: 6/10
Not one to talk about for years, the Atelier Series has been known for its slow burn stories with a big smattering of slice of life. While not mind blowing, it does give that warm feeling of a bowl of soup in a cold evening.

Gameplay: 10/10
Changing from Turn Based Combat to a ATB type of combat has made my time playing this game absolutely enjoyable. Timing attacks and managing your global party resource is important for bosses as they will absolutely destroy if you're not prepared or have a modicum of strategy in your head. This is where the Alchemy system in Ryza comes in.

Unlike other entries to the Atelier series, Atelier Ryza has streamlined the Alchemy system to a easy-to-understand and quite enjoyable experience from the complicated Tetris-like or the Shopping list forms of its predecessors. Gathering ingredients will now require crafting Tools using the new system to make them and building up your Alchemy experience and Party's Gears is more important than leveling up, since Atelier Ryza is more Gear dependent.

Graphics: 9/10
Stunning and beautiful at times. The models and the artwork of this game is top notch and the effects are quite beautiful. If you have played previous installment of the series though, you might notice recycled assets like the Apple Trees or the Cliffs near the Beach from previous installations. Gust Corp. has previously admitted that making new assets for every game would prevent them from releasing a new title every year and would very much likely kill the Atelier series due to its niche place in the world of JRPGs.

Music: 8/10
Great overall. Some are quite memorable like the Ancient Dragon battle with an oddly upliftting tone. Most tracks are quite positive, including the Boss music, which gives the idea that the music is not there for the enemies to intimidate but actually make you believe that you can do it.

Should you buy it? Definitely\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe adventures of disobedient, runaway children whose behaviour is encouraged by suspect adults.

- The crafting system is IMO the main draw of this game, it's easy to make basic stuff and you're not really short on common stuff, but for the higher tier stuff you do need to check the in-game guide for where to farm materials. At least they put it in the game!
- Combat is a simpler version than many JRPG systems but it still has a lot of flexibility, so that was refreshing
- This isn't a fanservice or waifu/husbando game, it's all about F R I E N D S H I P
- Character models look good and the environments fit really well with the charas
- Characters have backstories which play into the main story and you develop connections to them
- I'm not too far into the story, but so far I've been led pretty well through it with only small amounts of XP grinding
NOTE: Check the Memo, because quest requirements are put in there for the main story. I didn't notice that until about 10 hours in
- I play on Hard, which requires more strategising than Normal, but most enemies are still easy if you understand the combat well enough. If you absolutely hate grinding try Easy
- Once you hit a specific story point (earlyish) you get a travel map which cuts way down on travel times, a great feature
- The BGM is nice but slightly repetitive. You can change the tracks that play in different parts of the game and it comes with a secondary OST to select from
- I generally dislike console ports with no PC-native controls but it's not that bad to get used to them here. Make sure to 'Enable mouse operation' in the launcher settings

No real complaints, no crashes glitches or slowdowns. I didn't pay full price for this game, I got it around the AUD 40 / USD 30 mark which was I think a great deal. If I keep having fun I plan to buy the second game on sale as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first the other comments on this page kinda creeped me out a bit, because they mentioned boobs and the protagonist is a teenage girl - but I was pleasantly surprised that this game carries very little 'fan service' and that _hopefully_ they were talking about a different character.

If you're looking for difficult combat - this is not the game for you. The combat keeps your attention because it's real-time, but one of the points of the game is to craft items/weapons so utterly powerful that you completely annihilate anything that stands in your path - and that's fun!

The crafting system is fun to try and min/max, and if you don't want to bother with that then there's a helpful auto-craft button - or for if you just want some high quality components.

Come for the crafting, stay for the story and characters/world, put up with the combat.

As a sidenote, VA is only available in Japanese.

Art: 8/10
Gameplay: 8/10
Story/World: 7/10
Characters: 9/10
Combat: 5/10

Overall: 8/10 - Recommend playing if you like story-driven or crafting games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI promise to finish this review without talking about "thighs". So, this was a pretty wholesome, cute adventure. I like that the story isn't just about saving the world, and it has a more slice of life feel too it. Combat is also pretty cool and different from your traditional JRPGs, but I do wish characters got more abilities when leveling up. If you like crafting, there are a ton of to collect from poisonous flowers to peaches to burnt wood to trash to corpses - Ryza uses anything and anyone to improve her alchemy. Very very tight game, recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game while it was on sale and overall I am very happy that I did. It is a charming adventure and I have enjoyed myself so far. I haven't beaten the game just yet, but it's good enough that I wanted to make sure to give it a positive review. I will come back and edit this if it turns out to be vastly different than I expect from this point. There are not restrictive time limits like in previous Atelier games. This game is still slow paced, slice of life, with a greater focus on alchemy, gathering, and exploring than on combat. Combat is still present, it is turn based but relatively fast paced in its execution. I have not had any technical difficulties in playing the game. My only "critique" of it is that the system they have in place to trigger events and cut scenes often happens in an order that doesn't actually make sense such as switching me from night time to day time or playing events out of order but it hasn't ruined the bigger scope of the story or my enjoyment of the game even it is a little bit jarring at times.\t\t\t\t\t
