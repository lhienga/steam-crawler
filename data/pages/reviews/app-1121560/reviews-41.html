\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDifferent focus from most JRPGs out there. This game appealed to me as being described as a crafting game with JRPG elements and I would heartily agree. The games progression is focused on crafting more than anything else. Your character's become more powerful based on the best gear you can use with them. Many passives that most games would have baked into their classes are instead created as traits on items. This puts the drive on exploring and finding new materials to craft with and seek those rare traits.

That said the game can be a little challenging if you don't work with the crafting system at all. The stats you gain from Items can be the lion share of your power and without them you will hit walls in the story regularly.

I really am enjoying the game and would suggest it to anyone who finds themselves putting a lot of time into other games crafting systems.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Got bored within 25 minutes. Didn't like the dialogue. The graphics are poor. The gameplay isn't much fun and combat is about as exciting as writing a 2000 word essay about how paint dries. There are far better games out there right now that cost a lot less. Even at the current discount of 50% off Atelier Ryza is not worth it. Honestly I wouldn't play it even if it was free to play.

Let me start off by saying that I have ABSOLUTELY NO IDEA how this is scoring 5/5, 9/10, 8/10 with some reviewers, critics, publications, etc. It's a 3/10, maybe a 4/10 at most.

I've been waiting for one of the Atelier games to go on sale so I could try it. Sadly there was nothing at all that made me want to keep playing. Yes, I know I haven't seen much of the game, but I started skipping dialogue almost immediately, didn't care for the cutscenes, and didn't like the overall look or feel of the game. I'm sure it'll appeal to some, but it was bad enough that I've already asked for a refund.

One of the first red flags? The default sprint button is E, so they expect you to hold E while pressing WASD to run. Try pressing E and D at the same time. It's not fun. Yes, I know you can re-bind the keys, but why make the default something that bad to begin with?

Red flag number two. They didn't bother changing on screen button prompts from the XBox prompts when not playing with a controller. I didn't even have one plugged in so it should have defaulted to the keyboard controls, right? Right, it should have but it didn't.

Red flag 3. They also didn't bother putting camera controls on the mouse so you have to use WASD to move around. You use J/K for a/b, RTYG to move the camera, UO to zoom, I for Y... it's a mess. I have no idea how much work it would have taken to put zoom on the mouse wheel, and the camera on the x/y axis of the mouse. Probably very little. Other ports have done it, and done it properly. There's no real excuse here, just too lazy, I guess.

Red flag four. Some menus want you to use W and S to move up and down but the arrow up/down also works. But note always. A few menus won't let you use W and S, you have to use up and down arrows. It doesn't tell you which ones, either, so you mash the key a few times and then figure out that no, they didn't bother adding a few lines of code to make it work properly. More lazy.

Yes, you can re-bind most of the keys and yes, it probably works fine with a controller, but that's no excuse to be so lazy with the KB/Mouse that most PC users would prefer to use and that all PC users have. Not everyone owns, or wants to use, a controller.

Combat is some kind of hybrid real time/turn based mess. Turn based only would have suited the game far better than the system they chose, and it's simply not fun at all. I feel like it's almost and auto-battle where I just push a button to attack, mash it to attack a few times if I've increased my ability by burning action points (or whatever they're called)... and you have to re-purchase that double or triple hit every time you get to a new fight, like you've somehow forgotten how to swing more than once with your default attack unless you spend points on it. My two co-adventurers attack on their own, I can tell them to conserve mana (or whatever the game called it) but that's about it. I can switch targets, use a special attack that's underwhelming, and that's about it. The first enemy was a slime. Yes, they couldn't get any less original than using a slime and that's red flag number five, six, something like that. I've lost count.

Somehow in only 25 minutes of gameplay there were more red flags than that but I've said enough. It was so bad after less than half an hour I couldn't keep going. I gave up playing and asked for a refund. Don't waste your time here. There are far better RPGs out there with way better graphics and far superior game mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t
