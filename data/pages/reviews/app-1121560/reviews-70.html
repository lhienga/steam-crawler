\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAloha! Thicc thighs save franchise! This is my totally biased review of Atelier Ryza; the game is a bog standard JRPG from the venerable Atelier series, I've been playing the game series since PS2 with Atelier Iris and really, this isn't a AAA title, or even a BBB, more of a DD one. But puns on anatomy aside, the game isn't made for outsiders to the series, the game was made for the audience that likes it, Otaku filth like myself, and it sold to them so it has done reasonably well, memes aside. If you have played the other Atelier games then you can expect more of the same, but at a relaxed pace as this one doesn't have the time limit that other ones do, this allows you to explore a colourful world with cute characters to your hearts content. The game includes a photo mode as of the latest patch which allows for all manner of creativity, there is more content ahead through the season pass and extra side stories.

If you aren't a fan of the series or JRPG games, please pass on the game. You won't get it. It isn't for you. It is for people who want play a happy game about making pies with alchemy and hanging out with whimsical friends.

If you are a fan of the series or JRPG games? Don't miss this one for the world.

The Good:
Graphics are colourful, character design is creative, Japanese voice over is delightful, battle system is robust and fresh, environments are varied and exciting, the alchemy overhaul is a welcome change, the music is enjoyable, a relaxing game that is for casual play.

The Bad:
The seasons pass and day 1 DLC is an unwanted addition, but I'm happy to know it will support the niche game that I love and the development of future titles in the series.

The Ugly:
The perpetually running townsfolk that pass through you like ghosts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow I'll start by clarifying that writing this 36 hours into the game, I still feel like I am far from done and I have yet to go through a play session without finding a whole new mechanic to spice up the game and give even more things to do.
I'd also like to state that this is my first atelier game, so my opinion might be biased as someone who doesn't know things that might already be old news.
That said if you like games like neptunia and the tales of games then chances are you will love this one, each core part of the game has multiple ever evolving elements, from the combat's team actions, quick turns and finishers, to story telling being enhanced by the in-party quests and side quests that bring not just random rewards but actually focus on bringing the world to life and expanding on the inhabitants lives.
The alchemy/crafting has even more systems than that, but they are all tied to main story progression, so I will refrain from being overly specific about them.
The characters do fit certain stereotypes like "the buff warrior guy" in very general terms, but in this day and age who doesn't, and it doesn't take very long to see that they aren't just a stereotype but actual characters with their own personalities and relationships.\t\t\t\t\t
