\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuper fun game full of alchemy and exploration and cute anime girls doing cute anime things. The combat is just the right level of complicated, with an active battle system where your companions are AI controlled and you can switch between them at will, although it does skew a little on the easy side so you'll probably want to play on at least Hard if you want any real challenge, and there's not a huge amount of variety in enemies with most of them being palette swaps of the same basic ones in every zone. But the real main event is the crafting system, which seems simple at first but gets hugely involved and you can get sucked into spending hours making perfect items and maxing out all the stats. Great game if you enjoy the whole anime vibe, just want a chill game without any time pressures, and aren't bothered by the lack of English audio.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my first introduction to the Atelier series for starters lol and I'm partial to Dragon Quest, I'll get that out the way xD so lack of ability to go into buildings, climb things, talk to npcs that are overflowing with personality, explore, also specifically lack of ability to explore the world before a "prompt" that allows only a morsel at a time more access to new areas (ex: tried to explore the world a bit more after a minor adventure, had Ryza say that she should get back home after said minor adventure -- I could still explore the areas available to me indefinitely before choosing to go home, but could not go to a new area -- only for going home to consist of more cutscenes leading up to some basic tutorial mumbo jumbo that I wish was just covered on a sidebar or in the menus or something instead of having progression move at a snails pace lol it felt unfulfilling.) I know this lack of exploration is utilized for pacing, but the game has a veeery slow start(I'm about 4 hrs in and still in a tutorial limbo!) that is riddled with a suffocating cloud of tutorial text and what feels like incessant info dumping! I'm having trouble forming it into words, but I think a main issue at hand is that this pacing and restraint feels unnecessary. If the player was given free reign to explore as they wish while immediately being equipped with an interactive interface of sorts that the player could pop out of a side menu at any time that would explain different types of synthesizing by highlighting various "nodes" on the chart/showing a visual representation and then allowing the player to test a hypothetical recipe (without actually needing ingredients and without actually creating an item) on the run, I think everything would be more concise and streamlined, and would ultimately allow the player to feel a deeper sense of adventure right from the get-go.

Normally I wouldn't see this point of bringing up Dragon Quest as very important because it is in the context of comparison, but in this case, I feel that the world would feel a million times more alive and intriguing if many of the aforementioned things were implemented. For now, I feel like I'm entrapped in a very linear sandbox xD I also find the graphics to look very grainy despite having a powerful computer and the environments very simple so far and overall boring(with a few whimsical exceptions, but even those are lackluster/I feel like there's potential for them to be pushed a lot further into the fantasy look they're going for). I'll give the environments some points though in that Ryza's hometown is fairly big, so there is a sense of exploration to an extent. The other environments though...they get old really quickly ~ I want to see new places, encounter new creatures and find new plants!!

Combat feels dull so far although I can't really place why, some characters look ridiculous *cough* Lila *cough*, repetition of voice lines, repetition of end battle scenes(this one isn't the game's fault, it's just in typical jrpg fashion which I have a dislike for lol), repetition of characters saying how they feel/contemplating what's going on recycled, so many cutscenes and unnecessary/fluff dialogue, separation of each zone(I'd prefer it to be seamless), lack of variation in lighting effects, took too long into the game for tutorial to say that I was now allowed to press a button that teleports Ryza back to her room(where the Save option is as well as the alchemy cauldron). (little sidenote, but I checked out a video on Ryza 3 to see if maybe that game fixed some of the things I disliked about Ryza 1 and not even 10 minutes into the INTRO of that game I was immediately met with something that annoyed me: talking about unimportant things "anime-style" before battle along the lines of "I didn't expect this!"/"really? I thought you'd be used to fighting"/"it's really a summer with Ryza" and also talking about said battle instead of just doing it lol I want them to get to the point!! I was sitting there hoping that Ryza 1 at least would steer clear from that and I'm lucky to not have run into it yet if it does happen later in the game xD )

A HUGE ONE for me is the limited inventory space, especially considering how excited I got because running around collecting things freely was a casual experience I was looking for -- only for my inventory to almost instantaneously fill up after not traveling far at all ~ why not have unlimited inventory? Because each item of a single type has its own unique stats (e.g. in a collection of five Rainbow Grapes, each one has different stats), stack same item in inventory, but then create a separate User Interface for when you select each item stack that spreads them out like a deck of cards which showcases each of their stats.

The few pros I've found (aside from the ones mentioned earlier about the town and the semi-whimsical areas) have been that the creature designs are great, the character designs are also great for the most part, the voice acting is solid and paired with mostly pleasant voices, and that the characters are likeable ~ I know people tend to be divided on being fond of Ryza, and while I do find her rough around the edges/a bit prickly at times to her friends and headstrong in her unwillingness to acknowledge when one off her friends says something smart that is better than what she's suggested, I actually really like her -- she's blunt and very honest in regard to the former and a good thing to note is that she doesn't appear to have mal-intent with the things she says, although that doesn't fully excuse her of course lol as far as her not helping her family, yes it would be great if she garnered maturity to aid her family when needed and not just focus on assisting the big picture(the town), who can blame her? Yes, her family or anyone in a similar position 99% of the time do not work the farm crops because they enjoy it, but rather it is out of pure necessity, however, she knows that this is her existence and she probably wants to make the most of it even if it is not the most charitable thing to do for her family and is self-serving. She runs away because, in my eyes, she knows that this cycle of farm work, the reality and occupation bestowed upon her by her parents that has been drawn out to be her destiny essentially, will be quick to rope her in if she does not break free while she can.

Overall, I'm not enjoying my time with this game. I'm holding onto a small thread of hope that it might get better soon(or maybe another Atelier entry might be more fitting to my tastes?), but I can't recommend it at this time as I honestly am borderline hating it lol\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, yes - this game is perfect for those, looking to get into Atelier series of games.

The good:
+ Interesting, lighthearted story about the main protagonist and friends;
+ Thighs;
+ Alchemy system is well fleshed out and it feels rewarding;
+ Nice OST.

The bad:
- Everything revolves around the island, the story takes place in:
I mean, there is exploration and lots of stuff to find, but new towns is
not one of them;
- new areas may feel sameish if you don't enjoy exploring
what's already there;
- not a very big variety of monsters to fight;
- battle system is alright, but could be better, honestly.
Battle system tl;dr: keep calm and throw bomb;
- most of the stuff you collect early in the game, will be worthless,
so don't get too carried away at the start of the game.

Verdict:
It's a good Atelier game, worth getting, but, at this point - only on sale. Don't start Ryza game trilogy with the last game, start it with this one.\t\t\t\t\t
