\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just don't think I like Atelier. I keep hearing from established fans of the series that this game is relatively dumbed down compared to the previous ones. I shudder to imagine what it was like before. What's the point of saying that Ryza made gathering alchemy ingredients "easy" or "easier" if actively doing it is boring and you're uncertain what yields what? A major source of frustration that causes this feeling is the limited backpack space when you're out in the field - you can only carry around 100 items before you have to return to the Atelier which is just insane amounts of backtracking. Consider me filtered by the alchemy making almost no sense to me 15 hours in. It's definitely *unique* but they do it in such a backwards fashion that encourages a lot of boring jogging around, collecting items and picking up garbage that you probably don't need, but you don't know where to get the stuff you do need.

Maybe if Koei Tecmo allowed Gust for just once in their life to bake an Atelier game in the oven for more than a year, they could make something special that stands out from the niche low-budget affair with spreadsheet gameplay that is Atelier. I dig the graphics, music, characters, and even the basic story, but the amount of bumbling around required to fill your coffers with ungodly amounts of garbage you *may* end up needing down the road in alchemy is boring.

They very recently added a Very Easy difficulty option to possibly minimize the difficulty of combat or gear checks required to complete the game, but I'd rather they work on the game itself than just turn down numbers.\t\t\t\t\t
