\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably one of the best JRPG's I've ever played. At first I was very turned off by the craft heavy gameplay and the lack of direction in a lot of parts. You just have to kinda figure it out on your own which may stop a lot of people in their tracks especially with some of the harder crafts later on. But if you can use the magic of google/gamefaqs and youtube tutorials to muddle through. Then it'll be a little game worth remembering. By the end I hadn't thought about how attached I'd gotten to the characters and their stories or the music, the scenery and everything in between. The game sadly ended pretty abruptly for me and I wanted much much more but it was a good paced ride. And thank the creators of the game for teleportation Holy Hell I would have quit this game so fast if I had to manually run everywhere to get everything and back again. I'd have NOPED out so fast. But they thought of nearly everything.

The only thing I'd probably do is help newcomers a liiiittle more with the crafting system it's very confusing until you kinda begin to see the patterns and flows of everything to make this or that. They patched all the stuttering out of the game except I saw a bit in the ending credits but not a biggie. I may actually go back and play the other Atelier games just because of this one. Ryza is lovable and squishy looking. I demand hugs!

Worth every penny, get it if you love JRPG's and want something a little different...\t\t\t\t\t
