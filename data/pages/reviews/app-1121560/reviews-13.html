If you don't plan on using a controller, I suggest either plugging one in or using something like Special K to emulate having a controller plugged in. Without it, the game has horrendous stuttering.

The crafting system is significantly easier than the Mysterious series, so if that turned you away from that series I'd suggest giving this a try when it's on sale.

This game does not have a time limit for the main story quests.

Great music as always and the BGMs from other Atelier games come included as well in case you want to mix it up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtelier Ryza is one of those games that regardless of how much you hear about them you have to experience to fully understand.

It's a slice of life anime made videogame, and a freaking good one at that.

It has all that you would expect from a JRPG, the charismatic characters, the interesting story, the great mechanics that make you enjoy your time with the game i.e. The combat and the crafting. The latter being the central one of the game, which is so extensive, complex and yet understandable that you will love experimenting and discovering new items and stuff.

The game is not hard,only a couple of bosses found me unprepared and got my ass handed back to me. But some good leveling and improvement of gear (the latter more than the former) I went and kicked their asses.

A disclaimer though: do not expect anything groundbreaking regarding the story or characters, they are pretty formulaic, but that's not a bad thing per se, the story does manages to make good use of them and they are still really well voice acted and developed. My point here is that if you have been watching too much generic anime or playing too many anime games you might find these two aspects of the game lacking, and even so, the other aspects of it should be enough to make for an unforgettable experience.

Overall, I give this game a 9/10. One point subtracted because of UI issues, lack of enemy variety and some very simple secondary content. Still, I'd recommend this game to anyone without blinking.

Also, if you are into that sort of thing as a man/woman of culture: YES, THIGHS!!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you enjoy JRPGs, give this game a try. It took me a while to get used to the combat style (especially since I dropped it for a few months to play other games and forgot how it worked), but you get used to it pretty quickly. The alchemy system is also pretty fun, once you learn how it works.

For any new players, DO NOT IGNORE ALCHEMY. I made that mistake, and it made the fights a LOT harder than they should have been. The alchemy is VERY important. Create new equipment, rather than buying it, and then create stronger copies with better ingredients. Create healing items and attack items, and use them on enemies (especially bosses).
