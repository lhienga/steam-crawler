\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly recommended. Like really.

I honestly had no expectations and got this game on offer, there aren't even many reviews online about the game. However it exceeded all my expectations. I am new to the atelier series and I am pleasantly surprise to say I like it alot.

Blue skies, green plains, a calming and easy going atmosphere. The game is beautiful in its anime aesthetic, all the characters are well designs and fleshed out in its intriguing story. The music is top notch, rarely do I look forward to game music, but this one is superb.

I really enjoyed the gathering and crafting mechanics, simple enough for a casual gamer like me. I highly recommend anyone who enjoys a chill adventure and feel proud of crafting your own equipment.

I just finished the game and all i can say is this game made me happy and is worth the money. I look forward to my next adventures with Ryza 2 & 3!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely recommend. The story starts out as pretty standard RPG fare but I did end up enjoying it more as it progressed. The game is definitely a mix of RPG and crafting game and it walks that line pretty close throughout the length of the game. If you are looking for a lengthy RPG with lots of boss fights this might not be what you want as the main story really doesn't have that many boss fights from beginning to end (the post-game has more optional fights though if you want that). The crafting is very powerful and you can make quite powerful items even early on in the game BUT the game doesn't do a very good job at actually explaining the details of the crafting system. You can still craft better weapons and armor without knowing many details but in order to really push your items as far as possible you might need to look up some guides on crafting.

I'm definitely intending on playing the sequel and then eventually the recently-released 3rd game.\t\t\t\t\t

Contrary to popular opinion, I don't think this is that great of a game.
The crafting part seems alright, and the combat isn't bad. The tutorials leave something to be desired and they really aren't that helpful. The UI is simple and easy to use. Is there a story? It feels like there hasn't been one yet and my missions don't feel like they're part of one yet. The characters seem pretty bland, and every conversation seems... like it's missing personality. I'm a little disappointed with the dialog, and the lack of subtitles for even random spoken over-world phrases, which really nags at me a little. (On a side note, is there a reason there's only Japanese full audio? Is that just an Atelier thing? Or does Koei Tecmo just not include that in the budget?) The world seems empty, and as a crafting game, I was expecting a slightly more open world. There's a lot of repeat model use. Npcs, buildings, plants, monsters, you name it. The movement feels chunky and the environments have a bunch of invisible barriers that make exploring weird. Over-all, it seems like this game is an older game where they updated the graphics big time, but made no other improvements anywhere else. I feel like I'm playing an old PS1/PS2 game but the graphics keep tricking my brain into thinking is closer to current gen. This shouldn't be $60, this should be the sale price or probably less. It definitely seems like Koei is just doing what the makers of CoD and the big sports games do by updating the graphics of an older game, but keeping the content the same with maybe some minor updates, and of course the high price tag. I don't recommend this game the way it is.\t\t\t\t\t
