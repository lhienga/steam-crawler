\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA nice fun Atelier game. Story was consistent and steady and the main characters had good growth through out.

A few minor complaints:
*The game leaves you hanging quite often by giving you recipes several chapters before the materials become available. Not nuanced plot things that need a special drop, I mean you won't get all the harvesting tools for at least half of the game but you get the recipes at the very beginning.
*It also does this with some quests, where they show up on the map long, long before they can be accepted. For ones where you need to do alchemy to unlock them that makes sense, but some just require you to do other quests. (all of the side quests are tangled together)
*Quests also do this really annoying thing where you need to let an in-game day pass before you can do the next one. So you do a quest, go back to your bed, run back over to the NPC, do a quest, go back to your bed, over and over again for several very simple sidequests.
*The trait system is RNG hell, probably half of the traits should not exist (normal, plus, and plus plus... r-really?)
*Basket system is a nuisance, you whack a single tree and the thing is nearly full, even with a maxed out basket, as some of them drop like 150 items if your axe has bonuses.
*Because every item is treated as a unique entity it makes purging hundreds and hundreds of logs (for example) a real chore, whether you are deleting or selling or breaking them down into gems you will be clicking hundreds of individual items.\t\t\t\t\t

Ryza (The character herself) is not very interesting or likeable, and her default outfit makes her look like the victim of some sort of clothing based explosion. As always, the only reason she is a popular character is because Weeaboos want to stick their ♥♥♥♥ in it.

The story-line is boring and pedestrian. Nothing interesting ever happens, and the character development feels incredibly forced. (I was only able to get half-way through the campaign before I shelved the game, so I don't know what happens beyond chapter 5)

Where the game really falls apart is the gameplay, which is predicated on the Alchemy System. One can spend hours clicking around in the Alchemy Menu, and getting caught up in the minutia of the complex alchemy system, and get absolutely no-where. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tSince starting this game, I have broken off all ties with my wonderful mentor—Marie Kondo. The game just has too many ingredients to hoard and I didn't have the will to leave it alone!

Atelier Ryza is my first game from the Atelier series. It features a heartwarming story of adolescence and finding purpose in a seemingly uneventful land. The world features a lot of flavor text and there's much to learn about the residents even though half the residential maps may feel void of life. If you give it some effort in the NPC interactions, you are sure to find good backstories that show there's more to the game than meets the eye.

After finishing the game, I thought that the combat was a-okay. It featured the tactics system of progressively stronger attacks by building up a meter which I have seen in older iterations such as Legaia 2. While it's not groundbreaking, the combat does just enough to support the main star of the story—the Alchemy.

Alchemy/Synthesis absolutely carried this game. After beating most of the content, I found myself losing track of time figuring out material loops, unlocking deeper recipes, and gathering the right materials to create game-busting equipment/utility items. Putting a lot of care into this aspect of the game makes sense considering the meaning of an Atelier. I'm very satisfied with the amount of enjoyment I drew from this feature.

I also have to give credit to the composer of the Underworld theme and the battle theme for the Elementals. It's beautifully done, but it is such a shame that those battles were short-lived due to the depth of the combat.

It's a good game, but I'd be hard pressed to buy it at full price. The base game is already priced very steep here in Canada ($68.99) so as much as I want to explore the other games, I need to wait for a sale. I think that the paid cosmetic DLC for the various costumes is a good thing especially since I know there fans for this series that can appreciate and support cosmetic options for a fair price (ranging from free to $3.40 here). What I disapprove of is selling DLC stories that are either OP or VERY short for absurd prices ($6.69-10.29). It makes me feel like it was chopped up from the main game to sell to uninformed buyers. I am personally not paying the extra $30 for an OP island and content I can finish in 1... maybe 2 hours.

To summarize, buy the base game on a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never played an Atelier series game before so this one was my first. I really enjoyed it though, I had a great time from beginning to end. The combat was fun, even if more on the simple side. The crafting system was great, it was a blast to toil away for hours crafting insanely powerful gear. The story was interesting the entire way through, the characters were fantastic, and there was a variety of scenery and environments to traverse. It was kind of like playing through a Slice of Life anime with some action on the side. If you're a fan of JRPGs or crafting games I would definitely recommend checking this one out. Will definitely be picking up the sequels for this to continue the adventure.\t\t\t\t\t
