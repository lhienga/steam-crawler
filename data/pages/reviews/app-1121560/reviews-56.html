\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game looks very pretty, and as usual for Atelier games, it has a very casual relaxing atmosphere. The real time battle system was really hard to get used to, and I still prefer the usual turn based ones, but after doing it a few times, it wasn't so bad as it felt the first few times after all.
Synthesizing was fun, I loved how you could get to know new recipes while doing normal alchemy, much better than doing the weirdest thing to finally get the one recipe you want like in other previous titles.
For the characters and story, I sadly have to say I didn't care too much about anyone besides Klaudia and Ryza. Lent did have a ton of potential with his abusive father, but it got pushed so much to the side, that it felt kind of hard to sympathize with him or really anyone else from the party too much. Unfortunately, as much as I love the Atelier Series, too flat characters always was one of the things I'm not really fond of, and Ryza didn't really improve much in that aspect.

However, Ryza herself was a great fresh breeze of a main character, especially because she's the first not that skinny mc, and I really appreciate that. All in all it was a fun game, perfect to relax once in a while (even tho I have to admit it's very hard to pick up again after a longer break), nice graphics, good soundtrack and just a warm feeling atmosphere in general.
Sadly it's still not something that feels super unique, but if a medicore story and cute (but kinda not that deep) characters are enough to satisfy you, it's definitely worth the try.\t\t\t\t\t

Pros
  • Everything centers around the synthesis system; battling monsters, gathering resources, completing quests all relate
  • Unique combat system utilising real-time & turn-based; becomes much more complex the harder the difficulty played on
  • Location and aesthetic design is extremely detailed and a delight to explore
  • Plethora of effects & traits allow for an incredible amount of customization on weapons, armor, accessories, and usable items
  • Synthesis system becomes more of a thinking & calculation game as you progress and start to preserve more rare material
  • Story & general setting of the game is very wholesome; designed to be a more casual experience than hardcore
  • Koei Tecmo provides an official web manual that explains the basics of most game mechanics
  • The hideout OST will be engraved into your brain
  • Ryza {Best Girl}

Cons
  • About 90% of the monsters are reskins including bosses
  • Game can become very overwhelming and provides a lot of information in a short period of time; recommended for new players into the Atelier series to take time to understand the synthesis system
  • Content can feel short, story can indeed be completed in 25-40hrs, however, synthesising new items and equipment is what sells the enjoyment
  • Legendary scaling is not balanced for the end-game Secret Solitary island even with min-max gear
  • Pretty expensive price tag $68.99/$85.99 for Digital Deluxe, assuming you also intent to purchase all additional content it can cost up to another $62.99 for the Kurken Island Jam-packed Pass (Digital Deluxe has its own exclusive content that is not included in the Kurken Pass)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really really good game, definitely one of my favorites in the Atelier series! I was disappointed at first to find that the time system had been stripped completely (other than a simple day/night cycle that only changes when you move locations) because Atelier games tend to not have the most compelling stories, and the time system was what gave a sense of urgency, and made me want to keep going. However the story in this game is actually very nice, there were plenty of mysteries and fun things to discover! The main story reminds me a bit of the Dusk series in terms of theme, which is extremely welcome!

The alchemy system is, as ever, incredibly fun and entertaining. Due to the lack of time limit you can definitely get lost making things for a very long time! The battle system is also very different. The timeline of when who can take a turn is still in place, but it does not stop moving - it makes everything very fast paced. It was very difficult to get used to at first, but once you get the hang of it, it's really fun! You still have to choose between regular Atelier battle options (use item, skills, or regular attack) but you gotta click it Fast. You can switch between characters to command them, but you can also let them auto attack or focus on doing skills. It's a very fun system once you start learning more of the techniques!

Also, if you have been avoiding this game because the fanbase is freakishly horny, know that the game itself is not that bad! Only Ryza and Lila have weird designs, and the cutscenes (despite camera angles) aren't actually weirdly sexual like in many previous Atelier games. Even Lila, the ugliest horny character design I've ever seen, mostly just has cutscenes that treat her like a person instead of what her character design might suggest. I put off playing this game for a very long time because I was worried that the story and cutscenes would be full of pervert pandering but it isn't! It's very good, and you should give it a shot!

Overall it is an incredibly fun game with one of the best written stories in the Atelier series! I would highly recommend this title to just about anyone!\t\t\t\t\t

A Cheap Attempt to reinvent the Series (And not well done)
Before going into my review i would like to state two things, the game is actually a decent game overall, i would personally give it a neutral score if it was a option and additionally, the review is written but a long standing Atelier fan.

As some know, Ryza was Gusts idea of moving Atelier to appeal to new crowds, this involved adding a new live based battle system (personally i feel it failed but can see how this did appeal to new fans) and making the game more accessible (more on this below). A few problems became apparent before even booting the game, first off, you are now charged as extra dlc for character stories, this have always been a part of the Atelier experience, now you no longer will receive character events during the game but can pay a fee for them (they ain't even cheap considering you must buy them separately), this is KT/Gusts idea of cashing in on the modern scummy tactics of shaking every penny out of gamers, the icing to the cake here is the extra dlc isn't even voiced, you are literally paying for text!!! Oh speaking of not being voiced, another change you notice is now almost any event happening outside the main story is no longer voiced, even when it relates to the story, in short they cut about 60% of voice acting out of the game to save that sweet cash whilst charging extra for everything, yes its another example of removed content.

Characters - It's a weird thing to cover early but one i think is needed. Atelier normally sell themselves with likeable characters, our leads are either cute or funny or positive in some way, sadly Ryza really doesn't live up to much. Now don't get me wrong, she isn't a bad character but she is just really plain, she has no real extra personality to her other than enjoying adventures... of course this isn't complimented by the lack of voice acting, it makes her moments to stand out become bland and feel colourless due to Gust cheaping out. Klaudia is for the best part a nice character and was well done, Lila and Empel again felt simple and plain but i guess they served their purpose so i have no real issues there. The two guys who start with you suck however, Lent could of been more but sucks, he has a chip on his shoulder in the early game with certain characters which just vanishes with no real reason but this isn't what lets him down, he actually has a (unvoiced of course) back ground story which was ruined with a poor explanation and summed up to be nothing worthwhile. Tao... i literally cannot express this character alone would ensure the game a bad review, the first 5 hours of the game is him crying and complaining about doing literally anything!! Even in the end of the game he complains about anything which isn't him reading a book, i never have met such a annoying character in video games, EVER! Who sat down and thought he would be a good sub-lead had some real sadist thoughts going on.

Story - The story wasn't all too bad although a bit lacking. You objectives don't really ever feel hard, i almost always had every material i needed for story related items and despite playing hard, it felt like i was on easy. The story line however was enjoyable, it felt easy to go mission to mission and it didn't really feel like it wanted to spread the story out but i did enjoy the story, i felt it was rather decently written and the ending whilst rather basic did sum up and end it off nicely.

Battle system - The new battle system is just bad, It will take a while for old fans to adjust and they wont likely enjoy it. It combines the older queue system whilst making it so there is a "wait" between turns, this however doesn't work well. For starters, speed is useless, it makes out this is relevant but Klaudia can have 500 more speed than anyone else and still takes her turn last when entering battle and because her animation is so long, she will take loads less turns. Equally, the system degrades the purpose of items, you now have unlimited items which are controlled by "core uses" which essentially means you can use items x times. It's rather boring though, early game it makes them feel pointless and late game when you can use them, they are over powered in a dull way. Items no longer have a image, remember using a ice bomb and it was a ice bomb whilst a barrel bomb looked like a barrel, uni bombs was shaped like uni ext, now every item is pretty much the same and just uses the same way, its very cheap (again). The battle system also fails in that skills are pointless, on your "turn" you will waste ages if you don't know every skill to memory and thus waste "turns", using skills feels pointless as it takes twice as long to select a skill than just hitting attack and the time it takes to select a item, everyone has taken a turn, its so stupid. Equally, it's just the same thing, mash your attack button to up the tactics level (a new feature) until the enemy charges a skill, then use a quick command and spam skills and repeat, every battle including bosses plays out this way.

Alchemy - not all was lost, actually the games new alchemy system is amazing. I do prefer the older systems in that they took some learning which was kind of fun but i think the new system was simple for new players to follow but even as a veteran, i enjoyed the "skill tree" like layout of items, i admit this was a feature done right.

Atelier Ryza (Your "home") - another good feature, you can customise numerous elements of your house which offer minor effects outside such as enhanced stats in certain weather or at night, increase drop rates of rare items ect or if you are like me, it just added a fun way to add your own touch to the house.

Overall
As you might see, i am let down by numerous elements of the game. However, the game is fun to play, it still felt like a atelier game even if it did lose some of its identity on the way. I feel most let down in the stripped content, the game is actually more money than most other Atelier games and yet it has little voice acting present, removed character endings and content ready to be sold back to you at a price. It feels they stripped the game to it's core and asked a price, not to mention the insane priced season pass. It's a shame really, Ryza has potential to be good, heck it is actually fun but the positives are shoved aside by the need to want extra money... which in turn got them none from me, i don't mind buying extra dlc but i wont purchase content which was removed to be sold back to me.

Is the game recommended - yes but no. For new players you will likely enjoy the new systems the game offers and it should be a easier step into the Atelier world. Some older players will like the changes, others will feel it isn't quite the same. I commend them for trying new systems, even though i don't like the battle system, i accept it was to attract new people, but to think stripping content and going cheap on voice acting will really help new or older players think it's worth a massive £50? no thanks.

Not recommended to veterans because it's pricey with cut content and cheap excuse of voice work (Even Pamela isn't voiced)
For New players - Yes, the bad features shouldn't be so apparent for you and hopefully you will enjoy it to try some older games. Don't buy the dlc, its included in older editions...\t\t\t\t\t

Playing the game it had a slow start(It had nothing to hook me). But it grew on me to continue it BUT the main complaint i have about the game is the lack of depth and length of the story. There is basically just 1 main story that is resolved quickly. There didn't seem to be any hurdles for your characters aside from basic stuff about parents, life and chores. It felt lacking and the end seems to come too fast.

- Fun\\Charming world
- Alchemy is still fun to mess around with

- Just right amount and difficulty of achievements
