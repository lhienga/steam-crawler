\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI did sooooo much inventory management in this game (there's so many different items with different stats and bonus elements as well, trying to find a good build literally took about most of my game time).

I found Ryza to be a memorable character who at times could be a bit annoying and stubborn (Basically her way or it's a no go) she does however grow a bit throughout the main game. Her friends/party members were all good and provided variety and added depth to the story and also all had individual and unique personalities.

Combat was also different as it had a sort of turn based combat but in real time. So at first can be difficult but after a while it's pretty fun to think/fight quickly. One downside would be the enemy designs/variation as there is only about 15 enemies and new ones are shown with a new name and different colour design.

The main story overall was good as it is quite relaxed and easy to follow (It's basically a growing of age type game for Ryza and her friends), personally though I found the side quests to be a more interesting aspect of the game as you get a look at all the people in the town which made the game feel more lived in (I would recommend not using fast travel all the time as walking into new areas can set of a cutscene although they aren't of vital importance)

Lastly, when trying to run the game I came into a FPS issue that made it almost impossible to play even on the lowest quality so there might be issues with getting the game to work properly.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFull disclosure, I have not gotten far at all, but I've really enjoyed this game so far. It's so far a story that's more focused on coming of age and adventure, instead of some grand epic, but I feel that's a nice change of pace.

The combat is done in a menu-based real time manner, where time keeps passing while you decide what to do. Time slows down some once your turn comes up, but it does still keep moving. There are a few things to manage, but it's certainly doable.

The alchemy item creation system is pretty intuitive, though it took me a bit of practice to start figuring out good methods.

As for comparisons with other Atelier games, my experience with them is limited to a couple let's plays of some of the Arland games. In this case, you equip consumables to each character, and can use a pool of points to use them. It starts as a fairly small number, but I assume it increases as you go. I'm kind of on the fence about it, as it's nice to not have as much of a resource tax, and it keeps your inventory from being as cluttered, but it'd be nice to have less of a limit on your item usage.

As I said at the beginning, I'm overall enjoying this game so far, and am looking forward to where things go.\t\t\t\t\t
