+ Interesting characters (growing little by little)
+ character illustration (i think the illustrator changed but still superb)
+ great costumes (i mean the basic ones)
+ fitting OST and pretty good als awlways
+ for the thicc modern audience (more fanservice then you can imagine)
+ relaxing and chilled gameplay as always for atelier
+ indeepth alchemic system (but easy to understand and not as difficult as other installment of the series)
+ modern gameplay feeling with Unreal Engine here
+ quick and interesting fights with ATB instead of turn based
+ this ATB system is new to the atelier series and keep combat, fresh and interesting
+ many events to discover and reveal some sidestories
+ Melons and Tights, ripe and ready to harvest!
+ amazing sightseeing and dungeons as always
+ great fighting animations
+ the most advanced item customizing i know with alchemic power (items, gathering gear, weapons and equip)
+ small interactions and others events are soothing
+ greatly done (and that with a low budget)
+ amazing camera angles infight as well as fitting action and effects
+ yes, big and round they are....
+ combat is mix of strategy, quick and dirty battles
+ lovely designed areas and dungeons


+/- might be unusual for beginners of the series
+/- when you want to use the whole alchemy synthsize system you are in for tricking, grinding and experimentation (for me a positive point, but usually for majority of players maybe disturbing)
+/- I kinda miss the unique Sellers in this installment (ppl. like Pamela well yes, she appears...but not enough!! Hagel, well not the same but some unique characters )
+/- as always you have guides for alchemy or tweak a lot by yourself (i prefer the later one as always)

- runs ♥♥♥♥ without tweaks (fps drops, stuttering, screen tearing etc.) ->refer to the comment under this list
- some battle menu are reverted when the enemy goes into special attack preparation animation
- be careful with the screenshot function somehow it breaks your fps (alt tabbing after screenshot fixed it)
- some minor bugs (position bugs, strange character movement for example after finishing a quest)
- srsly too few rumble action for controller??????????????!!!!!!!!!!!!11111111elf

The new Entry is entertaining and it introduces some fresh things as well. First it has better graphics, better motions, a beginner friendly system without a timelimit (Atelier Games tend to have special time limits to main quest and sidequests), so you can
play at your leisure. The alchemic part is mostly not that difficult, well explained and some things you discover at your own. So that is up to the player how good and powerfull you want to be. The
characters are charming and the story is laid back and chilled as the other installment of the series (Some other great points are pointed out in the beginning in the list).
Thanks to the steadfast, helpful and dedicated community most of the technical problems can be solved, where as the devs always fail to deliver a stable game.
Thanks to this and other tips it runs steady at 4k with 60 fps at least (above 60 fps the game kinda breaks all with constantly stuttering).
So thank you dear community for fixing most of the problems. But else the game offers Atelier Adventure as it should be: Entertaining, Indeepth Crafting, Exploring etc.
A magical journey for those who know the atelier series. Also it is kinda Beginner friendly...

Well, the game has some flaws, but after deducting the minus points..we arrive at:
Masterpiece 120/100

13/10 The Melons on the field are ripe and the Tights are tight....
13/10 Ryza and Lila...hmmm yes,yes,yes,yes,yes....

The crafting/alchemy is the highlight. It's deep and strategic, and makes it fun to hunt down the perfect ingredients for good gear.

The battle system is cool in theory, but any strategy is trivialised by having well-crafted gear. Most battles are over within 30 seconds, which is good for item gathering, but feels like wasted potential.

Story is generic, but cute. I like that the main character (Ryza) is constantly getting nagged by her mother to do chores in between fighting dragons and whatnot.

Lastly, the endgame/post game was disappointing. Once you crack the alchemy system, you can craft some truly OP gear. But the super bosses weren't very super at that point. I would have liked a really high-level challenge.

But fun overall, and I'll continue through the trilogy.\t\t\t\t\t
