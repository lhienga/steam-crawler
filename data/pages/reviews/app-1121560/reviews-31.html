\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtelier Ryza: Ever Darkness & the Secret Hideout is a sweet and relaxing JRPG. I knew I'd love the game when I first laid my eyes on it. Let's explore the ups and downs of the game (+, +/-, -, ?):

+ The game look gorgeous. Everything looks nice, polished and colorful. It's very pleasing to stare at the sky, fields, trees, everything.
+ This is one of the best JRPG OSTs in a long time. It's soooo chill, yet inspiring. Could use a truly heavy metal banger track thou, but maybe that wouldn't really fit in.
+ Ryza herself is such a sweetheart. A suuuper adorable and likeable adventurer! Oh, and she looks hoooooot! I'm sure all reviews contain the word thicc . The rest of the cast is great too. I can so relate to Lent the tank, Klaudia has a great character arc, Empel looks like he should be a time bandit and Lila is my other wife. Hmm... oh, and Tao, the geek, also exists.
+ I like how side-quests are not just something random. It is fascinating how the main story and the side-quests build this awesome, and wholesome, experience.
+ The alchemy system is complicated, but addicting. It may seem overwhelming at first, but you'll grow to love it!
+ Collecting materials feels great. It's a lifesaver that the container at the main base is almost impossible to fill up.
+ It's like listening to Tiamat Gaia.
+ This game averted my expectations. Like, when I first laid my eyes on Empel and Lila , I was so sure they would be the main villains . But no. Even during the final moments of the game, I was waiting for some evil ♥♥♥♥ to go down. This doesn't happen too much anymore in any media and it was such a nice breath of fresh air.
+ With Lila on screen, it's impossible not to see the massive tits. But you know, they never become a plot point, no one makes jokes about them, they just exist. GREAT! Badasses are allowed to look cool, have tits (, hetero chromia) and beat ass!
+The story as a whole is very fulfilling. Politics, relationships and morals are discussed multiple times, but the game never gets preachy. It allows you to form your own opinion. The twists and turns are great! I enjoy seeing all this, and the whole world...
+/- ... but it has one problem: the Underworld . The place looks great, but the threat is not really there. It's mostly just left alone, with couple of enemies here and there when the cast needs to visit the place, but that's pretty much it. The Queen, the last boss, was way too easy to beat.
+/- Locations are nice, but they feel very small. I could use more of this beauty.
+/- The game starts of well balanced, but gets quite easy after the first major boss fight.
+/- The battle system is fast and cool, but confusing. I could barely see what status things were applied*
- There's one character who I really don't get why she's here: Kilo . Maybe, because she's gonna appear in the next game?
- Status ailments don't matter 99% of the time. During my journey, I barely saw or noticed them. It's way too easy to just keep beating ass by only following turn-orders, AP and HP.
? The lack of healing/buff spells is striking. I'm not sure how I feel about using mainly items to heal/buff everyone.
? This game brought great memories of playing Mana Khemia on PS2 back in 2009. Can we get that on PC too?

Atelier Ryza: ED & SH is a great JRPG everyone should experience. While not perfect, it's chill, relax and badass factors are through the roof. I can't wait to play the next one. Now, which one is better: Ryza or Lila ? 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI found the original description to be quite confusing, the best way to think of this is as a JRPG with crafting, but the crafting is fun. I'm not normally a fan of crafting games, I don't enjoy it and feel it takes away from enjoying a good game. However Ryza felt like an exception to this.

The story, exploration and crafting are well intertwined together that it makes it actually rewarding. You go out, do gathering, collect things, fight mobs, collect things, then you use those components to craft better gear, slowly and surely. The gear makes you more powerful, and you use it to go out again and revisit other areas, fight tougher mobs and so on, as you progress the story.


👍 The game introduces the mechanics slowly and surely, without doing a manual info dump within a few minutes.

👎 The character designs were hit and miss. Some are quite decent but some felt like a parody. Lila specifically felt like a parody drawing of a stick figure with balloons that accidentally landed on a designer's desk and they just went with it.

👍 Really nice music. One of the important parts of a JRPG is the music that accompanies you as you spend dozens of hours in its world. In this case you're spending dozens of hours crafting (synthesizing, the game calls it) and the game doesn't let you down here. It was so good I even created my own extended mix from some of my favorites.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5qyVk15Nh9U

👍 The story is short but still good. There's a bit of historical mystery going on which is the main theme. There's also some slice of life aspects to it. Because the areas that you visit aren't very large it feels very much like an adventure in your own 'home area' or 'backyard'.

👎 Mobs could have done with a bit of attention. There was an OK variety of mobs, but not a lot, they just seem to repeat across different zones.

👍 Beautiful environments The game also comes with a photo mode you can use to position characters, change the time of day, filters, and few other controls.

👍 That, combined with the gathering. As the game progresses you learn new gathering methods, which lets you revisit previous areas and gather new items in different ways. This actually makes revisiting interesting.

👍 Side quests, though optional, reward you if you pursue them. They show small tales of the stories of the islanders and really lends that 'homely' feel to the place.




Overall - I'd recommend this if you already like JRPGs, are unsure about crafting but what to try something new. It's casual friendly and quite relaxing.\t\t\t\t\t
