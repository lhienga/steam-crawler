\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a really great new entry into the Atelier series. What may make this a game for you:
-In depth item crafting/alchemy system, redesigned from previous entries into a more understandable but just as fun to get a hold on mechanic.
-Active time battle system that's pretty well thought out, giving you different actions (normal attacks, skills, item use) that flow well with a mechanic called "AP" which you gain by attacking, spend by using skills, and which, once you have enough, can be spent to allow you to chain even more manual attacks or make skills more powerful. AP can also be spent to interrupt turn order with a quick action.
-Colorful graphics and a pretty good art direction that gives this game some awfully pretty vistas, with a good use of bloom and brightness to get the summertime vibe through to you

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI dont get the negative reviews, so i am leaving my positive one here.

The game is very good, i got addicted and immersed quickly. I do not regret buying the deluxe edition one bit as the game holds up against other JRPGs very well.
Good story, good graphics, good gameplay mechanics, easthetic character.

Now the game is not without issues. You might want to play with a controller as kb/mouse support is not that good. The FPS was uncapped and my game was running at 300fps. Physics is disabled or doesnt work properly at higher FPS. You might want to limit your FPS manually to 60 in your driver software since the game doesnt have graphics options.

Overal i recommend this game a lot if you like JRPGs althought a few fixes would be appreciated. So far there has been an update with a mouse patch so devs so far seem promising to fix some of the issues on the PC port.\t\t\t\t\t

Edit November 12th, 2019:

I completed the game fully in just under 50 hours. I think the game was rushed during development, with graphical improvements and the change in development teams taking time away from a lot of content that is expected from an atelier release. It is definitely a great JRPG and I would even suggest it to non-atelier fans, but I think it's one of the more mediocre releases since the Mana Khemia duology or original Rorona (before they redid it). I will outline some design changes compared to previous releases, and what I perceive as shortcomings from rushed development.

Positive:
- The graphics are gorgeous! I paused constantly during my first visit to town to inspect the sunlight, the water, the tops of houses, and the ambient noise in each area. A strict improvement over any previous release and the biggest positive takeaway from this new series so far.
- New alchemy system, a more radical change than the usual adjustments between each release. It is pretty neat how you reforge equipment up to your alchemy level and unlock effects and recipes
- "Real-time" combat: this could be taken as a positive or a negative, but personally I am encouraged Gust is trying new things, and while the system didn't really prove itself or get fleshed out in any major boss encounters for me, I am really looking forward to its potential in future releases.
- Another artist this time around, as is custom for these trilogies. I had extreme reservations about their background and their designs for the playable female characters, but honestly I changed to Ryza's "city" costume that looks like Kaudia's, and her bulk wasn't too distracting.
- Rather than keep the core sound team and swap out one member every 2 or three games (Shimoda, Asano, Yano), they brought lots of fresh blood. The three new composers express themselves competently within the sonic landscape of Atelier, and thankfully Yanagawa stuck around for a lot of the more mundane tracks so the change isn't too jarring.
- All things considered, I think this is a worst-case showing for what Gust has in store for the franchise's future, and frankly it turned out excellent. I cannot wait to see what all these changes look like in a polished followup release.

Negative:
- Almost no dialogue is voiced outside of directly main-story-relevant. I think all the performances were great, but it feels very soulless going around town for sidequests with Ryza, Lent, and all the townsfolk mute. This is compounded by the fact the game is pretty short, so a lot of immersion is lost in the later game.
- The game is very short compared to recent atelier games. I would have liked to have more side-character stories after the climax, but I guess I can't fault them for not overachieving.
- Though the game only features 4 tracks from Asano (who composed very actively for Atelier Shallie and Atelier Sophie), they are weak performances compared to what I expect from him. My favourite out of the batch is the first battle theme, the others are more plain versions of tracks I've already heard half a dozen times from him.
- The series was long overdue for a significantly new feel for the music, but this time around the new recruits were too sparing on percussion throughout the soundtrack. The music doesn't maintain the energy from previous releases, but I am also pleased with many of the changes.
- Not a big fan of Ryza's design; seems like it was built off the template of Sophie with some unsavoury additions from the new illustrator. However, it wasn't too distracting, and some of the other characters designs were interesting.
- I was severely disappointed on completing the game to see there are not composer comments on the music tracks in Extras! This has been a staple of Gust releases for a long time and I love getting insight into what they meant to convey with each track (why was fretless bass used, did the track turn out very differently from what the composer expected, etc). Given the massive change in sound team, I would have liked some introduction from the new team and Yanagawa's thoughts on their contributions. I checked that it's missing in the Japanese console release, so it is not just the Steam port. A pity.

In summary, this release is streamlined for the general RPG crowd and is far less alienating while maintaining the unique atelier flavour that keeps weary RPG buffs like myself excited for each release. The game feels very rushed, but the product is splendid. Definitely recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWas a very enjoyable play-through and my first experience with the Atelier series.

The story itself gets a little weak at times but the overall game-play makes up for it in my opinion.
There is a pretty obvious cycle of collecting to improve your gear so you can collect even more, but it never felt like a chore or anything as there is so many ways to collect it's very easy to stumble onto something new in an old area helping keep things fresh.

The biggest downside would have to be the lack of graphic options but the game ran and looked great in my case so I can't really comment on that problem.

Had fun with the game and now look forward to future titles!\t\t\t\t\t

Quests are more interesting. Gathering is more involved. The synthesis system is challenging, but by far better than those puzzle-piece synthesis modes of past games.

CONS: Not many, really. I do wish they would make the costume changes a little more diverse. They are basically the same outfit, but with different colors. If we are paying for extras, we should get more than simply color changes . That's rather lazy on the developer's part, in my opinion. The tutorials seem like they might be a bit complicated for newbies due to the massive content - could be overwhelming. Of course, anyone can watch/read guides. Not all soundbites are subtitled, which isn't awful - just slightly annoying.
