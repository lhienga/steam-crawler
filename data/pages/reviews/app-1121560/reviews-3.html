\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWas hesitant to give this game a try because I was not a fan of Atelier Rorona many years ago, but this easily became my new favorite game! It's a nice cozy experience, and it's one of those games where outside of the alchemy, nothing really stands out, but there's also never a dull moment.

In terms of story, it was alright. Very short, typical anime plot, but it wrapped everything up nicely (except Lent's story. Disappointed that his dad didn't get any karma).

Sidequests are all just basic fetch quests (gather x items, craft items w/ x quality, etc), but every single one is part of a storyline, and in some cases connect with other sidequest stories, making them actually enjoyable to complete.

Combat was... interesting. It's in this weird position where it seems like a very fun system with a ton of potential, especially once you learn how to stunlock bosses, but A) there's so little combat, and B) it feels wasted on a game where its deep alchemy system makes balancing impossible since how difficult fights are depends entirely on how good you are at alchemy. Going into the final boss with the best gear with the bare minimum stats means you die in one hit even on Normal Mode, but you can craft some ridiculous gear before even the 2nd boss once you understand how the alchemy mechanics work.

Overall would recommend to anyone who just wants a relaxing game where you can just shut your brain off and enjoy the ride.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: This is a review of someone who's first Atelier game is Ryza.

This is genuinely a wonderful game. Very cozy atmosphere, funny, and engaging. The game is complex enough to be fun, but the synthesis systems and crafting are not too overwhelming to be lost in. In fact most systems in this game I learned just by playing and experimenting.

And I think that's what this game excels at. It doesn't waste your time. If you are struggling against a boss, you can throw yourself at the boss 100 times with different combinations while you brainstorm what may work and what doesn't. Try different traits, different item combinations, different team compositions. It's all easy to do. Every character also levels up at the same time so if you want to do a quick swap with a character you've never used, you can do that too!

The endgame bosses (dark elemental and true final boss) were both fun, and difficult yet completely doable using the crafting systems and methods to just create such overpowered items (Genesis Hammer is great btw)

Also the game is great to 100%, and feels satisfying while you're doing it.

Try this game out if you like crafting, progression, or just a cozy story to get lost in!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Is this a good game to start with the Atelier series?"

I have no idea. What I can tell you though is how good this game is from the perspective of someone who has never played any Atelier games before, went into this almost blind, and only sometimes dabbles in JRPGs.

Pros:
-Are you someone who's a fan of casual settings? This is basically perfect for you then. There are "high stakes" the characters go through, but the feeling is much lighter compared to other games that exist.
-The crafting system is very complex, so if you enjoy trying to craft the best item ever, or finding loopholes to get the best gear, you'll love how this game satisfies that desire for you.
-The fights are kinda unique, with its combination of turn-based/real-time combat. Some of the animations are also really satisfying.
-The game is pretty. Sometimes just stopping and looking at the map was very pleasant. The fact that the game also features a camera mode is nice.
-The characters are really neat. I liked Ryza, Lent was cool, seeing Tao grow was satisfying, etc. Even the side characters, AND the side-side characters were nice, like I grew to like them. The only exception to this are Lent and Lent’s dad, which I thought was the darkest relationship in the story (in comparison to other conflicts in the realm of this game), only to end up having imo the dumbest ending of them all.
-The cutscenes are all rendered in-game, and it’s kinda charming how well they managed to tell the narratives with the limited poses and options they had.

Cons:
-The way this game makes the player move from place to place for the story can be really annoying and stupid lol. (Ex: Go to your hideout for a cutscene, then head to the island's port for a different cutscene, go back to the hideout again, head back to another place on the island, go back to the ideout and wait 3 days - Like this is just dumb). Also personally speaking, the story sometimes felt kind of boring too.
-The crafting system, while interesting and complex, can honestly feel overwhelming and stressful at times. I really had to wrap my head what materials I was going to use for which recipe. And when you must make weapons, armor, 2 accessories, and items, + additional supplements for the list I just mentioned: That's just becomes too much at one point.
-The combat system can sometimes feel stupid. The characters can request you to do something that you will have no way of fulfilling. This ended up forcing me to play only one character towards the middle part of the game who could actually satisfy those orders (Lila). Sometimes, you can also fulfill the orders given by your party members, only for you to accidentally switch and then just lose out on the bonuses for no reason. Speaking of characters…
-The party size being a maximum of three members only is honestly such a shame. I played through the entire game never having played or tried out Empel, besides for the achievements, because it was too much of a commitment to bring him up to speed with the rest of the party + I never saw a reason or opportunity to do so. Which sucks. Same for the rest of the members being benched for eternity.
-Optimization problems. I had to change some settings so the game would actually feel smooth, and you are also forced to basically play with a controller, as the game will freeze up every once in a while otherwise. Like why is this a thing.
-Not enough search options. Where do I get this material from again? Idk somewhere around that place. Where is [item name] again? Idk but enjoy scrolling through all the names in non-alphabetical order.

Tl;dr: a nice game you can sink a lot of time into (in my case, I spend WAY more than I should have with this title), with lots of charming features, but also its share of problems.

Do you have a lot of time to kill? Do you like slice-of-life, wholesome anime, but want to add some action into it? Do you like grinding for OP gear? Then you'll love your time with this. Otherwise, maybe look for something else to play and buy.

Overall, a 6.5/10 from me.\t\t\t\t\t

Atelier Ryza: Ever Darkness & the Secret Hideout, the latest installment in the Atelier series developed by Gust, invites players into a charming world of alchemy, friendship, and exploration. With its vibrant visuals, engaging characters, and unique synthesis system, Atelier Ryza delivers a delightful JRPG experience that caters to both longtime fans and newcomers.

The first thing that strikes players upon entering the game is its enchanting art style. Atelier Ryza boasts a colorful and picturesque world that is a joy to explore. The character designs are expressive and distinctive, bringing the charming cast to life. The attention to detail in the environments, from lush landscapes to cozy villages, enhances the overall immersion and complements the whimsical tone of the game.

The heart of Atelier Ryza lies in its alchemical system, a central gameplay mechanic that sets it apart. Synthesizing items through alchemy is not only essential for progressing the story but is also an enjoyable and intuitive process. The game successfully strikes a balance between accessibility and depth, allowing players to experiment with various ingredients to create potions, equipment, and other useful items.

The narrative follows the journey of Ryza and her friends as they embark on an adventure to uncover the secrets of alchemy and explore the mysteries of their world. While the overall plot may not be groundbreaking, the character-driven focus and the genuine friendships that develop over the course of the game contribute to a heartwarming and engaging story.

Character development is another strong suit of Atelier Ryza. The main characters are well-written and undergo meaningful growth throughout the game. Ryza, in particular, stands out as a relatable protagonist with her aspirations, doubts, and determination. The interactions between the party members add depth to the narrative, and players are likely to form strong emotional connections with the cast.

The game's soundtrack complements the whimsical atmosphere, featuring melodic tunes that enhance the sense of adventure and discovery. While not necessarily groundbreaking, the music succeeds in creating a pleasant backdrop to the alchemical journey.

While Atelier Ryza excels in many aspects, some players may find the combat system somewhat simplistic. Battles are turn-based, and while they serve their purpose, they might lack the strategic depth found in other JRPGs. However, this simplicity may appeal to those who prefer a more accessible combat experience.

In conclusion, Atelier Ryza: Ever Darkness & the Secret Hideout is a delightful addition to the Atelier series. With its enchanting visuals, engaging characters, and a robust alchemical system, it successfully captures the essence of a whimsical JRPG adventure.
