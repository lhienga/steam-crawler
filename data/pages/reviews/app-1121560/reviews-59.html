\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtelier series was something I had not played earlier and this was different from Final Fantasy that I have played more. Game has nice amount of story, quests and small events and exploration, seeing new areas, monsters and materials from various sources. Game was not too hard on normal difficulty and as usual I did some grinding for experience and materials and getting better gear (crafting gear using alchemy) which also helped. This meant I was able to concentrate more on story than struggling to get forward. Downside was that only bosses did then provide some challenge. I did also all side quests I could find and those did give some more depth to the game world. In the end I liked playing the game and only issue was a few times losing one controller button in game menus while doing alchemy and or gearing characters which required game restart as right trigger was nice to have button on battles. Cartoon style graphics, good music and working game play. Get this game from a sale and it's worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

The characters were likable and everyone developed pretty well throughout the story.
The combat was kind of weird in the start but after understanding the usage of items (converting, etc ..) I got the idea.
The core feature of being an alchemist was a blast though. Searching for the right items and crafting the gear you need to either progress or just overwhelm enemies was fun and it didn't feel like a waste of time at all. Progressing further is a big part of the game and it was done right.
(This was my first Atelier, so I can't relate to the others)

I had absolutely no technical issues, it never crashed or anything similar.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR version
THICC alchemist saves the island.

Long version

The good :
- Battle is more action oriented and way different than your average turn based Atelier games. Don't expect monsters/enemies will wait for you to select your items bcs they will beat you up while you busy selecting your items. Battle screen information could be overwhelming at first, but once you get used to it, it becomes simple and enjoyable. Set your difficulty to hard so the enemies will not die quickly and you'll have a chance to see those awesome anime-like-special-move that's animated beautifully <3
- Synthesizing are amusing, i spend way too much time here trying to figure out how to get those quality up to 999 with the correct traits. You'll want to find more new materials than ever and hoard them.
- No time limit this time, ill list this as a good thing, no more restrictive way to play or goofing around with your party. even though this will take away some suspense the game potentially had.
- Maps are diverse and colorful, with full fast travelling system that will saves your time.
- THICC


The bad
- Story are interesting and have potential to be superb, but executed moderately. No real suspense (also when you die there won't be a game over screen, you just automatically return to your atelier but lost some things you collected at your current journey)
- No more character event like previous Atelier game. TBH some characters at Ryza have an interesting story and character development, but many of those things are shown off screen so you just have to connect the dots yourself. I still care about the character, but they will be feel more special if there was a dedicated character event to show how much your party and yourself grown.
- Need more variety of monsters.

TBH i enjoyed this game. i definitely recommend it if you are into thic---i mean atelier games. AFAIK, nothing is missable. Play at your own pace and enjoy the game. Have fun synthesizing and adventuring!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was REALLY hesitant to pick up this title. I have always gave a second glance at the atelier series but have always been turned off by the light hearted plot, item crafting and lack of english dub. I am glad to give this one a try as it has exceeded my expectations. The story was slow at the start but the character development was top notch. I found the crafting system addicting to dive into and was not only fun but relatively easy to understand. I found myself gathering everything i could to experiment. The middle of the game, the plot picks up a good bit and things get more interesting. My caveats with it are thus. They hold your hand way too much. I found myself unable to craft things due to being gated by main story progression which limited the fun of spending extra time on the crafting system. I also wish this had an english dub as i feel this would draw more western audiences but that is a small complaint as i dont mind ♥♥♥ voices.\t\t\t\t\t
