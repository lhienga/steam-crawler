\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRyza was an interesting experiment. It moved Atelier from slice of life plots to a more standard jrpg fare without pissing off original fans and coming up with decent new ideas. While you can see the rough edges in the main story progression (it relies a bit too much on "Read diary to see where to go next") and the experimental combat system, the fact that Atelier is still reinventing itself and being this good after 25+ years of YEARLY games is insane. Ryza 2 is even better in every way and Gust improved like crazy in these last few years, but Ryza remains the imperfect first of 'new-atelier' and it's pretty good. It may be a decent gateway atelier if you play Ryza's game first, but remeber each trilogy is different and while old time fans can see an interesting progression of game design you might feel some disconect if you start here and jump straight to say, Rorona. If you are curious about pure Atelier itself, might wanna start with Arland or Dusk, but if you want to dip your toes coming from more traditional JRPGS, start here, no doubt.\t\t\t\t\t
