\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished this game. Been a fan of the Atelier series since Apprentice of Arland, and boy has the series' direction shifted. To make it easier, here is a Pro v Con

Pro:
-On a 'good' spec PC, the fps is fine. I'm not having any frame issues. If I expected I would, I would had bought this game on the Switch. Though the port is a little saddening.
-MC and the other 5 playable characters have nice design, with great VA choice. The MC is by far the best in a while for Atelier series, one that makes the game attractive to even new players.
-Map is beautiful. Though there are only a few, the attention to details for each area is striking.
-Alchemy system got revamped into a more simpler version. By taking out the time limit, an essential characteristic of earlier Atelier games, gathering materials became more simpler. No punishment for just taking your time to gather materials of better quality. Though this could also be seen as a con.
-Alchemy creation, duplication, gathering worlds, improving created items, etc. makes the Alchemy system simpler and fresh. Easy to learn.
-Combat system is now strikingly similar to FF. Easy to see which character goes next, instead of the old ugly box frames. However, it could get repetitive.
-Fast travel is in this game!!! (T_T I didn't discover it till I was literally one map away from final boss. This is why you shouldn't skip tutorials people T_T)
-You can design and change how your atelier look, both inside decorations and exterior design.

Con:
-Lack of settings. Literally this game was just ported onto the PC without any major form of optimizing. The controls are bad, play it on a Xbox of PS4 controller instead. After all, it was designed that way initially.
-Major issue I had is this game lacking (I head they took it out) any character quests or storyline. There is only one ending, and the characters' don't have any quests specific to their growth. All the side missions is literally cannon fodder gathering and delivering quests.
-Only character development is through main story.
-Very hard and Very Very Hard difficulty are locked to New Game Plus.
-Lack of replayability. The only new game + content is 2 new bosses.

Other info:
-No romance, which I'm kind of glad. It's all about friendship here people!

Would I recommend this game?
-Well yeah. Though I list cons equal to my pro, I enjoyed the game. I basically played it nonstop just to finish it. The storyline is predictable but comforting. I found it enjoyable. Maybe after a couple months I'll come back to do some more alchemy. As for the price of the game, while I don't consider it a factor. Bottom line is, if you enjoy previous Atelier titles, or like a Japanese style Visual Novel RPG game, then play this game. If you find the price to be unreasonable, than just wait for an Atelier sale.
-Either way I hope this is helpful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBig atelier fan (played arland+mysterious on steam)

Don't know why this game has so many mixed reviews!

Pros

-Best graphics by far

-Battle system is new and fresh. Sure you can just control one player and that might be kind of boring; but trying to perfectly play all 3 characters at once is quite challenging and fun!

-Crafting system is fun, no more annoying "idea" discovery

-Gear progression system is cool, since you have to build your way up and cant skip to last tier instantly

Cons
-Optimization? I see alot of complaints; I run an i9 and a 1080ti so I can't feel it; but if its there I feel for you

-Lack of good keyboard controls? I suppose, but Atelier isn't really mean tot be played on keyboard; it's like trying to play Starcraft 2 on a controller; its just meant to be used on something else

-Filler quests - Kind of tedious, but every atelier has had these but they do seem even more fillery than other games

-Party size - Not a fan of the 3 party size. Understandably micromanaging 3 people with ATB is challenging enough; they could have made a "support" row like in lydie&suelle
