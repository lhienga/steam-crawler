\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Resident Evil Chapter Rocks. You get a really cool new character, Nemesis, and 2 new characters, Leon and Jill. You also get a map I guess.

Nemesis is an amazing killer. He manages to be really fun to play, and really fun to go against at the same time. His main ability is a long ranged tentacle strike that gets stronger the more you use it, with the trade off being that it takes a 3rd additional damage state before knocking down a survivor. Along with that, he has the very unique passive ability to spawn zombies around the map that can attack survivors independently. While they often just kind of hang around and don't do much, they can help you track survivors, and when they do deal damage, its a huge help. His perks are much less impressive, but don't do much to take away from a still amazing killer. He can see where all the survivors spawn at the start of the match with Lethal Persuer, which can be nice, but it usually won't take long to find someone anyways, so I usually won't get anything out of it. Hysteria makes all injured survivors oblivious when another survivor is injured, which could be nice, but the cooldown, and the general ease of healing, it doesn't do much. Eruption is his only good perk, but it takes a decent amount of setup to get to work. Every time you kick a generator, it gets highlighted, and when you knock down a survivor, every highlighted generator explodes and incapacitates and survivor working on it. If you have high mobility this is a really good perk to have, but if you lack map control it can easily just waste a perk slot.

Leon is the first new survivor in the pack, and is pretty ok. He can heal himself without making any noise, and see all regressing generators on the map after completing enough skill checks. These are both OK, but his only really cool perk is his flashbangs. If you spend enough time on a generator, you can hop in a locker and create a flashbang, that you can throw to stun the killer (or you and your fellow survivors if you aren't careful) which can be amazing for getting an escape or saving a survivor being carried to a hook. They take awhile to get used to, but if you get good at using them, they're a very powerful part of any arsenal.

Jill is the other survivor in the pack, and her perks aren't bad either. She can cleanse totems faster, and track new ones after cleansing one. She also gets a sizable chunk of the healing bar filled after being unhooked. Her best perk, similar to Leon, is another stun perk, Blast Mine. After spending enough time on a generator, you can install a trap on the generator, highlighting it for all survivors, and stunning the killer for a brief time when they attempt to break it. While not as handy as the flashbangs are, its still a nice want to deter killers from damaging generators, and is a lot less difficult to pull of than flashbangs.

The map included in this pack is really cool in theory, its a complete recreation of the Raccoon Police Department from Resident Evil 3, which is really cool, but in practice, this map is way too big, really hard to navigate at times, and has contributed to some of the worst games I've played, killer and survivor alike. Its become a joke to bring badges among my friends just to piss off the group.

Overall though, this is one of the coolest packs out there, and is a good buy for anyone who likes DBD, whether or not you're the biggest ResE fan out there, or you've never even heard what a T-Virus is.\t\t\t\t\t
