Things I found exceptionally fun when playing as Nemesis:

A giant fist to the face, walking sounds (like a tank on two legs), a Mori to die for (literally),
the perks, hitting survivors and destroying pallets with your ability, the occasional gigachad zombie coming from the bushes to block a loop, and finally Coup de grâce for tactical fist missile.
There is probably a few things i missed, but that's what stuck to me the most.



Things i found not so fun when playing as Nemesis: (oh boy)

The ability hitbox is really inconsistent and buggy, windows and low loops sometimes give you the hit you supposedly landed and sometimes not. The power is a joke in Tier 1 and Tier 2. When hitting (and infecting) an uninfected survivor, they get the mini sprint burst effect without taking damage, this makes chases even longer and doesn't reward you at all (except giving you progress to the next tier). Tier 3 is where the power gets decent, range is ok and the cooldown is a bit shorter, but at that time, you spent all that time jerking off the survivors with your tentacle so there is only 2 gens left. He feels and looks like an intimidating big strong killer, but in truth, you just need to run forward and get the occasional tentacle tickle from your friendly uncle Nemmy to win the game as a survivor.


I feel like they mixed the dough, warmed up the oven, shaped the bread, carefully put it in the oven, and just took it out early and said it's good to serve. The product is unfinished and much like half baked bread, you can consume it and somewhat enjoy it, it just isn't as good as it can be. Can't recommend giving money for the half finished product.

Also if all that sounded cool and fun, go buy it and regret it later, consumers unite!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite the multitude of glitches and bugs on launch, you can tell this chapter was made with nothing but love towards the Resident Evil franchise, even if you've never touched it before. The survivors and Killer have engaging perks and a fun power, while the map is detailed and massive, although perhaps a little too much.

LEON KENNEDY, NEW SURVIVOR -- His three perks are cool, and definitely encourage completing objectives. Bite The Bullet is useful for evading killers while healing, and Rookie Spirit has it's uses. The best perk from his personal set, without a doubt, is Flashbang. The ability to create a blind that the killer won't see coming until it's too late, and one that you get passively from simply doing your job as a survivor is a very good bonus as a survivor. Not to mention the blind on flashbangs is fairly long. It's a shame the Chris Redfield skin doesn't let you punch the rocks on the map...

JILL VALENTINE, NEW SURVIVOR -- I honestly think Jill's perks are the better of the two survivors. Blast Mine is a strong deterrent (although keep in mind it will be rendered completely useless by Hex: Ruin), Resurgence can help you recover from unhooks much quicker, and Counterforce can help you destroy some of the strongest perks a killer may have with ease. In my opinion, if you're a survivor main invest in Jill first; the Claire Redfield skin is also very cool. Two girlbosses for the price of one! (Well not really you have to pay for Claire but. You get what I mean)

THE NEMESIS, NEW KILLER -- The Nemesis has a fairly easy to use, easy to understand power. Hitting a survivor once with your tentacle will contaminate them, and hitting a contaminated survivor will injure them. On paper, taking three hits to down a survivor might not be the best, however consider that the tentacle has good range, can hit over pallets, and can even go over loops with lower-down obstacles. The more you hit with your tentacle, the stronger it becomes, being able to break pallets and doors and gaining range. My only issue with this power so far is that it feels rather... inconsistent at times. Sometimes it will hit when it doesn't look like it should, sometimes it won't hit when it clearly should have. Some times it'll go over obstacles without issue, sometimes it won't. With a little fine-tuning, the power will definitely be great at making certain loops very unsafe for survivors, especially when they otherwise would've been a nuisance.

You also have two zombies, who will stagger around the map, locking onto survivors who get close enough; If a survivor isn't paying attention, they can get hit by the zombies, which have the same principle as the tentacle strike (First hit contaminates, hitting a contaminated can injure). The zombies, unfortunately, are either going to be completely useless or will come in clutch. Hitting them with your tentacle will help strengthen your power.

His personal perks are alright in my opinion. Lethal Pursuer is my favourite, hands down. Having such important information as soon as the trial begins is incredibly important, and can help you potentially start a chase before the first gen is even touched. Not to mention, it'll be very handy on killers who require early-game setup to do good, such as Ghostface or Myers. Hysteria might be helpful in a smaller map where it's easier to get onto another survivor, and Eruption can create good gen pressure. Overall, a fairly solid set of perks that can be combined with other existing perks to create some unique playstyles for Nemesis. I just wish he got a Mr. X skin...

RACCOON CITY POLICE DEPARTMENT, NEW MAP-- as of writing this review, the map is disabled due to bugs and I haven't been able to experience it in the live servers, so things may have been changed about it; However, I experienced it on the PTB and it's a very large map, filled with long corridors and cramped rooms. It's as easy for the killer to get lost as it is to secure hits on survivors stuck in narrow corridors with no room to attempt a sly spin. My only complaint is that it might be TOO big; If you plan on bringing an offering to visit RPD, aura-reading perks may be heavily recommended.

OVERALL -- I'd recommend this Chapter, as you get quite good content for the price. It's filled with great references to the RE Franchise, and gives you three new characters who's perks and powers are great for those just getting into the game.\t\t\t\t\t
