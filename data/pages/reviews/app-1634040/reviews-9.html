\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti bought this DLC for christmas when it was 30% off after a few months of wanting it. mainly because i wanted to play the nemesis because his whip attacks looked really fun in videos and i wanted to do them too, and because i like playing the shape a lot because he is weaker than any other killer in Tier 1 and i like the idea of starting at a disadvantage until getting out of Tier 1. and the nemesis has that Tier up mechanic too!

but also because i wanted to use flashbangs as survivor because they work the same as firecrackers and i wanted to learn how to use those without wasting them and leons flashbang perk is great for that.

--

so the Nemesis is really cool and really fun. he has a tentacle that comes out from his arm and that he can use like a whip and it is really fun to use it, so sometimes i use it even though i could use his fists because of how fun it is to use the tentacle strike. but when you first start using it it feels way more shorter than it looks from videos so i had to practice first. it gets only a little longer when you reach Tier 3 in a match but it feels like a lot!

he also has zombies helping him a lot! because he can see their auras and when they extend their arms and walk somewhere you know that there is a survivor there even if you don't use tracking perks. and they attack survivors too! and that is really helpful sometimes and if you are lucky can even win you games! so they are very fun to have around and play with.

and i also like how the nemesis has a stomp sound when he walks. it makes him feel really solid and heavy and he's really big! and i think he's the best at breaking pallets and walls because he can keep moving after breaking them with his tentacle attack! he moves slowly but unlike the killers who have chainsaws to quickly break stuff he doesn't completely stop, which is huge!

--

so leon S kennedy has this perk called flashbang and they work the same as firecrackers so you can use this perk to practice them without losing the crackers :<

the flashbangs are really fun too but because he throws them on to the ground it kinda tricks my brain into pressing the wrong button. instead of the use button i press the drop item button instead and so i accidentally when i wanna throw i stop and slowly put it down in front of the killer instead and get hit. but that's just me being easily confused and might not happen to you! but anyhow when i can use them correctly they are really fun to use to blind killers while on the run and sometimes escape and i even managed to save a friend and another survivor from the killer once after they picked them up!

--

i haven't played jill valentine yet but when i do i might add that part in and i wanna try her too but later.

--

if you want a Tip against nemesis, try to not get hit by his tentacle early on if you are not already infected because it gives him 3 times as many points towards his tier up if he hits uninfected survivors. so sometimes it may be good to let him hit you with his fists to make it harder for him to break pallets. or purpusefully get hit by a zombie while in chase and not infected to get a speed boost and deny him infection points! so that he stays weaker for longer.

--

thank you for reading and have a nice day! and if you buy this i hope you enjoy it as much as i do. <3\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere to start? I love Resident Evil, I love Dead By Daylight, and now they are one. With each character boasting an impressive perk list, nostalgic cosmetic options, breathtakingly faithful model design, and accompanied by an amazing(albeit absolutely DUMMY imbalanced) RPD map. So much was done right. The inclusion of zombies, the animation, the "STARS!!" voice line for Nemesis. However, this does not come without a bit of baggage. The RPD map is easy for me to navigate, since I played RE2 so much, but any non-fan of RE will have a very difficult time with it. Certain killers are unplayable on that map. Ghost Face on RPD vs 4 toolboxes with brand new parts is borderline unfun.

But that is the only con I can think of. Leon brings a new item in the form of a flash bang, can track regressing generators, and hide when healing himself or others. Jill can set flash mines on gens, more easily hunt down totems, and recieves up to 40% free healing progress from being unhooked. Nemesis can attack from midrange, inflict an infection status affect, break pallets over range, and spawn two zombie AI that can lead to occasional team combos like you're playing Marvel vs Capcom 3. His perks let you find survivors easily at the start of a trial, set a trap on multiple generators, and inflict oblivious on pressured opponents. He's like a faster pyramid head without the free mori and trails of torment, featuring a long distance whip that's easier to aim, but unable to strike through solid walls(though it can go over them).

I wholly recommend this to any RE fan, and anyone who may just want the perks. These characters are just well made. Not perfect by any means, but definitely not bad in any way.\t\t\t\t\t
