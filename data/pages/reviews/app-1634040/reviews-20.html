\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis content was in a test branch for weeks and they still ended up disabling the new map on release because of issues. The new map is an entire chunk of the content, and they had to disable it, on release, after it being in public testing for weeks, From what I've heard it's just another obnoxiously labyrinthine indoor map where stealth murderers can instantly run around a corner and get free hits on any player not always running spine chill.

The new murderer has three core elements; their zombies, their tentacles and the vaccines. The zombies wander around the map either getting stuck on basic objects or rendering objectives and chases temporarily impossible via randomly inserting themselves as unavoidable obstacles. The new murderer's tentacle attack is just yet another slightly different variation of a ranged attack. Except, it requires little sacrifice to charge and activate; and launches faster than survivors can move. So survivors can't actually deliberately avoid it, they can only run erratically and hope their opponent misses. Everytime survivors get into a bottleneck, like where every single pallet is located, it becomes impossible for the murderer to miss while aiming properly. At any point if the survivor is running erratically and thus losing distance, or runs past a pallet so they don't spend too long in the bottleneck, the murderer can just retract the tentacle and get a melee hit with virtually no downside.

So this is yet another murderer who you can't deliberately counter, you just have to hope they're inexperienced enough to fumble their ability. Or, their mandatory damage abilities allow them to knock down survivors rapidly with ease and you just have to hope the team can rush generators faster than the murderer can chunk off health states.

Survivors can also use a finite vaccine to essentially safeguard themselves against one tentacle attack. Which just tells you that on some level the developers knew it was unfair and so instead of balancing the attack to have a coherent counterplay, they just give survivors the ability to make themselves immune to it. A total of four times per trial, shared by all survivors. When survivors open the giant vaccine box, it's empty apart from a single syringe neatly placed in the middle of the box, and when survivors use the syringe; the regular bandage healing animation players with the regular bandage sounds. Terrible visual presentation and absolutely lazy.

A third of the new survivor perks are based on light, so a third of the new survivor perks and arguably the most interesting ones are hard countered by an existing murderer perk. As flashbangs and blast mines become more prominent, murderers will run light immunity to counter it, making flashbangs and blast mines less common, making immunity less common, making flashbangs and blast mines more common, making immunity more common. So on and so forth, such awful balancing.

The new survivor cosmetics are of course yet again available from launch being sold each individually at around the price of the entire content pack and are bound as sets so you can't customize them. The murderer doesn't even get any at all.


Like, I play this game when I have nothing else to play because it's not always completely awful. But the developers are still greedy, lazy and uninspired all the same.\t\t\t\t\t
