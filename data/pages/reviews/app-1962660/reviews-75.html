\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I have been playing Call of Duty since 2007. I'm by no means a highly competitive player. I've played the majority of the titles since then. Currently, I play Modern Warfare 2 and Black Ops Cold War.

Now, with Modern Warfare 2, there's a lot of bad, but there's also lot of good. By weighing the good and bad of this game, I personally really liked this game and recommend buying it at a discount. Disclaimer, this is for the pure base version of the game. I did not spend a dime on the battle pass or any of the microtransactions.

Let's start with the good :

Gunplay : This game has some of the smoothest and slickest gun play of any video game I have ever played. The reloading animation is spot on, the hit markers feel solid, the movement is excellent and they all sound great. There is also so many guns that you'll have lots of variety here.

Gunsmith : I feel the gunsmith is very good. You can customize all of the base weapons in so many different ways and combinations even allowing you to fine tune each attachment to suit your playstyle. Definitely a highpoint of the game.

Campaign : The campaign was a mixed bag but I will put it as a good. There are a couple of standout missions and a couple very good characters. Overall, it's traditional call of duty fan fare with big set pieces but nothing too memorable. It's not bad, just nothing stands out. It's a fun play through once.

Modes : There's a lot of modes in this game. There is something for everyone. Excluding Warzone and DMZ since you don't need the game to play those, there's team deathmatch style of games, prisoner rescue (my favorite), control, domination, and a whole variety of other modes to play. Multiplayer is definitely a top notch offering when it comes to the variety of modes. Another great mode is Invasion which is basically the Call of Duty version of attrition from Titanfall 2. I liked it, but some people may not.

Prestige : Prestige is back in a different way. Black Ops Cold War brought it back but your rank resets every season. In MW2, it NO LONGER resets every season. You keep your rank and can keep going up to 1000+. But it still isn't perfect like the older call of duty titles but it's better than recent ones we've got. The emblems seem to be animated and recycled but it's nice to see they listened and brought back a version of the older system so players can grind out their ranks as much as they want.

Now, for the bad :

Microtransactions : Yes you do not need them to do very good in this game. But there's so many tracers, camo packs, characters and outfits, and special protuned weapons in their shop that it's honestly quite infuriating and brings the game down significantly in my eyes. It's not predatory per se, but it makes the game a terrible cash grab that ruins the overall feel of the game. You can ignore it play really well with the base weapons since the best weapons and best camo (TAQ 56/TAQ V along with the platinum and polyatomic camos) are the best camos in the game. Another one of my complaints is that you get very little character customization unless you either buy a bundle or pay for the battle pass. Missing the days where everything could be earned in game by playing.

Menus : The menus are atrocious. It's hard to find what you are looking for and the layout is made for a tablet/phone type of game and not for a call of duty game for PC/Console. Shame they moved away from the good menus of Black Ops, Black Ops 2, and MW2 (2009) with the simplistic designs and global player numbers. I wish to see a return of that. These menus make it hard to party up with friends as well.

Maps : The maps are average and there are some bad ones. Nothing really stands out as a "good solid" map. The good thing is there are about 2 classic maps from 2007s COD which are really nice to play. There wasn't enough maps in my opinion on launch and although there are a few more maps, a game of this caliber should've had more and better maps at launch and throughout the season.

Controls are perfectly fine and just like previous CODs. Not much to say but they are good.

Graphics are great. I think this is the best looking call of duty game to date. Lots of graphics settings to play around with. I played this on a Ryzen 5 5600X and Radeon 6750XT setup. 16GB of Ram and a 1TB SSD.

I am not going to comment on the SBMM because I dont care weither it's in the game or not. It doesn't make much difference to me but the way I see it, it's a good thing. Prevents the hard-core high ranking players going against the newer, less experienced players.

Overall, this game was fun. At the time of this review, it's been almost a year since the game came out. I have about 250hrs on it. This game will likely last another 1-2 years so it's worth the price tag of about $59.99 Canadian and no more.

In summary, I rate it a solid 7/10. The gun play, the gunsmith, return of an almost classic prestige system, variety of good camos and modes, and traditional campaign/spec Ops offerings are solid. The game will seem to be active up to about mid 2025 as each COD remains very active for about 2 years or so until it starts to taper off. Unfortunately, a 7/10 is not good for a franchise with this much experience. Given the absolute bangers this franchise released in the 2000s and 2010s, the recent titles should've been near perfect games but they are starting to lose their flair.

Hope that helps in your purchasing decision.
