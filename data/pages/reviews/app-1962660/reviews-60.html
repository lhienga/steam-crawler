\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere's several reasons why Modern Warfare II (2022), even if purchased at sale price, is not worth any money above $20.

1. Activation continuously fails at adequately addressing hacking and cronus use in ranked and unranked modes. The presence of these cheats effectively renders the game unplayable at the very least in ranked.
2. Infinity Ward maintains their often rejected trend of introducing innovative yet bad and ineffective gameplay mechanics. Infinity Ward removed many foundational COD elements in what seems like a maneuver towards a more tactical gameplay. There's no traditional mini-map, kill-streaks are some of the worst in the franchise, movement is almost compeltely absent; and the maps fail promoting fluid arena style gameplay, instead favoring a style more closely aligned with CS-GO type games. This maneuver fails on so many levels.
3. In my experience this COD is poorly optimized for PC.
4. Perhaps most importantly, Activision clearly care more about generating a profit from microtransactions in this game than the actual core game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMW2 somehow manages to fall short in comparison to MW2019 for so many reasons.

While 2019 had a lot to prove by reintroducing the Modern Warfare franchise to veterans and new players alike, this sequel had to provide a fun, engaging and creative shooter experience based on the characters of the first one, teasing the return of Ghost and Soap as well as some surprises.

The new Ghost and Soap were definitely the part I was looking forward to the most, with the highlight mission "Alone" being excellent in showing their dynamic and comradery. Besides that, there is little to praise in the second installment. The incredible visuals of the game are overshadowed by many small technical issues that severely hampered my experience, including stutters, frame drops, awful AI, artifacts and lighting issues. Also, I can't help but feel like the pacing and the writing in the sequel have received a massive downgrade in quality, with cringey oneliners, context inappropriate jokes and villains that are extremely predictable, cartoonlike. The only exception to this is as I've previously mentioned in the mission "Alone", which is all around excellent with great gameplay, new mechanics, amazing level design and dialogues. To only add to the problems, it seems the new voice acting direction for some characters is just not good enough to say the least. Maybe Soap's voice is embedded in my head from the countless playthroughs of the original trilogy, but at this point listening to him speak, I only want to put my hand over his mouth to shut it. Somehow this is not true for "Alone", which just makes all of this extremely weird. Same thing can be said about the audio direction. I'm sadly not impressed at all by the state the sound design is in, And last but not least, the god awful warzone integration menu, the "Call of Duty HQ". Just a UI design nightmare.

With that being said, I don't recommend this game, but if MW3 somehow manages to revert these mistakes, I will be more than happy to overlook this sad entry to the Call of Duty franchise which I've been playing for 16 years now. More than anything, I want MW3 to be a great game.\t\t\t\t\t
