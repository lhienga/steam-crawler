I just can't in good faith recommend this game to be honest.
I mean, if you're buying this only the campaign, then that's fine. You won't be getting much value in this other than the campaign. I stopped playing since Season 3 reloaded, and only watched videos on others playing the game.
Let's start off with the few goods:
  • The campaign is pretty good! While it sucks we didn't get a direct sequel to MW2019's ending cutscene (after Verdansk's Nuking), it still holds up pretty well.
  • The animations and attention to detail are pretty good, the reloads and all that stuff are pretty neat. HOWEVER, I doubt anyone other than people like me would care, as most people aren't into those little things.
Those are where the main positives end, and while they may be more... I doubt they'd be enough to save this.
[h2[Buckle up, here is all wrong with this mess.[/h2]
The Multiplayer and fundamentals. if you want specifics?
  • Let's say Engagement Optimized Match Making puts you into matches with the intent of either making or breaking your day. It's just so frustrating to join lobbies, only to leave it and join another lobby that somehow is very easy. It's just super annoying and inconsistent. This is a problem in Warzone and DMZ too, but Multiplayer really lays it on thick here.
  • I'm not a movement person, but I will say the movement feels very slow and clunky. Sliding feels very... weird, though the Dolphin Dive feels neat, even if it's a bit inconsistent. Still, it doesn't feel as smooth and arcadey as what I remember from MW2019.
  • The perk system is something I don't like. I can appreciate trying something new, but if it ain't broke don't fix it! (This can describe the game tbh). I don't really see the need to make it more like Specialist from the older games, and if they were to do this... maybe make it to where less integral perks are available from the start, then we can mess with the more broken perks.
  • The minimap, just like in MW2019 only is good for map locations. While there is a perk to fix this, it is only usable near the end of the match or so (this goes into the Perk problem). At least the maps are more acceptable than what MW2019 is.
  • The gunsmith and reciever system is just taking a great idea too far. Gunsmith is a great system, but I wouldn't go that far as to lock attachments behind a certain weapon (which is what they're going for, I think). I think Cold War and MW2019 did the system MUCH better, Vanguard is meh...
  • Spec Ops isn't interesting, and it's too out of the way to even look for it unless you really want to play it. Raids are pretty neat in terms of continuing MW2019's story, but there are TONS of issues with glitches and random crashes. Speaking of Crashing...
  • Crashing is VERY prominent on the PC version, like I don't know why but whenever I play for a certain amount of time the game just crashes out of nowhere. I don't know if this is global, but I don't feel like the game is optomized for many computers on my level.
  • Overall, the game just feels VERY unfinished, from the features we should have had a while ago, to smaller things that would have made things just better. It just baffles me to see a game made by many developers not feel like what it's supposed too.
That isn't even the worst part, let's get into the Monetization...
  • While I'm glad we moved past loot boxes, I think the "Live Service"/"Season" system is just not interesting or productive. Modern Warfare Remastered and WWII, while using Lootboxes, found a way to make content constant and surprising; while being able for people to play to get what they want. Here, we have to wait for a season to end or for a mid season update to happen in order to get new content.On the topic of Seasons, the roadmaps and season updates just haven't been interesting or engaging. That and you have to do most of the stuff on the Battle pass, which isn't easy as it says it is.
  • oh yeah... the Battle pass. Let me start of by saying Blackcell sucks, it just isn't necessary. In terms of the battle pass itself, I miss how they did it in MW2019-Vanguard. I get choosing certain sections you like, but with that many; it overwhelms a player. I never completed the battle pass, because I feel like there's too many sections to get to the stuff you want, and battle pass tokens come too slow. Fortnite did this concept much better, linking it to your progression.
  • The store prices are too dang high! And they might also have an effect on EOMM (stated above). If you have a good budget and a lot of time to spare, then I guess you're just making things easy. However, I don't have the resouces to invest myself into getting these flamboyant, eccentric, and overall unfitting skins. There is also a possibility that you're getting easier games with this, bu that is just a theory.
  • This is more of a general complaint, but why make this MWII DLC a Full on game? Why are they just now adding features that should've been here from the start? A better idea would've making the Multiplayer for MWII free, while the campaign and "Zombies" could be paid. It just baffles me to see this DLC be called "Modern Warfare III" instead of something like "Modern Warfare II: Integral" or "MWII: Substance"
In conclusion...
I really can't believe I thought it was gonna be a good year for CoD, especially after the failure of Vanguard. I mean, what else did I expect?
I won't delude myself with MWIII, and I really won't be doing anything more with CoD for a while (except for a few things). It's kind of ironic, there were so many supposed CoD killers (Titanfall, BF1, XDefiant) but it was ultimately up to CoD to settle their fate.
If Microsoft does control CoD post 2025, then I really hope they'll be able to clean up the mess Vanguard beyond has made.
Man... what a shame.
