\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBig downgrade from the big hit Modern Warfare 2019. I was unfortunately one of the people who got baited into this game after seeing what Infinity Ward pulled off with MW2019. The events and twists in the campaign were out of nowhere and some made no sense entirely. There were some good changes in multiplayer with some of the perk choices (Kill Chain will not be missed), but the progressive perk system as a whole is just questionable. I also question the removal of Specialist: giving up your killstreaks for more perks. That was my go-to in MW2019 but it just doesn't exist anymore. Gun customization doesn't feel nearly as good as it did before. I used to run all sorts of absurd weapons, converting my rifles from stockless speed carbines to long-range DMRs and making it work every time. Not much of that anymore.

The Ground War in this game just doesn't feel as engaging as it did in MW2019. I used to only play that mode in MW2019 but now I found myself just playing small team multiplayer again. Even then, pretty much all the maps have HARD camping spots or just poor layouts that guarantee uneven firefights. Ironically some of the better maps were the maps they brought back from previous games. Tracing a real life location doesn't make for a good map.

All of these issues only became more prevalent with each season. It seemed like Infinity Ward didn't know what they were doing and were just pushing seasons out for more skins, battle passes, and money. I was not much a fan of Warzone, but I would still play it with my friends. Warzone 2.0 is a joke and not even my friends who were big into Warzone play it. DMZ was a cool mode for a week or two but it lacks the meaningful progression to keep me invested. This will likely be my last Call of Duty title. I'm not looking to get baited again with a Modern Warfare 3 and Treyarch seems to be getting shafted by Activision. Goodbye, Call of Duty.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is an update to my original review of Modern Warfare II.

Campaign is pretty good. Not the best COD campaign & not all of it's changes to the COD formula work (looking at you armoured enemies & tank boss fight), but it's a good time and tells an engaging enough story.

Multiplayer can be fun if you jive with the faster time to kill, relatively more restrictive movement & more tactical feel, but if you prefer a faster-paced, more arcadey shooter you're out of luck and would be better off waiting for MWIII to launch in November (which is a pretty overt course-correction in this direction and whose existence flys in the face of the 2 years of post-launch support that were promised for this game).

Haven't played much of Spec Ops, but it's 100% much better than MW19 if you're into this sort of thing. Similar story with those Raids (although I haven't touched those at all).

The menu is also terrible BTW. Feels more like a streaming service than an actual game.

Overall, I recommend Modern Warfare II for the campaign, but not at $70 USD/$90 CAD. Wait for a sale. Multiplayer wise, unless what I described interests you I'd just skip this game and get MWIII instead. I kinda wish I didn't have to choose between recommending and not recommending, but considering most people buy COD for the multiplayer... I'm gonna have to go with No for this game in it's current state...

Ratings:

Creative score (story, gameplay, voice acting, art direction): 7/10 (would be an 8 or so if not for Infinity Ward's decisions with multiplayer & that terrible menu)
Technical score (graphics, audio, optimization): 8/10
Business Practices score: 4/10 (exclusive content on PlayStation, microtransactions & a FOMO-inducing Battle Pass, not to mention the broken promises of 2 years of post-launch support)
Overall score (my thoughts on a game’s overall quality, doesn’t consider the business practices unless they are detrimental to the experience): 7/10\t\t\t\t\t
