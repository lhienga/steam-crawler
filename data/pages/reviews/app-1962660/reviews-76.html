\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI shelled out 100 dollars for the vault edition. Why? Go glance at the store page for MW19 and see the difference in review scores between that game and this. Three years ago, Infinity Ward released a fantastic title that revitalized the series and kept me playing even through Cold War and Vanguard's lifespans in the following years. I had extremely high expectations and they were ♥♥♥♥ on by a very clearly unfinished title, a drip feed of a seasonal release cycle, a practically biblical swarm of bugs, and some of the most predatory monetization in a series that had already not been particularly respected for its MXT schemes.

But hey, ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, you put almost a thousand hours into this ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥? Why? Because honestly, there's SOMETHING there. Call of Duty's problem is that there isn't really a direct competitor anymore for fast-paced arcade military shooters. They got lazy but the game still does what it's supposed to and after a grueling work week when I don't have quite the brainpower for anything that requires an above room temperature IQ, then yeah, this hits that niche. The gunplay, sound design, and animation work in MWII is still the best in the industry. It scratches an itch neither of the other FPS titans have come near. (Seriously, go look up a vid of the newly released 2011 pistol's anims. They're insane.) The campaign was still as enjoyable as ever, even if it has a bit of a tonal whiplash and writing inconsistencies that its predecessor didn't. (Bonus, it's actually difficult for once.) The raids and story-oriented co-op missions on release were a solid addition.

Should you bother buying this? It's got the polish of a five dollar product, so if it by some miracle goes that low? Why not. Should you wait for MWIII? Probably not, it's got a horribly rushed development cycle and will more than likely have most of the same problems this title does. Should you just get MW19 instead? Honestly, no. The game's abandoned by IW and Activision and is in a barely functional state. It's disheartening, but this series is in the worst state of its life, and there's no reason or incentive for them to improve. Whatever love the development team has put into this game is deeply overshadowed by corporate greed and a complete lack of willingness by management to listen to the playerbase's suggestions. And unfortunately, I'm still gonna keep playing, because there's nothing else like it out there.\t\t\t\t\t
