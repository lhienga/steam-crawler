\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long-time Call of Duty fan, this and Modern Warfare 2019 have definitely been my favorite entries in the franchise since the original Call Of Duty 4: Modern Warfare.

It's not without its problems but on the whole, the gunplay feels like its at its best, the gunsmith that allows you to modify and closely tune all of your weapons is at its best, and the graphics are at their best.

One of my long-held criticisms of the franchise has been the push to crank out new Call of Duty games almost every year, and while Infinity Ward has delivered in my opinion with the two recent Modern Warfare titles, Black Ops: Cold War, and Vanguard were both abysmal games that felt like shallow copy-cats with terrible map design, terrible and grindy unlock progression, terrible audio quality, and yet they kind of hijacked the player-base of MW2019. MW2019 saw very few updates during that time period and Warzone had moved on to focus on all those terrible weapons from the other games. I still enjoyed playing MW2019 but it would be nice for them to let a game breathe a bit when they actually succeed at making a good game.

MW3 is slated to be the next entry, and for now it seems that they are hoping to "carry forward" content from MW2 so that it doesn't suffer the same fate as MW2019. Time will tell how successful that is, but I'm not getting my hopes up just yet on that. That being said, I still enjoyed playing MW2019 for years right up until the release of the new MW2, so hopefully I'll have a similar experience with MW2, so far my experience has been largely a positive one.

My favorite aspect of the game is the gunsmith. A major improvement over previous titles is that there is cross-compatibility with unlocked weapon attachments, so that as you play the game, each weapon can access attachments you've already unlocked from other guns. That way trying to get a gun to a level where it feels optimized doesn't require a lot of long grinding to unlock 50 attachments specific to only that gun. Now there are only 20 or so new attachments to unlock, plus all the other attachments compatible with the gun that you've unlocked from other weapons.

Weapon camos are similarly cross-compatible. Rather than having to unlock the same camos over and over for each gun, there is an incredibly huge selection of weapon camos that can be unlocked by completing challenges for particular guns, but they can be used on any other weapon in the game as well. This really makes the progression way less of a "camo grind" like in previous call of duty titles, and I'm finding that I am much more interested in the selection of camos in this game, whereas in previous titles, the only ones I was interested in were the gold and Damascus camos. Weapon styles from custom blueprints are also easy to attach/remove/switch on guns without having to start from a particular blueprint.

I adore the addition of fine-tuning the weapon attachments paired with the shooting range that you can instantly load into, even with loading into a game. It makes figuring out what works for you a lot easier and less time consuming, and relying less on looking up on the internet what attachments work best with what weapon. You can fine tune things to your own playstyle.

Like MW2019, the gunplay feels super responsive, the audio is crisp and clear, the in-game hud is pretty well polished (though the hud in this game has had a long road to get to this point, the hud was pretty messy at launch), and the selection of maps and modes has never been better. That isn't to say there aren't any bad maps, Black Gold (their attempt at a Night Vision map) is probably my least favorite of the whole franchise, mainly because the night vision mechanic really deserves to be separated into its own mode, since it really requires a completely different build for weapons since the night vision interferes with weapon optics and laser attachments.

While I played Warzone for a while in MW2019, the constant struggle to get into the game, get kitted up with weapons that weren't garbage before running into another squad and engaging, got pretty dull and annoying after a while. I haven't really spent much time in the new Warzone released with this title, but absolutely adore the new DMZ game mode, which features a fun combination of PvE and PvP gameplay, that allows you to infil into a match geared up, with a layer of progression that persists between games so that its not just starting over from scratch every time like in Warzone.

The singleplayer campaign is... Fine? The gameplay is fun enough but the storyline of MW2 and MW2019 is a far cry from the World War III narrative of the original Modern Warfare series, which had a much more climactic feel. Part of the problem is that the storyline is getting shoehorned into other modes like Coop, Raids, and Warzone/DMZ. So rather than having a self-contained narrative with a satisfying ending, the singleplayer campaign only feels like an introduction to the story, which leaves it feeling anticlimactic at the end.

The PvE coop isn't terribly interesting. There are a only a few missions to select from, and they aren't really anything to write home about. The Raids are more interesting, but require a significant amount of teamwork between you and two teammates that is really difficult to accomplish without two friends that you can rely on. The PvE is not only challenging, but often paired with trying to solve puzzle and maze-like elements, sometimes while under a time limit.

Aside from the occasional crash in DMZ, and annoying bug where you would sometimes infil without your weapon, I haven't experienced anywhere near the issues that other reviewers have raised.

Honestly the community is probably the worst aspect of the game. One only need to look at how heavily this game has been review-bombed to see how toxic and ornery the community is. I have to mute the in-game voice for most multiplayer matches because without fail there will be someone playing on console with a terrible controller mic with terrible quality and awful background noise and I don't want to have to mute people individually each game. This has kept me from playing a lot of modes that require voice communication like Search & Destroy and Ranked modes. I'll turn it back on for DMZ though which uses proximity chat.

I've never particularly noticed what people are calling "engagement-based" matchmaking that supposedly fluctuates the effectiveness of your guns so that you have runs of good and bad matches. My gameplay has felt pretty consistent, and provided that my team doesn't start leaving part-way through I can turn out a win more often than not, and it doesn't feel cyclical at all, and I can almost always end up at the top of the scoreboard. I do think there is Skill-Based Matchmaking to some extent, since there is a definitely a different spread of player skill on the opposing team when I play with lower-leveled friends vs when I join games solo. Overall none of that has ever been detrimental to my experience.

On the whole, this definitely was my favorite Call of Duty game to date, they have really refined the gunsmith and gunplay to really be the top of its class compared to a lot of other first-person shooters. That being said I've since moved on to mostly playing MW3, and am enjoying much the same. I'd recommend this game if it goes on sale, otherwise I'd recommend MW3 over this one if you are undecided between the two as MW3 has only improved on what I liked about this one.\t\t\t\t\t
