This is not a huge deal right now, but I think it does not bode well for the future of DMZ-- it's a free-to-play mode, and they might be making it pay-to-win, and I've also heard rumors that it will be either removed completely or replaced with the zombies mode. I played DMZ for 300-400 hours before I even bought the game (while it was on sale for ~$40).

Core multiplayer (the main part of the game) can be fun (mostly dumb fun, like I mentioned at the beginning), but has plenty of problems, and anyone who plays it will probably have no issue finding several things that irritate them.

The game is well-optimized, but out of all the games I've played, this is the one I've had to verify the integrity of the local files the most. I will randomly start getting FPS issues and packet burst, and when I verify it will tell me that 17 or 38 (17 happened more often in the past, 38 is more recent) files were missing. The updates that are pushed out seem pretty rushed to me, as evidenced by the various bugs in them; I know being a game developer is hard, but other games I play have much better releases, probably because they take their time. I don't know how much of it is the devs being bad at their job vs. Activision breathing down their necks, but it doesn't have a great result.

Basically, if you are thinking of buying the game but you're not sure if you want to, you probably shouldn't. Wait for the next Call of Duty title or just play a different, better game.\t\t\t\t\t
