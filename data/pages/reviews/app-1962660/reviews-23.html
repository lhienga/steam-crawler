\r\n\t\t\t\t\tModern Warfare II, oh boy, where do I even begin? If Modern Warfare 2019 was a gourmet meal, the 2022 edition is the equivalent of a microwave dinner that's been nuked one too many times.

Let's talk about graphics. While Modern Warfare 2019 left us in awe with its stunning visuals, the 2022 edition feels like a bad Photoshop filter applied to the original. It's as if the developers decided that a pixelated mess was the next big thing. Congratulations, you've managed to make nostalgia look worse than my grandmother's photo albums.

Gameplay-wise, it's like they took the smooth, refined experience of 2019 and handed it over to a group of interns armed with rubber chickens. The bugs are more persistent than telemarketers, and the crashes are more frequent than rainy days in Seattle. It's a sad state of affairs when the highlight of a game is its error messages.

The AI has apparently taken a hit in the intelligence department. In 2019, enemies felt like formidable opponents, but in 2022, it's like they've been replaced with cardboard cutouts that occasionally shoot back. It's not a challenge; it's a daycare for trigger-happy mannequins.

Weapon balancing? Forget about it. In 2019, choosing a loadout felt like strategic warfare. In 2022, it's more about picking your poison – or in this case, your glitchy, unbalanced poison.

And now, the supposed sequel, Modern Warfare III. It's like they took the flaws of 2022, turned up the volume, and added a soundtrack of disappointment. It's not even worth trying – a sad epilogue to a franchise that once stood tall.

In conclusion, Modern Warfare II - 2022, while attempting to continue the legacy, falls flat on its face. The only thing worse is the lackluster attempt that is Modern Warfare III - 2023. Save your time, save your money, and save yourself the disappointment, stick to the 2019 version, and pretend this attempted remaster never happened.

PS: COD HQ might be the worst thing to happen to the franchise.\t\t\t\t\t
