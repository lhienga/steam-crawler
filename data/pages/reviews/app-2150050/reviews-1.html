\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly disappointed with the flying foxes.

I don't usually leave reviews on DLC because frankly it's on me: the game is wonderful even if you don't add to it. If I opt to buy something extra to spice it up for some stupid amount, well I'm the idiot who shelled out real money for pretend things. And Frontier has put a ton of time and love into Planet Zoo.. there is just so much to like in this game (compare to Jurassic World Evolution, ex: the difference in effort is palpable)

But I didn't realize that the bats were just exhibit animals. Yes, the big exhibit is pretty cool, but the exhibits are, for me, the least interesting aspect of this game. You put them in, set them up, then just use them as money printers. Do I want the poison frogs or the scorpions? idk.. who breeds faster?

*sigh* my daughter made the point that it wouldn't make sense to have them loose in a habitat. She's right: in RL, one sunset and no more bats. But still disappointing. An aviary might have worked well..

Still posi because the animals are cute, the raccoon noises are cute, and I'm always down for new props and themes to add to my park (the mushrooms and benches are adorable.. really bringing me back to Theme Park!). But those bats..\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's times like this when I wish Steam allowed a "mixed" review option. For me personally, this is hands down the most disappointing DLC the game has to offer. The animal roster consists of the Raccoon, Red Fox, Striped Skunk, Common Wombat, and Egyptian Fruit Bat, the latter of which utilizes the walkthrough exhibit system that came with the free update to this DLC. If it wasn't for the Wombat and Egyptian Fruit Bat, I frankly wouldn't recommend this pack at all. The other three animal choices are so dull and uninteresting, and the building pieces aren't that unique. The masonwork bricks resemble the far superior stone walls from the Europe Pack, the roof pieces are fine but nothing special, and it also includes Halloween scenery, which is flat-out useless to a player like me that never builds seasonal zoos. However, the Egyptian Fruit Bat being able to fly around is such a novelty even now, and the Wombat is one of the cutest animals in the game, so I will recommend it, however hesitantly. Maybe this pack will appeal more to you than me, and that's fine, but overall I just find this pack pretty dull.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the weakest DLCs for Planet Zoo so far. While the general idea of a nocturnal themed pack is innovative, the execution is lackluster. The animal choices of this pack; the red fox, the striped skunk, the raccoon, the wombat and the Egyptian fruit bat, lack in diversity compared to previous Planet Zoo packs. Four of them can be found dumpster diving in your average suburb. While they do appear in zoos all around the world, they are not necessary animals for your average zoo experience. The wombat is the only animal with some appeal to complete Australian sections in your zoo. The addition of walkthrough exhibits might seem a good thing at first sight, but as long as we lack diversity in sizes for exhibits (walkthrough or not) I don't see myself using this in my zoos. The props in this pack don't feel like they fit into Planet Zoo and feel more like scrapped assets from Planet Coaster. While the wallsets and stone accessories are useful, you'll probably never use 60% of the props in this pack.

Overall this pack was a missed opportunity to introduce cool nocturnal animals and add much needed props for interior decorating of nocturnal (or reptile) houses. I recommend buying this DLC only when it's on sale, because it's not worth the full price if you compare it to Planet Zoo's other DLCs.\t\t\t\t\t
