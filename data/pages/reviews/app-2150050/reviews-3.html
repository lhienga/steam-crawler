Since the other animals are not huge selling points, I feel like this pack is just a bust. Yeah raccoons and skunks are cool, but I feel like it should have been something added for free or sell this pack for less than $5, since it feels incomplete. It would have been better if they added more creatures that come out at night and they really failed at not adding possums.

I do like the decor. It is themed to a degree, but not everything is "halloween" looking. I wish there were smaller mushrooms as well. They could have really added more plants for more of the spooky vibe they were going for.

I really hope they fix the bats, then this pack would be one of my favorites.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[TLDR: The bats look awful and do nothing - we are not one step closer to aviaries]

Frontier know there's a certain portion of the player base who are dying for flying animals to be added to the game and I just gave the walkthrough exhibit a go and got some bats in there and waited for something to happen and I thought: Well, they're not moving...Oh, I wonder if they've actually programmed the bats to be active at night time!
(Spoilers, they did not).
So I waited for a while longer while the bats did nothing and then it dawned on me, that like other exhibit animals they have their set places that change if you add other animals in the same exhibit. So I threw some more bats in there and was really disappointed to see that Frontier's usual standards for animal behaviours and quality of animations were nowhere to be found here. The short loop of animations is a worrying problem/sign of things to come as they are, but also the way these bats fly looks (and I'm gonna be blunt here for the sake of future attempts on Frontier's part, if you're reading this) just awful.

Seems dramatic but I've just asked Steam for a refund. That's where I'm at.
Disregarding any pondering on what Frontier's intentions were here, including these animals absolutely would be a main draw for people who have been shouting with megaphones to include aviaries and would have sparked their imaginations. I'm just really surprised that even if these animals are confined to exhibits, that they can't go from one set placement on a perch say, fly down to the water tray or over to their food, and that even if they do have looping flying animations, that they are so low quality - at odds with the amount of effort shown to other animals in the game. This is a premium inclusion, you're charging people for this animal.
I'm also very surprised walkthrough exhibits have been added to the game when they don't support functionality with any other animal. If they weren't ready Frontier shouldn't have pulled the trigger on them.

I know this comparison is brought up a lot when talking about flying animals making it into PZ, but this is even worse than JWE1's aviary and I did not think that'd ever be the standard Frontier would be happy with were they to put flying animals in PZ.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've stated in previous reviews that Planet Zoo has never had a bad dlc, to be bad I feel it would have to actively harm the game in some way. That said, I am sorry to say this pack has by far the best chance to being a bad dlc, and is by far the worst the game has seen, excluding the deluxe pack. When the game was launched, the community was united in wanting more animals. There were those who wanted more scenery, but the overwhelming majority wanted more animals for the zoo game. Even those who wanted new pieces were quite vocal about the specific pieces wanted. Chain link, faux rocks, customizable billboards, corrugated metal, flexicolor wood, wood animals can‚Äôt climb, netting and ropes, more realistic looking wood for climbing structures, and all those things have since been added to the game. I've felt since Africa Pack that the game was good on pieces, and the following packs have added decent stuff, but nothing exciting or well requested. I honestly think that unless the pieces are really amazing, we should just do animal packs going forwards.

And therefore, this pack was immediately met with confusion and disappointment by the community. The established cycle since SEA Pack has been Scenery Pack ‚Äì Animal Pack, so the community was excited to have seven new animals to play with this fall. Instead, we not only got another Scenery Pack, but by far the worst the game has ever seen. I often see people complain about the Arctic pack because the pieces were too themed, and that is valid, but it's nowhere near as bad as this pack is with the theming. The theme here is Halloween, which is a baffling choice. While some zoos have a boo at the zoo event once a year, have you ever seen a zoo with a dedicated Halloween section? Or the entire zoo? It's bizarre, and most pieces are hyper-specialized to a style I cannot see most ever using. Zoos do have winter areas, and some northern zoos are entirely winter themed, but I have never even seen a single zoo have a 'Halloween area', let alone be dedicated to it. You get pieces like jack-o-lanterns, gargoyles, giant glowing eyes, and giant glowing fungi. These pieces just don't fit with anything else in the game and are even more stylized than the Christmas content of Arctic. The more standard pieces are okay but nothing that would make me want the pack. Flexicolor corrugated metal is cool,and the wall peices are fine but similar to what we have seen in base game and in the EU pack.

The scenery is a complete bust, but what about the animals? This pack is also unfortunate in, outside of the deluxe edition, having by far the worst animal line up yet. It's hard to have an animal be 'bad', and there are worse choices, but the problem with this pack is 'sameness'. You get three small north American mammals, and a small marsupial. The Raccoon is a choice that the community is greatly split on, some people love it or hate it. I find myself in the middle, I don't think I will use it much if ever, it feels out of place compared to exotic animals. I understand that those in countries where it doesn‚Äôt occur might feel more special, but it doesn't do it for me. The skunk is in the same boat, but also suffers from being an animal nearly no one was asking for. I have said in previous reviews that an animal shouldn‚Äôt be popular to be worth including, as there are plenty of interesting critters that are obscure but would add much to the game. We don't have any small, hoofed animals like chevrotains, pudu, duikers or muntjac. None of these are popular but would add much. We have large habitat tortoises, but no large aquatic turtles. Imagine making viewing for fly river turtles, or giant Asian pond turtles! Or imagine a climbing habitat animal that is an invertebrate, a giant coconut crab! These are a few examples of obscure animals that would add a lot of unique flare to the roster. A small mammal that is both common in the wild, and not seen as particularly exotic, and doesn't have a lot of unique behaviors just doesn't do it for me. The Red Fox takes what seems to be a curse of packs, in which the canine of the scenery pack is hated by the community. The fennec dodged it once, but the red has not managed that. To be fair to the fox, it does have supporters, but unlike the raccoon that seems split down the middle, I feel like most of are against the presence of the fox. We have three foxes now, and three red canines. There are still interesting dogs like bush dogs and maned wolves that might never get in. I feel what happened here is that at launch, people really wanted foxes of any kind, and so asked for them, but after adding two, frontier did not consider that people might be satiated, and have continued down the path of 'people really want foxes'. To their credit, the fox has some cool texture mechanics that breathe life into it, but again it is an animal I will rarely if ever use, that really should have been substituted. The Wombat is the highlight of the pack, and the one animal that the community is united in excitement for, and I have nothing but praise for it. I hope we get to see a few more marsupials in the future as well.

I normally ignore the exhibit animal, but this pack added the first exhibit animal the community is engaged with: The Egyptian Fruit Bat. The first exhibit animal that can move, and the first flying animal in the game. Sadly, this animal really represents the 'two steps forward, one steps back' approach that a lot of planet zoo updates fall into. While the bats can at least move, they are forced into repetitive and obvious loops. Their cage can be made null, which is a great, but some elements cannot be hid, and the bat animations are both highly limited and often genuinely awful looking. When bats are allowed to fly in wide arcs, it makes a convincing image, but most of the bats fly in tiny, tight circles, forever circling a single point, or stopping to land before returning. This looks legitimately terrible, and I cannot believe it made it into full release. Worse, bats that aren't 'allowed' to fly don't even get interesting animations. One of the preset decors is a rope. I thought the bats would at least have an animation of them climbing down it, like they do in real life. If that is here, I have never seen it, they just perch on it like it was the wire mesh. Why give them enrichment item if they won't do anything with it? The most frustrating part is there were ways to do this system that would have been much better. JWE2 already has flying animals with actual AI that can move freely, and if they really wanted them to be looping, why not give the animals a system where they can choose from a selection of available loops, rather than the same one forever? That would also let players decorate more freely, the bats could just fly until they detect a player placed enrichment, then line up to do a preset animation.

This pack is a huge misstep. It could have been a nocturnal themed pack that added night houses, so we don‚Äôt have to make the game switch to night to get the effect. It should have been an animal pack with more options for players to use. What we have here is a pack with the worst pieces, the worst animal selections, and a terribly implemented flying system that makes the future of birds look grim. Would I rather have birds and bats like this then not at all? Absolutely. But it could have been done so much better, and in ways frontier has either already done before, or in ways that are as easy as what was done. Absolutely. If you love the game, you are stilling buying this pack, but I hope future packs are better than this. If you like the animals, this might not be a bad pack, but if you don't, and for the scenery and the way bats were done, it very well might be.
