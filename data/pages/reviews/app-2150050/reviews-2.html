\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis one was a complete surprise - so many small animal species in one pack! The animals are all great additions, and beautiful, but the build items are far too heavily themed and I really dislike them. The stalagmites/tites and other cave rocks, however, are essential items!

Pros: flying bats!! great range of small animal species
Cons: awful build items that suit a theme park, not a zoo
Recommend for: players who want small animal species, players who like to build tacky, heavily themed areas

Ok, I’ll get this out of the way first: I hate the build items. They are all Halloween or gothic themed, tacky and completely limited to use in a spooky themed zoo. Glowing mushrooms, gothic castles and Halloween pumpkins are great for a theme park, but not a zoo. I will never use any of the build pieces in this pack, they are hideous.

The animals, on the other hand, are excellent! We have five small nocturnal critters that will be suited to any zoo.

Flying foxes!!! I never thought we’d get them, but here they are! The model is perfect, adorable and realistic. The animations are amazing - they fly in the new exhibits and are very active. They eat fruit from feeders, they hang from exhibit items. They are perfect, and worth buying the pack for on their own!

We also got red foxes! These iconic and instantly recognisable animals can be used in any zoo, in any region. The colour variations are stunning and diverse, and I’m so excited to see my foxes birth some colourful babies. The animations are wonderful, playful and skittish and lifelike.

The raccoons and skunks are amazing! They look phenomenal, they behave like the real things, they’re fun to build for and to watch.

Wombats are boring, but very cute and we definitely needed them for our Australian sections! I’m very happy to have them.

Overall this pack is worth it for the animals. I’d rather have seen it as an animal pack and get rid of the silly scenery pieces. Hopefully the themed stuff is all done now, and we can have more versatile pieces (that are actually suited to zoos) in the future!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Twilight pack is without much doubt a somewhat disappointing DLC for Planet Zoo. Primarily, I think people were expecting this to be an animal pack and the species selections are not all that exciting. A lot of people seem to think this is a sign of an impending aviary expansion to come at the end of the year, but as I've said before, I wouldn't hold my breath. This is Frontier we're talking about. A video game developer that often over-hypes their products and then under-delivers.

Regarding the animals, the only two that I believe are of importance are the common wombat and Egyptian fruit bat. The wombat is a popular choice given the under-representation of Australian and Oceanic animals in general and the Egyptian fruit bat was an unexpected but welcome addition given that it's an exhibit animal that actually actively moves and flies about. Plus for a pack of this theme, the lack of any bat species would have been extremely odd. The raccoon, red fox, and striped skunk I am less than keen on. Yes, a lot of zoos keep such animals as what they call "animal ambassadors" to teach the public about local wildlife, but for a pack themed around nocturnal animals, while they do make sense because they are nocturnal, I can't help but feel they have shoved aside more important animals of this nature that should have taken priority. Examples would include the Tasmanian devil, ocelot, caracal, African leopard, striped hyena, African crested porcupine, one of the various jackals of Africa or Asia, and et cetera in that manner. I just feel like Frontier really dropped the ball with the animal selections this time. Then again, they've done that before, so I'm not entirely surprised.

The scenery and building pieces are far too niche in my opinion to make for useful zoo building components. Sure, Halloween-themed stuff is okay, but it's so time and location specific that it doesn't feel like it fits as part of a zoo build. Maybe a theme park like in Planet Coaster, but not a zoo. I have not looked at the new campaign scenario yet, but I feel like they could've been adding to the campaign all this time with the other DLCs and just didn't out of laziness or some other reason.

All this being said, I have to give this one a meh rating. The wombat and fruit bat are the highlights, but the rest of it feels too mundane or too time-of-the-year or location specific to really feel like a natural next addition to the animal roster and building inventory. Again, wish Steam had a neutral option for reviews, but sadly no. I would recommend this on sale, but not at full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI honestly don't know how to rate this pack, it's a very mixed bag of animals and along with some good animal choices and building items, there's also some disappointing.

1 - Animal selection; disclaimer, a Wombat was my last "must have" for the game, so there was absolutely no chance I wasn't going to get this pack just for that. The Raccoon is a really good choice as well. The Red Fox is alright on its own but it could have been the Maned Wolf instead - in general a similar model, and much more requested and exotic, and fits the theme. The Skunk was the "surprise" pick they usually have. If we'd gotten the Maned Wolf I'd have been fine with it. but we didn't, and I can only wish this had been the Tasmanian Devil instead. One or the other would have work, instead we're left with a pack where half the habitat animals are disappointing.

The Walkthrough Exhibit is a base game update, but so far only the bat can be used for it. I'm interested to see where they go with this (probably/hopefully birds) but the fact that it's not tied to the pack tells me they're definitely going to use this again for other content. -There's no way they would have made this just for a bat. Which, the bat is great, even if the WE concept shouldn't be reviewed as part of this pack.

The scenery....I love Halloween scenery for the Sims, not so much interested in building a theme around it for a zoo. And since it's so niche and themed, I'm not even all that interested in it. The non-Halloween building sets are nice though, although not enough to say they're essential or even one of the better building sets in the game. The new foliage is really nice though and probably the best part of the non-animal part of the pack.

In truth, I would have gladly sacrificed all of the scenery (including the new foliage begrudgingly) for this to be an animal pack, with some other very popularly requested, and some well known, animal choices. Of course, tossing out the Fox for the Maned Wolf. Oh sure, they're probably saving the sloths, porcupines, tasmanian devils and what not for other packs, but honestly the animal selection here could have used one more popularly requested animal.

Bottom line, if you're not like me and don't view one of these animals as absolutely essential, get this one on sale. I still recommend it but recommend waiting. It's just not a strong pack overall, so it's a very weak "recommend" here.\t\t\t\t\t
