Overview:
This was a very pleasant game that held my attention through to completion. As others have stated, it is somewhat a mix of Stardew Valley, Pokemon, and Slay the Spire; if you enjoy(ed) at least 2 of those games, I would recommend picking this game up.

Positives:
  • The combat was interesting and engaging, and I enjoyed trying out the different types of spirits.
  • The spirit designs were almost all interesting or cute, and the music/ambiance of the world is quite relaxing.
  • The NPCs are interesting for the most part, and I didn't find myself with an uncontrollable hatred for any of them like i did in similar games.
  • The whole map is essentially open to you at the start of the game, with the only limit being your method of transportation. Obtaining better transportation is fairly easy as well, once you figure out how to get the materials.
  • The dungeons were very fun at first, and I enjoyed challenging myself by completing higher-level bosses. This plays into the freedom of being able to go anywhere from the start, and I enjoyed the little warning at the bottom of my screen when I wandered into an area too high-level for me.

Negatives:
  • It felt that after Summer/Fall of the first year, there wasn't much point to battling in order to level up. Being able to farm for experience is very broken since plants can be essentially doubled every harvest. This is a personal choice of course, but I found it difficult to grind out levels on enemies when I can just plant hundreds of experience crops.
  • Dungeons became a little stale by the end game, with essentially the same layout and with my spirits being much stronger than the enemies. This is offset somewhat by the occasional introduction of new puzzles.
  • While I found the NPCs themselves interesting, the socializing aspect of the game was a bit lackluster compared to a game such as Stardew Valley. I would like to see a more fleshed out version in future updates.
  • The spirit type-balance isn't the best in this game due to the special effects each type has. While all types have an interesting, unique mechanic to use, Poison and Fire types seem to be much stronger than the others (especially with a trimmed-down deck).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an amazing game. The story isn't particularly up front and personal, but for me that's not an issue. Where this game really shines is the gameplay and general sense of impact the crafting and level up systems have for me.

I really love the "Slay the Spire" meets "Creature Collector" combat and leveling of mons. It's refreshingly powerful as a blend of two genres. The number of mons is small, but they're cute, and well thought out (though the names are really silly).
Crafting and the tiers systems are good enough to keep you invested in doing different things, and the plants are all so cute and unique.

I really like it. There is another review stating that "it doesn't do anything particularly well" and if you're considering NOT getting this based on that review, please think again. I don't like Stardew Valley for the same reasons I love this game: It's different from what I'm used to in this genre.\t\t\t\t\t
