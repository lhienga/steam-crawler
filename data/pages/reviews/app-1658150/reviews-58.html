\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have had this game for 7 days (I think? XD) and I have played it for waaaaaaaaaaaay too long... and yet not long enough! It's a beautiful blend of artistic design, fun writing (I have laughed at the villagers' comments and mail a lot), varied game mechanics and a lot of heart. I appreciate the various references both in game and surrounding the game (I see you grandpa's bed (if you know you know)) and I absolutely adore Paolo. I appreciate that all the characters have their own subtle little backstories going on that often intertwine, but there's no info dumping. I feel like a detective trying to figure out who the characters are and, honestly, this kind of thing is my favourite part of these kind of games.

All this to say; this is a fab game and I love it. My main issue with it is that it is so new there's not a lot of online fan content or real world people who know about it who you can get excited about it with yet.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great casual game, that masterfully blends several of my favorite genres, including creature collecting, deck building, and farming/building. If you're looking for a game that presents daunting challenges (and the dopamine rush that comes with overcoming them), this game will not scratch that itch. But if you're looking for a relaxing, go-at-you-own-pace breather from those types of games, Moonstone Island has got you covered.There's definitely room for tweaks and improvements, but the price point is fair for what the game is, and it could really become something spectacular if it receives future content additions.

I'd rate the game a solid 8/10 in its current state, and I do recommend picking it up if it sounds like the type of game you're looking for.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRECOMMENDATION: Wait and watch

Is Moonstone island a bad game? No not in the slightest. Would I recommend this game to the average person no.
It pains me to say this but this game feels like a early access game. The devs have clearly put in a lot of effort to create a very ambitious game but as others have said the game is quite shallow as of now. They seem eager to support it and that's why I say keep a eye on this one because if they continue to work on it it has the potential to become a great game.

So what is Moonstone Island? It's a creature collector with deck builder combat and a lite farm / life sim aspect. My biggest issue with the game is its pace. It's both a slow burn of grinding for resources / building relationships with townspeople and a race against the clock to clear dungeons / explore before you have to make it home or else collapse from exhaustion.

The best way I can describe this game is its like Pokemon but the only town is Pallet town. Your free to explore as much as the world as you want but make sure you make it back to Pallet town or you'll lose precious time you'll need the next day.\t\t\t\t\t
