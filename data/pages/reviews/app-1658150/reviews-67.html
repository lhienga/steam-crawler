This game is good. I don't leave many reviews but I felt like there something that needs to be said about Moonstone Island.
There's probably a million Stardew Valley clones around by now - what's good and what's not?

Moonstone Island is.

I was very skeptical. I thought I was just going to try it and not like it and refund it, 6 hours later I'm looking forward to play more.

The feeling I got starting this games brought me to an old Zelda for some reason, an old Pokemon, and Harvest Moon, in a very polished way. Plus a sprinkle of Animal Crossing when you receive letters from mom and dad (who you actually meet here).

There is something about the pacing that I feel is just perfect, is not as slow as Stardew or as fast as Sun Haven, it kinda keeps you stuck to the screen with things to do without overwhelming you.
The dungeons are probably the best I've ever seen in a game like this. I'm not a dungeon lover but here - I'm just looking forward to find more!
And I normally don't pay attention to romancing characters but this time I'm just already dating the whole town.

Every single mechanic feels very polished and perfected rather than done to the extreme making other things lack.

I am very very excited to read the newsletter and see what else the devs bring to this game\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA mixed review for me I think....but I am quite enjoying this game! It's definitely got some sort of cozy vibes to it; it's chill, the graphics are adorable, the music is decent, and it's a mix of Stardew Valley, Animal Crossing, Littlewood, Studio Ghibli and Pokemon all in one. I also love the idea of your little spirits following you around (they are absolutely adorable!), and you being able to fly around anywhere on your broom! The controls/navigation are pretty easy to use once you get the hang of them and memorize which key/control does what (you can assign them yourself in the settings). The turn-base card fighting game is pretty awesome, I could imagine it as a standalone game as well; it's so much fun! As for interacting with the other characters on the island, it's sometimes hard/risky to tell which option is best to talk to them with, as some responses lower the relationship level, others raise it up; I guess it depends on the current mood of the character. You also craft/mine in the game as well, and as far as mining goes....you're lucky if you are able to find a hidden cave somewhere on whatever islands; I find that part tedious and slightly frustrating because I'm chopping/smashing left, right n' center, trying to find another randomly-spawned hidden cave to mine in (since ingots and coppers are needed a lot throughout the game I assume), and just none found - and you can't even mine in the caves you've already done because if you're like me, you've emptied that cave completely out. Regarding the fishing, easy peasy, and I'm glad it's not like Stardew Valley's green bar one; you just drag the circle around and try to keep it on the fish, and voila! You can also sell things and make money. I find it easier to make money by selling fish, than selling the other random stuff, especially if you catch a gold-starred fish because they sell at a better price than silver and bronze (obviously).

The only things (that I can think of so far) that I wish this game had are:
  • A longer daytime (perhaps also having a 'return to home' feature would save time, especially if you're on another island far away from home and trying your best to get back home as fast as possible before 2am hits)
  • An automatic inventory organizer feature where you can just have your inventory immediately organized with a click of a button
Other than that, a good release that I was looking forward to, I'm so glad I was able to purchase it, and it's definitely a nice game to just chill out to and get mentally lost in. I'm looking forward to new possible changes being made to this game in the future!
(I may edit this review from time to time and add more suggestions into the list if I ever think of any more)\t\t\t\t\t
