\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: I'm genuely having fun with the game, but I'll leave the review as "Not recommended" since people tend to focus on those more.

- - -

If you're into a cozy creature collecting game, with little to no lore nor interesting characters to date, but with cool Zelda-like exploration and dungeons, this game is for you!

The graphics are lovely, pixel art will never disappoint. Character sprites are cute and the spirit's designs are adorable, but this game cannot survive in graphics alone.

Cons:

- The game relies too much on RNG. The combat system, NPC interaction, amount of hearts you get with NPCs... It's specially awful when it comes to combat due to the system implemented: shields you have to break to stun and then damage the enemies. Sometimes you will not get a single shield breaking card, making your other cards useless as you can't inflict damage. Sometimes you'll only get shield breaking cards and now you can't inflict damage. It should have been thought out better.

- There's little to no tutorials on how to get things done (you have to figure out yourself, which isn't hard but it can be annoying).

- Farming is extremely boring and isn't profitable for now (I'm only 4 hours in, so I haven't discovered many seeds yet). Plus, when trying to till/water a tile, the selected tile will just move and it's hard to get to the one you want.

- I'm not sure if you can get rid of the mines you find by breaking rocks, since I don't want a mine in the middle of the pathway leading to my home (there's one already and I want to get rid of it). Maybe you have to clear everything that's in the mine first? I don't know.

- You can only have 3 spirits, if you want more you need to build a barn and some sort of feeder/bed (forgot what it is) for them, else you'll have to let them go.

- The main character has no feeling of self; you are simply an alchemist. The NPCs know you are the new alchemist even though you never introduced yourself to anyone nor sent a letter to the town saying you were coming in.

- Due to the main character being their own character, there's no customization. I'd rather create a character from scratch than play as a character who has no personality and whose only trait is to be an Alchemist (did you know you are an alchemist?).

- The artstyle used for the NPCs' portraits is... bad. Most of the NPCs look ugly with those silly big ol' anime/manga eyes (modding community do your thing please).

- Speaking of NPCs, it is weird to me that all NPCs you encounter are dating candidates. It'd be nice to have NPCs not interested in dating, at all.

- The interactions with the NPCs depend solely on RNG. You have 3 interactions: Talk, Joke and Flirt, with their respective % of success. It's honestly annoying and building friendship with them is tedious. It's honestly discouraging to talk to the NPCs. Not to mention the NPCs' dialogues are extremely bland and boring. There is nothing interesting to them.

- For some reason, whenever you enter a building the game decides to zoom in, making the inside of the building and everything inside it big, which clashes with the smaller and zoomed out pixel art from outside.

- The game suddenly stops sometimes for like a second but resumes right after (it hasn't creashed for me yet).

- Try to avoid talking to the Blacksmith much if you don't want an NPC flirting with you out of the blue; she's too forward, maam needs to learn some respect. Just upgrade your tools and run out of there. Also Paolo being the stereotypical suave Hispanic man who plays the accoustic guitar rubs me the wrong way (probably just me).

- The game soft-locks you/stops working if you use a Shield Bash on an enemy with no shields. You cannot press anything, you have to close the game and lose your progress.

- There's plenty of bugs that have been reported.

- Devs can't seem to take bugs/glitches seriously and try to justify the bugs by giving them silly stories on the game's Twitter page. Not only that, but they are suspiciously fond of Ferra, the one NPC who catcalls and harasses the player with her sexual and flirtatious comments.


Pros:

- The graphics are very charming. The pixel art in this game is very well made and captivates you inmediately.

- I do LOVE the speed at which you walk, and you can run!!

- The combat system is very fun! With a few problems but fun! As someone with no experience with card games, the battle system is very novice friendly and you get the hang of it quickly. Though, sometimes you'll get unlucky and your whole deck will be from your dizzy/stunned spirit </3

- The exploration in the game is extremely fun! You are encouraged to discover different island to fullfil NPCs quests and, in the process, you discover more mines and temples and spirits! (Watch out for the elemental islands, they hurt).

- You can expand and decorate your home, as well as build new homes with different designs!

- - -

I was super excited to play this game, but I was let down by the gameplay and the NPCs. I wish there was more of a bigger story other than helping out Magic Man recover his memories, but once again, I'm only 4 hours in, so I ignore if there's more story to this game than that. I am very excited to decorate and expand my home though, so I'm looking forward to that <3\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; if you will strictly play on PC with decent specs - go for it, def an adorable fun game with some good spins on cozy farming/rpg/tamer sims. If you're looking for a Steamdeck game, skip it or keep an eye for later dev posts that performance has had some serious upgrading.

EDIT - After some time on PC ..... maybe give this a few patches if you're sensitive to FPS shenanigans, and for some balancing patches (the game really hits you with energy/time restriction, everything costs energy including opening doors when I went into town???). That didn't feel great to wake up, go do some rounds of hellos and convos, try to start farming 12 plots and then character goes into a ~2 second animation of exhaustion (which repeats... a lot... cause you're constantly fighting to have energy). That's not including the eating animation to add another second, and then you lose time on the clock which also goes a bit fast for how much there is to do. There do seem to be energy upgrades and movement speed buffs here and there but the opening of the game should draw you in, give you abundance to keep you hooked and coming back; give you extra so you can soak in the good stuff and not feel like wasting time watching your character repeat a heavy sigh and sweat 5 times in 20 seconds to lose an in game hour to animation locks.


It looks great, and the mechanics work with what they want to do. The sprites are adorable and feel like they were made with care. My only gripe in general is that the controls feel floaty(?). Something about control of you player character feels too off for a game zoomed out as it is and to place a VERY small cross-hair for farming but I would figure this just takes getting used to (Edit - the oddity here seems to be that the game fights you where it wants to take actions where your facing but also where your cursor is so you spend some time fighting that since it doesn't feel intuitive the way it is and i wish the game would prioritize where you're facing if the mouse is out of range of the action being performed).

Unfortunately for me - I bought this to play mostly on Steamdeck and this game behaves oddly there. It starts at 60 frames but quickly hops from 50-60 for the first few minutes of play then the gap widens after a few 1-3 second "stutters" (the entire game freezes, you can't move or act, then it stops and you can move or act again) or the frames are permanently dropping 1-2 frames every few minutes. This was largely a problem moving through just the opening town map. Going indoors anywhere could get the frames back up and consistent but going outside would annihilate them again. I tried everything I'm aware of to handle performance with the Steamdeck but nothing would stabilize the performance; even limiting frames both by the built in limit OR by restricting the refresh rate didn't help. I'd still end up with the game down in the 20s or teens in terms of fps.

I fully understand the steamdeck isn't a power house in comparison to a PC. I understand games can have a lot going on under the hood that you can't see as a player. But for a game that does what other games already do and do it with better optimization (my stardew has SVE + ~50 mods, cassette beasts, Fae Farm, etc.) it hurts to watch this game slowly chug into a power point presentation that you want to suffer through cause cute spirits make good friends.

EDIT - I've now put a few hours on PC and that stutter/freeze still happens. Strictly when moving cells(?) on the map. Distinctly when entering and exiting cells that freeze will happen and halt all movement/action for 1-3 seconds and it's very jarring. I have a ryzen5, gtx3070, and 24 gb of RAM - this seems more of a game issue than my system, not being to spec for the loading. FPS is more consistent but still get the drops from 120-110 or bellow (lowest I witnessed was ~100).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWe've seen many iterations of games that use this concept to the point where it didn't feel unique. At first when my gf mentioned it to me, I sort of blew it off until she showed me the battling mechanic and the dungeon delving (which in short is my favorite part of the game, besides the fishing). I got it opening day and played as of writing this are 12 hours in. At first as I was playing I wasn't a huge fan of farming or gathering resources as it felt a little too grindy for me. But as I was continuing, I compared this to something like pokemon and how I wish pokemon had more stuff to do outside of just battling and catching. So my overall conclusion was that it fills the game up and honestly pulls me further into the game which is a plus.

lastly, romancing. I cannot express how much I love the diversity. It's a breath of fresh air. I cannot recommend this game enough. If you like Stardew Valley & Pokemon, this is right up your ally. give it a try and support good indie developers!\t\t\t\t\t
