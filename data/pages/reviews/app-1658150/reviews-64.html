\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are looking at the reviews to figure out if you should buy this game, TAKE THIS AS YOUR SIGN TO BUY THE GAME.

First if you like Pokemon, Stardew valley, Dungeon crawler card building games, Old school Zelda Graphics, Ghibli films vibes or even if you don't know what anything i listed is... this is the game for you. Okay honestly you can tell this studio had passion behind this game, very well polished no bugs what so ever, little details taken care of. This is a slower game but very much enjoyable, very straight forward you never feel like you are out of touch or that it is too overwhelming.

I never write reviews for games but this one really caught me by surprise and i can only imagine how much better this style of game would get with more support, so im doing my part. it is 100 percent a sandboxy type experience they don't force you to do anything you don't want to do which is amazing and they also don't hold your hand, baby sitting you through the game which is another plus. This game is a 9/10 easily on my book which is a strong score for me as i'm very critical. IF you are looking for nit picking i would say to get to the 10 marker first they would have to give me more of a reason to actually get on the island look around and explore instead of flying near it to see if it has a dungeon or some other things i can just collect within a few seconds (maybe buildings/towers or natural wonders). Maybe some more artistic moments as well sprinkled throughout the game would help the overall immersion. But again that is just something to get it from a 9 to a 10, just to go over some of the good, fun dungeons that aren't over bearing, seamless transition into the game, simple but fun card game mechanics that gets your creative gears turning (for example i'm currently building a very tanky heal/poison-stacking deck), beautiful graphics, simple farming mechanics, city feels alive with NPC always moving about, noticeable progression in all things from mobility to spirits, and so much more its not worth me going on and on.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried the game, and I disliked it. I'm a fan of pixel graphics, and the ads I saw had it playing up the farming aspect. I really enjoyed stardew Valley, but this game isn't really like that as far as I can tell. Stardew Valley is a game that starts you on a farm, here are tools, here are some seeds, and from there, go wild. This game seems more focused on Monster Collections, Deck building, and exploration of different islands.

The tutorial establishes that you're an Alchemist-in-training, and you leave home to complete your studies. Father shows you how to harvest and plant crops, and I discover the first issue: the number of clicks to switch between tools. Hold Q, bring up a wheel menu, but the mouse wheel doesn't map to the Tool wheel. The mouse wheel is tied to the Inventory, which is a different panel from the tools. Mother gave me a broom, a bunch of seeds, and then father gives me several more tools.
The tutorial ends with me literally crashing in the woods on a randomly generated island, destroying the broom and emptying my inventory and telling me "I should go look for all the stuff I lost. I instead found a treasure map, moon stone, and pass out on the steps on a cliff.

The gameplay experience did not significantly improve for me, and I just don't think this game is for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game so far.... but it has it's flaws like what others say

9/25/23
1. Time dilation - Please make a slider in the settings menu or at least make it somewhat slower, a minority of people may like the challenge of making it faster, but I really don't have much time to do many things in one day, it goes by way too fast. Seriously, watering crops takes like 2 hours if you have a good amount, walking around the island and talking to others may take like 5 hours, resource gathering may take like 2-3 hours, then shops closes at 5 PM (I think they need to change it to like 7 or 8 at least to be honest) and you might miss buying stuff..... I didn't even explore other islands yet and it's already almost 6 PM LOL

2. Clicking and hovering detection - Ugh, this is really a pain too.... You have to spam right click to talk to NPCs and even picking up items, watering crops is annoying as hell as you might misclick and rewater the same patch while wasting stamina at the same time and hovering over the patch is very sensitive too.

3. More dialogue in relationships - The dialogue in here has very little depth, there's a little dialogue when you start talking to them, but you only have 3 RNG options that say nothing else (also, I never chose the 3rd option yet with 10% chance).... Dates (in the beginning at least, haven't played more in depth for dates yet) also have very little dialogue and they're very short dates too. I got used to the RNG options, but there's a couple times where you lose all 3 times which is annoying, but you do get skills later to increase your chances and increased rates with more hearts.

4. Sauna - This is probably just a minority maybe, not sure, but I would change the amount of stamina you gain per second in real time, otherwise it's not worth it, especially considering the bad time dilation you get wasted. I might as well continue using Stonefruits in the beginning instead of rather using the Sauna.

5. Money and Upgrades - Yes, both of them....it's very difficult to gain any money in the beginning at least so far, I'm considering just doing quests and catching spirits to turn them in the research center as both things gain quite a good amount. Selling items is very low gain so far, as I only gained like small amounts everyday. Upgrades are VERY expensive and there are many of them, so that ties in with the low gain you get.

6. Furnace - Probably another minority, but please make it so I can leave the ores and coals in the furnace and let it run the whole day (you can limit it to 15 ores and 5 coals or something), because I can only put in 3 ores and 1 coal, making only ONE ingot within 2 hours, and I am not even waiting in the same location, I have to go back to the furnace to make more while wasting time walking back and forth there.
Edit: It's not too bad if you make more furnaces, but it is still a little annoying to only put in 3 ores and 1 coal.

7. Character Creation - Not much said about here, you just start off with the default character without customizing anything.

8. Possible bug - Be careful when you use the balloon in the dungeons, because you can't change directions, when you collide with the walls, you are stuck there unless there are entrances to other rooms. And the usual "fall" button doesn't appear, so you're stuck until you run out of stamina or run out of time.

I consider the first 2 issues as the top priority to be fixed.
I'll continue editing this review in the future as updates are being made, if there are more issues, I will list the dates that I had it on.
I think these are my only gripes so far, I enjoyed the game so far and hopefully with future updates, they fix these major problems that me and others have.\t\t\t\t\t
