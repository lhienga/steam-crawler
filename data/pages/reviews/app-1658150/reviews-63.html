\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI LOVE this game. I have been following the progress and development for a while before it was released so I knew what I was getting myself into when I finally purchased and played it. I have played other farming sims for a while so let's get into pros and cons:

1. Fishing. Absolutely love the fishing mini game. Normally, whenever I start a new game that has fishing, the first thing I do is download a mod that skips it, however, Moonstone Island does fishing in a way that is not too frustrating so I never felt the need.
2. Farming is not very time consuming or hard to manage. I have seen people complain that there's not enough farming in the game or that the sell price for crops is not enough however, a lot of people failed to realize that farming in MI has a completely different purpose than in Stardew Valley and the like. Here we don't farm for profit, we farm for progress (with the spirits and potions).
3. I love the length of the day. It's not too short that you feel time pressured at all times but not too long that you get bored. I've seen complaints that you can't do anything if you go mining or spirit hunting during a single day but... that's the point of the game. You have to manage your time to do them either on separate days or be very mindful of the clock to get to everything.
4. I LOVE the perk system, it offers such good actual perks and you can feel the difference of each one while playing (Bless whoever invented the 'missed dates don't reduce friendship' perk because I always pass out in the dungeons)
5. 20/10 soundtrack, I sometimes dissociate just listening to the soundtrack while cutting wood.

Cons:
1. Prompts about lack of stamina/time close to 2am should be somewhat more noticeable. I almost never track my stamina well enough and have spent more nights in Waldo's bed than in my own.I have also missed multiple dates (sorry Ferra) because of this whoops.
2. Given that it's a very fresh game and not a lot of information can be found online, stuff about what spirit is strong vs what and weak vs what should be more better displayed or explained at one point. Same goes for the different types of buildings and such.
3. The dialogue with the villagers feels a bit bare-bones. I really dislike the fact that our only options are "chat/joke/flirt" with no actual words being spoken.
4. As many others have said, I dislike the lack of character customization. Granted, the character is adorable, but at least let me name them </3. I understand it was a choice of customization vs animation for launch and they chose the former, and are working on adding customization in later patches but it's a minus rn. It's one of the things that makes me wonder if there'll be any mods that can add that beforehand.

*To note, I have never experienced any of the bugs people have been complaining about.

All in all, for the purpose of the game, I feel like the dev team has done an amazing job. A lot of negative reviews come from the fact that people are comparing the game so much to the games it borrows elements from which is a pity.

Subjectively, the game for me is a 9/10, mostly because I know there's a lot of things the devs are still working on. I can't wait to see what they have in store for us.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo let me start by saying, if you're going into this hoping to dig into the "Creature Collecting" aspect, you might be a little disappointed. However for me, it turned out to be a positive, and here's why. This game makes you work to even start building up you collecting of creatures. You must build a barn, and then stables, which is indeed time consuming, heck, you pretty much stuck with a the first three creatures you catch for the first few hours. However, for me it felt extremely fun to build up you home base and get the space for more creatures, but thats exactly it. If you just want some straight up creature collector, this might not be the game for you. Though the combat is fun, even if it currently feels a bit easy. And the fact that creatures get the chance to learn three random abilities (some only that creature can learn) may be a plus or bonus to some people.

Meanwhile the game feels pretty polished. The only issues ive had is a gripe about the kinda weird stuttery movement of your character, and my biggest complaint being what i think is an annoying audio bug. When ever i enter a building and come back out, the music just goes off, and sometimes could stay off for multiple in game days. Then sometimes it just stops for no reason, which sucks because this games soundtrack is pretty decent.

All in all, get this game if you like games like Ooblets or slime rancher. You'll be putting more time into your base(s) than probably the creature collecting.\t\t\t\t\t

This game could be great, but right now? It isn't.

Let me preface this by saying the game is fine in it's current state, not unplayable. However, the gameplay loop is buggy and still very shallow.

First the gameplay:
- First, and the WORST part of the game at this time, there are little to NO batch actions, at least not when playing with controller. Want to stock your furnace with 40 coal? Have fun clicking 40 times individually to do so. Planted 200 crops that died next season? You have to scythe EACH crop individually to get rid of the dead weeds. I spend an entire DAY and a ton of stamina food just to prepare my fields at the end of each season. The scythe upgrade did not add any radius to clearing weeds either, still individually clearing each weed on each tile. Also, I dreaded needing to buy a lot of items from the general store for the same reason, just clicking hundreds of times was needlessly tedious. Some options like selling to the crate DO have an option to choose how many items you'd like to select, so this being absent in places like the shop make 0 sense.
- At this time, there is little to no point to deepening your relationship with villagers. Their dialogue doesn't even really change as you get closer with them, I didn't see a single cut scene, they don't really have much personality, and there don't seem to be any benefits from befriending them at all.
- While I appreciate the actual combat mechanics and randomly generated islands, I didn't find the spirits to be very well balanced and found the level scaling on islands a bit odd. Leveling up in this game is also very tedious. It isn't clear if I'm supposed to just continually catch higher-level monsters as I move outwards or grind excessively to keep the spirits that I spent a lot of time refining the decks of.
- Minor gripe, but you cant grow anything outside in the winter and the maximum greenhouse size is 64 tiles with sprinklers, which feels super small.

Second, the bugs:
- First, after getting the flippers key item, I swam in the pond near the bridge in town and ended up getting trapped in between the bridge and the waterfall, unable to move or even use the flute item to try and get myself unstuck.
- Second, I encountered another situation where, after falling off a cliff in a dungeon, I kept eternally falling until I passed out from energy loss.
- Finally and most egregiously, during the entirety winter I got trapped on islands surrounded by water MULTIPLE times. I couldn't fly off the island from land because the game wanted me to attach flippers (which you can't use in ice), and on multiple islands I couldn't walk on the ice either, meaning I either had to use the flute if I hadn't already for the day or just wait to pass out if I had.

I see potential in the game, but in its current state it's missing so many modern QOL mechanics and has so many bugs that it really doesn't stand out from the farming sim/monster raising/etc genre at all, and just provides a shallow gaming experience at this time.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs stated by many other comments, this game is very ambitious. Too ambitious actually so in a lot of places it feels not polished enough.
The characters need more depth. The friendship system also needs a little adjustment. It feels like it will take forever to befriend a NPC. We also needs more characters too.
The story needs more weight. After the final battle, I was like, that was it? I saved the world but nothing else. I didn't even know why I had to save the world. I have no idea what those lost memory (spoiler alert) even were after I recollected them for the magic man.
The randomly generated 100 islands are great but feels tedious to explore towards the end because after you beat the dungeons and got those specific plants/spirits you need there is no more reasons to explore other than searching for moonstones.
But don't get me wrong, I adore this game. The graphic itself makes it worth playing. I just feel like if they develop and polish the game for a bit longer it could have been one of the greatest game in this genre.\t\t\t\t\t
