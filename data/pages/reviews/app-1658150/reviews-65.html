\r\n\t\t\t\t\tat 36 hours of play time and having completed my first in-game year, Moonstone Island is a fun monster collection game with a creative deckbuilding system for conducting battles, and an engaging focus on exploration.

The farming system exists mostly as a means to an end, you *can* use it to earn money, not that I found myself needing it for that, but it can also be used to grow crops to use for buffing your spirits, taming wild spirits, and restoring your own stamina, so it's worth going into.

The system of befriending/romancing NPCs is there but it's...just kind of there, as far as I can tell there's no final payoff of getting married to the NPCs or anything.

The system of cards and energy for combat is reminiscent of games like Slay the Spire, with the twist that you're gradually leveling up your monsters to improve their base stats and obtain new cards for them to use, with items you can find to upgrade and remove cards from their deck as well. It's not that hard to break this system wide open once you get access to certain cards, but the process of breaking the system is enjoyable in itself, and the big highlight boss fights present twists to the system that certainly keep you on your toes.

Basically: if you're looking for an exploration focused monster collection RPG, I'd happily recommend Moonstone Island. If you're looking at this because you like the farming and romance aspects of Stardew Valley and the like, I'm not sure you'll find what you're looking for here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthe games good for what it is but there's definitely a LOT of room for growth. granted, i've only played for 2.5 hours at the time of writing this

the UI is very annoying to use (especially on controller) and other UIs look not too great (if i remember correctly, the decoration table or whatever is just like a 20x30 grid of tiny sprites with absolutely no sorting or organization).

using furnaces is a complete pain; having to manually place in 3 ore at a time rather than just put it all in at once and let it do the work automatically is very annoying. stardew does it correctly, if you're going to make it so you can only put in one batch at once, don't have a UI to begin with and have it just be a one click interaction instead. if you're going to include a UI, do it like minecraft does it and let it all be placed in at once. navigating through an entire UI (with clunky controls) to simply smelt one single batch is not even a little fun.

placing objects down and correctly aiming tools are more finnicky than they should be. maybe a skill issue, maybe a controller issue, no idea.

the lack of character customization is bizarre in a game that's so inclusive of diverse identities and races. this game is beating the "token black character" allegations so hard that it's looped around to having a token white character.

i'd appreciate if they added the ability to create texture packs, some of the characters are not the most visually appealing (do i even need to say who (fine, it's quill))

the "starters" are kind of mid. the pokemon formula works; one cute, one cool, and one funny, all being intuitively strong against each other in one way and weak against each other in the other way. i'm not sure this game has an element system like that or not (i haven't gotten that deep into the combat system yet) but these starters do not look to be strong/weak against eachother. they also don't match the cute/cool/funny theme or seem to have anything like it; it just looks like they picked out random spirits that were already in the game and called em starters. anyways, it's also a bit annoying that there's no immediately available "PC" system where your spare spirits go once your party is full. i just had to release them. that's no fun.

i appreciate the existence of a system that warns you that you're low on energy, but why does it have to be so intrusive and take so long? i have 50 energy and 3 swings left on this rock and i'm done. i don't need to sit there for 3 seconds catching my breath, i'll be fine. just give me a little ping in the corner and maybe play a sound. don't interrupt me.

it'd be nice if there was some way to understand the scale of friendship meters. what's the point of having little numbers pop up after you successfully interact with someone if you have no idea what scale those numbers are on? do i need 100 points for a heart? 500? 1000? who knows? maybe i'm just missing something.

i'll recommend it just because i'm sure a lot of these issues will be ironed out later. surely.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, I am absolutely loving this game. As someone who has several hundred hours into Stardew, I was really hoping for something new/fresh that scratched the same itch and this has been it. The blend of a deck builder, a farm sim, and adventure game has been so much fun. I honestly cannot recommend it enough!

With that said, I did want to share the few problems I have with the game. First and foremost… I REALLY wish I could edit my character (model + outfits). I’m okay without it, but that would take it to the next level. Everything beyond that is very minor. I would love more NPCs, more decoration items, more transportation options (like a teleporter), more dialogue responses from NPCs, and maybe even the ability to terraform a bit.

All that said, I love this game. This team has done phenomenal and I’m so happy to finally have this game in my hands (literally! On my steam deck). Thanks to all of you that worked on it. You crushed it 😊\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those games where they want to do a mix and match of many mechanics. Monster collecting, farming, etc. Would like to try it out and enjoy it....

If I could see anything. The text is super small and white. Doesn't pop out against the bright color backgrounds. Pop ups for quest appear in an incredibly small box in the bottom right corner, not the center of the screen. Unlike Stardew Valley, or any other game, the hotbar is to the side and small, not center screen. Things aren't outlined and don't pop making it easy to see. And then combat.... why.... why on earth are the spirits and numbers so small. Health bar is like 3 inches long and at the top of the screen. You could fit 1000 copies of the spirit on the screen. Instead of it being big and center like in StS or any other card battler.

And no, there is nothing in the Settings of the game to make anything bigger.

The game does not use its screen real estate at all. And in a game, with visuals and reading, being able to easily read and understand things is important.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a little bit stoned but I am going to tell you exactly what I like about this game and you are going to listen to me if you want to.

This game really has a lot of potential for VERY unique experiences each playthrough. Since there are 100 islands and you can set up your home wherever you want and decorate the space both indoors and outdoors, you could live a pretty different daily routine on a distant island dungeoneering than someone who lives very close to town on moonstone island to farm or be social. You could even build houses on every single island, if you wanted to and you could get enough moonstones. It feels like the whole world could be your home. Home has a lot of impact on the experience for me.

Also, the characters work for me. I love Stardew Valley, but only for the gameplay. The characters in Moonstone are actually unique and interesting to me. Living on the beach with Tobin and fishing together and going on dates with him has made me feel like doki doki for the first time in a game with romance in a very very long time. Maybe it's because I'm high idk. He's just so cheerful and his smile is so bright. Coming home after a long several days or so of exploring and just chilling out on the beach with my pretty fisherman wife and his radiant smile makes my soul feel like it's healing.

The spirits are adorable and I'm pogging all the time at the cute designs. The deck-based battles are interesting and fun, even if the system is not as deep as deck building fans want it to be, but also not what creature battling fans want it to be. In fact, it seems to be a major theme in negative reviews that the game is trying to bring too many disparate elements together and not doing any of them particularly well, but I think that's a pretty negative way of looking at it. I have ADHD, and games where there are lots and lots of things you CAN do but don't HAVE to do are my absolute jam. Fantasy Life for the 3DS comes to mind similarly.

This game is great. It's the closest thing I can get to living my actual fantasy. I played it for 13 hours straight yesterday. My greatest hope for this game is that it will continue to be developed for a very, very long time. If it had the support that SDV has, it could be something game-changing. I hope it becomes that. And I also hope it gets multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: 9 cute little spirits/10. Manages to take games like Stardew and Pokemon and not only fuse them together, but in some ways surpass them. Easily worth the price.

I remember like, a year or two ago, I was telling my partner how much I wished there was a Stardew-style game where instead of just having random barn animals, you had Pokemon-style creatures you could collect and who served various farm functions. In my head, it had the flavor of Howl's Moving Castle and a warm, cozy vibe where you co-existed with these monsters in the way that Pokemon always claims its universe inhabitants do, but rarely ever shows. I only mention this because it's SO bizarre that the exact dream of a game that I wanted is THIS game. Sub out Howl's Moving Castle for a charming pixel-art version of something closer to Kiki's Delivery Service, and you have the exact game I had always dreamed of playing but despaired anyone ever making.

This game is FANTASTIC. I'm surprised I haven't heard any hype about it. It's such a fun, clever blend of Pokemon/creature-collector style games and farming sims like Stardew Valley, but honestly, I think it improves upon both genres. You don't have to spend an eternity like in most farming sims grinding to automation, stuck toiling away on your farm waiting spending whatever energy you have left to hopefully level up your gear so you can eventually make coveted sprinklers. Instead, all you can farm is also forage-able, meaning you can turn a few into seeds to keep that engine started, but you can spend most of your day pursuing your other passions: talking to a cast of delightful villagers, mining in dungeons for resources, battling your spirits in fun card-based combat, fishing, or sailing the expanses of the sky with your balloon/broom discovering strange new biomes in neighboring floating islands. There's skill trees that feel thought-out and impactful, with every node that I've seen seeming like a valid option depending on how you want to play.

I was worried the spirit battling would feel clunky, or that the pet designs would be uninteresting and uninspired, like the majority of attempts to translate Pokemon end up being. But this is honestly one of the coolest pantheons of monsters I've seen in a game like this. You can tell there's genuine love towards the original titan of the genre, with spirits like the cute fire/earth fox Fluffox or the hilarious pseudo-wood-o long log simply titled Longlog. But there's also fun spirits that are more original, like the totally rad Coolslime, which is just a cool looking slime in a backwards baseball cap but MAN does he make me laugh every time I fight him. Combat is a deck-builder, where leveling up your pets alters things like power and health, but also drafts new cards into your team deck, allowing you to create really cool combinations between your three spirit teammates. It's easy to pick up but I can already see the potential for great depth in higher level combat.

The story itself is fairly bare-bones, but not in a bad way, with the premise being that you are a young Alchemist apprentice who's leaving their home to do their mandatory year abroad to become a full-fledged Alchemist. The vibes are VERY Kiki's Delivery Service, both in terms of premise and in terms of overall ambiance. The music has that Miyazaki wistfulness in 8-bit, and the aesthetic matches it to a T. You can't create your own character, which initially I was worried about, but honestly I think the game works better with a set appearance (you even get to see your character in beautiful hand-drawn loading screens), as the designers were able to really tighten up the aesthetic choices for maximum impact. You do still have say in customizing your world, between choosing what characters you elect to talk to, certain dialogue choices, and a fully-customizable home. The economy is also balanced enough that, unlike Stardew or Sun Valley or other games in the genre, I don't feel overwhelmingly pressured to save every scrap of money I make for farming equipment. I already have a nice little cozy spirit-communing nook/library in my home that I created! You can also decorate the world around you, and even move your home to settle on different islands!

Long-winded, but I wanted to really spend time writing this review because I haven't seen like, any advertising for it, and this game is an absolute GEM. If you're thinking of trying it out, take the chance like I did. It's not that expensive (cup of coffee to a dinner depending on when you buy it), and it's absolutely a game you can lose yourself in if you like any of the genres it flirts with. The developers should be VERY proud of this game, it exceeded my own expectations and quite frankly is a phenomenal entry compared to other games in these genres.\t\t\t\t\t
