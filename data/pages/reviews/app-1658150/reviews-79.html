\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend looking at this game more as a game about collecting monsters, building your deck, exploring, farming, crafting, and finding resources.

The social aspect of the game at this point is a bit bare-bones, there is little to do after you get maximum hearts with someone. Sure you can go on dates to their house and get unique dialogue but besides that there isn't mush to do in terms of interacting with the characters at later stages. I wish different villagers could help you with different tasks, like Gaiana helping you farm or Ferra collecting and mining ores but there is no reward or progression earned when you choose a romantic partner.

Besides that I really enjoy the game, there are lots of dungeons to explore, and creatures to catch, which makes exploring the wider world quite fun. After you explore you use the resources you got to farm different plants for different buffs, feed your spirits, and manage their combat decks/moves. The combat system is "Slay the Spire" style deck builder with each card using energy and only getting a certain amount of energy per turn, and can be surprisingly difficult when you are exploring new areas. The better you get and/or your spirits get, the more areas you can explore, completing the core gameplay loop.

In Short:
Explore for resources and spirits -> Farm/Craft items to aid in battle or exploration -> Manage your spirits (Your deck) -> Fight monsters -> Explore more areas

I wish the social aspect of the game was more interesting and more rewarding, other than that I find fighting, farming, and exploring really fun and addicting. Good game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMoonstone Island is a baby of Pok√©mon, Spirited Away, Stardew Valley, and Across the Obelisk. Awesome game.

Note: I will add more to this review as I didn't have the opportunity to play the game longer.

What I love about this game:
- Pixel Arts *chef's kiss*
- Building relationships with townies
- Flying with a broom
- Exploration
- Rent System if you don't have your own place
- Choice of spirits
- Environment & Buildings *chef's kiss*

Concern:

- Xbox controller inputs, this is better with a keyboard and a mouse

I prefer playing a single player game with my xbox controller. It didn't feel really smooth on Moonstone Island. The button assignments in the game weren't as intuitive as I expected, which led to some frustration. I constantly had to think about which buttons to press, making it difficult to perform actions smoothly. While I might eventually get the hang of it with more playtime, it becomes a problem if I decide to take a break and come back later. I would have to retrain my muscle memory all over again. This inconsistency in controls adds to the pain points of the user experience, so it would be beneficial if the developers could address this issue promptly.\t\t\t\t\t
