\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game but it feels like the devs took everything besides the combat and exploration from Stardew Valley and then they forgot at the last minute that they actually have to make it their own game.

1) Farming feels like a chore and a hassle because you have to be within one tile of the plot to plant a seed/water/hoe and because the tiles are so small, it feels like a constant battle of being on top of the tile or too far away from the tile.

2) The key binding options are far too limited for the amount of popups in this game. The controls feel clunky and overwhelming because sometimes there is 3 different buttons that do the same thing and you cant rebind half of them to your play style.

3) This goes hand in hand with the last one. They basically tried to copy Stardew Valley and yet somehow managed to make the functionality so. much. worse. For example, when you put stuff in the sell bin, you cannot add items by the stack automatically. With every item you need to choose the amount and confirm. This sounds silly but when you're trying to sell large stacks of multiple times, it can become an absolute chore.

4) The battle game play feels a little unbalanced. I feel like I can have an equal amount of card type and yet every battle my first hand is all attack cards and then I wont get a single skill card to remove the opponents armor until my 3rd or 4th and now suddenly I have all skill cards and no attack cards. This would be fine every once and awhile but it's beginning to feel like every single battle. This is especially annoying when it's 3 higher level spirits you're battling at once because now, not only have I lost but I've now wasted a whole day.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally cute game that tries to do too much. Instead of trying to jam in every game mechanic under the sun, they should have concentrated on a few to make a much better game overall.

+Really fun exploring different islands.
+Pretty graphics

-Passing out after using all stamina. Anti-fun mechanic that games like My Time at Portia removed. Instead, the game would just stop you from using the ability.
-Passing out when your team gets defeated. It's very easy to land on an island where the spirits would just wreck your team before you can even go, which brings me to my next negative;
-Flee being a percent chance. Games like Chained Echoes realized not being able to flee is a stupid anti-fun mechanic so they let you flee. Yes, there are cards but if you don't get it then you're SOL because even something 10+ levels below you will still do a lot of damage.
-Taming is a pain in the ass. Not being able to tame while they're stunned is very stupid, that's literally the strategy in every catch game (to weaken the monster while you spend turns trying to capture them).
-Not being able to heal your spirits outside of combat. There may be a way, but in the time I played I haven't found anything to let me do it so you'll have to get used to only doing a couple fights a day. This game would benefit greatly if your spirits just healed up after every fight, but I'd take just being able to use a healing item on them from the menu. Would make trying to tame spirits a lot less painful.
-Yet another social gift giving game. WHY?!

There are better farming/crafting sim games. There are better exploration games. There are better monster collecting games. There are better card based battle games.\t\t\t\t\t
