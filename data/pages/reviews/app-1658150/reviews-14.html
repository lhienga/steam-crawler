\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give a sideways thumb? I LOVE this game, but that's what makes it hurt so much. Idk, I'm still 70+ hours in, so obviously there's something great here. It's just not all there yet. It feels like version 0.8 or 0.9, not version 1.X, you know? It feels almost fully cooked, but taken out about 5-10 minutes too early. The story has such rich ideas, but they aren't fully baked. The islands are so fun to explore, but their individuality is half-baked. Collecting spirits is a delight, yet their existence before, during, and after capture feels half-baked. The plants are cute, but feel half-baked in that the early ones are effectively useless come Winter. Resource gathering can be tedious AND anxiety-inducing (trees give little at times and DON'T regrow naturally). The card game is fun, but it feels... half-baked, as all monsters of the same type have access to the same cards. The villagers are a delight, and yet feel REALLY half-baked.
To end my repetition, no one element in this highly diverse game feels "complete". All of them leave me feeling either wanting or irritated. It doesn't feel worth $20 in its current state. Or it WOULD, as an early access, which would come with a promise of fixes for its many holes, bugs, and emotional blueballs.
If the devs are listening (reading), it's the NPCs and the storyline that hurt me the most. They're SO GOOD, but they're not whole! There's SO much more to explore in the lore of this world, the development of Moonstone Island, and each individual character. There's also a lot of community building that could be done, too, like mini-events, heart event outside of dating, and community festivals. Something to make me feel like I'm not the only one living in these 100+ islands.

*Potential SPOILERS Ahead!*


And WALDO!!! I can tell you love Waldo! I love Waldo too!!! But he feels done so dirty, coming back to town a broken, wounded hero, only for NO ONE to acknowledge his presence, and for him to perpetually remain isolated in his home (that's in the square??). He doesn't even get to adventure with me, Chicken House, and Fishbo's Jr. and Sr. anymore. He's just... your prince in an ivory tower, living in isolation and worshiping you as his rescuer. It doesn't feel good, considering all he's gone through and how complex his character is. Every character feels underdeveloped, but him being half-developed without resolution is most jarring.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has so much potential. All the basics are here, but it desperately needs to be tinkered with.

1. User Interface is poor. I often missed with my tools unless I was positioned perfectly. I could be standing right over moonstones and still have to click 4 or 5 times to pick one up. It was annoying to have to open the tool interface to swap between tools.

2. Tools tools tools. Too many tools, some like the boomerang were used ONCE and then never again. The upgrades are easy to get, but completely unsatisfying to use. I leveled up to moonstone (highest) level quite quickly but other than speeding up my use of the tools it wasn't particularly useful (since stamina is SO easy to acquire and plentiful).

3. Money and Crops. Spirit barns and resource collection are effortless, I never needed to grow crops to sell for profit or really struggled to make profit at any point in this game. Since gold was so easy to acquire and there wasn't much it was needed for it makes a significant portion of game mechanics (growing/harvesting/selling) completely meaningless. The ability to create a sizable amount of sprinklers during the first week of play took away a lot of the "grind" that makes some of these games so appealing and satisfying.

4. Spirits. I LOVE the battles, but... there's not much to them. I leveled up one of my spirits 37 levels in one "day" by planting about 150 of a single XP crop that regrew every 4 days. I then just cycled through the rest of the month to get to the temple boss battles so I could finish the game. A boss I beat in 2 turns because my psychic spirit had cards to gain energy and keep pulling cards to make a never ending unbeatable hand. It felt like cheating.

5. Speaking of seasons, what's the point of them aside from dragging out the gameplay? There's no story, there's no festivals, there's no special events, there's nothing except a new batch of crops with new stats to collect. With only 11 NPCs you don't really need to talk with anyone, not that there's much for them to say either.

All in all, so much potential! So little to do. So many things that need to be tweaked to elevate the experience. I feel like it's incomplete as is. I played it to completion on the main storyline (didn't take the 130 hours it says I did lol, I just tend to keep games minimized in the background when not actively playing). While I would say I somewhat enjoyed it, I would not recommend it to my friends, at least not in the current state.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh Moonstone Island, my beloved!

Aesthetics—
Visuals: What an adorably cute game! Very pretty pixel art with astonishingly gorgeous animation for a small dev team. It has a lovely, calming palette and the designs for the NPCs fit their personalities. The creature collecting component was difficult mostly because it was hard to decide between all the adorable spirits. Also, I’m obsessed with the decoration component and my house is going to be the cutest. 10/10
Music: Truthfully I played this mostly at work muted, but I assume the music was good from what snippets I heard. Giving it 7/10 accordingly.

Gameplay—
Mechanics: This game is very unique in that it is doing a LOT but actually pulls everything off. There is creature collection, deck building, farming, exploration, dungeon crawling, and life sim components. Consequently however, none of these are exceptionally in depth, but I find that it makes the game less overwhelming personally. A point off simply due to some glaring issues, such as trees and grass not naturally respawning (though you can’t plant new ones. I’d prefer if they respawned between seasons). Even so, there are plenty of redeeming QOL features and a very helpful wiki. 9/10
Value: The campaign itself is relatively short compared to some games within the same genre at 20-30 hours, but this is a sandbox game where you get what you want out of it. In other words, it’s about $1/hour for the campaign and significantly better in value if you’re like me and are obsessed with completionism. I got it on sale so it was an absolute steal. 8/10
Replayablity: This felt like a young game to me which will benefit from updates and an eventual modding community. I’m excited to pick it up again in the future once I finish with this play through, which may be a bit! 8/10
(BONUS) Steamdeck compatibility: Plenty of games claim to be Steamdeck compatible and aren’t. This game is absolutely compatible and the layout is very clever.

Overall score— 42/50, B+. Plenty of growth potential with future updates.\t\t\t\t\t
