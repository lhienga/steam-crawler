\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMoonstone Island, a cozy game where you are an alchemist who leaves home to embark on your own adventure -- battling and taming creatures known as spirits and doing some farming as well. It's cute yet can be very grindy and it's actually a bit of a challenge to befriend NPCs unlike most cozy games.

Graphic designs are similiar to Stardew yet the devs made it their own as well. The soundtrack is great and very rarely is the game uncomfortably quiet when exploring different islands. The only issues I have experienced while playing this so far is one crash after defeating a boss and having difficulties clicking on a item (you have to be at a specific distance otherwise it won't give you the option to select said object or it selects the object next to it).

Beware the battle style however since I've heard from others who've played this they didn't realize it was turn based combat -- similar to Pokemon but with cards to choose from and not a hack and slash style. I, myself enjoy the fact that it's different but it may not be everyone's cup of tea.

Overall, it's a very cute and cozy game that works very well on both desktop and steamdeck. I also suggest getting the DLCs for the added objects if you like decorating in these types of games (really good pricing too).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to give this game a thumbs up, but there are several things about it that are subpar. First, the trees/rocks/grass do not respawn in this game. I never would have known this without looking at the Steam forum for the game. Granted, the developers did add a 'fix' where the player can purchase seeds/rocks to replant trees/rocks/grass... Finally, the story is very minor part of the game. The game is visually appealing, but there isn't much actual story available. This game has potential to be good, but it is just okay.\t\t\t\t\t

There is so much to do, so many islands to discover and explore, and so many cute creatures to collect. The art style is cute as could be and it's a great way to unwind after work with decorating, farming, or flying around the map.

The card game battle mechanic threw me at first, but I quickly found it to be a charming and fun twist on traditional turn based combat. The game has a few rough edges, but the developers seem to be adding consistent new content and updates to game play. It's for sure a game I can see myself putting more than 100 hours in and if you like creature collecting even a bit, this is a fantastic pick.\t\t\t\t\t
