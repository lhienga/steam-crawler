\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Fist off lets give a shout out to Studio Supersoft and Raw Fury for this delightful Gem!
I haven't written a review in a long time not since Little Wood I believe. but this game definitely deserves mentioning.

First off it's only $20 should be $60 (it's that good).

Take Zelda, Pokemon, Farming with new seeds you discover on islands that change to new ones during the 4 seasons, Crafting, Turn Basted Card Battles, Skill trees, Open world games, exploring and new island loads every new game like Minecraft, and adorable art, music, and graphics and you've got Moonstone Island. It takes the best parts of everything you love in a game and rolls it into this one.

I've found myself so engaged that I'm missing out on sleep. there's so much to discover, do, and never a dull moment. There's quests, discovering new islands, plants and seeds, Spirits to collect and discover (Pokemon), finding Caves from smashing large rocks with treasure chests that give new recipes and discover new potions to brew in your cauldron. Spirits to battle to collect or level your own Spirits in your team. There's a Spirit Barn where you keep all your Spirits. You can switch them out at anytime. You can feed found items or plants you harvested. To boost your Spirits stats or tame/level them up like Pokemon berries. There's also a really sweet new twist to Spirit battles instead of attacks you have cards to battle with and it's random so it spices things up and gives you more strategic thought into your battling. there's quirky villagers to meet and you can romance and even date too. You can decorate your really cool Alchemy Tent and you can make more tents and place them anywhere on the islands. Every tent will have all that you created and your stations in it so you don't have to make them over and over for each island you wish to stay at the moment. You only have to make the tent and place it where you want. How awesome is that talk about a time saver. There's also fishing too! Get lucky and fish a treasure chest! collect different fish from different island Biomes Fire, Earth, Water, Poison, Electric, and Physic. That's what I got so far from only playing 20 hours and there's still more to do like the Main Quest, Side quests (collecting a spirit for every villager) Discovering all the different Biomes and collect resources from them and deliver them to the Researcher Zed to name a few, and just random quests you get in the mail.

Starter Suggestions to help you:

When you find a whistle on the island try to place your tent and Spirit Barn as close to that. So you can use this whistle to recall to town once a day. Where you can feed you Spirits, collect resources, check on your crops, and talk to the towns people to boost friendliness, collect daily freebies, turn in quests, or go on dates. In my world it's East of Paolo the Carpenter in town. My mistake I liked the beach and fishing. So I placed my tent there first and had to hoof it into town or to my crops the first season of Spring wasting a lot of time.

-Dungeons: They are just RNG generated boring places and to make matters worse they share the same boring mini-boss. Each and every dungeon feels kinda same except the ones where you only fight buffed up enemies but even those dungeons have multiple copies.
