\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMoonstone Island is a very cute game with many wonderful mechanics that I loved mashed together into a perfect game. The world feels large and there is a strong desire to explore the vast landscapes. But don't think that is the only thing to explore, as you can delve into dungeons and even the Infinity Dungeon! A fun challenge to see how deep you can dive.

Beyond exploring, there are many wonderful NPCs that you will meet in your life. From the eccentric Ossono to the flirty Ferra, from the nerdy Zed, to a mysterous magic npc, and lastly your loving mother and father whom you say goodbye to in the beginning.

Speaking of the start, the nostalgia of a story where you leave home to pursue a new life, a quality from one of my favorite movies. Though they aren't connected, it does hit that part of my mind and gives a wonderful sense of familiarity in an unfamiliar world. Oddly, it makes me feel at home in this strange new world as I find myself and what I want to do.

There is a lot to do as well. Mining, crafting, farming, card collecting, brewing, fighting, capturing new friends, romancing NPCs, and the game feels wide with possibilities.

I've watched quite a bit of the game, playing some when I can. So this review is not only based on my playtime but also from what I've seen from online content as well as what I've seen from Twitter and the developers own page. I couldn't recommend this game enough as it's in itself a comfort game and an impressive title.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9.5/10: Highly Recommend!

One of the best things about the game is that as a player, I have a lot of control on how I play it.

Unlike other monster taming or farm sim games, there is not a linear story playthrough. Islands are generated uniquely first time you create the save file, and you can:
- explore new islands
- decorate islands... ALL the islands.
- socialise and romance (Hi Ferra!)
- add and upgrade cards, and trim down decks
- find holo (shiny) spirits
- hatch spirit eggs and collect resources in the spirit barn
- fish (it's fun, I promise)
- battle dungeon bosses
- complete quests
- find seasonal plants and plant them ANYWHERE, but mostly, your greenhouse
- create potions from plants

You don't have to do all that. I like that socialising never felt overwhelming or necessary.
You can also create strong potions that can help you in battles, making it much easier for those who are not as comfortable.

And above all that, the devs have consistently published free updates, including new spirits, and much-welcomed Quality of Life improvements (like auto-collecting Spirit Resources without clicking).

At face value, the game seems like it does a lot, but it never got to a point where it felt too much!

Thank you Studio Supersoft, your love for the game makes me Superhard. Oop-\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good game if you like farming sims and collecting games. The farming aspect is pretty secondary to the battling and collection aspects, but it's there. You also have the Stardew-style ability to interact with the villagers, though it's kind of limited (but still enjoyable). You can spend a lot of time exploring all the islands and then return home to decorate (or not, if that's not your thing). I like the deck-based battles.

If you like games where you can get into a comfortable groove, with repetition, this one is a good choice. It has a very relaxing and open gameplay style. I wish there were more sidequests than there currently are, because I feel like having those to focus on would give you more to do in the game.

I do want to stress that they're pushing out content updates all the time, and each update delivers a significant improvement. I got the game when it came out, and it's only gotten better and better since then. I've added the DLCs and are pleased with both (though the game would be fine without). I feel like there's a lot of promise here and that I'll keep coming back to this game as they deliver further updates and events. I really do like that it hasn't been a static release.\t\t\t\t\t

I found myself always having something to work towards up to the end. BUT I WILL SAY THIS, despite their being so much to do, nothing goes deep at all. its just a really wide and shallow puddle of content. examples such as:

only 3 different ore types
Love/dating aspect is a 1 sentence interaction and ends with nothing
combat has minimal diversity of elemental difference.
the UI is atrocious for navigating. i wish their was some sort of encyclopedia I could look at for the crops AND the monsters. I literally cant see what types of monsters I have on my list unless I had a turn in available at Zed.

i beat the game, it occupied me for many hours but it was just so unfulfilling once it was all said and done. I wont go into spoilers but the abrupt ending of the game was also equally so lackluster.

why cant I fish in the dark zones!? why are their no dark zone fish!?

talent trees were alright, nothing revolutionary there.

good game, but couldnt imagine their would be any incentive to go back and play again or even look for new additions to it at this point.\t\t\t\t\t
