\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonally, this is exactly what I was looking for in a game that has a significant farming sim aspect to it.
﻿
When I tried playing other farming sims, like Stardew Valley, the issue I had was that I didn't feel enough incentive to keep progressing. I like the farming sim aspect, but I needed something other than just farming & romancing the cast in order to keep me going.
﻿
I'm glad the main/side quests & creature-collecting in Moonstone Island have kept me hooked. I'm able to fully enjoy the farming sim aspect while having things to look forward to in the game.
﻿
As for the creature (spirit) battling, I suggest you see this as a deck-builder rather than traditional creature-collector battling, otherwise you might end up with massive deck sizes that are unwieldy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMoonstone Island has been such a delight to play. It's a mix of my favorite features, a little farming (kind of light on the farming which I prefer but still happy to have it there), mining, crafting, dungeon crawling and pokemon-esque style battle and collecting. The characters and spirits are so cute and the world they've created is so beautiful. I feel transported whenever I start playing.

PROS:
--> Mostly everything but I am easy to please. The game play seems to have a lot of substance, quests and things to do. I have enjoyed the 11 hours I've put into it so far and I don't plan on putting it down anytime soon.
--> The names and designs of the spirits bring me such joy. I won't say anything specific but, it's delightful
--> Flying from island to island on a broomstick. You have other options as the game progresses, but I take the broom for the aesthetics whenever I can manage it.

CONS:
--> Small glitches that will no doubt be fixed in upcoming patches. Nothing major or game ruining - mostly small freezes when loading another section of the game.
---> The days are so short to me - but I feel that way about literally every game with a timed day. I wish you could choose from a 20-45 minute day at the beginning of the game. Especially since you can save the game mid-day. This isn't an actual con, but a personal preference.

NO OPINION:
---> Relationship building: haven't progressed far enough or tried hard enough to get any impressions on this aspect. I can tell it probably won't have the depth that Stardew Valley does, but that seems rare to come by. The NPC's are cute and have distinguishable personalities. I've got my eyes on some of them.

OVERALL:
I highly recommend this game - especially for fans of Stardew Valley, Studio Ghibli, Harvest Moon, and Pokemon.\t\t\t\t\t

I've been looking forward to Moonstone for over a year and it immediately fulfilled the gaming 'drought' I've personally experienced within recent years. It beats triple-A titles by a longshot - you can feel the love that's gone into it. You have Stardew-esque farming plus tons of housing/decoration options which makes it feel very customizable. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just beat Moonstone Island in 23 hrs and 59 minutes (according to my save file, not sure why it says I have 28.1 hrs on record...). While I enjoyed the game and would suggest it if you're a fan of the genre, I do have some feedback that I hope the devs consider in any future titles. Keep in mind I fully support indie dev teams and totally understand there are practical limitations involved in game dev without a AAA budget. For the small team that made it, I think the team did great. It's also worth noting I don't recall running into any bugs in my time spent playing, which is highly commendable. It's also obvious, but the visuals are also stylized and come together great (except the pixelated character portraits when talking are a bit odd, I think cartoon portraits may have been a better fit if there were enough resources to make them happen).

Overall rating: 6/10

1. Lack of cohesion in character design - I've seen this feedback floating around, but the character design does not seem cohesive across the different types of media. The character's outfit seems to have slight differences between the into movie and the title screen and appears completely different in the splash art when you fall asleep in the field / lose a battle. The game also apparently has a separate outfit for when the player goes to bed, but you can never wear this while you have control of your character. It does not make sense why this second outfit was even added to the game / had art made for it.

2. Lack of character identity - You are an "alchemist" in the game, but I never once felt compelled to make any potions (or "concoctions"). While the option is there, concoctions only provide an additive sum of the two ingredients used to brew it, so there is no special reason to use them. Combat / training spirits is also fairly easy so you can just get by feeding the ingredients to spirits directly. Making concoctions is also cumbersome. First, making a potion-making table is locked behind rare resources and first even needs another crafting table (which is also locked behind rare resources) to even make it. Then in order to make potions, you need to first make glass bottles which relies on uncommon drops which you can only get if you have water-type spirits in your spirit barn. This incentives the mass hoarding of water spirits, taking up precious space in your spirit barn. And then once you get a potion brewing, each table can only make 1 concoction at a time and it takes a few minutes of waiting to do so. The overall story of the game also starts as you leave home to take part in your alchemist training (spoilers: you eventually "pass" your training), but there is no obvious connection between your daily activities and this broader goal. I can't help but compare this concept to Potion Permit which actually made you feel like you were applying alchemy knowledge to meet specific milestones in your training.

3. A parallel aspect to the story is that you need to close some evil rift by collecting 4 magical tokens. However, this story is never introduced. Instead, you stumble across a single temple and are given the quest to collect the tokens, but the reason why is never fully explained until the very end of the overall quest. And even then, closing the portal is said to have saved the world, but there is essentially no impact on the world to have known it was in danger in the first place. Sure, spooky dark-themed islands might indicate something is wrong, but their representation within the game is almost no different from the fire-themed islands (ex: you take hazard damage over time, enemies on them are highest level in the game).

4. From a user interaction perspective, I found my fingers getting tired from spamming inputs while navigating menus. Need to buy some seeds? Good luck mashing A over a hundred times to buy them individually. Did you just load materials into a furnace for smelting? You literally need to move your cursor over to a start button - why not map it to some keybinding?

5. Mining is critical for early-game progression but discovering mines is locked behind randomly mining large rocks and hoping to find an entrance. I don't see why they couldn't just assign mine entrances and have them be discoverable like any other landmarks.

6. Not being able to track skill level progression was irritating. Yes, we can craft a magic mirror to look at the skill tree, but even then its not clear to me how my actions translate into exp earned. And why even hide the skill tree to begin with?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis game has all the framework to be great, but unfortunately i think they took the stardew valley formula and diluted it.

1) the farming is more for useful items not for crops for gold - so far gold is best gotten via quest but also feels like the farming is more of an afterthought. not necessarily a bad thing but sort of misleading in this genre.

2) the battle system is good, but its a bit grindy for XP. there are 66 collectable spirits but you can only use 3 at a time. they dont feel super different other than element and element plays such a big deal that you cant really just run 1 party. hence i think the XP and exchangeability should be better. i've stuck to one party for a while and its not a good thing, but leveling isnt enjoyable in this game. I think there should be alternative ways to level your party other than banish spirits as the battle system gets repetitive fast.

3) the exploring is great, island hopping is very fun and well designed. unfortunately, the dungeon bonus of extra stamina is really the only good thing. I wish there was another key currency like moonstones that you get from the dungeon to improve another element. Overall i ran a bunch of dungeons and felt like they werent very challenging or beneficial even. just something missing here... doesnt need a complete overhaul.

4) time - game passes much too fast. i feel like its slightly faster than stardew and has more content than stardew so thats not a good thing if the time passes too quickly. i think movement speed should be higher than base right now and maybe slow down time 20-30% to allow for better gameplay loop.

5) end game - doesnt feel like theres much of an end game... that being said i only have 14 hrs in... but im starting to lose the desire to do anything else. and i ran into an island of lvl 60 spirits and thought omg i cant imagine grinding to lvl 60 to fight here.

6) some bugs - small polish things, some quality of life things that could make the gameplay smoother. even how you get through the UI sometimes is a lot of clicks and stuff. healing your spirits or modifying them is a whole thing in itself which it doesnt need to be.

overall its worth 20$ probably but hope they'll refine it and add some more content. in its current state though i wouldnt recommend it, i'd say you are better off sticking to stardew or playing slay the spire... or pokemon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t30 hours in 2 days, woops..

It's a fun little exploration creature capture game. There's romance and friendship, but while the characters have fun personalities, it's not that deep. There's farming, but once again you'll only really do it to spam farm certain crops or when you're low on cash. There's some puzzles in dungeons, a low-story main mission, and you can build lots of useless decoration to make your house interior look pretty. The bosses and their skills range from cliche to pretty interesting, and the creatures you can use have skills to either boost your fighting style or playing style. Which basically means that any team composition is a good composition as long as you like it. If you've played Slay the Spire you know the combat, but while having three decks instead of one can seem fun and diverse in terms of building a team, it really sucks when you have a hand full of cards that can only be used by your one stunned character, meaning you're forced to end turn with no energy spent.

In a nutshell. It's basically the Slay the Spire combat times three, worked into a fun, relaxing, and collection-focused game with exploration and plenty of surface level side-activities added.

Definitely worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm about 7 hours in so far, and I've kinda just been taking things at my own pace, which I think is a great part about this game. There are objectives and little side quests for progression, but you can get so much joy out of just exploring! I love the card based battle system, although I will say that it's VERY easy to overpack your deck with cards. I feel like maybe each spirit should have fewer starting cards, but it's not the biggest deal.

This game has definitely become my go-to comfort game over the last few days. I'm a big fan of the types of games Moonstone Island seems to have pulled inspiration from (Pokemon, Stardew Valley, Slay the Spire) so seeing this game blend those elements together is awesome!

It has some flaws that other people have mentioned. I already talked about deck bloat. So far, the NPC dialogue is pretty generic, but I'm also not very far yet, so that could definitely get better. And I have experienced some stuttering here and there, but not as bad as some people have reported.

Overall, if you love cozy life-sim type games, but you've been looking for something different, this game is for you!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would like to preface this by stating I played this on both PC and SteamDeck but I primarily played on SteamDeck and so that is what my review is based on.

I cannot say I recommend this game for many reasons that I think are based on my personal preference so take this with a grain of salt.

My biggest issue so far has been that when playing on the SteamDeck I have had NO audio. I feel like this has probably taken away a bit of my enjoyment of the game as on PC there is a general atmosphere of cute and cozy audio.

I do not really see why the monster catching is important. So far for me there is no reason to “collect them all” beyond just doing so. It took me forever just to unlock and build a storage for them.

The main story poses that you are staying in this place for a year. I am assuming that you can go beyond that year, but if that is the case why impose a time frame at all? The way the game is going I do not think I would even be able to complete the seasonal tasks let alone whatever it is the main story wants from you (I still have no idea beyond finding the memory of a magic man).

I don’t think it’s impossible to manage both the stamina and the rapidly moving time of day, but in context of the year it really sort of stresses me out because I don’t feel like I have the time. I am halfway through Spring and only just got my broom and barn.

I would not come to this game for the npcs and relationships. There is very little in the way of them. I’m sure you can read how the social system is mostly based on RNG in many other reviews.

I think this game was perhaps an interesting idea and the graphics are pretty good. Weirdly this game reminds me of Garden Story and has me feeling about as mixed as I do about it.

I think if you are fine with a hybrid farm/creature collector game that gives you a lot of work to do and very little reward (in-game and otherwise) then this game is for you. I suppose with a price tag like it has I got what I paid for but I ultimately can’t recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
