I absolutely recommend this game, let's start there! It's a really nice monster collector with a community sim with plenty of interesting characters to get to know and take on little dates, and there's a farming/ranching aspect as well.

The monster collecting, quests, and deck building combat is definitely where this game shines! It was very fun to build a team that feels unbeatable. I will say once you have that team, battles are not exactly threatening anymore- the difficulty curve does have an ending and if you're familiar with these types of games it's not hard to find- but that didn't detract from the experience for me, because exploring islands, completing quests, and taming new monsters was all part of the fun too!

The farming is not bad but not great. If you want a farming sim, this is no Stardew Valley- but honestly it is closer than a lot of titles. I hope they either get a good modding scene or make some little adjustments to make it better- mostly fertilizer feels really bad to use and everything takes a little too long- it takes 6+ in game hours to just pick, replant and fertilize the full grown greenhouse, tack two more hours on if you have spirits in the barn to check on, not leaving a lot of time in that day for exploring, which is really the bread and butter of the game. The farming system is not bad by any means either- just too lengthy. I actually love what they've done with the different types of fertilizer and making concoctions with your crops to combine their effects!

So farming felt kind of like a chore just because the length of it- but only because I wanted to get back to the rest of the game, which is great! I will not be 100%ing it, but I'm very satisfied with the time I've spent with it, and I think if you like deck building, monster collectors, or the social aspect of farming games, you will too!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like how the game looks a lot; it passed the eye candy test 100%. I also can appreciate that the game is not made by some huge company. From the patch notes I feel like the developer has a fine sense of humor and seems like a very nice person. I would like to give this game a thumbs up but it's just not there yet. I don't feel like I made a good decision buying the game in it's current state but I will not refund it at this time as I believe the potential is there.

I dislike the controls a great deal; they are clunky and could be simplified for a better experience.

I dislike that after catching three spirits I have to go through extra steps to make a barn for them; why can I just catch as many as I want as I go and use the barn for some other purpose? Why the extra step?

The above complaint kind of sucks the fun out of combat, any fights I get it don't seem to make me happy because they are missed opportunities to collect new animal friends.

I dislike the fact that I haven't gotten past a half heart of relationships even after chatting people up for an in game week or so. How long will that all end up taking? What is the overall incentive to do so? I just don't know.

I think that the "make your own bridge blocks" to solve dungeon exploration puzzles isn't the best route. I like environmental puzzles that are self contained in an area. Throwing down a bridge, while novel, doesn't do it for me.

If I am going to be forced to be a main character that I don't get to design myself it'd be nice if they had more going on under the hood, personality and what not.

I sincerely hope that Moonstone Island goes the distance and makes a game with enough polish and interest to rival Stardew Valley. I am rooting for this review to change very soon with continued development; but as of now I cannot recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t15hrs in (7.5 out of 10)

Startup felt a little shallow, character looks cute (lo-fi girl vibes lol)
Character is just told "Ahh so sad, my daughter has to leave to be an alchemist. You are of age." and then kicks you into a quick tutorial and you're sent on your way. Could have been a bit more substance there when describing the lore of the world.
That's just me being picky.
You do pickup some things through dialogue as you progress.

Farming feels like something tacked on to say that this is a "cozy farming game".
You don't really need to depend on it, as you acquire a lot of the plants from exploring, and money is better earned through other methods.

Taming your first spirit it fun.
Fights against spirits are card battles.
Not a fan at first, but as I got into it, I found myself enjoying this method.
Probably would not have been as fun if it was just another Pokemon clone.

Exploring was fun. AS you progress you go from a painfully slow balloon to a sick glider.
Nearby islands are just a forest/ beach and as you get further out you encounter different biomes. These present danger from just standing there. Each new biome had their own set of spirits so that was exciting to see.

Dungeons, as others have stated, feel the same after awhile. Maybe there is more variation as you get further and further out (wishful at the moment).


I see myself playing wayyy more, in order to uncover the map and make all the villagers fall in love with me.
I do give at an 7.5 out of 10, I don't see myself replaying this again once finished.
For the price and what you get out of it though, it is definitely worth a go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMoonstone Island hits the sweet spot with its gorgeous pixel art and animation. The gameplay loop is addictive, offering a bunch of goals and battle mechanics that keep you hooked. The NPCs are really likeable and the mail system makes each of them stand out with their unique vibes.

The best part to me is exploring—gliding around different islands, solving treasure maps, and stumbling on secrets.

Room for Growth
I feel like I did not get to know the NPC's enough, more interactive events with them would be nice, next to the short dates you can go on. As I got deeper into the game, the late stages felt a bit less shiny? There's just not as much going on, especially with limited farming automation. Also, for a game about alchemy, it could use more alchemy-centric quests, maybe some guild-related stuff?

Final Thoughts
Moonstone Island is a visual treat with addictive gameplay. It could pump up the NPC interactions and late-game activities. And hey, more alchemy adventures would be nice. But even with these points, the game’s exploration and attention to detail make it a cozy and enjoyable journey.\t\t\t\t\t
