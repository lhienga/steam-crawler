\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd recommend it as long as you realize what you're getting.

As it was fun to explore and build some things, and I enjoyed it more than similar games. The daytime clock gets a bit stressful at times. I get that they wanted to make things feel a bit more rewarding with the challenge and limited exploration time, but by the time you get a second house you have a choice between it and getting back to your resources where you need to visit almost daily. All of this makes exploration a bit stressful. A game like this can be extra epic if it's spread out a bit.

I would have more enjoyed a three year campaign with more lands to visit. Also a lot of mechanics aren't obvious enough to plan for. The game overall suffers mostly from a lot of great buildup and neat tools, but by the time you get the tools you don't get a chance to use them. Give the game a couple additional years, incrrease the strength of mobs and challenge of dungeons each year, and add some more islands with a few more neat things to do. This way you can develop your relationships better and have more capped by the end.\t\t\t\t\t
