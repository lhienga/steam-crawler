\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuper chill and relaxing. 10/10

This is a card combat / creature collecting game, with 30 unique dungeons, dozens of creatures to collect, and an infinite variety of possible deck builds.

The NPCs are boring, but when you take them on dates you learn a lot more about them. This isn't a social sim.

Combat gets incredibly fun as you get more cards and start making combos. The combat is really where it shines - I've put in so many hours and I have the best team ever - but it's so fun to build decks that I'm excited to build more decks with a different team of spirits, just so I can use new combos.

Farming gets more fun as you get into brewing, because the buffs your spirits get from plants just gets stronger each season; you haven't lived until you've brewed up 2 plants into a potion that gives your spirit +10 power and then gone on to destroy bosses that are 15 levels above you.

And that's not even touching on the massive interior decorating options; hundreds of items that you can buy and craft to decorate your (massively expandable) home. People get SO creative with their designs.

If you like gathering, creature collecting, and card based combat, this game is your dream. I absolutely love it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly Recommend this game.

Pros:
- Very cute vibe.
- Stardew Valley meets Pokemon reminiscent.
- LGBT+ Friendly
- Lots to explore, rarely have a day where you have "nothing" to do.
- Diverse cast of romance-able characters.
- Strange Wizard Man go Brrr
- Lots of deco options.
- Incredible visuals, the Temples are really well done.

Cons:
- Lack of Depth. Many mechanics are only skin deep. Whether thats farming, crafting, even battling. Many mechanics are gated purely by time rather than a real sense of progression.
- "Alchemist" confusion. The game calls you an alchemist from Day 1 but you don't get to start brewing concoctions let alone potions for a long time, up until that point you're more of a spirit farmer.
- Too Many Deco options, not enough usable stuff. I'm so used to Stardew Valley that maybe I'm a bit spoiled, but you can expand your house to frankly absurd proportions, but you have so little need for that much space aside from decorating. I know that this might just be targeting a different demo than myself, but I went whole hog and upgraded my house all the way only to realize that I didn't need almost any of it.
- Confusing tooltips. There are a lot of instances where you get told "Press Space or Left Click to do this, or E or Right Click to do that" and it's super easy to get them mixed up, especially if you're getting chased by spirits and trying to do it in a hurry.
- No Polyamory. I get that Poly does not equal LGBT+, but for a game very focused on inclusion I kinda wish they hadn't gone the stereotypical monogamy route.
- Lack of Modding support. From what I've been able to find the game is very un-friendly to modding, meaning that few if any mods will ever come out for this game. Disappointing, as I think if it was more mod friendly this could easily be the next stardew valley in terms of long-term modding communities.

All in all, I still highly recommend this game. It's a lot of fun and I've found myself sinking a ton of time into it. Definitely worth 20 bucks or if you can get it on sale for less.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this is one of those I wish there was a middling option, but all in all I did enjoy.
As far as light relaxing games go, this is a pleasant experience. Its got fun deck-builder mechanics; enjoyable exploration; and nice light tone.
But man oh man do I wish there was more depth to everything. Combat boils down to 'smash armour then attack constantly' with other abilities never really feeling to scale. Townsfolk relationships never develop unless you explicitly date them; but even then, though well written, fall apart mechanically. Marriage (bonding) doesn't do anything, nor does having a high relationship with people, and the biggest sin there was a character I dated coming at to me as Polyamourous, then getting upset if you date someone else.

It is an enjoyable game, and is worth a try; but I really hope it gets more time and attention and love to become something truly special.\t\t\t\t\t
