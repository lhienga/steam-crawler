My problem with the game is with the continuous bugs that I have dealt with since purchasing on launch day. I am super understanding that bugs are going to happen upon first launch and I think for the most part, the devs have been pretty good about hearing and fixing problems.

However, now for the second time, I have encountered a glitch that caused me to lose a significant amount of progress. I had to completely restart the game the first time, no save file at all despite the fact that the game autosaves every night in game. Now, after the first patch, I lost the ability to play the game completely and when I was able to play it again, days later, I had lost roughly 15 realtime hours of gameplay, despite diligently manually saving every in-game day.

At this point, after 26 hours of gameplay, the game has saved maybe 5 hours of it? I'm heartbroken, as I really enjoy this game and had fully intended to finish it. I really didn't expect to lose significant progress a second time, and I am awaiting a response from the support team before I decide whether to pursue a refund.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely adore this game! If you like Slay the Spire like card games, and Stardew Valley like life sims, you might adore it too!

That's not to say it doesn't have it's issues. I have a 1650 Super and experience lag in some parts of the game, especially the spirit barn and on poison islands, but I don't personally find it to be unplayable lag.
Also targetting feels a little wonky sometimes, but after getting used to it, it no longer bothers me. The only exception being using the hoe and planting seeds can't be done on the tile you're standing on.
In my opinion, the worst thing is that you can't give your spirits Names. You can try naming them in your heart, but that hasn't stopped me from ruthelessly setting spirits free as though they aren't my starter or didn't get me through a tough part of the game.
Taking care of spirits leaves a bit to be desired, the primary difference between spirits is their elemental type and aesthetics. Some spirits have more impactful abilities (the only unique attribute between individual spirits), but ultimately most don't matter enough to change what spirits you'll use regularly.

With all that said, I love pretty much everything else about this game. While there's not an abundance of things to do, I find the things you can do fun and engaging. I find the card combat system highly addictive if not a bit easy later in the game. The Alchemy is cute albeit basic, but it adds depth to the farming itself which I really love.
And it's a deck builder! I love deck builders! The intricacies of picking the right cards (maybe even ratios) along with stats is kinda a min maxers wet dream.

When I heard about this game, I really got my interest peaked by the deck building aspect. Deck builders are a genre I'm very fond of. I also have enjoyed games like Pokemon and Stardew, so I wasn't really worried those aspects would be turn offs. If that sounds like you, maybe you should get this game, you may find a new favorite game like I did.
If however, you don't like games like stardew valley where you have to talk to the villagers and farm, while you may choose to not interract with that part of this game, you'll be missing out on a large portion of content and may find yourself in a game that feels like it lacks depth.
If you don't like games like Slay the Spire, then this may not be for you. But I do think that the combat system is actually fairly simple compared to other deck builders so it may still be worth a shot if you're normally into cozy games. I'm not gonna act like it's a cakewalk, but it shouldn't provide so much of a challenge that it's unplayable.\t\t\t\t\t

You've got to dungeon crawl, farm, collect spirits, and battle other spirits in a deck builder format. It does feel like it's trying to do a lot, but it meshes well together.

Nothing of merit would make me put this game away, but I am eager to see how some updates with QoL or polish improve it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWould I recommend? Not at this juncture. I'd say I dont really have any new criticisms as opposed to anyone else's. Initially, I had opted not to play it due to complete lack of character customization. For a game that boasts diversity I thought it was odd that the player character is supposed to be a set thing, but the game was on sale for release, so I thought I would get past it and try it anyway because it's an indie game and I like to support indie game makers.

My complaint is perhaps that it doesn't know what it's trying to me. I don't care for the "cozy" genre in general, or what people are trying to make it out to be which is an oversaturation of farming simulators that feel like someone looked at Stardew and said "I can make this but better". I feel like they seldom are. But this one is perhaps one of the few where I wonder why farming was included at all? There doesn't seem to be much point to it in the beginning of the game, where the only seeds that you have at your disposal is forageables, and by the time your crops have grown you'll have quite enough of those in your inventory from exploration alone, so no real need to grow them. Especially *especially* because they sell for very little. I got though about one crop and surmised that there was no point to it.

Also, I know fishing minigames have always been divisive in farming sims, especially Stardew where it can be difficult in the beginning, but to not have any sort of game mechanic for it whatsoever just felt odd to me. I dont understand why it would be included aside from being yet another waste of stamina without much benefit.

Another thing I dont undestand the inclusion of is the romance system. This is another big deal breaker for me, because the thing I get most from these sorts of games are the NPC interactions, and here there really are none to be had. Others will mention, but the NPC daily interactions are limited to a blurb of dialogue, usually a line, and then 3 RNG choices of random success: Talk, Joke, Flirt. That was extremely disappointing to me, I'd daresay it turned me off. Because essentially it doesn't seem like it matters what you know about a character, which will be very little, so long as your random chance pans out. And it seldom does. It turned me off of speaking to NPCs if I'm honest, and I wasn't much interested in them to start.

The main focus is the monster hunting which is. Fine. Better reviews than mine have mentioned the issues with the lack of platforming to traverse the terrain that monsters can scale easily and pin you in. You're only allowed three until you build a barn and building a barn requires moonstone which, if you're not careful about examining the ingredients needed for every item, you might end up using and then needing in a different state for a different level of item (guess who did that one!).

Also, I'm sorry, that wheel to switch through your tools is so inconvenient. It's a small issue that felt like a big one, where the tedium is really felt the longer you have to use it. Often times I skipped resource gathering altogether simply because I didn't feel like hitting Q and switching my tools back and forth a million times

Honestly, this is one time that I wish the "do/do not recommend" was more than a simple yes or no. I don't hate the game, it was fun enough, but the cons that I had keep me from having any real desire to play more, I probably won't come back to it while it's in this state because I personally didn't enjoy it. I guess you could say that I don't recommend it, because I think it could be better. I don't think saying that it's made by a small team is enough when it feels like there were so many corners cut on unnecessary things that didn't feel like they needed to be added, at least until they were a little more substantial. I wish if this was a game about monsters, it had been a game about monsters and done it really well, than be a game about monsters and also All This Other Stuff and nothing really got much time and attention otherwise.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of monster taming games, Stardew Valley and Slay the Spire, this scratches every itch imaginable. The developers have taken the core mechanics and refined them into a player friendly experience. You have Stardew Valley farming and social/dating simulation, Pokemon monster collecting and raising, and Slay the Spire deck battling. The result is a cozy amalgam that doesn't force you too hard into any one aspect.

I disagree with the many comments about the days being too short or asking for adjustable time speeds. The days are a similar length to Stardew Valley's, and that mild pressure is central to the time management aspect. If anything it is more forgiving than Stardew Valley - you don't get penalised for falling asleep outside and don't lose stamina for sleeping late.

Farming is not the focus. You get sprinklers very early on, and a warp item (which Stardew Valley doesn't give you till the very end!). This immediately frees you up for other aspects of the game, which includes exploration and monster taming. The map is procedurally generated, adding significant replayability.

Combining the Slay the Spire card mechanic with a Pokemon-lite experience keeps things fresh. You carry three monsters (or in this game, Spirits) at a time with their own cards which forms the basis of your "deck." You vary your deck by using different Spirits or levelling up your Spirits which allows you to add cards to their set. In battle, being given a random hand each turn forces you to improvise, rather than repetitively selecting the same moves. You can also upgrade, add or remove cards with talismans, which are relatively uncommon.

My only gripe with the above is the game doesn't make it obvious that you can "skip" adding cards. If you're not familiar with Slay the Spire mechanics, you can easily end up with a very bloated and ineffective deck.

All in all, this is a wonderful game that streamlines and takes the most important bits of some very good games. Here's hoping there will be DLCs to really flesh it out!


tldr:
Pros:
- Unashamedly uses main mechanics of Stardew Valley, monster taming and Slay the Spire, but streamlines it into a more user friendly experience
- Adorable monster design and general visual feel - the Octopup is my favourite
- Procedurally generated map makes it very replayable
- Music is great!
- One of those "has a bit of everything" games

Cons (mostly minor):
- Slight lags here and there. Hopefully can be optimised out
- Most Spirits of the same element seem to have similar "cards", may only differ in passive ability and initial stat distribution
- Difficulty level is mild
- Finding mines is a bit random. Probably similar mechanic to finding ladders in Stardew Valley mine.
- Needs to make it clearer to new players how to streamline deck

Score: 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
