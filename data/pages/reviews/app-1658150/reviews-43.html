\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just 100% completed achievements, and have read some of the negative reviews here and don't really agree with them. For starters, a big complaint I'm seeing is that the monsters don't evolve. This is only a problem if you're playing a new pokemon game, but I knew I was playing Moonstone island. This was never an issue for me, and realistically if you want pokemon, play pokemon. This same arguement can be said about the farming, how it's not exactly like stardew... Stardew still exists...?

Honestly, it takes a lot of similar elements from these games but you cant expect an unrelated game to be a carbon copy. While I do echo the "monster battles feel boring" sentiment because theres no growth GOAL persé, I didnt really see this as an issue because it's the DECK that matters rather than the creature. What purpose would evolving a creature have if the deck would be the same? The deck revolves around the typing, so once I realized "oh, all the poison types have the same deck!" I just found the cutest one of that type and kept it, then went and found my favorite water type or whatever. You get my point. Evolving would do nothing beneficial. My team for most of the game was Taddle, Beardini and Wolfbane. My excitement came from collecting them all and upgrading my decks. Small decks win games people! Poison is awesome and dark is SO strong imo!

This is a great little indy game and I love the character design choices. The farming isnt really mandatory and neither is the decorating (I never decorated my house tbh) but I was impressed with the options I saw available! It made me realize nothing in this game is mandatory so those saying it felt like "there was no point" I'm like... relax. It's a cozy do-what-you-want game. If you dont feel the farming is lucrative (it is btw I made almost all my money from farming but you can also make bank from Zed and turning in dex... research data) then dont farm. Romance someone, decorate your house or something. Theres even a potion to stop time from moving IN your house so you can decorate! Why is this such a negative review standpoint? I am an achievement hunter, so I romanced magic man first and then broke up with him when everyone was max hearts so I could eventually get the "get rejected for a date 3 days in a row" achievement. It actually made me feel bad, so I guess I loved him after all ♡

Speaking of achievements, theres 2 that I would suggest edits for to make peace of mind for fishing for 1000... on the overnight stat screen where it shows dungeons cleared etc, if there was a "Fish caught: Total" so I didnt panic that a new indy game had a bugged achievement this would have saved me a lot of anxiety lmao. I had no idea what I was at on any day nor did I know if boots counted, so I just "hoped" I would get it. This sucked, please add this total somewhere, maybe even a craftable item from Tobin or something, similar to the calendar!

Also rumble on bite would be very nice, I hate having my volume up, ESPECIALLY grinding those fish. I threw on netflix and just fished for I kid you not, TWO WHOLE IRL DAYS. I had to always be mindful of that ding tho. I silently pleaded for a rumble on bite update before I finished.

The "clear 3 dungeons in a row" achievement cannot be completed if you've done the "clear all dungeons" achievement. It was the ONLY one I had to start a new game and grind from scratch for because I didnt see this until I was in end game. Devs, if theres a way to reset dungeons somehow I couldnt figure it out. Can there maybe be a potion that resets a dungeon you're in for a day? That would at least make the achievement not completely missable.

Every other achievement I was able to get on my main save. Big tip, hatch eggs. Lots of them. Theres some that can ONLY be obtained from eggs and I didnt know this until I looked it up! I luckily just grinded egg hatching while I fished but it still took those 2 days to get the last ones I needed.

Overall, this game isnt perfect. But for what it is, how much fun I had with it, and for this price? Absolutely recommend. I got animal crossing vibes from the decorating options, pokemon vibes from monster collecting, stardew vibes from the characters and farming (omg ConcernedApe make a seperate TOOL WHEEL. Stardew needs this!!! Brilliant idea from MoonstoneIsland devs!) Just, elements from every cozy game scaled down to fit nicely into one. Works great on my steam deck.

Thanks for the game! I had fun completing it!

Oh and Ferra best girl.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who's ex got them to start liking more casual games, like stardew valley, I always struggled with them. I'm someone who CRAVES RPGs. I like the numbers. makes my brain go brrrr.

That being said...This game is phenomenal. If I'm going to play a slower paced game, I still want to FEEL like I'm doing something constructive. And not just basic construction. And this game delivers. You could clearly play this game and get soooo much out of it and never even touch the farming mechanics. But the fact that the items you farm can help you in combat is such a smart choice. And that monsters you raise drop different resources, makes wanting to collect them all EVEN MORE WORTH IT.

With this game being new, you can tell there's so much love put into it. And its different enough from other games of the genre that I'm excited to see what kind of new stuff they implement as they game goes. A few things that maybe could be some QOL stuff to improve what's a pretty much perfect game would be this...

1. Can I PLEASE just move stuff from my inventory to the sale box. Having to go through and select each item and how many and then say yes I STILL want to sell these, it makes selling items in bulk tedious.
2. Maybe explain moonstones and mines and stuff that are on the different islands a little better? I spent two seasons not understanding that I had to find a hidden mine under a rock and I got pretty frustrated. That and since moonstones are used for SO many things...I think just explaining that they're scattered around the islands and that theyre important could help. I was THIS close to selling them cuz they were worth a pretty penny, and SOMETHING told me to hold on top them. Glad I did. Moonstones would normally be something you get later on in other games, so I wasn't expecting SUCH a precious crafting resource right off the bat.
3. PLEASE develop the characters more. They add charm to the place. THEY make it come alive. The fact there's so little interaction with them other than the generic options, which don't result in actually getting to know them more is disappointing. They have distinct personalities, but they never actually show them to us for more than 5 seconds. The character designs are great! But at right now, they're kinda just shiny and hollow.
4. PLEASE more MC customization. I get you're trying to do the androgynous thing so its easier to use one character. But I would love it if I could use the elemental stuff to like dye my hair and clothes. Allow me to put MYSELF into the game.


LONG story short. I really liked pokemon growing up. The casual genre is growing on me in my old age lol. And this perfectly combines an old love and a new joy. And I'm excited to see what they do with it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7 out of 10
Certainly worth its asking price.
+Cute artwork all throughout
+Cast is fun and endearing
+Combat and creature deck building is good fun, really power trip once you know what cards you can get and with what.
+Pretty smooth progression all things considered.
+Lots of decor items if that's your thing.

-Some creatures are kind of a pain in the ass to get. I tried getting them all but have decided to duck out for now as these last 4(?) are egg exclusives it seems and they just don't want to appear despite however many dozens of eggs I've hatched
- Each element has it's own card pool and general idea of what their win condition is. Good in essence, but there's a a clear tier list as far as the player deck is concerned. with Fire and Psychic being Tier 0 for aggro and support respectively, Followed by Earth in second place with the ability to play either role fairly well but not as good as the first 2. The other elements are then all in gimmick land. Which stinks cause they have a lot of fun designs.
-Like most games with open world farming and so forth, you do eventually hit a point where you just sleep sleep sleep to speed things along. It's not as bad as some games though, so this is a more minor negative.
-While the townsfolk are fun and have a solid assortment of dialogue, there's a stark lack of town events.
-Sinjin will kill itself in the final fight if you bring it :' )\t\t\t\t\t
