\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am only 11 hours in and this game feels super grindy. I don't really have any "Direction" being given on what to do or where to really go. What is this game about? What am I working towards? What is the point? This is the problem I have with a lot of "cozy" type games is there is nothing for you to even put as a goal.
You're just given free reign and sometimes a quest or two that doesn't really mean anything other than character friendship.
You can tell this creator was heavily ( and by heavily I mean use the exact same story but change 1 or 2 details) inspired by Hayao Miyazaki films. Which I don't have a problem with, I love those movies and it is fun to see the little bits and pieces of their take on the films in the game.... Also the battle music is almost a direct replica of Zelda gameplay music... I have no problem when creators use inspiration from other amazing games/movies but also seems like they could have put more effort into making it their own.
Befriending townspeople seems extremely redundant and very unfulfilling. They have Hearts at the bottom of their chat window, You can "Chat" "Joke" or "flirt" with them but it creates no dialog, just either hearts or broken hearts upon success/failure.... So there is no real interaction. Also I have talked to the same people for a month now and have barely reached a full heart... I am sure gifts would speed this along but also... why?
I do like the battle system, although I have encountered one too many times where all I get are non attack/attack cards and no armor breaking cards and can pretty much do nothing but waste my turn.
Leveling in the game is weird. There seems to be a huge jump in wild creature levels from level 1-5 and then all the sudden the next island is level 13s in a trio that can all just add afflictions and take out your armor in 2 turns even with a full team that is in similar level ranges.
Oh, get this element type it'll make this area easier!
Cool, that level element type is like 3x higher than the area I need to be in so I can't even use them if I tame one since I have a tame level cap?
They claim a material is super rare in the game and that only one material exists per island but then require 9 of them per building craft...
so far, I've only just hit summer, I know most farming games don't pick up til later on, but this seems like they added farming just to say it has a farming element... Its a "Oh get seeds and grow these 3 plants per season that all do the same thing except this ONE plant you will use relentlessly."
I am hoping I change my review later on, but so far it's very grindy for no reason, there is no real direction in the game that makes me want to continue.
I know a lot of people like those laid back cozy games like Animal crossing, and don't get me wrong I do too on occasion when they are well done and give atleast some reason to play it day by day. But you're not even told your character has the desire to see all the creatures, or gather badges to beat a final boss. You're literally just told to go "live on your own" by your weeping parents. Stardew was atleast escaping your old life and defeating the evil corporation, harvest moon games are saving the homeland, Animal crossing is a collection game that still gives you goals and achievements to work towards. Rune Factory you're trying to save Something and have a plot to go along at your own pace. Sunhaven is super cozy game but has better story telling and an end goal. Serin fate is an amazing farming/creature collection game that has a direction.
All in all. I might give it a few more hours... but I don't even really want to, which is a shame because I expected to really like this one.\t\t\t\t\t

Devs are so responsive and constantly releasing QOL updates, following player suggestions or feedback, and just making this an even more complete experience with every update.
