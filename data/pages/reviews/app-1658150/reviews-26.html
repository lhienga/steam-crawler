\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, it's basically Pokemon meets Stardew Valley plus flying between sky islands, and it's far better than that elevator pitch has any business being. Owing to the tried-and-true as well as innovative mechanics, the well-executed pixel art style, the enjoyable characters, the depth and breadth of the spirit gameplay, and the humor with plenty of pop culture references thrown in.

It has the things you would expect from a Stardew Valley-like game; farming, talking to villagers, mining, selling crops and other items, gifting, home upgrades, crafting, dating, fishing, but instead of farm animals, you get spirits - basically pokemon but with cards determining potential actions each turn - and combat is done through them. The deck building and synergies for the spirits is a good balance of deep and easy to understand.

The gameplay takes place across various sky islands, and technology advancement allows you to explore further islands as you progress. There are mines for gathering mineral resources, and there are also dungeons filled with monsters, loot, puzzles, hidden areas, character upgrades, and plot advancements.

My only complaint is that the relationship-building mechanic could be a little stronger. It's pretty similar to Stardew Valley, minus marriage and children, (which is significant) except it has sort of a Sims-style conversation mechanic, which is just a bit lacking in depth. And instead of friendship events, you can go on dates. Being able to plan when dates will happen is a cool addition, but the downside is that for anyone you don't want to actually date, you don't get the equivalent of friendship events with them.

Overall, I highly recommend this chill, farming sim, dating sim, crafting exploration, monster fighting pixel art game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to be honest and say I tried to love this game. I really did. It has a nice design, and I can tell the amount of love and effort put into this game. This being said, there are too many issues that prevent me from actually having a good time. I have too many gripes about this game to keep playing. I straight up cupped my face in my hands after it crashed again. I don't want to hate this game, but for those who want to buy this game, here's a few struggles I've had while playing this game, in hopes you have second thoughts.


Everything takes way longer and is a lot more complicated than it needs to be. Taming monsters is fun and all, but you can only carry along 3 monsters at a time. Not only that, if you want more monsters, you need to create a barn to hold those monsters. Even if you have a barn, you can't hold anymore more monsters if you don't have a feeder in your barn or your team is full. I went into the first boss fight and found a shiny monster, but wasn't able to keep it because my team was full and I didn't have a barn because you don't unlock it until around halfway to the end of the first month. You can't rearrange or get rid of another monster in your team or barn if you find a shiny, meaning you completely miss out on it forever, even if you end up taming it. Not only that, you need to reach a certain requirement to unlock the barn in the first, aside from beating the first dungeon. This gatekeeps a lot of content and monsters you might need to progress. I didn't even craft the barn until there were only a few days left in the first month.

Not only that, everything is too expensive, especially early on in the game. There's a material called Moonstone, which is required for multiple upgrades for tools, transportation, and buildings. This being said, Moonstone can only be found ONCE per ISLAND. Secret underground dungeon chests have a chance to drop them, but nothing is guaranteed. There's a way to increase the chance of chests dropping them, but it's still a 5-10% increase. The worst part about moonstone is that not only is it extremely rare, but sometimes recipes require moonstone bars, which you need 3 moonstones in order to make it, making crafting increasingly expensive.

Next is the monster problem and combat problem. Everything is out to get you, no matter what, monsters can and will chase you. The only way to prevent monsters from chasing you is to unlock a skill through progression, without knowing how long you will take to level up that skill, let alone knowing you had it. I didn't even know skills existed until the skill tree popped up out of nowhere when I went to bed. The only way to see your skill tree normally is to craft a mirror using moonstone.

Also, monsters in combat will absolutely destroy you if you don't have a proper team, which sucks if you want to level up. In battle, there are several things you need to focus on. Your health and your shield, and the enemies' health and shield. The shield is depleted using certain skills, which both you and the opposing have. While it seems fair, sometimes your fights can become unwinnable. When shields are depleted, the damage dealt is increased to 1.5x the original, with also stunning a monster for that turn. This being said, when you are stunned, and you're dying, you can't use items on that monster, at all. This means you can lose a monster sometimes to a crowd of monsters ganging on one of your guys. While I can understand that strategy also goes into play, fights can just seem unfair, especially against boss monsters. Right before my game crashed like I said earlier, I was fighting a local boss monster that sometimes show up. In this fight, they had a regen skill that they kept spamming that restored them to full health each turn, regardless of how many times I broke their shield or did damage, meaning unless I ran, I wasn't able to get out of the battle. Unfortunately, even though I defeated the other monsters on the field, my weaker monsters that I wanted to level up wouldn't get any experience, which means no level-up. There are ways to level up using crops, but those crops only give you around 100 exp, whereas monsters will need 1000s depending on the level.
Of course, there's plenty more frustrations, but that's enough about the combat and monsters.

The NPCs are alright. Nothing crazy, or even worthwhile. They're kind of just like cardboard. They have some personality, but I feel like this game is just a dating sim farming game rather than vice-versa. Not even the main character you play feels fleshed out. You can't customize or even name your character, to the point I'm not even sure anyone on the island you land on actually knows your name. It just feels shallow. For my one comparison I'm doing to Stardew Valley, one of the big reasons I liked the game so much was because not everyone was romanceable. People had lives and families, hobbies and interests. In Moonstone Island, everyone just walks around and owns a store for you to shop at. They're just convenient and pretty store clerks rather than people.

Finally, the game's performance. The performance and handling of this game is iffy, to say the least. I always feel like there's input lag, and the game sometimes chugs out of nowhere. I know my laptop doesn't perform the best while running games, but it has run well enough to play cute little pixel art games like this one. The main issue is that every time I load areas, I fear for my game because it might crash again. I've played whole entire productive days where I need to do it all over again because it crashed. There's a manual save function, but I shouldn't have to save every time I load an area.

So that's about it. I don't want to hate on the game nor discourage the people who like it. There are just too many problems for me to keep playing. I know that this game is new and is still being worked on, so I have hopes for it in the future, but if you're at all worried about finding a cute little farming sim right now, I'd honestly just save the money or buy Stardew Valley instead.\t\t\t\t\t
