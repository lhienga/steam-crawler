\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo what exactly is this game about? It's a bit of the following:

1) A pokemon like card game.
2) An open world exploration game.
3) A farming and ranching simulator.
4) A dating simulator.

In that order. If you don't wan't to play a lot of pokemon card game, you aren't going to have a good time

Pros.-
* The card game is simple, intuitive, and fairly fun, but late in the story you will be spending a lot of hours grinding experience. Many hours.
* I like the world building.
* I like being able to explore about 100 islands, each of which has at least 1 point of interest, but usually more.
* I like most of the spirits themselves. They do start out with some stat variance between individuals, but not between species. Early game spirits and late game spirits will have the same attributes, so don't worry about having to get the best for stats. Now each does have passive ability and some are super powerful, while others are worthless, but you can get through the game with your favorite spirits without having to use the most powerful.
* Also some pets give out of combat effects to you, such as +% to walk speed, bonuses on charisma checks with the villagers, a loot magnet.
* I like the art style, and vibrancy of the world, with one really off-putting exception. One of the early game earth spirits is straight up the coal spirits from Spirited Away. Also seems to be inspired by Castle in the Sky, and Howls Moving Castle.
* Like the food mechanics in this. Food heals, recruits spirits, and gives huge stat buffs. You'll be eating lots of your own crops and feeding them to your spirits. Seriously, food bonuses last all day and make a big difference at a low cost. Feed your pets often.
* The game seems really stable. Only soft crashed once in 95 hours.

Cons-
* The farming is really simplified compared to Stardew Valley, but then again you are expected to be putting the majority of your time into exploration.
* Not many NPCs. The village is tiny; 11 romance-able npcs and thats it. No families, no kids, no elderly.
* Alright, officially in game, you are the minimum legal age to be emancipated in this world. Whatever that is exactly. But you look like 10 year old girl. Maybe you suffer from symmetrical dwarfism like that girl on TLC. But all the rest of the villagers normal. Kinda. Like normal for Portland Oregon, but adults in a stardew like art style. Then you look like a little girl. Why??
* Also no character customization in any way. Your a little girl. Not that it ever matters in the story.
* A matter of personal taste, but I really wasn't interested in many of the villagers. As a little girl I wasn't exited to romance any of them, but I went with the pink haired, lesbian, tom boy, blacksmith, because she was the most normal.
* The romance scenes were really disappointing. Seriously the entire scenes are only 5 or 6 text boxes long then done and over in a matter of seconds.
* The islands have a lot of M. C. Escher physics. Sometimes you can use this to your advantage, but the physics really seams to struggle with the concept of height.
* Trees, grass, and weeds never regrow. Neither do ore nodes. It's not a big deal, but in a game where you are trying to fix the environment, I feel bad about stripping island after island of ever tree and metal ore. That will never regrown again. Ends up feeling like a dying world as the islands become more and more bare of resources the longer you play. A multi year farming simulator where plants don't grow seems really weird.
* Not the game maker's problem, but the game is very new, many of the elements of the game are not yet documented and some YouTube guides are giving out mechanically incorrect information.

Generally a pretty decent game. But again this is a card game with some farming and romance in it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could do a 50/50 type thing. I do enjoy this game to a point but there are a lot of things that people who either enjoy farming sims or monster taming games missing that make this a not really recommend for me. I got to fall of year 1 and was hoping some of my issues were 'oh it's meant to just take time and relax' but often times I found myself getting to frustrated at points to fully relax. To be clear, I think the game has enough in it that makes it worth the price. I think it could just use some polishing or ungating certain things.

And if you get this game, go into it thinking it's a monster taming game first with farming elements. I thought the opposite and that might be why I'm having such an issue with it. I misunderstood what the game was trying to sell to me, and it might just be my fault to misinterpreting the makers themselves or the online reviewers who gave me that impression.

First, before I talk about the problems, I do want to say, I do like this game. It's charming. The spirits are cute. The concept is cute. The art and music is great! However, I find myself not fully relaxing or just chilling out while playing, like with other farming sims or monster tamers, because I feel I have to manage so many of the below stuff.

I'll try not to ramble too much and break down some points that I think make this a harder sell:

1. The battles are fun enough, and the monsters are cute. I enjoy collecting them. But it honestly feels a bit like a chore. Not to mention, picking the earth starter really hurts you. For reference ( and I believe it might be like this on every world if not I was just unlucky ) The first temple boss is earth. The second is water. If you start with the earth you can only get earth or water spirits because the other elements are gated behind higher levels and you need to skill up to be able to tame them. Or get lucky and get an egg drop from their battle. But that's on the opposite path of the 'exp while walking' which became necessary for me because I didn't want to waste too much in game hours grinding up by battling ( which becomes harder to do at a certain point ). I haven't touched half of the spirits I get because I don't want to bother leveling them up, even if I'm at a type disadvantage, which I had to basically disown my starter because other earth spirits had better cards to start than him and I needed other elements. I don't mind grinding in games, that's when I get some shows watched or listen to a book, but it felt like more of a chore in this game and knowing I have bosses to face makes me feel mentally exhausted in a game I just want to relax with. And maybe I'm just bad at the game, that's 100% a possibility, I could not be using all the plants and potions to my advantage but I just wanted to turn my brain off and relax with this game. And I guess on the flip side of exp walking, you could level the 'tame higher level spirits' path and then go tame a really high level one. Maybe I should just focus on doing that instead.

2. Which brings me to 2. Some of the skills require you to plan. You want the max amount of stamina you can get? Don't touch any trees until you get the skill that bumps that up. Want a large amount of chest? Enter as few dungeons as you can before getting the bump the treasure chest appearance up. I'm used to some skills being really great and having to acquire skills you don't really want to continue down the skill tree. But some lock other skills and I'm also used to that system as well but...I dunno, the skills in this game seem really strange? Half of them seem to become useless after a certain point or the percentages are so low in comparison to the other skill that it doesn't even seem worth it. And in a game where it seems the main focus is on crafting and decorations ( the crafting giving you potions for battle and exploring ) with everything else just sort of helping with that.

3. Resources are...Hard to come by. You can plant stuff all day, and I love the greenhouse for that. But the islands are becoming ugly and barren because you cut down trees and...That's it. You can use that precious wood to make decorative trees but when you need wood to upgrade all the buildings, build all the stalls for spirits, and make ash for potions you can't really afford to use it for decoration. You can plant everything under the sun, except trees. And some moonstones come back to an island every season, why can't a handful of trees come back on every island every season? I don't want more spirits because I don't want to waste wood on crafting the stalls. It should be the most common resource and it feels like the most precious there is since so many things need it. And spirits all have the same drop when they are the same element. No unique furs or scales or things like that. You have an electric one, it drops a spark. I guess on one hand more resource management might be a nightmare but between that and the slowly dwindling trees I just don't care about having more than a couple of any elemental spirit.

4. The NPCs are cute but...There are only a handful. So if you're not a super fan of any of them...That's it. They are the only ones there. They have quests where they request spirits and it's cute seeing them in town with the spirits they request but it's a little annoying you have to remove someone from your team, put the spirit in your team, go talk to the person and hand it over, and then you'll need to remember to go back and get your original spirit from the barn. It's a bit tedious. The reward is...Usually a free item from their shop? It's a unique idea but I'd rather be able to have a tangible reward because honestly I didn't even realize most of them even did that.

A lot of these issues might just be my own. I set certain expectations and that's on me. In all honesty, the wood thing is the biggest deal breaker for me and why it's more of a negative. Honestly if that and a few other things are tweaked then it'd be a solid game. I wouldn't mind the battling and grinding up skills and stuff. But stressing out about what to craft on top of trying to grind up spirits for bosses just makes it not a really super cozy game for me. Which is okay, I still enjoy. I can tell a lot of love went into this game. And if they are continuing to work on it, that's great. I may end up changing my tune. Until then, this is just a warning to anyone in the same mindset as me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWas so excited for Moonstone island to come out before release and the excitement lived up to it. Spent like 12hrs on it on the second day of purchase. You cannot compare this game to stardew valley although there are many elements that are similar and those who go into Moonstone island with that expectation may be disappointed.

Moonstone island itself is more dungeon focused and card game combat oriented. With the identity as an alchemist, you get to tame monsters, harvest plants and craft items to assist you in beating the dungeons and other spirits. In some ways, it feels very nostalgic to pokemon wanting to be the very best at Moonstone island. But I feel like one of my favourite element of the game is island hopping on your glider once you unlock it. Zooming around islands to slowly uncover the fog to find new biomes and search for moonstones can easily kill a day in the game.

Villager interaction is very cute and in my opinion a little more fun than stardew valley's villager interaction. Like what some others have said, the routine for villagers however is a little bit dull and not as interesting as stardew valley. Maybe some room for improvement but overall I really like it.

Small complaints would be like tree cutting, mining and cutting grass is a little tedious at first. But worse of all would be cave spawning as it took ages just to find the second cave. Tool wheel can also possibly be improved a little more as it can be a little annoying to use.

Overall, game is fun, addictive and definitely a time killer. Would 10/10 recommend when it's on sale, if you can't wait I reckon full price is worth it and if you don't like it in the first 2 hours just refund. Hope the developers keep up the love and maybe introduce multiplayer down the road!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMoonstone Island provides many things to do: farming, crafting, spirit taming, dungeoning. I read a google article with just that description and bought the game 5 minutes later. All of it was appealing, entertaining and kept me hooked. I always found something else to do, but what kept me the most busy was exploring the map and spirit taming. I really enjoyed discovering the spirits and their deck themes, or exploring islands and seeing what they had to offer. Most of the time it was treasure, new crops to discover and add to the store's supply, and of course, new spirits! There was so much to uncover and that was a ton of fun.

Using the deck-building format for monster taming battles is a fresh take on the genre and what drew me to Moonstone Island. It offers a different experience in each battle depending on how big your deck size is but it also slows down the leveling a bit if you're trying to grind to get your spirits' levels up. Overall though I loved the card system and the amount of customization and synergies available to mix & match spirits is huge. You could go an explosion build with a fire spirit and give it intangible from the psychic spirit deck, and just deal massive damage for 0 cost. Or go poison and burn, bypass shields altogether. The possibilities and theorycrafting are amazing, and adds another deep dimension of exploration.

The spirits are cute, refreshing and unique. I do wish there were more ice spirits (since they have a whole separate element) and that they had their own island biome (i.e. frozen mountaintops) but since there are so few at the time of writing this review, I understand. I'm definitely looking forward to better documentation of all the spirits, such as a Dex, containing each elemental deck theme. Viewing card information + upgrades would also be very nice to have, so that I can properly compare which card to upgrade and see what exactly gets upgraded. It's a lot of going back and forth, upgrading, trial & error, removing, which is time-consuming and exhausting; could be a point of frustration for some.

I didn't use the potion crafting much, so for me it was a forgotten system. I loved using crops to boost my spirits' stats though, that was nice. The villagers had cute personalities and each had their own thing (except Rowan, I've no idea what they like). I do hope the village gets events added or something to jumpstart the liveliness of the town. It often felt like nothing really happened in town; it was just where you go to chat with people, do commerce and that's it.

All in all, Moonstone Island is a lovely developed game with charming soundtracks, beautiful pixel art, deep customization content in both decorations and spirit taming and just a great blend of systems.\t\t\t\t\t
