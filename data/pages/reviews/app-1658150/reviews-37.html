\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCute game with a good amount of varying content, it has monster card battles, hidden treasures and dungeons, and lots of farming and decorations.

Id like to give this game a title of jack of all trades master of none, all of the content it has is good but doesnt excell in any of it.

Its battling with monsters is well done but theres very limited cards and none of them are monster specific, only passives are and with enough overwhelming power or the right debuffs the element matchup doesnt matter.
Theres a good bit of hidden chests, hidden caves, and procedurally generated dungeons to explore but after the first like 6 or so it feels so samey.
Farming is limited to stat boosting items that arnet worth selling because u can earn way more gold in other aspects of the game.
Decoration is locked behind blueprints so u have to either earn a ton of gold or go exploring for alot of chests and then on top of that gathering all the materials, the decoration is somewhat well done tho cause unlike stardew where u place items on a grid, in this game u can place items on the half grid point.

Overall score of 8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the aesthetics of this game, it's really cute. The monsters are simple but still good and the musics are lovely.

Sadly, it misses on several others aspects.
The balance of the crops : winter crops have much strongers effects than the others, so, except in few instances, why use them ?
The money : very easy to earn but become useless very fast, as there is very little to buy.
The monsters 'drops' : same as money. Once you have some in reserve, you don't need more.
The NPCs : a diverse cast but there is not many events with them. At most, a few missions and some dates. And even then, the interactions are limited.
The world : vast but ultimately, not very detailed or anything intricate.

Don't misunderstand. I don't hate this game. There is some good ideas : the combat system, as an exemple. Or the number of decorations, which is consequential. Just, the rest of the game could have gained from the same attention.

At least, I had some fun.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t This game is closing in on a Stardew-like experience for me and I LOVE it. I think the thing that makes it feel Stardew-esque is that there is SO MUCH to do. Catch spirits, perfect their cards, prepare specific potions for different goals, fishing, mining, dungeons and puzzles, exploring randomized islands and following treasure maps--I feel like I could easily pack 100 hours into it over the next few months.

The ending feels a touch anti-climatic to me, and so do finishing temples. REALLY wish there was some sort of NG+ option.

I could easily do a second, perhaps third playthrough in the future, especially with what I'm learning from the first. I'm really looking forward to any possible future DLCs, as the houses they add are very cute and dont cost too much. If the extra support also means we get a few extra spirits here and there, I'm happy to keep collecting them!\t\t\t\t\t
