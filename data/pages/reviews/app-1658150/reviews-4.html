\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMoonstone Island is the perfect blend of cozy and exciting, a wonderful mix of gameplay elements and story tropes from all sorts of classic video games, like Stardew Valley, Pokemon, and Animal Crossing. It's a blend I didn't know I needed until I took a break from watering my crops to go do battle with Spirits (and collect them all!). The map is very large, full of secrets to discover, and traveling around the map is fun in itself as you consider travel modes, wind resistance, and warp points. Flying around unexplored areas to find mystery islands was some of the most fun I've had in a cozy game, ever! And the Spirits are so well-designed and thought through, from their punny naming conventions to their element-related visual designs.

The most impressive part of this game is how extensive it is for being less than a year old! It's jam-packed with colourful content and provides dozens of hours of entertainment, whether you're in it to grow the best crops, or clear all the dungeons with the help of your trusty Spirit companion, or make the most friends in town. Or, you could do my favourite pastime, and try to figure out what shape each island is meant to represent (I see you, Australia-shaped island with a treasure chest in Exmouth...).

To reiterate, this game is less than a year old, and already has several content DLCs released, so it is of course not really complaining when I say, however, that customization options were a bit limited and therefore disappointing. The main character, for example, is non-customizable, which is understandable as there are CGs featuring them, and yet I didn't connect as much as I could've to the main character because I simply didn't care for their lack of distinctive appearance (though the floppy hat is iconic I won't lie). Even simply picking a gender option would have gone a long way, which I was surprised was not already available since this game has a wonderful cast of gender-diverse and well-written NPCs. There are also relatively few home decoration options currently available in game (though I know this will change with upcoming DLCs), making it rather difficult to make effective use of the incredibly large space allocated to the player's home- one of the largest, if not THE largest I've ever seen in any sim game ever. Lastly, I was surprised by the relatively small amount of NPCs in the game, a fact that, again, might change in the future. I can only for now when I say that I was a little disappointed at how flat and un-lively Moonstone Island seemed to be. There are no elders, no children, no families, only bachelor/ettes of varying adult ages. The NPC schedules are fairly predictable and don't have much variation, getting old quickly. The socialization system is also fairly innovative, but simply having the option to "chat, joke, flirt" without associated dialogue doesn't do much to actually build attachments to the NPCs. You only get an inner glimpse at their lives/personalities/backstories if you take them out on dates, which is a little awkward when you're trying to roleplay an exclusive relationship with a specific NPC.

That being said, Moonstone Island is a beautiful, rich, and extremely well-conceptualized game, full of things to do and discoveries to make. At the time of this review, it is about to be 6 months old (as of launch date), which is an extremely short amount of time since release to be making up definitive opinions about the game. Still, despite the narrow spectrum of options in a few places, overall it is an expansive and engaging game that is easy to get sucked into and throw dozens of hours into without ever getting bored. I definitely recommend this game to any who enjoy the life sim/cozy/farming genre of games; Moonstone Island has had a beautiful launch, and has the potential to grow further and become a hallmark of the genre in due time!\t\t\t\t\t
