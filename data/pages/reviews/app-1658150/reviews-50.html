\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am truly enjoying this game! I love the concept. My fiance recently got me into Pokemon games as I never played as a kid. So I love the similarities and style. I love the art style and the Stardew feel (How can you not compare the two!)

That being said there are a few things that are frustrating to me. First off aside from showing you where the other Islands are (After you explore and fly around enough to find them and uncover more of the map) The map is not helpful. Personally I struggle with direction when I am getting used to a new game and where everything is. The map has icons for the shops but no way to see who lives where, store hours, or anything like that. So its a learning process just to learn where everything is, who lives where (Only if you happen to catch them in their house) and even who works where.

Second thing was just several small things I noticed that I felt weren't explained at all. The ghosts that appear in your house at night? No idea whats going on there but if you walk into them they disappear. *Shrug* Also I could be wrong on this one and maybe I just missed the explanation but after you build a spirit barn it tells you to go in there and feed spirits daily to keep them happy, but I didn't see anything saying that they run away if you miss feeding them until I was leveling up my skill tree and one skill was "Spirits will stay one day longer in Spirit barn before running away if not fed."
Just little things like that I felt weren't properly explained.

All this being said I am enjoying the game I know I don't have many hours in game these were just a few frustrations I had and I didn't see much written about them.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me just preface this by saying:
I love this game. Enough for me to give my very first review ever.

Now, while I love this game, it isn't without it's flaws. Specifically some quality of life updates.

While there are 100 randomly generated islands that spawn on the map, resources are technically finite. You chop down a tree? It's gone forever. You hit a rock with or without ore? It's gone forever. You cut down some grass patches for crafting or to feed your full spirit barn? It's gone forever. I know this has been spoken about already by numerous other players of this game, but changing the resources to refresh in some sort of way is a quality of life update that I hope to see soon. I do believe I saw somewhere that the devs plan to fix/update this in the future, I am just hoping it is the near future.

Another thing is the rare ore, moonstones. There is only one moonstone per island and from what I have seen, they do not respawn. You can upgrade your skills in the skill tree of the game to increase your chances of getting a moonstone in the various chests scattered around the islands and in mines, but it's only a percentage. (I am not sure if save scumming works on the chests, I haven't tried that and haven't seen any threads/discussions about this) So far, the only way I've seen to farm these is by fishing up chests or it seems like moonstones are a rare drop from battling other spirits. Maybe in order to even it out a bit and keep the moonstones a rare resource, have them respawn, but only once a year in game? And maybe have it only be 50% of the islands instead every island on the map. Not too sure how to even that out.

Rambling thoughts aside, this game is fun to me. While my main concerns/complaints are what others have already said, I would definitely recommend this game to anyone who enjoys creature collecting, farming sims, card based battles, and building relationships with cute island residents (Magic Man my beloved). I want to experience this game at least twice and see the difference with the island generations and several other things I don't want to spoil anyone on. I'm excited to play more and see what the developers come up with next.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really love this game. There is plenty to do and explore throughout the game and can keep you occupied for a long time doing various things. A few things I’d like to see changed is the time in the game seems to go to fast. Clearing out wood, stone or grass depletes the stamina pretty quick. You can eat things to recover this alittle but I’d rather be able to put my character to sleep to recover the stamina and have that count against time and then be able to wake up and explore whatever time it is. Sometimes I’ll be right out of stamina by 12 and then meander around or just put my character to bed the rest of the day. There seems to be plenty of things to do. I’f I go to buy something in a shop, it would be nice to have everything in your chests linked so you don’t have to go grab a bunch of stuff and put it in your backpack. What ever cost of wood for example would just be reduced right from the chests. But there is plenty of room in the chests to store stuff and it’s a minor thing. It’s great that the devs seem to be on top of the game, always working to patch things or improve the game and you really can’t ask for more. Totally worth the price of the game and totally worth buying the DLCs to help support these devs. Fun game and A++++ for the entire development of it. Thanks a bunch!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was reallly looking forward to this game as I love monster collector games. This game is like a dumbed down Stardew Valley and the Monsters feel like a complete afterthought. In order to tame more monsters you have to craft a spirit barn, in order to store monsters in that spirit barn you need to craft each individual stable. You have to feed your monsters daily or they will run away. The monsters dont help you farm crops, or mine ores. They exist for the sole purpose of battling other monsters and there is almost no real incentive to do so. The harder challenge of this game is the different temples. The temples have high level monsters you need to beat, usually to get a cool tool help you do core game loop activities. The levels for these temple monsters is TOO high. lvl 15 for the first one and the XP gains from an average fight is practically nothing.

There are good things however like the soundtrack and the graphics are both really good.

If this game gets updated to have a more in depth Monster system this game could be good, if they do update the game over time I will change my review, but there is no reason to buy this if you already have stardew valley. 3/10\t\t\t\t\t
