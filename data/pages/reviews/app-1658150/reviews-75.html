\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long-time Stardew Valley fan that has a knack for all things witchy, I obviously had to get this game. And I don't regret it one bit. The pixel-style is super adorable, the characters are cute (and so far ALL dateable, as far as I can tell), you have 'spirits' to battle and to follow you around (think Pokemon but with magic and card play) and the gameplay is cozy and relaxed but still diverse enough not to get bored.

Anyone who's played Stardew Valley (or Harvest Moon back in the day) will find familiar gameplay that's been tweaked and added on to. Grow crops for yourself and your spirits, decorate your farm and house by crafting your own decoration, forage on your own island and the islands you discover, mine for resources in the mines (if you can find them) and collect and battle the spirits you can find. So if you're looking for a "new" Stardew Valley, this is the game for you. And if you're looking for a "new" Pokemon where you can also farm and do witchy things, this is also the game for you.

Personally, I am in LOVE and can't wait to play more. I played 7 hours immediately after downloading it so yeah... you could say I'm hooked!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun stardew/pokemon hybrid

+ Fun creature collector combat with slay the spire type card mechanics
+ Team building is fun and interesting
+ Buildling and resource gathering is pretty standard but well implemented
+ Overworld pixel art is consistently good

- Character portraits and personalities can be a bit rough
- Needing to maintain boxed spirits is annoying, and the resource to avoid this is limited
- Bit of a weird disconnect between your character being written as a kid and the game being a dating sim
- Dungeons and islands can be a bit repetitive

Stuff I would like to see:
Multiplayer! Both a battle/trading option, pokemon style, and/or a stardew valley style option with multiple players in a world could be pretty great.
Spirits having both a combat and noncombat ability instead of one or the other. It's annoying to switch between parties depending on which half of the game you're doing and would be cool to feel like the guys who are helping you win fights are also helping you interact.
More island variety/more unique scripted encounters on the islands. The core loop is good but the islands don't feel unique since they just have different spawns.
Better ways to grind to make the team you want - maybe other alchemists you could fight against that won't end the day if you lose.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA little under 9 hrs at the time of writing this but I have been enjoying it immensely. The other reviews saying it's similar to stardew is spot on in the cozy farm life sim aspect and it doesn't block you from much if you don't want to run around every day and be friendly while worrying about decaying friendship but I haven't gotten far enough to test it. I almost didn't get this due to reading the review from siliconera and how they kept comparing it up against the RF3 Special, don't get me wrong that was the first Rune Factory I played in the series back on ds and it holds a special place in my heart but that game is HEAVILY focused on the relationship aspect and with the others in that series the relationships end up being a key component to the end game story or post game content. This feels more like hey you can be a friendly neighbor, be a reclusive hermit on an island, or somewhere in between it's up to you.

I do like the fact that you're limited to only 3 at a time until you build the barn, makes you really think about the type matchups and the first spirit you choose. Reminds me a little of re:legend where you can only bring two with you. Another nice surprise that I found out was on occasion you can find spirit eggs that you can incubate when you have the barn.

Lastly, I will say and I don't know if this is on my end or if others have experienced this too, but playing this with the nano switch pro controller connected via BT not wired I do experience some occasional lag on command recognition, i.e: I'll have to double press a button for it to recognize it or the screen will stop/stutter for two seconds while I'm running across the map it doesn't happen too much to deter me from playing but it is something worth mentioning.

Edit: I will add also I noticed I have not seen a way for you to see the times/days a shop is open or closed only if you walk in and they're closed does it tell you the next time they will be open. I haven't unlocked the calendar yet so maybe that's how you can tell but that is definitely something needs to be implemented if it's not already.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an amazing game. I played for 6 hours straight and only put it down because I needed to eat.

The pixel art is so cute, the designs of the monsters are fun. It makes the process of a sandbox style farming/crafting game so much easier as someone who couldn't get through similar style games because I couldn't figure out the best way to approach it.

I have spent 2 of my currently 6 hour playtime starting to build a bridge system between the islands and it is so satisfying.

The card based deck building style is also something that I really enjoy. Good system that allows for a bunch of customisation and strategy in combining which cards are in your deck.

The characters are so cute and although I haven't gotten very far (Focusing on the bridges) I really enjoy interacting with the characters as well as the great diversity of backgrounds and identities is a welcome part of each character.

Thank you for making this great game, I can see it becoming my longest play time in not that long.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*100/10* I've been following this game since late June, and played the demo back in July. It was fun then and it's fun now.
If you're a fan of Stardew Valley, and Pokemon I highly recommend this game.
There is a lovely card-collecting turned-based combat system (which wasn't difficult to learn), and relationship/dating building in the game game. There is also crafting/building, fishing, farming and dungeon-delving. It's a fantastic LGBT+ inclusive indie game, with a really great price tag.
This is controller compatible, and doesn't feel clunky at all. You play as a cozy alchemist, and even though the game has a "timed" day, there doesn't seem to be any pressure to get things done immediately for all my folks with task anxiety. As a streamer, I could honestly just play this game for hours. The creatures, characters and art are all so cute and amazing. Thank you to the developers. Y'all are amazing.\t\t\t\t\t
