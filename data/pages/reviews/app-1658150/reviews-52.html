\r\n\t\t\t\t\t***Update 10/27*** The devs came through and fixed all my nitpicks, so am happy to fully endorse this game and recommend it as a must-play for fans of cozy games.

A promising little game that still needs polishing. Mostly it needs small quality-of-life stuff, such as better UI and easier ways to transport items around. However, there's on major issue: resources don't respawn.

That tree you cut down? Gone forever. And no, you don't get a seed from it to replant, it's just gone.
That mine you cleared of resources? Empty forever.
That grass you cut? Never going to regrow.

This is genuinely a vibe-killer. I don't want to be clearcutting forests and stripmining for ore in a cozy sim game. Give us the ability to replant trees, make trees able to spawn on their own as the seasons change, make grass regrow over time, and give us some way to close old mines and have a chance of finding new ones, and I'll be much happier with the overall experience.\t\t\t\t\t

Comprehensive Review | After 100+ hours because I liked the Spirits enough to ignore the issues

EDIT: This is my final review AFTER the recent patches. Fortunately, it's become a better game since release, but you could enjoy the same elements this game has elsewhere and be satisfied more there than here.

TL;DR, this game is a neutral rating, but Steam doesn't allow neutral so unfortunately the issues outweigh the positives. The fun peters off relatively fast, for reference, roughly after the 2nd season (Summer). Afterwards, you find yourself repeating misc tasks without a true purpose. Normally in a sim, you end up falling in love with something that keeps you going, but there's very little of that here. Mine was the spirits, but that was with me ignoring the parts of the game that just weren't fun enough.

Moonstone Island captures the attention of gamers who like elements from different genres such as creature collectors, farming, life-sim, but ultimately fails to deliver by being too spread out and not thorough enough with what was incorporated.

---

I was one of the people who were invested in this game. The game-play shown pre-release had elements mirroring life/farming sim-games, monster taming, etc looked awesome. The snippets leading up to the game's release had a lot of promise because it had a solid foundation for an early build until I found out it was actually the end-product. There are a few positives, but there are too many negatives that I can't recommend this to anyone, especially my own friends, when they could spend the time and money elsewhere and I know they wouldn't stick to one aspect like I did.

The Positives

1.\tArt - Gorgeous. From your character, the environment, & to the townspeople sprites, it looks fantastic.
2.\tMusic - Fairly good. There weren't any specific tracks that made me stop just to appreciate it, but each track fit whatever it was meant to portray.
3.\tPrice - matches the quality of the game. This game is definitely $20 USD worth of quality. If it were priced any higher, there would have been more issues.
4.\tCombat - The combat is MUCH more fleshed out than the other aspects. It starts off as a slow paced deck-builder that ramps into combo setups or synergies that makes it fun. The combat is what made me genuinely stick to the game for so long.

The Neutrals

1.\tCombat (again) - Since this IS a deck-builder, I expected more unique cards for the spirits, but there weren't any. The cards each spirit comes with conforms to their element which is fine, but it does dampen the combat aspect.
2.\tSpirits - since I already talked about the elements above, let's get into the spirits. They gave each spirit something unique in the form of their own passive traits. Some augment tool use, some give boons during combat. The reason this is a neutral instead of being a positive is because most of these traits are HOT garbage. Unless you like the design of the spirit, there's no reason to use them because their trait has no value and there's no unique card in their deck.

The Minor Issues

1.\tDungeons - the dungeons you encounter in the game are lackluster after your first three. Most of them just have time consuming puzzles that serve no real purpose while some are a freebie. One jarring thing is that they all have the same interior design. You'll be in the middle of a poison swamp, thundering storm, or volcanic wasteland but then step into ruins with simple green grass. It breaks the immersion.

2.\tUpgrade progression - Beginning ore is easy to come across to the point where it loses value. The real ore you need, the moonstone, is more elusive as there's one moonstone per island and if that island also has a dungeon, there's two, rarely three. In recent patches, they made it so the moonstone per island refreshes every season, so it helps lessen the trouble. However, you still need three ores to make 1 bar. You'll need over 108, possibly 156 (60+ spirit feeders, at least axe & hammer tool upgrades, extra tent), if you want to have all the quality of life improvements for the mid-to-late-game. The progression from copper to moonstone doesn't scale well. Pre-patch, I've clocked in just over 100 hours, but I've only managed to scrape together ~99 moonstones (26 islands, 24 dungeons that had upwards of 2-3 moonstones each and treasure maps). It is much easier now so you can regard this as a neutral point if you want to.

Bonus: There's a passive skill you can get to add a small chance of getting a moonstone from opening any new treasure chest. If you don't want to deal with searching, you can save-scum it and it takes under 2 min/chest.

The Major Issues

1.\tSkill trees - The game has different skills with their corresponding skill trees that augment your gameplay. For example, there's foraging, archaeology, farming, etc. At first, the customization seems really cool; it introduced quality of life changes that helped you with your day, but you can't get them all. You can only choose certain boons over the other and you're locked out of the other boons permanently. There's NO resetting the upgrades and frankly, it sucks! If you chose perks that helped with early game, they become completely useless late-game and if you chose perks that helped with late-game, you won't get much value from them in early through mid-game. Not to mention, some perks are just so niche that it feels like an absolute waste of space. It felt like they were trying to copy how some other famous games like Stardew Valley split skill progression, but failed to make their perks universally valuable.

2.\tControls & UI - Horribly optimized. For a game releasing in modern times, the controls have no quality of life. Ex: when placing fertilizer down, your character has to stop moving, register you putting the fertilizer down, perform the animation, then register that the plot is fertilized before you can move onto the next crop PER PLOT. You can't hold the fertilizer and spread it while running like you can do in nearly every other farming game. For inventory management, you can't move stacks of items with a few simple clicks or inputs, but instead you either click-and-drag or hold down right click to rapidly deposit items one-by-one. Similar concept with seeds. You have to buy seeds one-by-one with no rapid purchases. You could button mash, but why?

3.\tFarming/NPC's - Very bare-bones. The farming is pointless as the money you make isn't valuable. Nothing in the game is expensive enough to make you save and most of the crops you use are for buffing stats of your spirits. The community of the main island are sadly not very interesting. So much time is spent exploring that it's hard to remember they exist at times outside of when you want money. The only unique events are through dates that you must initiate, otherwise events will not happen which feels robotic. Most interactions are a few lines then you choose chat/joke/flirt to raise relationship points. It feels incredibly shallow despite the diverse cast. Usually sim-games have good community aspects, but I can barely remember their names even after all my hours because you do other things most of the time.

4.\tTime/Travel speed - the time per day is too fast. By the time you finish checking on crops then feeding your spirits, you've lost more than half your day. Then it takes a while to travel to another island and you have to speedrun w/e you were going to do before returning home. Every day ultimately pressures you into trying to rush and it burns you out once you're in the third season.

All in all, this game is fun in the first 20 hours or til Fall, and even then others saw flaws like I did. This game could have taken longer to develop and brushed up on the issues I stated and it would have been worth it even if it was priced double what it is now. Instead, this game feels like an early access release. \t\t\t\t\t
