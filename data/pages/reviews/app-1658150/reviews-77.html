\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game lacks any real goals or objectives. It's not quite a farming sim, not quite a dating sim, not quite anything. You can do all those things, but they don't functionally achieve anything. The player has no identity or self-expression, the NPCs indicate that you're an alchemist, but none of them know what that is.

All actions seem excessively drawn out with no intent as well, you do the action, see the animation and then the game pauses a couple seconds for you to confirm. The target randomly will switch when hammering rocks because they're the same distance and it can't figure out how to react, not because input altered anything. This is just is lacking so much polish and that looks to be because meeting a release date they set themselves.

There's also a peculiar progression system in place. There's no compass that really tells you how to actually progress, just vague ideas of it. Skills linked by a swirl lock others off in ways that just don't make a lot of sense. (Extra experience or easier taming? Those aren't linked traits, no reason for them to compete.) The quest to unlock the barn so you can have more than 3 monsters, requires you to catch a monster. So, you're not "allowed" more than 2 monsters until that happens. (You can, but have to let one go.) Also the game says you can't catch that monster until you have a specific skill that's locked behind invisible progress and no indicator on how to progress that bar.

The creatures are neat, the character art feels off heavily though. Good overall character composition with manga baby face slapped on every character. The card combat is okay, lacks some balance for sure though, landslide wins or losses and no inbetween. Just feels generic.

It lacks enough direction that I can't recommend it at this time. If you like this style of game, take a look though, probably will vibe more with certain people more than it does me. Seems rushed. If their first update fixes enough of this, then I'll revisit this review for sure. Gonna keep playing, maybe it'll click, I don't think it will though.\t\t\t\t\t

I found this game through twitter, never watched the trailer or any early gameplay vids and just waited for release date so I was coming into this game not knowing anything other than it being a farming sim and that you could romance characters. Needless to say, I was pleasantly surprised with the turn-based gameplay and deckbuilding!!

The art is gorgeous, the music beautiful and relaxing. I appreciate how the relationship-building system with characters is different than most farming games because it gives you more chances to really build relationships with them, although for some it might get a bit tedious especially if you're unlucky. And the FISHING... its the easiest minigame i've ever played i love it

Only cons I can think of: the game speed is a bit fast, and you kinda have to do some trial and error at first with the whole flying thing + dungeons + mines (I was passing out and falling a lot in my first few days). Would love to have more character customizations for the MC.

Overall, you can tell the creators really put a lot of love into this game and I highly recommend it!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo overall the game seems to have a fairly casual aspect.

Generally enjoyable, it's not something you can shut off for but at the same time you don't need to be too focused. Gameplay is smooth and the loop is clear.

Major aspects:
Farming: Useful for growing crops you don't want to explore for
Crops/Forageables: Better for consumption/crafting than sale
Fishing: Better for Sale
Exploring: Probably the best aspect currently, good variety of locations with added reasons to revisit islands
Combat: Pretty good, not a fan of card combats but this one is alright
Pets: Nice pets, don't like how one of the main needs is locked away behind a mildly obtrusive quest
Housing: Easy to get new decorations

Most early game questions/tutorials are hidden in the villager quests which is a bit annoying but mildly reasonable as well. The teaching aspect is fine, the "you won't know until you know" aspect isn't.

One item is a major requirement and hidden behind a villager request:
(mild spoilers)


To get a spirit barn to house more than three spirits you need to tame, not have but tame a spirit above a specific level. If you already have 3 spirits and if you already have them above the level, too bad. Time to abandon one of them that you'll never get back (yes you can catch a new one but just feelsbad) so you can catch a spirit above the specified level. This is required for game progress as well. This really should have been locked behind having captured three spirits.


Games is fun but needs a tiny bit more polish.\t\t\t\t\t

Now Moonstone Island is a cozy monster tamer/farm-sim, so if you're a player that enjoys the monster tamer genre, farm sims, home customization, and relationships, this game was literally made for you. I will not do this game injustice by comparing it to other titles.
I'm a HUGE fan of monster tamers and farm sims, and this game is a wonderful marriage between the two. Seeing how well these genre's can work together, I only hope we can see more in the future as I am having literally the best time.
Moonstone Island is, hands-down, my current all-time favorite game. You can enjoy the game as a casual player or player who wants a challenge. The battles are turn-based card battles, similar to Ooblets and other indie titles yet-to-be-named in development. The game is easy to follow and immensely charming. I enjoy the writing and relationships much more than I thought I would. I really like how building relationships take a good amount of time (unless you're actively gifting and chatting everyday haha).
I also appreciate that there's no real pressure and that it's a go-at-your-own-pace kind of game, that you're open to do as you like.
The monsters are awesome, too. I love the designs, their move-sets, the battle mechanics, and sprites. It's been enjoyable to tame monsters and run into Holo Spirits (shiny-equivalent). I really love the idea of keeping Spirits in a barn to take care of and receive additional resources from for crafting. I really think the taming mechanic is refreshing, versus using a compact capture item. Makes the gameplay feel resourceful and puts every crop to use, whether for crafting, to tame, make potions or feed your monsters.
I also love and appreciate all the work put into Moonstone Island, seeing as there is so much to look forward to. You can decorate your home with such a long list of furniture items! You can even build a greenhouse and build your own home (which there are recipes for different types of homes)!
