\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played on Steam Deck. It's very good. If Stardew Valley is the gold standard in this genre, this is the first game I've played that deserves to be mentioned in the same breath, and the addition of creature collecting and deck-building makes it much more than a well-made clone. 100% worth buying.

Good
  • Great game loop with enough to do that it didn't feel monotonous after 30 hours, but not so much to do that it provoked anxiety.
  • S+ art and music
  • The deck-building is light but varied enough to remain interesting after several hours
  • The island's characters have a ton of personality, if that's your jam
  • Exploration and traversal -- far away areas actually feel a little scary early on so it's satisfying to go back when you're stronger
  • The skill trees actually force you to make meaningful decisions O:
  • I love the magic man
  • Temples add a nice layer of progression/storytelling that made me want to keep playing

Feedback for devs
  • Object selection when using a controller feels imprecise. For example when trying to harvest spirit poop (ðŸ¤­), it is frustratingly easy to accidentally select the adjacent spirit. This applies any time there are two interactive objects in close proximity. It just feels "off." This is the most frustrating thing about the game and seems like it's probably fixable.
  • Object placement also feels irritating sometimes, in that it's sometimes impossible to place things in the exact place you want when it feels like it should be possible. It tripped my minimal OCD tendencies often enough that I gave up on decorating my home, which is a shame because they clearly put a lot of work into creating assets with which to decorate.
  • The tool wheel needs some kind of selection/input buffer. Whenever I bring up the wheel to select a different tool, and I tilt the stick to select that tool, there's at least a 50% chance that the stick returning to its original position will cause my selection to change. It's another very minor thing, but it happens so often that it's annoying.
  • There is too much stuttering when moving around the map. It's not bad enough to make me want to stop playing, but it's very noticeable.
  • Potions: There are handful that are always appealing (e.g. extra resources, night vision, stopping time, showing psychic storms on map), but I never felt compelled to make any of the creature/combat-related ones. Even after turning up the difficulty, there was just never a time I thought, "I wish I could waste 1 energy to remove all the burn from my Marsha."
  • Concoctions: you play as an "alchemist" in this game, but the bread and butter of alchemy in this world (concoction making) feels superfluous and is the least interesting part of the game. This should have been the system with the most potential for whacky, game- and world-changing/breaking stuff. Instead, concoctions are merely a mechanism for temporarily boosting creature stats that are more time consuming to make that they're worth. If there's any continued development going on, I hope the devs are thinking about ways to make this system more fun and rewarding to engage with.
  • I suspect the only reason potion-making and concoction-making are separate systems is because the latter has a time limitation (you have to wait for concoctions to brew). I don't think there's a good reason to forcefully limit how many concoctions a player can make in a day (it's just one more reason to never use the system) so imo the systems should be combined and creating things should be instantaneous.
  • Yo, why are you so obsessed with bridges? So many dungeons were just tedious to complete because I'd first have to acquire 100+ planks, then very slowly place them one-by-one to cross dozens of long gaps in a single dungeon. I wish dungeons had more puzzle elements and significantly fewer resource roadblocks.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game feels shallow and the replayability is next to nil unless you enjoy decorating a little housing/farm area that ultimately doesn't matter or filling it with hundreds of spirits that ultimately don't matter or grinding their levels up which also doesn't really matter once you get through your first year and beat the main storyline.

The relationships in game are...nice...but they feel like window dressing and are equally as shallow compared to other games like Stardew Valley or Coral Island.

The first year in game is a grindy panicky rush and then there's just.....nothing else to really shoot for beyond the cosmetic stuff that I mentioned previously.

It's good for a single play through but that's about it.

The devs do seem to be updating the game at a pace that tracks for a smaller studio but even the DLC packs feel somewhat lacking and like they're just delaying stuff until the game gets released on Switch next year.

I wound up seeing/catching all the spirits in game in my first year, maxed out my talent trees, bought all the housing/farm upgrades, and dipped into the relationship stuff a bit but it all....didn't really feel very filling or satisfying at all.

It wasn't boring and it wasn't terrible, it was just bland and more of the same like a number of other games that have tried to emulate Stardew Valley over the years.....the magic just isn't there.

Speaking of which, the community is what ruined this game for me and what left a bad taste in my mouth.

I've witnessed the devs and their moderators actively discriminating against neurodivergent and disabled types in their discord repeatedly and have seen them treating those folks rather badly, all whilst high fiving each other, and encouraging other similar bad behaviors.

Which is ironic seeing as how one of the characters in game basically is neurodivergent/disabled...but I guess that's all window dressing too.

It reminds me of the same way the Arcane Waters community was acting before that game and community nose dived.

If you can look past that stuff and you're down for a quick farming fix before better games with more funding and better developers update or come out with their new games, then sure this game is for you.

And if you can't...then you're not missing much and you can just relegate this game to the "I'm going to watch this on twitch only instead of spending my own time and money on it" category.\t\t\t\t\t
