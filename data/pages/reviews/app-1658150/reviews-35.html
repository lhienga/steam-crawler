\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn honest review!

I heard about this game from a friend and from Twitter, so I instantly added Moonstone Island to my wishlist and excitedly waited for its release. Initially, I wasn't sure what to expect when I first started the game.

If you're an avid fan of both Pokemon and Stardew Valley, then I think you'd really find this game enjoyable as it has similar features of farming materials and collecting/battling spirits. I am someone who finds grinding/farming enjoyable and the simple and understandable mechanics allow for hours of farming as a form of stress relief. I also really like the turn-based gameplay when you battle with spirits and find this feature very enjoyable.

Despite the fact that there are a couple of bugs here and there, it doesn't affect your gameplay whatsoever. I think one thing that this game does lack is a plot/storyline. I would like to know more about the alchemist we play as and even the lore/background story of Moonstone Island and its residents. Although we can go on dates with a resident of our choice (which is a great feature), I would still like to know more about the story behind Moonstone Island.

Nevertheless, there were a few things that I had expected in this game but weren't a feature. Unlike Pokemon/Stardew Valley, there's no character customization of our alchemist nor can we name ourselves. I'm not sure if we already have a name, but if there is one then please correct me on that. We also can't name the spirits we collect. There are also no seasonal events/festivals that you could participate in. It makes me feel a little disconnected from Moonstone Island and its residents.

One thing that I do dislike in the game is the inability to move buildings unless you have to wait and craft for another one. This process usually requires moonstones and moonstones take some time to collect and farm for them as they are a rare resource.

Overall, Moonstone Island is a fun, enjoyable game for everyone and has huge potential for improvement. I even bought the DLC for additional furniture as I love gathering materials and decorating our home. I love playing this game, especially after a long day of work. So, I can't wait to see this game develop further and flourish.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of my new favorites! Definitely Pokemon x Stardew Valley vibes. Lots of flexibility to meet how you want to play and what you want to emphasize (exploring, resource gathering, creature collection, farming, dungeons/combat, crafting, decorating, relationship building, etc.). The deck-building combat system is very fun with great balance that feels satisfying and rewarding. Can easily sink lots and lots of hours into this game :-) it also runs well on Steam Deck!\t\t\t\t\t
