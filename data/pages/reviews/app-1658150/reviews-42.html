\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is cute and amusing, but every feature is lacking depth.

If they had a "meh" review option or "only worth on sale", that's what I would declare it at this time.

It is like an early access game, but not due to bugs, but due to content.
It has tons of potential, but not a single feature feels complete.

  • NPCs are boring and flat
  • Farming is a few plants, a few seeds, done
  • Combat starts well, but immediately is redundant, worse than Pokemon
  • Spirits (monsters you collect) do not feel unique or special
  • You're an alchemist, yet too 12 hours until I got to brew anything

So, good concept that has promise. I'd recommend if it improves more.
I would be willing to recommend the game if it was early access and there were signs that the devs did not feel satisfied with the game.
But it is considered released and therefore, lacking\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI watched a couple people play the demo of this game a while back but was unable to play it myself at the time, so I obviously jumped at the chance to play it for myself when it finally came out and... I am completely obsessed. No exaggeration, I've already completed every dungeon, every temple, collected almost every spirit (and two holographic ones!), and am in a very committed relationship with Waldo the magic man. This game is literally everything I wanted and more, with very simple and easy-to-understand overworld mechanics, but with complex and exciting battles mixed in to keep you on your toes. The pacing is great, the characters and story as a whole is delightful and fun, and 80 hours in, I'm still not bored! After beating the main story, I am now spending my time decorating my wizard tower, filling my spirit barn, and getting full hearts with all the villagers, and I'm having a blast! I do have a few complaints relating to small bugs (such as an invisible wall in one of the dungeons that prevents you from accessing an optional chest, and a treasure map that didn't lead to anything) but while I do think that a few quality of life changes here and there would help with a lot of the smaller issues I have, I largely I have nothing but love for this game, and absolutely recommend it to anyone who enjoys this genre! Super excited for the Halloween update as well :)\t\t\t\t\t

The game starts out promising but then it just.....introduces way too much. Between crafting, creature collecting, resource management, relationship building, exploring. There's just....way too much to do in too little of a day. The game puts too much emphasis on "if you dont take care of your crops theyll die" and yeah you can craft a sprinkler but you need resources for that which are.......kinda lacking. The creature collecting is also very limiting as you'll encounter a ton of creatures but you can only have 3 at a time, but then you can build a stable......but wait! When you build the stable that's just not enough you need more resources to have each creature their own separate stall..... it just feels like a never ending resource collecting when....you don't necessarily have the time to do so. There's 50 other things catching your attention and at the end you play for a few hours and don't feel like anything was accomplished.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's decent.

Moonstone Island is a cool game that blends elements from several genres, almost to the point of making up a whole new genre. It's simple, simpler than titles of respective genres from which it pulls inspiration, so don't expect battles as immersive as Pokemon or farming as engaging as Stardew Valley.

My one gripe with the game is that it's "slippery". The "hit boxes" around everything seem to overlap which easily causes mistakes when choosing objects with which to interact.

Playing this on Steam Deck was fun. I haven't tried it on PC and probably will not, but I bet using the mouse nullifies the above gripe.

Overall I recommend this game!\t\t\t\t\t
