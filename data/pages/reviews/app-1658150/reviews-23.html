\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMoonstone Island is a cute, fun, and fantastical game set in a post-apocalyptic world where “something” caused the world to shatter into many sky islands. It’s implied (but I don’t think explicitly stated) that this is Earth.

There seems to be a lot of hidden lore to this game. A lot of lore bits I’ve gotten from going on dates with the characters. Now I beat the “main story” within the first year so the romancing aspect took a bit of back seat for a time and by the time I got to the last boss I wasn’t even entirely sure why I was fighting it other than it broke Waldo (the best character in the game). Which is enough reason for me to horribly murder it in my opinion.

The main gist of the game is that you’re the most recent inheritor of a long lineage of spirit tamers called “Alchemists.” You primarily tame spirits and generally keep people safe with some alchemy on the side. The general drives of the game are:
Complete Quests from townsfolk and parents
Romance townsfolk
Finish your Pokédex… I mean spirit research…
Beat the dungeons and temples
Beat the last boss
Make things pretty. (Lots of customization)

-Con’s:

The farming in the game is honestly tedious and boring without the greenhouse. I tried it for 2 seasons in the game then stopped.

Some of the characters come off as pretty flat. I haven’t romanced every character in the game so I don’t know if those dialogues add more them but with some of the dates with a few of the characters I’m not really interested in further dialogue with them.

The dungeons all have the same boss. They’re slightly different because depending on the island you find them on it changes their element but that’s it.

From spirit to spirit there isn’t any difference other than passives and looks.

That’s it that’s literally it. That’s all the bad things I have to say about the game.

-Pros

It’s absurdly fun, I could not put this game down basically for the first week of owning it. I have a good bit of time invested into this game, and I’ve been playing since release (basically). It is so much fun and it feels so much like booting up Pokemon Emerald or Friends of Mineral Town for the first time. Those two games defined my childhood and this captures the feeling of both of those games while being unique and separating itself as a standalone that can hold its own.

The spirit designs are great. They range from really silly, to really freaking cool, to…why?… So there’s something for everyone!

The combat is probably my favorite card battler I’ve ever played. I generally am not a big fan of card battlers. There are far too many that I’ve played where building your deck feels grueling and difficult. This is up there with Slay the Spire with how fun and rewarding it is to build your deck in the early game and by late game building because very easy and you can really edit your decks to your liking once you’ve put in the time to get to the end game.

The puzzles are simple but fun. This game is targeted towards almost all ages. So the puzzles can’t be too difficult but it still feels good to solve them. Honestly my least favorite dungeons are the ones that are just a gauntlet of battles back to back it’s much more fun to explore.

The island exploration feels so cool. It’s so interesting going to see how all the islands work. There are a ton of island that all vary greatly and my biggest gripe with this is once I found them all I just wanted more!

The creators are constantly engaging with the community to see what they want and what they’re looking for.

The art style is very fun and cute. Honestly I’ve overall grown tired of the pixel-art style that’s everywhere nowadays, but this game really holds its own. Seriously the artists they have on this have done a great job and made so many good decisions with the direction they went.

The characters on Moonstone Island are (for the most part) great. I know one of my cons was that some fell flat but others are absolutely incredible. I want you to find out for yourself but Ossono is probably my favorite with Gaiana being my least favorite (out of the ones I’ve romanced so far).

I absolutely love this game and I want everyone to play it. My biggest hope for this game is that they release more islands and more towns with more NPC’s and more bosses. Just more of the same stuff and maybe another Element. If the devs do end up reading this, please release expanded content with all that asked for as paid DLC you’ve done great work and deserve to be paid for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've put 15 hours in, and will likely still play the game some more, but I'm not sure I'd recommend it as it is currently. I was really into it after the first 5 hours, but then it just... dies out on you and I found myself a lot more aimless.

All the parts of a great game are here, it just needs more time in the oven and some change in direction. This is one of those games that, yeah, it technically playable, but feels like it's lacking polish and more content.

The good:
- It's cozy and looks great
- Lots to explore
- The card battling system is interesting and I felt I could customize in ways I wanted.
- The spirits are varied and cute
- Quick and easy tutorial before you jump right into the game
- The NPCs that exist are interesting and I found one I wanted to date.

The bad:
- The controls need work. Why I have to hold Q to bring up a wheel menu that I then need to scroll through is beyond me. Let me hold Q and see all my items in a list that I can pick. I don't need the wheel unless there is some weird way to quickly select from it, which I don't think there is.
- There is not quick key to get to the quest screen
- The map needs more detail
- To complete each temple you have to bring specific items. You won't know what items you need until you get there and to progress you'll need to leave and come back unless you're lucky enough to just be holding things. I was not. Tell me upfront or don't lock progression behind items. Flying back and forth is not fun.
- It feels a little slow. Sprinting wasn't explained to me, but I found out about it online. Why it isn't the default I don't know. Battles are also a little slower than I'd like. Even with fast mode on.
- Needing moonstones for everything is a pain. They are plentiful, but everything good requires them and I don't want to spend days hunting for just them. Remove the requirements from stuff and use gold instead, which I have way too much of.
- Having to craft charms is more annoying than cute. The high leveled monsters are enough to keep me away, don't add artificial stamina draining that I need to waste an inventory slot to prevent.
- The flower/farming system is strange. I'm not sure I like that they're all over the place. It takes away the point of farming and I found I didn't want to sell them because they all had uses. Potions need to be sold somewhere and the plants need to be more farming focused.

Anyway, I think I'll come back to this game in a year or so since they're still doing updates.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely adore this game, before you think its just like stardew it is not. It has so many of its own charms, the gameplay is very endless. The story is very different with more quest based gaming, the dungeon crawling and creature crawling make it alot more inescapable. so heres my pros and cons of the game

PROS:
- the artstyle is absolutely beautiful
- minimal to no loading screens
- runs extremely well never experienced glitches or lag
- very inclusive characters and extremely charming
- very fun pokemon fighting mechanics
- sound effects and music are beautiful
- very easy to get into, nothing is confusing
- quests are more character involved rather than just being super straightforward
- decoration options are amazing
- different character sprites for different areas (still somewhat limited)
- dating each character is sooo much fun, its a mini dating sim lol

CONS:
- the characters could have more depth and dialogue
- being able to snoop through characters rooms etc
- the map telling u what things mean and whos house is whos etc
- more character customization, different outfits etc
- better farming mechanics, more reason to farm (hitbox makes it a little difficult)
- more food options so characters could have more unique likes and dislikes

but overall rating 8.7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game on sale and it was worth the price! There is A LOT to do. In the time I have played, I still haven't seen a fraction of what it has to offer(only on my first save). Overall 9/10. It is grindy and relaxing at the same time. The biggest complaint would be the villagers' backstories. I have fairly high relations with everyone but the Magic Man and Cleo and while they all do have different personalities I am wishing for something more. Also, you can't undo garden plots that you place down (or I haven't figured it out yet) and you can't place any paths over them; it is a small thing but it messes with the freedom of decorating your farm and such. Maybe this will change in time as the developers are pretty active. I believe this game can get so much better with time and fixes. It has a pretty solid foundation to add and deepen the aspects of this game. For the price it has so much play-ability and replay-ability as the maps are randomly generated each save I believe. I would highly recommend this game to anyone. It is slow starting out but the more you do the better it gets, so please give it a fair chance of more than a few hours. This game isn't meant to be finished in 10-20 hours. The music and art are also fantastic! Tip for new players: glowing grass = moonstones! every island has one moonstone a day; get a moonstone scythe/glider asap . I will be posting another update at like 50 hrs or so! Happy playing!\t\t\t\t\t
