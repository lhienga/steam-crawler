\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have fallen in love with this game so much. I intend to buy it when it comes out to Switch as well. It is a rather odd mix of farming sim, deckbuilder and monster tamer yet it works and combat is fun.

The world is vibrant, beautiful and colorful. The NPCs are lovely and the Spirit designs are delightful. I am rather hooked on Poison types and Psychics are awesome. Though Octopup might be my number one atm because he is best boy.

Combat takes very little time to get used to I think but I love how it's handled. Armor CAN be annoying but Burn and Poison are awesome. I adore that you can have your buddies follow you around.

I'm working on more decorating to make my homestead look cozy. And I know this is kind of for a lot of games like this but I wish we could use the furniture. Just a minor gripe in the grand scheme of things.

All in all I don't have very many problems with the game apart from the tiny RNG of Villager chatting. I'm greatly looking forward to the announced Valentine update and I think having sweets based Spirits would be awesome like an Earth type chocolate covered strawberry. This game is an easy buy for anyone looking for a new monster tamer game. It's well worth the asking price and it is being given regular updates and love from the team.

EDIT: I am making a small edit with a few things I would love to see; maybe an option for spotting Moonstones easier. Even with Rablit I cannot for the life of me spot some of these things without maybe chopping down every single tree which just leaves sad looking islands. It's worst for me on the Electric biome. I don't know why it's so tough for me and it IS a minor gripe.

And for decoration as things are being added with patches and free updates; maybe false walls! The basement is already huge with the second expansion. And false walls can lead to some nice ideas for room designs. And maybe some extra clothing options for the Alchemist.

All in all these little things would be great to see for me. But the devs are already giving great updates and they're doing a great job with actual communication!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game but overall, it feels half-baked. The devs are clearly working hard to address concerns with the game, but where one problem is solved another appears. I played at launch and stopped, then tried to give it another chance and all the sudden there was an abundance of lag and glitching.

Interacting with NPCs is like talking to cardboard boxes; it feels like Moonstone has no life to it. This is reflected in the main storyline and quests, where it feels like there's no incentive to these actions other than the game just telling you it's what you're supposed to do. It's really unfortunate because there's so much potential in terms of the world and characters but are doing the bare minimum.

The game has many different facets and none of them are executed particularly well. Farming is tedious and slow to get into because you have to collect seeds before they're sold at the store, and there's no real incentive to level up tools. Fishing feels like it was just randomly thrown in because it's expected of 'cozy games' like this one. The spirits designs are cool but battling feels unrewarding and the dungeons feel like they lack life and innovation. Decorating is fun, but some items are locked behind purchasing DLC, which feels a bit unfair given the game itself needs more work. So on and so forth.

Maybe if some major adjustments are made to the game I'll give it another try, but as it stands I would not recommend this game to anyone which is why I have to give it a thumbs down despite some great aspects, like the concept, visuals and music.\t\t\t\t\t
