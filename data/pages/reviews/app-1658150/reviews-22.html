\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game a lot as I came from different farm-style games like Stardew Valley and Sun Haven, I'm not sure if the devs will read this but I would like to provide some fair criticism because there is a lot of wasted potential that could be perhaps easily fixed.

First of all, the game is beautiful, I love the art I love the spirits, I'm a professional pixel artist myself and the art direction is nice overall but there is some lacking gameplay features could use some tweaking, such as:

- There is no use for gold except for some decorations and upgrades, I don't see why limit the player in doing everything every time like as the world cannot live without the player. You guys should reward players that for example, earn gold with fishing, and allow they to buy basic materials like wood, ores, etc. The world would feel more alive if the player could have access to lots of useful items, materials, and consumables making creating farms or grinding for gold a lot more meaningful.

- We need more dialogues, more random conversation, curiosities from the locals, and more events in depth with the character's personality and lore. For now, the conversations are limited to a button click and the only dialogue that exists is inside the Dates (which also can be used to more in-depth dialogues) which makes interaction with the locals a bit artificial most of the time.

- Moonstones are hard to find, Ingots take a lot of time to mine and smelt, and after you exhaust a cavern/mine you have to find another. Everything you craft uses an enormous amount of resources which becomes rapidly scarce and slows down the gameplay a lot. Things could use less material to make, this would give more time for the player to focus on things like the story, the locals, and the spirits.

- The spirits are lovely, I think the battle system is really fun and the thing that I love to do the most is finding new spirits to tame, but, the Barn is incredibly expensive, you lost a lot of spirits early game because you cannot store any since you can keep only three, I don't see why the player shouldn't start with a bigger limit or perhaps a small barn to keep like 10 or 15 creatures. Let me catch the creatures, please! Further, I don't see why the nests should be consumable on the egg hatching and the fact that you have to put the small wooden spirit-eating thing in the barn is another extremely time-consuming thing considering could you have like 80 slots, these things could come with the barn. All mechanics that makes the player spend more time gathering resourses for things they already have and that will not provide any advantage to the player will always drag the gameplay and feel meaningless.

- Consumables are not useful, they are rare and expensive and the effects could be better, most of the time they could double the bonus and last the entire day, to make their use meaningful for a great grinding/farm day.

I tried to give as much feedback as I could, I hope one of the devs read it, and I hope this game finds even more success in future releases I would love to see more improvements in the future patches, either way, thanks for your time, wish you guys the best. Regards!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is like a mix of Stardew + Pokemon + Zelda + deck-building game...so lots of fun :) I find that because there's these different modes to the game, I spend a lot more time playing it as I can change my current goal/gaming style to my mood.

*Art & Style*
The art style is very cute and apt for a cozy vibe.
The music is average, and I like that the options allow you to keep the sound effects + ambient noise but tone down the background music so I can play my own lofi/gaming music.

*Story & Character*
The character designs are OK, from what I've seen so far. (Progress in relationships is slow, and I'm not really into this aspect of games myself). Dialogue is a little limited, but I like the matchmaking quests (where you match Spirits to the characters based on clues).
The background/story is very simple, but I think it's because the game is more focused on exploration, so I didn't mind. This game isn't a plot-driven game, from what I've seen so far.

*Gameplay*
The technical aspects to the gameplay is pretty intuitive and customisable which is good.
The deck-building aspect involves a bit of strategy with how you use up your deck adjustment resources, but it's simple enough that you don't have to be a strategy whiz to play well and enjoy the deck-building mode.
The farming sim aspect is relatively simple but effective. I like that the recipe for sprinklers is accessible earlier in the game for those of us who like to automate the process (Y)
The exploring and treasure-collecting aspect is very fun. I enjoyed the way that there are increasing difficulties of the islands as you explore, the different biomes and Spirits you encounter, the rare Psychic storms and the treasure maps. Even the way that some of the gameplay works isn't explained so you have to experiment how things work in the game (and you don't get badly punished!) was fun. I think there was a lot of thought put into this aspect of the game, which I really appreciated. One of such things that I really liked was that there was early access in the game to the flute, which you can use once a day to teleport back home.
The potion-making aspect of the game is OK? I'm not big into experimenting/designing my own recipes but I enjoyed that there was a way of using the produce to assist with exploring/taming Spirits besides selling them.
The decorating aspect of the game is average. Some of the items are very cute, but I found most of them were very basic in theme or design. I liked that you could find lots of crafting recipes from exploring, rather those being rare and then only getting access to limited decor per day from the furniture shop (like in Animal Crossing).

*Price*
Very worth it!!

Overall, I would give this game a 10/10 for its different modes, affordability and good game design. Highly recommend for those who enjoy cozy games! Honestly, I'm glad this wasn't a Switch game because then I'd be hooked forever on this game.

Suggestions if there's room:
- an option to customise how the main character looks. Doesn't have to be the actual person, but even just outfits would be great :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPokemon Stardew Valley is an inept description for Moonstone Island. This game isnt particularly like either of them. Its a collectible monster combat-focused deck builder that has cozy elements (such as farming and friendship) which support the combat. You farm to buff and heal your spirits, and villager quests encourage your engagement in the spirit related mechanics. This is the mindset with which this game should be viewed.

The combat mechanics are great! Keep in mind, Im not a massive deck builder person, and dont have much to compare with other than my minimal experience with Pokemon, Digimon, and that one time my Dad tried to teach me Magic: the Gathering. I think the combat is fun, tricky, and allows for lots of versatility in terms of builds. Status conditions feel a bit overpowered, but maybe once I start using some on my team Ill feel a bit differently. The armor and bash/dizzy mechanics are very fun though, and provide a layer beyond most damage card. I might choose Mind Spike over Memorize because it reduces armor, even if its weaker.

On the other hand, the cozy mechanics are In theory, very fun. I like the idea of farming to train and care for your spirits, rather than the typical Fantasy Capitalism of the cozy farming genre. (Not that fantasy capitalism is bad, but its nice to have a change of pace!) I like that you gather seeds by exploring, rather than by purchasing them. I really really like the EXP plants, and Im excited to see how well they eliminate the end game grind.

That said, I hate actually doing any farming, specifically because of how object placement works. I have the same issue with decorating and base building, but at the very least most objects can be picked up and moved around. Seeds? If you place them wrong, youre stuck until theyre full grown, and its way too easy to misplace a seed. You cant place seeds on the tile youre on, and for whatever reason, aiming at which tile you want to place a seed on is incredibly difficult? I have the same issue with decor/object/chest/building placement, and its even worse because of the limited range that your player character can actually reach. You have to be very close to the tile you want to place the item on, but cant be standing on that tile. Its frustrating, and has led to me basically demolishing and resetting my fields for farming so that I can keep them more organized even when I misclick.

A lot of people complain about the friendship mechanics in this game. Personally, I love them! I dont typically engage in friendship mechanics (and when I do its because Im getting something out of it), but I find them quite fun here! Theres a large element of rng, which is probably the draw for me. You dont really need to care about the characters, but when you interact with them, talk or joke a few times to raise those friendship points. I like that you can see the numeric increase or decrease on a success or failure. There arent a lot of characters either, which definitely helps too.

My biggest issue with the game is the threat of finite resources. Specifically wood, stone, coal, copper, iron, and fiber which are most of the resources in the game. Copper and Iron can be purchased directly, while wood, stone, coal, and fiber can be purchased indirectly (i.e. buy a sapling, grow a tree, chop it down, get wood). This technically makes these resources infinite, but theres definitely frustration in the fact that youll eventually have to pay for them. Yes, every island has a mine, lots of trees, lots of stones, and massive plains of grass but at no point do they ever respawn naturally. Theyre so abundant its unlikely youll ever run out, but theres the threat that they do, which deters long-term gameplay, as well as hesitance when crafting that can hinder progression.

To add onto this, another frustrating progression hinderer is the Ando Acolyte skill in the skill tree. This requires at least two archaeology points, and at the end of each dungeon, gives you ten more max stamina. This means an extra 300 max stamina, total. If you clear the dungeon and claim your stamina before having this skill, however, you cant go back and claim the extra stamina from the skill. You can potentially be locked out of an entire 300 stamina (For context, you start with 300). This is a massive issue, and one that made me debate resetting my save file. I will never have those 20 stamina I missed from clearing two dungeons, and its incredibly frustrating -- especially since you need to clear dungeons to get the points to get the skill. (A workaround for this, by the way, is to clear the temples first, and fight wilderness bosses that arent in dungeons. Or just dont claim the stamina from the dungeons until you get the skill). Theres one or two other skills that make this offense, but nothing quite so egregious.

With that out of the way, lets talk about something I do like! Holos! Basically, this games equivalent of shiny pokemon. Like shiny pokemon, holographics are a visual difference, and thats it. Theyre way more common than shinies, but I have yet to encounter one. (Thankfully -- I really dont wanna find one above my taming level limit!) Still, the concept of their existence is going to make me plug so many more hours into this game.

To end us off, heres some smaller points I dont feel super positively or negatively about. Firstly is the number of spirits you can keep at once -- 120 (or technically 123, including your party). Its enough to have one of every species (69), but not two, which is kind of a let down but also kind of fine. I like that you can have multiple bases, and decorate them however you like -- and I love that all of your houses link to the same interior, so you dont have to shuffle resources around between bases. I wish fishing was explained better in game, because I currently have only ever fished to get a specific fish for a request. I wish there were more short term quests, or at least a help wanted board like in Stardew Valley because I often feel as though I have no direction and Im just waiting for things to happen.

All in all, I definitely recommend giving it a try! It has its issues, but I find myself enjoying the game when I play it. I can binge it for hours on end and still be having fun. I may be slightly obsessed with it at the moment. I may be wishing the wiki page had more details. Still! Play it! Very fun! and worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t
