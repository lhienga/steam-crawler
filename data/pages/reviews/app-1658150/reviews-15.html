\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun little twist on the genre with deckbuilding/pokemon flavors. My only complaint is that the game doesn't feel good to play on either controller or MnK. A lot of the UI navigation feels clunky (I'm looking at you item wheel) and some things feel much better on one than the other but neither feel completely comfortable. If you can get past that (I'm still swapping between the two 21 hours in,) it's definitely pretty solid and enjoyable.

Biggest pain point for me personally is probably the item wheel you use to select your tools. As you progress you end up not being able to fit every item on the wheel so you're constantly swapping stuff in and out of the wheel (sometimes accidentally.) Your means of transportation between the islands upgrades from a balloon to a broom to a glider but you still need all 3 for traversal through dungeons leaving a lot of clutter in the tool wheel.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTentative thumbs up. It took me a LONG time to get into this game. I don't know why I pushed past the boredom, irritation but about 20 hours in I started having fun regardless. This will be a very negative review for a thumbs up lol.

TL;DR I hate everything but battling but battling is fun enough for me to keep playing.

The interact highlights (I don't know what to call it - eg. the squares that highlight when you use your tools, characters highlighting to talk to them) not showing up was almost game breaking for me, it fills me with rage lol.

The tool wheel is INSUFFERABLE. It's such an annoying way to change tools and the number of times I accidentally unequip instead is another rage point in this cozy game.

The days are extremely short and that alone was the reason I almost threw in the towel. Even set to max length trying to do ANYTHING resulted in a day ending on me so paired with the highlight path whatever you call it issue I was more stressed than anything. It took SO long to get enough traction to actually play a day and it's still extremely limiting and irritating.

Paired with all of the above I cannot stand farming. Takes forever to highlight, changing tools and bam the day is gone. Thank goodness sprinklers are easy to craft.

And with all the above I am completely disinterested in any social elements of the game. While I like the way chatting is done as it eliminates repetitive responses, it takes so looonnnngg to gain half a heart I just can't care when the days are so short, the idea of wasting a day to talk to a character after approaching them from six angles just so they highlight to talk to them is just not worth the stress lol.

Annnndd I haven't bothered to decorate, again due to the day length and pathing though I do know there is a potion to freeze time inside for the day so that is likely the only thing I'll do beyond battling and exploring before I move on from this.

But exploring, battles, and leveling up my creatures is FUN. One I unlocked better cards, gained skill, had tougher battles and began building my team I became a bit hooked on it. I'm finding the dungeons fun, I love exploring as far as my broom will take me, accidentally landing on an island wayyy above my level and dying pitifully (but sometimes I'm able to hold on long enough to die smug!).

If I were not into card games I would not enjoy this at all but I am so if you are (or you don't hate the above) have fun!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat do you get if you mix Pokemon, Wizard101, and Stardew Valley? This game. :) There is a bit of eye-burning light-on-dark (dark mode) text, but none of it is essential to read. You can look away. The essential text is all disability-friendly (if your disability makes you sensitive to inverted contrast). The gameplay involves planting crops, watering them (or just place sprinklers, which you unlock pretty early in the game from a chest in an early mine shaft near town), and harvesting in a few days. You can sell the crops or use them to feed the spirits in your barn (which yields spirit resources you can use to craft other things). Spirits can be caught as long as they have a blue heart on their HP bar (spirits with a skull are bosses you can't catch but that give more EXP to your spirits and better rewards when defeated). There's a LOT to explore and, though some parts of the game play aren't explained terribly well, it's pretty easy to figure out. Overall, it's a lot of fun to play, and, if you're into games like this, then I highly recommend it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't believe I am writing this, it is genuinely unfortunate that I have to not recommend this game. I love the game, I am a big fan of this genre, I read other reviews about the gameplay itself and still was adamant everything was fine for me and I was enjoying it. I didn't want to give up on it too soon, that's why I didn't refund it.

However the constant crashing is unbearable, I try to save frequently still that doesn't help, I am writing this as the game crashed again. I was inside a dungeon and it crashed after I tamed three kinds of spirits and sent to the science center... I save after everything I do cause even if it's a small thing, I don't want to have to do it again; When I reloaded the game, I was outside the dungeon, the chests inside were open somehow but the spirits tamed were not sent to the science center. So even when I save frequently I have issues like this. I never faced these issues with any other game.

I am not sure if I'll try to play the game later again, But for now, I think I am feeling frustrated with this.

I contacted Raw Fury support and the email was unfortunately not helpful, the tips that I got to do 'basic troubleshooting' are ridiculous in my opinion even though I followed some of them with it being a long list that consumers wouldn't care about following... Especially with knowing that other people are facing the same issues as well. That being said I admire the developer for putting such effort into this game, and I hope issues like this won't persist in future updates as I see it has potential, Perhaps it was released too soon in its current state.\t\t\t\t\t
