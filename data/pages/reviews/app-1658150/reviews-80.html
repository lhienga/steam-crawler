\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a beautiful game! It plays like a cross between Pokemon, Zelda, Harvest Moon, and a deckbuilder. It's got lots of charm, lovely music and sound effects, and pretty art. There's mysterious dungeons with exciting loot, cool "spirits" to tame and battle with, plenty of crafting and decorating, and a lively village filled with a robust cast of people to date. It has a really good balance of all these elements and, while there are quests to keep things interesting, you can play at your own pace. I would place this in a category of relaxing life sim, though there are certainly adventures to be had.

Some features it has that I can think of off the top of my head:
-Growing plants & mushrooms (can forage items, and/or buy or harvest seeds to grow your own)
-Taking care of weird animals (feed and look after "spirits," collect products from them, train them for battle)
-Romance (date anyone, talk, give gifts, go out on dates to learn more about them)
-Shipping bin (sell anything you find or make)
-Shops (buy seeds, crafting blueprints, decorations. Also you can sell some items directly for higher profit)
-Fishing (minigame, trophy collection, various qualities)
-Mining (lots of mines you can find, collect rocks and different types of ore, smelt into ingots, etc)
-Combat (turn-based deckbuilding battles between your spirits and wild spirits. Catch new ones and train)
-Peaceful areas where no spirits spawn (town, main island)
-Dungeon-crawling (puzzle games, labyrinths, boss battles, loot)
-Hot spring and foods to replenish stamina needed for activities
-Potion brewing (many different types of useful effects)
-Long-term quests (collect special items from dungeons, rare fish, etc)
-Decorating (build a house, fill it with pretty stuff, decorate the islands)
-Crafting (collect materials and blueprints to unlock new items)
-Play as the Alchemist-in-Training (a cute young adult who has white hair and an adorable blue outfit)*
*Note that there is no character customization, same as any Zelda game, you play the protagonist of the story. This one happens to have no assigned gender (leaving it open to personal interpretation)*

There's probably more but my point is that there are a lot of different things to do in this game and things to love about it! I hope you enjoy it!

Just editing to add that I think the writing is beautiful and the diversity of the characters is really refreshing! Love that there's a buff female blacksmith (I suspect she'll be a crowd favourite), and I honestly can't say there's anyone in the cast who doesn't make me want to get to know them better. All friendly right from the start too, no jerks on this island (though you might hear a lot of creative excuses to get out of going on dates with you at first). The chat system is percentage based, and it depends on how many hearts you have with them as to how likely they are to laugh at your jokes or agree to a date. But there's always a chance it'll work out!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 2 hours of gameplay all on steam deck I will try to make my pros and cons list.

Pros:

- Beautiful graphs, islands and mons.
- LGBTQIA+
- Fun card battle system
- Fun decorating
- Cute and inclusive characters.
- Funny dialogues

Cons:

- Frame drops (playing with 60 fps but always going to 40's and 50's even tho it's not even using 50% of the gpu and cpu)
- Game stops several times for a second (probably bad frame pace)
- Short days (wish we had an option to make days longer)
- Energy system ( I just don't like to have an energy system when cropping and chopping. I wanted to chop and crop and be relaxed not always seeing the energy bar and trying to minimize the tasks)
- Unfortunately we do not have any character customization ( not the worst but it would be cute to create our own character or at least change some clothes)

Total: 7/10

It's been a great and cute game and I recommend it, but the frame drops and the short days takes some fun out of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWill probably edit this as I go, but first impressions are pretty good!
I like the Ghibli-inspired concepts and spirit designs. The card battles are pretty fun. Characters are lovable. Played a bit on both mouse+keyboard and with a controller, controls feel pretty nice. Also, bless you guys for the fast travel tool and the ability to save the game at any time. <3

I've completed my first dungeon and have built my first spirit barn as of writing this review.

One that feels a little funky to me is gathering and moving. There will be times that moving to either a tree or rock and clicking/holding click it will begin collecting animation, but there are times where it doesn't despite being highlighted for collection. It doesn't feel as fluid as I like when I'm trying to gather 150 logs/stone.

It'd be neat to have an auto-store/sort from inventory to chest or a way to store a whole stack of something with like SHIFT/CTRL/ALT+LC/RC (because it seems I can only store things one at a time with the hold of RC). I know there's drag and drop and all, but other games have spoiled me with the quick storing. ;w;\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot of performance issues with the game stuttering way too often.
There are some design decisions that I really question, like not being able to zoom in on the map screen nor mousing over a building on the main island to see what it is. A lot of things about the game aren't communicated super clearly, like the circular gauge in the top left surrounding the minimap.
It tells you the level requirement for the island you're on.
The tool wheel also feels clunky to use with keyboard and mouse.
Despite these issues, the overall loops is very fun, the characters are really cute, and I'm enjoying my time with it. Four hours went by in a flash, and the combat system is very fun compared to similar games in the genre. If the above issues get fixed, 100% recommended. Right now it's only like a 75% recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarly impressions (will update this after finishing the game):

So far the game is really quite good. Character movement with a controller is a bit clunkier than I'd have liked, but that's a nitpick. Combat is fun and not too difficult. The slight RNG of the deck building adds a layer of tactics to each battle, as you're forced to deal with enemies with the tools you're given. Understanding deck building and how to make good use of different cards is fundamental to having a good time. My advice is to not stack your deck with all attack cards, but instead to rely on upgrading a few good attack cards while always keeping a good stock of bash cards to break shields. By breaking shields, you essentially get a free turn, which means another free hand draw to get the cards you want. Really, it's quite a good system. Good stuff!

The farm sim aspect is there. I haven't really bothered much with it to be honest, as I find the monster taming to be more interesting. That said, I've yet to actually figure out how to tame monsters. I always seem to be too low level to tame them when I try. Oh well, my bee starter has been soloing the entire game easily so far, so I'm sure I'll figure it out eventually.

The NPCs are fun. Their designs are nice. I like them all really. Haven't gotten very far into the whole romancing thing yet, but I'm looking forward to it.

There is a lot in this game that isn't really explained to you. You sort of have to figure stuff out on your own which can be fun. Personally I think it's intuitive enough. Reminds me of SNES-era games where half the fun was just figuring out effective ways to use the game systems to your advantage.

I recommend this game. It's charming, looks good, has great music. I can't really comment on how good it is as a whole package, but for the few hours I've had with it so far, I'm very pleased.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've only played 1.5 hours of Moonstone Island, but it is everything I have been looking for in this genre.
There are definite inspirations from Stardew Valley, but it's also its own thing. You get to build a tent/house wherever you want at the start of the game. I love this much freedom! You can put down your house at the starting island or venture out and find some uncharted island to settle down in.

By the way, the game is split up in a cool way. You start on an island with a town ( and I did build my house here just to be near the town npcs), but there are over 100 islands in the game and you can travel to them with a broom that you can eventually fix or just by building bridges you craft from stone and rock. So, the flow of the game is up to you - which direction do I head out in? What's waiting for me there??? The islands have dungeons that have loot in them and combat. Combat is a turn based affair like Pokemon, where you can exploit weaknesses to gain crushing victories. There are apparently over 100 monsters to recruit that you can bring on your adventure.

There is also the normal gathering, crafting, building, smelting, fishing, mining - etc etc that this genre provides. It's a very satisfying loop in gameplay and had me hooked right away.

There are a few little issues that I made a separate topic about, but I'm sure QOL things will be added by the devs in the near future.

Music is also very soothing and perfectly fits the cozy vibe this game oozes constantly.
Graphics are 2D in what I can only describe as looking a little but like Zelda Link to the Past in appearance. I love 2D spritework games so this is perfect for me.

I have not played enough to get into the details of what else lies in store, but I can tell you that if you enjoy Stardew Valley or similar life sim games, and do not mind a moderate amount of combat added to the mix. then this is an easy recommend.

So many games launch into early access and languish there for years, never to reach their potential. But here, we have a fully formed game, ready for you to dive into in a complete state. To the devs - great job and I can't wait to see what's added over time !\t\t\t\t\t
