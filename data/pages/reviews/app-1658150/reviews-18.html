\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMoonstone Island is obviously a Stardew-alike and it largely does a good job of replicating pieces of Stardew Valley and adding a new mechanic (battling with spirits and deckbuilding). It's worth your time if you love Stardew. The visuals are charming and there's a lot of heart in it.

I found the onboarding to be pretty obtuse, with a lot of things not really explained (ex: you have to feed your spirits and they all eat fiber, which is easily available but not explained anywhere in the game that I could see) and a lot of the hitboxes of items are very difficult to work with (same example, feeding some spirits is very hard because your character tries to touch the spirit and not the box next to them, requiring some precise clicking that's hard with a controller). These are minimal complaints, but over a long playtime and for a cozy game they can be frustrating.

That said, I'm glad I played it and enjoyed most of the lengthy experience. Some of the elements feel shallow compared to Stardew but the combat additions and spirit-gathering element really feels fun on top of it, so it washes out a little bit. I'd recommend it, but some parts frustrated me because of how much I loved doing the rest of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis indie type game was a fun mix of elements from Stardew Valley, Pokemon, and Studio Ghibli. I played until about the end of Spring Year 2, just shy of 100 hours played overall. I finished all the dungeons and the main quest. I had not quite collected all the spirits and seeds but I was getting close. I don't feel like playing through 2 or 3 months just to finish all the side quests.

Pros
- Big map to explore
- Unlocking better means of traveling to different islands is satisfying
- If you are into collecting things then collecting all the spirits and crops is fun
- Music is excellent
- Turn-based card battles
- Modifying the card deck for your spirits gets easier and easier as you advance in the game.
- Moonstones are rare at first but quickly become quite plentiful.
- I'm not into decorations but if you then there are plenty to unlock through gameplay.

Cons
- If you play with certain spirits and tailor your spirit decks enough then you can easily dominate the game and be very overpowered. Battles can then feel rather boring.
- Dungeons are too repetitive
- Most of the spirits have a weak special ability so there is not much incentive to play with them. So even though there are 70+ spirits in the game in my opinion only about 10 of them have great abilities.
- There is not enough incentive for farming or fishing. Economics feels unbalanced because you can make a lot of money without doing farming or fishing. And other than healing or taming items I would argue you could get by without crops.
- Skill trees are OK but feel unbalanced in places.\t\t\t\t\t
