\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst four or so hours were fun. You get into the groove of exploring the islands, crafting, and meeting new spirits. The graphics and music are delightful. Then the problems start to become apparent, and they range from minor annoyances to things that make you want to give up entirely.

The villagers being boring and restricted to talk/joke/flirt didn't bother me, because I never read villager dialogue anyways. However, I can understand this would be a negative for someone into building character relationships. The story also didn't bother me that much. Compare it to many of the Harvest Moon entries. You have one goal, like save the Harvest Goddess. Then the game sets you free to explore, build relationships, and make money. To me, Moonstone does the same, except the days become insanely boring, you have no motivation to build relationships, and the methods of making money don't really matter/are too tedious. I found combat/spirit taming to be the focus of this game, whether it was intentional or not. Relationships, crafting, and story all become somewhat irrelevant or unimportant. This game isn't Stardew Valley and does not compete on the same level in terms of those aspects.

So, combat. I was having a blast. Then my team hit around level 15, and it became an issue of where to pick fights. Most islands near Moonstone have Earth spirits that are way too low level to for meaningful XP gain. Further out, most islands are Electric with enemies that are too high level. Then I found one Fire island and decided to grind there. However, unless you're stocked up on lilypads that take several days to grow, you're probably only going to be able to grind 2-3 fights a day. Maybe get 1 level a day if you're lucky. This game NEEDS more healing items OR other ways to heal spirits in between battles other than lilypads and spirits with passive healing (if you even want them on your roster). So if you have no lilypads, don't have a passive healer on your team... gotta go to bed. And that matters heavily in a game where time is paramount.

Another thing is the absurd unfairness of type weaknesses. I regretted picking Ankylo. I should've listened to the people who said Capacibee was OP. If you get to an island with poison with him or any other earth type - consider them dead. Maybe unless you are significantly higher level than them. The enemies will relentlessly attack them. It's not a fair fight. In Pokemon, for example, you wouldn't be screwed just because your opponent has a move that is super effective against you. You still have a chance, especially if you're higher or same level. Enemies in this game tend to bash the type that's weak to them, then essentially two or three shot them with or without the effect of bash. What's even better is poison (and I assume other abilities like burn) damage does even MORE damage when you're bashed. And it doesn't seem to have a cap of how many turns it can last. I've seen poison that'll last for 11 turns. So you better have a way to clear it (AKA a randomly given potion from a chest). If you don't, kiss that spirit goodbye for the day. He'll come back with 1 HP.

Do I let Ankylo keep dying, and then the rest of my team? Do I fly back to Moonstone and hope that Zed is still open? Or did I put that spirit in my barn? Do I give up on that island and spend the rest of the day chopping trees? So you MUST choose your team wisely depending on the island. That would be fine with me if there was an easier, quicker way to access all spirits AND if there were more healing items in the game. Why not have Zed open 24/7? Like how Pokemon has 24/7 Pokecenters with computers to access your boxes? \r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a farming game, it is very generous. As a creature catcher, it's a little flat. I didn't expect it to be a deckbuilder-- I only came for the other two parts-- but somehow that was the part that I enjoyed best.

This is a good fit for:
- People who found Stardew Valley stressful
- People who play creature catchers mostly to see cool designs
- Serial interior decorators
- People who can't get enough of deckbuilding gameplay

I still very much enjoyed the game, and I was tickled to see that they are still adding new creatures (and qol) even during my playtime. That was delightful. As of February 2024, this feels like a very solid version 1.0 or 1.1-- worth your money and worth its promises. Plenty of decorations to find and make, plenty of exploration... but it is not yet exceptional. It is a wonderful base that I hope they continue to add to and polish for years to come.

The above is my general summary. Below, I'll broach some specific points less for the benefit of prospective players and more on the presumption that perhaps some feedback will be heard.
- Not all starters are created equal. With a wider pool, that might be fine. These choices felt strange.
- I completed what appears to be the 'main story' within a year, and I don't feel the need to continue past. It feels like I have already accomplished the foremost goals. If that is intentional, the inclusion of years is odd. If it is not, then the pace is too fast.
Seasons are novel and a farming staple, but most farm games don't feature exploration so heavily. Each season's crops invalidate the previous, and some spirits are only available in certain seasons. So it feels to me like the best way to 'progress' was actually just to wait.
I would rather leave the season mechanic in other farming games and focus on the exploration of the flying islands-- spring islands, autumn islands, winter islands... Make the better crops relegated to further islands rather than time-based. Maybe they can only be planted in certain environments rather than times-- the game already seems set up to let you establish bases on outer islands. This is a ghibli-esque game, let's have a color wheel door like in Howl's Moving Castle. I'm just saying...
Ultimately the reason I am not continuing is because I know Spring Year 2 holds nothing for me, and if I don't find every Ice Spirit by the end of the winter, that's going to be a long time to wait.
- This might be an unpopular take, but the farming side of the game is -too- generous right now. Money stopped being an object for me by the end of the second season, so I no longer felt compelled to engage with the farming beyond harvesting EXP-- and with how easy the greenhouse was to upgrade and fill with reharvestable plants, that stopped being much of an engagement too.
- The glider is wonderful. Love for the glider, love for Nausicaa. But I would still love to be able to craft an expensive end-game even faster version of it for QoL. There are many end-game automation QoLs that I can imagine-- boomerang for harvesting rather than cutting, incubator for quick egg hatching, a way to remotely access the science center... so on.
- As a creature catcher, my biggest lament is that while types feel varied from each other, I do not feel like there is much meaningful difference within types. One water creature is as good as another-- maybe there are slight differences in what decks they get, but I can't really tell... but it's especially true for farming.
As an example, early in the game I found a big bricky spirit. I caught it, thinking "oh, this will probably be a good way to get bricks!" Fool that I am, bricks are not a resource. The bricky boi will produce exactly the same clay as every other earth spirit. And I am only fighting with three spirits at a time (and I am obviously going to use the cutest ones)... and the big brick buddy's collision eclipses the food trough so he's annoying to feed.... so he is goin' in the science center jail 4 ever.
But I would love if there were a reason to collect specific spirits for specific resources. bricks from the brickman. goop from the goop girl. heated wool from the fireysheep. The crafting could become a lot more in depth with more of that and more components/ingredients... In general, I would love more reasons to hunt and engage with specific spirits (matchmaking was fun) and intertwine the two main halves of the game more and more.
Plus, the fact that spirits are only ever found in groups by their also does a lot to contribute to them feeling homogenous. Give us bee in flowery areas! Give us water spirits from fishing! So on!!!
The devs are still adding new creatures, but what I'm really hoping for is depth over breadth.

In all, I hope to play this again someday-- in a lot of years, with a much deeper, fleshed out system of the same concepts. But even as is, the game is easy to enjoy for a short while.\t\t\t\t\t
