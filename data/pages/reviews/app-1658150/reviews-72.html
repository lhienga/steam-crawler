\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStardew Valley + Slay the Spire + Pokemon. A very ambitious mixture of my three favorite genres, and it turned out... pretty good.

The default-unity-lookin assets are a bit lackluster, and I will echo the very common complaint that there is no tangible objective. In just 4 hours I found myself constantly not knowing where to go next since the game doesn't give you much to go with. For a role-playing game, you don't really play much of a role in this world either. The worldbuilding feels rushed and incomplete. Farming just feels like a waste of time and has no soul behind it.

There's a lot of good in this game, though. The battle mechanics are pretty fun: familiar deckbuilder and jrpg aspects mashed into a pretty satisfying experience. The monster designs are nothing special, but just very solid. And despite not having a definite objective, there is still a lot to do.

I'm having fun with this game so far, despite the issues.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCool concept but poor execution. You may think that 1.5 hours is too short of a time to make a judgement but I assure you it's not. This game isn't that deep and an arbitrary amount of time isn't necessary to engage with most of what's on offer here.

The Good:

- Good graphics and music.

- Interesting concept where the game tries to combine elements of farm/life sims, creature collectors, card battlers, and Zelda-like dungeons.

The Bad:

- This game tries to be wholesome and cozy but it fails, in my opinion, do to the combination of a clock that moves too quickly and a stamina bar. If you're going to have a fast moving clock, get rid of the stamina bar or reduce the number of actions that consume stamina. The clock is enough of a limitation.

- The writing isn't great which makes the NPCs uninteresting. It's full of cookie cutter tropes.

- The NPC interaction system boils down to choosing one of 3 interactions (chat, joke, or flirt) which rely on RNG. There's no dialog associated with any of these choices. It's just the number goes up or down. It feels robotic and not at all like human interaction.

- Bugs exist that will soft lock your game thus forcing you to alt f4 and you lose the progress you made that day. Look at the bug reports if you're interested in what those bugs are.

- Considering this game just came out two days ago and it's "sink or swim" time, you would think that the devs would be more responsive to bug reports and hot fixes would be happening multiple times a day or, at the very least, daily. Instead they seem to be more interested in celebrating Moonstone Island as if it's perfect and focusing on merchandising...for a two day old game. Worrying about a MakeShip collaboration instead of hot fixes feels grimy.

- Released on day 1 with a paid DLC that's just a few cosmetic items. For a game that already costs more than SDV and offers far less in terms of customization, NPC interactions, and NPCs with complexity, it's not a good look.

Whether or not Moonstone Island is worth your money is up to you. I'm on the fence but leaning towards "no" unless some serious hot fixes come out quickly and options are added to either increase day length or disable the stamina bar. I could overlook the average writing (I'm being generous by calling it average), bugs, and shallow customization if hot fixes were incoming, customization options were being added, or the price was lower. As it stands, it very much looks like the developers are trying to cash in on the cuteness of this game before people realize the content is as deep as a puddle.

Edit: They finally squashed some bugs two days after release, about an hour ago as of writing this edit. I still stand by my statements regarding this game.\t\t\t\t\t

You can fish, do monster battles, farm, romance, etc. which makes the gameplay loop pretty entertaining and somewhat addicting.
