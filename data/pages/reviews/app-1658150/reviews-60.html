\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMoonstone Island tries to be a lot of different things, a dating sim, a farming sim, creature-collector, but because of this, it hasn't been able to excel at any one thing in particular. I like what they attempted, it can still be very fun, but the attempt feels shallow, never having a chance to flesh out its features further due to them being so spread out. Listed below are, in my opinion, the main aspects advertised that Moonstone Island had to offer, and a more in depth perspective of each one, in no particular order. I won't be mentioning the visuals at all mostly because it speaks for itself, this game is very gorgeous!

Creature Collecting/Battling: I adore the creature design in Moonstone Island, I think there's a wide variety of unique spirits to tame and befriend, and I'm a fan of the deck building approach to battles. I don't find that the deck building aspects are explained very well in game though, and a lot of battles feel unbalanced, and heavily weighted one way or another, it never feels like a neutral, balanced fight. XP gain feels slow, and taming a new, higher level spirit is usually easier and faster than training up a spirit you already own (provided you have enough space to tame a new spirit). Speaking of space, your locked to 3 spirits (including your starting spirit) at the beginning of the game, until you complete a quest that unlocks a crafting recipe which requires a lot of materials (considering this is something you want to build as early as possible) which then allows you to build housing for additional spirits. For creature collecting to be one of this game's pillars, this progression feels oddly paced. having only a maximum of 3 spirits during the time it takes you to complete this quest, gather the materials to then build the spirit housing feels off. There too many crafting recipes to prioritize right from the beginning of the game, and I think it would've been beneficial to spread these out somehow.

Farming: It feels obsolete, both lacking and not very rewarding, and can be avoided all together without halting game progression. It can also be tedious to set up a farm due to the wonky targeting of your cursor, and the drawn out animations. Farming feels more like a chore than a means of making money, which would be its only purpose. Any plants you can grow on your farm grow in the wild on islands you can travel to, and it feels more convenient to go grab the crops you need rather than wait for them to grow on the farm. The only crops I find myself farming are the ones that spawn in psychic storms, which are rarer and hard to find in some cases. You can feed crops to your Spirits for temporary stat boosts, but it doesn't feel worth investing a lot of time in because it's only a small temporary boost. I think if the crops had more of a lasting effect on your Spirits, like growing the right kinds of crops to make your spirits stronger where they're lacking, would make farming as a whole much more lucrative and worth the time!

Exploration: This is actually my favorite part of the game so far! The exploration feels slow at first, and I recommend saving up for the Glider as soon as you possibly can, but the ability to fly around to new islands, hunting for treasure, new crops, and new spirits, is what the game feels centered around, and I think that's perfect! The 100 islands you get to discover generate randomly around a large map at the start of your game, so no two people will have the exact same experience when it comes to discovery! Exploration feels like the strongest element of Moonstone Island, with the most freedom and reward. One small gripe I have though is the interior design of temples, structures that house puzzles and guardians to overcome. They're rather lackluster and empty in appearance.

Relationships and NPCs: I really love the 10 NPCs the game surrounds you with, their characters seem interesting and they all have different personalities and appearances, and I love the quests where they ask you to hunt for a specific spirit to pair them up with. The date mechanic is awesome and I wish more games implemented it like Moonstone Island does, where you get to pick a day and a place, the place where you need to meet is marked on your map, and the game gives you a reminder when your date time is coming up in advance! The content of the dates is minimal, but the ones I've experienced so far are cute; short and sweet for the most part! However, I want to know the characters more, but they have very little dialogue daily to offer you. While I like the chat options of "Chat, Joke, Flirt", along with the percentage chance to suceed or fail to earn points towards your relationship, this feature doesn't allow the NPCs to respond at all. They initially give you a couple lines of dialogue when you first talk to them, but once you engage them further, they have no dialogue to give you. Maybe after you attempt all your chat options, they could offer you more dialogue dpending on how well your chatting went? I'm torn with this section because I do really like the characters, but it's because of that desire to know them better that I'm disappointed I'm not able to interact with them further.

Material Management: There's a decent amount of crafting in the game at the moment, with around 4 different crafting stations to hop around (less crafting stations, the better imo), but I'm finding that too many recipes demand wood, and not enough recipes require stone. I have 8 stacks of 99 stone sitting in a chest in my house, and not even a single full stack of wood on my person at any given point. I also find the heavy reliance on Moonstones and Moonstone Ingots for nearly every recipe past the early game to be a hassle. I can understand the focus on Moonstones in the later game, but within the first hour of gameplay, you'll see a Moonstone in every crafting recipe you need to complete. On the topic of ore, smelting ore is incredibly tedious, you can only smelt one ingot at a time, forcing you to make a big ugly block of furnaces if you want to compensate and smelt more than one ingot at a time. Chests for storing items cannot be named/labeled or color coded, and there's no hot key to quickly move items from chest to inventory and vice versa, you either have to drag the item with your mouse, or click and hold the item down, which takes more time than an instantaneous hot key click.

Decorating: Upon first impression, I was pleasantly surprised with being able to place items on a sort of half grid system, where you can place items half a grid square off, the potential of that is exciting for builders and decorators. But once you start trying to place and pick up items, it feels clunky, unintuitive, and so slow. Placing and picking up items repetitively feels sluggish, and because of the half grid placement system, items can accidentally get overlaid strangely with other surrounding items, and be hard to target to pick up. I was really looking forward to putting some pathing down and starting the beautification of my base, but gave up quickly once I realized how slow the process would be. There are a lot of really gorgeous decorative items to craft and use, and they're all fairly easy to make, but if I place an item, have to hold middle mouse for a whole second to pick it up, then place it somewhere else, and have to do that more than twice, I'd rather be doing something else. Decorating could go so hard in this game, they just need to make some quality of life improvements first I think.

TLDR: If you like exploring and not settling in one place too long, you'll probably enjoy the game in its current state! Otherwise, I can't recommend it wholeheartedly until it receives some major balancing and quality of life improvements. Moonstone Island has a lot of potential, I'm eagerly awaiting future developments for the game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a weird game. For people looking for a pokemon stardew this isnt it.
Its more like a pokemon Forager (the game not the term).
I think this game is fun but is in need of tweeks to keep the game play engaging.

Monsters require what is basically a pokemon PC to be earned by catching a monster of a certain level. but I had a full party of monsters I liked and it forced me to say good bye to one of them just to get the essential building.

The dialogue for all characters feel like it came from the same writers mouth. Its the same personality & whatever outward trait the person exhibits. i.e. blacksmith, salesman etc. I am early into getting to know everyone still but after seeing everyones 1 heart story it feels that way currently.

Picking up crops isnt smooth, when mining I dig through an area but it wont let me walk back through it without clipping. The menu is very basic and the menu controls are weirdly hard for me to get my head around.

This is a No recommendation but if you want a cosy game to zone out to. This is the game for you. Its very good in the same way forager wasnt played for the gameplay but the grind.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGiving this a thumbs up, however, that's on accounts that I did have fun.
However, this game is not without flaws, in fact I felt this was more of an early access title than a complete one...
However I feel I would easily be dragged back in if certain things are improved on. In this games current state, it tries to be everything, but excels at nothing.

Pro's
- The combat in this game is similar to slay the spire, that being said, it's also quite easy and forgiving.
- The monster collecting, is there, it's solid enough.
- The characters, albeit fairly bare bones, were lovable.
- The farming, bare bones. Honestly this is fine for me. As I tend to streamline farming in all games, this just made it easier.
- Exploration was done well, I enjoyed flying out to each island and uncovering the entire map.
- Shiny Monsters exist. due to how the level taming works, I couldn't get it, but still, them existing is a plus.
- Nothing felt like a chore.
- Tutorials left me with a fairly clear path to travel, jounal gave me a clear set of goals.
- Pixel art overall was well done, as a pixel artist myself, this is always a treat~

Cons
- Characters leave much to be desired, I enjoyed my time greatly with Ferra, why is there no marriage or actual events? The dates are a cool idea, but run out quite quickly.
- Actual events non existent, leaving the town feel quite meaningless. I hope these are planned in the future.
- Combat unbalanced, it's quite easy to make a team that just makes combat too easy. the last "boss"? literally got one turn to act before I completely destroyed it lol. As I note, I just used monsters I thought looked cool. (torble, beardini, evileye)
- Dungeon bosses... You can only fight the guardian so many times before you question why they only have one design...
- Fishing... I like it, however, finding the fish needed for certain parts, made them feel like quite rare drops...

Suggestions for improvement
- Marriage, or at least some special events that happen at specific heart values.
-- The idea of having kids that would tend to farms/spirits could be a good addition.
- Town specific events, with some mini games or some unique items
- Small towns located around the map with potential new or recruitable (for moonstone island) characters.
- Some character fashion items, you already have a pretty heavy emphasis on house customization.
- Up to 3 player coop? you can use 3 monsters afterall, maybe each person can use a monster, with a reduced hand size or something.
- New npc models based on the clothing they're wearing at the time (thinking inside ferra house where she had a white shirt on, but the npc did not represent this)
- New monsters, as a monster collector I feel 66 is a bit low overall.
- Tweaking the combat a bit, certain cards just destroy any sort of balance, maybe have a cap to certain cards. I'd had it at a point where having 20+ ap on turn one with the ability to draw a card every time I use a card, just making combat a spam of attacks till the enemy dies.
- More consequences for fainting, waking up a bit late, was hardly a problem...
--Cut stamina in half, Keep monsters hp at 1, lose some held items etc.

Overall I got my moneys worth, I did enjoy the game, and personally feel it could be an amazing experience if the devs put a bit more love into each of the games elements. Right now it just feels like a tech demo. Granted, a good one.
Signing out for now, but will keep an eye on future updates, and if coop becomes a thing, will happily pull some friends in.\t\t\t\t\t
