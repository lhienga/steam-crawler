\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried to like this game, really, but there're just way too many things to improve.

The lack of explanation and hint is a major issue. I had no idea that TRANSPORTATION required stamina before I fell from the sky. I don't see why it can't be put in the tool description but have to be learnt from trial and error.

UI is another problem. Please learn from Stardew Valley and put the immediate crafting menu with the backpack, since we have to use items in there anyway. I don't understand the need to hide it with save and setting. And the tool wheel is so annoying! What's the deal of NOT having enough slot for all your tools? It's not like you provide a shortcut for those in slots anyway! I think it's the first game where I can't use mouse wheel to select which tool I want.

And then the card battle. I am naturally bad at this aspect so I won't comment much. At least make sorting out cards easier when I try to see what cards each creature has.

The relationship aspect is commented on by quite a few people already. Personally I find none of the NPCs appealing (except magic guy, but that's cause he's a mystery), the pronouns useless since I don't get to use them at all in conversation, and I can feel the devs trying so hard to make a point. Thank heavens it is not a necessary thing (I don't really care about achievements) or I would be more infuriated by the random chance of FAILING to CHAT with someone.

I was drawn to this game by cute creatures. If somehow this game improves itself in the future I may come back for them.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall I like the story and dungeon crawling aspect of the game, but that's all I really like. I have some issues with the gameplay balance, especially toward the mid to late game. Essentially, there's a lot of "things to do" that don't actually contribute to the main gameplay loop, so the game begins to feel empty and boring. This game feels very much like a "collect everything for collection's sake only" type of game.

The first ten hours of the game feel really exciting and addicting; there's a lot of promise of adventure with the boasted "100+ islands to explore", a town full of colorful characters, promises of dungeons and fishing and plants and raising spirits, etc.

It becomes pretty apparent quickly that:

- The "100+ islands" are lifeless; once you've accessed every "type" of island, they get extremely repetitive really, really fast. It's more honest to say there are 6 islands duplicated 100 times. There's little to no variation on the islands per type--all poison/electric/fire/earth/water/dark islands on the map are always going to have the same features. All hidden mines have the same features. The same Spirits appear on every island of the same variety. I'm disappointed that there isn't any sort of variation, or special things to discover on each island. I at least expected a different draw of spirits on each island. I've been to every island on my game's map and there's virtually no point in going to any of the islands that don't have a dungeon on them.

- The town is lifeless; there are no festivals or random events that draw me to be interested in the characters or the community. The "relationship" building aspect is also pointless--instead of getting typical, interesting dating sim dialogue, your interactions with characters are limited to three options, which have a percentage chance of failing or passing. It feels very mechanical and pointless. There's no apparent benefit to getting the villagers' hearts up besides dating/marrying them--which also feels pointless when you don't actually get to see the character fleshed out at all.

- Fishing is immediately pointless; you can't use the fish you catch for any purpose other than selling them or gifting them to villagers, or completing a questline if you care about completionism. Since there are faster, better ways to make money, fishing has no useful purpose in the gameplay loop, and thus I practically never do it while playing.

- Brewing potions is immediately pointless; there's no inherent benefit to brewing because all it does is combine the effects of two crops. It does not amplify them, it does not give any bonus effects, it just acts as if you fed your Spirits two fruits at once. Considering the fact that there are crops that can heal 100 HP in one go, and considering the fact you can just feed your Spirits any fruits you want BEFORE a battle and the status effects will stay on for the entire day as long as they don't die, there's virtually no benefit to brewing. It's also crazy to me that there's no option to add other things to boost the effects of the potion--like I'd imagine adding Moonstone could amplify the effects, or using certain fish could give unique effects, but no dice.

- Raising spirits becomes pointless quickly; Most of the items they give you are only useful for decorations or for random potions you don't usually need. The exception is the island anti debuff charms--but you only need one of each elemental's product to create the charm! The only spirit that creates anything "useful" are the water types; their seaglass is used to create potion bottles and the greenhouse. Also, all spirits give the exact same resource based on their type with no variation. The spirits don't walk around the farm area or do anything cute--it literally just looks like a place to show off your collection of spirits, if you're into that kind of thing.

- Money in general becomes pointless quickly; once you level up your tools, there's nothing to spend it on but seeds, house upgrades and decorations (which you can also just craft). I'm in early Fall of my first year and just have 100k gold sitting around collecting dust because I have nothing to use it on.

- Leveling/battling your spirits becomes pointless quickly; there are plants that you can get as early as summer that instantly give your Spirits an XP boost. Considering how easy it is to make sprinklers, you can easily plant 400+ Hot Air Plants or Willow Wisps and feed your spirits to level them instead of grinding EXP. Which honestly, at a certain point, is almost necessary, because the level scaling on the islands can be extremely bizzare. When my spirits were around level 30, I had trouble finding island that were at an appropriate challenge level--I'd either find water or poison islands under level 20, or electric/fire islands with level 46+ spirits. I just spam fed my spirits until I was closer to level 40 so I could have a fighting chance. If you really wanted to, you could skip the spirit fighting entirely by just growing a metric crapton of the EXP boosting plants.

In addition, this game needs some quality of life fixes. For example, navigating the inventory and crafting menus is so tedious and annoying because there's no way to quick stack items into chests, there's no way to quick stack items into crafting machines. I get so annoyed every time I have to interface with any inventory-related UI because it is so clunky and tedious to do so.

The storyline and dungeon crawling is the part of the game that kept me coming back, but it's timelocked to seasons, and there's nothing else actually worth doing to pass the time while I wait for winter to come. Once I find myself in a position where I'm just sleeping every day to pass time, there's something wrong with the balance/content of the game, in my opinion.

Overall: I think the game has a lot of fun ideas that quickly fall flat once you get about midway through the game, because you quickly realize how empty and pointless a lot of the activities outside of the main questline actually are. I don't think it's hopeless; I just think it needs more content. Add festivals/random events to make the town feel alive and worth investing time in. Make the fish useful, either in potions or special status effects for the player/Spirits. Add variety to the islands via special Spirit spawns, artifacts or unique things to find. I don't mean to dismiss the work already put into the game, because it is beautiful and definitely something that had heart put into it, but it definitely needs more in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am one of those who felt into a trap of: Oh look, it's like pokemon-stardew-zelda in open world and with card battles!
Well, maybe it's just me, yet the concept above sounds better than it actually works when put together.
I love the art and atmosphere of this game, I love that there is a ton of things in this cute world, but my main problem is - none of the features advertised work that good to keep your attention.

We have a mash-up of mechanics and features that are stapled together to form a perfect working ecosystem, yet as a result:
- this is not a good farming game;
- this is not a good creature collector game;
- this is not a good exploration game;
- this is not a good card battler;

Yes, all the things are in and everything works with minimum amount of bugs, yet do you know the difference between a good feature and just feature? It's excitement. It should be exciting to find new spirits, it should be exciting to get and use rare cards. or find and grow new seeds, explore dungeons, all of that. That should reward you with interest, progression, whatever.

To my taste - most of the things feel like a routine in this game. Wanna go there? You can't, your level is too low, kill 240+ mobs to level up to be able to bypass this dungeon guard. Want to have a better tool? You can't. Run around and destroy 200+ stones to discover a randomly generated mine, that will (or will not) allow you to mine out 10-30 iron ore within couple of days of game time to craft a better tier pickaxe. What? You got only 11/12 iron bars? That's a shame. Go buy some. Don't have that much gold? Repeat the 200+ stones step.

Yeah, you find recipes and new stuff pretty often, I must say, yet still the game feels like completing a dozen daily quests in a MMORPG, or filling in a Battle Pass goals.

Anyways, that's just my complaints for this game, Maybe it's the exact opposite for you. Give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t
