\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the most ambitious mashups of different genres I have seen in a while. Because of this, the game has decided to spread itself over a large variety of features and game play loops. These different loops can be tackled one by one, or simultaneously; which is one of the games' triumphs. Inversely, you can choose to focus on one thing (like farming) and completely ignore many of the features if that's what you want to do. No matter if it was a farming day or a battling day or an exploration day for me, though, i had a lot of fun.

There are a few things I wish were different, but I'm not sure exactly how I would approach these issues as they do stem from having played games with more focus compared to Moonstone Island. The give and take of having many things to do, but not a lot of depth to any one particular thing is a hard balance to find. I do think Moonstone Island found that balance in a lot of places, but left me wanting more in a few.

The Mashup -
Stardew Valley - The biggest and easiest to compare to - farming, planting, caring for animals, etc. Similar pixel graphics and similar isometric view. This will likely be what most people compare this game to - which is a shame.

Slay the Spire - Card Battler - this one isnt *really* like StS, but the card battle system is famous from that game. There arent roguelike or roguelite elements here; just the combat system.

Pokemon - Collecting Monsters. Nuff said. There are shinies.

Animal Crossing - House Decoration, collecting stuff for a museum, quirky villagers.

The somewhat divisive thing about this game is that if any person really likes *one* of the games listed above, and wants more of it, they will find themselves disappointed at the lack of depth. BUT. All together, this game has more systems and activities to do than any one game alone does. It's all about a nice casual mix of activities, not having a super long-term fleshed out system for each game trope.

So basically, do I recommend the game? Absolutely. However, dont go into it expecting it to have as much farming as Stardew, as many creatures as Pokemon, as many deckbuilding options as StS, or as many collectibles to find as Animal Crossing. Instead go into it for the potpourri of flavors that come together to make a really nice experience.

PS. My wants -
- Endgame Content - give me a reason to use my creatures more after the plot is done! Maybe some kind of island with a dungeon that just gets harder and harder that has chests in it. Idk.
- A Holo Hunting method - the Holo-ween event made me realize how much I want some sort of chaining feature to hunt for these little guys. I spent a good 6 hours hunting for the boyo that I wanted using a holo hunter potion, and it was a good time! Give me more of this please (but 10% is far too generous).
- A final payoff for the player spending 16+ days planning and prepping dates and giving gifts to our lover. Let them move in with us, or give us some sort of benefit for romancing them. It felt a bit strange to have the final cutscene only for them to go on with daily life as if nothing happened.

Aside from these small things, I loved the game. If i was to give it a rating out of 10, id give it a 8.5. With a little more love and extra content, i could see it going up to a 9 and maybe even a 10.

My playthrough took 65 hours, with 10 or so hours of resetting progress Holo-Hunting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI picked this game up last week and have really been enjoying it. My only real complaint is the lack of NPC "character" - There doesn't seem to be any point to gaining "hearts" (relationships) with the NPCs as they don't have a story to tell. The NPCs really need to each have their own, even small, story to tell a few times at certain heart markers (like 3, 6, 9). I would also say that everything is a bit too "easy" - farming, gaining gold, gaining relationship. I like that it's easy, but at the same time it means there is less to do in game. There also doesn't seem to be a point to do each of those things, because they are so easy and don't have seem to have any reward.

I love that the food I grow can be used for large stamina replenishment, so I can push hard any day that I want to with no issues. This is not typical of these sorts of games, where you run out of stamina quickly and then have to sleep. I love that I can get sprinklers very early on, so that I don't have to waste my time watering my stupid plants. I enjoy the spirits, they are super cute. I enjoy catching them, battling them, and exploring the islands looking for them. I like that I can smack a few boulders to find a "mine" when I need more ore. HOWEVER, because many things are so easy and quick, the game really needs to integrate more NPC story to keep things going - or to keep me enjoying the game for longer. I am almost through winter, and other than filling out Waldo's mirror I feel like I have generally done everything that I want to do.

With that said, I definitely feel like this game was a good value at $15 sale price. Unless NPC stores get added, then I won't have the same play-ability as SDV, but this has been super fun and enjoyable, although very short because of lack of diverse content. Because of the quick content / lack of content, I am not sure there is much more in the game for me to do. I could probably crank out another 4-8 hours finishing the winter and completing Waldo's story, but I may wait and revisit after some updates.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has potential... The graphics are cute and fun, and the character interaction is also fun. The overall concept of the game can go very very far...

---
BUT (note a lot of my comments are probably reiterated in the existing reviews, sorry in advance):

-That stamina bar.... stamina gets used SO quickly at the beginning... I spent a majority of my time sitting in the hot springs to regain stamina ... just to use it all within an hour (game-clock time) to clear my farm/living space/island out.

-Mines... once you're done with the mine, you're done. Nothing respawns, so you're forced to close the mine.

-Trees... unlike Stardew Valley where it sometimes drops seeds and you can replant them, the trees in this game don't drop seeds nor respawn, so I'm not sure where to get wood from...

---
Even though the game has potential, I'm not recommending it at this time. I felt I wasted a lot of time sitting around waiting for my stamina, and there is way more to this game than trying to regain energy to obtain more resources.\t\t\t\t\t
