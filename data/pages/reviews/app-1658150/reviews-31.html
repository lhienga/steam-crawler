\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a bit of a mix of Stardew and Pokemon (without evolutions.) It has a bit of a hidden story that you have to pay attention to to learn about it. You'll hear Gaiana talk angrily about what happened, Zed will talk about what some of the ancient world people would do, and so on. There is nothing overtly out there like books to read in-game to garner the information. If you take the time to read the comments that the Islanders make, you will learn the lore. The game-play loop is also fun as it mostly mirrors Stardew, but with a twist. There are dungeons to explore, spirits to tame, crops to grow, and people to flirt with.
I would highly recommend this game to people that want a more lax game to play as it doesn't have a lot of hidden mechanics to learn. It is pretty straightforward for 90% of the game-play mechanics. Makes it easy to settle into, even after a hiatus. I'd give it an 9.75/10. Those few unexplained mechanics and being open-ended kind of sours the game as it leaves you to loop infinitely. It does leave room for developments though.

Update: Now that I've completed all of the achievements, I would like to state the value of the game. It is very fun to play through, while not being tedious until you start working on 3 of the achievements. "Fishing up 1000 fish," "Earning 1-2 million gold," and "Find 50 mines." The first 2 are an actual grind and are tedious while the last is just annoying to keep track of. This tediousness could be mitigated by a stat menu. It would reduce the headache by being able to see how far along you are. Due to this lack of QoL, I would have to say the game is a 9.25/10.

I still need to complete Zed's research "Catch, see, and release." I also need to collect a living "Zed Dex" of holo spirits.\t\t\t\t\t
