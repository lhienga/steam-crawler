\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter putting over 100 hours into the game, I felt like it was time to leave a review. Moonstone Island has been a magical experience, and I've enjoyed every second of it. It did an amazing job blending the typical farm/dating sim elements with the monster taming and combat. The deck building aspects of the game are a ton of fun to get into, and I enjoyed experimenting with the different Spirit types and what they had to offer. The randomly generated islands were a treat to explore, and I spent hours flying around just to discover what was out there.

I feel like each of the characters was very fleshed out and unique, and I wish I had a chance to spend more time with them. That's actually my only compliant; I do wish that there was a bit more to do with the end game of the romance. Please don't misunderstand me; what is present in the game is very nice, and the dates I went on made me fall in love with the people living in Moonstone Island. I just wish I had more I could do once I've exhausted all the "date" events. Considering how in-depth everything else is in the game, I really can't be upset with what I was given. I'll be excited to see if they plan on adding more when it comes to that part of the game.

For those who don't care about romance and interacting with the villagers, there's still so much more to Moonstone Island. From the wonderful main story of helping Magic Man and becoming a fully fledged alchemist, to taking down fearsome boss monsters and discovering every Spirit, there's tons to do. If you like the farming, they did a great job making those crops mesh well with the combat, and I was always excited to discover a new plant to cultivate. Your alchemist tent (house) has plenty of customization options to decorate and expand it, and I'm sure people can waste hours on that alone.

In conclusion, I loved my time as an alchemist, and I hope the developers plan on including more in the future. I will be very excited to revisit Moonstone Island. If you're on the fence about getting it, I'd say hop off, jump on your broomstick, and crash-land into paradise.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6.5/10 and here's why:
This was a very ambitious project. The idea of merging a monster collector with a farming simulation game is not unheard of but is still very complicated to get balanced. While I enjoy the color, art, and concept. I can't help but feel like this game failed to achieve that harmony. For example, I adore the concept art on the cards and I love the designs of the little monsters, but the card battling is unfair at times. Despite what level your monsters are, if they are poisoned or burned a significant amount, they can be toppled by a lower-level monster. On the flip side of that argument, you could use the same strategy against a higher-level monster. This made Bosses feel too easy. The story aspect of the game also felt 2-D. The characters quickly go through all of their dialog and it doesn't take long for them to start repeating. There is also no character growth or change within the characters as the player develops relationships with them. The dialog stayed the same. This was a major let-down as personally, I like to focus a lot on the story. The NPC felt flat. The town felt Flat. The only character I was interested in was Waldo because he was a weirdo. I also didn't use the potion station. It felt useless since most of the time I burned my enemies to death. I also didn't start farming until way later in the game. There wasn't a real need for it since players could find most crops on other islands. I only started to grow exp. plants because I didn't feel like grinding up levels. That was also another issue, I love grinding levels in games but in this one, it felt... unsatisfying.

Overall, would I suggest this, yeah kind of? It definitely wouldn't be my first choice that's for sure but it's not a terrible game. I can tell that the developers do care but there's still a lot of work that needs to go into this game. As I stated before, this was an ambitious project, especially for a small group. I'll be watching to see if the developers add anything but for now, it's an "eh".\t\t\t\t\t

A simple game with tons of charm, Moonstone Island is a jack-of-all-trades game that remains fun throughout even though it never really masters any one element.

Visuals
The game looks pretty darn good. It kind of reminds me of the GBA Legend of Zelda titles but with greater detail. The art for the characters is also great. The game took obvious inspiration from Studio Ghibli and it adds a ton to the game for me. I could see it not being you're cup of tea, but it's an easy 5/5 for me.

Music
The game's music is gorgeous. It really fits the game and it never gets in the way of the experience. Great soundtrack, particularly the battle themes <3. They should really make it so that the menu theme's volume can be adjusted separately from in-game (it's always at base loudness when you boot, it's never hurt my ears, but it's a little bothersome).

Spirits and Battles
The deckbuilding battles are fun to play and even funner to build. You have 3 energy to spend on a given turn, and you spend them on a deck comprised of all 3 of your creatures moves shuffled together. You can add cards to Spirits decks, upgrade them, or delete them with runes. It's not particularly balanced (looking at you Psychic Spirits) and you can do some crazy broken stuff, but it is absolutely fun. Unfortunately all of the creatures of the same element are effectively interchangeable (they learn all the same moves, their stats are the same, and they will always share 1 specific weakness). This means that when you deckbuild, you do so with Spirit types and not necessarily with creatures themselves (with the exception being their abilities). While this is still loads of fun, something like a signature card for each creature could go a long way for making each of them feel more unique in battle. I love that some of them have abilities that effect the social/exploration/farming side of the game. Armbot's "Long Arms" ability allowing me to pick up items in the overworld from a longer range is a great example and such a neat idea. Unfortunately I couldn't keep him in my party without throwing my combat strategy wildly off balance, so I only brought him out occasionally. I would like a reserve system that would allow spirits to follow you for effects while also keeping them out of your combat. There also isn't anything you can do with your creatures outside of having them follow you, feeding/brushing them in the barn, or battling with them, which is fine ig, but anyone looking to interact with their creatures any more because its a farming sim will be disappointed.

The Islanders
I like the people on the island, I really do. They have their quirky personalities, their likes and dislikes, you get it. But the town itself doesn't really feel quite alive. Maybe it's because Moonstone Island doesn't really have much of culture. There aren't any events or festivals to partake in, no games to play, or anything else fun to do with them besides dates. It's all very insulated. I was shocked when I realized that the New Years fireworks event was the only one in the game and it's not much of one. The dates are quite good, and a highlight of this part of the game. Strangely, while you can "enter a relationship" with someone by giving them a moonstone ring this doesn't actually change anything about your relationship except allow you date them at their house and to sleep in their bed. While I understand the implications of such an act, particularly after an exciting date, I can't help but feel the game is missing something really obvious here. After exhausting all the dates and being in a relationship with them for a while I was expecting something to change. Unique text boxes to appear or perhaps marriage? While I wasn't excited for this part of the game in particular I am still a bit disappointed by its limits. The game, it's main conflict, and by extension the town, kind of stop existing past the first year (no new newsletters, events, or quests) which is a bit of a shame. The game has an excellent setup to add rich content to the later years over time, and I hope to see such additions in future updates.

Exploration
Exploration is fun, particularly at the start when you don't know what everything is or what to expect. Each island is based on a Spirit's element and will only have the matching creatures, fish, and that element's seasonal plants. The fruits of the fields are plentiful and goofily themed. Hot air balloons, pinwheel flowers, and lightning bolt shaped plants abound. These are all great to find and collect. The very act of flying from one island to another, when I don't know what to expect, is a joy. Finding statues that teleport you to other islands or rune gates to upgrade your Spirits are also fun treats. Lightning islands make it harder to traverse them they taking a bit of your stamina while you're on them, Fire island make it harder to traverse them they taking a bit of your stamina while you're on them, and Dark islands make it harder to traverse them they taking a bit of your stamina while you're on them. Yeah, it's a bit generic in that aspect. There is little else different between two islands of the same element outside of what I've mentioned besides layout. They have put a ton of effort into making each island feel distinct (as the layouts are all 'handmade' as it were), it will still suffer a bit from a lack of variety by the end of the game.

Dungeons, and Temples
Each island has a random chance of harboring a dungeon, which are Legend of Zelda like mini-trials that offer a variety of hidden loot and enemies to challenge you. These puzzles are fun, but repeat similar elements, losing a lot of their appeal after the 10th dungeon or so, despite their lovingly crafted layouts <3. You don't need to complete more than 10-15 or the games 50 dungeons, but I went for the achievements on this one. The game's Seasonal Temples are, in my opinion, the most enjoyable parts of the game and something I always looked forward to. They function similarly to how pokemon's gyms do, with a bit of legend of zelda items mixed in there. The 4 items you receive from doing the 4 temples are all very useful and interesting and the bosses are an absolute blast. I was worried that I would overlevel and overpower all of the bosses, and while I did still do that a bit, because the bosses are all gimick battles, I still feel like I got the interesting and engaging experience the developers intended me to. It doesn't hurt that the boss battle theme is amazing. An incredible, iconic feature of this game for me.

Farming and Customization
The game has farming. It works like it has always worked since the very first harvest moon game back in 1996. Here it is easily automated, so If you don't particularly like this aspect of the game you don't need engage with it. But if you are into this stuff the game gives you the tools to have a blast, make a huge farm, and some huge income. I enjoyed it, even if I didn't go quite as wild with it as some will. The same is true for customization. If you like the idea of making furniture and decorations for your house, this game has got it and you will get some good mileage out of it. You can even customize Moonstone island itself! You can't remove much there, but you can add! But, there's no "auto RIZZ" ability, so sometimes a "joke", or "flirt" will actually FAIL, and lose you points!! I actually really like that mechanic! Definitely a refreshing new take that adds some dimension and complexity to a classic element of most farming sims! I like that romancing a villager is more than just checking the wiki for their favorite gift and then spam-gifting them until they love you! Yes, you can still gift villagers, but I found that I prefer building hearts through talking and asking them on (romantic & friendship) dates.

The only criticism I have is that the controls and some things related to inventory management are not very intuitive, and could use some quality-of-life tweeks. For example, moving items in and out of chests, selling items in the sell crate, etc. (It can be a little tedious, at times, when sorting, organizing, & selling, because there's no shift-click to move a whole stack, so you either have to add 1 item at a time, or click and drag the stack into the desired slot). I like the tool wheel, since it frees up space in my inventory, but it takes some getting used to, and can also be a bit tedious at times when swapping between tools. The click & hold method for picking up items is handy (so you don't accidentally pick up something you didn't intend to)...unless you're swapping out floor or wall tiles for and entire room. There is definitely a learning curve when it comes to the controls, and sometimes I find myself checking the bottom right corner of the screen to double check which control will perform the desired action.

Controls aside, this is definitely a great addition to my farming/crafting game library! It's cute, cozy, and fun!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy feelings on this game are mixed - leaning towards negative at the moment.... however, I don't want to be entirely unfair since there are some decent things here... so I'm not giving it a Thumbs Down.

The environments are nice. The Spirits and Combat System are nice. Farming seems very simple so far, but I have yet to really dive into it.

The big negative here is how..... one-dimensional.... all the townsfolk seem. You don't really "talk" to them... you just hit buttons with percentage chances of giving you points towards Friendship.... beyond that, they only really say anything if you initially meet them or they have a quest for you. I feel like the developers focused too much on that pseudo liberal "diversity" of appearance and not enough on diversity of personality and depth and character - what's actually important in a person. And honestly... on that... the characters more or less look all the same... or at least enough that I don't really think any of them are more interesting or special than another. I couldn't care less about any of the NPCs, but if I had to pick one, it'd be the Blacksmith because at least SHE kinda seemed interesting in the one unique thing she said when I talked to her.

Anyway... I like the monsters and that there's a lot of them and dungeons to find, and the occasional secret hole with ore and treasure is pretty nice too. I like the concept of jumping from island to island and being able to build on any of them (though I feel like trying to build on any island but the one the town is on is hardly worthwhile and more of a time sink than anything else. There's plenty of space for likely anything you could ever do north of the town.

The combat system is pretty simple - pick cards to use, draw cards, try to strategically use the cards, upgrade them, or get new ones from leveling up your spirits or the occasional shrine you'll come around.

All in all, I give this a 5/10 - 5 points off solely for the dreadfully boring NPCs.... it's that big a deal for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the whole concept and the art style is well done, with awesome game-play loop and mechanic, the control and UX design of the whole game is just sooooo bad that I cannot recommend the game at this stage.

First of all, why the heck does the UI not support keyboard navigation? Out of all kinds of game, I would never expect 2D farming sim to not have that supported. Worse still, you also can't play the game only via mouse. You have to use keyboard to move around, no click and go there style of control. Both of this already turned it into a hard-to-control game that is not intuitive at all.

Second, the interaction keybind and the whole 'tool' slot and that tool wheel selection mechanic is just so bad that I wonder if they have actually play-tested the game. One example is, somehow, they messed up the scrolling so bad that it often only move 1 slot to the left or right no matter how much you scrolled. Then, there's that tool wheel. Below is an example on what you need to do JUST to swap to a different tool:

1. See that 'q'? Yeah. You have to hold it for a while, so the tool wheel pops up.
2. Oh are you expecting to just keep holding the 'q', and drag your mouse to the direction of the tool you wanna select? Nope. You have to release the 'q'. The wheel doesn't disappear btw. (Yea it aren't a so called control wheel but more of a dumb inventory UI)
3. Then, you have to click the slot of the tool you want to select. Oh but be careful not to scroll! Cause apparently those aren't tool slots, but instead kinda a 'quick access' binding slots. If you scroll, it instead just change the tool those slots bind to.

Who designed such a control system like this?! It would be miles better just placing all the tools on the hot bar instead! Remember this is just an example of one of the weird decision they made regarding controls. There's a lot more issues like that which I can't fully list here.

Next issue, a farming / collector sim like this should have focus on the character building of the villagers and the 'Spirits'. However, I am greatly disappointed when I clicked the 'chat...' button and it just pop up some hearts instead. They didn't even bother to write dialog for that! Come on!

Same happens to most of the 'expected' interactions, like you can't talk to your 'spirits', and such. Basically, all npcs only really have story dialogs and 1 to 2 lines of dialogs for when you start an interaction. That's it.

The whole game has really cool gameplay concepts, with wonderful art styles. I can see that there may be deep lore of the world too. However, the first impressions of the game just leave me with such a bad taste that I just don't enjoy playing the game, even tho the concepts sounds like a fun game. The terribly designed controls combined with lack of polishes for the npcs just make me feel like I am playing those simple story rpg game, with extra farming mechanics in it.

I would have to say I am greatly disappointed, and I hope that the developers would spend a few more months focused on polishing the player experiences.\t\t\t\t\t
