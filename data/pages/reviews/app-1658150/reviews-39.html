\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing my way through Stardew Valley multiple times and modding it for even more playthroughs, I was looking for a simple game to lose myself in. After trying a few other farming games, Moonstone Island is the one that has stuck the most. It is the simplest parts of Pokemon and Stardew mixed. It has a cozy, no-rush attitude about the game. Even the card battling system is easy to pick up.
This game doesn't hold your hand though. You drop on an island with minimal prep and that's it. I have a few websites pulled up on my iPad so that I can reference them, but nothing in the game tells you what to do. Some may love that (I kinda do too).
It would be nice to be able to improve your look, or upgrade the max energy outside of a fight, but those are minor issues. Otherwise, I have really enjoyed this game and all it has to offer. Can't beat a $20 game (I got it on sale though) that plays better than some $70 games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been 35 hours and I feel like I can make a review now. Usually I am super critical, because a lot of "indie" games can just absolutely miss the mark, BUT THIS GAME. This game. It needs work, but it is awesome. I have played so so many early access, farm sim, "cozy", rpg adventure games and this one takes the cake. I love this game. I am so excited to see where it goes and what they do with it. I plan on buying all the DLC to support the devs and artists.

Why is it good?
The introduction is quick and to the point. You get to start playing immediately. The balance between caring about NPC's and exploring is nice. There's not too much pressure to stay in the village. The game lets you know from the beginning that rushing is not an option and that you'll need to take your time to invest in your base and monsters before getting to the higher tier items and monsters. It's continually fun.

What's meh?
The dungeons aren't really that great. They can be repetitive and not very difficult. The days are too short so you don't get enough done, but you can make another tent on different islands to combat this so there's that. There is no monster evolution which is a bummer. I'm hoping they'll add more to it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR Pokemon Dew Valley with Card Battling Mechanics

Overall I like the game but here is a list of things that can improve the experience:

- Add Character customization

- The Caves and Dungeons are all copy pasted and boring

- Some of the Spirits are just rips from other properties, or are just boring and uninspired looking

- Spirits can greatly benefit from an "Evolution" or "State Change" after levelling to a certain point. This would make use of a lot of the weaker Earth or Water spirits you find early + add more team building options.

- Make new cave and dungeon designs that change based on biome + more cave/dungeon exclusive spirits

- For the love of god, add a "Feed All" Option for the Spirit Barn

- Make fibre last a little longer in the Spirit Barn (2-3 days at least)

- Allow for installation of Sprinklers in the Greenhouse - this is a HUGE oversight in my opinion

- Post Game: Add a "Battle Tower" like area in the Post-Game to promote people playing beyond the short story, and will also promote players to train more Spirits and trial different team compositions out for the hard challenge

- Make caves in later game biomes have multiple floors with harder enemies + better loot as you progress, would help make caves less boring

- Add a Pocket Dimension for Fishing, enabling us to catch every fish from all the seasons/biomes

- Ice Spirits feel like an after thought, either add more or make a few islands on each pole side (north/south or east/west) so they are not seasonal

- Add the option to "Assign" Spirits to different tasks/houses, example:
I assign my Armbot to my Greenhouse or Spirit Barn to take advantage of the item pick up range buff in that area
Or, I assign a Bulbot to accompany me into Caves/Dungeons so I can take advantage of the Light buff

- In addition to the above, add 3 additional spots to the Party, so we can better take advantage of the passives some Spirits have, without having to adjust my battle trio

- More Relationship/Character events

- More Township events

- Add a perk later on in the Skill Tree for "Plant new seed after harvesting", ideally after the "50% chance to get seeds on harvest" it simply saves us a bit of time/clicks

- Holo Radar or something to improve chances of encountering Holo Spirits (if it exists, I just haven't gotten there yet)\t\t\t\t\t
