\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally enjoyed this and will likely play again when it ports to switch.. The spirit and charm of this game are evident but a lot of polishing and additional content will need to be added. The early game challenges and seeing multiple spirits/building progressive modes of transportation is the most enjoyable time of the game.

Overall a positive experience but I do think some people will be thrown off and here are a list of cautions:

-Exploration/Task Management/Time Management is a challenge in the beginning. I ended up just taming stronger spirits instead of bothering with leveling.
-Resources do not respawn I was able to get everything I wanted built but it's a little offputting that I basically razed islands to the ground
-You reach a hard stop with relationships where your S/O ends up repeating the same 2-3 lines to you in a given season.
-2 of the 8 Spirit types are inaccessible for 75% of the game. There is a lack of end game content after year 1.
-Holos are not inspiring enough to keep me playing beyond the main game.
-Game Balance could use some reworking, one type is far and above anything else in the game and is attainable fairly early on.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game needs some love from the developer... It's in a rough state right now. The characters are charming on the surface level, and the art is nice... That's about where the positive things I have to say end. MOONSTONE IS A BLIGHT! Only getting 1-2 from each island is absolute abysmal. The ramping up from the early ores being available in excess to needing moonstone for damn near everything in large quantities is absurd. Also making the barn require 3 moonstone right off the bat to even start collecting creatures in a game where capturing a ton of these little/big guys is the main idea is asinine. There needs to be some sort of early game creature inventory and maybe another tier of ore to help make the early-mid-late game jumps not feel so soul crushing and grind-y. My other major complaint is that the writing and depth of the characters and interactions are abysmally shallow. It's not even a voiced game, there is room to have more dialogue, and it's an easy fix, but the characters all feel so shallow... For a life sim type game I want more depth please and thank you.\t\t\t\t\t
