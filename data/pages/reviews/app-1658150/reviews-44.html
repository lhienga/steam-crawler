\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game, it has all the things i like in a life-sim.
Bright, cute visuals, some combat elements, magical stuff, exploration, creature collecting, base building, but that is the problem right there. It feels like they tried to shove everything in this game without asking if it'll all mesh well, and it really doesnt.
The combat is fun if you like card games, but gets repetitive very fast. The magic elements are barely utilized at all, sure you get a broom but it all feels very cosmetic. The exploration is based on procedual generation of islands and mines, and like most games that use procedural generation it all just kinda feels like same after a while with some cosmetic differences. Creature collecting is too easy, and there arent nearly enough varieties of spirit to make it worth it, and you dont do nearly enough with them. Base building is empty, theres about a bajillion useless cosmetics you can make in-game to make up for the fact that you only have 4 crafting stations and barely any farming. As well as the fact that crafting is also useless, you have a smelter, which can only make one bar at a time and you have to manually change it out for every bar, a magic station, which makes the only useful stuff in the game, a decoration station, a whole crafting station just for the useless cosmetics, and the anvil, which makes charms that you're probably going to forget about in 5 mins because they are only used on certain islands against certain enemies(Poison, Fire, and Darkness spirits) and you have to keep them in your inventory forever just to use them which makes me remember that YOU CANT INCREASE YOUR INVENTORY IN THIS GAME.
I play lots of games with Stamina systems, they work decent in other life-sim games but the one here feels off, exploration feels like it was tuned for a game without a stamina system as you use massive chunks of stamina to travel between islands, which considering that travelling between islands is the only way to find dungeons and progress means that you feel like you never have enough to actually progress in the game without carrying around about 20 stonefruit everywhere you go.
And i know this is going on a little long but one more thing, the Moonstone Island itself is very nice at first, everyone is nice and welcoming and the portraits are cute, then you realize how empty it all feels. Then you realize that theres only 11 npcs and all of them are age-appropriate romance options, no kids, no old people, no married couples, it makes the island feel empty and artificial, theres just not enough people to talk to here, and the ones you get are all just 'nice' in a very bland way. Apparently the 'diversity' is supposed to be some big thing in the game but they all feel super bland even if they wear neon colors and call themselves 'they'.
Sorry for this massive rant but i just needed to get this all off my chest for a game i really wanted to like and just didnt.\t\t\t\t\t
