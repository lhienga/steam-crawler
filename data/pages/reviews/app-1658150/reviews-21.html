\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this because I generally enjoy the casual, cozy genre, but the progression is a little too frustrating imo. Too much progress is gated behind Moonstones (which can be found once per island per season), and not enough is gated behind the actual money in the game.

The start of the game is charming enough, and the card battling is implemented very well, but you will quickly find yourself drowning in gold with nothing to spend it on while you wait for the season to change so you can get a few more moonstones and actually progress the game. This frustratingly invalidates any reason to farm for cash; you will easily get all the money you could ever need by just turning in Spirit Research. By the end of my playtime I found myself just farming Moonstones as hard as possible to make as much Moonstone Grass as possible so I didn't have to spend half of every day feeding my mons. Needless to say this started feeling like a chore pretty quickly. This is not helped by how flat basically all of the characters felt. I enjoyed Ferra's dialogue, but most of the other characters have basically one personality trait (timid twink, mean goth girl, Irish fisherman, etc.)

Games like Stardew Valley hit a nice pace by making it possible to do a lot in a short period, but not punishing you for not accomplishing enough, letting you set your own pace. Moonstone Island hits a frustrating inverse by gating your progress with Moonstones and harshly limiting your access to them. If this game gave you useful ways to spend your money and let you play at your own pace, I would recommend it, but in its current state it leaves me more frustrated than relaxed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this game, but with some caveats.

If you like monster collection games, farming games, and card games, you will almost certainly get some fun out of this one. Game has heavy ghibli studio inspiration, good music, and a really relaxing game play atmosphere/loop - you will for sure get 20+ hours of enjoyment out of this game, no need to spoil your fun just go get it. Don't let the rest of the review sour playing the game for you.

Now for some things that may turn people off the game if they are on the fence still:

First off, it's impossible not to think of the phrase "an ocean a mile wide but only an inch deep" while playing this game. Sure, there are tons of islands, and ~70 monsters to explore and collect, but they are virtually all the same. Same thing with the dungeons, there is very little uniqueness to any of them. You've seen a few dungeons, you have seen them all. All of the monsters have extremely similar cards, the abilities on them don't change much - and even the different species of monsters all have fairly little difference between them. There is a little bit of nuance to some species, but even then it's all just kind of meh. If you play optimally most people would probably end up with pretty similar strategies I'd imagine - as there isn't really that much unique play styles between the monsters. Pretty simple to get a near infinite loop going for cheese, or later game you're really just stacking damage and ways to play more cards.

There is a wide variety of plants, and lots are actually useful in their own ways, but the plants you get from the later seasons are quite a bit more powerful than earlier ones that you really should almost sleep speed run through your first year.

Then there are the characters - I will be honesty this was probably my least explored option. I didn't do any dates, and only got to ~4/10 hearts with most people. It is possible this is where a lot of the story get's developed, but aside from 1 or 2 characters, none really intrigued me that much. The whole year they all say pretty generic stuff for the most part - again, maybe the dating system REALLY fleshes them out, but I never was interested enough to engage much in it. Also, if your not really for the whole "woke" thing, that will be a huge turn off for you as nearly every character seems to be gender non-conforming or gay. I know technically this is the same thing as something like stardew valley, but gender ideology among the characters seems to be much more prevalent in this game (each character has gender and pronouns for themselves with lots of non-binary they/thems, most characters have dyed hair, strong female characters, feminine male characters ect). For me it's whatever, but there was a couple parts where I couldn't help but think "put a chick in it and make her gay and lame!".

Lastly there is a swath of kind of poor coding things/minor annoyances. As an example, planting huge crops is a major pain because the clicking to place stuff is pretty inconsistent, and not smooth like it is in stardew valley. I have to really move my mouse around and character around all over to make sure everything plants properly. Harvesting you have to constantly move - stop - harvest. It's just not as smooth as most people would probably be used too from other farming games. There were a few crashes I had from entering/leaving dungeons while a monster attacked me resulting in some decent time loss. Getting around all the islands can be a bit of a slog - even with the max upgraded movement device it still takes a while to get around.

Early game getting some resources is an absolute pain just to do things you need to do for the main game loop (like getting stalls that auto-feed, tool upgrades, buildings ect.). Some things also aren't explained in game very well, like for example resources on some islands need a stronger scythe to harvest - I had to look it up to know that. Lots of little things that just could have been smoothed out with maybe a teleport device or some easier acquisition of some resources.

There are also temples - which are like little trials that you get a new item - the issue is the items you get basically are only used for that temple that can be ignored. Stuff like that just makes me think the game wasn't fully fleshed out or some ideas didn't get polished.

And that is kind of where I see it now - good game, but a lot of features just didn't get that ending polish that would have made it a great game. Is it good to blow through a weekend or a few days to satisfy some game A.D.D.? Totally - is it a game I will likely keep coming back to and starting a new file like stardew valley, probably not. Just not enough depth, not much of an end game, and doing one thing of a certain type you have pretty well done all of it.

So give it a go - but don't expect a mind blowing experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing the game for a few in-game days I was so excited by the premise I gifted my wife a copy of the game. You can tell the game is made with so much love, It is not fair to call this game a Pokmon/Stardew Valley hybrid, as it is clearly its own thing.

I had a huge problem with my PC and Steam Deck files - I played for hours on my PC and then wanted to play on my deck and even though the files appeared as updated on the cloud, I lost 4 days worth of progress. This happened in two different instances. For someone who has limited time (and has been this invested in the game) this is a huge disservice. I tried rolling back my save files and nothing.

This is horrible, but you know what's worse? The first time I lost my progress I lost access to the potion-making station, and because concoctions are so hard to find, my second time playing that save file it took me 5 days to get another concoction I could sell to unlock the station. Now I lost my save file *AGAIN* and who knows when I'll find a concoction again.

Other problems I have with the game that make no sense in my opinion:
- You can't use monster taming items on a dazed monster. This makes taming monsters unnecessarily more difficult. It's weakened. Why can't I tame it? Why is there no such item for dazed-taming?
- You can accidentally use a fertilizer infinite times on the same spot, spending your fertilizer unnecessarily if you're spamming.
- Accessing the monsters takes way too many clicks. There isn't a menu to heal monsters?
- Healing a monster takes several items, and you cannot heal if your monster is dazed.
- When trying to upgrade a card, there isn't an option to "upgrade again" if there are several upgrade items you can use.
- Forging takes 3 ore but you can't stack several ores to continue forging.

I'll finish this with something everyone is saying: The barn is *TOO DARN EXPENSIVE* to upgrade and use. There are so many requirements to be able to freely catch spirits. Level caps? available barn spots? Why gate fun so hard when you can just make it slightly more accessible to everyone?

Maybe I will revisit this game when I feel like my time has not been wasted. All I can say is I hate concoctions now :lol:\t\t\t\t\t
