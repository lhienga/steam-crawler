\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am the exact target audience for a game like this (cozy, wholesome, etc.). I'm a huge fan of games like Stardew, Littlewood, and Slay the Spire. This game is not really a farming sim, even though you can (and probably should) farm to get crops that will help you recover HP and tame spirits in combat. Rather than a cross of Stardew and Slay the Spire, to me it's more like a cross between Animal Crossing New Horizons and Slay the Spire. I mean that it's more focused on the card game combat, adventuring to different islands, and completing quests than it is on farming or earning gold. You can also ignore the quests that don't resonate with you without much (if any) repercussions. For example, I ignored all but the very first alchemy brewing quests in my first year and no one expressed disappointment at the end of the year (no <4 candle type situation for those who know Stardew). I've heard comparisons of this game to Pokemon, but I don't know how accurate that is since I've never played one of those games. I can say though that the combat is fun, the spirits are cute, the villagers are charming and diverse, and the fishing is now more accessible than it used to be. The game is regularly upgraded with improvements and I've had a fun time so far playing a couple of hours here and there over the months since release.

It's of course, not a perfect game. The RNG on the fish, for example, caused me to delay completing the temples for so long that they became comically easy. I would have liked to see those more scripted encounters scale up with my party's increased level. I also understand that the art was hand drawn etc. etc. but I would have appreciated the ability to change my character's physical appearance. Even just a slider to change the skin tone and hair color to more closely reflect who I am would have been nice. I also believe there are supposed to be holo spirits, but I've yet to encounter one, and some quests I want to do remain unfulfilled because it is so difficult to find the right spirit (I'm really sorry Ferra!). Things like this are not dealbreakers for me though, and I hope they won't be for you. The game has been a lot of fun for the past 60 hours I've played of it, so it's still incredibly worth the money even at full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is only barely a not-recommended for me, and since it gets regular updates I'm hoping that this changes over time. Mechanically, this game is phenomenal! The farming, fishing, crafting, exploring, etc. all work with no issues and feel good to do. It's fun! As far as the depth of the game though, that's where it falls flat and why I don't recommend it.

The characters are all incredibly interesting when you first meet them, and then your experiences with them are really limited. The town itself feels static, as nothing really changes over time or happens there. It feels a little like if you played the entire game without ever stopping to talk to somebody in town, your playthrough would only be marginally different from somebody who talked to everyone every day. I found that this meant I had to be really self-motivated when making goals in this game, because even for all the quest log updates I got, I never felt like I was connecting with the town and people and doing things because of that connection. I was just grinding through the game because I wanted to play it, and that motivation became difficult to sustain. Your character itself strikes a weird balance between not having any pre-set personality, but also not being customizable in any way I've found. So, they're not a blank slate because you're meant to be able to fill yourself into their shoes, they're a blank slate that somebody else has lovingly and intricately designed, which really exacerbates the fact that they're a blank slate. Why do I visually have so much personality if there is never a single point in the game where I express any personality? I never felt very connected to my character for these reasons, too.

The spirits are exciting and the elemental islands are cool as you discover each new one, but that hits a wall pretty quickly as you realize there are 100 islands but only around 10 templates for islands. So once you've seen your first poison type island, you've seen them all. That took some of the excitement out of exploring for me and was the point at which I decided to write this review. I enjoyed what this game had to offer as far as actually playing went, but the story that's present and the worldbuilding that's there is a weird mix of built up enough that you can't fully make it your own but also empty enough that you may struggle to get invested. And again, this game is receiving regular updates, so I hope this does change over time! The foundation for this game is 100% there, but beyond that I found it fell short.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn original concept combining farming life sims, monster tamers, and card battlers. It draws you in with the farming, then motivates exploration to advance and build your farm, which then requires raising a strong team of creatures, which then motivates more farming and exploration to power your team up. Very addictive and immediately satisfying, not a painful slow burn that requires at least 10 hours to start seeing results.

As with any new concept, there is tweaking that would improve the game's progression system, and I hope the devs read these comments for ideas in developing the sequel or DLC, which I am now excitedly anticipating. I find myself wanting to continue unlocking better modes of transportation and keep exploring wider maps or new worlds, and wish there was a level of procedural generation to dungeons that allowed me to keep delving. I spent a day just flying off the map to see if there was more to it and was disappointed there wasn't even an Easter egg. Farming is well executed, discovering new fruits, raising crops to craft stronger and stronger potions. I wish there was more to unlock, maybe some secrets that happen if you raise crops a certain way. I also wish stronger potions were more valuable. It would be interesting to have other NPCs as alchemists or monster tamers. The monsters are my favorite part. A fishbowl with legs, a newt with a snorkel, a beachball with sunglasses; when I first started playing I had a theory this game was a dream a child had and his toys had come to life. I don't necessarily think evolving the creatures in necessary, but it would be interesting to see a more rewarding leveling system, since it seems a change of seasons allows for more honing of a creature's ability than actually training and leveling them up. The battle system is cool and fun to play with, there are some clear winners as far as strongest types and creatures, identifying this had made the battles trivial but finding exploits is part of what makes card battlers fun. I'd like more types, more cards, more skill effects, varied abilities. The friendship system is fun because the artwork is gorgeous and my heart melts when I get the seed girl to smile, I've just been too distracted to actually learn her name.

I haven't been able to lose myself in a game like I've lost myself in this in a long time. I want more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt has unfinished aspects - such as mines not refreshing each season so you have a finite amount of ores but they do give you a ton of ores so I have never been left wanting for more and I've done do few mines and have more than enough. I would like to see more crafting recipes for useful equipment not just decorative items and I'm at the point where I have so much money but nothing to spend it on so I would like more items I could buy. I would also like to have dungeons reset every year but progressively get harder with each year.

With all that being said, I have finish almost every quest (only 2 unimportant side quests that i'm just too lazy to do), and I still have more I would like to do with the world past the main story line such as grinding holo spirits (Like shiny Pokemon), taming every spirit, building my team up, doing every date with every character, modifying every island, and decorating my home.

Things that I'm really enjoy:
-There are some amazing romanceable characters (Ofelia and Paolo are peak datable characters in any game I've played)
-I have never liked card based combat but this game has changed that for me. I usually brute force my way through combat but this game sucked me in with different card strategies and got me looking into other card based combat games.
-Farming holo Spirits with my favorites being Wolfbane, Evileye, Arkeya, and Marsha (although I think the holo versions need work on some of the spirits - like Bonguin and Sorbaton)
-The Dungeons
-Maxing out every holo just for the fun of it
-Strategizing with my cards

Overall. really fun game and I would definitely recommend it but it does feel like it has potential to be more.
