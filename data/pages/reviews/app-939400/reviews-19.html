\r\n\t\t\t\t\t!! The only way to get a happy ending is to diligently follow EXACTLY what it says on the Steam guides - you can't even mess up a single choice.

Didn't know you click this random thing in the background? Break up
Didn't know you can ignore dialogue options? Break up
Held my hand at the wrong time? Break up

Bruh...

Okay, let me explain what I mean here... I started playing excitedly, (games like these are usually such a fun time) only to get the worst possible ending on the first go. "Okay, fine" I said, and tried again making different choices this time, but the couple broke up again. Upon googling, I realised you can make 'out of the box' choices like clicking on other things other than dialogue, like items on the screen and wait for scenes to end without choosing anything. How is anyone meant to know this, I wonder... There's no instructions anywhere. (Also the 'good' choices never look right - probably translation issues?)

That's when I decided that I was going to follow the detailed instructions on the Steam forums to try and get the good endings to avoid ending up disappointed again. It was fun following these steps at first, but even that didn't last very long lol. If you miss one beat, done, you're not getting the good ending anymore. And the kicker is you need to be a magician to actually guess what you're meant to do and when. I thought that once I finish a scenario with guidance, it'd be easy to kind of guess how I'm meant to handle the rest by myself. But the things you need to do are just so random and unexpected, that I have serious respect for people that have 'cracked the code' on how to make these relationships work.

Long story short, the game has real potential with its creativity in design, but the only way you find out about that creativity is by missing it and getting frustrated and then heading to Google. The answers might be hidden in plain sight, but I consider it very odd that the game writers never let you know you CAN actually do x y z. It overall leaves you feeling unaccomplished and sad at the end.

I know it's a very affordable game, which would've been great for people looking to play something cute and cosy on a budget, but I still wouldn't recommend it, as in the end, I didn't have fun playing it, and I can't describe it as cute or cosy.
