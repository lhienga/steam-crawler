\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't really want to give a thumbs up or down for this game. Depending on how you look at it, it's both female and male oriented. It shows you what the woman would want in a man, however at the same time it feels very off. It's implying that the guy has to always know exactly what to do and what she likes. Instead of just asking her where she'd like to go you're forced to do it for her. The girl has very little role in the relationship, she just goes with what you do. There's a lot of good messages in here, but this game implies that men have to do everything in the relationship. Men decide where to go, men have to listen but girls don't, in fact you don't even tell the girl anything other then the one phone conversation, other then that it's all about pleasing the girl, men have to initiate and do everything. At the end, she writes a letter basically rating how you are. While it makes sense from a game standpoint it also makes you wonder things. She's talking about all your flaws, and then dumping you after 3 straight years of being together just like that. If you do enough things right, she'll continue her relationship with you. It's as if she is rating you, like you can't make mistakes in a relationship at all. And if you do then the girl will just leave you behind randomly in a letter. She doesn't even talk to you about it, she just throws this at you in a letter and then leaves.

Looking more deeply at this game provides new insight. It appears cute but it has some dangerous messaging. It's trying to act as if men dominate the relationship. Men don't necessarily want to do that, I don't think we want to be expected to stalk a girl's socials and then always know exactly what to do at every given moment. The game has some really good design, but you don't even realize it. There's no hints telling you that you have to click things in the game. I only figured this out because I spam clicked everywhere. I didn't even realize you had to scroll out in order to do the phone conversation correctly. I get that it's trying to make you seek things out but how was I supposed to know to do that? The only indication is the game telling you how to act, but you don't know what to do to act that way. When presented with dialogue in a visual novel we tend to just pick one of the two options, even if we don't like either of them.

The game is nice, and cheap and all that, but I have a feeling it pushes some bad narratives. I've only played the first story so I'll try the other two out and edit this review after checking them out. For now, I'm giving it a negative, simply because I disagree with what it's trying to push in relationships. The game literally implies that unless you do everything right that you won't get the 'best ending.' Therefore, if you make a single mistake then a girl won't love you as much as if you have to be perfect for the girl to truly love you. Maybe some girls are like that but if they're a good woman they would be able to love a man regardless, just as much as if they didn't make mistakes. Making mistakes is human. It's implied that the guy had no relationship experience prior. How would he be expected to be perfect? Nobody should be expected to be perfect, experienced or not. This game reinforces older male tropes and the idea that men have to get the woman, that men have to conquer the girl, men have to be in charge. At the same time though, it acts like both people in a relationship should be equal. But, that's not what is happening. The guy is doing everything. Just because you started cleaning up the mess the girl made and then she said 'oh don't worry I'll help you out,' doesn't change the fact that everything else has been you, the man. And then the game cuts to three years later, where the girl just suddenly decides to either dump you or like you. So she is just now thinking of the few things that happened at the start of the relationship literally three years ago and just thinks 'oh, I should dump him because of how he acted 3 years ago.' It makes zero sense. If you were together another 3 years, surely more then those few things have been going on, and she would accept the mistakes you made already, right? But nope.

Hopefully the messages in the other two stories are better but I'm not changing it from negative until I've checked them out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game that many players will call cute and endearing, but anyone who looks just a little deeper will notice that it’s all style and no substance.

I bought the game because it looked cute and reminded me of Florence (a similar game). I only played the first chapter and didn’t bother with the other two because it pissed me off more than any Ubisoft game ever could. Here’s why:

Bad Game Design

I have been playing games for more than two decades, so trust me when I tell you: the game design in this one-hour-long game is just bad.

I finished the first chapter thrice and received a bad ending every time. She breaks up with you, no matter the dialogue option you selected. To get a neutral or good ending, you have to click on specific items on the screen, and sometimes not select any dialogue option. The game never mentions or hints in any way that not selecting any dialogue option is a valid choice here. When given two options, the first instinct will be to select one, even if you disagree with both options.

The game also doesn’t make it clear that you can select items on the screen and that they have an effect on the story. How was I supposed to know that you have to click on the guy's hand to “hold hands”? I only found out that these two things have an effect on the story because I checked the guide.

Also, replaying the first chapter is annoying due to the god-awful restaurant mini-game that takes one-third of the time of the entire chapter.

Story and Message

I am sorry; the relationship in this game is just annoying. The male character (you) has to stalk the girl’s socials to get information, and plan your date. That doesn’t sit right with me, thinking that a relationship won’t work because you picked the wrong movie and restaurant just feels stupid.

The girl’s character isn’t any better. She abruptly leaves you after three years with a letter stating everything that YOU did wrong and never mentions any of her faults? Really, why am I trying to be with her again? Heck, I would have preferred if she ghosted me instead of whatever that ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ was.

Go play Florence instead; it's much better and doesn’t leave a bad taste in your mouth.
