\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn one hand, the game is so cheap and short that strict scrutiny doesn't feel justified.

On the other, I don't understand how anyone can find anything profound in this game as there are no hard choices revolving around the relationships at all - you just need to think outside the box of visual novels and don't jump at the first given option(s).

Some might say it has positive messages about love and relationships but because of how brief the game is, the subject isn't treated with the level of nuance it needs and thus, those messages reveal how hollow they are if you think just a minute about it. It truly felt like a Dhar Mann video turned into a video game.

It is by no means something unworthy of the money but that's not because it's great but it is just cheap af. I think the top comments and the overall 'Overwhelmingly Positive' verdict unjustly hype the game up, so I'm going with a negative.\t\t\t\t\t
