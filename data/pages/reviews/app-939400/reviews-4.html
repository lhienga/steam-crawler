\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI won't be refunding this game as I do like to support indie devs, but this was very underwhelming.

As far as actual gameplay, the restaurant search minigame is easily in the top 5 most tedious minigames I've ever encountered. I can't speak to anything else because the tedium of this one minigame kept me from progressing past the first third of the available game modes.

Where the narrative is concerned, this feels like it was written by someone (likely under 18) who hasn't learned how healthy and functional adult relationships are supposed to work. Boiling the complexities of a relationship down to a choice of first date dinner and movie, etc. is very reductive. Saying partners need to bow to each other and that's the only way is downright wrong. It's like compromise and communication was somehow impossible. I didn't find any worthwhile "messages" about love in this game aside from the obvious need to put in effort. Although, ironically, the game doesn't give you any depth of options for "putting in effort".

Big spoilers follow:
And to be honest, the female love interest seems INTOLERABLE. She showed practically no interest in the protagonist when they met and just kind of went through the motions with him until they moved in together. Then it seems like she habitually trashed the place and constantly had the same generic complaints about her job that she refused to take any actionable steps to address. Then, when all was said and done she didn't even show him enough respect to break up with him in person. She wrote a passive-aggressive letter complaining about how he didn't try hard enough in the relationship and digging up petty ancient history about their first date 3 years prior???

Protag isn't completely absolved here, as I suspect he was merely attracted to her physically and just kind of ran with the ball when she didn't outright refuse his attention. Even though she seemed to lack passion for him.

I feel like protag dodged a massive bullet. Maybe now he can keep his head space and apartment clean without her trashing everything. I feel like the breakup is the best ending, albeit 3 years too late in the making.
