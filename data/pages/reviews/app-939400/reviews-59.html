Love Actually -- Lessons for not only romantic relationships but it can be applied to any relationship (friendships)

When we think of an otome/visual novel -- we're usually given expectations of something linear. Something scripted in a way that either sets us up for a specific route in mind. Or something that's so wild beyond belief that it's easy for us to suspend our realities and play along in the fantasy world of the game. But that's just it, a game. But LoveChoice is more than just a game, it's... love actually. Love in a way that isn't always happy or isn't always perfect.

GAME PLAY:
I, like many others, played the first story and got broken up with. I didn't know why and I was thoroughly confused -- especially since the choices seem limited. It seems like you only have a few options, but after playing through it once I realized that the choices felt shallow. (minor spoiler) And in the end letter, the game tells you that to some extent . I realized that there was something missing, something that didn't add up, and I realized that it's not at all as it seems. The first part has the most variances you can probably get and by testing out different ways to go about it, you'll be able to pick up on the rest of the game pretty easily -- but I will admit I was pretty challenged for a bit. I even succumbed to looking up the answers a few times. However, REMEMBER there's always another way (minor spoiler) until you get to the second story where I may be a liar.

ART:
Let's start with the art, it's simplistic but meaningful. It isn't something that's intensely decorated like Heart Fragment or maybe even Our Life, but it is very cute and simplistic until you start digging a little deeper. It's good though because for the amount of solving that you (as the player) must do, it helps prevent any unnecessary distraction. The music is also soft and sweet, sometimes even bitter. It fits well with the story you follow along with.

STORY/PLOT:
Now I'll talk about the stories - I'll try to be as vague as I can while providing solid content.

Story 1 this story is the one with the most variances in how you play. Everything matters, and the game will try to help you a bit by showing you small prompts but realize that not everything is as it seems. I failed a few times before I even realized that there is a puzzle element and an interactive element to the game -- something besides just clicking on an scripted answer. I didn't even realize that the game was a puzzle (clearly someone did not read any tags) until I played it the 3rd time. That being said PLAY IT MORE THAN 1 TIME. Like many other users here, this story not only teaches or reminds us about what love can be but how to have a healthy form of it.

That's the key isn't it? A healthy relationship with people? I'm sure everyone's heard of a toxic relationship or have been in one (friendships count just as much as romances!). It's hard to create and maintain if not everyone's on board. In fact, I'd argue that it's impossible to have a healthy relationship without everyone sacrificing a little bit of something. Even though the game play makes it seem obvious on what you should click on -- don't be afraid to think outside of the box. But play the game first however you feel is right, and let your results speak to you. Let them tell you exactly what went right or what went wrong. You don't have to have everything perfect and that's alright.

What relationship is ever picture perfect?

Story 2 As you can probably figure out, this story is about long distance. I''ll be completely transparent. I've never had a romantic partner drift away from me due to distance, but I've had loads of friends who have. Friends who I considered to be family at some point. It's hard trying to cling on to a friend who tries to cling on to you, but your lives are too dramatically different for you to be happy in that sort of friendship. I'm not saying that all friendships/relationships can't be happy, but I've found it to be very difficult if you've met the person and formed a relationship in real life and transitioning it to online.

The second story has the best bitter sweet plot. To some extent we know what will happen. We know the very real possibility of things not working out, but the optimist in us wants it to work out anyways. You'll feel the awkwardness, you'll feel the distance between the MC's (Grace and Stan in this story), and your heart will fracture just a bit because... No one wants to let go. I know I didn't want to let go, I know I never want to let someone so close to me go. You might feel different and that's totally okay, because you can let go. But I tried to keep it together until the end.

Remember what I said about -- there's always another option? This game might make me a liar but you get to decide at the end of this story. This story line was the first one to really make me think about my own trauma and my own life experiences. Why did I choose specific actions knowing it might not work out? Why did I stay optimistic throughout the game? These questions didn't necessarily pop up during the 1st story for me, but jumped out in this one. It might be because I've figured out how to play the game but it also might be because this plot hits closer to home for me.

The moral of this story is still carried from the last, what make a healthy relationship 'healthy?' Even though we might not want to do something I know for me it's a trauma response sometimes it's better to bite the bullet and 'just do it' as Nike says. Because at the end of the day, we have to love and have compassion for ourselves too.

Story 3, again, triggers another trauma response (I'm trying not to use this term loosely but after going through some professional therapy -- trauma isn't defined as clearly as we may think so I think it's fitting in this context) from me even though I can masquerade it as curiosity. When we hear romance and detective -- we might hope for the game to be something like a mystery romance murder. But with the track record of the first two stories being so realistic, this one probably won't tangent too far away from that.

This plot makes you a detective, a super slooth, and an international super spy (not really). You'll understand right away as the story kicks off pretty quickly and has a knack for making you suspicious. Unlike the other two, this one is much more linear. Once you pick a poison, you have it until the bitter end. It's really engaging because unlike the other two you get to roam around your apartment you share with your husband. It seems innocent enough, curiosity and all, but realistically why would anyone snoop around? What are we trying to find? This plot, since it's so much more linear, brings out our curiosities but also relates back to that ever important theme of 'healthy relationships.'

CONCLUSION
All of these stories have their own special thing going about them, and while everyone will pick up different lessons that they want. It's undeniable that there is a massive emphasis on the question 'what makes a healthy relationship and what makes it toxic?' I think it's an important question to ask, because I certainly didn't know the difference for a while. In fact, I still probably have problems distinguishing between the two. I'm aware of my own problems to some degree, but I didn't realize that they were prevalent enough to bleed into a game that's a master piece in my humble opinion.

If you're curious, like I was, or if you'd like a mini-dive into your own psychology (or just want to reflect on life for a bit) and not have it be uber intense. I think that this game is worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is by far the best romance game I have ever played, one of the best game ever honestly. I played trying really hard to 100% the game and I loved every second of it. The game phenomenal in portraying falling in love and maintaining a healthy relationship, because of that the game was very relatable.

Playing the game at first I got the break-up ending, because I suck at reading into things and always take everything at face value. This game has made me realize how to be better at relationships. I did better on Love Distance as a result, but I still got the bad ending. The second playthrough I got a better ending, but it was still not the satisfactory outcome.

Now the last ending of Love Distance, the whole reason I wanted to right this review. The long distance aspect I relate to so much, spending the days talking to your partner, connecting through mutual interests and certain things we both love so much. The ending where you break-up so because of the distance and both of you needing to have your future was bittersweet to me. It's how me and my ex broke up. The part where you have to click the box multiple times and keeps moving around is so accurate to hard it is to say "I want to break up", especially when you both still love eachother and don't want to. It happened to me and those words were the hardest to say in my entire life, we both loved eachother so much. I was devastated for a while afterwards, I felt like an import piece of me was gone, and it would take a very long time to get back. It took a while, but I got over her being completely gone from my life.

This game makes me want to reach out to her, but there needs to be more time for us to grow individualy. We're not ready to talk to eachother yet, I will wait until we are ready. There are still things I want to apologize for saying right after we broke-up, as I was heartbroken and didn't reflect my actual feelings. We probably won't ever be together again, but I hope in the future we will once again be friends.

I love you Valy <3\t\t\t\t\t
