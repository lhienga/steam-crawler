\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLovely sad and worth it for the price.

LORE:
The game is super cute and includes very reflexive and important lessons about love and trust. Makes you realize that sometimes it is not just about the happy ever after. Throughout the game, I was surprised by how the narratives are constructed, and made me feel reflexive about how in love there is not necessarily a “wrong side”. Well made and written because it feels like

MECHANICS:
As a huge fan of choice games, I thought they would use the same formula and was surprised when the game made me look for what was written on the “underlines”. Very clever for the first half of the game. After you already know how it works it gets a little bit tiresome and thought the game made me lose the amusement that I had when I discovered the first time. You’re supposed to “fail”, do not get frustrated by it. However, it also works as a narrative element that adds a lot when you think about what the mechanic represents.

GRAPHICS:
It works for the majority of the game. My only real issue with it was the Love: Detective story. The graphics make it hard to understand what is an intractable feature and what is not. Not a big deal but the fact that I had to click like a maniac to find interactable objects made me a little bit annoyed.
