\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: The most disgusting relationship related game that I've ever tried. And I've played stuff that romanticizes women suffering through relationships. This is worse than that.

I've seen the reviews before trying, and noticed the pattern that positive reviews were, in majority, too short and not informative or straight out meme-y, or long and with... problematic takes from the game spun out as positive things. Negative reviews on the other hand had the opposite distribution in helpfulness-to-informative ratio and with... the exact same problematic takes from the game explained on why they were negative. And frankly, I was surprised when I tried myself and noticed that the reviews didn't lie. I didn't even wanted to try this, but Steam kept recommending it to me, so here, take it Steam. Your recommendation algorithm is awesome (not).

The writing can be a resumed as a self-serving narrative on how to be a "nice guy" and a stalker (wish I was kidding), and how relationships need communication but only from one side (the other needs to know stuff beforehand). Yes, writing a relationship from start to end on such a short game is a challenge, but the writer took the challenge and ran with it... down a cliff. Being a self-centered stranger talking about yourself is preferential over introducing yourself to someone who doesn't know you. Stalking her social network compulsively to learn what movies and foods she likes is preferable over asking what she would like to see or eat (but you get told that you need to be communicative when she has the same troubles she had on the past with her boss). There ain't even meet-in-the-middle options like "you pick the movie, I pick the restaurant, next date we swap" that would be actually endearing, fun, well written, and less toxic than stalking (although it could put a pressure on the partner on a second date).

The "warm" and "soft" advertised by the game exists only on the soundtrack and graphics. The writing is pure existential horror if you understand how relationships actually work. And the worse part is that this writing fits exactly how the average person thinks relationships goes: boundaries aren't important, communication isn't two-way, one side is needs to compromise for the other, and blame can be assignable based on non-communicated information. It's disgusting.

The ONLY positive thing this game managed to do as a visual novel is to implement controls that steer the story outside the text box itself. Putting action on a visual novel, where the narrative is constructed via text/narrative, was a really smart move, even if it flew past most players. For such a cheap game this would be a redeeming quality that would make me give it a positive review despite other flaws and not refund it. But when the biggest flaw on a visual novel about relationships is toxic-behavior reinforcement and contradicting narrative, it's impossible to forgive that for one great mechanic.
