\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love what this game has to say about love!!! (ironic, isn't it?)

The name and the end credit song imply very well that, whenever you can, you must make a LoveChoice. And it is interesting that, mirroring life, the choices that are made through love are not the obvious one. This game plays with our gaming muscle memory in the sense that we are trained to interact the way that the game display prompts us very explicitly. And we've been playing games and pressing X when the HUD says so (even without reflection sometimes) for years. So it comes as a shock how hard we fail at building relationships while doing what we have been doing without effort or a second thought for years. But it would only make sense narratively that when we automatize our relationships, loving as one would push a button (without care, attention and reflection), they don't move forward. And that is precisely what the game invoked on me!

That's where this game's brilliance lies. The LoveChoice is not an obvious action, but it is attention to detail. Reading the room, understanding when to remain quiet, when to let go, when to not push the boundaries of trust. It invites us to stop engaging with the narrative as gamers and start engaging as caring people. And in that way, it's a puzzle game instead of a visual novel. Much like the way meeting new people is puzzling, and there is often a third choice not prompted in the dialogue options.

It was really sweet, and i definitely recommend and will be keeping an eye on the Devs bc i loved their work (i suppose that's the LoveChoice I'm making now then).\t\t\t\t\t
