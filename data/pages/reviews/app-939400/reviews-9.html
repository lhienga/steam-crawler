\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow I feel about this game is that if it cost any more than two bucks, I would not recommend it. For two dollars, it's a decent, if a bit shallow, distraction. I was hoping for something that would be a little moving, and found that while the stories were told well, they didn't go beyond much of a surface-level love story compacted into a very short experience.

What you get when you buy this game is actually three little mini stories, each separate from the others - and with the game description stating the game is a 1.5-2 hr experience, that means even played slowly and thoughtfully and taking the full 2 hours, each game would be a 40-minute experience. To tell a full love story, this means a lot of information has to be compacted into a TV show-length episode, so naturally you have to 'skip a lot of steps' to get where you're going.

Unfortunately, what this means is that each story is over before you're even really very emotionally invested in it. I found that I felt very little difference between 'getting dumped' in the first story, and 'staying together' in that same story. It's so brief that both endings impacted me relatively the same, except that one kinda sucked and the other was ok.

I'm not saying it's a throw-away waste of time. The stories were ok and not cheesy to the point of being stupid. But again, it's so brief, and often so surface level, that there's just really not much personal investment in them.

I will do my best in this next section to not give spoilers, or use hiders where necessary.

The first story is about the development of a normal relationship. Depending on how you interact with the girl you'll either stay together or get dumped. I played this story twice and got both endings.

The second story is about love from a distance. Two kids are great friends and eventually are separated. How will you handle the separation? I only played this game once and won't reveal the ending. I don't know if there is another ending.

The third game is kind of a bizarre 'love detective' story. A housewife finds worrying 'clues' about her husband and decides to play detective - how will the situation turn out? Now for the spoiler. This one left me with weird vibes. Half the story is searching the house for clues of cheating, the second half is finding out that the husband made a grand gesture to show her how much he loves her... but then the last bit is just a reminder that there are still unanswered questions out there about whether or not the guy is staying faithful to her or not! Like, this one wasn't a 'love story' so much as just a woman trying to figure out whether or not her man is cheating on her!

I played the first one twice, to get the good ending, but felt not desire to repeat the 2nd or 3rd.

At some point, despite the game being very simple, it was not always clear to me what I was supposed to do next, which resulted in just kinda clicking things on the screen to see if I could prompt anything to happen - a good example would be the 'roast sausage' scene in the 2nd story. I knew I was supposed to do something in that scene but couldn't figure out what. I had to look it up. For anyone else who might get snagged on this scene, it's pretty obvious you're supposed to do something with the piano in the scene, but what? Each time, the scene will cut with the image of the sad piggy bank. Well, turns out that upon entering the scene, you need to start clicking the piano and just keep clicking it to cause the little boy to skip the roast sausage place and put money in the piggy bank instead. You have to do this three times to have enough money to buy the piano for the girl.

At any rate, for a couple bucks, it's hard to go wrong, but if you're expecting a deeply impacting, moving, touching story, there are alternatives that might cost more but will do a better job. I'd recommend the stories To The Moon, Finding Paradise, Imposter Factory, and A Bird Story for some touching love stories. But if you're fine with just a brief afternoon distraction, here it is.

Finally, I always ask in my reviews, worth full price? Eh, I guess. You're not really going to ever get much of a 'discount' on two bucks that will be meaningful. You may as well pay full price, because even a 50% discount is one buck, and at the end of the day, if one dollar makes that much of a difference, you probably need to be buying food rather than video games.

I'd say I passively enjoyed it and it was cute enough, but I likely won't play it again.\t\t\t\t\t
