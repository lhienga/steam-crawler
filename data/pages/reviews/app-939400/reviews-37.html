\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNice Stories, but unfair game design.

pro:
+ Worth the full price. And yes, it does not cost much.
+ The music and graphics are sweet and touching.
+ Large language support.
+ Short game focused on the core-experience.
+ Mini-games discribe real-life situations surprisingly well.
+ The game has nice lessons within the stories, that can be eye-opening. You need to think a bit, what could be the solution to a problem - BUT...

con:
- ...the game does not tell you, that there is a problem anyway. It does not tell your options of acting. You have to lose every story, to know what is coming and to grind to success in the repeat of the game. That breaks the hole concept of a serious relationship-simulation for me and makes it feel like a soulslike - "dying foreward".
Idea: A tutorial-story would be nice, that shows you, what the game expects as kind of game-controlls, for example: drawing your character from point a to b, very fast click-picking, searching pictures, concentration tests, NOT-clicking is same important as choosing correctly, profiling/stalking your mate, unexpected change of character, etc.

- Not shown timer for actions. Often you have 5-10 seconds to act, without knowing.
- Trying to find the solutions is tiring/exhausting.
- Female rolemodel seem a bit very passive and quiet. Could be a character-trait, but is also a bit outdated. In an adult realtionship you learn to express your expectations on both sides - equally. In the game the male person is realy the main acting charakter, that needs to take care for almost everything. -> The balance the game itselfs teaches is not embodied through the game itself. The line between balance and toxic-caretaking gets thin in this game.

Conclusion:
All in all a sweet serious game, that need to be more fair in player-game communication.
...it should enact/perform its own lessons...better.\t\t\t\t\t
