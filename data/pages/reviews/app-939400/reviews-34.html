\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnce again, wishing Steam offered a neutral rating.

The premise, gameplaywise, is actually superb. Superfluously it seems like a VN where you just need to click to coast from dialogue option to dialogue option and just test each conversation choice until you get the right combo to get to the right ending.

Turns out it's a little more complex than that; there are icons on some of the screens that can be clicked to look at new stuff that gives new information, offering a little flavor text - and, more importantly, unlocking new dialogue that leads to a more optimistic route.

Still, this discovery- and getting any use out of it- was limited for me to the phone screen, where you can click the Twitter icon - and may we please just praise that these absolute madlads had the guts to actually show *Twitter*, in their for-profit game, no parody name or icon or anything!? Major points, Akaba! We need more stones like this in public media publishers - to see the leading lady's profile to see a bit of her personality and unlock a movie option that she'll actually get excited about (all the rest, she's apathetic to).

Once it got to what seems like the next scene where this is hinted to be possible - a split screen of the boy and the woman in their respective rooms - you can make some objects increase in size slightly by hovering over them, but clicking on them doesn't do anything; you just have to advance through the dialgoue the obvious way until they get to an anticlimactic "I gotta go", which leads you down the disfunctional ending.

I did accidentally get them to start making content-specific speech bubbles about their things before that, once, but I haven't been able to replicate it - and the truth is, this sleepy little awkward "love" story doesn't really make me care enough to bother troubleshooting it, not when the option to refund and get some money back hovers overhead.

___

This game is meant for people who really, *really* like awkward and disturbing romance sims, and for people who have the spare time -and interest - in a "guess-and-check" style for playing multiple-ending games. For such folk, maybe there's something here; that "Overwhelmingly positive" rating obviously comes from somewhere.

But for folks who prefer at least some clues to piece together to get to the right answer, make the right timing, and who frankly want an experience they can squeeze between sessions of working on their college essays, I definitely can't recommend this in good conscience.

___

Still, I for one am glad to see some Chinese developers actually getting a chance to spread their wings openly and to a warm welcome in a Western Market. I definitely encourage Akaba Studio to continue; I'm ready for some healing between Chinese and American cultures wherever it can happen, even if it has to be through something so niche and silly as indie games.\t\t\t\t\t
