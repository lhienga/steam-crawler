\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove Choice is a short experience sharing the stories of three couples. The first two are more linear in the storytelling, while the third functions more as a point-and-click detective mode. None of the stories are long in of themselves - the shortest being the third - and probably won't take any longer than an hour and a half, give or take. How you are affected by the actual content of the stories will boil down subjectively from person to person, although I can certainly see how people could be moved by the stories, or at least the themes they personify.

The first story is the most straight forward of the bunch, but also isn't. Without giving away mechanics or story beats, it encourages failure your first time going, resulting in a breakup in universe. Upon subsequent playthroughs is when you realize what you need to accomplish to succeed. It isn't clear at first how to solve these dilemmas at first, but once you solve one, you can pretty easily solve the others and get that perfect ending. The one exception is a dilemma with a solution requiring mechanics that aren't prompted at any time or even used at any other point in the future in either this story or the other three. Once you get past that though, the result feels heartwarming and worth the effort, great or small.

Passing the first story successfully should make it easier to pass the second one. The mechanics required for success are indicated more clearly here as opposed to the first story's approach. The story won't even continue if you "fail" during dilemmas, and will even allow you to jump to specific chapters to pick up at any point. On a personal level, the story itself on this one was more relatable and compelling for me. Perhaps it's mainly because unlike the other two stories, the couple have their own names. I also like the story's approach to the endings, comprising of two (discounting the fail ending). Again without giving anything away, a damned if you do, damned if you don't scenario that is heartbreaking in concept.

The third story is very simple. There isn't a fail state here at all and the pace of it falls onto the player. Just follow the clues and find the answers you seek. Once you get the ending normally, you can go back to the first chapter that instigated the sequence of events. From there, you do what you have to do and achieve the game's main ending and end credits. The song at the end is really nice btw.

All in all, the game is small, affordable, and pleasant in it's presentation to it's simple storytelling. There's also some good wallpapers you can get by playing the game as well if you like the artstyle. It won't take you long to finish or even to complete either. Whether you purchase on sale or whole price, give this game a try. You may like what you see.\t\t\t\t\t
