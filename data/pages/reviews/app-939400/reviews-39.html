\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove Choice has 3 mini stories. All of them have multiple endings. It's not obvious what to do at first. My first few tries I got the bad endings. However, taking the time to focus on the text and experimenting a bit with clicking around rather than just choosing from the text box options, I quickly figured out what I was doing wrong, and managed to get better endings.

Even with experimentation though, it is hard to figure out by yourself how to get to certain story-lines. If you care about getting a 100% in achievements or getting all the in-game album photos, you might have to look some stuff up.

While it's not the most complex game about love, it sure is an emotional and (bitter)sweet one. I definitely recommend giving it a go.

IRRELEVANT RANT AHEAD: Like others have said, this game made me realize my own shortcomings. It gave me a new perspective on my own relationship (and a really good anniversary gift idea for when me and my boyfriend will finally move in :). It also made me really miss him, ldr really sucks. I know story 2, the distance story, aims to portray the harsh fact that sometimes things just don't work out. However, I wish there was an optimistic ending for that story, and a bitter ending for another one, because I rarely happy ldr stories.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYeah I'd recommend this game since it's extremely cheap and pretty well-made.

There are three modes that you can play that revolve around love: First Love, Long Distance, and Detective.

First Love is your basic romance in which you ask out your crush and become her boyfriend. Depending on your choices and actions, the relation can either flop or continue on.

Long Distance is similar to First Love except you are faced with trying to maintain a long-distance relationship. Once again, your choices and actions affect the outcome of this relationship.

Finally, we have Detective which is about a wife trying to find out what her husband has been doing behind her back (it's not what you think). This mode is unique in that it's basically a mini point-and-click puzzle game.

Anyway the game plays like a visual novel except for the fact that there are moments in the game where you really need to think outside of the box. For instance, you'd need to drag the boyfriend to the girlfriend in a moment. Another one is simply not pressing the dialogue button while the couple are trash-talking each other. Hence it's extremely important to use a guide to play this game because some of the endings are locked behind stuff that you wouldn't know to do at first glance.\t\t\t\t\t
