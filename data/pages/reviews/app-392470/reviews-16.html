\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a tough one....I really like the innovative and fresh approach to the strategy game genre that NA:SoI offers....this game has a certain charm all its own. I am approaching this game from a Paradox/Matrix Games/Stardock background, so perhaps I was expecting more...its basically a "light" strategy game that has a certain RPG or Adventure appeal due to the customization, battle and other options. I have had fun with it but became bored after a time, due to the repetitive nature of the game. At first there seemed to be a plethora of things to do, but after a certain time, you realize that the strategy and empire building options are limited. It is certainly not worth the $60 price tag. I would recommend the game @ $30 or less. The AI seems very weak to me...after learning the game (which wont take too long if you have played "heavier" strategy games, albiet the UI takes some getting used too...) make sure you set to High or Max Aggression and Max AI Capability. Its a fun and unique game but I am feeling that I blew $60 for a weekend gaming experience...your mileage may vary.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I have been playing this game since release like mad. It is so fun and addicting. I have to admit I first pirated the game and played non-stop. I love it so much I decide to pay 90 dollars to get all DLC + the game on Steam. This game got the history right and is more focused on complicated AI, diplomacy, politics and strategy rather than combat. A lot of people compare this to Shogun 2 and the major difference is that Shogun 2 is more combat oriented and this game is much better in the things I mentioned above. As someone who love the period and knows the history, this is like a dream come true.
Pro:
1. fairly advanced AI tested (combat, diplomacy, coalition, province control etc) The AI is much better compare to a lot of other games and it makes the game interesting. They behave like a real person. They get afraid when you grow powerful and forms coalitions against you. Your allies will respect you and aid you with all they've got. Province AI is very aggressive and very much involved with the game
2. Politics and Diplomacy is very much functional and has drastic effects in the game. Playing without the use of these functions is very difficult.
3. Game runs smoothly with no crash or any problem whatsoever.
4. Allow players to fully customize the experience such as editing every character, create new ones, change development speed etc.
5. Rich with history

Con:
1. Not very new player friendly. The tutorial is lackluster and a lot of the things are not explained making it hard to gain new player's favors.
2. Have some errors and problems but non that will break the game.
3. UI is a bit annoying and switching between modes can be a lot of work while you try to micro manage things on the map. Certain stats disappear but it is much better to show them all so we do not have to constantly zoom in and out. A lot of things can happen on the map if you do not pay attention!

Guys, try this game out. It really is something!!!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was very hyped when I heard Nobunaga's Ambition : SOI was released on Steam. I had been very fond to NA series, even though I had only played the NA : Iron Triangle series on the PS2. After quite a decent play time, I decided to make a quick review. Note that I hadn't played other mass strategy games such as Total War, etc. , except Sid Meier's Civilization 5.

You should buy this if :
1. Are very interested in everything about the Sengoku Period.
2. Have loads of time to sink into this game.
3. You are proud of the Oda badge on your steam profile ! XD

I'm comparing NA : SOI to NA : Iron Triangle ( IT ). SOI have many improvements and new features compared to IT, but has major flaw in its battle system.

Improvements:
1. Game Concept
SOI has a much more enjoying game management by introducing the Council Phase every early month, which is much more sense in ruling an empire. This phase is where you carry out strategies ( diplomacy, survey, incite, etc )and town developments ( agriculture, crafts, etc ). After this phase is known as The Battle Phase. The Battle Phase is very similar to the previous NA series.

2. History telling
SOI narrates Sengoku Period in very detailed and interesting. Its CGI are very beautiful, the actors scripts are also really enjoyable to read.

3. Quests and Events
I was very thrilled with the brand new Quests concept. At times, there will be objectives for you to complete before deadlines such as capturing castles, gaining diplomatic points, or even attempting to employ Hanbei Takaneka which was very funny. Upon completing the Quests, rewards are given and some quests changes the world.

4. Defined Battle
You actually get to completely control your units in a Battle and Mass Battle. This simple Defined Battle can actually turn the tides even when withstanding greater foes. The environment during these battles are also very picturisque and enjoyable.

5. New Policy Concept
Policies allow you to determine your government form, either militaristic or economical, or somewhere in between. Each policy have their own merits and demerits which are makes the game very diverse. Policies utilise the " Innovation " system, which have the Progressive, Neutral and Traditional stage. Most policies can only be enacted when their requirements had been fulfilled.

Flaws:
The only major flaw in NA : SOI I can point out is its battle system.

1. Army Composition Too Simple
Your units are basically infantry and archers. They can be equipped with horses and muskets, giving them " Charge " and " Release " tactics respectively and some officers benefit these equipments ( Nobunaga, Shingen, Shigehide, etc ). This mixed army compositon in turn make battles repetitive and boring in a long run. It would be more interesting and challenging if unit type was the absolute unit type ( Only Infantry, Only Archers, Only Dagoons, etc ). So I would place Infantry in front, Archers in back, all that kinds of formation instead of head-on fight and still win.

I guess siege units were removed from this game? It's very dissapointing because siege units bring strategy diversities. Back in NA : IT, I used to enjoy blasting enemies with cannons from afar !

2. Technology System removed
In NA : IT, each clan have their own specialty in technology (Oda with muskets, Tokugawa with Infantry, Sanada with Engineering, Mori with naval, etc ) . But this was removed in SOI, making each clan somewhat similar, repetitive and greatly reduces the replay value of SOI.

3. Officers Multitasking ?
I noticed that Officers in SOI can carry out both political and military tasks. Example is that while Toshiie was busy battling the Azai, he can still build roads from Nagoya which was quite far away. Comparing to NA : IT, an officer who is engaged in battle is not available for political tasks.

Bugs:
The only bug I encountered is when changing your clan's daughter ( maiden ) name. The GUI got all messed up after changing the name. I was super lucky that I had just save that time after a long battle of Oda with Takeda. I'm using Windows 8.1 .

I guess that's it for my quick review. Overall, NA : SOI is very enjoyable to play and I'm looking forward for more NA series, and restore the Technology System , just like or even better than in NA : IT !\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is the first Nobunaga's Ambition being translated into English on PC in a decade. Not gonna say much, but I think I just put some pros and cons in this review.

Pros:
+ English version
+ With PUK (expansion pack for this game)
+ Deep and complex strategy
+ Easier to learn than previous game
+ Have the best graphic in the series
+ Very low system requirement, even notebook with onboard graphic card can play this decently
+ Feel refreshed in each entry (this game feels different enough than Tendou, and much more different than Iron Triangle)

Cons:
- Feels more shallow than previous game like Iron Triangle (PS2 only for English version)
- Deep and complex, sometimes even felt intimidating for most people to play

All in all, this is a must have game for 4x strategy fans. You will find it much different dan more refresh than other 4x strategy games you've played.
Hopefully, this is not the last time they bring this series on PC in English....

Also, if you love Pokemon Conquest for Nintendo DS, I think there is a big chance that you'll love this. Actually, Pokemon Conquest is the crossover of Pokemon series and this series.\t\t\t\t\t
