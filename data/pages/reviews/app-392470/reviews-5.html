\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWriting a review for Nobunaga's Ambition: Sphere Of Influence is going to be tough the game is truly amazing. please dont let my hour's make you think differently of my review, I played this game quite a bit offline when I had no net where I played it at times, anyway lets get down to business!

Nobunaga's Ambition: Sphere Of Influence is an historical strategy simulator game, where its based of of the warring states of feudal Japan the Sengoku era that was between 1467-1615 when the last of the Toyotomi oposing the Tokugawa shogunate was finally defeated.

the game is about you playing as one of the famous warlord's during that time, on a map of Japan, you can choose almost any character to play as, your objective is to become the next shogunate to unify Japan under one rule

Game Mechanics :

You start off as one of the lords of a clan/province and you have to maintain your economy, food, technology, while your trying to decide who you should strike first, or whether to make an alliance which can take a year or longer to make, to boost up your defences as you both work together to take out your enemies. you can also upgrade your castle's and towns/the province to boost your economy/food/army etc.

its an pretty fun and interesting strategy game, it is very tough if you do not pay attention to the tutorial well for me it was lol. anyway, you can choose to make your own custom difficulty if you wish to do so, to make the game easier or much harder then the base game diffcultys you can choose from.

Alliances/Confederations :

As I mentioned above you can make an Alliance with another faction, but if one of the clans are getting to strong and gaining to much territory you could try to make an confederation with multiple clans to form an huge alliance to fight the major power taking over Japan that threatens you, you can issue commands to your allies to tell them where they should go so you all can work together much more stratigically to take down your enemy.


Battles :

there are 2 types of battles in the game, ones that will only be on the map where you see your units just shooting arrows n such, and then there are ones where your on a battle field and you command your units and officers to route the enemy, using skills and tatics, there are also siege battles as well.

Custom Officer/Character Creation/Own Clan

the bigest thing I enjoy the most out of the game, is where you can create not one but many customized officers, to when they are born, who they are related to, how long they will live if not killed in combat, how much loyalty they have, and what skills and or how they will lvl up and what path they will choose to go with as in skills. you can then use the custom made characters and make your own clan, which is very weak and vunerable to most of the other clans in the begining, but it is very fun to see your clan rise up in the ranks and try to unify Japan yourself.

a little bonus you can marry in the game and then get children and to me that adds to the rp aspects of the game.


there is quite abit more that i have not mentioned in the game, but id like for you all to figure them out for yourself, if you choose to buy and play the game, dont want to spoil everything, but the game is really fun and I do recremend you buying the game if your a fan of the ambition series or just for history of japan.

I hope you enjoyed reading this review and I hope that it helped you decide whether you want to play the game or not

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't be fooled by the seemingly overwhelming menus and "things to do". Once you learn how to use the menus and the options, this game becomes boring FAST.

This is a numbers game.
- No officers? Can't do anything.
- Little troops? Loose the battle or can't capture town after loosing most of your men
- No gold? Out of luck, can't do anything meaningful. You'll have to wait a few months and then spend it all.

Every clan is the same, there are no unit types, no special clan abilities nor buildings. Once you beat the campaign with any clan, that's it, you have seen everything the game has to offer.

Campaign quests are very difficult to complete in the sense that to trigger, certain conditions have to be met. If for example, a quest triggers from a certain clan in a certain year, but you have already wiped them out, that's it, the quest line is broken. Hilariously, even if you know what to do exactly to meet all condtions, the AI may sometimes break the quests by simply attacking other clans.

There are no objectives (other than quests mentioned above). But you could say "it's a game, so it's all good as long as it is fun, right?". Of course, but it can never be fun when the AI cheats at everything it does, such as "recruiting" large amounts of troops without the infrastructure to support them (notice that AI always have round numbers of troop, increaing by orders of 500, such as 1000, 3500, 12000, etc). or creating alliances with very powerful clans right before you wipe them out, without going through the necessary months and coin expenditure that is required for diplomacy.

If you want to play this game particularly, just watch youtube gameplay videos or wait for a sale.

If you want to play a Sengoku Jidai game, I would recommend Total War Shogun 2 or Mount and Blande Warband with the Gegokujo mod installed, as others have suggesed before.\t\t\t\t\t
