\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a love letter to those who enjoyed the previous games in the saga. Not only it brings new elements to the table, but also improves upon several aspects that make the experience much more enjoyable. For example, the amount of historical events is way greater than in previous installments. Also, for the nostalgic ones, every officer's page in their biography includes all the portraits from other games, as well as some remixed tunes.

The game itself is a masterpiece from my point of view. Managing your territory and carefully choosing alliances, that without getting too tedious when it comes to control large portions of land thanks to the province system of the game.
If you like the Romance of the Three Kingdoms games, or Koei's strategy games in general, this is a must have, being one, if not the best Nobunaga's Ambition game ever created.

Only problem I have with this game is that it lacks a way to sort officers in alphabetical order, which can lead to some tedious officer managing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn in depth strategy game that is easy to pick up, but difficult to master.

I am in love with everything about this game, from the absolutely beautiful and epic opening
( https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Tizg90zPonE ) right to the equally beautiful and emotive ending ( https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e-JQmydc188 ). The stories taken from real life that weave and change as you play the game make you appreciate every general, every player in the world. Each with their own strife and problems that they overcome to the point that I had to turn off battle deaths just to see how the stories of certain gernerals would lead.

Having not played any Nobunaga's Ambition game before but knowing some of the history of the Sengoku Jidai I found the game doesn't weigh you down with walls of text explaining the history but rather by quick cutscenes that rather than taking you out of the action put you deeper into it.

The only gripe I have is that the battles do seem to be a matter of throwing a block of units at another and hoping you have numbers to beat them, with the odd special ability thrown in. But I ended up not really playing battles but rather staying on the world map.

When it comes to armies, the game is very indepth, with you choosing how many units to send out, but larger armies needing more food,, meaning they may not be able to go as far as a smaller force. Its that balance that makes it sometimes difficult to plan an attack.

The score for this game is beautiful as well, with each track suited for the moment, I sometimes found myself just sitting there and listening to the music rather than playing the game.

If you get the chance, play this if you are a fan of strategy games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNobunaga's Ambition is a great game! Unlike a couple of people in other reviews, this was the first time i had played a game in the series. I am constantly blown away by how complex, yet simple the design is. Even without the tutorial, the game takes you through every step and informs you of what you are about to do when the right time comes, so you are not thrown in without knowing what to do. How you go about combat is quite simple but in a good way and they don't drag on and on, making you wanting to invest time in those battles. All in all it is a very smart and fun game to play, and i would buy the next installment of the series in an instant.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was wondering when this was going to get released outside of Japan. Heh, maybe I should have paid more attention to the news. I would've bought this day one.

I am a long time fan of both the Nobunaga's Ambition, and Romance of the Three Kingdoms games. We're talking since the NES era. A friend showed it to me, and all I could think of at the time was, "what kind of names are those? How am I supposed to remember who's who?".

Over time, these games opened my eyes to a type of gaming I haven't seen before. Managing many aspects of a country/empire. Things most games at the time didn't do.

As time has passed, other developers have taken the leap and joined in the crowd in this niche. Which is great. Game series such as Europa Universalis Crusader Kings, Total War, if you like those titles, you will like this game.

Anyways, about this title specifically. Last NA game I played was Iron Triangle. That game is tough. Easy to pick up the basics with a strong clan on easy/normal difficulty, hard to master with a weaker clan on hard. This game is showing me much of the same.

They have expanded on the domestic aspect of it. You now have to focus on improving roads to both increase trade income as well as troop movement. Planning out domestic district expansion is also key. You have to figure out if it's worth it to choose the expansion upgrade, or one that exploits what you have on that tile, and passing up the expansion (ex. You can choose an option that lets you get additional farming in the next tile, or you can forsake that to focus on lowering the cost of purchasing horses). That was a very nice touch in this game.

In former games, there was essentially one city per province. Now there are multiple cities per province. Again that opens up a new aspect to the game. Do you focus on food? Troops? Income? Innovation? A lot of strategies to employ even on the domestic level.


I've barely scratched the surface so far, having played less than 4 hours currently, but I am really liking this title. If you're a fan of grand strategy like Crusader Kings 1/2, or Europa Unversalis 3/4, you should get this game. There is no multiplayer, but there is an abundance of single player. More than enough to warrant the purchase.

EDIT

I'm now about 15 hours into this game. There just seems to be a new surprise around each corner. Now I can build new fortresses in my provinces, thus increasing my harvest/income/draft pools even further. Enabling the option that allows maidens to become officers adds another dimension. Some of them are best used to seal alliances, or raise an officer's loyalty. Others, become better than officers, so why not let them loose on the battlefied?

As far as content is concered, this game is a 10/10. So much replayability, plenty of tweaking of options, and yes, make good use of the in game encyclopedia.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a fan of KOEI games and strategy, you'll like this. If you've played A Romance of Three Kingdoms, but want a Japanese/Samurai warriors version, or if you're just looking for an amazing experience with a grand strategy game, this game is for you.

9/10

Pros:
*Historical Mode is awesome.
*Awesome soundtrack.
*Awesome gameplay.

Cons:
*Nothing of note. Some may not like the "Sphere of Influence" (the region your base is in depends on how much you can directly manage before having to put it with a reagent and give general orders. Of course, you can change your base though to continue managing, so this isn't really a big deal either way).

A great game that lets you pick a faction and lead them, or even create your own (and your own character/links to families in game!), Nobunaga's Ambition: Sphere of Influence is another game in the great series of strategy games KOEI puts out. The politics, development, and management of everything is fun and addicting. I found myself upgrading towns, building new ones, setting policies and skipping out on battles just to micro-manage everything because I was enjoying it, experimenting with alliances and marriages and messing with officer's loyalty are all fun features in the game. As for combat in the game, it can be resolved automatically. General's abilities, unit's skill and abilities, and allies all come into play when auto-fighting. You can also manually do the battle, allowing some input and tactics in an otherwise hopeless situation, or simply just because it's fun. Unlike some grand strategy games, the game actually has a story you can follow too, given via historical objectives to complete. These are optionable and exist to add flavor and fun, and can be completely ignored if you'd prefer total freedom in how you rule and act.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t30+ hours now.. and I have decide to do a review for this...

I will go straight to the point! This is a hardcore Grand Strategy game that's the newest "Nobunaga's ambition".
It's got wonderful graphics, Smooth Economy controls, Engaging sound, Historial Event and Light Battle field strategy...

It may look very simple on the outside but does have alot indepth hidden strategy that you need to employ to end the Sengaku Era Japan.

The indepth tactical element is much simpler then Crusader Kings 2 but it's more complicated then all previous Romance of the Three kingdoms or Nobunaga's ambition games.

Each turn in this game is 1 month instead of of seasons or years make the game feel very slow at times but once combat is on.. it feels very fast at times...

Everything was done really well but it will feel very repetitive to any new comers of this game serie.

I have played over 30 hours now but i only unified less then 1/4 of Japan on my very first game. So this game will probably rack up the same amount of hours I have played for CK2 sooner or later.

There is a in game achievement system but it doesn't have steam achievement.

Price of this game is also very high like most japanese game that's landed in Steam store.

8.5/10 but I can only recommend to veterns of this serie and hardcore grand strategy lover... Since it's not very friendly to new comer of the serie and it's very pricey(and you won't get a 25% discount for a long time)...\t\t\t\t\t
