\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are two ways of playing this game:

1) trying to follow the historically-accurate quests

2) ignoring the quests and making your own story.


Playing without trying to trigger any quest is quite fun. The AI is decent so there will be plenty of opportunities for massive battles, difficult sieges, etc... Unfortunately, there are not whole lot of repeatable or generic events like the ones you would find in a grand strategy game from Paradox, which is my main complaint.

If you're in for the events and the history, then unfortunately playing with quests is not the best experience. They only trigger under some very specific conditions that are almost impossible to achieve without a guide. And even if you manage to trigger the quests, it leads to a boring playstyle where you'll just be waiting for years and years for something to happen. And then something goes wrong and the quest doesn't trigger anyway which is quite frustrating! So my advice is not to follow the quests at all, except the first ones. If you want to see the events try starting in different scenarios instead of wating for quests to trigger.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game looks exquisite, but the gameplay is laborious due to some artificial limitations. For example, true to its name, you, the player and daimyo in-game, have a sphere of influence: you can not command your clan armies outside this sphere of influence, but you can work around that by instantly relocating your main base to another location. Like, WTF?

You can also set up provinces and order the assigned commanders to invade, but bear in mind NPCs can not set out to attack, except to defend from, enemies not sharing borders with them, even if there exists a safe path en route. Due to this questionable logic, coalitions are often just for show, and, combined with the inconvenience of the sphere of influence, the war against the Hojo clan will inevitably drag on.

I have also encountered two prominent bugs on Windows 10. The first being that graphics settings are not persisted between restarts. The second bug occurred on my second story play, during which the in-game audio would sometimes die on me and couldn't be fixed without a game restart. These are well-known issues, not one-offs.

It's also a pity that the game doesn't support Japanese or Chinese subtitles, so it is hard for me to remember names. I had actually wanted to buy Nobunaga's Ambition: Iron Triangle, but due to misunderstandings bought this one instead. Oh well, I guess after this experience I will not buy another Nobunaga's Ambition again, as they all look similar and will get repetitive and boring fast.\t\t\t\t\t
