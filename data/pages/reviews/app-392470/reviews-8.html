\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSadly, japanese games like this type are always overpriced for what little they have to offer. So wait for the discounts, don't rush. It's not something special, infact its rather ordinarly.

Is this game fun though? Yes of course, it has elements of fun that you won't get from total war shogun 2 like personalized generals etc. What's is bad is the combat system. For people who complained that Romance of Three Kingdom 13 is a number game. Well, they have to realized that Nobunga is the reason why RTK 13 feel that way. In Nobunga, it's alll about out numbering first, tactics and strategy later. So for people who likes to play their own created fictional generals, this game is going to be a great challenge.

In combat, flacking and pincer attacks has little advantages, almost none. Even if your generals out class your oponents and you used the right tactics, prepare to lose alot of men. Well its not eactly a 1 to 1 exchange of casualties, its pretty close.

I am disappointed with this game probably because I had already played RTK 13, which is the polished version of KOEI strategy game. So when I play Nobu sphere, it feels lacking and less immersive in comparison. Regardless, its still enjoyable and challenging in a strategic level, but be prepared to be disappointed at the tactical level.

What good about this game? Well I would guess, resourse management and strategic alliance is its' only saving grace. Sadly, having more generals or retainers doesn't give you much advantage early in the game like ROTK 13 because there isn't much you can do anyway.

Is this game worth buying? Not really, I would suggest you try RTK 13 first, if you don't like RTK 13, you would find this game less appealing. Don't be fooled by the combat grahpics though, its misleading. You would see boxs more often than a total war like combat grahpic.

Will I recommand this game to anyone? Well its not for everyone I would say.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one that I keep coming back to.

A grand strategy game with a few rpg elements in it, I knew I had to have it. Though the price is steep, not once did I regret my purchase. I've gotten some two hundred hours out of it, and I still keep coming back. Whether you want to reenact history or alter it, you can. Or why not (as I do) create your own characters and have their clan (unknown to historians) fight for domination of feudal Japan.

Pros
-Fantastic character artwork.
-The game feels polished.
-Historical events make history come alive.
-Lots of scenario options, offering replayability.
-Nice music and sound effects.
-Manage your clan with politics, development, and war.

Cons
-Late game might get a bit tedious.
-Setting the AI to higher agression levels tends to screw up history.
-Those used to older Nobunaga's Ambition games might find this one less complicated and deep, though depending on your opinion, that is of course not necessarily a bad thing.\t\t\t\t\t
