Pros:
  • Historical Setting I have always been a big fan of history, and I especially like Sengoku Era Japan. The background is emense and the creators have done their research into every clan.
  • Girls ayy, you get the hot grils as your generals if you really want that. (You can also turn it off for a more historical/realistic route.)
  • Create Your Own Officers Pretty obvious, but it adds a bit more RPG element into your strategy game.
  • Create Your Own Clan A nice touch to the game allowing you to make your own clan at the start of the game. You just have to survive the on slaught of larger daimyo at the start.
Cons:
  • Strategys in Battles I felt the battles were rather boring, almost to straight forward. Any strategy in the battle was usually defeated by just pure number, who ever had more seemed to win.
  • Defeating Generals In a player fought battle, any time the main general of an army retreats, that entire army is destroyed, it doesn't matter if that army is larger than the other, it is completely lost.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell it has been quite some time since the Nobunaga series release another English version one into the market. But this this we get a well, pretty good release.

Essentially, this is the power up edition that the Japanese and Chinese market has got. Which is a plus for new gamers in the KOEI strategy esque like game(meaning that you can use the inbuilt editor to 'train' yourself.e.g. changing the amount of stats for your generals etc, ).

The latest series introduced the option to choose an alternate war mode (when your troops are close to the opponent ones), with gameplay style similar to Total War series gameplay (in terms of controlling troops etc).

Of course, choosing Nobunga as the main player unveils his life to the player with multitude of historical events (beautiful CGs) BUT at the same time letting you choose whether you would like to execute the choices that historically happened (e.g. Killing Azai Nagamasu?? Up to you , let him live and he becomes your general in the end, cant bear to kill that ATK 89 general for me : ) ). Choosing other Damiyos for this series introduces more story-life historical events with the related damiyos too(Shimazu, Takeda to name a few does have alot of events for them to play with too).

Definitely one of the better Koei strategy games I have been playing so far (having played RTK from genesis to PC platform and Nobunaga series from series 10 till now).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would like to start by saying I am a big fan of Koei. I have played many of the RotTK, Dynasty Warriors, Nobunaga's Ambition, and Samurai Warriors games. I wanted to like this game when I bought it.

I played through the tutorial where you are guided into selecting what to do on each turn. The city commands it was instructing me to do felt trivial: Press button->More rice | Press button->More soldiers. It felt very uninteresting and did not make me feel like I was preparing for the upcoming battle the tutorial was eluding to. Seeing all of the grayed out buttons I could not select did have me optimistic, though.

After too many turns of making more rice, I finally got to enter the battle. The battles in this game are like a real-time version of Dynasty Tactics, if you are familiar with this old title (also made by Koei). Troop-blob attacks troop-blob and there are officer commands that are usable that cause status effects for your troops or the enemy's. Again, what I was doing felt very trivial. As the battle went on I just sat there wondering, "Am I winning?"

After the tutorial, I started a new game. I chose a random daimyo to play as and got into it. The next hour was me pressing buttons to make more rice and make more troops. I increased my trust with a neighboring clan (This feature, btw, makes no sense - You simply gain trust then spend it as a currency, effectively making the AI do what you want). After the "build up", I attacked a nearby clan with the help of a tribe and my neighbor's forces I called in. We walked up to the city, stood around for minute, and won. Such fun. I am not doing this for hours/days/weeks. Escape->Quit Game

I understand that there may be more to the game than what I experienced. I looked around in all of the menus trying to find something interesting, or something I was missing, or not understanding. My imagination cannot even trick me into thinking there is more depth to this game than what I saw. It felt like I was playing a SNES game with simplistic mechanics but today's graphics. In all honesty, the SNES version of Nobunaga's Ambition is more fun than this. I guess my expectations were entirely too high for what I got.

Pros
Graphics
Music
User interface is clean
Officer edit/creation
Story events

Cons
Sense of strategy/tactics is lacking
Trivial city upgrade system
Battles are too simplistic and not fun enough to manually control
Diplomacy system is incredibly flawed
Lack of meaningful information menus
Game lacks any new innovative systems/mechanics to make it stand out

If you want a game set in this time period of Japan, Shogun 2 is still the go-to title.
If you want a complex strategy game, play Crusader Kings 2.
If you want a simple strategy game, play Civ 5
If you want a city building game, play Cities: Skylines
If you want a rice farming game, play Farming Simulator 2015.

If you still feel like you might want to buy this game, I implore you to watch many videos of the game actually being played. I bought the title based on being a Koei fan and the reviews that compared it to EU4 and how it was better than Civ 5. I feel lied to.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a pretty awesome KOEI strategy game. It's actually very difficult, but feels rewarding when you do conquer your rivals. Like other KOEI strategy games, there is a heavy focus on personnel and on making good use of your officers. However, this game is a bit different from other "ruler only" KOEI strategy games because you can also play as a vassal clan. The game gives you a wide array of options for acquiring new officers, developing your lands and managing diplomacy. I can praise KOEI on a lot of other things as well. For once, they actually decided to release an English dub for one of their strategy games, and it's actually awesome. Additionally, this game is a translation of both the original game Nobunaga's Ambition Souzou and its PUK (expansion pack). It's the first time that KOEI has localized an expansion pack for one of their strategy games. The localization is also overall pretty good. There are a few spelling errors here and there, but nothing serious. As a PC game, this title is also better than all of KOEI's other English deliveries. The load times are practically non-existent, the game runs beautifully and it has not crashed on me once even though I tend to experience crashes with a lot of other video games. Finally, the options for customizing the game are amazing. You can edit all sorts of things and create your own unique characters/clans.

It's worth mentioning that the scope of this game is enormous. It seems like there are four or five times the number of bases that existed in Iron Triangle. Moreover, due to the way troop mechanics works, it's actually not so easy to conquer the land quickly. The large array of scenarios in tandem with the length it takes to progress through each one means that players will have hundreds of hours of potential playtime on their hands.

The best thing about this game though (at least for me) is the fact that Koei included a huge array of historical events, some of which even have cutscenes. These historical events sometimes manifest themselves as quests that allow you to relive some of the historical drama of this period. Oftentimes you will have to make a tough decision about whether you want to follow a historical questline or go off and do your own thing. I would definitely recommend getting the Nagao Kagetora (Uesugi Kenshin) DLC if you buy this game. I've been having a blast with it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt recaptures the feel of the older games, and is one of those titles you can lose hours in without realizing you did it all while looking fantastic.

Minor complaint, there is no controller UI - which althoughnever promised on the steam page, the promotional media was taken from the PS4 version that shows a controller UI (bit misleading if your like me and didn't pay attention). Fortunately, I'd of purchased this regardless, and honestly it's probably better with a mouse. It also has an odd bug/glitch for me where the display settings reset everytime I launch it, but it's an annoyance as it's easy enough to reset once the game launches.

Minor complaints aside, it's a must buy for classic Koei fans, and probably a safe buy for strategy fans in general.
