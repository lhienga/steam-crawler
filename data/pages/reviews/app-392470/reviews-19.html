\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOBUNAGA'S AMBITION: Sphere of Influence is kind of like the Total War games but with a lot more empire management and less focus on controlling combat. The build up with empire management is very slow, so battes will not happen until you have taken the time to develop your empire. In battles, your generals have "special abilities" that can be used to give an edge in battle; these tend to make the battles more reliant on proper use of abilities moreso than anything else. You can also ignore the battles (let the AI run them) and just focus on the empire management and moving your armies, and given the depth on the strategic side, this is a viable and enjoyable playstyle.

I've played KOEI games in the past, so this was kind of a nostalgia purchase for me. It pushes all the right buttons for me just like the original Romance of the Three Kingdoms did 27 years ago.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI often do not write reviews but this game is amazing, if not one of the best games I have bought on steam in terms of Grand Strategy.

First if you love Japanese History or wish to learn more about it then this is a great game for such (In terms of events and facts the game provides). Second if you want to have a long save that is enjoyable throughout then I recommend this (I'd suggest starting as a clan such as the Hojo, Amago or Ouchi for starters as they are the strongest).

I've done two playthroughs. One as the Satomi (a small clan which often is crushed when used by the AI) and the Takeda (a hard starting clan because there are two stronger clans South of your land).

With the Satomi game it came to a standstill because I had all of the east and required a mere ten more castles plus Kyoto to become Shogun. Where Nobunaga Oda held most of the West par Shimazu land. With only us three factions and none able to break the other it didnt seem anyone was getting anywhere.

My new save I took over Japan fully. Even though I became Shogun and could enact a ''No More War'' Policy which would win the game for me anyway. I thought to myself ''Largest army... Best Generals.... Still got my orginal ruler...'' and continued to conquer until Japan was united.

The main thing is in this game is to make sure Oda Clan does not expand. If they do and Nobunaga takes over then you will struggle and end up like my Satomi as he becames all too powerful.

Good things:
Ending is amazing
Game overall is easy to grasp after 5 hours
Tons of clans to play, so if you finish one you can pick another
Good for learning
Nobunaga is a monster to face in battle

Bad things:
Religion has next to no affact in this game which is a huge dissapointment for me
No Ikko Ikki (as far as I have noticed)
Your not able to check your rulers age
Would be nice for a family tree. So I can see who is in my family (Not all sons have the last name as ruler making it slightly harder)

Overall I rate this game 98/100. Only issues is no family tree which would of been somewhat good and useful and lack of religion affacting clans.
To those who buy this game have fun ! I know I will continue to do so.\t\t\t\t\t

The UI in the game is simple, yet the asthetics is beautiful. I went from thinking I'll try to game out for one hour to 6 hours later trying to battle another rivial clan for some territory. Pros-
-The right balance between simplicity and complexity for the user interface
-Challenging and fun AI to fight, found myself battling another clan back and forth for like 2hrs over a few towns.
-Dynamic ways to fite you battles, (Politics matter for once in a game)

Cons-
-lack of spy game system
- not as many stategies as the ROTK seriers

Overall Great game if you are a fan of the ROTK series, if not buy it on sale or when the price drops.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuick review followed by a story

Have you ever played Total War: Shogun 2 and thought "wow this game would be great if the A.I. wasn't horrible" or "Would be good if this game was challenging without just being A.I. army/agent/Retarted diplomacy SPAM"?

Well Nobunaga's Ambition has you covered, focusing mainly on the Map-based this time with polished gameplay (I hate using this word) and A.I. that makes sense. It also gives much more information about the time, giving you visual novel style information about different clans, people and historical events which is an unexpected joy, making the country feel more alive.

There is still fights between clans, these are not as good as aformentioned Shogun 2, but you cannot just abuse the AI and win when it is 10 to 1. In this game it is much more simplified, but difficult (watch let's play vids for a good look before buying), every loss will affect you, every win will make the game hard later on as other clans start fearing/admiring you.

TLDR
Want an great strategy game that focuses on the Sengoku Jidai where there is plenty of detail and a game to waste plenty of hours, try out this.

Story time
After downloading the game, I started out the game playing the 'tutorial' scenario where I lost the first battle and was booted back to the Main Menu. Annoyed at this, I started the second scenario, focusing on the events of Nobunaga's father, Oda Nobuhide, Stating off in my small province of Owari, I started playing with the features, slowly building my towns and my armies. After 1 hour of messing around, I started to attack a single clan, winnning a province and stroking my ego. Choosing to follow historical missions of the time for the daimyo, I eventually came to fight the Imagawa and the Saito. After securing allies, I attacked with my petty force of around 3000 with my allies force of over 10,000, I defeated the Saito, caputing the daimyo and coverting him to my side (a real prize).

After defeating him, I made my way south, attacking the next enemy, over the next 5 hours, I took that clan down to one base (not with frustration or Save Scumming, but with fun). Once they were no longer a threat, I started to regroup and attack the nearby TAKEDA clan because every time I play a game about this era, I always attack them. Well turns out that was a stupid idea, continually being defeated by their strategy and my poor logistic handling. When I finally got a truce, I had only taken 1 province and it was continually being attack from a northern enemy clan, trying to take it without success.

Leaving 10,000 troops there, I pulled back and began focusing on the last Imagawa base I had ignored, putting together another 10,000 to try and take it. There was a catch however, on the other side of the Imagawa were the powerful Hojo clan besieging them, so I smashed their army in the side, taking the castle without loss. HORRIBLE CHOICE IN FORESIGHT. For the next hour or so was mainly diplomacy and building, making the Takeda neutral and I could ask them to lead a hand if needed. Then THIS HAPPENED, Oda Nobunaga appeared as an officer and straight away informed me that 40,000 Hojo troops are comming my way. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, 40k? I have 10k left at home and 10k at the north, how can I do this? The enemies came thundering across their land, besiging my castle that I had taken, smashing my armies and forcing me to ally with an enemy clan of the hojo and requesting the Takeda (who were close) to give me a hand.

THIS is where the game started to shine for me. Losing battles against them, I realised something, I still had 10k forces north to use and they were close to the hojo, but on the other side of the clan, So I had an awesome idea. I got those 10k soldiers to storm into their land, besiging 3 castles at once. The enemy AI came to realise what was happening and pulled some of the forces back to defend when suddenly the Takeda forces showed up at two sides, blocking the force of about 35k.

With MASSIVE AMOUNTS OF TROOPS IN ONE SPOT, the game stopped and told me of the 'MASS BATTLE' mode. "What is this?" I replied before clicking this. Entering a battle with 40k soldiers, equally matched I had a battle with the enemy. Eventually winning, the Hojo were in retreat, the 'spine was broken'. Seeing this other neutral clans started attacking the hojo, becomming a land grab.
That is where I am up to after playing 20 hours. Still have the of Japan to go. ^_^\t\t\t\t\t

Anyone who's a fan of the series would have happily invested any asking price for this game. Don't waste your money on this crap, or at least wait till the price drops to under $20, you'll thank me for warning you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBig RoTK fan, 3-12 still play them to this day.. Big Nobunaga Fan, Fav SNES game. So in the case of this game I could justify paying full price on release, but you can tell Koei took more inspiration from their arcade like Samuri Warriors series than the RoTK that is touted. I can just say this game may be for casual strategy gamers.

I can see some people may love it, but instead of expanding gameplay mechanics, you can tell they simplified/streamlined them. I really can't even find many uses for my officers, it generally takes one (your highest skilled) to do a task, he recovers next turn..

The officer stat-gains are non-interactive, they train themselves which is cool but.. you feel like you have no say and the interaction is basically reduced to reading repeating messages in your monthy log about training.

This game does not compete with games like CK2, Europa Universallies or even the Total War series\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a ROTK fan who played 3, 4, 11 and fan sub 12 on pc, 8,9,10 on ps2. I am also fan of Romance of the three kingdom's stories, manga and movies. This is the 1st time I play a nobunaga series and I hope it will bring the same enjoyment and interests in the sengoku period that ROTK 3 brought to me the interest/addiction of the Romance of the three kingdom.

Best game tutorial ever! step by step introduction which helps to understand the basics of the game and cold jokes. 1st game that i lost the tutorial battle because i set the game speed too high. 10/10 will restart the tutorial. nice to have fast forward button for the conversations because I can't stand the jokes.

Found out why i my fps was hovering around 40+ fps and occasional drop because i ran the game with my integrated intel gpu. Change to my gtx for smoother 60fps. Nevertheless, integrated gpu works fine means this game does not need a gaming pc/laptop to play.

settings will revert back to windows mode and 1280 x 720 when i restart the game.(known issue that affect most)

After playing for a while on the scenario where nobunaga became a daimo, i have finally face my 1st true test where my alliance is being ganged by 2 of the largest clans. I realised the following:

- This is a very complex strategic game where strong alliance, right buildings built, efficient resources management and and even luck (i had famine event during harvest month on both my cities when i planned for an offensive which i had to delay for a year) play a part in expanding and survival of your clan.

- Build more fortresses to increase income and troop count.

- Set policies to boost your clan

- increase diplomacy with as many clans as possible (+20 helps with gold income +40 allows you to summon help)

- I am facing the current adversity of 2 huge clan ganging up on me from the east because i was focusing on the west and naively thought they will not ally to attack my alliance.

-building relationship with the shogun and bribing the official for higher ranking post

-Battles can be settle automatically in the strategic map by AI or in a tactical format controlled by player


In my opinion, the battle system is very flawed. After so many battle in tactical form, i have yet to get a hang of where tactical movement and placement actually works.

For example surrounding a single enemy unit with 3 allies will see the same amount of troops losses on both side. having horses and musket does not seem to improve damage other than having the charge and release tactics. Using them placed the unit uncontrollable even after enemy in front had lost and retreated.
Another example is in an arrow battle, my unit with superior general and 3 times more troops takes more damage than a weaker opponent facing each other both with musket.
That means battles will most likely be the force that has higher troop count will win although not always true. (I have lost or win with more or less troops before but do not expect any significant victory using a lesser force)


The only times that i saw any difference are the tactics used by various units. However the tactic bar(command bar) increases so slowly which limit tactic usage to change the battle. Both side starts with the same amount even when the troops count are different or number of general/units are different. This means a smaller force can cast the same amount of tactics as a much superior force.
This is unlike ROTK 12 which has faster regeneration of tactic bar and troops movement placement are important. In this game it feels non-existance.

Tactical battle map are small and maneuvability is havoc when you want to aim an enemy unit but is near to another. Mass tactical battle map feel the same small size as normal battle and there is hardly any tactical movement to be made like flanking and sending decoy which could be made in ROTK 12.

For those who played koei ROTK games, this game battle system is more like ROTK 12 than to ROTK11 due to it being real-time. And i prefer the battle system of ROTK 12 than this game.

For those who played medieval 2 total war, the battle system in this game is nothing like total war. This is due to the fact that there is only one type of unit and medieval 2 has many different types. Therefore do not expect it to be the same or even better.

However I will still recommend this game for its strategic part of the game, story, orchestra music, pleasant graphic, difficulty of the game and the complexity of game. There are many more reasons listed out by others and given my hundreds and hundreds of hours playing ROTK games, this is a potential hundred+ hours game for those who have the PATIENCE to conquer japan. If not I would recommend skipping this game as learning the basics and the tutorial will be more than 2hr and you can not accomplise and gain any satisfaction in a short term.\t\t\t\t\t
