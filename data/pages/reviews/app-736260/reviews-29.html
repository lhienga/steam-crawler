\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA challenging puzzle game in which you create and break rules by pushing around the words making them up.


The basic gameplay of Baba Is You revolves around you moving around in grid-based 2D levels and pushing around different objects. However, each level also contains words which can be similarly pushed around. When arranged in the correct way, these words will form rules, i.e., causing specified objects to move, block other objects from passing through them, act as the player character or interact with certain other objects in different ways.

Using this system, levels are set up by placing their rules within them and require you to break and/or change some of them in order to get to a winning state. What exactly constitutes this winning state can vary depending on the rules, though you most often have to touch a specific object with your own character. The gameplay is somewhat hard to define here, as the rule-system coupled with a pretty big number of words that can be used in them means the things you have to do in each level vary widely.

Baba Is You has an overworld, divided into different sub-worlds. Each of these has a unique graphical theme and introduces a couple new words, centring its levels around their mechanics. Completing levels will unlock adjacent ones and allow you to progress further in the overworld once a set number of levels have been cleared.

While the early levels are quite easy to figure out and are mostly concerned with showcasing the possibilities the rule system provides, the games difficulty quickly picks up. Later levels take a while to even figure out, require a lot of out of the box thinking. The last world(s) step this up even further, taking the concept of what rules can do about as far as possible, which certainly left an impression on me.

In addition to the large main world, the game also includes additional level packs, introducing new mechanics and showcasing in-development levels within them. I found these to be a bit less cohesive and interesting than the main game, but they certainly provided a fresh experience with more of the established gameplay. Theres also an included level editor, coming with the ability to play other players custom levels created within it. Given this, theres more than enough content in the game to keep you busy.


Baba Is You is a unique concept executed wonderfully and has a huge amount of varied content. However, be aware that it also quickly becomes quite challenging, meaning most of the game will require great puzzle solving skills.\t\t\t\t\t
