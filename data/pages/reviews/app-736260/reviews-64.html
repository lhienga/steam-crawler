\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA charming, minimalist puzzle game that has a straightforward premise, yet has amazing (and very difficult) levels.

You are given all the rules in the level, and your goal is to alter them to make the level doable. There is nothing hidden from sight - all the information you need to solve the level is directly presented to you. It's very much a case of show and not tell: the game teaches you how different rules interact with each other by using them in puzzles. There are often several ways to do a level, none of them better or worse than others.

The level design is great, starting with relatively straightforward puzzles introducing basic interactions, and slowly ramping up towards the end, having some absolutely fiendish puzzles towards the end. Despite the insane difficulty towards the end, it rarely felt unfairly difficult. The answers were always there to be figured out, and with enough creativity and trying things out, they were always doable. It was rare for the solution to be a stretch.

The amount of content is staggering too, especially for the price. Even in the base game, there are enough puzzles to last 50+ hours, and that's before considering workshop level packs, which includes an official levelpack introducing many more blocks and almost as many levels as the base game.

Overall, one of the best puzzle games ever made. Would highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
