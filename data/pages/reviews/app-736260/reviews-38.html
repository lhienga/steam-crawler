\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've taken a look at other reviews, you'll see this game is almost universally loved. And don't get me wrong, there are plenty of things to love about it. It takes a unique approach to puzzle games and with over 200 levels, even experts will find a challenge. Combine that with the fact that the rules are easy to learn, and you are encouraged to break them as soon as you learn them, and Baba Is You has all the markings of a fun, novel puzzle game. So why the thumbs-down? Because the novelty quickly wears off.

A lot of people, whether they love it or hate it, will talk about how the game is deceptively hard. Take a quick glance around the community hub and you'll see testaments of players spending days (literal days) on a single level. Whether or not this appeals to you is entirely subjective, but it is worth noting.

My main issue is that Baba Is You is presented as a game where you can break all the rules, but it soon becomes apparent that you are breaking only the rules that the developer wants you to break. This doesn't lead to creative, unique solutions to the puzzles. It leads to one single solution for any given puzzle, a sequence so complex and restrictive that if you do not follow each step to the letter you will not beat the level.

What this means is that you don't have a game that capitalizes on some wild new concept, you have a game that plays like every other puzzle game on the market.\t\t\t\t\t
