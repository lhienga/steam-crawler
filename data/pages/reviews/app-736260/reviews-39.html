Prepare to have your mind blown by "Baba is You," a puzzle game that redefines creativity and problem-solving. As a dedicated gamer with a passion for brain-teasing challenges, I can confidently say that this game is an ingenious masterpiece that will keep you enthralled and delighted at every turn.

"Baba is You" is a unique and innovative puzzler that allows you to manipulate the rules of the game itself. You control the adorable character Baba as you push, combine, and rearrange words in the level to alter the game's mechanics and win conditions. This mind-bending twist opens up a world of endless possibilities, where you become the architect of your own solutions.

The puzzles in "Baba is You" are brilliantly crafted, requiring you to think outside the box and experiment with different word combinations to overcome obstacles. Each level is like a puzzle playground, where you are encouraged to break the rules and challenge conventional thinking.

The game's difficulty curve is masterful, gently introducing new mechanics while gradually ramping up the complexity. The sense of satisfaction when you crack a particularly challenging puzzle is immensely gratifying, motivating you to take on even more mind-bending enigmas.

"Baba is You" boasts a minimalist and charming art style that keeps the focus on the puzzles and mechanics. The simplicity of the visuals adds to the game's elegance, allowing the gameplay and creativity to shine through.

What sets "Baba is You" apart is the boundless creativity it encourages. The freedom to experiment and create your own solutions fosters a sense of ownership over each level's outcome. There's no single right answer; instead, the game celebrates the multitude of ways players can approach and conquer the challenges.

The game also provides a sense of camaraderie in the form of shared solutions and community collaboration. Engaging with fellow players and discovering unique solutions to puzzles adds a social element that enriches the overall experience.

In conclusion, "Baba is You" is a brilliant and mind-expanding puzzle wonderland. Its innovative mechanics, creative challenges, and delightful simplicity make it a shining star in the puzzle gaming genre.

If you seek a game that celebrates your ingenuity, inspires creative problem-solving, and rewards experimentation, "Baba is You" is an absolute must-have in your gaming library. Prepare to embrace the role of the puzzle conductor and embark on a captivating journey where language becomes your most potent tool in this exceptional and endlessly entertaining experience.\t\t\t\t\t

BABA IS YOU / REVIEW IS WRITE

As much as I hate some aspects of the game, I just can't bring myself to give it a negative review, as the things it does well are exceptional. I'm writing this review after beating every level available currently( 231 in total). I'll also say that I had to look up the solution to a few levels, because some levels didn't make sense to me even after knowing the solution, which I'll elaborate on later in the review.

WHAT IS BABA

Baba is You is a Sokoban* -like game where your goal is to rearrange words to satisfy or create a winning condition by establishing rules. 
The most common are : 
BABA IS YOU and FLAG IS WIN, meaning if Baba(you) occupies the same tile as Flag(win), the level is considered completed, but you could swap the words and make : 
FLAG IS YOU and BABA IS WIN meaning that your goal now is to reach Baba as Flag to win.
Throughout the game, you will encounter more words and more possible combinations to solve levels.

GAME IS PLAY

As already stated, the game is divided into singular levels and most of them can be solved in under a minute if the player knows the solution. The levels are accessed on a world map, represented by an island. You can move around the world map and travel to different zones, which serve as hubs*². The game does not require you to finish every level to advance to the next area. Upon entering a hub, you can always see the number of levels required to unlock a "key item". New zones are unlocked by just having a certain amount of "key items", so the player doesn't need to decide which area they want to unlock next.

THING IS GOOD

  • One of my personal favorite things in the game is it's art style, as everything in the game is easy to identify and understand, with the added bonus of being able to run on almost any computer.
  • Levels where new mechanics are introduced are easy and let you play around with the rules to give you a basic understanding of them.
  • The "undo" button helps a lot, since it's easy to make a mistake in the game and not having to restart the entire level each time is great.
  • Most levels are designed pretty well and sometimes give hints on how to solve them, without it being shoved in your face and looking unnatural.

THING IS BAD

  • The difficulty of the game is insane in some cases; it ramps up from some levels so quickly, that you don't even have time to learn how to use a mechanic properly and the game already expects you to know every possible interaction.*³
  • A few words have the issue of being hard to use or comprehend, as the introductory level only shows you a few possible interactions, which makes the easy introductory levels a double-edged sword.
  • Some levels verge on trial and error, as some mechanics don't seem possible when you first encounter them.
    There are some interactions in the game that look like the dev was just messing around in a sandbox trying to find the most obscure interactions possible and made them into a level without giving you the opportunity to see them in action.

  • Now, my personal biggest problem with the game is the cumulation of the above to some extent:
    You would think a game about breaking rules and remaking them would have a lot of freedom for making solutions, but it's the exact opposite; after a certain point in the game, you are restrained from doing the majority of interactions. 
    If you somehow manage to find an unintended solution, chances are that the dev will just update the level to make that solution not possible anymore, meaning the game turns from "think outside the box" to "think inside the developer's box; otherwise, you're just exploiting the game's mechanics".
    In my personal opinion, you should have the decency as a puzzle developer to admit when you have been outsmarted, but I guess changing the level to make your solution the only one is sort of meta in a twisted way.

THOUGHTS IS FINAL

As you see, I've written a lot more about the negative aspects of the game, but I still do recommend it, as it is a very unique game and despite all the issues with it, I still had a lot of fun with it, and the feeling of being a genius after solving a level is more than worth the frustration in some parts.

REVIEW IS END

*  - Sokoban (eng. "warehouse keeper") from 1982 is also known as a "Box pushing" puzzle game where you have to push a box on a specific tile on the map. Many games since it's original release have implemented it as a mini-game; a lot of these games are of Japanese origin.

*²  - Hub refers to a zone that houses a set of levels with the same visual aesthetic and theme; these levels quite often introduce a new word or mechanic that is expanded upon throughout the entire zone and the rest of the game in some cases.
