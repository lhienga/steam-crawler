\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was intrigued at the first few levels of this game, since it has such an original and thought-provoking premise. Soon though I grew frustrated and unhappy with my time working on the puzzles. It took me awhile to figure out why, but I think it's kind of like this. Imagine you're playing chess, but in the middle of the game, if you say put the queen next to a bishop, then the rules for rooks change so they move like a knight. I mean one step like that is sort of fascinating, but if you make a puzzle that requires multiple such rule-shifts, then it begins to feel like an impossible job to look down the tree of possible solutions as it all becomes opaque quickly with each context change. So unfortunately I can't recommend it, since it's core cool idea is also the one that makes it exceedingly arbitrary to play, almost like you just have to keep moving tokens around until you stumble upon the right setup that the developer designed for that particular puzzle.\t\t\t\t\t
