\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI’ll avoid the tantalizing low-hanging fruit of writing my thoughts for this game in its native syntax. Baba is You is the latest gift from the Finnish game development Gods housed in the frame of one man: Arvi Teikari. Just like previous eccentric auteurs before him - Lucas Pope and Victor Frankenstein, Teikari’s vision is uncompromising, innovative and brutal. Brutal not in the sense of a monster you’ve created murdering your fiancé but that the obstacles in the game and their levels of abstraction are tough. It goes without saying that despite Baba is You’s cute aesthetics, the puzzles become immensely complex as you progress.

In order to lessen the mental burden upon the player, so one can avoid the fatal conclusion of “I ain’t playing that”, Teikari designed the entire game around working backwards from the solution. This easier-said-than-done concept is the the game itself, from the numerous ways one can tackle the very first puzzle to the meta-insanity of the final worlds. Perhaps the game should have been titled "Baba is Creative", though that does not pique interest nor is it fun to say out loud, but it is the principal theme of the game: there's as much freedom as one would expect from immersive sims. I could go on and on about how the design should be the poster child of the overused term “thinking outside the box”, but that’s not what matters. What matters is Baba is You is a unique experience unlike anything else in gaming, and it’s a great one at that.\t\t\t\t\t
