\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBaba Is You feels like trying to write poetry in a foreign language. Box has love. Love is defeat.

It's perfect in its form, no more nor less than it needs to be. The mechanisms are stripped bare for all to see. They're simple, easy to understand. And yet there is so much depth to be drawn from just bumping words around, altering the rules, opening new possibilities, and, more often than not, completely ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ yourself over.

The puzzles are exquisitely crafted. Most will, at some point, seem impossible. As if they were created for a laugh. A joke aimed squarely at you. But keep trying, tinkering, and you'll get there eventually, the solution so simple once it's finally revealed itself.

A masochistic streak will help, sure, but if puzzles is love, buy is must. Baba Is You is an indie masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t
