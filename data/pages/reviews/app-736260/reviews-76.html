\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA 2D puzzle game seen from above where you push things around to form a path for you to progress.
Now, add the fact that the rules that define the game are themselves displayed in several blocks on the screen, blocks that you can push around to form new rules.

This is Baba is you.

In this game, this concept is used in incredibly effective and various ways. The level design is well made. First it start with simple rules and concepts easy to grasp. Baba is you, rock is push, wall is stop, flag is win. You are baba, you can pushes rocks, walls block you, the flag is your objective. Soon you are pushed to change rules and do things that can appear silly or bizare, like being a wall, walking on lava, pushing water, these little things you do not necessarily think to do.
I laughed when once, I spent 20 minutes on a level, when I found that in fact I COULD go through walls, because there wasn't any instruction 'wall is stop'. And yet I subconsciously thought that walls would block me
Then little by little, new mecanics are introduced in graspable ways, and the enigm complicates. Where the first levels will not require more than three minutes to win each, you will quickly find yourself breaking your head for half an hour (or many more) over a solution to win a single level in the more advanced parts of the game.
There's hundreds of levels, what's more you can create your own or the play the ones other makes (and god! People are amazing!)

This game is great if you have the patience to put effort into it. (and I understand that people will not necessarily have fun playing it.)\t\t\t\t\t
