\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy brain is in a knot after a session of this game, but in a good way.

This game throws out all the familiar puzzle games tropes and let you define what is winning.
And if you said that this might seem like the game could have any win condition and the players would have multiple ways to solving almost any puzzle, you would be correct.

This is one of the most exciting and innovative games I have played in recent years and I fully endorse all this game has to offer, although I am of limited mental capacity and was not able to complete all of the levels, I did make it to only with about half a dozen puzzle unsolved and that's as far as I can go without watching youtube videos for the solutions. I can't say that the path to completing the puzzles I did was straight forward, as I have wracked my brain trying to figure out a few of the puzzles, and there were plenty of frustrating moments. However, every time you figure out a puzzle feels like a triumph and it consistently provides that through out. It has simple controls and mechanics, but they are all of them difficult to master and some of the mechanics take a while to get used to, but they are all individually simple and easy to understand. When you combine these mechanics, however, the game becomes infinitely complicated and the final puzzles in the game really test your understanding of the underlying mechanics.

The way this game forces the player's brain to twist and develop out-of-the-box thinking is something not a lot of games do. And even with knowing to think outside of the norms, the game's solutions might still delight/surprise you when you figure them out.

This is a consistently difficult and thoroughly enjoyable puzzle game. Everyone should give this game a go, even if you are not interested in puzzle games, because this is utterly unique and there is nothing else quite like it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI genuinely love the game. I love the simplistic looking puzzles that can take a while of thinking, I love the atmosphere, I love everything about the game.

Now, you may ask, why then I give it a negative review:

Spoliers ahead

The game lies to you. Several times. These are just the two most prominent I remember.
There are a handful of puzzles within the first two areas that straight up lie to you for later puzzles. One that comes to mind is a puzzle involving a conflicting block, something like an immutable "Flag Is Flag" in the upper left, and if you try and make Flag something else, it says you cant do that. OK, that makes sense. But it doesn't tell you that order matters, a puzzle or two later requires that you have that "Baba Is Baba" BEFORE you slide a block upwards that tells it "Baba Is Crab". I thought it would just break, or turn me into a Crab since I had slid the block in. Order matters.
Fine, that can be intuited. I'll concede that. Maybe.
It would've been nice to know at some point earlier "Hey Order Matters" without having to trial and error it.

Then Later on there is a puzzle involving a robot and the solution I could figure out broke the rule about conflicting strings. Or at the very least didn't tell you that a broken string can still work with a broken part
ie. an immutable"Flag is Flag" then, the solution being to line up another string that reads "Flag Is Rock And Win". This will just exclude the "Rock" part from working and keep the rest. There's no tutorial for how operators work, and there's no way to learn some of the rules except by sheer dumb luck (or looking them up). I want to know the rules for the game, rather than having to figure them out through trial and error. I shouldn't be able to solve a puzzle because I'm a big dumb stupid thick idiot, not because I don't know all the rules. \t\t\t\t\t
