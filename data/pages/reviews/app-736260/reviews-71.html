\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is, in my opinion, one of the best games released in the past decade. It will be looked upon as a classic game for years to come. I would recommend for those interested in the game to watch the two trailers, and then get through at least the tutorial section of the game (first 9 or so levels). If you like it after this, then you'll like the rest of the game. It is very consistent in how it adds new rules and tiles so it doesn't ever feel old. I would give it a 9/10 score!

Overview/Pros: As of writing this review, I have 90 hours on record. I'm not using a guide or looking up answers, and I've just reached the halfway point. This length will vary greatly from player to player based on their problem-solving techniques and if they use hints/look up answers. It is a very challenging game as it makes you consider several fundamental issues when solving a puzzle: 1) the tile pieces needed, 2) where the tiles are located (and how to access them), 3) the order of operations when adding or removing a rule, and 4) tile/item placement. Sometimes the solution to the level seems obvious, but getting to that end goal is not as clear as it seems. The game has 200+ levels, and they get progressively harder as new tiles and rules are added. The graphics are very simple and stylized, which is good in my opinion since it helps you focus on solving the puzzle. I think the music is nice and relaxing, although after hearing some tracks for 20 hours it can get a bit repetitive. I will be playing this game for many years as I try to achieve 100% completion, but for now I can say that this game is one of my favorites. Every couple months I'll try solving a handful of puzzles to progress. It is one of those games I think is best played in shorter bursts rather than longer play sessions.

Cons: Not all levels are created equal, as sometimes it seems like most puzzles are set up to be solved only one way in and in a specific order. You are granted a lot of freedom in some puzzles and not so much in others. This adds to variety, but I think it makes some puzzles more frustrating than enjoyable to solve. The rules are also sometimes hard to understand, such as EMPTY. I think sometimes if you are placing several tiles on top of each other it can be very confusing how they operate. At times it seems like puzzles can be too open ended to where you can't figure out what the solution or the starting moves are, which leads to confusion in what it wants you to solve. It can be a frustrating game at times, but I think it is also very satisfying to overcome the challenges the game asks of you. Of the roughly 120 puzzles I've beat so far, there have been only a handful that I spent 2 to 3 hours on solving. As I reach these later levels, I'm starting to take more time (sometimes reaching 30 minutes to 1 hour per puzzle). If you see time being a restraint for this game, I think this could cause some frustration for some players. With all my complaints I still can't fault it for what it is. The game does such a great job at what it does for me to hand-wave these complaints. At the same time I think these may be what makes or breaks the enjoyment of the game for others.

Overall I think its a very challenging, creative, innovative, and polished game! :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Baba is Lazy and Not Read: Baba is you is a phenomenally creative collection of brain-twisters, that leaves you both deeply anxious about your inability to solve puzzles that toddlers would find visually appealing and extremely euphoric when you finally solve them anyways (take that, toddlers!). Wholeheartedly recommended.

Baba has Charm: The game is simple, visually appealing (might I say cute), with an equally simple premise. Baba is you is a sokoban-style tile based pushy puzzle game, where you aim to win isolated levels by... well, any means available. The unique twist is the game is that every rule of the game is quite literally spelled out as tiles. The sore reason you control the titular character is due to the titular rule being made in the game (clever, eh?). This means that fundamentally every level has an out-of-the-box, meta-ish solution, making for a very rewarding experience.

Content is More The game initially has 10 worlds all with more than a dozen levels, plus the early tutorials and more difficult extras, all grouped around themes and novel game rules. The game also boasts 2 free level pack DLCs with new and exciting content, and also has a secret end-game that you access by a clever trick you should figure out on your own. Seriously, everyone always clicks spoiler tags anyways. All in all, a vast amount of tough and unique puzzles to solve. The game also boasts a level editor and custom levels, which is a vast rabbit hole of extra content once you inevitably get addicted to the style of puzzles you find in the game.

Review is Done In conclusion, the game is probably the bet puzzle game of the last 10 or so years, and is easily value for money. Game is Buy.
