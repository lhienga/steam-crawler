\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFabulous and difficult little puzzle game. I love the concept and it's well executed. However, I do wish the controls felt more responsive. I'm not sure if the issue is a delay on the movement animation, input lag, button queuing, or some combination of these, but it's really hard to move your character accurately when holding down the inputs. The only solution is to press the arrow keys the exact amount of times you want your character to step, which is a pain.

Update: I think I came to a conclusion too quickly on this game. The concept is indeed great, but as time has passed my experience with it has gotten worse and worse. I echo the other review citing puzzle design that is meticulously restricted to the developer's one intended solution, leaving no room for creativity. Many puzzles would require a new and counter-intuitive trick to solve, and those tricks frequently felt like borderline exploits. It was frustrating to spend like an hour on some puzzles only to realize that the one possible solution is something I would have considered a bug or glitch coming in, like being able to SWAP yourself inside an object that has the STOP rule applied and then simply walking out of that object instead of being stuck there. It doesn't help either that the rules often overlap with each other, meaning that you have to memorize which rules take priority over each other in addition to what the rules mean. I feel like I'm having to luck into the developer's interpretation of ambiguous logic via trial and error as much as playing around with established game mechanics, and it's gotten to be quite a slog. The occasional a-ha moments just aren't enough to redeem it. 6.13 was the straw that broke the game's back for me.\t\t\t\t\t
