\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review does not contain a single 'X is Y' joke.

I've never been more upset that I'm too stupid to solve a game than with this one.
I knew I had something special in my hands as soon as I started roaming around the world-map and accidentally seemed to have solved a puzzle that sent me to a completely different world map somehow, but man, some of the logic here is just far beyond my reckoning. Having to figure out the order of specific things to do here is like trying to learn a programming language when you barely understand math. This game has given me some of the biggest 'AHA!' moments of my entire life, but I found myself becoming stumped way too early in the game, and it wouldn't be much of a game at all if all I did was glue my eyes to a walkthrough and refused to work things out on my own. So I'm personally stuck in something of a limbo with this game, not able to rise to the challenge of making Baba somehow push things around so they work correctly in my mind, but wanting to play it nonetheless.

Would I recommend this brain-scramble of an experience to anyone else? Yes. To every single person I've become friends with, actually. Even if all it'd be for is just to watch them try to figure out how to turn a sheep into a brick wall so they can clone flying letters that need to destroy a flag to put the key in lava and win the level so Baba can take a dang bath.
I'd bet that's actually one of the level solutions.

For the record, I normally HATE brain-teaser puzzle games like this. I'd never be able to play through something like Fez or The Talos Principle without going mad. This one is just somehow... pleasant. Like someone hitting you in the face with a foam noodle instead of a baseball bat. Its out-of-the-box solutions are somehow perfectly logical, once you get a handle on this game (not that doing so is particularly easy), and it just appeals to that secret-hunter in me who so badly wants to know what lays beyond the Baba-shaped veil.

's just good. The devs knocked it outta the park with this one, and I'm excited to see what else they do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this game has a clever concept, but I think the introduction of the new mechanics and pacing are not that good. New mechanics are introduced through tutorial puzzles, which can be a good way, but there are not enough tutorial puzzles. Many ways in which a new mechanic like switch or fall are used, are newly introduced through the more difficult puzzles, which resulted in me constantly opening a wiki to look up what something exactly means, because I didn't know what to do with it. There are also many instances where ways of interacting with a mechanic used last three worlds ago are then again used in a new difficult puzzle, so that by that time, I've already forgotten that a certain way of using a block or rule is possible.

I see many people say that this game is difficult, and in general I can enjoy difficult games, but I find that for me too much of the difficulty comes from not remembering how certain things work and not being properly introduced to a mechanic and needing to look them up in a wiki.

I will end on a positive note, because this game has many settings for how intense you want the visuals to be, plus some ways to customize audio, and as someone who gets easily visually and auditorily overstimulated, this is great in terms of accessibility.\t\t\t\t\t
