\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish there was a "Mixed" option in Steam rather than binary Positive/Negative, but here we are, and given how overwhelmingly positive most reviews are, I feel like I need to throw in a negative one.

I cannot in good faith say that I actively dislike the game. At 19 hours of playtime, I enjoyed a lot of levels and various mechanics that this absolutely groundbreaking concept provides.

However, I expected this game to be the pinnacle of creativity, where you bend the rules to your liking to reach the goal. This is very much not the case. Most levels feel incredibly rigid, and are hardwired against ANY possible alternative solution, even if that solution isn't much easier. You have to follow a very specific and narrow path the the dev intended, and god forbid you make a slightly wrong move. A lot of levels also exploit various unintuitive interactions that aren't explained to player in any way. For example, I had to google what "empty is push/pull" does because I couldn't derive it just from trial and error.

Right now I've completed about 130 levels and I have used walkthrough hints for about 20% of them. Did I enjoy solving the puzzles? For most of them, I did. Do I regret buying the game? No, not really. Will I try and solve more? Most likely not, since I feel like I've exhausted all puzzles that don't require your brain to have the power of a corporate neural network. As I progress further into the game, more and more puzzles feel just frustrating.

PS some meta level stuff in this game is also absolutely genius\t\t\t\t\t
