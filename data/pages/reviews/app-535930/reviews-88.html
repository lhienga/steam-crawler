\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: It's fun enough, but gets very samey very quickly.

Two Point Hospital (Two Point Studios, 2018) is a hospital simulation game in the vein of old 90's games like Theme Hospital - indeed, many of the same developers from Bullfrog Studios formed Two Point Studios nearly 30 years later!

In Two Point Hospital, you play as an administrator hiring doctors, nurses, janitors and assistants, laying out the hospital and otherwise making sure that the patients are reasonably happy and not keeling over dead in the middle of the hall.

First things first: the animations in this game are great. From the little things like the walk cycles to the more obvious ones like the treatment animations where are pretty much all unilaterally funny. Unfortunately, you're often playing quite zoomed out and on 1.5x speed, so you don't often get to examine the animations close-up unless you're going out of your way to do so, but regardless, a lot of time and effort was put into this.

What about the gameplay? Well... this is where Two Point Hospital starts to fall apart a bit. The basic loop of a patient is as follows:
1. The patient arrives, and a front-desk assistant guides them to a GP's office.
2. The patient meets the GP, and gets a partial or complete diagnosis of their issue.
3. If the diagnosis is incomplete, the patient goes to a more-thorough diagnosis room, like a General Diagnosis or a MEGA Scanner.
4. If the patient's diagnosis level is below the user-defined threshold (90% accuracy by default), repeat steps 2 through 4 until the diagnosis is above that threshold
5. Once the diagnosis is complete, they go to the appropriate treatment room and get treated.

This is the core loop of the game: making sure the above 5 steps go by as quickly as possible. As such, the game is really more of a layout simulator, putting rooms at appropriate locations such that patients don't need to travel far, duplicating rooms that are bottlenecks to increase patient throughput, and minimizing distractions to patients. And this is where the first major issue of the game rears its head.

Each patient has five sliders: hunger, thirst, and bladder, which are self-explanatory; boredom, which can be resolved via reading material, arcade machines, fountains, etc.; and health, which is essentially the patient's 'timer'. If their timer runs out, they die, you take a hit to your reputation, and you might even have a ghost show up, spooking your other patients and staff. And if any of the other sliders runs out, they leave the hospital angry, also taking a hit to your reputation. The thing about this is: because the most important thing of the game is getting patients through the medical process quickly, handling any of these sliders is basically pointless. Hungry patients will leave their lines to go buy snacks, even if they're next in line for the room, holding up not only their treatment, but the treatment of everyone behind them. So, counterintuitively, it's best to ignore all the sliders until you can easily afford good ways of handling the other sliders, like treating boredom with fountains, which take only a second per patient and can handle four patients at once.

Further, the money situation in the game is pretty shakey. When you're going for your first star in any given level, it's fairly good - your income is pretty low, but managable. But it's pretty trivial for the amount of money you get to snowball, leaving you with millions and nothing to spend it on in the mid- and late-game. Getting 2- and 3-star ratings in most levels was just a function of training up all my staff as best I could and otherwise keeping the window open long enough to meet whatever arbitrary goals I needed (usually some variation of 'cure 100 patients')

Maybe it's just me, but the game also has a lot of cruft that you should never reasonably use if you're trying to play efficiently. There are tons of lovely placable items to make your hospital rooms feel like real hospital rooms - but it's much cheaper and more efficient to just plaster treatment room walls with gold-star diplomas to get that important prestige rating up (so, maybe not that different from real life, after all...?). There are a lot of different diagnosis rooms - but you really only need three, max. I literally never used an X-Ray when I could just train up a doctor to use the way better MEGA Scanner instead, and I stopped using the fluid analysis when I realized the animation took 10,000 years without an appropriatly-good bonus to diagnosis. There are news stands, gift shops, cafes... none of which are actually useful, for the above reasons of just getting in the way of efficient play.

As far as the music goes, it's fine. Very catchy, but in a bad way. I found myself incessantly humming some of the background music, even though I didn't especially like it. The soundtrack, being diegetically a radio, has occasional ads and host breaks, which are mostly funny but start to repeat themselves pretty quickly.

There's a number of other things I didn't really get to experience, like the multiplayer challenges or Superbug System (none of my friends are playing a sim game from 4 years ago, bizarrely) or the REMIX levels, and I skipped out on the DLCs, which are pretty expensive for only adding 3 more levels each and a number of new diseases (which are, honestly, the least interesting part of the game, since it fundamentally just means you need to build New Room to treat New Disease).

If the game had more variety, I'd be more generous to it. There's one level, Duckworth-upon-Bilge, that I really loved because it turned the above loop on its head by preventing income from any source besides regular challenges and emergency treatments. But largely, most of the game's 15 levels are just glorified tutorials for individual game mechanics. "This is the Hot Level", "This is the Cold Level", "This is the Surgery Level", "This is the Epidemic Level", etc., etc. One-star ratings were more than enough for this, and going for two- and three-star ratings ended up being more tedious than anything else.

Oh, also, the game is WILDLY expensive at full price and regularly goes on sale for sub-$10. Don't buy this at full price!

Overall? It's okay. If you're a completionist, you're gonna have a miserable time going through the super-tedious and easy two- and three-star objectives, but you can have a nice time taking it easy, playing sub-optimally and only going for the basic objectives in each stage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. I do. It's rare for me to write a review but I felt compelled to after I surpassed the 200 hour mark.

For those who know what it is - it's Theme Hospital. I mean, it's a new take on it, so let's call it Theme Hospital 2.0. I love Theme Hospital, have it on Origin and still play it from time to time, but Two Point Hospital is one of those 'remakes' that actually really does scratch that itch.

I find it very addicting building the hospital ground up and trying new layouts, and the game has genuinely made me laugh out loud with it's illnesses and descriptions. The radio hosts and the reception voices, while repetitive after a while, I find charming and also very funny, especially if you enjoy dry humor.

The cons of the game are after a while it becomes the same thing over and over. While there are a few objectives that are harder than others, at the end of the day it's still just the same gameplay loop in every level.

Another thing, like many games now a days, is there are many different expansion packs that all give 3 levels, some fun, some meh, but if you are purchasing retail prices it can turn out VERY expensive for honestly not that much more content. To be fair it does go on sale often.

Overall it is one of my favorite games of all time, a worthy remake, and the base game is well worth the price, and even better on a deal.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHello friends,

Today i will review TWO POINT HOSPITAL. This game is a spiritual success to Theme Hospital. If you never played theme hospital, this game plays alot like it, but also has newer things, including costumes for doctors, epidemics, new diseases, even new illnesses. This game is more like a newer version of theme hospital, but with all the bells and whistles the original game did not have. There are also many things about this game that are quite fun. If you love micro managing games, this one you will really enjoy as a simulation game, even if the game does not take itself serious with all the funny diseases and such. The game can actually be challenging on the higher levels, or getting three star hospitals. Anyways, here are my pro/cons:

PROS:

- Cartoony diseases and funny ways to treat patients.
- Micromanging at it's finest.
- Silly costumes for your hospital employees.
- Challenging on harder levels and three start hospitals.
- Lots of dlc to add to the game new things.
- Steam workshop support so you can add even more items to your game.



CONS:

- A few minor bugs i ran into, including some lag, minor stuttering, and even a superbug project that kept staying in my superbug project page. It eventually went away though.
- Repetition seems to be the worst thing in the game. After awhile, every level and goals feel the same, even if there somwhat different. I suggest if you get bored, take a break from playing, then come back to this game after awhile.

RECOMMENDED! This game can be fun if you love micromanaging games and simulators. This game is not for serious hospital simulation players. There is another game on steam for that. The game is fun, but after awhile, can become repetitive.\t\t\t\t\t
