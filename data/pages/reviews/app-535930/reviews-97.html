\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAddicting game, although it feels a bit repetitive after several levels. However, each level does present a new primary object or focus (such as a natural disaster or a specific type of illness). Essentially, you manage a hospital by diagnosing patients and curing them. It can get quite difficult to hit a 3 star rating for your hospital. For clarification, each hospital level provides objectives, you complete them to earn a 1 star rating, 2 star rating, or a 3 star rating before proceeding to the next level. The primary challenge is to keep your hospital debt free while ensuring your patients are waiting in line for a long time. Otherwise, their health continues to drop and they eventually die. Overall, the game is a lot of casual fun. However, I do believe the game itself could use some quality of life updates to make things easier to manage. Regardless, I recommend this game and can't wait for Two Point Campus.

(EDIT) I now have almost 100+ hours logged in this game, I can confirm that getting a 3 star on every level within the base game campaign is time consuming but still fun and/or challenging. If that is not enough, there is also plenty of DLC and a workshop on Steam.\t\t\t\t\t
