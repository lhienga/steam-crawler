\r\n\t\t\t\t\toddly satisfying and addictive game. I normally not go into management games like that, but 2 point Hospital is really in league of its own. Does require some planning in making your hospital efficient, and can be very unforgiving if you built it wrong. At the first impression it feels like relatively simple game with simple mechanics, but dont be fooled, there are layers beneath the layers. You can kinda leave your hospital manage itself or you can micromanage it to fine detail.

Also the illnesses in game are absolutely hilarious.

Graphics are bit candy-ish but pleasant for the eye, not some AAA cryengine raytracing madness, but it dont need it.

also very well optimized, little to no bugs (I haven't encountered a single game breaking bug or glitch so far) Big hospitals can be bit heavy on CPU but game seems to recognize multicore quite well so it still remain playable.

only cons I could so far mention is that sandbox options are quite limited, especially in chosing which diseases you want or not, so kiss goodbye on making small GP clinic in starting map, as 1 hour into play you will cover it just with GP's alone.

also DLCs prices and number are criminal, only buy on discount as its relatively just few new diseases, new map and some visual stuff, just more of the same in different color basically.\t\t\t\t\t
