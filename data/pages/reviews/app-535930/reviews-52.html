\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of these games I would recommend but still call missed potential. With a small work game could have far greater level. Instead writing detailed review you can find everywhere I just point out couple things that got my attention.

First some good things. Game is smooth and generally to play. It's simple and enjoyable gaming. So overall experience as whole game is there. Games won't need too much of a challenge as far it can keep you otherwise engaged. Can it?

There a lot of things that gets on you eventually. Before I go on some details that annoyed me I want to point out weakness of general design. Hospitals in this game feels more of a factory line medicine than actual place for healthcare. Whole point is to get people through which is logical but it kinda skips part of the game. There is little point having services other than keep quick comfort around. Your staff is only people spending time in your hospital. Patients just get through it with easiest path.

But now those little things I wished wasn't:
1) Research is not very innovative mechanic. It really is there to just unlock things and is very boring clicking game for money/kudosh which you rarely need like this.
2) Epidemics aren't fun. You don't want to visually find your patients and magically apply cure on them. Mostly not worth of the effort.
3) There is no logical illness tracking. Patients get checked randomly and head to treatment room. Certain illness should require certain diagnostics.
4) Building rooms and levels to them should be need based and attraction should be only one part of it.

So game is good but not great. Worth playing but not quite worth remembering. It just requires that last step for depth so you would like to build your own little perfect hospital.\t\t\t\t\t
