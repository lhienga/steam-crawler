\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is years late. I started playing it upon release because much like you I wanted Theme hospital. So, is this Theme Hospital? Yes. Absolutely yes. Is it the Theme Hospital you remember? Yes, absolutely yes. Ah, so its still a lot of addictive fun yeah?

Welllllllllllllllllllllllllll lets take the rose tinted glasses off a bit shall we? For me, this game checked off every expectation I wanted and did it in an authentic way in the exact style I wanted but after an hour I start to get bored. So, whats the problem? Why can't I play this nonstop like the original?

... Did I actually play the original for 5 hour gaming sessions, or was that a fever dream? I think it was a fever dream because as much fun as I had, and as much as I loved the style of the game i don't ever remember beating it.

I think the original has a lot of rosy memories and this game is a sheer tribute to that. Keep that all in mind. If you want a game that you expect to keep you engaged heavily for 30+ you will be hard pressed to hit that limit, but if you want to find a modern take on some of your favorite nostalgia, you'll definitely be happy.

I don't think I'll ever beat all the missions on this game, and you know what? It was still worth the money in my minds eye. I wanted a modern take on Theme hospital and I think I got it, but this ain't Tropico and you're not gonna lose track of time while playing it. It is fun, it is quirky, it is everything you are expecting.

WARNING FOR YAKUZA FANS: the intro will trigger you\t\t\t\t\t
