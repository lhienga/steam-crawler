\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing on and off for over 3 years, I have decided to give the game a thumbs up. The spiritual successor to Theme Hospital, but I always found it lacking the Bullfrog touch to keep playing through the night.

It took me years to work out why and it is simply down to the 3 levels. You see each hospital has three stars that you can drive towards. Whilst it is fun to drive towards the first star, it starts to become tedious towards the second star and then monotonous towards the third. Doing so does give rewards in the form of Kudos points which unlocks more items. Some are essential, others are not.

In Bulldog games you started a new level each time you completed a challenge. To go back to a one star hospital later on in the game and have to virtually collapse it all to undo the mistakes you made, in order to achieve 3 stars is not fun and I found it to be like a form of punishment.

My advice to new players, on your first foundation, go through the game only ever getting one star and then move on. Once completed then restart with a new foundation and aim for the 3 stars. This way you have learnt all the basics and tricks and best strategies and can enjoy the second play through.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother game where I Wish there was a neutral option. Two Point Hospital is a cute management simulator with a lot of tongue-in-cheek humor thrown around. Overall it was a moderately enjoyable experience to play through once. Minimal bugs, and a good chunk of content.

My problem with it is that as a management simulator, it's sorely lacking. There's not a lot of controls over your hospital or how it functions. You can restrict certain employees to certain types of work, but you can't restrict the areas they can go to. Ultimately a lot of it is just leaving the job to the AI. There's a pattern to getting the game to work in your favor, and by the time you're 1/4 through the campaign, you're playing every map the same way because it always works. The only thing left to do from there is to check off your little goal boxes.

It's also lacking in the design department. There's no planning tools (that I could find) for setting up your hospital. You're either building a room or placing an item. No placeholder indicators for something you're reserving space for later, no templates to work with (outside of templates for the rooms themselves).

So yeah, overall it was a fun little experience. But as a management sim: incredibly shallow, and little to no replay value.\t\t\t\t\t
