\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTheme Hospital Tw...uhhh I mean Two Point Hospital
Some 20 years on we got a successor, and it has a lot to live up to.

TPH follows the same style as its progenitor - limited story, focus on management and completing objectives to unlock subsequent levels, very un-serious tongue-in-cheek style. So far so good right?
In fact, there's very little other than the (mediocre) graphics that has changed, and that's actually a problem. See, although Theme Hospital was (is) a very good game, it was released in the 90's, and as such had a number of issues and limitations that came with the times. These are all still here, but it's not the 90's anymore.
There are still too many bugs with patients getting stuck in places they shouldn't. The hospitals are too small and in weird shapes, making room placement problematic at best (and efficient placement often impossible). The staff available to hire are often made terrible with either bad traits, or training combinations that make them inefficient or ineffective. It tries a bit too hard to be funny and ends up in that awkward place of seeming bland. Animation time for everything is incredibly slow so takes ages for thing to actually happen - and it's not in line with other timers in the game (day/month progression, health decay time, challenge timers).

But worst of all is the queue and job management algorithm. This is so bad it gets it's own section in this review, and is in large part responsible for it being negative.
Here's a few examples:
-Janitors assigned to only repair machines will wander around "looking for work" when there are machines that are not at full health. They will only take action when there is a machine below 50%. There is no way to adjust this.
-Sometimes the game will decide to send all patients to a single reception desk (even when there are 5+ desks, and 100+ patients). When removing the desk (to try and make it recalculate and fix a problem that shouldn't even exist), all 100 simple go to another single desk, instead of being split. This happened multiple times across multiple levels.
-When attempting to staff a room, it will sometimes pull staff out of an idle room 2 buildings over rather than the "looking for work" staff member (of the correct type) nearby.
-Patients being added to a room queue will get added to the far away room with a queue of 1 rather than the nearby room with a queue of 2. Why is this a problem? Because the walk time for this is often far longer than simply waiting in the closer "longer" queue.
-If a machine sets on fire and has to be put out, it will remain on low health. The janitor does not then automatically fix it, and will likely leave the room, allowing patients in to use the machine again. Which sets on fire again. How this got allowed to release I'll never know.
-Patients in a queue will wander across half the hospital to find a vending machine that has a queue of 0 rather than a nearby one in use. Again with a walk time that amounts to several patients worth of treatment time, for which - if the patient is at the front of the queue - the room has now been idle.
-The bug where a patient will just stand at the front of a queue and stop. And simply stand there until they die, holding up all the patients behind them (who are now nearly dead as well).


I fully recognise that these are very non-trivial problems and that making an algorithm that can solve these kinds of scenarios is difficult; but this level of failure is just not acceptable in a modern game. Yes Theme Hospital had similar issues, but that is not a modern game.

On top of all this, the price is too high. TPH released in 2018 and has a price (non-sale, at time of writing) of £24.99. PLUS all the not-exactly-cheap DLC on top of that (which don't have a whole lot of content for their price).


So overall, it gets a thumbs-down.\t\t\t\t\t
