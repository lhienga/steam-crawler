\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is virtually abandonware at this point. I liked the game. But it's lacking very basic realistic things for the hospital setting.

1) Majority of hospital campuses are not single floor buildings everywhere. Main building is usually 3-9 floors tall, and their campus buildings are 1-4 floors. The quantity of buildings is accurate, but the overall scope, not so much.

2) The ability to send customers home en masse or prevent new patients (unless certain criticality in illness as filter) is realllllly necessary. Once your hospital gets a certain reputation level the hospital just gets overwhelmed with patients and you have to do Patient-Culling as described by other guides. This will have to be performed regularly as either your Diagnostic wing or your General Practitioner wing is not yet large enough to accommodate the size of the incoming patient pool.
This is a reflection of real life when Private Practices are so busy and see so many patients, they no longer accept new patients and they'll advise you to go somewhere else instead.

These two things seem like very reasonable and easy Enhancements.

3) Where this becomes a thumbs down is THE BUG. There is a bug in the game that appears to happen once you have done a hospital for several years that will completely paralyze your run. Eventually the NPC's abruptly can no longer find pathing to anything and they all just freeze up. This inevitably spreads to more & more NPC's until your hospital is completely frozen and you cannot bail yourself out of it. Doesn't matter how many hospital sections you buy, how many rooms you sell, or anything.
This bug was detected by players more than a year ago and it still has not been addressed. I completed every hospital to 3 stars (only 1 DLC (Big Foot)) except for one hospital where the bug crippled my hospital before I could get to year 7.

3/10
Was fun while it lasted. Became more of a chore than fun in the late game of most hospitals.\t\t\t\t\t
