I have a doctor's appointment coming up and I'm going to bring it on my Steam Deck (yes, it plays on it, but I still use a bluetooth mouse with it in portable mode - can't speak to how to deck or a controller works on it as I use the mouse when I'm on PC too) but at my doc's appointment I'm going to bring it on my Deck, play it loud until they come check out what I am doing, I'll be sure to point out how much better a little video game can run medically more efficiently than they do. :P Then again if they didn't make me wait 3 hours after my scheduled appointment time I wouldn't have anything else to do except stare at other patients that are ahead of me and have been waiting since 1987. Now I can play and set up a better medical experience than they offer; no patients bored waiting in my queue! ;P

It's a well done enjoyable and totally chill kind of sim. Sit back and take your time, or rush through. It's your call and regardless of how you choose to play it, it's a lot of fun. I wasn't sure about if I should get it, but snagged it on sale and can honestly say I would pay the full price for it knowing there's so much to it. I'm only 9 hours in and know for a fact that this is a keeper that will long outlive a lot of my other games. I didn't get any DLC yet but I like knowing it's there when I get through it. That being said I know it will take awhile as I'm heavy into micro-managing in the game already so I can get every bit of fun and goodness out of it!

Good game, lots to enjoy with it - it's a game you can play however you want to without following a structured plan. For me, it's in micro-managing (you don't have to, but I'm glad it's there), and getting attached to what I've created, the staff I've hired and I plan on being the top hospital company in all award categories there are out there in Two Point County. Big tech, look out! :D

I'd give it a solid 9 out of 10 in my book if you're really into simulation games and milking the game for every bit of fun you can get out of it. That said, there's a lot to be had! Could be a perfect 10, but in this far I'm already happy with a 9 such a short time in. Who knows, I could update my review after having spent more time with it as I know there's so much ahead waiting for me to explore and crazy illnesses to cure ahead!

On a side note, I don't recommend getting it just for the Deck alone without using it docked in conjunction with a big screen or a larger monitor. It's just easier to set up the scenarios and hospitals on the PC. Once you get a hold of how it's set up and plays, the Deck is great and you can follow along well knowing about icons and stuff. I must say I was tickled at how it is on the Deck, but the PC is better for initial setting up of your hospitals.

No matter if you play it on PC or the little screen, (or enjoy on both like me), it's a game of big fun - good times if fun and slightly silly sims are your thing.\t\t\t\t\t
