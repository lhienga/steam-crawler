\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI must say, as a big Theme Hospital fan, this here was the sequel I was waiting for. Even though it isn't a official follow up, everything really does have the same feel and atmosphere while evolving into a far superior game in it's own right.

The cartoony graphics really do fit into the overall comedic theme as nothing in this game is taken seriously. The patient's illnesses, the treatments, even the in game radio and PA continually try to poke fun. And for the most part, if I wasn't laughing, I was revelling in the fun atmosphere that is created from the visuals and sound combined.

As for the game-play, it is a blast. Plenty of features and personalisation options for just about everything. Lots of management options from wage amounts to the overall cure targets. Very balanced progression and easy learning curve. But most of all, this UI is extremely intuitive, making the game-play as a whole a breeze to pick up.

This is just a solid game and is a must for anyone that loves their management sims. It might seem a bit pricey, but good luck finding another game like it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a management sim, I found it to be simple and tedious. Setting up a profitable hospital is usually straightforward, and then there's a lot of waiting. Waiting to accumulate enough money for expansions, or to upgrade machines, or to complete research projects, or to raise staff experience, or to roll new staff with better traits. These things in turn allow you to incrementally scale up or optimize your facility, but the management tasks and decision-making remain mostly unchanged. Similarly, completing level objectives often involves doing things like curing X number of patients, which, again, just comes down to waiting. Each scenario focuses on some specific challenge, but most won't be demanding enough to force you to rethink your approach. I got all the stars in the base game but wouldn't really recommend the experience.

The humor felt very sanitized and was hit or miss for me. Regardless, the game has a lot of personality, and the radio DJs and PA announcer do a decent job of breaking up the monotony. I didn't have the audio on the whole time but I'm not sure I ever actually heard one of the radio DJ skits repeat.

I think this is best approached as a building sandbox that happens to have some objectives you can complete if you feel like unlocking new decorations. I can also see the appeal if you just want a casual family-friendly management sim to kill time. Whatever the case, I'd catch it on discount.\t\t\t\t\t
