Over-heated my computer and had to turn the graphics down, they aren't that great anyways. All patients have non-real world problems easily cured, but fails most the time. Room building is clumsy and error-prone. Staff wander off from their jobs while their patients stagger into the hallways to die and I constantly have to put them back to work before more patients die. Janitors fix medical equipment and chase ghosts of dead patients. Research involves chasing some alien rats/worms, which I never found. Kinda more like a hospital flipper with all the decorating...I'd rather cure real world diseases, perform complex surgical operations and research virus vaccines - I could care less if a flower in the waiting room isn't pretty. Doesn't seem to have much depth...I'm trying, but I'm already bored and want something more complex and engaging, so it goes back on the shelf probably forever. No bugs or big problems, so I have no excuse to return it, unfortunately. Very disappointed in Sega and will probably not buy anything from them for a while. And to all the fan-boys whom don't like my review, you can go and give yourself a lobotomy, you'll enjoy the game then even more.\t\t\t\t\t
