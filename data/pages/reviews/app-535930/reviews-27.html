\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTwo Point Hospital drew me in with its nostalgic charm reminiscent of 'Theme Hospital,' but sadly, the excitement fizzled out as I delved deeper into the gameplay.

The repetitive nature of the game becomes apparent as you progress, with each level feeling like a rinse-and-repeat cycle of building the same rooms over and over again. Once you've optimized a GP office or a treatment room, there's little incentive to deviate from the formula, leading to a sense of monotony.

My biggest gripe with Two Point Hospital, however, is its lackluster solo experience. Many items are locked behind group projects, forcing players to either team up with strangers on Steam or pester friends to participate. Alternatively, some items are tied to social media groups, requiring additional sign-ups or logins.

This reliance on social interaction and daily tasks to unlock content feels more suited to mobile games than a PC title, especially considering the upfront cost. It's disheartening to see a game I've had for years become more about habit-building and fear of missing out than genuine enjoyment.

Despite its potential, the game's emphasis on social engagement and repetitive gameplay has left me feeling unmotivated to even launch it. For a game that promises a trip down memory lane, it's a disappointing realization.\t\t\t\t\t
