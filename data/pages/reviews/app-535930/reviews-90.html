To start off with, the art direction is fun, the ailments are silly and the basic designing of a hospital is satisfying. For both me and my partner that got us interested and in the door.

As the game progresses though, the optimization requirement really kicks in and it is both unclear and incredibly unfun. Things like ensuring you put up art on every wall, even if it's the same piece a hundred times. Training staff is clunky and annoying. The stream of patients seems to always test your max capacity, so unless I was missing some core mechanic, you could never be "on top" of the demand for hospital use. If you got more staff, you started getting more patients flooding in. It always had me sitting behind the curve of success. There was a level where all of your doctors started off poorly trained with the benefit that you got paid for training on the job. But the hospital was always brimming with patients, so I struggled to find time to do any training. Failed treatments lowered your hospital ratings, which affected your ability to complete levels and move on.

I don't know if I was missing some simple core mechanics of the game, but it felt like you ended up worse off as you built up your hospital, so later levels became more of a headache then an engaging management time.\t\t\t\t\t
