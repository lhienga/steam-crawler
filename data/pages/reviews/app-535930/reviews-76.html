\r\n\t\t\t\t\tORIGINAL REVIEW - PLEASE SEE ADDITIONS BELOW FOR WHY THE SWING TO POSITIVE. MINOR SPOILERS IN SECOND REVIEW.


It's a negative but only just.

It runs fine, I'm not aware of any crashes so far. It's bright and colourful with lots of amusing animations. As the game develops there are more and more rooms to unlock for both analysis and treatment. There are lots of objects to add to rooms, some of which need to be unlocked by tasks - and a lot, disturbingly and irritatingly, of seemingly misplaced Sonic themed items. There's a lot of content, some of it quite funny, on the incessant in game radio shows. The announcer has a lot of lines too.

So why the negative vote ? This is an issue you'll only encounter after putting 20+ hours into the game. The game is heavily weighted towards payment for cures. Diagnosis is basically a loss leader with the expenses barely covering your doctor or nurse costs. So the two most basic "cure" room s are Pharmacies and Wards, both available from virtually the start of the game. But 2.0 Hospital unfairly in my view frequently starves you of the more basic illnesses so you constantly end up with that even with an overcrowded and understaffed hospital you're still not making enough money to cover costs. You can end up spending long times just watching your hospital spiral deeper and deeper into debt with realistically nothing that you can do about it. This isn't fun.

For anyone's who's played Theme Hospital it's very similar but with obvious updates for a game made decades later. The blatant sexism of Theme Hospital with all Doctors and Janitors being male and all nurses female has been removed in Theme 2.0 hospital. Earthquakes and epidemics are back but so far they've been nowhere near as lethal as under the previous game. Research takes far longer but perseveres to all future hospitals - you never have to research the same thing twice.

I've played a lot of Sim Games - Theme Hospital to conclusion multiple times, Theme Park, Rollercoaster Tycoon 1,2 and 3 and all versions of Tropico up to 5, completing 4 and 5. So I know how to play Sim Games. IMHO 2.0 Hospital is too difficult on the mid levels onwards to be fun.

S.x.

REVISED REVIEW - REFERS TO ORIGINAL REVIEW

I was wrong and dumb. I'd been increasing staff salaries on promotion from content to happy which bled my profit margin.

Game tips - start off with these basic rooms as soon as you have them and can afford them - GP's office, Pharmacy, General Diagnosis, Cardiology, Psychiatry (if you have an appropriately qualified doctor), Ward, Toilets, Rest Room all of which should be decorated and facilitated to a good to high standard. There's a very useful button on the staff tab that allows you to meet all salary demands that should be used regularly to keep staff content. Add research later unless you need to research a new disease to progress in which case it should be prioritised. Add new doctors and nurses when you're finding that there are delays in staff answering the calls to a room. This way you won't get overstaffed. On the main option screen I reduced the queue warning from six to five - persistent queues of six and higher impair performance badly. I made cure percentage 100% rather than 95% - death rates are high enough without guessing at cures but checked the option that patients who had 100% diagnosis didn't need to go back to a GP's office for a final diagnosis - you'll already need a dozen or more GP's offices in a large hospital and they're a loss leader, a break even at best.

So further impressions having finished the game ? First the good.

Contrary to my dumb original review the levels are all logically winnable (maybe not the public level - see below) with a set strategy taking you through all levels. It's a question of prioritising purchases according to profit. Note that an epidemic will normally more than pay for the room to treat it and at times you will have to take out loans. My main joy was the huge amount of content in the radio shows - I think I hadn't heard all of it by the time I three starred the last level (what was in the box ?) and the content varied between mildly amusing and genuinely funny. For a game that can get repetitive this was a worthwhile investment by the developers and much appreciated. Earthquakes and epidemics are inconveniences rather than absolute disasters like they could be in Theme Hospital and epidemics are far fairer although in a huge hospital they're difficult to spot in such a mass of people. The skill trees were a welcome addition from Theme Hospital but research was more equivocal - narrowly in favour of 2 point hospital overall. It remained colourful, robust performance wise, and endearing right to the end.


The bad ? The "public health" level is horrible - you don't get paid for cures but for arbitrary tasks which messes up your planning completely. The last two levels were time intensive with much time spent just waiting for figures to clock up to progress level - similar to the original in fact. There's no "victory" screen at the end of the game - unless it comes from three starring every hospital - just credits which was disappointing and a definitely worse than Theme Hospital which had a good (if now very chunky) end video. The muzak that backgrounds the game is unlike the DJ commentaries repetitive and dull.

Having now three starred the final level I'm unlikely (1) to try and three star everything (2) to pay out for the comparatively far more expensive DLC. The game is good, colourful fun but the 93 hours I put into it more than scratched my itch for replaying a Theme Hospital type game. Now the new game with setting up a university campus - that I'm price tracking.

However for the current sale price of £6.24 or lower you're getting a good, fun game that should keep you pleasantly engaged for many hours which is definitely more than good enough.

And to the devs sorry for my original review. I got it wrong.
