\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo let me preface this by saying 2 things:
1: This is the first mech game I've taken even semi-seriously, so this can definitely be considered a "new player experience" review. (Completed all major DLC quests + mainline quest except Inner Sphere DLC quests)
2: PLAY THIS GAME WITH FRIENDS

Unless you are a super hardcore Battletech/Mechwarrior nerd, playing this solo is best described as "Grind Hell". Once you plug in even 1 or 2 friends though to balance out the basic AI, storytelling via paragraphs, the monotonous repair/rearm tossing and time management, and the weird min/max mission design at times, the game becomes an awesome almost drop-in party game whether people are on PC, Xbox, Steam Deck - whatever. But, there are a few caveats:

Quick aside before I get to that, especially in case a dev is reading: ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, these mod tools. You guys packaged an entire version of Unreal 4 and you guys market it just as "mod tools". Y'all need to spread that like wildfire, because that level of high-end-user support - ESPECIALLY IN THE CURRENT YEAR - is nothing short of astonishing. I'm already starting shenanigans with it. Also, the fact that game hosts owning the DLC's for Co-op games means that anyone jumping in can access, mess around, and play the DLC’s is a massive consumer W. I already have played them in my Co-op group but bought the DLC’s anyway for both of these reasons. Great Stuff from a game dev in the current year.

The Mech stuff:
Mech Choices, especially when combined with DLC's/YAML - nothing short of awesome. Instant action is great for getting in and messing around with different builds, and the Career mode - while I haven't messed around with it personally a lot -definitely will add a lot of replayability and allows players to jump back in quickly. YAML (Yet Another Mech Lab) is a also a fantastic conversion mod which basically turns the editor on its head and allows you to re-engine, re-engineer, and re-spec the mech you have for almost anything and come up with some really, really tilted builds. Light mechs are insanely speedy but definitely light armored scouts, harassers, and snipers - mediums (my fav, honestly) feel diverse in each of their chassis specializations and maintain a good portion of the light's speed while adding in extra survivability (and therefore, mission time!) - heavies either kick the specialization up to 11 and allow you to come up with some really surgical combos, and assaults, well… they’re chunky and they break ♥♥♥♥!

Soundtrack: I am convinced at least 10% of the game's budget was spent on raw, freshly mined titanium just for the construction of the soundtrack. Seriously, it's metal as hell and works SO well with the atmosphere and mechs. Give the team that put them together a raise.

General Combat, especially Mech v Mech: While I am a Mech Game Novice at absolute best, jumping in for the first time with the Javelin and Centurion on the initial missions was relatively straightforward, plus the game gives you a wingman "Lancemate" with their own mech on basically the second mission. Once you start completing contracts and start getting money in the bank, it’s very easy to pick up more mechs and equipment as long as you’re willing to play more conservatively with taking excessive damage and are smart about contracts (less mechs and more standing, the better the mission for $$$)

The Meh:
Enemy AI. On solo since the player tends to put out the most dps, AI will focus you more and if you aren’t careful you can go from a great mission to contract failed REAL quick. When you’re in coop and working with human players you can more rapidly communicate stuff like that, dive for cover while pulling agro and work through stuff like that. If you are gonna play this in SP, definitely pick up YAML, Pilot Overhaul, and maybe another AI mod to make them not suck. Be careful, though, as that will affect the enemy AI in certain situations as well!

Story Presentation:
This one is hit or miss at times. I’m a veteran of Ace Combat games and those in that genre so getting dialogue crammed in my ear in the middle of a firefight is kinda par for the course for me. My main issue is that when you are pulled out of gameplay, your only other options for how story nuggets are given are: A, actually decently planned but kinda meh executed in-fps-character scenes on the bridge and mech bay of your dropship that are just a lot of talking that is interesting and mundane all at the same time- B, a wall of text vomited on screen- and C, news reports. There are a handful of short cutscenes sprinkled in, mostly around the DLC, but other than the big opening cinematic about your dad there’s… pretty much nothing else in that department. A missed opportunity during some of the main quests if you ask me.

The Mediocre:
The Pacing:
Alright I’ve been saving this for now but I’ve gotta rag on the main game loop for a second. In combat, you have three main resources - Heat, Ammo, and Recharge - Heat is a factor of how much you fire your weapons in total as a over a short time - Ammo is how many shots in total over the mission you have for missile and ballistic (and if using Chem variants, laser) weapons - and Recharge is the individual reloading/recharging of the weapons to fire again after you pull the trigger. That’s fine, they integrated well into the mechlab and aren’t really gameplay biased one way or the other IMO. That’s good and built fine.

My problem is out of the game-game though. When you’re jumping around and doing not mech stuff, you “only” have really money and time to worry about, but every individual travel/repair/rearm/training/etc is so intricately woven into every game event that you end up getting into a loop of: “Drop into a mission, walk around for a few minutes dealing with scouts and stuff in a sneaky style, find the objective, go hot for maybe 3-4 minutes, bee-line it to extract… and then click through menus for 30 minutes.” I get that having industrial and conflict zones with varying repair times is more realistic, and having literal weeks for repairs and overhauls to mechs is more realistic, or that tech isn’t so advanced that being able to jump around inside of months is more realistic, but… I feel like if there was some more testing on the game loop in a singleplayer setting, someone should have said “hey, isn’t there a way to make this more fun?”

Don’t get me wrong, this game is absolutely still a “recommend”, but if I’m honest it’s only really a “recommend with friends”. The operations, especially the curated campaign ones, are very entertaining challenges 95% of the time that will have you and your buddies juggling incoming enemies while moving through an exoplanet hellscape. It’s awesome, but, unless you are REALLY focused on just grinding missions, it’s much too easy to fall into a net of resource balancing and menu dragging instead of actually playing a game. So, my best advice to a new player would be:

See if your friends have it, snag the base game, set up a good 3-4 player team, and just play it for a bit. Let them build some different mechs if they’re familiar, focus on acquiring a boatload of money for more mechs, and just run missions. Save the really in depth refitting and min-maxing for a slightly later time in the run when you have enough money to really go nuts with it. And when you do - don’t be afraid to go nuts

PHX-KB (50N-4HT) - ER L Laser, 2x M Laser, 2x SRM 6 for a disgusting amount of alpha and dps, LosTech’d out the ass and typically juggling a MASC, Jump Jets, and Beagle

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor fans of Battletech, this is a great game. For non-fans, this is a mediocre game. Most of the story is told during radio transmissions during missions. This often happens in battle so I could not hear or read the radio transmissions while focusing on fighting. Another problem is that most mission markers do not appear on the radar. Main ones will, but things like salvage and repair bays do not. The AI lance mates are also dumb as rocks. Often getting stuck on rocks, marching through buildings you are supposed to protect, stepping in line of fire, and firing into mountains in attempt to hit enemies behind the mountain. They will often charge then stop right in-front of an enemy and get destroyed even though they already have little armor. Enemies occasionally spawn under ground so you have to restart missions. Game can be very frustrating, but nothing else is available if you want to pilot a Battlemech. Therefore, I recommend this game if you like the Battletech universe. If you do not like Battletech, do not get this game.\t\t\t\t\t

Graphics and UI
Besides the flashy visuals of the mechs the cracks in the game become fairly obvious after a few hours. Elements of the UI feel bland, unresponsive and look more like a poor placeholder - like the dialogue boxes for instance, everything is very block-y and texture-less. The art portraits look terrible and feel out of place. The main menu while you are in the ship is a nightmare to navigate, it's a vomit of ugly menus and unintuitive tabs.

Gameplay
Gameplay becomes boring and repetitive. The grind to upgrade your mechs feels unrewarding and more like a chore. The maps have no distinct quality to them - unlike the maps in Mechwarrior 3 where each map had an interesting and unique design to them that made them memorable and fun to play in - I distinctly remember every detail in that opening map on the beach side village. Ending each mission by stepping inside a ring feels abrupt, jarring and lazy. Couldn't they have animated the dropship landing, have your mechs require to get inside as you take heavy fire and launch off? Heck, there is even some Mechwarrior 3 missions where you have to do that. The AI is frustrating. Your team feels more like a liability than anything.

Story
The writing in the game is downright awful. The story is non existent (can't a Mechwarrior game begin without the forced hook of loosing a family member within the first 2 minutes before you can develop any sort of bond, and then be sent on a revenge quest for someone (as a player) I couldn't care less about - it's been done to death, it's uninspiring, and lazy. Take Battletech, the writing is an incredible step up for the franchise and the political story line has depth, with unpredictable twists and turns. Even the random events that occur in the spaceship between missions has better writing than anything this game can offer. All the dialogue is cringe and annoying, and all the characters feel soulless and one dimensional.

Music and Sound
The music is a departure of the established tone of the franchise - dark, atmospheric, orchestral, ambient, industrial, synth, metal. It blares over the top of everything dulling all your senses, whereas in previous games I felt the music harmonised well with the sounds of the weaponry and the tense tonality of the fights, as well as the general sound design and sound effects - in this game I feel there is no atmosphere at all, it's drowned out. I personally don't like the voice of the computer. It's feels too much like Siri or Alexa and lacks the robotic, marshal, disposition in it's tone. I feel less like I'm in war machine, and more like I'm at home hearing a personal reminder for my doctor appointment tomorrow.

I keep coming back wanting to enjoy this game, but as a long fan of the franchise I can't bring myself to play it longer than an hour.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the Paradox BattleTech game. This is a first person shooter in the same universe. It's great!

There's plenty to do and customize:

- collect mechs, customize their gear, upgrade it over time
- recruit & train pilots by putting them in mechs where using it will help improve their skills
- fly around between industrial areas for cheap repairs and conflict areas with missions you want to do
- pick factions you want to help, and crush those you've deemed an enemy (or play all sides, which is much harder because there is only a small reputation margin in favor of helping people)
- use the story as a guide through increasingly difficult encounters

The soundtrack is solid, and while a bit repetitive, it hasn't started to annoy me yet after 17 hours.

I'm now in the 200-220 ton deployment range, and I'm starting to wonder if I've sort of seen it all in terms of game mechanics. Mechs only have specific weapon/utility slots, and room for generic stuff like ammo and cooling. Engines and internals are all fixed. There are no bonuses to specific weapons or extra effective cooling, or things like that. It feels like some of the underlying complexity of the game system was simplified to streamline this game.\t\t\t\t\t
