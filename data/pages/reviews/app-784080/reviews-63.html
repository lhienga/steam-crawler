\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI won't lie, there isn't a lot of depth to MW 5 mercs as far as i have experienced in the 30 hours i have played. and by that, i mean that this game GREATLY reminds me of the all time classic horde/co op shooters on the market. things like deep rock, payday 2, killing floor, vermintide, and earth defense force. those games, at their core, have really fun, but basic, gameplay loops. MW5 is no different.

so while the story may not be in depth, the combat might get repetitive, i would consider it just as good as any of those titles. it reminds me a lot of EDF where you pick a mission, get your squad together, choose your gear (and there's a similar "tonnage" limit mechanic like there is in EDF), finish the mission to get your rewards. with MW5 having more out-of-combat meta progression mechanics.

if you, like me, enjoy hopping on and just doing some missions in a horde-type shooter then i would recommend this game on sale. and the reason why i don't recommend this game at full price is because i think the gameplay variety is greatly increased by buying, minimum, the "heroes of the inner sphere" DLC. and these two items at full price, is $50 which is a little much IMO. but at 50% off, $25 is a great deal for that amount of content. If you are on the fence for buying, even when the game is on sale, I would check out a few simple youtube videos of some missions. as i said above, the gameplay loop is fun, but reptitive. so if you enjoy the gameplay you see in those videos, that's most of the game.

and while co op does not share progression, which is unfortunate, there is an EXTREMELY easy to install mod on nexus called "co op rewards" https://www.nexusmods.com/mechwarrior5mercenaries/mods/598?tab=description which mostly resolves this issue. and it is a client side only mod. so you can use it with any host regardless of the mods they use.

have fun!\t\t\t\t\t
