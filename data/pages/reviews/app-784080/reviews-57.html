\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game with good bones.

Steam shows 0.3 but that is only because I bought a second copy. My original copy must have hundreds of hours into it without question.

The team behind it has put in some excellent effort and I have stuck with it from the start. Still awaiting the alleged Clan DLC which was initially supposed to come out "next" but we received the armed combat thing first. They have given players a solid bulwark to build from.

To REALLY get into the game you need to explore the MODS. Once you can edit out and replace the vanilla auto-spawn-wave combat from the base game and have explored the depth of graphics tweaks such as moisture / rain / ice, lighting, sound effects, gameplay enhancements, weps, etc... it adds that extra level of depth and immersion to the worlds and biospheres encountered.

These things all put together, it is an EXCELLENT game. I don't go to the effort to leave comments on Steam, so take this all as high praise. I should also add that my interest in MechWarrior goes wayyyy back to the OG Battletech board game, then the initial Crescent Hawk's Inception and all the myriad games to follow with exception of Solaris at which point I dove in on MW5 when it released.

That said, as the game ages and the devs push out DLCs, fewer and fewer of what I consider to be the core 'required' MODS survive or are updated to function with the current build version the number of involved modders and players for the game fall off. So I may own all the DLCs, but I had to wind it all back using Steam to keep it playable in the manner which I prefer. While I've played Rasalhague and CtoA DLCs, I wound it back to Kestrel. And now I guess we have a Dracs DLC coming in later September. Ehhh.... really no interest. I read all the novels back in the day, too, and Dracs were just rigid automatons as soldiers with highly untrustworthy political masters. I'd rather burn the Combine, even if ComStar Precentor Martial Victor Davion did marry the old man's daughter.

I am only guessing that the Clan DLC which was promised will be a separate game altogether. No point milking the rest of these DLCs only to push that out three or four years after release to try to get a few more bucks. Especially when there are several full 3D modeled Clan mechs already available and the weapons have been usable since Day 1.

Re DLCs - it would be more interesting to follow events from the novels and history of the 4th Succession War and the pre-Clan years, Clan invasion itself, and the many ebbs and flows of alliances, battles, and batchalls than the largely peripheral involvement offered by the DLCs. Are the rights to characters from the novels not owned? Kai Allard-Liao, Aidan Pryde.... and an entire game storyline can be written to flesh out the experiences of Jaime and Joshua Wolf as they came to the IS after the Dragoon Compromise in 3004 and can be spanned straight through to the Word of Blake / HPG baloney where the entire franchise basically began to lose all stability and interest among readers and players alike.

Good game, this. Don't let my meandering Battletech droning turn you aside. Snag it, mod it, dive deep. And I really suggest the '5 hours of Relaxing Psychedelic Space Rock' (now partly muted?) on yt play in the background to flesh out the feel of non-mission time in the game. Melded great for me with the game.\t\t\t\t\t
