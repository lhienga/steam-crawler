\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMW5 is a pretty good simulator for mech fighting, but it has some problems with balance. It appears that the missions undergo some sort of auto-difficulty scaling, depending entirely on you merc company's reputation and the make up of your drop. For example, if you're playing a rep 6 mission with an allowed drop weight of 200 tons, your mech company is rep 8, and you decide to drop three heavy mechs totaling 200 tons. There is a good chance that you will face 600 or 800 tons of opposition and enemies will be spawned or dropped nearly on top of you in the middle of an already difficult battle. This leads to reloading the same mission over and over or save scumming as a method to abandon the mission entirely. If you don't you will face repairs, lost time in repairs, and lost equipment that will eventually bankrupt you.

The economy is fairly well balanced, but in career mode the economy is pretty bad because most of the missions do not pay off enough to make good progress. Campaign mode is much easier in this regard. One of the problems I have with the economy is that your reputation level dictates what weapons or mechs are available on the market for sales. Besides being unrealistic, it ends up causing an unnecessarily slow progression through mech weights and advanced weapons. The other issue is the you have to travel all over the place to find anything good for sale. You can see that a rare mech or weapon is for sale in a nearby system, but you don't know what it is until you get there. When you find out that it isn't worth my spit, you end up save scumming to avoid wasting the time and money spent just to see what it was. It's stupid. I can't understand why the developers can't see how stupid it is.

As a sim this game is fun, but many of the mission types are so bad that if I see then in a single or multi-mission operation, I simply don't take it. The worst of these are "beachhead" type missions. These will have artillery constantly bombarding you, requiring you to traverse the entirety of the map to destroy several satellite dish sites and destroy all of the artillery sites, and fight defending mechs, and then fight a huge onslaught while trying to kill all enemies too close to a blue circled area. It's a mission type that will cause you to frequently restart the mission because one or more of your AI lance mates will be shot up to scrap by the time you get to the final onslaught. It's also a ridiculously long mission type that with any restarts, you may spend over an hour trying to complete.

The Lance mate AI is generally bad, you have to constantly babysit them, ordering them, placing them, etc or they will happily sit in a mosh pit of fire taking enormous damage. Some missions you are so out-weighed by enemy tonnage that you have to restart then carefully place your lance in a position away from triggering the enemy, then you carefully trigger the enemy and back up towards your defense line. They will attack the enemy, but you can't order them to attack anything specific because there isn't a way to order them to fire in place, they will move toward the enemy if you order them them to attack a target. They will end up shot to pieces because there are just too many enemies that will pour out or arrive via drop ship at the same time. This situation doesn't happen all of the time, but it is irritating when it does.

Regarding the missions, they are incredibly repetitive. The defense or garrison missions seem to be the easiest, although the rival merc company DLC function that can drop right at the start of a mission can make these missions go sideways fast. The rival merc company drops sometimes help you, sometime hurt you. When they help, they mostly just dilute what the enemy attacks, so you take less damage. When they hurt, you're fighting a bunch of extra tonnage. In some missions it doesn't do all that much damage, maybe you take them out fast before engaging the actual mission. But there are some missions where they drop on you at the same time as the enemy spawns on you, and you end up save scumming your way out of even taking the mission.

In the end, MW5 is a good mech simulator with an under-developed set of lance mate controls, mediocre economy, very repetitive and frequently frustrating mission system. The campaign is OK, there are choke point missions that are a big challenge. The career mode isn't really fun and most of the DLC (I have all of them) don't end up adding anything that makes the game feel especially better; rather they add more mech types and a few more scripted operations (merely sets of existing mission types) with new voiced dialog).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are a fan of Mechwarrior / Battletech this is an ok game and worth it as long as it's on sale. I've played most of the mech games down the years and this follows the same template. There is a story arc to run through if you are new to mechs and fortunately a "sandbox" where you can just run a company without being railroaded.
The downsides are poor AI for enemies and you unit , missions that can be a bit too similar. Tbh I think a lot more could have been done with this game, apart from the mission generator sandbox mode there is not that much progress from mechwarrior 4, and that was a few years ago! If I'd have bought it when new ,then bought the DLC's etc it would NOT be worth it.
For those like me who also play MWO it's much easier (due to poor AI) and much less frustrating- you don't have a light with 10-20 sm lasers and mg's taking you back out in 2 seconds...
I have found the game very stable ,amazingly no crashes yet . Works fine on a Ryzen 5 with a GTX 1660 super. No moneygrabbing pay to win crap in here thank f (as of 12/23 ! )
...Just re read what I'd typed and realised I was a bit underwhelmed with it, it's not bad, just ok, like the company bought the righs then thought "what do we do now?"...

One day, I was so angry with everything spiraling out of control that I punched a concrete wall in a moment of overwhelming emotion. This resulted in breaking my fifth metacarpal in my right handthe hand I worked with, played games with, and used to carry my children to bedthe hand I desperately needed to ensure I could continue providing.

Upon learning the severity of the self-inflicted damage, I became almost suicidal. Keep in mind that just a few months before this, I was the happiest man, with no history of depression or anxiety. That being said, after doing so, I had a new joy and hope for life. I managed to leave behind the pain and suffering that had been thrust upon me. I could experience the joy and happiness of other people. I relaxed for 5 minutes listening to this music, long enough to realize that I would be okay.

After realizing this, I turned off the game and went back to work. My hand hurt a lot, but I was motivated. I stopped feeling so sorry for myself and became the father I needed to be at that moment, not the weak boy I was behaving like.

Today, I am close friends with the mother of my children. We don't fight, argue, or say hurtful things to each other. We are parents and friends.

Now I have 3 children. My third child is, wait, ALSO 4 YEARS OLD. The woman I am with was going through a very similar situation at the time of my separation, and we just unexpectedly stumbled into each other's lives. We have been dating for a year and are very happy together.

Moral of the story: you never know what life has in store for you, and if I had given up when all odds were against me, I wouldn't be where I am today. This silly little game helped me realize that.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has some of the worst writing, worst story, worst characters and absolutely ridiculously bad voice acting. The stories are repetitive and are just variations of "Walk to objective marker and kill everything there". There are no special set pieces, no truly exciting missions.

Why am I recommending this then? Because I love big mechs and the gameplay loop is so satisfying to me. The negatives are tragic, especially in comparison with the old games, but bearable, as they are mostly just dressing on the side. You dont even need to play the campaign, and maybe youd be better off for it. I was sick for a few days and sunk about 40 hours into this. It made me play Battletech (the video game on the PC, with much better story/setting) again and genuinely made me think about buying some minis for the tabletop. It tickled a part of my brain just perfectly and Ill keep playing.

If youre not sure if you wanna buy this, play Mechwarrior online. If you like the general gameplay loop but dont like the massive mech battles and want more management on the side, then this is it.\t\t\t\t\t
