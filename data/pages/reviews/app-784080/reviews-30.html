\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReal fun, but the salvage option feels very unrewarding and counterproductive.

In Battletech, if you put in the effort to de-leg or depilot an enemy mech (regardless of tonnage) you get rewarded with getting closer to getting that mech for yourself. If you rush and just outright destroy it, then you get a scrap or none at all.

In this game, if you put in the effort to de-leg or depilot a mech, you have a high chance of not getting it because of the insanely high salvage points requirement even if you maxed out the salvage points available for the mission. If you rush to destroy it, you get a small chance of getting it for considerable amounts of salvage points (still depends on tonnage) or get none at all.

Example: In HBS BT, if I encounter a heavy or assault mech in the early game, I could risk my entire lance and pilots (basically the entire playthrough) just to get multiple parts of THAT mech.

In MW5, I could risk my entire lance and pilots to deleg or depilot a heavy/assault mech early game, but I won't even get a lick of it because I lack the salvage points for it (I cap at 25 points while needing 30-40+ points to salvage THAT mech) so I end up with getting crappy equipment like low tier weapons, ammo, heatsinks. Feels very unrewarding.

This means that the only real way to play is always max out cash in contract negotiations unless you like collecting tier 1-2 weapons and single heatsinks.

TL;DR, gameplay is very fun but lacks the high risk-high reward of HBS Battletech.

And it's still a lot better than Armored Core.\t\t\t\t\t
