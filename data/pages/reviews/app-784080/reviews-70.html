\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo cut to the chase, it's a fun game, but it has a lot of flaws. It's immersive, the mech building system is nice, and there are plenty of mechs and their unique variants to play with. Definitely scratches the mechwarrior itch, but is not an amazing game.

PROS
-Mechs look good and feel good to pilot
-Destructible environment that actually does allow you to be strategic about how you position your lance and fight around it.
-Weapons are awesome, and better balanced than MWO online.
-Friendly AI is dumb, but not dumb enough to be useless. High level AI pilots can handle their own without your supervision.
-The grind is not as bad as MWO. Didn't take me 3 months of gaming to finally have an Atlas in my mechbay.

CONS
-Gameplay is painfully repetitious.
-Balance is terrible, and late game stages may have you pulling your hair out as you battle 3 lances of assault mechs, an entire company of tanks, supporting aircraft, perimeter defenses, and artillery..... with your single 4 man lance. No way to add an extra lance of your own goons to even the odds. Enemy AI is stupid and can be baited into losing situations, but it's time consuming and boring.
-Bugs...........game is a bit wonky.

Overall, definitely worth getting if it's on sale. If it's not on sale and you're a MW fan, then it's worth it. Otherwise, if you have the patience, MWO is better overall in terms of gameplay.
