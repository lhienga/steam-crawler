\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis seems to be decent sequel to the previous Mechwarrior games. The mods make the game and worth buying on Steam versus the other stores (unfortunately). I didn't play the campaign beyond the first couple of hours and went to career which is relatively freeform. The mission types appear to be very unbalanced with garrison being a walk in the park and target kill contracts on the opposite end of the spectrum. The AI is awful and relies on numbers. Naturally this applies to your own AI as well, they don't appear to have any idea of tactics or heat management or strong personality, it's like leading a squad of dogs that follow you around. Overall, it's grindy, repetitive but if you love the Battletech universe then this is somewhat acceptable and getting to experience the universe in a game is pretty great. It's certainly more satisfying than the HBS entry and I'll pick up the DLCs over time.

If you don't love Battletech then I wouldn't recommend the game because it's grindy, repetitive and opaque.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe ai is bad. Enemy responses feel scripted. Enemies spawn in at the same spot at specific points in the mission. You cannot kill enemies in the order you want. You have to kill them the way the developer intended when the mission was designed. Because of this the game feels more like it is on rails then you are on the field of battle firing and maneuvering.

The graphics are ok for the time period but it only implemented ray tracing for Nvidia cards. AMD and Intel both support ray tracing but nothing from the developers to add it to the game.

I gave the game a thumbs down because of these short comings but it is still a fun game. It is nice to walk through the drop ship in between missions and see the state of the mechs and not just view menus. If you enjoyed the old school Mech Warrior games and you can get it on sale it's a fun game.\t\t\t\t\t
