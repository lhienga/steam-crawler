\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter nearly 40 hours played, but still having only scratched the surface of this game, I heartily recommend it for those who enjoy the premise, which is as follows:

The year is around 3015. You play as a mercenary commander of a Mech Warrior unit, with your own space ship capable of traversing between the stars and maintaining your group of Mechs. You travel from system to system and accomplish contracts on planets for various factions, such as being told to destroy buildings or assassinate a specific Mech Warrior or so on. With these funds and with the salvage you gain from those you defeat, you can build and upgrade your Mechs and increasingly customize your combat potential.

Combat in the game is weighty, with you independently controlling the movement and aiming of your mech. There are a wide variety of mechs and weapons to be found, and the trade-offs and tactical differences between them are meaningful. For example - you can build a mech to use Long Range Missiles very heavily, which is great at extreme distances, but these missiles have a minimum range. Or you could use Short Range Missiles, but risk being outranged and building up obscene amounts of heat during combat. During combat you must balance your damage dealt to enemy mechs - each with specific components on them that can be targeted and destroyed (including your own) - with your heat generated, while leading yourself and the other ~3 mechs in your Lance to victory.

Mechs of course can be customized over time. Each component you find throughout missions and stores in various zones will have various strengths and weaknesses, and fit into specific slots. Energy weapons for example are split into things like Lasers (Small, Medium, Large, B Lasers, etc) and PPCs (Lightning Guns), while ballistic weapons and missile systems also have a good amount of variety. Certain mechs have certain hard points and weight capacities for weapons, and you can min-max builds quite a bit within a mech. For example - you might decide that your Centurion mech is too lacking in armor protection, so swap out its LRM-10 (Long Range Missile with 10 missiles/volley) for an LRM-5, then put the extra 3 tons of saved weight into armor. Or you could go for a heavier laser build and put on some more heat sinks to cope with the change.

The game has working co-op, and a good selection of mods (which I use very heavily already). Most of the better mods are on Nexus Mods (Steam doesn't have them all), and I recommend personally the YAML (Yet Another Mech Lab) mods, TTRulezAI, and VonBiomes. I have a lot of other mods running as well, but these mods in particular add by far the most additional content and meaningful changes to the behavior of the game. While I did play 10-20 hours with the game unmodded before jumping into modding it, I highly recommend running it with mods to maximize your content available.

All in all, it's a good game. I recommend getting the DLC cheaply - it adds good content, but isn't necessarily worth the full price unless you love the base game extremely so.

The game is not without its flaws of course. The enemy and friendly AI can be a bit limited at times, and the mission selection and such is sometimes a bit limited, but the story and campaign missions are great and with the right mods all issues are more or less minor in comparison. In vanilla they are a bit more glaring of issues, but can still be worked around.\t\t\t\t\t
