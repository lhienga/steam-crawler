\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first mainline Mechwarrior game in nearly 20 years, but is it good?
Short answer is , yes, but it's frustratingly close to greatness.


Mechwarrior 5 presents itself similarly to its predecessors. You are in your own personal big stompy robot, and your goal is to wreck other people's big stomp robots over the course of a campaign. Additionally, this is the first time in the series for there to be 4-player co-op.



In this game, managing your mechs is just as important as actually piloting them into battle. Bringing the right weapons for the job is very important, but making sure that those weapons stay intact to the end is even more so. Battlemechs aren't cheap. and every bit of damage that you takes will come out of your (in-game) pockets. Mechwarrior 5 is very good at constantly keeping your expenses in the back of your head, and in turn this makes dealing with even fodder enemies very interesting.



Like other games in the series, this one comes with a campaign, but unlike the others it isn't the star of the show.
'Career' mode is the meat and potatoes of MW5. It's essentially a sandbox mode, where you can travel the galaxy and accept procedurally generated missions as you slowly accumulate your arsenal. This is loads of fun, but reveals a lot of glaring issues with MW5 the more you play it.



It's at this point in the review where I'm now going to separate the vanilla experience of MW5 from the modded experience.
In general, the vanilla MW5 experience feels extremely underbaked for the following reasons:
There aren't many mech types.
You're very limited in customizing each mech. Basically only allowed to change weapons.
Every single mission feels like it's on the same map.
There's only like 5 mission types, and some are just better than others.
The AI is so terrible that it is straight up immersion-breaking.


Mods, friends, and DLC fix basically every single one of these issues. This turns the game from passable to amazing.


The YAML (Yet Another Mechlab) mods introduce a wide variety of new mechs, and mech variants.
YAML also unlocks the customization and gives you full creative freedom over your creations.
VonBiomes mod injects an absolute crapload of map variation that is sorely needed. (DLC does this too)
VonBiomes, DLC, and CoyoteMissionPack add significantly more mission variety.
And last but not least, playing co-op or using TTRulez_AIMod2 will give your teammates a brain!



In short: If you want a fun, slow-burn game to play with friends (or solo), and building/piloting giant robots sounds fun, give it a go.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR ( my personal summary ):
New players: ( start with MW4 Vengeance/mercenaries then go to MW5)

veteran MW/Battletech/mech warrior online players: This game is decent. It could be a lot better, but its worth playing if you have the same mech addiction that i have.

if you are new to MW, then this game will be a bit of a learning curve.. for both game play and 'story'. I would honestly recommend finding a MW 4 executable and playing that first to get a feeling for what the game is all about. MW 5 is good, as you can see i have sunk a lot of time into it. But the game drops you into the thick of things with very little world information.. which will turn a lot of people off IMO. MW 4 has a much more stream lined story and choice selection, which will land you more into the feeling of the universe and the actions you make. If you say **** that and go into MW5 anyways, strap yourself in and prepare for darksouls.

Veterans, you know what you're getting into; Big shooty pew pews, an attempt to keep the universe accurate, and lots of odd bugs. Your Ai teammates are as competent as a lance of drunken squirrels. But, that's all part of the charm isn't it? Once you get that itch for MW, it always comes back.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA little part of me has died when I decided to write a review to not recommend this game. It is like that old joke, "What do air, adult relations, and battletech have in common? None of them are important until you're not getting any."

The base game is meh. Toss in some of the Yet Another Mech lab mods, and this thing becomes interstellar!

However, there are a couple bugs that has been going on for quite a while now. I didn't start experiencing it until about 2 years ago. Basically, enemies either fall through the map, or spawn under it. When that happens you have to completely redo the mission and hope it doesn't happen again on the same mission. You simply cannot complete a mission when this happens unless you abandon the mission taking the hit to your in game wallet and reputation.

The other bug is when an NPC dropship comes in to drop off more mechs, but the mechs don't drop and hitch a ride in the dropship for a while. Now, normally I could live with this as it is like shooting trap when they finally fall out. However, once in a while it drops the NPCs outside of the map boundaries. If it is an enemy you need to kill to complete a mission, well, you're reproductive acted.

These bugs have been going on for years. I've looked a couple times and not found a fix. Battletech is one of my favorite universes. I've played the vast majority of PC games, going back to AOL Mechwarrior online, and the Mechwarrior(The original 1989 one, not the 1993 reboot, although, I played that one as well).

If you can live with these bugs, more power to you. But, it is ridiculous that games are released in states like this. It is long past time game companies should be doing better. Vote for games with your money.\t\t\t\t\t
