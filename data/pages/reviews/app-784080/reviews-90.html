That's just sad, and really makes me want to change my review to a thumbs down.

I think at this stage the best to hope for is the next game does better.

....


Everything about this game screams 1980s / 1990s game-loop. It is grindy-grindy mission after mission, with a classic contract, starmap, and mech purchase/layout interface. The old-school vibe doesn't sound amazing as described, but it's part of the charm, especially for folks like me who have been playing Mechwarrior since the first one.

The graphics aren't exactly fully modern quality either, but the core stomp and shoot is really fun. Even better is the fact that you can play any mission in co-op with friends.

From what I can tell, though the mod support may have slowed some, it's still present and sustaining the life of this title some years on. I have just scratched the surface here but look forward to exploring more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t--If it's on a discount, you might as well just buy everything. Otherwise, at least get the Heroes of the Inner Sphere DLC for career mode. Rasalhague adds other mercenary companies. Call to Arms adds a lot of melee weapons (which, speaking as a newcomer to Battletech, are surprisingly lethal).

--Without mods, it's serviceable. Stomping and shooting in the vanilla game is fun, but mech customization is pretty shallow compared to what you can do with just the Mechlab mod on the workshop, and the mission variety can feel a bit stale after a while if you aren't actively planning and testing out builds. A full-price purchase is not recommended if you do not intend to mod the game.

--With mods, it's one of the best giant robot games on the market, especially if you're into PvE content. Other than Mechlab, I personally recommend 'vonBiomes' on Steam Workshop and 'Coyote's Mission Mod' on Nexus. Those three mods alone turn the game into a sandbox where even the lightest mechs can be turned into monstrosities of engineering with enough C-bills, some lucky salvage, and a little bit of creativity.

--For the love of god, and the sake of your own sanity, download the the 'jumpship animation skip' mod. I didn't think it would bother me until the first time I checked every stop in an industrial hub.

--Modded co-op with friends is possible, but not flawless. I strongly advise running similar mod lists to minimize jank. Only the host can use the starmap, initiate and negotiate contracts, view the shop and mech market, and skip forward on the timeline. Story/Career progression is saved only for the host. Jumping to a different system will cancel all changes in progress if one of the other players is customizing a mech.

--Make sure to keep your weapon groups simple for any mech you're giving to friendly AI.\t\t\t\t\t
