\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a "yes" based entirely upon the fact that I downloaded a mod to decrease the damage you and your lance takes in missions. I fully understand that this puts a different skew on my words - I don't care.

I really like this game. At first, I was just frustrated terribly. I kept getting destroyed so quickly, I wasn't able to really get "invested" into any mech because they'd just explode immediately. I couldn't do any better than breaking even. So...I tried to decrease difficulty, but there literally is no option for that in the game. So here I was, stuck trying to enjoy this game, but totally unable to.

All I wanted was to enter the game a bit more casually. I already have to learn the systems, how to outfit a mech, why one choice is better or different than another...I don't want to also be demoralized the entire time by just losing again and again - never knowing if it's because of my skill, or the loadout choices I made, or something else about the game I didn't understand yet.

More than likely I'll remove the mod after a while - once I know what the heck is going on. But if it weren't for that mod - I would recommend strongly AGAINST anyone buying this game unless they are a huge fan of steep difficulty curves. With the mod, I strongly recommend in FAVOR of getting the game - it's really cool.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only have a small bit of time into this game and haven't tried co-op yet, but so far, this game is nicely done. Visuals are modern, but the ambition in the gameplay and strategy, as well as the dialogue quality, remind me of an early 2000's game which, to me, is good.There's a good bit of depth here. It's not just "drop in and wreck everything." There's hiring pilots, customizing loadouts for your 'Mechs, knowing which 'Mech to take out into the mission, negotiating payouts, salvaging parts for reuse later, gaining reputation, etc.One of my gripes (if you can call it that) is that it's very easy to run out of money in the game. I appreciate this difficulty and have multiple saves because of it, but it's something to keep in mind. I've had to reload from the same point twice now and one of the times was because I spent every credit fixing my 'Mechs only to lose them in the next mission. The other time was because the final enemy I needed to take out was stuck below the map.I definitely recommend getting this game during a sale. It might be a bit harder to recommend at normal price due to the difficulty.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave to say - the meat of it is great, and core loop is even greater. It feels great to pilot the mech, and controls are perfected to the top. Its really fun to tweak your mech, to get the best performance for a specific tonnage, create your lance with differing strengths - and its even more fun to use simple commands during deployment to command your lance and utilize every mechs' strengths and weaknesses.Some things, I wish are a bit better. For example , you have the ability to walk around your ship - but literally no reason to do so. There are terminals, sure - but each and every terminal opens the unique interface that lets you access everything, and furthermore - hit Tab and you get the same thing.You have this real cool on-foot mechanic that is completely unused; it'd be pretty cool to be able to exit your mech and go recon on foot or something, maybe enter a building and shoot some people. A game from like 2000, called G-Nome let you do this, and you could snipe enemy mech pilots to gain access to their mechs.I don't fully understand how to get better weapons either, I hope its not tied to my rank but to a place in galaxy. If its tied to my rank, I'm gonna be pretty disappointed.Finally, the command system is a bit too simplistic. There's this yet another old game "Heavy gear 2", where upon hitting tab you had access to a full RTS-like interface, and could prep some really cool plans of attack. What you have here is basically - attack this, go there, follow me.Grind all this down, and what you get is not quite a war simulation with giant robots - which is what I was hoping for - but almost an FPS shooter. Sure there's some tactics you need to employ when fighting... distance, which side you turn towards the enemy, which weakpoint to attack.... but ... but.... I keep slaying about 5-7 mechs by myself, my lance steals my kills on additional 2-3, and I end up barely with orange damage on armour.It doesn't really feel like I earned those hits because of my spectacular mech driving skills, or because I am an amazing lance commander... instead, all those wins feel cheap and easy (just like all of my exes).... so yeah... I'd say, if you want some great action and 10-15 minute combat - go for it. If you're looking for a simulation, nah, not really the thing.\t\t\t\t\t

I used to play old school MechWarrior games when I was a kid, but stopped after MechWarrior 3 came out in the early 2000's. Picked this up partially because of nostalgia, and partially because it was on sale, and I haven't been disappointed in the least bit. It's lots of fun to see your pilots level up their skills, find/loot/buy more advanced weapons and parts to put on your mechs, and to even target enemy mechs in a way that allows you to take them out but recover and fix them up later and add them to your arsenal.There's a mech for any playstyle/situation, and with them being modifiable you can tweak a lot of mechs to your particular liking. On top of that you have all the fun of running a mercenary company that includes juggling finances, contracts, pilots, weapons, ect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really did try to enjoy this game several times over, however there are too many grating gameplay bugs or design choices made by the base developers to make it playable for a long time. If my brief look into the scene is correct, modding is a hellscape of mod conflicts and hackily subverted hardcoded behavior patched together by compatability scripts, very particular load orders, and prayers. Even with all of that, and after all this time of being released, there are STILL singleplayer campaign level issues. Don't even mention Co-op, which has long-standing, unaddressed bugs in base game that only seem to get worse when modding is introduced. Lets just talk about a few things that really stick out to me:

Story: Ill be real nobody really cares. The story is a convenient gateway to throw you in charge of your own mercanary company and the only thing that it drives are specific scripted missions with some predefined stuff thrown in for a curve ball or a challenge. I didn't really care why Inferno was terrorizing effectively nameless planets and people because it virtually all happens off screen anyways. I promptly went to go find and finance my own adventures which thankfully the game lets you do.

Combat: I think the mech part of the combat is pretty solid for the most part, but the base game has some annoying flaws that keep bleeding their way up front. Once you graduate out of lighter mechs, there is virtually absolutely no reason you should be using them. Bigger mech = more armor = more guns = more staying power = you won't die as easily because you have to take out 20 other mechs each mission, not to mention the countless number of tanks, turrets, and aircraft that beset you every waking moment. That being said, I still think combat is "okay" and fairly satisfying vanilla-wise, but they don't seem to have a really great variety of weapons beyond "laser" and "missile launcher" and "cannon", and besides the latter two needing ammo there isn't a whole lot different about them.

Maps and Missions: They had a good... base to work with for this, but the fact of the matter is after about 10-20 procedural missions you are going to notice some similarities and that's way too soon to be noticing that your missions are copy/paste cut and staple. You get a
  • Mission type (Assassination, Raid, Demolition, Defense, Warzone, or Multi Mission (2+ missions must be done consecutively)
  • Biome (Frozen, Desert, Temperate, Rocky, Volcanic, off the top of my head)
  • Map Layout (there are perhaps 5-6 unique terrain layouts)
That's it. Pick one thing from each of those three bullet points and you have your "unique mission". Of course, enemy types and structures are randomized, but structures are clearly just prefabs slapped down and enemy types are not diverse enough to be interesting (tanks, helicopters, turrets, mechs, that's it, they all shoot you when they see you).

And here is the other thing, sensor range is heavily gimped to 1km. Your super advanced mech cannot detect a radar signature of a helicopter or 60ft tall mech outside of 1km at all. If the line of sight is broken by a single leaf on a tree branch, you will entirely lose that contact. The game knows this is a problem, so they compensate by periodically revealing enemies to your sensors at random intervals further enhancing the experience of your sensors being schizophrenic.
Enemies like turrets conveniently pop out of the floor whenever you get remotely near them and promptly shoot you before even registering on your sensors. There are quite literally turrets littered everywhere, you cannot step more than 50 meters without another turret popping up and trying to snipe your cockpit or weapon arm. Mission types are extremely lackluster. Raid and Demolition just make you blow up specific targets or a whole area, but they somehow manage to make that boring. Defense is just sitting around a base and whacking the trickle of enemies that come through. Warzone is defense except you can shoot as much as you want before leaving. Assassination is just walking around until you find a slightly beefy mech to blow up and leaving. Beachhead added by the DLC doesn't even seem new, it looks like two or more mission types haphazardly mashed into one. Campaign missions can be somewhat unique but still often follow this formula of "go here and blow up thing then leave".

AI: Simply put: both friendly and enemy AI is absolutely abhorrent. Even overhaul modifications can only do so much to the base AI which is so incredibly simplistic and absent-minded it might as well just be called "A" instead of "AI". Mechs want to fight at their "optimal range" and disregard the situation as a whole in order to do that. They walk in a straight line then sloppily try to strafe around their target while firing to deal damage. If they have jump jets, they jump jet at random in a straight line and begin anew. Your friendly pilots will often trash the mechs you give them as they have little sense of self-preservation or tactics. The only reason enemy AI still poses a problem is because you can only bring a lance of four mechs with you and you will inevitably end up fighting 12-20+ enemy mechs every mission in addition to 40+ turrets and ground/air vehicles. There's no way to fix damage mid-combat, so its just an attrition game. Except the AI is very good at hitting your cockpit or critical weapons systems no matter the range or situation or peek time, so you will have significant problems not taking damage and preserving yourself.

Modding and Co-Op: Overhaul mods do their best to fix the base game's horrendous shortcomings but they can only do so much on top of terrible systems to begin with. They can adjust, but ultimately cannot directly fix, base game issues and mechanics. Mods are very particular, hard to get working, have little official mod management support, and it took me the better part of several hours to revert the game's version, install the correct version of mods from [vomit] nexus mods, and get it to work without mechs being invisible and killing me while I am unable to fight back (that was fun to debug).
Co-Op works at the barest level but has significant issues that significantly impact the ability to play. Jump jets don't work correctly for coop players. Desynchronized targets. Coop players must assume the roles of one of your AI pilots. Coop players can't see the travel screen among other things. Just a laundry list of tiny issues that add up to frustration. Modding generally makes these issues many times worse either due to base game issues being magnified or modders unable to control or decipher why coop is so bad.Avoid.\t\t\t\t\t
