\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanna point out to anyone that might have low patience (like me) that the game takes a little while to really ramp up. Starting off feels frustratingly slow with the cooldown time on fire rates, and getting accustomed to the awkward controls (no sidestepping, turning is slow as F***, takes awhile to adjust to legs walking in one direction while torso is moving and aiming in another). BUT, once you start filling up your team and going in with 4 pilots / 4 mechs, and unlocking different types of mechs (also rare/hero versions that come with special bonuses) the game starts to ramp up very quickly, and finding a style that fits your play comes much faster. I would say that for the first 4 or 5 hours I was really not enjoying myself at all and debating asking for a refund, but since i missed the 1 or 2 hour mark that is standard for a refund, I figured I might as well force myself to get my money's worth out of it. I'm legitimately glad that I did because the game started to fill out more and I started enjoying it more, to the point that I'm upset that I don't have more spare time to invest in it. If you catch it on sale, its absolutely worth your money. I recommend giving it a chance, but go into knowing that starting off is slow, some missions you may choose to repeat a few times simply due to taking too much damage (costing you credits and time to repair), and that the fastest way to stockpile money is to just spam some lower level missions instead of pushing for higher level content faster. It has alot in common with a standard grindy RPG, except that getting your ass beat can bankrupt you if you're not careful.\t\t\t\t\t
