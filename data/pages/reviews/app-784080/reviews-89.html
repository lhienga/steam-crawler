\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros: The game is very good. It feels very similar to MWO gameplay-wise. But aiming is somewhat different, felt weird at the beginning. Probably because Torso turn speed is limited. There's enough options with hardpoints to use most mechs the way you want to and to make them fun or interesting, but its more limited compared to MWO. All weapons have space and purpose, wich is very cool. LRMs are very usefull for dealing with lighter vehicles and machine guns are very effective at close range, SRMs are 10x as effective in close range as MWOs missiles. And the gameplay loop of managing your Mechs of looking for mechs, managing money, time, etc is very addictive.

Cons:
AI : Mostly the AI sucks the fun out of the game after a while. They get stuck in terrain, can't pathfind, walk in circles after you command them to move, or just take a long ass time to move. The more obstacles in the way, the worse it is. Changing mechs might get them to completely stop responding, they won't shoot or move. They are bad with LRMs, also sometimes they don't shoot after a focus fire command, and you see them walking in circles, looking backwars and stuff. This makes it so that, most of the time, you'll be the target. Since they sometimes miss, you'll be the one dealing most of the damage and getting aggroed. You'll need to switch mechs to avoid taking too much damage and risk getting a bot unresponsive. Also, when you switch, they sometimes lock you from rotating your legs, so you have to switch all the way back to "fix" it.

Enemy spawns: Having a dropship drop on top of you is so cool. It's beyond me why they make mechs and vehiles spawn out of thin air. If you're playing with friends, it gets pretty easy to pick them off on certain missiong, since they might spawn 1-2 at a time and run 1+km to get to you, while you have the target at any time and can rain LRMs or hit them with long range weapons. Paired with the bad AI, sometimes they might spawn far away and not come close to the objective, and when you get close to them they just run away, wich is very frustrating.

Mods: how TF can a company make so much money, be able to hire so many people to make a game, and still have modders come up, sometimes single handedly, with a game changing solution to design flaws and bugs. From functional stuff like faster projectiles, being able to purchase salvage, removing the extreme screen shake from cannons, to stuff like adding mech variants and unlocking hardpoints, engines. Mods made the game 10 times better and less frustrating. But, unfortunately, they themselves can introduce more bugs, sometimes game breaking bugs, or just break after an update.

Performance: its . I have an RTX 3070 with a gen 9 i5, and with DLSS on (native 1440p) i have drops to low 40s and 50s on certain maps or areas of a map. Lowering everything to low doesnt make a difference to me. The fact that MWO performs worse as a 10+ year game tells me something about the developers ability to make games perform good.

For as much fun as i've had with this, for as long as i played i've had many frustrating moments due to what i belive is a general lack of competence, and for that i won't recommend it. Not because its not a good game, if you like MWO you're very likely to stick around, but constantly dealing with those flaws took a look of the enjoyment out of it. This is more of a message for the developers to get their together.\t\t\t\t\t
