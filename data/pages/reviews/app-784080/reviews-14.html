\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: it's sooo boring

Context: I loved Mech Warrior 3 and have always been wanting a mondern iteration of that game, a single-player mech game with an interesting premise.
I played the base game without any DLC, and only in single player.

The good: it's a mech warrior game, weapons, mechs etc. it's all there. Destructability of the surroundings is nice. Graphics are nice. There are 300+ mechs, from what I can tell.

The bad (IMHO):
- The combat is boring. I don't know what I expected but it is, from my perception, a very arcady game. It feels like a clunky first-person shooter. Everything moves fast: the mechs, weapon reloading, projectile speeds. This eliminates a lot of the strategy. Just aim for the legs - killing the legs kills the mech - and do it to every mech all of the time. Shoot the legs from behind to make it faster. Advanced tactics, e.g. the mech equivalent to "hull down" positions or the like might work but are unnecessary. That also means that the different weapons don't really make much difference.
- Mech customization: it's rather limited. MechWarrior 3 was much better than this. I get that you can't bolt any weapon onto every position on every mech, but honestly, what's the point then? I also don't think that there is good weapon balance. Bigger gun > smaller gun. You might argue that smaller gun means more armor, but with respawning enemies and the way combat works, the optimal way against the AI is: sweep the leg, the faster the better. In MechWarrior 3, the tolerances were much tighter, different options were far more viable.
- Mission design: horrible, very very simplistic. You start the mission and go to check points. There you will engage waves of enemies, or you have several waypoints and there is a wave at each one. The other main issue with the missions is that there is no rhyme or reason to these waves. These are not tactical formations cooperating. It's a couple of mechs and vehicles spawning in some distance away and running towards you. That leads to your lance engaging individual oncoming enemies. You focus fire on one mech, then the next. To me it felt more like whack-a-mole than a tactical situation. Also, the majority of missions feature endlessly spawning waves and you have to get back to the landing ship. Gets old really fast.
- Campaign: I've played 13 hours and was bored 5 hours ago. Maybe more. You have this set of 4 mission types, e.g. assassination, demolition, etc. but the different missions do not have any impact on how you play the mission or how you prepare for it. There is very little meaningful decision-making. And it's not just limited to mission planning either, e.g.: are you going to repair your mech? No, game, I'll walk out there on foot next time. Where do I want to go? Over there looks nice. What's that, that's 3 levels above my current reputation level? I like a challenge! *20 minutes later* Ok, so that's what the levels mean: getting assaulted by waves of mechs that are 50% stronger than yours...and infinitely spawning...great.
Once you hit level 6 reputation, advancing takes forever, and that is tied to the story. So you play the same boring missions with the same boring combat over and over and over.
- Story: you are the child of the head of a merc company. Another merc company attacks and kills your dad - who gave you the tutorial which seems to mean I should care. I don't. Now we are on our way to get revenge for them killing your dad. The fact that I have been killing lots of dads and moms during my game doesn't seem to trigger any kind of self-reflection on the part of the protagonist and the sleeping pill that is his second in command. It's a very, very cliched story, and what's worse: it's done in a very simplistic, unengaging way. E.g. I met the dad person in the tutorial and then he died. It's probable that there is more to the story, but after 13 hours that's all I got...and all I ever will. And it didn't need to be that way. You could have had a hundred different stories. And perhaps there is better stuff in the DLC.
But just to give some context: in MechWarrior 3 - love that game - you are some grunt in an army lance on a counter-strike mission in the outer reaches far away from home. Your transport gets shot down and you and your support group (mechanics, mission command, 3 maintenance vehicles) are the only survivors and you are now in enemy territory and have to find a way off the planet. You are cut off from supply and can only get stuff recycled from killed opponents. That creates such a unique atmosphere.
In MechWarrior 5, you are working a scrap metal business.

In summary, I wanted to like this game, paid money for it too. But the combat is too simplistic, story is irrelevant, and most of all: the mission and campaign design offers very, very few meaningful choices. As a single-player game, the game loop is unenjoyable.
I was bored after 6-7 hours and thought: I'll at least finish it to get my money's worth but it's honestly just throwing bad money after good.
I hope one day we get a more interesting and tactically nuanced successor and who knows, perhaps even an engaging story. Looking at you, Mech Warrior 3 <3\t\t\t\t\t
