\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think I've felt so conflicted about a game before. I grew up playing Mechwarrior 4: Vengence and Mercenaries and never enjoyed Mechwarrior Online.

The good:
The base gameplay, blowing stuff up in while piloting a big stompy mech, is _very_ fun.

The bad:
A lot of game design decisions leave me baffled:
  • mission tonnage limits,
  • repetitive and useless traveling animations,
  • obtrusive subtitles and overly talkative allies and enemies,
  • no assisted zoom mechanic,
  • the arm-aim breaks away from the torso-aim at any speed,
  • poor salvage mechanics,
  • bad path-finding algorithms for AI in battles as well as in the starmap,
  • bad AI (enemy mechs only YOLO charge or stand around in the open, friendly mechs hardly shoot or miss most of the time),
  • exceedingly limited information gathering range in the starmap,
  • ridiculous amounts of screen-shake from nearby orbital cannons firing,
  • poor performance (low fps and responsiveness)
Like MWO but not as badly, the mechs feels like they are skating around the environment.
The movement animations often don't match the mech speed.
The stomp-speed in the cockpit does not match the stomp-speed in third-person.
The voice acting in the base game is wanting, but good in the DLCs.
The writing in the base game is atrocious.
  • The story feels like an initial draft.
  • There is no sense of urgency or danger when friendlies and enemies talk while trying to kill each other.
  • Ridiculous and annoying dialogue.
The writing and sound design are significantly worse than the 2017 gameplay preview.
Several of the mission environments look horrible, most of them from DLCs.

Overall, I recommend the game, as the base gameplay is very fun, but there are a lot of little things that detract from the experience for me. It is nice, however, that the game has mod support as almost all of the baffling design decisions can be fixed with mods. I'd suggest picking it up on sale if you're at all interested in the genre or setting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou've read the good reviews. They are most likely true (Graphics, Mech customization, Gameplay loop).However, you MUST do this 1 thing or else your gameplay will be ruined.

Max reputation with 1 faction above all else or end up financially ruined.

All the game revolves as if you were getting paid with a high tier salary with an allied great house. You will get fees left and right for everything you do (Traveling, repair costs, maintenance fees) and no matter how hard you play/work, you'll end up in the negative. Insurance costs only covers partial mech destruction, not complete blow ups. If you barely complete a mission with 1 out of 4 mech standing, you might often end up losing more money, even if you invested highly in insurance.
But you can salvage enemy mechs! Isn't that worth getting money? Nope. You have to pay for the parts that aren't destroyed. So even if you had a clean kill and ejected the pilot, you still have to pay "full" price for the mech, which is often worth more than the missions at lower levels. So your ingame skill cannot circumvent the botched economy and an example on how the game actively punishes you by causing a hefty fee out of everything you do.
This game has some good game designs (Mech collecting, Merc contracts, Mech hunting for parts) but it all depends on if the player is already rich. That means the upcoming updates are mainly for players who are already bankrolling in money leaving the new players with a high barrier of entry.
Of course, there are core mods to fix (but they still have in mind with you being a veteran player). Once you've faced a wall and gotten a taste on how some of their ideas are badly implemented, you'll have a good idea on what to tweak using mods.

Don't get me started about the other issues (No reliable ingame tooltip (tons of stats/jargons) ,High barrier of entry, Jargons only recognizable if you've played a previous mechwarrior game, AI unreliable for 4+ years,etc.). Unless you're a devoted Mecha fan or have played previous Mechwarrior games, this game will turn sour once you've played a couple of runs and end up penniless.\t\t\t\t\t
