\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game leaves a lot to be desired. While it provides the core experience of piloting a giant mecha and smashing things to pieces the title falls short in several places.

The core gameplay is satisfying. Walking through a building and watching it collapse feels good and always works the way it should. Firing the weapons and watching them impact enemy mechs tickles the brain. A large enough variety of mechs are featured that you'll always be able to have fun in a quick skirmish or while you're hunting around the Inner Sphere for that coveted mech to add to your lance. The maps in this game are procedurally generated. While this is obvious the first time you see a square little city dropped in the middle of a wasteland, it gets the job done. The variety of mission types is acceptable enough to hold your interest. This game admittedly lacks a lot of the gameplay choices Mechwarrior 4 had throughout their missions leading to a lot of the contracts feeling shallow and repetitive.

A lot of the downsides of this game revolve around the poor AI of your lancemates and a boatload of cut or unimplemented features to support this game releasing on consoles. Advanced zoom, a staple from MW4 is not present, Infantry enemies were cut to support performance, mechs do not have separate walk or run animations, third person locomotion does not sync with first person cockpit bob, mechs do not have inverse kinematics to ensure their feet line up with the terrain.
It's very frustrating how little control you seem to have over your AI lancemates. You cannot tell them to fire from a position, go to a position if you do not have line of site with it, prioritize destroying buildings (unfortunate as several mission types revolve around this), prioritize a type of unit such as battlemechs, VTOLs, or tanks etc. Ultimately, you're left to do almost everything yourself while these idiot bots snipe at the enemy.

This game is saved mostly by co-op and mods. Co-op play is a blast and I've put almost 100% of my gameplay time into this with at least one friend. Several mods either fix or restore features that should have been in the base game. TT_Rulez in particular makes the AI much smarter.

While I ultimately would recommend the game if you're a fan of the Mechwarrior series or the Battletech universe, a lot of mech chassis and the entire career gameplay mode is locked behind the Heroes of the Inner Sphere DLC. While I would describe the other three DLCs of MW5 as optional, Heroes of the Inner Sphere feels essential to the core gameplay experience which is very unfortunate as it almost doubles the price of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

Im an old Mechwarrior, MW2, Battletech player from the 1990s. I played those games for 1000s of hours and fell in love with the MW fiction as well as some board games.

I understand folks in the MW5 community *heavily* mod their clients to make it look and play better, but the sheer number of those mods... Ill get to that some other time.

"Out of the box", the graphics on this seem dull and lackluster. Even with RTX enabled and everything cranked up, it looks like a 10+ year old game, and it plays poorly on a high end 3090ti based system at any resolution. Disabling RTX doesnt seem to improve this.

I havent really gotten past the tutorial stuff at this point because I expected more and felt immediately disappointed. Because I am an old fan of MW, I will come back... and try this again, possibly with mods, to see if I can bring the fun factor up a bit. I will revise my review at that time, should I feel better (or worse) about the game.\t\t\t\t\t
