\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been long-time fan of BattleTech and Mechwarrior, so I was thrilled to finally get around to playing this most recent entry to a loved series: I was not disappointed.

It felt like a love letter to Mechwarrior 3: Mercenaries (unsurprising given the name), which I spent uncountable hours playing back in the old days. The mechs feel sold and powerful, combat alternates between strategic maneuving and chaotic blasting, and even when I was soundly destroyed, I was only thinking about diving in to try again.

Graphics are pretty good, the keyboard+mouse controls are quickly mastered, and pretty much every fight gave a solid feeling of challenge. There's a solid campaign mode, both with and without story, as well as a instant action option. I'd like to add that the english voice work was solid and unlike many games, AI squad callouts didn't become irritating.


My only gripe, and it's an expected one that I can't really see a way to make better, is that the storyline is sporratic, not particularly inspired, and felt less like a reason to play the game than a footnote pointing you toward where to go next. To be honest, it was neither bad enough to detract from the game, nor good enough to enhance it.

All in all, 8.5 out of 10.

I've been told a lot of the game engine and mechanics were taken directly from MechWarrior Online, so I plan to take a look at that next.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis would be a "mixed" review if it was possible to give those.

TL:DR:
An acceptable slower paced mech shooter but one that REQUIRES mods to really enjoy.

Gameplay:
Slower paced, more deliberate type of gameplay than most people are used to. A time to kill that is quite high and a game that requires you to develop and/or discover some specific tactics and techniques tied to it in order to become an effective pilot and lance commander.

Missions are presented on the open world map for you to choose from, and range from defence missions to assasinations to making preparations for a landing force etc. The game has 2 primary parts to it, 1. The "overworld" game, where you make decisions on what to buy, what to sell, what missions to take, what to repair and what to refit and 2. The combat, which consists of you piloting 1 out of a maximum of 4 mechs that you can deploy, with AI lancemates piloting the other mechs.

Each weapon and system costs heat in addition to potential ammo costs, meaning that when you are building your mechs you are trying to balance damage output with heat generation.

Now I want to be upfront about something, if this game did not have modding, this review would be an easy thumbs down, because it's too simple without mods, specifically MechLab mods like "Yet Another MechLab" that allow you to change engines, armour types, structure types and more. Without mods like this the main games mechlab loses most of it's enjoyment after 3-4 hours at tops, and for this reason I'm going to say the following - if you get this game, do NOT expect to play it unmodded.

The game is at best a baseline platform that modders built upon to make an enjoyable experience and I'm willing to support that, however, this game genuinely REQUIRES mods to be enjoyable for any extended period of time. Unmodded, I doubt most people could get more than 10-20 hours of enjoyment out of this game. With mods but it's a lot more.

So my final verdict is a follows:
Get the game and the DLC's on sale, unless you are really itching for a moddable game mech game that has replayability in both singleplayer and coop play. If you aren't going to mod the game, then I'd have to recommend against getting it at all, that said, modding it is easy, almost painless even and provides so much that you're really shooting yourself in the foot if you don't explore what the modding community has to offer.

In short, a mediocre and quite empty game that was not only saved by the modding community but even elevated beyond it's orignal design and production. If it wasn't for the modding community I would not recommend this game, and if anything happens to the modding community this will turn into a recommendation to stay away. Modders saved this game, and continue to keep it alive.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecent choice for anyone who likes mechwarrior franchise. Combat is fun and the graphics holds up quite nicely. AI wingmen are a bit retarded but you'll learn to work with that. Problem is that the game seems a bit rushed (or lazy) in the story department. Characters are flat, storyline cheesy and frankly not that interesting. Cutscenes (if any) are "in engine". Whatever happened to nice cinematic cutscenes that were the staple of the franchise back when it begun? Whatever happened to multiple endings and ending cinematics? Missions are randomly generated around the same sets of objectives and the game does not force you to use anything less than your heaviest mechs or any other tactics that head on combat. The game is still fun but the potential for replayability is very low. Overall I'd give it solid 3 out of 5 stars.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long time MW fan this is disappointing.

CAMPAIGN
Pretty open campaign with lots of side quests, but ultimately most of the missions are the same with varying numbers of enemies. You can only change what mechs you bring, no conquering worlds, no space battles, no adding enhancements to your ship or even planetary bases. You literally just fly around from location to location fighting in battles that are extremely similar.

MECHS
A decent lineup of mechs out the door, with many more locked behind DLC (which is negated by just downloading mods off the workshop). Customization is basically copied/pasted from MWO.

CITIES/DESTRUCTION
A fair amount of destruction, really one of the specticals of this game. Nothing gives weight to weapons like seeing them punch through buildings. Unfortunately, MW5 suffers from poor map design and has for a while. All the cities look like some arena tossed slopily together. Nothing remotely resembling anywhere people would actually live. Roads lead to nowhere, buildings are placed oddly, structures clearly to large to be supported under their own weight, cars smaller than building doors. Just ♥♥♥♥ that really makes you feel less like you're in a towering mech and more just a power ranger in a vehicle suit walking around a comically fake landscape.

CONCLUSION
Overall disappointing. MWO is arguably better in almost every way and its FTP. Id have thought the money they made off that plus selling this at a AAA asking price would mean they put more effort into this.

tl;dr save your money play MWO\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst thing to know is that the DLC is absolutely necessary . The base game is a very bare bones platform in which actual content can be added to. The entire base campaign is very tutorial like, doesn't have cinematic moments, and often doesn't make sense chronologically. The DLC campaigns are very good though. One of them reminds me a lot of MechWarrior 3, which was the very first non-kid's game I played.

As for the base game mechanics, they are serviceable.

The faction reputation system doesn't make sense sometimes. For instance, many times raiders will be considered part of the Independent faction instead of the Pirate faction, which means you'll be paid in beans for a lot of your missions.

The graphics are 10 years old, at least, but it does simulate combat damage well.

The tonnage limits don't make sense narratively, and are often too restrictive. The thing I don't like the most in the game is that the low tonnage missions pay the same as the high tonnage missions. Yes, they are the same difficulty, but high tonnage missions are going to cost you twice as much in repairs. This makes the low tonnage missions more financially worth it. There will often be times you have to grind low tonnage missions in order to afford playing a high tonnage one.

I will come back to replay the game if a Blood of Kerensky DLC comes out. Hopefully, they will cut the grind by fixing the tonnage problem by then.\t\t\t\t\t
