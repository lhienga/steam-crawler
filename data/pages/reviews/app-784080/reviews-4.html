\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright so I recommend this game, however I will fully say EXPECT GLITCHES!
I love this game, it scratched an itch I had for Mech games I had for a while since playing Mechwarrior 4. Noting that, I've noticed a LOT of glichtes in the game. Ranging from simple stupid things like different laser sounds or the dropship sound going off constantly, even outside the mission, to mechs and different enemies getting stuck underground and stuck in their dropships until the dropship leaves orbit whereas the mech is stuck in upper atmosphere due to what I believe is a sky border issue.
I know there are a lot of MODs (Workshop content) for the game, though I've been and still am playing on the Base game.
Buy the game if you love mech games like me. I mean I love robots, the idea of replacing an arm that's damaged is COOL! But do not expect perfection. It's a good game! But not perfect! They constantly add patches, one recently being that you pick up "Objectives" or "Items" (Ammo or sometimes weapons) that now if they're close to your dropship on pickup they'll automatically get picked up. Which has screwed up some of my missions before.
I like this game! But don't expect perfection!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts a bad console port with the tactical depth of a puddle.
Its a slow plodding shooter that has made some interesting decisions with things like sensors or RADAR, it is a quite bizarre situation when you are VISUALLY engaging an opponent that your sensors simply refuse to acknowledge the existence of, this appears to be a desperate attempt to hide the spawn mechanic, which is exposed the first time a mech spawns directly underneath YOUR dropshop.
The controls scream console with the gameplay being heavily skewed toward gamepad use.
The biggest elephant in the room however has to be lance mate AI, it is moronic. Ordered to hold position? Charge toward the enemy, got a close quarters mech with all close in weapons..dance about max range doing little, got a ranged LRM heavy mech? CHARGE!
When a little indie game like mount and blade has a better control system for its allies than this supposed AA game..its an indication of something badly wrong.
The final issue is the way they have integrated DLC, siren calling you to industrial hubs with the promise of a "rare" weapon, that doesn`t show up if you don`t have the required DLC, its not that its not purchasable..it simply does not show up.
This is another game that needs a 3 option besides the Yes/No Recommend. The base game and DLC...its just a hard no, the basic game has been done better in previous versions of mechwarrior..its like playing a dumbed down version of Mechwarrior 4 Mercenaries. However the modding community is once again the saviour, if you add the right mods it turns this game from a tactical puddle to a deep ocean, more mechs, more equipment, COMPETENT lance mates and a command system that recognises such basic concepts as close to engage, or hold range open and manages to achieve it.\t\t\t\t\t
