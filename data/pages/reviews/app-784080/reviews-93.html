\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the bigger disappointments I've had in recent years. I loved Mechwarrior 2 and 3 (4 less so) and enjoyed the recent Battletech turn-based game. But this just isn't up to the same standards.

It's fun for around ten hours or so, but around then you start to realize that there's not much left for you to see anymore. There are very few mission-types, and out of those there are a few you will probably want to avoid entirely (like beachhead) due to them being both tedious and unrewarding. These missions play out in procedurally-generated levels across a small number of biomes, each with the exact same buildings, turrets, vtols, tanks, and so on. The game is balanced such that you need to rely on heavier, slower mechs, and with the overly large and empty levels much of your time will be spent not in combat but slowly walking across a boring, featureless map you've already seen countless times. Campaign progression is agonizingly slow as well, with most of your time spent in it feeling like filler. The reality of the game is that you'll be very slowly playing the same three or four mission types in near identical-looking levels, over and over again for dozens of hours, without any real context or feeling of consequences for anything you're doing.

Couple this with the poor voice acting, story, and the general lifeless and sterile feel/look of the game, and it just ends up feeling like a slog to get through. You can improve it somewhat with mods, but these mostly fix things along the game's periphery, things like the lackluster AI, weak selection of mechs, gimped customization, and the like. The core of the game though is beyond fixing. Mods don't make the story or characters any less dull, the campaign feel any more meaningful or its mechanics any more fun to engage with, or make the game feel like it has any sense of style or identity.

What makes it all depressing is that the actual gameplay and combat itself is fun. Especially with mods to tweak the visuals and audio, it has a good heft and punchiness to it that is very satisfying when everything clicks. It's just unfortunate that everything built around the combat fails to do it any justice.

May be worth picking up on a sale, but you'd probably be better off playing one of the older Mechwarrior games or the more recent Battletech game, which while turn-based does a far better job of capturing the Battletech universe and lore.\t\t\t\t\t
