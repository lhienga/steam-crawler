\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteam really needs a "maybe" rating.

I would characterize this game as spectacularly ignorant. Game design: ignorant. UI: ignorant. Mission design: super-ignorant. Your AI lancemates: so incredibly ignorant that they can't resist shaving you with their PPC's as your other lancemate goes full Iraqi deathblossom and the third carefully manuevers to continuously block your line of fire on the target you're engaging. The orders you can give them are limited to "go here" and "shoot that", and there's nothing you can do to keep your long range mechs from enthusiastically closing to point blank range with the enemy's close-in brawlers. But boy is it pretty, stompy and explodey!

And sometimes, that fits the bill. Sometimes you're in the mood for a Michael Bay movie, or to hang out with those friends in the suburbs, or to eat Kraft Dinner.

Sometimes it's... okayish, especially if you're in the mood for it.

But you don't want to invest a lot of money or effort in it.

A better choice if you want to be able to do anything resembling a tactic: Harebrained Schemes Battletech

Edit: The non-sale price seems to have dropped significantly since I first wrote this review. to a point where it's a stronger maybe than it was back then. The game hasn't really improved, though there's more of it DLC-wise. It's gradually shifting from a pot of Kraft dinner to that 27 lb bucket of macaroni and cheese that you can get at CostCo. So yeah, go ahead and have some shooty, stompy, stupid fun maybe.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll of the mech combat stuff is stellar! It's a good thing too, because aside from that this games got some problems.

The worst part of the game is the structure of the campaign. It's really slow progress through a barely-there story where most of the time you're not engaged with it, or any meaningful side mission, at all. You'll be running through randomized battles until your rep reaches a certain point, at which time you'll get drip fed a little more story.

The mech customization is also really good, but slightly less permissive than Mechwarrior Online. It's still good fun to make a mech match your playstyle.

There's also TONS of mods to make the game feel better in most every way. It took me a while to get things to my liking, but it was worth it. I'd give the game one thumb up without mods, two thumbs up with mods!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been incredibly hesitant to get this game, since I watched a few let's plays and noticed that the salvage system is kinda BS. Well it was on sale for 15 bucks, and it looked like some of the bigger issues were smoothed out over the years - plus that mechgame itch just didn't go away.

It's an okay game, especially since I enjoy managing my overhead for costs - but it's quite lazy in its logistical implementation. Unlike in the parallel Battletech turn-based game, if you leave negotiation points on the table you *don't* get any increased standing with the faction you're working with (I know if I was an organization and there was an outfit continually underselling themselves as a valuable asset I'd prefer to hire them vs another group). This is avoidable, until you get missions where you just simply have more negotiation points than there are spots to put them, then you just feel like you're getting screwed....especially in combination with negative modifiers like overtonnage. Which leads me to the next lazy logistics issue:
Stripping down your mechs does NOTHING to bring you under tonnage requirements of a job. That's absolute BS. Nigh unforgivable.
I'd like to micromanage my stuff if you're A) giving me the means to do so, and B) instituting punishments for 'going over'.

TL:DR It's a mech game that feels more or less similar to the previous versions, but doesn't quite measure up in a few annoying ways. Still will probably sate your jonesing for mech combat etc :|\t\t\t\t\t
