\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the universe, but this games goes to such extreme lengths to frustrate me it is hard to believe the developers did that just out of incompetence or oversight and were not actually malicious.

In general, the main thing that annoys me is the blatant disrespect for player's time.

You cannot save during missions, which is understandable somewhat to raise the stakes. But the problem is the part where you die is usually very much by design at the very end and the boring stuff is crammed in the beginning. There's not even any checkpoint and these missions can take 30 minutes.

The game crashes frequently (compared to other non-bethesda games), which really combines with the previous point into absolute hell. It is clear that there was little effort to even handle crashes, seeing how I get just a default "Fatal error" popup without any other info.

Unskipable cut-scenes are another example. Both the ship travel and mech deployment animations are cool, but not so cool I should be not able to skip them after seeing them 30 times.

The game is also mostly unwieldy in general. There are few things that are designed well, like firing groups, but for the most part the UI needs a lot more work.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great game that plays very well and has a lot of positives. Smooth controls, a great story, and high detail, plus a ton of variations in missions and various interactions with NPCs. Immersion in the world of Battletech is quite high, with your lance of Mechs participating in some of the most pivotal moments of the entire Third Succession War. This increases even more with the addition of the DLCs.

The biggest downside to me is that the main storyline ends on a cliffhanger, and there's no conclusion to the story offered.

Additionally, the ending relies on heavy inferences, and refers to a future event which is not present in the game (the Clan invasion). Anyone not remotely familiar with Battletech lore will end up scratching their heads in confusion at the ending, and even as someone who knows what it is referring and inferring to; the ending offers more questions than answers.

Because of this the game feels incomplete and half-finished. as an entire half of the story is missing. It's still a great game, but it's missing a satisfactory conclusion. It needs either an epilogue or a few more missions to round out the game and bring it to its' real conclusion. Hopefully, with the coming of the next DLC, Dragon's Gambit, we will finally get an actual answer to the game's premise: "Just who was Nikolai Mason?"\t\t\t\t\t
