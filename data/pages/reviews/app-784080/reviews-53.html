\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game: definitely worth a play if you like Battletech.

However, only get if it goes on sale. Although it's great fun (if you like stomping around wielding awesome amounts of firepower), there are a number of things that drag the experience down and will eventually ruin it.

1. Poor map design in campaigns: They lack strategic depth and scope. Often enemies will spawn in or you'll be forced along a valley with enemies merely hurled at you. This severely limits how you can choose to deal with the forces arrayed against you, so you just have to slog through.

2. Careless use of lore. With a backdrop as rich as the Battletech universe a lot more effort could have been made with cool little side missions or different challenges.

3. Turrets. In an old game called "Mechcommander", you took control of a lance of mechs with a top-down view. If you played the game carefully you could greatly mitigate the damage done to your lance by careful scouting and observation. Once you knew what a pop-up turret looked like, you could shoot it before you were within its range to pop up and damage your mechs. In this game, there are many of these turrets littered around the battlefield with an easily discernible base. That said, they're indestructible until you're within THEIR firing range. They don't do much, but their damage taken cumulatively can tip the mission against you.
If you want to complete the mission using only a single mech, then it's this sort of thing that kills the game. I'm all for the challenge, but if it's indestructible pop up turrets or lances of mechs constantly being dropped right on top of your position, it doesn't feel challenging, it feels messy and lazy.

4. The AI. Both the enemy and friendlies behave as though they have rocks in their heads. There are mods that improve it, but I feel that previous versions of this game had better AI. Your buddies will constantly run across your firing line, and fall off high vantage points after you spent time that you'll never get back coaxing them up there. You can get around it by giving them specific orders but a lot of people are simply not that patient. If you don't like herding cats, this will definitely kill the experience too.

5. The music is limited and frankly sucks. Definitely put together a playlist before doing a mission, as that improves the game exponentially. Arenna's "Eclipse" is a great tune to rack up c-bills to!

6. Only Inner sphere, plenty of Clan mech mods, but mods get broken with new patches and DLC. Without mods I wouldn't consider this game to be a complete product.

Still, it was a lot of fun, despite the many issues. As a result, its shortcomings aren't so much failings, as a work that's incomplete.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love Battletech, MWO and Stompy Robots. This game is right up my alley in that respect.

Pros:
-Stompy Robots
-Many Pew Pews
-Customize your 'Mechs and crush the enemy
-Make money
-Play with Friends
-Replay-ability and difficulty controls are solid
-Looks good, sounds ok

Cons:
-Quite a few mods needed to increase content to what a lot of players feel like it should already have, especially when you compare it to MechWarrior Online which is made by the same studio.
-Repetitive voice lines
-Eventually no matter what the biome or mission type, gameplay variety is lacking. Not as large scale or bombastic as it could be.
-For players that enjoyed HBS Battletech, being stuck in the Leopard forever and not being able to spend your money on other upgrades is bad. Need more things to do outside purchasing 'Mech stuff and repairs. Interact with your crew?
-DLC is pricey for what it provides
-AI is dumb. Enemies and Allies
-Story is meh. Presentation of story is more meh

Overall I really enjoy this game. I love rolling into a mission with my friends in Co-op and our heavily customized machines of destruction. It looks great too for people who have been fans of this universe for a long time. Really wish they would push more lore into a better presentation, embrace ideas from the community, and just build upon what is already there instead of trying to drain as much cash out of it as they can with the least amount of content for highest profit. I want these games to exist, and I just want them to be better.

Give me options to upgrade my drop ship, let me expand my 'Mech company to be bigger with more lances. Give us the opportunity to buy property planet side and set up a base with some City Management aspects. Let me dispatch portions of my Mercenary Outfit to other jobs with customized gear and roll the dice on their success. Yes I know the main fun of the game is jumping into your customized 'Mech and smashing stuff, but there can be other things added to expand upon what is already a fun game to make it so much better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo honestly I can not recommend this game as it is fairly shallow. It is great that you technically will always be able to drop into content whenever you wish to, but practically you are unable to go back to easier areas without cold storaging your bigger mechs. You will not make enough dosh in order to stay in those areas and need to go onto higher paying jobs.

The biggest issue is it is a war of attrition each game, with enemies outnumbering you greatly. Being able to take more than a single lance, or expanding up to a Company of three lances would have been the better move, especially when mechwarrior 4 let you take two lances. This would let you spread damage around better as well. Being in an union drop ship for instance would have easily worked to fit the size.

Overall, the game just feels like a chore to get through and not something I really want to slog through hours at a time. I keep finding myself just doing other stuff to try to refresh myself to try to play again\t\t\t\t\t
