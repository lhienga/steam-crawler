\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI said pretty much what I had to say in my review on release back when PGI swerved everyone with the last minute Epic store reveal and the one year of exclusivity.

If steam ever does get around to making a mixed option for reviews I would update it for MW5 but currently it has to be a thumbs down. The game looks decent enough, graphics aren't everything and compared to MWO the game is a huge step up but the gameplay compared to previous numbered MechWarrior titles is bad.

The game relies entirely on building the campaign around the same missions you play in the randomised procgen guff you take between campaign missions. All of them boil down to "blow up building" or "blow up 'mech". No attempt to have some interesting and unique missions like MW2 onwards. No escorting the moron noble in his 'mech past patrols, no escort missions, no recon, no unique environments for the battles like going into underground facilities or underwater.

The game brings zero new 'mechs to the table, just PGI porting their MWO library over for the base product. The DLC's so far add a single new 'mech in the form of the Hactetman and even that is inspired by HBS Battletechs addition of the same 'mech over a year prior. The handful of vehicles all act the same way in that they drive right at you and don't use their weapon loadout logically. LRM carriers just drive towards you despite only being armed with long range missiles. Tanks like the Partisan don't keep range with their AC loadout while 'mechs act even dumber, both friendly and enemy.

The AI is broken and hasn't been fixed or improved since launch in fact they have gotten worse as DLC's come out. Now AI can shoot the floor until the mission ends, they don't use their weapons properly, they die extremely quickly and don't coordinate fire on targets even after being told to focus down a single enemy. Enemy AI will just run at you, even lights with paper thin armour will charge assaults head on because PGI can't be bothered to actually work on AI programming. Plenty of games before this have made AI that at least looks like it can use the tools it has been given properly, but by gawd PGI really embody the philosophy of MVP (minimum viable product).

Mods are this games only saving grace and even then the poor bastards get shafted every time PGI patch this game. Breaking the mods so often at this point that some modders have just walked away from the game entirely. PGI promised a full suite of SDK tools for modders to expand the game potentially forever but just gave them the minimum once again. If you can get some friends together, install a selection of the better mods out there either from Nexus or here on the workshop you can have some fun but you have to do all the work to find that fun.

Vanilla the game is a chore. Unfair enemy buffs that increase their damage and damage reduction to compensate for player skill. Missions that can see your 4 'mechs taking on over 20 enemies which can mean multiple assault lances of enemy 'mechs. AI friendly units that spawn with no armour making them pointless. A parade of missions that all boil down to kill X number of units, X number of buildings or just to make your head spin with the variety, protect X number of buildings. Assassinate is just kill X number of 'mechs with lower numbers. The best mission is beachhead which is just a copy paste of one of Coyote's missions with some minor changes.

Rant over, don't bother with this unless you have friends to play it with and you have mods to make it bearable. Buy it on the biggest discount you can if you really want to play it, trust me you aren't missing much by waiting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(I'm only starting off with "No" because there's no option for "I guess, if you're bored or have friends who are into Battletech".)

MW5's been thoroughly irritating because of the seemingly split attentions of the developers or just a lack of long-term thinking.
Looking at Piranha Games Inc. their M.O. is being an "action gaming studio with unparalleled experience developing licensed products for big-name franchises." Breaking this down for what the game is currently: I don't know if Epic Games just do not release the documentation for Unreal Engine and let developers eyeball it because functional graphics integration is simply easier, just by virtue of being Nvidia's right hand which bludgeons people with its high-or-die baked in "fidelity" bloat. I'm a nobody but I'm frankly tired of Unreal Engine being either: a) incompetently used or b) functionally unrefined (dithering, model/texture pop-in, RTX problems, memory leaks, list goes on; looking at you Cyberpunk 2077 and 40K Darktide).

To get back to the actual game, MW5 is an unflattering use of current engine technology, save (perhaps) the details of models, textures, effects and the ease of complexity achieved by modders who are always picking up the slack. I wouldn't personally harp on the engine so much if it didn't actively hinder my enjoyment of the game itself, because it's been this way since release.
As games do, they get updated and should one wish to mod it, they typically need to find a corresponding file or develop for the current 'edition' and many mods that aren't radical changes to the game's native mechanics or an overhaul can generally be found to be update-agnostic - MW5's progression through its development has seen several excellent visual, QoL and sound mods to be - for the foreseeable future - defunct, which is disappointing to say the least, because many people aren't a fan of the basic HUD, or you know, they want to see the fictional style of luminous lasers from MechWarrior 3 and 4. For the most part, mods are supported, which is a big ask in the current brain-drain of modern games development, yet PGI seems to have shrugged about the state of their difficult platform leaving more than a handful of good visual and sound addons in the dust.

Currently, the mission variety is still lacking for what is essentially a sandbox (or should be) of the Inner Sphere and the Rim, and yes, Coyote's Mission Pack is an option but that's besides the point (the mod itself also has glaringly stupid issues and questionable design flaws as if to blow smoke on YAML content, also contains a self-insert Mech' pilot).
Only now do we actually see other mercenary outfits in the Inner Sphere, as prior to that content, we had to pretend or it was implied we weren't the only mercenary outfit around; yeah, coulda fooled me. Call to Arms continues to be a worthless spend that - as the playerbase suggests - should have been a free update for being sidewinded that Legend of The Kestrel Lancers might've been some actual meat, yet wasn't.

Going backwards here, but, Heroes of the Inner Sphere added what was reasonably assumed to be apart of the base game: you pick a position, hopefully some other additions (which weren't there when HoTIS released; again, mods have to fill the gap) and launch, party on John - it was a much needed 'release' from the confines of campaign content, especially for multiplayer action where the sandbox was appreciated more (yet still shallow). Legend of The Kestrel Lancers was a "fool me once" scenario, I think. Generally considered the worst written piece of content since the main campaign prior to the current stage of Rise of Rasalhague (also written and presented terribly - it's a geopolitical shift in the Inner Sphere, for God's sake), which both of these DLC storylines connect with my issues of the frustratingly underutilised ability of the Unreal Engine that dovetails right back into failing to competently set the stage for these historical plotlines and giving them the objective gravitas they're implied to have. Last stands are flaccid, warzones, beachheads and frantic pushes we never get to see yet are explained like they exist, or panicked holdouts contested by predictable floats of DropShips that still seem to employ the same typical formula of generic mission content without so much as the developer's hand coming in to craft a challenge (which does rarely happen) that isn't carried on the shoulders of Assault-class Mechs.
At least the terrain setpieces contain some kind of intentional design later on, which is a weak point to land on, frankly. The illusory practice of trying to weigh some kind of concern on the player like attrition, time and danger is rife through the story DLC, easily bypassed with some basic planning and a surplus of Mechs without so much of the game interceding and placing a limitation on us or our lance outfit, according to the tactical or special considerations that we're supposed to care about.

Relating to the workings of a lance itself; this has been tackled from other reviews and comments - it's that the requirement of Light and Medium-class Mechs in some function largely dissolves as you progress through the game, and the only thing that seems to enforce their use is the arbitrary tonnage limit despite there being no internal reasoning for this, even if we're a standalone mercenary company and we can choose to field what we damn well please, so, it feels like this self-refuting problem of that being able to field what we like can refute the requirement for using Light-class and Medium-class Mechs, yet, here's the tonnage limit, but it makes no sense since we have our own transportation asset: a Leopard DropShip.
Another element to this set of arbitrary gameplay mechanics that makes up the last point in the triad of this incoherent weight-to-power dynamic is the difficulty scale, which I and a few friends considered that it may as well be a progressive list of available assets the game is allowed to spawn and in what number, though governed by the mission type at-hand, ultimately leaving you little flexibility because Heavy and Assault-class Mechs have the best chance of repelling the difficulty scale's typical process of sending the same mix of units to challenge you (sometimes with intercepting Lights and Mediums coming from anywhere - you can tell when they spawn because the engine has a heart attack). Reasonably, you don't want to be sent packing with a bunch of deep-fried Mechs with missing centre-torsos, or legs, which will set you back financially; you want to win so you can earn C-Bills and salvage... so what the ♥♥♥♥'s the point?

Probably like a lot of other people, I could go on about the lack of other content that's been offered, such as the afterthought of Mechs presented (nobody cares about the Grasshopper, for real), but I think it's all well-known at this point.

Aside from all that, MechWarrior 5: Mercenaries - at least - carries some work with updated and progressive additions to the chief idea about MechWarrior: the simulation of driving these warmachines. Give or take, even if I've heard that MWO has - apparently - a more solid establishment in that area (as well as the MW5 Pre-Alpha footage). What I would recommend, despite the big, red thumb out of lack of a middle road, is that if you have friends that enjoy Battletech, or a community, etc. and they own all the DLCs, they can host a mercenary company for multiplayer and all you would need is the base game to benefit from their official DLC content.

I bought everything because I was curious, and I feel stupid for doing so. Don't settle for second-rate, no matter what Reddit tells you or your buds.

That's it, have a good one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen the game was released on the Epic game store, it was frankly raw, there was a lack of content, and the gameplay quickly slipped into repetitiveness. Now, going through it with all the available add-ons, I can safely say that this is a great thing. I happily spent almost 40 hours on it. In essence, it has almost flawless basic gameplay. Crushing mechs are fun and exciting, and there are now a fair number of missions and campaigns to do so within a fair number of different victory conditions. The weak point of the game is certainly the plot, the enemies here are evil because they are doing atrocities and we must defeat them because we are good and the truth is behind us. But for such a military genre, an intricate plot is not something mandatory. Enough that I understand who to shoot.
I hope the devs will continue to release add-ons with new campaigns and mechs. This is a game that is nice to come back to sometimes and just shoot a couple of dozen mechs for fun.\t\t\t\t\t
