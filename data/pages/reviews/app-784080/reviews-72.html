\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally played this game upon release and it was okay. I recently returned to it and it's remarkably improved. While the original game was fairly basic, with a campaign revolving around rather mediocre and predictable plot points, and the mission variety was pretty lacklustre, the DLC expansions solve a lot of these issues.

Difficulty:
You can essentially set your own difficulty by which regions you choose to operate in. This makes the game quite flexible. If you want a challenge you can try to punch above your weight. If you find yourself getting stomped you can hang around some of the lower areas. If you want to really ramp up challenges, there are some mods that introduce scary tanks or further tweak things.

Visuals:
Visuals are pretty good but I think they put too much effort into the destructibility of the mechs and buildings. What I mean by this is that almost all damage is faithfully shown on the mechs, making it quite immersive. However, I might have preferred a much more highly polished environment and look to the mechs than quite so much environmental depth. The buildings are almost all entirely destructible, with the exception of any building over two stories - not sure why honestly, it's kind of annoying you can't knock them down or make them fall on people (or risk them falling on you...)

Sound:
The FX are good and fairly meaty, and if you prefer different styles there's a fair few mods to amend music, sound effects and so on. The comms between antagonists etc are often scrambled but there's no real clear need for this. It just serves to make it harder to understand people. I assume this was done for immersion purposes, but like the mech damage, I feel like it was misplaced effort.

Gameplay:
This is the main aspect of any game. The gameplay loop can vary wildly depending on how you play this. In either case you find yourself engaged in pretty short range combat most of the time, without much opportunity to repair or re-arm mid mission. This means you are in a war of attrition and the chief way the game ramps up difficulty is just throwing more and more at you as you wind up in knife fighting range.

Solo gameplay:
Your lancemates aren't super helpful. They're a lot better than when this game first came out, but they're not good at thinking independently. Tell them to attack an enemy and they'll invariably close with them to slug it out, even if they're not optimised or suited to shorter range combat. You'll find yourself either micromanaging their targets, or just throwing them at the most serious threat and taking care of the most pressing enemy units yourself. Your tactical options are limited to the four of you stomping around the map murder-hoboing anything in your immediate vicinity.

Co-op gameplay:
So much more fun. With human players who can act independently, as well as form and initiate more complicated strategies, your options wildly open up. You can finally use indirect fire en masse, or snipers and scouts. This kind of tanks the difficulty of the game, but as mentioned that can be mitigated by punching up or using some mods. And you'll have to take your human lancemates preferences into account, shopping to meet them and keep them happy. Or at least, if they're any fun you will.

Missions, objectives, etc:
Thanks to the DLC you get a much better variety of mission types. You can plug away at the basic types, or you can sign up for campaigns. These follow a number of lore-related events which are great fun to come across or anticipate if you're familiar with the universe. If you're not, you'll either find this an interesting, deep universe full of lore, or you'll skip all the text because you only really care about blowing stuff up. But these missions and campaigns have a lot to stand out from the crowd and make the DLC very much worth having.

Should you buy this game? Well if you like giant mecha sims then yes. If you like Mechwarrior and are willing to embrace changes from some previous versions of the genre, absolutely. If you're into battletech lore, you'll definitely enjoy living through it. If you do buy this game, you want the DLC as well. I realise that hikes the price but it's worth it.

What's offputting? Fahad, your mech tech. His terrible accent is bad enough but he's such a whiny sod.

Overall this isn't the most fun Mechwarrior game ever released. It puts a lot of time and effort into the lore, the environment and granular level of detail that it almost feels bloated and sluggish. If you played Mechwarrior 4: Mercenaries you'll remember a fairly fast paced, action oriented gameplay that felt fluid and fast paced.

I do recommend it but with a few caveats. If you have one to three friends to play it with, it's an absolute yes. If you're going to play it solo, be prepared to spend a lot of time in it. Missions are about 20 minutes max so you can have shore sessions, but it'll take a long time to complete a career or campaign.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI found MW5 to be much better than I expected after reading a lot of negative reviews.

This was my gateway into the Battletech universe (for better or worse) and I am so happy I gave this game a try.

It's a pretty immersive sandbox-y mercenary sim that allows you to pilot a huge variety of really cool mechs. With severe lack of mecha games on PC, I think it's easily one of the best ones.

You get to travel around the galaxy picking up your own procedural/scripted missions, building relationships with different factions, which in turn provides you with economic benefits when you trade on territories of said factions.

Maps you get to play on are also procedurally generated, so there's plenty of fun to be had across plenty of different biomes. Still, I'd say that scripted campaigns are probably the best content in the game, with both interesting storylines and great rewards.

While not the most visually impressive and not extremely well-optimized, the game still looks and plays really great. The scale of destruction you can unleash on both the environments and other mechs really makes you feel like you pilot a big badass ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.

I didn't play MWO so I never found base game customization to be particularly lacking. It's nowhere near something like Armored Core IV, but it's still pretty neat and allows you to have plenty of different builds for most mechs. It's enjoyable and not super complex, I honestly couldn't ask for more.

Alas, not everything is perfect. The enemy/lancemate AI is pretty barebones. You won't see other mechs use environments/maps to their advantage or try to do any tricky stuff to get behind you. AI in general usually just charges at you most of the time. The high difficulty missions will sometimes drop massive assault mechs right on top of your head. The difficulty can jump a bit because of that, but it's nothing unmanageable, and still generally very enjoyable. Even with limited team commands you can still play around with your squad and use positioning to your advantage. It feels pretty satisfying to do when you get used to it.

For all of my platyime I've enjoyed the game without any gameplay-altering mods (I've added some cool stuff for visuals that I really liked, but it's still not even close mandatory), and it took me a while to really get bored in any way. Fighting progressively bigger and meaner mechs, reading about all the events that are happening in the universe and dealing with different factions, scavenging new parts/mechs and then travelling across the galaxy to get some rarer/Hero variants - all of this felt really enjoyable for a pretty long time, so I'd say I easily got my money's worth.

I plan to come back to this game someday and play a lot more of it, maybe even with some bigger mods, but at this point I can only say that these 100+ hours that I've spent on it were definitely enjoyable as heck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played this franchise since MW2 way back when. And the biggest change I've noticed is how you spend WAY more time outside of missions shopping, repairing, outfitting, and managing your mechs. And it's just too much. Like to the point where I felt like I wanted to play a soccer game, but got Football Manager instead. The management is so aggressive, that I even have trouble enjoying the actually playing of the game because if a bunch of stuff breaks, it's going to be a pain in the ass getting that mech back up to par again. The management is far too granular as you not only have to manage all your armor on all your parts on every mech, but each part has a different armor value for the front AND The back of everything.

On top of this, the mechs and weapons didn't even feel that different. Like, the only difference of the mechs is just about physical appearance. Every mech just does certain weapon types better than others. Like, you might have a mech that's better at energy weapons vs ballistics. Or this mech weights 20 more tons that another. And that's just not really a whole lot of diversity or unique-ness. I was even running mods that added new mechs and quirks/traits. And everything STILL felt same-y.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend MechWarrior 5: Mercenaries to anyone who is a fan of the series, and looking for some progression aspects, in the form of building up wealth, mechs etc. The game seems to generate most of the missions randomly which can get a little old. I strongly recommend the DLCs which have better designed missions in them. If you played this game vanilla, I recommend a second try with the DLCs.

The good:
The DLC missions are pretty good.
Multiplayer is excellent!
Building mechs is fun! But I wish there was more flexibility.
Walking through Battletech universe history as the years go by in the game. The news reports are fun.
The DLC introducing melee combat is a MUST HAVE. The Melee Combat is very satisfying.
All mech weights are valuable. Even having player driven light mechs in heavyweight battles can be good.
You can choose your own difficulty here. You can go in overweight on a mission and curb stomp it or go underweight, or even with less than 4 mechs to make it more difficult.

The bad:
Not entirely bug free there is the occasional crash, or people losing the ability to lock targets in multiplayer.
The vanilla missions can get repetitive.
It is hard to find what you are looking for. For example, you see a news article about a new mech in this region of space, you go there and find nothing, then 10 in game years later when you have completely forgotten about it, "Oh look! There is that mech/item I read about. Too bad I don't care anymore!"
Being a neutral merc isn't a great option profit wise, but siding with a house, while very profitable, can cause people to miss out on a large part of the game and get bored quickly. Would be nice to have better negoiation based on your overall level rather than how much they like you.
Would be nice to scale up, have missions with more than 4 (a lance) of mechs like 12 (a company) of mechs. In all the old books most of them followed 12 person merc companies rather than 4 person lances.
It is difficult to be a "mech collector" as it becomes prohibitively expensive quickly.
The pre-mission intelligence is low and the missions are often completely random which makes it super difficult to tailor pick your forces to the mission at hand. Sometimes you are forced to tick a square peg into a round hole.

The ugly:
Nothing! When I first played vanilla it got old pretty fast, but I've put weeks into it with the DLCs and still going strong.

If you are a fan of the battletech universe, you will like this game. If you are looking for fast paced, mecha combat you will NOT like this game. The DLCs are important to the experience, in particular the melee combat one.\t\t\t\t\t
