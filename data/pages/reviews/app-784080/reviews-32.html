\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoam the galaxy with your crew, collect big stompy mechs through salvage or lucky markets.

Find a mech with more Large Energy slots than it can handle for its size, replace them all with PPCs and shoot them at the same time, overheat, repeat.

Take out all LRMs (Artillery missiles) and replace them with SRMs (shotgun blast of missiles), hug the biggest threat on the mission and kill them in two volleys, overheat, repeat.

Find a King Crab and find out why two AC/20s are much better than just one. Remove all other weapons to fit as much ammo as possible for your two giant ballistic cannons. Watch it turn into firework display as a leg that lost its armor instantly has the 3 ton of AC/20 ammo you put there explode and cause a chain reaction through the mech.

It's the best mech sim game, nothing out there feels as immersive sitting inside a big ass robot.

Keep an eye out on sales, the game and its DLCs goes on 50% sales often which is the pricetag the game is recommended at.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I think MW5 is well worth the money that I spent on it.

*It helps if you don’t have expectations too high. I did not expect a AAA game, and so I was not disappointed. I don’t know much about the dev studio, but they didn’t invest AAA money in it. (I suspect that it leverages some work from the online game, which I haven’t played).

*Graphics are good, and sometimes very good, though not at a AAA level.

*I played it on a GTX 4060Ti and 4070. With the 4060Ti, the game ran mostly fine: my computer would infrequently struggle for brief periods when on near-max settings at full HD (1080p) resolution. I have no trouble using max settings on the 4070 at WQHD (1440p) resolution.

*The game is very stable.

*I had only a few problems with bugs which prevented me from finishing missions. Maybe it happened 3 times, and 2 times I was OK after restarting the mission. Since the missions are short, it wasn’t a big deal. Only one mission (a campaign mission in career mode) was fully broken.

*At time of writing the game with 5 DLCs is $105, after a bundle discount. This is expensive for a non-AAA game. I bought what I could (game+3 DLCs) at 50% discount and I’m OK with waiting for the other DLCs to be discounted. I think the discounts occur when a new DLC is released.

*Multiplayer works well.

*Gameplay is very good. The missions are short, ~15 minutes each typically. It is a nice little gameplay loop.

*The developers deserve a lot of credit for making this an open-world style of game, which I’m sure added extra challenge. Ditto for including melee.

*Customization of mechs is OK. It’s similar to vanilla HBS Battletech, but much less than modded HBS Battletech. For example, you cannot add XL engine, ferro-fibrous armor, endo-steel. Hardpoints are limited to what are used in the mech that you start with. No complaints though.

*I really enjoyed the pirate (i.e. low-tech) -style weapons that were included, like chem lasers and heavy rifles.

*I get the sense from other reviews that modding is much less supported by the developer of this game, relative to HBS’s support for modding Battletech. So, I didn’t try to do any modding beyond very simple ones.

*There is not much variation in mission types, but I still enjoyed them.

*There is not much variation in biomes in the base game but with the expansions, it is sufficient.

*Some of the voice acting is OK. But some of the voice acting from actors with limited lines are cartoonishly bad (e.g. the air support + allied support guy). (Again, not a AAA game).

*There are quite a few typos in the text for missions, “news feed”, etc. (Again, not a AAA game).

*It is more fun if you are a fan of the Battletech fiction, like me. I put a lot of hours playing modded versions of HBS Battletech (Roguetech and BTA) before playing this. And vanilla HBS Battletech of course (a fantastic game). And I played some earlier games going back to 1988.

*Career mode is fun. I especially liked the early stage (I selected the Marik/FWL start), where I was barely scraping by, and had some very crappy mechs (e.g. 2 Assassins) in my rotation because I couldn’t afford better.

*Campaign mode is fine. I understand that the developers had a big challenge in designing this in a way that was compatible with the general open-world play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreetings! I’ve been a big Battletech fan for years, playing both the HBS Game and the Tableotop and recently got into the MW: Online and loved it except for one thing. I’m not a fan of PVP, enter “MechWarrior 5: Mercenaries” a fully co-op game that had everything I wanted. But, I kept seeing how bad the base game was. Was it? Let’s Discuss!

Send in the Scout Mechs: Because we all know, the moment we can hop into a 100 ton Atlas they’ll have to pry our dead hands from the controls. What was I doing? Reviewing? But I still have things to stomp on make full of holes and make pew pew noises. Fine… MW5 is a First Person simulation game where you pilot a huge and can barely hear the laminations of the women over the roar of your engine. What? Fine, fine…for real this time.

Let them Fight: Okay, review…review. MW5 is probably the closest thing we’ll have to the feeling of actually piloting a giant warmachine. The graphics are presentable and the sound is on point, both the music and the ambient. The first time I heard a ballistic rush past me was intense and hearing several during heavy combat is still an amazing experience.
Combined together there is something special that happens when you are piloting anything from a Flea zipping around to an Annihilator cutting swaths of destruction with your lasers. And that's the thing, the single biggest enjoyment of this game is the massive difference of feel that each class can bring. A light mech can feel like you are rushing through a war of gods while taking exposing shots and hunting for that unaware Assault who happens to have its back to you. Where as trudging along in an Assault the game slows down, you pick your shots more deliberately. You are no longer speed and impulse, you are a god-machine and every shot from your Gauss rifle is precisely planned. The campaign is mostly forgettable, or at least the story is. The combat itself and the managing of your mechs is an enjoyable experience. The game also does a great job at punishing you for failures without making it a mission or even campaign ending level of failure. But the real pleasure in this game is playing with friends, as the AI isn’t great and something special comes along when playing along side other living pilots.

Unmodded, like God intended: I played this game unmodded but from what I’ve seen and heard from the community this game begs for mods. In fact I couldn’t really find anyone who didn’t play with at least a few mods. So, my suggestion is to do your thing, maybe play a bit without then grab some that look good. I know I’ll be coming back to this game in time with mods.

Tower Defense: This game (at core without mods) has a lot of issues, which says a lot considering how much I still loved this game. To begin with there really needs to be a lot more customization (resolved in modding) especially with the mechs and the pilots. Although I do like how you can be over-tonnage and still launch I wish the game encouraged this more at higher difficulties as all the missions just become a Steiner Scout Lance. I also plainly just hate things spawning in, with all the effort that went into this game why they couldn’t allow underground bunkers or dropships to deploy OpFor. The later missions also feel like way too much walking, not saying the maps need to be smaller but when the mission calls for 400 tons and you send me 3000+ meters away, can take sometime of boredom. The campaign could also use a bit of work, I do not understand why they make your partner faction the same faction as the most common target (I’m sorry that I kept attacking the Quickie Mart but I don’t know why Value-Stop is mad at me). Oh and the way the AI allies work…just a shame, there are 20 year old RPGs with better AI management. I don’t expect them to be amazing but I shouldn’t have to rig up their weapon groups and equipment just perfect so that my Archer doesn’t charge into melee every fight when he has LRM40.

Overall, I love this game, sure it had frustrating bits and yes I definitely had my moments of defeat into the late game but I always loved it. The game tremendously improves with human allies and I’d go as far as saying it is essential for the fun of the game. Additionally, I’d imagine (from what I’ve been told) that the DLC is required purchasing too. So, highly recommend…but learn from me and buy the DLC…or use Mods…or both… Enjoy!

Final note, the community is extra helpful with this game. I had a lot of frustration getting into and past the 70 difficulty bracket and thanks to their help and encouragement I was able to find some mechs that worked for me and improved my tactics to eventual move into the endgame.\t\t\t\t\t
