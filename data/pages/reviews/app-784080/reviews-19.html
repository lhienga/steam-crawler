\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWaited two years to try it (because PGI) and just bought all DLC on heavy sale. Modded it before first launch (YAML, Better this and that) and have been having a good time.

It has scratched an itch I didn't know I had and I will definitely complete it, so "recommended"... warts and all.

The warts...

PC graphics are ♥♥♥♥ compared to what they could have been. I assume this was for the lowest common denominator console. The loading animation, something you will see ad nauseam, is something a bored kid could have put together in a day or two with existing assets, which PGI has no shortage of.

Pre-rendered, lo-rez videos are streeeeeetched to fit widescreen displays, making CD games from the early 90's jealous. What idiot thought that was the best option? Very unprofessional. Black pillars would have been better, at least the aspect ratio would be preserved and you would only be able to make fun of the the awful story and video and not the stupid implementation too.

Procedural terrain variations locked behind DLC is a huge finger in the face to early adopters. This is the opposite of how you build goodwill for your company, but it reeks of "The Golden Mech" for $500 USD in MWO, so it's typical corporate PGI ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.

Procedural map items floating in the air. Rocks, trees, shrubs, you name it, they too can float. Half-assed effort, likely due to corporate/management pressures.

Lance AI doesn't seem to care about using cover or trying to move around to make themselves harder targets. The best you can do is actively move to force them to stop standing there like the 20 million c-bill target dummies they want to be (e.g. you're hiding behind a building from multiple heavy hitting snipers and your lance just stands in the open staring at the enemy while taking every hit). They also seem to enjoy positioning themselves in front of you to then mirror your movements with excellent precision while you try to shoot around them. Rage inducing stuff when it happens near the end of a mission.

Lance AI has no concept of proper missile usage. Regularly firing volleys into obstacles that are clearly in the way, sometimes destroying buildings you're trying to protect. Ignore perfectly clear opportunities to target enemies in bad positions. Rarely use missile fire even when loaded with thousands of missiles and 3 missile batteries. Lesson: Don't place AI in your missile boats, they'll get 250 points of damage by the end, if you're lucky.

The soundtrack sucks. It's bargain basement noise to me and has been permanently muted while playing ancient MW2/Quake2/other soundtracks in the background. Jesper Kyd could have knocked this out of the park (Darktide). Quality music makes a big difference. I was hoping someone had modded something to add replacement tracks but haven't found anything.

Dialog/story is all hit or miss and very skippable. Several of the voice actors sound bored or don't know how to sound engaged with what they're regurgitating for money.

Complaints aside... building, upgrading, and battling with big stompy robots never seems to get old, but I wish a better company would take license ownership. PGI has repeatedly proven they will only give minimal effort/investment with this license to turn some kind of profit. If they "love" this universe it's hard to see it.

It's time to hand over the keys to that kingdom PGI, we know you won't get better.\t\t\t\t\t

Liao--still my favorite targets.
