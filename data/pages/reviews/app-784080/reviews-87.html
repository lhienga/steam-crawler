\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's taken some years to get to this point, but we're finally here. We're in the dreaded Skyrim Dance: modders make something the community really likes, the devs release DLC or a patch that breaks everything, everyone sighs and goes off to do something else while waiting for their favourite mods to work again.

I know where this goes. Bethesda showed us. They'll release mod-breaking patches to force modders to the latest version and players to buy the latest DLC. Of which there is a lot, mind you, and none of it worth asking price.

I'm sick and tired of this. The base game is barely tolerable without all the mods. The best feature of the game, by far, is up to 4 player co-op, and with mods it turns into an exquisitely enjoyable time to chill out together. But this constant DLC release and mod breaking cycle reminds me of Skyrim way too much.

And it's not a good memory.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really love this game, it's fantastic. It hits a very nice blend of 'shooter', 'tactical' and 'make a number go up' that I enjoy. It's so cool how you steadily build up your funds and resources by taking apart enemy mechs in combat, salvaging them and upgrading your own arsenal with what you find.

The flexibility of customization is immense, and with mods becomes genuinely ridiculous. I've spent at least a few dozen hours if not hundreds just in the mechlab, fiddling around and choosing what I want.

The story and world is honestly pretty good, too. It's grown on me a lot, even though I was apathetic to it at first. It's out of the way, so you can ignore it, but the world of BattleTech is very fleshed out - it is a long and tumultuous history of human folly and cruelty coming together to prevent progress. I love it because I feel like I can really have fun with the giant robots without feeling like I am playing a awful propaganda simulator like Call of Duty. You are just a cog in the machine, but not big enough to stop the whole thing - there is a kind of tragedy in the fact that you keep doing what you're doing as a mercenary. I do not feel like a 'good guy' or a 'bad guy', I am just some random person doing what they can to live, even if it sucks.

Incredibly highly recommend. Extremely worth the price at $30, even more at half off.\t\t\t\t\t
