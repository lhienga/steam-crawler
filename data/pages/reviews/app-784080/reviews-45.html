\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo. It's slow and clunky... and not just the mechs.

Graphics: Worse that what I've seen from games made in 2005. EXCEPT for the mechs themselves, which are "barely acceptable" for games made in 2015.

The drop ship frequently lands 3KM from your target with a 1KM trek to be picked up. With a top speed of 64 for a "fast" mech, it takes about 5 minutes of *nothing* but walking to even start, and 1-2 more when leaving.

All combat is clunky. It's *extremely* easy to hit something, so "FAST" mechs aren't viable... No such thing as tactics here. All parties basically stand still and fire until one dies, then repeat. Ally AI pretty much never misses, enemy AI pretty much never miss. I never miss, except when PPC goes *through* my target without hitting it. Long range fights aren't a thing, as you only have a max of 2x zoom, and terrain is always heavily mountainous. All the fights are *very* samey.

Stats on weapons make no sense:
"Damage 10" with 12 shots per *minute* somehow does more damage than "Damage 1.63" with 10 shots per *second*.

I wouldn't recommend it any price... but...
If you want an extremely slow walking simulator you can veg out on... this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI fondly remember Mechwarrior 4 and its various expansions from way back when, but when I first got MW5 I was very disappointed at how poorly the AI fought and got a refund after literally stomping my way through an entire battalion of very stupid tanks. It was actually far more effectively to just step on them - despite the fact that they were faster than I was - because while my paltry lasers took a while to carve through their armor, physical contact was an insta-kill.

This made the entire exercise seem silly, especially since I'm a Battletech grognard who understands tanks are to be respected even if not feared - as opposed to being the centerpieces of a BattleMech dancing "La Cucaracha".

So Dragon's Gambit came out, and I'm being told by all and sundry that the AI and other factors have been juiced up. Not only that, but there are mods which deal with the insta-kill problem! Okay, I tell myself, I'll give it another go.

First mission: I'm seeing derpy AI doing the same dumb things I remember from before. The overall game looks a lot better and has more to see and do, but the actual combat is STILL silly! And what did the mod which "dealt" with the insta-kill problem do? Nothing but turn off collision detection for tanks. They're STILL insta-kills, so now they're completely invulnerable to physical attacks even though melee's been added in. Because I guess why bother enacting melee rules for tanks when everyone's already used to just stepping on them anyways?

Nah, nah, nah. I've been enjoying Battletech proper (with Roguetech and BT 3062 modded in) much more, despite its lack of in-the-cockpit focus, because it's actually faithful to the tabletop. I get a much stronger feel for combined-arms warfare, tactics, and proper payoff for use of support assets, instead of the arcadiness of MW5. Despite multiple DLCs and updates since its launch, this game still feels half-finished.

Which is a damned shame, because I really loved being able to walk around my own DropShip and sit in my own cockpit. The strategic side of the game looks and feels great too, as does the attention to the lore. But while the 'Mechs themselves seem to have gotten a lot of attention, non-'Mech assets seem phoned in and I generally felt like I was surrounded by idiots fighting both for and against me.

Just not my cuppa.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a reasonable entry into the mech warrior series. The graphics and game play are acceptable. It does have the ability to keep me entertained. It is nice to see the inside of a drop ship and the environments are okay. Combat is enjoyable and the ability to punch with some mechs is very satisfying.

Down sides: Its more enjoyable and unique missions come from DLC. The base game can get pretty repetitive playing normal missions, along with the same set of lines from your XO. Even some of the campaign missions are just basic ones with a unique written briefing. Once you investigate the drop ship environment it feels rather small, its only there for set dressing so your XO can tell you the next mission and your Chief tech can complain about having to fix mechs. None of the other six or so people you see have any lines.

There are quite a few mods that add sound effects from the older mech games which I find enjoyable, plus the option of a female main character and VA (i found the basic player character to be very bland)

I do like the Mass Effect style of flying about in a ship picking missions, it could do with more of that feeling like maybe some missions you can do on foot and you can recruit an infantry spec ops squad and your own aerospace pilots.

Worth a look if you like mechwarrior, maybe not worth the full release price but on sale with some cheap DLC it will scratch that itch.\t\t\t\t\t
