\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've ever wanted to run your own mercenary company in the Battletech universe... you're better off looking elsewhere. This game is a walking death tank simulator, you, and three poorly optimized AI pilots, fight in big stompy robots against other stompy robots. A LOT of other stompy robots. And if you survive you get to pay for it. But at least you get to do so with a fantastic soundtrack.

My biggest gripe with this game is the missions themselves get stale very quickly. You and your battlemechs enter the field and you'll fight wave after wave of opposing battlemechs and vehicles. More often than not, you're just slugging it out with the opposition. And you'll face between three and five times your number in opposition. There aren't things like tactics or positioning, interesting skills to combine, or different game mechanics that you can use to give yourself an advantage, it simply comes down to making your mech's numbers better than the opposing mech's numbers. That gets boring very fast, and the missions feel much longer than they need to be. A twenty minute mission stops being fun after about ten minutes, but you can't quit without losing.

My second biggest criticism of the game is grind. You'll be doing a lot of grinding in this game. Rewards are very small, very same-ish, and don't feel good. Progression doesn't feel good, it doesn't feel like you're progressing at all, really.

The next biggest problem is the AI pilots that make up your lancemates. Your lance is made of four battlemechs, which you pay for all the upkeep and repair, and you need the firepower of all four to make it through most missions. The AI pilots are not good. They do not position themselves well, do not prioritize targets well, do not maneuver well, cannot be ordered to do anything very complicated, and generally get in the way as often as they help.

How would I improve this game? I'd change up the missions a lot, give players an opportunity to create tactical advantages, and throw fewer waves of enemies at the player. I'd create incentive to do something more than bring the heaviest battlemechs possible. I'd opt for more, shorter, missions. Lastly I'd change the AI pilots. As it stands, I can't recommend this game to any of my friends.\t\t\t\t\t
