\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would not play this game unmodded nor recommend to anyone to buy this game only to play it unmodded.

If you want a feel for what this game plays similarly to, try Mech Warrior Online, it's free to play and PvP so if you're good at that you'll slay here. That said, MW5 allows you to have fun and do that isn't meta where in MWO that can backfire. Also, bring back armor. Some Warthunder player in a tank will spawn 2km behind you and nail you in the back and suddenly, you lost a side torso. Also, no clan mechs to be found here. So that's sad.

Kind of like Fallout, this game is carried by mods...specifically mods that bring back basic customization that was in MWO (YAML/Yet Another Mech Lab) or add more weapons.

Bring friends. 3 of em. The friendly AI is at best a bullet sponge.

Gameplay? Pretty fun. You shoot in giant robots. There's a variety of giant robots and poor bastards not in giant robots to shoot at and get shot by. However you'll basically do all of the missions there are to do within 2 hours of career. I've exclusively played career mode. Kind of like all shooters, this gets repetitive and the only thing that'll bring variety to spice up is how you choose to play said missions.

There's guns. There's lasers. There's missiles. No like, hammer of God giant beam cannons type you see from anime though.

The most complicated the combat gets is heat management if you're boring and use lasers, less so if you use kinetics. That, and shielding using certain parts of your mech by turning the parts you want to shield with towards the enemy to say, protect your torso with your gun in it by shielding with your arm.

Battle mechs are not similar to the infinitely cooler Gundams or Armored Cores. You are piloting a big, slow clunky machine of war designed to be shot at. It will be shot at. Mitigate what gets shot at the most by having other things get shot at.

Oh, and melee is fun (with mods that actually let you change your engine, because a 48km/h assault mech isn't going to get to melee anything 99% of the time. At the same time, you get masc and a supercharger, suddenly your assault mech is going 150 km/h and you destroy everything because melee does a metric ton of damage. I guess it's a balancing feature so this is up to you)

The AI? Well the enemy AI works, your friendly AI lancemates and any friendly AI for that matter have an IQ rating somewhere between and all. Do not put your lancemates into a mech you aren't willing to lose and spend millions repairing because they're going to lose it, or all of the damn weapons on it. The enemy AI can sometimes absolutely fold the out of you by hyper-focusing specific parts of your mech, or instead shoot whatever and do no significant damage. It's incredibly inconsistent and can be very obnoxious.

There's a story mode but I've never played it, I play career. Battletech has a been around for a while and does have a pretty badass established universe like WH40K and what not, so it's definitely worth looking up lore videos or the sarna wiki if you want to get into that.

The graphics? The game looks good. Environments are cool. The weather even affects gameplay like sensors and heat management (don't bring a mainly laser weapon mech to a volcanic world unless you want to suffer).

Ok now I'm just gonna start talking about the game (ignoring mods as I think I've said everything that needs to be said.)

Your operator is impressively useless. Enemies are in the area? Reinforcements are approaching? Where? What direction? To my south? North maybe? Not gonna tell me? Why the would you tell me only enemies in the area and not a general direction? I've talked so much about the operator being useless I don't actually have any idea if its because of lore or not.

This games spawn system is appallingly retarded. Behold, lance of 4 slow ass assault mechs. Giant map. Guess where the dropship drops you? On the opposite side of the map. Have fun literally walking for almost 5 minute straight for a 2 minute fight. It'll either be that or you'll be dropped right in front of the enemies and literally be shot in the face while loading in. Bonus points if the mission doesn't actually bring you in via dropship, and you just load in on foot, so while the black screen clears and the camera gives you control of your mech, you're getting shot in the face.

I feel like ballistic weapons are outclassed pretty hard by laser weapons until you start getting towards RACs, UACs, and Gauss rifles. The AC2s-AC20s are okay I guess. Slamming lead into things is a lot more nueron engaging for me so this is just nitpicking. LRMs (long range missiles) aren't the most fun to use in this game because they have a minimum range you must be at to fire them so, in a game where enemies are facetank-mcbroadfacing you, this can get pretty obnoxious. That said, they're still more satisfying to use than lasers. The only laser weapon that really makes you feel like you just did damage by shooting it is the PPC and therefore it gets a pass. The other laser weapons are there I guess. They do good precision damage and are very easy to use so you'll always end up having them around.

The combat doesn't really challenge your ability to think outside of basic self preservation skills like not standing in the open bent over with your pants down. Your enemies do the same human wave of throw themselves at you and really, this makes playing lighter tonnage mechs a pain in the ass. Higher difficulty missions == higher tonnage lance required. As someone who likes playing assault mechs this doesn't bother me that much but who's to say your friends want to do nothing but 400 ton lances for 3-4 hours straight?

Eh, buy the game on sale. Download some mods. Bring friends. I bought it at full price and still play it and almost have and will eventually break 200 hours on it.\t\t\t\t\t
