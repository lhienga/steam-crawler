\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNGL this is the best mechwarrior game i've played in years.
Having not played since the era of Mechwarrior 1 and 2 its been a bit of a SOLID ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ MINUTE, i dipped my toe back in with Mech warrior online and liked what i got to experience (mostly) and after a time finally picked up MW5 and i gotta say...its fun.

its really nice to properly explore the inner sphere, to see the different systems even if aburdly slowly and at a time. its nice to see a news feed slowly detail new mechs and events, its fun to have a solid date to events and even feel like you participated in some of said events. its cool.

combat is fun, an AC20 actually feels like a real big slap stick against people you can reliably hit the cockpit of, unliike in online where a cockpit shot takes either 1 shot or 12 depending on ping, i feel like a actual mechwarrior in this.

until you get to a certain point. once you hit rank 9 or so in the difficulty tier things take a noticeable uptick in difficulty. your ally AI is partly to blame on this as they will never utilize their mech at its truly ideal distance, give any of them a LRM and you'll see what i mean. Its fine when they have one as a backup but if they are Trebuchet or something its so noticeable that part of your lance is doing ♥♥♥♥ all to help you, requiring constant micro managing to make them even sort of useful.

fortunately if you have actual frineds this becomes less of a issue thanks to Coop leting you get a living breathing person to control said support mech instead of a brainless Ai.

that said the bulk of the game is still great and if you're creative with your kills and your lance you'll survive just fine. Some of the DLC is worth your time others aren't and you can't readily tell this by looking at its review average.
The DLC that adds melee weapons is worth it even its score says other wise for example meanwhile "Heros of the inner sphere DLC is good but doesn't really add to your basic campaign. they're a mixed bag but mostly good.

MW5 is really good if you are a fan alredy of the setting and like what the modern MW experience can bring you. If you're unsure give Mech warrior online a try, its free and is bsically the same game with extra steps and a purely PVP tilt.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has the most excruciatingly boring and drawn out tutorial with such hits as "move your mouse to look around," and "press w to move forward." It also loves to interrupt your mech battles with exceedingly uninteresting and overly verbose characters and plots that prevent you from actually playing the game for a long time.

However, with just a few mods that add for example: up to 8x zoom to allow your sniper weapons to actually function at their effective ranges, thermal vision to see through the intense smoke and debris that combat and the improved effects from WARFX, more interesting and varied maps from vonBiomes combined with rain and mud visuals on Cockpit Glass, and finally some AI tweaks from TTRulez that turn enemy lances from braindead suicidal conga lines, into devastatingly clever tactical units; the game becomes an absolutely unparalleled mech-on-mech-on-tank-on-VTOL combat experience.

Unfortunately, the mech outfitting isn't terribly sophisticated or varied, although I am sure that can be corrected by mods as well (or you can just play Mech Engineer if you want something really, really, granular); but that stuff is very much secondary to the time spent in mission blowing the hell out of enemy lances.

Overall, if you're willing to install at least the aforementioned mods, the game is a straightforward fun piece of software with loads of unique features and gameplay experiences that can be enjoyed in up to 4 PLAYER CO-OP.\t\t\t\t\t
