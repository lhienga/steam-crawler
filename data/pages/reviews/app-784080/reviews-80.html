\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechwarrior 5 has seriously sucked me back into Battletech after years away from the franchise. Flying around the Inner Sphere collecting and customizing 'mechs is immensely satisfying, and the gameplay loop is surprisingly addictive. I must add that the Heroes of the Inner Sphere DLC is practically mandatory, as the Career mode is the real meat and potatoes of the game. The Campaign is decent, but it uses a map with a "fixed" path around the Inner Sphere that severely limits your options regarding jobs and reputations. The completely open-ended gameplay of Career mode is the true MW5 experience, and I highly recommend it.

I've noticed quite a few negative reviews complaining about difficulty spikes and such. It's true that MW5 can be quite challenging if you don't know how to properly build 'mechs and pick your battles. 80% of your success in battle is achieved in the Mechlab. A good player can compensate for the flaws of a bad 'mech, but a well-designed 'mech can carry a bad player (or the AI) even harder. Never use stock builds, they almost universally suck. They make sense in the lore, but are terrible for gameplay. Research how to optimize 'mechs and know your limits in regards to which types of missions you can handle with the 'mechs you have.

And for the love of Blake, learn how the missions work. Don't mess around in an assassination or demolition/scorched earth mission, they are literally designed to punish you for overstaying your welcome. Those people complaining about the enemy pulling 20+ 'mechs out of nowhere? This is why. Be quick and efficient. As it turns out, an Atlas trudging along at 48 kph isn't the answer to everything, no matter what House Steiner tells you.\t\t\t\t\t
