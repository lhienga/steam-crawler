I really love this game and played well over 100 hours on a console
Really what I like about it is having a reliable big mech game where I can paint mechs and stomp on civilian infrastructure *for money*
something about this game just clicks with me

Mechwarrior 4: Mercs is probably a better game
Some people don't like the dlc questlines for the cost personally I don't care I thought they were alright because I had no expectations other than "it's more MW5" which was all I wanted
the voice over in kestral lancers is really funny

It is annoying the devs have fallen into the pattern of "new dlc quest line = 1 new mech chassis"
One of the more exciting parts of the game is discovering new ways to put together an absolute cheesemachine of a mech and it'd be nice if they added more or maybe added in new weapon types

fr tho who tf uses chem lasers

although if you play with controller you use to have a torso centering button that they replaced with melee/call to arms and call to arms is probably the weakest dlc
sure it's cool to punch mechs but I have never put a sword on a mech and felt like it was a worthwhile investment

For a first time player go to Valentina as soon as you can travel you get a free mech it's above where the campaign starts you
I would recommend starting a save in campaign and if you don't have a lot of upgrades or decent mechs with you get the invitations for the kestral lancers or rasalhague just hold off and import your save to career later
don't get too attached to your pilots because there's only maybe 3-4 voices per gender so just pick the voice lines that are the least grating or that you enjoy and be on the lookout for pilots with that voice actor of higher level capacity
honestly if you find someone who maxes out at 50 something that's good enough

It took the developers years to figure out how encounter design works and artillery is the worst addition to the game, pretty much if there's artillery in a mission you can't do the mission until you destroy it
it just targets you no one else
if you're doing a high level base defense contract and there's artillery just load to before you took the mission
the game is very generous with autosaves
The early game as in the first few missions are probably the hardest in the game simply because you have no upgrades or any component above tier 1
The campaign is very much *who cares* some of the later missions are alright and you get a unique mech for completing the campaign

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlay it with mods. If you're looking for a fix after having played MW4: Mercs years ago, this game can scratch that itch perfectly with mods.

The core gameplay loop is as follows:

Pick a mission > Pilot large stompy mech into battle > Get salvage > Get paid > Repair/Upgrade/Replace mechs > Repeat.

I'd strongly recommend the YAML series of mods, as well as the "Rebalanced and Expanded Weapons" mod. They add a good deal of variety in your ability to make builds.

Here are a couple of the mech builds I've made so far:
- 80 ton Luchador Charger which flies at the enemy and punches their core out
- 30 ton Speedy Firestarter which runs around and melts enemy mechs with flamethrowers which can cause enemy mechs to overheat and shutdown
- 4 100 ton King Crabs, one shoots volleys of lightning from afar, one which can almost continuously fire mid-damage ballistic rounds at range, one which has 4 sniper cannons in its claws, and one which has a near comical amount of rockets mounted on its back.
- A 100 ton Annihilator named "Lil Annie" which moves at a comically slow pace but carries two enormously long range heavy artillery cannons in both its shoulders. It can only fire twice before overheating and shutting down, but landing a volley from both barrels on any target will not only destroy it, but remove any trace of it from existence. All parts will be destroyed. You will not get any salvage, but it will be funny.
- A 75 ton Archer packed to the brim with short range missiles which will shred any target that manages to get within their relatively murder radius.
- 85 ton Stalker running a truly comical number of long range seeking missiles. Send your buddy in ahead to scout, and if they can see a target, you can lock on from behind a hill and rain a continuous stream of missile fire on them until they eject.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game will appeal to you based on your expectations. If you are just a fan of Mechwarrior 2 and up and want to play for nostalgia then you will enjoy this outing. Now, if you are a die-hard Battletech fan then not so fast, you will need to install some mods first and thankfully the makers have provided that feature!
The good - it tries to introduce you to the mechanics and keeps things open for you to lookup the specifics.
The bad - if you didn't like Mechwarrior 4 then you are going to have to make good use of the readily available mods in this game.
The ugly - the mission variety in Vanilla is limited. The Mechlab is limited. The AI is also a bit lacking. Both the Mechlab and music remind me of Mechwarrior 4.
Mind you, this game is highly mod-able which allows the community to tweak, if not, outright overhaul the issues most would have and most importantly, it's ridiculously easy!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo not play if you wish to use a joystick.

Despite listing joystick support, this game lacks one of the most obvious and important parts of using a joystick: cycle active weapon group.
That's right. Unless your joystick has 5 trigger buttons, or a trigger button and 4 other buttons you can map with your thumb.. you cannot change what the pull of your trigger does without a cumbersome in-game mapping feature, or pressing esc to enter the menu.

That this is 2023 and one of the most basic and obvious button maps does not exist blows my mind. I couldn't even make it past the tutorial because of how frustrating it was not to swap what my button did. Seriously, Piranha - fix this. You've launched 4 DLCs and not one has corrected this most basic of oversights.\t\t\t\t\t
