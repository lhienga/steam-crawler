\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have not, in the past, given much thought to the mechwarrior/battletech franchises. I don't really care about big robots or mecha stuff. My co-op group picked this up so I gave it a whirl. At first I was just enjoying it as something like an FPS with a different skin, just give me a robot and let me at 'em, that kind of thing.

The real meat though is in the modification of the Mechs which kind of turns this game into a RPG merc company sim where you marry brains (pilots) and bodies (mechs) to make 4 combatants on the field. Pilots have basic skills they improve through use. Mechs are super customizable, and there are also scores of them, with some clearly better than others. You will no doubt spend a lot of time trying to acquire the best Mechs and the best equipment for those Mechs. The pilots are basically just a collection of voiced lines with skill numbers, so don't expect cool dialogue trees or companion quests.

There is a campaign, which serves as an excuse for you to wander the galaxy taking contracts with a plot leading you along. The career mode is basically campaign mode, but it jettisons the plot entirely and you are free to roam the galaxy and take up missions however you desire. Most missions on the map are procedurally generated, but there are still plenty that have been hand-crafted by the designers, and those usually come with mini-storylines.

Turns out I like RPG merc company sims, so I ended up liking this game a lot. The FPS thing turns out to be a nice touch, a good way to punch way above your (literal) weight and headshot mech after mech in a fight you would, on paper, have no business winning. The expansions provide a ton of extra content, so if you find yourself into the game you can buy them for more things to do and acquire - two of them give you campaign-style mission chains that come with FMVs and voice acting.

This game also has co-op, and I am a firm believer in co-op making everything better. This is no exception. The only drawback to co-op is that the host is the actual leader of the mercenary company, so they are the only ones that can shop, hire new pilots, fly the ship, and pick up missions. The other players can of course pilot Mechs in battle, but they can also still tinker with the Mechs the same way you could in your game, so they have things to do during downtime. The co-op is very robust - if you are playing a co-op game, your friends can just drop right in even if you are mid-mission, and people can come and go all the time without the game being affected at all.

I feel like this franchise has a big fan base. The following three points get mentioned a lot in their reviews:
- Bad AI. I did not find the AI so mind numbingly awful as some of the other reviewers - mechs will get stuck on terrain on occasion, but you can always go back and order them off of it, or take control of them in a pinch to maneuver them yourself. They fight other mechs just fine, especially if you pick them a target.
- Need to love Battletech. I was actively disinterested in this franchise before I picked this game up so I straight up don't agree with that. This was my entry point into the series and I think it's a good one since they have apparently rebooted the timeline and this is the very beginning. The game will give you a lot of lore about this time period if you care to read the events that pop up during your career.
- Required mods. Have never used a single mod. Obviously I thought it was very good without them. Perfectly playable and tons of fun. Did I mention the xpacs were great?

So yes, overall, all the thumbs up, highly recommended. One of those games I have been very surprised to enjoy as much as I have. Maybe that'll happen to you too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChecked out after several hours of singleplayer. The AI "team" needs constant orders and micromanagement (and even whines at you for orders when there's a bunch things to fire at.) Four? repeating mission types in slightly varied landscape was quite obvious from the outset as well.

Your team does fire and manage their own heat, but have no concept of cover, or turning the mech to shield a damaged side, and happily plough through buildings that should be used as ablative concrete. Despite having mechs similar tonnage to mine, I don't even think they managed to kill anything other than a vehicle - it was always my firepower that downed the enemy.

I played through MW2, MW2:M and MW3, and the AI back then was plain stupid, but that's 20 year old AI (I still miss my Nova that could strafe using jumpjets.) To micromanage my way through singleplayer is a definite "no thanks" to a game and sadly, MW isn't a game my friends go for.

Needs better AI, more varied missions and larger battle zones. Also skip the deployment sequence. Seen it once, don't want to waste 30s every mission on it. On the plus side, it is an arcade game, and relatively easy to get to grips with.\t\t\t\t\t
