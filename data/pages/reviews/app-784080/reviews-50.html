\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSolid entry into the Mechwarrior universe.

About the only thing I dislike is how locked in each mech is. While the number of variants and their potential loadouts are quite variable, sometimes there just isn't one that fits the bill for how I want to set it up, so concessions have to be made.

Some of the difficulty ratings are WAY out to lunch with what the actual mission presents. Some can be super easy when their difficulty is rated high, and some are just a face roll for the NPCs when the difficulty rating says otherwise.

Lots of content though. The DLCs add a lot of new mechs and weapons to play with and the patches over time have made lots of QoL improvements. The number of maps are pretty decent but can start to look similar after enough time. I do enjoy a lot of the unique mission maps where the layouts are vastly different from the standard ones.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm 30 hours in and incredibly disappointed by the lack of story. I played 2k hours of MWO (realizing my 2016 recordings are identical to current gameplay + extra mechs and a few maps) Growing up with Mechassault (not mechwarrior) I thought a single player campaign game would be a nice changeup, and it's in unreal engine so it should look cool too. Battletech was cool but I wanted to experience a story with my MWO skillset.

There's zero story in this game. An early cutscene about your fathers death who you're basically not attached to at all, and now this is your whole plot motivation. There's two NPC's you "interact" with, one for some reason has a heavy aussie accent and has an amusingly low poly skull, the other gives you some mission briefings. Both might as well be statues, they never move, you can't interact with them, they don't even follow you with their eyes. There's no additional cutscenes in the time I played, some voice acted hologram updates, any side missions you have to read for the details. I don't really know that much about the factions/houses and it doesn't seem to matter who I side with or piss off.

This game feels like an AI mode in MWO, while at the same time not having any of the established MWO things that you'll have to relearn like the mechbay menus and control settings. The graphics are mediocre, somehow not even surpassing their 10 year old game. The cockpit view is somehow worse. The commands you can issue to your squad are basic and tedious.

The core of the gameplay seems to be almost a complete rip of HBS's Battletech, with the ship, monthly bills, fast travel using the starmap, repairs, etc. Except now you can wander around the mechbay as a floating camera with no legs or body. The modeling of the ship is pretty cool, so it's a shame there's not much reason to explore it.

I don't see any reason to keep playing, it seems the mechs just get bigger. I've repeated the various handful of mission types already and enjoyed the Hunchbacks. It's obvious the story is not going to hook me. I'm barely learning anything about the lore. Out of all the Battletech books and ♥♥♥♥ it's really confusing how weak this is. If I was interested in meaningless battles I'd go back to MWO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game at its core is absolutely worth buying. This is what AAA games ought to strive for. Right out of the box, you have decent Mech customization and the Singleplayer story is very much worthwhile. I appreciate the fact that you can get people to join your Singleplayer game by loading the file in the multiplayer menu. The music is wonderful and I'd love to get a copy of the soundtrack to listen to on occasion.

The single player story is wonderful and ties in well to the lore, which I recommend delving into while playing, especially if you're not doing something story critical. YouTube channels I highly recommend for lore dives: Big Red 40 Tech (for technical delves into individual mechs, some lore, and even exposure to the TTG) and Sven van der Plank (for raw lore). The lore of this franchise is rich and certainly worthwhile.

Pay attention to the news crawl on the bottom of the screen when playing or in mission selection.

While the game is wonderful on its own, it's best with mods, especially the ones in the Yet Another series. YAML (Yet Another MechLab) is an absolute must as it drastically improves the customization of your Mechs. Armor Green and Colorized Heat Gauge will make it easier to track the status of Mechs (yours and your targets). Mod Options to have a tighter control over how the mods behave, MechAssault Mechs will bring in the heavy metal found in that spinoff. Finally, Yet Another Clan Invasion to add Clan Era mechs, the Clan faction and some absolutely worthwhile parts; this particular one is not only large (2GB download at time of writing), but is practically its own DLC. No Vortex required, you can mix Workshop mods and Nexus mods and it's easy to mod the game. Furthermore, the devs do a wonderful job supporting their modding community. When Rise of Rasalhauge dropped, most of the big mods (especially of the Yet Another series) had updates within 48 hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo not bother. The missions are highly repetitive, the enemies spawn in on top of you and sometimes in absolutely absurd overwhelming numbers. It is a pretty, but poorly scripted game with very little content and obvious, frustrating and quite frankly lazy pacing mechanisms, the most annoying of which are upkeep and repair costs which really don't enhance or provide any real experience or consequences other than limiting where and what you can do. There are far better ways to push players into desired zones, interactions and mission lines - but nope, they chose the old fashioned monthly payment/mech upkeep cost to 'pretend' like we're doing immersion. Absolute garbage.

The AI is stunningly awful, like actually funny if it weren't screwing up your plans constantly. You cannot rely on your lance to do anything intelligent on their own, they must be micromanaged almost 24/7 to be effective - all the while opposition AI plays a heavy numbers game and tries to overwhelm your lance with enemy spawns and/or specifically ONLY targeting the player even when the rest of the lance is clearly doing more damage. It's absolutely a shame how a game in 2021 like this gets so many basic things wrong.\t\t\t\t\t
