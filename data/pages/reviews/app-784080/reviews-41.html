\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a recommend. However, to make it a good Mechwarrior game you're going to need a lot of mods. And unfortunately for PGI every update breaks a massive amount of mods every time they release a new DLC. If the game is played vanilla without mods, it's a mediocre game and a bad mechwarrior game. Also unfortunately for again for PGI a lot of the DLC does not add a lot more to the game. And again a lot of those DLCs have to be played at specific timelines in the career mode or campaign or you will lose access to them in that playthrough.

I say all of this because the only thing that has kept me playing this game a lot are the amount of mods that add so much to the game that should have been in it from the start. Mechlabs, more mechs, more mech variants, more biomes, more mission types, etc. are all on the menu for it. I myself use a lot of mods (*cough* over 50) and it has me in an addictive loop for quite a while now. But I am also an avid Battletech/Mechwarrior nerd so keep this in mind when taking my advice.

I will say however the combat in this is fantastic, some of the best in the series. Melee being added in the Call to Arms update made this even more fun which was unheard of in the Mechwarrior series but in the lore happened quite a bit which is a great add-on. WITH MODS (Another Mechlab Mod being the best of the best and most recommend), you can customize your mech to insane details, such as engines, armor, heat sink kits, targeting systems, and so many more things. Another add-on mod for this adds in weapons from Inner Sphere and even Clan allowing for even more customization of your big stompy boys.

With mods I do recommend this game as it adds a lot of replay-ability with entertaining combat. I would definitely recommend getting it (and DLC if you wish) on sale.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPaperWarrior, formerly known as MechWarrior, left me feeling underwhelmed and disappointed. In a game where you pilot giant mechs, I expected an immersive experience that would evoke memories of the classic MW4. Unfortunately, it fell short of those expectations in several ways.

First and foremost, the game's balance issues are glaring. It's frustrating to see even the most basic tanks pose a significant threat to your mighty mech. Survival often requires a constant, hectic dance, leaving little room for the tactical, methodical gameplay that MechWarrior veterans might have hoped for.

One of the most significant letdowns is the control scheme. Gone are the days of immersive joystick and HOTAS setups; instead, we're left with a dumbed-down mouse and keyboard control system. This shift robs the game of the authentic mech-piloting feel that enthusiasts crave.

In the end, PaperWarrior feels like an egoshooter with a mech suit skin, far removed from the cherished MechWarrior series. The nostalgic experience that fans were hoping for remains elusive.

To me, PaperWarrior doesn't capture the essence of the MechWarrior series, and it's been a bit of a letdown. This is just my personal perspective, but I hope the developers will consider what made the previous games great and strive to recapture that magic for fans like me.
