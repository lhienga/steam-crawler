\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am old school - I played the original mercenaries when it came out in the 90s and it was way more engaging than this - the music, the sound everything was spot on. I've played the online version and it was better than this - go figure. This latest installment makes it look pretty, but is super shallow from there. There's some annoying gameplay mechanics like having to run up and talk to Rayna when it would have been easier if they started you there, she talks too much and during missions, The story kinda blows and isn't engaging at all. The music isn't varied either - just heavy guitar and that's it - not environmental or anything. Better settle on your unit's battle flag - cuz you can't change it. It's very rudimentary when it comes to kitting out you're mech too. No engines, very rigid as to where you can place equipment like masc or Electronic warfare components etc. Weapons get pretty boring over time and drop rate of the more rare stuff is sparse at best. Enter the MODS - it makes the game fun to play - without them, I'd just uninstall and lament the money spent and how I should of paid more attention to all the reviews that warned me. But beware, if you have too many - they can break your game and will get out of date quickly due to patches from PGI. If your a clan-er the Mods are you best friends - because the base game is completely devoid of clan mechs.

I'd buy it on sale - it's not worth 30 bucks; Ditch the DLC and just play the mods.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was introduced to this game, and the Battletech universe by friends recently. I honestly felt like i had been sleeping on a great title.

Gameplay:

This game on its own is a fantastic time. The beginning of most campaigns can be a little rough until you figure out how to play, but there's plenty of variety to the gameplay loop and plenty of mods to help expand your experience. The DLC's and quest missions are the highlights however, make sure to chase them down in the galaxy map once you've collected a few mechs.

Issues:

The biggest con i can think of is the teammate ai when playing without three other players. They are pretty dumb when left alone, but giving them mechs with good heat management, full armor, and using the command functions can help a lot.

Assassination and other mech kill missions early on can break your bank and kill your campaigns. Stick to defense, raid, and demolition missions for the most part until you've got a few mechs in your hangar. (AC20 Trashcans will ruin your day)

Multiplayer:

If you try this game and like it try to have some friends, or even just one jump in with you on the four player co-op. Friends can switch in and out without locking out play-throughs, design their own mechs and ride around with you as you progress. Some reviews i read say this game isn't true co-op, but i would say it is. The campaign map is sort of like a car pool though so somebody is going to have to 'drive', but important part is being able to play together and customize mechs.

Taking on challenging missions and the chaotic missions of the dlcs can make for some amazing moments with friends!

Summary:

This title has a fantastic campaign map and provides an old school self guided and open ended experience that's uncommon in the recent era of games. I do recommend getting the dlcs and doing the story missions however. Some of them are just crazy and the story itself is complex and representative of Battletech's long history. Mods are a great addition and the game works well with them (Check out the YAML stuff on the workshop :] Very fun additions). Friends can really help jump start a campaign, but they aren't required. The game isn't exactly easy and provides a good challenge with the option to continue challenging yourself or to take it easy (until the dlc's come knocking at least--be ready for them they're a great time, but you'll need mechs).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really great sandbox FPS/RPG + mercenary business simulator that is really brought to life by the hard work of the modding community.

The game has a deceptively steep learning curve for people like me who aren't familiar with MechWarrior games. If you can't wrap your head around the "tank" style controls and find you are getting destroyed in every mission and can't kill anything, don't let it ruin this awesome game. Set the difficulty to Easy and switch to FPS controls and go commit some war crimes in the name of the almighty C-Bil! You'll have a much better time and will quickly find you want to bump the difficulty back up as you figure out how the outfitting and damage systems work.

Also, the game really shines in coop (especially with the "Co-Op Unlocked" and "Co-Op Rewards" mods on Nexus Mods) and for someone like me with unpredictable schedules, the short mission structure is very compatible with my group's limited and unpredictable playtime. Pop in, crush some rival mercs for 15 to 20 minutes, get some cash and loot, get back to real life.

Super bonus, ONLY THE HOST NEEDS THE DLC! I just gifted the base game on sale to some pseudo-interested friends and every now and then they pop in to help me in my main campaign save. Highly recommended if you think the game play looks interesting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like MWO (Mechwarrior Online) Then you will enjoy this. MW5 Is pretty much MWO but an offline mission based version of MWO. It is very much like its predecessors from Microsoft. Its also nice for those of us that were banned from MWO due to unscrupulous players making false claims which sadly PGI cannot even be bothered to look into and just take people at their word.

I do not fault PGI for this as I doubt they have the manpower to investigate every claim, but it would have been nice. Still I find that they still make an excellent game and enjoy MW5 as much as I enjoyed MWO.

For those playing MWO, be warned, some players will report you for pretty much anything they can imagine when they feel like someone may have cheated against them. I'm fairly certain that the person who got me banned was the guy i got a lucky headshot on when he peeked over a hill and then resorted to contacting PGI and made wild claims about me threatening to kill them, but whatever.

Back to the subject on hand, Plenty of big stompy robots from the franchise to pilot, with more being added with each DLC released in MWO (I understand the clans are coming next) with some modders even bringing some over (YAML) to use. Lots of different biomes/environments to fight opponents . It does lack some features from old games such as crouching or getting knocked down, but its just as well as the AI is pretty straightforward and pretty much only uses 1 tactic which is attack. The AI really has no tactics so don't expect "Real" ambushes or 1 on 1 duels (unless you order your lance to stand somewhere while you run off to solo them). Some fight can get intense though and PGI has made many improvements such as enemies not spawning on you (yeah, it was a thing at launch, but that got fixed) and the lance AI pathing isn't bad now, could be a little better, but if/when your lance mates get stuck, you can now just swap to that unit and get them unstuck yourself.

MW5 is certainly worth picking up, though some of you may want to wait for sales, but its a fun time killer regardless of your financial budget.\t\t\t\t\t
