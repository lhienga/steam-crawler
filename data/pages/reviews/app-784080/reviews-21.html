\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is the epitome of making you feel like an absolute badass. There's tons (no pun intended) of things to do in the game with tons of loadouts and mechs to stomp around and annihilate every single thing around you. PGI has done an outstanding job taking what they built through MWO and making a single player Mech Warrior game from it. Planets and missions are procedurally generated (the ones not story related), so there's an unlimited amount of content here to sink your teeth into. The campaigns introduce some really challenging segments that get you out of your comfort zone by totally destorying your fleet and pilots, forcing you to plan ahead and make the best out of bad situations.

The music is top-notch too, there's something about stomping around and blowing up things with some industrial-metal sound track over it all that's the icing on the cake. Also, I think this is the first game that's ever allowed you to walk around close to your mechs and admire them from upclose. The amount of detail in each mech is really impressive and immerses you even more. Get the game; it's amazing. Easy 9/10\t\t\t\t\t

1) The game feels empty without the DLC content. Don't buy the base without the DLC pack. Between the world map additions and the extra environs and minor variations to the mission types they are really not optional.
2) I love mechs.
3) The lance mate AI is pretty bad. They all hang about 50m from you and run in front of you a lot. Long range mechs will not hang back, so all builds must focus on mid range.
4) The story lines and writing are really bad, was there a writers strike on? If not, they should have used chat GPT instead of whomever they paid to write this.
5) Mechs stomping through cities and buildings is awesome.
6) This game didn't need much more to be fantastic, but fell short in a lot of places. If you can get the base game and 4 DLC pack at 50% off or more and love Mechs, I recommend. At full price, no.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would place this game firmly in the 'OK' category. Not really a must-play, but it's fine for the sale price I picked it up at. I would be very disappointed if I had paid 60 bucks for this on release, even 30 seemed to high for what it's priced at now.

Here's an unorganized list of the good and bad:

Good:
  • Shooting at and stomping on things are great, ballistic weapons and PPCs have some real oomph to them. The lasers and Gauss Rifle are just okay though.
  • Mechs look good, I am a fan of most of PGI's mech redesigns. They've done alright there.
  • Occasionally an enemy will ragdoll in a spectacular manner when they die. Sometimes it's mild, like a Javelin flailing its arms about while spinning into a nearby wall, but every now and then you'll launch something into the stratosphere with SRMs, which is hilarous.

Bad:
  • Serious lack of variety in missions. The campaign is padded with a lot of grind between story missions with the same 4 objectives (assassination, warzone, base defense, raid) and you'll be doing these same missions over and over for hours.
  • No pre-drop mission info other than general objectives weather conditions, which barely matter anyway unless you run LRMs since it seems to affect target lock. The game won't give map overviews, total number of targets, or size of the deployment area, which really chafes when you take a 48kph assault lance to a map that places your objective 3km away and you have to spend over 5 minutes of walking just to get to the action.
  • Mechlab is fairly restrictive, for instance, being unable to adjust engine sizes to make mechs faster or slower. Superchargers exist for this specific grievance, but I saw a grand total of 2 in my entire campaign and they were for light and medium mechs around the time I was fielding assaults.
  • Lack of control over AI lancemates - they can be given basic commands, but MW4 had a much larger list of commands you could issue. They also have little sense of self-preservation, and will often walk their very expensive, crippled assault mech into the middle of a firefight while their other 2 lancemates are at full health with close-range weaponry and have somehow found themselves behind the guy with one missing leg and 30 LRM tubes.
  • The AI does not understand how to manage weapon ranges and you can sometimes build them to be totally useless if you're not careful. The way the AI interacts with weapon groupings is also not explained anywhere in-game, so expect to read a guide for that.
  • Story is pretty boring, but it does have somewhat unique missions - usually the locations are hand-crafted, and have some series of small objectives like demolishing a base and then fighting an enemy lance. However, you don't get very much of it. There are about a dozen story missions and you'll probably be doing 5 times that in random missions between.
  • Minor spoiler: The game has you chasing after Star-League technology (i.e. REALLY good ) for the latter half of the game, and rewards you with CRIMINALLY tiny amounts of it. You get a really cool Star-League mech for the last 2 missions, and some weapons here and there. To compare, HBS Battletech straight up GIVES you a Star-League mech halfway through the game, and the black market is a consistent place to buy great mechs and equipment that you couldn't get otherwise. MW5 lets you buy pulse lasers or ERPPCs in whatever store they're available, but expect to see them once in a blue moon.
  • Salvage shares are extremely stupid and inconsistent. Again, to compare to HBS Battletech, in that game you get 1 'piece' of a mech for each kill, earning 2 pieces for blowing off both legs, or 3 pieces for a headshot kill. Combine 3 pieces to make a complete chassis - in this way you're guaranteed to get *something* for your efforts, and seeing that rare mech enough times you can eventually piece one together. In MW5, salvage is seemingly random, no matter if you core out the mech, blow the legs off, or kill with a headshot, so no matter how you destroy it there's no guarantee you'll see that chassis in the salvage screen. I think I destroyed half a dozen Awesomes before I had the opportunity to actually claim one.

Mods could potentially fix all of these issues, but the devs shouldn't be farming out basic fixes to the community, and I shouldn't have to go hunting them down just to play a decent game.\t\t\t\t\t
