\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter the release of the last DLC I have finally given up all hope of the game being fixed. The AI of this game does not function on any meaningful level and its apparent that there will be no attempt to address it. Do to how bad the pathing finding is your lancemates cant even follow you reliably. They constantly wonder off, get stuck on terrine, take detours that make no sense or my favorite just stop moving and stare off into space. Anything short of an open field just confuses them. They also cant defend themselves, or you for that matter, they have no concept of self preservation, threat identification, focusing fire or engagement range/angles.

I'm tired of them walking into my missile barrages, or shooting me in the back with lasers because they dont understand beam duration.
I'm tired of watching my lancemates ignore actual threats to shoot at nothing or not fire at all while said threats shoot them in the back.
I'm tired of telling my LRM boat to stay at one position for fire support only for it to Leroy Jenkins into the fight on the 1st attack order (even after removing all close range weapons).
I'm tired of this game not having basic unit scripting. The AI pilots a Locust the same way it does an Atlas.
I'm tired of fighting at a 1/5 disadvantage, having to rely on cheese tactics cause the only way for the AI to be a challenge is overwhelming numbers because the AI doesn't understand the principles of the setting its representing.

I know there are basic lance controls like "move here" and "shoot this" but its no where near good enough. "Move here" but shoot at what? "Shoot this" but what range, what weapon, what body part? Mechs in Battletech have roles. Brawler, Direct and Indirect Firesupport, Scout. Not in this game, every Mech acts the same. Walks in a line towards target, fires weapons. Thats it.

I got almost 200 hours in the game. I've tried unmodded and modded campaigns. I love Battletech. I wanted to like this game, but the complete lack of any unit behavior controls just rips the heart out of it. There is fun here but this game is just a glorified shooting gallery with no depth, with other humans its probably much more fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Bug-fest piece of crap grind with virtually no plot.

Longer version:
The base game was playable, even though still plot was practically non-existing. Game is pretty much a mindless grind all the time. There are just a few mission types over and over again. Could be fun for a while to someone with nostalgia towards old Mechwarrior games. And the gameplay itself - it's like mech walking simulator with a bit of combat. I mean the maps are terrible. So much pointless walking, which is especially infuriating with heavy, slow mechs. It takes ages to get anywhere. At least half of the time spent in the cockpit is just pointless walking around.

The DLCs however, is where the real $hit starts. Game became so bugged, it's really a masochism to play it (I don't know why I'm still doing it). They appear in pretty much every single mission and consist of:
- one particular sound (laser, dropship engines, etc) loops. It remains even in game menu. Only way to get rid of it, is to restart the game, but it's pointless because in few minutes it will happen again. I just turned of the sound completely, because it was getting on my nerves.
- enemy tanks spawning below terrain, so you cannot destroy them. For some mission types it requires you to restart the mission, even if it's practically over.
- your lance mechs or enemy mechs cannot pass terrain obstacles and get stuck. If it's enemy, you need to go kilometers to hunt them down, if it's your lance, you need to play around with move orders to get it unstuck.
DLSc are introducing supposedly new mission types, but it's a joke. It's the same mission types, but with a different name, e.g. "demolition" = "scorched earth", "assassination" = "target kill", etc. There are just 2, really new mission types: "infiltration" and "beachhead".

So game definitely is not worth full price. If you enjoy grinding, go ahead and get it on a sale. If you decide to play it, here's a useful tip - do 'cantina - mech hunter' challenges asap. Level 10 will unlock an upgrade of +15% to mech max speed. It is the best upgrade of all, cause it reduces the time of mindless walking around.\t\t\t\t\t
