great concept but just slightly lacking in everything (disclaimer played without mods or DLC that im told makes the game way better but i dont want to spend anymore on this game and i shouldn't need mods to enjoy it) the game seems to be set up in a way where you just almost have enough to do what you want with your mech but never actually have the room to equip what you want to equip and so it feels like a constant cockblock. the weapons/mechs seem to be balanced to reference the bored-game (battle tech) more than for the actual video game so this causes a few issues

you will never have enough heatsinks installed causing you to just stand there with your thumb up your a$$ while you cool down after a few bursts or to sit there peeking around rocks only using 1 of your 5 weapons to make sure you dont overheat while you slowly chip away at the other mech

some mechs are useless due to there stats being set for a turn based board-game not transferring over well to a video game, the cicada is the perfect example of this its a 40ton mech that you will never make better than a 20ton mech due to not having the space to equip both weapons and armor.

you will have weapon slots that can fit other weapons you have but you will almost never have the capacity to equip them due to the limited space and those weapons taking way too much space to use for what they actually do forcing you to have useless weapons like the machine guns.

mission types all take part on square sections of map and are very repetitive,

your mechs are very soft and your AI team is pretty stupid so once in a while they well do something dumb and have your mech blow up and at that point you should just quit and reload save because the cost of fixing a destroyed mech is so great that it'll take you like 3 missions to get the money to pay for it and it will take over an hour for your mech to finish repairing plus the pilot will be injured if he lives taking you down a team member for roughly 90 ingame days

they have a crap time system they use. everything takes time so if you wanna do anything to your mech it will take multiple ingame days to finish that is changing armor balance, taking anything off or putting anything on and most of all repairs so if you get a new mech you will need time if you got it already damaged and more time to customise its equipment so you might not even get to use it for a few missions while you wait for work to be done on it.

just a lot of unnecessary messing around in this game that takes away from the action and the voice acting is just bad. the plot is just daddy died after years of being a mercenary (killing people for money mind you) so you need to get revenge by killing more people for money till you track down the one that killed your dad. (this counts killing innocent people, in one of the early missions a guy helps to save you then wants you to help raid farmers so it takes away from the whole revenge for a good man thing like at what point do you just accept that its just an occupational hazard?)

its likely better in co-op but i couldn't bring myself to get my friends to buy this game. lastly there are a lot of helpful people in the discussion forms and you are likely able to get fast response or find people in the community to team up with and though the community seems to be pretty nice be ready for lore dumps because there are a LOT of battle tech lore obsessed people in this community so if you are interested in the lore you'll make good friends real fast\t\t\t\t\t

I am getting to this review a little late. MW5 at its core is a good game that offers a lot of nostalgic feels and fun Co-Operative Play. The Cross-play works as I have linked with Console-players and those that bought off of Epic Games; all possible through the Invite-Code mechanic. The Steam Workshop and Nexus Mods website are alive and thriving as well.

I definitely recommend this game if you find it on sale for 50% off. Same for the DLCs. The only DLC I would budge on is the first one, “Heroes of the Inner Sphere”, which opens up the game in the most bang-for-your-buck way. All other DLCs add some new features, but I am not super thrilled by them. I have reviews going into more depth on the feature additions on the shop already.

The Vanilla: This game offers a decent campaign and experience. Beyond that, anyone that wants more customization or to tinker more with builds will find the vanilla-experience severely lacking. Vanilla offers a bit of a grind, a lot of stomping and shooting. Nothing more.

The necessity of Modding: Modding is super easy, takes maybe 10 minutes to pull files and extract once you know what you want to use. I have 20-some active mods currently. Many are cosmetic, some add some features, but more importantly some change the game experience.

If you are looking to start a new game, want a bit more difficulty, and want a more robust experience in your game; I whole-heartedly recommend these CORE-GAME Mods and where to find them:
Yet Another Mech Lab (“YAML") – Steam Workshop (by: Cszolee79)
Yet Another Weapon – Steam Workshop (by: Cszolee79)
Yet Another Weapon Clan – Steam Workshop (by: Cszolee79)
CoyotesMission – Nexus Mods (by: coyotekins)
PilotOverhaul – Steam Workshop (by: Wpnx330)

You will likely need the ‘MW5 Compatibility Pack’( – Steam Workshop) and ‘Mod Options’ (-Nexus Mods) to make them work.

These mods offer variability of missions that break the cycle, in-depth customization of mechs, more equipment options, a lot more build freedom, and pilots you will actually care about.

I will only play Vanilla if someone wants me to join them and they want the dev-intended experience first.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed BattleTech as a kid many many moons ago.
Took a long break and then had my arm twisted into joining a co-op group playing MW5.
Had a blast.
So I bought all the DLC and played all the solo campaigns. Great, well written, if a little short, but that's just the beginning...

Where the game has legs is with the right set of Mods building a long term career, having fiends drop in and out of your Merc Lance (Though TTrulezAI might break your Big Stompy Robots less than them!).

If you love Battle Tech, this is the best there is - though sadly no PvP or Multi-Player lance v lance battles. Great PvE Experiences though.

So if Big Stompy Robots and hours of tweaking in your MechLab (YAML ftw), beautiful environments (vonBiomes is a must), extended mission types (Coyote's Mission Pack) or even going for glory in Solaris 7 (vonMissions - Highly recommended) are appealing - get it. You'll have a ton of fun - even better, it's gloriously free of Micro Transactions!

9/10 Recommended (With Mods. Without, it's a much duller experience. Get them, you won't regret it)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was honestly positevly surprised by this title, especially after reading some of the reviews. I have played Mechwarrior 1-4 and a couple of similar games in the past.

Maybe first the negative aspects:
- AI is dump, especially my lancemates it seems. They prefer to run around like headless chickens, get stuck everywhere instead of actually helping me. Enemy AI on the other hand seems to be incredibly focused on me, to the point where they have literally pushed though the mechs of my lance to get to me. This usually reasults in my mech taking the most damage in the lance. I just bought one of the DLCs that supposedly fixes some of the AI issues, so we will see.
- Especially in MW4 weatherfactors matter a lot more, be it heat (you could cool of faster on an icy battlefield) or visibility. Both thinks mostly dont matter anymore. Also the punishment of overheating your mech seem to be far lower than before (like amunition explosions), and the overriding emergency shutdown mechanic felt a lot better too
- You cant shoot of a mechs legs, well yes you can, but not really. Even in earlier Mechwarrior titles you could shoot off a leg (visibly), immobelizing an opponent. Now you cant do that. Destroying a leg will just slow a mech down, you need to blow up both legs to autodestroy it. I mean really, cant we have just a "mech on the ground" animation by now?

Positive aspects:
- Weapons seem to be better ballanced than in the past. I have fun with lasers, missiles, ballistic weapons and have a good feeling using them in terms of power.
- I like that you cant completly change a mechs weaponloadout, but still can modify it within some set guidelines. After all, inner sphere mechs are no Omni Mechs
- I really liked the story campaign, although i had my suspicions concerning the twist at the end, since i have read my fair share of MW backgroundstorys and novels :-)

I think that covers the most of it, all in all i can recommend the game for any mech fighting enthusiast :-)\t\t\t\t\t
