\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy issue with this game is basically that while the game presents itself and people talk about it as a snappy action game, the reality is that its moreso a PMC simulator where the battles are really won before you even start the mission.

The combat just really isn't all that satisfying when its really just a numbers game. Sure you can work the odds into your favor a little bit through positioning and baiting, but in a fight between, say, a locust and a centurion, the centurion is going to obliterate the locust every time. Light, medium, and heavy mechs aren't classes like in other games. Light mechs are garbage that exist to die, mediums are actually usable, and while I didn't make it far enough to see heavy mechs, all of the footage I've seen of people actually having fun with this game are where they are piloting heavies and destroying other mechs effortlessly, so I can only assume they are pretty OP and terrifying to fight against. This is definitely just a personal thing, but I really don't find this kind of dynamic satisfying in a real time game like this. I'm much more a fan of games where you can take a bad situation and turn it into a victory, but here you can basically predict if you are going to win as soon as you see what you are up against. Trying to use fancy maneuvers (if that's even possible considering you are basically piloting a brick with legs) mostly does nothing except mess with your accuracy. I was interested in jump jets for a solid 5 seconds before realizing that using them induces fall damage to your legs. If nothing else, a "briefing" mechanic where you can get a quick look at the battlefield before you deploy to see the mission area and the enemies already there Ace Combat style would be pretty cool and I don't think it would mess with the game that much. You can check any possible enemy mech spawns for missions in an area but that's basically just so you can loot hunt.

That's basically my big issue with the game, I enjoyed most other aspects of it but those aspects werent enough to keep me going and they weren't the main gameplay. I guess if I had any other suggestions I think it would be cool if you could spend a bunch of C-bills to change a mech's variant/loadout to another since I think that would greatly enhance customization, because otherwise the things you can do to actually customize a mech (that don't involve making it completely worthless in combat) are pretty limited.

Maybe I can be convinced to give it another shot, but I think if I want to get into Battletech I'll check out one of the other games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a little bit of a mix for me, i overall give it a positive review but if i could give it a score out of ten i would give it a 7, not amazing, not terrible.

Positive: you are in a big stompy 'mech that can shoot shells the height of you, and you shoot other big stompy 'mechs.

Negative: the game gets repetitive quite quickly, the missions are all basically the same and the 'mech customization is nothing compared to other battletech games such as mechwarrior online or BATTLETECH.

Positive(?): the base game campaign is... fine, i didn't hate it and the custom missions were fairly fun, however its nothing to write home about.

Positive: mods are the only thing keeping this game from getting deserted, mods such as the YAML (yet another mech lab lets you customize your battlemech much more freely than the base game, i would highly recommend it as your first mod) line of mods and many new 'mech chassis.

Negative: this game is poorly optimized, there are mods that help a little with it but if you don't have a fairly good computer then i would skip buying it.

Positive: if you plan on buying this game, get it on sale, it goes on sale fairly frequently at 50% off.

Negative: the AI is not the brightest bunch by a long shot.

overall its a good game for a little bit, held up by the modding community, but i do highly recommend getting it on discount, its not worth $30.

(the campaign, in my experience takes about 40 hours, but you can probably do it in 20)\t\t\t\t\t

2. Smooth handling of the mechs, and easy controls
3. The DLC missions are worth playing
4. Melee combat is worth it

And the bad, which is ALOT:

3. The main campaign is REAL BORING. Bad voice acting, bad script, bad bad bad

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI came to this game late, well after release, and IMO it is very decent and quite polished.

The game in general looks pretty, sounds good, and the mechs handle well. The combat system and things like targeting / hitboxes / line of sight are handled pretty well.

As a PC gamer, I find most of the controls and interface to be vastly above average. They seem actually set up for mouse+keyboard play, whereas in many games I've played recently (Borderlands 2, Fallout 4 etc) the interface is crippled by a requirement for console compatibility (i.e. deeply nested interfaces with huge fonts and saturated colours, for legibility on television screens, no drag-and-drop mouse support in inventories, etc).

I've had exactly zero crashes, which is nice.

Most of the game play problems that existed at release have been fixed, I only know of them from seeing the complaints on forums.

The main remnant problem is that the lance controls could be a lot better (more detailed, more formation options), and the AI has some path finding glitches. The latter problem seems to be baked into almost every game from the last 30 years or so (e.g. the dragoons in Starcraft had worse path finding skillz) - it sure isn't unique to this title.

The downsides are:

- the randomly rolled missions can be a bit bland
- there's a fair bit of "fake difficulty" that the player has to find out about through trial and error. i.e. some mission types are hard in ways that don't make sense. IMO If a mission is going to feature implausible ♥♥♥♥ (like an inexhaustible monster closet), the game shouldn't be coy, it should directly tell you about that implausible element.
- the starmap is a bit naff. IMO if a planet nearby has a "rare" mech, you should be able to tell what it is without having to fly there. Similarly it is dumb that to recruit a pilot from a nearby (linked industrial) world, you have to fly to them, hauling all of your hundreds of tons of gear, rather than the other way around.
- the scripted missions are all over the place, and the "quest marker" system to point you toward them is rudimentary.\t\t\t\t\t
