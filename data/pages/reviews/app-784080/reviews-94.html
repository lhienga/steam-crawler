\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove this game. The game play is solid, and the story is pretty good. That said though, I think that it would be great for there to be more RTS style game play as well, like a entirely new game mode where you build up your mercenary company from just your character(Commander), to a merc company the scale of other companies like the Wolf's Dragoons, or the Eridani Light Horse.

I also love the mod support for the game, and the additional stuff that allows. I would like to have the Clan Invasion occur, with more content focusing on that being added officially into the game, maybe even adding alternative results for situations in the BattleTech universe based on your merc company being involved.

On the RTS front, I know of the game BattleTech, but it's more turn based, so I would love to see a full on RTS where events occur in real time, and you build a merc company from a single lance, to a full on army. I think that would be fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game turned out to be a childhood dream I never knew I had =)
Mechs are fun, great sounds, damage/destruction mechanics and kills are very satisfying.
I like the way achievements are earned in this game, where you see the progress until it is achieved.

It was an 'Impulse purchase' but I have absolutely no regrets.
I am no good at it atm - and I do not understand a lot - but I do want to get better at it - unlike other games that I either refund or uninstall and never play again.

Some of my learning by doing wrong on YouTube, I've kept some failed attempts to remember the pain =) :
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=COR8xECunhc

I highly recommend watching YouTuber: 'Baradul' - to see quality playthrough.

Some quick tips: learn keyboard shortcuts:
R – Target Enemy
X – Full stop
F1 – Command to Lance/ Attack my Target
B - Show Battle Grid

I hear it is even better with mods, but since it is my first MechWarrior game I had no expectations and so far I quite like it.

P.S. MECHS ARE NOT ROBOTS! =)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnlike older Mechwarrior games, this one doesn't really tailor a story as much as it acts like an RNG game with a small sniff of story that's at the very start and is never seen again.

In the early game you're constantly punished through making just barely enough money to cover repairs and have something left over. Then the game slaps you with time and money pits through storage, hiring, modification, and travel fees. You can easily blow through several million credits by failing a single mission, but if you win that mission? "Here's 900k and a wet fart for salvage".

The enemy spawns and mission layouts are so bad that it's incredibly easy to get peppered every 5 minutes by pop-up tanks, turrets and aircraft coming from all angles. Which I think as a mechanic is just to guarantee that you'll need to repair something when you're done. Or you get stuck in a spinning/ducking match with a bigger/smaller mech that RNG spawned with high level AI and a build made of the raw malicious intent to forcibly spread your cheeks... Sure they have a difficulty rating that shows before a mission, but it's just a numerical value with zero context, so thanks I guess? But you have to waste the time and money to travel to that system to even be able to see the difficulty rating and how much the mission pays. You'll run into 6's and 7's that will wipe your entire squad, and even higher levels that you can do in a superlight without breaking a sweat. No consistency whatsoever in this numerical system.

I think I'll stick with Mechwarrior 3 and 4, or see if there's any mods for this that take out the idiotic travel fees and fix the rating system/mission detail visibility.

Edit: Yeah once you mod the severe money pits to calm their tits the game is a lot more balanced. And you will definitely need a mod that lets you see mission levels BEFORE traveling to a system. Should have been this way from the beginning though. Also the early level enemies cannot deal with LRM's at all, so go ham.\t\t\t\t\t
