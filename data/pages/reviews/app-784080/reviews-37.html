\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor some reason I decided to pretty much 100% the game and all DLCs before reviewing, oops! This game is a ton of fun. It's my first MW, so I can't compare it to previous entries, but it's a blast to play, missions are varied enough that you don't ever really get bored, and there's a solid do missions -> get money -> get better 'mechs -> do harder missions gameplay loop that keeps you occupied into the endgame.

The story isn't anything special, standard revenge plot with perhaps a bit too much mystery to newcomers (they don't explain much, but if you know the lore, the motivations for the antagonists are obvious). The presentation is a bit awkward, but it's all really just setup for bigger, cooler missions, so it's hard to complain.

The DLCs are all great, adding in dozens of planned missions to counter the procedurally generated ones that form the core gameplay loop. I'd say they're all solid and worth getting, even the one that "just" adds melee weapon loadouts (which I think are just dandy, and really change up how you have to approach situations, both when the enemy has the axe-wielding death machine and when you do).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing this game for about 2 years now and I just can't get enough of it. The first video game I ever played as a kid was Mechwarrior 4 Vengeance and I've been obsessed with this universe ever since. One of my core cringe memories was pretending to be an Atlas at elementary school and "battling" with the other kids on the playground. As I grew older I got my own xbox and then found the Mechassault games which I played for many hours as well. I patiently awaited a sequel to MW4 for years and was excited when Mechwarrior 5 was announced in 2009 and then subsequently crushed, as I suspect many other fans of this franchise were, when I learned the project was locked up with legal troubles and then eventually scrapped. I thought all hope was lost for ever getting to see a modern installment from this universe. But then again another announcement for Mechwarrior 5 was announced under a new developer. I knew I had to have it and knowing it was going to be a PC game I finally researched, hunted, and waited in lines at Best Buy over night to gather all the parts I would need and built my first PC. MW 5 was everything I had hoped for after waiting for over a decade. It was fun, addictive, and challenging. I shared some of the same gripes other players had with it like how repetitive it could get, some of the cheesy dialogue, and lackluster story, but none of it mattered. All of these things I feel were outweighed by the challenging gameplay, endless customization, constant hunt for bigger and better weapons and mechs, money management, and excellent sound design. Unlike some other players I even enjoyed the score for the game. It wasn't as electronic as some of the other games but I thought it fit well. It was perfection in my eyes.... until my friend showed me how to mod. After my manipulation it wasn't even the same game anymore. I couldn't believe how much more fun it was after that. Sometimes I wish this was how the game was released but in the end it doesn't matter. The developers made a solid game that could hold its own ground and then the excellent community elevated it to a whole other level. Overall, I give this game a 9/10 with a solid 2 thumbs up! Can't wait for The Clans to release next year!\t\t\t\t\t
