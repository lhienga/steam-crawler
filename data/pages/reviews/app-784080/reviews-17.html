\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those games where the developer really had fun and passion with making this game. Absolutely perfect for any mechwarrior/battletech fan. Or if you like flashy graphics war games and blowing stuff up with lasers.

Mechwarrior games are like Tank war games, except your military vehicle moves much faster, takes way more hits, shoots lasers/missiles/cannons/etc, gigantic, and is a freaking robot. Also Mech combat is extremely strategic as it involves many variants/ weapon builds as well as usage of many different Mech Chassis and their strengths/weaknesses. Do you like running around extremely fast and distracting everything while dishing tons of damage? Maybe you prefer to just be a slow giant monster that runs over buildings and blows everything up? Or perhaps you want to stay back and snipe enemies? Do you like Gundam swords? This game has it all.

Cons:
1. Really needs you to buy DLC for extra mission modes / biomes, or game will feel repetitive after 40 hours. (story mode is easily 100s of hours)
2. Game feels incomplete without mods. Mods clean up graphics (ray tracing is also broken) add new mechs/ new biomes.

I've been playing these games since Mechwarrior 2: 31st Century Combat.... and finally playing Mechwarrior 5 was a dream come true. I can't wait until Mechwarrior 6\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a serious impact and feel problem - the fights between big robots do not feel real - more like two toys walking around each other, spewing sparks until one falls and "you win".

The weapons don't feel punchy, so you end up just clicking an enemy to death, mashing buttons in the order of your Weapon Array cooldowns. And that just never changes - the gun may change it's colour from green to blue, but that would still be the same gun that does a red pew every 4 seconds or one that does green bzzzt every 5 seconds.
That, coupled with the barren, flat maps with nothing interesting going on, turns the phrase "armed giant robot raid" boring and feeling like work.
How can you do that? It's giant armed and armored robots! How did you make them boring?

I want to commend good music, reasonable destruction physics, great immersion via the opening and first person view on your ship and robot bay. I can see that the developers are doing a lot with very little - I've never seen an in-engine opening cinematic that seems so geniune in it's cheapness.
But I can not recommend the game.\t\t\t\t\t
