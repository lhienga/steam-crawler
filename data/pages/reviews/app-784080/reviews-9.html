\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really tried with this one. I've done a lot of mental gymnastics to try and call this a neutral recommend, or a great game with flaws. A pretty large number of players, even ones that love mecha, are going to be put off entirely or quit early due to the many issues with MW5.

I'll describe why by describing my most recent session. Mission 1, easy defense contract in city biome. Reset due to enemy getting stuck in floor. Mission 2, Warzone in rocky biome. Took twice as long as it should because the enemy AI kept getting stuck on corners across the map. Mission 3, Snowy biome: First enemies are glitched into a corner. They can be hit, but it takes a long time to kill them. Mission 4: 8v8 duel, scrapyard biome. Allied NPC mechs all get wedged into a choke they think they can fit through but can't. Wait a while to see if they get out. They don't. Close the game in frustration.

The AI only functions well on flat terrain with minimal obstruction. The map design of MW5 is often cluttered, with lots of obstructions, choke points, corridors, buildings. Maps can end up with massive ridges that can really only be gone around. AI gets stuck on the opposite side of the ridge? You're going to be walking for 10 minutes.


Visually the game looks great. When you're not on a map that's blindingly bright. Or on another map that's pitch black at the same brightness. It's headache inducing to constantly adjust.

The learning curve is steep. The game is hardest at the start. There is a lot of frustration in learning what contracts are doable starting out. When your game is modded, this means a lot of resetting. Lots of loading screens. Refitting the mechs you've already refit. Tweaking settings you already tweaked. It bogs down the experience.

MW5 is very much a Bethesda game in spirit. Janky, QoL starved, lacking in variety, issues that have plagued the game since release remain unaddressed as more DLC is announced. The Bethesda solution is to mod it, and there are great mods from a talented and passionate community. They might even save the experience for some people. Their quality is what kept me playing.

The game excels when you pick up a new mech from a tough fight, outfit it, and really feel a power spike. These moments are sublime. Unfortunately, they are few and far between. They have not been enough to carry my experience through the rough parts.

All this has led up to me realizing my time with the game has been defined by frustration, menuing, loading screens, and jank. So little of it has been what makes Mechwarrior/Battletech a classic.

If MW5 is a game for you, you probably aren't even reading reviews. Battletech is an awesome franchise, and devoted fans will still find much to enjoy here. That's why it pains me to not want to play anymore. If you're on the fence, really consider how patient you are before buying this one.\t\t\t\t\t
