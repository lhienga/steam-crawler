\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot of positive reviews of MW5 but I read the low rated reviews of the game and didn't ever think I would buy it. I did put it on the wishlist and so when it went on sale again recently, I read about the mods available and then bought it. Steam has mods in the workshop that are easily integrated. NexuxMods has others.
I'm not trying to tying to impress someone so I run in Godmode, used mw5 Save Editor to give myself 200,000 cBills and picked up about a dozen mechs that were available due to Yet Another Mech Collection and the Save Editor (I think, for sure one of the mods) and started learning how all this works. First thing I learned was I needed a whole lot more cBills. This style of cheating makes the game fun for me. Some people want a challenge and there definitely should be one if you don't cheat. I think you would be the primary target if you aren't in Godmode. There would be a dire shortage of cBills at first considering what all there is to buy along with the travel costs as well as repair costs.
The complaints about the sameness of the missions is true. It depends on what you want to do but I have really enjoyed it playing the way I am.
I think if I took it out of Godmode it might be a pain. (I ran one mission with Godmode accidently turned off and wondered why I was being attacked so heavily. During a lull between bad guys I saw I was almost destroyed.)
Some people think lore is important, I haven't read any of that. I am picking my sides but in ignorance of what the News is.
What I read is that YAML makes the mechbay portion of the game more useable, I haven't tried it vanilla so I don't know. I enjoy using the mechbay thus the game is more entertaining than just blowing things up.
Some of the developer choices are weird, eg why does it cost me 13 days and 50k to travel somewhere? Or why does it take so long to modify or repair a mech. And how is it that it would take extra time to repair a mech when in a combat zone, after all it's done on ship, I'm not sending it out to the cleaners.
Another weakness is the manual spends it 29 pages telling you why you should be glad you bought the game. But it doesn't tell you how things work. And I don't think it was updated for any DLCs. There is a large community online that have answered a lot of questions I had. for example Sarna.net created a huge wiki for the game that seems useful.
It's a simple game; just go out there and tear things up but it's cheap entertainment when on sale. I got the bundle with the first two DLCs.\t\t\t\t\t

...
...and having said that, it is likely only fans of the franchise will enjoy this game. There are some excellent creative choices here that allow the game's battles to be played while the overall narrative feels like the original tabletop game in which the imagination was employed for the battles themselves. If you just run around the map fighting battles, this game will bore you in short order. But what I am enjoying, aside from customizing my mechs and experimenting with different builds, is the overall feel of impacting the shifting battle lines of the Innersphere by taking selective jobs and aligning myself with specific houses rather than taking any and all contracts. The news feed helps fuel the narrative along with various important missions along the way. In this regard the game feels like an enjoyable combination of FPS video game and role playing table top game.
The drawbacks are almost enigmatic. Maps are somewhat lazily designed, despite use of Unreal Engine. While weather effects and differing times of day are used in battles, every single planet has a close-orbit planet nearby that simply has different skins for different battles, while map borders are randomly rippled mountain ranges with unremarkable terrain skins stretched over them. None of the maps have water features save for the rare oceanfront. Without too much additional effort, maps could have had a great deal of variety, but buildings, settlements and map layouts will feel repetitive just a few hours into playing the game.
In addition to the ongoing narrative fueled by the news feed and changing borders on the Innersphere map are the running narratives one might experiment with in hiring different pilots to crew your mechs. Once I maxed out a pilot's skills, I developed a running storyline in mind of his becoming disenchanted with working for my merc crew (Knight's Riders) and selling us out on one of the missions that didn't go so well.... which is another enjoyable aspect of the game; getting your butt handed to you and having to rebuild your outfit and reputation. Setbacks are part of the enjoyment of this game, as frustrating as they can be at times.
Non-Battletech fans or those without much experience with tabletop gaming will likely not enjoy this title. Fans will likely overlook the game's flaws. Take that for what it's worth.\t\t\t\t\t
