There are multiple cities, too, each with their own unique culture featuring different gods, politics, race relations, and prejudices to uncover, so get ready to eat sand for breakfast. We get a great representation of Egypt by the developers, towns and villages are bustling with citizens, farmers, workers, and soldiers. There's plenty of dangerous (and eventually tamable) wildlife from vicious crocs and hippos lurking in the Nile to lions and hyenas prowling the sand dunes and rocky hills to flamingos and egrets that take flight when you thunder past on horseback. We get also the chance to be present of some major events changing the course of history and meet some of the most iconic/ important faces of that time such as (Caesar, Cleopatra, Ptolemy XIII, Akhenaten, Nefertiti, Ramses II and even Tutankhamun -the last four from the Curse of the pharaohs expansion- ). At some point the game becomes repeatable but in a way you might don't even notice it . Devs provides you with tons of gear pieces- char upgrades so you can be fluid and try different playstyles as long as the story carries on. The parkour system/combat system takes a huge upgrade ,targeting the stealth part, and in my opinion the game becomes the Assassins creed that the fans of the series want after many disappointments that Ubisoft published before Origins (excluding Black Flag). In essence there's a lot of grinding to be done and prepare to be charmed with the beauty of Egyptian civilization, I think its worth giving it a chance on this game even in 2024++\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNever liked Ancient Egypt so the setting is pretty much lost on me. Graphics are great, very detailed and lifelike, but sandy environments and never ending ruins start to grate on you by the 30th hour of playing. Story is nothing special, I didn't like the plot much, just a device that drags you through all the map areas. As for the characters, Bayek is ok, everybody else is slightly worse, nothing to write home about. Combat is good for around 10 hours, then lack of variation kills all the enjoyment from it. Played on normal difficulty so it’s gotten pretty easy pretty fast, maybe should’ve played on higher difficulty. In the end it was a big adventure which I halfheartedly enjoyed just because of its scale. Recommend for Ancient Egypt lovers.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hated the level and crafting system, Both existing exclusively so Ubisoft can sell "booster packs". But nowhere NEAR as grindy looting/crafting as in Odyssey or Valhalla. Still, could be worse. Could be Dying Light 2(1 was good) where micro looting is about 90% of the game.

But while there is relatively little resource grind, in Origins, it's still annyoing how a random viillage guard is an immortal, one hitting God to Mr. magical super assassin, because they are 3 levels higher.

When cheating to max level, the world scales up to you! It suddenly feels almost like a real AC game from the start. No immortal god NPC, Assassinations kill instantly, as they should. Odyssey and Valhalla have guaranteed assasination kills, as gameplay option, which i highly recommend using.

But even beyond the less craft/XP grind, Origins is by far my favourite of the 3!
Ancient Egypt is just so much more interesting and has more variety than Odysseys's Greece, That's part real world, part gameplay reasons. From very impressive deserts to mountains, oasis's, marsh/farm,land to really big cities. Covering egyptian, greek and roman culture.

Few but big forts, with no ressource micro looting, are actually fun to take out. while Odyssey is spammed with 1000's of idiotic mini-camps for ressource/xp grind, making it's world feel even more Copy&paste.

https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=1204910406

Odysseys lone unique feature are the (few) RPG quests with choices and consequences. But Orgins side quests generally feel better written and fleshed out.
While all require the god awful Ubisoft connect to run, Origins seem to be the only one to have Steam achievements.

Afterthought on AC Mirage: It's good, i liked it enough, They went somewhat (but not enough, beacsue of the juicy microtransactions) back to classic AC. But that means it stands against the classic AC's. It hasn't really gone back enough to compete with 2, Revelations or the very underrated Unity. But i'd definitely put it above 3 or Syndicate.\t\t\t\t\t
