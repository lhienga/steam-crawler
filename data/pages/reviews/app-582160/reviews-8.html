\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother "late" review - by a gamer who waits for the sale price. 136 hours gaming for Â£14.99 (gold edition) makes ACO one of the best value for money games I have played. I guess like others I have had a love/frustration relationship with the AC series since the beginning and have felt the decline, wishing for a new title which could come up to Black Flag. I had read good things about the "style change" taken starting with ACO. As a "real" RPGer I often scorn the use of the term for video games. I saw it used for ACO but if so it does not deserve it. There is no character personalisation whatsoever. We all play the same Bayek. We can choose whichever weapons we prefer and which skills to select, but that doesn't make it an RPG. IIRC there was similar for previous AC games - and I think I actually preferred the old style of getting new weapons and tools as a direct part of the story.

Overall I very much enjoyed the game and probably more that the previous few in the series. The movement and combat is probably a sensible evolution - although as a hater of "boss fights" certain aspects of the combat annoyed me, but they still haven't fixed some of the clunkiness: Often Bayek would come to a complete stop at an imperceptible obstruction in the terrain, and when climbing he might suddenly move to the left or right, ruining the attempt at stealth. There are far too many weapons and the differences between them have little impact beyond fighting style. There's the usual pilfering sacks for goods - even in temples where Bayek is told about a thief (what me?). But there is little in the way of finding special items beyond the repetitive side-quests. I was annoyed that there are no progression metrics so I couldn't tell if I had found all the sites without searching.

Whilst I generally like the story, I felt it had too many branches and it was difficult to know the best path to follow for the best gaming experience as the designers intended. In the previous games I knew that I had to progress through phases to get the tools and weapons for the next phase. I like "sandbox" style games, but the ACO story does not lend itself to the concept. So many times I decided to infiltrate a soldier or bandit site to get the identified treasure, only to later have to return there as part of the story. I know I should follow the story, but that might mean missing the XP and money I can get from the non-story sites that I don't know are non-story. Aside from the early part of the game where Bayek level is critical, there is no guidance on where he should be for best enjoyment of the storey.

So that's it - if anyone should read this. I would say that ACO has successfully rejuvenated my interest in the AC series so I shall continue playing them - when I can buy them at a good sale price!\t\t\t\t\t
