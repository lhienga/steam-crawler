\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like games with an anti-colonial narrative and strong female characters then this may be the game for you.
You'll mostly play as Bayek, who is actually a good character, being relatable, competent and altruistic--except that we're supposed to believe that he would slip and botch a stab which ends up killing his son. Anyway, Bayek plays second fiddle to his flawless wife, Aya, whom the designers forgot to make likeable or relatable in any way. Of course, she elevates herself by taking on the name of a goddess late game. The relationship between Bayek and Aya feels forced and unnatural at every moment they're together, which was probably intentional.
A revenge-driven story is usually a good one, and a good motivator for games like this, but the messaging within this narrative is off-putting and laden with unnecessary social justice tropes, which means it's actually a series of ambiguous circumstances where you never feel comfortable with who your allies are; something that Bayek justifiably points out in regards to Cleopatra, but is ignored.
The gameplay is fine, I guess. I like stealth games, and taking out an entire outpost single-handedly can be quite satisfying... for the first couple of times. After you've done it 50 or so times it starts getting old. It is a fairly repetitive game. Also, the enemy leveling system sucks. It's just a cheap way of blocking you from certain areas until you've done enough grinding,
As a final compaint, you are forced to log into Ubisoft every time you play, which is utterly stupid and annoying and a disincentive to keep playing. I can't abide games on Steam that do this and force you to be online, etc. They should have a clear disclaimer that pops up before confirming your purchase to tell you that you are forced to use the Ubisoft platform for this game.
As for the good stuff, the devs did a brilliant job of illustrating late Ancient Egypt, and the game may very well be worth it just for the visuals if that is the aspect of a game that you enjoy most. Also Senu is very cool as Bayek's trusty flying sidekick.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAssassin's Creed Origins is a spectacular game. Having just completed the main story along with the two DLCs, I can say that I thoroughly enjoyed my time spent in Ancient Egypt (and beyond) and I really appreciated the masterful environments of the game. I appreciated the music and scenery far too much with how many screenshots I took, but the game is beautiful and, according to experts, quite historically accurate. I have always had a passion for Ancient Egypt and this scratched that itch well. The Discovery Tours mode was amazing and informative. As for the story of the main game itself, I wasn't overly enamoured with it. I really felt connected to Bayek as the player (Layla), but didn't feel much toward Aya or her lack of affection toward Bayek. I felt Bayek's rage and sadness and desire to avenge his loved ones, but beyond that, I wasn't very invested in it. AC games seem to have a common theme of being a sort of hack and slash type game with some extra mechanics like sneaking, which I really actually enjoyed and didn't find mindless. I 100% completed the game, which goes to show that it was compelling and enjoyable enough to clear the rather large maps of all side quests and question marks. This is the same for the DLCs as well. The Hidden Ones DLC was great, it was rather short and only took me about a day to complete, but I liked it and the new environment to explore. I really loved the Curse of the Pharaohs, which took me a few days to complete. The story was interesting, the boss fights were actually difficult, and the environments were super cool and compelling. I also didn't expect the twist in the story. Either way, I did really enjoy the game overall.

A few small criticisms I have with AC Origins are mainly surface level and didn't affect my enjoyment of the game, but a few things annoyed me slightly - like having to rescue people from bandit camps, carrying them out because they complain they can't walk, and then they instantly stand and walk on their feet AFTER they are carried out. It breaks the immersion, which AC Origins tends to do very well, especially with the historical accuracy and stunning environments. NPCs are also just pretty stupid, some don't move out of the way of Bayek or the mount and are overall not as advanced as RDR2 NPCs, but that isn't too fair for me to compare the two games based on access to technology. The boat speed isn't realistic either, as it goes way too fast, and then somehow the horses and camels are sluggishly slow and only have one speed. The mount also can't be manually controlled to jump, which I found quite annoying. Combat and parkour controls are a bit janky at times but otherwise fun. I also absolutely hated the "confirm kill" mechanic, having to hold a button to confirm a kill as a bunch of enemies ganged up on me was infuriating at times.

Side quests did kind of feel all the same, or at least very similar, but as I said previously I enjoyed the general grind of the game enough to complete everything. Not going to lie, even the main story felt very "all the same."

Besides my criticisms, AC Origins was a spectacular experience and I'm glad to have seen it all the way through. 8/10, with bonus points for the historical accuracy and the amount of work poured into the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a mixed bag for me.

I really liked the beginning and the first 15 hours I was hooked in. But as I progressed, the sheer scale of the world swallowed me.

There were some towns that had more unique stories, more soul, and those were cool and satisfying to go through. But as you push towards the mid-late game you get overwhelmed by the content and the quests feel like a chore rather than pleasure. For example I leveled to 33-34 then decided to finish the story because I got bored of exploring.

Overall as I look back the positives still outweigh the negatives because the story had some really touching parts, which is the main selling point for me besides the gameplay, travelling, combat and the world, which is beautifully built, if you're a fan of old cities and architecture you're gonna love this.

Story wise it has a great idea. The ending is logical but still not fulfilling for me as there is a lack of closure and emotion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI find it fun and enjoyable to play and I love the Egyptian theme and the world is beautiful and interesting to explore. I enjoyed playing the game mostly.

I love using the eagle Senu to scout.

It seems like they are trying to make the world more alive and vibrant,
however it's a Ubisoft game and it suffers from the usual boring repetitive map activities that ubisoft games are full of that don't have any depth.

Majority of the story is forgettable and it mostly feels about revenge.

It did feel quite dull and tedious at times.

The combat isn't the best, it is very clunky and can be frustrating.
Taming animals is almost useless and is a waste of points. As if they get a bit of fire damage they will no longer be tamed and attack you and they die very easily. Fast travel makes the animal disappear and going into a cave also untames them, they also disappear when there is a cut scene.


I'd rate it 7/10. It's mostly enjoyable but a bit too tedious.\t\t\t\t\t
