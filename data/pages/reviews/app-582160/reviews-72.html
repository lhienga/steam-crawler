\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAssassin's Creed Origins stands tall among the pinnacle of open-world gaming experiences, offering a riveting journey through ancient Egypt that solidifies its status as one of the finest historical action-adventure games.

Set against the backdrop of a sprawling, meticulously recreated Egyptian landscape, Origins unfolds a narrative that transcends mere historical retelling. Bayek, the protagonist, is not just a character; he's a testament to meticulous storytelling, layered with care and detail. His personal journey, interwoven with the tapestry of ancient Egypt, adds emotional depth that elevates the overall experience.

As we navigate Bayek through the remnants of pre-Assassin Brotherhood days, the blend of historical authenticity and carefully crafted character development becomes a hallmark of the game. His past, traumas, and motivations intertwine seamlessly with the rich historical tapestry, creating a protagonist that resonates on a profound level.

Visually, Origins is a masterpiece. The high-quality graphics and realistic direction catapult players into an immersive reimagining of ancient Egypt. The sheer attention to detail in recreating landmarks, cities, and the lush landscapes of the Nile Delta contributes to the game's success.

The musical score, akin to a harmonious companion, adds another layer to the depth of the narrative. It doesn't just serve as background noise; it becomes an integral part of the storytelling process, evoking emotions and connecting players more profoundly to the plotline.

Assassin's Creed Origins is a true all-rounder, seamlessly blending engaging gameplay, a captivating narrative, and stunning visuals. It stands as a testament to the evolution of the Assassin's Creed series, offering an experience that transcends the boundaries of mere gaming, immersing players in the captivating world of ancient Egypt.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed the story of this one! Without spoiling things, the themes of family and loss are stronger than ever in this installment. I'd recommend it for anyone looking for an emotionally heavy adventure full of history and light touches of fantasy.

That being said, I'm not a fan of the combat here. It felt like it relied heavily on staggering to make up for what the gameplay difficulty lacked otherwise. Hit by a big weapon? Staggered. Hit by a strong attack? Staggered. Block a strong attack? Staggered. Encounter the dual-wielding enemy type that carries seemingly infinite smoke bombs and uses them nonstop? Staggered for minutes straight.

And the worst part about staggers is it often ignores dodges, attack animations, and just about anything that would counter it. So certain enemy types, like the aforementioned swift sword duel-wielders, will stagger you at max level with a smoke bomb, run all over the place while you're in the animation, throw another smoke bomb the second you regain control, and repeat this process until you can finally get an attack off or get lucky with his AI changing tactics. Mind you, this can be happening while enemies damage you for free.

Another thing I disliked was "allies" in battle. Them existing makes sense, but I don't enjoy how they're implemented at all. They're often found in enemy camps in those wooden cages you find everywhere with the doors that break from any ounce of damage. This is good for freeing them quickly with a light attack or arrow, but you'll often find that the enemies manage to break the cage on their own. The big issue here is that allies, unlike prisoners, will attempt to attack any enemy without consideration for what you're doing once freed. Keep in mind that enemies that are in battle have a heightened awareness and are erratic. So sometimes I show up to a bandit camp and the whole camp spots me immediately, or the enemies are already lighting the bonfire for backup, or my target is fleeing despite being a hundred yards away. Some enemies who are late to the brawl will even see the dead bodies and immediately enter combat with me just for the crime of being within eyesight, regardless of where I am or what I'm doing. It's annoying when the game punishes me for its own automated behavior that I have no influence over. Like yeah, sure, it's dynamic and unpredictable, but I don't always want that from my RPG which heavily relies on stealth.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played AC Origins right after AC Odyssey, so this is be used for comparison.

AC Origins as well is very beautfil, immersive with a nice sound track. Respect for making special content ment to be informative about ancien Egypt. I didn't realize it was available in Odyssey as well (so it helps that Origins has achievements related to it).

World/Quests felt much less redundant/excessive/bloated than in Odyssey, even though it was mostly about clearing another camp. Story/quests also felt better than in Odyssey, less stereotypical and predictable.

With regards to gameplay, to be honest fighting wasn't very good, rather inconsistent. Much less options to fight your way around, dodging was bad and would usually only make it harder for you to actually hit your target. On many occasion I got nearly killed by basic opponents only because I tried to dodge them only to strike in the void while getting damaged in return. Fighting a shielded enemies is frustating.
Also, balance between fighting styles was ridiculous to a point it does not really make sense to go melee. Assassination is quite OP (can 1 shot almost anything, including elite unites), ranged is very strong and fighting on a horse/camel is just brainless easy (you do more damage, you can hit several targets at the same time, you hardly take damage etc). On the other hand, there is a basic opponent which can kill you in just a few seconds, fire, especially when you are riding. It's ridiculous how many times I died only because something set the ground aflame and I couldn't stop it in time, you get like 2s to react otherwise you are dead regardless of your level or equipment.
Also, several boss fight were boring and frustrating. Many times the only viable solution was to hit and run with your bow until your adrenaline was full so that you could activate your overpower ability and deal real damage. On the other hand, some enemies, especially bosses, could freeze you in place for seconds while performing their animated special attack and there is nothing you can do if you were in range except wait that you get control back to run away. Often these attacks could 1-2 shot you (damn that Tuthankamen).

I appreciated to not find in Origins the same bounty mechanic which was very stressful in Odyssey, but to be honest this was making infiltration/assassination far too easy since there was no penalty for being caught.

All in all, I had a good time exploring and completing all locations. It's great if you have spare time to invest. I had some frustrating moments though, but more relaxed than in Odyssey.

PS: At some point I had nearly gave up playing the game due to increasing crashes blocking my progress, when not corrupting the save and making me lose progress. Updating the GPU driver solved this issue, fortunately.\t\t\t\t\t
