\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow.
After the pointlessness that was Rogue, the "neket iadet" that was Unity and the mediocrity that was Syndicate, we finally get this, which is, in terms of an Assassin's creed game, the closest thing we could get to a masterpiece. Now, don't get me wrong, this game still carries a lot of faults including the new combat system, which despite offering a refreshing approach of freedom can be infuriatingly inconsistent at times (I advise anyone to begin this game on the easy setting to start with. You can change the difficulty at any point you feel you need more of a challenge from the combat) but this game gave me everything I ever realistically expected as an AC fan and the game delivers so much choice and option to tailor your preferred experience of it.

The series is finally given the overhaul it desperately needed and this flourishes all the better for it and it's difficult to pick an aspect of it that fails to entertain. I've loved every minute of it. I'm a little biased with the setting, I admit, due to my love for ancient Egypt but it felt a perfect location to kick start the creed and in many ways I see this game as a reimagining or even a reboot of the series as much as a prequel. It's root influence is Black Flag providing plenty of stalking zones for stealth. Chuck in some HZD, a pinch of the Witcher and an overall feel of the original Tomb Raider (In my personal top 5 of all time) and you essentially have the components that make this game what it is.

Bayek, the lead character, is briliantly portrayed and a massive improvement on the cardboard cutouts from previous installments and his altruism propels you through a gargantuan story which is nothing short of epic in scope charting the power struggle in Egypt for thier independance from Romans, Greeks and thier own Phaorohs. The more I played, the more I liked and the further up the AC ladder it got. In fact, I think I have a new favourite. Thank you, Ubisoft (Never thought i'd say that...)\t\t\t\t\t
