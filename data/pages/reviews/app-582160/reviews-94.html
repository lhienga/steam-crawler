\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy dream game come to life with some caveats. As a long time Egyptology enthusiast, I had waited years for something half as good as this to be released. The attention to detail is phenomenal, such as: exploring the pyramids (Khufu's, The Bent Pyramid, The Step Pyramid, etc.), looting tombs, exploring underneath the Sphinx, boating down the Nile, gazing upon Alexander the Great's sarcophagus, competing as a gladiator in the arena, racing in the hippodrome, cult worship, beautifully recreated temples and cities, the Library of Alexandria, the clothing, memorable boss battles, the music, the controls, the weapons (especially Khopesh-style swords), etc. I could go on for paragraphs at a time but you get the idea, it is the most comprehensive and well-executed game set in ancient Egypt ever created.

There are some drawbacks, however. The overpriced DLC, extremely repetitive side quests, needlessly slow grinding, missed opportunities to flesh out the world (such as playing classic Egyptian games like: Senet, Mehen, Hounds & Jackals, etc.), and the copy and paste approach to enemy encounters throughout the entire land all suck the joy and life out of what would otherwise be a flawless work of art.

Even with all of that considered, one has to have reasonable expectations. The sure herculean effort to bring ancient Egypt to life with intricate details as far as the eye can see, set during the perfect time period in order to let the player to visit the most iconic locations (still mostly intact), is genius. If you are a fan of ancient history, well-executed stories, smooth combat, exploration, or simply Ancient Egypt, this game is definitely for you. Give it a try sometime soon, you won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t
