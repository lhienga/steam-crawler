\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA generally good game that's packed with the most tedious side activities, a horrible level-design system and an ultimately bleak story.

The Good:
+ The graphics are outstanding. Egypt looks great, the environment is impressive and makes you wanna walk/ride rather than fast travel.
+ Visually stunning.
+ The main character is decent.

The Bad:
- The story follows a revenge plot against the Order who killed your son. Easy enough to follow, right?
Wrong.
It goes from killing the target that directly affected your son's death to killing an entire nation of leaders and generals because your wife is too obsessed with Cleopatra's power-hungry plans. Bayek, the main character, is too much of a simp to stand up to the foolishness that you're forced to watch unfold.

This game's called Assassin's Creed Origins but it has 0 to do with the "origins" of the assassins. It's laughable. Bayek cuts his finger by mistake during a failed assassination attempt and this becomes the representation of the most loyal members of the Creed. Are you serious?
Yes, of course you are.

I liked Bayek and Aya's relationship at the start. I thought that together, they'd work to avenge their son but Aya couldn't give a rat's a-- about her son, instead, she's gotta please Cleopatra and nobody can do anything except Bayek. So you, the player, have to go here, kill this guy, kill that guy, investigate this, kill here, kill there, cutscene plays. The dialogue after each confirmed death is the same crap we've heard since the beginning of Assassin's Creed 1.
" Am I the villain?" " Are you sure we're not the same" " I've done what's right for Egypt" etc. etc. etc.
The characters you assassinate are 0 in the story with no proper build-up except a minor introduction before you kill him/her.

Oh no, Cleopatra's plan was bad?! Unbelievable. Who could've foreseen that?
After succeeding in his mission, Aya divorces Bayek, the ONE thing of this stupid story that I liked, so that at the END, after all the level-ing, and all the upgrades, you have to play as Aya and do the last assassination so she can get her bada-- moment of being a bada-- assassin vanishing in the crowd. So the "brotherhood" should've been called a "sisterhood".

DO NOT get deceived by the trailers and the introduction of Bayek having that amazing assassination and dialogue. That's the BEST this game ever gets with its story and nothing after it comes close.

- The world design is good, amazing even, the region of Siwa from the beginning is impressive with plenty of activities to do and places to explore.
The issue, however, is that once you're done with the starting area, you'll soon realize that the rest of Egypt is the same.
Every NPC looks the same, boring and lifeless, the dialogue is horrible, recorded by interns who clearly weren't paid since their delivery is beyond awful.
You can't buy into any of these characters. They're legit walking NPCs.
I swear, their dialogue repeats over and over everywhere, and even though they speak their language, by the end of the game ( if you can last till then) some of their stupid lines will burn inside your brain.
0 Atmosphere and lack of music does not help.

- The level design system
The main story isn't the best, but compared to the disastrous side quests, it is significantly better. The game takes you out of the main story because you have to do side quests to level up.
Do you like this cool weapon you've unlocked that's legendary? Well too bad. If the enemy's 5 LVLs higher than you, you will wack at him with a wooden stick, not a sword you've wasted money on upgrading.
This takes me to:

- Side quests
Side quests are hands down some of the worst I've ever seen in a video game. It's as if the game doesn't know what to do. Should it tell a dark story? a funny story? Should this world be taken seriously? Not seriously?
They drop a bit of each and it's not enough to fill a cap of a water bottle.
You'll be locked from doing the main quest and forced into doing side quests for a good chunk of your playtime. While some of these quests provide a small level of entertainment, most are just garbage, outright garbage.
Who are these BANDITS I have to kill? Why are they even everywhere??
The game tries to explain some of this to you in the laziest way possible: notes - most of which have these names that you've never heard unless you've taken some Egyptian history lessons I suppose.

- Historical accuracy.
It's completely out the window. The game adheres to the current political American view.
Women gladiators and fighters are everywhere around Egypt. There's more interaction with women in cutscenes than with men. Not that it's an issue whenever a game has its liberty with its choices, but it's done to a ridiculous amount.
Don't go into this expecting any sort of resemblance to historical Egypt. All of the NPCs are Americans in disguise with bad accents.

- Where's the assassin?
The gameplay's decent. It's not great but serviceable. The assassination options are still there but the level of enemies ruins the experience. If an enemy has a higher level, your assassination won't work, but instead you cause critical damage.
The parkour is mostly gone, you can still climb around a few buildings but it won't make you feel like an assassin. Gone are the days when you stealthily assassinate someone, then blend in the crowd once panic ensues. The hidden blade is massively nerfed and needs plenty of materials to upgrade in order to make it efficient.
The execution animations are cool, but the lack of "gore" really tells you how "safe" everything is.

- Lack of rewards:
You're playing in the vast magnitude of Egypt so you expect some amazing outfits and rewards right?
Wrong. Some of the coolest outfits are locked behind DLC. Others you have to farm for hours on end doing more tedious stuff to unlock.
The shop outfits are just rags over and over again and the pyramids are barely utilised with no true reward given.
The game took inspiration from The Witcher 3 but it's so shallow.
The investigations are lazy and uninteresting. Instead of a "witcher", Bayek is a "medjay"...wow...
They should've dropped the Assassin's Creed name and given it its own identity.

If you've never played an Assassin's Creed game before, then you'd probably like this.
Assassin's Creed Origins feels like work. Pausing the game brings up Ubisoft ads of other games...
Tired of lvl-ing and grinding? Buy the exp pack.
Oh, you're tired looking like a poor man assassin after 20 hours of playing?
Here, buy the Gladiator DLC or the already used outfits from past games.
Pathetic and sad.

Don't expect an unforgettable experience.
Get it on a sale.

5.5/10 at its worst
7.5/10 at its best\t\t\t\t\t
