\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a sucker for ancient Egypt and AC games, so Origins hit right home. It's my favorite one from the series when it comes to atmosphere and aesthetic - the environments, architecture, ornamentation, fashion, Duat sections as well as interface, visual and sound effects are all outstanding.
The world is meticulously crafted and highly detailed. Map size is just about right. Controlling your own eagle was a great addition for exploration and it provided some amazing vistas. Although there is not a ton of different activities to engage in, it feels very immersive and alive and is fun to explore. It's well populated and NPCs seem to have elaborate daily routines. There are constant dynamic events, radiant quests, frequent patrols belonging to different factions, caravans, sailing ships, small scale fights, animal attacks, people coming to pick you up on boats when you're swimming and so on. Layout of cities and various points of interest is well done with some fairly intricate level designs. The clash of Egyptian, Greek and Roman cultures is interesting and portrayed well, both visually and thematically. There is a lot of spoken or written information, as well as some environmental storytelling that provide insight in daily life and history of the setting. Soundtrack is subtle, yet effective and adds to the sense of mystery with slight tension.
Gameplay doesn't lag behind either - I think it is the perfect middle ground between classic ACs and the newer more RPG ones. Both the stealthy and head on combat approaches are viable and represented enough, there's still some fun parkour and climbing opportunities, actual assassinations... Combat is slightly more complex which is a welcome change, it's fast paced with enough weight to it for hits to feel satisfying and Bayek's animations are smooth.
The RPG elements are not particularly intrusive - ability tree and gearing are simple, side quests are mostly short and basic. I consider them a good addition to further incentivize exploration (instead of borderline pointless collectibles for example) without being overwhelming.
Story is kind of simple, but still reasonably engaging and Bayek is a cool character. Main missions are the most fun parts. Camera feels more cinematic and the cutscenes are pretty good.
The Curse of the Pharaohs expansion seals the deal. It doesn't significantly change the gameplay loop, but it adds a fair amount of decent content (new maps, quests, enemies, a lot of weapons, a couple of shields, outfits, mounts and abilities) and takes the beauty of base game and ramps it up, due to leaning way more into mythology and fantasy. Story and boss fights are serviceable, but Ubisoft made this DLC absolutely shine with fantastic visuals. The 4 afterlife areas and their boss arenas are just stunning. Each one is distinctive and very atmospheric. They're all intriguing and large enough with a lot of verticality and their own plot and lore. The final one, Duat, is drop dead gorgeous and amongst my favorite zones in gaming.\t\t\t\t\t
