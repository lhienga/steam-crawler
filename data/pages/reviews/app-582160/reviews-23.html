\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I don't love this one quite as much as Odyssey, it's my second favorite in the franchise. (Valhalla would be third.) To be fair, I don't play the games for the AC mythos so much as for the feel of being in ancient Egypt, Greece, or what-have-you.

In that regard, this game is fantastic. I've seen Egyptologists reviewing it, and it's hands-down the best simulation of Ptolemy Era Egypt around. The game is likewise terrific, though I'm not a fan of You-killed-my-<insert person here>-and-now-I-seek-vengeance plots. They're pretty over done and tedious. Despite that, being a killing machine who can climb anything and leap off buildings (and survive) is always fun. And the way Bayek's personal vengeance plot is woven into historical events around Julius Caesar and Cleopatra is entertaining.

Don't take the game's interpretation of these characters to heart, though. Both were far more intelligent than portrayed. And despite Cleopatra's political machinations, getting herself impregnated by both Caesar and Marcus Antonius—though not at the same time—she was motivated by a love of her country and her children. She comes across a bit shrewish here.

When you finish the game, you get the option to start a new game with your character's current stats, armor, weapons, etc. Most of the guards and phylakes (bounty hunters) you come across are at your level now, so it's not a cakewalk, but you don't have to spend forever getting your best stuff back. It's a great option.\t\t\t\t\t
