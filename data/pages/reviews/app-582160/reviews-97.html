\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteam really needs a "meh" option, rather than just thumbs up or down, because games like this truly deserve a meh rating. If you are a fan of Assassin's Creed, I'm sure this game will be meaningful and awesome. For everyone else, meh.

Let's get my pet peeve out of the way first: Key bindings. Although this game allows you to rebind away from WASD, it is only partially possible. Things like menu navigation and increasing the number of a thing you are buying is set. This can make things a bit annoying if you are and ESDF user, but it was not terrible.

The best part of Assassin's Creed: Origin is its graphics. It is absolutely gorgeous. This isn't just a matter of well done character models with nice textures, it is the obvious care that has gone into creating this fictitious Egypt. Hands down the best part of the game is just exploring the world and enjoying the sights.

The core combat mechanics are decent, probably more so for its time. There's myriad kinds of weapons, so you can find the fighting style that works best for you. Unfortunately, it doesn't truly evolve. All but a handful of options are immediately available, and most of the mechanical character growth is vertical, meaning you just get better doing the same thing, rather than opening up new ways of doing things. This means you are constantly going through the same routine, and that isn't really fun.

This lack of evolution is felt most keenly as it expands into the overall gameplay: The vast majority of scenarios all play the same. This, coupled with a story that was on rails and not terribly enthralling meant that I started losing interest in the game relatively quickly. Cut scenes became entirely skippable, and, by the mid point continuing the game just felt like a chore. Consequently, I just race to the end of the main story abandoned a lot of side content.

Regarding how the game treated history, I will say that some things, like the general depiction of life in this fictitious Egypt, were pretty amazing. I understand the need to shrink the world and to change the terrain to make it more varied and interesting, even though I felt that detracted. The treatment of major figures and events was obviously warped to fit the game premise in a way that you may appreciate more if you are a fan of the series. Still, it felt more accurate that the Netflix documentary on Cleopatra.\t\t\t\t\t
