\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really liked the AC games from the launch of the first, however AC Rogue was the last I played because I was disappointed they moved away from ships and Unity was buggy. Then Destiny and R6 Siege sucked me away for many years.
Coming back I have to say I missed out. I was continually impressed with the scope of the game, it really feels alive in the villages and cities. The landscapes are beautiful and pretty varied, and exploring the Pyramids and different Vaults was quite fun. There's ton of great flavour text littering the world, and some really amusing interconnected ones. As you complete side quests the NPCs lives will change accordingly, and you can even seek them out in their new circumstances and have a little dialogue line. The side quests felt worth doing because of this, plus your characters whole deal is helping people so it made sense to complete them as well.
It's an RPG in the same way the Witcher games are, in the fact the you play as a specific character: Bayek. It's not an RPG in the sense that you have dialogue options or choices, but this game puts a lot of effort to make you feel like a part of the world. It's not always engaging when the story takes over, and the intro is a little weak, but I can say I thoroughly enjoyed all of the side content and playing through the game.
The Discovery mode is the perfect cherry on top of a fantastic game, that really goes to show that there was a ton of genuine passion behind the game, it's just a shame that so many talented teams and individuals are stuck in the horrible corporate abyss of ubisoft.\t\t\t\t\t
