\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImmersion 4.5/5:
Not one of my favorite AC games but I definitely enjoyed it. It is my 2nd AC game with the action RPG style with Valhalla being my first which is also my favorite AC game of all time. The reason why Valhalla had left a big impact on me is because of the immersion and love for Viking culture. So I think for people that love Egypt and the lore behind it will enjoy this game even more than I did. So if you love stuff like Egypt go for it!
It is an incredibly beautiful and detailed game.

Combat 3/5:
The combat is simple but still enjoyable at the beginning stage learning all of the tricks and extra combat abilities you unlock from the skill tree, however, near the end game it does become repetitive since there is usually one or two move sets that work the best and that you can spam to defeat most enemies. But this is only nearing the end so not game breaking. Another point of contention is the weapon throw ability, the animation for this is way too slow and breaks the flow of combat.

Stealth 3/5:
Compared to Valhalla I found the stealth in this game better and the way the enemies are placed, but this is definitely not the best AC game when it comes to stealth. However, the stealth locations which are often military camps and guarded locations are very enjoyable to complete. But like the combat the stealth felt a bit repetitive, there are not many creative ways of assassinating guards. It is mostly just navigating, anticipating and using the sleep darts. All the other stealth tools, while I have tried them, are not very useful or fun to use. I was also missing the double hidden blade stealth kills. It wouldn't have been lore breaking to put this in imho because most people can come up with putting another hidden blade on your other arm, it isn't something we should wait centuries upon centuries to be invented. Definitely missed that. The chain assassination animation is clunky and way too slow. Before I initiate a chain assassination going for my 2nd kill I am already discovered.

Archery 3/5:
Clunky targeting, doesn't play smooth with a controller. I mostly resort to auto targeting and using a light bow or warrior's bow. But still playable and you will get used to it after a while, nothing game breaking.

Story 3.5/5:
It was basically a revenge story, decent story, nothing special. The truly touching parts were near the end whereby you could see a bit of how the creed originated. I wished they would have started with the actual origins of the creed much earlier in the game, I was really looking forward to see how the creed started and how it progressed. Another point of contention with the story is that the characters are bit bland and not memorable.

Side quests 2/5:
It is mostly: go there, do x and go to y, complete. The dialogue of the side quests are boring. I skipped most of the side quests dialogue and just used them as XP farms. There are not many unique cut scenes for side quests and the characters are not memorable, they have bland personalities. I'd rather have fewer side quests but with a higher quality like RDR2 and Cyberpunk 2077. I did read a lot of AC players loving the fact that the side quests actually had some dialogue and where much uniquer than the repetitive activities you had to do in older AC games so this may be a good point for players looking to try this game.

Abilities 3.5/5:
The abilities are nice, you unlock new abilities and additional skills for combat, archery and stealth but I found most of them to not be of much use. The best abilities are located in the combat section of the skill tree.

Upgrades 3/5:
I did enjoy upgrading my equipment, you have to hunt a lot for this and you have to break into military encampments to find upgrade materials. You will get a cosmetic upgrade on your armor and better stats if you upgrade them. But these are minor visual changes the outfit you wear which is different than your equipment dictates mostly how you look.

Outfits 4/5:
The normal outfits you can get and buy at the beginning are not that special but there are some legendary outfits that are really cool looking, perhaps this AC has some of the best outfits and also mostly unique outfits with entirely different looks and feel to them instead of feeling like re-used outfits with different colors I am talking about the legendary outfits.

Horse riding 3/5:
The camel is very slow, the horse is faster but also a bit too slow for my taste, you can't really do much, the sprint button is permanently turned on. You can't go faster or slower, to me this didn't feel intuitive and made riding a horse boring. But it was still doable and it got the job done.

Overall it is a good game especially if you love the theme of Egypt and the lore behind it. And I did enjoy it for the most part. But not one of my favorite AC games and the only one I probably won't replay since Egypt just didn't speak to me in the same way Viking culture did in Valhalla. But for me personally the game had a bland feeling all looks with no substance.\t\t\t\t\t
