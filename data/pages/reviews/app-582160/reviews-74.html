\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely worth it when one of the big 70-80% off sales rolls around. I would recommend playing the "Mythology Trilogy" (Origins, Odyssey, Valhalla) in order. This one will feel somewhat simplified compared to the other two if you start with them.

The combat is a very light version of the Souls series. The map is large but doesn't feel too overwhelming as the majority of it is literally empty desert. There is a decent amount of weapon variety. You use bows and melee weapons. Those are in turn broken down into more subclasses. You also have a very rudimentary crafting system to increase your stats.

Again, I do recommend this game on sale. I'm not sure if it's worth it at full price unless you are a die-hard AC fan or just completely love ancient Egypt. The graphics still hold up quite well for being a 6 year old game and the story is not too bad for a typical open world Ubi game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, it's certainly a better experience than Assassin's Creed: Odyssey, but I can only put that down to there being less content to inevitably bore you. It doesn't feel quite like other AC games either, as you barely leave the Animus to attend to current day Assassin/Templar business, which is a refreshing positive. AC: Origins is set in the Ptolemaic period of Egypt, and is centred around a man named Bayek and his wife Aya, who are seeking revenge on a group who've wronged their family. The main story is fairly straight-forward, but felt transparent at times; with some interactions making you question the path your character is choosing to take.

Even this long after release, certain parts of the game still feel unpolished, especially climbing and free running. You often get stuck or slowed down when the ground isn't quite level, but it's not for realism's sake, it's just because your character can't figure out where to move to next. It's not a huge issue, but it can get frustrating when you're on the run and Bayek leaps atop a table and waits motionless, no matter your input, until he's been killed by an arrow. Cut-scenes are a bit of a mess as well, as you'll often notice an NPC or animal walking straight through the person or object of your attention.

In terms of combat, melee felt clunky and the shield was a totally unwelcome addition, for me personally. Dodging makes much more sense for an agile Medjay warrior than lugging around a shield barely large enough to block a dagger. Luckily, stealth and ranged combat didn't have the same issue, and the addition of specialist bow types makes for more versatility in your approach to encounters. Clearing guarded locations with stealth is extremely satisfying.

Much like AC: Odyssey, the best thing about the game is the beautiful environment, which is a genuine joy to explore. I felt that the size of the game world was much more appropriate here, so I didn't feel like it was becoming a grind until I was just about done. There's not much else to it, just a basic revenge story, set in an interesting time period and a beautiful atmosphere. I had fun with it, but I'd probably only advise picking it up on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAssassin's Creed Origins had major potential to be the ultimate third act for Desmond Miles' saga, but it missed the chance to weave the series threads together. Personally, I mashed up AC3's key plots with Origins in my head, making it my ideal ending. But that's just me. Sadly, the narrative lost its punch, leaving me craving a deeper link between past and present stories.

The gameplay? A legit win! Weapons and skills galore, letting you flex your style. Side quests get a bit dj vu, but the RPG vibes hit right. Leveling up feels lit, powering you up against foes.

AC Origins, IS the Desmond finale we hoped for , but it's still a captivating ride through ancient Egypt. Memorable story, diverse gameplay, and epic side quests make it a solid play. Despite the present-day plot lacking past game vibes, Origins is a journey worth revisiting. #AssassinsCreedOrigins #WouldPlayAgain \t\t\t\t\t
