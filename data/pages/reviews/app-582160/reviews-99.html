\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommended: Yes

Playtime: 162 hours

Achievement Completion: 100%

Game Length: 30-84 Hours

Reviewed on: PC

Pros:
1. Stunning Open World: Assassin's Creed Origins introduces a beautifully crafted rendition of ancient Egypt. The attention to detail is commendable, with sprawling deserts, bustling cities, and lush oases that breathe life into the world.
2. Engaging Combat System: The combat system has undergone a significant overhaul. It feels more dynamic and strategic, incorporating elements of timing and positioning. The introduction of hitboxes adds depth to encounters.
3. Rich Historical Setting: The game excels in immersing players in ancient Egyptian history. The meticulous recreation of landmarks, costumes, and cultural elements adds an educational dimension to the experience.
4. Deep RPG Elements: Origins introduces RPG-like mechanics, such as a leveling system, skill tree, and loot. This provides a sense of progression and customization, allowing players to tailor Bayek to their playstyle.
5. Compelling Storyline: The narrative is well-crafted, weaving together personal revenge with larger political intrigues. Bayek's character is both relatable and driven, making players emotionally invested in his journey.
6. Immersive Side Quests: Side quests are not mere fetch quests but often mini-stories in themselves. They provide context to the world and add depth to the characters, making them worth pursuing.
7. Fluid Parkour and Navigation: The parkour system has been refined, offering a smooth and intuitive experience. Scaling structures and traversing the environment feels natural and enjoyable.

Cons:
1. Occasional Technical Glitches: While generally polished, there were instances of minor graphical glitches and occasional AI hiccups. These, however, did not significantly detract from the overall experience.
2. Steep Learning Curve: The game's systems, especially the RPG elements, might be overwhelming for newcomers to the series. It takes time to fully grasp and utilize the depth of the mechanics.
3. Pacing in the Middle Act: The middle act of the story could feel a bit drawn out for some players. The pacing dips slightly, and certain missions may seem repetitive.

Overview:
Assassin's Creed Origins is a monumental step forward for the series. Its breathtaking rendition of ancient Egypt, combined with engaging gameplay and a compelling narrative, sets a new standard for open-world action-adventure games. The game's attention to historical accuracy and commitment to RPG mechanics are laudable.

Gameplay:
The gameplay in Assassin's Creed Origins is a true evolution of the series. The introduction of hitboxes and a more intricate combat system adds depth and challenge. Players are rewarded for strategic thinking and mastering the timing of attacks. The RPG elements provide a sense of progression, allowing for customization of Bayek's abilities and playstyle. The integration of stealth mechanics feels intuitive, providing players with multiple approaches to missions. The world itself encourages exploration, with hidden treasures and dynamic wildlife. Overall, the gameplay is a well-balanced blend of action, stealth, and RPG elements that keeps players engaged throughout.

Graphics and Sound:
The visual and auditory aspects of Assassin's Creed Origins are nothing short of exceptional. The attention to detail in recreating ancient Egypt is staggering. From the architecture of cities to the subtleties of daily life, every element feels meticulously researched and faithfully represented. The lighting effects, especially during dawn and dusk, enhance the beauty of the world. The sound design complements the visuals perfectly, with a sweeping score and immersive ambient sounds. The voice acting is top-notch, with Bayek's portrayal being particularly noteworthy. The overall presentation elevates the game to a cinematic experience.

Story and Narrative:
The storytelling in Assassin's Creed Origins is a standout feature. The narrative weaves personal tragedy with larger political struggles in a seamless manner. Bayek's journey is both emotionally charged and politically significant, making players deeply invested in his quest for revenge. The supporting cast is well-written, with each character having their own motivations and arcs. The historical setting is utilized effectively, providing context and depth to the plot. The game strikes a fine balance between the personal story of Bayek and the broader implications of his actions within the historical context.

Performance and Technical Issues:
While generally polished, there were occasional technical hiccups. Some players may encounter minor graphical glitches, and there were rare instances of AI behaving unexpectedly. However, these issues were infrequent and did not significantly impact the overall experience. Load times were generally reasonable, and the game ran smoothly on a mid-range gaming PC.

Multiplayer and Community:
Assassin's Creed Origins primarily focuses on the single-player experience, with no traditional multiplayer modes. However, the game incorporates a community aspect through the discovery tour mode, which allows players to explore the world in a more educational context. The lack of competitive multiplayer is not a detriment to the overall experience, as the single-player content is rich and engaging.

Final Thoughts:
Assassin's Creed Origins is a triumph in the open-world action-adventure genre. Its stunning recreation of ancient Egypt, engaging gameplay, and compelling narrative make it a standout title in the series. While minor technical hiccups are present, they do not significantly detract from the overall experience. For fans of the franchise and newcomers alike, Assassin's Creed Origins is a must-play. Its rich historical setting, deep RPG elements, and immersive gameplay combine to create an unforgettable gaming experience.\t\t\t\t\t
