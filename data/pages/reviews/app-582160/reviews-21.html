\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite the usual Uplay nuisances, Assassin's Creed Origins manages to transport players to the vibrant and intriguing world of ancient Egypt. The breathtaking historical setting and landscapes are the game's strongest asset. The times where I was exploring in a carefree manner, without any specific goal were the most enjoyable.

However, beneath the surface level lies a bunch of underdeveloped and poorly thought out systems. I wouldn't discount the game for this reason alone, but the most egregious misstep is the narrative execution.

What begins as a seemingly promising tale of revenge and redemption soon loses its way, mostly due to the mishandling of Aya, one of the central characters. Initially introduced as a strong and capable ally to the protagonist, Aya's character development takes a nosedive halfway through the story, being suddenly transformed into a one-dimensional plot device.

After these issues became apparent, I still forced myself to complete the game mostly because I wanted to see how it would end. My journey through ancient Egypt ultimately culminated not in a triumphant flourish, but with a whimper of resignation. I was glad to be done with it.

Unfortunately, in the end, this game stands out as unfulfilled potential. While the allure of exploring one of history's most fascinating eras initially captivated me, it became impossible to overlook the poorly crafted narrative, as it completely undermines the overall experience.

If you're a fan of open world games or this particular historical setting, there may be something for you here, but my journey through ancient Egypt was nothing more than a climb up the pyramid of disappointment.\t\t\t\t\t
