Pros
+ I skipped from Black Flag to this AC game and the series has made major strides. There is now a full RPG-style talent tree, a revamped combat system that isn't just holding block and countering, gear slots, mounts, and a lot more.
+ Massive game. To get all collectibles and achievements would be a monumental achievement. Even sticking to main content, this game will take a good long while to complete.
+ The world is beautiful. The way the sand and water move, monuments, statues, the diversity of markets and natural environments, the stylish combat... it all looks excellent.
+ Rooting many elements of the world in actual Egyptian history is cool, if a little heavy handed at times.

Cons
- Like every AC game, the story is convoluted nonsense. Is it about revenge for killing a man's son, who sits on the throne, Roman cultural competition with Egypt, or all the above? Who are the people with the animal nicknames and why are they "controlling the people"? What are the politics of the revolution we are participating in beyond vague hand waving at "freedom"? Don't expect answers that make sense.
- The side missions are extremely repetitive and uninteresting, with only a few exceptions. By 20 hours into the game, I was mashing buttons through side quest dialogue scenes. You're rescuing someone from a bandit camp or grabbing an item, with some very long and boring dialogue scenes in the middle. I would recommend sticking to the main quest, but you will need to level up on side stuff or be under leveled.
- So. Many. Escort. Missions.
- NPCs are have very little "intelligence", being easily predictable and glitchy. My favorite is the guards on horses that shoot you with an arrow and the gallop away right to the maximum aggro distance, come charging back, shoot once, repeat.
- The dialogue seems like it is written by a teenage boy capable of brief moments of profundity.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAssassin's Creed Origins marks a significant departure from the traditional formula of the franchise, yet it emerges as a shining example of innovation and reinvention. Origins transports players to the vibrant and mysterious world of ancient Egypt. This installment redefines the Assassin's Creed experience, offering a sprawling open-world adventure filled with rich history, captivating characters, and dynamic gameplay mechanics. At its core, Assassin's Creed Origins retains the fundamental elements that define the series, such as parkour traversal, stealth-based combat, and a narrative-driven storyline. However, it introduces a plethora of new features that breathe new life into the franchise. The most notable addition is the overhaul of the combat system, which shifts towards a more action-oriented approach reminiscent of RPGs. Players engage in visceral melee combat, utilizing a variety of weapons and abilities to dispatch enemies with precision and skill. The introduction of a loot system adds depth to gameplay, allowing players to customize their playstyle with a diverse array of weapons and gear. Moreover, Origins introduces a vast open-world environment ripe for exploration, with dynamic day-night cycles, wildlife, and diverse landscapes ranging from bustling cities to expansive deserts. The introduction of a quest-driven progression system encourages players to venture off the beaten path, uncovering hidden secrets and engaging in side activities that add depth to the world. While Assassin's Creed Origins deviates from the traditional Assassin-Templar conflict, it delivers a compelling narrative steeped in ancient Egyptian mythology and history. Players assume the role of Bayek of Siwa, a Medjay tasked with unraveling a conspiracy that threatens the stability of Egypt. The storyline is masterfully crafted, weaving together themes of revenge, redemption, and the struggle for justice in a world teetering on the brink of chaos. As players journey across the vast expanse of Egypt, they encounter a diverse cast of characters, each with their own motivations and agendas. Origins also explores the origins of the Assassin Brotherhood, shedding light on its early foundations and the ideological principles that define its members. While the game deviates from the traditional Assassin-Templar conflict, it offers a fresh perspective on the series' lore, enriching the overall narrative experience. Assassin's Creed Origins sets a new standard for visual fidelity and atmospheric design, showcasing the beauty and majesty of ancient Egypt in stunning detail. From the towering pyramids of Giza to the bustling streets of Alexandria, every location is meticulously recreated with breathtaking authenticity. The game's attention to detail is evident in its environmental design, with lush landscapes, bustling cities, and bustling marketplaces brimming with life and activity. The dynamic weather system further enhances immersion, with sandstorms sweeping across the desert and thunderstorms lighting up the night sky. The audio design of Assassin's Creed Origins is equally impressive, with immersive soundscapes that transport players to ancient Egypt. From the bustling streets of Memphis to the tranquil shores of Lake Mareotis, every sound is finely tuned to enhance the player's experience. The soundtrack, composed by Sarah Schachner, is a haunting blend of traditional Egyptian instruments and orchestral arrangements. Each track sets the tone for exploration, combat, and emotional moments, capturing the essence of ancient Egypt and immersing players in its rich history and mythology.

Assassin's Creed Origins stands as a testament to Ubisoft's willingness to innovate and reinvent the franchise while staying true to its core principles. Despite its departure from the traditional Assassin-Templar conflict, Origins delivers a captivating open-world experience filled with rich history, dynamic gameplay mechanics, and stunning visuals. Whether exploring the majestic pyramids of Giza or engaging in visceral combat against ancient foes, players will find themselves immersed in a world of wonder and adventure unlike any other in the Assassin's Creed series.
