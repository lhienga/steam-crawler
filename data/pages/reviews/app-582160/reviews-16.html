\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComing in as a not-so-traditional Assassin's Creed Fan, I have thoroughly enjoyed my 40+ hours of playing from the main quest alone. As a game itself, it is absolutely stunning. The graphics (although quite demanding on your average computer) looks and feels great, and the whole experience of an open world exploration is addictive.

I played this game mostly on hard mode controller, and the enemies were quite alright at the beginning of the play-through. Once I started focusing on the upgrades for the hidden blade and archery damage, it definitely became a whole lot more easier to clear forts. The combat mechanics of this game are also quite logical, although I do find myself neglecting the use of the shield around 90% of the time (if I did have to go into the combat state).

If there is one thing to criticise about the game, it is the movement mechanics. Some of the movement (especially when climbing) feels a little unresponsive to what your intentions are. Riding any sort of animal also poses a movement challenge since the animal feels like it doesn't really want to do what you are inputting. As a result, travelling over some of the terrain, whether it is world landscape or trying to climb into a fort, feels very janky and unresponsive. This discourages the player to actually explore the world and prefer to use fast travel to get from place to place.

Overall, I would encourage anyone to play this game. Although it is not quite your traditional way of playing, I think this is a highly enjoyable experience, and I can see myself returning to the game (even with the main quest concluded) to completely finish the game and all it has to offer.\t\t\t\t\t
