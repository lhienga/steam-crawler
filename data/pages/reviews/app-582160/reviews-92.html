\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAssassin's Creed Origins was a game I eagerly anticipated because it promised a new direction for the series, taking us to a stunning recreation of ancient Egypt. As a history enthusiast and a lover of exotic settings, this installment immediately piqued my interest. While it does deliver on its promises in many aspects, it also bears the hallmarks of Ubisoft's open-world design that can be both a blessing and a curse.

First and foremost, the world of ancient Egypt that Ubisoft has crafted here is nothing short of breathtaking. The attention to detail and the sheer scale of the landscape left me in awe. From the sprawling deserts to the bustling cities, every inch of the map feels alive and authentic, making it an absolute treat for history buffs like me.

However, it's here that Assassin's Creed Origins encounters its first stumbling block. The vastness of the world, coupled with the Ubisoft penchant for filling every nook and cranny with collectibles, objectives, and activities, can be overwhelming. It often feels like you're drowning in a sea of side quests, collectibles, and objectives that become repetitive after a while. The game is teeming with content, but a lot of it lacks the depth and engagement that would make it truly satisfying. I found myself sighing at times, wondering if I was just going through the motions.

The side quests, in particular, left me wanting more. They tend to be rather lackluster, and while they occasionally provide interesting stories, the majority feel like uninspired fetch quests or glorified busywork. It's a missed opportunity to delve deeper into the rich history and culture of Egypt.

On the bright side, the main story of Assassin's Creed Origins manages to rise above the clutter. It offers an intriguing narrative with well-crafted characters and a glimpse into the birth of the Assassin Brotherhood. Exploring the backstory of the Assassins in this ancient setting was a highlight for me, and it gave me the motivation to push through some of the game's more tedious aspects.

In conclusion, Assassin's Creed Origins is a double-edged sword. On one hand, it's a visually stunning and ambitious take on the series, transporting players to a meticulously recreated ancient Egypt. On the other hand, it succumbs to the Ubisoft open-world formula of quantity over quality, resulting in a bloated map filled with repetitive content. The side quests, in particular, are a letdown. If you can overlook these issues and focus on the main story, there's an enjoyable experience to be had. But be prepared for a fair amount of grinding and repetition along the way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a person who played Assassin's Creed Odyssey first, I have to say I enjoyed this one more when it comes to story and exploration.

Bayek of Siwa is a medjay that will do everything to save Egypt and its people from the Greeks and Romans alike. We go through quests based on historic events that happened in real life and get to meet iconic historical characters such as Cleopatra, Ptolemy XIII (her brother), Julius Caesar, Vitruvius, etc... We also get to visit historical places like the pyramids of Giza, and even some of them that are long gone, such as the Great Library of Alexandria.

I have to point out the existence of the Discovery Tour mode, which I highly recommend if you want to get to know more about Ancient Egypt's culture and history!

Overall, Origins is a great game that will tell you about, as the title suggests itself, the origins of the Assassin's Creed.\t\t\t\t\t
