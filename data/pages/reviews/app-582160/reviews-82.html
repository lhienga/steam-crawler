\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHands down the best of the RPG trilogy of Assassin's Creed games. Bayek is a fantastic main character and very well voice acted and the world of Ancient Egypt is absolutely incredible to explore. Most importantly though, Origins still has the spirit of Assassin's Creed that Odyssey and Valhalla have lacked. You still feel like an assassin and you are still eliminating targets even though the Brotherhood itself doesn't exist yet.

Also, even though you gotta deal with Uplay, Origins also runs fantastic on the Steam Deck. Granted, you can't leave your house as it requires a constant internet connection, but to just chill around your house it runs fantastic at 30 fps and was a blast to play. On PC it is fairly well optimized and I haven't really had any issues.

Overall, I highly recommend this game. Even though I don't like where the series went after this it does not change that I think Origins is a great game. It's not the best open world game you'll ever play but it is still a great time.\t\t\t\t\t

I mean, the first 20 enemy camps with with pattern "kill yellow fellas and collect treasures" was nice, but God, nobody can enjoy doing 1000 of these camps. The main story is just right, main characters are great.

The Hidden Ones DLC is pretty mediocre in gameplay and area (same brown rocks and yellow desert as Egypt), but the cutscenes and story are great.

The Curse of the Pharaohs DLC has amazing locations (pieces of Afterlife) and interesting bosses, but the story aspects are meh. The ending is just not there. You can feel the rushed development in this DLC. But still not bad.\t\t\t\t\t
