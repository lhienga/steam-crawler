\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom a graphic perspective, the game has received a lot of praise. The atmosphere of Hellenistic Egypt is truly amazing. The plot unfolds against the backdrop of real historical events, and it's closely intertwined with those events. Of course, the developers had a lot of freedom to let their imagination run wild, so there are some differences from real historical facts. However, if we consider this as a work of fiction, then it's acceptable.As for the main events in the plot, from the perspective of the series, they are once again focused on revenge. In this regard, the fourth assassin still stands out in the entire series. However, despite the banality and the well-worn theme of the hero, I must admit that the plot is not dull or tedious. This is because the main character is very human, and his character is well developed, with clear and justified motives. He does not seem like a cardboard figure, which is certainly a pleasant surprise.Among the drawbacks, I would like to mention rare bugs and a slightly monotonous gameplay experience. The latter may not even be specific to "Origins", but rather to the series as a whole. Another issue is the tendency to create worlds that are simply enormous in size. The map in the game is incredibly vast, which is a controversial aspect. Egypt, for example, does not have a wide variety of flora or fauna, so we see mostly deserts and oases in the game, which are not very different from each other.
I cannot comment on the in-game store, as I did not purchase anything or donate items during my playtime. I have played the game twice, and both times without making any donations.The bottom line is, if you are not a big fan of the Assassin's Creed series, you might enjoy the game. This game is not an Assassin's Creed game, as the Order has not yet been formed in the plot. I don't particularly like modern elements in the series and don't fully understand Laura, so I simply enjoyed the game without comparing it to previous parts.
The plot is interesting and the graphics are beautiful. The soundtrack is impressive and the historical setting is amazing. If you are a fan of Ancient Egypt, it is a must-play!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwful. Awful. Awful. Awful. Awful.

Everything is awful. The stealth. The combat. The writing. Invisible ledges that stop you in your tracks. Anything interesting is immediately ruined by gameplay that actively fights you.

This experience is hazardous to your mental health. Save yourself the heartache and read the wiki for the lore.

Edit 2/5/24: Since I've calmed down from my tilt I want to throw in real quick that Abubakar Salim's performance gave us our most enjoyable assassin since Ezio and Edward. However, since this game was developed during the Me Too movement, he's consistently sidelined as a hype man for Aya so she can get the major story kills (spoiler alert but I stopped caring), or suddenly reduced in intelligence so she can display arrogance and insecurity that makes her incredibly unlikable (if a female character has a side-braid, that's usually a sign she's terribly written). I understand Ubisoft originally wanted to make her the main character, and Bayek was added due to a lack of confidence with having a female protagonist (I beg to differ with Aveline, Shao Jun, and Elise), but this was not the way to handle it. It was the same deal with Evie and they botched her too.

The tombs, especially in Giza, add a mystery and immersion that I enjoyed. Combined with the mirages you experience in deserted regions, there's an atmosphere that harkens back to the first Assassin's Creed. Other than that, it's the same tedious fort over and over again on the map. The DLC is even worse.

That's about it. I guess it wasn't so quick.\t\t\t\t\t
