\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter AC3, I've fallen into a years-long fatigue of the series. Following Desmond's story as an anchor tying the different story threads together, it felt concluded and the setting was not tickling my nerves as much as the previous settings did.

Narrative was always what was driving my interest in the series - the mix of present-day and historic memory, the intertwining of real historic events with the fictional, world-encompassing conspiracy and then of course, the Isu background story. A brilliant mix of rich history, mystery and fiction. And the titles after AC3 abandoned that formula to quite a degree, where none of the story threads seemed to really matter in the bigger picture.

With Origins, my excitement for the series has returned. Not only is the historic part rich in vastness, narrative and interesting characters, the dramaturgy plays out in quite a unique and emotional way. There are several goosebump moments that lead into the formation of the Brotherhood that are brilliantly executed, especially with the use of music.

In terms of gameplay, I've been surprised that the main story does actually not lead you to explore all regions. That leaves for more exploration and interesting side quests rather than just going for an achievement hunt after "finishing" the game. Of course, certain types of tasks start getting repetitive after a while, but the story and beautiful environments to explore help.

I've finished the main story after 67 hours and still got lots to explore. But after this, I am curious what the next titles will bring, since there is some foreshadowing of a more continuous story.\t\t\t\t\t
