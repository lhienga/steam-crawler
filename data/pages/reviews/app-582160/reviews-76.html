\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOrigins left a somewhat positive impression on me years ago so I thought why not replay it? The general state of the game is weird. It still looks pretty good apart from minor pop-in, some issues LOD and some low res textures but the cut-scene have some godawful stutter which is annoying. The game runs fine but sometime you can see that the entire thing is held together by duct tape. Enemies will start spazzing out, Bayek will refuse to climb, regular sound clips will play during story cut-scenes etc. There are a lot of questionable decisions surrounding the game like the OST. Which is actually not bad at all but you barely get to hear the music because the default setting makes it so the music plays only on the seldom occasion. The story is incomprehensible and meaningless gobbledygook, modern day and some alien nonsense that Ubisoft cant help to include. The in-game story fares slightly better. Is a simple revenge story with clandestine organizations woven around a historical time period and surrounding events. But is suffers from half a dozen of issues. The pacing is all over the place due to its nature as an open world game and its rushed approach to the subject matter. The villains also get almost no time to develop. There is another big issue with I am going to mention when I talk about the first DLC expansion. The only saving grace regarding the main campaign and the games story and characters is the protagonist Bayek, voiced by Abubakar Salim. He does a fantastic job and Bayek at least feels like a character when he deals with his tragic personal events and when engaging with the various NPCs. Probably the most positive thing about the game is its rendition of Ptolemaic Egypt. It is fun taking in the sights and sounds and marveling at all the ancient wonders of the world. There is also a dedicated mode teaching a lot of things about the time period and its people and I wish more games with a historical setting would incorporate something like this. There are two things that you will be doing most of the time throughout the game and that is fighting and sneaking/assassinating. Both things unfortunately don't work and feel under-cooked. The combat system feels tacked on and should have either been completely reworked or scrapped entirely. One major issue is that it feels like a bad turn based system even though it isn't. The enemies will do an attack and then you will punish them with three attacks of your own until they magically recover, repeat until someone dies. Some enemies even have a dodge that wont allow you to hit them until you evaded one attack. Or will tank through your attacks and stunlock you into oblivion. And some mechanics that could have given the combat more depth like perfect dodge and parry are locked behind the skill tree which gives me the opportunity to talk about the RPG mechanics of the game. Its become an unfortunate reality within the AAA industry to shove in systems and mechanics that are poorly implemented. The entire XP system and all the incremental upgrades should have been left out of the game since there are no meaningful player choices to be found here. The skill tree is just a perfect example of everything wrong with AAA games. Incremental 1% more damage increases are of course to be found because lazy design decisions are apparently mandatory within the industry. Abilities like sleep darts are unlocked through skill points rather than being an upgrade to be found and earned by doing a quests. QoL like loot pickups upon defeating an enemy are also locked behind a skill same with the ability to unlock the option to buy chariots which is just baffling. The biggest issue I have is that combat and stealth have no good upgrades that enhance the game. There are things missing that could have alleviated some issues. Lets give an example by talking about the stealth and assassinating mechanics and the issues surrounding those. The first issue comes from the RPG mechanics which means the one hit-one kill assassination stops working on higher level targets. You need to upgrade your hidden blade so its works most of the time. This is more of a minor nitpick. Biggest problem of the stealth system is the inconsistency of the enemy AI. A good stealth game will make sure enemies don't suddenly turn towards you when you are approaching them from behind. Which will happen all the time here. The cone of vision for hostile NPCs is also screwed. Sometimes they see you even though they shouldn't, again being a detriment to the stealth. There is also some sort of noise system in place that is never properly explained. Some Assassinations will alert nobody and some times soldiers will hear it and turn to discover you even though they are far away. It of course does not help that the prompt for assassination stops working half the time especially if an enemy is sitting at a desk. The skill tree also fails at mitigating the design issues and enhancing the stealth. There are only two skills that are decently useful but they can also be counted as QoL. Now I already mentioned that the story isn't really working and I will explain that further while about the first DLC although it is pretty clear that we are talking about cut content here. The main story suffers from being rushed and incomplete while introducing the nonsense Assassins Creed lore. The main story, the conflict between Ptolemy and Cleopatra takes to long to get going and is cut short because it apparently was necessary to shove in the Roman part of history. But that whole affair between Caesar and Cleo is again rushed, just a couple of missions and this part is also done. Mark Anthony's part in all of this is never shown, Cleopatra also basically disappears . I think the first DLC is cut content because it takes places after the main story,even though it really is a continuation of it. It continues with the Romans but in Sinai. There are character form the main game that appear here and the whole Roman civil war is never mentioned. The ending of this DLC feels like the actually ending to the main campaign. Now there is the second DLC Curse of the Pharaohs which I played but never finished. And I was looking forward to exploring it thoroughly. It not only takes place in Thebes and introduces a lot of mythology but I also was hoping for a lot of influences from the book Sinuhe the Egyptian by Mika Waltari. But there is another big issue that I have to mention. There is so much padding and bloat due to the sheer size of the game world. None of which was really necessary. The game cant depict this part at an actual scale which is fine but there are countless unnecessary parts of the map that either because there is nothing or little to do or because they are just filler. It would have gone a long way at alleviating some of the visual fatigue by integrating the Sinai and Thebes maps into the main campaign, apart from aforementioned story issues. Main draw of the second DLC is its big focus on mythology. The game looks pretty good already even if its is starting to show its age but those vistas within the mythological realm are stunning. There are a lot of details that hobby historians will understand that help making it feel authentic. The story at least feels somewhat likes its own thing and not some sloppy seconds like the previous one. But it also suffers from a weak villain and not enough time exploring the history and mythology. It also has some of the worst combat encounters in the game. And as far as I could tell there were no really Easter eggs related to Sinuhe the Egyptian which was thoroughly disappointing. Under-cooked, unfocused, incomprehensible and incomplete are some negative words I would describe the game with. Only play if you love the time period or want to waste your time with a disappointing AAA open world game.
