Still a tasty treat!

PROS: Overcooked! All You Can Eat is a complete edition consisting of both Overcooked! games, all DLC, & some additional stages to entice owners of the previous titles. The games still have that goofy charm to them with their silly plot & irreverent stages. The controls are tight & fluid, leaving little room for error outside of human control. The variety of stages & dishes is still just as nice as ever, with different mechanics unique to each stage. The soundtrack is equally as charming, with the overworld theme being the easiest to stick in one's memory. Finally, the co-op is just as fun as ever, making the stages both easier to complete & more chaotic to run about, as well as increasing the star required score due to the number of chefs running about.

CONS: Overcooked! 1 feels slightly out of place, the lack of a throw food option making the game feel a bit slower. Where the stages are varied, some can be quite annoying due to the individual level features, such as ice physics in the ice world. The largest con for fans of the series would be the game's status. As a complete edition of both titles, this is not a bundle, meaning players who already own both games will already have about 90% of this in their collection already.

ACHIEVEMENTS: This is actually an easier list than the original titles this was based off of. The removal of the heavier grind achievements have been replaced with ones requiring multiple chefs to be specific chefs. This needs to be done online, or locally using a controller & keyboard/second controller. There really isn't too much difficulty here, other than completing the main campaigns if one finds them difficult. One doesn't even need to finish a majority of the DLC to clear the list either.

FINAL: Overcooked! All You Can Eat is a fun bundle of two fast paced, enjoyable games with some extra DLC added for those who want more. It's colorful style & frenetic gameplay creates a wonderful experience, be it solo, couch co-op, or online. Though owners of the titles would be much better off waiting for a sale, newcomers should jump on this first rather than start with the earlier games. A solid recommendation for anyone interested.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing this game with my sister crossplay with steam and xbox, it has worked almost perfectly for me on PC, she has experienced things like lag/glitches on xbox but nothing that entirely broke the game or made it unplayable. Our connection only crashed once over two 4+ hour play sessions. The assist options also make solo play enjoyable for me! The array of assist options are great, from simply upping a couple timers to being able to skip annoying levels entirely.

My only complaints are:
-the DLCs (extra trimmings) are more prone to lag and glitches with crossplay, as well as any levels with portals
-World food festival has a level that randomly stopped allowing me to plate dishes even while playing solo.
-Some arcade versus levels have no sink to wash dishes

All this to say:
-This game is everything I want from overcooked, 1 and 2 are reliable games, the DLCs have their issues but all in all are enjoyable and playable.
-If you like overcooked and want to be able to crossplay with a friend, it is playable and works well the majority of the time (In my experience for steam+xbox with only 2 players total).
-If you want to play overcooked but you were turned away by the difficulty of later levels, the assist options are incredible for making the game as easy as you need it to be.\t\t\t\t\t
