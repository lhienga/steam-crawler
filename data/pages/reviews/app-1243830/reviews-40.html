Furthermore you can play with friends around the globe without worrying about finding a host that doesn't lag. There will be lag and glitches still but it seems to be very likely that it isn't unplayable anymore when your friend is on the other side of the continent since this is server based instead of peer to peer. Considering the slow update rate (1 week to fix controllers partially) maybe the devs should think about taking advantage of the beta release option to further test their updates after QA is done with their tests.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYOU ABSOLUTE DONUT

Real talk. The game is Overcooked 1 and 2 with all the DLC repackaged. Some visual optimizations have been done but it's not all that different of an experience from the other games. If you already own the other two, it's likely not worth the purchase. The ONLY reason I'm purchasing this game is because of the inclusion of cross-platform multiplayer: I have a niche case where netplay options like Steam Remote Play Together and Parsec aren't viable. In my view, there isn't anything else here at launch that would convince me to buy.

Don't get me wrong, it's a good game. It doesn't give much value beyond the original games, but if you haven't played Overcooked before, this is a fantastic bundle. And who knows: maybe the free content promised later this year might make up for the lack of new content... I'm not crossing my fingers for that, but we shall see.\t\t\t\t\t
