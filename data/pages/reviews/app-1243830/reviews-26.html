\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is chaos

Im not exactly the greatest example of manual dexterity, but this game (or rather, OC1 and OC2) is nearly impossible when playing alone; the craziest levels actually give you more than one chef to control at once, which might be easier on a controler because I guess (?) you dont have to swap which one youre controling at any given time

But then, there are the obstacles

Cooking on a ship which moves some of your tables as it rocks to the waves
Cooking over ice, which means the floor is slippery
Cooking on a kitchen standing over a tectonic plate
COOKING ON A KITCHEN DIVIDED INTO 2 OR 3 <REDACTED> MOVING TRUCKS

Some of these are truly aggravating (so far, for me, the truck ones are the worst)

But

The game is cute; it has charm. The characters are always with a happy face. They seem to truly enjoy what they are doing. And once you find friends to play with, you can kinda understand them. Sure, your pal just threw your perfectly fine dish of fish and chips into the ocean and half the kitchen's on fire because you yourself slid off the platform, but most of the time you'll (probably) be thrash talking eachother because of those mistakes.

I bought OC1 originally, but kinda gave up on it because of the difficulty I had. Now Im playing with my GF and having a blast with it. And we converted a friend of ours to come get his ass served with us.

As others have said, if you have one or both of the other games, dont buy it full price; there SHOULD be a discount for ppl who have one or both games IMO. But if you're completely new to this series, and/or got to this game during a sale, buy it, convince friends to buy it too, and, as they say, "Bone Apple Tea" (yes, this is wrong)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are some issues with the online play, but it's playable. Not as enjoyable as Overcooked 2 online though, it feels like there is some input latency. I might need to play it more to judge it properly.

You get all the levels from Overcooked 1 and 2, achievements also don't require you to perfect those old levels again.

The way I see it, you buy this game for the "cross-play" feature if you already have Overcooked 1 and 2. It's a shame they didn't just update Overcooked 2 with the feature, but oh well. Cross-play works for me. Maybe it varies for different PCs or regions? I tried UK PC to UK Switch and it seemed to work fine. It's not straightforward, though. You have to add each other with Team17 IDs and you can only see this when you go into Manage Team on the Menu screen (Y on Xbox controller) > Online Play. I thought it was in the settings or on the general menu, it's a bit obscure.

Arcade Public I usually find one other person, but haven't encountered a full house yet like I have with Overcooked 2. Versus Public on both games from what I've seen is dead. Playing with friends is probably the way to go if anyone is online.

At a 60% discount, it's worth it for me.
