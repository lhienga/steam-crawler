\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot this game a while ago and I've been really trying my best to get into it. Let me preface this by saying that I work actual food service. I find cooking to be quite enjoyable and a little bit of micromanagement, especially in a real world setting, is absolutely no problem to me at all. When I played this game for the first time, my experience was a bit akward and uncomfortable, much like how you'd feel at your first day in a real job. This feeling didn't go away however. I initially wrote it off as just me being bad, however the more patient I try to be with this game, the more frustrated I get, and I think I should get my views on this game out just to validate the feelings of those in the same predicament as me, since I really didn't see anybody acknowledging this in the reviews. Maybe this is just me and my play style, but this game is FAR too gimmicky for me to play. The base gameplay which consists of prepping food and serving it has virtually no depth to it, making a good 90% of this game pointless, impractical, micromanagement due to all the pointless and nonsensical gimmicks in the game. If there's anything I know from my experiences with games, it's that difficulty is no substitute for gameplay, and this game is practically the reason I believe that. Sometimes you'll be prepping food and the entire kitchen starts moving away from you (WTF??!) or the kitchen will have an infestation of super rats that can locate and acquire food as soon as it's dropped. My personal favorite is that level where people are just casually walking through the kitchen. That kind of stupidity and spatial unawareness makes me absolutely livid. If somebody walked through the kitchen at work we'd literally just kick them the ♥♥♥♥ out. So why the hell should I take that ♥♥♥♥ in a game? Every level there's a dumber, more bizarre obstacle that holds dominion over the kitchen that you just happen to be working in. I understand that this is a video game, but this is a game about cooking. Why the hell am I forced to play in these levels that are were so obviously not designed to be cooked in? The level choices are creative, but frustrating and practically designed against you, which I have to hold against the game since it's just so unrealistic for a food service environment. There's a way to make your game difficult without making it this ridiculously frustrating. Take a game like Touhou 6 or Crash Bandicoot for example. Those levels are clearly designed for you to play them, and the difficulty is based on skill, where as in Overcooked it's more about management. You can't just make a game super overwhelming and substitute that in for difficulty. I'm a bit of a perfectionist so I found it hard to even get started on most levels let alone get a good flow going, and that's basically why I'm writing this review. In my opinion, it feels like the player has very little control over the game, and instead of actually introducing skills that let players thrive in that environment, the game focuses more on just putting the player somewhere and forcing them to just deal with it. If I wanted to deal with annoying customers, I'd go back to work and actually get paid for it. This game is really cute and has a lot of accessibility features, but it feels like you're trying to do a job while a group of mentally disabled children running amok and wreaking havoc on your workspace. To give you a better example, this game is like a Mario game where all the levels are water levels, and instead of making them harder as they went on they just added ridiculous spams of enemies. Cute game, not worth the stress at all imo. This is the only game where I can be bored of the gameplay while still stressing out about the levels. Not fun for me, 6/10.\t\t\t\t\t
