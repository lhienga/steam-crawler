\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt took me a few starts until I could figure out many of the core mechanics, but I think I now have a reasonably solid grasp of the game. Overall, Cartel Tycoon has bags of potential, and with the suitable polish, could be the crime strategy game fans of the genre have awaited, particularly in the wake of flops like Empire of Sin.

Pros
+ Appealing design. Other reviews have claimed the game evokes Tropico, and they're not wrong. The map is vibrant and appropriate to its setting.
+ The core mechanics surrounding the production and smuggling of goods and the generation of legal money are pretty satisfying.
+ Cartel members have their strengths and weaknesses and can all serve a particular use within your empire.
+ There is bags of potential for modding in this game.

Cons
- It is not always entirely obvious why a building is not working, why illegal money is not being laundered etc.
- The Loyalty v. Terror balance is a bit off at the moment, in my opinion. It is easy to accumulate Terror but can be prohibitively expensive to diminish it. Whilst there's nothing wrong with this principle, it does lead to large stretches of playtime where you are not doing very much other than waiting (for money to accumulate, for loyalty to grow etc.)
- Enemy gangs and law enforcement are pretty bland at the moment. They should have more flavour.
- Combat is very simplistic; what "score" your cartel members have vs the enemy's strength. Something like temporary bonuses to sue in individual fights could help spice combat up a bit without radically changing the system. For example, a one-time use bomb for attacks on enemy gang holdings increases your attack score.

I will write a full review when I have played more hours. On the whole, though, if you are a fan of tycoon games or crime simulators, this is worth checking out. There are still plenty of kinks to be ironed out, but Cartel Tycoon has the makings of an excellent game when it is the finished article.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is hard at starters but when u get the hang of it - it becomes pretty fun and chill. Also since it launched there was a few minor updates that added some extra options and balanced the game better which made it a bit easier , so thumbs up on that from me. On the plus side soundtrack which is top tier , the graphics/artstyle are nice , management could be a bit better but its not terrible as some ppl complain about. One thing that bothers me is that the fps dropped a lot when I took over most of the regions and had many buildings running at same time (settings dont matter- game runs same on highest as on lowest) but then again it still is early access so I'll let it slip for now. Overall its a decent game with a few flaws here and there and a bit of a hard start but once I played a few hours and learned everything I really enjoyed it.
7/10 (will update the review when more updates roll out)\t\t\t\t\t
