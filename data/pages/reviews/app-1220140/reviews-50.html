\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the concept of this game, but there are a lot of "features" that make it unenjoyable for me. Although I recognize this is still an alpha, I suspect to final game will still inherently carry the excessive hand-holding and flipping of switches on/off.

1) The only way to manually save your progress (disabling auto-save) is to exit the game?

2) Dirty money doesn't balance quickly enough between Stash houses and Money laundering buildings. For example, my Hotel will be overflowing with cash ($65k+). Further collection attempts from smuggling operations result in tens of thousands of dollars in losses while my terror rating explodes and my Casino across the street sits virtually empty (ie $3k / $45k capacity). There's no "smartness" to collection. It should function more like this:

Deposit Cash in Hotel? No, Full.
Deposit Cash in Casino? No. Full.
Deposit Cash in Residence (if connected) Yes.
Top off production/warehouse operations within vicinity? Yes.

Hemorrhaging cash with literally dozens of other places to stash it is stupid.

Edit: Today something broke with the Journalist where I'm holding over $200k in dirty money and can't 'see it' or access it in any of my buildings. Hours into a game and it's ruined. GG.

3) I want my Lieutenants to get paid with dirty money and to go get it themselves wherever closest--rather than stealing goods that are valued 2x-5x more than their normal pay. NOBODY steals from Papi Capo and lives!

4) Production Buildings turn off automatically when funds run low, but don't turn back on when replenished. This is annoying.

5) Police raids on smuggling ports are fine, but more automation is needed here. Give users a policy feature to control the behavior of what happens during these events. Toggling switches all game long is tedious and annoying. Let users automatically shut down and resume operations during these events.

Overall, this game has a lot of potential and could become great with just a bit more automation. For me, I don't particularly enjoy all the hand-holding and turning buttons on and off. I do enjoy the elements of entrepreneurship and free enterprise. But the game is incredibly punishing if you make the wrong move. And rather than find a means to recover from your mistake, you're often sent down a cascading landslide of bull$%^&. Manually saving your game helps alleviate some of these unfortunate mistakes so that you can make more informed decisions, however it forces you to exit the game to do so.\t\t\t\t\t
