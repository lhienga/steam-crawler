Story 2/10
Controls 8/10
Graphics 8/10
Difficulty 10/10

Positive Aspects

  • The soundtrack is truly amazing.
  • The setting and the graphics are really good and welcoming.
  • Easy to use controls. Scrolling should be a bit faster though.
  • Frequent updates and a detailed Trello with a roadmap. Active developers and community at their Discord-server.
  • Runs smoothly and stable. No crashes, bugs, or other errors.

Negative Aspects

Cartel Tycoon is pretty much unplayable at this point.

  • Your buildings require lieutenants, and you can only have 14, plus the leader. 15 is not nearly enough considering some structures need them or they will be lost, and there are other gangs/cartels attacking, and not to forget raids by police, DEA, CIA, military, and eventually US military as well. Since there are 10 areas and in every area, and you need at least one freight hub and one city, which both require lieutenants guarding them or they'll be given up, it's not possible to hold the entire map with 15 lieutenants. And that's not including all lieutenants needed for regular buildings and to repel all attacks, which happens each and every minute. Not to mention you need lieutenants to take over/destroy other buildings and move resources.
  • You need to laundry your dirty money into clean money. Problem is that is also very unbalanced and slow.
  • Output levels from buildings are also unbalanced and the margins are razor-thin. If you place one building too much, you'll go bankrupt.
  • Once you made a few mistakes, and trust me, you will, you'll have the authorities after you permanently. You can temporarily lower the raids but you can't go back on your wanted level.
  • The cooldowns. All of the above would be manageable if the cooldown(and the cost) were lowered a lot. As it is right now, the terror(wanted-level) rises faster than you can lower it just because of the cooldowns, making the game impossible to beat.
  • The journalist events. Don't even get me started on those. They pop up frequently for no apparent reason(though the game states it's because you've spent too much money which is not true), and you have 3 options, bribe them(which is very difficult considering the authorities keeps attacking everything so you cant get dirty money needed to bribe them), kill them, or let them be. The last 2 options increase the terror(wanted-level) a lot. This mechanic is game-breaking and will trap you in an infinite loop that requires your whole playthrough to revolve around combatting this. This type of event needs to be decreased and/or the timer to complete it needs to be a lot longer as it takes away all the fun in playing.
  • The lieutenants require more and more money all the time. In the end, you'll need to kill them(and increasing the terror(wanted-level) and you'll get even more authorities attacking you). And as mentioned before, since there are too few lieutenants available you'll also need to move them around constantly. As a result, gameplay becomes hectic and tedious.
  • Authorities don't raid other gangs/cartels. Only you. It's everyone in the world against you.
  • Even though you go legit and only grow vegetables and try to sell them, the terror(wanted-level) will rise since the authorities believe that you are spending money too quickly in that case as well.
  • Lack of exportcenters. Some regions don't even have a single one and the only one you can build is a small airport, which you need to research first. And the 3 different types also accept different types of cargos. And the export rate is very slow, the airport and harbor are not worth it. It won't matter how many farms or plantations you have, since the airport and harbor hold so little cargo and there are several days between exports. As stated earlier, it's not possible to complete bribing missions with an airport or harbor. You need the road export structure which is only available in 2 regions and cant be manually built. So basically all the other 8 regions are unplayable.
  • You can't change the production output in your buildings, making them overfull and throwing away resources. And having your buildings overfull will also cause a raid from authorities, which will either seize the property or destroy it, and the adjacent buildings as well. An option to stop production at a certain value is needed.
  • You cant change difficulty(yet).

Summary

Extremly unbalanced, making it very difficult. It's not worth the full price. As a matter of fact, it's so unplayable right now that it's not even worth $1. But, it's getting updated regularly. It's an early access game and I don't regret the money spent as I believe this will be a really good game over time based on received feedback. I'm glad I supported the developers and hopefully, they'll sort out these types of issues. I'll make a revisit in half a year and see if things have improved.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe logistics of this game are challenging at first, but if you go through the story mode, read the tool tips on the side with more in depth explanations of how to use different aspects of the game such as delivering drugs to warehouses, combining drugs with legal supplies, how to make more in depth drugs than just opium etc... It starts to make sense.

Yes it autosaves anytime you quit, thus further challenging you to stick it out. Over my 26 hours, I've reset this game countless times and my girlfriend even asked how many times I reset. I had lost count, but that's how I enjoy games, I love making mistakes, going (Oh, THAT'S how that is supposed to be used) and then trying over. And I completely restart because I want to see how good I can get.

Over the course of doing this, I eventually got to the point where I was making way too much dirty money and couldn't hold it in my hotel, residences, or at the Seaport/Checkpoint. I literally didn't have enough buildings to clean all of my money, and started looking at how I could optimize the cities so I could have more residences and less hotels/research centers near where I was exporting so I could continually keep up with cleaning all my dirty money.

I ended up finding out you can directly store money in cities, something I didn't even realize you could do.

I think this game is unique, a lot of fun (personally) and challenges you to make mistakes, learn and keep going.

If you're looking for a straightforward play through and something that will hold your hand, this isn't really it. The devs do guide you in the right direction but also leave a lot to learn. Be patient and try not to get frustrated.

The only thing that's frustrating to me is once you do you get these concepts and learn, the game quickly becomes pretty easy, redundant and boring, but this can be chalked up to it still being in EA, I can't wait for further updates to release more playable characters in story mode, the ability to use the prison and international airport. But until then this game does provide a good amount of content that is enjoyable. I'd recommend a sale if possible, but still a great game nonetheless\t\t\t\t\t
