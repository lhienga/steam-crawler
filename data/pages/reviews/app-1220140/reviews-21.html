So I caved and got a game in its Alpha stage since it seemed that good development was put in from the get go. The mechanics are good and the game sure has potential. I am intrigued to see how far the developers are planning on taking this game. Right now, I do sense the repetition and setting up chains to produce everything or one of the tough products does prove to be its own challenge. Achievements so far are a good balance and I can see the basis of somewhat story lines being developed however characters from all stories appear in each other story lines as recruits so you'd have to think of each scenario as something that exists in a parallel universe of sorts. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not really a fan of the "fix it in post" method of game development, but I'm also not going to complain that a game that started in a bad state ended up coming around after another year of development. That really is the review I have for Cartel Tycoon. When it launched, it had some good ideas but couldn't really figure out what to do with them. After a major revamping of how gangs, logistics, heat and combat worked it has reached that coveted plateau of basically okay. I hesitate to heap too much praise on it, because it's still a fairly simple game, but it's mechanics all finally work together.

The core premise is drug production. You make them. You sell them. After that the game avalanches into a fairly robust number of routes to take. You just need to keep making cash and laundering money, so ... you could build all the production and do all the sales yourself. Or, you could buy drugs from a rival gang. Sure, it takes a chunk out of the profits, but it also bypasses a lot of logistics and tech restrictions. You could do it to curry favor, keep the heat off your back, or set up recruitment. Will you take a route of terror to power? Better stockpile arms by making nice with local militias and gangs, or giving up infrastructure to run them yourself. Hell, combat isn't always the answer: gun smuggling can be quite profitable too! Or maybe you'll just grow vegetables and go broke. It's up to you, it's interesting, a little chaotic, and a bit annoying, but it's not boring. That is how I know the game improved. I went from, "Okay, what now?" at launch to, "Hrm, could I improve my cocaine throughput with the seaport and reduce operating costs? I should probably source avocados from the local towns and I'll need a few new workshops for packing."

As I said before, I think the game still needs work. In the modern day it's easy to think games can always get better so I would like to be clear: my complaints there sum up as balance issues and things that just don't matter. I will avoid hammering them here because you can have plenty of fun before you run into them. Still, what matters is the core loop works now, and the game gives you quite a lot of ways to go about running your drug empire. After winning a game I felt smugly confident going into the next and had all the same issues running my empire, which is a compliment. I sincerely want to see some more polish, QoL and mechanics but as it stands today I can flip my thumb from a down to an up and recommend it for what it is, not what I think it ought to be.\t\t\t\t\t
