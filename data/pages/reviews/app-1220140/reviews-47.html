\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - worth the money, great potential, yet far from being great, finished version.

Game is incredibly immersive and can easily make you stay for longer time.
It has one big flaw, that I hope will be corrected in future updates - balance.
Current one is cool if you wanna play hard mode. But it does not offer you relaxing approach, or even sth in between.

My biggest issues are:
- offense bar - it does not go down with time, and you do not have way to prevent it rising. Also, My wishful thinking makes me want to have this bar local, so it's unique to each city and only highest level would give you national attention.
- Lieutenants - that's the biggest of em all - they are just not worth this insanely high upkeep and they do make me wanna swear a lot while playing.
- No security options - no way to defend myself from police etc.
- Weird mechanic with money tracking - if my 'legal' businesses produce good income, why is there an interest in me spending 30k? It should be somehow adjusted to your possibility of "clean" income.

And a small one - I'd like to see further expansions in smuggling points - additional ships, planes etc.
All this flaws have their place in EA and are somehow expected, so they do not impact the fact, that I am very thrilled to see, how the game will be developed and I am glad I bought it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do not normally do reviews, but I felt this particular game deserves one. I have been playing this game for a while now to include repeated hours from the demo that came out for free, lol. The game is currently playable and I have not encountered any crazy bugs since the last update. What is currently implemented is working as it should. Example, I never really relied on residences because the function to provide dirty money to every facility within range was not working properly, now it is. Also residences now seem to know when to stop collecting dirty money once its storage is full, that was quite annoying especially if it caused attracting the attention of law enforcement every single time. There are a few things I would like to see and hope that they can consider it. First off I like that you cannot just simply afk and make tons of money, I was angry at first cause it seemed like it was cool I can go to bed and wake up in the morning to find i have millions of dirty money and a few hundred thousand clean money. Nope, game pauses cause some cartel member wants a raise or promotion so that scratched that. While the game is called Cartel Tycoon, I still do not get the feeling I am in a cartel or at least running one. We have a nice Cartel family tree so to speak, but it's quite limited. As you progress and eventually have a money making system in place, you really do not need members who have money making skills, what you really need is members in your cartel with more combat or attack points. I have been trying to figure out how to keep myself from getting any terror stars, but every time I've attacked a city controlled by a rival gang it takes so long to take over that I always seem to get that first star. I have tried every trick i can think of, I've even hired several members just to have that extra fire power. You would think odds of 26 cartel vs 6 enemy gang would mean I should win relatively quickly, however that was not the case. Every city, and transportation point needs to have an officer stationed there or else it generates a meter indicating a rival gang member will attack it. This makes good sense, but again we are limited to members of our cartel. I do not feel at this time we have enough to cover the map, and as I already set as an example with combat, try taking over the map with less attack power because you need to keep people in place. I hope they will implement the ability to recruit not just officers, but perhaps troops or guards for each area. I know this is just a game, but when I see shows like Narcos on Netflix lol, those guys had some serious hired man power. Each lab, building, and residences all had guards. This would help alleviate the need to leave officers behind, and also add more reason to make all that dirty money and clean money. I wanted to hire guards.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game seems to be focused on logistics, unfortunately the logistics aspect of the game is one of the worst. You often find yourself building many warehouses (tasked with collecting items from grows and placing them in a central location and/or delivering them to a production workshop). However, there is no way to determine which warehouse delivers to what workshop as long as the workshops are in the sphere of influence of the warehouse. This is fine in the early game but as the game progresses, you need more granular control to determine which warehouse is delivering to specific workshops and which warehouse is collecting items from specific workshops. So mid-late game you end up having to hire and utilize your lieutenants for remedial jobs.

Residences are supposed to help with collecting dirty money from the various seaports, airports, etc into a central location but the dirty money needs to be distributed to various places which have been selected to run off of dirty (rather than legit) money. But the residences only collect the money from an extremely limited number of export points (because there just isn't a place for many additional export points, nor a need) so the residences are mostly useless since they do not distribute the dirty money. So again, you're relegated to using high paid LT's to do remedial tasks.

The whole logistics aspect of the game is rudimentary and without much intelligence or control making the mid-late game far more complicated than it needs to be. If the game is focusing heavily on logistics then the entire logistics gameplay needs a serious look and quite possibly an overhaul.

At this time, this is merely a fun distraction for about an hour but quickly becomes a frustrating game of destroying and constructing buildings a few millimeters away to attempt to manipulate the rudimentary logistics intelligence.\t\t\t\t\t
