\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6.8/10

This is a good game with lots of potential, but it has a strong need for an improvement of quality of life. It's fun, but I would suggest to buy it only on discount -- there are too many things to fix right now.

I would group the needs into 2 categories:
- Lieutenant Management
- Better Logistics

Lieutenant Management
My two struggles are in the needs to have so many lieutenants and micromanage them.
If the enemy attacks you (normal difficulty), they attack with 30+ power. Even if you have researched better defenses and bring weapons, you still need to use nearly all the lieutenants to win. Same story if you want to attack the rival gang and win their city. We are talking about 14~ lieutenants where for each we need to move them to the location with weapons, pick up weapons, say how many weapons, and then tell each to attack the city far away. I basically played this game in PAUSE because it would be impossible to get them all to the city and fight at the right timing otherwise, with the risk of loosing lieutenants.
In all fairness, it's possible to select multiple lieutenants by pressing CTRL, but the issue of picking up weapons persist. Since you cannot manufacture weapons (can only purchase once a while or exchange for goods) it becomes challenging and bothersome to move the weapons around manually with lieutenants (who can carry just 15-20 weapons each max from point A to point B).
Once the fight is over and you won the city, all the Lieutenants are too injured, so you need to move all ~14 of them to the hospital, and then move them back to their original locations (e.g., River Pier) again one by one. It's painful.

What I see as needs:
- Lieutenants should have the option to always carry weapons with them, maybe have a button which makes them go get weapons at the closest location and come back to their current location.
- It should be possible to "group" some lieutenants, so that one can move them all as one group.
- 1 lieutenant should be enough to unlock all shipping options for the Seaport/Pier/Aerodrome.

About this last point. In my last game I could barely cover the Seaport and 2 Aerodromes (both with full upgrades) with my lieutenants. The seaport would require 3 lieutenants, each aerodrome would require 3-4 lieutenants. If the lieutenants are not there, the extra shipping slots are not used and there is a strong drop in revenue (therefore the risk to zero out on cash, given the expensive maintenance costs of level 3 structures).
I did notice that Pier level 3 allows for shipping to all options without the needs of lieutenants, so that quickly became my fav smuggling point.

Better Logistics
There is probably too much to say here.
Logistics are the biggest turn off of this game. It requires, especially in the first few hours of game-play, to micromanage the lieutenants so that they can carry items around. When you need to make drugs in the Lab, the struggle is even higher because the Lab requires lots of resources, but it has a small storage, therefore if you are sending there materials (as you will, because the highest productive locations are far from each other), you will have severe logistic issues. I am not understanding yet how many trucks does the Transport company send to each location, I believe only one/two per material (even if more are available), therefore I try to add more warehouses at destination so I can have more trucks unload in a nearby area. Still very painful, as all my production is so high that even with multiple warehouses and multiple transport companies I see all the red warnings that attract the police.

If for example I wanted to make drugs in labs, my current workaround is to have 3-5 warehouses at destination (next to the labs and plantations), and use 2-3 transport companies and 2 warehouses (at origin where I produce chemicals) to send trucks to those warehouses. This would be supporting just 2 labs manufacturing drugs. Chemical production is influenced by the location like plantations.

Overall it's a nice game, I like the story, but I have played this game more on pause than on play.
The bottlenecks/logistic issues are severe, and I really hope devs will improve it, including granting larger capacity of storage, because right now it's ridiculously low.

It is a "bottleneck" game. I had issues using 1 Seaport and 1 Pier as there was too much money and 4 different residences (level 3) couldn't keep up, so both the Seaport and the Pier would keep being on pause automatically, waiting for the money to be removed, but the storage in the residence level 3 is also very low (90k if I am not mistaken?), with trucks that can carry max 5k, so then I had to wait for the residence to bring the money to the plantations to pay for maintenance, but in such cases each truck brings only 300-400~ to the plantation or 5K in laundry locations, for about 5-6 trucks available per residence...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like the game but it all feels so shallow/incomplete like it's mostly relying on the novelty of being a crime theme'd game.

There's no real interaction with the other gangs and they're all lifeless. They're effectively just tiny-quest hubs with an occasional timed event where they send out 1 unit to take one of your buildings every x minutes if you have low reputation with them. Your only repeating interactions with them are "talking" (which is solvable so you always gain positive rep) and "trading" (which is exchange x drug for y money) with a cooldown.

The other gangs don't build buildings or have any player-like interactions that you'd expect for them to have in this sort of game.

The research tree also seems uninspired with only a few buildings, tier x/y/z of the same building upgrades and some buffs to those buildings for the most part.

It also has a fair few bugs around the police stars system which is core to the gameplay loop. Unfortunately for me, it also has a game breaking bug that's ruined my save and hasn't been addressed even though I sent them to save over 2 weeks ago and they acknowledged they encountered the same bug in the save but were unable to find the root cause.

Nothing really changes between playthroughs so once you've figured out how to make money reliably the game is basically solved. You can start in different cities of the map (singular in the base game) which restricts starting resources and trade options, but that doesn't last very long so it's a pretty minor difference.

In its current state it's like a good foundation for a complex game, but since it's considered complete there's no way I could recommend it to any serious gamers out there.

There are better games you could spend your time on.

PS. This would actually make a pretty sick multiplayer game, that'd make up for the lackluster singleplayer features.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI initially found the game intriguing after watching the trailer and a few let's play videos. The concept of managing my own drug cartel with micromanagement and police interference seemed appealing. However, as I delved deeper into the game, my enthusiasm turned into disappointment. Contrary to my expectations, the game felt more like a survival challenge than a tycoon or cartel management simulation. The game play revolves around building something up only to see it inevitably torn down without much player agency. Even with careful planning and preventative measures, losses seemed unavoidable. In my first play through, I encountered a frustrating situation where a rival cartel threatened to take over my operation. Despite pausing the game to strategize, I found myself severely outnumbered, unable to defend my empire effectively. Even recruiting lieutenants for additional manpower proved insufficient, and my empire collapsed without much chance to retaliate. The most disheartening aspect was discovering that the rival cartels lack true AI. Instead, they follow a scripted pattern of sending waves of attacks, lacking any dynamic behaviour or strategic decision-making. This repetitive and tedious game play quickly became boring. A major factor contributing to my decision for a refund was the flawed Lieutenant (LT) system. Attempting to raise a lieutenant's salary backfired when I realised I couldn't lower it afterwards. This led to all lieutenants having the same high salary, draining my resources and causing my demise. Additionally, the limited functionality of lieutenants, mainly as soldiers, felt restrictive and unfulfilling. The assassination ability, meant I thought I could deal with rival gangs, turned out to be ineffective as it could only be used on my own lieutenants. and why would I do that when I've already sunk so much money into them unwillingly? This lack of strategic options and meaningful decisions made the game feel like an endless cycle of misery. Overall, the game's deceptive appearance and lack of depth in AI and game play mechanics left me thoroughly disappointed, prompting my request for a refund despite surpassing the two-hour playtime limit.\t\t\t\t\t
