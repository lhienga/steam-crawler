\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played Cartel Tycoon for a little bit now I can honestly say this game has real potential. I love the challenge it poses to try and build an empire while staving off all the aspects set to destroy it. For an EA, it's in great shape, very playable and has a lot of different ways to try and beat the system, but does really become labor some later in the game trying to micro manage all the aspects with limited personnel for the amount of coverage needed. 1. Let my cartel fight back against the DEA, Army, Police, etc...In the way cartels really do, let me bribe and buy every politician, judge, police chief..everyone in the area to protect me, run interference, block raids, all the good stuff that comes with buying crooked people, if they can't be bought, let me blow them up in car bombs like real cartels do.

Let me do what all cartel leaders do...let me bribe one or more of the incorruptible US politicians, CIA or the DEA, let me buy protection from them, allow me to corrupt the CIA into smuggling my drugs for me. I have millions upon millions of drug dollars, let me buy something more useful than more drugs.

I should be able to bribe people to cancel all DEA investigations and remove stars, i should be able to work quietly controlling my empire right under the DEA's nose because I bribed everyone i could and have the full protection of my local mayor and police chief.

If you get a 1 year reign as Drug overlord, that's lucky....why do you want me to have such a short run at the top, Pablo Escobar had 15 years at the top.

TL;DR: Great game; very challenging and difficult (kinda part of the fun) with active devs. Worth it if you are into Early Access purchases and know the risks involved.

I don't think my play time is sufficient enough to give a full, in-depth review but in the 8-ish hours I have played the game, I have thoroughly enjoyed it!

As it stands, this is a very difficult game, but the devs have a plan for that and every update brings balance fixes which do lead to longer play sessions. But, it is still a very challenging and punishing game - you will lose, in many different ways, but you will definitely lose (watching all your meticulously planned production lines slowly deteriorate into chaos and flames is actually a really fun part of this game).

There is one area that potentially needs more balance at the moment: Terror. It goes up FAST and once it is up, you will not make money fast enough to bribe a mayor to bring it down (the drop isn't even substantial enough to justify the bribe and the loss of Loyalty).

As for the usual goat/10 stuff people expect in reviews:

Graphics - Top Down / 10
Sound - Appropriate / 10
Music - Loopy but really fun to listen to / 10
Gameplay Difficulty - Get a pillow to scream into / 10
Story - Go read a book / 10

Ok just one comment on the story aspect: It's there, characters have backstories, there is a "Story" game mode but as this is a tycoon game, how you play generally informs how your own particular story unfolds (more applicable in sandbox mode since "Story" mode exists).

Overall, it's a lot of fun, challenging and has TONS of potential, with a decent amount of replayability (the map is the same, but you can choose where to start - some areas are harder, some easier etc).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarly Access Game Test Review
Version : 0.9.0.1327

Short Version of the review is this : Currently it is NOT playable and you should NOT buy this game at all till later.

Long Version : The game is a interesting Tycoon game that harps me back to the Sims franchise with a illegal aspect to it. In its current state there is no bugs or glitches to speak of which make is easy to move around on. The interface is a little odd as you will see the same building located in different area of your building menu. You will have fun starting out and enjoy the easy life of simple money. It goes down hill SUPER fast when you start expanding.

~ This is the MAJOR issues list with the game and why it is not playable. ~
1) Baby Sitting Trouble : You are not able to recruit enough lieutenants or people to baby sit your air strips, docks, or cities. Because of this you have a hard stop early on that wont let you grow any more because you are required in game to have a person at a shipping location or you will lost it. ~~ Simple fix is let us hire more people or make the penalty reduced efficiency
2) Forced Logistics Nightmare : The game is a logistics nightmare as each location can only ship to one or two locations but has needs for 3 to 4 things. Such as you need dirty money but you have no one to deliver the money due to SEE ISSUE NUMBER 1. In the game there is a possibility of self sufficient networks but your hard stopped in your growth due to shipping restrictions on each building.
3) The Worlds Against You : This game has no chill factor for anything you do in it. Every move and action you take is hard coded to be very bad and start a domino effect against you. I know the game is try to show case what it would be like in real life but its a game and makes it unplayable and not fun. You move a employee to a city, fear goes up. You research some thing, the DEA is on to you. You build a road, the USA Army is called in.

All in All, It could be better with some tweaks or possibly by firing a project lead or two. It has clear signs that the game leaders have not played the game and are just tossing out ideas. Nice try guys.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely LOVE the music in this game!!- have it running to listen to the music while I write this review.

I'm not really sure where to start other than it needs a lot of polish. I will just start by saying the price is a bit higher than it should be for what it is currently offering, but that's how EA works. You may want to wait on a sale to purchase, unless you are like me and buy in because you like the idea and want to help support a company develop a new game.

That said, I will just kind of touch on a few things I found annoying and limited my play time. I am not saying these things should change for sure, just saying these things made the game less enjoyable for me, and they may have reasons I didn't pick up on for being the way they are...

-Paying employee- I like the choice of dirty or clean money, and especially product in a pinch, but I feel like they want money far to often. Since you probably need them moving something around, or at a place that doesn't have dirty money sitting around, they likely need to use clean money to be able to get paid while doing tasks. I like the system overall but it was to demanding to pay 8 employees or so anything other than clean money, unless they were sitting in town basically.

-Building in towns- I feel like there probably should be more than 1 of each type per city, and more buildings per city. Some small balance there, to make it more enjoyable and feel more custom, and less limited. Just some tweaks to make it a bit more involved or something.

-Micro managing of deliveries, especially money- This happened to me early, that I was not able to have enough people carrying enough money from my seaport to the town. Especially then from that town to the next one over, both of which have airports. (that cannot move money) That seems like a good option in my opinion, that if you have a port in each region, you should be able to move money easily between them. Or just let people carry more? Not sure how to tweak this without running into the next issue.

-Money Management- I liked the problem of needing to launder the money. I just didn't feel like it was moving around as much as I would like, say, to fund the development of another region. It just takes far to long for employees to move money 8 or 12 thousand dollars at a time. Possibly the solution I present above for the previous problem solves this?

Overall it feels like this game is promising, but has a lot of overall balance work needed. Which from the frequent update pace it looks like they are working hard on. Hopefully they have a good community and keep getting the feedback they need to find that balance of new/familiar and fun/challenging/rewarding.

Overall happy with the purchase if nothing else for the music!! Hats off to the composers, the yes recommendation is for them.\t\t\t\t\t
