\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an absolute waste of potential, this game is. It instantly reminds me of and makes me feel the pain, which was Evil Genius 2. If you love to play a game with a half-hearted build/logistics model, dumb-as-rocks AI, and useless events/goals ... this is your game. This is Evil Genius 2: Narcos Edition (not to be confused with the original classic "Evil Genius")
It is extremely shallow and barely rises above a mobile game. The AI is non-existent and poses zero threat. You can literally not attack once, and the AI will zerg you, woefully outmatched if you lean heavily into an assault-focused lieutenants, until it/all gangs commit suicide (they lose 1 power per loss -- zero = death/gang removal).
Teching, focusing past the base opium drug to higher tier meth and coke ... pointless. Spamming opium plantations, without a further need of refinement/tech, will keep you overloaded on dirty cash, which bottlenecks at the very few buildings you can build to clean money (banks and casinos -- the only two worth building) you will have millions in dirty money, and slower than a snail, clean the money in mere thousands.
Please don't get me started on the "logistics." Non-existent.

The artwork, graphics, music are amazing. However, this game also has the audacity to give you a disclaimer referencing the 1980's trug trade, circa Pablo Escobar. What a bait n' switch. It's not deep enough for actual, intelligent gameplay and rated Peggy 13, at best.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredibly addicting, Its a nice mash up of Tropico, Factrio and Banished. Game play is smooth, and easy to follow along. Often times I'll sit down to play for an hour or so and next thing I know its 5 hours later and Im giving Pablo Escabar a run for his money.

Small issues
Once you get a little farther along the interval save feature is long and often which does impact play, often times i will turn it off. Im running a 4TB SSD with 128 GB Ram so these should be quick.

The police aggression is a little to aggressive. The police action at 1 and 2 stars i believe are flipped, Cordon should be the 1st star, 2nd star can be when the police seize a building.

The production ratio and everything I would prefer to be smooth out, its almost impossible to get your production balanced without backing up anywhere. I understand that is part of the game but once you get 1 full production proccess going, your going to be swimming in dirty money and stashing or laundering that can be where the police get you.

Other than that its an amazing indie game, that after 25 hours ive still only completed the 1st story once. Theres so much to do that i dont want to stop.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is an interesting concept, but some mechanics need a bit of work. You can set pretty simple production lines, but the residences all hoard money for some reason only sending out the excess to the buildings that launder it. There should be an option to decide how you want to distribute your money (like sending more to the buildings and leave more room in the residences). The mechanic of legal goods also might need some revision. As it is, the game assumes that you are selling a mix of legal and illegal goods (using the legal packaging to use the high capacity ports). But for some reason when selling only legal goods, they are still classified as "suspicious" anyway and it forces you to change the type or suffer more "terror." The combat mechanic is also a bit too simple, as is the diplomacy (in my opinion). Other than that, it's a relatively straightforward game, just make sure you use the in-game encyclopedia to learn how to set up your production chains and you should be good.\t\t\t\t\t
