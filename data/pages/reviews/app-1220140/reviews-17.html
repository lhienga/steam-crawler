\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, after 12 hours of playing, I got bored. I already have millions of dirty money and clean money. What to do next? My suggestions for improving the game and creating "infinity":

1. Character auto-generation.
At some point, I stumbled upon the problem "Ran out of lieutenant characters", the game as a whole becomes unplayable, because. you simply do not have enough strength to attack other cities. How can this even be? Why make each character with a unique face, name and skills? I think you need to seriously think about the system for auto-generating characters so that they cannot run out. Like in "Crusader Kings", where the face, name and skills are generated in any quantity and are always unique.

2. The strength of the gang.
Now the main force is the lieutenants. Their strength is pumped by "work" in the delivery service (in my case they carried money from one residence to another). I think their "strength" needs to be completely reconsidered. To become strong - you must seriously think about creating your own army. It might be worth adding something like "Sports fields", where you can recruit new fighters (not lieutenants), which would increase the power of a lieutenant.

3. Politics.
Sooner or later, when you no longer have money problems, you want stability and influence. Here you need to participate in the political life of the country. It would be nice to add political parties to the game. Some parties will clearly be against the cartel, others will be neutral, and still others will support it (for money, of course).

4. Journalism.
When you bought all the politics, then the journalists will bring you problems. They will write the truth, which greatly spoils the relationship with the political parties that are "in your pocket." Therefore, such journalists must be eliminated. The "silent kill" skill, which is currently not used in any way, would be a good help here.

4. Diplomacy.
Ability to form alliances with other cartels to eliminate competitors. Of course, diplomacy is not eternal and at the most inopportune moment your friends will leave you.

In general, the game is great! But don't stop there!\t\t\t\t\t

I saw complaints from other people about "desired terror levels being unrealistic" for lieutenants.
To address that the Los Zetas cartel in mexico famously had members disguise theirselves as soldiers and stopped multiple buses (I believe it was 107 people) and had the people kill eachother and the winners were forcibly recruited. They do this to scare both the government and rival cartels but jimmy fat-ass is typing about cartels in his RGB room in his upper middle class home in San Diego. - Also it's an easy mechanic to avoid by simply recruiting cartel members without a desired terror level but whatever.

Others i've seen are lack of difference of play I agree with this, there is no reason it should be difficult for the devs to add procedural generation to the map and more variety in gang leaders, i've only noticed like 7 different ones in my entire playtime in this game and their dialogue is static, in other words if you know the dialogue tree and how it will affect relationships you can easily cheese a good relationship with the rival cartels no matter the difficulty, this needs to be randomly generated for a fresh experience every playthrough.

Other than that the only reason I can think of the common gamer wouldn't want this solid game is because it's a tycoon, tycoons are confusing when you're first learning the logistics and the economy, I rarely play video games but this stole my life and I highly recommend it if you're into the genre.

Here's one thing that isn't advertised that I love: You don't have to manufacture your own drugs, you can simply run drugs across the border for the rival cartels through deals and pay them off after you profit, much like how the mexicans are to the columbians.

Zero crashes, I can't recall any minor bugs but i'm sure they're there\t\t\t\t\t
