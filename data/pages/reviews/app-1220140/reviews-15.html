\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros
-It has different substance than other games for resource management.
-Terror and Loyalty system is good and keep you occupied.
Cons
-There is no aim like keeping your settlers alive or happy to continue producing anything, just trying to not get bankrupt.
-Aimless missions to stole your time to just produce more and different than what you produce at the time.
-No reason to occupy other continents, you dont need more land just save some money and research upgraded versions of encampments so you can produce more expensive stuff to sell.
-After some time game clugs itself with haul of production materials and you need to close your encampments to prevent seize.
-Transportation is worse than other management games that i played and eventually it becomes your main problem.
-You dont have any purpose to continue after research everything and have best production in line.
-If you want to produce everything, you need to go on war with army of cops because of terror level goes high and you cant hold the lines because this isnt a battle strategy game so game blocks you from the main thing of 'Production'.
-You need to close encampments and do this too many times with micromanagement, after a while it gets overwhelming.
-When you reach stabilized production you will swim in money but what to do with it?

Conclusion
I got this game at Early Access and dissapointed but wanted to wait for full release because of the theme. After release now i can see it more clearly that was right thought to dislike this game. Theme could executed better but they choose to stay in pointless management game genre. There are better management games in the market dont waste your time with this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed the game very much, however there are some very annoying features that give my carpal tunnel a run for my dirty money, and I don't even say that about playing a game like CSGO.

There is too much micro-management especially when you reach the end-game where you expand more and more.

Your lieutenants start off basic and then they end up like children that has to be told what to do constantly when your empire reaches its peak. I'll give you an example, I have a ton of dirty money that needs to be laundered across several houses, hotels and whatever property. They have to be sent to the bank and the taxi company to fund it with cash. The problem is you can't keep them there for too long cause if there's too much dirty money it attracts the attention of the police. Imagine doing that for 10 different lieutenants and 20 different properties while your storages are full, police are raiding you constantly and you have to micro manage that stuff too.

It becomes less of a game later on where you can kick back and pretend to be a drug lord to a chore where even your capo is an errand boy that has to give your lieutenants a helping hand smuggling and doing whatever, because again, they have to be told what to do constantly like they're children who never learn how to do their jobs.

Another example is when you go to war, a lot of your production gets shut down. Think of having multiple cities you are running and having to re-open over 100 production sites ONE. BY. ONE. click by click because there's no way to queue this somehow.

Those are a few examples. You still have to feed the general of some barracks, some prison warden a constant supply of whatever they want in order for them to keep working for you as you are constantly micro managing the other stuff I've just mentioned. Go tell your Lieutenant to do that while you are bleeding out of money to play the game with, cause they weren't ordered to deliver the dirty money for laundering AGAIN.

It wasn't obvious to me for the first 2 hours of the game, but now that I'm past that I regret that I can't ask for a refund. I wish this game would work on these issues but it seems to be a common complaint that hasn't been addressed since release so I'm not feeling optimistic. Please correct me if I'm wrong and it has been worked on since release but it needs more improvements in my eyes if I were to change the feedback of my review.

Overall, I love the game's artwork, music and its feel. It is definitely the best cartel tycoon simulator I know as of this review since this is the most in depth one I know, however, I don't wanna play this anymore because I can't actually just enjoy the game, personally, if I'm constantly micromanaging everything and telling veterans in the business what to do because they still don't know how to tie their shoelace by now. I hope the game works more on automation, and as much as people would disagree that they dont want some passive game, I'm not asking for that here, I just want significantly LESS micro management.

Sadly, I still cannot recommend this game because I don't see anyone with a lack of free time being able to play this since it becomes a "babysit some drug lieutenants simulator" later on. I do recommend you give it a try when it's on sale if its your cup of tea but it isn't mine.

It is a shame because clearly so much effort went into this game, but it just needs that 25% more to make it the perfect cartel tycoon simulator. The issues are too glaring for me to even accept it as it is.\t\t\t\t\t
