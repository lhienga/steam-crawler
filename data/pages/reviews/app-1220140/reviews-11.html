\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI give it a 2 out of 10, and here's why. It really boils down to two reasons.

The biggest reason is that it requires unsustainable micromanagement, beyond what you are capable of doing in the game. Your men (the trucks going to and from farms, warehouses, transport companies, etc.) are always either doing too little of something, or too much of something. You are always producing too much product, or not enough. You always have too much dirty money, or too little. And somehow you never have enough warehouses. Even with an independent transport company dedicated to each one. Doesn't work, I tried. And all of these issues will end up piling up on top of each other, resulting in an almost unsolvable mess. I've tried many different strategies to remedy this, and nothing really ever seems to work. I can't even imagine the logistics of producing something more than weed, given the logistics hell of just getting illegal goods to ship properly inside legal goods. Which is too bad, because there's quite a few illegal products to produce. But in the 10 hours I've put in, I have yet to even touch those products because of how much of a pain handling beginner products is.

The second reason is that the power of your lieutenants is so low, they are basically worthless in a fight with another cartel. The enemy cartel will always roll in with 20 or so strength, and with 7+ lieutenants, I'm lucky if I can break 15 power. I guess you're just...meant to lose every time. Which really doesn't make sense. Oh and while you're busy losing, that raises terror, which sends the cops in. And guess what, they almost always overpower you too. And don't even get me started on the horrible balancing act of sacrificing all your respect to lower terror...

So ultimately, this game gets a 2 out of 10 for very poor scaling and just...terrible to handle micromanagement. Which is too bad, because I really wanted to like this game. And before you think, "oh well he's just bad at playing sim building games," NO that is not the case. That is my bread and butter. I've been playing macro and micromanage sims for over 15 years, and this one is the first that I have had to put down due to piss poor design. The devs could probably fix the scaling of enemies in a future update, but unfortunately I doubt the management could ever get fixed without a complete overhaul.\t\t\t\t\t

If you're someone who's a fan of the cartel genre, then you might find this game to be right up your alley. However, if you're someone who's just looking for a fun and engaging experience, then this game might not be the best fit for you.

One of the biggest issues with Cartel Tycoon is how quickly it gets boring. The game starts off with a lot of mechanics to learn, which can be overwhelming for new players. But once you get the hang of things, you'll quickly realize that the game doesn't offer much in terms of variety. You'll find yourself doing the same thing over and over again, with little to no change in gameplay.

Additionally, the game suffers from a lack of balance. Certain mechanics are overpowered, while others are virtually useless. This can make the game feel unbalanced and frustrating at times.

Overall, while Cartel Tycoon has potential, it falls short of delivering a fun and engaging experience. The overwhelming amount of mechanics, lack of variety, and unbalanced gameplay make it hard to recommend to anyone who's not a die-hard fan of the genre.
