\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm torn on Cartel Tycoon. I think there is a good game in here somewhere, but currently there are too many nuisances for it to be enjoyable.

The short overview of the game, you are the Capo of a Drug Cartel. You build production and delivery chains to smuggle drugs, which give you dirty money. You then ferry said dirty money to cities, where it is laundered into legal money. You research new industries and upgrades to make everything bigger or better, and have lieutnants and your own character who can drive around the map to either help your production/delivery, or fight enemy gangs and occasionally the cops.

Currently, there are these major issues that break the game for me. And this is after giving the game a lot of time to try to convince me otherwise:

- Lieutnants will give you random quests. Failing, or even declining to do them, results in a loyalty loss, that must be compensated with a payraise. These missions are random. Lieutnants can suggest something you were going to do anyway, or a mission that is effectively just free benefits... or they suggest completely inane, stupid stuff that makes absolutely no sense. Why would I build a new Aerodrome? No, better question, where would you want me to build it? We have one region and there is literally no place where it will fit.

The dumb part is that they are never related to anything you are doing, or your current situation. And they can literally come one after the other. "Give us all a medium payraise!" demands a random new lieutnant, even though everybody is at high loyalty. Which means either just accepting that you will suddenly spend a massive amount of extra cash on upkeep, or take the loyalty hit. It's stupid.

- The logistics and production amounts are incredibly annoying to balance. This goes for both the laundering of cash and the export of drugs. The game encourages a "More, bigger, faster" approach to empire expansion, especially with research. But you will rapidly run into constant issues of full storage. How many farms do I need to supply a drying rack, without having my transport company or warehouse overloaded? How many Opium farms can I afford in relation to my export? Normally this should be an engaging question. Instead it basically amounts to "Produce until full, turn production chain off, wait until everything is sold. repeat". If you try for diversity instead of specialization, you will REALLY resent this later in the game.

Same goes for laundering. There is just no engaging way to try for a blanced input to output ratio, other than just turning everything off and on again occasionally.

- The game does not encourage production diversity enough. Opium and dried cannabis are all you will need, ever. If you research upgrades to your smuggling points, you can export so much of the stuff that you never even need to take a look at anything else. The basic River pier / Aerodrome can eventually ship such an absurd amount of stuff, that sinking prices don't matter. And that's before I mention that certain lieutnants have an abilty that lets you sell the produce at markups. I was rich beyond anything I could ever spend without needing to touch cocaine or meth. Which is a shame.

- Lieutnant management is tedious. They want a promotion or raise regularly, and the only way to do this is to just open their individual profile and raise it manually. The limited cartell slots also mean that eventually certain Lieutnants escalate their pay to dizzying heights, at which point the best thing to do is have them shot or sent to prison. You will never really get attached to any of them, and it's a shame that the only interactions you ever have with them are almost all negative (I.e. having to raise their pay constantly, them giving dumb missions etc.). The Lieutnants that want a certain terror level above 0 are especially crap, since there is no reason to play that way. The fact that they lose loyalty if you don't play suboptimally makes them annoying, and not worth bothering with.

All in all, a lot of this is really fixable. And if you are happy with excessive micromanagement, the game can be fun. But for me, it heavily depends on what the future of the game looks like.\t\t\t\t\t

One of your Sicarios getting a little too ambitious? Maybe it's time for him to go. Made some bad decisions as the boss? Adios, vato! It some ways it's also quite innovative, with the best soundtrack this side of Tropico. A worthwhile purchase if you're a tycoon looking for a novel experience.

Recommended!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the aesthetic and really the gameplay is quite fresh. It's very different than most other tycoon games, in some good and some bad ways. The good is that you really do feel like you have your finger on the pulse of a cartel.

The bad is that the cartel is about as fragile as a 55 year old cartel members heart and as manual as rolling a new joint for each individual toot. It's fairly miserable.

They seem to have embraced a roguelight approach where everything is so complicated and manual that your capo is going to fail by design so that other cartel members can be promoted. That seems pretty fun on paper, but I'd argue that the game is way to slow to execute this roguelight approach (it's similar to the nemesis system). I honestly don't have endless patience for it and because of all the micro, you really need all your idiots doing specific things because the micro is so heavy.

I love tycoon games and may bang my head against it again, like in 10 minutes after this writeup. But I don't wish this on anyone.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, I will talk about some negative points which should be fixable by time:

When I have figured out making money, I got bored as the game becomes too repetitive. There should be variety of options to spend money to feel more like a boss while AI is getting smarter. But there isn't, after learning the game and getting your first win, you can feel the boredom in your veins.

There are several bugs, it really needs some more time to develop. For example, you can not stop your lieutenant if you clicked once to move. Warehouse&transport companies are sometimes not working properly. Their logic should be restructured. Police is sometimes not acting. Enemy gang is going back instead of attacking somewhere else if you burn the building.

I think the game also has some performance issues as even in the lowest settings, my computer is crying for some ice. Also launcher itself is problematic, it stays open and you can not close it after the game, you need to wait.

Tutorial is not good.

But overall; it's a great game in the tycoon genre with a huge potential. If you like cartel things (watched Narcos series etc.) and you like this kind of tycoon/simulators, go for it but be prepared, it's not easy to learn.
And the above issues (and more) are going to get fixed by time as it seems that developers are very active.
