Visually pleasing, absolutely bug free, tycoon game with a nice amount of sand added in how you do things. The main detractors for me come from the UI. You control up to 10 individual characters, and they often need to work together at a location. But you have to click on each one, then click move, then tell them where to go. The game is also about managing your dirty assets so no amount builds up, but the balance is difficult when the game only gives you manual one time operations or "move everything forever". That doesn't help me when I'm trying to keep my cash reserve high enough to pay my maintenance, but low enough to avoid triggering a raid.

The clunk is in enough places that it got on my nerves. Not enough to recommend against the game, but I see what people mean about the micromanaging being a pain.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot this game while in early beta and absolutely love it. The devs have kept updating, balancing, and fixing bug issues throughout the duration of the beta, and have kept doing so through the full release of the game. I have seen some negative reviews from some of the newer players who have decided to give this game a try and maybe this post will help get them/you a foot in the door and off to the right start.

TIPS: (Recommend playing the campaign before trying any other modes)

1. The pause button (spacebar) and fast forward buttons (1,2,3 numerical keys) are gonna be your best friend. pausing the game as soon as you begin either a new story, sandbox, or survival mode is a must to get your bearings and to plan out where you will be building your facilities. Since you can still interact with the map and most features while the game itself is paused, this allows you to get most of your building placed before any events may pop up.

2. When you first enter the game and then pause the game, THE FIRST THING YOU SHOULD DO is select your neighbor’s territory specifically the RED territory is what you want to focus on as that will be where enemy gangs will launch attacks from. In those red territory(s) you need to click on the city for each of the red regions and “talk” with the leaders in those cities (the GOAL is to INCREASE LOYALTY) as the higher the loyalty; decreases the chance to be attacked by those rival organizations. Doing this first will completely remove the possibility of being attacked early/ or potentially at all in the game. There is a cooldown to when you can talk with them again.

*Note: You will begin dialogue with each of the leaders and be given options to choose from in response to their questions and statements and your choices WILL ALTER your loyalty with them EITHER POSITIVELY OR NEGATIVELY. (Low loyalty = gang attacks, High loyalty = no gang attacks). It is up to you the player to find what dialogue combinations work and what doesn’t work as there are to many of them for me to explain.

*THIS IS IN REGARDS TO AMADO STARTING REGION*

3. After finish talking with the mentioned leaders, what you want to do is while the game is still paused click on the hammer icon “Buildings” and select farms and build 3-4 in the highlighted green area for increased productivity Make sure that you leave enough space to build a warehouse that can cover your farms or else you will need to manually transfer goods from farms to your storage/warehouse.

4. While the game is still paused select the research button (The light bulb) in the very bottom right hand corner select the river pier 1 and research all 3 (minus the tank icon) options each one cost only $800 but will drastically increase your cash flow in the short run as you will be able to hold more resources at piers, sell more resources at piers, and have the ability to send lieutenants to accompany shipments increasing volume.

5. After selecting the research mentioned above, click on the river pier, and upgrade it in order to start using it as a distribution point. Once its operational you can either use a transport company or lieutenant to move goods to the river pier (This will begin generating you dirty cash). Also make sure your residence once you select it has a route to the pier (Click the plus button at the bottom of the residence and select the pier, as this will have your residence consistently gathering the cash from the pier.)

NOTE: when selecting the pier there will be a cargo tab, everything is initially selected to be sold. You may want to deselect (grey out) everything except for the goods you want to sell or else the AI will automatically begin selling goods you may want to be saving for packaging at your workshop.

6. Once all the above is done you should select your city (Amado) and click the plus buttons to build a taxi company as that will begin generating passive clean money. You can use either dirty money(tan) or clean money (blue) to build. I recommend dirty money as you have stable source coming in from the pier.
__________________________________________________________________________

Additionally stay away from lieutenants with high terror levels at the beginning of the game or you will run into issues with them going out of control and becoming volatile as some may have already experienced.

*These high terror lieutenants can be managed in 2 ways effectively from personal experience *

1. Only higher them Under 2 circumstances in EARLY GAME:
A. Early game and you are under attack from rival gangs and need the extra combat
power to defend your buildings(If you followed the above suggestions in dealing
with red territories this will be a mute point.)
B. You plan on attacking an enemy territory such as the yellow territories which are
not ruled by enemy gangs and then plan on assassinating that lieutenant after
accomplishing your goal, thus removing the possibility of them going out of
control (you can also increase their pay to increase loyalty but with lieutenants
like *TONY* unless the world is burning he will never be happy).

2. Save them for late game:
*You can manage high terror lieutenants in the late game where you have large sums of dirty and clean cash by consistently running festivals at certain laundering facilities which increases your regions overall loyalty level. *IF YOU MAX REGION LOYALTY THROUGH FESTIVALS AND THE LIKE YOU WILL GAIN A PASSIVE BUFF* This buff will passively increase all of your lieutenants loyalty over time.

*BY MAXING "REGION LOYALTY" and keeping it maxed out*

It becomes impossible for lieutenants to become volatile regardless of their desired terror levels. But again this is only possible if you HAVE A CONSISTENT FLOW OF CASH to keep festivals going.

THE ABOVE SHOULD HELP YOU WITH THE BEGINNINGS OF THE GAME BUT DOES NOT INCLUDE THE LITTLE TRICKS YOU WILL DEVELOP AS YOU PLAY. HOPE THIS IS HELPFUL.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this game, but a lot of features are half thought out and some are completely useless. Hiring anybody with a high Terror Level desire is useless. Because within five minutes they go Volatile and sabotage your operation. And Terror is something you avoid at all costs because it means having cops gun after you at every step. There's zero benefit to keeping it high, it only causes problems.

Keeping their loyalty up also sabotages you because it quickly results in paying more money than you have. So you either keep them stuck onto a Residence to siphon up dirty money or bleed all your clean money by having them actually do something productive.

And to make it worse you can't fire them or kill them. At best you can have them sent to prison, but this sparks off into a bad cycle where you keep a guy around you just long enough for them to be a burden. Then you toss them in jail and hop they get killed. Except if every available lieutenant dies then you get a game over.

There's a genuinely fun foundation that's gimped by how downright contradictory some of the systems are. Hopefully they can rework some of these ideas, because there *is* potential for something special. But as it is now it just feels half baked and downright hostile in a way that isn't very fun.\t\t\t\t\t
