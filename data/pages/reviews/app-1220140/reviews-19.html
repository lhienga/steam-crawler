\r\n\t\t\t\t\tman, I really want to like this game, but the mechanics of the game just don't really work. It seems the AI plays a completely different game than the player. You are constantly having to manually manage and re manage money/supplies b/c the buildings that are supposed to do it just will completely ignore buildings that need materials or money. Really need the ability to auto manage that or update the buildings to effectively move goods/money around.

example:

AI captures things at a faster rate than player. 13 v 10 when you are defending takes 3x as long to repel the force (increasing terror a ridiculous amount) versus the same weight when you are attacking. if a fight breaks out and you are under by 1, it will take re-enforcements more time to arrive at the fight (even on straight shot in same territory) than it will for the enemy to defeat you. I've had maybe 4-5 runs end simply b/c of dumb mechanics like this. A way to intercept attacks or raids would probably fix some of this. The AI basically gets zero draw backs from attacking you, and even if you do defend they will attack your only port and basically end your campaign b/c your will get too far behind (the port shuts down while waiting for the battle to end, so you stop making money). You have no idea what force will come to defend if you attack (legit had forced in same provide on multiple games have force strength defend b/t 8-28). And since you can't re-enforce fast enough, it is pretty much suicide to attack an enemy.

Minor note, the campaign needs to have a better quest update system (game in general), I can't tell you how many times I've had to build buildings just to destroy them only to move the quest/story along (b/c I had already build the suggested buildings the stage earlier, again, needless hand holding that really ruin the fun this game could have).

The game is so stacked against you, that you pretty much just have to play by numbers exactly as prescribed by the game (even though the game isn't clear at any point what that is), Any sort of deviation and you might as well just restart your game. These things just suck all the potential and fun right out of the game.

Just a lot of great ideas being executed poorly. An Idea would be to allow slots in all your buildings to put toons in that will aid in the regular activities (much like some of the buildings do). Also limiting delivery cash/supplies per building. It is probably not worth having the buildings do any automated movement unless that is implemented. otherwise you are better off just doing it manually (for everything).

Needs to fix the campaign in general. This game really doesn't want you to play it, it really just wants you to follow along and play exactly the way it wants you to play with no deviations. Generally make the game play less restrictive. Especially if you are going to have a "sandbox" mode.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fun and worth your money.

The game is a little light on the management side of things, and there's not a lot to do once you've conquered the whole map except to deal with annoying events. The money laundering aspect needs a serious buff, I think. You can basically max out the entire map's laundering capacity with a couple of reasonably small production chains; if you have visions of filling the map to capacity with meth labs, that won't be necessary. I had hoped that I could launder dirty money by producing food and other legal exports on a large scale, but then I discovered that exporting spice via the land border can lead to an event that makes exporting spice generate terror for some reason, and I just gave up.

This limitation to laundering money creates a number of significant problems. First, it means that in mid-game, I no longer care about the police/roadblock/dea. Because I have more dirty money than I know what to do with (even with the best laundering buildings in the game) it doesn't really matter if a production chain goes down. Second, the game just turns into an annoying grind while I wait for my minions' power to get to the point where I can go on the offensive. I don't have jobs for them since they can't export, and so I'm just left to micro their loyalty and deal with them creating terror. Third, production chains get backed up and you get a notification for EVERY building that is overflowing with any item (which in turn means that I just ignore any notifications because there are too many).

As for exporting itself, I don't see any value in the seaport or land trade since I have to keep track of what items are generating terror. This is just pointless micro when I'm trying to min/max a production chain. Having to keep lieutenants there is also more micro that I don't think is really worth it, since I'll probably end up generating terror. More control over what is exported and when would be helpful. For instance, if I have 100 of an item in the port and that item is now generating terror, I can't stop the export except to find an idle lieutenant who can deliver and give him an order to remove the item. This is just annoying.

Optimization doesn't seem great. Again, with a few small chains the graphics were getting pretty choppy.

Micro with lieutenants is pretty tedious and uninteresting, as is monitoring and making deals with cities. There needs to be a better warning system for when my loyalty is too low. I can't tell you how many times I lost early on because I didn't know my loyalty was critically close to zero. A better system for determining what is generating terror would also be helpful.

All in all, a good game worth the price I paid of $30. Probably not enough logistics management, and a bit too much micro, but overall pretty good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game, love where it is going - I have had a pretty solid interest I suppose in Organised Crime stuff, watch all the shows on TV, read about real criminal organisation throughout history and just find the topic fascinating especially regarding South American/Mexican cartels and their structure I have always thought was really fascinating and I have done quite a bit of reading into the subject and I can say that the people that made this game really have done a bit of research, even the materials required to make end product narcotics and stuff is quite realistic.

The game feels like Transport Tycoon, Settlers and Tropico got together and made a love child together that grew up to be a criminal.

What I think is missing from the game is several other rackets and hustles you could setup - prostitution, extortion rings maybe even have a "Crackhouse" type structure you could build in cities that when you supply drugs to they slowly offload it for dirty money on the spot but also slowly increase terror as you flood the streets and locals with your product based on how much dirty money it produces (makes sense if you are filling your crackhouse with meth and heroin and the dirty money is going up fast but due to the severity of the drug and what it sells for directly affects the terror increase), same with extortion rings - you can set them up to milk money from locals at the cost of slow increase to terror due to your cartel intimidating the locals maybe have 3 levels of extortion, light-medium-heavy each having a direct effect on terror gain.

Should also be able to build like "Soldado Outposts" (name could differ) but basically a structure that automatically protected owned structures in an area a bit like the Guerilla Aerodrome automatically send guerillas to confrontations with other gangs/cartels/authorities. When your business is about to be attacked, "Soldados" automatically head to that location and await the arrival of the enemy, there could be cooldowns and such like if the Soldados lose you had to wait a good while before more Soldados became available again or it outright turned the Soldado Outpost into a Gang Owned Outpost which would slowly over time start taking over your other buildings if you didn't do anything about it. This would come in handy when your organisation starts to get really big and spread out. Maybe they could bring arms dealing in somehow and you had to get weapons and deliver them to the outposts to get more Soldados if they get killed?

The building limit thing they have in settings should just be made permanent and a maximum of certain structures can only be built in each different area i.e "only 2 aerodromes to each area maximum" kind of thing.

The game does not have enough point to need to really get to those big dollars yet so building an empire bringing in hundreds of thousands of dollars every few seconds has no real purpose - I have this setup currently in my save but what I have realised is having so much cash really has no purpose after awhile. Needs to be a reason why you want to have millions of dollars saved up and so far I can't find one. Like what if gangs from abroad came from time to time to make trouble for you and somehow you could start sending large amounts of money offshore to fund gangs abroad like the Cartel actually does fund/supply some Mexican gang bangers across the US. What if doing stuff like this you could actually start to affect the drug prices themselves but it was costly to do so? What if you could send Lieutenants on special "Foreign Missions"? Like obviously these wouldn't be actual areas you could build on but as a micro management text based side thing that actually affected your cartel members, your buildings and other elements such as the in-game economy that could also have some serious consequences i.e Lieutenants killed abroad, attempted assassinations against you or your lieutenants that arrive at a random dock or airport, damage to your prices or buildings - you get what I mean.

But with all this said, I truly believe this game is at a good price for what it is and reading the developers roadmap they have some cool stuff planned - I just hope they read reviews like mine and at least consider some of these ideas to make the game have more to it - more reason to have millions of dollars.\t\t\t\t\t
