\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteep learning curve but pretty fun after you learn.

Can be a bit punishing if you forget to do certain things (dealing with a debt in time always catches me out, or turning off a smuggling point when it's under investigation), and your suddenly are at the next tier of enforcement action which can quickly spiral out of control.

If that happens, the building can be seized and (at the 2nd enforcement level) the whole block of surrounding buildings are seized alongside it, which can really screw up your entire product supply chain. Warehouses need more efficient collection mechanisms from farms to prevent these, or a way to assign a higher priority to collect from overflowing buildings.

Needs more late-game content, too. I'd like to see more special buildings, more drugs (Heroin?), more gang and law enforcement events, more smuggling options, more slots for City Buildings, more City Buildings, more ways to pay for automation, so you don't have get caught out by small mistakes.

In its current state I'd give it a 7/10. Fun game but needs more content.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought the game in early access. I am assessing it as I played it. Currently the unique buildings around the map: Mayan/Aztec village, Guerilla fighters, Prison, Airport, Army Base and any others aren't implemented. Also the meat building and lumber mill aren't in. I also haven't played the survival mode or the hard? story mode, but I don't think these would solve my issues only exacerbate them.

In my opinion, this game suffers from too much micromanagement, not enough ways to move resources, capos are boring, not enough ways to dump dirty money (w/o a lot of micromanagement) and that with industry in just 2 or 3 regions you don't need to expand anymore.

Sorry this is gonna be rambly.

1) Not enough ways to move resources.
This is a game about moving drugs and dirty money around to get clean money. Yet because to pay for upkeep with dirty money you have to either use one of your limited and expensive residence buildings, or micromanage a capo to keep money stocked up, you end up building small production plants around the residences. These plants are then placed based on where the efficiency of the base resources is best. Here's the issue, often the resources you want to combine don't have 100% efficiency spots next to each other, and you CAN'T SHIP RESOURCES BETWEEN WAREHOUSES. This means that if you have a coffee plantation at 100% efficiency, it probably isn't next to, or even close to, your production plant for meth or cocaine so you have to choose to sacrifice the efficiency of one of the goods and massively expand its footprint, which likely means there will be buildings outside the residences' range, which means you can't build the interesting production chains the game wants you to believe you can make. This is especially bad when you consider the toll road export spots have an interval of like 4 hours, and the highest throughput of goods. So if you thought "Ah Ill build my facilities to fill up that toll road and make big bucks" you can't because you won't be able to cram enough facilities in range to both grow drugs and package them in clean products to ship out without having the entire thing be dragged down by warehouse trucks shipping product out of town when they should be moving internal to the production chain and vice versa.

Ideally I think you should be able to have warehouses ship to other warehouses, so I can build my coffee farm in one zone and ship it to the meth labs / workshops in another zone without the trucks abandon the production chain so by the time the ones shipping the product get back there is product ready to get shipped again.

2) Not enough ways to dump dirty money
Because residences are so constrained its difficult to make sure that each one maintains a stable supply of money without the production chain breaking down because the trucks are driving all the way across the map to a port or toll road to collect money, and then all the way to a city to dump it in buildings which leaves little time for maintaining the local production chain. And yet, its still very easy to earn 500k a month in dirty money with just one seaport. problem is, its virtually impossible to clean all that money without having capos constantly shipping it out to whatever city you put the hotel in or constantly doing the "Favor" micro for the lend money (ugh).

Also the clean money is just boring, raise the loyalty of the people, what does this do? nothing. just lets you reset your terror level. wooooo... oh wait you can use it instead of dirty money to pay people and buildings, oh it just teleports to them, in a game about logistics... woooo... Let me do something cool with the clean money, make it the end game condition or something in story mode, cause right now you conquer the whole map and nothing happens.

3) The ****ing Capos
I like the idea of the capos, its cool to give your organization some depth and internal politic-ing. Problem is they do nothing on their own unless they hit the "rebel" zone, do one tiny annoying thing and then go back to being satisfied or whatever. Why? This is probably the defining part of this game as a city builder / management game right now aside from the theme and yet its squandered with crappy micro heavy pawns with crappy skill sets that constantly demand more pay(without notifying the player when it enters the rebellious zone) despite just sitting on a port or city not even using their skills / perks. There's like 10 different versions of "X farm is a little bit better, but its going to mess up your whole production chain causing an imbalance that'll get the DEA / Police / whatever to come shut the building down" there's only like 2 guys in the whole game that have bonuses that are good for having them sit in a port / toll road / city, and yet that's what you end up doing with *literally all of your capos* as you expand across the map. The "arrange a deal" skill is super micro-y and has annoying animations that are slow to load and can't be skipped like with the mayors. The kidnapping and robbery missions are only useful early game to get cash but they'll push your terror too high unless you micro it like crazy so its not worth it. The assassinate skill is cool for internal politics, its a shame there aren't any other than "i want a different capo to be the head guy now" and after mentioning all of those that leaves the capos with what? being mules for dirty money? how does that make sense in the setting? It also seems like theres a fixed set of capos that appear in order? maybe this changes based on the starting guy / starting location? but i couldn't be bothered to find out. Conquering the map was dull enough as is.

Its not a major bullet point but also:
The villages feel super micro-y and not worth it so i never touched them, maybe add them to the story / tutorial to show their value? The Depot needs an explanation as well. The aerodrome upgrades don't say if they increase shipment size so I never bought them because just having more storage space isn't useful for them. I wish there was more to do with the mayors. It takes way to many clicks to interact with the mayors (part of the micro issue)

Positive things to end on:
Theme is great, its a shame the mechanics let it down.
Story mode was cool, shame it just ends once you hit the mid game.
Idk what the unique buildings are gonna do, but hopefully then build on the above and aren't just more micro like the capos are right now.
The game is fairly pretty with a good art direction.

Hopefully further content expands on the good bits and changes or improves my gripe areas, cause right now i can't recommend this.

Edit: I forgot to mention that the music is okay but its sooooooo repetitive that it grates my ears after like 15 minutes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary - July 2022:
This is probably one of my favourite games at the moment. It has so many strengths which lend to replay-ability. The fact that the dev team have worked so hard on it shows that there is real passion around making a good game. This excites me a lot and I'm really excited to see what DLC and other stuff come out in the future. I definitely recommend this game to anyone who loves the RTS genre. It has a unique take on RTS that allows you to play it without fear of a significant overlap with other games you might enjoy.

EDIT 2: It's the end of July 2022. a LOT has changed since my last review. I feel like i'm parroting myself. The improvements and changes are moving at an amazing pace. The dev team is so interactive and working so closely and openly with the community, taking feedback, fixing issues, etc. I am SO excited for v1 release. I have raised a bunch of bugs and feedback around improvements. So far it looks like everything I would like to see to fix/improve the game is being released in v1.0

EDIT: it's the end of November, 2021. A LOT has changed since my review & I can say with absolute certainty: This game has improved amazingly well. The dev engagement via discord for bugs and feedback have helped shape this in to an exceptionally fun game. I just keep wanting to play it, but at the same time I want to give it another miss for a few months to see what else they do to make it even better. A lot of the issues & balancing that I was having probs with have gone away.

July 2021 - I really like the concept of this game. There are *SO* many issues & balancing that need addressing, BUT it is alpha and that's as it should be. If they can fix the majority of the balancing issues & bugs by the time this goes Beta, it'll be a really, really good game.

For now, I don't think I can play it much more. The complexity curve goes up very quickly the bigger you get, to a point where I legitimately can't play anymore. I had to reduce a lot of the settings to make it easier and even then by the time I'd captured my third city, it'd gotten to a point where the balance was too skewed to one side and I couldn't continue.

I did enjoy the game and I am really looking forward to coming back to it in 6 months to see the progress.\t\t\t\t\t
