\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAny long strategy game should have a save feature - it sucks making it 8 hours through the campaign to only get shafted by a series of random events and have no save to go back to. Strategy games that have mandatory iron-man mode always on use it as a crutch for not having enough content. Give me the option to turn it on and off.

Even worse than not having saves is that you will run into scripted events in which you are often not given enough information to make informed decisions (you will not know the actual requirements for succeeding until after you select a choice), its game over. For example, you are told at a point in the campaign that you either need to pay someone off or kill them. If you choose to pay them off, you are given an absurdly high dollar amount with a time limit. Ops, sorry for not letting you know how much and that it's timed before you clicked it! In my next runs, now I know I need to save money in preparation of this event. That is crappy game design.

The difficulty level is unreal, and it is also almost impossible to come back once you fall behind even a bit. I'm a legendary/ironman xcom2 player. I enjoy a good challenge. This game is not a challenge. To do well in the normal campaign, you will need foresight into what works and what doesn't, with no room for mistakes... which means you will likely have to play the first 50% of the campaign multiple times to really get the jest of it.

I might still try and power through the campaign, not because I like it at this point, but because I put in too many hours to not finish it.

From a game mechanics perspective, many systems are pretty wonky. There is no way to split orders, or have certain amounts of money go to certain places. You can't control stockpiles. Resource management is essentially non-existent.

Finally, the two most annoying part of the game: 1) The constant popups of your underlings asking for raises and promotions. Its just not fun to navigate. I have too many other things going on to deal with their whining. 2) The ridiculously over-tuned police constantly locking down your stuff.

I WANT to like this game. The setting is great, and I love that we finally got a mafia/cartel game that focuses on the business rather than guns blazing. However, the developers have a lot of work to do it get it up to scratch.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is a lot of fun even in it's current state. I see people have been complaining about the heat system but it really isn't that bad. You can lower the heat and freeze the stars by talking to city mayors. In my complete play through I only had 2 stars and kept them both permanently frozen. So it is VERY manageable once you pick up how to play. Even money is not an issue late game. It did take me a few restarts to fully optimize my play through once I figured out how to play.

My only complaint involves the thug system. You have a limit on the thugs you can hire. Once you hit late game you have more buildings than thugs to maintained occupied areas. Also some of the thugs skills seem pointless especially late game. For example, I don't need a thug at a plantation to increase opium production when I need him to hold a city. So eventually almost all thug skills become pointless.

It is early access so not all options are available yet but very much playable. I am also excited to see where they take this. Overall a great game for the price.\t\t\t\t\t
