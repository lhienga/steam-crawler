\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the few games I have played in a long time that is not a hot messy glitch fest at the Early Access state.

Good:
- Simple yet fun. Breaking this game down it's a really simple game, but that's not a bad thing. I love these kind of game, start with nothing and can take everything by the end.
- Easy to learn. Nothing really complicated in this game, you will learn all there is to learn if you just play the story.
- Story. I like it so far, can't wait for the full version.
- Style. Really nice and smooth design, everything fits so well together and a lot of thoughts have gone into the mix of 2D and 3D. The animations is pretty amazing too~
- No glitches. Just wow, I can't remember the last time I played a early access that didn't have a single glitch... can't really remember any game I have played in the last few years that didn't have any glitches. No idea how they did it, but I had almost forgot how a game is suppose to be, with all AAA games you would think glitches is a part of the games these days.
THANK YOU MOON MOOSE! <3

Bad:
Sound and music. This was just kind of boring, bland and nothing special, I think you could add some more original music and sound effects and it would really be a banger!


All and all:
This game is worth all the moneys and I would probably pay more for the full version once it comes out. If you love management games, this is for you. If you hate management games, try it either way, it might surprise you!\t\t\t\t\t

Over all this is just an extremely long rant but all i want to say is try it. I think the game is amazing but it has some balancing issues and it needs a bit more content but I am very excited to see what the future brings for this game.\t\t\t\t\t

Hired guns should be easy, I should be able to hire a few lieutenants and their crews without effecting my cartel's roster.

Muscle should be kept separate from administrative, or make it easier to organize within the cartel itself.

Keep up the good work, and once all the little things are worked out give us more involved smuggling, like different routes and territories. More in depth gang wars would be fun too, it's very easy to avoid attention if you're careful, but forcing us into bloody conflicts with other gangs would be very fun. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR: Really solid Early Access gameloop, very promising future, supply chain builder with really well delivered Cartel theme.

Having got hooked to the demo these guys released a few months back I had been patiently watching the 18th of March approach with interest. Cartel Tycoon is a supply chain builder with a really well delivered Cartel theme. It's Early Access currently and it shows with various 'coming soon' features dotted around the game, but I'm still enjoying the current game loop enough to have got 14 hours of gameplay with no sign of stopping soon.

I think there are clearly a few balancing issues to be addressed that potentially sabotage the late game but I haven't hit that yet and am still enjoying growing my Cartel operation. I am very excited for the future of this game, even now with its' bones bare it's fun and addictive.

I love the thematic features such as dirt/laundered money. That combined with the various other mechanics such as bribery really capture the feeling of being a Cartel boss. I thoroughly enjoyed realising I was making more money than I could launder, to the point that it was becoming a hazard and a burden (gave me Breaking Bad vibes). I was desperately trying to spend and distribute all my dirty stacks as quickly as possible. While simultaneously planning expansion into new territory to help me increase my laundering throughput.

I've seen people criticising the game for bottlenecks in export and laundering but to be honest I really think this too fits the theme, it feels like the game is not about building the perfect engine and letting it run. But instead about having to constantly problem solve and be creative to overcome all nature of wrenches thrown into the works, again really playing into the theme of Cartels who have to operate in a constant state of flux due to Law Enforcement and other outside influences. Bottom line is I really felt like a criminal businessman and I loved that.
