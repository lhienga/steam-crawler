\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA pretty fun game, especially after all the recent updates. If you like the drug war / cartel topic as well as supply chain management and strategic planning - you'd definitely at least spend quite a few fun hours. Also it has a bit of a board game vibe, in a positive sense, with all the character and resource allocations around the map. I always enjoy games where your hard work results into a self-sustainable clockwork of businesses, and you can just take a moment to watch how it all works.

While it might look simple on the surface, it's grown to be fairly complex, especially if you want to do things right and not destroy a whole play-through accidentally. It's actually quite a remarkable & motivating feature to know that even if your empire is doing well and growing, a chain of small mistakes can all result in a (realistic) disaster. For example, a seemingly successful empire of mine got into an eventual collapse that started from one of the lieutenants losing loyalty and starting to uncontrollably kidnap people, which made my terror level (how wanted you are) skyrocket, resulting in DEA paralysing a crucial part of the business. And all this happened while most of my thugs were far away fighting a rival gang. And from there it went down like domino

This is just one story of many that the game can generate, which makes it reasonably replayable. Especially now that they have several campaigns that can go into quite different plot branches. And then there is also a grind element that I always both love and hate - while spending a whole play-through to unlock just one new character or a (optional) building may seem excessive - it does give an extra motivation push that I often lack in other games. So let's summarise

What I enjoy:
+ complex production chains
+ non-trivial supply chain management
+ frequent strategic redistribution of limited resources
+ many characters with a story and unlockable abilities
+ Rich world with many unique factions (mayors, militarymen, rebels, natives, gangs...)
+ Enough diversity in each playthrough and sufficient replay-ability
+ Humor and good dialogues / storylines

What I enjoy less:
- too much micromanagement sometimes (which is avoidable though)
- somewhat plain combat system, which could definitely benefit from more non-trivial player involvement rather than just comparing strength numbers of two armies
- The map is great but you wouldn't want to restart in the same region too many times. Some sort of procedural maps (even if not as balanced and perfect) would be a great addition. Or at least randomizable soil fertility areas
- Diplomacy with rival gangs is also a bit shallow at this point. It's not so fun to just trade resources manually back and forth across the map to improve relations

All in all, it is totally worth it if you enjoy some of the described aspects. And given how passionately the developers have been listening to feedback and improving the game, I am sure more great improvements are coming!\t\t\t\t\t
