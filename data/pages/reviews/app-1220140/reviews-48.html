Absolutely amazing 1.0 launch, very well rounded, fun additional mechanics make the game great. 100% buy this, play this and enjoy this game!!!!!! Great addition to tycoon game libraries every where!! Thanks for hours of fun adventures smuggling loads of "vegetables"


Great concept game, great building blocks to be a really in depth game. Early access gives enough features and things to do for a couple of play through's. Story mode is fun and challenging but ends at about the third city, it will be fun to see how they incorporate mid and end game conflicts, gang wars and money laundering. Once you kind of figure out the formula for keeping your terror down and respect up it can get a little difficult to micromanage each city and its operations. Supply chains are either packed full or waiting on crops and more dirty money. Great graphics, fun game play and a decent learning curve. There is so much potential for this game to take it to the next level. More shipping options (shipping depots?), larger lieutenant pool/more recruit slots (i get having someone in each city, just need more slots/tiers or bosses that manage lower level lieutenants). or a "armed guard" style upgrade for farms, warehouses and workshops. The potential for much more gang war challenge is there, (Didn't really have to do anything except defend and burn down rivals buildings to finish story mode). The frame work is there to have an immersive, challenging, and fun tycoon game, can't wait to see the progression to the finished game. (game play hours are much more than recorded by steam, estimate 48-72 hours easy.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR : Great Graphic, Great Music, Terrible Balance.

the game is what you expect.. a great drug lord management sim, with great graphics, nice GUI and nice music.
but that is where my praise ends.
At the current state of the game, i couldn't recommend the game yet.

the game is ridiculously hard. definitely not for casuals.

Levelling up your liuetenant raises their salary to a significant amount.
plus, there are finite number of people you can hire
but you still need people to guard your aerodrome/seaport as well as cities you own.

there are 3 levels of drug (opium, cannabis and cocaine) you can sell,
but to sell the 2nd and 3rd level of drug you will need significant amount of money to research the buildings needed to grow and process those drugs.

In addition, the penalty of getting your heat is very high
DEA keep preventing you to export your goods
Police will seize your building if you store too much product
and to reduce the heat is very costly.
you need to a lot of money to reduce it.

So what will happen in most games is that you need to really build your empire slowly
otherwise you'll end up unable to pay your lieutenants
or getting raided/investigated by police
or both.

Oh in addition, you don't wan't to get you Capo @ your main character killed.
the game does continue as such that one of your successor became the next Capo
but the penalty is too great
your buildings get seized
you lost your friendship with cities you already owned
you might as well restart your game.

In short, you better watch how the developers play the game to get the optimal build order
or your drug empire will crumble.

Oh, and did I tell you that there is no manual save?
There is no manual save.
The game auto saves on exit.
if you f'd up, then you might as well restart your game.
It feels like They Are Billions all over again.

So, that is what i feel about the game at the moment.
note that the game is still in early access, and the developers may not yet get the balance right yet.\t\t\t\t\t
