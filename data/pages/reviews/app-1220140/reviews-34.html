\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCartell Tycoon is a light- to mid-weight economic strategy game in the style of Tropico.
Most tycoon games are pretty bad, because they just try to mimic the greatness of true milestones like Railroad Tycoon and just slap on a theme. There have been exceptions of course, and Cartel Tycoon is a decent take on the genre. The cartel theme is nicely implemented, with money-laundering, gang wars and of course, a production chain with plantations, airports, and luxury mansions. The presentation is nicely done and builds on stereotypes known from gangster tales like Scarface or Braking Bad.
The story is mostly told by text dialogues - I have not yet played enough to tell you if it's a compeling story or not (I mostly played alpha).
While the basic economy and building part of the game is pretty basic stuff, the devs added some nice flavour like the money laundering mechanics: Police will raid your buildings, if you just expand your business without taking care of getting all of that money "cleaned up". Also, you don't just play as a single character: Your capo and his thugs all have special abilities, but might get killed if they expose themselves too much. (Probably also by some story-driven events, which I have yet to see). Then, another character will take over. The player also has some control over who gets into a potential successor position.
Best part about this game is the great soundtrack, which gets you into a caribean (middle american?) summer mood.
Recommendation: Pick it up on discount, when available.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't write many reviews, but I feel Cartel Tycoon deserves to be recommended. It is a game that right now, is in early access. It has come a long way thanks to the developers. They have been listening to feedback apparently because they have incorporated great ideas to make the game not only more fun, but also more engaging. You actually start to develop relationships or loyalty to your lieutenants, which is a cool aspect. I have ran a story mission where I picked a lieutenant at the beginning of the game and tried to do my best to keep him happy and promote him until he was right under me. He became so expensive I ended up just having to have a new lieutenant kill him so that the cartel could survive. This game reminds me of the game Godfather 2. It is a management game, but not just managing resources and money. You're also managing a family, which is super cool. For that fact, this game, right now, is unlike anything else you can get right now. One of the best games I've played as an Early Access title. Definitely think about getting it if you're a strategy or management fan.\t\t\t\t\t
