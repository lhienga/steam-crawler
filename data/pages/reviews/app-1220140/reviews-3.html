\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game offers a fun experience and represents an innovation in the world of management games. However, the challenge it presents can be considered quite demanding, although it may be somewhat unbalanced.

At the beginning of the game, you are faced with the challenge of managing the needs of the characters with limited financial resources, which can be quite challenging. However, as you progress and obtain higher-level products, you may experience a reversal of the situation, with a surplus of money and the difficulty of deciding how to best use it. This aspect of the game can be compared to the situation of the famous Pablo Escobar, who eventually had so much money that he didn't know where to put it.

Despite these challenges, I believe they are an integral part of the gaming experience and contribute to making it interesting. Furthermore, the game has the potential to become even better with developer support through the addition of new content and improvements. Ultimately, it's a management game that deserves attention, especially considering its affordable price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall while I'd recommend the game, I'd caveat that I'm recommending it as a story driven campaign game. Not as a sandbox game. If you're annoyed by how other sandbox games in this genre don't let you run a narco empire or the like, this game is not going to scratch that itch.

The various missions they have with plot lines, scripted events, etc are fun. You get a decent amount of freedom in setting up your drug empire and the plot pacing keeps things fresh. The scripted events also compensate for the poor AI and some of the repetitive game play aspects.

However as a sandbox game, this game is bad. Poor AI, extremely static maps, limited play styles (you can basically either do optimal path supply chain every time or get into micromanaging terror level and other issues). No matter what part of the map (and there are only 2 maps) you start on, things are going to devolve into either you doing the standard approach or you fighting the core mechanics of the game. They have things like map sectors with guerrilla camps and covert airfields, map sectors with international airports where you can bribe customs officials, map sectors with harbors, etc. But nothing really changes, it's same supply chain, same shipping, and maybe slightly different flavor text in the dialog screens. You don't change your play style based on the kind of cartel you're running and your partners. Overall it's just not engaging.

Initially when I purchased the game I actually was pretty disappointed in it, I did the tutorial and then jumped into sandbox. Put the game down after that experience. I've since come back and played a couple of the campaigns and enjoyed all of them. At the sale prices it's a reasonable value.
