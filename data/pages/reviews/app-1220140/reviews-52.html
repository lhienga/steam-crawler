\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to say I absolutely love this game. I love economy management games, and this is very nice for that itch ... BUT there are some major bottlenecks and mechanics that are kinda annoying that pop up when you wanna grow.

1) Terror/Loyalty System - I like the idea of this, basically knocks you down if you grow too fast. However my big issue with terror there is no way to get rid of it on any real effect manner. You have to go to a mayor and spend loyalty which makes sense, but this leads to the problem of loyalty being very hard to get early game. This forces you to grow very very slow at the beginning which can get boring just waiting to launder money to then buy loyalty to then decrease terror. I think this issue can easily be resolved by increasing the number of building that each city has... which is another complaint.

2) Money Laundering bottleneck- I've seen other comments mentioning this and it can get to be a problem but you reach a point where you simply can't launder enough of your "dirty" money so you again just have to sit and wait for your buildings to produce clean money. This I believe can be fixed with increasing the number of buildings that you can have in a city. I will say once I got to around 3 cities each with banks it wasn't too much of an issue.

3) This is probably my biggest complaint, I really really REALLY want the developers to add the ability to move resources from one warehouse to another. This would really open up automation, cause another bottleneck is if a warehouse has too many buildings it has to attend to in its radius it can get bogged down. So I can design very neat supply chains that would be effective if this mechanic were added. A side of this would be I would really like if they added money houses, I was reaching a point where all of my residences were full, which also caused smuggling routes to overflow with money (1 border checkpoint, 1 seaport, 2 airfields, had a hotel and 4 Residences). This is partly the money laundering bottleneck but I would really love a dedicated building where you can just store cash, make it to where if the cops seize it, you lose it.

All in all, this game was a breath of fresh air, causing me to leap forward in time when I really got invested. I cannot wait to see what the developers add, but if you like any tycoon games, this is a worthy addition to your library.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the game... I do. AND it's early access....

BUT....
1) The production (being the majority of the game) cannot be automated in any real way. So it becomes tedious FAST, and that's from a guy that loves spreadsheet games. You have no control over a buildings holding. From how many of each resource they hold, to money... or to where resources are shipped to. Really none of it. So setting things up to run efficiently isn't really possible at this point....
2) The Terror system.... it's TERRIBLE. No matter what you do you have to ramp it up... quickly... to get anywhere. There also seems to be no way to lower it beyond letting your main Capo get killed. Although this too may change with one of the features that aren't yet finished.... but it ALSO makes the game pretty unplayable.

I like the game very much. But I would wait until it's a tad more fleshed out before buying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a fun game and has great potential. The pricing is good aswell, buy and enjoy!

Edit: You can only save once and it seems to overwrite the previous save, one misstake and you might be doomed... That aspect is a big thumbs down... Do i really have to start over now or what? :(

Edit2: I started over and manualy(copy - pasted) the saves to an empty folder.

So, i've completed the game before any patches so have that in mind. It flowed pretty well early, it was fun to build new stuff, research and buy different town buildings. The thing is that once i reached two stars the whole seize thing started and It changed the gameplay drasticly. Now you "have" to move your lieutenants to fight off the police and keep a check on the lojalty/terror meters - which costs clean money. The clean money takes a long time to make fluent. It was a pretty big grind on all these things and surely frustrating from time to time.

Once i finished the main story and built everything, there is (from what i've seen) nothing more to do. It's a little thin atm but i still think it has great potential - hell it's probably going to be great! A small indicator to that is the 'coming soon' buildings on the map etc: a prison, military base, guerilla camp and hospital. Hopefully we can keep our savegames in the future updates aswell.

TLDR: Buy if you wanna support the devs (as you should!) and personally i see this game (at this stage) as a big tutorial for the more juicy content updates. It's frustrating, grindy/micromanaging, has very nice music, good graphics, clever made, from time to time pretty damn enjoyable.\t\t\t\t\t
