\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIgnore the time played. I experienced a bug where the game continues to "run" after I save and exit. Had to have steam end the game.

The game doesn't tell you basic information. Why are deliveries down? Why is the airport not selling the vegetables? Time to investigate! Why is my transport company not moving coffee to the workshop? No idea! It is connected to both. Transport company is set to only move coffee. Why let me connect to them if it does not work? Why does the transport company have so few trucks? Even if you start the game with a million dollars you still only get the one truck per company. This is a game about logistics!

The quests your goons give you are annoying. They constantly want you to build stuff whether you need it or not--whether you can afford it or not. Not accepting the quest reduces their loyalty. Lose/Lose. Just leave me alone and let me build.

While taking over a vegetable farm got a quest to not take over buildings for 30 days. I said sure, that is fine. This is the last farm. Accepted quest and immediately failed because the attack was in progress. Why was that one counted? Why give me that quest while an attack is in progress?

The laundry buildings suck up your dirty money. I basically have to pay for my operations in legal money because otherwise my economy gets shutdown because of the laundry operation. Why doesn't it pay my workers first before cleaning the money? Also, you have to deliver dirty money to build which is an annoying step to take when constructing using dirty money.

The police will absolutely seize a farm shortly after you pay 4000 to free it up again. Due to how easy it is to have logistics issues the police end up visiting quite often due to your stuff piling up. The game starts with several buildings laying around to use. Saves money right? It is a trap. Your terror will build up and your stuff gets seized. Just build things and ignore the free buildings.

I want to like this game but it has too many little issues like that which makes it frustrating to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCartel Tycoon is a business management game about plants!
I'm kidding, it is more than this.

Cartel Tycoon is a standard survival strategy business management simulator. As with any business management, there are managers, resources, products, transport and money ... all to be managed. What is different in this game is that it is inspired by the 1980s/1990s drug trade in Latin America. Growing, producing and smuggling drugs then money laundering and dealing with other cartels is one thing ... however, since when a significant proportion of video games do not involve doing extremely illegal things?!

Unlike other similar games, try to step away from the game while it is running and you will come back to a deficit, full warehouses, buildings and seized by the police. Yes! The game requires a tremendous amount of manual control.

Is it fun? ... Actually yes.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm surprised at how much I'm enjoying this game. I avoided it in Early Access so I wouldn't be burned out when it finally reached 1.0 and hit the street. There's a lot of deep gameplay, great graphics and the game after twenty hours is remarkably glitch and bug free. I thought perhaps I'd find the gameplay would get tiresome after a while, but it hasn't.

I played the Tutorial campaign for seven hours, four hours more than needed but I was enjoying poking around and seeing how things worked. I then started a Sandbox campaign with all the given settings. I expanded for the first ten hours, then Terror got too high and my capo died in a US Army attack. And then during these next three hours my new capo, knowing better, has kept his head down and the gameplay has shifted to more of business sim, but still with a nice narco flavor.

Overall the whole experience has been very good. I've certainly gotten my money's worth.\t\t\t\t\t
