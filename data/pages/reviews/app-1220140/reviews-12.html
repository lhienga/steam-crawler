\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeen playing the game for about 15 hours now and got somewhat bored after that. I mostly played the game in sandbox mode with standard difficulty. First of i really enjoyed the concept of the game but i did find the implementation to be lacking. I played in the sandbox starting region marked as having unbeatable difficulty but i eventually ended up having millions of dirty and clean money without expanding to other parts of the map and eventually got bored and realized there is no real reason to expand or start new production chains to further increase my money even though i defeated 2 rival cartels by them essentially commiting suicide by constantly attacking my buildings till their total power went down to 0 and this caused their whole operation to collapse for some reason. Combat is as basic as it can be, basically to win you need to have a greater power rating when either you attack or they attack and this was easily remedied by just hiring lieutenants who has a higher power rating than 0, together with my leader i was essentially able to repel all attacks coming my way with no issues. The game also has mechanics for how much people like you that give you bonuses depending on the rating, let it go to low and your leader dies, it was very easy to just buy a casino and just spam the charity event so you the rating goes up and i used it to buy a favor from the major to lower my terror rating.. The terror meter rises when you commit violent acts or smuggle drugs without packaging or smuggle with a package type that is considered suspicious (i assume this is caused by using a certain kind of packaging for too long but that is very simple to change). And the most tedious part of the game is the constant micromanaging that is needed for keeping your production chains in the working order if a warehouse is too full you generate terror so you constantly need to change your production in order to adapt. This would also be a very trivial thing to do but not if you end up having several production chains to watch over. It become clear that the best thing to do was to just maintain one production chain and then optimizing it as efficiently as possible and then occasionally just change the packaging depending on what package type is deemed suspicious at that smuggling spot. Generally speaking the game does seem to hold a lot of potential but i really cannot recommend it as the game is just very shallow and simple when you understand the mechanics. Improving combat as in having to actually buy weapons for your cartel (or why not armored cars and similar crazy stuff) and also implementing a diplomacy system for the rival cartels would be a big improvement and in general it would be great if there would be more uses for the money you earn because after a certain point i did not find anything to spend my millions on. All in all could be recommended to buy on sale and could keep one busy for an evening or two but not really any more than that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun to set up little production lines for various drugs. Challenge in export without garnering too much attention. Needs some hot keys but otherwise easy to use and understand. Graphics are nice and pretty. Some balancing issues (briefly touched on below). Needs scenarios not on the main island. Diplomacy is key, and buying from villages/gangs. Recommended, but enjoy it as needing work.

I found though late game was a downhill spiral as everyone just wanted to go volatile regardless of how much money i pumped into them on a regular. Production chain and logistics was a little too simplified or unobtainable. You'll play with opioids for too long. Need some early game diversity. Research takes 5eva, go straight to canniboids and then coke. Ampths were so out of the research tree I didn't need to even run a criminal empire when i got them. Don't attack unless you're ready to pay for eased tensions.
Can the depreciation of goods be a little less steep; it'd be cool if a 'legit' business was actually viable too?

Some issues....
Impossible to stop warehouses from getting completely full on a regular. Became too tedious to worry about and I just had to constantly pay to bring the terror down instead of managing the empire. Interesting mechanic but late game its a notification nightmare. Oh, the entire production line is full again... maybe don't drop off that much then!!! Shutting down production buildings doesn't fix this issue either.

Transport buildings are very limited. Possibly intentionally, but this prevents spreading facilities around the map which might have provided a bit more industry. Otherwise production is a very localised matter.

Transport and Warehouse builds need to be able to be configured while building. They grab goods I didn't want them to have before I could do anything about it. Then I have to either tediously make a lieutenant move the goods to where they should be (and likely loose it in full buildings), or just leave it and have random stuff everywhere. Worse when they are higher tier.
Construction also needs to show a boundary line to help line up additional builds and provide space for roads. Liked the way roads are built; nice, simple, clean.

Seaports shutdown some 'container' resources commonly, so get the harder stuff out by plane, and bulk out by pier. No warning either (or hidden in a flood of other warnings!), you'll just look and it'll be confiscating vegetables. I just had coffee and vege containers and switched whenever it happened, but frustrating.

Money logistics is a little impossible too. Need some better ways to automate that around the map. Perhaps allow residence to chain to other residence to keep a balance between them? Can't get Lieutenants to do it cause the capacity of the residence is too small and the lieutenants will just leave the excess on the sidewalk. I had a port sitting on a constant $900k and I had to waste a connection on residence all around the empire to distribute it. Hotels in every city might help. Why cache it when it'll be full again and I can't launder the buried money ?

Lieutenants need to stop requesting stupid things at a penalty of loyalty too. There is only so much space on this island for another airport. Ended up ignoring it or burning down an existing airport just to put the new one in. Can't I just send them to the Salsa club to build up some loyalty ? Late game trick might be to just not answer the phone.. but thats a bit close to 'stop playing to keep playing'.

Combat is unbalanced. Either the gangs will wipe out something you need constantly, or their constant undefeatable raids will frustrate you to end the game early. Solution, just diplomacy the hell out of the gang leaders. Buy whatever they are selling... throw it in the bin if you need or direct it straight to a port. Talk the talk. Forget fighting, once you have enough power to face them you don't need to face them cause they are already diplomacy'd down or you've quit.

Combat would also be cool if you could build something like a compound, which prevented intruders from going past the gates without a fight/warrant. This would prevent the guess work and rapid slamming of spacebar when the sirens or gunshots go off - followed by a rapid re-deployment of any lieutenant close enough. Otherwise they just hit where-ever they want, while you have to pay a trespass penalty. Needs more active defensive combat, as well as aggressive steps to lower enemy gang strength. They seriously had 34 strength most of the time while I had 17.

Edit: why not make production take much longer? It'd be more valuable but a bigger gamble. Much more risk to protect ?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall: 8/10
The game has been dumped on quite a bit, but when something positive is said about it, it's always true. The game has multiple systems that all work well, and when you progress in it, it feels really rewarding. Progress is a big part of the game, it has a gigantic research tree, a big map to take over, and there's always multiple ways to progress.

Story: 6/10
The game has four campaigns to play, one of them being the tutorial, which is one of the only things I do not like about this game. The tutorial is buggy and limited, and just a big mess, but i'll get to that later. The early campaigns that you play feel like you have to rush through all of the quests, and just leave a mess of unorganized and ugly systems behind you when you finish the campaign. The later campaigns are especially long, and do not feel like the earlier ones. The good thing about the campaigns is the length, each one becomes longer and longer, and overall you're going to be spending forty, or even more, hours on the campaigns. Each one becomes harder too, the fourth and last campaign even being listed as impossible.

Gameplay: 8/10
Tycoon games often manage to become idle games about halfway in, Cartel Tycoon does not. It feel involved, since if you even leave the game on for half an hour, you'll come back to burnt down buildings, full warehouses, no money and a load save button that is looking as clickable as ever. One of the systems that really immerses you is the loyalty/terror system, it is a sort of reputation and moral part of the game. You get loyalty from citizens that live in regions that you are in control of, you can build casinos in their cities for them, you can donate money to them, and make their life better overall. This will be met with rewards. The terror system is more punishable, if your terror keeps going up, by gang wars, failing to smuggle illegal goods etc. You will get attention from the police. At the start, local police arrest your buildings that are full of illegal goods, and eventually it goes up to the police blowing up your territory. If your loyalty goes down to zero, you get killed, if your terror goes to the max, you get killed. And another member that you hired in your cartel will become the capo. I expected the terror system to also have benefits too. For example, you are feared, not loved, and you are notorious. And you will need a lot of power to keep up with the attacks, but only bad things come from terror, which wasn't too much of a deal breaker though. The game has a lot of good gameplay mechanics, but I can't list them all here.

Community: 7/10
The developers of this game are always responsive to suggestions and reviews that they find, the community page on steam is overflowing with developer responses. But they don't respond to a lot of the big problems. The community of this game wanted Cartel Tycoon to feel like you're really making progress, you're building an empire and you're getting rich. They wanted to spend a day in the office of their gigantic mansion, then go out to the pool and swim in a pool of cocaine. But the developers wanted it to be more of a survival game, running from the police, managing your money, seeing what the market is used etc. The voice acting in the game is very good, though it is not present in most of the conversations outside of the campaign.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like the game more than I do, there is potential here but the game is marred by balance problems and strange design choices that just leave the game feeling too frustrating and chaotic to be enjoyable for extended periods of time.

The game play feels far less like slowly building and expanding your empire, and more more like a messy juggling act where every step forward throws another ball in the mix and if you drop 1 ball everything comes crashing down instantly.

Between mini quests, gang attacks, federal raids, stock control etc there is far too much micro to get anything useful done. There's always some more pop ups or events coming to ruin your day, or you know just prod you a bit while you're busy trying to do other things.

If you try to do pretty much anything meaningful, the terror will rise to the point the feds will sporadically shut down your assets meaning you have to go through things 1by1 to shut them down so you don't create MORE terror from having full warehouses then switch them back on again when things calm down.

The more you build and expand the more this happens meaning it's far easier and more manageable to have 1 small but efficient and high tech production line. Having multiple lines and export points etc very quickly becomes unmanageable due to the sheer amount of manual inputs and micro managing required for each one.

So with this in mind you spend a couple of hours managing and researching this, set it up and... then what? You've got more money than you need, everything you need researched and things aren't rapidly collapsing on top of you due to your very small size.

So why scale up? It only brings more problems and issues that usually mean you make less money, lose gang members, raise terror and have police on your back etc. It's way more efficient, safer, easier and usually more profitable to stay small. Even if you did choose to grow, there's nothing to spend the extra money on.

There is 0 point to expansion and way to much micro management to make expansion fun on it's own. The game is essentially won or lost in the first few hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter a good 30 hours or so in this game, I have some issues

First of all, had a blast for about the first 15. Learning all of the production flows and logistics systems was fun.

Once that feeling wore off, I started to see all the systems and was left really disappointed.

First of all, gang attacks are dumb. I couldn't spend any time setting up better working production chains because I was attacked every 2 minutes. Granted I was playing at the fast speed, but it was extremely annoying to take everyone off their jobs to defend my Aerodome for the 100th time only to spend 3 minutes after the fight putting everyone back where I had them (god I would kill for a button to "Return All" or something.

Second, the other gangs aren't simulated at all. Once I realized this my immersion in the game dropped tremendously. You can capture their export routes, you can capture their farms and production, but they will still remain as strong as they were before. Not seeing any other trucks/vehicles in the map should have hinted at this but I guess I was too involved with my own stuff to care.

The diplomatic approach seems severely broken. I could sweet talk all the gang leaders and have them at zealous level but they still would not join. There should be options to force gangs into cooperating. I don't want to send all my people to kill every single opposing gang just so they stop attacking me.

Lastly, without customizing sandbox options, Lieutenants will continuously ask and ask for more money while their loyalty falls. This led to extremely highly paid people that offered nothing in return. You can't fire anyone, only murder them, which makes everyone else's loyalty drop leading to a never ending cycle of pay raises.

TLDR:

Have option to stop gang attacks or make them less frequent.
Simulate other gang economies (you're the only one building, exporting, producing).
Add diplomatic options to force gangs to join or cooperate. The dialogue system becomes tiring and seems too random.
Stop endless pay raising and loyalty drop among your people.
