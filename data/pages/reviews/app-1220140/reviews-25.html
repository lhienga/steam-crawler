\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game on and off a few times in the lead up to 1.0, and to be honest, it's always ended letting me down. The most fun I've had is just jamming along in Sandbox to the (genuinely EXCELLENT soundtrack,) building up my empire and expanding at my own pace. The problem is that the game forces you to play the story mode should you wish to unlock more than a tiny handful of options for your playthrough.

Here's some additional problems I have:

1. Failure is often creeping and unknowable. It's perfectly possible to lose a game because you expanded an hour and a half ago and didn't realise you'd set a series of dominoes in motion that would slowly drain your entire balance dry. This is neither fun nor engaging.

2. The way the story saves is utterly obnoxious. For an early example, I was replaying the initial storyline, where you have to pay off a character with legal money. I stockpile over the right amount of legal money, and then without realising, a day before the due date, I drop a few hundred dollars below the list, thus failing the quest. Can I go back? No, because the game has now quicksaved the split second the 'you failed' dialogue began, quicksaved after my Capo changed, and then quicksaved a third time when the next quest started, thus, locking me into this situation unless I reloaded a save from two hours ago. True genius.

3. The way logistics works can be infuriatingly terrible. I've had trucks dump 15K directly into a laundering facility that is turned off with no way for me to re-assign it without the trucks immediately funnelling the money straight back into that same laundering building.

I've given feedback previously on the Discord and through the official channel, and it's pretty obvious that some of my major irritations (especially the story mode/sandbox mode one) are intentional design choices that I can't avoid if I want to play this game. Which, unfortunately means I probably won't be playing this game that much going forward, despite being a fan of city builders and the aesthetics and sandbox gameplay of this one being damn good.
