\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst 10-15 hours are great, but once you start getting into the mid-late its just a micromanagement hell. The automation of cash distribution to each and every building of yours doesn't seem to have any logic, it just maxes out the capacities at random first before going to the farms where its needed in order for the supply chain not to fall apart.. so you have to manually manage each and every delivery, and the you more you have the more tedious it becomes.

Dealing with police and politics just becomes a matter of waiting until the timer runs out and clicking the bribe/payment button, then waiting for the cool-down and doing it over and over again. You figure out or or two productions which are the most profitable and stick with it forever, so not really needed to upgrade the techs to others late game.
Your gang system (henchmen, whatever they are called) become disloyal and leave if they don't get a raise, which sounds expected, but giving them the bare minimum increase every month or so works much better than 10x that amount right up front, where they would earn much more.. So just another tedious thing to constantly check on unnecessarily, when the last time when the last raise was before it becomes an issue.

Anyway, game felt great and cool concept in beginning but quickly falls apart once you get into the mid to late game with so much tedious micromanagement that it becomes a choir, stripping away all the initial enjoyment you felt. Its not a skill issue as you would have with something in other factory-style management games like Factorio, its just the way the game was designed and no way around it as it lacks any real depth.\t\t\t\t\t
