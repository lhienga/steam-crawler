\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good for what it is. It's not L4D3, its closer to a semi-generic horde shooter co-op campaign with some roguelite mechanics. You can do some interesting builds with the cards and survivors, and there's a decent variety if you like to experiment a bit. But the campaign itself is a bit dull. It feels like it would have been better if they went with the L4D1 route and had each act be an independent short story, or just sent it and have all the acts be able to be played in one long campaign with scaling corruption cards and player cards so it gets utterly ridiculous. As is, the vanilla and DLC felt pretty corporate/safe and doesn't stray too much. It felt like they didn't really want to take any risks.

Would have liked it to go a LOT heavier into the roguelike aspects, like more opportunities to get cards during the campaign, cards with a lot more game changing effects, more variety in the corruption cards, etc. Like if Risk of Rain 2 had a baby with Left 4 Dead 2. Would make the game have a lot more replay value.

The Hives and Deeper Hives were really interesting, but like the campaign, kinda wished there was some random-ness, as it doesn't do what L4D does with random routes. In L4D, there would be like, 3 paths you can take, 1 or 2 routes would sometimes be randomly blocked, which would change up the path you'd need to take each time. I don't believe B4B does that, which makes the maps feel very same-y on multiple playthroughs.

Pick it up on sale, I think its worth it if it sounds interesting to you. If they ever fully abandon the game, I hope they allow the game to be peer-to-peer if it isn't already, and the community get mods working for stuff like custom characters, cards, and maps. I think mods would greatly improve the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally decided to give this a 2nd chance in case I was ever wrong on my first review. I'm kinda asking for it, but prepare your Jester awards...

Pros:
>Gunplay, very snappy and I love the animations. Best part about this game fr.
>Card system, but it could use some work
>Cosmetics, but it’s sadly lackluster. Love the full set outfits though (but not all of them). But why is Doc’s pre-collapse outfit her in a punk outfit instead of being in med school uniform? Since she talks about being med school a lot, wouldn't it be an obvious choice?
>Game looks pretty in most areas, modern graphics n all. Love the lighting in some areas too.

Cons:
> Can’t choose which survivors to bring in singleplayer
> Survivors don't bicker, banter, panic, or even do any social interaction with each other and they don’t react a lot to their environment. I was expecting anyone to react to some of the places (esp the cool vinyl shop safehouse) but they just stay silent aside from saying repetitive one-liners. My goodness are these guys even human? They don’t even act like one in-game!

I know the multiplayer’s the main focus of this game, but survivors doing that was my most favorite thing in l4d1&2, which added a lot of life to the game more than I and anyone else could realize. Most people say it’s a good thing because of B4B’s bad writing and that the characters are flat and one dimensional, since one of l4d2’s greatest strengths was characters like Nick developing through the story.

TO BE FAIR, there are moments where they show parts about themselves and their past, but it’s rarer than a shiny Pokémon to even see/hear them.

> Hordes don’t feel like hordes, the adrenaline rush from L4D is basically nonexistent and I nearly fell asleep.
> The game pretty much tells us what the director does. It’s like a top legend mega master chef revealing their most secret ingredients.
> The special infected.

Other Comments:
> What makes this game 'bad' in a sense is that the devs stopped developing the game when it could’ve been better to improve most aspects of it + the reason why people compare it to L4D a lot was due to the marketing saying that it’s a L4D successor, which fumbled so hard. If anything, if it wasn’t marketed as the L4D successor it would’ve been an OK game. The expectations couldn’t be lowered if they were already set sky high to begin with. This game is wide like an ocean, but it’s as deep as a puddle. Most of the game could’ve been good or even better if they actually followed through.

> This is probably a good example of how not to reboot a series. It feels like L4D but with all the life, detail, and replayability sucked out of existence. I think the biggest difference between this game and L4D in terms of lore/atmosphere(?) was on how light-hearted this game is compared to its predecessor. L4D can be light-hearted, sure. But looking at the bigger picture, it’s an absolutely hopeless existence to be a survivor and that was the very essence of L4D 1&2 lore. I, at least, didn’t see that in B4B (despite it having the same thing going on). And I think it’s what made most L4D players look the other way.

I know B4B wants to have a more hopeful and cheerier vibe, but the existence of hope persists due to the lack of it. This is something B4B fumbles to show, especially when the characters pretty much feel like they’re detached from the situation most of the time with the only thing going for them being how badass they are.

> ”Well you’re just too biased to L4D!”
L4D isn’t perfect, L4D2 is
lol jk
L4D, the series, isn’t perfect. But the good outweighs the bad tenfold. There were times when I also didn’t like L4D, especially during multiplayer. And singleplayer bores me from time to time + the mere fact that valve can't count to 3. But its charm woos me back like a toxic 11/10 ex-lover. I wanted to enjoy B4B. I really do. But I just can’t… It’s like watching a Phase 4 MCU movie that isn’t Guardians of the Galaxy 3…

VERDICT:
It does some of the things better than L4D, but executed badly. It’s like having a really good framework for a house but the roof and walls are made out of onion skin paper and scotch tape.

TL;DR: A very hit-or-miss game. When it hits, it hits hard. When it misses, it does so a lot. Buy it on sale if you really wanna play it. I should’ve bought SimCity 4 though with that money I had (it was from my grandmother too, which I feel really bad). Thanks for reading if you read everything though. I hope you have a wonderful day!
