But, I learned to move away from that idealization and the game actually got a little better. When it first came out there were definitely some bumps which the devs addressed for the most part, but I feel like they also taking away the more enjoyable parts of the game (such as the speed decks). I feel like ultimately they never proved themselves with this game, and after their last DLC "River of Blood" they stated they were moving onto a new game which was pretty shocking. I thought it was first said that they would give love to this game for a while and provide consistent updates but that is definitely not the case.

If the game is EXTREMELY on sale, I would say yeah buy it as it can be fun for a bit. If you plan on playing with 3 other friends, only one of you needs the DLC to actually play the DLC with each other (wish I had known that instead of telling my friends to get the season pass prior). You could also get DLC 2 and 3, and skip the first one in my opinion. I think game devs really need to move away from the idea of saying they were associated with X game, because I feel like it ends with a disappointment for everyone (another example aside from this game is the callisto protocol).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, this is definitely a 10 out of 10 recommendation. To me is one of the best zombie/horde games I have ever played and definitely a spiritual successor to left4dead. I have no clue why it gets so much hate but it is not deserved. Maybe it had a rough start but come on, in 2023 this game rocks! Super fun, super tactical, 100% shooter.

First the graphics! This game even in low res looks phenomenal, super immersive, claustrophobic at times. The inside environments are gross as the bathroom of a chili restaurant and the outside is... well depressing (as it should be of course) and at the same time beautiful (call that irony).

The monsters are BIG, super big but they fuel the adrenaline you already have with the horde of zombies x10. Lethal machines of dead. And in one ACT... damn!! Never played a more epic bosss fight with a huge lovecraftian monstruosity with tentacles... it was amazing!

I m used to play online with strangers and it keeps being truly rewarding. No voice chat only gameplay and my team was really tight. We did the run in veteran and seriously recommend it. It s a bit more difficult than beginner but adds friendly fire that always teaches you proper aiming. Some scenarios I played solo online and the bots did great teamwork. These are probably the best bots in this kind of games. They kick ass. You don t feel you are playing with stormtroppers, they know their business and are good at it.

I was surprised to see that at some point I ve got a message asking if I wanted to kick a player that was doing a lot of friendly fire. Of course I passed and presume all the rest of the team did as well as who cares about that in veteran level but it is nice to have that automatic alert to prevent big loses in nightmare mode.

On the technical side, on PC at least, I had no technical issues at all. My only complain would it be that this game doesn t have exclusive full screen but have to say I had a descent frame rate and in low you can enjoy the graphics and flow with the wind sending undead back to their graves like a strange death fairy from the Hades.

Anyway, I recommend to any buyer not to pay attention to the negative reviews. I found them unfunded and can only explain them as left4dead fan boys critizising this is not left4dead. Try the game and if you dont like it you can always refund it. But if you are looking for a good shooter to have tons of fun this is the game for you, whether it is solo or coop. Haven t tried PVP as it is not my cup of tea.

Oh, and be prepared for the gore. This game is sometimes pretty disgusting :P

One important thing I believe this game excels at is short scenarios. I played a lot of Vermintide 2 and lately stopped playing because the scenarios are super long. In this one you finish one and want to continue with the next one.

10 out of 10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game, marketed as the spiritual successor to perhaps the greatest COOP game ever made and one of my personal favorites. Alas, they strayed way way too far from the formula and created a clunky, uninteresting mess of a game. Some portions were enjoyable, but overall this is a steaming pile (if you ask me).

Here are some of the pro's of the game:
+ Graphically pretty good, it has certainly modernized many of the infected (ridden) but to be honest, they look kinda cartoonish. I do prefer models of the L4D2 zombies and if you want to really enjoy modernized character models of "zombies" checkout Days Gone.

That's about it for pro's.

Here are some of the cons:
- The card system is clunky, it over complicates the game, and destroys the immersion element, it feels contrived and forced to try and bring in a way to create a re-playability experience by inserting some sort of randomness.
- Utterly forgettable characters, I cannot really relate to them or at least empathize with them, unlike virtually all the L4D characters.
- Too many characters, this has to do with the above, utterly forgettable characters as well.
- Did I mention the card system destroy the immersivity of the game??? Well it does, and it should be clear that I do not like it.
- The straight through linear, single campaign compares poorly to the segmented campaigns with 4 or 5 maps of L4D
- Too many weapon options, becomes laborious building a load out.
- Story elements are boring and uninteresting much like the characters. Seems like it was written by a bunch of millennials.
- Match making system was overtly complicated.


Overall the thing that elevates L4D way way above this game is it's simplicity. Carry one weapon, a health pack/defib, pills, grenade/moly/bile, and a melee or pistols, and all of that you had to scavenge from a clearly decimated apocalyptic level, you would have to make choices when deciding what to carry, simple choice like to I drop the bile to get a pipe bomb. The campaigns were broken into 4 or 5 maps, which stitched together to paint a visual story from beginning to end (rescue) that was both immersive and fun to replay over and over. The director changed things up enough in terms of horde timing, random special spawning, weapon placements and ammo piles making re-playability a blast (and a feature), but most importantly the mechanics and design focused on and emphasized cooperative play which means you can play over and over with a mix of new and different people to get a whole new experience. B4B had none of this or what little it had it was done so poorly that it left me puzzled at what they were really trying to do.

There is no way I can recommend this game, I think even at 5 bucks it is not really worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
