\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBack 4 Blood has made itself one of my favorite co-op zombie shooters of all time. I know the game got a bad wrap at the beginning of its life because it wasn’t Left 4 Dead but I think that’s honestly what makes it as good as it is.

Pros:
•Fluid movement mechanics. Sprinting, vaulting/mantling and shoving zombies out of your way feel really good. This alone stops me from enjoying L4D as much these days.

•The Card System allows for dynamic builds that let you play the game differently every time (or do the same thing really well that you like forever). At launch the card system held the game back and added another barrier into a game that was brutally difficult (and gave it its bad wrap of *Not L4D*). My first night playing a friend and I played the first two levels 7 times before giving up and calling it a night. The current card system lets you feel the full power of your choices immediately.

•The guns all feel really good. The attachment system adds depth to your choices. You may have a green tier gun with a lot of good attachments and find the same one in blue tier that’s not instantly better.

•The DLCs are very player friendly. B4B just wants you and your friends to enjoy the game. If one player has the DLCs purchased everyone in the group can play the DLC content. You cannot play the DLC characters if you don’t own it. This feels like a fair trade off.

Cons:
•The story forces you to spend half the game at Fort Hope. Unlike L4D the game doesn’t support the standalone act design. Every section of the game is a continuation of the last section (up until the DLCs). You will be spending a lot of time on the maps you’ve already been to.

•Most of the special risen are bullet sponges. You feel this the most on higher difficulties.

•The game is designed around you having a strong deck. If you don’t optimize (or have the cards) you’ll struggle.

•The unlock system forces replay ability into the game. I’m glad Turtlerock didn’t add mtx to the game but the way new cards are unlocked can be frustrating. There are several premium currencies that you can unlock after each level you play which unlock cards on different tracks back in camp. Having to grind lower difficulties in order to unlock more cards to play on the higher difficulties doesn’t feel good.

Middle of the road:
These are things that I personally like but I know some people have their gripes with them. Love em or hate em.

•The characters have a lot to say.

•The copper system in general. Having the ability to buy the gear you want as well as find it in the world is good. If you don’t coordinate with your team it’s hard to get the most out of the shops during the missions. You can also purchase new cards to add to your deck if you find them during game play. They’re not permanent but it’s a fun mechanic that adds to the rogue like feel a deck builder system typically supports.

•The level of difficulty. It’s really been tuned back from what it used to be, but the high end is still challenging.

•No versus mode substitute. Unlike L4D the only competitive pvp mode is dead. People don’t play it. I know I never did.

Overall B4B is a great game that suffers because of a rough launch and misconceptions of it being “New L4D”. The movement, gunplay, and card system feel good. Trying new builds specifically adds a lot of replay value into the game. It’s a great game for full price so you can never go wrong buying it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
