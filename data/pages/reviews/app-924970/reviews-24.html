\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBack when L4D was released I played that game (and L4D2) extensively. I knew about Turtle Rock and the history behind it. I enjoyed the L4D series for a long time. When B4B was announced I was kinda hyped, but that quickly changed after reading lots of reviews and watching review-videos. I never bought the game due to that.

When it was on sale, we (a group of four) bought the game just because we didn't have anything else to play at the time. We though it wouldn't hurt to try it for that price.

What can I say? We were dead-wrong to trust all the reviews. This game IS an enhanced version of L4D, and I can't put my finger on it why people hated it so much back then. I rewatched some Reviews (i.e. the Angry Review from Angry Joe) and most of the criticism stemmed from the poor story telling or immersion..... ok, so what? The gameplay itself is very good.

The ONLY thing that really stands out is the EXTREMELY high difficulty even on the lower-medium setting. Yeah, that's a thing. This game is very very difficult, and you will die and retry A LOT. But we, as a group, grew on that and constantly adapt our tactics and get better at our personal skills and communications.

All I can say is: We were wrong to just trust all the critics and not try the game ourselves. And in response, it's a shame that the Devs practically had to abandon the game because it didn't gain any traction. I don't know if the game was patched since all those negative Reviews, but in the end, we enjoy the game more than L4D at the moment. Diffiulty is a real thing, Story is lacking more and more... but in terms of gameplay... I can't complain. Quite the opposite. This game is VERY enjoyable with a group of friends if you are ready to get your ass handed to you and try to get better!\t\t\t\t\t
