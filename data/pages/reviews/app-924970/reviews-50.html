\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally avoided this game due to the announcement of DLC on launch. Coming from the expectations of Left 4 Dead 2 and its abundance of content/mod support, it just rubbed me the wrong way. After release, I didn't hear anything good about it.

Then it went on sale, with DLC, for $13.50; low risk. And the game? Its actually good.

Is it better than Left 4 Dead 2? Yes. But I can only say that because after playing this I reinstalled Left 4 Dead 2 and realized this is better. My memories of Left 4 Dead 2 betray what it is today. Left 4 Dead 2 doesn't look great, its weapon options limited, no character customization, and no progression. Back 4 Blood is what I wish a Left 4 Dead 3 would have been.

Does Back 4 Blood have flaws? Sure. Its matchmaking is slow, though at least its cross-platform. It does come at the risk of encountering console players with hot mics. You can't change your skill/stat cards mid-run. Not a big issue later, but early on it makes getting cards while running the starter deck a bit of a let down. When you join a quick play game, you have no control over the deck you get until you finish the round and load into the next map; you end up playing a build you don't want to play.

But for $15... I'll let it have its flaws. I rather feel guilty about getting this much game for that price. But I'll take it.\t\t\t\t\t
