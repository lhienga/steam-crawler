\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter reading the reviews for this game I was undecided if I should try it or not, the reviews are not good and that almost persuaded me to not bother with the game, however, I watched an actual game play video and it looked like something I would enjoy so I decided to buy it.

After playing the game for about 1-2 hours I soon realised I'd made the right choice so I bought the season pass soon after.

The game offers a good selection of weapons to choose from, ranging from pistols, assault rifles, sniper rifles and shotguns, you also have melee weapons to use too. These can have new attachments added during play as you find weapon crates scattered around the map. You can also find legendary weapons which also come in a variety of firearms.

The game also has a card system that can be set up and saved before a campaign, these cards are varied and will allow you to focus on a specific skill during that game, e.g. sniper, medic, etc.

The campaigns are spread over several maps, each with objectives to complete to open the safe room, the objectives can range from rescuing other survivors to planting explosives, which objectives you have will depend on that particular campaign.

The zombies or ridden are the main things you will kill during a campaign, they range from basic cannon fodder (common ridden) to much tougher opponents (special ridden). The common ridden are easy enough to put down, a single shot to the head is sufficient but the special ridden will take one or more magazines to put down, these specials have weak spots on their body so shooting a weak spot kills them much faster. The common ridden can also appear in a horde if they're alerted by car alarms, birds, etc.

If you're playing solo you will get help from 3 bots, compared to most games, the bots are really good and will stay with you during the campaign. They can't start events but will give you good protection.

One feature I do like is being able to save a campaign once you reach a safe room, when you want to play the game again you can continue from that point.

Where this game really shines for me is the character movement, that is so fluid and smooth, moving around a map or climbing/jumping over obstacles feels perfect.

I'm glad I picked this game up, it was a risk based on the reviews I had read so if you're unsure whether to buy it or not I'd suggest watching an actual game play video and base your decision on that, I did, and I'm so glad I did, after playing the game, I think some of the reviews are very misleading and I almost missed a gem.\t\t\t\t\t

I am playing solo and on the easier recruit difficulty.

Bought the game on special just to do the campaign then uninstall as I wanted to see what the negativity was about and even though I played that beta free weekend a few years back, I wasn't impressed then nor am I that impressed now.

It's fun for a while as you just mindlessly shoot zombies but the main problem I have with the game and what I dislike about it is that when the "ridden/special zombies" spawn...

The way they spawn is lazy, reminds me of Cyberpunk Police how they would appear like 30 metres behind, infront or either side of you... That is not fun at all, it is ABSOLUTE BS.. The AI for the special infected are way better in the L4D games than this B4B crap...

If you want some modern mechanics and visuals for that nostalgic L4D feels then I would say get this game on HEAVY discount, play the campaign then uninstall.

I didn't want to bother any of the mates with this game, hence the solo campaign run then call it a day but honestly... it isn't as fun as the L4D games were imho..

Reason why the reviews are mixed as it's 50/50 depending on the individual..
