\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBack 4 Blood is a game that I really wanted to like, but unfortunately, I just can't get behind a number of flaws that prevent me from fully embracing it. I think many people share this sentiment, as the expectation for this game for many was a modern Left 4 Dead experience that takes a working formula and improves upon it. I didn't expect a game that completely reinvents the wheel, but rather one that builds upon the success of Left 4 Dead and finds ways to enhance the experience. In its attempt to differentiate itself from Left 4 Dead, Back 4 Blood loses sight of what made it so enjoyable: its simplicity. Left 4 Dead is approachable and easy to learn, but hard to master. Unfortunately, Back 4 Blood tries to do too much, and the time spent developing additional systems could have been better utilized in improving other aspects of the game. Small details are what can make a game feel great, and Left 4 Dead is a perfect example of this. The effort put into making shooting zombies feel as satisfying as possible in Left 4 Dead, with various mocap deaths and a gore system that still holds up pretty well today, are details that elevates the game's overall experience. However, Back 4 Blood lacks many of these details, which results in the game not feeling as enjoyable to play.

Of course, my opinion comes from the perspective of someone who wanted "Left 4 Dead but more." I don't want to discredit the game's audience, as I'm sure there are people who enjoy it, and that's great. If you're part of that audience, keep enjoying the game, even if others don't share your sentiment. But for people like me who were hoping for a modern Left 4 Dead experience, Back 4 Blood just doesn't scratch that itch. I think it's worth buying only if you're willing to take the risk and make that decision for yourself. Ultimately, Back 4 Blood isn't necessarily a bad game (even if there are some design decisions that are quite questionable), but if you're going into it with the expectation that it will deliver the modern Left 4 Dead experience you've been craving, you're going to be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
