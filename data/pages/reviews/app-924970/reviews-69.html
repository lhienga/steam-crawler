\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game IS NOT worth the full price tag.
However, when on sales, down to about $10. You will get your fun worth.

PvP mode is mega dead. 1hour+ long queue. If your expectation is from L4D Versus, probably won't enjoy this game.

PvE Campaign, however, is 1000% fun. Replayability is through the roof. Randomized difficulty every time you replay the same levels. Customized/ personalized loadouts and guns. There is a lot of wacky and fun build to tailor your gameplay. Or you can also do vanilla shooter build. Saferoom Shop and Scavenges are randomized. It is a unique rogue-like experience to bring a build and find whatever you can to adjust your gameplay.

If your expectation is from L4D Co-op, B4B takes a little of time to grind progression to unlock builds. Unlike in L4D, you can just hop on, pick a gun and shoot. In B4B weapons have stats and rarity. Also Health Mechanics/ CC Mechanics are unique about B4B. It might take some learning curve.

Each whole act is pretty lengthy. About 3-5 hours to finish the whole act from start to finale, depends how many Hives you go in/ how your team pace the game. I suggest to host your own, so that you can pause mid-act and continue some other time. If you just join a random lobby, well.... expect to lose all your gear and loadout when the run fails cos you ain't the host.

Should you buy the DLCs?

DLC 1 is the only important one. It unlocks you Hives. They allow you to skip some levels, otherwise tough ending, as well as strengthens the rest of your run by giving you the chance to find more cash and better gears.

DLC 2 and 3 unlocks you Act 5 and Act 6 respectively. Only Act 1 - 4 are base game.

And of course, unique characters and cosmetics in each DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot this one sale to finally try it out and actually found it pretty fun. If you ever played L4D2, if you ever tried those modded servers where you can upgrade stuff only to end up ridiculously broken and super powered while blasting through endless waves of infected...then this game is basically that. Is it a proper upgrade/improvement to L4D2? Hell no, but it seems divergent enough from the L4D games to be its own proper title. Customizing your perks and loadouts its a fun game to jump on into with friends. The campaign itself is alright, doesn't quite deliver as effectively as the horde attacks and panic moments of L4D 1 and 2 but still works in certain spots. Having mutations occur each map keeps challenge from getting too stale, though with the right perks and loadouts it almost feels like cheating sometimes going through an allegedly tough area without breaking a sweat.

In short: fun but not as polished as L4D2. Still a blast to try anyway. Definite recommend if you get it on a discount.\t\t\t\t\t

That's apparent in Back 4 Blood. The characters are lifeless and the dialogue is dull and uninspired. You're allowed limited replays in a single play through which is ridiculous (like 1 or 2). The damage weapons to infected is considerably different than what they're capable of in real life, even with the idea of balancing the damage in mind it still falls short and Turtle Rock should be embarrassed. There is no support for the modding community. However, the idea of character perks and weapon mods are cool. It's a shame Turtle Rock shilled this garbage as a "Left 4 Dead 3". I wish I got a refund. I'll never buy another turtle rock game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBack 4 Blood's fundemental problem was that it had no idea what it wanted to be.

Several of the game's systems went through pretty major overhauls over its initial few months, including wild balance swings, and then, once all obligations were completed, it was promptly dropped like a hot potato. The last update was in December of 2022 (so almost a full year on from when I'm writing this review,) and with nothing suggesting that they're coming back, I feel safe in calling this game abandoned.

This is a massive shame, and is a direct result of yet another company falling prey to the idea that modern games all need to be live-service, constantly pumped for money ongoing projects, instead of fully complete products that can be added to or serialised naturally.

But enough about that, what's the game actually like?

Overly
Complicated.

Left 4 Dead 2, the game that Back 4 Blood desperately wants to be, is shockingly simple at its core. Characters are identical, weapons follow a pretty simple tier system in terms of power, and equipment and utilities are all very straightforward. None of this is true in Back 4 Blood. Characters now have associated skills that means playing different ones can be very different experiences, and because the characters are single-pick, you've now introduced fighting over who gets to play which one in the core experience.

Weapons are now a mess of attachments, attachment rarity, weapon rarity, weapon types and how those types interact with skills and 'cards,' (we'll get to that later.) In L4D2, you'd naturally gravitate to the weapons which you personally found the most effective and useful for each tier of progression, here you're stuck in wondering if you want to swap out the scope you like for a scope that's a higher level and offers better stats, but that you don't like. Or, finding a new weapon that's objectively better than your current one, but of a type or for a build you don't want. It's silly nonsense looter-shooteresque gimmicks that doesn't really improve the core gameplay - plugging zombies in the head.

Then, there are the cards and decks. Because these are cool now, I suppose, instead of a skill tree or simple progression system, you now have this bizarre roguelike progression system where you unlock 'cards' entirely independent of your actual progression through the story. These can be anything from minor statistic upgrades to build-defining slots that completely change how you'll be going through a questline. Also, because they needed yet another progression system the decks that you assemble aren't handed to you all at once - instead you unlock them, level by level, during a given questline.

Finally, what about the enemies you're facing? Well, L4D2 had eight special infected, each highly identifiable, with a specific role to play in punishing the group. Boomers forced you to spread out, smokers and hunters made sure you couldn't wander off alone, jockeys and spitters stopped you from camping in a spot... Look, you don't need me to explain this, you already know. They're effective because they're immediately identifiable and their roles are narrow and contained.

Back 4 Blood ♥♥♥♥♥ this system. You have different classes of special infected, all with names similar enough that it's easy to get them mixed up, and no less than three different boss mutations (and two more, but they're excused for being story-based 'final bosses.') Is that a Hocker or Spitter on that lamppost there? Good luck deciphering that in the often dark or foggy maps. Is that a crusher, tallboy or bruiser stomping towards your team? Better figure it out quick, because they each do slightly different things. There are some inspired ideas here - Sleepers in particular add an element of paranoia to the game's narrow alleyways and dark tunnels, while the 'Sentinel' family requires you to mix up how you approach them, and that's all I have that's good to say about them.

Ultimately then? Back 4 Blood is another in a now long line of Turtle Rock failures. The gameplay is perfectly serviceable for the most part, but it doesn't matter. because the core gameplay loop that has kept people playing L4D2 for over a decade is completely snowed over by unnecessary complexity and now-redundant live-service nonsense. Could the game have been rescued? Absolutely. But it's not going to be. Just keep playing L4D2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was never Left 4 Dead 3 and was never going to be. Turtle Rock has never had a clear vision for the game, with most game mechanics changing dramatically over the game's short one year life. There were some good ideas in here -- and I mean that genuinely -- but none of them are given the focus they need to actually be realized. In the end this is just a game where some devs had a hundred different ideas on how to make the L4D formula better and instead of trimming down that list they just haphazardly implemented all of them.

It's possible that the many, many holes in the game could have been fixed. Just look at how NMS and more recently Cyberpunk have bounced back after launching as complete messes. But Turtle Rock doesn't want to put in that kind of effort. They fulfilled the Season Pass obligated DLC and then immediately dropped the game. They gave Evolve much more post-launch support than this game. Think about how sad that is for a moment.

If you're looking for Left 4 Dead 3, just play Left 4 Dead 2 again. No, it doesn't have a progression system and a battle pass and currencies to grind, but the gameplay still holds up. It's a damn good game. And if you've played Back 4 Blood, you'll appreciate it even more because you'll spend your entire first campaign back constantly thinking "huh, yeah, why DID they change how that worked?" or "why DID they get rid of this?" or "why DID they change how that worked?", and you'll realize there's very few good answers.

Maybe we can look forward to Back 4 Blood Stage 2 in a few years.\t\t\t\t\t
