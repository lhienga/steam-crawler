\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR Very fun and underrated game with a bit of a learning curve.

Back 4 Blood in 2024 is a competent co-op horde shooter that's worth your time. It's plain ol' zombie killing goodness. The twist revolves around deck building to create useful, unique, and sometimes broken classes. Be a melee tank that leeches health from kills or a demolition class that runs around the map with a pocket full of explosives. Play the team medic that keeps everyone buffed and alive or play a high damage sniper that drops threats from long distances. Shotgun builds clear rooms and stumble big threats, LMGs and ARs can mow through Ridden if you've got ammo bags and reload cards, and sidearm builds let you weave through hordes while taking them out with precision headshots. Even the character you choose can play a role in buffing your build.

There's a couple hundred cards to choose from so get creative in the ways you min/max your decks, but be aware that there are certain cards that are pretty necessary for the challenge of higher difficulties. Don't expect to jump into Veteran or worse and just start slaying. Clear the campaign on Recruit (yes, the easiest difficulty) a time or three. That will reward you with a chunk of "supply points" that are spent on "supply lines" to unlock cards. Joining random in-progress runs through Quickplay gives a supply point multiplier, but playing offline with bots still works if you prefer that.

Playing the campaign on recruit will also help you learn important game mechanics. A lot of levels have unique puzzles and strategies that are best learned when you're not fighting to stay alive. Learn how health works, how trauma damage affects it, and how to heal it at first aid stations or manage it with temporary health like pills. A good newbie character is Karlee, as she has a passive that outlines mutations (special infected) for you. Learn the signs and sounds of mutations so you can deal with them before they overwhelm your team with heavy damage and hazards or incapacitate teammates with grapples.

Teamwork! Pinging dangerous mutations to alert your team or pointing them to equipment, first-aid, and item stashes can really help influence the success of a run. Difficulties above Recruit introduce friendly fire so be aware and try not to kill your teammates. Avoid going solo as you'll be picked off if you're caught unaware with nobody to help you.

Be on the lookout for the currency you pick up and use during runs called Copper. Copper is split evenly among all players and you all manage your own balance. It's locked into your in-progress runs and wipes when you beat or fail the chapter. Copper can be spent at a supply chest at the beginning of every level before you leave the safe room. Copper cards and careful saving can allow you to buy permanent team buffs for the rest of your run that increase the quality of items and gadgets or increase the amount you can carry. Second to those are small team stat buffs for health, ammo, and stamina, but I wouldn't prioritize them if copper is tight. Guns, melee weapons, mods, ammo, and items are available but resist buying too many of these as you can easily find all of that during a level.

Every level has randomly selected Corruption cards that influence enemy types, environmental hazards, special bosses or events, and extra challenges that you can perform for bonus Copper. If you have the DLC (or play with anyone who does) then you might find Ridden Lair entrances during a level. Be aware that these skip the rest of your current level and are meant as a challenge. They're unique underground hives that have extreme Corruption cards active and are your only way of acquiring the Skull Totems used to unlock rare and powerful DLC cards. Take the risk if you feel up to it but I'd recommend learning the main levels first.

Play with friends for maximum fun but I don't find playing with randoms too bad, even with Crossplay enabled. There's plenty of good players that hang out in Quickplay and people that make public online runs you can join. The PvP mode (Swarm) was basically DoA and is a ghost town at this point so don't bother. The campaign is plenty long and varied with a lot of replayability due to Corruption cards and deck building mixing with classic horde shooter mechanics.

I recommend Back 4 Blood now in 2024 wholeheartedly. I've played it on my old Xbox and now my PC. I've probably got a couple hundred hours combined between the two and it's definitely an enjoyable and worthwhile experience. Throw out your expectations, grab it on a crazy sale, and give it a fair shot.\t\t\t\t\t
