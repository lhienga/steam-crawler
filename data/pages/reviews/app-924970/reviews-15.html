\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who absolutely adored Left 4 Dead and heard a lot of bad things about this game, it's been a bit of a roller coaster experience. I begrudgingly bought the game whilst it was on sale with my mates (Was annoyed that it was 50gb for a game I wasn't interested in) and upon booting it up, really felt the things the game wasn't, we between us commented on the hammy dialogue, how crude the currency felt and the broken health system. It was what I expected it to be.

But, the level design was varied and there was a pretty staggering amount of content ahead of us, so we forged on with it through the first act. We did some reading into strategies and I settled into the character of Doc, which made a colossal difference to my experience and that of my teammates. We were still struggling but with a better grasp of the trauma system (TRUST ME, READ UP ABOUT IT), we began to function better as a team. They'd pass me health kits and huddle around as I topped people up and we settled into weapons we were comfortable with.

37 hours in and it's just been such a blast. We had a 4th friend join us, we've all done extra research to inform our decks and we've hit the point where we're doing team builds and tearing through maps like a finely oiled machine. The map variation and set pieces have far outclassed its predecessor and what initially from the outside looked like an unnecessary scale of complexity in unlocks (akin to many live services game) has provided a depth that is so satisfying when you have a deck and weapon combo that works. We did our first inner hive tonight and we absolutely blitzed it. We've also had a fair share of moments paying homage to evenings spent playing Left 4 Dead, tonight my friend Jack was the last man standing and we were all screaming for him to reach the end of the level and in his desperation to reach the escape boat, he accidentally jumped off the cliff and died, we all burst out laughing at his mistake, despite us losing. Proper had us tickled.

That isn't to say there isn't issues, by far my greatest complaint that I plainly cannot understand is why flashlights aren't toggleable, it's infuriating stumbling around in the dark waiting for it to turn on or having it switch off without warning once you've left the designated "dark" area (PLEASE FIX THIS). But if you're patient and relatively forgiving with this game, it honestly has so much to offer. Again, Left 4 Dead is one of my all time favourite games and I was ready to not give this game a chance, but I'm glad for my time with it and to share that experience with my mates.

It's honestly quite sad, Turtle Rock got a lot of flak for this game at the time and whilst I can't comment about how the game was at launch, given the time we've spent with it (And in the absence of Valve making another sequel) they have created something that is truly a worthy successor to Left 4 Dead. As a games developer myself, players can be quite unforgiving with their opinions and it's particularly crushing when you don't meet the expectations of your fans, it's somewhat humbling to admit that I was wrong to dismiss the game off the opinions of other players (In particular that crowbcat video) without giving it a try myself. That is with the very big caveat that I didn't pay full price for it, there is a chance that if I'd not picked it up on sale I'd have never tried the game or I'd have refunded based on my early experiences, at Â£50 it's a hard sell. Wait for a sale and bring friends.

If you are a fan of Left 4 Dead, I would genuinely recommend giving it a go, try to go in with an open mind and the willingness to do a little reading on the topic and you'll be in for a hell of a ride. There's definitely parts you'll miss (E.g. For me it's the AI music system) but there's still so much more this title brings to the table.\t\t\t\t\t
