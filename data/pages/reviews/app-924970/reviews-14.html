It's Not Easy To Like Back 4 Blood
(no worth full price, buy during sales)

Where RNG Is Lord and Fail the Beginning
My first impressions were solely positive: juicy picture, gore, familiar run/survive/reach the exit.
There's plenty of content e.g. roughly 10 missions per each campaign, a long journey to make!
Moreover, you can select one of many playable characters and enhance your experience with buffs (in-game cards).

Unfortunately, the game will do the same. Its AI keeps adding ominous cards i.e. armoured enemies, oxygen starvation, burning zombies and many alike. Imaging how annoying it is to die instantaneously from an unlucky hit that sent you overboard, because the game activated elite zombies or a roaming boss, who now block the only escape route during infinite assault. What initially looked like a cool feature, quickly turned into a frustrating mechanic.
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3182353553
May the Rare Loot Shine Upon Us
B4B has random drops and weapon attachments! Common, rare, epic, legendary - all that jazz. Higher rarity means better damage. Plus each level has card spawns, where you can add (buy) a random card for your current campaign.

However. You're kind of glued to one weapon/playstyle type, because of the cards which you put into your deck. That means that any sweet high damage gun is of little use for a melee build. That explosive hammer is fun and joy, but you have invested into a sniper build... Let a sigh out and continue checking other crates and boxes.
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3182354876
Sinister Fields of the Defiled Ridden Kingdom
Let's talk levels?
Act 5 and Act 6 are my favourites. Even though these had the least maps (6 & 5 respectively), I replayed them more. They had more traps, new dangers, cool museum exhibition, pushing the cart with explosives, detonating houses, travelling through tv news building and then surviving fierce onslaught.

Don't get me wrong, original campaigns have memorable episodes too: defending a pub with jukebox, firing up a cannon (with a possibility to kill a boss with it!) and that boss fight, which somehow was a lot easier than previous acts.
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3173483546
And then there are hidden lairs - ridden hives. Randomly spawning entrances leading to meaty caves with skulls to find (and later trade for cosmetic or cards) and get out alive. Shorter than a usual mission, but fun to explore and shoot hordes within.

Back 4 Blood is a nice game, but it might take a long time to grow on you. Certain limitations and game-mechanics will frustrate, so be ready to sharpen pve skills and avoid playing with cross-play.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCharacters.
Have no personality traits and are super underdeveloped. There are no memetic/comedic interactions between the main protagonists which makes this game simply boring and not fun to play. In addition, all cleaners are incredibly ugly and lack any sort of sexualization. I picked Doc as my main character which turned out to be the only good looking character in this game. Yet, even here, unfortunately, I was reduced to disappointment. To my great surprise, the lady which clearly is a 30~ year old female, sounded like a 60 year old hag. I bet the devs were smoking bamboo during important decision making phases, as I can’t understand why one would make a young Asian lady sound like a grandmother.

Gameplay.
Way too fast-paced and, well, there’s really no such terminology in my vocabulary to describe all my bitterness and regret regarding the gameplay of this game. In Left 4 Dead series you’d take cover near the corners or walls and carefully clean the zombies and super infected (what are called riddens in this game) from the remaining directions. You’d always keep an eye on your buddies as there could be a sneaky smoker or jockey to pull away your teammate during the transition phases (i.e. dropdown to the sewers in the Paris). In Back 4 Blood all of that is simply irrelevant. There’s no point in taking cover; the zombies are spawning from every direction regardless of your position. The super infected classes are retarded; you can no longer do super combo-wombo’s like you used to in Left 4 Dead 2. Killing zombies in general just feels awkward and unrewarding. Shame.

Verdict.
Turtle Rock Studios had a brilliant asset at the tips of their fingers prior developing Back 4 Blood. It’s not really clear to me why they decided to exclude the things that ‘worked’ in Left 4 Dead and, instead, made a completely different game which is rather oversaturated, yet underdeveloped. To me, it feels that the devs were cutting corners while making this game, and I would only recommend buying it for a huge discount.

Game is actually ok, I liked the gun sounds, problem was, when I played this back then... I got stuck at the last boss. AI allies were bad, getting a human player was hard already, and that last level was punishing, especially if you don't have a full team. At that point the game was already relying on player count due to the difficulty, and we all know the game's state.

Card deck system was a good try for a twist, and it was fairly ok, but these things will tend to lead to meta builds... There was also character progression, which was nice, but I didn't really feel that power from character progression that much, I wish I did though!

Game actually had potential, but sadly, it didn't live up to it.
