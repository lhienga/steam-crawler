\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a huge Left 4 Dead fan since playing the first one as a youngin in the 360 era to getting the second game fairly recently, I kept hearing tons of things from the community about this game. I decided to wait for the game to go on sale to see if it's as bad as us fans normally make it out to be.

From playing the game for a few hours, I can confirm that game actually isn't terrible, it's a very good horde shooter, but nowhere close to the long desired L4D3. Keep that in mind when going in. Of course there are various similarities between the two, including survivors, special infected and gameplay mechanics, however, one of the main differences between this and L4D is the card system used to improve your character's stats.

Survivors seem to be your typical zombie slayin' hardasses, with which there are 8 with the base game, 12 in total with the added DLCs. A few roughly correspond to the classic L4D characters from personal pattern recognition (Holly=Zoey, Jim=Ellis, Karlee=girl Francis), but there's a few that don't seem to be based on any character I can think of, such as Hoffman (guy with glasses, slightly chubby) and Doc (small-ish nurse girl)

There seems to be a slightly fresher variety of weapons this time around, crossbows, Wolverine claws and the spiked bat (default with Holly, the only character I've tried out so far)

Special infected are slightly harder to kill this time around, their weak spots being the red blisters on their bodies (for TallBoys, the game's "Chargers" it's on their big arms), occasionally a huge "Ogre" boss spawns requiring everyone on your team to shoot at it before it disappears.

The card system and currency are both fresh additions to the game, being used to improve your character's stats and buy weapons, respectively. At first I didn't care for either, but currency (coppers) are pretty common sights in maps.

TLDR: It's actually a pretty good shooter, a solid horde game with some classic L4D cheese, but is still good enough to stand on its own. If you're planning on getting this expecting the sacred third game, keep my words in mind and enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t

For full price, I think this is even usury.
The story is tacked on and annoying. Most of the time it is just walk from point A to point B, or help the stuck step-bro. Gunplay is mediocre - Mayor improved compared to the beta!
Map design is good, but it blends in so after 2h you don't care about it.

A fun thing are the "deck building" mechanics, as they offer some variety to your gameplay and allow you to specify into a certain niche - if that is allowed from the game. Because you are very much limited from what is available, tank, healer, shotguns, sniper, rifle.
Wanna make a SMG build? Well, no thank you. Wanna make a Pistol build? Such a silly thought! Wanna stack those two cards together and use them like the description mentions? Oh no, sorry, but that's not allowed.

These booster cards want to make you farm the in game currency to get access to them, but they are so limited you get most of them when playing about 75% of the game, for the rest you need to grind dungeons.

Once you unlocked every card, you are forced to unlock skins you don't want, so you can keep unlocking stuff, or other skins. And oh boy are these skins bad. The weapon skins are just tasteless texture slapped on every single gun/melee. The skins for the characters are good, with some exceptions.

Talking about the different playable characters: Each one has a special trait that makes them unique, so you may try one or two. But they just won't shut up - the witty line one pulls is maybe funny the first few times...

4/10

If you preorded this game, then maybe your dad should have pulled out. L+ratio

People, this is a prime example why you don't pre-order!\t\t\t\t\t
