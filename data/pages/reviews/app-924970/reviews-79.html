\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBack 4 Blood : By Triple F

In a gaming landscape hungry for cooperative experiences that blend heart-stopping action with strategic depth, "Back 4 Blood" emerges as a triumphant successor to the legacy of Left 4 Dead. Developed by Turtle Rock Studios, the creators of the iconic Left 4 Dead series, Back 4 Blood not only pays homage to its predecessor but also injects a fresh and thrilling vitality into the cooperative zombie shooter genre.

"Back 4 Blood" wastes no time plunging players into a world overrun by the Ridden, monstrous infected creatures hungry for flesh. From the first gunshot to the final stand, the game is an adrenaline-fueled roller-coaster ride that never loses its momentum. The mechanics of teamwork and resource management shine brightly, encouraging collaboration and communication among players as they fight tooth and nail to survive each escalating onslaught. The feeling of unity amidst the chaos is where "Back 4 Blood" truly excels, bringing friends and strangers together in a symphony of gunfire and strategy.

Central to "Back 4 Blood's" allure is its diverse cast of Cleaners, each with their unique personalities, abilities, and play-styles. Whether you prefer the upfront brawn of Walker, the tactical expertise of Evangelo, or the healing grace of Holly, there's a Cleaner to suit every player's preferred approach. This roster of characters not only adds replay-ability but also encourages experimenting with different team compositions, enhancing the game's strategic depth and fostering a sense of camaraderie among players.

The game's level design boasts a menacing beauty, from the grimy urban streets to the desolate countryside, each environment meticulously crafted to offer both visual appeal and tactical opportunities. The unpredictability of the "Game Director" AI ensures that no two runs are alike, as it adjusts enemy placements, weather conditions, and even the scarcity of resources, forcing players to adapt on the fly and always stay on their toes.

One of "Back 4 Blood's" most innovative features is the "Card System." This deck-building mechanic adds a layer of depth, allowing players to customize their experience by selecting cards that modify game-play variables, such as increased health, unique weapon drops, or enhanced team buffs. This system not only empowers players to tailor the game to their preferred play-style but also contributes to the replay-ability factor, as each run can feel markedly distinct based on the chosen cards.

"Back 4 Blood's" online functionality seamlessly integrates matchmaking and cooperative play, ensuring that players can always find a team to tackle the Ridden horde together. The game's inherently re-playable nature, coupled with a variety of difficulty modes and an engaging progression system, keeps players coming back for more. The thrill of chasing higher difficulties or discovering new strategies with friends turns "Back 4 Blood" into a dynamic, evolving experience that doesn't easily grow stale.

In summary, "Back 4 Blood" truly rekindles the cooperative zombie shooter genre with a roaring blaze, capturing the essence of what made its predecessors great while infusing its own spirit of innovation. The heart-pounding action, dynamic character dynamics, strategic deck-building, and online harmony combine to form an experience that is both exhilarating and unforgettable. Turtle Rock Studios has resurrected the spirit of cooperative chaos, creating a game that's a must-play for fans of intense multiplayer mayhem and a testament to the power of unified teamwork. So, grab your weapons, gather your friends, and prepare to fight for your lives in the thrilling and treacherous world of "Back 4 Blood."\t\t\t\t\t

Back 4 Blood is sadly a victim of the ravenous mob mentality of the gaming community. It does not, and never did deserve the level of hate it has received. Is it not for everyone? of course, no game is. Back 4 Blood was effectively murdered by unreasonable expectations. People were too busy comparing it to Left 4 Dead with Nostalgia glasses to give it a truly fair chance. People wanted more L4D even thought they knew it never would be more than a spiritual successor.

Even today, I see reviews from the past couple days, where people are hating on it, not because of the game. but because the fact that Turtlerock opted to move on. The community wouldn't give them the chance with the game. Basically these recent negative reviews show that somehow people have the expectation that Turtlerock should continue supporting the game they supposedly hate. "I don't like this game, keep giving me content. Otherwise, I'll tell people how bad this game is" It's nonsensical. The community killed a game that didn't deserve it. as Turtlerock has been a better developer than quite a lot of other 3A developers out there. but the world seems to just keep kicking them.

B4B Is a LOT of fun. It takes the co-op zombie shooter formula, and adds layers of personality to it that makes it stand out. While I do agree with some of the criticisms when comparing it to L4D such as character voice lines, or variable animations. Those are just small pieces in a greater picture. It's like calling a slice op pepperoni pizza "bad" because it's missing a single piece of pepperoni because there was enough space to fit an extra piece.

I would LOVE if the community could come back and give the game another go, but sadly that is unlikely to happen unless Turtlerock turns around and tries to do what they did with Evolve Stage 2 (minus the part where 2K shut them down before ES2 could get going)

Gameplay:

So, most people, myself included, are automatically inclined to look at this game by a comparison to L4D. The problem with this, is people wanted it to only be another L4D, and refused to give it credit for what it accomplishes

B4B has a bit more of a story to it at the fore-front. Each act follows a progressive plot. It's there for those who wish to experience that story, but it also doesn't shove it down your throat for those who just wanna rush through like they did in L4D. L4D was mostly a "get from point a to point b" type game. with a little narrative flavor thrown in.

B4B plays less fast and loose than L4D. using item tiers, team upgrades, shops and weapon upgrades, and at it's base, feels more sluggish than L4D due to the variations of including stamina, sprinting, ADS, and variable movement speeds beyond "injured and not injured" However, this is not a negative because of the next point.

B4B's biggest, and arguably my favorite element, is the level of customization that is ingrained into the game (independent of mods like L4D needs) The deck system, while not perfect. counter balances the more rigid nature of the game when compared to left 4 Dead. The 15 card deck can be customized with a LOT of different cards, allowing you to approach and tailor your experience in a way Left 4 Dead never could without massive modding skills beyond just skins and sound packs.

You want to be the objective player? done. You want to be Lightning Mcqueen? done. You can be the team Medic, the packmule, the run and gun, the tank, the Sniper. or any balance of abilities that make your play experience varied. Unlike Left 4 Dead's static "everyone runs exactly the same, shoots exactly the same, and approach the maps the same)

The AI in Left 4 Dead I will agree is stronger in a few ways, but I chalk this up to the fact that was Valve's forte, not Turtlerock. The B4B AI isn't bad by any metric, but as a die hard L4D fan, it is noticable. it is most apparent in the spawning limitations and map navigation of the Mutations. These issues were compensated for with some variations not only in the subtypes of mutations, but the addition of a few new ones in the expansions.

In terms of general feel, B4B is a clean game, even before the updates I encountered very few bugs. I appreciate the greater variety of main and subtypes of the Ridden. Though with the muted color palette I would have liked the subtypes to be a tad more distinct so it's easier to adapt to spawns in the chaos of the hordes. And it's not even that bad, it just took a lot more getting used to than say, L4D which had clear, concise sound, visual, and environmental communication of the far fewer special infected types. But that also goes back to L4D being more fast and loose than B4B.

I could probably say more, admittedly I am typing this up while I am a bit upset seeing the negativity this game has been getting in just the past few days alone. But to summarize, Back 4 Blood is a great co-op zombie shooter with a lot of character, polish, and replayablity. Coming from Left 4 Dead to this is quite Jarring, but giving it a chance is where I feel too many people failed. The only real way Turtlerock would possibly change their mind at this point would require an utterly massive outcry for the game's revival. While I would argue buying the game at full price would help in that respect, I would be a little bit delusional to say that there is a good chance of that happening. As a result, buy it on sale, maybe 40$ or less, because you can have a blast with your friends. or if you just wish to experience the game and it's story, There is enough content there to validate such a price range. The only reason it's not worth full price is because the community killed it.
