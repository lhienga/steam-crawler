The game is good. Stop comparing this to Left 4 Dead. It has maybe some parts from L4D but this game as a standalone, without comparing, is great. Nice amount of levels, the weapons feel awesome, and the story is good as well. Music is good too. Good graphics and I didn`t find any bugs either, and I played for 21 hours.

The only downside is the price. 60 euros is too much, and is only for the base game, for 4 acts. If you want to play the remaining 2 acts you need to pay extra, and each act is 15 euro. so in total, to play the hole game is 90 euro which is too much. I got the game on a sale and it was a very good choice. I really enjoyed the game.

Some tips for beginners

If you want to play the campaign try to use the medic, it will be useful especially because you heal for more, and with a good set of cards, you heal even more, even your teammates. That will really help.

Don`t stay too much in the same location, like of course don`t rush without your teammates because you will soon die, especially if you play on harder difficulty, but don`t waste time either in 1 spot, because more and more zombies will spawn and specials as well, and you just lose your health items.

The weapons that I found that worked the best is m279 and the automatic shotgun. The other guns are okayish but they not compare with a m279 with a lot of bullets. It reloads slower but you can increase the reload speed with some upgrades. It has a lot of bullets and you can headshots the zombies easy, so is a good gun. The shotgun is good because it covers a good real estate, but of course at long range is kinda bad, so you have to go a bit more aggressive with it.

Try to use the pipe bombs. They are better, a bit more expensive but it can save you.

The health is a bit tricky and in the beginning I didn`t understood. So basically you have 3 health items.

3 Types of Health

Red: Scars and trauma
Blue: Health that drops over time
White: Health that is permanent

Bandages: When you take damage from full, you will lose health and you will get scars and trauma, the red bar. That red bar in your health is recoverable with bandages. are pretty cheap and with some good cards, you can get a ton of white health back and blue health.

Painkillers: Are good because you can use them instantly,recovers a lot of blue health.

Health Kits:This recovers a good part of white health but from what I saw not too much blue health, but Is still good to use this when you have money.

Useful items

Lockpicks are good because you can open doors where you can find goodies or open crates that have a minigun or flamethrower, so its pretty good.

The ammo pack is okays but you don`t really need it with some ammo management, like if you shoot for heads and with trigger discipline you will be fine without ammo pack.

Razor wire not too good. Slows down the zombie but not so much. I don`t recomment.

The stun is okay as well.

I think The lockpicks are the best.

So this is a small tutorial and what I thought about the game. Definitely buy it, because you will have fun, but buy it on a sale. Thank you for reading and don`t forget to check my YT channel for more reviews and full gameplays.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBack 4 Blood was supposed to be the current-gen successor to Left 4 Dead 2 that zombie slayers had been clamoring for for years. This idea was supported by the fact that Turtle Rock, (who previously worked for Valve on the L4D series before going independent), were the ones developing the game. It'd been a while since the zombie-game craze that plagued the late 2000's and I was ready to dive headfirst into a new horde shooter, but unfortunately, B4B was unable to really infect me.

I'll start on a positive note by saying this: Upon loading the game, I was VERY excited to be playing. The graphics were pretty good, the zombie models and variety were all creepy and visceral depictions of the typical zombies you expect to see, and some special zombies even had unique properties that could be denoted by subtle differences on their body. Visually, I couldn't complain at all; it looked great and far from the generic green/brown aesthetic that seemed all-too-prevalent in other, similar games.

After being pretty impressed by everything I was seeing, imagine my surprise when I found out the game wasn't actually very fun. The story missions had clear times between 3 and 13 minutes, and unfortunately, tended to err more on the short side than long. 10+ minutes isn't bad, but anything before that makes it very hard to actually get your bearings and figure out your objective, let alone care about any piece of it. I feel like you could simply combine some of the missions and that would vastly improve the experience. It just got jarring to play for a couple of minutes before seeing VICTORY pop up on the screen just to go through a couple of loading screens and do it again.

Keeping the previous point in mind, I want to talk about difficulty, because we did play on the easiest setting. This is because, when we tried the normal difficulty, we ended up having to redo some levels upwards of 3 or 4 times, which is absurd. This was particularly annoying depending on what level you failed, as the game doesn't save your campaign progress after each mission, but only after certain checkpoints. So if you beat 1-1 and 1-2, but die on 1-3, you need to start back at 1-1. After a few failures, we decided the game wasn't fun enough to justify trying to force our way through it. It's not like we aren't good at games either; we've all played competitive shooters with some degree of success, and have beaten famously hard games like Fromsoft's catalogue. This is all to say: normal difficulty should not be this cumbersome to go through.

B4B also employed a deck-building perk system which, while cool at a glance, is not explained in any meaningful way whatsoever, and we just ended up trying to cobble together a semblance of a build with what we were given. Luckily, I decided to go melee and it included some health/stamina regeneration, so I personally breezed through most of the game. I do attribute that more to luck than skill though, as I don't know when or how the perks are actually applied in a game, because they are not all present at the beginning of a campaign.

Honestly, I really didn't HATE Back 4 Blood, not by a long shot. I think there are some obvious improvements that I'd make, but in the absence of an actual Left 4 Dead sequel, you could do worse. At the end of the day, you're still carving up zombies with friends, which is truly what the zombie apocalypse is all about. B4B does demand quite a premium however at $60, so I wouldn't bother unless you can cop it for sub $20.
