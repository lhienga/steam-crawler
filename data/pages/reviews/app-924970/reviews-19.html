"Don't melee teammates!" the game says as I swing my bat at the horde of zombies only to miss and nail my allies right in the head.

Back 4 Blood is the closest people will get to Left 4 Dead 3, being named similarly and being developed by the studio responsible for the first L4D. As someone whose only actual experience with Left 4 Dead was playing an L4D2 demo on the Xbox 360, don't expect me to offer proper comparisons to Left 4 Dead in this review. I will solely be discussing the game itself, though I've seen enough L4D to have a basic understanding of it, so I'll make one basic comparison.

Gameplay
B4B follows the same basic formula as L4D; four people need to get from one saferoom to the other, fighting hordes of zombies along the way. To accomplish this, you have various weapons at your disposal, such as melee weapons, handguns, rifles, shotguns, LMGs and explosives, as well as tools such as medicine and traps.

One of B4B's main draws is the Card System, which is supposed to spice up playthroughs and enhance replayability. Player cards, which are both found in player-made decks and found in the environment, give effects, such as stat increases, new abilities, or unique passive effects that aren't exactly stat boosts. At the start of a campaign, each player can select a Cleaner to play as for the rest of that campaign, each of which also being their own cards with unique effects. The AI Director also has Corruption cards, designed mainly to beat you into a pulp and reward you if you avoid said beating.

The game offers four difficulties. I mainly play on Veteran, which is reasonably balanced most of the time but occasionally the game becomes HELLBENT on ending your campaign by spawning an insurmountable amount of enemies, knock you down, get someone to resurrect you, you come back at 20HP, you get knocked down, you get resurrected, and by the time the situation is sorted out you're at a point where the next time you go down you will need to wait for respawn.

The game has other modes as well, such as Swarm (PvP) and Trials of the Worm, but I have not played those, so I cannot offer insight.

Note that while this game is playable offline, you cannot pause at all, including offline mode. Keep that in mind.
