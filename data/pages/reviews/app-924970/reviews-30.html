\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBuy this game on sale when it's less than $10. $60 is just insanely overpriced with devs given up support and game still unpolished. Gave this game chance 3 times and it failed to deliver each time.

Pros: Good gun/shooting mechanics. Just buy when it's less than $10 and if you have friends to play with.

Cons:
- Game has too much RNG at every stage aspect making it hard to compare any runs. Too much RNG factors just ruins the game. At Nightmare/No Hope you cannot play a specific map unless you have done previous ones. At trial of Worms, you cannot choose a specific map and modifiers and just have to keep rolling dice. Too much RNG means winning or doing good is dependent on luck alone and not on strategy or skill. This is just gambling.
- Having bad luck with corruption cards will ruin your run and on harder difficulty have to replay the entire campaign of multiple hours again. Game adds too much RNG then forces you to use burn cards to compensate the bad corruption cards.
- Spawning in this game is atrocious possibly the worst of all games. Bosses, Ridden can spawn behind you giving absolute no reaction times. Ridden can respawn at areas you already cleared making the objective of killing useless. This game is just doing objectives as fast as possible and getting to saferoom rather than killing.
- No proper scoreboard. It's not a killing game despite the shooting mechanics being good.
- Nobody plays in public matches, even if you do find a game and join, your cards/perks will only be activated when they have done the map until then you will waste time playing as a bot with the bot cards.
- Game forces you to buy tons of skins just to unlock the other cards.
- Shooting range is incomplete, you cannot test your deck, stuns, etc.

The game has many flaws in the basic gameplay and QoL, this is why it failed and it has nothing to do with failing as a L4D successor. Even if we did not compare this with L4D, it is still a disappointment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played the beta of this game and didn't quite like it. I want to remember it having performance issues (which might be expected) but also that the gameplay wasn't that much and it was a bit convoluted. So I sort of wrote the game off at that point.

During the Steam Holiday Sale of 2023 I saw that the game was on sale and since it's a co-op game I picked up a copy for me and my friends so we could try it out. To my big surprise the game is actually really fun. It's been so long ago since the beta that I can't remember how it exactly played at the time but suffice to say I got a lot more enjoyment out of it now compared to then. And the game runs like butter.

The campaigns are also very meaty. In L4D you can run through a campaign in maybe 60 - 90 minutes. In this game, even though we first tried it out on easy, it took over 4 hours to complete the first campaign. And there are 6 total (or Acts as they are referred to in the game). Me and my friends look forward to continue playing this game.

I am glad that I eventually tried it out but in hindsight I should have given the game a chance much earlier.\t\t\t\t\t
