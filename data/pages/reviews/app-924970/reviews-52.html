\r\n\t\t\t\t\tZombie Brains Delight

Ingredients:

1 box of strawberry gelatin
2 cups boiling water
1 cup cold water
1 cup milk
1/2 cup condensed milk (for extra gooeyness)
Red food coloring
Gummy worms or other creepy gummy candies
Instructions:

Prepare the Zombie Brains Mold:

Find a brain-shaped mold (available at Halloween supply stores or online).
If you can't find a brain-shaped mold, get creative and use a regular mold or even a brain-shaped ice cube tray.
Mix the Gelatin:

In a large bowl, combine the strawberry gelatin with 2 cups of boiling water.
Stir until the gelatin is completely dissolved.
Add Cold Water and Milk:

Pour in 1 cup of cold water and 1 cup of milk.
Mix well.
Create the Zombie Goo:

Add a few drops of red food coloring to give the mixture a realistic brain color.
For an extra gooey texture, stir in 1/2 cup of condensed milk.
Pour into Mold:

Carefully pour the gelatin mixture into the brain-shaped mold.
Chill and Set:

Place the mold in the refrigerator and let it chill for at least 4 hours or until the gelatin is completely set.
Serve:

Once the zombie brains are set, remove the mold from the refrigerator.
Gently shake the mold to loosen the gelatin, and then turn it over onto a serving platter.
Garnish:

For an extra creepy touch, scatter gummy worms or other creepy gummy candies around the brain mold, as if the zombies left some bits behind.
Enjoy:

Invite your guests to dig into the deliciously gruesome Zombie Brains Delight. Encourage them to use their hands for an authentic zombie experience!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, I played the Alpha, and then the Beta. I was very disappointed, but they were literally for play-testing, so I didn't really hold it against the game. After the release, I wasn't going to pay 60 dollars for a game, so I waited for a discount, and I got a discount. 85%.

There are minor inconveniences that really add up to a full on disdain for the game. There's no window of recuperation after an incap revival. If the jumpy thing is pouncing at you as you're getting up, you're instantly destroyed since the game runs on a 2-strike system, which I do prefer to a 3-strike.

The pin/grab/pounce has no proper animation and feels really weird to witness. A pinned survivor literally does a 🧍‍♂️, and a pounced survivor freed goes straight from "on the ground" to "🧍‍♂️" in less than 0.5 seconds. It also is very frustrating that there is no reactive counterplay available to these other than strafing and getting lucky. I do emphasize reactive since you can just stay alert and shoot them ahead of time, but that's not really interactive.

I don't know why ogre even exists cause all he does is being fat.

I do like the visual aesthetic of the cleaners and the environment, but videogame doesn't run only on visuals.

Campaigns are very long, and it honestly feels like a mini game of The Division where you hobo around for a better equipment, except you lose everything at the end of the run and also not as fun.

While I like to stay hopeful in situations such as this, there is no hope since the developers officially announced discontinuing further support.
