\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPicked this up quite cheap along with the DLC. Really enjoy it playing with friends as there is a lot more things to take into account in comparison to Left for Dead.
For example as a team, taking into account the character's buffs that you are playing as and build a deck of 15 cards to increase your chances of success. Those 15 cards can honestly help you in sticky situations, especially if you run out of continues.

What is also fun is that the runs that you do are not the same because of the corruptions cards that can be pulled in each act, which then you need work against to complete the level ahead.

One drawback I find with this game is that there is a story but you only grab bits and pieces of it when the characters talk. This interactions can differ, depending on the specific characters you play (which does encourage you to play over and over again just to hear these interactions) and considering there can be a lot going on at one time, you can easily ignore these because the game can overwhelm you when you are doing well.

You can play solo with 3 bots, but it is of course more fun when you have a full team of human players. Also to note that it is cross play but we found issues with the ingame chat not working, so a workaround is using Discord.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely give game reviews. Let alone long-ish ones. But after having logged in hours of gameplay on B4B, I am in a better position now to give my thoughts. And yes, from the start I planned on giving my thoughts on this one, because, obviously this game tangents from L4D and L4D2 – games which I and everyone enjoyed thoroughly and up to now still play.

It’s a THUMBS UP for me. Not for reasons of sentimentality.

I can understand why a lot of people do not like it. My first thought – Confusing. Yes lots of mechanics, too busy, tons of stuff going on (what’s with the bloody cards anyways?) – felt like I have to read a thick manual to start enjoying it.

But guess what – START enjoying it – start shooting. Never mind the confusion, never mind the stupid cards, just start the campaign – even on veteran mode – and you’d be fine with what you have initially. The game is very generous. The characters are great (love Hoffman). The story is solid. The guns are Goooooood.

To my thinking, why expect sort of something like L4D and L4D2? Those are classics not meant to be touched anymore. They are perfect the way they are. If B4B went down the same-ish track, then everyone, will utterly, completely rubbish it. B4B would have not evolved.

So, my hat’s off to the developers. Good job. If I gave a thumbs down, it will not be for reasons of game abandonment or some other non-game related reasons. We review primarily for the quality of the game, be it good or bad. And for me, B4B is Good!\t\t\t\t\t

There's tons of bugs, and the difficulty scaling is poo poo. This game lacks polish and flow. The parts it does well are heavily borrowed from L4D2, and the parts that suck are, well, pretty much the rest.

I liked the weapon upgrade system, and the stamina mechanic is a much needed upgrade to the original L4D formula. One of the biggest flaws is that weapons and upgrades do not persist across acts. 3/5 - Not recommended when playing sober or alone.\t\t\t\t\t

But not at this price.

As much as I enjoy this game, I just cannot recommend it at this price, especially considering you need to pay extra for the Annual Pass or Deluxe Edition to get all the campaigns. At least wait for a sale. The Deluxe Edition should not cost more than half of a full price game, especially considering it's 2 years old now and has lower average player numbers than Killing Floor 2, a $30 - $60 (Ultimate Edition) horde shooter from 2016, ever had.

First off, the storytelling and character writing just isn't on the level of Left 4 Dead, so if you're here for this, you're not gonna be satisfied with Back 4 Blood.

Personally, I like Back 4 Blood more than Left 4 Dead from a point of pure gameplay feel.
L4D had better gore, sure, but the pure physical impact just didn't feel so good. Firing a rifle / pistol at a Zombie and not knocking them back may be realistic, but it's unsatisfying. I just prefer ragdolling them with gunshots and stumbling and knocking them around with melee weapons over how you just slice through them like a block of jello with near zero resistance in L4D or how gun impacts have no knockdown power and instead they play their death animation, that leaves you guessing whether or not they're dying or just injured.
L4D has the gore, but B4B, I feel, just has more weight in your attacks. The Addition of Aim Down Sights also lets you feel a guns recoil, as opposed to the "crosshair-gets-wider" random spread of hip-fire that's completely disconnected from the player's input.

I love how melee is much more viable, if not almost OP with how many cards you can put in your deck that allow you to resist damage and heal yourself and even your team in a 10m area with melee kills. I often get 500+ team healing at the end of a campaign while hardly using any healing items at all. There's also much better secondary guns than the puny pistols from L4D, with M93 Raffica and Full-auto Glock and Tec 9. Just nothing as unsatisfying as a perfectly aimed headshot from the L4D pistols failing to kill a basic Zombie.
One card even allows you to pack in two long guns

We need to talk about cards too. Your favorite or most hated feature, depending on whether you like complex skill customization or prefer to keep it simple.
The way you unlock them may not be so great, having to buy your way through multiple linear "Supply Lines" until you get to the stuff you want and having to look up online where the cards you want are hidden, but once you got the right cards, you can specialize your playstyle and you're gonna see some serious sh*t.
I run a tanky melee build that lets me cleave through a hundred common infected and sometimes come out with more HP than I went in, If I play it well and that's just something I threw together cause it looked good at first glance. If you like min-maxing and pushing the game, you can surely do some crazy sh*t.

And apart from your own starting deck, you find cards randomly in the level that cost you 500 copper to buy (alongside free basic stat upgrade cards and free team ability cards) so your power curve can go up even further. Guns come in increasingly stronger rarities as you progress, which you can find attachments for in increasingly stronger rarities as you progress. So you can not only customize it to your liking, whether you want to make it a powerful, scoped long range rifle or a mobile, low spread, high capacity quick-ADS short range rifle, it will also keep getting more powerful (or just handle better).
Yes, there's ADS now, so it's less high-mobility hipfire than L4D, but considering hipfire in L4D wasn't really that precise either, even moreso on the move, I prefer having an actually accurate zoom option, especially since special infected now have specific weak-spots that reward precise aim.

I just really enjoy B4B's constant cycle of getting stronger within a campaign. There's so much to find and even locked rooms that need toolkits to open (or alarmed doors that will call a horde if not disarmed) that can hide some good loot. It keeps you excited for finding better stuff and makes it more fun to explore, where L4D would just have a pipe bomb, some Pills, a Molotov or adrenaline syringe hidden occasionally, giving you more level to explore, but less reason to.
If you have a bit of a treasure hunter in you, that really tingles that part of your brain.

In the end, B4B it may not be the better game overall, but it does some things that might give it the edge for someone.\t\t\t\t\t
