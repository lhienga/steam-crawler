\r\n\t\t\t\t\t75/100 score I give to the overall good (but not too good) game, called Back 4 Blood. You have a wide variety of guns & tools to crush various Ridden. Game is really similar to Left 4 Dead, mostly in a good way. I was going to put a review even later in time, but since I see all maps doable on hardest Campaign difficulty (No Hope) without having to be a masochist, all maps doable on Nightmare even with the least useful Cleaner (Dan), well, the rest is more or less of a grinding 250 mission & Nightmare Acts with the rest of the Cleaners and the rest of the 1000 bosses. I have all achievements that I care about at a 92/93 right now and I am likely to get the remaining accomplishments that I care about - the coop&solo ones, for a 195/211 in total. Tbh, while I enjoy most enemies, the idea of putting those cultists is not good. If it was regular humans such as terrorists, bandits or whatever like that, there wouldn't be hundreds of hrs or a review from me in B4B - this game would be just an addition to my Ignore List. Ik it's personal, but not only. Still, they aren't humans, they are specials as they are worm-hybrid mutants and I kill them at will. The monstrous variants are a bit hilarious. The rest of my review is details.
\tThere are more than 50 missions, more than 40 weapons, more than 20 random accesories - grenades, medkits, bandages, traps, toolkits, stunguns, etc, lots of stuff, you are likely to spend 20+ hrs in the game having fun crushing Ridden. Still, I recommend going for Recruit at first to grab needed cards.
\tAbout the enemies, while dambzombies such as those in L4D are a fact, still they have hilarious & monstrous variations with worms and bile coming out of them, burning, fire-resistant ones, etc. Specials have interesting horryfic variations, e.g. the Sentinel monsters such as the Lobber, and the Hag Boss.
\tThere are lots of cards to unlock and enjoy - more than 190. Still, the least useful card in the game (Hell can Whatever) being the most expensive is a bit of silly. There are not any OP decks, don't get lied by anyone. Experiment, find out what is best for you and have fun building your card combinations.
\tWhile difficulty balance overall is not very good, at least I can call it relatively adequate, or fine, s.th. like a 62.5/100 score, while L4D doesn't deserve even a 50/100 overall in that relation. The online mode, however, has serious counterbalance. Bots have unlimited ammo, trauma immunity, give all money, some ammo, etc.
\tOn the question "Left 4 Dead or B4B", well, it really depends on the player. Back 4 Blood is more tactical, it isn't all just run & gun, you have to prepare cards, scavenge more, get needed slot items & weapons and I find it better balanced than L4D, things relatively adequately doable. On the other side, B4B doesn't have good modding options, does have some more AI inadequacy, some glitches and the most of all...those cultists that aren't as interesting as any other B4B or even any L4D enemy. I wouldn't say L4D is a bad game, but I happened to prefer B4B, it is also a good game, alright. Everyone has his choice and preferences.
