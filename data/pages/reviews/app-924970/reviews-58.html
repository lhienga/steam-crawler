\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverything except the graphics and the card deck system are straight-up downgrades from L4D2

You only got 4 Acts. You might feel there are up to 7 safe house checkpoint for each act, so there's gotta be a lot of map and area to explore, right...?

That's where things are rigged.

Each Act is using one big map, and each checkpoint are just constantly repeating on one big map with minor variation, and each checkpoint is also extremely short, about half (some are even shorter) than one checkpoint of L4D2, so in total, you actually have way less content than L4D2!

And the final boss fight is... trash. It feels like a chore, especially if you're playing solo (game is kinda dead), since bots are completely braindead when it comes to boss fight. They will either never shoot the weakpoint or they will attempt to shoot the weakpoint despite it's closed. The pathfinding in the last map is also horrible, bots will either commit-suicide or unable to help each other just because they'll keep running in circle.

Combat rhythm and game flow does not exist in this game. Because each checkpoint is short, you'll basically always be fighting until the next checkpoint, with no break whatsoever. And you know what's the side effect of it? Completely forgettable characters. In L4D2, there will be combat phase and there will be break phase, where folks will be waiting for something and having small talks. That's where you get to know the characters via their conversations. In this game, I can barely focus on character's voicelines cuz I'm fighting all the time, so at the end of the campaign, not a single one of them made me an impression, they're just people that shoot, bland and forgettable.

Also, this game has no survival mode, where you carefully design your defense and survive as long as possible. Do you want to know why? It's because the map design of this game is so bad and simple and bland that none of them are good for any sort of point defense. L4D2's map and map mechanic design, despite being linear, has a lot of creativity, uniqueness and space for exploration, while all maps in this game are just going from A to B, occationally waiting for something to open, with little to no side exploration potentials. It's just so simple and lazy, also forgettable.

Lastly, for anyone who's trying to say "stop comparing it to L4D2, it's not the same game", KEEP COPING. You know deep down in your heart that this game is the L4D2 wannabe, all the way through their name, their advertisement and their game mechanics. And from what I can tell, it's a completely failure. It also proofs that Valve are the true designer behind L4D2, not Turtle Rock, at least not the Turtle Rock that made this piece of junk.\t\t\t\t\t
