Even now I think that holds. B4B isn't as bad as you think it is. It's just an OK game, back then and right now.
B4B suffers from not really knowing what it wants to do, and not doing much of anything extremely well.
It wants to be a zombie shooter, it does that alright. It wants to be a deck builder. It wants to be a linear level game. Again, all of these are done ok.
Most of my fun was spent speedrunning and seeing how absurdly stupid you can make certain card builds. When you can get like +400% move speed and 300% use speed, its fun to see how far you go before you die. Or how much money you can accrue. But ultimately if you tried using the deck building system to play "as intended", it wasn't really that cool or fun or interesting.
The DLC's added some cool levels and weapons and stuff, but ultimately B4B still isn't worth it. Turtle Rock was trying for a bit, tooth and nail, to provide updates and content to make B4B noticed in the scene. But because it's launch and reception was lukewarm, it suffered a similar fate. To be kinda forgotten.
As far as we know, this is the final product. All DLC's are out and all updates are out. I cannot recommend this game. I would on sale, if you can get ALL content for ~30$, and can get 3 buddies who all would get drunk with you to play.

The different "corruption cards" (random modifiers for each game) make the game pretty replayable. The different stat builds you can make allows you to have different strengths (super health, melee, ranged, etc.) that can really change your playing experience (also adds to replayability based on what kind of combat you want). Finding matches takes a while, especially if you have crossplay disabled, but if you have friends to play it with, its a lot of fun. Overall, I would definitely recommend to those who play solo or with friends.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTries to be Left 4 Dead, but fails in just about every way possible. There are many characters, but they're all bland and don't really distinguish themselves in terms of personality (unlike, say, Ellis, Nick, and Coach in L4D2). Instead, they make lots of one-liners which I found to be annoying and grating. The "card" and augment systems makes everything overly complicated (one of L4D's strengths is its simplicity). Finally, the developers don't seem to understand that there's a "happy medium" between no zombies ("ridden") and hordes. Either nothing is happening or you're getting swarmed.

All in all, it's not terrible, but it's not a good game. I may give it another shot at some point in time, but if I don't, I won't miss it.\t\t\t\t\t
