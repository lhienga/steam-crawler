\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreetings! Today we’ll be looking at “Back 4 Blood” a co-op level based FPS that is the closest thing we have to a spiritual successor of Left 4 Dead. This genre and theme have been a bit saturated as of late and I wasn’t completely blown away by the beta years ago but a friend talked me into grabbing it for co-op. So what did I think? Let’s discuss.

Wanna Play a Zombie Game: We’ve all seen cooperative FPS shooters, nothing special here and we’ve all see zombie games. We’ve even seen this done a few times in tandem, what makes this game a bit more unique is the itemization and deck system. Items have a level and attachments meaning that you are constantly looking for a better weapon and better attachments which mean you are also looking for money to buy upgrades at the beginning of the next level. Additionally you also acquire cards that you place in a deck to unlock as you progress through the levels as well.

Spiritually Dead: At the core, this game is just Left 4 Dead with more characters, and a few RPG elements. I’m not necessarily saying this as a negative thing but more as something everyone should be mindful. The audio is decent, so are the graphics and music, nothing is terrible. The banter between the characters can be amusing at the beginning but ultimately I’m left with a ‘meh’ feeling unless I’m playing with friends; which is the big issue with this game.

It’s dangerous to go alone: Boredom is the greatest threat in this game, specifically solo. This is a great example of a game that is completely amazing or a waste of time by the people who play with. There isn’t much to complain about bug wise and the game plays fairly stable but if you aren’t playing with a group of people who are playing the way you’d like this game can be boring at best and downright toxic at worst. The community has heavily thinned and what is left seems to be people grinding for…well, I’m not sure but they definitely know the levels and honestly make the levels a bit unfun to play, so I just played solo with Bots. Bots who are simultaneously Rambo and incompetent, sometimes you can tell them to pick up bags, open doors or such but other times you can’t and if it is objective based you are the one who picks it up. Creating a very ‘bleh’ experience that just has you hoping to get to the end of the level as fast as possible.

Overall this is a decent game, if you are playing it cooperatively. Solo it is one of the most boring slogs I’ve had in a long time and with that said I really can’t recommend it unless you have a few friends to play with. The game core is there, but it’s old and the community has thinned out and completed the content leaving the ‘oo shiney’ phase of the game long gone. Ultimately this game sat in my library for a few weeks waiting for friends to play and I just sat down and grinded the story with little enjoyment. So… I’d pass on it and just jump on the next co-op shooter that comes out. Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t
