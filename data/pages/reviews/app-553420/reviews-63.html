\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReluctant recommend but objectively this game is good. My biggest problem is the combat and souls-like nature. Exploring and finding secrets is a hardcore chore when you have an army of respawning trash everywhere. Obviously i can just get good, but therein lies the problem.

I don't want to get good at this game, i just wanna discover stuff.

Thankfully you can enable god mode in the options otherwise i probably would've stopped playing after getting the first major plot item. Boss fights were pretty fun though, did those normally.


If you're like me and are intrigued because it's somewhat commonly mentioned along with Outer Wilds:

It's not really anywhere near similar. I went in expecting a similar experience mystery/plot/exploration wise and was hardcore disappointed. In Outer Wilds all rewards for exploration are intrinsic, Here they are the opposite. Clues are given in the manual you get which CAN be reminiscent of Echoes of the Eye at times but the difference is the secrets you find are largely unrelated to the plot. They dont push you towards closure or a resolution, they all just feel like boxes you tick until you get 100% and are rewarded with the true ending of the game.

The secrets dont actually build towards this ending, theyre just secrets for the sake of being secrets. It's not a bad thing, it's just not what I wanted personally. (Note that i didn't actually do most of them, just went and read up on it.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't recommend this game, but that doesn't mean I think it is bad. The first half of the game is incredible. The combat is fun, the bosses are tough, and the music/visuals are great. Where I think the game loses steam is in the last half. To keep this spoiler free, I don't think the game does enough to encourage the use of the D-pad. It's a feature almost exclusively used for secrets and not really for anything in the normal gameplay loop. The most obvious use of the D-pad is for opening doors, but it was easy for me to think that opening doors was the only use for the D-pad. I'm sure someone could say "skill issue" or whatever, but a game like The Witness does a better job of forcing me into situations where I have to think about using everything available to me. If you aren't actively looking to try to find secrets, the D-pad is rarely used and you'll finish the game wondering if that was everything, which is really a shame because the developers did an amazing job with the secrets in the game.\t\t\t\t\t

Amazing World, Story and Gameplay to Figure Out

There isn't a single line of dialog and only a few understandable printed words, but the game says volumes. It wants you to figure out its secrets, and working to figure them out was joyful.
  • Lovingly crafted world, story, gameplay, visuals, sounds and software (was bug free for me)
  • IMHO don't look up spoilers until you are ready to stop playing
  • Secrets come in all levels of difficulty, but some are so complicated, I didn't mind missing them
  • It pays to use items, but items used on a boss are (mostly) lost if you die
If you enjoy figuring things out, buy this game now. If you are unsure or worried about complexity, grab it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
