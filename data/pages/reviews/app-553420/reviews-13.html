\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was a joy to play through. Exploration is the main course and Tunic delivers with plenty of secrets, beautiful environments, and a rich soundtrack. Collecting pieces of an in-game manual is one of the most unique mechanics I've seen lately in an indie game and it fits well with the Zelda-like gameplay. Artists really outdid themselves with the pages- I felt like I was reading an old SNES manual.

Boss fights were tough but the real difficulty comes from the puzzles. I'll admit, I'm not a huge puzzle person. I typically get stuck and this game was no different. I got about 80% of the way through most of the secrets required for the good ending before I had to use a guide. The game does a good job giving you clues and the sense of discovery once you realize what's been under your nose the whole time is palpable.

If you like old-school zelda and want to experience a sense of wonder while exploring some old, abandoned spaces, check out Tunic. It is a spark of joy.\t\t\t\t\t

If i had to COMPARE this delightful game to THREE other games, I would say its a mix between Dark Souls, Zelda and well....i cant think of a third...This game is really its own.
