\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI not only recommend Tunic but also think you should play it with almost no knowledge of it. It’s just better when you go in with not much of anything. Tunic is an action adventure game where you play as a fox guy exploring some islands, finding treasures and solving puzzles.

\tThe Exploration in this game is bordering on being too good. You will find new and interesting things through the entire game and some of the stuff is hidden so well in plain sight you will be kicking yourself when you notice it or at least I was. The exploration also allows for a completely different play through once you get into replays and/or new game plus.

\tPuzzle wise this game stands above any others I have played, which is not much I will add but they are still amazing. I have not even solved all of them in the game sense; many are just side exploration stuff that you don't need to win.

\tA big part of the game is the game manual which you literally find the pages of in game as you explore and solve puzzles. The book is mostly written in a language unique to the game but still gives info throughout that allows you to move forward on your journey. Also though I have not done it, the language is functional and can be learned and deciphered into English which is just awesome.

\tTunic is a game I honestly think most people should play especially if you enjoy the older legend of zelda games or just adventure/puzzle games in general.

Thanks for reading if you did and I hope you play Tunic.\t\t\t\t\t
