\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery beautiful but very inconsistent.
You can fight the same enemy doing the same patterns and get different results. Sometimes you parry and they're stunned. Sometimes you parry but it's ignored and their attack still reaches you. Sometimes you dash through the enemy and can backstab, other times your dash is blocked by that enemy's knee, other times you dash in their back but their attack gets instantly redirected to you anyway so their sprite gets flipped 180° before the end of your dash. Sometimes enemies are stunned from just a basic attack for no reason. Some enemies can dash backwards + lunge forward and hit during a single attack animation of the player. It doesn't happen frequently but when it does you have no way to dodge it. Movement is extremely clunky with the teleport dash because frequently there's only 1 tile to teleport to and it's not in one of the 8 targetable directions. Most of the game is searching for 1-tile gaps, hidden paths to chests , which is ok when we see a chest and look for a path, but it becomes ridiculous when the chest is hidden and the path is hardly noticeable so we have no clue there even exists something to search for, AND hidden paths to progessing the story, which is ridiculous especially for the west hero grave where the game tries its best to convince you that there is no way to go west anymore at that moment. Some walls have to be bombed but there's no visual hint about it. Frequently the access to a later zone is denied (even though it wouldn't be a big deal to allow the player to access it early) but instead of making it clear and blocking the player early the game lets you wander in and bump your head into every wall until you finally give up and try another zone. Some musics are way too close to zelda musics. The lore is very messy, going in all directions. There's an infinite production of power supplies going on but actually just a few dozens are needed to power the entire island. It's trying to bring a very industrial explanation to the global divine atmosphere of magic in the game. Wood objects like planks and ladders disappear during nights and reappear during days. Several items are barely useful and not relevant to the story, obtained at moments that feel very random.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed the game well enough and would recommend it (even more so if it is on sale), but there are certain elements that didn't work as well as others and I think it somewhat falls apart towards the end due to some pacing issues. Tunic managed to fully captivate me in the first half though. Exploring the map and figuring out how the game works was constantly fun and I really liked the minimalist but charming art style. Unfortunately as time went on I noticed that the combat was solid, but not outstanding and that the enemy variety overall felt lacking, because the enemies felt somewhat bland and similar after a while. I think it works well enough and expecting more might not be fair considering the scope of the game and the fact that it was a solo dev project, but I can see how somebody who is less interested in puzzles and exploration might not find as much enjoyment in the game. Speaking of puzzles and exploration, I think the game for the most part knocked it out of the park, but some of the more complicated puzzles fell flat for me, because in my opinion the clues were sometimes too vague and not properly communicated. On a few occasions I was expecting to find the solution in a different way than actually intended or wondering if I was missing some crucial item when I wasn't. It's not exactly moon logic territory, but in a few spots it came extremely close to it. The boss fights were also not that great, but not terrible either and at least I can say that I liked the ideas that the dev had for them, even if the execution wasn't always up to par. It is incredible how much this developer was able to build on his own and I can only compliment him on this incredible achievement. Even if I did not love the game as much as I expected to at the start I am still interested in seeing what project might come next.\t\t\t\t\t
