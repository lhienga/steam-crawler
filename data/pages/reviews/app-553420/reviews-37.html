\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat atmosphere. Great music. Great graphics. I love the guide book being a part of the game. It's all excellent.

Until you actually have to do it. The combat is terrible. 75% of the problems with the combat engine is the targeting system. The game really seems to want to target something 30 feet away that isn't going to attack me over the large hulking monstrosity that's 4 feet in front of me an actively trying to kill me. The game also auto targets... sometimes. Not all the time. Just sometimes. So when I'm running away from some enemies to give me some room to pop a quick heal, I may just end up turning 180 degrees and running face first into the bad guys because the game has decided that's what I need to be doing.

When I'm not dealing with trying to run away from enemies, I'm instead dealing with the other 25% which is enemies running away from me. You see, we're a tiny little fox with a tiny little sword and tiny little legs. We are not fast. We do not have range. We can't reach anything. But just about half the enemies in the game including bosses, move like cheetahs, get in, wreck your face, and get out before you can reach them. So you either spend most of your time chasing after enemies - bosses or not - or trying to block/evade without getting yourself too far away to actually hit the damned thing.

It really is a shame that it's such a good game and so not fun to play. I've played other games with similar styles and they eventually grew on me and I was able to enjoy it. Tunic, unfortunately, isn't doing that. I'm 3/4 through the game and the awkward controls, the poor targeting, and out little fox friend being unable to get anything from the top shelf really puts a damper on the enjoyment factor for me.\t\t\t\t\t
