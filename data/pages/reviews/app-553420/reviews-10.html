\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTUNIC is an absolute delight of an experience. Every single pixel is not just a heartfelt love letter to the earlier Zelda games, but also a strong homage that general era of gaming. The use of a game manual (that looks and FEELS like a printed piece of paper, even if it's just an in-game render) is a FANTASTIC mechanic that slowly drips information to the player in a way that no other game does.

I can see people getting stuck and/or frustrated with the game, and I honestly think there's no shame in looking up a guide if you're in that camp. But the feeling of finally figuring out an obscure instruction in the manual, and the way the game world opens up more and more with every discovery is amazing.

Definitely worth getting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTUNIC is beautiful and deceptively complex game that was fantastically presented in the early/mid game, but tends to fall flat in the later section due to a lack of combat and an overabundance of puzzles.

The combat was personally challenging when I first tried the game, but on my second playthrough, I found it a little more bearable when using all the tools available to me. There's also a reduced difficulty option for those who don't have time to throw themselves at bosses over and over.
Beautiful atmospheres, lots of secrets to find, a surprisingly complex language that was a lovely challenge to decipher (and only truly necessary to complete optional secrets).
Overall a charming game that happens to have a few questionable choices bringing it down a notch or two.

Definitely worth checking out on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

Summary
Tunic is a Zelda-esque souls like with complex puzzles and a charming atmosphere.

Achievement hunting
This game is incredibly tough to 100% if you don't use guides, and I would highly suggest you use one for the later puzzles as some get incredibly complex.

Pros/cons
Pros
-Charming
-Various routes to use/sequence breaking
-Great for puzzle enthusiasts

Cons
-Incredibly unforgiving at certain points; e.g. the quarry, cathedral...
-If you miss a page in the guide book you'll be left in the dark about very important game mechanics
-Missable achievements like the piggy banks require an almost full run to acquire if missed

Opinion
Tunic is an incredibly aesthetically pleasing game with an incredibly hard game at its core.
It has various weapons and items which are enjoyable to use, but in limited supply usually. The limited amount of mana makes magic weapons feel quite shoddy at times imo, as well as some just being far better than others (gun op).
Whilst I enjoyed the game, I can't see through some of its faults, and I will say it clearly has rough edges. This in various forms such as weird checkpoint placement, AI being unnecessarily aggressive (you will be chased no matter what), and limited directional and map indication. As a metroidvania I find this game to be quite lacking as there's quite few game changing items, and all the stat buffs are in normal (easily missable) chests.

In terms of speedrunning this game is amazing, I can't deny that. But for a casual player it can be confusing and frustrating.
More extensive methods of traversal earlier on would've been nice as the fast travel is quite limited (and a tad slow) compared to what is usually given. Also having no signage or naming on fast travel points makes it quite confusing which is which and will oftentimes end up wasting your time as you try to find the correct one.
Also the night is a nice addition, but limiting the areas one can visit is quite annoying for post story gameplay.

tldr;
Difficult puzzles, mediocre combat.

Should you buy it?
If you really want to delve into it, go ahead. But personally I'd recommend against it as it's not at all what I expected/wanted out of this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a very good game. I think its called a knowledge based game. You don't know what you don't know till you figure out you could always do that. The puzzles are good and feel great to figure out. The answers are all pretty much given to you, it just might not seem obvious to you at the moment. Throughout the game you will pick up pages from an instruction booklet you would find when you bought a game back in the day, Sometimes it will be a map of the area, or the knowledge that there is something that you can do like running. You could always run, but you don't "unlock" it till you get the page that tells you how to run. It is a fun game that requires some thinking to get through.\t\t\t\t\t
