\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRating: *10/10*

Current tags: Exploration, Puzzle, Souls-like, Cute, Difficult

As many have described it, Tunic is a Zelda meets Souls masterpiece. I highly recommend diving in with a blind approach, letting yourself get lost in its world. The game is a treasure trove of puzzles subtly integrated, which, when solved, gives you that big brain feeling.

The manual is feed to you page by page but it is filled with so much information so be sure to pay attention!

The story is engaging, but I'll leave it at that since anymore would be spoiling the adventure.

The combat is very much like a souls game in the way you interact with it but not in the way it punishes you. From dodging and blocking to utilizing magic, consumables, and passives which of course all of it is *clearly* explained in the manual.

The puzzles are anything but straight-forward and I love it. This game really hits that unknown magic factor that I miss in many other games that just dumpster everything on the player. It feels akin to reliving the experience of playing my first game.

Well worth the price. Playtime ranges from 10-20 hours depending on play-style.\t\t\t\t\t
