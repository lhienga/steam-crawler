\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow, I'll be honest. I did not get through this game without help. However I got through a very large portion of it without help and tried my hardest to do so. This really is a game where you are doing yourself a disservice if you don't try your hardest to get through on your own. Part of makes this game so incredible is how detailed and creative the puzzles are. Once you finally start to figure things out the game really opens up and it really gives a classic feel to it. I do have to warn however that it really isn't your average puzzle game where you're just gonna get to a point where you can breeze through the puzzles. It's like next level hard on the puzzle/cryptic front. Which for me provided one of the best gaming experiences of recent years, but I know for others will be a big turn off. The combat is quite fun too, it's pretty challenging, but compared to the puzzles it's a cakewalk. The music is also top notch and I'm a big fan of the art/world design. I'm excited to see what the developers do next!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like finding secrets, TUNIC is a great way to make you feel like a genius. The game has a lot of knowledge based progression, which makes for a ton of possibilities. The Instruction Booklet is TUNIC's guide, map, and the key to these secrets. You collect pages of the book as you progress and any bit of information in it can have meaning, definitely play blind if you can, because putting the clues together and finding the secrets feels so rewarding. In addition, these secrets aren't "unlocked" by the pages, you can access them just by knowing about them, so if you play through again your route could be completely different, in fact the developers left some more unintentional secrets in the game just to encourage speedrunning or finding these obscure routes. I think that's really cool, and makes TUNIC all the more compelling beyond it just being a great game in the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
