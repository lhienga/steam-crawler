\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTunic wants to be a game about exploration- but it is coated with invisible walls. You don't choose where you go.
Tunic wants to be an adventure, filled with combat. The combat is overly simple and often janky.
Tunic wants to be a puzzle game. Maybe it has puzzles later, but I didn't see a single one.
Tunic wants you to engage with its cool little in-game guidebook. But chances are you'll figure out what the book is trying to tell you retroactively, after you've already done the thing.

Tunic wants to be a lot of things, but it isn't willing to actually be about any of them. if you want mediocre combat, exploration, and puzzles wrapped into one, then this is for you. But it's not particularly good at anything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe only games I remember from E3 2018 are Cyberpunk 2077 and Tunic, and this very much lived up to my expectations. Having to gradually find the game manual in order to learn how to play the game is a very interesting mechanic, one that scratches my itch for games like Outer Wilds and other knowledge-based-progression games.

A good chunk of the game is fairly linear (even if it feels like you can go wherever you want), so there's rarely a feeling of being lost. There's nuggets of information that will drag you along, and context clues that will make you think and discover solutions on your own.

Some of the late game puzzles are very creative, and would only really work in a game like this, and includes one of my favorite puzzles in gaming. That being said, if you're not keen on the idea of these more difficult puzzles, you can beat the game without doing any of the big-brain puzzles\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of: I am not a furry. I swear, I am not identifying as this fox character..

..However, if i did consider myself a furry.. ....and even if I was identifying as the fox... ....this masterpiece would still be an absolute 10/10!

The game looks cute and childish, but do not let that deceive you - stumbling into the 'Dark Tomb' for the first time without the lantern is spooky and the lore of the game is kinda messed up! If I was a furry I would probably also tell you that the game strikes a perfect balance between open-world and preplanned experience, where the map usually opens up in digestible steps, but you can also always choose to do things in your own order and the game will make sure to make you feel it when you are entering territory where you really are not supposed to be (yet). The world contains close to no filler space, so exploring felt really rewarding. There comes the point where the sudden realization kicks in that everything, everything in this game world is part of some kind of puzzle, and that every little thing has purpose. The game wants you to decrypt a whole fantasy language and if you want to 100% understand the game, it even wants you to translate a whole book. I didn't really care for the fox character, since I'm not a furry, but as you can guess from the trailer, the game also looks gorgeous, some landsights even stunning! There is not a huge quantity of bosses (sadly), but the boss fights are really fun (e.g. you fight a gigantic golem that shoots a fat beam from it's face; no one can hate that) and they are pretty different from one another! If you want to solve the final puzzle, you will have to do some Sherlock Holmes type ♥♥♥♥ and really, really think outside the boundaries of a game..

My final verdict is:

It was perfect. P e r f e c t . Everything....Down to the last minute details.\t\t\t\t\t
