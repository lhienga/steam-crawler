\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the best game in my Steam library. It's the best gaming experience I've had in literally over 20 years. My top experiences being mostly in the 90s - Zelda: Link's Awakening, Zelda: Ocarina of Time, and Final Fantasy VII. The game's aesthetics are simple yet gorgeous; the music/sound-effects are stellar. S-tier indie studio game that is better than most AAA titles. Is it perfect? No, it has flaws for sure. But it was a pleasure to play through and was a fun challenge. Highly, highly recommend this.

The gameplay is a mix between a chill, low-key Zelda-like dungeon puzzler and a hell-hole, get-yo-ass beat souls-like boss grind. To be frank, the difficultly of the combat can be a bit frustrating at times - that is my major complaint. But I don't really play souls-like games and this level of pain and difficulty will be enjoyed by many. The only reason I got past some of the boss fights was because I figured out a strategy to abuse some gaps in the AI...

Rather, I vibed with the more Zelda-like exploration, puzzler, and dungeon crawler aspects of the game. I did find the cryptic, figure-it-out-yourself design quite fun and refreshing. Flipping through the manual and putting the pieces of the puzzle together of where I had to go, when I had to go, what items I needed, etc. Very satisfying. I highly recommend you do NOT use a guide for your first play through - at all. Good luck!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI adore this game, to me it was a perfect mix of challenging combat, a unique premise, and fun puzzles that are very satisfying to solve (for the most part).

Late in the game there are some somewhat annoying fairy puzzles that were less about brainpower and more about patience. However, the solutions can always be looked up if you feel that figuring out the puzzle is the fun part and painstakingly jotting down d-pad directions in a separate notebook is not.

My advice for anyone trying to enjoy this game as spoiler free as possible is to just take a closer look at the manual when you're stuck. You might've missed something the first time or only understood half of the secret to a certain mechanic.\t\t\t\t\t
