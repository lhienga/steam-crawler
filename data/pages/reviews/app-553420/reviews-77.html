\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe central mechanic of discovering bits and pieces of the rulebook and figuring out how to even play the game is delightful. The art-style is immensely cute and has a very pleasant stylized feel to it. The puzzles are delightful, although I personally found the combat less fun. There are a fair amount of weapons to use, but I ended sticking with just a few through the whole playthrough. It was slightly too difficult for me (very bad at video games) and I wish there were a few more options to adjust the difficulty of the game in the settings. One area in particular (the mountains) was a complete slog for me, and I ended up having to turn on invincibility a few times. Anyone who plays a significant amount of video games will likely find it much easier than I did. Still recommend wholeheartedly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately, a beautiful game with some seemingly rich lore marred by its extreme difficulty and pacing issues. I was not expecting such bulls*** combat from this game.

Good:
* The art style & graphics are great
* Music is great
* The store/lore is good

Bad:
* If you don't pay attention to the "manual" you collect then you may miss crucial game mechanics.
* Lots of wandering and trying to figure out where to go
* Unmoveable camera is used to obscure paths you need to find
* Very difficult. I liked Hollow Knight but this is harder and the main character just feels slow during later boss fights.
* I did not like the stamina system as I feel it contributed to the overall slowdown of combat and punishes you for blocking and dodging.

Ugly:
* Enemies gang up on you, will notify other nearby enemies, and they will all chase you forever
* There are limited ranged weapons so most combat is a slog as you chip away at their health, dash back, wait for an opening, and repeat
* There is a "no damage" mode, so the developers acknowledge the BS difficulty, but you still get knocked around if you get hit so it's still a slog trying to get through an area.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHEY ! LIST-... READ !
-----

This game is an absolute GEM. Do you love secrets ? Everybody loves secrets !

Unless someday you become amnesic (which... I hope won't happen), this one will be added in your list of : "oh man, if only I could forget about this just to do it all over again !".

No way it doesn't take you back. Let me picture you this : you're 12, math class, boring, summer around the corner, afternoon, back from lunch, and the only thing you can think about to not fall asleep (aside from the cute girl / boy sitting nearby) is that game you are playing and trying to figure out... and then it hits you ! You may have just found a solution to one of the game's puzzle or mechanic ! God... now you can't wait to get back home to test if it works ! Yeah... that. That was awesome. This game does that.

It will not hold your hand, yet it comes with a warm blanket full of nostalgia (but with question marks printed all over it). DO NOT look for answers online ! Go in totally blind. EVERYTHING you need to know is in the game itself. And I can only encourage you to figure out the symbols you'll see for yourself. That alone is another +10/15 hours of failures and successes until you master it.

Currently putting this as my #2 favorite indie game of all time right behind Hollow Knight and now just before Celeste. It took things from Zelda, it took things from FEZ, it took things from Monument Valley, and it also took things from any 90's gamer kid's memories. And for this, it took a special place in my heart.

-----
Thank you Andrew for attracting such a good crew around yourself and for making a game that looks, sounds and plays amazing.\t\t\t\t\t

So, Tunic is a... Have I spoiled anything yet? Did you know the title? Ok, just needed to make sure. It's a rather spoilerific game, isn't it? I don't mean to say there's a lot to spoil, I'm keeping that ambiguous as to not spoil anything, but rather that this game is over the minute you've fully "figured it out". And if a game that needs some figuring out seems fun to you, this is absolutely the game for that. If you aren't interested, know that Tunic still has a fairly large, well designed world that's fun to romp around in, and the joys of progression and understanding will still come to you even without the extra effort, though in lesser doses.

That's really the crux of Tunic. Your mileage will vary depending on how much you want to put in. If you want to really sink your teeth into this game and explore every corner, you can. If you want to go beyond that and search for deeper secrets in the game's code or in its most hidden places, you can. But what makes me recommend this game is the fact that if you don't really care, if you just drift through the game's progression and play for the combat and levels, you'll still have a good time.\t\t\t\t\t

Absolutely and unequivocally the greatest game i've ever played. you may think to yourself while reading (if you are) "that has to be a lie, there are so many other great games." although true, this game holds dear to me for these reasons: Firstly, The graphics are stunning and are low poly, i suppose. Towards the ending i started crying, even though i knew damn well i could replay the game as many times as i wanted. I'm at a loss of words to attempt to describe my feelings of the game, but i thoroughly enjoyed it, so did my friends that watched.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTunic seemed to be more geared towards exploration than combat, puzzles rather than skill. In this regard, it succeeds, with some puzzles only found by the most meticulous of players. I found myself stuck at certain points in the game, without any idea where I was meant to backtrack, which at times was frustrating.
Despite the game being puzzle and exploration focused, I really enjoyed the boss fights. The combat mechanics does not have the snappiness of modern exploration/combat games, but you can eventually get used to the delays between certain combat skills.
Overall, Tunic was an enjoyable experience, and I would recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
