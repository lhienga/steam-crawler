\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTunic is an absolute gem of a game that effortlessly captures the essence of the NES era while taking inspiration from timeless classics like Link's Awakening. As a lover of both retro and puzzle-filled adventures, Tunic struck all the right chords and left me grinning from ear to ear throughout my entire playthrough.

One of the first things that drew me into Tunic was its captivating art style. The adorable protagonist, a tiny fox exploring a vast and mysterious world, is a perfect embodiment of cuteness. The vibrant colors and attention to detail truly brought the game to life, immersing me in a whimsical experience reminiscent of beloved NES titles.

Speaking of which, Tunic successfully channels the spirit of those nostalgic NES games that captured our hearts decades ago. From the tight controls to the challenging puzzles and exploration, it's a joyous trip down memory lane. I couldn't help but feel a rush of nostalgia as I embarked on epic quests, encountered quirky characters, and delved into ancient dungeons.

The puzzles in Tunic are both ingenious and satisfying. Each area is cleverly designed, presenting intricate challenges that require keen observation and logical thinking to overcome. Whether it's deciphering cryptic symbols or manipulating objects to unlock secret passages, Tunic keeps you on your toes and rewards your perseverance with a genuine sense of accomplishment.

The world of Tunic is a wondrous playground filled with hidden secrets waiting to be discovered. Exploring every nook and cranny feels like embarking on an epic adventure, as the game gradually unfolds its rich lore and presents you with captivating stories. Every encounter and interaction with the charming inhabitants adds another layer of depth to the experience, leaving you hungry for more.

What truly sets Tunic apart is its ability to combine nostalgia with modern gameplay elements. While it pays homage to the classics, it also introduces fresh mechanics and surprises that keep the gameplay engaging and exciting. The balance between familiarity and innovation struck a perfect chord, creating an experience that appeals to both seasoned gamers and newcomers alike.

In conclusion, Tunic is an enchanting game that effortlessly captures the essence of NES classics and draws inspiration from beloved titles like Link's Awakening. With its delightful art style, challenging puzzles, and heartwarming world, it's a true treasure for anyone seeking a nostalgic and adorable gaming experience. Don't miss out on this delightful adventure—Tunic is an absolute must-play for fans of retro gaming and puzzle-solving enthusiasts alike!\t\t\t\t\t

Overall – Tunic is a great throwback to games like The Legend of Zelda: A Link To The Past and Brave Fencer Musashi. The combat feels very solid, the exploration is fun and rewarding and the puzzles are unique. I really enjoyed my time with Tunic and feel it’s one of the better throwback style games recently released. Highly Recommended.
