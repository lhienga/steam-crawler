\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, just, wow. The visuals, the story, the mechanics, the soundtrack. Tunic is so beautiful and polished. The developers managed to do something to me that I haven't experienced in over 20 years. You are a charming little fox character in a deceptively difficult game that makes you feel like a kid again. Tunic is just the right amount of cute, has just the right amount of drip-fed game mechanics, just the right amount of tiny bits of story to transport you back in time and slap you in the face with nostalgia of an era when things were more difficult to understand, back when games came with a little book, that told you what the mechanics were, and your older brother handed it to you to decipher while he tried to figure out how to play the game without reading it. Except you're playing the role of both you and your brother. Idk how else to explain it! Play this game. If you grew up playing games in the 80s 90s 2000s play this game.

disclaimer: Tunic does have a level of difficulty to it, I do highly recommend it for an experienced gamer chasing that childhood high. However, if you are a casual gamer who likes laid-back story driven games, this may not be your vibe.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally fun Zelda clone with a light Soulslike twist and a creative navigation system. As somebody who grew up with the overhead Zeldas and despised BOTW, this game was a breath of fresh air.

The first 3/4ths of the game are really well-paced. Not too hard to find cool stuff as long as you keep looking for manual pages and look at them. My main gripe is that the endgame and true ending questline are too cryptic. I backed off after the normal ending when I realized there wouldn't be any new bosses.

I also wish there were a proper way of tracking unclaimed power ups in the postgame. I reached a point where I would've just looked up everything because I was tired of going in circles.

Regardless, the game is well worth a playthrough at least for the normal ending.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTunic is an incredible game that plays well, is very fair in its difficulty, and crams a bunch of puzzles basically everywhere that you'll never see until you know to look for them. In this way its kind of a combination of Outer Wilds and even The Witness as much as it is the more obvious Zelda and Dark Souls.

I can't overstate enough how well balanced the normal difficulty is. Every occasion I got wholloped in a new encounter and thought that maybe I'd hit a difficulty cliff that would be more frustrating than enjoyable to climb, I ended up working out a way to mitigate most of the problems with it and learn my way through it in short order.

I was constantly thrilled by the discoveries the game leads you into, and spent a lot of time... probably too much time trying to work out all the puzzles myself. I will say that I did get *very* far, completing the Golden Path, finding most of the hidden treasures, and all but one of the faries (bombs were my "everyone always forgets about cryonis") without any assistance. Doing the first of those was pretty great, and the last was alright too. But at this point I'd spent so much time trying to unravel everything that I just wasn't up for decoding the runes and whatever is going on on top of that special rune tower place not to mention cleaning up the remaining few hidden treasures, so I just went to the end.

...and it was pretty anticlimactic. I guess I expected more from taking the Golden Path, but it is what it is I suppose. The only real shame of it is that, having come this far, I can't take my rightful place instead without running through the game again.

So yeah. Really incredible game, but maybe leans a bit too hard into the secrets at the end, if you choose to engage with them. I only spoiled myself on two things that were really bothering me (the aforementioned bomb-related fairy, and the star message, but it remains to be seen if I will crack the runes myself.

...that's a lot of spoiler text. Game is good, just buy it I guess.

Addendum: After working on translating the runes for a while I managed to get the base concept only to stumble significantly because, as I found out looking at more and more hints in frustration, the cipher is phonetic, not alphabetic! which is frankly completely time-wastingly insane. Reason: Is there a language on the planet earth with more variation in pronunciation than English? And yet we're expected to decipher a phonetic script for it based mostly on ambiguous context clues!? And then you put a line through the middle to obfuscate further!!?. Frankly, that's infuriating. Not because it is a difficult puzzle, or obtuse, both of which it is, but because basically none of the games other puzzles are anywhere near as difficult and obtuse, so there's just a giant difficulty cliff on one of the game's final mysteries for no readily apparent reason other than to amuse people who had oodles of hours and interest enough to spend on it. Which, again, would be kinda cool *if the game didn't lead the player to believe it would be as straight-forward as every other puzzle*.

Honestly, I briefly considered changing my recommendation based on that. However, because all that is completely optional, even if you do want the Golden Path ending, I realized it isn't that big a deal. Just... holy crap why did you do it this way?\t\t\t\t\t
