\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is easily my favourite game right now. It has the perfect blend of exploration, combat and puzzle solving all bundled up into a gorgeous souls-like. It's not as punishing as Dark Souls whilst shamelessly borrowing from the game. The gameplay loop contains the right level of confusion mixed with aha moments and all the items you can find gives it a Zelda touch which rounds it out as one of the great video games.
The isometric style is new to me but I love how the developers have hidden things in corners the camera can't reach. I've always enjoyed over exploring corners, I like to get into every area of a world the devs let me and this game rewarded me every time whilst keeping frustrating dead-ends to a minimum. Part of me wishes the language was easier to decipher but the result is incredible amounts of replayability. Where Dark Souls started to grow stale as I learnt everything about the game, Tunic feels like it has a lot more "mystery" time.
Definitely give this game a chance.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a preface to this review, I want to say that I think this game is absolutely amazing and 100% worth a blind playthrough by anyone remotely interested in the genre. That being said, I'm using this review to vent my autistic nitpicks with certain aspects of the game. Despite everything I'm about to say, the game is still a 9 or 10 out of 10 for all the reasons stated in other reviews.


The worst part about the combat system in this game is the incredibly short iframe window on the dodgeroll, and how long it takes to execute. This really comes apparent once you reach the final portion of the game and unlock the teleportation crown. The final boss is significantly more fun than every other boss that came before it purely because there is more engaging gameplay that can be done around the improved dash. I unironically believe that if the dash had been that way from the start (obviously minus the ability to teleport across gaps) and the game was balanced accordingly, the combat as a whole would be much more fun. The distance could even be shortened until the crown is acquired later.

Consumables in this game outside the blastbomb and the fire bomb are completely useless. The frost bomb is a neat idea but is completely overshadowed by a magic item that does the exact same thing you will acquire very early in the game. The ivy and hot pepper both do not provide noticeable enough stat increases to warrant consistent usage. At most, you may use them a few times before a boss, but ultimately they really don't have much of an effect. The lure, as far as I can tell, is only useful for speedruns or specific challenge runs and had basically no application in my first playthrough. The fruits are only helpful in very select circumstances when your flask charges are completely empty AND you aren't in combat. Which brings me to the main problem with the consumable system: the hotbar. Only three items can be equipped to it, and many of them are not worth a slot that could otherwise be devoted to a precious magic item. I'm not sure how exactly the developers could've addressed this--my only thought would be a system similar to Dark Souls with a slot in the hotbar that can be cycled between consumables.

Continuing the hotbar discussion, it's unfortunate that for much of your exploration one of your hotbar slots must always be relegated to the magic orb for grapples. I think it might have been best to allow you to grapple over to a hook with a certain button whenever focusing on that hook.

For the most part, the endgame puzzle solving in this game is absolutely genius and incredibly fun to play through. That being said, I have a few nitpicks of a few puzzles that felt flatly unfair. My beliefs in these criticisms are not nearly as substantial as the previously mentioned ones, and it's entirely possible that I'm missing something with the puzzles. If so, please correct me.

-Fairy 8 requires the player to go to a certain location in the Eastern Forest at nighttime and follow the movements of a phantom fox to solve the puzzle. The issue is that there is absolutely no indication you should visit here at night. In fact, the area is seemingly completely blocked off during nighttime and can only be reached through a secret path up to the fox. I would be fine with this if there was any clue at all that hinted at returning at night, but as far as I can tell there is absolutely none.

The Eastern Fortress is one of my favorite areas in the game in terms of design, but unfortunately my experience with it and its boss was weighed down because I began it after already completing the quarry and ruined atoll. I'm not sure if this is true of whichever of these three zones you tackle last, but I steamrolled through most of the enemies with little difficulty which was a bit of an underwhelming way to end the second act of the game.

-Fairy 15: Much like the previous fairy, the issue with this one is not the puzzle itself but rather the means of accessing it. To reach the puzzle, you must use a bomb to blow a hole in the wall the fairies lead you to. Again, however, there is no indication that this would make sense to do, and (at least as far as I can tell) you never blow up a wall at any other point in the game.

-Fairy 20: I adore this fairy conceptually, but again, the issue is actually gathering together the pieces of the puzzle. The game relies on you either having an incredible memory and returning to all the locations with golden obelisk fragments OR you being willing to run throughout the entire map trying to find every last piece of the obelisk. This would be acceptable if there was some indication on the map of where golden obelisk fragments are, but only some are actually given this.

-Golden Path Page 8: My only issue here is that the first time I exited to the pause screen, the secret save was not there. I assume this is a bug, but it's something I've seen talked about online as well and would expect to be fixed a year after release.

As I mentioned at the start, this game is a must-play for basically anyone. I just wanted to share my few nitpicks with some aspects of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
