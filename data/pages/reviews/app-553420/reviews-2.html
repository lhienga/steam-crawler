\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI walked into this game completely blind and really enjoyed it at first.

The manual was useful in some cases, like telling me what I need to get, and explaining what each plant upgrades, and useless in other cases like telling what each ability card does (it tells what some of them do). Funny enough, the fact that it was written in a made up language kind of made it easier to understand.

The map isn't so big so going from one place to another didn't take that long. however, the fast travel system is tiring and having to pull up the manual and flip through the pages to see which area you want to go to gets boring. But exploring was fun, it wasn't very difficult to get to the places I needed to go and collect the keys.

Now, after I put all the keys in the right place, and went to what seemed like the final battle, I lost, and my health, stamina and mana were all reduced to the minimum amount and I was sent to the beginning as a ghost.

Naturally, I assumed that this section would be short and unchallenging, but after further exploration I found myself in an arena where I had to spawn 5 different types of enemies and defeat them, trying out different orders until I got the right one. After multiple deaths, I finally got to what I left as the final enemy (the big robot) with full heals and almost full mana, everything was going so well until another one appeared before I could even kill the first one and I died. Now I have to repeat everything again from the start.

All of this would be fun if I had all my upgrades, but without them it's just frustrating and exhausting.
If you manage to beat them, you can get all your upgrades back but you have to run around the entire map for them.

I stopped playing after that but I really enjoyed the first half.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is genuinely a masterpiece.

Let me quickly say before anything else that this game is a massive puzzle game at it's core. This is one giant puzzle with many puzzles of varying scale inside it. Along with action combat, platforming, as well as upgrades and unlockable abilities (unlocked through item discovery).

I have mixed feelings about puzzle games because almost universally you will eventually get stuck on something. This did happen to me here to the point where I had to look up information but it happened only a couple times and only really in the post game where the puzzle's escalate in scale and difficulty to the point where I bet the vast majority of people who found those achievements looked up some of the answers. Long story short this game hit my specific sweet spot of challenging yet solvable.

To go through all the attributes of the game briefly:

- The music is rarely something I felt was good but the use of perfect sound effects, occasional music tracks to highlight specific moments, and the creative use of silence has got to be some of the best sound direction I've experienced.

- The visuals are amazing as well. The art style, the menus, and presentation are all incredibly good, set a fantastic tone/atmosphere for the game, and are generally pretty to look at.

- The story is the least clear aspect of the game to me. I still after finishing the game don't fully understand it but to me I consider it an element of the puzzle. There are many clues to a greater story being told here, or at least for there being lore. I'm just not the person to put it together. That being said I will be doing research on my own time to see how other people have put the pieces together.

- Finally the gameplay. What can I say this game gets nearly everything right. I think that the combat isn't always the cleanest but it's usually still very good. The sense of adventure and discovery are delivered on just great. At the end of the day however it's the puzzle(s) and it's insane to me how they conceived of this game. The idea to make pages of an instruction manual a collectible item for you to slowly learn information about the mechanics, world, and everything else is brilliant! Eventually (unless you really like puzzles) if you want to really complete the game you will probably have to look things up but I still feel like the game is just next level game design and is unbelievably fun.\t\t\t\t\t
