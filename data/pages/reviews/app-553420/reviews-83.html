\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a beautiful game :)
I originally purchased it because it looked absolutely gorgeous, and I was not disappointed in that regard. It has such a beautifully stylized, highly detailed, handcrafted world for you to explore. Basically every 10 square feet of this place has something important going on with it. It's not particularly big, but the stark lack of dead space here makes it feels like a continent.
But OH BOY it is so much more.
Basically everything that you need to know in order to play the game is kept a secret from you in the beginning. There is an in-game manual, but its are pages scattered throughout the world, and you need to collect them in order to get any sense of what you're doing (otherwise, it's trial and error). I'm a person who loves making secret codes and conlangs in their free time, and finding the first few pages of the manual lit that part of my brain up like a fireworks show. While there are parts of it you can get from context, more than half the thing is written in a secret language you have to solve. Learning how to play this game is a puzzle.
Remember how I said every 10 square feet of this game has something important going on in it? Well, this game actually has another name, "Secret Legend", and given how many secrets there are in this game, it fits very well. While this game has been appropriately labelled a Zelda-Souls-like, it is first and foremost a game about learning secrets. There are collectible upgrades and special abilities you can access, but secrets serve as your primary means of progression. I was afraid that this would hurt the game's replayability, but here's the funny thing: IT DOESN'T. While there is a New Game+ option that makes things a bit tougher, restarting the game from scratch with a totally new save is a completely different experience, since the secrets you have learned from completing it still apply: while in-game you are as weak as you were when you first started out, you can use your old knowledge to completely overthrow the order of progression.
It is a wild experience, and I would highly recommend it to anyone who loves a mystery. A few things you should know, though (and keep in mind, this is a person who likes solving puzzles):
1. If you get any pleasure from puzzle-solving, PLEASE STAY AWAY FROM INTERNET GUIDES AS MUCH AS YOU CAN. While there are a rare few who respect the player's intelligence by tastefully dropping hints, most resources you find on the Internet just give you the answer, sometimes spoiling more than you'd have liked to know. There were a few times I had to look something up (one of the late game secrets required me to know a game mechanic I should have learned in the early game), But if you really like the thrill of solving a puzzle yourself, read the manual closely. Try different tactics and perspectives. Heck, if it's still pretty early in the game, save it for later. It might be frustrating in the moment, but once you solve it, you'll be glad you did.
2. While there are some opportunities to softlock yourself, there are even more ways to get physically stuck that aren't actually softlocks, i.e. getting temporarily stuck on a ledge or cheesing your way into an area before knowing how to get out. There are a few game mechanics that are particularly tricky on this; they've been fined-tuned to greatly reduce the likelihood, but it can still happen. Usually, getting stuck is just a matter of finagling your way out, but it you do softlock, you can just reload the save to escape them.
3. Keep an open mind going in. I went into this thinking this was going to be an ordinary adventure, but I was completely caught off guard by its sheer complexity, in more ways than one. I don't want to spoil the surprise, but all I'll say is this: This is a beautiful game, so be ready for anything.\t\t\t\t\t
