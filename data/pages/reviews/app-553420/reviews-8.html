\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTunic is a game that I came to hoping to scratch my itch for something like Outer Wilds, and excelled entirely in a different direction. This review will be somewhat cryptic due to the nature of the game, but I can assure you it is spoiler free.

The puzzles in this game are fantastic, and the realisation of how to do so many different things is similar to getting hit by five or six different trucks across the course of the game.
My only critique is that the combat is a little janky - towards the final act of the game, I had to turn on Reduced Difficulty to keep it fun, and I have no regrets in doing so. Don't be ashamed - the real game here is in the puzzles, not the combat.
The actual length of the game is also just right, but I wouldn't advise doing the Extra Secret Final Final Puzzle yourself - you'll be disappointed by the end result for the amount of work it takes.
This game is worth playing for the puzzle called The Door In The Mountains. Whatever you do, do not look up the solution to this one. You'll kick yourself if you do. Grab a notebook and a pen, and just think about it. You can do it. If you're really confused, move on and collect more pages of that manual.

Best of luck, little fox. Remember, wisdom untempered by kindness is no wisdom at all.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a fan of games like Death's Door, La Mulana 1-2, The Witness, Outer Wilds, Environmental Station Alpha, Hyper Light Drifter, and Fez, just to name a few, do yourself a favor and get this game. Especially if you like very challenging, cryptic and brain melting puzzles. It's up there with the best puzzle games out there, IMO. Just get it and enjoy the constant dopamine rush.

Now, if you're not into those games as much, or the puzzle elements at least, I still think this game is very much so worth checking out. The exploration is exciting, combat and bosses are fun, not insanely hard but not baby mode either. It can get a little stale though outside of the bosses. There's also lot's of little secrets to find, even without doing all the more optional hard puzzles. The game has a mysterious story, it's told more visually and less through dialogue. There's additional lore if you get into the hard puzzle elements of the game, but you get a very vague and general idea of what's going on just by progressing.

It's a solid adventure game for more casual players, but I think it's even better for those who like tough puzzles and like taking notes and staring at a screen for 1 hour straight trying to solve a puzzle.\t\t\t\t\t
