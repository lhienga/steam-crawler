\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBy far the most fun I've ever had trying to play a 53 hour tutorial.Game is HARD but if you have experience with souls-likes then this should be nothing new. What is interesting is how you'll have to figure out everything you can do all while fighting for your life. IMO the best way to experience this game is by NOT looking up any material for it until you are well and truly stuck with no way forward. Until then, the game practically begs you to try random things and learn how the mechanics work, all while subtly hiding things from you to give you a sense of wonder when you find them. I for one found myself cursing the game devs several times for making me walk all the way around the map only to reveal that a shortcut was just around a hidden corner right where I started.Perhaps my favorite part of this though is that even after you've finished the game and learned all the mechanics, unlike other hidden knowledge games TUNIC is still fun to play NG+. Now that you know how the game works, you get to play the game again feeling like a real gamer using mechanics you weren't supposed to be aware of at that stage of the game.10/10 wish I could forget everything and play this game fresh again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had heard really good things about this game, so on the good faith of reviews, I tried it out. For the first while, I had a good time playing through a charming little Legend of Zelda inspired rpg. Then I started to notice a lot of mysteries and puzzles to solve. I mostly ignored them and continued through the game. I hit some pretty difficult boss fights that almost made me want to stop, and a few segments that were particularly frustrating, bit eventually I arrived at the "end" of the game.

Only after beating the final boss did I unlock the ACTUAL game. It was then that it went from "pretty neat little game" to "masterpiece created by a lunatic". The puzzles, man. The puzzles go so deep. I have never before had a game so quickly and dramatically shift genres on me, and never have I been plunged so deep into the rabbit hole. I can't explain anything, because it will spoil too much and ruin things, but I did share many of my puzzle solutions and attempts with friends. Here are some actual reviews - not of the game, but of me playing the game:
"Holy Moly"
"This is insane"
"Wow I love this"
"I am genuinely concerned about your sanity"

One gripe I did have was that the music was a bit too mellow for me. Some of the boss fights are legitimately quite difficult and intense, meanwhile the music didn't reflect that. I ended up muting the music and playing much heavier stuff during boss fights as it actually helped me fight better. That was probably a placebo but hey, it worked for me. Overall, not a big problem, and years from now I will not remember the lackluster music, but I will most certainly remember spending 3 straight days going way too deep into the secrets this game hides, with the help of a few great friends.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr at the end.

The game is fun at first, the art style is beautiful, the combat is weighty and fun, but the obtuse level design with poorly hidden pathways that you would never be able to find without a guide, as well as the way the game refuses to tell you any information about any of the items (because 99% of the text is in a fun made up language that you cannot decipher) gets old very quickly and starts to take a tax on your enjoyment on the game.

When you can't reach a new area and are stuck because you need an item that is hidden behind an impossible to find cavern it's not fun. When the game fails to let you know that "praying" is a thing that lets you interact with the world in new ways, so you spend hours wondering how many items you're missing since you can't interact with the big metal pillars or the shiny golden platforms. There is no prompt, too. There is no hint, no tips, nothing.

The devs just magically expect you to find out that by holding *Lock on + Dodge* you can pray and therefore power the statues (which i did not do because i encountered about 10 of those pillars before finding out -in a guide- that you can actually power them) and use the teleport platforms (because I only found out after looking up a different guide on where to go that teleporting exists, but don't worry, it's not fun either, since there is no way to know where you will be teleporting since all you see is blocks of light).

Also, did you know you can run in the game? Because I sure as hell didn't. You have to hold the interact button to run. As well as finding out there's a grappling hook hidden away behind 4 or so caverns.

Is that not enough to discourage you? Oh, well in that case the game also has special magical cards you can equip. I obtained about 6 of them. I have no idea what they do after trying every single one of them, healing, dying, fighting, they seem to have no apparent effect on gameplay. As well as the cool upgrades you can spend gold on! There are about 4 of them. Only one of them is useful, which is the one that makes you have a bit more HP, so you can get stunlocked for even longer.

The combat is also not fun. It's like the worst aspects of a Kirby game combined with the worst aspects of a Souls game. Some enemies have tells so you can dodge or parry. Most enemies don't. Most enemies simply charge randomly with no way to counterattack or parry (See: large dogs, crows, frogs, large frogs, mages, etc).

I wanted to love the game, but it feels like there is no learning curve as the game doesn't give you the tools to improve, it just drops you into the world without letting you know that those tools even exist, and when you get them, gives you no way to find out what they do.

tl;dr: the first 30 minutes you'll think "oh man I can't wait for the game to lead me in any way to have fun" until you realize that the game will do nothing to help you have fun with it, and just trips over itself due to its lack of explanation so you know what anything does. Don't buy it.\t\t\t\t\t
