\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuch a Dissapointment
I had really high hopes for this game. I bought it on sale after it had been at the top of my Wishlist for a long time. I loved the conceit of finding bits of a partially-translated manual as you play, and said manual gave me serious nostalgia for old NES games. The Zelda-meets-Dark Souls gameplay was great, as I'm a huge fan of both those series. I don't know how far I got in the game, but I'm assuming somewhere around 80% of the way to hitting credits. Unfortunately, after having a great time exploring numerous beautiful areas and unlocking the central mystery of the early game things changed drastically.
Up to this point the game had been moderately difficult, with some occasional spikes for bosses. This made it challenging, forced you to learn boss attack patterns, and gave you ample reason to scour the world for powerups to cope with increasingly tough baddies. Suddenly, for story reasons, all your powerups are taken away, your stats are reset to base levels, and you are ushered in to a previously inaccessible area. The enemies here aren't terribly difficult, but because you've lost most of your progress (you still have extra potions, tho they now heal far less), taking a single hit typically takes over 1/2 of your HP. I found this frustrating, but adapted, "got good", and made my way to the next area, hoping the game would soon return to its previous form.
Here the game ramps up the difficulty by making its systems unfair. Not only do you still have only base stats, but the enemies now hit for over 1/2 of your HP AND reduce your max HP. This means that you have to heal after every single hit, and even if you do, after about 3 hits everything will one-hit-KO you. It's made worse by you having to fight shadowy versions of yourself who don't have a stamina bar to manage, can endlessly dodge-roll-counterattack you, and have the ability to instantly chain a thrust onto any basic attack (something you can NOT do). Even after swallowing my pride and turning combat difficulty down in the options (something I *hate* having to do; a properly-balanced game doesn't need an "Easy" setting, but I digress) the problems weren't alleviated, as all this did was change the number of hits before a OHKO to about 5 or 6, and didn't change the nature of combat at all. Sadly, I got so frustrated that I had to give up on a game I had previously been loving (and a friend assures me that there is an even more unfair, unfun hurdle to clear at the end of this zone), and I was really looking forward to trying to figure out the secrets after beating (I found one of what I can only assume is a bunch of secret treasures during my play by puzzling out a penned-in note in the manual).
Bottom line, it utterly sucks that such a great game idea is ruined by a section that breaks the "tough but fair" standard, leaving you feeling super-frustrated instead of fulfilled by a beautiful, cryptic world that challenges and rewards you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good game that feels like it could be a great game if it wasn't held back by some bizarre and frustrating game design choices.

Most of the game's text is in a made-up alphabet, leaving it unreadable without a guide. I think this was supposed to simulate playing an imported game in a language you don't understand, but it just makes the game frustrating to play since you have extremely minimal guidance on what any items do (it isn't always obvious even when you use them), how your abilities work, or even where to go to advance the story. There are even entire gameplay systems that you won't learn about without looking it up, including a stat upgrade system that is mandatory to stand a chance against bosses, but can only be discovered by happening to press the right (unprompted) button in the right map location or by looking it up.

Bosses are a HUGE difficulty spike every time, being endurance battles about dodging and whittling down their health while common enemies are more hack-n-slash in how you deal with them. It creates a bit of dissonance and makes boss battles feel out of place with the rest of the game. Your items don't reset after a loss either, so be ready to grind for gold and hike back to a shop.

Some mechanics are very hit-or-miss with whether they'll function as intended for you, with the shield not always blocking attacks for some reason and the invincibility period at the beginning of a dodge roll sometimes being completely ignored, especially by bosses. Locking onto enemies is also wonky since you can't pick which one to lock onto, which makes fighting multiple enemies needlessly tough as you get slaughtered by something six inches away but can only lock onto another enemy across the room.

There's fun to be had here, but you'd best at least have some online hints handy because you'll either have to look everything up or spend ~70% of your playtime running around aimlessly hoping to stumble on what you're supposed to do next with no indication of what that is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPositives: Nice graphics, not buggy, nice art direction, its not a bad game on the whole but it does some stuff that make made me really detest it and never want to touch it again...I think it does enough right to make you have strong feels about the ways it brings the experience down. If you want a Souls experience this is not it.
4 BIG ISSUES with this game that add up to a poor experience.
1. Having the doge/sprint button hard locked to the interact button...just hideous. If you rebind the button to where you prefer the doge button it makes navigating menus really annoying when you mess with muscle memory, but far more debilitating is rolling every time to want to go up a ladder, not to mention the interact ranges on stuff if find to be way to small.
1b. Not having dedicated slots for consumables and magic attracts, I don't mind that you can organize it how you want but it leads to constantly having stuff in different slots which prevents you from building up muscle memory to use you items and abilities fluidly in combat...just use the souls system where i can cycle between items of each category.
2. Combat has three main issues for me.
A. The lock on system is atrocious to this point its still feels unpredictable. It tends to hold lock on for crazy distance sometimes and when you feel it would be natural to break lock on like say when you are running away from a enemy and trying to point you character at the enemy in front of you it fails to switch. Basically, its a pain in the ass to break lock on in a natural way without actually inputting the button press.
B. The range of Tunics melee attacks, the game wants to be a Souls-Like and it succeeds in a lot of aspects, but the melee range is so short you have to be right in the enemy's face which means it can be hard to read attacks, but more so it makes it so you just want to spam when in attack range because its not like you will be able to doge and attack, you will need to doge and then move back into attack range which is boring and doesn't capture the satisfying dance the is Souls combat because its boring it encourages hack and slash gameplay.
C.) The roll or iframes seem pretty imprecise or maybe there are just very few iframes and doge tends to follow the character orientation rather than the camera orientation which leads to rolling sideways a lot. The biggest issue though is because the roll feels hit or miss the skill ceiling also feels capped. I've beat my head against souls bosses for days because it felt like there was room to improve precision and get gud and its enjoyable...this game feels to loose, you just hope the roll works for you 3 or 4 times when you needed and during an attempts when you beat the boss it just feels like good RNG and when your dodge successfully it a "o it worked" feeling rather than something you can learn from and get more consistent with. To sum it by 8 hours in combat felt maxed out like not really any room to improve just have to rely on getting more powerful rather than better at the game.
4. Finally, the whole hidden game mechanics thing...was cool at first, I like games that don't hold your hand, but the big secrets are what your items do....give me a break I know games have certain types of items its just a matter of which one is which... I'm not trying to wait 2hrs to find out that this is a freeze bomb and not a FP replenishing item...I guess I'll just burn pick up to see if I can figure out what they do. My advice just look up a guide to see what items do so you can incorporate them into you gameplay right away.
(Small Issues) Not the biggest issue but things could be a big more zoomed out cause the maps get a bit confusing and tons of the hidden stuff is just hidden by geometry and odd angles not so much clever level design. Finally, the story is whatever, goes to far into the obtuse realm and you just end up praying at the generators and collecting keys and for its own sake rather than any narrative motivation.\t\t\t\t\t
