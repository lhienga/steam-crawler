\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTunic is an incredible fusion of all the best bits of Dark Souls and 2D Zelda. It feels great to play start to finish, and has a lovely art style. Its usage of a made-up language for most of the text is clever and brings a sense of mystery and discovery that permeates the game, and the instruction booklet that you fill out as you play fits the style perfectly and is a great way to feed the player information.
The combat is refined and fun. The progression is well-paced so that you feel yourself getting more powerful as you easily destroy enemies that used to give you problems, and you unlock more options to play around with as you grow comfortable with your abilities. The game does get difficult, primarily in the boss fights, but much like Dark Souls it never feels unfair. I really enjoyed the puzzle aspects, although I did cheat a few times.
An unfortunate consequence of most of the text being unreadable is that the story is harder to understand. I found that I understood enough to know what I need to be doing, but I still don't really understand much of the lore or why things are the way they are.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTunic was an unexpected gem of a game to me.
From trailers it looked like a bit generic rpg game, but in reality it's so much more.

What I liked about Tunic:
- Beautiful minimalistic art style
- Nice non-intrusive music
- Suprisingly fun combat system with a lot of tiny details and alternative styles to combat
- Pleaseant difficulty curve and nice mix of metroidvania and corridor rpg elements

Where the Tunic shines the most:
- Puzzles are amazing, they are very well-thought and fun to solve, they are different and add so much to gameplay and story
- Game Manual! At the first glance it looks like a small nice progress indicator, made as a tribute to old games' paper manuals, but it's so much more. I would say it's a core game mechanic for Tunic - I never felt so much joy and fun finding some in-game writings instead of upgrades and items.

What I didn't like:
- Most boss fights while not being that bad just feel a bit off, those fights fall out the game flow and fight mechanics.
- While most puzzles are amazing and fun, some are a bit twisted and not clear (f* you, chime puzzle) and some leave too large error margin making time to solve artificially longer than it needs (like Mountain Door puzzle, that could be split in parts to make it easier to test solutions)
- Small bugs and glitches can be seen here and there - nothing gamebreaking, but they do feel a bit off.

Overall, I think Tunic is definitely worth playing, it's very smart about it's complexity - it will let you dive as deep as you wish without forcing you to dig as much as the game goes.
