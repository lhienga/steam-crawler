\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic game hindered by terrible combat.
I saw Tunic and said "Ah, an old school Zelda with some souls-y combat. How quaint." but what I got instead was a wonderfully cute game, deceptively so, jam packed with bad game design.
Want the sword that cuts tress? Gotta manipulate the enemies with swords to cut down trees so you can get the sword.
Want the shield? You gotta fight an enemy that you're meant to fight with the shield to get the shield.
Oh but don't you worry since the parry is completely useless, your parry has a ton of wind up frames building up to the action AND enemies have little to NO wind up frames, which makes for an infuriating experience- BUT WAIT it gets better because at certain points they throw enemies who's proper way to deal with them is PARRYING, which lead to me spamming dynamite instead.
Parry is too slow, pulling off the strike-esc lunge attack requires you to suddenly stop moving and push the stick and use the combo of buttons, in a game were constantly moving is paramount to combat's flow, to pull it off consistently, can't dodge out of a swing, etc. It's like Dark Souls levels of "commitment" in a game where the enemies do not also have to "commit" to their moves.
The world is cute, the exploring is fun, the "Leveling up" mechanic is unique, but the amount of money required to do so ramps up far too quickly imo.
I have experienced 0 genuine puzzles so far, unless you consider "Oh my! the forced isometric perspective that gives me no right stick control to (But I can use the lock on button to slightly shift my perspective up) do anything and so I missed this thing that was impossible to see!"
I would not recommend this game solely because its focus is on the combat, and the combat is bad. Again the game isn't bad- solid 7/10, would be an 8/10 and switch to a positive rec if I could change the parry speed by 15% - 20%.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a tight, excellent game. Fantastic art-style, soundtrack, combat, exploration, this game really does do everything it sets out to do perfectly. The best part is that this is game is similar to "The Return of the Obra Dinn" and "Outer Wilds", where the player's progression is primarily based on their ability to uncover and understand secrets. The fact that the creators of the game made a new written language with a cursive form is astounding, although luckily the player is never expected to be able to understand it. Even two months after playing it I still can't stop thinking about this game, I'm definitely going to be remembered as one of the best games I've ever played.\t\t\t\t\t
