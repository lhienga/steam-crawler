\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile Zelda Souls is a proper description to this game, I think it needs some expanding. The puzzles in this game are brutal,
it requires you to think about and analyse every detail you see. Its not that the puzzles are complex, but they are well hidden and so very varied, with many puzzles you see only once and are not repeated.

The world is surprisingly big, and exploration is rewarded so well. There are so many ways the game hides its secrets, and many times I leave something for later thinking that I do not have the tools to solve it, only to find out I am not thinking outside the box.

I would not call the combat easy, but it is not crazy hardcore either. While combat can be difficult at times, dying is not punished as hard as Souls and recovery is easy. Bosses are very fun and never unfair, with a variety patterns to learn and some can have puzzly aspects to them.

The story is very much in tune with Souls and Hyper Light Drifter in that you must force the game to give you a story, it will not give you one if you don't try your best to find it. The story is puzzle in it self and absolutely engaging, I feel so invested emotionally in the world.

Also, super cute foxes.

Bottom line: 10/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first I was hooked. A Zelda-esque action adventure.

The guide book mechanic is INGENIOUS! You figure out things on your own or you find a new page in the guide book and get that Eureka moment. I like the idea that the game does not hold your hand in every step. "Here's a piece of knowledge, let's see if you find it useful." The in-game guide book itself is reminiscent of the good old times using actual guide books. And the reveals are awesome! If you love the feeling of discovery, I would really recommend it!
The translation took some work, but my fiancee and I managed. It was a great bonding experience too.

Now for the BUTs.
-Enemies are RELENTLESS. They will follow you even if you changed rooms. Will keep on attacking and attacking. Their weapons have much longer reach than yours too.
-Clunky rolling. You can't roll/evade right after a sword swing. I understand why, but I dislike this mechanic in games. If your life is in danger, you'd drop whatever you're doing to evade, right? (EDIT: not a problem in late-game)
-And the difficulty spike. In a certain part of the game, where your power ups are stripped off, the game will throw a gauntlet at you. (EDIT: hardest challenge but worth it)

I am not a fan of souls-like games. If you're like me, I don't recommend it. If not, then feel free to play. Don't let the Zelda look fool you though.\t\t\t\t\t
