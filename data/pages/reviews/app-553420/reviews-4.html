\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuick note, my play time keeps resetting as i have mostly played this on my steam deck offline.

Firstly, this game looks really good, but don't let this fool you!

Gameplay wise, there aren't really that many puzzles, if any (so Zelda like isn't how i would describe it). Most of the game you are just trying to decipher the manual pages that you pick up throughout the world, which are often quite cryptic. An example is quick travel (which is needed to access some later areas) is only accessible once you figure out you can just hold you primary interact button down while standing on golden pads on the floor, but the game only hints at this by showing a picture in the manual of the primary button and saying "3 seconds", along side a picture of the golden floor pads.

Then we get to bosses, which are pretty much an execution not a boss fight, they are brutal, not fun at all. The bosses don't miss, and are almost constantly attacking, and require you to keep dodging which uses up your stamina really quickly. I'm an experienced gamer and i was pulling my hair out on the second big boss until i found out you could turn on "no die mode" in accessibility, which is obviously cheating, but when a game is intentionally brutally hard just to make the short game longer, then i have no regrets to turning these kind of options on.

It's not a cheap game either, and just relying on it's looks and "Zelda like" reviews. If i had known all this beforehand, i wouldn't have bought it, my recommendation, save your money!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a big fan of Zelda games and Outer Wilds, Tunic was sadly kinda disappointing.

The main draw of this game will be the mystery, how to play and what is happening with the story, which at the start is an incredibly strong hook and makes the sense of discovery very big.
There are definitely magical moments where you suddenly realize how to do something or what is going on, which is important for this kind of game.
But these moments happen only a few times and the rest of Tunic is just unsatisfying.

I think my biggest gripe with Tunic is that most of the "puzzles" aren't really puzzles but rather just something you will eventually be shown within the manual pages you will find, yes technically it isn't an item you use to do the thing, you have to put in a bit of mental work to figure out what the page is telling you, but it's still shown to you through the page you just picked up instead of you applying a known mechanic in a new creative way.

Especially the final big puzzles fail horribly, a key to a good puzzle is that it takes a while to figure out what you need to do, but once you realize the solution, executing it should only take a few seconds.
In Portal for example, you try to mentally figure out how to place the portals, which can take a good half hour in some of the harder levels. But once you know, you just place them, go through and finish the level.
However, in Tunic you know pretty quickly what it is you need to do with the endgame puzzles, but you have go back and forth to note things down (no way that you can mentally keep track of all of it) and be busy for nearly 15 minutes just doing the thing you know will solve it, which makes it drag on way too long and just takes all satisfaction away from solving the puzzle.
If you still like the idea of those kind of puzzles, then this game is probably very fun for you, but it just doesn't work for me personally.

Besides that, there's some items you need to figure out on your own what they do, but unlike how most games will put you in a situation where you can clearly figure it out, here you can go a while without really figuring out what it does...causing it to not be used at all.
And in general the combat just isn't all too interesting, most basic enemies can be dealt with very easily, only the boss battles provide an actual challenge and the difference in difficulty is sometimes a bit too big, since it's not the main draw of the game though that is not a huge problem, if you are struggling too much there's settings to pretty much disable the combat.

The story is interesting, but sadly leaves a bit too much open to interpretation in my opinion, there is so much to the world that I would love to be explained just a bit more.
Showing instead of telling is good, but showing a bit more would've made it more fulfilling to get the true ending so you can truly understand the impact you are making. It just feels like a cheap wholesome ending at the moment.

On the good side, the visuals are honestly really pretty, and the world design is phenomenal.
There are countless secret passages that connect different places together, making it feel as interconnected as Dark Souls 1.

But sadly it just left a bitter aftertaste in my mouth, it could've been a real gem but just ended up being disappointing, so sadly I can't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game that kinda falls in between a No and a Yes when deciding what to vote on. I'll list the cons and pros that I found when 100% the game.

Pros:
+ Art style is great, absolutely love it.
+ Soundtrack is actually really well done. Great mix of tracks that are very ambient and have alot of depth. Suited the game very well.
+ Skill system is pretty nice, getting powerups and beinh able to improve 6 different aspects is a good baseline.

Cons:
- The puzzles in this game is way to extreme. You have to be extremely intelligent and put ALOT of time into reading the in-game-manual if you want to 100% this game without a guide, ALOT of the puzzles, not a few of them, made me think after following a guide for the secret treasures and fairies that there's absolutely no way in hell anyone would figure this out casually. There must have been some extreme theory crafting from the community to figure out all of the stuff in this game.
- The combat. It's actually really, really bad. Had this game had combat like "Hades", the game would actually be a VERY, VERY good game. Instead, it's clunky, it's slow, it's punishing. Very poorly balanced.

The negatives sadly overweighs the positives quite heavily. It's clear what the devs had in mind, a Zelda game with a twist, with mixed influences from Dark Souls. But nothing really hit the spot, they really did nothing right that Souls and Zelda did amazingly.

I can't really recommend this game. The first few hours was quite enjoyable, but the end was very sluggish to get through. The game looks and sounds great, but a game can't just be about visuals, the gameplay have to be there. Sadly this one doesn't hit it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort but a high quality experience. If you like Zelda but want something a bit different, this is good. It takes some inspiration from games like Dark Souls, games I do not like, and incorporates them well, as I still enjoyed this game quite a lot.

Two major complaints:
- The main character's reach is extremely small. This is usually fine, while fighting enemies around their size, but is particularly awful in certain fights, such as the Siege Engine. That was a particularly bad fight.
- There is a mechanic for finding secrets in the world very similar to a realization I had with The Witness. I did not think this was implemented very well in the vast majority of places you could use it. A character dancing in a certain pattern does not at all equate to patterns you see elsewhere. One of these puzzles had 100 button presses that you must make without error. If you ever design a puzzle where figuring it out is interesting and rewarding but completing the task is awful, you need to keep trying.

Outside of these two issues, I found the game to be consistently high quality and an enjoyable experience. The exploration and world design were top tier. The music and sound design was great. I personally enjoyed the artistic style.

I think there is a lot to love for those who enjoy these sorts of games.\t\t\t\t\t
