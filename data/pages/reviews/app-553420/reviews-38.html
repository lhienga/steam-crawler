\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start off by saying that this is a good, greatly crafted game and you'll probably enjoy it. However, it's so, so much not for me, and I want to explore why.

Do you know the stories of when Kojima worked at Konami and that he was so weird with his gameplay ideas that there was always some dude watching over him and occasionally saying "Kojima, for the love of GOD, dial it down a notch"? I'm a game developer myself, experienced seeing such people as well. Tunic was made as if it never had such a dude at all.

I cannot comprehend who on earth would think that the first NES Zelda is a decent game. I also think this way about Dark Souls, but that's a more of a personal thing for me, as I know lots of people enjoy it. This game just smells of this idea "hey wouldn't it be cool if we crossed the first zelda with dark souls?". The whole manual-collecting thing is a cool concept, but it wants to play so hard into the mystery that the game has its own language to obfuscate info. But, oh gosh, QA figured out that this way nothing can be understood by some people, so some important text is still in English, making this obfuscation automatically artsy-fartsy pretentious.

Tons of the game also relies on unmarked secrets for progression, leading to getting stuck and being like doomguy hugging every wall in the original doom, only there it was for bonuses and was not vital. Some mechanics, although were explained in the manual (I think), could not be reliably executed, roll->attack for example, leading me to question if the game gaslights me with insufficient info, or if it's just a janky bugfest.

Oh, and the whole DS stuff. "Ha-ha, anything kills you in 1-2 hits, ha-ha!". I personally very much dislike this design and consider it cheap. It's not at all how zelda games play out, not even the latest, harder BOTW and TOTK. I could enable the relaxed difficulty in the accessibility settings, but I don't trust the game already! What'll it do behind my back with this option? Will it force me onto an automatic bad ending? Lock me out from it altogether? I'm paranoid already! The game made me paranoid!

The production of it is so, so great though, with genuinely great soundtrack, sound design and cleverly realized artistic rendering features. It's a joy experiencing it. For that reason alone I wanted to finish it, but the entire gimmick of it and the gameplay drive me nuts.

The developers clearly wanted to invite you and explore its every nook and cranny, and it has secrets in spades, even leading up to ARG content for the most dedicated ones, and that's great. However, in the end, at least for me, it doesn't become cryptic or mysterious, it becomes outright pretentiously hostile due to a portion of these secrets being obscenely mandatory and cryptic. I didn't enjoy solving them and battling against the mechanics of the game when I just wanted to move on. And that's unfortunate, because I can clearly see the passion behind it.\t\t\t\t\t
