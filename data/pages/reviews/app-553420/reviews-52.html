\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like top-down Zeldas, if you like Dark Souls, if you like exploration and puzzles, play this game. It's awesome.

The graphics are beautiful, those lighting effects are amazing. Combat is perfect, can be difficult sometimes, but there are difficulty options (in the accessibility menu) to tune it down. The rest of the gameplay is mostly exploring, and a few puzzles (or a lot, depending on where you are in the game).

You aren't guided a lot, since most of the text is written in a weird language (or is it that weird ? maybe you can translate it later). You're pretty free to go anywhere (sometimes you need to get a specific item somewhere before), it just depends if you have understood enough mechanics or not. There is still this in-game manual of which you collect the pages scattered around the world. It's the most important object in the game, since it gives you (almost) everything you will ever need to know about.
I will give you one hint : if you're stick anywhere, just look precisely at all of the pages, or try to find more in the areas you've explored.

TL;DR : Tunic is an amazing game and you should buy it right now.\t\t\t\t\t

It will throw you into a world with no explanation, barely any readable text, letting you learn the ins and outs of the game using clues left in a instruction booklet. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tDark Souls meets Zelda - except not really, as the latter informs only some of the game's aesthetic; I'd argue that Tunic owes much more to FEZ, in almost every regard. The Soulslike cues are very on the nose, but they mainly serve as a good, simple and familiar RPG baseline. Overall, this game's combat isn't notably difficult outside the handful of boss fights (though not trivial either).

While it is inevitable that descriptions or even recommendations of Tunic get boiled down to namedropping two existing game series, the real secret sauce, the innovation that sets it apart, is the cryptic scattered in-game manual, which adds a whole other dimension to puzzles and exploration. That and the absolutely stellar and charming presentation, which is on par with the best and most characterful indie games, make this game absolutely worth playing, despite some potential frustration here and there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an excellent game! In Tunic, you explore to discover not only the world around you, but the game mechanics themselves. The combat is engaging and approachable in many different ways, and the boss fights can be as difficult as some of the hardest fights I've encountered in any video game (though there are ways to make them more manageable).

I also enjoyed how the story is told throughout the game through the game's environment and the occasional cutscene. It drove me to do more exploration than I'd usually do.

Finally, a shout out to the many accessibility and difficulty options included in the Options menu. While I enjoyed the base combat difficulty, there is a "No Fail" and easier difficulty mode for those who want to focus on exploration and puzzles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have never before experienced such joy while playing a video game. The game innovates almost every aspect of a what video game is. Everything feels fresh and new. The puzzles use unique mechanics which makes them satisfying to solve. The combat is simple but difficult. The manual is something I have never seen in a video game. It is not your typical old school game manual as most of it is incomprehensible at the beginning.

I have to give props for such a small file size. Many games take up to 30 GB of memory and still be short. In this case there is about 15-30 hours of content only for 2 GB. Moreover the graphics are beautiful for such a small game.

I recommend completing the manual if puzzles are your cup of tea. They do take a lot of time altogether but it is easy to invest time in them. Especially deciphering the language takes dedication. If it's too arduous, there are lot of resources for translations (you will eventually get hints for the process.)

TUNIC holds special place in my list of best game experiences. It hits the mark on many aspects and keeps being fresh despite my huge list of completed games.

Overall: 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
