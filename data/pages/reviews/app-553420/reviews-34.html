\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust wow!
This is basically an isometric 3D Zelda game inspired by Link's Awakening or A Ling to the past. Tunic goes for a more calming/mysterious atmosphere, handing you manual pieces with very important tips that are partly unreadable because of runic symbols that replace most of the text. I think what they were going for is those game manuals of old that we used to be too young to properly read when those games came out (and instead "reading" the pictures and the few words we were able to read).
As this is exactly the gaming generation I was born into (my very first videogame being Link's Awakening), Tunic feels like a love letter directly catering to players like me. It also helps that the game is far more demanding (in terms of combat) than older Zelda games which basically means it feels as challenging as a playing a new Zelda back then as a kid.

10/10 experience if you grew up in the 1990ths.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel like a good way to describe this game is a cross between The Legend of Zelda, Dark Souls, and Hyper Light Drifter.
Tunic does no hand holding and aims for an old school Nintendo kind of feel. The player collects pieces of the game's instruction booklet as they make their way through the game. The instruction booklet resembles an old school Nintendo instruction booklet and contains hints to the story and what the player is supposed to do as well as hints to secrets and puzzles that are genuinely fun to figure out.
The puzzles I feel are Tunic's strongest aspect. There are lots of hidden secrets to uncover and I personally like the "golden cross" mechanic tied to a lot of them as it's a nice nod to old school gaming.
Combat is fast paced, focused on timing dodges and attacks in windows of opportunity as well as the players ability to be resourceful with available items. It can seem tricky at first but once you know what you're doing combat is not particularly difficult. Tunic is best played with a controller and the design of the game clearly indicates that. While it is possible to play with M+K it feels very clunky and awkward.\t\t\t\t\t
