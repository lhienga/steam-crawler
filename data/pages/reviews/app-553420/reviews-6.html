\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have complicated feelings about this game. I loved collecting the manual pages and gradually solving all the various mysteries and puzzles along the way. However, the combat (especially the boss fights) were a real drag overall. The huge emphasis on i-framing through screen-filling AOEs and managing your stamina really felt out of place in this game to me. Also, why on earth does the camera need to rotate around so much during boss fights? I assume it was to make things feel more "cinematic", but I nearly got motion sick a couple times from it. Moving the camera around that much (without any player control over it, mind you) seemed like a really weird choice to me.

Regarding the puzzles, while many of them were really satisfying and fun to figure out, many of the late-game optional puzzles reminded me a little TOO much of Fez's late-game puzzles, in more ways than one. That ended up feeling like a bit of a let-down, because I just felt like I'd done this kind of thing before. I'd normally recommend not worrying about going for 100% completion, but if you don't want to be stuck with the "bad ending", you basically have to. So...don't be afraid to look up hints when necessary, I suppose.

Overall I had a great time for most of it, but these things took some of the shine off by the end for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short: This game is fantastic, and would recommend it wholeheartedly, IF NOT for two specific long-term puzzles that sour the 'Achieve the Best Ending' experience.

I really dig the combat. It has a 'Dark Souls, but cute fox' vibe that I found really endearing. You even inherit the Etsus flask mechanic, and the 'restore souls on previous death-spot' mechanic. Exploring the world is fun, the boss battles are challenging, and backtracking with new skills is fun. I enjoyed my time with this game... Up until trying to achieve the Best ending.

See, to achieve it, there are two major puzzles to solve: The Fairy gathering, and the Mountain Door puzzle. Both of which lock you out of requirements for the best ending. And both share the same flaw: Unreliable inputs. You have a few 'spells' and puzzles/doors that activate by inputting patterns in the D-pad. However, said process has NO FEEDBACK. And ocasionally, you may go through one of these without the desired effect triggering, even if your input was pixel-perfect.

Regarding the first long-term puzzle: Trying to find the fairies with an unreliably-activating Gathering Spell on your own (no guides online), while needing to find at least half of the fairy-spots distributed in the world just to unlock one of the last pages of the manual required for the Mountain Door Puzzle makes this more of a chore than a puzzle.

Regarding the second long-term puzzle: Just outright what the ♥♥♥♥? That's about 99 inputs (I counted) with no feedback and said unreliable input. And mapping them out from the manual not only requires near-full completion, but the Golden Paths are unclear in most of the puzzles. In particular, the one that requires you to load another save-file to find out, I think it was Page 13 or something, is so inmersion-breaking that makes the puzzle unsolvable for anyone on a non-meta-knowledge playthrough.

You may enjoy this game if you just go through it blindly, and don't care about 100% too much . But if looking up guides is not up your alley, the Best Ending will sour your experience of this game. It certainly tainted mine, while checking the guide for the Mountain Door puzzle.\t\t\t\t\t
