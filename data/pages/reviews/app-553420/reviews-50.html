\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCertamente um dos melhores jogos que eu já joguei.

Jogo absurdamente lindo (de longe o indie mais bonito que eu já vi e supera a maioria dos AAA também), gameplay e ost ótimas, exploração e ambientação excepcionais, progressão comparável a Hollow Knight (você começa paia e termina como um deus do combate) e na minha opinião tem o tamanho ideal considerando que eu demorei mais ou menos 14 horas pra zerar e pegar o final secreto e eu saí extremamente satisfeito, diferentemente de outros jogos que é maçante pegar 100% (novamente Hollow Knight como exemplo).
A única reclamação que eu tenho são os spikes de dificuldade em algumas das bossfights.

E o mais foda são os segredos, eu raramente vou atrás de fazer muito conteúdo extra em um jogo, mas esse jogo me intrigou a ponto de me fazer ir atrás de TODOS os segredos.

Esse jogo é um exemplo de criatividade e ingenuidade e mostra o quão insanamente brabos os jogos podem ser.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have many fond memories as a kid playing Zelda: Oracle of Seasons, Oracle of Ages, and Link's Awakening on my Gameboy Color. After starting Tunic these memories immediately came flooding back.

Tunic takes the Zelda formula and not only executes it, but improves upon it. Combat and movement are clean and crisp. The music and atmosphere were two of my favorite aspects of Tunic. The artistic design is breathtaking. I especially loved the in game guidebook idea. There was absolutely no handholding for Tunic: you were given all the information that you need to solve the games many puzzles. This is something that many games today are lacking.

That said, towards the end of my playthrough I came to the bitter realization that I lack the patience I once had as a kid. Technically everything you need to know is explained in the guidebook, but some of the more nuanced details can be difficult to figure out. Without spoiling anything, I got stuck trying to find a specific key item, which completely halted my game progression for some time. Because I couldn't figure it out using the guidebook, I looked it up online. This was a big mistake, as doing so immediately killed my immersion within the game. The entire point of the guidebook is to find things out yourself, which prior to this point provided a great feeling of accomplishment figuring things out on my own. While this was mostly my own fault, I would argue that the description of how to get said item (or even the existence of said item) was not entirely clear from the guidebook. General ordering/progression could have been made more clear.

Overall I would give Tunic a high rating, but it is far from perfect. With some minor improvements Tunic has the opportunity to be a truly incredible game. I sincerely hope they decide to make a sequel!\t\t\t\t\t
