To further the jank it has a parry that is bound to the same button you use to block you just tap it instead of hold and the parry takes about as long if not longer to complete as a great weapons parry in dark souls 2. I got past the forest guardian boss and made it up to this giant spider crap like boss but did I feel like it was a test of my skills up to this point; no. I also didn't see any classic kind of zelda puzzles just figure out hidden information stuff or walk around the isometric view to find a hidden area. Granted this was only in my 2 - 3 hours of gameplay but if there were any it would've been nice to see them earlier so I could justify keeping the game for the puzzles and art. Really wanted to like this, the instruction booklet is so cute and well done but man am I tired of every game trying to be dark souls when there's more gameplay options that could flow better\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, this might live in my top 10 games of all time that I've personally played. At first glance, it's like a old school zelda game, but better in most aspects. There's combat, exploration, puzzle solving, atmosphere, lore and story telling. Each level is well crafted, and the story has you traveling back to certain locations previously visited, but with new skills and weapons in your arsenal, it's a fresh feeling. I can feel the love and effort that the team put into this game, and this indie game puts many triple a games to shame.

If you adore games like Zelda, Myst, Outer Wilds, and Hollow Knight, I highly highly recommend this game. The only thing that could've been done better I think, is the soundtrack (not the background or atmospheric music, that was fantastic, but it doesn't have something memorable that one can hum to).

If you hate puzzle games or don't like to think things out, this game may not be for you. Go into this game spoiler free, don't look anything up, just enjoy the story.

9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a 10/10. No jokes. As people have said, go in blind. All I knew going in is the game borrows heavily from Zelda and Dark Souls, and that one of the "gimmicks" is learning how to play as you go, no hand-holding.

What most don't share however is the feeling of genuine discovery in this game feels surpassed only by Elden Ring. Every reward is a discovery, and every discovery feels like a reward. The game takes you in so many different directions. You go right, left, up, and down. And I'm talking about the GAME, not simply the story.

And the combat and difficulty isn't even the selling point like in a Souls game. By all means, if you're having trouble, turn the difficulty down (it's an option). The game to me isn't based around its difficulty or hardship, its based around the feeling of discovery. Do what you need to in order to enjoy your experience, because there will be so many discoveries to enjoy. The game has a wonderful habit of showing you something that was always there but still blowing your mind.

There's so much more I want to say, but in good conscience I can't. You have to experience this yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is definitely one of my favourite games I've played. (btw, this review is spoiler-free)

Most of the text in Tunic is written in an illegible script. The gimmick of this game is an absolutely gorgeously-illustrated in-game manual which you use to make sense of it, collecting pages as you play. It perfectly captures the feeling of buying a foreign game; if you've ever been abroad and enjoyed translating street signs, menus etc. then this game will be right up your alley.

Contrary to what some other users say here, the game mechanics, enemies are solid and the level design clearly had a lot of thought put into it. The game's so polished in fact, if you told me "Nintendo made this" I wouldn't question you. There's just so much charm and love in this game, I can't recommend it enough.\t\t\t\t\t
