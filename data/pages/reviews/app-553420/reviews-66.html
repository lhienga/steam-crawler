\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhilst I can see why people like this game, given my own experience of the game so far, I can't help but feel that 92% positive almost seems like those 9/10 or 10/10 reviews often seen from games journalists. So far, I'm a 4 bosses in and there's not much I can think of that lends to an overall positive experience. To me it seems like a lot of people just like that this game is going back to how old games used to do things. Lots of reviews detail why they think the game is great, so I'm going to go through my gripes with the game. This is really one of those games where I feel like it deserves something in-between a positive and a negative review. If I were to put a number to it, it would probably be something like a 7/10 (on an actual scale out of 10, not a the games journalist scale which only uses the top third). I'd only recommend it if you're looking for this specific kind of game or you can take the gamble when it's on sale for a lower price than it is currently (30% off, ¬£17.49). Jump to the last paragraph for a TLDR.

The exploration isn't particularly fun. Nothing that other games haven't already done but with some overdone aspects. Lots of things are hidden from view of the camera, so you just end up sticking to walls all the time to find all the hidden alleys and rooms. Having to do this everywhere isn't particularly fun and dulls down most "aha" moments due to the many things that are hidden this way. To me, this kinda of exploration isn't all that fun. It's exploration turned into busywork, especially when a lot of the rewards for doing it are just money and the occasional consumable. The mystique and intrigue that Zelda dungeons create is quite absent from this game and the bosses don't really do anything to balance that out.

The puzzle element is mysteriously absent from this game, despite being tagged as so, unless the dev's idea of a puzzle is trawling through an area just to flip a switch. This game is as much a puzzle game as dark souls is if you consider opening doors from the other side or knocking down a ladder is a puzzle element. I haven't played through the whole game, so maybe all the actual puzzles are later, which is a failing of a game aiming to be and is lauded as a puzzle game.

Of the bosses I've encountered, only 1 really felt all that well designed with the others having major issues with the camera that make focusing or moving accurately far more difficult than it should be. Imagine trying to beat a group of enemies whilst your camera is spinning just so it can keep the boss on the same place in view. You have no control of the camera throughout the game and it's just for the cinematic aesthetic and hiding things out of sight. I'd also recommend turning off depth of field for at least the boss fights. The effect is way overdone and often leaved the boss and the environment blurry unless you're up in its face.

The combat overall doesn't feel particularly great. It's relatively basic and of a similar vein to that of Zelda but they've tried to make it more challenging. Personally, I don't find it all that interesting or challenging after you get to grips with the general feel. There's not anything fundamentally flawed about it, just nothing that makes it something I actively want to do more of. Most encounters practically can be attempted the same way with a handful of enemies needing something other than your sword and shield to initiate. I've enjoyed a number of Zelda games, but so far, this game has too much of an emphasis on combat without making it interesting. The game forces you to do things different very infrequently. It's only when you get to boss fights do you actually have to use your brain in a meaningful way. Where in many other games, the encounters you have leading up to a boss will teach you things that are useful when facing the boss. In this game, every boss (of the ones I've faced) have been completely blind and will be the first time you actually need to use core mechanics. To me, this means a significant portion of the real challenge in the boss fights is in learning the mechanics in the middle of a boss fight (or relearning them because you haven't had to use them in a long time). This only creates needless frustration and means the devs could make simpler bosses with the illusion of difficulty.

The game takes an old school approach to tutorials in this game, relying on a manual to communicate mechanics, objectives and the like, but the manual is built into the game and you have to collect the pages as you play. The execution of this is mostly well done. You get more pages as you explore. It's one of the few actually meaningful rewards you get for exploring outside of upgrades and equipment. That's not to say it's flawless. At the start of the game, you're given very little direction, aside from a few pages in the manual. This manual is mostly written using pictures and a cryptic unreadable language with the occasional interpretable thing or english word. There's a lot of guesswork that goes into understanding what the manual is going on about and on a number of occasions, I only understood the actual point until I had already done the thing, practically making portions of the manual redundant.

The game is also specifically advertises its soundtrack as a point to buy the game. I love a good soundtrack. A good soundtrack can be the cherry on top in a truly awesome game or can save an average game from too many bad reviews. Hollow Knight, Vermintide 1 and 2, Link's Awakening, and Octopath Traveler are a few games that come to mind with great soundtracks. This game's soundtrack is a bit of a let down without even considering those prior mentioned games. It's just okay. It's a bit of background music. It doesn't do all that much to set and maintain the atmosphere. It is truly just an accessory to accompany the rest of the game. It is not a soundtrack that is worth my money.

All of this is to say that in my experience, there isn't much to cry home about. It relies on an old school way of designing games which is absent from modern game design and that alone seems to be one of the major features that people love about the game. I think it has plenty of flaws and is far from a perfect game, but people are willing to overlook that because of the different design philosophy used in this game. There's very little hand holding. All the gameplay is reliant on exploration and using your brain when you're stuck. With all that said, I am mostly enjoying the game. It's not amazing but I am entertained and I don't feel like I'm wasting my time most of the time. If what I've said above doesn't sound all that bad to you and you want an exploration/combat game with very little hand holding and some occasional difficulty, then it's probably the game for you. I'd still recommend waiting for a sale though (larger than the 30% at the time of this review).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA love letter to old games, developers, and dedicated players.
This game is hard, like really hard. The combat can be frustrating, the puzzles can take multiple hours, and some bosses will make you scream but the game is even more rewarding. An extremely well designed game that makes you figure everything out by yourself while giving you the help you need.
I had to look up a lot of this game and I know that hindered my experience but it may have taken me 3-4x the amount of time to figure some of things out. The only thing I don't like about this game is that you can feel so lost at times and have almost nothing to help you out in the game. Having to open the map every single time and flip to the right page is annoying at times but the pages of the guide are amazingly done.
Get this game if you love puzzles, fighting, Zelda, and a challenge.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan I love this game. On first glance, it appears to be an isometric Zelda clone where you play as Fox Link, and it is, but it's so much more. The game is just so smartly designed.

You start off on the beach with no items, no direction, no story, no nothing. It's up to you to find out where to go, what to do, how to win, and how deep you want to delve into the world. It completely respects the intelligence of the player and spoon feeds you very, very little.

The game most resembles Zelda 1 of all things. You know, the one you couldn't beat without reading the instruction manual? Well, this game also has an instruction manual, that you collect the pages of in game. It details the maps of areas, how to play, the controls, the steps you need to take to complete the main game, and all the secrets.

Except for one thing. The game isn't in English. It's in Tunicese. If you've ever had to play an unlocalized game from Japan, like if, say, some mediocre publisher never localized it and you had to use some bootleg ROM (*coughSuperRobotWars*) then you know that maybe 10% of the text and interface would be in English, and with enough intuition and trial and error you could figure out how to play the game.

I was not expecting this game to so accurately reflect such an experience but it does, gloriously. By putting together snippets of readable text, along with the pictures and things you notice in game, you can figure out almost everything. The game relies on it, because while you also have your standard Zelda items that unlock ability to traverse the map in ways you couldn't before (hook shot, etc) many times you had the ability inside you all along - you just didn't KNOW it yet. Shortcuts that you could have always used were hidden just out of sight and you'll be startled you never noticed they were there.

So when you finally figure out how to perform some new skill it completely recontextualizes a lot of the things you've seen in the game so far. Knowledge is just as powerful as some new magic item. It reminds me a lot of The Outer Wilds and it's just so well done in this game.

You figure out the secrets, you unravel the story, you earn every breakthrough. Or you could look it up online, but really that ruins the fun. There's a few optional puzzles that are sketchy logicwise that I had to check solutions for but the vast majority of things can be figured out just by paying attention and keen observation. It's so enjoyable. This game is a gem.\t\t\t\t\t
