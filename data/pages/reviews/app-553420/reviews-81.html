\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was not for me.

I have friends who like it and I honestly cannot imagine why.

The controls are sluggish. The character movement speed is tediously slow. Weapon swing distances are very unclear (which exacerbates some distance gauging issues caused by the fixed isometric camera. Enemies are very fast. Dodge rolling does not animation cancel attacks. The upshot of this is you plod from one place to another and basically only fight on the back foot, prioritizing defense over offense. In a game about exploration, defense = stasis and offense = progress. Most chests are full of money and an hour and a half into the game I'd yet to find anywhere to spend that money, so opening chests just feels bad. Speaking of feels bad, 90% of the text in the game is gibberish. Found something new? Cool! What's it do? No idea. Try it out! Oh, so that's what it is! Now you're out of them. Wasn't that fun?

It's like someone said "I wonder if we can make something that's very much like old school Legend of Zelda but worse in every conceivable way."\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I quite liked this game. It's short and sweet and well polished. Feels like a very efficient game - almost everything is important in some way, and nothing is ever wasted. The cryptic nature can be love it or hate it, but I loved it up until like the last 2-3 things I couldn't figure out.

Having played it up until near 100% completion, with a fewer of the more out-of-the way achievements left, I can say this is the first time in a while since I've played through this much of the game. Helps that it's short and tightly packed all the way through.

THE GOOD:
The art style is lovely - both for the game itself and the accompanying documents.
90% of the puzzles are fun to solve, even if most use the they mechanic.
The combat feels fun. even if very simple. Bosses were pretty satisfying to beat, although often cheeseable.
Music and Sound Design is great.
Progression is tangible, which is nice.
This aesthetic is incredibly enjoyable.

THE BAD:
10% of the puzzles can range from annoying to frustrating. I feel like this would depend heavily on your personality though. I feel as if past me would have enjoyed these way more than present me.

Overall, I would say this falls at around a 7.5-8/10. If you like puzzles, play this game. If you like exploration, consider playing the game. This is a game for people who like to connect the dots between somewhat vague clues with a side of fun adventure gameplay.

(NES/Sega/PS1). Even as a foreign to this "manual-driven-tutorials" I was captivated completely and enthralled to do my best to find all secrets.

I was not able to find every secret or the main ones on my own due to lack of time, but if you are a gamer with 30 to 40 hours to spare, it should be more than enough to crack everything up.

A MUST-HAVE for anyone who likes puzzles/Zelda-like games. Combat can be tuned down for easier approach-ability anyways.\t\t\t\t\t
