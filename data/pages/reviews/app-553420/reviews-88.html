\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is definitely a game for the secret lover. On my playthrough, I have failed to discover even half of the hidden small things here and there, because I simply didn't care to look for the extra stuff, and that made the manual hunting mechanic fairly pointless as well. However, that just means I have something to come back to, however minor.

Putting that aside, it's a good game! It's really pretty and the gameplay is mostly fun, definitely feels like its inspiration and perhaps even surpasses it, so if that's what you're here for you're probably going to enjoy your time with TUNIC.

Secrets aside, the game is pretty short, especially once you learn the map, which lends itself to replayability as well. The NG+ is a mix of "less difficult" and "more difficult" that I haven't really seen done before.

Do beware of the difficulty "curve" for this game though, as it is brutal, and spikes severely in certain places. There is both an easy mode and an immortality toggle, so it can be bypassed, but it may feel cheap to do so to some. This is perhaps the sole aspect of the game which I feel needs serious improvements.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTunic is love letter to Zelda like games. It is a isometric action adventure with difficult encounters. However, Tunic is also a puzzle game where you collect the pages of the game manual to solve puzzles. It uses this mechanic very cleverly, as there are notes on the pages left by an unknown brother like figure.

Tunic was overall a very delightful experience that made me wonder what was going to happen next. It is a game where you learn more as you play, and each new mechanic you discover feels fresh and rewarding. It isn't very in your face, and it expects some deductive reasoning from the player part. The puzzles come in varying degree, and the ones that are too difficult are left as side content, which I really appreciated. The puzzles also require no combat capabilities, which give a nice break between fights. The visuals are also pretty and cutesy which create a contrast between the brutal battles.

Personally, I would recommend this game to people who like puzzles and also like or don't mind the challenging combat. It has a very nice twist on an already established genre, and overall, a very fun game.

Also the holy cross is not an item, please don't make the same mistake I did and don't try and look for it in game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have a lot of mixed feelings about this game. If I could give it a "meh" recommendation I would. If you read the reviews on this game you'll notice a trend, often there are complaints of puzzles or directions being cryptic or obtuse. I'd agree that this is true, but this game is so perfectly crafted in that way that it HAS to be a very intentional design choice. The creator of this game very much intended this game to be the way it is. For better or for worse.

I think, ultimately, that is what makes this game hard to recommend for me. This is not a game for everyone. There were at least two points in this game where I understood what the game wanted me to do, and I knew what obtaining the solution was going to require. The problem is I just actively didn't want to do it. It wasn't going to be fun for me. I pushed through anyway to see if I was underestimating the fun factor for these roadblocks, but, sadly, I was not. Getting the "good ending" was neat, but I'm not sure I enjoyed myself the whole way through.

There's a lot of really neat ideas in this game. I so very desperately wanted to like it more than I did. But when I'm actively debating just googling the answer to a puzzle because the act of actually going about finding the parts to solve it (not UNDERSTANDING how to solve it, but the actual part where you put the footwork in to find what you need) well... I'm not sure I'm having fun at that point. I think that's when you've lost me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a lovely game, do buy it and try it out for yourself; it feels very much like a loving tribute to The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past.

The premise to this game is simple and straight-forward. You start off with minimal gear and set out to get more and more useful things while exploring the world. Often, you have to retrace your steps when you get new items that let you traverse the world in different ways. Enemies have attack patterns that you're encouraged to memorize and patiently whittle them down. You can't button mash your way to victory here.

It's brought back how it felt to me when I had first played Link to the past and I've been wonderfully exploring this game ever since. I can't recommend it enough to retro gamers or those who are in a nostalgic mood, but are curious to see what a modern update might look like.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal Score: 78/100

I give this a "Yes" with a caveat: if you're craving quality Souls-like gameplay, you should probably look elsewhere. While TUNIC may tick some basic checkmarks of Soulsborne gameplay, on the whole it doesn't do it well, and to frustrating effect.

That said, the Zelda-like gameplay is delightful, albeit a little slow to unfold in a compelling way. The innovation of the manual and the depth of its secrets will keep you hooked, and seeking all of this game's secrets is satisfying in a way that no other Zelda-like of which I can think has ever achieved.\t\t\t\t\t
