\r\n\t\t\t\t\tArt and atmosphere are great. I actually enjoyed the combat a lot and it becomes very rewarding in the second half of the game, but I didn't like at all how the exploration was designed. Trying to find a specific area or item that's hidden somewhere on the map forces the player to run around and backtrack through a complex isometric view which purposely hides many elements from the player. Navigating the overworld from one point to another, even if they're within eye distance of each other, never felt easy at any point as you would need to take convoluted paths in order to get anywhere.

I didn't enjoy how the puzzles in the later section of the game were presented. For getting the "good" ending, a multitude of puzzles need to be solved but there is little feedback as to whether you're close to getting a puzzle correct or if you're completely off. I didn't feel like my time was being respected in this manner, but I would still recommend the game due to the artstyle, atmosphere, tight controls, and mostly great boss fights.\t\t\t\t\t
