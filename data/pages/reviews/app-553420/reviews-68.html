\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very well put together game that mixes 2D Zelda with Souls-like combat. Tunic makes for a great combination of both sides, creating what it would be for a Zelda game to have that kind of combat. The combat is a lot simpler than most souls-likes but you still got the essentials, being the dodge roll and health potions. The combat being simple doesn't mean that the fights were easy though. Many fights had me stuck dying over and over but I was still able to get through. The boss fights were all very enjoyable (even though I fought one of the later ones earlier than I probably should've). The puzzles were also pretty interesting, with all of the obelisks and how you needed to get to them to open the big door. Especially the puzzle to open the mountain door, I thought that puzzle was so interesting I didn't mind that it took me a while to complete it because it was honestly that good. The enclosed instruction book is also a very cool part of the game. How they were able to make it actually feel like an instruction booklet with pen marks, coffee stains, but also how they were able to still integrate it into the game is what makes this game extra special. Definitely recommend if you'd like a game that's similar to LOZ: A Link to the Past in a sense.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know why everyone keeps comparing it to A Link to the Past. It's much closer to to Link's Awakening. Especially in that almost all the puzzles are necessary to progress(so far). The difficulty is about on par with it as well.

It's basically furry Link's Awakening if you turned it into a souls like game. You are playing most of the play style of the first Dark Souls with the item and dungeon layout of a Zelda game. It's fun, you should try it. Nintendo couldn't have done a better job than this if they tried. In fact, I doubt they'd ever make another game on par with this. Nintendo games have the annoying kind of frustration as it is just not fair at times. This gives you every opportunity to learn and never makes you feel robbed by the mechanics. That being said, it *does* like to surprise you.\t\t\t\t\t
