\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough calling "Tunic" an in-depth metroidvania from an isometric perspective is correct, it doesn't properly convey what an adventure this game was.
More than just beating bosses to unlock gameplay mechanics, "Tunic" also unlocks gameplay mechanics by awarding players with pages of a manual — one that's written almost entirely in a fictitious language accompanied by colourful symbols and illustrations. It is then up to you, the player, to give your best guess in interpreting the pages because these pages hold the secrets to abilities you've had ever since the beginning — you simply hadn't known yet.

In a literal sense, knowledge is power.
More than collecting items that add abilities to your arsenal, more than testing your technical skill with the controls, "Tunic" is a game where your curiosity in exploration and intuition in cryptic data are rewarded with power. More so than most metroidvanias, knowledge makes you more powerful.
That feeling of acquiring knowledge gives a potent shot of exhilaration surpassed only by another knowledge-based game I've played — The Outer Wilds.

I strongly recommend playing Tunic.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished the "True Ending" and wow. This is one of these rare gems you find. This game easily pushed down a lot of other titles in my top 5 all time.

Mix of Zelda and Dark Souls, with extremely well designed progression. I mean you'll have a bunch of "Oh wow I had access to that all along" moments where you'll re-evaluate everywhere you've been through with that new knowledge in hand. Said knowledge from figuring out the manual pages you'll collect over the world. It respects the player's wits and intelligence to figure it out and it's hella refreshing.

No hand-holding here, so be warned. Might be a little tough for casual gamers combat-wise, but there are many difficulty options to suit your needs. The charm of game really resides in it's intuitive combat, fun puzzles, charming graphics, great OST, and best pacing (up until figuring out the true ending vs bad ending) I've seen in a while.

Please do yourself a favour, get this game, play it blind (couch co-op is best, as you'll get stuck less often with 2 brains), and enjoy the ride.

Wild props to the team for bringing a game that has given me many hours of very-high quality entertainment not seen by many AAA releases lately\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best secrets-oriented games I've ever played. I was extatic, on the edge of my seat, the entire time. Accessibility options means I didn't even have to care about the more boring/frustrating aspects of the fighting system and could just focus on solving mysteries. and boy is there a lot of that to do. The hints toward the greater mystery are wery well-distributed, just subtle enough to not appear to you until you are looking for them and, outside of a couple of the very last (not required) ones, give out just enough info to make the discovery feel organic and natural. If you liked going beyond the normal final boss - credits sequence in games like FEZ or Anodyne, if you like taking notes and screenshots and racking your brain over cryptic symbols, then this is one of the best to ever do it.
