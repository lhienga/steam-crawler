\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTunic has just about everything that I look for in a classic adventure game- tight controls, mysterious lore, vibrant visuals, and oozing with soul. The second the camera zoomed in on that little fox and the beautiful music started playing, I could instantly feel the game was going to be one to remember. I'd be lying if I said the nostalgic hook was the only thing that kept me playing, but the game has so much more to offer than just "cute little guy with a sword". The OST alone would be enough to sell it to me- it really carries you through each area and matched every one's vibe perfectly.

Echoing what some others have said, I will agree that the gameplay, while simple, is very "soulslike". I played on the normal difficulty and died dozens of times to each boss until muscle memory kicked in, then it was secondhand nature and at that point the combat got really fun. The consequence for death isn't much of a setback though, but still- go in with the mindset that you'll be learning the mechanics by dying. A lot.

Above all else however, the one thing that kept my absolutely hooked was the overarching meta "manual" collection quest, where you come across little pages of an actual game manual that subsequently also doubles as an in-game strategy guide. The more I collected, the more immersed I felt, and the more it took me back to feeling like a kid literally looking through the guides of my old favorite games. It's a very unique and charming implementation of directing the player, without being overtly direct or holding your hand (which Tunic does pretty much none of), and towards the end, I felt like a madman mulling meticulously over each page over and over again for hints to the final puzzles. And if you're like me and couldn't get enough of the manual aspect, you probably wanted a physical version (which are becoming a reality and up for preorder!)

Overall a truly amazing experience that I find my review can't do any justice- the only way is to pick this up and play it yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game to you as it is the best solo-player game I have ever played. The secrets within are hidden so that even on your fourth play-through you find something you never noticed before. The storytelling is environmental, so instead of being told what to do, it is up to you to figure it out. The combat is very satisfying to learn, and the better you become the easier it is to fight the enemies, especially as you learn new techniques. If you find the combat too hard, you can reduce the difficulty via the settings, or even turn on invincibility. The music and sound effects are just as well designed as the rest of the game is, and really complement the art style. I have played for over 90 hours and still find it fun and interesting to play, as well as being worth the money I spent on it.\t\t\t\t\t
