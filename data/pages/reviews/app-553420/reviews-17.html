What I'm getting: A cute adventure game with a clever find-the-pages-of-the-manual mechanic. It'll make you say things like: "Could I have done that the whole time?" and "OH NO, OH HECK! WHY IS IT FULL OF PURPLEâ€½"

Post-game review update:
Part of what makes this game so good is that it drip-feeds you information about itself, and you as the player have to puzzle it out yourself. As such I can't say much in a review, but if you liked post-game FEZ, where in you discover a fundamentally new way to interact with the game world to solve puzzles, you'll love late-/post-game TUNIC.

This game was just about perfect for me. Cute world, fun music, surprising and clever hidden mechanics, and fun language puzzles. It's one of the few games that I expect to 100%, and one of the few that I'll interact with outside of the game for a long time. (The runes are super fun!)

TUNIC is easily going into my list of favorite games.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thought this was gonna be an enjoyable game and for the first half, it was. It kinda reminds you of Zelda like games and exploring and going through the manual trying to figure out stuff was really fun. However, this is kinda like a dark souls game, and a bad one honestly. I get that the game is challenging but the difficulty in this game is right out unfair and just annoying. At one point in the game you encounter these black masses that if you get close to it your health bar starts draining, not your current HP, your health bar. It starts to shrink. But whatever, with upgrades is not a big problem, and eventually you find an item that nullifies its effect. But then the game throws at you enemies made out of this stuff, and when they hit you, your health bar shrinks, the item you found has no effect. Later in the game, you encounter complete invisible enemies and also other enemies that have this effect of draining your health bar.

When it comes to controls they're ok. Not the gratest not the worst. The parrying on the other hand is awful. Is not like other games where if you time your block properly you parry an attack. Here your parry has a delay, and every enemy attack is different so it's hard to time your parry correctly. By this point I was annoyed by the game so I wasn't paying attention to the manual which you are supposed to read in order to figure out stuff (kinda) so sometimes I didn't know where to go. The game became a chore so I stopped playing it.

If you like very challenging games, maybe this would be ok and even easy for you, but if you think this is some casual exploring zelda like game and you are not to used to hard games then you won't like this. I do like my fair share of hard and challenging games, but for me this game was just annoyingly difficult. So it's a pass for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't have the patience for this game.

The Manual in Jibberish gimmick was just whatever, it ranged from tutorial messages to near useless.
The gameplay was, fine? Responsive enough, Certain Hitboxes are deceptively longer lasting to stop you just i-framing through everything. The Card patches that the player has to find coins to expand capacity was a neat idea, I didn't use most of the ones I found. Certain cards are actively detrimental for very little benefit, so after getting 3 slots I didn't really bother with it from that point onwards.
You have a parry that I didn't find the page for or the game never explained, far too slow to be effective. For any attack faster than Elden Ring, you effectively do not have the option. For example, there's an enemy with a straight thrust, if you don't parry as soon as you see the animation start, you'll get hit.
The game doesn't have an item to return to the last checkpoint, so if you're exploring a large area with no teleporter, you're stuck walking.

I gained no satisfaction from finding a new page, I'd get a page that told me how to do something and my only reaction would be "Huh." I wasn't excited to return to old areas, or even get to the next section of the game. The map, should you have that page, shows you were you are at all times. Some maps are better than others, but the areas with poor maps aren't so expansive or esoteric that you need a map.

I finished the game, got the now standard replace the final boss bad ending for Souls-like games, and I have no desire to go back. Outside of Visual presentation which has clearly had a lot of effort put into it, I can't say this game reaches any height beyond mediocre. If you have nostalgia for the SNES era of gaming, or are a fan of classic Zeldas, You may get something out of this. The Price and Mediocrity of the game play and story stops it getting a recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
