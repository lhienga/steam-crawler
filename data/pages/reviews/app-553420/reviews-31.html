\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFew games really grab me any more, and this one did. It obviously takes inspiration from Zelda, which is what piqued my interest in the first place, but there is also plenty of game mechanics which reminded me of Dark Souls and The Witness (save the latter's irritating postmodern ending)... both in terms of the game's difficulty and the satisfaction of improving your character and also the secrets & lore you discover along the way which were hidden in plain sight. It should go without saying, but try to avoid spoilers!

I also really appreciated the built-in difficulty adjustments including a "no-fail mode" which, if I were younger and had more time I would probably have avoided using, but for those times when I just wanted to experience more of the story & puzzles without getting stuck, it came in super handy and I recommend not being ashamed of using it if needed. The game has some *very* difficult moments, at least from my perspective... but, as they say, none of the challenge felt unfair.

On top of the mechanics, the beautiful visuals, unique tilt-shift art style, and the amazing soundtrack make for an all-around unforgettable experience. Highly recommended!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game starts out as a pretty decent zelda/souls-like experience. The main issue I have with the game is that it becomes a complete slog near the end, where the game asks you to seek out secrets and solve some puzzles that aren't necessarily difficult to understand, but take ages to actually find the answer to, which take the form of extremely long button sequences.

While I can see the appeal of this game for people who enjoy its elements. I think each of these elements is serviceable at best. Combat is sluggish and not particularly deep. Bosses vary in quality. The manual mechanic is inventive, but a way to translate it would've been appreciated. Being unsure how to progress will happen, and you'll look through the manual for answers hoping you didn't miss the page telling you the answer somewhere along the way. Making the ordeal frustrating at times.

Still, I can see why people would enjoy a game like this. Still, I don't think it's worth the asking price and would wait for a deep sale.\t\t\t\t\t
