I finished this game half a year ago and I still think about it sometimes.

Recreates the vibe of playing games as a young kid--you can barely understand one in ten words, fighting anything is super hard, and you desperately want someone to give you hints.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the biggest gaming disappointments of my life.
Incredible atmosphere and aesthetics with some mind blowing puzzles.
Unfortunately this game is HEAVILY bogged down by its over reliance on an outdated combat design style that feels very unpolished. Combine that with the games' insistence to be somewhat of a souls-like and it comes out feeling like some sort of bizarre amalgam of ideas rather than a focused project. I definitely think that the 'Majora's Mask' type of combat could work but the lock on mechanic is straight up terrible. Most enemies (including most bosses) can be bullied into oblivion with ease, although some require precise strikes which does not blend with the clunky Zelda-like swordplay. Overall it's an okay game, most of the positive reviews are here however because of the nostalgia factor or the admittedly great vibes.\t\t\t\t\t

WARNING:
This game has 2 very different aspects to it: A beautiful and enthralling puzzle game, and an atrocious and overly punishing action rpg.
I very much want to recommend the puzzle game but in order to enjoy it you need to endure the combat, so take this into account before buying.

Combat is extremely basic and janky, with no input buffering, no animation cancelling, terrible hitbox size and timing, and the cheesiest AI possible.
To make it all worse, the dev decided for some reason to also make it VERY unforgiving, which means fights turn into a central part of the game where you will spend a long (bad) time.

The puzzle part is a very clear homage to Fez, but does enough to stand on its own. The game gives the information in very clever ways for you to piece together the solution to the world's mysteries.
It has perspective puzzles, some cool eureka moments, a full cypher language to translate (optional) and more. Unlike the combat part, it's clear that the devs knew what they were doing. It's very well crafted to great detail. My only gripe is that it relies too much on perspective gimmicks, which forces you to try to constantly wiggle your character in obscured areas in search for secret doors.

In summary very hard for me to rate this game since it has a half that I love and a half that I hate. The whole also suffers because of the stylistic clash of the chill/mellow mystery solving and the harsh/unpolished/hostile action segments.

If you love puzzle games and can tolerate some (a lot) of senseless souls-esque BS, give it a try on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
