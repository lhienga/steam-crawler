\r\n\t\t\t\t\tit was an amazing game up to a point. score 7/10

For me, the stamina feature doesn't belong in this game, it gets in the way, it's annoying, it makes the game unnecessarily difficult, luckily the devs decided to put an option where you can turn this off, so this pretty much saved this game for me... after that it became a great game until.... you reach an area of the game in its final phase and they take away your upgrades, so already 2 of my least favorite design choices in this game. 1. giving an ability and punishing you for using it, which is the stamina system that punishes you for rolling, and 2. taking away everything you worked for during the game just to make it unnecessarily difficult at the end.

The puzzle part is amazing... IF you like puzzle solving because these are one of the most crazy puzzles you will find in any game, and quite frankly I don't even think they are worth solving, you need to solve them to get the true ending, at least, some of end, but the true ending is so disappointing that I am glad I didn't care for solving the puzzles.

In the end this is a zelda-dark soul puzzle inspired game, I would of liked it did 1 thing right instead of doing 3 ok-ish. 11 hours is the most I could invest in this game after that I lost interested and just went God mode and got the bad ending. Still I recommend it, because there are a lot of ejoyable parts in it, so maaaaybe you will like it, I recommend watching some reviews on youtube so you get a better perspective and decide if this is a game for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an amazing quest, ok-ish platformer, and a questionable souls-like.
1. Collecting pages of the game manual and deciphering them to progress further and to eventually finish the game is a wonderful and unique experience. It really brings you joys of a long journey full of discoveries. If you're into liguistics it's even possible to get understanding of Tunic's language without direct hints from the game :)
2. The isometric platformer part of this game is decent with some nice level design. Though the latter part of the playthrough is a bit less polished. After you get a *certain ability* it's not so rare to get stuck in random obscure places and then spend some time attempting to get out.
3. The combat system is what sometimes ruins the pleasant experience. Bosses can be tough, but as it turns out, some of them are surprisingly weak to stupid attack spamming. Also there's a number of common enemies which are designed to be quite annoying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was originally turned off by the heavy zelda inspiration, and thought it was just a zelda clone for people who dont have a switch, but its seriously unique. This game is incredible, and sets itself apart very well from Zelda games, and I honestly can't even think of a game to compare it to.

Do not look up any guides for it, however, as much of the fun of this game is all those little "aha!" Moments and surprises.

Things I liked about the game: the combat. It's very simple, yet very rewarding, and it's super responsive. Rarely will you ever die and yell "but I dodged that!" Or "my character wouldn't attack!" It's somewhat souls-like inspired, and kind of feels like if BOTW and dark souls had a baby.

The puzzles. The puzzles are super hard at times, but they are ALWAYS rewarding. It's a euphoric feeling to be stumped for half an hour, only to finally solve it and obtain a valuable item/piece of info. One thing this game does really well, is that the tutorials are these pages that you have to FIND to put it together YOURSELF, and the words are all gibberish! This makes you still have to use your brain to decipher these tutorials using just the pictures, context, and your mind.

It's also good to note that this game is very puzzle focused, so if you aren't a fan of being confused, don't get it. Most of this game is spent wondering what in the hell you're even supposed to be doing.

As for things I didn't like: the bosses weren't super difficult except for maybe three towards the end, however, this isn't a deal-breaker as all the bosses are fairly fun and rewarding, and the combat really isn't the main focus of the game. It's more like a tool to make the rewards granted from puzzles more exciting.

Overall, I'd say it's easily a landslide 10/10 for me. Not many games are as rewarding as this, and even fewer trust you like this.

If you are tired of games holding your hand, and treating you like a child, this is the cure.

10/10\t\t\t\t\t
