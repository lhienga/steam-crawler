\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has loved A Link to the Past since childhood, I was skeptical of all of the praise of this being a good successor. After playing it through and eating every puzzle it has to offer, the praise is quite warranted. The puzzles are more mature and complicated and there is less item progression compared to ALttP, however I do not feel the game suffers for it. It is not totally similar to ALttP and similar games but I feel that it really scratched the same itch of a million secrets and things to explore.

The combat is enjoyable and the bosses feel the right amount of difficult, however on keyboard re-focusing is a little cumbersome, but there is only one fight which benefits from doing it so it isn't a huge problem.

The story is the weak point of the game however that doesn't mean it is unenjoyable. It is merely a little simplistic compared to similar titles like Hollow Knight and I was never particularly drawn by the story, but then again I wasn't much drawn by ALttP's story either. I don't feel the lack of a great story detracts from the game experience and the story still has a couple of "Oh wow" or "Aww cute" moments which will stick with me.

The game is extremely rewarding for someone who loves devouring puzzles, returning to areas, noting little things that don't quite seem right, and watching everything come together in the end. You are given no direction, no instruction, and no goals by the game. This game will not hold your hand, not in the hardcore way, but in a way that makes you feel like you are able to do all things at your own pace and really makes you feel special each time you discover something new entirely on your own.

There is a lot going on in the puzzle department and a lot of areas to return to, so this game might be a bit tricky to come back to after putting it down for a month or something. You know its a good puzzle game when you get out the pencil and paper notebook, and this is one of those, so don't be afraid to write down stuff!

The manual adds a ton of charm to the game and everything feels lovingly crafted. I cannot recommend the game highly enough. I hope they will make something similar in the future. Thank you TUNIC team for your hard work and love!

If you think you may like this game. You will. Buy it. My greatest recommendation is to absolutely NOT look up anything about this game. If you are *really* stuck, have a friend look something up for you and tell you the piece you are missing. You can spoil so many good puzzles with the smallest idea or spoiler and there are so many layers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan, I was so looking forward to playing this game, but I was quite disappointed in the ned. Maybe I didn't do my homework first, but it had been described to me as, and the previews made it look like, a Zelda clone. But it really isn't. The basics are there, you have a sword, a shield, and a green tunic, but that's about it.
On it's own, the game is pretty challenging... until you get to the bosses. The bosses are incredibly difficult. I found myself spending more time trying to beat the bosses then playing the game. Yes, there's some of the "git gud" mentality to them, but even after learning their attacks and how to dodge them, it still mostly came down to luck. For example, most games with difficult bosses, once you learn their patterns, you can beat them pretty reliably. In Tunic, you can beat a boss one try, and not even get a single attack the next. Not only that, but the boss battles are incredibly boring.
The other thing that I disliked was the game instructions. Without giving away spoilers, there are no game instructions at the beginning. As you start to go through the game, you start getting some of those instructions, but at that point you don't need them. It was pretty interesting how the instructions tied into the puzzles though...
And speaking of puzzles, the one part I really loved about the game is the puzzles. They were very difficult, and I love that. The problem is, they are not advertised to you at all. I didn't even know I was looking for puzzles until I was looking up help on beating a boss. Then I had to slog my way back through the game looking for all of them.
All in all, it was a cute game, but I found the bosses too much of a pain, and there wasn't enough other stuff to keep the game fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTunic is one of the most enjoyable gaming experiences I've had in a while, and it is definitely the most special game I've seen so far. The thing that made it so special was the huge sense of discovery that you feel throughout the entire thing. From the start of the game you are given little to no (written or spoken) information about the world you are in, how to play, and what anything does. As you explore and make your way through the world, you find pages of an instruction manual that gives you information, but still is mostly in a constructed language. It is such an unconventional but really engaging way to tell a story and to introduce gameplay mechanics. Even up until the end, you will find things out that completely blow your mind and recontextualize earlier moments. It's hard to convey to someone who hasn't played it just how brilliant this design is. The combat of the game is also really engaging.
Another thing that really draws you in is the super charming artistic and audio/music direction. The graphics are really beautiful and the game runs perfectly (like seriously I didn't encounter a single bug or performance hitch), and the vibe and artstyle are something special.
I seriously recommend this to anybody who enjoys video games. 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first I thought the main inspiration was Zelda, then I thought it was Hollow Knight. Now I'm pretty sure the main inspiration is the Redwall book series. Specifically the riddles and secrets.

Solid top down fighting mechanics. Leans towards slow, uninterruptable actions. More like Curse of the Dead Gods than Hades.

The metroid-vania elements are really fun, and are kind of meshed into the heavy focus on secrets riddles in a really fun way. Lots of secret passageways and backdoors and multiple ways to get into any given area.

Art is absolutely beautiful. Sound design is solid. The game generally plays, looks, and sounds smooth as butter.

That said, the part of this game that sticks with you afterwards is the riddles and secrets and puzzles. They are tough as nails. There are generally different riddles you can focus on at any given time, but you will certainly spend some time pulling at your hair in frustration if you don't want to look at a walkthrough. The puzzles are very satisfying though, and I recommend sleeping on them rather than looking up answers. And always think outside the box.

Highly recommended, and it's my personal favorite game of the year.\t\t\t\t\t
