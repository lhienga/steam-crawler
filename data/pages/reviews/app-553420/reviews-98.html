\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMaybe one of my favorite games of the past few years. The combat is okay and the story is interesting, but honestly what really enraptured me were all of the secrets hidden all over this world. I was blown away as secret after secret after secret were revealed to me in ways that I never even imagined. The way the "manual" teaches you and drops hints for you and the way the world is structured around all of these little surprises. It was incredibly satisfying to uncover and solve all of these secrets. I went down a route that probably 90% of players sadly won't stay long enough to see, but I honestly enjoyed all of the secrets and puzzles even more than the gameplay. I had an overwhelming urge to solve everything I possibly could. I used a guide for a few of the late game secrets since.. they are absolutely NOT meant to be solved by a single person. I will not spoil much on that though. Go in as blind as you possibly can and, when you feel like you can't continue or do not want to muster the energy to solve things, whip out a guide and keep going. There's no shame in that if you'd like. I had an absolute blast through it all.

Also, my favorite puzzle in any game in existence, dubbed the "Golden Map" puzzle, is something I'll remember for a while. Slowly connecting the pieces in my mind and having a huge epiphany that this puzzle was giving you hints from the moment you stepped into the game, all culminating into this solution, was extraordinary. Truly a unique experience.

If you are masochistic 100%er and love to solve puzzles and uncover every nook and cranny no matter what it takes, and you like to be surprised by new things: THIS GAME IS FOR YOU.\t\t\t\t\t

SPOILERS: To get the good ending, for some reason Tunic decides the best course of action for the player is to suddenly become a worse version of The Witness with more cryptic solutions. It's completely antithetical to the original game design of being a nice 2D Zelda/Dark Souls fusion. My inputs were only working half the time for these puzzles (even though I got the logic behind all of them), and I just resorted to looking up the solutions so I wouldn't waste anymore time with faulty inputs with no sort of audio feedback for what I was doing.\t\t\t\t\t
