\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is spoiler-free. TUNIC is a game like no other, and I'd recommend everyone to pick it up and give it a chance. But before going more into detail, you should know that this is a knowledge based game, looking for guides or tutorials WILL take away from the experience.

TUNIC is all about the feeling of finding secrets, discovering things you didn't know of and that were possible, like mastering a fighting game by using secret movesets. It's a kind of language that you will learn to pick up, but it takes a while to get there, so be a bit patient and enjoy the other aspects of the game.

It is true that the game is hard and that it uses the Dark Souls principle of dying and trying until you get it right. It can be quite challenging in both difficulty and progression, but it is accessible enough for you to only focus on the things that you want to do, don't be ashamed to reduce the difficulty if it's too much, just enjoy the game at your own pace.

Exploration and problem solving is very Zelda-like, as in the first and original The Legend of Zelda. You are just thrown into the world, no objective, no cutscenes, no preludes, just you and the world that TUNIC offers. Most of the hints and guidance you will receive come from an special in-game item, and believe me, it really is your best and only ally.

To fully enjoy this game you need to be willing to get completely lost, you must accept that there will be times where you have absolutely no idea of where to go next or how to make progress. These are the main puzzles of TUNIC, finding the way forward.

The music in this game is amazing, it never gets repetitive and really helps a lot to define the style of the game. It may not be very orchestral or incredibly iconic like other games, but there's definitely a lot of tracks that stand out! Lifeformed and Janice Kwan really put something very special, and you can feel it.

I do admit it, there are times were some secrets are just a slap in the face, but must of the puzzles and secrets felt right and natural to solve. There are a couple of bugs here and there, but none of them are to the detrement of the game.

If you care about the lore and story of the game you might have to go a bit far to get the whole picture, and even then it's quite hard to make sense of it, but the story isn't really the point of this game.

If you couldn't tell by now, I absolutely love this game, it's a 10/10 for me, and it even made me enjoy playing video games all again. It might not be exactly what you are looking for, and that's fine! But I do think that everyone should give it a try, it really is that rewarding to complete the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of those games that is best played with little to no information going in beforehand. Elements of this title game me the same sense of satisfaction and enjoyment comparable to elements of The Outer Wilds.

On the surface, Tunic is a well crafted somewhat metroidvania styled adventure game (desperately avoiding describing it as "darksouls but zelda" as that's such a lackluster way of putting it) that cleverly uses discovery and intuition as its strongest elements to make the experience unique to other games of its kind.
Though since you're reading this far into reviews, I'll assume you want to have a little more information than that, though without revealing too much, this game somewhat goes... off the rails in the best and most unique of ways, taking the term "Hiding in plain Sight" to the extreme.

If you enjoy unique puzzles, exploration, a sense of wonder, and simply having to figure games out for yourself rather than looking up tutorials or guides, this is certainly one you don't want to have spoiled.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnly 5 hours in, but i'm gonna write a review anyway. I thought I got this game for like 5 dollars, but I got it for 25.97. I was thinking the whole time "what a steal!" I am not unhappy with paying as much as I did for this game. I will beat this game.

The game rewards exploration with hidden chests, hidden shops, and hidden areas. Very often i'm just looking for the way forward or a hidden chest and then I stumble upon something rewarding like a shortcut, loot, a new map that I didn't suspect was nearby, etc; it's all good stuff.

Combat is easy, you've got your 1, 2, 3 combo, you've got a shield, bombs, pots, and other utilities that are easy to use.

The music blends in with the scenery and is easy on the ears.

There are various enemy types from slime likes, knights, skeletons, angry birds, and so so.

There is no dialogue and the story is up to interpretation. You develop an understanding as you progress and retrieve pages of information that are still not completely spelled out. It's very fun to feel that there is a mystery to be unraveled when it comes to what's happening in the game and "what the heck does this do!?"

There is no difficulty setting just like a souls like. It's challenging enough for me. You can swim past some of the fights, other fights mess ya up. I like that variation in difficulty with enemy types just as much as I like the variety of enemy types. I like them good nuff!

I like them colors! So vibrant! The look of the game, so simple and eye grabbing! Much wow!

Alright cons if any:

There's the targeting system which is simple at first. You just hold shift to lock on to enemies. I have not found a convenient way to switch between enemies when there are many of them and so I could be swinging or blocking in the wrong direction and obviously that leaves me vulnerable. I have had another problem with that while traversing the map with a tool for crossing gaps. You have to lock on to points that allow you to traverse gaps. There's an area that spawns enemies on top of the point you need to traverse to. What this does is it causes you to pull in the enemy and in this situation I was on an island of sorts, a minecraft block wide, and as I pulled the enemy they fell below my attack range, dodging my attack. There were two others who proceeded to blast me while I had little space to dodge around on. I was a sitting duck because I could not easily traverse like I wanted. I found that using the directional keys allows me to change my target, but it feels inconvenient to use when you have to use semicolon for blocking along with j, k, and l for attacking and such and so I cannot simply defend myself while switching targets.

There is a mechanic in the game, that I'm not sure if I should speak on in detail about so I won't, which you need to know in order to progress in the game. I had not seen an indication for its use in the player book in game. I was stuck in multiple areas and spent time roaming around the large map looking for things that I had missed until I eventually just played around with some things and accidentally found the way to progress. This is me saying that the element of mystery can lead you to become lost or confused. I suggest that if anyone with low patience becomes stuck you should google it.

Again, I've only played 5 hours. My feelings on the game may change later, but as for right now I'd say get it if you're looking for a challenging yet relaxing experience. Or maybe it's challenging at times and relaxing at other times. It's up to the player, I reckon.

Beaten it at 28 hours. Final notes. The game has so much mystery because you're constantly trying to interpret characters on paper that you cannot understand, which is the intention. I learned about the end game secret mechanic, started doing the secret dance and what not, and the whole time I was so confused. Does it want me to input this or that? Oh, it wanted something else for this one. I felt like some details were missing, which is usually intentional, but I had no idea how to go about doing some other things that I found for the find all the secrets challenge. It has kept me up 'til 5 in the morning. I punched in so many key combos, obviously first doing the ones I was supposed to do, and nothing would work so I just went and had my 35 attempts at killing the boss and got the sad ending. Sad ending didn't make me feel great, but it was still a great game. Maan, that sucks...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo spoilers- I was disappointed by Tunic, but still glad that I played it. It had been hyped up based on the "meta", being a mechanic in the game where you collect pages to a game manual which provide solutions to the game's puzzles. I grew up with NES games in the 90's, but didn't import games. For that reason, I have a familiarity with using manuals as a kid, but perhaps not the same nostalgia as someone who remembers trying to understand how their new game works by relying on the manual they can't read.

My first experiences with games that have a meta are Doki Doki Literature Club, followed immediately by the Stanley Parable Deluxe. I won't spoil those experiences, but they are in a category of game that I discovered I love, and I believe Tunic belongs beside them. Although those first two are absolutely 10/10, GotY, Tunic was more of a 7 or 8.

My first impressions of Tunic were loving the art style, but absolutely hating the unresponsive controls and clunky combat. I played on default settings until my first death taught me what was lost, at which time I turned on easy mode for the rest of the game. I enjoy roguelites and Demon-Souls-likes, but didn't like how it fit here. Again, trying not to spoil anything.

I found the environmental puzzles to be a bit too easy to recognize. I discovered many of the game's secrets prior to when I should have learned them in the game manual, just because the clues are so salient. That sounds like a positive because it indicates the game is intuitive, but the converse is true- I found the few times the game blew my mind by showing me something I hadn't realized before, to be much fulfilling.

It's easy to understand HOW to solve most puzzles, but the physical act of doing so is an annoyance. Pen and paper are required, which is acceptable, but (redacted...) can be frustrating.

Getting around the world is problematic, but made easier once I realized I could track my location in some way...

I played entirely on Steam Deck, and it was a technically perfect experience. It took me a few days to complete pretty much everything except two achievements, which came in at 17 hours. I missed the two achievements because I completely avoided any spoilers, guides, or even looking at the achievements list until I was done with everything I could figure out on my own. And the feeling of accomplishment when I figured out the GP and reverse GP puzzles on my own are just about the only reason I am giving this game a recommendation.

Pro Tip- Playing on the Deck also allowed me to assign one late-game ability to the back grip button and have it "turbo" when held, greatly increasing my incentive to mop up every last secret.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTUNIC is a very cute-looking game which brings together elements of Zelda, Fez, and classic 'hit and block' combat games.

The wonderful part of the game is the on-screen manual which you slowly build throughout the game by collecting pages. This contains cryptic and complex instructions and maps on how to play the game, written in a special runic language (translation not necessary to complete the game). This means the game has a real sense of intrigue for the first 5-10 hours or so. After that it's still good, but loses some of its magic and becomes a bit more generic.

The puzzles throughout the game largely revolve around the manual and finding out secret mechanics/places. I enjoyed the puzzle elements generally.

The combat is smooth and well put together. I found I could handle most of the enemies generally, but had to resort to the reduced difficulty option for most of the boss fights which were particularly difficult.

Both the 'bad' ending and the 'good' ending were quite satisfying. However I decided to stay on to play the extra secret puzzles and have to say I didn't think it was really worth it for the effort you have to put in (a lot of translating the language... or googling the translations of the language).

Would recommend this if you're looking for something interesting to play and like Zelda and/or Fez.\t\t\t\t\t
