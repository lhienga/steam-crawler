\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTunic is a masterpiece.

If you like puzzles in any capacity, play it, you'll find something in it to love, if not all of it.

Tunic combines the fluid combat, ability unlocks, and exploration of any great Zelda-like with the added unlock of knowledge. Throughout the game, you will learn information that allows you to progress just as much as any hookshot would.

Think Outer Wilds - a large portion of what holds you back is what you haven't yet learned about the game world. But there's the addition tons of unnecessary items, shortcuts, and other details that make every moment of your exploration through Tunic's beautiful world a joy that keeps you invested and coming back for more. While Outer Wilds took the view of "make everything matter" to keep you invested, Tunic tries instead to "make every moment a discovery." In most ways, it succeeds.

In my opinion, the one place Tunic fails is when you try to go for total completion of the game. In contrast to Outer Wilds, there are unimportant things, and you can look for those things after the game is over. But because the motivating factor in Tunic is the consistent availability of secrets to uncover, it falls apart somewhat once you've found all but 2 or 3 things.

If your stuck on a puzzle, even if you have everything you need, you might just end up trying the same things over and over, praying it'll work. And it gets frustrating, and takes away the wonder of this game.

So if you play this game, which you most certainly should, complete it. Finish the game to the end. And let that be the end.

Or, keep going - try to discover everything the game has to offer, - but don't be afraid to get hints from friends or the wonderful spoiler free secret guide you can find on Tunic's community page on Steam.

Whatever you chose to do, this is one of those games that will leave you wishing you could play it again, knowing it will never be the same. It's a once-in-a-lifetime experience, and one that will leave you feeling proud, lost, curious, and excited for the future of the art form that is video games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe combat of Darksouls meets the exploration of Zelda with no actual puzzles.

The combat is dodge roll attacks, attack with your slap-range sword, and spam attack until it dies.
Bosses will be constantly moving, so you're trying to reach the boss more often than actually attacking.
Alongside that, endgame enemies reduce your max health whenever you get hit by them, and the only way to regain that lost hp is by resting at the game's version of a bonfire.

You run around and explore. You can TWO tools good for unlocking new areas to explore in, one mid-game, the other late game.

I only played through the main game, no super secret ending or anything of the sort.
I saw zero puzzles I can think of. you kill enemies, flip switches, and proceed.
Any puzzles would be from figuring out what the hell the guidebook means, translating text, and using a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ save file that gets added in as a meta puzzle piece for another puzzle.

The art is great, the music is great, the story would be great for someone who actually wanted to translate whatever the ♥♥♥♥ is being said/written. The gameplay has much to be desired. I suggest anyone wanting to experience the gameplay anyway, to enable "no fail mode" after Reaching the ziggaraut \t\t\t\t\t
