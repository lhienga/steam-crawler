\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those games that's ideal to play with as little knowledge as possible. The only preliminary knowledge you might want to know to decide if you'd enjoy this game is:

- It's very reminiscent of the original Legend of Zelda in terms of exploration and secrets. If you love that game (or Zelda games in general), you are very likely to love this one.
- The combat is similar to Dark Souls and can be challenging at times but nothing insurmountable.
- There are secrets everywhere, and the game does not hold your hand. Explore to learn.
- You collect pages of the game's manual as you play. Do NOT look up the manual in full as reading it is spoiling the majority of the fun.
- The language in the game is actually translatable.
- Game is relatively short; I 100% it with less than 20 hours. That said, the game feels much longer than that, in a good way.
- The music is amazing.

If any of that and the trailers have you interested, it is absolutely worth your time playing. It's a phenomenal game that's going to stick with me long after I finished it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's really frustrating seeing more bad reviews than I would have expected, as this is now easily one of my top 5 indie games of all time. The bad reviews seem to come from either the heavy puzzle aspect or heavy combat portion of the game.

Puzzle-wise, this game completely blew my mind in the best way possible. Nothing in the game is put by accident. There's layers upon layers of puzzles in this game. And it's really up to you how much to invest on them. One definitely has to do part of the puzzles to advance the game's main story. But the more obscure ones are completely optional. The amount I discovered on my own left me very satisfied by how well designed they are. And I looked up the rest just to have a better sense of how deep the puzzling gets. I will say here that in order to enjoy the game, you do need to really enjoy puzzles (as there's no direct accessibility option to completely eliminate that). And some puzzles might take multiple tries.

Combat-wise, it is definitely a tough game. However, although it was my first souls-like game, my skills got better the more I advanced in the game. To the degree that when I did one of the bosses out of order, I was able to kill a previous boss on my first try. However, there was a section where I think it got significantly harder in compared to other sections (which I found more challenging than the final boss). Because here, your upgrades didn't matter as much, and it was all about skill and weapons/items. It was also particularly harder for me as I missed a weapon that would have made it easier. Regardless, I was able to beat it after a very stressful try. And if I would have been really stuck, I could have just used the accessibility options!

It's also worth mentioning how amazing the artstyle is. The cute vibe is all I ever needed, we really need more cute yet challenging games. But also, the aesthetics and texture of everything is so good. And the manual is wonderful, of course.

Last, but not least, the music and sound effects are incredible as well. They both effectively make the game significantly better. From the very beginning of the game, to the jumpscare of finding a boss, and everything in between.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never had a grandiose "aha" or eureka moment when playing this puzzle adventure game. I either understood everything clearly enough, or with time, went "well that's annoying." The secrets and puzzles gave me no sense of happiness, purpose, or meaningfulness when playing the game. It's honestly very exhausting looking through the manual (which is a crime to flip through) to either decipher stuff or look for clues. The game was pretty decently made (it has many small annoyances that compound and make the game less fun, and sometimes, infuriating to play) and is obviously very clever and well thought out (in concept, design elements are hit-and-miss.) It's incredibly cute in design and graphically very consistent and pretty. Combat is okay, not the best or most polished I feel, but easy enough to get into. It just didn't jive with me enough to call it a masterpiece, but it may be a masterclass in puzzle adventure games.

I thoroughly enjoyed the first 10ish hours of the game. Just running around hitting stuff, going places, and getting stronger. The secret items, pages (manual), fairies, mountain door, holy cross, etc were the downfall in fun and ability to enjoy the full scope of what the game has to offer. The holy cross thing was simple to understand (its written pretty clearly in the manual) but finding all the fairies and what not... no... that's awful. I found no enjoyment whatsoever in trekking through the game to try and find each fairy holy cross spot, or tediously inputting the combo.

I think the game is good enough when you ignore the puzzles (get the "bad ending") to buy it on sale, just enjoy the (shortish) adventure with very minimal puzzle solving, and beat the final battle; a casual run doesn't really require all that much thought put into it. However, if you really enjoy puzzles, obtuse linguistic deciphering (like Fez,) and adventuring, you'll absolutely love this game because it delivers on all those fronts spectacularly and you should buy it regardless of its current price tag.

Tunic... is a good game. It isn't the best. It isn't the most fun I've ever had. It doesn't have the most annoying puzzles. It doesn't have the best combat. It doesn't have the best adventuring or exploration. Its map design was probably the single best element of the game. The interwoven passages and round-about-ness made me feel like I was discovering Lordran all over again. It's a fine game that delivers an experience. That experience is what you shape it to be, whether its full dedication to the puzzles and unlocking every secret the game has to offer, mastering its combat and plowing through every battle to race to its finale, or figuring a ton of stuff out and being bored of the game so you then resort to using a guide because the tedium of discovering everything on the map left you feeling unmotivated, bored, and vexed.\t\t\t\t\t
