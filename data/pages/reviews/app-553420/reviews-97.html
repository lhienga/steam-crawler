\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm personally not big into mertoriodvania or legend of zelda style games, but I do enjoy dark souls so that brought me over here, and I find this to be a really fun experience. I never digged the legend of zelda formula very much, I often found it bland and overly complicated but this game while being challenging that is for sure, it never felt impossible. You can always find a way to get where you want if you put your mind to it, and where you can't go, you can go later once you get the upgrades through playing the game. While some of the puzzles are just wild (looking at you mountain door) and 90% of the text are in a made up language, it gives you just enough if you use context clues and with what info is given you can figure things out. While the story is open to interpretation, I think it is one of its strong suits, your not really sure what is going on, but the more you see, and the more you read, and the more you get actual hints, you end up creating your own version of sorts and why lil tunic is doing what he is doing. One last thing I might add, I though the camera would ♥♥♥♥ me up or be in your way, since there are times where you go behind a wall and you just see the characters shadow, I never felt as if it was in my way, say, maybe once or twice, trying to get some dumb chest. I can't recommend this more, all the legend of zelda fans bought this I am sure, but if you enjoy dark souls, explorations, or just something challenging and interesting, then your 30 dollars is well worth it, especially that most triple AAA companies are charging 70 bucks for a 8 hour adventure, its well worth the money. The team did a great job and I am looking forward to see what they work on next in the future\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t NO SPOILER REVIEW

So no joke part of what influenced my purchase of this game were the NEGATIVE reviews. The amount of skill issue reminded me of so many of the games I love that I knew I would at least appreciate this one. I wanna just praise every aspect of it be here's the gist. Tunic has that blend of that classic Legend of Zelda style with a Dark Souls 1 cohesive world. The combat is simple but tight, the world is littered with secrets and worthwhile exploration, and the map design is top-notch. The game is designed around you picking up pieces of the Game Manual which is written in a language that's decipherable but there's no need. It's the most rewarding, dopamine inducing, original idea I've ever had the pleasure of experiencing. Do you need it to beat the game? No, but it gives context to everything you're doing and seeing. I honestly have no complaints. This is one of those games that sticks with you and every other game in this genre you play you will compare to this one.

tl;dr Tight combat, clean aesthetic, and one of the best "map" mechanics I've ever seen. You'll know within 10 minutes if you like this game or not.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've seen the far shore with my own eyes, and I didn't like it.
I haven't played Tunic in about a year now, but every time I think about it, see someone else playing it, or even just randomly stumble across it again on steam like I have just now; I get a 'flutter' in my chest and my eyes water a little bit. Tunic is one of those games that I wish I could erase my memory and play it again. I have a small scrapbook that I filled with notes and drawings for Tunic, and while I'm sure I don't need it anymore, I'm afraid to throw it out because it still feels very important. Steam says I only have 40 hours in the game, but I feel like that must be a gross understatement. Tunic is a meticulously designed masterpiece that can be a fun and fairly simple Zelda-inspired exploration and puzzleish metroidvania, or you can dig deeper and find the puzzles within the puzzles (within the puzzles?), learn to fluently read a made up language, and try to develop some kind of synesthesia.
11/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTUNIC is probably the most fulfilling puzzle game I've ever played. I bought it because I thought it'd be a Soulsborne-like adventure in a Zelda-esque package. And while I *did* get that, I ended up staying for so much longer for the step-up in puzzles during the second half of the game. I ended up busting out an old college notebook and pencil to work out some of more cryptic puzzles, and I even went so far as to reverse engineer the game's own language. (It's doable without looking it up, but you might need to be very patient and observant!)
I played this the whole way through completely blind, and if you're reading this and thinking of picking it up yourself I highly recommend you do so as well. It almost felt like an ARG at times, and the puzzles feel so satisfying to solve on your own.\t\t\t\t\t
