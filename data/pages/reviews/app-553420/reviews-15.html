\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game about adventure and discovery. If you're looking for a game that lets you learn at your own pace with plenty of mysteries, this is your game. This games most unique element is the inclusion of an in-game manual that you must collect the pages of. The manual itself contains many hints and secrets for you to unravel. This manual is your best friend, and I recommend spending plenty of time poring over it.

When it comes to difficulty, the game starts out fairly easy. However, is a huge difficulty spike in the combat, exploration, and puzzles towards the end of the game. While I enjoyed the added difficulty, it may be daunting for more casual players. There are accessibility options to reduce or entirely remove the combat difficulty.
The puzzles cannot be made easier, but there is an accessibility option to make the inputs easier (which I strongly recommend using when you get to the relevant portion of the game).

While I initially assumed that translating the in-game language would be necessary, it is not needed for any of the important puzzles. That said, I do recommend giving the translation a try (if you're interested), as it's more than a simple cipher. If you're not interested in doing so, I recommend looking up a translation, as the translated manual is interesting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best Zelda game ever made.

The whole world is a puzzle, and not merely a straightforward wysiwyg logic rooms like Zelda dungeons. You have the whole solution to the puzzle already, but you have to decipher it piece by piece to complete it. Unfortunately, there is no cryptography involved. The language is IPA symbols of English existing in a custom glyph set.

Also the combat sucks, but not as bad as Zelda combat (both before and after BotW). Enemy hit points and whatnot, however, acts as more of a skill check rather than as a difficulty feature. Hopefully the devs can address this and make the combat more enjoyable as the roll mechanic is cool and all, but there are no active defensive mechanics, and this one passive mechanic is, as one would expect, clumsy and awkward-feeling in execution. When making swings, you expect at least some sort of stun mechanic, but that's only if you do enough damage to a given enemy, and due to the tendency to sort of spam and inability to dodge during attacks, you just get hit which is absolutely trash combat that deserves zero praise whatsoever (there's no skill in waiting for things to happen so I can do what I must, even on the order of milliseconds). This reduces the skill gap check to a monotonous "trudgingly survive" (in certain instances) until you can level up your stats enough to the point where it doesn't matter anymore. Terrible UX. Fix your stupid combat, and definitely don't pretend to fix it and then call it fixed either, or I will fight to the death for a refund for what would be nothing less than an inexcusable compromise no different than contemporary Nintendo!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR In a Yes or No situation: Yes, you should get Tunic. HOWEVER, there are plenty of similarly priced games that I enjoyed more...

Tunic takes it's inspirations from the Zelda and Dark Souls series. There is an interconnected world with unlockable shortcuts and secrets galore. Some progression is gated by item acquisition. And bosses aren't there to make you feel good.

What I liked:
- The interconnected world. This is Hollow Knight level of connections and secrets
- A ton of items to collect
- The graphics
- The Audio
- I liked that "leveling up" involved finding items corresponding to specific stats
- Combat was satisfying, until the end. I enjoyed the boss fights less than the regular encounters
- Short-ish playtime. This reminded me of Super Metroid in scope and playtime

What I did NOT like:
- There are only 2, maybe 3 items that develop movement in the world, the rest are focused on combat. That's boring for me
- The end game falls apart for me. It takes Fromsoftware's "tough as nails till the last drop" approach. I'm old and short on time. I found the end un-enjoyably tough.
- Being an old smooth brain, there are puzzles that I had no idea existed until accidentally coming across a "spoiler" comment online. I would probably be into finding EVERYTHING if they were hinted at better.

All-in-all an enjoyable experience. What I liked I liked a GREAT deal but I am glad I waited for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like finding secrets, then there's probably no better game about secrets than this. I would describe the game as being Darksouls but Zelda, or vise versa. It has lots of Darksouls but in a Zelda type of vibe. Combat is pretty basic but still fun imo. The main difference in the gameplay loop is the massive amount of secrets. Almost every screen has a small little corner you can go around and find some loot or a new item or anything, it's really cool and satisfying. The secrets make heavy use of the isometric perspective which might sound annoying but works really well especially when you find a shortcut to an area that you've already been in that was there the whole time.
But secrets don't stop at chests obscured from view. The entire game has interesting progression not just in power or exploration but knowledge. There are doors that you literally don't know how to open, not that you don't have the key, or don't have an ability, you just don't know. Items and abilities are figured out through experimentation. A lot of the knowledge based progression is done through the manual so make sure to pay attention to it. It is not required to understand the language. Look at the pictures, diagrams and readable text for information.
I should also mention that this game includes THE BEST PUZZLE IN ALL OF VIDEOGAMES.\t\t\t\t\t
