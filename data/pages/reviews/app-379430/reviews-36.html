\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun and immersive. The beginning of the game is a bit tough to get through. The issue is that this game has a lot of RPG elements going on with skill levels and stats that isn't really explained. You go from being level 1 where everyone is is level 10 and just getting destroyed in every fight. You basically overcome this by training with Bernard with wooden swords when you get to that point and entering a tournament at Rattay. Once you level up your fighting skills a bit you quickly become the big fish where you're basically level 20 and everything else is level 10 and it's smooth sailing. I feel like the progression could have been a bit smoothed out more. It just felt like I was very weak and then suddenly was over powered. I really enjoy the voice acting and the world that they created here feels very authentic. I'm not a historian by any means but I feel like this is close to what it would have been like back in those times.\t\t\t\t\t

TL;DR:
Ultra low quality coding: 2024, high end PC trying to take off running this game on medium settings & screen tearings and hiccups left and right
Half the game is pitch black on TN monitor, no gamma setting
Borderline unplayable without quality-of-life improving mods (listing below)



All in all, I'll probably play through the game but it's not entertainment, it's a mixture of that, ruined over and over again by dumb bugs and annoyances (cancer-like quests). Bought on 80% discount, it worths that price, but not even a penny more...

Issues & Cancers (after the first few hours in the game)
  • HORRIBLE coding: 2024, high end PC running a game from 2018 & trying to take off on medium settings
  • Movement gives me motion sickness, can't be fixed (motion blurr on or off, no difference - there's serious screen tearings due to the pathetic software quality, also some random hiccups)
  • No gamma setting. Problem is I have a TN monitor, so dark areas are pitch black on the default settings - can't even see properly inside buildings in broad daylight....
  • Often one can't step over a magic pixel and gets stuck walking straight, so has to jump. Not much fun in stealth... Millions of glitches of the kind... horrible...
  • Same applies to vaulting, at some places it's magically not possible...
  • (melee) Fighting is dumb and rigged: slow and annoying. Kinda got used to it, but too sloppy to work with a rhythm and a pace, the AI just tries to make it entertaining (or challenging?) instead of "playing its game" / "fighting its style", so it's irritating... In the end, turns out best if one keeps using RMB ONLY, in any weapon combat too, as for the slow, sloppy, jittery combat & animations don't allow anything else
  • Speaking of rigged, everything is... e.g. some mobs cannot be stealth killed, because of reasons (as soon as one starts the action, magically get busted, 10 out of 10 times), but the dice game is rigged as well: the higher the bet, the better the NPC rolls...
  • "Save Schnappes", or you can't save the game. Deeply, DEEPLY retarded. Well done! So, one has to spend a fortune of grind like an idiot and carry tons of weight in the form of these "potions", which also makes one drunk...... otherwhise no save game... Pathetic...
    I understand they wanted hyper-realism or such, but first, learn coding video games so anims, locomotion, AI, etc. so the overall game experience IS in place, second, if hyper-realism was a good thing & fun, it would take 2-3 hours in Minecraft to dig out a 1 by 1 dirt block...
  • A lot of things are clipped and mega-slow animated. e.g. gather stuff: external cam, anim plays you bend over and pick up, back to game mode. Need to collect 20? Awh, sad! Enjoy this magic 20 times in a row! Even if no cutscene when gathering, the crouch, pick up, stand up anim is still there, so annoyingly slow, especially by having e.g. 3 breads on a plate, and one has to pick them up one by one... See "hyper-realism" above. If this was realistic, first, I'd do it faster, second, I'd pick up 2 or more at a time... Just add to inv., despawn, call it a day ffs.....
  • That dog (?tinker?) is dumb and useless, except when it pulls in a trash pack on you. Do yourself a favor and tell it to "Stay!" and never get back to it again (only because you can't kill it yourself). This goes for that quality coding mentioned above, "AI"...
  • I'm Hungarian - the Hun VO is disturbingly disguting (swearing and cursing big time)
  • Crazy amount of bugs! So far none of them are game breaking, but there's a lot, many of those are super-annoying (hence the need to save often, which is cckblocked by the creators...), e.g. a sleeping person, try pickpocketing (lvl11 for me) in stealth (lvl10 for me) and instant busted & wake up & you will go to jail! message. Huge WTF.
    But saving often also is a must due to traveling a lot and e.g. getting damage from a "fall" (means: step down from a rock to a brutal half a meter-ish lower grassy surface)
  • Trespassing: You only realize you are going to trespass after it actually happened. No warning beforhand, a sign on a door or whatever, nothing. You just casually walk one room to the other, that other one counter-intuitively is a private area, and boom, everyone start screaming for guards, or just get pissed off with you.
  • Speaking of trespassing, the easiest way to manage such scenarios (e.g. "stealing a dice", you'll understand) is doing it fast. Yup. Rush inside in front of everyone, pick the thing up, run outside. Wait for them chill / reset, repeat with the next item you need to grab. There likely is a timer, an annoyance-meter and as long as you don't stay long enough, no matter you are robbing someone, they just don't trigger. Issue is, "ninja style", trying to "play the game properly" wouldn't work (at least didn't in this dice thing I noted, and at another scene already)
  • Some ?quests? are super-annoying. The one pissed me off so much I wrote this review, and I used the built-in cheat codes - I NEVER do that in ANY game, this one made me...
    The so called "quest", with MINOR spoiler:
    Gather some stuff to heal the brother (in front of the mine). In pitch dark with patrolling mobs. Now, so called mobs here cannot be stealth-killed, because of reasons (as soon as you are in range and start it with the press & hold button, you immediately get spotted). Probably the studio decided the player MUST suffer and struggle with their retarded idea here. Issue is, the mats needed are impossible to find, again, in pitch dark on a large area, it's just nowhere to find... - At least I couldn't, likely would be a challenge even without the unkillable mobs...
