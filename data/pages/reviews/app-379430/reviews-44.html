\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall a great game with flaws.

The best part of this game is 100% the story. The main quest and side quest are all really good. All of the side quest felt like they were worth doing and fit into the main story. So many fun and unique quest in this game, you can tell a lot of effort went into it. The DLC stories were also good, I especially like the Adventure of Lord Capon DLC. I also really like the NPC design in this game. Each character feels unique and memorable. It's just top notch story telling all the way through. Truly a gem of a story, I never felt like I played 97 hours. The only down side of the story is only that I don't feel like I would ever do a second playthough. There aren't really many choices. Just a single good story. I just don't feel like I would need a second playthough even if they make KCD2. I also really like the stealth mechanics in this game, they can be cheesed a bit once you understand the mechanics, but overall fun.

The big down side is combat. It just isn't good IMO, and it never really gets better. It does go from hard to tediuos to ok, but never good. First off the combat is going for a realism style designed around 1v1 combat, but the game never lets you 1v1 really. Next every NPC in te game is a mster swords man who will block every attack you make, so best way to fight is to just perfect block and poke or master strike while constantly back peddling. Because the NPCs will also constantly rush you and kill you. This gets a little better once you get the dog companion and better armor, but stays annoying through end game. I ended up just using stinger short sword and r1 everyone in the face, not really fun but effective. Also the archery is not realistic, I get drawing a bow is hard, but the bow aim sway and slow arrows are way overdone. In the end the best way to handle combat is to avoid it or stealth.

Story 10/10
Combat 5/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most immersive RPG games I have ever played.

This game will reset your standards for how a proper RPG game should be. It's not a casual game by any means, so if you're not into taking your time with things and building up skills at a steady pace then this game won't be for you. The first few hours of the game unfolds the story and eases you into the world of KCD. You will die...a lot...and then after spending time with your weapon of choice, working on skills and acquiring the right gear for your play style, you will find that you die a LOT less. In fact I haven't died for well over 100 hours. This is because your skills increase, you choose perks, you learn IRL, and you make realistic decisions based on the the situation. I.E - You're on your own, you see a big group of bandits/Cumans...don't be a p***k! Of course you won't survive (sometimes you can) So go about it another way.

DIFFICULTY - This game starts off really hard and does get easier but the game is hardcore. It's not a button mashing, console jockey, lets not do a lot of thinking kind of game. You have to THINK, there is ZERO hand-holding...NONE! So listen to dialogue, think about what your character's dialogue options are, analyse the situation, use your brain! It will help you. Spend time with quests, don't rush through them, treat even the small activities like you would in real-life. Plan ahead...make sure your weapons and armour are in tip top condition. Make sure you carry provisions. Visit shops and traders. Sleep, eat, bath, visit a wench, practice archery, practice sword wielding, earn money, make potions, get p****d at the tavern, be a bastard, have your wits about you, just live the life of Henry in Bohemia in 1403! LIVE HENRY'S LIFE! GET IMMERSED!

COMBAT - The combat is great...and then it's bad...it is certainly buggy, you will find yourself elevating up bushes, hills etc and the terrain can cause havoc with collision. The design isn't great overall, but you can get around it. Be patient and remember to analyse the situation before thinking you can John Wick your way through the game. The best combat moments are the tournaments in Rattay, mainly because it's a 1 v 1 fight and you will win EVERY single fight when you hone your combat skills, IRL and in game.

DLC - The DLC isn't amazing, but it's worth it for how cheap it is! My favourite is "From the Ashes" where you have to earn a huge amount of money to rebuild a village. Earning the money however you want is the best part, more so than the actual DLC, but like I said...it's cheap! And the DLC will give you a ton of gameplay hours.

Graphics - The graphics are stunning! It's truly a beautiful game.

BUGS - When this game first released back in 2018, there were tons of bugs! They have been patched for the most part but they haven't all gone...They can be minor and mildly game breaking. The worst bug I have is wayfarers randomly running off to tell guards on me even though I just ride past them. I have no fix or reason for why this happens but it does...There are visual bugs, nothing game breaking that I have experienced so far. Early in the game I experienced a save bug where my crime status had merged into a past save, similar to how Skyrim bugs occur (although mostly exploit bugs in Skyrim).

AUDIO - The in-game audio is incredible. You feel sucked into the world with the sounds of woodland areas, ambient noise, and small details that add to the experience. Voice acting is great for the most part, with some minor wtf moments with some of the delivery/performance.

DICE - You will play a s**t ton of dice games! Top tip: practice playing dice by only wagering 1 Groschen and then once you get the hang of it, up the wager and earn some money! It's super fun! And collect unique dice!

There is so much more to say about the game but I'll be here until the year 2403 typing away...

Buy the game (especially on sale - in fact it's a crime how cheap the game is) and give it some time to see if it's your thing.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do not recommend this game to everyone, BUT to those who take interest in historical settings and want to be immersed in medieval Bohemia this game is a definite go. It requires patience from the player, especially in the beginning of the game when you start as a poor, weak Henry. The combat and the save system take getting used to, and some people refer to the open world as “boring”, but I see it as a realistic simulation and an immersive, meditative experience. When you ride on your horse during dawn and pass through beautiful, most realistic forests you really forget about anything in the real world.
I had moments when I left the game for weeks or months due to frustration, but I got through it (I’m talking about the monastery quest).
The game is so cheap that it’s worth a shot even if you abandon it for whatever reason.\t\t\t\t\t
