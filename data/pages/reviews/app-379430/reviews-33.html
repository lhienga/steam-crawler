\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably one of the most immersive games I've ever played. The game isn't overly punishing despite its reputation may imply. These mechanics are masterfully balanced between being immersive and not being a nuisance even hours into the game.

The biggest strength (imo) of the game would be the atmosphere and world Warhorse have built; this is then amplified by the fact that its a first person game. I don't think I've ever felt so engrossed and a part of a world (except for in RDR2).

Another highlight would be the combat of the game. It feels a bit clunky at first, but once you've put some time, you'll find that duels in this game are some of the most engaging and gratifying this genre has to offer.

If you enjoyed The Witcher and RDR and don't mind playing something a bit more hardcore, I would definitely recommend this game.
