\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[here's a FAIR warning: full character limit rant ahead, need to vent a little - just ignore this if you're not in the mood for it, otherwise.. hope you enjoy my waffling]

KC:D is SO hard into degrading you and making you feel inferior in your character's role, that unfortunately, it does not shy away from breaking the rules of the video game that it puts you into.
If that sounds weird.. buckle up.

this has been one of the nuttiest rollercoasters for me personally, going from absolutely loving a game after just messing around at my own pace for the first 24hrs of playtime and only thinking "oh.. not a great choice to do this in a game, but okay :/ " a few times here and there.. to whole-heartedly hating it a mere 10 hours later. i will say right now that i will likely continue playing it to give it a chance because i'm committed, (the whole setting, atmosphere, characters and a whole lot of other things are honestly really beautifully done) and i will consider changing the recommendation later on, but wow - let me tell you that there are some things that i found absolutely infuriating that you -unfortunately- just cannot avoid. right now - if i could - (!!and i want to point out that i don't know if this would be just poor decision-making of being in a bad mood!!) i would probably just refund this in order to save me from going back later and probably getting disappointed even more.

right of the bat; yea the combat is challenging like soo many ppl feel like pointing out, but i don't dislike the fact that it is difficult, i actually enjoy a tough challenge - however, i despise the KIND of difficulty here. now this sounds like a cop-out, but let me explain. ..this game is a prime-example of something that is super PSEUDO-difficult. it isn't difficult in itself, you can master it without too much of a challenge if you can manage to nail timings - there are no crazy button combos required to win a fight, but the game itself goes OUT OF ITS WAY to convince you that you are completely incapable as a player, without making transparent that you cannot win an encounter and it plays diiirty for this - OVER, and OVER, and OVER (one QUICK example? will get back to this one later-> simply give an enemy INFINITE hp. no matter how well you do, you will get defeated and yes, it is as frustrating and uninspired as it sounds).. ppl keep excusing it like "oh, you're a blacksmith's boy, of course you can't fight well at the beginning", but what i find BS here, is different.
in a way, i can say in hindsight that you can read this between the lines of the reviews; they are kinda agreeing that it's insane at the start and then you get better - but honestly, that's a pretty BS way of characterising it -probably unintentionally- and i only understood this when i experienced the progression myself.

want an example? here's one (and don't worry, there's more..)

there is a strong kind of combat move, called the MASTER STRIKE. in itself, it changes the dynamic of a fight so much, that having or not having it, can change a battle from "wow, this is actually impossible" to "yea, this wasn't too difficult". ...and it has -ultimately- nothing to do with your skill as a player. why? because you can already block before getting that super-move, it is more or less just as difficult. once you get the super-move however, you chain your stuff into a counter instead of only blocking and then blablabla. the game essentially locks a magic trick behind a progression wall and makes you think you're quite the failure at the game until you get it and then it feels almost unfair to use it. is this just a problem that i am having, am i just the idiot? i've asked myself that; turns out: apparently not and here's why.

in short: it boils down to an early tournament that you're supposed to enter; a quest tells you to. the instructor tells you that there are 3 opponents you need to beat in order to win and if you manage to best the first one - you will get hard stuck on the second opponent. just me, maybe? turns out these opponents seemingly have access to move-sets that are out of your league. so i googled what i was doing wrong and searched for guides on youtube and how i can use more powerful combos; i wanted to find out - even if it's super difficult - how i can pull this off. turns out: your character can only perform these OP moves once you are taught additional lessons later on. THIS is what i mean by pseudo-difficulty. it throws you in, says good luck, try your best and only behind a curtain, it is scripted that you can't actually win this yet, no matter how well you perform.

now here's my favourite part: i thought; ah well - i'm probably just unable to get the timings right, must be me, it's ok, i can get better at this later on, will have to practice etc. - you get the idea,.. and then in the youtube-comments of a guy explaining how to pull off these moves: he says, NAH, you just have to press the counter button at the right time (which is actually not too difficult). rest of the performed action is automatic. the comment section is FULL of people wondering, asking just like me, OK, but what else do i have to press or hold in order to be able to do this.. :D TURNS OUT-> you just don't have the scripted move yet and all it was, was the game BS-ing you into thinking you COULD be able to pull this off, IF you were good enough. ...and it's all just a massive pile of poop. in other words, enjoy wasting your time, trying to do better over and over, only to find the -more or less- same ending of getting defeated in slightly different ways.

oh boy.
coming back to another encounter that i mentioned earlier and related to the tournament experience; here's something that i think is constructive: if you WANT to do this, as a design-choice, if you insist on putting me on my character into an unwinnable fight, then put this into a CUTSCENE. not a huge fan personally, but let my character get defeated in a way that makes sense - or otherwise, at the very least, come up with something creative that happens in the fight. i might not like what happens, but hey; you write the story and decide where it goes. just don't tell me: fight this dude and then make him magically invulnerable or give him moves that defy the rules i have to fight by. if at this point, you're asking me: why not? -> because this is changing the universe that this is set in. i just cut this guy open a thousand times, is he supposed to be a god?

..did i just say infinite hp? yup! there's a scripted encounter you get thrown into that you cannot avoid as part of the main questline and no matter how much you destroy your opponent; he's a god that touches you once and you colllapse.

as many people have rightfully ciritcised - KC:D has a questionable way of respecting your time and making you try fights over and over, only to find out that you cannot actually win them, is making things even worse..

----

There's so much more i wanna write, but there's the character limit..
the game drops you into an OPEN WORLD and then it does annoying things in regards to that, ALL-THE-TIME..

early on, says: GET A HORSE; BACK TO YOUR HOMETOWN. doesn't say it's time-sensitive, just gives you the quest and no warnings. now, if you go a liiittle bit too far into the opposite direction to check out an area over there: BOOM, cutscene skip - you are now on the other side of the map.

.. ???
best part: on screen-> QUEST FAILED, didn't manage to get a horse.
...uh, i was literally looking for a horse when you teleported me across the map, but there weren't any apart from the ones i would have had to steal in front of witnesses.. dear lord.
then other times, you want to do something important in the afternoon, but a LOOONG quest that you start in the morning just skips straight to night time without any prior warning; -> other quest: FAILED. you can go back and TRY doing everything all over again. enjoy


honestly, what a disappointment.\t\t\t\t\t
