\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game gives me a lot of mixed feelings. On one hand, it's a fantastic depiction of medieval life and conflict in the era as you follow the story of a blacksmith's son named Henry after his home village is raided by the Cumans as part of the historical conflicts over the crown of Bohemia at the time. It takes good advantage of the growth-focused nature of RPGs by starting you out as a naive child and through the course of the game grow into becoming a true knight of the era. On the other, several decisions made for the sake of realism are extremely frustrating to encounter as a casual player. Quicksaves being limited by a somewhat expensive consumable, not really having a crosshair for ranged weapons, abysmal carry weight, quests often having time-sensitive elements, and statistics for not just nourishment and energy to consider for your character to maintain their health and standing, but also cleanliness, how conspicuous things look, and everything having durability all play a factor in adding realism at the cost of player enjoyment on some levels. It's a tradeoff between immersion and fun that isn't going to work for everyone. Credit where its due, the developers made the game they wanted in largely the way they intended it seems, and its resulted in a fun game especially for fans of the more hardcore elements. I enjoy the game and think many others will too, but just be warned if you're thinking about getting it, that there are a ton of gameplay elements added for realism that you might not like as a player.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn immersive RPG. It's quite realistic and I like the medieval settings of the story. The combat system is good once you understand the mechanics and when Henry gain more experience to become more powerful and well-equipped. It's a great journey. If you enjoy a good story with good characters and good music this game is for you.

I have done multiple playthroughs on the playstation console, and found the PC version crashes much less. But I would still recommend you to save frequently whenever you get to 'sleep and save' or if you have enough savior schnapps because there are still bugs that would make you to reload a previous save. I mourn the hours of gameplay that we all suffered from bugs and crashing ;_; I also found the achievements a bit buggy on the PC version, but I probably won't aim to acquire all of them on PC.

For mods, I recommend those the quality of life ones, like skipping flower picking animation (may not seem much but when you have to pick so many of them...), clothes stay clean for longer, sorted inventory (this is a wonderful mod). I don't recommend to turn on the archery retical cheat because I found the archery mechanic quite realistic, that you must learn how to take aim with reference points in your head. Henry will get better aim with higher strength and agility.

Overall, a great RPG, highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
