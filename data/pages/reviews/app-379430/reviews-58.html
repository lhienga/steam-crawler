\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the if not the best RPG game I have ever played. This game actually feels like a historical RPG, down to the dirt and grit you get on your clothes when you travel. The main story might be a little far fetched but I don't care because of all the other things you can do outside of it in this expansive world that feels lived in.

Even though I feel a *little* too powerful at times (especially with a bow), that was after I had both stole my way into good armor and weapons, as well as spent multiple IRL hours practicing the combat so that I wouldn't be cut into pieces by anyone with a sword. Yes half of that was grinding an xp bar, but the other was actually learning the skill required to "git gud".

I have played many a game that has hunger and food meters, but this game actually feels like it *needs* those mechanics just due to how detailed everything else in the world is that not having such a system would feel like a void.

Even though I have yet to finish the main story, I have gotten more than double my money from this game. It is well worth it at twice its price. Granted, this type of game is not for everyone. To those it *is* for, it is a MASTERPIECE.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far....Brilliant.

Gameplay: 10 the world just sucks you in. All your actions feel like they are impacting something in the world in one way or another.

Sound: 10 Excellent, the weather effects are realistic with huge storms coming through, walking on cobble stones then stepping one foot to the left on grass totally different sounds, clinking of chain mail when you land a jump, going quiet when your stealth all very Immersive.

Graphics: Better than Witcher 3 Skyrim and Starfield, close to Red Dead Redemption 2 level. I run 4k Ultra but keep shadows at medium. I dont think the shadow processing is optimised but even at medium its fine.

Story: Very good, I thought it would be boring being a true to life account, I was wrong.

Combat: Fine so far, bit weird early on because you dont run in thunder and fury like Geralt of Rivia. Starting to like its realistic sword fight nature, you get trained early on in the main quest.

You just gotta perservere early on, once you see the opening credits the game opens up. Follow the main quest until you get some training/tutorial stuff done. You get a nice save spot at that point and your own place to sleep and a chest to store your stolen I mean your loot. I got it on sale at $8 and you would be mad to not get it, its totally worth the full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best open-world story-driven European-based medieval RPG.

It has no magic or sorcery elements. That fact, combined with a great narrative that heavily leans on actual historical figures, kingdoms, and events, makes it feel realistic and historical. If you ever wanted to play as a warrior in the late European medieval period, during one of the many wars, this is your game, hands down.

The combat has a certain learning curve, and it never just lets you hack and slash through opponents. A heavier armor might help you take more blows, but taking multiple blows is still pretty dangerous and you always need to be concentrated when fighting. It gives you a nice challenge, and it's fun to abide by its rules.

I hope they make a sequel with more of this history-driven narrative while avoiding hack-and-slash, magic, and monsters. Other great RPGs already do that. This is great in its niche.\t\t\t\t\t
