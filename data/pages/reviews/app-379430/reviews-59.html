\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf Steam used a numerical rating system I'd give this game a 6, it's decent but there are enough bugs, flaws, and outright baffling design choices that at times make this a truly frustrating slog.

First things first, the combat. It's bad. There's a learning curve, you can get better at it, but even if you level up and improve it is still clunky and often unresponsive. It wouldn't be so bad if it was avoidable but it isn't. If you don't master the combat this game is unforgiving. If you're attacked by multiple enemies, which happens entirely too often, you might as well reload your last save immediately.

The character models other than those central to the story are embarrassing. NPCs cycle through the same three faces, the clothing and armor looks like a child is wearing it, and the voice acting and animation is inconsistent. I wish Warhorse had committed to either a blank slate make-your-own guy or an established character with their own personality, but Henry oscillates wildly between both. There are some impressive RPG elements at work in much of the dialogue and quests, but Henry suffers from the old "dialogue choice varies from what character ends up saying". Many times it feels like something only happens because Henry takes it upon himself to do or say something stupid and suddenly you're in a fight.

The save system sucks. In theory you should have no problem with saves if you're constantly doing quests and sleeping regularly, but sleeping doesn't always create a save for some reason. Whereas in other games you can supplement this with quicksaves, here you have to be on alert and constantly pause and check your saves to see.

All that said, coming from humble beginnings is a cool dynamic and it plays well. Henry can't read, can't fight, and no one respects him, so it can be quite rewarding when you accomplish things. The world feels rich and authentic, nothing in the environment feels out of place, NPCs actually live their lives, they have schedules. They *work* rather than cycling through an animation meant to look like activity just long enough for the player not to notice they aren't actually doing anything. This is a period piece in game form, and as such requires a significant amount of patience to get to a point where you can enjoy it.

TL:DR this game isn't for everyone, and if it doesn't sound like something you have the patience for, you're probably right.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKingdom Come: Deliverance has been a fascinating journey through medieval Bohemia. Having sunk 30 hours into the game, it's safe to say that the attention to historical accuracy is a standout feature. The level of detail, from clothing to the overall ambience, immerses you completely in the 15th century.

The narrative took an unexpected turn with the life of Henry, a blacksmith's son turned unlikely hero. The lack of a predefined hero archetype adds realism, and the moral choices you make genuinely impact the game world.

Combat is both challenging and rewarding. Mastering different weapons, strategic blocking, and managing stamina are essential. The learning curve is steep but adds to the sense of accomplishment.

The open-world design is vast, offering various activities from sword training to medieval contests. The day-night cycle and dynamic weather contribute to a living, breathing world.

While the game had some initial bugs and performance issues, they've been addressed through patches. The save system, initially restrictive, has seen improvements based on player feedback.

In conclusion, Kingdom Come: Deliverance is a historically rich and challenging RPG that has kept me hooked for 30 hours (and i imagine many more to come). The attention to detail, engaging narrative, and realistic combat make it a standout experience. Despite some initial hiccups, the dedication of Warhorse Studios to improving the game shows in the overall quality of the experience. Highly recommended for those seeking an authentic medieval adventure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is good but exceptionally frustrating at times. You can lose out progress by no fault of your own beyond bad luck. Like getting jumped on horse by cumans in the wood. Since it's impossible to run away, you will die early game almost every time and depending when your last save was, it can be problematic. That said, once you can brew/buy a sizeable amount of Savior's Schnapp (The item to save.) It becomes less of an issue to deal with.

The combat system is an acquired taste to say the least. It's solid enough in 1v1 but because clunky when fighting multiple opponents as you keep fighting the softlock mechanic. You also cannot dodge at will, (Got to wait for a prompt when being attacked.) which cuts your mobility making group fights even harder. Did I also mention you have no companions in this game? So, yeah, good luck if you get jumped by a large group early game. I hope you saved recently.

The game is frustrating early on. Unfortunately, not always for the right reasons. (As said, sometime you can get f- with no fault of your own.) But, the game is absolutely great and once you get the ball rolling, a lot of these issues tone down a bit. But, I still wish the devs went with a more leveled difficulty curve since it is kind of mental right now in the early game.

Still, thumbing it up because I love the game still. It's just incredibly frustrating at times and, again, not always for the best of reasons.\t\t\t\t\t
