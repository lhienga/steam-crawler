\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst hours in were very nice, but I also got frustrated and lost how to do things a few times and had to "internet searchengine" my way out of it. My overstimulated millenial mind had to get rehabituated to play and enjoy this brilliant piece of work, cause it's different alright, it takes patience, it's got a steep learning curve, it's intelligent, unforgiving and very immersive.

Once in the right mindset, I learned this game is about roleplaying first, you being a peasant and surviving in an unforgiving medieval setting. It's not about doing "here and to" little quests, it's not about becoming a God-like OP machine, it's not about getting the best items and outrich the nobles. It's doing what fits your character and current development and it challenges you to play the game as such, being the imperfect Henry you are.

I'm hooked, It's like you ate the best sushi ever... and now cannot enjoy anything else, ever again, forever spoiled, I want all my games to be like this! I want to train with Bernard forever, learning crossbows, poles, group-fight, what have you! Warhorse made some real quality stuff here, very impressive to go with their own ideas instead of just copy-paste whats already out there, being a smaller studio.

Fifty hours into the game, I'm remodding and starting over. I want to play this game to perfection. That is... more imperfection... more realism. I can only do my first walkthrough once, and I have a feeling it will be a longtime before I'll experience the likes again, games like these only come around once in 5000 years, and the next one sure has big shoes to fill ;).\t\t\t\t\t
