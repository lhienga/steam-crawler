When you start the game, you are weaker than a pigeon fart and have a lot, Lot, LOT (!!!) of work to do to develop your character, and none of it comes easy at all. I actually lost interest in the game before I could get my character's skills developed enough to survive consistently.

Also, the save feature seems kinda screwy in this game. It does not always let you save your progress when you attempt to do so. As a result, I end up playing the same scenes over and over again until I finally get it all correct. Then there are the inconsistent fast travel allowances. Too often, an error message comes up letting you know you cannot fast travel from that location. VERY ANNOYING!!!

However, on a positive note, the game has gorgeous graphics, interesting characters, and a nice story that keeps you engaged and interested. I give this game a 40 percent score on a hundred-point scale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely could not stand the combat system in this game for the longest time. After forcing myself to grind it out another time, I can safely say, that once you learn the basics ( like so many reviews have stated) that things finally do become great. Being attacked by more than one person used to drive me insane in this game. Now I relish the idea of a horde brawl. The riposte is OP in this game. Every enemies' armour rating is behind a shield wall until they take a sword to the face from a riposte, then it's just a matter of watching that enemy health bar get "sliced" in half, haha...ah, anyway... The tournament is now a favourite money printer. Before being under equipped, I can now scour the land of poor groups of enemies gear and make a nice tidy sum. Not to mention, swap out gear for sometimes the same gear but in better condition or just better gear all around. And I can never say no to a round of Farkle or two , can always use more groschen.
