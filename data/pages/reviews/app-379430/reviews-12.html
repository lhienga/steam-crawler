\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimply an amazing game with a captivating story, engrossing dialogue and unforgettable characters. There are no divisive politics ruining it either; just good story writing, immersive environments and an intricately detailed world. You will care about your character and those around him. They will remember when you visit them and help them. They will also keep a tally of slights. The world is alive and breathing.

The combat is realistic to the point of frustration in the beginning (you will not be ♥♥♥♥ of the walk as a newb swordsman). Each weapon has a weight and feel to it with well fleshed-out moves and effects. Swords flense the lightly armored, but glance off and spark on plate. Maces thud on padded armor but crash satisfyingly into plate; stunning the knight within. There are probably two dozen real medieval moves you can learn and use. Horseback combat is even well implemented. Want to learn to be a mounted bowman? Go for it.

There are plenty of systems in place to keep you busy as well and all of them are visceral. You will pump the bellows, grind with the mortar and pestle, mix, boil, cool, etc.. You will forage for hebs and mushrooms, collect honey from beehives. You will hunt game from rabbits to deer...watch out for snakes though. You will maintain your armor and weapons and even your clothes. You will even have to wash the dirt and mud off of yourself. Take care not to walk around covered in blood if you don't want to frighten or repulse people....maybe you do, you have a reputation to maintain after all.
