\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHistorical fiction done right. Granted, I'm playing the game with the benefit of years of updates and DLC. I'm sure it was in a much different state when it came out. And yes, just like any ambitious game, it could've been so much more.

But what we received is a wholly unique experience, one where the power creep applies not only to your combat skills, but to almost everything you do. One where you can truly feel the payoff for spending time practicing skills, and taking time to exploring outside the box solutions to your quests. The writing and voice acting are excellent, and make you wonder why RPGs from bigger studios often struggle so much with it. They do have the "Bethesda" model of using a few voice actors for many of the background NPCs, but they're all quality so it never really bothered me.

My main critiques are yes, the bugs. They are numerous but most of the ones I experienced are more immersion breaking than game breaking. It seems most of the major quest bugs have been ironed out. Additionally, I don't feel the "survival" aspects were done all that well. Sleep is easy to do with little risk, food is extremely easy to come by (the pots kind of ruin the need for it), and hygiene does affect your Charisma score but little else. In addition, if it weren't for the Pribyslavitz DLC, there would be almost nothing to spend money on outside of a horse. Pretty much all my gear was looted, and looting one enemy with high tier gear nets you thousands of Groschen in one go. Also, I loved the idea of the Pribyslavitz DLC and using funds to rebuild an estate from this bandit stronghold, but in reality it comes off a bit rushed and not all that rewarding outside of a few unique items.

And of course, the ending. I won't give spoilers, still stop here if you don't want any hints at all. This game has the most incomplete and cliffhanger ending I have ever experienced. Unless you play the game, I'm not sure you can understand how much of a tease it is. There pretty much has to be a sequel to have any closure at all, so here's to hoping that gets made! Otherwise, if you don't like unfinished stories, DO NOT play this game.

Overall though, if you've ever wanted to get a very small taste of life in 1400s Bohemia or have just wanted a realistic medieval RPG, then I highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
