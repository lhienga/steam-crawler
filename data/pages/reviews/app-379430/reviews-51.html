\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRead the negative reviews and it quickly becomes clear, this game has a strong niche appeal which helps overcome what most people will consider large flaws.

I wanted a "realistic" medieval rpg and I'm confident this game can deliver that. But I didn't want it enough and this game has an obvious lack of polish that WILL waste your time if you can't enjoy the tedious aspects of it. So either buckle up and lower your standards, or skip this game.


If you buy this game, you will find yourself asking the following questions:



Why can't my character run across a small rock/bush on a generic path?

Was 2011 Skyrim a revolutionary parkour game or is this just pure crap?

Why do I have to turn the voice volume down whilst exploring just so Henry can STFU about missing breakfast this morning?

Is Henry a five year old who whines just because he got bored?

Why do I feel limited not by skill or stats, but by combat mechanics?

If this game is realistic, why can't I just outrun a guy carrying 30kg of armour and a sword?

Do i really have to save everywhere I go in the early game in case I get jumped by 3 npcs?

Should my horse be able to glitch out on the stable where it lives?

How did they make an open world that manages to be both so full and so emtpy?

Is Henry as brain-dead as he looks and sounds?


So it feels like the story and rpg element should be the saving grace...... But honestly there's no excuse for any writer to offer something this generic. Being an RPG, presumably with branching storylines, perhaps there will end up being a reason for this. But realism should not mean boring and predictable. There's been no special effort here for story. Maybe it improves later but my god, just read a damn book, atleast the book won't try purposely waste for your time for some poorly implemented idea of "realism". Atleast losing your page on a book won't straight up cost you an hour of your life.

Watching cutscenes is torture, it's like an early elder's scroll game but without the lore and charm. You get a snippet of history and then irrelevant exchanges that show no charisma. I'm so uninterested, so I feel forced to watch the cutscenes in order to get invested in the characters. But half the time if i watch them, I'll hate every character for wasting my time. I don't know what to skip and what to watch. One that i should have skipped was when Henry wakes up after the death of everyone he loved, not even having buried his girlfriend, and hes already fawning over the girl who woke him laughing like nothing happened. So not only is Henry some bloody chump with nothing going for him, but he's also disloyal. GTFO here Henry. I am not helping your Bitchass get laid. you Henry.

I feel like i'm crapping all over this game unfairly. But I despise this character and the controls for this game. I see all the reviews for this game and wonder how anyone got past the start of this game.\t\t\t\t\t
