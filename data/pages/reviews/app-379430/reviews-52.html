It has A TONE of friction though - it is difficult (dying is frequent) AND saving system is a nightmare (I can't even tell how many hours I've spent on replaying same parts of the game because I was not able to save normally). Combat is difficult and far from intuitive, inventory management can be frustraiting. There are some bugs, but in comparins to the game's lenght and overall amount of content and mechanics, these are not important.
Setting is great, world is well crafted and full of interesting places, characters and stories. There is a huge number of interesting mechanics that interlinks. Graphics are great, as well as sound design and music. The story is interesting and non trivial.
I highly recommend this game, but it is clearly not for everyone (and it has the gut to be like this).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts a fine game up until a point. Despite my steam time, I've put about twenty five hours into it on GOG also, but the combat is just not worth the price of admission. It's fun against a single enemy, you have to actually think about what you're doing, but once there are multiple enemies it's just pointless. And fast travel in the late game usually involves a random encounter with almost no chance to avoid that then just starts you in the middle of four or five heavily armed combatants that you immediately lose.

It's a fun game for quite a while and I really admire the concept of it, but the execution is just not that great. Also, the save system is terrible. If you do decide to play it, just mod in unlimited schnaps so you can save anywhere. I'm very close to the end of the game, but I just can't bring myself to finish it. I keep trying to get back to the quest giver and initiate the final assault and the game just insists on repeatedly killing me in random encounters, or forcing me to spend twenty minutes holding down B to ride back there in pitch blackness. I'd wait for daylight, but then I get a random encounter and die also.

Not recommended overall, despite it's good points, its dogmatic design philosophy ultimately ruins it.\t\t\t\t\t
