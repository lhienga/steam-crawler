\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA bit over 8 hours in and it feels like a cheap project game from a college group assignment. The game is visually great, and the voice actors are superb. The story is also very engaging, and I want to play more but with the controls and combat system I just cant. Directional combat is already a terrible way of implementing sword combat, but usually a games quirks can be learned and eventually it becomes unconscious. The camera mechanics can be learned. The weapons can be learned. The enemy AI can be learned. But this game just fumbles the ball. The controls and enemy AI are so clunky, un-intuitive, and unresponsive that it makes the game so un-enjoyable to play that I just have to stop. Cannot recommend this game at all. I'm honestly sad as I would really enjoy working through the story, but the act of playing is such a poor experience I would rather watch a video of all the cut scenes.\t\t\t\t\t
