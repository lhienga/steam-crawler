\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStrongest points lie in aesthetics (aside from quite a few armour antics) and rpg mechanics , it's quite atmospheric and can be immersive. Graphics, world design and music all work really well.
The story is okay it does it's job, it's not exactly deep or engaging but it's not horrible. I feel like it does drag out quite a bit and tests your patience after one particular infiltration quest. Side quests are made to be more meaningful than just fetching. Side Activities get repetitive fast.
Combat is not all that great really. It's really easy, clunky and turns downright horrible against multiple foes with the AI camera and lock-on all working against you.
The game also is very buggy, i did not have the pleasure of encountering any soft locks, but many smaller bugs happened and a few times action in main quests also got tangled by this (e.g having to wake up an npc by shooting at him with arrows because he got stuck sleeping and won't ever wake, having to kill an npc with meta knowledge because he won't ever appear during the quest objective)
Sometimes the game fails to explain some things at all or in parts.
A strong 6 or 7 would be fair, still better than what is being pumped out now.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is awesome! Don't be discouraged by the difficulty, as this is a really fun game. It starts you off as a weakling that can't even cut a stick and transforms you into a Greek God. The game may piss off people who get mad easily. Just remember to actually read the tutorial or you will lose the easiest fights. This game doesn't hold your hand as you progress; if you are told to do something, you may fail at it as most things do have fail endings.

The only super bad part about the game mechanics is the clunky outnumbered fighting and the lockpicking on PC, which is way too easy. That being said, you should use your keyboard and mouse for lockpicking and your controller for combat. The graphics are okay; the game was made in 2015 so it really doesn't matter. Sometimes you can see through people's armor and sometimes under the ground. The story can get boring, so take that as you will; it's super long and can get confusing if you forget to read the quest book like I did.

The game is mostly historically accurate, containing real places in Czechia/Bohemia. The replay-ability is pretty strong as there are multiple endings for quests that give you different clues or information, as most of the time you play as a detective. Have fun!

WARNING! This game is super addictive. I spent 40 hours last week playing.\t\t\t\t\t
