\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll probably try finishing this game but I'm so close to just straight up quitting it and it comes down to one thing, the combat. At first I really liked it but the more I play I just see that it really only works when fighting one enemy and otherwise you just die and there is nothing to be done about it and it kills my mood every time. I'm literally at a point in a quest where I don't want to rat a guy out but I probably literally HAVE to do it to even continue because the only other option is to fight 5-6 people that surround me and the targeting system on top of that makes it completely impossible for me to complete any other way. Even if I try and run they catch up and even if I get on my horse I could be 10 feet away from them and my horse just kicks me off and I die anyway. There is NO winning in any 1vX scenario.

Edit after completion-
Not much changed tbh, combat is the main thing holding this game back. People can say skill issue or "just learn the combat", but in reality, there isn't much to learn. The "skill" or "learning" is just get master strike as early as you can and then backpedal while waiting for the extremely easy timing for the master strike and that simply isn't fun or at all skillful. Not to mention it isn't even the most annoying part of the combat. The targeting system is just horrible, you fight it more than you do the actual enemies to actually get it to lock on who you want it to, and the option to toggle it off to look around more freely would be good to help with this... if it didn't force your weapons/shield down when you do it to where you can't even use them to block and you just give them a free hit. Another annoying part is when the enemies lock you into an animation that you can do nothing about, you can be walking backwards and an enemy will rush up to you and all the sudden you get thrown into an animation where the game acts as if you charged them and they move hitting you in the back and thrusting you and there are instances that this happens multiple times in a row. Another issue with combat is that there are combo strings that just don't matter at all. You can learn all these combo's and try to use them sure, but you will hardly ever land one because anyone who can live through more than one hit will instantly master strike you, or they will take one hit and block or master strike/animation lock you on the rest so they don't matter at all. Even sometimes the literal peasants who hardly have a weapon and no armor at all will just randomly master strike or counter 5 attacks in a row no matter what you do and same with the animation locks that you have no control over. The bow could be better. You have no crosshair and a ton of sway even when you've leveled your archery so anything that isn't a close to mid-range shot, you might as well not even go for in most cases. Another thing, sometimes, and I get yes they have armor, what's the point of the piercing arrows if it takes 3, 4, 5, 6 shots to kill one man with a slow reloading weapon that isn't easy to shoot accurately? It's honestly odd to me that it's easier to shoot from horseback than it is on foot, if I'm being honest, the best way to combat in general is just sit on your horse and shoot, run, shoot, run, shoot, run and again just isn't very fun but you don't have much of a choice. I get the game is meant to be really realistic, but that doesn't matter to me when it just leads to boring and unfun gameplay that even still requires such little skill to even overcome. You might as well not even use the dog companion in the game, it has pretty much no use in combat and half the time will just run in front of you while you're fighting and you'll friendly fire it, it's more useful in the short DLC than in the actual main game.

Everything other than combat-

One other big issue I had was just armor and weapons variety and choices in general. From start to finish I hardly even changed my gear and if I did it was just to something that was more or less the same but with better durability. One piece of armor was an outer jacket and I literally only found one other kind to put there the entire game, and it wasn't even as good as mine while it was full durability and mine was complete 0. I used 2 weapons the entire game as far as swords go. I found one early on I used for a while, then I used my money to buy Herod's Sword, and there was never any need for another weapon the entire game.

Any other issues I had with the game were so minor they hardly matter such as dialogue in some cases is whatever and goofy animations in dialogue, it's fine. The story is good and really does a good job of getting you to connect with how the characters feel in certain moments that would be devastating to them, this game was not afraid to hold back on dark and sad themes and there are a good amount of moments that do feel tense where you don't know if something bad will happen or not. Some plot twist were pretty obvious but for the most part good. The all out battles were pretty interesting in the ways of being able to explore and scout areas before and having those things you saw actually come into play when planning (In the actual combat of these battles however most of the time you can just walk behind the enemies and hit them all in the back and they won't even turn to fight you, if they do only the one you're hitting will).

I enjoyed the alchemy system with how interactive it was without being extremely tedious and just overall annoying they did a good job with it.

It's actually not as bad as you would think having to buy food constantly, or having the need to sleep, to study books for skills in free time, or even only being able to save on sleep or by having to have the Saviour Schnapps Potion in your inventory. The potion that you need in order to save your game whenever you want is very easy to make and the ingredients are easy to find especially from any herbalist.

If you're looking for a game that you can enjoy the story and connect with character and can bare dying to bs a decent amount of times you'll enjoy the game, if you want something with good and challenging combat, there isn't enough of anything to learn. Learn to backpedal and press Q on a very easy timing check, or sit on your horse with a bow, that's about it.

Game has a ton of potential for a sequel if they do it right.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely love this game. The story is phenomenal and the gameplay mechanics create immersion in this medieval world. All of the side quests and dlc make coming back to the game an enjoyable experience. The progression system make you feel like Henry is just a normal guy who is learning about the world he is in, just like the player. There are a few big problems:
-Combat can be very janky. If you engage in combat in the wilderness, bushes often get in the way and cause either you or the enemy to rapidly move up and down. Any difference in elevation between you and an enemy may cause attacks, combos, and master strikes to hit the air or cause you to teleport to the opponent. Fighting multiple opponents at once is difficult at first, but once you learn master strikes, it is usually a pushover.
-npcs can be very stupid and easily fooled. If you stand on some logs or in a wagon, no npc will be able to touch you. The dog npc will often run in between you and an enemy causing you to hit the dog and for it to run away. Sometimes shop owners will leave their front doors open at night for no discernible reason.
-There are a few game-breaking bugs that can be fixed by loading a previous save. I had one where a story essential npc would not talk to me because the game still thought I was in combat. I had another similar one where I could not sleep or heal because the game still thought I was in combat
-Pausing the game or going into your inventory may cause dialogue to cut out for the rest of the line it's on and resume on the next.
-npc clothes clip constantly: The top of their bust may clip through their shirt, an arm may appear though a sleeve, knees clip through dresses and hose, etc. A lot of this happens when the npc loads in but it may stay that way until they are loaded in again. Weirdly enough, this also happens with Henry. When looking down while wearing leg plate armor, you may see Henry's hairy legs (when they probably don't need to be rendered?)

There are also a few non-issues that I'd like to see fixed in a sequel:
-npc dialogue can sometimes seem out of place. Shop npcs can sometimes ask "What do you want?" in a condescending tone but then the next line of dialogue will be welcoming
-There are some perks that you'd never want to take. Some of the main level perks give you a buff but give you a debuff at the same time. Those really only make sense if you are creating a character (like in a lot of rpgs) but the majority of those perks can only be chosen levels in. So you'll be in a situation where you may have leveled up your strength and warfare a few times but that progress goes away because you choose the stealth buff half way through the story (or you could just not select a perk and keep the perk point for a few more hours.)
- A lot of characters will walk around in, what would be considered at the time, their underwear. It's really jarring and immersion breaking seeing a character walk around town in a gown and sleeping cap in the middle of the day.

I wish there was a bit more post game content. You can go around and do the same quests that were offered before, which I like, but I can only imagine that a lot of the characters would behave a lot differently after the finale.
I don't know how they could do a direct story sequel where you play as Henry given that a lot of the mechanics revolve around progressing and at the end of the game, Henry has likely progressed a good few levels and mastered a lot of skills. Maybe they could do a game where you play as Capon (the end of the game seems somewhat like the start of Capon's journey). I would love to see a sequel to this game. Because this is Warhorse's first game, I know that they will definitely improve on the next. 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
