\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had KCD in my library for a few years now. Never really played it and now i can't put it aside.

What a charm. Immersive gameplay and hours of exploration to do. Me and my trusted steed are roaming the countryside, villages and cities carving up bandits, gaining love and wenches while i go. Fast travel has no use for me as me and Bayard travel everywhere to take in the scenery.

Most striking is the resemblance with the actual territory in which the game takes place. Google maps shows the same roads and places we travel to in the game.

My wife is complaining that i'm never with her anymore, but who needs a live if you can play KCD?

Graphics are great, sunrise, sundown, rainy days, it doesn't matter.

Fighting needs some getting used to. Now i got the hang of it and charging into a camp becomes fun, Come and get me! I'm collecting ears like a madman.

I hope this game never ends because i'm in love with it. I'm robbing towns blind and get a fancy coin for it which makes sure i can sustain my habit of hygiene and all the rest which comes with a bathhouse. Hope STD's aren't an issue in the game, because i still have my dearest girlfriend in the game.

I somehow became something like an academic in the game as i learnt how to read. No book is safe for me and i even stole every book in the inventory of the scribve and returned them after a good night of reading.

So Henry became a thieving thug, dressed in knightly armour, riding a sturdy steed and is fond of fighting, shooting arrows, making love, travelling, solving tasks and reading books, collecting herbs and cooking potions. Somehow i wish this was my life.

A real gem and if you like somewhat slower game and building up a character, i really would recommend this game in particular.
