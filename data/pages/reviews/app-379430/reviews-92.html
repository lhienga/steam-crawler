\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKingdom Come: Deliverance is a true gem of a RPG title. The setting, attention to the smallest detail and the pretty advanced fighting mechanics making it one of my favorite games in this genre.

Let's now go a little bit more into detail, shall we? The world you are going to dive into is just phenomenal and atmospheric. The developers were able to create a believeable and authentic story, which is set in the heart of europe during the middle ages and includes likeable and interesting characters. Pompous castles for the nobility who lives in sheer luxury, while the regular peasant have to plow the fields and live in his small wooden house outside the city centre. Different classes of people are working were you expect them to work and every person follows his or her own daily routine. Roaming around the coutryside or inside the villages is a pleasure to the eye, thanks to its very realistic art style. Everything just looks and feels right. It also includes the best looking forest i have ever seen in a video game until now.

The gameplay is very multi-layered and you have tons of different items you can craft and use. You start your journey as an illiterate peasant who will have to work his way up the foodchain. Henry will learn to read so he can study different books, which will give him better character traits, you will brew potions, go on hunting trips, take part in tournaments, go on treasure hunts and waste your hard earned money on dice games. The protagonist also have to do basic necessities like eat, sleep and should take his daily bath, because no one likes smelly people, right?

Now let's talk about one of the biggest critique points. The combat mechanics. Forget senseless button smashing, you need to build up skill through spending time with your trainer, so you will be able to become precise with your different weapons, learn the right attack patterns and get the feeling when the right moment for a counter comes. Some people were pretty overwhelmed by these machanics, it just takes time to get used to it and become good, however i really enjoyed this journey and it will gives you an extremely satisfying feeling, when you are able to fight against 4 enemies at ones and finish the fight as the last man standing. Are you an impatient person and become frustrated rather fast? Then this isn't for you. However, if you are still interested in it and want to give it a try, i would highly recommend to get all the DLCs. They add more story variety with diverse characters to it and also new items like armor sets and swords.

All in all, a pretty unique experience that puts you into a vast world without holding your hand. I liked the developers realistic approach to the topic and hope the second game will be as good as their debut title.

Have fun!\t\t\t\t\t
