\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is good overall, but there are a couple of fatal flaws for me. And had i known these beforehand, maybe I would have liked it better by playing it a bit differently.
The first is combat. I found it very frustrating at first until I got the idea that we have to do it a bit differently and more complicated. That could be great for some players, but you really need to want to do that sort of type of combat. You can have up to four strike combinations like stab, slash, stab, slash; all with five different locations on the opponents body using the target reticle. If you really are into that sort of "depth" say in your sword play, then this is probably good for you. But I am not interested in grinding out complicated sequences in attacks so I can finally deliver the strike, other when I do it by pure accidental. Later in the game you can use higher stats and just pummel opponents without the very complicated attacks. I am not sure about further down the questline because I am done with this bad rep thing.
The worst problem is the reputation thing. I have seen the whole "just be good" posts or the "just tip the merchants." But if you do not understand this reputation thing before you start, you will have a lot of time invested before you realize you are just screwed. The game has aspects similar to other games like stealing and pickpocketing. And I have never been much of a bad guy player, but I understood that quest lines will require somewhere along the line that I pickpocket someone, for example. So you go out in the middle of the woods and do what you need to do to get the skills up a bit. But even with no one around, you will get into trouble days later. It feels like a set up. Like when the bathhouse girls start to give you freebies because of, I think it was charisma. So I thought it was great that I could take care of clean clothes, clean guy, top off the food and rest stuff all for free. No, no ,no! That will get you into trouble too! Drinking is a skill. You have to drink in the questline. But it will get your reputation down!!! And until you figure this out, you are on a slow slide to the bad rep basement and you are done. I hit a thief being chased by his victim, and the guards attacked me! I had a guard come out of nowhere at a tavern and punch me. Well, in that sort of situation, I punch back. I mean, the dude is hitting me and I am supposed to get on my knees and beg his forgiveness??? Fun game! And now your rep is really bad and everyone is on your ass and there are for too many, now compounding, negative rep things going on, so you will never be able to correct it, even if you wanted to. But if you want to play a lily white character, and also one that will do what he is told, cower to authority even when it is wrong, and the game will be wrong in situations that occur all to frequently. You can not avoid it. But if you just do all that you are told, no matter how wrong for you that feels, you will do fine. Otherwise, you will have to figure out what the game makers think is moral or not, and walk that thin line carefully, because there are a lot of traps out there for your rep, and there is a point of no return.
But by this time you will have a lot of time invested in what seemed a promising game, other than early combat, and then becomes just, well, no fun to play anymore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKingdome Come: Deliverance

May God watch over you, dear reader!

I came, I saw, I delivered.

Henry is a sexy fella, and he knows it. He's a cheeky little monkey cutting about these Bohemian streets, but he ain't singing no rhapsody! He's cutting mo-fellas down where they stand and wooing the wenches while he's at it. He's got no issue running a brother through with a longsword, smiting a dude down with an axe, or simply giving a medieval Czech lad a thump on the 'ead when he deserves it. He'll strut about town on Pebbles his trusty steed (rev up the Bugatti, wheeeey), pedestrians be damned, on his way to his morning battering with Captain Bernard, the cheeky nonce. Then after a hard day's yakka getting clobbered to within an inch of his feeble existence, Henry hobbles back through the streets where he's met at the front door of his girlfriend's dodgy uncle's house by Theresa (isn't she lovely, wheeeey), who will undoubtedly comment on the appalling state of his clothing (pretty good given the absolute battering he's copped all day if you ask me, but I digress). But not before stopping of at the pub for a cheeky game of dice and ale or ten with the lads (WHEEEEEEEY!). Then he'll slurp down a lovely three course dinner of perpetual stew, followed by another perpetual stew, and a cheeky little treat of perpetual stew for dessert (ooooh, naughty), before climbing into the palace of dreams, otherwise known as a pile of straw with a pillow, where he'll snore the house down before doing it all again. What luxury!

What a life, just people living in the moment, not a single phone in sight! Just people, real people, being cut down in droves by invading Turkish bastards and, if they are lucky, surviving an invasion only to violently ♥♥♥♥ themselves to death with dysentery, just as the good lord intended, Jesus Christ be praised!

Happy Easter.\t\t\t\t\t
