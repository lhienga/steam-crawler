\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a good game! If you can get past the janky bugs, and can be bothered to figure out/deal with the saving and combat system pain points, you'll have a great time. Combat IS pretty tedious. You're gonna have to accept that and either learn to enjoy it or learn to cheese it. Chances are you *will* struggle and be frustrated sometimes. Take the game up on it's offers to teach you things, you're going to need it.

I find some of the choices made for the sake of "realism" to be silly and only really serve to be annoyances in the long run. Particularly since the game rejects realism often enough in other cases, it seems odd to die on some of these hills. Like bushes being made out of basically concrete. I get it, horses and people can't run through giant patches of bushes, but some of the smaller shrubbery should not be like running into a rock. Another example is guards harassing/arresting you for not using a torch at night, which would be fine, except none of the townspeople wandering use them and are left alone. You'll come across lots of little annoying things like these, guarantee it.

All that being said, there's plenty of great points. The game does it's best to adhere to life in Bohemia in the middle ages, and makes for a fairly unique experience. There's some cool real world history to learn along the way, personally I find this makes for good reason to go explore and unlock more of the codex. I find the herbalism/alchemy gameplay loop to be fun. You can drink your time away, gamble in taverns, participate in a tourney, hunt, turn to thievery and mischief, or you can be the unwavering good guy. Imagine that!

The main story is great, I've enjoyed pretty much all the side quests I've played as well. Note, I haven't bought any of the DLC so I have no opinions on those and what they bring to the table. The characters you meet are largely interesting, and Henry is a likable protagonist. The writing, environments and audio work (frankly, with a few VA exceptions) are beautiful and immersive. All of this is a perfect recipe to find yourself caring about what happens to people and places, and enjoying the journey.

Ultimately, I recommend this game. It's well worth giving it a chance. If you are willing to stick with and adapt to the rough bits, in my opinion, KCD will return your efforts in spades.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: This game makes you work for EVERYTHING, but Jesus Christ be praised- it is rewarding.

Training your skills has an entirely different meaning in this game because you ACTUALLY train. It gives you confidence, muscle memory, and strategy to use later in fights where you're exceptionally outmatched- and if you stay calm, you'll win. It's roughly similar to Mount&Blade/For Honor with the directional swinging, combos, and parrying, but it's noticeably slower than both. The smoothness of the animations makes up for the slower pacing since you don't feel like you're just spamming one attack (unless you are). In my opinion, Combat is the most off-putting part of this game but it feels so damn rewarding to just git gud at it. Practice with Captain Bernard every chance you get and the game will feel much better overall.

You are the main character, but your plot armor may as well be non-existent. Armor itself is dramatically important, and it's reasonably accessible early in the game to compensates for Henry being the trouble prone young lad he is. The stat screens for armor can be a little daunting, but spend a good while looking at a blacksmith's inventory and clicking over items to see how the stats look. There are more equipment slots than most games, so seriously take your time. It'll help you identify loot quickly later and be less overwhelming overall.

Alchemy is a gem. An actual minigame with tons of ways to mess up because you misread your recipe. Oh, and when I say MISREAD I mean that because Henry literally can't read until someone teaches him. What an amazingly immersive concept, and implemented in a way that doesn't feel overly hindering. Whoever came up with those two systems- I genuinely can't praise enough.

The story is well written, but I am writing this before I've finished the main quest- so I'll withhold overall opinions on this. I don't really have criticisms about it.

To wind down my dissertation on KCD, I do genuinely enjoy this game even if it's frustrating to high hell at points. If you like immersion, intensive combat, or just want to try a different style of medieval game, then I highly recommend this. Watch some videos online before you buy it so you can see for sure what you're getting into, but I hope that you enjoy this as much as I have!

Overall rating: 8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
