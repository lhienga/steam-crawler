\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou start as some nobody in the middle of nowhere with no skills whatsoever. And it shows! You quite literally cant even cut down a small branch with a sword. A brigand on the road? Nope! Gotta go fast!
But while the game progresses the protagonist becomes better and you wiser. Literally! There are tons of small and unexpected little things to find and learn here.

Game doesn't shy of inventing its own mechanics instead of borrowing pieces that work in other games. It also isn't afraid of being hardcore. And I'm not even talking about the difficulty of the same name, it is already pretty challenging on "Normal". "Hardcore" with all the negative perks - is an ultimate exam to what you've learned so far, and I've loved it!
The game also encourages logical thinking and creative way of solving problems:
- Need to infiltrate somewhere but you being stripped of everything when you enter? You can break in there at night and leave future self some money, weapon and lockpicks to have a headstart.
- Need someone dead in the heavily guarded area but don't want your reputation to suffer? You can wait until dark, knock them out and then kidnap to bring somewhere with no witnesses.

I see reviews with complains about bugs. Well as of current state it is quite stable for its scale. I had only 5-6 crashes for 3 walkthroughs, mostly during frequent alt-tabing. So it's already more more stable than Skyrim after 13 years of patching.

Overall: 10 broken legs out of 9 alcohol-induced deliriums!\t\t\t\t\t
