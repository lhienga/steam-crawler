\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLooking forward the second part!

I am glad to finally complete Henry's story! There are tons of stuff and the places of interest where you can spend your time with. Strictly recommended with DLCs making the game polished from prospective of characters' natures.
Regarding other aspects which are definitely might be improved:
1. Great combat system is vibrant and captivating at the beginning. Unfortunately, after 20-30 hours it feels mundane and even nerving closer to the end.
2. The last 2-4 hours of gameplay are extremely long and boring. Despite excellent and intriguing plot KCD makes you hang around forests to fight bums and look for enormous amounts of herbs like some grindy game.
3. Sometimes you stumbles into narrative dissonance because of clumsy plot turnings (ending of love story with Theresa, sir Radzig's snatch, etc.)
But totally it is impressive title having a great graphic even after six years from release.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKCD is a truly brilliant game that exceeded my expectations. Definitely among the most immersive and well made RPGs I've ever played. Keep in mind that this game grounds itself in historical accuracy and realism almost to a fault; it can be a bit tedious and challenging at times especially with it's save system that relies on consumable items to quicksave. I nearly ragequit and gave up on this game early on after losing progress so often but the system grows on you and I'm really glad I pushed onward and was able to experience this masterpiece. If you're looking for something different, a bit challenging, and interesting KCD is definitely worth your time and effort.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most Immersive and well done RPGs I've ever played. While there's not too much in character customization, Henry of Skalitz story is compelling and feels like a true to history rendition of 15th century Bohemia. With locations ripped straight out of the real world that you can visit on google earth. Arms and Armour straight from museums, and combat taken from HEMA and Real life fencing manuals. Deeply realistic, very fun game and the DLCs have added so much heart to it. Highly recommend this game for any history buff.\t\t\t\t\t
