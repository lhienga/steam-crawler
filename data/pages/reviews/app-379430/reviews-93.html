\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for a challenging and immersive medieval RPG then you'll probably enjoy this game. On the other hand, if you're looking for an experience akin to (vanilla) Skyrim, then look elsewhere, this game isn't it.

First thing's first, keyboard and mouse controls suck, I highly recommend using a controller. If you have some irrational thumb stick phobia and insist on using KBM controls, then... good luck, I guess.

Alchemy is a thing (and the system is pretty interesting to boot) but the 'potions' are somewhat more realistic, like buffed up herbal tonics. That said, there is no actual magic or weapon enchantments—unless you count things like poison or bleed effects, but those exist to some extent in reality.

Combat has a steep learning curve, but it's really not that hard once you figure it out. You can't just stand there and exchange blows with your opponent and expect it to go well for you, some amount of tactics and finesse are required.

On that note, until you figure out the combat, or at least get some decent armor, archery is your friend. Bows, if you can use them well, are kind of OP in this game. That said, archery still isn't exactly easy, you're basically blind firing unless you use console commands or mods to add the fire cursor and the arrows don't always go exactly where you aim. Again, though, once you figure out how it works, it's pretty manageable.

The stealth mechanic is fairly well done. There are no stealth indicators, so you kind of have to trust your gut when it comes to telling whether or not people know you're there. There are actual stats for noise, visibility, and conspicuity, all of which are effected by armor/clothing, footwear, character stats, and perks. It is immersive to the point that two otherwise identical pieces of clothing that are different colors will have different visibility and conspicuity modifiers. As a side note, stealth is also your friend early on. There are no sneak attack bonuses, but the benefits of getting an arrow or two off before enemies know you're there should still be obvious.

The story is actually really good, I think. The plot, broadly speaking, is the typical revenge narrative, but the storytelling techniques are outstanding. This is highly subjective, though, and there's really not much more I can tell without including spoilers, so you'll have to experience it for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t
