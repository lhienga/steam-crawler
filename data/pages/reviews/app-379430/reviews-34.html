\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had this game for a while and just now finally played it after watching a friend of mine play a bit of it. What drew me to this game is the indepth rpg elements, the unique combat that's difficult at first yet rewarding when you get the hang of it and the story. The historical setting is appealing and the game sticks to it even through it could be frustrating at times being dressed like a literal knight yet treated like a lowly peasant (because I am one). I really enjoyed my time with this game overall. I had moments where it started to stall and I started to lose interest but once I got over the compulsion to do every little quests (especially the activities) then the game started to really sink its claw into me. I would say 75-80 of my hours were fun with the 5-10 of it dealing with bugs or trying to grind some cash.

There are a few cons and I'll list them out.
1. Many of the quests are well done and I had fun with them but quite a few in this game are either poorly designed or just straight up broken or at least easy to break the progression in them. Luckily none of the buggy quests are the main story so I was largely fine.

2. The stealth in this game is pretty much broken until you level it up pretty high. I had people knowing some of the things I did without even having line of sight on me and somehow catching me. I ended up just leveling my stealth up by traders and books just to reduce the occurrence of this (plus the perks helps).

3. archery is kind of dumb. I understand the reasoning for not having a cursor but I shoot bows in real life and it does not make logical sense where I have to look to "aim" plus the sway side to side is over-exaggerated unless Henry is somehow shooting a 80+pd bow starting out. I ended up using the console commands to keep the cursor on and I would recommend doing that if you want to get good at using the bow.

4. This one is easily fixed via a couple of method but it bears mentioning. The save system is novel but It does not respect my time. I'm in my 30s. I don't have time nor the patience to just brew up a bunch of potions or find a bed to sleep just to save. Luckily it can be fixed by mods or changing something in the game files to be able to save mostly anytime (can't save in combat). This would have been a serious deal breaker if I couldn't remedy it because this game is hard when you're starting out so it's very easy to lose progress. I had times where I got ambushed by a bunch of Cumens and couldn't run so I ended up dying and causing me to lose a couple of hours worth of progress.

Negatives aside, This game is overall very well done and unlike most other rpgs i've played. Many of skills felt natural to level up and I just eventually became a very adept swordfighter and alchemist. Only other game to make me feel like a accomplished sword fighter probably would be the mount and blade series so that's great in my book.

If you love jumping into history and don't mind a little punishment here and there then I would recommend this game. I would also recommend keeping the wiki up in case a quest randomly doesn't make sense or just seems like it's not working. Often time it just requires talking to someone that isn't in the journal. Get the save mod or look up how to do it yourself. It's not worth doing it the intended way imo.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Combat is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ terrible and ruins the game entirely. Do not buy unless half price at least. The game is "fine" in spite of the combat, not because of it, and it actively makes the game less enjoyable.

- The enemy spams master strikes. This locks you into animations that guarantee you get hit (and also hit by their friends). They can master strike you even if they're not blocking in your direction, even immediately after you've clinched and won, even in the middle of being battered by a combo.
- The only way to win later fights is to spam master strikes yourself. You cannot use combos in 1v2+. You cannot use clinches in 1v2+. You cannot even attack in 1v2+. The camera and lock on system do not work AT ALL in 1v2+. It's just a complete mess of spazzy camera shake, forced animation locks, and poor controls.

- Enemies bum rush you in 1v2+ scenarios. You are given no tools whatsoever to counter this, except hope for a master strike opportunity. Enemies instantly tackle you when you turn to run. You cannot backpedal faster than enemies. You cannot attack one enemy as that allows the other to hit you in the back. You cannot change lock-on target if they are to the side of you, so you cannot block their attacks, nor target them with attacks.
- In real life, you're not just gonna sit there and let a guy run around the back of you with his weapon down, and that guy is not gonna want to rush headfirst towards a guy with his sword pointing directly at him. It's bad immersion AND bad gameplay.
- 1v2+ combat devolves into master strike spam, like the rest of the game, as it is basically the only feasible option against multiple heavily armoured enemies (other than using your horse to run away).

- Horse combat is absolute trash. No lock on, no combos, no parrying. Every enemy, no matter how weak and pathetic, can pull you off your horse immediately.
- Archery is terrible. No crosshair, no aiming, nothing to guide your aim at all. Also no archery perks. Random spread no matter how much of a god you are in agility, combat, anything.
- Polearms, despite being THE most used weapon in medieval times, have no perks, and cannot be your main weapon. You cannot store them, you cannot buy them.


1v1 the combat is ok. Except it's still master strike spam on either side, and requires mods to reduce their frequency, or to make them require even a minuscule level of skill.\t\t\t\t\t

I found myself asking "is the game going to decide to let me simply turn around so I can run from this ambush of 6 guys in plate armor?" Sometimes it lets you, sometimes it won't let you... It's hard to describe how frustrating it is being unable to simply turn around when you need to. The enemies also have this amazing vacuum fist power that can pull you into a grapple or tackle from 6 feet away - which whirls the camera around uncontrollably, takes control away from the player, and for someone less resistant to motion sickness I'm sure it'd be intolerable.

I like so much what this game was trying to do, but it's just not a good experience.

update: additional has about the most useless quest markers+map that I've seen in a long time. Either the marker is such a large area as to be of no help, or the quest marker is in a specific spot...that has like 5 different levels and is laid out like a madman's labyrinth. It is such a waste of time. It's like they didn't want to "lead the player around by the nose" but also they didn't GIVE YOU ANYTHING LIKE REASONABLE DIRECTIONS TO FIND THE PERSON. So many times the guy telling you to go find someone is like "well go find such-and-such; where is he? Well he's...around...somewhere..." and then there's either no quest marker or a useless one. Man alive! It is intensely frustrating!

update: way too many quests are like this:
+go here and talk to guy
++except woops can't do that do this other thing
+++other thing can't do it either right now go do other thing
++++well can't do that because...
+++++++++++++++++++AHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH
I'm way past the point of caring about what happens and just want to beat this dumb game because everything is so tedious and poorly done.

DON'T BUY THIS. I myself am gonna suffer through it because all of these ugly issues really reached a boiling point AFTER the refund threshold and I'm going to pile drive this trashgame.

addendum: it makes me laugh there's a claim to "realism" with this combat. Ironically a thing people seem to hate the most is the clinch mechanic but it's by far the most realistic thing about how armored combat actually works - I put on armor and fight pretty regularly: you want to know what almost never ends a match? Donking a guy in plate armor and a good helmet with a sword - because it does pretty much nothing. Most of the time what's going to end it happens in a clinch, even in an outnumbered situation. It makes me sad to give this critique because they clearly did some good hema research - but the "moveset" the bulk of the combat system is designed around was mostly for fighting a guy that's not in a buncha armor. What they actually did was take the "fight style" of fighting a single unarmoured opponent, and copy-paste it onto other situations it doesn't translate to: like outnumbered fighting or fighting well armoured opponents. Unsurprisingly, the devs failed to understand this and it doesn't work well at all.\t\t\t\t\t
