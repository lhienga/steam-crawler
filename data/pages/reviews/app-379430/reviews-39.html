\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI knew I would like this game, but man can I not recommend it enough. The game gripped me from start to end, the only times that I did not enjoy any of my 75 hour experience were when I had to replay parts of the game (usually due to my own incompetence), or during a known bug after a cutscene where the game enters an infinite loading screen. But even this flaw has a known fix with a mod, and mods don't disable achievements!
But enough about the few complaints I have, now for the plethora of compliments. I initially played this game because I am a perma DM for my DnD group, and playing this game has given me a greater understanding of medieval life in every facet, from the villages and towns, to the nobility, to combat, and everything in between. So this game has, and will, make my world far more immersive and interesting.
The writing in this game is also top tier, the progression from peasantry to near-nobility is very natural and feels earned. The characters in the game feel very realistic in a way that hasn't caught my attention in other games like it has in this one. There is one character in particular that starts as a very hate-able character, but turns to a good friend in a well-written way that, again, feels earned.
I also do just about every side quest in most games I play, and only one quest comes to mind as being a boring fetch quest, or a go here do this come back. All the side quests have an interesting narrative that actually makes you want to pursue them, and the comedic or strange quests don't feel out of place at all. And many of the quests feel as if they have actual repercussions, as the outcome of the quests regularly come up in dialogue options, or other world effects caused by your actions. Oh, and all of the quests I believe to be boring and repetitive are still there, if that's something you're interested in, which exist in the form of activities, a separate quest tab from the side quest.
And the DLC are just the icing on the cake, most of the DLC adds substantial new enjoyable content. I'll go over each here in order of my personal least to most favorite. Treasures from the past adds the least, with a couple treasures to find and a unique armor set that isn't too good. A Woman's Lot puts you in the body of a separate person during the tragic events of the beginning of the game, which is alright, but it also adds a great side questline where the outcome of each quest along the way majorly affects the ending, which I thoroughly enjoy. Band of Bastards add more large combats between many people, which were one of my favorite parts of the game, and the characters added with it are fantastic as well. The long-named Hans Capon DLC adds more quests involving one of my favorite characters and his goofy pursuits. And finally, From the Ashes lets you build up a ruined town from nothing, which is a specific niche that I very much so enjoyed, and it feels so rewarding to see all the effort put into the town make itself evident.
This game is enjoyable from start to finish, and if you are interested at all, I would highly recommend the game.\t\t\t\t\t
