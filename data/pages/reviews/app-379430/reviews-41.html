\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery mixed feelings about this game. On the one hand, the progression systems are interesting, the plot and characters are interesting enough, and it looks great. But the actual gameplay, the combat in particular? It's difficult to describe exactly what's wrong with it if you haven't played it yourself, but it's just...it's just terrible. It's a directionally based combat system, like in Mount and Blade, in that the direction you swing your weapon determines where you hit the enemy - so you can hit them in the head to knock them out, injure their limbs, etc. Blocks are also directional. However, Mount and Blade is arcadey enough that it can get away with the jankiness inherent to a system like this. In this game it really doesn't feel good at all. Maybe if the controls weren't so unresponsive and unforgiving - your inputs will constantly be ignored if you didn't do it at precisely the right moment, for instance. But at the same time spamming inputs will cripple your efforts, so...

I can understand what they are going for here at the macro level: you are a dumb medieval peasant who doesn't know ♥♥♥♥ (even though you are a blacksmith's son/apprentice and should logically have picked up some basic skills, plus you wouldn't be a wet noodle where strength is concerned, but alright...). So obviously, attempting to engage in combat with trained soldiers will see you get your ass kicked. Constantly. IT MAKES SENSE, YES. I don't mind needing to avoid and run away from enemies I know will kick my ass.

But the actual mechanics of the combat system are ridiculous. They are not intuitive at all. I think you need to be a certain kind of person for this kind of system to click, but for anyone else the controls feel just bad and unresponsive, and trying to fight two or three enemies at once? Yes, in most scenarios IRL you will lose a 2v1, a 3v1, that's realistic. So maybe they justified the terrible lockon system (seriously, try fighting more than one enemy at a time in melee and tell me it isn't the worst implementation of a lockon system you've ever seen) and janky controls by telling themselves it simulates you being overwhelmed and dominated by superior numbers? I don't know.

Fighting a single opponent feels alright, you have time to actually make use of the obtuse directional combat. But it very rarely feels FUN, and even basic enemies you encounter just going about the main quest have an absurd amount of health and combat ability that feels cheesy. Even early on, you can be ambushed by groups of very well equipped enemies as you travel the map. Even if they are poorly equipped bandits, there is no amount of skill that will save you when you are ambushed by a bunch of dudes who constantly knock you off your horse, surround you, and beat the the ♥♥♥♥ out of you. Of course that's realistic, and that's what they were going for. But I don't think they ever once stopped to ask themselves, "is this actually fun to play?"

Alright, so the combat is still ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, the restrictive save system is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, but I got drunk with a priest and beat the ♥♥♥♥ out of the bailif. So, 10/10. Also Theresa is a gem and a seriously underutilized character. 10/10.

Basically, screw the obnoxious combat and other absurdly harsh systems that do nothing but waste your time. Use the cheat mod and enjoy the characters and plot. It's pretty good in this case. At the end of the day, you see the same end credit scene as anyone insane enough to play through this masochistic nightmare "legitimately".\t\t\t\t\t
