\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fun, not really as an RPG but more as a sneak peek into medieval life.

You're completely railroaded into playing a defined character which is meh, and there's really not much in the way of choice. You're playing out the defined character's typical rags to richest story instead of your own. There's some flavor choices you can make along the way, but mostly it just changes a few lines of dialogue, and the story plays out the same way no matter what you do.

The combat is also straight up a**. Like alot of people have said, the main problem is target lock on + the parry system.

Lock on makes fighting multiple enemies a chore, since the whole game is mandatory first person, which means your field of view is really narrow. Your character automatically tunnels onto one enemy, meanwhile two others are wailing on you from behind and the camera is shaky and janky and hard to figure out what's going on or who's where, as you constantly get stuck in forced animations.

The parry system is timed blocks, which sometimes result in RNG "master strikes." The problem is everyone can do them, and the enemy master strike RNG is 100%. Low level enemies can master strike. High level enemies can master strike. Bathhouse wenches can master strike. So...you'll be a plate armor wearing knight, armed to the teeth with a heavy warhammer and shield, highly skilled in a variety of weapons, max warfare and strength stats, riding proud on your warhorse, that's also armored to the teeth and looking fabulous in noble finery... then get taken down by two Jedi peasants dressed in rags wielding sticks.

You can win those fights, but the cheese for combat is literally kiting in a backwards circle, the whole time spamming block and waiting for master strike RNG. As long as you never actually attack you can win pretty much every fight no matter the odds, which as soon as you figure that out makes combat really tedious and dumb. It would take alot of attacks to defeat a armored knight...or just one face stab master strike. That's all there is to it.

So why recommend it? Mainly because the world is interesting enough that I put 70 hours into it, which is worth the cost. I didn't finish the main quest but who cares, it's pretty boring and turns into fast travel across the whole map fetch quests by the end anyways. The scenery is also great to just roam around the countryside exploring, and by far the side quests and DLCs were way more interesting than the main quest and are the only content with actual consequences.

There's just not many games out there nowadays that take creative risks. This game is a pretty unflinching view of medieval times and social hierarchies, and it really focuses on the mundane stuff which makes even the smallest village feel alive, NPCs have daily routines etc. There's no way a AAA studio would have the b***s to make this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly this game is very interesting and has a lot of fun systems. Literally just going hunting can be cool, or making potions, interacting with NPCs. The story isnt that awful, the main issue is that the game has a humongous flaw in one of its main systems - Combat.

The main issue with combat in KC:D is the introduction of the "Master Strike" system, aka parrying. The issue, mainly, is that every single enemy in the game can do it. Peasants, bandits, farmers, man-at-arms, everyone. For being a "master" skill, its very ubiquitous. Why is parrying an issue, and enemies being able to do it an issue, you might ask? Because they can, and will, master strike/parry every single attack you throw. Doesn't matter what direction, whats happening, they hit the input and now you're put into an animation where you loser all player agency and are forced to receive damage because the game said you had to.

The secondary issue of this, it completely removes any amount of "skill" from the combat, or tactics, because an enemy can literally be facing away from you, but if you attack them and theyre aware, theyll master strike, stab you in the gut and send you falling forward. So how do we handle this? The main way is to ONLY master strike your opponent. So you sit there, staring at one another, waiting. Occasionally your enemy will "clinch" you, think boxers hugging. 9 times out of 10 they win and push you around and open you up to an attack and since you're in an animation of falling around, you cant parry. Glorious.

If youre determined to participate in the rock paper scissors match of combat this game has, I recommend wearing no armor and just using a shield and warhammer. Hit them in the head, warhammers have a chance to automatically knock someone out if you hit them in the head, and theres a perk that increases this chance.

Good luck, have fun, but i really cant recommend a game that constantly breaks up its somewhat interesting story with some of the most frustrating combat ive ever experienced.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter suffering through 103 hours of this train wreck, the game decided that Bernard and everyone at the main quest location wanted to kill me. So I resolved to use cheats and kill them all to ease my indescribable amount of nerd rage. Then I discovered that the game will simply not let me attack Bernard, and no amount of cheats would sooth my seething anger.

Story and VA is 10/10, very lovely setting and game world. Mechanics are for the truly self loathing and masochistic, complete garbage gameplay every step of the way. The eating sounds in my headphones alone should warrant me a refund, but I did enjoy my time as Henry, from a character standpoint. Difficulty in this game is not based on how skilled you are, how well timed your parry and riposte is, or how high you raise any of your weapon skills. Every swing, every block, every arrow is essentially RNG. Any fight you find yourself in without your weapon already drawn and fully ready for combat is a near guaranteed loss.

Do not put yourself through this like I did. Over a hundred hours of my life wasted. I'm off to youtube to watch the cutscenes and see how Henry's story unfolds.\t\t\t\t\t
