\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's some real charm to this game- the world is beautiful and the developers clearly have a real appreciation for the period and region. Just starting out, the game seems huge with so much to see and do in a living, breathing medieval world...

...but, sadly, so much of that is an illusion. As the game wears on, you realize that many of the organic systems... aren't. Hunger? Free food is literally everywhere. Sleep? Henry can go literally days without it. Combat is directional and has lots of combos, but the only thing you'll ever do is parry until the enemy is exhausted. Money is way too plentiful and trivializes large chunks of gameplay. Quests are often buggy or end in unsatisfying ways with no followup- one quest has you heal the sick and injured, but even hundreds of in-game days later they're still ill. Another quest involving witches has no real player agency; the correct choice is to just ignore it. That expansive world? Empty. Barren. Nothing to do, nothing to see- unless you count deer, hares, and randomly generated bandits as content.

On top of all that, the game is jank and still buggy even years later... and the save system egregiously punishes you for the crashes the game will inflict. Ultimately, it feels like a passion project where the developers' reach exceeded their grasp. Hopefully, they'll be able to realize their vision in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly, I will say that this game has a lot of hardcore fans. That is because it is an extremely hardcore game.

I thought long and hard about how to rate KCD. It is very immersive, has good graphics (It also has a lot of copy-paste buildings though) and decent voice acting and the few story line arcs I completed were pretty well done. Initially I thought that I was in for a really great time.

Then it became clear that while the developers had put a lot of effort in creating a hyper-realistic medieval world simulator that they had done such a good job of that that they have accidently sucked all the fun out of the game.

Because it is ultimately a game and games are supposed to be fun, which unfortunately this is not.

So why is it not fun?

Well it is extremely unforgiving. Save games are rare (you can forget save scumming) and the controls are poor. You will find yourself playing for an hour only to find yourself in a fist fight where you get the tar beaten out of you due to the terrible combat mechanic and then find yourself going back to where you were sometimes hours ago.

One time I was walking in a town and a bandit ran past with another villager in hot pursuit, which was kind of cool, so I joined the chase and tackled the perp. Then the townie caught up, fists started flying and suddenly I was being attacked by both of them. I guess I must have accidentally hit the villager while I was fighting with the idiotic combat system. Uhoh, time to lose an hours worth of progress I suppose (no good deed goes unpunished)

Before the game even starts you are thrown into a scene where you must escape a rampaging army by stealing a horse and trying to ride it away with no idea how to control it while several enemies are determined to turn you into an arrow pincushion. I almost rage quit right there.

However I persisted and got through that but really it doesn't get much better after that.

The way the map works is also annoying. It puts like a giant chess piece on the map to represent where you are and it blocks out whatever is adjacent to you. If you could zoom further in then that would be OK but you cant.

I do like the idea of managing food and sleep. Also filth as well as the state of your clothes affects your charisma and ability to speak to people. It's all good in theory but it also adds to the overall difficulty. On their own these things would be fine, add the combat and it becomes not worth the time.

Many people will (and apparently do) like this game and if you are into hardcore realism with poor controls then you will like KCD I'm sure.

For me I don't want to waste hours of my life replaying the same bit over and over again (sometimes having to walk for miles and miles just to get to the bit that was troublesome only to die again, rinse lather repeat) and being terrified that a hostile stranger might be just around the corner causing me to be confronted by the abdominal combat mechanic once again.

Perhaps if I was younger, had better reflexes and was willing to sink hours of time into honing my combat skills then it would get better. But then I just want to play a game, not have what amounts to a full-time job that doesn't pay my bills.

It is clear that a lot of effort has been put into making a quite beautiful game but I think the developers dogged insistence on showing no mercy to the player is ultimately where it is let down.

If they had provided more options in the area of controlling the realism/difficulty to make it more accessible to people who don't want a hardcore experience (while still offering that as an option) then I would give it a thumbs up.

Other people have complained that when you are not being annihilated by the combat mechanic the game is tedious and boring. I can see how some people might think that but I am somebody who played the entire Skyrim main quest without ever fast travelling (because I thought it broke the immersion lol), so that kind of thing really doesn't worry me personally.

P.S. I played with KBD+MOUSE and things might have been different if I had used a controller. Perhaps that is how it was designed to be played because the UI does seem to be designed for a console controller in my view.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDispite my nearly 70h of playtime in KCD, I tend to NOT recommending it. The story is "ok'ish" - neither boring, nor absolutely stunning (like TLoU for example). There is an hugh amount of cutscenes during the main storyline. And dispite my absolutely love for such kind of storytelling through lots if cutscenes / cinematics I couldn't develope any love for this game. The main problem for me was the way the developers tried to keep certain aspects of the game "realistic", like the weapons, the combat, the travelling (including sleep/food) but couldn't decide, if they should've double down with ALL the aspects. It appears, that they got lazy over time or were too afraid of the lashback. It is unnerving, that some aspects were kept "realistic"-hard , others very easy and again others so absurdely hard, that you get frustrated over time... For example: You can whistle and your horse magically teleports directly behind you (which is nice). Then there are situation in which you can ride with full speed nearly through giant trees - then other situations, in which you can't get past a tiny bush or any other obsticle and die from 4-5 hits from a club...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Worth a shot if you don't mind a more "Realistic" open-world game that doesn't hold your hand.

This is one of those games I wish I could give a "sideways" thumbs up to but I feel I can still give it a tentative recommendation. The story is interesting enough to keep you around but the game really zeros in on the fact that Henry is a peasant blacksmith's boy and starts with virtually no skills, which can cause the game to be frustrating to start when you have no weapon skills and can barely talk your way out of any crimes you might commit. But it starts to become more and more engaging when you hone your skills, I for one found it very fun to be a amoral person who knocked people and and lock picked their goods until I can actively fight more effectively... but even then there was a few times I almost dropped the game with how often I needed to reset, as the game wants you to live with your actions instead of being able to get away with everything ala Skyrim (even though you can still save scum). So if you can tolerate having to reset often or live with your actions and you slowly get stronger then I can say it's worth a shot, otherwise steer clear\t\t\t\t\t
