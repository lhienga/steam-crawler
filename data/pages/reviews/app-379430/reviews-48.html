\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo begin, overall if you like realistic games and medieval settings, this is really the only game like this. For $30, it is definitely worth the price.

Pros:
- The combat can be really fun, especially once you get good at combos, reading attacks, parrying, and balancing multiple enemies.
- The gameplay is realistic, and you will die whenever you try to attack large groups, unless you are a full knight and they are peasants.
- A lot of game time and a lot of good quests. A good story as well.

Cons:
While I recommend the game for the price, there are some areas the developers did poorly or got lazy, and I will outline them here. Overall, I still recommend, but I want to draw attention to these.
- A lot of quests have multiple spin offs. And then more spinoffs off of those. It is really annoying to have a quest lead to you doing 6 other side quests. Person A tells you to go to Person B to get something. Oh wait, Person B can't give you that until they get something from Person C. But Person C needs you to solve the black plague in a town before helping you. It's annoying.
- A plus of the game is combat and how realistic it is. The issue is, you get punished immediately for making a mistake. I've beaten Elden Ring so I get that. But when combat is clunky and screw you, it's really frustrating. I really recommend pulling fights only into open areas. Anything close quarters is really awkward.
- The final quest detours a solid 100 times and I ended up skipping all the cut scenes because I got so bored.
- Good physics exist but aren't applied to all events? There is a trebuchet that is very nicely done. But when you load it it's static. Another example is when you get tackled by an opponent. Prepare to complete lose your orientation.
- There's a quest where you can't ride your horse. You can call it and mount it, but it just kicks you off. instead of not letting the horse spawn near you, they coded it to kick you off? Why.

So yes, a ton of pain points. Overall worth it but the developers really make this game feel like it is still in beta.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played through Kingdom Come: Deliverance to close out 2023. I had previously tried both in 2018 and 2020 and both times I bounced off without much enjoyment. But upon my return I found that the issue was not with the game itself, but with Henry!

Let me explain: Henry is your player character, he is the son of a blacksmith and a peasant through and through, hes never fought with a sword let alone killed a man. So starting the game you have nothing, no skills, no knowledge, and absolutely no gear. Henry was no god of war, he was no legendary hero, he was just a boy on a quest for revenge.

I as a player didn't understand that, I thought I would be a lethal killer from the get go. When confronted with the truth I was initially turned off but upon my return in 2023 I chose took take the time to understand. Which led me to my second revalation: I was henry!

I was a noob who didnt know how the combat mechanics worked. I didnt know what the map. Heck I didn't know anything. I was the peasant son of a blacksmith on a quest for revenge. Together Henry and I would grow and learn, we would begin to excel and triumph in the face of adversity.

Kingdom Come: Deliverence opened up into one of the most immersive and well thought out RPGs I have played in recent memory. Every part of the game was generally fun and enjoyable. The combat is unique and immersive, the armor system makes sense with different specialties depending on your playstyle. The world is expansive and each town and hamlet has something unique and enjoyable about it.

Overall I really enjoyed the game and would recommend it to others who enjoy immersive RPGs or games with a historical focus.

8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe story... the combat... the characters... this game is so immersive and fun that after 160+ hours it feels like the game is just beginning.

And that'd be my only complaint; I wish there was more content! Every quest has a lot of love and detail put into it... it never feels like the modern Assassin's Creed games, where it's clear that they just wanted to fill it to the brim with repeatable, bland activities for the sake of making the game bigger. Everything has a purpose, everything feels well thought out.

Yes, it definitely has its flaws, as you might expect from an indie studio taking on such an ambitious project. However, I would say the imbalances add more character than they do frustration (other than the very slow rendering times, which is more concerning than charming).

I can't wait for the sequel! Thank you, Warhorse, for a great experience.
