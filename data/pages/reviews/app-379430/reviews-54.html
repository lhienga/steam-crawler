\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, this is a good game. I thoroughly enjoyed every minute of it. Yes, it's true what they say, that the combat is very different, frustrating and downright angering at times. However, that is a part of what makes this game great in my eyes. It forces you to play differently, to think differently, and to actually get good at something. Most of the other games nowadays, the combat is pretty much all the same. You swing the sword, have a blocking mechanic, maybe some spells, but overall, most games featuring swordplay are basically the same in the combat department.

Not so with KC:D. You have to time your strikes, look for openings, take your time, and think about the fact that yes, even on this 1v1 fight, you could die, and if you didn't save at some point close to this battle, you will lose your progress. It makes you look at the combat, and your choices of engaging or not, a very real decision that can have very real consequences.

The fact that they took a real world place, made the map after it, the buildings, etc, is just very cool, especially for a gamer that enjoys this period of history. This was also a major motivator for me to purchase and play this game.

The fact that it's not all just infinite currency, getting caught doing crimes will land you in jail, people react to you differently based on your choices of behavior, etc, just add to the complexity of the game.

Now, don't get me wrong. The game is not perfect. But I do think people nowadays, have a very high standard, when in reality, a game doesn't have to be perfect to be a very enjoyable game. Are the graphics perfect, no. Are the dialogues perfect, no. Is the combat perfect, no. Did the game leave some very unsatisfying moments, especially near the end and the end of the game, absolutely. I'm not going to go to far into the whats and whys, because that will leave spoilers for those of you who wish to play the game. I will say this. You can get very much into the story of the game, and like reading a good book or watching a good TV series, you WILL want to know what happens next. You will want there to be a grand conclusion, but, you will be left with questions unanswered, quests not complete, and the story will come to a very abrupt end right when the climax happens. That, indeed, was VERY disappointing for me. I very much love medieval history, and the stories that come from that era in history. And when the story, even though largely fictional, is not done, and I'm ready to explore more, and complete that chapter of the game, and I cannot, that leaves a very large hole for me, and I don't like wondering what happened next.

People say that a part 2 of this game is in the works. We haven't heard any news on it, nor any teasers or confirmations at the time of this writing. That is sad, because this game is one of the few that really hit the mark for me, despite all of it's shortcomings. I still play it, after 72 hours in game so far, because it allows me to scratch that itch of living a medieval life, even though it's through a screen.

The world is beautifully crafted, although, parts of the map with the colliders on the bushes that you can't jump over or can get stuck in, or force you to walk the long way around just to go down a hill, that can be annoying for an open world game. The forests are nicely done. The castles are not overdone in a way that makes them feel made up and fake, or more fantastical than real. The battles are decently done, not an industry changer but also not poorly integrated into the game. You feel like you are a part of the unit of fighting men, and not the hero of the land, which is nice. You don't get special treatment, and you are just an average guy, who came from nothing and fights and makes his own coin and way in life.

The game has a few glitches in quests, but they can still be completed for the most part. You just have to figure out how to get it done, or do a quick google search for others who ran into issues as well with certain quests.

Overall, I think you can get a ton of enjoyment for your money out of this game, the team who made it was passionate about it, and that means something, unlike many large studios today who are only out to make millions off of a cookie cutter product with pretty visuals (partly to blame for many modern gamers thinking a great game is all about the next level particle effects and next tier resolution textures on their low poly models).

We need the part 2 to this game though. We need the story to be concluded. We need to explore more of this land, the people, the time period, and the unfinished quest. I'm not going to accept the (the moral of the game is some things just can't be completed in life). That's insane, and the people who have said that, that's cool for those of you who are OK with that, but many of us want the conclusion, the sword, and to go to with Lord Capon to deliver the message and kick some butt!!!

If you love medieval history, medieval time periods, and good stories, pick this game up and give it some hours. It's good, just look past the flaws, get good with your sword, explore the world, make sensible decisions with when to engage etc... and you will be alright.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know that I'm not far into the game by any means at this point because I saw that a basic play through for this game is about 130 hours, but I had to write a review for this game anyway.

First off I would like to say that this game is amazing. I'm enjoying the story and the overall feel and look of this game. The graphics aren't knock your socks off status, but for what this game is, I find it extremely beautiful and I cant wait to just wonder around and experience everything KCD has to offer. Aside from the known minor things this game has gotten a bad wrap for like the combat system, It's actually a very different and ingenious way of going about it. Once you level up Henry and become familiar with the fighting, It all of a sudden doesn't seem so "clunky" anymore and I genuinely enjoy it.

The story itself so far is something I have been really enjoying also, Overall This is worth the buy and play through.

I don't know if I can make recommendations for future projects here, but I'm going to anyway and hopefully some people will agree. If this game is this good, I could only imagine what a KCD based game would be like in the Viking/Nordic times. With this Fighting system and the possibility of it being refined over a few games to make it even better, I could only imagine what that game could come out to be, especially during any raiding scenes. With this game being what it is, Warhorse studios could knock the ball out of the park with this setup in the Viking time era. I really hope they see this, and if they do, I would love to help out with ideas.\t\t\t\t\t
