\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKingdom Come: Deliverance, has so much heart.

I've not played a game like it since Dragon's Dogma or Fable. You can tell the developers put their heart and soul into making something truly special. Everything from the story, graphics, sound design, OST and characters are absolutely phenomenal. I think it's such a huge achievement for a start up game developer to deliver something like this and it should be celebrated.

However, with great aspirations can come great technical drawbacks. There are still many bugs and issues with this game that they're kind've hard to count. Things from frequent texture pop-ins during cutscenes, floating voices, model clipping & holes, infinite loading screens to name a few. They're not game breaking, but they do take you out of a lot of pinnacle moments in the story.

Additionally, the game does fall short on a few things. The dialogue conversations are frequent and pretty boring to watch. You will go through hundreds of scenes of characters talking to each other, stood still with a thousand yard stare, monologuing about who knows what. The combat can be super frustrating pretty much through the whole game, with constant NPC reversals of your attacks making combat arduous and drawn out. A lot of quests are go here and talk to this person, then this person, then this person etc.

With all that said, Kingdom Come: Deliverance is a monumental achievement in every other way for a new studio. I loved the main characters, I loved the story and I loved the world. With a bit more polish, a bunch more cut-scenes and some more varied quests, they could be onto something even more groundbreaking than they've already achieved.

I am definitely looking forward to a sequel.\t\t\t\t\t
