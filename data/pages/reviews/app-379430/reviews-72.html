\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEver since the announcement of this game 6 years ago I wanted to play this but I never could because at the time I didn't have the necessary groschen and my PC was not that strong. Time passes by and I get it on sale last year but don't really play it until a month ago.

Let me tell you that I've been playing for 31 hours and I'm only at the beginning of the game, haven't done main story quests yet and I'm having a really good time. The game starts pretty brutal and it might threw off even the most hardcore and most casual players out there. At first I was almost angry when I got stun locked in combat because I couldn't fight back, but once you realize you're the son a blacksmith without fighting ability you start to learn the basics, level up, get your arse handed a bit and finally get a sense of satisfaction I haven't felt since I played Dark Souls in 2011. You can't read either so you need to find a scribe, practice your speech, maintain your own weapons, sleep and get drunk. Eat too or you might get quite hungry.

I'm one of those guys that don't quite enjoy gaming anymore, I play online video games from time to time but since KC:D I've been playing for hours now and I'm glad I waited all this time to enjoy this game. The medieval realistic setting is gorgeous and if you want to play a ''realistic elder scrolls'' this is your game!

In my 30 hours of gameplay I've stolen the Necronomicon from a monastery to exorcise a ghost, knocked out a monk for a key, got jobs for my mates, 1v5'd some Cumans, poisoned a bandit camp, won an archery contest for some wine to get laid by m'lady, serenaded a horse, lost to a mercenary on the road and then stealth choke'd him (I restarted don't worry) and got a haircut. There's more but you catch my drift.

Buy this game if you love immersion in your games... I'm feeling quite hungry.\t\t\t\t\t
