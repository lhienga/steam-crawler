\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would say if the intro doesn’t immediately captivate you, then this game may be an unforgiving slog, and you may want to request a refund in that time because it takes < 2 hours to get there. If you’ve played through to the next town and enjoyed it, then prepare for a very satisfying, immersive RPG experience.

The game is ‘hardcore’ but only in the sense that it tries to be realistic. You’ll be constantly repairing clothes, shoes, armor and weapons. Henry needs to eat, sleep and bathe as well. The game does, however, weave all of this masterfully into a satisfying gameplay loop that reinforces the immersion. You’ll find yourself planning trips to town to take care of your armor, make some potions, bathe and talk to some people of interest, making sure you have enough time to make it back to Rattay for the big tournament!

There are even some days where you essentially become a monk. The Monastery mission and the Theresa DLC are good examples of poorly received quests that I feel are masterfully done if you have the patience to follow through Henry’s story, and it is definitely satisfying to do so in the end.

In terms of actual gameplay, the combat is clunky. Once you get the hang of it, it becomes very rewarding, but the game really does make sure you know in the very beginning how weak you are, and you’ll have to plan your strategies accordingly. Still, if you’re looking to role play in 15th century Bohemia, this only added to the experience.

I give it 5 master strikes!\t\t\t\t\t
