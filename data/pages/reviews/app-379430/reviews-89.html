\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommended with a warning.

I can see why this game got mediocre reviews and just why so many people quit. I stuck it out and the game is finally fun, 30 some hours in.

Not enough was done to actually teach how to fight in combat as it is an odd way of using a mouse and keyboard. Once a person "learns" a new way of PC combat things get better. I am proficient now but that learning curve as opposed to the "regular" way of fighting in games is a wee bit of forced learning, I cannot say it was fun.

The game is fun now and I am 80 hours in taking my time with the main story line, though taking 20 hours plus to get to the "enjoyment factor" is a tough sell for the average gamer and likely why so many became frustrated and quit.

Once in, and once a person has learned how to fight, it is a great game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright so 30 hours into kingdom come and past the first boss I've got thoughts

TLDR the game is a great 8/10 it has amazing combat and depth in the roleplay, has many glitches reminiscent of elderscrolls and only has a few flaws that hold it back.

full thoughts
The good
The depth of combat is excellent, on par or surpassing dark souls making good use of rhythm, moves and counter moves, and finding patterns in your enemies
The depth of the rpg is excellent, you feel absorbed and connected with your character both in a relatable way and in an intrinsically linked way as you make the character what you want.
The story so far has been wonderful along with the setting and realism adding to the worlds substance and the feeling of it all being alive with characters having schedules and lives while you're not around.

The not so good
I wish there was more information on different things, status effects, bleeding, weapon effects, piercing vs armor, what durability actually does, poison, who counts as nobility/ who counts as peasantry. Many things that there are no tool tip or description for in the codex that leave you confused when deciding weapons or dialog that would be helpful to know.
The bigger one, skill level and character level. So there is a point when playing with his game that despite all of the combat depth, complexity, and intuitivity, that you can be outmatched by an enemy not because of a lack of skill on the players part, but because of a lack of levels on the characters part leading to situations that andur and I have both had where it becomes physically impossible to deal damage to an enemy. You can stagger them, repost them, kick them, or swing at them, but in all situations if you are not on the same level as them they can recover fast enough that you literally can't get a hit in even being frame perfect, this leads to 2 problems
1: player frustration. This occurs in andurs instance where to advance the main story he has to beat a character like this, if you do not spend a substantial amount of time on the side quests like I did almost 30 hours, then you can very easily come into this for that unprepared making it not just hard, but physically impossible to beat normally. My instance of this happened on a side quest that is available right after the end of the introduction in the tournament grounds, I could beat the first character in the tournament but no matter what I did to the second person it was physically impossible to over come them or even land a hit, but recognizing this I could back out of the tournament and come back later, in andurs you can't back out unless you go back a substantial amount of time, you have to fight the guy and win which leads to the second point
2: the bow is cheese. I know completely why they removed the cross hair for the bow, it's game balance. The problem with the bow is it does a realistic amount of damage with an unrealistic use case. The bow can 1 or 2 shot most enemies in the game, even the most heavily fortified knights stand no chance against a bow with piercing or wounding arrows, the problem comes in the fact that as they player, you don't have fear of dying and you don't have limits of movement, so you regularly use it at obscenely close range to heavily damage an enemy before finishing them with a sword all without needing to change out of full plate armor or taking of your shield, or anything. 0 sacrifices must be made in order to take full advantage of the bow and arrow so they had to take away the accuracy to balance it. Meaning if you've a cross hair built into your monitor or a mod that adds one, you now have a weapon that can be used in as tight a space as a church attic or as far away as to make the ai unable to respond to your arrow shots while still being fully protected with armor, shields, and swords that it makes most weapons secondary to using the bow. This is without mentioning horse archery which is so absurd that you could invade all of Russia, China, and Rome without breaking a sweat which is realistic but damn it makes the game trivial

The bad
There are lots of bug, many funny, people spawning in, disappearing, walking through walls, doors ECT ECT but, the problem comes with game breaking bugs, I've had 2 that completely made the game unable to advance and I had to download a cheat console just so I could fix the game. The first was Hans not showing up after the hunting trip. I waited 3 days in game and he did not appear, come to find out he was stuck somewhere in the woods so I had to either find him and shove/punch him or get a cheat console and teleport him to me to complete the quest. The second was the mission stealing wine from the cellar for sir Hans, I did this mission about 5 times while another friend of mine watched and couldn't complete it, I had to go back and restart from a prior mission to get it to let me proceed this was really annoying\t\t\t\t\t
