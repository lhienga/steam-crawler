\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game that tries to be realistic, but ends up being a pain in the ass. Is set in medieval Bohemia, where you play as Henry, a blacksmith's son who has the charisma of a wet sock. The game boasts a complex combat system, a detailed open world, and a branching story with multiple choices and consequences.

The game also suffers from many technical issues, such as bugs, glitches, crashes, and long loading times. Also very demanding on the hardware, requiring a NASA computer to run smoothly at maxed out settings. It's realism also comes at a cost of fun, as the game forces you to deal with tedious mechanics like hunger, thirst, sleep, hygiene, and inventory management, and has a steep learning curve, making it hard for newcomers to get into.

KCD is not a bad game, in fact, it's actually very good, but it's not for everyone. It is a game that requires patience, dedication, and tolerance for its flaws. If you are looking for a realistic and immersive medieval RPG, you might enjoy it. But if you are looking for a casual and fun game, you might want to look elsewhere. Or better yet, play Skyrim.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is pretty fun and prides itself on somewhat realistic gameplay. The game is pretty fun, but suffers a bit from execution

- I think the game's combat 1v1 is very entertaining.
- I think the game's leveling system is very cool. It isn't just numbers that go up. when you level up reading, the words start appearing easier to read, when you level up defense you won't stumble as hard when hit.
- the main quest line is fun.

-Combat completely breaks when there is multiple opponents. I understand the "realism", but switching opponents feels not smooth.if you break the target, you automatically just get tackled and your screen shakes and now you're getting your ass beat by low level peasants. The tackle is absurd. Enemies will straight up dodge and block behind their back sometimes when you have allies. the enemies you fight in the multiple ally/enemy scenarios are weak and all timing and dueling prowess you get is thrown out of the window. You just swing randomly at the end and that is generally more effective.
-Sword combos are cool but largely poorly designed. Hard to execute them because the enemy just counters or dodges. 3 strike combos are OK, 4 are impossible versus good opponents.
- No companion system outside Mutt in the DLC. Realistically, you would have allies or help for these very important missions.
- prides itself on realism but shooting arrows at the archery range doesn't improve XP?
- Side quests are kinda crap and not really note worthy. They aren't entertaining mostly and some are kinda buggy. DLC's are largely uninspired.
-some quests are timed and it doesnt tell you. I failed a few quests that way.
-Theres a lot of tedium. Like an animation for picking up herbs (starts off 1 at a time), lots of travel back and forth, and the worst offender is slowly walking with NPCs or dragging their bodies. Sometimes these slow walks can talk 10 minutes. Also, if you want to fast travel quicker... you need to get on your horse which you can teleport to you. so X to whistle, jump on, M and then fast travel or its slow. Why not always default to a horse if you stolen one or own one?
- There are bushes that aren't really easy to pass, making it insanely annoying
- Money system gets out of control quickly and there's nothing to spend it on.
- Leveling system should go higher, it stopped about 3/4 of the way and I wish I could keep improving.
-Stealth system feels jank, sometimes I feel like I shouldn't be caught and other times I feel like Im clanking around in 100 noise armor and stealth killing everybody.
- By the end the game is starting to drag.


I have a lot of annoyances, but I didn't hate my time playing the game. It is still a fun game and I can't wait for the next installment of the series.

6/10\t\t\t\t\t
