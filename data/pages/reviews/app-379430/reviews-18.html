\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis says I have 51 hours of play right now. Honestly it's probably about 30 since it counts Pause time (if you have kids, you know how easily this can happen).

I was looking for something like Ghost of Tsushima. Obviously the setting is different. The combat is not as fluid. Really, the game is very different but ... it still scratches the itch so to say. Open-world, bows, sword, horse... the story seems alright so far. I understand it's a very "Christian" time but it does get a little annoying that literally everyone starts their greeting with "Jesus Praise" or alike. It's not the religious tone but rather the repetition. I wouldn't care if they were saying "Hello Friend"... If it was every person, it would get old. Really though, I don't care much. That is literally about my biggest gripe.

Once you get to where you can train with Bernard, TRAIN! OR YOU WILL BE COMPLAINING! The same with archery, TRAIN! Combat in this game takes practice. It's definitely no Dark Souls but if you don't practice and get your skills up, you're going to die pretty fast. With no levels the minimal bandits were killing me pretty quick. At higher single digit levels, it takes me 1 or 2 blows and I don't have the most fancy weapon.

The weapon choices are fun. Maces, Axes, Short/Long-swords. There's an option to train with a Halberd but I haven't personally picked one up yet. I really love the bow especially since it's fairly realistic as far as aiming (IN A VIDEO GAME!). Some people will not like that there's no cross-hair while aiming. There is a cheat to enable it but it's much more satisfying when you learn to aim correctly. If you're half decent with a bow in real life or can even hit a target more than once, you know you can base your shot off your knuckles or arrow tip. Same in the game. It is a little funky on the horse though ... something just seems off. I can't hit jack but I don't try much either.

The quests are alright. Probably good. Truth is most of my time has been exploring. The introduction to the game definitely reminded me of Ghost of Tsushima to an extent. I won't say anything beyond that. As for any other comparisons, there really aren't any. While I'm satisfied by far, it is again a completely different game with just some similar aspects.

I've done some side quests. Some are okay. Mostly what I've done seem more like training/tutorial though so they'll likely get better.

The main quests are decent enough. I can't say I'm as ecstatic as some other players but they're good enough to keep me playing for sure.

Probably about 30 hours of TRUE play time and I just finished the hunt with Hans. It was interesting. I hated him before the quest. When it started I thought I may like him. Then during I kept going back and forth. That's something that stands out to me in games - when they're able to make you "feel" (anything) towards a character, especially conflicting feelings. They've done that well in this game.

I want to add a note on Nudity because I have kids and had a hard time finding answers and they love watching me game. The first sex scene I didn't even realize happened because I skipped it due to the prior portion being so boring I just hit Skip All (or whatever). So, the scenes can be skipped. So can the quests themselves. Another scene did show missionary with breasts but nothing too graphic. Not even the typical over-the-top moaning many shows/games/etc have. If I went with typical USA rating, I'd say "PG-13" but, that's for the ONE I've seen. The game overall is obviously MA.

Swearing .. there's a lot. There's a review online about the "c" word being used a lot. Like that's worse than any others...? I've only heard it once, training with Bernard after successfully getting in a lot of good blows. Do expect a lot of other swearing though. If you're a responsible parent, your child should be able to handle hearing "bad words" and know not to repeat them.

The actual violence isn't too crazy. You'd be surprised how hard it can be to draw blood at times. The worse scenes are ones you do not cause yourself (for me so far anyway). Like the earlier part of the game, hanging corpses, attempted sexual assault. It is a violent game - at times.

On the flip side, it is also A VERY RELAXING GAME. Most of my time has not been fighting. It's been exploring, gathering herbs, seeing what-is-what, etc. I build up enough groschen (the money in the game that apparently used to be used, typically made from silver but sometimes other metals) to buy a decent but not top-notch horse and have very much enjoyed exploring. It wasn't really until yesterday that I bothered leveling up my combat much and started crushing some bandits.

I can see myself putting a lot of time into this. I already plan on playing Hardcore.

The only real pain is the carry weight... because most things valuable are quite heavy! Some people may say otherwise but, I strongly suggest getting a horse early. You get a free one during the main quest that isn't that great but purchasing a better one will allow you to carry a lot more loot and slowly build up a little fortune.

Playstyle ... you could act as a brute, stealthy, archer ... anything mostly realistic. No magic of course as it's attempting to be mostly historically accurate and we can't fling fireballs from our hands but no complaints there.

You do have to keep up on your gear. I strongly suggest repairing it before it's "health" (as I call it because it has a little heart symbol) gets below 80). If it isn't too damaged you can repair it yourself with kits which is more expensive but can lead to unlocking perks which only apply to stuff you repair on your own. Also, the cost of repair can be drastically higher if items are too damaged.

Speaking of perks - there's quite a few. This goes back to the play styles I mentioned. You'll be able - to an extent - make your character how you'd like. There's not A LOT in each category but some are pretty unique and can strongly affect your "build".

I strongly suggest not looking for "meta" builds. I haven't bothered. I'm still single-digit combat. Some people on Reddit say to level everything to at least 8. I haven't even done that and the enemies so far can barely hurt me after practicing combat with Bernard. If you want any challenge, don't be in too much of a hurry to get too strong.

I could keep going but that should give you a good idea.

It's worth the money. I don't see myself beating it in under 100 hours, the first time. Also on Reddit, it appears someone has got VorpX (if I have that right) to work with this game so you can play it "in VR". Not true VR mind you but, at least with a similar view. You will however not see your hands and will likely still want to use a controller or K+M. I'll try this on my next go-around.\t\t\t\t\t

Medieval life simulator by all means

From being an apprentice of a blacksmith, with an oath of revenge, you rise all your way up. You get beaten, stolen and mistaken which are all reflections of a human life. That's what makes this game realistic.

The main plot is meh, but still, there are quite amount of side quests that I haven't been able to finish in my 100 hours playthrough. As I kept playing, those side quests became not that interesting to me anymore and I just decided to rush down the main story.

I've seen plenty of negative reviews about the combat, and I don't agree with the way it's being criticized. IRL, it would be insane to think a human-being being able to fight two opponents (which are skilled enough) at the same time. Since this game can be called a simulation, which is the word I've started with to my review, I don't think it's a major disadvantage. You can't always be the John Wick of a universe, especially in Medieval Age.

Overall, it's a great game with great atmosphere and mechanics, and there are lots of stuff to discover which is all you could want from an RPG game.\t\t\t\t\t
