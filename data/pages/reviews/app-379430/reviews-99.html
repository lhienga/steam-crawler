\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not for everyone. But if you approach it with an open mind and some determination, you are bound to fall in love with this game. Henry, the character you play as, is a blacksmith's boy. He doesn't really have any skills when you start out. It's up to you to decide what he should focus on to improve. You have to keep working at it to ensure that Henry learns and improves, and I frankly LOVE that aspect of this game. I love the power fantasies of Skyrim and other games, but being a little peasant boy who works up the ranks and becomes a hardened knight is so ungodly satisfying.

If you are a medieval history buff like me, you'll find that Kingdom Come Deliverance scratches that itch for an enjoyable singleplayer medieval experience. And hell, you'll learn stuff about real world history, too! KCD is rooted in the very real events of 1400's Bohemia and the work Warhorse has done to bring it to life really shows.

This game does have its issues. It can be buggy. It's not the best optimized game out there. Some mechanics can be frustrating. But these are all issues that can be dealt with, and in my experience, rarely ever take away from the game. I'd say it's similar to Oblivion in its charming janky-ness. The DLCs can be a bit underwhelming, but I am begging you, PLEASE play A Woman's Lot. That ♥♥♥♥ hurted. That DLC is heartwrenching and just so well done.

Over all, I'd say give this game a shot. Don't let the high barrier of entry at the beginning discourage you. Take a moment to watch a few videos about this game if you're on the fence. But I assure you, if you give this game a chance, it will be well worth it. I've played this game a total of three times between PC and Xbox, and it has to be one of my favorite gaming experiences in recent memory.

Oh, and lastly, the soundtrack slaps. That is all.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-- GAMEPLAY --
KC:D is a true, hardcore medieval reality simulator. This reflects on the gameplay, especially in combat. And it makes it really hard and time consuming. Just to be able to, not die instantly you have to train for multiple in game weeks, with a trainer, to learn to fight with both swords. You need to raise both your and your character's skill level this way. But unlike in other games, the level of realism makes it fun and challangeing. You have access to a wide range of weapons, each with their stregnhts and weaknesses. My biggest beef here though, that we can't use halbeards and spears, even though they would be perfect weapons for this game. And the vision limit.
Since GOT, we know, that you should wear a helmet, right? The problem is, in rela life it limits your vision. And in this game. I mean, i understand they added it for realism, but it takes away a big part of the enjoyment of the game, actually seeing the combat.
And no, stealth archer is not as viable here as in other games. Archery is hard as f#&@ and you have no crosshairs. And stealth, well, it is as realistic as possible here too.
But, i mean, medieval life is suffering, so the game should be like that too.
Jesus Christ be praised!
10/8

-- GRAPHICS --
For a 2018 game, it looks really great. Even in 2023 it looks nice.
10/7

-- SOUNDS --
Voice acting and sounds are generally good, but i speak hungarian. So i understand what the cumans are saying. And that part, is really bad. They shout cringeworthy things and they choose terrible voice actors for any cuman role. For non hungraian speakers, this will not be a problem, but for us who do, it will ruin the immersion.
10/7

-- STORY --
The story is pretty great. I don't know how historically accurate some of the events, but there are a lot that it is.
10/6

-- CHARACTERS --
We have some great multi dimensional characters here, whom will make you more immersed in the game.
10/7

-- WORLD && WORLDBUILDING --
This part is amazing. Everything in this world, as historically accurate as it could be. Just the amount of reshearch work they must have put in is just another level.
Jesus Christ be praised!
10/10

-- REPLAYABILITY --
There is not much. Mostly for achievments, or just to play the game on hardcore mode.
10/4

--TL;DR--
KC:D is an amazing game. It recreates medievel life as accurate as possible, from a video game's perspective, and let's the players lose themselves in it, with a great story and fun game mechanics. It takes some time to learn, but well worth the time.
Jesus Christ be praised!
10/8\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Medieval Masterpiece.

As an avid fan of simulation games like Arma and DCS, and with a deep interest in military and medieval history, Kingdom Come Deliverance has been a remarkable journey for me. It's not just a game; it's a portal to another universe, one that meticulously reconstructs the medieval era with an immersive authenticity.

The combat system is a standout feature. With some basic understanding of historical swordsmanship, I was thrilled to see how the game attempts to replicate these techniques. It's not just about swinging a sword; it's about the art and skill of medieval combat, making every encounter both challenging and rewarding.

The narrative is another aspect where Kingdom Come truly shines. The storyline is gripping, weaving the personal journey of our hero with the intricate politics of nobility in a way that is both engaging and enlightening. It captures the essence of the medieval period, not just in grand battles but in the nuanced struggles of daily life.

One element I appreciated particularly is the game's portrayal of the difficulties of medieval life, integrated into its survival mechanics. However, I feel there's room for a more intense survival experience, pushing the boundaries of what it's like to live in such a harsh and unforgiving era.

A feature I missed in the game was jousting. Including this could have further enriched the depiction of knighthood and the era's martial culture. Looking forward to Kingdom Come 2, I'm hopeful for a bigger map,

Kingdom Come Deliverance is a masterclass in historical simulation and storytelling, a game that not only entertains but educates and immerses its players in the depth and complexity of medieval life.\t\t\t\t\t
