over all this game is amazing through and through the game play makes you feel like a proper knight the swords once you level your strength and agility up feel like swords not hunks of metal you have to approach fights with a head on your shoulders like i just love it i love the adrenaline rush i get when i desperately try to stop four bandits from surrounding me the story is just amazing every character is well written and serves a important purpose in the story

Henry is very well written and has very well thought out dialog that always feels in place for the type of character he is and his over all arch he literally goes from the son of a black smith to basically a royal spy/night and they don't sleep on the fact that while talented he has no skills for this type of work which really sends home the feeling of growing from a black smiths son to a knight I mean it when I say you yourself will feel like you are learning to fight like a night you will need to train you will do torments the same as knights did

and well the graphics arent world class they look very good they fit the game very well and really help to build out the world into either a vibrant beautiful landscape or a dark battlefield the towns and castles while not totally accurate to how they where and or how they are in real life get very close and really help to drive home the time period and type of world you are living in i will say the church in sassue is extremely good and a very accurate depiction

any ways this game is amazing and you can tell the devs poured their hearts and souls into it please dont sleep on this game and don't let the difficulty and slow pacing turn you away i promise you it will be worth it


also i love the armor in this game one of the few games that has historically accurate armor and weapons im not entirely sure about time period correctness but the armor looks immaculate

read some of the reviews and imma add this i see a lot of reviews with less then ten hours of play time talking about combat especially with multiple enemies first off you need to level up i have a few hundred hours on console and 40 on pc you need to be training with bernard for like half hour every play session until you get your preferred attributes to level 13 i highly recommend always using the perk that gives chance to insta kill with a hit to the head this

now for fighting multiple enemies at once there are three things i do that almost always keep me alive
#1 maintaining spacing and how many enemies im letting close enough to hit me at once if i need to sprint back a little i will i generally only like to let two guys in close at one time
#2 your choice in weapon and the skills you have leveled up matter if you do not have the strength for a higher level mace than you will swing it slowly and it wont do very much damage you will not have any set combos making it even slower combos come off faster than standard chained attacks
3# this is by far the the most important one never ever let the enemy get behind you or surround you that is a death sentence and no i dont complain about it if you got jumped by 5 dudes with spears and axes your gonna have a hard time fending that off

Bonus tip if you are struggling with combat use a shield and a one handed weapon preferable NOT a sword axes are OP in this game just like in real life the mace is okay it gives a pretty nasty bonk if you are leveled for it

bonus bonus tip train with the weapon you want to use and train with it a lot if you feel like the weapon you are using is slow clunky or not damaging you likely dont have enough levels to actually be using it

bonus bonus bonus tip you are better off using a lower level weapon than you are trying to use a weapon even one level over you

the lower level weapon will get a buff do to you being over leveled for it but the higher level weapon will be significantly debuffed to the point where using it is more damaging to the weapon its than it is to what you are hitting

even more bonusing of a bonus tip is to actually play into the strength of your weapons

and don't sleep on archery its a pain in the ass but it definitely pays off to level it up

yes the combat system is a little clunky when your low level you know what else is clunky a guy whos never worn armor in his life let alone swung a sword becoming a knight please learn to appreciate the game for what it is if you came here expecting historically accurate skyrim then this game probably isnt gonna be for you but if you want to feel like your living in the 1400s and becoming a knight then this game is for you\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy original review was made in bad haste and was not in good faith due to my headspace at the time. In a more stable mind at the time now, the combat in this game is fun when you fight someone! The combat is really responsive when you get into a rhythym. The enemies feel a little survivable for the whole realism thing on occasion, like firing an arrow into someone's chest then their stomach, then they continue to eat 6 more sword slashes and stabs. This isn't really an issue though, it's fun to fight that person for the time you do! The issue comes in when fighting multiple enemies nearby.
The lockon combat system is servicable when fighting one person, however, when fighting multiple enemies it is difficult to accurate switch to different enemies on pc. It feels very clunky, and the best way i've had so far is holding shift so you enter run, disengaging you from combat until you release it or get hit. Along with this, enemies will instantly gang up on you and encircle you, stunlocking you with stabs and slashes and occasionally clinching you. The only other issue i have is that clinching is not very well explained. Unless i did miss something, the game doesn't explain very well that you must first hold block in order to then initiate a clinch. This is another very small issue, and may even be a non-issue, as i may have simply overlooked something.
This game is incredible in its sound design, both the music and the sound of combat. The horseriding feels alright, it was nothing too special. My only other issue is needing to have a light at night or pay a fine.
Overall, a very good game, with a janky combat system when fighting in groups. Very worth it.
8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - 4/10, the game has potential but it sacrifices fun in the name of realism, which is then undermined by bugs and poorly implemented mechanics resulting in an unexciting if not frustrating experience.

Kingdom Come: Deliverance is an ambitious open-world RPG set in mediaeval Bohemia where you play as an inexperienced commoner learning the ways of knighthood on a quest to avenge his dead family. The game strives for realism but ultimately fails to implement fundamental mechanics and provide a fun experience.

I’ve tried getting into KC:D twice now and after several hours of playtime both times I’ve come to the same conclusion. The game has potential but at its best it’s serviceable and at its worst it’s clunky and frustrating to deal with.

The most common complaint amongst reviews, which I also share, is the combat. Fighting people in this game is simply not fun. Forced lock-on and ridiculous stun-lock animations make you feel like you have no control over your character. After playing a lot of Mordhau which executes a similar style of melee combat near perfectly engaging with this game’s janky system killed most of the enjoyment for me. I don’t mind difficult battles but when I die I want it to feel like it’s my fault, not because the game is preventing me from turning around to face an immediate threat.

I see the open-world getting a lot of praise but personally I found it rather bland. A large map consisting of mostly foliage, some copy-pasted mediaeval style villages and a few castles sprinkled in. I can appreciate the historical accuracy but I didn’t come across any breath-taking views that would grant the virtual sightseeing aspect significant value.

The story and missions were just about tolerable but not very exciting. Mostly travel to point A, listen to some not-too-great dialogue, travel to point B, pick something up and maybe fight a bandit, rinse and repeat for a few hours. I also have a personal complaint which I don’t know if anyone else would agree with; I found the protagonist's appearance, manner of speech and voice quite annoying and remarkably unattractive. I was kinda upset that everyone else looks and sounds normal but I have to play as the one character that resembles a special-ed neanderthal. I know this sounds silly but it’s genuinely how I felt, to the point where I avoided interactions with NPCs so I didn’t have to look at or listen to him.

KC:D is also littered with minor gameplay as well as audio-visual bugs. Nothing game breaking in my experience but enough to consistently break the immersion that is one of this game’s key selling points. Besides that there are a lot of smaller mechanics to play with (trading, lockpicking, gambling, etc.) most of which are fine but do not provide enough entertainment to mask the glaring issues described above.

Overall, Kingdom Come: Deliverance isn’t a terrible game but its highlights are overshadowed by its flaws and in general is too dull of an experience for me to honestly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
