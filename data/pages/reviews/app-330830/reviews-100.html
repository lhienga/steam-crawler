\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was my first game by Telltale, but not my first in the Borderlands series. From what I can gather, this game style is very in line with what Telltale makes, and is apparently even very good when compared to the rest of their games, but approaching this from the perspective of the Borderlands universe and not as a Telltale game, it was incredibly underwhelming to the point of being totally boring. I made it through the entire first episode and a good chunk into the 2nd, but the game is all story with no substance. The visuals are good and very in line with the style of Borderlands, the characters are good, and the stories are well built. However, as a franchise Borderlands has always had really polished FPS mechanics, weapons, and combat, with a huge emphases on multiplayer at its core, and this misses all of those marks. There's nothing to do other than listen to what the characters have to say and then click on one of the very limited options presented to you. There's no consequence to messing up, whenever that's even possible (which from what I can tell is rare), and it just kind of plays itself. I was personally sad at how well built of a game it was to be cornered into such limiting interactions.
For people who like "point and click" adventures and other Telltale games, sure, this is probably great. But from someone who played this looking for something in line with the rest of the Borderlands franchise, I couldn't advise against it more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, shame to see it go off shelves.

My personal thoughts:

To start this off let me clarify I'm a huge borderlands lover. I love and will always love anything borderlands, so give me that blood stained flavored Tiny Tina endorsed crumpet because let me tell you this game is great.

It has a great story and it doesn't really break the cannon of the borderlands much more than they seem to break it themselves, so all around pretty damn good.

Before I really get into it, I want to pressure you now. If you have this game in your library like I did and you love Borderlands but you don't like story games all that much, I /urge/ you to play this. Everyone I have ever met that has loved borderlands has loved this game.

Now back to the actual review:

Rhys is a good character and a good look into the life of the corporate no goods that are Hyperion, and you have to feel sorry that all his friends want to kill him.

Fiona is great, she's the protagonist everyone pretty much expected from a TellTale game. She's heroic, in her own right, and she's a con artist. (Its almost like that's the worst they could come up with).

Now for as much as I love this game I want to point out that it has a lot of flaws.

First of all the game didn't mesh well with what it was based off, for those of you that don't know, borderlands is a fps with rpg elements, so it didn't really mesh the best with TellTale's game design of "pick option, hear dialogue" this really didn't make the borderlands fans that excited. When you're used to something most people don't really like to stray, and so it got a lot of backlash.

On the same note the game really suffered from its "your choices (don't) matter" gameplay. I personally think it would have been interesting to see multiple endings, to see where my choices really lead me. I want to see if I let Handsome Jack (yes he's in this one too) tell me what to do what that would change. Turns out it's nothing. It changes absolutely nothing.

This really shows complications especially in Episode 5 where your choices should really come into play.

If you follow Jack all the way through and borderline befriend him you still get this awkward moment of "what the f*ckery" where it seems like the story just grabs you by the balls and makes you play the game/story how they wanted you to play. It's not ideal especially for a company who said "Your Choices Matter".

The game is also pretty glitchy for how TellTale made it run off the same old engine all its games were on, but despite that the graphics and game holds up. For the most part.

In the end this is obviously a cash grab, but it's a cash grab that made it into the cannon lore of the borderlands. It's also not that bad. Beyond small glitches and huge story fumbles it still managed to be a decent game. I would recommend you buy it, if it wasn't already gone.

Edit: It's no longer off shelves so I say you go and buy it!\t\t\t\t\t
