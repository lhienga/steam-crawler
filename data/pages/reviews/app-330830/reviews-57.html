\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7/10

Tales from the Borderlands was my only experience with one of Telltale Games' unique type of games, and it was a very fun and enjoyable experience. As a fan of the previous games (BL1, 2, and TPS) Tales really was a fun way to expand upon some of the characters and story featured in the previous games. The game focuses on Rhys and Fiona teaming up to find and open a vault, with plenty of appearances from characters both large and small from the core games. Decisions made throughout the game come back to have different consequences later on throughout the story. Players are also presented with some data at the end of each chapter regarding the decisions they made and how they compare to the rest of the playerbase. While Tales is a fun game in general, I really recommend playing it with another person, if you can. That's how my wife and I played on each of our computers, and we'd decide who was going to take which action when we made a decision and then split our playthroughs, seeing how this impacted the game (and the subsequent shenanigans that would ensue). Tales is also very smartly portioned into small chunks, making it easy to play for a bit without starting to be distracted by the slower pace of play compared to most other games. If you're a fan of the Borderlands franchise, you'll definitely enjoy spending a couple hours with Tales, despite being a very different type of game, just for a really fun time. It's unfortunate that it's not available on Steam anymore, but if you can get your hands on it somehow, it's worthwhile to do so.
