I loved the story from Borderlands.

Another great story by TellTale. This time is being told from the perspective of two characters Rhys and Fiona. Its a great idea as they are telling the story from their own perspective. In the game you will meet some of the vault hunters and you will meet Handsome Jack. Game has excellent voice acting, excellent humor, and constant backstabbing. Its hard not to fall in love with characters and NPCs in this game.

Pros
+ Great story with fresh take on Borderlands
+ Loved interracting with Rhys, Fiona, Sasha, Huges, and Vasquez.
+ Game is beautiful with cartoonish look just like regular Borderlands
+ Voice acting as always was superb

Cons
- No ClapTrap

I played few Borderlands games and all I remember them for is looting and shooting. However, this game puts a fresh take for me on Borderlands and as always offers compelling story that fits with lore and universe of previous games and expands upon it. Its a must own to anyone who likes Borderlands or TellTale games.\t\t\t\t\t

This will probably $uck

Second Take
This actually doesn't $uck


Thoughts Going In
Borderlands story has typically not been one of the strong points of this franchise, even though this is a Telltale game and Gearbox didn't write the story I was still a bit wary. So I approached it cautiously, did some research, read a few reviews, and eventually decided I would take a chance on it. I waited for that sweet sweet steam sale we all know and love and got it nice and cheap. It sat in my library for a long time until I worked up the nerve to try it out.

I gotta say I was very impressed with the presentation. Felt just like a movie. After watching the kicka$$ intro to the first episode I was hooked pretty hard, had to know what happens next.

Plot
The story takes place after BL2. You play as 2 different characters. First there's the low-level business executive named Rhys trying to climb the corporate ladder at Hyperion and get that juicy personal parking space. The other is a con-artist named Fiona whose always looking for the next score and generally mistrusts everyone. You team up with a group of rag-tag misfits and find yourselves fighting to survive and learning to work together all while hunting down the greatest treasure in Pandoran history: The Vault of the Traveler.

Final Thoughts
Save for a few brief moments in the story where I found myself mentally outside of the narrative, I was totally locked in. Finished it all in 2 days. There are plenty of relatable and flawed characters, surprising plot twists and reversals throughout to keep you on your toes. For long-time fans of the series there's plenty of acknowledgements to past characters and lore that are sure to make you smile.

I would've like to see Krieg/Gaige make an appearance and maybe gotten a bit more Scooter/Jack/Moxxi out of the story but besides that it was solid.

You'll never be able to look at Loader bots the same way again.


Word to the Wise
You dont need to play BL1 and BL2 to appreciate the game, but it sure don't hurt.
Also I'm very glad I watched Cowboy Bebop in its entirety before I played this game ;)

Bottom Line
Time and money well-spent.
I got it on sale for $6.24usd. but would totally have been fine paying full price.\t\t\t\t\t

Note though that it's not a looter-shooter -- it's a Telltale game, and like any Telltale game, it's chapter-based, letting you make choices which influence the outcome or arc of a story.
