\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, I recommend it HIGHLY. Absolutely amazing! Here's the run down on what I felt was good and bad about it.

GOOD: Strong and funny dialogue. You feel like your choices matter, especially by the time you get to the end of the game. Moving and heartwarming portions of the game. You end up caring a LOT about the characters. They become meaningful to you. Also, it's a good bridge into Captain Lillith's DLC for BL2 as 2 of the characters in this are in that as well. It also explains why certain things are different in that DLC...because those things happen in this game (and not in the main BL's games.)

And great voice acting! Many of your favorites probably are working on this project: Troy Baker, Nolan North, Laura Bailey, Ashley Johnson...and others that are part of the original BL's roster.

Beautiful visuals (typical for BL's). Well done.

BAD: Some FPS drops, usually before changing character point of view or right before a part that includes quick choices that must be made. QTE's suck in every game they show up in, and that's not just for this game. Most of the QTE's aren't that difficult, but if you die (and I did twice in the game), you have to start back at wherever the last save point was. You can't choose your save points. It's only at checkpoints that the game has programmed into it. That can necessitate you re-watching parts of the narrative that you'd prefer to jump past.

BEST: I won't ruin anything, but there's a character that shows up (under specific circumstances you have to fulfill, but not too hard to do actually)...and that character was a nice surprise and HILARIOUS as @#$!

In essence, this game had everything I wanted from it. I felt choked up at a couple of points. I laughed like crazy at others. I was amazed by it and entertained. I feel most people who are familiar with the BL's story line will enjoy what this adds. I am not sure if a new player who has never played any of the other games...would get much from this. I would hope so, but they'd likely miss out on some of the jokes and subtle moments.

Regardless, it's good and I highly recommend it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFunny, bizzare, crazy, peculiar and eccentric spirit of the previous titles is well carried to this episodic story driven game. I'm hugely drawn to the mega-corporations of Borderlands universe and it shows you a little bit of inner working of the Hyperion due to main character being a corporate man himself. Also it shows that the Helios is very populated place and there are simple company workers that are residing there unlike previous games, where Helios was just an empty place, turns out the loaders have a more advanced AI so they can hold up a conversation and of course Handsome Jack the fate of whom we will discover after what's happened.
Overall, the story is quite good, but the only thing I personally was interested in was everything related to Hyperion and this game is packed with it.
7.8/10\t\t\t\t\t
