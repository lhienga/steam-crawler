\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComing from the disappointment that is Borderlands 3's story, this game reminded me of my favorite parts in Borderlands 2- I liked the writing, I liked the characters, I liked the humor, and there weren't any cringy characters I couldn't stand just taking up screen time.

Tales from the Borderlands fleshes out a few characters we've met in previous games, just piling on the reasons we should love them, and introduced several new characters that I ended up liking quite a bit as well. I'd be lying if I said that I wouldn't die for Loader Bot; that guy's a cutie.

You'll hear some criticism about Jack coming back, and complaints that it's "ass-pully" after killing him in 2, but I think it was done really well and easily fits the in-universe explanation. It made sense and it enabled some pretty cool storytelling, so I think it's more than justified.

It's a little tragic that the game created Rhys and Vaughn, two pretty cool dudes with plenty to each of their characters, just to have Blands 3 reduce them to caricatures that have none of the nuance displayed in Tales. The dialogue between all of the characters was lively and humorous, and I enjoyed both playthroughs I completed simply due to the other conversation trees I was able to explore.

One of my biggest gripes with the Borderlands series as a whole is that the cutscenes are usually pretty stiff and robotic when it comes to the humanoid characters; even in 3, none of the scenes that have any sort of fighting or dynamic action look good, but Tales manages to make these really cool looking fights and action sequences at every turn, so this game was also a lot of fun to kick back and watch.

Ultimately, it was really cool to see a game series that I love adapted into an entirely unrelated genre that had absolutely no reason to be as good as it was. It was a pleasant surprise, and has me cautiously optimistic about its upcoming sequel!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreetings as I hope all is well! Below is my Steam Review for Tales from the Borderlands, thanks for reading as I wish you well in all your gaming adventures...

STORY/LORE - 5/5

The story of Tales from the Borderlands is absolutely incredible. Now, keep in mind, if you’ve never played a Borderlands game, it’s best to at least play the first two in the franchise so you’d understand what is going on and appreciate this even more. Handsome Jack is all over this game so if you have no idea who he is, play the first two and you’ll be good to go to play this before you jump into Borderlands 3.

The characters, events, situations, and hilarious moments make this story one to remember. I won’t ruin anything for you but you’ll enjoy all the little nods and Easter eggs to the series when jumping into this game. The story here is completely unique to this game and Telltale Games is legendary when it comes to immersing fans of a video game series in something you’ll remember for many years to come.

GAMEPLAY - 5/5

The gameplay is great in Tales from the Borderlands, even though it’s nothing like a traditional Borderlands game. You’ll have to be quick with your decisions in the heat of the moment as the controls are pretty simple (left, right, up, down, spamming Q, hitting E, etc.) and all your choices will definitely matter throughout the course of the game.

I especially loved the final boss scene as it was beyond epic when it came to the gameplay. Again, it’s pretty straightforward and delivers well from beginning to end. I enjoyed it since you do have a timer when it comes to your decisions which means the replayability is great if you’re looking for multiple playthroughs with entirely different outcomes and dialogue.

GRAPHICS - 5/5

As with any Borderlands game, the cartoonish graphics separate this from any other game as it looks great. No realistic graphics here but continues the usual aesthetic of a Borderlands game as Telltale Games really did their homework to keep everything as they are in the series.

SOUNDTRACK - 5/5

I absolutely loved the music in Tales from the Borderlands as there are certain events that happen with unique songs that just capture some great moments. This was brilliantly done and when you get to them, you’ll see exactly what I mean. No spoilers here but wow, all the songs used were epic and perfect.

MULTIPLAYER - N/A

Tales from the Borderlands is a single-player game with no multiplayer. To be honest, multiplayer wouldn’t make much sense here as it’s a story-driven game where your specific decisions and choices matter. It would be a mess trying to do that in a co-op situation but yeah, this game should take about 20-30 hours to complete on your own and is perfect to fly through over a long weekend.

FINAL SCORE - 5/5

Overall, I give Tales from the Borderlands a perfect 5/5. This is a fantastic video game that captures the essence of the Borderlands series after you’ve finished Borderlands 2 and are ready to jump into Borderlands 3. It’s a nice break from all the incredible FPS action and delivers a wonderful story that is filled with emotion, laughter, and just sheer awesomeness.

I know Handsome Jack has a bazillion fans all across the universe and well, if you love him, you’ll undoubtedly love everything he brings to Tales from the Borderlands. The gameplay, music, graphics, and story are all top-notch for what I would consider an incredible gaming experience. If you enjoy playing Borderlands, definitely play this one as you won’t regret it.\t\t\t\t\t
