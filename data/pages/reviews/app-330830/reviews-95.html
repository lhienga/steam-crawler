\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a big fan of the Borderlands franchise, I must say that, without a doubt, this is the best Borderlands game yet. Before you give me weird looks, yes, I know this is a spin-off, and I know that Telltale's games might not suit everybody, being more like interactive movies. Even then, I think it fits the Borderlands franchise fantastically, allowing for better storytelling.

The story is not what you'd find in the main Borderlands games, that is, the ones published by Gearbox itself. Rather than being about a group of Vault Hunters, you play as two different characters, switching between them at certain points of the story. Rhys is a company man working for Hyperion, wanting to become the next Handsome Jack, while Fiona is a con artist from Pandora trying to score it big with a scam. Well, the thing is, they end up running into each other due to some unfortunate events, and from there they form an alliance to search for a Vault. Sure, I said they were not Vault Hunters, but it wouldn't be a Borderlands game without a Vault, would it? It's a pretty good story, but I feel that I can't do it any more justice here, and it's something that you must experience yourself.

The writing is fantastic; while I haven't played many of Telltale's storytelling games (in fact, I only played the first season of The Walking Dead and Back to the Future), I think they did an excellent job here. Like I said, the story is pretty good, and there's tons, and I mean tons of funny moments throughout, which don't rely as much in crude humour as the main games do. This game had me laughing out loud a lot, which not many games have managed to do. However, there's also some serious moments that are very well executed, two scenes in particular almost made me cry.

The gameplay, like in all Telltale storytelling games, is sort of an interactive movie, with quicktime events here and there, as well as dialogue choices. The game says that the story adapts to your choices, but I'm not completely sure of how much your choices impact the overall story; at least I could see previous choices referenced in later episodes, making it feel consistent.

The soundtrack is fantastic. As the tradition in the Borderlands game goes, each episode has an intro song, and they're all pretty good, setting the tone for the episode itself. As for the art style, well, it's Borderlands, so of course it has that sort of cell-shaded but not really cell-shaded art style going for it, which is really good.

As I said, I'm a big Borderlands fan. I'll admit that I'm relatively new to the franchise, having only gotten into it around 2016 with the Pre-Sequel, but I did get the two first games and played through them after that. All that said, I still stand by my statement that this might be the best Borderlands game to this date, and I'm hoping Gearbox will let Telltale have a shot at a Season Two.\t\t\t\t\t
