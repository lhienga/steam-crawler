Looting And Laughing

Those two are what I play a Borderlands game for. And considering the latter: This definitely is one of the best comedy-driven adventure games out there. One would not expect the charme of Borderlands to work this well in the Telltale Games format, especially considering their games are usually on the more serious side. But they pulled it off. And the final product is nothing short of amazing.

The gameplay is like any other Telltale game: Examining your surroundings, making dialogue choices and the occasional QTE (Quick-Time-Event). And yes, "your choices matter" is only true to a certain degree.

Where this game stands out is definitely its writing (and the accompanied phenomenal voice acting).
It got A LOT of laughs out of me and I appreciated every Borderlands reference. You would probably still have quite a good time playing this even if you didn't pick up a Borderlands game before. (Please give those games a try though). The humor was almost always spot-on and despite that managed to give the story the space it needed to progress. At two particular moments I was a little grossed out (face pizza and spork). They fortunately didn't last long.

The story was short, but great either way. One could nitpick at a couple of instances, but it's Borderlands.. I can easily turn a blind eye to those. Many jokes about storytelling and character tropes are made by the characters; I loved those. I didn't expect certain twists in the story and for them to be that genius, too. Some segments were so funny in their execution that I couldn't stop laughing and others were rather depressing and sad, which I didn't expect at all.

Character-wise: They are charming and I grew fond of them. There was not as much character development as I would've liked, but it was pretty enjoyable to see these characters befriend each other and have hilarious dynamics at the same time.


If you're fine with the gameplay of Telltale games and searching for an unconventional story approach with a focus on humor, then please give this game a shot. It's worth every penny (and will hopefully stay on Steam this time)\t\t\t\t\t
