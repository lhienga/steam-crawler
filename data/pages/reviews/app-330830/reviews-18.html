\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game where you listen to Marcus tell you a story, in which a con-woman and hyperion worker tell someone else a story. Until most of the last episode, at least.

This game is a very, very well-done take on a more narrative-driven game set in the world of Borderlands. As was Telltale's fashion when they still existed, point-and-click adventure with QTEs and very little thinking beyond choosing your next line. Though you know, by now, it's not going to perfectly influence everything in the story and it's sad that Telltale games advertised themselves as fully choice-driven, however the choices you do make in this game do feel they have somewhat of a difference in the short-term and a selection of unique options for very specific choices I wouldn't have honestly seen coming. Even after apparently playing this game a lot when it came out, I still found new things on my most current replay.

As for Borderlands lore, it pretty much nails the time of the series and what BL2/TPS had set up already, builds upon it well enough to not have any glaring issues (how refreshing) and actually has a few moments that are monumental within the story as a whole, which is surprising for a spin-off game but I'm definitely glad they were allowed to do the things they did.

Without getting into spoiler territory, there are some big implications that could have been built upon in later games and a lot of setup potential. On its own, it is a very compelling story only dampened by the fact that later entries (as of writing) have either ignored or outright gone back on very important character/world moments set up within this game. Feels like they can't decide whether they wanted the game to be canon, having recurring characters and whatnot, but then outright changing them to the point where they're not actually themselves anymore. Or putting them into places which make no sense.
The biggest shame being that this game, as you may know, ends on a very important cliffhanger that nobody has answered yet and I can assure you that whatever idea you have in your head is probably better than whatever the new games can come up with at this point. I'd love to see them prove me wrong, though.

I am very glad, however, that the game is still up for sale despite Telltale's demise. The experience I remember many years ago was still fresh (aside from Telltale jankiness and very dated graphics even for its time) and was fun to watch/play through. £15.99/$20 is not a big ask for one of the best-written Borderlands pieces of content out there so far.\t\t\t\t\t
