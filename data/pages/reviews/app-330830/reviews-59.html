I was never accustomed to any Telltale game. However, when Tales from the Borderlands was released, I could not hide my excitement. As a big fan of the Borderlands franchise, I had to try this bad boy and see for myself.

üìñ Setting:
Summary
After the events that occurred in Borderlands 2, it has been discovered that there are numerous other Vaults scattered throughout the galaxy. This leads to a search for more Vault Keys that can open these new Vaults.
You the player control separately the choices and fates of 2 main protagonists Rhys and Fiona.

Rhys
Rhys is an ambitious Hyperion employee. With the assistance of his best friend Vaughn, he aims to get promoted into the higher ranks of the company. This dream however does not last long, as his old rival and now new boss Hugo Vasquez gets in their way.

Fiona
Fiona is a con-artist working on Pandora along with her younger sister Sasha. Under the wing of their mentor and father figure Felix, they try to pull off a dangerous scheme in hopes of a better future. Things do not go as planned though‚Ä¶

Story
Throughout the story you explore how the protagonists eventually came together, showing common events from 2 different perspectives. In addition to the original cast, the game also features beloved characters from the main Borderlands games. Who you may ask, that‚Äôs for you to find out!

Freedom of choice
The plot in general is an emotional roller-coaster with lots of funny as well as sad moments. You really ‚Äòfeel‚Äô the characters in a certain way, their emotions and motivations, making the decisions more impactful to even a personal degree. Your every choice has an impact and will backfire in the future. However, that does not mean you should not play the game according to your beliefs and opinions. There is no right or wrong option here, so feel free to explore.

Lore, lots of lore
Since the beginning of the Borderlands series, I‚Äôve been intrigued by its vast story. In this game, however, the developers heard my prayers and did indeed satisfy my lore hungry needs. To be more precise, the adventure either crates certain foundations for the upcoming game (Borderlands 3) or expands the lore of old characters and places alike. You get to learn more about Hyperion, Atlas, Dahl as well Jack and the planet Pandora in much more detail than before.

üïπÔ∏è Gameplay:
Gameplay-wise, Tales from the Borderlands is very simple:
  • You have 5 options ( if you include silence [‚Ä¶] ) for every conversation, as well as sometimes "reflex" moments that require your attention.

  • You can move the 2 playable characters, Rhys and Fiona, around the world's environment, interacting with different objects or people.

  • Simple puzzles which involve interactions are also included, a neat feature that makes the game more immersive.

  • Like in every Telltale game, you cannot forget about the loot system. You can collect cash scattered throughout the game's locations, which can be later used to purchase items useful for certain events.
There are also some tweaks here and there, but I won't spoil the fun parts.
