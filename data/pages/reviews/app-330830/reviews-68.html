\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTelltale may be gone, but its legacy remains, and what a legacy it is.

Tales from the Borderlands was a bit surprising when it was announced; a heavily story-driven game set in a universe that mostly centers around shooting insane shirtless bandits with mommy issues in the face. But yet, from the get-go, what you have is an honestly compelling narrative with with Fiora and Rhys, the main characters, two people that the game readily admits are grifters.

There's something about sharing a laugh with characters that makes you legitimately more inclined to care about them then just about any other thing a character could do; just look at Undertale. And the to my pleasant surprise, the narrative of the story weaves in surprisingly well with the overall goofy tone of the Borderlands universe, even fitting in a few cameos from other Borlanders characters here and there to make it feel more legit. It's a testament to the writers that the adventure is almost seamless, and while the sum of your choices usually still result in the same overall end, the differences from scene-to-scene make it feel like your choices matter, which is just as important.

There's something of a consensus that Tales from the Borderlands might even be the strongest Telltale game there is, and honesty, I'm not one to argue. Great characters, great choice in music, captivating story and pretty much a laugh-a-minute riot that goes into some pretty heartfelt places, it's the kind of game you'll binge over the course of a few days. Can't recommend enough.

Oh, and save Loader Bot.\t\t\t\t\t
