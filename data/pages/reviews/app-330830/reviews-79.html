\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThought I'd finally write a review for this game considering the Telltale games are going to be removed from listing in the following 2 days (hopefully 2K found a way to keep Tales on the market by then)

This game is something I think everyone should experience - whether you're a borderlands fan or not, you will enjoy it. I consider it to be what Ant Man & The Wasp was in the MCU, Something most people skipped but then were confused when they watched Endgame (In this case, Borderlands 3).

The point and click play style in addition to quick time events might turn you off, but trust me - it is worth it. You'll fall in love with the diverse cast of characters (Loaderbot in particular) and there might be scenes where you'll cry but at the end of the day, you'll leave the game with a smile - whether is be because you're happy to have seen the characters grow along a journey or because you'll know that this isnt the last time you'll see them again.

If you have never even heard or touched a borderlands game, trust me when I say you'll enjoy it. I played Tales without even having any prior knowledge to Borderlands, and yet it ultimately introduced me to the franchise and made it one of my favorite game series. There might some references you'll miss but don't worry, the games still enjoyable.

Overall, Tales from the Borderlands is personally what I believe to be one of the best Telltale games and it is certainly an experience anyone would enjoy. I think its a great shame that the game is soon going to be delisted.\t\t\t\t\t

An amazing game that adds quite a lot to the story of the Borderlands games.

Before anything else, it's worth noting that this is the only Telltale game I've played so far, so I don't know how well it compares to other Telltale games.

The best thing about TFTB is that they manage to create new content (considering all the new characters they've created) while also staying true to the content from the previous Borderlands games and it's done in such a way that it excels in both areas. The new characters are interesting, having their own personalities and defining features, while we also see the return of some well known characters from the previous games as well. And while the game tells a story of its own, it also shows some interesting pieces of information related to the other games that weren't seen before, mostly related to Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel.

My only two issues with the game are, first, that playing the game offline won't earn achievements and there's no way to obtain those other than replaying the entire chapter for the achievements that were missed (but that's a minor issue anyway). Second, that the game doesn't allow the player to replay sections of a chapter, so if you want to replay a specific part of a chapter, the only way of doing so is to replay the entire chapter. I could also mention that there are a few animations in the game that feel a little stiff - but only a few, as for the most part the game looks great.

100% recommended and now I can't wait to see the return of some of these characters in Borderlands 3 (and also an explanation for what happened at the end of Tales).\t\t\t\t\t
