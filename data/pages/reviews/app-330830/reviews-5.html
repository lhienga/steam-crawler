I never mentioned that you had to play the game in windowed mode - which isn't a huge issue, but the game also seems to run too fast on PC causing later QTEs and dialog option to move to quickly and force you to go mute or make decisions you didn't want to, ruining you're whole play-through... I'm going to have to change my review, as that's game breaking. I'd play this game on console and avoid the PC version. I want reiterate that it's a great story, it just happens that PC isn't quite the best way to experience it.

An honest masterpiece of a game. The best of the Tales games in my opinion (slight bias to the franchise), but all the games are great at representing the universes they take place in and this is no exception. Nearly everything about this game covers what you saw in the first two as far as charters and lore, with this game obviously introducing a whole new cast, which I greatly enjoyed and I thought each did their roll quite well honestly. Without getting into the whole structure of the Tales games, it's a very engaging story, is good to relax while you play and I am having a good time being able to relive the story again after so many years. Would even recommend to those who haven't played Borderlands, as it gives you a taste of what the franchise set out to be before BL3.\t\t\t\t\t
