\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWithout delving into spoilers, this is easily the best Telltale Game in terms of narrative. The style, humor, and action (even without any FPS elements) of the Episodes perfectly matches that of the Borderlands games -- and while that can definitely add or detract from the experience depending on how you feel about those titles, this series is able to stand all on its own while delivering a satisfying experience with a compelling plot and engaging characters you won't want to forget.

The performances by Troy Baker, Laura Bailey, and the rest of the talented cast is unrivaled are worth buying into this game alone, and the opening title card sequences for each episode is so catchy and fitting that you'll enjoy watching it even through subsequent replays of the game.

As far as the consequences of choices go there aren't any significant ones that impact the story here, and while that itself is a slight disappointment it isn't really anything unusual in the standard of Telltale games around this time -- and the story is seriously so fun that you most likely won't care anyway.

All in all, a remarkable game that stands up years later and is definitely deserving of a sequel in any capacity.\t\t\t\t\t

The story and characters draw you in and keep you entertained throughout, faithfully adhering to the Borderlands universe. Small segment of SJW-heavy side arc regarding a lesbian couple, aggravated by a nails-on-chalkboard Aussie-accented character. (They go on and on about their wedding, and on and on and on, and then on and on some more. Enough, I get it already) Anyway, not too bad that it can't be tolerated.
