\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTales From The Borderlands feels like a mixture between a point-and-click adventure and one of those old FMV games from the '90s. You won't be doing any "Use item with environment" puzzles, though there are some easy puzzles. Most of the game is spent watching the story unfold while every so while you pick a dialogue choice (which is timed, I guess to simulate real conversation), as well as Quick-Time Events, so keep your hand on WASD. Sometimes you'll need to aim your cursor at an object. Sometimes if you fail, you get a game over, and the cutscenes are unskippable. Other times, you have to live with your choices.

The storyline looks to be standalone at first, but it does have characters and plot points that tie into The Pre-Sequel, Borderlands 3, and the Borderlands 2 DLC "Commander Lilith and the Fight For Sanctuary". It's not essential for playing any of those games, but it does fill in the gaps, like what happened to Scooter. The game is split into 5 episodes, and I mean episodes like a TV show. It has a "previously on..." segment, a "Next time on..." segment, and even a title sequence, which felt odd to me at first. I'm on Episode 2, I should hope I know what game I'm playing.

The game isn't hard at all, just make your choices and expect a QTE to come out of nowhere. The achievements just require you to beat the game, so this is an easy Perfect Game.\t\t\t\t\t
