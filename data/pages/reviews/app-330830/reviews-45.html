\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first I found it hard for me to get into this game, as I'm so used to the classic Borderlands gameplay. It was odd for me to be thrown into this universe I knew so well while not actually shooting any of the bad guys. That made my time on this game really fractured, with a few sessions here and there for the first half of the game, then a big pause of several months, to finally end it in just two days lol. I guess I stopped just before it was getting good.

Anyway, having played BL3 before this, I guess I knew what was coming for Zero, Rhys and Vaughn, but these are really some of the best characters this franchise has spawned.
I also loved Fiona and Sasha, and now I'm a little sad they didn't make an appearance in Borderlands 3, maybe next time.
One big surprise was that Cassius was actually introduced in this game and not the final DLC for Borderlands 2, which is something I didn't knew.
The whole story ties neatly into the lore of the series and Gortys felt like a very smart way of introducing how human technology could interact with a Vault, on top of being a super funny character.
As always, the humour is on point, just the right jokes at the right time, my favorite being when Rhys got all dramatic while Sasha was letting herself fall into the jungle that was... barely two meters lower lmao.
Graphics were surprisingly nice given the sort of game this is, though it was quite inconsitent, sometimes the game looked great, sometimes the textures were kind of nasty. Also the animations really make it feel like the characters are some robots, their movements don't look natural in my opinion, though it did not put me off that much. So overall, pretty good visually.
Music was as usual had a strong impact and the licensed songs were just the perfect choices, my personal favorites being "Kiss the Sky by Shawn Lee's Ping Pong Orchestra" and "To the Top by Twin Shadow".

So in conclusion, a great game with a great story with great characters but will maybe unsettle the diehard Borderlands fans with the classic Telltale gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an adventure set in the Borderlands universe with quicktime events. Now is the time to scratch your head and say "huh?"

If you loved any of the Borderlands games and like the universe and over the top - well, everything - then you'll enjoy this.

Your in game dialogue and action decisions have some effect on what happens later in the game but mostly just in terms of your relationship to your companions and other characters. You can rewind chapters to make different decisions if you want. You CAN die but it will just reset you to a point where you can save yourself from doing that again.

Pros:
- the Borderlands universe
- the jokes are everywhere. The dialogue, the Jackopedia or whatever, the tooltips..look out for the jokes..they're awesome
- some of the characters - loved some
- Jack (HoloJack?) - have to give him an honorable mention here :)

Cons:
- Movement (walking and moving about is soooo slow) this made me google alternative endings to see if it's worth replaying - I probably won't do it
- some of the characters - yeah..just don't like some of them for whatever reason
- quicktime events - who even loves this?? It's not a dealbreaker for this game, but definitely won't play any other Telltale game

I can easily recommend the game to people who love the Borderlands universe (the story is playable to newbies too) and fans of the Telltale series. Adventure lovers, maybe.

I don't think I have ever seen the game under 15€ which looks a bit much compared to some other bargains that are available all the time. Got it on discount and it's still one of my most expensive purchases on Steam. No regrets, just saying. Enjoyed it thoroughly!\t\t\t\t\t
