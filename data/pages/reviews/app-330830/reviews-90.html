\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I haven't played that many Telltale games, but so far this has to be my favourite. I'm also saying this from the start, if you don't like Telltale style of gameplay this game won't change your mind. Now moving on...

Taking the Borderlands IP and making a spin-off adventure game with story focus could sound kinda weird at first, but oh boy did they make a good job. First of all, the tone of the game is similar to the main Borderlands games, although not as wacky in some ways, it isn't a very serious title in comparison to other Telltale series. You also get the chance to play as 2 characters, which gives more variety overall. Very welcomed feature.

Now, you may be thinking "What if I don't like Borderlands? Will I enjoy the game?" And the answer is mostly: Yes. Even though they share the universe, story elements and some characters, there's a different feel to the main series overall, specially with the humour and writing (you won't get all those dank memes and such), which I find both to be better overall, after all that's what Telltale excels at.

Still bear in mind, it's a Telltale game, so in my opinion, is more movie than game, which I'm fine personally, but TftB does have some really nice choices which can shape up the game in certain situations. You'll see what I mean when time comes.

When it comes to technicall aspects, not much to say. It's still the same outdated engine, which works fine I guess, some weird graphical glitches here and there but nothing major so you should be fine.

Overall this game is highly recommended if you are looking for a fun, not really groundbreaking story, which is great.
I give this game 9 out of 10 Buttstalions\t\t\t\t\t
