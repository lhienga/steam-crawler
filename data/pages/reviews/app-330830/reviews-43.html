That next contender is you, Rhys, and it is time that all your hard work, underhanded dealings, backstabbings, and alliances place you in the place you deserve, the CEO of Hyperion. It isnt until you arrive to get a speech about what youll need to do as the head of Hyperion, when things go totally wrong. This is where the real adventure begins. Your actions throughout this game will help shape the final outcome. You can control either Rhys or Fiona, a very clever, good looking con-artist who only has dollar signs in her eyes. The actual game will center around telling the story of how the craziness that you find yourself in all went down.


I freaking love TellTale Games. Im always blown away by the complexity of the story, and how my actions will truly change the overall narrative. There were always tons of twists and turns just around the corner. For the most part, whatever decision you make will be written in stone and the story will continue. There are a couple of parts where you may have to restart a section because you let a main character die, but theres not a whole lot of those sections. Tales from the Borderlands is broken up into 5 different chapters, each of them building off of the previous episode all the way up to the end. You can always restart a chapter, but to be honest that feels cheap to me. You have to ride this horse all the way to the end.

I have to hand it to the writers/directors of each of the chapters, I was locked in and engaged almost 100% of the time. The flow of the story was constant, many of the choices weighed heavy on me after I made them, and that made it all the more enticing to keep playing. I create relationships with all the characters and when a twist happens I feel the pain of betrayal, or the soothing relief of something going your way. Each of the characters that youll be interacting with have their own personality. I didnt feel like there were any cookie cutter characters outside of the random NPCs, but thats to be expected.

It does remind me that this game, Tales from the Borderlands, happens between the events of Borderlands 2 and Borderlands 3. Ironically I picked up Borderlands 3 after completing this game and immediately felt that I had a leg up on where some of the story was going to take me since this game definitely has some parts that happen after the actions Im taking, currently, in Borderlands 3. I think it is abundantly clear that I NEED good voice acting, and this game, in all of its chapters, freaking nailed it. The look of the characters combined with the voices, which conveyed a full range of emotions, made this game a must play for me. If you have ever played a TellTale Game I know I dont have to tell you how amazing this game is, but if you havent played any of the TellTale Games before, you are really missing out on the high quality, well put together stories that are crafted through their games\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTales from the Borderlands is an episodic Point & Click adventure game, following two unreliable narrators who begrudgingly team up together on a quest for power and riches. Its also one of my favourite entries in the series and a strong contender for being one of the best games Telltales ever made.

If youre a fan of Borderlands style of writing and dont hate the standard Telltale game mechanics youre almost guaranteed to have a blast with this game as it positively nails the humour and wit that the rest of the franchise is known for.

Introducing a new cast of characters, Tales from the Borderlands puts you in the shoes of its everyday denizens with dreams of grandeur and serves as a direct sequel to Borderlands 2 following the power vacuum that Handsome Jacks death left behind. It encapsulates a side of the world that the vault hunters never get to see and despite all its zaniness, it still manages to feel somewhat grounded in an insanely violent world. Well, as grounded as it can be with the two protagonists' frequent habit of embellishing the details of their journey.

The main characters are incredibly charming and the supporting cast is just as memorable. Some familiar faces from the Borderlands franchise return here and for fans of the series this adds further depth to these characters and acts as a great addition to their stories but newcomers wont feel lost either as they blend in seamlessly with the plot and the unique chemistry they bring to the table is well earned over the course of the game. The relationships you build with these characters feel truly special and that is thanks both to the excellent writing and stellar voice acting from the cast.

The visuals are great and consistent with the franchises style with some really interesting character designs for its main cast. From the flora in the wastelands to the corporate style of Hyperion, everythings a delight to look at.

The game plays like any other Telltale game and is welcoming to all kinds of players. The interpersonal relationships of the main duo are at the heart of the story and change how characters will interact with each other both in the present and past leading to some truly funny, tense and heartwarming scenes! In my opinion it has excellent replay value as the writing is so good I wanted to see every line of dialogue it had to offer.

If you want the full Borderlands experience, this is a game you have to play as it changes the world in such an interesting way.

10/10 Would look at mushrooms again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the Borderlands style of comedy, and I thought that Tales of Monkey Island was a very good game. I didn't really like Tales from the Borderlands though for the following reasons:

1. There is not a lot of game to play here. It's mostly a series of unskippable cutscenes with a QTE thrown in here and there. So you just watch, listen, pick dialog choices, and do QTEs. Most of the dialog choices don't really seem to affect the game's outcome, and it is nothing like the point-and-click formula in Tales of Monkey Island.

2. One of the few things that *do* affect this game's outcome are the QTEs, and failing one usually results in a game over which means it will kick you back to the last checkpoint which could be many, many minutes back in the story. (And remember that you can't skip or fast-forward the cutscenes so you're stuck watching everything that you just watched again in real-time.) The QTEs are also timed and tend to just pop up without warning which means that you will often find yourself leaping for the controller/keyboard and in hopes of pressing the right QTE button before the short timer expires so you don't have to watch the last 10 minutes of the game again.

3. The writing is terrible. It ranges from boring to intellectually insulting. None of the characters are likable (even by the generally "comedically unlikable" standard set for characters in the other Borderlands games), and the story is mostly meandering. It's like Telltale decided that they wanted 5 episodes lasting around 2 hours, but the story that they had written couldn't be stretched out that long so they just padded the game time with whatever they could scrape together in order to fill their 2 hour quota for each episode. One of my major gripes about the writing was the way that they killed off Scooter and Sasha. Killing off Scooter was an insultingly cheap and ineffective attempt to evoke emotion, and it just came across as incredibly dumb. Then they killed off Sasha in a similarly dumb manner to try to make the ending "emotional" but decided to resurrect her using a largely ignored and unexplained deus ex machina device because...reasons... The semi-cliffhanger ending with the vault was incredibly unsatisfying as well. And then much of the rest of the dialog was just this awkward brand of "humor" which didn't live up to the normal dark humor of Borderlands.

So in summary, the game isn't really a game as much as it is a mildly interactive movie, and the light narratives of the main-series Borderlands games are more enjoyable than this game. I'm not sure about how much lore from this game makes it into Borderlands 3, but if you're worried about it I would recommend just reading a summary of this game's story instead of wasting time actually playing this game.\t\t\t\t\t
