WHAT IS TALES FROM BORDERLANDS
A pretty good five part episodic series from the very well known developer: Telltale Games. It depicts the events on Borderlands 2' aftermath, on what has occured right after. You play as new characters but you also meet the known Vault Hunters from the previous Borderlands games. I can honestly say that this is one of my favorite Telltale game. Fantastic story, great voice acting and lots of humor. Interesting mysteries to keep you guess right till the end too. You play the two main characters, Rhys, an Hyperion employee trying to rise up the corporate food chain and Fiona, a bad@$$ con-artist chick who's trying to just live her life by with her sister, Sasha.


PROS:
+GREAT voice acting!
+Good, interesting and mysterious story.
+Strong, eccentric and unique characters (even include some Vault Hunters from past BL games).
+A love letter to fans of the Borderlands games.
+Lots and lots of humor, very fitting of Borderlands!
+Every "important" decisions taken from the start to the end matters. (hint: last boss fight).
+UltraWide 21:9 3400 x 1440 perfect support


CONS:
-At times, you feel the illusion of choice by making a decision, or lack thereoff of other possible decisions. Like, no matter what, SOME OF the decisions proposals from the game are artificial since the linearity forces an outcome out of any chosen option.
-Some QTE requires really quick reflexes. You're watching the scene unfold, take a sip from your beer and QTE! Unpredictable and puts you in constant alert. Thankfully, you can PAUSE the game at all time!
-Pacing felt weird from Episode 4 till the finale. EP 1 till 3 were rightly paced then once you reach EP 4, the story progression feels rushed.


OVERALL:
Along with The Wolf Among Us, this is clearly one of my favorite Telltale game. Lots of action, humor, drama, mysteries and all set in the Borderlands universe. The main story is mostly mysterious but the main focus and appeal are between the characters interaction. Decisions matter and when you least expect it, a decision could have a badder impact than another. I loved this game from start to finish even if it seemed to lack the proper pacing around Episode 4 till the Finale but if you're a fan of Telltale games in general, you will just love this game anyway! If you are a fan of Borderlands, HUGE BONUS!


RECOMMENDATION:
If you're a fan of Borderlands and Telltale games, you OUGHT it to yourself to get this game and play it ASAP. Love great storied point&click interactive games? GET THIS!

Oh I so wish a sequel get's made out of TFTB!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\thonestly one of the best games I ever played!
Within playing only two of the Telltale Games I have become a worshipper of that studio.

Tales from the Borderlands is a fantastic, incredibly dinamic Point and Click Adventure, capturing perfectly the essence of the Borderlands lore.
In this Game we take parts of two very different protagonists, who will live this adventure from two points of view, bringing us from hightech spacestations, seedy towns, dusty deserts to fantastic landscapes, trying to score the real big coup, obviously about a Vault.
The Story is a good balance between yet unknown content and fanservice, veterans of the Borderlands will obviously recognise an infinity of sidekicks, nevertheless for the story itself knowlege of the Borderlands story is not strictly required.
Both veterans and newcomers to Pandora will be able to enjoy Pandoras madness and rough beauty wich TTG in my eyes presents even better than the original Games, without breaking any lore.
The Characters are designed detailly, dotated of background and emotional depht and the evantual relationships evolve in a slow and fairly realistic way.
The Conversations are actually timed quite short, forcing you to make quick decisions, some of wich are irreverseble.
Often you will enconter Quicktime events, wich usually are not very hard, but still need you to be in Attention inevery moment, for as in the conversations the time you have to interact is quite short.
The diversity of different walkthroughs I con not yet tell, for I just finished my first unsighted, but there seem to be at least a hand full of different ways to go through the storyline. I reccomend to allow yourself to forget a little over some time before starting a new walkthrough, otherwise you could spoil a lot of the atmosphere of the story by always comparating it to the former walkthrough.
Do NOT expect a shooter, this is definitely not a classic Borderlands Game, but however not lacking the spirit of it neither of action.

For myself this is an absolute mustplay for anyone who loves Pandorian lore, enoys a good story, or likes Point and Click Adventures.\t\t\t\t\t
