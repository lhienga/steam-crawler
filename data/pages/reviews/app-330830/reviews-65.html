\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst thing I've got to say about this game, it's really funny, not your sarcastic Shadow Warrior like funny, or the more basic type like in Orcs Must Die, but a more satisfying, breaking the mold, out of nowhere funny, it makes a good effort to make one laugh.

Tales From the Borderlands does a great job being true to the essence of Borderlands, with ridiculous situations and crazy people all around in a kind of anarchic planet it becomes a great adaptation of the original titles. Played Borderlands 1 and 2 and even though I was skeptical about a Borderlands title without loot or level ups this game keeps your eyes peeled with Fiona's constant search for money that becomes important towards the end and the many fast paced scenes with quick time events. Also by the end one realizes how relevant it all was for the Borderlands universe, it's really not a

As for a Telltale title it has great story telling, fantastic scenery, very cool music, pertinent moral options and a unique build up of one's connection to the characters (Bro, bro, bro, brobrobro). My experience with the game was amazing especially because of seeing again and interacting with Jack, Zer0, Scooter and the loader bots, getting to understand how Hyperion works on Helios, the moon space station, and getting to know all the characters, especially loved the unexpected possible relationship with Sasha. The only meagre parts were when it got maybe too ridiculous, like with the finger fights in Helios, but oh well, I could see how it can be entertaining to some.

All in all I recommend playing it even if one never played the other Borderlands titles. "My righteous father! I have found you."
