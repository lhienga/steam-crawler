\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNever played a Telltales game before, but for a decent price on sale and a glimpse into Rhys from borderlands 3’s origin story. Though I’m not positive on whether it’s canon or not.

As a fan of the borderlands franchise. I decided to pick up this interactive novel. The story is a bit generic. About an average guy trying his best to move up in the company but gets pushed around so he goes on a journey to become a man and through the power of friendship mountains are moved 🙄. Though chapter 4-5 felt short and rushed to be completed. Really just slapped together. You’ll know by the end of the game when you see some characters go out of their way to round off their story arc with an improbable explanation.

I played though the story twice and from what I gathered your dialog options don’t matter. It creates some different dialog retorts but as far as the story goes it makes no difference. Which is a shame. I have heard people praise these games for their multiple paths but this one was pretty linear.

It’s an easy game to get 100% in if that’s what you’re into. I would have liked to see more achievements based on different decisions and outcomes. This game also unlocks some starter weapons in Borderlands pre-sequel (B:PS). Which will have no impact on those games. However, if you enjoy playing B:PS or speed running it. They are decent levelling/utility weapons but by no means OP or necessary.

I would really give this game a neutral rating, but since that’s not an option. Based on it being an alright Rhys origin story, easy 100% achievements, unlocking basic guns in (B:PS) and an alright price on sale. I’ll give this game a 👍🏻\t\t\t\t\t
