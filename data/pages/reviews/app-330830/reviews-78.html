I have a similar history with Telltale as a lot of people. Loved Walking Dead Season 1. Loved Wolf Among us. Started to get tired of their repetitive phoned in crap at some point and stopped playing their games.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave you ever wanted to explore Pandora more? See perspective of regular folk? Well, Tales from the Borderlands is for you kiddo.
Gameplay wise do not expect anything great. It is glorified walking sim in classic Telltale Games style, where you explore small place from one camera angle, check out some clickables and interesting things, choose dialogue options with people and try to find things that move you forward in the story. And the story is where it is all about.
We follow story of two Hyperion workers, who just want to get rich and two Pandoran thieves, that want the same. In some convoluted action their stories tie together in the biggest twister and you follow the entire story from the beginning to the very end. Including those there are few other original characters, but most of them are from original Borderlands series and to say writing is meh, would be an insult. The writing keeps true to the series (thanks to work of Telltale with Gearbox writers) and exploring Pandora from different way was unforgettable experience. All the small details and easter eggs filled game was a treat to play for Borderlands series fan. All the new characters fitted world and I felt fully sucked into the lore.
This is one of few games Telltale didn't ♥♥♥♥ up. Maybe gameplay wise it's trash, but for all the fans they will find themselves intrigued and interested while playing. If this is your first instance of any game from Borderlands franchise I would recommend playing Borderlands 2 first (The first one and Pre-Sequel you also can, but mostly it's important to play 2nd one).
7/10 not enough Vaughn abs\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn my opinion, this is probably the best of the TellTale games. If you want to have a chill time and take in a cool story while having fun, this is a pretty good pick. It does have a couple of intense and emotional moments, but approaches most situations with a sense of levity that makes it all quite enjoyable.
Like alot of TT games this one is all about choices, and boy does this one have alot of cool ones to make. But also like most TT games there are gonna be some choices that just don't matter at all and have little to no consequence, much like jon's heritage and the nightking.
While this is based in the borderlands universe/world, I dont think you have to played or liked the other ones to enjoy this game, I think it can stand out on its own, but playing the other games definitely does add more value to the experience and characters. Its a shame there probably wont be a second in this series.
If this game ever goes back on sale, I would definitely recommend picking it up.
Also, The soundtrack is absolute fire.\t\t\t\t\t
