\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTales from the Borderlands is a typical Telltale Games (R.I.P.) video game. The decision based gameplay is something that Telltale has become very skilled at, tailoring it to the various IPs they have licensed to carry these games and their stories. This game is no different. The Borderlands art style fits right into the cartoonish and comic book look that Telltale games have had in the past and makes this pairing a match made in heaven. However, the typical faults of the engine that Telltale uses for their games shows itself once more. There are various points where the models in the game or the stuff on the screen just wonks out and completely takes you out of the story and immersion. Which is a shame because the story is very compelling and exciting for anyone who has played previous Borderlands games. It is interesting and important to the overall lore of Pandora and the Borderlands world which makes this a necessary play for any die hard fans of the franchise. The pacing is terrific and even though the gunplay (a known characteristic of Borderlands games) is sparse, the situations and events of the story will leave the player feeling like they experienced an action packed game. This is definitely one of the better Telltale adventure games and belongs with the likes of the early seasons of The Walking Dead and The Wolf Among Us as one of the best games in their library with this type of format and gameplay.

8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the Borderlands series, the gameplay is great, but the lore is something that I really appreciate, because I'm a guy who digs good stories, this game could possibly be one of my favourites in the BL series. One thing that the BL games lacks is giving personality to the characters you play in that game, it's usually the game after that you get to know the vault hunters on a personal level, I don't like that. In this game the new characters you're introduced to, especially the protagonists you play as, are extremely fleshed out. You really get to like them, instead of in other BL games where they're just a vessel for you, who you pick doesn't really matter other than their skill trees and action skill. but they're lacking personalities.

Having said that, this game is very bare bones gameplay wise, there's not much to do. A few segments where you walk around and interact with objects, it's a TT game so as you're most likely aware it doesn't matter what you choose in the end you get the same result, but choices you make do give you different scenes so it is worth a second play through to choose something different and see if you get something new.

It's unlikely we'll ever get a TftB2, but I really hope we do.\t\t\t\t\t
