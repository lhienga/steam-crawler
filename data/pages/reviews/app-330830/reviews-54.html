\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs this a game? Does this game have gameplay? Sure it does. It just doesn't have a challenge, is all. If you're looking for a game with a challenge, this probably isn't it. But if you're looking for a GREAT story, I have truly never experienced finer. It is absolutely one of the best video game stories there is. Familiarity with the Borderlands franchise is a huge plus that will inform you regarding the depravity of Handsome Jack, and what it means for Rhys to pedestalize him. However if you've never played the prior games you can still enjoy a rollicking adventure about super heartwarming friendships.

It's still funny to me that the game has an inventory but you can't interact with it. It feels like they originally designed it to be at first. But the linearity is preferable to the 90s point-and-click adventure games which are pretty much my least favorite kind of game I'm sad to say, despite how much I've tortured myself trying to enjoy them. As a result the puzzles in TftB are not hard. (But it's more fun than the old days of spending hours rubbing everything in your inventory on every single thing in a room, in some totally illogical and unintuitive way, trying to see if it advances the story somehow.) So in Tales it ends up that the punishment or reward you get is typically the reactions of the characters and is purely psychological. Works for me, because it's just so engaging. There are a few variations on how the story can proceed, which in retrospect may feel less significant. What's much more significant are your choices in your characters' personalities, and this is enough of a sense of control. I gotta say, I watched countless playthrough vids and streams of this game before I was finally able to play it, and playing it is STILL better than watching.

Wouldn't call it the best gameplay ever (to this day I still give that one to Katamari Damacy, haha). But best story in a game ever? It's my favorite, now. I learned a lot from it and will definitely apply that to the way I compose my own comics.

Hint: Do not be afraid of "..." answers, if you feel it's appropriate. Much of the time it will simply advance the story along without a hitch. However, there are occasions where if you leave room for the other character to speak, they will offer insight that they wouldn't normally. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think anyone expected this title to be as good as it is. It's not often you see a game series genre switch from a loot-based first-person shooter right into an interactive drama, and somehow it works so, so well. The game switches vibe throughout yet still feels super coherent, filling you with a variety of emotions. There's lots of heart from both from new and returning characters, incredible performances from all of the cast, it's surprisingly funny, and there's even a unique choice mechanic not seen in the other Telltale games whereby you obtain currency to use or save at your leisure (I recommend the latter, thank me later). If you've found yourself missing the quality writing seen in Borderlands 2 after playing 3, this is a must play. 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t
