"Let's take the combat out of this franchise and put in writing instead!"

When I was younger, Borderlands 2 and Borderlands were some of my first introductions to the looter-shooter. The prospect of spending time on an alien planet that's ratchet as all hell and shooting things because it's fun was pretty alluring and a real hoot. That had me hooked. I'd go on several quests and follow through on a lot of goofy shenanigans.

Of course, when I got older and replayed those games and listened to the goofy shenanigan dialogue by the Borderlands 2 characters, I felt like I was getting too old, because some of these lines felt either aged like mozzarella left out on the kitchen counter or just kinda obnoxious. Obnoxious, sure, because there are going to be crazy people on the planet Pandora who aren't going to be as good with talking with others as they are with shooting their face off, but...dare I say...I was craving some of that.

Okay, well not from Borderlands of all things, but Telltale's writing staff on this one really had me hooked.

Okay, so I didn't really think this was 100% worth picking up when it first came out, but in my defense...not a lot of people did.

If what I recall is correct, then compared to other Telltale games and especially Borderlands games, not a lot of fans picked this up compared to its subsidiaries and cousins. Tales from the Borderlands tells a very entertaining story with a lot of elements from tales about heists and misfits becoming a sort of pseudo-family. Characters like the unlikely protagonist Rhys and the other unlikely protagonist Fiona are a joy to watch fumble through with their plans. Watching (relatively) normal people stumble around the universe of Borderlands among other established characters is fantastic, and I really think the writing here describing the lot doing their thing is really good too.

There's a sort of "♥♥♥♥-talking" vibe I get from the characters where they constantly make fun of and/or threaten/are trying to kill each other, and that makes for some hilarious interactions as well as some genuinely nice ones between the main cast. I think the setting of Borderlands lends itself well to writing that doesn't take the events too seriously, but just enough to make all the jokes land while telling a story with some interesting stakes involved. For the most part, you don't want to see the main cast get pulverized, but not in a way series like The Walking Dead might make you attached to the characters. You get the feeling that "these characters are pretty close to dying" from most encounter they have, and it just uses that as a vessel to deliver a lot of really good bits of comedy, dark and otherwise.

Even without playing a Borderlands game, this game carries itself up pretty high. Though, I feel like any fan of Borderlands who wants to see more captivating interactions and dialogue between characters should try to find a way to experience this game, be it playing it or watching it. That's not to say that non-fans won't enjoy it, but they'll definitely enjoy it more if they get into Borderlands.

...Though, some of the dialogue in the Borderlands games isn't exactly what I'd recommend trying the series for.

Though my choices don't really matter much in the end(Borderlands 3 is already out and I feel like the cast's storyline is already pretty set in stone), I thought this Telltale game was great for what it set out to accomplish using the Borderlands setting, stealing our hearts as these characters stole, shot, killed, and defiled.\t\t\t\t\t
