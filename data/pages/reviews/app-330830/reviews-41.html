\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPresentation (3.5/5) - This game continues to render 3-d graphics into 2-d as in other Borderland games, giving it that comic style. The graphics, including animations, however do feel a tad below the standard set by Borderlands 2, despite being released after it, but not to the point of being a distraction from the game.

Story/Dialogue (4/5) - I was a bit conflicted when I was playing this. After the hilarious confrontation early on with Vazquez, it sank in that the humor doesn't feel that much like Borderlands. Not just the humor, but the dynamic between the characters is more genuine, less wacky, and the general tone is overall a bit restrained for a Borderlands game. However, I learned to appreciate it a bit differently. In this game, you have a very coherent narrative with character development - the writing is in some ways superior to the other games in the franchise. It might have sacrificed some of its trademark comic appeal to get there, it might have sidelined most of the characters we love in the franchise, but the new characters offered here are very likable, have depth, and come with top-tier voice acting, some of the best in videogames or animation. Thankfully we still have 'Handsome Jack' and the lines he is given are as good as it gets.

Gameplay (2.5/5) - One way the game falters is how it missed opportunities in the gameplay department. Nearly all the action is discovering what to click to advance the game (interaction with environment isn't that great), and survival and narrative is driven by quick action events where you press certain arrows or keys as directed, not that exciting. The exploration component has so much opportunity for witty descriptions and observations, but it feels very constrained sadly - there is very little to do and it feels like the game is holding your hand to progress further. The money element is very inconsequential as well. The game could have been so much more with a little bit more excitement in action or if this was an intended decision to have a very low difficulty, I wish they could have expanded on interacting with the environment better.\t\t\t\t\t
