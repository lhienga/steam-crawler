\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an amazing game. It plays like a 12-15 hour interactive movie with quick time events and choices definitely matter. The story is rich with characters and an interesting plot. It'll make you feel emotion for things you spent hours killing in Borderlands 1 and 2. Gives you a different perspective and for me, refreshed my stale outlook after countless replays of the first two games. It made me want to experience them again, which I intend to do before the third installment is released. Plenty of humour to be found, the game had me laughing a lot- and what a soundtrack. Absolutely on par with the rest of the franchise and every episode has a signature track for a total of 5 great tunes. This was my first Telltale game, but I've joined the general consensus that this is their best game. Too bad there won't be more, they'll be missed. If you love Borderlands as much as I do and are wondering if you should pick this up for a bit of a fix until Borderworlds/3 comes out, grab it on sale at the very least. You definitely won't regret it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never played Borderlands or Telltale games, but this series stands on its own. And it stands tall; every inch of the game shows evident, a thousand little jokes are scattered throughout the world, the dialog is charming and funny and the plot that holds itself together is strong in its own right. I laughed a lot, took some real emotional punches and really came to care about these characters and the crappy world they struggle through. 9.6/10, just watch out for a few scenes where walking forward too soon abruptly dumps you into the next sequence without properly finishing the last one. Also, it helps to veiw the game as a story you're experiencing rather than one you're writing from scratch; better quality but not totally freeform (and that's ok!).\t\t\t\t\t
