\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTales from the Borderlands is a story-driven game made by Telltale Games, and follows the story of a Hyperion employee, post Borderlands 2. The game itself is a blend of point-and-click, and interactive dialogue choices, with some QTEs thrown in for good measure. It may not sound fantastic, and if you came looking for action you will be disappointed, but the mixture works surprisingly well, doubly so in the context of the material.

Done in the same style as the Borderlands games, i.e. cell-shaded to create its own distinctive look, the story adapts based on your choices what you choose to say, how you choose to treat an individual, etc. The setup is made all the more interesting however by the fact that you follow this path, and interact in the manner stated above, with not just one, but two characters you will find yourself in situations where you are effectively playing the two of them off against each other. The characters also act largely as a human would so you will find yourself becoming quite attached during the playthrough, which may make certain choices more difficult.

Given that this was my first Telltale game, I did not know what to expect, but was very pleasantly surprised by the quality of the storytelling, and the mechanisms in which you are made to feel invested in the game world. There is a lot of humour to be found, and the quality of the writing is better than you would expect from a simple game.

Highly recommended for Borderlands fans, and those who enjoy story-driven games.

Played on Windows 10 Pro x64 without any issues.\t\t\t\t\t
