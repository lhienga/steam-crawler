\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTelltale's best series. Their Walking Dead games are great but oppressively bleak. Tales From the Borderlands is almost a remedy. its funny, having most, if not all of the charm of Borderlands 2, thanks in part to writer Anthony Burch's involvement. Its emotion, packing surprising moments of character, especially for the robotic allies you gain along the way. The voice acting is top tier, our protagonists being voiced by industry veterans Troy Baker (Joel: TLoU, Talion: Middle Earth series, Batman: Telltale's Batman, Kanji Tatsumi: Persona 4. etc) and Laura Bailey (Rise Kujikawa: Persona 4, Fetch: Infamous, Kait: Gears 5, Nadine: Uncharted 4 and spinoff, etc) as well as the excellent Patrick Warburton, Chris Hardwicke, and almost everyone else is equally top quality. Similar to the main series, each episode starts with a bangin' licensed track, starting with Jungle's "Busy Earnin'" and featuring other tracks from The Rapture, Shawn Lee, Twin Shadow, James Blake, Excision, and First Aid Kit.
If you like Borderlands or the episodic point and click style, this ones a no-brainer, decidedly more light-hearted than most of its contemporary counterparts such as Life Is Strange, The Walking Dead, and The Wolf Among Us. Similarly, if you're looking for a uniquely funny game, something thats kinda rare, this is a solid choice. the humor gets going really early and is a bit smarter (although not entirely) than the Borderlands 2 humor. There are Butt Stallion level jokes, but theres also some excellent situational comedy, something possible thanks to Telltale's tight direction and less player interactivity.
I can't recommend it to everyone but those that can stand this genre of game will find what is in my opinion, the best Borderlands game, full stop. the humor was more consistently funny while the characters mostly received a level of depth that the story so far has sorely needed. Handsome Jack is a brilliant character here, almost a precurser to Cyberpunk 2077's version of Johnny Silverhand. The story is nothing special, another vault hunter story, but the fact Telltale did a more epic Vault story than Gearbox ever could, without much shooting or real fighting at all is to be commended.\t\t\t\t\t
