\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately after many years of waiting this game seems to have learnt very few lessons from past asymmetrical attempts within VR by heavily restricting the giant's ability to move, as well as straight out ignoring basic VR accessibility options.

There are no movement options beyond some levels having pre-placed portals that re-orientate the giant to a new spot. Beyond this there's no option to rotate, move, teleport, or get around beyond physically moving in your play space, which doesn't seem to have any restrictions. There is only 1 button assigned to re-align your play space which is what VR developers were doing back in 2016.

What I can only assume is an attempt at balancing, since others have commented that this didn't use to be the case during the alpha play tests, has only made it so those with a large sizeable play space and house are greatly empowered vs those without the space, who are heavily disadvantaged. Even with a sizeable play space that I have I found myself feeling very restricted as the combative nature of the game encourages you to swing and slam your arms/hands, but having to move so much within your play space to get anywhere increases the risk of accidentally damaging your hit or environment without any built-in options in-game.

I doubt they keep this in as it's fundamentally poorly designed, especially as some maps have not been modified with this restriction in mind causing the giant to be unable to reach the player outright (unless they have exceedingly large play spaces to move of course). Until basic VR movement options have been added I can't recommend this and for how many years it's been in development there are other alternative asymmetrical VR games that came out years ago and did it better.\t\t\t\t\t

I feel as if both sides are pretty balanced and the gameplay for both PC and VR is addicting

My only real concern is that if you have a smaller play space in VR, it makes it difficult for the giant to be able to get at the knights in certain areas and the game is so physically active that you can easily lose where you are in real life which means you sorta have to reset yourself in your room mid-game which is distracting

I think this could be easily fixed by giving the giant some sort of smooth locomotion besides teleporting

Also the maps that have a lot of points to teleport, sometimes you can teleport to an undesired area because of how close the teleports are to eachother and once your grip teleport locks onto a teleport location, it doesnt switch up until you let go and grip again
this is really easy to accidentally mess up when you grip too early in the heat of battle so I think it would be better if instead the grip would change what teleport point it locks onto dependent on where your hand is pointing even while still holding the grip

besides those things, the game was still felt pretty balanced, was really fun, and I do recommend it\t\t\t\t\t
