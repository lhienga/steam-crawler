Giant has no cool down and can walk wherever if the vr user's room is big enough, and knights can grapple and smash you with a hammer and its almost impossible to avoid. I have been waiting for this game to release on steam and the trailers do it more justice than the game itself. My second pc game in, everything on the map completely vanished and made an extremely loud buzzing sound and everyone got kicked out. For how long the game has been in development, its unfortunate how it turned out, there is also no background music or theme, its just sound effects, so it makes the experience even more meh.

I know the game is early access, but at its current state, there are way more uses for your money VR wise, and even then if you got the knight pass, the game play is too generic. If you are on the fence about the game, wait until the game releases more bug fixes and major updates or just only play as a free knight.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThings this game does well:

Bridges the gap between friends who have VR and friends who do not
Makes for an interesting party game, as it can be played locally with 5 people on the same screen (1 VR and 4 Xbox controllers)
Gives the Giant player a satisfying feeling of superiority and power
Gives the Knight players a satisfying experience maneuvering the battlefield to avoid incoming attacks and deliver their own

Things the game still needs (Keeping in mind it's still Early Access):

Music.
There is little to no music in the game. Some intense tracks would really amp up the party and make the battles feel more epic.

Sound effects.
Some sound effects are okay, like the fissure attack the giant delivers by pounding his fists on the ground. Others, like the grapple beam and the rocket launcher feel a little lack luster, they could use a little something to make them feel more impactful, especially when landing a successful attack against the giant. Powerful sound effects really make a difference with gameplay experience

Mobility.
The Giant has the ability to warp between different points on the map, but it feels clunky and doesn't always work and I have trouble getting where I want to go. Sometimes the wrong portal gets highlighted and I can't make quick escapes from attacks, or make quick surprise attacks by warping effectively. Once this gets polished it will be a fantastic feature.

The knights have amazing mobility, but I still feel like the rockets could be controlled a little better when riding, as could the grapple beam. I find myself ragdolling and falling a little too often from my own attempts to jump and grapple. Maybe I'm just bad at it and need more practise

TL;DR Game does a great job at combining VR and non-VR gameplay, allows for local multiplayer for 5 players on the same screen and has a whole lot of potential. Very fun. I recommend supporting it
