\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame Context: Big giant head (person on VR headset) versus knight (person on Mouse and Keyboard). Knight has to use projectiles and close range combat to destroy all the giant's armor/health; while the giant has to use his hands to throw objects at the knight, can ground slam or throw knight out of map when he gets close, dodge/block projectiles, and shoot laser beam out of hand after blocking projectiles. Whoever loses all their health, loses. There can only be 1 giant and 1-4 knights (game is more fun when doing 1v1).

Review: Very fun, I've been waiting since 5/2020 for this game to come out, and it finally did. It's very knight sided on default rules, but you can change the rules manually. You also need a lot of space irl to play this game, you will break a window or two (jk... or am I...). The Valve Index controls are just borderline stupid, pause shouldn't be my thumb grip button, I keep accidentally pressing it, causing me to die. There also needs to be more cool-downs on certain moves like the roll, hammer, rocket spam, grapple, etc. Knight spawning with hammer is not a great idea. It's so easy for them to deflect thrown objects, and just spam grappling my head, hammer, grappling my head, hammer, so on and so forth, it's such an op meta. During the alpha, hammer was obtainable as an item to pick up, which was fine. Again, I know that most of these settings can be changed in the rules, I'm just saying that the default rules are just way to competitive, especially for the giant. This should be a more casual party game to play with family and friends. Grabbing knights is also really hard, your hand would have to be completely still to do it, or else the game registers it as a hit that isn't powerful enough to damage them. Game is very optimized though, so you can probably play with anyone who has a low-end pc. I'm also glad that there is a much more developed tutorial now, but still, changing binds should be a feature to best suit the players needs and play-style. I know I'm saying a lot of negatives about the game, but I still really love this game. It's my #1 favorite VR game now, and I'm really glad that they made knight pass free. It just need some balance changes, that's all. Just for a more casual, fun, family-friendly experience.

Rating: 8/10

TLDR: Best Asynchronous VR game ever!! Game is very knight sided, but rules can be changed, so it doesn't matter; Valve Index binds could be remapped better, give us custom bind changes; Very competitive game, not for casual players; VR players might break something irl with controllers if your room is not as big as a sound stage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game because it plays fantastic and is a pretty great party game so far.

HOWEVER:

1. I didn't get to play the alpha, but I agree with everyone else that the giant's movement needs to come back. The teleporting just isn't cutting it and if you play this in a smaller play space like I do, you don't have a lot of free movement to go after knights that just sit on missiles/cannons.

2. Cannons suck if you're playing anything more than 1v1. It's just a feels-bad mechanic that the knights get a ton of free, unblockable shots literally every 15ish seconds. It doesn't help that some maps have not just one or two, but six or more cannons lined up for a knight to easily access. This feels even worse as the giant when you realize that you get no i-frames between taking damage so you are literally one or two bad dodges from losing an entire match.

3. Knight powers are waaaaay overtweaked. The cooldown for anything that they do is nearly nonexistent, with the parry being especially egregious. 1v1 feels reasonably balanced, but once the giant's attention is split between two or more knights, they are simply able to move too fast and have too many ways of damaging the giant for the giant to win without a sizable life handicap.


I really like this game and the idea behind it is fantastic. The fact that knights can play for free is also a real stand-up move and I commend the devs for doing that. I just don't see myself playing this long term if the balance isn't fixed in a timely manner.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game at release. Some pros and cons of the game:
Cons:
- Some maps still need a bit of an update for giants to move around (teleport) so that there is an even playing field. Only one map is able to teleport for now ***Some maps are unplayable for Giants as they need to move around physically in the room, can be very limiting for some VR players that are more comfortable with using joysticks.
- need faster response time with hand movement in game if playing on VR
Pros:
- Very fun game to play with friends
- Easy to navigate controls and understand game play, great tutorial before getting on the game.
- Many variations of maps and utility for Knights
- Very cool use for utility for knights, Giants only get environment utility (trees, boxes and towers)

Suggestions for the game:
- Two player VR game mode, as more people can play the giant at once and also possibly the transition of switching roles from PC to VR
- More utility for Giants and movement for Giants around the map\t\t\t\t\t
