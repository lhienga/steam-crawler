\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot overstate how ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hard it is to write a review of Katana Zero. Which raises the question: how the ♥♥♥♥ did the developers write Katana Zero????

Is this the work of a genius or of a madman, we will never know. What I do know is that I did not expect this neon hack-n-slash game to shine through it’s story of all things, but my god does it shine.
It’s funny, it’s clever, it’s surreal and deals with ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ time distortion -excuse the cursing, even writing this review has me going slightly mad. I should be studying law right now. I cannot, for this game won’t leave my brain alone. This is catharsis.

The gameplay is also mind-bogglingly good. The levels are surprisingly varied, they sit juuuust at the right difficulty level for me, and succeeding at them never failed to make me (mid gamer) feel like an absolute badass (and a monster. I don’t care about killing people in video games, they’re video games right - but Zero cared, and so I cared.) Everything about its aesthetics serves the story and gameplay, from each lair you have to infiltrate from the city, your apartment, and especially the excellent soundtrack and the whole…surveillance camera system.

Free will is a lie, my life is a lie, aaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaah\t\t\t\t\t
