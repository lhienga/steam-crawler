\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA beautiful action-platformer that's a puzzle game of sorts -- in the very same way Hotline Miami is -- in that it's all about you finding the best way to kill a level full of guys without getting one-shot yourself. You're a crazy bathrobe samurai living in a cruddy apartment and you have a dependency for "chronos," a die-and-rewind drug that has permanently screwed up your sense of time. You gotta have it; if you don't, time will freeze for you and you'll be stuck in a single, subjective moment forever.

The feels, the kills: Cool story interludes in a 32-bit futuristic city after a devastating war. Psychiatrist is your handler and dope-giver. Blood flash-sprays everywhere when you kill. Cartoon goofball humor is mixed in with the serious bits -- anime-like in that regard. Slow down time to surgically slash and roll past gunfire; fight a few tough bosses who are chemically or genetically modified; choose your dialogue or literally skip it with an imprecation or brusque grunt; reflect bullets back at gunmen with well-timed swings.

I really can't find anything to dislike about this super-fun game, other than that the story is not complete -- but the devs are working on free DLC to shore that up. I feel like, for now, full-price is pretty steep if you're not figuring on getting gold in the Time Attack mode after you beat the brief and unfinished -- but very action-packed -- main campaign. I might actually go back and get that gold, though . . . In the Devolver "Die by the Sword" bundle, it's a no-brainer.\t\t\t\t\t

It's Like a Gory Version of Celeste
This game RIPS so hard and really embodies the idea of a "combat puzzle" game. Most rooms require you to evaluate the situation and find the correct plan of action, and then execute that plan of action with decent window in-between each action. Choosing which enemy to kill first, which barrels to shoot, whether or not you should stealth a section, the game has plenty of variety in approaching situations and its amazing. Some of the situations do offer quite a bit of challenge, especially the boss fights.

That's just the gameplay though, the small segments in-between each killing floor really flesh this game out even further. The dialogue options you are given when speaking with the "therapist", the small talk you can make with the receptionist, it's not necessary at all but the fact that it's there elevates the series tremendously. The story is also fantastic, with you questioning "What the ♥♥♥♥ is going on?" quite often. I got very invested in the story and I eagerly await the DLC.

The 2D art style is also fantastic. The blood and gore in the scenes look great. My only detractor from the game would be that the stealth sections are kind of a drag if you choose that route. Dying requires you to keep doing them over again and waiting, but you do always have the option of going weapons loud. It's just you'll have to deal with the potential story consequences later.

I would HEAVILY recommend that everyone pick-up this indie classic! Great story, great gameplay, and fantastic execution.\t\t\t\t\t
