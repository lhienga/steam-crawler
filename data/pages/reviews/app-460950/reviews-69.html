\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI swear, it must be a fundamental law of the universe that every violent action indie game has the most bangin' soundtrack you've ever heard in your life. Hotline Miami (1+2), ULTRAKILL, Furi, and now Katana Zero. I went into the game expecting a fun little game where you slash some baddies. I did not expect to have an eargasmic experience. My favorite tracks in the game have to be Overdose, Nocturne, and Blue Room (11 minute verson). Sneaky Driver and Third District are also fantastic.

And the writing is... surprisingly good! This game has a really compelling story and some genuinely impactful dialogue (and a great sense of humor). I was also surprised at the technical depth of the dialogue system, with variations on how dialogue is presented and often allowing the player to interrupt NPCs for unique interactions.

My only problem with the game is that it is too short (2-3 hours total). However, a second chapter is planned to come out... soon, I think? You can also go for achievements if you want to extend your time with the game but be warned -- they can be quite difficult (I still haven't gotten the speedrun achievement).\t\t\t\t\t

It's very comparable to Hotline Miami, but with more heavy and movement-heavy gameplay (and it is very very clearly inspired by Hotline Miami).
The extra features of the game, being the Hard Mode and the challenge sections, are just so immaculately done. They do exactly as advertised and are just so good at it.
