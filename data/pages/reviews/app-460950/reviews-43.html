*put on your headphones, turn on your synthwave, draw your katana*

I don't normally enjoy "precise" games, in fact: I'm a shameless button-masher that gave up on dark souls after dying to the Asylum demon 15 times. But something about Katana ZERO makes me want to reconsider.

The controls are so tight and responsive that even my button-mashing a** was holding back on spamming x. The fluidity of movement, the 1-hit-1-kill violence of combat that somehow still feels fair – it all comes together to create an experience that's just. so. satisfying.

And then there's the super quick respawns – an absolute godsend for those of us who have enough frustration in their everyday lives, and don't want to waste 3 min getting back to the boss every time we miss a dodge. The game respects your time by letting you dive back into the action almost instantly. It turns failure into a learning opportunity, and before you know it, you're seamlessly stringing together combos like a true samurai master.

Finally, there is the atmosphere: the story is a lot deeper and more interesting than I expected. Zero is a deeply broken man torn between his role as a killing machine and his kind nature, and the game doesn't shy away from letting you explore that. Paired with the absolute banger of a soundtrack that has made it's way into my focus/study playlist makes this game easy to recommend!

Post Season 17 Pinkachu FTW.\t\t\t\t\t
