\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn extremely good game, and for 15 dollars you get a very good story, however, as per usual with ALL indie games that have cliffhanger endings I reccommend extreme caution, as if you get invested in the world or story there's ALWAYS a possibility that you won't see the ending come to fruition that you spent time on. With that preamble out of the way, the review:

GOOD:
* Game plays incredibly smooth and is beyond amazing at what it does despite the simplicity of it. There's only a few bosses, only a few enemy types, but the game gets MILEAGE out of it.
* The story is amazing and perfectly told, most notably with how amazing the dialogue options work and feel.
* Art and music is amazing.
* The game is 2 hours, but it has a hard mode, a speedrun mode, and at least 2 variations of every cutscene.
* Two endings you can get in a single playthrough.
* At least 2 different variants of every cutscene and a single secret boss, although you'll likely need a guide to get it.

Bad:
* A core issue is that the game's story is not finished nor fully realized, meaning that when you BEAT the final level, you will not get any extra confirmation on things that matter. It's honestly SUCH a let down given how awesome the game is up to that point, to a point where I'm surprised this game only cost 15 dollars instead of 30 cause, outside of it's shortness, it's lovely. Now I am worried about the fact that the DLC is still on the way after 4 years cause development hell is a thing and I'm especially worried cause the world, the story, everything is so unique and thrilling that if the DLC comes out and doesn't satisfy, what is such a unique and amazing world. I can't express in words how captivated I was with the world, with the lore, and while we don't get the FULL picture you get enough that it makes me crave more. If, for whatever reason, the DLC can't happen or it's ballooned [Hopefully] into a 30 dollar sequel I'd gladly pay up, but good golly this is the second game I've played this year that was absolutely stellar but has the POTENTIAL that a sequel / finalization will never see the light of day which is just so depressing.

OVERALL: I love the game, I'd recommend it, but don't get hooked like me, you WILL be dissatisfied with the ending and that dissatisfaction is honestly quite sad given that the game JUST starts answering / asking questions then it ends. I love it, but that disappointment is very real.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAskii jumped from creating a few (very cool) flash games, to making what is probably one of the best things I've played all year. The gameplay here is incredible. It feels like a combination of dead cells and hotline miami (without the roguelike part of course). Though the game is a bit on the short side, it is wonderfully replayable and they knew that when adding in speedrun mode. The story telling is really good, and the cut-off choice mechanic is something unique I don't think I've seen in any other game. The visuals are great too.

15$ is a perfectly fair price for the game, so don't feel pressured to wait for a sale. It's definitely something I'll be coming back to time and time again. If looking as stylish as possible, speedrunning, or fast paced die-learn-repeat loops are things that interest you, pick this game up IMMEDIATELY.\t\t\t\t\t
