\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game RIPS. Honestly didn't know what to expect because I bought this game while it was on sale. I just saw the art style of it, which I liked, and it was different from the usual copy paste modern FPS aesthetic that I normally play. I wish I could refund my money just so that I could pay full price, because the game would be worth every penny spent.

The story was very mysterious and shrouded in unapologetic violence. The soundtrack is perfectly matched with the dystopian future aesthetic. I love that the fighting in and of itself is a puzzle. Trying to map out how you will take care of each room and then applying your corrections only to die and discover something else you could have done differently to yield a different outcome, is very pleasing and lends to a creative side that I wasn't expecting.

Great game, and I will definitely be replaying it to see what else I can get out of the story, and to see if your choices really have a greater consequence to the way the game plays out.

This masterpiece easily one of my favorite indie games of all time. Everything about it is amazing. The only issue is that it doesn't go on forever. The combat is really satisfying and getting the achievements for this game was fun as well.

The game is structured around that fact that you die in one shot. The enemies however die in one shot too. What that means is that you have to weave around bullets and dodge incoming attacks with just your sword. The rolling, time slowing down and throwing mechanics work very well. On normal mode the game can be played reactively with you just parrying and dodging everything that comes at you. On hard mode however, you are forced to learn the layout of the level and find a strategy which will get you through as there are simply too many enemies.

The story is also amazing as there are many dialogue and in-game choices a player can make to affect dialogue and the endings.

I cant write a lot about it because its just too good. Buy it right now. You wont regret it!\t\t\t\t\t
