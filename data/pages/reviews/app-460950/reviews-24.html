\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish there was a more nuanced option than "do you recommend this game or not", because objectively, Katana Zero is a pretty good game. That being said, I didn't like it.

A good chunk of my playtime was stuck in level 4, where I wanted to be The Good Guy and not kill anyone like I was asked to. I had to remap the controls to do this because the controller support was poorly implemented (holding Y sneaks, pressing Y enters a room, and it's very bad at detecting which of those you want to do). The plot, which I've seen a lot of people praise, didn't really impress me. I found the main antagonist to be a stereotype -- the pathetic, sexually perverted fat man whose insecurities drive him to cruelty. It's an archetype that I find offensive, although I don't blame anyone for being able to look past it. I didn't play through the multiple endings (routes?) because, frankly, I really didn't want to see that main antagonist's writing any more than I already did. I guess it might be a hate spiral because of how much I disliked the earlier levels? But I still really did not enjoy myself.

The pixel art is, genuinely, gorgeous. The animations were very fun to look at and were a highlight of my experience. A lot of the background dialogue was really enjoyable! I had some trouble on the level with the Slender Rickies, so I have a soft spot for them. The relationship the main character had with the little girl was sweet too. As many issues I have with the writing and plot, I can't deny that the game had really good dialogue, with the kid especially. I could tell that the author knows how kids work.

That being said, the main antagonist really did ruin everything for me. I found myself wanting to kill him immediately so that I didn't have to deal with the way he was written, but he fell into every stereotype and trope that I hate. I was really looking forward to finishing the level where he first appeared so I wouldn't have to look at him be the Pathetic Evil Fat Man, but he continued being that until the very end of the game.

If it isn't clear, I'm a bit of an SJW type. If that means my opinion is nothing to you, then that's genuinely fine -- you'll probably enjoy this game.

The gameplay loop is a bit frustrating, but I think that's the idea. I found myself trying to do cool stuff so that I would be able to watch the replay at the end of the screen, but it never really came out right. All of the satisfying sound design -- which is, honestly, very satisfying -- was muted and the slow-down-time system wasn't complex enough to do stuff that was really genuinely awesome you know? I think I would have preferred a rewind system over the slow-down system, it would have made the replays at the end way cooler, but I get that that's probably not feasible for this kind of game.

The story kind of felt like a bunch of different cool ideas strung together by some vague thematic ties. That can work, but I didn't enjoy it here. It reminds me of Little Nightmares, another game I don't really like, where the plot is confusing on purpose so that the player puts the pieces together in a way they think makes sense, but really wasn't intentional at all. I don't necessarily think that there isn't an answer for the questions that get asked here. Chronos is a fun concept. I bet the ending where you don't die is interesting. But I found myself really... detached, I guess is the word. I was playing the game thinking to myself about how little I cared about everything that was going on. Maybe that's the point? The character you're playing as is meant to be completely detached from existence, only hanging on by the thread that is that little girl, but it wasn't enjoyable for me. I just kept dreading having to deal the aspects of the story I didn't like. Which were most of them.

It does a lot of cool stuff. When I said that this game is objectively good, I meant it. Occasionally flashing back to the scene in the chair is great fun, the ACTUAL scene in the chair where you die over and over again and learn new things about the situation to tell V is genuinely great writing, something that could only ever be effectively done in a video game.

But it just didn't grab me. I found what little plot there was to be a slog, the gameplay was fun but often inconsiderate and only difficult in a way that felt cheap, and the pixel art of the same five guys dying started getting really old by the latter half.

I just didn't like it.

But, y'know. You probably will. I'm just a ♥♥♥♥♥.

5.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
