\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this game is complicated. A little history for why my playtime wont line up with what I say. I played this game a few years ago and put it down because I had more important things to attend to only to return to it recently. Now the review.

To put it simply this game exceeds at everything it attempts. The story is great, visuals are amazing with some of the highest quality pixel art you can get, and gameplay is snappy and fun. The game plays almost like a puzzle game with you needing to figure out how to approach each room to kill all the enemies. So to put it simply this game is great.

But, it's really short. I finished the game after playing for 4.5 hours. Which for a 20$ price is awfully short even for me which normally doesn’t mind as long as the game’s good, which again this is very good. But it's hard to recommend outside of a sale because of the price.

This game also has the potential to annoy some people with its ending. How do I say this? It gives a conclusion that I personally find satisfying, but does leave some loose ends and a blatant “TO BE CONTINUED” screen. All for a DLC that has no release date even 5 years after the games launch. With the last update simply being gameplay of level 1, 1 year ago. Though I was able to find a post by one of the game's artists on Mar 5, 2024 showing some Aseprite files that could be for the game, but its vague enough I can’t say for sure it's directly related to Katana Zero.

The current ending isn’t any worse then most games that leave room open for a squeal matter of fact I think it's better than most games that leave room for a squeal. But if you are wanting an ending with no loose ends whatsoever, you're in the wrong place.

TLDR: Its a great short game with a more open ending. If you don’t mind a quality over quantity approach go for it, can’t recommend it unless your one of those people.
