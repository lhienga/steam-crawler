This game however has one thing that a lot of games don't have. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort Review : Overall this is a tremendous game from an indie dev, it is short but the dialogs really adds to the replayability, it is flawed notably enemies bashes (that mechanics needs to go man) and on its own the game isn't finished (storywise i mean, the game doesn't have bugs by the way) it needs a sequel and i can't wait but with polish and more content, this franchise has potential. Also tremendous music and art style

Long Review:

Great game, although throughout the whole game i kept comparing it to hotline miami because it heavely took its inspiration there. It is the same kind of game even though it's not the same camera and the gameplay widely differs because your character uses katana and throwable items you can find on the through the level and you can slow time and it is heavely linked to the plot (Nicely done not much game try to do that anymore).

The story unlike hotline miami 1 doesn't propose a real end but an opening for the next game in the franchise, i'm fine with it but it bugs me a little bit. I wish you could avoid killing "innocent" cops, there's just people doing their job but to proceed to next levels you have to kill everyone.

This game is done rather quickly it took me 4 and a half hour, and after finishing it i wanted to do another run to unlock everything like these kind of games usually do, they compensate small time play with replayability but the last levels of the game are really really hard, not as hard as i would get stuck on it for 3 hours but the kind of hard that gets tiring real quick and because of those level which are a bit dumbed down to die and try.

I am not a big fan of this as i prefer some level that require problem solving or finding a boss weakness but jesus the last boss and the one controlling the room or the bike level is what's keeping me from doing it again.

Even though i'm not a fan of the end game i still really liked the dialogues, at the beginning of the game i pissed off the guy on the phone to see what would happen and the result was funny. The option to talk is a huge plus compared to hotline miami and gives another reason to understand fully the whole picture as the game is very vague at the beginning but just like a movie with a plotwist during a new game you can pick up foreshadowing and understand why something specific happened etc.

Toward the end i still was trying to block with mouse2 like an idiot because of other game reflexes haha, and by the end i still got fair bit of misinput and this is why this game absolutely need a sequel.

The Music is great, it's what got me to play this game i knew after listening to Tunç Çakır - Blue Room (feat. LudoWic) i knew i had to play this game.

The Artistic Direction is well done it's pixel art but the colours are not picked blindly there's thinking behind it, the picture is pleasing

P.S: I already said that the game includes the game mechanics in dialogues and in the story but it also clearly include the fact that you are dying and coming back to restart a level, with your enemies saying remarks about it or giving new dialog! And this is pretty brilliant\t\t\t\t\t
