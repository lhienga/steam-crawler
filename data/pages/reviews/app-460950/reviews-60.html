It's a problem because what sets this above many of the other Hotline Miami inspired games is the story. But ultimate it ends up going nowhere with no resolution because the game just ends.

The other reason I lean towards "no" is the gameplay has just enough things that are unsatisfying. You see all there is to offer in terms of gameplay reasonably early in the game. You've seen all the enemy types and the general kind of level designs. The gameplay leans a little bit more in the Dragon's Lair "memorise the events and twitch respond" direction than I prefer. There are a lot of corridors here and often only 1 or 2 ways to get through them. The way it handles death along with the pixel art occasionally makes it hard to tell exactly what you did wrong. I would have liked to see the option for a slow motion replay. How exactly did I pull those enemies from the second floor that time? A lot of the later levels increase the difficulty by just making the level longer, so a mistake on the 26th enemy means death and resetting to the beginning. I would have preferred if difficulty increased in some other way, since it often got tedious to redo the first 2/3rd of a level 20+ times.

Don't get me wrong, I mostly enjoyed the game. I think if the story had a better ending...or any kind of ending at all, really, it would tip me towards a "yes" despite the shortcomings in the gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
