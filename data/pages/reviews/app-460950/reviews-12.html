\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA captivating storyline with choices that have (at least some) repercussions down the line. An intriguing setting. Good spritework and smooth pixel animations. New mechanics or gameplay ideas on each level. Great soundtrack. Well-written and well-presented dialogue. Perfect blend of drama, gore, sweetness and comedy (... that moment when you finally get a gun...).

However, after playing through the whole ordeal:

- I absolutely despised the overall gameplay: Katana Zero is not a proper insta-gib shooter (say, like Hotline Miami), because there mostly are just one or two correct ways to clear each room. For a puzzle game, on the other hand, Katana Zero is too fast (so you don't usually understand where your mistake was) and too unpredictable (because enemies move differently on each run). The levels are very short, but it still isn't fun to restart the same thirty-second-segment over and over again until you randomly make it through.

- You can and will be shot dead from off-screen enemies.

- Controls are either too imprecise or too stiff: Movement is floaty and hard to control, attacks and dodges with their forced timeouts just don't feel fluid, and jumping up platforms is excessively unreliable. The last one, in particular, is annoying because you can safely stab enemies by jumping up from below, but if you accidentially climb onto their platform, it's restart time.

- In contrast to the spritework, the level and background art varies greatly in quality and style --- as if the game had no art direction at all.

- The core game is too short. I have 9 hours of playtime on account, but that includes quite some idling in the background and a second playthrough for secrets and alternative dialog options.

- Somewhat related to the above aspect: Half-way through the story, the game just abruptly ends. I wouldn't even call this a cliffhanger, because there is no tense situation that requires immediate resolution. It's rather that all open plot threads are left dangling without any closure at all. The ending makes the game feel unfinished.\t\t\t\t\t
