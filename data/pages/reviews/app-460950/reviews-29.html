\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's fantastic. It takes the Hotline Miami idea, switches it to a 2d hack n' slash, gives it an interesting context and killer style. Some may take issue with the short runtime (less than 4 hours in my case) but I tend to prefer short focused games that leave me wanting to replay them. I've already done 3 playthroughs at the time of writing (my 1st, one for all the secrets I missed, and one with the glorious April Fools update)

The gameplay loop never stops being satisfying. The on-command bullet time is a great way of maintaining the fast pace without overwhelming you, and the quick restarts lessen the frustration of many deaths. The story is also good*.

The few gameplay issues I have are minor, but them being addressed would elevate the game even higher:
1) Enemy spawns aren't set, so in every attempt the same guy can be at opposite ends of the room. Just having the enemies on a global cycle that resets on death would fix it, making every attempt have consistent enemy placement.
2) The final few levels are big. As in "I just got shot from off-screen" big. Either limiting the levels to the size of screen, or giving us the option to zoom out the camera and keep it there, would fix the annoyance.

*I said the story was good, that's because the few character moments we get and the overall presentation are good, even great. The mystery it creates regarding Zero, the war, and the girl are all very interesting. But... None of those have concrete answers yet, so they're hinging on future reveals to make sense of it all. Hence the asterisk.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKatana ZERO is a fantastic game well worth the $15 price tag and an absolute must buy if ever on sale.
When starting early in the game you might take things a little slow, but as the game progresses you'll pick up the pace and eventually never want to use the Chronos mechanic during your runs. Yes this game can be beaten in one sitting but you'll want to do run after run after run. Music is phenomenal and has made it to my playlists for late night driving, you know the Blade Runner 2049 and Drive aesthetic. The story is great (nothing ground breaking though) and by the end you'll want to go through every possible interaction the game offers (absolutely flirt with the receptionist). Oh and mess with the therapist as much as you can ;)

My one and only complaint is that the DLC has taken its SWEET time and I DESPERATELY want to see what's in store.\t\t\t\t\t
