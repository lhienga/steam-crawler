\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKatana Zero is freakin' addicting, man. This dystopian action platformer mixes difficulty, and and reward extremely well, due to the almost instant retry speed when you fail. Because of that, the game never feels frustrating, barring one forced (optional) stealth sequence that fits in as smoothly as a cactus in the rectum. It feels very... Hotline Miami in that sense, where you can always just throw everything at the wall and something will eventually stick. However, there is a lot more opportunity to plan your play out in KZ than in HM.

I do have a gripe, and that is that you can't... idk, really skip the story parts on subsequent playthroughs. There is a speedrun mode, for sure! However, there is a story specific part of the game that can only be unlocked via specific choices in the story mode. So, if you didn't get it on your first run, you can't unlock it in the speedrun mode. You have to deal with the entire story, as the secret is at the very end. That's not to say the story is bad, or not fun to explore. But it's so omnipresent when I just want to hack up some dudes and find more routes/unlockables.

The control is crisp as hell, and movement feels really good to execute. Pressing up to jump feels really weird at first, but now it feels more natural than space bar. I tried to go back to a similar sort of game, My Friend Pedro, and found myself pressing up all the time expecting to rocket off the ground.

I hear there was some DLC planned, and a the game has such a sequel hook on it, you could get it lodged in your lip like a sturgeon. But so far... nothing else has materialized for that, at time of writing. I had so much fun playing this, and routinely come back to it, but since the levels are preset, I don't have anything new to do in it. Game is dying for an endless mode, or a procedural floor sort of run mode.

Here, you can watch me hack, slash, and joke my way through the base story on Overlord Arcade!
