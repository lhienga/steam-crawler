\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an absolute slog to play. The beautiful pixel-graphics lure you in, keeping you engaged for the entirety of around 30 minutes. After the initial intrigue has waned, there's nothing about Katana ZERO that leaves you entertained. This game is simply boring. Each level involves the exact same set of mechanics: i) slow down time, ii) right click to cut up the bad-guy, and iii) throw a beer bottle at a foe to either down or kill them. That's about it. As for the story, there is none. Okay, fine, there is a story; but, it's so vague and unclear, that even a stickler for reading character dialogue meticulously like myself will get lost in its intricacies. This isn't the type of story that intentionally starts out vague, only to lead you to an 'ah-ha'-like moment of realization which puts all the pieces together. In fact, it feels like the exact opposite. The more you play, the more you realize that your current understanding of what has transpired is incorrect.

I have no doubt that the developer of this game is very talented. My personal opinion is that the game just simply is not fun to play.\t\t\t\t\t
