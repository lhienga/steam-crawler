\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't often post reviews but honestly this game really blew me away. I'll keep it short and sweet. The gameplay loop is addicting with things constantly getting switched up at the different bars so you're always kept on your toes. Being able to switch seamlessly between difficulty levels is a wonderful addition considering how challenging the night shifts are. The storytelling is incredibly relatable, which is a surprising treat for me personally as I don't feel that way about games often. The music is ridiculously catchy. Probably my only complaint is that I want more levels to play, even though I was pleasantly surprised by the length of the game already. I'm already intending to start on my second playthrough right after I finish writing this, so if you're teetering on the edge of whether or not to buy this, let this review convince you to do so.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first saw this game on one of Steam's NextFest in 2023. I've played the demo and surprisingly I found this seemingly-simple game to be very enjoyable and fun to play! It has been a long time since I was hyped up to a game and the official release of Pixel Cafe delivered way more than I expected.

This game has an amazing and addictive gameplay, nice story, appealing pixel graphics, amazing and relaxing soundtrack that I still play and hear, and a difficulty slider where you can make this game relaxing or challenging. The game starts off simple but it gets more complicated later on by managing more mechanics. There are also optional nightmare levels which are way harder compared to the regular day levels but still satisfying to beat. I also really loved the soundtrack of this game because it is so relaxing, nice to hear, and catchy, even if suddenly there are customers everywhere that you have to serve and to rush in making food. I still play and hear Pixel Cafe's soundtrack after I've finished the game because it's simply too good.

Overall, I highly recommend playing this game even at full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing nonstop for two weeks, finally completed the game in the Master Barista mode, so now I can finally write my thought about it.

I am saw the ad I Reddit and decided to try the demo and I got so in love with the concept that I bought it as soon as I could. I enjoyed a lot the story, the pixel art and the gameplay. It’s a cool game, pretty chill at the beginning, most levels are not way too difficult —even in the Master Barista mode—but some of them are hard, I mean, like REALLY hard (they’re called “nightmare” for a reason.)

Recently they added three difficulty modes, so the experience for people who just want to enjoy the game should be a lot better.

TL;DR: Just but the game. You won’t regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just can't remember when was the last time I loved a game that much.

For the most part the story was grounded, realistc, and simple as a woman trying to figure out what to do with her life. We all can relate do Pixel. I would say most of us have this feeling of being talentless, of not being really good at anything. We all have different kind of employers, each one with its problems and qualities, and Pixel face situations with them that we probably also faced at some point in our lives. At times I reflected on my own life watching the protagonist's journey. I thought about the people we love, the people who help us to grow up and made us who we are. Everybody has these people in their lives, and so did Pixie. That's what is so amazing about the story: it was our very own. Pixel Cafe brings us a story that could be that of any of us: a person looking for the meaning of their life, who doesn't think they're good at anything, but still tries to do the most with what they have.

Also in terms of gameplay, the Pixel Cafe is wonderful. I played with the joystick and was able to complete 100% of the game. It takes time to learn the controls and mechanics, but over time it was possible to pass most of the stages with plenty of time to spare. It's difficult in the right way: easy to learn, difficult to master. Nightmare Mode was really challenging but the big secret is to memorize the orders as much as possible, the rest is execution.

The game's soundtrack is simply perfect. The Pixel motif will be stuck in my head for a long, long time. Of the more than 50 hours of gameplay, I think at least three were spent on the porch listening to the songs and petting Rambo. Each level has its own theme track, and they are all very good (although I admit that sometimes I needed to turn down the music volume to better hear the noise of the meals being prepared and thus be able to pass some of the Nightmare Levels).

But now that I've done 100% in the game, I have this weird void, because I really wish there were, I don't know, different game modes? For example, an infinite mode, where I could play random stages, which mixed the different game mechanics. I hope the game is successful and expanded in the future. Thank you to everyone involved in the development of Pixel Cafe. In an industry that is unfortunately increasingly greedy, it is comforting to see that there are still games made with passion, willing to tell a good story and provide challenging but rewarding gameplay.

TL;DR: buy this game now!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you love the early diner flash games of the 2000s like I do then it's the game for you. If not then wait for it on sale and get it if you love the demo. Be forewarned that it doesn't have the complicated orders that some of the harder flash games had. There's a bit of a challenge with the nightmare levels but there's no endless mode for any of the restaurants.

Anyways, I dislike the house upgrades because I couldn't figure out what you could upgrade.There's no visual indicator for some of it. So, I had to trial and error my way through it to figure out what you could upgrade. Also, the game would insist that I wanted to buy wallpaper when I meant to upgrade something because I didn't click perfectly on it. I have no idea if this is an issue with the gamepad controls but it is if you're using a mouse.

The controls are fine if you're using a mouse for gameplay. Just a lot of clicking for some of the drink/food prep. Ought to be streamlined for some of it as in just use one mouse click for entire machine instead of clicking twice for each drink.

I really don't like the frosted glass/whatever it is mechanic. It's a pain in the ass to use when you're trying to get through a large order of them. Nor do I care for the whole need to have a skill upgrade to get through the nightmare level bits but that's on me.

The plot is fine I guess. I haven't finished the game but I was pleasantly surprised by more of the world unlocking after the credits fake out.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe dark souls of cooking management games.

In all seriousness though, this game is TOUGH. It starts off pretty simple and relaxing at first but after an hour or so you'll find yourself juggling between 3 different counters, 15 different recipes, and hoping that you fulfill everyone's orders in a timely fashion without trashing any food. And that's just on day levels. Night levels are way more hectic.

And I'm only at the SECOND restaurant!

This game has a wonderful artstyle, a relaxing ost, great characters, and very fun and challenging gameplay. I've been looking for a difficult cooking/restaurant style game and this scratches that itch. Definitely a "just one more" kind of game.

I'll be playing this a lot more.\t\t\t\t\t
