\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI highly recommend this game to any time and food management games enjoyer, such as Diner Dash or Cook, Serve, Delicious!. However, be prepared for some challenging moments, particularly on the "night" levels, if you are aiming for a Perfect Game (this having played on Standard difficulty). Despite having played a lot of other time management games, I was pleasantly surprised by the level of stress this game can induce, given its cozy and relaxing atmosphere. While I thoroughly enjoyed it, it's worth noting for those seeking a cozier gaming experience (there's also an easier difficulty option available).

The game features a modest narrative to accompany you through the levels, which, while enjoyable and endearing, lacks depth and has a repetitive nature, so it wouldn't be the sole reason to pick up the game.

Its strengths lie in its delightful music and charming sprites, with a variety of tracks and colorful pixel art designs that perfectly complement the atmosphere of each level.

The food assembly mini-games are generally straightforward and enjoyable, with one exception: the "sandwich" assembly. The pixel art during stacking can be challenging to decipher, and it would benefit from a counter upgrade to streamline the process. Additionally, unlike other mini-games that offer slot expansions (which I didn't have a use for most of the time), the lack of such an option for the cutting boards in this particular mini-game feels like an oversight, given its many components. While this minor issue didn't detract from my overall enjoyment, there's certainly room for improvement.

In summary, if you're a fan of time and food management games, this one is definitely worth trying out. With its great music, charming sprites, and enjoyable food assembly mini-games, it offers a great experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn awesome game. The gameplay is exactly what I was looking for. BEWARE, however, IF YOU WANT 100%, it is not relaxing, but fast-paced with punishing timers, if you want to serve all the customers or get all the cups.
It is varied enough that it did not get boring, especially if played as it was likely intended, over a longer period of time rather than all at once.

The story is relatable, with just enough video game nonsense (like zombies) to serve as breaks. The protagonist is decent and the ending is predictable but worth it. There are also other elements besides the management, progression and such, a nice touch which I leave to you to discover.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a fun game, it made me nostalgic for the old games i used to play as a child! While i don't really care much about our protag's story (as it feels too real HAHA sorry), i still enjoyed it!

There are some aspects that could be better. Around the 10th hour mark it begins to feel incredibly repetitive, and the difficulty progression is a little messed up & non-linear; it starts off incredibly difficult with the first few restaurants (with complex multi step orders and other handyman fixes all at the same time), and then drastically becomes a lot easier in the later stages, at time i just catch myself waiting for customers despite my counters being clear. It's become a bit difficult to get that 100% completion for some restaurant areas cus it just takes so long.

but based on their latest update (Dec 2023) and the discussion boards, the devs seem very responsive to concerns, so that's nice!

I do recommend you to try the demo at the very least, and see if its a game you'll enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t
