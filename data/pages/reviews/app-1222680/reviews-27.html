\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll right, I'll tell ya the good and the bad stuff about this game.
The Good:

Return to Form: Heat borrows ingredients from fan-favorite titles like Underground and the original Most Wanted. It’s a back-to-basics approach with a fresh twist. The result? A racer that feels faithful to the series’ roots.
Deep Customization: Say goodbye to the dreaded free-to-play lottery system. Heat lets you customize your ride without any nonsense. Body mods, performance upgrades—everything’s fair game.
Hectic Cop Chases: Remember the thrill of outrunning the fuzz in Most Wanted? Heat brings that back. The streets are alive with flashing lights, and even stone walls crumble when you veer off course.
Miami-Inspired Map: Palm City is our playground, and it’s neon-drenched and vibrant. Sure, the surrounding countryside lacks memorability, but the city itself? A heady mix of sun-soaked streets and adrenaline-fueled races.
The Bad:

Story: Heat’s story plays out like an off-brand Fast & Furious. It’s not terrible, but it’s no blockbuster either. Fortunately, the writing is more restrained than the cringeworthy Payback.

Online Mode Clunkiness: While the single-player experience shines, the online mode stumbles a bit. It’s not as seamless as we’d like, but hey, the streets are where the real action happens anyway.

Note: I got this game when it was on sale 95% off ($3.49), so if there is another sale like that one, I extremely recommend getting NFS Heat!
