\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts fun at first, but gets old fast.

The game launches through the EA app thingy, takes ages to open, login, etc. It even takes a while to close the game properly.

The gameplay is incredibly fun at first, but when different systems are introduced you realize they were not made for you to have fun with, they exist to slow your progression.

Heat is just a level gate, so you need to do stuff you don't want to before you can go back to having fun.

Police shows up multiple times in pretty much every race, mainly at the end, and also at the end of main questline missions, so you need to evade them before continuing to play the game. Police also sticks to you like never before, your best (and fastest) bet to evade them without googling it is to jump into a body of water, either that or alt f4, I caught myself alt f4-ing more and more frequently as the game went on, closing the game and opening up again is faster and more fun than escaping the police for the 200th time. If you manage to play at night for 20 minutes in a row, you'll need to drive back to the warehouse and watch 2 loading screens to cash in your exp and reset the heat, to then be able to go back having fun (maybe they intended to have ads on loading screens, that would explain why so many).

The best time I had with the game was repeating the same drift minigame for a couple of hours to get enough exp to buy fun stuff. I did this because you could quickly go back to the warehouse before the police shows up

The driving mechanics seem to get worse as cars get faster, the slidy drifty gameplay with slow turning does not scale well with the absurd accel and speed you get.


This one is obviously subjective, but the music is kind of bad. What isn't subjective is the rage when you're driving and vibing and a cop shows up, you then get stuck in the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ suspense music for 10 minutes.\t\t\t\t\t
