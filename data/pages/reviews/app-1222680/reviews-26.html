\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNFS Heat gives the closest resemblance to the quality of the NFS golden era games - Need for Speed Underground 2 and Most Wanted. There are also new ideas and features here that are exciting. The day/night feature is thoroughly enjoyable and there's an inherent and unique purpose to both.

Where the game shines in customisation, car library selection, and the gameplay loop racing for money and shiny new vehicle parts, the drawback is the rep level cap at 50. I felt disappointed after 10-15 hours that I had reached the end of the game, even without trying out some of the other types of experiences like tuning a car for offroad events. But once the XP bar stops moving, and unlocks begin to fade, the excitement really dwindles which is a shame. Make the max level cap to 100 rep, not 50.

Nevertheless, this is a positive review, and I thoroughly enjoyed playing. The music wasn't quite my taste, so I played my own racing music in the background which multiplied my enjoyment ten-fold. I recommend.

I give this game 9 lazy daytime cops out of 10 steroid-chad nighttime cops.\t\t\t\t\t
