\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not really sure what I expected. It was practically free so I tried it. Driving around and racing is really satisfying. Unfortunately, as is the problem with so many modern games these days, little effort went into balancing the progression or the difficulty.

As the game progresses and you unlock more high-end parts/vehicles, they naturally get more expensive. In theory, you should also be making more money, but you don't. You consistently make LESS money as you get further, AND things get MORE expensive. You unlock longer races with slightly larger payouts than the initial races which where shorter with slightly lower payouts. Since you need so much cash, and there are only so many races, you end up repeating them. The shorter early races make money significantly faster than the longer races with slightly higher payouts.

Night-time heat and the police pursuits are terribly unbalanced. The player is driving a 500,000 super car with top of the line parts and the police force somehow manages to have dozens of cruisers that are 5x heavier, 5x more durable, twice as fast, and handle 5x better. There was a complete inability to design an engaging, enjoyable, and challenging pursuit system. They employ perfect RADAR that somehow penetrates multiple layers of concrete structures and mountains. They can accelerate from 0-100 in a second, and the inverse, while the player isn't even close to hitting a 0-60 in 3 seconds. They will ram you head on with no regard for their lives. It's completely broken.

The races are fun. Driving around is fun. Grinding the same races for dozens of hours to get the cash need to unlock and upgrade new vehicles is not fun. Police crashing into you, head on and repeatedly, with absolutely no consequences and it being impossible to escape from them is not fun.\t\t\t\t\t
