The Need For Speed: Heat
DON'T buy this game for the full price, it goes 95% off.
I played the game on a G923, haven't tried with a controller.


Day/Night
Let's start with day. Day is legal, no traffic in the races, and cops only chase you if you crash into them. Day earns Bank (basically like credits). Day isn't much different from other racing games. Night is illegal, with traffic and cops. The attention you get from the cops is dependent on the amount of heat you have. Heat increases when competing in races. Heat also has an interesting effect; it multiplies the amount of REP (reputation) you gain, and since heat can go up to 5, you can turn 200 000 REP into 1 000 000 REP. It makes that one part of your brain always want to continue doing events for even more rewards. Races at night end without stopping for podium animations, and instead you drive away since you don't want to get caught.
Now, the cops. The cops in Heat are good, but there is a catch. The police is very hard to get away in the beginning, but at the end when you have beaten the story, you can get away from the cops a bit too easily for my liking. This happens because the cops don't get faster while you do get faster, and a lot faster.  The cops are slower at lower heat levels, but after reaching heat 5, they don't get any faster.

The story
Heat's story is not good. The beginning is okay in my opinion, but the story ends out of nowhere. It feels like there was supposed to be some sort of twist that would make the story go for some more time, but it just isn't. It isn't a good ending, and it just leaves me thinking: Why would I want to play this game for any more time? Even after the story, the cops are still the same, the rewards are still the same, the loop is still the same.

The handling
With a steering wheel (G923), Heat feels good to drive and especially good to drift with. I don't really have anything else to say. One thing I want to say though, is that at least for me, I couldn't edit the bindings for different things and so I was forced to use bindings that didn't recognize my wheel correctly. I somewhat fixed this with G hub but it should have been in Heat.

Variety
Heat has few race types: there's street and off-road. There is also the drift events. This might sound like it will get repetitive, and it does, at day. Day doesn't have some sort of thing you need to watch out for while racing, so it just becomes boring. But you still have to do the day events to get Bank. Night, on the other hand, for me, didn't get repetitive, since you have to watch out for traffic and cops are a thing.

Conclusion
Heat is an okay-quality game. It's fine to drive with and fine to customize, but the story just falls over and fails. If you want a good story, it's not here. If you want cop chases, then this is probably the game for you. I would give this game a 7/10 .
