\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game for Rs. 175, which is like 2.1 USD and suffice to say, I'm totally satisfied with what I received.

-> It's got an adequate number of great cars of multiple variations. Customisation is top notch. You can even customise the hyper cars, which is something that I haven't seen in much arcade racers. The upgrade options in game are also quite fantastic- from upgrading your tires and brakes to literally swapping engines, this game does not disappoint in terms of vehicle tinkering.
-> The open-world in this game, while not the best in any way or form, is still quite good and entertaining to explore. It can be bland at times, but at others, it's breathtaking.
-> The story is mediocre, but let's be real here- who on earth buys a NFS game for its story? The story here is cringe, uninteresting, with mediocre characters, that come off as too cheesy and off-putting. Even Payback had a better story. Nut since I'm really not here for the story, I'm willing to overlook.
-> Graphically speaking, this game isn't outstanding or anything, it's not mediocre either. It looks fantastic most of the time, but if you have an eye for the tiniest of details (which again, is not something that most "normal" people would do in an arcade racer), you might get a bit disappointed.
-> In terms of the gameplay tho, it is peakkkkkkkkkkkkkkk. During day time, it's all happy go lucky- you have lots of normal activities to do, like participating in races, drifts, off-road races, etc. But it's during the night time, when the real fun begins. Cop chases are better than ever and it's back to the Rivals system of adding multipliers, relative to how long you survive out in the open world, with fast cop chases that are quite daunting initially, with slower cars. But as you progress and get access to the supercars and the hypercars, it becomes a little less challenging, but it's still fun and highly entertaining when you are running for your life, driving down the highway at 350+ kmph with the Aventador SVJ, at max throttle with a million rep points on hand, and chasing after you is a gang of hypercars and rhinos.
-> The sense of speed in this game is also quite amazing, especially at higher graphics settings, with Motion Blur turned on, the cars feel blistering fast, to the point that the mini map starts to shrink, like literally. It's exaggerated but it sure is fun.

The End.\t\t\t\t\t
