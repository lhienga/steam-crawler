\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPrefacing this as someone who loves cars and has never really touched a Need For Speed game.

I really feel like the developers have little knowledge of how cars or racing actually works. I know this isn't a racing sim, but I still expect some realistic behaviors when driving a car even in an arcade racing game. Engine/exhaust sounds are all over the place. You'll get a supercharged sound with a turbo'd car. Some AWD cars handle worse than RWD cars. For a game about street racing it's missing some amazing street racing cars. It's also missing some amazing off roading vehicles.

The Day/Night cycle is visually appealing, but earning rep at night and money at day feels horrible. If I'm winning sanction day races my reputation would rise. I shouldn't just have to race illegally to earn a reputation.

This turns to my biggest hate for this game. The cops. I don't know how anyone could find it fun to be rammed to death by cops as part of the game play. I have to deal with the cops to earn rep and I dread going out at night just because of frustrating that game play loop is.

Luckily I got this on sale for real cheap($3ish), which is the only way I would recommend anyone buying this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis a tepid thumbs up. NFS Heat is a decent arcade runner held back by how the rubberband is built into the open world. The game basically should be called NFS Participation Trophy. I've never played a racer where my goal has consistently been to get in 2nd place. The rubberbanding AI is terrible (both because it is too perfect and how it hilariously eases up the moment you begin to struggle) and the game gives you significant rewards no matter how poorly you do.

The result is that none of your actions in the game really matter or have consequences. Race poorly in the day? That's fine, you'll still roughly a 50% payout even if you finish last to grind out new car parts. Race poorly in the night and get busted? That's fine, you'll still get a decent rep payout and Lucas will tell all these fine news cars you've unlocked. What happened to Most Wanted 08 when you risked losing your car to repeated arrests?

In the end most of my playthroughs are like this. Pick the race closest to the garage. Finish 2nd to the rubberband AI. Next, pick a race I'm underleveled for. Cruise to victory because the rubberband is now set to let win mode. If day, grind races till I have enough money. If night, grind races till I'm busted (sometimes you esacpe but again has more to do with the rubberband setting). Starr a new day and repeat, usually winning alittle more or alittle less depending on how the AI adjusts the rubberband. You don't have to win in this game so long as you keep playing\t\t\t\t\t
