\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary:

- Extremely satisfying racing game
- The integration between oficial and underground races is very well made
- A general out of touch ESG aestetic and story
- Very shallow story which breaks down if you think about it too much

Explanation:

The game progression

The progression is mechanically very satisfying, however, overall very weird. The gameplay loop is that you race in the day in official races to get money, and you race in the night in underground races to get reputation. Reputation unlocks better cars and parts, which you can buy with money.

There is no explanation given why you need reputation in order to buy parts, and it seems like a heavy handed way to force you into underground races. The game has its whole narrative of 2 brothers, one who pushes you into oficial races and the other who pushes you into underground racing. However, you can't really chose to go one way or another since reputation is worth nothing without money, and money is worth nothing without reputation.

The night gameplay is very interesting as you need to weigh risk and benefit. The more you risk, the more reputation you get exponentially. However, unlike NFS most wanted where you had clear ways to get rid of the police, all you can do on this game is shove your car into the police and destroy their cars while destroying yours, which is a valid strategy until there reinforcements behind you. The police cars don't seem to follow the general game phisics and seem to be able to accelerate, make turns without restriction. You might be going at absurd speeds, the cop will simply zip past you without any effort and position himself in front of you with the goal of boxing you.

This reality breaking police is specially a problem since they are not completely avoidable. You can see the police in the map, but sometimes they show up during the race, in which case you can do nothing about it. If you don't destroy the police car in the first second, they will start calling reinforcements infinetely untill you are arrested.

The game is generally easy, specially for someone who played racing games in the past, however anything involving the police spikes the dificulty from easy to impossible and unfair. The parts specific for police chases don't seem to do anything since police chases are often impossible. Nothing in the game teaches you anything about how to deal with the police.

The game also has some particularly annoying things such as

- You can't just buy a part and have it available to all your cars, You need to annoyingly remove from one to place it into another, or buy multiple parts. This is specially annoying since you need multiple cars for drifting, off road and normal road.
- You are not able to sell your useles outdated parts, so you need to pay full price for everything you buy.
- The car customization is locked at the beguining of the game, and it is not clear why. You tend to just try progressing through the game to try unlocking it, and then in the middle of the game you randomly discovers that you can only customize cars bought in the store for some reason.
- The customization is overall confusing, however the "import customization from the web" feature is an amazing one.
- There is a strangely low number of underglow and nitro boost color options.
- The sound customization is nice but overall confusing since you can't hear what you are selecting. It is hard to tell what you are even selecting about.

The mechanics and progression are overall very solid, with some low points wich are not enough for me to not recommend it.

ESG slop

The game seems very ESG oriented. It tries very hard to pain the aestetic of a battle between the oppressed minorities and the corrupt white cops. All of the antagonists are white male cops and everyone else is not, including the player character, which, instead of being created in a character editor, are selected from a big array of minorities to chose from. And yes the game is set in the US, as there are always US flags present around the antagonists.

This clash between white male cops and everyone else is specialy out of touch since the playable character and many of his allies are criminals, so it ends up creating a "US is being swarmed by criminal aliens" narrative accidentally, which is very funny to me.

Additionally, the game seems to actively prevent any woman from being too good looking. All female characters range from extremely ugly to "not ugly"/normal. At lease you can select one of the female characters to play as and replace the ugly clothes and hair style with good looking ones.

The story is a simple one, corrupt cops are extorting underground racers. A narrative like this would work very well if they made it clear to the player that the racers are not harming anyone and they are just defying unjust and oppressive laws. However, the game makes it clear instead that the racers are causing a lot of property damage to the city, which ends up justifying their persecution. So the fact that some cops are profiting from extorting the racers falls completely in its face since in the worst case scenario, the cops would be stealing from their own department and benefiting the racers in exchange of money.

This is particularly noticeable since nearly everything in the game is phisics wise made of paper except the buildings themselves, so you are heavily encouraged to cut corners by trampling over everything. The game even rewards you for destroying billboards by ramming your car on them. So instead of having a night gameplay focused on victimless crimes and defying illegitimate authorities, it is about random criminal acts and general lawless behavior for the fun of it.

The main story involves two brothers of different ethnicities, which either implies they are addopted, half brothers or that the game just randomly selects a non white ethnicity for their characters. One brother pushes you into official races, and the other one pushes you into underground races. However, the underground racer when discussing with her brother only make arguments in favor of racing in general, and not specifically racing illegally, so the whole narrative becomes a very strange one. Why does she need to race illegally? No explanation given besides she likes racing.

The music selection seems to also fall in line with the minority aestetic, wich a large array of songs from latin america. Some of the Brazilian songs are quite popular here in Brazil, and are heavily associated with Brazilian marginalized communities, and I'm glad they avoided including the nasty and annoying ones.

Overall this general inclusive aestetic feels there just to earn some DEI and ESG points and not a genuine advocacy for minorities and social comentary against police corruption. It is a lefty looking shell without any meat inside it. While this ESG slop is detrimental to the game, it is not enough to make me not recommend it, as it is just an aestetic thing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNEED FOR SPEED HEAT is a really fun racing game. I have personally haven't returned to this franchise after mostwanted(2005) but i am glad to return with this title. The game does not focus much on realism in driving which made me hesitant playing at first cause i wanted some realism like steering, weight of vehicle etc. But the game really shines in it core gameplay mechanics. I have loved the day and night cycle system. The police chases at night are very fun and test your quick thinking ability and reflexes. The police aggressive and strongly reminded me of mostwanted(2005). The customization is top notch you can change every last part of your car including sound system. The graphics are amazing and so is the performance. I am not sure if its constant 60 fps but definitely a consistent fps which feels like 60fps . I have used a medium spec build , therefore results may vary.

Speaking about the cons the map feels small because while travelling at high speeds the huge part of the map is explored which may feel repetitive. The game has a problem with its online servers but we can still play solo.

OVERALL NEED FOR SPEED HEAT IS 8/10
