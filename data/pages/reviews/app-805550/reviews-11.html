\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game. I came over from F1 23 and I was blown away by the realistic mechanics, visuals, and playability. I came into sim racing as a new motorsport fan. So, I learned everything from scratch. There is a learning curve in learning how to drive a car, but there are plenty of videos to sort of guide you through the basics. The rating system, though imperfect as it is, is a great guide to help you learn the car. There is also a car for you, a front-engine (Mercedes) mid-engine, McLaren, or the (near) rear-engine Porsche. The AI is more than challenging for anyone new to sim racing. If SP is not for you, you need the MP experience. The public lobby races can indeed be a wreck fest of inexperienced learners. However, there are a lot of alternatives to MP. There is a group for you out there.
There are also several different graphics setups that can help make the game enjoyable. This is featured in the video. You can drive as one of your favorite GT teams or create your livery. Another down note is the career mode. It has not been updated since launch, so you drive cars a couple of generations old (e.g. Mclaren 650s instead of the Mclaren 720s Evo.). However, you can set up your own championship by choosing your tracks and car of choice.

As a new Motorsport fan and new to sim racing, this game has been a source of frustration and happiness whenever I hit a great lap time. I wouldn't recommend any game at full price given the number of sales on Steam. However, this game is an absolute steal on discount.\t\t\t\t\t
