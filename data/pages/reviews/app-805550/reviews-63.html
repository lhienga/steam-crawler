\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is the real deal.

Imo AC has one of the best physics to date, and ACC continues that trend. What I really think benefits ACC is that it doesn't overextend itself with a massive selection. The development was focused to GT cars rather than having to adhere to multiple classes such as open wheel, rally, sports cars, etc. What this resulted in was a very disciplined sim, becoming a FAR more complete package than AC.

The UI feels like a real game this time, multiplayer functions are easy to understand, singleplayer is viable, and the AI isn't hopeless. The immersion is even better, cars look fantastic and sound impeccable. The tracks are beautiful and diverse. The game helps you understand the philosophy of racing with it's rating system, and creates conditions just consistent enough for you to notice your short comings and where to improve. Out of all the sim games out there, this is the one that I like to actually race championships in, shave time off of lap times, and experiment with setups, other than maybe Dirt Rally.

It really is a fantastic motor racing game, really the top of it's field in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t
