\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn my personal opinion. This game is the epitome of sim racers for GT series racing. IE: GT4/GT3/GT2 (although GT2 isn't really a thing anymore). But if you are looking for an authentic feel in GT series racing, this is the sim for you. I play a wide arrangement of 'racing sim' style games. From Forza Motorsport, to the original Assetto Corsa, to ACC, to iRacing. I even do rally with games such as Dirt Rally 2.0 and Rally Sim Fan's Richard Burns' Rally. For GT3 racing, ACC is king. Even compared to iRacing. The brakes are realistic, the tire model is realistic, and the cars handling is realistic. This feels like driving an actual car, as it should.

The only drawback I can find to this game over something like iRacing is the damage model in ACC is a lot more forgiving. Tapping the wall in ACC won't force you to limp the car around the track to the pits, or have to tow in, whereas in iRacing, it's a bit more realistic in that aspect. Hitting the wall would do detrimental damage to your vehicle, and in iRacing, it does. So this game does have a little bit more forgiveness when it comes to incidents on the track. The audio is also much more immersive in ACC than other sims, even getting to the point that driving through closed in sections of the track will cause the audio of the car to echo back onto you. It's nice. And the game is beautiful.

So, would I recommend this game? Yes, for GT3 racing, this is the top dog. For everything else, I would say get iRacing (especially if you are into dirt oval racing... iRacing does that SOOO WELL!).

So, yeah... 10/10, would review again.\t\t\t\t\t
