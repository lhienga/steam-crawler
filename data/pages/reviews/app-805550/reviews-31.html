\r\n\t\t\t\t\t02/16/24 Update: Played iRacing for a few months and cancelled my subscription after becoming annoyed by their safety rating system. I'm pretty capable of running clean races but found myself constantly being penalized after being struck by others. After browsing the iRacing forums, I found most people who publish similar frustrations are usually attacked for being "bad" drivers and causing their own accidents. I'm well capable of taking P1 and have done so on numerous occasions during my iRacing "career." I became tired of the hostile environment surrounding discussion of the SR system and even more tired of having my license penalized for actions beyond my control. I think iRacing's strength lies in that it does an outstanding stand up job of organizing race events. The cellphone app is handy for someone like me who has limited time to burn my life away in front of a computer screen and would like to be able to plan to start a race at a specific time. I'm aware of Low Fuel Motorsports for ACC, but I haven't yet acquired the necessary SA to get my LFM license here. Also, as far as I'm aware, LFM doesn't have an app for me to browse my dorky virtual racing career on my phone.

For a while I'd already known I liked ACC but felt it didn't have the same depth as iRacing (in terms of physics and handling). After I cancelled my iRacing subscription I spent more time with ACC. Well it turns out I just knew nothing of real world GT3 cars or the assist systems they use. After learning how to adjust brake bias, traction control, abs, engine mapping, setup, and how to approach driving these cars which designed with the assists in mind, I gotta say there's a hell of a lot of depth here in ACC that wasn't immediately apparent to me. I'd always known I liked the FFB and graphics better than iRacing, but now I'm starting to think I may like the physics better here in ACC as well. I think iRacing still has a massive leg up in multiplayer, but I now think ACC is a "better" overall game. (better is subjective, find what you like best)

Something else worth mentioning is I bought several tracks and a handful of vehicles to race on iRacing. I've probably spent close to $400-500 on iRacing in the brief period I spent with it. This does not include the $800 wheel I purchased. I went and purchased all of ACC's DLC after realizing I could get multiple cars and multiple tracks for roughly the cost of one "rented" track on iRacing.

I didn't intend for this to end up as a sim vs sim posting but it's hard for me to not compare the two. I spent a good deal of money many hours on one sim that left me more frustrated than entertained. Now that I'm focusing more on ACC I find I've spent far less money and have been much more entertained. Your mileage may vary.

I'd be very excited to see more touring cars and overall slower racing classes introduced to this platform. At the moment there's only one touring car in the game. I'm eager to see a front-wheel-drive race car introduced to the ACC physics system and FFB environment.

If you were to introduce a spec Miata series to ACC may never leave my computer desk again.\t\t\t\t\t
