\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAre you a hardcore sim racing enthusiast with a thirst for the most challenging GT3 experience on the market? Did you intend to spend ungodly amounts of money on high end sim racing gear and PC hardware?

Perhaps its my own experiences with this and other racing sims, but I look at this game, its market, its audience, and of course the game itself and it strikes me as no wonder that, while being pushed as the most authentic racing sim on the market, is extremely niche' compared to its contemporaries (which are hilariously, niche's in themselves).

Yes, it is a more realistic experience compared to your Gran Turismo's and Forza Motorsports. It is also far more difficult, which works against its detriment to not only long time sim racers but newcomers as well.

Feel, grip, and braking are all an enigma in this game. Where in other sims, even the console offerings, cars have inertia and weight, tires gradually respond and provide feedback, and you have to modulate your driving style in order to be smooth and fast, ACC feels the exact opposite. Cars feel disconnected from the road, tire grip is unresponsive, and so far as I can tell, the way to be fast in this game is to mash your inputs abruptly (yet subtly) to even stand a chance at being competetive in the online scene.

Single player lacks content and direction. Yes there is a "career" mode, but there's no gradual build up to hone your skills or teach you anything. Its straight into the fire without so much as an introduction to what a GT car or even what the GT World Challenge Championship (the game's premier partner) is.

If you're the hardest of the hardcore sim racer, you'll at the very least give this game its fair shake as I did. But if you're a casual or worse yet new to this little niche' hobby, avoid this game at all cost. There are far better and more welcoming options on the market to delve into.
