\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAssetto Corsa Competizione, heralded as a pinnacle of racing simulation, falls victim to a critical flaw that undermines its entire experience: the woefully inadequate Force Feedback (FFB) settings. In a racing title where precision and immersion should reign supreme, the unnervingly light and numb sensation transmitted through the steering wheel plunges players into a bewildering state of disconnection from the virtual track.

Attempting to wrangle a high-performance race car with the responsiveness of a deflated balloon, I found myself grappling with an infuriating lack of tactile feedback. Every turn, every maneuver felt as though I was guiding a ghostly apparition rather than a finely-tuned machine hurtling through legendary circuits.

Desperate to salvage any semblance of enjoyment from this ordeal, I delved into the settings menu, spending countless hours tinkering with sliders and toggles in a futile attempt to coax some semblance of realism from the game, even using LUTS. Yet, no matter how meticulously I adjusted the parameters or experimented with different configurations, the elusive sensation of tire grip, road texture, and vehicle weight remained stubbornly absent.

Assetto Corsa Competizione's failure to deliver a satisfying FFB experience not only detracts from the immersion but actively sabotages the gameplay itself. Racing enthusiasts seeking the adrenaline-fueled thrills of high-speed competition will find themselves stranded in a virtual purgatory, condemned to navigate tracks devoid of the visceral feedback essential for mastering hairpin turns, controlling slides, and pushing the limits of their driving prowess.

While the game boasts stunning visuals and an impressive roster of meticulously detailed cars and tracks, the fundamental flaw in its FFB implementation casts a shadow over its otherwise commendable features. Until addressed, this deficiency transforms Assetto Corsa Competizione from a potential masterpiece into a frustrating exercise in futility, leaving players adrift in a sea of disappointment. Proceed with caution, lest you find yourself lost in translation between aspiration and execution.\t\t\t\t\t

Just make sure you enable 'Gamepad' in your settings and assign your pit limiter to a button like 'A' or 'X'
