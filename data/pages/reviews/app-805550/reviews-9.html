\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome game to dive into to get better and competitive. Because it is just GT racing, you can really feel yourself getting better when you spend more hours in a car. The handling feels realistic, graphics are really good and the AI can make you have a lot of fun while racing against them.


Starting out

When starting out I would recommend to pick a single GT3 car to spend your hours in to get familiar with the handling and learn how to be fast with it. There's several YouTube video's to pick a car that suits you best, as all cars have different driving styles and characteristics. I picked the Ferrari 488 GT3, as I don't like the front-engine understeer and spent my first 100 hours in this game basically in that same car. After you gain complete control and feeling in this car and you can drive it casually at a fast and consistent pace, you can also dive into the rabbit hole of setups to go even faster.

Competitive multiplayer

If you want to start playing competitive multiplayer, please avoid the open lobbies as they can be full of Forza-style rammers. You can quite easily boost your rating by doing single-player races against AI, which are quite allright but a little predictable to race against. Usually I do races of about 20 minutes, but longer races are better to rank up your consistency and car control if you can get into a good rythm. Just focus on consistency and clean racing instead of position and race result. Mainly safety rating is important, but you'll rank up all others along the way. Once you get your safety rating to about 85-90 you can join most competitive multiplayer races and they provide great racing with serious and really fast simracers.

Content

I'm buying every single DLC so far in the game as they always add something new to the game, both the new tracks and the new classes offer some fresh variety. Having said that, GT3 racing is still my main class I always go back to. I am really looking forward to the upcoming Nordschleife DLC which might actually make this my all-time favourite game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAssetto Corsa offers adrenaline-fueled racing action that will satisfy any speed enthusiast's craving for high-octane thrills. With its sleek graphics, realistic physics, and impressive roster of cars and tracks, this game delivers a pulse-pounding experience that is sure to keep players on the edge of their seats.

However, it's important to note that Assetto Corsa falls short of being a true simulator when compared to the likes of iRacing. While it boasts realistic physics and handling, the overall driving experience leans more towards arcade-style gameplay rather than the hyper-realistic simulation offered by iRacing. This can be a letdown for players seeking the ultimate in authenticity and realism.

That being said, Assetto Corsa still offers plenty of fun and excitement for racing enthusiasts. Its extensive selection of cars and tracks provides endless variety, allowing players to customize their racing experience to suit their preferences. Whether you're tearing up the track in a high-performance supercar or drifting around corners in a souped-up tuner car, there's something for everyone in Assetto Corsa.

Ultimately, while Assetto Corsa may not offer the same level of realism as iRacing, it more than makes up for it with its thrilling gameplay and immersive racing experience. If you're looking for fast-paced, arcade-style racing action, Assetto Corsa is a fantastic choice. However, if you prioritize realism and authenticity above all else, I would recommend iRacing for a truly immersive simulator experience.\t\t\t\t\t
