\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game might be better for those using full racing sim hardware set ups; however, I was only using a game pad, and it was awful. I could not find a setting that wasn't too twitchy at speed but could also make a realistic slow tight turn. My first problem was that there was zero tutorial at all, the closest thing to training was testing to see what difficulty to put you at. As someone with significant high performance track time, I was most disappointed with realism. It was more work than fun, like a sim, but it wasn't even very realistic. There were also way too many helper systems build it that you couldn't turn off, like counter steering in a slide was always on; this really made it hard as sometimes the system helped and sometimes it didn't and the transitions were awkward. You could also mash the gas and sometimes you'd uncontrollably spin out and letting off the gas wasn't enough to recover traction for a few full seconds; otherwise, it would just keep spinning the tires. The control system seemed to punitively break the car if you ever lost control and didn't allow for a realistic quick recovery from minor losses of traction. I've intentionally and unintentionally put real high performance cars into slides and the most amazing thing about them is how fast they recover full traction the moment you let off the gas or ease the steering input.

Also, like many simulators, be prepared to spend a ton more on adding cars; the game price only includes a few cars so you have to spend a lot to get to drive everything you want to drive.\t\t\t\t\t
