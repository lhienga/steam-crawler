\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I like the game and it has potential but just too many issues. I've only played the game for a few days and there are several bugs. Since it is early access I'd accept some bugs.

I've put this review as negative but it is borderline - I wish there was a neutral option. It is essential a Banished clone albeit with some notable additions but even with mods it doesn't significantly improve on Banished (despite Banished not being developed for years).

I've played multiple survival/city builder games. In Foundation the village only grows by immigration. I much prefer the systems where the individual people grow, get married, have children, get old.... and you need to educate the children who then become workers when old enough. It is a lazy and much simpler system of just having workers and increasing their number simply by immigration. This is a big step backwards.

Games such as Banished have a grid layout whereas Foundation doesn't meaning you don't have straight lines and everything is more "organic". I like this and it is done well but there are some consequences. I don't know what happens of buildings slightly overlap and I have encountered some issues that might be due to buildings being too close to each other. Also the people just build their own houses which in some ways is fine. However, it removes some important control within the game. In Banished it helped to carefully manage the sequence of early building whereas in Foundation they can just go off and build a house or not build enough houses it is up to them. Also I haven't found any way to control upgrading of houses. If you want to decide the placement of all of your buildings it doesn't seem possible with the houses or the upgrades of the houses and other buildings don't fit together well which is fine when its a tiny village with dispersed buildings.

I like the organic (no straight lines) style but if, for example, you have a farm in Banished you can set the number of workers to be just enough to properly grow and harvest the crop. In Foundation you "paint" the area for the farm to cultivate but it is impossible to know how many workers are required for a certain size of farm. Also some buildings it isn't clear what's happening. For a chicken farm you can buy multiple groups of chickens but it isn't clear whether that has zero affect, whether it increases the rate of production or the duration of production. For a sheep farm you can get some sheep but they just wander around. In many respects Foundation just seems like a poor clone of Banished. Farms are just another example. It doesn't seem that the animals breed and increase in number and there isn't feedback in terms of the farming (production of goods). I would much prefer fields than animals randomly walking around.

In other games you can see the amount of resources - ie click a tree and see how much lumber is in the tree or click a stone and see its value.... In Foundation you can't. I haven't figured out whether some resources are infinite - ie berries or stone which doesn't seem very good/realistic. I would like to see how much resources are available. Thus if there are two clumps of berries I would like to know how many berries so I can decide where to put buildings.

Some of the critical game play isn't obvious. I'm sure that once you've figured it out you will know what is required but some aspects not intuitive.\t\t\t\t\t
