\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst and foremost, this is NOT a hacking game. It is a hacking THEMED puzzle game. It's one of those types of hacking games where there's clearly a specific path forward, but the technology in-game is not consistent with the real world so you're forced to act as the game expects and not rely on your technical expertise or intuition. This might be fine for people who don't know anything about computers, though, but I have worked in IT for over 20 years and find this extremely aggravating as I enjoy actual hacking games and detective/mystery stories where I can put that to use. It doesn't need to be literally analogous to the real world, but there's a certain aptitude you develop in IT that aids you in trying to figure things out and none of that is applicable here.

For example, email addresses unrelated to what you're doing are masked, which is not consistent with the platform since you can see related emails. You have the ability to search using the resources provided (search engine, database), but you can't search for emails that the game doesn't want you to search for, even though it's directly related to the task at hand and would be a way to get the solution you're looking for in the real world. Another example, in the tutorial mission, I accidentally skipped past a point of dialogue where the woman has given you the next task, but since the game is presented very rigidly I can't for the life of me figure out what the game is looking for, and your hands are tied despite having the "web" and database to search through. Searching the "web" only works when it's extremely specific to the case, and searching an email address should pull up a ton of info but this is not an option due to it not being what the game is specifically expecting. Anyone familiar with technology would be able to tell you that there's always multiple ways to get the information, but the game does not allow for this. It's a puzzle to figure out, not hacking. As far as hacking goes, it was a stereotypical fantasy magically-get-the-answer thing to click. No technical knowledge needed, and in my case it worked against me.

The translations or pronunciations of technical jargon are off, enough to also be aggravating if you're familiar with the terms. Non-tech folks likely won't notice, but it makes me wonder who the game's target audience is? Games like these should be about immersion, and they snap you out of it quickly and it's very obvious that this is in no way related to hacking or the tech industry.

I have no idea why this game is rated so highly. I picked this up because I love hacking and mystery games, and this had almost 10k positive reviews. After playing for just a few minutes it was the complete opposite of what I was expecting in an entirely negative way. It reminds me of Orwell, but Orwell had much better presentation and design and wasn't trying to be something it's not. I actually enjoyed Orwell a lot but it was clear it was in the Orwell fantasy world, so it was logically consistent. This game mimics real world applications in a way that is completely misleading and jarring, and equally inconsistent.

Do some heavy research before purchase. If you're technical, hard pass, there's too much suspension of disbelief and plenty of other hacking games to choose from that are better suited. If you're not technical, and you enjoy puzzles and the idea of movie hacking, you might like it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fun game.

But be aware that this is NOT a "hacking sim". This is more like a hacking themed point and click detective game. It is VERY linear and often un-intuitive.

You might have the right idea of what to do, but the game won't let you do it if you're not at the right part of the narrative progression. Even though at some point you need to do exactly what you already tried. For example, you do a certain internet search and you get a "page not found". But then later, you do the exact same search in order to progress the game.

Also, this must have been made by non-native English speakers. Some really WILD vocabulary errors. Referring to hotel guests as "passengers", and a lot of cringeworthy grammar mistakes.\t\t\t\t\t
