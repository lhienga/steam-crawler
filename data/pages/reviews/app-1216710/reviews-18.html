\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame & Board Game review IG (Chinese): @alixe.ecila

Used around 8 hours to complete the game. To be honest, Cyber Manhunt is an excellent game but to deduction gamer like me, it is a bit overrated. The game is recommended for newbies who are interested in hacking, deduction and social engineering.

The script is better than I have expected. The cases are inter-related as usual, but the difficulty advanced in each chapter, so the experience is not v repeating. Yet, the deduction part is a bit simple and less challenging to experienced player. I love the last intensive hacking part but it reminds me a lot of the stage design of Hacknet: Labyrinths. It is a good try though, may explore the collaboration with Team Fractal Alligator i think?

Compared to Black Watchman, which in the same social engineering game genre, I prefer the latter one for experienced player. Reason behind is that the latter one provides a more immersive game experience than Cyber Manhunt. However, Cyber Manhunt is still an exciting and outstanding game for those who have not played similar games before, in which the game gives player good knowledge in social engineering while delivers a positive message about data privacy.

The UX has rooms for improvement. Many steps of cracking can be simplified and the "copy and paste" part keep repeats and annoys me a lot. I found myself not patience and concentrated enough to focus on the cracking part and this is quite pity and a waste to the wonderful story of CM. Though, the UI part such as graphics, music and videos are surprisingly well-established.

Overall, the game is outstanding. Just keep in mind that data privacy is important and although you may have a good means of improving the society, invasion to other privacy is still illegal and an act to breach human rights.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally wish I could recommend this game but the bugs and poor translation ruin what would have otherwise been a cool experience. Ran into two game breaking bugs, one fixed after a reset and the other remains to be fixed, in the last chapter. After solving a puzzle the game reverts back to a state before the puzzle was solved, while the timer continues to countdown.

Another huge problem, especially in the last chapter, is the game is throwing a ton of background lore at you while an onscreen timer ticks away, in a scenario where the game can't even be paused. The interface already makes it hard to quickly grab information, but now you have to grab it while racing the clock, and solving puzzles. Your option is either to go slow and actually absorb the lore (failing and having to repeat after multiple attempts), or go quickly and miss anything story related.

The concept of this game is great and almost done well here. ALMOST. It needs better translation and better writing, way less bugs and a better UI. Whether it was script or direction, the voice acting was not up to par either.

Playing for so long only for the game to break at the end really soured me on it, but I'm hoping the devs can fix it for their next iteration, because there is definite potential here.\t\t\t\t\t
