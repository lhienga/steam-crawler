I think the biggest problem I had with this is during the "tests" they give you, you're given all your notes for reference so you're not expected to memorize copious amounts of info but during the phone conversations the person will say "Hey who was your friend?" and you're given three choices and if you didn't happen to remember that and choose wrong it is instant fail. The big thing that keeps this from being totally game breaking is that it lets you very quickly reload your game and choose again but it is very frustrating and immersion breaking to just hammer the same obtuse dialogue tree over and over again.

However the rest of it is really well done. It is a nice combination of handing you the answers and letting you hunt for them yourself and the "Deduction" mini-game is really well done because it forces you to synthesize all this data you've collected into actual information.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely post negative reviews cause I usually pick the games I buy very carefully. I was looking for a fun hacking game though and with the limited selection out there I chose this based on its "Very positive" user reviews. I was actually going to give it a thumbs up too, until I actually read the question Steam asks:
"Would you recommend this game to other players".
To that specific question I would definitely have to say: No.
It's tough, cause I kinda like the game, I just wouldn't expect other players or a friend to have the amount of patience with it that I've had. Subjectively, I'd score it a 7/10, objectively I'd give it a 4/10.

TL;DR The game sets you up for failure. Timers on puzzles mean replaying sections where you fail to solve the puzzle on time, which in some cases are virtually impossible to do on your first try. Then short cutting the info gathering to buy time on your next try, because you already have the info in your head, sometimes introduces game breaking bugs. All this also pushes the player towards walkthroughs completely ruining the experience. Game also has some immersion breaking unnatural language issues.

PROS:
- Interesting story with some good twists.
- Immersive. You can really get into following along with the stories of the people you "hack" and the hacking makes you feel empowered.
- Valuable message around personal data, AI and social media in general.
- A few fun, innovative puzzles.
- Well made UI and visuals (Nothing special, but not bad)

CONS:
- Convoluted puzzles. Some puzzles require you to assume things that aren't intuitive. Like having to use concatenated dates as a password but not being told that specific parts of the dates need to be left out. Having these on a timer seriously exasperates the problem.
- Language issues. Whoever wrote the script and content for this game is definitely not a natural English speaker. All the spelling is correct but the grammar is flawed and the manner of speaking is jarringly foreign. Like everything was written in Japanese and then run through Google translate or something. Unnecessary extra words in places and weird grammar immediately make you feel like "This is not the way people speak". It's honestly quite immersion breaking in a game that involves a lot of reading.
- One or two bugs. Mostly the game runs just fine, in fact you could pretty much run it on a potato. I literally only encountered one bug, but it was game breaking and required that I reload which lost me some progress. The biggest issue about it is that it was caused by trying to short cut problems with the first con I mentioned meaning that alleviating the biggest issue of the game isn't an option.\t\t\t\t\t
