\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this, but there are a number of things that make it hard to recommend this:

* A majority of the game involves finding text that will highlight in red when you click it to allow you to "gather clues" -- which leads to clicking every line of text, just in case. At some points, you'll be missing a single clue and have to go slogging back through all that you've previously read to find the one little sentence or phrase that you didn't click to highlight and collect.
* The translation to English is absolutely terrible. In some places, that actually makes trying to figure out what the game wants you to do more difficult.
* There's also some comically bad voice acting at times. To make matters worse YOU CAN'T ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ SKIP the spoken dialogue scenes. This is particularly egregious because in many sections you have to listen to entire conversations before you can collect any of the evidence (that is, click every sentence trying to find what will be highlighted in red as clues).
* At some points, you're asked to piece together what happens, but you CAN'T TAB BACK TO WHERE THE INFO YOU COLLECTED IS STORED TO FIND THOSE ANSWERS!
* While it can be satisfying to poke around to get the info you need in the investigation stages, some of the other minigames/puzzles are just not fun to play. This is particularly bad when you reach the final stage(s) of the game. I was on my way to 100% completion, but that final stage is a such a slog of *TIMED* minigames, I stopped playing the main story and moved on to the DLC (they were cheap, so I thought I'd give them a try).
* A few stages are timed, and although some portions are generous with how much time you're given, it definitely makes it far less fun.
* The main story takes a twist that is just... asinine. You discover you're not a real person, but an AI--just like the antagonist. Seriously, it killed the immersion the gameplay and story had managed to build.

It's a very low cost game, and I did get some fun out of it--but I don't think I can, in good conscience, recommend it to others.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I found this game I didn't expect much, other than something in the vein of ORWELL, which I did thoroughly enjoy. But the stories it has and the method are just... good. It hits on mature themes that might go down into horror, showing one just how bad it can get. The stories had me hooked, the mysteries raised hairs and it really showcases the world as it is. You are left to be a detective to piece things out from the safety of a chair, going over the internet for everything, and bonus points that you don't have some state-appointed communications platforms and instead you hack into other mediums.

Pros:
With few exceptions, everything is relaxed. Play at your pace, figure everything out.
The stories are deep and the characters have a life to them, things seeming natural for them... with few exceptions.
You feel like a detective as everything is there to be found and pieced together.
Long enough to give you a good day, and for that price it's worth it.

Cons:
Ever so rarely, you do see a slip-up of translators here and there, but it is thankfully nothing crucial
Sometimes you can spend a long time to find your next lead
No good guys, or few... as fitting of real life. Even the DLC shows how it's just gray against grey.\t\t\t\t\t
