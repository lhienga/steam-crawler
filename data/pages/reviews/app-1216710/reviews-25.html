\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is supposed to be an investigation game, but there is some real lost potential and issues that make the game more akin to a "hidden object" game. So far I have only completed the first chapter, but there are glaring issues in the design.

Most of the time you're not actually required to think for yourself. Consider the tutorial, in this section you have to find the birth date of a person to hack their password. The person has a newborn picture on their Facebook account, the photo has a date on it, and (if i remember correctly) there are also comments or a title of the post confirming that this is the person you are looking for. So what do you do then? Do you read the date and manually input it into some sort of of account recovery? No, instead the picture will immediately highlight upon mousing over it, and after a surprisingly long loading animation, you have the picture stored, and after an even longer loading animation the game just highlights the date and auto stores it, and finally you can just copy paste this info into a program which just gives you the password to all their accounts.

Let's also talk about these loading animations. The game has some real issues with user experience. These 1-5 second animations completely disrupt your gameplay with a zoom and locks your cursor until it's complete saving the information you just found. I don't know who thought this was a good idea, in any given "mission" you might do this between 30-60 times or more.For example, when getting someones password, you find two pieces of information, and then you have to use a database to get 6 pieces of information (all need to be individually saved with its own animation) only to feed that information into another app to get the password. The 6 pieces of information are never used again as far as I could tell.

The "browser" that you use in game is also quite finicky at times, it wont let you close tabs unless they are active, and clicking a link will sometimes overwrite other tabs which you may have wanted to keep open.

A much better experience would be to honestly just remove the "saving" or highlighting of information and just allow the player a notepad or something, as well as allowing security questions to recover passwords. With security questions the player could scour the targets social media at the same time they use the database.

The game sometimes gets close to this, with a date of a seemingly ordinary picture being posted coming back to being important, but its instantly highlighted by your "boss" in a dialogue. The developers had a great idea, but I fear that lack of playtesting might have led to several poor decisions in regards to the core gameplay loop as well as the user experience.

I can't really recommend this game in it's current state, even though I do love the core idea. The idea is good and the plotlines as well, but the above mentioned issues make the game very tedious to play. Nevertheless I'm looking forward to updates and or sequels to hopefully improve and expand on this game or the core idea behind the game.\t\t\t\t\t
