\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe characters don't talk, write or act like normal people at all, which does make the game kinda hilarious at times, but also makes it extremely hard to take the cases and characters seriously. You can tell that the creators are not native english speakers, which also results in some facepalming, but funny, mistakes at times.

Even looking past all this though, the actual investigating that you will be doing isn't at all as free as i would like it to be. It mostly consists of you finding details about people, which you usually get from one specific source. Gaining information this way, does not make the player come up with any kind of creative solution to finding info, since they know they just have to find that one spot where someone mentions it, which you usually just have to guess how to find.

When you have gathered enough information, you need to try to make deductions from it, which is one of the more fun parts of the game i guess. After you do this correctly you have reached a sort of "milestone" which results in the boss lady giving you some kind of detail, so you can continue the search. Making the game this way does not make you feel like a detective at all. Why not just give the player all the details from the beginning, and let them explore the case openly?

I could probably write more about why i don't like this game, but i don't really wanna spend more time on it. It's not a horrible game, but i would say it's decent AT BEST. Some people will probably enjoy it a lot, but it doesn't really do anything that other games haven't done in a better way.\t\t\t\t\t
