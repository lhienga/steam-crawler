\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMostly a hacking game about copy and pasting information from one box to another. I think there's a good idea here somewhere, but the execution is lacking. I like the idea of information gathering from social media and other open information sources. However, only the clues that are important from the game's perspective really matter and they're only found by mousing over in-game text or images, so you're really running your mouse over everything by habit, and there are few opportunities to really make impactful decisions.

A game like this could do well if the writing and setting are done well, but the situations presented so far seem really contrived. Combine that with dialog that sounds awkward and stilted, with word choices and grammar that were obviously not written or proofread by a native English speaker and it really takes you out of the game. What I've seen so far is not broken English, more awkward English, like it was translated automatically.

Most other reviews paint a good picture with similar information, so I think it comes down to personal preference. Overall I agree this game plays more like a visual novel and less like a puzzle game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCyber Manhunt is a pretty good game. The story is well-written, if a bit difficult to follow sometimes, and has a lot of good elements to it. There are flaws, however, and many of them interfere with the immersion. This review will be free of major spoilers, with only a few plot points hinted at. I won't ruin your play experience if you decide to buy it yourself.

In terms of visuals, the game looks great. The art and animation are well done and have their own unique style to them. The UI looks pretty great too, though there are problems there that lie beyond their sleek exteriors. One of the most annoying parts (something that could have been easily implemented, I'll bet) is that you can't use the TAB key to move between fields. The password cracker requires you to input several pieces of information in across multiple fields, and you'll do this several times in each case. You can't use TAB, though, so you pretty much have to do everything with your mouse. It's a minor gripe, but I found myself annoyed at these sections of the game. They included CTRL+C and CTRL+V, but not TAB?

Sound design is mixed. The music is great, from the actual lyrical songs, to the general background music. It's a little repetitive, but it fits the game and doesn't distract from what you're trying to do. I think the Reasoning music is my favorite just for how nice it sounds, adding a bit of urgency to the normal background music. The voice acting, however, is mixed at best. Some roles sound better than others, but the game would have benefited from better voice direction.

However, the story suffers the most, because whoever was in charge of localizing the game seriously dropped the ball. The tutorial and first case (so the content included in the demo) have the best localization, but even then it's not great. The wording of hundreds of messages in the game is awkward and fake-sounding, some parts even sounding like those phishing e-mails written by the prince of Nigeria. There were places where the localization was downright unintelligible, and I ended up having to make guesses in certain parts of the game to progress the story because I couldn't make sense of the options provided.

The story itself, localization issues aside, is good, but feels disjointed at times. There are two major plots that run simultaneously, and they seem completely separate from each other, even when they should be working together. Also, characterization for the main NPC was just wrong - through other dialogue and flavor text, the game seems to be trying to establish this character as your friend, but they act distant at best and antagonistic at worst, so your motivations get muddled at certain parts of the story. Puzzle design is pretty good, though there are two or three puzzles that will practically force you to look up a guide because their design is pretty obtuse. But overall, the game has a good message about privacy in the digital age - it's just a bit hampered by the way the game is presented.

Overall, Cyber Manhunt is a good, but flawed game. After playing other hacking games like Code7, Hacknet, and Uplink, I found it to be more on the point-and-click adventure side of things rather than an actual hacking simulator, but it really needed a final layer of polish if it wanted to be one of the greats in the genre. I think the game's overall appeal would be greatly increased by having a good localization team go through and fix a lot of the rough translations and making the game more accessible and immersive.\t\t\t\t\t
