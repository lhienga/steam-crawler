\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCyber Manhunt is kind of an interesting, 90's era adventure game but set in today's social media / online internets world.

Why is it an adventure game? Well its because it has that point and click nature to it, you solve abstract puzzles but instead of checking under the rug for a key you're futzing around in a fake GUI and logging into websites searching for info on people and their personal lives.

A lot of the websites you visit has a lot of parallels in real life like Facebook and e-maiil. And your task is generally to find enough personal details of your target to be able to hack into their personal accounts. Then you piece together information to learn what happened to that person or some other nefarious thing happening in today's world. You have scandals, you have CEOs, you have missing people. And somewhat predictable twist at the end. Its Metal Gear stuff but very very very light and you barely give a crap about any of the characters at all.

The relations between the characters can almost be confusing at times cause near the end of the dozen hour campaign you're really fed up with reading all the social media, chat, and e-mails of these subjects. Much of which are complete fluff.

But since this is an adventure game you may find yourself click spamming text to see if its a vital clue to your investigation. There's not a huge amount of options so if you're lost but have all the clues its just a few clicks to see what's next, but if you don't have the clues from the text you'd be click spamming for a while.

Now when you reach the last mission, which is 5th case it gets pretty absurd. There's a lot more timed hacks or actions. There's very abstract and annoying codes you need to figure out. You didn't experience any of these new puzzles from earlier cases, so it can be very testing. I'm not going to lie, I didn't want to keep seeing a game over screen so I used walkthroughs. Guessing games aren't my thing.

That said for a 10-12 hour game its not bad. Graphics are decent and the fake GUI you use to hack and browse websites is kinda cool. So for presentation style its decent.

But the game has a stellar mystery style soundtrack - kinda like the theme to Unsolved Mysteries. Its ominous and creepy and there's just no other game out there that evokes that awesome spoopy feel. They have many different variants, and I feel like the devs actually have a great pianist / composer among them. Though I didn't check.

So to end this review, Cyber Manhunt doesn't push the boundaries of its adventure game-inspired hacker game. It doesn't push the realism factor either, as instead of real hacking its just cheap ebay puzzles with a timer sometimes. But its soundtrack is amazing, and its affordable. Get it if you want a basic adventure game with a hacking slant and nice tunes. 4/5 Stars.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic, high paced game with a plot that totally sucks you in. So many twists and turns, it does keep you playing just to find out a little more each chapter.
Some of the mini games are a little tricky, as they kind of throw you into them without any explanation and they are timed. I learnt the hard way that pausing the game does NOT stop the timer on these games, and sometimes you can't even pause at all.
There's a lot of heavy material in the game as a warning, such as bullying, stalking, murder, assault, r*pe and domestic violence. Plus more. Some chapters are so heavy I had to take a breather after them, because you really do get sucked into the character's lives and stories.
One thing I really disliked about the game was the use of the r word slur multiple times in the game. As a disabled person myself I found it jarring and slightly offensive. I get that the character saying it is portrayed as a horrible person, but I would have enjoyed the game a lot more without the use of this slur. It can easily be replaced with a less offensive word.

Overall, it is a fantastic game. It has its faults, which are still being worked on by the team to their credit, but the positives very much outweigh the negatives. If you like plot driven, dystopian, light sci-fi and heavy themes in your games, this will excellent for you to try. Sit back, prep yourself and enjoy the narrative of the world the developers have created.

If you found yourself upset or related to some of the content in the game, please consider talking to someone and getting help. You're not alone, and help is out there for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCyber Manhunt is a very interesting game drenched in adult themes and unethical hacking.

=====

I will keep this one short, so as to not give anything away.

You don't need to know how to hack to play this game, obviously. It's a lot like Orwell, though darker in tone, and I actually prefer this. The characters are interesting, have deeply complex personal relationships, all of which comes together at the end of the line.

The twists and conspiracies throughout are thoughtful and engaging, while provoking all sorts of mixed emotions and at times, apprehensions or even excitement from the player.

If you want to have an experience, it's definitely worth a go - assuming you're mature enough to handle such content as r*pe, abductions/kidnapping, animal abuse, murder and murder schemes, theft, trafficking, spying, identity theft, infidelity, perversion and more.

The puzzles involved, and secrecy, at times, can be just challenging enough to prompt some deeper thinking and thinking outside the box to accomplish your end goals. Numerous times I found myself stuck until inspiration reared its head, and a hunch worked out for the better. You need strong deduction and a very analytical eye for this game (at times) ;) There are moments where progression is obvious, but other times where the game can throw you for a loop, so you have to pay attention, and remember what you've seen, heard, and read, to properly pry information and obtain more clues. Sometimes, a hunch is all you need to kick things up ;)

=====

Do I recommend?
Yes, I do, but warn it may not have subject matter that easily emotional people can just walk away from. Take your time with this one, there's a lot to uncover. I plan to revisit it and achievement hunt; I want the whole story~!

If this is your jam, then give it a go. You won't be disappointed.
