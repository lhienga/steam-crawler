the "hacking" is complete nonsense, and the thrust of the game is really you just guessing what information the developers want you to click. the UI, especially the fake webpages (such as... "Toothbook") look really awful, and the english is - at best - not very good. the UI has no shortcuts, you have to click a piece of text, click "copy," then click a text box, and click "paste" - it's a hacking game with no keyboard controls. All the character art is awkwardly proportioned, sometimes-slightly-better-than-deviantart sketches, and every character talks like they're trying Very Hard to sound as Human as possible, especially in the "phishing" conversation minigame. the story is based on the horrible, basic-ass "oh nooo everyone is too addicted to social media!!!" old people trope, and then immediately puts you in the shoes of the hacker we're meant to be so afraid of?

really i should just get a refund and go back to play hacknet again, but...

it's so unintentionally funny. the game is set in 2021, and the mission giver lady, right off the bat, gives you this impressive stat: the company you're working for has amassed ~*~over 1 billion bytes~*~ of user data. that's right, player, this company has amassed an entire One Gigabyte.

This game is one where no one (not even the tutorial text!) seems to understand how to communicate, and hacking is done by putting a person's name and birthdate into a form which will run nonsense Hacker Code and somehow figure out the One Password everybody uses for everything. and

...Toothbook.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I got this game because it reminded me a lot of Orwell. I've grown to like playing detective games where you learn more about others through their media and all that. And yeah, I know this is a hacking game and different, but it's clear this game was inspired by Orwell, at least to me it was. Now, first going into this, the game was kind of fun. Simple, but fun. It does seem to get harder as you go along and it's interesting to learn more about the chapter your in.

However, the game has two major problems for me. First off, the interface is a bit annoying. Like, to erase something, you need to actually hit your own delete button. I know this may be nitpicking, but you don't have to do this when copying and pasting, and you are going to be doing a lot of these two. It's also tedious going through the apps. When closing them, for example, you have to recopy the password in should you need to go back to the same users file.

Another thing that makes this game a bit annoying for me is the tutorial. This game, unless your good with tech and/or hacking, doesn't do a good job at explaining certain aspects of the game. A good example of this is when you need to hack into someone's phone in chapter two. It's also vaguely explains certain things later in the game and this is done by your jerk of the boss. I don't get this since the beginning had a pretty nice tutorial.

I don't want to say it's a bad game. It's not actually. But honestly, the interface and lacking tutorial for later and important parts of the game make this not fun for me. If you're someone who doesn't care much for these things or good with tech/hacking, I'll think you'll enjoy it. The game is lacking a bit though in terms of really getting into these peoples' life and getting to know them. I wish it had done more of that because the chapters are interesting. Still, I'll change my review should these two things be changed (or at least the interface.) It's not a bad game and is decent, but for me at the moment I don't want to keep playing it.\t\t\t\t\t
