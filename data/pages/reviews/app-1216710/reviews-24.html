\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is relatively similar to Orwell, which I enjoyed very much. It got some clever gameplay aspects to it and most of the puzzles are neither too easy nor too hard. The storylines, while a bit predictable at times, are fine and easy enough to follow. Also, the voice actors do a pretty good job for a game of this scope.

Why the thumbs down then? Because the game really suffers from the fact that the writers and developers don't seem to be fluent in the English language. Unfortunately, this leads to grammatical errors, a strange choice of words and stilted dialogues at every corner, which constantly breaks the immersion and sometimes even makes it hard to understand what the game is trying to tell you. In a game mostly relying on text, this is a huge issue. Getting native speakers on board to help with the translation could help a great deal, I think. Considering this game is in Early Access, I'd gladly turn this negative review into a positive one, if the language problem gets fixed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall a good game, especially if you enjoyed Orwell and Hypnospace Outlaw. Here the major focus is on compiling peoples information, so that you can generate their passwords and find out more about them. All that works well enough and it's fun working out what happened, along with how everyone connects together.

However the game does have some issues that stuck out to me:
The first chapters has a poorer English translation, than the rest. It's not so bad that you can't understand whats going on, but it's still kind of annoying.
While the translation improves, a few issues do creep in later on, such as a female character being referred to as 'he' in a phone call, or the voiced dialogue not quite matching the text.
Considering the type of game, it was probably inevitable but I sometime found myself pouring over everything trying to find the one piece of information I needed to continue.
Sometimes, even when I had collected enough data, the related event would fail to trigger, leaving me unable to progress. I was always able to reload an auto-save, and the event would then play normally, but it often took me a few minutes to realise I needed to do this.
One type of puzzle , is a mobile phone lock-screen which was very annoying to solve. Even with a clue it was tedious to work out the answer, resulting in a lot of guess-work. It also was done under a time-limit.

It's still a good game despite these issues, and I look forward to more content being added in future.\t\t\t\t\t
