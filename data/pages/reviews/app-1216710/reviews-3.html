\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEnjoyable game.

~9 hours to complete (without DLC).
Some puzzles are a little obscure (wave-length matching? one very specific phone password), but the rest is very intuitive and compelling. The stories definitely hooked me and left me wanting for more every time I'd delve a clue further.
The game does lack a little bit of polish, the interface, while intuitive, does tend to be a little non-responsive at times. The translation to English is a little lacking in polish as well -- The intent and details are still very easy to understand, but the syntax and tone often feel off.

For someone that liked Ace Attorney and Hacknet, this feels like a nice fusion in the middle of both genres. Would recommend to a fan of both of these types of games, while staying wary of the lack of polish.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you think about it, much of this game is just clicking on links and copy/pasting info. But it ends up being so much more. It is framed as a hacking sim, but really its more of a detective sim with some puzzles on the side. The story may not be to everyone's tastes, but I like the dark and mature commentary on big data in the future. It does it however through focusing on the people, which makes it connect to us... as people. It gets a little edgy, but I never felt it was unjustified. I had some gripes about how the text selection can be a bit finicky, but overall I really liked the story and the gameplay felt like a good complement to it.\t\t\t\t\t
