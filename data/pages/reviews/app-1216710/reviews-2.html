By getting a handle on the puzzles in the DLC first, you can save yourself from having to replay parts of the game over and over.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t2/5

Cyber Manhunt falls disappointingly short of what I was expecting. While it's evident that the developers poured time and effort into this game, the end result is a mediocre experience. Nothing is horribly bad, but nothing is great either.

One of the major letdowns is the story line, which is... cliched. I could figure out where it was going almost beat-for-beat. The characters feel two-dimensional, and most of the time I could skip past their dialogue and just find the highlighted bits I was meant to find without losing anything meaningful from the parts I skipped.
Sleuthing, he game's core mechanic, turns out to be a painfully limited and repetitive experience. The investigative elements promised are reduced to basic point-and-click tasks, and copy-and-pasting things into boxes. The puzzles lack complexity and fail to challenge the player's intellect, resulting a cycle that gets old after chapter 2.
The dialogue delivery is often wooden, making it difficult to immerse oneself in the story.

I can tell the devs really tried, which redeems this to a point, but the overall experience falls flat. With a more engaging story line and more diverse detective mechanics, Cyber Manhunt could have been a standout title.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYeah this is pretty fun if you like this kind of game. A solid 7/10 If you have played similar (Orwell) then you'll enjoy this one too, even if the experience is a little more barebones.

I enjoyed it and had a great time, but it's one of these games the really annoys me. It lacks any form of polish and it really holds it back.

Honestly, a whole lot of decisions were clearly made in development that have resulted in what I can only describe as a lazy implementation, such as having to wait a long time to from clicking on data, to having it appear in your own 'journal'. They fixed this in the DLC but didn't patch it into the main campaign experience? Why? Someone decided it wasn't worth the dev time and the game is worse off for it.

I appreciate English is not the first language of the dev team, but there are multiple cases where writing doesn't make sense, subtitles are way off or text is malformed due to there not being space. There are also typos throughout the experience. How hard is it to hire a native English speaker to proof read the script? Again it just strikes me as lazy. And I won't even get started on the voice acting. Let's just say it leaves something to be desired...

There are lots of other little issues throughout the experience that just make you feel that the amount of care put into this project isn't high enough.

Some of the mini games throughout are unnecessarily hard and detract from the experience and story which is actually pretty good.

Recurring characters, interesting twists and somewhat believable characters actually made me invested in each case, especially seeing how they are all intertwined.

DLC is also good, with 'Home Sweet Home' actually being better than the campaign itself. Brilliant! And most of the issues I had with the main campaign were improved upon.

As I say I enjoyed this game, it just sucks to see a potential 9/10 end up being a solid 7. It's good enough that I'll likely buy their next game.\t\t\t\t\t
