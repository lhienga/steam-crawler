\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly for an EA game, this has potential.
-Well acted voice overs where appropriate
-Excellent little mini-games and timed sections which break up the regular pacing of the game for a small hint of excitement
-Comfortable UI and UX aspects.

However, that does not mean that the game is free of bugs, nor does not need some shine and polishing in other areas.
-The localisation for English needs a lot of work. I understand and appreciate that English may not be the primary language of the devs, it can be jarring and de-imsersifying (probably just created a word there) in places where the flow and writing gets jumbled, lacks conveyance and flow. -If- English is going to be the primary release language, then additional work needs to be done polishing that up, as reading through news stories, toothbook posts and hitalk app conversations are so critical to gameplay in Cyber Manhunter.

- While the gameplay is interesting, and slowly reveals itself over time, save for a few set pieces (phone trojans, the chapter 3 end setpiece, and phone conversations) the gameplay is very samey:
- Get information on Person A
- Crack Person A's stuff using the database to get incomplete information Person B
- Rinse and repeat for B to C and so on.
Don't get me wrong this is a fine mechanic core mechanic but in the second and third chapters (which are where the breaks from core mechanics really should be kicking in) there's not enough divergence from that key mechanic which in my opinion mutes the game just a little bit.

- Finally: Replayability at this point is low. 4 hours in and with enough snooping and combing through what is a series of engrossing stories, I can truthfully say there's nothing to come back to for now. Certainly if you just focused on drilling down exactly what you needed for the main objective of each chapter you would find that yeah, coming back to perfect a chapter would be worthwhile, but then after that you're pretty much done.
I'm not sure if its on the horizon or not, but something that could randomise a mission, or a free play or something that would beef-up the very nicely put together experience that is Cyber Manhunter and keep me playing it longer would be appreciated, as I thoroughly enjoyed my Friday night with it.

I know it looks like I've gone into more detail with the current drawbacks than I have the pros of the game, however what Cyber Manhunter does right it does beautifully. This review is more....feedback than anything else, and hopefully something the devs see. For you the player, this is definitely something you can pick up, play and get hooked into. A good Friday night relaxing beer and investigate game that makes you think. Well worth the price of admission at this point and I look forward to what is provided by the developers moving forward.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven though it's not perfect I really recommend the game, also cause it's for a small coin. But instead of giving a full review I just write what issues the game has as it is still work in progress:

I really loved the investigation parts like looking up stuff but I felt that there was not all possibilities used. Like in the last episode where Cyclops wasn't mentioned as a character even though he was one of the prime persons. I would have loved to get into his case as side investigation instead of having him just as a pressure against Isabelle. The last investigation felt off overall, where I saw the lack of information on major stuff. Like they talk about the lost person but not the fire, you also couldn't "gogo" the fire. The situation was pretty complex where I saw that the information also has to get more complex as well but it stayed the same amount as the other cases. So I would look at the cases and think about what the players might look for and provide some information even though it gets nowhere like the novel with its summary. That was a good example, interesting to read and giving the idea what may have happened.

Secondly I didn't like the phone calls, they felt like try and error for the most time and in many cases it didn't make sense that the other side just cut the line and you get yelled by your boss. Dialogue systems like these need more finesse, you still can bring other arguments until you completely mess it up. One good thing was the girl which you had to bring into almost a mental breakdown to get your information. But instead of being just hit or miss you need maybe to say: 70% of the choices are enough for your goal, so you can like mess up 1 or 2 choices and still win. That feels more RPG like in these situations, you just don't get burned to dust instantly if your counterpart gets an answer he/she doesn't like.

Thirdly the hacks of the phones have been a bit strange. I don't even know why you don't have much time, cause it makes no sense. Its not like the user of the phone is counter hacking or whatever. But yeah I understand the urge to make it difficult through that. Just a bit waste of the nice informations inside the phone. The idea of finding swipes was cool but the one of Jim made me crazy that the "2" was a part of starting at the second dot of the swiping patern was a critical one. The rainbow one was far more intuitive. The main issue in puzzle games is when the puzzles start to make not much sense in a logical chain. And this one was pretty close to that imo still I got it after some time, but phew. Then the time issue is really annoying going through the whole "hacking in" again. Instead of the time trial I would rather fill the phone with more information where you have to dig through to get what I want. Don't know if it's too much to ask for but that would be just my idea of making that part more enjoyable than stressful and annoying sometimes.

As I said the game overall is super interesting, like Welcome to the Game without murderers, just investigation how many fans of WttG wished for. I hope it goes on. I played the cases again to get missing info etc. Just my type of game. Keep up the good work.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am thoroughly enjoying this game. The gameplay and story are both super interesting, and I quite like that you have the option to continue the investigation after the required information has been collected.

A few criticisms: The dialogue left me wanting a bit, the writing and grammar can be a bit clunky and often characters will use specific uncommon phrases often enough that it becomes noticeable. There were also quite a few spelling and grammatical errors, as well as spoken dialogue not properly matching the subtitles, which can be distracting.

I also noticed that though dialogue can be skipped sometimes, often in a phone call, if the player has just spoken, the preceding sentence cannot be skipped. I'm aware this is a minor nitpick, but it can be annoying when replaying a certain chapter.

Gameplay wise I have very few criticisms, though I did find that controls in both the inference and telephone passcode sections are not outright broken, but can be quite finicky at times. Also, after a failed attempt at Phishing or Hacking, Ashley has a voiceline where she says she will give you "one more chance". Though this doesn't really make sense within the story of the game, because it is assumed that after you failed in one of these crucial sections and you try again, you're not doing so directly after, but instead you're reverting to a save before you tried the first time. Also a bit of a nitpick, but it still bothered me personally. I don't know if this would be possible to implement, but I think it would be a lot more interesting if the player were reprimanded by Ashley, as usual, but then instead of simply retrying the task again, the player would continue, and have to find a different way to gain the information that they were seeking.

Overall, I think the idea for this game was amazing, the story is gripping, the gameplay is decent, and the dialogue is lacking. I would definitely reccomend this game, though I do think it's in need of improvements.\t\t\t\t\t
