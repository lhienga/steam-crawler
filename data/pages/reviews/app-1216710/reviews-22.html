\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI became intrigued by the game based upon the concept and decided to purchase it while it was still in early access, however, did not fully delve into the game until it was fully released.

Gameplay loop
The basic principle the game follows is that you are working for a company named Titan Matrix, and are assigned cases where you need to investigate a specific person or event. This is done by investigating a person’s online presence, gathering information from for example articles, messaging services and social media. While the game might have the tag of “hacking” this game play element is reduced to using automatic tools for cracking passwords or invading someone’s phone. Most of the game play consists of the player searching for possible clues, in most of the game automatically highlighted in red while moving the cursor over them, with the notable exception starting Chapter 4 where the player can choose the play the chapter without this assisting tool. The found clues can then be used to investigate other events and uncover more of the story. As a last note, not all clues need to be found to solve a case, however finding all can show a new perspective on the story,

Story
As stated earlier, you are working for a company named Titan Matrix. Your job is to uncover the reasons why certain events occurred during the game by investigating the people involved. The reason for this seems to be financial or even political gain for the company. Story lines from earlier chapters do pop up in the later chapters, and do play a part in the events that unfold. This leads to a conflict with a hacker (the main antagonist), who is seemingly doing the same thing as you and seems to know you quite well For now I will leave it at this, as this is primarily a story-driven game.

Strengths
The game has a nice design and art style with an intuitive user interface making it easy to get into. The story line despite becoming more convoluted as the game progresses manages to capture and hold the player’s attention without overstaying its welcome. The core idea is something many people can relate to, as I can imagine quite a few people fell into the rabbit hole of researching someone online.

Weaknesses
There are a few glaring issues currently in the game.

Language
For a story driven game there are quite a few grammatical and/or spelling errors. While this seems to have improved over time this issue still persists. This can easily lead to immersion being broken, or in the worst case confusing clues halting progress. The worst offenders seem to be passwords that are based upon dates, as most of the time the format YYYY-MM-DD is used, but in rare cases a format such as MM-DD-YYYY occurs, leading to unnecessary frustration. Overall, some issues in translation can be overlooked, but the sometimes confusing clues or weird formatting of certain clues are an issue I would implore the developers to address.


Chapter 4: Public Opinion
At the end of the fourth chapter a certain mini-game needs to be played where the goal is to sway the public opinion. After retrying this section multiple times I just made a spreadsheet noting down all the results from my inputs, and then replayed it with “perfect” inputs, and still had difficulty. This mini-game is not much of a puzzle as it is a test of endurance and trial-and-error. This mini-game is not intuitive in the slightest and unnecessarily difficult. I implore the developers to either change the set-up of this mini-game, or at the least write a better explanation for this mini-game.

Puzzles and phishing
While the smallest issue, I did find that the difficulty for certain puzzles are very odd. Some puzzles are incredibly easy, to the point I would hardly argue that they are even puzzles. While others can suddenly be quite difficult. Some of these more difficult puzzles are quite clever in hindsight, but when looking at a broader definition of the word and including the phishing “puzzles” these seem to sometimes lack rhyme or reason, quickly devolving into trail-and-error. Perhaps this latter part is also due to the issues with the translation. As DLC seems to be in the works currently, I would like the developers to reassess how they set-up these phishing sections, as a simple rewrite of dialogue options might not solve the issue.

Conclusion
While this game is far from perfect it is an admirable attempt from a young studio. It is a fun short delve into a concept that is becoming more and more to the forefront in current day society. While I primarily focused on the flaws in this review (in an effort to help the reader know the current issues as well as bring them to the developers attention) I can wholeheartedly say that I did enjoy my time with this game and found it to be worth the couple of euros I put down for it. While the story at times predictable it did enough to lure me in and left me wanting more. I will most likely be back for the sequel/DLC.

As the studio still seems to be pushing updates and fixing some of the issues this review might be updated in the future to more accurately represent the state of the game. \t\t\t\t\t
