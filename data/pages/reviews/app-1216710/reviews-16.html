\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPoint and click cyber adventure game

Gameplay: Point and click browser game sorta thing. You go looking for information through various apps and websites linking everything together. It’s pretty straight forward, there are a handful of mini games as well which are all pretty straight forward too. Is the game play loop fun? Yes and no, the story is what keeps the game interesting, the actual game play isn’t particularly compelling.

Story: The entire five episodes including the DLC all tie back into one another which is nice and if you’re paying attention you get some unique insights into what is going on. The story itself was serviceable and nothing astounding, the big reveal was kinda obviously after the first episode.

Engrish: So this is a Chinese game translated into English and for the most part its fine, there are some grammar issues here and there but nothing you can’t understand with context. The English voice actors are mostly fine too, though some are kinda funny since they don’t deviate from the horribly translated script, and others are way too emotional whilst some are bored senseless. Heh.

Speaking of bad translations the instructions for some of the mini games are truly terrible but you’re get the hang of things in the end.

Overall it’s an OK browser, detective, police, thrilling, mini game, bonanza.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor an indie developer's first game, I'm impressed. Definitely give this game a try. I believe that they'll improve over time.


The good

- The story is very pertinent to how the internet is today. Data breaches, cyber bullying, abuse, cover-ups, flame wars. There's a lot of despicable things that we don't want to acknowledge. We know that the fathomless depths of depravity exist. This game delivers on reminding people that nothing is ever truly 'gone' from the internet.

- Generally, the puzzles are manageable with a bit of critical thinking. It's quite a bit of fun seeing different puzzles and piecing data together.

- The artwork is great. It's not the typical dingy and dirty cyberpunk that we've grown accustomed to. It has the near future feel that is just right.


The bad

- The end of Chapter 5 needs to be rebalanced. There's a puzzle that is too clever for its own good. The one in particular involving the numeric keypad. Other players will recognize this infamous headache.

- The virus puzzles will take many attempts to complete. The race against the clock becomes an issue with trying to grasp the sequence aligning the pieces.

- I had completed every possible task to achieve the Perfect Ending, yet somehow I failed that. There's an anti-frustration measure. I succumbed to take the 'easy' way to solve the most challenging virus puzzle. I was treated to the 'general' ending. I feel a bit cheated for doing everything else right, only for a very vexing puzzle to snatch that away from me.

- Minor gripe about the ending credits. The transition between the different characters we interacted with passes by way too quickly. I wanted to read each of their picture's snippets.


Growing Pains

- Translations. English is a monstrous hybrid language that plays by and its own rules. As a native speaker, it's easy to pick up on phrasing, typos and other grammatical issues. I don't fault them for this. It's a very good attempt. The actors convey their lines good enough to get the point across.\t\t\t\t\t
