\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat story, love the gameplay (At times, clarified below).

Pros:
- You will love this game if you love Orwel / Hacknet.
- The story is entertaining, and the DLC leaves you wanting for more
- The art is decent
- The mini-games are fun as well

Cons:
- Must be played with a walk through in hand. To progress in a chapter, you must collect all pieces of information for a "Reasoning", but sometimes if you missed one piece it can be infuriating to try and find it. 40% of my time playing this game is due to this, even with assistance from walkthroughs. At times you might need to look at a video walkthrough, and watch for when the video maker's compilation % goes up and see what they had collected and if you had done so as well.
- Some of the puzzles are infuriating. There are puzzles in between some chapters that are timed and difficult, to the point of requiring multiple attempts on my part. This wouldn't be so back if one fails on those means you need to go back to the last autosave.
- The Dialogue puzzles, as I like to call them, are annoying. They are usually the sort of "Choose the right dialogue choices or restart '' style of puzzle, except they don't tell you exactly when you chose the wrong one. Some of the info needed for these dialogue options are also obscure or just never hinted at or explained, leading to either using the trusty walkthrough, or guessing and failing until you get the right one, resetting to your autosave each and every time.
- The VA sucks. It's hard to explain, and some of the actors do a decent job, but the low quality is noticeable and apparent. I understand that English is not the dev's primary language, as I believe they are based in China, but some of the dialogue would be better without the voices, epcialy a certain assistant in "Hello World".


Despite the lengthy and detailed Con section, the Pros far outweigh the Cons for me. I give it a 7.5/10. 2 points off for the failure triggers / systems (puzzles, convos, etc.), and 1.5 points off for the VA alone.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros: I enjoyed the gameplay since it reminded me of the Orwell games and the investigation and twists reminded me of a Ace Attorney game. The story is pretty good as its combined into 5 or so chapters where you have to find out what happened to a certain person why this person was killed. So if you're a fan of detective games like LA Noir, this definitely a good game to buy. Also it adds some pretty heavy topics that may be triggering for some people but I feel like it was a good attempt at trying to address them.

Cons: With all the pros, theres some things that really bug me about this game.

  • Story - As I said, the story is really good and the investigation keeps you engrossed to find out what exactly happened or led to the incident that took place. But around Chapter 4, the game shifts from a political/suspense thriller to a weird sci-fi "Deus Ex" type story. It really didn't mesh well with me in trying to connect all the previous chapters. And this is around the same part where the main antagonist gets introduced. And by God is this one of the most over-dramatic mustache twirling villains I've ever seen. Their backstory is slightly explored in one of the DLCs but even then it doesn't really help to justify their actions in the main campaign nor those it add any really depth to their character. The only sub-antagonist that got any type of depth and I went "Oh wow, I can see why they did this" was Lisa Snyder in Chapter 1 and returned in one of the DLC chapters which in turn made the later my favorite chapter.

  • Puzzles - I don't normally criticize puzzles because I dont want to come off as that guy that cant do puzzles because I'm too dumb or whatever. But I'll take that risk for this game. Some of the puzzles and mini games here are difficult to a frustrating degree. Mainly because I felt there was little hints given to me on how to solve them. And this definitely goes for the mini games. There's one at the very end of the last DLC chapter that was so hard, I pretty much just gave up after going at it for an hour or two and just watched the ending on YouTube. One of the walkthroughs I found online even made the statement that some of the puzzles were not explained well so I'm gonna guess its not just me who feels this way.

  • Voices - While the DLC chapters are fully voiced, I suspect some of the main campaign voices are synthetic and were maybe spoken by AI generated software A bit ironic considering the story of the game . For the most part it sounds ok but some words come out very weird.



Summary: Cyber Manhunt is a good game and very much worth the price you pay for it. But the weird Deus Ex story keeps me from fully enjoying it. Had it stuck with the political/corporate drama of Company X trying to sway and control the population through Big Data, I would've absolutely loved it. It really did not need the Deus Ex element and should've been completely left out. And when you do take it out of the game, it really doesn't take a whole lot away from the story, so its strange as to why it was even added in the first place.\t\t\t\t\t
