\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Cyber Manhunt" is a visual novel disguised as a detective game: it has a few timed puzzles and optional information, but in general the game is linear.
The story is far from perfect: it is too dark and not depressing enough at the same time to be realistic. The ending chapter is just meh. The gameplay has a few shortcomings: the in-game desktop is not responsive enough, the data gathering process is tedious, and the puzzles can be bothersome given the running timer. The stylization is fine, I guess, until it comes to anything computer-related (Of course, it is of little importance, but now I know that viruses are always written in C and bash while any database with private data utilizes systemd. Wait. What?) The English translation is sub-par and has lots of typos.
It is a bit sad, because I see the potential in this game, but I cannot recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

The game is solid for it's price. The story is very intriguing, and the mysteries are pretty dope. But in a game that is almost 100% about reading, the language can't be broken. I can understand using broken English as a tool of storytelling, but when you find broken English in places where it wouldn't normally be, immersion is broken. And having a disclaimer of "please e-mail me if you find something wrong" is very lazy; quality control it yourselves dude.

I hope this serves as an incentive to fix it, because I love this kind of game.
