\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gameplay itself is worth recommending but the rest leaves a bit to be desired. Of all the similar “you’re a hacker collecting info online” games this one does have the best mechanics. It’s honestly more similar to real hacking or social engineering since you’re just mostly googling people and using found data. It becomes a familiar and somewhat repetitive process once you complete the first chapter but it never becomes boring. It is often inconsistent that 99% of the data you need is collected and added your your tray but every once in a while you’re just expected to manually find and enter a date or phone number. It’s hard to trust what data is important and what isn’t because of this.

The writing and subsequent voice acting is what keeps this game from being great. Every character is so one dimensional that the final credits actually recaps them all with a single word to define each of them like “mean,” “snobbish,” and “greedy.” As a result all the dialogue is so simple and matter of fact. There’s are also a handful of mini games ranging from stressful to frustrating due to often unclear directions. There’s a PR mini game in the back half that made me want to quit and my 4th attempt at guessing what it wanted from me.

At 12 hours it honestly starts to feel a bit exhausting by the end but all in all it’s a decent effort that kind of reminds me some of the more interesting 3rd party titles that used to drop on the Nintendo DS.\t\t\t\t\t
