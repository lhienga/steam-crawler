\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIF you liked Papers, Please and Orwell, you'll like this.

It has an interesting story (if not a bit cliche), nice artwork, decent voice acting, and I don't think it overstays its welcome. I do suggest getting the 2 DLC missions and playing them after completing the base game. I think it's well worth its asking price, even with the extra few dollars.

There are a couple of design decisions that I do not understand however:
1. You can't use the escape key to get to any sort of menu. It will just bring up a little graphic saying that you cant use it.
2. When you're using one of the tools (phone calls, phishing, hacking, etc.), you usually can't get out of them by anyway but failing. This is a problem specifically for the phone calls, where they will ask you specific information about the case and you can't look at any info. It's frustrating
3. Some information you collect will not increase the objective progress, which is fine, but if you're missing some of that info, the only way to find it is to go through EVERYTHING again. You don't need all the info to complete the mission, but I wanted to. THere is one level I had to so through 3 times with a guide and still couldn't find it. It would be nice if they included a sort of hint system to find the final information you're missing.

Those are small gripes for a fun game. And the translation isn't bad (though in English, you'll see a couple of silly errors here and there, nothing bad).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6/10 Decent.

It's an okay investigation game, where you basically act through a computer terminal and utilise database tools to compile profile/evidence.

Sometimes you'll 'phish' someone with link to get their phone information, sometimes you'll call them and impersonate someone to steal information etc, it's all very simplistic, like a rudimentary version of Orwell (similar themes really).

So, if you liked Orwell, you'll probably enjoy this one.

The downsides?

The translation is iffy, there's a lot of broken English, some atrocious pronunciation (dahtah (for data) and yet they say database absolutely fine?), and dialogue always doesn't flow very well.

The game is also keen to throw one off mechanics at you that never crop up again, rather than focus on the fundamentals of the investigation story ~ you can also sequence break by accident, which can skew the flow of the narrative as well.

It's nothing like Ace Attorney series as one review has misleadingly pointed out ~ you're not presenting evidence, cross examining witnesses, or coming to any conclusions on a regular basis, or even at all. You're essentially collecting data as a whole and then submitting it as a data sheet. You never, ever, come to any conclusions within the game or within any the cases in the game. You go round hunting information and clues and submitting it to your boss, who makes any inferences etc, but you, yourself won't have anything to do with this.

You also won't have any sway in changing the outcomes; You can't wrongfully accuse anyone or anything like that, it's a very linear investigative information collation game ~ and that's absolutely fine, it works, it's an enjoyable one with a bit of a try hard story and bad English, and it's worth it for £5 or whatever it goes for on sale.

But please, don't come into this game thinking you have much power to change things (beyond the ending), don't come in thinking this is an indie Ace Attorney sort of game, it's nowhere near in terms of mechanics or otherwise.

Do I recommend it? Based on the price, yes. I think the pricing is absolutely fair for what they're offering. If you can put up with some broken English, you'll be fine playing through this one.\t\t\t\t\t
