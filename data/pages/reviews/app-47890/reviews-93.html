\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is still my favorite in The Sims series, but it also represents a turning point for the franchise. This was where The Sims stopped being a game and truly became a digital dollhouse.

Sims 3 has tons of DLC, most of which is pretty good, but not all of which play nicely with one another. There are some unfortunate dependencies which break the game entirely (depending on your operating system) if you don't install them in the right order. The content can become a bit overwhelming, with every young sim going to university all the time and random extended vacations to Egypt and spontaneously becoming a vampire... it's just a lot. Add in the stuff packs and all the community content...

Oh yeah, all of it is micro-monetized like crazy. At the time I bought the game and DLC, it would have been easy to sink two or three hundred dollars into all of it... is it worth all that much? At the time, it was. Now, maybe not.

We now have the Sims 4 (or whatever the next version will be) and all of the above persists... Except with better visual appeal, fewer game-killing bugs and more expansive community integration with more customizable content.\t\t\t\t\t
