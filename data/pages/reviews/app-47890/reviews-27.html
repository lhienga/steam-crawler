There's just so much for your Sims to do in each of the vast open neighbourhoods included. Throw a couple of DLC packs in there and the possibilities become near-endless. It really feels as though I've barely scratched the surface with my modest 113 hours. It's wonderful.

Like I said at the beginning though, getting things to run smoothly is a little bit of a hassle. Thankfully there are tons of helpful guides and fixes if you're willing to do a little digging. Rarely do you need to worry about anything else once those fixes are implemented too. The only other downside I can think of would be the prices of each DLC pack. While I believe a majority of them are worth the money, ten year old content should never remain full priced. Best wait for a sale on some of them. TS3 really is fantastic though and I'd recommend it to anyone who's in the mood for a really engaging, slightly chaotic life simulator.
