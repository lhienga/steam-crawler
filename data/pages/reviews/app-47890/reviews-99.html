\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll The Sims game up to 3 have always held a special place in my heart, meanwhile 4 can go WooHoo itself with Malcolm Landgraab with their greedy monetization, expensive DLCs / expansion packs, and severely reduced features from the previous games.

The Sims 3 follows up from the previous game, while the previous game had solid emphasis with its memory, storytelling, CAW, wants and fears, and the setting up shop system; the third installment does fall short in some of the areas, they excel with its open world design, ditching loading screens altogether, though is not entirely avoided when travelling between worlds.

The game introduces the new moodlet system which pretty much determines the mood of your sim, compared to the previous game where mood is determined by your current sim's needs. They've also introduced the powerful CAS system (create-a-style) where in you can fully style any object or clothes in the game, which is honestly mind blowing.

Though the game has dropped some of the features from the previous game such as the good memory and fear system, they're replaced with inferior but not bad systems; fears have been dropped altogether, now only offering wish or wants, memories have less impact in the game and overall more clunky with a lot of insignificant memories clogging up the sim's memory. I do emphasize that although they've dropped or downgraded some of the system, it doesn't mean that the game has fallen off in terms of gameplay, as most of them are really not noticeable to the regular playerbase.

The game also provide excellent value for each expansion packs offered, much more feature-packed compared to the previous installments of the series, and definitely much more than the ridiculously overpriced, greedy, and lackluster expansion packs of The Sims 4. I could not say for the stuff packs as they were the first signs of how greedy EA can be, and it shows.

tl;dr The Sims 3 is a good game where their strong point is their open world system, create-a-style, and excellent expansion packs\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've always enjoyed The Sims, but this version is so wonky. People work a little more smoothly with their autonomous decisions in comparison to Sims 2 (I'm finding that two Sims who like each other will choose independently to sit next to each other with EVERYTHING; If two people in a relationship are struggling, they try choosing social decisions that boost their moods and make things better; They cry in their sleep or hum happily in their sleep depending on what's going on; etc), but decisions and actions themselves are odd. Gift-giving is gone. Cooking recipes require more skill from me than the AI - And I don't even know what to buy for some of them! Gardening takes up so much time, and I can't hire a Gardener anymore. Maids don't clean the whole house. Taxes are unbelievably high. Burglars for some reason steal the cheapest things from my house. Random unmentioned Sims are sending me gifts daily for no reason. Putting away groceries and organizing things out of the Inventories is probably the biggest pain-in-the-ass thing - Sims 2, they could just "Put Away Groceries" themselves with one click. And the last thing that bothers me is the fact that I downloaded items from the Sims Store to install - All of the files failed. Wasted SimPoints. Good job, EA.\t\t\t\t\t
