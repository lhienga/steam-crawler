\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow THIS is gaming.

Let's go back to 2009, the beautiful late 00s. Gaming was PEAK at this point and fans had no reason to expect something revolutionary. The Sims 2 was considered nearly flawless, making the obvious followup one of the most hyped releases of the decade. What new adventures awaited in the next installment?

Well I didn't get to find out cuz as we all know Walmart laptops from 2007 were terrible. Wit no $$$ and hopes of getting any game to run, I gave it up and stuck to owning noobs in Call of Duty.

Fast forward to 2021, and I decided that since gaming went to ♥♥♥♥ I'd look back at some titles I missed out on when I was coming up. Obviously, the Sims 3 was a biggie and h.o.l.y.s.h.i.t.

This game is perfect. Addicting game play, an open world, TONS of customization, great music (the fact that Need You Know is in this game is so 2009), and just a sense of care and polish you don't get from many games. I refuse to note the crashing errors because 1.) They're surprisingly not as bad as you'd think from a game this old and 2.) This wasn't really designed for Windows 10, despite the fact that it runs well regardless. If you have performance issues, there are a lot of simple things you can do to improve the performance or you can just download some mods.

What other game let's you play as a household of R. Kelly and die from sleep deprivation while stalking Lil' Bling outside his gated mansion? Or let's you play as an obese, vampire OJ Simpson and cause havoc on the town? None that I know of.

Buy this game. Buy the DLC. Download mods. Escape reality. You have no choice, I am literally forcing you to play this masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t
