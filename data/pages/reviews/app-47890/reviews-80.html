\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Sims 3 is a great 'open world'(in the sense that it loads in at once, no in-between screens to go to another zone) game filled with lively characters and things to do. It balances Mood icons, character expression/NCPs/customization, Wishes, the building, random encounters better than any Sims game in my own opinion. Super fun!

Literally does not work on modern PC's. I say modern as in Windows 11 or 12th gen Intel Core processors. It is, I think, unfair to recommend a game that literally does not run without knowledge of modding on a modern device. Even when the Sims 3 DID run it ran badly. The memory leak is so bad and chews your RAM up so much I personally modded anyways just for some patches.

Pricing and DLC:
One DLC is no longer available(lost license with Katy Perry). The SIms 3 is super old, well over a decade actually as of writing, and yet it and all its DLC are ~$20 USD. 19 DLC at $19.99 USD plus base game, which although plenty fun is bare bones considering the amount of content locked behind said DLC. To own all of The Sims 3 game+DLC is ~$400 USD(no tax). But that's unfair! Sales and bundles exist and I should take advantage! For a decade+ old game that rarely goes on sale, bundled with DLC I might not even want, anyways! With all the bugs and no fixes from the DLC's own issues they had a decade or longer ago! Nothing new for The Sims fan, doesn't mean it's right.

Generic pro-tips for the interested:

-Dumpster dive regularly for random fun items and easy cash selling free stuff

-Play randomly to get the most out of the game over repeating 'your' ideal family. It's more interesting and fun to explore

-Mod the game to save your PC some trouble\t\t\t\t\t
