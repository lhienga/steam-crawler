\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI moved into a cheap one bedroom house with a household of four people.

Over the years, I rarely spoke to any of them. Didn't have any friends, except the car I bought. My sister became the CEO of a multinational corporation, one roommate landed on mars, and the other became the president of the United States of America.

However, I was stuck upstairs, in a (now furnished) attic remodeled to be a study because the house was so small, behind a computer churning out smut novels to my 3 adoring fans and gaining less than $500 a week in royalties- I worked a desk job at a low-budget newspaper company to try to even keep up with the success around me.

Eventually, the Astronaut got married, moved out, had a kid, started a life. I heard he died peacefully in his sleep. His passing got me thinking about myself; is there anything more than this minutia? Anything more than the grind of living day to day in this infernal repetitive hell?

I took a trip to the graveyard, visiting the headstone of my passed acquaintance, to think about how I could possibly save my future. Sat down on a bench near a pond and observed the carefree and unaware fish meander about and passively feed on algae and the like. Didn't really keep track of time, It's pretty dark out now. In the corner of my eye a dim, pale-blue glow engulfed half of the bench. Slowly and carefully, a Translucent, Floating, Dripping(???), 30-something woman sat down next to me. I wasn't sure if i accidentally ate some bad food or maybe i was up too late, but before me she sat, as real as i could perceive.
She introduced herself. I put the immediate shock behind me and hesitantly introduced myself.
We talked for hours, eventually we started flirting.
I left that graveyard that night with a renewed sense of self esteem and a kiss from a hot ghost milf.
I now KNEW what I had to do.

I quit my job.
I petitioned the graveyard to give me her headstone.
I committed a heinous sin against the gods and paid mystical people money I don't have to allow her to remain in the realm of the living during all hours of the day.

With both of our lives together planned and the road forward paved, our love blossomed. With all the additional free time away from work I fixed my social life, purchased properties, continued my writing, and furthered hobbies I haven't touched in years. She saved my life from ruin and I couldn't thank her enough. I proposed to her on a quiet beach at sunset.

Before I knew it, I was pregnant. Had three little ghost babies with her. Even though I hated children, seeing my own fruit grow before my eyes was really moving.

The president died. I didn't attend his funeral, neither did my sister.
The reality is that we were all aging. I could start to count my grey hairs and my older sister already had a head of silver.
I thought about my wife. Her age suspended in time, just like she was the day she drowned.

She was at work. I was sitting on the beach, next to the ruined pier, watching the starts orbit above me, immutable and piercing as they would ever be. I look to the heavens, up towards the dark and hallowed sky. That night, one lone star in the sky was unfamiliar, larger and brighter than the rest. On closer inspection, it appears to be getting brighter by the sec-

I awoke on a small wooden boat opposite to a hooded figure with dark robes, rowing aimlessly across a black sea with no horizon. I didn't speak. After a few moments of silence, he looked up, noticed me, and calmly removed his oars from the water. Setting the oars besides him, he told me that it was painless, and that nobody was around to witness my body become viscera. I asked him what he meant. He took his hood off, his flesh was missing entirely from his body. He told me that he was the harbinger of souls, and that I had died. I stood up and begged him to return me to my family. He shook his head and told me there was nothing he could do. I began to sob. He snapped his fingers and everything went black.

I was in bed. My wife was besides me. It was dark outside. Bad dream. I was kinda thirsty, and a little cold. Some tea would warm me up. I went to remove my blankets, but from beneath them came a flaming hand glowing with a fierce orange hue. I screamed. Jeannie stood up and seeing my distress, immediately went to comfort me. She tells me that I had died. She took my tombstone to the mystics like I did hers and made me like her, an immortal spirit unbeholden to societal expectation. I began to cry, I'm not sure if they were tears of joy or sorrow.

I do not know what gods put me on this earth and I do not know what sick form of humour they have. But I am thankful for the boons they have gifted me. This new life, this new ME, is invigorating. I knew that from this day forward I would seize every single day and enjoy every bestowed aspect of my new self. I am now, finally, me.
