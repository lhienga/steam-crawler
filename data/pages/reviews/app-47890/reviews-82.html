\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTECHNICALLY I would endorse this game, but only if you're willing to put in a lot of legwork to make it function. It's insanely addictive, but so, so broken.

TL;DR: Check out Anime_Boom's wonderful "The Sims 3 Performance & Bug Fix Guide". If you like management games and life sims, and if that seems like a reasonable amount of work to put in just to enjoy a game beyond an extremely cursory level (Wherein you'll have to accept the presence of bugs that, at times, can ruin entire save files), then you'll have a total blast with this game! If not, I'd look elsewhere. The Sims 2 is also pretty busted, but it can still work as an alternative.
---

A good summation of that is the fact that my playtime for TS3 is actually *much* higher than the 93 hours Steam has logged, but it can't track my playtime anymore because I've had to start bypassing the launcher when I load it up. If I don't, the game gets stuck running in my system tray, and it won't close out normally when I quit.

If you're playing with hardware that's even remotely modern, you'll need to manually reach into the game's guts to force it to recognize your graphics card, or else it'll act like you don't have one at all. You'll also need to manually allow it to utilize more system RAM, because on default it caps at 2 gigs. So basically, the better your hardware, the worse this game will run.

In terms of actual gameplay, the main things that makes this game stand out from others in the franchise are Create a Style (Wherein you can edit almost any piece of furniture or clothing to be whatever color, pattern, or texture you desire, which is an INCREDIBLE feature) and the Story Progression, which I /LOVE/, to the extent that it makes it hard to go back to TS2 (Which I do prefer in a lot of respects otherwise) now that I'm accustomed to it. Watching your town grow and change is really nice, especially for Legacy challenges. But.. Oh good lord, the save bloat. Even with using every possible tweak, mod, etc, imaginable to help with performance and reduce bloat, my most recent Legacy challenge wound up bloating so much by generation 5 that the game refused to save anymore. I had to give in and download Onebeld's Regul Save Cleaner in order to trim it down and make it playable again. A lot of people turn off story progression just to avoid that issue, and I can't really blame them.

Still, the animations are cute, this iteration of Create a Sim gives you a lot of freedom to work with in creating unique sims, and the traits are a really fun direction to take in terms of giving sims distinct personalities. I'd recommend this game any day over the Sims 4, even with its bugs, but if you're not interested in putting in all the work I alluded to above for the sake of Story Progression, traits, and Create a Style, then I'd just go with the Sims 2. It's still broken, but at least it's held together with duct tape vs the stale bubblegum keeping this game from falling to pieces.\t\t\t\t\t
