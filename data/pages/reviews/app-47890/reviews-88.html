\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy intro into the sims series was actually Sims 4, so I've gone back to Sims 3 after putting in 250 hours in 4. Just so you know there's no nostalgia behind this review. Also, TL;DR at the bottom!

Sims 3 is really freaking good in an old kind of clunky and chaotic way. I'm playing this [so far] on my 7 or 8 year old laptop so initial loading times are long, there's some frame rate hiccups and crashes but seeming only when using a monitor.

The biggest change that I knew and cared about going back from 4 to 3 was the separation of build and buy mode. I'd heard it was insanely annoying to load between the two but honestly... it's not that bad in my experience. It's about the same length of time as switching categories in Sims 4 for me, so about a second or two. Other than that... it's pretty good. The lighting is a lot worse than 4 and is a bit hard to properly light everything, and the overall graphics are... muddy. Everything is dark and almost over textured if that makes sense, but it's still possible to make good looking early to mid 2000's houses.

And that's where a lot of the joy comes from for me, this is truly a step back in time to the early to mid 2000's in all it's quesitonable glory. The Emo-over and guy liner, the *juicy* pants, the fur collared vests... it's glorious!

TL;DR. For me, the game runs fine, with only a few crashes. build buy is find seperated, and the color wheel is honestly a game changer in every way. The gameplay is the same, though a bit clunkier due to lack of mutlitasking, but it's still a very good game.

HOWEVER. I would recommend buying this on Origin/EA App as it's got 3 extra patches and the ability to buy 3 or 4 different worlds from Origin it's self rather than the in game MTX store, and they are not available on Steam. Also, the expansion pricing is very different and I think goes on sale more often? Overall all, amazing game and worth a check out.\t\t\t\t\t
