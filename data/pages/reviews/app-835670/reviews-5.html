\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving pretty much finished a campaign of this (in Head to Head), I have found it to be quite fun to play as these guys.

They have a fun roster based upon lots of weak pirate crew members to hold the line, with rifle infantry and other gun-types shooting and destroying enemies before they get close. They have good artillery, and lots of monstrous units which can be used to terrify enemies in powerful flanking attacks or simply used as meat shields to let your ranged firepower do their work.

Aesthetically they are pleasing. I love the look of their Necrofex Collosus for example - their end-game humanoid ship-thing with a giant cannon on it which canonically is made by fusing dark energies through the sacrifices of dozens of men. Their monsters are also very cool, such as their Mournghouls - stalking monstrous units, or their various kinds of Promethean units (giant crabs effectively).

The campaign elements are fun as well.

You can get special units of renown for defeating neutral pirate fleets sailing around the world. You can establish pirate coves in enemy cities (after winning battles against them, or every once in a while with heroes) to massively boost your income or get other benefits over time, and these coves provide vision as well. Elements like these lend themselves to you spending a large portion of your time sailing the seas and fighting just about everyone, while reinforcing a handful of home provinces with garrisons and such so that you can more or less play the pirate game (though building an empire is not impossible). Treasure can be dug for by your Lords or Heroes in certain areas to get special rewards as well, and alongside fighting sea battles and looting treasure at sea - you can get quite a bit of income needed to eventually have multiple armies sailing around and raiding/sacking/pillaging all you would like. Lastly, Infamy is a stat you get that scales up as you defeat enemies or raze cities or such - eventually letting you fight special neutral fleets which give you access to a special faction-wide army ability as a reward.

If you like the idea of basically having an army that relies on mid to long range firepower and the heavy application of artillery and monster swarms, then these guys will be up your alley. They have the monstrous capabilities and trash infantry of a faction like the Vampire Counts, to a point, yet also have the ability to field anti-armor ranged firepower like the Dwarves or such. While they can be extremely vulnerable if their ranged units get attacked (lacking the defensive abilities of Dwarves or Dark Elves or such), their monstrous units and such can do very well.

They also have a unique lore of magic, which lets you summon groups of giant crabs (this is often best used defensively), boost the accuracy of allies, slow enemies, and basically complement your ranged firepower army very well.

All in all, I think it was worth my purchase of this DLC. I'll be playing the Tomb Kings next!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Vampire Coast is a bad faction. It's a cool and fun idea, both in mixing vampires and pirates and in actual gameplay (a half horde, half empire building faction), but ultimately just isn't all that enjoyable because it lacks some things.

TLDR: Unbalanced and disappointing.


1. The starting units are awful. (Excluding the exclusive lord specific ones)

You start with four unit types: melee mob, gun mob (short range), gun mob (long range) and bloated corpse.

Of these 4 only two are worth using. The long range zombie gun mob, because their damage is actually good and their range is far enough to actually shoot the enemy before the enemy reaches them, and the bloated corpse, which can wipe out whole elite units in a single attack.

They do, of course, come with downsides. The long range gunners are actually Tier 2 units so you can't recruit them normally. You can get 1 per territory from the Raise Dead pool. So numbers are very limited and take a while to recruit in decent numbers.

The bloated corpse explodes. It dies whenever it attacks. So you have to pay 300 every time you want to use a bomb against the enemy. And if you don't use that bomb it drains 90 every turn. It also means you can't auto-resolve if you want to keep one as a staple in your army since they will always explode in auto-resolve.


2. No landmarks.

An exaggeration but honestly not too far off. I was playing co-op with a friend and every couple of turns he'd tell me about this new unique building he was able to build that would grant huge bonuses. I'd conquered the entire starting area for the Dreadfleet, including the capital where Harkon (the king of the vampire coast factions) lives. Not a single unique building. Having looked them up online it seems like there are maybe 6? Across the entire map.

It's a real missed opportunity. They could have had unique buildings for some of the lizardman settlements, who are your first major enemy as Dreadfleet and Awakening. They could have had a unique building in the vampire counts faction capitals. Or at the very least in Castle Drakenhof which is basically the centre of the vampire society's nobility (from what I gather). The tomb kings, as fellow undead, could have had some interesting ones. The high elves, who could be considered a major starting enemy for the Dreadfleet, could also have had some interesting landmarks.

Either way, there's just way less for this faction than there is for other factions. It makes the longer endgame less exciting because there's not as much discovery of unique buildings and inevitable growth into an unstoppable force thanks to all the unique legendary buffs you've picked up along the way.


3. The ships don't grow!

The central part of the vampire coast is having city-ships that you travel around with, acting like a horde. Except the ships don't grow. You need 20 growth to get 1 population surplus. You need 4 population surplus to get the first upgrade. Your first ship stats with 5 surplus and this is enough to buy the upgrade that gets you +5 growth and actually lets you grow the ship. Every other ship you eventually unlock (which costs 2000 infamy each and will set you back from the story objective on Eye of the Storm mode) starts with 0 surplus. This means you get to wait 80 turns to build up the 4 surplus you need to buy the FIRST upgrade! Not sure how other people's games go but by turn 80 I'm well into the mid-late game.

I'm hesitant to draw comparisons between these ships and the dark elf arks because I haven't played the dark elves yet. However, my friend, who was playing them in our co-op game, noted that he had +50 growth per turn on one of his arks. It was easily maxed out and could build 2.5 surplus per turn. Even fully upgraded my best (starter) fleet ship can only gather 22 per turn. The other two I've hired simply haven't gotten off the ground yet.
The balancing factor might be that arks can't move onto land. However, personally, I don't think that's a good enough reason. In the world factions conquest co-op mode you only need 100 settlements captured or razed to win. You can easily do that without leaving the left half of the map, which is covered in water.

You can make them grow slightly faster by buying the 1 surplus costing main ship upgrade which increases growth by 1 per turn so that it only takes 10 turns to get a surplus point instead of 20, and then maybe getting the 2 surplus point costing 3rd tier to the main ship building to bump that up to 3 per turn before saving for the 4 surplus costing building that provides the +growth.
However, that would still be 20 turns for the first upgrade. 20 turns for the second upgrade. Then 24 turns for the growth upgrade. So basically, 64 turns instead of 80... Not much of an improvement.


4. Diplomacy is dead.

Unlike pretty much every other faction in the game the vampire coast factions aren't capable of confederating. This means that players using them can't take advantage of the cheating AI to get a significant early/mid game boost.

Beyond that most factions have -40 aversion. Even the other vampire coast lords have -20 aversion.

It's strange because the vampire coast can get some decent trade profit upgrades through their pirate coves. (Which I'll cover further down). They just struggle to actually sign any trade agreements.

What's especially strange is that despite the diplomacy penalties and despite being able to get trade bonuses... Their trade resource buildings are severely underdeveloped. Or underdevelop-able I guess... You can only buy a tier 2 building that gives you 10 of the resources. It gives NO EXTRA MONEY, unlike for most factions, and instead gives some very small bonuses of -10% building cost in the local province and -10% recruitment cost in the local province. (That second one is entirely worthless because you're supposed to be recruiting from your ship anyway... and if not then it's just infinitely easier to have all your recruitment facilities in your starting capital since it will always be the most developed.)

So basically diplomacy is a no-go. Trading resources are near worthless for this faction.


5) Pirate coves are extremely limited.

There's little point in getting them honestly. They're extremely expensive to set up and have a cooldown timer that makes them more expensive and/or simply not an option to place for 15 turns.

There's a couple of ways to set up a cove. One, defeat a settlement and choose that option instead of 'occupy', 'sack', or 'raze'. Two, have a vampire hero march up to a town and plant a pirate cove for the cost of about 2000, usually.
After setting up a cove you then have to build the building in it. This costs and additional 2500 for 3 of the buildings and 5000 for one of the buildings. The three 'cheap' ones get you either vampire corruption, 2 hero slots (1 for Morghul Haunter, 1 for Gunnery Wight), or a flat 200 income, 2% trade bonus and 3% research speed. The final building gets you 50% of what the settlement makes.

Here's the problems. If you've defeated a settlement why would you put a cove in instead of occupying it? You're at war with them, so if you've any settlements nearby then they'll just recoup and attack you once you've moved on. If you're miles away from your own empire then sure I guess there's a point but you'll probably just make more from sacking it anyway, or you might want the infamy from razing it, or you might want the troop replenishment from capturing it. I'm pretty sure planting a cove counts as sacking anyway so the settlement you could potentially get 50% money from will be damaged and have it's income reduced anyway.

As for heroes. You only get 2 vampire heroes. There are no buildings that increase the amount of vampire heroes you can get.
Each hero has a 15 turn cooldown on their ability to plant a cove and future coves cost +200% for 15 turns after either plants.

Ran out of space... It's disappointing, don't get.\t\t\t\t\t
