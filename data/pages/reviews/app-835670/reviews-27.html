\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThus far, the Vampire Coast is rather well put together, my only gripe is I'd wish a little more unit diversity when it comes to unit choices.


This campaign does however manage to blend a Horde playstyle with your legendary lord and a regular conquest playthrough with city building, which is rather impressively done all things considered. Though I would've liked to see actual naval combat, the campaign's ship mechanics are done so well that I don't actually care it isn't part of the game. As the Vampire Coast, you go on treasure hunts, capture other pirate hideouts, and create an entire underworld with factions you're at war with by establishing coves at their ports, and the landmark exploration has actual large payout and it's worth it to go exploring if only to expand your armies back at your hidehout.

Overall, it's a rather enjoyable DLC, and for once I would say that it's worth the money for what it offers in terms of playability. Well done CA and GW.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have bought and played every single piece of DLC content for WH I and II so far, and this one is, I'm sorry to say, the only one that has left a bad taste in my mouth since the Chaos race pack. And while Chaos had the excuse of being half the price, the first DLC released and basically a trailer for the actual Chaos faction(s) (game 3), Vampire Coast comes after almost half a year of content drought, and about 10 months after the latest race pack (Tomb Kings).

I'll start with the most subjective criticism : The concept of releasing yet another undead faction so "soon" after the last one and that feels so similar to the most fleshed out WH 1 race (which got fleshed out even more alongise this DLC, for some reason) seems off to me. I understand that they're out of official factions for this part of the map, and that they can't release, say, Ogre Kingdoms or Chaos Daemons. But there are other, more interesting factions out there they could've implemented: The Dogs of War and their exotic units, Araby, the Amazons or even the Halflings (yes I know they're a joke faction, zombies with pirate hats don't strike me as incredibly serious either.) This is purely my opinion, but I feel like Vampire Pirates are a tired fantasy trope that should've died in the 2000's, and I get no emotional connection to my troops and lords while playing as them.

Well anyway, now that the setting is out of the way, let's talk about their gameplay.

In battle, the Vampire Coast feels like the Vampire Counts but with cavalry swapped out for ranged units. Two of their new units feel fun to use, the exploding abominations and the necrofex (giant boats). Other than that, you could get a very similar gameplay experience by using a mod that adds gunners to the Vampire Counts, they even have the same lores of magic (+ lore of the deeps). Some of their new units look pretty cool (giant crabs, for example), but in terms of gameplay they're very similar to most other large units in the game.

As for campaign mechanics, they feel more like a mish mash of other races than something new. They have the loyalty system of DE/Skaven, the offices of Empire, the incredibly boring and tedious waiting minigame called "vampiric corruption", the horde mechanics of the Chaos/Beastmen (but only for legendary lords/admirals), and the running around the map to kill rogue armies mechanic of the TK. On top of that they have 2 new mechanics: A leaderboard for the pirates with the most infamy (which works kind of like the Bretonnian chivarly system, excepts reversed) and pirate coves, which are hidden settlements you can plant when conquering coastal cities.
While this sounded possibly exciting to me when I first heard of it, except for vampiric corruption which should go die in a fire already, in practice I got bored after around 30 turns of it. I rarely use Coves, as I'm usually better off getting rid of opposing factions that will never accept a peace treaty rather than playing hide and seek with them. As for the rest of the mechanics, they really do feel recycled to me (which they are, of course, but I was hoping mixing them all would be exciting)

And an extra note: the voice acting is all over the place. Some of it is pretty good, while the "GUNNERY MOOOOOOB" and "maaaaasteeeeeer" get old very very quickly.

If you don't own the Vampire Counts or are big into roleplaying as an undead pirate, then I could possibly recommend this DLC to you. Otherwise, I'd consider waiting for a sale or skipping it altogether.\t\t\t\t\t

Total War: WARHAMMER II - Curse of the Vampire Coast's new faction is especially weak becasue of this AI bug on all ranged units.

Only user controlled archers and gunners can fire ranged weapon at point blank. some of squad men behind still fire their weapon.
however, AI(computer player) controlled Archers and Gunners can't fire their ranged weapon at point blank becuase their AI uses melee mode once they are attacked by melee.

reproducing step:
1. user controlled archer can fire their ranged weapon once they are attacked by melee.almost 50% of squad men behind shoot ranged weapon.
2. AI controlled archer can't. they go melee attack mode once they are attacked by melee.

close-quarter missile infantry such as gunnery mob (hand cannon) is really bad for AI army because AI army can't shoot ranged weapon at melee. only user controlled close-quarter missile infantry are effective because of this bug.

Bug range :
All of ranged units on all factions such as archers, gunner, close-quarter missile infantry etc

How to fix:
ranged weapon unit AI shouldn't go melee mode or charge once they get melee attack. current ranged unit AI always go melee mode and charge to enemies when they are attacked by melee. this make them not use ranged weapon in close situation.

or, change melee mode mechanism, so you can make the men of squad behind use ranged weapon always.


Remember. if you do not fix this, balancing close-quarter missile infantry is meaningless and really hard.


extra experiment data :

user gunnery mob(hand cannon) vs AI deckhands mob = user win with remaining 50% hp of gunnery mob(hand cannon)
AI gunnery mob(hand cannon) vs user deckhands mob = user win with remaining 60% hp of deckhands mob

this shows that close-quarter missile infantry with point blank shot is very important factor. AI ranged units shouldn't do charge and melee mode when they are attacked in melee. charge means automatically melee mode so they can't use ranged weapon at melee.

i wiil change recommendation to yes when they fix many bugs i found.\t\t\t\t\t
