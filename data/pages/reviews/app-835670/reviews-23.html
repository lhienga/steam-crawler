\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is practically the very opposite of the Tomb King expansion. That faction was all about expansion, to expand your troop options in particular. Money wasn't an object for troops, only infastructure. The new pirate cove system makes expansion unessecary and especially for the Lords who start on an island of some sort. By effectively infiltrating and building under someone's settlement you really don't need to create a sprawling empire to make a buck. The starting island provides more than enough room to recruit everything and if that werent enough, special Lords get to function like hordes and build up their mighty ships to produce units whenever they want to.

The Vampire Coat's units bring a new style of gameplay heavily based on ranged units but certainly not without some serious mele ones. Unlike the agile wood elves and their non-vegan counterparts who served as the go-to ranged factions, they have plenty of artillery and a ton of options in terms of ranged units that put every other to shame. Deadmen to monsters, pistols to cannons that tower larger over the battlefield than the Hellcannons of Chaos, they are short of nothing in the way of firepower.\t\t\t\t\t
