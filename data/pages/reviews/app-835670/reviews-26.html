This patch is why Vampire Coast is so good. These are four factions centered around fighting at sea. You are pirates after all. The factions have an amazing roster of zombies, elite infantry depth guards, ranged gunners, ghost of the depths, artillery, crabs (cavalry), flying ranged units, three very fun Tier V monsters and hopefully you get the point.

These factions can be played in many different ways. Your LL as well as up to four special captains can build their "ships" (army) bases to buff their army or build units. They can create pirate coves on all coastal settlements which means instead of conquering you can genereate income, allow recruitment of more heroes or spread vampirie corruption. Its completely viable to never occupy a single settlement throughout the entire campaign. However, if you want to conquer settlements its perfectly fine as well. The playstyle allows for you do to what you want.

The legendary lords are a varied bunch.

Harkon leads the Vampire Coast and is quite mad. He is on a quest to cure his madness while taking over the sea, He starts next to lizardmen and pirate renegades giving him ample opporunity to conquer by land in the west or wall up as he goes plundering.

The Count Noctilus leads The Dreadfleet from the maelstrom in the center of the map giving him the highest potential to do whatever he wants (his base is pretty much unattackable from the start). He does start at war with the high elves, but its up to you as to where you expand or if you just want to set up pirate coves. His favored unit is the new Colossus which acts as a treeman with artillery capabilities. Its quite a fun unit.

Saltspite leads out of Sartosa. Shes not actually undead, but rather has been blessed by the sea god after being cast into the sea at birth by Norscans. She hates Norscans quite a lot, but has ample opportunity to raid the high elves or even the tomb kings. Her island is quite secure giving her the ability to be free to pick fights with anyone and everyone. She also has the ability to recruit Sartosan infantry (humans) that are very strong early game and have Vanguard deployment.

Cylostra leads The Drowned just south of The Cult of Pleasure. She starts with a minor settlement giving her the least defensive start of all the new LLs. She starts with an immortal undead Brittanian knight (you only get the one) and has access to spells that summon undead Brittanians to aid her in battle. Shes pretty crazy. Wants to sing to the high king of the high elves after she drowns them all to oblivion with her. Early game she is in quite a bad spot just north of Hex and south of Cult, but its possible to simply leave the area or just fight for your turf and show off her extremely good army buffs. By far she is the hardest start of the four new factions and likely the least loved - her voice can get pretty annoying.

Of the four the only new LL i dislike is Cylostra. Her voice/character is fairly annoying. Shes literally just an insane Britannian siren.

Finished Harkon on Legendary clocking in around 90 turns. Its a fun campaign that you can take at whatever pace you want. Personally I conquered two provinces and then went for pirate coves around the map. The freedom the priates have give them the ability to ally/war with pretty much any faction on the map. Their locations do semm to tend to have them gunning for the High elves/Lizardmen. Upon going to war with Lothern and fighting a few battles every pirate faction allied me which was quite funny. We were all attacking the high elves and splitting the loot.

The campaign features fighting pirate captains once your infamy hits certain levels to get verses for the final battles. These give a once-a-battle useable buff for melee def/reloading which is quite nice. You can also fight various pirate captains around the map to unlock Regiments of Renown. You don't have to, but they are fun to fight and are a mixture of many of the factions.

Overall: Its the best DLC TWW has ever gotten. Very fun and gets a 10/10 from me.

Note: Pirate coves are only destroyed if a settlement is razed. Changing ownership does not make you lose the cove. Result: You will possibly lose a few, but most you will not.\t\t\t\t\t
