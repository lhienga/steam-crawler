\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, I have played with these guys quite a lot as of recent time and I must say, they're awesome. I love their unit design and their monster units. Noctilus and Luthor are likely the two coolest lords in the game imo and that necrofex colossus is one heck of a cool unit. They have some cool campaign mechanics and such. However, there are a few things I have to mention:

1. They are really nasty. Like, stupidly strong for the players in campaign. Their roster is almost exclusivly filled with cannons and armour piercing ranged units which are probably the strongest units to use in auto resolve. Giving undead units with high unit counts ranged is scary since they don't flee but crumble. Also many tech effects can give them up to almost 190 range, that is the same range of Waywatchers. They are also a horde AND a base building race which can also sail out, assault other races and construct pirate coves on coastal regions. Their hordes create growth very very fast, making other horde factions in the game seem lame in comparission. They are really strong and expect to feel rather OP when playing them. I think they should get campaign nerfs less than battle nerfs. As of right now they can stomp almost anyone to the ground in campaign. Which is why CA had to nerf their campaign AI quite substantially.

2. No naval combat. This was expected ever since WH1 was released, so why create a PIRATE faction? Why create a Pirate faction, without the mean to use their ship in battle like the older titles? I can understand why they wanted to avoid Naval combat for this game due to cost and development time. But the seas in this game are barren and uninteresting, even if there are good loots to be found. But that doesn't matter that the seas are BORING. The only sea region you have is the Galleons Graveyard, which would be the only thing to bother sailing out. Which is not much to go by. This breaks their intended fantasy a lot.

Expect these cons, but I can assure you that they are fun as heck and definently cool. I just wish that there was something more to the seas than Island battles and some loot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell made DLC for a reasonable price. You get a new faction with an interesting army roster. Must buy if you have a gunpowder fetish like me.

Legendary lords:

There are 4 legendary lords with this DLC. All of them have their own faction specific bonuses and mechanics. Harkon for example encourages you to have multiple heroes in your army. Noctilus can make your mosnter units better and with the right buildings and skills you can make your own undead transformer doomstack for nothing. Each lord has his own unique ship that he can upgrade. It's very similiar to horde gameplay but faster thanks to the increased growth from building chains. With this mechanic you can focus on building your empire or just sail from one part of the world to the other not giving a crap about settlements. Thanks to the vampire coves you probably wont go bankrupt if you choose the later.

Generic lords:

There are 2 types of generic lords for the Vampirates. Ordinary vampire pirates that have loyalty, which are recruitable from the start. The other ones are "infamous" vampire pirates tha you can recruit for infamy (unique resource gainded by combat, razing, building coves and other). They are immortal, have unique traits and access to the ship building mechanic. You will need a couple of turns though because they require a technology to unlock.

Heroes:

Vampire fleet captain: generally there are 3 types of these, specializing in the lore of deep, death or vampires. The lore of the deep is a new lore created by CA. It's a hybrid lore that has summons, buffs, debuffs and damage spells. The casting animations are amazing. One spell literaly summons a ghost ship that fires it's cannons at the enemy. Simply wonderful.

Gunnery wight: the gunpowder specialist. He buffs ranged units with an aura and can refill their ammo count. Most useful on arty units like Queen Bess. He has a couple other active skills that make it harder for enemy units to engage. Deals also decent dmg to lords or cavalry/monster, especially with some levels.

Mourngul haunter: absolute unit. With levels he can become a very powerful monster unit. He can decently tarpit infantry units and deals good dmg to them. A very good adition to the army.

Army

Infantry:

deckhands: zombies with cutlasses or polearms. Weak melee stats, high model count, high health, good for tarpiting, can hold the line for some time. They will get obliterated by any decent infantry like saurus warriors but they will hold them long enough for your damage dealing units to destroy the enemy.

Gunnery mob: zombies with pistols, handguns, bombs and hand cannons. The pistol and bomb versions are decent when facing lightly armored infantry units like skaven or high elf spearmen. Handgunners are a staple in your army. Like thunderers and imperial handgunners in their respective factions. Hand cannons are a mixed bag. The projectiles are in a higher arc so you need big blobs of infantry or big targets. With some micro and effort they kill big things like mammoths in seconds.

Syreens: ghost unit like cairn and hex wraiths. Decent frontline but squishy if they get hit by magic.

Depth Guard: elite infantry with double axes or polearms. They are a supporting unit not meant to be used as frontline because they have a low model count.

Deck gunners: long range missile infantry. Decent option againts valuable targets.

Bloated corpse: suicide unit. Does massive damage but is vulnerable and you need to recruit one after every battle. Fun unit but not worth it.

Scurvy dogs: same as their cousins in the Vampipre Counts faction.

Flyers:

fell bats: same unit that the Vampire Counts get.

deck droppers: flying skirmisher cavalry. Personally I rarely use them. I think they can have some niche applications but I just don't bother with them.

Monstrous infantry:

Mournguls: sneaky and killy but they are vulnerable and squishy. They were a little overnerfed because they were strong in multiplayer. Not very useful in campaign.

Animated Hulks. Good dmg, low melee defence, mediocore survivability. Mediocore at best.

Rotting Prometheans: giant crabs or giant crabs with gunnery zombie on them. Awesome unit, excellent frontile. Cast an invocation of nehek from time to time and they will hold for eternity.

Monsters.

Necroflex Colossus: animated ship, with big guns and decent melee stats. Basically your second undead transformer. Superb unit, very versatile. Deals heavy damage in range and can hold in melee.

Rotting Leviathan: an even bigger crab. One of the more tanky monsters with very high mass. Compared to other monsters he is unusual because of the high melee defence. Can hold the line or break enemy formations.

Death Shriek Terrorgheist: your ordinary terrorgheist just without the bonus versus large.

Artillery:

Queen Bess: big cannon that requires support for optimal results. Get Bess a gunnery wight and lore of deep vampire fleet captain into your army. Cast buffs to accuracy and replenish gunpowder with the wight. Enjoy hundreds of kills in big battles.

Mortar: exactly the same as his imperial counterpart but the vampirates have more stacking buffs to them so they remain useful for longer.

Carronade: same as the imperial great cannon

Overall the DLC is very good. The faction has different start positions. Legendary lords are interesting, the army rooster is good, the new lore of magic is amazing. Campaigns are fun. Highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
