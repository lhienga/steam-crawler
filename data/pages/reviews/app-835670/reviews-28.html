\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst:
If I could give it a "mixed" review, I would.
Because every aspect but one in this DLC is well done, the new mechanics give the faction a uniqe feeling and playstyle and playing them feel nice.
"But why a thumbs down?" - You may ask, and I will explain:

If you can ignore the fact that the "pirates" in this DLC are just plain and basic landlubbers, you can ignore this review completely.

I'm disappointed to be honest.
Not about the content of the DLC, at least only indirectly. I think its overpriced, 9.99€ would be a fair price in my opinion.

But the game mechanics are forcing this DLC to fail.
They give us Vampire Pirates (sounds cool, is great) but what we don't have, is SEA BATTLES.
You see what they did here? We have Pirates but we can't see ANY ships in Action.

I love the factions, love the handling of the DLC factions but whenever you want to battle "at sea" both factions land on an island that appeared near battlegrounds SOMEHOW and battle on the island.
Ridiculous in my opinion.
We have a bunch of pirates that ALWAYS will attack their opponents on land...
I would LOVE to see this old, decayed vampire pirate ships come bursting through the mist and wrecking havoc on their enemies with their ship cannons
But since their is no naval combat, this DLC misses its point, in my opinion.
if the devs would have made the effort to put naval combat into this game, i would have bought that DLC even for 19.99€ or 24.99€ or even 29.99€!

But without naval combat?
I'm slightly disappointed.
If you are looking for the badass naval combat that "Empire total war" or "Napoleon Total War" had to offer: Just don't buy the DLC you will be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
