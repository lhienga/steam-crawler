\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe entire Total Warhammer franchise seems to be one big experiment. Which corners can be cut? Which price are those idiot gamers willing to pay? What can we sneak under the customer's nose? Realm of the wood elves, grim and the grave, king and the warlord, they are basically all scams. Attempts to ease you into doubled prices. But they are skillfully mixed in with 'good' updates like Norsca or the Tomb Kings, by which I mean, appropriately priced products. To be clear, Vampire Coast is clearly one of the better expansions.

Total Warhammer 1 was basically a mod. An expansion pack. The only thing that made it a 60$ game was the price, nothing else about it was justified. But it was also a pretty good game. Clearly in the "buy it on sale" category that Steam still doesn't have.

Then came Total Warhammer 2, which was an expansion to an expansion. Another mod priced at 60$. Something you wouldn't pay 30$ for, in another franchise. And that's the whole deal here: experimenting with prices. Trying to charge more money. Trying to fix less, make lower quality products.

Without it's prior legacy, I would upvote Vampire Coast. It's a coin flip, clearly "buy it on sale" material. I'm going to downvote mainly because of all things that still haven't been fixed:
- Sea to land movement (and land to sea) for armies is still a screwed up mess. The sea is important to this expansion, right? So improve this.
- Diplomacy is still borked. Pretty sure they've given up.
- I'm fairly sure that allied AI is intentionally designed as a complete failure. They will pointlessly suicide their armies as soon as the alliance is formal. And I'm fairly sure they get penalty to auto-resolves. Diplomacy is basically a trap: avoid it.
- There's an entire string of mechanics involving enemy unrest and rebellions in enemy provinces. Corruption, too. There's buildings you can build, skills you can acquire. The implication is that there's some sort of strategy here that you can use. Don't bother. It's another trap.\t\t\t\t\t
