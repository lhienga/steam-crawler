\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really good DLC and a great faction, a welcome change of pace from map conquering or map razing factions.

The legendary Lords are all pretty amazing, personally I really like Luthor Harkon and his insanity, however Noctilus looks and plays awesome, one of the best legendary lords in the game with a great starting position and lord effects, the pirate city of Sartosa and Aranessa would have been alot cooler if it was a purely human pirate faction with a couple different units but I understand why this would envolve more work, Cylostra works really well with her ethereal units and bretonnian ghost summons, this new character besides not being cannon, it does give me hope for CA to get creative with future lords for factions such as Cathay or Araby after fleshing out the main races of course, one can only dream.

Props to the voice actors, Luthor Harkon himself sounds masterful, it really turned my campaign into such an excellent roleplay of himself, absolutely love his character and his lunesy.

The design of the faction is awesome, it sets them apart from the Vampire Counts in terms of economy and army composition with a heavy focus on artillery, aquatic zombie monsters and undead pirates.

Their pirate coves system also allows for great extra income without having to worry about constantly protecting those provinces or aggressively expanding to create a pirate empire which would make them function exactly like any other faction and let's face it if you want to create an empire then the Vampire Coast shouldn't be the faction you pick, you can still do it if that's your thing but you can also just sit on your Coast as Luthor Harkon and be an absolute douchebag to everyone in Lustria, essentially just sacking and stealing income from other factions which is so much fun and much more rewarding since you get so much XP and loot.

Even if It is a really fun campaign it is also pretty overpowered on all levels, if you're playing against them you're gonna get pissed alot since they spawn entire stacks in 2-3 turns, can sack a city and still encamp and replenish with 0% movement range and with the Gunnery Wight replenishing ammunition, you can wipe out entire garrisons just with artillery.

You also have ridiculous recruitment, with Lord recruitment, local, global and also Raise Dead.
Horde growth and economy is pretty solid too when compared Chaos or Beastmen.

The Necroflex Collossus unit spam is a problem, it's an awesome unit but it makes the game incredibly easy even on VH/Legendary on a faction that is already strong all around, probably the end game for most players is to spam them and that's why I've become hugely anti meta, I do have alot more fun nerfing myself to trying out different armies of Gunnery mobs with artillery and monsters even in the endgame with just 2-4 Necroflex in each army, and even then it feels like cheating, the franchise always had these cheesy endgame army compositions but Warhammer turned it up, this unit really shows that Total War should have a limit on specific elite units per army or globally, to avoid spamming entire armies of them and actually use tactics to gain victory, of course there's mods to fix that but still the problem remains.

I would definitely recommend this DLC, there's a really good amount of content here that really deserves its full price, not to mention it adds alot to an already great game that I hope will cover the whole warhammer world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have just played this DLC for just a few hours into around the 30th turn (Mortal Campaign). I have not encountered any crash or freezing so far. All went smoothly. And I have not found any bug so far (not that I am aware of). And I am starting to like the new play style of this race.

This race has more freedom than any other race on how you expand your power. You have both the ability to occupy settlements (just like other races) and travel as a horde (similar to the horde system in Rome2) but in this game you can build and recruit directly from your flagship. Plus if you choose not to occupy a settlement, you also have a choice to secretly build a hidden pirate cove in the settlement where the owner faction will not know it. And your pirate coves will drain money from the settlements while the owner faction will not even know.

This campaign play style is ideal for those who want to live and enjoy a Jolly Roger life where you don't have to worry about managing and protecting settlements. You can freely sail your ghost ship around the world sacking and planting your pirate coves and enjoy other people's money.

For the battle gameplay, while most people have complained that the cannons and magic of this race are too powerful. I view it differently. I think this race deserves to have their big guns and magic more powerful than other races to compensate the extremely weak and pathetic infantry. Of course you cannot rely on rotten zombies. So the big guns and magic are their only chance of survival. I think CA should NOT reduce this race's gun power. Otherwise this race will not survive against strong races. It's already good compensation.

CONCLUSION

- Buy it if you are a fan of pirate life style, who does not mind living and eating among disgusting, pathetic and weak zombies or creatures which you don't even know what they are. And you don't even care to name them nor that you care if they are dead or alive, you just send then to die, no one will even recognize their corpses anyway. These are worthless walking things but you enjoy having them roaming the battle fields. If that is you, buy this DLC.

- Don't buy it if you hate weak, unreliable, ugly beyond repair, pathetic units.\t\t\t\t\t
