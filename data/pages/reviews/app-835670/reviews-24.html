TLTR: From a single player perspective this DLC is impressive.

The Good:
Thematically perfectly executed. I had doubts before release, how could a bloody "Pirates of the Carribean" -esque faction fit the WH-setting? They removed the Disney bubble gum gloss, and added gore and dismemberment. It just works. In fact it works very well.

The Campaign-mechanics are unique and exciting. Some of it takes some skill to make effective use of, which makes it even better.

The new Vampire Coast factions are asymetrically balanced, both on the campaign-map and in battle. Both with some unpresidented strengths and some fairly painful drawbacks. It makes them very fun to play.

For the first time TWWH2 have a faction so object-oriented and with the tools to win the Vortex-campaign without conquering. Its a viable strategy to complete the game with a single region.

It's possible to have (and win) a very short game. I won at turn 130 without optimizing for it. Pretty sure it can be done in less than 100 turns. You don't have to make it short, but the possibility afford the game an extended audience.


The Bad:
The sea-battles don't have appropriate battle-maps at the time of writing. It breaks immersion when I engage an enemy on the high seas but my army is suddenly deployed in a dense inland forest. Luckily it's been said there will be an FLC with island battle-maps in the near future.

There is some muttering about power-creep. It's a point that is mostly relevant in multiplayer. I don't play that mode so wouldn't know. I would however argue that concluding on battle-balance is still too early. This roster is fairly uniqe and asymetrically balanced. It's not immideately apperent how well it can counter other armies and how easily it can be countered itself. One of the Vampire Coast roster drawbacks is being quite inflexible. It's got low mobility for instance, even lower than dwarves. However, unlike dwarves they have some faster elements to compliment their sleepwalking brain-gobblers.

At the time of writing a few bugs eliminates some of the Vampire Coast weaknesses.

Some players attribute crashes and frame-drops to this DLC. 95% of them uses outdated mods and doesen't know how to properly uninstall them. After unsubscribing they would need to delete the mod-files from their mod-folder manually, then run a file-verification. At the worst, uninstall the game, delete all folders, and reinstall.


The Description:
The roster specialize in an armor-piercing gun-line that doubles as durable cannon-fodder. They make massive use of Zombies with a variety of weapons. They suck in melee but takes a lot of time to chew through despite having no armor.

They have some very cool specialized ranged units, such as Zombies carrying 4-ponder ship-cannons instead of handguns for damage and range somewhere between handgun and artillery. And animated walking ship-wrecks doing melee like a treeant and shoting a battery of 6-pounder cannons.

While their gun-line is very effective Zombie-speed is their down-fall. Their melee capabilities are specialized on tying up the opponent affording their guns time to do their work.

Their melee damage are generally lower than many other rosters at all tiers.

While the non-Zombie units are much faster than the rosters slowest elements they are still generally slower than cavalry-like units from other rosters. This means the opponent can (with good play) choose their engagements, often winning the rock–paper–scissors game. If the opponent can connect with fast and durable melee-units before the ranged zombies can eliminate too many of them, Vampire Coast may have a hard time winning.

They have a unique hero that can replenish ammo and buff ranged attacks for multiple units. Vampire Coast have alot of ways to buff their ranged attacks in general, making their guns very powerful in single-player.

Vampire Coast leaders have very poor leadership-abilities, making them vulnerable to desintigration. Their vampiric regeneration is far less effective than their Count cousins.

On the campaign-level one general trait is unpresidented army-speed. Haunted ships and a crew that never rests enables Vampire Coast armies to outrun any opponent.

The player can establish organized crime in enemy settlements for one of four effects. It can be done either by using an expensive hero-action, or as an alternative when attacking a city and winning the battle. This eventually adds up to high income without any expenditude beyond the initial investment, and without any need to defend the settlement. It's a very powerful option, possibly overpowered.


On the flip-side the Vampire Coast factions have a harder time unlocking their Regiments of Reknown than other factions.

Their rites are fairly weak.

They only have one kind of generic lord, whitch is a hybrid caster/melee or caster/melee/ranged. Their unlockable legendary items are comparably weak.

They are diplomatically very weak until they are able to maintain 1st rank army strength. They start out surrounded by enemies.


In all an exemplary DLC. a candidate for Hall of Fame.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my favourite DLC for Total war so far, the new faction has multiple army compositions and fantastic LL's. The new campaign is a lot more enjoyable than the regular vortex campaign and whilst the shipbuilding mechanic can feel a little overpowered (due to other factions having nothing in comparison) it gives you the ability to actually explore the map instead of being stuck in a starting area you don't like.

I've completed the Cylostra campaign and was able to dabble in every area of the map, having bases in the west, pirate coves in Ulthuan and sacking Nagarond just for fun (and its insane gold value).

The new units give you a variety of army selections, the most obvious being gunpowder heavy. This isn't an all ranged faction though as its monster infantry is amazing and syreens/depth guards can provide solid frontline ability. Combine this with a new school of magic and some great summons and you'll find a mixture that suits your playstyle.

I never really give reviews but this DLC is superb, CA is getting stronger and stronger with armies and I can't wait to play the next one.\t\t\t\t\t
