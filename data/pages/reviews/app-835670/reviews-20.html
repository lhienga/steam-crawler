\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love TW:WH 2 and highly recommend the game in its base form. I also like all of the new DLC factions in both tomb kings and curse of the vampire coast. Vampire coast has interesting meshing of horde and regular empire mechanics and along with the coastal coves really enables to play like a pirate with a focus on raiding and aggression as you seek to meet the requirements of the faction campaign. its a shame there are no actual sea battles but the ability to actually fight on sea rather than always auto-calc is a welcome addition. all in all i would recommend this DLC if not for one thing - the GAME BREAKING bug it introduces.
At almost a month after this DLC released i am unable to continue any of new games using these factions as, when one of the factions is defeated - by you OR THE NPC FACTIONS - and they have a cove then the game will crash - and there is nothing you can do to stop that short of trying to protect every single vampire coast faction from all their enemies which is not viable. I would not normally write a negative review over a bug except that this is so severe and THERE HAS BEEN NO COMMUNICATION FROM THE DEVELOPERS IN A FULL MONTH SINCE THIS WAS INTRODUCED.

I really hope this gets fixed soon so i can carry on playing the game i love but at the moment would not recommend this as is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA pirate themed dlc without the actual naval combat which was a glaring error from the initial launch of the warhammer themed total war games. Fighting at sea as a pirate now means pulling alongside your opponent sending over a messenger to arrange a friendly punchup on the nearest available landmass... which then entails a land battle. Very disappointing. A highly profitable, very well selling game series which deserves the time spent to flesh out the glaring error of having no naval combat in a game whose map is largely covered in water.

Stop trying to do the least possible for the most profit, a half hearted attempt at best and do it properly. The game is extremely popular and always sells well and deserves better. Or just remove all the water areas and simulate the water experience, it couldnt be much worse in design.

Now saying that the dlc itself is well done. The new faction is well made and unique. I cant say I have any interest in the non warhammer made opera singer CA designed but apart from that, good stuff.

On balance despite the glaring fact they have released a pirate themed dlc with no water based combat of note still! I will grudgingly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat DLC for a fantastic game. The Pirates are uniquely different enough despite being a third undead faction. Lots of gunpowder, giant crabs, walking ships with cannons. It all looks fantastic.
It's not perfect; I'd have liked Sartossa to be a living pirate faction, and I'd have liked the Infamy system tweaked a little bit as it's far too easy to earn at the moment.
Overall though, I'm extremely glad this patch introduced island battles for fights at sea. This was always one of the most annoying parts of WH2. I didn't expect or even really want full on naval battles and this is a good compromise.
Overall, if you have TW:WH2, you should get this to add to your game.\t\t\t\t\t
