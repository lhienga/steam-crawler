\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my favorite of the four DLC factions between TW1 & 2 (Norsca doesn't exist) and I highly recommend it. Now that I've finished a campaign with each LL (Noctilus on Vortex, other three on ME), I feel like I can write a proper review for them. Like Vampire Counts, I find them equally frustrating and fun, but with a much more varied and exciting end game. They have a lot of unique opportunities and tools but you'll have to learn a lot, even if you're experienced with the game.

VCoast are a lot of fun because of their sheer unit variety and build options. In addition to your run-of-the-mill zombies, they get access to living pirates, aerial support bats carrying bombers and gunners, undead lobsters, and eventually undead mechs called Necrofex Colossus (one of the best units in the game). There's also Mortars and Cannonades (similar to the Empire's versions), and they get access to the single best artillery in the game, Queen Bess. LLs and four researchable heroes get access to shipbuilding, the VCoast version of a horde army. The majority of your recruiting is done by building walking cities on your heroes (like Beastmen) instead of standard recruitment, making murderhoboing a viable strategy most of the time (especially for Noctilus). Pirate Coves (similar to Skaven Undercities) can be built by heroes or as an option for when you win a settlement battle. However, unlike Undercities, these can only be destroyed if the city itself is destroyed, essentially providing permanent buffs or income.

The main drawbacks for this faction revolve around meta game design issues, most notably that they're one of the few factions that can't confederate. This makes their campaigns artificially drag on longer than necessary in most of my experiences. Regiments of Renown are tied to "Pieces of Eight", gold doubloons that are carried by rogue pirate factions roaming the seas. This isn't a huge deal but it's annoying to be one of the few factions to be handicapped by RoR being locked behind a mechanic. If you do plan on actually finishing a campaign, all of their LLs have very difficult victory conditions on ME. The most noteworthy offenders were Aranessa having to wipe out Norsca on the other side of the map and Cylostra having to control most of Ulthuan *and* build a T5 structure in Lothern in addition to their other victory conditions.

Going into this, I expected "zombies with guns" to be undead Empire, but the amount of cannon fodder they run makes them feel more like undead Skaven. Their best units are stronger than VCounts, but their weak units are worse. Overall I think this is the most balanced of the four DLC factions and would recommend purchasing it on sale. All of the Total Warhammer DLC goes on sale regularly, so wait for that 50% off. Otherwise, they're a ton of fun! Nothing else in this game quite compares to the scene of Noctilus riding a Necrofex while commanding a firing line of Necrofexes.\t\t\t\t\t
