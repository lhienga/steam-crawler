Jokes apart, the more DLC and updates this game gets, the more I like it.
Original base game had Dark Elves, which was great, then came the Tomb Kings which also was great.
And now we get the most similar thing to an Empire Total War army who got lost in the Old World, with some interesting race mechanics and unfortunately some weird bugs like cove battles autoresolving themseves or not happening at all or not being able to recruit renown regiments in my own territory while garrisoned after doing their quests.

As much as I hate the image of pirates distorted by popular culture, this is a very nice DLC which combines the best of both worlds: the undead, guns and free roaming the seas raiding and looting without having to focus on just controlling territories and empire building: you against the world.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCurse of the Vampire Coast is the second faction DLC for TW WH 2, and like the Norsca DLC for the first game, this is a faction that, to my knowledge at least, never actually had a definitive army in the tabletop game (Although two of their Legendary Lords appeared in the boxed tabletop game, Dreadfleet with their iconic ships), and dare I say, GW might have really missed an opportunity here, as they're probably one of the coolest factions yet. The Vampire coast are all about pummeling your enemies at range, and they may very well be the strongest ranged faction in the game. Their gun infantry might not have the range of the elves, but boy do they hurt more, not to mention they have plenty of options for mid range combat, and some nasty artillery choices for long range. Even if the enemy does close with your front line, they have some nasty monsters that can finish what your opening gun volleys and cannon salvos started. Like the tomb kings, they also have their own unique objective in the Vortex campaign, and the map for the final battle of their campaign might just be my favorite in the whole game. Easily one of their best faction DLCs to date, showcasing a really cool and unique faction that sadly didn't seem to get the same treatment in the tabletop.\t\t\t\t\t

I would say without a doubt! I'm playing my first VP or even undead campaign and I'm loving it. I paid full price and I'm happy I did because it's the most fun I've ever had on WH.

I've got just the 1 settlement and 5 armies, losing 15k per turn but over 300k in the bank due to sacking and battle loot. It's great having no responsibilities just sailing around making havoc! I'm 185 turns in and no where close to finishing because I have to help my allies get the land I need to finish. It' so refreshing than the usual late game steam rolling and boredom. I have strong enemies in the Dwarfs and Elves still plus Chaos hoards popping in but I can run easily and have no towns to defend.

The units themselves are awesome to look at and very strong once you level them up. I love it. I'm playing as Count Noctilus on very hard. Highly recommend this DLC.

I failed my first game 100 turns in because I tried to land grab as usual but playing without cities is much better as the you can build from your army/ship. All my other lords are the ones you get from research and they don't have the annoying loyalty mechanic and can also build/recruit from their ship.

Also, raising the dead means pop up armies if you need them in a jam.\t\t\t\t\t
