\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC adds the Vampire Coast as a new playable race. There are currently 4 VC factions. They are a wild, magical, gun powder focused, undead faction with sea creature monsters. Whether you play as them or against them they offer a unique play style that is both fun and rewarding, most of the time.

I really like the new units. I used to not like gun powder units in Total War games, but they have really grown on me and do need to be used tactically to be successful. Monsters are always fun. The flying creators are a little under powered, but still usable early game and one that is good throughout. The magic is ok since they have access to Vampire magic, but Deep is pretty bad in my experience. I'm not great at the game, so it could be me.

The Legendary Lords are interesting and varied. I only wish you could confederate the other VC factions so you can play with them all in one play through without mods.

Unique mechanics:
I think they did some different things here to make them feel different and I appreciate that, though I don't think they did it as well as it could have been in some areas.
I loved that Legendary Lords functioned similar to hordes with access ship building, they can construct military and other buildings that provide bonuses attached to their character, so they can potentially recruit anything on the fly(makes sense since they are summing undead, so they shouldn't need a city). Generic Lords can't do this.
You also unlock your special units by defeating other sea captains, which I thought was a nice touch and makes sense.
I didn't care for the treasure hunting. It was very tedious and was just me paying a hero/agent to run around the map to a specific part a dig up a treasure that didn't cover his upkeep cost when including his travel time. I would have preferred each chest be locked by a puzzle you had to solve and you would get items and money that would make it worth it to do.
I think if you used your agent to set up Pirate Coves, mini hidden buildings under other factions settlements, it would make some sense to have them treasure hunt and set up coves at once. I didn't and hardly used the function in my play through. I think it could have been very helpful if I had used them as a back up source of money in case I ever lost my main settlements and could keep you in the game if you get beat up at some point. They are pretty expensive to set up and would have been more viable if treasure hunting was more rewarding.

Overall, I enjoyed it and think it is worth picking up, especially if you can get it during one of the many sales.
