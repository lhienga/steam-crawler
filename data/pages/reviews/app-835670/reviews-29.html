\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCA has set the bar pretty high with this DLC, which could lead to no DLC being as good from here on out or they work even harder to push themselves, guess we'll see but I trust CA will continue to work hard on this game. Either way though this to is by far the best DLC for the game yet in my opinion.

The Vampire coast is just really fun to play and opens up many styles of play and campaign options you don't get with most factions, a lot of factions tend to make you feel pretty restricted on what you'll be doing for the forseeable future in a game but with the Vampirates you can just easily pick up and leave and go somewhere to do something else. This faction gives you a lot of freedom.

The units themselves are amazing, Depth guard in their fancy vamp armor, giant crabs, massive raised ships walking around blasting cannons from their arms. It's just fun. I really hope CA continues to make quality DLC like this and if they do then I will buy them. I'm one of those folks that really hates the way DLC has gone in the industry for the most part but if there's this much put into the work for it I can stomach the price for the quality and quantity of the content.\t\t\t\t\t

Beautiful, Fenomonal, Down Right Terrifying.

Vampire Coast has become my top DLC since I got it (Sorry Tomb Kings and Dancy Pants Magic Orc.).

The Unit roster is nothing like the origional vampire unit roster, and the units themselves are unique and open in variety.
However (yes I know) There is always more that could have been done. (my favorite thing to say)

While I cannot deny that every little thing about the VPC has me caged in it's stunning experience, I feel as if few (Very few) things were left out that made me sad.

First, and more important, I would have liked to see more zombified creatures. We have giant hermit crabs and squid chaos spawn hybrids, But I would have liked to see a Litural Zombie Crab or a Undead Octopus Abomination of sorts, Both falling under the Large monster catagory. Especially a Shark based unded creature. To be honest, normally i'd have more units to beg for, but this DLC really does cover most of what I had wanted out of any DLC or faction. Those 3 units that I mentioned in specific are really the only additions I can think of. Hopefully we might see them later, Mainly I suggest it so that the Norsca Faction has a reason to go out to sea and Hunt upon the waters.
If CA wants to take this idea, I ask for nothing but the satisfaction of it being in the game, because that would be a great addition to both factions, and would allow them to interact way more in the field.

Second, only 2 things which reffer to Animations.

Handgunners, and the Abominations. Both were done splendidly, but 1 thing was over done, and 1 thing was underdone.
Their combat animations.

Handgunners (or riflemen) have blades on their weapons. some dont.
As a first, I think those models should have seporate combat animations. those with the bladed rifles should fight with them instead of their swords, while the ones with the swords and no blade on their rifle, use their swords.

second, is the abominations. as far as i've gathered, they always have a cannon arm. and rather then always swing it, maybe they should have an animation where they suddenly fire a unstable shrapnel shot in replacement of a sweep attack ocassionally.

These are just small things that I think would really benifit the Vampire Coast DLC ten folds and I had hoped would be ever since I first saw the units.

Weither they come to light or not, I will have to accept. But I really do wish that something ressembling these ideas might occur.

Regardless to say, this DLC is worth it. I was one of the people who Pre-Ordered this DLC, and I can say indefinantly, while the 5 things I had hoped for were not included, I was still not dissapointed.

This DLC is the First DLC that in my opinion, could add dozens of new units as time goes on. As we know, the Vampire Court faction has the most "Heroic" Lords in it's roster, and while at first I thaught it was exessive, I now honestly hope that the Vampire Coast can reach that point.

I look foward to playing as the Vampire Coast, and Look even further to see this DLC evolve.

Though two questions do arise from this DLC.

Will there be a Fish Based Faction later on? Prehaps one that wishs to truely impeade the Vampire Coast for corrupting their Waters.
We know of the ultimate beast within the Depths, But is that really all there is to it?

To those that are considering this DLC and Skipped (or didn't) all my blabbering.
Get it. Nothing more that needs to be said. 10/10 Would Zombie Hermit Crab Again



Edit: I have come to realize that when you recruit a lord or hero as the Vampire Coast, you can't actually see their debuffs and buffs. Ya know like "+5 Defence" or "+5% Upkeep for all units in army" and that is really getting on my nerves. I'm extreamly picky about my lords, and i'm probably spending 2X as much on upkeep because I keep recruiting lords who have Crippling Organization skills.
This is because that info has been replaced with how they gain infamy.
I think you need to add 2 Icons. 1 for the "Murderer/Smuggler" etc, and 1 for the Personality like Every other lord and hero has in Every other faction so people don't end up with 5 "+10% Upkeep on army"

If there is a way to view said stats before hireing them, please in nurgle's name tell me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo understand how jaw-droppingly amazing the very concept of this DLC is, here's a little history lesson:

Back in the early 2000s, shortly after the first Pirates of the Caribbean film made pirates awesome again, the GW hobbyist magazine White Dwarf featured an article with a new army list for an obscure part of Warhammer lore called the Vampire Coast. The article introduced its readers to Arch Commodore Luthor Harkon (or at least it fleshed out his backstory for the first time) and his silly, pirate-y realm on the shores of Lustria. The army list was small, gimmicky, and not very competitive (like, at all), but it was definitely one of the more fun and memorable army lists White Dwarf put out.

Then in 2011, GW released a board game called Dreadfleet. While the game itself was "meh" (though the ship models were FRIGGIN' AMAZING) and it had no connection to the Vampire Coast, it did give us more undead pirate-y goodness. The main villian was Count Noctilus, a vampire pirate who was almost on par with Nagash in terms of magical power.
We also met a human pirate named Aranessa Saltspite, a mutant Norscan pirate who hated Noctilus and had a special connection to Manann, the main human sea god of Warhammer.

Then a few years later, the End Times happened and GW blew up the world. At that point, the Vampire Coast was the last thing on fans' minds as they mourned the setting.

Now, thanks to the spectacular success of Total War: Warhammer, the Vampire Coast army has returned like a revenant from ages past. Using the original White Dwarf article, Dreadfleet, a bit of High Elf lore, and some undead creatures from the Monstrous Arcanum, GW and CA have turned the Vampire Coast into a fun and thoroughly unique race focused on gunpowder. There's tons of love to the lore and the players in everything from the Sartosa faction getting human pirate units to Luthor Harkon's multiple personalities affecting your campaign depending on which one is dominant at the moment.

We even got a brand new character, Cylostra Direfin, a ghostly Bretonnian opera singer. (She's great. She actually sings as she fights. It's amusing and it really sets her apart.) This is the first time GW and CA have made something completely new just for this game. There's even a new lore of magic, the Lore of the Deep, that never existed in Warhammer Fantasy before. (Spells include summoning a ghost ship to rake enemies with a broadside. It looks just as amazing as that sounds.)

This DLC isn't just an excellent addition to a great game: It's a huge step forward in terms of GW allowing CA to explore the races of Warhammer that never had an army book. With the Vampire Coast's addition to the racial roster, suddenly the likes of the Southern Realms, Araby, and Cathay don't look so far-fetched anymore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVampire Coast arrives with an amazing patch that fixes the majority of the first game Lords; making many of them competitive in the meta once more. But the reason you want to buy this is for DA GUNS. This army is inspired by an old White Dwarf article; supplemented by monsters from Warhammer supplements like the Mourngul and Necrofex.

Vampire Coast has the raise dead and corruption mechanics from being vampires, Loyalty and Horde Mechanics from other races, and several new campaign mechanics based around earning a lot of money quickly. The real kicker is their Legendary Lords have flagships that can be specialized Horde-style for several different purposes. This allows you to leave your territory and sack the other side of the map while still being able to quickly top up your army.

Pirates have easy access to ranged units of practically every variety, making them some sort of undead napoleonic faction. While you do have basic melee infantry, it's meant to protect your ranged section. You have cheap and immediate access to pistols and rifles, with shot-cannons (Foot AND riding Crabs!) and powder bombs as your up and personal defense. Ideally you hold your line and keep shooting, pulling your guns behind your swords so they can tie them up. Next step is to move up your bombers/shotguns so they can wreck your bunched up enemy.

As a result, their monstrous infantry is not very tanky either; and while you do have a readily availible suicide bomber unit, it's particularly gimmicky. It doe insane damage, but relies on the enemy not realizing they need to shoot it. Rotting Prometheans are going to be your go-to Monstrous Tanking, again with Hulks as a support attack role.

All of the new Legendary Lords are fun to play, have personality, and most of them actually fit with the games lore. Looking at you Non-Canon fat ghost lady. They easily could of made the ghost Jeago Roth and it would of made sense. It's different if CA picked a name that GW never expanded upon, but inventing this strange singer chick and nailing her into the lore seems... dumb.
