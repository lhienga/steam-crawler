\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC was slightly disappointing. It doesn't do a ton to depart from the usual mechanics of WH2. Naval battles are still just battles on an island, you don't have any unique ways of securing the oceans, and moving through the water/disembarking/attacking coastal settlements are still broken as hell, virtually untouched except for a few lord bonuses.

This could have been so much more, but it really feels like a revamped Vampire Count faction, with some new skins and ranged units. The emphasis on sea battles and coastal dominance is pretty much lost except for a few parts. Ironically, the Dark Elves with their black arcs are far more inclined to naval dominance than the coast are.

Lore of the Deep is still extremely busted, which is unfortunate because in multiplayer it just overshadows the other lores of magic.

The mechanic of establishing pirate coves is pretty unique, you're encouraged to build small, sturdy empires that can withstand assaults while your main pirate(s) go around to wreak havoc. However, it progresses slowly, painfully slowly, and the coves only provide 1 building slot, as opposed to the Skaven's under-cities which have 4. It just feels like an inferior mechanic.

The units are pretty cool, but again, don't really offer any new battle styles from what we've seen from the high elves/wood elves or other ranged-heavy factions. To make things worse, the units are all straight-firing with a few exceptions.

None of the lords really stand out in particular. Noctilus is a BAMF, but his campaign is so balls easy that it just feels cheap.

Overall, some people might get a good bit of fun out of this, but I can't recommend it for the price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC is a nice addition for your Total Warhammer collection. The Vampire pirate faction acts partially like a horde but also like a "regular" city owning faction. In addition they have a mechanic where they can build coves when attacking a city; these act like permanent spies but can hold 1 building slot. In this slot you can choose to get a fixed income, percentage income, or you can do things like make a building to help spread vampiric corruption.

The Vampire pirates have the Death/Lore of Vampire trees similar to the regular vampires as well as a new magic category focused around summoning things from the Deep. Their army composition has both melee and range as well as a few nice monsters and artillery. Their weakest element is that they do not really have cavalry as they only have some low tier dog style units and high tier dragons. Their army composition is fun and powerful without much cavalry but might struggle a bit with low tier units (then again what army does not).

The price on this one is kind of steep but you get 4 lords so the replay factor is pretty high. Like All Creative Assembly content I highly advise you get this on sale. If you are an undead faction lover this is a must buy!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Bullet Point DLC Review:

-Adds an entire new race, the Vampire Coast! Comes with 4 Legendary Lords, all of whom have their own basic mechanics, similar to the Tomb Kings. They're an Undead-Vampire focused race, that acts as a hybrid Horde-Settlement faction. They thrive off vampire Corruption, like the Vampire Counts.
-Lords include Count Noctilus, Lord of the Dreadfleet, who focuses on the massive Necrofex Collosus, and starts in the very center of the map, in the Great Maw Whirlpool. There's Luthor Harkon, on Lustrias' Coast, looking for ancient Lizardmen trinkets to fix his shattered mind. Cylostra Direfin is a Brettonian banshee, landing off the coast of Ulthuan, who has unique access to Bretonnian knight-ghosts, and the Coasts' unique Lore of the Deeps magic. Finally, Aranessa Saltspite, a non-undead Lord, focuses on the living Sartosan Corsairs, and starts in Sartosa, close to the old world.
-All Legendary Lords build Infamy and can challenge other rogue Pirate bands around the world for rewards.
-All Legendary Lords receive random "Treasure Map" quests that have you exploring the world, and looking at the very detailed campaign map, to seek out gold and item rewards, as well as Infamy.
-Killing Rogue Pirates, as above, gives the factions access to their Regiments of Renown.
-None of the Lords do the Vortex Race. Instead, they all seek ancient Shanty Verses, and the Starmetal Harpoon, to hunt the Great Merwyrm Amanaur and become the most infamous pirate around.
-Similarly to the Skaven Underempire, The Coast factions can establish Pirate Coves, that can give income, spread corruption, grant Infamy, and more. The Coast can use this to profit from areas they might not otherwise conquer towards, and in Vortex, can beat the entire game without settling another settlement at all.
-A lack of settlement also ties into the settlement mechanics for the faction - Legendary Lords act as Hordes, building ship parts and upgrades. Normal armies act as standard, and cities can be taken over and buildings built, with recruitment options on land as well. This makes the Coast very flexible, and allows a player to do whatever they'd like - the freedom of a pirate.

OVerall, the Vampire Coast is a completely unique faction with a combination of mechanics found nowhere else in Warhammer. Their hybrid horde-normal settlement focus, along with a variety of quests and treasure maps mean you can swashbuckle all over, doing your own thing. A fantastic addition to the game.\t\t\t\t\t
