\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdated Review:

So it took 2 months but the update is finally out that fixes the Vampire Coast campaign ending crashes. So take that for what its worth. I am updating the review to a thumbs up as it is now certainly worth buying. The new VC factions are all pretty interesting with a new gameplay style that kinda sounds silly on paper but is actually quite fun in practice. All their new units are interesting to play as, and their focus on ranged combat makes them especially brutal against smaller armies. So far on my play throughs they do seem to be a bit overpowered against the other AI factions so plan on fighting them a lot. The AI is not typically very smart when it comes to managing the VC armies, especially in defensive sieges, and savvy TW players are sure to find ways to exploit their AI. However, typically when outnumbered, you will find a challenge.

Overall anyone who has enjoyed TW: WH 2 up to this point will enjoy the new content. Its a little on the pricey side, so maybe wait for a sale, but certainly adds a lot of that extra variety that fans are looking for. Grab it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Vampire Coast features some really cool and unique hybrid horde and traditional campaign mechanics, however there's a lot of copy-pasted "Imperial Office from the Empire" mechanics that just adds stats rather than having any cool impact on your game, CA missed a great opportunity in regards of the Loyality feature for the faction itself.

The Vampire Coast at sea is challenging with focused pirates of each their own army quality however on paper isn't a bad idea the feels emty and all right boring at times since you are most of the time focused in land rather than out towards the sea.. Feels empty in that regard.
Something added for the faction is Treasure Maps, they are buried treasures around the known world. As you win battles at land or sea, you may find the treasure maps marking their rough location. Solve the riddles that shroud them to gain some sweet bounty.

The overall focus of either legendary lord is to head out and slay Amanar in order to rule the high seas, no tools nor expanded anything related to a mechanic for this great beast, all it really does is harm some settlements at random while not making any impact on the late game whereas you have taken control of him...
He just doesn't harm your settlements anymore after you take control of him.. Now that is an extremely lame feature.
You have a ton more freedom when this is all said and done with the hybrid horde plus overall (normal) settlement mechanic which by all means adds so much flavor to the faction with pirate coves, raiding and that you're attacking out of riches and plunder. (A pirates life for me)

The overall design of the faction is amazing, the watery effects the unites have in regards of being raised from the depths is really cool, the texture and rotten corpses are so on point and the range focus and zombie meatshields really sells the factions strenght and of course its weakness as well.
The Vampire Coast is great at it's core of flavor, but a bit rough around the edges in regards of CA missing out on so many new feature that could help shine this DLC brighter.

This also brought about Lokhir Fellheart for the Dark Elves and without spoiling anything anything major he really has some nice impact on the story.
Yet the Lord itself seems dull compared to the other power creep DLC packs that came before it which really lowers the level of which we got Fellheart.

I would obviously recomend this new added DLC by a long shot, however its not on the same scale as the Tomb Kings DLC. 6.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the closest we're going to get to something that feels like a full-fledged expansion for any of the Total War: Warhammer games. Curse of the Vampire Coast is a DLC that goes far beyond the base sum of its parts; providing a faction that melds in so well with the base game's design to the point of enhancing and validating it to a drastic degree.

The Vampire Coast in and of itself is not just a new faction for Warhammer 2 like the Tomb Kings were. Oh no, the Vampire Coast is a veritable melting pot of old and new mechanics. You can conquer and use settlements just like other settlement-based factions, but your legendary lord acts as a horde army. Players can therefore decide to ignore settlement management and stick to pillaging to make their legendary lord's horde army self-sufficient... Or stick to coastal settlements and take advantage of the pirate cove mechanic, where you don't outright conquer a settlement, but instead gain access to the construction of a secret building there that generates income for you regardless of ownership. Toss in the raise dead mechanic that players previously only had access to via the Vampire Counts from the first game, and you've got an incredibly versatile and varied faction when it comes to campaign playstyle.

The best Total War expansions have always been those that have added variety and unexpected new experiences to an already solid core game. Barbarian Invasion for Rome 1 and Fall of the Samurai for Shogun 2 are two excellent examples of this. Curse of the Vampire Coast is up there in quality with those expansions. The Vampire Coast isn't just a new faction for Total War: Warhammer 2, it's a new EXPERIENCE for the game. One whose mechanics synergize incredibly well with the streamlined campaign design of Total War: Warhammer; moreso than any other faction in the game.

If you have Total War: Warhammer 2, Vampire Coast is a must-have.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHands down my favorite faction so far. The units look great and are unique, although 75% of the roster is variations of trash mob zombies, they merely have different weapons. The faction feels completely unlike anything weve seen so far, and the numerous Legendary Lords also have unique playstyles making plenty of replay value.

If you disliked the incessant nagging of resource hoarding from The Vortex campaign, this is the perfect DLC. Like Tomb Kings, you wont feel rushed to take the most effective path or make the most logical moves. You can take your time, explore the map, undertake pet projects like capturing islands across the map and not be locked out of winning the campaign.

Not being a part of the Vortex race, most Vampire Pirate factions will be ignored by the super-power factions like Lothern, as long as you stay out of their way. This affords a lot of lattitude, lets you become a nuisance to the minor factions, and alleviates the pressure associated from numerous 20 stack doom interventions manifesting out of thin air to attack your capital during rituals.

Its nearly $20, which is a steep price. Fortunately CA keeps raising the bar with DLC quality, with each new one were seeing more and more mechanics and playstyles. This is definitely their best one yet.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t
