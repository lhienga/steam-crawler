\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn a word: Jack of All Trades, zero of fun.

Pros:
- Zombie Pirates.
- Units say: "arr" when told to move.
- Makes having naval forces actually matter.
- Brings life to, what was once, a dead expanse of empty water.
- Adds "Bloodlines" mechanic to Vampire Counts making them less of a vanilla faction with some added variety. (Not much but some).

Cons"
- Hybrid horde/settlement faction drains gold very quickly.
- Recruitment is hampered by hybrid mechanic. Requireing production buildings to, essentially, be built (and paid for) twice. (Once for the land settlement and once for the ship) Causing constant backtracks which stall expansion/renforcement efforts.
- Counter-intuitive roster focuses on missile/artillery units yet said units are less accurate and less effective than other races units of comperable tier/type. (Example: two Mortars are unable to reduce one Reaper Bolt Thrower to 50% life in an acceptable amount of time.)
- Given above mentioned focus; infantry are embarassingly weak performing very poorly against comperable units. (Ex: Zombie Deck Mob loses to BA Corsairs decidedly in every engagement. Despite both being of comperable level, experience, and weapon type (dual sword infantry))
- Lack of frontline infantry options makes defensive play impractical, if not impossible. Infantry is almost exclusively "berserker" type with low/no armor requireing headlong, d!ck first charges into enemy lines who are vastly better prepared.
- "Bloodlines" mechanic doesn't allow for the recruitment of lore friendly LL's (such as Neferata for the Lahimian bloodline) instead spawning a vanilla VC lord.\t\t\t\t\t

Lots more things to do such as dig for treasure, build infamy by defeating rival pirates, heal Harkon's fractured mind (allowing him to use magic again), plant hidden Pirate Coves within conquered enemy settlements (giving you benefits or stealing income from the settlement).
Campaign is actually fairly difficult to gain ground as Harkon because you basically start with just zombies & are at war with nearly everybody nearby, including lizardmen who are vastly superior to your forces, at least early-game.
You do have answers for problematic enemies right from the beginning though, the suicide zombies are devastating, even against elite & heavily armoured enemy infantry, and a few zombie handgunners can bring down large monsters, which otherwise would flatten your melee zombies.

Thankfully your capital is well defended, letting you go do pirate things rather than sit in your city because of a nearby enemy army.\t\t\t\t\t
