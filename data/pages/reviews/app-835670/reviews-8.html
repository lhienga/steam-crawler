\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter much thinking about whether or not I should buy the DLC, and reading positive and negative reviews, I have decided to buy it at half price. I completed the main Luthor campaign on "very hard". I have to say it's good, I liked it. It doesn't seem like the best DLC to me, but I would say that it lives up to the rest of the DLCs, the quality being good in general terms.

About the new units, I quite like them. It's true that the final ranged monster units are very strong. Raising the dead is very easy and fun, there are many units and you can quickly rearm the armies. The mechanics of infamy, caves and doubloons are fine. As the comments said, searching for treasure maps is boring and tedious.

The pirate vampires' playstyle is quite in line with mine, since I basically only have one main city to develop, and then I focus on developing the general's main ship and building caves, building a strong economy little by little.

The game with Luthor has been very balanced, I have done a bit of everything. I hope I can continue to enjoy the other generals and specialize in the strengths of each one. I recommend it. I have not had more than a couple of bugs with the caves, nothing important.

I don't penalize the game for not having naval combat and more innovative mechanics. If I had to put a mark on it it would be a 7-8, which I think is acceptable for € 10 and many hours of play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAhoy there! If you are wondering if you should get this DLC, don't! Just get it! Everything is awesome! From zombie pirates, to huge animated walking shooting ships, it has everything a zombie-pirate fan would want!

The main strategy is to make your meelee infantry soak up damage until your gunny boys take everyone out.

I'm nearly at turn 200 in mortal empires, conquered all lizards, skaven and imperials South West and I'm going straight to the middle, for the high elves (confederated).

My experience is with Noctilus. He's perfect for those who like playing as the villains.

The dreadfleet faction's mechanics are neat. I'm especially fond of plundering resources to strengthen armies. I have made countless alliances with mostly non human and living factions, like the greenskins, tomb kings and other pirates to take on the "good guys" (high elves, brettonia and the empire)

I've heard that Harkon and Aranessa are also awesome, but I've never got to play their campaigns.

Pirates' names make nice memes :)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo another one I recommend at sale price, not full.

All the vampirates play similar to eachother (which makes sense), sadly for Sartosa this is a really bad thing since they were meant to be the "anti-faction" in this pack and fell short.

They play as a horde with their ship buildings, that has raise dead from undead and can actually take territory. So you have variety in how you play, you could go expansionist (which is really slow imo, don't recommend). You could go around and cove the world with the new pirate cove mechanic this pack adds, you could do what I do and play the vortex and win the campaign while pretty much never setting foot on land. If you "roleplay" the pirate mentality, they fit pretty well. Problem is that once you've done it a few times, not much changes. Unlike your typical land factions, being on sea only adds so much variety in terms of strategy and battle variety.

Battle wise, they're a powder faction. Yes, they have monsters but it's mostly to support or protect the gun line, I wouldn't make a full monster army with this roster personally, with the exception of Necrofex colossus which are absolutely amazing. However, if you're into pirates (thematically) you will love this faction. The Queen bess, mortars, carronades, and zombie gunners all look and sound undead-piraty. They are super satisfying to watch. It's not always the most tactical gameplay but if you like watching powder factions go to work, you'll likely enjoy this dlc.

So again, I recommend grabbing it but on sale. Full price would be okay if:
-You love horde factions
-You love pirates and/or undead
-You want a good/fun vortex map faction (vortex has a lot of water to feel more piraty)\t\t\t\t\t
