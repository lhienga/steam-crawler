\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe trailer video was way cooler than the actual DLC.

Don't bother looking for treasure, it's mostly trash and a waste of time. Rather go hunting for the pieces of eight to get the revered units.

I was actually expecting the pieces of eight or the treasure hunt to be the main goal as in the trailer, but it's actually about getting cred from the other factions and defeating some other pirates that come four times to attack the main city as one progress.

Being the fist faction I played in Total War Warhammer, I thought there would be somewhat naval battles or something similar. The closest thing one get to anything that has to do with a ship during battle, would be that one can get an ability to fire canons from the ship against the enemy, This is basically just the same as a any other spell ability.

The units look cool and there's a lot of canons and guns involved, but they're very slow moving and aren't really that easy to maneuver them around to preform flanking and such. Most of the fights was about standing my ground and bombarding the enemy as much as possible while the melee units tanked. For comparison, I've played Skaven who are much more easier to flank enemies with, break out of combat, to retreat and generally move around. The vampires's units basically just start hitting an enemy and are either stronger and kill them, or weaker and get defeated.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great DLC that's for sure. How can you go wrong with undead pirates, eh? haha

Their pace is much slower than other nations so be warned. Also as a note it takes allot of turns to establish pirate coves and big time gold too if you use a vampire hero instead of combat so pick your targets closely, because no one likes pirates.

Each pirate nation is unique from the other, some more than others, but they do all start in very different locations and each have their own flare. Luthor I believe is over-all the easiest to play because of his location and campaign path. And he is funny too (dlc video) because he is mad crazy. (heh) I've beaten the game with all four of them, so each of the pirates are ALL winnable.

A fair word of warning to any who plays them, make sure you keep close to your cities, because as you gain infamy, rogue pirates will spawn and attack you (by boat) - four times this happens. Each spawn being more and more powerful. So keep an eye on your infamy and plan ahead or your see many of your nice undead pirate cities go POOF when they attack you.

The undead pirate nations are not a static nation, so be prepared to travel all over the game map to kill/defeat the npc rogue sea armies (and any nice juicy port cities) which YOU need to kill the rogues to get any of your renown units (pieces of eight) - yep eight of these buggers. Some of them very, very tough so again plan ahead. Each one of these rogue fleets have a certain area they like to hang out, so you don't have to worry about them ganging up on you.

Since the pirates don't have many friends with the other nations, means that your not going to be trading with many other nations. So pirate coves are one the keys to success you'll need to win. The best aversion in diplomacy you get in the game is -10 and that's with the vampire counts, -20 with your fellow pirates, and it only gets much worse from there. Just fair warning. Even the Tomb Kings who are usually 0 with everyone, have a -20 against you. heh Because you've been robbing them blind for centuries.. haha..

Good luck raiding the coastlines fellow pirate!\t\t\t\t\t
