\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best Faction DLC in the Total Warhammer series.

The Vampire Coast Factions are a blend of Vampire Counts (Obviously) and The Empire. While Normal Vampire Counts have no missile units, the Vampire cost has many, and these are basically essential. Your gunpowder units are very strong and surprisingly accurate. Your beasts, instead of being quick and high damage, are slow and tanky, serving as large meatshields for your ranged fire. They definetly have a unique niche in Total Warhammer 2, while they are similar to Dwarfs and Empire in the Mortal Empires campagin.

Playing as the Vampire Coast on the Campagin is very thematic, they put in many things for you to do. Which is good, because unlike basically every other faction, The Vampire Coast really aren't good at empire building. They have no defensive buildings, and would much rather be at sea. All of your armies work as normal on land, and at sea become horde armies. This gives you a lot of reach and flexibility. In addition, you have treasure hunts to get cash and solve riddles, "Pieces of the Eight" to unlock some of the regiments of renown, the Infamy meter to compete with other pirates (Which Lokhir Fellheart Should have CA, he is a pirate too) You have a lot of goals besides building an empire.

If you like slow, overwhelming fire power, mixed in with beasts then you'll enjoy the coast. If you want to have a Sandbox feeling on the campaign, then its for you. If you like living pirate ships filled with dead bodies that wield quad Cannons, its for you.

However, If you want to play as an authentic Sartosian Pirate, and not a vampire, its not for you. Personally I'm really dissipointed in this change to Sartosa, as its completely lore inaccurate. Modders please fix this and just move Swordfish lady somewhere else.

Other than a few minor things, this DLC is excellent, and 100% worth its cost, which really couldn't be said about many of the older DLCs from Warhammer 1. Hopefully Araby gets the same treatment, your ball CA.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright so, it's VERY GOOD. If you get one DLC, get this one.

Pros

* Immensely flavourful. Heaps of fun. I know this is hard to quantify, but everything about this just oozes personality and it's an absolute joy to play.
* Vampirates have a campaign playstyle that is unique and immensely satisfying. They can easily get huge movement speed bonuses for their stacks, they can become immune to most forms of attritionthis means clever campaign movement can be a huge asset. You can lead enemies into reefs or storms then slam them with armies they didn't even realise were there. Lothern declared war on me and had me massively outnumbered and outgunned. I sent a stack to run around the outer ring of Lothern, setting up coves and burning every non-port while successfully evading furious pursuit of multiple large stacks. When they came for the Vampire Coast, I led them into a storm then slammed them with two stacks that I called up out of basically nowhere. They'll be telling tales in hushed voices in every tavern in Lothern. It feels like undead piracy should.
* I never new I could love a unit as much as Mournguls. A lot of the battle strategy is pretty static (see below) but a unit of large creatures that can hide anywhere is just wonderful. You can do so much clever stuff with them and they manage almost single-handedly to keep battles from getting stale.

Cons

* Bugs still really need ironing out. The final infamous pirate battle went hilariously wrong because I had two stacks sailing towards him and they "crashed into" each other and got stuck. Like, one ship tried to cross another's path and then they got paralyzed and I had to disband two full elite stacks and frantically call up more while the dude just went around them and came straight for my cities.
* I wish Sartosa had been less undead. Yeah I know, it's the undead pirate DLC, but they're the explicitly human pirate faction and it would've been nice to have other pirate options.
* THE ENDING is disappointing: you slay and raise the merwyrm and then ... nothing? Nothing changes. Congratulations! You don't get a unit! You don't get anything!

Could go either way

* The playstyle leads to some wild final killcount screens. Luthor is a beast, Lore of Deeps is devastating, your artillery is incredible, mournguls are the best light cav replacement a man could wish for and everything else ... survives. The Rotting Leviathan is probably the apex of that: it's a pretty slow and mediocre monster but it just does not die. I often end up seeing like LUTHOR: 400, OTHER HEROES: 350, ARTILLERY: 800, MOURNGULS: 100, EVERYTHING ELSE: 6. I don't even pack that much artillery! In an ideal stack, it's Queen Bess, two Colossi, and four stacks of swivel guns.
* Tying in with that, static gunline battles might not really be for you. There's definitely strategy going on but a large part of the battle is finished after deployment. Your job is to set up killzones for your artillery and spellcasters: make 'em bunch up, then knock 'em down.
* Luthor's Vortex start is rough. You're going to get dogpiled by multiple large stacks and there's very little you can do about it until you start cranking out mortars.\t\t\t\t\t
