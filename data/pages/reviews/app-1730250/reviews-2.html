It doesn't help that the game is just way too easy: I was up to tier 3 buildings on my first play-through (which was my only play-through, by the way), placing tiles without too much thought, and I was absolutely swimming in new tiles, no pressure of losing at all. They need to either make losing far more likely and add some sort of meta-progression system; or if the game is supposed to be easy, they need to make zoning out placing lots and lots of tiles more convenient.

That's where the UX comes in, as unfortunately the game has some nagging issues with controls, camera, tooltips, the English translation, etc. None of them are critical, but it's enough to become annoying and take you out of the meditative state the game is, presumably, going for.

There just also needs to be more variety in tiles: the ones there are are really nice and work well to start with, but after a while they do become quite repetitive. It would be great if there were biomes, seasons, or some other sense of "sets" of tiles, that could create more varied regions on the map. It might be a good way to add a second layer to the puzzle mechanics too?

Overall, it's not bad, but it could be so much better.\t\t\t\t\t
