\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played much of Dorfromantik, but I can see why people note the similarities. Here's my take on Pan'orama - it's a good game that delivers what it intends to, and not a huge amount more so far. It reminds of Townscaper a bit - the more chill mechanics and general vibe are decently relaxed. Unlike Dorfromantik, you don't have to worry about the rotation of singular tiles - a tile is whatever type it is, across the board.

Some issues are the level of tutorial - it took me a few games to really understand what I'm trying to achieve. It's a small thing, but I wish the mixed-tiles with water matched better instead of having a pond on them, but that's a pretty minor gripe. My biggest actual issue is that sometimes the tiles bug out, particularly with specialty segments that want a certain number of X Type Tiles touching them, where you can move or rotate a tile/segment around only to suddenly lose all projected bonuses when putting it in the best possible spot. It's very strange.

Things to like - there's animals to pet (mainly cats, plus some crows, gulls, and frogs) and that even gives you a gameplay bonus. I think the art style is fitting and fun. It's really enjoyable to build these huge sweeping maps of different zones as you go (and there's a creative mode available if you prefer to focus on that!). The Challenge Mode is in Beta but it's become my favorite part, oddly enough; it reminds me a bit of a roguelite, with the way you can build on past victories and also 'win' downsides to make later games more of a challenge.

Overall it's a good game that I feel confident in recommending.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCute game, definitely not without lots and lots of little flaws.

1) It's fun, I finished it in about 3.5 hours. Don't see much reason to go back to it after that unless more is added.
2) The tutorial doesn't really explain how scoring works, the basics, yes, but there seems to be a lot of extra stuff going on that vaguely seems to make sense, but never quite fully.
3) There's a lot of little bugs. Scroll wheel zooms in and out, unless it doesn't. It doesn't when you have certain pieces selected, which is common. Then, it just rotates that piece instead of zooming in and out.
4) Speaking of, "we used the same controls for multiple things" pressing spacebar does several things, sometimes all at once. Want to find the little animals on the map? Press spacebar. Except, that also brings up the paperdolls for buildings, which will block line of sight. Just...weird control layout.
5) Starts to lag even on a really high end machine towards the end of your "run".
6) You get tiles that have multiple terrains on them, but once you've placed it, there's no way to tell what they are (it's not always obvious). There's no tooltip to tell you what terrain that tile includes.

I'm perfectly happy to get 3-4 hours out of a $15 game but I'm acutely aware that not everyone is. If you would be disappointed that that's all it had at this price, wait, either until they add more features or for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
