\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's rather similar to Dorfromantik but simpler in execution and style. It gives a neat dopamine rush while you still have cards to see your tiles growing and giving more and more points, particularly when you get to the multiplier buildings.

However, the mid/late game turns into a slog when there are so many quests and buildings and you start double-guessing every single placement, not to mention that scrolling becomes more and more unfriendly the bigger your landscape is. Hopefully devs will check into performance and not abandon the development cycle (they seem to be rather silent in terms of updates).

Clicking on cats and frogs spread throughout the map is rather endearing at the beginning, but it's something that you quickly forget to do, even if you just want to relax, when you consider everything else that's taking place onscreen.

Another reason for not recommending this game is the cover art, it's crummy and looks like it was generated by an AI. Big no-no for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPan'orama is just a very cozy game. That's the best way to put it. It sets out to hit a very particularly itch and it hits that itch perfectly.

It's simplistic "city-building" quickly becomes addictive. It feels super satisfying to just lose yourself placing tile after tile. Although it technically has puzzle elements and a lose condition, as long as you are half paying attention, it's nearly impossible to fail.

My biggest complaint is that it lacks content a bit. In the future, I'd like to see more tile types and a longer "campaign" that allows you to build more impressive buildings.

Overall Score: 7/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE:
My original review was a positive one, regretfully I have to update it to reflect changes made in the most recent update. I can't currently recommend this game as it feels like it belongs in early access. It's not terrible, but it needs some work. After the most recent update I have no idea what is going on - how can I unlock all buildings when I am constantly left with only the final building as an option. It forces me to place it so I can see other buildings available. Even after placing it I will be left again with the final building as the only option. So I have two of them placed but do not fill them in completely so I won't end the game prematurely.

The tutorial, credits and the word "future" show some translation issues and I found the instructions difficult to understand. I did not figure it out by playing the game. I would like the ability to change the keybindings. As mentioned in another review, some games are over far too quickly - I would love to see an endless mode.

The sound and music are fine, nothing extraordinary. The graphics are alright, nothing particularly innovative but pleasant to look at.

I was excited to see that achievements were added until I saw how grindy they are. They are currently impossible to obtain as I am not able to access all buildings. When I have around 50 of the buildings placed the "finish the game" building shows up and will not go away until I place it. I have tried regular mode and challenge mode and the issue persists. I'm not going to play 60 rounds of a broken game to obtain an achievement - it's just not happening.

Wait for a sale- do not pay full price for this title.

I want to love this game- I find it very relaxing and fun until it suddenly isn't. I'm so annoyed by having the final buildings show up prematurely and multiple times. This was never an issue before.\t\t\t\t\t
