It's also an excellent choice for online meetings with mentally impaired co-workers.

Compared to Dorfromantik, you're not rotating and looking at the edge of tiles but still building large areas of forest, farmland, cities and water. Beside familiar quests, you will unlock buildings and landmarks which give bonus effects, maximized by optimal placement. There are more creative possibilities and as a puzzle game I don't feel the game is less complex than Dorfromantik. Some buildings improve existing land, some give bonus for new tiles, so you'll be balancing when to finish each. If you forget to check remaining tiles occassionally (like me) and don't find that final forest piece to complete a blueprint before decks runs out, you lose.

My biggest issue is the repetative music, it needs longer soundtrack.

And during the meeting,it will calm and distract you from thinking about why so many people have jobs doing nothing in office/home comfort, instead of being sent to the fields or mines so that they learn to work and contribute to society.\t\t\t\t\t
