\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI won the game. Was I supposed to do that?

Definitely similar to Dorfromantik, sprinkled with some Islander, made cuter and more lively and much, much easier. I don't actually see how you can lose this game (or maybe I just got super lucky.)

Nonetheless, I think it's fair to say it's a different experience. In Dorf and Islander, you're trying to beat your high scores (and/or leaderboards), and boy do you have to be on the ball, strategy-wise. You barely look at your countryside anymore, you're focused on numbers and stats and locations. By being easier and more pleasing on the eye, Pan'orama allows you to be a little creative, or at least whimsical. Create a large city-island in the middle of a huge body of water, or go crazy and make a hash of all your biomes with holes everywhere... Since there's an actual end to it (if you choose to create the final monument), you can determine how long your play is, while still aiming for a high score and being strategic.

There are some cons, which it'd be nice for the devs to address:
. Needs a hard mode, where you get fewer points/tiles, for those who want the more stringent and strategic experience (while keeping the current Normal mode for those who just want to flit around putting down flower fields and finding cats to pet)
. Additional biomes. Dorf had train tracks, for eg, and Islander had a few different types of fields, it'd be nice to have more than 6 different types of terrain. More monuments with different effects would be cool as well.
. A key stroke that would temporarily hide all the stuff on biomes and just show their tile compositions. The game is super cute and all, but with all the distracting ducks, hay bales, houses and butterflies, it's REAL HARD to tell the difference between a forest, forest/flowers, rock/forest, flowers/fields, or a water/forest/field biome mix.

I still recommend the game. As it's easier and has a definite end goal, it can be more easily picked up between other games, played for a half dozen hours, won, and then put down again until next time. A pretty, pleasing palate cleanser of a game, and I will definitely be playing it again, and keeping an eye on updates too.
