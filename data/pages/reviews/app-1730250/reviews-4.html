\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not a fan of games where you chase a highscore. Pan’orama might be the first one to win me over. I might make it a habit to play this more often. It is challenging, but not punishing. It is simple, but not simplistic. It is beautiful to look at. You basically get a stack of random tiles to place on the map, and must figure out how to place them in such a way as to earn enough points (your highscore) to keep getting more random tiles. When you run out of tiles, the game is over. There is also a list of 70 special buildings that you can unlock, giving something for completionists. All-in-all, and considering how cheap this game is, I can recommend it to just about anyone. A shame it is not available on iOS. I was about to get it for my wife. Maybe something for Chicken Launcher to look into?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNever have I been more grateful that I didn't spend full price on a game. I could not get past the tutorial. I know what you're thinking: that sounds like a personal problem, maybe you're bad at games? Maybe your computer specs aren't any good? I work at a tech company, I play plenty of PC and console games--I couldn't get past the tutorial because it forces you to place a forester building and then will not let you proceed with the tutorial. It doesn't matter what controls you use. I thought, "Well, no big deal I've seen youtube videos of play throughs, I can piece it along if I skip the tutorial, probably a bug, nothing to get mad at." Nope. Went to start a standard game and it won't let me place a tile anywhere. It would only allow me to view the exit menu (thankfully). I'd like to tell you the things I like about this game, that despite the frustration, it has it's fun parts. I like to look on the bright side, but until someone can provide me with a tutorial for the tutorial it appears I wasted my money.

Once there's a way to get past the tutorial I'd be happy to amend this review, but until then, walk away.\t\t\t\t\t
