\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPicked up the relaxing bundle and was really pleasantly surprised by the game. Early doors for the release so really there's only creative and a single challenge mode. I was able to beat the challenge mode on my second run once I'd worked out what was important to prioritise in terms of maintaining the tiles. That's fine by me, it's a relaxing game, I don't want to be stressed out by it. Took around 4 hours of normal playing as well so it's not quickly over which is nice. I guess I'd like to see the bonuses for placement feel a bit more intuitive, I think either a tooltip on the title or just making it so placing things near other tiles of the same type made more sense than just trying out all the available positions until you find a decent number. The music is great and the graphics are cute but well made. Petting animals gives extra points but is just fun in general.

I'm hoping what we get is some new aesthetics (different lands with different themes even if they are just reskins), some new tile types, maybe some ways to play about removing tiles or converting them from one type to another and having some starter maps that have preset tiles on them to try and aim for or have to build around. There's definitely a good thing at the base gameplay level that should be explored and built upon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think the visuals and aesthetics are top notch, watching the cities grow and clicking on cute little animals is fun. It's a nice chill game--my main critique of it is that I wish it had more happening. Some sort of strategy, maybe events you have to react to, or even just watching a city or mountain get REALLY huge (they reach a level and then stop visually changing no matter what you add to that area). Maybe add more animals to search for with special bonuses (like deer in the forests or rats in town), maybe make it multiplayer and each player is taking over tiles from each other... I don't know, it felt like I ran out of things to do.

Because basically once I figured out the mechanics of the game it was just 2-3 hours of doing the same things over and over until I won. So I'd definitely pull it up to chill and just relax again at some point in the future, but there isn't much motivation to sit through an ENTIRE game again from start to finish. You get the point after the first hour!

Overall I'd recommend it with the above points in mind. I did still enjoy it and like I said, the visuals are top notch.\t\t\t\t\t
