\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would consider my playtime of 16 hours a completed game. Throughout its short runtime however I came back to write this review several times only to give up half way through unsatisfied that what I had written best encapsulated this game. With fewer and fewer words every time all I can cogently say about this game is that it is a wonderfully relaxing (at first) hex-puzzle map builder that serves a great distraction with zero aggravation to play.

Where many similar city-build style games require resource generation to permit building or upgrading of technology, Pan'orama has you balance tile combinations resulting in optimizing point sums. There is also no parliamentary decision making to please the people, or warring marauders or need to reinforce buildings or armies. It is a peaceful game requiring no resource management other than choosing when and where to play the next of the same six basic tiles you are provided throughout. Once you reach the high-point threshold from expanding your city outward, you can build a new special structure which adds to the value of surrounding areas. This provides bonus points and generates tiles to be played. Once you run out of tiles your game is over.

As the game progresses however, poor move choice overtime can stunt your available tiles and how you play long term determines whether or not you will see the end of the game which happens after all 52 structures are completed. Accomplishing my win happened on my third attempt but also took nearly 7 hours with breaks. Best of all, winning or losing is not contingent on what little RNG this game does implement. The success or failure of the game hinges entirely on the players ability to plan and anticipate potential best case scenario outcomes of tiles.

My only two criticisms are that the amount of game we get for the price we pay isn't particularly great. The game would be best valued for $7 or less because once you do accomplish your win, there really isn't much to encourage you to keep playing. As joyful and relaxing as the rest of the game is, the tension certainly builds when you know you are close to the end. Overthinking and over planning can lock up your mind especially when you know you are always only a few poorly placed tiles away from a total collapse. Although snatching that victory from the jaws of defeat feels so rewarding this is a game that when you do win you are happy to retire but fond of your overall experience.

9/10.\t\t\t\t\t
