\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played through this game a few times (though only once to its conclusion), and it's just a very cozy, relaxing game to play. If you go in familiar to the game you can try and almost min/max your cottages to get the most value out of them the fastest, or you can take your time with the game, do less, and get the same result in the end over more days. You're not pushed to do one over the other.

Personally, I enjoy the early stages of this game more. Once the game introduces food management into things, there's a level of tedium that comes into play for sure, but it's manageable. Though I will say that having to manage the heat level of the cottages has never seemed to work all that well (though I believe there were at least some fixes to it since I last got that far into the game). Eventually you are able to get these things automated, which helps -immensely-.

The story works well with the rest of the game, though like many others, I'll admit that the ending of it left me a fair bit unsatisfied.

Overall I think this is a very nice game, and I've definitely gotten my money's worth out of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBear and Breakfast is a good example of why Steam could benefit from an option between Recommend and Not Recommend. I'm enjoying the game overall, so I "recommend" it more than not, but there are definitely some issues.

-------------------------
- Pros
-------------------------
Despite the game's issues (see below), it is genuinely fun and the progression feels well paced. The quests for each location are reasonable, and it never feels like the game suddenly asks for something too complex too soon

Other than cooking, it's relaxing to play. The days are long and sleeping is optional, so you have lots of time to collect resources, craft, build, decorate, do quests, etc. This is one of the few games I've played with a day/night cycle that didn't make me feel like I'm running out of time.

I like that fast travel is available between locations by repairing bus stops. It makes the locations feel like they're being built up again, and guests tend to leave trash behind at the stops.

Trash is a currency to buy decorations from raccoons, gold is a currency to buy blueprints and renovate the inns

Cute art style

-------------------------
- Cons
-------------------------

Bugs:
The game has frozen on me 5 times, requiring a restart each time
I got stuck in an animation that prevented me from interacting with objects and had to restart the game
Game got stuck at 30fps for about 5 in game hours
Game crashed once (although this fixed the fps problem) (UPDATE: game has crashed 5-6 times since)
(Controller/steam deck only): when editing a room, the UI says to use LT to toggle between removing and adding space. It's actually mapped to RT

QOL issues:
Controls on steam deck are weird and unintuitive. The worst are the controls for cooking. The controls make cooking a frustrating, tedious chore that almost ruined the game for me. The game was obviously designed for keyboard and mouse only, but since it's been ported to Switch and now PS4/5, I figured that a good controller layout would have been figured out
Can't manually save the game
No sorting for furniture or decorations you can buy, so as you unlock new things to buy you have to start wading through an ocean of decore
No sorting for furniture you can craft. You end up being able to craft furniture for bedrooms, bathrooms, kitchens, etc, as well as different levels of quality for each room type. But you can't sort through them, so if you want to craft the highest quality dining room set available, you have to scroll through every single crafting recipe to get there....

Other:
NPCs feel useless when they aren't actively involved in a quest. They don't have new dialogue, don't help you, don't trade or sell with you, etc. They just look cute while being empty, like decorations

Like I said, I like the game overall, but maybe wait for a sale to purchase\t\t\t\t\t
