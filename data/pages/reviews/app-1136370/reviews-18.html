\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy feelings about the game are mainly neutral, leaning negative. I went into this game expecting more of an emphasis on customization, crafting, and management elements. While I liked some elements, my main issues with the game are the linear progression, limited customizability, and player character.

Linear Progression:
Another review said that they felt there is only one "right way" to complete the game, and that echoes my feelings exactly. For example, the quests may require a certain number of bedrooms at a certain level. The quests are required to complete before additional furniture or locations unlock, essentially holding your hand through how the resort must operate. Additionally, each resort is limited in the special room types offered. I might be able to tolerate this if each resort were themed or had a coherent style, but this is not the case, mainly because of the limited customizability.

Limited Customizability:
You are able to change the size and shape of rooms, alter the placement of furniture, and add additional decorations throughout the resort locations. That all sounds great, but is essentially the limit of the game. Each level of room only has one style of furniture with very few (if any) matching decor accessories. The baroque-leaning higher level rooms do not look good with the bulk of the decor offerings, which have a strong rustic wood cabin feel. Because of the forced progression, you must pack as much floorspace as possible with decorations, which would be an interesting management task to balance if there were more matching pieces for each style of room. I would love to make the A24 resort look like a retro motel, but nope, it looks like a wood cabin with baroque bedrooms. I would love to make Highlake a Shining-esque midcentury nightmare but nope, it is also a wood cabin with baroque bedrooms.

Player Character:
This is a personal gripe more than an actual criticism of the game, but I felt that playing as "Hank" was unnecessary. I would love to make my own character, name them, and dress them up. Hank has no personality and no back-story. There are some very light outfit collecting and dress-up elements, and I feel it is a shame that they are so minor, because the bear is very very cute and should be focused on more. Since I not only have to watch Hank move around, but can also see him during dialogue, I think this is such a missed opportunity to add some more customizability.

Overall, I will complete the game, but the customization elements need a major upgrade before I could confidently recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
