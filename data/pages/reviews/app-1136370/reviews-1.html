\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not a bad game, but it's not an exceptionally good game. If you enjoy low-energy resource management and foraging, this is a good way to scratch that itch for a little while. The story and the structure, however, is a bit lacking. The majority of the plot is backloaded, meaning you'll be about 30 hours in before you get anything beyond vague implications, and then you'll get an entire plot's worth of revelations for questions you didn't even know you were supposed to be asking dumped on you at once.

The worldbuilding and characters are all promising and interesting, but there's not much to it beyond interesting concepts. You are given best friends who you more or less immediately stop talking to, and stop having anything beyond their one mandatory line to say, you cannot make friends or progress any sort of relationship or story with these otherwise very interesting characters, and it makes the whole game feel a bit underbaked and lopsided. Ideally, the story would be spread throughout the story progression, and the questions planted early and given time to grow.

It's not a bad game! The story, when you get there, is interesting, but not quite enough to make up for the time it takes to reach it if you don't already enjoy the resource and hotel management simulation. If you do like resource and hotel management simulation, absolutely, give it a shot, you will probably have fun.\t\t\t\t\t
