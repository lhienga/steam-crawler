\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a wonderful game. The art style is great, the writing is fun and the characters are likable.

What's that? You thought this was a game about running accommodations for people? Well it's that too.
The core gameplay of fixing up and running/designing the lodgings is fun too.

At first I was annoyed to learn that I'd be managing multiple properties, but after I got into it I realized it was an excellent move; the game uses this as an in-game tutorial, by working you up to more complex tasks and larger areas to build in. And the location variety really keeps the gameplay more interesting. (Plus, yes, you do acquire mechanisms to help deal with the building workload.)

Some minor nitpicks I have; not having your scrolling inventory stay scrolled down to where you had it last time you had it open, and they could stand to have a bit better sorting features to find what you're looking for in some places, like searching by item value/decoration/etc. in your inventory, or the list of recipes, or Took's "shop" (though I understand being jumbled together there fits, thematically, the non-searchable/sortable recipes don't).

There is a lot more to this game than I was expecting. I think the devs have done a great job, and I'm very pleased I picked it up.\t\t\t\t\t
