\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe quick version: The story has me intrigued and the environments are vibrant and detailed. If you like building, decorating, and managing BnBs, then you'll have a pretty good time, but be aware of the tedium, maybe one or two annoying bugs, and the fact that we're currently waiting for the story to be finished.

The longer version:
I enjoyed my time with this game, it's very relaxing and pleasant with a nice sense of exploration and management, and I enjoyed customizing and decorating every room and location. However, there are a few grievances I hold that I would want to see resolved.

I wish the UI for managing the resorts was a little more intuitive, like I want to be able to see more easily whether or not the room a guest is staying at fulfills all their requirements, instead of having to click multiple buttons and go in and out of several menu screens just to check on each individual guest. It's not too major of a thing, probably more like a nitpick, but it did bother me a few times. One particularly annoying thing is that there's currently a bug where some guests will want to see a certain amount of points in a stat for a room, but that amount is physically unreachable by the player (note: this only affects the endgame, with the last resort currently in the game). Also, there are a lot of points where the game felt like it dragged due to the sheer tedium and repetitiveness of the day to day gameplay. Some quests will have what feel like needlessly lengthy requirements, and when I say lengthy I don't mean a large involved quest spanning hours of adventure, I mean the kind of lengthy where you have to wait 5 in game days for enough guests to go through your establishment to fulfill what would otherwise be an extremely simple quest. This normally isn't a problem since the game has enough stuff for you to do that the time will pass normally while you're off accomplishing other things, but it's especially noticable in the end game, which brings me to my next point.

This may be a particular sticking point for some, but the story has yet to be finished. At a certain point in the game, you get all these clues of something stranger going on and hints of a larger journey ahead of you, and then the game runs out of content. To be fair, the developers have already released a roadmap stating that this would be resolved with a large update coming later this year, but I figure it's worth mentioning for those looking to sink their teeth into this game.

Even with all that, I do recommend Bear and Breakfast as it's a very chill game with a fun and quirky premise, and I did thoroughly enjoy my time with it. Something you'll probably be able to notice from all the screenshots and trailers is that the world and its visuals are wonderfully drawn and packed full of detail to really make it feel like a vibrant environment. The characters all have their fun little quirks to them, and I did get a genuine sense of intrigue from listening to the characters talk about the world, the events that took place, and where things might go next. I'll be looking forward to returning to this whenever it is that the content update releases so that I can keep managing more resorts and see the story to its end.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBear and Breakfast is a life sim/management game that was supposed to be cute and cozy (which is definitely was!) that turned into an endless cycle of tasks and moving from place to place to collect resources such as crafting materials and ingredients for cooking.

In all honesty, it was a slow start but as you progress, it gets busier.
Personally I enjoyed the game as a whole. I loved the concept. The artwork was adorable and the music was chill. The main story was interesting. Some of the quest stories kind of left loose ends that were a little unsatisfying but I guess the point was to have the player make their own subjective assumptions.

I guess I can admit that some of the tasks were overly tedious and nearly impossible to complete without having to sacrifice something for another thing to work. I will not say what because I'd like for other people to figure it out on their own cause eventually they will with time...literally.

Overall, creating my own space and organizing the structure and decorative items to meet certain criteria was a blast. I live for these types of games lol.
This game was ridiculously addicting to me and I found myself playing this for 8 hours straight at a time.
Having completed this game at 100%, I'd say this game is worth the play and money.

9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was kind of a disappointment! I'd looked forward to it, and it looked adorable, but the game is so railroaded down to the dialogue that I didn't feel like I was doing anything. The art is indeed adorable, and the characters are quite cute, but I found it hard to have any fun when I was following quests so rigidly. Perhaps it gets better when you've played for longer - maybe the quests peter out? I didn't stick around long enough to find out.

I also have a complaint about the story; the first section of the game ends up not mattering at all, since 'Hank' gets sidetracked by this hotel quest for an indeterminate amount of time, which was frustrating as a player who had gotten invested in that story. It was also advertised that the hotel-goers will respond to the fact that you are a bear, for example, but beyond a repetitive animation not much happens, and it had no actual consequences in the game.

A very cute idea, but sadly not executed well enough to keep me interested.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far, I've been enjoying it a lot.

You're a bear named Hank, and TLDR; your job is hosting bed and breakfast at many different locations (and even more to come!) You can customize your establishments to your hearts needs. You start with only being able to build bedrooms, but as you progress, you unlocks rooms like bathroom, kitchen, dining room, bar and many others. As you unlock kitchen, you of course, also unlock cooking!

Beginning is kind of a slow one, especially since you're short on money for a while but when you get the motel nice and running, it becomes a much more nicer experience since you have cash flowing to you every day. I love the unique look of the game and my favourite thing is the way the animals talk. Can't describe it, you just have to hear it.

There is also NPC:s and some sort of a story that I'm not going to comment at all, because to be honest, I have just skipped all of the dialogue. :D

Something negative has to be brought to the table, and it's in the controls. I find them kind of janky, both on mouse&keyboard and controller. Especially in cutscene dialogue options, and any type of menus. Especially on controller, building rooms easily took me a few hours to learn how to do efficiently and not accidentally closing the menu I needed. There is of course controller button mappings on the HUD, but I use a PS4 controller, not and XBOX, and I've never used an XBOX controller so when the screen tells me to press Y or A, I have no clue what button to press. But this negative notice about jankiness might just be my own r trd-ness.

If you enjoy decorating rooms, collecting resources, crafting, cooking and overall adorable games, I highly recommend that you get Bear & Breakfast. Very chill and laid back game. If you're an Animal Crossing, Stardew Valley and/or The Sims enjoyer, I bet that you will love this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very adorable and cozy game and Hank is absolutely delightful. If you are casually playing this game and taking some time to decorate and build your inns, I would say this gives you a solid 40-50 hours to finish the main/side quest and achievements. The story gets suddenly really deep at the end, but it was still interesting nonetheless.

The main meat of this game is in the early game, when you have to keep gathering materials (save the mahogany wood!!) and tend to the kitchen. After you unlock the autonomous services that manage the food and heat for your inns, the only thing left really is to optimize your inn rooms and decorate, decorate and decorate. So it definitely falls off and there is no late game content, but this game is definitely for you if you love management and decorating games!

On the side note there are some ongoing bugs and here are the ones I noticed most:
> Sometimes guests will ask for a bathroom of score of 42 when 36 is the highest
> Guests will complain about the food score even if Julia is turned on and its high enough to satisfy them - just go into her management options and click save changes even if you didn't change anything\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my new favorite game! I love that the game is centered around helping the local community and environment while still being a decorating game. You collect trash as a form of currency and fix up abandoned buildings to help the local community thrive again in this cute little forest town! The art style is super cute and the story is captivating. I highly recommend this game especially if you love decorating/fixer upper games. Plus, I would say this game is beginner friendly since you can keep the tutorial up the entire game as needed and the controls are very basic.

My only "complaint" is that you cant place furniture outside their designated rooms. For example, a couch can't be placed outside of a lounge room and not all buildings let you build lounges. However, since the game is heavily reliant on decoration, comfort, hygiene, and many other things kept on a point system I understand (which makes sense once you get into the game).

I'm about 15 hours into the game play and still have a good bit more to go in terms of the story and everything which is awesome. I hate it when cute games only provide a few hours of game play and so far this game is doing a great job to provide long term fun!\t\t\t\t\t
