\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo cute and fun! This game is great for anyone who just wants a wholesome, low-energy game that doesn't require a lot of stress or grinding. The character designs are adorable, and the main character Hank is so lovable and sweet! It unfortunately can be buggy at times which is frustrating. I've had to close it three times and restart it because of bugs (once I was stuck in bathroom building mode where I could not place furniture and was stuck in a purgatory of creating new bathrooms over and over, unable to escape, then I threw something away and could not dismiss the "Throw Away" popup, and once I had two guests stuck at the bus stop who seemed to travel with me and would not check out because they were stuck)

I understand the devs are actively working on resolving bugs (and integrating controller compatibility which I am very excited for!), so I hope the little bugs here and there get addressed. One MAJOR flaw that is rather annoying is when you go to build it automatically lowers the walls both in build mode and play mode, but the icon for raised walls remains the same so every single time you enter and exit build mode you have to click the button three times to return to raised walls. I would love if the devs would address the wall glitch, as well as maybe add an option to only show the back walls much like Sims, because it can be frustrating trying to add paintings and wall decor onto a wall when the front of the building is in the way! I would love to be able to see the walls ONLY INSIDE the buildings!

Another downside which has nothing really to do with the game itself is that though this is very wholesome and cute, I wanted to be able to share the joy of this cute building game with my daughter, but despite its charming exterior it can be inappropriate at times with swearing, jokes about hiding dead bodies, and jokes about gonorrhea. It's really a shame, because she loves animals and other than the writing the game hits all the marks for something little ones would love to play, but sadly I think in the attempt to stand on in quirkiness and add a little shock value of adorable animals talking about STDs, the developers lost touch with a key demographic of plays under 13. I understand this is rated 10+ for crude humor, but as the crude humor really does not add anything to the plot, it really could do without it.

Love this game, hate the bugs, hate the unnecessary inappropriateness even more. I'd rack open the game and replace the text if I could LUL\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBear and Breakfast is a game about playing as Hank, a young bear, who has found his calling to be an owner and manager of several bed and breakfast places around his forest home while also having memories of the nightmares they had once (maybe more) and introduced at the start of the game.

It is a good game as you collect resources and design rooms that can satisfy the needs of your guests. With more 'management' missions you complete, the more requirements the guests expect of you and you are pushed to build and decorate your BnBs with comfort and design including other facilities that they may ask for. I use the term management lightly because you don't really 'manage' especially at the end of the game.

It starts very slow but also ends very slow and the downside I always have with this game is waiting for the management quests to complete with nothing you can do but collect resources. I ended up waiting and idling for several in game days just to progress. I just wish there are story elements being sprinkled all over as you wait.

I just wish there is an "okay" verdict for the review. It is a good CASUAL 'management' game with tiny sprinkles of story. However, I'd recommend getting the game if you want something relaxing and chilling to play instead of a full on management game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is good but its not perfect. If your looking for a light game with a quirky set of characters, some low stress management systems and an adorable bear, this game is great. There's so much to like, I was a bit disappointed I couldn't love it though. There's some big QoL improvements that are sorely needed (For example - ability to sell, or at least rapidly trash multiple items, ability to select multiple items, ability to send all of one type of item back and forth).

I really like the characters and the story but I think it needs a bit more polish to tie everything together. Quests don't seem to flow nicely into one another. Sometimes its a bit confusing where to go next. Sometimes the game will highlight exactly who you need to talk to next, and sometimes you have to hunt around to continue a quest line.

The pacing could also do with some polish. The actual management didn't seem to really kick in until the last location. Some important resources are in tiny amounts that it can become very repetitive each day collecting them, particularly with the long load times between areas.

Overall, I had a nice time with it. *Content bear noises*\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a pretty relaxing little game. That being said, there are some things I have issues with.

- It's a pretty short game, which is both a pro and a con. PRO: You can quickly finish the story your first round and then focus more on finessing a replay. CON: It can get repetitive as you get further in.

- Skip dialogue should be available after completing the story once.

- Valuables are so difficult to find, unless you hire Wade's services.

- There isn't too much variety in the design elements. For example, there is only one wall lighting.

- Cooking. Auto-fill cards should also load ingredients/amount of dishes wanted.

- This is a personal belief, but there should be an item that shows where treasure chests are.

- For endgame, you cannot reach hygiene requirement for Hygienic guests in Pinefall (as of 9/10/2022).

- The cost for helpers is too expensive, even as a luxury feature. Almost no profit with everything turned on.

- Getting early game resorts to Level 7 seems to be extremely difficult.

- WHY IS IPSWITCH'S PINEFALL STORE SO FAR AWAY!?

- Personal opinion, available quests should be marked on the map.\t\t\t\t\t
