\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed this game although it's not without some minor issues. The game play loop was enjoyable. You fix up various locations of bed and breakfasts in kind of a similar way to Two Point Hospital, although not as in depth and more laid back. The art style is very nice and cozy. Progression of unlockable items felt good and timely. It has a bit of a back story, but in all honesty, I didn't really get it. To me, it felt like chunks of it were missing, but it doesn't effect the game play so it didn't bother me. There is a weather system which I didn't really care for. In my play through snow and rain consistently blew across the entire screen even when indoors making it hard to see, particularly at night (fyi, you can sleep through the night) Just wish there was a on/off toggle for that. Quests were a bit ambiguous at times, but luckily a quick search usually brought the answer of which direction I needed to head for. My only other gripe is the cooking system. I like the way it worked because it's unique from other games but without the ability to select a number of dishes you want to make it quickly became tedious for me. All in all I really enjoyed this game, none of my gripes were bad enough to make me not want to keep playing and I definitely think it's worth checking out if you like these sorts of games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game but ultimately it falls too short for me to recommend it. The graphics are quite cute, and the concept of the game is great. However, the game is clunky, tedious, and buggy. It is simply just not much fun to play. There are lots and lots of little annoyances that add up to an overall unsatisfying experience. If the game had a total user interface overhaul and some significant quality of life adjustments, it could be a lot more fun.

The environments are neat to look at but awful to navigate. Lots of small ground clutter items have collision enabled meaning trying to walk through an area is an exercise in frustration. I was constantly getting hung up on various small rocks or branches. My hand also really started to hurt after holding down the WASD keys so long with this game. There is a fast travel system, but not all areas have it and you have to walk around quite a bit to collect materials anyway. If I didn't get stuck on so much junk in the environment it wouldn't be quite so bad.

The game claims to have controller support. With controller enabled, I found walking around less strain on my hands. However, the user interface was not designed for a controller and is extremely difficult to navigate using one. Most of the game is spent in menus to manage the building, crafting, cooking, and other hotel aspects. Thus, I was forced to go back to using keyboard and mouse for this game despite wishing I could use a controller. I couldn't even switch between controller and keyboard while playing because only one is enabled at a time for some reason.

The user interface is a total mess. I frequently ran into bugs that duplicated items. The menus are difficult to navigate and often needlessly complicated to use. The cooking interface on the surface is an interesting system using action cards that are filled with the appropriate ingredient. But despite giving you an item to unlock auto-filling recipes later on, you can only cook 1 item at a time using this feature unless you drag over more ingredients for each card in the recipe. That completely defeats the purpose and it's very tedious to fully stock the dining rooms.\t\t\t\t\t
