Things I Like About Bear And Breakfast:

1. Adorable artstyle.

2. Design the layout and decorate each room to your pleasure.

3. Advancing to next area also unlock new features to further customize your BnB.

4. Julia is my kinda girl~

5. Adorable NPCs.

Things I Dislike About Bear And Breakfast:

1. Because different beds and furniture have levels and better stats, in late game you kind of just build the samey bedroom over and over.

2. Also because of growing demands and because of better furniture requiring bigger space, the size of each bedroom can't be too small.

3. I wish there were options for floor and wall textures.

4. Controller feels janky to use.

5. Julia's service only works for the buffets in the same building with kitchen installed. Multiple buildings require multiple kitchen.

From the trailer and description, you think you're going into a game where you run a singular B&B and you upgrade it overtime, which sounds amazing. But when you get into the game, you find out your bear is going to open SEVERAL B&Bs, motels and resorts, each one bigger than the last, and that you have to run around managing them all, never really getting the chance to connect with any of them. Why weren't they upfront with that? The description literally says, "...trying to run a B+B in the woods." Not a great start.

Pros:

- The art is adorable, truly. It's beautiful and satisfying to look at. The artists are talented and it looks AAA.
- A few of the characters who help you are super helpful once you unlock what they do (Wade & Julia).
- Some of the dialogue made me laugh out loud and I love the bear's emotions during conversations.
- Most of the NPCs you meet are super cute.
- The bear's personality is adorable
- Most of the items in the game are cute
- The devs updated for Christmas and left the items in the game and they are so cute, especially in the winter area
- Fast travel is nice and makes sense
- I love the different areas you can visit

Cons:

- The NPCs you meet are only in your life for a short amount of time. You meet two of the bear's friends in the beginning and I haven't seen them since I opened the first B&B. Why even have them in the game? They seemed to serve no purpose.
- You have to run several B&Bs in different areas instead of one B&B that you get to upgrade and make your own.
- The game isn't balanced; some of it (getting supplies for upgrading) is way too easy and other parts (cooking) way too hard or they take too long.
- You can build the rooms yourself and add items to it, but you have to upgrade the items in order to maintain the "score" your customers have, and those scores force you to add more and more and more to the room, resulting in it looking SO cluttered and horrible. The rooms end up looking like an episode of hoarders.
- You can put things in front of doorways or in customer's ways without realizing you are; the game lets you. Then you have to spend time looking at your cluttered rooms, trying to figure out where the item is they can't get by.
- Customers walk through walls or refuse to use items. One room you have to build in order to progress in one area is a movie theater and I have tried EVERYTHING to get the customers to use it. They won't touch it.
- There's an incredibly beautiful snowy area which was my favorite and the customers walk around outside in blinding snow in shorts and a tank top. Holy immersion breaking, Batman.
- The music is just....no. I hated the music so much, I turned it off and literally played Stardew Valley and Undertale music on Spotify.
- Cooking is awful. They made it a sort of weird card thing? But the worst part is that it takes FOREVER, and it doesn't make sense. How do I only get one bowl of soup from making tomato soup? Who only makes one bowl? Is it one of those single serve to-go soups?
- There's an NPC you can get to help book customers and he doesn't even book them by their stats, resulting in unhappy customers, and he's SUPPOSED to work that way. Why? I didn't even bother using him, as useful as he could be. Why make him so useless?
- Instead of getting money back for decorative items you no longer need, you have to throw them away. That is just SO frustrating.
- The place where you buy decorative items is a mess when you're late game. It's hard to find the items (needs a way to categorize) and you can't tell how big the item is until you've bought it and tried to place it, so a lot of times you waste your money.

I was really looking forward to this game and now I'm just really disappointed. I think I would recommend the game if you're playing very casually and understand going in that you're going to be running around to different areas, taking care of multiple hotels. But if you're thinking this is like Stardew Valley or want something with real heart, this isn't it. In the end, for all the clutter, it still feels empty.

TLDR: Nah, bro. Go play Stardew for the 3rd time.

Edit: I went back and finished the game. Still 2.5 stars. This had such potential, but it's really disappointing. I hope the dev keeps trying, though.\t\t\t\t\t
