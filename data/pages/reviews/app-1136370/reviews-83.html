\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for your typical management game, this isn't it. If you like management games but want something a little more chill to play slowly, this is it. It's a very linear, story/quest driven management-type game with exploration, crafting and cooking. However, I really enjoyed it for what it is. The art style is beautiful, I enjoyed the challenge of trying to be creative within the limitations the game sets and trying to keep my guests happy with limited resources. It's a slow build up with a lot of fetch quests, but it kept me entertained and intrigued. You complete quests to access new items and types of rooms you can build in your motels, and to access new locations on the map in which you can build another motel. Each quest gives you something new to play with, so you can't just jump all in from the start and build the greatest motel with staff and lounges and so on. The main cons I would say are: the dialogue is god awful and there's way too much, walking around certain areas is a pain as the dozens of random little stones and bushes all over will have collision (while others don't), sometimes there's a stand still when you're just waiting for your guests to finish their stays and you can't sleep yet, and I do wish there was a tiny bit more freedom when it comes to expanding the size of your motels. But overall I think its worth the play through!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter about 100 hours , and 100% completion in the game I feel like its time to revisit & review.

The game is fun to decorate rooms, hank the bear is witty and the story took twists and turns that were not predictable.

The gripes I have is having to build in a set structure, rather than having the ability to build anywhere on the map. Or not being able to add a bar / other rooms that are recreational at earlier locations.

The new recycle ability is good, but I put in 3 whole max level sinks and only got 2 mahogony planks back.. not sure if its a bug or whatever but i feel like it wasn't exactly fair for the price to make those. Those planks you need 5-8 to build anything at 500g a piece.

It was tough doing the last of the achieves to get 100% in the game, because you need 30 5star ratings across the board to finish the quests. I actually watched tv, and just waited. It's not really interactive and feels like a weird way to end the game.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


I feel like the potential for future hotels, worlds and such is huge- but I wish there was a sandbox mode to build how we want on the map, and like If i wanted to build near the beach or a small house near mama bears house it would be cool.

Overall the game is a blast nonetheless, I enjoyed the story and collecting. Plus the new winter collectible and outfit is adorable. It was good to see Fin again :) I look forward to future updates and seeing what they have planned.

If they don't and this is the end of the road, it was worth the money playing. Just understand what you're buying when you buy.

GLHF!
