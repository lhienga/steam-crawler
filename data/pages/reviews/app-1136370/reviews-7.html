\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe fun part is the first few hours.
I really enjoyed my first half of this game. There were some fun writing and the mechanics were fairly interesting.
But at my 20th hour, I really wanted the game to end.
The game starts to feel like a slog halfway through.

As you unlock the mechanics, they start to feel more cumbersome than rewarding.
You start automating some of them but there's also the fact that certain materials for building will need to be farmed each day in the game where you have to keep walking and looting the same areas over and over again as you wait for a small amount of materials to respawn so you have enough to make a small number of higher level objects that you need to satisfy the customer.

You also do the same thing to progress the story.
You gotta keep walking back and forth to NPCs doing a mechanic that you thought you already automated.

On top of that, the player path is not very clear and has so much obscure obstacles that simply walking gets frustrating because you keep running into things that you can barely make out slowing you down.

At the end, it felt like there was less of the enjoyable part, (building management) and more of the other things that were not very enjoyable. (Walking to farm materials and waiting each day to farm the same things because they barely give you enough.)

I'm writing the review because I'm almost finished the story and really wish I'd stopped playing. I kept pushing just because I wanted to see how it ended but it really just wasn't that interesting. I'm almost at max Level objects for my cabins but I'm done with the game because I know that even though there's only one more level, the amount of farming I have to do would probably take me more than 10 hours for a very miniscule reward.

TLDR: Cute but overstayed its welcome by becoming a chore to play rather than enjoyable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy review is from that of 1) A new Steam Deck user and 2) A casual gamer who plays on the Nintendo Switch exclusively prior to this review. If you're relatively new to the Steam Deck and this is your first "cozy game", here are my thoughts:

**PROS**
- The game's art style is super cute and well done. It reminds me of "Cozy Grove"
- The story dialogue with characters are amusing and funny and make me want to keep playing to see what happens
- This game is inexpensive regularly, but a great buy when it's on sale (purchased during Winter sale 2023)
- In game music is soothing and not over bearing or distracting
- The pace of the game play is chill, with no rush even with the day/night cycle. There is also no health meter so you never have to worry about tiring out or needing food. You can sleep whenever you want (Sleep only gets you through the night cycle if you want to play in daylight only. That's it!)
- Cute little stickers and achievements for your steam profile are easily obtained by just playing the game :)

**CONS**
- The controls will not be to your liking right out of the box. Ultimately, I decided to play with the pre-set controls by the Steam deck, but there are some User profiles created that you can try. This game, I feel, was originally created for mouse and keyboard so navigating the various menus with Steam Deck controls WILL take practice and getting used to. If you're not a patient person with this type of customization, SKIP this game entirely and find something else. (I knew this was what owning a Steam Deck would be like, so I was already prepared for some level of difficulty)
- There are many inventory menus, crafting/building menus and quest log menus. If you're a gamer with poor eyesight (like ME) you may find the on-screen Steam Deck menus to be a challenge playing in hand-held. I recommend docking and connecting to a larger external monitor OR skipping this game altogether if you're committed to hand-held game play more. (It's a strain on my eyes, but I'm enjoying the game too much to back out now, after all I've already gone through figuring out the controls)

Overall, I really am enjoying the game and have tanked 6+ hrs into it right from the start. Half that time was just figuring out controls, but overall I have enjoyed the play and I will continue.\t\t\t\t\t
