\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf a warm cup of tea were a game, it feels like it would be Bear and Breakfast.

With over 30 hours spent exploring and building its world, and a very busy schedule constantly trying to tear me away from it, I still find myself coming back to what is currently my happy place.

+ Delightful art, animations, and sound
+ Adorable cast of characters and great humor
+ Relaxing and fun gameplay in a variety of regions

- No ways to create storage or mass-drop items as of my latest session (Oct 30)
- Guest characters underutilized as NPCs
- Little motivation to return to some regions in the story up to where I am

An overall wonderful experience so far and while it might feel a bit bare-bones for those who are more into packed building/base management games, for me it's the right amount of relaxing "busy-work" in a fluffy, cute world. I would gladly play a free-build mode where I can just create my own buildings in all the regions, a sequel/prequel/spin-off dedicated to a specific region, or even just a Bear and Breakfast 2 with different locations and/or characters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, so by the time I write this review, I've unlocked 3 B&B, and here are a few things you might wanna know before you buy this cute game:
***PROS:
- It's cute
- Lots of activities to do (aka lots of gathering to do to past your time)
***CONS:
- Valuables (trash) is one of the currency in this game and I swear it's so hard to spot. So spend your trash wisely because trash-hunting when you need them is NOT fun T^T
- Teenie-tiny build space: You'll be asked to cram stuff inside teenie tiny space, it's fine for 1-2 room. But gradually it gets frustrating the more time you do it.
- Don't play it using gamepad/ controller.
***DEBATABLE:
- Repetitive gameplay: By 9hr-in, I'd say 80% of the time you'll be moving things around in your tiny bedrooms, INCH BY INCH in order to fit in the ever-increasing in size, higher-level furniture. Just that, 80% (or at least it feels like that to me) of the quests is upgrading bedrooms, and this also linked to the teenie space mentioned above. But to some, this might be their cup of zen so~
- Dark humour and lengthy diaglogue: Some might appreaciate this, I don't. So there you go.

REPLAY VALUE? - Nope, at least not with the current state. I even struggle (due to lack of, or insufficient level of, enthusiasm to play) to boot the game and I'm only prolly 30-40% through :v

SHOULD YOU BUY? - All in all, it's a cute INDIE game. Not really hits that sweet spot for me but it's personal preference. I'd say give it a shot. Despite all the annoyances, I still like Hank the bear - our protagonist.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I just finished this game (the story part at least), and I'd say overall its pretty good. The end to the main plot was underwhelming, but I think that's partially because it was unexpected when I began playing and so interesting I started skipping by the hotel management parts just to progress the story line. If you're most interested in the management part of the game this shouldn't take away from that, though perhaps that strategy saved me from some issues other people had, as I basically gave up running the later hotels until I had unlocked at least some of the help staff. Personally I am hoping they'll still add more to the plot (especially after Barbara), or at least side quests to meet some of the other gods (and do more with Vlad, cause I like Vlad and we didn't get a lot to do with him), but I don't regret buying it. All of the characters had a lot of personality that didn't feel forced or shallow, and I had a lot of fun meeting them all.

The walk speed and collecting items didn't bother me very much, the only problem I had was much later in the game concerning iron plates, but it was an incremental issue, where I didn't have enough each day to craft ALL the items I wanted. If you like exploring the environment and collect the things you see as you go, this probably won't be a big inconvenience. I rarely ever ran out of materials other than cooking items. It took me way to long to find out you buy eggs, cheese, pasta etc at Ipswitch's shop though. I don't know if the game said this and I just didn't see it, or if they just expect you to find it if you're buying fuel (which I never did), but I didn't notice this until the Sous Vide Cheesecake quest
