\r\n\t\t\t\t\tbear & breakfast is as described - it is a cozy management game! after finishing the game (perfect completion), these are my thoughts:

pros
- multiple in-game QoL features compared to other life sim games (i.e., fast teleportation, crafting, etc.)
- good in-game time progression (i.e., able to progress in-game time if needed, time pauses when crafting/building, etc.)
- cute art style and good in-game locales + background music (ties in well with the story line while being distinct from one another)

cons
- despite being released for 1 year+, this game is still buggy where in-game information will not load properly (e.g., stats about room temperature, food, etc.)
- many players seem to have issues with steam achievement syncing, i was able to fix this by enabling steam overlay (disabled on game launch) however this fix might not work for everyone
- story progression becomes disjointed at times where you are able to manage multiple in-game locations, but progression in multiple are blocked by certain checkpoints/goals that are either dependent on RNG or the progress in other in-game locations (to where it felt like a large roadblock)
**in my situation, i was able to determine that the prestige/rating of my in-game locations were the cause of the roadblock so i suppose being too efficient is a drawback?**

other comments (neutral)
- not story heavy compared to some other life sim games, especially if you do not plan on achievement hunting
- furniture aesthetics are tied somewhat strongly to in-game function
- relatively few interactions with NPCs (limited character/relationship building)
overall, i would recommend this game (with a grain of salt) if you enjoy management style games and are looking for a casual one to try out!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never really write reviews, but i don't want anyone else to mistakenly buy this game.

There is just a lot of lost potential in this game. Interesting characters, environments, mechanics that are barely touched upon. Like a character and want to see how they develop, whats their story? Too bad, you see them for one quest and never again. You think when you unlock a new area you're gaining something, but it just becomes more and more tedious and uninteresting. Decorating and cooking, things i was looking forward to the most from the trailer, are made to become more unfun as time goes on. Decorating environments just begins looking cluttered trying to raise ratings. Cooking is entirely locked to one area of the game, the mini game to cook goes from something interesting looking to a slog.

I echo the same thoughts and feelings of a lot of other negative reviews too that I don't have time to express.

The games overall a huge disappointment and unless they do some major overhauling (seems very unlikely given how things are going for them) I can't see myself ever touching it again. I feel like I wasted my time and money, I kept thinking "oh this is where things unlock/progress and get good" but just kept getting more and more let down.

I highly recommend watching game play footage for a while and reading a lot of reviews if you're really set on trying it out, it isn't the type of game that it seems to be via trailers. Less bed and breakfast and more fetch quest and micromanaging.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- The plot is interesting. The way the game starts feels very silly (in a good way), and the individual NPC storylines are the real draw.
- The quest progression feels very natural and it feels GOOD completing them. (However, I do also just very enjoy getting achievements.)
- The gameplay is very addicting. I feel like I'm constantly changing layouts and rearranging furniture, it's GREAT!! Additionally, crafting and collecting is also really good, though I never feel like I have enough ingredients for cooking.
- The day night cycle doesn't really matter outside of aesthetic purposes. You CAN sleep if you want to, but it's not necessary and there's no penalty for not doing so. It's very useful for figuring out where you need to put some lights down though.
- The art style is unique and very cute!
- There's LORE and SECRETS

Cons:
- Room ranks (specifically decoration) is VERY unbalanced. You can work as hard as you want to upgrade all your decorations, but if it happens to be next to a kitchen or a utility room (or god forbid BOTH) it TANKS your scores. Granted, this can be very easily ignored (especially if all other rooms scores are high), but it's just very frustrating.
- You also can't sell items, but this is negligible because of how quickly collectibles respawn and money accumulates.
- Late game is mostly just waiting for more money to come in and waiting for wood to respawn for the chance to get mahogany (if you particularly care about upgrading all of your furniture).

I know some other reviews talk about bugs, but I haven't had any problems yet - knock on wood. Definitely worth getting during a sale or if you're on the verge of another binge of The Sims. 4/5 stars\t\t\t\t\t
