\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: This is a very cute game that's good for about 2-5 hours. Unfortunately, those 2-5 hours aren't anywhere near enough to finish the game, and at a certain point the game's highlights fade away under a mountain of small janky bits that culminate in an overall dreary experience. By the end, the game feels unfinished; this is at best an early access title without any indication of such.

This is going to end up as a ramble if I don't bullet each cut that contributed to the death by a million cuts, so let's go from the top:

* The game's story makes no bones about the fact that gentrification and the rise of AirBnB is a bad thing, and as a leftist I agree, but then the entire game is about managing a swanky BnB chain/empire. It brings down the mood quite a bit to constantly be reminded that you're buying into a very corrupt system while I'm just trying to build nice hotel rooms, and the game fails to ever truly reconcile these conflicting ideas. In fact, by the end of the game, it stops caring about your business altogether and begins to tell a completely unrelated story about the evils of zoos and animism being real.
* When you're crafting furniture, there are filters for functional furniture and decorative furniture. However, you cannot craft any decorative furniture.
* You can drill down further on the functional furniture filter, but the UI for this isn't particularly clear, and there's no tutorialization for this. I had to read the Steam forums to discover this.
* There's no way to sell crafted items; the only way to get rid of things you don't need is to throw them away. Throwing away items is a tiny icon in your inventory, and there is no tutorialization for this.
* When you choose to build a new room in your BnBs, you choose from categories: "comfort," "hygiene," "dining," "utility," and "special." However, three of these categories only ever have one room type under them, and "Special" has six.
* Taking care of management aspects takes only a few minutes, and days are much longer than that. You can spend the rest of the day gathering items, but gathering isn't very exciting (every point is two clicks, and all points are predetermined) and by endgame you have more stacks of crafting mats than you know what to do with (except for 3 specific bottleneck materials). By endgame, I was leaving the game window open in the background and doing something else just to churn guests.
* The cooking minigame is a little confusing/convoluted, and then the recipe card unlock makes it mostly just a time waster. The cooking recipe list is far too small a dialogue window to view recipes easily, and there's no way to sort or filter this list, so good luck finding what you need to make for quests!
* Mid- to late- game guests will request room stats (notably in hygiene) that are impossible to hit. Not impossible in a "this takes too much effort" way; impossible in a "there is a hard cap on the number you can achieve and the guests ask for more than it." This is a known issue that is lessened but still present as of the 1.2 patch.
* The material bottleneck/money sink in early endgame is ludicrous; I think I got to the final resort at about 10 hours in and couldn't build a single room in it for many more hours. Bedrooms with the highest quality furniture costs a minimum of 1400 gold to make (1800 in 1.0) at a point in the game where you're fortunate to have 2,000 to play with after maintaining all your other resorts at the highest quality. This game was not playtested with the mindset of "I want to build a really nice resort for the first guests," but by the time I realized that, I was in too deep into playing the game wrong to course correct.
* There are no decoration variants, so all your endgame hotel rooms look basically the same. You can't even change the flooring or wallpaper, or even make recolors of the same items.
* Late-game decor objects are focused almost entirely on bringing the score of special rooms up. This raises your hotel prestige, but you are likely to cap hotel prestige without having any decor in endgame special rooms. Conversely, there's very few options for decor that makes sense to put in guest bedrooms, which have ludicrously high demands in late game.
* Late game story feels buggy. I'm pretty sure I somehow missed an entire cutscene that was supposed to explain what I was doing post-hotel managing. Late-game quests didn't track properly and weren't even marked in the journal as story quests. The last bit of dialogue had an obvious typo in it.

The devs are very active and seem very enthusiastic about making the game better. That's great! But I do QA as my day job, and I bought this with the expectation of a mostly finished experience that might get some content updates in the future. As it stands, though, this game is far from finished, and the earnest but unfocused mess that's present might never coalesce into something great. I'd say check back in a year or so, but definitely stay away for now.\t\t\t\t\t
