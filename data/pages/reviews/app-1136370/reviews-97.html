Simply put, Bear and Breakfast is EASILY my 2022 game of the year.

There is so much heart and soul to be found in every inch of this game; and every character you'll run into is unique and interesting to co-exist with in this wonderfully crafted slice of business-management capitalist trashpile.

Underneath the sentimental, there's an extremely competent management game here; with a tight focus on juggling multiple spaces at once, incrementally improving them as you continue to gain resources.
Quests are thoughtfully laid out, and aren't don't linearly per NPC: You'll find yourself needing to complete quests for one NPC before heading back with a new resource or item required to finish another side-quest; and it's all somewhat metroid-vania-ish, but gives the game nice pacing without feeling padded.

The number of furniture and decoration items available to you makes planning room density and layouts a balancing act between aesthetics and min-maxing profits: There's really a lot here for all skill levels and play styles, and I appreciate that the devs give you almost infinite flexibility for customising spaces.

My casual playthrough took 33 hrs to complete for the main story and to clock all the achievements; well worth the humble entry price: The story beats get a little slower near the end, but it's mainly a soft-gate to make sure you're able to efficiently run your business; which will come naturally with game-play regardless.

Artstyle is wonderful, with great character animations for the MC and NPCs just going about their days, and the environments are illustrated wonderfully in a mix between painterly and cartoonish: It's certainly not dull to look at, with dynamic weather creating a nice variance to the maps, from clouds and trees casting light-dappled shadows to wind, rain and snow blowing around the trees and shrubs.

Sound design is lovely, with lots of options for mixing the various, sfx and ambient sounds that make up a dynamic and relaxing sound-space overall.

Minor bugs aside, quickly being addressed by the devs on their Discord channel; Bear and Breakfast has been the most satisfying game I've played this year, and I wholeheartedly recommend anyone with even a passing interest in management games or cute bears give it a shot!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely ADORE this game. The art style is awesome, the dialogue was super funny without trying to be TOO clever. It was a lovely, cozy experience. Reading other's reviews, I think many people's experience with this game was that the gameplay wasn't super intense, but that was one of the things I loved most about the game. I do agree there are some things that can be improved and expanded on, but for the most part, I was happy just playing a simple, cozy management game. My biggest problem I think was that I wanted more of it! Perhaps if there is a dlc or a sequel in the future, it would be lovely to hear more from the characters. That being said, definitely worth a play, especially if you're a fan of cozy games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was so excited for this game for months and I completely binged it from start to finish as soon as it came out. The visuals are so very charming and so are the characters and dialogue. It has all the aspects of a cozy management game and more.

The core gameplay of decorating, building, and managing guests is pretty simple and well explained though there are some smaller essential details that could've been mentioned more clearly; things like rotating and deleting objects. As the game progresses, you get access to more complex features for your lodges like cooking and heating and I feel this is definitely where the difficulty spike is. Thankfully, the game provides you with some features that helps automate some parts of your lodges.

Not counting the bugs and glitches, the only thing I disliked about the game was that the way you get that sense of progression in the game is by building better and better furniture and lodges, makes sense. The drawback of this is that you end up with bedrooms and bathrooms that all essentially look the same. You always want to use the best furniture so there's little reason to use furniture you got early in the game once you're ahead. I find this kind of frustrating though, for a game that has so much to do with decorating and making each location feel unique, they almost all visually blend together by the end.

Overall, Bear and Breakfast has all the parts of a really fun and charming management game and I think as more patches come out to smooth out the bumps, it'll be even better. I also give it an extra thumbs up for having a unique and interesting cooking system!\t\t\t\t\t
