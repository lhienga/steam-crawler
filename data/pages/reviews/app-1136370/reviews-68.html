\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was hesitant to buy this game after reading the negative reviews but I'm very glad I did. Is this game perfect? No. Do I love it? Absolutely.

Like many others have said, it's a very laid back stress free experience in both good ways and bad. It happened to be exactly what I was looking for at this time, a progressive management game with RPG elements and fun characters I can hop into for a short period as a busy adult with a job and not worry about people starving, freezing, being killed by raiders, or meeting other horrible fates.

I agree with some of the bigger criticisms about this game, namely that the pacing and progression are poorly balanced. As you unlock new levels of furniture and decorations, the previous levels essentially become obsolete and just clutter up my bear stash after I replace them. I do also find it a little annoying that you unlock new types of rooms that are then only usable at the location you unlocked them, rather than being able to go back and add them to your older properties. I haven't had as much of an issue waiting for quests to complete or things to become available as other people, maybe I'm just playing at a slower pace but I've found I pretty much always have something I could or should be doing.

TLDR if you're considering buying this game, ask yourself what specifically you're looking for. Do you want to be a wholesome himbo bear running around in the woods with next to no concerns and decorate some pretty houses with your friends? Go for it, 100% recommend. If you're looking for a more traditional, polished, and challenging management game, maybe pass on this one and go buy Rimworld or Banished.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was drawn to the building/decorating in this game and overall it doesn't disappoint. However, I wish there were more decorations. It would be nice to be able to put furniture in the hallways such as couches, tables, etc. More diverse or customizable furniture would be cool as well. Near the end, all of my hotels pretty much had the same decorations and furniture. Made the game seem a bit bland. A great addition would be bigger and better paintings. The paintings at the beginning were tiny and the only larger pictures are two of Hank.
The only issue I had with the gameplay itself was Julia would stop making food for Highlake every so often, despite still being hired and paid. To fix, I had to turn off her services there and pay to turn them back on again. The game also didn't explain how the heaters worked in accordance with the weather, so despite having the best heater, the cabins sometimes got anywhere between 20C (too cold) and 45C (which is insanely hot but not an issue to guests).
Overall, great game IMO but would greatly benefit from additional furniture/decorative options.\t\t\t\t\t
