\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I could give this game a "MEH" review instead of a down, I would, but as it is, there ARE some good qualities about the game, but ultimately it is rather lackluster. At the time of writing this review, I have played through the entire game as it stands now and collected all achievements.

The characters are cute, the artwork is adorable, and I enjoyed the otherwise wise cracks about life that can come up. There were parts that looked into the current look on our lives and our place in the world. This game even had a promising start, a dark foreboding plot that really captures you. But, that's about as good as it got. I kept waiting for myself to really enjoy this game but it never quite happened.

I love management style games, but this one felt disappointing. I never felt challenged and even collecting resources was boring. It was too easy to do what you needed to do and many of the area building quests you could complete one after the other in a short time. The majority of the game too you learn how to do more steps but only find yourself having to combine everything you learned together at the very last area. Plot wise, the story doesn't have an ending. It leaves too many unanswered questions. In fact, you don't even really learn about any of the characters in the game, even the "friends" that the game sells you on about starting a bed and breakfast with never actually involve themselves with the main game.

Otherwise, if you're looking for a cute management game where you don't want to really challenge yourself or deal with plot, then this is the game for you. Saying that, it's a great game for if you want to shut off your mind after stressful days at work like me. I do hope the creators work a little more with the game because it holds a lot of promise as it does now.\t\t\t\t\t
