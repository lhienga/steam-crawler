\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBear & Breakfast is a cute little economy game with a great balance of character and game play.
You play as Hank, a charming character who is about to go on an entrepreneurial journey around the valley.
I really enjoyed some of the character stories, the art style & the mini cut-scenes you would get to tell more story along the way.

I don't have many holes I could poke in this game as I enjoyed it very much, but if there was anything it would be a little more guidance on some sections.
For example the shop where we could buy food, it took me a while to realise where to purchase dairy items.
Or it would of been nice for later game play to have more sources than just the junkyard for iron sources, as the bathroom items can take quite a lot of it and your need to do lots of frequent trips there to achieve some of the loan shark signs requests or build later game items.

I really like how you can get the critters around the woods to help you out in your business, although the fact in the beginning our best friends seemed to be portrayed as very important and then they were tossed to the side for the rest of the game and not used to the very end until you'd get museum pieces, felt a little silly and it felt like these characters needed a big role to play in the game or to be made out as less important as they were used very little.
Perhaps they could be used in a future game or dlc where they go off and do there own hotel businesses inspired by Hank.

Overall though I couldn't stop playing this game till the very end, I only came across 1 bug in my entire experience and I played the game to the very end, completing all the achievements & quests.
It's an addicted sim, with a charming conclusion that could lead off in many other directions if the dev were to take it to another game or dlc ect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStarting off, I will say this is not a perfect game and it does have its flaws, but I have been enjoying just sitting down for 20 to 40 minutes at a time to play and build up the Valley's various buildings.

The primary problems I've experienced have been due to some crashes when in the building and editing modes, getting the temperate and food ratings to customer satisfaction, finding materials I need, and going through blockages and monuments that require tools, kits, and resources I do not have.

All and all, I would recommend this game to those searching for a calmer game with cute characters, beautiful artwork, easy to understand mechanics, and the patience to explore while waiting for the game timers to tick by.

For those starting, your first 2 sites are going to be worth upgrading first and keeping upgraded for customers as they are the least maintenance-dependent. I would also recommend not starting to book customers for areas 3 and beyond until you have to for story progression to unlock more options in building and resource purchasing.
Finally, I have to advice patience when playing, as you will hit some walls that require waiting or inventory management to get through. For these times, explore, find the chests that have decoration or furniture in them, and gather some of the plants and food ingredients. Building materials and wood fuel stack to 99, ingredients to 40, meals to 10, and furniture and decorations to 5, but you should not be afraid to throw away these things, especially the furniture and decorations as those will likely be replaced with better ones and wall decorations.

When upgrading, keep in mind the higher level Comfort items take up more space (beds, food rests, and closets), but decorations should be limited to rugs and wall items to help conserve space. Also, it really does not matter if customers can get to the bed or not, so do not have that as a concern.

Once more, I have been enjoying my time playing the game and am looking forward too continuing, even starting over a new playthrough once I am finished with my current one to do it all over again.
My biggest gripe about the game is how the other two characters, Hank's friends, seem to completely walk out of the story once you get started on the resort management. I would have loved to see them following me around into the new areas, talking to them and learning of their opinions, or seeing them roam around in the unoccupied rooms or buildings, but I would say the game is worth its cost and then a little more.

Happy building and nature watching. Also, don't be a downer like Fin, be a go-getter like Hank.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very cozy game with amazing art style and lovable, interesting characters. There is some repetitive dialog outside of quests, but in terms of the general main story line the dialog is good and often great fun. The music and art style is very appealing, and for the price of the game it's well worth the time you'll spend if a cozy, no time pressure, management game is up your alley.

The only things I have small issues with myself is that the sorting of your inventory is hard and a bit frustrating at times, and the cooking mechanics of the game would be good fun if a) ingredients such as butter, milk, etc weren't so expensive and b) if time froze while you were cooking. Oh and maybe c) some way to have your ingredients from surrounding fridges go into the cooking mechanics because I never had enough space in my inventory to carry the multiple stacks of misc items since I didn't have the recipes memorized for what I needed.

TLDR: This game is fun, beautiful, and worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t
