Hank is a gullible yet lovable character that is makes some of the dialogue and interactions with other characters priceless. The game mechanics and overall strategy to complete goals are well done and not overly complicated. One of the biggest draws for me is that this game moves along at your pace and is relaxing. Overall the entire premise is well worth the effort. For those that like to speed run games and not immerse yourself in the strategy, this is not for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tat first I thought it was cute and adorable but the longer I played, the more glitches I noticed in the game.

the first few ones were more harmless, like when I found myself cheating the bathroom door block;
Supposedly, the door(s) should be inaccessible when it's occupied but I happened to be inside the bathroom when the NPC walked in and thus locking me in. Overall it was harmless because i was able to leave as the NPC did

the next glitch I noticed was when the bathroom door (again) became inaccessible after the NPC used it;
the context was I had a bathroom situated among 3 bedrooms, perhaps the tenants all went for the bathroom at the same time but since only one can enter at a time, there was a queue. The problem was, after the first tenant used and has left, the door remained inaccessible (I have screenshot the event) and it made all of the remaining tenants in a constant loop of trying to access the bathroom and failing. They kept hovering and loitering around the door. Only when I clicked 'sleep through the night' did it reset their pathfinding and the door. (I have screenshots)

another glitch I noticed was in the room building phase; In making the floor plan in the Farmhouse, I was laying down just the bare-bones AKA just empty rooms so that I can go by trial and error of whichever seems like a passable layout. It is a given that when necessary items tied to a room isn't placed inside, it will be gray in color to indicate something is missing. But upon adding the furniture, the room still remained gray and I had to delete the whole room, then add in the furniture just to fix it.

the latest glitch I encountered was after I unlocked the furniture for the kitchen. Reading the tutorial, there should be an area debuff surrounding the room, as per the game rules, but placing the items (eg stove, cutting board) it didn't show a red line of debuff. To make matters worse, upon exiting build mode, my cursor remained in building mode- by that, I mean I can see tiles, gridlines etc, things that should only appear in building mode. Not only that, it glitched my whole game - the ambiance music stopped, my camera is stuck in the house mode and I can't exit the game by pressing ESC key; I had to alt+F4 just to exit the game.

I have numerous screenshots and will be posting them for reference, should the devs read my review.

another minor but undeniably essential feature the game lacks is the ability to sell unneeded furniture. As a loot maniac, I found out very fast, very soon how little my inventory space is, so with only the stash by the shark mobile, I am limited in all the items and resources I can collect.

Overall, I feel like this game is unfinished. Perhaps on Alpha even, I might give it a chance to improve but considering how generous and forgiving I am when it comes to games, this game specifically is the first and currently the only negative review I gave ever. With how complicated the game already is, I believe that glitches only makes it unenjoyable.

Really enjoyed all the cute characters, each of them have their own story to tell some funny while others actually pretty tragic. The charming visual design and especially the catchy music is so well done, definitely a highlight. The endgame was a little drawn out for me, felt grindy. but the game was totally worthwhile, I had a lot of fun with it, even got all the achievements. I do wish there were a few more main story quests to balance the side quests.
