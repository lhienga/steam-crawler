\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, so I've been playing this game for about a week, and I'm on day 55 or so in-game. It's honestly way more fun than the videos on the page make it look. I'd say it's probably worth a little more than $20, but you have to actually play it to know that. Every character is unique, lovable, and has a purpose. The art style is charming (reminds me of Adventure Time and Gravity Falls) and probably my favorite thing about the game. The story is nice, but not mind-blowing (you're a bear who runs hotels, that's about as deep as it gets) but that's not really a con. There's the option for automation for all the tasks that tend to get tedious, but you also can't really automate EVERYTHING because the cost would be too high, so it keeps a nice balance of necessary tasks and tasks you actually want to tackle. Really, my only small gripe is the lack of recycling materials. You can't sell unwanted items or break them down to get your resources back, which can be frustrating in a game like this where the goal is to constantly upgrade furniture leaving you with lower-level furniture you don't want or need. At least you can delete unwanted items to conserve inventory space tho.

I give it an 8/10. Not at all disappointed with my purchase. Having a blast.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay. I struggled to determine what I should put down for feedback. I selected positive because this isn't a bad game. It's fine. It's not a great game either.

The storyline and characters are there, but I ultimately feel the "big" plot fell flat and felt contrived somewhat. They tried to include some fantasy aspects that didn't translate well because they were more afterthought and there lacked a sense of accomplishment.

I liked the idea of designing the rooms. For the most part, that felt good except the requirements of certain customers pretty much guaranteed instead of nice looking rooms, you needed to stuff the spaces with as much comfort items as possible to get your numbers high enough. I do like a lot of the decorations, but again, I felt decorating wasn't as essential as room stuffing.

I felt the storage space we had was too limited. Just my personal opinion since cooking mats take up a ton of space.

The clothing rewards were pretty stupid. A few blah garments that afford you nothing and take up valuable storage space since you can't trash them.

Fuel is a pain and constantly worrying about feeding my guests until I can pay an employee to do all my cooking was stressful.

I really loved the concept here and there are some redeeming points, but the game is just ok. If you want an amazing story, this isn't the game for you. If you want an awesome main character...Hank probably isn't it. If you want to grind hotels and pick up trash, this is fine. It's an okay game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's pretty good in the beginning, I tried really hard to like it but I'm noticing new glitches every day (even if they're 90% visual it just feels janky) and multiple times the game has frozen while the bear is sleeping and I've lost progress. The inventory is very hard to manage, I didn't find the delete button until like 10 hours in and even then there's some items you can't delete if you bought too many. Moving items from the inventory to storage is honestly what made me give up on this game. You have to drag each stack from opposite sides of the screen one by one, and usually I'd have to swap 10+ stacks at a time. And if you had two menus open, scrolling would scrolls both menus at the same time which is so annoying and such a weird choice. Most of the inventory system I would call a weird choice.
The story is very odd, it starts in a creepy dream sequence that's really interesting but then they just don't mention it until the end of the game and I didn't even realise it was over. There are so so many cute characters but once you meet them there's zero reason to ever see them again. For example you supposedly live with your two best friends in a house with your mum. But after you build the first house (which is the first 10 mins) talking to any of them just repeats the same one line for the rest of the game. The game just feels empty which is such a waste because of how beautiful it is. There's a lot of areas to explore but finding a new person was just kind of sad because you do one quest for them and then probably never see them again.
You don't really manage anything, you just build copies of the same room with really limited decorations and then any guest will pay for it even if it says they want something specific. Unlike other reviews in the beginning the time system was alright for me, I spent most nights just picking up extra materials. But now circling the same 2 or 3 areas for the same materials I'm always low on gets boring. There's also nothing to do but earn money so I can buy blueprints to finish quests, so I often just go afk for a bit so I can sleep and get paid in the morning.
Overall I'm going to finish it and get all the achievements but I'd wait a while longer for this game, it feels unfinished.\t\t\t\t\t
