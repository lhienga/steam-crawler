\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first started playing the game, I really enjoyed it. The visuals are very appealing and cute, most of the characters are fun to talk to, and the music is really good, to the point I was surprised they don't have the ost for sale on here.

But as you play, there are a lot of small things that build up into larger and larger issues. I honestly checked if this was in early access when it really started to get on my nerves because I can't believe that they haven't fixed or addressed many of these issues yet. These are basic things like inventory management problems- it's annoying to even move things around, there's no breaking something down, and you can't delete items quickly. The crafting for housing items is fine but I have absolutely no idea why they decided to drag out cooking into some bizarre card based mini game abomination. You can get an item that helps you a bit with cooking but you still have to manually do a lot, further driving home how irritating the inventory management is. That was the point that the game started to feel more like a chore than something fun.
There's also a few glitches mixed in too, which doesn't help.

Waiting is an annoying theme with this game. There's waiting for specific resources to respawn, waiting to skip time, waiting for the trash currency to accumulate so you can decorate. I'm only writing this because I'm having to stand in game idling since you can only skip time at night and I told myself I'd finish the last quests. And there's nothing to do while I wait because I've done everything, there is no way to skip time faster at this point. I've tabbed out to play other things and that's a bad sign in a game to me.

I won't spoil the story but it just feels a little all over the place. Interesting in a way but confusing world building and back loaded into fetch quests you're doing at the end. Or they added in weird money gatekeeper quests that are pointless since most people are going to have that amount easily covered and then enough left over to throw a giant party. It's like there should have been another area or two for all the quests to play out a little more slowly over the course of that zone or something.

Overall, it's a cute game that I can't recommend because there are a lot of flaws that bring the experience down. Unless you are very much in love with the art style or bears, it's best to pass on this.\t\t\t\t\t
