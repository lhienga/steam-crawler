\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not a bad game. It's cute, has good music and art, but I've just finished the storyline so I feel it's time I share some things on my mind. TL;DR - if you are anxious, this isn't a cozy calming game.

I went into this thinking it was going to be a cute and easy paced little game with some cute story. What I got was 25 hours of stressing over where to find ingredients, constantly having to upgrade every single room any time I got a new recipe, running to bus stops every five minutes, and then a story showed up in the last 5 hours where the 'ending' was so abrupt that I ran around looking for someone to tell the credits to roll (they don't, you just...finish it).

The whole time I kept thinking I was playing it wrong or out of order - I stumbled across NPCs with quests or no quests and spent a majority of the time wondering when they were going to be something to interact with only to be sent to each one of them later on in the 'main story'. The characters want to be cute and funny and tongue-in-cheek, but your two best friends fall off the face of the earth until about hour 20. If I wanted an experience of not seeing any of my friends and working thanklessly for ever-increasingly demanding customers, I'd stick with my retail job.

Once Pinefall was mostly up and running the whole game started to chug as well, mostly with autosaves, and loading times between areas growing from 2-3 seconds to almost 10. I'm guessing this was caused by the amount of useless garbage the patrons were demanding to be shoved into their rooms in order to get the decor rating up but seeing at the game doesn't run that heavily it was a bit confusing for me. Then while moving one of the museum pieces the artefact completely vanished and was never seen again. I don't know where it's gone but I hope it's doing well.

Despite all this, I think it's a decent game. Needs a lot more polish (PLEASE LET ME SELL STUFF I DON'T NEED ANYMORE OR GIVE ME ADDITIONAL STORAGE I AM BEGGING YOU) and set off my anxiety, but I'd give it another go. A lot slower based on how underwhelming the story felt to me and now that I better understand what it has to offer: a game of trying to stay one step ahead of the most demanding vacationers and never having enough mahogany or iron ever.\t\t\t\t\t
