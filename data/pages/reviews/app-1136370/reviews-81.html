\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm really enjoying this so far! It's so cute. I adore how sweet and naive Hank is as a character. Game is cute as heck and has kept me sucked in. Overall a cute and fun time killer. Definitely satisfying as a Stardew Valley player, but still has a ways to go in minor things to make the game play more fluidly. As long as the developers continue listening to the community and fixing things up. This could turn into a new favorite of mine. Highly recommend to any Stardew fans.

My suggestions and issues I'm having are minor.
1. The beginning of the game feels really as it's mostly just waiting to be able to sleep to progress to the next day and there's not enough to do in the meantime.
2. I wish I could right click or something to quick move a stack of items. When transferring to the bear stash you can transfer/take all or have the painful task of clicking and dragging every item you want to store one by one. So I have resorting to just carrying everything around with me.
2a. To add on to the point. If I use a filter in my inventory and then were to press the button to move it all to my bear stash, it'd be nice if it moved all items under the filter but instead it still just moves my inventory in it's entirety.
3. There needs to be some way to sell old furniture (or break it down/recycle for parts?). For now I'm still setting up Pinefall so I can use my old level 1-2 furniture, but it's starting to clutter things but I can't do anything but delete them which seems a shame. Maybe you can sell them for coin, break them down for materials, or give them to Took for garbage?? Also if you somehow manage to purchase a recipe twice, you can't sell it back. So I think that you should be able to sell it back or it should only allow you to purchase one. Like maybe there's only a single quantity. Same for the Bus Stop Signs, Logger's Tool, etc. Maybe The Bus Stop should run out of supply once you've used said items in all possible places (or be able to sell them back). If you were to bulk buy or accidentally buy one too many, you can't sell it back and now you've lost money.

I absolutely love this game and have been playing it nonstop since getting it. These are minor but when I run into this being an issue is down take away from the game a little and suddenly I'm not as immersed and am frustrated a tad. Fixing these things will make the game feel much more fluid.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you just want something cute to fill some time, and don't mind stuff like a meandering plot, weak gameplay, and what feels like an endless tutorial, go ahead and get this game. Maybe wait for a sale, though.

Bear & Breakfast is a title I was excited about for a long time, so I'm saddened that I can't recommend it, at least in its current state. The first two hours or so of gameplay are the strongest part. After that, I had a growing feeling of dissatisfaction, and by the 5 hour mark, I'm ready to put the game down altogether.

When I say the gameplay is weak, I mean it's REALLY weak. About half of the quests just involve sitting around and waiting for days to pass, which would be fine if there was something I could fill that time with other than rummaging around for crafting materials. Guests at your resorts are hand-selected, and their expectations are spelled out right off the bat, so there's no real stakes involved; you can just pick people who you know will be pleased, and since there's no way to interact with them during their stay, you just have to wait for them to leave so you can pick a new batch of perfect guests.

The story-based quests are painfully trivial, even for a casual game. New characters are introduced constantly, only to be basically irrelevant for most of the game. Dialogue during scripted events (like cutscenes and progression-based conversations) drags on and on, even being lampshaded at several points, but if you feel like chatting with an npc in your downtime, don't bother: they'll only have a single line of irrelevant dialogue.

All of this boils down to an experience where you spend most of your time doing little or nothing, and what you DO accomplish feels unsatisfying. In my opinion at least, this game could seriously benefit from, say, guests having special requests WHILE they're staying at a resort, just to give the player something worthwhile to do. That, and maybe introducing most of the game mechanics a lot earlier so the actual gameplay feels more like experimentation & mastery, rather than having your hand held through extremely simple tasks for hours on end.

Unfortunately, those fixes would probably be too much to ask of a non-early-access game. If this game WAS early access, I'd be more willing to give it a chance. As it stands, it feels muddled and poorly planned, and just makes me hungry for a better management sim.\t\t\t\t\t
