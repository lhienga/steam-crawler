If you don't mind grinding in games, you could probably get past it. I still would recommend it for the rest of the game and even though the end I have found to be tiresome, I still enjoy the quirks of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start off by saying this is a charming resource management game and I've enjoyed playing it. The characters are cute and I've enjoyed all the people and animals you meet along the way. The casual way that the park ranger talks with Hank and nobody blinks an eye at a wild bear managing rental properties is hilarious and I love it. The aesthetics and sound design are great and wonderfully pleasing.

But while the game is enjoyable, it has some pacing issues. The conversations between characters feel overlong and the banter back and forth has humor that doesn't always land for me. I enjoy the light snarkiness, but I found myself skipping dialogue because it took so long to get to the point. I do love choosing Hank's well thought out responses only for him to vocalize the line as "bear noises" which is funny. So far for me, the bird and the dog friends don't really do anything. I wish they were more integral to the story since they're part of the opening.

The natural progression of the story feels rushed. At just under 15 hours, I've unlocked most areas without actually building rooms or renting them to anyone in the newest ones. There may be more and I know there is more to the main story, but most of the map is open now. I wish that there was a way that enticed me to spend more time at each location and work on interior design and management before rushing me off to the next area to unlock. I don't have any suggestions on how to accomplish this. The shark speaker quests were fine and paced well by requiring a certain number of guests and other tasks, but the signpost quests almost seemed too easy. I almost always had all materials on hand for each one, so I'd plow through a handful of requirements in one sitting the first time I talked to it. But I'm not sure how to balance that out without making it too grindy on materials.

I haven't gone back to it in a few days because there's less sense of exploration and expansion and it's now reduced to doing the rounds of properties to pick up money and materials which has started to feel like work. This may be more of a personal preference for how I like to play. In terms of light engagement for in-game accomplishment, this does scratch a lot of itches for me.

The variety of furniture designs is also a little lacking. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite honestly, the most charming thing about this game is the bear's walking animation. I would buy this game if i could just watch him walk around a lovely & well crafted world for 20 hours. It's a perk that I actually get to participate in quests and run different B&Bs too! The art style is cute, the writing is fun, and the game's pacing keeps it engaging without feeling too quick. Some things feel a bit too easy-- like a quest where I needed to find an object and the quest marker immediately pointed me directly to it-- but I bought the game expecting a management/interior decorating sim; which is what i got, and that aspect is well balanced, in my opinion.

Overall, it's obvious a lot of care was put into this game. For it to be truly enjoyable, I think it does require some self-pacing, much like playing the Sims; just focusing on achieving quests to get to the next task doesn't yield a very rewarding gameplay. That being said, it delivers on cozy vibes and creative exploration. It sure as hell charmed my socks off :)\t\t\t\t\t
