\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a very cute concept held back by some extremely frustrating elements experienced over and over again. Mainly rough controller support and needlessly complex menu navigating for a very chill my-first-simulation game.

The bear moves so slow. The menus are impenetrable (it takes at least 4 button presses to cancel your way back to gameplay after opening your inventory). It makes what might be a fun, creative exercise into a frustrating series of mispresses. The cute animal quests are all fetch-quests and 99% of them I completed before talking to the character giving the quest. The dialogue is great, except that there's WAY too much of it and it has no bearing on gameplay.

I stopped playing when I reached a point where I couldn't fast-forward the game because it was daytime, had completed all side-quests, had no money to upgrade, and literally had to put my controller down for 10 minutes to wait for Guests to finish their visit so I could start playing again. Not for me.\t\t\t\t\t

Hiring employees for example is an absolute rip-off. fully automating one of the inns I could understand losing 50% to 75% of the profit to be able to ignore it while I work on the biggest and newest Inn that I've unlocked but I barely make anything at all, in fact some days I even lose money on the staff. trash collection is better to just hire for one day every now and then and fire the same day because my god is it expensive and impossible to keep up with.

It's trying to be a bit too much all at once. I'm struggling to keep up, once the heating element was introduced I was pretty much almost giving up at managing all the inns and took the massive hit some of the staff would give me for some form of relief on what I needed to do each day to keep the inns running.

it's cozy and cute but a bit stressful if you stress over keeping to all going and getting lower ratings because the staff aren't assigning rooms with guest stats in mind and instead just at random. I personally really enjoyed the game but near the end it just all became too much to juggle at once on top of running out of resources and having to go out to collect more not only for crafting items for the new inn but also to make food for the other inns because automating cooking is ridiculously expensive.

I eventually just let the first few inns stop running and just decided to run three of the inns at max capacity to keep it from being as much of a mess with juggling it all. this made it a lot easier to manage but I was also just letting two of my inns be unused which is not preferable. I encountered some glitches as well, the most prominent being when I placed items in storage it just doubled the stack and left me with more, not even at a rate that I enjoyed getting free materials but to the point I was overrun with berries until I finally got rid of most of them.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, I've finally finished every achievement (play time 29 hours, for you achievement hunters) and can officially say that I can't recommend this game. Let's start with the positives:

- The art is BEAUTIFUL. I love the variety of trees in each landscape. It is obvious that the artists did not skimp on assets, and the color palettes are lovely as well!

- Decorating the rooms is fun! Trying to tastefully arrange furniture in the limited space while maximizing room score is challenging, especially as higher value functional furniture needed in late game takes up more space, but it's a good challenge, especially going back to earlier hotels and trying to upgrade them to the highest level.

And.....those are the best things. For me however, the list of complaints is much bigger.

- There are so many bugs and glitches! I view myself as an average player, not trying any crazy speedrun glitches. But i still ran into several issues that caused me grief. None of them were game breaking or caused me to lose progress, but all of them were annoying. Here are a few examples:
* Items refusing to place. I had to restart the game once to get this fixed, though other times, it resolved itself after exiting from the UI build menu and then re-entering.
* The escape key randomly stopped allowing me to exit out of menus. 90% of the time, I used it to exit from the build menu. Sometimes though, it just wouldn't work.
* Recipes can be bought multiple times (unlike other one use items) if you don't close the shop after every individual purchase. They can only be used once, and cannot be sold or trashed, so they can just take up permanent inventory space.
* Visual glitch after character introductions and image flashbacks - these happen in the middle of dialogue, then clunkily makes you repeat the dialogue from before the cutscene, and it looks..... off..... when transitioning between these.
* The most annoying bug for me is the AUTO set-up button on cooking does not function if you load the page while something else is 'cooking', even after the previous item finishes cooking. Every time the timer finishes, i have to back out of and reload the next recipe i want in order to get the AUTO set up button to work was incredibly annoying. It happens every time and should have been fixed at this point in the full release.
* Visual glitch in loading the Guest Booking UI - if you click out to the 'Cabins' menu, then open any other cabin's booking UI (which you will do when booking up all your hotels in a row in late game), the booking menu shows the previous subpage highlighted, though you always open up on the first one. This is a small issue, and apparently a common unity menu error, but it is confusing. I often though that i was on the second subpage but was actually on the first. Just annoying enough.

- There is no way to speed up time during the day. You can sleep the night after 21:00, but you have to wait until then. A lot of game progression is locked behind completing quests, and several of the quests are locked behind waiting for several days to pass (such as getting several 5 star reviews, or booking several of one type of guest). In the mid game, this isn't *too* bad, as you will have many quests running simultaneously, but at the beginning and end game, it's very frustrating. You can't get to new areas to explore, even though you've basically finished the management/decorating part for that section. At the end of the game, i just had to wait through the days, as i had done everything else possible and just needed time to pass.

- There is no way to sell or trash items! All items are assigned to price value in your inventory.....which is useless, as there is no place to sell them. There is limited storage and inventory space, but no way to get rid of unwanted items. This becomes a major problem when paired with the ability to buy multiple of the same recipe, which cannot be used. You could functionally soft lock your game this way, if you wanted.

- The types of UI pop ups and keyboard button needed to press is a mess. For example:
* Item pick ups can be dismissed by pressing E
* Dialogue can only be progessed by pressing Space
* Dialogue choices can only be selected by Mouse, not Arrow keys + Space
* The Tutorial pop up, which does not pause time or take priority, need to be dismissed by Mouse.
* All UI menus can be dismissed by pressing Escape (unless it glitches)
* Random story progession pop ups will pause time and take priority, and need to be dismissed by Mouse, not Escape, which works for all other priority UI.

- Fast Travel is limited to a few locations, making some tasks very arduous. There is a loading screen between every location, and it's frustrating to have to walk through multiple areas and their loading screens JUST to get to the bus stop to fast travel to another area with a loading screen.

- COOKING. Don't even get me started. When I saw the cooking UI at first, I was pleased by how unique it is! And it is: using action cards to follow a recipe, and adding ingredients to each action. It's very cool! But it's also very frustrating to use in a way that could be easily fixed. Examples:
* There is a set timer for every recipe. It's 15 seconds long - not long enough to leave and do a different task around your hotel, slightly too long to just wait through. I figured that I would just set up my next recipe during this period..... but the cooking menu locks until the timer finishes! All you can do is look at your next recipe until the timer finishes.
* The AUTO set-up button sounds great, but you need to exit and re-enter the recipe to get it to work if you pick the recipe while a prior recipe is cooking. annoying bug.
* The AUTO set-up only stocks enough ingredients to make one of each recipe. You then have to manually drag in more of each ingredient to set up multiples. This half defeats the purpose of the AUTO set up button.
* After making a recipe, extra items sort incorrectly back into your inventory. Thankfully there is an auto sort inventory button.

- Some areas are very dark - I get that this is aesthetic for places such as 'Darkgrove', but it is hard to navigate without turning my computer brightness up a ton (and then getting blinded when i open a menu).

- The music is very repetitive. It's pretty lo-fi and fades in the background easily, which is nice at first, but there is so little variety that it felt lacking pretty quickly. At night, there is no music and it is very eerie.

- The Character dialogue and story is kind of boring. Unless the character has a new quest for you (rare), then there is only one piece of dialogue per character.

- The two biggest characters, You (Hank the bear) and Fin (robot shark) are SO annoying. I started skipping through their conversations, as the player character of Hank the bear boils down to 'I'm stupid! Whaaa?', and Fin the shark just spews out corporate jargon.

- There are tons of quality of life functions absent from the game, such as the ability to drag items between inventory and chest, or for items to auto-sort when moving between storage. You have to click the item from a chest, then scroll down all the way through your inventory to an empty space to place is. It could be much easier.


There are too many bugs and quality of life issues for me to recommend this game to anyone. It's nice to look at, but for an adventure game, your exploration is limited, and for a management sim, there is surprisingly little to manage once the hotel is built. At it's best, Bear and Breakfast is a decorating simulator with limited decorations (why can't i put couches in my lobby? there are no general use chairs!), quests, and gathering materials.\t\t\t\t\t
