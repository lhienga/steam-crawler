\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is such a great and cute game. The art and design of the game is so well done and aesthetically pleasing which makes for a cozy atmosphere. It does have a few bugs though. There's a way to duplicate furniture which makes the need to buy more for other rooms not needed. Also, money in the game seems to be a struggle until the end of the game when at that point, you don't need it that much anymore. I also felt the comfort expectations for the rooms raised too quickly and I didn't have the right level of materials to meet the requirements without, for example, putting two wardrobes down. The decoration items are much easier to obtain as you can buy them with valuables, but comfort items you have to craft and are much bigger and harder to fit in rooms. I think the game could benefit by adding some comfort items to took's garbage shop as well just to give rooms that extra boost of comfort to satisfy the harder guest requests a bit quicker. Overall, this game is absolutely adorable and I had the best time playing it despite any minor bugs or faults. I highly recommend it for a chill gaming experience for those who love interior design or just anything cute.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery cute game and there are a lot of things I love about it! I'm a big fan of the art style, the dialogue is fun and the backstory is interesting, and I find the whole system of crafting and decorating satisfying, and I wouldn't mind the cooking system either if it were a little less frustrating. And that's where I seem to be at with a great deal of the aspects of this game. Crafting and decorating is fun until you start taking on new quests to improve the comfort level and realize that the furniture gets bigger the higher its level, which makes it difficult to reach the level you need and also keep the decoration level high unless you want the room to look more like a storage space than a bedroom, especially when you're looking at some of the smaller properties or have a minimum for the number of bedrooms.

The cooking is fun until you find yourself having to keep three different properties stocked, at which point you begin to realize how tedious collecting ingredients and making dishes can be. The heating system is so broken that I eventually resigned myself to the fact that I would be getting no five star reviews from the winter resort starting out, because keeping it at the required temperature with the starting heater is damn near impossible. Apparently you can eventually hire roles to make these two things easier, but it comes at a hefty cost, while chances are you're already also paying for someone to collect 'valuables' for you so you can have even a hope of affording new decorations.

None of this is to say that I don't find the game delightful, as I noted at the beginning. This game has so many good ideas and the concept is absolutely adorable, I just wish it were balanced better to make it more of a relaxing experience and less of a keeping the balls in the air experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm really sad about not liking this game. I originally bought it when it came out because of how cute it looked, but couldn't get into how slow the tutorial was and how annoying the cutesy dialogue was (maybe I'm getting old, who knows). A few days ago I decided to properly play and gave it my best shot for 13 hours. I'm not sure it was worth it.

In the beginning the game is very manageable, but the more locations and features you unlock the more it starts being a chore instead of something to enjoy. It's very repetitive, and what attracted me the most (crafting, decorating) is not really an essential part of the game. I found myself roaming around the map constantly day and night looking for "valuables" (which is the currency used to purchase decorations) and just repeating a loop of collecting resources around various hot spots. Managing the resorts is maybe 1% of your day, the rest is just... filler.

It gets old rather quickly. About 13 hours of gameplay quickly.

I think the biggest problem for me is with how unbalanced this game feels.

Progression goes either super quickly - by unlocking tons of new areas pretty much immediately, so you get overwhelmed with thousands of things to do - or grinds to a halt entirely.

For example you're given quests you can't complete because you don't have the necessary thingamajigs/don't meet specific requirements, but you can't meet these until you get the thingamajig or more decorations (which aren't really easy to buy, considering "valuables" are pretty hard to come by after a while). So you end up just running around, completing the same loop without any diversions, and to me it was too much of a chore. I had to step back at some point because I was just playing and playing but I was bored and frustrated.

The art and concept are great, but unfortunately it's a miss for me.\t\t\t\t\t
