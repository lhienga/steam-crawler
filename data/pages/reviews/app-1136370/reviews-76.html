\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGet Bear & Breakfast if you are looking for some visually adorable, stress-free, no hurry cosy hotel building game with bears and other humorous & memorable animal (and some human) companions.

The game allows you to build resorts in different areas (desert, lakes, snowy mountains), each a bit more different to manage. There is a lot of focus on room planning, furniture or decoration choice and placement, cooking, and material gathering. New characters in each new area give you a bit of lore and their own story, but in general, this isn't story-driven game.

The game peaks around mid-game (say 16-20 hours), later due to various reasons the gameplay turns into a bit of a chore, but that's only when you don't find decorating and redecorating rooms for its own sake fun. I managed to get 100% achievements in around 33 hours, but that I think was fast.

The game is definitely not for those who are looking for a management sim, as there is not so much of a challenge in this area, and a majority of management aspects are simplified or streamlined.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I saw a cute hotel management game and that’s what I got. I love the characters (Hank is a really satisfying and cute protagonist), the dialogue is funny, and the whole concept is definitely worthy of the “cosy” title. It’s fun and addictive and I enjoyed it enough to play it through to total completion.

I am aware that fixes are being looked at and added as time goes on and the developers appears receptive to feedback, so I don’t feel the need to be critical. However, my main feedback is that the game in it’s current form can be overwhelming due to the inventory management and lack of ability to sell or stack larger quantities of items. I also found the scarcity of resources more frustrating than challenging as a full junkyard rarely covered the crafting needed to keep up with objectives. I was also left with the final goal of achieving 30 5-star reviews at Pinefall which meant I was essentially leaving the game running so I could restart the day and refresh the junkyard materials.

However, it was overall really positive. The dialogue is genuinely hilarious, and the story is engaging enough for a short game. I’d love to see the overall mechanics and gameplay shift slightly more towards challenging rather than stressful, but at the same time it did mean you always had something to do until the last bit. I’d recommend purchasing if you enjoy management games.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike most of the 'negative' reviews here, I really wanted to like this game, but everything about it just seems to come up short.

The story is cute, but wandering around so much is not. Maybe I'm crazy but building a resort was just deeply unsatisfying, bordering on frustrating. The locked off angle of the camera, the doors/furniture seemingly getting in the way of seeing what you are doing, without first moving said doors and windows (annoying, the doors and windows should mostly disappear with the lowest wall setting), and not really having a lot at your disposal early on without wandering around picking up trash. I agree with other reviewers that it just felt like an endless tutorial that makes the accidental mistake of not really respecting the type of player that would play this game.

Managing guests seemed rote and sadly uninteresting; basically match a handful of numbers to the types of furniture that give you those numbers. I felt like improving the rooms basically required you to spend more time wandering around looking for trash, than you did actually managing. I could go on, but you get the idea. Death by a thousand cuts. I got to the cooking section, and that essentially just broke the last of my good will for the game, as now we had card mechanics to cook things. It feels like it's trying to be too much, and sadly isn't great at any of it.

There was obviously an idea that was this game, and then a struggle to justify that idea through myriad gameplay elements. I feel like if they improved the resort building by making it a bit less frustrating, and gave you more freedom up-front, such as allowing you to craft more decorative items (rather than buy with trash), I would have stuck with it, but the slow gating, the distance between resorts and the need to manage them (without easy bus stops), just drove me to call it early.

Like I said, too bad, I really wanted to love this game.\t\t\t\t\t
