\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of those games I really wanted to like (and in some aspects did), but it's too rough in places where it really counts.

tl;dr: The story is too weak to make it interesting, the management tools are too weak to make this a simulation and the controls with the Deck are abysmal. It's very pretty though.

The good:

- great art style and animations
- they came up with very unique places, props and characters
- tood sound design and music

The bad:

- playing this with a controller almost broke me; drilling down into menus, moving items in the inventor, etc. is extremely tedious; sometimes you wonder if they really tested this; this is meant to be played with keyboard and mouse, not on a Steam Deck :-(
- there are weather effects (quite dizzying at times) and they are not cropped out when you are inside a building; that makes it actually hard to do things in there because you can barely see;
- the cooking mechanics are punishing and an absolute catastrophy on the Deck; I found out way too late that I could outsource this to Julia if prioritize some quests for her;
- the story and lore is pretty lame and I don't know how it should relate to the actual gameplay; I didn't care for it and the "conclusion" of the main quest at all;
- no satisfying end at all
- it's not a business simulation either as it doesn't have the depth nor does it give you the tools; if you want to see graphs and reports this is not for you;
- the building mechanics are mediocre, but they really crumble when you want to remodel and optimise your resorts
