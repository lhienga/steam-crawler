\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuch a beautifully and carefully made game, but as other reviewers have said before me, is extremely misleading as you are running an entire empire of bed and breakfasts instead of just one. Rather than relax and focus on one location, customizing it to my heart's content, I found myself stressed and running around trying to progress the story and keep guests happy.

I found myself asking when I'd get staff after opening my second location, as I was constantly juggling booking guests to rooms, gathering resources, and doing side quests. The juggling was even fun for a little bit, but more and more was being added to my plate instead of less as I progressed. When they finally gave me staff by the time I opened the third one, they gave me one single employee (Gus) who can only work at one location. On top of that, he does not care if the room doesn't meet guests requirements and will place them there anyway, getting you less money and more negative reviews. I would rather do it myself than pay him to only do half of the job.

This was a huge let down after such a good start, despite the introduction feeling a bit longer than necessary. The game itself looks gorgeous, the dialogue is light and humorous, and the developers perfectly captured that relaxing vibe. I had some minor gripes about the inability to rotate furniture and some annoying menus, but I was optimistic and was willing to look past them. After all, you could dress up the protagonist (the bear) in cute clothes!

Until they had me running three bed and breakfasts with little to no help. They truly were expecting me to be the janitor, the concierge, the cook, the designer, and the builder for more than one business in a game that was marketed as a cozy relaxing simulation game. The nail in the coffin was when the game wanted me to cook 3 meals a day for my guests at one of my locations on top of everything involving running the other two locations. I think if you could focus on one location at a time without worrying about the others, this game would be an instant recommend at full price. Why it wasn't designed that way when it was marketed that way, I have no idea.

If you're looking for chaos, constant running around and/or a challenge, this game is for you. If you're looking for a way to unwind and relax, look elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like this game. It is in the same vein as Stardew Valley, especially with the aspect of repairing old bridges/monuments with resources, but unlike lots of Stardew Valley-esque games lately it is also completely original and doesn't rely on Stardew/HarvestMoon/AnimalCrossing tropes and mechanics. I like the management style a lot- I like to progress slowly, and I am completely free (and sometimes encouraged) to do so, because each business owned has its own (mostly) standalone quests. The only dislikes I have about this game are not large flaws, but little things that make it feel less professional and seamless. For example, it is incredibly slow to move large quantities of items from storage to inventory and vice versa. Other than "take all" and "dump all" there is no quick way to do it. Also, it is frustrating that the humans in the game can fit in spaces that fatty Hank can't, so there will be trash you cannot get to and have to move furniture to remove, every single time. I do feel the game progresses a little too fast, but like I said the player is completely able to continue working on the same business(es) for as long as they want. I haven't finished the game yet, but I've played 12 hours over two or three days alone, and I know I'm very pleased. :)\t\t\t\t\t
