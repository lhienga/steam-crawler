\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was as many really excited for this game, so much so that I didn't wait for the switch release but bought it for the PC, but I am still glad you can now play it with a controller.
My biggest issue with the game is the never ending upgrade quests for hotels. Without too many spoilers, you start out in smaller places and find larger places along the way, however your upgrade quests for the smaller places never stop. So in order to continue the game you are basically forced to cram as much ♥♥♥♥ as possible into tiny spaces, just so your star rating goes up and your tiny cabin in the woods becomes a five star hotel (paraphrasing), that just does not make sense. Why not give each place a different final level that it can achieve? That would also allow for cuter designs, better layout managment of hotel rooms etc.
This also forces you to constantly travel back and forth and collect ever more ressources, it just never feels like progression. No I haven't finished the game, at this point I am not sure I will b/c I just refuse to turn my b&bs into cramped, full of random knick-knacks, places just so I can keep their star rating going up higher and higher.
This could have been a really fun game b/c the design is excellent and adorable and it has so much potential that just falls flat for reasons that it should not struggle with.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was so incredibly excited for this game. I hyped it up because I’d enjoyed the demo so much and I was thoroughly looking forward to the game release. I want to note that I rarely leave reviews and I’ve never left a bad one before. That doesn’t mean this game is awful, it just means that I sincerely feel that it could have been better. I would like to help warn people, like myself, who look at the art style and read ‘casual’ and ‘relaxed’ in descriptions about it, and perhaps don’t realise just how grindy this game is.

The story is cute, and the game itself is (as I said) just so adorable and the design of it is so consistent and beautiful (whoever does the art - just wow, so precious!). However, the game mechanics that others have also mentioned becomes increasingly exhausting the further you get into the game. While cooking is fun, that same innovative spirit has not spread to any other elements of crafting.

Gathering materials by slowly lumbering across the map and picking things up is not particularly enjoyable. There is no fun element of exploration or surprise after a certain point and the gathering of ‘stuff’ only consists of clicking on something. You walk to find the materials. You walk to your different bed and breakfast locations. You walk to the shops. You walk to do cooking, and to place your cooked meals in tables. It is walk, click, walk, click, walk, click, walk, click, and there’s a LOT of it to be done. At one point, I checked how much trash I needed to gather to buy some cute decorative items, and upon realising how long (and how much walk, click, walk, click it would take me), I closed the game and figured I’d get back to it some other time. I just never did.

I feel, personally, that spreading it out over several different inns rather than letting us choose to have one big one is very detrimental to the game. I feel there needs to be some way of speeding up the tedious gathering of ‘stuff’. The bus mechanics of travelling across the map isn’t as helpful as it could have been because you still have to slowly lumber your way back and forth to the bus stop. There is nothing new and exciting about your everyday chores, and after a while, it really does just feel like chores.

I felt I should leave this review because I checked back on the game to see if they’d fixed the balance somehow in the early access process… only to realise that the game is, in fact, not an early access game like I had somehow thought it was.

It’s not a bad game but I can’t in good faith recommend it unless you really, really like clicking on stuff on the ground.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like this game. It's a lot of fun but I do have a couple bones to pick with it.

It feels kind of incomplete. For example, the pacing of the game is strange. One minute you'll have a ton of things to do, lots of quests to complete, and new places to explore; then the next there's nothing to do but wait. I also feel like there should be more to staffing your b&b. Like I know that you can eventually hire some forest folk as staff members, but it feels kind of out of place and like an afterthought? Like why not just have a pool of staff to chose from that have varying wages. You can hire them to clean up, and do concierge, and maybe a couple other things. Like cook, or in some areas, you can hire staff to keep the fridge and serving station full. Also, I feel like there should be some way to expand you b&b's. There really isn't a good way to upgrade the starting b&b past lvl 5 without needing more space since the furniture gets bigger and bigger as you upgrade, especially if you have 4 rooms and a foyer for the welcome desk. So if you wanted to upgrade them all well and have a concierge and trash pickup? Good luck fitting enough things in the rooms to keep up with decoration and comfort demands. Last, I think there should be some way to either sell or deconstruct things in this game. I've never played a game where you were forced to purchase things, but had no way to sell things back. Especially in the case of accidentally buying something you didn't need. Also, when you upgrade rooms, what are you supposed to do with all of the old furniture besides throw it away or store it?? There should be some way to sell things, or at the very least make it so that upgrading to the next item requires the current item you have. Idk.

I do like the game though.\t\t\t\t\t
