\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuper cute game- excellent lil woodsy ambiance management sim with lots of options for building and customizing resorts. The game falls somewhere between Littlewood and... Diner Dash? Maybe The Sims? The core gameplay loop is repetitive, sure, but the world is charming and funny and the mechanics are executed efficiently and rationally, making it easy to get into and satisfying to fall into the rhythm of. It isn't a game for everyone, but it is the perfect kind of soothing escape game that doesn't require a ton of brain power and problem solving, but requires focus and critical thinking - great for when you want send your brain away for a little woodland retreat.
Plus, Hank is adorable and relatable.

The main downsides of the game are mostly all inventory and item-management related- which gets frustrating when furniture/decor and crafting items are central to the main mechanic.
First and foremost, the game desperately lacks a way to SELL ITEMS!! Items that you craft display a monetary value, but you have no way of selling them, so it's entirely arbitrary. It feels like an oversight or a mechanic they didn't quite figure out, but forgot to remove? It's almost a Chekov's Gun situation: don't give an item a price if there is literally nothing you can do with that price, be it buying OR selling.
There is plenty of decor to purchase, but since you are working with limited space in your B&B's, it frequently becomes necessary to replace decor items with higher-quality versions, but this means that you're almost constantly left with (purchased) items that end up just being thrown away. There is no way to scrap for materials or sell them back, so it feels like a waste of resources. (The consolation here being that decor is purchased with a separate currency that is constantly regenerating, as opposed to the far more limited 'monetary' currency earned from guests staying at resorts)
Storage is intensely limited, and with the sheer volume of crafting materials, cooking ingredients, craftable objects, and decor items, it is extremely easy to run out of space, FAST. Given that the only alternative is just destroying the items you have no space for, it can get frustrating fairly quickly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you really enjoy building rooms, decorating them, Gathering Resources and managing guests expectations then this is your ideal game. Resort Manager Simulator and running errands for NPCs is 95% of the game. Tips: Having more rooms early on lets you book more guests and earn more coins; however as guests expectations for a resort increase, you'll need more space to fit the upgrades. Once I got to the last resorts (6th,7th & 8th) I did a complete remodel of the first 5 resorts to increase the size of rooms-in order to complete all quests. Also, anytime you're in A24, go to the junkyard and gather the scrap; trust me, it's the only things that couldn't spawn fast enough for how much copper and iron plates are used to craft furniture.

If you expect much in way of storytelling, you'll be sorely disappointed for the time spent vs. the payoff. You have to get through many quests before anything resembling a story is presented and then it sorta implies that it's made-up leaving you wondering what the truth is. That would be enticing, except it doesn't lead you to clues to piece together what happened (like a mystery would). More like you talk to some characters a couple times during each quest (while managing several resorts), hunt down some object, ingredient or fix up a building until you've done enough grinding to progress the 'story' because it's never clear which quests will. It goes from hardly fleshing out the world (NPCs don't have anything different to say except when they give you something to do for them) to introducing...'larger than life characters' to explain this important event. And then it just ends. I sadly wasn't satisfied and feel quite cheated on the mystery aspect. The bread-crumb pacing was so spaced out between the grinding that it all felt hollow. I would've liked to see them pursue the other mystery clearly going on with Finn; sadly his involvement in anything also just ends, with no answers. *sigh* I wanted to enjoy this, it sounded great. I even finished all the quests just to see if it would give me something more but no. I can't recommend this game; save yourself the great disappointment if you want a game to invest in.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was so excited for this game when I first saw it. I though it was adorable and the concept seemed like a lot of fun. However, when I actually started the game I was highly disappointed. For the entire time I was playing there was no sign of the story that was teased at the beginning of the game and all of the NPCs had so little dialogue that they were completely uninteresting. The gameplay itself was tedious, forcing you to go through multiple menus that didn't save your work at times, holding your hand in tutorials but also not showing you how to do basic things which made crafting difficult. Most of the quests were so time consuming and waiting for them to be done was boring as you often had nothing to do but forage.

My favorite part of this game is it's art style and animation. It's simple and cute which makes it really fun to look at. However, this is about all of the praise I have for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tbeautiful game! the artwork is amazing, i always took a moment to appreciate it with every new character and location. very chill and cozy with good music, characters, and great cabin management system. you get more and more support as the management gets more complicated, and it's always fun and balanced!

that said, i'll get into a few negatives you can expect, in order of what bothered me most: my biggest problem with it was the quest distribution. at first, you get a lot of stuff to do and basically never sit still, but then you start having to just wait. a lot of times i had to stop playing and just leave my character standing there while the days went by (in-game) cause there was nothing to do but wait. then you get a lot of quests again, then a big waiting period again, etc. a lot of the mechanics are still kinda clunky, and you frequently catch yourself thinking "wow this could be easier to do", but you get used to it. also, i usually love decorating in games but it was my least favorite part in this one. it's not really fun and rewarding, you just kinda stuff the rooms as much as you can for points, not caring how it looks. and the dialogue could be better too! i loved the characters and i wish i got more involved with them and their stories, but i usually just skipped the dialogue pretty fast cause it's long as hell and most of it not really important, they're just SAYING .

i'm pointing all this out as well intentioned criticism! and so y'all can get into it knowing what to expect. all things considered, i still played 124 hours of it lmao, and i recommend it very much if you like cute/chill management games!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game could be really cool, but sadly through a significant number of bad choices the experience is really really bad.

After playing for nearly an hour, all I have done is read dialogue and get stuck because the games' tutorial is so poorly structured that you will actively get stuck when playing the game. The player shouldn't be able to get stuck in the tutorial, ever, the tutorial is the safe space, where failure is shown, but never punished.

Additionally, narrative is a layer on top of a fun gameplay loop. If I have to rely on the narrative pulling players through the gameplay, there is something seriously wrong. Stardew Valley knows this (one of the inspirations according to the devs), so i am curious, and a little disappointed that the devs weren't able to replicate this for their own game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a pretty good game, got a good story, and fine gameplay. Fun for a few days, pretty easy to 100% as I did it in 3 days. However, there are some bugs and decently large flaws. Due to the nature of the game and the lack of a general time skipping button, if you don't have anything to do, you'll end up effectively just leaving the game on, waiting for time to pass. There's the occasional hitbox bug, AI being weird, etc, however, I'm not sure if this is a bug or not, but at the final resort with all the quests completed, some guests will ask for a hygiene value higher than the highest possible value you can possibly get. Lastly, the cooking system is just really bad, and generally needs to be reworked. It is effectively a massive waste of resources and time, it takes forever to gather ingredients, especially since you get a largely limited and partly random amount of ingredients every day. After making a bunch of meals however, all of them will most likely get eaten in a single day, despite it taking a good bit of your day to do and a fair chunk of your money. For example, I waited a few days, gathered a bunch of onions and other ingredients needed for a lot of french onion soup, I made like 15 of them for about 6 guests, and in a single day, all of the soup was gone. This is only remedied in the late game where you effectively get someone to do all the cooking for you without the need for ingredients, equipment and time. However, despite the massive flaws in one of the major systems in the game, and my paragraph long about said system, I still think it's a fine game, and I would recommend playing it.\t\t\t\t\t
