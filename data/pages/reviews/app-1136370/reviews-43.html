\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBecome the Landlord of the Woods and pick up trash! It's fun and addicting, but it's also boring and repetitive. This is a recommend, but with a big fat *

Bear and Breakfast is relaxing fun in the same way as Stardew Valley or Minecraft. It's a Podcast game, one of those where you can turn off your brain and focus on other stuff. You play a teenage bear who fixes up abandoned properties in the woods and turns them into money machines.

The sprites are cute and the writing is good. Each character has a distinctive writing voice that helps elevate potentially bland gameplay. The PC, Hank the Bear, is especially fun and has a lot of dimensions to his character.

The first few hours of gameplay are great. There are lots of side quests so you don't have to focus on running the properties. You can basically make a 4-bedroom cabin and let it run itself. Money is tight at first, but quickly becomes so abundant that you can buy anything without fear. As in, by the early-mid game you will be building $120 rooms with $50,000 in your account Likewise, you use human trash as a secondary currency and the more rooms you fill the more humans leave trash. It's a really effective feedback loop and it plays into the visual theme.

Now here comes the but. The early-middle section is *rough.* You can get trapped in several story quests where you need a certain number of guests pass through your hotel. During these portions I found myself having absolutely nothing to do. My hotels were optimally arranged and decorated. I had all the materials I needed. Each in-game day is 25 minutes and it took me almost 2.5 hours of waiting to move 8 guests through my newest hotel.

Then the micromanaging phase started. Each hotel adds a new gameplay element. The first cabin just requires rooms. The next one needs rooms and bathrooms. The next two require that you personally cook increasingly elaborate meals for guests. Then one requires that you both cook for guests and also keep the heater running (which requires you to collect a TON of food and fuel). This basically means the first 2 hotels run themselves and only require you to accept booking requests each day while the other 3 hotels all require micromanagement. Also, if it isn't clear you're managing these needs across all 5 properties at the same time.

So the issue with the mid-game is that now you aren't doing quests for quirky side characters nor are you decorating your rooms. You don't have time for that anymore. Your job now is to serve as personal chef and groundskeeper for 3 hotels on different maps. Also, there's is a fast travel, but they don't take you directly to your hotel, they take you to bus stations on the map. Also, each time you unlock better furniture, you are practically obliged to go to every location, build the new version of everything, and upgrade every room.

Here's my beef: For how utterly relaxing the first 6 hours are, the next few hours are pretty stressful. You need to go to each hotel every morning and book guests for the next day. Then you need to go to the supermarket to buy ingredients for foods. Then you need to cook food and plate it at each location. Then you need to feed the fire at your ski lodge. And by the time you've done all that, it's time for you to do it all again.

In fairness, this isn't permanent. Later on you can hire someone to cook and serve meals for you as well as man the heater. The problem, for me, is that there is a long waiting period to get those abilities and the compounding mechanics for each location makes for an experience that is less relaxing and more stressful.

Finally comes the late game. To fully upgrade everything you need special materials which can rarely be found in the world or (more practically) are purchased from the supermarket. With your food and heat mangement on autopilot and faced with such exciting quests as "complete 20 hotel stays with 5 star reviews" this part of the game completely falls off the engagement curve. The late game is basically collecting wood and then just sitting around waiting for days to be over so you can go to the store. The developers are so aware of this problem that they put a second, unlockable supermarket on the final map so you don't have to take the bus every 5 minutes.

At the end of the day I still recommend Bear and Breakfast. It's fun and the constant stream of events is extremely addicting. It's got a good story and cute characters. Sometimes there's nothing to do, other times there's too much to do but the art style manages to capture the management sim itch.\t\t\t\t\t
