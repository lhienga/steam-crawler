\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely adore this game.

This is a cozy, and hilarious, property management sim game. You're handed properties, 1 at a time, to build rooms of and manage booking guests, matching rooms with their tastes and providing for them if needed (bathroom facilities, food, etc. - though the game eases you into those mechanics). You go at your pace, there's no penalty for say, not sleeping a night away.
This has awesome dialogue, the characters' personalities shine.
I'm starting into the fourth area, and much of the game so far is resource gathering, clearing out areas of trash/valuables, building up/decorating the properties, booking guests, providing for them, optimizing.
Some things get automated as time goes on, so getting overwhelmed by managing so many properties at once is minimized.
I was just starting to struggle with inventory management a little when I unlocked a way to expand it, YAY.
Note, there is no combat or relationship/dating mechanic.

The cons - the day/night cycle moves fast. The game only seems to pause when you're in build mode to edit your properties, or when you go into the main menu for the game. it moves as you're reading the tutorial, and it moves as you're in dialogue. a lot of the feedback page is filled with people requesting more pauses or the day/night cycle to change, so hopefully that won't be a permanent complaint, but for now, time passes too quick.
That's basically the only complaint, so far. and that's overall a pretty minor irritation for me, though I also see it as an accessibility issue (I read quickly, not everyone does, YMMV).
EDIT: I'll also add, there's room for improvement with the UI. I could see a lot of potential for quality of life changes, stuff that'd improve efficiency.

Anyway, 100% recommend. If you're into cozy management sims like Stardew Valley, Animal Crossing, or Spiritfarer, you're bound to enjoy this too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved the art! The cute characters are the reason why I bought the game. Hank is adorable!

Remodeling, furnishing, and managing your inns and motels is fun at first. But it becomes tedious as you either have to do everything yourself. Or you need to pay an exuberant amount to have staff do it for you.

The main story quest line was good. However, I don't like how some tasks were ordered. You'll be told to retrace your steps across the valley between several legs of the quest line. While you can quickly travel via the bus, not all locations are reachable this way. There will be a lot of "aha, gotcha!" moments. "Oh, you want to continue the quest line? Well, pay up 50,000 coins." Or "go back and talk to the people you just spoke to and then come back to see me again."

Inventory management was done right in this game. You don't really have to worry about throwing away items to make room for other stuff. I ran around and picked up every crafting material I wanted without issue. I never even used the shark trunk.

The game can get annoying sometimes, but I'm glad I played through it.\t\t\t\t\t
