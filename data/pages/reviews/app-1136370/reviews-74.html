\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA cute game where you play as a Bear named Hank that refurbishes old, abandoned buildings and turns them into Bed & Breakfasts with the help of his friends. The art style is wonderful and the different decorations are really cute. However, the dialogue can be long and unnecessary. I tried streaming it for a few days, but the copious amounts of dialogue made it a pain after a while. Definitely much nicer to enjoy on your own. The story is kind of interesting and so are the characters. Some areas of the story are vague and I got stuck a few times and had to google how to get to the next part of the story. I think it's missing some quest hints in some areas, and the info online is very limited, so good luck finding the hints! Overall, I quite enjoyed it and I love winding down with this game before bed. I recommend it if you like cute, indie, cosy games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to give this game a five-star rating, I really do. But there's just enough little hiccups and holes in the wall to keep it from reaching that pinnacle. Still, it's a lovely game with fantastic characters and beautiful art design.

First of all: I love Hank and his crew. They're fun and feel diverse and dynamic. I'm a bit sad that most of them only end up having limited screentime, but they really make the world feel like a world and not just a blank canvas with some NPCs.

The gameplay is fun, with an engaging gameplay loop that introduces new elements to keep the experience interesting. There's been a lot of tweaks made to the inventory and item collection system since the game first launched, with more to come in the future that will further refine the experience.

WARNING: FULL GAME SPOILERS AHEAD

If I could sum up the main problem with this game with a single word, it would be Pacing. The game starts off smooth, mixing gameplay and plot with relative ease. But by the third area of the game the overarching plot comes to a screeching halt and doesn't pick up again until the last area. Even within the management aspect of the game, Winterberry is almost completely skipped over, the character being ushered into Pinefall as soon as the player finishes the 'special' mechanic tutorial of the area. This gives the player the daunting task of trying to build two resorts with the resources of one, and it can take a long time to build up enough for it to make a profit. This could be easily remedied by taking the portions of the overarching plot that involve Ciran and Vlad and moving them into the Winterberry section of the game, giving it a bit of padding and some breathing room before being pushed into Pinefall.

The other big issue I have with the game is with the guests themselves. Not as a mechanic, but as a collective of people in the world. For a game that is about catering to guests and bringing People back to the Valley, I feel strangely unattached to the NPCs. Except for the ones with absurdly high requirements that I'm not sure are even achieveable within the game, I shove guests into rooms and call it good. I don't feel a need to care about them or why they're visiting. This could be fixed by having a few 'reccuring' NPCs that visit for a specific reason that Hank can interact with. Maybe someone that's researching the "gods' of the world, or studying the forest and how it is recovering after the aftermath of the fire. This could also give the 'overarching' plot a bit more depth and explain the human's side of the Great Fire. Maybe we could even meet Fin!

My only other critique/suggestion is to make the 'decoration' need estate-wide and not just room-wide. I don't have an incentive to place decorations anywhere but the rooms, leaving me with a lot of 'empty' space in hallways that could hold shelves or posters to boost that score, but don't.

END SPOILER WARNING

All in all Bear and Breakfast is a lovely little indie that blends management with engaging story. It needs a bit mroe work before it truly reaches its potential, and I look forward to the day that it does.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAre you a cozy gamer? Have you seen everyone talking about this game and playing it in videos? Do you think it's the next best thing in comforting games that give you endless opportunities and hours of fun?

Welp, sorry, but this isn't that game. It's super enjoyable, at times, and very cute. There are some things about this game that are great, especially when there are tasks within your control, but outside of that the game feels rushed by developers and lacking some seriously important traits of a good cozy game.

Number one, there are countless impassable objects. Everywhere you walk there are trees, logs, stones, water formations, etc. that you can't traverse. It's a big pain, as they add nothing to the game other than annoyance. Sure, there are a few that block you from areas you aren't supposed to go to yet, but those make sense. The rest? Just something that seems like a bad decision on the developer's part.

Second, this is a cozy game that, at times, requires you to walk away and leave the game running for a few hours. You will get to points where the quests you need to complete are out of your control. You can't just go to sleep at any time (like Stardew) and there's nothing you can do but wait. I don't know about anyone else, but when I play a cozy game it's because I'm anxious and need an escape, not a window of time and then another stressor I have to come back to.

Third, and maybe most important, this game is stressful. As you level up, the items you need all seem to require the same materials. These materials are far and few between, and you'll constantly feel like you're behind. This is, in my opinion, a big issue in the design of the game. You shouldn't need the exact same items for every craft at one level. It literally makes the game impossible once you reach a certain point (without, as I said earlier, walking away for a while).

Honestly, and this is an honest review from someone who just loves games, this game had a ton of potential to be better. It's fun, it's cute, but it isn't well made and thought out. Genuinely, Cult of the Lamb has better crafting and tool plans than this game. I'd still recommend it, but very cautiously. K thx.\t\t\t\t\t
