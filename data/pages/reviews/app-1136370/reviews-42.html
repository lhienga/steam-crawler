\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(tl:dr pros and cons below)
So on one hand, I recommend this game, on the other hand, I don't. I really wish I could enjoy this game more. I originally set a timer for two hours when I started because I wasn't sure whether or not I'd like this game, so if I didn't, I could just refund. That being said, the first two hours are really enjoyable. I had more pros than cons and I felt like I would want to finish this game to completion. However when I got to about 5 hours in, I was getting bored of it really fast. Don't get me wrong, I have a lot of pros: I love the art style and story line, and it's a great concept for the game and it has great mechanics for the building and management part of the game.

However, this game feels very tedious. You're constantly having to run back and forth to get more and more resources. At least one hour of my gameplay was grinding resources I need for building, which become pointless in the next 30-60 minutes when you unlock beefier items and have to grind yet again to get items you need for making furniture. Honestly that's my only real con of the game but the endless grinding only for it to become irrelevant almost immediately after you craft and make the improvements you need just make the game boring. It feels like most of the game is just walking simulator. If they were to add a feature where you could use money to just buy the crafting resources it would literally fix this issue.


Pros:
-Great art style
-Cute Story
-Building mechanics are nice
-Cooking mechanics are nice
-I love management style games where you run your own business and this has great mechanics for that.

Con:
-Game gets boring fast
-Even with fast traveling it feels like a walking sim how often you need to go back for resources (Game should just add a resources shop where you can spend money for it if you want (they could still allow searching for it at same time)).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBnB has a great start but it start dropping fast somewhat due to how the game scores the player. Motels have two core factors and outlier factors that come into play as you progress and unlock more motels. The outlier factors are fine, micromanage x until you can pay someone to do it for you. The problem is the core factors of comfort and decor. These are purely mathematical therefor you cram everything you can into the designated guest rooms to meet their requirements. It's fine if they can't get into the room they'll still enjoy the wall of lamps blocking their entry and leave a 5 star review. The money you get matters very little, I'm told the game requires a large amount at the end but other than that you can unlock everything just off of the first two motels. That being said you'd think these are simple flaws but aside from the gameplay that was mentioned the side quests are few and far between offering very little substance aside from the motel business. Those amazing and funny characters we saw at the start? They're gone after the intro,

All this being said it is not a bad game per say it is however very shallow hence why people call it a cozy game. It is charming and very funny at times but at this price it's really not fair to not have more content in such a simple framework.

Update: Now with the price increase especially I highly suggest everyone to avoid this title for it has no where near enough content for the asking price. DO NOT BUY\t\t\t\t\t
