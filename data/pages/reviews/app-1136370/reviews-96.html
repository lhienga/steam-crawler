\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this game but there are still a few caveats you should know before buying it.
At the time of writing this, there are still various bugs and gameplay balancing issues yet to be solved. However, there is still a lot to like and enjoy about the game and it looks like the devs are actively listening to and implementing feedback.

Pros:
Great art style
Exploration + story driven gameplay with an economy + sim management aspect (Can also be a con)
Fun cast of characters and continuity within the story / setting
You can progress at your own pace and not have to min/max everything to progress further into the game

Cons:
Cooking mechanic is extremely clunky and not worth the effort
The building aspect can feel repetitive and tedious especially when needing to build multiple rooms to hit a quest objective.
There is not much flexibility on what you are allowed to build until the end of the game when you finally have all of the recipes.
Lacks a scrap mechanic which makes reusing lower tier furniture awkward and unfun
Could do with a shop mechanic for those who want to purely build and not have to run around the map collecting materials
Generally needs number tweaking with most systems inside of the game

I still enjoyed the core gameplay and story of the game to warrant me getting all of the achievements. I just believe that in its current state, it could do with a good amount of QOL changes to make the whole package a lot smoother and less frustrating in some places.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't stress how DISAPPOINTING this game was. The art is fantastic and the premise is cute. I wanted to like this game. But the economy is unbalanced, the game is full of glitches, and everything is so tedious. The amount of half torsos from guests in sleeping animations you see in the middle of the dining room, glitchy employees taking money for services that won't turn on, guests saying their path is blocked when there's nothing in their way, bathroom doors locking when no one is inside, etc. just made the game PAINFUL.

The game basically boils down to this: pick up all the random stuff on the ground, craft, guests are unhappy almost immediately, find out there's a better updated crafting recipe to replace the stuff you previously had, that stuff is slightly bigger than the old stuff, so you have to redo your entire floor plan to increase the place by a few comfort points, carry a bunch of outdated crafted items you can't do anything with until you cave and throw them away, find another resort, that resort requires a new special room or labor intensive mechanic, and repeat. Also, the game economy is busted. You suddenly go from having more gold than you know what to do with, to getting LITERALLY 30 new blueprints you need to buy for about 1.5k gold each. Then you're broke and need more money and materials and suddenly your guests are unhappy all over again.

Two things were just bad enough to make me stop playing. #1 was that all of those upgrades included upgrades to those special rooms that I thought we were done with because the game showed no sign of updating them for several hours (like the bar from A24 gets an upgrade in the LAST LOCATION YOU UNLOCK). I made so many bar stools and tables and now they're all useless. #2 was Julia, one of the animal friends you can hire, glitched out and took thousands of gold to turn on her services without activating. The game stole my money not once, but twice. At that point I had to rage quit.

This game desperately needs a quality of life increase. Here are several suggestions. #1, create a way to recycle old crafted items for some of the materials you used to make them (or just for gold); the beaver friend you meet in the beginning would be perfect for this. #2, some of the quality of life increases are way too hidden and need to be given earlier in the game (like building from anywhere and managing your cabins remotely). #3, remove inventory maximums from the bear stash (please, the inventory system in this game is unforgiving). #4 make it so you can craft with what you have stored in the bear stash. #5 please for the love of God give this bear a journal of some kind with what's left to unlock; for example, gray out the higher level bed crafting recipes so someone can know that the beds improve after level 3 (that way people don't waste all of their materials on lower level upgrades thinking that's the best they can do for their cabins).

These things plus fixing the glitches would make this game playable, but as it is the game is a hard pass for me. I tried to like it and gave it several hours hoping it would get better, but it just never did. I hope some of the bugs get fixed and the quality of life increases because otherwise this game has so much potential.\t\t\t\t\t
