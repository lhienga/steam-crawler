Overall, a cute game, pretty enjoyable for the most part.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for a deep, strategy driven management game this is not it. However if you want something cute and comfy I can recommend Bear and Breakfast.

The management is fairly simple. You build bedrooms (and other recreational rooms later on) and decorate the rooms with furniture you've built using resources you collect while exploring trying to match your guests requirements. There is some thought required when designing room layouts since you can't change the exterior shape of the building. At a few points collecting resources felt a tad grindy, but the devs have been working hard to balance the game based on community feedback.

I was surprised how much story content the game has as I was expecting it to have just a bit to set the stage. The story is mostly light-hearted with some good jokes and fun, but doesn't shy away from having a few gloomy bits either There are many areas to visit and a cast of colorful characters you get to meet along the way. I wish there was more interactions with the characters outside the story beats though, just a bit more dialogue would be enough.

Completing the game 100% took about 23 hours. I'd say that completing the story with bear minimum would take about half of that.\t\t\t\t\t

Overall a relaxing game that was just ok. There were some design choices that made the experience a bit tedious and annoying. Do not go into this game thinking it's a management game, because it's not. The B&B's mostly manage themselves while you gather materials and complete quests.

What I liked:
  • Art direction and animations were so charming. Best part of the game
  • Character designs were memorable and incredibly cute.
  • Sound design and ambiance made everything feel more immersive and have character.
  • Music was super chill. Good vibes all around
  • Room planning and decorating felt mostly intuitive, which is impressive considering the fixed perspective.
  • Writing was light-hearted and wholesome.
  • Never felt pressured to complete any tasks in a hurry. Was free to always take my time.

What I didn't like:
  • The UI. Needed more tabs for finding things. Had to keep scrolling a ton playing Where's Waldo.
  • Not being able to sell items made my inventory suffer quite a bit.
  • The story felt tacked on since it loses focus and characters disappear from plot for long periods.
  • Sometimes hard to tell what part of the terrain blocks you while walking around in the wild.
  • Putting decoration as a measured value made me more inclined to stuff duplicates of the same items in every room to save space while increasing decoration rating; rather than having furniture compliment each other or have any themeing.
  • No real endgame. Once the story is done there is hardly much to do.
  • Some bugs with item placing and guests AI pathing. Had to restart the game or skip the day multiple times to fix them.
