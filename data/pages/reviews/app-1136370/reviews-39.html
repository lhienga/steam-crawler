\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCAUTION - NOT FOR YOUNG BEARS

OK so for a human this game is nicely made, plays reasonably well but can be quite annoying, for one thing it is not at all consistent with the recipes. If you acquire one in a quest it slots immediately, if you buy one you have to right click or it just sits taking up an inventory slot and annoying you until you guess how to do it.
For another thing, the juggling of several quests can be quite irritating if you are a stress A type because they are not designed to be done one at a time. I spent the first hour extremely anxious about mama bear because he hadn't found the stupid alligator weed. I bought this game after seeing the flash of annoyance on CallmeKevin's face when he played it, and I have to say I made much the same face.

Now if you are a young bear playing this I do not recommend this game at all, this is for several reasons:

1. Hank is nice, but he is not a good delegator. If you have friends mooching off your family and they suggest a project, do not allow them to stand around doing nothing whilst you run about daft doing all the tasks. Delegation is something you need to get a handle on to get on in life. Also, he has a nice answer for almost everything and trust me when I say nice guys really do finish last. Someone else is going to get the benefit of his work whilst he continues to slave away.

2. Both young children and young bears should be aware that humans are a pollutant and you do not want them on your territory. As the story lightly touches on, they set fire to things and leave garbage everywhere. Look what happened to the Native Americans, do not encourage humans anywhere near you if you are sensible. We do not wish to see future generations of bears having to join the army to have an income or sitting around drunk running casinos on crap land set aside for reservations.

3. Hank is not considerate of his caretaker mama bear. He should have gone straight home and let her know he was OK and pursuing this project rather than taking several days to remember about her. This is not good bear behaviour.

4. This is not Hank's fault, but the game advice for room sizes is not at all sensible, Hank needs to be encouraged to think bigger.

5. Hank needs safety gloves and boots if he is to pursue a career in building. Goat lady also needs to be more protective of her stuff. A baseball cap and shorts is neither going to protect him whilst building nor keep him at all warm.

Rant over.

Even though there is an autofill button for all your action cards and placing the food in the correct cards, I find myself doing it manually just because I enjoy how the game has it all set out.

Would highly recommend this beautiful and quirky game if you like games similar to Stardew Valley, Harvest Moon and The Sims, as it is very much a mash of three in the best of ways!\t\t\t\t\t
