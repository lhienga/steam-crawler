Review at 20 hours: I’m sad that I waited so long before getting this game. I absolutely love it. There are a few minor issues dealing with inventory, but nothing that I couldn’t work with. I love the different areas and how much you can go around collecting. The characters are fun. I adore the art style. Some of the quests for the “inns” seem a little like busy work, but they’re incentive to “upgrade” the place. So it makes sense. A few times, I had to wait to progress further in the main story before I could finish certain upgrade quests. If you enjoy management games; Bear and Breakfast is perfect.

I played on Steam Deck, and that’s where most of my issues arose. I had to manually match a controller to the game. Luckily there was one labeled for use for the steam deck. I had to figure out a few control buttons though. Like L2 learns recipes and blueprints. LB puts items into your inventory from the building screen. Etc. Also, the text between characters would get cutoff by the character image. I had one cut scene that I didn’t see any dialogue at all due to it getting cut off screen. Regardless of the steam deck issues, I still had a blast playing.\t\t\t\t\t
