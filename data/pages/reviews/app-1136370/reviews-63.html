\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA beautiful, chill game to play when you wanna unwind, even for people who don't usually play games, and with a more interesting story than I would have expected from this genre.
It's also beginner/non-gamer-friendly because even though the pacing might be a little faster in the late game (if you want to make the most out of all your cabins per day), the game hardly punishes you for not being efficient, fast enough, or for not having good enough rooms or enough money. You don't need to spend money to maintain your properties, just certain resources you can find spawning around the maps, and the same goes for crafting furniture. So instead of having to do a lot of math in your head and stress out about your profits you can just play at your own pace.
I like the style of humor in the game, it's a mix of innocent/cute and sarcastic sometimes, the characters are likeable and funny, so I encourage you to talk to them and do the side quests, especially since they'll unlock items and other interesting things for you.
Bear and Breakfast is indeed missing some quality of life things (too little inventory space, no auto-stacking, etc.) and has a few bugs here and there, but the devs seem very willing to listen to feedback and improve the game. Despite these things, I still recommend this game without a doubt. Give it a try, forget about your complicated life stuff and relax a little.\t\t\t\t\t
