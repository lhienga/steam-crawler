\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBehind the awesome graphics, the game hides a quite bad user experience in the leveling and the gameplay.
  1. I wished the player had the actual feeling of improving one B&D, its very own unique B&D; instead the game constructed each area to introduce the player to a new room in a new B&D. You spend your time waiting for the client to come and leave, to go back and forth between the zones etc. At the 3rd zone you will start abandoning the 2 previous one after leveling them.So you don't feel really invested in that world. You cannot really improve the world, everything looks like trash even at the end game. For instance you will be asked to renovate a radio station and the lighthouse, but just in front of their door it's still a landscape of devastation and trash piles...
  2. Even if the mysterious background story seems appealing,the narration is frustrating due to the UI. The dialogues are lengthy which is not a bad thing by itself. No, what's bad is that the dialogues are displayed one sentence after the other, so you basically keep smashing the space bar 20+ times for each interaction.
  3. And about the rest of the gameplay:
    • technical rooms have a bad aura which should be countered in theory with decoration, but you don't have to take care of those maluses since bedrooms are never next to them. So that game mechanic is pointless.
    • the heater consumes supplies but you have so much and you very quickly go from lvl1 to lvl3 heater
    • the end game is artificially limited by money because you have to buy the last ressources to build, while all the game you just collect them>the game totally eludes how the cute animals get animal products such as milk and eggs... Again an artificial limit to force you to buy something to pass the quests
    • the pathfinding of the clients is quite awful but at least you can let them get lost, it doesn't affect your incomes.
    • the heat criteria is buggy. People are feeling cold while the temperature is higher than their needs. Agin it doesn't affect the gameplay.
I really wished I could loved it after all these hours but here is my final observation: I finished the main quest while I never opened the penultimate B&D and just occupied one third of the last one, while abandoning all 3 first B&Ds...\t\t\t\t\t
