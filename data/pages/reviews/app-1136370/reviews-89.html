\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNice, whimsical, casual game for people who don't like time constraints and tight linear stories. The idea that people are staying at a hotel run by a bear is hilarious. What's even more hilarious is that you win more and more clothes over time that you can put onto your bear and the customers actually think you're a person. They no longer run away in fear when you're around the various properties and I think that's hilarious.

Some aspects I think need tweaking or changed

- taking furniture out of a room is a huge pain in the ass because there is no key binding to just put it away into your inventory. you have to actively put the piece of furniture into an empty space in your inventory otherwise it will just stay in the room. this is especially annoying if you're trying to upgrade your room levels and have to switch out multiple pieces at one time.

- there is no way to run so going back and forth between quests is kinda tedious. the bus system helps but if you're running back and forth between a property and a quest giver, it can have you running all over the map at a single speed.

- there's no way to pass the time except for at night when you're allowed to sleep. this is especially relevant in the beginning of the game where you're just waiting around for customers to stay a night and then leave. sometimes i'd be done decorating or setting up my location before mid day and i'd just have to alt tab and do something else waiting for the time to pass. at that point my inventory was pretty full so there was no use in scavenging.

- the way the game is drawn, it's hard to tell what you can or can't walk through. there are textures like rocks and other formations where it's not obvious on whether or not it's an obstacle. especially in the more detailed areas like the forest.

- there isn't a way to sell items yet so there was a point where i had to wait for guests to leave because i had no money for my next quest but i had tons of items in my inventory i could have sold for extra cash. same goes with the trash store. there isn't a way to accumulate trash other than guests leaving messes or discovering a new area and clearing out the empty buildings. i hope they implement a trading system so you can get rid of things that are clogging up both the stash and bag. i also had the issue of buying one too many furniture blueprints so that was automatically money wasted.

- there needs to also be a deconstruction function where if you have lower level items then you can salvage them for resources. i have tons of level 1 and 2 items that are just sitting in my stash right now doing nothing and they took a reasonable amount of resources to make so being able to get SOMETHING back instead of just trashing them would make more sense.

- there needs to be away to see what level a room is. it took me a while to get my dining room to level 2 because i couldn't figure out which pieces didn't fulfill the reqs. in the bedroom menu at the desk, it shows the raw values for each thing like decoration and comfort but it doesn't help you figure out what level the game considers each room. especially since many bedroom items look very similar despite being different levels, i think there needs to be a system or some indication to help make it easier to know if you're fulfilling the requirements for leveling up.

- there's no way to split stacks so if you're cooking, you'll cook the entire stack of whatever you put in so i may not want to make 40 bowls of apples. also sometimes the inventory will not collapse and consolidate stacks so you might have like 4 of the same furniture taking up space but you have to manually put them in a stack.

overall i'm enjoying the game. i play a lot of simulators and crafting based games and so far i'm impressed. i like the art style and i like how there are different themes to each map area. the characters are likable and the dialogue is entertaining. i'll be on the lookout for updates.\t\t\t\t\t
