\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't consider this a negative review, but the only reason I continued playing and endured through a repetitive and quite unsatisfying game loop (especially near the end) is to see how this game ends and sadly it wasn't worth it for me personally. It was fun and quite addicting at the start, But the way you learn how to craft new essential items to progress the story wasn't fun because of how repetitive it was. I absolutely loved the art direction. but the confusing story, tedious game loop and all over the place progression made me not recommend this sadly. It's also quite buggy rn, so if you wanna pick it up, maybe wait a lil.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBear and Breakfast's main attraction is the management simulation, not the story or the characters themselves. The story is simply a curtain for the much more fascinating cycle of building lodges, hosting guests, and then spending hard-earned cash on grander renovations.

There is a lot of linearity in Bear And Breakfast. Quests are required to unlocked new areas and advance the plot, but the quests themselves are tedious. Making certain dishes for the NPC to progress the quest is an example.

While Bear and Breakfast does have some flaws, it's a game I hadn't realized I wanted. It's charming, funny, and addictive. As I worked through the quests and upped the values of my properties, I couldn't pry myself away from it. Even though this game has some drawbacks, there are great rewards that keep players coming back for more. You'll enjoy playing Bear & Breakfast from start to finish because it's full of all the cozy vibes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBear and Breakfast is a wonderful, funny management game that chronicles Hank (a bear) and friends as they (or more accurately, Hank) build lodges and hotels for incoming tourists, as proposed by the inflatable mascot of corporation Pawn Voyage, Fin.
Many people have negative or neutral reviews of the game due to its "long wait times", but recent updates have decreased wait times to the point where it is not difficult to always be doing something. An excellent, however not perfect (bugs, minor detail misses) game that is fun to play.
After another 20 hours of playtime, I still recommend it. Later, when you need to cook and heat the inn, it was very frustrating and annoying, but you eventually (assuming you are doing side quests) get automation for those things and more. At one point in the game, with the aid of some staff, I gained about 50,000 gold by strictly caring for resorts and spending very little for about 10-20 in-game days. Believe it or not, that required part of the game is actually very nice and satisfying, especially when you do get that 50 grand.\t\t\t\t\t
