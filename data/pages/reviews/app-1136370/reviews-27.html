\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you want to run one bed and breakfast like they promised you... you might be disappointed.

This is my first review on Steam ever. Unfortunately, it's not a positive review. I am so sad to admit that I simply don't enjoy this game. Maybe it's my fault because reading about the game I thought I would run a bed and breakfast being a bear. It turns out that this game is not about running one place. For me it felt like It was a mass production without a soul.

You simply progress in the game by discovering new empty buildings to prepare for robotic guests to arrive and stay and as soon as you are done with one place, you go for another one, and you have to keep coming back to the previous building just to level it up for your guest's growing needs. I don't feel attached to any of my bed and breakfasts; it seems like the buildings don't really have a soul and I'm just creating more and more to go through the game and finish it.

I wish I could just have one building and make it better and prettier...
a building that would make sense.
A bed and breakfast that would make me feel like home.
A bed and breakfast where the reviews from my guest would really count.

Not putting million objects in a room which looks ridiculous and doesn't make any sense, just to get points to be able to progress in the game. So sad. So disappointing. But I'll go back to the game now and I will give it another and another chance and maybe I will like it this time...

Fingers crossed.

I just felt I needed to write this review for someone who wanted the same as I wanted from this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was addicted to this game for a while and think the art, design, story, and mechanics are super fun. The menus were a little more of a learned process instead of intuitive, but once you learned one the rest of the menus are super easy to navigate. My only thing I hope eventually will be optionalized is the furnaces and food tables. "Unsightly/loud" rooms such as boiler rooms and kitchens were set to be at a disadvantage in stats when you install them into the hotels and you would need to use mufflers and decorations to rise the stats back up to acceptable ranges that guests would tolerate. It was hard enough trying to mask them with decorations so that the surrounding rooms didn't suffer, but keeping them full of wood in between refilling kitchens and keeping track of how your desk managers were assigning rooms, it just become really tedious. I haven't played since I hit that point cause it feels more like mental math work than a cozy hotel sim. I am crossing my fingers that there will one day be a toggle for those kind of things to be self sustaining or if you want the challenge to do them yourself cause I would prefer to just focus on the story since it is such a cute game. One of my friends had the same experience, but otherwise great game and overall a fun time.\t\t\t\t\t
