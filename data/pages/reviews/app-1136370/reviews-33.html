Very nice and relaxing game with original topic and beautiful audiovisual design, that you can play both alone and with your family. Convenient combination of a management simulator of running multiple hostels/hotels and a story-adventure, which will explain motives and history of the main protagonist in a pleasant way.
Most of negative reviews stem from the current hectic way of life, which is also reflected in spending free time - this is neither an fast paced action game nor a micro management sim. where you can watch your guests and perfectly tune up every non essential nonsense. In the same way, you won't equip all hostel rooms to the maximum on the first time, you don't "level up" immediately to full reviews of your guests and the highest financial rating, and you don't finish the whole game in one run, if you dont want to ruin your gaming experience. This game deserves a bit of your time and patience. You should remember the times when there wasn't another new and exciting game every week with even higher ratings and better graphics, where you went back to the pieces you played a while ago and enjoyed them in depth, when you engaged your imagination while playing and weren't bound by one single optimal layout, where you didn't just go for maximum yield, but played a little with the freedom and options that the game gives you, play not only the game, but also with the game...go back and renovate rooms in already "finished" maps, go back to places that you have already went through and enjoy incredible detail, which the creators kindly incorporated into all locations. Enjoy the brilliantly executed vagaries of the weather and a great soundtrack, stop and watch what's going on around you, brew a mug of cocoa or grog to go with it, take your time! Don't try to finish it in 18 hours, if you don't have time, get the game in your library and keep it for the moment when you want to stop the everyday hustle and bustle for a bit.
There is also this mystical "think it through" moment with mystery godly line of this adventure (dont want to spoil it for ya) for brooding types of people who want to delve deeper, that is not common to these types of game, but it fits exactly where it sits :)

So, should you be newcomer and not experienced gamer or experienced matador not looking only for first person shooters, there will come a time and life situation, when you will appreciate this little gem of a game. It is like this old picture book that you liked as a small kid and you discover it reissued in your small book store at the corner.

The Christmas DLC and previous patches are an example of good support from the creators - thank you!, from whom we will hopefully see many expansions with new locations, tasks and items - I will be happy to return for another quiet afternoon at B&B!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never had so much trouble keeping myself busy in a management game. Been really difficult for me to play this game for more than 10 minutes. It honestly feels like a waiting simulator. The system for collecting valuables does not feel rewarding at all- simply waiting for more people to visit my rooms for the chance they drop maybe 6 valuables? Not much to keep me occupied during that in-between time that I'm waiting for humans to leave and come back either, which just makes me close the game. Tried really hard to like this game since the art is really adorable, and overall the decorating+management aspects are fun, but the waiting and waiting for valuables to show up is really just grating.

Overall, wouldn't suggest this game if you like to go hard in sims and really decorate the hell out of em. If you do get it, have some youtube on hand cause your gonna be bored waiting for day to be over so you can sleep lol.
