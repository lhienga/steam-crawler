\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is pretty much perfect for a busy college student. The art is easy on the eyes, the story line is low pressure, and the entire game follows a pretty basic formula for completing various tasks.

PROS: Fantastic art style and character design, charming dialogue, and very easy to play once you get the hang of things. The NPCs all feel like their own people (my favorites being Ipswitch and Jax) that have their own lives. Lots of things to collect for any item hoarders like myself. I have yet to finish the story as I am taking my time with the game, but I do enjoy the snippets I have seen so far. As someone in an intense major with mental illness, it is a soothing game to unwind with and genuinely enjoy after a hard day.

CONS: This game is not for you if you do not enjoy management games as the game does not stray far from its genre. The NPCs are fun, but you do not get to interact with them much at all beyond their quests. I will say the game can feel tedious at times due to having to wait for guests to stay a certain amount of nights, waiting to unlock new areas, etc. As an item hoarder I do wish there was more storage, but I have not completed the story yet so I cannot say if this is remedied later in the game. I have noticed when I am transferring items from the shark's bin to my inventory (by pressing the Take All button), things will sometimes get deleted in the process when I do not have inventory space. It is not a huge deal, but mildly frustrating for sure.

Overall I would rate this at 4.5/5 stars!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLovely game, but I was left with mixed feelings.

The art style is top-notch and the concept is nice and fun. What I didn't like was the pacing of the quests and progression, and how the darker story teased at the beginning is explored only at the very end of the game, leaving a weird gap in the middle and then ending very suddenly. The NPCs have lovely designs and clearly they had some personality build into them, but they fall a little flat because there's very little dialogue outside the quests, so they don't get an opportunity to really shine. Which is shame, as the dialogue too was well written and hilarious.

The gameplay itself is simple, if a bit tedious towards the end? Some quality of life improvements are needed in my opinion, such as letting you sell items or ability to select multiple items at once. The cooking minigame was my personal favorite, though collecting ingredients felt like a chore and took most of my time because how limited some of them are in quantity. Same goes with crafting materials.

Overall a cute, decent and casual game with a lot of potential. Despite my complaints I did have a good time.
