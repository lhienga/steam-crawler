Sad Bear Noises

I really tried to give this game an opportunity but, when I got to Winterberry and saw the TERRIBLE layout of the place, that just defeated my spirit. Just make a straight wall, for heaven’s sake! Developers, give us actual SPACE to work with instead of trying to get cute with pillars! If this game doesn’t even give me the little consolation of not firing my OCD, then I shall not overlook all its other flaws anymore.

This game doesn’t respect your time

Most of the gameplay, specially early on, it’s just a waiting game. Put two pieces of furniture and wait two days. At the half hour of playing, I was already standing with nothing to do but wait. Other than going around picking stuff just because, there’s nothing else to do a lot of the time, and even that can play against you because they put limited inventory space in the game.
Most of the “story” missions consist of “Go do X and come back” which it’s exclusively used to pad out gametime. And example: In Winterberry, you meet this guy who knows how you can heat up your new location, he will teach you but first you have to help him, sounds fair. Now, repair the resort, go back to him, he tells you to find sauna rocks, you go elsewhere and find then just laying around, go back to him, he teaches you about boiler rooms, go to the resort, put a boiler room in it, go back to him, he tells you to fuel the thing, go back to the resort, fuel the boiler, go back to him… see where I’m going with this?

This game has little to no actual content

At some point I sincerely came back to the store page to see if this was an Early Access game, when I was still defending the game in my head. The world feels completely empty, every NPC repeats the same two lines of dialogue again and again and in some cases doesn’t even move from its place. The two characters that are supposedly “best buddies” with the main character disappear completely after you open your first location and don’t reappear in the story at least at the point I left it, the same happens with the main character’s mom and the beaver gent who helps you repair the rundown building. The ominous thing that happens at the very start of the game didn’t came up again in the ten HOURS I’ve played so far. Unlike the animated trailer seemed to imply, there is no interaction AT ALL with your guest, the most that happen is they having a thought bubble with an emoji on it when they see you. Everything in the game is controlled by literally switches, in sign form, that supersede any character interaction, just asking “Want to repair this location? Yes or no” and the bear doesn’t even answer in-character most of the time, just with the same generic “yes” or “no”. It really feels like an unfinished game.

Conclusion

There are a lot of things still to discuss, like the excessive quantity of roadblocks that confine the player’s freedom; the fact that progression is poorly paced by delegating mechanics to specific buildings that coerce the player’s creativity; some weird duplication bug that I seemed to stumble upon at random but never was able to replicate… but all that it’s too much already.
I really expected to love, and I mean LOVE, this game. The concept has a great amount of charm and the genre is one of my favorites (just look at my profile), but this is just not good at all and that really saddens me. Like seriously saddens me. I can’t understand how Bear and Breakfast has the score it has on Steam.

tl;dr: This game had great potential but it’s empty and barren like my soul.\t\t\t\t\t

It's going for a story rich management mix but it falls a bit short on both fronts.
