\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter fully completing bear and breakfast, i have mixed feelings. I wanted to love this game. I told all my friends and family how excited i was for it. But overall, it feels like it just wasn't playtested enough and I think it really would have benefited from early access. It's a fun and adorable concept that you really want to like. But the more you play the harder it gets to enjoy it? It has horrendous pacing and the quests don't line up with how you actually play through the story. It's misleading to say that you run a bed and breakfast, because really it becomes you barely managing 5 different properties where each one felt like a waste of time? In the beta you focused more on just the one and making guests happy and cooking. In the full game you do that a little bit until you automate everything and there's no reason to manage anything anymore, and then you move onto the next property? Not to mention the "managing" isn't really great and you have limited options. The story was cute but felt poorly done due to the bad pacing of the game and the story progression. The characters are all really well designed and interesting but once you meet and basically use the characters for what they are meant for, you barely interact if at all, with any of them again. You never talk to your friends again until late game, and once you get the full ending, the story just. Ends. And there is absolutely no reason to keep playing because there is nothing left to do or interact with. No reason to keep the b&b running because you don't need to? It's just so disappointing. But it is a cute world and it's fun to gather and decorate, but it just feels so hollow. I think if you like cozy, less in-depth management games it can be enjoyable to play, but just not satisfying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished the game at 35 hrs. Overall a very cute game, and it hits on that management/collecting resources that i enjoy.

Positives:
-Art style is adorable.
-Inventory and how to select things (after you figure it out on your own cause its not apparently clear) is well organized.

Negatives (that are more annoyances):
-navigating the areas is sometimes annoying as you cant tell what you can walk through verse what you can not, so you spend a lot of time bumping into things.
-no way to re-sell your old items. you can only trash them. It would be great if you could take all your old items back to Took (a cute garbage raccoon) and sell to him. Even if its for like half resources back (example spend 6 wood and 2 glass to make an item, sell back gets you 3/1)
-the quests seem either too quick half the time or at the wrong time. There would be quests i unlock BUT i cant do them until a different quest is completed but the quest that needs to be done hasn't been unlocked. (hopefully that makes sense). So I would look up play-throughs cause i was getting so frustrated thinking i was doing something wrong...but nope just strange timing.

1 Big complaint: The story has no conclusion... it just ends. There is not even a banner that appears that says something as simple as "main story concluded". Because it ends by the dialogue just stopping and you're left wondering if there is something else.

All of that considered, i still highly recommend this game. It has a ton of appeal and I found myself wanting to play it in all my spare time.\t\t\t\t\t
