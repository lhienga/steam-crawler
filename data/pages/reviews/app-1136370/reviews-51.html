\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot this game on sale for black Friday and I am officially obsessed. To list some of the positives:
-From the simple base of the game play to the ways different mechanics are introduced to liven it up, the game manages to be interesting enough for my tastes to keep me playing for hours.
-The art style for the characters is cute, and the backgrounds and stage design is stunning.
-The whole game feels like a love letter to the diverse beauty of different parts of the american west coast, which is an area I have a personal attachment to after moving here for university.

There are a few downsides though that should be mentioned, though to my knowledge some of these are possibly already in the works for addition to the game in later updates.
-You cannot sell items you have already crafted, even though said items do have a coin value listed on the little card that pops up when you hover over them in your inventory. In fact there is no ability to sell items in game, which puts a lot more pressure on how you spend resources and coins in a way that doesn't add positively to game play.
-There currently isn't multiplayer options, which I'm personally 50/50 as to whether or not I think would be a good addition anyways.
-The game does have a few glitches and bugs here and there, but to be perfectly honest that could just be from the garbage laptop I play it on and none of the glitches or bugs have fully broken the game in a way that couldn't immediately be resolved just by exiting and restarting the game.

I can't wait to see what is added in future updates to the game, I have a feeling it'll kind of update like Stardew did where the base game was already fun, but updates over the years have kept adding fresh mechanics and activities to keep people coming back.\t\t\t\t\t
