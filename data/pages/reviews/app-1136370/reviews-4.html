\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game isn't horrible, it has a cute design going for it, is very fun early on as you rebuild and then keep remodeling these different "resorts". But this game becomes a horrible grind later in the game as you need high end materials and recipes that cost outrageous amounts for what you're bringing in. Sadly the trick to bypassing this is stocking up the resorts that require heat or food, hiring the right help to book your rooms, and then just walk away from the game as your money builds up. You'll be doing this often, sadly because there's really nothing to keep your busy and entertained in the time that it takes to build up the massive fund you'll need to buy the things you need to upgrade and to offer better foods.

Then there's the story and characters. The characters don't bring too much. You'll interact with them once introduced but they won't really offer much shortly afterwards. You'll need to go back to them at some point to get a quest item, and that's about it. The guest coming to your resorts and just random nothing npcs. The story beyond playing as a bear, rebuilding these locations and bringing back visitors feels really out of place.

I recommended the game because it has things going for it, but this is really a game that requires an additional options. There are things you'll probably enjoy about the game if this is the type of game you play, but it is very flawed and has a bit working against it that might turn you off, even if this is your type of game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy thoughts on this game are very similar to everyone else's lists of cons you've probably read already in the reviews, and like others have said, it truly is so upsetting to not want to recommend this game. As much as I love the premise of this game and really enjoy the art (which is something that has carried my enjoyment of games before), the gameplay loop and mechanics really just bog it down so much even this long after its full release + many patches. I enjoyed the first part of the game, and that made me very hopeful for the rest of it, but after only about 10 hours (and probably more like 8 hours because I came back after a year or so of initially playing and made a new save), I feel like I just can't bring myself to open the game again. It has become a series of incredibly simple fetch quests, and waiting around for materials to spawn and for the days to pass. There is only function to sleep through some of the night, and otherwise you just have to wait the day out for the next morning to progress or to get the money you need to upgrade your locations. In terms of decoration and management of the inns, you are kind of trapped into using very specific items and layouts to meet requirements, and with how many different locations you unlock in such a short time the game kind of flip flops between being too overwhelming/confusing or so incredibly boring. The limited inventory space and clunky buying and selling/trashing options also tend to outweigh any potential for designing the inns to be enjoyable after the first couple locations. Obviously there are people who did love this game though, and maybe you will too. I never really got invested in the story and dialogue, and that seems to be something that elevated other peoples experiences and motivated them to finish the game. I think there is some really good concepts here and clearly a lot of love put into it, and so I hope see this teams future endeavours either in updating this game or in their future projects!\t\t\t\t\t

Pros :


- Cute and appealing graphic
- Dialogue spoken by the characters is great and I can see that they thought about it for a while at least
- OST is great most of the time



Cons :


- UI has some weird decisions that are unpractical and annoying
- OST picked for certain scenes was unfitting especially at the end which made the game ending even more sour in the mouths than it already was
- The game focuses on the motel decorating and managing but you can't rotate the camera to see other parts of the wall
- None of the characters appear for long enough, so all of them seem like side chars with little backstories with some of them
being unfinished
- Cooking is wonky and unpolished, some features are missing that would make it more enjoyable
(later on, you get an option to automatically select all ingredients needed to make a recipe which makes it easier but not fun)
- You have plenty of materials lying on the grounds, once you pick it up it will re-spawn in a few minutes so what's the point of it? We could have unlimited materials from the get-go then because it kinda feels like we have already.
- You get quest after quest without any time to breathe in between them. At one point it gets ridiculous...
You finish a certain quest from one specific character, and the moment you're done with talking, that same character has a quest marker on it (all quests work this way btw). What does that character say as their first line?
"Hi bear, it's nice to see you again after a long while." What long while exactly? It felt so wrong and out of place...


SPOILER CONS

----------

- The beginning of the game starts great and even has some spooky vibes which I liked.
You start your journey with two other characters and at one point they just leave and you never interact with them
afterward.
- Your first quest is to get something for your mama bear and till the end of the game, you never give her what she
asked you for. Instead, you derail into making motels which is fine in a way but they never touched majorly on that
part.
- At the beginning of the game you have some weird dreams, and until the near end, this is never touched on. The
whole ending seems like it was made on the spot and they never gave it too much thought.
The way this gets touched on also feels weird and random... Once you finish Grandma's storyline you get
a quest out of nowhere to find out most of the things about your weird dreams.
- Once you finish the whole storyline you don't get any credits and there you are, mindlessly wondering for a while
what to do next as the game isn't telling you anything. (Until now game was holding your hand)
You go looking for walkthrough just to find out this was ending which didn't feel complete as there are
some things left unresolved...

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWas a joy to play through, and originally caught my eye due to my love for eg. Stardew Valley. It was nice to not have to think about any fighting elements, so you were pretty much casually just unlocking new areas and managing your hotels along the way.
Some quests were not very clear to understand and follow, not to mention some actions not being very clear. While I understood why we wanted nights in the game, it was very tedious to play around those times due to poor visibility. Also, I felt like due to the gameplay I can't really skip nights as there is so much to do and keep running (especially after locking food). Most annoying part is NOT easily understanding what items or areas on a map that block or don't block you. I kept running into things or getting stuck, as it's definitely not clear. Eventually you'll learn in some places, but it was frustrating at times. Rotating items also sometimes annoying as you can't most of the time clearly see how they are placed (as you can't move the camera angle), not to mention difficult to pick up trash that is hidden behind decor. I also only at the end realized that trash in rooms made the decoration value of the room go down - why wasn't this explained early on?

Nevertheless - I liked the game more than some aspects annoyed me. Casual, not too hectic, nice look and feel, and definitely worth the money. Do play if games like this appeal to you! :)\t\t\t\t\t
