\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you’re into management-type beat games like stardew valley, have a deep and unexplained love for the tv-show, Gravity Falls, or just love bears and cosy artwork, then I’d recommend this game.

I started playing this game upon its release, and while I enjoyed the premise, there were far too many bugs for me to find it enjoyable, so I set it aside and waited for the developers to fix it up a bit. I definitely agree that this game would have benefited from an early-access release. So, almost a year later, I fully finished. I'm glad that I was able to have employees and give purpose to certain characters, although I'll admit, I got a bit stressed out in the middle of it all when I was trying to run three different inns and had one erratic possum that didn't help all that much. The art style is gorgeous, and the characters and the story are really sweet, if not a bit choppy at some points, but otherwise, I had fun (the goal).

I’m looking forward to picking this game back up after they release updates to the storyline/world later this year because this game is perfect for getting into the fall/winter season. ʕ ꈍᴥꈍʔ

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you want a slow, peaceful game then, yes, you'll like this.

Some lacking Quality of Lifes areas have ground away at my willingness to play this, but I suspect I'll cycle back to it. Really it's a sideways-thumb for me. A yes with caveats.

Biggest caveat: I tried playing it on controller but found the UI navigation too frustrating. Definitely a mouse and keyboard game, imo.

Now for the nitty-gritty complaints:

You can see all your rooms stats at the Check In desk.
The Build Menu is a separate item that you must walk outside to use. In the Build Menu you can only see a room's name and stats when you hover over an empty bit of floor. Doors interrupt your cursor and cannot be hidden. You can also place carpets and rugs which will interrupt your cursor. An overlay option in this menu to quickly review your rooms without packing and redeploying half your decor would do wonders.
I recommend making nice, big, spacious rooms if you play this game. I had fun dividing the resorts into dozens of rooms, stacked with nice comfy footstools, but... Yeah. Probably not the intended experience of a B&B!

You spend a lot of time plodding through the environment, scrounging for things to interact with and collect while you wait for progression to be made possible again by guests finishing their stay.

My cramped motel has definitely given me enough money to start afresh and make some nice, spacious rooms, but I've lost motivation. Main reason for this is the wish for a more robust filtering system. The different crafting menus and stores share this issue: the broad categories of "Furniture" and "Cooking Ingredients" don't cut it when you need to find the Bedroom specific furniture or just one piece of corn in an ocean of options. An option to filter by item level also wouldn't be amiss.

There are some Quality of Life features, like Shift+LMB to split a stack, so maybe there's others I'm unaware of.\t\t\t\t\t
