\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; left with more questions than answers, unfulfilled, and with a void in me. Buy during a sale if possible. :)
-------------------------

I tend to hyper-fixate on games and enjoy life-sim/management games (I am probably a Type A), so when I saw this game I was intrigued. On top of the genre, the art style and story line were another selling point. I remember opening this up and playing a bit and thinking to myself, "This is cute but the tutorial bits are just too much" and I had been slightly off put by it. I put the game down a few days later and would end up scrolling past it every time I went into my Steam library.

Eventually (3 days ago after one-ish year since purchase), I gave it another go and boom I was sucked into it (33+ hours in 3 days). I grinded it out night and day trying to rebuild the Valley and restore the relationship between Nature and Humans. I restlessly spent time ensuring every square was utilized in the best way possible, but also looked cute and appealing. (I admittedly got too invested after I found myself creating a backstory for each bedroom but that's just a personal issue as a chronic daydreamer) I even had a journal where I would math out how much was needed for the cabins and if hiring the gang of Gus, Wade, Jax, and Julia was necessary.

I loved it. I was enjoying myself with the side quests of restoring the inn and there was the entire build up of the story line with the Valley and its backstory and the characters we met. Imagine my surprise... when after investing so much time and energy into this game, the story just kinda ends with no real closure?

Like come on, how are you gonna feed me so much lore about Hank and his dead brother(bear) and these Gods who were having some sort of Celestial war and just give me nothing after that.

Also, Barbara I have beef with you for taking all of my hard earned money for some cruise. I thought you and I were building a bond and were forever.

And why allude to a captain and have this lighthouse fixture if he never even showed up ??

In the end, the story line just kinda seemed rushed and I wish there was more of an explanation because there were so many cool spots in the map that alluded to so much more. I just kinda wish I could replay it and experience it all over again for the rush, but the thought of having to replay the story line makes me too upset lol

So yeah, it's a great game with a one time play through for me but would purchase during a sale. :)
