\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give a neutral rating. It is not a BAD game, and I had fun playing and will finish playing it. The aesthetic is really cute and everything, but I think once I am finished with the missions I will close the game and never think about it again.
I am (from what I can tell) pretty close to finishing the story missions. Maybe some of these things change later on...?

Major things:

- The building layout being fixed is an understandable choice and allows for the buildings exterior to look really cute. However, it makes retroactively making rooms bigger pretty much impossible, unless you want to scrap your whole layout. With furniture getting bigger the higher the level gets, this makes upgrading rooms in earlier buildings something between mildly irritating and really annoying.

- Very few options to actually customize your rooms/buildings. Since you want/need to use the high level furnitur to meet the guests requirements, you don't really have any creative freedom here. The clutter decor items are nice, but i wishe there were more furniture decoration items - different chairs, table, more cupboards, etc. Relatively simple changes to help with this issue could be introducing different color varieties for items or giving the option to customize the wallpaper and floor of rooms. Or maybe let me place furniture from different room types in bedrooms so I can have a sofa. Or let me freely build walls to make more interesting room layouts with more space for wall decor.


Minor non-QoL things:

- It would be cool if there was some sort of interaction with the story-related buildings you fix across the map. They look cute and stuff, but they feel pretty useless because you rebuild them in a couple of clicks and then they never do anything ever again. Making them into shops, or additional quest givers (e.g. being able to sell food at the diner) would be cool.

- There is pretty much no interaction with the NPCs from early on. Your mom and friends stop existing after their bit is over and there is no reason (at least at the point I'm at) to even visit the starting area, whose name I forgot, again. Thinking about it, I don't thing I ever really had to visit the other areas without hotels (Dark Cove, Sawdust,...) either after finishing their story bits. The map is really cute and I like the design, but they are under utilized gameplay-wise imo.


Minor QoL things:

- I accidentally bough a recipe twice and now I con not sell the extra copy, unless I just misse dhow to do that. Why can I even buy a recipe twice, when there is no way to get rid of my first copy on accident?

- I wish the snow partricles in Winterberry disappeared when entering buildings and/or building mode, they are imo somewhat distracting when placing decor.

- Bathroom effect range is kinda low, which makes creative room layouts even more difficult.

- Hovering over the area exits on the map should tell me which area they lead to.

- Menus like the fast travel menu are half-manuverable via keyboard, but not completely? I can select the destination with the keys, but I need to confirm via mouse click. Would be great if I could control them entirely via keyboard.

- There is no way (afaik) to deposit something without dragging it to an empty inventory/chest slot. Having a hotkey to press for that would be sick.

- I feel like a lot of the lots are awkwardly sized, e.g. being 13 squares wide, meaning it is not possible to divide them into equal rooms


Overall, I think the game is missing an identity. There is not enough customization to make it a creative outlet, managing guests is not interactive and/or difficult enough to be a management sim and there is not enough interaction with the overworld and the characters to catch me with exploration or the story. I focussed mainly on the problems I have with the customization options because that is what the game is closest to doing right, but I wouldn't have a problem with these things if the game instead focussed on one of the other gameplay aspects more and did that better.
Conclusion: Nice mindless fun for a couple hours, but even for the 12€ I paid on sale, I wish there was a bit more substance.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor those considering Bear and Breakfast, as most people reading the reviews are, here is some things to take into consideration:

Pros:
The gameplay is addictive! It's more of a motel simulator than a bed and breakfast but it's still fun regardless.
The graphics are great! There's immersive weather designs, the furniture is SO so cute, and there's even physics with some of the clothing options.
The characters are lovable! And the character design is great. I felt like I emotionally connected with all the characters. (Except the old lady, she knows what she did ›:()
Did I mention it's addicting? I've spent about 40 hours on this game. It's normally something I play when I need to listen to something and need something to do with my hands.

Cons:
The mission guiding system is glitched in a few of the mid game/end game quests.
It's hard to find some quest items because they aren't being tracked and sometimes you can't find quest items until finishing other questlines. I go to the guides and discussions for a bit of the quests after spending hours looking for quest items…
Early on, there is a campfire site which is glitched. Sometimes if you place something in the campsite or try to remove something it'll start placing infinite items onto the ground or in your inventory.
Mid/late game there is a significant increase in needs for resources but not a lot of sources and sometimes you can only buy the items, which is rough when you need a bunch of it.

Overall, it's a cute game with some quirks. It just needs a few glitches worked out but other than that I can tell the developers and everyone else involved put a lot of work and care into it. I hope this helps for those deciding whether or not to buy it.\t\t\t\t\t
