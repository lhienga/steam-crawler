\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I'd recommend Bear & Breakfast, but not for the reason I would usually do for a management game.

Basically, I found myself really enjoying the story - firstly, it was a nice surprise to have something that was properly interesting and genuinely funny and touching in a genre that doesn't usually even bother with story, but also it went to some quite unexpected places.

However, I'm a little more torn on the core hotel-building mechanics of the game.

It's functional (occasionally guests bugged out and got stuck for no apparent reason was the only bug I had) and it's reasonably extensive; however, I found it quite repetitive. While you do have multiple types of rooms to build, a lot of them use the same basic approach (like area of effect) so it doesn't really change things up that much. A good example of the problem is that you have to put a minimum set of items into each room to make it meet the requirement for that type of room, but there's no options around that - you can't create just a toilet, it has to have a bath and sink too. Or you can't have a combined cinema and museum. And high level rooms require so many items I ended up cramming things in with little regard for layout and decor, especially in the smaller places.

It could also have done with a few more quality of life features (e.g., the ability to filter and sort things in the shops). And for a game with some environmental messages it was weird that you had no way to sell items back to shops or recycle them - you just have to trash things you don't want/need and then they magically disappear. [edit: recycling has just been added to the game.]

There was also an odd balance to resources availability where almost everything is so abundant it can re-spawn before you've been fully round the map and you have to throw a lot of it away, and then a handful of things that are very rare/expensive and create a sudden roadblock. They did tweak some of these, to be fair, but towards the end I was sleeping multiple days away just to get cash and it just made it feel a bit pointless.

But I'm being harsh - I did enjoy the game and I would freely admit I did rush through the story and it really shouldn't be played like that. It's supposed to be a chill game and you should play it focusing more on developing each location properly, rather than just hitting the minimum requirement so you can get to the next bit of the story like I did.

I'm really hoping there's either a DLC or sequel - I felt there were quite a lot of dangling threads that could be picked up (some of the NPC stories don't really get resolved as such) and I'd love to play more.\t\t\t\t\t
