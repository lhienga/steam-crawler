\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk I have complaints, but I'm still going to recommend it.
The game is really cute, and has a decent pace. I like being able to flesh out the hotels and not be limited by required room sizes. The writing and dialog were good, and it will take you a decent chunk of time to get everything.

My problems with the game are more tiny quibbles, but they should have been fixed long before now. There are some UI and key binding choices that make no sense. For example storage chests like the dining room or fuel chest are 2x4 tiny windows. They could have easily made it 4x4 and showed the entire thing. There is no key binding to quickly move things from inventory to chest or back. You will be going through a lot of items in this game. The map scroll speed is also very slow.

The worst thing is that crafting menus have no real way to organize what you can make. Things for every level and every room are on one single page that requires you to scroll through. You can have upwards of 50 items on there trying to find the one item you need. This is also an issue in the junk store. Where everything from every level is just thrown around with no way to organize by either level or bonus.

Still it isn't a bad game, and I see a lot of love put into it. So I'll still recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall I enjoyed playing this game. The pacing felt slow in the beginning (waiting for night with nothing to do), although one of the first updates addressed this by allowing the player to sleep earlier. I still don't feel like there's a lot to do before unlocking the second area. However after unlocking the third area I felt pretty overwhelmed because I couldn't keep up with all of the quests/upgrades. Then in the late game finding enough of certain materials became difficult (iron plates specifically).

With that being said I enjoyed playing despite these few issues because it was very relaxing and fun to keep making improvements and completing quests. The music was amazing and I wanted to know how some story lines continued to progress. I do think there could be a few quality changes (being able to craft while building a room, adding uses for old furniture, etc.) but I would definitely recommend this game to those who enjoy the genre.

Also the team was VERY helpful whenever I came across a bug or some oddity! The response time was great via email, and there were tons of people to help on the discord server! It is obvious the developers care about the quality of the game and player enjoyment!\t\t\t\t\t
