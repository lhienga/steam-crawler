\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent management game! I really enjoyed it and had enough fun to 100 BEARcent (percent, I'm sorry) it. The devs have been great at fixing the launch bugs I had initially (there's still a couple small ones but nothing game breaking and the next update should be out soon to fix those and add gamepad support which will make the game incredible on steam deck!).

I do think the game gets a bit grindy at the end, I had to leave it open for a while to let time pass for me to complete the last few quests, its not a major issue and I could have spent some of that time further optimising my BnBs but I had completed every other achievement and had almost unlimited money so didn't really feel the need to.

I also wish you had infinite (or at least more) inventory space. You can't sell items so its either throw them out or put them in storage and I didn't really want to just trash my items so I just wish I could hold more and not have to worry about it. The lack of space just feels like its there to be annoying rather then serving any real purpose. This only really becomes a problem near the end of the game and is honestly not too bad as you can easily throw out items you have duplicates / multiple stacks of but I sorta feel like the game would be more enjoyable if you had more.

Anyway back to the good: If you enjoy this time of game I'd highly recommend it! The characters are fun and the story is (mostly) lighthearted and overall the game isn't really stressful and I really like how there's no rush to complete things, you can take your time and slowly optimse to get better reviews and more money.

Pro tip: Add a ton of possum clocks to the walls of your rooms, they give a decent amount of decoration for the price and you can easily fit tons on the walls without taking up valuable floor space :) (I put about 20 in some rooms and its horrifying but worked really well lol)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was so excited for this game before it came out, and I'm so sad with what I've received. The game looks and sounds great; I love the designs and the aesthetic. The characters are charming and 'quirky', and although the dialogue is trying too hard at times I can forgive it. It has a lot of heart and good intentions.
However.
The pacing is atrocious. It feels so bad that it's hard for me to come up with a solid fix, or even multiple fixes. The days are way too long. The map is too big. I liked how accessible resources were at the beginning, but then it felt like there was nothing to do. You can't fast forward; you can 'sleep through the night', but that option only becomes available at midnight - halfway through the night. It doesn't take nearly enough time in the day to juggle all your resorts, and that's not even when using the bus stop. This is my biggest issue, because the game drags and leaves you bored, purposeless, and with no drive to keep playing. It may seem like my complaints don't go hand-in-hand with each other (the day is too long but also it takes too long to get anywhere?), which is why I said I don't have any solutions at the top.
Some other issues; the tutorials are long-winded and often poorly explained. It's not often clear what to do, because the game both holds your hand too much and drops you completely at times. I started a bunch of different quests in the hopes that they would fill time, but I finished them instantly and with hardly any work involved. I've noticed that other reviews mention cooking, which I haven't reached yet (and sadly won't, as I don't want to put more time in), and it feels weird that I can have multiple resorts open and yet not have unlocked all factors of gameplay. The inventory is too small. The process of building rooms is annoying, because you can't switch between crafting and building; if you start making a bedroom but haven't built the furniture yet, you have to completely destroy the bedroom in order to craft the furniture for it. I don't like the way wall hangings float on nothing; maybe walls can be visible when within the building, and not from the outside? The initial resorts feel too small for the guest expectations, and I don't want my rooms looking clunky because they're filled with decorations. The story is weak, and makes little sense. The beaver 'sonny' thing is weird. Full stop. Will is annoying. Full stop. Hank is GREAT.
Another HUGE thing: what you can and can't walk through is horribly unclear. Especially in the swamp map, and the forest around your house. Oh my god. It took me so long at the start of the game to just navigate through. Some bushes can be pushed past, some can't. This is the biggest, if the only, design flaw.
I'm really sad this game is as clunky as it is, but I'm more sad that I paid 25$ for it. It needs a lot more fine tuning to be enjoyable - right now it's just playable. I appreciate that I have very little time put in so far, but I can't do more than four hours of drudgery, and I think four hours is a generous amount of time to give a game to 'kick in'.\t\t\t\t\t
