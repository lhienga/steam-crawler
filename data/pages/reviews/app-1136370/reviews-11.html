\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 34 hours of game-play, I unlocked all the achievements on day 100. I am not typically an achievement hunter, but this game was so enjoyable and relaxing, that it didn't feel like a chore to complete. I kept progressing through the story, falling in love with the adorable and charismatic characters. The art style was cute and the game-play overall was really good. There were a couple minor bugs with NPC routing and a couple typos and grammatical errors, and the game froze and crashed on me once during a storm in game, but otherwise the game ran quite smoothly and I didn't feel like I was being rushed, and it didn't feel like it was dragging either.
My only real complaint is that there is no option to turn off weather effects, which as I mentioned earlier, did crash the game, and also made me a bit dizzy at times (I played the game while I had a particularly nasty cold). I didn't listen to too much of the music because I like to listen to podcasts while I play cozy games, but what I did listen to was enjoyable. The sound effects were nice as well.
Overall I would say that this is a great game and I highly recommend it to anyone who enjoys cozy games, management games, or cute forest animals with fun personalities.
9/10\t\t\t\t\t

It's not as chill as advertised for someone with executive function issues, but even my ADHD brain can manage it -- and as you progress you open lots of automation options, so it never gets off-puttingly overwhelming. My only gripe might be the cooking: the recipes and ingredients are fun, but there are so many of them it's hard to keep track of when cooking without simply carrying a ton of stuff in inventory.
