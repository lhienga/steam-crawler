\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort Review: The game is good but isn't a smooth experience. The game does a lot well, but does nothing outstanding. Game took about 22 hours to complete the main story. I wasn't playing fully completionist, but I also wasn't going directly for the ending either.

Longer Review:

The Good:
* Art is cute and appropriate to the mood. Does a good job of conveying what things are and do.
* Music is atmospheric but not amazing. Does it's job.
* Writing is goofy without devolving into 'lol randumb' territory. Effectively hints at more without being overly blunt about it.

The Bad:
* Gameplay is simple and doesn't require a ton of thinking or skill. The difficulty is less about the "management" side of the game, and more about the "planning" side (layouts, build order, effective time usage, etc). Once you get things running smoothly, things kinda run themselves.
* Quests don't always give great directions on what they want/need.
* Progression is inconsistent: Sometimes you'll be progressing smoothly, and then you'll hit a weird block that halts all progression until you complete it, and then it goes back to being smooth again. Sometimes the block is a quest, sometimes it's a lack of a specific resource.

The Ugly:
* The ending feels abrupt. I continued playing for another 10 minutes before I realized that was the end of the story.
* Stuff that was hinted at ended up being both less interesting and less explained than I would have prefered. Not sure if it was the result of cut content or leaving hooks for a potential future expansion/DLC.
* Too much of the game is spent running across the map gathering materials that are semi-randomly generated or standing around waiting for the next day to happen. There was a patch that helped with the sitting around issue a bit, but once you have thing somewhat automated you have fairly little to do.\t\t\t\t\t
