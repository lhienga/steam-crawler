\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a cautious recommendation because I think you enjoyment of this game really hinges on how much you are actually into management sims. The story of the game is a little muddy and isn't paced all that great, and as a result the game can really feel like it drags in the middle. I wound up just leaving the game to idle in the background while coming back each morning to get all of my rooms booked for the day, as there isn't really much to do other than scavenge while working on main quests. So if you're not really into making sure each guest visiting your resorts has a perfect stay, decorating each location perfectly, and fetching all your own ingredients for cooking, maybe look elsewhere. But if that sounds like a good time to you, then jump on in!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've just finished the game (at least I think?), now for the pros and cons:

Approving bear noises. The good stuff.
- Bear noises.
- I had no trouble getting behind the concept of how the rooms and numbers for those are working.
- You actually have to use most of the services you unlock, unless you want to go insane....
- The tutorial explanations are good to understand.
- You get a full refund for destroying your rooms and thus get not punished for messing up or trying a new layout.
- Time does not advance in build mode, thus you can freely experiment with stuff.

Confused Bear noises. Most of those might be due to bugs, maybe wierd game mechanics. Some of those are good, as in: would be stressfull if working correctly. Some of those might want to get a fix...
- Guests do only care about numbers, not the layout or how many different kinds of things are placed in the room. All I got was a bed and tons of footrests in order to increase the comfort levels. All you need to take care of is to grant access to the beds, usually by placing them next to the doors. Maybe fix this? That has a very negative side effect, see the section below.
- Some things take more space when rotated sideways. For example, I could get away with a Utility room in the size of 3 hight, 6 length and place 2 level 3 heaters and a large storage. I was unable to fit those in a room of 3 length and 6 hight. Once rotated the storage and an opposing heater needs more than 3 of space. Needs a fix, don't care if it's for the better of the worse...
- If a bathroom is blocked the guests show angry faces, however that doesn't seem to affect the reviews. Still get constant 5.0 ones, even if a buggy door prevents any access all the time.

Disappointed bear noises. Should be changed / fixed if possible.
- The start of the story (your dream) disappers completely until the very end.
- In the end Eris talks about 5 of them, you only meet 2. You might be able to fix both of those story points by introducing Eris much earlier and include the other 3 throug your progression.
- Cooking is a pain. Gathering all the ingredients is a pain because all they do most of the time is blocking your far too small inventory, fridges don't fix that issue. That mechanic works for the heaters because there is only 1 thing to keep track of and put in the slots. With all the different ingredients you need to take care of there is no reason to explore anything more complicated than mached potatoes, strawberry bowls and sliced apples until you unlock the auto cooker. For the requests only you gather the ingredients once and then forget about the stuff. Can we get an auto filler for the cooking station for ingredients at least?
- Can we get a seperate inventory for clothing and other "one of a kind" stuff? Else those things are blocking valuable inventory space if your unlock 2 or more of the same kind.
- Most of the stuff in the trash can is useless due to the fact, that you need your whole floor of the room for your crafted stuff that increses comfort. Unless you also include comfort items there the only places left for decoration are the wall stuff and carpets. Also, why should you buy anything that is more expensive for less decoration value, that doesn't increase your level of the room? The onyl thing that does inclrease the room level seems to be the bed, everything else doesn't seem to matter.
- There are some questions missing if you want to save your changes to stuff for setups like the auto configurations! I can't count how many changes I lost due to the fact I just clicked on X without clicking on save changes first...


All in all I'd give this game a 7.5/10. Aside from missing "want to save" questions that anoyingly make you loose progress and a nonexisting story progression of the starting dream it is quite enjoyable and can you keep being entertained. It also does a good job in holding the hand through the quests and tutorials.
I don't see the point of those "gods" or anything going on around them, that point kinda drowns itself within the rest of the game. All I see is a good place to build up your Hotels until they run themselfs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is great except for inventory management which killed it for me. By mid-game I was already playing "what is the least painful to ditch" when I can't see a design point to limited inventory space besides "make the player waste time running around," which is a poor reason. On top of that, its rather hard to move stuff around, if you have two of the same item taking up slots they don't auto stack and you have to stack them yourself, you can't mass buy objects, and the like.

Once inventory is less miserable I see myself coming back and changing this to a thumbsup, but until then I can't recommend it. Not for a game where most of your play time will involve interacting with your inventory in some manner.\t\t\t\t\t
