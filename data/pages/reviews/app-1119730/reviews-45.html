\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun, mindless game for the most part. You can build an ever expanding, customized farm with things to do to keep it running. The graphics are great and the weather effects add a nice, moody ambiance.

There are issues. The biggest ones are polish of basic game mechanics. Driving is very poor. The physics do not make sense and it's almost as if the vehicles are driving on ice with no traction. The trailers swing in a fashion that's quite ridiculous. If you run into deer, they are stronger than the most expensive pickup trucks. If you hit a deer, your truck will come to an abrupt halt, and the deer will continue eating off the ground and go about their business. There are also odd clipping bugs in the game where your vehicle will inexplicably get stuck on an invisible object. You cannot turn venison into jerky or sausage.

The relative cost of things is also a bit unusual which leads to an overall imbalanced game in terms of progression.

When building things like fences and buildings, there is no easy way to align structures without having to place an object, tear down the mock-up, and start over.

You cannot build sheds even though the game starts off with a shed.

The hunting isn't actually hunting. A single handgun shot to a doe, two for a buck. 4 for a bear. There's no actual stalking or need to be quiet or approach animals slowly. They just run around in erratic circles. If they run away, they invariably turn around right back toward you.

I think there are a lot of small improvements with basic game functionality that would make this such a better experience overall. That said, as it stands now, it's worth purchasing. It is fun, just with many annoying quirks.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I got this over a year and half after it entered EA. I'd been unsure if I wanted to dive in because it seemed incomplete. But then there was a sale and it seemed like there was enough to do to try it out.

It was/is fairly simplistic. You can raise chickens, pigs, cows, and goats. You have to build pens and can build some structures but the offering is simplistic. They have fully furnished farmhouses you can "build" (which consists of delivering wood planks you can cut and other materials you have to buy). There's a small selection of cars of which most are not really worth grabbing with low, low prices (I'd love to buy a pick up for $2k).

As EA progressed, they added some interesting things (greenhouses and small crops) and basic farming (definitely will not compete against Farming Simulator). Then they decided they needed to upgrade the engine from UE4 to UE5. They told us it would take a lot of time but it would be worth it. They also threw in the idea of adding...oil wells you could make money off of?!

Six months or so later, they released that UE5 release into an unstable branch. Some visuals were updated but it still looked...average. But now, we had oil rigs to make passive income from!

Meanwhile, the upgrade to UE5 introduced a new physics engine and a bunch of weird issues (cars feel like they're running on ice, many things will go flying, cars can drive up trees). Everything else was the same (including bugs from the previous version like the fact that eggs laid by chickens that were fertile would "forget" they were fertile when you reloaded your game.

But the weirdest thing was that they announced they were going to exit EA and go gold...with bugs and all. They promised more updates but instead of fixing glaring issues which make the game LESS playable than it was under UE4, they want to give us pets.

This game really should be still in EA or treated like a beta game release. It's just not worth $25 in its current state. There's stuff to do but it's grindy and it's more because you just want to set up your ranch than it is about completing an objective. Instead of the above (odd) decisions with recent and future updates, they could do a quick bug pass to fix the outstanding issues and satisfy all of us who helped fund them through EA while ensuring new players won't have as weird an experience. Instead, they're trying to get pets to work in-game.

I would steer clear until they sort out those issues or else catch game on sale for $10 or less.\t\t\t\t\t
