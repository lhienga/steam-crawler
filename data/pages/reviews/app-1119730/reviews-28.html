\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRanch Simulator - A Budget Buy for Casual Rural Sim

Rating: ⭐⭐✩✩✩ (2/5)

Ranch Simulator presents a mix of building, farming, and hunting activities, but its overall execution leaves much to be desired. For those intrigued by the concept but wary of its shortcomings, it's advisable to consider this title only during a significant sale.

The building mechanics offer a basic foundation for constructing your ranch, but the lack of depth and limited options may leave players wanting more from this aspect of the game. While it provides some initial enjoyment, it fails to evolve into a truly engaging experience.

Farming in Ranch Simulator is straightforward but lacks the complexity and variety found in more established simulation titles. The limited range of crops and basic agricultural features may appeal to casual players but might not satisfy those seeking a more immersive farming experience.

The inclusion of hunting adds variety, allowing players to explore the wilderness and track wildlife. However, the hunting mechanics lack the depth seen in dedicated hunting games, and the repetitive nature of these activities may not hold the interest of all players.

Graphically, Ranch Simulator falls short of delivering a visually striking experience. The rural landscapes lack the polish and detail seen in more refined simulation games, contributing to the overall feeling of mediocrity.

Considering the game's limitations, it would be prudent to wait for a sale if you're interested in trying Ranch Simulator. While it may provide some casual enjoyment, the lack of depth and innovation makes it difficult to justify a full-price purchase. With an emphasis on budget considerations, Ranch Simulator earns a modest 2 out of 5.\t\t\t\t\t

(For instance if you hunt all any animal on the map which takes a significant amount of time, consumes bullet and gas for the car), you get around 200 or 300$ a day.

DO NOT TRY TO PLANT TOMATOES.
You basically handpick tomatoes one by one and you can imagine how much you sell a tomato...

There is no goal except making money to buy things that help make more money (not all the time tho)
And in the end, the money is only used in order to buy horses but there is nothing you can do with them, except try to train them and make a profit. Spoiler alert, read a guide cos the system is broken.

To train a horse, you need to run in a circle and jump over obstacles.
If your horse is too slow, then it is not possible. High end horses are too fast so you can't turn enough to jump properly.


You can build a barn, did not find any use for it.
You can build a house, did not find any use for it.

You basically just build things to make your ranch look like a ranch but any building is entirely useless which is quite unfortunate.
Ho and be prepared to spend hours in front of the saw to make planks.

I wish the devs put a little more effort into the game and its progress curve as after a couple of hours, it simply feels like you already saw everything and there is no point in keep playing the game.\t\t\t\t\t
