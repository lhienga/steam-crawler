\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the time of this writing I only have 2.5 hours of time, so bear that in mind.
If I was told I can only play one more game, and when I was done I would die, I would choose Ranch Simulator. It may have only been hours, but it felt like forever.
I know everyone talks about the driving, and it DOES need a lot of work, but the building and demolition... That's where things fall apart.
First, to finish the tutorial, you are instructed to tear down the old house. To do this, you have to begin on the roof, and remove every block before moving down.
Every.
Single.
Block.
Windows have to be removed from their frames before the wall can be demoed. Doors too. The handrails have to be taken off the balcony separate from removing the floor. Did you miss a piece of debris on the floor? Too bad, because you can't remove a block of floor until the entire floor is 100% empty.
The building is almost as tedious, but at least it's broken up by long stretches of harvesting resources. You'll chop down trees for 3 logs, that you saw down to 2 boards a log. You'll need 6 boards per foundation.
And remember, you have no inventory. You can carry a single item at a time. Need 3 logs? You're making 3 trips. Yes, you have an ATV, and there are trailers to buy that would make it take fewer trips... Honestly though, other reviewers have covered exactly how bad the driving is. It doesn't make it any easier, it just takes fewer trips.
Most of the 2.5 hours of time I've spent playing this game was in the tutorial, and I can't say I'm looking forward to spending any more time Simulating a Ranch. The game has potential, but the mechanics all seem to need work.
There's also player hosted multiplayer, and much like everything else, it needs work.

I really wish steam had a "Meh" button. I can't recommend this game as it is, but maybe someday.\t\t\t\t\t
