\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAhh yes, a very delightful game I should say. In the beginning, I went on a little hunting adventure to help make myself money. I also found out that there are people who give out quests around the map and give you some dough upon completion. There is one man though, who I call Old Man Wrinkle Bum, that gives out the most exquisite quest of them all, Treasures of the Ancients. This quest gives you $2500 and it comes back every so often.
After collecting some money I decided it was time to buy some material for two oil rigs. After finding that liquid gold I built the rigs, gassed them up, and let them work for me. Passive income! I like it. There is quite a bit of money to be made with these things. With more bands stacking up in my bank account I went on to build a barn and buy 1 male and 6 female pigs. (Big mistake, definitely should've gotten a little less females. Did not know that the great pig death wave of '24 was gonna happen) These pigs were the best, they were loved. I fed and watered them every day until eventually I noticed something. A pig was pregnant! After a long few days the pig finally had some babies and I got to watch them grow.
When the pigs started having more babies I realized I needed to make a silo and a windmill to easily fill up the troughs with food and water. I just simply could not keep up with feeding them. While waiting for enough money to buy the materials I saw that I failed myself as a rancher. I neglected to feed the pigs that day. One of the pigs was dead, and then another, and another. Multiple pigs had died because of my incompetence. From that day forward I swore I would never let that happen again.
I skinned the dead pigs and filled about 6 coolers worth of meat. I went on down to the burger restaurant and sold the meat for 1.9x and got almost $9000. Now that is epic.
Anyways, I'm really rambling on about this game so I should stop. The bottom line is that this game is really fun to play. I would highly recommend playing with friends because it makes the game easier and way more fun. Yes there are some things and little fixes that I'd love to see but the game is still great considering the fact that it's so new. I can't wait to see what the future has for this game. Thanks for reading my long review.
Edit: OH MY GOSH. THE FIREWORKS IN THIS GAME ARE INSANE. After I got enough money to live comfortably I bought 2gs worth of fireworks and had an amazing show. The only thing I'd like to add is the ability to hear fireworks from further away.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't mind the grind, but the game feels still early access, not full version. I don't regret buying it as I hope it will improve in the future, but am glad I got it on sale. I neither recommend nor don't recommend it, it's a "got potential" purchase.

Some of the things that need improvement to make it playable without frustration:

- an option to turn off head-bobbing. For people with motion sickness, 1st person might be unplayable. Sometimes drastic camera movements occur while driving, so be aware if that is an issue for you
- make at least small objects movable. It doesn't make sense that I cannot move a wall light after having placed it once (you can only destruct and rebuild - without reclaming any material)
- UX needs some improvement. You pick up logs with the left mouse button, but if you want to insert them into the table saw, you need to press E. Accidentalliy clicking will pack up the saw immediately and drop the log. Better continuity of controls would help a lot
- some involuntarily comic situations - there's a store with an (un)helpful lady telling you to use the self-checkout. If the store's closed, she can be seen through the window, sitting and the counter, same expression, unmoved by her nightly freedom, just awaiting to continue her fate as a one-sentence NPC in the morning
- town feels reaaaally empty
- dialogues are lacking
- if you're playing for the first time, building blueprints is hit and miss. As you don't know (and can't see) which sides need to be accessible, you might end up placing constructions in a wrong orientation. You cannot fix it, you have to destroy and rebuild (with new material)
- radio doesn't seem to work
- it does not make sense that plants don't receive water from natural rain, not even from downpour during thunderstorms. It can rain all day, but if you get home you'll might still see your plants drying out
- lack of item descriptions - you won't know if you buy a package of seeds for 10 tomato plants, or for 100 unless you hold it in your hands (it's ten), same for building, you won't know if a planter box holds one plant or ten until you build it and try to fill it

That said, I did enjoy playing it, but the frustrating bits are...frustrating. I think I'll come back to the game after some more QOL updates.

Positives:
- character customization is great! It's always a big plus if you can play female characters and have the same range of options for all genders
- the landscape looks nice and believable for the setting (albeit a bit empty). "Waking up" to a crisp blue sky almost feels real and is relaxing
- the devs seem very involved and accomodating
- great potential
- harvesting crops and seeing growth is fun, as is getting livestock
- I like the customization options that are there even for chickens, and your car
- driving is hilarious
- being able to do everything yourself (sawing logs, collecting them, building, having to drive to the store to sell your wares)

If the devs keep on working on it, this game definitely will be great in the future. As of now, it doesn't feel like a 1.0 version, but like early access. I hope future updates will adress the remaining issues and fill the game with life.\t\t\t\t\t
