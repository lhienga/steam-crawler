\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a wonderful little game which I have played for 361 hours so far. But there are many things that can drive you insane. The vehicle handling is horrendous and needs sorting out (especially the farm equipment), I was warned about the farm equipment and advised to ignore the farming side. But I gave it a go and almost pulled all my hair out. Since that first foray into the farming I have left that side of the game well alone, as it is anything but enjoyable. For farming I will use other farming sim games. Building and running the Ranch side of the game is fantastic to be honest and very enjoyable. There are a couple of frustrating bits though, animals that don't wake up, a box truck that if you fill it with goods either freezes the game at selling points or just keeps flipping over ( I would recommend using the car transporter trailer, which is great for transporting almost everything except animals). The box truck is however good for transporting the livestock. I would recommend giving the game a try, just expect some frustration mixed in with your enjoyment! As the game was only fully released last November, hopefully the developers will work on this little problem areas. I also hope they introduce more livestock to help fill out your ranch. As you have a large area for the few types of livestock in the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this game, I bought it several years ago and never installed it due to being deep in Farming Simulator. I decided to try it being that it has been out for several years now thinking it should be plenty of time to develop and work out bugs. I honestly couldn't be more wrong.

I'll start with the negatives as that's what really people should be aware of.

No auto save
You better make sure to save regularly manually. How you don't implement this feature from the beginning is beyond me. Nothing worse than doing several hours of work to realize most of it is gone when the game crashes. Before the developer blames my computer I can assure that my system wasn't the cause for the crash. AMD 5800X, RTX 3070, 128gb of RAM, 2TB Gen 4 NVME dedicated to games only, no OS on that hard drive. Bought a vehicle and the game crashed.

Driving Physics are terrible
You're driving on ice the entire time, god forbid you have a trailer and have to make a turn. The trailer whips like crazy. Both keyboard and controller driving is not a fun experience. There's no finesse or throttle modulation what so ever. Hooking a trailer is a royal pain. The game could really benefit from having gears. Even if it's just drive and reverse that are controlled with the throttle button and braking is done on a separate key. When using farm equipment with a controller the throttle key is the same key to activate the equipment. It's a different key on mouse but make it a bindable key. Nothing about driving in this game is realistic.

No dedicated server support
Playing with friends is a pain because if you start on your save or someone else's you're SOL if you want to play for a bit alone. I saw on the Steam forums that someone (possibly a person on the development team) recommends sharing save files. Could it work? Sure, however that is the biggest headache to manage if several people are trying to play the same game. The idea behind it is just asinine.

Vehicle bugs
Vehicles parked dance and float all over the place. It is to be expected in the very beginning of the game but a game that has been out several years should not have this many bugs when you have to use vehicles as often.

No keybind changes for controller
I get alot of PC gamers use M/KB, how ever with how bad this game is to drive on keyboard a controller aids it to some degree. If the game does change keybinds for controller it was not allowing me to do it.

I'm sure it sounds knit picking and I can go further into negatives about this game but I don't think anything will get changed. These are the major things I noticed that make me not want to play the game.

Now for the positives

Character Creation
The character creation is pretty good. It's limited but overall good

Building
The building is probably the best thing about the game. It's interactive without being overly complicated for no reason. It's not the type that as long as the materials are in your inventory something just pops up. It takes the right amount of time to collect materials and plan out your build. The models seem well put together and make sense to the game. Nothing seems out of place with how the models look.

Harvesting Materials
I've seen several reviews of people complaining about the hunting and guns however it's a good aspect of the game. When you start your ranch it's a bit grindy to obtain starting materials but it's to be expected in this type of game. The process of cutting trees and creating planks is fun. I would like to see concrete and metal be more interactive than buying a prefab bag or sheet of metal. Mixing the cement with water would be a good way. Metal is what it is as I don't see it being a mining sim to dig and melt ore to obtain metal.

If you made it this far thank you for the time to read this long winded rant. Overall the game needs a lot of work and I can not recommend someone paying full price for the game. The amount of time that has passed that this game is out and the amount of basic features the game is lacking makes the game not worth $25. If in the future they implement stuff like auto save, and the ability to create a server without having to purchase a second copy or over pay for some garbage rented game server I could see the game progressing. As it sits today the game is fun for a couple of hours when you're bored. Just my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun and fairly addictive game... preferred it in some ways over the other Farming Simulator franchise, especially the animal husbandry and making money.
If you're into arable farming, it's kinda lame however. Growing wheat in order to feed animals was hardly economically more beneficial than just paying to fill the grain silos themselves, after you've forked out for fertilizer and spent time cultivating, sowing and harvesting the land. I literally grew wheat once and then abandoned that part of the game. Horses, although cool and fun to ride and bid for at auction, have limited interaction capability apart from riding. You can't breed them either. There are also a couple of weird bugs, like fertilized chicken eggs not hatching after a while. Vehicle handling is also a bit on the wild side.
Having said that, I did have fun getting rich selling moldy cow cheese and micromanaging my bred farm animals! And building in the game is good. You can choose prefab farm houses that you supply the material for and then, once complete, spawn furniture and have a nice lived-in feel. Or you can build your own structures, although the lack of ability to repaint or texture the brown wood is a bit disappointing. Definitely recommend though for what the game CAN do\t\t\t\t\t
