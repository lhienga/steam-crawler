\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOof. Where to start with this one...

There's almost no audio in the game which makes it feel hollow.

Driving is BEYOND horrible.

Getting Multiplayer to work was a nightmare (took the majority of my 'play time' to be able to connect to my wife's game) and the integration with steam is quite bad.

Chopping trees is dumb. You press E, the entire tree falls over, press E again and it instantly turns into perfect logs. Then you chop it into planks. Chopping a single log into 2 planks takes longer than felling the entire tree. This in particular was an extreme disappointment to me, as most similar games have far more in-depth logging/forestry options.

Building is extremely basic for a SIM game, it feels more like an arcadey survival game (Grounded, Valheim, Etc), but without the functioning systems that make those fun. In Ranch Simulator, it's slow, boring, clunky. --- I do want to acknowledge that the end result can look good though.

The movement animations are so bad. When you jump, you flail around weirdly and your legs kick straight back. It looks weird.

Opening the build book with the flash light on is like getting flashbanged IRL.

FOV Scaling makes some stuff impossible to read.

Shop menu/navigation is terrible. Things that should be in certain categories are absent. I.E. buying the chicken egg basket - this is only visible under 'all', and is not considered a 'tool' or 'farming item'.

The font for quests/completing shopping purchases does not fit the game at all...

For a simulation game, the systems are barebones (and even that doesn't go far enough to describe how empty everything feels IMO), not engaging, boring, and plain. There's a gross lack of actual simulation to begin with. Everything is just Press E Simulator. Also, it is the opposite of immersive, which is a problem for a sim.

***No horses.*** - EDIT - I am incorrect here as per developer response.

Gunplay is bad. Reloading is unresponsive. Wasn't really expecting something in-depth like theHunter, but I was expecting more.

Most of the stuff in this game is done better in the Farming Simulator series, theHunter, or other similar sims.

I could go on about I encountered in this game rubbed me the wrong way, but I think I've described enough.

Edits - Softened some of the harsher language that resulted from being extremely disappointed, and focused in on review points. I play several sims that do each element far better and was hoping for some sort of incorporation of them all with fun gameplay elements. I simply feel let down by nearly all of the mechanics in game. They exist, but they're not fulfilling (in my opinion).

I would have loved to get lost in the game, particularly since co-op exists and I love these style of games with my wife, but unfortunately this one (in its current state) is not for us. And unfortunately I do not have confidence that every system will be reworked, so I will not be purchasing the game again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are familiar with this genre, you probably will like this game. Since I am rather new I think this game is too difficult.

You play the tutorial, which is nice, but after you finish this you don't have a clue what to do next. Of course you can watch lots of videos, which give you an immense amount of tips and tricks, but to be honest: they scare the heck out of me. All those huge farms at a time where I am having problems coping with 2 chickens.

The other thing is that there is no way to pause the game. That means that if you encounter a problem or you just want read something to help building this ranch, you must end the game - otherwise the time runs on and on. This game is made for multiplayer, but since I am not schizophrenic (and I don't have friends, who play these kind of games) I just play alone, but then I must have a way to pause the game.

Okay, okay, I was curious enough to continue trying to build and I am still not very far, but it is a challenge (also for me as noob). It is at least good and challenging enough to keep trying to build. In this case I must be fair and change my review from negative to positive.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNope.
The world is devoided of life, and even the stores are empty like my brain.
Other than that, the game keeps crashing - I lost two new games in a row prior to writing this review.

The error is this:
LowLevelFatalError [File:D:\\build\\++UE5\\Sync\\Engine\\Source\\Runtime\\D3D12RHI\\Private\\D3D12Util.cpp] [Line: 873]
CurrentQueue.Fence.D3DFence->GetCompletedValue() failed
at D:\\build\\++UE5\\Sync\\Engine\\Source\\Runtime\\D3D12RHI\\Private\\D3D12Submission.cpp:939
with error DXGI_ERROR_DEVICE_REMOVED with Reason: DXGI_ERROR_DEVICE_HUNG

So, maybe I'm ranting a bit, here.
But this game is no more in Early Access, so ofc I'll rant AND apply for a refund.
If this is the quality being considered acceptable for the [unreliable] product that has received the GO light, I can only wonder how many mid things could have been awaiting for us diving further in the game.

For sure, I'm not wanting to discover them, wasting ~13€ in thin smoke.

One can argue that 13€ is very cheap, and it really is, so I should keep this product rather than return it.
13€ is very cheap, yes.
Sum it up with the monthly 11€ of Spotify. the 30€ of Netflix, the 60€ for my ADSL line, the house bills, the fuel + maintenance of the car needed to bring me to work, the average cost of the life in this fifth world country where I live in, and so on, and you'll likely see that even a 13€ expense in the household balance isn't SO cheap, after all.

Apart from the price tag, in my forcibly limited playtime I've encountered some inconsistencies:
  • you can ram wildlife fauna with your golf cart or whatever it is, with NO effects at all;
  • you can ram trees, RVs, rocks, cars and other physical obstacles with your vehicle without any damage;
  • you must shoot twice a single deer with your revolver, even with an headshot, to kill it;
  • when you managed to kill a wild animal, say the aforementioned deer, you can skin on site
  • you'll get some meat pieces, that you have to pick up and handle one by one placing it somewhere, but NO skin nor extra raw materials at all;
  • sometimes the said animals will flee directly in your direction - yass! come here, right to your daddy, cutie!
  • if you buy something from a store, like two 5kg concrete bags from Hardware Store, the items you just bought are placed ab mentula canis in an outside area of the said store, and neither piled up;
  • and can we talk about the complete emptiness of said stores? Apart from the color machine, the HW store is a bunch of random (but on topic, tho) assets aligned on the perimeter of the room; you can peek inside shops, thanks to the windows, only to see how much the interiors are devoided of anything (like with the fuel station) apart some other random and very sparse assets;
  • if you buy a chicken or a rooster, you have to place it on your vehicle flatbed, without a chicken cage nor any little decoration that would have enriched the overall experience, instead of having to treat animals just like they are inert flour sacks;
  • not only you can't till small patches of soil with an hoe, having to resort to greenhouse planters instead, but the fields you can plow with machinery are pre-made rectangular shaped area - more hand-holding that this, you can maybe found only railroad tracks, I guess;
  • don't make me criticize the chaotic driving mechanics & overall feelings that arise from that. Really, don't;
  • and I hope that forestry would be implemented, at some time, at least to give your axe some usefulness after you have cut down all of the trees that were available for you;
  • tree cutting animation: a single axe swing, and that's all. Tree trunk cleaning & parting animation: nothing, just press and hold E. Stump removal animation: again, hold E whilst you dream about an actual animation that could have been there. Also, removing stumps is pretty useless, apart from cleansing the land to build on it, because trees can't regrow and the stump won't give you any scraps or sorta either;
  • overall, the demolition of junks and things with your crowbar won't give you any scrap/material back;
  • oil rigs. I mean, are we building a ranch above the Gobi desert? I know that you need fuel even to run your table saw, because manual handsaws are something that had probably existed only in 3rd century B.C. in rural Turkistan, but having to drill for oil by sentiment, in a Montana-like landscape, seems a little too sci-fi for my tastes;
  • you have a nice ruined windmill in your ranch. Why renewable sources of power & fuel are so hard to accept, my dear muricans cowgirls and cowboys of the XXI century? Burning oil for everything doesn't seems very pretty, even if I can imagine that living without a 864982498 HP jeep or an energivore humvee isn't very enticing;
  • having Unreal Engine 5 makes us yell "YAAAAY! IT'S BLEEDING EDGE! TOP NOTCH TECH!" in ecstasy and with starry eyes. UE5 gives striking lightning storms, yes, I get it. And some other pretty special effects too, of course. But pairing those with the 2015-era overall quality (my blonde character seems a poor old white-haired grandpa!) seems to me that it is like having a Chevrolet Camaro but driving it like a Fiat Duna. Honestly, IMHO it is just some easy marketing effort for the gluttonous kiddos and tech-above-all enthusiast out there;
  • why the fans of my rig seems like C-130 propellers when I play this game? Even that unoptimized mess of Starfield (nor Farmer's Life, just sayin') didn't manage to make my PC ready for takeoff from the desk.

Perhaps I could go on, writing down other notes, but I'm too lazy for that.
So, I'll bug out.
Wish all the best luck to the devs - please, don't abandon this game now that has been rushed out of Early Access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNone of the different mechanics are very fun or good. It's mostly a lot of un-fun grind. The vehicles drive poorly. Once you scale up your farm and want to sell a bunch of stuff, you have to buy and stack multiple coolers in your vehicle--like 15 of them, because the biggest inventory storage (coolers) only hold like 8 things, which depending on what you're growing, is like 2-3 grow beds. You can work your way up from farming to chickens for egg, pigs for meat and cows for meat and milk. You can also get horses and better bigger vehicles. However, those things only make you money a faster and aren't really fun either.

There are quests you can do from random townsfolk with no personality--they aren't worth it. You can't buy new properties, or a store of your own. Roadmap and development looks sparse, slow and contains features that really should have been in the game at 1.0, while not really fixing anything to make it more fun.\t\t\t\t\t
