\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give this game a positive review - it has so much potential - but given the state of the game as it "exits" Early Access, I simply cannot. The game feels incredibly incomplete. The building and decorating tools are *extremely* limited and the landscaping tools (planting trees/shrubs, placing paths) are non-existent. The lack of angle tools for fence-building leads to major frustration. Manually stacking dozens of items (which needs to be done constantly) is a tedious chore because there is no snapping. There is no progression path, there are no money sinks, there is nothing to spend your money on once you have everything up and running, the economy is grossly out of balance. You cannot pause the game, even in single-player. The vehicle handling is awful and making even slight steering adjustments can send you spinning out of control. The game doesn't save the state of the lights, so every time you load the game up you have to go around and manually turn on each individual light. The farming is pointless because you can just refill your feed silos with a single click and the return on investment for the farming equipment is poor. The horses are pointless, because you can't breed them and training them does not yield enough money to be worth the time that it takes.

Honestly, I could go on for quite a while, but these issues already far exceed what should be able to be said about a game that has "exited" Early Access. The devs say that more changes are coming, but all I've really heard about is adding the ability to keep cats and dogs as pets, which I think is a significantly lower priority than these very basic game components that are sadly missing. I hope things get better in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRanch Simulator is a game that lets you build, farm, hunt and trade in a large open-world environment. You can play solo or with friends in co-op mode.

Pros:

- The game offers a lot of freedom and customization for your ranch. You can choose what animals to raise, what crops to grow, what vehicles to buy and what buildings to renovate or construct.
- The game has realistic graphics and physics, as well as dynamic weather and seasons. The animals have different behaviors and needs, and the environment is full of wildlife and resources.
- The game has a variety of activities and challenges to keep you engaged. You can hunt deer and bears, fish in the lake, trade with other ranchers, explore the map and discover secrets, and more.
- The game has a friendly and active community, as well as regular updates and patches from the developers. The game also supports mods and Steam Workshop, allowing you to customize your experience further.

Cons:

- The game is still in early access and has some bugs and glitches that need to be fixed. Some players have reported issues with the multiplayer mode, the animal AI, the vehicle controls, and the performance optimization.
- The game can be repetitive and grindy at times, especially in the early stages. You may have to do a lot of chores and tasks to earn money and resources, and some of them can be tedious and time-consuming.
- The game can be challenging and unforgiving, especially for beginners. You may have to deal with harsh weather, predators, diseases, accidents, and other threats that can harm you and your animals. You may also have to learn a lot of mechanics and systems by trial and error, as the game does not have a comprehensive tutorial or guide.

Overall, Ranch Simulator is a fun and immersive game that lets you live out your ranching dreams. It has a lot of potential and room for improvement, and it is suitable for fans of simulation and sandbox games. If you are looking for a relaxing and realistic game that lets you create your own ranch, you may want to give Ranch Simulator a try.\t\t\t\t\t
