\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter over 100 hours into the game, this is my takeaway:
• Freedom of Play Style: You can be a profitable ranch regardless of the type of ranching you want to do (pigs, dairy, garden/crops), although time until profitability will differ between them.
• Freedom of Building: You can design your ranch however you see fit using prefabs and blueprints. Has a decent selection to choose from.
• Interactions: The interactions between player/animal or player/environment like collecting your own eggs, capturing your own wild bees, and milking your own dairy animals are one of my favorite aspects in this game.
• Scenery: While it doesn’t live up to the RDR2 vibe, the rural mountain landscape is beautiful in its own way. I miss the deciduous trees though from the original UE4 map.

Cons:
• Limited Progression: Only 2 “tiers” of progression - beginner equipment vs updated equipment (no actual automation). While this will not really affect the imaginative builders, for those of us looking for a challenge or progression-led ranching game, this doesn’t quite scratch that itch at the moment. Hopefully future updates will remedy this.
• No Server Hosting: Max of 4 players in co-op, no permanent server ids or server renting/hosting for additional players.
• Wonky Animal Models: I was hoping they would be polished up before the actual release. Pretty sure they are free downloads. Chickens are decent though with the recent rework.
• Too Easy: Easy money (pigs or oil), few challenges, and very few ways to spend and/or lose money.
• Grind Heavy: Due to a lack of automation, most systems (even the updated ones) are very grind heavy, especially any meat or wood production.
• Quick Gameplay: If you’re only looking to run a profitable ranch, it takes about 2-3 hours at most with minimal infrastructure and input.
• Oil Rigs: The addition of oil drilling doesn’t make too much sense to me, as it is a cheaper and more efficient way to get money than anything else in game. No point in farming or raising animals if you go that route.

TLDR: Despite the con list, Ranch Simulator is worth it for those that enjoy interactive farming/ranching games. I wouldn’t say it’s worth $25 yet until there is more content, progression, and overall bug fixing BUT it’s definitely something I’d recommend trying if it goes on sale. :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Ranch Simulator is a game that has shown great potential and improvement since it came out of beta a few months ago. However, there are still a few main problems that need to be addressed.

Firstly, the game still has some bugs that can be quite frustrating at times. These range from minor glitches like items disappearing, to major issues such as the game crashing unexpectedly. While the developers have been actively releasing updates to fix these bugs, it's still a point of concern for players.

Another problem is the presence of flying vehicles , which can sometimes disrupt the immersion of the game. Seeing cars flying through the air certainly breaks the realism that the game aims to provide. It would be great if the developers could find a solution to this issue in future updates.

Lastly, there is the peculiar occurrence of animals made of bricks . While it's unclear why this happens, it can be quite comical to see bears , wolfs or dears made entirely out of bricks roaming around the ranch. This might seem like a minor issue, but it does detract from the overall experience of the game.

Despite these problems, there are some positive aspects that make the game enjoyable. The potential for fun gameplay with friends is a major highlight. The game allows you to team up with your friends and work together to manage a ranch, which can lead to hilarious and memorable moments.

Additionally, the sales system in the game is well-implemented. You can raise animals, grow crops, and gather resources to sell and earn money. This adds a layer of strategy and economic management that keeps the gameplay engaging.

In conclusion, despite the bugs, flying vehicles, and animals made of bricks, Ranch Simulator is a game that is recommended to all lovers of this type of simulator. With its potential for fun multiplayer gameplay and a well-implemented sales system, it promises an enjoyable ranch management experience. As the developers continue to update and improve the game, it has the potential to become an even better simulation game in the future. \t\t\t\t\t
