\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely hilarious to play with friends.
It runs like absolute horse poop (like the ones you have to clean). Good luck trying to get it above 60 FPS...

Game's generally a 5 or 6 out of 10. Handling feels like you're driving on ice. It also feels like there's a delay from the moment you press to drive forward to the moment you actually get going. Driving with trailers is a nightmare as they're really skittish.

Apparently, trees don't regrow and I didn't see any way you can replant them.

Quests seem very limited, and there's no real guidance on what you should do first - like I don't know, achievements that earn you cash as you progress through them such as "build your first coop, get your first egg, hatch your first egg, sell your first egg" so on so forth...
Once you're done with the tutorial, you're on your own - I mean I like that in a lot of games, but sometimes just a small idea of what you should do would do it good.


For now that's all I can think of, but I'll probably update this as I play more/it gets more updates (hopefully!).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a masterpiece of (probably intentional) poor programming. It has the same physics as GTA 3. The deer fling themselves into traffic with reckless abandon and hitting them is like running into a brick building. Animals can breed through the walls of the barn and fencing. When you load the bed of your vehicle, everything you put in is basically super-glued down and you can do a triple barrel roll down the side of the mountain with no hard feelings and everything still in place (including animals). Once, I bought a car trailer and the rear quarter of it clipped through the edge of the building. Nothing I did could get it out so I spent the rest of that game just going around it to get in and out of the store. I got mauled by two bears at the hardware store in the tutorial. You can't pause the game even if you're playing by yourself. Every time you open a gate, every animal in the pen turns to face you and starts zombie-shambling toward the opening. When you skin an animal (especially pigs) they EXPLODE like a meat landmine and chunks of pork get flung EVERYWHERE. It's HILARIOUS.

It's incredibly satisfying to build your ranch empire up from nothing, breeding your stock and processing animal products into cheese and sausages to sell. I've only played single player but I think having a friend and dividing farm chores would make for a fun evening or three. I'm reaching the point in my second save that I have a solid production line and predictable income, and at this point I am at a bit of a loss for what my "next objective" is- after the tutorial you're released to do your own thing and build up the ranch as you see fit. I've built what I want and my trajectory is clear- I suppose at this point it's a matter of amassing a fortune.

I love everything about this game. Its programming quirks have given me several good chuckles, but it's nothing game-breaking and I'm still having a lot of fun. I may have just about run my course on it unless I find some other thing to do or build that I missed (or if I can con a friend into playing with me), but I think 60 hours is a fair amount of gameplay for the price.\t\t\t\t\t

As an avid player of similar types of games I can say that I hope the developers of this one will continue working on it. The interactions with objects in the world were much less complex than I have seen in other games. That is a plus. But some improvements still need to be made.
Some of the major things that still need work include, vehicles behaving as if on ice. Kinda fun when you are just playing around, like drifting almost. Bottom line is that the game has potential but needs some real work to be recommended.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnly buy on sale & only if you're FULLY AWARE this is a far from complete game with many issues. Plenty of UE cut&paste elements do not make a game, there's a decent "idea" somewhere here but the execution is quite poor to be honest. Early grind is looonnnggg so be aware you'll miss the 2hr Steam refund window. I don't actually mind the grind like others who've posted reviews mention, there's some satisfaction about chopping down 12+ trees and manually sawing all the planks needed for example but co-op would help with this as well. There's only 4/5 NPC's & only a couple have a very limited number of quests for you to complete. There's no shopkeeper interaction as all transactions are via a self-service till (like you get in McDonalds). Physics of the game is all over the place. Running is like trudging through syrup, driving using mouse & keyboard is a task in abject patience as its like driving a rolling skate on ice! The building is solid but uninspired, if you've played other UE built games such as Stranded Deep etc you'll know how all this works. If you just want a really chill game to waste a couple of hours with I can recommend Lake by Gamious but that has no building at all so if you want a really basic game with very limited content to just build a farm with...I'm sure there are games out there with much more content. Depends what you want but buy with all eyes open as there's not much here to engage a player for very long unless you enjoy long, laborious tasks, maybe skip this one until its closer to being "finished" (though its been in development for a while now?) oh! Almost forgot the audio... its virtually non-existent & just has some very basic, cheap sound files.\t\t\t\t\t
