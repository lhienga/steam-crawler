\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot worth full price. The map is poorly designed and not realistic. Roads are a bit wonky, and traffic are just empty husks moving at 10mph. There is virtually no AI to speak of. It is a very simple game, but tedious. Moving animals to separate males from females is obnoxious, the animals immediately head for any open door or gate the instant it is opened. You cannot tell gender until the animal is old enough to breed, and they do so nearly instantly. There is a large lake but no use for it, the hardware store is tedious to get into and out of, there is a random franchised burger joint in a small town buying local meat, when it should be a mom-and-pop diner, weird town design and layout that is ultimately pointless and is just set dressing. At $25, it is overpriced. This is a $10 game. Buy it on sale if you know what you're buying. I don't regret the purchase, but I'm glad I bought it on sale.

This game would be improved with more realistic terrain, more realistic roads, rain actually watering crops, and more appropriate set dressing. It would be improved with simple UI fixes, like showing what gender the animal is since birth. Even better would be a more rigid animal moving system, improving vehicle handling, increasing the amount of planks you receive from a piece of wood, removing the grass from underneath placed structures (there is a lawn mower, so we already know grass can be trimmed, do it automatically so it doesn't grow through the floor), and improving the animal behavior so they're less robotic "must go through open door right this instant, stop eating, a door is open," and improving the gunplay and wild animals so the hunting aspect is a little more immersive, worthwhile, and engaging, rather than "drive around until you find an animal and poof it into meat." Also, players should start with a low grade horse. By the time you have enough money for a horse, they're useless. That animal can help work.\t\t\t\t\t

Ranch Simulator is straight forward enough, but honestly isn't that engaging and, to be blunt, is kind of an unpolished turd.
