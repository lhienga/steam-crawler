\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, an exceptionally addicting game.

The movement and combat is exceptionally clean & tidy, and extremely satisfying. The game design & lore collectables add a real nice unexpected bonus, and the skill tree feels exceptional - putting points, even early, in the trees gives you real tangible benefits.

The seperate classes are very interesting and enjoyable. I've played with at least one of each style, and they all change things up enough to really transform how you play the game & consider your relic/elixir choices.

The rarity tier system feels like a real treat across all aspects and the unique modifiers the Legendary ones provide turn some relics from an instant-skip to a won-run - one of my favourite items I located was an Impossible Ordinary sword, which had a 1/10000 chance of instantly killing everything on screen with every attack under the debuff known as 'Programmer's Rage'. Was fun to see.

However, it's not all good. Here's a short list of issues with the game currently.

* Spelling errors & typos in transaltion - many elements of the game aren't even translated at all, and while that could be purposed as flavour in some games, it's missing in several important aspects here where the game is trying to convey vital information to you without you being able to understand
* Lack of a simplistic stat sheet. Although there is a help menu that explains what each of the conditions do, there's no way to see your current movespeed/crit%/Flying Sword DMG etc etc and I feel the game would really benefit from this clarity
* In another vein on transparency & translation, many skills will tell you there's a 'chance' of something happening without in fact telling you what that chance is.
* The harder difficulties are an excellent way to step things up - HOWEVER, unless you're running two very particular builds, certain zones are full of bullet sponges - it took me 20 minutes to kill the Concubine on higher difficulties with Infinity & at several points I considered just letting her kill me after watching her jump up the the ceiling for the 100th time. Admittedly, this is likely a skill issue - if you know you're on a hharder difficulty, force a specific build.
*The runs are LONG. Even with a god-like run you can be playing for hours before you get to the final boss. Now admittedly, I play games like Noita so I'm no stranger to throwing hours into a run for purely prep work, but in a fast-paced hack & slash this can be really noticeable after Chapter 3. If you're looking for a rewarding quick dip-in-dip-out game, this isn't it.

With all that said, that may cast a rather negative light on this game. But no, even despite all it's flaws, it's a solid 8 or 9 out of 10. Rewarding, addictive, satisfying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
Mechanics on controller are tight, never felt like the character did something I didn't want.
Builds on the different character classes feel interesting and engaging.
Hit and hurt boxes are tight. Can stand just outside swipe animations and not get clipped.
Artistic ascetic and lore history felt consistent based on what I know from Asian culture.
Flying Swords are cool.

Cons:
Story in total feels very lacking, needs more fluff in between the bosses.
Needs more character building, who are the people that we saved.
More English would be cool, there is a lot translated already, but I have no idea what the second boss says. There a few potential mistranslated descriptions.

Game is too easy - I know there are harder difficulties, but i don't know how to access them.
Played 4 runs and have beaten the final boss twice.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a fan of snyergy-heavy roguelikes in the vein of Hades or Dead Cells, you'll enjoy this game. Straight up.

Is it perfect? Absolutely not. It's extremely messy currently. The translation is spotty at best (literally in many cases, as some of the subtitles are in English, and others are in Chinese, and not every voiceover has even an attempt at subtitling), the tooltips are sometimes far, far too obtuse (things like mechanics not being explained, probabilities for something to occur left completely open-ended, etc.), there are definitely still bugs, and there is a suspiciously large number of visuals/themes that are lifted from pre-existing games.

But it's honestly just fun to play. The synergies and scaling build really fast and hard, which makes progression very exciting, and there are a TON of different ways you can build that can all be successful. There is a simplicity to the actual play that some may not appreciate, but it works perfectly for me. As it stands, you have either 3 or 4 abilities to utilize at any given time. I prefer it that way, personally, but I can see where others would prefer more. I actually tend to opt specifically for the builds that eliminate the 4th button because I would rather focus on other things.

The enemies and abilities are complex enough to be challenging in a lot of circumstances (nearing bullet hell levels in a lot of cases), but not so punishing that you have to slam your head against the same boss for hours at a time. I finished my first run about 5 hours into playing, and I'm not the best roguelike player around by any means. Don't let that make it sound too fast or simple, though; I had only unlocked 2 of the 6 base builds at that point, and full clearing only unlocked 2 more of them. There is a ton of replay potential. Maybe not as much as Hades or Dead Cell, but still plenty to keep you entertained for only $18.

For story, it's a complete fail. The obtuse translations that do exist are nowhere near enough to give you even a clue what's going on, the voiceovers aren't translated, and it has the sort of barely coherent, hazy storytelling that is typical of Chinese high fantasy (think Wuxia movies) and that can make it difficult to understand even when the translation IS perfect, which this is clearly not. Buuut you don't always need that much story in a mechanics-heavy roguelike anyway, so it doesn't really bother me.

Die-hard roguelike players: 7/10, definitely worth buying, and has 8 or 9/10 potential with fixes and refinement
General gamers: 6/10, maybe wait for the rest of the bugsquashes and translations to be finished, but you also won't be completely disappointed if you try it now.
Casual gamers: 3/10, nah. The issues with the game can make it very, very confusing if you're not the type to dig into these kinds of games on the regular, and there is 0 comprehensible story to fall back on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that ive cleared the game on every difficulty and unlocked everything i feel like its time to review this game. Il start by simply saying that for the price, this game is well worth it - but i will probably never return to this game again, and the reason for this is that the devs have no idea how to handle difficulty.

I wont get into the sounds and looks of the game since frankly, you can judge them for yourself in the trailer above here. I personally found the look really neat, but the music boring and standard - you make up your own mind.

Let's start with the good stuff;

The combat during the first 3 difficulties feel smooth and fun, something akin to a faster-paced Hades where the attackspeed is counted in nano-seconds instead of attacks per second.

The ability to have 6 different "classes" or builds or whatever you wanna call them with 2 variations of each at the beginning feels great aswell (the first 3 difficulties)

The different items or "relics" or "elixirs" (depending on where in the game you read about them. Their translation isnt up to snuff yet.) have a really cool feature of doing different things depending on where you equip them - giving the same item different uses with different builds. They also vary between synergising with a specific build or with itself in some cases - so having 4 of the same item isnt always a bad thing.

The weapons, or "excaliburs" as the game calls them, are also pretty nice with cool random buffs and each weapon having a synergy with either one passive skill or item in the game, making it feel really neat when you just happen to find a weapon that synergises with your items or choice of passives.

The meh stuff;

The bosses.
The ones that dont just run up to you and hit you or put down the big "Dont stand in the fire" attack instead have these invulnerable states that pose no threat and merely adds to sinking you down. The last boss on the final difficulty is a nightmare due to him going invulnerable every 10 seconds and doing this "dangerous" super attack that you can literally always walk away from with no threat to you whatsoever, all it does is stop the game for 20 seconds and then allow you to keep hitting him - more on this particular bastard below.

The enemy variations.
The enemies are fairly few and where there is some difference you quickly realise they are just skins. and later in the difficulties all enemies are showing up in all different locations causing the variations to become even more repetetive.

The really really really BAD;

The ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ difficulty.
Jesus H Christ the difficulty.
This game is NOT a hard game, its just a VERY taxing game. il try to explain - The difficulties dont really add more difficulty, all they do is beef up the enemies hp and armor.
The bosses gain no new mechanics, just ALOT more hp and armor, making you able to easily evade everything they throw at you but instead deal 1% damage every few seconds or so ending up taking time and not skill to beat.

The regular mobs gets the same treatment and in the last difficulty just clearing an area can be you permastunning every single mob on the map and doing thousands of damage per second to them with no threat to your life whatsoever - you will just feel the slog as you slooooooowly drain away their hp.

Then you will have the CC immune mobs that wont stay still when you hit them and jump around making it take even longer.

They also add some demon-fish tentacle that spawns every 30 seconds or so, on the 4th difficulty 1 pops up and the last difficulty 3 of them pops up at the same time - this once again IS NO THREAT. it merely forces you to run around for abit wasting even more time to an already EXTREMELY slow gameplay pace that wont threaten your run in the least.

And the final nail in the coffin and main-reason to why im so burned out on this game. The final, ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, boss.

The only reason i stuck around long enough to kill him is that Roguelikes and roguelites is my cocain. I need to clear them to move on - and boy, i HATED the last couple of hours in this game due to the fact that i knew this guy was coming up.

The first 75% of his bar isnt a problem, he stands there and you swing at him, unmolested for about 10 minutes.
then he reaches his 25% mark and decides that enough is enough.
He then starts calling down aoe attacks on the ground, basically all the time - these in turn occasionally turn into poison pools that apply a debuff to you that deal more damage the longer you fight him.
Then every 10-15 seconds he turns invulnerable, teleports to the middle of the arena and teleports you in there with him, he then begins charging some doom-attack which you can easily walk out of and then he continues to rain down poison pools and rince repeat forever.

Doesnt sound so bad, the thing is - these pools NEVER go away. so pretty soon the entire arena will be filled with this extremely damaging aoe fields. and youre still doing 1% every few seconds with him going invulnerable for his attack every 10-15 seconds. Btw, this whole thing clocks in at about 10 seconds - so thats you not being able to hit him in a field of poison every 15 seconds for 10 seconds.

This boss is only a DPS check and this wouldnt have been a huge problem if it wasnt for the fact that the devs went haywire when it came to adding armor/hp buffs on the enemies. were not talking x10 or something, more like x50 (im not even kidding).

Killing this guy was not fun. I didnt feel "yeah, take that you ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, now to try another build" - i felt "finally, now i can play something fun."

Ending this slog of a difficulty with a boss that essentially is only a dps test is the worst idea imo.

This coupled with certain items just not working against bosses makes you really have to plan your builds instead of making do with what you get - and that goes against what i like with Roguelites. All together with the difficulty only being artificial to certain builds just not working at all, to boring harmless meatwalls called bosses standing in your way to the only real threat which is the final boss (or possibly the tiger-lord in the room before him) just makes me soured on the whole game.

Im sure there are some people with more addiction than me that loves this aspect of hitting the same target for an extended period of time with no threat to themselves, but personally i prefer the difficulty of games like Hades that add things other than just massive HP to the enemies.

In Hades ive unlocked and done everything and I still come back to it just cos the gameplay loop is fun, its challenging and im still enjoying it.
This game? Ive gotten every unlock and cleared all the difficulties - I cba to get the secret ending, I just dont want to play it anymore.

I still recommend it for the price though, seriously good price.
