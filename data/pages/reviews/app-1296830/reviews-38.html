\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBasically a budget, Eastern themed version of Hades. Has a lot of potential but suffers from several translation issues (a lot of stuff isn't in English yet); however, the game is plenty playable despite that.

It also has some bugs, though most/all the major ones have been fixed.

The biggest issue right now is that the story is basically non-existent and the two highest difficulties are based on severe number bloating, especially the highest one. This also leads to the second biggest issue: class balancing.

While all builds are viable on lower difficulties, the higher you go the less options you have in terms of efficient or even functional builds. Several builds would just not work on the highest difficulty setting or would, at best, be terrible slogs.

Overall, I would say hold off until they add some more content and finishtl translating, but as far as Hades clones go, you could do a lot worse.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT Dec 5 2023: The initial review was written on Feb 14 2022. Who knows, the game may have become fun by now, but I have no desire whatsoever to try it out again. If I find time and willingness to do so, y'all will see it in another edit. Even if that happens, the initial review is here to stay so that newcomers would see the long path of improvement. Now stop showering me with Jesters. You like the game – good for you, back then it was a boring crap and I'm willing to die on that hill.

An okayish roguelite but a bit too monotonous to have much replayability.
Once you complete it with each class and subclass - there's nothing you haven't seen and nothing you haven't done in the game.
And even that feels quite repetitive. Different classes are not so different per se, you have cold/lightning/poison-bleeding damage which are basically different animations that feel exactly the same in the end. You run to a group of enemies or to a boss, hold left mouse button, everything dies, or you shot them from a distance and recall you swords back to you, just dealing different types of damage. This way it feels even less different since in melee you at least have different animations.

Difficulty scaling is absurd and boring. Enemies just get ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ of HP (which feels good at the beginning cuz they just die to fast when you get your build going so you don't feel the difference after getting some near-OP skill). But don't worry, you don't feel that difference at higher difficulties either except that if your build is bad, it'll take a good minute to kill a bunch of normal opponents and much longer to kill a boss, if the build's good, you still one-shot most normal foes, maybe two-three shot more resilient ones and spend about a minute to kill a boss (well, minus the cutscenes and phases of invulnerability). The game doesn't become difficult, it just becomes utterly boring as you do all the same stuff but it takes much longer to kill anything. I've played until Heavy snow, as I understand, there's two more "difficulty levels" but.. What's in it? Just more health for enemies, you hit them, you evade, you kill - nothing new, done it for almost 40 hours now. Still taken down all bosses in practically the same time at "heavy" and at basic difficulty, you push down their health bar in seconds, watch the animation, evade some projectiles/red circles/huge red rectangles, push it down further, evade more stuff and voila - you go to the next chapter.

Also, it is beyond me why they couln't make boss entrances/changes in phases skippable or faster, same goes for the ending cutscene and the final titles (sure, they are skippable, but you have to hold escape button twice and still spend time for something absolutely useless and unentertaining, gosh I can't even read these hyeroglyphs anyway).

Translation is just awful. I don't know Chinese but I'm well aware that there's a lot of people who know both English and Chinese, and I'm sure they could help. Even some fans could probably help for free. Still we see what I'd assume machine translation with a bit of editing (hopeffully) looks like. Dream gate developers, for instance, asked their fans to help them translate it. I didn't care to check the translation in-game but all the phrases on the website they used were translated right when I went there to help. Some developers care about their product and find ways.

For the record, I have 393 hours in Hades, half of that before the release, then I started anew to see new dialogues and stuff that was added after. And I still occasionally play it.
This game, on the other hand, has little to no replayability. What a shame for a roguelite.\t\t\t\t\t
