\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike other have said and likely done so better than I will, but if you liked Hades, roguelikes and fast paced action, you may like this.

I stumbled over this game on the shop and when I saw it had a demo, I downloaded it and played - and I think about 20 minutes later, I bought the full version, because I couldn't stop. I am usually not very competitive or much of completionist either, but this game really has me strive to complete everything and every difficulty.

The available sects are varied and offer plenty of alternative play styles and you'll come to love some more than others. The RNG and variety of the upgrades/skills you find during your runs allows for some meh runs but also to some ridiculously powerful ones if you're lucky.

The translation is overall fine, though could use a little polishing and it would be nice to have a menu where we could review all the different skills and effects, instead of the incomplete one we have right now.

I think my only gripe right now, is that the Poison sect actually causes self-damage enough to actually get me killed in missions (and there are still 2 or 3 I have to unlock) - but maybe the poison one is just me not planning accordingly.

Anyway, what I criticize are minor things that don't really distract from the overall enjoyment. The game is very much worth its money and I haven't even touched the DLCs much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have finally regained all achievements in this game after the last DLC, unlocked every skin, every upgrade, and gathered a frankly disgusting quantity of time shards or whatever they're called, so I feel ready to say this is possibly my favorite roguelite of all time. It's innovative use of inventory management perfectly balances itself between items, weapons, and passives buffs in a way that feels satisfying both aiming for certain builds or experimenting to see what works. Difficulty modifiers that utilize risk/reward in every step to bring harsh challenges without merely weakening the player to uselessness. The playable classes, or sects as they are called, are all wildly different with their own focuses through unique buffs and what synergies they optimize or even have access to. Seriously, I could write a thousand words on just what each sect excels at and combos with if I thought anyone would read it. The story isn't top dollar, but for a roguelite it's existence at all is something to celebrate. Honestly my only real complaint is that I think there's too many items that focus on defense, in a game where raw strength and massive number clouds is more rewarding than anything else. However, this is thankfully mostly negated with an item-ban system! After getting deep into the game (which is perfectly held off to force new players to experiment and try out all the items before access is readily granted) you can remove them from the pool.
All in all, incredibly strong recommend to anyone who wants fast paced, cathartic action all wrapped up in an acceptable average runtime of about 20 to 30 minutes. But let's be honest, you can never just do one run.\t\t\t\t\t
