\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredible, the power fantasy is extremely strong, and it isn't afraid to let you run away with a specific build. It definitely feels like the game is trying its best to make me as strong as I possibly can. The balance seems very good, and it's extremely nice that the base difficulty isn't balanced around any meta progress updates. After beating the Chapter 2 boss who seems like a skill and DPS check wrapped into one, on my third run I was able to face tank my way to the end of the game.

Now for the bad: the localization is awful, some dialogs have absolutely no translation at all, and some pretty major bugs exist with only potential workarounds.

That being said, I 100% recommend giving this game a try, especially if you're a fan of "Hack and Slash" style gameplay.

EDIT: Officially beat all the content the game has to offer, and I have a few edits to the review but for the most part everything holds true, although most of the major bugs have been fixed. Difficulty scaling seems largely just be "more bullet sponging" and while it makes you feel phenomenal when you finally crack the curve, the final difficulty mode Blizzard feels kinda rough. Although, I really like the increased minimum rarity that comes with the difficulties, as it really lets you push builds to their limits.

The unlockables definitely need some more thought, as I finished the final difficulty before I even managed to unlock the last playable character or unlocked all the hidden stories (Which are an awesome part of the game). Also, the meta currency could use some in-game uses, as once you've maxed out the skill trees, it's essentially useless. The NPCs are great and I love the new Blacksmith NPC conceptually, although I'm not a huge fan of the implementation of the sword upgrade feature as the chance to upgrade to impossible feels too low on high difficulties.

Localization is still bad, but its definitely getting better slowly. I'd really like to see the distillation dialogs fixed, and the text runoff on the item dialogs is really difficulty to play around, and it feels like a lot of the difficulty in item selection is based on trying to parse out how certain interactions might work together on the fly from poorly worded text.

Also, when you sects that render other sects completely useless, it would be nice if those were removed from the pool of items. It can be a bit disappointing when you've taken a sect that removes your dash, only to be presented with a longer dash or longer invincibility after dashing as your sect choices.

Generally speaking though, the game is incredible with tons of replayability and I highly recommend it to anyone looking for a great Hack n' Slash roguelite! You can easily get a few hours out of the game, and the devs are actively supporting the game which makes it much easier to recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
