A Hidden Roguelike Gem

Man, I am having A LOT of fun with this game.

I'm the type of player who has 200+ hours on Hades, Dead Cells, Nuclear Throne, Hollow Knight and ETG and Skul. What can I say? I love Rogue Likes/lites.

This is an _Excellent_ roguelike/lite albeit some minor translation issues which do not impact the game play at all ( Someone has also translated all the dialog online should you wish to read it)

The game play is extremely smooth, very tight controls with a great input buffer and amazing visuals.

Overall a solid 9/10 for me. Definitely recommend if you're into these types of games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely addictive. The vast majority of the game is best played by limit testing - rushing in as fast and stupidly as possible and then learning your limits from there. Gameplay is smooth and fast paced, and dodges are best used proactively rather than reactively (fast, low/no start up lag, low I-frames). By far the best aspect of the game is the build crafting. I've played over 20 runs and am still experimenting with new builds I've never tried before regularly. Most classes and builds are viable, with only a few feeling a bit lacking.

A few cons to note are translation, which while much better still has a few untranslated lines or confusing text, and difficulty scaling. Relies a bit too much on making numbers larger instead of introducing new mechanics. Numbers are undoubtedly important, but for instance, on the second hardest difficulty at the first boss the game was still slowing down time to allow you to read the enemy and react/dodge appropriately. Removing said time dilation at higher difficulties would be a good way to force you to improve your skills to beat the boss, rather than the game hoping you mess up because the fight takes longer. Overall, still an absolutely amazing game that is well worth the money. The devs also seem to be updating the game for a long time to come as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFast paced action rogue-lite where a badass swordmaster who can telekinetically toss around storms of extra swords faces a Resident Evil style zombie apocalypse in ancient... China, I think?

The game is such a rush to play and the gameplay is relatively simple, but satisfying with a great feel to it. Randomized powers and items are incredibly powerful and full of awesome synergies to explore and abuse. Stack a ton of damage over time effects with a ton of "on dealing damage" effects and ramp up into insane damage. Toss swords to make your melee attacks more powerful, and then melee to let you toss swords more, and throw in some stuff to do additional special attacks for free while you're doing it all. It all feels awesome.

On the downside, the localization into English is rough. Terminology is generally consistent, but can be difficult to figure out at times (primary weapons are all referred to as Excalibur which confused me for a bit, for example). Turtle boss and shopkeeper have voice lines but no subtitles, so I entirely miss the meaning of their dialogue.

I also feel obligated to mention, the game is full of excessive gore and some nudity, which might bother some people. I felt it was appropriate to the dark setting and helped further make me feel like a total badass, but your mileage may vary.

Overall, I love the game.\t\t\t\t\t
