\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is the perfect formula for roguelite as I like it. You start humbly, with nothing, and try to get as far as you can, then each run you get resources that will make your next run easier.

For each run, you choose the powers of a deity of your choice from among 6-7, then equip a weapon and 4 artifacts and obtain new skills along the way, all with different tiers of rarity.
If you manage to finish a run, you can increase the difficulty several times, which also increases the drops.
For even more challenge, specific game rules can be activated in exchange for a powerful reward at each checkpoint.

For me, a good roguelite is one that offers plenty of permanent upgrades, and this game doesn't lack any. The skill tree allows you to gain raw stats, or for exemple, to select a legendary artifact at the start of each run, making it much easier to create your build, or to experiment with others without having to wait for randomness to be on your side.
A special store allows you to unlock more bonuses at checkpoints, which are an opportunity to modify your weapon, or gain stats/artifacts or resources.
There are also small gains in stats by completing lists of items to find (lore).

What's more, the free DLC offers just as much content as the base game (it could be its own standalone game), adding special rules and powerful buffs in a new zone separate from that of the base game (in short, at the start of your run you choose either the base game or the DLC).
And even if the two zones aren't connected, the DLC's new special skill tree also allows you to obtain certain upgrades for the base game, a very useful boost for more casual players, or to unlock difficult achievements.
The DLC also adds an infinite mode that increases difficulty as you go along, providing players with both powerful buffs and debuffs, making it a great mode for testing out the strongest builds, as the duration is longer than the basic runs.

In conclusion, I really like these games that offer content accessible to players of all levels.
If you're pretty hardcore, the game offers many options to increase the challenge, and if you're more casual, it offers many different upgrades that can compensate for your lack of personal experience. Great game.\t\t\t\t\t
