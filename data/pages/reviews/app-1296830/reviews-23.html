\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLate 2023 Edit: With the release of the new DLC, I've gone back and played through it. They've cleaned up a lot of the translation/localization, so it's easier to make sense of things. This did involve some changes to names, but no mechanics actually changed regarding that. This is now much more accessible to an English speaking audience. Also, some balance changes happened, and there's new Statues/Paths to play with, along with relics and weapons. Still a recommend from me. Enjoy!

This game is a lot more engaging than I expected, though before you have any unlocks in the beginning it can be a bit tough--particularly the DLC, since it uses the same permanent upgrades as the main game on top of its own. An inverse bonus is that the DLC can potentially unlock the Blood Soul(?) currency for upgrades at a faster/quicker pace.

I find the art style to be pretty good. Specifically, they really fill out the detail on a lot of stuff in environments, characters, enemies, or the visuals in general.

The only *real* drawback to this game is that the translation/localization is really bad a lot of the time. Sometimes the UI is uncooperative, particularly when doing the DLC and it not allowing you to hover over and check your current textbook buffs. However, some of the skills, debuffs/buffs, and abilities aren't conveyed very well until you actually start playing them. The Supreme Bodhi is a good example, as the translated explanation for No Trace was very confusing until I did a run and experimented with it. Others are how mechanics work, and in some cases there's a lack of explanation. I didn't know you could start with a relic until after I beat the third difficulty and had unlocked quite a bit of stuff already, and further after that I didn't know about recycling extra powers for an attack or HP bonus. I don't really want to fault the game for this, since it's not a native English game. It just means you're going to have to work some things out on your own with experimenting and playing... or read some information online. A lot of the 'older' base content explains things well enough, even the lore, though you'll find a lot of typos and grammatical errors.

There's a big emphasis on choice in builds. The general idea is that your endgame will have you pretty OP once you have a lot of things unlocked, and the higher difficulties are definitely challenging. The DLC is a bit crazier, since some of the bosses are straight up much harder than the main game, and pushing that to finish does require a more cohesive build. I'd say the top difficulties in the main game end up harder, though, since you can't accelerate builds as quickly.

Adding to the above, there's different playstyles between the statues to worship. Most of them have a Melee/Ranged (Flying Sword) option that will focus on a different aspect of your damage. Some will allow you to forgo Melee or Ranged altogether, and Supreme Bodhi is melee focused, but it's a high damage/precision melee that is intended to be less intense and spammy as the others can get. One is built around Poison/Toxin and DoTs. A nice touch is that you can hold down the attack to spam your melee attack. That might seem lazy, but you can get to some absolutely insane attack speed levels that will be impossible to manage with spam clicking. With the Lightning statue I can straight up dip my framerate in the DLC's later stages with a bunch of trigger and lightning effects during Sheathe on a room full of enemies.

As far as performance and bugs go: Overall, I didn't really experience many bugs, and no framerate issues except the above hyper-niche scenario. The only one is the aforementioned DLC issue where I can't check my text upgrades for some reason. Charge enemies, and the "Fish Tail" (high difficulties) that has following ground attacks chain-stunning you off the ground is pretty obnoxious. Sometimes it's hard to spot the suicide stun bots, too, though I can't blame those for ever actually getting me killed. You can play around all of this with a little practice.

Overall, I quite like this game, and recommend if you're into the genre. Like I said: Translation/localization in English is really bad, but other than that I found it to be a good experience overall. The visuals are nice, and generally clean to discern until you get a giga late game build. The bosses are actually wicked, and I was surprised how challenging some could be, and how cool some are. I do dislike Fate Tower, since the Library stages are lacking in detail and variety, often getting the same room format and enemy spawns twice in a row in some runs. Fate Tower's final boss can also drag a bit due to her phase two being time based, rather than exclusively health based. (It speeds up considerably if you run the challenge that causes you to bleed out in combat over time.)
