At first, I felt like the combat might be too "one note" for what I'd expect from this type of game, but once you get a hang of how things work, it's pretty fun to zoom around and splatter enemies. I'm still not sure if I enjoy only being able to slot one "relic" to each of the four slots; especially with just how fast they throw relics at you... it seems like you're getting new relics constantly, and you're always having to switch it out with the one you picked up two minutes ago. However, I am having fun. The animation is really cool, and it seems that there's a-lot of vertical progression (for a rouge-lite). Overall, this game feels fun to play... it may need some tweaking as far as putting builds together, but I could be wrong too. I've only played a couple of short runs.
So far, I'd recommend to those of you who really enjoy rouge-lite's such as "dead cells" or "hades".
*There's a demo, and I'd certainly recommend giving the demo a shot before purchasing, just to see if this feels good to you.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright finally beat the game once, took a while. The game is absolutely amazing aesthetically and the build variety within it is super neat, you unlock different "classes" from beating the bosses each playing differently, and each having 2 subclasses, and within each class there are skill modifying passives you can get combined with the generic ones shared across all classes. Other than that there are randomly rolled weapons with a base weapon type that buffs you if you get a specific combination of items, like turning your throwing knife item into frost damage and increasing the damage if you have another item for it.

The game falls into the middle ground of "between run progression is too strong" and "you can win without", they range from straight up just boosting damage by 30%, to doubling healing, to adding other effects, i almost beat the last boss with some 20 statpoints out of 100 ish unlocked, but couldnt heal anymore due to running out of healing items since i only had 2, which i could have had 4 or 5, on top of a free once per run revive if i had more points, so at moments like those it can feel frustrating to lose since you know you could have won, not by being better, but by having more stats. Rough estimate if for all of them would be doubling health and doubling damage atleast, on top of the entire utility tree.


One of the downsides to the game as it stands right now though is that the english translation is... rough. If you play some of them becomes more obvious, but some of them are still quite confusing, such as "backdraft" being ignite, and "sheath" being your recall sword ability.

Outside of a few bugs though the game has been super smooth, only one i got was the boss kept attacking after it was dead but i could still run to the next area, so it was just unfortunate. Some have had trouble with bosses being immortal, but so far havent managed to run into it yet.

Regardless of issues i might have i keep coming back and i keep seeing a ton of ways to build a character, with each class playing differently and there being real choices and consequences in your skill selection such as 300% more damage done but also double damage taken, or super tanky stats but cant dash. If you are looking for a game where you can bite your teeth into optimizing a specific build then this is for you.
