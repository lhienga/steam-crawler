THE UNPOLISHED HADES

It's fun, it has potential, but damn it is unpolished, and i have a lot of criticism for it.

You can read up on other reviews for pros, I'll elaborate on criticisms here.

Here you build your characters using Relics, Sect Skills, and Excalibur(weapon). You can only equip max of 4 relics, but you can keep on piling Sect skills, and equip only 1 excalibur.

In most roguelike, you constantly build your hero till the endgame, every upgrade is additive and gives you the incentive to look for a certain build, not here. The problem with relics is, there are tons of variety, probably 15+, but they don't sync with each other, and here's the deal, everything you get is totally random. So essentially you get your 4 relics very soon but in the later stages, they keep on giving you relics as upgrades, which is totally useless.

So imagine in Hades, if you get Zeus boons, you know they'll have more Zeus boons later on so that you can build it up and hopefully land that ultimate Zeus boons, and that they limit it so that you have only 4-5 gods' boons appear in 1 playthrough. But here, imagine you get Zeus boons, but there's 15+ other gods' boons that appear that doesn't sync with Zeus, and that you might get Zeus once....and never again.

Imagine in Hades where you can buy different boons, but any boons you buy adds to the character, here the shops sell you 4 relics, but if you have equipped 4 of them, essentially all the shop items are useless, because you have to replace your existing relics.

Then you have your weapon. Some high tier weapons have bonuses if you have 2 specific relics, but the stupid thing is, those 2 relics may never appear in you entire playthrough, so you can't prepare for any build and rely purely on luck. The only thing that keeps on upgrading is Sect skills. I have tons more criticism but I'll probably write it on the discussion forum.

Also, some people say there's tons of playstyle variety...I tell you honestly..NO, there's only 2, close or range. There's no animation differences to all the weapon, no length no width differences. All the different builds amount to the starting area where you can choose whether you want your weapon to deal fire/ice/lightning/poison damage etc, or you can choose that your range is now more powerful. Don't expect anything like Hades or Devil Slayer:Raksasi where you have different weapons and they work differently.

It's still fun, and I want to support the developers, but it's so unpolished. If Hades is diamond, Warm Snow is still carbon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is I guess a slightly lesser version of Hades , it is still pretty high quality for a 2d sprite game, but certainly isn't Hades quality. The game lacks the story and focuses more on the gameplay.
And as such you go trough rogue-like dungeons collecting random upgrades , tho to be perfectly frank the list of upgrades at my current gametime is rather limited, I know there are unlocks down the road so obviously it will be expanded. Regardless it is easy to go for a build of your choosing.

If I had any complaints it is about the Dashing mechanic and auto-attack range.

First the so called "flicker" dash has wierd delay at the "arrival" point , which makes the combat more clumsy than it needs to be, this has also been an issue for me in Hades UNTIL you unlock double dash, because you can rapidly switch directions. I don't think there is double dash in this game , probably because it isn't really needed.

The auto attack range is rather low on the first 3 strikes and then there's a longer attack on 4th.
It's little bit short for my taste.

So far I got to the 2nd Zone end boss and the fights don't feel too hard and will become much easier on consequitive playtroughs , there is a Upgrade tree which gives significant buffs.
You get Souls-Like Estus flasks
Around 50%+ damage boost , decent amount of attack speed, various foraging upgrades so it's safe to say
that as you expand on this you will be 1 shotting things.

I feel like the difficulty is too frontloaded , the upgrades feel OP , compared to default state, which might result in players having really bad experience first and experience that is too easy later.

All in all as a fan of this type of game I give it 8/10 if you don't know what you're getting into , this is
the type of game that has polarising effect , you will either love it or feel really frustrated.\t\t\t\t\t
