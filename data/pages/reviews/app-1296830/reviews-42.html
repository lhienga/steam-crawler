\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI found the demo of this on the Steam demos front page, and picked it up. I then played the demo as hard as I could. Surprising, almost every time I thought the demo would suddenly end, it didn't. I got to fight the first main boss fully and unlock a second sect/class, the Carousing Acalantha (the flame one). That's something big I can give this game, and these devs.

The game itself is really fun, the gameplay is super satisfying with the slashes and hit sounds, and the relic system reminds me of Transistor. The relics themselves are well designed and fun, and get more complex and powerful as their rarity increases, same with the Excaliburs (swords) you pick up that synergize with a specific thing, relic or passive or otherwise.

The sects are divided into two subclasses, usually one using the default throw swords/recall swords moveset, and the other sacrificing the ability to throw swords to augment your basic attack or your dash (Flicker) or give you a floating gourd that shoots alcohol and fireballs at people.

I already know I'm going to have so much fun with this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best Roughlites I've played recently. Incredibly satisfying and enticingly addicting gameplay; albeit a little short on content.

Artwork is interesting and unique. It feels a bit low budget but it fits the general aesthetic and vibe of the game. Gameplay wise, the synergies and maniacally powerful builds you can come up with really keep you coming back to see what crazy run you can do next. Difficulty scales appropriately and the pace of the game is just right.

The caveat is that the translation is somewhat poor or sometimes even lacking all together so it's hard if not impossible to keep up with the story. It seems like an interesting narrative concept, if only we could figure out what it all means. Also, the haphazard translation of some text in the abilities and boons make it challenging what effect hey truly have. I'm sure this will all be worked out with further development and it's by no mean a deterrent.

All in all it's an incredibly fun game from a small indie studio that is absolutely worth the price and the time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, a very fun roguelike to scratch that Hades itch. A good variety of unique aspects with cool specializations, and some really fun options to choose for relics. I thoroughly enjoyed playing through most of the game.

With that being said, as I push through Snow Storm and Blizzard difficulties, I'm not enjoying it nearly as much.

Now, it seems that the massive increase in monster health is supposed to be offset by the higher chance of epic and legendary item drops, and impossible Excalibur drops. And some of the special mods that the legendary relics have are really awesome. But in the end, it realllllly falls flat on feeling "good". The monster HP scaling is absolutely off the charts, and I have yet to see a build that really counters that.

My last run was on Snow Storm difficulty. I played the blood aspect with a legendary Bruise Herb in my skill slot, so I had insane healing. I was largely unkillable. My other 3 slots were filled with legendary Hydrargyrum in the Core and Power slots, and a legendary Poison Sac in the agility slot. I was never offered a zombie finger, as I think that would have been the only saving grace for my damage if I was able to get a legendary zombie finger in my skill slot for additional stacking. Every normal room was fairly quick and entirely effortless, and every boss took ages. The final boss took about 10 minutes to kill, and I was at absolutely 0 risk of dying at any point of the fight - I ended it with full hp and didn't use a single flask all run. Since I had the bruise herb fully sustaining me, I took every single damage buff offered....and I still felt like I had no damage. To me, that isn't "fun". That felt like a but of a god run, but I left it feeling super weak. Oh, also the alternate ending choice after the final boss bugged out on me and disappeared so I was stuck in the boss room. I had to save&exit and restart only to slog through the boss a second time for another 10 minutes. That sucked.

Also, there is the meta upgrades.. I already have the entire meta tree allocated, and another 50 some coins for it. I feel like it maxes out far too quickly with nothing else to spend the upgrades on. Some of the meta upgrades are really cool and useful, but if you can max it before even unlocking Blizzard difficulty, theres not much point in pushing further is there?

So at this point I am tempted to go back to the Fine or Medium snow difficulties just so its not a slog fest to complete a run, yet I want to play with some of the unique legendary and impossible items that are super rare at those difficulties. Kind of just leaves me with a sour taste in my mouth.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am nowhere near beating the game -- just managed to get to the end of the second chapter, and the second major boss... but my God is it amazing.

Two major things:
-build variety (each new statue/class creates A LOT of possibilities and synergies, and most of them are hella fun in their own way. it's not easy to keep the game challenging while also constantly creating the feeling that your character is OP, but that's exactly how I feel almost every run);
-convenience and flexibility (for example, changing the position of your relics on the fly? easy! don't commit to ♥♥♥♥, improvise and have fun!)

It also has a lot of flavour. I do love me some super-Chinese games -- and one part of it is definitely because I lie to myself thinking that playing it helps a bit with my very rusty Chinese... but if you're looking for something very fresh and chunky, lore-wise, this game'll be right up your alley.

also, if you love wuxia stuff, this game is cocaine.

EDIT: just got to the ending for the first time, with a build so OP that even the heavens looked at me with nothing but disgust. It was glorious.
NG+, here I come!

While it has a similar look and feel to Hades, it does bring other systems making it feel fresh.
-Twin stick use for your movement and aiming with flying sword attacks.
-Distilling items for benefits during runs.
-Weapon upgrade system with two mechanics (currently).

While I have run across minor bugs I have not come across anything game breaking yet.
-Settings randomly changing such as language, resolution, muting all sound, etc. (Rare)
-Being near debris on a lunge attack can trap you between the debris and a wall temporarily. (Rare)
-Delayed hit from AoE attacks that have been exited. (Uncommon)\t\t\t\t\t
