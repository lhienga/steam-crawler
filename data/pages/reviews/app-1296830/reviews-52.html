\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is OK. I'm recommending it, but you probably want to buy it when it's on a decent sale. As everyone else has said, this game is Hades: if you've played your fill of that game and want a different but weaker in every aspect clone, Warm Snow is your ticket. The concept is strong, the parts are all here, but the polish is completely lacking.

Part of this is a translation problem, without a doubt.As the player, you'll have an impossible time telling how different abilities actually interact with each other. Are bonuses multiplicative? Additive? Who knows. There isn't a player profile with numbers, the tooltips are inadequate, and abilities have unexpected results. Planning your build is replaced by praying your interpretation is approximately true. (It won't be, half the time.)

Even beyond the lack of a profile and adequate English explanations, the game suffers from major balance issues. In Hades, you need the grind to win. Winning was an excruciatingly hard process. Here, some combinations are so completely, senselessly broken you'll win without effort. I facerolled the entire game on perhaps my sixth attempt - after dying to the first major boss every other time, with hardly any of the unlockables unlocked - on the basis of mechanics I couldn't possibly explain to you. But the bosses died when I hit them, so hey, victory.

If this game had been beta-tested and tweaked for even a few more months, it could be quite solid. The controls are tight, and the developers brought some interesting ideas to the table. As it exists, it scratches an itch left by an infinitely-stronger predecessor, but fails to stick core expectations; a stellar roguelite experience demands an interface by which a player can plan. Warm Snow is a fun, random crapshoot of affixes and abilities, but it lacks replayability due to loss of control. Having beaten the game once (by sheer chance), I have little desire to return and find out which builds I think should work don't (for no explicable reason), and which random ones do lethal damage due to ???.\t\t\t\t\t

Updated as of 07/06/2022

Since the Major bug that kept the games progression locked in place was fixed, Warm Snow has proven to be a very enjoyable title. It's a good game if you feel like sinking a few hours into something and relaxing with some mindless hack 'n slash.

That being said, with maps remaining static for each biome (the maps aren't randomised) it tends to get repetitive rather quickly if you binge play, so try keeping it to an hour or two and you might draw more enjoyment out of it in the long run.

Other than that, the only thing I can speak to is the Price Vs. Value of the game which is completely fair and justified. You get what you pay for, a good game that you'll enjoy playing.

So if you are new to Warm Snow and you want to make things a little easier here's a hint...

Pick the Goard and Skill that makes it "Alternate" fire turn to fire. I know this sounds Vague now but sink a few hours into the game and you'll understand what I meant, Promise !!!\t\t\t\t\t
