All of these can be overlooked because the main hack and slash gameplay is satisfying enough to get stuck in the "just one more run" loop hoping to get the dream build.

No, the one thing that will break that loop every time and make me put down a game is getting stuck on the edges of maps on random pieces of "aesthetic" terrain. Some are breakable, but many of the objects on screen are completely unpathable, which should be fine, however, there are visual traps where you may or may not be able to move around an object.

And yes, I'm writing this because I got stuck on some random piece of the map by somehow dashing into a liminal space and lost a good run.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the Hades successor I've been waiting for, at least in terms of gameplay.

If you enjoyed the core mechanics of Hades, you'll likely enjoy this too. You start runs by choosing what is essentially a class (think a weapon in Hades), then fight through levels, picking which path to take to earn different weapons, or skills, or heals etc. It'll feel very familiar to Hades players. The controls are very responsive, and I've never had a death that I felt wasn't my own fault.

The story and world aren't on the same level as Hades, but what is? The story is pretty basic and simplistic, there just to drive the game forward rather than deliver a compelling narrative.

There are a couple of issues, though;

First, the translation is really lacking, or outright missing, in places. Some things aren't translated at all, including boss dialogue and some on-screen alerts in the "base" stages. There are also typos all over the place.

The other major issue is poor information presentation. This comes in a few forms; some information is too long for the box it's meant to fit in and overflows or gets cut off, especially with high-rarity weapon stats & info. There are also some descriptions which just don't make sense thanks to poor translation, and it's also really difficult to compare the effect of different weapons/skills etc, because there's no place to just see your actual stats like damage output etc. That makes it pretty tricky to plan out a build or really figure out if a change will be a positive or a negative.

It wouldn't take much to fix these issues, so hopefully it's something the developers will address quickly.

That being said, if you can put up with the janky translation and having to figure out what some things do on your own/using the steam community, and you liked Hades and want something familiar but fresh, I highly recommend this game.
