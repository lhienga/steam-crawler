\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an action roguelike which means repetitiveness is part of the core gameplay. Putting that aside, Warm Snow is incredibly shallow and unpolished. I felt like I am wasting my time just by playing it.

My main complaints are:
- Story is almost non existent. This can be looked past if the combat and mechanics are fun.
- But no. 90% the enemies including bosses are just mindless meat shields that you don't need any strategy or positioning to take them down.
- Starting skills : There's a huge variety of them to choose from, each requires different playstyle. However, the balance are off the charts. A few of them are so powerful it's a guarantee win even on the hardest difficulty, while the rest are so garbage I don't know why they are there to begin with. Hence for people that love min/maxing stuff, you'll probably end up only using a few and ignore the rest.
- Weapons : There's also a lot of them. But, they are all swords with the same movesets. The only differences are the % modifiers as well as some passive effects.
- Items : The ONLY useful one that is a game changer is the one that offers lifesteal. In fact it's so broken you can win the entire game without even healing once.
- Elemental damage : Sounds cool, but it's a wasted feature in this game as they feels nothing more than a % modifier with colored words. There isn't any real weakness/ resistant mechanics in this game to begin with.
- Status effect : Also add nothing in depth other than % modifier. There also isn't any weakness/ resistant/ immunity. It's just very weird to inflict frost or poison on machine type enemies.
- The difficulty scaling is the worst. Even with the best possible setup, I find it straight up tedious and unfun grinding down bosses's massive lifepoints. Even killing normal enemies felt like a chore. Imagine a sheer amount of randomized creeps that come at you all at once, each with huge hp but no brain. Yeah sounds fun.
- Infinite mode : I don't get what's the point for this. Can't find the motivation to do mindless grinding.
- DLCs : Cost more than half the price of the main game. I can't find the point of buying this either.

Conclusion:
- A lot of people said that Warm Snow is a discount Hades. I think it's a huge insult as Warm Snow is nowhere close to that.
- This game at discount is probably worth it, as it offers more than 10 - 15 hours of gameplay. It's the type of game you wanna buy, experience it, then uninstall it.
- I had my fun in the first 5 - 6 hours, after that it's just a snooze fest.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWarm Snow is an unusual game. Part melee, part twin-stick shooter.

Your intro to the game will begin with clearing your way to the temple. With a trusty sword in hand, you'll defeat a few enemies and acquire a few flying swords as you progress quickly to the temple. You are met with a world that has been diseased by a "warm snow" - a type of curse brought on by four beings of power, transgressing their gods, maybe. This is just the beginning of your progression.

The temple in which you enter is the main hub of choosing your deity. Including DLC, there are a total of 10 statues to choose from (base game = 6, but the sixth deity is allocated to specific unlock requirements, as are the other four). Each deity provides two paths - sects - each with their own attributing gameplay features.

You will die, very likely, on your first playthrough, but don't worry. Warm Snow requires patience and realization that each run is going to be very different depending on the choices taken. You may have a very bad run one day versus a really good OP run the next.

Nuance is the name of the game. Swords, relics, sect skills (or, "doctrines", as I like to call them), and the skill trees - they all play a part. There are three skill trees for the base game. All are unlocked over time via certain points you can acquire from bosses. I cheesed the first boss over and over again for it's 1 attribute point for a few hours... Pretty easy boss, though.

There are many unlockable stats, relics and swords to gain in the game. The accompanying DLCs allow for unlocking more for the base game. But, for the most part, the base game is where you're going to be spending your time, likely, in the beginning. Each document and item you acquire within Warm Snow's world will help in unlocking a few extra attributes and more items (swords, relics), but also help provide some story / background to the "universe" Warm Snow is surrounded in.

It's somewhat a deep game filled with action, nuance and fun. If you think that this game is interesting, spend some time on YouTube and check out some gameplay videos.

The only down-point that I will say, any English used in the game may be a little difficult to understand, BUT knowing what is meant will definitely help in progressing forward.

Other than that, I recommend this game for a bit of fun, for all those OP runs.\t\t\t\t\t
