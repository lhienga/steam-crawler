What I love about Warm Snow:
- Tons of build combinations makes each run exciting and new.
- Player movement is super smooth.
- Enemy hit boxes don't feel like they're cheating you.
- The game is challenging, but not overwhelmingly difficult.
- Art style looks great.

What I don't like:
- Some features, like forge, aren't explained (unless I just missed it). Overall, this is an awesome game!\t\t\t\t\t

A Unique and Fun, Albeit Flawed, Isometric Roguelite
My progress as of this review: I have cleared 3 difficulties.

My pros:
  • The combat feels good when you're attacking. It feels very impactful and quick, the movement is smooth, and you have a lot of attack and movement option.
  • The building is fun and there are a lot of choices to make.
  • The initial difficulty is extremely approachable. My first clear was 4h into my playtime. There is also no artificial gate or meta-progression preventing you from beating this game on your first attempt, which I see as a win.
  • Ghost lady booba
  • The meta-progression is meaningful. You can actually feel the effects of your progression and there is not an excessive amount of grinding required to complete your tree.
  • You can quit and return to where you were in the middle of a run. This is a great QoL feature that some roguelites don't include, but as a father this is very important to me.
  • Beautiful hand-drawn art and awesome particle effects.
  • Good sound design, music isn't memorable.

My Cons:
  • Excessive knock-downs from enemies. A lot of the time this CC will come from offscreen or an enemy will rush you with not nearly enough time to react. This issue is only exacerbated as you progress through the difficulties.
  • Lots of choices, but not all choices made equal. I feel pretty confined as to what I can build or what God I can start with. Some just feel straight up useless.
  • HUGE ramp up in difficulty in the final area. You may think you have a fantastic build going only to find out the sponges in the final area take 2 full minutes to kill and hit like a semi.
  • Too few i-frames. Especially considering some enemies have attacks that have very small dodge windows. Fighting the concubine is an absolute slog because of this, unless you have a broken build. There is an item to increase the i-frames, but I feel as though they should just fix the dodge by adding innate i-frames..
  • Unskippable boss cutscenes. Every time I get to the bosses I have to watch an unskippable mini-cinematic that I've already seen 10 times. It just adds unnecessary time to runs and gets annoying.
  • If possible, combat clarity needs work. A lot of the time I find I'm just face-tanking without realizing because I can't for the life of me see what the hell is going on behind all the particle effects.
  • Lu Kunhai auto-fail mechanic. No. Just no. Remove it. If you have the indomitable perk it makes this fight literally not possible to complete unless you react 3 seconds in advance to a randomized moveset. Also, indomitable sucks, please fix.
  • Enemies get very spongey later on, and without a crazy build you will be trudging through an absolute swamp of HP trying to get to the end. I've had several runs I just straight up reset because I was on the final stage and healthy but didn't want to spend the ridiculous amount of time it would take to whittle down the enemies and bosses.

    TL;DR - It's very fun but has its share of flaws. Overall, I'd say the pros outweigh the cons here and I'd recommend it. I'm sitting at just over 20hrs and planning to put plenty more in.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo let's get the elephant outta the Way, I won't compare this to Hades, since I never played it.

Secondly, yes the Translation would really benefit from some polish, and some Weapon Text should be optimized since they don't fit the Box.
But for the most Part every Item is understandable enough to play it.

And third, there are a lot of reviews about a Bug with the first Boss being invincible, as far as I can tell that has been resolved a long Time ago.

All in all I'd say Warm Snow is a nice Rouge-lite experience, that has a multitude of Factors that can alter your run, most and foremost Sect's (Scrolls) that provide passive Bonuses, but the Weapon of Choice also has a huuuuge effect, specially on 'Classes' that have a Signature Weapon that's benefits them most.

The thing I found most interesting were the Relics, Items that provide Bonuses depending on where in the four Slot 'Inventory' they are placed, the huge thing that can easily be missed/underestimated is, you can always reallocate the Relics order, to adept the effects to new Relics or a new weapon/sect you found along the way.

Coming to the Classes, each run you choose one Avatar which provides a General passive and a secondary playstyle defining passive of two Options. At the start you only have 1 Avatar to pick from, with 6 when you unlocked all of them trough Gameplay.

All in all the Game isn't that long, one run on average took me one Hour, so it lives from repeated Playthroughs, for which you have 6 Classes (12 if you count the Subclasses), and 6 Difficulty Levels.
Apart from the First and last Act, all Acts consist of multiple Loots Areas, a Mini Boss and an Act Boss, while there is a pool of 2 to 3 Minibosses per Act, the Act Bosses are always the same.

The great thing about the Difficulty design is, that at first all Acts are separate, which each Difficulty bleeding the Enemy Types and Mechanic over into all Acts when you hit Difficulty 5 (Blizzard).

The Final Difficulty (White Ash) where you can obtain Master Skins for each of the 6 Classes by playing trough with them is basically identical with Blizzard except that all Act Bosses (and some Mini Bosses) have an additional 'Move' which makes the Fight quite different, especially for Melee Focused Classes (only beat it with a ranged Class yet).

Against some negative Reviews I read, I found the Controls to be spot on (using a PS Controller), and never had any Issues in that compartment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLatest edit: tried this after the DLC dropped... yup, game 's balance is still a joke.

Edited after finishing on the 2nd highest difficulty.

So... the base game is fun, and I like the variety of builds that are actually different. But there are several glaring problems with this game that makes it questionable as of this moment.

1. Terrible difficulty scaling. The enemies and especially bosses scales way too hard for the higher difficulty to a point that is not fun to play. A great example would be the 2nd boss. In lower difficulties you can finish her first phase in less than 5 mins. On the 2nd highest difficulty it took about good 10 mins with one of the highest dps builds I could make. And don't even get me started on the last boss, which is about 20 mins of repeating the same patterns. It got so bad that I actually fell asleep for when I was fighting the 3rd boss, luckily I woke up in like 2 seconds and pause the game. Still, falling asleep while fighting a boss should never happen in a game. My runs now consistently goes from 90 mins to 2 hrs. And that's from the 2nd highest difficulty. The runs on the highest difficulty will be even longer.

2. Some builds are too weak. It's a balancing issue, that some builds are just easier to beat the game than others... well at least they can do it much faster/safer.

3. Too many set pieces for bosses moveset. Like the last boss, he'd do a move in his last form trying to suck you in, and then if you escape, you'd be fine. The problem is this move takes forever, and it's really not a challenge... so it just becomes a time sink. You'd see him repeating the same move - and I'm not exaggerating - more than a dozen times in the fight. You only have so much of a window to burst him down when you can, and then he goes back to repeating the same cycle again. It's stupid and a waste of time. IMO the boss should only do that move once to signal that he's in his last form now, and then go on to do other moves.

4. Hard to read what's on the screen for some builds. I like melee builds, but some of them you literally can't see your character nor the enemies since the special effects basically cover everything. Like using the electric melee build to call down thunder - I can't see where I am or what my character is doing or even what I'm hitting or what's hitting me. At that point I'm just going off intuition but honestly this should not happen.

5. The bosses get boring after the 5th playthrough or so.

If you think you'd only play this game at the first 2~3 difficulty, go ahead. It's quite fun that way. But as you go up in difficulty, it quickly becomes more unfun because the way the devs think difficulty works is by making everything a sponge without adding any new elements.\t\t\t\t\t
