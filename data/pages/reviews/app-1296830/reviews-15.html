\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is what Hades wishes it could be. 80 hours in and it has not felt like a grind at all. INCREDIBLE variety and depth of builds, even more than Dead Cells. Has the same addictive feeling of Vampire Survivors.

I truly hope the devs keep updating and improving the game, there is so much potential here.

TIP:
- Sword/Relic synergy is the best part of the game. Take advantage of the sword reroll mechanic as much as possible. It would surprise you how often you actually get the sword that you are looking for.

PROS:
- Insane amount of builds/playstyle. I have played for 80 hours and new builds/combinations still come up
- Imagine if Hades and Vampire Survivors merged
- Devs show great understanding and creativity on power ups and abilities. Is not just "Do 50% more damage"
- New DLC content is extremely good, I legit would have payed for it. They did the Boss Rush system better than Dead Cells
- Bosses are unique and refreshing

NEUTRAL:
- Dashing is quite odd in this game. iFrames are pretty short so do not really on them, you actually have to dodge at the right time or you will get hit. I think is raises the skill cap of the game but it was annoying at the beginning.

Get the skill that increases the invulnerability during dash, it makes it feel better.

CONS:
- Is not super polished
- The English translation is very rough so it takes time to figure out the systems of the game. I think this may turn off a lot of people. Once you understand the relic/sword synergy, the game opens up and it has hundreds of possible builds.
- DLC content is also a bit hard to understand at the beginning. I was about to give up on it until it finally clicked; once it did, I could not stop playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me just state up front Warm-Snow is a good game. The art direction,moment to moment combat,customization,and setting are amazing. Though mostly "heroic fantasy" it also has a horror/hopeless desolation vibe that turns the brutality and creepiness up to eleven. If you have also enjoyed titles like Kuon,DreadOut,Nioh,Onimusha,Bloody-Spell,Eastern Exorcist,and Fatal Frame in the past Warm-Snow is very similar with its' visual designs and cultural inflections.

Likewise if you are the type of gamer whom enjoys having access to many different kinds of character builds,customization options,and procedural generated levels with branching paths that occasionally throw in alternate mid-bosses or unique treasures then Warm-Snow is going to be your next obsession for awhile! It is a commendable rogue-like done by developers whom put a lot of love and heart into this project.

As such it is a pity I'm not the biggest fan of rogue-likes. I'm often "suckered in" because on the surface they appear to be satisfyingly visceral and flashy hack n slash action rpgs. The caveat always is the very hard initial difficulty and "starting over from the beginning" upon death just a little stronger than you were before.

This means you are left re-treading the same scenery and often facing the same enemies. Red exp. crystals which you use to permanently raise buffs,stats,and abilities whilst securing stronger equipment you find in your travels are given to you at a trickle until you defeat one of the "bigger bosses". Don't misunderstand me. This is a power fantasy and "eventually" you will become a war-god! It's just Warm-Snow wants you to grind for it and "earn" your place among the heavens.

The "Enchanted flying-sword style" makes this hero feel distinctly unique. You can do more damage to enemies by impaling them with your floating blades before attacking them in close melee. In an impressive flourish you can call all your swords back to your side and even charge them up with various mystical elemental attributes. It's a welcome change and this basic building block can be expanded upon in unexpectedly devastating ways! (It is possible to become the ultimate enlightened dervish of destruction ascending into speed-running nirvana!)

For 17.99 Warm-Snow is a solid purchase. Just be sure you know what you are getting into as far as rogue-likes are concerned......

Pros

+Game is as beautiful as a painting come to life in all its' beauty and all its' grotesquery.

+Intriguing heroic fantasy/horror setting. (The giant White-Lotus reminds me of The Marker from Dead Space.)

+Moment to moment combat is a visual feast of speed,grace,mystic mayhem,and brutality.

+Plethora of customization/character build options.

+Branching paths with varied rewards and a few alternate mid-boss encounters.

+Interesting challenges for those willing to take on more risks for greater rewards.

+You can pick up your quest from where you quit. Use this strategically because you know what happens when you perish!

+If you are smart enough and know where to allocate all your relics,bonuses,and skill points you can "break the game" in the best way possible. I've seen this done in play-thrus but I'm still a learning novice.

+Rescued side characters offer up their unique expertise to strengthen your weapons or offer you more gifts and power-ups.

+Sprawling titan-sized ghost-lady merchant will scratch that particular itch!

+Defeating the main lords unlocks new elemental fighting styles accessed by the increasing prayer statues in the main hub.


Cons

-Excessive grinding

-Upon death you start over from the very beginning

-Red exp. crystals funneled to you at a mere trickle until you have your first major victories.

-Certain bosses have cheap one-hit-kill techniques. (But you can learn to recognize and avoid them even if it is by the skin of your teeth!)

-Despite the "random generation" of locations they still are uniform in theme depending on the area. Aka,don't expect too much variance each time through.

-Repetition. Lots of repetition.\t\t\t\t\t
