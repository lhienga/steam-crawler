\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a great shame that Dungeon Defenders II is as dissapointing as it is. I loved the first one and played it loads in splitscreen with my partner. While I bought a few DLC packs (that I understand were fairly controversal) the base game felt like a complete experience with plenty of levels and characters and tons of replay value.

I actually bought Dungeon Defenders II before it became free to play for around £18, due to my love of the first game. My initial reaction, particularly of the artsyle, was of how much it resembled those crappy mobile games like Clash of Clans. Dungeon Defenders 1 was of course very cartoony but had a level of charm that elevated it above the generic cartoon style of F2P cash-cow mobile trash. Dugeon defenders II on the other had looks very tacky.

The problems don't end at the artstyle though; by going F2P the game now feels like a rubbish F2P MMO. When launching you are greeted by messages asking to buy things and enter a lobby with loads of other players milling about and spamming the chat with nonsense. As a result I overall did not enjoy my experience with the game so far.
My reward for both buying in to the game early and spending £18 was very little, maybe 1 skin as far as I can remember. Performance isn't great although the game is in early access so this may change.

Overall I feel the move to F2P was a huge mistake. While the tower defense genre is hardly an easy genre to compete in what with many other games existing in this genre I feel Dungeon Defenders 1's blend of tower defense with Diablo-style classes and loot was unique and interesting to stand apart from the competition. On top of that brilliant levels, music, art design, and charm meant the first Dungeon Defenders was well worth the up-front investment. I feel the developers almost didn't have faith in their own product with Dungeon Defenders II in their decision to go F2P, feeling they needed to rely on the game being free to attract an audience and that an up-front cost would turn away buyers. As a result the game has taken a huge hit to it's value; feeling tacky, grindy, and just generally unappealing.

There is still hope I suppose, the game is still in Early Access and there is time for the devs to reverse the decision of going F2P or at least improve the game signifiicantly that it becomes worthy of a try. By the time the title releases (and exits early access) I will give it another shot and see if it is any better, I hope so because I want to enjoy this game as much as the first but so far I've not been impressed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ abomination. To call this *dungeon Defenders* makes me sick. The greed on display here is baffeling. While it may *technically* not be pay to win, the associated grind outs battlefront 2 to shame. The most obvious and egregious is unlocking new characters. A new character costs 10k *defender tokens* (which is a 2nd ingame currency along with gold however tokens only acts as a tool to regulate progression). How quickly can I rack up 10k tokens you ask? Well lets see, there are daily missions which reward around 300-400 and weekly missions which are around 800 and then every map you complete rewards between 2 and 23 tokens. Yes, those are the numbers, I didnt miss a 0 anywhere. If you add this all up saying you can grind 50 tokens per hour from random maps and you complete your dailys and weeklys. It will take. 3. WEEKS. playing 8hrs a day, every day. To unlock a new Character. oh yeah not to mention that you have a limited number of character slots, so you will quickly need to buy *character cards* wich are another 2k tokens and the cherry on top? theres a token cap of 12k. But 10k + 2k is 12k, so its fine right? yeah, but I hope you dont want the new character when it comes out because they release at 12k.

Moving on, The oversimplification of the gear system in this game blows my mind. It took an already very basic system from the first game and GUTTED it. The first game had 14 universal stats in total (4 being resistances). In practice this was more like 4 for builders and 7-9 for dps with the last, movement speed, being neglected almost entirely (due to its low cap and power creep). each piece of gear could drop with up to 8 stats and 4 resistances.
Seems this is too complicated for DD2 which has 2 stats + armour. They have however, added something. Gear can now have modifiers wich effect your abilites and defences, which sounds cool right? Sadly in all the time ive spent with this game, I have yet to see them implimented in the ways spoken about when they were announced, ie build altering/game changing.

And now for (in my opinion) the worst part of this game. This game uses a *hero deck* system to manage your characters ingame. This system allows the player to chose 4 characters for a certain map which can then be swapped between on the fly using hotkeys. So of the 10+ characters released with more bound to come. At any given time you can only use 4 of the characters you own! This system takes a dump on build creativity and character synergys. One of the strongest aspects of the first game was how builds utilised all the available characters unique defences. I can't say im an expert on the current meta but generally you would have at least 1 character dedicated to dps and the others all built for defences, so in practice you've only got 3 types of defences per map. Here's a fun thought. That new charactrer you spent the last 3 weeks famring doesn't not fit into your setup so now you need to rework you're 4 characters so you can use you're new character. You can argue that this is a good thing that premotes creativity which is fair, but I'd argue that it only serves to do the opposite. While the process of finding the best paring for you're new character is a good thing, you will soon find the best conbination and then... thats it. No more messing around, no more creativity. The more I think about this 4 character system the more of a failure it becomes. It's ludicrous, that with a cast of 10+ characters, that you should be limited to 4 at any one time. It's basic math, there are vastly more combinations with 10 characters than 4. But then it hits you. *oh this you, this game was so obviously built around console controles*. Which is fine, games can be made with console in mind, But the main problem (at least for me) is the reason why this choice was made. Lookin back into the developement it becomes blindingly clear why this game is what it is. The devs spent 2 years trying to make DD into a MOBA (because at the time MOBAs were the big thing). How a company could be so ditached from its playerbase did not boad well for the games future. I feel this makes it clear that the devs weren't trying to improve on the game they had but rather, they were aiming to hit the biggest audience and cash in. This mentality, unfortunately, came with them as they resumed work on the DD2 we see today.

On paper DD2 seems like it would be successful. The barrier to entry is quite low. There is a lot to do and the endgame takes a while to reach so the longevity of the game is a sure-fire thing. However, in reality, this is a dull husk, crapped out by a machine with no concept of fun. All the devs want is to have players logging in day after day to eventually get frustraighted and start spending cash. oh god I haven't talked about the microtransactions. yeah, they're here. They're as poorly done as you can imagine. I won't get into it because it seems like every game nowdays has horribly predatory microtransactions. All I will say is that in the *home base* type area, the npc's stall/shop is in biggest and in the centre of the map and the first thing you see when you walk out of the spawn room. Oh also they updated the same shop in private taverns to be comically larger than everything else (it really hurts the asthetic).

This game has absolutely no regard for its old, dadicated fanbase. Instead opting to go for a most dumbed down, casual friendly experience, to appeal to the widest possible ordiance. For no other reason than to rake in as much money as possible. This *game* is a gigantic burning trash heap. I STRONGLY reccomend you DON'T INSTALL this abomination and above all never give this company money.

THIS. GAME. IS. ♥♥♥♥♥♥\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDungeon Defenders 2 is a prime example of what you get when you take a good game (the original Dungeon Defenders) and try to turn it into a cash cow. A turd - and we all know what you can't polish.

If you can make it past the poorly designed menus without banging your head against a brick wall, then congratulations, you now have to deal with creating a character.

In this revolting mess, you get to pick between a whopping two characters. Don't get too excited trawling through the abundance of stale appearances, or all the other characters and their abilities, because if you want these you will have to search deep into your wallet.

The gameplay is trite and unchallenging. Tactics are few and far between. Gear is the most influential factor in determining your success with various dungeons and difficulties and most of the game revolves around grinding gear. Even the most challenging of dungeons are a cakewalk with sufficient gear. The small number of dungeons in the game are badly designed and lack variety or scope for testing different strategies. There is no challenge. In addition to this, the game is filled with too many unnecessary complications and too much garbage. For example, having to sift through a plethora of junk items after each dungeon to work out what you should sell or equip to make more bag space for the next dungeon.

The bad news doesn't end there. The endless amount of bugs, the sluggish mouse response time and the eye splitting aliasing are enough to make a kakapo question what the developers have been doing in the 5+ years this game has supposedly been in development.

Trendy have tried everything they can to milk this pathetic excuse for a game from charging for characters, pets, appearances and bag space, to equipment and upgrades. These pitiful attempts at stealing your wages would give even Martin Lewis a seizure.

The game has been in early access for years and, judging by the state of it, needs to stay there for a lot more. It showcases everything that's wrong with modern gaming. It truly is hard to find anything good to say about Dungeon Defenders 2. It pales in comparison to the original. I would not even waste your time downloading it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat I really liked about the first Dungeon Defenders had little-if-anything to do with actually getting out there and fighting; the repetitiveness even before the Quest for the Lost Eternia Shards stuff didn't bother me, because you had multiple defense zones spread across the map to keep intact, and a solid mix of options to make it happen (with each class being unbalanced, making it worthwhile to shore up weaknesses with another class's strengths) before the DLC heroes came out, who were overall stronger, and negated the use of apprentice / squire towers almost entirely. (I still like to try with my mage / ranger mix, as if it's True Defender Mode, but I digress.) What was fun was that defensive focus: I'd use my ranger to clear a lane to protect some towers before zooming across the map to heal the other side, and running off again to face the ogre on another end. I felt like my mana spending mattered a lot, and my presence in one area could make or break the win; my towers were doing most of the killing, but *I* was their salvation and guardian while they, my army, fought the endless battle.

DD2 doesn't feel that way at all. Between the large influx of mana, the layouts (I can't explain why they're worse, but they all feel tight and close compared to any map from QftLES or later, making it that much more apparent when you don't have the right power level, as you feel powerless standing there, desperately trying to repair things.), and having one less tower per class, it doesn't feel like my tower choices are what count; it feels like I just need gear. A mage's barrier and a squire's are about even; instead of wimpy mage barriers stripping elemental resist, both do spike damage as things hit them, with health determined by gear. Instead of having lightning AoE, fireball splash, arcane haste and sniper towers, I have front cone attack towers. The class towers feel like they're supposed to be balanced. And now they don't matter.

I wanted desperately to like it, based on affection for DD1. But, DD2 has a larger focus on the action side, with each class getting four combat spells and the mana split to builder's mana and self-regenerating fighter's mana. The four spells are pretty weak if you're builder specialized, but the little bursts of balanced power are still more productive to the defenses' aid than actually standing there and healing things - making it feel that much more useless to be a builder. Honestly if you want to do well solo, it seems you should just action specialize, build tons of barricades just to slow down the waves of encroaching enemies, and wipe everything out yourself.

Honestly, it feels weird saying I hate it because it's balanced, but... I hate it because it's balanced. DD1 had clear fantasies for each of its eight classes that centered on what their towers did or didn't do (i.e., mage's glass cannon towers that hit really hard and died really fast, Squire's stocky, slow things that could murder eventually but didn't die often, hunter's tricksy traps that had to be replaced often, monk's impressive area control...) but I've tried interchanging towers in strategies for DD2 and it didn't seem to matter at all. I've tried one of the DLC heroes, the Mystic, and she just feels like she does all the things better than the core four, which is what led me to write this review. (She has one of each tower type, and her front cone attack tower has a really big front cone.) Look. I liked you guys back in DD1, Trendy. But I felt a lot smarter in DD1 because I was picking towers based on what they did, rather than how good the gear was on the guy who placed that tower. Hell; I hate to say it, but I even liked Eternia better because, even in Eternia, dungeon defending meant playing a tower defense game. If I wanted to play Diablo with cute graphics, I wouldn't be looking to something called dungeon DEFENDERS. Maybe play a bit of Kingdom Rush to clear your heads and get back to me?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played DD1 and loved every second of playing. I was extremely excited about DD2 but when it came out as a MOBA, it bombed royally! I was extremely disappointed with this and even paid money to support their plans to change it back into its original gameplay. Instead of redoing the game, it appears they kept some coding and left a bit of a MOBA game play.
Here are my issues with the game.
1. Time - It’s been years and this game is still in alpha/Beta testing. Extremely disappoint considering how much time it’s been.
2. Price for heroes - The price for heroes jumped from $2-$5 to $99!!!!! What were you thinking?
3. Maps - The maps are just wonky to say the least. A whole wide hallway and one blockade in the center can stop them?
4. Towers - Some hero towers are either underpowered (mage) or overpowered (Lava mancer). One Volcano can hit an entire map from the center of the playing field. What’s the point?
5. Gear - Your gears IPwr is off. One level 50 green with less stats have higher IPwr levels than a level 50 Mythical with more stats. This makes no sense.
6. Heroes - You limit the heroes to four unless you pay more money. Not even LOL, Warcraft, Clash of clans, or other iOS games do this. If I am going to pay money, then you better offer me something great in return not BS costumes!
7. Costumes - its overkill! Fix the game and make it actually work instead of playing dress up with your hero.
8. Abilities - The old abilities are gone and replaced with junk abilities which require mana that cost too much and feel like a MOBA game.
9. Mana - The mana is way too small to see and having everyone run around the entire map just to collect mana that only they can see is annoying. If Player 1 already covered an area, why does player 2 have to run that same area to get mana.
10. Mana - The amount of Mana a player receives is way too low.
11. Pets - You no longer receive pets like last time, instead you have random drops with random stats, with random eggs, and no real way to farm. it’s all just luck. Which really sucks
12. Bags - I have 12 bags or so with 16 slots and scrolling and trying to organize them is a pain and a hassle. No real way to organize all your gear, instead you have to play move and shuffle just to get gear in the right place.
13. Tutorial - you really get no tutorial on anything, it all just click and figure out on your own and hope you don’t lose anything.
14. Towns - You get randomly disconnected from town because a host has left. Why do I have to rely on a host for a town meetup, shouldn’t this be a server hosted town and not based on someone’s connection. Does this mean that one person’s PC is hosting all our heroes at once? Seems crappy.
15. End of map - You have to wait for all players to make a decision before replaying the map or going to town. if a player disconnected or leaves the session before you all press G, you all have to wait for the countdown to be over before you can go to town. Seems like a complete waste of time.
16. Hero’s orbs/Spheres - The cost is too high and the abilities are too low.

I can go on and on about how much this game is nowhere near game ready. I recommend either waiting to even see if this game will finish or just going back to DD1.

As of right now, I cannot stand playing this game anymore. I have four toons, all 680 ipwr levels and am bored with farming maps that have no real direction.
