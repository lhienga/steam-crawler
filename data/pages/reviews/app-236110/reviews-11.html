\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really loved the first part of the game. Dungeon Defenders 2 has decently fun gameplay, and while it plays different from the first part, definitely has a right to exist. They even planned on making a pretty fair free to play model; so where is my problem with all of this?

Their free to play model is based primarily on cosmetics and inventory space, which is great! However, they did not think that through to the end. To explain why i'll need to describe one of the systems that is primarily responsible for making your defenses stronger: Shards.

So, what are Shards?
Every usable item had 3 shard-slots. Shards give additional stat boni or effects, like more tower health, more tower strength, or a chance for the projectile to stun, explode, .. you get the idea. Each shard can only drop from a specific difficulty level. Each difficulty level however, has ~30 shards that can drop there. The first problem that arises from this is that you basically have to grind lower difficulties, which can be very boring. Even with gear completely overpowering the difficulty you play at, a run takes about 7 minutes (with loading, building, completing, ..). Through concesutive wins you can get 2 shards from that difficulty per run, so let's do some quick math assuming you always get 2. Since there are roughly 30 shards per difficulty level, you'd need, on average, 15 runs to get a single copy of the shard you want. With 7 minutes per run, which is about the best possible, we are talking about 105 minutes. Sounds fair so far, however you need multiple copies of most good shards, since they are used in multiple towers.
The real problem arises when we consider a mechanis called gilding shards. Gilded shards are basically better version of the normal shards, or in other words can be upgraded slightly higher than normally possible. To gild a shard you need 10 copies to sacrifice, thus combining 11 shards into 1. Now, gilding is not necessary for the stat gains, or the fact that normal shards get "reset" in endgame often, meaning you have to pay gold to level them again, while gilded shards stay at max rank.

The real problem is the inventory it takes up. Shards do not stack. A single inventory is 8x8, so 64 spaces. You can only buy up to 8 bags and 8 bank inventories of the same size. From the sheer amount of shards there are, it's easily tellable that you cant keep everything, but even when you only focus on good tower shards and shards for the characters you play most (plus maybe keeping one copy of all shards for other heroes), you will eventually run into problems.
This is coming from a player that bought close to all available inventory space (with cash ofc), so it's not a "free players convenience problem" - which is should be honestly. I'm currently using 3 bags and 5 bank inventories for shards alone and they are bursting full. Another huge problem is that each time you get new shards (or a bag full of new shards) you'll have to sort them into your bags, manually! The game becomes an inventory management simulator in lategame, which is a shame, but throwing away your precious shards is also a no-go. This results in very inconvenient inventory space problems, mixed with very inconvenient inventory management.
All of this could have been easily fixed as well, if they simply implemented a single bank inventory where shards could stack. This would allow us to sort shards instantly with the automated "transfer bag" function that already exists, and they could even make it way more expensive than normal inventory space. We already have something like that for collectables, so i dont know why we cant have it for shards.

To summarize this one major problem: even as a paying user you run into convenience problems of the kind normally used to get free players to pay for convenience.

There are more, smaller problems tho. Like how specific monster types disable entire categories of items unless you use specific modifiers on items, that are very rare to find and on top of that, again, most efficiently farmed in lower difficulties.
All of this results in gameplay that doesnt let you get to the real fun part. Instead you are often farming lower difficulties, which is in theory possible to completely-afk, but at the same time the game tries its best to make sure you dont afk. This is the case partly because you have to press G to go to the next wave every minute or so, and on top of that because there are bosses that "require" player-interaction the be killed, no matter the difficulty.

All of this results in a game that has horrible inventory space problems, bad inventory management options, no purchasable items to compensate these problems, while forcing you to farm lower, boring difficulties for a disturbing amount of time, just so you could eventually continue progressing, but at the same time does its best to prevent you from afking those boring farming sessions.

I really wanted to like the game, and up until you get to the huge problems in endgame it can be fun.. but i would strongly recommend everybody who read this comment to simply stay away. Let's all just hope Dungeon Defenders Awaken will not suffer from the same problems this one does.\t\t\t\t\t
