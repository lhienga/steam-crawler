\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI realize the game is still in alpha, but the game is playable enough and their design choices are clear enough to write a review.

For starters, they have improved upon the original quite a bit. Grouping is quite a bit easier and generally handled pretty well. The grind, while still pretty redundant (again--it's in alpha), is much less annoying. Pets are much easier to get, and leveling gear is more than just a money sink (kind of). Many of the game play aspects (such as pets) have much deeper design.

Now for the bad...

I do not know any of the Trendy developers, but I have been repeatedly disappointed with their business model. Time again, it seems they have put money over development. Now it could be they're hard up for cash and need the money for continued development, but my impression is otherwise. For instance, in the original, Trendy continued to release new DLC (that understandably cost money) where they improved the game mechanics, while the original levels stagnated without an update. A good deal of the DLC (mostly the holiday) even reused the old maps and only added minor, themed changes.

Then they announced DD2. Awesome. But a couple of months later, they release a "new" edition of DD1 with new and improved features that fixed a bunch of the issues with DD1. Great, right? Yep. Except rather than release it as an update, it was released as an entirely separate game that required a new purchase.

So now they're charging for the pre-alpha of a soon-to-be free-to-play game. That makes some sense. Gotta pay developers. Sure. I get it, and I'm willing to support it. But then you get into the game and you realize just how "freemium" they're making it. Sure, they say it won't affect gameplay, and I think I believe them. But it's really annoying that the costumes are absurdly expensive (the only ones available now are $50 for the set, I believe). Even worse, one of the item drops in-game is a character-specific chest that requires a key to open...or you can pay $1.50 for a single, random accessory. Did I mention you can get the key for free? Yep, but it'll take you a couple of days using in-game currency that you need to buy stuff that will affect the game play.

Oh, and their introducing a wait time. Pets take 8 hours to hatch. In general, I'm not opposed to something like that...but not on a PC game...maybe mobile. You can (currently...though I'm seeing costly upgrades all over this) only hatch one pet at a time, and your eggs rot after 48 hours. And who knows? After 8 hours, you most likely have a worthless lemon.

None of these are deal breakers by themselves, but put together, it paints a picture I'm not sure I want hanging on my wall. I love the game concept, and it really is fun to play. But the business and design choices leave me wondering if I want to invest any time (or money) in a game I know will be wrought with frustrations down the road.\t\t\t\t\t

I really liked Dungeon Defenders 1, but Dungeon Defenders 2 is by far NOTHING compared to the first one.

What I like about Dungeon Defenders 2 so far:
- Characters are grown up

What I don't like about Dungeon Defenders 2 is:
- Defense towers feel pathetic (doesn't mean they are, but how they feel if that makes sense)
- Max level is 25, where in DD1 it was like 80 or 70 or something
- Some defense towers are completely useless.
- Maps are VERY simple.
- No attack speed
- No endless waves/survival mode

There's some more things I like and don't like that I can't think of right now. Mostly don't like.

I heard that Dungeon Defenders 2 was SUPPOSE to be a MOBA. That would have been brilliant until "fans" complained that it's not Dungeon Defenders. I think Dungeon Defenders should have been made a MOBA with the strict tower defense as campaign coop multiplayer mode. I do enjoy completing waves and beating stages but the lack of PVP makes end game boring and...just boring. It's like playing Diablo 3 with less character customization, adding towers, both without pvp. Once you beat it, you delete it.


I understand that this is still in development. But ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, this game is starting to get REALLY, REALLY,,,really lame.\t\t\t\t\t
