\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has suffered from a rough development, and for a long time my review said not-recommended. It felt like the developers were just throwing random ideas together and hoping they would create a fun game, and for years, their efforts failed miserably. They completely rebuilt major parts of DD2 over and over again, often leaving it even worse than it was before.

But somewhere along the line, something changed. I honestly don't know if they finally got their act together and laid out a real development plan, or maybe they just finally got lucky and stumbled upon a formula that works. Whatever the cause, I can now say that Dungeon Defenders 2 provides an entertaining experience that is worth the purchase price. It's still got some rough edges, but devlopment is active and moving in the right direction.

For those not familiar with DD2, it's a tower-defense game, where you build a variety of towers each with unique abilities and strategically place them to fend of a stream of monsters who are trying to destroy your magic crystals. What helps DD2 stand out from most other tower-defense games is the fact that you also play a hero who can run around fighting the monsters, helping the towers deal with bosses or groups of more threatening enemies. And in RPG fashion you will level up and equip new weapons and armor, progressing through the campaign and extensive endgame.

If you enjoy strategic thinking, fast-paced action, and RPG progression, and you would like to see all three blended together, then I recommend giving Dungeon Defenders 2 your money.\t\t\t\t\t
