\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I play mainly on PS4 which uses the same patch version as on PC.
So don't look at that 0.7 hours. I do have hundreds.

This game is awesome. But in many ways, it has to be compared to Path of Exile.

The game has a constant evolution. We get large content patches multiple times a year.
And it is free.
Real-Money-Shop offers MOSTLY visual upgrades and Quality Of Life, very-optionnal stuff like more-storage.
There is game-money that you can buy. But the price is ridiculously high. It could help START a new toon. But it's absolutly NOT reliable to live on on the medium/long term.
Middle and end Game won't have any need for Real-Money-Shop.
That, for me, is NOT Pay To Win.

I gave 20$ for an advanced hero (Dryad).
I've been playing for hundreds of hours.
Worth it.


The game is way more sophisticated than it seems. It surprised me many times.
It sometime feels like one of those super-complex pay-to-win cellphone games. Yet, it's not.
The game sets ridiculous objectives in front of you.
And ridiculously complexe leveling/gear mecanics.
But as you move forward, it all becomes surprisingly reachable.
And rewarding once you do reach it.


It's an awesome time sink. A map takes 15 to 45 minutes (depending on many things) or it's kind of easy to take "small gaming sessions"... But you WILL want to come back for more.

I simply love it.
Yet it's clearly not "for everyone".
It CAN be quite punishing at times.
Or tedious.


Oh yeah. It's also a little more glitchy than it should. But that specific part COULD be specific to PS4 version.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played this game since alpha, and have been up and down on it while they made it better and tweaked some things.

I think the things that most people did not like through development have been addressed and fixed, or at least adjusted to make it more player friendly. I like the f2p now (i payed for it in aphla) because trendy will keep updating to keep players playing the game and to draw in new players with updates. Which means more content for all players.

The people who complain about cash grab methods really make no sense. I payed a price up front and got some inventory bags and some swag for characters but since then I have not had to pay anything else to enjoy the game and play it. If you have progressed in the game to the point where you are building all the characters on endgame then support it because you obviously enjoy it enough to sink time into it. Don't complain that you can't keep playing a free game for free.

Some things are unbalanced when the updates first arrive, but Trendy addresses it based on the community feedback and fixes it. It is tough to replicate dd1 with serverside only play but mods ruined dd1 and made it a totally unbalanced afk farm game with zero effort or progression required.

There are some things I would still like to see added but overall it is a really good game with a lot to give now for diversity in builds and the design to use status effects and map environment to stack damage means you can use many different ways to build and clear waves.

TL;DR:
Game is updated regularly with feedback from community

Game has been made to have diversity with recent updates

It is really f2p and not p2w, with optional cash purchases to speed progress up if your impatient\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've tried this game out 3 times now hoping that I'd like it, since I love DD1.

Everything that makes DD1 a good game feels missing.

The loot feels insignificant, and not much of it. You finish a level in DD1, and 70 items drop. You finish a map in DD2, and maybe 5 drop.

It seems like WAY too much effort went into making the character's personal abilities/attacks be useful. What made DD1 a great game was not personal character ability synergy, but TOWER synergy. Gas trap + strength debuff and then either darkness trap or multiple forms of damage. You had to find traps that were useful in many circumstances.

DD2 feels like you just pile on any ol' trap, and then deal with annoying enemies that are always out of range anyway.

DD1 scales like a mother. The auras for instance start as small balls and become HUGE half level balls. This means DD1s sense of progress is much more extreme.

Complex maps retracts from the experience imo, not make it better. The focus should be on towers/scaling/items. DD1 feels like a tower defense smart building game. DD2 feels like Orcs Must Die co-op only.

TL;DR: DD2 gets boring fast, and has the totally wrong focus.\t\t\t\t\t
