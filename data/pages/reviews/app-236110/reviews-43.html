All you will get to do is run around and attack things with your character, granted you'll get loot and money, but you get more xp when your towers kill stuff and you won't get to place a single one with these guys. At least though I understand them, cause that's my next step, recommending that IF you try this out, to become the 'master' type. Learn how the game works, learn every level well enough that you can play it entirley on your own. Then when you get idiots who don't know how to play (some don't even know how to place towers, which is actually helpful if they're that stupid) as long as you can build a solid defense they can hop about like idiots and all having more players does is increase the loot quality. Items and money seem to be exclusive to the players, so you don't have to worry about other players running around and picking up all the money and loot, what you see in the world isn't what they see and hence they can't loot steal. However build resources (green magic) is NOT exclusive to players and whoever picks them up first gets them. That can be annoying, especially with players who don't build.

Past the players you have the money mongering. So yeah, you can spend money to get better loot, essentially proof they know they stretched the grind beyond the point of being rewarding and making it tedious to make you willing to spend money just to make it end. They also locked the classes and their cosmetics up. Now cosmetics I'm used to, and a few of them are pretty cool. There are nearly a dozen classes though and I honestly don't know how many you get when you're a free player. Again, I bought into this before it was F2P, so when it went F2P they gave me a bunch of in game currency, free back pack slots and some cosmetics of my choosing. I have four classes, luckily the only four I"m interested in, but all the classes are rather interesting so I can see someone wanting them all. Also, I said backpacks, yes you have limited inventory and it can be a pain to go through the inventory. If I had fewer back packs than I do, it would be more annoying. Good news though, XP is shared between your characters! The character you play as gains XP much faster than your unused characters but hey, it's nice. Also inventory is shared between characters, so if you get a nice sword for your knight, no tedious mailing items between characters or banking it between them, with the press of an "F" key (F1-F4) you instantly change characters and can equip the item.

Honestly, over all, it's a pretty fun game. Though I put probably triple the hours into the first game, I also had a friend to play that with and I have no one to play this with. The drag is, cosmetics are pretty awesome, which I don't usually say about games, and if I could grind for them I would, but they are real money only which sucks because getting 'gear' just isn't worth the work. The further into the game you go the more vicious the levels become to the point of near impossible, so I just play the first five levels over and over, they're wonderfully desgined and while challenging don't present so many pathways that the level can't be managed. Since it is free, hey give it a shot, it actually is a pretty good 3rd person action TD hybrid game. Just beware that the devs obviously decided they were more interested in micro transactions that just making a good game, which is true for virtually all F2P titles I suppose, so you'll run into design choices that pressure you to spend money to avoid almost punishing game mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was a fan of Dungeon Defenders I and found its artistry really beatiful. Gameplay needed a bit of work, but overall it was a great experience, just needed more people.

DD2 however feels like a half-done game that was forced onto interns to complete.

Match making isn't very good, finding people to play with is incredibly difficult in-game.

Item drops are almost all junk, with no method of sorting. Have to sort through 30 items after every map and compare
to your gear sets.

Game randomly skips whenever moving around map, I have high PC specs and haven't had that issue in any other game.

Art style is cartoonish, but bland. Doesn't have any of that high-contrast lightful beauty DD1 had. Its unique, but isn't much to look at.

No multiplayer on the same system. This kills the experience with friends. Expected it'd be nice to get a few 360 controllers and level up together with a few friends like we did in DD1.

This is the only game out of my extensive library I'd actually like a refund for after waiting well over a year expecting a not yet complete, but decent game, but getting a college art project.

Overall, 3/10, there was an attempt, but this entire game has went the direction of 'open testing' to make money off of an uncomplete product that will never be finished and instead woud rather nickle and dime you to get perks that were available in the first game for free.\t\t\t\t\t
