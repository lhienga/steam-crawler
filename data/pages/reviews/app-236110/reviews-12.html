\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI put a lot of time into the original Dungeon Defenders and was quite happy to hear about the sequel. I gave it a play back when it was first released I believe, it didn't really grab me so I decided to let it sit and come back when the game got its legs.

So right off the bat, this game is messy. The UI is hard to navigate and the game insists on you being stuck in multiplayer lobbies or at the least logging on to their servers. This makes it annoying as, to play a solo game, you still have to create a 'private' game. For some stages this is bizarrely easier to do than on others, as some give you the option to simply 'play solo' while the rest require you to go through several steps before creating a private game. There are also frustrating parts about the inventory and how things are sorted and displayed.

But let's get into the meat of the game. It starts you out with an unskippable, rather long tutorial. It does teach you some important aspects, so that's not the worst part. Then it allows you to play through the campaign while steadily adding in new characters and mechanics. This part is rather good, I enjoyed the campaign! However it is very short, only consisting of 5-8 levels. After that, the game just throws you to the wolves. This is where things take a dark turn.

You are given no guidance as to what to do next and the ugly UI problems rear their head again. None of the stages are listed in any order, or give you a sense of difficulty. You are very likely to enter a stage (which is a lengthy process, especially as you may want to set up ALL your defenses at the start) where the enemy completely overpowers you, requiring you to quit and try another stage.

In addition to this is how difficulty is seemingly based on other factors I couldn't figure out. Playing through one of the after campaign stages, I made it to the final wave with only a little trouble. But, there was some event running at the time where a special miniboss could appear that would probably drop some special loot. Fine idea, but this miniboss was tuned for, seemingly, much higher level players and proceeded to completely demolish all my defenses. Several tries later and I was not even able to damage this boss. I gave up, proceeded to go through the main campaign again on hard mode to power up. Which I did, probably doubling my heroes power and defenses strength. I came back to the exact same level, only for the very first wave to absolutely crush my defenses in a matter of seconds. I have no idea why as I had no trouble with this wave earlier.

This is where I gave up. I could not find a way to progress in this game that was enjoyable. Beyond trying to power up in the 'hard campaign', which did in fact have a limit on the quality of equipment you could receive, the only other option I had was to throw attempts at one of the MANY strange challenge modes. Onslaught, chaos, expeditions... There were too many and there was no explanation to what they entailed or how challenging they would be.

To be fair, the game plays 'ok'. Though the game makes it mandatory to have four heroes and swap between them in order build your defenses. This sounds fine on paper, but it makes things much more complicated than they need to be. Instead of learning a handful of towers, you have to learn up to 16. In addition to this is each heroes special abilities, of which they usually have 4 each. And again, the UI problems return as the only way to find out a vague description of the towers and heroes abilities is to try to create a new hero and read the descriptions there. While I enjoy the versatility of having multiple heroes and towers, this system is overwhelming and just makes the game complicated as there are far too many towers/structures to sort through and build, adding on to how the game seems to want to push this as a mandatory mechanic. This is to say nothing of how each hero has roughly 8 gear slots you will need to mess with.

This game is touted as a multiplayer game, however I admit I had no interest in this outside of playing with friends. Joining up with a random group is going to lead to very little but 'one person builds all the defenses (since build capacity is shared), the rest just play a passable action RPG' due to how each player has 4 heroes and a laundry list of towers, with gear leading to one of the players being the 'best builder'. That one person will have all the tools they need and will be better at it than the rest.

In conclusion, this game has potential but is just messy and extremely unfriendly to the new player. The difficulty is confusing and players are left directionless. It's time consuming and progression is unguided. I sincerely doubt that at this point things are going to improve, the game has been out for a while now. If you paid for this early on, it may be worth playing through the campaign and using the currency given to you to buy some heroes and mess around. If you are looking to start now, I feel you'll get very little out of it.\t\t\t\t\t
