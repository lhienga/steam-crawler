\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*** Protean Shift Update ***

Ok so I finally had a chance to play the new content update and please believe me when I say this was a MASSIVE update and overhaul. The protean shift update fundamentally changed the way the game is played and in my opinion for the better. I have been reading a lot fo posts from some long time players that are abandoning the game and writing negative reviews after this update. I wanted to take a momoent and comment.

Are their some large issues with this update, of course, but look at how large the update was. If you have been with DD and Trendy for awhile you will understand that this is typically how it goes and Trendy DOES listen and fix the issues it just takes time.

The biggest complaint I see right now is around inventory and people having to pay for vault space. Trendy has already stated they are looking at making inventory management better and I believe them when they say it. My inveotnory is a NIGHTMARE right now.

MOD system:
MOD managmenet is beyond a nightmare, there is no easy way for me to see what mods I've collected or what level those mods are without looking at EACH AND EVERY item individually since there is no rhym or reason to which MODs are on which piece of gear its just RNG. I'm forced to SPEND hours doing inventory managment solely for the sake of sorting through hundreds of mod drops to make sure I'm not losing an upgrade on an existing MOD or missing a new mod that may have dropped.

The other issue with mods is I don't feel they should be attached to gear. There is one HUGE reasons for this. The cost for moving MODs between equipment is high and requires many hours of game play to get the required materials when you start talking aobut chaos 7 gear and above. I can't tell you how many times I found better gear to use but it had terrible MODS on it and there was no easy way for for me to take the mods I already collected on my current gear and transfer them to better gear without spending some key resources to perform the transfer and permanently destroying my original piece of gear. This was a fundamental design flaw in my opinoin. I hope that trendy takes a close look at this and comes up with a better mechanism for moving MODs around.

I still LOVE the MOD system. This is just another example of where Trendy needs to make some changes to what is otherwise a fantastic addition to the game. I believe they will get it right. We just need to provide them with some positive critical feedback that will alow them to design the system in a way that is enjoyable to play and does nto force players to spend money to enjoy it (vault space etc.)

The new change to "Trials" is great! The ability ot select which maps you play is HUGE. This was a much needed update Thank you Trendy!

Overall I love the update and ask that long time players be patient with Trendy there is a ton of feedback and a lot of game mechanics to look at here and make better. I call on the community to continue to provide quality feedback good or bad and be patient with Trendy while they work through the mountian of comments that inevidably come with an update of this size.

I have been playing dungeon defenders since the original game..... I have read some of the latest reviews and all I must say is take them with grains of salt. I’m not saying they are incorrect some "Features" of the latest update are frustrating but its the first release we need to give Trendy time to adjust it based on player feedback as is the case when any new system is introduced into a game.
The new system while frustrating in its first iteration makes replay ability better than previous versions or end game systems. Many of the negative reviews deal with not being able to complete a map of highest level in a few quick minutes…. That was never the intent of the game. The new lane modifier system in onslaught mode makes each and every map unique because the setup of defense changes based on those modifiers. IT also causes players to build out their decks (another complaint I have seen) as you never know which set of modifiers you are going to get on a map and may need defenses from multiple heroes to complete the map. IMO this makes for a better game experience as you get to learn and play more heroes and defenses. It makes each ancient power climb unique and worth the true reward given on reset.
Trendy is community friendly and takes player feedback seriously. I am confident some of the more irritating aspects of the new expansion will be worked out soon in fact Trendy has already mentioned it with regards to the must win all maps or start over … which is the biggest frustration right now with the new mode https://dungeondefenders.com/2/topic/150977/3-maps-without-save-in-onslaught-needs-to-go?scrollTo=1306849&page=3 “.. We've got a replay system we're working on (with maybe a little bit more), just takes time to implement, test, and then push. Never fear, if everything goes as planned, it is on its way! :) ..”
My point here is I understand the other reviewers frustration with the latest update it fundamentally changes the play aspects of the game but IMO it did so for the better and prolongs end game play for many players.
If you like tower defense and enjoy a truly free to play and free to win game…. I HIGHLY recommend you download and play this game.\t\t\t\t\t

The reason I played is because I enjoy games with a sense of progress, usually through acquiring loot and levels, that can be played quickly and without a large time commitment per play session. Up until the new expansion, progress meant maxing out gear in chaos 7 trials and acquiring ascension levels. They put a cap on how many ascension points you could spend per skill tree node, but that was fine with me. It didn't impact play much, and it meant I could focus on one hero per class, rather than having a hero for each and every tower I use.

Before the expansion, you would play through the campaign, fight your way up through the trials and spend the remainder of your playtime mostly in chaos 7. The climb was painful, but in the end, you become overpowered for the content and waiting for new chaos trials (or incursions, though I don't much care for those). I could set up and complete a chaos 7 map in roughly 8 minutes. I could gain 25-30 ascension levels in a good day of play (8+ hours). The progress wasn't much, since ascension level spending was capped, but it was nice to feel overpowered for a bit while waiting for new content.

I don't know that adding new chaos trials would've been better than what Trendy came up with, but I do know I'd still want to play the game. Progression in the new expansion means going through the new Onslaught challenge and using the Prestige system. You can choose to avoid the Prestige system, which many do, but then you're not really progressing. You can only go so far in the infuriating and frustrating game mode that is Onslaught right now.

So progress is no longer about acquiring gear or ascension levels. It's all about resetting. It's all about doing it over. And over. And over again. It's all suffering, from start to reset. At least until you've reset 50 times, which is the cap. The top players will manage this in roughly 1,250 hours (being generous), while the average player can look forward to considerably more. I doubt they'd come close to 50 resets before quitting in frustration though.

The rewards for resetting are too small, imo, and mostly inconsequential. The biggest reward, raising the ascension spending caps, won't matter until you're done with the Prestige system and are back to grinding out ascension levels. You'll be able to feel overpowered again once you reach ascension level 3000, I suppose. Who will honestly make it that far? That's thousands of hours in a frustrating system.

What makes it frustrating? Aside from all the bugs and disconnects, the bulk of Onslaught floors require you to beat multiple maps to move on to the next floor. It takes a lot of time, patience and scrutiny to set up a map now (15-20 mins), and a single mistake means failure. There are no do-overs. Sorry, no mulligans. If you fail once, you're back to the beginning of the floor. It doesn't matter if it took you over an hour to get to the third map of a floor, and a bugged hex thrower blew up your core. Sorry, start over.

"Sorry, start over." is the running theme of this expansion.

That's fun, right?
