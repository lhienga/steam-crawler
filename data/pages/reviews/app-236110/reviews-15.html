Easy to understand at first
Controls aren't too bad
Cons: Game gets really complex
The game becoms the grinding (spending hours at a time to get something) game
You have to clear inventory about 1-2 matche(s)
There are way too many things for someone starting to look at
Game updates just make you more and more confused
The game wants you to spend money even though it's "Free"
No easy way to get character tokens

Overall, the game is pretty bad but, it's free so...you can't complain unless you bought the early access and got ripped of.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI might change my review at some point. However at the moment I can not recommend DD2.

In general the game is decent. Had some fun stuff in it that I generally enjoyed. I played the game for 277 hours for a reason. I have never been one to enjoy the experience of leveling so being able to get 4 characters leveled at once was amazing to me. Being able to carry my own alternate characters through content to get them leveled is even better in my opinion. Running with friends and playing around figuring out the levels and what is needed where was something I enjoyed.

Now for the negatives. The chaos mode enemies annoyed me, not all of them but most (specifically the geodes and EMPorks mostly) did. I got through that though. Then the Trials started, I participated initially and then realized I hated it. It literally forced me to go through all the Chaos modes again, while restricting me farther with special things I had to accomplish. BTW chaos mode enemies basically restricted what you could or could not use effectively. So you had to learn what the enemies were for a difficulty, figure out what they restricted or negated of your defenses, swapped your gear all around to accomadate the new enemies and figure out a completely different build with the limited towers you could use. Chaos mode was already annoying me, then they compounded it with the Trials. People were already telling them that the chaos mode enemies needed tweaks at the least before they even released the game, because the enemies take the fun out of playing with all the heroes you have. Being limited to the towers of half to a quarter of the heroes to complete a level is just stupid. And then having to figure it out 7x as you gear up, and then negating ALL of that to make people go do the trials with even more restrictions... To note, it probably wouldn't be as bad in the Trials if you could keep the gear you grinded for 200-300 hours for to accomplish it quickly and efficiently but Trials forces your gear and even Mastery levels down to what they want you to do it as... I.E. you level up to cap and then are able to attain extra levels to throw bonus points into your stats, those bonus points are reduced/gone for trials.

Overall I've played the game quite a bit. I enjoyed quite a bit of the time I spent doing so. In the end, the dev team was not listening to the playerbase and did some stuff that I disagreed with. And well here we are. To be completely honest, if you do try it, you will likely enjoy the game initially. You might well enjoy it more than I did. However unless you want to sink a lot of time and effort into the game it might not be worth it in the long run.

In it's current state I will not recommend this game. Obviously will be checking back to see if it improves, and if it does I will change the review. After all I sunk 1200+ hours into DD1. And 277 hours into this game. I really do hope they improve.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI likeable free to play game that mixes tower defense with 3rd person slashing and some rpg elements like stats, loot and stat points.

Give it a try, it's free.

You start the game with 4 different characters, each character has 4 battle skills and 4 buildings to build. There is a campaign, then the so called Trials, then Incursions, also mastery stages and then the Onslaught. There are a lot of things to do and you can play up to 4 people, which makes it easier to clear stuff since you have more characters on the field to do damage.

There is of course a shop and a medal/gem system going on as in all free games. Pretty much everything in the game can be bought with the in-game earned Defender Medals, but you can also spend the real currency Gems to buy the stuff without the effort. Some people don't like systems like thi but I thik it makes it fair. There are characters to buy, costumes and cosmetics, shard packs, special pets and so on. If you want. I've bought 2 characters with real money and unlocked 2 with in game currency, there are of course daily and weekly missions you can compete to earn more medals than t he ones from the maps.

It's free, so try it!\t\t\t\t\t

New Players view:
Ive played this game since early access, checking in on the game every other month and watching the system take on many shapes. The game itself is okay, the concept of tower defense mixed with rpg elements will be interesting for new players. With most "Free to Play" games their is a cash shop which the players spend money. However the company follows a great cash shop system that doesnt lock content to non paying players.

DD1 view:
Although the game is semi balanced to not allow AFK builds the game lacks the creativity and fun compared to DD1. With the challenging special mobs they have implemented which straight up makes some builders just not viable at all. No crazy map covering aura's from monk and no 0.02 fire rate on towers. In my opinion they should have just kept the old system with the following changes:

- Updated graphics
- New builders
- Different maps
- Special Mobs (like the ones they are implementing now)

I thought the item set effect was great from the previous game. Their is nothing wrong with keeping the same system as before to a sequel. AFKing and coming back to a floor of loot is what made the game special. Aiming for higher and higher stats and unique pets/weapons was a great carrot on a stick. The challenge in Nightmare Mode was great, really ramped the difficulty and the reward of mystical gear was awesome.

As a old DD1 defender I'am sorry, but you will not find what you are seeking for in DD2.\t\t\t\t\t
