\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGiven that the game is free for people to play and you get the whole experience without needing to pay for anything, the game is pretty alright.

That said, once you've invested yourself into the game, there is a lot of glaring issues that quickly stand out.

For the sake of saving time, i'll just list the issues off in short formfactor:

-$300+ worth of DLC.

-Newly released heros are $15 per, at base.

-The new hero designs are odd as they encurage you to stay on your builder, but the end game NEEDS your DPS
character or there is no point in trying.

-Speaking of end game, there is hardly any. I hit it in less than a week, and that's just due to grinding and not knowing the systems. I could've gotten there in 3 days if I wanted to.

-The scaling nature of stats in this game do well in giving the player a sense of progression, but simply take any semblence of skill out of the game. Nightmare 4 is hard as hell at first, but get your toxic shot bow and **boom**, it becomes a joke of a game.

-The tone of the game at the start makes it seem as if the game is taking itself more seriously, but that is quickly turned around by the "bubblegum" art style, which is good and bad in it's own right.

-The story mode is something akin to the original "Titanfall" and I have no idea what is going on. For all I know, a bad guy manages to smash the cyrstals that hold "The Old Ones" back and now you have to kill them, again. This game is clearly not relying on the story to sell, but more on gameplay.

-Balancing is fine from what I can tell. Nearly every hero is great in their own way, but holy crap are some heros downright needed. The archer is a must or a bust for high end gameplay.

-With regards to leveling, it's basically the same thing as end game. Your hero never really changes significantly. You just become bigger and deal more damage late game. There is very few ways you can make a build.

-Most of the complexity comes through managing the four heros you can have at a time, which makes you the builder and dps guy, reducing the need for other players. So unless you make a lobby yourself, you often make all the defences yourself and have someone else just dps the siege engines when they come along. This ends up making this werid multiplayer where people become their own island, but need other DPS people to deal with one ad. It's poor gameplay, at least with regards to teamplay mechanics.

Even though this game has many gameplay and fundemental flaws, it's a decent game to play for 30 minutes and come back to in a month.
Do you want a game you can sink hundreds of hours into to learn all of the deep stratagies? Look somewhere else.
Want a game that feels like it would fit in on a moblie phone that you can occasionally play when you have nothing else to do? This game is perfect then!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDD2 is a very polished game with some very flawed fundamentals. It's not really a tower defense game. More of a baracade game, with shooting. But not really fun shooting. Most levels boil down to standing in the same spot the entire level and with four huntresses hosing down their lanes. I want to like this game so bad. But honestly, it's just boring. DD1 was much more fun.
Allowing players to hot swap heroes seemed like a good idea. It's not. The dps heroes are too powerful.
Here is a short breakdown of what you will experience:
Start a level and use your builder to place initial baracades and towers. This part is fun. Probably the only fun part.
After spending all your money, switch to the huntress and position yourself where you can see the most lanes.
Level begins - Hold down the left mouse button.
Level ends - Make a sweep of the level, heal some towers, lay some more towers, upgrade others. (again, somewhat fun)
Level begins - hold down the left mouse button.
After you've beaten the level, the game has automatically picked up the prodigious amount of loot that dropped. Now its time to sort through all that crap like its a sale at the thrift store and you're hoping to find at least one thing of value. Chances are, you wont. But that wont save you from having to sort through it all for every character you own. Its as bad as doing your taxes, I kid you not. I cannot express just how much crap gear you need to sort through. It's awful.

TLDR: Instead of this, play Orcs must die 2, or even OMD unchained. Also, Kingdom Rush does TD way better than this. This game wants you to grind your life away. Don't do it.\t\t\t\t\t
