\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I've written two other reviews, and I'm coming back to write a 3rd. What can I say? I bought the first Dungeon Defenders on my Playstation 3, loved it, so I purchased it on my pc with all of the DLC. One of my all time favorite games, this one had a lot to live up to. So does it? Heck yeah it does! I purchased the game on Early Access, and let me just say that this is Early Access done right! With so many dev teams abusing the Early Access platform on steam, this definetly gives me hope for Early Access in general. The developers respond to feedback and even allow players to vote for changes to the game on the launcher. Early Adopters were rewarded with $30 of in-game currency as well as a unique cosmetic for each of the 4 heroes currently available in the game (although I do wish the items were a little flashier for bragging purposes :D) I would say the campaign is a bit shorter than the original, and develops the story very little. I would assume that expansions are on their way, as I cannot stress enough how little the plot progresses, so if you're looking for a full story, you won't find it yet.

But how's the gameplay? The tower defense is amazing, although there are many tower's that haven't returned or have been altered significantly. But to make up for this, there are a few new towers. Hero spells have also been changed, and a whole new type of energy is used for them, which frees you up. You don't have to choose between using hero abilities, or getting that tower upgrade you desperately need. The action element of the game definetly feels nice, but you won't find yourself being able to completely ditch towers like you could with a maxed barb or huntress in the first Dungeon Defenders. You could argue that this is actually a plus though.

Should you play it? F2P? YES! What are you waiting for!? P2P? YES! The Devs deserve the support! But I recommend trying it F2P first, just to make sure it's your thing.\t\t\t\t\t
