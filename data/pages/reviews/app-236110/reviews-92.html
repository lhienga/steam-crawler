\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's bland, it's boring, it feels sluggish and feels a lot more like a rip-off of dungeon defenders rather than a sequel.

Yeah, yeah. Beta beta blah...

Remember in Dungeon Defenders 1, before the DLC flood gates were open? You had to work your way up the levels, true, but the rewards would feel like they had an impact.

Got your mage, barely got the fireball towers, they feel like they have an impact.
Lightning tower? Looks like it's makingan effort.

Then that freaking OP sniper tower, after all that work... It's barely useful, but it's satisfying.

Sequel? You get one less tower, gear still looks the same, you have three currencies now...
I stopped liking DD1 after the flood, but this game I can't bring myself to load again. It's just bad.

... And WTF is up with having to go back to a root menu when you level up, if the playable missions seem to be level locked anyway? That's just forcing us to jump through hoops.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a Pre-Alpha game, there's so much to do in Dungeon Defenders II. With the success of the original, I know Trendy will give us an awesome game especially since they're looking at fan feedback for balancing, etc. Hopefully, their team's morale is also a lot higher than what it was during an incident with their former president a few years ago. Anyway, currently, my hero deck consists of a huntress, apprentice and squire.

Unlike some players, the game can definitely be enjoyable as a single player game. I've had no issues in the later half of the game with my trio of characters. As the more difficult your characters become, specific gearing becomes a necessity to ease the stress of completing a map. My squire is built as a defense tank. All items consist of adding defense health to his defenses, but I only have him set spike blockades. My huntress is used as a damage dealer. I don't really use her traps other than for utility, so I decided to increase her own damage instead. My apprentice's defenses are the damage dealers and I found myself mowing down enemies effectively.

Each character has a set of passives you can upgrade as you level, so there's a variety of builds you can work with along with item passives as well. Items come in a variety of tiers like many RPGs such as Diablo with legendaries being the best items you can get. Each item can be upgraded a certain amount of times and can add to improve your character's stats.

The game will drop a lot of loot..Like a lot even in single player. You'll feel like a rich snob and become very picky about which items you pick up. The good thing is if you regret not picking anything up, there's a temporary item box that allows you to get items from previous maps you've done. This allows for selling and upgrading items without having to fill up all your item boxes. The chances of higher quality item drops increases if you're in a group.

Leveling isn't much of a grind either. It feels just right. It might take a while in a pug map, because people will take forever to set up their defenses. However, as a single player game, as you get used to the maps, you will set up your defenses quickly. As stated above, you can definitely play this game solo even in harder difficulties. As this is an early access review however, it will be difficult to see how this review scales as the game adds content, but right now, it's highly enjoyable. I recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWas a big fan of Dungeon Defenders, and I have been awaiting DD2 with much anticipation.

I definitely felt that there was more emphasis on player defense as opposed to the more tower focused DD1. Not that towers aren't important, but (so far) I haven't felt confident enough in a defense to let it handle it's lane while I focus on another, I feel the need to keep tabs on all lanes to make sure nothing gets through. That may be because it's early on, I'm not sure yet. Personally, I like taking part in the defense, some survival games in DD1 got dull after you upgraded, because the defenses largely took care of everything.

One thing you'll notice straight off is that there are few towers for each hero, and more abilities than before. Most of the abilities feel strong and useful, though so far I find the Squire's to be the least helpful. Huntress has some very strong damage abilities, and the Apprentice has a great control in Tornado. You'll notice some towers are missing now, since there are fewer tower slots, but staples like the Ballista, Fire Tower, and Lightning Aura are still there.

Another change is the mana system. You have two bars, blue and green, for abilites and towers, respectively. Corresponding mana crystals drop from mobs, and chests give green. Honestly, I don't see much reason for abilities to have anything except cooldowns. I've only rarely used abilities so often that I ran out of mana, and then it only took a few seconds to run over to pick some up. Plus, some armor regenerates the mana, so at higher levels it probably wont be much of an issue. I'd rather have the old mana system in place, but I'll touch on that below.

As for negative things, it's hard to say since the game is in pre-alpha, but two things have really been annoying me so far.

First, all melee attacks move the heroes forward a significant amount. This means you can't backpedal and attack, but instead always charge into a group. It's really just personal preference, but I hate it when a game moves for me, or adjusts aim (which also occurs for ranged attacks, but that might be able to be turned off).

Second, it's not as satisfying to kill mobs anymore. In DD1, mobs died in an explosion of colorful mana crystals, each one worth a different amount. Now, some mobs drop crystals, and there are only two colors, blue and green. They don't roll around anymore, and blend into the scenery since they lack the bright colors of DD1. Again this is more personal than an issue, but it takes a lot of the fun of seeing piles of mana everywhere, and rushing to collect it before it disappeared, or fell off the edge of the map.

I still recommend the game, for a pre-alpha it looks and plays great, but it's obviously missing a lot of content since it's so early. The final game will also be F2P, so if you'd rather not pay for the early access, then just wait awhile.\t\t\t\t\t

- runs smoothly most of the time considering it's still pre-alpha

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played some 500+ hours in DD1, and while that isn't a ton of time, I'd like to think that I am a fan of the games. I've only played upwards of 25 hours in DD2 and to be honest, I couldn't be more pleased so far. That said, judging this game now would be ridiculous, it's in PRE-ALPHA, which translate to this not finished game, they will add more things and the game will continue to improve. So just wait a bit, taking a pre-alpha game at face value would be silly. So a word of advice from a random gamer, if you're on the fence about this game wait until it's released to give it a chance.

Pros of the game:
-Great graphics
-More levels
-New enemy types/ bosses
-New abilities
-Specs
-The Squire found pants.

The cons:
-Must be online to play. Who knows though, that may change.
-Some of the spec options are "Meh" at best.
-You're unable to out level an area, If I'm 16 in a 10-15 zone, I'm scaled down as to not over power the level.
-Increasing the range of your defences can be a pain as it is not a stat, you find it on gear.

All in all this is a wonderful foundation for a game, As they add more into the game, I'm sure it will continue improve and it will be a worthy successor to DD1. As for now, it's one hell of a fun game.\t\t\t\t\t
