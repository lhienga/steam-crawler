\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDungeon Defenders 2 is far from a horrible game, but it just doesn't capture the magic of the first.

For what it is, Dungeon Defenders 2 is an alright game. The free to play mechanic and loads of microtransactions and sub currencies add a lay of annoying complexity to an otherwise excessively streamlined experience.

If you're coming to Dungeon Defenders 2 after playing Dungeon Defenders 1 be prepared to rethink some core mechanics. For one, gone is the diablo-style equip screen, and in its place is a boring horizontal grid. Items are stored in bags, look very similar to one aother, and lack in general variety.

Dungeon Defenders 1 had charm and loads of it. Dungeon Defenders 2 feels like its trying to be charming (like that annoying friend you had in high school who tried really hard to be cool. They wore all the cool clothes and listened to the trendy music but they always looked and acted a bit odd).

I don't recommend getting this. Play the original. Even though the endgame is pretty terrible, the ride to get there is a lot more fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA serious disappointment if you've already experienced the glory of Dungeon Defenders 1. DD1 has a fun progression -- your journey into nightmare and up through the eternia shards was well paced, and you be completing the entire task with your friends effortlessly. I followed the news posts, waited anxiously for release, and was highly excited to try it when I could finally play it. However, I was disappointed at almost every turn. The menus were bad, localized coop was nowhere to be seen, the new "tavern" was a confusing mess, and it required a much more active participation with your hero without sufficient direction.

Even with dwindling community, Dungeon Defenders 1 is still a thoroughly enjoying and relaxing game. However, Dungeon Defenders 2 is not fans who supported the studio through countless DD1 paid dlc's was hoping for. They choose a too large of scope, in their changes to the towers, and sacrificed quality in many areas of the game to compensate. I have no hatred or resentment towards the studio and will happily consider future games of theirs, but I just cant in good conscious recommend anyone waste their time on this particular product.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a pretty interesting difficulty curve after you've finished the campaign. There aren't any real play-to-win that you can't get without just playing for a few days.

I've played this game on and off since it's release after playing DD1 for a pretty large number of hours. It takes simply playing the game for awhile to pick up some best practices so that you can work with the rest of your team. If someone else is building, upgrade their primary defences. If you have a question about what to do just ask.

After campaign, get into the Chaos levels and just follow around the people that know what they're doing. The viability of every character is a major improvement in the more recent upgrades so just play what you think you'll enjoy.

There are a lot of guides around in terms of how to build characters and such, your first real task is just getting to level 50. Great chance to learn the game. Good luck.\t\t\t\t\t
