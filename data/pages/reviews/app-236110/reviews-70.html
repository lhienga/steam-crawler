\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLets try to make this quick. I have played DD but not DDE.
This review is also written from Pre-Alpha. (7-30-2015, [m-dd-yyyy])
Also I have only play Monk lvl 25 and Apprentice (Mage) lvl 15~
Pros:
+Great gameplay, smooth and good flowing combat feel
+Matchmaking better than it was in DD1
+Funny story line, Most NPCs even though 1 liners right now I already feel like they have some sort of tale to tell
+Loot loot loot. (sometimes too much?)
+Keeps the cartoon style of DD1 but slim and tall instead of short and stalky
Bias+ I enjoy the pet system of having to hatch your pet from an egg, feels more of a pet than equipment
Cons:
-Still early access ofc
-Premium currency? even if it isn't P2W so far (It is looking like $1 = 100 runes and cheapest skins are 400, 800, and 1600 runes so skins are not extremely expensive and a nice way to support the devs)
-Once you get about the last 5 campaign lvls its hard to find people playing them in MM
-You have to finish campaign before dailies/monthly (this is only con because its hard to find people for last 5 missions)

Conclusion:
Buy this game if you're interested and have the money. If it is on sale just buy it you'll enjoy at least a few hrs of play.

This game is still in Pre-Alpha which for most games out there it looks pretty much done. For full price of $24.99 It is worth the 11hrs I've played so far. (The week I write this it is on mid-week madness sale of $15) The only bugs I've really had to deal with in my hrs of play is the Drakins will get stuck on small senery and they update almost every time I want to play :P
Edit: After about 20-30 minutes of posting this review I had a DEV! comment. A++ they're asking players how the game is and getting our feedback to know how to make it better. I can no longer recomend this game more now. If you enjoy any kinds of games that are cartoony and/or dungeony and/or deffending Buy this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate 2/19/2020:

I still wouldn't recommend this game, but it is at least playable now. Having played both DD1 and DD2, I vastly prefer DD1 to DD2. DD2 feels like it should be a better game, but it just isn't. Everything is set up like a pay to win mobile game, constantly nagging you to buy things with real money. If you go in knowing it's basically a F2P mobile game on a PC, then you might enjoy it. I did not.

Compared to DD1, there are some things that were implemented cool. The hotswapping characters without going to a "jukebox" was a great addition. The character variety and different play styles were awesome. Being able to earn the characters by playing rather than having to purchase them with real money as DLC was a fantastic change. However, the 'fun factor' just wasn't there. I couldn't bring myself to even finish the story mode. I went back to DD1 instead. I didn't quit playing the game because it was bad, it just wasn't as fun as other games, and I didn't feel like being hounded constantly about MTX.

The first DD was a blast, but this pre-alpha is little more than a cash grab. Would not have bought the game until it was playable if I knew it would be unplayable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR If you've never played Dungeon Defenders and the trailers, pictures, and description looks interesting to you, you're the most likely person to enjoy the game. If you're coming from the first game, and are expecting a sequel akin to the first game, then you may be disappointed.

It's hard to leave a good or bad review on this game, as it succeeds at what it's trying to be, while claiming to be something it's not. This isn't a sequel to Dungeon Defenders, but rather a re-imagining claiming to be a sequel. On the basis of it being a sequel to the first game - I wouldn't recommend it. Many people may enjoy this game, including people who enjoyed the first game, but it's nothing like the first game, and to a veteran DD1 player who invested hundreds of hours into the game and all of its DLCs, earning almost every single trophy and achievement it's.. disappointing, however that doesn't outright make it a bad game on its own merit.

I believe Trendy wanted to create a new game of a different kind, but being an indie development studio who's peak player numbers for the original game was a mere 12,000 in its entire lifespan, they couldn't afford a flop. They built momentum with the first game, and they wanted to ride that momentum by creating a sequel - meanwhile using it as an opportunity to implement their new ideas. This sadly led to a sequel that isn't true to its roots, one that doesn't capture the spirit of the first game and can't really be labelled a sequel. They had the right idea when they decided to separate Dungeon Defenders Eternity (when that was still a thing) from the original game, rather than calling it a "2", but when that game flopped they learned the wrong lesson and dropped that idea entirely, opting for a direct sequel instead.

To be frank, this isn't anything new. This is a very common occurrence in the game industry. The Halo franchise is a prime example of this, but due to the popularity of Halo and the sheer numbers of its community, it received a lot more scrutiny than Dungeon Defenders. Halo 4 was treated as a bastardisation of Halo, even though it wasn't truly a bad game in the sense of having core problems with its gameplay or its design, it simply didn't live up to the expectations of what a Halo game "should" be. It was a bad Halo game, but not a bad game, and I enjoyed it jumping in blind. I feel the same way about Dungeon Defenders 2.

Many features have been put in a blender together with new components, and many have been outright removed, however there are also a lot of new features that are pretty neat, but they never get a chance to shine under the shadow cast by the expectations coming from the first game. The mechanics of the game have been altered too much for me to really enjoy it as a sequel.

The fact that it's a free to play game with in-game currency with a centralised lobby system and "communities" akin to clans, just goes to show that this an entirely new concept hiding behind the skin of Dungeon Defenders, and not truly a continuation of the first game - not a sequel.

Nevertheless I wish all the best for Trendy, as I owe them the countless of hours of fun, the meeting of new friends whom I still talk to today, and the wholesome escape from reality that the first game provided.. but this isn't a game for me. Maybe one day, letting go of the past, I may enjoy this game, but in my heart there's only one Dungeon Defenders, the original one. This.. is something different.\t\t\t\t\t
