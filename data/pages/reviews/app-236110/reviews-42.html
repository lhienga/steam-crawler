If you're looking for DD1, play DD1.
There are a lot of ups and downs on the ride with Chromatic over the years, but overall, I find myself always coming back.

Pros:
Seemingly endless amount of grind and progression to be had.
I like the AP & Shard Progression addition vs the first Dungeon Defenders.
The game is heaps better looking than DD1.
Relatively easy to pick up and lean into the franchise.
Multiplayer is always fun and full of helpful people who are always willing to give pointers.
The community in general is positive, aside the one that only bashes on the game.
The MODS and elemental combinations are a nice addition to have.
The amount of love and updates recently to the game are a huge win. (Survival, New Heroes, Difficulties)
It's free to play, that being said, see cons.

Cons:
Chromatic seems to sell half baked things and by no means should be trusted.
The game is HEAVILY monetized but wouldn't go as far as pay to win.
If you like the campaign and chaos one and you know you want to drop the 1k hours into getting to end game, buying bags is a must. It's like $20 to max them...
That being said everything else can be unlocked through loot boxes that can be earned in game from onslaught and doesn't have to be purchased.
Heroes as well can be unlocked in game through grind and doesn't have to be bought.
If you ask me, $20 some is relatively cheap for how long you're going to be playing.
The MODS is a good system however I find it dumb that most of them are useless and the ones that are "good" aren't really effective unless they're maxed out to 10-10.


A lot of negative reviews I see is either that people want a DD1 replacement (this is clearly it's own game)
Chromatic's practices (rightfully so IMO)
and how the game is "FREE" but have to buy Bags and people are unwilling to grind for heroes. (they have to get money from somewhere)

That being said, I been around for years, and plan to be for several more off and on.
If you like slower paced games with grind, especially now a days where everything is quick and p2w, it's nice to settle down and just grind it out.

As Trendy had created in the first installment of the Dungeon Defenders series, there were multiple other characters implemented into the game. Although not free, through in-game currencies their purchase deemed completely possible. The game itself ranges from a loot-base system to determine the strength of the player for competing at specific tiers of chaos.
