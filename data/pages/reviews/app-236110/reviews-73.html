\r\n\t\t\t\t\t------------------------------------------------------------------------------
December 18 Harbinger Awakens
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Finally after so many months of bad experience, the game is slowly moving towards the right path.
Loots are finally getting better, easier to get upgrades.
Character stats has a limited customization, which is better than none.
Althought huntress' role is still pretty muc fixed to be either hero dps or defense dps (the class still lack supportive item passive, afaik)
Game optimization seems a bit sluggish, it feels that the game require a higher spec to play smoothly.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
August 1 Loot and Survive
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A lot has gone on.
The last couple days, Trendy has issued a lot of hotfixes, that patches some of the most blantant bugs in the game, such as certain maps spawns only the small units and boss units, making it the best places to farm EXP and pet-related items. As well as that NM mode bug, which I am not sure how, but loot drops from small enemies are now restricted... meaning that there was a pretty mean exploit around small enemies in NM mode, that benefited a very small portion of players.

Matchmaking is a lot better than before, but still have its issues, since player lacks the ability to choose a more than 1 map to queue for (it is either 1 map or a random map within a category)

Survival (Onslaught) Normal is very lackluster, loot and exp is not good at all (blue reward items for reaching wave 6!) Free Play Normal offers similar loot with easier difficulties, and Free Play Hard has similar difficulties yet much better loots. Maybe it would change if there is a Onslaught Hard.

Personal opinion on loot drops and difficulties is that the game has difficulties set too steep. Monster already go up in levels in harder content, yet they get more traits and more resistance in higher difficulty (read level range). There is no Normal mode for higher level players, higher level means you are forced to do Hard.

It is the type of difficulty needed in Prepatch when players scale with both defense power and defense speed. Monster triple-dip on its stats (higher level+resistance+trait), player double-dip on defense speed and defense power. Now that players only get defense power, the whole game feels very stressful. There is a lot of running around like headless chicken, repairing everything, which could be results of a bad build, I will be honest. However, I have been in a number of games, and never have I felt that the build is "correct", since we all have been so busy getting everything fixed and fighting for our lifes.

However, there is one thing I really enjoy. In onslaught mode, there is a set called "Horde", basically a swarm of small units with some bigger units and healers would spawn, and sometimes players has to abandon their lane and all rushes to a single lane to help hold that lane. That is the best moment of teamwork I have seen in this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt feels like this game is having an idenity crisis at times, DD2 was supposed to be a moba but they scrapped it. And now its a dungeon defenders game with mobile game elements (you have to wait for your pets to hatch and you have to sacrifice items to make items stronger) where as in dungeon defenders 1 you didn't have to wait for your pet to hatch and you could upgrade your weapons with mana (the in-game currency)

I am glad that they are taking the communitys feedback about what to put in their game though, if you look on the dungeon defenders 2 website you will see that they are putting up polls on what people want in the game. I am sure this game will come out great, but in its current state it feels lack luster (as expected from an early access game)

So far, DD2 looks pretty nice. Great colors, great animations and fun towers and weapons to mess around with.\t\t\t\t\t
