I've watched the game devolve into a live service game that anytime I've gone to play I've suffered performance issue. And at some point for a reason not obvious to me, I have lost cosmetic skins for characters that I once had and would need to pay to have them again.

Secondly most of the ranged enemies int he game out range most of the tower making it near impossible to focus your attention elese where. The constant baby sitting of your towers is kind of counter-tower defense in my opinion.

- Different resources: you have different "mana" for summoning/upgrading towers and for doing actual abilities.

- Combat is more Hero oriented.

- The tavern is huuuuge.

- Specs : Every lvl you get one point that you can choose to put into one of about 8 different skills rangeing from all sorts of things.

All in all, the game is still g2g in my opinion. Extreamly playable for an Alpha game, I Recommend it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am writing this review based off of the fact that this game in Pre-Alpha (1.1), and I am going to assume most things like the following will be fixed and ironed out in time:
-The ♥♥♥♥♥♥ progression.
-The matchmaking works, but the system itself (Dividing players and maps by level) is odd.
-Some perks are bland, and there are not enough perks.
-No corny story to half care about.

DD2 is a lot like DD1. The best way I can describe this game for DD1 players is this: if you enjoyed playing a damage dealing hero you will love DD2. The removal of directly upgrading stats upon leveling up has made every character more well rounded. So if you focus on tower stats with your gear, you will still be able to make a difference by directly attacking. If you focus on player stats instead of towers, your towers/auras will still be able to do at least something for the team. This allows you to not have to choose a playstyle (towers or personal dps), and instead mix the two, choosing which one you want to be a better than the other. This does not mean that your towers are that much weaker though. A squire with Tower-HP will have barricades twice, if not three times as strong as someone without the necessary points invested in Tower HP. But the game expects you to use this fact that all characters are more well rounded. You cannot play this game like a TD. You have to be active on the field or your towers will be overwhelmed. Take that as a con or a pro, I personally and caught between the two.

What you do get for leveling up is a perk point or two. These are like talents that you dump points into to buff the way you want to play your hero. Currently each hero has 6 perks, 3 focused on towers and 3 focused on the hero. Good system with potential, but right now is not much to freak out about.

A few quick notes to finish this off:
-The game is really pretty.
-Good map design. Overwhelming at times when you first see a new map, but they are open and easy to learn.
-It is really really nice looking.
-It isn't a MOBA.
-New enemy types demand new tower / aura setups to deal with them.
-It is difficult.
-It runs very well for a "pre-alpha". FPS drops almost never happen and loading is fast.\t\t\t\t\t
