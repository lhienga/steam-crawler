\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoved Dungeon Defenders 1 and so far this one has been better in every regard. My only complaint so far is that it takes so long to hatch an egg and they are filling my inventory.

~Edit~

It would be cool if the classes had secondary weapons to give them a bit more variety. Such as bringing back the crossbow to the huntress with a faster fire rate than the bow but lower damage per shot or a two handed weapon for the squire forgoing his shield in favor of a charged attack.

The individual construction gems is a nice feature but also a burden when you're playing with pre-constructed teams. A prime example is I often play with a group of my friends and enjoy playing hero spec'd huntress and squire where they enjoy playing tower spec'd monk and apprentice. When we complete a wave I have to go gather up my gems from the chest and drop them for my friends to use since they will have the much stronger buildings. It'd be a nice if I could pass my chest off to the other players automatically splitting the resources among the other players or having a feature for private rooms to allow one person to gather all of the gems from the chest they open.

I would also love an endless mode where you just try to survive an endless amount of waves.

The new tavern is nice and actually feels more like a social place rather than just a hub for in between levels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery disappointing.

I wanted this game from release and happened to see it on sale and quickly bought it. I know its still in its Alpha stage, but I don't feel I can play it just yet without being left frustrated.

After loading I was forced to join multiplayer tavern with NO option to choose not to (I'm on wireless and lag ruins any online play). On top of this the sheer amount of menus and the lack of tutorial (what happened to the simplicity of the first game) make navigating an absolute chore (its not about learning what goes where over time, I just want to kill stuff, not to dress a frigging barbie).

After eventually finding my way into a single player tavern, I found in-game playable but it didn't grip me, it's not even a patch on the first DD. The graphics have been increased (and by that I mean, the screen is now filled with useless things such as more grass and frilly bits to make the game look better) making smaller enemies and important pathways harder to see, it looks great but its just annoying. Some of the interactable objects are difficult to click on as it is, these small irritations just make the experience frustrating.

Lastly the most annoying 'feature' of all - What game in the history of non-mmo games has ever forced you to join multiplayer and then and only then can you move to single player?

I know I am reviewing an early access alpha, but I don't see these things changing after reading various forum posts, especially the starting social taverns.

I think I will probably stick to the first game to get my tower defense fix, at least its my choice if I want to go online.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: So it has been almost a year since my last review and since having taken a hiatus I came back to this game to see how it was getting on and give it a fair crack of the whip. Turns out it has made a lot of changes and feels like a much fuller and complete game. At this point it is worth it based on just the fact it is not free to play and offers a lot of content. This game has found its own charm and balances the tower v player aspect very well. Progression feels so much better and lets you play through without getting stuck or wondering what to do next. They seem to be keeping up with monthly events and things like the carnival to keep people interested, and to give them the opportunity to own unique skins and items. Pets are in FINALLY and have improved significantly but I still feel like they don't do as much as they should and they don't feel unique at all. I'm still confused as to whether or not the only difference in the pets is the skin and the active. The pets in DD1 were a lot more useful, offering a wide range of abilities. Pets don't seem to change much upon levelling up. It would be nice to have something more like the engineer that helps you repair or increases tower attack. All in all good game.

As of right now the game is very much incomplete feature wise, with a completely different feel from the original, kind of more professional but that was what the original has as a charm aspect. The second game focuses more on the player taking part rather than the turrets doing most of the work. This somewhat takes away from the strategic placement of turrets in the first game to where the player is instead. The game is fun to play and has a bright future ahead of it. However right now it is missing most of the important parts which make it a fun tower defense, collect em all, achievement heavy, grind festy game.\t\t\t\t\t
