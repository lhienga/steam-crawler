\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHas a lot of potential and if you like survival sim or base building sims then the game is well worth its 20$.

It has much need for tweaking and balance but the game is not near 1.0 in my opinion and already works well enough to play and be entertaining. Some improvements it could use, to not get frustrated with as a new player.


- A way to pin or mark tiles on the map or pin animals or other entities would be nice
- A bog pit or other place of making compost would be nice, and fit the 1300s setting given by the game. Bones ash and other "Waste" category items except corpses could be "fermented" in this to make compost/manure, this could also be used to quickly regrow grass over bare soil, and the structure
could passively encourage mushrooms specifically to grow next to it.
- The planter will self prioritize running all the way across the map to pick up 2 Seed that they need to sow in the crop or tree plot, rather than coming to the storage or stockpile and getting that seed from storage. It seems they use the oldest instance of item on map regardless of distance perhaps which can be both good and bad. Imo this should be separated so pops prioritize getting item from storage first over laying on the ground across the map in all situations where both are possible, and then use the First in First out or use the lowest decay timer remaining on either storage or the overworld, only when that item is not stored anywhere.
- The settlers get more mood bonus just eating on a random Wicker stool placed around the base, than from eating in a Modest tier Great Hall
- The Shelf building item that is used to store 3 items in one tile as opposed to one needs examined. Currently you cannot empty Shelves or move the items stored in it to a new stockpile or shelf elsewhere without deconstructing the Shelf itself. If there is an item already in the shelf, and you forbid that item there afterwards, they will still fill up that item that is now forbidden until the stack that's already there is full.
- There is no current way to keep or pen dogs/wolves, there is no way to feed dogs/wolves other than putting raw meat on the ground for them to eat, or making animal feed which is much more expensive than losing a stack of raw meat.
- The pops can currently get themselves stuck while building walls or mining or placing soil, they are not able to detect standing on a certain side to construct it so they do not get stuck inside the construction.
- Carrots, apples, smoked meat for a few examples are all considered "Raw ingredients" by the game, and the characters eating smoked meat or an apple will affect them in the same way that eating raw meat does. Cooking could use a better or more coherent system than this.
- A balance of perks for spawned pops would perhaps be interesting, right now it seems mostly random so the desperate refugee your starting pops save can have all negative/debuff perks, or only one positive perk. Making it less likely to roll all negative or all positive perks, closer to half and half, might be useful.
- In the refugee/exile event, the number of enemies stated by the event actually seems to be the amount you have to kill for the rest to retreat, rather than the total force. The game will state that X archer and X melee unit will arrive in X days. But this information is never correct.
