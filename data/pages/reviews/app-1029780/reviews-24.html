On the other hand you'll get well-made updates with overhauls or new mechanics that improve the game and just make you want to build another castle/cathedral/whatever suits your fancy.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has all has all the markers of an award winner. There are many clones out there in the survival genre, but this one is attention worthy. The mechanics are smooth and it plays well. Bonus for being able to make your own coat of arms. As the medieval history buff, I think the overall concept is great (not that this game bleeds historical accuracy or anything,,,). It's fun for a few hours, although it is certainly not a finished product yet.

I've been playing it on early access for a bit now, and I can tell you that the Devs actually listen to the fans. I love that about the team. They keep adding on and making it better. If I had to list flaws, I'd say the game is still missing things to do. It would be great to raid other settlements, have more complex trade, and different biomes, more furniture variations, other in-game events, etc. Much of this is already in the works, but not done. My biggest fear is that I'll get bored of it before the final product is released.

I recommend the game in its current state to a casual player or someone that wants to help the Devs with advice or funds. Wait for the final product otherwise, I think it will be worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
