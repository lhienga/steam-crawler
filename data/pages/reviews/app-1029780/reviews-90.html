\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo here's my official review of Going Medieval: If you were a fan of the building and the events where your base gets attacked in Valheim, and you're a fan of colony sims, and you're ok with long periods of idling (even at 3x speed), *and* you're willing to put up with some **piss-poor performance** while you have a lot of construction queued up (it is still an early access game, and ***it shows***), it's pretty good. The early game kind of sucks because you have a lot of construction to do, and by the time you hit 6 settlers even a top end PC starts to experience major stuttering, which appears to be tied to large scale construction projects. If you aren't able to keep up with the amount of work orders also seems to cause this issue in the early game, but becomes less of a problem as you gain more settlers, and domesticate and tame more goats and cows. Getting a self-sustaining population of pack animals going is ***CRUCIAL*** for success. But now that I've finally hit my stride– I have an easily defensible keep containing all of my production and domestic facilities, I have plenty of food production and have pared it down such that my overproduction is minimal, and I have found a stable priority system; all of which took 5 and a half years of in-game time in my current save alone– I'm really enjoying building out and optimizing my settlement while fending off waves of attacks from ne'er-do-wells. I'd give it a solid 3 out of 5 in it's current state.\t\t\t\t\t
