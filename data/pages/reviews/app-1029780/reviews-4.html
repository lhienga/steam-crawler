\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreetings! I’ve been trying to find the best way to review “Going Medieval” for some time now and having written and rewritten this review I few times I’m still not sure I’m going to articulate my words as well as I’d like…but I’m going to do what I can.

Let’s Start Small: This genre is quickly becoming my favorite; at the core you maintain a small group of settlers and slowly build up a city of some stripe. It’s smaller than ‘city builders’ and I’ve taken to calling them ‘colony builders’ and you typically have more individual control of your settlers than city builders but still don’t typically have direct control over them. With that, you gather resources, fight off invaders and typically just use what you have to survive and hopefully thrive.

A New Discovery: The single biggest thing that the game seems to have is the multi level design. You can build towers and entire complexes that are really intricate. The interface for this still needs work but it definitely will be something that separates this from the other games of the genre. You also have a pretty heavy ability to terra-form, digging deep underground cellars and shaping the world and it’s resources as you see fit, given time and effort.

Faceless in a Crowd: I don’t know if it’s intended or not but the resource management is a complete mess, it is difficult to understand what you have of what especially when it comes to food. I also had several crashes and would have played this longer if not for that. Almost every other issue I have with the game just revolves around the game needing longer to develop. The settlers also heavily lack any real form of character, I was hoping for something like Stranded has where the characters are bespoke and not randomly generated like most games but I’m not even sure which one they are…they are completely bland currently. I’m absolutely sure this game could be something great; just needs longer development time and I’d urge the developers to reach out to gamers who would like to provide said feedback while developing it.

My single biggest issue with Going Medieval is that there are games out there that do this already what this game does better and the niche of the game, multiple levels, just doesn’t grab me enough to justify the game. The game is not bad, it just seems to need more time in development. With that, I can’t really recommend it in the current build. Given a few months I may have to come back to this game but as of now I’ve refunded it. Enjoy!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis has to be the best kingdom/town building game I've ever played.
I like depth and the ability to creativity pave my own way.
The information in the intricacies and details is incredible in this game and there is huge depth to playing. Mastering the mechanics will take some real dedication.
You have to account for temperature of beer vs hay vs various foods. Storage isn't easy.

Its extremely playable in this state and the developers are steaming through the road map, clobbering bugs I've never even encountered, and heavily interactive with the community.
10/10

Notes:
Each settler is an extremely valuable individual and has unique traits, attributes, perks and as a result needs as well. Its similar to the Sims in that regard, but not easily comparable.

It does have some micromanagement moments and can be tedious in that way but as the game develops there is big progress towards the smoothing of these wrinkles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t41 hrs and I haven't done much yet in this game. That's a good thing (to me). Granted, I've restarted many times (I like to do that with games until I feel completely comfortable with them and understand how everything works).

I think it looks great, runs great, haven't had any issues.

Although this is probably one of my favorite game genres, I must admit I'm not a HUGE fan of all the "personality" stuff in this and similar games. If I'm out to build a major civilization, the last thing I want to be concerned with is somebody whining because the trees aren't pretty enough or something. Feelings? Who cares? We're building Empire here! Mush!

But seriously, this game does give you good options for addressing that stuff (adjusting hours spent sleeping, leisure activities, work, or a more generalized "just do stuff" directive (good if, like me, you just click a bunch of stuff on the map that you want collected, for example), or just eliminate certain activities altogether. And you can at least "vanilla down" your MC by not giving any weird traits, or only giving ones with benefits. It doesn't seem to be a "prevent-all" though, but at least you're not going to start out with a weirdo right out of the gate.

I play on Lone Wolf on the Peaceful, Normal setting (for now) on a large random map.

A couple FYI's:

If you want to mine a large area, don't create it all at once. Start off small, and once you've got enough space to build stairs (if needed) then build them. I recently had to load a previous save because all three of my peeps were mining a large area and before I knew it all three were down on the next level with no way to get out (no sloping land, no way to go get resources to build stairs and...

HOLD EVERYTHING! EUREKA! This is another thing I love about this game. As I was writing the above, it finally clicked in my head: hang on, I was only thinking about wooden stairs! If you pay attention to what you're mining (ahem), you may just very well mine the very materials you need to build other types of stairs.... duh.

Anyway, here's another: if you want to kill someone in Peaceful mode, select your "MC," click "attack," then select the target with a right-click. AND BE SURE TO turn off the "tend/treat wounds" action on everyone else, or once the target is unconscious somebody will attempt to treat the wounds - and that's just opening up a never-ending circle of torture, lol. Also, once the target is unconscious, keep attacking to finish him or her off (otherwise once the target falls unconscious everyone else will leave off and go do other stuff).

Research can be slow unless you pretty much have a peep who will tend to default to that action when not doing other things. I'm sure I'm making things harder than they need to be by going Lone Wolf (and so far only admitting two others), but hey, that's the way I like it.

Let's see, anything else? Food is important. Gardening is important, even if you only have a handful of peeps, because the cyclical nature of the resources may lead to some empty stomachs. Unless you want to have someone spend pretty much all their time chasing down wildlife.

Anyway, guess I'll shut up now. This is hands-down the best early-access game I've ever played.\t\t\t\t\t
