\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn alot of ways this game is just middle ages rimworld but with less features, very little mod support, and stupider pawns.

- In order to get your villagers to do anything but 1 sigular task, or sometimes nothing at all, you have to set the tasks priority to 2, and when you want them to stop, you have to set the priority of the last task to 3 and the next to 2. otherwise you can set tons of buildings and tasks and they won't ever touch them, even when they're starving in the middle of winter with food to be cooked and fires to be lit 5 feet away. I've also had villagers somehow get themselves stuck inside holes they're digging and refuse to build a staircase out, or sit staring at a pile of food as they slowly starve. it feels like you're fighting your own dumb villagers more than anything else.

- Trebuchets are completely overpowered. once they show up its practically game over. they're sniper accurate from many yards away, only take 1 enemy to operate, fire quickly and are difficult to take out. they even deal stupid amounts of splash damage below and above where they hit.

- When you make a villager you're incredibly limited on what you can do. I wanted to do a save where I made a single villager start with nothing and all stats set to 0 but it just won't let you do that. you have to have a title that gives bonuses, you can't make their stat total below a certain amount, and you can't even make them start wearing no gear either. Ultimately it just ends up being very boring.

- as I said, little mod-ability. theres currently a nexus for it but the selection is sparse and its not even clear what a few of the mods even do. thats not even mentioning the mods that just don't work anymore. the devs also arn't clear on weather or not they'll add workshop support, wich is sad because if any game absolutely needs mods right now its this one.

- Little to do. you just sit there and watch your villagers struggle their way through tasks and maybe, at the end of the day, you get someone asking to join you or get raided. those are the only 2 events that ever popped up for me. your area is small even if you choose large so there ends up being very little happening and very little to look at. it gets boring very quickly.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have always felt there was a gap in the market for a 3D base-building game surrounding medieval castles. There are SO MANY banished-style city builders which lack creative innovation in the modern gaming landscape. This game brings a new twist on the genre by focusing on defence rather than resource management. Of course, resource management is included but instead of being forced to make pre-made woodcutting and fishing buildings, it has an almost minceraft-level of creative freedom regarding how you go about the game. You can create a keep surrounded by fields of wheat, or a village with castle walls and a moat. The 3D aspect allows for multiple floors which is a feature in 2D games, like Rimworld and Prison Architect, which always slightly held them back from achieving a truly realistic final product. I have now created two castles based on the floor plans and architecture of REAL castles as well as two of my own designs.

The game has a long way to go. A lot of defensive features like machicolations haven't been added yet and building is still a bit clunky, but it's getting there. I highly recommend this game for fulfilling an itch I've had for a long time but what I would say is do not expect a refined and polished product yet. There is still so much work that needs to be done on sieges and raids especially, but also so much POTENTIAL. I would love even just simple political and trade mechanics because as it stands there are just pointless friendly and unfriendly neighbouring civilisations. Creating a safe space for traders to stay, maybe an inn feature, would be amazing to create a prosperous marketplace. It gives an incentive and narrative for jealous raiders and hostile civilisations to lay you to siege. As mentioned earlier the sieges themselves aren't great yet and are very unrealistic and clunky but I'm sure the devs will improve them in time.

My one criticism is there can be a bit too much micromanagement around your citizens. There is a Rimworldesque system in place for their skills and focuses. It's good but in the late-game you have a lot of civilians, and if one dies, you have to go through and re-organise all the other civilians to make up for the specialty gap that the deceased left. I suppose some may like this micromanagement but I find it a little tedious. I would love presets like "farmer", "miner" etc... which fill in the focuses for you as well as custom presets.

Please get this game if you enjoy base building, city-building, resource management, historical forts and castles, or just have any kind of creative inclination. The devs also are consistently updating the game so you can expect a constant flow of improvements!\t\t\t\t\t
