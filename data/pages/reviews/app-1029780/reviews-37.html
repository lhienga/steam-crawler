\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile it has a lot of good ideas and a fairly firm starting base, this game just doesn't function well. I'm not sure if the days are too short or just feel that way, but even if left alone with a single set of tasks for the day (for example, a single hut to build or a temporary "you are only hauling today" job setting), the AI struggles to get anything done. Tasks are queued up in a hilariously inefficient order, with a single hauler running back and forth multiple times to gather up piles of sticks instead of taking the shortest path to the next pile of sticks. Often times, I will see a hauler run past three or four batches of sticks they are tasked with collecting to take a farther pile before then running back to get the sticks they just ran past. I do not understand what logic they could possibly be running off of.

Heaven forbid two people try to do the same task. Haulers will actively try to collect the exact same (wrong, far away) piles of items, causing each other to constantly cancel their tasks and seek new piles. Trying to have two people build one thing also gets messy, with one builder "reserving" pieces and causing delays because they sometimes refuse to grab more than one wall's worth of resources.

I love the z-levels in Going Medieval and the combat is kinda nice (when the enemies don't fail to realize they can walk up your stairs), but the colonist AI is so inefficient that it's hard to get anything done, even when each colonist is tailor-made for one or two jobs. They will also just... give up on a task sometimes? I'm not sure what's going on, but especially with hunting or attacking while drafted, colonists will just stand in place for several moments, do some kind of idle animation, then decide to go do something entirely different. They aren't hitting leisure/sleep time or otherwise tending needs, they just give up and pick a new job to do.

When and if this game's AI can at least prioritize by proximity, it will be a lot more enjoyable to play. As it currently stands, your own colonists' AI is actively hindering your survival by wasting time by doing simple tasks as inefficiently as it can manage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt has potential... I have an excellent computer (new, near top-of-the-line graphics, processor, unnecessarily large aio, etc...), and this is the first game in which there was a noticeable, somewhat significant uptick in operating temperature. I turned the graphics options to their lowest settings and the results were the same. There are a lot of bugs, and the game play is rough-hewn stone. If Banished and Rimworld had a child, this would be it... which sounds awesome, but... it is barely newborn and there are no mods (I mention this because both of those games arguably depend/ed on their modding communities to be as good as they became.) People will demand mods for this game. I have a feeling that even after "release" it will still feel some what lacking without mods.

I'm giving it a thumbs down, because I don't feel like it's all that entertaining in its current state and I'm tired of new games being designed to run on hardware that has become prohibitively expensive for many people. If and when the state of the game changes in a significant way, I will update my review.\t\t\t\t\t
