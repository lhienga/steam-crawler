\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo not buy this game if you are looking for a resource-management sim, like many other city builders are. Dwarf Fortress players begone.

I love this game. I've poured hundreds of hours into it and even written a long-ass guide. I've seen everything this game has to offer. I'd best describe this game as "unused potential".

This game severely lacks if you are looking for resources, management, and production. The developers have stated their goal is a game "more on the Sims side" of a "Factorio to Sims" spectrum. Do not expect anything like Rise to Ruins, Dwarf Fortress, or similar titles with management focus and rich mechanics. The game runs out of content quickly, and difficulty is nonexistent or artificial due to poor balancing in both economy and combat. Most mechanics are fairly shallow and do not attempt to weave with other mechanics to create depth. Some content is so shallow it barely even qualifies as content at all.

This game is for you if you are looking for a casual experience focused on people, not resources. Again, "more on the Sims side". Your settlers make friends and bad dreams make them unhappy. Keeping them happy is an expansive mechanic similar to Rimworld. Settler wounds are largely inconsequential. You will still build, produce, and defend, though not to the degree of other city-builders. Difficulty isn't very present. However, you will still run out of content quickly, as core game systems lack depth and, well, content.

The development team is communicative and releases consistent updates, but unfortunately many of them lack depth and actual content. The last few updates released have minimal impact to gameplay, and the aforementioned "more on the Sims side" bit is used in the devlog as an excuse to omit meaningful content. The most recent update, for example, adds bodies of water and all you can do with them is fish. No water power, no water-related recipes, villagers don't dehydrate, no boats, no bridges, no moats. Nothing. The update before that added a socialization system that barely even affects your settlers' mood. Months have passed as well as multiple major updates, yet I haven't felt a need to return to the game as substantial content just isn't being added; the game plays the exact same. It appears to be a trend.

The game itself has tons of potential, but this is a game that will take years to exit Early Access. It has been two years already, and the roadmap looks to be another few on top of that. I have no doubt this game will develop steadily and healthily, though I cannot guarantee new features enhance gameplay. The developers frequently tack features on instead of weaving them with existing systems, which makes the gameplay loops lack content and bulk even if they may be solid and fun. It's also a game that tries to appease a niche casual audience in a genre built around intensive management--a development direction I find confusing. The design just feels aimless and inexperienced, and I'd fear the end product will be flat and unengaging.

I am hoping the game is simply unfinished, and we will see more depth/content as mechanics are expanded/added and the game is playtested and polished. I will change my review if/when this happens, but until then expect a dull city-builder with too much unused potential.\t\t\t\t\t
