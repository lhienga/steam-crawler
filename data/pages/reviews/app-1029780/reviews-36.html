\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRimworld, but medieval. If you've ever played Rimworld with the medieval mod, it's very similar to that, except there are plenty of features that make this game unique. The first of which is the 3D rendering, it gives access to multiple levels of the environment, so you can build tall towers and even underground structures, which can be useful for storage and giving your colonists more room to expand without actually having to build towards the edge of the map. Another thing I really like about this is the fact that there is no electricity at all, so freezing food is extremely difficult, since most of it has to be done by putting it underground. Though, I think a passive cooler would be neat to have in this game, as it's something quick and easy to keep food fresh for longer, but that's something that doesn't exist in this game (yet). Otherwise it takes a bit of thought to figure out how to keep your food from spoiling.

I also really enjoy the bartering system, where you don't have silver or anything of the sort, like Rimworld has, so you just have to trade valuables, as if you're just trading in Rimworld without any coins.

Overall, I believe if this gets several more updates, it can be on par with Rimworld, especially since I love the medieval asthetic more than the space sci-fi one. That's not to say that you shouldn't buy Rimworld, as I still thoroughly enjoy Rimworld, but both games have their own fun to be had, so if you're ever bored of one game, try the other, as if you enjoy one, you'll most certainly enjoy the other.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Going Medieval" goes strong. I hope that more improvements are in its future.
It's pretty hardcore, and it is a lot like Dwarf Fortress or Rimworld.

It has potential to surpass the other survival games but is currently held back by some basic flaws which can be ironed out - or not. Mid 2023 written.

Everything is like 80% there and is fairly convenient to use. By everything I mean:

* Crafting is convenient: no tough requirements to get what you want.
* There are some tactics available in combat, but mostly involves killboxing or Blob.io style tactics.
* Trading is nice because there's not as much random that happens to your caravan. But there's also not much diplomacy or wagons or random overworld quests.
* Crops have some nice options to manage seeding, but fences (And Dogs) don't stop wildlife thieves.
* Livestock is available, but without auto-slaughter.
* There are some deficiencies in A.I. hauling/job logic which becomes critical in Winter.
* The building system is really nice, but we NEED an easy wall-hiding option because every wall is in the way and the half-hidden layer is ugly.
* No warning to enemy incursions

I think the game shines in the visuals of its stockpile management where things pile nicely and have nice models.

So once again another game that has tons of potential. Only time will tell how far it is developed (which isn't helpful for a review lol).

Had fun, 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
