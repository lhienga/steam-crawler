\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt really saddens me to give this a negative review. The initial concept of a 3D colony sim is amazing but the choice of theme appears to have completely handicapped the team's imagination. It ends up being a subpar clone of rimworld.

I first tried this when it came out and was quite excited to see it uses the 3D space appropriately. After over a year, I gave it another try after half of its planned features are implemented and I have to say I'm only disappointed. All of the new features are lacking in depth and feels very optional and all the upcoming features appears to be straight from RimWorld.

Take the water feature for example. If you've played other games like Timberborn that is built around this mechanic, you have to ask what it is for beyond making things look prettier. Nothing in the colony needs water (fish are an alternative food source, but it's easy enough to get food as it is). You can make a moat, but enemy already doesn't climb down a trench so it serves no functional purpose. In games like Timberborn, water is an essential mechanic that feeds into everything else (from thirst, to irrigation, disaster, entertainment and even generating power).

The other disappointment is the tech tree. Progressing in the tech tree should feel the game changing. For example, in RimWorld, progressing into the modern area unlocks power, which requires you to add wires and power system to your entire colony. Oxygen not included takes this to an extreme with each tech significantly changing how you design your base (like the tube highway or mechanical heating/cooling). Going medieval tech tree is at best boring. You unlock the highest tier of building material from the first level of tech (limtestone brick) and all weapons you start off making are functionally the same as all future weapon. Nothing in the tech tree beyond the first line appears to add anything drastic to the game. This seems to be a common issue with realistic medieval themed games. The tech progression tends to all look the same and does little to change the game.

Perhaps mod will eventually fix a number of these issues, but I'm rather skeptical at the moment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately, I have to play Devil's advocate here, and say skip this in favor of better options.

The game is enjoyable.
It has some early access quirks, but is also mostly stable.

But this genre already has many entries that are not just good, but absolutely great. This is a relatively weak entry that doesn't bring much new to the field, other than serving as an easy-to-access primer for superior, but more in-depth options.

If you're new to these types of games, it's a great option.
If you're a seasoned gamer, or have any experience with colony management sims, skip it.

Research is fairly simple, and while there are a number of levels to unlock, each level doesn't unlock much more to do.

Combat is, again, fairly simple. Raids ramp up in size and complexity, but aren't too challenging. Traps and defenses exist, but again - are pretty simple and limited.

Food and various options such as preserving or having it go to waste exist. But...again, it's a pretty simple system that doesn't go much farther than what you see early on.

Construction is the same, clothing is the same, decorating is the same, mining and resource harvesting is the same. Basically everything content or mechanic wise is the same in that it's there, but limited,\t\t\t\t\t
