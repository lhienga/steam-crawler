\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a lot of fun and a brilliant edition to the game library of anybody who enjoys settlement/survival games. Whilst it offers fast paced, more intense game play with the editions of raids from over villages, it also gives you the option to turn these aspects of the game off, if you're after a more relaxed experience.

I think the building mechanic is what sets this game out from the rest. Buildings are built bit by bit by you, giving you the chance to completely customise your village. Even down to the placement of doors and windows, everything is designed by the player, which I thought was a really refreshing take on the typical settlement type game where you're placing pre-made buildings.

I think the only issue this game faces is the lack of villagers coming to join your settlement. Unlike many games in the genre, Going Medieval does not have a family system, and so there isn't a consistent flow of villagers. In the almost 7 hours I've played, I've only managed to recruit 2 new villagers. I know the devs have said previously they don't want to add the ability for villagers to have children due to some of the content in the game (raids, etc..) and that's fair enough, but I feel like some sort of consistent flow of villagers needs to be added, either by adding a family system and making children immune to the more sensitive aspects of the game, or have a consistent way for players to recruit new people, such as by having new villagers seeking homes visit your town on a seasonal basis, giving the player the opportunity to select a certain amount of these villagers to join the settlement.

Overall, this is a great game with a lot of interesting game play mechanics. There are a few issues that need addressing, but for an early access game, I'd definitely recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
