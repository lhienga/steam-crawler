\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing off and on for many months. I keep hoping that the game will improve; however, it's still a hot mess. The level of micro management required is beyond stupid. My absolute gem from my last game: food reserves are running low, so I allow hunting a deer. The hunter does her job and hauls the carcass back to the stockpile.... and nothing. No one butchers the animal or cooks any food. Instead the hunter goes off to mine some iron, one of my other folks sits idle, and my third villager tries to tame a goat clear across the map. The villagers do their thing for multiple days, even though they are now out of food until the deer carcass rots on the stockpile. The fact that I have to micromanage every aspect of life for these idiots is just pointless.

And don't get me started on the "attacks." You will get to the point where the attackers bring catapults to reduce your buildings to dust. But there is no technology available for me to build my own catapults. And they come with large numbers; there's no way to send my villagers out to fight the attackers.

Just be prepared to watch your buildings blasted to dust and your villagers starve themselves. Perhaps someday the devs will release an update that makes the game fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very easy title to get lost in. There is a lot to do in terms of keeping your settlers alive, happy, and well defended from enemies and the elements. Some of the game-play mechanics in this title are very unique or more often overlooked by other games in the same genre. Being able to build structures / terraform vertically, you can quite literally move mountains or create a deep cold cavern to preserve your food during the sweltering heat waves of the summer months. This makes building your settlement more engaging as you have to plan ahead with structure placements as you can build on various levels stacked on top of each other. Be mindful of your structure stability.
The research system is genius as the game requires to have your settlers create tomes which have to be stored in your settlement's library. Each item researched requires X amount of books to fulfill the research requirement. If you lose the books or they are destroyed you have to re-meet the researches' book requirement to be able to continue to use that unlocked research.
Graphically, even with the minimalist art style, this title is very well polished and charming to look at for lengthy game sessions. I do wish there was more variation available in terms of in-game music. Granted this is still in development and that can be added later. Running YouTube in the background makes for an easy fix currently.
I was hesitant to pick this title up as I wasn't sure how much the game play loop would keep me engaged, but now nearing the 400 hour mark, this game has so much potential to have a long life-span. I hope the developers are able to open up mod support as this has the foundation to really be a game with a flourishing fan base.

Having spent a few hours playing the game, there are few items that are currently not on the development roadmap that would be a nice quality of life improvement. Hopefully the Dev's read this stuff.

1. Add horses / stables. This will help offset the movement impairment heavily armored settlers have and also make tracking down and destroying trebuchets more viable when under siege.
2. Being able to host a feast in your settlement. You can currently make Lavish Meals in game, have it so you need X lavish meals, X type of ales or fine-wine to be able to host the event. Doing so makes varied use of the items but also could add a settlement happiness bonus.
3. Being able to have your merchant caravan go to more than one settlement before having to return home. If the caravan is twenty tiles away from home, trades their goods at their first location, but is currently two tiles away from another town, make it so you can travel to the other nearby town(s) before having to start to long return trip.
4. Being able to fulfill trade agreements. When visiting a settlement or a merchant visits yours, add a "looking for / needed" tab to the trade window. If you can fulfill a request within the allotted time, you get a reward (coins / livestock/ etc) and also improve your relations with that faction.
5. Being able to buy dirt as a trade good from other settlements. Small, but needed.
6. Marry and produce children. This could add a lot of potential. Children would be limited to menial tasks such as stewarding, gardening, or animal husbandry until they come of age. Historically that would around 14 during this time period. The biggest added benefit of raising the child for years before they can do other more skilled labor in your settlement would be adding the ability to customize the child as they are brought into your game word. Give the player the ability to customize in game children when they come of age the same way you do when creating a new game using the character creator. This would help fill labor gaps if your settlement is in need of a certain missing skill sets.
7. Add a room type for an "Inn." This would require X beds, X entertainment buildings. Add in new workbench for a bar for this room type. Drinking here could give an added happiness bonus, and also have a higher chance of positive socialization chances with other settlers. This same building could also increase the odds a new settler visits your community by peaceful means. As it sits currently, majority of new settlers stumble onto your lands usually with a group of pursuers right behind them. Adding this would give a peaceful option for newcomers without combat following suite shortly after, but also giving more variation to commit to a newcomer to take up residence in your newly established lands without a guaranteed negative response from your townspeople if you deny them residence.
8. Add in a training ground (ie. archery target for bowman, training dummy for melee.)

Current bug:
Deceased animals and people are unable to be buried or cremated if tagged by a wild animal. A game reload generally fixes this but will persist if not done.

Keep up the great work. Wonderful game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you love Rimworld, but hate all the blasters and 2d graphics? Despise all those pesky mods and expansions and the depth and replayability they offer? Then this game is for you.

All joking aside, I dig this game for the same reason I dig Rimworld. As well I should, as it is basically the same game, same as Stranded:Alien Dawn is the same game. Most of the game mechanics are the same. I'm honestly shocked that Ludeon hasn't sued the bloody hell out of these blatant IP thieves... but still, a definite recommended buy if you're looking for the same gameplay as Rimworld but with 3 dimensions and improved graphics.

Not for you if you want to grow your town organically. No babies. No families. Weird, but apparently the devs will never add them for some reason probably having to do with public outcry about the harsh realities of simulation gaming, but I digress, so... meh.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've sunk a couple hundred hours into this over the last few weeks - this game scratches a lot of colony-builder itches, and the devs are doing an awesome job of rolling out cool features and QOL updates.

I read some other reviews that describe Going Medieval as a "cross between Rimworld and Banished" and that's a concise way of putting it. I like to think that this is what Stonehearth could have become had it not been abandoned.

It's early access, but worth the current price tag as it stands, and the devs are continuing to build and polish the solid foundation they have already. Highly recommend this one.\t\t\t\t\t
