\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a huge fan of the city builder-resource manager-crafting based game family, I think Going Medieval is worth checking out in Early Access, despite some gaping flaws. I will likely keep playing it for a good amount more hours, but in its current state I know I will soon drop it in favor of other games. Here's a short overview:

- Very solid game mechanics with small but really cool innovations -
The backbone of the game is solid. As solid and similar to Farthest Frontier in terms of resource management and difficulty and timing of raiders. I like that you can control which villager does which type of job as well as their daily schedule, this combined with the villager's preferred jobs offers a lot of customization and different management styles that are unavailable in other similar games.
- Graphics MAY need small improvements -
Depending on what's coming in future updates, this game has the potential to be beautiful. Currently, the graphics are a bit rustic and basic. However, this may come down to player preference. There are players who will be totally happy with the graphics as they are, but personally I think they could do with some polishing. Too much contrast in the shadows sometimes makes resources hard to see, and there's a mix of very detailed elements and very pixellated/polygonal elements which overall makes it look a bit janky, IMO it should be more one or the other. Lots of opportunity for more medieval style and flair here.
- Great SFX and music -
Whoever composed the music knows their stuff! Everything "sounds" medieval and feels ethereal and environmental. A+++
- UI needs a LOT of improvement -
This is the one glaring downside to the game. The UI is functional, however it is visually ugly, and needs more and different kinds of information in some info panels for ease of access to important information. The information that is there is not well organized in some situations, making it difficult to understand what it's trying to tell you. You can still play a good game with what is there, but this is the one thing that makes it look like a truly EARLY access game, which, to be fair, it is! I hope the devs have plans to improve it because if they do, it will make a vast difference in the overall "feel" of the game.

If you're new to city builders/resource managers, this is not the game for you - yet. Too much info too poorly organized in too ugly of a fashion to give a good impression of the genre. Try Farthest Frontier, Northgard, or, for a throwback, the Stronghold series. Longtime fans of this genre will appreciate what the game is trying to do despite its rough edges. Looking forward to more updates to this game!\t\t\t\t\t

Settlers will run across the entire map for one unit recourse of milk, eggs or anything. So when you have harvesting happening it takes 3 times as long just because the AI force the settler to haul anything they harvest (I have banned hauling for all my settlers I can't select haul item, manually, but the AI forces all my settlers to work like idiots). The fix would be an actual ban on hauling by specific settlers. You can make dogs haul items (it's silly and broken) and settlers never have to haul anything. If the devs dont like that then they can just allow for true banning of hauling and I can delegate one settler to hauling duties like how the real world logistics supply chain works.

Trebuchets have infinite range. You have nothing that can reach them at their range. They don't target your outer walls they target your homes and you cant defend them unless you build walls 4 tall. You have to run out to fight them and raiders will always be stronger then your settlers. Defensive structures are a key part of this game. You will never get enough settlers to fight them in head on fights. You have to use defensive structures and fight efficiently. The fun of combat in this game it defending from a stronger force that makes you afraid they will destroy you if you don't defend yourself properly. There is a fear of breaching the gates. Trebuchets create an area of effect for targeting and launches a projectile which damages in an area of effect. All you would have to do is limit the center of targeting to the smaller range.

You could also just get rid of trebuchets all together and create sappers. They would just be a specialized unit that destroys walls very efficiently in melee range. Your archers could target them but by targeting sappers your ignoring the enemy archers and will take more damage. If you take all the sappers out the other raiders can still target walls (theyll just be slower). You could also have scalling enemies that just climb over wall and ignore them. The numbers of scallers should be limited but they do harass you defences an force you to target them first.
You could also give the player trebuchets. They are fully ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ in the game but the devs road map shows they want to make a new asset that isn't movable. It's like they forgot trebuchets have infinite range. They're working on nerfing player trebuchets because they know the current asset is broken, and they can do the easy option of just giving players trebuchets.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by saying that this is my first civilization/colony building type of game that I have played. I would absolutely recommend it if you like;
- full character customization: gender, face shape, height, weight, hair, skills, background story, skills, personality traits, religious alignment, work schedule/jobs, etc
- full building and terrain modifications: block by block building, doors, windows, stairs/ladders, roofs, defensive structures, decorations to improve "impressiveness", work stations, religious affiliation decorations and statures, etc
- random raids, settlers looking to join your colony, merchants, environmental events
- ability to: fish, hunt OR train wild animals, farm, tailor clothes, make weapons, make medicine, etc

I do have some things I wish the game would have included like roads to have your settlers follow, but I do see that in the roadmap for something that will be updated however it isn't currently a feature.\t\t\t\t\t
