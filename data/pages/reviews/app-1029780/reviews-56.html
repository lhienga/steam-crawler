\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's fine but it will never be anything. Updates are mindbogglingly slow and add next to nothing of interest. It barely has the features that similar colony sims have at release. It's got no creativity in terms of what it wants to accomplish. I'd wait maybe 10 years and this will hold your interest for maybe more than one colony.

The gimmick is you can build vertically (like dwarf fortress) and so can make castle like structures. There is no real siege mechanic beyond forcing you to abandon your castle and sally forth (relax, not on a horse) to deal with trebuchets because you can't build those those. The structural integrity system is basic, roofing is basic (no idea how you are going to deal with snow accumulation with what you have set up), and taming/husbandry is basic.

Meanwhile the game is pre-occupied with how to trick you into running out of seeds through a convoluted farming system that adds functionally nothing to gameplay other than flipping a switch once that should be automated. This is the most innovative feature of the game and should be completely abandoned. At best set a "stock minimum" for seeds and have the AI reduce crop yield to generate a minimum of seeds, Nothing more complicated than that. Frankly even that is unnecessary unless you add vermin or events that destroy stock. The devs need to think about how a system is going to be a meaningful part of the game that the player pays attention to and not just check boxes.

You set up a castle but they have trebuchets so you have to abandon it
You set up some fields but did you toggle the get seeds button or try to min/max and forget to change it back so now they don't work until a merchant comes (there's a reason that only happens with cabbage on default... GOTCHA!)

See the theme? At best this is rock, paper, scissors. At worst this presenting you with a way to mitigate a problem with planning and infrastructures (good) just to take it away or hobble that feature (bad). This isn't an escalation treadmill though, it's simply you try to mitigate things and it doesn't work as good as you thought... End of adventure.

Lastly, ticking off taming and husbandry at this point is wildly premature. It's not even at the level of Rimworld and almost every other system is a copy paste of DF/Rimworld...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fantastic. The TLDR is that it's basically a 3D Rimworld. However, the experience is so much more. I enjoy Rimworld but always felt limited creatively. It does the best it can with a 2D top-down perspective, but there's only so much artistic expression you can do in it. Going Medieval completely eliminates most caps on creativity. Grand halls, fortified castles, bustling cities, they're all just waiting to be built. Just look at the community screenshots. There's so much satisfaction that's gained when you make a stone block cathedral that rivals Notre Dame, or replicate Notre Dame itself (though it's been done a few times already). And this isn't just cosmetic. Bowmen get bonuses for shooting downward so there's a strategic reason to build watchtowers and fortifications. Planning where to place your build is important. There's a tactics to it.

The cons? Well the game isn't perfect. The tech tree isn't expansive. There are a few bugs. The game is still in early access, so expect the same caveats that you would see in any other early access game. However, the community is great. Regular updates, player feedback, creative expression with screenshots. The discord group is filled with fun, knowledgeable people. However, I've been playing for just a few weeks and there's been so much growth already. I've never had a game-breaking bug and the tech tree isn't my main focus. It's similar to Minecraft in the way that there's no real end, just what you can imagine.

My recent addiction, this game is honestly great. By buying it you're not only getting yourself tons of game play potential, but supporting a dev team that are passionate and involved.\t\t\t\t\t
