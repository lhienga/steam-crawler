\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI highly recommend this game to intermediate and advanced players of colony sim games. Going Medieval is a fresh take on the genre, and despite being in early access it may surprise you.

I, however, don't recommend this game to beginners or first-time colony sim players as in its current state there are issues with AI that may irritate new players, especially those who aren't used to micromanaging a colony. It is not a sandbox, and there are challenges such as harsh weather and raids which may require you to 're-load' previous saves. On the plus side, you can adjust the difficulty before you start a new game or turn raids off completely.

There's a lot on the road map still but I've already gotten 10 hours of game-play out of this game and I'm not bored yet - I will probably stop playing once I've researched the full tech tree - and revisit the game when more of the developers road-map is reached. This includes raiding other settlements, mounted siege weaponry and having prisoners; features which are not yet implemented in the game leaving it feeling a little empty of things to do.

Building and trading are fun and the caravan system is miles ahead of Rimworlds system which I always felt was super buggy and difficult to use(just my personal opinion). This is also a very fun building game if all you want to do is make a massive and beautiful castle or a quaint farming village - there are enough features in the game to do that and I recommend going over to the community hub to see some of the amazing creations people have made.

For now, I'm giving this a YES, but only for people who have experience playing colony sim games(there are plenty of tutorials if you are still super keen!) - if you are a total noob I suggest trying out 'Banished' it's a good gateway game into this genre.\t\t\t\t\t
