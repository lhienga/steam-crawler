tl;dr: Great game as it is right now, worth the cash out. Looking forward to the full release!

I love colony sim games and Going Medieval just hit the mark for me. Let's put things aside early - yes, this game is pretty similar to Rimworld. But Going Medieval's setting and execution make it so that it has its very own identity.

In-game mechanics such as the ability to build vertically, the new fermenting system, the cold storage system, and the building structural integrity are just examples of some my favorite features of the game. The medieval setting makes it fun and challenging to plan these things out given that there isn't any modern technology to help.

The game isn't all perfect as it is right now (but it is expected given it's Early Access). In my opinion, the villager AI needs a bit more tuning cause they sometimes screw up their priorities. One of my villagers bled to death because he'd rather drink his ale than recover in bed despite recovery set as a priority. Other than this, I didn't encounter any other problem.

A key feature that make or break colony sim games is also missing as of writing; colonist interactions . But again it is currently planned out on their roadmap, so I look forward to that and the many more features to be rolled out.

I love this game as it is right now and surely as it's updated it would be part of one of my favorite colony sims of all time. I can't wait for the upcoming content updates. I'll be watching the progress of this game as it goes. Thank you Foxy Voxel for your take on the genre. Well worth the buy.
