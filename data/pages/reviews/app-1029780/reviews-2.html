\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRimworld, but medieval technology only, and in 3D.

Years and years ago I kickstarted a game called Timber & Stone, which was a colony sim game with a very freeform building mechanic. Sadly, it was abandoned. Years later, Going Medieval fulfills the missed potential of that game.

The ability to construct complex buildings is absolutely awesome for us wannabe architects. You can integrate buildings into land features (e.g. a castle built into a cliffside, or over top of a waterfall, or a walled city that can only be accessed via an underground tunnel, etc), you can do complex multi-level and even suspended structures. You can change and edit as you go, replacing and building on your existing structures as you unlock new building materials.

If you enjoy Rimworld, Stranded: Alien Dawn, or other colony sims, and want one that is set in a low-fantasy medieval world, Going Medieval is that game.

Cons:
It is definitely still early access, and certain features have the bare-minimum needed to say, "x feature is in the game". Hopefully as development progresses, we'll see more *content*.

Highly recommend, and I'm excited to see what else they add.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is quite fun, but it is optimized extremely poorly. I have an AMD Ryzen 7 1700 Eight-Core Processor 3.00 GHz (overclocked to 3.7GHz) CPU and an AMD Radeon RX 7800 GPU. With nothing else running on my computer and all in game graphic setting put to the lowest setting possible AND running the game in 1920x1080p I can BARELY break 30 fps (I can run almost any other game well above 60 fps). Now granted, this is on a world that has been running for ~3 in game years and not at the beginning of game. I have no idea what could possibly gobble up so much in this game because at a glance it does not seem to be intensive to run.

With that said, the game itself has been extremely enjoyable. It has a robust building system and a unique camera tool to assist with building on different z layers (like up and down relative to the ground), a detailed task time assignment system that allows you to automate almost any of your npc's jobs, and an engaging RTS style combat system featuring a multitude of different weapons. The game-play feels reminiscent of Rim World, but the building system paired with its medieval theme set it apart.

One aspect of the game that I believe is still lacking is any interactivity with settlements outside of your own, besides trade. It feels a bit silly to have a small bandit camp continually raid your settlement, without the ability to stop them in any way for the entire game. This is hopefully something that will be added as the game continues with its development, but in its current form it feels a bit lacking. There is an unlockable technology that lets you view your current region map. This area of the game ONLY allows you to trade with the other settlements, does not allow you to see the settlements (the entire interaction is done through the trade menu), does not allow you to attack any settlements, and generally feels underdeveloped. I can only hope the devs stop messing around with how water flows and create engaging content.

TLDR: Enjoyable game, but extremely PC intensive. Fun in many aspect, but some portions of the game feel as of yet underdeveloped. I would still recommend this game as it is worth the price, but if development slows or stops in the future, it may not be worth picking up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally making myself review this game. I'm honestly surprised how many hours I've poured into this game, and I'd say I've been following this game for awhile now. Anyone that knows me knows I am very much a medieval fan, and this game defs scratches that itch for me. I enjoy the building freedom (love doing an underground bunker run) and the visuals. While I agree with other reviewers that it can get quite laggy in late game, it hasn't been the worst for me. There are complaints that this has been in early access for a long time but for me that's fine, I have seen the work the people have put in and have been happy with the updates. What was big for me was the willingness to listen to the community. I remember a couple of years ago, before they added advanced customization on characters, asking if they would add that kind of feature in a twitch comment. At the time they said no but in the end it got added in. It's nice to see that.

My really only con is that I wish your colony had more people and that you didn't have to rely on randoms coming to your colony to get more people. I'm loving the addition of relationships between villagers and hopefully instead of relying solely on randoms coming into the camp we can had actual families. But it's not a con that stops me from playing, I think for what I paid, I got more than enough out of it and can't wait for more.

Anyways defs one of my fav colony sims\t\t\t\t\t
