The trick to surviving early attacks is dorky maze/trap designs or just kiting the insipid AI around while your lone archer slowly picks off stragglers till enough of them die.

It is also sadly way short of Dwarf Fortress on the personal interaction and mood aspects.

Very micromanagy to get simple things like food production prioritized. It's weird. As arcane as DF was about that, this game is worse.

I'll be happy to change my review if things get better, and regardless I wouldn't read this as a 1 on a 1 to ten scale. I think there are better games coming up though, and unless you are just a compulsive player of builder games, this one is not yet ready for prime time.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I quickly got hooked by this game upon purchase. I genuinely enjoy this game and i bought it at price. Very much medieval Rimworld but in my opinion does things a bit better (not everything though!), and makes the game refreshing and exciting. Building your log hut up to a impending fortress feels like a real accomplishment, and something you've actually worked towards not something simply handed. The minor complaints would be the limited crafting menu and options, there's quite a bit and I'm sure more will be added, however right now it feels small. There is also something to be said for the AI, they are very easily distracted by the 4 pyres I've built in the corners of the map rather than getting at where my good stuff is. The only other thing would be the trebuchets in the game. You can lose quite a bit to their random fire. I've found the only real counter to them is to leave your walls to go confront their force, it would be nice if the player was able to build them as well to give fire back.

TLTR: Love this game 8.5/10 AI is somtimes funny\t\t\t\t\t
