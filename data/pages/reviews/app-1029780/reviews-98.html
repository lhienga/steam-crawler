\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBuy Rimworld.
The trading is bad there is no money and often traders offer garbage and won't buy weapons and armor.
The combat is extremely basic but at the same time healing is nonsense at best. I had a settler die after tending to her wounds and letting her rest.. This settler died of a "serious bruised nose"
The building is also bare bones, If you are looking for a way to build a nice village, castle, or fort you'll find very few options for building material and style. The decor and leisure for your people is also really lacking with only 2 prayer sites and and some game table for your people to have fun.
The progression is unreasonably slow as you ONLY get settlers through random events that give you one settler and at least 5 enemies to fight each time. So taking somebody in can potentially set you back on your population count that is already so restricted.
The pathing is poor and you can really abuse it if you'd like by making tight paths for raiders to go through but it's not so fun when your people jump into a hole and cant get out.
If this game reaches it's goals it will still need a lot in the way of customization to really make your upstart feel like yours.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is noticeably in early access but playable.

The dev is active and the game has good bones - think medieval Rimworld with simple 3D graphics. Many features are still in development, which can lead to surprising mismatches in realism levels. As an example, you can set up a smokehouse in a basement and the worst that happens is it works a bit slower due to being indoors but, if your fermenting fruit is sitting at the wrong temperature, you will never have anything to drink.

Content is a bit sparse but there is enough to keep it interesting for a while. I look forward to seeing the additions from the developer and can't wait until the game is finished and easily modded. The potential is there and I have spent more on less.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game *IF* you like building bases and castles and that is the sort of gameplay you are looking for at the moment. That is, by far and away, the biggest draw of it. The options for what kind of base / village / kingdom to build are as limitless as your creativity.

Pros: Build super tall towers and deep underground! The z-levels aspect of base design is fairly novel within this genre of game. The backstories of the settlers are funny. Easy to suddenly sink 8 hours into mining out your basement food cellar lol. The devs are also constantly updating and adding new content which is great.

Cons: Zero settler interactions. I know this is coming up on the roadmap, so I'm trying to be patient, but I've had the game for a year now and have stopped playing because after a while it starts to feel empty without any story. However, plenty of management/building games have zero story to them (ie, cities: skylines and ONI), so if you don't mind that, then ignore this con altogether!

My only other beef with this game is that it does take a heavy toll on my computer (for reference my specs are Intel i7-8700, 16GB RAM, GeForce GTX 1660) and I can't play with the graphics turned up all the way without occasional performance issues, which is a bummer as the graphics are super pleasant (especially the shadows and weather) when cranked up.\t\t\t\t\t
