\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not very far in the game yet (restarted once already, after I've learned a lot), but wanted to write a review already anyway. I might update it later...

First off, I love that this game has a "peaceful" mode, where raids/fighting are turned off. I like to focus more on the economy part of colony building, so this is awesome for me! Especially after seeing how detailed and complex this game is so far, I guess fighting will also be pretty detailed.

I'm having a lot of fun with this game :-)
I've already run into a few bugs or weird things, but nothing game-breaking or too annoying so far. Nothing unexpected for Early Access.
There is an in-game bug report tool that's pretty convenient. Although it's not very clear what exactly is send. I can enter text and that's it. So I assume it's sending a save game. The latest one? Making a new one when I submit the bug? Is it also adding a screenshot? Or maybe only the screenshot? I don't know, I just hope it's useful for the bugs I reported, as describing it can sometimes be a bit unclear.
Content is good for Early Access I think, but I'm already looking forward to what else is to come. There's a lot on the roadmap still.

You create buildings by placing down walls, floors, windows and doors tile by tile. On ground level, higher up or below the ground. There are no pre-made buildings that you just place. When building multiple floors, you have to pay attention to stability. You also have to look out for temperature. Settler's mood will go down when they're cold for example. Food will rot when it's too warm or there's no roof over it, etc. Even opening or closing the windows has an influence on this! Next to of course fires and some production machines that also produce heat.
It takes some getting used to navigating between all the floors and placing things where you want.
This is a very interesting concept I think. I can see that this will become annoying to some people though.

You control people's schedules (when they work, sleep, have leisure time or can just do anything) and assign priorities for certain categories of jobs for each person. They don't have fixed jobs, unless you set it like that. And even then, they won't just do one thing on one machine, but are responsible for a whole category of jobs. E.g. "cooking" could be cooking meals at the campfire, but also making fermented juice (which will turn into alcohol after some time) and some other things. Depending on which resources are available, how you set up your production places etc. You can choose to produce certain amounts of certain products or just produce endlessly.

You can tame animals and also train them. Although I don't know if that works with all of them, I've only had trained dogs and untrained chicken and cattle so far. You train the dogs to transport things, which is very handy.

I think this is a game for people that want to be able to control a lot of small details, build very freely and be creative. Somehow the building part reminded me of a medieval Sims. Less colors, less fancy choices, but a lot of freedom. If you don't want to bother setting up schedules, giving job priorities, caring about room temperature etc., better skip this game.\t\t\t\t\t
