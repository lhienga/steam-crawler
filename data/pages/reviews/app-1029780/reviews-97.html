\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is awesome. It has a ton of potential. There are definitely tweaks that need to be made for a better user environment.

I.e., When clicking on a settler from the side menu there should be a queue of tasks that they are doing. When you set a priority task it should go to the top and you should be able to cancel others. This is not the case and you can never really tell what people are doing beyond the half cut off text under their respective names.

The game needs updates/patches, but I believe it can turn out to be a fantastic city builder game. For what it is, I would definitely recommend it. I bought this game at full price so hopefully it gets a ton of dlc and other fun things added to it within the next year. Sooner would be great but progress takes time.

If you are on the fence about getting it, just know it is only $25 and can be returned within 2 hours of gameplay. You start off pretty confused about how to get things going, but 2 hours should give a rough idea of what the game is about.

I don't know how far you can get before it gets boring to play though. Once you unlock all the buildings and objects (which should take a fair chunk of time) you basically just live through seasons and raids. This is where I am hoping for more content. Maybe the settlers decide to move to a new area or some settlers branch off to make a new settlement and you can control both. Who knows how far this game could go. I hope the devs keep up with the game and listen to their money suppliers for feedback and actually utilize it.

-Cheers\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an early release, so you will find bugs, but overall I love this game. Very resource management/sim style. Yes your settlers don't have a single working thought between them and you have to manage their tasks, but if you need a beautifully detailed graphic game which allows you to build an old world settlement from the basics, this is your game, (The TREES!!!!) The combat is basic, point-click and attack, but you really start to care about these stupid villagers (Dammit Aiden, GO TO SLEEP). To be clear, you do not HAVE to micro-manage every aspect, but as you go along, you find that every single thing has different choices you make (check out that EDIT button and see what your choices are), and that can make you very successful. Recommendation - start with easiest mode and 'New Life'; the one that starts you with three settlers in the Spring. Keep at it until you've mastered that level (re-starting/re-rolling as needed) by never losing a settler, or making a huge building mistake. Once you've made your mistakes and learned from them, move up in difficulty levels. Do not expect to succeed on hard mode 'Loan Wolf' as a newbie. Oh, and yes, the damn dogs do NOT live long enough.\t\t\t\t\t
