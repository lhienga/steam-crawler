\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has been such a pleasant surprise. TLDR fun, chill, rimworld inspired settlement builder.

Some of the mechanics resemble a lot of Rimworld's, which I love. Each character has a name and stats and traits. Makes them all unique and interesting. I'm happy with the job priority system, It's easy to navigate and seems to have been working much better with the recent patches. Characters aren't able to have families, which feels like a miss in my opinion. I think families and family trees would add a lot to the roleplaying aspect.

The building is very much freestyle. Each room must be built from scratch. It takes little time to get used to and proficient with the building tools. I do wish there was an option to upgrade or change certain items like walls, without having to deconstruct first, and building in an unoccupied space. Unless of course I missed something, it seems like an obvious QoL to have. Overall, building is simple in design and allows room for much creativity.

I wish there was a tip explaining that not having clay is not the end of the world and I can make up for it with limestone by changing the type of building material. If there is such a tip, I missed it. I was stressing.

Combat is surprisingly enjoyable. I found attacks on my settlement, from bandits and such, a true call to arms. I find the parameters I'm able to set for which equipment certain characters should or shouldn't use and even the quality of said equipment to be satisfyingly detailed. While overall it's decent, I do find the attacks to become stale toward the later stages of my playthrough. I wish the encounters could be more organized at times with, perhaps, some sort of siege mechanics. Let's be honest, I would just love to dump burning pitch on the faces of the foolish invaders.

While I do appreciate the somewhat robust customization of each character, I wish those traits were more than just static buffs/debuffs. It would be interesting to see my boys display unique mannerisms based off their combinations of traits and interact with one another with their traits affecting the dialogue. I think that would add a lot more memorability to each settlement and the stories behind them.

I think the seasons are well implemented, and carry with them certain benefits and challenges. I enjoy fall the most, seeing leaves blow off trees, and generally enjoying the changing of colors as we make our final preparations for first snow. It's chill. In the winter time, I couldn't quite figure out how to get the homies to take a chill pill and hang out in the tavern instead of breaking themselves, slaving away in a blizzard, passing out in the snow. I didn't want to make too many changes to their custom routine I set up for fear of forgetting later, I wish there was... a winter mode of some sort where the characters would prioritize staying warm, healthy and fed over other tasks without having to change their "normal" routine. There may be options I haven't clearly understood, I'll have to keep experimenting with that for now.

This has been a relaxing break from faster paced, more competitive games. I've enjoyed my time and will likely enjoy more. The patches have been improving my experience, and I'm patiently waiting for more content to drip down my dopamine tubes.

At the end of the day, I would like to remind that this is in early access. On one hand there is potential, on the other a myriad of examples of how similar potential got squandered. I recommend you take the early access into consideration and be weary. I would also recommend you try this out if the gameplay sounds compelling, as I think that within the refundable play time you will quickly realize whether this game will appeal to you or not.

But yeah. Thanks for reading.

IGN 10/10 would accidentally forget about a worker stuck on the roof and let them starve again.\t\t\t\t\t
