\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have stopped playing this game for long enough to write a review.

After, I will play more.

I am a huge Rimworld fan and also really enjoy Kenshi - this game feels like those games got together and had an amazing game baby. Building feels easy, resource gathering can be difficult but not grindy, feels simple to play at first (to a Rimworld player anyway) but there is really a lot going on.

My favourite is the combat. I love that anxious feeling when the raid warning sounds and you see a bunch of people on the outskirts of your map. Then you wondered why you were concerned as you watch them run through your gauntlet of papercutty death and laugh as your archers finish them off.

That or you lose your best runner and then you get mad and reload and now you have a drink to clean up. But honestly that was just the one time.

Are there bugs? Yeah, there is this one very annoying thing that is happening where the settler will check access to an item ONCE at the start of a job, and if the job is unable to be completed the item is flagged as inaccessible for the rest of the game (seemingly). This can be countered if a change is made to the item. The easiest and most basic thing is to forbid the item then unforbid it which seems to force the game/colonist to do a second check on it, which now they are able to do the job so the job is completed without issue.

There is also a kind of hauling bug where it seems a bit like colonists don't want to haul crops into my cooler but that's being looked at as well.

Most colony sims I've played inevitably end up being some kind of monster balancing act between supply chains and population, and then at that point they haven't got much else to offer. This isn't that game. I'm very much looking forward to seeing where Going Medieval goes once a few more checkmarks are put on the road map. Of the $30 games I've bought in the past year, this is the one I regret the least.\t\t\t\t\t
