\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot bad, not bad at all - that is, really great! I am in the process of building a viking-esque long house, but you could equally make quaint villages or ginormous castles. There are no limits once you understand the stability mechanism.

Like many others, I adored Rimworld but found it rather flat. Going Medieval takes the mantle and in many areas delivers.

The game is still early access so it is lacking all of its features, I am particularly looking forward to the prisoner system (I still remember an old game of Rimworld where I caught one of the enemy and tried to turn him, but it didn't work, he murdered other settlers years later.) In anticipation for a prisoner system, I have built an underground dungeon.

I hope there is some impact on the raids or faction relationships - if you have an enemy prisoner you should be able to prisoner swap or get raids who target your cells to free prisoners. I also hope that the enemy can capture your settlers and ransom them, or you can liberate them via raiding.

I do also wish for more building pieces like fence types and basic 150hp internal doors. That said, the building potential is already huge - that little wooden beam is truly revolutionary. I have to say the game is sometimes just beautiful in a way Rimworld never was - the textures, the environment, the 3D design, modelling, just sit together so beautifully. The landscape has true seasonality.

I hope to see better interpersonal relationships between settlers, and children. If the settlers will die, there should be dynasty. Settlers should also catch illnesses more often overall like flus.

There is also no obvious way to impact on the number of settlers, i.e. greater influence attracts more people looking for a safe place to live. It feels like you get 2 new settlers every year which is rather linear.

Also hardest difficulty is not that hard. Trebuchets are easy to defeat. Enemy AI overall is not very clever, and doesn't have much of an attack plan. The enemy could protect its trebuchets better, for a start. They could seek and destroy my archers and tanks.

Since the game is early access I may stop playing for now and wait for more updates but definitely see this game as top tier and many more happy hours of gameplay to come. I have reserved it for the Christmas holidays, and you can't give higher praise than that. It is an awesome colony sim with a lot of potential for strategy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would have given this game a thumbs up if it continued the path it was taking in the early days, but it seems every time something new is added something breaks and becomes worse.
In the early days job priorities worked in the main for your villagers, but now in the main they have no logic to the things they do or prioritise, walking round naked in winter when winter clothes are available. Ignoring building even though that is their key job to just farm when it is the lowest priority on their list. It gets bloody frustrating having to literally micro manage EVERY action and even then they still ignore some.
The raids are pathetic as well, the AI does zero, except charge the raiders in and they then just start smashing up stuff set up outside, fences funeral pyres. You build a fort they attack the door after attacking those oh so important funeral pyres, then they either lose (you kill enough they run away) or win, sometimes without destroying or taking anything. Now when the trebuchets come out that is even better. They destroy walls internal and external even underground but do your villagers repair them if destroyed, nope you have to search for all the walls and items destroyed and fix them yourself.
It had so much potential but is rapidly becoming pure grind with zero enjoyment.\t\t\t\t\t

They feel far too easy and trebuchets just end up being a minor annoyance more than an actual threat. I would love to see more siege weapons incorporated like rams, towers, ladders, bridges. I think having the enemy lay down their own merlons or small wooden defensive structures would be cool as well. Almost like they are setting up a full on siege, I would also prefer if the ai had to assemble the trebuchets giving you some time to interrupt them before they start hitting buildings. Just more interactive raids would be a really cool addition and I feel like the raids are underwhelming at the moment. if the raids could also build and were setup like actual sieges in the late game I think that would be dope. Other than that though the game is really fun, especially the layered building.\t\t\t\t\t
