\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game in the making. I'm writing this with 93 hours and am currently on my 2nd playthrough.

The combination of game play nearly identical to Rimworld (but not half as deep) and the 3D view similar to Banished is a smart combination. In my first game the lessons came hard as there aren't as many opportunities to recover from a mistake. I also learned that if you choose certain starting zones (Valley) you don't have access to the more advanced resources like iron, limestone, precious metals. With few trading options I started a 2nd game.

Right to the point, I've maxxed out all the research and still have not developed relationships with traders to the point that I can exchange weapons or armor. I dug a moat around my starting base and after 60+ more hours have yet to really have a challenge with any raids. At this point I'm struggling with a reason to keep playing other than designing a fortress for it's own sake because there isn't any real risk of raids that can challenge you, trading is too infrequent and often not useful, and the bugs where settlers don't perform assigned tasks have just made me want to take a break.

edit. I just upgraded to the experimental version and started over on Survival diffulty. Wish me luck!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor early release, Going Medieval is fairly enjoyable. With this game, think Alien Dawn in the Dark Ages. There are quite a few tools and charts in GM that Alien Dawn uses, so if you're familiar with latter you'll get into the former without too much issue.
PROS
- Building is fairly seamless, with only a few oddities to work out. Lots of different design options, and changes can be made to design without having to tear down pieces.
- You can dig and build cellars! Not sure what the limit is as to how far you can go, but you can play like mole-men if you like and just live down underground from what I'm seeing.
- It still needs some work and additions, but the tech tree is fairly engaging and logical. It feels good, opening up new tech and playing with the items it reveals.

CONS
- AI needs lots of work. Villagers get stuck when there's obvious paths, and they dig themselves into corners.
- Holy hell, if you want to avoid frustration and heartache, AVOID GETTING CHICKENS. I've had a lone fox appear on the edge of the map and immediately sprint straight for my chicken pen. Ran right past a villager who attacked and knocked off half its health, ignored the fact that it'd been attacked, and focused entirely on killing that one chicken before the villager could take it down. Foxes and polecats become guided missiles for chickens, and it leads to no END of frustration. If you're going to raise those birds, I recommend making a chicken fortress first!
- Tutorials are sparse and there's no real story or goal beyond 'build up a settlement and survive' from what I've seen. It would be great to at least have missions early on that guide you through how to do things, and missions later that encouraged trade or faction (Haven't built the map table yet, so perhaps the latter is there). Hopefully that sort of thing is added down the line.

In all, I'm enjoying myself beyond the chicken thing. For an early access, I consider this one to get.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove this game.

After quickly putting in 25 hrs over the Thanksgiving holiday, I can say that Going Medieval is my favorite colony sim game to date. Yes, even over Rimworld (I haven’t played DF). I recommend GM as a must-buy for anyone who likes these types of games.

The vibe gives me a lot of nostalgia from older RTS’ and colony sims. That 3rd dimension really does a lot for me. I play mostly on normal speed and it’s just a joy to watch things unfold. Some of the features and mechanics feel more simplified than a RimWorld and I'm into it. Also, the roadmap is on-point and I can’t wait for this game to implement all of those great remaining features.

With that said, ‘tis the holidays and my wishlist contains 1 major feature that isn’t on the roadmap, along with a couple of minor ones.

Marriage and Family:

It hurts. To build these wonderful stories with your settlers, only to see them dying alone with no offspring to look after the family farm 😢. I know FV has said they’ve “opted out” of having children… While I understand their reasoning, I find it leaving a large hole for anyone who looks to these games for story building and role-playing. Think about all the juicy chat-logs about new flings or bickering old exes. Or that unexpected pop-up when Orsic finally ask’s Molle to marry him! This creates more overall depth and changes player decision making. Strengthening settler narratives = deeper relationship with game’s content. Not a bad thing. Well, maybe for sleep schedules. I know children can be tricky in gaming, but I have a few suggestions for workarounds:

Child Combat: Or lack-thereof. Obviously, nobody wants that…(right?)… Children should have very limited amount of “jobs” and should not be able to engage in combat, as well as be attacked. However, there should be a risk. This ties into the future ability to raid other settlements/factions. What if, during a raid, its possible that enemies may target your children to capture and escape with. If successfully captured, you have 3 options: A) Raid the enemy settlement and save your hostage. B) Pay the enemy faction a hefty ransom to free them. C) After some time passes (A season? A year?) the hostage converts and becomes a member of that faction or bandit camp. This doesn’t harm the child and brings in a new level of stakes. I understand that child hostages could be worrisome, but nothing ever actually happens to the child. Either they are saved or convert. Ok, maybe some negative mood spikes, but that’s it.

Population Issues: Is there a hard cap? If not, I assume one would be needed if reproduction is in the game. Maybe even a separate child cap. One way to solve these population issues is to have the ability to create multiple settlements around the map and designate your settlers accordingly. Not sure if that is planned or in-the-works, but if raiding settlements will be added, we might as well be able to strategize expansion and control territories. I’d love to cozy up next to those Kingdom of York blokes.

Aging: This one is tricky for me. Not sure how this one gets tackled, but maybe we accelerate their aging process to like 2 yrs a season (2.5 yrs total)? I know the parents ages won’t align, but maybe that’s not a big deal. All that matter is that you see that “(Son)” or “(Daughter)” or “(Sibling)” tag next to their names in the relationships tab.

Again, kids should be limited in what they do. Eat, Sleep, Study, Play. Just like the good ole days. As for skills, I see a blank slate with predetermined passions and character traits based off parents and randomness. Through learning specific studies, by the time they turn 18, they would have the normal number of skills as a newbie settler. Maybe more.

Minor wishes:

Horses - They were around back then, right? I see how balance could be an issue, but cavalry can be countered with other cavalry, pikes, spears and arrows. I’m sure they would be a rare and very expensive commodity.

Celebration & Events - It would be fun to see the little peeps party once in a while. Maybe close friends gather at the pub for a birthday? Idk, sounds like fun.

DLC - This is more of a question. Do they plan on releasing DLC after full game release? I could see some of these features coming in the form of DLC and would prefer to see at least the Family/Marriage feature in the base game.

I know mod support will come later, but I’d really like to see that Family/Marriage stuff come to the base game.

With all that said, this game is a 4.5/5 for me. Amazing as is and will only get better upon full release.\t\t\t\t\t
