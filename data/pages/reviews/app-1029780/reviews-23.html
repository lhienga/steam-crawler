\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing Medieval, is one of those City Building / Colony Sim type games that is more than likely in most Gamer's Top 10 games of all time list. If it didnt make that particular list, then it would definitely be on every Gamer's Top 5 City Builder / Colony Sim Games of all time List. Im pretty sure some people will be like, "This game isnt on any of my gaming lists, Im not big in that type of Gaming Genre, or The Big Kicker of All, Ive never heard of that title or Never played that title.

Well, in my opinion, if this Game doesnt make it in the Top 10, 5, or Top 3 of any gaming list that Gamers keep. Then, whoever you are that makes that list, you are not a true Gamer or you would have to be a part, part, part-time Gamer. LOL
Anyways, Enough of my lame jokes, Puns, Pushes, or just outright bombing on other Gamers. It's time to move on to the actual review of this Wonderful Game, GOING MEDIEVAL !!!!!!

Going Medieval, is a really fantastic Game and if you are a big fan of the City Builder, Colony Sim, Survival City Builder, and the Medieval City Builder Genre. Then, you dont know what your missing out on by not owning this game. Also, If you are a big fan of the following games like Rimworld, Frostpunk, Noble Fates, Dwarf Fortress, and Banished. Then, there is basically a 150% chance that you are going to outrageously love this game title either as much as the games listed above or if not MORE!! This game has brought home a great deal of what us Gamers love, when it comes to this Multi-Genre Categorical Title known as Going Medieval.

The Developers of Going Medieval, Foxy Voxel and The Irregular Corporation (Makers of PC Building Simulator), have made a pretty top notch game which was released into Early Access on 7/1/2021. From the start of this Title being released into Early Access, the developers have been NON-STOP releasing updates, bug-fixes, new features, creating & releasing DLC Sized updates for free, and The most glorious part of all, the Devs have never left their fan base in the dark for months upon months on end when it comes to the games updates/new features.

Every Monday, the Devs post a bulletin on Steam that showcases what the Dev teams have been working on within the Game, the Dev Team Goes into great depth about any new features being added to the game on the next update, The Dev Team Gives proper explanations on why certain features couldn't be implemented in the game, and the Dev Team always reviews the Fan base's ideas when it comes to what features we would like to have added t the game. The Devs 100% make this game even greater than it already is because of their outright loyalty to their fan base. These two companies not only made a great game for all of us to enjoy but they also showed us how other game companies should be acting towards the Fans of their games. Foxy Voxel & The Irregular Corporation, set the bar pretty high when it comes to customer service and just honestly releasing an outright amazing Title.

The game itself, is in a bright Voxel Type art and the overall aspects of the graphical output of this voxel art style is just downright amazing. Everything in the Game world is balanced perfectly, looks great, and Plays at a smooth 60 to 110 FPS (Depending on what type of gaming Rig you are using).

Going Medieval, in my opinion, is kinda like playing Rimworld in 3D Voxel Art and while using all of the Medieval Overhaul Mods that can go together. The reason I keep comparing this title to Rimworld, is because you are basically playing a game where you will be creating a Colony, Creating you Colonists, Picking a Procedural Generated Map using a seed system, and then you are doing everything you can to help manage your colonists Survival Needs.

Like I said above and Im sure I said it a few to many times. If you are a huge Fan or even just a regular fan of this Genre type of games. Then you should 100% be purchasing this game to add to your collection and I will promise you that purchasing this game will never come back to you as a regret.

This Game is one of those 1 in 1,000000 games that will be played for many many years to come.
