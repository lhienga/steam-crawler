\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe easiest way to describe Going Medieval is "Low Tech 3D Rimworld."

If you've played Rimworld, a lot of the features and mechanics are going to feel very familiar to you. On the other hand, if you've played Rimworld a lot of quality of life features are noticeably absent here. It impossible not to make comparisons with so much inspiration clearly being taken from the game.

With that being said, at this stage it's much less feature complete than Rimworld and there's still a lot that needs to be added before it's truly compelling. On top of that, there are some incredibly frustrating issues also holding the game back. I think the game is more good than bad, so I want to start with the good:

BUILDING
Unlike Rimworld, with the transition to a 3D environment, this game places far more emphasis on the intricacies of building. You can make buildings that are multiple stories, encircle your town with 30 foot high walls, set up elaborate gate houses, dig cold storage cellars under your settlement, etc. You can build a massive elaborate castle, a modest wood and wicker village, a stone fortress carved into a mountain, etc. This is a great game if you're a more creatively inclined individual. Your settlement can focus more on functionality or aesthetics, but the latter is much more fun - especially considering it's the real meat of the game currently.

Theres even structural stability mechanics you have to worry about. Different materials have different structural stability modifiers, and even different insulation values. This is what propels the game and kind of overshadows the rest of the mechanics that feel comparatively lacking. This is the reason to play this game.

Sadly, there are definitely a lack of block shapes currently that hamper the building slightly. You can still do a lot with what you're given, but I'd like to see more variations.

RAIDS:
Raids are actually a mixed bag. Because of the setting, there are relatively limited enemy/weapon variations, and they largely play out the same way after a while. They get stale pretty quick.

However, with your ability to make incredibly elaborate defenses in a 3D environment and the inclusion of siege weaponry, it certainly elevates raids by a significant margin. It's not enough that I'd say the raids are good, but the groundwork is set for this to be something great one day.

Okay, now I want to talk about the bad. This is actually a much longer list, but it's not enough to make this a bad game by any means.

AI
This is the biggest issue this game faces. While the 3D perspective is the games biggest boon, it's also It's biggest hurdle. Simply put, settlers have an incredibly hard time properly pathfinding and prioritizing tasks, especially when you have too many things queued up. This is particularly bad when it comes to constructing and mining.

Construction workers will travel across the map to pick up 20 logs, run back, build 4 walls, and then do it over and over. Miners will trap themselves or run away from the patch of dirt they're currently mining to a different patch of dirt for seemingly no reason. I have no idea how the prioritization of these tasks is handled, but it's frustrating to have to micromanage because of how senseless it seems.

The micromanagement is made even more frustrating by the lack of ability to shift queue actions. This is something that needs to be in this game. I'm not sure the devs realize how much the lack of a shift queue hurts the game when it's so reliant on micromanagement because of the AI.

TRADING/ECONOMY
Trading is incredibly half baked right now. There are a handful of things that are actually useful to buy from traders, and the prices for buying and selling make little sense. It's hardly even worth bothering with the mechanic as a whole. Additionally, by the second year I essentially had an unlimited source of income because beets are so absurdly lucrative to sell for some reason and are harvested in massive quantities.

SETTLER PERSONALITIES/SOCIAL
This is another really big weak point, especially in comparison to Rimworld. In Rimworld you have complex social interactions and clashing personality traits. Colonists have unique needs that can often be very tricky to accommodate. If they aren't accomodated, mental breakdowns can occur and colonists can wreak havoc on the settlement. It's a fantastic system that adds a lot to the roleplaying element of the game.

Going Medieval has some perks, but they are so mundane in comparison. Everyone likes everyone else for the most part, has the same needs, is fine with sharing a communal room, etc. this is so watered down it may as well not be in the game at all.

EVENTS
Another pretty significant downgrade from Rimworld. Again, the setting inherently limits events, but there needs to be way more variety here. These are almost entirely relegated to weather events and raids. That's it.

In general, this game lacks a lot of personality. Again, this is inherently limited a bit by the setting, but more needs to be done to make this game feel more dynamic and lively. Your settlers, (and those of other factions as well,) end up feeling a bit more like automatons than people.

And finally:

MODS
While it's not really fair to list this as a negative since it's not necessarily the fault of the developers, it needs to be talked about, especially considering how huge of a role mods play in Rimworld.

Devs are apparently working on proper mod support, but I just think people to know that, currently, there are very few mods out there for this game and they largely only change some small QoL things. Hopefully it develops a healthy modding scene in the future when mod support is fully integrated, but right now there's really not any particularly noteworthy mods available. This is important because this game is going to live or die based on the quality of mods that come out in the future.

Overall, this is a pretty good game that I hope develops into a great game over time. There's such a strong foundation, but it feels like the outline of an experience rather than a complete package. I'm really hoping once the fundamental systems are in place they ramp up the content, but there's unfortunately no guarantees for a developing product.

All that being said, even if this game were to stop active development right now, there'd still be a decent amount of enjoyment to be had for people who are interested in the building aspects of the game. Just don't expect something with the same complexity as Rimworld, as Going Medieval is a pretty far ways off from that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has potential, but In current state, I can't recommend it. As you see I have spent quite some time in this game, but honestly, I think most of what I played in past couple of weeks I would give them commands, click in the menu that it operates in background, then I would go to surf on the internet, and check from time to time. So what's the problem?

You can build a huge city, but for whom? I always start, but when I finish the center so to say, I see that building everything else makes no sense, because if you are lucky you will reach 20 players. You can build many shapes so to say, and that's fun, but usually my village has 12 - 13 members.

Game is not optimized. Many times I have read some people writing buy a better machine. Listen people, this isn't some AAA game built on a newest engine. You will come to one point when it will just start lagging, and this happens before I even start building the town properly. I play much more demanding games without a lag.

AI is useless and schedule doesn't mean anything. So you will put them on straight schedule, but they will go to pray during work time, they will go to work during fun time, hit a rock 2 times, and then go back for entertainment, I would put 4 hours of fun, character would do 1h, then go hit a rock, then come back to fun times. So I don't have to check on in every minute, I square a huge portion for them to mine, logically they should start at the nearest point. Nope, will dig one node, and then move to completely other side to mine another node. Then idiots will dig everything around them and get stuck on one node. So much time is wasted on that and then you have to micromanage every villager and it is not fun. You will have to check on everything they do, and that becomes a hassle when you have like 8 villagers. So for example, you will tell them to craft, if you don't turn off haul, they will make a brick, then they will carry it to storage, and then come back make another one, and so on. So every time I want to craft something, I have to turn off haul. For example, I will put craft on level 1, which is highest priority, haul on level 5, which is lowest priority, doesn't matter, same thing happens. In one moment you won't have enough resources, you will send them to cut wood, they will cut wood, take that wood and go to continue constructing. So you will have to put every time construction to 0, or prioritize cutting. This isn't something that happens every 2 - 3 hours, this is through what you go the whole time. So instead of them doing assigned tasks, and me focusing on building the town, I have to micromanage. Or what you can do is prioritize first gathering of resources, but with everything that happens in the game, it's a hassle also. So people will work normally, then at one point people will go to work, do one cycle of working, then just stand there for like 1 minute, then do another one. Then I would prioritize one task, someone would continue working normally, with others I had same problem. Highly inefficient.

You can't control where your trained animals can eat, you can build stuff for that, doesn't matter.. So they will eat your crops and everything that they can find in storage or on the map, which can become irritating.

I wish I could give a positive review, because there is something addictive in this game, but every game I start it ends in frustration. When you start, it's all fun, but moment you need to start expanding a bit is when problems start. This is the third year of early access, and I understand it is a small team, but I just can't recommend it.

I actually bought this game several months ago, but haven't gotten around to playing it until the last few days. I have definitely enjoyed it so far, just having finished my first playthrough. The game borrows heavily from Rimworld, which is a pretty good place to start. I think Rimworld is the most polished and definitely the most balanced of the colony sim genre, so I think these devs were smart to emulate it. They have basically taken the basics of rimworld and made it 3d. Sure the setting is medieval England, but that doesn't seem to change much in the core gameplay.

I will probably update this after another playthrough and a few more updates, but so far I'm excited to see how this game develops.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing this on an M1 Macbook Air for a bit and the game runs smoothly in the apple game porting toolkit.

While they haven't yet, my hope is the developers go ahead and take the few steps needed to make this official and add native macOS support.

I'd still recommend an M2 with > 7 GPU cores since my M1 is pegged with 8 GPU cores, but it still plays smooth - with just one minor issue; occasionally temperatures go all wacky in the game, going as low as negative 2 million degrees and as high as ~1.8 million positive degrees. if I save, quit and reload, that fixes the issue. I suspect it's a very minor thing like using a directx method or something to calculate the manhatten distance or something for temperature drop off.

Either way, if you like Rimworld, and always wanted to see how it would work in a 3d setting, this game is definitely for you, regardless of your platform. It still has bugs in early access, but it is definitely playable for hours and hours.\t\t\t\t\t
