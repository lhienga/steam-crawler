\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood, incomplete game, yet. It's just simply raw at this Q4 of '22.
Yes, it's still good. But after awhile, you realize you don't get what you had in Rim World (yes, this is kind of a copy of it).
Those automation-management tools to work on more leveled management.
Not even close.
it's a city building game with some parts of colony building. Just an opinion.

I assuming there are lots of things in road map already, here are details of the current game shape...

Reasons to avoid:
- if you need to get more less complete game
- if played at Rim World, especially with mods
Reasons to get:
- if you'd like bring some input to devs, like feedbacks
- if enjoy the type of game and would like to test something new by yourself
- you would like to get some fun in a medieval city building (it has huge potential)
- If you didn't play in RW :-P

Pros:
- 3D, multi-level
- Medieval age for funs (don't expect guns =) )
- Good looking
- (Great) City building
- Fights are promising
- Interesting research approach

Cons:
- raw hauling engine (RW isn't perfect at it as well, but there are excellent mods)
- no blueprinting (look at mods from RW, Captain of Industry - best with copy/paste, StoneHearth)
- poor managing engine (I do like a lot "managing desk" mod from RW)
- poor hauling engine (see mods from RW)
- poor production management (two meals per colonist, no haven't heard)
- some strange things like: "in a storage box you can put only 2 things" - what?! Ok, let it be. I'll update my JSONs, which probably will be overwritten after a next update... =)
- ...and bugs (there are no games without it, are they? :) ) but not often you can face them
- no mods (Workshop) in steam at this moment
- no mods management in the game (but community built some, find
GoingMedievalModLauncher)

RW didn't become a good one immediately.
I'd say community built it with mods, but not without vanilla course.

Wish successes to this project. Don't die like Stone Hearth.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing Medieval is a nice game to play on a lazy day. The graphics are simple but give enough definition to know what everything is, and the tutorial is informative and gives enough information to learn how to play the game quickly. G.M. is built around the same mechanics as Rim World but is different enough to make each game unique. I do wish however, that settlers could form a relationship and have children that grow up. With 260 hours play time I do recommend this game for anyone who has played Rim World but want a medieval twist instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI used to love this game but the addition of the trebuchet makes long term progression feel pointless. From a historical perspective it is laughable that wandering bands of raiders can somehow tow in trebuchet on wheels(historically a joke, trebuchets are massive and immobile and were constructed in place). Even if we disregard history it is not fun that raiders and bandits have long range missle type weapons that deal massive aoe damage when the players weapons are capped at longbows, it just feels helpless. Why would you spend so much time making a mechanic and then NOT giving it to the player in a colony sim? Lastly even though the community has been crying out for this to be changed or removed the devs went on vacation for a month and then returned talking more about pets and animals.... yeah.... they prioritized adding domesticated cats over balanced combat in a colony sim where raiding is a major mechanic. I wish i could give this game a thumbs up but I honestly feel like the devs have lost their direction with the game and what little changes are being made are on 'sidequest' level components. ie they are starting to polish a turd rather than fleshing out the core gameplay. I will continue to watch the development and hope things improve and maybe one day i can change this to a thumbs up; for now though i am sad to leave this a thumbs down.\t\t\t\t\t
