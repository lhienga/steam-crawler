\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll the videos and the advertisements don't do the game justice, because they focus on the faceless characters. When I first saw these ads they turned me off to the game, because how difficult is it to add some faces? It just seemed to make the game unpolished and it certainly wasn't a selling point, so I don't know why it is made so obvious.

It turns out that when playing, I don't notice that the characters are faceless.

There are plenty (!) of things to complain about in this game. For example if I want a moat dug around my territory, the worker assigned to this task will not dig them according to proximity - instead they will run in the most random fashion making sure they waste time running all over the map - until something inside them "clicks" and they start doing 1 after another again. Hopefully those sorts of annoyances will eventually be fixed.

This game has been compared to Rimworld only this is in 3D. Some of the path indicatons are borrowed (ahem) from Rimworld, so it has some resemblance. But I don't think it feels anything like Rimworld - this is more realistic and not cartoony at all.

There have already been plenty of positive reviews given, so I hardly thought it was worth the effort to write anything. But today I enjoyed playing it so much. I had been up until 2:30am last night playing - I couldn't stop, and then today I finally got to execute plans with fake "drawbridges" (which are just paths that use floors and a single unit of wall, and when the wall is destroyed by one of your units the "bridge" is completely destroyed). I had 3 of these "drawbridges" next to each other: 1) as the main entrance to the village (the fast path in), 2) the outlet of a long winding moat-surrounded path and 3) the entrance to that winding path. Above the path on the inside of the village was a 2 wall-high "tower" with floors at the top that I could place 2 archers on. After the bandits arrived on the map, I could destroy the number 1 "fast" entrance, and then I just had to wait for them to get on the windy path before destroying the others. They were wounded badly enough that there was no need to destroy them all - the plan worked almost perfectly, other than the annoying archer they had brought with them who had done some unwanted damage and almost killed someone. He was still easy to take out with the 2 archers in the tower.

https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2999006558

The life the people experience is fairly difficult. First of all - if I had to run for 30 minutes just to do some work and then 30 minutes AGAIN just to take a break... well that's insane. So the real effort in the game becomes figuring out how to organize the layout so workers can get to where they want to go quickly and efficiently, so they can enjoy the services they have worked so hard to set up.

The technology tree reminds me of Factorio (you know with the red, and blue and green "bottles"). Only here they are books, not bottles. But trying to get to higher technologies requires combinations of technology output.

Some anti-immersion features: there is no water apparently. I mean it rains, but there are no wells and the people complain they need something to drink, which to them means alcohol. Maybe that will change later.

Other features I think are great:

- Temperature calculations are very realistic, and food-rot will be affected where things are stored.

- People form relationships after interacting (usually positive - they'll become "friends" because they had a nice conversation.

- The cat seems to know how to feed itself eventually.

- Wild animals can be tamed with enough attempts at taming them.

- Winter gets brutal. Yeppers.

https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2998996284

I'm happy I tried it. It's a keeper.\t\t\t\t\t
