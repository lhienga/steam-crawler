\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would not recommend this game to anyone, not even to the RimWorld fans.

In the current state the game is, simply put, a compilation of not-so-vast array of unoriginal ideas and pretty much raw visuals (both in terms of graphics and UI). It as well lacks content, since there isn't really much to do except, maybe, build some square-y looking castles which you could as well build in Minecraft.

Even though the roadmap of this game's development seems quite intriguing (and maybe by the end of it Going Medieval would become a somewhat decent game), the developers don't really haste with it. The updates are scarce and they are not "impactful" - there has yet been nothing added that made me think "wow, that's cool - I'm gonna launch the game and check it out!".

All of that has not been as much problem for me as the next issue, though. The main reason why I write this review as a negative one is because this game lacks any original ideas. All of it, and I mean completely all of it, is just RimWorld. There is not a single unique or distinctive thing about this game that make it (or would potentially make it) better than any other colony sim. There are only two things that may be somewhat interesting - a 3D world which doesn't really make too much of a difference and... Yes. Black skinned folks in a """"historical"""" setting of 14th century England... Foxy Voxel surely do know what to prioritize in video game development.

Long story short - this game is an unoriginal, sparse, visually bland and lazy clone of RimWorld made by an SJW developer. Does this really cost 24.99$? I personally wouldn't say so. If you want a good, surprisingly unusual and even more surprisingly a very high quality medieval-ish colony sim - try Songs of Syx. It costs as much but offers tens times more. And I'm not joking, try this game - it's probably the best colony sim I've played so far. Much better than a piece of raw code which is Going Medieval.\t\t\t\t\t
