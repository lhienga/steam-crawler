The difficulty seems good enough, it can be punishing. If someone does not run home during a raid in time, they are just dead, nothing you can do about it. This can happen if a raid arrives same side as the one you build on.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is really a fantastic game, and I'm excited to see its continued progress.

If you played Rimworld, you'll already have a good sense for how the game works. Some of the primary differences are the era of technology and the 3-D, voxel nature of the world. But building above ground or into a mountain work similarly, aside from the presence of levels. Navigating each level (either a building floor or layer of terrain) is simple, using a button to shift up and down (very similar to "The Sims").

Overall, the game is very accessible and easy to learn. The creative potential is highly rewarding to explore, and protecting your slowly expanding underground city, sprawling mountaintop castle, or riverside fortress is a rare experience presented with an excellent UI and serviceable, stylized graphics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't hate this game at all and this review shouldn't stop anyone from buying it. I absolutely love Rimworld and this is basically a 3D Rimworld, a lot simpler, but very fun. There are just 2 things that make it unplayable for me, and I really, really hope they'll be addressed some time.

The first is priorities. You can't make one job an emergency. I spend too much time changing the priorities in the work tab, something I don't have to do in Rimworld. You can select a person and right click an object but that's just tedious to do.

The second problem is what prevents me from going back to the game at all. Having the game in 3D is great, but the whole layer system just makes me cry.
If your shelter has a basement (a necessity in order to store food), it doesn't matter what layer you are actually on, the camera will bounce down into that basement every time it moves over it or anywhere near it. Which means ALL the time. That camera bouncing is so frustrating and pointless it just breaks the game.
Also, to get from floor to floor, you can't just go up or down stairs. There's in infuriating "layer" system that forces you to peel the walls down gradually (so you're either clicking endlessly on arrows or rolling, and rolling, and tediously rolling your mouse wheel to see what's going on in the annoying basement or upstairs).
But the worst of all is that when you're on the right level (at last), the level above stays visible in transparency which makes no sense whatsoever. So you can't even see what you're doing properly, because you're looking at a room you want to see, through another room you absolutely do NOT want to see, but have to (why, just why?).
The entire layer system really needs to go, and if it does, this will be a tremendously fun and relaxing game to play.\t\t\t\t\t
