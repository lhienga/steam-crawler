\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a wonderful game that has one glaring, pervasive, and unresolved problem: pathing. This problem is insanely frustrating for a number of reasons, but a basic rundown would be like this: you have a chasm, a pile of wood and a couple people on one side, and a bit of land on the other side of the chasm. you can make a bridge piece by piece from the side with people and wood just fine, but if you want to break it apart, your pawns will often have to run ALL THE WAY AROUND THE MAP TO THE OTHER SIDE OF THE CHASM WITHOUT TOUCHING THE BRIDGE FOR NO REASON!!! This is even more frustrating when it only becomes an issue during a raid of some sort.
Another example of this is: if you have a 4x4 block of stuff that needs to be built, 9 times out of 10 your pawns will intentionally barricade themselves into a hole they cant escape from. This happens with any variation of building in which self-imprisonment is possible. Which forces you to even plan your settlement's layout painstakingly slowly, because your idiot bots will try to trap themselves incessantly for no reason.
There are many more ways in which the pathing is mind-numbingly infuriating, but those two are the critical ones that make playing this game go from 'hey im making a cool settlement yay' to 'wtf why do i have to spend 2 ingame days walking in circles to go somewhere the enemy ai can just walk over in a straight line???!? this game sucks' in about 5 seconds flat.

And this pathing issue has been present since it was first available on steam, so idk what the dev's plan is for resolving this 'minor issue' that has caused 8 of my 10 settlements to just die for no reason due to either starving to death or being murdered by raiders bc my pawns refused to break a single path block they were standing next to or on top of.\t\t\t\t\t
