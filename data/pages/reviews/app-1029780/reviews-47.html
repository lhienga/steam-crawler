\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis WOULD be a good game - but this is where the Steam review system fails because you can't "rate" the game. You have to give it a 100% thumbs up or a 100% thumbs down when most opinions should be a 1-10 rating or even a percentile number. This "social media" crap forces people to give carte blanche "likes" to games in a black and white manner - Steam ought to be ashamed of itself for being so sophomoric in its rating system.

I WOULD give this game around a 75% approval.

But the devs ruined the game with cold snaps. I hate cold snaps so much that I probably won't ever play this game again unless there is an option to disable them.

I've been playing computer games since there WERE computer games back in the 70s. And I can safely say that cold snaps in this game are easily within the top 5 WORST things I've ever seen in a game. Not because cold snaps are difficult - because there's nothing you can do about them. Which is why they suck so much.

Essentially, getting a cold snap means you put the game on maximum speed - then you go do something else for half an hour. Go have some sex. Make some food. Walk the dog. Go call your mom. But you won't be playing the game. Because there's nothing you can do.

Your settlers will just keel over unconscious the moment they go outside. You're liable to have almost every last settler lying around outside. You can easily lose your entire village through this stupidity.

All you can do is take your hands off your keyboard, off your mouse - and hope the AI doesn't get all of your settlers killed. Because YOU no longer matter. The game may as well play itself.

Until the devs fix the utter stupid trash that are cold snaps, the game isn't really worth playing unless you just like torturing yourself. I'm not saying that cold snaps are impossible to survive - no you can survive them. It's just a question of whether its really worth your time. For me, I'd rather being playing a game that doesn't try to kill your settlers for doing nothing more than leaving their ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ houses.

I'm only giving this game a "thumbs up" because Steam hasn't figured out yet that games are too complicated and subjective to saddle the players with an "all or nothing" review system. Reviewing a game shouldn't be as banal and vapid as "liking" a kitten video - but apparently Steam thinks that you either LIKE a game 100% or HATE a game 100%.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like RimWorld, but sci-fi isn't really your jam but you love the medieval period...

...have I got a game for you.

But that's not the only thing Going Medieval does well. It distills a lot of the complexity of games like RimWorld and Dwarf Fortress down to something a lot more manageable and dials back on the "losing is fun" ethos that animates those two genre titans. It even goes so far as to add a "peaceful" mode that's a bit too easy for my taste (you really can't do a Dwarf Fortress-like without something to keep you on your toes) but it's there if you want a more casual experience.

The best games in sprawling genres pay homage to the games that defined the genre they're in but truly carve out a place of their own that other games in the genre don't do.

So while the short version of this game is "RimWorld in the time of knights and damsels", that doesn't give it enough credit for forging its own path.

Anyway, point of all this is that the game's already very much playable and continues to get frequent updates throughout its long Early Access development.\t\t\t\t\t

First the good things: the idea of 3d building of a medieval settlement is really nice, and you become proud of your build (and sometimes even beautiful) structures.
but now the sad part: :-(
the temp. system keeps only warmth in, not coldness. so you have to dig like a mole to the ground to keep your food fresh. But the cellar had to be with dirt walls and twig floor, because of the random set heat isolation factors:
Walls: dirt 0%, wood 60% , clay 93%,stone 70% and stonebricks 75%
Floors: dirt 0% (but pushes rotting), wood 94%, twigs 40%, stone 70% and stonebricks 90%
Buildingactions: your settlers often fix themselves in a wall or digging all fields arround them and than starve on their created "Pile of idiocracy". I don´t wanna know how often they will burn themselves to ashes, when the feature gets implemented^^
terraforming: there often spawn some resources from digging through vains. and the settlers often dig from above. when the mats are spawning in the hole, and there is no path on the same ground lvl. Now you have to build a ramp or a pathway, to get the stuff away from there, because it blocks the filling of the hole.
Tasks: the settlers have only a small amount of "task-categories". So "cooking" means foodmaking, butchering, fermentation, smoking. But often your dead animals rots to pieces and you have no food, because high chief Jolanda is running thrugh the whole map to pick up some berries to make wine^^

I was really sad after I realized, how often I had to fix stupid behavior of my settlers.
But maybe, I just have to high expectations, because of my expierience of (modded) rimworld. I hope. the ultimate castle building/settlement simulator of my dreams will come true, when I test it aggain in a few months. I wish for the developers, which created a cool basement with enormous potential
