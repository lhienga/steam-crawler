\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview will very likely change with time. I think the game has a lot to offer. If you want to support a development team financially, buy it. Don't sink a ton of time into it or get yourself hyped until it's in a better state.

Rimworld with awesome vertical building features but 2x the micromanagement and not in fun ways but rather ways that force you to contend with the weird engine bugs/limitations. When the game is running smoothly, it's quite fun. The aesthetics are there. It feels like Rimworld with medieval mods, except you don't randomly get an occasional synth raid or bug infestation that is way too OP for your medieval settlers. Definitely missing the QoL that Rimworld has come to provide over the years, though for an EA game that's somewhat expected.

TL;DR: the game has a lot of frustrating bugs or "features" that do not appear to be seen that way by the dev team, who are looking to add new items, buildings, etc. The game is in EA so I cannot give it a thumbs down for bugs alone. Rather my thumbs down is because I think some of these things are design choices and not bugs.

The vertical building is great until you have a pile of dirt/stone in a hole. Example: I'm flattening the land near my base so it can be farmed. I am filling in holes with dirt. One of the holes managed to get a pile of dirt in it. I cannot reach the dirt from one level above. Fine, okay. So we'll dig enough around the hole so we can build a slope down just to retrieve a pile of dirt. Great. Got the dirt pile. Now destroy the slope so I can fill the hole. Nope, destroying the slope created dirt, which is now down in the hole again. Only option is to tunnel my way to the hole to pick up a pile of dirt to continue with my blueprint. A pile of dirt - not a full stack, but just 3 dirt - is going to set me back ~30-45 minutes.

Settlers will constantly get themselves stuck on surfaces or in holes. With some tasks such as hauling, they seem to have weighted priority per item to be hauled. But for building, it seems they will often opt to queue a job that is closest to them. So, if you ask your settlers to remove dirt in an area, they will remove the dirt closest to them and now do not have a way to reach the dirt furthest from them, instantly making the task impossible until you actually rebuild that tile for them to walk across. For this reason, you must specify a few blocks at a time and create an ideal situation for your settlers to work with, or else they will 100% make things worse. In some cases, this is fine and expected. But with very menial tasks it gets frustrating and time consuming to address, and happens almost constantly.

Enemies do not run for cover and will always attack doors. In combination, only one enemy pawn can attack a door at a time. For this reason it's virtually impossible to lose a fight if you're using archers properly. The enemies will stand there and die and will make no attempts to take cover. If they have an archer, he'll stand in the open and die, and then the entire raid is a failure. Like Rimworld, there are late-game sieges. I never have the patience to get that far in the game due to dirt piles, so I can't speak about them.

No urgent hauling or "focus on this" options. You can force a settler to prioritize an action but it's even worse than the Rimworld solution. If you tell a settler to focus on mining a node, he/she will walk up, mine it once (requires 4 times to break it) and then leave. Setting your settlers to focus on hauling by changing the job priority, you'll have to either go and forbid everything on the map (extremely tedious and an insane thing to ask of players) or you'll need to block your settlers from accessing areas by building walls and locked doors. Otherwise your settlers will traverse the entire map to pick up a dead rabbit that is 4 minutes away from rotting. I just need mining rubble cleared so I can build. It seems this is the last thing they will care about hauling.

Minor gripes (likely coming in the future):
- Pawns constantly change Z levels to build/deconstruct, making them unpredictable and difficult to keep from getting stuck.
- Can't hide stockpile zones. Very ugly and jarring to see. Also, stockpile squares look as if they are cave drawings. Visibility should be a toggled option.
- Can't enlist/draft to hunt mobs.
- No breeding/heirs. Especially disappointing in a medieval kingdom-focused game.
- As far as I can tell, no upgrades to tools. Mining speed is dictated by the pawns skill level and nothing else. Unlocking steel doesn't matter.
- Still no workshop support (which would immediately supply fixes to nearly everything here while devs iron them out in the core game and move it towards official launch).
- Extremely limited scenario options. Can't change frequency of events, time/effort/resource modifiers/multipliers (a few in the game options though).
- Traversing Z levels with the camera is obnoxious. There should be an option to turn on "snap-to scrolling" where I scroll from floor to floor in an ideal fashion rather than scrolling back and forth just to hit that small and magical sweet spot that lets me function/see properly. It looks cool to scroll in/out so linearly, but in what situation do I need just enough for level 3 visible to both not be able to see anything on it AND not be able to click on anything below it?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game!

This my very first post, so apologies if my comments are a bit random.

I'm only 100 hours in and just playing in peaceful mode to learn the mechanics of the game, but I've ended up being down the wonderfully soothing 'build my favourite town' rabbit hole side of the game.

There is so much enjoyment in testing and adjusting the internal layout of each building and your town, so that the settlers move around with smiles and thumbs up to their co-workers. The nuances to each aspect of the game are quite surprising and challenging to get just right. Fortunately, poor results in one aspect sometimes have positive outcomes in other skills:

E.g. I have yet to tame a boar..... but my settlers are getting VERY good at tending wounds!

As for game play, I initially struggled with farming. In particular, finding the alternative crops to sow. But the positive support within the steam forum was amazing and I would recommend you go there first if you are unsure of what to do.

I still haven't quite worked out how to effectively manage the job prioritisation panel, so I still end up with the odd settler working outside in a -20c gale, despite my best efforts to stop them. But that is the beauty of the game. It sets you challenges and there is a lot of pleasure in solving each puzzle.

I'm hoping you can glean from my post that I think this game is really enjoyable and has a really high level of repeatability. You really are only limited by your imagination.

Finally, I want to highlight and compliment the game developers. They have clearly put a lot of effort and love into this game and, unlike a number of other early access games, they are (at least so far) very committed to listening and responding to player input--with ongoing activity in the update space. Seriously, this is a lot of game for early access and what I consider to be a very reasonable price.\t\t\t\t\t

Original review:
I can't recommend this game as is.

Coming from Rimworld, this game is quite lacking, especially in QoL (god, hauling needs to be made better). It might be an unfair comparison, but with Rimworld right there in my library of games, it is hard not to use it as a sort of benchmark for games of this genre (especially games taking such strong inspiration from Rimworld).
BTW RIMWORLD has mods to make it solely medieval based (Rimedieval and Medieval Overhaul (plus dependent mods and more)).

There just isn't much that happens. In a whole year, I was attacked twice, had a cold snap, a blight and hail, oh, and 3 visitors (one of them a merchant specifically, with guards)... That's it (maybe something else, but it was entirely forgetable). Needs more event variety, and not just negative ones. Blight was also annoying (not hard, just a chore) since I had to micromanage my pawns to cut them one by one due to a lack of chaining forced jobs...
It'd also be nice to be able to stop pawns from going to haul dead animals (that you did not hunt) on the other side of the freakin map...

Most importantly is the fact that you're always interacting with the damn floors when trying to click on things. Get rid of that for the love of god. It'd be better to enter a sort of "floor" mode instead of ALWAYS having to be careful to not click on them. Makes clicking on ANYTHING a major chore. That and the fact that the camera dips each time you move over a zone you dug out (especially your cellar, ffs) is very annoying. You might be zoomed on a certain Z level, but it doesn't ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ matter... making it more similar to dwarf fortress when it comes to moving through Z level would go a long way, otherwise makes it annoying to select particular object on certain floors (worse when building multiple floors at once, gets SUPER confusing with everything overlayed on top of each other in hazy blue like that. Hell, it's a pain on the eyes even when everything ISN'T blue).

From what I see, updates seem to be quite frequent, so will come back in a few months and see where it is at.

EDIT: Also, holy hell does the AI need A LOT of work.

Hauling is a major pain and you NEED to make it so that builders can move items ASIDE when they are building... having construction interrupted because an item is in the way and the stockpile is full (or non-existant) is peek bad programing. In Rimworld, they just move the stuff aside...
Also, how the hell is the AI stupid enough to dig itself into a damn hole. It happened to me twice in 3 hours of gameplay (restarted a game after learning early game basics in the first hour, thinking I could also avoid trapping one of my guys in a hole...).
A build order would be nice too. You plop down a building, then a second one later... So they leave the first one unfinished and start working on the second one, before leaving that one unfinished and switching to the first building again. So many almost finished buildings left to collect dust. (Also when forcing a construction job, it'd be nice to be able to just click on the construction you want to finish without having to first force them to haul the damn item blocking the construction, especially when the construction gets in the way of clicking on said object to haul, forcing you to cancel the build just to force haul... Jesus).
Also, it would be damn nice to be able to queue forced orders (like in rimworld) instead of only being able to give one at a time.
Oh, and man is the AI annoying when it comes to pathing and job orders. They'll haul something, then move away 7 tiles to pick up one more item, then move back to the original spot to pick up an item that was right next to them, and so on and so forth... Same with mining: they'll start at one spot, jump to a few tiles away, come back to initial spot, rinse and repeat (sometimes you're lucky and they go to the next closest logical job). Needs serious improvement. (EDIT: This is made worse when they go to move crops and seeds off of fields. That happens even though their hauling priority is low, but nooooo, they have to go out of their way to move 5 cabbages off a field, run back to original task, come back to haul 5 cabbages, rinse and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ repeat. They're not even storing it for gods sake, just setting it next to the field... so they ran many tiles, just to move a handful of cabbages 2 tiles to the side. FFS)

Finally, why the hell do every new settler arrive damn wounded and why do they move around, collect food, go eat at a table, walk back to fill their pouch ALL THE WHILE BLEEDING OUT??? I think there is a damn priority, and it's to go lay in bed to get frakin treated. Would be nice if setting your pawns to priority 1 for tending meant they'd wake up to do it, instead of staying asleep, letting them bleed out.........

AI is really terrible in this game. Just play Rimworld. The pawns themselves even have more personality without the benefit of being 3D (plus lots of mods to make them look better)....\t\t\t\t\t
