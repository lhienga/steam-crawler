\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great roguelike that plays similar to Enter the Gungeon and Nuclear Throne. The introduction of a laudable Mario stomp mechanic that functions at the same time as a dodge, in place of the typical dodge mechanic, mixes well with buttons, combo stomps, and momentary stuns. Having the bosses flee when they are damaged and enter into an enraged state is effective but occasionally irritating. The design of weapons, bosses, environments, and characters is fantastic but it quickly wears off as these elements later in the game become repetitive and bland due to the lack of new content and the grind. Unlockables, for example, feel comparatively meaningless to unlock in this game yet they are the driving force in rougelites. Implementing a system of unlockables with cut-scenes, challenges (I mean fun, unique challenges in a more global, achievement oriented way not a collection of thematic doors or kill 20 enemies with this weapon), and thematic relations to certain bosses would better suit this game. Further, failure to integrate more secrets into the game besides the occasional glittering crack in the wall is a disappointment to the lineage of roguelike predecessors that did it better such as the Binding of Isaac, Nuclear Throne, and Enter the Gungeon. I imagine later on in development with enough content updates this game could become an 8/10 or a 9/10. For now, I rate this game as a 7/10 and I would recommend anyone excited enough about similar roguelikes to purchase it. Although, wary buyers should stay away from this product until it receives more content updates or it goes on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
