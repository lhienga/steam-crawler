\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA beyond-charming rouge-like with incredible humor, style and visual identity. Similar to the game-play of enter the gungeon or nuclear throne with similar mechanics such as a large variety of weapons and power ups and unlock able characters and weapons.

The boss fights are the high lights with you having to explore each floor of the map fully to find key points to activate which make the boss vulnerable, creating a bit of involvement on each floor.

The combat feels competent if not a bit simplistic, with most difficulty coming with added chaos and multipliers of enemies on harder difficulties.

The greatest weakness of the game is its lack of depth in the combat. Compare to something like enter the gungeon where every weapon and loot chest are somewhat balanced to give you a unique, but challenging run each time and bosses that are truly unforgiving but fights you can master.
In Voidgo combat and weapons mostly all play out the same way each run, leading to tedium. Bosses are not inherently difficult but when they get increased speed and damage and then start multiplying, the game simply becomes chaos, which IS fun, but not necessarily challenging in the same fashion. It just becomes harder and harder to react to things until you get hit by the flashes of things on screen.

Overall I would say a great rouge to check out but don't expect the replay-ability of games like Enter the Gungeon or Binding of Issac.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely write reviews on steam but I have to give this game a warm recommendation.

The pictures on the store page look confusing and convoluted but once you get into the game its nothing crazy.
I play a lot of roguelike games and I was so pleasantly surprised I wanted to write this review so other people might not miss out on this great experience. I'm only at the beginning but I've already fallen in love with the charm of this game.

The voice acting is suberp, I personally love the aesthetic which some might not, also love the music and the droplets of humour sprinkled in.

The maps are generated like old rogue games rather how roguelites usually are which I really really dig.
There's so much build variety with different items , weapons and perks you get each run I don't think i'll get bored of this game in months to come. There are also different characters with totally different playstyles.

In short I personally think this game is very cheap for what it has to offer and if you are into roguelites don't miss out this hidden gem.\t\t\t\t\t
