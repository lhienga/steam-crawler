\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuick Description:
-Voidigo is the latest roguelight bullet hell and is the clear spiritual successor to Nuclear Throne. Voidigo has a gorgeous and vibrant aesthetic, rocks a sweet soundtrack, and is the perfect blend of chaos and precision. I have loved every minute that I have played so far and can't wait to see what they add next.

What's Good:
-I'm in love w/ the brightly colored and wacky art design. The environments are very simple, just like in Nuclear Throne, but there's far more charm scattered throughout the levels. Each world has a unique theme, has tons of destructible environmental pieces, and are filled w/ tons of creative and dangerous baddies.
-The devs were heavily inspired by Monster Hunter when they created this game and it shows. There is always a degree of tension and fear b/c every world there is a ginormous monster boss is chasing you down. This was a brilliant choice imo. You always have to be on your toes, balancing exploration/secret hunting w/ the ever present threat of tons of enemies and the monster tracking you down.
-Voidigo crushes the fundamentals. Moving, shooting, and picking up items is silky smooth. You always know exactly when you take damage, and there are tons of visual cues to help you pick up powerups, avoid projectiles, or enter huts. You don't take damage from touching enemies and jumping on enemies is heavily encouraged.
-There is a great balance between weapons and tons of options for players. If you want to play cautiously and shoot from afar, there are tons of traditional weapons like assault rifles, shotguns, and grenade launchers. However, melee weapons are far more powerful and offer up a great risk and reward b/c you need to be up close and personal. You can also deflect bullets by swiping w/ your weapon, you can block projectiles w/ a shield, jump on enemies heads, and you can unlock environmental hazards that attack enemies. I love the really unique weapons like fish guns, banana shooters, and ice daggers.
-Imo, the difficulty curve is perfect. Voidigo has a similar level of chaos and game feel as Nuclear Throne. You are always in complete control but there are a ton of different variables on screen at once. Over my 7 hrs, I became far more competent in mastering the mechanics. As you get better, you will feel more comfortable dodging projectiles, jumping on enemies heads, and using melee weapons. There are tons of accessible options so no matter what your experience level is, you'll find an experience that is perfect for you.

Could Use Some Changes:
-There are only 3 world right now w/ no alternatives. The devs plan to add another world and several alternatives which will really keep the runs feeling fresh and unique.
