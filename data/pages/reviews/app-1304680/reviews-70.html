\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVoidigo is yet another indie roguelite that hits it's mark. The game is reminiscent of Enter the Gungeon, but is less of a bullet hell. You select your character, ability, weapon, and start the run. It sounds pretty typical, but it is more unique than it seems! As of right now, there are four playable, unlockable characters. Each character starts with a single ability, and a second one that can be unlocked later on. Each character also starts with a single weapon, with 3 others that can be unlocked!

There are two types of weapons in the game. Guns will fire bullets, while melee weapons deal contact damage. both weapon types have secondary abilities, though they aren't extravagant. Guns can use a weak, short ranged melee attack as a secondary, while melee weapons can block and reflect enemy projectiles (at the cost of durability). Guns require ammo to shoot, while melee weapons require "durability", but don't worry because they're both treated as the same thing. Picking up an item that "repairs" a weapon will give you either durability or ammo depending on what type of weapon you're holding. There is a large variety of weapons to come across during gameplay, and on top of that there are also upgradable accessories and addons you can obtain to further empower your character during a run!

The art direction and design are super original. If you've ever watched the developer's animated shorts on YouTube, you'll know what to expect. It's whacky, cute, weird, and veeery pleasing to look at. Every enemy and item moves so smooth, and they all feel super unique.

As of right now, the game consists of 4 maps to battle through, each with a boss you have to defeat to progress. The bosses are really enjoyable and (I've said this word too many times...) unique! I haven't gotten tired of seeing any of them! In order to defeat a boss, you must unlock every... Obelisk(?) in the current map. There are 4 in a map. Each obelisk unlocks a quarter of the bosses health bar, so break all 4 and them give them a WALLOP! Defeating the boss allows you to progress to the next zone! Neat!

Overall, I give Voidigo, in it's early access state, a solid 8/10. What is currently available is more than worth what I paid. There is clearly a lot of love and care being put into this game, and the developers deserve more recognition for their hard work. I can only imagine it will get better from here!!

p.s. There is an in-game roadmap that gives us an idea of the content being released in the future, up until v1.0! Pretty nifty!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like Nuclear Throne you will like this game. Nuclear Throne is one of the action roguelike classics that helps both define and path the way for the roguelike genre. It is in my opinion that Voidigo does not mix up the formula enough to warrant an overwhelmingly positive review score. I picked this game up after it had been in EA for over a year and perhaps I went into it with too high of expectations. However the game is much too shallow, there is not enough offered in the game to help mix up what is a relatively shallow gameplay.

I remain hopeful that the 1.0 version will offer more mixups to the overworld, more items to change up the gameplay and hopefully more meta progression. Currently the different characters and starting items do little to mix things up, especially as you progress through the harder difficulties and more specifically rogue when the game looks more like a bullet hell than anything else. Given that the difficulty is a core part of the gameplay it just renders a lot of the pre-run autonomy to be moot.\t\t\t\t\t
