\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely an underrated action roguelike that deserves more attention. The "fever dream Dr. Seuss book" aesthetic it has going on is unique and refreshing, the gameplay is solid, and the "stomp" mechanic of bouncing off of enemies is a fun gimmick.

My main gripe with the game is the lack of information that is given to the player about... pretty much anything. The powerup and weapon descriptions prioritize quips and flavor text over functionality, which gets old pretty quick when you just want to know what the hell something does. The most egregious examples of this are the various animal and elemental damage types that are used across a wide variety of upgrades: frogs, rats, snakes, bats, ghosts, fire, electricity, water, ice, gunpowder, poison, and wind. I have beaten the game multiple times and I have no idea what any of of these actually do. The elements also apparently have unique effects when combined with eachother, but the game also never tells you what these are or what they do (I only knew because I would randomly get achievements for combining two elements).

Despite this gripe, the game is still fun. Just beware, if you are a player like me that feels the need to understand every mechanic in detail in order to make informed choices, you will not get the answers that you seek and the wiki is pretty barebones. Best to just go with the flow of chaos that is this game.\t\t\t\t\t
