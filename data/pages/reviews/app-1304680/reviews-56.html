\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is seriously great. It feels like a perfect mashup of Gungeon and Nucelar Throne, along with interesting boss mechanics that aren't seen in any other games in the genre. In addition, weapons and upgrades are really weird and unique, and animations for them are top notch. I think the number one issue is amount of content. 6 hours in, and I'm seeing the same guns and upgrades often. Granted, this is still in early access so not really a big concern. But if this game gets more content over development, this may become one of the best rogue lites out there.

Update 6/17/23: Game has now released with version 1.0. They have added more content and more replayability. There's a new system where you need to get a certain amount of kills with each weapon, or max level an upgrade during a run. This is good meta progression to unlock what I think is a new stage, or something along those lines (I haven't unlocked it yet).

Basically, game good. Now gooder because more content.\t\t\t\t\t

What force could be so potent as to be able to control my very being? I looked left, and then right, observing the ruins of what once was, an immense city now in ruins, consumed by oblivion. Then, a realization: everything once known, will one day be forgotten.

Every memory and every last atom, will one day be consumed by the great abyss. Upon the threshold of infinity, there exists a realm of infinite darkness and infinite peril - the Void. And in the deepest recesses of this eldritch landscape lies the realm of Voidigo, an incomprehensible place where time and space have no meaning, and the inhabitants of this forsaken abyss live in a state of perpetual torment and horror, doomed to walk through the realm in an endless cycle of suffering.

Behold, an ancient civilization who had discovered the ability to access a higher dimension, where the entities that exist there had the capability of manipulating time through the use of arcane portals. As the young protagonist of this game trudges deeper into the abyssal lair, a pit opens up from the floor revealing the macabre sights of the tortured. Their screams and cries of agony seem to never cease and they reach to the protagonist, begging him to bring them mercy from their misery, while the voices in his head, urge him to give in to his desires and take a taste of their flesh.

As each step in this eldritch labyrinth is made, the once bright and jovial disposition of the lone wanderer becomes evermore dreary and somber. A perpetual state of mind altering depression sets in, as if the reality before him is naught more than a mere manifestation of his deranged psyche. He delves ever deeper into his maddened thoughts, in order to attain the forbidden knowledge that will free him from eternal torment and restore his will to fight. And what greater way to unlock the secret knowledge of the cosmos, then by facing and conquering his very own inner demons?

As a member of a once prestigious order tasked with ensuring the survival of the human race, there is much to be said about the tribulations we face both within and around the confines of this space. I often look at my fellow order members and ask myself, “what is my purpose? Why do I fight?” This thought often strikes at my very soul, causing me sleepless nights, as I stare into the darkness and ponder on the absurdity of it all.
