If you've played twin-stick shooter roguelikes (the one that jumps to my mind is Nuclear Throne, but it also has some Enter the Gungeon-esque DNA), then you have an idea of what to expect here. Enemies will end your run very quickly if you're not careful, and you have to acclimate yourself to the way the game operates if you want to enjoy it ASAP.

+++Pros+++
+The art and animation is fantastic. Characters, weapons, enemies and projectiles all have readable, fun personality and it's a joy to look at.
+Guns generally feel good, none of them feel particularly weak or "too joke-y to be useful" like some of the ones in Gungeon. It also helps that the melee weapons are also great.
+It has plenty of micro-decisions during runs that let you play your way, which is important to me in a rougelike.
+The sound design is also incredible. Guns and enemies make very satisfying noises, and the few voice clips in here are very funny.
+Some of the weapons and companions are downright hilarious.

---Cons---
-Music could stand to be a little more standardized. There are several events you experience on a given floor and they have unique music tracks, so you end up going through songs too quickly and have them be cut off too soon to really latch onto any of them.


The game's out of early access now and it's really excellent. My issue with the music still stands in the finished game, but other than that 9/10 it's one of my favorite roguelikes I've ever played.\t\t\t\t\t
