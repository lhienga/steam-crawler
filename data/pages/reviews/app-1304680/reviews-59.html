\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think there is a fundamental problem with this game's design, all the upgrades seem gimmicky. Let's say I farm the crystal like crazy, I can get 30 together before I see a shop. Okay great right, at least I can buy a cool upgrade. But wait a second, here the problems start: Maybe I get lucky and there is a blue or purple weapon there so I buy that one, okay that's going to be an upgrade. But I probably already have a good weapon I like so I don't want to risk swapping it for all my crystals just to try out a weapon.
So I buy one of the powerups instead? Nono, that too kinda sucks too. For example, I buy the coffee upgrade. I even had it upgraded twice. I literally never saw what it even did. It's really a theme, these upgrades/powerups are all so weak and vague on top of being weak that I don't even notice that I have any upgrades. Then one time I got a "spider on walls" upgrade. I never saw any spiders on walls, nothing seemed to trigger it. I still have no idea what it does.
Or one time I got the purple(=epic) melee poison powerup and even had it upgraded twice. Now I finally have a useful powerful upgrade right? It says that it poisons enemies on melee hit. Fantastic right... except no, I never saw it proc, ever. Lol. I melee hit the bosses, the mobs.. I dont see it proc. I watched the boss health, it didn't seem like it was poisoned/going down on its own.

I just don't get who plays this game, it all looks pretty neat but they would have to rework all the powerups to be stronger.. but if they do that I can totally see that the whole way that combat works doesn't make sense because if you make stronger powerups then wouldn't the enemies die too quickly? It seems the devs wanted to make tactical smart combat with enemies and that's great but it all amounts to being a 2d shooter where you only ever use the gun. For example there is another powerup that randomly spawns traps on the ground or whatever. It almost never hit any enemy. It's just.. cmon, what do you think I play this game for? So I can put down a crappy trap that never procs? Remember: You are farming crystals and sometimes even exchanging permanent life just to get a random upgrade and then you get unusable garbage again.. it does *not* feel good.

Then even the default ability of the first hero you have is so *weird*. I have to jump on top of the enemies but it only really does anything if you manage to jump on multiple enemies in succession. But that's really hard to ever get right so you kinda never use the jump ability of your guy because getting so close is dangerous anyway, you only really use melee if you run out of bullets.
So essentially what I'm saying is: the game looks neat and has many powerups and features but all of them seem kinda bad and the fact that your hero can only carry two weapons at a time also means that you can never really try out weapons you might want to buy at the shop. I mean you can but then you have to run back to the shop again to pick the dropped weapon if you dont like it. Why not at least let me auto sell my old weapon for crystals.. it just all feels so bad to spend crystals on a weapon or upgrade that both seem to do almost nothing. The only upgrade you will ever realistically find is a better gun. Once you have that, I dont see what else you could find to really have better gameplay.

Then sometimes there is a station where it lets you get a powerup for a *random* powerup you already have. Cmon, is that really necessary? Why not let me select which powerup I want to get rid of? Just so weird and not designed around fun imo.

Or lets say I beat the boss: I dont even get anything for it, it seems. Just.. why? Why not give me something to beat a boss? I think the only reward for killing a boss are these endgame crystals to unlock more stuff in your starting zone. Nothing gameplay wise.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've put far too many hours into games like Enter the Gungeon and Nuclear Throne. Many other games have adapted that formula in one way or another, but none of them seem to do very much to stand out or capture the same magic. Voidigo is the first Rougelike Top-Down Shooters I've played in a long time that actually feels like it's progressed the formula in creative ways with smart additions and twists. The system of hunting the boss throughout the level and making it vulnerable by destroying beacons feels very dynamic. The ability to stomp on enemies is very satisfying and surprisingly easy to use in the top-down perspective. There are smart design decisions all throughout. The art style is very colorful and detailed, but fairly easy to parse even when there is a lot happening on screen. The animations are very bouncy and expressive, and the game just generally feels good to play. Even in early access, you can't really go wrong giving it a shot if this type of game appeals to you at all.\t\t\t\t\t
