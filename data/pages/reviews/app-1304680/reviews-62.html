\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI may change my review but at this time my main problems are

1) No incentive to kill anything other than the boss. As it stands the best course of action is a break neck run through everything in order to destroy the boss pillars and gather anything of value. Once that's done you just focus down the boss and only then kill and mobs that threaten you doing that easily. I prefer the Nuclear Throne style where you have to kill everything to progress and the game is balanced around it.

2) Game needs a balance pass. Most of these guns are hilariously bad, and you can tell the dev thinks they're good, because for example, the solar laser rifle has an extremely slow rate of fire, and small ammo pool. Which would have you saying, "Oh it probably one taps most things" It does not. There is a melee weapon that takes FOREVER to do a kick animation, I thought "oh this has to one tap every mob I'm on the first floor, IT DOES NOT. Final example, Witch's Broom with cat on the end. "Green tier" weapon according to the game, OP AF, literally god tier weapon. Not only do you fly around at super speed, the DPS from the cat is through the roof. After getting that weapon I basically just feel like I'm going through the motions of dying on runs until I see it again. It was 100x better than any other weapon I've been given, mostly because the "powerful" weapons have totally trash DPS. I worry that the dev will read this and be like OOF gotta nerf that witches broom. NO YOU NEED TO BUFF LITERALLY EVERYTHING ELSE. Game is not fun because most of the guns suck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVoidigo is a chaotic roguelike with interesting mechanics, unique character and design elements, and a flexible difficulty setting that lets you decide how much of a challenge you want the game to be.

It is very important to keep that in mind when it comes to enjoying this game because the systems and balancing are both incomplete, especially regarding weapons, items and characters. Although it's fairly easy to get a feel for them, weapons and items don't offer any information regarding their stats or utility, and your only saving grace is that each bullet type has own set of stats independent from the gun it is fired from, assuming there are no modifiers to the bullet itself.

On one hand, I'd say that this is a bad thing, but the initial experience of the game hits that much harder when you're discovering everything on your own, leading to an interesting period of experimentation that lends itself well to the tone of the game. However, this does lead to the realization that there are a number of weapons items that are just genuinely bad, the majority of which being that almost all of the starting gear is garbage, and age horribly as the difficulty increases.

Although you are meant to switch out starting weapons, you can't switch out starting items, and that becomes an issue when you only have two options, and god help you if you dislike any of them, which I most certainly did.

I think these issues will be addressed in time, though. For now, the game is fantastic and fun. I love it a lot.\t\t\t\t\t
