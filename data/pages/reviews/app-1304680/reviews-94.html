This game is a must have for roguelike passionates.

Its comparison with Nuclear Throne is quite well resembled, however this game looks alive and very colorful, looks fun,
Commands are intuitive however the tutorial will introduce you quite well to the game mechanics. As you won't find your way to the boss always, might happen that the boss finds yours first as it moves on the various rooms on the map.
I thought it was easy to beat it first time, because the balance of the game let you find many powerups, ammos and hp to restore yourself, however the boss became angrier and i died.

It was a funny experience so i wanted to repeat it, just to notice that the enemies and the boss were different on 2nd run, subsequently on 3rd run the 1st boss was different again, VERY WELL DONE DEVS!

The weapons are varied and nice and the powerups are quite original. I played less then 2 hours by now but i can clearly reccomend you this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wasn't sure what to expect when I saw yet another action roguelite with Nuclear Throne type level generation, but I have to say that I was very pleasantly surprised. This game has a lot of charm and the gameplay is already pretty smooth, fluid, and has just enough depth and skill to keep me engaged. The mechanics surrounding the bosses are genuinely pretty engaging and the boss fights themselves are exciting and difficult. Most of the weapons so far don't feel too exciting to me; they all feel a little bit samey at the moment(especially compared to Nuclear Throne where each ammunition type has a lot of distinct mechanics and playstyles involved whereas these all just kinda feel like the same projectiles but with different speeds or appearances). However, the melee weapons are genuinely super fun to use and have a satisfying weight to them between timing your swings and the massive feedback you get from swinging them.
For an early access title especially I'd recommend this if you're looking for another action roguelite to try out, and hopefully it'll continue to get more exciting as development continues.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis morning I took a look at an old Youtube channel called Thunderhumor, because I had gotten a notification that they had posted for the first time in four years. Apparently, they'd been developing Voidigo in the time that they'd been gone. I grew up admiring their talent and sense of humor, so I had to give the game a try.

I don't always click with the roguelike genre, but Voidigo is an absolute joy. The gameplay feels great. the challenge is there but the game isn't so hard that it demoralizes you. There's a nice variety of unique ranged and melee weapons to find, and the powerups you'll find make progression exciting, and there's enough of them to keep you coming back. But above all else is the creativity. The music, sounds, and all of the enemies feel unique. Everything about this game is a breath of fresh air.

If you enjoy roguelikes, buy this game. If you don't enjoy roguelikes, maybe give this one a chance. Voidigo oozes creativity, and I can't wait to see what's next. I hope this is the start of something great for these developers, and I have utmost confidence that this game will succeed.\t\t\t\t\t
