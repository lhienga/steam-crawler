Gameplay is precise and intuitive; and the tutorial presents all its functionalities in an organized and accessible way. There are a good amount of weapons, items, enemies and extra challenges, allowing the player to build his strategy to explore and battle in the beautiful scenarios of the game - the pixel-art is original and presents a stupendous quality, being accompanied by a soundtrack perfectly in tune with all the gaming action.

Even in early access, in version 0.5 at the time of this analysis, Voidigo is already an extremely competent title in its proposal, as well as having great potential to be explored by developers. Content is added on a monthly cadence and I can't wait for the finished product. A pleasant surprise that deserves your attention!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic, punchy, lots of variety in weapons and loadouts, monster hunter-esque boss battle hunts. One problem I had with Enter the Gungeon was that you had to min-max with your starting weapon and it was so boring for like 1-2 levels until you felt comfortable enough to swap to the fun guns. Here you have loadouts you can change from the start, like what passive and starting weapon you want from a pool, which helps with that new-run burnout. They are also generous enough with ammo and health that you don't ever feel like you can't use the cool weapon you just got. Also ammo and health refills can be left on the ground for later, which adds a layer of planning that Gungeon intentionally got rid of for some reason with their item thief. Somehow they have a bottomless pool of guns all with unique and punchy animations for shooting and reloading. The art and animations are insanely good and the game oozes character.

If you like these kinds of roguelikes it's an easy recommendation even in its current form. I'll be checking back in for each update.\t\t\t\t\t

i think i'll be playing this game for a while, it scratches that intense bullet-hell rougelite itch i've been craving after playing long runs in nuclear throne.\t\t\t\t\t
