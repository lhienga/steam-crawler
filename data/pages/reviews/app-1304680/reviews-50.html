\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI normally am not one to write reviews, but I have to make an exception here if it means that more people will consider purchasing this.

Voidigo is such a unique and fresh take on the roguelite genre. See, rather than mindlessly wandering through rooms, it feels like you're actually exploring a world. This world is absolutely FULL of personality.
There's so many little details & sounds that really makes you immersed into the game - accompanied by a stellar soundtrack & gorgeous pixel artstyle that is way more smooth than it has any right to be.

The combat is so much better than I would've expected. Ranged weapons have really crazy synergies that you can achieve with Powerups (that are all very fun and unique), and Melee weapons are so much better than I would've expected.
The amount of interactions between weapons & powerups is mindblowing.
I won't spoil any of them though, because it's something that I highly recommend experimenting for yourself.

Dodging attacks feels great too. Rather than a roll (or whatever is the most generic means of dodging things that injure you), you jump over the bullets on screen. Your jump (or dodge) can also be used to stomp Enemies, which stuns them and gives you the advantage. Stomping is way more fun than it has any right to be, and the fact that you can make builds around this mechanic only reinforces that.

The difficulty of Voidigo (or, difficulties) is welcome too. You can really choose how easy or tough you want the experience to be. While some may complain about there not being a set difficulty, I personally am all about accessibility. So if you're worried the game might not be for your skill level, you can just... Change it before starting a run. It's wonderful!

If you can't tell by now, this is just a really good game. It's a must-buy, by my standards.
Buy it for yourself, buy it for a friend, maybe even buy it to play with your friend (cause this game has cooperative play), just give it a try already.\t\t\t\t\t
