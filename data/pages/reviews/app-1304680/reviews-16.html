\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVoidigo is a hectic, fast paced, charming and most importantly an extremely fun and creative rogue-like! The game oozes a special type of charm that you can see in the animations, voice acting and all the cooky weapons you can find. How about a sword that has a gun? Or a tiny dog with a scorpion tail? The game is all about hunting down bosses and they're equally as fun and creative.

But no game is perfect, despite my personal like for Voidigo there are several things that can turn someone away from the game. For one the game is brutally difficult, especially on "brutal" and above. Even on average difficulty I struggle, but the game is 100% beatable. There's also easier difficulties out there if you need extra help. The game has surprisingly little to unlock compared to other games in the genre. The game does a good thing where all weapons and power-ups are available from the start. As you complete the game the void fragments you collect quickly become almost useless. You can use them to create a ton of pets, but that's pretty much it.

Depending on what bosses you encounter during your run the final boss can get really hectic, so much so that you'll end up in situations where avoiding damage is nearly impossible when three bosses attack you all at once. Not to mention some extra stuff in the end game runs... Also the games visual style can be a bit too much for some people, but that depends on taste.

If you're looking for a charming cooky rogue-like Voidigo is the game for you! And who knows what else is in store for this game in the future, the foundations they've created are great.\t\t\t\t\t
