\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun, flashy, surreal rogue-like, similar to Enter The Gungeon. Instead of a dodge roll with i-frames, there is a functionally similar jump/stomp move that can be used to dodge as well as land on enemies to stun them. Personally, I love this mechanic and I think it makes Voidigo stand out among all of the roguelikes I've played. All of the guns/melee weapons are extremely stylish and fun to shoot, as well. There is a mechanic where the bosses can move between the rooms of the dungeon when they choose, which ends up being really fun when it comes time to chase the boss down.

The art style is beautiful. It's cartoony, a little disgusting, and very visually busy, reminiscent of Loony Tunes or Ren & Stimpy. The character and enemy designs are all amazingly colorful and memorable, especially the bosses. If you get overstimulated easily this might not be the game for you, but I haven't ever really had a hard time parsing through what's on the screen in my few hours of playtime.

The music and menu design is amazingly polished and professional-feeling as well. I haven't looked into the identity of the developers, but I'm assuming that they're a small indie team, which makes all of this even more impressive. There are a few different difficulty settings, so it should be easy to find one that strikes the balance between too easy and too punishing. I'm playing on Moderate right now, which is probably a good fit for most reasonably experienced gamers.

tl;dr Would absolutely recommend\t\t\t\t\t
