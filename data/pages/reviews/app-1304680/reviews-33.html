\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this is a good game, but it has some pretty blaring flaws that prevent it from being a GREAT game.

The combat feel and overall ability to move around and fight are mostly really nice. Most weapons are interesting and they are all very different and fun to experiment with. Some melee weapons propel you forward which does suck though, cause it effectively locks you into whatever you are doing with no ability to change. But its not bad and overall i like it alot.

Exploration and getting around is fine. There are plenty of challenges and layouts etc to do and the replayability is overall good.

Each character plays very differently and has plenty of options to try, and there is alot of things to unlock and keep you playing. nice work here!

However, some negatives do exist that in my eyes shouldnt be as prominent as they are.

1. Ammo is a -PERPETUAL- annoyance. You are always out of ammo, you are always begging the game for more to drop, and you have to juggle an absolute ton of finding and recycling extra weapons to get a few extra precious bits of ammo. This is -INCREDIBLY- unfun to me and the reason im writing this right now and not simply playing the game. It drains the life out of me to fight a boss and have to leave to go look around for more ammo to keep fighting. This is awful, from start to finish.

2. The boss spawns whenever and will chase you around, and you have to run around avoiding the boss to smash its health limiters before you can actually kill it. I hate this mechanic. It just sucks the fun out of exploration for me to have to constantly run away from an unkillable boss.

3. You have no idea what kind of damage weapons do without lots of testing. I would like there to be some sort of visible stat somewhere either on a weapon or your character to indicate a general idea of how good something is.

Overall I would give this game a solid 7/10, and think its pretty good. It could be great, but its not currently.\t\t\t\t\t

That's a little unfair to EtG. I enjoyed EtG back when I played it. But it was VERY stingy with the cool guns and currency and the upgrades to health. It was like they said "if you aren't good enough to flawless the bosses, you don't deserve the good items."

Voidigo on the other hand seems to recognize that we're ALL really playing the game to get a turn shooting cool guns, and that while getting a good gun should feel special, letting that special feeling occur more often than once every 15 runs makes for a better game.

Yea, I don't have a ton of banked time in Voidigo yet, but I love that the art style seems to channel the quiznos hampster commercials, and the balance so far has seemed just right for maximizing fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVoidigo is an incredibly well animated and polished game with a very bold and unique identity-- the sounds and visuals are overflowing with life (sometimes to the point where it's sensory overload). The game itself plays really well-- for the most part, it feels the best of any of the twin-stick shooter roguelikes I've played.
There's a healthy amount of difficulty presets and the item pools are fully unlocked, save for a few starting weapons-- you won't be grinding nearly as much as Isaac or Gungeon. I definitely recommend giving Voidigo a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
