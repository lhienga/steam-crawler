cons:

Anyway good game, give it a go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's good but I think the detailed artstyle subtracts from the gameplay at times. Especially near and including the final boss, there's so much ♥♥♥♥ happening on the screen that it's hard to know what is happening. Also I am upset at whoever thought it would be a good idea to zoom way in on the player character whenever you're not in combat. I guess the blending of top down action roguelikes is kinda cool but i'm not impressed. Even though the game was supposedly in development for 6 years, I find the lack of content perplexing. I guess it's not really bad variety, but Enter the Gungeon really spoiled me for this genre. I consider Enter the Gungeon the gold standard for this genre, and I will inevitably compare all future games like this to Enter the Gungeon. This is not Enter the Gungeon, and that's good. I like the way bosses are handled in this game, and I like how creative the weapons are. I just want more weapons, items, enemies, bosses, etc. Also the narrator sounds so weird. It's like I have a child predator in my ear whispering to me at the start of every level. Games like this generate a lot of value to me when they surprise me with clever secrets and dynamic runs. This game wasn't dynamic at all, and at no point did I find a secret that really engaged me. I know i'm digging into the game, but I actually enjoyed it. I just wished that it was a bit more ambitious in it's scope. Overall it was solid, but if you haven't played a game in this genre before I recommend starting with Enter the Gungeon. After around 10 hours I feel like I've seen the majority of what this game had to offer. By comparison, I have played Enter the Gungeon for 160+ hours and still haven't done everything there is to do.\t\t\t\t\t
