\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTight, intense roguelike with a definite bullet hell flavor. A couple of really good design decisions take this one over the top - the jump/stomp dodge, incredible interaction between upgrades, a lot of extremely weird weapons, plus a solid base of crunchy combat that "feels" right.

It's not perfect. Multi-boss fights get arguably too hectic and start to chug on weak hardware like the Steam Deck, and item balance is highly variable. Some weapons essentially become useless with certain upgrades - Axe-o-lotl is especially bad for this - and others are just too powerful and make the rest of the run very one-note. But I think that's a good tradeoff to keep everything from feeling same-y or constrained.

The aesthetic is... unusual? I'm not sold on it. Creepy narrator and villainy villain are well done, but the piecemeal efforts at a coherent story or lore are uninteresting. Things can get visually muddy when there are void enemies on the field or if you're in said void, which detracts from the experience.

It doesn't dethrone Hades on polish or narrative, but it's very, very hard to put down. A good run with high-powered unlocks feels satisfying and snowball-y, right up until the brutal difficulty curve on a "looped" run smacks you back down. I fully expect to get a solid 100 hours out of it even if they never patch it again.\t\t\t\t\t
