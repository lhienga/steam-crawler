This is an edited review after the first patch update, as well as after beating the game for the first time.

Story and Presentation are kept the same. I'm gonna talk about how the game has been for me after beating the game(on Intense difficulty as well!).
First thing first, I had stated about how the 144 Hz monitor refresh rate affects the game and they fixed it cleanly. The game is running smoothly at solid 60 FPS. This shows how well the devs listen to their community, and optimize their game.

There are 3 stages you have to beat in total, and the last stage is the reason why you should never underestimate the first two stages' bosses. The weapons get so much more diverse as you progress through each stage, and the last one.... My. God. The last stage has the best game design I could ever ask for. It was an intense fight, small but tremendously fun mechanics. It is a challenge to you regardless of how powerful you get. You're constantly adapting and improvising as the fight goes on and your ammo runs out.

Below is the review I gave before beating the game.

Story is not much... but do you really need it?
I can't say much because I've just beaten the first area. I know what you're gonna say. "Hades" has raised the bar of the story aspect much higher for roguelike/lite. But in my opinion, you don't need a Supergiantgame-writing story if you already have a good roguelike/lite. And Voidigo is an exceptional roguelite.

Presentation is excellent.
The artstyle and the animations feel so bouncy, smooth, and alive. You'd probably get reminded of Forager. HUD during gameplay is easy to understand; however, their positions are too far away from one another. I have to draw my eyes across the monitor to look at each of them. But, that's just my problem. You probably will like it.

Voidigo has multiple difficulties for you to challenge and multiple weapons for you to use, ranging from heavy/light melee weapons to guns. There are also power ups which modifies/strengthens your weapons and attacks, as well as gives you other utilities to help go through the game, or make you so smug that you get killed by accident.

You can get power ups from breaking objects or beating the small dungeon areas that often spawn in the main areas. There are also ammo and health flasks at a trading post, which you can only choose one between the two per purchase. Your main currency are the shards dropped by killing and breaking things.

The boss fights, at first glance, seem easy and predictable. But, trust me when I say that you'll get surprised by the challenge. You can meet the bosses pretty early on but you can run away and prepare yourself for the fight, as well as breaking the "Locked Health Bar." The bosses can run away during fights and you'll have to hunt them down. There are many ways you can approach this and it's so refreshing to see something like this in an indie game.

Gameplay is gold.
As you've probably seen in the info page, or you have not because you skipped the entire section just to read some dude's review. You'll play exactly what the videos/gifs shows you.

You can carry only 2 weapons at a time. There are melee weapons which can block and reflect projectiles as well as destroying them. Also, branching out from that category are heavy and light melee weapons. They function the same as above, but their attack speed are different. Next, the gun collection are diverse so far, and they're equally balanced as long as there's no power up affecting them. The actions you do with these weapons are powerful and smooth like butter.

Jumping on enemy's head and stunning them is probably the most fun mechanic for me. It just makes the combat system much more smooth and compelling.


Game runs smoothly with solid 60 FPS. Optimization has been well implemented.\t\t\t\t\t
