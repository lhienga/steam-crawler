\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a gorgeous roguelike with insane attention to detail and a graphical style. The gameplay is very polished, insanely fun, and it's made by only four people, which is unheard of.

Is this game worth getting? ABSOLUTELY ... but. There is always a "but," isn't it?

It's infuriating how close this game is to the hall of fame roguelikes like TBOI, ROR2, Slay The Spire, etc., BUT it's not there YET .

Pros:

- Gorgeous art style, with the best-looking 2D animations I've seen. Every inch of this game oozes style.
- Fun, new ideas to spice up the Nuclear Throne-esque formula (jumping, combos, a great way of traversing the map, buttons).
- Did I say it's gorgeous yet?
- Great bosses, very fun and memorable.
- Runs very well.
- Great accessibility options.
- Very creative-ish weapons (I'm going to touch on that later).
- Great progression!

The problem is, as a self-proclaimed veteran of the roguelike genre, for me, this game is too shallow to be a roguelike that I play for hundreds of hours.

Cons:

- While the game is gorgeus, it is not always clear what's happening on the screen, which, for a bullet hell-esque game, is almost a dealbreaker. Extremely often I've found myself being confused about which bullets are mine, which bullets are the enemies', what can hurt me, and what can't (This problem is especially frustrating when you have power-ups that leave things on the ground, puddles, fire, etc. I have 0 idea if it's mine or not).

- The weapon types seem very sparse. So far, I've only found a few types of weapons, the main being melee (which I'm going to talk about later), and guns which split basically into three categories: normal guns, grenade-throwers, and a weird mix between shotgun-like weapons and weird spread weapons (such as the weapons that shoot bats, for example). There just isn't enough variety and not enough wacky weapons to keep me engaged. This sucks especially because the graphics for the weapons are insanely well made and fun, but when you actually use them, they seem like any other weapon.

- Not enough combos/interactions between weapons.

- In my opinion, 99% of melee weapons are unusable. The melee weapons in this game just don't feel good. Most of them are too clunky and too slow to use and don't deal nearly enough damage to be worth it, and they just don't feel good to use. I don't expect them to be OP or anything, but IMO they need to be reworked.

- While this may be an extreme nitpick, I don't enjoy the money system in this game. It feels weird to collect tens of gems and get maybe 1 coin at most. For me, it creates a weird dissonance between the primal feeling of collecting something shiny and not getting much stuff at all from it.

- I've not unlocked everything yet, so excuse me if I'm wrong, but I wish the game had more types of shops, events, etc. Right now, it feels repetitive and sparse.

- I just wish that it had MORE. I hope the devs make enough money on this game to support it further.


Of course, please read my review from the perspective that this is a 4-man indie team, and what they've got here is a rare and special achievement. The people who made this game are INCREDIBLY talented game developers. <3

Looking forward to their future updates/games! Love ya. <3\t\t\t\t\t
