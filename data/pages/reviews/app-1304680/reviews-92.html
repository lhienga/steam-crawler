\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoy oh boy!

This one has HUGE potential. You've probably heard the countless references to Nuclear Throne in the reviews and how this game is a potential successor.

Well? is it? In my opinion: YES!

However, this game does so much more and adds enough of its own flare that to call it a Nuclear Throne copycat would simply be untrue.

PROS:
- Different difficulty levels! (Easy to Impossible).
- A unique stomp/dodge feature that can be chained onto multiple enemies.
- Awesome dynamic bosses that either hunt you or flee from you, randomly.
- Interconnected areas in each world, sporting separate biomes, enemies and structures containing loot.
- Colourful and vibrant worlds that breathe and live sci-fi.
- Punchy animations and feedback for weapons, movement and enemy attacks.
- Great sounds for weapons, enemies, powerups and overall atmosphere of the different worlds.
- Good music.
- Plenty of accessibility options (Already, in Early Access!)
- Buttery smooth 60FPS at 1080P.
- Lots of hidden lore and secrets, plenty future content in the works.

CONS:
- Needs more worlds for longer runs, there are only 3 worlds at the moment. (Although there is enough to keep you busy until the next updates!)
- It's not finished yet!

The developer/s appear to have made this game with love and with the player in mind. The fact that the game is already so stable with many quality of life additions and optimisations proves that it deserves your support. I am very excited for what they are planning to add to this gem in the future.

Give it a try. If you are a fan of rogue-likes such as The Binding of Isaac, Nuclear Throne, Enter the Gungeon i promise you won't be disappointed!

Even if you've never played a game like this before, the varying difficulty options open the door to players of all types!

Support the devs and let this game grow into the masterpiece that it very nearly is - even during it's infancy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, last time I reviewed Voidigo was early into its development. 3 updates have come out- that's right, just three- and literally every single problem I had with this game has been solved. Here's a list.

'The game ends too soon. Adding a looping system that let the players keep equipment while making the run harder- but still calling it won from the initial clear- would be a great idea to improve it.'

They did EXACTLY THIS. Word-for-word. You're forced to leave 3 things behind, but those things can be weapons, which are usually trivial to replace, or sometimes even unnecessary.

'Waiting around for smash-grab money pickups slows down the pace too much for my taste.'

Boxes/urns smashed by the player now have their drops auto-magnetized, completely negating the issue I had with it. Money still erodes if you broke things at a distance, but at that point, it's kinda on you.

'Enemies stand in one area or randomly walk in directions, waiting to be attacked. Getting hit by them feels more unfair, because they suddenly become faster in combat.'

This is indirectly solved due to adding Void variants of enemies, which make it VERY clear they're a threat right out the gate, even moving after taking attacks that would stun normal enemies.

'Things are far too cluttered to be comfortable reading the environment- everything moving to catch your eye when it needs to is great on paper, but overwhelming en masse.'

This has moved on from a slight inconvenience to being literally part of the mechanics. If you let the situation get that frenetic, it's your problem- there's ways to prevent that. EDIT- Apparently, they also added a Max Particles setting. Problem solved.

In my time on steam, I've played over 25 roguelikes. I've enjoyed exactly 7 of them. And Voidigo, out of my entire steam library, currently takes the #3 slot on that list, even amongst giants like Hades, The Binding of Isaac, and Nuclear Throne. I expect that, when this game releases 1.0, it'll knock The Binding of Isaac and Noita clear off that list and take the top spot.

Amazing job, Semiwork. Absolutely impeccable.\t\t\t\t\t

It has a very interesting artstyle and even more interesting gameplay! I mean, have you ever chased a boss that's running away from you? I haven't! I'm usually the one being chased! And being able to stun enemies with your dodge move? I just love it! But there is one thing that threw me off a little bit...
I finished the game on the Balanced Difficulty almost immediately (on my second try), but when I moved on to the Intense Difficulty...I felt like I had no chance. Don't get me wrong, I'd understand this if there were only three difficulty options and this one was the hardest, but...there are three more harder difficulties after that. LIKE WHAT?! I think it's a bit too much of a difficulty increase for the fourth hardest difficulty in the game.
But other than that, I love everything about it, and I'm excited to see where this game will head next!\t\t\t\t\t
