\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven after grinding out and getting 100% in everything, this is one of my favorite roguelite games, which is definitely saying something given my library of roguelites. So many gameplay mechanic interweave and work extremely well together- the powerups are interesting and there's a lot of potential to become extremely powerful in a balanced way- especially given that there are few items that actually protect your vulnerability. The dodge is really satisfying to use, and is quite modular in it's use with specific upgrades if you want. The wrong circumstances may mean an easy death- especially if you take the wrong risk. But you are given customization to your difficulty, if you want to go solely for just generally completing runs. The game rewards you for your efforts though- giving you more and more stuff to do if you show that you want things to get harder.
On another note, the artstyle and aesthetics are extremely vibrant and appealing, evidently having a lot of heart put into it- Plus, the devs are still somewhat active in the community- criticism is taken on a genuine basis, rarely implementing things unless it actually has a solid basis (not to say every change is ideal of course, but they do put effort in making sure the game remains to their standards). The game is also pretty open to the various ways to play this game, and as previously mentioned, has considerations for players of all skills. If you like Gungeon or Twin Sticks/Action Roguelikes in general, you'll love this game.

My major complaints lie primarily with balancing and RNG- the former of which is specific to various enemies and their attacks, especially on the hardest difficulty- occasionally having attacks that come from off-screen, or being inhumanly fast even with the best reaction times, primarily due to the screen spam that can occur under some easy circumstances (most notably, again, on it's hardest difficulty). With over 100 weapons, there's also a lot of variety, and a lot of freshness- even if some weapons may be pretty similar, the slight differences boiling down to just the rarity sometimes, the simple change in visual design/aesthetic, as well as the (occasionally small) mechanical differences between ammo counts, projectile types, attack speed, reload times- not even accounting for unique mechanics- all play into how differently your builds work- which while good for the most part, can be to a imbalance, between uncommons that are better than the super rares, the rares that are useless, the few that have literally no useful purpose, or the ones that can be way too strong- which while interesting to note on their individual basis, does create a disconnect between expectations and reality that can just be frustrating. Powerups too have this "problem" but become not as bad given how rarely you're only allowed to get one powerup without given two more options to choose from- as well as the potential that powerups can hold through further upgrades, even despite it's initial appearances, in which rarity becomes more of a surface level determination as opposed to an overall one. Weapons though, don't have the luxury of being upgraded, and have to be chosen around the less flexible powerups, which exacerbate the problem.
This leads into the problem of RNG- where it *can* be manipulated, and easy to work around for the *most* part, but has no guaranteed failsafes preventing you from getting the worst possible outcomes- which gets worse when grinding essence, or looking for any form of consistency to cling onto. Because the only consistencies in this game are the individual behaviors of things. Anything that encapsulates a broader picture- enemy placement, specific bosses, interactables, weapons, and powerups, can screw you over due to just not providing what you need. For the most part, this probably won't be a problem- but when it comes down to the defining runs, or just the final things to capture- you might be left holding out empty.
Yet, failing runs isn't even the worst part about RNG- viable builds are extremely important- and again, while they're common enough, if you get unlucky, boss fights start becoming *tedious* or *frustrating*. This is where it all comes to play. Unbalanced *bad* weapons, when paired with overall bad RNG- means that boss fights boil down to spamming a button and dodging attacks for*ever*. Now, I still should reiterate- most of the time, this won't happen. But a specific situation that has happened more than once from result, once you commit to muscle memory the attacks of a boss, the rooms are empty, and you have a bad build- it's perhaps one of the game's lowest lows- contrasted heavily with it's highest highs of the most tense and exciting boss fights, while you're on low health and you've built up a uniquely strong set of powerups- which I wish was at least a little bit more of the game's norm, though is still occasionally something I have still experienced 100s of hours in.\t\t\t\t\t
