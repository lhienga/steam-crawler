\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thought I was going to really like Voidigo, but that was really not the case. I really don't believe this game deserves an Overwhelmingly Positive rating when other roguelikes such as The Void Rains Upon Her Heart do it way, way better.

Firstly, even if I try to ignore any of this game's issues, I can't play it well because of the sprint mechanic. I hate sprint mechanics in games. So much. Like, I can take it if it's a first person game, where i can just hold W + Shift, but for something like a top-down game, it's just not possible. My hands are usually resting on the keyboard at an angle, because it's the most comfortable setup for typing and my desk isn't that big. This means that my pinky finger is usually right between Caps Lock and Shift, which makes holding A + Shift very uncomfortable. I would use the "tap to sprint" option, but it's completely useless. Please fix this damn thing, every other action cancels the sprint, it's so unpredictable. I played most of the game with sprint bound to the scroll wheel button, but it's impossible to unbind the scroll wheel for switching weapons, so I'd constantly switch when I didn't want to.

Alright, now for some problems that I feel are worth talking about more.

This game is short. Really short. Why are there so few worlds??? I thought there would be more added before the end of early access, but not really. It just feels really wrong. Instead, there is the option to loop runs... which is not fun, like at all. The game is only slightly harder every loop, so it's moreso about how long you can take playing the game. I had a really long looping run that was just so mindbogglingly easy at the 10th or so loop that i decided to die willingly. It would be way better if there was more to do per loop and looping was harder. There's also just... not much to even see. There's the shop, sometimes a ghost event, the boss, the enemies, a few items and pickups, challenge rooms, and that's basically it.

I'll be short for this one. THE ITEMS ARE SO BORING. AND USELESS. I don't need more than one button item. I don't need more than one boot item. I'm never going to use a melee upgrade EVER. I'm literally never going to need a beacon upgrade without Mother Ship, and Mother Ship is useless without a beacon upgrade! This is embarrassing, ! Everything just does the same exact thing, do damage, so you have no reason to just pick the same strong items over and over again. the only variety that this item system offers is the variety in your DPS. Also, why would anyone ever pick Inner Fusion? It's literally a debuff!

Balance. Really bad. Melee weapons are all trash, not worth the risk, too much maintenance, and literally anything that shoots bullets is 10x better because bullet upgrades scale way better. The weapon variety is completely arbitrary. Just pick something that shoots a lot of bullets and ignore everything else. Weapons are worth nothing, so never buy any of them and keep sacrificing the ones you get for items. It's laughable. More bullets just mean you get more bullet lotion procs and that wins you the game. also, JIMBOT. WHO THOUGHT THIS WAS OKAY...????? you can just spam the jump button to become nearly invincible while Jimbot shoots for you! I completed the hardest difficulty of the game as easily as the easiest difficulty because of this! Also, the upgrade system is bonkers, and it falls apart because of one machine, the reusable upgrader. It is ALWAYS worth it to upgrade *anything* to maximum every time you find one of these machines, because the payout for a max level item is ridiculous. The item that you upgrade doesn't matter, the items that you sacrifice don't matter, not even the items that you get matter, you don't need to think about it, all that matters is that the total value of your items is increased regardless, which is completely backwards... for what is supposed to be a sacrifice. The challenge rooms are also really unbalanced. Some such as Prophecy Laser are straight up beneficial, while others such as Zoom In and Gridlock are ludicrously punishing and never worth going for. Oh, and in the final boss area, you can straight up just leave and re-enter rooms constantly to make the fight way more manageable. Why?

I know that I am being very negative, but playing this game seriously gives me more questions than answers every single time. Who playtested this? at least there aren't many bugs.
...I do really like the music, artwork, humor, theme, etc etc, but the gameplay is mid at best, broken at worst, and just not worth investing your time into otherwise. I personally enjoyed 20 or so hours, as well as the aesthetic of the game, but for a roguelike, this game is incredibly unfortunate.\t\t\t\t\t
