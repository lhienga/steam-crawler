\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game really deserves a mixed review, but I'm gonna leave this at negative for the sake of awareness.

Great concepts with poor execution.

I guess my biggest complaint is unavoidable damage. The game incentivizes you to jump more often, not only to dodge attacks, but to stomp on enemies or buttons to make use of certain upgrades. But really the fights sometimes get so chaotic that you are guaranteed to land into another bullet or a melee attack. The final bossfights make zero sense and just turn into "hopefully I can kill them faster than they kill me", which becomes less and less likely with the growing difficulty. Not to mention both jumping and running stop reloading, which gets really awkward.

The game also seems to have no clear reading on numbers. I have no idea how much damage I'm dealing to an enemy, or how close I am to killing it. Something just isn't right in this game from that perspective. It could be lack of visuals of enemies getting hit, difficulty scaling, many things. I also have no clue how much damage will this particular projectile deal to me. I'm baffled by the fact that they use hearts as health, while still counting damage in numbers. If you represent a heart with 100 hp and the enemy deals 80 damage, just put those numbers on the screen or make it deal half or full heart of damage.

It's wild to me that I lost interest in this game after only 20 hours. I was expecting more engagement, but like said in another review, it falls short really fast due to lack of content and consistency. I've played far more engaging games for a far lower price. The final nail on the coffin was the devblog where they said they aren't getting enough money from this game. With the game having above average price amongst the genre and barely ever going on big discounts. Well, if you made this game better, who knows. :shrug:
