I really loved it that you only could have two weapons, so you had to think which weapons you were going to bring., However, it got repetitive fast. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finally slapped together a winning build and beat Voidigo at around the 8 hour mark. I may be very burnt out on it right now but I still think it's worth checking out if you're a fan of twin stick shooters even if it gets stale faster than some of the rogue-like/lite greats.

The Good
  • I love the art design on the bosses.
  • The absurd weapon designs are fantastic.
  • Jumping to dodge and being able to stomp on heads is great.
  • You will routinely have to give up things like weapons, items, and even max health to get new things. It's an interesting design that really puts some weight on your decisions. I have gotten entirely screwed by this repeatedly insisting on
    making me give up my max upgrading skill I absolutely need in exchange for anything but more often that not it wasn't that cruel.
  • Difficulty settings! I played through on the hardest available to me but there were easier options available and some unlockable harder options. This is always a good thing!

The Bad
  • The color code rarity on weapons seems to be entirely useless at indicating if a new weapon is good or not. The Cloud character's starting slap weapon literally AND figuratively slaps WAY harder than whatever pink/rare katana I found on my run with whatever build I had going. I'm not saying that shouldn't be able to happen with some weird builds but this was not an isolated occurrence.
  • Pairing a slower unlock pace with having no idea what you're spending unlock currency on back at camp between runs is pure misery. For the love of all you hold dear, don't waste any currency on the pet until you've handed it over to all the random walls.
  • The UI is rough at just about every exchange, purchase, or upgrade in the game. No stats on new weapons for comparison, no indicator of the weapon being a gun or melee type in a game where that "sword" you bought very well can just fire off the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hilt, no text when saying what item I need to sacrifice, inconsistent placement of item text in the shop, and awkward wording/symbol placement on what your giving and what you're getting. It may seem silly but in a game packed with frequent loadout changes that can make or break your run, this is essential to get right.
  • The difficulty spike on the last boss is absurd. This is not made any better by the environment's color scheme blending right in with the color of incoming attacks indicators and projectiles.
  • Stage and enemy variety gets stale pretty quickly. They eventually give you the ability to unlock having new enemies and bosses in stages but by the time I unlocked that, I had completed my first run and wasn't eager to go back again.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great entry for people who are unfamiliar with roguelike/lites due to its simplicity and charm, but falls a bit flat for people (like me) who have a bit more experience with the genre due to the apparent lack of overall buff variety which mainly consists of mixing a buff "species" (eg. bullet type), with an element of some sort.

Weapons also feel pretty bad to use, often tickling bosses until you run out of ammo or die because a melee weapon flung you into a stray bullet. Speaking of which, most melee weapons are painfully slow with not much payout, only dealing what appears to be ~5% of a bosses' health after a base second or two charge up while the screen lights up with chaos that damages neither you or the boss. To try to fix this, the developers have included an elemental synergy system where if 2 elemental effects are applied simultaneously, a strong effect occurs. However this either doesn't apply to or is extremely inconsistent when these effects are applied to enemies, with effects overwriting the previous effect more often than not, causing anti-synergy between weapons that apply their debuffs directly instead of through the terrain.

Another glaring issue is the feel of the game, with most runs consisting of a very slow buildup towards the "final" boss and only picks up some traction as you enter the first loop which can take upwards of an hour to reach with mediocre gear, then you have to sacrifice 3 items/buffs just to do so.

I apologise if any of what I've said has been worded poorly (I'm not the best at English) but I hope this review has warned you of some possible turnoffs during play despite the overwhelmingly positive reviews this game has as of writing.

tldr: game is a good start to roguelikes but game balance and variety needs to be changed if you're looking for a game to play long term or provide satisfying runs
(Also feels very Gravity Falls inspired, both design and overall message-wise)\t\t\t\t\t
