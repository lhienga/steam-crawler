\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVoidigo is a very good game with very good devs. It takes the bullet hell Gungeon design and distinguishes itself from it. Honestly, I have a bit of a hard time comparing it to Gungeon aside from the genre. I was already going to leave a positive review before the most recent update, but this "feedback update" really solidified my opinion. As the name implies, the devs implemented features suggested by the playerbase. It is quite possible one of the best integrated updates I have seen.

There are many weapons and power-ups (I'm talking well over 100 for each). Each one is unique though some are less useful than others. IMO, roguelites need some bad items assuming the bad items are well-designed. What's better is they have a phenomenal system for encouraging you to use/upgrade ALL powerups and weapons which doesn't feel like a chore - even for the bad items.

I don't like this sort of game (Nuclear Throne, Gungeon, etc) but I knew I needed to try this gem. It didn't take me horribly long to beat the final stage on moderate difficulty and I ended up with most achievements. However, if you DO love this genre, you will not stop playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game entered the roguelite scene and said, I am the rightful owner of the 2nd best roguelite in the world (first being isaac ofc).
This game kept suprising me and impressing me throughout my playtime. It doesn't have quite the build variety of isaac or close to it, but it has enough to keep it interesting.
It excels mostly in it's weapon variety, and they VARY, some extremely creative weapon designs.

There are 5 playable characters, which are all pretty unique and interesting, I do however wish there was one a bit more, Doom Eternal looking, than rainbows and daffodils but you can't have it all.

The creativity of the developers shine throughout various parts of the game, be it through the weapons, the characters, the companions or otherwise, there will be points where you'll just be thinking, there is no way this is a real companion/weapon etc

At one point my companion, was a literal floating, arena stadium of people, cheering you on and jumping out of the stadium to help occasionally and applaud/boo you on.

The combat is actually quite satisfying too, and the game does really well of rewarding your progress, using different weapons, killing different bosses, contribute to progress, and after every run the progress you make , pushes you further to destroying the crystals in your homebase, I haven't figured out quite what is under those crystals, but heck does it motivate me to find out.

Every run you also get void crystals, which you can use to unlock new zones of your homebase, new starting weapons/perks, you are constantly rewarded for progress.
Heck, in one of the rooms of the starter zone you unlock, you can even start building your own pet, from the groundup, choosing the name, head,body, weapon etc that upon completion will actually help you in battle.
I've said enough, it's a good breathe of goofy whacky fresh fun.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving finished 'The final run' I think I can review this.

I love this game. It's a twin stick roguelike. Certainly played a lot of those: Nuclear Throne, Gungeon, Synthetik, etc. But I really dig this game.

The art style is fantastic. Very bright and colorful, and no visual clutter that plagues some other games of this kind. I really like the character designs and the boss designs in particular. The sound design is also top notch, with a lot of neat details with items and weapons combined.

Gameplay wise, the game is very solid. You try to beat up the minions of the Void and progress your characters all the while. You shoot but you also have a jump which evades a large number of attacks (if not all) and a sprint. You also have melee weapons that can also block at the cost of weapon durability as opposed to ammo.

As you progress through the higher difficulties, the runs get proper tense. Bosses work in a way similar to Monster Hunter: They will move between rooms and they will either flee from or chase you given the situation, and I SWEAR they favor rooms you haven't fully cleared yet. You're able to flee from conflicts as well to recover and gather resources, which is real important in higher difficulties. This game gets brutal real fast.

There's a wide variety of weapons both melee and ranged, and plenty of items to synergize with all of them. There's also items to synergize with other playstyles: You can stack turrets for those beacons, or have a stomp favored build that rewards skillful stomping on enemies by jumping on them. I really stuck with melee synergies, had a number of successful builds with those.

Overall, Voidigo is a fantastic shootin' roguelike. Lovely to look at, really fun to play, and a lot of builds to mess with. Sure can't complain.\t\t\t\t\t
