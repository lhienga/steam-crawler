\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played a lot of roguelikes. Binding of Isaac, Nuclear Throne, Enter the Gungeon, among many others. I've gotten to know the very saturated genre quite well; let me claim that Voidigo belongs among the best of the best of them.

If you're familiar with Enter the Gungeon, Voidigo follows a similar rhythm, providing a suite of quirky and strange weapons to use alongside passive upgrades as you proceed through your run. However! where Enter the Gungeon stumbles, Voidigo picks up the slack in spades. Combat is fast and satisfyingly punchy, the character design is full of charming life. Voidigo is a labor of love and it shows.

I bought this game just a few days ago, and I've already sunken 17 hours into it. I've not been this excited to hop back into a game in a long time. So do yourself a favor! If you like roguelikes, get Voidigo. You will not regret it.\t\t\t\t\t
