\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti'm a big fan of roguelikes/rouguelites and it's been awhile since one has captured my attention like Voidigo has. Found out about the game from a YouTuber called sl1ppey, the way they presented the game hooked me immediately. The art style is charming, the animations are crisp and well done, the sound design is excellent, and the gameplay is smooth.

Some of the gameplay decisions that make it stand out may seem simple, but it makes a huge impact. Something simple as having a sprint button gives you so much more control of your character. Combined with the dodge jump that encourages you to use it to bounce off enemies and stay on the offensive. It makes just navigating combat feel great as you weave through all the attacks flying your way.

There's a variety of bosses to fight and they're all fun to fight. I have yet to encounter a boss that I thought was unfair or just annoying to fight. Another thing I love about the bosses in this game is how they move from room to room. It turns hunting the boss into a game of cat and mouse. If you get the boss low, it can run away from you and move to a different room. The same can go for you if you're getting your butt kicked by the boss, you can easily escape combat to catch your breath and get ammo/heal/seek upgrades/etc while the boss chases you.

The game isn't perfect by any means, there's a couple of things I think could stand to be improved. The first one is an issue that feels to be a common issue in the genre and that's powerups and other items having vague descriptions. It's annoying when you go to pick something up and not having the stats or knowing exactly what it does. I suppose it's just knowledge you gain as you play, but it doesn't make it any less annoying especially as you continue to discover new stuff. I'd like to see better descriptions and more information given on what they do.

While there's a great deal of weapon/powerup variety, i feel like some of them straight up make the game feel awkward to play. Maybe some of them just require some getting used to, but some of them like the robot that the professor character has just feels odd to control. Another example would be powerups that change your bullets into other things. They seem cool on paper but some of them just make it harder to reliably hit anything. I suppose it's a trade off for more damage, but they can still feel incredibly awkward and leads to me avoiding those types of powerups.

The only suggestion I have to this would be to have a way to remove these things from your character during the run without hoping you find a drop that will let you exchange it for something else. Maybe the shopkeeper could have a scrapper similar to the weapon recyclers that you could dump items you no longer want.

Overall, this is a fantastic game and I highly recommend it.

The game also runs very well on Steam Deck if you plan on playing it on there, which at least half of my play time has been on so far.\t\t\t\t\t
