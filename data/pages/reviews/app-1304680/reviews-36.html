\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSUMMARY: A colorful, wacky, and deep roguelike twin stick shooter with plenty to do. Must-buy if that's up your alley, but there is some grinding involved.

Voidigo is about . . . well OK that's hard to explain. You start as the last survivor of a sort of religious order, the Void invaded your world, there's the Antivoid, mysterious beacons . . . things aren't so much explained as hinted at. Your goal as the survivor - and later people you unlock - is to battle the forced taken over by The Void and ultimately defeat it's ruler. Gear up, start shooting and start thinking.

You start by selecting a character, their weapon, and special ability - more of which unlock later. You then venture into four different worlds in order to battle monsters and bosses, find and buy weapons and power ups, and prepare to battle your ultimate enemy.

A twist on the standard game is the bosses. Bosses can be wounded but not defeated until you free all of the Beacons. They can also show up any time, meaning sometimes you're the hunter - and sometimes you're the hunted.

Between random environments, equipment, boss attacks and hunts, and special events, powerups, challenge rooms, and more every game run is very different. Even when you might get a little tired of a grind or a defeat, the next game is DIFFERENT.

Weapons and powerups are wild, interesting, and create fantastic combos - made even more compelling as powerups can be improved. Ever want to spawn lasers as you jump that then spin around you due to a special gravitational field? Ready to transform your bullets into flaming rats? The synergies of the equipment and abilities really affect the game - and lead you to trying new things.

Between runs you can spend tiny "void creatures" to unlock new characters, equipment, abilities, and pets that will show up in game. Note that you cannot make yourself more POWERFUL, just give yourself options in game. The game relies mostly on skill and learning the system, so you can't grind your way to easy success - but you will grind a bit.

You also head towards the final confrontation by collecting "essences" of bosses, weapons (by using them), powerups (by fully levelling them), and characters (by winning with them). You have to collect enough for you to get to the end gme - which is also a bit grindy (you'll notice a theme here).

And this leads to one (slight) flaw with Voidigo is that to win, you really do have to grind in a way that's options over power, reaching milestones not getting some benefit. That might not be for everyone who wants to grind until invulnerability.

At the same time, if you're going to have a grind-until-you-achieve-certain-things game, doing it like this - colorful, fun, compelling, is a damn good way to do it. Even when I got a little tired of the grind I went back in and had fun because the game is FUN.

So who's it for? Well if you read the above and it sounded good, you'll enjoy it. It really is an exceptional game.

The only warning I'd add is if you're used to "grind to unlock more power" roguelikes then this might not be for you. It's finding your best option, not leveling to invulnerability.

If you're on the fence then I can say get it. Because it is that good.
