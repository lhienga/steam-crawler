\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn the obscenely crowded genre of top-down bullet hell roguelites, I honestly feel that this game has the potential to be one of the best of its kind, maybe even the best. It already stands yards ahead of titles like Dungeon Souls and Mana Spark, but in my ideal world, this could be considered with the same reverence as Nuclear Throne or Enter the Gungeon. The main thing that's missing right now, in my opinion, is content, of all sorts.

The game's selection of weapons and upgrades are already a great design, comparable to the greats of the genre. Between items that transform your gun's bullets into homing mice and give you an extended arm to increase your melee weapon range, and guns that fire live fish or snake eggs, catapult exploding ghosts, or kick opponents with a giant spring-loaded boot, There's another fantastic item type, the buttons, but those also act in service of the game's best mechanic: the jump. Acting like an evolution to Gungeon's dodge roll, the jump allows you to avoid enemy attacks with greater mobility, but also stomp on enemies to stun them momentarily, stomp on buttons to trigger traps, and even chain together countless enemy stomps in a way that's more fun than function. And the fact alone that I said that, "more fun than function", should speak volumes about the quality of what they've built so far.

Now, I mentioned that the game is still missing content. Unfortunately, it's more than just true, it's very true. With only three levels available and 3-4 possible random bosses for the first two, with the last level being a combination of bosses from the other levels, it's currently very easy to see everything the game has to offer in a short period of time. Part of what gave Nuclear Throne and Gungeon their longevity was the sheer amount of variety available, between secrets that could lead to entirely new areas, hidden bosses, NPCs that introduced brand new gameplay systems and the like. I don't know what Voidigo's solution to this could be, but I think a good start would be to have more areas to explore. Maybe one more level before the final round in the void, but currently the loop system seems to be balanced around three levels. So maybe the levels could be randomly drawn from a pool each run, I don't quite know yet.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoguelike/lites are some of my all time favorite types of games, and I have played many. Voidigo takes many of the positive aspects of games like Nuclear Throne, Enter the Gungeon, Isaac, etc. and blends them with entirely new ideas and gameplay mechanics. This game is incredibly unique and satisfying, the base mechanics/movement/combat are very well implemented. I really enjoy the dynamic boss fights; instead of always having one room at the end of a level with a boss, you might get the boss in your second room. That sounds frightening, but you can run from the boss, or conversely you will later hunt down the boss as it runs from you! This means the boss fights take place across the entire level, which makes things much more varied and interesting than your typical boss fight. Coupled with the very smooth and satisfying controls/gameplay, Voidigo is a gem in the making! I absolutely cannot wait for more content, and for this game to grow. This could be the next Gungeon if the devs keep it up! There's already been numerous substantial updates since I bought this game, so I am eager and hopeful for this game's future. If you are a roguelike fan you must get this!\t\t\t\t\t
