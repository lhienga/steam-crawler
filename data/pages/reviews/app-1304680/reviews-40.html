\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs it is, it's extremely fun and an encouraging foundation. The way enemies attack, the player moves, it's all great. The way maps are laid out and how you go through levels is fun and I love the variety in the stages. That said, there are issues I ran into that bother me and I can't leave it unsaid, and right now the game feels a lot like a hyper polished demo. It's not that the content is lacking, but it's the choices they made in designing the passive items and how shallow the build diversity is.

At the moment though there's a serious choke in the passive items you receive since the VAST majority are clones of each other with different elements. The amount of synergy is actually quite low, particularly because all melee and ranged items are separate. Even basic "Your attacks are now water element" stuff, there's one for bullets and one for melee. There are a HUGE amount of these "elemental" passives completely flooding the loot table, so individual effects that you might want to make a build are extremely hard to find.

Melee weapons suffer a bit from what seems to be late-addition oversights. There are noticeably fewer of them, and since all passives apply to only one or the other, if you decide to play melee for a game, you're gonna have a rough time. Overall the system is super fun and I love the Monster Hunter feel, but a few bugs and lack of item and weapon diversity is keeping it in a chokehold.

Right now this game probably has the most bland item synergies and builds you can find in any game of this genre, mainly because of the fact that there's like 9 elements and SO MANY items are duplicated for each element. Once you see about 15 of the items, you've seen them all basically. A lot of them also gimp your weapons, like the homing and snake coatings. They don't really work like you'd expect, making the weapons either WAY too long range, or short range, but it's hard to find a happy medium.

I put in about 10 hours of solid gameplay, I unlocked all 5 characters and their weapons, and I've tried a lot of various things out, and I think I'll set the game aside for a while. They have a really good idea, the bosses are REALLY fun, the engine seems to be really good, but the items and weapons are not entertaining after the novelty of "Wow! What does this do??". Once you know all the items, that's when things should get interesting because you can start building crazy ♥♥♥♥, but this game gives you fewer choices overall compared to similar games, and the choices are pulled from a much shallower pool due to duplicated item types. Many of the items you gain also require you to give something up, so your power creep is also quite slow.

I'm going to give it a 5/10 overall. I will definitely be trying this later though. It just became bland very quickly. Powerups should make me excited, but I feel nothing when I see them appear because almost none of them are fun, AND they usually have costs associated with them. No thanks :(\t\t\t\t\t
