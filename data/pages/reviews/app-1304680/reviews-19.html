\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually write reviews, but I LOVE this game.

In some ways it feels like a spiritual successor to Enter the Gungeon, lots of fun, great mechanics, creativity to choke on.
The care in the animation part is just amazing, everything is so expressive, silly and charming. Have to admit, I would love to be able to talk with your characters.
Can be dense sometimes and very very chaotic in the last levels, but it has a lot of neat ideas that make it fluid to navigate and play.

It is quite hard, but it has difficulty levels, something rare in roguelikes.
My only gripe in this, is that bullets get faster with higher difficulty, so it can mess with muscle memory.

Nevertheless, any problem I find fades with every little thing I discover. It is just a joyful experience for me.

Thank you so much Semiwork, as someone who loves game design and animation, I take my hat off for you, I LOVE YOUR GAME.
I do hope it gets a physical version someday and maybe we can see some sort of early concept art. I need it on my shelf :D\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent moment-to-moment gameplay. Weapons feel distinct. Developing a build via pick-ups and item upgrades is satisfying and adequately change how you approach the run. I found myself gravitating towards certain builds more than others though. The sprint and jumping mechanics encourage aggressive gameplay and make the game stand out in a crowded genre. An accomplishment and an easy recommendation.

HOWEVER, though it is complete as is, and very good, I still want MORE. More floors, more build archetypes, more characters, more NPC encounters, more sidequests leading to different types of meta-progression. Maybe I / we all have been spoiled by Gungeon's mountains of content. The devs haven't provided any road map, but hopefully their success will lead to more Voidigo.

Runs on Steam Deck, but beware of slowdown when there are a lot of particles on screen, especially as you get farther in a run or loop runs.\t\t\t\t\t
