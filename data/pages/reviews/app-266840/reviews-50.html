With the battle cry of an immortal titan echoing in my mind, I present to you the Age of Mythology: Extended Edition. Strap in and be prepared to embark on an epic journey fueled by drama, action, humor, and of course, some signature gaming quotes. And worry not, for I have fact-checked and cross-referenced every bit of data that will be presented to you.

Just like a fresh layer of ice covers a frozen lake, Age of Mythology: Extended Edition has covered the classic Age of Mythology with a sheen of modern gaming technologies. The extended edition, much like its predecessor, is an epic adventure in the realms of resource management, strategy, and mythology. But let’s not sprint ahead and take a moment to appreciate the starting line.

The game works on a relatively simple concept. You collect resources, manage your economies, and deploy armies, much like its parent game, Age of Empires 2. The joy of the game stems from its unique twist on resource management, involving the fourth resource, "Favour." It differs based on the civilization you are playing, adding a layer of uniqueness to each race. And yes, as you guessed, this concept brings in the gods and their divine powers into the game, setting the stage for divine interventions and cataclysmic battles. It’s like Zeus tossing a lightning bolt right into your gaming experience. ⚡

The gameplay is an evolution from the Age of Empires series. It’s all about building your forces, upgrading, managing your population cap, and ensuring your economy thrives. It’s the classic rock-paper-scissors concept in play, with a dash of heroes and myth units to spice things up. The single-player campaign is an immersive experience that takes you through diverse landscapes and epic battles. Plus, the revival of multiplayer in this edition is like a phoenix rising from the ashes, albeit not as grand as the original AoM. 🎮

When it comes to the technical aspects of the game, Age of Mythology: Extended Edition is like a well-crafted ancient artifact. Despite being a remake, the game is relatively bug-free. Although it does reflect its age, subtle lighting effects, water, and shadows make it a lot more appealing. The game is very playable, and for a dedicated fan of the original, it wouldn't be a bother at all.

The campaign is a trip down memory lane, filled with quests across Greece, Egypt, Scandinavia, and finally Atlantis to stop a power-hungry cyclops from releasing Kronos, the King of Titans. Sounds familiar? Well, this new edition brings back these moments in an even more immersive environment. However, if you still have a copy of Age of Mythology and The Titans expansion, this edition might not add a lot to your collection. But for new players or fans of older RTSes, I strongly recommend it.

Now let's compare this mythological beast to its original. If you're looking for fresh content or gameplay changes, you might be a bit disappointed. This game is pretty much the same as the original, just with a better presentation and support for high-resolution screens. It's like meeting an old friend in new clothes, still the same at heart, but looking a lot sharper. For me, this familiarity brings a sense of comfort, reminding me of countless hours spent strategizing and battling against mighty foes. As an ice wolf who thrives on the thrill of the hunt, it was a pleasure to dive back into the same world, just with a fresh layer of snow. ❄️

However, there are a few concerns that need to be addressed. The multiplayer mode has faced some criticism due to consistent crashes and lag. As someone who enjoys a good multiplayer battle, this did put a dent in my gaming experience. Additionally, the AI during single-player matches can sometimes feel a bit off, randomly stopping in the middle of matches, which can be frustrating. Despite these issues, Age of Mythology: Extended Edition has mostly lived up to the reputation of its predecessor.

To wrap up my icy ramblings, Age of Mythology: Extended Edition is an impressive remake that manages to maintain the essence of the original while updating it for the modern era. However, if you're expecting something entirely new or ground-breaking, you might find it underwhelming. But if you're like me, a fan of the original who relishes in the art of strategy, then this is a game you cannot miss.

As a frosty wolf from the ice world who has spent countless hours gaming and supporting indie games on Kickstarter and Patreon, I give a frosty salute to Age of Mythology: Extended Edition. Despite some shortcomings, it managed to rekindle my nostalgia and provide an enjoyable gaming experience. Stay frosty, my furry friends, and keep gaming! 🐺💙🎮
