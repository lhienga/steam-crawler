\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood:
+ Repeat build que makes this reasonably playable with minimal button mashing, or bigger handholds like a villager AI.

+ Amazing campaign, missions aren't directly skirmish missions, but instead blend normal game-play very well with story concessions.

+ It runs stable on modern systems.

+ They tried to improve balance of a very exploit driven game. Can't fault them for that.

+ New units to old factions. They tried, even if there are issues.

+ Any graphical issues are easy to fix, great workshop mods exist to improve the textures massively. It won't look 2023, but it won't look worse then 2002 if you look at the most popular items for half a second.

+ You can draw walls in any directions and have them mesh in with your buildings/outposts, combining the base style of AOE2 with the building QOL of AOE4.

Bad:
- DLC where all the ideas that were rejected from being Egyptian features for various reasons got ported into a new faction, the Chinese. Given, these features aren't in Egypt because they were well, dismissed for balance reasons... Its a bit of a disaster.

- Loki's ability can crash the game of your hesir spawns a Chinese unit on accident.

- The new units mess with faction vibes by breaking weaknesses. Norse being countered by air is now the norse casually stomping all air better then anyone else. The hydras now can easily be healed until they have 5 heads consistently (Giving them titan level damage.) The atlanteans have a third unit that totally shuts down naval and fortification based plays. ETC ETC. The only faction whose vibe isn't substantially changed is the Egyptians.

- As the game is super asymmetrical, and very exploity, multiplayer is possibly difficult to get into. Netcodes also reportedly bad so good luck even trying with friends.

Neutral:
~ The one underutilized aspect of the campaign is here, factions almost always fight their own culture in the campaign. So many of the cool aspects of the different culture groups fighting in non symmetrical ways is mostly missing from it. (With a few cool exceptions.) Still, not having the factions be a mishmash still keeps each chapter as being tightly themed.

~ The campaign often starts you at age 3, so you often end up with a repeated god power loadout of regeneration/bronze/bolt. Which limits how many choices you can make. This isn't a bother the first time you play, but it is something you will notice if you are vaguely familiar with the game.

~ Only atlanteans have recharging powers, so very marginal benefit powers are still single use. (But are often compensated for by great unique techs and powers to go with them... Still annoying to have weak powers be single use.)

~ Villagers are capped, pop sizes are small. So some build creativity is limited, but you don't have to worry about having zero military pop from economy units overbuilding without you trying to break things with markets/docks.

~ The settlements system gives the random map design some control over how your base is shaped, but it also lets the devs implement one of the strongest versions of the market in a way that is fair.\t\t\t\t\t
