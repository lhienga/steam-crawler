\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a longtime fan who has sunk hours into the original version, the Titans expansion, and the Gold Edition, I can definitively recommend this version of the RTS classic. However, I will warn you that it is not a perfect version.

The short video tutorials are fantastic at teaching newcomers the ins and outs of each culture, which I have been told is slightly daunting. The single player campaign modes, in addition to telling a pretty decent story that weaves together popular myths from each respective culture, acts as a fantastic way for beginners to deepen their knowledge of the game, and actually start coming up with strategies (also shout-out to the mission 21 in the original campaign, the best one). Most importantly, playing online with friends is the easiest it has ever been, thanks to Steam.

Unfortunately the "extended" part of this edition is misleading. Sure the improved visuals are nice, but they occasionally bug out leading to wave-less oceans, pitch-black ocean floors, and other texture glitches. Even worse, the game also tends to crash to desktop or lose connection with other players. However, the joy of sharing one of my favorite video games easily with my friends is enough for me to look past these annoyances. In my opinion, the fun of the original game should still be enough to draw people in, and it is enough for me to recommend it.

Overall, this game is the reason why I got 100% on my Bullfinch's Mythology exam in 10th grade Honors English, wrecking the curve for the class and alienating me from my peers. Would do again.\t\t\t\t\t
