\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first I really didn't like this game.

However, once I stopped being annoyed that is was so dissimilar from it's predecessor, and stopped thinking of it like a flight simulator and more like an aerial combat simulator, I grew to like it. Now I really like it.

The flight dynamics are bit to get used to if you're used to the likes of FSX other early simulator platforms. The flight in here is a bit more arcade without actually being arcade, yet still feeling more like a sim. If that makes sense. Do yourself a favor, let the game manage your mixture, fuel, trim, and other flight management and just focus on getting used to the game and fighting. Then, see if you want to turn control of those features on.

As far as the rest or the game? Great. The damage is awesome, and I find the AI to be the very fun to engage. There is a broad variety of missions.

There is paid DLC, and considering the creators of this game are a small independent group of dudes, I'm fine with that.
