\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIl-2 Sturmovik is a great sim. I highly recommend it, and it's a game worth supporting.

My biggest complaint about the series is that it still feels more like a framework for a game than a game. There are no tutorials; the campaigns are rather dry. I generally feel like I am playing a flight sim that faithfully recreates these war machines, but I don't really feel like I'm a pilot fighting in a war. I don't really identify with my in-game character as someone I care about, nor do I really feel like I'm impacting anything. Radio chatter is generic, rather than feeling like actual communication between people fighting for their lives. When you go back to the great sims of the 90s, I think they did a better job of making you feel like you had a stake in the game world.

That being said, I'm hopeful the devs will work to improve this aspect of the sim. And, despite what I think is a need for some major rethinking of gameplay design, this is probably the best you can do for a WWII sim these days, so it's worth supporting.\t\t\t\t\t
