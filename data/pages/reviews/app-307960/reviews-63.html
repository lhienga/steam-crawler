\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI must admit, I bought this Sim because it was in the sale and it was a path to the Hawker Tempest, the top dog of the British piston era aircraft, but I just didn't expect it to be this good. The immersion, when in the cockpit, is staggering. The sounds of the engine and guns is the best I've heard anywhere. I would class it as the best WW II sim available.
Quick Missions is the place to get you started and practiced in the art of aerial combat and setting up of the controls, which will take some time as they vary between different planes, but you do have a choice of levels of reality.
I soon bought ten DLC which included Battle of Bodenplatte, a Rhineland late war map and I am currently running a Tempest career and three scripted missions, Russian Yak 1, German Bf 109 and American Lightning. This lot would be expensive normally, but in a sale it's peanuts.
I've never enjoyed air to air in any other sim and I've been flying them for years, but this one does it for me.
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2227684541
A few more hours and I love the Tempest. Powerful, fast, with a heck of a punch from its 4 20mm cannon. My career is going well.
I usually start my mission in the air and land back at base. You can also finish in the air, but take a tip, if you do so, make sure you're not over enemy territory, or "for you Tommy ze war is over" and you have to start over again.
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2213013697..
80 hrs now and I'm sharing time with Rise of Flight.
Bought, B o Kuban in the sale and have started a career with the P-39.
This is hard work compared to the Tempest, as it is a much less capable aircraft. Flying against 109's is a real rush. The 37mm cannon sounds good, but its low velocity is poor for turning fights.
Anybody who flies DCS, will be at home in Kuban, as it is part of the Caucasus region and it will include anti ship strikes. I bought it for the P-39, (hated by the British, loved by the Russians) and Yak 7b. A-20 is included if you're into bombing.
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2264546854

225 hours and I'm starting to get the hang.....

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is good fun for me. I do enjoy seeing the progression of my squadron pilots as the war progresses, seeing one get shot down and captured and thinking "well, he's not coming home". Graphics are good, damage models seem somewhat basic but the damage simulation itself is decent enough. So I recommend the game for fans of the series or maybe some of the old Combat Flight Sim crowd.

I cannot speak to the quality of the DLC because, to be quite honest, they are almost all completely out of my price range. They obviously put in a great amount of work on the game and I want to support it, but holy crap do I have better things to spend $70-$100 on. Maybe if they were just reduced a bit they might see an increase in sales of them, and thus more money for their work.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood simulation game which really gives you the emersion of WW2 fyling.

I like the planes and the maps.

However, many Multipayer maps are late war meaning you have to buy all the DLCs or otherwise you don't have competive aircraft. Good things though is that the base planes in the DLC seem to be competitive and you don't need the collector planes.

When playing bombers you are fine. Plan your flight path carefully and you can succeed.
Yet, many Bombers are early war versions in the game so it can take you a while to get to the target.
60 mins for a mission in multiplayer should be accounted for (take off to landing).

Yet, when you want to have a figthing chance in a dogfight with a figther, you need a head tracking systems.

These range from 70 to 200€, depending on the system.
Otherwise you don't see the enemy and loose the dogfight.
So, consider this when you wan't to focus on this type of plane.

Spotting is a real issue most times you don't see the enemy.
Which is understandable, yet it can be very frustrating flying 20 mins with your ground attacker to a target area where you get immediately shot down by a plane you don't see.

I stopped playing fighters for now and wait for my head tracker.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBad controls, and the gameplay isn't really fun enough to want to sort them out.

Even on a good PC the game can take awhile to load the 3D menu, and it pesters you for an IL-2 account. The program itself hates to minimise.

The controls are a nightmare throughout the game. The cursor can duplicate itself, so you get separate cursors that move at slightly different speeds, and you have to keep track of which is the real one. The pause mechanic is actually separate from pause menu (this is the only game I know that separates them, and it's just a pointless feature). By default the game uses mouse control for flight, and it's awful, which blows my mind considering that Battlefield 1942 nailed it 12 years earlier.

The engine controls are a disaster. Even holding down the higher engine setting, my plane sometimes fell out of the sky from speed loss at level flight. They wanted to add unique buttons for each aircraft's engine systems, but I don't have a real cockpit for each plane. Controls get even more complex as the pilot head movement is separate from pilot head pan which is separate from the camera controls, ontop of the confusing gun turret/sight/zoom functions, with the options to toggle or reset being broken or missing.

Even the gamepad (I used a Xbox 360 USB) was terrible by default, and the lowest sensitivity setting throwing the aircraft around in jerks, leading you to only take potshots as your crosshair passes over the target for a moment between each jerk. There's a deadzone style sensitivity setting which helps, and it visually shows the impact it has as you change it, which is nice, but it's hidden away in the key bindings.

The joystick (Saitek X52) is marginally better, but trying to take the game seriously is so frustrating with all of the control problems.

VR mode (Valve Index) was clearly an afterthought, as I had to keep switching between VR controllers, mouse, keyboard, and gamepad/joystick - awkward when you've got the headset on. It's a bit too pixelated to shoot accurately, so the zoom function would be critical but it's broken in VR mode.

A lot of the cannon shells are next to useless, as you can pepper tanks, boats, and planes with hundreds of hits and they survive. I literally kept hitting a plane with unlimited 37mm rounds until I got bored, and it was still flying. Trying to hit trains is ridiculous because they often snap into reverse at full speed.

The DLC looks like a mess, with individual planes for sale in a game that's already expensive and only offers you a few in the base game. There are more aircraft in the downloaded game files but you can't fly them.

As a simulator you can get some fun out of it, but it's so user-unfriendly that overall the experience is awkward. Where are you supposed to get the motivation from to deal with how messy it is? I can't think of anyone that I'd happily recommend it to.\t\t\t\t\t
