\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis has been my favorite game of 2020
I play almost exclusively multiplayer, so my review will be geared towards that.
As it stands, the IL2 Battle of X series is the most comprehensive and most active WW2 Air combat simulator out there.
The only real competitor is DCS (Which has its own merits), but the player community and plane selection is tiny in comparison to this.

The servers I most play on:
Finnish Virtual Pilots - Active during peak EU and US times - focuses on a dynamic frontline - rotates plane sets/maps every couple days
Combat Box - US server - Focused on Late War aircraft and custom theme based missions
Wings of Liberty - Russian Server, Active during EU - Mainly Early-Mid War focus

So which DLC Do I buy?:
!Note! You can buy the base game on steam and subsequent DLC on the developer's website. The items there go on sale more often, and you are able to buy "Deluxe" editions of all modules giving you extra planes

Generally servers (and thus their planesets) will fall under 3 eras: Early, Mid and Late
In Chronological order, the main modules are Moscow, Stalingrad (The base game on steam), Kuban, Normandy (Early Access) and Bodenplatte

With the base game, you'll be able to participate in almost all scenarios except the very late war. (Which is exclusively Western Front)

If you are strapped for cash I would recommend this purchase order:
Stalingrad,
Kuban,
Bodenplatte,
Moscow (This module has some iconic/interesting planes, but all multiplayer scenarios I've played also feature Stalingrad era aircraft too)

Extra DLC:
There are other "collector planes" which can be purchased standalone or as part of deluxe editions.
The most interesting/fun/best planes go as follows
LA5/LA5FN - Two of the fastest planes available to the Russians, extremely competitive
P-38 - American fighter/bomber - not the best air-air platform, but extremely capable ground ordnance - very unique and fun
FW-190 D9 - The longnose Dora, one of (if not the best) planes in the game (German latewar)

Addendum:
VR in this game is absolutely fantastic, it will spoil any other flight simulators that don't support it for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOriginal Review at the bottom just for transparency
This is a game that you need to spend time with. There are many controls, many things to learn, with no tuition. But the upside to this is once you let go of trying to perfect it on day one, and just have fun, it becomes immersive quite quickly. There are enough settings to make flying easy to start off with and you can start your missions already in the air - so no take offs etc. Gradually though, you'll start to turn off the auto help and eventually you'll be able to fly the plane fully manually, at this point it becomes a very fluid natural simulator.

I'm playing on a NVidia RTX 3090, but before on a RTX 2080 Super it was still amazing graphics. The cockpits are incredibly detailed in 4k, and the towns, rives, roads and sea below - even though a bit pedestrian sometimes are still interesting enough to get satisfaction on bombing runs.

I should also say I am playing this on VR - the Oculus Rift S, and this is why I really love this game. There are some glitches ie random strobing when the game is starting, but once the game is running I havent had any crashes or spinning rooms etc. I couldnt find how to reset the centre of the VR, but there is a button that resets you in the centre of the VR, '3' does it. Once I found this is was much easier.

The game can be frustrating if you die, or land on enemy territory as that character is no longer payable, but the satisfaction of bombing runs, or ground attacks, or dogfights is really amazing. I hated this game to start with and now I really enjoy it.

I now own an X52 Pro Hotas, and I even bought Rudder Pedals, there is nothing better than this now in VR !

I am changing my review to a Recommended.

Original Review
Lots of fun, but there are problems with Oculus Rift. When the loading screen starts ie when quitting/starting a game you get a mad strobe effect, I have to take the headset off at this point. Also it seems sometimes when the game starts and you're in a plane you end up sitting in the floor of the plane, you cant re-align the view using the Oculus menu, neither can you windows key out the window to use the Steam VR view reset. The game itself is great if it works, but there are also problems with some of the planes, a few slight movements and you are upside down, this is with all the assists on and weather off, so I dont think its due to being realistic. I think rather than creating more content for DLC, the developers should play the game themselves with the Oculus, get a feel for whats not working and get the quality up by fixing some of the bugs. Then I would recommend the game as a 5 star, its definitely a huge achievement, and its so close to being bloody amazing - but that is the story of 2020.\t\t\t\t\t
