\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery immersive, detailed and historically accurate combat simulator. The learning curve is fairly big for an aviation rookie like myself, meaning there are countless hours of progress and learning to be had. One of the bigger downfalls is that multiplayer servers are fairly empty - with only a few lobbies generally populated at a time, there is no issue finding games just you will come across the same players and find the same servers populated regularly.

Also you will need the proper hardware to be competitive in this game, that means head tracking/HOTAS/Rudder pedals a decent sized monitor and a PC with averagely good spec. If you go in with just a Logitech joystick like myself you should be ready to accept you are fighting an uphill battle and just enjoy the game for what it is.

That said you can have tons of fun with bombers using just a joystick, the battles are strategic with each side having objectives to destroy and protect.. this can range from factories or oil facilities to tank columns and anti aircraft guns each needing a different approach to destroy. There do not seem to be very many good bombardiers that play this game - despite them having the largest influence over battles on the popular servers by destroying all objectives and ending the game abruptly (this is where I have all my fun).

Generally you will find a lot of people playing fighter planes leaving the bombing down to others, so there is definitely currently a void to be filled there and a lot of skill that comes with precision bombing. The required on the fly bombsight calculations and engine management also adds a nice touch to the whole game.\t\t\t\t\t
