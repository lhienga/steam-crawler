\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt only looks like I have 5.1 hrs in this game. I do have a lot more as I purchased it when it first came out. IL2 looks amazing. In VR, it is very impressive and a delight to look at. If you have any inkling that you might like to try this, or if you like flight sims, this game is worth the full price. I see it is currently on sale. How could you not pick it up for this price? Even if you wait a few years before trying a sim, its totally worth it.

The only complain I have about this game is the time warp feature. It doesn't really get you into the action quickly. You can do quick missions, but those are not tied to a character you create. So, depending on what type of plane you want to fly, you might be spending a lot of time twiddling your thumbs traveling to your target area. While this might be an accurate representation of what it was actually like, it doesn't always make for the most fun gameplay. So, pick a fighter or ground attack squadron closer to the front if you don't want to spend tons of time getting to your target.

Other than that, there are a lot of planes to choose from with just the base BoS. You get more with each DLC. I was happy to get some of my favorite planes with some of the DLC. Especially some of the lend-lease US fighters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOH YES.

No clickable cockpits and no complex startup procedures, other than that there is no WWII simulator --or dare I say game-- so intricate, historically accurate and replayable as this. This is the end of the line. One could write eternally about all the good things, about how it's possible to stick to a single plane for hours just to try to get that landing right, engine management, etc.: the point being: this is a proper simulation through and through.

Disclaimer #1: you need a joystick to enjoy this game to its fullest, and the DLC, which you can get on a discount, for it's otherwise extremely expensive (but I would say worth it even at full price, for every plane and scenario is filled to the brim with details).

Disclaimer #2: even though the included career mode is glorious (it requires an internet connection), the SP portion of this game shines with PCWG, which lets you create in-depth careers within every time-frame and for ever nation.
