\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall: A fun and/but challenging true combat flight simulator with a steep learning curve

Having played many different combat flight games over the years (Microsoft CFS1 - 3, the original IL-2, Wings of Prey, etc), here are my thoughts on Battle of Stalingrad. This review only reflects single player as I have not tried multiplayer.

Positives
Visuals / graphics:
-The aircraft and ground vehicles are beautifully rendered on the inside and outside. A lot of attention to detail, especially on crews of other vehicles. Bombers are especially detailed.
-Scenery is not bad for a flight sim; doesn't compare to FS2020 but this is an older game and won't kill your PC
-Combat destruction physics and animations are very detailed. Broken windscreens and mirrors, etc. Ships burn and sink based on where you hit them. Individual AA batteries on ships can be knocked out

Simulation:
-I haven't actually played this on full manual control yet - too challenging for me, see Cons below. But you can fully customize the difficulty settings to maintain realistic flight models and pilot physiology while still automating engine control. So the game is still challenging but not too overwhelming
-AI pilots are decent and actually seem to employ offensive and defensive tactics
-Can't say for sure since I've never flown a plane, but the flight models seem accurate. Each plane has its own strengths and weaknesses, and there is no artificial balancing. You have to learn what tactics work for each plane, which is part of the fun and authenticity. Also, the physics are realistic. Damage impacts performance depending on where you are hit. Ordinance adversely impacts performance, particularly the heavy bombs

Interface/gameplay:
-It's easy enough to set up a variety of quick mission modes if you just want to fly
-One of the few WW2 sims you can fly bombers with detailed interiors

Negatives
-The original game only includes a few flyable aircraft (though this includes bombers). This is understandable given the excruciating level of detail given to each plane, but it gets old fast, especially as I find the early-war (Stalingrad) German fighters significantly superior to the Russian. Add-on packs and planes are rather pricey but you can sometimes get them on sale

-Key bindings are complex, and sometimes glitchy when trying to customize your own mappings. Also, the default viewing controls are not intuitive at all

-AI anti-aircraft and bomber gunners seem unrealistically accurate. On Ace difficulty it can be almost impossible lining up for an accurate ground attack run without quickly being shot to pieces by your target

-My biggest complaint is that there is no real tutorial for bombing / ground attack or dog-fighting. Even for an experienced flight sim person it would be really helpful for learning the default key bindings.

None of these negatives are deal-breakers and I still find it to be a fun and addicting game. Blowing up ground targets in a P-51 and dog-fighting in a Fw190D-9 (my favorite plane) never get old!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll heartily recommend IL-2: BoS to anyone who is interested in fighters and air combat on the Eastern Front during the second world war. There are a bunch of caveats to this recommendation, however:
  1. Il-2: BoS is incredibly, almost unbelievably, bare-bones. The developer has put absolutely minimal effort into making a campaign engine, or any interesting single-player campaigns. There's a lot of excellent player tools that have been built to make things more interesting... but is that really a recommendation or a condemnation?
  2. 1C have made a fantastic airplane flight model, but tying into the above caveat, they have put borderline negative effort into actual simulation. You have a couple radio barks... and that's it.
  3. Incendiaries are only now starting to be maybe modeled at some point in the future, now six years after launch.
  4. There are (ahem) Zero plans to do anything with the PTO, mostly because the developers are real sticklers for documentation on aircraft and it's hard enough to find a surviving A6M airframe, let alone the plans or specs for them. Also, the USSR/Russia didn't really have any combat in the PTO so 1C have zero interest in making the PTO happen. (A fair decision, even if my favorite warbird happens to be the F4U.)
  5. There are no heavy bombers. Yes! You read that right, there are absolutely zero heavy bombers (the principal weapon of the USAAF/RAF) in this game, essentially because they weren't used all that much on the eastern front so 1C has no interest in actually modeling them as player aircraft, or even as things to escort, flying targets, or even just as decor. This, combined with the first two caveats produces the final conclusion:

IL-2:BoS is fantastic as an asymmetric PvP dogfight game, with many realistic flourishes and elements to add depth to the experience. It is absolutely lackluster outside of the blood gulch it lives in, barren of simulation that it is, without putting a lot of effort into modding it or otherwise taking matters into your own hands.

It's a good gulch, though.

e: I almost forgot - any airplanes with multiple fuel tanks and a single fuel gauge (e.g. IL-2, Fw 190, etc.) switch which tank they're showing the reading from every few seconds. 1C insists that there is some technical issue that prevents them from letting pilots select which tank is being shown on the gauge, even though there's a knob or lever right there next to it, even though the game engine itself is doing it already in an automated fashion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game overall and I would argue that it does WWII substantially better than DCS World overall. The missions are fun and the aircraft look, feel, and handle exactly as they should. I found that although the level of detail on my Mk IX Spitfire in DCS is a bit higher, the aircraft handling of the models in IL-2 are surprisingly better and more authentic feeling to true aircraft. If you are a pilot or someone whose ridden in aircraft while they are performing to their max, you'll appreciate the buffet, wing drops, and stall/spin characteristics of these historic aircraft.

Pros:
-Fantastic graphics for aircraft and terrain
-Good sounds and effects.
-Excellent damage models
-Decent value for the amount of content you get, even in the base game. Remember that this is a simulator after all and you can't expect to get 30 aircraft in the base game like you might with a simple arcade game like Ace Combat. Each aircraft model is meticulously crafted from its handling to its fully functioning instruments and aircraft specific features.

If you do compare this game to DCS aircraft by aircraft, DCS is definitely better but the cost barrier in DCS is massive compared to this game. Even at full price this game is absolutely worth it if you want a true WWII aircraft simulator and excellent aircraft models.\t\t\t\t\t
