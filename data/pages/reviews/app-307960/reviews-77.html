\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIL-2 Sturmovik is a game series that is often spoken very fondly of by its dedicated players, and this is not without reason--the games are intensively realistic, and are sometimes a joy to play in my own experience. This one just doesn't sit well with me for a number of reasons.

For one, the business model of the game is absolutely awful. I remember playing IL-2 Sturmovik: 1946 for the first time, and being blown away by the fact that there were so many aircraft to choose from and fly, where in this one, You have just a handful of aircraft to start out with, and the rest have to be acquired through purchasing DLC. Whatever happened to the days of a complete game being sold where the DLC just felt like icing atop the cake, I don't know; but this game certainly falls prey to this as well.

For two, it requires an extremely beefy computer to run well on. I played on a potato for seven years before getting a new laptop purposed for gaming (yes, I did commit that sin), and even then--this game still struggles to run at 30 FPS with minimum visual settings applied. In other words, if you have anything less than a full, dedicated, gaming PC, don't even try this game--it just won't run well, and you'll be disappointed like I was.

For three, and this is a sin of ALL IL-2 Sturmovik games, it has a learning curve worse than many, many games I have played before. Custom key-mapping will take you a very long time involving much trial and error, and for the new and uninitiated to the IL-2 Sturmovik franchise, you are in for one heckuva time trying to learn how to play.

Overall, to those who have been playing IL-2 Sturmovik games for years (not me), this game is probably quite fun--and I do intend to try playing it more when I finally possess a computer powerful enough to handle it. But, overall... I just can't recommend this game for the reasons I have outlined above.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is easily one of, if not THE best WW2 flight sims out there. I'll attempt to break it down.

Pros: While being behind a paywall, there are so many aircraft to collect, and if you go the route I did, and buy BoBP here, along with the standard BoS, you can buy the collector planes and other campaigns and maps for what seems like pennies. At the time of writing, all of the single player campaigns on the IL-2 store are under $3, and I picked up four planes for under $18. It is absurdly simple to link your accounts, and I would fully recommend doing so.
The community in the public servers, and on the forums, is, with a few exceptions, one of the friendliest I have ever seen.
The game itself is well-made, with a few bugs here and there, but nothing game-breaking at this point. Beyond that, it can be simple, belly-laughing fun!

Cons: The learning curve is steep, with every aircraft being different. If you are used to a DCS-like combat system, you WILL be frustrated, and you WILL die. Often. In horrible, embarrassing ways. You will have to memorize start-up procedures for many different aircraft, ballistics, engine settings, and flight characteristics for each plane as you buy and fly them.
Spotting is difficult. It often becomes a game I like to call, "Is that a plane or a speck of dirt on my monitor?" If you fly in, or want to fly in VR, It becomes orders of magnitude more difficult. There are ways to make it simpler, such as the 3Dmigoto mod, but until VR headsets reach a level of resolution that has yet to be seen, you will suffer from "screen-door" and various graphical issues that make it difficult.

I would never want to scare anyone off of this game, Lord knows the flight-sim community needs all the support it can get, but I want to be as un-biased as possible.

My personal opinion is this: Get this game. Join a squadron that can help you grow as a pilot. Play the single-player missions and get used to the mechanics of the game.

Fly safe!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat simulator for having fun fast and without any troubles.
---------------------
Sound effects, flying physics, picture that you have on screen - incredible.
In order to play normally you need to have joystick and trackIR / VR headset
---------------------
There is a nice PVP server named "Berloga". Tons of dogfights, tons of fun, tons of crashes and struggling, but come on, it's a simulator, not an arcade game!
---------------------
A lot of planes are included in DLS's but you can buy even more of them if you want to.
(wait for sales, aircrafts are really cheap those days)
And if prices are still too much for you - look at the DCS prices, or go and try to play survive in War Thunder without donating in it ;)
---------------------
The only big disadvantage of this game that I found - that there is no real reason to play. You can't research anything, can't unlock something special, can't have medals or special skins for some amount of kills using particular plane.
So you play just to play and kill only for fun, but maybe that's what simulatoirs are invented for.
----------------------------------
I recommend this game, but not for beginners in flight simulators. If you are a beginner and you have some thoughts about buying this game and putting a lot of time in flight simulators - maybe try simulator battles in War Thunder. It's free and might give you basic understanding of that type of simulators.
And if after playing that you are stil interested in this - go ahead, buy IL-2 and be ready to enjoy this experience!\t\t\t\t\t
