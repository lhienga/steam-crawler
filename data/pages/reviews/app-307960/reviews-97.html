There are many DLC's that make the game unbalanced in multiplayer (late war planes against earlier models). Whenever new content is released, I find it hard to get excited because I know it's going to take more money out of my pocket to buy it, because this content is accessible only by paid DLC, not by base game.

That being said, the DLC's are as expensive as the base game. This is because all the DLC's you see on steam would be separate games if you bought them from their website. That means you don't need il-2: Battle of Stalingrad to purchase DLC. If you buy from the website you can choose what game you want to purchase without spending 2x as much as you would on Steam.

For example, if I was interested in buying il-2: Battle of Bodenplatte, I would have to pay over $100 on Steam because I would need to buy il-2: Battle of Stalingrad first. But if I bought it online, I can simply buy il-2: Battle of Bodenplatte by itself, saving me about $50.

Overall, the games themselves are good, this little cash grab isn't. If you're looking to buy any of the games/DLC's, purchase it from their website (https://il2sturmovik.com/) to avoid this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an excellent flight simulator and currently the best one on the market. I bought this way back in 2014 when the game was only the battle of stalingrad. Back then the game was a horrible mess with all kinds of performance issues and major russian bias that would make the early days of war thunder blush. But, they have continued to update it with a major focus on accuracy and now the game runs vr and overall performs great. The damage model has been improved even more over the original ones and they have been able to update this game through the development of their new games.

Now one thing to get strait, There are 6 Games right now that are playable and 1 in the works. Tank crew, battle of kuban, battle of moscow, battle of bodenplatte, and flying circus are all sepparate games. They all include 8 planes (10 for special edition) and 1 map. All but flying circus do also include a career mode or campaign and can be flown without owning stalingrad if you buy off of the website. However unlike games like battlefield or call of duty which release their games as sepparate entities which do not include crossplay with older versions, this game does. In doing so it makes it so that a game released in 2014 is still getting updates and in 10 years from now will most likely keep up with the times as the engine continues to grow.

Overall I highly recommend this game to people who want a realistic flight sim and althentic depiction of the battles in the skys over the various fronts represented in this and all of the other il2 great battle series games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't have interest in the flight sim, but bought it as I love tank sims. I've played panzer elite, achtung panzer, kharkov, Hell Let Loose etc so this is just my impression from the tank sim side.

The controls are incredibly un-intuitive, it's as if whoever designed the controls has never played a tank sim before. You can tell it was made by someone who designs plane simulators, not tank sims. A good example is simply moving positions and zooming in the gun turret. Almost every other tank sim lets you do this by pressing a number key and using the scroll wheel. Here you have to hold down multiple keys to do it. Wanna zoom in on the gun? Hold down shift AND scroll wheel. It's pointless and makes the learning curve needlessly harder.\t\t\t\t\t

I have problems with my hands after many ops which makes keyboard controls painful after a while which means I am more at home with a controller than a mouse. I've had the game some time and when the tanks were introduced I just had to try as an ex cavalry man. Now I sit in pain having a ball, I admit I am still rolling around the quick missions but this is enough to keep me happy. Graphics OK, key mapping means watch out for ANYTHING that may coincide with the camera controls if you are not a mapper by nature (I'm not).
If you aren't into flight sims (and this is one of the most satisfying) but enjoy being one of the elite (cavalry...I did mention my bias :) ) then grab this next time it is on offer, well worth the time spent learning the game. The first hit is satisfying and when you get your first tank kill I bet you're smiling and thinking "F*ck, yeah".
if I did fractions it would be an 8.5/10 but I don't and a 9/10 is more worthy than an 8/10.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been flying flight sims since the early '90's and this is by far and way the best I've played.

Whether you will enjoy the game depends on a lot of things.

First, this is a flight/combat simulation. This is not an arcade game. As a simulation you need to
1) manage your engine or it will blow up.
2) learn to fly smoothly through the air or you will stall, rip your wings off or blackout and crash into the ground
3) learn to deflection shoot by leading your target.
4) drop a bomb from sufficient altitude or the the ensuing explosion will kill you
5) don't fly over concentrations of enemy AA or it will kill you
6) learn to land by cropping your speed, extending flaps, locking your tail wheel and flaring just before touching down
7) learn to manage engine torque at takeoff or you will ground loop or worse

As I said this is not an arcade game.

Second is how much money you are willing to spend or situational awareness and flight controls.

For situational awareness I use Track IR. Others use 3D VR. I personally think the little "hat" commonly found on joysticks to be useless. In a dogfight there are a lot of planes swirling about and it's easy to lose SA. I suppose you could play it without Track IR or 3D VR but I personally don't. and I'd be surprised if you would like the experience.

I use the full Thrustmaster control set, with joystick, sliding throttle, and rudder pedals. I consider the rudder pedals to be optional. If you only use a mouse and keyboard to fly, look somewhere else.

I'm not a pilot so I can't comment on the authenticity of the flight models, but they seem right. Dive too fast and your control surfaces seem less responsive. Climb straight up and you will stall. Or kick the rudder at the last second and hammerhead and head back down. The WWII era planes have enough power to complete the basic loops, Split-S, scissors, snap roll, barrel roll etc. without being overpowered. A typical dogfight may start high but usually ends up on the deck after a prolonged fight as energy is lost. An intangible is that these simulated planes seem to have mass and inertia. I now that all flight sims use physics based flight models at account for mass and inertia, but in this game you can feel it though the response of the controls

Initially I stayed away from the game because it was too expensive and had some teething issues. First, the developers have done a good job constantly upgrading their game. Updates and content addition are frequent and useful. Second, wait until one of the seasonal sale prices on Steam. I got my game at a huge discount by being patient.

Lastly, the developers have made great strides in AI. Even as little as five years ago, when you got on the tail of and EA it would just dive for the ground and turn in circles until you killed it. Now I've see it zoom climb away, go into a horizontal scissors, drop flaps in a turn fight, fly into the sun to spoil my aim, or just use superior speed to extend in a climb an reengage with and altitude advantage.

At the same time the AI doesn't seem to cheat. For example, I've often gotten on the 6 of an EA and been below him in his blind spot. Rather than being "magically" aware I'm in his bland spot the EA will fly straight and level. Enemy "Ace" EA however are apparently programmed to "clear their six" every 30 seconds or so. You'll actually see then jink their tail, look for an EA then pull hard G's if they see you.

Lastly the in game campaign is a vast improvement over earlier attempts in Rise of Flight or Cliffs of Dover. I would give it a solid "B" now. But I would highly recommend getting Pat Wilson's IL-2 Campaign generator. It gives you much more variety in missions and encounters and can be tailored to your tastes.

In summary this is the most immersive combat flight sim I've played. So enjoyable I plan on getting Bodenplatte and Flying Circus after they are available on Steam
