\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust hard enough to make you feel like you're playing a true flight sim, without having to sit through ten hours of ground school to learn the mechanics and keybinds of flight first. Graphically it's very stable and looks pretty good. Not as great as DCS, but you don't get FPS drops on public servers like you can on DCS. Much more optimized. Most if not all of the aircraft are finished excluding ones that are clearly marked in the early access queues [which you can't buy into unless you go to their website rather than through steam] so you don't have to wait for six months for them to add vital components to an airframe [coughf18dcscough]. Comes with normal campaigns for each DLC/expansion as well as the main game, as well as a dynamic campaign, single missions and an easy to use quick mission launcher. The mission editor isn't difficult to learn and there's external tools like Pat Wilson's Campaign Generator that'll allow you to toss together missions for co-op or single play.

The DLC can be a little pricey on release, but unlike another flight sim [DCS] where you'll rarely see primary modules go on sale for significant prices, you can usually snag the expansions for a song.

Really my only issue with the game in general is how they have the multiplayer setup. In order to host your own server for friends to join it requires a little router port forwarding which is very 2002. Not entirely certain why they split the multiplayer modes into co-op [no respawn, PVP and PVE] or dogfight [respawns can be added, PVP and PVE]. Just kind of an odd choice.

If you're looking for a flight sim that won't break your wallet or computer, this is probably the one for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere do I start?
I have always played the Il2 Sturmovik series - that is no exaggeration - I picked up my first installment at age 10, which means I have played for half my life.
To talk about the series as a whole would take up so much time that you'd probably die of old age by the time I finished, so I'll keep it simple. It's had it's ups and downs.

This installment though? What a bloody masterpiece. If you're here then I'm guessing you already have some Idea of what the IL2 series is, and that is a Combat flight simulator with a heavy emphasis on realism, through its flight and damage models. In all of these aspects Il2 Sturmovik Excels, aircraft are painstakingly researched and their flightmodels studied in order to bring them as close to life as possible.

Every instrument in every single cockpit works as it did during the second world war, every pull of the flight stick, every bullet fired immerses you in a way that I have not felt in any other game, having been a pilot myself in my spare time, I can say that of the limited simulators I have used, this one feels the most like reality. The way aircraft flutter and buffet during high angle of attack stalls and turns, everything feels fantastic.

All this when combined with an excellent multiplayer community brings the game into a league of it's own. Flying with friends or alone will have your hands sweating while gripping the flight stick as you check your 6 over and over, waiting to be bounced by another fighter before it's too late.

Visually IL2 is absolutely stunning, never have I seen aircraft looking as sexy as they do in this series. Perhaps DCS might have the edge in terms of cloud formation (although the dev team is working on this) but in all other facets, I am of the opinion that IL2 is the superior game.

This review has been a little bit of a mess, I'm tired - but it shouldn't matter, what matters is that if you're thinking of picking this game up. Just do it.\t\t\t\t\t
