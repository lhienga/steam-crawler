I usually play War Thunder in arcade mode....so, even on the simplest settings, IL2 is no arcade experience. If you want a quick easy game with super easy and fun planes to fly, this really isn't it. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tok time for the proper updated review. this is an incredible game

in terms of sheer content, IL-2 Sturmovik: 1946 perhaps takes the edge, as that includes 5 previous titles in the series in one package. both the old and new titles all have different things to offer, and while often the classics are the best, both the old and new games of this series both offer great experiences. this game sticks to its roots, and very much captures the feel of the originals (i was pleased to find my much loved cinematic camera view in the reboot!), while modernising it to look better, and feel better, and adding in very enjoyable modes and features! i still suggest trying out 1946 as well as this (and especially if you enjoy this game/sims in general), considering its price, and its quality, but do not cast this title as "just another reboot". it is a very competent entry in the series and improves many areas of the franchise. for this reason, i recommend this entry over 1946, especially for newcomers. ill start with the cons however.


number one: the other content packages are really expensive. the fact that each "dlc" is a separate game does kind of excuse the prices but its slightly annoying nonetheless

number two: requires third party account to change multiplayer display name. its a bit of a weird requirement. the account system *is* useful because it would allow you to buy and play each entry (stalingrad, bodenplatte, moscow etc) separately from the """base game""", as steam doesnt do this by default. for some reason, stalingrad is a requirement for the other "dlc" on steam. but it is an annoying requirement besides that

number three: for the best experience, a flightstick is required. this is a simulation game designed for simulation enthusiasts. while it is still very fun for more casual flight simmers, a joystick enhances the experience to the point where its so much better with a flightstick that its worth the extra cost. some multiplayer servers also require the client to use a flightstick to even connect, although these servers are a minority

number four: button layout by default is very confusing, and i had to completely remap my flightstick due to ridiculous standard controls from plug and play, e.g. the throttle control was set to an axis of the stick itself?! however this would take 10 minutes maximum for the most dedicated of players to resolve, for an infinite number of hours of enjoyment afterwards


ok now for the pros


number one: damage modelling is b e a u t i f u l. ive never seen anything like it. i wont even bother describing it, just go and buy this game and see for yourself

number 2: realism. this game is much more realistic than other games in the genre like FSX. the sheer force the propeller puts on the plane during take off is mental. it takes real skill to be able to dance through the skies without stalling from violent turns and banks

number 3: scale. the maps are huge and realistic within reason to the areas represented at the time

number 4: sound design. weapons sound heavy and destructive, engines sound realistic and powerful. the sound design is great

number 5: immersion. as the realism and design elements come together into one great product, a huge amount of immersion is created. the entire plane, yes even the bits you cant see, are there. when your fuselage is shredded by AA fire or your wing rips off and spirals away, everything is textured to the same quality as the cockpit itself. you can have a photo on the instrument stacks (which you can customise if you can use a program like Photoshop and add the file to the directory in the right format) and radio chatter barks in your ears.

number 6: the career modes are epic. very high detail, with the ability to customise and create your own generated pilot and his backstory, plan and see missions before flying and earn promotions within your chosen squadron. its better for you to just see this bit for yourself

number 7: a fully featured and enjoyable tank *simulator* included! yes thats right. this is also a tank simulator. not war thunder or world of tanks, but a simulator, just the same as the flight sim aspect. you can take several roles within your allied or axis tank and form a crew in multiplayer, launching attacks on enemy convoys or assaulting enemy controlled towns.


i could go on and on about how much this game has to offer but its better for you to stop reading this review, buy this game and stop wasting time which you could spend playing IL-2 Sturmovik: Battle of Stalingrad!\t\t\t\t\t
