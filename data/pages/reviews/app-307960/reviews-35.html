\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimply amazing WW2 flight combat simulator even for casual players, but primarily gives a good challenge for the most determined sim players out there.

The good:
+ Highly realistic flight models and aircraft
+ very customizable settings: for example you can change variety of things which make controlling your aircraft more simple and you can set some assists which makes this sim good even for those players who don't have a good HOTAS setups.
+ Can be played with mouse and keyboard
+ A lot of content even in the base game
+ Very good replayability: You can play either on axis side or on allies side. This determines what missions you are going to play and which planes you can use. This also determines all the lore your character has and what your character is going to look like. All these things also apply to their name and unit.
+ Voice acting is very good: it really feels like you are in the battle and you are playing with real living people and they have their own personality. Also if you play on Germany's side, character voices are going to be DUBBED in German language, so its not one of those games which try to dub German character which speaks english but accent is ''german''. I usually cringe very hard when devs do this accent thingy in movies and games. You either give them a good voice acting with their native language or don't give them any voice overs at all.
+ Historically accurate planes, units, battles, maps, environments and weapons
+ A lot of game modes to play: for example scripted campaign, career, quick missions, co-op, dogfights...
+ Menu layout is very well designed and easy to understand
+ A lot of DLCs which give you even more content to this game
+ aircraft cockpits are very accurate and well simulated
+ You can use bombers, attack airplanes and fighters depending on your play style and which you are the most interested in
+ Very good devs which care about their game: These devs still after all these years put a lot of new stuff into this game and try to improve the core game itself with good amount of updates and content.
+ You can download custom scripted missions/campaigns for free (basically mods) which add more variety to this game.
+ Well optimized
+ Amazing sound design: aircraft, guns, explosions ect sound very accurate and good.
+ Very friendly and helpful community
+ When playing career for example, your actions can determine how the campaigns turn out, so basically things you do and how you succeed determines the outcome.
+ Well made cutscenes which bring a lot of immersion to this game
+ You can customize your plane's weaponry, paint job, armor ect...
+ Nice graphics, but can be somehow outdated in 2021 (still looks great IMO)

The Bad:
- No music in battles: Some music for battle situations would be really nice, because this brings more immersion to the game itself
- Some DLCs can be really expensive: I HIGHLY recommend you to wait for sale if you want to buy this game and get all the campaign DLCs and deluxe edition. These DLCs bring a lot of new things to the game and for me they are really worth it.
- No tutorial: One of the biggest problems this game has is that it doesn't feature any tutorials which can be really big problem for new players. This is why I recommend to play quick missions first and read all the basic control key bindings.
- No Steam cloud saves: Make sure that if you want to backup your saves do it manually. Especially do this if you have a lot of progress in this game

So overall this is a great game and a great simulator experience. I personally highly recommend this game if you are interested in these kind of games. If you are somehow doubtful about buying this game you should first just buy the deluxe edition and try it out (just make sure to play under 2 hours because if you play this game over 2h and you want to refund it steam isn't required to refund you this game) and if you don't like it just refund it.

My rating: 9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, the biggest problem here is that the game has been chopped up into a bunch of tiny bits which are being resold at far above what they're worth. They seriously expect people to pay $20 for ONE plane in a nearly decade old game?

And to add insult to injury, none of that is every properly marked, the game is happy enough to let you start a campaign that you're not allowed to play, and go through the rigmarole of loading and cut scenes, and generating information that really should already be there, and then loading some more, only to skip over the actual game play without explanation, leaving you to search for why, to find that, you're just not allowed to play that campaign because you didn't pay for a specific plane.

Second, the fact is that you really do have to have a joystick to get an acceptable experience. Sure, it does support keyboard and mouse, but steering with the mouse just feels off, and doing all the buttons with the keyboard requires intense memorization. Playing without a joystick is, well, frankly it's just a bad experience.

Third, the AI also felt simplistic. In the dog fights I did have, planes either just headlonged me and we slammed into each other, or we just circled each other forever. Like seriously, in a one on one fight, me and the plane must have just circled each other at least thirty times before I got bored and started trying to come at them from another angle.

As for the positives, the VR support is pretty good. I felt like I needed to put a fan or something in front of myself to get more of a sense of speed, but that felt more like a limitation of VR itself, rather than the game, but the experience of being able to physically look around searching for a plane I knew was nearby but that I had lost sight of was cool and very immersive.

If you can comfortably throw somewhere between $1,000 and $2,000 dollars at a game, including buying a quality joystick (maybe as low as $500 if you get it on sale with a medium priced flight stick), then I think it might be pretty decent, but even then I'm not certain it would be a particularly great game.\t\t\t\t\t
