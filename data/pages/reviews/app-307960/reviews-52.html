______________________________________________

So there I am flying my BF109-F4, following my wing leader and squadron. As we close into the cluster of Soviet bombers and escort fighters,I break off and ascend upon them like a hawk catching its prey. Tracer fire licks the air from the bombers tail gunners but it is hopeless in the face of German superior engineering. I laugh in schnitzel as my gun sight lead on target and my 20mm and twin MGs start barking back in response. My 20mm guns sheer through the body of the first PE-2 as one of its engines catch fire. I move on to the next looking for targets of opportunity in this cluster of absolute chaos. PE-2 Soviet planes start breaking off into random directions as the air is filled with plumes of smoke, oil, and burning metal. Tracer fire criss crossing in all directions while I meticulously viff left and right firing while listening to some ACDS through my Oculus quest 2. What more can I say its the most fun iv had in VR, leading targets through my gun sight while keeping my eyes on the various gauges, coolant, the ammo counter and making sure my engine does not over heat during these frantic dog fights.

Highly immersive with realistic damage physics. This game keeps getting better with every update. The Devs and the community are super passionate and will only add more as the years progress. The absolute best WWII air combat sim on the market. 10/10 can't recommend it enough.

*Con

Needs a brief tutorial on how to actually fly the plane. It is easy to figure out after a bit and YouTube tutorials help. Just needs one in game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, the entire game costs $656.78 at the moment, so there's that. I tried to start a campaign and it told me I don't own the plane needed for the campaign, so it's not like the DLC is superfluous. Also, I can't seem to get the escape key mapped to a controller, so I keep needing to get up for the sim rig to walk to the keyboard to access the game menu.
Other than that, it's great experience that seems very realistic, but the base game feels kind of like a demo and it definitely doesn't feel like a $700 game. Maybe when all the DLC goes on sale I'll pick some up and try to see what the full game feels like. For now, I'm going to give a thumbs down, because it's not worth the price tag, but it is good and probably worth buying on sale.
Update: now the game won't launch at all in Steam VR mode, so it looks like I won't be buying those on sale DLC's. It's a shame, because I was looking forward to trying them. I really hope the devs get these issues fixed, because at this time, the entire game is worthless.

Update: The game eventually started working in VR again, so I picked up some of the DLC while it was on sale. I bought Battle of Moscow, Battle of Kuban, Battle of Bodenplatte, Ice ring and Flying Circus for about $70 more (than what I already spent on the base game). I've only played a handful of hours so far, but I can already tell you that it feels like a fully developed game now that the DLC is installed. The base game really feels like early access in comparison. The map I'm playing in Kuban, the Black Sea map feels a lot more detailed that the maps in the base game. I will update this review again when I have some more playing time invested in the DLCs. For now I am going to change my verdict to "recommend" with the qualification that I have spent about $110 on the software so far, and waited for a sale.

Update: Now that had some time with the game working and a decent amount of content, I can say that I've really fallen in love with this game. One of the most challenging things is landing these old tail draggers without trashing the propeller. Accomplishing that after a successful mission is such a satisfying feeling. If you have a joystick and this game is onsale, absolutely puchase it if you have any interest in flight sims. It is an amazing deal when onsale. I still haven't been able to get the escape menu button working when mapped to controller. That's pretty annoying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you don't already know how to play this game, this game isn't meant for you. There is no tutorial. When you Google for the manual, you don't get any immediate results. You get a link to the general forums, and the link under the thread "IL-2 Manual" on the forum is dead. I see a thread that says they're working on a new one, last post January 2020. So I guess there is also currently no manual.

Before you fly a single flight, you are expected to map all the controls. Imagine if before you could play a single game of Call of Duty, the controls were blank and you had to make your own. All you have to go on is the text. How could you bind "use special ability" if you don't even know what the special ability is? And there's maybe 100 actions you have to read through. I am not the first person with this joystick, why am I forced to repeat work others have done? I'm sure someone out there is smarter than me and has made a much more ergonomic profile. Again, if everyone had to make their own mapping just to play CoD, not everyone would invent the WASD and QE keys. If you want to use someone else's profile, you have to google it, get sent to a thread where people post "input.zip" files, then find that file on your computer and replace it with theirs. Why can't my joystick be visualized and why can't it take two clicks to try out someone else's profile? It's literally a mapping of action names to axis names, why do I have to open a file browser for this thing that everyone has to do? All of the files in that thread open to a permissions error page so it doesn't matter anyway.

With flight simulators it feels like you spend more time learning to configure the flight simulator than learning to fly, which makes sense, planes were designed to be intuitive for people who can't think straight because they're about to die and flight simulator interfaces were designed with a "if it's physically possible to achieve the end result, don't worry about how long it takes people to figure out how to do it". Not all of us are nerds that have the time and patience for this mind numbingly boring configuration. Why does everyone have to repeat the work of making their own profiles and researching how to do it just to use the game they paid for?

Like... I gave you money. Why am I the one who has to figure out how to teach myself to use your software? I don't mind reading a manual, but you don't even tell me what I need to read and where to find it.\t\t\t\t\t
