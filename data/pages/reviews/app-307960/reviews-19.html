\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great game, but almost nobody's playing it (250 players / month avg)

The overall DLC setup splits the playerbase so badly that, unless you're ready to shell out 250$ for all of them at the start, you're only going to be able to join about 15% of the 2 populated servers at any one time. I suspect many people have bought this game, and were disappointed at the fact that the 1-3 servers that exist, had no planes for them to fly.

Eventually I will get the DLCs and planes I want but holy hell its lonely until you do.

The developer's tools for map makers and server hosting is atrocious - Modders need to find weird workarounds for making scenarios and dynamic systems, since there are no native mechanisms to help them. You'll get kicked for violating rules that are never displayed clearly, because the game doesn't support text or HUD outputs via the server in any meaningful way.

The simulation is second-to-none. But we already knew that for the Il2 series. It was already basically perfected by 2003, and nothing really new needed to be added here. Some physics situations can show a few flaws in how the game deals with broken airframes - Planes will fly with no wing area at all, violently stop from 600kmh to 0, or even spin at 40000rpm, in place, after being shot, etc. The ground impacts can be quite comedic and silly: hitting the ground at 40deg and 400km/h can result in intact airframes bouncing like they're made of titanium (without turning your pilots spine into dust somehow). Don't get me wrong, the crashes are still hilarious and fun, but often the physics go haywire after a plane's been heavily damaged.

Great sim engine - 10/10, but a little unfulfilling to play just ~8 planes in a ~$60 game after having like 500 in Il2:1946, but times have changed. 1C has got more and more competition in this space, however, with DCS etc, so they may need to pull a new trick out of their bag.
