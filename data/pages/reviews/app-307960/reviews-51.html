I like that there's also ground forced, flak, artillery, tanks, that you can blow up bridges, etc.
It makes for a complete WW2 experience.
On top of that, VR works like a charm, despite the fact you can't directly interact with plane controls.(you must use keyboard/controllers keys)
And last but not least, there's a mission editor in the package for those interested in creating their own missions.
My only complaints would the slightly dated graphics and the price of the DLCs that is on the border of ridiculously expensive. (Wait for specials)
A must buy for every combat plane simulator lover :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is currently the best WWII Flight simulator on the market. The only real competitor is DCS, with War Thunder having a little bit of overlap, but not enough to be a direct competitor. In my opinion it is much better than the DCS WWII fighters simply because it was developed from the ground up for WWII combat while DCS was built for modern combat, with some WWII planes and maps tacked on. The damage model is significantly better (a necessity for cannon and MG armed planes), the G simulation is actually a bit better, the effects look better, and one crucial difference is that in Il-2 the clouds show up in the same place for everybody in a multiplayer server, while not doing this in DCS. While, the DCS world devs are working on addressing this issue, for the time being it makes piston engine combat a significantly better experience in Il-2 for the time being. Lastly, the fully clickable cockpits offered by DCS WWII planes offer a good deal of immersion, however Il-2 feels so much more like actually being in WWII, that it really isn't worth the $50 per plane, and $50 per map (the modern DCS aircraft are great though).

If you aren't sure about getting any WWII combat flight simulators, wait for a sale, and this one usually drops down to $7.50. I know hardcore sims aren't everyone's cup of tea, but if you enjoy it, the level of immersion offered by Il-2 makes it a truly special experience, which only gets better in VR. VR in this game makes you feel like you are right there in the battle, and completely forget about reality. It is honestly the best VR experience I have ever played (with Elite: Dangerous in a close second), and it makes you feel genuine fear and adrenaline from the dogfights that you might find yourself in.

TL;DR Better than DCS for WWII combat (DCS modern combat is still great though), and feels extremely polished for a flight sim, which makes it incredibly immersive. This only gets better in VR, where you completely forget about the outside world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you remember Red Baron, Aces of the Pacific and Aces over Europe and have been waiting for almost 30 years to get your hands on a worthy successor - gues what, here it is.

A good balance between action and sim with a lot of settings and helpers to opt for the first or the latter. Good graphics, great sound engineering and good VR performance.

Comparing it with DCS? - Mkay, let's say this is DCS light. It focuses on having fun, while DCS teaches you how to operate a plane properly.

It is a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ of fun in VR and even better with friends. You can even host your multiplayer sessions from your home-PC - awesome.

P.S.: Don't mind the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ reviews about having to port forward for multiplayer or the complaints about having to pay money for a good game. 1st: It's how OSI layer 3 works; making your private server on your private address visible to the outside world and helping the packets from the Interwebs find their way back to your server - it works damn fine this way. 2nd: You get what you pay for - same here as anywhere else.\t\t\t\t\t
