\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave not logged enough hours on IL2 but so far so good. There are a couple of problems right out of the gate. While a tutorial isn't fully necessary with all the forums and YouTube content out there, it still would have been nice to have some sort of introduction to the basic controls.

Key binding is labor intensive, and without proper research into exactly what commands you'll need early on then it can be daunting. Flight models are really slick and control input feels very responsive. Of course, this all varies on what plane you are flying. Damage modeling also feels deep and realistic. Graphics look very solid as well even if the sim is aging.

Practice, patience and more hours are needed for a much more in-depth review but I am liking what I have played thus far. Also, the price is a little steep for a sim that was release several years ago. Add up the add-ons available and money can definitely be burned. Maybe I just missed the sales that took place in the past? Not sure, but price is another negative factor.

***UPDATE** 12/10/21
Buy with confidence. My first review upon purchase and not many hours spent on the sim has given way to two thumbs up. IL2 is the best WWII flight sims out there. With enough reading, experimenting, trial, and errors, once you get a grasp on the intricate details of each plane, mission, and scenario, there really is endless amounts of fun at hand. The community at large is super helpful. The customization and 3rd party add-ons add so much more depth. The additional 1st party planes and maps are far from a money grab, showing the amount of work and dedication that this team has invested in a gracefully aging sim. And all of this insane fun hasn't even mentioned the online component, which requires it's own in-depth review. Bravo to the developers, their continued support of their creation (the new patch adds the highly anticipated cloud update), and the countless hours of fun this flight sim brings with it. Buy it, play it, and enjoy it. IL2 is impressive.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played Warthunder for years and although i found that highly enjoyable as an introduction to flight sims/ games il2 battle of x is simply next level with its realism and immersion. The single player career is a good place to start when you get into the game but the multiplayer is what i have enjoyed the most. The game encourages you to not kamakazi and throw your life away in an unrealistic attempt to gain a kill. instead it encourages you to preserve your life, pick your fights and make it back to base or at minimum ditch or bail from your damaged aircraft behind friendly lines. The thrill of constantly scanning for enemy air craft, keeping an eye on your 6, engaging and recovering from a dogfight is not something that can be found in a game like Warthunder anyway. I also find attacking ground targets far more enjoyable and rewarding in this game compared to Warthunder. Picking your route on the map, following the real life landmarks on the way, staying low to avoid detection (in a striker anyway). When you get to where you think the targets should be you scan the ground for what you're looking for. Bunkers, parked planes on a airfield, factories, other miscellaneous facilities, tanks, trucks you name it... The AAA in this game is not to be taken lightly though so you better come in hard and fast and with my lack of skill quite often leads to me limping home with half a dozen ground kills but with holes punched through my canopy with the sound of gushing wind in my ears, fuel spewing from my fuel tanks making me a easily spotted target to circling fighters over head and oil splattered on my windscreen from my damaged engine which i now have to carefully nurse before it abandons me powerless over enemy lines which would certainly lead to death or capture.

Anyhow, i have enjoyed this game so far and i encourage others to make the purchase next time it is on sale. If your not sure you'll like it just get the Battle of Stalingrad base game for like $7 on sale and give it a go.\t\t\t\t\t
