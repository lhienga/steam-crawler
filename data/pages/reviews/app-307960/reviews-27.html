\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend it but before anything, realize that this is not a GAME, but a SIMULATION. There is no beginning and no end, so do not expect to unlock every achievement after 20 or 30 hours. Rather, you impersonate a virtual pilot, and like your real like counterpart, be ready to rack up 100s --if not 1,000s-- of hours flying.

But no worries, these many hours will be filled with great pleasure because the simulation:
-Aims at being as complete as possible (looks like the game aims to have you fly any possible plane on any possible front)
-Has a friendly community with passionate people who like sharing knowledge (many videos, active forum)
-Is complex, but the learning curve is adaptable to your expectations (you can specialize in single seaters, one specific plane or more). You just set your own objectives, reach them (or not) and adapt them if necessary
-Is realistic and authentic (calculations in game are based on the technical data of the planes in real life, maps are also created on the basis of archives at the time). So you keep discovering (or rediscovering)new stuff
-Gives you a feeling of reward (you feel real pride and satisfaction if you land your plane properly, or understand the principles of flight for instance)
-Is often heavily discounted (but as with any flight simulation, is of no interest if you don't invest in a joystick and a throttle... also bought pedals for a rough $250 to $300 new). However, the number of hours being infinite, the more you play, the cheaper the investment.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[EDIT: Having played now for many more hours, I've changed my review from an 9/10 to a 8/10. This isn't necessarily through any fault of the game, but simply because I'm not too keen on the way the development team handle criticism or bug reporting. They would probably do well to employ someone part-time to respond to customers issues rather than taking personal offence at any suggestion that there are some issues with their product.]

Was aware of this game years ago but held off purchasing it because of all the problems that came with CLOD which I had ended up refunding. Honestly I'm a bit gutted it took me this long to get into IL2, I'd been playing Warthunder for years and had always wanted that game to be more "Simmy", yet all the while this is the game I should have been playing all along.

Get yourself into on the sales and come join the Finnish server which has a persistent online battle that can last weeks or months at a time. Buying all the modules can be steep but there's a lot of time and effort that went into modelling these vehicles.

Graphics could be modernised slightly but its a sim, you don't play for eyecandy necessarily. Works fine with VR - I've been using a Quest 2 with my old dinosaur of a card (GTX1060) and it gets near acceptable frames, anyone with a better GPU than me can only hope to get a better experience. All in all, aside from the prices of the modules which I think are sometimes way overpriced, it's a great game if you're into WWII sims.\t\t\t\t\t
