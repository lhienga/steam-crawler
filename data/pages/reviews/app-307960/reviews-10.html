\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIL-2 Sturmovik: Battle of Stalingrad is a solid entry in the IL-2 Sturmovik franchise. Having played IL-2 Sturmovik 1946, I can say that this game trades off the sheer quantity of aircraft (especially when equipped with third party mods like BAT) for detail and quality - both afforded by the newer engine used by the sim.

The entries so far focus on more specific battles: Stalingrad, Moscow, Kuban, Normandy, etc. This allows for highly detailed depictions of these moments in the Second World War. Flying in Stalingrad, for example, gives you chilling glimpses into the totality of the conflict, as you take off and see the city burning near the banks of the Volga River off your wing. The flights are smaller than what I've seen in other World War II sims (my first being the classic European Air War back in 1999, which could put more than 200 machines in the air during any given mission, a number which would utterly break the BoX series on current hardware). But again, the trade off is in the details.

The flight modeling feels more granular than in other sims, including 1946. You can feel the age of these machines, some now more than 75 years in the past, as they buffet, roll, and dive through the air. The damage modeling is also impressive. The flight model feels more active and alive. I've obviously never flown a Second World War fighter, but the feel of the machines is spot on and often replicate the accounts of real pilots from the period. The damage model is really the stand out for me. I was fighting with a few Russian fighters in my 109 and noticed that they each broke up differently depending on where I hit them. The first machine taking a lot of damage before finally knocking the engine out, the second one detonating violently in front of me after I hit the fuel tank. I can still see the retracted landing gear flying a few feet from my canopy as the rest of the machine fell out of the sky, pilot still trapped inside...

As with all flight simulators, the question of "is this the right game for me" often comes down to what you're looking for. If you view this more as a study sim in the style of DCS or even Falcon 4.0, where you can really learn an individual machine inside and out, then this is a rewarding experience. If you want to bounce between hundreds of aircraft, I'd recommend picking up IL-2 1946 on Steam and then looking up the BAT total conversion. As for the battles in BoX, I don't know if I really feel like I'm in the war... but rather experiencing a small slice of it at a time. That said, the baked in career mode is developed enough to keep me from looking for third party programs to further flesh it out, and the scripted missions, and the quick mission launcher are more than enough to keep me busy. The experiencing of flying through a career is a satisfying one and even though I don't see flights of hundreds of planes, watching one explode a few feet in front of me was enough to get my attention!

A few final points. One being price, and the other being the community. Much like our racing counterparts, flight sims are an expensive hobby. These are not games that sell in the millions of copies and, as such, each company has to find ways to recoup their costs. I'm happier overall with BoX model versus how 1C (777 Studios) had packaged content for Rise of Flight. This model (buy a battle, get 7-9 planes plus a map) feels more effective than assembling aircraft collections one at a time. That said, the collectors planes are exceptionally well done and if there's a machine you love (such as the P-38), the fidelity of the aircraft is excellent. The cost per package is expensive, but they do offer numerous sales throughout the year.

Finally is community, and BoX has a large and passionate group. The official message boards are always filled with helpful advice and I would highly recommend looking up the IL-2 Sturmovik BSD program, which will easily allow you to find and add meticulously detailed paint schemes to the game with a quick click of the mouse.

So if you are looking for a detailed study sim of the machines of the Second World War, have in interest in higher fidelity flight and damage models, are curious about parts of the war that many of us in the West haven't been exposed to, and want a robust game you can add to over the years, then check this one out.\t\t\t\t\t
