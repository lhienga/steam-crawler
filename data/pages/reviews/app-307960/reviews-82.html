\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a massive fan of the IL-2 series! I have played countless hours with the 1946 version of the game, and for a long time i was hesitating to buy this, as it doesn't have Pacific theater in it. I just love carrier operations. Take of from them, land on them, and the fact that you can loose the only place you can land on is also a nice strategic point. Sadly IL-2 BOS still don't have it, but i don't regret buying it, as when i first started it up, i had the same "WOW" factor as when i first played with the old one. It just feels so realistic, even without VR googles (which i plan to buy), although it will be problematic to manage all the keys, but i plan to buy a separate throttle stick too then, as i only have a built in throttle on my joystick, and it has "just" 12 buttons which is hardly enough. That being said a joy is highly recommended. It's hard to go back from this kind of fidelity and realism, once you get used to it. Although i don't know why my plane doesn't have an effect on the water when i fly a couple meters over water, every graphic setting should be on and on ultra settings. And sometimes the missions are a bit strange, let me explain as we go over an area to protect the convoy, we circle for half an hour (you can speed up the game) and then mission accomplished we go home. But just as my squad is preparing to land, 2 enemy wings show up to the nearby target area. And i was the only one trying to do something, my wingmates were with the landing lights on and landing like nothing is happening just 5-10 km's to the south of our position. As i play hardcore campaign (it feels more realistic as you almost care as much about your own well being as in real) i just tried to lure them to my wing, while like 5-6 were in my tail, but my wing didn't care anyway as the mission "was" accomplished and afterwards nothing matters just land in one piece. And in the end i had to land without elevator controls which is always making me sweat it is so exciting. All in all it is a wonderful simulator, the ground ops (tank operations) are a nice touch but a bit barebone now, but they are adding more content to it. I did get it on sale, as i just can't justify spending hundreds on a game to my family, but if the Pacific theater comes i might not even wait for a sale! \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is for the VR version of IL-2 Sturmovik (it can be run in Steam VR), rather than the more traditional game format. More than anything, this is about the VR implementation and how incredibly awkward has been using the game in this format.

TL;DR - VR implementation is awful, and the technical support personnel apparently lack experience with the VR version of the game and do not seem to be able to help with the problems I experienced. DO NOT BUY THIS GAME FOR THE VR EXPERIENCE.

When I got a VR headset (Rift-S), iwas rather excited because I would get to try flight simulation in a real 3D space, and I'd heard great things about the IL-2 game. So I purchased the game, downloaded it, and ran it in VR, and that's where the problems began. In other VR games, when you start, you generally have a flat interface to interact with before you get into the game. This includes things like menus and the like. However, generally they show up relative to the headset position. In my case, I play my flight simulators seated, including in VR, but when I start the game, it renders my position a few inches above the ground. IF I stand, I can somewhat see the menu screen in the hangar, but then I have to step back about 3 feet to actually see it properly, which takes me away from my desk and keyboard entirely, which means it's practically impossible to use the menus with out constantly changing position. This is extremely difficult to do in VR, because you are blind to your physical surroundings while the headset is on. It takes me about 10-15 minutes of fumbling to get to create a pilot and try to fly an aircraft. When I do, finally, get into an aircraft, much to my horror, my head is outside of the aircraft entirely and nestled to the left side of the engine cowling and I cannot see the cockpit unless (in a paused game) I stand up and position myself three feet from my keyboard and flight stick. I have tried to describe the problem to Support, which literally gave me a two sentence response that was essentially "save your position with F10 or send a screenshot" and I can do neither of things while in VR. I have tried reinstalling the game, moving my desk, and none of these seem to work. I have to conclude, then, two things:

1 - The support staff do not have any experience with the game in VR, or VR in general.
2 - The VR implementation is extremely flawed and janky.

I cannot recommend this game to anyone with a VR headset after my experiences and I do so sadly, because this game seems like it would be a lot of fun otherwise. The engine cowling was rather lovingly rendered from what I could tell and I was practically screaming in horror when I attempted to unpause the game and proceeded to crash into the ground because I could not effectively control the aircraft. I'm tempted to get a refund, but I'm hoping that 1C Game Studios will take my experience in mind and find a way to resolve the problem, so that I can instead write about how immersive and fun the game would be in VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t***EDIT 2021***

So I feel the need to add this caveat as the game has vastly improved since my original review although some of the criticisms are still valid.

1) They finally have a meaningful mission generator system that adds more offline playability. But it's still not quite as good as 1946. For example, say I want to line up a bunch of T34 tanks and blast the hell out of them with a Stuka, I cannot do that still. But it is however MUCH better than the campaign mode. And offers much more entertainment.

2) They do have a lot of sales very frequently, so you can pickup the additional content at a good price if you are patient. And I definitely think you should be patient.

3) The developers are constantly updating the game, I think most of the issues I highlighted are a reflection on the fact they are a small team with a small budget and a lot of work ahead of them to be competitive in the market. It's clearly a labour of love and they deserve respect for that.

In it's current state, with discounted purchases, I would rate it a 8/10 from a 5/10


Quite disappointed with this game to be honest. It's lacking in content significantly, I have finished the campaign without doing anything remotely interesting, just rinse and repeat bomber escort missions until suddenly it said the Battle of Stalingrad is over.

No major air engagements, or close air support style missions.....nothing. Just taking off, meeting up with some Stuka's, escorting them, occasionally attacking fighters, escort home, repeat.

I thought it was a slow grind build that reflected the kinds of operations actually undertaken during this battle but it's actually just extremely boring and deeply unsatisfying.

I have been a fan of the series since the first game arrived and loved it for it's historical accuracy and depth and breadth of content but even the instant action component of the game is severely lacking. You can only fly the aircraft that are available to you in campaign mode despite being able to square off against or be escorted by a number of other aircraft in the game.

Worse still, to get more content, you have to pay full AAA prices......why would I bother when this game was so lacking in content to begin with? what compelling reason is there? The actual simulation itself is fantastic as has always been the case with this series but I feel totally ripped off regarding the lack of content and the terrible campaign. Wish I could get my money back but I stuck with it hoping it would get better......

I had hoped to be excited to buy the expansions after completing this and very nearly pulled the trigger in anticipation and desire to fly a larger assortment of aircraft but I am bloody glad I didn't now.

5/10\t\t\t\t\t
