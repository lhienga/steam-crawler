\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can not reccomend this game.
Review is written after I learned the game and started getting kills in MP. I will try to summarize some pros and cons:

+ rolling on the runway, that is very good in my opinion
+ engine management, that is also good, although models seem relatively simply made

o graphics - on max settings they seem quite nice, but give you bad FPS, with terrible FPS drops. On low or
balanced settings the FPS can be good, but still expect FPS drops, but not terrible. Game also looks
comparable to IL 1946 at that point. Since it is a mixed bag, i take it as neither pros or con. Also water
and drops of water look great and smoke bad, so still a null point for me.

- flight model feels rather arcadish, with situations triggered by meeting certain parameters. Some planes
have nice models, some have bad models, with the number of planes, that is unacceptable.
- spotting is terrible, planes flicker in and out from vision. There is even a setting for this, and it helps, but
not much.
- damage model is bad, 37mm cannon shot between wing and fuselage doesnt do a thing, fuel tank on
ground (airfield) can not be damaged by MGs, while artillery explodes after one shot, its all over the place
- weapon spread is too accurate, i could get regular hits at 500 meters no problem, ground attacks or air
(for some reason, tanks do have possibly even greater than real spread, compared to the planes -
comparison looks silly)
- tanks are an afterthought
- user interface is terrible. Absolutely unintuitive, unresponsive and sometimes just bad. For example: you
cannot assign a two key combination for anything unless its shift, ctrl or alt, you cannot set different
convergence for cannon, mg and rockets, you must set joystick sensitivity somewhat to get anywhere with
the game, this may seem like nitpicking, but there is too much of this and it adds up too quickly.
- single player campaign is a mission generator with walls of text. Some of these texts are even interesting.
but not too many. Missions depend on the next point:
- AI is bad. Unless they are gunners, in which case they will headshot you when you pass around at 600 kph
at 45Â° vertical. On "average" skill (second worst).

Some funny remarks i had with the game:
At first I thought my Joystick broke, so I tested it in other games. Was not the case, I just had to set sensitivity in the damn interface. So it would respond like in those other games.
Ground attack is undoable on low (144fps with least drops) graphical settings. Ground targets (dis)appear at cca 400 meters distance. So i switched to balanced (120 fps with drops).
Then, after I learned the quirks of the flight model, and when they trigger, I made people who were dueling me trigger these quirks and forced them to crash. This I did not consider as getting kills, but still got more kills this way than with shooting.
Also spotting is so bad, that I usually saw them already on my tail. Sometimes people disappeared from my view, but then I learned to use it - and indeed, I too zipped away from the enemy and simply vanished. Which I felt quite silly and doumbfounded about.
After finally getting shooting kills I was shocked how arcadish this game feels.

Final note:
On youtube, game does not look too bad.
Comparison with historical footage (also on youtube - even for the same planes) however, puts this game in not so great light. When I added sturmovik 1946 to the mix, I had to admit that 1946 looks more like the historical footage. DCS the same thing. War thunder simulator battles footage actually bears comparison to this game.
People playing the game do not take it too seriously. Which I think is a good thing.

Game runs on modified Rise of Flight engine that is more than a decade old. Analogy with famous Bethesda studios and their games presents itself. They are in quite a bad spot now too - because technology has advanced. We have high refresh monitors with adaptive sync and multicore processors now.

For great money I expected a great product. I did not have overwhelming expectations, but the game failed to meet even the basic ones. I suggest that you try free to play alternatives first or buy 1946 on GOG like me.
I hope this review helped you decide and wish you good landings.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIL-2 Sturmovik is a really great World War 2 combat flight simulator. I fly exclusively in VR, and I find the airplane modeling and handling to be excellent, and the feeling of being "inside" the plane is really immersive. Combat is tense and realistic. Damage models are very detailed -- you can lose part of the plane and it might still fly, and you can maybe limp home. Or maybe your stabilizer got shot off and you're fine until you attempt a maneuver, then the plane goes into a spin. Or you damaged something and don't notice until you lower flaps for landing -- all these types of damage are modeled in this game.

Dynamic career mode is a bit of a weak point for this game. Whilst it might be realistic, some missions that are auto-generated can be quite boring with long flight times. But additional game modes help compensate. You can get hand-crafted campaigns (free or paid campaigns) that are very interesting and each mission is specifically building into a story. Or you can use a third-party campaign generator, PWCS, that is very tweakable and can generate a long campaign for you and your friends.

Speaking of which, the multiplayer community in IL2 is pretty great. Personally I think this is the best part of the game, and I much prefer flying with real opponents than Ai. There are several lively multiplayer servers with interesting missions. At the pinnacle of IL2 multiplayer is a server campaign called "Tactical Air War" that runs over several weeks, with the results from each mission impacting the state of the campaign for the next. Some of the best pilots in the community, with decades of flight experience, fly here. You can also host a private server with friends and run a persistent campaign there. There is a vibrant squadron community, with people running squads ranging from informal and newbie friendly through to those simulating a historical squadron. You'll find the forums are mostly newbie friendly and you can find advice there or on one of many Discord servers.

Many people will want to compare this game to DCS. I think the two are very different. A common complaint about IL2 is that it doesn't have clickable cockpits. I think the lack of clicky cockpits is actually a positive for IL2 and for flying warbirds. I don't actually *want* a clicky cockpit, I want to bind everything to a hardware button so I can find it in VR. This game does that for me nicely. In further comparison to DCS, I think you get a lot of game for your money with IL2. You get multiple planes per 'game' in the "Battle of X" series (Battle of Stalingrad, Battle of Kuban, Battle of Bodenplatte, etc) whereas with DCS you have to buy a lot of separate aircraft packs. If you're flying on a multiplayer server, as long as you have purchased a plane that the server uses, you can fly on any map. I'm not dissing DCS, but I do think IL2 provides a lot of value for your money.

VR performance is a bit of a challenge for simulators, and IL2 has its share of those. If you really want to fly in VR with full framerate, you'll either need a very powerful rig or to accept lowered graphics settings. Personally I fly at half framerate with interpolation, and I think it's a pretty good experience (Oculus Rift S).

Game versions, collector planes and campaigns regularly go on sale on the Developer's website. I bought the base Battle of Stalingrad game on Steam, then everything else through the developer. This gets me game updates fast through Steam, but money directly to the game's creator for everything else. If Battle of Kuban is on sale I would recommend it as a good buy from the series. If you want to fly the newest planes, you need Battle of Bodenplatte, which is excellent but unlikely to be on sale anytime soon (early 2020 writing this).\t\t\t\t\t

The Flight model and damage physics are second to none.
Best comparison would be War Thunder........ But for Simulation nuts. The missions are nice and long, with lots of extras on the store page, and new ones are still being developed. The 2 i bought have an estimated completion time of 6 hours each. And the main Campaigns are also very lengthy. Well worth the buy. Good sales sometimes. I picked up the base game and the 2 DLC for 30 bucks. Great Value vs the Play time.
Only thing i would like to see are larger aircraft. Some 4 engine bombers would be nice. Now with all this said. Be warned. The sim has a VERY steep learning curve. Nothing really in the ways of tutorials or anything i could find. But youtube and google have plenty.
Hope this helps with your decision to purchase this awesome title. Plan on playing this one for a while as at the time of writing this review, i have barely even scratched the surface. Looking forwards to MP! See you in the skies!
