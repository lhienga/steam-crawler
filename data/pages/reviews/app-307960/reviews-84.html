\r\n\t\t\t\t\t==== Technical Stuff ====

I really love this game. I just wish it was better. I've mostly played in VR, and I've mostly played it on Linux (yes, it works on Linux through Steam Proton). I even took a required script that you must run in order to save the game on Linux from the official IL-2 forums and edited it for the Steam version. Hell, Proton was even updated a few days after I reported my first experience with the game, fixing an issue that I mentioned.

I want to really recommend this game - especially for 2D players. In VR, the performance can be really bad. Again, I *mostly* played this in VR on Linux, but recently installed Windows 10 on another hard drive for VR games. While it does perform better (as it should, SteamVR on Linux is still in beta), it still has a lot of issues. The framerate is all over the place - during takeoff it will usually sit at 44-45 until something happens and it'll dip into the 20s. Once you are up in the air, it can range from 35-90. I've really tried to get this game running well, but it just won't. I'm thinking it's probably because it wasn't made for VR in the first place, so maybe some engine difficulties or other settings affect it.

==== Game ====

I'm no expert at flight sims, but I've played a few before this one that gave me experience and allowed me to jump right in. The handling is pretty damn good - the controls are the tightest I've ever experienced in a flight sim before.

The combat, while robust and exciting at times, usually takes a nosedive (hah) once you understand how the AI works. The AI isn't always great - 1 on 1's will usually just end with you chasing after them and spinning in a circle (left or right) until you kill them. I had a lot of fun with this until I realized that every single AI enemy would do this, then it quickly became stale.

Multiplayer is pretty good. If you like PvP, this is your game. Again, the combat is polished and works very well, the only thing holding it back in my opinion is the poor AI. When fighting real players, you really begin to understand just how different the combat can be. The only thing lacking in multiplayer is dedicated co-op missions. There's just a handful, and they aren't super exciting.

I do want to address the price of the game and it's modules / DLC. The base game does come with a good amount of singleplayer content that will keep you happy for awhile. However, if you're wanting to play multiplayer you will absolutely want to pick up the DLCs for this game. Planes are locked unless you own the DLC, which means that you can't even play on some servers if the only planes available are ones from DLC. If the AI was better, I'd say the singleplayer content in the DLC is worth it too, but ONLY ON SALE. At $12 a piece, they are worth picking up if you get into the multiplayer more.

==== FInal Thoughts ====

If you aren't playing in VR, you're interested in flight sims, or you're an experienced simmer, you'll find joy inside this game that is worth your money (again, on sale). If you're playing in VR...you may have some performance issues. You will also never be able to go back to non-VR flight sims. Sorry bud, you're trapped.

My suggestion to 1C is to focus on Virtual Reality when they make their next IL-2 (or flight sim) game. When the game runs at 90hz, you feel like you're in the plane. It's a great experience and one that had me smiling the whole time. Until the framerate dipped down to 20.

Oh, and consider supporting Linux please. :)\t\t\t\t\t
