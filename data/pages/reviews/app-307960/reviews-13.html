Its not 100% perfect, but damn is it good.

While it has several "Battles of" expansion packs, including one that adds some of the american army air corps force with an Me 262, some WW1 planes, its OBVIOUSLY trying to fight back to something like IL-2 Sturmovik 1946 which had an insane amount of planes and different theaters. These planes are much better simulated, the game more modern and better looking, and with VR support, but im hoping to see more varied terrain like north african, pacific. Hell, the thumbnail for "Dogfight" mode on multiplayer servers is a picture of a pacific dogfight with wild/hellcats.

There is west european theater content incoming, but the DLC "Normandy" wont be available for sale on steam until its finalized.

Unlike the failed Cliffs of Dover, the cockpits here are NOT clickable, although the switches moves and lights turn on and off as the plane does stuff like losing fuel pressure, or oil pressure, or engines, etc.

You can control ground vehicles, but its so-so, while war thunder might be publicly hated and meme'd (specially by me) for its realism, this game cant hold a candle to it in terms of ground vehicle simulation, and i dont mean just because of the low poly quality terrain or the lack of textures essentially making you drive over huge empty grass plains with little to see for several km nonstop, but also because all vehicles seem to be reduced to only having a driver and a gunner, with only the driver interior modeled, and a mediocre tank crew health system. Im pretty sure even Red Orchestra 2 did a better tank crew simulation.

By FAR the worst part of the game however has to be the stock keybinds. Look, im an Arma player, I play DCS too, Deus Ex, flight or vehicle sims, anything with a huge list of keybinds i can get used to, but not here. The stock keybinds are some of the worst ones i could ever see, not because of how there are so many options or that there's nearly no plane-sensitive control, but because the stock binds are very weird things like "Right Shift G to unlock tail wheel" (im making this one up but you get the poin) whereas G or pressing any other combo with G gets the wheels up. This is specially troublesome for tanks where the stock keybinds are incredibly confusing and seem to make no sense. L-Shift 5 for binoculars? Really? Right Windows C to open driver hatch? Who decided all this crap? And for some reason if you have a big plane, to get to the gunner position you first have to press Left Control C to switch to gunner and THEN press T to take the gunner weapon. Why? Why cant i just switch to gunner and automatically be able to control the gun? Very weird design sometimes.

There IS a "fly by mouse" style like in war thunder, but it works nowhere near as well, intuitive, or smooth as there or even as in rise of flight, sometimes your plane will pitch when you dont want it to, and sometimes the plane will stop rolling before it turns to where you want it to, whether this is made to nerf mouse aim, by design, or because they dont know how to make it better i have no idea, i wouldn't try to fly seriously with this, although to be honest if you want to buy this game chances are you aren't gonna try to anyways. The big issue with this is that it is the ONLY WAY TO FLY WITH A MOUSE. You DO NOT have Aces High/War Thunder/Most flight sims out there way of using the mouse to simulate a flight stick, and albeit i think you can bind keyboard keys to ailerons and roll and so on im not sure if its allowed while not having a flightstick, your only 2 options of controls are the mouse-aim as it is or getting a flightstick. Screw you and screw alternative controls, this is something that bothers me the more i play since i actually really wanted to experiment with other ways to fly planes.

So lets get to the weird thing about the game. "What is it called?"
WELL:

The GAME ITSELF is called "IL-2 Sturmovik Great Battles". But its called so in the same way "DCS" is called "DCS: World", the Great Battles game is essentially a DLC/Expansion pack/Modules collection of maps, nations, missions, and vehicles just like DCS has their own modules. However the Battle of Stalingrad was the first one to be available. Even if IL-2 Great Battles has NO free content at all, i have no idea why the game essentially forces you to buy BoStalingrad, at least on steam, if you are interested in any other section of the game. The official retail store lets you download whichever module you want and mix them up, however steam forces you to always get Stalingrad, for now with the few modules i suppose this is yet to be a major problem but if more different ones get released people will start wondering why are they forced to purchase BoStalingrad when they might not want to. This game has had this business model since release and i think they could really learn from DCS in the way they offer the game itself as a free manager/launcher to let you buy whatever you want.

♥♥♥♥, they dont even need to offer anything free, just say upfront that Great Battles is a module manager or a launcher to have all other "Battles of" DLC, and if Steam doesn't allow you to, you could always just offer a free Po-2 or An-2 over a plain field to get around it the same way DCS gives you a TP-51 and the Su-25T.

That being said, this is still the best WW2 combat game out there until DCS's WW2 content catches up, the multiplayer is alive and im told the AI is good, the game features several singleplayer content, including random mission generators and dynamic campaigns.\t\t\t\t\t
