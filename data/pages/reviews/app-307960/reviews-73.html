\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is not worth it's price tag. There are 4 planes for each side which is a total of 8 non-clickable cockpits (although the models are beautiful) and any other aircraft are $15+ (in a game that you already spent $50 on...). The default controls (for my stick, anyways) are simply put, ass. This may not be the case for everyone, but oh lord is it for me. The single player would be alright if it weren't for the fact that the AI is also ass, and an ace level 109 will perpetually turnfight you no matter what aircraft you are in...and usually lose, so there is no real skill in killing the AI in a 2 or 3 versus one fight, so long as you're reasonably fast. Really most of the difficulty is just not running out of ammo. The multiplayer is also okay, and you actually need to try to beat other players most of the time. But for a $50 price tag with single player that is basically bust if you want a real challenge, it is definitely not worth full price. I understand that PvP is a big part of combat flight sims, but there is a big portion of players that don't want to deal with other players and want an enjoyable single player experience. Getting into PvP can also be really difficult if you don't already have head tracking and pedals, which can be expensive. Not that there is any issue with those who do PvP with those, but players without will find themselves at a significant disadvantage simply because of lack of hardware. I can only really recommend the game with a sale, or if you're okay with paying $50 for multiplayer and an almost alright singleplayer\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere do I even begin with this one...

IL-2 Sturmovik is by far the most immersive and realistic Flight/Tank sim I’ve ever played. Each plane in this game is a treat to fly, and is totally worth spending the time to learn about each and every one so that you understand how to use them effectively, (especially planes like the JU 87 Stuka which is a real pain to take off with heavy ordinance if you don't know what you're doing).

Flying itself in this game isn't all too difficult, for newcomers to flight sims like myself, the controls/difficulty can be customised to make the physics more simplistic, and could essentially be your preferred way to play IL-2. Singleplayer that is... in multiplayer i'm sure there are servers that'll allow you to play with simplistic controls but most will require you to play in expert. The learning curve is harsh but trust me... it's worth it.

But for me personally, I do prefer a singleplayer experience and this game most definitely provides you with that. From Instant Action, to Scripted Campaigns and even a Mission Editor, IL-2 has a lot to offer those who just want to immerse themselves in whatever vehicle/role they so choose.

Despite all this however, IL-2 will get pretty expensive if you want to truly get the full experience. Sure Battle of Stalingrad (BOS) has a lot of content on it's own, but if you want access to all the new maps and vehicles, you will need to buy the other expansions for roughly £40 each (or just wait for a sale). A great thing about IL-2 is that you can create an account on their official website and link it to Steam. Anything you buy on their store will automatically be playable if linked correctly. In the time of writing, I have: Battle of Stalingrad, Battle of Bodenplatte and Tank Crew (BOB and Tank crew were bought on their site). Purely because I wanted a mix of Eastern and Western fronts along with the tanks.

Speaking of Tanks...

IL-2 Sturmovik was never meant to be a tank simulator. The Tank Crew expansion along with the two tanks available in BOS were originally a mere side project the devs made in their free time. Tank Crew at this time of writing is still in Early Access but from what I understand it is almost complete with the Elephant Tank Destroyer coming in the next patch. Tanks are amazing to play in this game, honestly blowing other tank games like 'War Thunder' and 'World of Tanks' out of the water. It truly is insane how well they play and feel here and i'm so glad I bought the early access for Tank Crew to use them. Tanks are alot easier to control than the planes and have a lot of singleplayer content available for them as well.

Overall, I love this game. Although my current playtime is a bit bad, there truly isn't that many other games that give me the satisfaction and immersion that this one does.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs of 20 Jun 2020: Frame rate has taken a very hard hit after some updates. I can play DCS with medium settings decently on my laptop. But with IL2, the frame rate is bad/jerky unless I play at lowest settings which looks bad. This should not be because DCS graphics are superior and modern fighter sims have avionics/weapon systems that are far more complex and demanding on the CPU. I bought this game for multiplayer. But in Asia, game lobbies are mostly empty. So I thought I could play the campaign. But alas! there was none as campaigns are sold separately. So I would not recommend this game unless you have a very powerful PC and love WWII planes.
