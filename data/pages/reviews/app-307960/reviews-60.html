\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIL-2 Sturmovik: 1946 was one of the first games I've ever played, and it's safe to say it shaped my life forever. I've never really returned to it, but with my excitement for flight simulation coming back big, and a sale happening, I figured I'd give it a try.

Just from how the planes handle, how the entire thing feels and looks, I can confirm, this is still the good old IL-2 but polished, refined and modernized. That itself is enough for me to warrant a good review.

However, it's not all good news. Compared to 1946, Battle of Stalingrad's amount of content seems more like a demo than anything.

I don't really know how this franchise was built - apparently at some point there was a base game that was expanded on over the years with substantial DLC - regardless, the single-purchase IL-2 Sturmovik 1946 had somewhere between 300 and 400 aircraft in total, coming from all over the world. It had scenarios spanning almost all major battlefields in the second world war, it probably had thousands of missions as well as a very powerful but simple mission editor, as well as a lively modding community pumping out ever-new content for the old game - at least that's how I remember it. It's been over a decade since I last played.

Battle of Stalingrad seems to be a new standalone game, which, as the old series will be expanded upon in countless DLC, maybe one day reaching the scale of what 1946.

Now compare to that this game. No mission editor from what I can tell. The modding community seems near non-existent. The game seems to have launched with eight aircraft, four german and four russian (It also has tanks apparently but come on who the hell plays IL-2 for tAnKs). The battles seem limited to parts of Russia and some regions in germany.

Any extra content will have to be purchased seperately, apparently each adding a generic european theatre of war and a handful of planes - many of which are variants of planes already in other DLC or the base game.

That's not to say the game has nothing to offer. The content that is there is much higher quality than it was back in the day. The plane models are beautifully modeled, look, feel and sound amazing. The terrain has also seen a big increase in fidelity.

I haven't really played it much yet. I heard it has very high quality campaigns. This is just some first impressions.

Whatever they do, there's too much nostalgia in this series for me to dislike.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Absolutely 100 % recommend this simulator. It flies fantastically with lot's of aircraft involved in the missions and dogfights. I personally find the graphics excellent, with the cloud effects and looking out to the side to see your flight members just off your wing, really draws you in to the moment. Same with looking down below and picking out the landmarks and targets. The campaign areas are pretty vast with lots to do and various battles going on around the region. Each plane has its own feel and 'talks' to you, alerting you when you are on the edge of a stall for example.
I did have massive issues linking profile steam with the game so couldn't use the iL2 store or rename online profile. However the iL2 support team and Olga were fantastic and resolved that for me, which considering the size of their team and amount of players is amazing.
Setting up controls and the UI is nothing like Cliffs of Dover and although as with all flight sims you have that period of tweaking controls to tune them in over time to just as you want. You can pretty much be up and running in no time at all.
Gamewise you have full career campaigns set in each Battle - Stalingrad, Moscow, Kuban, Bodenplatte and soon to be Normandy. Remeber the game has been out and constantly improved over the years. Along with this there are supplemental mini campaigns / scenarios available like Hell Hawks - Battle of the Bulge taking place within certain campaigns i.e Bodenplatte in this case. So plenty to get from this simulator to keep it fresh. They are also trying to build in a ground battle with fully simulated multicrewed if wanted Tanks. Plus there is a WW1 add on though from what ive read in reviews this doesn't have a full campaign like the others do, but does have the main WW1 plane selection to give a change of pace.
Pricewise is where the game seems to get a lot of hate, but with regular sales and what you get for that Battle scenario - you get alot for your money. Campaigns, editor, co operative play, multi player. Obviously the main goto comparisons are DCS which is more jet age focused and firing missiles at a distant target vs the more personal up close n dirty warbird style of combat. I guess personal preference is a major factor if you were forcing yourself into one camp here. However no reason not to play both. The other comparison seems to be one of the original iL2 releases 1946, which is available at a bargin price, offers a vast amount of planes in the pack and campaign regions, along with mods improving all aspects of the original game. Once again no reason why couldn't play this one for a change, but il2 Great Battles is so far advanced in comparison in all respects. So yes, highly recommend this game....Edit The Tanks and campaigns is to the same high standard as the flight and cand stand on its own merits. The fact that it links in is outstanding.

My 1.2 hours on this game were entirely spent in a single mission pausing and unpausing over and over checking my keybinds, while spending much of the time searching for conflicts in controls. All around pretty nice, changing my recommendation to a yes but be prepared to struggle for a bit trying to map everything.\t\t\t\t\t
