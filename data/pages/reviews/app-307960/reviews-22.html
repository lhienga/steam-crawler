\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, this is a fun World War II sim that strikes a great balance between realism and accessibility. Like DCS, it's realistic enough to feel immersive and rewarding, but unlike DCS it comes with lots of quick mission and campaign generators so you don't have to fiddle with a mission editor for hours just to get into some combat. Like War Thunder, you can fly lots of different planes and get into the action quickly, but unlike War Thunder there is no predatory, exploitative pay-or-grind system underlying the whole thing. You buy individual planes and campaigns a la carte, when they are on sale they are quite a good deal, and there are frequent sales.

That said, there are some relatively minor but annoying nitpicks you will notice after you've been playing for a while:

-- There is no way to skip to the action in a campaign mission, and the time acceleration flat out does not work properly in a campaign mission. Like, you'll set it to 8x as fast as normal time (as high as it goes), but you will observe things happening barely any faster than with no time acceleration at all. This means that in campaign missions, you will spend a LOT of time just sitting there, watching the Russian steppe scroll slowly by, while you fly to objectives dozens or even hundreds of kilometers away. It's like watching paint dry. This REALLY kills the enjoyment of campaigns that are otherwise pretty fun.

-- [EDIT: since I wrote this review, the developers updated how machine guns feel, which resolves that complaint. That's another great thing about this game: the developers are continuously expanding and improving the core game engine, and you the player get the benefit of those engine updates even if you don't purchase the expansions.]

-- Getting multiplayer to work with your friends is very fiddly and finicky. I've spent hours digging around in my firewall and router settings just trying to make it so my friends and I can join each others' games, and it's only worked intermittently. Inviting people to play with you via the Steam interface does not work. It's like we're still in 1996, (To be fair, DCS is just as bad in this department, and this is the one department where War Thunder does a much better job. The better to get their hooks in you...)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNoob level hours - so ill give a new player style of review.
Considerations;
- Yoke and throttle quadrant played with
- I know the Principles of flight/Flying
Size - So to start this review i'm going to actually begin with the file size for 25gb this game is amazing for the file sizes so if space is an issue that is the general amount.

Ui - The main menu is the simplistic approach in terms of ui but the trade off is sharp and user friendly layouts.

Settings - Settings for keybind's can be a hassle to navigate however once you get a general gist of where things are then its very easy to find.

Visuals - Graphics are absolutely outstanding it is a bit difficult to identify as i'm still learning the silhouettes of each side which can be a problem if your playing as a fighter however i see this more as a experience and knowledge gap.

Gameplay - Gameplay this is actually really really good as their is two main ways i've seen so far of directly playing the game itself which is Quick Play/Skirmish/Multiplayer or Career/Campaigns you have the power to choose if you want to jump straight into fights or play true to life and take missions day by day with set flight paths to follow

Features - High Quality Assets as developer standards, flexible styles of playing (bomber, Tank, Fighter), High Functionality of all dials and systems on aircraft and tanks, no paywall at all, DLC's are very high quality, Constant updates and transparency from the developers.

My thoughts:
Personally i see this as a "go to" sim. For the first few hours of playing there are many things to learn and understand if you are completely new to aviation as you'll need some concepts drilled in your head however give it time keep taking small steps towards making a full flight (none assisted take-off, patrol, landing) and the games potential sky rockets.

I do wish they showed off more naval aspects of the game however at the current pace from the devs and their connection to the community i can confidently say they wont miss it out its just a matter of waiting for it, i have a lot of faith in 1C as they have proved nothing less than complete trust and passion in the community and great detail and attention to history and respect to WW2 as i have yet to see any form of bias.

I Would urge you to get this game as the money may be steep for the game and DLC's combined however once you have the grip on flight and gameplay you wont think twice about it at the time of this review it is only £6 (on sale!) for this Base Game experience which is unbelievable. to put this short this is a gem of a game and will live on for me as a go to for my WW2 itch.\t\t\t\t\t

Not recommending because it misrepresents itself which means people should take a very hard look at it. This game's focus is serious WWII Eastern Front flight sim PVP. Very niche. Also they threw in some tanks, AA guns as well as some WWI stuff and there is a limited single player campaign of a sort, but all that is to sucker in people from outside the game's very limited intended audience. I have less than an hour in game. It's pretty dull. Which is fine, very flight simy. I did a mission. Ground attack. I shot up some pixels. Pixels bounced off the pixels the way pixels do. That was a thing I have no desire to repeat.
