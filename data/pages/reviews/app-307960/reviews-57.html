Born in 1918, the third son of a Naval Officer stationed in Vladivostok quickly showed an aptitude for mathematics and physics. While attending the Far Eastern Polytechnique, he grew his aspiration to become a pilot while interacting with the Red Army pilots. His opportunity came earlier then expected: a squadron leader saw his potential and enthusiasm as beneficial to the progress of the motherland, and recommended him to be enrolled into a flight school. There, he excelled as usual, earning is wings sooner than most of his peers and flying passenger planes around the Far East regions of Russia.

When Soviets signed the Non-aggression Pact with the fascists, Georgy moved to the other side of the Urals to become a commercial pilot connecting the routes between the newly befriended nations. However, the peace would not last, and when he heard the news of the fascist invasion of his beloved country, he immediately volunteered to become a military pilot, just hoping that his skills that served him well during peace can equally be useful for the defense of the motherland.

Despite the early defeats of the Soviet Union, he knew that his people, and the Comrade Stalin, would not let the invaders desecrate the motherland without being punished. He bided his time, learning tirelessly about his new plane, Yak-1, far behind the front line--and at the last stages of the Battle of Moscow, he was able to taste victory regardless of how small he contributed.

A year passed, and the fascists have not yet backed down, this time marching towards the southern city on the banks of beautiful Volga, Stalingrad. He was re-assigned to 11th IAP near the city of Stalingrad where the ruthless and barbarous steps of the Nazis were terrorizing its citizens. He knew then it was here that his selfless courage will be tested.

Soon, he distinguished himself among his peers for his exceptional skill as a dogfighter, but also his determination to survive. But he was also a good friend; while he once shot down 4 planes in a single day, he simply shed tears for the fallen comrade whom he could not save, but only avenge. He was soon promoted to the rank of Senior Lieutenant, while also receiving numerous medals in honour of his services.

But even he could not stay invincible. As the fascist onslaught decimated the ground forces in the City of Stalingrad, the air force of Soviet Union also took a significant toll. Soon, they were using Yak-1 to perform a bombing run, without any escorts. When his captain told his squadron the details of the mission, there were many disgruntled murmurs. But soon, everyone understood: they had to do everything required in order to win.

But the sortie was also to be Georgy's last flight. As the German interceptors closed in, he bravely fought them off to make sure his wingmates can return safely to the base. However, one lucky shot from a German Bf109 set his engine on fire instantly. Comrade Klubov, one of his wingmates and a friend, saw a parachute opening as Georgy's plane was going down in flames. He was not seen since.

No one knows what happened to the brave, kind-hearted hero of the Soviet Union. Considering the barbarity of the fascist invades, he may have been shot on sight when he was found deep in their territory. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish that I could actually review the gameplay, but I can't. I've got hours and hours of time in the game now, and maybe fifteen minutes of that has been actually flying planes. The rest has been controller configuration. I could rag on about how game developers should include default joystick mappings for popular controllers, and how I'm not a game developer and so I don't know where the buttons should go, or if I should map them at all (there's somewhere between 100-200 axis and buttons to map, and even with control map layering they aren't fitting all on a joystick and throttle), but I can't spend too much time on that, because then I wouldn't get the chance to mention how difficult the developers make it to use a HOTAS in the first place. There aren't any double-mapping indicators. There isn't an option for control map layering. You can't even navigate menus with a joystick. Menu navigation inputs just aren't there in the key mapping menu, so I guess that they just forgot to add a key feature literally every single other game with HOTAS support has. In order to use the game with my VR headset, which is set up on the other side of the room from my desk, I need to start the game, open services, enable the TARGET service, open the TARGET GUI, and start the menu navigation profile that maps the up, down, left, right, enter, and escape keys to the joystick. And then open device manager, find the joystick that gets disabled when I turn on the TARGET profile, every time I turn it on, and reenable it. Then play the game for five minutes, fail miserably at doing anything, turn off SteamVR, turn off the game, relaunch it in the normal mode, drag my HOTAS across the room and set it awkwardly in a space on my desk that's not large enough to accommodate a HOTAS, do the TARGET dance, re-bind keys to improve the gameplay and playtest the mapping with the awkward setup on my desk, think that there's nothing else that needs to be done, turn the game off again, turn the game on in VR mode again, do the TARGET dance, find something else wrong with the keymapping, continue my circuit through this layer of hell, cry, scream, wake up at 5 PM in a chair with a VR headset strapped to my face, etc.

2/10 Could use some improvements to user friendliness, the plane on the menu screen is kinda cool\t\t\t\t\t
