\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview For Tank Crew - Clash At Prokhorovka.

This game is part of the Great Battles Series of IL2 Sturmovik. It is a standalone Game which is still in early access and currently can only be purchased direct from the IL2 Sturmovik website. However, it is planned to be released onto Steam and already links with any other Steam purchased modules you may already own such as Battle of Stalingrad, etc.

At this stage the game is thoroughly playable and comes with several missions and a couple of scripted-only Campaigns. However, if you join the official forums accessible from the IL2 Sturmovik website you have access to hundreds of fan-made Campaigns and Missions. Not to mention some extremely professional skins for the various AFVs in the game. Also mods for those of us who prefer historic accuracy and wish to see the Hakenkreuz (Swastika) on both the models, splash screens and Third Reich medals and awards. Additionally a mod for a uniformed Waffen SS tank crew in 4K.

The vehicles in the game are beautifully modelled both inside and out with a lot of functionality. Any dog-fighting or bombing you see is up to the usual standard of Great Battles own aircraft in appearance. Track IR is supported, but is not essential for the tanks. This is a very good simulation of Tank Warfare.

However, there is a downside at present. The buildings are very good, as are the roads, but the flora and fauna is not so good and your tanks tend to bounce off trees rather than uprooting and flattening them. Tanks simply go straight through fences and smaller obstacles rather than knocking them down. At this stage I cannot yet order my tank to move to a point - though this is clearly in progress as the commands are there, they just do not work yet. Nor can the player pop-smoke to hide and change position when badly threatened which is something I could do in M1A1 Tank Platoon back in the late 80's! There are no Infantry in the game yet, but again this is planned, but will apparently only be visible soldiers running around, but not taking an active role in the battle. They will not be able to mount and dismount vehicles either and are basically eye candy for the purposes of immersion. There are only scripted Campaigns and no Career mode as with the other modules where anything can happen and medals and awards are given. I cannot ascertain if this is even planned, though I hope it is.

I do recommend this game if you are simply wanting an up to date Tank Simulator that is Tank-v-Tank and you do not mind playing missions and Campaigns that are linear and are always the same so that if one fails the first time, one now knows how to do it on a second attempt and knows the correct route to take.

If like me, you have played the heavily modded version of Steel Fury Kharkov 1942, then you will be somewhat disappointed by this game. It would appear (and I hope I am wrong) that no attempt is going to be made to at least produce a modern version of that game, never mind improve upon it. If we are not ever to have functioning infantry and other vehicles in the game that follow orders and mount and dismount and play an active part in the battle and can help sway the outcome (again, something that was included in the old aforementioned M1A1 Tank Platoon decades ago), as well as a Career Mode, then I think ultimately this game will fail and flop.

It is true every cloud has a silver lining and the devs have just requested on the forums that players submit to them skins, missions and Campaigns. Those that are included in the final version of the game will be paid for. This indicates that the game still is far from finished, so perhaps the improvements I would like to see may come to pass.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest flight simulator you can buy.

*edited 2021-10-5*

At the time of this review I'm around 300 hours of playtime. If you plan on buying this know that it rewards patience. This isn't a typical video game and to get the most out of it you will need to learn how to fly and operate these aircraft. The fidelity is high, the attention to detail is astounding. There are other flight sims on the market to be sure, but for the best WW2 experience the IL2 series continues to lead the way.

Also be aware that Battle of Stalingrad is part of the "Great Battles" series of modules. They all use the same base game, each just unlocks a time frame in the conflict, and planes relative to that time frame. I recommend starting with one that has your favourite aircraft and if you like it, go from there.

Also be aware that the developer's website has a great webstore where you will want to go check out. Frequent sales can be found there and therefore the point of entry is cheap.

This is best enjoyed if you have a joystick, even better with a HOTAS, even better if you can set yourself up with head tracking via a webcam and free software, or another method. I built a simple 3 point head tracker for under $10, guides to do this are found easily online and add to the immersion.\t\t\t\t\t
