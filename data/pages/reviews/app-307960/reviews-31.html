\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember very long in my teenage years i got the IL2 sturmovik that would become the 1946 collection. This review is mainly aimed at people who might have tried the older titles and are not so sure about this one (as it was the case for me). I will not get into the full details because more experienced people have done better. What i will say is that with the base stalingrad module you will receive a number of planes that you can use in a dynamic campaign were you can chose how many missions you can have (do you want to fly every day or every other day) as well as adjust realism to your liking as in the older titles as well as how many enemies you are going to face. You can climb up the ranks and as a commander you can put more pilots to various flights or change aircraft for your team members. You can also have single missions or scripted campaigns (some you have to buy individually however). Overall pretty similar to how it functions in 1946 but much more polished in terms of graphics and flight models. German planes are for newcomers because of their automated engine management (just dont burn the engine with full throttle) allied planes you just need to do your homework with how to fly them effectively (loads of tutorials online) also there is multiplayer which is very fun but hard as well. Overall if you like ww2 or ww2 planes this is were its at. On a final note a have seen people complaining about the repetitive nature of the dynamic campaigns. This is true it can get like that. I guess for me coming from 1946 it was pretty much the same but the scripted ones do provide more flavour but you have to buy them. They do go on offer on steam or on the developers site though.\t\t\t\t\t
