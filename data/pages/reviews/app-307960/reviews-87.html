\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI write this review as someone who does not usually play flight sims, only came into this game because it is VR compatible (HTC VIVE, first gen).

IMPORTANT, THIS IS A 'FLIGHT SIM', which means if you play it like an arcade war thunder, you will miss out alot and probably not get your money's worth. (this is coming from someone who used to play quite a bit of war thunder)
The game is also a tank sim, but as of now, it is not as fleshed out as the flight sim part, in which I don't have much to say as I have not tried it. I don't recommend to get this game just for the SP, and even then I would say this game has the best SP, compared to DCS. Get it with the expectation that you are going to take part in the community. I talk about how good it is as a VR game in the bottom of this review.

Note that this review will get into some of the 'DLCs' of this game, because currently, if you buy through steam, you will need to own BoStalingrad to get the other ones. In terms of where to buy, steam is maybe more frequent with price discounts, but buying straight from their website can allow you to pick and choose which time periods you are interested in and all profits goes to them. IL2 has superior controller support, so not really a problem either way. However if you're going to play using SteamVR (HTC VIVEs & Valve Index), I recommend you buying from Steam.

Reason I recommend this game is, although I had a lot of upfront costs, I enjoy the game & especially its community, I felt I got my money's worth.

I can recommend this game on a few conditions:
  1. you have a flight stick. (important for anyone looking to flight sim)
  2. plan to get trackIR or any VR, or already own one.
  3. you are going to invest time and money to getting your setup right, be warned. (i am 100h+ in and I am still looking for ways to better setup stuff in game.)
  4. is willing to cope with the lack of in game tutorials, and depend on the internet for much of the training stuff.
  5. is willing to fly online multiplayer at some point, because this is where the game shines (as compared to other flight sims, my opinion)

Let me explain:
  1. I for one I imagine this game to be absolutely horrible to fly with a mouse and keyboard, never tried it myself. This game is definitely NOT comparable to War Thunder. If you have at least a cheap flight stick, it will certainly up your enjoyment of this game (or any flight sim for that matter.. not trying to be an elitist, but don't even bother with DCS if you don't have this in my opinion, its not going to be worth your time.) Now the quality of the joystick is also very important, so also shop carefully. If you don't have a joystick, I would suggest to play War Thunder, which is quite fun in my opinion, and come back if you feel like putting in more towards getting better immersion.
  2. This is not a huge problem starting out, and you can look around perfectly fine using the mouse (if you already have a flight stick). The thing about this game is that the control configuration plays a huge part in whether you will like this game or not. Having trackir or VR will allow you forgo a few buttons that control your view in the aircraft. (its not going to be like war thunder where you fly third person, like I said it is a flight sim) TrackIR is what most people use and seems to be the gold standard for competitive multiplayer. As for VR, it is also very immersive, although there are some caveats I will explain below.
  3. The default controls is probably not the best setup for you. Be prepared to continuously experiment with your control setup, this is a simulation in that sense. As of now I fly using a relatively cheap flight stick (TFLIGHT Hotas 4), with VR. I have a keyboard and mouse, with a PS4 controller which performs some less crucial functions. AFAIK I've changed my setup at least 3 times, twice was a huge overhaul. Once was because I changed over from the Yak1 to BF109s, the other was because I saw a post that allowed me to add in modifiers into my joystick. My recent purchase was Voice Attack, which helps to take off some of the burdens of multiple button pressing. As you can see that is quite a bit of time off game and money that I spent to improve on immersion.
  4. there are no in game tutorials. you have to get on youtube to learn the basics and there is nobody going to say what is right/wrong unless you look for help in the community. there are some flight school missions that you can download, and these are community generated missions, and does not come right out the box. (Credit to Utopioneer on IL2 GB Forums). OR you could try doing a Yak-1 run (simulating soviet pilots, who allegedly had minimal training before they had to do combat missions), which I used as my first aircraft, knowing only how to take-off, taxi and land, not knowing much else. (the amount of deaths you probably will have can be quite demoralising, but i'd really learned how to pick my fights.)
  5. Once you get familiar with the a/c, played a few missions in career mode, you will probably be craving for some coordinated action and a more realistic one. In SP, the AI seems to be able to spot and identify aircrafts at the get go. They can be somewhat lousy shots though. In MP this is reverted, it will be hard to identify and spot enemy aircrafts, but don't expect to live long once you're in bandit's sights. I prefer IL2 over other sims because it has quality of life stuff like stat tracking and SP & MP campaigns. There is an MP server with persistent tracking, mimicking something like a real life, real time campaign called TAW. Another is coming out that will be on a smaller scale, but with a more diverse mission set including logistics. (check out 40 Mike Mike Clowns Server, planned be out in 08 MAR 2020). All MP servers usually have stat tracking, down to the sortie level, and the realtime logs for analysis.

Do I recommend it as a VR game?
TBH, you will still need to get a flight stick to enjoy this game. Even the cheapest one can do wonders. I got a THRUSTMASTER HOTAS 4 (ps4 version of HOTAS X, its cheap, but if you can put in extra, it will go a long way to buy the T1600m).

In my opinion this is one of the better flight sims to buy as your first one. The game is not a hardcore simulation every single thing of the plane, where plane start up is simplified to a point where 10 buttons/knobs are just simplified to one keyboard press. This is what I prefer, but I can see that some of the more hardcore simmers might want to give this game a pass.

Otherwise the VR implementation is in my opinion functional. This is because the game runs quite bad if the map is pretty populated, with BoBodenplatte career mode being the worst. I had the best experience in VR in BoMoscow and BoStalingrad, not so much the rest. You are going to have trouble getting 90 FPS, even with a high end rig. I personally don't have a problem with it, since we have motion smoothing, (or ASW in Oculus).

VR for MP is pretty good, but it will not look pretty, because you will probably need to turn the game graphics down to get better rates and to be competitive. Either way flying in VR feels really great and immersive ( you just need a flight motion chair :) ) I've got plenty of good moments. Another caveat of VR in MP Expert mode, is that the cockpit will move if your head touches the cockpit hard surface, which can be somewhat disorientating. By default this is off in the non-expert ones, but in MP it is usually on, because of accusations of people it is somewhat abused in MP. If you really hate this, you will have to either just play in SP and find a non-expert MP server.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIL-2 Sturmovik: Battle of Stalingrad is one those games that if you transition from War Thunder, you get to feel the thrill of flying in this game. This is far far better than anything War Thunder have to offer.

This is also the few rare flight simulation game where mouse and keyboard is a viable option(yes, you can fly with just mouse and keyboard though you need to adjust to the game's physics and engine). Unlike War Thunder, your aircraft will have resistance in flight, even snapping your wing or lose control if you push it too hard. Understanding the plane is how you learn to fly, thankfully this game is extremely forgiving even if you're a casual sim player. Options were given for those who want to fly, shoot, land and enjoy the spectacular view of the ruins and snow steppe of Stalingrad. The AI engine start up allows people to quickly hop in and start taking off, there is even an AI literally fly for you(I used it once and found it nice as a baby step, but ditch it after I learn how to take off, flight controls and landing for an hour)

This is truly a spectacular flight game, so good that I officially ditch War thunder for this. Best of all, you can play this OFFLINE, YES YOU OWN THIS SON OF A GAME. Play single-player even if my internet service is not available.

Is very expensive for a game, so plan what to buy, when to buy. Personally my next aim is the Battle of Moscow, Battle of Kuban and finally Battle of Bonderplatte. These package offers missions that simulated the epic battles of WW2 which I am a huge fan of.

I am a casual, pathetic flight sim player, and I highly recommend this game. You need to learn a bit on flying, but once you get the hang of it. Experience a flight sim that offers true hardcore simulation and extremely fun flight combat for the casuals. Be wary what server you play, if you play mouse and keyboard, go to mouse and keyboard friendly server. If you save up to get a Joystick or Hotas, go to those flight stick server and experience getting wrecked by veterans :)

Be warned however, this flight sim graphical requirement is really really steep, my GTX 1080 can run this fairly easily though minor stutters did happen from time to time. You can technically run this on a GT540M but is literally 8 bit lag fest and barely playable...... Get a good rig if you want to play this.

Recently I bought the JU-52 transport plane DLC at a discount, now is the very first time I get to experience the horror of flying an unarmored, pathetically armed, painfully slow and sluggish plane that it's main role is to transport supply, drop troops and maybe drop a parachute supply(not fun, not fun at all) from one place to another. Flying one without fighter cover is suicidal, and I love seeing myself in game screaming in agony as my planes burns and crash onto the snowy plain of Moscow while my radio chatter scream in panic = The reality of flying a JU-52 during WW2.

Updated:
I finally bought Battle of the Bondenplatte, man, flying the P-51 Mustang, P-48 Lightning Supermarine Spitfire Mk.X, Me 262 is truly phenomenal I now understand why this game is truly superior vs War Thunder, [cause I never able to play THESE DAMN PLANES in WAR THUNDER!!!!] Hmmm, I might actaully tryout WW1 game mode.

Updated 28/11/2020
I finally got my HOTAS FLIGHT STICK 4 and PSVR. Playing this is insanely immersive. Cockpit views is insanely good.

I love this game, DREAM COME TRUE.\t\t\t\t\t

TDLR: Go with DCS
If you are buying to play IL2 on screens or in a cockpit using screens, by all means have at it. If you want a VR experience --- might want to think twice.... Also major security concern.... passwords are stored in a text file lol. Also need a separate login. We all love to do that don't we. Lastly, the "feel" is off. Its hard to explain, but I have personally logged over 20 hours of actual flight as the pilot and have many more as a passenger inside numerous platforms including helicopters, cesna's, commercial jets, military prop planes, blackhawks, and cargo planes. IL2 is less than a simulator, but more than an arcade game. The flight physics feel more correct in DCS and X-plane 11.

The cockpit controls cannot be manipulated, keyboard commands only; they have specifically said that they will not change this.

PROS: Looks very nice. Really pleasing to the eye. Open world, multiplayer, lots of freedom. Highly and easily custom set controls. Sounds are well done.

Cons general:
They flight physics do not feel right. The game tries to be somewhere between an arcade and a sim. It doesn't seem bad, but don't expect an accurate level of realism. For some players this may be a pro.
- The tank missions leave something to be desired. Good ideas but the required amount of effort was simply not put into them.


Cons VR:
- When acting as a gunner, the way you latch into the gunners sites feels off. Its satisfying to get a kill from a gunners position for sure. It might be better standing up, but the controls are generally wonky. It could definitely be improved.
- Cockpits are not interactive and there are no plans to ever make them interactive.
This one for me is a big deal. It breaks immersion because there are too many keyboard commands necessary to play it as a sim.

When I get a 2 VR setup in one room I might go back due the multiplayer cockpit/gunner options. Make the cockpits at least usable and I'd spend a good amount of time in it despite the other drawbacks which I could definitely get over.\t\t\t\t\t
