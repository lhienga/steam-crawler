\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUsing an Xbox controller works fairly well with this game, but a hotas and/or VR setup is probably ideal. Mouse & keyboard is an option too. It took me about 30 minutes to bind all of the different functions. Binding pitch, roll and yaw trims is essential if you want a smoother flight.

The damage models on the planes are really nice. It's nice to know that if you target specific areas of an enemy target, the damage you cause to that area is clearly visible and affects the aircraft you've hit. Watching water coolant piss-out all over the place is a great sight, and knowing that your enemy's engine will cease up in time because of that is just awesome. The autopilot function is great - especially if you're in a multi-crewed aircraft and fancy switching between the gunners for a more relaxed game session. The ability to speed up and slow down time is a very handy function when running long career missions.

I got this game during a sale as well as an additional dlc pack and a Spitfire that was on sale. I haven't played much of the dlc "Battle for Bodenplate" but the Spitfire that the dlc comes with is really easy to fly and is a joy to pilot. I haven't tried the additional Spitfire I purchased yet, but I'm sure it will fly just as well.

I really like the career mode in this game. I haven't touched multiplayer, but the career mode generates missions and territorial control over the course of the campaign. The beauty of it is that you can change squadrons whenever you like. (Different squadrons use different aircraft).

I am yet to try a scripted campaign, which I believe are meant to be historically accurate, so I'm looking forward to that.

The maps in the game are the closest renditions of what the areas looked like during WW2. Previously classified aerial pictures were used by the developers in this game to attempt to recreate how everything was back then.

Enjoying it a lot so far.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a long time fan of IL-2 games. Having played Il-2 (2001) on my family computer at one point when i didn't know what I was doing. So this review may have some bias. But I say this is the best flight sim for WW2 planes, and is easily one of the best out there.

Picked it up on sale and very quickly bought almost all of the dlc. Make sure to checkout their own website for forums and development updates (Battle of Normandy dlc coming soon) and everything from tips and tricks to full on cockpit breakdowns.

That said, this isn't a study sim (like DCS: World,) and has lots of options for making it easier and more approachable. There's even mouse aim, so no need to hesitate if you don't have a HOTAS. While the mouse aim isn't as smooth as Warthunder, it's really not bad, and isn't a bad way to start. However, I would recommend a joystick.

Only 30 hours in and I can predict many, MANY, more hours will be played.

This is where it gets long and I drone on:

Game seems gated at first, but isn't. The base game is just that. A repeatable career with 8 planes to choose from, and a campaign. You can take these planes into multiplayer, they are good enough to win fights. However, it is only the battle of Stalingrad. You'll want to grab the other "Battle" dlcs for the best bang for your buck. I'd recommend getting Battle for Kuban for good Russian and German battles. Battle for Bodenplat for American and British planes and careers.

AI is pretty good, yet fairly limited. I don't think a lot of people realize there are difficulty settings. Playing on hard, I definitely got beat handily by an La-5 that just out turned and retained more energy than me in a 109 G6. Also had a P-51 out energy me and just run until I couldn't get a shot.

This game is also BEAUTIFUL. Recently, all planes were updated to 4K textures. I'm only on a GTX 1660, playing on very high. It's not super demanding and looks great doing it.

Lastly, the game is modular. I said it before, and I'll reiterate it here. You can buy the base game here, and go buy the DLC for LESS on their website. Large packs - you'll save at least 10 dollars. Best example is Bodenplat. 60+ CAD on steam, 49.99 on their website.

My only issues are that in Career, some of the missions get repetitive. Doing the same bomber escort can be boring sometimes. If that happens, request a transfer to a different squadron in the headquarters. Fly a different plane. The other issue is keybinds. The defaults are weird.

If you're a fan of WW2 planes/combat and simulators, this seriously can't be missed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR the best all-around combat flight sim in 2021. Just buy the game already! It goes on sale regularly for $8. All titles and DLC are of similar high quality and also go on sale.
BUYER GUIDE:
- On Steam, buy Battle of Stalingrad, it's a good intro time period for World War 2. Buy all future DLC on the developer's website (http://il2sturmovik.com) to give them the most money. If you link your Steam account to the website, all the content will work.
- Buy Bodenplatte DLC to fly USA, UK, and late-war aircraft over Germany.
- Buy Kuban to fly mid-war aircraft over the Black Sea (a very pretty map, and good planes).
- Buy Moscow to fly early-war aircraft and defend Moscow (historical interest).
- Buy Flying Circus, Tank Crew, or collector planes depending on your interest - I fly Soviet and can highly recommend Yak-1B, Yak-9 or 9T, La-5FN.

IL-2 Great Battles Series (Stalingrad + expansions) does something really impressive for a milsim: it is approachable for a casual new player without compromising on its fidelity to source material. It could use a better in-game tutorial, but there's tons of tutorials online and it's fairly easy to learn if you have some patience. I came to the game knowing nothing and was able to pick up a ton of info in a short time.

RECOMMENDED FOR: People into wargames, military history, aviation, complex sims, online multiplayer, and joystick, head-tracking or VR users. People who want the most modern WW1 flight sim (Flying Circus DLC) or a WW2 tank sim (Tank Crew).
NOT RECOMMENDED FOR: "Pick up and play" with no effort required; Folks who get upset easily when they are bad at games; or dislike flying WW2 Eastern Front (although more Western Front content is being added). Nazi fanboys (you all should stay away from games in general).
COMPARE: IL-2 Desert Wings Tobruk (North Africa), MS Flight Simulator 2020 (similar quality civilian flight sim), IL-2 1946 (old but more aircraft)
CONTRAST: War Thunder (arcade game with no historical fidelity), DCS ("study sim", less accessible, more expensive, mostly modern aircraft)

STRONG SUITS:

- HISTORICAL AUTHENTICITY: All airplanes in this series are presented in their proper timeframes. Regiments in campaign mode are located exactly where they were historically, flying the aircraft they really flew. With DLC you get to fly Soviet fighters, bombers, and attack aircraft from 1941-43; German aircraft through 1945; USA & UK 1944-45; an Italian fighter and WW1 aircraft, even drive WW2 tanks on the same map that fighters are dogfighting above.

- AIRCRAFT ACCURACY: The flight model and game engine feels super accurate. Over-pull the stick in a turn and your wing drops in a stall; you'll have to learn real-life fighter maneuvers and gunnery to succeed. The cockpits are modeled accurate to the real thing. The damage model in 2021 feels very true to how the real airframes would have reacted to real shooting - small guns, 20mm and 37mm cannons peel and break off pieces from the target, resulting in reduced airplane stability or damaged engine. Your pilot can get shot and lose vision, or pull too many G's and black out. Different aircraft fly completely differently, and to succeed, you will have to learn details about your specific aircraft: the engine (how not to overheat, get max performance); the guns (aim and conserve ammo); how to use the airplane's strengths to your advantage.

- IMMERSIVE & BEAUTIFUL: It's a really gorgeous game graphically - check out some Youtube videos. The canopy gets water droplets when you fly through clouds; the sun shimmers off rivers; the first time I crash-landed my Yak in a grassy field, I had to open the canopy and just take a breather while I looked around. If you have a head tracking software (SmoothTrack is $10), you can move your head IRL to look around the sky, and the game works with VR.

- FULL OF STUFF TO DO: Fly a quick mission to take down bombers. Do a single-player campaign mode with historical regiments, fighter bomber or ground attack. Hop on a multiplayer server and fly sorties with mates. Play coop campaign using the PWCG app. Learn any of the 8-48 WW2 aircraft. The player determines what challenges they want to take on.

Is it for you? If you're interested, I think probably yes. I'll go through some 'cons' and rebut them.

ACCESSIBILITY: The game doesn't give you much of a tutorial, leading some newcomers to be confused.
SOLUTION: Youtube and the IL-2 forums have TONS of detailed tutorials on every single aircraft in the game. Check out the "Flight School" campaigns on the forums. Using this knowledge is easier than it seems, especially if you pick one of the "friendlier" aircraft like the Yak. There is a "reduced complexity" setting where the game auto-manages most of the difficult stuff, but I suggest learning to fly the real thing - it's half the fun.
IL-2 is best with a joystick but you can play just with your mouse. It also doesn't have crazy-high graphics requirements.

MULTIPLAYER: Many folks are scared of flying multiplayer since they'll get shot down, and they're worried about toxic community, which is common.
SOLUTION: You will get shot down, but just roll with it - happens to everyone. Join a popular server like VirtualPilots, watch Youtube videos from Air Combat Library, Sheriff's Sim Shack and others to learn more tips, and you will get better and better. Try joining some Discord servers that are focused on the game.
I've been impressed with the community. It's always a crapshoot with World War 2 games, you can get the Nazi idolizers, "old-timey" sexists, all that kind of thing - shockingly little of that. People can be really helpful towards new players. You'll find spaces that don't tolerate toxic behavior, and there's litle opportunity for griefing. (I'll update if that changes)

SINGLE-PLAYER: Career mode is well-researched, but missions can be a bit boring and the AI simplistic.
SOLUTION: Download the free PWCG app from the IL-2 forums, which gives you a community-created campaign mode that creates much more interesting missions, with AI flights that have their own tasks to carry out, much greater enemy variety, historical aces, etc. The game's "quick mission" feature is also implemented well and lets you get into a dogfight immediately. There are also multiple great free and paid scripted campaigns - check the Stormbirds blog for reviews.

UNLOCKS/DLC: Some reviews mention the game used to have to 'unlock' airplanes through gaining XP. That's not the case in 2021 and you get immediate access to 8 aircraft with each expansion, as well as individual DLC collector planes. In my opinion, all the DLC is fair price for what you're getting - historically-researched and modeled aircraft from 1941-44 using real historical sources. The developers are very transparent on their site about the research that goes into the game.
Multiplayer servers are generally restricted by historical timeframe, so late-war airplanes don't give you an automatic leg up - unless the real-life airplane was OP for its time.

CONTROL BINDING: Yeah, this mostly sucks. But you only have to bind controls once, and you can double bind or leave stuff out so that your setup works for most aircraft that you fly. My control setup works for most Soviet-built fighters. I had no problem with the game recognizing my joystick (VKB Gladiator NXT).
SOLUTION: Google the "Chuck's Guide" which tells you which commands are most important to bind for your airplane. You can also use this spreadsheet of "plane-specific controls", and search IL-2 forums. Make a spreadsheet of your bindings/setup, or use Joystick Gremlin. And learn the specs of your airplane, so you know what you need to bind.
