\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis as a fantastic flight sim that offers all the simulation and realism you need to make you FEEL like you are piloting many of your favorite aircraft of WW2 and for that alone this game is a thumbs up. However I also want to address a few things. First, I see a lot of people complaining or worried about the level of knowledge or skill needed to be able to play this game or claiming the learning curve is just too steep. YOU DO NOT NEED A JOYSTICK TO PLAY THIS GAME. Yes, it is completely playable in a mouse control that acts similar to War thunder although it has its limitations. The point being is that while some claim that you can't have a good time without a joystick or you have to learn every detail about every aircraft just to get it off the ground, that is complete nonsense.There are selectable difficulty settings such as some for automatic engine management or visual icons showing friend from foe. The best part about this game is that if you want a fully immersive WW2 flight simulator (and tank simulator with paid content) this IS the title to get. But if you are also looking for a casual yet highly detailed flight experience and you do not have the luxury of owning a joystick OR a high end computer this can still be the game for you. (I play on a laptop) For those who own a joystick, the key mapping in this game has some great default settings with a remapping that works great. Track IR and VR work pretty well although I played on someone else rig for those.

Now, the career mode in this game is frustratingly simple at times and lacks depth to why you should care about your pilot or the pilots around you and more serves as a mission generator. It is completely functional however, and while I wish for a better career mode I still have logged most of my hours in single player.

The base game gives you a small slice of the Eastern Front in the form of the Battle of Stalingrad and related aircraft but you can spend a fair price on a sale day, campaigns such as the Western Front in 1944 with Bodenplatte, or early war Moscow, or a refreshing change of scenery with the Battle of Kuban. There is also paid Scripted Campains which are actually quite good. Tank Crew while fun and interesting lacks any real content to be considered part of the game. I see it more as an investment into future tank content in Il2. In all, whether you are a casual gamer looking to get into flight sims, or a flight sim expert this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t
