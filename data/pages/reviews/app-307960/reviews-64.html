Only if the base game would offer a bit more of the content that is available currently. It is nice how often sales happen, and when it happens they are priced really reasonably, except for the yak-9t.

Has some weird little bugs, most serious bugs can be avoided by using semi up-to-date hardware.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery good sim, every time I was upset about some weird characteristic on a plane, a quick google search would and I would learn that its actually an accurate representation of an issue the plane actually had. One example I can think of, is taking off in a Mig. I'm no pilot, but I do have years of flight sim experience, from your arcade games like Ace Combat, to the more hardcore flight models like DCS, all of the Janes series in the 90's, and even Top Gun on NES. I don't care who you are, landing on that carrier is way harder than real life!!!! So after all that "experience", I have never had a problem taking off, the first first thing I had to learn to even play my first sim cause it had no easy mode. I kept checking my bindings, joysticks deadzones, rudder, etc, nothing was wrong. At this point I thought the game was crap and the bindings are not working properly. Nope, the sim was actually way more accurate that I would have thought and was actually modeling an issue that plane was known to have, pilots had to apply hard rudder to keep it from drifting off the runway.

Now as for gameplay, like I stated before, this is a simmers sim. You try doing a hard bank like your the kid in Iron Eagle blasting One Visionon the radio, and your gonna stall and drop like a rock. No fly by wire here, and if you have it in full realism, which in my opinion thats why you buy a game like this, you have to pay attention. So if you've read this and find yourself thinking, "this guy doesnt know crap about real flying" this is probably the game for you. If nothings I have said makes sense, it is no offense to you, but this game might be a rough intro. I say that not to scare people away, but just as good advice. You force yourself into the wrong game that doesn't fit you and you might not end up getting into sims at all. Then it'd be a loss for yourself. I personally think these kind of sims are incredible, not only for their realism and difficulty, but also for building an appreciation for what these people did in real life. It would also be a loss for the flight sim community, that's one less member to fly with and to support this awesome hobby and the developers that make them.

I also recommend having a hotas and rudder for play. I never tried to fly without them in this game, so I don't know if its possible to set up mouse and keyboard or gamepad. But if your going to this level of simulation game, you need the proper instruments to really feel that simulation, if that makes any sense to you.

Pros:
- Super detailed flight models, you better know what trim is......
- Graphics engine that is still very flexible, can work on older machines easier
- Historical campaigns to play a part in
Cons
- Not beginner friendly
- Hotas and rudder can be a pricey barrier to entry
- That "flexible" graphics engine means you wont be seeing Microsoft Flight Sim level of graphics
