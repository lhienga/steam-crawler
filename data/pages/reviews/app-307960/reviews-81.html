\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel that this game has astonishing replay potential. Because of a big sale, I got off the fence and bought this and the downloadable additions -- with a certain amount of skepticism. But I am a skeptic no longer.

It doesn't play like an arcade game -- it definitely feels like a simulator. Indeed, it takes practice -- as it would in real life -- just to take some of the planes from the runway into the air without looking like a doofus.

I am running Windows 10 on a pretty good computer rig -- with a Vive VR-setup -- and I like it as well or better than any other flight simulator I've experienced. But it's not civilian -- it's military -- and it feels as I can only imagine it felt to fly the real aircraft in the 1940s, minus only the gnawing fear.

There's a fairly steep learning curve, but with patience I think it can be fairly surmounted. But the need to practice and work things out provides a feeling of accomplishment. This is a genuinely interesting game. I wouldn't expect to jump in and master it quickly.

I needed to do some key-binding (using the game's own available menus) to eliminate much, but not yet all, of the need to use the keyboard while in VR (which is not ideal). But I see no reason to quibble about that. I give this game an overwhelming thumbs up, for those with an interest in history and flying, and with computer equipment that can handle it. I am especially happy with the game because it's working with my Vive VR set up.

Knowing what I know now, I would have kicked myself if I hadn't picked this up during the sale. Indeed, I wouldn't feel cheated if I'd paid full price.

I can see myself still coming to this game years from now. It seems inexhaustible.

-------

Added a few minutes later. After writing this review, and upon further reflection, and partially as a result of cabin fever, perhaps, I picked up the downloadable package of World War I fighter planes, as well (Volume I). Others described how well-crafted those planes are for VR flying -- and I couldn't resist.

So what happened? As the result of inadvertence on my part (after first finding the Arras Map in the list of maps and using it in VR), I accidentally pitted my World War I biplane against a German World War II fighter plane that was much. much faster.

I survived that encounter by diving to tree line, and remaining inconspicuous.

10 /10 -- would flip the bird to a 109 and run yet again.\t\t\t\t\t

You are required to setup your controls (easier that way in my opinion), learning curve is steep with no in-game tutorial. Some basic knowledge of basic fighter maneuvers is really advised.
Decent amount of Planes (Allot of variations of the same ones though, but there is a steady flow of news stuff, prices for them are on the high end, unless you wait for special deals.)
Very good graphics and damage models.

Some people may say that there is a imbalance with respect to planes, on paper there is, but like in WW2, a inferior plane can still take down a more advanced plane with the right tactics, so again, some basic knowledge is required on this subject before going into multiplayer.

This flight sim somehow also manages to be ironically amongst the best tank simulators as you can play (DLC) in tanks with full interiors which is also VR/Tracker supported and possible to play with friends and others as multi-crew online in the same vehicle (same with planes).

My main issues are that, there is a lack of tutorials, and the campaigns could be done more interestingly.
I would really like to see Rise Of Flights basic tutorial in this game with more of that in-depth chalk board explainations, with tutorials explaining basic fighter maneuvers in depth of how to preform them and why they are good. Because for a newcomer to the Flight Sim World it can be really difficult to get into.

Heck, I haven't dared yet in my 90+ hours to try to play without the Auto-Management of my Engines (Which the game thankfully provides.)
But again, there is no tutorial or any real explaination of how to manage your engines etc manaually and efficiently. I would have to look for external sources for this.

Overall conclusion is that the game is great, its worth getting, but it lacks a bit in the single player department and tends to be on the pricey end of things, 1000 euros/dollars plus if you really want everything and your not buying on a sale.
On a sale with up to 75% off it still can be around 175+ euro/dollars for everything.

With everything I also included the Tanks DLC and Flying Circus (World War I Planes from Rise of Flight) so if that stuff does not really interest you we are still looking at 150ish to get everything the game has to offer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an amazing piece of flight simulator software. The Airplanes behave like real planes, and the Simulation of G-Forces, Stalling, Spinning, shaking by flying a damaged plane feel quite real. Weapon damage seems to be correct and unlike other "here not mentioned games developed by other russian studios" flying with a cut off wingtip is not really possible. Neither for you nor for the enemy.
Shooting down planes is not very easy and every airkill is like a little victorie on its own. Mostly they are going down because you shot off the rudder or otherwise damaged the steering hard enough to make it impossible for the enemy to control his aircraft anymore leading to the inability to resist gravity... But sometime... if you land some real good shots you are rewarded by watching the enemy plane catch fire and going down like a fiery meteor or in even rarer cases you hit the Tank which is already loosing fuel with a explosive cannon round igniting the fuel leading to a amazing explosion tearing the plane totally apart. Those Moments are worth playing this game on their own.
Of course, if you meet an experienced enemy pilot, who flyes his craft the way it should be flewn, and knows how to aim his weapons, you will start to feel the stress of beeing fired at. Even being hit feels kinda real and the emotional depth of this Game is realy cool.
The regularly delivered newspapers you get on the frontline every 2 weeks or so open up the competition with real life Aces of that time by listing their airkills to compare with.

in a short: absolute recommendation for every person who loves flying combat flight sims.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIve played this sim now for over 16 hours and all I can say is what a learning curve. It has taken me this long to learn how to map the keys to my controls and then hours to learn to fly the He111 (nothing like FSX). Now that I think I am ready to start the career missions I find the AI aircraft all start to crash on take off. There is no real help out there even though there are many posts on various forums about this issue. It would seem a little odd that the development team at 1C have not sorted this out seeing that the game has been around since 2014. If this had been an issue when I had first started playing I would have asked for a refund. Just my hard luck I suppose. Therefor I cant recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
