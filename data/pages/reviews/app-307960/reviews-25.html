\r\n\t\t\t\t\tright so this is my review, and is not intended as a rant, for a bit of context I got this game in 2019 around november when I was starting to fall out of love with DCS, and only based off the experiances I have had during this game before im deciding to shelve it, I condone doing research before buying and making a informed decision

when I first started out I fell in love with the game, compared to dcs it was alot more polished, running smooth on FPS around 60-80 on med-high settings and still does, my first dlc that I bought was the P40E as I was a really big fan of that plane and planes like the Huricane and Zero, I prefer the pacific theater but this was a breath of fresh air, I would commonly use the P40 in places like Combat box, while being at a huge disatvantage, I liked challanges like that and it just felt rewarding to get a kill like that. I mainly flew allied planes but would switch teams if they were very unbalanced, but after a few months i notice some things start to become off, my first issue, the community

not bagging on anyone in specific and I do not hate anyone, but its more a mix of confusion, in my experiance I met quite a few bad apples on Discord and in game, as well as the players in general both on the Axis and Allies but more so on the Axis but I can see its probably cause they had more players. but i would see people and be on the reciving end of people stepping over eachother, teamkilling eachother, ramming eachother, and waisting ammo on one dude(sometimes me at times). its like if you took the mine seaguls and gave them rabies and then put them in a G-14, its kinda funny in a way. on top of that people in chat are just not very friendly ether at times, but ive met quite a few good people aswell, the kinds who would do S! in chat after a long fight and the ones who don't endulge in rape trains

but after awile I start to notice my second problem, the flight model. now in the middle of 2020 summer had just ended and im soon to start Jr year in HS, i hop on every now and then but I kinda just stopped having fun and one of the reasons is the flight modeling, I didnt know it at the time but the flight modeling is flawed to a large extent. I would be confused and angry on why a full dump of 50 cals wasnt killing this one fighter, and it wasn't like I was missing, i mean yea sometimes I do suck, but another days you guys lite up their wings, tail and fusaladge, waist your engery and watch the guy just scoff, do a 180 in the sky enough to make an f-22 blush and one shot you like an anime. or get so target fixated that another random 109 or 190 does the exact same, so I put the game down for a wile, did some research looking around on forms, YT and Redit and found out, but it was more so complains about the 50 cals but ill get into that later. fast forward to now, SR in HS and even stopped playing flight sims all together and had the gall to go be a real pilot, currently in FS and hoping to graduate within the next two months and make a career. I hopped back on Il2 to see if anythings changed for a week and im disapointed to say it hasn't. but the main problem I believe with the flight models and the strange things ive seen is a mix between the weapons, DM, and flight model and not just a single problem. the reason I say that is because mainly in the 109s the tail section, and wings eat up all of the rounds. and in the DM itself wing spars, fuel tanks, and pullys and everything in between. 50 Cal Ap rounds for example are supposed to go through the plane and damage vital components, as expected a burst into a tail will damage or destroy the aircraft's pitch capability, however that does not happen no matter now much you shoot it. fuel tank fires are also next to zero in chance, and happen way too few, if you manage to get one, log off and go buy a lottery ticket. the reverse is reality for allied aircraft, a single round can be crippling, even if its a hit in a wing tip, and they feel too fragile at times, even if you can keep it in the air it leaves you at a very low chance of winning the dogfight.

and the last gripe, the weapons, I didnt want to go on the whole "5o CaL IsS UnDERPoWErD" train so i wanted to see for myself and you can replocate it too, if you already own the game, just make a quick mission with an enemy fighter on the axis and a plane of your choosing withought a cannon, and get into a turn fight, try to hit their tail and use snapshots. dont see it yet?, well theres multiple problems that I see are plauging the guns that are not 20 mil, first being convergance, in a straight shot its fine but vertical convergance is needed when you are manuvering and firing, your weapons going bellow the nose at the point of being fired, and behind the enemy plane, in order to compensate you need to position the enemy bellow your engine cowling, obscuring the view, forcing you to shoot blind, and waisting more energy. you can set the horrizontal convergance but vertical needs to be implmented. second problem, ammo, for some reason there is only one type of ammo for the 50 and 30 cals being AP, I find that concerning since 50 cals also shot and mainly used API belts and others, I also read somewhere on a fourm that they are not even "combat rated" belts but I could be wrong. and again the opposite is true(sorta) for the axis, their cannons are horrendusly overpowered and its clear as day, shoot anywhere I mean anywhere on a allied plane and you will cripple it, as in you could have the aim of a Jhadi and hit a wing tip and manage to damage the engine from that. and that brings us to the last problem, damage. Damage is very offset to the point where anything that isnt firing cannons is unrelable and cannons are the meta, problems with that is there are a large chunk of allied planes, esspeically late war with 50 cals, yet they are peashooters ingame because of the things ive listed above. the only way for it to work is to kill the pilot of the engine and a single mistake can be fatal. 50 cals also lack the muzzle velocity and impact speed to do any damage even at point blank range. while cannons are lazer beams of death with very durable planes and physic defying technology.

in conclusion, I have lost intrest and lost joy in playing this game, it was fun while it lasted but good things never last forever :( and I think its time I move on from flight sims

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI play largely offline in campaign mode so this review will reflect that. It is a hugely immersive campaign mode which draws you in to your squadron's life. Sqn members get shot down, in some cases killed and that is reflected in the rosters. There is a 'league table' of sqn kills so you can see how you stack up. Missions are challenging- in my last I got into a fairly nasty dogfight and counted myself lucky to escape, flew for 15 minutes back to my base to be jumped by 3 ME109s in the circuit. Cue second dogfight of the day until ammunition dispensed, some desperate evasive manoeuvres until finally my luck ran out. Flames erupting everywhere and trying to get that canopy open on the Yak-1 had me pooing little blue lights until finally I was out and under canopy.. that was mission 1. With visual cues and assistance turned off its a frenetic yet disciplined experience that I thoroughly recommend. FYI I recently brought the Cliffs of Dover Blitz version too. Its come on in leaps and bounds over the last couple of years and is currently being upgraded for vr. For that title I really dont understand the reviews as Ive had zero issues with control binds and the campaign is almost as good. Some poor narrative and lack of a 'logbook' in Cliffs of Dover lets it down but thats my only complaint.
Anyway- as regards Battle of Stalingrad- big thumbs up. The ai is totally believable, and you will not find any predictable flightpaths in a dogfight with them. You will absolutely have to play to your airframes strengths or you will be shredded. A very nice product indeed that will see many many hours of use by me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI try to make a short review, explaning how the game is currently, and who should strike, especially when it's on sale.

1. Should you get it?

1. In my humble opinon, if you are the type of person interested in aircraft, strategy, navigation, cooperating with others, eager to learn more, and don't easily frustate about things, always seeking to improve yourself and accept that if a bad outcome happens, you most likely did something wrong, and you don't mind flying without seeing much action, if any at all then yes! definitely get it. Especially when it's on sale, you get a lot of value for your bucks. Have a Joystick ready, and you don't necessarily need a Trackir or VR, if you have a comfortable HAT for your POV.

2. The Game in general
2. As seen, the Game released in 2014, and overtime the game grew, content via Battle of .... got released, with new plane sets and collector planes, as well as general game updates and quality of life. I have been playing only since this year (2021), so I cannot talk about the past. Yet it is very obvious, that if you get one part of the game, see how much is available, you feel like your are missing out, or that everything is locked behind a paywall. It is a common for games that build onto itself, so when someone later in the games life joins, the person feels like they missed a great part, or are missing parts, feel like it is cause they only have that one module of the game. So if you get the main game, don't be discouraged, fly and learn the plane you are interested in, see if you enjoy the game, and then decide on how you want to move on. There is always great information to be taken out from various Youtube creators, so before deciding, gain as much information as you need, and then make a judgement based upon your thoughts. The Grapics are great too and holding up well, especially with the reworks of every plane skin to 4k which is also close to completion.

3. Online Community
3. Even tho with the games age, the online part is still active. At prime time you can see the servers filled with 84 players, depending on how many they allow, and what kind of servers these are. I think the dynamic campaign servers are the most popular, and after losing my training wheels in singleplayer, I moved to multiplayer as well. It is sort of a community, where you get into, and suddenly you see the same people over time again. It's like back in the past when everyone played on their favorite servers of whatever game, and you met the same names, and it makes everything feel more friendlier and more human like, less as Names you just read, forget and never meet again. Servers also got Discords, you could connect to, and talk to people on your side, or use a program called SRS. There are also groups that formed, and you might see them randomly in game flying a 3-5 ship wing. But you gotta remember, it's an online community, and even here you will meet people being salty and whining about how plane x is that way, and plane y is much better but plane y still lost. It's a tight community, and I like it a lot. Streaming of the game isn't that active, but at times you can catch some, and if you do, don't mind to ask how they feel about the game, or in general about things, they'll always be happy to help and appreciate.

4. The Ground Combat of this with ''Clash at Prokhorovka''
4. Actually, I am on the verge of getting this DLC, but money is a bit tight for me personally right now. I cannot talk much about the Singleplayer part of this DLC, only multiplayer. Depending on the server, it may be that it is full tank combat, or combined arms. On the Server I play on a lot, which is the Finnish Virtual Pilot server (huge shoutout to the bois), Tanks can spawn, capture points, even attack objectives. Tho it is important to keep in mind, that only the Map Prokhorovka is made in mind for real tank combat and possibilities, the others are rather long when it comes to drive times, and the possibilites of routes you may have. So if you are not only an avid aircraft person, but also enjoy tanks, but don't know yet if you should get it, try to look up gameplay on Youtube, and make an informed choice, before rushing in.

5. Collector Planes
5. Collector planes are planes that are sort out of the ''norm''. Meaning fighters that joined at a certain period of the war or Planes that bring more utility than fighting capabilities, such as the Ju-52. From Multiplayer perspective, the fighter collector planes will always be worth, having something more ''unique''. But the price tag only really makes it worth, if you are really interested in these planes, otherwise the ''normal'' planes are more than enough, and rather just as interesting as the collector planes. The plane as of now that is standing out, is definitely the Ju-52, being able to run supplies and drop paratroopers at certain points. Depending what kind of server, and how thought it is, the Ju-52 can have purpose, or non at all. So be wary.

6. Final thoughts
6. This game offers nearly everything someone interested aircraft of the WW2 era, strategies, tactic, how to manage your plane and fly to it's best ability could ask for. I have to mention that the Career of the ''Singleplayer'' needs to have online connection, cause the missions of that career are generated by a server at 1C Game Studios. However, there is a great offline career mode, made by Pat Wilson. There are various sources if you google an offline Campaign Generators, not to mentioned the Scripted Campaigns people are creating! Not only that, but you can also create your own skins too, and have custom skins on your plane or tank. I try to keep myself short, but google will always be your friend in such matters. What I feel tho is missing from the Game, are bigger bombers, something akin to the He-111, for example a B-25 in the Rheinland Module. Currently, you have attackers like the Il-2, BF110, and Dive Bombers like the Pe-2, Ju-88, Ju-87 and A-20, but only one ''real'' Bomber and that's the He-111, which saddens me greatly. I wish for more variety in attackers/bombers. But besides all that, the Game brings a lot and I am thankful for the sales and for still working on this game after all those years and expanding upon it.

I hope this review is able to provide a non-biased, neutral and explained wall of text for you to make an informed purchase, to decide if the game is fitting to your liking, or not. As I mentioned, I only got the game and the modules only on sale, and I am a huge fan when it comes to aircraft, and WW2, as I read diaries and books about these times! So everything aligns with my interests and I have something where I can lose myself in. I hope it also aligns with your interests, and you won't feel regret after getting it and playing for the first time.

I apologize for any grammar mistakes/incorrect sentence structure. I wrote it from the top of the dome, after an all nighter.

Good hunt, and always fly safe.\t\t\t\t\t

Yeah, you need a HOTAS but it's such a good experience in my Samsung Odyessy, that I purchased the Valve Index to take it to the next level. Because of this BASE/DLC connection, you will see planes and campaigns as locked if you have not purchased them, but you can still see them in multiplayer. It's all connected and it's AMAZING.

Do not do what I did, which was purchased this game in 2018 only to sleep on it until the '21 Summer Sale. If you enjoy the fundamentals of air combat do yourself a favor and get this game. The dogfights are intense and the sense of flight is incomparable.\t\t\t\t\t
