\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWay too complicated with no tutorials at all, also, no practicality taken into account whatsoever while making this 'button simulator'.

This game absolutely chucks you into the deep end with about 1000 key binds that feel like somebody just mashed their face on the keyboard because they are all spread out with no coherent order or memorability. The worst part is, in this state it expects you to have fun, when in reality it takes a good test of patience to start working the "simple" aspects like turning on a plane and taking off.
I don't understand why a simulator this complicated and overthought doesn't have a tutorial or ANYTHING, it seems like a no-brainer.

I may seem harsh and I understand that this is a 'realistic flight & tank sim' thus, over-complication is somewhat the selling point of the game. I just feel the 2 major issues i have, while they can be overcome, stand as MASSIVE and pointless hurdles in the beginners path - the keybinds are seriously forgettable and disorientating while there isn't even a tutorial to help with that matter, OR OTHER MATTERS - like learning about the gamemodes or what the hell to do in multiplayer.

P.S. The keybinds wouldn't matter if there was a good way of learning how to use all of them, but there isn't, so the keybinds become problematic as a result.
If I bought this at full price I don't know what I'd do with myself. Good thing I bought this at $10 (which is arguably too much for this headache lol.)

(EDIT) Forgot to mention, THE ENTIRE SCRIPTED CAMPAIGN IS SOLD SEPARATELY. Considering a scripted campaign is likely one of the best environments apart from an actual damned tutorial to learn the game, this only lessens my would be rating further.

If you are thinking of getting this game, get War Thunder first because it's free, and you don't have to kick yourself over wasting your money like I am, it might not be as complicated as Sturmovik but.......... actually... you'll thank me for that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this game because I had fond memories of playing IL-2 Sturmovik on the Playstation 3. I had hoped that this game would have catered as much to the controller crowd as the ps3 precursor obviously had to... but this game really isn't controller friendly. You can map any functionality you find important, but the game doesn't recognize the triggers as buttons, you can only map them as an axis (so no using the triggers to fire the guns). You CAN map throttle up/throttle down to the triggers, but they act as an axis, so every time you let off the right trigger, the throttle drops to 50%, every time you let off the left trigger, the throttle rises to 50% (because it's treating L2/R2 as a stick that has been let go to spring center).

Additionally, I wasn't able to find any sensitivity or dead-zone options, and in my experience it seemed like it had somehow set a dead zone at the outsides of the stick extremities instead of in the middle. So just ever so gently pushing the roll axis left would roll the plane over way faster than the movement in the stick warranted, but pushing the stick about halfway to one side was as much turn as you were going to get (as if the outside 50% of the stick was dead zoned). It really limited what you could do with the controller because if you put your rudder on the right stick horizontal axis, you pretty much can't put anything on the right stick vertical axis because it's pretty much impossible to perfectly accurately stay horizontal-only when trying to use the rudder (in my game I though it would be good to have the right stick's vertical axis control head swivel up and down, but any time i tried to use the rudder it was making me look straight down at the stick or straight up out the top of the canopy due to no dead zone).

After playing for only about 30 minutes today, my thumbs were fatigued from trying to hold the plane steady without over correcting, and my trigger finger was tired from having to hold the trigger at about 20% pulled to keep the throttle at around 80%. I wish they had just committed to their intention not to support the controller and had just prevented its use altogether and not put any kind of controller support indicator on their store page. If you are thinking about buying this game and using a controller instead of a hotas... don't bother.\t\t\t\t\t
