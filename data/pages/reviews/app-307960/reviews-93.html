\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnly put 3 hours into it but i like it so far. It feels like the most realistic ww2 combat flight sim out there. I have played all kinds of sims from early on so i have reference.
I had only done 10 mins or so of this one before i realised i had to get either VR or headtracking. In the end i bought a trackir 5.
Now i racked up a few kills against the AI, but i sure feel troubled with spotting the planes. Apparently more people feel about this, but im not sure what to say myself other than me finding it tricky.
Maybe this is realistic?
I play this without icons and all and ID'ing the plane is really important. With these graphics it actually feels not boring to do so.
I sincerely like the immersion of the entire sim right now.

Maybe this review will change in time, i don't know.

Pros:
Great graphics and sound.
Seemingly good flight physics.
Love how they "humanized" the pilot. Huffs and puffs he has to do to stay conscious, somehow it all feels acceptably real.

Cons:
I didn't get the menu structure sometimes, when doing career.


Edit 20 july 2020.
I still struggle with spotting planes so the search for an improvement continues.
My best tactic is still to fly lower than them, making their silouettes more clear.

Bought bodenplatte plus p-38 and d-9.
Because i hadn't played for months, starting with this mod was quite interesting. Normally i would have clear reference, now it was vague.
The d-9 so far is my favo from the late war range of planes.
An hour of playing and i knew again what il-2 is, the best ww2 combat flight sim out there.\t\t\t\t\t
