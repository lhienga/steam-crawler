\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI liked JC3 a lot. It was a very well made game and a ton of fun to play. It wasn't perfect, but it's drawbacks were small. I picked up JC4 on sale because I had just finished JC3 and wanted to see if all the negative reviewers were really accurate about how horrible it was.

It's really not that bad. In fact, I've truly enjoyed it for almost 30 hours at this point and I would recommend it. The mechanic that I've seen that gets the most hate in reviews hasn't been an issue for me at all. The new liberation mechanic requires you to take over the main military base/factory in the region and then use your available squads to advance the front lines to take over said region. The way you get those squads is by causing chaos objects to fill your chaos bar. Some have found that to be a tedious task, but I don't feel like I had to grind to fill this chaos bar at all. I have taken some time to destroy chaos objects after taking over a region or while assaulting a region's base/factory, but I haven't felt like I need to go out of my way just to fill the chaos bar.

I will say that you can choose where you spend your available squads and it will definitely help you to be strategic about that. Some regions will require more squads until you take over more adjacent regions. I've seen some drop as low as 0 squads. I've also noticed that, as I progressed past about 50% of the regions taken over, that sometimes when taking a region, I would actually gain squads, rather than lose them. I don't know if this is something that was added in a later patch to make it less grindy (in response to the criticism), but it seemed to help things flow more smoothly and overall I've enjoyed playing through this game.

If it is on sale, I would highly recommend picking it up along with all the DLC. (You'll want to equip the Skystriker for getting around quickly.) I got it all for under $15 altogether.\t\t\t\t\t
