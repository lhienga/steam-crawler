\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJC4 is an extremely fun game. BUT, it is pretty janky.

I feel like they forgot how to program physics properly after JC3, and they wanted to nerf the wing suit from one of two awesome JC3 DLCs as much as possible. I don't know what they did to screw up a Just Cause game with the size of the location we're in this time when they made JC3 which had a much larger map.

In terms of Story, Characters, Gunplay, Vehicles, and Concept... they did a good job on this one. What they got wrong was Physics, and the Control Responsiveness. JC3 had the better of both.

They added in new tether specific gadgets, which is fun.

For those who've played JC3 with the DLC, let me lower your expectations. The wingsuit no longer has awesome homing missiles, and now shoots 4 missiles that go in a single selected direction at once. There is no machine gun, and there is no dodging SAMs (though they eliminated the need to dodge SAMs). There is only the Brawler Mech, it can only punch, dash, jump, grab, and make cute beeping "honk" noises, and the gravity grab is very unresponsive. There is no orbital lightning laser gun, just a gun that shoots a short and seemingly ineffective beam of electricity (with a grenade launcher that basically creates a small temporary storm to strike an area with a smattering of lightning).

What I can say that I love about this game is the new tether gadgets that add new features that all have a set of effects you can use to change how one of three "Tether loadouts" behaves.

My biggest complaint about this game is that the game itself is seemingly free but you HAVE to buy it with DLC and you don't even get to select each individual DLC you want... you just have to get the one that has the most of what you want, and that'd probably be the complete edition. Enemies now immediately alert all others and their headquarters without the need for one to call it in with their radio, which frustrates me and kills the immersion. They also added a new hoverboard that goes across water and has magnetic rails you can ride on in only a few very specific locations... and has no other use than what the game teaches you to do with it. If you run into something, it'll lose all momentum. If you have too much momentum and take a sharp turn, you'll ride it off the intended path. You can't do tricks with it, you can't upgrade it, and you HAVE to propel it with some form of momentum or your grapple hook. It is single-handedly the worst item they could've added in my opinion.

To the devs, in the words of a man talking to a missile while riding it... F&%K YOU!!\t\t\t\t\t
