\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you have played any of the previous titles, then there won't be much surprise to this. There are more weapons and tools available this time around. The map is large and varied, which at times becomes a pleasure to traverse. This game is heavily about mobility with the grappling hook, and chaos. With no pun to be intended, this game revolves around chaos which can be both good and bad. It will keep encounters fresh with lots of explosions to watch.

The downside to this is, this can also turn against you. If you aren't aware of your environment, pieces of rubble or explosive devices will collide and be your demise. This is offset from a frequent and forgiving checkpoint system. In addition, guns don't really have much variation in purpose, so there are no upgrades or things to seek to optimize weaponry. Just pick up a gun and shoot.

Vehicles come in a wide variety, but unless equipped with weapons they are a sideshow. Controls are not great in vehicles but manageable.

Story is nothing incredible, but keeps the pacing and flow going. It would be noted that the DLC is good if you are seeking an increase in tools (hoverboard, crossbow, vehicles etc). None of the DLCs was incredible by any means, but served their purposes. The demon DLC was more annoying if anything, but the crossbow it comes with is a powerful weapon. It is important to note these DLC are available from the start, so you can use in conjunction with main campaign.

Graphically it runs well, and looks good for the amount that is happening on screen.

Overall I would recommend this if you enjoy the prior titles or enjoy open world chaos. If you are new to the franchise this would be a recommended buy on sale including any dlc that may be speculated.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust Cause 4 is certainly a contender for the worst game I've ever played, especially given how much I love the previous two games.

It's essentially a downgrade from Just Cause 3 in almost every way, despite being mostly made with reused assets - first of all, it reneges on the destruction based gameplay, instead opting to structure the game around poorly designed and eye-wateringly repetitive missions. Destruction is still there, but there's no incentive to engage with it beyond just wanting to blow stuff up for fun. The range of weapons and their unique abilities are nice, but combat is ridiculous thanks to the disparity between enemies, with some being paper-thin annoyances, and others incredibly spongey and overpowered. The controls are finicky and unresponsive, especially when it comes to mucking around with the grappling hook and all of its novelty options. Even when story was never much the focus of the previous games, the writing really takes a dive here, to the point where the plot is pretty much non-existent beyond "Rico shows up and is now in charge of a rebel army", and dialogue is painfully bad too. Even on its highest settings the game looks absolutely awful, undoubtedly worse than 3 and possibly even 2 in a few areas, and cutscenes are all rendered on a lower setting as well as being locked at a low frame rate. The DLC packs exacerbate some of the game's worst aspects in the driving, combat and writing respectively, but at least Danger Rising tries to resuscitate the destruction based progression at the last hurdle. There's a couple more saving graces to be found, for one the open world is large, diverse and well-populated with a variety of locations, even if many of them feel reused from one another, and amazingly I ran into very little in the way of bugs and glitches, but overall the game is just a plain old mess that feels like it was made without an ounce of passion.

3/10\t\t\t\t\t
