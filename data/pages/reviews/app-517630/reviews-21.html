\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is honestly a mess and a nightmare to play.

Same missions over and over (destroy the generators, flip the switch, hack the terminal, raise the transmitter, and then the dreaded defend said transmitter). Rinse, repeat ad infinitum.

Horrible UI and controls in general.

Driving makes you go mad, and with the amount of driving in this game, good luck. The camera angle sucks, and no way to change it. While driving you will get stuck on every little piece of debris on the road and will constantly spin out of control, and crash.

Terrible missions that are borderline impossible. Defend X mission objective - good luck without some of the DLC with overpowered weapons and vehicles. Waves of enemy tanks spawning from nowhere that will blow your defense object to hell before you even know where the missile came from.

HORRID performance. The game will stutter at times, will go from 160 fps to 20, will microstutter, and some of the "fixes" are hilarious. One common fix is to unplug your ethernet cable to fix stuttering. Even blocking all online components of this game doesn't fix the stuttering / lag, but does help a tad.

The frontlines part of the game aren't bad, just badly implemented. Just Cause 2 and 3 had it down where you just blow ♥♥♥♥ up, in this one, it doesn't matter. You don't "create Chaos" like in the previous installments, instead you fly into a base, flip some breakers, kill endless waves of enemies, die from a single sniper/rocket shot from nowhere, reload the save, and try again.

I love Just Cause for it's mindless braindead fun, but this game is just braindead unfun. Play Just Cause 2 or even 3 (which has it's own issues). This game looks great, when it's running at full performance with no hiccups, which never happens.

If you want this game, buy the complete edition for like $3, and you'll get a weekend of half-fun, half insufferable pain.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery disappointing game.
I played through the entirety of JC3 with the DLC and found myself extremely satisfied with the game's polish, performance and experience.
Enter Just Cause 4
This game taught me a brutal lesson about what happens when you don't read the Steam reviews.
Performance
Anywhere that had any sort of action immediately capped the GPU usage on my 4070 despite changing all of the settings to the lowest option possible.
Left the game alone for a month and tried it again after upgrading the RAM capacity... ZERO improvement.
Experience
All of the vehicle controls (specifically the helicopters and tanks) felt like a clown show at best.
There was no reward for blowing things up and causing actual chaos
The deployable drones were useless in combat
The weapons felt half-baked... except for the weapon ability feature; that was pretty cool

What was the lesson learned?
Immediately ask for a refund the moment you detect any faults in a game\t\t\t\t\t
