\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 6 years this game still feels unfinished.

Trash Graphics:

Bad performance,
TAA is trash - it gives ghosting. Instead we have to use FXAA which sucks,
Motion blur makes everything look like a blurry mess. It also gives ghosting.
There are also stuff like the depth of field and edge fade etc which only makes the game look even worse.

Story:

I played through the game once when it released and the story was pretty bad. nothing really happened.
It feels all the budget went into creating the map and making the weather.

Environment:

There is the desert with mountains, plain terrain, snowy mountains, and a rain forest.
It gives a big variety but feels too unrealistic. The Black Hand can control the weather but it feels impossible to switch from a warm desert to snowy mountains in 1 km
I would say 80% of the population are in tiny villages across the entire map. There are only 2 "big" cities and maybe 10 large villages. Most are in the plain terrain and a few in the rain forest.
The problem is when you go to explore them closely. You will find props that are floating or inside of other objects. Even both sometimes.

Also a lot of things are destructible but it feels like they are not taken advantage of. They are just there and can be destroyed but give no real purpose. It could have been used in missions like "destroy the powerlines" or "damage the power supply" - instead of blowing them up.
So I feel like the game could have been much better if they planned more and had more time.

Anyways, sucks to be honest.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel the same about this game as I felt about Forza Horizon 5 when it came out. It's a good game on paper.

I have beat this game twice and neither time did I truly have fun (during the story). Even ignoring the texture/graphics quality issues that I really just cannot get over, it's just boring. All the missions are the same, with the beginning, Zona Uno, Dos, Tres, and the final mission being the only 5 memorable missions in the entire game. Everything that the game requires you to do in order to complete it just feels like a massive chore that you need to get through. I had much more fun ignoring the story and just messing around in the world.

As for the story, it isn't that great. I wasn't blown away by the story, and it just feels as if it was made on a single afternoon. I get that the just cause series isn't huge on a story narrative but even JC3 had a better story than this game.

They completely went backwards on the quality of the game and its environment. I had been excited to try it out when it came out because I loved JC3, only for it to sit in my library for 3 years before I finally decided to beat it. I beat it again recently in order to see if it was really as bad as I remember, and it was, to an extent. Do not buy this game if you anticipate it being on the same level as the last 2 games, because it's not. It's only fun when you blow things up, that's it.

The game is fine as a sandbox shooter. It's not fine as a sequel to Just Cause 2 and 3. I hope that the next game, if there ever is one, is of a higher quality than this one.\t\t\t\t\t
