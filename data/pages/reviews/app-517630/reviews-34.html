\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis game is a pretty big step backwards from the previous game. in just cause 3 you would liberate cities and bases individually, each one presenting a new challenge and a new reward. the story was great, filled with well written lovable characters and great missions. the bases were all unique, featuring crazy new things. there are bases in huge caves, bases on huge mountains, there was one base in a large crater with rivers running around it. i'm not looking these up, i'm telling them from experience because of how memorable they were to liberate. i cant remember any of the JC4 bases in the same way as JC3, mainly because the liberation system is terrible. you do one lame mission, of which there are only a few different types, and take over one or more entire regions. for whatever reason you decided to remove the pistols, even though they were one of my favourite weapons. the story is easy to forget, and the characters have little personality.
JC3 had:
Mario, the charming guy with the cool shotgun
Sheldon, the selfish, Texas guy with a "corporate" personality.
Dima, the awkward scientist with a good backstory
JC4 had:
girl whose name i actually don't remember, i want to say Myra?
her sister or cousin or something, i don't remember. she runs the black hand.
the hacker. she actually did have a great personality, unlike most characters in the story. i'm pretty sure her name started with an L or a Z. i think it might be Liza.

aside from removing some of the better guns, including pistols, just cause 4 mainly focuses on sandbox features, with the story and liberation being a secondary and mostly optional thing.
while i do admit i love the sandbox abilities and the added features, i am seriously disappointed with the story and the game design. i can easily beat the story in under a day, and be bored most of the time.
JC3 also had cool easter eggs like the rubber ducky jet ski, or being able to go the hidden "boom island" if you travel far enough.\t\t\t\t\t
