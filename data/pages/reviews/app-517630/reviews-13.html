\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI typed up a long review covering my various criticisms and annoyances cause by JC4 but steam helpfully deleted it before I could post it after I clicked away to check my hours in JC3.

In short. It's not just "JC3 but slightly worse and only like $5 on sale, so still worth playing" which is what I thought going into it. It's annoying and frustrating in ways that JC3 just wasn't and overall feels like a massive step backwards.

The combat gets samey quickly, you can't so much as fire off a shot without having an infinite and oppressive army sent for you which gets old quickly. They've added so many bells and whistles that it becomes annoying to navigate and everything seems to not work as well as it did in 3 (please get rid of these stupid balloons and grapple thrusters and just give me back my old grapple and booster system). I appreciate the jet powered wing suit isn't locked behind DLC but it's a less manoeuvrable shell of its former self. I also appreciate the rebel drop system not having the same degree of annoying cool down + beacons (before you max out everything) but other than that it's just a downgrade from 3. While the map is cool, and feels like they fully filled it out vs leaving the top 1/3rd more or less unfinished like in JC3, the whole game looks on par if not worse than the previous one, especially the characters weird beard hair which kinda looks like they wiped a hedgehog across their face rather than facial hair.

Overall, definitely feels like after putting their A team on JC3, they passed their golden goose over to the B team, possibly even C team based on the lack of play testing the devs obviously did.

If you haven't played JC3, go play it + the DLC instead. If you have played JC3 and just want more, give it a hard think and only buy it if it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[general thoughts]
1- It's hilarious to think that 2 corporations (the developer and the publisher, that's clearly at least 2 corporations) both looked at this product and thought 'Yep, it's good, let's ship it' lol
2- Others would have bought Ubisoft's 'Steep', instead I bought this. I hope this clarifies my state of mind. Don't care about combat, only the fun flying stuff.

[the good]
- it's a funny game. Funny in the sense that this game manages to be accidentally cringe.
- the flying is nice.
- the game can be played in offline mode.
- nothing else seems to be good about this game.

[the bad]
- ugly visuals unless you're flying.
- the 'feel' of it makes me think of Fortnite.
- voice actors are bad - because the dialogues are bad. Can't really blame the voice actors if they don't have something good to work with.
- stupid scenario nobody cares about (fortunately you can skip cutscenes by pressing backspace).
- complex control layout for no clear reason.
- gosh this game is stupid on top of being ugly (but yes, it looks nice from the air)

[verdict]
I'll enjoy this game in my own way, but I can't recommend this mess of a product.

And no I haven't played the previous ones, I suspect they're just as cringe.\t\t\t\t\t
