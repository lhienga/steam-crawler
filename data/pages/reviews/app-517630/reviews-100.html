My disappointment is immeasurable
Rico Rodriguez is back, the man that is too cool to thank his army when giving him a ride in a helicopter or send him a drop off.

So, you remember how fun was JC3? Destroying stuff to liberate zones and such? It has the perfect recipe for a fun game where you could pour dozens of hours in and you'd never get bored.
Well, that's changed in JC4, and it was pretty shocking and disappointing.

You see, the side missions/chores were the best part of JC3, they added to the game a lot of extra fun and everyone loved that (or at least, almost everyone)

In Just Cause 4, you don't really do that anymore, i guess Rico figured he's too old for this ?, to take control of a zone you do some boring mission and that's it, if you want full control or whatever, although, it doesn't really serve a purpose, you need to jump through some circles in the air using the wingsuit. It's as stupid as it sounds.
They also removed grenades, you only have certain guns with an alternate attack that has some extra boom, but well, that's stupid too.

The destructible environment is still there, but it's pointless, it doesn't serve any purpose, and what's even more stupid is that these explosible environment elements respawn, sometimes when you're still there. If this was something you enjoyed in JC3, well... you will feel like a hamster on a wheel.

The story is pretty 'meh', full of cliches, but i don't think many people would care that much about it in such a game (I for certain, don't, not much anyway)

Bugs? Yea, it has 'em. For example, it happened multiple times that i was in bum nowhere, calling for a heli via loot drop, and as soon as i got inside, some enemy tank or another heli popped right next to me out of nowhere.
Most of my play runs in this game had an annoying bug where the game just froze completely and i had to alt-tab for it to work again. It's odd, i would've expected it to be some RAM error, but i am not encountering any issues in other games.
It also has mini-freezes due to the online features (you know, these little -measuring contests between you and your friends such as who had the biggest killing spree or drove for a longer distance), on the Steam forums i saw people talking about disabling it, good to know there's the possibility, but personally i enjoy seeing how much i suck compared to my friends.

The game is not all that bad, but i figured i would only focus on the bad aspects during this review.
I also don't want to set the review to 'Negative' because it would feel kinda -ish on my behalf, the game was playable and it was mediocre (at best), but it wasn't completely broken, and it wasn't a total failure for me to mess up the review score, although, i do wish Steam had a better review system, maybe with stars or something (JC4 would deserve maybe 6/10).


Bottom line:
If you don't care about continuity or whatever, and had to choose between JC3 and JC4, just go for JC3.
If you get this, manage your expectations. If you do, you might not be disappointed, you might enjoy it more than i did.

My purely personal experience/opinion is that it's a mediocre game, gets boring halfway through. Sure it's a good way to spend some time relaxing, but it's a waste of potential, it could've been much better if the devs would've stuck with what already worked.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit 19/01/22: After having spent more time playing this, I've come to the conclusion that JC4 truly has the most pointless open world environment to date. It is just a super boring bland game.
There is NOTHING to it at all, it feels and plays like a concept. Challenges are a joke, driving and flying is somehow WORSE than previous entries, missions are even more shallow than before...

And what's sad is that it's an absolutely redeemable game. It's really not far from the glory of the previous ones. The map is huge and varied, some of the mechanics are tight, like the grapple. But there's really nothing to keep you there.

Original review:
By far the weakest link of the JC series.

IMO, these games peaked with JC2. Blowing up never felt so great, and the progression felt natural.
JC3 was really fun and improved on the mechanics with the wingsuit, which was INSANELY fun. But JC2 holds a special place in my heart. JC1 was just a great game for its time, but certainly doesn't hold up to today's standards.

JC4 however, despite looking relatively good (vehicles popping out of nowhere in the distance and some textures aside) and having vastly improved grappling and traversal mechanics, is just a major step down in major aspects.
It feels like more of the same JC, but just isn't... The previous entries were far from being masterpieces, but they did progression and epic mayhem very well. You can NEVER go wrong with having a complete checklist of stuff you've yet to blow up, like in JC2.

None of that is in JC4. You can still blow things up, but it's just... Meh. It doesn't achieve much, it's not really rewarding, and so all you're left with is the plot. But the plot was never these games' strong suit.

One last major gripe I have with this game is the UI/UX. The menus are honestly pretty bad. Calling for vehicle/gun drops is tedious as hell. Not to mention configuring your grapple... Why can't it be as simple as say JC2 or 3 for that matter, where you could order anything you wanted in a few clicks. Here, you have to assign a supply to one of your many available pilots from the menus, and then call. It's not straightforward, and the terrible menu navigation make it just so bad...

It's really too bad because the mechanics are OK, the possibilities are somewhat expanded, but it's just such a chore... There's almost nothing sucking you in.

All in all, get this at a discount if you insist, and do yourself a favor by playing the earlier titles.\t\t\t\t\t
