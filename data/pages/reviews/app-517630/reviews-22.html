\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think Just Cause 4 is just fine. I played it over 50 hours, which is rare for me, and even got every achievement. As someone who loved Just Cause 3, JC4 is a good followup with some of its' own issues. It removes some things I liked about JC3, and adds other things that I think are good. Overall, I see a lot of people comparing both games, and while that's fair and JC3 is definitely better, this is still a great followup if you want to explore, make stuff explode and follow a dumb (and at times kinda funny) story.

I did prefer the progression system of the previous game; here, you expand your frontline across the island of Solis by finishing missions in specific outposts and causing destruction to recruit more squads. It feels a lot less "direct" than in JC3, which makes it less satisfying as a result - you have to navigate to a menu, then choose a region, then expand into it - but it still has the same general effect. Destructible objects respawn after leaving and coming back later, so you can destroy them over and over - good for farming, bad for the feeling of destroying the infrastructure of an island step by step. Progressing in JC4 feels a lot more floaty and disconnected as a result, but you still do the same things you always did: blow up things and be happy about it.

There's tons of weapons, locations, stunts (a few too many to be honest) and vehicles. The driving system here is massively improved and it's actually fun to drive around! Exploring the island in your wingsuit, parachute and vehicles is as fun as ever, and coloring more and more of the island in your colors still ends up being pretty satisfying overall. It's more Just Cause, just with bad weather. Speaking of: the weather does not impact that much because it is pretty rare. There are sandstorms, blizzards, rainstorms and tornadoes, but they are all confined to rather small areas and once you finish the missions associated with them, you can turn them on and off at will. They never affected too much, but they were visually impressive at least.

The storyline is forgettable, but told tongue-in-cheek, which I appreciate. Rico is Rico, there are some familiar faces and it's all around a good time. Missions could use some more variety, as you always flip breakers, blow up generators or turbines, or hack and defend consoles, but whatever. More excuses to shoot things and blow things up. Solis as an island is pretty generic; you got desert, rainforest, lowlands and snowy mountains. The game is visually impressive however, and there are some beautiful vistas.

Lastly, the DLCs are actually pretty good as well, with one exception. Danger Rising is fun and the Hoverboard is pretty cool; Dare Devils Of Destruction is awesome because the driving is very fun. Los Demonios sucks; the infestations are annoying, the flying enemies constantly pester you and destroying the specific parts in the infestations is a hassle. It seems like a DLC not made for a Just Cause game, but luckily it's short. Still worth getting though just for the complete package.

The game runs pretty good, with one exception: on my system, any weapon that creates a smoketrail or a smoke explosion caused micro stutters. Some people report explosions in general making their game lag, but that did not happen to me. It was annoying, but rare and not unplayable.

Long story short: JC4 gets a lot of flak, some deservedly, but I think it's still an all around good time. Movement is fluid and awesome, making things explode never gets old, there is a huge variety of weapons and vehicles and I appreciate the developers being willing to try out and swap around some things to see what works while sticking to the original tried-and-true formula. If you liked Just Cause 3 and want more of the gameplay, you can't really go wrong with JC4. And if you don't like some of the changes to progression, it won't take you two hours to realize that and a refund can be made. Vamanos!\t\t\t\t\t
