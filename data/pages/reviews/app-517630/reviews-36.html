\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompared to JC3
Pros:
- More unique vehicles and weapons
- Better grapple system, which although occasionally tedious, is an overall improvement
- Solis is a much more diverse and fun open world then Medici
- Easter Eggs are even cooler this time around
- The Hoverboard is sweet
- While the jetpack is weaker, the game is better off for it
- Radio is a great addition, makes driving and flying much more fun

Cons:
- Graphics took a serious dive, its not your computer its the game engine
- Story is much worse, and the villain is no where to be seen throughout the campaign. Di Ravello is infinitely better
- Not replayable, once you capture a base, you can't replay it like in JC3, same goes for advancing front lines
- The challenges are so much worse then JC3, massive downgrade

Overall, if your a fan of the franchise it's worth it but if your new try JC3 first.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy one thought is a confusing one to me; "What is this?"

Many people seem to complain about Just Cause 4 in relation to Just Cause 3, but I think that is just rose tinted glasses talking.

All of the Just Cause games have sickening amounts of pop in. Turn around and new enemies have, somehow, manifested from thin air. This is bad enough on foot, constantly having to mow down yet more cannon fodder from this "elite private army."

Take to the seas? Turn the camera and Bam! A new enemy naval squadron has somehow just deployed from that shallow inlet they couldn't possibly have come from. The air is even worse, since a constant stream of choppers, jets and drones will just fly at you.

Make no mistake, I like challenge, but I'm serious. It. Is. Constant. Game development like that just suggests that the maker couldn't be bothered with meaningless stuff, like challenging enemies, so they just lazily threw in the towel and set them to constant respawn. It's stone cold disgusting.

Another thing that bugged me was the enemy A.I., simultaneously hyper capable and suicidal. How do I mean? Well, suicidal in that those constant enemy air units will happily commit aeronautical seppuku right into your tail fin, killing the both of you. "It killed you, right? So it's a good challenge." I know someone will think that and they are stupid. A good challenge is something that I can actually counter, which I can't if the entire enemy air force is willing to kamikaze me.

Also, for hyper capable, every enemy sniper is ridiculously, superhumanly skilled. Forget that garbage you see in movies where people just rack up headshots as soon as the enemy comes in view, shooting is hard. Shooting a target zipping along in a wingsuit, while you are in a helicopter bobbing around, should be outright impossible, but I guess that shows what I know, since every shooter can slot one in your dome 9 times out of 10 in that situation, so I guess I just need to git gud.

Even after all of that, what made me ragequit and uninstall the game this go round was when I shot at an enemy tank, whereupon it flew into the air. As it was cartwheeling around at about 150 feet above me it, and this is real, instakilled my own tank with an immaculate shot. I already was, at best, only mildly amused with the game, but if the makers are just going to give the enemies aimbots, why should I play?

The missions, while I think were more varied than in Just Cause 3's one-two punch of either blow stuff up or drive someplace, were still rather dull. The story was bland. Yet another dictator needs to get taken down. Wow. Never would've seen that coming. The characters in the story were, while not grating or offensive, well...how do I put this...they were too "quirky and upbeat" for a gaggle of people who should've been terrorized and victimized for years by a tyrant and his private army.

If there is something good to say, the landscape was beautiful. That may not sound like much, but I liked the view of Solis whether I was driving, sailing, wingsuiting, etc. Looks like a neat place to visit when there isn't a war on. Speaking of war, I also liked the fact that there were outright warzones on the borders of regions controlled by the good guys and bad. That was a slick touch, even having the ground scorched and pockmarked with damage. Gorgeous, gorgeous, devastation.

Ultimately, though, visuals aren't enough. Absurd A.I. alongside dull missions and a bland story mean that, no. I can't recommend it to anyone.\t\t\t\t\t
