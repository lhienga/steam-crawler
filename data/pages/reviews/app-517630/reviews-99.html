\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely disappointing.

Thought there was gonna be a cool boss battle like the previous games in the series? Well, nope. In fact, the ending felt jank. The rest of the story was surprisingly interesting.

But, it doesn't help that this whole game feels bloated. In order to replace the previous game mechanics of liberating settlements by destroying specific infrastructure that has to do with propaganda or production, they generalized the chaos you built into a meter that'll give you "reinforcements" that you can spread on the map to capture regions. They decided to fill this void with 100s of stunts that get old after the 40th. The collectibles in Just Cause 3 were 1000% better in comparison.

The DLCs were kind of good, but the whole game feels like they added stuff just to pad it.

And by the way, the selling point of this game was you can control the weather...but you never do it in the story (technically). You just go to these tornadoes and try to deactivate them and then you can use them, but only if you go to there specific lab locations that they were in originally.

One more thing, there's a part where you send a tornado to a major city to contain with big wind cannons on top of skyscrapers FOR SOME REASON and then you're supposed to dive down through the eye of the tornado to turn off its core. It glitched out for me and I couldn't see the tornado being contained. It made that whole mission lame because I wanted to see the tornado in the middle of the city.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust Cause 4, although a decent game, isn't as great as Just Cause 2 or Just Cause 3.
The map is huge, campaign is decent and the environmental destruction is a blast.
However, with that said, I didn't enjoy the fact that you could destroy a base and come back within a day to find it was rebuilt by some workers on some sort of super-drug.
I also encountered a couple of minor bugs, which even though were indeed minor, isn't something I've come to expect from a Just Cause game.

After owning JC4 for well over a year, I haven't been drawn back to replay it. Unlike JC2 or JC3, which I've replayed countless times.
So with that said, a decent instalment, but it's only worthy of a purchase at a 50% discount.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you haven't played Just Cause 3, get that one instead

This game isn't exactly bad, but it's a clear step down from JC3. The Military has been given better pursuit abilities so it is even harder to lose them and easier to get them to hunt you. They didn't get more of a threat, just more of a nuisance. Also the minimap got removed, because orientation in such a big open world is obviously nothing important. The world got a bit more diverse, they added deserts, but overall it feels emptier. The supply-drop mechanic got "improved" as well, instead of just ordering a new loadout and a vehicle you now get to use 3 separate drops for that (3 because they also removed pistols, but guns got a secondary fire option now which is nice). And the gun cases are like buttered toast: they really like to land upside down, giving you a good opportunity to try out the new grappling hook when you painfully have to flip these things.
The new weather stuff is fun though, and overall the game is still good enough I can recommend to get it when it is on sale, but if you have to decide between this one and Just Cause 3, get 3 instead\t\t\t\t\t
