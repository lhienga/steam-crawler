\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just found this game... not fun... compared to Just Cause 3. I've barely spent a fraction of the time on it as I have in JC3 and I've already uninstalled and reinstalled twice. If you're reading this and ready to find my address to bash in my family's skulls with a pipe wrench in the dead of night, then consider hearing out my arguments below.

The additions to this game compared to the last are of minimal importance. New vehicles are kinda cool but redundant when the wingsuit is more versatile. New weapons are kinda cool but don't provide anything other than a greater extent of overkill. New storms are kinda cool but ultimately prove to just be eye candy (tornado) or an annoyance (all other weather). And new grapple abilities are kinda cool if you go out of your way to use them instead of your exponentially more destructive weapons. I would assume that all the new toys exist to allow the player greater freedom and creativity in choosing their method of destruction, but unless you deliberately limit your options, the desire for creativity quickly goes out the window in favor of your portable WMDs. If anything, more options lead to less creativity because you'll never be in a situation where you don't have the perfect tool for the job.

Meanwhile, the change to the core gameplay loop of systematically liberating the country was not a change that needed to be made IMO. Liberating every named location in JC3 got tedious toward the end, but no more tedious than completing one of a small handful of BASIC mission types: this game's way of liberating the map. Any by "basic", I mean stuff like wave defense; that's just pure laziness! On the other hand, even more laziness might have been a better choice because I think the game would be improved by just recycling the liberation mechanics from JC3. Blowing up every single ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ red thing on the map got mind-numbing, but at least it provided brief moments of badass-ery when swooping in to utterly annihilate a unsuspecting outpost in seconds without ever touching the ground or, on rarer occasions, the hyperactive mega-explosion ♥♥♥♥-fest of destroying a whole port single-handedly while every carbon based life form in a 500m radius tries to snipe you out of the sky. Maybe other people play differently, but in this game I just avoid tackling big bases because they are no longer mandatory and I can fill my chaos bar up a lot more efficiently by just flying around and hammering any small installations I see.

I could go on but I'd prefer to go do something other than feverishly tap out my feelings for a barely AAA game (despite how cathartic it was), so I'll leave you with this: If you wanted more Just Cause 3 but bigger and better then this might be the game for you. But it probably isn't.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJC4 seems to be less polished than JC3 to me but the massive swarm of pissed-off reviews is quite misleading I think. As for playing with a controller, the whole JC franchise is rock solid there. If I had to recommend a game for someone to switch from mouse-keyboard to controller (in many games it makes absolutely sense) among Witcher 3, Horizon Zero Dawn, newer Batman games and Tomb Raiders, Just Cause games would be included in my list. Yes, Just Cause 4, too.

In many aspects JC4 seems to be a step-up compared to JC3 but also some small things are sort of less and some others that were great in JC3, missing. Pros are the improved, more alive environment with an ample of new layers/details and variation, scenery overall, car handling, the engine runs smoother (at least on my system) and the difficulty. Cons are the difficulty again (tactics that work in JC3/JC2 will not work here, ammo etc) where changes may not apply to a lot of players, some odd bugs, strange pedestrian driver AI, strange enemy AI and mission direction that makes you lost a little of what to do around the beginning. JC3 provided a more solid build from the beginning to demonstrate all the features. Overall a more solid experience.

But does that make me think that all the pissed-off reviews reflect Just Cause 4? Not at all. People love bytching much more than playing, it reflects that only in my opinion. It is a quite capable game. If you are into GTA, Far Cry / Assassin's Creed or even Cyberpunk 2077, you will probably enjoy this one quite much, especially with DLCs included. Anyway, none of others have Rico the Batman Rambo Terminator. Just think about it.\t\t\t\t\t
