\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fun for maybe 20 minutes, but it's full of extremely frustrating issues.

- Some of the transmitters I'm supposed to protect are always stuck at zero (the transmission bar). It doesn't move at all.

- You have to pay extra if you want the jetpack, which you get early on in JC3. In this game, you never that jetpack if you buy the game. You have to buy a deluxe version.

- The water ... the waves a mile away move faster than the waves 50 feet away.

- Flammable vehicles - Half of the missions where you must escort someone somewhere in a vehicle, or drive a boat to a harbor and keep it there for a certain duration, or drive a boat off a waterfall... If you play the mission for 25 minutes, then your boat catches fire because some other boat drives into you (they drive into your boat, 10 out of 10 times, several boats at once). When this happens (and it ALWAYS happens), JC4 will ask "Retry/Quit." Click "Retry" and you START at your latest checkpoint WITH YOUR VEHICLE ON FIRE. Ten seconds later your vehicle explodes. "Mission Failed: Vehicle Destroyed." Clicking "Retry" is futile. You have to start the mission at the beginning and do the same boring part (it eventually becomes boring the 15th time you spend 25 minutes carefully driving your boat). Then it happens again. "Retry?" Why even ask? Don't start me at a checkpoint with this precious vehicle I need to protect, in flames.

- Seems like every 10 minutes I was googling what I need to do next. With Just Cause 3, I don't recall ever doing that. I was engrossed in Just Cause 3 and looked forward to liberating more towns and outposts. After playing Just Cause 4 for a couple hours, I never looked forward to playing Just Cause 4.

- The map is always zoomed in. You can never see the entire map. Why in the world would the designers do that? To make it feel bigger?

Just Cause 3 was fun. I loved it.

Just Cause 4 added stress and frustration to my life.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSadly, I'm only 4 hours in and I don't think it's worth the time to complete. JC2 and 3 had Rico as the lone hero Dictator Removal Specialist. Here he's a heavily armed Doordash driver, being sent on missions that rely on comms, while any non-mission activity doesn't count. So you never look at the map and think "hmm What shall I blow up now..." because there's no point. The enemy has infinite respawn/rebuild and you can only free the map through scripted missions.
Following on from breaking the open world nature, the cutscenes and story were less involving than the original Ghost Recon mission briefings. The map mechanics for progress are obtrusive and irritating, in a way that JC2 and 3 never were. Also why did he throw away the Bavarium powered wingsuit? Suit was cash.
I understand what the graphics are trying to do, having got Rage2, capture the humidity and sunlight/shade of the jungle and they do that extremely well up close, along with the trash and world-making detais, but rendering at distance gives the jungle a kind of impressionistic filter, while the unaliased text gives an unpolished feel. JC2 and 3 had great water and boat feel. JC4 boats and water brought back PS2 memories.
I can't even say that "I want to like it". I don't like it, it doesn't have the magic or humor of the previous 2.\t\t\t\t\t
