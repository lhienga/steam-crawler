Just Cause 4 - Unleashing the Chaos in a Bigger Playground

Graphics: ★★★★☆
Just Cause 4 continues the series tradition of delivering a visually impressive open-world playground. The South American-inspired setting introduces diverse landscapes, from lush rainforests to arid deserts. Improved weather effects, especially the dynamic and dramatic storms, add a layer of realism and spectacle. However, occasional graphical glitches and pop-in issues can momentarily disrupt the experience.

Gameplay: ★★★★☆
Building upon the foundation laid by its predecessor, Just Cause 4 refines the formula with enhanced grappling hook mechanics and an expanded set of tools for creative destruction. The addition of extreme weather events, including tornadoes and lightning storms, introduces new dimensions to chaos. While the core gameplay remains enjoyable, some may find that the incremental improvements don't revolutionize the experience.

Story: ★★★☆☆
Just Cause 4, like its predecessors, prioritizes action over a deep narrative. The plot serves as a backdrop for the chaos, and while it provides context for Rico Rodriguez's missions, it may not leave a lasting impression. The characters and storyline are serviceable, but the focus remains on the player's ability to unleash havoc across the vast landscapes.

Soundtrack: ★★★☆☆
The soundtrack complements the game's high-energy action with a mix of dynamic tunes. However, the music may not be particularly memorable outside of intense gameplay moments. The sound effects shine, especially during chaotic encounters and explosive setpieces, adding to the overall sensory experience.

Level Design: ★★★☆☆
Just Cause 4 features a massive and diverse world, showcasing different regions with unique challenges. The improved weather system contributes to dynamic gameplay scenarios, and the variety in landscapes keeps exploration interesting. However, the lack of significant improvements in settlement design and the occasional repetition in objectives may feel familiar to series veterans.

Replayability: ★★★★☆
The sandbox nature of Just Cause 4 offers ample replay value. Liberation of regions, completion of challenges, and experimenting with the extensive array of weapons and vehicles provide plenty of reasons to return. The addition of extreme weather events introduces new possibilities for creative chaos and adds to the game's longevity.

Overall: ★★★★☆
Just Cause 4 builds upon the series' chaotic foundation, offering a larger playground for destruction and mayhem. While it may not represent a revolutionary leap forward, the refinements in gameplay mechanics and the introduction of dynamic weather events keep the experience fresh and enjoyable. For fans of the series and those who relish open-world chaos, Just Cause 4 provides a thrilling, albeit familiar, adventure in explosive liberation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played through Just Cause 2, 3, and this one. I enjoy all of them. Just Cause 4 gets a worse rap than it deserves.

In some aspects it's better than 3. Better gunplay (in my opinion), more robust grapple/tether, better vehicle handling, more engaging open world, more interesting enemies, the weather while gimmick-ey is quite cool, the whole frontline mechanic is pretty cool if a bit underdeveloped.

However in other aspects, Just Cause 4 is worse. The actual acts of liberating areas is not as fun and much less destruction focused, some parts of the graphics (namely the water) looks worse than 3, there are less destructible objects in the environment, you don't have infinite remote mines anymore, you never find out what Mr. Snowman wants from Rico.

Overall even with its downsides, it is a fun sandbox game and is absolutely worth getting on sale. If you enjoyed the previous games this is still a fun one! And the Hoverboard you get from one of the DLCs is freaking awesome.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust Cause 4 is a fun game and I think you should play it. You may be turned away from the game due to community backlash over Just Cause 4, but I find most these criticisms uninformed, repetitive, and similar to the initial criticisms of Just Cause 3. Whilst comparing Just Cause 4 to its predecessor is valid, I find that if you look at Just Cause 4 away from the rest of the series, you can truly see the blossoms that this game has. There are some critiques that I see that are valid, such as the lack of a "sandbox" mode in Just Cause 4, like there was in Just Cause 3. One that I see seldom brought up is the story of Just Cause 4, specifically the conflict. Espinosa is the Antagonist of Just Cause 4, and in universe has been indirectly the antagonist of Just Cause and Just Cause 3, via his army and mercenaries, The Black Hand. For what is probably the second largest villain in the Just Cause world, he has near zero personality and barley any substance to his character beyond "I'm the big bad fear me" (and other tidbits excluded for spoilers). It makes it hard to care about the Just Cause 4 story due to this, and the previous game outshines Just Cause 4 in this regard. Di Ravello is a much better villain for the Just Cause series, due to his personality, repeated appearances and background if you go out of your way to find it. Espinosa is met 2-3 times and has barely any background, at least comparatively to Di Ravello. But thats my largest concern with the game and I know most people don't play the Just Cause series for the story. Regardless, I highly recommend this game to both newcomers and repeated players to the series. It seems a little weird at first, but trust me, it only gets better.

as a final though, Stop being so harsh on Just Cause 4 because it "isn't Just Cause 3". The same thing happened to Just Cause 3 when it "wasn't Just Cause 2".\t\t\t\t\t
