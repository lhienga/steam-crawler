Along time ago I 100% JC3 on Xbox and comparing that experience and gameplay to JC4 makes me quite dissapointed- JC3 never even had that amazing of a story it was quite simple but the characters had a lot of charm like Mario but comapred to JC4 it's so so much better, JC4's story feels blank and progressing through it feels sluggish especially paired with mediocre to sucky mission design. To stop comparing it to JC3, JC4 does improve in some aspects besides cut-scenes which are weirdly horribly rendered in JC4, JC4's combat is pretty fun like every Just Cause and the new grapple mechanics are quite enjoyable and better then JC3 even if they're buggy at times, the other aspect JC4 improves upon is graphics, the island this time is very enjoyable to play on with massive mountain's poking up everywhere and the beautiful jungle is also great paired with the weirdly majestic views. Moving on the gun play is better for the most part- besides the spongy-ass enemies which feel out of place, the vehicles are also better and driving feels smoother same with air vehicles. Lastly, the weather is also a new enviroment change which I honestly love a lot even if it's a bit random, nothing much to hate on about tornado's and such.

My main gripe is the story, missions, and quality like I've said before. If JC4 was delayed a couple months and the story was actually good than It'd be much better, just a lot of fun ideas somewhat-mediocrely executed. I'd give Just Cause 4 a 5/10, if there ever is a JC5 I hope it's amazing, I love this series.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like the Just Cause games because of their main gameplay loop (destroying everything until there's no more) then you'll be utterly disappointed, because while this game still has that, it's kinda been just moved to the side in place of (admittedly tedious and repetitive) missions that you have to do to complete the game and advance the story. You can still destroy things, it's just not the main objective anymore, which can make it feel less rewarding.

That being said, i still enjoy the game a lot simply because i love sandbox games. There's a ton of options other than destruction. I love the gun play, the vehicles, the weapons at my disposal, the movement, the tools to cause absolute madness, or even just do nothing and drive around or stand in a random place looking around (though all of these are my opinion).

I personally enjoyed it just as much, if not more than the previous games, but it's easy to see why people are generally not happy with this game. The missions you have to do to advance and complete the story are something i really dislike and make replaying the game not as enjoyable, on top of removing the main aspect of the franchise and making it essentially irrelevant, but i still managed to push through thanks to the otherwise enjoyable freeroam that the game provides.

To put it simply, this is a bad Just Cause game, but a good sandbox game. If you're in for a sandbox game to do crazy stuff in, then get it (preferably with all DLCs when it's on discount, it gives even more to mess around with). If you played JC2 and/or JC3 for the destruction-based gameplay, then don't bother getting the fourth one unless you want to continue Rico's story or something, because the gameplay loop is simply not the same.\t\t\t\t\t
