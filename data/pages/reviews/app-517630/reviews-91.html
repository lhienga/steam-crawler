\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBased on a lot of the reviews, I expected this game to be horrible. It wasn't. Is it as good as JC3? No. Not at all. If you want a better experience, just play that. The devs removed both the remote explosive and grenade features (although the grenades have been replaced with weapon "abilities", which I guess is somewhat clever), and most noticeably the liberation mechanic. They basically turned the map into a war strategy game, where you advance your front line across the map. It's a good mechanic, but it doesn't really belong in a Just Cause game IMO. The main game was also a lot shorter than JC3's. That game took me about 60 hours to complete, but this one took about 25. I won't spoil the story, but the end of the game sets up for a Just Cause 5, so I hope that the devs take the feedback from the community, as this series has the potential to be once again amazing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt really depends on what you are after, if you want the main focus of the game to be blowing stuff up, Just Cause 3 or 2 is the game for you. If you like to see character development and have more missions/tasks to do things, Just Cause 4 is for you.

Pros:
-Great Story-line, lots of fun missions.
-More missions than the previous games.
-More diversity on the map, free-roaming is more pleasant.
-The grapple hook and wingsuit can be modified with upgrades.
-Character development adds depth to the plot.

Cons:
-Poor performance after some time.
-Some bugs here and there.
-Not the easiest to mod.

Overall Review:
It's a great game, it's just really what you are looking for in a game that will determine your opinion on this compared to the previous games in the series.

Notes:
-I would install a performance boost mod.
-The DLCs are incredible, I really liked Danger Rising and Los Demonios\t\t\t\t\t
