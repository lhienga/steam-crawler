\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is not a better version of Just Cause 3. It's a different version. Some better, some worse. Not as good as I'd hoped, but worth its money when bought at a major discount. Definitely more modern than JC 3, even though they re-used a lot of models. Don't forget to manually set your graphics, by default it sets you to the lowest resolution it can think of. Some people forget to do that and complain about how bad everything looks. Go figure. Resolution goes nicely up to 2k though.

Ammo works different than it did in JC 3, just like it worked differently again in JC 2. At times, you'll run out. Learn to plan your fights around this, as supplies are plentiful and easy to get. You just need more of a hit-and-run tactic at times to make use of this. You don't have explosives, but it doesn't take long to unlock boosters that can explode. Basically the other way around from JC 3, where you had explosives that would later double as boosters. The modification system takes some getting used to, but overall adds some creativity to the game. 3 configurations is enough for most situations.

What's even more different, is how enemies spawn. Respawning is a thing now. Enemies showing up only after you've hit a trigger is a thing now. At release, this had many bugs. Now, years later, most of that is patched. This has benefits and drawbacks.

The story is tolerable, the side missions don't make enough sense to be interesting and there's quite a bit of grinding involved with completing villages. The map is massive, but they've improved fast travel so it's only a benefit and no problem at all. During missions the markers are often in the wrong spot, but at least you get a general direction. The AR eye takes some getting used to (using it to identify vehicles from a distance by pointing at it and hitting Tab is a great help for some of the objectives though), but is overall nicely done. There are way too many side-objectives and Garland is an idiot. Many of the objectives are easy, although unless you're a natural with it you'll find some of the wingsuiting ones to be needlessly tricky.

Don't buy this game for the story. It's a chaos game. You get shot at a lot and you blow things up.

This game is a bit sadistic at times, like how an objective is positioned and how short some of the timers are. Much of this can be fixed by trying and trying again and/or planning ahead. If you get stuck, there are YouTube videos. Sometimes the comments on those can be more enlightening than the video itself.

Don't buy this at full price, the DLC will break your wallet. Do you need those? Not really, although you really want the Sky Striker wingsuit. Playing without it is like playing this game on a harder difficulty setting. There are major discounts for those willing to wait. The bundles drop to 11-15 Euros during some of the sales. Only play the Los Demonios one if you like shoot-em-up zombie games.

Overall, it's good. Not great, but good. Just, not as good as anyone had hoped it would be. Here's to hoping JC 5 combines the best parts of 3 and 4 somehow.\t\t\t\t\t

Just Cause 4 is a mess. An oversized map, a confusing and convoluted story, terrible visuals and repeating quests lead to an experience will make you want to stop playing at any moment and play anything but this game. The story is confusing and tries to tie the events of past games together as if they were always intended to be some kind of interwoven masterpiece, when instead this game just feels like Just Cause 2 - just freshened up a bit and with a worse story.
The Story is actually incredibly short - play the tutorial, play 3 missions for 3 different 'zones', then a final big mission where everything finally comes together. That's 11 missions, and some are really short. To actually play these missions though you need territory, which is gained through completing area quests, which are extremely repetitive. The oversized map doesn't help here either as simply travelling between zones is tedious and long, and using any kind of vehicle other than aerial vehicles always ends in pain and suffering.
The Gameplay has been improved in some areas compared to its predecessors, but the actual game UI itself has had a huge downgrade. While movements with Rico now feel more fluid than ever, actually finding where you need to go is infuriating as the map doesn't zoom out enough, the interface is actually quite horrible to understand, and the drop system sucks.
Overall, Just Cause 4 is not enjoyable, Just Cause 3 is far superior. Its hard to believe this game came AFTER Just Cause 3, as all the building blocks for a great game that 3 set are present but just badly executed. The visuals of this game were clearly based on Just Cause 2, but even that was a far superior game. Do not buy this game unless you really want to, Just Cause 2/3 are better.
Just Cause 4 gets an 3/10.
