\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just finished JC4 and played it for 66 hours. If I had written a review after an hour or two I would have slammed it. Although I had read negative reviews so my expectations were low, my initial impression was total disappointment. Having enjoyed JC, JC2 and even JC3 (which I felt was a bit of a let-down compared to JC2) this one seemed all wrong. I think it was mostly because of the new method of taking ownership of territory using the chaos meter and of course the weird menu. But it also felt that the game was forcing me into it too fast and I was just fighting enemy forces instead of being in control of a new adventured.

Having paid over 10 for everything (which is a lot for someone who normally only buys games at 80% discount in the Steam sales) I had to persevere with the game so I took my Rico away from the main action and focused on the game mechanics and menu so that I could adapt to it, and then played some of the dlc "side missions" and challenges at my own pace. That made me feel back in control so that I could then progress the main missions. Gradually the game felt a whole lot better and I started to enjoy it.

As others have noted, some of the graphics aren't great and the mechanics aren't as good as they should be - grappling hook accuracy varies and some of the vehicles are almost undriveable. The game design is reasonable, although there is a bit too much repetition where it could have been avoided, and some of the challenges are badly thought out and a bit pointless. The extra features of the grappling hook seemed clever but I couldn't get into them. They felt over-complicated and not entirely intuitive. Often I had to edit the mode in order to get it to do what I wanted. That's probably why the designers had created profiles but I just wasn't that interested. I enjoyed the story and the addition of the 3 dlcs lengthened gameplay to more than 60 hours.

I think I disagree with many reviewers: In my opinion JC4 is in some ways better than JC3. However I think that JC and JC2 were more fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not bad, but its not great either. Coming off JC3 I was very disappointed with many of the changes they made here.

The BAD

1. No handheld explosives like in JC3 - instead you have to either engage with the new grapple systems or use the new alt fire modes found on most guns to get sort of real damage output against objects and vehicles.

2. the new grapple mechanics - imo the balloons are a gimmick which are good for a bit of messing around but not very helpful for actually clearing bases and the novelty wears off quckly. I miss the much stronger grapple from JC3 which could easily pull helicopters out of the sky. The default grapple here feels much weaker even when toggling it to its strongest pull.

3. enemies - once your alert meter goes up it can feel like a chore to get enemies off your back as they seem to pursue you for far longer and further than in JC3. they also seem to spawn more quickly as I rarely felt like I had actually cleared an area because new enemies would always pop up

4. activities - the flashy 5 star/gear system and reward screen in JC3 made every activity feel meaningful. In JC4 side activies like wingsuit courses feel hollow and not worth doing as there arent any great rewards and theres not even a screen popping up to say you did it in most cases.

5. bases - the goal is no longer about destroying red objects - you know, the straightforward objective of each base in JC3 that encouraged using the sandbox to wreak havoc? Red objects actually respawn now and the only point to destroying any of it is getting points towards the conquest mechanic which is token at best. This is already a huge disappointment but then each base now has an actual objective to do. This sounds cool on paper but the missions get recycled a lot and many of them now involve escorting NPCs or protecting an object - which I shouldn't have to explain is a very poor choice for a series about high mobility and explosions

6. vehicles - vehicles have ammo now which seems unnessecary. Some vehicles have enough ammo you'll never run out but others like the bomber jet seem to have enough for 1 or 2 runs before you may as well ditch the vehicle. Ground vehicles like tanks seem to be made out of paper now while air vehicles seem to be able to take more hits than in JC3 which is strange.

7. graphics - my PC is not very high spec anymore so take this with a grain of salt but I swear the graphics are worse here than in 3.

The GOOD

1. story - its hardly a work of art but the story is fine for the type of game this is. Its mostly held together by a few entertaining characters though Rico himself seems rather low energy for most of the game

2. story missions -credit where its due there are some cool moments here - the region finales involving the weather mechanics are the highlight with tornadoes, thunderstorms and sandstorms. Id even go so far as to say that the best missions here are a way bigger spectacle than any one mission in JC3.

3. map - personally I like Medici more but theres decent variety here with jungle, desert, and grassland regions

At the end of the day its a JC game and there is definitely some fun to be had here, especially if this is your first time with the series. For me though I felt that JC4 made unnecessary changes to the formula that took away what I liked so much about JC3, and it ends up being a very hollow experience where I finished the game and immediately went back to JC3 instead.
