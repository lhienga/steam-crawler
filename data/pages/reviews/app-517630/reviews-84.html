\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is evident just how much content is reused from JC2 and JC3 in JC4 - the vehicles are all but exactly the same as JC3, many of the visual assets used are clearly the same, character modelling is very similar - only changed slightly and the map is clearly very closely based on that of JC2, only updated and re-arranged for JC4. Even the menus and title screens are practically identical to that of JC3. All this does make for a bit of a let down in terms of overall enjoyment but the story is still pretty good and it has had a lot of new elements added to it since JC3. The map is also still rather unique and beautiful - these are probably the only reasons why I would recommend this game - story and the open-world.
It has many technical issues including glitchy physics, response times for keyboard/mouse presses are slow meaning many are either missed or delayed when you are in the action and in terms of compatibility with newer versions of Windows i.e. Windows 11... Well since this game hasn't been updated majorly in the last 2 years there are many issues especially with controller support. If you use a wired xbox controller then it is ok but forget using wireless, with constant disconnects every time you enter the map or menus, then it takes forever to reconnect.
It feels to me like JC3 was meant to be the last in the series but this opportunity of a money-grab was too tempting for the devs so here we are having something that is a re-skin of JC3!
Look, for new players to the series or those who wont mind this sort of stuff mentioned here then it is a good game to play, but if any of these things are deal-breakers for you then maybe its worth pretending JC3 was the last of the series!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think it's a pretty good game, and I've played the story a couple times through.

Other stuff to consider:
1. There are a ton of small collection and regional missions, and I wasn't sure what many of them unlocked besides some drops. There's a bunch of mini missions where you have to go and do puzzles to discover a lost history of the island, but it does nothing and isn't central to the game's plot except for one early-game mission.
2. The DLCs aren't very good. Picking up with the comic-style cutscenes of JC3 for DLC missions was okay, but the missions themselves are either attempts to bring back the base liberation mechanic from JC3 (Danger Rising) or a ton of weird flying enemies that are hard to see and track (Los Demonios). The race DLC was okay, but many of the challenges can be beaten by dropping in late-game choppers, which seems broken.
3. The map is massive, but liberation is not player-controlled in the same way of previous games. In JC4, you complete a bunch of copy-paste missions first (hack a console, defend a radio tower, flip breakers, destroy generators, submerge car bombs in water, or liberate three prisons), and then manually unlock terrain on the map after the story missions. There are chaos objects like previous games, but they don't matter for base liberation. The repetitive missions don't do much more than eat time and unlock a single new weapon, and they get tedious quickly.
4. Removed pistols, so you can't try and solo a mission on the machine pistols or the revolvers.
5. Vehicles no longer carry infinite ammo.
6. Guns don't feel as good as they did in previous games, although some of the gimmick guns can be fun to use.
7. Story is kinda meh.
8. A lot of stuff is locked in cutscenes. You don't get to kill Espinoza (main boss) or fly down into the tornado fully. The end of the game lines up with the "hacking is everything now" gameplay, but it isn't as satisfying as when you got to take down DiRavello's chopper, execute him, and drop him in a volcano.

I would recommend it so you know whats going on for if/when JC5 comes out, but not really for the gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
