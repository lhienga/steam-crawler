- no unliberation
- fewer destructible trees
- fewer/worse bridge destruction
- limited vehicle ammo (forces you to not endlessly use a single vehicle, which isnt that bad)
- bad looking water

upgrades from jc3:
- more content overall
- better, more engaging combat
- pve air to air combat (not the most in depth but its better than jc3s, which has none)
- more vehicle diversity
- way prettier graphics (besides the water)
- - explosions also look alot nicer
- multiple driving camera angles (hood cam is very fun)
- frontlines are quite a bit of fun, having such a high saturation of friendly and enemy forces to mess around with (however this feature is hindered by the inability to unliberate regions)

story dlc:
- danger rising: 8/10, pretty fun, introduces new enemies, vehicles and a new movement ability
- dare devils: 5/10, its alright, not the best
- los demonios: 7/10, the graphics, models and animations for the enemies are absolutely gorgeous, the gameplay itself tho tends to be a bit tedious

overall each game is fun in its own right, i find myself playing jc4 more often than jc3, mainly because of the better combat

for a first timer to the franchise i recommend jc3, however if youre tired of jc3 i recommend jc4\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was really let down by this entry in the series. It's riddled with bugs, from vehicles freaking out and stuttering all over the place, to randomly clipping through the world, or just random things not working in general. In just 2 hours of playing this game, I saw more bugs than I have in games releasing in alpha with a single developer.
Not only is the game buggy, but the AI is as dumb as a bag of rocks, and they all share a single brain cell. I don't think the AI even knows how to fly helicopters. I've seen them crash into each other, the ground, or anything and everything else around them. When driving, they will straight up drive off cliffs to their death at full speed. I had 3 or 4 in a row drive off one after the other while chasing me, and I didn't even lead them to it.

When it comes to gameplay, it just sucks. I was constantly running out of ammo, and collecting data was extremely frustrating. You can't cancel it, it resets any time you get shot or knocked off of it, and it takes a solid 10 seconds of waiting for the AI to spawn before I'd be able to finish collecting the data. They would then shoot me, causing it to reset again. Having to do that 3 or 4 times each time I had to collect data wasn't fun. The flight controls for the helicopters are horrible as well, and it's not something that you can just fix by changing your controls. It's just how the game is. Unlike most games where you'd expect using your mouse to rotate the helicopters, it doesn't, or it does but really slowly. So rotating around is slow and clunky. It's also really stupid that the AI will shoot missiles at their own helicopters if you're hanging from them. Every time I tried to hijack a helicopter, by the time I'd reach it, it would immediately be hit and take damage.

Another thing that I found frustrating is how many AI they will send after you. You can be swarmed in seconds by a couple helicopters, jets flying overhead dropping bombs, and trucks with mounted guns firing at you. You get overwhelmed so quickly, and when you're just starting out, it isn't fun.

The game also doesn't like showing or telling you what you're supposed to be doing. After completing every mission, I'd be left confused, wondering if I was actually done or not since nothing would pop up or there'd be no dialogue telling me either. If you use the new AR thing that's supposed to light up objectives, it really isn't that helpful and only does anything when the game tells you to use it.

The menus are also confusing and the controls are weird. Why can't I just press "ESC" to go back? Why does it have to be only the delete button? "1" and "2" open the loadout menu, which is another thing that just doesn't work how you'd normally expect it to. When customising loadouts, you have to press and hold enter while using A and D to switch between options. I've never seen any game do their menu like this before. Even navigating the map or the UI in general just doesn't feel right.

I normally don't care about graphics that much, but for a Just Cause game, it is kind of expected to look good. And for something that came out in only 2018, even on very high settings, it looked like Just Cause 2 on the Xbox 360. I was also sad to discover that unlike in Just Cause 3 where you can take down entire bridges, we're back to not being able to do that again, which was one of my favourite things to do. What surprised me the most was how badly the cut scenes looked, somehow they looked worst, it was like I was watching someone else playing the game but on the lowest settings it could have been set to. They should have just animated them instead of doing it all in engine, I know that would have been extra work but at least it would have looked good, unlike what's in the game now.

For only around 2 hours of playing this game, I had to compress my list of complaints down to a few main points. Overall, I was really let down by this game. It just feels like a cheap knockoff. Even though I bought this game on sale, I'm still going to try and refund it. Even at a price tag of $5 for the base game, and a couple dlc packs, it's just not worth the time to play the game, even if this game was free I'd still avoid it.
