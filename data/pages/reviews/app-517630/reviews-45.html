\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished this game and immediately uninstalled it, not because the ending was especially horrible (it's a stinker though), but just because I'm completely sick of playing it. The thing that made its predecessor Just Cause 3 fun is taking over military bases by just blowing the ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• out of them with missiles and gradually taking them apart one petrol storage container at a time, like eating a large explosive meal that leaves you very satisfied.

What they decided to do for this one is to have the bases be the same 4-5 mini-missions spread across about 30 bases. what these base missions have in common are that they are all uniformly bland, boring, and irritating all in their own unique ways, and most require just standing at a console and doing "hacking" for 5 minutes while you shoot the dull weapons at the dull enemies. It's such a baffling design decision, because they have all the explosive tanks and whatever scattered around, but destroying them is more or less a waste of ammo apart from seeing the pretty explosions, they don't progress the missions. Even Just cause 2 had that and just cause 2 is ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• tedious to take over bases in.

The story is bland, and the characters are about as boring as revolutionary war leaders could possibly be. The humour is hit and miss but mostly miss (and to be honest maybe I was just in a good mood when it "hit"). Just cause is not a game you go to for a good story anyway but not one of these bland-as-a-cement-sandwich moon-faced cretins has a quarter of, for example, Dimah Al-Masri's personality.

So we have poor gameplay, ignorable story, and all the good gameplay elements are transplanted from JC3, what do I LIKE about this game?

The setting! The map is absolutely beautiful, huge, and varied like just cause 2, only with better traversal. For some reason they made the cars all as fast as the bavarium supercar from 3 to push it towards making driving a thing that anyone would ever want to do in a game where you have a rocket powered wingsuit (spoilers: I never used cars in this game apart from in missions). And I like that they made the cars fast, I like that they have Jets and planes and helicopters absolutely everywhere, it's almost like half of avalanche knew what kind of game they were making. Just Cause 4's map with Just Cause 3's mechanics would be a marvellous game. If only the mission designers had ever played a GAME before, it might actually be a fun to liberate however many bases and play through the missions, but instead they went for "Breaker box breaker box breaker box hack the terminal destroy the generators defend the satellite dish drive the ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• explosive cars in to the river (because god forbid anything explode in this just cause game) et ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• cetera.

It's an exhausting mess of a game to play through, and the only times I felt like I was having fun were outside of missions, just messing about in the sandbox. They give you like thruster and balloon grappling hooks for some reason, probably so they could put it in the trailer, and apart from the missions where they introduced them I NEVER used them for the entire game, it's like a fun thing to have I guess? Maybe I wasn't creative enough? I don't think it would've saved this from being a slog though.

TLDR: Listen to the other hundreds of reviews that say to get just cause 3 instead, the whole thing plus DLC is usually less than a tenner on steam sales.\t\t\t\t\t
