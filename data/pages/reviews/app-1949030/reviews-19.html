\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been craving a new game from the point and click genre for awhile and the remake of Sherlock Holmes: The Awakened doesn't disappoint!

Sherlock Holmes: The Awakened continues the adventures of the young private detective from Sherlock Holmes: Chapter One and a young Watson also makes his debut too. The game follows a similar formula of Chapter One but instead of exploring the open world of Cordona players instead will be exploring the cold and rainy streets of London, the streets and swamps of Louisiana, creepy underground areas, and a disturbing institution in Switzerland.

Some areas are semi-open world with a fast travel system while others are more self-contained. The maps don't have a player cursor so figuring out where you are on them can take a minute or two but luckily with the game not being full open world makes it easier in that regard and it doesn't take long to learn their layouts.

The game has a more linear narrative compared to Chapter One and gone is the disguise system of needing to blend in but if changing outfits is still your thing then you're in luck because you can change both Sherlock's and Watson's outfits! Another thing that's also gone in the base game are the bonus cases that littered Cordona in Chapter One, unless you buy the deluxe edition or the Whispered Dreams DLC pack.

Sherlock Holmes: The Awakened is a fantastic remake of a beloved older game and in my opinion shows how to do a remake right!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow HERE is a real example of a proper remaster of an older game! This work is so lovingly crafted from start to finish. This took time to do and the developers took the time to do it. "A right proper job", says I.

10 out of freaking 10 (Detective/Horror fans must get this)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played the other Frogwares Sherlock games, but I really enjoyed this. There are some minor issues, but on the whole the game has really engaging mysteries, fantastic investigation mechanics, and a fun and creepy story.

Voice acting was very good, except it felt like maybe the VAs had some less than stellar direction: a decent number of lines are read too quickly, and some conversations can feel a little disjointed. However, this is a problem mostly in the early game; once it really kicks off, you almost entirely stop noticing it.

The evidence collection was a lot of fun, and I thought the Mind Palace and Imagination mechanics were the highlights of the game. Mind Palace has you answering a specific question at the heart of the current mystery (e.g. "How can X be opened" or "What happened to Y"), and you do so by selecting very specific pieces of evidence that combine to create the answer (with some color-coded hints to start). Imagination has you looking at multiple different parts of a crime scene and, at each node, cycling through 2-6 available options that each paint a slightly different picture of the events. These two mechanics together make you genuinely think about the mystery, how it might have gone down, and you have to piece together all the many pieces of evidence you have to understand the solution before you commit to an answer (and then get feedback on whether you were right).

Story-wise, I think the game is relatively strong. The pacing is remarkably consistent so you never feel dragged along or bored. The atmosphere builds up very well, and the sense of unease and unraveling sanity does increase. There are some holes here and there with awkwardly shoved-in characterisation of Holmes and Watson or poorly executed dialogue lines, but the story is good much more often than it is bad. Stakes are understandable and large enough to make you care. The biggest unfortunate thing to come out of the whole bag is the ending, which was unfortunately rather anti-climactic.

All in all, the game knows what it does well: providing a mystery, letting you read through evidence, and providing a way to input your answer in an engaging and intelligent way. And it does everything in its power to make sure THAT is what you're doing as much as possible. It plays to it's strengths, and it has a lot of character. I think I'm gonna go pick up Chapter One in a little bit and continue following the series.

P.S. If you do pick this up (and you should!) make sure to flip to the custom difficulty mode before you begin the game. Move the options for everything to "Off" or "Challenging" EXCEPT the interaction indicators. The interaction indicators are a quality of life mechanic and they don't ruin the mystery. Everything else, though, I think I was better off for turning off. You have the mystery in your own hands if you do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think I might place this in my top three Sherlock Holmes games, after Crimes & Punishments and Vs Jack the Ripper (I want to replay Chapter One to make a final call on that). I enjoyed the original The Awakened a good bit, but there's a lot of clunk in that original game that didn't really allow the team to fully execute on ideas.

What's refreshing here is that this remake, set after the new Chapter One continuity, is both faithful to the original game's beats, while also doing its own thing in a lot of respects. Old investigations are redesigned with the new structure Frogwares has started using, and it works out really well, still offering a lot of satisfying investigations, while shaving some of the bloat (ie goofy puzzles like the lemon juice one). This gives the story a good pace, and the game usually adds something new when something is removed. The lack of the bird cage puzzle is a shame, but giving Watson a more direct role in the asylum chapter was a great addition to the story.

I also like this new narrative more. Despite all the repeated beats, the context is new and follows after some dark revelations from Chapter One, giving this Holmes and Watson a slowly more even dynamic compared to the classic games and stories. Watson being Holmes' anchor is a great idea, and I want to see it explored more in future games. With hints at possible remakes of Nemesis and Testament, I'm excited to see what direction they go in.

Well worth picking up, looks beautiful to boot, but get the deluxe edition for the extra side cases. Some of the new cases are on the thin side, but others, like one revolving around a man in Louisiana with a spell of bad luck, was surprisingly involving and well written.\t\t\t\t\t

Why rather not recommending it:
1) annoying and repetitive mechanic most of the game, dark locations build as labyrinths, not very logical combinations in mind palace
2) outdated graphics (pretty much at least 5 years back)
3) story line is not always coherent and has some strange dead end as well (like a side investigation of Hawk house leading nowhere)

I would say, it is a good piece if you are a fan (although weaker than all other games in these series), but it is definitely not worth that price.\t\t\t\t\t

DISCLAIMER: This is a full review, and NOT a first impressions review

Sherlock Holmes The Awakened is a game developed and published by Frogwares

The game offers some nice graphics and a superb aesthetic, although some ugly glitches here and there, it is pleasant all the same

The game does suffer with some repetitiveness at certain areas of the game but a fun game nonetheless

Sherlock Holmes The Awakened overall, however, is an incredibly fun game to play, yet sometimes repetitive and it does get boring if being played for a long time, but is a really good mystery adventure game to play in short bursts!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed The Devil's Daughter and Crimes & Punishment but this game is really disappointing. The gameplay is as jank as you would expect from Frogware but actually worse. The entire game is very short and the story very lackluster. The control and gameplay is really annoying. The puzzles are not interesting, the deduction is weak, the crime scene investigation is just plain annoying and jank, and the mind palace synapse thing this time just doesn't make sense. It used to be the final part of the deduction to draw an conclusion to the case and now you need to do it throughout the chapters and most of them don't even make sense. You know what to do or the answers to the question but you need to connect the synapses first to even progress the game, and pin the stupid evidences before you can talk to people or finding the next evidence. Is Sherlock blind before pining the evidence? Also why is the scanning surrounding interactables cooldown so long? The final puzzle in the final chapter is also annoying to solve, just plain cumbersome, and if you chose the wrong dialogue option the autosave will have you do the stupid puzzle again so please manually save to save yourselves the trouble. The story builds up to nothing, the ending is anticlimatic, uninspiring, not even half as satisfying as that of The Devil's Daughter.
Technically, the graphic is very mediocre and the lip-syncing is so bad and immersion breaking.
Unless you are a diehard Frogware Sherlock Holmes' fan I don't know why you would be getting this game at this price point, or in fact at all.\t\t\t\t\t
