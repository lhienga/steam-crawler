\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompared to the usual reviews, Sherlock Holmes The Awakened (2023) by Frogwares has actually more advertising and third-party referal/trackers to offer, which are mostly activated by user participation.

What does this product do?
During the initialization process, Frogware servers are contacted and provide the user with free advertising, which can be admired in full beauty in the main menu.

Besides, the product also tells NVidia that someone is online, since it is now common for games with DLSS to contact their servers.

Various networks are advertised in the main menu. When users click on one of these links (Facebook, Youtube, Discord, Twitter, Instagram) they are redirected to the respective page and are welcomed by various tracking mechanisms.

Sherlock Holmes The Awakened is protected by SteamStub DRM

Who is running the product
Sherlock Holmes The Awakened was developed and published by Frogwares

Where are the service providers located?
Frogwares - Kiev, Kyyivs'ka Oblast', Ukraine
Google, LLC - 1600 Amphitheatre Pkwy Building 43, Mountain View, CA 94043, USA
Twitter - San Francisco, California, United States
Facebook & Instagram - Menlo Park, California, United States
Discord - San Francisco, California, United States
NVidia - Santa Clara, California, United States

Which trackers and advertising services are active?
GoogleAds, Google DoubleClick, Captive Portal checks, Google PageAds, Conversiontracking, Referaltracking, Graphql/Logging by several platforms, Trackingcookies/Pixel

User agreement and Opt-Out/In
As usual - Does not exist. Neither for imposed in-game advertising, nor for the tracking mechanisms that are waiting in the supposed social networks.

Can this behavior be blocked?
Yes, without restrictions

Price question
If displaying of ingame advertising is a must, the question would be whether this must be loaded necessarily live from a server instead of being local delivered with the regulary game files. Games (better said, products) are usually patched from time to time, should new content be needed.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished playing through The Awakened remake.

I have played the original - though not since it was a new game. The original was frustrating and tonally absent of any sort of menace IIRC. The remake is a bit of both of those, though less frustrating and a little more atmospheric.

The game stars Sherlock Holmes and James Watson - they are not monster hunters, occultists, nor tomb raiders. They solve mysteries, involving people. The game is about Sherlock hunting down a weird cult, not hunting down Cthulhu. Keep that in mind, and recognize that the cosmic horror elements are going to be minimal, and you'll be less likely to be disappointed.

I liked it - though it seems many devotees of the original game consider the original to be better. My memories of the original were me beating my head against obtuse puzzles so much I'd forget where the game was going overall. If you remember the original fondly, played through it a few times, and enjoyed it, I suspect this'll disappoint (it's shorter, and holds your hand a lot more). If you never played the original or remember it as frustrating but with promise like I do, you might enjoy the remake.

Pinning evidence in the game was a confusing concept at first. If you get it, pay a lot of attention to that explanation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, but. Sherlock Holmes The Awakened is the sequel to Sherlock Holmes Chapter One. The first game (Chapter One) was big and open-world (well, a whole city) with tons of side quests and lots of choices and different outcomes. The Awakened is disappointingly small by comparison. The open-world-ness is downgraded to small areas that are locked to the chapter plot you're playing at the moment, and it gets old quick. The story itself is okay but just like any Cthulhu premise it is predictable and nothing new--- there's a secret cult of Old God worshipers who want Cthulhu resurrected, and the hero has to stop their plans. I'm glad I waited for this game to be on sale, but wish I'd waited for an even better discount. Also got the DLC which added a few more quests--- without these quests the game would be very bare-bones, it's practically the size of a DLC itself instead of a full game. I am disappointed that there was not more content (was only 10 hours tops), but still recommend playing it when it's at a good price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved this game as much as the previous one, though I might have enjoyed Chapter One a little bit more. Still, it was such a great journey, very mysterious and scary at times. The only problem I have with it, it's too short! :)

I do believe the game would have benefitted from the similar ending to Chapter One where we could choose how the story ends, but judging the circumstances under which this game was made, I can't really complain, it's still great though rushed sometimes.

The game itself was very good and entertaining, and I absolutely adore the way we see Sherlock and John's relationship develop. Oh, and I love how you could feel that Watson was important. In older games, he was more like a robot following you around, but here he is actually important to the plot. You can't finish the game without him and I like to see it!

I do hope to see more of this franchise, especially now that we got teased in both Chapter One and The Awakening about mysterious M. ;)

To summon up, the game is great, not as good as its prequel maybe, but still very nice. I think it's worth the price and both Lovecraft and Sherlock fans can enjoy it.\t\t\t\t\t
