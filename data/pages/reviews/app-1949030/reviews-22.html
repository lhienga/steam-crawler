\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe devs will tell you the Ugly Beige Suit is the worst outfit in the game.

I'm here to tell you that they're just jealous of Sherlock's drip.

---------------------------------------
In all seriousness: Bravo Frogwares, bravo!

I only have a bit of hours into it but I can already tell that Frogwares really went out of their way for this. If you've played previous Sherlock games or The Sinking City, you can tell that the devs have been listening to tidbits of criticism and have enhanced the best qualities from both games to make this extremely awesome remake.

Not only that, but this game was made in ONE YEAR. Already sound impressive? Well let me clarify: ONE YEAR IN UKRAINE DURING AN ENTIRE ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ WAR.

Frogwares had every excuse to call this game off. At any time, they could have stopped development to pick it up later and absolutely no one would have blamed them. Instead, Frogwares shrugs it off and releases a game in such speed and in such a stable condition that you almost have to buy the game for the efforts-worth alone. If this was any other AAA developer, this game would come out 5 years later at a unstable 30 FPS with 1280x1080 resolution, 125 gb, and a $70 price tag. But this is Frogwares, who released the premium edition of this game for an astounding $55 in a stable condition that only takes up 25 gb of space.

I'm always excited to see what Frogwares brings to the table with each game they make. The cases are interesting, the environments are well-made, and you can tell the devs love improving these games with each iteration they make.

Well done Frogwares! I'm impressed to no end, and I'm excited to add another Sherlock game to my collection (with Lovecraft in it too!)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm only 4 hours in but I wanted to say that I love this game. It's different from the other Frogwares Sherlock Holmes games in that you are solving one grand overarching mystery rather than individual cases, so I haven't had the experience of having to pick who I thought did it with what in that intense and wonderful way that the other games have, but the story that it is telling is so damn compelling that I don't care. I'm still finding clues and slowly piecing together what's happening, all while people are dying and crazy supernatural stuff is happening around me.

The juxtaposition of Lovecraft and Holmes works perfectly, the aesthetics combine well, and I love Holmes' analytical mind trying to work out why there's weird supernatural ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ happening. I'm super excited to find out what is going on.

On the mechanical side, this game uses the same engine as Chapter One but adds some excellent quality of life improvements. For one, I think the open world was a detriment to Chapter One, and this one is much more linear which is good. For another you can click Z to have all of the interactable items pop up around you, which is an amazing thing to have in this sort of mystery adventure game.

Special shoutout to the characterization, writing, and acting of Watson in this, he is so good.

Very excited to continue, and congrats to Frogwares for a great game.\t\t\t\t\t
