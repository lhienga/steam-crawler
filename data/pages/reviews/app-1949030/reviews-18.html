\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSherlock Holmes The Awakened cements Frogwares as the masters of Holmes and Lovecraft titles, blending everything together for an excellent experience.

The Awakened is a remake of an older game and essentially follows the same plot verbatim, what is refreshed is the visuals, the voice acting and more importantly the gameplay bringing the title in line with the newer games in the Sherlock series and The Sinking City. The more modern gameplay makes this a far more than a basic remake, ascending the title to new heights of both fun and accessibility.

The games basic plot has Sherlock and Watson fresh after the events of Chapter One (which the game refers to frequently) pursuing a cult that worships Cthulu and they must risk their own sanity as they travel the world to stop them.

Gameplay involves finding clues, solving puzzles, deducing what to do and typical Sherlock type things, the only mechanic I myself am not fond of the is the "pinning" evidence to find more evidence which feels a little badly implemented as it did in Chapter One, but overall it's not a huge issue.

The game adds in plenty new scenes to the original title including greatly improving the final battle as well as other key parts of the story to just make everything feel more fun to play and a more complete package, I am very excited to see what Frogwares do in the future and I want more horror and detective titles from them thats for sure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was pretty torn about whether to recommend this or not as I, overall, had a good time. If you really enjoy these games and want some more content, this will scratch the itch to the extent. But I couldn't help but feel a little disappointed after how much I enjoyed Chapter One. The mysteries all feel rushed and half-finished, with many interesting plot points showing up right at the end of chapters and Sherlock basically just shrugging and leaving before it goes anywhere. The side quests are weak and don't lead to anything interesting, and overall I was kind of left wondering, "Was that it?" There were also multiple sequences that were heavily reminiscent of the Fade in DA:O, which is a huge turnoff for me.

The world felt so much smaller than the world of Cordona or even of Crime and Punishments, and the characters were generally far less interesting and less fleshed out. I really wanted to enjoy this, and there were some parts (a certain mystery scene in a mansion), that I loved! But in the end, I'm pretty bummed that I bought this at full price and would recommend waiting for a sale if you do still want to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this game... but I don't. I don't like it at all.

This game feels incredibly rushed and it feels like a series of cool ideas more-so than a game that has any cohesive ideas, themes, or gameplay mechanics to tie to all together. The deduction mechanics are a huge step backwards from prior games (specifically the game I like most in this regard is actually sinking city).

The worst of this game is evident in the confronting portion of the gameplay where with one exception in the latter half of the game... I'm pretty sure it HAS to be trial and error. I don't think there is enough logic built into these sequences for anyone to utilize true intuition to navigate these conversations. And then when you fail you start the whole conversation over and listen to every line of dialogue again. It is truly horrendous game design. It is the worst example of bad game design, but honestly almost all the deduction mechanics suffer from similar problems.

I think the best deduction mechanic is where you rebuild a scene. That actually kind of works and has the feeling of the player actually making deductions.

The final encounter may be the worst gameplay experience I've had in the last several years. It's truly horrendous.

The story is a string of sequences with no logical throughline except for "chase the macguffin". Things happen that seem important... you meet characters you assume will have arcs. Only for the story to continually drop them entirely and never mention them again.

For the pros: The lovecraftian elements feel good. Good art representation of them. This isn't surprising given how effectively it was done in Sinking City. It's good here too. The ending cutscene is particularly good and I think a fitting end to a Sherlock juxtaposed with Cosmic Horror storyline. umm... it was intriguing enough to pull me all the way through mostly thanks to the fact that it includes lovecraftian elements... that's it for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautiful. I want to thank Frogwares for taking their fans comments into consideration, and making such a beautiful experience. This game is perfect for adventure players.

1- The mysterious scenes in Sherlock Holmes are back: We have 6 different, beautifully designed, main locations with lots of details as we expect in adventure games, I have enjoyed exploring every location,

2- They went back to the roots: after trying for 20 minutes or so when I finally opened that door to a room or got access to the next scene I had that spooky adventure feeling which I had when I played Sherlock Holmes devils daughter and previous titles.

3- Character design and presentation: I dont know why but I no longer feel that anime style character design as I did in Cordona , characters are more matured and suited for a Sherlock Holmes game the main outfits are designed more carefully, (in the middle of the game Cordona costumes will be added to customization but I never used them)

So overall a perfect game
Here are some more comments to make Frogwares journey even more fascinating

1- First person view or a camera tool: I cannot stop thinking about the scenes which I could have taken some very nice pictures and use them as my wallpaper or but I didnt have the camera tool or first-person view mode. As we all know there were first-person view mode in all the previous games (except Cordona) and since they went back to their roots it would have been a good idea to bring back the first-person view

2- I know that this team had a very bad situation when they made this game, and I send my deepest condolences to them, and I hope that war never happens to anybody, but as a FAN these situations does not affect my expectations from them, that being said, each location lacks at least 3 side quests and when I imaging the game with these side quests it becomes even greater.

Respects and I want to thank Frogwares team again for continuing to make my favorite games

I FREAKING LOVE THIS GAME. 11/10. It absolutely hits the sweet spot between cosmic horror and investigation. The voice acting is stellar, the relationship between Holmes and Watson is wonderful. Controls are easy and puzzles are fun to solve.
My only complaint is that it's too short T_T, I didn't want it to end. I'm an explore/completionist and it took me 16 hrs to finish. I'm missing one achievement because of a choice I made. I will definitely go back and play it again, but I would love a sequel or some DLCs with more spooky Lovecraft cases. Going to go find more Sherlock and Lovecraft games now...please make more of the Sherlock franchise playable on Deck!
