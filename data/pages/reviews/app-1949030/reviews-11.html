\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as I know, Sherlock Holmes the Awakened is a remake, after watching the video with the original gameplay, there are no questions left - why the developers decided to re-release the game in a new and modern form.
The game includes 8 chapters and is linear. The lack of an open world is quite logically justified, since SH travels around the world. The game is built on the Chapter One engine and basically looks exactly the same, but there are small visual artifacts that are rare and raise questions. Characters periodically disappear even in cut-scenes, sometimes after opening the door strips from the doors remain on the floor, and during the game sometimes the camera randomly switches from a third person view to a top view for a few seconds (as was in old Resident Evil games).
All mechanics are transferred exactly from the Chapter One. But the plot is addictive, it is very interesting and makes you play further. The game in terms of plot is more similar to the Testament than the Chapter One, since there is one mystery, which is divided into 8 chapters, so there is no cross-cutting plot.

I really liked the game, the whole passage kept me in suspense and each new chapter created more and more questions. The last chapter caused me a couple of questions, the so-called boss fight is not hard, just drawn out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game, I've enjoyed it.

Graphics are pretty and music is good, so together they create rather atmospheric experience. The game is pretty stable too, excluding some minor glitches - Watson often were running in place if you just stand still.

Story is interesting, but if you've played original, you already know everything and there's not much of mystery. And, comparing to original, remake has less puzzles. On one side, I understand it - some puzzles in original were a bit convoluted like puzzle with pipes and syringes in Edelweiss, or getting finger from raccoon, or the whole getting to lighthouse section. And I never understood why in original Moriarty now is in asylum. . But on the other side, detective game with not a lot of puzzles feels a bit strange.

Though, there's some things that are a lot better in comparison to original. Like relationship between Sherlock and Watson. It feels a lot more natural and sweet. I can feel how much they trust and care about each other. The moments that come to mind - scene in the train when Sherlock tells Watson about his mother, their banter about clothes in New Orleans port and confrontation between Microft and Watson.. And Watson now has a lot more to do than just be Sherlock's sidekick - he's the one examining bodies and injured people (you know, cause he's the doctor), shooting if needed (cause he was a soldier and for Sherlock it's just a hobby) and he's the one that realised some things like figuring out which lighthouse they need .

I'm conflicted about the ending though. Like, before I understood why Sherlock is the one that sees all the hallucinations - he has the history of mental health issues from the first game and also it runs in the family (his words, not mine). But the ending implies that now he's a lot worse - doubting every action, cannot sleep without sedative, and in general is really unstable. And they've pushed you to this too! In the location it is insta-fail if you choose any dialogue option other than accepting the madness. If you wanted to make Sherlock mad in the ending, why even give us other options? And what, in the future games Holmes will be unstable with Watson acting more like a caretaker than a friend? That's not very good. . And why in the world there're QTA?
That thing villain said about meeting before long time ago, eternal cycle, old becoming new - obvious hint at the original game - was pretty cool.

In conclusion, the game was pretty fun and if you liked the Chapter One or detective games in general (or if you want to support Ukrainian game company), I recommend you try this one too.


Дякую за чудову гру, Frogwares! Слава Україні, разом до перемоги!\t\t\t\t\t

Short but sweet
After the longer, open-world Chapter 1 Frogwares set out to re-make one of their previous adventures a second time and I have to say it was well worth it!

There are lots of places to visit and secrets to uncover, and unlike entries from Crimes and Punishments on, this one feels a lot more like a classic adventure- barely any side-content, a linear path, investigations broken up by puzzle sections and item hunting, it was really refreshing.

The qualities one (or at least I) look for in a Sherlock game are still there and perhaps even stronger than in Chapter 1, be it the strong voice acting of the main cast, the palpable atmosphere of times gone by, the shiny presentation and of course the detective aspect, somewhat combining both the gameplay mechanics of C1 with Crimes and Punishment- separate cases that are connected to a larger narrative thread, making use of the evidence pinning mechanic of the former combined with the mind palace and confrontational dialogue of the latter (though as far as I can tell, there's always only one way to solve each case).

While it was short and the (surprisingly numerous) hubs largely only served as backdrops I thoroughly enjoyed the game as it effortlessly put Sherlock in a Cthulhu-inspired world that was fittingly eery sometimes and improved on the clarity of the mechanics in comparison to C1.

I would not have minded more content of course (Probably the biggest flaw of the game is that it ended), but given there's a fricking war going on over in Ukraine it is a miracle that a game of this caliber was released at all- only the comparatively low number of cutscenes and reduced scope is telling of that fact.

So I'd say anyone who enjoys detective games, the Cthulhu mythos, past Sherlock games or British culture in general should pick this puppy right up.

7.9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played the original one so long ago, this one is an improvement on the story and graphics. Unfortunately the puzzles have been dumbed down to simple find item to open lock or press button to see highlighted clue to help players move along without straining their mental muscle. Not something a real puzzle game would be. Though I still enjoyed the story, characters, and horror from this game, it is a lot less whimsical than the previous one, being a lot more darker and actually being supernatural, whereas the original one only hinted at supernatural. All-in-all it is a good game to get when on sale. I just hope the developers, when making follow up games, they actually put work in making real puzzles to solve instead of just running around trying o find the right item to fit the slot or using forced guess-work.\t\t\t\t\t
