\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell after Sherlock Holmes Chapter One, I didn't really have high expectations for the sequel. And to my surprise, after my playthrough it's actually not bad. It's definitely better than it's predecessor. Although it's shorter, I'm still quite hooked on the game itself.

Starting off with it's game mechanics, most of it are basically carried over with the exception of the mind place. For me it felt much more mature and a revamped of previous games. The reconstruction mini game is back and it's even better than the previous games albeit linear. Yes the game doesn't have branching storyline or ending as it's previous game and it's mostly linear with some story choice that will lead to death. Now I will have to say that the open-world element has definitely taken a step back but it's still fun regardless. I rather have some section of the map be clunky instead of the entire map. My previous complain were the map being too empty, but with limited space to move around I definitely like this approach more as it makes the map feel more condensed and alive. Some other noteworthy mechanics which are removed were the disguised system, throughout my gameplay I don't think there's any disguise mechanic involved and the awful combat system has also been removed.

As for the graphics, I don't think anything has changed it still looked pretty much the same as the previous game but the OST is definitely a huge improvement. Some of the soundtracks fits perfectly with the environment creating unsettling and eerie feel whilst you playthrough the story. I think for the most part the game does have better optimization as I experience basically no FPS spike. So far my only complaint is it's poor story and short gameplay time.

In conclusion, this is definitely a solid sequel and it deserve praises as it has numerous improvements. For me, the game is a strong 7/10. Aside from it's short duration and a few story flaws, the game is pretty much as good as it can get. Though i do wish they allow for branching storyline or choices with impact like it's previous entries. If you're expecting more freedom or replayability, this game is definitely not it.\t\t\t\t\t

Falls short in comparison to the previous title

After playing all Sherlock Holmes games and most specifically Chapter One right before this one... I am very disappointed. The Awakened falls short, even as a remaster. Comparing it to the latest game, it doesn't have any of the shining points I liked in Chapter One:
- No side quests (expect for one short one)
- No open world
- No way to decide in what order to do things
- No realistic plot line which is so very much connected to Sherlock

In short, this game is only successful in atmosphere and graphics, and even if it did have some points that made me happy to be playing it, I soon became disappointed once more as the game took off from the good trail it set on.
The Awakened is more focused on horror-like setting and seems more like a walking simulator than anything else. As a Sherlock Holmes game it doesn't give you the satisfying feeling of completion, getting to solve a mystery with facts and with facing the one and only truth.

SPOILER ALERT
As you go through hallucinations and visions, eager to find out what caused them and trying to figure out how it was done, the game leaves you hanging with "the stories, the fiction and the fantasy was real all along. The Eldritch God is real and there was nothing you could do about it". That it lame story writing for me and doesn't leave anything to give you that "wow" factor that I so much enjoy in Sherlock stories.

Long story short, if you enjoy classic Sherlock Holmes stories, or liked Chapter One, this one is not for you. I bought the game on sale and I still feel like it was not worth it. Mere 9 hours of gameplay for a bland story and no space for decisions of your own.\t\t\t\t\t
