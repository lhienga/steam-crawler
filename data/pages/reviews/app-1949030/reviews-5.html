\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really good game that flew under my radar for too long.
After finishing Dredge I needed a new game too scratch that Lovecraftian horror itch. After a rather short search this gem popped up. The trailers were enthraling and the reviews were generally positive.
After playing it for 10 hours and just starting to wrap up the story I feel I am due a review.

let's start with a few positives:
- the game looks stunning!
- the characters are written beautifully. Watson and Holmes are written as the dynamic duo they should be: night and day but that creates a wonderful connection
- the story is well paced and easy to follow
- it's not a handholding kind of game (I personally really like this but if this not your cup of tea I can imagine this becoming frustrating)
- It balances the aspects of 19th century England and the Lovecraftian themes perfectly

now for some less than stellar points
- the intro mission is incredibly dull
- the controls take some getting used to. sometimes I still don't really know what the symbols next to the pieces of evidence mean. Might also just be me
- you sometimes kinda loose the plot. it feels like a lot of seperate stories intertwining at times but I found myself wondering why I was doing something. Like what am I actually doing, where am I going with all this.
- no native ultra-widescreen support (I know this is nitpicky but having to install a mod to play the game in full glory doesn't feel like I should have to do nowadays. Again, very nitpicky, I know)

so, do I recommend this game? Yes I do. but only if you love story driven games with lovecraftian elements. if you are looking for something more action driven and high octane skip this game.

This is an adventure puzzle game that combines Sherlock Holmes and the Cthulhu Mythos, this team paid homage to Lovecraft's short stories in many of the game's details, such as The Shadow Over London( The Shadow Over Innsmouth ) and The Outdoor Horror( The Dunwich Horror ).Like the previous work of the production team, many side cases have been added to the game, but these side cases don't affect the acquisition of game achievements.Although they have supplemented and enriched the story background of the game to a certain extent, all this cannot affect the beginning of the second half of the game. The production team is somewhat unsatisfied.That's why I can't recommend this game to you, I respect the original intention of the game production team. I think they want to show players a brand new remake, but due to force majeure factors, it's difficult for them to perfectly realize this idea.
The most exciting part is still in the first half of the game, and there're many details worthy of your consideration.
These are my thoughts on why I don’t recommend this game.

这是一款结合了夏洛克·福尔摩斯系列与克苏鲁神话的冒险解谜游戏，制作组在游戏的许多细节中致敬了洛夫克拉夫特的短篇故事，比如说伦敦疑云 之影 （ 印斯茅斯的阴霾 ）和门外恐怖事件（ 敦威治恐怖事件 ）。像制作组的前作游戏一样，游戏内也同样加入了许多支线事件，但这些支线时间并不直接影响游戏成就的获得。尽管它们在一定程度上补充和丰富了游戏的故事背景，但这一切并不能影响到游戏的后半部分。游戏制作组有些心有余而力不足。这也是为什么我不能向您推荐这款游戏，我尊重制作组的初衷。我认为他们想要向玩家展示一个崭新的重制版，但由于不可抗力因素，他们很难完美实现这个想法。
最精彩的还是在游戏的前半部分，有许多细节值得去推敲。
以上就是我为什么不推荐这款游戏的观点。

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA couple of weeks ago I tried the prequel, Sherlock Holmes: Chapter One, and bounced right off of its annoying characters, boring setup, stiff controls and dreadfully uninteresting combat. I wrote then that since I had already bought the sequel, I would give it a chance in time. And boy oh boy am I glad I did, because The Awakened for me was like a complete 180 in terms of appeal right from the first few minutes.

While I wish that the real-world attack on their home country had never happened (and ends ASAP), I cannot help but think that the horrible reality has seaped into the developer's design of the second game. The Lovecraftian imprint on this game may be the selling point and motivation of the story, but even without it the atmosphere and depiction of nearly all manners of society are desperate and dreadful at best. It was like watching an episode of Penny Dreadful, a world so dark and devoid of hope that it was chilling. And the music, oh my god, the music did its best to enhance the feeling of mystery, looming darkness and inescapable terror so perfectly that I increased its volumne specifically to delve deeper into the mood of the game.

With the setting and atmosphere being set and the cosmic horror looming, I shook off my initial gripes with the controls (similar awkward stiffness compared to the first game) and set off on being a detective. And thankfully, no combat. Grandiose design decision to get rid of it. The story alone and its many well-written characters created enough tension. There are a handful of quicktime events, which were unnecessary but also simple enough, so they didn't hurt the enjoyment. The main gameplay loop was puzzle solving, exploration and experiencing the slow progression of Sherlock (and Watson) into the mouth of madness.

I wish the developers well and that the real world horror ends as soon as possible. If they get to make another game in the future, which I would certainly consider buying, I hope that they can build on what they did here. Holmes and Watson at the end of this game are flawed and partially mad individuals and certainly as interesting to me as any other iteration of them in other games, movies or TV series. Well done.

Very strongly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
