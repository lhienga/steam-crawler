\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGenerally I am big fan of Frogwares; their games are always a bit clunky, maybe a bit "chore-y", but with the satisfying case solving, good story-telling, and nice ambience, I can overlook a lot of that.

I appreciate what Frogwares attempted to do here over Chapter One where you go to multiple locations, experience new areas with their own charm and personality, but the downside is every area you went to felt..small. Unsatisfying. Too small to feel immersive, too small to grip me.

Along with that, the cases were short and generally unsatisfying. The only main case that felt like you were were "Sherlock" in my opinion was the Chapter 5 case (which seemed so out of place considering how "open" that chapter felt compared to the next 2 chapters, which are basically just walking simulators into a cutscene with very little detective work in comparison)

Plus, am I the only one who sees many features/downgrades over Chapter One? Remember in Chapter One how you would need to wear a certain disguise to talk to certain NPC's and get them to talk? Remember the gun fights? Hell remember how LARGE the map was in comparison to these tiny maps in the Awakened? Remember the side quests working for the police department? Remember the interrogations? Potentially coming to different conclusions in the Mind Palace?

It's pretty short (I completed it in 9 hours, completing every quest/side quest and scouring to try and complete everything) and in my opinion, certainly not worth at full price in the slightest.

This feels more like watching cut scenes and walking around than it does actually feel like you are solving a case and being a detective. The Lovecraftian element should have stayed with Sinking City. It worked there I think (again, cases were longer, felt more satisfying, map was much larger and engaging) and does not work here.

I would like to see Frogwares go back to the things that made Chapter One great. Let me do little chemistry experiments, actually go to city hall/police department/library to gather evidence and clues! It's not tedious, it's immersive! The main focus should be THIS! NOT feel like I'm watching an interactive movie.

I have a lot of sympathy for the studio making this in a time of war, and I'm sure that's why the last 3 chapters feel so rushed, but it's simply a downgrade in almost any way you look at it from what we just got with Chapter One.

My only criticism is that there is no indicator of your location on the map, i.e. where you are or what direction you're facing. Other than that, I couldn't recommend this game (and the rest of Frogwares' work) enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing this game almost three times over now, I'll admit to being enthralled. I am 100% biased, and unashamedly so. The memory of my first playthrough will sit with me until the next game, surely, and its beautifully fitting ending haunts me even now.

Every aspect of this remake is a vast improvement on the 2006 original and 2008 remaster of the same name. The Music? Haunting. The voice acting? Impeccable, and more often than not horrifying (in a good way). The gameplay? So much more refined and interesting compared to its predecessors - and even improved from its implementation in Chapter One. (For example: the white on white indicators were hard to see in CH1 - but the green ones of TA stand out and telegraph intent better.) The game is almost three times the length of its original and has significantly more relevant-to-the-main-story side-content overall. (When I mention side-content here, I am NOT referring to the DLC side quests - I am specifically referring to the self-contained stories within each location that feed into the overarching narrative.)

But how does The Awakened stand on its own? It is a perfect game for ME. Frogwares knows their audience and it's my belief that they've got their catering down to a fine art at this point. So, if this recipe for a game sounds great to you, you're going to love it: Heavy narrative focus, lots of walking and collecting things, solving puzzles that are designed to intrigue rather than frustrate, PRETTY visuals and ambient sound design, a gratuitous amount of literary and pop-culture references that are not necessary, but are greatly appreciated by a book/cinema nut - AND SHERLOCK HOLMES. Frogwares adores the Doyle-Canon and their passion for that original source is absolutely second-to-none in the industry. The best part is they're also not afraid to dig into non-canonized Holmes stories and draw from the cream of that crop.

I really appreciate their attention to detail, their tiny idle animations, their character design choices, their art direction for each area, and their VA direction/casting choices. I wouldn't have considered myself a fangirl before this game, honestly, but this thing has absolutely shattered my expectations, and I simply cannot wait to see what next chapter they've got planned for Young Sherlock, but you better bet your bottom shilling I'm gonna be playing the crap outta that installment too.

Obviously this is a winner for me. I don't even care if this destroys my credibility as a reliable reviewer. I need Frogwares to make more of this stuff, because this stuff is my kinda stuff. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
