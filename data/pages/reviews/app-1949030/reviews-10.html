\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnce again Frogwares makes a game I am in absolute love with. I had no idea a new Sherlock game was in the works and was pleasantly surprised for my birthday. This is arguably one of my favourites so far, and I've played all of them! It's not easy picking which one has been my favourite since I enjoy all their games.

Here's some stuff I noticed that is new or got upgraded that I would love to see more of in future installments!
ENVIRONMENT/CHARACTERS
The character AI is smart and feels realistic. This goes for both sherlock, watson, and all the NPCs. They all of different walking animations, or move around depending on the time of day (how far along you've gotten in your case), and they have a wide variety of personalities.
Watson will go and look at bookshelves, tend to patients, interact with his environment when you're not playing him. He feels real for interacting with the world. I don't think i've seen much of this in earlier frogware games.
You get to play as Watson!!! I think this has always been a dream of mine to have more Watson screen-time and give him the spotlight every once in a while. I was beyond excited when I got to walk around and figure stuff out as him. The moments were too brief for my liking, but I would love more of this in future games!! Maybe make him some more optional side quests for those who want give Watson some love and play as him more. I cannot stress enough how I'd love to have more of Watson in the future. He was overall extremely likable, not just a sidekick following sherlock like a puppy, was useful, smart, and handsome ;)

I also enjoyed how the environments change as you move through your case. There's no day/night cycle, but the devs have found a creative way to have a dynamic environment. It makes the game so much more immersive than previous ones.

OUTFITS/DIGUISES
Unlocking outfits via clues instead of buying them is a nice trade off. It makes you hunt for all the clues and you get rewarded for it. I love all the call-back outfits and spotting the easter eggs :) My only complaint is I wished the disguises were more useful. I loved in previous games where you had to get the disguise correct to talk to people, otherwise you can't move along in your case. I don't feel the need to change outfits in this game unless it's for my own pleasure.

I love the call-backs to previous games. You don't need to play them to understand the plot which is perfect, but it also lets long-time fans view a subtle ongoing storyline.

GAME MECHANICS/CONTROLS
I feel like the clue controls were easier to understand in this game. I struggled with learning the mechanics in Sherlock Chapter One and I got frustrated early on, but I understood it quicker in this game. I do wish the tutorial case was more in-depth on how you need to play the game and went over the symbols on how you need to proceed in your clue search. I'm thankful the "Help/Control" section of the game menu was there, otherwise I wouldn't have known what each symbol meant in the case book. The mind palace was also easier to control and understand in this game.

And for those who enjoy the subtly of sherlock and john's unique relationship: If you squint there's definitely Some Moments in this game if you get what I mean. I think my heart got wrenched out a couple of times hearing Sherlock cry out for John. I know not everyone wants/agrees Sherlock and John have queer/gay moments sometimes, I'm not one of these people. I'm very much into the "they totally have a thing for eachother". This game balances these two perspectives on Sherlock and John's relationship very well. There's nothing outright saying "they're together in a relationship" or anything close to that, but they have their special moments every now and then that makes me go "hmmm, this makes my gay ass happy." I would love to see a Side Quest or Optional Choices to talk to Sherlock that would insinuate them having a relationship or suggest being more than colleagues.

Overall, I love this game and I want to throw more money at the devs for more. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love Sherlock Holmes, the books, tv series (especially the one from the 80s and early 90s with Jeremy Brett), some of the movies and of course the Frogwares games. Overall Sherlock Holmes The Awakened is a good game, with a few issues:

What i liked:
- It's a single case, not several short cases like in the three previous games (i still liked Crimes and Punishments a lot).
- Performance, graphics and sound design are pretty good.
- It's an interesting story. Not 100% Sherlock Holmes, more like 50% Sherlock, 50% H. P. Lovecraft.
- It never got frustrating.
- After I didn't like Watson in Devil's Daughter a lot, I like him much better here. And he also becomes active more often and follows Sherlock not just with occasional questions and comments.
- It's not an open world game like Chapter One, it's divided into chapters. The open world in Chapter One was quite nice looking, but imho filled with too many repetitive side tasks.
- No more shootings! Seriously: Those were horrible in Chapter One.

What i didn't like:
- No classic adventure game puzzles. I liked them in the old games. Unfortunately they decided to mostly get rid of them around the time Crimes and Punishments was released. Probably because younger audiences want a more cinematic experience that isn't interupted by "annoying" puzzles. Anyway: I like "annoying" puzzles.
- No more difficult decisions where you have to figure out who's the culprit. Not entirely bad, but this game is a lot more story focussed, less real detective work. It's still there, but pretty simplified compared to the previous Sherlock Holmes games.
- Some annoying and repetitive (but fortunately not very difficult) quicktime events towards the end of the game.
- Small side missions are sold as a separate dlc. Imho they're mostly good, but they should be part of the main game. If you want them, wait for a sale. If you don't want to wait and still want them... go buy them. They're good, but i still don't like them being a seperate dlc.

TLDR: Good Sherlock Holmes game, but imho not as good as Jack the Ripper, Testament and Crimes and Punishments. Those are still my favorite games of the series.\t\t\t\t\t

I mean that was basically what he was, the Scoobie-doo of the Victorian era, exposing the monsters and boogiemen and always showing them in the end to be, just, well, men. So to be able to take both of these source materials and enmesh them this well in a narrative together in a convincing way is incredibly impressive. And believe me, they really did their homework with the source material. This is Sherlock and The Mythos at their very best, taking great care to be as accurate and yet as subversive as possible. Narratively, it's wonderful. The story was, despite a slow beginning, utterly phenomenal. And the end! Holy damn Watson, the end! The entire game is fantastic, but the ending goes far beyond everything I expected, as I never played the original. What happens to an obsessive logic machine like Sherlock when the impossible is the only possible explanation? It's madness. The two ideas enmesh so perfectly, and the ending truly shook me with it's absolutely brutal take on everything. Takes a lot for a horror game to do that for me, and I enjoy horror, I play a lot of it. But what they have done here, blending The Mythos with the Holmes stories like this? They have a grasp of their source material to rival historians of the original authours. It's magnificent.

The voice acting was a little all over the place, sometimes AAA, sometimes sounding like it was a mate of the development team recording it in their flat on a laptop. But this is a small complaint, and mainly reserved for the potted lines random NPCs give you if you ask them questions when you don't need to. (The lines they give you when you have evidence that DOES actually require you to speak to random NPCs on the street are just fine. It's the useless ones they all repeat endlessly when you don't have any evidence to ask them about that are the most annoying example.) And I mean hell. Considering how the team had to work, that's hardly worth complaint (and the laptop thing might even be true just out of their necessity!). It's a miracle it exists at all.

Considering the conditions this game was made under and it still turning out like it has, you should literally be THROWING money at these guys. I'm even going back and buying "SH: The Devil's Daughter" and "Chapter One" it was that well written. I already own "The Sinking City", which also really, really understood the source material they were working with, and did NOT shy away from confronting the thornier parts of Lovecraft and his abhorrent views, a thing I found brave and that they handled admirably, while also giving voice to the greatest part of his work, your own cosmic irrelevance and the utter horror of that.

It IS a little janky. Sometimes the puzzles can be somewhat obtuse and overly specific, but it's hard to avoid that when designing those kinds of challenges. So all they take are a little thinking outside the box, or just a small step back to take in the bigger picture. Or, sometimes I'll admit it was just running round smashing buttons on likely things, but that was in the beginning when I was still getting used to how the game thinks. After the second chapter, even though the puzzles were harder, I solved them faster and easier, because now the logic made more sense. That is literally the end of my critique. Considering the conditions mentioned before, this team deserve every penny and more. What they've been through and to still manage to pull this off? Incredible.

TL;DR Great fun. Spectacular ending, one I won't ever forget. Chilling to the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ bone and pure Lovecraft. Never turned the soundtrack off, which is rare. Just get it. Full price is good, on sale is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ great (At English prices, that is. 80 Australian Dollars full price might be a bit much, but what can you do). A wonderful combining of some of the finest narratives ever written, even with the caveats, and that doesn't overstay it's welcome. A solid and genuine 9/10, even with it's flaws.\t\t\t\t\t
