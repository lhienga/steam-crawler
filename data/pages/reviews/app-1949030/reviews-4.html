\r\n\t\t\t\t\tsome major points of criticism:
- for its full price outside of seasonal sales, this game is just too short. at the time of this review, it took me slightly less than 8 hours to complete the main story.
- no brightness settings. i know it's tied to the ~atmosphere~, but still.
- from the bottom of my heart, ♥♥♥♥ the boat segment. it goes on for way too long.
- same goes for the wandering in a cave part.
- the story is a disjointed, chaotic mess that ultimately falls flat due to the very simple fact sherlock holmes - the most briliantly rational man - simply doesn't mesh well with the lovecraftian. the eldritch horrors are meant to be incomprehensible; and sherlock holmes was supposed to be someone for whom incomprehensible doesn't exist. it's like trying to combine tuna with milk chocolate - by itself both these things are fine, but they simply do not pair well.
- of course this story makes use of the oldest ableist joke in the book: a crazy guy thinks he's napoleon. hilarious. comedy gold. totally haven't been done 10000 times before.
- the world ends up being saved by a power of friendship. i wish i was joking, but no.\t\t\t\t\t
