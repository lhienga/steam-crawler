\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSherlock Holmes: The Awakened Remastered is a captivating adventure that takes players on a journey through a chilling Lovecraftian world. The updated graphics and improved gameplay make for an immersive experience that is truly enjoyable.

The game's story offers a diverse array of locations, from Victorian London to the Louisiana swamps. The puzzles are challenging, requiring keen observation and logical thinking. Frogwares has done a commendable job of creating a game that doesn't hold the player's hand but rather encourages them to explore and experiment.

However, the ending falls short of expectations. The last 15 minutes feel rushed, with forced decisions, unnecessary character deaths, and a frustrating puzzle that feels more like trial and error. Despite this, the overall experience of the game is enjoyable, and it's impressive that Frogwares was able to develop it amidst the turmoil of the Russian invasion.

In conclusion, Sherlock Holmes: The Awakened Remastered is a great game with a compelling story and intricate puzzles, but it suffers from a disappointing conclusion. Nevertheless, it's worth playing for fans of the genre and those who appreciate the unique blend of Sherlock Holmes and H.P. Lovecraft.\t\t\t\t\t

Unless you are a massive fan of Chapter One, and would be happy with a linear follow-up, Sherlock Holmes The Awakened is a case best left unsolved.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving only played Testament, C&P and Chapter One before of the Frogwares Sherlock games (and having tried to play their The Sinking City, but I stopped playing that one an hour or two in), I loved Sherlock Holmes: The Awakened!

This game basically takes everything I thought didn't work in Chapter One and either throws it out the window or improves on it. No "cluttered yet empty-feeling" open world, but a more focused level-by-level evolution of the story. A great main story of which you put the pieces together bit by bit. Improved mind palace gameplay.

I spent half the time that I spent on Chapter One in The Awakened (and I am far from 100% completion on C1), yet I feel like I got more fun out of The Awakened it's a great twelve-ish hour game if you're like me and investigate everything (and get the side missions DLC, which due to the game's linear focus you should get in advance instead of afterwards, if you want it at all).

This was the first Frogwares game I bought at full price (partly because I've enjoyed C1, partly because I love Lovecraftian horror, partly to show some support for a small dev I've been enjoying for a long time), and I'm glad I did.

I'm looking forward to whatever they do next, whether it's DLC to this game or - my preference - immediately moving on to a third young Sherlock game (some mythology gags in this game could be interpreted as the next game being Sherlock going up against either Moriarty or Lupin), I'll be there!\t\t\t\t\t
