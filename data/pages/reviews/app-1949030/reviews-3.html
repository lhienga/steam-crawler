\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has great atmosphere, visuals are holding well. And dialogues are on the level.
One thing to note is that investigation mechanics are better in the Frogware's later games. But playing this one actually made me think that Alan Wake 2 developers got some inspirations from The Awakening. You'll see some striking resemblences as you play.

The invastigations are well though out, but individual mechanics could be improved: "collecting" evidence is a cornerstone of any investigatory game, but maybe moving gameplay from the world into a notepad simulator isn't the best idea.

Better if instead of pinning evidence we could pin questions and then mark objects in the scene that would answer those questions. This would result in us working in the actual game world.

One of the later games(Crimes & punishment?) will have it better - you pick a more likely theory out of the few after examining as many evidence as you want - this actually activated some deduction neurons in my brain.

Never the less, this is an exciting game. But to save any potential frustration, have a walktrough browser tab on standby.\t\t\t\t\t

Won't recommend to anyone unless for at least a 75% discount. 4/10

General quality:
Map much smaller compared to Chapter One. Severe issue of reusing materials. England and New Orleans streets basically looks the same except for the weather. Modelling is chunky and coarse in some scenarios. Little or no side quests. Linear gameplay and without multiple options for a single case like in Chapter One and previous Sherlock Holmes games. I'm aware that this game is made under extreme circumstances, but also keep in mind that they priced this almost the same as Chapter One, and quality of Awakened is not close to a game made ~3 years ago.

Gameplay:
Very, Very hard to navigate, for EVERY SINGLE objective, to an extent that made the game completely unplayable. You will spend half of your game time, after finishing a half decent indoor puzzle, and simply trying to discover "where should I go next". There is no guidance in game whatsoever, not even the casebook will give you any clue. You basically have to search every inch of each scenario, but thankfully, they are not too big (thank god they are on a rush). I spent ~1hr just to find which ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ warehouse with the red paint is, and another ~1hr in the swamp. I literally have no more energy to keep playing.

In terms of gameplay, it's exactly the same as Chapter One. Clicking on the glowing boxes on the ground, press Q a few times and combine clues in mind palace. It's okay for me. If you haven't played previous games before prepare to get lost in the pin evidence and mind palaces mechanics

Arts and Story
There's several scenario of acceptable arts quality. I don't think they are as good as in Chapter One but I won't be picky given the situation Frogware is in. I'm mainly very disappointed about the story so far. Sherlock should be digging into grimoires and ancient scrolls, to show players more about the natures and stories of the Old Gods. Frogware could give player options, to either explain the supernatural phenomena out of reason, and say everything is just illusions which caused by drug, or admitting the existence of unspeakable entity. Instead, the whole plot was reduced to "from an ordinary kidnapping to tracking down a bunch of cultists". These cultists could be anyone, or don't even have to be cultists at all and the plot would still be intact. From what I experienced so far, which is 5.5 chapters, more than half of the game, the Cthulhu elements presented in the game are just meaningless symbols and occasional octopus and tentacles. This is the WORST way of depicting Lovecraftian elements, especially from a company that made Sinking City.\\\\

Conclusion
Due to the fact that this game is a rushed product, unpleasant navigation and guidance system, lack of polishing and mediocre plot, I doubt I would ever have the courage and strength to finish this game in the future. It won't be the worst game you ever played, but it just doesn't meet my expectation. Frogware have made Lovecraftian games (Sinking City) and Sherlock Holmes (Chapter One) games before, but I personally think Awakened does not match the quality of either one of them. The game overall just feels so mediocre. If you would like to have a taste of Awakened, please wait for a big discount.\t\t\t\t\t

If you're a fan of Sherlock Holmes and/or Lovecraft, you should buy the base game when it's on sale and you won't regret it. I'm a bit on the fence about the DLC.
