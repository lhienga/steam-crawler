\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Mountain Royals expansion for Age of Empires 2: Definitive Addition has gotten alot of hate for not having enough content for its price, but the hate is simply undeserved. This expansion has 3 campaigns, but they are *really* good campaigns with some interesting new civilisations.

The Armenian campaign is pretty enjoyable with a focus on choosing between allies with different playstyles. One can either be supported economically or militarily depending on the ally chosen. This one also has interesting mechanics like breaking free from a tributary relationship and being on the defending side of a siege. The Armenians are an interesting tanky civilisation with battle monks with +100% healing, infantry with more HP, Churches that can be garrisoned and mule carts that can be used to gather resources easier.

The Persian campaign is pretty simple and standard with its mechanics, but its about an era and religion (Shia Islam) never really given any attention and features a Persia that has been reworked to better represent its history as one of the "Gunpowder Empires". Its pretty enjoyable to play with a civilisation with cheap archers, excellent late-game heavy cavalry and gunpowder castles that destroy siege equipment.

The Georgian campaign is excellent. Its a cavalry spam campaign with a focus on liberating territories for economic allies. It lets one focus on being constantly aggressive and using units efficiently with good micro. The campaign also features a set piece battle with a preparation stage, and scenarios with optional pacifistic paths. The Georgians are a pretty simple civilisation. They have the mule carts and garrisoned churches (that boost villager gathering) that can be used to keep their economy safe. However, their main appeal is that one just has to spam cavalry with with and try to build lots of castles that are buffed by higher elevation and arrows that go through multiple units.

This is a good expansion and on sale it is about the price of a donair platter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeen playing AoE since it was released back in 1998 or 1999, can't really remember the date.

As someone that took a degree asa historian and a Jurist then proceed to take a master in Legal History i was always fond of AoE games, well apart from the AoE3 campaign that was more make believe than an actual historical campaign. Nonetheless the game mechanics were quite and the dlc nice.

With this out of my chest it does pain me that the last dlc's are lacking in quality. Return of Rome delivered a much wanted faction, the roman, but no roman campaign in the AoE2, it was ported to aoe1 and it was good awful one of the worst things i've ever played.

These new campaigns, the ones in mountain kings, were quite frankly average. I enjoied the new civs, but that was it, not good or great, just ok and, on top of that, a bit overpriced since they added only 2 new civs. Remaking the persian civ was a nice touch for those who play it, but for me it was just another generic civ, since elephants are not exclusive to them anymore.

I think AoE teams should try to make a new game, and not ask relic to make a game for them, since aoe4, even with the byzantines, is not what i had expected. Played a good time on aoe4, but the game looks pretty, but is souless. It appears that in these games, aoe 2, 3 and 4 the only viable factions are English and Franks, for some reason. Overall i count them as really good and conforting civs, although they are not my favourite ones.

This dlc is overpriced, delivered not enough content and i miss the old days when campaigns where 6 mission long, or even more like aoe 1 (albeit these missions were quite simple). Now we have 5 mission casmpaigns for overpriced stuff. Some of the previous dlc introduced unique mechanics, like the romans attack boost, same going for the bungurdians (although that campaign was miserable), cover behind the wagon gunpowder units. But this new dlc is just a bit souless. The game is good, but it is time to innovate a bit more instead of adding factions that are copy paste with unique units. Changing the aesthetic of units would be nice, since it is so off putting using knights that are all the same and whatnot. at least on this regard aoe 3 was so unique. I ve the premium edition with the box which i played for hundred and hundreds of hours back when it was released and when i was a teen.\t\t\t\t\t
