I remember playing the original on my iPad when I was around 10 ten years old and recently revisiting it in the past year. the unorthodox nature of the game just fascinated me, controlling two characters at a single time while each character having an almost equally significant role. me, and players alike of the original have always wondered what it would be like for the game to have strict co-op features similar to joseph fares’s other titles such as “a way out” and “it takes two”.

the remake delivers, the game marketed a co-op mode and it’s here. a fully functioning co-op mode, the game’s designed to be played by a single player but players do have the choice of either playing it single player or co-op. I can’t help but notice that the clunkiness of controlling two players as a single player is still somewhat evident. meanwhile, the co-op mode feels way smoother.

remote play, I wish it was present but it isn’t. I feel like there was definitely room to implement something similar to “friend’s pass” from it takes two or remote play in general via two players owning the game. granted, there’s the difference of different studios working on these games. however, surely the technology to implement this feature isn’t that complicated.

there’s also the argument of not enabling remote play as a choice since the game’s not designed to be played that way since co-op mode’s an additional feature granted on the behalf of the community. I can’t support this argument since this is a remake we’re talking about, I don’t think it’s unjust to say that these demands could have been met in 10 years.

again, the base price of this game is $20; $5 pricier than the original which is a very reasonable price to sell the remake at. I anticipated a much higher price, the developers had a clear opportunity to rip the audience but they didn’t. I strongly recommend brothers ‘24 to new players and players of the original alike. I wasn’t bereft of the impactful feeling the original notioned while playing the remake.\t\t\t\t\t
