\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved the original and saw Josef Fares's journey from Brothers a Tale of Two Sons, A Way Out, F the Oscars Declaration and the highly successful and critically acclaimed It Takes Two.
Josef Fares had nothing to do with the Brothers: A Tale of Two Sons Remake, this has been made by a different studio, he just signed the go-ahead and that's ok for someone to do it.

I 100% both of the games but unfortunately this is a massive letdown.

It's not a remake, it's barely even callable a remaster.

It's the same game with better graphics... but it just looks like it. All textures are upscaled and then covered by blur.
My beefy system and GPU is running vents at 100%.
Sometimes the Physics engine is bugged as I had a tortoise that wouldn't drop or struggled to slide down.

I truly feel there is nothing better in terms of gameplay and story than the original version.
I guess it's easier to do online co-op now cause of Steam Remote Play and being able to play with 2 controllers. Although I had issues with that, as he either had a black screen or no game world audio.

But if you play this single player it will take a long time to get on with the controls.

All in All, I recommend getting Brothers a Tale of Two Sons Remake only if you haven't played the original title, but if you already got the original, there is nothing new or better here.

As someone said,
The Most Unnecessary Remake Ever Made

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe remake is the same great game you remember, but rebuilt in Unreal Engine 5. Aside from a few quirks, such as brief texture pop-in and a few stutters, it looks great and plays well. The quirks only showed up in the beginning and seemed to sort itself out as the game went on.

The updated visuals enhance the game, and the re-worked cutscenes help sell the great story even more. Some cutscenes are 60fps while others are 30fps; a bit jarring, but maybe a patch can fix it along with the quirks.

I don't think you need to run out and buy it; playing the original still holds up and will work for most people. The remake is mainly for higher end PCs with NVIDIA 3000 series graphics cards or later (or AMD equivalent). I have an RTX 4070 and a 12th gen Intel i7 CPU, and at 1440p with high settings and DLSS set to Quality, the ran smooth at a locked 60 fps. I would also highly recommend using HDR if your monitor/TV supports it as the game looks noticeably better with it on.

The remake is nice to have, and if you own the original you get a discount. Whether you play this or the original, it's terrific and the story will stick with you long after playing.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original Brothers - A Tale of Two Sons is one of my favorite games. Despite the clunky controls, the rich art and music, vibrant world, beautiful story, and one of the most brilliant marriages of mechanics and narrative I can think of made the experience extremely memorable. Was it a game that particularly needed a remake? Not really. So how does the remake measure up? Overall, fine, though I wouldn't call it a straight upgrade. The environments and the lighting look gorgeous, but in some places they've lost some of the style of the original. The soundtrack, too, sounds wonderful independently, but in some places it sounds a little too grand for the story. The biggest detractor from the remake is the new character models, which don't really suit the game or the story. The best thing the remake did is improve mouse and keyboard controls, which work much better than they did in the original. I enjoyed my time with the remake, but overall I think the original was the superior experience--minus the frustrating mouse and keyboard controls of the original.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNah. The original is stylized and still looks amazing. This just lacks much in the way of an individual personality, and I find it tough to look at in motion with all the artifacting or whatever's going on with the graphics. Also the paths don't stand out as clearly during gameplay. Won't spoil, but there were also unnecessary changes to the way a key npc was introduced that make the environmental storytelling a bit weaker. But most crucially they changed the bittersweet, subtle, yearning background music to something happy and cinematic and oblivious. It feels much less immersive and iconic to me, it's a tone killer.

My opinions are from playing this level by level alternating with the original. I didn't get past two before I really started to dread playing the remake's levels, which is why I'm writing this. I'm still going to play the whole thing through because this is one of my favorite games of all time and I want to see what else is different, so I'll update if my opinion changes, but for now I'm disappointed overall and can't recommend the remake period.

Edit: Ok I finished it. Cons:
-More missing atmosphere, replaced with music (the music gets better).
-Glitchy animals messed with the vibe of two different sequences for me, and it was so hard to see the warning to avoid a certain hostile that I just assumed there wasn't one until I got to the same spot in the original.
-Multiple puzzles were made longer and/or harder to do. I found this engaging in some cases and frustrating in others; it's a con for me because one of them adds a whole cutscene to explain how to avoid messing up. No. Let me be careless, kill me, and restart the puzzle instantly please, it's just as effective as a lesson and doesn't make me watch the cutscene again.
-Glitchy shadows and models. I think they updated it to be a little easier to look at on Steam Deck but I still have a LOT of weird graphics nonsense.
-Further confusing layouts (why is everything so dark??). I am convinced the environments in the original just look better period, the visuals are more cohesive and appealing and particularly the water is much prettier. Whatever corners they cut work within their limitations; the remake just tends to look like hi def textures got slapped on for the sake of hi def (with the exception of a location at the very end which I found very impressive).
-Some minor to major changes that seem to be in the vein of "let's make it more realistic," overlooking the fact that this is clearly a fairytale world with unrealistic logic at every turn, there's no need to shoehorn in realism. The distinct footprints and foggy breath of an invisible creature of unknown size are WAY more visceral and terrifying than an obvious dusting of snow on its back and some obscured footprints that don't leave much of a trail, Because Realism.
-There's this very specific conflict towards the end that may take you a few tries to figure out (unlike me, who is definitely very good at video games and didn't forget how to play this part). In the remake, you are aided in this effort by a long, unskippable cutscene that wasn't in the original, which is there to tell you something the original didn't take nearly this long to convey (because the remake makes it deliberately more confusing for the sake of drama, thanks, I hate it; it does not have the desired effect my fourth time watching).
-Co op mode is a neat gimmick, but if you play it that way the first time, then (unless they've managed to account for this in some way I'm having difficulty imagining) I think you'll miss out on the game's most iconic moment.

Pros:
-Some extra voice bits got added in a spot where they were lacking in the original, I thought that was nice. There are some other little attentions to detail that prove some passion went into this.
-Extra animations in the cinematics with more dynamic angles. I found these mostly unnecessary and a little melodramatic at times, but they don't radically change anything and the ending applies them really effectively.
-The ending was easily the best part of the whole thing, I had exactly one minor gripe because of course I did, but there was also an altered sequence I liked so much I wish it was in the original.
-Still can't recommend it, but I don't regret buying for the fun of playing them both side by side and getting into my emotions about the story again.

tl;dr: Letdown. Play the original if you haven't. If you already have, play the original anyway, it's the superior experience.\t\t\t\t\t

A classic
This version looks gorgeous. Areas and characters look better than ever, new design elements were added to them, and some unvoiced characters have received one. Some scenes were remade, and those are a huge improvement!
...but there were some missing voice clips, slight graphical errors and the game even hitches above recommended hardware specs. Music is radically different, some hit a lot less hard during the most important scenes.

Would say that the final ~30 minutes of the original are much better, but the first ~2.5 hours are vastly better in the remake. The remake also added a few new cinematic events which I quite liked. These are only a few seconds long at most, and you most likely wouldn't notice unless you played both back-to-back. A lot of them are an improvement to the game.

If you have the hardware, play the remake. If not, the original is also arguably just as good. And again, the last two areas are much better there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis "remake" is not designed for Steam, Steam Controllers or co-op via Steam 👎🏿

I understand the game originally was not intended for co-op and i myself created and shared a very custom Steam Controller configuration for the original release back in 2013, which allowed co-op with two separate Steam controllers (or whatever you wanted to use), but with the freedom of assigned buttons, sticks, sensors, etc. This "remake" however does not have the same freedom as the original, due to it being specifically designed for stupid games console controllers only, thus hugely limiting co-op using Steam Controllers.

I will attempt to do their job (again) and create and share a Steam Controller configuration for this "remake", but meanwhile, it is not playable.

Not only did the developers do a bad job the controller methods, but also a lot of the graphical options do not seem to work well. As usual, Nvidia's DLSS helps a lot in keeping a steady 60fps (in HDR), but then anti-alias seems to be disabled, making it visually harsh and dated, but also ghosting trails, seen in the early days of DLSS.

This is not a full review, but should be enough to help others looking towards purchasing it, until i have more time to tweak the controllers, but also to get the attention of the the develops, as this great game requires more to actually be suitable for Steam, fans of the original and to actually be recommended as highly as the original.

...until then, it's a firm no from me 👎🏿

Side note: Cut scenes are over compressed and not in HDR, you can not have multiple saves (single player or co-op) and 21:9 is all over the place (tested in both 5K and 8K).
