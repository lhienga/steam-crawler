\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNever thought I'd see the day I'd be giving a thumbs down on a DLC for one of the Truck Sims. I've really tried to like this one but just can't. So what are the issues?

1. They completely revamped the lighting engine across the entire franchise and for the most part I feel it's a step backward. Colors are washed out and drab looking. Night time is so dark now it's a struggle to see anything. While tolerable, I'd much prefer they make this "new and improved" lighting engine a menu option.

2. Extremely narrow roads and turns (more accidents and getting stuck), Incredibly goofy, twisted, convoluted intersections and interchanges that look like they were designed by a 5 year old with a crayon and poster board and walled off freeways. If you miss a turn (which is really easy to do in this one) you'll often have to drive 100s of miles before you can turn around. No thanks.

3. Roundabouts, roundabouts, roundabouts. Seems you can't drive for 5 minutes without running into one. It's very annoying.

4. Toll booth, toll booths, toll booths. See number 3. You'll spend more time stopping at toll booths then driving in many places.

5. Clueless AI drivers. The AI drivers have never been great but with this release it seems they're even dumber. It's like they're on rails and don't even see you. More accidents and you always get faulted.

I bought this one mainly for the community event rewards but I just can't stomach finishing driving here. I'm out. Surely this is not representative of what things are really like in Spain. I honestly don't see any country could function with such a messed up infrastructure.

It's still a great game otherwise but sorry SCS you guys just missed the mark on this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: This is a review of the expansion as it stands on May 2nd, 2021. Given they've already redone Germany and Poland, added an entirely new duo of islands for us, and released three very good expansions since the launch date of the adjacent expansion, I have zero doubt parts of this may not age as well as others, but I can't review a product that's not there, can I? Conversely, provided I can remember to, I do intend to update this thoroughly when they have done an inevitable rework of this part of the trucksim.

It's been a very long time since I wished there was a third button for being on the fence on recommending a product on Steam where SCS is concerned- if I'm honest, it's almost exclusively been an uphill positive experience since the launch of ATS in 2016, and that's where that'd firmly last apply. No matter how many reasons I'd like to justify for the devs in any possible defense of how Iberia is, SCS has always released expansions when they're good and ready, of their own admission. That's the line, the standard we've come to expect, and I've seen them only up to go in quality since I got into ETS2 in late 2017, until now. Take a close look at the roads on Ibera, compare it to the rest of the map, and it's fairly clear they didn't give this one enough time to be up to the standards of ETS2 or ATS' (worked on by separate teams, of course) work of late. Road to the Baltic Sea was pretty spot on for the 1:19 scale the game's working with, and satisfying to drive through. Much as it pains me to admit it, in the state it's currently in, Iberia, especially outside of cities, honestly feels like I'm driving a good portion of the time on something that reminds me more of the more lifeless sections in ETS2 before the Germany reworks, at times borderline launch ATS (back in 2016 when the game was oh so very rough compared to now) than the rest of the map.

The accuracy of roads and connections is a bit off, especially in the southern region of Iberia. That's fine where scale just makes things too clustered, but if you look at an actual map of Spain's roads it wouldn't have been too hard to come up with suggestions on where to add more roads (sorely needed anyway, if I've not made that clear) to resolve some of this more accurately. Barcelona in the northernmost is an absolutely bizarre mess to navigate, for example. Now granted I play both modded and unmodded ETS2 and given that ProMods' version is... shall we say not dissimilar in many ways- I have to believe they were just trying to faithfully recreate the city with the scale at hand, and that's definitely one of the more distinctive areas- but so many of the routes to jobs there highlight a problem that exists fairly often specifically on the coasts of the driveable area.

In order to best accommodate the more recently added accident system where entire highway segments become impassible like in ATS, you get similar compounded issues like you did back in the rougher days of ATS due to this lack of highway loops that exist and could have been added. When you encounter those accidents you will have to go up to 350 miles out of your way at times (I'm talking worst case scenario here, it was more commonly 180-250 miles) because the sim decided 'obstruction here'. You can turn that feature off entirely, but the second I delve into that argument I think I can safely rest my case in the 'needed more time in the oven' department. This is a problem most of the rest of the ETS2 map doesn't have all that often, as it's fairly rich with side roads you can detour to in areas where these can happen. It's not a dealbreaker, but it emphasizes the undeniable dead endedness of a fair portion of the map- there are many, many dead end roads of fair length, most accommodating pickup/dropoff locations. And especially in the southern coast, a fair few of those could have easily been circumvented by actually finishing roads that exist in the real world, and for some reason just weren't connected here. Iberia is fraught with such roads, and I really hope SCS takes the time to finish it. As it is... it's not. It's much larger than France and despite having more real life major roads to draw from... has roughly half as many.

I'll leave much for muchness on the general lack of distinct new fabs outside of cities for a majority of the expansion area, because frankly, the roads are far greater a concern to me and the cities feel pretty distinct from the rest of the map, but some of the decisions they've made just leave me scratching my head- the Sierra mountain ranges near Madrid? Not really that pronounced. So much of Iberia's roads are on the coasts, the inland parts nearly feel neglected for loops and side roads. I'm still going to recommend it because this is still around double the amount of roads seen in ATS Colorado (in roughly six times the area, contrasting slightly smaller scale in ATS than ETS2, it should be noted), This isn't barren by any stretch... but it's also just not as much effort as they've put into equivalent areas (Istanbul springs to mind if you want a high bar on city density and making a city feel like a proper city, and only Madrid and Barcelona come casually close to being as impressive here).

Hopefully by the time they come around to the usual rough release date time for ETS2 expansions late in the year, it'll be in a much better place and they'll have finished what is by a fair margin their biggest area yet, with more roads and getting it much closer to the standard the rest of driveable Europe in it provides. Until then, be wary, for this one has some very rough edges and rarely, even roads that weren't properly aligned to each other (evident by the visible gaps in between road pieces). There's still plenty to love here, and it goes without saying, if you mod, you're going to get this anyway. Getting this is not buying a bad product, not by a long stretch, just one that could have been and I'm sure will eventually be a lot better and deserved more thought.\t\t\t\t\t
