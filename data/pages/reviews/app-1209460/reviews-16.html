\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWARNING: I'm portuguese, so there is a degree of bias on this review. I paid full price for this DLC, for patriotic reasons. Lo siento, España.
I recommend waiting for a really good price to buy this DLC. Get the cheapest map DLCs instead, as they're more varied and balanced.

Pros:
- The scenery is beautiful. They got the overall feel of everything just right.
- Nice level of detail (down to "Obrigado. Boa viagem" (Thank you. Have a nice trip.) after paying for a tollbooth, for example).
- Good sound design.
- There are places where you can park and get cinematic shots. I never thought I'd be into that, but they're cool.
- Sandy roads (the few that there are, leading to certain companies) have a nice feel to them, with the truck shaking around, slight loss of traction and controller vibration to make things more immersive.

Cons:
- Pretty much nothing but highways and cities on this DLC. Portugal (and Spain too, I would assume) has wonderful backroads that are often driven by trucks. Why weren't they featured at all?
- I know that Portugal's a small place, but it feels particularly tiny (I'm aware that countries are scaled down, so that's understandable to a degree).
- Portuguese cities are barely drivable, for some reason. Spanish cities have more driveable roads in them. Why is Lisbon barely featured at all?
- Where's Andorra? They missed an entire country. It's small, but not THAT small.

Minor complaints:
- Typos on certain signs ("Vianna", for example). The typo report process is way more complicated than what it needs to be, so I didn't report said typos.
- You can't rest on certain places where it feels like you should be able to (although I feel like that was an intentional decision, to keep things varied).
- Most roads appear to be in fantastic condition, which (sadly) isn't the case around here. I'd like to see some roads in worse condition.
- Apparently there should be more roads connecting France to Spain, according to certain comments I've read. Not a deal breaker, but still worth mentioning.

To sum it up: This DLC captures the overall feel of driving in Portugal and Spain, which is what I assume they were going for. I assume that foreigners will not mind most of its downsides. That's why I chose "Recommended" as an option. However, I feel like a significant portion of the experience was lost due to the absence of secondary roads (i.e. not highways).

This review may be edited at some point to add certain information, or correct mistakes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this DLC like a minute after it's release and now I have been playing around with the DLC for about 20 hours and I have completed the "Crusing Iberia Event" so now it's my time to write a review. Note I'm not from Iberia so if I make any mistakes, sorry :/

Pros:

- Beautifully detailed and probably the most realistic DLC to date

- This DLC really captures the mediterranean vibe, climate and architecture, my favourite area to drive in is Southern Spain, along the cost from Almería to Algeciras, that stretch of road is spectactular

- The cities are big and vast, note that as usual you only drive in the industrial areas and not in the city centres, you only see the city from a distance (which is meh)

- The road system is fun to drive around with and the intersections are actually pretty fun and provides enough of a challange

- I also like the new toll booths which are automated with cameras. It feels really nice to not have to stop everytime you have to pay a toll

Cons:

- For me I don't mind the consistent highway driving my biggest problem with "Road to the Black Sea" DLC was the amount of country roads and lack of highways. But in the Iberia DLC I'm having the exact opposite problem. There are too many highways, which again I don't mind but sometimes country roads provides a bit more challange and detail not seen from highways

- And then there's the city design. I get it, trucks often times drive in the industrial areas but why not have delivery missions into the city to shops or malls in the cities? Like it is in real life. The only time you *kinda* drive in the cities is when you drive to the harbors. Don't get me wrong the buildings and details that's there is amazing but this restriction to me is one of the weaker aspects of the game

- And then there's the road texture which looks the same in both Spain and Portugal. The only time the road texture switched was when I came across cracks in the roads in certain industrial areas. But nothing more. Sure I don't mind it but it just irritates me that there are only one road design the majority of the time. Again I'm not from this area of Europe so I don't know if the roads are like this irl or not.

So to sum up....

Iberia is the biggest DLC from SCS Software and I get why, it's technologically the best but design wise not the best. I would not recommend people who wants this as their first DLC to buy it, buy Scandinavia, Going East and Viva La France first since those DLC's are a must have. My favourite DLC is Italia just because there is a good balance of country roads and highways there. So I would prefer to buy Italia over Iberia. I had high hopes but I'm somewhat disappointed, more so than I was with "Road to the Black Sea". But Iberia is not the worst DLC. Only if they add more country roads, change the road textures and add some more downtown areas this DLC has the potential to be the best. They have a good foundation. I would recommend to buy on sale, but it's 100% worth your time!

Overall Rating: 6/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith around 2700 hours of cimbined experience in ETS2 and ATS, having bought every single map expansion of SCS on release, this one is the biggest disappointment for a variety of reasons.

First of all, good things first. The overall quality is standard SCS. It's absolutely not awful, it's a step forward in terms of visual fidelity and it captures Spanish landscapes and road layout very well. What I really do like are many of the new assets, sounds, plants and company logos aswell as billboards. The abundance of grafittis is also a welcome nuance.
I enjoyed quite a few of my trips I had completing the "Cruising Iberia" event and I would not tell any ETS2 fan "DO NOT BUY". My aim is purely at SCS with this.

Many have mentioned the big problems already: Too few rural roads, too much generic cities, no rememberance value, absolutely unintresting roads. Added to that I found quite a few severe problems with the AI behaviour. Being an experienced driver in the Sim I had quite a few crashes because the traffic just behaves awful on some of the new roads. I have seen odd banking in front of tollboots to the traffic approaching, some teleporting and also some trains doing the double track drift. There are some lights you just cant properly see when sitting at the line. Some lights are WAY too dim to make out during day light. Sleeping spots and fuelstations are WAY too rare. The GPS does an AWFUL job in annoncing necessary lane changes. Some company entrences are ridiciously narrow.

Quality control and the lack of intresting small roads is a great annoyance and I think the 18 bucks is certainly too much for what is delivered. I know you can do better, I know the pandemic hit the development, but this was NOT ready for release. Please, SCS, listen to your more critical fans. Less spaghetti trips, more connections, more memorable twist, turns and places to visit. This is all just a highway grind. It has it's plus because I can watch Youtube in the meantime, but it really doesn't want me to revisist Iberia. It's a relatively soulless adittion.

While at it I want do add, that the daylight tunnels are still a great annoyance considering we had an update to the lighting engine. Why are license plates not reflective, yet stickers on trailers are? So many other problems not related with the DLC, but in whole, I find many MORE important issues are not adressed besides expanding the map. YAY for going Iberia, nay for not having it as diverse as the Black Sea or France addition. You can do better SCS! Contact me for more import. MANY more easy possible changes I have to adress.\t\t\t\t\t
