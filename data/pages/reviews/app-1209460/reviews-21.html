\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIberia for me is the most disappointing expansion to date and a considerable step back from the wonderfully crafted Road to the Black Sea. Despite being a significantly large piece of content, this one feels very limited and rather uninspired.

Cities feel extremely bloated with so many unnecessary roads and rounderbouts, yet the rest of the map is dominated by long motorways. There is no countryside or scenic routes along the coast which is a shocking omission - you're either driving around convoluted cities or on bland motorways. There is nothing else to see and the overall design feels extremely dated.

If you're gagging for more grey to fill then this is still worth a purchase but I expected much more with this DLC. The map template feels very 2017 and the lack of secondary and coastal roads is quite frankly unbelievable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[UPDATE] Review after i've visited nearly every single city in the DLC and have driven down nearly every road.

Besides being an eye candy, looking awesome and stuff, it's more of the same at the end, more road, that's it. ETS2 has be foundamentally the same since it's release no matter how much more road they add into the game, visually the game is awesome, but the gameplay is untouched since the release. Ibeira lacks country roads, it's painfully obvious, there is also lack of gas stations, just the other day I went 500km without passing one gas station while running low on fuel. Trailer ownership is still halfway finished, if you want good jobs in Iberia without running empty from one city to another, get a curtainside trailer, everything else is "meh".

Bottom line: Iberia looks awesome, but it has one huge problem (country roads) and several small ones. At the end of the day, it's just more kilometers of roads. If you have millions of km in the game already, you have seen it all, Iberia is just more of the same old. If you are new to the game, Iberia is a must have.

For me it is the last DLC I have bought for ETS2. If we say ETS2 was Fiat Doblo at the release, visualy now it is a Ferrari, but it still has the engine of the Fiat. To me the engine is more important than the visuals, you can't have a Ferrari with an engine of Fiat Doblo, but you can have Fiat Doblo with an engine of Ferrari. ETS2 is at that state for me right now, if SCS isn't going to update the gameplay I'm not buying the next DLC.

Firstly I'll jump to the bad (not a lot but very noticeable) and then to the good.

The bad:
-connection to the Black Sea. Not having Balkan in the game hurts Iberia and Black Sea, two map expansion with high standards connected thru Austria and Hungary only, this wouldn't be a problem if the two countries would be reworked.
-Iberia makes all the other map expansions look like a joke, Black sea is probably the only DLC worth being mentioned in the same sentence as Iberia.
-Iberia shows what ETS2 could be like, but we will never get that, Germany rework is the proof of that, German motorways are awesome but as soon as you are off the motorway, Iberia is light years ahead of Germany. if I compare France to Iberia, France is very generic, all intersections are pretty much copy/paste, roads are not copy/paste but now we see how much better they could be and pre-release rework of France is... let's not talk about it.
-two lane one way roads, i just hope SCS will add more of those

The good:
this is what you are here for.
-Iberia is ETS3 standard, it's just something we've never seen before in ETS2
-usually I say ATS is the better game, now with Iberia I'd think twice before saying that
-it's the only map expansion of ETS2 you'll ever need, if you are new to the game, forget every other DLC.
-Iberia feels alive, the views, the roads, this is how world looks like from a cab of a truck (been there, done that)
Do you need more? Fine...
-generic is not a thing in Iberia, gas stations, intersections, cross roads, all unique
-tons of tunnels, bridges, long uphills, long downhills, flat straight roads, all in one map expansion
-pretty big

If by now this isn't a no-brainer for you then i don't what else to tell you. Oh maybe that, two weeks ago I decided to nearly stop playing SCS games, after 3200 hours in ETS2 you get to see everything, you know the game better than your own backyard and it just gets boring. So I wasn't sure if I should even get Iberia. Here I am now telling you to buy it.

TLDR; -this map expansion is the map expansion which will bring people who moved on to other games, back to ETS2.
-the only map expansion you really need\t\t\t\t\t

Spain is well know for its beautiful landscapes: mountains,hills,arid flat areas, Scottish like sceneries,etc. But when you drive on the roads you dont see that much.

the A2 Barcelona-Madrid. This road has been well put together until you reach Zaragoza. The arid deserty area is great. The section between Barcelona-LLeida is exactly how it is, but then when hitting the section between Zaragoza-Madrid it becomes inaccurat af. This roads consist of the most diverse sceneries. The road starts of flat and arid. After a while you'll climb and have a beatiful overview on the sistemica iberico. Then after medinaceli the road becomes less hilly and eventually flat. This has been made poorly by SCS. In dlc the road is a little hilly at Zaragoza and then mostly flat.

This goes for the:
- A44 Madrid-Granada
-A1 Burgos-Madrid
-A23 Sagunto - Zaragoza
-A3 Madrid-Valencia


too innacurate. I know that they work with a scale of 1:19 but atleast put the most beautiful parts in. I personally think that they rushed and just got the easier parts into the game.


Another thing: The lack of National roads. Spain is full with them. People will say that people complained about them in the previoys dlc, but the thing with the previous dlc is that Romania doesnt have a lot of 4 lane highways irl, so they got put them in there. Spain on the other hand has got both of them. It would give us more options. But after seeing some exits with city and villages names not being crossed out and seeing the construction of the roads, i think they will be added in a future update.


Overall the dlc is a beatiful one, the price is worth it.
Verdict: 7,5

-A lot to explore
-very diverse

cons
-Inaccuracy in some places
-Roads missing
-Bad optimaztion in certain cities (fps drops or lag spikes even)
