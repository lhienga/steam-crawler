\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell well well, Iberia is finally here and after forking out £13.49 for SCS's biggest DLC ever, I thought I'd have the goodwill to leave my thoughts.

So, I booted up the game and teleported to my garage in Montpellier ready for a drive into Sunny Spain. It started raining.

When I crossed the border I was distracted by the new scenery, looking at what I suspected were new assets and having a look around - unfortunately I encountered a maniac on the motorway which seemed a fair representation of what I'd experienced on the roads in Spain. What happened next however deeply concerned me. I collided with him and then flew for miles - somewhat discombobulated by an apparent lack of gravity. Spain appears to not be affected by physics as the rest of the map is; I smell a conspiracy.

When I arrived in Barcelona thanks to the ever-reliable F7 tow service that managed to dig me out of a river bed and transport my truck safely, I heard some background noise. It sounded at first like some traditional Iberian music and I was curious to see whether I was about to recieve an education on the local culture which would have been appreciated however I was left disappointed when the realisation dawned that it was probably just some drilling SFX that SCS decided to add...

As I continued my triumphant voyage down the peninsula, I arrived in Portugal where I was somewhat baffled by the lack of beaches in the area. This was sad to me because I couldn't test the game's law enforcement by peeing in the ocean - something that is only illegal in Portugal. Perhaps SCS could add such a feature as well as a couple of bottles by the pedals to truly simulate a life on the road?

Finally, when I reached the end of my trip I realised there had been no Andorra - this was either lazy by SCS or extremely clever. Why? Andorra is so small it's impossible to find in real life anyway. Perhaps SCS is really pushing simulation?

If it is, I've got to get down to not-so-sunny Spain as well as the beaches in Portugal to test the gravity, police as well as to try and find the elusive Andorra (when covid decides to go away). Iberia is clearly one of, if not THE, best expansion of ETS2 yet and it's already provided me with some interesting moments... definitely recommend, could be a 10/10 if someone confirms whether gravity exists in Spain.

Very strong work.\t\t\t\t\t
