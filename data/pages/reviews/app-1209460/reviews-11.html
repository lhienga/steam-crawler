I will give this map a 8/10 stars.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving purchased and played, this DLC for a while now. I have this to say about it.

Whilst the scenery is nice to start with it does become boring after a time. The Spanish toll booths are slightly too narrow, you can damage your truck and trailer, the worst one so far to take through is the Yacht. The new light box that they have incorporate into the game does not suit this DLC as in makes entry to some places extremely difficult especially at night, even with full beam on you can barely see where you are going as some of the alley ways are very tight and narrow.

Talking about the entry / exit points for delivery pickups and drop off's some are easy, some very difficult, there are some where you cannot get a double trailer out.

The road mapping needs sorting I lost count how times the line markings don't meet on turn off's, also rest points are hit and miss the same goes for truck maintenance bays, some places have them some don't, BE WARNED!!

My conclusion on this DLC it needs work and refinement, there is lot of scope for future development.

I recommend EUROTRUCK SIMULATOR 2, I do not recommend the Iberia DLC, so there you have it\t\t\t\t\t

What I think about:
  • Yes, it is mostly highways with modern infrastructure, elevated viaducts, wide and long tunnels, but the amount of environmental detail put into it outweighs it all.
  • Compared to the Black Sea DLC, Iberia is not only huge in size, it is much more interesting for a non-Iberian native like me, and fascinating to see with so many various landscapes.
  • I've never seen hills, mountains and fields so detailed as if every tree, bush and flower was planted by hand.
  • The desert in the southern part of Spain is truly a splendor to drive through and isn't boring at all in.
  • The latest lighting system works amazingly with the lights and colors, not to mention the realism put into the skybox.
  • GRAFFITIS! I don't remember any graffiti in the previous DLCs. Iberia adds more realism to the game giving us the true look of the locations you drive by. Simulation become reality.
  • Last but not least: VIEWPOINTS! This DLC has 15 of them I believe with one of the most important ones spread across the peninsula.

Some honest stuff to note:
  • I could not deny though that Portugal does seem to be small in size compared to what the Spanish road-network has to offer...
  • Roundabouts, roundabouts, roundabouts. Yes, they are very common in this DLC. But, compared to North and Central Europe, Southern Europe has an entire culture around roundabouts in reality. The game just mimics what's true.

PS: Please note that this comes from my experience playing the game at Ultra settings.\t\t\t\t\t
