\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter a week of playing I have my review clear and can say that in general I find the DLC somewhat disappointing. For someone who has been waiting for years to drive in Spain and Portugal in ETS 2 and the hype that came along beforehand, I give this DLC a 5 out of 10.

What struck me positively in the DLC:
- Driving in Iberia gives the feeling that you are really there. The environment is completely different from what I was used to in ETS 2 and that is a good sign because the landshape in Spain and Portugal is different than the rest of Europe. The environment in Iberia fits well with the environment where you drive at that moment. For example, when you are in the Basque Country, you will see many trees and hills, where Andalusia, for example, looks like a desert. These differences in landscape stimulate you to discover Iberia;
- A number of cities are well developed, including Barcelona, ‚Äã‚ÄãPorto and Malaga. You can recognize these cities by their great architecture and a number of well-known landmarks;
- The highways in Iberia are well developed and the well-known toll roads are also present;
- With the DLC it is also possible to discover shortclips that you encounter at certain points. These videos let you taste the atmosphere of a city or place;
- When driving through Iberia, you are tempted to take pictures of the area. The details are generally well worked out.

Now for the negatives:
- The entire Iberia DLC consists almost exclusively of highways. There are hardly any secondary roads and villages to be seen and that is unbelievably unfortunate. Especially compared to the previous DLCs, which scored well on this point. Also, when driving through Iberia, you will see a lot of cities on the roadsigns which are not present in the game;
- Above I mentioned a number of cities that have been worked out well, but most of the cities in Iberia are very disappointing in design, such as Madrid, Lisbon and Bilbao. I've lived in Madrid for a while and can't recognize the city at all in this DLC. The city center is missing and there are only industrial areas. Why not show some sights such as the Cuatro Torres or Santiago Bernabeu? And Lisbon is not recognizable either. Only the famous bridge and Jezus statue are present in Lisbon and this makes this famous city looks more like an average village. A missed opportunity from SCS;
- I do miss places like Andorra, Gibraltar and Mallorca in the DLC. It is true that you can still see the Rock of Gibraltar from a distance, but it's a shame we can't get there. Important cities such as San Sebastian, Braga, Alicante and Toledo are also missing. Another missed opportunity;
- I would also have liked to see more landmarks in general. Cities are often almost a copy of each other and I would have liked to see a little more authenticity;

I hope SCS will listen to the critics and improve Iberia somewhere in the future. Because this version isn't good enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tif steam had a "meh" recommendation, i would give iberia that. but it doesn't unfortunately.

while iberia has amazingly-made assets that were used to make the dlc, they can also benefit modders which can help a lot. the different landscapes that i have traversed thru so far were great. gave me a taste of spain and portugal virtually, which is amazing.

unfortunately, there are also flaws to this dlc. the signage is a pretty big one. some signs display "Seville" instead of "Sevilla". there also a lot of listed cities on some signs being crossed out with a red line. while they may not be accessible, this should not be happening. it is a slight immersion-breaker for me. another issue is how there are only 2 ways to enter or exit the dlc area apart from ferries. a route from pau to huesca via andorra would've been amazing. there's enough space for it i believe and i have heard that trucks use this route. also there were other small mistakes, like how no guardrails are present on the a-66 north of seville for example, which occurs sometimes irl.

overall, this dlc feels very rushed. a part of me enjoys it, while the other part wish it was worked on more. feels very rushed and rough. i know scs can do better. iberia has a lot of potential.\t\t\t\t\t
