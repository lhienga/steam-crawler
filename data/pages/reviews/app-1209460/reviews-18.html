\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm very conflicted about this one.

From a technical standpoint, it's an achievement. Once again SCS has surpassed itself and created a DLC that brings something new to the table, like many different assets tailored for that one specific location, signage as close to life as possible. And that's great.

But the creative and mapping direction changed with the Black Sea DLC. Black Sea brought numerous black spots, and sacrificed map representation for atmosphere. Which is fine and all, but there's a limit to that. Paris strikes the perfect balance between realism and artistic expression - its layout is very similar to the real thing, but locations are vague. And that's neat. But Madrid feels like a copy-paste circle with some buildings slapped in, yet that city is a maze of motorways, with the M50 being a diagonal route bypassing the mess. And that's absent.

What's also absent are C routes north of Barcelona. These are mountain routes that guide you towards Andorra and France, more importantly to the Tunnel du Puymorens and Toulouse via N20 and A66. This could be a scenic alternative to the A9-AP7 pass, pretty much bypassing the mountains. There's more than enough room for towns like Manresa and Vic, and some other scenic towns you might encounter as well.

So yeah, not a lot of options and liberty when it comes to route building. This makes the map very repetitive and tiring, and I want to choose between smooth motorways or rural backroads, especially on the A7-AP7 parallel from Barcelona down south. There's a non-tolled motorway running in parallel, transforming into a city circuit, later becoming a regular road, constantly changing shapes and sizes. But no, we got the boring road instead.

I like choices. I don't like it when my choices are taken away. I don't like it when someone tells me how I have to play a game, especially a game focused on the liberty of driving anywhere on the map.

Promods spoiled me.

PS: Please, not every speed limit in France changed from 90 to 80. Please fix, guys\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA DLC from SCS Software that provides a realistic feel of the Iberian landscapes and takes advantage of the recent improvements of Prism3D engine. If you take a drive from the North to South of Portugal, you'll be amazed about how well the vegetation and overall rural landscape transition was depicted.
After exploring every city in the map, I decided to change this review to point out that, in my view, Portugal didn't receive the due attention by the developers. The Portuguese landscapes and assets are nice, but when compared to Spain as a whole, Portugal felt very rushed and generic.
In the future, it'd be great to see some Portuguese cities depicted with greater detail; maybe this is the only "downside" I can point out. For instance, cities like Faro and Olhão do not exactly depict the true feel of the Algarve region and seem very generic. I have the impression that Spanish cities are depicted with greater detail than Portuguese ones, with the exception of Porto and Lisbon. Going from Porto to Vigo feels like a short walk around the corner, which should not be the case. Still, a pleasant drive is guaranteed for sure.
Kudos to the developers!\t\t\t\t\t
