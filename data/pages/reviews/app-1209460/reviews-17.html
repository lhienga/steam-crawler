\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter driving for roughly 15-20 hours in Iberia, I've gotten to see most of the peninsula. I have mixed feelings towards the area, and as many others have said, I wish I could give a "mixed" rating instead of a negative rating.

Iberia is a step forward and a step backward for SCS. In many ways, it's an improvement on previous DLC releases, with much more realistic-feeling map scaling (especially compared to base game areas), and much more variety in scenery, landscapes and textures. Despite this however, pretty much the entire area just feels like one big highway loop. There are almost no non-highway roads, which doesn't even make sense considering the giant gaps between the highways, the likes of which can't be found anywhere else on the map. If you compare it to France, with a plethora of B roads all over the country, not only providing alternative routes but also displaying tranquil little French towns and villages, full of character, then you can tell something is lacking. They are completely absent from Iberia, which feels more like a single highway with gradually changing scenery on the sides.

Even besides the highways, the in-game cities are also a step backwards, quite a big one at that. Gone are the easily traversible grid-like road networks from the base game, and now you have companies in the same city 100-200km away from each other, which can be a real pain, is unrealistic, and feels pointless and forced. I don't want to take a 300km trip from one city to the next, only to deliver the cargo, and then have to drive another 150km to another company in the same city to get more - it's just a huge inconvenience, and it has become a source of genuine frustration that I have only ever felt in this part of the map.

The design of the cities is also lacking, since almost all of them just feel like a giant industrial complex. The cities barely have any character besides a few sightseeing attractions - the rest is just typical industrial square, grey blocks. The opportunity to showcase beautiful Spanish and Portuguese architecture was missed.

I have enjoyed my time driving here and that's why it's difficult for me to give a negative review. The map does feel much more high quality and has much more diverse scenery than earlier DLCs, particularly the ones before Vive Le France. It adds variety to the game just by giving you more space to drive in. However, I think SCS have made a mistake in two major areas: making a "Iberian highway simulator" DLC instead of offering a more varied view of the country, and in also by making cities much more difficult and inconvenient to navigate.

TLDR: good graphics, bad gameplay!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be honest, it doesnt really feel right to give this DLC a negative review, and it will be the first time I downrate a ETS DLC. However, since we waited quite a bit for the release, I thought this DLC would be just perfect. And it is, indeed, very beautifully made in many parts, such the SPanish Border region in La Junquera and the Mediterranean area. They managed to create the feeling here and I really like many of these parts.

Nonetheless, the review is still negative due to some factors which I know SCS has delivered successfully in the past with other DLCs, such as ATS or also in RttBs, for example.

1. I am missing some back roads. I believe some of them will come with future updates, but I really think they are necessary for a country like Spain.
2. Some areas, as already mentioned, are really well done. Others, however, just feel a bit rushed. An example which I came across was the region of A6/A66 between Valladolid and Leon. In the game, it feels quite generic and I dont feel the immersion there. It is just a lot dryer with less vegetation in reality, and especially when heading towards León, the huge mountains appear in the background, which just did not happen at all in the DLC.
3. Talking about the mountains, especially in comparison with some ATS DLCs, the elevations dont really feel like real. For example, the highway around Teruel.
4. There are still some signage issues, which will probably be patched out. I am also sad that no signs exist at all like in real life when entering a new Spanish Autonomous Region. In Italy DLC, for example, they are existing.

I know that the scale makes it difficult to include everything into the game, which I can fully understand. Still, some parts are not quite represented according to reality when it comes to scale, so distances dont seem to be fully correct. For example, when entering Asturias from the south, about 70 km of highway have been completely omitted, not even some houses of the city of OVIEDO can be seen, which has the same size as Gijon and is the state capital. Consequently, I feel that Road to the Black Sea was more consistently detailed, and I am a bit dissapointed about the actual quality of the DLC, which varies a lot depending on where you are.

Maybe SCS will improve the DLC a bit more with future updates, and I will be happy to change the review to positive, since some the DLC has many parts that are just astonishing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have decided to leave a negative review for this DLC for a specified reason in my opinion, I had waited for a year and a half for Iberia, but I think it's a step backwards compared to previous DLCs for me.

In my opinion, cities with a garage that have at least one service, resting area and gas station is the most important thing for me because of accessibility reason.
In previous ETS2 cities before the Black Sea, every city with garage had service, resting area and gas station (in certain cities).
My complaint was started since Oregon DLC (In American Truck Simulator) when Bend is the very first city to feature a garage without a service station.
The complaint has to escalate further in Black Sea cities when more cities had a garage without a service station especially in Galați, Constanța and Ruse, if an accident happened, you will be forcing you to tow your truck to a nearby station with a hefty distance and time spent.
The complaint even further in 1.40 when truck dealers with service in Italy, Baltic, Black Sea and Iberia had removed the resting area (intentionally), forcing you to find a nearby resting area or even rest outside the city, and you might be getting fined for avoid sleeping violation.

However, the services, resting areas and gas stations are less common and inconvenient in Iberia due to complex road systems compared to previous DLCs. Córdoba, Almeria, Valencia and Coimbra had a garage but don't have a service station for accessibility, and also including Lisbon, the city had a garage and service, but the service is too far away from the garage. Truck dealers with service don't have a resting area as I mentioned before.

The Iberia needs to polish more service stations, resting areas and gas stations before I would recommend this DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, a solid DLC pack, but not as impressive as Black Sea or Scandinavia.

It captures the feel of Spain and Portugal pretty well on the whole, from the green forested areas in the north to the vast, sprawling desert-like terrain of the south. It isn't perfect - biggest flaw is that there is a distinct lack of secondary routes, all of the cities are connected by freeway only, and the cities themselves seem to be spread out over huge areas which can be somewhat frustrating.

Even the woefully ancient UK map has more secondary routes than Iberia DLC. I was disappointed that they did not implement a mountain passage over the Pyrenees. If you don't own Vive la France, you can get there by ferry from Plymouth, UK (also from Italy if you own that DLC).

In summary, if you play ETS2 regularly this is worth picking up, but bear in mind that this is not as impressive as Black Sea or Scandinavia, which both had more interesting secondary routes, terrain, and more detailed villages to drive through.\t\t\t\t\t

ETS:2 Iberia Review

Overall a solid DLC and one that took a long time to be released. The views and environment are breathtaking and very well made. It perfectly showcases the Iberian climate and adds some unique intersections and other assets.

Despite this there are very obvious flaws with this expansion that need to be mentioned.

Firstly I'd like to talk about some of the new roundabouts. I first encountered them in the city of Valladolid. They look cool, but the AI traffic just doesn't know how to drive them. The results are insane traffic jams and constant collisions. It has gotten so bad, I had to disable traffic just to get past the roundabout.

Secondly I have encountered some serious frame drops when driving in or along a city in Iberia. Normally my FPS sits around 144 and 150 FPS, but when driving in some cities in Iberia it drops all the way to 60 and 70 FPS. I think it needs some more optimisation.

Thirdly there are some new tollbooths. These have two separated booths for Trucks. But somehow cars still pass through them, which really bothers me.

Fourthly the DLC seriously lacks national roads. It pretty much only contains highways, which is a real step backwards compared to the Black Sea and Baltic Sea DLCs.

Lastly the cities included in the DLC are pretty small compared to cities from other DLCs. Even the big cities feel don't feel all that big, since they've been broken into smaller sections that are not very well connected due to the lack of secondary roads. Don't get me wrong, the cities look very nice, but the parts where you can actually drive are pretty small and sometimes disconnected.

Conclusion
Overall this is very big expansion to the Euro Truck Map and pretty well made and needed addition. It includes lot's of new intersections and captures the Iberian climate and environment almost perfectly. However the DLC pretty much only adds highways and pretty small cities that lack sufficient connections. I do think people that like to play Euro Truck Simulator a decent amount should buy this DLC, but only if you already have or are planning to buy Vive La France, since it is kinda required with this DLC.
And if like to drive on highways. If you're not into driving on highways constantly this DLC is not for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this is the first time I'm going to downrate a DLC for ETS2. (And if Steam offered a neutral rating, I probably would've chosen that).

It's not a bad DLC, but Iberia has one major downside for me; it's all pretty much exclusively highways. And while the Iberian highways are a pleasurable cruise, without any sharp corners and with reliable signage, it does get boring after a while. I've been cruising from and to 10 Iberian cities so far, and I haven't really had any secondary road (other than a few kilometers of dead-end secondary road to an industry). Looking at the roadmap, there's like 3 secondary roads in the southern part that are actually part of the road network and form a connection between cities. But apart from those few, it's highways, highways and even more highways. And that makes Iberia feel like a one-trick pony.

I know that Iberia released later than SCS originally had hoped, due to covid slowing down the development, so I do hope they'll be adding in some extra secondary roads in future patches. I'd love to be cruising some winding roads through small Spanish and Portugese villages.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing to be a bit controversial for a lot of people, but I think in terms of map design quality, Iberia is actually a huge step backwards compared to Black Sea.

My first trip from Toulouse to Barcelona felt great, nice hills and Barcelona itself had some interesting and complex roads - though I noticed it was all just industrial zones. As I kept on grinding out trips for the Exploring Iberia event (and it is a real grind), I started noticing everything was becoming very familiar. The sheer quantity is there, and Iberia is absolutely the largest expansion they have done in terms of sheer number of cities, but it's all just so damn generic. There are literally no rural back roads at all in the game, every single town and city is connected by motorways and apart from a handful of exceptions the industrial zones you are relegated to are all very much the same. I was expecting that there would be some of the windy hill roads and sleepy villages to pass through that I remember from when I've been to Spain, but this theme is completely absent.

It doesn't quite feel right giving this a not recommended review, as the expansion isn't exactly awful, and this is one of those ones where I wish there was a middle ground review. But Iberia feels like such a step backwards compared to the quality, fine details and variety of different routes in Black Sea. Many of my trips through Romania and Bulgaria are still memorable for me after more than a year, whereas I'd struggle to remember anything about the Iberia trips that I've literally just done.\t\t\t\t\t
