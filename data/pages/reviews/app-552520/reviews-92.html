Intro
Many people regard Far Cry 5 as a bad game. I think for new and casual players, it is alright: there is value in the exciting gunplay and beautiful scenery even though rapid despawning and frequent pop-ins can be annoying. But if you are critical about games, it is an mediocre game that you should steer away.

Stale Gameplay
Since Far Cry 3, the series has become a sandbox experience, where the game provides tools and means and challenges, and lets players approach objectives however they want. A good example is outpost taking, my favorite part of the series, where the players can take on it in many ways, such as direct combat (with various weapons), stealth, sniping, etc.

With the success of the predecessors, Far Cry 5 follows the same sandbox design and mechanics, but adds a few things: companions that aid you with various skills, a bigger and easier-to-access category of vehicles offering new tactics like bombing; and more side quests and activities such as auto-generated events, fishing and arcade (warp to other maps to complete objectives), though at the cost of being overwhelming at the start.

(Control of aircraft and wingsuit is secretly terrible, except you mitigate by enabling aircraft mouse control, but you can't with wingsuit.)

In the sense of being a sandbox like the predecessors, it is a step in the right direction as it adds more means, tools and challenges. But after Far Cry 3, 4 and Blood Dragon playing exactly the same, these surface-level changes are not enough since returned players can quickly realize they have already done the same thing three times. I guess if you’ve never played any Far Cry game, it is alright. Yet the series was in need of revamping its gameplay to prevent things from getting stale, but Ubisoft failed to deliver.

(Far Cry Primal is a bit different in gameplay for it largely focuses on repetitive material gathering and doesn’t have diverse gunplay as you only have three weapons.)

Fragmented Experience
The biggest change to the game is the mission structure, and it is not good - players must finish open-world content to accumulate main quest progression. Technically, there is a mission category called main quest, but most of them are just open-world content rebranded, and only a few are real scripted levels, like destroying the statue or escaping the bunker.

Far Cry series used to have these long, well-crafted and enjoyable scripted levels for main quest sequences, which are essential to the storytelling, character development and playing experience. With this change, they are gone in favor of players doing more open-world stuff, which are short, tasteless and hastily put together. Even for the few remaining scripted levels, they are short and you probably finish them right when you start to enjoy yourself.

I don’t think it is because they ran out of development time, but an ongoing trend for Ubisoft to replace handcrafted scripted level with generic open-world content, seeing other Ubisoft titles have the same change in design. It seems Ubisoft has become lazy and stopped caring about their games.

What’s worse, it is not just the scripted levels, but a large portion of missions in Far Cry 5 are short, even outpost taking somehow is briefer than Far Cry 4. I don’t know if many people notice, that many missions take just a bit longer, or even shorter than the time to travel there to finish. Essentially, players spend lots of time traversing whereas they should’ve spent more time in action, and this unreasonable time distribution makes the experience feel fragmented and dissatisfying. It is probably why a few players I talked to state the game feels weird yet can’t pinpoint why.

(Took me 32 hours to finish everything except collectibles and arcade in the base game, much shorter than 45 hours of Far Cry 4; there are lots of collectibles in Far Cry 5 to extend its playtime.)

Empty Story
I see the above two aspects as flaws, but the story is the severe blow: it is lazy and sloppy, and the first time I feel the scriptwriters were incompetent while being self-absorbed in writing the story.

An evident example is that, as the progression bar is full, players will be automatically teleported to the main quest, no matter where they are or what they are doing. It is so hasty and hilarious seeing players get kidnapped/OD’d/hypnotized alone in a car, in a chopper, during swimming, or flying a wingsuit, but that isn’t the worst part yet.

Far Cry 3 and 4 are great in storytelling. Take 4 as an example, there are complexities in villains and heroes to convey a story about there is no definitive black and white, whereas Far Cry 5 only has a blank story with unrelatable characters. With story time has already been cut short because of the change in mission structure, that precious time was dedicated to four villains talking some mumbo jumbos that make no sense. I don't know what those monologues are for, because they neither develop the characters nor convey any constructive message, and they aren't even relatable or intimidating to audiences. It seems to me they are just there to dial the edginess of villains to eleven, to cover the fact that there is no substance in character and story.

Heroes only exist to give missions. So, despite a much larger cast of characters than any predecessor, all of them combined have no more personality than a piece of wood, making the story impossible to empathize with. Yet somehow, scriptwriters were fixated on making a surprise at the end - they wrote a deus ex machina ending(s) which may be offensive to some people, to impress no one but themselves, because the story is so empty that no one cares.

Verdict
As a Far Cry fan, it is sad and disappointing to see a great series end up like this. It is probably a good example of why Ubisoft is getting less and less relevant in recent years: always picks the tried-and-tested safe route and lacks love, care and creativity.

For recommendations, Far Cry 3 and 4 are the best if you are interested in the series, or Blood Dragon for something shorter. I heard even the latest Far Cry 6 is much better than 5, though I have yet to play.\t\t\t\t\t
