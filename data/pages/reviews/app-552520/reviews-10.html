AI is terrible. Constant explosions, firefights, people constantly dying from animals. When driving they like to run into the middle of the road to get killed?? I don't understand how the AI is suppose to work in this game. This is Montana where everyone has a gun, a car and a fishing rod but whenever a wild animal appears, they just don't do anything and die. If the cult didn't exist, i wouldn't want to live there with all the animal attacks, random planes falling out of the sky and exploding and i certainly wouldn't want to be in a car with anyone since they just drive off the rode and hit anything and everything there is. I had the helicopter lady fly me to a destination once and my god did it take forever. She would just randomly slow down and or stop while flying. There was no rhyme or reason to the NPC, just udder chaos

Flying doesn't uncover the map?? One of the biggest things my brain pushes for, is to uncover the games map. Getting to explore is fun but when a lot of it just mountain ranges and a whole lot of empty, it will get irritating running around. But why make that decision to stop that from happening? If there is a location discovery, i will check it out, that's why im out there!

When doing anything such as hunting animals, especially when you shoot more than one, one of the carcasses will disappear when its out of LOS. It happens with enemies too. Reaping trucks, armed vehicles etc will pop up on the map and when you rush towards them, they will pop out of existence if you lose LOS. Towards the end of my run, everything would just pop out, right on screen. Why? The amount of vehicles alone that i commandeered or bought through the garage was a waste of time, knowing that it would just go poof when i would get out of it just to grab a perk mag. It make it extremely hard to try and do some of the achievement for planes because they will spawn in the air, day and night, but the moment you get up into the air, they rarely spawned. And when they do its a total crap shoot to shoot them down before the AI just decides to crash the plane themselves.

A small negative is the coloring of the icons. Red is bad guys, easy enough. But white is everything else. Even when i turned down the brightness a bit, the white icons were still impossible to see during the day time. And this is for everything that has a white icon. Good guys, animals, backpacks and sacks, etc. Just a simple option to change the colors would be immense, especially for people that have issues seeing.

Forced game mechanics can't be stopped and that's just crappy as hell. I started in Johns territory and when i took an outpost, i heard the call go out over the radio, followed by a message saying that i was marked. Then a swarm of peggies rolled through in trucks and literally popped out of the bushes that started a firefight. At first i thought this was cool as hell. This is how you keep things interesting. A mechanic that retaliates against the player to make things harder. But then i got shot with a "Bliss bullet" and i couldn't control my character. Then i realized that there was nothing i could do to stop it from happening and annoyed the crap out of me. What a stupid thing to implement.

Regions: One of the things that bugged me the most was how much it felt like i wasn't doing anything worth while. I know that the game says there are about 41 quests in each region. Sure that seems like a lot. But a bit of it is just small fetch quests with no meaning to the fight or to a cause in game. The entire thing felt hollow. We have this huge map that is filled with bad guys and when you take over a region, they are still there, roaming around and attacking people. Prisoner vans, supply trucks are all riding around like John, Faith and Jacob are still alive. Faiths region really gave me a headache because its the most brazen in end phase for her region. You kill her and take down all the chapels but all the bliss containers are still sitting around at gas stations. The barrels in the lake are still there and the flower gardens are everywhere. Where is the clean up quests to get rid of their stain on this map?? Why did no one bother to call me and tell me to grab a flame thrower and to burn them all down? To hit the reservoir to stop the bliss from continuous contamination of the water supply?? Why couldn't i blow up speaker trucks and tear down the cults flags and other stuff? This is something that happens in a lot of open world games and it makes it dumb. It always felt like a group of cultists with enough confidence could just take back the regions if they wanted to. All the guns and ammo and trucks and everything else they need is still sitting around for them to use.

Fighting the villains at the end phase. John was the only one that didn't piss me off. Why would you go through the trouble of getting me invested in taking these people down, just to have them die in dream like state?? It was irritating as all heck to have Faith float around while i pumped her full of bullets and once that was done all she had was a black eye and a busted lip...... LIKE WHAT???

Hunting and fishing felt the same: Hollow. Once you skin an animal or catch a fish, that's it, there's nothing else to do with them. Sure you could sell them but that's not really necessary. A big map with a lot of nothing to do felt like a waste. It also felt like an after thought to Ubi. Just make it big and throw in some animals, that should keep them busy for a while. Eyeroll

Companions: This was a step in the right direction but it was still terrible given that its AI. The only companion that was worth while was Jess only because she made you invisible to animals. But if you have two companions out with her, you were no longer visible. Ntm the many many times i had given them an order to attack someone and they would just sit/stand still, or run away?? And the times where you didn't need them to shoot, they would just shoot randomly. I like Hurk but the moment he started shooting his rocket launcher that killed me, i tossed him out. How absolutely annoying.

Then there's the story and villains. Im not sorry, they were terrible. You got caught out 7 or 8 times by them and you escaped every single time. All 4 of them are the very definition incompetent. I know this is a game but for the love of your god, can we have smart villains?! They were always gonna die when they just spew some dumb god logic instead of putting a bullet in you and carrying on with life. Its also getting old with the one man army, mary-sue, 80's action hero trope. Why the f can i not get help?? And im not talking about the companions here. A full fledged cult took over a huge chunk of Montana, somehow took out all the roads leading in and out of the area and took over the skies, but when it comes to actually fighting them, its up to me and only me?? I don't understand how we can't have a game where the AI are there with you on every mission. OG COD did this and that was like 20 years ago. STOP THIS ALREADY

Then there's the Marshal. Ubisoft is really gonna convince me that they had a US Marshal show up to arrest a high value target, get captured and no one bats an eye outside of the map?? No one can convince me that even when hopped up on bliss and made to call his HQ to tell them whatever(They never showed him doing that at all btw), that no one bothered coming to check things out? Lazy

Also the ending is just stupid. The man preaches about god, tells you everything is your fault and then detonated nukes?? Yea sure buddy, your god needs those eyeroll\t\t\t\t\t
