\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFar Cry 5 is a game of highs and lows. It has an incredibly memorable campaign and characters, but is also so frustrating, rigid, and nihilistic that I honestly cannot say if it is good or not.
The core gameplay is peak Far Cry where all of the innovations from the previous titles work together to make combat fun and engaging. Stealth and going Rambo are both viable options to take down most levels and outposts with plenty of equipment and skills to help you play how you want. This also leads to one of the games greatest flaws in that unlike 3, 4 and Primal where there was an XP system, in 5 you have to do actions to unlock skill points. This involves playing certain ways that might not align with your style in order to get ahead such as getting X amount of flame thrower skills even though you want to stealth, hunt X amount of specific animals, take x amount of drugs, etc. They probably implemented this to try and get the player to try out all the different weapons and activities but instead it just hampers the fun if you like to play a very specific way since you get penalized for it.

Montana is a cool if nonsensical setting for Far Cry, taking place in a rural American county under siege by an apocalyptic Christian cult that has cut off all access to the outside world. Apparently if you listen to the radio and read some notes it explains that the US and the world at large is in the middle of huge political upheaval and that is why no one outside comes to help but it is still a pretty dumb excuse that you have to actually hunt for and destroys the suspension of disbelief.

Far Cry 5 takes the popular story dynamics of 3 and 4 and ups them to 11 but in my opinion fumbled it badly. 3 and 4 were known for their strong antagonists with extremely memorable dialogue. Vaas and Pagan Min were equal parts charismatic, humorous, and intimidating. However you only meet Vaas a few times in person in the game and Pagan Min is only really communicated with on a walkie talkie the entire game. To try and one up this in 5 they introduce 4 antagonists being the Seed family with the leader Joseph and his siblings. To circumvent the lack of face to face interaction the players had in previous title, the Seed family will abduct you at certain times in the game so they can have a scene with the player. It is incredibly poorly done being that you can be in the middle of a mission and suddenly you black out so that one of the Seeds can just insult and torture you for a few minutes to show off how evil they are. Unlike previous titles, the player character does not have a name and does not talk. This makes the scenes even worse as the "Rookie" goes through worse scenarios than even Jason Brody and Ajay but cannot say a single thing when the Seeds go on their annoying diatribes about the apocalypse, sin, natural selection, etc. The game basically makes the Seed family these omnipotent demonic entities that can kidnap you at any time just to let you go and insult you until next time. On top of that the game ends on such a terrible nihilistic note that it makes you wonder why you even bothered to play the game to begin with. New Dawn, the sequel to 5 makes it a little easier to stomach but barely and it still by far the worse story for a Far Cry game in my opinion.

TLDR: 5 is a game with clashing gameplay mechanics and a story that can be annoying and hard to sit through. The core mechanics however, are better than they have ever been and the game can be extremely fun if not a little easy when played coop. Just be aware that if you do play coop, none of the coop partners progress carries over to their solo game.
