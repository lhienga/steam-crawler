\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is probably fine in single-player, but it advertises itself as having "Online Co-Op", which is borderline false advertising. Co-op exists, but it may as well not be there, having the most "tacked on at the last second so they could add a co-op store tag" version of multiplayer I've ever seen in a game before.

The non-host players are locked-out of pretty much everything. As a non-host player, you can't obtain any of the story, event, or challenge unlocked weapons, skills, or upgrades, including additional weapon slots and health upgrades past the first tier, so the co-op players are forever gimped on weapons, health, and ammo.

You also can't use in-game companions, so you're locked out of a large number of challenges and perk points. (Though, to be fair, you don't need these points because you're also locked out of all the skills and perks that you'd want to spend them on.)

You also can't interact with most objects in the game world. You can't talk to NPCs to obtain map updates or side quests, interact with anything that would progress a quest or sidequest, nor loot any of the special alternate currency that's used to purchase weapons and upgrades.

Not only that, the game doesn't save any story progress for the co-op player, so if you want to progress your character at all past the newbie stage, you have to replay all the content over in single-player mode (or host your own multiplayer game) starting from the beginning and unlock them there.

By the time we'd finished the first of three boss encounters, we realized the co-op players were essentially as maxed out as they could ever be, wouldn't ever be able to unlock anything else, and were going to be stuck in "newbie mode" forever. At this point, we lost the desire to play and all quit.

If you're interested in buying this to play with your friends, pass on it.\t\t\t\t\t
