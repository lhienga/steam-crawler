\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProtest downvote due to the ending. Spoilers ahead after the first paragraph, be warned.

Game itself is fine, really enjoyed flying, driving, running, shooting and looting all through the first Far Cry countryside that isn't in the tropics (because that was getting old). Missions are fine, sidequests fine, plenty of stuff to do and different ways to do it. You can smash through most main areas fairly fast by following the plot quests which give hefty Freedom points, or you can take longer by grinding out the side missions and taking strongholds/killing convoys. Fine. All solid and good enough. The perk system is a bit of a pain because I don't want to be forced to kill X enemies with every single weapon type in the game just to get my skill points, but whatever.

But the story. Sweet Jesus H. Christ the story. It's *bad*.

3 areas. Each area has 4 'chapters'. So 12 chapters in total. In pretty much every single one you get magically kidnapped by being shot with drug bullets, tranq arrows, or just get super stoned and fall over all by yourself, *even when you're 2000ft in the air flying a plane*. Then you're taken to the boss of that area, stuff happens, you escape. This happens 12 times. 12. I kid you not.

And the story itself is the kind of thing that weird IQ 80 incels will think "Aww man, this is so deep" but actually offers nothing worthwhile. It's just the usual brutality-porn clad in am-dram Bible clothes. I tried to watch the cutscenes in the first area but after the first 3 chapters I just mashed Esc all the way through the rest of the game and didn't feel like I missed out.

And the ending. Urgh. Here, listen closely:

- If you tell a story, and that story ends with "Rocks fall, everyone dies" then that's a *bad ending*.
- If you tell a story about a hero spending 26 hours of game time fighting to the end of a long campaign to defeat a villain, and the only alternative to "rocks fall" is to *not* defeat the villain, and just walk away to leave him to continue slaughtering innocent people, that's a *bad ending*.
- Any story that ends with no resolution, or with both options being negative is a *bad ending*.

There is a *reason* that none of the classic great stories that have survived for millennia feature endings like these. Because they suck. Do better. Normally in games like these I'll finish the story and then go back in and wrap up some more quests or collectables or whatever until I get bored, but that ending has killed any desire to do so because it's utterly pointless.

So if you want to spend ~30 hours messing around in the countryside, go right ahead. Get it on sale and it's value for money, no doubt. Just don't invest anything at all in the plot, and only do the final mission when you're happy to put the game down for a long long time.\t\t\t\t\t

I was wondering how Ubisoft would top Far Cry 3 and 4, but they did so in my opinion with villain Joseph Seed the companies' greatest ever creation to date. The game feels like three games in one as you must first defeat Joseph's two brothers and one sister before you get your final showdown with him. The gunplay is awesome with a wide array of guns available alongside multiple accessories such as grenades and molotovs. The twist ending solidifies this game as an all-time great in my book.
