\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a first-person shooter, the flow of the action in this game is oddly...relaxing. Traipse across the countryside and you're bound to run into cultists or wildlife you can engage in combat if you want, or probably avoid if you don't want to. Blow away some cultists? Give it a minute, and a few more are bound to show up, just to keep things from being boring. Run over to intercept those guys, then down the road you see a cult van with a hostage, then you hop on an abandoned quad to chase that van down, and along the way you run into a cult technical, get into a big firefight in front of a gas station loaded with cultists, hop behind the spades of the machine gun in the back of that truck to hose down the cultists at the gas station. Meanwhile, during this time, two more cult trucks have randomly meandered on through and joined in the fight, along with a fuel truck, a combine and, for some reason, a grizzly bear. When everything nearby is dead and you realize that the longer you stand here in the back of the truck, the more Peggies are gonna show up, so you split off into the brush, and forget why you headed off there in the first place, or even what mission you were actually planning on doing to begin with. Good times.

One downside, though, is the fact that as you commit all this mayhem against the cult, you'll steadily rack up "resistance points," a metric by which the game tracks how much of a pain in the local cult leader's butt you're being, and once you hit a certain threshold, they'll send hit squads after you or otherwise subdue you in some inescapable fashion. At which point you're whisked away so the game can force some plot on you in the form of a mission of sorts that you can't avoid and must complete before you get back to what you want to do. It's almost like the game is periodically punishing you for "having fun wrong." However, if you can just accept these periodic interruptions, it's not so bad. When the game railroads you into following the actual story and the screen blurs out to lead you to the inevitable cut scene, just remember "This, too, shall pass." And you'll be back to wrecking the cult again or fishing or completing Clutch Nixon missions or whatever again in no time.\t\t\t\t\t

The story is not that engaging or deep and without a voiced protagonist with an actual personality a lot of the plot elements do not land. Especially with certain reveals and implications. I do not like the Montana setting as not only does the open-world poorly capitalize on it, I think it does not fit the style of game this is. I do not like a lot of the side missions as it's just looting, freeing camps, or doing unremarkable quests for unremarkable characters. I can only name a handful of characters I found interesting.

It's not all negative cause some parts of the map looks gorgeous, this game can be fun at times, and the gunplay is quite excellent. It does have a nice co-op system so you can easily play with the bois (or girls I'm not sexist). The soundtrack is actually decent too and fits the game. Otherwise, there is not much I connected with or liked in this game. I genuinely do not get why the people I've talked to adore this game but hats off if you liked it. Overall: 5/10. Wouldn't recommend.

I think the games that do similar things to this but way better is Red Dead Redemption 2 with its setting, story, hunting mechanics, attention to detail, and a lot more I could list, and Spider-Man 2018 for doing an Ubisoft style open-world game like this but far superior.\t\t\t\t\t
