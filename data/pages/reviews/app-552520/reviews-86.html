\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Sneed family apocalypse cult vs Waco County police department

What is essentially Farcry 3 with some minor improvements and flair here and there; in other words this is Farcry 3.5. Mechanically and gameplay wise it does feel like the most well designed of the Farcry games, but there aren't any surprises here. You'll roam about the countryside, doing side quests and liberating outposts; hunting for money to buy guns etc, etc.

The setting and the characters are the most interesting parts of the game by far. Though the game's statements are somewhat (understandably) superficial, there are things that it's trying to say. Joseph and the cult try over and over to forcefully convert the player in the same way they've been indoctrinated. And they'll go to extreme lengths to justify themselves and keep their moral superiority, all the while purging the countryside in rural America. It's a bit spicy of a premise, but in practice, the game waters it down quite a bit. The most cogent theme of the game I could muster is man's ever-burning desire for simple solutions to complicated problems. Joseph builds the rock of his cult on giving people simple solutions and a reason to lash out against the world.

It's a fun time. There are always going to be spots that could use more polish in an open world game like this. I was starting to get a bit tired of the game around the 20 hour mark. Though the gun roster isn't expansive it's fine enough. Mostly by the end my pattern recognising brain was ailing. These cultist types love their big coats, beards, scraggly white sweaters, and stealing every possible kind of vehicle in the county. You also get captured 20 to 30 times during the game. All of this with a factory model silent protagonist.

Overall, it does what it does pretty well, ending notwithstanding. It certainly is one of the games of all time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFar Cry 5 is considered by many to be the last good Far Cry game. I agree. Overall I think this is a worthy entry in the FC series and worth a purchase (especially on sale which it seems to be often). However, in many ways I don't think it lives up to FC3 and FC4. Like all FC games it has its' issues.

Where FC5 succeeds:
This is the first FC game that felt "next-gen". It's a significant graphic and performance upgrade vs FC3 and FC4. It played well on my PC minus a few non-game breaking bugs. Hope County looks really good.

The music is great. Has some of the best music in the franchise if not the best.

The sandbox part of this game is much more expansive and open compared to previous entries. Tons of weapons, vehicles, and allies you can use. However, this leads into the bad parts of this game.

Where FC5 fails:
FC5 has an open mission structure. This means you can approach one of three main regions in any order to progress the story. From a sandbox perspective this might be good, but it breaks the game up and really hurts the story.

Speaking of story, it's...ok. The story itself is interesting especially if you dig deeper into it. However, it really fails in its presentation. All three regions basically capture you (several times each) to get to the next major plot point. The Deputy must be the luckiest person alive, the player, not so much. It makes the villains seem incompetent.

What is truly incompetent is the enemy AI. They aren't the worse in the franchise but they aren't improved from older titles. The difficulty in this game is odd. If you play on the hardest difficulty available at the start, the enemies hit really hard. This makes the few scripted sequences frustrating since you don't have control. However, due to how open the game is it doesn't matter how hard the enemies hit. With certain companions and weapons you basically play on easy mode.

The vehicles controls on m&k are pretty bad, especially the planes. This isn't unique to FC5 but it has to be mentioned.

A quick note on the DLC:
I really disliked the Mars DLC. It was a slog to get through. However, if you beat it before starting the main game, you'll get some Mars weapons right off the bat which makes you basically unstoppable.

The zombie DLC was also pretty bad. Just completely forgettable. I actually forgot this game had it until I looked it up.

The Vietnam War DLC is the best of the bunch, but not by much. It's very linear but at least it has some replay value. The issue with this DLC is you can just run straight to the end and beat it. I beat it in around half an hour on the hardest difficulty.\t\t\t\t\t
