\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has some problems that tend to be problems for all the games in the Far Cry series, but I'd probably put it as being as good as 3 and 4 in the series. I really like the villain and the fact that it takes place in Montana is both preposterous and intriguing. I suppose it's a more interesting location to have a Far Cry game than another uninhabited third world country, but all the same the premise makes no sense. Only after killing thousands of people does the plot give the option of walking away and calling the national guard, which would be the logical solution since you're playing as a police officer to begin with and there's nothing stopping the character from leaving and doing this.

Other than that, this game feels a lot less "checklisty" than the other Far Cry games. One stupid thing they do is group all of the missions into one big clump of 40+ missions in each region. You can't just check and see which missions you're missing. It's not necessary to complete all of them since you're only trying to fill up a resistance bar to force a confrontation anyway.

If you play this game on hard, the difficulty is all over the place. Most segments are easy but some are insanely hard. In particular the driving sequence at the start of the game is almost impossible. You're supposed to be firing at enemies while the car jerks all over the place. Then you have to fling dynamite sticks that need to be perfectly timed to blow up vehicles. Once you get to the open world stuff, the game is easy. You can still use an item to detect all the enemies and you also get a companion dog that can do this for you.

There is so much to this game that it's hard to really write a review under several pages, but some of the highlights are:

1. The use of aerial combat adding a whole new dimension to the game play
2. Tons and tons of side content
3. Each of the nine companions plays totally differently. One of them will actually call in an aerial strike for you, another will deliver a helicopter that you can take from that region
4. Lots of mission variety, objectives vary so much that the game never feels repetive. Even the fishing minigame is fun.

I only have a few nitpicks with this game. The variation in difficulty is really annoying since the game doesn't gradually get harder. It gets easier as you pick up the different perks the game gives you. Also, your teammates are supposed to revive you if you die but sometimes you just die even if they can revive you and I don't know why. Far Cry: New Dawn had a better teammate system, but it is cool that this game lets you have two different teammates at a time. It really makes the game feel tactical even though it's too easy for it to ever require actual strategies.

If you beat this game you can play through a new game + but you still have your perks so it's even easier on a second play through. Also, Jacob Seed's missions are very repetitive. Other than that, it's a contender for the best game in the Far Cry series and I love the nihilistic twist ending as well.\t\t\t\t\t
