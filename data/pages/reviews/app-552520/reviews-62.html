In a few more words. Some fun is indeed here to be had in that - turning your brain off and playing on auto pilot - kind of way. Graphically it looks okey-dokey though the colour scheme is mostly a drab blur with a touch of green. The setting is righty-ho though I never knew the countryside in fictional America was as densely populated as downtown Tokyo and with more Pickup Trucks per capita than the average Trump rally. The gunplay and weapon variety is just peachy, though largely pointless, because you are handed the best weapons right off the bat if you get the edition of the game with all DLC.

The thoroughly overused Ubisoft sandbox (litter box?) is, as usual, as wide as an ocean and as deep as a puddle. Full of inconsequential busy work that is constantly interrupted by hicks riding around in their pickup trucks, eating crackers and giving each other blow-jobs, insulted by you interrupting them. You are constantly assaulted by cultists, battle music, cougars, quest markers, side objectives, calls from your allies, etc. never letting you breathe or take the world in. This I assume is on purpose, since the developers obviously think of their customers as ADHD riddled 2 year olds who will immediately lose interest in the game, the second the key jangling stops.

Usually a game company creates a great story with great protagonists or antagonists to complement the "litter box" they have created, but that too is questionable here in Far Cry Mambo no. 5 edition.  

The biggest eye roll for me (as in my eyes literally poping out of my skull) is this franchise's obsessive compulsive need to oversell their main antagonists in face to face meetings with the player. You are, as usual, forced into multiple scripted and unavoidable cut-scene encounters with them, (through the use of magic .50 cal bliss bullets, invisible unbeatable hunters or just bliss drugs) where they pathetically spew their sewage level exposition garbage straight to your face. "OMG I look so cool when I say this Bible verse, don't I??" "I am so quirky and unique, if you leave me, you will never find another antagonist like me!!" "Colour tinted glasses, never used before by a villain, am I right?? So radical" "Ahh lol, you totally thought you were gonna die here right.. riiiight??, But I will give you a mentally deficient, contrived way out, because I am both stupid and because the plot demands it." - You know, the 'Hot Topic' plastic junk "dialogue" from Wish dot com.

Even after having met the antagonists 3x3 times, I forget their names - was.. was one of them José Dweeb? I dunno, the game doesn't even sell my main allies well enough for me to care to remember them, or the people I am supposed to rescue. One exception is Boomer, because he is a good boy <3.

AAAAnnnd.. Enough.. 

** Edit after completed playthrough - Momma didn't raise no quitter (TM)

I stand by every word in the review, though there is one thing I need to add after having completed the game. I will not spoil the ending(s) as you should experience them yourself, though I will say that the level of contrived BS is off the charts with either, no matter what you "choose" to do. Much like the game, all in all, it is an exercise in futility.

My biggest gripe, however, is the overall lack of agency you have as a player, the constant forced rail roading where the game takes you out of the game and forces you to sit back watch predetermined cutscenes, all of whom are connected to a lack of meaningful chioces with real impact, which is rooted both in all endings of the game and of the narrative itself. Everything is predetermined, there is no way as a player to impact the narrative. Any game, that can claim to be great or just good, has exactly ONE or maybe two instances where agency is stolen completely from the player and we are forced into situations that we have no chance or choice to fight our way out of. This game commits this gaming sin more than a dozen times and all of them are contrived beyond belief. For this sin, and others, I hereby forswear the FC series and it is the last FC game that I will ever buy.

Play the game for the missions and the gun-play and whatever else fun you can find in the litterbox, but for the narrative, just watch an abridged Let'sPlay.  \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tForced sh-tty "narrative" content that interrupts you at random times regardless of what you are doing. Doing a puzzle? F-ck you, have a forced walking sim "story" moment, all your progress in the puzzle is gone and you get respawned halfway across the map. Hunting animals to unlock gear? F-ck you, have a cringe cutscene and go spawn in another random place halfway across the map. And so on, on repeat. The most glaring issue of this game is that it does not, at-f-cking-all respect your time and your choices. So much for a fun open world sandbox where you can approach things at your own pace.

Aside from that, all of the issues sice Far Cry 3 remain - cringe characters, story written by angsty teenagers - this time by Timmy who did not want to go to Church with his parents on Sunday morning (previously, we had one by Billy who did not want to go to a summer vacation with his parents and Raj whose mother cooks crab rangoon way too often for his liking), mediocre gunplay and combat, bad AI, repeated technical bugs even after several patches, and so on.

Far Cry 3 was a good game. Not great, but good. Far Cry 4 was good, but it was slipping towards mediocrity as it again had the same issues and it was also largely just a reskin of 3. 5 is again the same thing - a reskin with only minor changes. If you are going to charge AAA price for a game + extra for DLCs + microtransactions, you better deliver AAA quality, or something groundbreaking. Far Cry 5 has neither AAA quality, nor does it bring anything groundbreaking - or rather, the only groundbreaking thing it brings is groundbreaking levels of frustration as your gameplay gets interrupted in the worst possible moments by the least interesting story in the series so far. If they wanted to put bigger focus on their narrative, they should have made a linear narrative driven game, instead of f-cking up the only somewhat fun part of the game - the open world activities.\t\t\t\t\t
