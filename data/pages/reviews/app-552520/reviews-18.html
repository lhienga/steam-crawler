\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComplicated. In brief, the game is very dumb but it also rocks.
The core loop of the game is fun and the graphics (in 2024) have aged pretty good. FC5 is a first person shooter and doesn't seem to want to be much more than that. Most of what you will be doing is going to a place and shooting some things, cool. The stashes provide some interesting puzzles but require finding and talking to very boring paper cutout NPC's to get the locations. There are a variety of weapons and vehicles but they don't feel very distinct and are mostly forgettable. I ended up picking my favorite 3 and never looking back. The Arcade mode is genius and the editor will keep me coming back to FC5 for awhile probably.
The worst part of FC5 and almost a game breaker is the story PRESENTATION. Now it should have been good, the writers had a great idea truly. The presentation had me yelling at my computer in frustration though. Without too many spoilers there are 12+ moments in the game in which control of your character is taken and you have to endure the most unforgivable and unnecessary railroading I have ever seen. I ended up skipping a majority of the "story" moments as they were generally pretty cringe, eliminated player choice and made such terrible assumptions about the my gameplay that I somehow felt like the designers were insulting me.
But you should probably play it, especially if its on-sale.
One more kinda side point, near the end of the game I was looking for the DLC as it hadn't come up yet and was quite surprised to realize they are just completely separate from the game and accessed through a different menu. It's just another opportunity lost to do some organic storytelling by Ubisoft.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFar Cry 5 is a fun, silly, action-packed open world game that I can launch anytime and have fun without having to relearn the mechanics or the input bindings of the game. The world is huge while also feel very much alive, peaceful yet chaotic at the same time. The landscapes always amaze me. It's majestic, immersive, almost feel like home; aside from occasional dead bodies lying around.

One feature that always keep me coming back to the game and that is companionship. Each has their own perk and unique personality as a character. The animal companions are the fun ones. You get to choose a scout dog, stealth killing mountain lion and a diabetic obese grizzly BEAR?! I couldn't ask for more! The pet button is essential. I also love the soundtrack that plays during loading screen with lovely imagery for each chapter/region you're in. It's a very beautiful touch, I just couldn't get enough of it.

The story doesn't quite hold up to scrutiny but is exotic enough to make it quite an experience. The test of will during story where you are abducted and forced to play through a mini-game with no option of skipping is what irritated me the most about the game, especially when it comes to race against the clock.

I've got more than enough hours worth of entertainment as a casual player and will continue to play it. For $9 on sale, the game is definitely worth looking into.
