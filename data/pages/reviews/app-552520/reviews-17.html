\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFar Cry 5
As a person who played through Far Cry 1 to Far Cry New Dawn I can say that this series had it's own falls and rises. And Far Cry 5 stays somewhere in the middle. Anyway, let's get to the pros and cons.

Pros:
+ Cool partner system.
+ Decent story. It was interesting at the start, boring in the middle and cool at the end.
+ Stashes with cash are mostly interesting to solve.
+ Clutch Nixon's challenges are really cool to do especially with a friend.
+ Planes and Helicopters are really fun and good addition to the game.

Cons:
- There are some funny additional missions but most of them are boring.
- Most of the additional and even main characters feel poorly written and not interesting.
- It's mostly a game with the same mechanics and I don't mind it in some way but why remove good mechanics? For example, you don't need to hunt animals anymore because they only give you money for their skins and not an upgraded holster, etc like in previous parts.
- If you play co-op your friend will not complete the story on their character which is some straight ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ because my friend literally couldn't level up skills because the requirement is to kill a boss who we killed but it counts only for the host and he couldn't even buy guns because in his story he didn't reach a resistance level for it.

I give this game a 6/10.

Far Cry New Dawn
And I want to give a quick review of this game here as I didn't play it on Steam.

Pros:
+ Cool aesthetic design for everything. Map, animals, bikers, guns. Guns are especially cool.
+ Really cool animations on silent kills. Made me wanna say "oof" for that poor guy who I stabbed in the eye.
+ Story is a sequel of course. Simple and good I would say.
+ Sorties are especially cool and even unique to the series. Capturing a valuable bag and getting out in many different places is what I really enjoyed.

Cons:
- Partners are even cooler and funnier here but sometimes I don't get how they are so weak to instantly die from a lvl 1 biker.
- Kinda short for its price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a huge fan of the Far Cry game series. The new installment continues the successful elements of the previous games: a brutal open world where most living creatures try to kill you. While the fourth installment took place in the Himalayas, this game has been set in America's state of Montana, home to a diverse range of colorful characters.
Comparisons to the previous game are inevitable. The storyline of the fifth installment was well-crafted. I particularly enjoyed the concept of a cult, a drug that brainwashes people, and a small squad on a recon mission. There are numerous beautiful details, such as a monument to the game's main antagonist, three loyal companions with their own backstories, and the opportunity to reenact the exploits of a legendary stuntman from the past.However, the videos featuring the pseudo-philosophical musings of the villains were dull. And in general, the main antagonist is inferior to the charismatic Asian character from the previous installment. In this installment, the towers that could be climbed in order to activate the map have been removed. However, there are altars and wolf beacon that need to be destroyed, as well as bomb shelters containing rewards and piles of cash that can be found on the tables.There are still a lot of missions to complete if you play through the game like me. The game can drag you out for a long time, so after going through the main menu, there are still a couple of missions left for me. One of them was to catch a salmon, which was not on the map, but luckily another task required me to get salmon for the bear. I also had difficulties finding the remaining altars and lighthouses that were mentioned earlier, as they were not displayed on the map. I had to search for them using a hint on the internet. After completing all the missions, I was surprised by the ending. It was an unexpected conclusion of the story, but it didn't affect anything in general.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhy I Am Giving Far Cry 5 A Glorious Big Ol' Thumbs Down
By Farbik The Olden (find me on onlyfans !wishlist)

Firstly, there are some wonderful moments in this game.
There were parts of this game that I absolutely adored, like the world itself, hunting, finding little camps where people are playing guitar and dancing. Really nice moments.
It's your usual Far Cry: liberate settlements/free the section of a map, which I was down for. Lots of explosions, great. Guns feel good. Great. You get pets that you can pet, and will kill for you. Fantastic! The npc's, in general, are pretty great. Great.
The problems start early on though. I had all the guns and all the perks I wanted basically half-way through the game, iirc. The crafting seems like a throw-on afterthought, and is fairly useless when you have so much money half way through you can just buy it all.
Do not even get me started on that guy in every place with an arcade, yelling out, trying to sell you on, "what a great game mode this is!" It's not a great game mode. It's running through player made levels killing baddies for no purpose. It's boring. Stop trying to tell me, in-game, that it's not. What an obnoxious addition to the game.
But what really did it in for me: they have this mechanic throughout the game where you get abducted and forced into these boring wave defence things. Super easy, super boring, super dumb. They add nothing to the story, imo, and completely destroy any immersion you have by, forcibly and unceremoniously, pulling you out of the world and putting you in these stupid drug induced levels. The drug levels are traditional Far Cry fare, fine, but where they worked (and had a sort of zany, if menacing, vibe to them) in Far Cry 3, they do not work here. They are an annoying bummer and every time they happened, after the first time, I just let out a big sigh and went through the motions so I could get back to the beautiful world the artists involved had created. You consistently destroyed my immersion. Bad Ubisoft.
The final nail in the coffin, and what made me slam-the-breaks-full-stop on this game at 80hrs (despite being on the last section of the map, and pretty close to being done I'm guessing) was the worst offensive violation of all: they traded an actually interesting antagonist (like Vaas or Pagan) for story moments that are just straight up gaudy, offensive, and wholly in bad taste.
In that last play session I had the character I'd searched the majority of the game for just nonchalantly blow his brains out in front of me (like, why? It didn't fill me with a feeling of, "I must get revenge". It left me with of feeling of, "did I really need to ingest that? Do I really want to engage with this?" It added nothing to the story.) followed by a scene shortly after where the main character slowly, and thoroughly, describes how he murdered his own baby child with his bare hands.
Maybe if I played this when I was much younger it wouldn't bother me, but I'm not. I guess they were attempting "shocking"?These moments weren't shocking. This is not good satire (if that's what they were going for -insert confused face emoji-). They were tacky, badly written cop outs, and in bad taste (which is what I walked away from this game with).
Imagine uninstalling a game at 80hrs when you're almost done. Yeesh.
