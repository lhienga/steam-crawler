\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been enjoying the game, but I work 12hr day/night shifts and the grind to build a base it too much. I think the game play is on a good path and I like to play with the combination of 3rd person and 1st person with mele and ranged weapons.

Starting a new character is where the game is most fun and challenging. I will usually play a character until I die and then start a new one.

I appreciate the steady progress in development of the game and this is one that will always be installed on my rig. I'm willing to buy DLCs to support development. I'll get tired of playing it from time to time due to having to run so much, but I always come back to it because it has the potential of being the best open world survival zombie game until I find something better.

I'm going to start adding to my review after every major update.

v.95 Update
The Devs removed the player ability to set the number of puppets (zombies) in the wild, outside, and inside. Instead, if you had before set everything to max zombie, now you come across an empty town. Just searching something will call a horde. You don't even have to make much noise. The horde pops out of nowhere, even sometimes directly in front of you. How can a zombie hear you searching a cabinet upstairs in a building or outside of a building for an example? The horde has made the game more challenging for when you spawn in. I had to make a detour from my usual begin run to get antibiotics for the infection I was getting. There's no real difference in my spawn strategy. Only that I know I'll be fighting the horde almost constantly and might have to make getting antibiotics a priority.

I think, before the update, setting the zombie population to it's highest felt right. The zombies would already horde me if I shot a gun. Even suppressed. The Devs are right to let them jump into windows. I'm thankful for the setting to turn off the ability for the zombies to open doors. Opening doors requires more thought than the zombies should have. Having the zombies bust down a door or window would be better.

The return of the truck is welcome, but all of them won't be ready to drive just like every other vehicle. You're still going to have to treat the game as a running simulator, having to stop when your stamina is exhausted, until you're able to get a vehicle operational. Running long distance and having to stop to rest is plain boring.

Today is the update for v.95 and I've already quit the game multiple times due to the running simulator aspect of it. Fighting the horde that spawns out of nowhere gets exhausting. You can't recon a destination and tactically plan before entering because the zombies will spawn out of nowhere. I believe we should keep the indoor, outdoor, and wild settings. The horde should run in from outside your immediate area and maybe only come when you're fighting a particular zombie or triggering a particular trigger.

I haven't used the new crafting UI much, because I'm used to selecting the ingredients of something I want to make and right clicking to select the craft. I don't like the new health UI. I should be able to click on an injury like before and be able to select what to treat it with in the pop up of what I have in inventory. Now I have to find what I have in the inventory screen in order to treat. It takes longer to treat an injury and time is life!

This update feels like two steps forward and one step back.

After day 2 of this update, I stopped playing.


UPDATE: 3/2/24 I tried playing after several updates since the Horde update and the Horde update still hasn't improved. I now don't recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

Although it has not happened weirdly while I'm encountered in a raid or in the middle of pvp I always crash or disconnect while I'm roaming solo or in base. Which is a win, mind you it could still be my pc's fault as I have not upgraded it in....5-6 years.
