Even with the beginner help turned on I was Googling and looking to YouTube and the Steam Community for help on what to do from the very beginning. That being said, I still find myself drawn to playing it, but in an almost masochistic way. I've only tried Single Player mode so far and, as a gamer who loves (and still plays) RUST and Stranded Deep, this feels very rough. It has a bit of a PUBG feel to it. After 4 hours of solo game play, I was finally able to craft a bow and kill some puppets, but struggled for almost an hour of real world time trying to craft and light a fire pit and cook chicken on it. It was almost comically difficult. I've had my eye on this for awhile and decided to finally by it when it was 50% off at $19.99. I don't think it's worth $39.99. Not yet, anyway. It's been out so long, I thought it would be more finished by 2024, but it seems like the developers and more interested in making DLCs for hair and cosmetics for an unfinished game, then polishing the roughness out of the ambiguous crafting and cumbersome inventory systems. In summary: Good graphics, good map and environments, great idea for a game, but the crafting and inventory management are so hard to navigate and understand, they need a lot of work. One of my biggest complaints is having to craft the same thing multiple times because the games default is to drop EVERYTHING you craft onto the ground where you can't see it among the grasses rocks and weeds, instead of just letting you hold it in your hands. Very bad design. Would I recommend this game to other players? Hmmm... I want to like this game, because it looks, based off of trailers and game play videos, like it should be awesome; but the confusing crafting system and frustrating inventory management ruin what should be by now, an awesome game. Side note: the players who understand and excel at this game have put 1000s of hours into it. Maybe after a couple months of playing this I'll revise my opinion, but as of April 2024, it still needs some work before its ready for prime time... and it may never be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a ton of potential, I spent close to 150 hours on a binge, what I've gathered is that they really hit the nail on the head when it comes to their food, and metabolism. The skills and leveling them is interesting, different and a fresh approach to leveling that is seen in most survival games. I have had a ton of fun on SCUM, in the short time that I've played this game I have absolutely loved it. I have even met fun people on official servers and enjoyed chatting and interacting. Like any survival game with PVP there will be toxic players. However I would not recommend the game in its current state. The sole reason is the overwhelming number of Cheaters in the game, My close knit friend group spent hours only to be killed by a flying car. I've encountered less cheaters on dayz official as a long time player than I did in less hours on SCUM. The Devs are super responsive, in discord, through Jagex and through their other means of reporting cheaters. To no avail as the cheaters will buy another account and continue to grief players that choose to play the game legit. If you choose to play, find a server that is monitored by an admin or team of admins; otherwise hang it up because you'll spend hours looting and someone will kill you with a flying car, or waltz right into your base. Be warned it can be absolutely amazing, but its current state is absolutely frustrating. Thanks to xKiCkx for the timely response in regards to Chinese hackers in the West servers, but its sadly to little avail.\t\t\t\t\t
