\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is excellent BUT, there are a few things that are rough. let's get into it.

First thing, I appreciate that the developers are continuing with their hard work to implement the small things that they want to add, but i think that game should have an overhaul with the connections, which I know is not directly linked to the developers, but they do have final say. I am fortunate enough to be able to rent a server exactly how i like it to be set up. however, I feel like I should be able to jump on an official PVE server and have dedicated PVP zones for those who don't know how to play or need a little more time to learn the mechanics so they're not just getting slaughtered by vets. you could say that's what single player is for, but let's admit, survival games are a lot more fun with friends or just other people in general. I like that they're implementing more puppet types but I don't like that they're only in specific areas, the rippers are currently only in specific bunkers, which is fine and adds a new style of going about looting high value loot areas, rather than just going in guns a blazing and getting great loot for the cost of a few mags of ammo,

The weapon design is phenomenal, and as an avid firearm enthusiast, I have only found a few games with the same or better weapon design, so for all of the gun nuts out there who like to butcher zombies this one is for you. however, the lack of various or realistic damage is a turn off, it was so close, but I understand for balancing reasons.

The lack of puppet varieties is more than likely going to change, but since the game has been out for 5 years now, I wish it had come a tad sooner. I'd like if they implemented large wandering hordes who could actually wreck your base if you're not paying attention, something akin to Conan exiles without the summoning.
Too many times have I been surrounded in a town and all I had to do to get to safety was jump in a window, I think it would be more intense knowing puppets can and will come through an open window rather than just stand there, occasionally jump and get picked off, it takes the thrill of surviving a town down quite a bit.

As for the reviews that thinks its appalling that they're selling DLC while in early access are delusional, as they are just cosmetic and only add flair to your character, no PTW at all, I never bought it. I understand the people who don't like it and say just add a donation pool, but I feel the developers wanted to give something in return for the continued support. they are still very active with updates and continue to further the game.

After 230 hours I would like a little bit more variety of weapons and gear rather than making very similar load-outs after about 5 hours in a server, military gear is great and all but the storage space on some items of civilian clothes is abysmal, I want more variety of people running around, maybe add a clothes type crafting system you could add some animal skin pockets onto for extra storage, or perhaps ALICE or MOLLE pouches, or if Croatia has their own version of the US MOLLE system, to implement that.

Inventory management is one of my favorite things in video games since RE4 and I feel it's pretty similar to that, however I do wish that if you have mags in a bulletproof vest, that you could simply replace that one you reloaded with rather than just going to the first open inventory slot. More than a few times, I've dropped mags on the ground because i didn't have a free slot until I've reloaded, small complaint, but i think it could do a better job of letting you know you dropped a mag because you were in the heat of it.

All in all i'll give scum an 8/10, it's an enjoyable game for that state that its in and really gives you that survival feel, so if you've been itching for something new, I say give it a try. There have been a lot of QOL improvements already, but it's just not quite there yet. Thanks for reading the review, I hoped it helped you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSCUM is one of my favorite games. I've been playing for a little while now (over 3 years I think) and I keep coming back. Whether or not you'll like this game seems to depend on whether you're looking for a PVP game with survival elements or a survival game with the potential for PVP.

IMO this game is geared far more toward the later. SCUM has a pretty in depth metabolism system and (unless someone disables it) a fatigue system. The kinds of foods you eat and how you rest really matter in how your stamina recuperates, how your health regenerates, and how you're body looks overall. If you eat a lot of fatty foods and drive everywhere you go you'll gain weight and lose dex/con if you eat high protein meals and do a lot of walking/biking your character can slim down if you carry a log around while doing all of that you can get pretty buff. A really slim toon will mind the heat less but be more susceptible to hypothermia than a chunky one. You can hunt and fish for your meals, forage and eventually grow your own food. Not everything you eat is safe. I don't know if you still get the runs when you eat bad food but you used to and i'm sure it will be back. nothing is worse then getting caught with your pants down when you're being attacked by a puppet because you waited to long to go...
I've died of sepsis at least once because I couldn't find antibiotics, had radiation sickness that took forever to cure and ate some mushrooms that had me seeing tiny puppets for hours, I've been blown up, killed by a goat (they used to be vicious), a bear and more wolves than I care to think about. It's always been fun (but seriously f*ck them wolves).

The base building has changed substantially since I started and there are still a few things that need tweaking but overall it's pretty easy and if that's not your jam you can always just lock up an apartment or house. You cannot add furniture into the houses right now so wardrobes and respawn beds will need to be outside. On servers that allow it, your base can be raided, the game favors the raider more than the ones being raided, but that's an optional part of game play so you can either load your base up with traps and bombs or play on a server that doesn't allow it.

How successful you are in PVP is going to depend on your graphics settings as well as the skills/specs you choose. The render on the plants can make you feel like you're hiding but completely visible to someone with a set of binos because there is a vast difference between the two. Binos and some scopes can make the terrain look free of bushes and scrub. People with high stealth and camo can look like they are TPing because of how the game deals with your ability to see them based on your own stats. It also sounds like you are angry-stomping through the leaves whenever you walk on grass no matter how high your stealth is, it won't necessarily effect the puppets but to human ears it's pretty obvious.

This game isn't perfect and there are certainly things I'd like to see changed but it isn't finished yet either. It can feel very frustrating seeing things added/removed/re-added to the game and little things that should be there but aren't but at this point I've certainly gotten my moneys worth.
