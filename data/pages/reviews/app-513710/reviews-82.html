\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSCUM had a VERY rough launch and to be honest, I thought the hype would die down fast and this would be another H1Z1 Just Survive scenario. But oh boy was I wrong. Five years later and I am still here.

Constant hotfixes, dev updates every Monday, teasers on Twitter and features requested by the community; the SCUM devs have proven themselves to actually care about their game. There are obviously many issues with the game, hence the Early Access. One of which is performance. The SCUM veterans here might remember the launch five years ago where multiplayer was a hot pile of garbage and performance expired itself as soon as you entered any given POI. But now? Multiplayer feels smooth and I rarely see any lags. (You can eliminate that by limiting the ping in your server setting, standard is 200 for whatever reason. Set it to 140 and you will never see teleporting players.)
As for POIs, those are completely smooth nowadays. Behemoths like Samobor or Krsko can knock your frames below 100 at times, but that only really happens when you enter those cities for the first time or if a lot is going on around you. I can't remember the last time where I got angry because of frame issues, which is a good thing
It would take too long to list all the things I love about this game, but here are a couple: Clothing variety, weapon variety, map design, graphics, gunplay, DLC contents, traders, customization options for servers, POI variety, atmosphere, the transparency of the dev team and more.

But I gotta be honest, sometimes I feel like SCUM is trying to be TOO realistic. The whole exhaustion feature for example was a neat idea, but I dislike it. They did make it more manageable a couple hotfixes later, but I have turned it off on my server. It's great that I have the option to disable it.
Here is the one thing I hate with a passion: Hunting. The devs reworked the hunting system at some point. It went from "I hear animal, I found animal, I shoot animal" to "I hear animal, I found track, I hear animal, I found track, I hear animal, I found track & repeat." I see what they were trying to do, but dear lord is the Hunting 2.0 frustrating. Might be a skill issue on my part, but why are the tracks making animal sounds. Just give us the animal and the tracks can be support. Also, please change the track color from blue to red. Blue blends in too much with the green grass AND white snow, making it even harder. Red would be perfect. (While we are at it, please let us track players after wounding them.)
The last negative point I have are the zombies, or "puppets." It feels like the Stealth skill in this game doesn't do . You can crouch-walk in the middle of the night with the darkness set to extra dark and they will still see and hear you from 50m away.

Overall, I can see this game being at the top of zombie survival games one day. DayZ still reigns supreme, but SCUM will dethrone it as soon as it offers modding and Steam Workshop support which is the only thing keeping DayZ alive and at the top.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSCUM - A Thriving Survival Experience with Room for Improvement (since it is in Early Access)

I've spent over 2300 hours playing SCUM, and throughout my journey, I've witnessed numerous highs and lows within the game. What SCUM consistently proves is its commitment to development, close connection with the community, and a genuine effort to fulfill player wishes while maintaining a balanced PvP experience.

The game holds immense potential in the roleplay scene and boasts the best features for long-term enjoyment, thanks to its intricate metabolism system and skill progression mechanics. However, achieving success in SCUM requires not only dedication but also a fundamental understanding of its mechanics.

One aspect that I can never quite view positively is the introduction of new vehicles. I'm not alone in this sentiment, as I wished that the old vehicles had been retained until an equal number of variations were made available through the new modular system.

Nevertheless, at the end of the day, SCUM remains a 5/5 for me. I firmly believe that the game is yet to tap into its full potential and is definitely moving in the right direction. The dedication to ongoing development and the game's profound commitment to its community are proof that SCUM is a continually evolving and rewarding experience.
