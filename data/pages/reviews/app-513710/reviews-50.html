\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recently changed a negative review to a positive one after some of the horde spawning issues were made more tolerable and the performance issues seemed to have been resolved.

However, after now being able to play the game again I do not recommend it in it's current state.

As is, not recommended. I thought the hunting had been reverted to the old system. It hasn't. It's terrible. You hear an animal, you focus in on the direction and follow it (clues and sound) halfway down a mountain, going South. The next audio cue/clue happens and it's now 180 degrees in the opposite direction, North, back up the mountain. Almost back at the top another audio cue/clue, 180 degrees again, South, back down the mountain FFS! PLEASE, listen to the community and bring back the animals, all of them.

The horde system is way better then at 0.95 release, they at least spawn behind objects now and out of sight, which is still not ideal, we want to clear an area, again, please listen to the community. Many people have also requested a massive roaming horde, this would be awesome, maybe 2 or 3 big roaming hordes at any one time across the map would be amazing.

Some loot-able objects appear to be bugged and will trigger a horde when searched. You can sneak around an entire POI undetected until you search that one box that will trigger a horde and then they coming running to your exact location from very very far away.

This horde system and hunting system cannot be tweaked, they need to go, tweaking will not change what it is, it will only change how it behaves and we don't like what it is or how it behaves.

On a positive note, the way the crafting menu is now organised is great, so much cleaner and easier to navigate and craft stuff. I'm crafting things I would never have given a second thought about. The tips / guides as a new spawn are also very helpful, I learnt about things I never knew about previously.

I am still having fun when I play but the devs are pulling an "Apple" on us, taking things away and making the experience terrible, they then re-introduce the things they took away later but with serious limitations and constraints and we are "happier" because it is way batter than the current state of things - but - it is not what it was originally.\t\t\t\t\t

Has Potential, But Is Currently Quite Rough


Scum is an open world survival crafting game with a few elements from games like DayZ, Rust, and 7 days to die sprinkled through-out, while still largely being unique. Your diet macros impact your stats, your clothes dry at different speeds, and there are a few other unique mechanics and oddities sprinkled throughout.

The game is in Alpha, so many if its rougher around the edges condition can be blamed on that and there is a likelihood that it will be fixed in time. But other issues strike me as a fundamental confusion of the Dev's focus. The fact that there is a fully integrated and voice acted 'go to the bathroom' system with particle effects and yet we can not zoom in, pan, or mark the map is something so incredibly bizarre that I actually struggle to put it into words. The fact that any dev time got put into that at all before quality of life features were done is frustrating.

The Character creation is a bit odd, and missing quite a bit. You can choose between male and female, about 8 different base characters with a dozen skin tones and a dozen hairstyles each. The hair has the 'early days unmodded' conan problem of not coloring well and being incredibly ugly regardless of what you choose. You have a few variations of three eye colors to choose from and the ability to adjust your 'iris' for whatever reason (something literally not a soul will notice in world, and is even hard to tell looking at your own character in 3rd person). Also on the odd level, the game does have a genital slider. Which, with nothing else really being customize-able and the differences being mute, feels more like an appeal to horny teenagers going "See, you can have big honkas or a huuuuuuge schlong!", not that either get that impressive in size.

You have four main stats that give you skillpoints for substats. Many of the substats can be categorized as 'wishful thinking' or non-functional. Items like driving, diving, and flying are exclusively end-game skills that you'll likely have time to train if you're that far ahead anyway, and other skills like 'awareness' don't seem to actually have any impact outside of the PvP stealth mechanic.

The strangeness continues with your infill method. You are chucked from the sky with your hands bound, your character wriggles free at a certain time regardless of your input and then you can try to direct your landing. The problem is that your time and direction is incredibly minimal and you can't change direction or even slow your descent once your parachute is pulled so at most you'll be avoiding landing on a tree or building.


Combat is incredibly chunky, for melee at least. Skill appears to make a marginal difference in damage alone, although it claims to add a 'combo' system which, given that you cannot attack in different directions or choose between light and heavy, means that your left click will sometimes thrown an alternate animation, or a second attack, which can often be more harm than a help if it throws off your attack timing with the enemies stagger. Bows were service-able, even the starter one, though the fact that the crosshair goes away when you start pulling the arrow back is incredibly annoying, especially at longer ranges. There also doesn't seem to be any kind of sneak attack or silent take-down to kill undead, and your options, especially early, are incredibly limited and not quite meaningful.

Despite the map being uninteractable, navigation isn't that bad. You get a red dot to show your location on the map, plenty of clear landmarks, and the ability to enter 'focus mode' to see cardinal north. Exploring is a bit less engaging, with crawling into hard to reach places rarely rewarding anything of note, and with many of the buildings devolving into near identical layouts and spreads over time. There's enough variety for moment to moment play, but when you're scavenging for hours and hours, it becomes highly noticeable. Enemies can also spawn randomly in areas you've already cleared and even on the same street as you, so keep an eye on that.

Inventory management is a mess, with half the things that feel like they should be stackable not being stackable, item condition impacting inventory space, and you not being able to store things in the world means that you're back and forth to buried chests. Because your space is so limited and items are so large construction is largely around placing highlighted 'blueprints' of the thing you want to build in the world and then adding ingredients. If you've played Green Hell, it's like that. Which means you are not building any large structures or even a simple hut reasonably. To the point where many players do not bother, relying instead on stashes and simply lean-to shelters if anything. The Dev's know this is a mess and a rework is supposed to be due soon according to the dev blogs.

Movement is clunky, adjusting speed with the scroll wheel, which given that you only have walk, jog, and sprint, is unnecessary. Arma 3 handles the changing of the crouch height and lean angle in a far better way, with the standard key to toggle walk, key to sprint, and the control + w and S keys to adjust height. The scroll wheel is just an awkward solution in its place. You can't attack while crouched, which is a problem as well when enemies run around the corner while you're trying to sneak. There's no vaulting, though you can sometimes 'step up' onto ledges. There's no sliding or rolling, and given stealth is your best friend and the high energy cost of running, much of your time exploring will be done in low, slow, tedious movements, dragging out the experience.


Items in the world don't update to reflect their status (Opened, has items, looted, etc) visually. And it's all RNG, so you can search an entire corn field and not find a single ear or corn, making food incredibly hard to come by. Supposedly you can hunt but the only animal I ever saw was a bear, who was incredibly aggressive right off the bat and nearly mauled me to death. So food is incredibly scarce and luck dependent and farming is a laughable distant future for the vast majority of the early to mid game. Its also hard to tell what you're looting as sometimes you're looting an individual box and sometimes the whole stack of boxes is one inventory, of which the game could badly use a highlight function to show whats being looted.

Public servers are also super sweat zones. Like Rust had an unholy child with League of Legends levels of toxic, and the above systems sort of guarantee that.

All in all, this game was quite miserable, and not for the reasons I feel like it should be. Luck had an absurd outcome on my runs and with how restrictive the design is the game is quite difficult, but also clunky and tedious. Loot settings can be adjusted on private servers but without mod support my biggest gripes wouldn't be addressed by one. I haven't even talked about the god awful decision to put absurdly short timers on the available minigames, which among other things make cargo drops useless deathtraps, or the worst respawn mechanic and perma-death system I think I've ever seen.


There are some fantastic ideas here and ones I hope the devs iron out with time but at this current junction I can't really recommend this game to anyone in good conscience.\t\t\t\t\t
