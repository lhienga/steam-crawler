\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this game, but there are too many things working against my view of the game to recommend it. The game is early access, so I understand a lot of the issues may be fixed at some point. But here are a few of my complaints. First, I think it's bad game design to have enemies spawning as they do in this game. They pop into view from thin air, on top of buildings, on un-climbable objects, directly in front of the direction that you're moving. It feels super immersion breaking when you're heading out to loot, and as you cross a field, 6 RIDICULOUSLY strong and fast zombies spawn perfectly obstructing your planned path, from thin air. As a new player, you end up having to take detour upon detour upon detour just trying to walk down a dirt road, and a 15 minute hike turns into a 45 minute prone waddle session. Second, you have to have "renown" to buy any item from the trader. This can turn into a ridiculous grind fest, killing zombies to farm enough to buy bandages and other small, but very necessary essentials. And lastly, the game is centered around pvp, but death is so punishing that it isn't even fun to risk things for the big loot gain endorphin rushes. You lose HALF your current renown when you die, along with your inventory. So not only do you have to farm up another kit and medical supplies, you also may need to farm zombies for hours to regain your renown for simple essential items. The treadmill isn't very fun.

EDIT: Thank you for the response Beda. I'm sure I'll try the game again once mod support is introduced in 1.0, as the team previously stated. To briefly touch on the fame point system, my only suggestion is to plain remove most of the items from the trader, and make the necessity items like medical supplies more abundant and definitive to find in the open world. This will lead to a player supply / demand situation, which may lead to more play interaction. Or simply add a make-shift inventory craft to handle most of the negative ailments, which may be a longer chore to craft, but ultimately help players with bad looting luck. I'll be watching and rooting for the game to succeed!\t\t\t\t\t
