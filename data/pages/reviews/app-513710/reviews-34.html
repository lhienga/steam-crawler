\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSooooooo, this is the first I've felt compelled to leave a review, but my thoughts on this game are a bit complicated and conflicting to the point I feel like it might be a benefit to others. So, I'll try to give you the basic pro cons as best I can in the hopes you find it helpful.


TLDR: This game is both super realistic, and highly unique, even compared to many of the other survival/looter/building games out there. That said, it feels like the devs have focused on realism and difficulty more than fun. The result is a game that has some extremely cool and tense moments, but the sparse excitement that comes from this game is often scattered across an extremely tedious, painstaking, and overly laborious timeline full of mechanics that seem to pay no mind to whether or not they're any fun at all. I think there is true greatness in this game, but there is WAY too much attention being paid to the absolute smallest of details, and not enough being given to even the most fundamental basics that a 'good' game would normally have. If you absolutely love a punishing and brutal experience based more off poor design than true difficulty, then this is the one for you... but, otherwise, the juice just isn't quite worth the squeeze on this one in my opinion. 6/10


NOTABLE AREAS OF GAMEPLAY:
Realism
- Now, before diving into why this is bad, I'll also mention that it's a very GOOD aspect of the game as well. That said, there are aspects of this game that are added in for the sense of 'realism' but, they're literally nothing but detractors for the overall experience. Having to stop to take a piss, or the tedium of washing clothes is a REALLY cool novelty that feels kind of immersive at first since most games don't bother with it, but once you have an understanding of everything these novelties entail... there's just a massive list of obnoxious nonsense you have to do to sustain yourself. Tracking macro nutrients, vitamin intake, physical conditioning, etc... It's really cool that it's there, but it's a HUGE detractor in terms of actual fun/gameplay. Your truck handles differently if it's loaded up with weight compared to it being empty, you need to wash your clothes regularly to avoid infection, and you have to have to collect ammo, magazines, and supplies for each gun in the game... individually. It's a level of depth and realism that is absolutely astonishing as you first start getting into it, but after getting past how cool it is on an intuitive level (Which... it's not all that intuitive), playing the game and managing the various aspects of gameplay are as boring and tedious as... well... real life in a lot of ways.
Loot
- For a looting oriented game, it is nearly impossible to find anything worthwhile. And if you DO find something worthwhile, finding space for it often requires a significant loss of whatever low level loot you may have found already. Beyond that, I've played on some 10x PVE servers as well, and even with loot drops cranked to the absolute MAX, find worthwhile loot can still be a bit of a headache and is limited to the extremely dangerous/difficult bunkers or kill boxes. Walking for hours (which you will do often) is just NOT fun when going out of your way to check out a small village to walk away with essentially nothing of value. That added to the gripes above in the 'realism' category... well... it makes the loot really underwhelming in this game.
UI
- The menu/UI/Interactivity in this game needs SERIOUS work. Often times you'll simply have items disappear when trying to move them into your inventory/hands/bag, That cool gun you had a hard time getting... GONE. That food you were about to eat. NOPE. This bug alone has single-handedly made me stop playing the game some days. Beyond this basic kind of issue still being present, you'll spend as much time organizing and managing your storage and chests just as much if not more than any time you'll spend collecting loot. That wouldn't be the worst thing in the world, but the inventory management is just ... not good. You can double click to pick up, but not to put away, there's no loot all or speed loot options similar to rust, and managing inventory on the fly is maybe the most tedious and boring experience in the game.
DESIGN
- Holy wow... Of all the things in this game that I'm split on, the design has got to be at the top of the list. While this is ALSO a pro, I think it edges slightly into the negative category for me overall. Things like having to unequip your primary weapon to close a car door? wtf is that?? Because of how unintuitive the cooking system is, I've actually 'made' all of 1 or 2 meals over 100 hours of gameplay.

Ultimately, I want SO BAD to love this game. It has a skeleton in place that's actually REALLY strong, but in it's current state (even though I know it's early access) there's still too little polish being applied to core components that lead me to believe it will always be 'realistic', but the game might not truly ever be 'fun' (in my *personal* opinion.
