\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've invested over 2000 hours into this game since 2018, and in 2024, I'm sharing this review as a cautionary message to the community.

This game used to be one of the best zombie survival experiences I've ever had. Scum drew me away from the Dayz universe with its straightforward crafting and base-building elements. I loved the simplicity and ease of looting various boxes, containers, puppets, and other players I encountered. The freedom to explore buildings and bunkers at my own pace was truly enjoyable. Over the past two years, I even went on to rent my own server, hosting up to 60 players.

Regrettably, the recent direction the developers have taken is bewildering. They introduced a convoluted cooking system and implemented an exhaustion mechanic that immobilizes you, forcing you to recuperate in bed. Crafting anything without gloves now results in numerous hand lacerations. They removed valuable content and replaced it with undesirable features. Server performance has declined, causing lag and rubber banding even with just one person present, worsened in full servers. Zombie spawns are erratic, with the new horde system leading to zombies appearing right in front of you or dropping from the sky. Clearing out a town or bunker becomes a futile effort as they respawn within ten minutes. The hordes are a formidable challenge, making the game unbearable for even seasoned players, let alone newcomers.

Regarding bunkers, instead of addressing lag and server stability, access has been restricted to mitigate stress on the servers. The last time the game felt enjoyable and stable was probably before they removed the old vehicles and introduced the new ones. I'm skeptical about the game improving; it seems to be heading towards increased confusion and bugs with each addition rather than addressing existing server issues. I didn't write this out of hatred for the game; it's actually my favorite, but it's currently in a dire state, and I don't foresee any improvement.\t\t\t\t\t
