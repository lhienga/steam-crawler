However if you are new or coming from these games you will get bored and frustrated in the beginning because is annoying to spawn where you want to and bases should not be your first priority because you won't be able to spawn at it. The reason I prefer this game is due to the Gold system which gives you the feeling that in some way you are making progress and is worth investing time, Wipes are not that constant and even when you have one you keep the gold unless there is a major problem with the game or is necessary. Try to spend more skill points in running since you will be able to run for more time because that's what you will be doing when you start the game and probably the skill that unlocks more crafting recipes.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had purchased scum a couple years ago and at the time i was living somewhere with poor internet and that made this game difficult to play considering my connection speed, so I had left it in my inventory and maybe played solo a couple times.

Recently my brother and I were looking for a fun, somewhat grindy, PVE and some PVP games to play in the Open World Survival Craft genre, and I had remembered this game, and now I have much better internet. We have put several hours in so far and I will say we are enjoying this early access game.
Here are some thing we like and don't like about this game.

Don't likes:
At default settings, this game is WAY more grindy than most OWSC games I've played, but the solution is finding a server with an 8-10x loot modifier.
The game can crash at times, and it's annoying, but luckily your character isn't idle in server time, so when you crash, your character disappears until you log back in.
There can be some glitches at times: drinking or eating food straight from a box's inventory, can sometimes cause your characters ability to interact with anything disappear. Most of the time, a re-log will solve this issue.
The most recent glitch me and my brother found is a random occurrence where our truck, while driving, no clipped through the map floor, we were spawned back on the ground, but our truck and all the loot from a bunker was lost to the abyss.

Now the positives:
This game is possibly the most accurate OWSC game in the gun department. IRL I am a gun nerd and seeing a game that implements correct loading animations for mags. drums, shotguns and also has accurate quantity counts (like 7rds. in a 1911 or certain shotguns have shorter feed tubes) is really cool and speaks to the research the devs have done.
The complexity of crafting materials, or "grinding" to build bases, the inner-workings of vehicle mechanics, charging batteries, repairing guns with the parts of an identical gun and resource gathering restrictions (only carry 2 large logs at a time) are just a few ways this game immerses you into the survival mentality.

Don't let haters scare you off, but if you want the cleanliness of a AAA title then play a AAA title, but what I'll tell you is, I have not found a AAA title that itches all the spots of an immersive open world survival craft like this one. This isn't pay to win. Play with friends and enjoy a real walking dead world simulator where your day to day survival is based on how you maneuver through the challenges.
