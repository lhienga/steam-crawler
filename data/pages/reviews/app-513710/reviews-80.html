\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE:

Okay im going to change my review to a positive one because im having fun now. I mean Im having fun NOW. The first 60 hours wasn't fun, it was just a long string of fiascos and failures. Constantly dying, losing my stuff, spending ages working myself back to a small meager collection of possessions, then dying again, starting over again and again. But once you get a GOOD grip on the game, it actually starts to open up. And from there on it astonishingly gets better and better. Right now after more than 120 hours Im having a shipload of fun actually. I cannot deny the fun I have now that I do not anymore constantly run my head face straight into a wall of death and suffering.

If you are willing to suffer through the pain of learning this game for many hours there is a LOT of fun to be had. Just know that it does not come easy. I advice against starting on the official servers. Choose a server with 10x loot and no mechs (mega deadly killer robots that guards locations with good loot) and perhaps even a pure pve server with no pvp. Once you reached a very good grip on the game, you can try official for the tough challenge and you will be ready to enjoy it.

And there IS an incredible amount of fun to enjoy. This is by no means an empty game or a lackluster experience. It's just really tweaked to suit the hardcore players.

OP review:
tldr; There's a lot of stuff wrong with this game, tons and tons of gameplay annoyances.

I cannot recommend the game, it's not fun. It's truly a shame because there's actually a lot of great things about the game that COULD have made it fun, like great visuals, like really cool looking cities, a big open beautiful world, killer robots(!), a world void of the dense cancer pvp you find in Rust. But everything is hampered by abysmal design and when you FINALLY have gotten past some of the awful ♥♥♥♥ that sucks like hell and aren't fun at all, you die. There you go, be so kind to start all over with collecting and crafting all the ♥♥♥♥ for hours and hours and hours until someone just kills you or steals your stuff.

Inventory management and crafting system is the cancer of gameplay fun. You have no inventory space but crafting anything takes TONS of ressources and each of thoese ressources takes TONS of inventory space. It's ludicrous. While I was trying to manage this friggin awful inventory management game of how to get 4 wooden sticks (among other stuff that you dont have enough inventory space to carry with you) so I could finally build a bigger backpack, I died from thirst. It just took too long to figure that crap out.

When crafting your character will automatically use items in vicinity and in your inventory. A couple of different items can serve the same craft slot for an item, and some of these items are pretty rare while others are very common. Unfortunately the game often chooses to use the very rare item to craft the item instead of the extremely common item and there is no way to tell the game to just use the common item (there's no different in the crafted item it is the exact same result). Only way to get around this is to drop the valuable into a crate you build OR drop it on the ground and move far enough away so that your character can't see it, then craft the item so the game only has the choice of using the common item, then move badck to the valuable item and pick it up again.

There was also this time I made a fire in the woods, using the mega clunky crafting system I was unable to figure out how to make a fire. So I headed to Google to look for tutorial (Google searches, the hall mark of a lacking game design) and finally figured it out. Once the fire was lighted I wanted to get a little closer to the fire and while I was visible more than half a meter from this tiny fire I magically received burn injuriies amost all over the body. Great design, truly. How about only burning me if I actually go into the fire OR if I stand too close to the fire FOR LONGER? Instead of instant burn injury all over from half a meter from this tiny fire. I tested this with many layers of clothers totally soaked from rain: same result instant burn injuries all over the body for less than half a second within a half meter of the VERY tiny fire (literally a few sticks and a couple of tiny logs)

Melee is broken like hell. Often when you swing your knife at a zombie while being right up in it's face, but nothing happens evne though you should have clearly hit it, same with any other melee weapon. It's just going to miss for no reason at all. And then the zombie will hit you and you start bleeding all over. And even when you DO hit it, you need to hit it 10 times, which is far more swings that you have stamina for, and when you have used all your stamina your attacks do -90% damage. When using a spear after stabbing your character for some strange reason needs to pull the spear up in a vertical position and to stab again take it down from that vertical position before being able to stab again. I mean it actually takes time to pull it up after the stab and pull it down again before the next stab. Why can't I keep the spear in the horizontal position ready to stab again?

If you commit suicide (either deliberately or by accident) you will loose 0.1 point in the Intelligence stat. But the game never tells you this fact, when you choose "Commit suicide" it just asks you if you are sure. Why not inform the player what the consequense will be? Is it fun to learn this after the fact? Leveling up intelligence is a very difficult tasks that requires braking into and looting dangerous military bunkers, hoping to get a rare item and then spending multiple of the item in certain place in said bunker to increase intellect stat. However this has been disabled, so you can't increase intellect, only thing you can do is increase temporaily your Intellect stats with another special item, that works for 1.5 hour. How is that going to help much if you permanently have lost some of your intellect stat? To build the best engineering items you need at least advanced engineering skill, but if your intellect is below 3 you can't do that even if you engineering is advanced, as the advanced tier still also needs intellect to be 3. And 3 is the default value for the stats so start intellect 3 - suicide 0.1 = 2.9 intellect, no longer can you build the best items, unless you use a temporary buff for 1.5 hour of build time via an item that is pretty rare.

Above is a small selection of the problems I encountered, but I reached close to the end of the maximum review length.\t\t\t\t\t
