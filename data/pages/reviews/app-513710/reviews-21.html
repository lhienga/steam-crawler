\r\n\t\t\t\t\tScum is my favorite game of all time. I've been playing since .7, and I'm sitting on almost 2,000 hours. There have been times that I get bored with the game, and try something else, but I always come back.

It is not an easy game by any means, but extremely rewarding when you figure it out. You will die a lot in the beginning, but you will learn. There are servers out there that are high loot, no mechs, pve zones, nerfed mechanics like accumulated fatigue, metabolism, infections etc. but those will get boring much quicker.

I buy most survival games, DayZ, The Forrest, Project Zomboid are among my favorites, but none of them are as complicated, fun, rewarding or beautiful as Scum.

It is in early access, and you/we are the ones testing each build. You will encounter bugs at some point. In 3 years of playing, I haven't seen a game breaking bug. You might loose a backpack or gun at some point, and if that is enough for you to rage quit, you probably won't like the rest of the game either. This is the best dev team I've encountered, and they fix/nerf things and mechanics very quickly, usually within the day. They also listen to the community which is something I haven't seen before.

Buy it. It is absolutely worth every penny at full price, it is a steal when it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

I have played this game for 46 hours.

So, the graphics are pretty good, but there is a lot wrong with this game.

1. Rendering is so slow.
I sometimes land and am able to walk through houses and other objects as they have not yet rendered. This can also happen when I run.
2. Food is very hard to find.
I have died numerous times as I have been travelling and unable to find food, or enough food to make a big difference to your hunger.
3. You can hardly carry anything with you.
To start with you only have pockets, and you cannot stack items. If you are lucky you might find a bum bag. I know you can craft a backpack, but you need a lot of stuff to do so.
4. Items are sparse.
You can travel far and wide, but you will not find many needed items. It's not too bad in the city, but it's one thing finding stuff you need, it's another being able to collect it and take it with you (see 3).
5. Zombies are too fast.
You can't outrun most zombies (you can, if you are lucky, run out of their line of sight).
6. Zombies are too strong.
Even with simple weapons it is hard to kill a zombie.
7. Zombies are too numerous.
Especially in towns and the city, you will likely get attacked by more than one zombie. Killing one zombie is hard enough but taking on 2, 3, 4 or more zombies is impossible without a gun.
8. Base building is a nightmare!
a simple foundation requires several items which have to be added after you have placed the blueprint. 3 rope, 3 logs, 3 sticks (for example). so, you have to go and collect these items (as you can't stack items in your inventory) and drop them on the ground. Then go and find more.....and so on. SO a 4x4 hut would require so many items that it would take ages to build even if those items were available in close vacinity.
This is the worst base building I have come across in all the many games of this ilk that I have played.

660 hours later....

1. Well, the rendering is no longer slow.
2. If you run at the default pace (second click on the scroll wheel) your food now lasts quite a long time, long enough to find a few veggies to keep you going.
3. Still ca't carry much unless you are lucky enough to find a backpack or, even better, a large backpack. It can take you hours and several lives to get that lucky.
4. Items/loot is still sparse and it can take forever to just get some decent clothes.
5. Zombies are still too fast. It's not a problem if you get a head start, but if you come across one suddenly, you can't outrun it. Even running through bushes won't help.
6. Okay, so, get your timing right and you can deal with a single zombie without too many scratches. But...
7. the horde mode is just crazy. I've been in a house making not the slightest noise when, suddenly, the horde starts yelling and gathering and they will normally find you even if you don't move. Fighting a horde without ranged weapons is (almost) impossible unless you can fight them in a doorway or other narrow place that will prevent them overwhelming you.
8. Still not liking the base building system, though I did manage to build a 3x2 base, which took HOURS.
So, I managed to exceed 300 fame points in single player mode, but multiplayer is another problem (even with no PvP) as you have to start from scratch.
