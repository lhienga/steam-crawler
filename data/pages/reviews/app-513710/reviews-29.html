\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game which looks very like very fun, but soon you find out that it does not seem to know what it wants. To be a realistic survival game or a looter shooter. At one hand you have the weapon handling, very realistic in which you first have to load the rounds into a magazine and than need to load the filled magazine into your semi automatic pistol, aaaand on the other side you can load a big ass tree log into your quiver and go roaming. In my 21 hours of playing i have not encountered a single complete vehicle, seems you need to puzzle every vehicle together and even a bicycle seem to not have escaped the wrath of the puppets with them removing the tires. Now that would not been such a problem if the map wasn't so super huge that you can even use planes. There is not a single piece of lore to be found, the reason that you character is there is still up in the air. The enemies are dumb and spawn from under you, behind you, above you, in you, you can even see them spawning out of thin air which is very immersion breaking. There are no bosses or raids in the field, and after you have gathered a certain amount of loot you are basically done and there is no need to explore. Another problem i encountered is that certain buildings always give the same loot, so for example: In a regular house you rarely find weapons, or food and drinks, only clothes or kitchen knifes and dollars. So if you dont need clothes you skip all the buildings that look like regular homes and skip halve the content. The environment looks awesome and vibrant, runs amazing, no micro stutters and the sounds are well done to. Recommended if you love to explore and have time to burn. (not engaged with base building and pvp)

With all this said however, the developers are hard at work to make this a very good game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot a Vitamin D deficiency, then tried to run 50 meters and got mauled to death by zombies while having an asthma attack, 10/10 realism and immersion for fat sweaty gamers like me.

Okay, serious review time. Here's the stuff I like:
- Gunplay is really, really good.
- Puppets feel like a legitimate threat for far longer than they are in a game like DayZ.
- The minigames for certain activities mostly feel really good with some exceptions.
- The sheer variety of things to do. My group can, and DOES, have individual members who focus on specific activities like fixing cars, gathering or farming food, cooking, base building, or just picking through specific points of interest that they personally know well.
- Gorgeous views in a variety of different biomes, on a massive map where it feels like players have plenty of room to spread out and avoid one another if they want to, while having certain areas where there is plenty of reason for people to congregate and compete with one another for the highest concentrations of good loot.
- Weapon painting using paint you find in the world. This has allowed my group to continuously create the most tacticool camo patterns we can dream up, the most absurd COD gun skins we could desire, and the most horrifically gauche color and pattern combinations we need to make one another groan in pain at our favorite guns being defiled.
- At the risk of slipping out of objective review, the vibes of this game and its world are really, really interesting.

Now for the stuff I don't like.
- The lockpicking minigame feels goofy and unsatisfying. A lock having a timer to solve the picking before the tools randomly break doesn't make any sense. Screwdrivers degrade too quickly and picks snap far too easily. These decisions were made to balance base raiding, but they also effect the PVE gameplay a lot since much of the best loot is behind high-tier locks. Some players can get this mechanic instinctively, but I am not one of them.
- The horde mechanic needs a bit of work. Puppets (This game's name for zombies) pop into existence too close to the player and too quickly fade away when the player slips away from them without server settings being tweaked.
- Inventory management can feel annoying at times. Moving items to base storage containers requires dragging them one by one, and I have lost more than a few pieces of gear out of containers by double clicking them, thinking they will be placed in my inventory or equipped on my character, only to then have that item placed on the ground under the container, never to be retrieved.
- Stat leveling can feel obtuse, and progresses at a glacial pace. Skills are less offensive in this regard with a few exceptions (such as lockpicking, where even the easiest lock on the training device will be too hard for new lockpickers to train on either for personal experience or to level their character's skill) but your base stats of Strength, Constitution, Dexterity, and Intelligence, depending on the one you with to level, require you to: for Strength and Constitution, spend actual, IRL days with your character spinning a wheelbarrow full of logs while you AFK; for Dexterity, to defy all survival game instincts by leaving your food bar half empty and run a calorie deficit; for Intelligence, brave end-game areas for an implant that upgrades your brain to work more gooder.
- Blood types should be implemented. They were a great source of fun and funny moments when my group would play DayZ with regularity.
- While I personally don't mind much, YOU might not like how much of this game's cosmetic content is in paid DLC while the game is still in early access.

Overall, I think even with many of the minor annoyances, this game offers a lot already, and they're not done yet. There are plenty of flaws, but for me and a lot of others, those flaws are far outweighed by the compelling and deep gameplay on offer.

Now to cap things off, I'm going to issue a wishlist for the devs, which does NOT include bug fixes because I assume they already want to nail the bugs anyway.
- Craftable boats and rafts.
- Bigger land vehicles with more cargo and passenger capacity, maybe some sort of military cargo truck like the American M35 2.5 ton truck or the British Bedford MK as endgame vehicles?
- Just in general more variety of vehicles would be nice, but especially for air and water. Hot air balloons, helicopters, small sail boats, bigger boats that could conceivably be mobile water bases, possibly even vehicles inspired by the so-called "narco subs." Let your imaginations run wild.
- Shipwrecks further out to sea in deeper water, along with SCUBA gear, dive knives, and spearguns. Just give us more stuff to do in the water, please.
- Possibly car trailers for carrying loot, camper trailers to sleep and cook in while on the move, and/or boat trailers so you can lock up your boats behind walls instead of beaching them on the shores?
- Modding support, ASAP. I heard somewhere that this is a goal at or after the 1.0 release, but it would be cool if people could have the opportunity to start working on their mods sooner than that so a healthy modding ecosystem has the chance to develop BEFORE the full release, if that's in the cards.\t\t\t\t\t
