Frankly, it should have already been there but for a lot of very poor decision making.

That said, if you own Rust, DayZ, and maybe even Generation Zero, then you already own SCUM, but SCUM is far worse in implementation.

Without going into the long list of things wrong with this game and server stability...that would take a while...suffice it to say that SCUM is nothing short of a cheap, poorly implemented, money grabbing knock-off of the aforementioned games. I realized this the more I played with mods on DayZ. SCUM is a shameless, badly done copy with a myriad of done-to-death elements from said games. But the map is pretty.

The only reason I have as many hours in game as I do is I believed in a developer that does not warrant that luxury from anyone. I've put in quite a few bug reports in an effort to help SCUM develop as have many others. They've proven time and again with poor updates pushing the game into Early Access purgatory, ignoring the voices of the community, and snarky comments on the forums for anyone who doesn't like what they're shoveling out, that SCUM is not worth the time and effort. Don't take my word for any of this. Go read the forums.

I strongly recommend serious players who care about game quality steer clear of what is an already rapidly sinking ship.

To conclude: SCUM is one of many poster children for why we gamers, who want to see an improvement in game quality, need to stop pre-ordering, stop buying early access, and absolutely start refunding games at the first sign of problems. We should all stop rewarding fly-by-night developers for incomplete, unprofessional work.

As long as we continue doing what we're doing, things will not change and we'll continue having a long list of low quality products to choose from.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis would be the only truely poorly managed game that I've ever played. I've played it on and off for years as I do love the design and concepts - but for ALL of those years, it has been nothing but a frustrating battle against all of the bugs and glitches and game killing faults. Cars drop off the map when the terrain doesnt load in time. Magazines disappear from your inventory when you simply reload. Weapons switch spots in your inventory every time you change out, so when in battle you press #1 and your weapon that was supposed to be #2 comes out instead, and you die. Zombies spawn on top of you, and the exploding ones too so you die automatically, vehicles glitch into fences and blow up, the "extras" in a town dont spawn in when your driving thru them until after you pass them so cars end up inside fenced yards or blow up when a lightpost spawns INSIDE the car, food magically disappears when you press "eat". Every single update over the last 4.5 years that Ive played ends up with multiple patches within days cause the devs mess the whole game up every time. The game has SOOOO many ammo and weapon types, yet no one can understand why cause the gun play mechanics are so bloody bad - how bout developing the game instead of just adding more and more stuff? Why on earth have so many ammo types? No "real" shooter in the world has this many ammo types, and those are games with real and working gun play mechanics so why has this poorly developed survival game got like 70 odd ammo types? Its stupid. Depending on which server you play, the game can run very poorly due to stupid server settings. I wish I didnt like the game as I'd just never ever recommend it to anyone. 5 years in "beta" is an eternity and it hasnt improved much from the early days when we use to have to stand on the back of tractors to get around in a squad. This game will never take off in the way it has potential too because the devs are not competent to get it running correctly. I would strip all of the stupid crap from the game and make it run better before spending resources on dumb crap like "cooking food". To recommend this game would be like recommending someone watch the TV show LOST - an intersting concept that has an appeal to it, yet will cause you nothing but irritation over the longer term.\t\t\t\t\t
