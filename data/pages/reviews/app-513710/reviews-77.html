\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove this type of game so got this when it came out. I played around 16 hours solo and PVP but didnt enjoy it so left it to bake for a bit. Recently downloaded it again to have a fresh play but found it to be utterly boring. The world and look of the game is great with a few bugs but.......... it just isnt fun to play. You get dropped randomly across the map at the start. There doesn't seem to be a starting area to break you in and allow you to explore the crafting and building before you try and explore other areas and die. Then you get charged to respawn with in game currency and you can go negative if you die without earning any ( which i never did). I just wasn't interested enough to find out what i needed to do to earn it and theres nothing that explains anything to you. Feels like being a student in debt and you'll need to work that debt off because i assume you need that currency to progress somewhere later in the game. I died a lot due to the game dropping me in areas that had more than i could deal with and fists are not enough. I suppose i could spend a few hour making all the basic stuff by searching rocks and cutting bushes ( while i'm slowly creeping towards another death) before i go in a building and actually enjoy the game but doing that every time i die.........neh. Searching and finding anything useful is frustrating as most things are empty. The game seems to be designed to be slow and grindy from the start and i would guess you need to put in the time to enjoy this game completely because its far from bad but from what i've played it doesn't make me want to continue playing after a few deaths and getting nowhere, i'm not interested in finding out. I don't want a game where i am a hermit in the woods for days before i go into the actual content of the game and then when i die it charges me to spawn anywhere near my stuff and i'm sure PVP is worse in terms of progression. BORING.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a very difficult survival game to start. You have to build a stone knife and cut up your clothes to make a backpack and then a bow, every time you start or after you die (unless you have a base) After you figure out certain things like controlling infections and blood loss - it's actually quite a fun game.

When you die, you don't just spawn back in your base (if you have one). You have to get enough money in the bank (by selling stuff at the trader) to have $1k to sector spawn. You can then spawn in the sector and run back to your base or a stash or your dead body. If you avoid people, you can stash stuff in chests and lock them and hide them, but if you play with others, you're going to have to build a base. It takes a long time to get the resources to build a base, but it's very rewarding. You have to protect the base with gold locks, BCU Lock and explosives, so someone can't just get into your base and take all your stuff. If you are playing solo, it's a bit tough to get guns unless you master the bunkers or find good loot runs with guns. If you get enough cash you can always buy things at the traders.

To get a car, you need to literally build it. Find a chasis with an engine and then get the parts for it. Then you have to build a garage for it and lock the car and the garage.

It's mechanics are like PUBG, so pubg players will be familiar with the guns and the gun game play.

All in all.. this is a GREAT game... you can spend thousands of hours on this thing, as the map is huge, there are tons of things to do, even if you don't want to confront other players. There are bunkers with puppets (zombies) and special bosses, kill boxes that you need to lock pick (lock picking is a whole other skill in itself) and the character advancement is really rewarding.

Confronting players is a whole other factor to this game. Many seem to squad up, so if you don't have a squad with you, you should probably avoid other squads.

You can play this game as well, with no base. You can hide your chests, you can leave stuff at the traders, and everything you have on you, will be protected when you log off (your character disappears) so if you want, you can just have a lot of storage on your character and then log off and when you log back in, your stuff will be there. When you log off your body disappears, so no one can kill you while you're gone.

All in all.. this is a GREAT game. It's not for a casual player unless you're really patient. It takes time to learn all the little details of this game, but very rewarding.

Eventually as PUBG dies, I can see a lot of those people moving towards SCUM because it's so similar in the fighting style.

Get this game... spend the time. You won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t
