\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSooo, when am I supposed to start having fun?
Because it's not fun to die of starvation or thirst, Or to bleed out from a scratch from a Zombie (puppet) that somehow managed to not get hit while I'm swinging a spiked bat.
I've played Day Z and not a stranger to survival games. At least Sum has better graphics than Day Z, but in my opinion, Scum is what Day Z should've been. Basically feels like an updated version of the same game.
When I started this game, I expected a Storyline? I expected to be told what time period this was and why the Mechs are here. Nothing. Just falling from a plane and parachuting to the ground and you're on your own. Not a single tutorial in the game options. I had to go to the internet for Toots.
The absolute worse aspect of this game is the Crafting. Whatever is in your hand is dropped and whatever you Craft is placed on the ground and NOT IN YOUR INVENTORY. If you're holding 2 weapons on your back and one is in your hand and you search and pickup something into your inventory, whatever was in your hand is on the ground. And if you don't pick it back up and put it back in your hand, hopefully you'll remember where you left it?
That right there is the sucky part for me.
The graphics are fantastic and it really is a beautiful game, that's why I'm not giving this game a thumbs down. But Dammit Man, when am I going to have fun?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot of good and LOT of bad.

First of all, it's early access. So naturally, that comes with a fair amount of jank. I don't want to write a college thesis (even though I probably will) so I'll just briefly go over the goods and the bads:

Good:
-Receives updates fairly often
-Lots of weapons and a pretty fun looting system (although I recommend turning loot up or playing on a server that does so, since in my experience base loot gives you pretty much nothing even in dangerous/hard-to-access areas)
-Added outposts and outpost NPC's that are really nice
-Large map that isn't hideous to look at, is pretty fun to explore, and has quite of few POI's (and more added all the time)
-Some pretty nice realism details that generally (in my experience) don't ruin the fun, like manually loading bullets in magazines and a pretty in-depth health and nutrition system
-PVP is chaotic and insanely fun in the way that only a giant survival game like this could be. Seriously, play on a server. Single Player SCUM is just sad rn.
-Money and trading system that's pretty reasonable (and also abusable, but whatever)
-Servers are completely chaotic in a good way (usually)
-Tons of small details (more than I can list) that show that the devs care about the game and that give off some polish even if there are very glaring flaws

The bad:

-The jank, obviously. Not a dealbreaker since the game is both is both early access and huge, but falling through the map, glitches, stuff not working right, and teleporting enemies are common occurrences.
-Lack of enemy types. All Puppets (aka zombies) are pretty much the same except the explosive gigglers and the new type added in bunkers. This is the big reason I recommend playing on servers for this one, facing the same braindead npc enemies over and over again gets very boring. I'd love to see more changes implemented in this area.
-Screw the mechs. I prefer to play with them off. They plague basically every place on the map where you'd expect to get good military-level loot. Thankfully they can't aim, but they're an ever-present problem that are hard to dispose of without lots of explosives. I can see why some would like them and see them as a challenge (to avoid), but I don't.
-Melee weapons are wholly unreliable. The damage variance is huge and sometimes a direct hit does nothing. I know this technically counts as me complaining about the jank again, but I'd love to have a reason to wield an axe or katana after I find a gun and ammunition.
-The interface makes sense once you get used to it, but it is the total opposite of beginner friendly. Yes, there's a tutorial, but most people don't want to bother and if you try to get your friends into this game (because let's be real, you want to play with friends and not alone), you're gonna have to baby them for quite a while and explain a ton of stuff.
-I know I praised the realism earlier, but there's such a thing as too much. Having to deal with things like hypothermia in a solid 1/3 of the map, hyperthermia from wearing all the good gear in the daytime, leaning from being overencumbered, and getting trenchfoot every time you step into a boat is not fun. I don't care how "realistic" it is.
-Minor complaint, but please add more vehicle types.
-The map gets points for being big and pretty, but man is there a LOT of empty space. Again, early access, yadda yadda, and more POIs are added pretty much every update. There is a lot of stuff to see and loot, but it's usually a several-minute long trek from point A to B unless you have a car, with nothing but roads and nature in between. A somewhat unavoidable problem for a game this big I suppose, but a problem nonetheless.
-Hard to fault the games for this, but public servers can sometimes the bad kind of chaotic. I recommend a server that doesn't allow building at Places of Interest, otherwise players will just wall them off and camp there. I feel bad for blaming the game for players being lame, but I felt the need to bring it up.

Overall, this is a fun survival game to play with friends, but do not expect a flawless or polished experience. There are a lot of issues that will hopefully continue to be resolved by the dev team. There's a lot of potential here, and I do think it will be realized in time. This game will take a lot of time to learn, be frustrating to play, and will have a very slow pace at times. Even so, I've had an absolute blast playing it with friends and will continue to do so. 6.5-7/10.\t\t\t\t\t
