\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEditing Review

There have been some patches since the horde update and I noticed that one specifically addressed "fixed puppets spawning around the player when they shouldn't" and decided to try it out with a friend.

So far I've managed to survive for a few days in the game, get some decent loot and have a few encounters that were very terrifying, but survivable. My friend did not survive the one horde we encountered, and unfortunately his body despawned because he had to choose between dropping across the map or suicide and dropping closer to his body.

I am playing on a PVE server, since the threat from players is honestly more than I can handle. I would rather make friends than enemies.

Things I like:
1. It is challenging.
2. Some changes seem to have been made on infection status, and it doesn't seem to be inevitable from every injury anymore. For example, you can sometimes use regular rags to heal injuries and not get sick immediately.
3. The environment looks really nice, shifting light patterns from clouds keep things dynamic.
4. The ability to stash some money and use that to respawn in a specific zone on death is useful, once you can prepare for it.
5. The updated crafting system is easier to use than before and has come a long way in one update.
6. There are helpful tips that randomly pop up at the right times.

Things I don't like:
1. AND THIS IS A BIG ONE...Melee doesn't feel like it works. I can be standing directly in front of a puppet, and with my reticle over it's face swing at it and nothing happens. I know that this game "enforces" your skill tree, such as when things that you "shouldn't be able to see" might be visible in 3rd person, you still won't see them. Is melee like this as well? When I am swinging is there a % chance to hit? If so, this just feels broken and is very unsatisfying. If I hit it, I should hit it. Right now, If I can't run away I have to find a roof and pray that the puppet won't randomly teleport on top of it.
2. Sometimes puppets come at you from absolutely nowhere, and for seemingly no reason, despite moving at a snail pace while crouched. Stealth seems to be meaningless. Though this is improved somewhat since the last time I played, just not to a point that I feel is realistic. The puppets are like stupid ninjas who immediately know where you are, are able to vault through windows like parkour enthusiasts and just generally have more skill than an animated dead person seems like they should.
3. Unlike DayZ, puppets make very little sound when just lurking around so it's possible to just walk into them out of nowhere. I appreciate what this adds to the game, but I think some balancing needs to be done.
4. The rain effects are disappointing and I would love to see some attention paid to them. It could add a lot of ambiance to the game and that would be great.
5. Player body should stick around longer on death.
6. Some of the crafting recipes don't work correctly, and it is not clear what some of them are asking for.
7. Auto-Equiping gear on pick-up should be optional. I have lost a few items because I'm double clicking to pick things up and suddenly my good shirt is on the ground and I'm wearing garbage.

The developers seem to have reacted quickly to complaints about the horde, and the game is playable at this point, though I think it still has a lot to balance out before being officially released. The dev response is commendable, and hopefully will continue.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly wish I refunded this as it seems to have just gotten worse with patches. Long story short this is a C potentially even B tier game with so many QoL issues dragging it down it feels more like F Tier.

Spawning in is slow and tedious with no way to manipulate the speed of the parachute once it has been opened.
The (broken) quests they had when I first started that guided you through the basic mechanics are gone, now you have to look through an ingame manual because quests are hard apparently.

The animations across the board are a joke, as you statically parachute down with no motion in you or the parachute, zombies glide across the ground like its 2002. Player movement speed is controled by the mousewheel, yet there seems to be only 3 speeds, walk, run, sprint (Aka attract every zombie in 40 feet regardless of line of site to come slide attack you to death) either add more speed increments like tarkov, or don't copy tarkov.

The inventory system though is by far my biggest issue, your inventory slots are determined by your clothing, this means when you drop you have, a half full 2x2 storage with 2 slots used by a credit card, and pants with an empty 2x4 storage capacity, you might also be lucky enough to spawn in a cold area which nets you a hoodie with actual F capacity, but lets assume you didn't get that lucky. You'll need a knife, thats 2x4 and it can't be equipped into a holster slot to free up capacity even though you'll literally always want a knife on you. You now have 2 slots, which is only enough for extremely small items (Money, needles,etc), clothing degrades so don't get hit before getting real clothing otherwise you wont be able to carry that knife anymore.

This game has good features, character, skill, health and vehicle systems on their own aren't bad and have a lot of potential, the problem is first 1-2 hours of this game are a slog at a minimum and anyone who thinks that this is "close" to a 1.0 is insane, unless they somehow pack in a few hundred QoL changes, New Zombie AI and new animations, I can't imagine anything being enough to look like an actual 1.0, unless it's a No Man's Sky style of 1.0 where that actually means 0.1,0 Beta.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR- Scum is like a modded DayZ server that has added things you may or may not want in your survival game.

Overall it's a more "gamey" version of DayZ. Don't let that fool you into thinking it's any less complex. The survival mechanics are deep and very deadly.

There's live traders with a full in game economy, a mini-map, mechs that patrol the high tier loot. Think the same general gameplay loop of DayZ but a totally different vibe. The island feels more alive, there's more things going on than the imposing cold, dead feel of DayZ. And the player interactions are a little bit different because you can spawn anywhere in Scum, so anywhere on the map you could run into someone super geared or a fresh spawn and everything in between, as opposed to the general fresh spawns being huddled on the coast in the south.

Overall I like it and do recommend it but if I had to choose just one I would still go DayZ because of the overall feel of the game. Things that I think Scum has going for it are better graphics, more fleshed out and better crafting and building with it's own UI, and better(?) zombie AI. It has a bright future with constant development being done.

Also there is no mod support as of my writing this review, but I understand it is coming one day, which may be the final nudge I need to get off of DayZ.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing this game on and off through the various updates.
I have only played it PvE with friends, and we have always opted to rent a server and tweak the settings to make the puppets more numerous and harder. While this is more of a PvP focused title i do believe this is up with the best survival simulation experiences i have had. Especially with friends.

Climbing the survival ladder, growing stronger, finding vehicles, and finding a spot to build an outpost is all done very well in my opinion. I wish the animals worked better, but we never eeeeeever see deer or boars or the likes anymore. We can spawn them, but hunting animals for food is simply a no go, wich is a real shame.

As we edge ever closer to 1.0 release i see vast improvements since i started playing at day one.
Looking forward to giving another real go at 1.0!\t\t\t\t\t
