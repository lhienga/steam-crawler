I want to play all these games but i dont have the time or attention span to learn that i need to turn a rock into a knife then cut a bush, chop the logs into sticks, then into smaller sticks, then cut more into rope and a make shift rag and still battle the elements....just to make a freaking stone axe? If you play rust or Dayz and are familiar with those game mechanics and crafting style this game will be great for you.

If you're trying to enjoy the content style Dayz and rust have to offer without the STEEP STEEP learning curve, this game is no different than them.


Tl;Dr: Learning curve is 10's of hours like Dayz, rust, project zomboid. If you were expecting to get a taste of those games content from this game with an easier learning curve; this isnt the game for you.


I would like to see the devs put more effort into making the learning curve of the game more enjoyable, whether that be through tutorial missions or a seperate map without the dangers. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tGuys..... this game..... hands down, another ridiculous survival game! If you've been watching my twitch or kick streams recently you'll see a bunch of my base building, gardening and cooking, car upgrading, an assortment of gun upgrading and customization gameplay. If not be sure to alt-tab over and check out twitch.tv/dubido or kick.com/dubido to get a small glimpse at some of the new 9.5 updates (12/21/23) that "The Devs" have released.
I first started playing this game back in 2019 when it was first released.. ish.. and loved it. Even though it was an early access game back then it felt like a full game. There was a huge map to explore, tons of cool and interesting crafting options, all kinds of neat gear to loot and find, vehicles to find and fix up and drive and fill up with all sorts of gear. Usually for me, in "zombie games" when they can run as fast as you and take multiple hits from your weapons unless your actually aiming for the head. I'm talking about the amazing end graphics and the realism and heart pounding moments when the zombies are on your ass. Usually in zombie games, for some reason I for real get scared lol. I know chicken right.. I'ts okay, I just tell my self I'm saving my real heat attack for when something like a zombie apocalypse like 28 Days Later or Dying Light happens.

Needless to say this game strives for realism. Down to the calories and vitamins your in taking affecting your stamina, constitution and all sorts of stats.. I'ts actually almost too in depth tbh. But I like it. You'll burn hours without even trying. The amount of zombies and the intensity was actually not too bad for me so I was able to put some time into the game. In the new update the zombies can now actually jump through windows and break down doors and that put my anxiety to the next level lol and I'm sure that's what the developers were looking to do for all those seasoned players out there.
But anyways, back when I first started playing this game I was able to get up to a point where I had survived for so long, had a truck full of all the guns and gear I'd need to survive. I turned my back for a few minutes to kill a deer for some food I had left my truck too close to a supply drop. The supply drops give you a chance to get some awesome gear that you would not otherwise been able to get and give you a little more of and edge to survive. They have a countdown before they self destruct and explode and when this one that I had just cleared blew up it took my truck and everything in it :'(. I rage quit then and there. I managed to clip the moment on twitch so it would haunt me for ever because I was ready to never play again..

Now, 4 years later I decided to pick the game back up and play it with some friends and I'm having a blast. I'm not big into the PVP aspects of these types of games because of raids and looting other players things, especially after you've put hours and hours into a game and then someone comes along, robs and destroys everything you have, one random day, while your offline and at work in real life trying to pay the bills JUST KILLS all of the enjoyment of the game for me.
I was able to find a PVE public server with an awesome admin team and discord server and have managed at this point to put just over 207 hours into the game. Thanks to these friendly and super helpful people that I've met on this server I'm learning so much more about the game I either didn't get to experience or try or that has come out in updates since I first played.

So regardless if you are into PVP or PVE this game has huge potential and I can see why even after 5 years of being out its still under development. If you haven't played, I highly suggest it if you into survival game. It has a PUBG feel with regards to graphic and weapons. A Rust or ARK feel in the PVE and PVE aspect and is its own mutated zombie creature when it comes to crafting, food/ cooking/ metabolism and in my eyes #1 in a zombie survival game.

Thanks for reading if you made it this far,
I appreciate you taking the time.
Please leave a like and or a comment. Let me know what you think.
Also if you didn't already, check me out on twitch, Kick & YT.
Thanks guys!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're thinking this is a PVP game then you'll be slightly disappointed. It does have dozens of PVP servers and the game has all the PVP elements but it is primarily a PVE game with PVP bolted on.

Most of your time will be spent looting. Around 70% i'd say. The rest will be spent travelling, base building, and trading unwanted stuff.

This game is more of a survival simulator. It has punishing mechanics which'll kill you if you're not aware of them, and enough dangers to keep you on your toes. Add in PVP and you WILL feel the lose of all your work.

I recommend this game because it is by far the most realistic survival game I've come across.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame has it's flaw, but pretty solid. It's so close to be the ultimate survival game and should borrow some features from other game out there.

Played 120h on v095, PVE only with friends.
Encountered some bugs like stuff falling under containers when equipping directly from it. Player corpse disappearing after death from time to time. Puppets spawning out of thin air in our face or inside room we just cleared. Melee hitbox not registering quite often(mostly with knives). Battery vanishing from battery charger.
Got stuck in the floor in some area with sentinels (friends had to punch me to get me unstock lol)

Insane skill grinding.
Game is lacking PVE content a little bit and would love to see some kind of missions. Escort, fetch, rescue, doesn't need to be too complicated.
Fighting non-puppet NPC would be so cool.

In PVE there is no point in upgrading the base and twig is fine. I think that need to change and an optional game mode similar to 7days to die with horde coming to you at night or something like that would be a good incentive to improve base defense. It could scale difficulty based on fame point.

Weed growth in garden is insane and should be tuned down, or give a huge buff to weed repellant.
I liked the cooking system, but it could use some QoL, dragging item one by one on a recipe of 10 ingredient is tedious. Merging stack like we do for bullets is a must or you get stuck with ton of 2/10 stack you can't use. Shift + click to transfer items over container to recipe should be bare minimum.

when switching firing mode on riffles, I couldn't see what mode I was on most of the time, the switch is outside the screen most of the time. It should show on the gun icon on the toolbar like it does when looking at the number of bullets.

Loot quality vs risk felt off quite often. Mostly in POI guarded with sentinel, giving the same loot as the police station while some other POI gave military stuff for free (the school and basket ball court in the big city near C3).

Melee weapon damage felt off and could use a rework. We should see some kind of indicator. An axe taking 5-6 hits to kill a puppet and a cleaver taking only 2 hits. Chainsaw taking 5-6 hit, while a bayonet is one-shotting every puppet.

More animals would be nice, we didn't bother once with the hunting minigame. I tried a few time and never found anything.
Wolf and bear were pretty cool, but encounters happened very rarely.

Vendor should sell screw drivers in PVE or at least be a setting easy to change in the server settings sell screw driver= true or false...

Water well need a buff... that thing was constantly dry just from the cooking recipe.

Cleaning cloth was tedious and required wayyyy too much soap. We didn't even bother cleaning dirty piece of gear and just eat antibiotic instead to deal with infections.
This could be fixed by allowing us to wash cloth with water only (soap making it quicker), or using like 2 use of soap to clean 100% (I think it's currently 1 unit for 10%).

Painting vehicules would be awesome...\t\t\t\t\t
