So much potential in the game sacrificed to new features, faulty flows, and bad jokes
You will find yourself stranded with unfinished or bugged mechanics, an intentional lack of information or care for the player immersion and time, and outright usability traps.

● Crafting is bugged with the game unable to tell you why you can't craft something for which you have all the items on/around you, and the 'Craft' button is enabled, indicating that you can craft … but you can't.

● There's only one map available, no proc. gen maps atm. But you still find rocks, trees, or walls levitating mid air. You can get stuck inside poorly designed building volumes where it will let you go through a stairway but not return, blocking you with an invisible wall. Jumping through the windows you will often see under the terrain, and you can even get killed for falling into the void after such a jump. Lastly, water reflections are broken.

● Highly unrealistic character movement and fall mechanics – simple jump while running the terrain can result in the same minor injury as a 5th floor fall from a building. The running speed can be ridiculously fast, and running to the sides has no slow down effect; same for the jumps.

● No UE5 benefits, as the game engine is a custom clone of UE4, making it hard to upgrade.

● You can't eat with a mask on, but the game won't tell you, just show the 'Eat' option disabled with no explanation why, so newbies need to ask in chat. A simple 'Eat (get your mask off first)' would do. Some doors suffer from the same, when you see the interaction hand but it won't tell you why you can't open them, while some other doors actually will.

● Drink petrol for fun – when you approach a water well, the primary action would be 'Drink' or 'Check' (no need to check, you know it's empty, otherwise it would be 'Drink'). When you approach a large gas tank at a Petrol station, the primary action is 'Check'. But when you approach a Petrol pump, the primary action you unintentionally use is 'Drink', ruining your vitals, because that's for sure the first thing you want to do.

● Super un/realistic sound fx of a farting person is more important than believable animal or storm sounds (wolf is a joke – sounds like one of the devs simply used his own voice).

● Hunt animals by solving riddles – they don't appear until you find 3 'clues' to their location. Even though you can clearly hear them, you can't see them. Wolves and bears are an exception, those can be seen without any magic; go figure.

● Poor communication of changes to crucial game mechanics – the game will just let you think you are still good until you aren't, when you learn the hard way, even if that means losing hours of progress.

● Poor official servers management with no waiting queue while connecting to a full server (have to reconnect just to be booted again) … frequent lags and restarts, no warning or countdown when a restart is about to happen, the generic message of the day every 10 mins that makes you feel absolutely at the bottom of devs priorities list.

The sad thing is most of these issues are easy to fix or at least copy a well established patterns from other games. The deeper you dive into the game, the more you are reassured that even after years of development, the game immersion, realism, and your experience are simply not a priority. Some of them (like the can't eat with a mask on) are even faulty regarded as 'realistic', while completely misunderstanding and disregarding basic usability principles.

With this being said, the hope of fixing the game seems slim, with new versions mainly focused on new features and mechanics. Investing too much time in the game might leave you with a funny taste in your mouth (after drinking the fuel), feeling robbed of your time, and deceived with glancy visuals with rotten guts.\t\t\t\t\t
