\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a REAL LIFE type game. If you don't like long drawn out tasks, this isn't for you. If you want the challenge of doing things in a process like you would in real life then go for it. It's a lot of fun PvE style which is how I'm playing but, you can jump in a public server and try to survive against other players in a PvE and PvP environment.
The tasks are tedious and drawn out to build a base and gather supplies. Over 40 hours in and I still haven't found all of the things I need to make a proper recipe for GOOD food. You can run around and grab little things to snack on to stay alive but, you really want those recipe books to get the right stuff.
Fighting Zombies is hard even on the lowest settings. You will take damage, you will bleed out. If you manage to stop the bleeding you will still get an infection that you need to find antibiotics to cure.
Th good thing is that there are towns where you can find Pharmacies to get the proper items for this. You will also find police stations for guns and ammo. The traders are there but, not super helpful. They charge a lot for their items, you don't get a whole lot for that price and, they don't give you much money for the items you take to sell them.

Over all it's a lot of fun if you have the time to put into it and it's not a one hour to check on things and your done game until you've gathered, ground out, died, re-spawned, died again and ran for your life for a few hours at a time.
I recommend to anyone that likes a challenge. I don't recommend if you just want to shoot zombies. There are other games for that! This is as close to real life as it gets. It's only going to get better from here!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, I just started a new game after the .95 update, so I can't comment on how "broken" the new features are. But what I CAN tell you is this game has great potential, but it suffers from what most modern games suffer from, viz: trying to do too much while neglecting the core game play loop. And on top of that, there is a REALLY annoying lack of QoL options, like a rest till daybreak option, for example! Swimming also needs an overhaul, I can't tell you how many times I've drowned because I had gear on. The overall game play IS fun, granted, however, it's the small things that really break this game for me. One good example is zombies reaching through doors and windows to hit you, even closed doors. There aren't enough enemy NPCs (human ones) in the single player game. Not to mention, not a lot to actually DO in SP besides being a loot goblin. Why have SP if you aren't going to work on making it fun? I have no desire to play this game in multiplayer. At least not right now. Lets move onto the crafting system, it's totally GREAT! I think it's probably the best crafting system I've ever run across in a video game. Not only does it give you multiple crafting options, but the UI is smartly done, and easy to use, even for a first timer. That being said, however, I thing an opportunity was lost with not including more craft-able weapons in what I would call "middle primitive" category, like a flintlock musket, or an early 1851 Colt Navy, Black powder guns, I think would be a great middle ground for the player character, instead of jumping straight into improvised modern firearms from a wooden bow and arrow. Just my two cents. Next let's talk about the base building. It's...alright? I mean, personally I don't actually build bases, it's VERY grindy. My biggest complaint is two-fold. First, if I use a preexisting building, and set my flag beside it, I shouldn't have to worry about puppet spawns inside my "safe" area. It's just annoying. And second, I would VERY much like to see the ability to destroy all the existing furniture (once empty) and fortify the structure. I mentioned these two aspects, because in reality, that's all there is to do in SP. I hope they add in more features and NPCs and interactions into the game, as it is, it's a bit too bare bones to pay 20 bucks for IMO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've enjoyed this game in the past- even playing only on PVE servers. The survival mechanics, the rarity of loot, and the puppets made it an interesting challenge to scout out a town and scavenge for supplies.

Unfortunately, I can't really enjoy that aspect of the game anymore with The Horde update. Where, previously, working my way up to finding a gun or two would feel like a good goal, now it is a death sentence. If you use a gun- even in a small town- it will cause puppets to randomly spawn all around you and rush you. With their ability to jump over terrain and track you rather quickly, this means that you either leave and hope they don't follow, or you die.

This discourages the systematic clearing of buildings that I once enjoyed because it doesn't matter if you cleared that police station if one bullet will spawn more puppets than were even in the building to begin with.

Additionally, it has been my experience that repair kits are some of the most rare loot to find. This means that if you succumb to the random horde of puppets that spawned because you used a gun you finally found, your gun and all of your gear will be mostly broken, and it will be a long term struggle to even find items to repair them back to normal. Where once that was a risk that you had to consider (do I want to bring my nice gun and risk dying, and lowering its durability), now it is simply a mechanic that, paired with the hordes, means you should never use the nice things you get. Therefore, there's no point in getting them in the first place.

This game really is one of a kind and I cannot stress how hopeful I am that they continue to add content and, hopefully, to fix things like The Horde.\t\t\t\t\t
