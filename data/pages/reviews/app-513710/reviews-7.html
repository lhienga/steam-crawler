Operators, I have a briefing!

Overview:
This is an attempt at a hardcore zombie survival with elements of science fiction. The lore isn't the most interesting for a zombie survival, with pretty basic Sci-Fi (ish) take on things really.

Positives:

Survival Mechanics: The game has a highly detailed survival experience, with mechanics that go further than hunger and thirst, including bodily functions like metabolism, mineral and vitamins, excretion. (You can pee on your enemies) This realism adds depth to the game play and challenges players to manage their diet. So don't only eat trash food like me.

Character Customization: The game allows for decent customization of your character, from physical appearance to skills and abilities. This depth of customization adds a personal touch to the gameplay, allowing players to tailor their experience to their preferences a bit. It feels similar to choosing something in fallout for a characters beginning stats. Kinda sorta.

Crafting: The game has some pretty decent crafting, with different tables and stations allowing you to craft more things than you can with your bare hands. Its a pretty solid formula for crafting, and is much like any other successful survival game with crafting.

Variety of Weapons and Equipment: The game offers a not so large array of weapons, gear, and equipment for players to scavenge and utilize in their survival efforts. Basic firearms, melee weapons, and ranged weapons.

Positive Note: The game COULD BE.............so much more.
It feels like a great foundation for a survival PvEvP game.

But for why you're here. Why you've read this far, and want to know the big secret, you will read further.


Negatives:

Performance Issues: I and many others experience performance issues and bugs, including lag, crashes, and optimization struggles. These technical issues detract from the overall gameplay experience and can make the game difficult to enjoy. My cherry on top for these issues; in SINGLE PLAYER, I've even had tons of items I've collected in crates, in a garage, and my own inventory, DISAPPEAR, after logging off and back on from a break.

Lack of Content Updates: Despite early promise, Scum has struggled to deliver consistent content updates and improvements. This has led to a stagnant gameplay experience, with little to incentivize long-term engagement. BUT, they haven't had a problem shoving out a sponsored DLC with a very famous actor. Cause that's what we ($$$they needed$$$) needed.


Conclusion:
While Scum offers a unique survival experience with its realistic mechanics and extensive customization options, it ultimately falls short due to its performance issues, and lack of content updates. Unless you're a die-hard fan of hardcore survival games and willing to overlook these flaws, it's advisable to steer clear of Scum and explore other titles in the genre.

I really cant put it simpler, its just a buggy boring game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has been in 'early access' for like 6-7 years now and it still is massively unfinished, broken and full of bugs - yet it costs like a full price game with extra DLCs to purchase on top of that. Yet the game fails to function even at a basic level.
Essentially this is a running / bandage simulator made in UE so at least the trees look realistic I guess? Which makes it look and feel as generic as any other garage projects made in UE after 2 weeks of experimenting with its asset store.

The mechanics that are 'implemented' in the game are utterly broken for the most part:

Unarmed combat doesn't do anything - even at max level and top strength you get killed.
Melee combat is even worse because half the time the weapon just swings through the puppets.
Then, through a combat armor, helmet, backpack filled with stuff, a hoodie, shirt, undershirt etc... a puppet even just glances at you causing your arteries to burst and you have to bandage yourself for 2 hours to stop the bleeding. I guess being punched through several layers of armor is somehow causing one to bleed from 5x open wounds?

My character is 150kg according to the BCU, just because he / she has muscles. That's really an accurate simulation.

Character stats are tied to character looks. You can't be strong AND durable without being fat? Also being intelligent to any degree means you somehow can't be also strong, because.... reasons.

Stats are also randomly assigned to skills. I guess it takes tremendous dexterity to drive a car, and parachute all the same, and both of these activities really really require you to be skinny. I guess fat people can't drive? And what does intelligence have to do with stealth? Having high stamina means you can run forever or stay in a fight longer, but... having high stamina (and running everywhere) somehow doesn't count, you are still fat because you aren't 'dexterous' enough to lockpick or improvise gunpowder - yeah makes sense -.-

Nature respawns into areas where it has no business being anymore. You chop down a tree, build a house of it and the next day the very same tree is grown back into and through the house you've built from that tree.

You may disable puppets vaulting through windows and opening doors, but fret not because they can still go through walls at their leasure or hit you through the wall, again causing you to bleed for 3 hours.

Everything causes the player to bleed. A puppet hit you through 50kg backpack from behind - you bleed 3x C1 and a C2.
You land a bit harder than should from a height that DOES NOT break your leg but your sole bleeds?? You get bleeding abrasions for walking barefoot, but your completely fine and dedicated combat boot falls apart after having walked in it for an hour. That's some military quality boots right there. I wonder if my character will start to bleed from being hungry or thirsty? Which is just obnoxious too. Can't go half an hour without moaning for being hungry or thirsty, then you drink two gulps of water immediately causing bladder issues.

Puppets struggle to find their waypoints. They relentlessly keep playing the running animation against a railing or wall instead of just going around it. Puppets colliding into one another does the same thing, the keep running in place forever or until one of them is killed.

Parachute spawning is bugged. At least one out of ten times it lands me in places that force me to suicide. Such as landing on top of large trees. Landing on utility poles. Landing on windmills (if it has a slight angle then you can land on its side so you have to press any button first to fall to your death). Once my character was parachuted out of bounds into the open sea because of the wind.

Things that you would use disappear. Such as building / crafting materials, vehicles, weapons, food, puppets and their clothes. Things reappear that should not - such as cleared trees and bushes (in single player mode). I mean can I at least control the vegetation within my base area?

All in all this has the potential to be a good game, but until the devs put more effort into adding willy warmers and 'celebrities' (whoever they are, never heard of any of those names) in the game instead of finishing the game actually, it remains to be EA for another 10 years while shamelessly asking for your money now for even stuff that should just be part of the game by default.

DEVS: fix the game as is before adding a new pink fluffy unicorn skin to the AK47, I don't think anyone cares how creative you can be with willy warmers and xmas ornaments as long as pretty much everything in the game is broken. It's been 7 years in EA ffs. Entire franchises rise and fall during this time so give me value for my money please.
