SCUM has a lot of impressive features and without doubt some players love it. It doesn't upset me that I disagree with numerous design choices in the evolution of the game since I bought it. SCUM was one of many games I bought because of encouragement of friends that wanted me to join them, and having kept my eye on it since first release I knew at the outset that for me it was a gamble.

Pretty soon those friends dropped out and I went on learn the hard way, playing quite a bit of solo, mostly on survival servers with little pvp apart from hosted events. Scavenging, crafting, building, surviving puppets and animals was a good pastime now and then.

The game world is intricate and varied. It can be beautiful in some ways, yet you are not surviving in an idyllic world such as some central European woodland campground of DayZ for instance. The island terrain is rough and challenging and you can seldom just bee-line from point A to point B. Like many elements in SCUM you need to be focused and willing to master many skills to be effective. This relatively hard core survival challenge is something that SCUM does better than most any other single game at this time.

When raft crafting and sailing became functional they worked great and became one of my favorite features in solo play, so when the rafts were removed for the modular vehicle re-work (and possibly water physics updates?) I was disappointed. For a long time I kept checking updates until I lost confidence that there was an effective plan and execution.

I've got loads of early access experience in many games and SCUM has actually been one of the better ones. In time I will likely try it again. Meanwhile I find the game traders and economy system quirky and not enjoyable. I was subscribed to Luthais way back in Miscreated and recommend him for players who are serious about SCUM. He hosts some good servers too.

I respect that some friends and others are unhappy with the development and sale of numerous DLC during early access. To me that's not a big deal as long as they continue to develop in good faith. At least they are not "pay to win" items as far as I can see. The supporter pack is comparable except for the wristwatch. It is not "cosmetic only" since you can use it to tell time within the game UI when others cannot.

As a budget gamer that paid at or near full price just to jump in and play with friends I never could bring myself to donate extra for the watch when I remain on the fence about the game and many players have bought SCUM for 50%. If I'm mistaken about the watch I welcome a dev response, otherwise please add some form of this feature for all players.

I will update my review in the future based upon subsequent game play experience.\t\t\t\t\t
