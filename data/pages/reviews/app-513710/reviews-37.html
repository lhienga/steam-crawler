\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview Updated on 2/1/24: (increase .5 stars from Jan-17th) = 4/5 stars

TLDR: This Open-World Survival (OWS) game is very fun once you know what you are doing and can tweak some of the settings for your playstyle. There's been a slew of patches recently that's greatly increased my the enjoyment and I feel it's on track to be close to a 5-star game.

Top Features:
- Fantastic graphics (absolutely stunning vistas on a 4k GPU)
- Exquisitely detailed (every in-game object has a purpose or use)
- Great sound effects (positional audio and environmental sounds are well done)
- All the main OWS activities: weapon/tool crafting, base building, hunting, fishing, farming, drivable vehicles (including airplanes), squad-based combat, and a ton of random loot to find.
- Driving mechanics and physics are nearly perfect (you can slide, drift, offroad, and use your vehicle as a weapon)


Individual Ratings:

- Graphics (4/5) - varied terrain map (essentially warm and cold biomes); gorgeously rendered (you get better vistas than Ridley Scott's "Rebel Moon"); very realistic weather and daylight effects; great item display as well (with nearly everything showing on your character).

- Sound (3/5) - sound effects are good; could be better in places. I'd like to see more atmospheric sounds; more environmental sounds; and varied weapon/firing effects (all the guns sound essentially the same).

- Gameplay (4/5) - You can tweak just about EVERYTHING to your liking; so if you are finding the default game too difficult, go into the Server Setting and adjust to your liking. This can GREATLY improve the game if you're less into the hardcore survival and more into exploration. I found that increasing the fame and skill progression rates, reducing the puppet damage slightly, and increasing the day length turned the game from 1/5 to nearly 4/5 stars for me.

- Travel (3/5) - The island is HUGE with no fast travel (admins can teleport), but that's to be expected. Bicycle is FUN! Great way to see the map while avoiding most aggro. Dirt bikes and vehicles are the way to go for trips into dangerous areas to scavenge.

- Lockpicking (2/5) - works like Skyrim (insert pick and then turn with a screwdriver until the time runs out); however, it's too easy to break a pick and/or screwdriver. Improvised picks should be stackable.

* Fishing (2/5) - I really wanted this to work. I loving fishing in my other open-world survival games; but this requires too many components (including some rare components) that are lost when your line breaks. Too easy to break the line. Too hard to reel in fish. This should be simpler.

Cooking (3/5) - Cooking is good; but finding things to cook is hard. Animals are nearly non-existant; I've played for hudnreds of hours and have seen less than a dozen animals in the wild. Cook books are rare and necessary to cook anything beyond grilled meat/veggies and coffee/tea.

The bad:

Item Loss (CRITICAL): lots of item loss bugs. Sometimes items will just randomly disappear from your inventory if the server is restarted; or are lost by putting them in your hand, taking them into your hand from a container, or simply moving them from one container to another. Putting non-base items on the ground for any signification period will also cause them to despawn. Good for an improvised "trash can", but terrible for actually trying to hold onto stuff you find and deal with once you have some storage.

Combat bugs - puppets will jump around you (disappearing and reappearing) as you hit them; causing your previously accurate strike to miss. This is both unrealistic, looks terribly, and is extremely frustrating. The hitbox is also funky in both 1st and 3rd player. Seems to be a "optimal range" for striking the enemy depending on the hit location; but understanding where those are are difficult to figure out ingame.

Falling through geometry - puppets will frequently fall through the terrain, such as rocks, floors, trees, etc and sometimes disappear through the ground. There is some mechanic to pop them back up to the terrain layer, which causing them to bob like a cork, disappearing into and out of the object.

NPC Pathing Issues - the "find path to player" algorithm needs to be tweaked; puppets get stuck on corners or in open doorways quite easily. The server also doesn't seem to respect the settings for "puppets can open doors" and "puppets can vault windows".

Original Review:

Too difficult to start. Not enough inventory space; not enough survival items. I can play for 2 hours and die of starvation because there's no food to be found. Needs a lot of balancing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComing from 600+ hours of gameplay

This to me is an all around great game. This is exactly what I look for when coming home after work, sit down, relax and just play and I have the ability to chose whether or not I look for trouble or not. Without having to worry about sweating profusely in games like CoD. This is also a really good game to play with friends, as it's something that can require a good amount of teamwork. Or just something to relax and build houses with friends. See each others creativity. The activities are also very enjoyable, while still rough around the edges, I.E fishing it's still a lot of fun! Although, I do wish the hunting was a little more on the realism side. Like tree stands, or maybe an improvised blind? The sound design is something worth noting as well, to me the gunshots carry a good long ways inside the map. Haven't really tested how far one can hear them in the distance.
But, now we move on to the difficult factor. If you want difficulty. Well here it is, especially in the new abandoned bunkers. One wrong move and your getting mauled by Razor. And if you don't medical on you well just commit suicide, cause you're at that point. Probably just saying that because I suck at the game. Like I mentioned before, if you want difficulty, it's here and in a lot of ways, exhilarating.
One thing I do want to mention is the developer communication with the player base, I really feel it's some of the best communication out there, but I still think it doesn't get noted enough.
Yes this is an early access game, and I've had it since .3 and to watch it grow to what it is now is remarkable to say the least.
Try it out you might like it!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter leaving a "not recommended" review, I still ended up playing Scum more. I had been unhappy about several glaring flaws in a game that has been in early access for 5 years but mostly hated not being able to simply start your own server to play with friends. You have to go to some website to pay for renting a server to do that. I want a good and challenging PVE co-op experience with my family, not one of those games where you come back to find that other players raided your base and took your stuff, or one where other players far more established with armor and weapons kill you on sight, making you start over with nothing every time. I also really didn't like that this game, still early access, already has several DLC packs with basic things like hairstyles and emotes that should have just been added in for us players who already paid to be play testers for the devs...

All that aside, I did as a developer who responded recommended. I found an unofficial PVE focused server with less painful death penalties, got more used to how the game works, bought it for my daughter to play it with me, and some hours later I decided it was time to come change this review. After the learning curve, it can be pretty fun and often beautiful to look at. I switched my review to recommended, but not for long...

After dumping probably 20+ hours into playing together on the server we liked, we came back to find that it was gone. Guess the owner stopped paying for it or something. Neither of us want to start all over on another server, especially knowing this will likely just happen again.

My daughter and I both got this on sale for $20. At that price I would call this a fair deal if we could host a game just for friends. We can't, so if that sounds like the kind of sad total waste of your time that you don't want to deal with, avoid this game until many years from now when they finish it, assuming they even will.\t\t\t\t\t
