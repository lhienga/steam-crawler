\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSCUM is realistic take on survival mechanics, That has both impressed and frustrated me since its early access release. While the game's attention to detail and innovative features are admirable, it's essential to address two significant issues: poor frame rates and the impact of overly detailed "jiggle physics" on the female character models.

One of the main concerns for me revolves around SCUM's performance in terms of frame rates. The game's demanding graphics and physics simulations can strain even high-end gaming systems, resulting in sub-optimal frame rates. This issue can severely hinder the overall gaming experience, causing frustration and detracting from the immersive survival atmosphere that SCUM aims to create. This notable in the latest release which along with low frame rates suffers from late object drawing.

To address the frame rate concerns, the developers could consider additional game optimisation. This might involve refining resource-intensive graphical elements, tweaking the rendering pipeline, or providing additional graphics settings to allow players to tailor the game to their hardware capabilities. Frequent updates have been forthcoming from the developer so there is ample opportunity to roll out improvements.


While SCUM strives for realism in its character models, the implementation of overly detailed "jiggle physics" on female characters can contribute to distracting and unrealistic visuals, particularly during intense gameplay moments.

To address this concern, the developers might consider implementing an option in the settings menu that allows players to adjust or disable the jiggle physics effect to their own tastes and system capabilities. Providing players with the ability to customise their gaming experience in this manner would strike a balance between realism and player preference, ensuring a more inclusive and enjoyable environment for all players.\t\t\t\t\t
