\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember when this game was in development, and it was pretty apparent that Ubisoft was going to go back to the basics, this time with the age of imperialism, or at least Napoleonic era. The DLC has been massively serialized to make the complete game a pretty strong addition to the series. While it was pleasing to see the base game getting back to the roots of the series, so the trials, tribulations, and rewards of colonizing a new and unexplored world, after a couple of additions to the canon moving into the future and space, it was also a little disappointing to slam through the main campaign in the 10-20 hour range.
Microtasks were adapted from other popular franchises, but were not perfectly implemented. This is a nice thought, but can easily add a couple of hours of gameplay. Moving away from historical alliances was also a slight loss. This may have been done to give writers more narrative freedom, but a large part of the allure of the earlier versions was the feeling that you controlled Jamestown, Barbados, New Orleans, or Ft. Orange, if you had the skill to build to a historical level of accuracy.
Where this game wins big is through the engaging narrative, tasteful integration of just a little bit more technology, and dogged defense of proven mechanics. There were moments when I felt that a certain formula had been lost and was rediscovered in this edition.
In terms of complexity, going back to the original Annos, it is a little difficult to say. This game definitely has a lighter learning curve than the early games and there are not really hidden mechanics in play. It also feels more intricate than the space-faring versions, though I would not say it necessarily strikes the ideal balance yet.\t\t\t\t\t
