\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTentative recommend on this one. I had a bit of trouble figuring the game out at first as there are a lot of mechanics and while the game offers guidance there are many mechanics that slip through the cracks. Once you do get a hang of your finances though, it's quite fun to build up your company.

My main complaint though is that it just feels like the game ends by the time you get to engineers. There's no further military advancements as artisans give you access to line ships, and military in general doesn't come up at all unless your AI opponents are aggressive, which they only really seem to be at the highest level. Most of the engineer advancements are really just to optimize the production of your factories through electricity rather than add further mechanics. Also, the new world is unlocked once you reach artisans, but there's not a whole lot going on there. There's only two tiers of citizen on the new world and it's really just a pale shadow of farmer and worker. It's little more than an extra step towards providing what artisans and engineers demand, doesn't add a whole lot.

I should note though, that this is my experience with the base game with no DLCs. I feel somewhat ripped off having purchased the base game, as every season pass altogether costs the same price as the gold edition of the game, which is the game+all season passes. I can understand missing out on a little bit of a deal having just bought the base game, but that seems like an insultingly wide margin for anyone who purchases the base game like me. If you are interested in this, just buy the gold edition and don't rip yourself off like I did, as the DLCs do seem to add quite a lot.\t\t\t\t\t
