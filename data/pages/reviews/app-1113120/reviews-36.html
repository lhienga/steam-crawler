\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's like Frostpunk, but in space. Where in Frostpunk you're constantly burning coal to survive, here you are constantly consuming Iron to repair your hull.

The game starts out strong and is quite engaging. The story and setting are compelling, and the first half of the game is pretty good. You're constantly challenged and solving issues and it's fun.

That said, as you get to late game, the game mechanics start to get in your way in an annoying way. You're constantly cramped for space. You might be rebuilding your base multiple times as a result, so it kind of feels like you have to solve the same problems over and over. You have to turn things off and on a LOT, because for some reason they consume power/workers even when they are idle. This gets very tedious. In short, the challenges are fun to solve at first but it kind of gets old by the end.

If you happen to do well and collect too many resources, it can also be a huge problem, again because of space limitations. And the last chapter... is pretty much another re-organizaiton task. You need to build a lot of XXX which likely means demolishing something else.

I would've liked to see more "scenarios" in addition to the main campaign. Could maybe track a different ship's story. Because of this, I would recommend the game on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIXION is a fantastic game and beating it for the first time was a really fun and challenging experience. The store is REALLY interesting and rather unique with some Battlestar Galactica-esque feelings of "you can't stay in one place too long, you never know what could throw a spanner in the works" at times and the first time I saw the jump animation left me with chills.

Unfortunately, the game does lack a lot of replayability, you can play again to go after certain achievements, endings or make different choices, but as far as general playthroughs go, there's very clearly "right and wrong" and "better and worse" choices to make that funnel your playstyles down a certain path. It'd be cool to have some update/DLC to add branches to sectors instead of the linear progression the game has, give the players the opportunity to explore off the beaten path to find more lore and give a bit more freedom in where the player chooses to go.

The difficulty is something that hit me like a freight train on launch day and it's absolutely unforgiving at times, with a lot of management to do from making sure facilities have the right resources being produced and ships are bringing in the right materials (SPOILERS FOR LATER CHAPTERS) to rapidly managing your defensive countermeasures against the Protagoras . Failing, learning and replaying is the way the game is meant to be played.

The later addition of really in-depth difficulty options that you can change ON THE FLY whilst in-game did allow me to go back and play the game just to experience the whole narrative without the difficulty from beginning to end and return again to try at harder difficulties as the narrative and story is honestly pretty interesting and something I'd love to see built on. This does add a small level of replayability but not really enough to keep you engaged for hours on end or keep you coming back.

One more thing: THE SOUNDTRACK

Guillaume David wins my "ABSOLUTE BANGER SOUNDTRACK FOR A SCI-FI GAME" award until the end of time with the IXION OST. This soundtrack has no right going as hard as it does and I'd highly recommend giving it a listen on Spotify/YouTube even if you have zero plans on playing the game. It's truly something special and total ear candy with it's synthy orchestral goodness!

Overall, I'd recommend picking this up during a sale. Don't expect to get a lot of time out of it but it is a truly incredible game with a lot of quirks and a nice degree of challenge should you wish to find it.\t\t\t\t\t
