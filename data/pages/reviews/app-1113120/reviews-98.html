\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Steam refuses to recognize newlines. My apologies) I hate this game. It's sad, I loved Mechanicus so I figured I'd give it a shot. The game isn't fun, and the "challenge" is mostly trying to min/max a system that attempts to kill you at every turn. This is not player agency that requires skill and planning, this is finding the one critical path playing the game in exactly the one way required to not lose. Choose the wrong option? Death spiral. Build too fast? Death spiral. Build too slow? We need to punish the player with a "you took too long" penalty. Death spiral. The fact that, after the end of the game, it announced one last accident was the perfect cap to the game.Even if you follow every single rule the game gives you, you'll have random accidents constantly. And if that happens in your nuclear plant, then you lose power across the entire ship, and you take a massive hit to trust. Start the death spiral.Then there's the constant micromanagement required to move resources between sectors. Unless it's a resource you can't. Speaking of sectors, there's a mandatory collection of "please don't die" buildings that need to go in to keep morale up, but that means transferring around a bunch of stuff, cleaning the debris first, and generally doing the same frustrating things over and over again. Actually, doing the same frustrating thing over and over is my one sentence review of the game. And then your crew, in a life or death situation, including in the middle of a firefight, will go on strike because they're not happy enough with an entertainment center, food, a dog, some memorials, and a breathtaking view of a planet. So they'll go on strike in your nuclear plant and death spiral.The game practically requires you to play the campaign over and over, either save scumming or from the beginning, to understand the next challenge and tailor your entire ship to overcome it. So long as you can do so before the penalties kick in, of course.The graphics are fine, the voice acting is appropriate, and the music is fantastic, if underutilized. The gameplay itself is terrible, and no amount of little tweaks will save it. Plenty of people enjoy this game, so maybe watch a playthrough or spend some time reading both positive and negative reviews. Insult to injury, they used il2cpp, (which actually takes 10-40 times longer to compile, so it's a real choice) so making mods with Harmony is not an option. I don't get this decision, but it does mean modding is basically off the table. I gave this game more of a chance than I should have. Any team can release a bad game, but I'm sure going to believe the reviews more next time. I hope their next game has a better gameplay loop.\t\t\t\t\t

Why? Reasons that's why. what could have been a great storytelling mechanic ie. you blew up the bloody moon and now there's lots of micro meteorstrikes until you can get the engines working enough to put us in a safe orbit. is instead a dreary pay X materials to maintain the ship. even in places where it shouldn't be a problem. this is simply an example there are many more parts of the game where this is a problem.Then there's the crew asignments, So I'm coming at this from dwarf fortress, where if you don't have enough folks to do the work then the work don't get done. Or even TROPICO But in this game a building is either ON or OFF and if it's ON it must use it's full complement of employees, even in cases where It kinda doesn't matter like storage bays. What are those five dudes gonna do, play cards all shift? What's a doctor going to do when no ones sick or injured. there's only so much paperwork charting a man can do when there's work to be done. Adding to this that there's a lot of work which needs doing at any given time means that it feels like half of your population is wasting their time doing things, which are gameplay wise essential but Diagetically meaningless. All of these little thing add up to break the immersion, you're trying to feel like it's a thrilling adventure through deep space where one wrong step can be your last with dwindling resources but because you are forced to expend so many in game resources on thing which only are the way they are because the game says they are the way they are means that the struggle feels forced. it's hard to feel the desperation when five dudes have been playing cards in the same storehouse for six weeks with nothing better to do. If you want Deep space desperation rimworld is a better choice. by far. Fronstpunk is nice too. Taken from a verisimilitude and immersion perspective TROPICO is a more accurate serious dictator simulator than this is a deep space desperation simulator.\t\t\t\t\t
