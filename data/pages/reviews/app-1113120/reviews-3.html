\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeat this game in under 25 hours, I definitely recommend the wiki if you want to know the outcomes of research expeditions. I've played a ton of different resource management games, with Frostpunk being one of my favorites. So this game came as a fun and a bit challenging of a game. If you want to do a good run, you gotta progress fast and avoid the penalties of sticking around in a system too long.

One thing that was a bit annoying was being unable to upgrade housing without demolishing and replacing with the newer kind. But as far as how well the game preforms, it does really well. Deconstructing buildings gives you back the resources, there may be a modifier for it alongside all the others you can change.

Story is decent, it works for the premise, nothing that grips your attention that particularly leaves you wanting for more story, but rather just to progress.

If you want a challenge, you like frostpunk, and want something to burn through 30-40 hours, pick this game up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCool take on a city-builder.
Runs alright on my outdated hardware, playable.
Epic soundtrack.
Took me about 4 re-tries to finally make it on normal difficulty.
There are quite a few cons that make this game harder than it should be, mostly the lack of information when going in blind. It is still perfectly doable without any guides if you accept your losses and learn from them.

So if you are looking to just enjoy this game go for the easiest difficulty because normal will kick your ass pretty quick in some way, challenge mode expects you to be very familiar with the game and its mechanics.

Overall a solid 8/10 and not in an IGN "anything below 9 is ♥♥♥♥" kind of way. An enjoyable experience which made me melancholic about existence.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are into city games at all, this game is worth playing for you.

I am not recommending it to others or perfectionist type city builders, (which is a lot of us, let's face it). This game is unfinished. After being released barely serviceable, it received no polish on the problem areas. Get Frostpunk instead.

Several annoying user interface issues which are huge time wasters and extremely annoying. Also it is lacking key binds for several basic commands.

Event choices lack some handling, warnings and feedback. Mostly they lack consistency with how they are handled and described. Some events are funny as most are stolen from other stories, movies, tv etc. However, this means that most are ridiculous, make no sense and have no real way to make good decisions.

There is no way to gauge time for storms and anomalies that pop up due to the idea that your ship apparently lacks any sensors to keep an eye on them. Upgrading them is also impossible. Lack of explanation is common for games, but it creates multiple exasperating situations in this one.

Many game like this give you timetables based on having things like working sensors or... eyes. This is pretty basic for city builders and is seriously lacking here. You will find many reviews, good or bad echoing the lack of polish; potential unrealized.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo: I finished the game on Challenge mode, with a million extra resources, not much difficulty. However, I'm a god ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ gamer, and did frequent save/loads, and I'd actually argue the game is very poorly balanced for the average person looking to have a good time with a narrative-based city builder.

The issue with this game, as other reviewers have mentioned, is artificial difficulty. The game is trivial if you know which decisions to make. Also, certain upgrades (namely rushing the research per week upgrade) equally trivialize the game.

I compare this to Frostpunk, which I've spent many hours (over a hundred) constantly optimizing my Survivor runs, their starts, my order of progression. Ixion, on the other hand, really only offers one linear challenge, one that's actually pretty simple if you slow down and avoid the stress the game hoists upon you.

The writing, while interesting, is filled with a million and one cop outs. You will never get answers. It's all a bunch of handwaivey nonsense and untelegraphed twists. I really do like the setting, the initial premise (of being the last vestiges of humanity after seeing yourself destroy Earth ) but the actual storytelling leads a ton to be desired. Much of the game is just chilling and optimizing your city, which, while fun, is really no different to any other city builder out there.

I really wish that this game had a more concrete focus on its own storytelling, as well as less events with strictly superior options to others. The primary issue with this game is that it can only be played once. Buy on sale, but honestly, with games like Frostpunk out there I can't honestly recommend this over others - even things like Against the Storm have captivated me far longer. I just wish I could appreciate the writing in this game but the execution really prevents me from doing so.

Bugginess and a lack of QOL also plagues the game. You basically feel like you're fighting against the game to make any meaningful changes to whatever sector layouts you have. The lack of a buffer system plays into this. The difficulty comes from various awfully designed and difficult to deal with features (the stockpile system, the medical system, the food system) rather than any conscious decision making. It just feels like I need to 'game the system' to make any meaningful progress or win. That's really not how a survival city builder should feel.\t\t\t\t\t
