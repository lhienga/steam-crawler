\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCould have been an interesting colony builder, but there's just so many things wrong. The technology research system is hilarious... it takes like 30minutes+ of in-game time to research a single thing. The starting building area is hilariously small, like so small that you can't even fit one of each of the starting buildings in the area. The game doesn't have a tutorial either; all that it has are those stupid 'encyclopaedia' entries which tell you about a specific topic, but it requires a carptonne of reading and doesn't guide you through the process like a tutorial would. Exploration is borked and stupid... there are many, many planets which yield no resources or anything of any kind.... so you spend time going over to the planet only to be rewarded with literally nothing.... it's such a waste of the player's time. Also, the game uses 250Watt of GPU power in the bloody main menu alone, before you're even in the game so instead of using a pre-rendered 4K video, which would put almost no strain on you PC, the devs instead opted to have a live-rendered scene, which is hella demanding and would look literally the same as a pre-rendered 4K video... and while we're on the topic of pre-rendered videos: The game's pre-rendered videos have pretty bad pixelation, so the compression on them was done all wrong. Also, the regional pricing for the game is out of this world (pun intended). The game is at least 5x more expensive than what it should be in multiple regions. In summation: Avoid this game, there's MUCH MUCH better colony builders out there for $35.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith the new difficulty update, this game became much more approachable. Reminiscent of Frostpunk, this game is a brutal survival city builder set in the not-so-distant future. Even on easy, you'll find yourself constantly fixing hull breaches, repairing buildings after random explosions, and reaping the consequences of what you thought was a good idea at the time. The tech tree is a lot deeper than it first seems, but the policy tab seems under-utilized. That said, this is still an Early Access title, and future updates should do more to flesh out the already impressively robust catalog of upgrades and missions. 8/10, if you see it on sale, it's definitely worth your time. One thing I'm not a fan of though is the scripted and unavoidable deaths. EVEN ON EASY You can do everything right, have every upgrade available, and yet there's nothing you can do to reduce the likelihood of accidents, and many events in space are scripted to kill you. Perhaps a true rng for dangerous missions would take some of this bitterness away, and we definitely need some safety upgrades, because it's rather ridiculous that a frickin ice storage pile has the capacity to randomly explode at any moment, even with optimal conditions met. Replayability might be an issue for you if you aren't a huge fan of this genre, but if you found yourself replaying A New Home in Frostpunk 100 times to see if you could do it better on a harder difficulty, you'll find plenty to do here as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE 2 MONTHS LATER: Skip this game. There has been zero patch and no communications from the devs since release.

- Game is unplayable on 4k and 2k even if you have a NASA computer.
- It's tough on 1080p too if you don't have the latest and greatest hardware.
- Performance gets worse with each population and sector. Playing the prologue doesn't guarantee you will be able to play mid game in decent conditions.
- Game doesn't save graphic settings between restart, so you can't even lower resolution or settings.
- Some chapters are broken and cut your FPS in half for the entire chapter.


Some guys in the forum did some great troubleshooting. Basically, everything is full 3D rendering, that's poorly optimised. When you look inside the ship to build your base, the game is rendering deep space (not visible) behind the ship, hence terrible performance.

One person identified some broken visual effects in deep space in some levels that destroy performance when they are rendered (hint: all the time because bug above). You can try to tinker and delete some texture files to resolve. Sadly there's been no reply from the devs in the thread and no patch.

:(

Do not buy if you have 4k display.
Game is unplayable on 4k with a 6650 XT and that's latest GPU I could get this year for 300.

Started the game. All my fans started blasting so loudly like my computer is taking off to space.
This never happened to me. I didn't know my computer had fans until today.

Quick debugging for the devs:
- The AMD overlay shows 37 FPS with the GPU at constant 100%. Most settings are about medium (default).
Changing all settings to the lowest doesn't get beyond 39 FPS (rounding up!).
It's not playable and the game is supposed to get slower as you get more population and buildings.

- Lowering resolution to 1080p, I get 80 FPS with GPU at ~35% utilisation.
But the text is blurry so cannot play like that. :(

- Trying a resolution in between. Performance is right in between but text still blurry.
Forgot to write down the numbers.
Too late to restart the game now, I only have a 2 hours refund window.

Basically, it's like the game is trying to render 10 times the amount of details as the resolution increases, killing performance. I don't need more details, I just need the text to not be blurry.

I am refunding the game.
Do not buy if you have a 4k screen.
Think twice if you have a 2k/ultrawide screen with GPU a bit dated.\t\t\t\t\t
