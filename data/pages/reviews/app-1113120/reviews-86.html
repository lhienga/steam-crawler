\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was right up my alley and I completely loved IXION.

•In terms of the narrative, it was absolutely phenomenal and gripped me from start to finish. It is a Space Odyssey, humanity is giving its first steps into interstellar travel but instead of tiptoeing into the lake nearby their house they just Bomba'd the ♥♥♥♥ out of the water and the subsequent tsunami caused by their fat ass caused Earth and most of humanity to go extinct. Now it is up to you to pick up the pieces scattered across the solar system and set off in a journey to find humanity a new home. Although the end became a little cliché, it did not ruin my enjoyment of the narrative one bit (I guess no space odyssey is complete without a wicked AI, huh?).

•In terms of the soundtrack by Guillaume David, the composer for WH40K: Mechanicus, did an excellent job and truly immerses you in the atmosphere of IXION from incredible awe provoking tracks like 'Pulsar' 'EMV Charon' and 'Aventine' to tracks that fill you with determination like 'No Turning Back' and 'Vanir's Legacy'.

•In terms of gameplay, if you've played Frostpunk before, you'll be right at home with this game. Difficulty wise, this game is pretty hard since there are several ways to just game end yourself yourself without even knowing it, so there is no shame in reloading saves. This game will ask a lot of you and you will most certainly have to reload a few times or even die from running out of basic resources, but I feel this is all part of the experience keep trying and you will find a way my best advice is to dedicate the first two sectors you unlock for Food and Industry then turn sector one into a big space port for improving resource collection and keep you afloat through some of the harshest events in the game.

•Performance-wise, this game can be quite heavy to run, especially in the later stages when all the buildings and environmental effects outside can tank FPS a little bit. I have an RTX 2060 Super, Ryzen 5 5600X and 16gb of Ram and I managed to play on Ultra settings just fine but the FPS drops and stutters became more prevalent by the end.

IXION is a 9/10 very recommended if you've played Frostpunk!\t\t\t\t\t

Overall bad design choices which make no sense and does not contribute to a fun experience but just makes the game feel like a chore

For a "finished" game it has several game breaking bugs, I can see why people say its "Impossible" to beat and thats mostly because the game makes you soft lock yourself throughout the game which quickly turn into a hard-lock so thats just bad balancing and poor design.
It also has some poorly thought out mechanics such as your hull (HP) falls rather fast "Just cause" which doesnt make sense on top of that by advancing the story you lose HP that you cant get back, you also lose it by opening more sectors so you can have a bigger pop and the needed factory's and food to do anything and you lose morale way too fast as well that you cant get back either since its permanent, I.e you lose morale for having too many people, you lose it for not having enough, etc.
You can build buildings that "Help" regain the lost morale but they only have a small effect as well as you cant build them in the sector that needs it since the buildings wont fit, they only effect the place they are built which is pointless.

There are plenty more issues to mention but due to a "bug" which stayed after release It makes me wonder if its a feature to counter the hull and morale loss events.

This is it----just ignore (Click the X) the pop up that comes up which normally means you lost, Before I lost interest I got close to beating the game on 0 hull and 0 morale, which makes the "Impossible" claims and other game ending complaints moot since you get "God mode" from the game itself lol. For the record this was in since it was first announced and after testing just prior to the review is still in, with the amount of players hard to imagine this "Bug" went unnoticed for so long

I for one didnt find it that difficult (Not counting the poor design) since I grew up playing city building games so I already had a good idea how this game would go it doesn't bring anything new to the table.\t\t\t\t\t
