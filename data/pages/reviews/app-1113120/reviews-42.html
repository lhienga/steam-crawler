\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to tell you things I didn't get from seeing the game description or the video. Things I would have wanted to know before buying this. So I'm going warn you: You may or may not consider what I'm going to say as a spoiler. It is up to you if wish to read on.

Other reviewers have talked about having bugs, etc... I haven't had any bugs, or performance issues with the game. My critique is purely with the game play.

I am a sci fi guy. I like the general premise of the game: You are the captain of a interstellar ship. Vey configurable ship.
You can configure it almost any way you like. Just you and the stars. Look for habitable worlds, leave a colony. That sounds pretty fun to me.

Spoiler Warning! You have been warned.

First, I realize you need a premise to want to leave the earth. I have to admit I'm tired of the idea that man has killed the earth and people have to leave. Tired to death of this idea. <Hands story board team an original thought> To the rest of the Sci Fi Dev teams out there: Somebody get an original thought please. Your killing me.

Lets put aside the story line. I don't like the mechanics of the game. The first jump you take *permanently* damages the ship you are on. No way to fix it. I killed two ships trying to figure out how to fix the ship then I looked it up in the forums. It is a game mechanic. Now here is the fun part. Every jump you take after that damages the ship even more. No way to fix that ether. You can compensate with some of the things you can do in terms of adding components, etc. But each time you jump permanently breaks the ship more. So what does this mean? The game ends after a few jumps... That's it...

I find this crazy short sighted... Why? I realize you are going to have to deal with wear and tear as part of managing a interstellar space station. But... This ship had to be built in high earth orbit. You have manufacturing centers on board your shiny new space station. In fact, the resources were probably sent up and built there because that would be the most efficient way to do it. In theory you could make a whole new space station if you needed to. So not being able to fix the ship makes *no* sense at all. The game could make mining asteroids much more efficient... because since no one has mined them before there would be almost unlimited resources there. Except in this game they are in fact limited. Just enough to get by...

Yeah sure, make it to the planet, colonize it. Why stop there? If the mechanics were just a little different you could explore other, even procedurally generated, systems... build space stations you can use as way stations.. or anything really. A creative developer might be able to do a lot with that mechanic. Colonize more planets... Again, more support for the ship... Maybe designate one as your home base... Customize it. Maybe encounter aliens... Explore. See? Captain of your own ship.

Somehow when I saw trailer, I thought: Wow a configurable space station, you can take to the stars... How cool is that. This is not that game. you have a half dozen or so jumps before your ship dies. You find that out in the very beginning of the second chapter.

This game had such potential. What a loss.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat story driven city-builder game with questionable mechanics

So here is the recipe for IXION:
- Take 1 pack of Frostpunk, simplify policy, choice and building mechanics
- Add a ton more story/adventure content
- 1.5 spoons of nice graphics
- Bake for 3-4 years
- QA is optional

Jokes aside, if you liked games like Frostpunk, you will likely find IXION very enjoyable, similar concept with more focus on story elements. It looks gorgeous, the story is gripping and the soundtrack is truly exceptional. However the game is a lot less refined than Frostpunk and plagued with questionable design choices in its core gameplay loop:

- A tetris-like city building is certainly unique, but with every building being a square and lack of certain sizes, like 2x2, end up making the entire mid game an awful chore with hours spent redesigning the station each time you get a new research tier and buildings no longer fitting your grid.

- Logistics is annoying at best. There is little to no automation and half of late game is filled with endless manual movement of people and resources across 6 districts and 40 storage facilities. It gets so bad that some of the quests start to bug because game fails to assign resources due to a full storage somewhere in the ship.

- UI is anything but useful. It does the basics, but you wont find any real quality of life features. Want to calculate your production? Have fun do it yourself. Want to prevent your ship from suicide charges into the storm? Enjoy manually banning every single asteroid in half of the system.

- Lack of proper tutorial. The game is difficult, however what often left out is that the biggest challenge is total lack of explanation of its core mechanics. Wanna know what's gonna happen if you fly your cargo ships into a storm? Or why half of them didnt die? Welcome to google. By the end of your 20 hour run you may fail only because you wasted too many resources in act 2 and 3 while you figured out how the game even works.

- Some story points make no sense. No spoilers here, but some of the key story choices and characters receive little to no explanation, leaving you in the dark about half of the key plot mysteries. It gets especially bad in the late game where some of the ingame events/quests dont get any resolution at all. In addition stability system is just weird. People unhappy that you stuck in dead solar system? Now they will be pissed that you left. Workers unhappy that they work extra hours? Now they are on strike and the ship is about to explode cause you don't have any materials to fix it. Your human sized lemmings would rather die than go to work for a few extra hours.

Ultimately, I would recommend this game, as the journey still worth the troubles. Its just a shame that it could have been so much better, but instead it leaves you with a pile of tedious mechanics that sour otherwise great game.\t\t\t\t\t
