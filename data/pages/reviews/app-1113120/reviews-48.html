\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great colony sim / city builder with survival elements. I feel the game scratches a similar itch to Frostpunk; a race against time to save your people.

I enjoyed building up my colony and trying to optimize it once I got in a comfortable spot. Exploring the systems was also very interesting with flavorful events. Visually the game is quite good as well; I often found myself going to the external camera to view the Tiqqun and it's surroundings.

On the default difficulty the game felt a bit easy. Negative events are easily avoided and there is an overabundance of resources. I never felt pressured for time as I managed to make the Tiqqun self-sufficient in regards to food and alloy production about 10 hours into the game. Events that have consequences can also be left alone until you are ready for them.

I recommend the game; I enjoyed my time and the ending was quite satisfying. If you are looking for a challenge you probably want to play on a harder difficulty.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSoooo... there are some things I like about this game. But you're interested in what I don't like.
TL;DR: as it is right now, I'd call this a $10 - $15 game. Some bug fixes, a tutorial on how to use the advanced view resource transfer (very important for food distribution), and more content (especially involving game lore) would easily make this game worth $35.

1. Camera angles.
a. The 'city view' defaults you to a specific angle that is quite useful. Fortunately for the player, you are forced back into that angle every time you re-enter city view. It's fortunate because it's effectively impossible to manually control the angle back to it's default.
b. The solar system view defaults you back to close zoom. Which is annoying, as I keep having to zoom back out.
c. The outer hull ship view is pointless. But since they included it, make sure to play with it a bit as it's decently pretty. But definitely not worth paying for.

2. Storyline.
a. Too short. There are only 5 solar systems you visit. I'd call this $15 dollars max worth of gameplay.
b. Choices don't matter. Yes, a few of your choices actually do matter, but most of them don't do anything except give different reward values and/or blow up your scout-scientists. Which is truly sad. Some of the ♥♥♥♥ your scientists will dig up are shocking and disturbing! I was expecting this game to lean into the choices. But instead, most of what you do appears to be ignored.
c. There is lore in the game that adds to the craziness your scientists will discover. But finding the lore doesn't do anything or unlock anything. Very disappointing.

3. Gameplay
a. Buildings cannot be grouped. If I'm trying to budget my power grid and want to turn off my carbon factories, I have to turn off each factory and turn off each warehouse. This is typically 4 buildings. And that's not too annoying...the first time. But when I'm doing it repeatedly, and doing for my processor factories, and my steel factories, and my cryo facilities... it becomes very tedious.
b. warehouses are idiots. You will repeatedly find that your warehouses will stop working because of a delivery between sectors. This is a bug, as the game explicitly states that delivery between sectors uses a different truck than delivery within the same sector. Next, add on that the warehouses will often deliver a single item between sectors simply because it wouldn't wait .1 seconds for the next truck to add to the load.
c. transports are idiots. If you watch, you will see your transports go after the same ore deposit. If there's enough ore, it's fine when multiple transports go after it. But you will often see multiple transports go after an ore deposit that is nearly empty. So if 5 transports go after a deposit that will run out with the first ship, the next four transports KEEP FLYING TO THE EMPTY SPOT. AND THEN THEY GO BACK TO YOUR SHIP EMPTY!!!! ARGH!
d. mining ships are idiots. There's a weird bug in the game where you can have two or more mining ships go after a deposit, and the mining ships keep trying to position themselves on top of each other. So the adjust themselves, and wind up on top of each other again...and again...and again...oh, and they're not mining, so this is a massive waste of time.
e. paradigm shift. This is a space ship game. And you rescue people from other space ships. And you find that these people are mostly useless?!? Why are the crews from other space ships mostly worthless? This makes no sense. As a game mechanic, this was the biggest trick that forced me to restart my game. Once I wrapped my head around the understanding that I didn't need to have redundant sectors, and instead had to set my ship up to handle useless refugees, I was able to beat the game fairly easily on normal difficulty.
f. AI helper not helping. What's the point of having an AI assistant if it won't tell me that my missile countermeasures are built in the probe launch facility? Or that my docking bays that have no free slots to build ships are still able to build shuttles? For the most part, the AI assistant is helpful. But there are a few flaws. Btw, "A probe has completed it's scan" is not useful! Don't tell me that. Tell me when a probe is ready to launch instead!

4. Cutscenes
a. I have two issues with the cutscene you get when you engage your FTL. First, it's exactly the same every time. It would have been nice to see the ship fly to different exit points. Second, the video and the audio look mismatched. If the audio were to start about half a second later, the deep notes would match with the ship drive animations. And then extend a half second of video of ship flight to balance out my change at the beginning.
b. There is a public address cutscene at the beginning of the game. But Dolos CEO is annoying. Which is sad, as I kinda wanted to watch the very end of that cutscene again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOK so absolutely love this game its gorgeous! I can spend time just admiring the outside of the space station and the planets you find, all while it falls apart. This game is challenging but its at a doable level once you figure it out and its not badly balanced or anything like that despite what some say. If you want a city builder which is different this is the game. It rewards you for forward planning, positioning the factories etc and managing your crew. Dont make promises you cant keep, dont put your iron smelter miles from the iron stockpiles, or in a different sector to where the cargo ships are bringing the ores in. The more you work as admin to make it work the more chance to survive.

The first go I had was difficult, figuring the game out messing up and soon dying within a few systems, the crew survived but ripped me out of my command sleep pod and murdered me. They thought they could do a better job without me. The second time I was doing really well but made a mistake, i tried to sit and weather a storm thinking it would pass but should have moved, my fault. Even with that mistake when I started moving I almost made it, but only almost. The ships hull collapsed and we all died. Third time I did it, wasnt easy but i took these lessons from the first two games and made it with some really close moments that made the game epic.

So graphics its stuning outside (inside the station is not amazing but works go out for your views) the strategy layer is tough but rewarding on the normal difficulty its doable. (im going to try the harder one next). The story is amazing if a little confusing at times, im going to google what was going on but no spoilers from me its good to discover as you go alone.

Basically if you want a challenge a great game, dont get stressed with the permanent mood debuffs the crew get as you can counter them with buildings etc. Stick at it have heart and you can win.

Some semi spoilery hints but not story spoilers, personally I would play the game and learn and thats part of the fun but if you want some hints to get going my biggest would be.


************************ **************************** *************************


Keep on top of crew mood, as soon as you see it go negative at the top and drop do something about it before it gets too late. Often i lost because i thought i would sort something out later then I ended up having other problems arrive.

Dont rush out of the system even though the crew get upset you are staying, if you can stay with a slight mood drop it disappears when you move on, so you can gather and prep before you move on to the next system.

Stockpile things you need, mainly metal and smelt what you can, you can never have enough metal to build and repair its your lifeblood. Same with food, keep it in the positive.


Keep going if you have a setback or bad experience exploring, its all part of the game and they are all recoverable.


Hope that all helps thanks for reading and try this game. Excellent.\t\t\t\t\t

I didn't expect such a captivating plot and moral choices from a city builder.

The station, based on an O'Neill cylinder, is executed flawlessly and is really fun to navigate.
The music is fantastic and really contributes to the "cosmic horror" feel of the game.

What more can I say?
Great game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off: if you didn't know this already, demolishing buildings gives you a FULL REFUND. That means you can, and should, remap your sectors whenever needed. You can also sacrifice some stuff to rebuild later if you are short on materials. This little fact makes the game a lot easier.

IXION is an amazing game, but probably not for everyone. It is one of the most satisfying city builders I have played, but the difficulty level is intense, even at Journeyman, the easiest setting, I find myself constantly challenged and restarting after another dumb error on my part or imminent less of the station. This is like a roguelike, but it's not, it is very scripted.

It is Shortest Trip To Earth but with amazing graphics, more story and probing, and without the fights. In this game you build, upgrade and steer your space station through levels that get progressively more difficult. Unlike STTE you don't have to worry about weapons, but the resource management is just as painful. Also, you have to keep people happy. Bring aboard ever more cryo pods. And raw materials. And this. And that. With everybmew tech it becomes more complex. And rewarding.

Mining, events, catastrophes. If you like a desperage fight for survival in the form of a city build, definitely pick it up. It will make you think, strategize, and smile when everything comes together.\t\t\t\t\t
