\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNeat story, hampered by the annoyance of resource movement and storage. Not resource *management*, movement and storage. This game makes city building into tedious excel charts.

Why wouldn't members of one ship share food between them? Why does the game show one resource stockpile at the top when the game actually treats it as many different tiny stockpiles? Resources won't be shared unless you set up a spreadsheet, a spreadsheet that has to be changed every 2-4 minutes. Why can't resource collection ships land at different docks on the same ship? Having all of your citybuilding take place on one ship and then having people who are literally next door to each other act like cities leagues away from each other is silly, tedious, 4th wall breaking, and worst of all, boring. I'd love to play through the story but I just don't care to deal with the actual gameplay.

People starve to death because the ship is flying? Why don't we do meal time immediately after?? Why doesn't food stay on while the ship moves? Who invented this thing? Ixion actually *punishes* the player for moving their spaceship in a story about your spaceship moving to a new place. Wtf?? Almost none of the challenge makes sense, it's just, "Look how poorly I designed my game around this narrative! I hope you enjoy excel!"

Soundtrack slaps though. Could listen for hours. Graphics are lovely and the feel of the interior of the ship and the various solar systems you explore are great while being easy to quickly grasp. Honestly if they had a button that automated resource sharing between sectors the game would improve dramatically.\t\t\t\t\t

The main problem is that the "city building" part is shallow and bad. You can get to a place where you're self-sufficient(ish) even at normal difficulty pretty easily. But there really isn't a tonne of depth to be had; make sure you have enough housing, enough stability, etc. It's very primitive as far as city building goes. You'd think the confined building space would be the challenge but no; it's really not.

Compounding that is the mandatory events that introduce permanent negative effects. I understand the goal is to force you to move along but it basically punishes you for not knowing how to proceed in the most efficient manner from the get go. Actually using the mechanics of the game? Gather resources? You took to long, here is a permanent -1 stability you can't get rid of. There's a late game system where it's a permanent -2 modifier and you have no way of speeding through on your first attempt - you can not POSSIBLY know what needs to be done from the start.

But by far the worst is the story. It has an interesting premise. It kind of takes that premise and starts running to a place that by chapter 4, I was genuinely excited! I though I had an idea of where it was going on and I was interested. And then it went the most generic and uninteresting route possible. Also I accidentally skipped the ending cutscene because I assumed the first bit was just the usual "Engines powering up" cutscene and skipped it, but no...missed the whole thing, cut to credits. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed this game throughout.

The story is told nicely, the technobabble enjoyable, the events could have some more diversity but still are enjoyable. Getting sectors well organized and running smooth doesn't get old. While there isn't much to do in the exterior view, it's is a nice breather, a moment to appreciate one of humanity's last arks. It's nice how the different views are all tied together, I really enjoyed watching the Tiqqun move in the exterior view. I also really enjoyed the jump sequence every time, it's such a nice moment, tied together with great music and visuals. I really enjoyed the soundtrack too, while basically three tracks on repeat with some silence in between, it's just a style I really enjoyed. I like the voice-acting too, just a tad wonky enough to add to the overall atmosphere.

My main criticism would be: I wish there was more. More and more diverse events. More variation in the buildings, especially sectors with lots of crew quarters become just a clump of grayish geometry at later parts of the game. More visuals for exploration events.

Bought and played after the Difficulty Settings update on Journey Difficulty.

+1 Dog \t\t\t\t\t
