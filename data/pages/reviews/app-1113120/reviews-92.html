\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really really want to recommend this game. In terms of graphics and playability, 100% spot on. The storyline is strong, full of mystery and intrigue. Interesting and quite catchy. But there are a couple of things that ruin this game completely.

1) Resource gathering and usage. No issue with how resources are gathered. Keeping an eye on food and making sure crew is well fed, not overworked and gathering a correct quantity of everything else is fine.

What annoys the cr*p out of me is how much alloy this super modern spaceship burns through. An amazingly modern spaceship whose hull is constantly deteriorating and consistently needs iron to make alloy to keep it going. Lets not forget that every time you open a new sector, the need for additional alloy increases. As I have just found, taking your time and then trying to solve discovery point to get as much fun out of the game means I exhaust all Iron (Fe) resources which means no alloy which means the hull of a ship designed for the survival of mankind deteriorates at an unprecedented rate, sometimes in as little as a few days depending on what you do.

Completely unrealistic element to the game in my opinion and needs tweaking, devs. Or give us a ton more resources or adjust the rates but... Come on.. I know the game should get increasingly harder but its a huge turnoff to keep playing the game.

2) Crew Morale

No issue with keeping crew happy and prevent them getting from overworked. Tired of how the crews gets a huge sad on every time they get a bit of bad news and you have to consistently build memorials everytime they hear a snippet of bad news. Therefore half the space I used to build buildings which I should use to feed them, I am using to please them.

So, a crew designed to leave Earth on a quest to save Earth then gets sad about every-single thing they knew getting destroyed (which was to be anticipated) on a spaceship which has a constant thirst for metal without enough resources to supply it...

Don't get me wrong, its a really pretty and interesting game to play but the difficulty for me is too much unless I am missing something or building a certain building..

I will keep continuing to play because I am only on Chapter 2 and already struggling but, devs, this consistent burn for alloy just seems... An achilles heel and unrealism in a game that is fun yet frustrating in equal measure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've read some of the reviews of this game though I'd add my own voice. The game is a survival resource management game, in space - think Frost Punk, but in a move-able space city.

It is very, very difficult and when you start playing some of the problems you'll come across will appear to come out of nowhere. I can appreciate the reviews that say that the game "hates you" - it definitely feels like that at the start. The system actively punishes you if you make a mistake - One time, i accidentally overworked a sector and that cascaded into a full blown riot over food shortages.

You really need to be able to balance population, resource collection, public opinion, material refining, power, water, hull repair and science to be in with a chance and it can be difficult to keep track of everything at once.

As you gain time with in game on board the Tiqqun, you start to understand the nuances of the game structure and some of the problems start to vanish. You then realise that the game is only as hard as you make it for yourself. Some key things I learned over my 90+ hours playing were:

1. Don't ever have more jobs than workers, and turn buildings off if you have too. You want to have no accidents!
2. Always be gatherin' resources.
3. Don't be scared to move the Tiqqun.

I'd say this game is for experienced resource management game players. Players who are new to genre will may have some problems with managing all of the systems. I'd definitely recommend this game, but be aware its not an easy trip through space.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is extremely good.
Amazing story, great voice over, stunning graphics, great new mechanics.
Ixion is also hard - the only difficulty it seems. However as a city survival "pro" I managed to finish the game with just one fail, so it's definitelly not *utterly unplayable* as some say.
The only quirk is that the game is very "fiddly". Turning buildings on/off, constantly remaking your base due to new unlocks to achieve higher efficiency, balancing manual actions versus costly automation that improves your sanity by taking over. In a way this is the ultimate sim city, in space, with a great story.

I waited for months because of the non stop negative reviews rambling about how "the game hates you", "UI interface is unusable", "economy is broken and hard". From all that, the only truth is that the game is hard.

I think people miss the mark because Ixion doesn't play as a "normal" RTS. You can't just build a bunch of stuff and be fine. You have to fiddle and tweak and work with within the limits which you keep pushing - grow too much too fast and you die. If you like fiddling with your colony and slowly growing it in a controlled manner, this is a game for you.

One more thing, this game scales into late game. I have never seen this type of game so well balanced into later stages. The game is hard all the way, there are no notable difficulty spikes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game feels like a meditation on what would a survival city builder be like if it largely consisted of following the designs, ideas and instructions left by an arrogant, self-centered and reductive cult and figuring out how to survive in spite of destruction, decay and limitation inflicted on you and everyone else by their failures. I say this specifically because this - to me - framed a lot of the game's more limiting and unpleasant mechanics that make the the city builder gameplay less enjoyable and more finnicky in a much more positive light. Of course, that doesn't excuse the gameplay failings entirely, but in my mind, they ultimately served to reinforce this game's themes and I found these themes compelling and worth exploring. Combined with the exceptional way in which the game handles its sci-fi core and a fantastic soundtrack, all of the above makes me feel safe in recommending this game, warts and all.\t\t\t\t\t

I had one playthrough and took me around 17h with almost all research. I bought the game because I really liked the idea of a wandering space station and in the end I somehow enjoyed the story but the game does lot of things wrong and often breaks "the immersion".
Positiv - The story is nice an well done (even it is not 100% logical / explaining everything)
Negativ
- Warehouse Management System - you need to manually manage your stores which goods they pull and you can not multi-select
- Stability Rating - Is very simple to handle - no real choices here
- Population Handling - It is painful to manage your workers between sectors
- Miner & Freighter are also somehow troublesome to handle and the broke toggle switch in UI makes it annoying...
- The overall ressource Management breaks the immersion of the story completly - you "mine" a complete sun system without any ressources left, your ship are crossing the whole sun system but you need to mikro manage storage your Space Station with the magical space drive.
In Summary - the Building Part of the Game requires a at least three good ideas and is not much fun to play, but the frustration is limited

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played a lot of base-builders. Many many. This one is made by developers who clearly love base building games (and Frostpunk in particular) but fail to understand the different between a challenging game and an INTERESTING challenging game.

They have a number of ways to ratchet up the tension and keep you pushing forward, but none of them involve CHOICES. They aren't "this or this" it's just "deal with this or die".

When did I quit, you may ask? I lost a ship to space weather. Why, in any possible discussion of how to design a game, would this have been a good idea? The choice is "babysit your ships at all times or else one of your human-crewed vessels will enter a storm they know will kill them". There is an upgrade to make ships more survivable, but . . . . what's the point of that mechanic at all? You can also set certain specific points in the system to be avoided, but how am I supposed to know what Hohmann transfer orbit my ships will take to get between two spots that aren't my home base? Why could I not just tell my pilots to . . . I dunno . . . . avoid space hurricanes?

If you want space weather to be an INTERESTING challenge, then maybe: make vital and unique resources only available within the storm and give ships a limited timeframe / threshold of damage before they leave. Make probes an expendable resource to map the storm in limited areas and increase survivability. Playing "The ground is lava" with ships on the screen that is NOT the primary focus of the game is . . . . I refer back to my earlier statement; a basic misunderstanding of what makes a challenge fun.

Same deal with stability. They tried to make political stability the same as the cold mechanic in Frostpunk, and it just feels "this is hard" instead of "this is challenging". I'm on the 2nd system and I'm already at -4 Stability: PERMANENT in big ol' letters. That is punitive, not challenging. The interesting way to make that mechanic is to make it costly to avoid or lessen those impacts. Hell, the worldbuilding even says "some people will start to develop DES" and then anytime something bad happens, the WHOLE STATION gets a drop from that new issue. Why not have that be a sliding scale depending on a number of people who affect it, and have a long and arduous process at the infirmary or psych office to move people back to good. There's an interesting choice. How many days can you take people off production lines to invest in your long-term stability? Can you keep things afloat while you fix workers or do you need to bribe them and keep them on the line? Instead, we just have a "do this or die" rolling boulder, Indiana Jones style. Challenging, but not interesting.

Tack onto that a fiddly resource management system, fully-scripted events, no way to return to an event later (??? Why?), a weird mechanic where your science ships get science and then have to hang around to "harvest" it (WHY? So I have to click into the large map 30 seconds after I just did in order to move the ship that I could've just sent new instructions when it finished the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ mission? WHY???) and the "non-worker" mechanic that clearly exists as just another way to siphon resources without providing an interesting CHOICE, and you have . . . Ixion.

The devs clearly wanted to build a challenging game, but forgot the fun.

Edit: Well, I kept playing it out of spite (after I went and did a game of Frostpunk in between), but damn if it still isn't just overly fiddly for no good reason. Having to babysit individual ships, blacklist specific asteroids (can't swipe a box and select those? Any reason why not?) and god, the research tree is just offensive to me. Locking useful things besides useless ones, or my personal favorite, the "research this to unlock something in the UI to explain the game" button. Beware devs that force interaction with parts of the game because they clearly don't trust it enough to be useful on its own.

Oh, and I'm just save-scumming the encounters, too. No reason not to. Plus when I make choices in the game portion, there seem to be just no repurcussions. Nothing narrative, no hits to Trust, nothing.\t\t\t\t\t
