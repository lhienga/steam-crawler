\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great strategy and resource management game with a story-driven playthrough.

You will direct humanity's last hope, the VVS Tiqqun, a space ark, on its journey through the stars. Due to the dangers of space, constant reinforcement of the station's hull is necessary, costing you alloys you need to produce from minerals that need to be mined in space. Meanwhile, you will need to feed your crew, take care of growing energy demand and all the while be very mindful of the limited space available aboard the station, reconstructing your sector's layout over and over again as research allows for better, more efficient buildings and means of production.

I played it for about 50 hours for a single playthrough. I played on hard and had to restart one of the chapters because I failed once, so if you don't play on hard/ironman/etc., I suppose you should end up with a playing time of 40-55 hours.
Due to the story-driven mechanics of the game, replayability is very low. You will probably be only playing one run - but for me that one run was lots of fun and totally worth it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIxion is a space survival and city building game, the goal is to manage a colony while searching for the renmants of civilization jumping between star systems.

It usually gets compared to frostpunk but in space, this can't be the furthest from the truth. It is a city building and you have to manage your colony in order to survive (like frostpunk), but there's no dept in the research system, virtually no politics option and no controversial decisions to make (sometimes you can make some decisions, but they have virtually no consequencies to the story). So, it doesn't have what makes a game like frostpunk so iconic, nothing is grim and there are no bad choices to make.

The game duration is around 30h if you really set yourself out to do everything in each chapter (there's no point in collecting all resources because you don't have enough room to store them, and don't need that many). After your first run there's really nothing different to experience, you can go back and take slightly differents paths but everything is scripted, down to the death of some people and accidents happening.

Now that I mention it, accidents are annoying at best and can end your run. There's really no way to stop them, even if you've plenty of workers they still happen. After some accidents the workers can become non workers (so they're basically useless) and there's no way to change that. Sure, there're plenty of people frozen and floating around but there's a hard limit on how much you can do (you could argue that it's hard to run out of workers but not impossible). With all my sectors running at optimal condition and everything upgraded there were around 2 accidents every 10 cycles happening (it's annoying).

This game has a good soundtrack but that's it, there's a lot missing and it desperately needs a lot of QoL features that every other city builder has today, some things are just tedious and some are really annoing. There are penalties for some strange stuff, like if you stay in a sector too long (in a space colony!) your citizens get demotivated, or as soon as you leave earth they feel bad and homesick (like after the first major plotpoint, you have a permanent effect during the whole game), you cannot fully repair your spacecraft (structural integrity apparently) so your health will lower every new chapter.

In conclusion: IXION is a great concept but it really lacks polish or straight up love from the developer\t\t\t\t\t
