\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like survival city-builders generally, this game is definitely bottom half in the genre. It does some things well, the premise is cool, the being trapped in a cylindrical city in space vibe is nice, and it has a lot of promise but ultimately doesn't quite live up to that promise. My first objection is that there is a linear plot line, so however many times you play the game the "right" decisions are the same every time. This makes the replayability appeal pretty lame. Secondly it's not super polished, the game pace starts out very slow and then eventually speeds up to uncomfortably fast with no really good way in the settings to regulate it. It also doesn't do a very good job explaining its own mechanics, I found myself re-starting my first playthrough several times because I ran into space/economy/plot issues that weren't apparent until I learned them through failure. The vibe also isn't well polished. It doesn't feel as desperate as Frostpunk or Surviving the Aftermath, and it doesn't have the charm of Surviving Mars or Banished. My advice is to buy one of those instead.\t\t\t\t\t

Impressive attention to detail in models and animations as well as brilliant sound design. I never encountered a single bug in my playthrough, everything was smooth and silky.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFeels like early access. If you can endure the suffering, its a good game.
This game is filled with unnecessary tasks that prevent you from actually playing the game.
Lack of shortcuts, and quality of life features, tons of bugs and clunky animations/ poor optimization.
i7, 64 ram, 4080 and still frames drops to 4 when you load a save, open a new area or click too fast.
You open 5 areas through the game, and every time you have to build roads get all the resources spread across the room, around 8 minutes of work just to demolish everything and clear all the roads, so you can actually build. You can't demolish a selected area, because there is no such thing in this game.
Just like frostpunk you use the pause a lot, but the problem with this game is that you have to deal with 6 separate screens at once and its impossible to automate resources between them, 50% of the game is on the resources tab just to move stuff around.
70% of the late game buildings are useless, but you still need to spend a lot of points to unlock them so you can unlock upgrades for your OLDER buildings.
The beginning of the game you really feel like the "administrator" but it fails to keep the narrative as the game progresses. Overall its a good game, but I wouldnt recommend as a full game. It should be an Early Access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinished Ixion PROS: 8/10 Graphics are beautiful, relatively low space, resource system was easy to understand and master. Even on normal it's Fairly easy to manage everything (I was wasting so much space during my games). Many of the science missions are fun to read and some very interesting concepts. Voice acting is top notch, no one is annoying or under preforming. none of the systems are lacking in things to do in my opinion. Options for tailoring a game to your liking are moderate-high. Music is fantastic. I normally don't listen to the in game music after a few hours. The soundtrack however kept me listening to the game the whole time. The sound of sectors building, refining and just carrying out their duties is also well done. I also found it funny that one of the achievements is turning all of your sound to 0 for scientifically accurate sound. It's pretty well polished, didn't run into many bugs. Nothing game breaking but 1 time the UI for a cargo ship would not go away so I had to save and restart the game. CONS: There is only the campaign which is 5 chapters. With it being on 3X time for most of the time it was about 20 hours if you do everything in every system. Now theres nothing wrong with a relatively short game. But for 35 dollars it's a bit too little. Now they may add DLC in the future to add more missions or another campaign. I did not mind spending the 35 on this game. but for those who have a tighter budget for games I'd recommend waiting for a sale. This isn't really a con but more of a warning. Pay close attention which ships you send to deliver or pick stuff up. A cargo ship will not deliver unless it can hold it's max cargo or all the required material. There has been several times where I spent 10 or more minutes trying to figure out why a cargo ship wasn't going. Most of the time it was storage problem but 2 times I'm certain it was a bug because they didn't go until I restarted.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd like to give a quick qualifier on my "recommend" for this game. Do I think it's worth a buy? Yes. The hours I got out of it are definitely worth the price. It's a pretty solid colony builder/manager game . No real bugs, keeps you on your toes. Will I replay it over and over again like Frostpunk or other colony management games? No. After a certain point, it just doesn't feel very rewarding to keep going, and I was just looking forward to finishing it.

If you're familiar with Frostpunk, Rimworld, Anno, or really any other builder game recently, then you'll really enjoy the building side of this game. Your station is split up into six sectors that you unlock and start using as need arises. You get bonuses for specializing the sectors, so it is fun to work with the different types of buildings and resources to find the optimal layouts. However, you will lose space to stockpiles for building resources. I found myself having an ugly corner in every sector that just held alloys and electronics in case I needed to rebuild or add to my sector. When demolishing buildings, if there isn't a stockpile in that sector that is designated for whatever resource that building is made out of, then the building won't demolish, so I never demolished my materials stockpiles. I would have loved to just designate a sector as the stockpile sector, and have all the others draw from it.


Ixion does a fantastic job of drawing you into it's story, and making you eager to explore the star systems you find yourself in. The star systems are unique, each with their own challenges and mysteries. You'll be learning on the fly and dealing with costly mistakes to contend with new challenges, just as you would if you were really jumping into these systems blind. Each system has a lot to explore and offers opportunities to improve the Ixion station. However, early on in the game an npc informs you that space is unforgiving and dangerous, and this proves to be true. Sending a science ship out to investigate an anomaly, and choosing the interesting option to investigate, will usually end with that ship's crew dead. This works with the tone of the story the devs are going for, but as a player makes you feel punished for, well, playing the game. They've built up this veneer of mystery that you want to peek through, only to punch you in the face when you do. The devs really expect you to come across something terribly interesting inside an asteroid, and just walk away without poking it. When it comes to the mysteries, I'm more looking forward to the youtube videos than trying to sort them out myself.

I enjoyed the story immensely up until the last 1/5 of the game. Without spoiling too much, some new mechanics are introduced there that at first felt like they really breathed new life into what was at that point becoming something of a formula, but they got tedious pretty fast in my opinion.

The visuals, atmosphere, and sound design of the game are incredibly good. I really enjoyed the feeling this game's setting evoked in me, but the lack of certain features I consider to be just QOL improvements, and the rapid pace the devs try to push you through the final chapter really leaves a sour taste in my mouth. As an enjoyer of all things space-y and mysterious, I loved it. As a player though, it left a bit of a bad taste in my mouth.
