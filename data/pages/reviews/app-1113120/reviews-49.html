\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. The concepts are cool, the story is very captivating, and the soundtrack stellar (pun intended 🙃).

I finished the story 19 days after buying the game, but I played literally every day. The beginning was very rough and I died over 10 times before I finally got the hang of things and was able to get past chapter 2. I only died 3 times in chapter 3, and I never died again after that.

The thing I liked most about this game was that most of the story is mostly found by you through exploration. It's 80% lore and 20% you getting told the story. It reminded me of Subnautica in that way. You have to read all the expedition logs. Don't just gloss over them. If you do, you won't get into it nearly as much as I did.

Even after finishing the game, I have a lot of un-answered questions about the story. Most things were not explained. Instead, you have to use your imagination. But I also think I missed a lot of things I could have explored.
I love this about it.
I definitely see myself re-playing the game in the future.
The way the missions work, it's almost infinitely repayable because on top of the main story, each individual mission had its own outcome, and they affect each other.
So while the underlying campaign story is always more or less the same, each playthrough will be unique, which is super cool!


This game's one downfall is that it suffers from many performance bugs and some occasional visual glitches which I believe are attributed to the developer's decision to make the game using Unity.
I have not played any PC game that has been made with Unity and has not had similar issues.
For a unity game, this is actually pretty stable compared to some (cough, cough, Hearthstone). I only had to save and quit the game due to visual bugs twice, which is not horrible.
There are also a few minor bugs with Eden (your personal assistant) in the last 2 chapters.

The performance bugs are annoying, but honestly it was fine. This isn't a competitive game where you need 120fps. I just feel like I should get better performance.

I have a 13700k, 32GB of RAM and an RTX 2080 (which has 8GB of VRAM - they recommend 6) This game manages to use 100% of my GPU (3D and VRAM are alwyas pegged) at all times, and my FPS would fluctuate from 28ish, all the way down to 4 depending on what I was doing. That was at ultra settings, so I turned everything down to the lowest it could possibly be, and I'm only getting 48 (which is playable, but still sub par)

I do have a 4k monitor, so I tried turning the resolution down to 1440p and that helped a bit, but then the next time I loaded the game it was 4k again. Then I tried turning it to 1080p, saved, played for a few min, quit, re-launched, and the same thing happened.

So if you're buying this, be warned: you cannot change the resolution, so make sure you can run it acceptably at native.
Even at 4k though, I should be able to get 60 FPS or more with my specs.
This game is just poorly optimized, which again, I believe is largely due to the Unity Engine.

But, also some of the bugs do feel like they could just be bad implementation.

There are a lot more minor bugs, too many to list here, but just we warned that it is not an optimized game.
But you're not playing it for the FPS you get, you're playing it for the story.

I definitely recommend this game to anyone who loves the concept of space exploration and enjoys the dystopian future genre. This game portrays a more realistic vision of space exploration than a lot of games like it.
and I still find it very fun to play despite all of the bugs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great punishing survival settlement builder type game.
I saw the demo on a youtube video and knew it'd be exactly what I was looking for, downloaded the demo and preordered the full game as soon as I could. No regrets.
If you like Frostpunk and that type of game, this'll probably be for you.
It's got space, peril, bleakness you can just about manage through to occasional moments of hope, attrition in bucketloads, lots of times when you'll feel like you have the resources and time to do something and then realise what a mistake that may have been... even on the easiest settings you can make a wrong move that'll leave you in trouble, as it should be. One thing I hate to love is that there's a lot of damned if you do damned if you don't situations well implemented, like a system that I won't spoil the details of but suffice to say it punishes you for trying to do the smart thing and there's a logical reason for that.
The tutorial is built into the story. At no point do you feel like your hand's being held, equally you're shown what you're supposed to be aiming to do in the next few hours, but at a surprise third angle the stepping stones you know you need aren't mapped out in front of you so if you're REALLY not paying attention and you REALLY waste opportunities you can potentially end up stuck.
All in all, an excellent game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI liked this in the way that I loved Frostpunk.

BUT

I hate the trust system in this game. Getting hit with a debuff for staying in one system for too long is such a middle finger. It's a survival game, why would I not want to take as long as possible to gather as many resources to survive?

The tech circle is also rather painful, rather then being able to work my way to the exact tech I think I need I'm forced to research every tech in the tier before I can move on to the more powerful upgrades and buildings. This means you spend a lot of time researching buildings and tech that's useless to you before you can unlock those that would actually help you out of the death spiral you're stuck in.

The game also requires quite a lot of back tracking, as you'll unlock upgrades for buildings and instead of being able to upgrade them you'll have to tear them down and then rebuild the new building. This isn't too bad as you do get all of your resources back, but it takes time. TIME THAT YOU DON'T HAVE, BECAUSE THE GAME DEBUFFS YOU IF YOU TAKE TOO LONG. This is the second middle finger.

All and all if you like frostpunk I think you'll like this game, just maybe play on an easier difficulty then what you would in frostpunk (So if you completed frostpunk on normal, choose easy for this game.) I just don't think the mechanics and game design of Ixion is a match for Frostpunk. The price is likewise a bit high for me considering it has 0 replay-ability. But I got it for 50% off and that price feels quite fair.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpace station builder

A city builder, but the city moves, and it’s in space. Oh and everyone eats insects.

Gameplay: Game is broken into three sections. City building, space sailing and decision making events. The city building is fun with lots of options available to build the perfect city. Space sailing is OK, just an ability gather resources and avoid ‘space weather’. The decision making is where a lot of the flavour and story come into play, with various options some being good, others not so much.

Story: The basic idea is you have to find a new home and the core storyline is fine. But there is a lot of weird and wacky things going on all around you. So yes, the story is very entertaining, but the ending is sadly a bit under developed.

Graphics: Very pretty, and runs nice and smooth, for the most part.


A fun city builder in space with lots of story beats to keep it interesting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think the criticism is fair. It's a really cool and addictive game. I don't think it's that difficult. I managed to play to Chapter 3 and then lost but I could have salvaged the situation. The game will put you into some crisis situations and push your base to the edge of collapse, but you can usually rectify the situation if you take the right measures. And you can also prepare for the next time you hit the same crisis. There is just some learning curve.

Something that bothers me little is that it's very scripted for a city building game. Sometimes it feels like you just need to build certain things at certain times. Especially in the beginning.

To me Frostpunk was much more frustrating. I restarted 4 times and never got really far. In this one I'm at my second campaign play-through and I'm much more efficient now that I know the mechanics better.
