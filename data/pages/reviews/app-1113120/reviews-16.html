\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have hundreds of logged hours each on Factorio, Rimworld and Oxygen Not Included, and I can say that this game is in their league of challenge, mechanics and storytelling. I was a bit skeptical in shelling out for a game that had so many detractors, but I can say that every penny was well spent.

I am picking up the game about 1 year after release, so perhaps I have a different view from others who played at launch, prior to the numerous patches.

This game has that rare accolade in my world of depriving me of sleep and practically any other activity, so engrossing was the gameplay. I have done little else in the last week! Yes, it is hard, it took me two reboots (starting on Normal difficulty) before I really got to grips with the many competing demands placed on you as the Administrator of a human space ark. I'm mostly through my third attempt now and everything is running very smoothly.... too smoothly.

UPDATE: After 76 hours of gameplay I not only completed the game on Normal mode, but, now knowing exactly what to expect and become expert in managing the ship, the population and the fleet, was able to complete in Challenge mode. Now I can sleep. Bravo to the development team for this epic game! My GAME OF THE YEAR 2023.

9/10 - Superb!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tContext:
• 40 Hours played.
• 1 Full playthrough of the story on "Journey" difficulty.
• Limited RTS experience as a player.

In Short:
A fun RTS/Sim space game with a focus on the narrative. 100% Worth the price and time invested into it.

Positive Standouts:
+ In-Depth non-combat city sim upgrade/building system & population system.
+ Story unfolded at exponential pace, and rewards the player for their choices/effort with fragments of narrative.
+ Story events are able to generate a "feeling of urgency/danger".
+ Configurable difficulty settings allow for full control of the imminent danger/rush.
+ Effort has been put into the small details.
+ The game succeeded in capturing me into it's atmosphere/setting until the end.

Negative Standouts:
- Exponential pace of story → Lead me to be unable to enjoy the end at my own pace, making me miss narrative fragments.
- Multiple ending choices (plus), however doesn't entice me enough to play through again.
- Lack of an actual RTS opponent leads the game to become stale in its RTS mechanics the longer you play.
- Technology upgrade system is odd → A lot of unwanted upgrades, and a strange choice to give the player plenty resources to unlock upgrades at the end, when it provides no value to do so.\t\t\t\t\t
