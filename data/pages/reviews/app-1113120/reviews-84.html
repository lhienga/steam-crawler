\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is good, not great but definitely good.

The visuals are stunning but there were a couple instances where I experienced frame drops while nothing was happening, I'm not sure why this happens but the frames usually never drop below 40fps anyway.
Some of the gameplay elements had me grinding my teeth together for a couple hours, but otherwise everything is relatively easy to pick up on and you'll likely know exactly what you're doing by the time you've unlocked the third sector.

I do have some minor complaints about crew and resource management between sectors, and unlocking a new sector brings so many unnecessary challenges that I found myself actively avoiding the chance to unlock a new sector when I could.

All in all this is definitely a 6/10 game at least. It's not perfect but is good nonetheless.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game after a bad day at work. I was pleased to play on "Journey" mode that would introduce the story without the difficulty issues that the other reviews indicated.

However, the game didn't auto-detect good graphics defaults, so on my (not new, but not ancient!) laptop I spent the first 15 minutes making the resolution lower and lower until I found something where the options screen didn't move around in juddering leaps, nor have the text too tiny and fuzzy to read. OK, not a great start... then the game crashed.

OK, not a good start... so I start it again and start playing through. UI seems quite complicated, maybe they could have added feature-by-feature rather than let me blunder around. I had to look at a guide to work out how to build roads, I weirdly missed the button in the UI for a good few minutes of looking. And I like the idea that buildings have doors that need to be on roads, though that wasn't introduced in the game, just in the guide I read about building roads. Hmm, I had to use a guide during the tutorial...

...then the game crashed again after about an hour of play, just as I was building the big spaceship engines. I have not checked for an autosave feature, because even if there is one, I'm not sure I want to keep playing.

This game seems like it has cool story, cool choice-based branching mechanics, fun tech tree, cool music, cool building mechanics... and a QA/playtest department that needs more staff and more clout. I can't recommend this game, and I'm considering asking for a refund.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove this game. As far as indie titles go, IXION is crazy high quality. Great direction, polished UI, crisp sound design, incredible soundtrack, beautiful illustrations, everything looks and sounds great. Feels like it could be a AAA game if AAA publishers made space station management games.

I'm a bit of fiend for management games so this game was already my cup of tea, but if you don't like managing things, collecting and moving resources, putting out fires (figurative and literal), you probably wont like this game. There's a pretty compelling, if sparse story and interesting little pockets of sci-fi stories on a lot of the planets you visit but the main event is managing everything mentioned above.

It's pretty meaty so if you are in the market for a game that you can spend a good amount of time in, this is a good one for that. There are moments of frustration as there are in any difficult game but the satisfaction comes from resolving those moments using your big ole brain.

I adore Bulwark studios, IXION and Mechanicus prove they are a game dev team that makes products at a higher quality than 99% of what's out there. If you want to try something new and discover a new game studio to look out for in the future, that's another good reason to pick this up.\t\t\t\t\t

A Stellar Journey Worth Taking

As an avid gamer and lover of space-themed experiences, I couldn't wait to get my hands on IXION. I am excited to report that the game not only met but surpassed my expectations. Developed by Bulwark Studios and published by Kasedo Games, IXION is a breathtakingly beautiful and immersive space survival game that is both challenging and captivating.

Graphics & Visuals: 9.5/10

From the moment I launched IXION, I was struck by its stunning visuals. The developers have crafted a truly immersive experience, with gorgeous space backdrops, detailed character models, and intricate station designs. The lighting effects and particle systems add depth and realism to the game, creating a sense of wonder and awe as you explore the vastness of space.

Gameplay: 9/10

IXION's gameplay is a refreshing mix of space exploration, resource management, and station building, with a healthy dose of survival mechanics. Players must carefully manage their station's resources while exploring the universe, discovering new technologies, and making critical decisions that will shape the future of their space-faring civilization. The game is challenging but never overwhelming, striking a delicate balance between strategic thinking and moment-to-moment action.

Story & Narrative: 8.5/10

The narrative of IXION is intriguing, with players tasked with guiding humanity's last hope after Earth's resources have been depleted. The story is well-paced, and its branching paths ensure that players' choices have a meaningful impact on the outcome. Though some plotlines feel a tad predictable, the game's rich lore and background more than make up for any shortcomings.

Sound & Music: 9/10

The soundtrack and sound design of IXION are truly exceptional. The game's music is hauntingly beautiful, perfectly capturing the isolation and wonder of space exploration. The sound effects are crisp and immersive, further enhancing the game's atmosphere. Whether it's the hum of your station's engines or the eerie silence of deep space, the audio in IXION adds a layer of depth to the experience that cannot be overstated.

Replayability: 8/10

With its procedurally generated universe and branching storylines, IXION offers a high degree of replayability. Players can experiment with different strategies and choices, leading to multiple endings and outcomes. Although some gameplay elements can feel repetitive after multiple playthroughs, the game's core mechanics are solid enough to keep players coming back for more.

Overall: 9/10

IXION is a must-play for fans of space exploration and survival games. Its beautiful visuals, immersive gameplay, and engaging story create a memorable experience that will leave players eager for more. With its blend of strategy, exploration, and resource management, IXION is an addictive journey through the stars that is not to be missed.\t\t\t\t\t
