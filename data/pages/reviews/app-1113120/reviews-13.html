\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIxion is sort of Homeworld plus Frostpunk but without the virtues of either.

It's not exactly a bad game, but I don't recommend it. I really wish Steam had a "on the fence" option for this one. I have a hard time putting my finger on any one thing that's a dealbreaker for me. There's just a lot of little bad choices or poor implementations that add up to not liking it.

The biggest complaint I have is just that the story is...not gripping? I'll put up with a lot of gameplay annoyances if I like the story. Homeworld was really fiddly but the story dragged me along. Frostpunk has the same dumb scripted events that Ixion does but the story was good enough I didn't mind flipping away to the wiki to see what the "right" choices were for events. To be fair to Ixion's story, like most of the game, it isn't exactly bad--it's just not good. There isn't a single character in it that I found interesting. Literally everyone is a boring, unethical, jerk. Except the child AI, I guess. That kid was okay, even if the game's narrative seems to regard being turned into an AI as a crime for reasons I never found explanation for. I don't mind morally complicated characters, but nobody actually was complicated. They were just one note--almost caricatures. CEO guy and the ship AI were as close as the game got to nuance. CEO guy was clearly written to be a sympathetic sort of Paul Atreides character but the writers did not pull off Paul's tragedy and just ended up with a genocidal maniac in the end that I felt no pity for. I was hoping the ship AI was going to turn out to be the character I found interesting but she's just... confusing I guess is the word? Maybe I missed an event that made her face-turn-heel toward the end make sense, but all the optional stuff I did made her attitude change toward the end of the game make less sense instead of more.

The music is great. The cinematics are pretty good, though they had the weird side-effect of making the in-game graphics feel worse than they actually are. The in-game graphics are adequate and the O'Neil cylinder city design is memorable. The voice acting is...um. It exists and I kind of wish it didn't. Overall, Ixion is just...a little too tedious for me, I guess? I can definitely see a sort of person who would like this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes I recommend but there's one major caveat.
Only play custom and turn resource levels up to max. It will more closely resemble reality.
I did an annoying amount of math and found that there is at least 750k square feet of buildable space inside the Tiqqun. I did that math based on the approximate size of a human in the thing, estimated each square to be about 8ftx8ft, then counted how many steel mills I could fit in a given sector, multiplied by the number of sectors.
Even if a square is 10x10ft or 15x15ft, we aren't talking dyson sphere here.
Then, even if the rest of the structure of the Tiqqun was 10x the internal buildable size, there would still be thousands if not millions of years of material to maintain it in just our solar system, let alone a solar system where no one has inhabited it.
In most games the rate of trees regrowing is unrealistic, the rate of fish restocking is unrealistic, things like that. Resources regenerate too fast.
In this game, resources are unrealistically scarce by default. But you can, fortunately, adjust that.
I kinda wish the devs made the resources harder to access instead of just capping the quantity. Use distance or hazards or whatever.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately after six hours I can't recommend this game. It is painfully close to being a game I spend ~200 hours in, but some gameplay issues hold it back.

Good

This game has a very high level of SFX and VFX polish, and the game is generally presented well. It looks beautiful and the animations of your ship are a dream. The soundtrack is incredible - one of the best I've ever heard in a city-builder. There are compelling cinematic moments that punctuate an interesting story, and events that keep the story moving forward. Fans of hard science fiction stories will enjoy the narrative and general vibe of this world.

The general idea here (city-building on a ringworld) was novel enough to bring me in and entertain me for a few hours, and the game loop of star system exploration in combination with managing your ship has interesting bones/structure. There is a lot of depth and complexity in how you set up and manage your city.

Bad

The gameplay pacing here is very poor. I'm generally either speeding the game up 3x to get to my next decision, or I'm pausing the game constantly to race the clock against some impending accident or deadline, with few moments in that pleasant in-between. There is too much time simply responding to events, and not enough time where you can create and build the type of ship you want to create. The decisions in front of you often feel too constrained, with a discrete set of buildings required to meet goals, instead of having any sense of self-expression in how you manage your city.

Resource management is confusing and hard to control, which turns the game's novel concept (handling separate sectors that comprise a whole on a ringworld) into a source of frustration. I also spent ~2 hours building my ringworld in only one section - this game should really open up the ring faster, so I can interact with that interesting concept. After six hours, I had only unlocked 2/6 sections.

There also seem to be ways to essentially tank or impair your file permanently, which just gave me anxiety with no upside; this is a very story-driven, linear game that you don't want to attempt multiple times.

Ultimately feel bad giving this game a 'Don't Recommend' but there are some key issues that turned me away after six hours. It's a shame because there is an incredible game here that could've been great with some different systems design decisions.\t\t\t\t\t
