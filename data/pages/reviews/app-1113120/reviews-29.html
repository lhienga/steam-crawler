\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere's a game which I walked into, thinking it was a chill, space-themed city builder. Couldn't have been more wrong there.

Played on Challenging Mode, IXION will test your patience and limit, and have you save-scum like there's no tomorrow. The food rapidly depletes fast unless you balance yourself, and gathering resources must be balanced with space constraints, as you're limited to six maximum localities. And G-d help you if the hull stability falls below a certain point. These are but some of the trials you'd face.

The storyline was mildly interesting, what with its exo-apocalyptic premise, thrown in with the hostile AI/alien (can't tell) you encounter in the second half of the game. In many respects, although it may be unfair to make such a comparison, IXION resembles Frostpunk. You have to make hard choices, which can be unpopular with the population but may be necessary to survive. Although, IXION has more in-your-face surprises when making choices while exploring space.

I bought the game on sale after keeping an eye on its general reviews. For the most part, I agree it's a well-crafted game. My only gripes are the lack of space to build (a moot point given IXION's premise) and the apparent lack of modding. No support is something to expect, but no modding tools may well hamper IXION's longevity. This is an age-old stance of mine; games, particularly city-builders, would thrive with modding in this contemporary gaming period of ours.

So I found IXION enjoyable enough to play, but doubt if I'd replay the game again, now that Challenging Mode has been completed. I may be wrong, who knows? Still, it was worth the money.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has the potential to be a lot of fun, but it's easily the most unforgiving real time city building game I have ever played. I had to restart the campaign several times because even on normal difficulty, it's nearly impossible to avoid making simple mistakes that cause your ship's crew to spiral out of control and mutiny, the hull of your ship to collapse because you can't keep up with necessary repairs due to resource constraints, or simply running out of that critical resource needed to progress in the campaign storyline.

This game even on normal difficult is a constant struggle to keep everything balanced and operating smoothly. You'll need to keep a constant awareness of your resources, the demands of the crew, your upcoming mission objectives, and more. Efficient and methodical micromanagement of your assets and the limited build space you have available is essential to your survival and continued progression in the game.

After my third restart, I decided constantly being kicked in the nuts wasn't my idea of fun, so I went ahead and customized the difficulty setting a bit before starting over again. I slightly increased food production (+25%) and decreased the number of triggers and the severity of penalties for failing to satisfy crew requests. The majority of my game settings are still left at normal, but with these few adjustments, the game is no longer stressful and is a lot more fun. On a related note, I suggest against increasing the resource abundance because you'll find yourself spending a whole lot of time in each system, and before you know it you'll have unlocked and built in all of your sectors way before you ever reach even the halfway point of the main campaign storyline.

The graphics in the game leave a lot to be desired, but with some minor tweaks in the difficulty settings, this game is very fun and can be very addicting. Most games don't hold my interest these days, but Ixion will most likely keep be interested at least long enough for one full playthrough. I only crashed one time so far. The missions are thoughtful and interesting. Thanks to its many customizable difficulty settings, I'll give it a solid 7/10. It's a good game. Buy it on sale unless you have an irresistible itch for a challenging city builder RTS game.\t\t\t\t\t
