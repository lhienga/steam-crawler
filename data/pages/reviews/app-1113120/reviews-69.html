\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - 8/10 - Highly recommended for anyone who enjoys city-builders or strategy games, especially in a sci-fi setting.

---

It's as if Frostpunk, Startopia, and Battlestar Galactica had a beautiful demon baby that will stab you in the eye the second you neglect to feed it or change its constantly-refilling diaper. Oh, and you can't change the diaper while walking around with the baby to get it to the food. But anyway ...

The game is hard. It is hard by design. At least in default mode, you are meant to be under constant stress - that is the point, you are on the brink of extinction and there is no time to do anything but push forward. Having just finished the default mode, I am afraid to even start the challenging mode.

The story is interesting, but at least after a single play-through it leaves too many unanswered questions. It is also fairly linear - the only major fork isn't presented until very late in the game, possibly too late to explore it.

Game mechanics are nearly identical to Frostpunk. They work fairly well, with some minor annoyances. For example, micro-management of resources and population across sectors feels like an unnecessary and artificial complication, it doesn't fit well with the rest of the game.

There are some oddities on the star map that feel like bugs - for example, you cannot abandon a mission that requires a cargo ship to bring back resources from the Tiqqun. If the mission has already been started, the science and cargo ships assigned to it are dead weight until all the necessary resources are dragged to the sector to which the ship is assigned.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the idea is different and interesting and really well implemented, the game lacks replayability. Main story is interesting at the beginning, although it becomes a bit boring at the end. It lacks some cool features that could have made the game indeed interesting like proper planet colonisation outpost (instead of the actual ones which feel more like another science mission...), a real population system (like births, education, specialisation), a proper star map/navigation or a well thought research generation (not only based on scripted events).

Research tree is a bit unbalanced and lacking, some improvements feel overpower, other just useless, building upgrades also feel like out of place as science is limited and best spent on new buildings rather than a 5%-10% improvements. Some high tier buildings like the hydrogen plant are just not even necessary and they feel completely under-utilised.

Cryogenic pods just a huge pile of storage space and the whole system doesn't really create a feeling of having real issues, just like a way to see workers count going down.

I guess the idea of the developers was to develop a fast pacing game where players would be constantly tense due to the multiple issues but in reality hull damage is not substancial enough to prevent players farming every single asteroid in a system, space threads are mostly static/scripted that doesn't force players to keep moving and science missions didn't really cause any impact on the story.

In summary, great idea, great implementation but not really sure if the game is worth the price, specially when it offer no replayability after you finish the story once.\t\t\t\t\t

Pass.
