\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPicked this up on sale as the concept sounded great, plus it looked like 'Frostpunk in space' and I ****ing loved Frostpunk. Unfortunately this game is let down by poor pacing, poorly-thought out game mechanics, a badly planned tech tree filled with trap options and an incoherent plot filled with incomprehensible technobabble.

As an example of what I mean, there's a mechanic introduced early on where if you don't have enough workers to staff all of the buildings in a sector, the sector switches to running on 'extra hours', where accidents can happen that can kill workers and damage buildings. But these accidents happen so frequently and are so deadly that there is no reason you'd ever choose to do this, and the mechanic is only there to punish you for taking your eye off the ball and putting up a building without first checking you have enough staff. This is particularly bad when you remember that Frostpunk (which this game obviously drew a lot of inspiration from) implemented a very similar mechanic in a much more meaningful way.

The game is sadly full of poor design elements like this - to list a few there's plot quests that have you doing nothing but sit idle for long periods while a building cranks out a few hundred of a particular resource, side quests that 'reward' you only with a worthless 'trust' resource that is triflingly easy to max out by other means, an entire resource and its associated tech tree that you will never need, and an overly-complicated system for managing resources that will still leave you struggling to get the game's virtual trucks to deliver things where you need them.

I won't say too much about the plot as I don't want to spoiler things for people who do still want to persist with the game, but I found it largely incomprehensible due to the level of technobabble and constant reference to lore items that I don't remember it ever introducing.

It's a shame because there was obviously a lot of potential here, but the many issues with the game design put this in solid 2/5 territory, maybe 3/5 if you really love the themes and setting enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIxion is an overall enjoyable frostpunkesque city building game with a few bad choices along the way.

The first few chapters of the game are great, getting a hold of managing tile space and unlocking new buildings that force you to rebuild while you are (almost) always against the very slow clock of running out of resources.

Where the game falls apart is in the later chapters where the only challenges are "Did you save up 600+ Polymer!" or "Did you spend a bunch of Alloys to fill every inch with batteries?". The game attempts to force you to move onto the next chapter but attempt is the right word as you can just ignore the consequences which means you can just have a gigantic stockpiles of every resource to solve the previous problems.

There are no real moral choices in the game, the game does not judge you for forced rationing or overworking everyone, there is a cult that seems to be cut content or just forgotten. Even with all my gripes I would still say this is a good city builder. with games like frostpunk and banished being far cheaper and in my opinion I would not pay near their full asking price for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally played IXION as a demo and was immediately impressed. The atmosphere and building mechanics were really engaging and I hung on until full release. It wasn't that well received due to lack of difficulty options and bugs, but it's had a ton of work done on it since then. If you enjoyed Frostpunk, you'll probably like this. Personally, the amount of micromanaging and lack of QoL features stopped me from playing past the first chapter.

Pros
- Looks awesome
- Runs well
- Voice acted
- Nice UI
- Send science ships on missions
- Tech tree for upgrades
- A HUGE number of difficulty settings. Not just hard or easy, but you can change all the metrics of how the game works to suit your playstyle. I don't have the time or patience to replay hours of the game if you fail, so being able to turn off some mechanics was wonderful. Most of the negative reviews are about the difficulty, which has been rectified with the addition of all these difficulty settings.
- Interesting story and events to follow
- Amazing aesthetic and atmosphere

Cons
- Building mechanics are annoying. You can't move things, and you rapidly run out of space, so you have to deconstruct, store the materials, add a new building, wait for materials to be delivered, then constructed
It's very easy to misclick while looking for probe locations, which launches it and wastes the resources
- I found the food mechanic confusing. Food is measured every 5 days, with 1 insect farm making 1 food per day. Which means every time I gain crew members (all the time) I have to think "number of insect farms * 5 / 10" instead of just looking at a number
- There are no buildings that take up 2 tiles, so there's loads of wasted space
- In order to transfer resources from one sector to another, you have to have a stockpile in each one. If your ship brings in resources to an airlock in a sector where you don't want those resources, you still have to build a stockpile to hold them, to move them. Which means you need all 11 stockpiles in every sector, even in sectors where you don't want that resource
- I dislike there being arbitrarily workers and non-workers on the ship. It just adds another layer of annoyance
It's one of those games (like Frostpunk) where everything starts against you, and just gets worse. You get penalised for staying in a system too long. You get penalised for moving to a new system. You get permanent hull damage for moving. - Accidents happen all the time even when everything is happy and well staffed
Mining ships do not avoid hazardous areas, so unless you watch them every 10 seconds, they fly through danger clouds and die\t\t\t\t\t
