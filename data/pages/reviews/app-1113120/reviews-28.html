\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmbition of this game is obvious. Art direction and music are all great. Ohhh and that Space bending cutscene after chapters UFFFF! Nailed it, awesome!

The thing is... things fall out pretty quick. Here is what I can tell you after what ended to be disappointing and exhausting playtrough:
In first mission or prologue you can hear all these sectors coming online checking phases, Homeworld vibes, then in Space - managers show up and introduce themselves. You keep thinking oh this is gonna be great.
But then, it all ends.

All of those characters are gone, GONE. Don't even bother listening to what they are saying, they are not gonna give you any quests or anything similar (which I bet was the original concept).
Nah you are left with owner of the company - edgy looking dude with prepuberty voice trying to voice act. Very, very cringe. Also not fitting the characters body. Off-puting.
There is even a big bad ship with horrible voice and script, paraphrasing but it goes like this "ooohohoho I am evil I am baaaad, oohohohoo You think you got me?! But here I am again! OOoohohoho"
Luckily there is not a lot of voiced dialogue.

When it comes to management, its awful. At first, like everything in Ixion is fun but as you unlock more sectors you are forced to specialize them if you want to be fully independent which is a must most of the time. For instance a bunch of quests asking of you materials that you cant even find on the bloody map. Switching resources from sector to sector becomes a nightmare and will give you a headache.
Contrary to badly executed micromanagement chewing your time, you are also left with a lot of speeding up time and sitting and waiting... Now, I am aware game was short on budged but come on, at least play your game before you release it guys, you know balance it a bit. Space/planet exploration quest info also gets boring fast, irrelevant, don't read it, which they clearly show with repeatable sketches. Sometimes on map there are "toxic clouds" and behind them are minerals, but your ships cant navigate around the clouds? Or am I missing something?
I think worst part of management part is being unable to simply destroy buildings! You just have to... WAIT!
Ending felt like slap in the face, narrated by that prepubescent person accompanied by few sketches. Big meh, 0 reward.

PS. one time you unlock higher level building, you cant upgrade it over existing older one? Why? It was so enjoyable in older classics, why?

Anyway I wish I could recommend it, it looks and feels good when it gives you a breather. Vision and passion was obvious, however in this state don't bother (unless it gets serious overhaul which it wont).
There you have it, frustrating game that had idea about making Frostpunk in space(no easy task), ultimately failed and ended being mediocre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA rare combination of narrative, survival, city-builder. In that order.

To help level expectations, this is not an open world survival/city-builder guided by narrative. This is fundamentally telling a story around the journey of Tiqqun (the ship's name, IXION is its engine).

To get from one story beat to the next, you are overcoming the challenges that space travel has thrown at you, by developing the travelling city that is Tiqqun, and surveying then harvesting the star systems you visit.

The ring-city is neat, and the soundtrack along with the cosmic views all combine with the story's tone well.

Overall, this is a unique game with challenges and constraints similar to Frostpunk's main story challenge, of building a city where there was none in a hostile environment under time and resource pressure to save as many as possible and survive.\t\t\t\t\t
