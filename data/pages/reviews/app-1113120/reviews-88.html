Ok, so after playing the game for a while, and completing the storyline, its really disappointing ending. Probably the most disappointing ending for a video game i've played in a while. Once I finally understood the mechanics of the game, the last playthrough (after many deaths) seemed like easy mode. Resources were super easy to come by, the stability and hull integrity didn't even matter as I was sitting at +16%. So yea, the story was good. If you like story's and immersive gameplay then this game is good. But the fact that once you complete the story, theres nothing to come back to. Like theres no incentive for me to ever hit play ever again. I've completed the game. Theres steam achievements sure, but theres no continuing on your save. Once the story ends thats it. done. Theres no difficulty modes, while there is work by the dev team on this coming in the future at the time of this review, theres no real challenge to the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are looking for another game that has you make choices on morality and city building like Frostpunk, than this is probably your next choice. The story line is good and the resource management has another level to it as well, letting you choose to dedicate specific sectors on the ship to certain jobs for bonuses. The gameplay as mentioned before is sort of a cross between the city building/morality choices like Frostpunk and the space exploration/events like Stellaris. I'd give it a 7.5/10. It's worth a playthrough for sure once the Difficulty Options update is out at least in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a relatively short campaign, but lock yourself in a dark room and keep the volume comfortably loud for a sci-fi experience. It's not visually obscene, but it has some dark undertones that highlight the tragedy you are challenging. The cinematics are golden, especially for a sci-fi dork like myself.

The building mechanics are fun. It's not as complex as a game like Stellaris but the limitations on real estate guide you into very interesting moments where you have to decide how you want to commit your research and infrastructure.

The game incentivizes you to progress but doesn't necessarily force you to leave each level upon some completion. I did not expect many of the story's developments and twists (thanks to a relatively spoiler-free marketing campaign). You know how movies show you every. single. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. punchline? Not this game, thankfully.

Highly recommend it if you like builders, simulators, survival, and story based gameplay. No regrets on the money spent.

I make this recommendation after finally beating the game. I will be playing it a second time on max difficulty when difficulty settings get introduced.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI LOVED playing this game. Reminded me of games like Theme Park World but with narrative choices and chapters with challenges that acted like thresholds to break through with my smarts.

To be fair I was sold within the first 5 seconds of the trailer with the vole jump.
The sound design, music and sci-fi themes are *chief's kiss*.

The weakest part for me was the ending because I was honestly expecting another chapter or two. Just more to really test my ship build and explore, expand or explain the story beats that were only really hinted at throughout the game.
I was waiting for the chapter where you would jump to a system with zero or very few resources forcing me to survive on what I had stored and prepared from the solar system before. There are so many things the game gives you to become self sufficient. To the point where I thought the game could have an endless mode. But then it ended.

The reviews are right that the game is hard. But it just felt old school in that regard. It expects you to replay it and really dig into it. Save often! I only had to do a hard reset once and then save scum my way to the end. And that felt like the right way to play.

Trial and error, discovery, intrigue, set backs, risk taking and over coming the impossible.

It was awesome.\t\t\t\t\t

Ixion is a maximisation problem posing as a game. It has decent graphics, a believable atmosphere helped by the stellar soundtrack and premise, and enough depth to keep you interested until the end of the campaign.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game !

Kind like Frostpunk in space but with less dilemma/moral decisions, or at least they don't have that much implication on the story.

In Frostpunk you have sometime to choose between very questionable paths for what you think at that time as the way to the greater good. Ixion have those kind of event too but I would say the "good way" is less blurry or problematic, but scenaristic entanglement are too rare in those kind of games so it's nice anyway.

Second soft spot is the space / fleet management wich is a bit clumsy and make, especialy the weather events, sometime painfull to micro manage.

Beside that, the station construction part is excellent, and the very enjoyable thing is that you can chill on a sector to optimize your ship before going further with the story.

You can see in many aspects that the developers made it with great implication, music & global artistic direction are great, the details in animations, some beautiful cutscenes, a cool scenaristic effort, sci-fi references. Thoses folks wanted it sleek and you can feel it.

Hope Ixion 2 can be a project, with a refined UX (possibility to recall and assign ships, drag and drop to select planets, better stocks transfer managment etc..) and globally more advanced option for fleet and space management, manageable planets colonies, exploration, those kind of things.

Anyway, I had a great time. Cheers to the devs.\t\t\t\t\t
