Resource management is horrific, especially if you over-mine, resource management between sectors is just awful. If you accidentally have a ship bring a resource into a sector for which you don't have a stockpile then it can't be transferred to sector with that stockpile. Why? You can't make very basic blanket management policies like 'if stockpiles are full for x resource then stop mining x resource'. Or 'if x sector has surplus workers and y sector needs workers then send the required workers'. Why? I can't help but think it's a deliberate choice and I think it's a bad one. It seems like they want you to restart, learn your lessons, repeat your tweaked build order, and go again. It's not how I like to play these games but perhaps it's how some do. Plays more like a rogue-like than a game like this should.

If simple quality of life automation could be added then it'd be a really good game. Comparing it to Frostpunk, as a lot of others have, is a joke, it really doesn't compare in terms of quality.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I never write reviews. But I have to say a few simple things for the potential buyer of this game.

For starters, I absolutely loved this. The atmosphere, the music, the story and relationship that it creates with certain crew (ships mind you, not individuals) if they have been with you for some time was a wonderful experience. And I'll admit- it took me I till the 3rd (I think) save until something clicked. Even after though, there were more than a few times that I was on the edge of my seat trying to resolve a catastrophic oversight that I had not planned for.

The game has a linear story. Yes, you are designing and managing your 'own' station, but it has a driven narrative with handcrafted events. That being said, there is replay ability depending on if you want to play out all possible events, or try alternative 'administration methods'. I played through on 'Default', but am already looking to play again on 'Challenge' difficulty with all the newfound knowledge. I *highly* recommend you either play on 'Default', or switch down to 'Journey' if you find it too difficult.

As far as "city builders" go, this is unlike any other that I have played- so much I wouldn't necessarily classify it as such. Yes, you have a population that you have to manage and support. However, I find that it fits in the rift between a "factory" game and a "city builder", creating a unique blended experience that just... works. Each sector of the Tiqqun becomes it's own unique atmosphere. They can be self-contained eco-systems at first, but as you progress you end up specializing them and managing resource streams across them.

The game is MEANT to have micro-manager level details- it's even a part of the story, of the 'Administrator' role you play. To those comments mentioning poor design due to lack of automation, I implore you to try it again and play around with the systems. There IS a level of automation there (you can assign 1st, 2nd, and 3rd priority to resource gathering ships). There is some complexity with managing resources in more than one storage bay, but it is do-able. I frequently doubled up or even tripled up to tackle certain tasks. From there, it all comes down to how you design your station.

Eventually the difficulty ramps up and you have to manage a whole lot in an incredibly short amount of time. I will not give any spoilers here, or anything that might hurt the intended experience the developers are trying to give you. I went through completely blind, and am extremely glad I did.

What I will say, is that the time control is your friend. Use it. A lot. Make sector plans and play with the un-relenting tetris game of making everything you need work. Things are oddly shaped for a reason- to add difficult decisions and encourage opening new sectors.

If you've read this far, I hope you're considering it. The Tiqqun has all the tools you need administrator. Remember why you were chosen. The next chapter that brings Humanity to the stars is under your watch.\t\t\t\t\t

There's 6 sectors and resources can't automatically be shared between them. This requires you to build many duplicate storage facilities in every sector, then you have to transport those resources to that sector. Each storage facility can only hold 1 resource. Most of my storage buildings were only 10% full, but I had about 20 of them because there's a dozen resources and each building only supports 1 resource. If they supported multiple resources, I would probably only have needed 5.

Very easy fix. Allow resources to be shared between all sectors without duplicate storage facilities. Or allow storage facilities to hold more than 1 type of resource.

Either solution would go a long way to making this game playable. I can't recommend this game until that's fixed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMasterpiece of a game. Many people call this "Frostpunk in space" & I can definitely see the similarities.
This game is a lot more forgiving than people give it credit for (On the normal difficulty) there's definitely a pattern you should follow in the early game to get your economy up and running, but after that it becomes very hard to mess up irreversibly.

The story is fun to follow & involves a lot of reading with the odd bit of dialogue here and there, so if you're not a fan of a massive wall of text when exploring planets, that might put you off a little. (I personally didn't mind)

If you like to min/max your structure positioning and economy, the space station features 6 separate hubs for construction that all somewhat act like you're starting from scratch, there is import/export though alongside subtle bonuses for specialising each zone.

The soundtrack fits perfectly for the atmosphere, the main theme although played relatively often, is always a pleasure when it plays.

The game features a lot of micromanagement with power & economy, it would be nice if there were a few more TLC features like being able to completely remove every ship from collecting a specific resource instead of having to click several times, late game this becomes quite a pain. But generally speaking any issues I do have with the game are very minor/mild annoyances at best.

9/10 game, would rate this higher but due to what I mentioned before & also the fact that it sadly lacks replayability, the relatively linear progression of the story holds it back on this front, often times if you do things differently option wise it'll just lead to bad things happening to you or your crew.
Hoping for some DLC in the future, there's definitely potential given all that happens in the game universe.

Game is worth the price if you value a single gripping experience over bloated filled out games that we too often see today. Quality over quantity I respect it. It's better to be left wanting more, than to be burnt out before it's even over.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just don't feel it, there's some fundamental conflicts with the game that makes it not feel right.
For example the game is dead serious, one of the guys even straight up tells you "space is much less forgiving than science fiction would lead you to believe", but hey you are a science fiction game so what's the play here? It's so cliche like a movie character saying it's not a movie.
On top of that, the visuals have been designed to have a realistic style but immediately at the start of the game there's resources just scattered randomly everywhere in this empty room, why? how did this happen? How is this realistic? There's random pre-built path with workers just mindlessly walking back and forth non-stop like RCT2 guests which immediately contradict the realistic visual design.
Even this space station itself is a contradiction in design. It's got this ring shape that's supposed to generate artificial gravity, but somehow you need to build tiny solar powers to generate power, like what? You have an interstellar spaceship that's relying on solar panels for power? That makes absolutely no sense because the engine power of such a ship will be many many orders of magnitudes higher than what you can get on some tiny solar panels. In fact what it'll need is radiators to remove power, not solar panels. besides, what exactly happens to the buildings and workers when the ship stops spinning to accelerate? It just makes no sense.
I'm not saying everything needs to be realistic of course, but the game is constantly beating me over the head telling me it's supposed to be the most realistic so I can't even suspend my disbelief like I can in another game like for example Anno 2205 or something.\t\t\t\t\t
