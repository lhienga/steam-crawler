Intro
I rarely write reviews - but since this is not a AAA Developer and they really pulled off something unique here I feel compelled:

In the past most of the negative critique this game received has been regarding the rather tricky default difficulty (and of course fairly for some bugs after initial release). These were both fixed in the many patches since and the latest bigger patch added custom difficulty options which makes this story experienceable for anyone - and the journey of that story alone, the storytelling and the way it’s wrapped up in sound design, music and art style, is worth the purchase. If you're unsure grab it during a sale - you won't regret it!


Storytelling and Atmosphere
What makes this game such a gem for me, despite its gameplay being right up my alley, is that it convinces with great storytelling and incredible atmosphere.

For a story to be great it needs many factors coming together. Two of those which are often underestimated and fall short in many games (indy or AAA) are atmosphere and pacing. Not just what happens but how it happens, where it happens - the environment surrounding it or background to the story - and when it happens or the speed with which you get to experience it can lift a good story with decent storytelling to an amazing experience. I believe Ixion is one such case where the developer managed to pull off just that.

Since I don’t want to spoil the story let me explain by using the games opening as an example:
The game opens with the launch of a space shuttle in which you - the future station’s administrator - ride up to the station to take over after its structure has been completed and it’s ready for the first crew and system tests (aka tutorial) before the station can take on the tasks it has been designed for.

The opening animation/cinematic manages to convene the right vibe of what you can expect from the game with not just the chosen art style and world design, but especially with its sound design and music. While the intro is just one example for the games audio being outstanding, it is a great example of how sound and music come together and manage make that feeling of epicness and grandiosity of the undertaking almost palpable: the underlying joy, anticipation and hope for the journey into the unknown and the slight unease that the same entails. Later this mix only gets better as you also get to also feel the drama and urgency in the music that surrounds your mission and the challenges you face, which are constantly forcing you to push further with your exploration.
If you think this sounds great already (I know, I know…) then let me point out one more strength of Ixion: It manages to control the tempo of it’s storytelling and let’s you breathe in the right moments while keeping you tensed and stressed during the rest. You can already get a glimpse of that in what is arguably a short opening cinematic, just by how it is cut & paced and how sound & visuals come together, before it smoothly transitions into the gameplay. This masterful choice of tempo - that is so hard to get right - is not just where the game really shines, it reflects on many layers throughout the game:
  • During the intro cinematic you find it reflected in the amazing music and outstanding sound design which inarguably not just enhances your experience but manages to put you immediately into the right mood for the game and what awaits you. And I can gladly say that this level of music and sound design will accompany you throughout the rest of the game.
  • You will find it in the pacing of the cinematic’s narration and after that the artificial voice and its isochrony of your AI Assistant Edden which has just the right rhythm to let the words sink in and create meaning.
  • It is reflected in the overall tempo of the story managed through it’s chapters, creating a constantly growing feeling of urgency and drama and uneasiness, constantly pushed by limited (and yet somehow plentiful) resources.
  • And in the necessary balance to make that pacing work: you will have phases of seeming rest and peace where your station runs smoothly and you can focus on peaceful exploration and expansion - till it doesn’t anymore and the story or your exploration throws you a curveball. After all space is one big void and you never know what you’ll find in it or on the planets in the system.

This balance and focus on atmosphere and tempo - especially knowing when or how to slow down and take speed out of the experience and still create tension - is exceedingly rare nowadays. This is true regardless of media but especially games too rarely show a mastery of that. This is where Ixion shines outside of its gameplay for me. It’s not just a great game: It’s a great experience of a game.


Gameplay
The gameplay can be split into 3 main loops:
  1. You manage, build and organise the interior (and to some degree exterior) of a massive, moveable space station. Balancing production, efficiency, the needs of your crew (food, housing, maintaining their their sanity and trust in you) and your own strategic goals or priorities as the stations administrator with at times limited resources and always limited space. The more you progress the more it can turn into a puzzle game requiring you to rebuild entire parts of the station after unlocking new features or researching new technologies to make the most of them.
  2. On the solar system map you get to move and reposition your station to increase efficiency of your support vessels, engage in story events or avoid obstacles or threats such as weather effects or whatever else can threaten a massive space station. This can sometimes be just the station itself, since moving and accelerating such a massive piece of metal has a price on the stations structural integrity that needs to be paid.
  3. On said map you also get to launch probes and command mining & exploration vessels which allow you to upgrade, sustain and fuel your station and its crew, find new resources and explore the solar system and its hidden surprises. Exploration is one of the main driving factors for the story. Here you will face many decisions - some small some large and many with lasting effects or consequences
    This ranges from finding or missing out on additional ressources, accidentally killing potential new crew, losing your exploration team and/or vessel and might even affect the relationship of your station’s crew with you. Some decision have lasting effects and can even influence your ending or your variation of it.


Cons?
If you want me to find some negative critique then it would be that researching all you have access to and optimally preparing before progressing the chapter can at times feel a bit grindy. That is however only true if you want to optimise your playthrough and optimally prepare for each next chapter. Time is after all a resource in this game that you are meant to use wisely and I highly doubt you are meant to play the game in that way. It is trying to force you to make uncomfortable decisions after all.
Some people just like that playstyle though and feel more comfortable being prepared. That is okay and rest assured: you can still do it. Just know that if that happens to be you, then you could have periods of a little grind waiting for your research to finish before progressing (especially in the first chapter).


Conclusion
Wether Ixion and it’s gameplay is for you I can’t tell you - you have the make that decision for yourself. But I can assure you, that you will love the experience and the journey that the story will take you on if you decide to give it a try. And you will want to put on good headphones for it to really enjoy the atmosphere that the game is capable of creating. :)

Overall I give it a solid 9/10 with a 10/10 for sound and music.
I hope there’s more of it in the future - with support for mac and linux as well.\t\t\t\t\t
