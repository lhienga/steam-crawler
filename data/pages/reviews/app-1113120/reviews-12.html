\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's good. If you like Frostpunk, you'll like this.

I particularly enjoyed the writing and the setting, which the game focuses on quite heavily.

I do have some minor gripes with the game, however. The game is also considerably easier (IMO) on 'normal' difficulty than Frostpunk. Personally, I would've preferred more tough choices with serious consequences - this game seems mostly limited to killing your science teams and their ships when you pick the wrong choice.

The biggest issue I have with the game is that the tech tree is set up in a very strange way, and the game doesn't make it very clear. In order to unlock the next tier of research, you need to research every item within the current tier. What really gets me is that the final tier of tech is almost completely useless - aside from progression-related tech, I didn't even bother researching anything beyond the nuclear plant.

There's also weird bootstrap paradoxes in the tech tree - you can unlock the mushroom farm before having a DLS center to enable waste recycling, IE it's useless until you do. It's also not immediately obvious that you need to set waste recycling up in the first place, since buildings generate waste regardless (they just purge it instead of recycling it).

I also wasn't very fond of the final chapter, but that's really up to personal taste.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really hate to write this. I want to enjoy every part of this game and the story, etc. However, the entire difficulty of this game comes down to the devs not really thinking things through and really challenging you, but instead creating "crisis" that don' make sense and are intended to make even skilled players feel stupid.

Virtually every aspect of" city-builder in space" as far as difficulty goes comes down to them introducing something new that causes you to rage quit and restart or reload a save. The level of forgiveness is little to none and the training within the game is worthless. They are truly counting on all of us to simply go on YouTube and watch someone else suffer so we don't have to. Which also means, it is significantly less fun.

It truly is sad, because there is much here to enjoy and consider. However, when I encountered "the storm" and my own people of course don't report on any of this, it broke the wall for me. How does nobody report to me that the storm is moving our way? How does nobody start mentioning the various bits of space weather that we've encountered previously and/or how this is different? WTF are these people doing all day?!

It would have still been difficult if there was a warning. It would have required adjustment for the player and a quick re-planning of next steps. But no. It simply happens, ships start exploding, and you are left in a position where you simply cannot continue and must quit/reload.

How is this fun? It isn't about being smart or even having fast responses. It's about memorizing the moves. I feel likely somebody training to play a video game by simply timing Up/Down/Right/Right/Down vs. actually playing the game.

I enjoyed quite a bit of this. However, there simply is so much lacking here as to make it entirely stupid. I waited a good amount of time before I picked this up. Not simply so it was on sale, but because I wanted the bugs fixed (which early reviews listed many). And while this game is completely up my alley, I just don't enjoy it. It is not just treating me like I'm stupid, but like everyone is stupid. Unless you're playing on Easy mode, it is just going to rub your face in the fact that you're an idiot for not knowing what's inside box #4, despite there being no way you can survive unless you guess (cheat) correctly.

DO BETTER. For all the gameplay pieces here, how do you miss this? It is just bad. If these devs had built a fully immersive VR simulator of eating dinner, then the 3rd course would be broken glass.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven now I can still hear it in my head: "Administrator, an accident blah blah blah blah blah blah."

Interesting story premise, barebone, repetitive and tedious gameplay with minimal bugs/crashes.

Completed the default mode on my first and only playthrough with one forced load because somehow food was failing to transfer between sector and nothing I did work so everyone not in the food sector was doomed to die of starvation. Magically, loading the most recent autosave fixed it. lol.

They have a great concept and thats basically everything they have. Story is incomplete, ingame mechanics are few, ingame choices are limited and events basically are only for progressing story with minimal impact that either rewards research points or punishes science ship/crew dies 90% of the time.

Lack of Quality of Life tools aside, things are simply not balanced in the game. Either I am severely lacking something or I am in huge excess of it, stability or sector specialty whatnot. The hardest and most unbalanced mechanic is the neverending and unpreventable accidents that can escalate things very quickly - i.e. medical center has an accident and 60-70 people is injured, in supported and optimal working conditions, sector becomes overworked from losing 60-70 people and accident can happen right after from increased chances and pop dies, etc. etc............. by the way other building's accident typically cause 5-30 injured only :)

Most of my time was spent waiting for the gametime to pass and trying to maximize the space used to not have any leftover space because it triggers me - one which the game design is also very not friendly on so perfectionists, beware.
