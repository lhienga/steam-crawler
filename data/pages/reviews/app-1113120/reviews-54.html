\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe ambiance in this game is fantastic, and the story (though a bit shallow) is exciting and compelling.

It is exactly the kind of universe where I would love to spend a good few hundred hours... if not for the following:

I find the actual planning/building/management mechanics incredibly frustrating (as others have mentioned). Any sense of satisfaction via progress is constantly bogged down by relentless, tedious, eye-rolling roadblocks and setbacks.

I don't mind games with a steep learning curve or high degree of difficulty... if the payoff is a tangible, iterative sense of achievement. In this case, however, it just feels like the game is throwing an endless series of chores at you, without any corresponding sense of mastery once each chore is managed.

TL;DR
- Superb aesthetics and music
- Interesting and compelling story
- The degree of satisfaction provided is just not worth the amount of tedium and punishment

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you happen to purchase this game because it reminds you of Frostpunk, just know that you'll likely be disappointed within the first hour of the game. It's a long, drawn out and boring tutorial that you'll likely not make it through. Even if you do make it past that, you'll probably be wondering how they managed to waste so much of your time while not actually teaching you much about the game. The first 80 minutes is completely on rails and yet you can still make game-ending mistakes if you deviate at all from the poorly executed tutorial. Typically, a well thought out tutorial won't unlock features it hasn't yet explained to the player. It normally wouldn't be such a big deal but the tutorial phase is so long and boring I couldn't bring myself to restart all of that just to avoid what is likely the first of many poor design choices the game has in store.

I wish I had tried it out before I missed steam's 14 day refund window. Don't make my mistake.
