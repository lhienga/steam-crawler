\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYikes. I was SO close to loving this game. There's a lot this game does great. Sadly there are a few things that are done so terribly it ruins it all and after 30 hours I'm left feeling like I just completely wasted my time.

First run; 4 hours. learned a lot, restarted and applied what i learned. 2nd run was very smooth and overall enjoyable until chapter 2. Tech progress in this game is scaled poorly. you advance through all other aspects of the game much quicker than the tech tree. as a result you can get 4/6 sectors open pretty easily by the end of chapter 2, however your buildings are early game tech still. as you tech the better buildings are just...bigger. so you now need to re-do every sector completely. can't just upgrade the buildings directly for some reason. so chapter 2 and beginning of chapter 3 became a MAJOR grind to just...rebuild everything with better buildings. Zapped a TON of the fun out of the game for me. even still I was willing to get through it to continue more efficiently and get back to the enjoyable parts of the game.

Chapter 3 spoilers below:
Midway through chapter 3 the giant space storm on the map just...switches sides. no warning, no explanation that that's even a possibility. literally destroyed all 15 of my mining/cargo/space ships within like 5 cycles (yes i had the tech upgrades to protect them from storms. no it did not help). Up to this point in the game batteries were not really needed much so i did not have many. therefore could not get out of storm. storm lights fires on ship, fire takes out the hull repair building. you see where this is going. game mechanics that were not introduced/foreshadowed/explained well just destroyed my 20+ hour run. I could restart the chapter (my loads would not give me enough time to restructure to survive) but I would be replaying 5+ hours of the game, 5 hours that were not enjoyable to begin with since I was making large transition/changes.

Maybe it was bad timing/preparation. but I HATE when games introduce surprises that completely wreck you without ANY heads up or mention of that being a possibility. Not a big deal in games where the time commitment is minimal to redo a section. but this game is very much a grind/time sink while you slowly build and destroy and rebuild your sectors as the tech SLOWLY trickles in and then you'll lose everything so quickly you won't even know what hit you.

all of this was default difficulty because god forbid we make hard difficulty hard and standard difficulty enjoyable.

If you like getting slapped in the face with hidden mechanics and then having to replay large amounts of hours doing the same re-organizational crap you just did...then go for it. otherwise beware. you're unlikely to make it to the end of this slog in a reasonable amount of time to see any payoff for your planning/time\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo all the people complaining about the difficultly. That's the point, you aren't supposed to just stay in the Sol system building up so the rest of the game is a cakewalk.

Difficultly is fine, found it a little too easy after the main schtick of Chapter 3 which was the only time I came close to losing the ship, Though about to try challenge mode so we'll see how that handles. Liked the music, very haunting looks nice for a city builder and runs really well, balancing all the parts of the ship, your fleet and the needs of the crew is really rewarding.

Getting everything to fit while trying to keep everything working and all your resources up can be a challenge but not overly so. But until you are food and hull secure, slap dash over 3-4 sectors works fine. Dedicate a sector to food and hull early and everything falls into place.

The events can be unpredictable but very rewarding and will probably be your main cause of savescumming.

Only downsides I can point out for people a fan of this genre is the sector specialisation past level 1 are a bit pointless (Short of the Space one, get that 100%) for the effort required and some parts of the research tree being a bit off. Some things are relegated to late game and are pointless by then. Also the way the tech tree works, you unlock all the Base T1 Research to get to Base T2 and research is scarce before you fully upgrade the Tech Lab.

All in all a very rewarding game, doesn't seem like it would have much replayability after 2-3 playthroughs but definitely recommend this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIXION is city-building game, but set in science-fiction near future. In addition to managing resources, workers, and building-placement-Tetris, you set off on a journey across several star systems. A unique twist is that you bring your city (semi-mobile space station) with you across the chapters of the story, rather than starting over from scratch in each new area.

The soundtrack is phenomenal. To the point that my roommate, who generally has no interest in video games or anything science fiction, heard it from another room and had to ask about "what awesome music are you listening to?" - so be sure you buy the soundtrack as well as the game!\t\t\t\t\t
