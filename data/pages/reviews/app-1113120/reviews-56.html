\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI (mostly) enjoyed IXION. The game starts of strong, the prologue video is very-well done, and really had me wanting more. The game play takes and implements many of the same mechanics that made Frostpunk so great, and applies them to a sci-fi story/setting. However, the game lacks in a few areas as well. First, there is a fine balance between consequences and straight-up blindsiding. In Frostpunk, when you make choices, you are presented with the consequences for that decision right up front. In IXION, many times you never know what the choice actually did...ever. Whats worse, there are a few "side-mission" choices (looking at you Chapter 4) that if you do not complete them, and then make another series of choices in the next chapter (details omitted as to omit spoilers), you cannot win. I had to replay the entire chapter 5 no less than 3 times to move on successfully, despite beating the objective all but once. In a few cases, the only way to beat IXION is to play it blind, and then replay it again...which ruins the immersion the story presents, which is rough as when you finally make it to the VOHLE jump, it feels very rewarding...pity.

The last negative, was the ending. It was flat. Such a hard game, such a great opening, and you get a graphical depiction at the end versus the prologue's beautiful opening video. It honestly felt like a hard push to the last scene, huge buildup, and then just getting the old-school "FIN" screen, fade to credits.

Would I recommend it, yea probably. I'm just disappointed it didn't live up to its full potential. I probably won't go back and play it again any time soon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally great game from the Mechanicus devs. There honestly aren't that many story-driven colony managers that have a beginning, middle, and end. The mechanics are solid; if you like colony management it'll be right up your ally, the only thing they dont really emphasize is that you REALLY don't wanna waste space to keep things easy. I played about 10 hours then just restarted because i knew I could do much better.
The 2nd run though to be honest was pretty easy. I might in the future try the harder difficulty and see how it goes, but the base game on "normal" is pretty easy to get through. There's SO MANY resources in every sector to keep everything going; No chance of running out of ice, iron, or or carbon. Once you have your recycling set up to produce stuff as well it gets even easier to meet demands.
The only thing they don't really inform you is that by the time you get to the final chapter, there's a time limit in it, but its not really displayed. You kind of just have to infer it and you can lose quite a bit of hours if you're not ready for it. It seems like an upcoming update is reworking how chapter 5 works though so I'm excited to see where it leads because that was the only downside here.

TL:DR:
Great colony manager with interesting story and fun flavor text (via science vessel exploration).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI realy wanted to like this game. I have played it right after launch, saw how it was and gave it a few months, coming back to it recently.

The concept is great, the story is engaging, the way the game captures you at the beginning is perfect. The problems start to happen when you have advanced and you start to notice things simply stop working:

- Mess halls cease to provide sustenance to population even when food is available an the amount of mess halls is enough.
- Goods stop moving from sector to sector once you reach a certain level of complexity. It doesn't matter if you reprioritize them, if you build drones, etc. No matter what, once goods stop movign around, you are screwed and you may as well throw the savegame away.

It seems to me there are certain issues with the game mechanics once you reach a certain critical mass that make it unplayable in the mid to late game.

The whole sectors mechanic is incredibly flawed, stocks and crew should be a single pool and that alone would fix the issues I am highlighting, but this looks to me like the developers are too much in love with their quirkiness and particular way to complicate stuff than they should.

I REALLY wanted to like this, I have given it two chances. I doubt there will be a third one.\t\t\t\t\t

But, this game is just incredibly unbalanced and at times just plain dumb. I hope the devs do like Paradox and make a gigapatch along with an expansion DLC.

The entire game changes once you unlock certain tech because you can basically just turtle the game after that point, removing all urgency and risk. Research in general feels incredibly arbitrary, some research costs 30 research points and amounts to literally nothing tangible, while other tech costs the same and changes everything.

This whole system basically feels important until you get that one building, and then it merely becomes a problem if you need to move that building.

Some events also don't make a lick of sense. The crew, on board of a colony ship in space, complain about recycling materials into food...... yeeep. And they demand you shut down all production from a food source using that, for 30 cycles, which is a loooong time.\t\t\t\t\t
