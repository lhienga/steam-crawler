Where I've progressed to now, when I have the game paused looking at my base it runs at around 35 FPS, 99% GPU (1080p maximum settings). Changing the graphics settings from maximum to minimum doesn't seem to change the FPS much, on minimum settings I saw about 39 FPS. Even if I set the resolution to 1600 x 900 with minimum graphics settings then I still get max 39 FPS having the game paused looking at my base.

My current hardware setup:

CPU: Ryzen 5 3600 (recommended CPU: Intel Core i7-4790 or AMD Ryzen 3 3200G)
GPU: RTX 3060 12 GB (recommended GPU: RTX 2060 6GB or Radeon RX 5700)
RAM: 32 GB DDR4 RAM (recommended RAM: 16 GB)

If my PC is not good enough then fine, yes, I can understand why the game won't perform well. I thought my hardware would run this game smoothly based on the recommended system requirements. I'd be happy if the game ran at about 60 fps on average, it looks good at 60. When I have the game paused I get around 37 FPS, but when I unpause and move the camera around then the FPS dips lower to around 30.

I will gladly change my review to positive if either the performance is somehow optimised in an update, or if the recommended system requirements are changed to a hardware configuration that is capable of running this game at around 60 FPS from start to finish. I think that if I have a PC that is better than the recommended system requirements then the game should at least run at 60 fps 1080p, especially when the graphics settings are set to minumum.

I was really enjoying this game. Everything about this game is great for me, except the performance. I was considering pushing through and trying to enjoy the game despite the dip in FPS, but I think it would be better to wait for either an optimisation update or when I have a better PC.

Please developers, optimise this beautiful game of yours! Or at least change your recommend system requirements so that I know what kind of PC I really need to run this game smoothly.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI simply love this game.

I watched at release as reviews complained about the difficulty, comparing it to Frostpunk. I was very intimidated by that, and stayed away. When I saw that a difficulty update had been released, I took another look, read some more of the recent reviews, then decided to give it a shot.

I am playing on the default difficulty, the intended experience in spite of a simpler mode being available through the Difficulty Update. I have to say: it is absolutely worth it.

I am a functional idiot at games like this. I play a lot of them, but I know I suck. That said: this game is really not all that hard or intimidating at all! You will probably restart chapters a couple times while you figure it out, but after you get the mechanics down, it's very straightforward. In fact, the mechanics are actually pretty simple in the end, it's about finding and keeping a balance between your resources and expansion.

This is not a "creative" game. It's fairly linear, and there is only one way to win: balance. But that doesn't mean you have to build things a certain way, you just need to think ahead a little bit with how you layout the limited space in each sector. You're free to do that as you wish, but you will quickly learn that there's a proper order to things. Once you find harmony with that, the game gets right out of your way.

I highly recommend this game. Even if you think you're a moron like me, this game is not as hard as the reviews imply! It's very rewarding to learn.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely not a game for me.
Despite the fact the game is well executed, the overall balance and tedious gameplay killed the vibe.
On one side, there is a nice story, the music is ok, the life and animations inside the stations are neat, the research and progression through the opening of new sections of the station is nice but all this is killed by an overwhelming sensation of the game attempting to harrass and kill you on the long run, which is not fun to play.
The game keeps putting more and more pressure on you to build and expand to survive because if you don't build enough buildings to repair your hull, you die. So you need to build more resource industries, need to harvest more on the map, need to build more and more ships, build more electricity, open more sectors that will in turn lead to more damage and so on. It's like going over a hurdle to find a even bigger one at each step, no time to juste enjoy playing and take time.
You also spend the rest of your time exploring events on the map and there again, the game just wants to kill you. Even though most of the time the description of the event might give a hint on the outcome to punish greedy people, there are also completely random events that will just kill your crew or your entire ship while your resources were scarce. Yes, of course, you could still save and reload to avoid the event but that's litteraly cheating.
Space weather is sometimes very difficult to predict and will also wipe your entire fleet.
All in all, a genuine pain to play for me. Too bad, it really looked nice.\t\t\t\t\t
