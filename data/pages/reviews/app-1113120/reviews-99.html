\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComparison to Frostpunk:

First, I love the hell of out Frostpunk, and have played a lot of games looking for another one like it. This comes closer than most, but it's not there.

Second, this game, as of March 2023, feels incomplete, so it may get better.

The bones are good. It feels like the people who made this played Frostpunk, and liked it, but it's just doesn't compare.

Morality:
The "moral" choices don't matter. Taking a bad choice will at worst result in the deaths of a few people or some lost science opportunities, but with thousands of people at your disposal, and no distinction between them other than workers or useless people, losing a handful just doesn't matter, and I ended the game with a spare 999 science, so that doesn't matter either.
Any other moral choices, like whether or not to let the dog onboard have an obvious right answer and an obvious wrong answer, and you get a ridiculously huge reward for the obvious right answer (Think a permanent +20% Hope and -20% Discontent)

Resources:
Resource management is just too easy. Pretty quickly your biggest problem with resources is where to put them all. I had half of my ship devoted to storage, and that wasn't enough. There was just no difficulty to come by anything you needed.

Difficulty:
Honestly, it's hard to lose. There's only one campaign, though it is long, spanning 5 chapters, each of those chapters is effectively about 1/4 as long/dense as a Frostpunk scenario, and while Frostpunk has great replay-ability just due to how difficult it is (at least on extreme settings) This game doesn't have variable difficulty settings at all, there's one setting, and it's easy as hell. Worst case scenario, is that you didn't know that that one nonesensical requirement would be heaped upon you, with too little time to have any possibility of doing it, so you just reload an older save, and get ahead of it.
The equivalent of that would be something like "The Refugees" scenario, where you might learn, after the lords start showing up, that you need Infirmaries. But just because you know that in advance, doesn't mean that doing it will be realistic in any way, even if you reload, so you end up having to take a different path which has a meaningful difference to both the outcome and moral concerns.

Anyway, I still hit recommend, because it's got potential. It's probably the closest I've seen to Frostpunk, at least in terms of opportunity, I'm just hoping that my instinct is right, and this isn't a truly finished product. A DLC, for instance, with new scenarios and increased/variable difficulty could put this up on par with Frostpunk, but until them I'll just go back to eagerly anticipating Frostpunk 2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaw this on my watch list after it being recommended because I played Frostpunk and I'm hooked, my only issue is I don't have enough time to play IXION more at the minute.

If you don't like a challenging game then I don't think it's the game for you, there's planning and micro management required to run a successful ship and if you mess up it (sometimes even just a tiny bit) you can easily spiral out of control and struggle to get it back on track.

I managed to get a couple of chapters in on my 1st play through before I spiralled out of control with stability of the crew and hull repairs (I ran out of resources to mine because I hadn't kept on top of polymer production, so I couldn't create any probes to send out which resulted in no alloy and I couldn't keep up with the continuous cycle of resources D: ).

So I decided to start again with a 2nd playthrough taking what I had learnt after about 8 hours or so and things are much easier after getting used to the game a bit more and knowing how everything works. It's important to plan what you're building so you can maximise building space efficiency and create a balanced infrastructure of resource scanning, mining and gathering which should then make everything else a bit easier.\t\t\t\t\t
