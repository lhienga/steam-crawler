\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlmost 300 hours in IXION, I think I'm qualified to leave a review. Sadly missing one achievement because I can't be bothered to light 20 buildings on fire, but I digress (also potential spoilers ahead)...

At its core, IXION is a population management and exploration game with a good story and city-building mechanics. It is very similar to Frostpunk in the way you must manage the issues facing your population onboard the eternally withering Tiqqun, events that occur during exploration and how easy the game becomes once you understand what the game wants you to do. Short intro over since you've likely read all the other reviews up to this point about how the game is or isn't like Frostpunk and is or isn't very difficult.

The thing is, IXION isn't difficult at all. I had to put effort into losing enough trust with the crew to cause a mutiny. If you don't ignore your people, they tend not to have a mutiny (imagine that). People also tend to complain about the game "forcing" you to leave systems early. Amusingly, no one seems to notice there are buildings that provide bonuses that cancel out the penalties for staying in a system too long (happens once per system and disappears upon leaving, so just take the penalty and enjoy your stay) which aren't difficult to acquire and instead are so focused on being told there's a penalty that the ability to mitigate said penalty with the provided in-game solutions becomes impossible for them. It's not like these buildings are locked behind end-game research, they're available as soon as you unlock the 2nd tier of research (which you do after the prologue anyways). I've tried everything to make the game more difficult and honestly by the end of chapter 3, you're rolling in so many resources (even at -75% available resources in custom game settings) any "challenge" playthroughs are pretty much a walk in the park if you get to that point (and its not hard to do so after a few tries). For the record, there are more than enough resources available solely from asteroids to complete the game.

What I will say is that the game doesn't necessarily make it obvious what it wants you to do in some aspects. For instance, you'll want to research the upgrades for your Tech Lab to make it produce more research more quickly. Makes sense, yes, but understandably if you're newer to the game you might not fully grasp that the research is the biggest bottle-neck in the game early on and not be too concerned with upgrading your Tech Lab as opposed to obtaining other satisfying research upgrades. This can lead to not having enough research to get upgrades that are direly needed as you progress.

The game also does have its moments with logistics - as in they break completely. I would argue most issues with logistics are self-inflicted with regards to accidentally asking the game to do something it can't easily do. For instance, if someone is trying to move iron from one sector to another and they accidentally change the destination warehouse in another sector to a different resource without telling the game to stop moving iron to said destination, ALL of the trade across the sector the iron was destined for will break. You have to actually turn off the trading to the destination sector for that particular item to prevent this from happening. That's just one example of the game showing its flaws and while this particular one (and many others) can be mitigated, there are definitely times where logistics can break down entirely without any observable cause leading to everyone starving or running out of alloy to repair with.

So should you buy this game? Why not, just give it a try for an hour and refund if you don't like it. I'm avidly awaiting DLC should it ever come out - though I am inclined to believe at this point there may not be any (no indications there will be from the team). If the game is too hard or too easy for you, there's now custom games settings to tailor the game exactly to the way you'd like it. The one thing I will say is that, despite my many hours in this game, there isn't a lot of replay value.... or really any once you beat the game. I've enjoyed going through with different builds, exploring different options with regards to events, challenge runs, etc... but some enjoy that and most don't. The story doesn't change, though there are two endings (just save before chapter 5 and reload from there) and it is relatively short if you don't take your sweet time absorbing every available material in the universe. What is there, however, is really enjoyable and the details in the game design and models are fantastic.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe hull repair mechanic is super forced. It's also really unfair that you are forced to travel which will further disintegrate your ship. There are many moments where further progression makes speeding up degradation absolutely unavoidable, while also not really guaranteeing you can counteract it - very RNG-y.

Then there's the station build space aspect. Either you don't have the space to build the building you need and get punished for that, or you expand and get punished even harder for that. If you think that you can take it slow and work around that constraint, people start demanding it.

That can result in a relatively quick cascade downhill - people might not have food because food production riots, then people die and then the rest are overworked (and thus cause accidents), which further reduces all production (including food). Same thing can happen with the cryogenics building, they riot that they're overworked when thawing people that could work. Well, sure, you can complain, but for five cycles? There's no way to avoid situations like these either. (At least not until you've progressed much further.)

Research missions might or might not lock your research vessel (you won't know beforehand), often leaving you without the ability to return later (for better outcomes). Which further exasperates any resource constraints you might have.

The game has a nice concept, but some of these mechanics are SERIOUSLY unfun. The unfun parts aren't because of difficulty, the game just keeps on forcing you to do things and then punishes you for doing them and then punishes you once more for not having immediately fixed those things. I've played Dwarf Fortress for a while, where every downfall is of my own making, that's not the case here.

The fact that these mechanics are a problem and the game developers know about it, is that there are so many saving graces built-in. Magic morale boosts and hull repairs? You're welcome.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaw a lot of negative reviews and wasn't sure if I should pick it up.

Boy am I glad I did! What a gem of a city builder!

People might not like the difficulty or resource system but I think both are great. The challenge makes space feel like a dangerous place where you're fighting to survive (for most of the game at least) and the resource systems are complex but understandable making for an extremely enjoyable experience. Organizing populations, working with layouts in limited space, zone specializations, adapting to unexpected challenges, managing your fleet, it all comes together for a tight and intriguing management system.

The music and sound design are amazing. Unfortunately a bit too repetitive and by a bit I mean a lot. Still sounds good, but it's a missed opportunity to not take it a little further.

I'm impressed with the story too. There's some real jaw dropping moments. Like frostpunk but with a more cohesive and long term attachment to the narrative of your city and people.

At the end of the day I can't express what a great concept and atmosphere this game has. One of the most immersive games i've played in a while!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate:

After playing this for over a week I have changed my recommendation from positive to negative. I do not recommend this game as it stands right now.

The reason being (and you will see many other people saying this as well) is that unlike, say, Frostpunk etc. where you are at leisure to explore, discover, build etc. at your own pace, this game DEMANDS that you go on the exact track and exact pace the designers set for you. Any other course will be disastrous.

So, no, this game is not for me. I don't like to be led by the nose through the one and only solution. I mean even Myst and Riven etc allowed you to find things at your own pace, and not necessarily in the order that the designers envisioned.

A LOT of people have submitted their feedback (read: complained :) ) about this so hopefully the design team will listen and lighten the game up a bit. Then it will thoroughly enjoyable.

On the plus side, the graphics and music are first-rate. The help system is pretty good to get you going through the basics (but you will probably have to restart a few times once you learn how things REALLY work).

So... my advice is wait for a major update, then jump in.\t\t\t\t\t
