\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIXION is a gem of a strategy/city builder that has me dying for more.

The Short - 9/10. It's tight balance and interesting story are only further accented by absurdly gorgeous scenes and immaculate sound track. The game is hard for most of the right reasons. The only negative I have with the game is the trial and error aspect of the science expeditions where only 1 choice is usually the "good" choice while the others could, and most likely will, end in catastrophe. If you want a relaxing city builder, or don't want to fail from time to time, this is NOT the game for you.

Visuals - The visuals of this game range from stunning, to absolutely breathtaking. So many times I would press pause and just look around at the beautiful backdrops and really appreciate the detail that went into making this game. Hands down the prettiest city builder out there.

Story - No spoilers here. The story starts off with a great hook at the end of the prologue. From there the story seems straight forward at first but it isn't until you start reading some of the events that you discover that this game has a lot of mystery to it. It is no surprise that people have taken to forums to try and piece together theories. Multiple endings do not require a full new play through either as the decision is, as far as I know, only in the final chapter.

Gameplay - The core gameplay of IXION is a matter of just barely scraping by until you manage to become mostly self sufficient. You will have to demolish buildings and rebuild them as you unlock new technology and as you expand the Tiqqun, your spaceship. Due to limited space on your ship, you have to take the time to plan your sector out before you jump into building. I will cover some more of this later in the difficulty section. Events are mostly scripted and mostly only have 1 or 2 "good" outcomes. This is probably IXION's greatest flaw. There is no real way to know if the choice is a bad one or not in a lot of the exploration sequences. Some risky maneuvers are better than cautious ones in some cases but deadly in others. This leads to either accepting it (which is what I did on my first playthrough) or use a Wiki/Save Scum. This wouldn't be so big of a deal if some of these choices weren't CRUCIAL to making chapter 4 not a living hell.

Difficulty - Let's address the elephant in the room. IXION has no difficulty setting. This creates a tight balance in the game that can easily lead you to a death spiral if you make too many mistakes or are playing it casually. This is not a game where you can relax as you take your time designing a beautiful station. The game hammers in that this station is not meant to be your home, it serves only the purpose of getting you to your new home. You build only what you need to make it to the next chapter. With limited space and limited resources, you will feel like you are just slightly behind most of the time. A lot of foresight is required in this game. Predicting what you need before it becomes an issue is really the only way to make it through the game. What makes it more challenging, is the tight window of when you can build predictively versus reactionary. As a side note, once you learn the core system of the game, you can be pretty much self-sufficient by chapter 2. Just don't expect to beat this game on your first try.

Final Thoughts - In a time where games are easily beaten on the first play through, "Right" choices are made way too apparent to the point of the game practically backseating you itself, IXION is a breath of fresh air. The difficulty of the game is not an issue of accessibility either, no fast reflex requirement, perfect timings, etc. The difficulty of the game is through trial and error and getting it right the next time around. This game is hard and it might not be for everyone (if you filter to negative reviews you will find no shortness of anger at the difficulty of the game) but if you take the time to play it and learn from your mistakes, the pay off is a stunning space survival city builder, incredible story, amazing soundtrack, and a mysterious lore that can lead you down a rabbit hole of theories.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like Frostpunk, then you will enjoy this game aswell!

Set in the not so distant future, you will be tasked with establishing a self-sufficient ark, that will carry the seed of humanity to new habitable planets, to save it from a dying earth.
However "simple" this task might seem, there will be a few hurdles thrown in your way, that will (to put it mildly) make the task rather challenging.

The game does a great job to emphasize the vastness, yet emptyness that is space.
The mood, music and style\\art is superb, and this games intro is the first time i have gotton goose bumps and actually got fired up to tackle the game!

Having completed the game 3-4 times now, i have memorized the events, managed to min-max fairly well, and squeezed as much i could out of the resources given during the campaign.
And if there is anything game\\content-wise i would complain about, then it is the need for more scenarios\\campaigns, just like Frostpunk (It is impossible to NOT compare these two games, as they are so similar in their concept).

With addition to DLC\\FLC\\Expansions, this game will be utterly amazing, and something i would come back time and again to revisit. There is so much un-explained lore, characters and potential in this game, that would merit countless of new content for us to explore this universe.

As of now, i would rate the game a solid 7\\10!

Some wonky\\annoying gameplay mechanics, minor bugs and lack of additional scenarios\\campaign deducts from the score, but with this fixed, i would easily rate this as one of the best strategy\\base\\survival builders out there!\t\t\t\t\t

It offers a great story line filled with mystery and speculations that will keep you up all night to discover what lies ahead in the upcoming chapter.

That said, it does end a bit abruptly and leaving many stuff to personal interpretation, but regardless, the journey for me was very fun, the survival mechanics are all there, and the chase to lock in the perfect design for each sector kept me going and redesigning over and over. That's what you expect form an engaging city builder.

Problem is, once you get the perfect design (crack the puzzle - "trash based eco") and finish the story line, there isn't much to do, other than maybe do another "perfect" run like I did. But the lack of difficulty settings or an endless mode leaves no space for repeatability. Devs promised these things about 2 months ago, so until they do, don't expect more than 50 hrs of game play, which in my opinion is completely worth it, hence my recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI actually would not recommend this game for anyone who isn't a hardcore resource management game junkie, but I enjoyed the 20 hours I put in to it, so I still gave it a thumbs up.

This review is from the perspective of a casual gamer. I don’t have a lot of time after work but I’m really interested in sci-fi and post apocalyptic games. I play resource management games from time to time, but I’m not used to ones with insanely finite resources. (i.e. this game vs Satisfactory where you can find whatever you want, but it might take longer).

This game hit ALL the notes for me. I really enjoyed the story, and voice acting, and especially the soundtrack. I liked being able to explore and have choices that have consequences. I don’t mind needing to reload to learn from my mistakes. This game however takes it to another level by chapter 3. Resource management is the name of the game in the first few chapters, but once you get further into the game it gets kind of insane. The one solar system map is covered an in unexplorable cloud of some anomaly, and there are very few resources on the side you're on. Stuff like this is frustrating, since you know you probably messed up and you’re just waiting to die.

I like to optimize my areas, but the game feels like it’s rushing you constantly. I get it. You’re in space, and you need to hurry up….but then when you make rushed decisions there is a multi-hour long negative cascade effect (that you don't notice for awhile) and then need to go so far back so in a save point that it almost feels like a giant waste of a night. I really want to see how the story progresses. I’m really into this game. It’s just too hard and too much of a time sink for a mid tier player to progress through it, so I have to drop it for now. I realize that yes, I’m describing a hardcore resource management game, but the story is so good I wish there was a difficulty setting for people like me.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRight off the rip, if you're looking for a space Frostpunk, this isn't *quite* it.

There's little replay-ability, no other strategy other than to "find out" if you had enough resources. Save scum is your sudo difficulty modifier - because there isn't one.

(I'm writing this after just completing the story, so about 30h of gameplay.)

That out of the way, man, what a thematic game. Soundtrack is fantastic, masterful even. Perfectly works with the gameplay setting at hand and is worth the price of admission. (even though it's on Streaming services lol)

Visually this ticks all the sci-fi boxes for me, the depth, detail, and the lighting are all on point.

Gameplay is similar to Frostpunk in that there's a nuanced building layout, however I share the gripes with others in that you'll often have wasted space due to most buildings being odd dimensions. IMO this is negligible, you won't need every square. I'll save you a few runs, put the storage next to the things that need it. Took me a few tries to figure out traversal time for materials is a thing.

Folks gripe about hull damage, at first, yeah I agree. But once you figure out that overbuilding is the way forward, you start to understand why the Tiqqun has 6 sectors. it also is part of the story progression, and although it frustrated me at first I think the game would be less... pressing without it.

Lastly, the story. This is like a DnD DM's malicious dream. I personally found it both gripping (playing til 2am for a week) from a sci-fi perspective, and unnerving from a "space is scary" point of view. That is to say it's excellent, and honestly it's the main thing that kept me playing through the challenging and sometimes frustrating bits. I may steal some themes of it for a DnD campaign myself.

The choices you make on research missions matter majorly at first, being the difference between receiving enough research points to get that next factory that you need to progress. But later in the game, you can make more risky decisions in order to satisfy curiosity vs meta - and man are there some "curiosities" ZY6073.

TLDR: Story, visuals, soundtrack, and theme are worth the difficulty. Gameplay however becomes scripted and this would be superb with an endless mode, which I hope to see.\t\t\t\t\t
