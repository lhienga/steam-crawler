\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Has some oddities, but has a good story, good music, good visuals, and a solid foundation. Worth the investment for < $30. Spinny ship go wurrr.

Review:
Interesting concept. Resource acquisition works a little like Mass Effect 2 where you send out probes to find raw materials. Game play has a few quirks that hamper it a bit: (moving / destroying buildings), odd resource movement / management, NPC chat boxes and descriptions cover options on the screen. Quirks to be sure, but they don't make the game unplayable by any stretch.

You can send out science vessels to carry out P.O.I (point of interest) missions where you can select options to get a predetermined outcome. These are totally fine but most the time there is no rhyme or reason as to which answer is "right", its more of a, "select option 2 cause the text is longer" and hope for the best. I wish there was more information so I can actually make a decision, not just a guess.

Despite all these flaws, the game gives you plenty of options to create a successful colony.

When you are playing it you do feel that sense of "humanity's last hope for survival" immersion you'd get while playing Frostpunk (the games are often compared, which I would mostly agree with). The game-play is decently engaging and rewarding. If anything I'd support them for just releasing a game that actually works without being in Alpha / Beta stages for forever.

My Opinion And Thoughts:
Found this game on a while after launch and instantly bought it. One of the best games I have EVER played with the story rich gameplay to the music and SFX that brought me to actual tears because it was so good. The art book that comes along with the game is a PDF on your hard-drive so keep that in mind. Ixion is very hard even on easy. It forces you to make tough decisions to keep order. Ixion is very reactive EX: If a science ship gets destroyed or anything else then the crew will morn the losses and in some cases request promises to prevent deaths. The management of building materials is a constant challenge that never stops due to the alloy hungry ship repairs. The story to not spoil has hope turn to heart break turn to perseverance. Decisions on planets effect how you will handle the effects of the game EX: Not investigating an object in a way that can yield new research or crew mood buffs. The only bug in the game I ever found was a soft lock in chap-2 that was fixed next update. In my option the city building aspect of Ixion beats Cities Skylines: 2 in its current state as of writing. The only real complaint I have is not being able to name mining, cargo, and science ships. Other then that Its great in my book!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr The soundtrack is amazing, the graphics are great, the mechanics make you think, the DOLOS guy needs to stop taking so much f*cking Lithium, there's no endless mode, don't touch the shiny balls.

If you're new to base builder survival games, this will be a challenge for you. It's completely doable, but you have to think.

This is not a "sit back and watch and react" type game. When others compare this to Frost Punk, I get it; you have to have a plan and you have to be best friends with the pause button. You have to be decisive and sometimes dispassionate about your citizens requests. You need to plan out your base as far in advance as you can. You have to deal with resource limitations (specifically people, early game) and plan for dry spells. This game can be hard.

If you're a veteran city/base builder, give this a go. Instead of falling back on my experience with other games all the time, this game handed me plenty of new mechanics and features that got me back into the "How can I exploit this?" mentality. Some things seemed dumb at first, but once I learned how to work them, the game got much more interesting.\t\t\t\t\t
