\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI seldom review games and I have played many city builders. After completing this title on standard difficulty I am overall mixed on it but leaning to negative.

Pros:
The music is excellent and the game has an interesting premise. It is overall similar to Frostpunk though there is plenty to set this title apart.

Cons:
The game has a LOT of fundamental issues. Filled with bugs. an un-intuitive sector logistics system which only adds annoyance to the player. A large amount of the challenge for me was having resources properly transfer between sectors. Key resources would often stockpile on the producing sector while the destination you set them to sits empty. This is a game where all can quickly spiral into failure from a breakdown in your planned logistics. The story, though starting out compelling, is presented in a fragmented log-style format from points of interest and is not particularly deep. Without spoilers: the ending also severely disappointing both in content and presentation (brief slideshow of painted images with some generic narration)

Overall: There is some enjoyment to be had but the design feels overall frustrating. You will reload your saves quite a bit due to game-breaking bugs. The story though initially promising is lack-lustre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been quite a long time since I've purchased a game where I felt good about giving a thumbs up. I was able to finish this game in just under 40 hours. I call that getting my money's worth. But I wouldn't say that at the non-discounted prices. I'd definitely say that this developer punches well above their weight class. For a small team, they definitely cram in a lot.

If you liked games like Homeworld, then this game will likely appeal to you after you disable some of the more draconian game mechanics.

I admit the negative reviews had me wait quite a while until the product went on sale. For those reviews that state that there's too much focus on hull damage, they are 100% correct. If it weren't for the fact that after starting chapter 2, I tripped over the settings which allowed this to be disabled, I would have quit after five hours of play and given this a 👎. But after you turn off the hull damage - it still nags you in-game - you then have the ability to enjoy the story and actually get through to the end.

Pros
  • Great atmospherics
  • Decent story
  • Polished graphics
  • Good voice acting(English version)
  • Good background sound affects AAA quality
  • Top-notch soundtrack


Cons
  • No user manual. I think 90% of the negative reviews would be avoided if they would just tell the user how to play the game. The in-game help is not sufficient.
  • Punishing game mechanics that leave you feeling that game play is pointless, that is until you disable it which is not obvious.
  • Way too many gotchas that are undocumented such as the missile countermeasures.
    There is a balancing point between the joy of discovering something in-game and having to hunt for it. This game misses that point.
  • The component layout is a tad unpolished. As an example, there are too many places where one cannot select more than one item or edit in bulk. A simple example would be street edits can not be done in two dimensions. Selecting multiple objects using a CTRL-Click or SHIFT-Click or using a lasso are completely absent. This forces the user to do too much clicking.
  • The administrative interface is clunky, there needs to be a consolidated dashboard to be able to see and manage all of your assets.
  • Explanations are missing using hover over or some type of hint. As an example, when one commits to a science mission and has to transport resources, there is no visual queues to indicated what ship was deployed, it's status and what is holding it up. I spent the better part of an hour before I realized that my cargo ship would not dock at a bay whose sector did not have resources.
  • Way too many places where one gets stuck. Such an example is when you disassemble a building, if there is no storage in that sector, the screen just stares back at you without an explanation.
  • Lack of a quick save. It takes 5 clicks to save and get back to the game, pure nonsense.

I believe that the cons are similar to others that I read and it would not surprised me if people passed on these criticisms. I mean who wants to play a game where you're constantly met with roadblocks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIxion is a truly fine compact experience.
Really atmospheric. I felt like a space station administrator indeed. Loved the art style and overall visuals. Looks very polished with an attention for details. Sound and music top marks.
Similar to Frostpunk. Inspired by the movie Sphere in a good way. Bit easy and straightforward for my taste.

I would appreciate the option for resizing the texts. It's too small and barely readable. Also keybinding options could be broader.
Cargo ships heading towards a recently emptied location cannot redirect itself automatically but blindly go to the location and return empty to the station. Bummer.

I enjoyed it a lot and I'm looking forward to some additional content.
For resource management and city building lover it's a must.
8/10\t\t\t\t\t

Ixion is difficult, interesting, and requires constant inputs from the player for you to be successful. Highly recommend if you like difficult management games. RIP my AI boy Valhalla. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've done a full play through on Normal, and from that experience I've got to say this is a great game.

The narrative and visual style is top notch, and its woven into mechanical aspects of the games various chapters extremely well. I went into the game fairly blind after trying the demo last year and must say I did not expect the trajectory the story took. The events throughout various missions have you making choices with ongoing consequences, and based on how many achievements I still have to unlock I expect there are a few paths to reach differing endings. The lore is fantastic and its left me with a bunch of questions I'd like to get answered some day.

The game is difficult. While many other games in this genre will laugh at your poor decisions while you struggle back to normalcy, this one will throw you in a pit and leave you there to die. It has no issue letting you fail. The last chapters especially are on a timer, so it's either be efficient or lose.

The different chapters offered a decent variety of change-ups of objectives. Careful selection of technology unlocks is important, and the game will throw things at you over time that will be harder to deal with if you neglected certain buildings or research.

The only downside for me mechanically was that difficulty often came in distinct waves. There were hours in the game where I was living on a razors edge with every choice being critical, and then an hour later I'll be risk free and drowning in resources. It's something you adjust to though and by the end instead of reacting to what I was told, I started anticipating and planning for new things the game could throw at me. The 'Stability mechanic' could use some review too: While maintaining favour was difficult in the first couple of chapters, by chapter 3 I had enough bonuses through passives and researched buildings to basically ignore it. It could be the difficulty I was on, but by the end of chapter 3 I was able to just stay in system indefinitely and strip mine the place because without the stability penalty stacking there just wasn't any pressing reason to leave.

Overall I recommend it to anyone who enjoys the survival city genre: It will keep you on your toes, but it is a nice expression of the game type and beautifully presented. Be the hull, trust in genetic conatus, and always pet the dog Administrator.\t\t\t\t\t
