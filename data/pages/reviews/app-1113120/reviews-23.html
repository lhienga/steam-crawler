\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a really great game inside this one. Unfortunately, it's covered in so many bad elements it can only be seen on a distant horizon. I'll try to avoid spoilers, but no guarantees.

EDIT: After another thirty hours, I think I might have to say there MIGHT be a great game inside this one.

You are the administrator of a ship. A ship that is effectively a space station with enough structural support to survive short bursts of thrust to move it from location to location in space - and it also has an FTL drive of a sort, so it's a ship designed for the purpose of going out to set up and support a colony. You're put in charge to get it finished off, and get it to do its shakedown flight from here to another star and back. Seems like a decent enough idea and in fact this prologue is the closest it gets to being the great game it could have been. After the prologue everything comes apart though - in rapid succession.

Without spoiling it, you find yourself alone in the universe. This is an important item, and IMLTHO is why the game falls apart. I'll explain and again try to avoid spoilers and enumerate the issues.

1) First, and most importantly. Your crew are SNOWFLAKES IN SPACE! I'll explain.

2) The crew is psychologically unsuited for space. No, I'm serious. This ship's original mission was to leave the solar system and support a colony. For any practical purpose, it's a one-way trip. Sure, after ten years or so at the other star system there MIGHT be time for a vacation back on Earth, but that's at best a distant hope. Meanwhile, your people get "the sad" because they aren't able to be on Earth any more, and it continues to get worse over time. Ladies and Gentlemen, SNOWFLAKES IN SPACE! Add to this that you have a medical facility that is supposed to be able to help heal the mind... but this kind of depression I guess isn't considered a mental issue that needs fixing because you can't.

3) Let's see if anyone can finish this famous line from the Age of Colonization right here on Earth: "If you don't work..." (Hint, it ends with "you don't eat.") Once the game gets out of the prologue and the known population of the human species becomes the sum of your crew plus whatever cryo-stasis pods you can find (because they started freezing people before you even left the first time)... well, that's kind of a big deal. Then you find out that the people in stasis are a combination of "workers," those who can do things, and "non-workers," which are basically SOCIAL MEDIA INFLUENCERS THAT GOT SELECTED TO BE COLONISTS! That's right! You find yourself randomly unfreezing bodies and they might be useful, or they might just be useless eaters on your ship. And no, you can't change them nor can you specify at the start which ones you want. Ladies and gentlemen, SNOWFLAKES IN SPACE!!!!

4) Which leads me nicely to the tech tree. This one is a two part issue. Part one, not knowing things you really ought to know right up front. For example (and it's only the easiest of MANY examples), There's this thing called DLS which the creator of the thing crows about it to you in the prologue and how critical it is to you (and it IS critical to your success). But somehow, you don't know ANYTHING about it... in fact, you have to research a entire tier of technologies before you can have it. Stop for a second, and think about that. You are the administrator of a first-of-its kind ship with a mission critical to the survival of the species (even before the mission goes pear-shaped), and you don't have the ALREADY DEVELOPED technologies that YOU MUST HAVE TO BE SUCCESSFUL! Great design, guys! Part two is a bit more of a nit - you really can't develop technologies on your own. You can have this massive, power-hungry and manpower-heavy building that is called a research center... but it would literally take you almost FOUR YEARS for this group to figure out that you can eat bugs as a sustainable food source in space and how to do it. Thanks for nothing guys. Guess they're sad because there's no Starbucks on the ship so they can't do research on something so obvious that we already know how to do it in the real world.

5) Finally, what I call the "game over" McGuffin. This requires a little explanation. Space stations and ships too large to build on the planet's surface are built in situ - in orbit. This is for obvious reasons. Ships in space have a myriad of countdown timers that exist - maintenance of structure and systems, food, air, waste products, power, and on and on. The ship you are the administrator of has the capability to build and repair itself. It can (and does) build the first-ever faster than light drive in history on itself. This is a good thing. Then you find out about the "game over" McGuffin. See, every time you travel faster than light, permanent and irreparable damage happens to your ship... and the maintenance required for the structure gets ever more expensive. Remember, this ship can BUILD ITSELF, including the FTL drive, as well as MULTIPLE varieties of other craft!!!! So "permanent damage" should be able to be excised and rebuilt. I'm not saying it should be easy - it certainly shouldn't. But it shouldn't be a "well, if you jump more than X times your ship will no longer be structurally sound." STFU, we can build this ship from scratch if we had to!

EDIT: A sixth item turns up later. SPACE WEATHER! Yep, you read that right. Weather. In the vacuum of space. And NO, you can't build weather stations to predict it, you can't plot courses around it, and you can't build structures to endure it. It's just there to destroy your stuff... nothing else. It adds nothing to the game, it provides no challenge to overcome, it only destroys what you are trying to build. That makes this a Dark Souls City Builder.

So yeah. There's a really great game hidden in this. My guess is the game got laqurered over with messaging and the critical thinking component was dropped in order to serve a particular point of view. It's not unfixable, but it's probably not going to be fixed... hence the thumbs down.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am into the third chapter, restarted once after learning the basics including what not to do and am enjoying this game. It is what it looked like before playing, a bit grindy and not very forgiving of mistakes even at the easiest level. It has good city/space station building features. Interesting main story. The tension of balancing resources and morale (stability in this game) to avoid disaster has a similar feel to Frostpunk. It has a significant tech tree for research but some of the options don't seem very logical. Example: start food production with Insect food factory that requires no resources after building?
Dislikes: Very long loading times for initial boot up and cut scenes. It gets there, but be ready to wait a while. Plot line feels very linear and constraining you to one right course of action, dictated by new objectives provided during the game (that is more guess than certainty since it is my first play through). If that is right, replay value is also limited. Mentioned before that it is a bit of a grind, and that gets worse as you open more sectors (just opened my fourth), trying to keep resources and population balanced, managing more and more ships taking different actions as the game progresses. And don't expect a pat on the back for accomplishing those objectives...continued survival and a new objective is your only reward.
Overall worth getting if you like sci-fi themed city builders.\t\t\t\t\t

Main Review
So I have mostly good feelings on this game with a couple of caveats (I would love a DLC), I've played this game through (fully) twice now, the second time on the hardest difficulty and I want to lay some things out to people that like the look of the game and I think its good to know some things going in, I'll break it down into a few categories;

Music and Vibe: S+++ - Best part about the game and really immersed me in a world that feels real even if the science isn't there, everything feels so mechanical, real, deadly and truly like you are on balancing on a knifes edge at all times. I love the game for this aspect alone but the rest holds up, may even do a third playthrough in the future and bump the difficulty up beyond challenge. The music is one of the best I've ever heard in a game, deserves awards.

Story: A - (Very vague but hidden anyway) It starts out really good and I think it falls off a bit at the end as others have said, there are some things along the way that make almost no sense without the full picture, only after reading about it after did it fit into place. Maybe someone else will catch it but I didn't work it out from my first playthrough, I think that its more built for multiple playthroughs as you have to do very specific things to get to the full explanation of these aspects. In my opinion the story should have been simpler and stuck to the story that was being told rather than all the extra fluff. That being said it was very compelling.

Game play and difficulty - B - Now while I'm rating this as the lowest part I want to be accurate with this, I love the actual colony management, its diverse and stands out but Ixion falls into the same pitfalls as frost punk for me as to why I have trouble with this specific game type (and yes it does feel similar to frost punk) is that it gets a lot easier to know what's coming and I think this is why you will see a bunch of reviews saying its either too easy or too hard. To beat my first playthrough (which I think was on normal difficulty) I had to restart 3 times as I just failed to prepare for what was coming. But on my first time back in a year I was able to beat hard mode and really had so many less close calls than my first. So much of if you are going to beat a section is based on whether you have prepared correctly for it and it is kind of infuriating a lot of events feel impossible the first time you try them but once you know what to do before and during them it almost feels like a formality to beat. Which is probably why I enjoyed this playthrough the most(gameplay wise) on the higher difficulty.

I really feel like difficulty like this shouldn't be tied to the narrative or at least some events should crop up that are random, so you have to be ready for anything, or, as my preference have these events be entirely random and not tied to the story. As I just feel like a psychic when I prep a bunch of x resource for an event I could not know was coming. (heavy spoiler)although in-game this could be to do with the multiple universes in a meta way? Not sure if that's intentional

Tips
So I want to end this with some tips that I think are spoiler free and will I believe make Ixion a better game for you;

1. Iron is your oxygen - Your time in a system is really only limited by how much Iron there is to mine in that system, I recommend if you want to min max your resources that you stay until you have just collected the last drop of iron in the system and then move onto the next one, doing objectives at a pace you can manage. If you run out of Iron you can't maintain the station and you lose. Getting other resources should not be put off but Iron is just the one you need to be on top of.

2. Food is your food - While this might seem obvious it is the next resource of importance under Iron, there is a bit more leeway with it as you don't need any external resources at least early in the game to produce it but mid to late you will need to rely on Ice which thankfully there is usually plenty of. But the tip I really want to give is that tech improves how far your food goes than anything else, policies and tech can improve your food output and consumption by about 4-5x if you look at the mess hall and farm upgrades. Compare this with other resources that at best you can get an extra 50% out of.

3. DLS center is stronk - Feels like a bit of a miscommunication by the game but this building is so important for success with stability and improving other resources, it lets you set policies and gives large stability increases depending on what you can afford but just gives you +2 all on its own and waste management isn't even usable if you don't have one. Which is so odd to me as to why the game offers you to research waste management buildings in the same tiers as it? Should really be in a tier below.

4. Housing tech upgrades are essential - Everything but the waste upgrade (depends if you want to go that route) is absolutely essential, every tier up of the regular crew quarters is a huge upgrade, they not only give one extra stability if that's the most common living area in that sector for your crew, they also hold more each time, meaning more space for other buildings.

5. You can get (sort of) softlocked in the first system - Stupid I know, but just make sure you are researching what you need in order to complete the first set of objectives, happened to me in my first run I'm afraid to say, its odd as a lot of research that is story related later in the game is free. You are never technically softlocked by research as your research center does produce it passively but its so slow you might be there for a very long time or run out of iron before you research what you need.

6. Stock Up - If you can afford it you want to make big stockpiles if you find yourself with a lot of resources, a lot of these events come down to how much resources you can dish out to something other than the station maintenance and development. And there is really nothing stopping you from being in the system too long, there is a stability penalty but in my last playthrough on the hardest difficulty I found almost all my districts were +4-6 stability most of the time and I believe it caps anyway (not 100% sure on this). Also, speculative tip; something I did in my last playthrough but I might start just having a dedicated sector for storage with a drone bay in the later game, may not work as well early without a drone bay, not sure!

7. Be flexible - There is no penalty from constructing a building and then taking it down, no resources are lost, only the time taken, so if you don't like your particular early game layout when you find a quiet period use that time to tear stuff down and put it back up in a better layout that works.

Everything else its best for you to work out on your own, I just think the game should communicate these specific mechanics better and their importance.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tINTRO
OMG... what a game. I went from being annoyed at it's systems to absolutely loving them, Loves the story from the start, and this is an easy MUST PLAY if you're into building / colony genre of games. People keep comparing this to Frostpunk which annoys me honestly, it's like comparing any game that has a dodge roll to dark souls. Yes, some systems are similar, however the game is so vastly different in it's feel and execution that i wouldn't even bother drawing any comparisons. Forget the bs of "it's like Frostpunk but in space". Play the game yourself and find out what it's like for real. Alright, lets get to some basic breakdowns of things i'd love to note:

GAME MECHANICS / SYSTEMS
My first play through was an expected failure. I didn't understand the systems and there were a lot of them to learn, so i got completely beat by them my first time. After starting a new game and really treating the station as an actual professional work environment i really got into managing it well and optimizing some of the game's systems. In the mid to late game, once you understand the systems, both integrity and stability become trivial which did take away from the fun of scrambling to fix things, but it also made me feel like i "figured it out" and was on top of things so it's largely a good thing, but a little bit more challenge in the late game would have been appreciated. 8/10 on the systems in the game.

STORY / AMBIANCE
The story, visuals, and music in this game are just wow. An experience for sure, and i loved every second of it. So many fun story beats, sad moments, sweet ones, and fighting tooth and nail trying to survive at points really got me emotionally invested in my little space crew. I even sat through every single VOL jump cut scene cuz they just feel, sound, and look absolutely bad-ass. Easy 10/10 here.

CONTROLS
Controls and interface are on point except for 1 single annoyance.. when using mouse wheel to scroll through your ships and their assignments, you end up actually zooming in and out, and if you zoom out enough (scroll down) it will kick you out of the menu because it's not thinking you want to zoom out to outside of the ship. Was very frustrating to experience that every time. all other controls and interface items were perfect and the game had clear data about what's happening in your station at all times and it helped me understand what to work on next. 9/10 for this one.

GAME FEEL
The game really makes you FEEL like batman.. err... i mean an ADMINISTRATOR :P You do need to be very selective in what you do, especially early game, and you need to be always thinking what you'll be needing next and work towards it before it becomes a problem. You get the feeling that things need to keep happening, and you need to figure out what those things are, before things fall apart right in front of you. 10/10 on the feel of playing the game overall.

PLAY THROUGH NOTES
I won the normal mode on my 2nd play through (after my tremendous failure from the first try and not understanding the mechanics) and i feel like i had my fill of the game. That's not a bad thing to me at all, I vastly value uniqueness and the experience of a game than the total hours or replays i get to have with it. This is a story game first and foremost, and that's what makes the game so amazing. And for that, the overall plaything of the game easily scores another 10/10

SUMMARY
So yea, overall it's a must play. through and through. honestly i can't say anything more here to convince you if you aren't already. Pick up a copy for yourself and enjoy the struggle, then embrace your success.

GL HF <3\t\t\t\t\t
