\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI managed to beat the game after restarting a ton of times:
-The endings are stupid
-Some story elements have plots holes and seem to be mysterious for the sake of it
-The buildable area is slightly too small, I do like having to optimize space but even with super careful management I lacked space for some late game buildings and never got to use them
-The game is slightly too hard even on the lower difficulties, making it often hard or impossible to bounce back from mistakes
-Some mechanics feel underutilized, for example outside of a few story elements and solar panels there is nothing to really use the exterior building mechanic for
-The research mechanic gives the impression of free choice, but if you aren't super careful with what you research until the last 2 levels you will back yourself into a corner you can't recover from.
-As I mentioned in a previous point the game gives you too little space, this is a huge issue late in the game as the game will punish you for not waking up survivors cryostasis, while there are policies to lower the trust penalty this also comes with drawbacks. Late in the game your ship will be seriously overpopulated and strained for space, making you unable to use late game buildings that take up a lot of space.

tldr: game lacks proper balancing, gives you the illusion of choice even though there isn't any, and is overall kinda frustrating.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIxion is a great entry in the Colony Sim genre. It boasts a robust balance of strategic economic management combined with a healthy dose of random events to keep you on your toes. I think what makes the game truly shine, however, is it's strong narration and story driven gameplay. It manages to rooted in 'hard' science fiction (such as The Expanse) whilst also giving hints of more fantastical elements that less grounded science fiction offers. Despite being a linear story, I still felt as though the game scratched the itch of exploration and discovery with all of the science ship events. Each system offers a unique puzzle to solve and figuring them out is incredibly satisfying. The game does, however, somewhat anticipate that you will retry many times. It is hard by design and trial and error is definitely a strong flavor of this game's recipe. If you don't like 'starting over' to try something different, this may not be the game for you.

Ultimately I found the core gameplay loop to be incredibly addicting and the story / world building engaging. My only real complaint is that due to it's linear nature, there is limited replayability aside from Achievement hunting. Each system is laid out the same with the same planets, events, and resources. Once you know it, it does take some of the magic away.

If you're a sci-fi nerd and a colony sim game lover, this game is a no-brainer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game. It's frustrating for the wrong reasons. You are constantly having to go back and babysit the original areas you built, and constantly mico-managing resources between sectors. There is a lot good with the game, but it's completely irrelevant given the poor design of the actual gameplay. Below is a summary of the things that irritated me the most, and if these were corrected it would be a substantially better game.

- No way to move people from cell housing to open quarters. Higher quality housing should be prioritized
- No way to purge resources from docking bays. This means your docking bays get clogged up with stuff you have a lot of already, and the only way to avoid this is to remove the ability to collect those resources.
- Needs better resource collection & management system interface / capabilities for moving resources between sectors
- The penalty for having more cryopods in a sector than people is really counter productive to trying to rescue as many crypods as possible. This should be removed entirely
- There needs to be a better interface for the requests- it's pretty annoying to have to search to find them
- No way to move buildings without destroying the old one, this is especially frustrating because one of the core mechanics of this game is limited space and you are constantly having to tetris everything together\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I am more a mix feeling then fully recommending this game, the cinematic is well done.

Graphics are very good, sound design is excellent.

The game idea, concept is grounded in a reality and not too far fetched. But the mechanics of the game are more of gimmick, I would call it more story driven then gameplay driven. You are very restricted on what you can do.
There is a concept of sector in game, the main issue is that each sector must have ressource depot in them, a wall is separating them but even with a drone bay, trains and all path cleared they must have their own supply of food and whatnot.
They added the concept of hull degradation but it seems more like the ship is breaking apart like it's on its last leg. Like a terminal state then actual degradation. The crew is never happy you must waste space to satisfy their unhappiness.
The law building for decision of each sector policy is boderline worthless. Comes with too many drawback that are not enticing to use.
They cannot gather resources outside of the roads which is mind boggling idiotic. Just make it a debuff and make resources slower if outside of the path.

To make it short, it copies certain idea of frostpunk but result in a sloppy execution that makes the game more tedious then fun.
With no real freedom to do anything except play the game in the specific way the devs intended for you to play.
If you try to make a sector a jack of all trades it doesn't work. You are forced to make each sector a specialty in production of something.

The overall first run is fun since it's story driven. I don't see the second run to be fun since the game had shown everything I needed to see and I don't want to be micro macro managing things that could be automated.
Overall, great game on a discount for 15h solid of gameplay. More if you love tedious gameplay and illusion of freedom.
But do beware, it's not a great city builder with survival mechanics. It's not bad but not great.

The missile system defense requires you to manually trigger it. Once you get the notification you have 1s to react since the missile launched by the enemy vessel travels at the speed of light it seems.
They can hack you and disable food storage despite having protocols to protect you from that.
Adding salt to injury, you cannot plot path for the Tiqqun to travel, it will just brute force itself into a storm instead of just bypassing it on the side.
The whole crew is has some kinda of sloppy way of working, they always get themselves into accident despite being given the best condition to work.
There is some mechanics that are not clearly explained and you will loose space vessel in idiotic ways since you need to remove them from the bay if not they will just let themselves explode in a storm. \t\t\t\t\t
