\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game isn't going to be everyone's cup of team, it's unforgiving and straight up tells you in a big speech at the start that space is dangerous as f**k and sets your expectation. There are a lot of people complaining about the difficulty of the game but it's honestly one of it's charms, if you want a laid back colony sim play through this one probably isn't for you. If you want a challenge on the other hand and a game that does a pretty good job of portraying just how dangerous and tough it is to exist in space and tells a story it's a must play.

Pro's

Good story for a colony sim game.
Great visuals and the music is awesome!
Good mechanics and resource system.
Wide range of researches and sub upgrade that give you a variety to you play through and create a few different play styles depending on how you want to achieve the outcome, pretty much all viable options.
The game is hard for me thats a pro i enjoy difficult games.
Decisions are meaningful even if they are preset outcomes this one is a bit of a pro and con because it definitely makes save scumming something people will consider but if you just play the game and commit to your decisions outcomes can be brutal and meaningful which feels genuinely refreshing.
Gameplay rewards optimisation and with notable rewards that feel good and have a significant impact on the game.

Cons

The resource storage systems can be a little finicky with transferring resources until you understand how to use it properly (see tips how to manage this)
Save scumming is totally possible which for me breaks the immersion a little bit and no matter how much of a hardcore gamer you are you'll contemplate it even if you don't actually give in. You'll have an internal struggle of god I wish I could go back and change my decision here.
Limited replay ability maybe 1 extra play through maybe 2 expect 20-30 hours if you play through on 1 speed per play through for 60-90 hours total.

Tips

For resource management never set it to maximum for a sector always leave 10-20 resources free this will make resource transfers work properly out of the sector.
Make sure you have resource storages for your offloading of each resource that can the transfer out to other sectors or completely specialise the sector for haulers and the raw resources they return to each individual sector.


Devs if you see this thank you for a good experience :)

Suggestions

One request, please make some setting that forces you to leave the prologue eventually. You can literally just afk in it and max research for whats unlockable (T2) and then store max science before moving on, takes a lot of patience but it's doable and feels really counter to the spirit of the game.

Allow building overlay upgrades, for instance the single 3x3 houses and 3x6 variants instead of having to fully deconstruct them let people build over them and just need the added resources to be move and reconstructed and same with the upgrade of both 3x6 levels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I bought this game I was looking for something to itch the scratch that FrostPunk had left on me, then I started playing this game and was wowed by how great the graphics are, how interesting and immersive the games world is, and then the difficulty hit and I felt like I was dealing with Frost Punks unrelenting older brother. Now having played it for ~18 hours and ran into bug after bug after unplayable bug, I realize I'm really dealing with FrostPunks gilded cousin, Ixion looks appealing, but whatever spaghetti code is supposed to be functioning on this game is just not holding the game together. I could go over all the little bugs I've found, but really I want to talk about the three main bugs that make this game quite literally impossible to play.1.) You lose the ability to click on all buildings? Clicking on a building is the most essential part of a city builder type game, you click on a building, you turn it on or off, you dismantle it, you see its stats, you select the units this building is creating/has deployed. For whatever damned reason, a building will just not be clickable occasionally, and this will affect all buildings to not be clickable. My go to fix for this was to simply save and reload my save so that the game would fix itself, but finally that fix stopped working, so that ends that several hour long campaign.2.) Ships get stuck?So the only way to gather resources in this game is that you have a fleet of small ships which go out and collect resources then come back to your main ship, they dock on your ship, unload their cargo, and then automatically go back out to get more for you. But what happens when a ship randomly just wont dock? The game designers made it so that when a ship is docking you can't try to move it to another dock, or forcefully undock it, so when it gets stuck docking, you might never be able to collect the resources it has, it'll never go back out to collect more and god have mercy on your soul if you don't realize quickly that one of your cargo ships which could be 50% of your fleets total population has just been missing from the game for some time. Eventually I realized you can destroy the ship when it gets stuck docking, and then rebuild the ship afterwards in order to get it 'unstuck' but really? that was like my third work around for bugs in the game already.3.) Population just disappears while migrating?So the game splits your 'city' into 6 sectors. sector 1 might have too many idle workers while sector 2 needs more, the obvious solution is to just send more workers to sector 2 from 1. But what happens when some of those workers you sent just vanish into thin air? Well better send another population migration, but now sector 1 has not enough workers and sector 2 now has just barely enough, and I thought the problem was just me, but eventually I caught on and saw other threads of this issue. The problem was not send 'Only workers' wasn't checked, the game just didn't know how to send workers to this new location.Honestly what a let down to learn the mechanics of the game, get a little lost in the lore and love the graphics, only to suffer at the hands of what seems to be more and more common these days where a game is just released before being play tested properly. Because all the mechanics, graphics, story, and love from the developers is very evident in the game, but the bugs are just too much to call this a finished game. Maybe give it another half a year from this review and the game might be bug free enough to give it another shot, but as is this game was just frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIxion... An amazing game concept that is soooo much fun.

Unfortunately, the game hates you. It hates you SO MUCH. It't like an angry teenager screaming at you in the kitchen how much it hates you because you overworked a sector for 3 seconds. It slams the doors and screams profanities at you because you didn't plan 10 hours ahead or stuck at precision planning because you missed the timing and don't have enough to build an extra farm, so now everyone riots with NO regarda... lmao I like making them mad.

This game gives you micromanagement of everything and you must perform the most precise (almost scripted) actions to maybe have a chance of making it to the next level. The residents couldn't care less about the situation, (which actually sounds kind of like humans today, so I guess they got that right...) and they will do EVERYTHING in their power to make life in space way worse for everyone.

And who came up with the idea of an UNFIXABLE HULL!?? Its a brilliant plot device that moves the game narrative forward!

Anyway, this is a great space colony survival sim and an apocalypse simulator. I spent 9 hours playing this game and my wife yelled at me Nine out of Ten.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game, so it's going to get a positive review from me! Even then, I want to talk about why I think this game might have so many negative reviews, and why they might be justified.

The game can be stressful, and if you up, it can be a lot of exhausting hours of gameplay to get back to where you were previously. I can't recall a single of the challenges in this game I wanted to do more than once, though that one playthrough was thrilling. You can save, but failure usually spans hours, so you'll still lose a lot of progress. As a result, the death penalty feels too high.

If you don't up a whole lot though, the game is basically one of the better city builders I've played. Not as good as Frostpunk, but it's up there. There's so much attention to detail in terms of worldbuilding and the technologies in place, that you really truly feel like you're in space. There's so many ways to get the station functional, so many different strategies, it's awesome.

One of the major things Frostpunk has which this games sorely lacks is difficulty settings. I've absolutely no idea why they decided not to include any.

All in all, if you want to feel like an administrator of a failing spaceship desperately clinging on to hope as your steel mills burst into flames, this game might just be for you!\t\t\t\t\t
