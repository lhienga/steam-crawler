\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. I kind of fell off simulators over time. My love for building sims started with the OG Sim City. I loved that game. I also loved Roller Coaster Tycoon (again, the original). I kind of fell off after Sim City 3000, for no particular reason.

But this game has definitely reached back and brought back the joy I used to get with those old style simulators. The ambiance is wonderful, the soundtrack is relaxing, the sound effects are clear but remain in the background. The struggle between unlocking new tech and upgrading existing tech takes some thought as I feel a lot of the upgrades are wasteful. The exploration leaves a little to be desired as there's no risk assessments, no footage or animations to watch.

All in all it has been very enjoyable thus far, and I'm only to the second system. I had to restart because I was careless about paying attention about staying in front of the population happiness, and this can get away from you VERY quickly. But restarting is where I realized my mistake in not paying attention to the upgrade system to better your ship instead of just growing and growing.\t\t\t\t\t

They key to this game is about forward planning and getting your underlying infrastructure together. Without that, you may pass one objective, but the demands of the next one will cause a spiral.

Simply do the minimum and fail. I think that's what many people find frustrating about this game.
Recommended if you want a cross between Factorio and Darkest Dungeon (strangely).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIxion is a decent game that has some issues and limited replayability. I do recommend it, but not at full price.

Graphics and sound are great. I think the background music does a great job of filling the silence and setting the tone of the game. I think the look of the Ixion's interior and buildings are utilitarian and distinguishable (something I have been frustrated with in Frost Punk, for example, is having trouble telling the buildings apart - Ixion does a much better job of this).

Story is good too, it's got that nice mix of bleak hopelessness, and all the encounters throughout the game vary from encouraging to depressing. The voice acted sections are done well and like all cut scenes can be skipped easily if you want. The pacing is very much at the players control, as you'll stay in one chapter as long as you want. There's a limitation of resources to create an absolute upper time limit on this, but in reality it's quite generous.

Core gameplay loop is management and building of your station sections, meaning getting the right mix of buildings and layouts to minimise wasted space is engaging. The fact that any time you demolish a building you get ALL the resources back is a great choice. Sure it removes some consequences of making mistakes, but the time it takes to demolish and rebuild is enough to make me want to get it right first time. The additional punishment of lost resources would just be a frustration.

Difficulty.. well this game reminded me of Final Fantasy 8 in a way. Both games have a high learning curve and poorly explained mechanics or decisions. Ixion is very much about trying, failing, and starting over. While that is not inherently a bad thing it does have some consequences. The fact that you can speed the game up makes rehashing these early steps less problematic - sure you can revert to a save file before things went bad but often you'll have learned a lesson that makes you want to re-do your entire approach to whole sectors and it'll often be easier to start from scratch.

The biggest problem is that because of that, once you have beaten the game once your understanding of the mechanics and decisions means the game ceases to be a challenge. Which again, reminded me of FF8. Difficult to learn and trivial once mastered. And because this game isn't procedurally generated, and relies on a single scripted sequence, you're out of challenges.

I do think that a game being good but having limited replayability is good enough to make it worth playing, absolutely. I think the obsession with replay value and making games as much of an eternal time-sink as possible has led to some games being less pure an experience. Some of my favourite games are really only challenging the first time (Portal, Gunpoint, etc).

I had fun playing this game, and once I'd completed it, I was happy to stop. This game gave me 100 or so hours of enjoyable, challenging (if a little frustrating at first) fun. So as to the price - I'd spend whatever 100 hours of enjoyment is worth to you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 35hrs, deep in second run, I can say that this is great game for FrostPunk lovers.

Interesting story, events that full of misteries you are unveling overtime.

Soundtrack is ideally tailored for overall narrative and space exploration, especially when you use headphones.

Managements aspect is great and overall complexity unraveling at comfortable pace.
Big research tree that gives you oppotunity to close different problems differently.

As was afraid to buy at start because there were lots of people complaining about micromanagement and it is true - there are a lot of aspects you need to manage and change slightly - resources allocation between sectors, space exploration, building best routes for your ships, but you have all the mechanic and contol you need. I found myself playing at normal speed a lot, just checking how my plan is coming along, randomly checking different aspects, making small adjustments that I have not planned along the way and just thinking overseen how the things go.

Defintely very worthy title, thanks developers, if you are reading it, you made my several weeks ;)\t\t\t\t\t
