\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Anything below, while worded as objective truth is only my subjective 'Opinion'. Your experience may vary depending on your preferences and past experience with like games.*

The setting, aesthetic and vibe are all A+. The Mechanics are interesting and you could spent hours designing and redesigning everything from top to bottom; And unfortunately you'll have to.

Unfortunate, because the city planning elements of this game are extraordinarily frustrating, requiring you to have storage for all resources in the sector you build it in, wait for resources to be delivered to buildings, wait for the buildings to be constructed, as well as all the above steps again should you want to remove a building you placed. Both steps again twice if you want to re-build a structure somewhere else.

This means that a simple redesign of a sector is all but impossible. You are better off building a new sector and replacing the old one after you have it's replacement. Long story short, the 'City building' aspect of this game can be extraordinary unsatisfying and frustrating, and since it is 85% of the game, I cannot recommend this game in good faith.

So while I will personally have to persevere through this extremely annoying and time consuming endeavour as I have already been hooked and want to see where it goes, I do not recommend this exercise in frustration on anyone else, especially if you are used to stronger city builder titles (i.e. Any of the impression games).

TL;DR A game with a great aesthetic in both visuals and music, a story more interesting than city builders usually have and a thematic delivery that is excellent. Unfortunately also suffers from deeply faulty city building mechanics, causing at least 50% of your hours played to be excruciatingly frustrating, compounded by a time limit on all your missions.*1


*1 Revision 26/07/2023 The aforementioned time limit is pretty much removed around mission 3. The time limit can also be extended or removed through the difficulty settings.

The rest of the review still applies, after 15 hours of additional play. In fact, both its strong points and it weak points have only intensified.
Enjoying the excellent music, theme and storyline, I do not regret continuing, but have found even more very specific examples as to why this is not a 'good' city builder, and requires any who try it to approach it with extreme patience and a lot of time.\t\t\t\t\t

I'm really not good at city builders, I've just got some awful mind for urban planning, so I really have trouble getting into them, the only city builder that ever really roped me in was Frostpunk due to it's -excellent- story and unique mechanics.

IXION is the second city builder to do that, owing to, again, it's spectacular story and unique mechanics. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tIxion is wonderful and I am happy to recommend it.

Graphic and sound design are both nice and suitable for the game itself. Special praise is given to the soundtrack of the game which is phenomenal.

Ultimately the game is a narrative driven city builder. The mechanics are tight and fun. Towards the end of the game managing resources can become tedious, which is my only complaint.

Otherwise I would certainly recommend Ixion to anyone who likes a good logistics / city builder, especially if you enjoy a solid story.\t\t\t\t\t
