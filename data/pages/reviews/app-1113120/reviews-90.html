\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game. The presentation is really good. The premise is compelling. But, I cannot recommend it for 3 reasons:

1) The random events are horrible. There are fixed events that never change, and you are better off following a guide than playing through them. Then there are random events which are always bad, like something breaks or someone dies. It turns you into a person who just dreads and hates random events, which is not fun.

2) The game still has terrible bugs. At one point, I unlocked a section of the ship, which required moving about 25% of my population there. The population just disappeared. They left the first section, never showed up in the second section, and the population count went down by 25%. Others have noted how they get quest breaking bugs, that makes continuing the game impossible. This is really disrespectful of your time.

3) Lastly, it's not fun from an automation perspective. The game forces you to constantly shut down one building, to power or staff another building. This becomes micromanagement hell. This is not fun, if you are looking for a somewhat self-sustaining, automated system.

I think if just one of these didn't happen, I would have been happy to give a thumbs up, but all three together makes a poor experience.\t\t\t\t\t
