Like the concept that your workers are upset because they lack food only to strike in the food production facilities is mind boggling to me or to strike in the steal mill that is crucial to the maintenance of the station itself.

They could have so very easily solved this weird feeling by switching the power requirements with the steel requirements so you need to constantly collect power resources to fuel the station.

The best way to describe the game is obnoxious.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI watched Spanj do a play through, and at first I thought there would be no way I would enjoy this game. It looked very frustrating, but after watching a few episodes I decided to put it on my wish list, and after playing for 38 hours I can say I really enjoy the game, and I would recommend it.
The tech tree is a bit frustrating, but then most games with tech trees are. I can see picking up some knowledge along the journey, but it seems foolish to begin an epic journey with the basic building blueprints restricted, and all of the advanced blueprints locked until late in the game. Who would get on a ship to travel the stars without knowing how to make and grow food, let alone the fact that is is extremely under powered, and you have to build the solar panels as you travel.
I realize the tech tree is only there to slow the pace of the game, but it just seems silly.\t\t\t\t\t
