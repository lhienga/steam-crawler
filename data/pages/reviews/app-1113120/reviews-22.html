\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDemocrats in Space would have been a better name for this game.

Apparently they rounded up some of the absolute dumbest human beings on the planet and put them on a spaceship to go colonize a planet on another star.

Some of the game design choices in this were just awful. I mean truly awful. Too many things that should be basic parts of the game require you to research them and then place down horrifically oversized buildings to give you a super minimal amount of utility. Want to be able to govern your spacemonkeys? Need a giant building for it, one in EACH sector. Want to be able to move resources from one sector to another faster? Oh thats just one of the biggest buildings in the game and unless you have one in each sector does nothing. Can you train the unproductives on your ship to be something other than neon haired basement dwellers? Nope. Can you space the unproductives out an airlock? Nope. Gotta starve them, only option. Hey maybe we should launch this new spacedrive from out in open space? NAAAAAAAAAAH lets just fire up those engines while practically a stone's throw from the surface of the moon... truly this game is like watching Darwinism of Democrats in action.

Want to be able to collect waste from the buildings on your ship? Apparently you literally need to research feces before you can collect... which requires that other ginormous building i mentioned earlier that has virtually no benefit to your long term play.

Never seen so many bugs in a fully finished game before either. From constant crashing to bugs that prevent you from progressing through the game being literally all over the place. The story requires you to send a ship to deliver goods to a planet at MANY points in the game, if that ship is destroyed en route the game breaks and you can go no further. You cannot send another ship. The people on the planet will sit there waiting for a delivery from a ship that was destroyed, another ship will not take it, you cannot unassign the destroyed ship to assign another, your only option is to restart the chapter... and funny enough after three attempted playthroughs on normal difficulty i went back and cranked everything to basically the easiest settings possible. Suddenly the gas cloud that was never, not once, fatal to any ships in any normal playthrough is instantly deadly to every single ship that simply touches them...

The reviewer who gave this a 10/10 should be banned from reviewing for life. 6/10 at best.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first saw this game, it was advertised as a city builder in space, which is something right up my alley.
For better and for worse, that is not what this game is. A city builder allows, and encourages you to build the best you can, it encourages perfection, here you constantly have a timer on your head that forces you to just move on to the next stage.

You start by building a station so that your people explore another system, but you must first clear out the "debris" scattered all over the sector, this wouldn't be a big issue, if it weren't for the fact that the available space is very limited, so you'll end up building a bunch of stuff, that you will later need to deconstruct and build again in another location, once you clear out enough rubbish (and then you'll have to repeat the process every time you unlock a new sector), I don't really see the point of this process, other than to waste the player's time.

The logistics of the game are a bit of a mess, you have to create resource depots that will store your resources, but are also responsible for transporting them wherever they are needed, but the resources always need to pass through the depot, if you have a bunch of resources in a producer, and a consumer that is across the road, the resources can't just be carried over from one to the other, a group of carriers always need to ferry them (potentially across the sector) all the way to the depot, so that the resources can then be carried again from the depot to the consumer. Each depot shares 5 carriers between the task of retrieving resources and distribute them, this means that if you are deconstructing a building somewhere on the sector, your factories will not have their resources collected until the buildings are deconstructed, if you are at the same time constructing another building, this gets even worse, and often results on your factories shutting down because their output storage is full.

On the topic of factories, for some reason they only store enough raw materials for the current batch being produced, and will not start the next batch until all the required materials are in stock, this means each time a batch is finished, the factory sits idly until a depot provides enough resources. It wouldn't be difficult at all to change each factory to be able to hold enough resources for two batches, that way the depot could be sending the resources for the next batch, while the previous is being processed.

When you send a science team to investigate a location, you then cannot move that ship until the event is resolved, in some cases you get to the event only to find you are not yet in a condition to resolve it, but the game does not allow your ship to move and come back later when you are better prepared, you have to immediately deal with the event, or just let the ship sit idly until you are ready.
For some reason science ships will just twiddle their thumbs while they are waiting for some resources to be delivered to them, they should just be able to collect science while they wait for those resources. Also, for each science event, the game forces you to use a single ship for delivering resources to the site, doesn't matter if you have a dozen ships available, you *have* to wait for a single ship to complete all the necessary round trips.

Also the tech tree is constantly forcing you to research technologies that you probably aren't really interested in, as most technologies require some other(s) to have been researched previously. Which is a bit limiting in a game that forces you to manage each resource (and especially science points) very very carefully.

*Spoilers warning*

The game seems to be constantly trying to invent excuses for punishing you... You are penalised for lingering in the Solar system for too long, but then you are penalised for leaving, next you are penalised for people finding out what happened, etc etc. At one point, one of my ships got stuck in a planet, I sent another ship to help them, recovered the crew, ditched the ship (was hoping the second ship would recover the first, but whatever), shortly after I get slapped with another permanent penalty for "losing the entire crew of the ship", even though they were all aboard the transport ship returning to base.

Then there is space weather, some of which will destroy your ships in a few seconds, can you go around that weather, no, there is no way for you to define way points for your ships to follow, you'll just have to order your ship to go through it, and because in this game scientists are completely brainless, they won't even move ten millimetres to the side to save their own lives.

In my first run, and given the premise of the game, I decided to build the most efficient ship I could, opened only a single sector (other than the one you start at), and micro-managed everything so I would need the absolute minimum crew (and got penalised for having too many unopened cryopods), I was happy building the station my way, and finding my own challenge, but then the game just forces me to double the number of opened sectors, and activating 2000 more cryopods, why, just because, I don't think it even matters if at that time you had opened ten pods, or a thousand, you *have* to open 2000 more.

At many times it honestly feels like the game is going out of its way to punish you, just so it can claim it is hard.

I really want to like this game, it definitely has potential, I really was enjoying the story, exploring each system, trying to build as an efficient ship I could, but too many of its core systems are just frustrating to deal with, it is as if the game developer has a way for the game to be played correctly, if that happens to be the way you like to play it, this game will likely appeal to you, if not, you (like me) will likely just end up being very frustrated.\t\t\t\t\t
