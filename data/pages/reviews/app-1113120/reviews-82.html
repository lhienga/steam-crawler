10/10

This game has an amazing system where you aren't punished for a build. Yes you may have to start over, yes you WILL have to rebuild, but you get all your resources that you spent back.

Story is confusing but if you've ever played a builder game this one is mid tier, 60 hours maybe. Little replay value compared to DSP/satisfactory/etc.

Got it on sale and have nothing bad to say expect they are using Unity ECS(Entity Component System) and it really doesn't need it. It causes some major issues with will cause you to reload over and over again because something will just stop working. Resent patches have made it nearly bug free however.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt a loose end and not wanting to play the rest of my back catalogue I did what any good PC game does and bought a new game. This one however I have put 25 hours in across Tuesday and Wednesday (This year I have so far gamed for *checks notes* 25 hours!).

The reviews seemed mixed but the premise appealed and I'm glad I gave the game a shot. I'm playing it VERY slowly but on the default difficulty. I came into the game having watched all of 5 minutes of a Lets Play. I can kind of understand why some may have complained about difficulty, but if you are used to any normal resource/people management type games then this really should be fine for you.

I'm only on Chapter 3 and had to reload once due to an event triggering a storm cloud, but apart from that I've been able to plod along at my own pace. Very enjoyable game and I've been saddened that I had to stop and go back to adulting during the day, otherwise I'd be 50 hours deep by now, 4 days in.

Looking forward to getting to the end of the story and replaying now that I know WTF I'm doing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game because I like city/colony-builders and I read in a lot of reviews that the game is challenging. I'm playing on standard difficulty, which is the original one. (There's also an easier and a harder one since the last update, plus custom diffculty settings.)
The game starts out nice and easy, but constantly ramps up from there. At the point I'm at (chapter 2), it's not that hard for me yet, but I can already see how it will become a challenge, especially towards the end. So far, I'm really enjoying it and can't wait to see what else there is to discover!

The story is interesting, I like the mechanics and that you can't just build anything without thinking about what you're doing. Sometimes it's like a puzzle, trying to fit as many buildings as possible into a sector (plus balance the needed energy and workers). After all, space is limited in a space station and the buildings also have very different sizes, so you can't really get away with a standard grid pattern :D
I can already see me playing the whole story again, now that I know how things work and can also plan ahead better, instead of constantly having to move buildings and reorganize the limited space you have.
Next to the usual building and resource management, you also have to make sure that your people are well cared for and society is stable, or you have to deal with the consequences.

I also like that there's no mandatory hand-holding tutorial. The first quests feel like an introduction that eases you into the game and there are additional help articles you can look at or not (any time after they first appeared). The UI isn't hard to understand, you can just look at everything in the beginning and get a good picture of what and how to do it. I think the learning curve is great, you first deal with a subset of mechanics and as the story progresses, one by one more of them are introduced and the difficulty is raised.\t\t\t\t\t
