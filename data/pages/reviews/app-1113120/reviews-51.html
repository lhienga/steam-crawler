\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBottom Line: A great space city building game with a good story line. More difficult than average.

Most Enjoyable:
1. The feeling of exploring space, while trying to save the remnants of humanity.
2. Puzzling out the resource management, correct upgrades, and construction sequencing in order to capitalize on small margins of success.
3. Balancing Time, Happiness, Power, Population, and Resources. The game leaves very few exploits. You must keep moving (along the correct sequence of paths) or you will find yourself stuck in a downward spiral.

Least Enjoyable:
1. Building descriptions and concepts are poorly described, so that you don't fully understand the mechanics until AFTER you attempt something. Leads to a fair amount trial/error.
2. Micro managing the power grid (mostly by continuously turning stockpiles on/off)
3. The game will often require you to build seemingly useless buildings to continue the story line. The problem is, you can't forecast what will be required, so you end up having to undo your delicately balanced layout to build story line buildings (often very haphazardly).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is so close to being an absolute masterpiece, but there's just a few little things that keep it from being one. But it's an easy recommend if you like story-focused city/colony-builders and/or sci-fi settings.

Positives:
- 11/10 sound design
- very solid and well-designed gameplay mechanics
- amazing story*
- beautiful
- general feel of the game

Negatives:
- The difficulty feels off and badly communicated. The normal difficulty feels extremely hard and restarting isn't really fun because it's the exact same things happening every time. This is generally not a problem but I feel like it could be better communicated how difficult the game is. For everyone that is looking for a non-stressful but still challenging experience I 100% recommend the easy difficulty. It's what I would call normal difficulty in most other games.
- *The story is amazing in it's idea at least. I would easily watch a series or read a book with the same premise as IXION. The execution also starts off extremely well and while the story keeps being interesting throughout the game, in the end I felt a little disappointed. Not because the ending is bad but because many questions are left unanswered.

It's a very good game, 9/10 overall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I started playing this game yesterday, and now it is today. All my play time except 30 minutes was a single sitting where I beat the story in one go. I don't normally do that with video games, the only other ones I can think of that have grabbed me like this are Subnautica and Dyson Sphere Program. I think this game is incredible and very thematic. The "Frostpunk in Space" comparison is obvious, but this game is more than just a clone. The developers obviously put heart into it. The tech tree descriptions are great and there are tons of little nods to other classic science fiction throughout the game. Also the last couple levels did stuff I didn't even know a factory game could do. It was an incredible down to the wire finish. As far as replay value I definitely think there are enough random events that things will be fresh (and for a second playthrough you can always just work through slowly reloading a lot to see what all the choices do). Anyway, my brain is way too fried to give much more of a competent review right now. I am just disappointed that this game got such a mixed release, because it really is a great experience and a unique world. Anyway, like the dude says "Farewell Administrator. For the few who stand in the light, and the many who dwell in the dark, you carry the fate of us all." I'm going to bed.\t\t\t\t\t
