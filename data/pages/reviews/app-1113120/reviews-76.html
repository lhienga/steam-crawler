\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun game, but gets harder and harder as you go on even on Default setting. It starts out with pretty easy resource management, but gets increasingly harder as resources take longer to get and your people start to get uneasy about being in a system too long, etc. You will have to find a balance between energy usage, resource usage, haul integrity, and keeping your people happy via food, housing, and not to overwork them. It can get frustrating really quickly if you decide to neglect something for a bit and realize that you don't have enough resources or enough time to repair a bad decision you made. Overall, the game is fun. And I find myself thinking about what I could have done differently when I'm not playing the game.

The only things I would like to see is ability, at a glance, to see my workers to population to housing numbers for each sector without having to go into the personnel management screen. Maybe there is a way to see this at a glance, I just haven't figured it out yet.

Overall, the game is SUPPOSE to be hard to complete and its a game that will either frustrate people wanting to continue to the story but can't get past a certain chapter, or keep bringing you back for the challenge to get the perfect resource management setup.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere we go, my first review of a game. Buckle up!

With ~120 hrs under my belt i managed to complete 2 whole runs where i got (almost) all achievements and anomalies, missing only the fire and overwork ones. One was shortly after it's release, the other one in the last 5 days on the new "hard" difficulty.

tldr: Game is good. Bit pricy for my greedy, moneyless soul, but good. Buy with a discount and you will get your moneys worth of fun. It also has a monstrous amount of potential if they keep adding more content to it.

After reading through the comments here i have to say that i really don't understand most of the negative ones. The game is what it is: a Space-Story-Survival-Basebuilder. It is hard (if you want it to be), you follow a scripted story and the challenge is to survive while advancing said story. Getting raised by Star Trek and Sci-Fi-Novels, i enjoyed this unknown (sadly not so vast) endlessness of cryptic space and mistery. As long as you manage to keep calm in your ever-exploding Donut of an Arc, you can explore, question your understanding of human destiny and repeatedly smash that load button, while another one of your science Ships managed to somehow explode on another planet (or accept your fate, build a new one and repeat at the next anomaly)

Pros:
+ I love the music! Despite the fact that you get an achievement when you turn all sounds of, the plain music induces a feeling of (happy) fear, being all alone in this unexplored nothingness. It has subtle drops here and there where you can feel your stomach sink into doubt and disbelief, while simultaniously lifing your spirit to explore the next rotating rock in the system. Subjectively speaking, it really hit the mark.
+ Min-Maxing is working but not a must. In my first playthrough i played like a suicidal monkey and stil managed to somehow reach the end. On the new difficutly i explored slower and acutally used the spongy thing in my skull. I think i had 12 dead crewman at the end and not a single overworked sector the whole game. If you take the time to understand the game it will be kind to you.
+ The UI is working. I know, strange positive point, but here me out: I explained and showed the game to my dear, old mother - a being, which is further away from gaming or a PC then any other child of the early 60s. Net gain or lose of Steel? Hover over there. Enough Food? Right next to it. What about the workers? Down here. Can you tell that strange yellow ship to pick up more Iron? it's the red one, but only 3 clicks aanndd done. Take your time to learn where you need to hover (hint: it's the big picture of the ressource you want to look at) and you have all the info you need. Sadly, not so many games manage that nowaday.
+ You have a save haven at the start. I repeat: Save. Haven. You can sit around in the prologue as long as you want to learn the game. After that: Good luck.
+ The game is very rewarding when you theme your sectors. If you manage to specialise a sector you can really start producing "the goods". Sadly not every specialisation is as good as the others, but it's enough.
+ There are consequences. It is a survival after all and it keeps true to that promise. ♥♥♥♥ up and you are better ready to reload to a save 1-2 hours ago. The good thing is, that those mistakes are mostly very correctable - you don't have to start from the start again.
+ Massive potential. Imaging a "free universe gamemode" beside the story, where your engine drops you of in random generated systems with different storymissions or hazards. Survival against all odds... i hope that this is possible with this game.
+ ... .... .... *Sigh*. There is a wiki for it. If you don't want to explore the mysteries of space, you can just look up the right answer... (totally not judging you for it).
+ There is a lot of heartblood in IXION. The Devs tried to make a wholesome (yet deadly) game, thank you!

Cons:
- it's... short. My first playthrough was around 50 hours, my second around 40, while the rest of the time was me staring at the menu/pause screen and thinking about layouts. You have 2 endings and many hidden mysteries, so ""some"" good reasons to replay it. I could have done the second ending by also reloading a save on the chapter before, so yeah... i hope for more in the future.
- The story is kinda... strange. I know: "contradicting myself", hear me out: i like the way the story is and what homages it is hinting at but the games uses a lot of strange "pseudo-physics-words" that my after-work-brain simply refuses to understand. Aha.. "this-particle" .. "that-protocol", etc. If you have no problems with that, just ignore this point.
- stil randomly buggy. In those recent 40 gameplay hours, i had 3! (i know, heretical!) instances, where the ressources weren't transported to another sector. I fixed it by un- and reassining the storage. Next.
- I want a button that does only one thing: "Assigne max 1. transport per astroid". I hated having to watch 3 transports beelining for the same "20 Fe" drop, only to then return and waddle to the next.
- In the cutscenes when you arrive at new planets, the ships is always rushing right at it and then just.. stops. That's not how it works! (Once again.. if you have no problems with that, just ignore it^^')

In the end i will give IXION a solid 8,5/10 (Frostpunk was a 8/10 for me), mostly because of the missing replayability. It will now retire back into the vault of "want to play again, but need a reason for that - games". Hopefully there will be more on the horizon, it just has so much potential!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinite yes from me. It is Frostpunk in space like stated by many. A bit more micromanagement involved with transferring resources and manpower between sectors in comparison to Frostpunk. The story is good with all the humor and scifi elements. Graphics are very pleasant to look at.

Replayability comes from learning the game mechanics and optimizing the run once you get the jist on everything. As far as I can tell, there is no random elements to the game. Each choice and their outcome as well as locations are the same on each playthrough.

The game offers fair amount of challenge but I never felt that it was impossible to recover from any mistake. Some resources are finite, seems that there are enough to go around still. Dismantling a building yields all the resources you put into it, and this you will be doing quite a lot... Mainly by unlocking new tech and correcting one's mistakes.

I think I'm about half way through the game at the time writing this. And this is my second stab at trying to beat the game. So far I'm having a lot of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
