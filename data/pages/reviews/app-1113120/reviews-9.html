\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI actually have mix feeling about this game.

IT start off really nice, with the premises of making a mobile space city.
On top of that it has a very interesting story oriented mechanics, to break up the building faces.
Unfortunately on the long run there are several thing that are off balance and need reviewing.

First, to keep surviving you need a constant repair of the ship, which use finite resources. On top of that, over time your people will randomly unhappy, making each stage of the game a run against time rather than peaceful city builder.
Couple that with the fact that the more building you unlock, the more you'll find yourself short of space. Even though the penalty of demolish and reorganise is minimum in term of resources, it takes time, which is the only thing you don't have.

and then there are the bugs and bottleneck. I was stuck on a stage for ages, cause one ship was stuck unloading. Had to demolish the ship and the dock to get it forward. by the time I realised it was not happening on its own, the population was basically insurrection.

Then, there is the big big problem of the logistic. At the beginning of the game you have tutorial on the multi sector management. The fact that sectors also use bonuses for specialisation, it leads to think that you'll have one sector for each discipline (food production, forgery, etc etc etc). the reality is that the more people you have (and for story purpose, you need to keep increasing it) the easier is the logistic to bugs out, leaving people starving, that lead to a cascade of event from which is hard to recover. By the time I reached the last stage of the game, I had to demolish half of the ship and distribute at least some food production in each sector, to make sure people were fed.
I also had to disable any other trade between sectors, making very hard to build almost anything at that point.

The fact that logistic works, until it get too much for the simulation to handle, sound to me like a bug, that render the game barely playable. I think until that is solved, I'll be playing something else.
And... if it is NOT a bug, but a game mechanic, then it need better clarification on the tutorial / info menu.

I appreciate the fact that difficulty can be heavily customised. I turned off the fact that repairing the ship used finite resources, and that allowed me to finish the stage where my loading ship was stuck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I had to pick one word to describe Ixion, it'd be "annoying."

I saw Ixion ages ago and was intrigued because of the theme, but avoided it because of the middling reviews. I came back when a friend started playing it and read the reviews again. Most of the negative ones I saw seemed to be complaining about the game being too hard, while the positive ones seemed to be complimenting it on being a Frostpunk-like experience. Great! I love Frostpunk! Maybe the folks had bad expectations due to marketing, or maybe they just got lost? Either way, not relevant to me.

Here I am 11 hours later. First off, about the difficulty, Ixion is, as far as I can tell, not difficult--at least on the recommended base difficulty. At no point have I felt like I was under any real threat of losing the game. So the other stuff in my review isn't about finding the game too hard. Nor is it about finding the game too easy, because I'm happy to play chill city builders too.

As I mentioned above, it's just annoying.

Frostpunk and Against the Storm are two city builders I really love because they do a good job of presenting you with a hopeless-feeling scenario and asking you to solve it. In Frostpunk temperature drops you aren't prepared for can feel brutal. When you go back with a better plan and easily slide through them, it feels great. You solved the problem and the game rewarded you for it.

Ixion is nothing like that. It wants you to think you're playing Frostpunk, but really you're just playing the illusion of Frostpunk. You're told, for example, that overworking people is problematic because it can lead to accidents. Great, I'm going to play it safe and not overwork anyone! Too bad, the accidents constantly happen anyways. Things blow up, people die. And the thing is? None of it matters. They don't make the game harder. They don't really achieve anything except forcing you to go click the "repair" button. They're just annoying. They're the game trying to pretend it's hard and desperate but not actually putting any work into the game mechanics and instead just blowing things up on a random timer. Oh, and there's a hidden tech which clicks the repair button for you. Tech is pretty expensive, at least as far in the game as I am, so the big payoff of this explosion thing is apparently "you can pay to make it less annoying."

Speaking of stuff blowing up, your storage bays can also blow up, even if nobody is using them. But they don't blow up if you power them down, so there's this little voice in your head telling you to micromanage your storage bays and turn them off whenever they aren't active. Sure, you know it doesn't really matter, but things blowing up is annoying and seems bad. But micromanaging them is also annoying. I ended up not micromanaging them, but I'm sure many people do.

The events are equally horrible. Your scientists go out and land on Mars, ready to make tough choices. The facility is collapsing under the weight of the sands, and you only have a bit of time. What do you want to do? Loot the storage area? Check the science labs for their research data? Try to grab everything despite the danger? Some of these feel ok, like being punished for trying to grab everything. Some of them don't. You were interested in science, so you picked the lab? Too bad, there was actually _more_ science in the storage area (completely untelegraphed), and there were some other resources too. Hope you checked the wiki before making your choice! You want to establish a colony over on Somewhere Prime? Great, that'll be six four-day round trips of resources. No, you can't assign more than one cargo ship. No, your science ship can't leave for the literal weeks they'll be waiting on resources. Is this an issue? Not really, because there's no time pressure. But you bet it's annoying!

The game cares a ton about building placement in ways that are pretty obtuse, but moving buildings around is a nightmare. The logistics system is so inefficient that dismantling and rebuilding a single building in your industrial sector can completely grind everything to a halt for minutes. This isn't particularly a problem--remember that lack of time pressure--it's just boring and annoying. You unlocked a cool new building but didn't plan out your endgame layout on a spreadsheet before figuring out where to place it? Sucks to be you! Your punishment is to sit there on x3 speed for 5 minutes waiting for the bots to sort everything out! You open a new sector and the place you want to place your free workshop is covered by a random bit of debris? Too bad, if you dismantle it and rebuild it once you've cleared the space, the new one isn't free. Do the 20 alloys you lost matter? Nope, not really. Is it annoying? You bet it is!

At one point hundreds of my workers went on strike, somehow causing about 100 injuries as well (completely unexplained, there were no explosions for a change). Injuries take weeks to heal and need a lot of infirmary space. A helpful strike tutorial popped up to explain why this had happened. Strikes, apparently, are caused by low stability. So I checked my stability: it was medium, and the only reason it wasn't high is because of the strike. Was this a bug? Was this just another example of the game being annoying to try to pretend things are more desperate than they actually are? Who knows, just another day in Ixion.

Practically every aspect of the game is like this. They have some half-baked, poorly explained system that doesn't really work but then they wanted to make it frostpunk-y, so they glue on some explosions and call it a day. There's no evidence that they really thought about how to tie their theme in with their game mechanics, they just figure that if they _tell_ you the situation is desperate then it'll feel like you're playing a desperate "survive against the odds" city builder. Looking at the positive reviews, this clearly works for some people. I'm not one of them.

I might end up finishing it anyways just because I have some time to kill and like the theme, but unless you really really really want a space station city builder then I'd say this one is a skip.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you loved Frostpunk you're going to probably love this as well.

Ixion is basically Frostpunk in space with a bigger map (so you have more area to build), more story but less focus on the moral implications of your actions. Its a really fun game in my opinion for those who love the city building/colony sim genre and also want a little bit of a story line to go with it. In my opinion its a great and innovative addition to the City Building and Colony Sim list of games.

However, despite all its caveats, Ixion is a game with a lot of annoyances. The game often somewhat buggy and laggy when you tab in and out. Whats also annoying is that despite creating the game in such a large map and area, everything is micromanaged, which makes it really annoying to control certain parts of the map. For example, you want to stop ships from going to certain resource deposits, you need to click and toggle off each individual resource deposit manually, which is frankly really annoying. The game is also much less forgiving than Frostpunk, meaning that you're likely going to snowball into a restart really quickly if you don't know what you're doing.

That being said, if you can ignore the issues above, Ixion is a great city builder that has an amazing story line, captivating exploration and classic colony sim/city builder premises that will entertain you for around 30 hours.\t\t\t\t\t
