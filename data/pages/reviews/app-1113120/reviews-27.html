\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a game. The music, the atmosphere, the S P A C E. So much of this game was just a treat to play. I loved the visuals, the mechanics of exploration and especially the soundtrack. Seeing amazing sights such as the chapter 2 setting and especially the jaw dropping chapter 3 setting made the game that much more memorable. The world building done for each location scattering lore for those willing to read it got me very invested in the world as a whole however that is not to say that I am completely in love with every aspect of the game. It can be a bit frustrating and confusing at times, especially when certain mechanics seem to work against you. Resources, especially your population can lead to frustrating events where you are struggling to acquire workers with more regular citizens being mouths to feed. The story is a bit lacking despite the rich and enthralling world. It is certainly a serviceable story line but it certainly is not the centerpiece of the campaign. The decision system feels somewhat superfluous as they don't necessarily contribute to future events, they are really just a resource expenditure or bonus. Research can be pretty inconsistent as well since you tend to get large science bonuses and end up stuck afterwards and the finale leaves something to be desired with a victory lap type of objective that doesn't really feel all that impact. Overall I would rate the game a solid 8/10 for its stellar world building, soundtrack and overall game play loop and mechanics. It nails the feeling of being truly alone in your journey and the set pieces and secrets scattered throughout are a joy to discover but some frustrating mechanics and a less than stellar story and ending end up taking away some of the excitement of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
