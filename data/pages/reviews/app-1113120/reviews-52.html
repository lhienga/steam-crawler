\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrostpunk in space

I really liked Frostpunk, and for me, this game is better.

Make sure you're okay with dying to something you probably couldn't see coming and restarting the chapter with the new knowledge you need to complete the challenge. That will probably happen your first play through.

There's a few chapters where you do feel rushed, but about half way through you get to a chapter where you can just chill for a bit, do some city building, build up your supplies and progress the tech tree. It was a welcome breather for playing through my first time. I'm sure you could rush it if you're really experienced with this game or city builders in general.

I'm already thinking about how I'd improve the layout of my starting sectors for my next play through. In terms of repeatability I think you'd probably want to do it a few times, but it would drop off once you've worked everything out. With the structured story you'll know whats coming up and how to prepare for it. I think even the mini events aren't random, so you could figure out which ones to do and which to avoid. I've only played on the default difficulty, so maybe if you pick a harder version things become random?

Overall I'm really enjoying this game and would say if you liked Frostpunk then just buy this, even at full price its worth it and has enough hours of gameplay to be good value for money.

Edit now that I've completed the game:
I've read through some other peoples reviews and can absolutely see where they're coming from. Some of the mission requirements that get thrown at you seem a bit unreasonable to be able to plan for. I just completed the last chapter by the skin of my teeth and this was by prebuilding/stocking materials needed and planning my layout needed before the mission requirements came up. It would be very unlikely that as a first time you'd be able to adapt on the fly to finish the game.

Now that I've completed it I can say that I did still really enjoy it. If you're looking for more of a city builder then this might not be for you, it plays more like an PvE RTS where you want to have the bare minimum facilities and a tech tree planned out. Those looking to build and manage a space station will likely feel rushed through each chapter as the story demands to be completed and the resource clock is always ticking down,

I've just finished my first play through and I'm thinking of starting a second go immediately after to see how I can improve. Not sure it will have much replayablity after that. I hope there is an expansion or second one coming out at some point that maybe has a bit more of an open sandbox feel to it. There's some really cool stuff in the tech tree, but you barely get to use any of it before the game is over\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA flawed masterpiece.

The world, the art, the ideas, are wonderful, intriguing, and will make you crave to learn more about these characters and places. The simulation is fun and engaging, if not particularly realistic, and everything is so damn beautiful. And I'm in love with the music, as I am any time Guillaume David composes anything.

That quality is bogged down by 2 issues:
1 - Big gaps in QoL features, indeed many of the challenges you end up facing are around making the game's logistics work as a sane person would want them to work (resource priorisation algorithms have a tendency to ignore deadly issues to address minor ones).
2 - The way the game's quality degrades as you approach the end in what are clear signs of an overambitious scope that was later cut. Staff that worked on the game admitted as much in Reddit.
The mix ends up really degrading the experience.

As it stands, this is a weak recommend from me. If you feel like braving its defects, this game is amazing and could easily be a cult classic. But you will have to look past its flaws to see that.

The price feels right for what it is\t\t\t\t\t

Ixion takes a unique approach to space building and survival.
Some of the uniqueness is constantly replanning what you build in which sectors due to sizing limitations; some call it micro-management, I call it evolution. What city doesn't have to change over time?!

This is not frost punk, but it has elements of it. It certainly could lean in deeper to the emotion; it doesn't create any connections to the character it focuses on the situation.
The tech tree is pretty weak.

I enjoy the exploring to find resources. You do have to micro-manage around storms otherwise you will lose mining ships.

Ixion could be great if it took advantage of the events, address some of the above, and fix a few annoying bugs.\t\t\t\t\t
