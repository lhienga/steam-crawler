\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIXION is the bastard child of Frostpunk and the 4X genre. Which honestly sounds like a great deal for anyone familiar with Frostpunk or Endless Space etc. It is sadly at this time more frustration than fun.

It tries to marry the familiar tight-rope act of balancing "Hope and Discontent" with constant and increasing consumption. However, they missed one of the fundamental lessons of Frostpunk: "It's not arbitrary".

This is probably one of the most fundamental faults of the entire game as of now. Everything is or seems arbitrary.

Accidents/Fires/Explosions - Meant to partially simulate the "event" system of Frostpunk instead become repeated punishments without recourse. You can't mitigate or prevent these in any way.

"Events" - Trigger with no inherent logic. For example the event for permanent -10% trust due to "not listening to their needs" triggers regardless of "happiness" and "trust" levels. In my most current save, both were maxed.

Research expeditions - Whatever choice you make is irrelevant and the effect, gain or loss, is completely arbitrary. This is obviously not true, but the game does nothing to inform, guide or caution a player on what each choice might mean, or involve or result in. You simply roll the dice. every. time. There are even interesting and I'd argue important lore implications and mini "missions" that are utterly missable and never foreshadowed or hinted at in several locations. Every location is a meaningless list of buttons with no relevant information available to the player (with one exception so far, out of 16 locations). This doesn't just invalidate the players agency, it invalidate the whole mechanic. Why even present a choice when the intentional lack of information makes the act irrelevant.

Hull degradation - This is IXIONS version of the worsening temperature logic from Frostpunk. However it sets itself up so poorly and the information about the ship and the story you are served keep you in the dark about it's functionality and importance and even the mechanisms for hours until you have by force of experimentation figured out the ACTUAL way the system works by yourself. The game also flat out lies to you several times and omits critical information for decision-making about this central mechanic.


Finally, gameplay system legibility is incredibly confused.
The various systems that govern "trust" and "happiness" also interact with at least 3 more subsystems that are explained and displayed separately and with different units of value. Making the quick estimation of your current "state" incredibly annoying and unclear. The "Section" system exacerbates this even further due to some systems only displaying per section when others have a full station indicator. Other "resources" can have per section breakdowns integrated into the tooltip when hovering over the total value, but as "sentiment" values are a product of sever different types of values this doesn't work.

Research and Tech tree progression seems at first glance to make sense, the expanding rings take over for the "levels" of Frostpunks research tree, but then again the arbitrary hammer comes whacking. "Sub" research on already researched technology is suddenly a critical factor once you travel beyond the first "chapter". But how to unlock the ones you need is never explained, neither is "tech tiers" explained.
Unless you happened to click on an old tech you had already researched you wouldn't even know about "sub" research at all.

I could keep writing, but honesly, these are the biggest hurdles right now. If this is fixed the game as it is would be great fun. The flaws this game has can be fixed via better writing and UI tweaks. Meaning, the "game" it self is already there, the ui, the graphics, the modeling, the setting and moderately entertaining story is all great.

It just doesn't feel great right now.

No one plays Colony/4X games to be punked by "gotcha" mechanics and literal missing information on mechanics and game functions. Fix that and you're pretty much there.\t\t\t\t\t
