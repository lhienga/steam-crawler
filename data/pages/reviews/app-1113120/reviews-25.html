\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not what I thought it was. I did not read the description. It looked like I had to manage a space station and I hoped there would be aliens and weird encounters that would affect the population. However, when I started the game, I was greeted with a campaign option; immediately clicked it. The visuals are amazing, game play is fun, and the events are also very interesting. I am sad this doesn't have a endless mode or something. I didn't need/ get to use all the "endgame" buildings like the reactor, observatory or mech arena. It is a fun game, definitely worth a play.

There were a few cons I have noticed. There are still some glitches, some events don't make sense, and the story could have so much more to it. While playing, I have noticed a glitch where some resources don't get refunded. Also if you deconstruct a a building after researching a cheaper blueprint, you don't get the original materials, just a nit-pick. It's also a weird choice that water isn't shared between sectors. I loved the planetary events, but the events at the end contradicted the game play in the beginning. If you spent more time and resources exploring a planet, usually good events resulted... not at the end of the game though. As we explored the wrecks of battleships and future spaceships, we rode a deteriorating obsolete ship. Why didn't we try to retrofit or pick up parts from the advanced wrecks? My biggest irritation in the game was that if we stayed too long, people got depressed. I stayed in the Earth solar system too long and my people got sad, saying they don't want to look at a destroyed earth anymore. So, I left. They got even more sad saying they cant believe they are leaving their home system. I understand its a game mechanic to keep the game moving, but the people on the station must also know resources are needed for them to live. It's supposed to be filled with the worlds smarted people, people who are supposed to be cool and clear headed in the face of danger..

Its still a good game though, The glitches as not game-breaking, the events are still interesting, and the story line is still enjoyable. I hope to see an "endless" mode in the future. There's still a lot of potential for this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWARNING. This game is HARD.
Not quite Dark Souls level of hard, but when you're talking about economic strategy, you won't feel like you lack skill, you'll feel like you're being gaslit.

It's frustrating when your people get unruly about staying around a dead Earth too long and then get depressed because you left it. Honestly, when faced with disaster, people do be like that sometimes. The situation is terrifying and leading terrified people is a thankless crapshoot.

Remember that space wants to kill you. It really, really wants to kill you and I kinda felt like a constantly disintegrating hull was going easy on the players because alloy production is very easy to set up, just difficult to manage.

This strategy game isn't about skill, it is about patience, observation, compromise, and future planning on top of that for a successful run.

That being said, the challenge has been a highly rewarding experience for me. If you go in expecting to win your first go, you will be humbled. If you go in expecting to grow, you will grow.

Only negative: Lack or replayability, once you beat it on hard there's nowhere to go from there\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like Frostpunk but want a scifi experience?
I got you fam.

I like this game, the mechanics are solid and the stakes are good.
It's also just one campaign which makes the experience a bit shorter than I'd hoped. I get that the premise of this game and it's story don't really leave room for "scenario's" but it still makes me sad to see that I have "only" clocked in 22 hours and will probably never pick it up again because there really isn't any replay value.

There is one campaign, one story, one way of playing it. And now that I know how the story will play out and what hurdles I'll face the game won't suprise me anymore.

So don't expect a long, big citybuilder and economy sim. It's a solid and short experience. I liked it and I'd wish there was more of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIXION is a remarkable achievement in the genre of space management and survival games, blending intricate strategy with a captivating narrative. Set against the backdrop of a dwindling Earth, the game tasks players with the monumental responsibility of guiding the Tiqqun space station, humanity's last hope, through the uncharted cosmos.

Key Highlights:

Innovative Space Management Mechanics: The core gameplay revolves around expertly managing resources and infrastructure aboard the Tiqqun. Players must balance the needs of their inhabitants with the limitations of space and resources, creating a compelling and often challenging experience. The level of detail in station management is both impressive and immersive.

Dynamic Storytelling: IXION is not just about survival; it's a narrative-driven journey. The game's story unfolds through critical decisions and events, weaving a tale that is both grand in scope and deeply personal. The narrative choices impact the gameplay significantly, offering a rich, branching story that keeps players engaged.

Stunning Visuals and Sound Design: The game's aesthetic is a standout feature. The visual design of the space station and the celestial environments is breathtaking, complemented by a soundtrack that perfectly captures the wonder and isolation of space exploration.

Complex Risk and Reward System: IXION introduces a unique twist with its 'Doomsday Clock' mechanic, a constant reminder of the perilous journey. Balancing risk and reward becomes a pivotal part of the gameplay, as players must make tough decisions that could either ensure survival or bring disaster.

Robust Research and Development: The game's R&D aspect allows players to unlock new technologies and upgrades, adding depth to the gameplay. This system encourages experimentation and strategic planning, offering various paths to success.

Engaging Survival Challenges: From managing dwindling resources to facing unexpected space anomalies, IXION keeps players on their toes. The survival elements are well-crafted, demanding strategic thinking and quick decision-making.

Conclusion:

IXION is more than a game; it's an experience. Its blend of intricate space management, compelling storytelling, and stunning visuals make it a must-play for fans of the genre. The game challenges players not just to survive but to thrive in the vastness of space, offering a journey that is as perilous as it is exhilarating. Whether you're a seasoned strategist or a newcomer to space simulation games, IXION promises an adventure that's both challenging and deeply rewarding.\t\t\t\t\t
