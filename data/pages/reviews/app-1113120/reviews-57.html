Unfortunately, I've come to the sad realization that, after struggling every step of the way to maintain sanity, I have not collected enough cryopods to achieve self sustainability. I've spent far too much iron on maintenance for the last thousand cycles and there is not enough iron left in the map of Chapter 3 to make it out. This game will chew you up and spit you out if you don't understand the limiting factors on progression early on. You will spend many tens of hours struggling to reach the next nut, only for you to realize very late in the game that you cannot win and you've strung yourself on for so long to be disappointed in this reality. Perhaps don't make your first run on the highest difficulty, but for anyone who struggles with repeatability, you may find easier levels too easy to provoke any sense of challenge. Don't get me wrong, I like the game a lot, enough to spend many hours playing and learning how to play it, I'm just very disappointed to learn that I've wasted many hours to not be able to make it to the end. While I embrace failure, It seems cruel to endure 20+ hours of trying so hard to continue to the next chapter to make it to a point where you have no chance to continue and are forced to start over. My advise is to understand how iron and cryopods are finite resources that require certain milestones to be met for you to be able to finish.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not one to generally write reviews, but I love this game.

SUMMARY:
I get everything I want from a city builder with a decent story that is challenging but not too difficult with customizable difficulty settings. You're constantly having to manage resources and space, researching upgrades only to find that it may require more space and/or resources than you were anticipating, forcing you to think ahead constantly. There are a couple different endings, but coming from someone who loves decision-heavy games, it didn't feel like either choice was that big of a deal, but was nice to have decisions nonetheless.

GRAPHICS:
Graphically, I love the game. It's truly beautiful and there are some cinematics in the game that they clearly put a LOT of time into creating - others, however takes on a completely different art style, which almost feels like laziness compared to how the others look.

DIFFICULTY/GAMEPLAY:
The game certainly becomes more difficult as it progresses, but I was never ripping my hair out trying to progress. However, the game difficulty scales tremendously based on the settings you select and can certainly be as easy or hardcore as you want it to be. Lots and lots of options in the custom difficulty settings. The gameplay feels very well-balanced and the nuances, as well as the cause-and-effect relationship mechanics feels super solid.

SOUNDTRACK:
1000/10. Enough said. Absolutely breathtaking.

PLAYTIME:
I completed my playthrough in 23 hours. You can certainly race through it faster or really get into the weeds with things taking much longer. If you play at more extreme difficulties, expect the game to take much, much longer, as you will need more and more resources to accomplish your mission.

STORY:
MAIN MISSION: 6/10. It's got great roots and keeps you captivated, but I finished the game with more questions than answers. The main story will have you questioning what you can and can't trust towards the end, but that's all I will say for now.
SIDE MISSIONS: It's hard to rate. If you're someone that loves reading lots of content like in Stellaris, you'd love the side stories that happen on various planets. There's lots of lore and information to read. However, if you're more interested in getting the main mission completed or you dislike reading loads of information, you'll probably only care about gathering your reseach data and not bothering to read much.

TLDR: I'd give the game a solid 8/10. Highly recommend to all the city-builder fans out there who also enjoy some heavy logistical thinking. My best way of putting it: The logistics of Rimworld with the story content of Stellaris that looks like Homeworld if it were a city builder which also has one of the most beautiful soundtracks of all time (same composer of Warhammer 40K's "Mechanicus").\t\t\t\t\t

Much like Frostpunk, Ixion is a worthy addition to the little known sub-genre: survival city builder. You will build habitats, factories, agriculture, and resource gathering facilities to support your crew while exploring several solar systems.

My only gripes with this game are:
(1) Moving resources between sectors can be a bit clunky and can sometimes be (frustratingly) unreliable.
(2) There are "quests" that your science ship(s) will have the opportunity to go on where you'll be given a handful of (possibly) branching options for how the crew will explore the location. Outcomes are not always well telegraphed beforehand and seem to be much more trial and error than based on planning. As an example, just because a quest requires more time/resources doesn't automatically mean that it will have the best outcome.
(3) (Minor Spoilers) There is an environmental hazard in chapter 3 that is affected by how many quests you complete and is best avoided by your smaller ships; this is not well explained at the beginning of the mission. I had, falsely, believed that movement of the hazard was time based and not quest based which almost ended my run at that point (End Spoiler).

Aside from these issues I had with the game, Ixion is absolutely worth your time if you want to play a city builder with challenge in space (or perhaps at a more casual pace with the recent difficulty settings update).\t\t\t\t\t
