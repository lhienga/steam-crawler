\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to love this game, yet it won't let me.

Pros:
- interesting story and setup
- atmospheric soundtrack

Cons:
- utterly utterly failed game design that makes interaction with the game infuriating and eventually led me to churn
- The logistic sim is illogical and can get stuck upon itself, with you being unable to fix it as all its moving parts block itself
- The energy management is boring and comes with silly penalties to unhappiness
- In the end, unhappines and hull don't really matter anyway
- The resource management is a chore and the sector resource transfer system cumbersome and unecessary
- The geospatial gameplay is made more punishing by lack of meaningful UI to predict quality placement
- The game's research is completely open to you going after the wrong things and hence eff yourself for no other reason than limiting research by points
- The systems behind happiness, population, cryostasis etc is strange and opaque, leading to constant missteps as the system cannot be predicted
- Citizens will happily strike on the things they demand, preventing their fixing and end in death
- The resource system is self-circulatory, meaning a resource can rely on itself for production, creating a deadlock
....
I could go on.

Such great potential - such a wasted opportunity. :(\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpent around 15 hours finishing one run of the game. Overall 4 out of 5. Towards the end, game play leans towards repeated clicking and waiting, not all that satisfactory in action, but once you hit the ending, it somehow felt worth it.

Don't buy unless you're really into city builder with a HEAVY emphasis on resource management. Again, this is a resource management game most of the time, with a storyline to propel you forward in expansion of your city.

The story is good with some choices to be made. The biggest one is not until the very last chapter, by which point you'll have pretty good understanding of how the game works, though the tasks will still come as surprising and need some crunching to do by that point (unless you're a resource hoarder...).

In all, if you liked Frostpunk, this would be a slightly harder version. Get it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is kinda rough to start with... I played about half way through previously and halted, as it seemed excessively difficult with expectations of it just using squeeze mechanics until unbeatable (deteriorating hull severity increases with each sector expansion and chapter).

I picked this up again perhaps a year later, with a better idea of what was to happen. I played half way through, again, and abandoned my game when I realized rushing through chapters is a mistake (you're "punished" only once per chapter for being slow... but this is really intentional and not practically avoidable). This time started anew, used my familiarity and experience to build better, and it was much more enjoyable until the end, when selections I had made chapters prior really came back to make it seemingly impossible to win.

So yea.. if you play, be thorough, and I encourage researching every decision, at least in chapter 3, so you'll be outfitted to win at the end. While I didn't finish, I did reach the final encounter, and really enjoyed so many aspects of the game... The lore, story, and voice acting I walked away especially impressed with; Which I've never said of strategy title until this one.\t\t\t\t\t
