\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, regarding all those who think the game is very difficult, I don't understand this - I think it was quite easy, I played it on default level. If you take your time during the chapters and just use your head a little bit, I don't think it was particularly difficult anyway.

The story was good in my opinion, however the endgame was pretty thin and very easy, but the road to the endgame was pretty good.

Unfortunately, there is not much replay value. But it was worth the money, especially at the discount I bought it for - 24 euros for 68 hours of playing time is really ok. Note though that I gave myself plenty of time during each chapter, you can definitely do it a lot faster.

I usually ALWAYS play my games with the music almost off, for the first time ever I had the music almost at full volume... AMAZING background music/soundtrack. I give this 11 out of 10... insanely good music and sound.

The game itself I give 7.5/10, would have given a solid eight if the endgame wasn't so easy and thin.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's no "mid" review. First, the art is amazing. It's so beautiful. This game has a GREAT core, but it needs a ton of polish. A ton. They did help with the difficulty settings. It's a great step in the right direction. The mechanics are pretty good, but the UI and tutorial are very lacking. It's so frustrating bc I know this could be great, but it needs a ton of QOL features.

Improvements it needs:
1) Food/population management. Food is eaten every 5 cycles, but produced every 1 cycle. Every time you need to calculate if you've got enough food, you have to add up all the food producers you have, make sure you've enough, multiply by 5, etc, etc. A simple food->estimate ppl fed would be a great QOL.
2) picker/eyedropper tool/copy bldg/move bldg. You have to destroy/rebuild things constantly, especially as you learn. Buildings essentially need to move. A move building tool or a copy building tool would improve things drastically.
3) What the heck is space weather? There's no tutorial for this. I only noticed it when my miner got destroyed. You can't manually pilot ships! How are you supposed to drive around it?
4) When do I open another sector? I didn't even know there were other sectors until I accidentally clicked on it.
5) Explain sub menu research. When you research something, there's a main menu with increasing circles, but there's a sub menu! I didn't notice that for about 6 hours of game play. It's never explained.
6) There's a second menu of solar panels! I'm so frustrated by this. Why is there a 2nd menu? They're so cheap, too. I assumed they were super expensive or something because they're level 2. A standard linear progression to put the more expensive ones on the 2nd menu. They're not. It's a second row that's identical to the first.
7) Why do I have to have a storage building in one sector to push inventory to the other one? Sector 1 doesn't need silicon, but you have to have a storage facility set to silicon with inventory 0 to push it to sector 2. Complete waste of space and resources.

I'm only 9 hours in. I assume there would be more if I played more but I'm gonna give it a break until they make some more updates.\t\t\t\t\t

I get why people would prefer this to be autonomous, but it would take away a lot of the atmosphere and allaround feeling. Balancing the sectors, and deciding which projects need to be prioritised is an important part of the game, in my opinion.

Ixion is often compared to Frostpunk. I think that only applies on the surface, as Frostpunk was more focused and more on point (keep your people from freezing to death), and Ixion has a broader spectrum of fail-states and how to avoid them. Or at least try to ;)

At my first attempt it was a close race of what would kill me: a mutiny or a hull breach. When it finally ended I was just glad, that I could restart :D\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely ever review the games, but as I love sci-fi and Frostpunk I decided to give this game a shot and didn't regret it at all. The story is set up pretty well, but the exploration, progression and events felt like I was reading the 'Foundation' by Azimov.

The best bit was discovering all the science locations and reading what anomaly has the scientific team discovered and especially discovering paradoxes that could not be explained in any possible way.

The city-building mechanics on the Tiqquin is pretty difficult and you have to rebuild and optimize as you progress in order to keep increasing population and satisfy demand for expansion and upgrades. It might be difficult, but it is actually very easy to complete the game on normal difficulty at least. The most essential tip is to take your time and not rush going to the other system and always firstly do the side tasks like discovering places of interest and extracting resources in order to prepare for the next journey.

I coudln't get myself off this game until I finished it as the plot is pretty exciting and I coudln't wait until the finale which was great in my opinion.

If you don't know much about this game, do yourself a favor and don't even watch trailers to not spoil too much. Definitely worth the experience of completing it for the first time.

Also if you wish to see different outcomes, just save and reload ;)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts a pretty good Anno style colony management game. There are some aspects of it that were frustrating, particularly the limited space for building, but that was just a part of the challenge. Its a pity the sector specializations couldnt be taken much advantage of, eg Recycling tier 2 increasing industry and food production, yet in order to do so took up nearly 2 thirds of your build space in that sector, so then adding in industry or food production to take advantage of said benefits were next to nil, so ultimately was a pointless venture to try and use any bar the one for space buildings.

All in all though, was pretty enjoyable, even though it did kick my arse for the first week before i got the hand of balancing what and when to build/research. Would recommend if you enjoy colony managements.\t\t\t\t\t
