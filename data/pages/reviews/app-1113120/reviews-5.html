\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIxion is the game you need when you're bored of everything else, still want a challenge, but don't want to have to come home and 'work' at something to have fun.

The visuals, the story, the music, all serve to create one of the better gaming experiences you're going to have. When your hull is degrading, your people are nearing mutiny, you're thinking about risking another in-sector move to get closer to mining materials, a science ship just got lost to a freak accident, and your stockpiles are going through a re-organization you've determined will raise efficiency..the music will hit and you'll feel like you're doing the right thing for humanity's future.

Is Ixion difficult? Yes. Will it punish you? Yes. Will you be wondering if this is the right technology to unlock with the current science you've been able to uncover when you really think that tech over there might benefit you more in the long run..but you need to survive today? Yes.

If you're looking for something a little different, a little challenging, but very enjoyable, then look no further than Ixion.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the best space-age city building game made, period. It is incredibly fun to manage a flying space station/city/factory/humanity's last hope of survival. It takes a little bit of time to learn how to manage things and it is very easy early to find the station headed down a "death spiral" of cascading system failures. For example, I flew into an ice storm, my solar panels took some damage, I made the choice to shut down some farms to save power, my employees/citizens started starving, then they started hating me, then they started not wanting to show up to work, then everything started shutting down. The way you have to manage all the Tiqqun's interconnected systems while trying to complete some objectives is very engaging,

About those objectives. The story is really told through a series of encounters as you explore different solar systems. There's a prologue and 5 acts of varying length, each act is a new solar system with its own unique challenges. Ixion took me about 35 hours to beat, but I also died about 4 hours in and decided to restart from scratch with what I learned. If you have the time, I would actually recommend that. I love how the story is told: you have to explore your solar system to find resources you need, gather useful research, uncover the galaxy's deep dark secrets, but exploring can be risky and sometimes your science ships don't come home. Space is dangerous, yo.

The immersion is incredible. Ixion is the first game I've seen iCUE integration implemented, and it was awesome. My PC, keyboard and mouse would flash red when my station had a power blackout to match the inside of the station. I felt like I was actually onboard the Tiqqun. The music is really good and adds to the storytelling and really enhances the tense moments. The pacing is just right for a challenging city builder: it keeps you on the edge at all times and it keeps you coming back for more. This is the kind of game where the minute I turned it off, I was thinking about the next time I could play it again.

Ixion is a 10/10. One of the best games I've ever played.\t\t\t\t\t
