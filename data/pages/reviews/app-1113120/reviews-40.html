\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall I think the game is decent. I had fun playing and it's an experience I will remember even if I didn't enjoy it (Just saying it's a fundamental good experience at its core). It's not something I would go out of my way to recommend, but it's a game.

Positives it has:
1) Looks - The game is really beautiful. Everything kind of is with the exception of zooming into your city.
2) Music - Unique music which 100% fits the ambience. It has happy music, sad music, ect. You will mostly hear all music on repeat, but the correct music is played when it counts.
3) City building - it's pretty fun with the objective being optimising space in a tetris like environment.
4) Story - If you're willing to piece the puzzles together as you go along the story is pretty good (with the exception of one particular character which is comic book type material).
5) Cutscenes - There's not many, but I like them

Problems it has:
1) Art - Lacks the pictures on each event (many will be reused) and also feels like they wanted to finish the game quicker due to lack of eye candy in the end. If you're coming from Frostpunk the difference is noticeable.
2) Weird Difficulty - If you could graph out the difficulty it would look like stairs with you having to cope with lots of problems immediately and then absolutely nothing for the majority of the time
3) Events - Events CAN get pretty repetitive in terms of the choices you make. Some event starts to kind of feel the same and the choices you make have limited morality/logical decision making behind it and similar rewards. Because of this I started to cheat by looking on the wiki since choices didn't seem 1:1 (maybe I'm a noob for this, but maybe you'll understand. I didn't feel a need to cheat in Frostpunk)
4) City looks bad - When you zoom into your city it's full of random people walking around with no direction and people doing the same animations in a endless loop on every building. It's not great.
5) Ending - I'm a little bit disappointed in the ending and it feels a little anti-climatic, but it was enough to conclude.

If you're going to get this game, my advice is to (regardless of how annoying it may be) constantly adapt with new technologies and play that game of tetris because if you don't it can make your life way worse than it needs to be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti have played 8 hours by the time of writing this review (and in chapter 3). I am really enjoying the game. While i hate comparing to other games, it did give me frostpunk vibes after playing for an hour or two. The game is decently challenging which i love. It is not a relaxing city builder game, it is a survival game that the odds keep getting stacked against you with time (which reminds me of frostpunk).

The game setting is in a spaceship in space, you have to build a self sufficient colony and manage food, happiness, repairs...etc. Their is a story for the game over 6 chapters, each chapter is completed by finishing the main story quest. The story is interesting so far. I am curious to see what will happen next

The tutorial need to be better though, it leaves you to read things and many options are opened before you need them so they are confusing till you trial and error with them.
The mechanics of the game is tricky, there is good amount of self moving parts, however, there are enough parts that i saw myself micromanaging some bit. for example, you have different sectors in the colony, each with their own independent food and workers and housing requirements, so every now and then i need to balance the workers between sectors by manually requesting number of works to move from one sectors to another, or i will need to manually move around some food between sectors to make sure they are fed.

All in all if you like survival games with decent amount of challenge, this is for you. Beautiful graphics and soundtrack by the way, it is engrossing.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIxion was fascinating, once through. Unfortunately, for me it lacks replayability.

The tech tree, resource management and city building features were all well balanced. It's story driven and difficult. Space is unforgiving of mistakes, and Ixion captures this. These are great things and I really enjoyed playing it through.

However, when you've survived it once, all the mystery is gone. By requiring you to tread a very narrow path to survive and thrive, by the time you've finished it once, you know everything you need to win every time. Deviation from the path kills you. So there's not much point or fun in playing it again.

If this were more open, more random in the locations of key items and choices you could make, it would be much more replayable. The core game play was fun, the graphics and mechanics excellent, and the stakes were huge... that was all excellent. It's too bad the story couldn't be wider, with multiple paths to victory.

Still worth playing. Just understand it's a chose-your-own-adventure story that only has one acceptable solution, ultimately. I honestly hope they expand the story. There's a lot here to like.\t\t\t\t\t
