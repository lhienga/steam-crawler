\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who's an avid space buff and currently only on chapter 2 of IXION, I can say that this is an incredibly engrossing game. Even though I've been playing for 12 hours, I've been having an absolute blast exploring the game's many intricacies and uncovering its secrets.

Yes, the game's hard but that's the point. The people who aren't recommending this game because of the difficulty are like the people in college who rate the professor horribly because they didn't do the work and got a bad grade. While the developers have released an easier mode, the difficulty in IXION is a deliberate design choice that adds to the game's sense of immersion and realism. Those willing to take on the challenge will find an incredibly rewarding and immersive gaming experience, making IXION a must-play for those who appreciate a game that doesn't hold your hand.

What really stands out to me about IXION is the team's passion for science and realism. From the realistic technologies and impressive graphics to the carefully crafted environments, everything feels like it was created with a great deal of care and attention to detail. Watching the station use the warp engine or even just travel across the solar system to another planet is breathtaking. It's clear that the team behind IXION is truly dedicated to creating a game that not only looks amazing, but also feels authentic and grounded in real-world science.

But it's not just the attention to detail that makes IXION such an amazing game. The storyline is incredibly engaging, with plenty of unexpected twists and turns that keep me hooked. And even though I'm only on chapter 2, I can already tell that this is a game with a lot of depth and complexity.

The gameplay itself is also incredibly well done. The puzzles and challenges are challenging but I've never found myself getting frustrated, I just take a step back and do some risk and resource management. The controls are also intuitive and easy to use. It's the kind of game that really rewards careful exploration and attention to detail, and I've been taking my time to really savor the experience.

Lastly the music and sound design are astonishing! There's not much to say except this soundtrack is ******* amazing and it's probably one of the best I've listened to.

(The only thing I hope for, and this is just a personal opinion, are more complex AI and a form of realism when it comes to the citizens moving around on board the station. Something along the lines of zoo tycoon/park builder or something like that.)

Overall, I would highly recommend IXION to anyone who loves science fiction or space games. It's a truly exceptional game that's definitely worth the investment of time and attention. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic game that takes heavy inspiration from man-against-nature city-builder survival games like Frostpunk, Ixion ends up exploring similar gameplay concepts in a way that more often than not is just as successful as its peers. Make no mistake, this is not a city-builder in the vein of Cities Skylines or a Banished clone, but one that very much has failure states.

When the game first came out, there was a lot of complaints about the difficulty. I think a large part of that comes from the fact that the marketing material heavily references standard gameplay fare for city-builders, but doesn't emphasize just how intense the survival element is. Ixion is through-and-through a survival strategy game with a city-builder varnish on top of it. It also has probably one of the best soundtracks I've heard in a sci-fi game in years. The composer, Guillaume David, absolutely knocked it out of the park with this game, and really helps sell the atmosphere that this is a space odyssey with existential stakes.

The complaints about difficulty led to the company making dramatic steps over the last few months to rework their game to allow for the inclusion of difficulty system that was just implemented today. I really have to give Bulwark credit because this is a really good system that allows for a lot of customization that does wonders to make the game more accessible to players, not only at the start of the game, but at any point while playing, since custom difficulty settings can be changed on the fly during your play-through. I can easily recognize just how much work was put in to get this new system working in a way that makes everyone happy. If you were thinking of playing Ixion in the past, but were put off by the complaints people had about the difficulty, I would highly recommend checking it out now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: The game has huge potential and really scratches that itch of space survivor / colony builder but so many of the core mechanics are flawed or simply not fun that I can not recommend this game.

Off to a great start with a clear tutorial that only is held back in how often it tells you about another new system or mechanic. Story appears to go in a really interesting direction, visuals look good and gameplay feel good maybe even great!

Then I got to the second system, learned that for every story progress I will not "get stronger" and be better at facing challenges but rather my ship will be permanently damaged for every progress step. Huh, well that's not motivating. Some systems are starting to become just pure inconvenience without adding anything worth while. Especially the accident mechanic, they happen so often and have so little impact on your colony other than keeping your hospitals reasonable busy and sometimes leading to the death of one or two ppl because of overful hospitals...so? I have another 1000ppl in the freezer waiting to take their spot so I'm almost hoping to loose a few here and there.

Other "friction" points in no specific order:
- Wait time, I was waiting for the mobile game "premium" currency or add to pop up but no. Sometimes you just sit there and wait for 10 - 15min for your cargo ships to collect literally all the garbage in the solar system while taking some of the most crack brain paths possible.

- Fear of tipping the balance. Remodeling or changing your sectors layout is just not fun. Once I found something that worked, i left it and never touched again unless I really had to. Also every building is F***ing H U G E giving you VERY limited possibilities of changing the layout or optimising since some building only fit in 5 - 6 places that make sense.

- 1000% scripted events. They are all preprogrammed with set outcomes, no skill checks (some research gates AFAIK) and most of the outcome are either GOOD, BAD, MEH or (insert animated squiggly text) STORY/LORE. There are some hidden ones that sound interesting but that's not enough to keep me playing this game for another 10-20h to get to what they ment or implied for the story.

- Mechanics upon systems, upon systems upon mechanics. There are so many and so few of them are linked in more meaningful ways then "building X costs A, B and C and gives you D". Most of them are however, "consume X to get Y while expending some of your power budget". Examples of this are the Cryo ppl thawing, Hull repair and food and most of them have really simple and predictable consequences if/when you fail them.

Summary:
Buy it cheap if you really like space survival and/or city builders but don't expect anything revolutionary. The story sounds interesting but the gameplay just made me quit before getting particularily far and that despite playing for 9 h, this is not an MMO ffs!\t\t\t\t\t
