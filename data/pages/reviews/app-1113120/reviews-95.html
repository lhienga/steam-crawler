\r\n\t\t\t\t\tyOuVe StAyEd iN tHe SyStEm ToO LoNg!!!

An otherwise really pretty and pretty good game, but littered with mechanics that make playing annoying instead of hard. The game is unrewarding and the ending is glitchy and makes 0 sense. Very disappointed.

The game is designed to punish you for the sake of punishing you. Your people randomly demand you shut down production buildings even though they are happy and if you do shut them down nothing happens, but if you don't they riot. The tech to protect ships from space weather literally do nothing. Space weather on mission three after moving very slowly will suddenly move over 1/3 of the entire play map. Of all my ships 11 of 15 were destroyed within half a rotation, only the ships already at my main ship made it, and that was with the tech to make them resistant to space weather... That may seem like a small thing but this is just one example of many where they take a fun game and then add things to just f**k the player for no reason other than to make it 'more challenging', the thing is the game isn't even hard its just frustrating.

Cargo ships are beyond dumb, going out of their way to go to further resources when there are closer ones. Ships will all go to one deposit even though there isn't enough for all of them so after the first one grabs everything the other two will still go to the empty deposit then getting there take nothing, even if there is another asteroid five feet from them with resources, and go allllll the way back to the main ship empty handed. And for a game that punishes you for playing on a map too long having such inefficient cargo pilots is just another way the game is designed to be frustrating simply to be frustrating.

After having you fight and scrape for tech the whole game, the last mission gives you more than you can possibly use in the time it takes to complete the mission. Another just poor designed choice.

If you're a person who likes to yell 'get gud scrub' you may like this game. For anyone with limited computer playing time or more casual gamers you might want to skip it.

The end is very poorly put together and very unrewarding. For a game with a lot of frustrating buildup and 0 payoff is itself a reason for a negative review. Devs should redo the entire ending mission.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIXION is a cool game with an interesting setting and a very cool atmosphere, but yeah, comparing it to frostpunk should start and end with "Yeah it's the same genre".

IXION is sadly unbalanced, and there's been large amounts of silence from the devs about any sort of patches or changes to certain mechanics that others have touched on.

My BIGGEST issue personally, is that there's no way to "centralize" your storage. Every single section of the ship needs to have it's own set of storage bays, and you have to manually adjust each ones policies regarding storage and transferring materials. You open a new sector and boom there's a ton of resources that are just sitting there, but you can't ship them to other sections, first you need to build storage in that section, gather the resources, then repeat the whole "Oh gotta set my storage transfers again"

The game itself, I'd give it a solid 8 if tiny QOL changes were made, or at least if they added a "Hey, here's a slightly easier mode for people who want the story more than the struggle and challenge." So many other games have this feature, I genuinely hope that the developers decide to also do this.

All this being said, the game itself is not bad at all, it's definitely just geared more towards people who've played this genre of game extensively in the past, and not someone who's enjoyed some games in the genre but also enjoy other genres more. I definitely hope the developers continue their work on this, and listen to the complaints by the community. As of now, I'd say "Wait a few more months, at least to the next patch, see if they make more changes for the better and address the balance issues the community has been talking about."

All the bones are there for a solid and very fun game. The story is genuinely interesting, the setting is cool as hell, and the designs themselves for buildings, the ship itself, and everything else are super cool. It's just lacking that little bit of user friendliness for the people who can't dedicate the time to memorize the exact winning layouts and all the possible events that can and will happen in rapid succession.

With the fixes I've suggested a few times, and many others have way more than me, I'd definitely say this game is well worth the full price of 35 USD, if just for supporting devs willing to tackle a more hard-sci-fi setting without extremely fancy impossible technology!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a beautiful game, feels like a product of love. The narrative component is great.

As other have said, the game is hard, the management component takes a bit to learn, but the real difficulty comes from the price you make from mistakes (mistakes that are impossible to predict the first time around). You play a chapter, make the wrong decision, and maybe 30 minutes later you see you are dead and have to reload or restart the chapter. Then, you will be ready the second time. This is no different from frostpunk.

It is a puzzle, wrapped in a management game, tied together with great narration. I seriously recommend it, worth its price.\t\t\t\t\t
