\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSemi-difficult colony/city/simulation builder that took a lot of new steps outside of the standard genre, with some steps being great and others not so much.

A solid 8/10 if you enjoy city builders etc, but note that it is not an idle builder; you will have to be constantly managing resources and population. Around 90% of my playtime I was on the standard speed, sometimes going to 2x speed but hardly ever 3x speed as too much would be going on. An interesting story as well (for a city sim haha).

Soundtrack is honestly 10/10. David Guillaume absolutely did an amazing job on Ixion’s sound track. The same music producer as several WH40K games, so you know it will be good haha.

I got it on sale for 25$, so about a dollar per hour of gameplay so far. I may go back and replay in a few years.

Good game overall, would recommend, just know that it will not be an easy game. If you don’t have much experience on management games, be aware this game has a steep learning curve. I have over 300 hours in Frostpunk and still had to restart an Ixion chapter a few times.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolute cracker of a colony sim with a strong story line and lots of interesting lore via its science missions. Highly recommended for colony sim enthusiasts or sci-fi strategy enjoyers!

If by chance someone reads this who is struggling and/or seeing some of the common complaints, here are a few beginner tips:

The Tiqqun needs constant hull repair, this is done by spending alloys at the EVA Dock, so make sure to build an EVA dock and prioritise gathering alloys above anything else in the beginning.

Build a science lab asap, as you cannot do research without it.

Look to research the Foundry to convert Iron to Alloys. Iron can be mined with mining ships.

Ships can be told what to do and in what priority via the fleet button.

Build a Probe launcher and probes, probes are used to find more materials to collect or mine.

Both the Probe Launcher and EVA Dock can be set to auto-build and auto-repair respectively.

Keep an eye on power and working conditions. Powercuts kill production and can cause accidents, harsher working conditions result in more frequent and lethal accidents. These events all deplete your working crew population, which can death spiral your run if not managed. Don't be afraid to power down buildings if they are not needed, including ones you may not consider immediately like storage areas.

Make sure to wake up as many cryo pods as your food production can handle, since this is the only way your population grows.

The beginning solar panel upgrades via external construction are quite cheap and well worth investing in almost immediately.

Finally, don't forget that each tech has additional upgrades, make sure to look at them before considering new techs depending on your situation!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor what it is the game is really pretty good, a lot of reviews compare it to Frostpunk which is pretty accurate, however it's nowhere near as difficult as Frostpunk can be. As a whole, there can be a lot of micromanagement initially but once you have your sectors plotted out then by the end of the game you're basically on auto-pilot. The story is interesting enough and the game play is engaging but it feels like somethings missing. Playing the game on challenge adds quite a bit of grief but it's really the in game choices that are lacking in the end. I denied a ton of my crews requests and never suffered a single percentage of trust for doing so. Also the achievements seem sorta broken, I'm missing a few that are built into completing chapters of the main story but have ones that come after which bugs me a bit. Get it on sale and burn a week playing it, then forget about it till you find another similar sim game to compare it to.\t\t\t\t\t
