\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel compelled to give this game a positive review.
Homeworld but as a frostpunk-esc city builder as well?
Its such an incredible idea executed so very well. The atmosphere is engrossing and makes you want to follow more. Take your time with this game, its a long game though and you need to pace yourself. If you try and play this in two to three sittings you will get burnt out very quickly. I didn't face difficulties with the original settings, I did have to reload some saves when I lost all my freighters and miners to a space storm so do be very cautious in this game. It is not forgiving and hits hard when you take risks.

Its a very ambitious game and hits all the right buttons and feelings for me but if you are looking for a casual experience this is not for you, maybe with the difficult update you might be able to have an atmospheric romp but be cautious in this game because it does not hold back.

Oh and if you are here just for the music that is perfectly acceptable, Guillaume David is incredible.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you liked Frostpunk, you'll love this. Yes, it's hard - at least at the start. Yes, it's unforgiving - you'll restart a few times until you get the hang of sector layouts and resource production. But this is also one of the most cleverly constructed city builders you'll ever play. So many interlocking systems: resource gathering, exploration, research, population management, logistics, production. Not only do they all work, and work together, they're mostly extremely satisfying to manage once you understand them.

That's the gameplay mechanics. The story? Well, it's every bit as atmospheric and compelling as Frostpunk's. The setting, writing, voice acting, music - all superb. Ominous apocalyptic sci-fi vibes. Deep Blade Runner horns bellowing in the background. Bodies donking off your space station hull. What more could you want?

I was put off buying this for ages by all the reviews saying it was too hard. If you know the genre, ignore them. Ixion is brilliant.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing this now for a good 40 hrs and I quite enjoy it.
It is a "colony builder" but I like the setting. It's a dark and gloomy world, this is set in. And I really like the soundtrack. It makes me feel, like there is a classic cyber punk story going on in the streets of my sprawl.
The game takes place on 3 levels. Or more like 2, to be true. The first one is the actual colony, where one tries to fit all the buildings necessary into the cramped space of a space vessel.
The 2nd is the vessel itself. It is a beautiful view from space onto the outside of the space craft. This is where the energy production is managed on for the most part, since this is where the upgrades for the solar panels are bought. I'm not sure if this is a necessary level, but it works fine, although I usually spent very little time here.
And then there is the 3rd level, the current solar system. This is where the story of is being told through out the missions. This is also the primarily source of resources. This is done by sending probes into locations of interrest or with a suitable high chance of resoureces, e.g. astroids and planets and stuff like this.
The tech tree does feel a bit simple at the first glance, only a few buildings to discover and at the end of the first levels one usually had enough points to unlock all of them, but then there are upgrades to most of the buildings. And this is where it get interesting. The upgrades are not liniear to a building, will mean there is no level 1 and level 2 of a building. They are aspects of the building to be changed. And they often depend on certain extensions of other structures. So upgrading the missing feature might end in upgrading 4 others beforehand since they build on each other.
In general, I'd say this is a really fun game. Not as hard as e.g. Frostpunk, but I'd say still with a similar vibe. I'm pretty sure, after completing, I'd give it another go on a harder level. Not sure yet if this is something one might enjoy over and over again, like civ or games like that, but it is good entertainment for quite a while.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMultiple small annoyances add up to big annoyance that makes you want to stop the game. Played for 9 hours, got to close to end of chapter 2 and had to put it down.

1) Your 'morale' choices don't matter. Turning down a request gives no penalty, completing it gives no extra reward. Why do i have to clear all the available choices in a planet? Why give me the choice at all then?
2) Lack of freeform or choices for tech tree. You only get that much Science each chapter and you have to complete everything in 1 tier before moving on. Not much options to pick an upgrade to specialise a little bit because the story pushes you in another way and you have to clear the rest of the tier or else get stuck either way.

A particular instance where a the building that you unlock after research, actually needs another research to be used at all (Waste Treatment and DLS Policy) . That was the nail in the coffin for me when i had to spent another batch of resources and wait around to use it.

3) Workshop's priority in building/dismantling vs transporting goods. The resource generating structures gets filled up while the workers decides to go dismantle something else first
4) Workers will definitely move a resource to the storage, then to another location that needs it, instead of skipping the storage
5) Roads have little meaning here since they can be build and dismantled so quickly and doesn't cost anything but they only serve to get in the way
6) Severe lack of keyboard shortcuts
7) Artificial difficulty from hull repair and hull max hp after every chapter. Having to use the same resource to keep your entire game alive and to build your structures puts too much strain

Go play Frostpunk instead\t\t\t\t\t
