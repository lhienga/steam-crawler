\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: A decent survival city builder with great lore, but it would be better if it didn't make me always fight with its gameplay systems all the time. The constant micromanagement might be a turn off for some.

*Edit*: After 100 hours in this game, the "original" ending is pretty bland but the Romulus secret ending is pretty cool . If the devs are reading this review, please add side campaigns for the Ashtangites and the UN . The events leading up to you reaching your destination and all the lore around it is much more interesting than the actual ending. Overall still a decent game.

What I like:
- Really good universe lore, even if the story is shallow
- Art style and music are amazing
- Tutorial/Prologue chapter is better than most city builders
- Homeworld-like story progression - jumping from system to system
- The two part gamplay loop is satisfying for those who enjoy micromanaging in games: a very simplistic Caesar-like base building, that has you trying to figure out how to place all the buildings so that you maximize the space of the station, together with a Frostpunk-like Solar System overview with missions and various environmental effects

Quality of Life stuff / Bugs that I would like to see fixed (in no particular order):
- Allow placing of new buildings over ones that are to be destroyed
- Option to pause game when science ship arrives at destination or event occurs.
- Research Queue for more than 2 techs.
- Transferring resources between sectors is way too complicated if you don't want to micromanage it. Transferring colonists for example between sectors involves way too much work to do correctly.
- If you have a docking port, have it act as a stockpile so that resources go straight to the buildings that require those resources.
- Fleet management screen needs some work, it is too simplified relative to the rest of the game. It needs an 'Assign all to X resource' button.
- The death spiral of station not having enough power, then not having enough alloys/food to fix the station, which causes riots in the station, which causes power outages, which causes alloys/food not to be produced is pretty bad because it is inescapable. Difficult puzzles like this need to have some kind of solution to make it fun, otherwise it's not fair gameplay and in the end, just frustrating.
- If a science ship gets stuck as unassigned, it stays in the UI with no way to get it back.
- Notify me if a ship can't unload resources at a docking port
- When you select a point of interest, allow traveling with waypoints or route it in such a way that you have the option to avoid weather effects as much as possible (using planets or point of interests such as in Chapter 2)
- The UI toggles on certain buildings are very unresponsive, such as the "resolve starvation" toggle on the Mess Hall
- Let me decide to build a probe from the deploy probe screen instead of always flicking back to the sector view (sometimes I don't want to autobuild probes). Also let me fire the probes that I built already from any probe launcher in case I have more than 1 launcher\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttldr; cool enough game, derivative of/iterating on frostpunk in a generally positive way but unfortunately bug ridden and plagued with quality of life issues. game breaking bugs will make you replay scenarios constantly until you give up

The good:
- looks amazing and well written story
- interesting gameplay mechanics that drive the story
- good sound design

If you got everything you could get out of Frostpunk and wanted more, you'd probably enjoy this game

That being said, the game is plagued with a lot of issues and where it has a lot of visual polish, there's a lot of lacking backend polish:
- game breaking/save breaking bugs. certain actions brick your game, enjoy replaying it for hours to reach the same place, and a lot of these bug triggers are normal game actions you may perform but completely destroy your save with no fix. You don't know if your sace is bricked, but once you perform a save-breaking action every subsequent save and autosave is unloadable
- simulation ai is really poor - a lot of the things in frostpunk that "just work" don't here, you will be bashing your head in trying to figure out why the ai isn't performing an assigned action
- sometimes, it is actually your fault (you deconstruct a building and didn't notice it's not on a road anymore) but the game has very little UX to point this out to you, so you need to hunt for the issue
- decision making process doesn't actually change very much - a lot of story decisions seem important but I suspect weren't finished/polished out so are pretty arbitrary\t\t\t\t\t
