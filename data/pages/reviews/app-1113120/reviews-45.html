\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed the game, but it feels a bit off. Its like 80% good, but could be much better with just a bit more.

There are a few bad game play decisions, like locking policy management until you research and build a certain building. This means you will probably encounter sector specialization without being able to actually see it. Some of the tech seems incredibly useless, others are just useless by the time you get to them.

There are also a few things which don't work very well. You cannot assign multiple cargo ships to a mission so one lone ship needs to make several trips. Cargo ships do not consider the activity of other ships or adjust routes most of the time. 5 ships can head out to pick up 1 load. The first to arrive picks it up, the other will continue to the empty site. When they arrive at the empty site they will go all the way back before looking for a new target. Combined with the way some resources are distributed as a cluster of locations with small quantities makes a real mess of this. You can sort of work around this by changing the cargo type priority, but that just highlights how easy it would be to have the game to just internally recheck priority when cargo at the destination is picked up, or at least when arriving at the destination.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: ANNO meets Frostpunk on an atmospheric space adventure with difficulty level out of hand. If you are a fan of ANNO series, give it a shot and if not, stay away, you will suffer.

I really struggled if I shall give this game thumbs up or not. It strongly depends on your relationship with ANNO games series. The core game mechanics, which are very much like in ANNO games and which we know and love, combined with some Frostpunk-like events. The twist in this game is unfortunately the ridiculous difficulty (not in a good way like in Souls games).

Don’t get me wrong, I don’t mind difficult games (I finished Dark Souls after all). Problem is, that this game is punishing even when you have done everything right. It just swarms you with plenty of problems with no means to solve them. It is this kind of artificial difficulty which annoys me in games. Hey, if you want to make my playing even more hardcore, why don’t you break a few of my fingers? That would make things more difficult for sure.

There is constant supply of problems from the very start. To demonstrate, let’s take work force as an example. You expand your crew by finding life pods and from them you can get either workers which run your ship, or freeloaders which do not do anything but consume resources. And there is no way to train them to become workers, no sir. They just sit there, sipping their afternoon tea and watching the poor workers die from overwork, while enjoying the ride…

And this for me kills the potentially great game; the constant never-ending micromanaging. And I am not talking about the fine-tuning management which weirdos like me actually enjoy (that is after all what ANNO is about), no. It is the mind-numbing unnecessary hassle between sectors and bad storage management, energy production (which is simply too low). Now after finishing the game, I know how I should have played it and it would be manageable but now is too late. This is due to non-existing tutorial which could help me avoid all of the pit falls.

I could go on and on about all the flaws, but at the end of the day, they got the basics right and this is why I gave it the thumbs up. All these problems could be easily fixed and this game could be turned into a gem because the core gameplay is really fun! On top of it, the story is interesting and the atmosphere is great, mainly thanks to very well-chosen music.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere used to be a demo for this game, and that is how I decided to buy it. If the demo was still available, that would help make a decision better than any of these reviews.

Then there's the comparisons with Frostpunk: I loved Ixion, and didn't like Frostpunk at all. Many people, maybe expecting Ixion to be a Frostpunk-like, were disappointed. That might give a good hint as well.

To me this game reminded more of Northgard, gives me a feeling of a board game, with the complex balance between many difference resources, and having to do magic with a small-ish population where each person, like the viking villager, can do many different trades.

As a strategy game, it's better than most in the genre. I got my achievements in a weird order compared to the rest of players, and to me this proves that there are different ways to win. But, more importantly, there are ways to improvise and error-correct and do a comeback, so one doesn't need to go back to a save 50 turns(cycles) ago to save a run. This is rare in this genre, but really important in my opinion: there must be different ways to win (even mine :D).

I have played the "intended" experience: the game wasn't too easy, nor too hard. I had to do some save/loads in the last missions. I have not followed a guide, and I don't like games that require guides or save/load almost as a "game mechanic". Definitely not as hard as other games in the genre that are more popular, but, consider that "easy" doesn't necessarily mean "popular". You know which "other games" I'm talking about: they have more players, lots of negative reviews with somehow lots of hours each.

Amazing music (electronic). If you have a Hi-Fi system, this is one of those few games that will somehow make use of the absurd amount of money spent on sound equipment. The OST is in FLAC. And that bass, oh man... definitely don't expose your monitor speakers to that bass... it will destroy your monitor's speakers, maybe also your monitor.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game. Some people are saying its too difficult, they are partially right in that there might be too many accidents happening all the time (you do get a tools to manage and reduce their frequency though, but they are never completely gone), however there is a way to stop this from happening completely - just don't get your first accident. Yeah, its more of a workaround for this problem, and it worked for me, but generally accidents shouldn't happen that often when everything is in the green, so that's a con.
Otherwise, if you take this game slow, it is pretty easy. Some comments are saying the game is pushing you forward - well, kinda, but not that hard really, no. It is a narrative game, so you are not expected to just chill and build your space city indefinitely - this game has a definitive ending, and its pushing you towards it, but very gently. You have all the time in the world to build whatever you want to build, and research whatever you want to research.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who loves seeing a unique take on a genre, I so so SO wish I could recommend this game.
I love it in a colony survival when I can dive into its rules to make a system that is it's very peak of efficiency.
However, after 120H of playing, I have yet to play past the second chapter as I try to wrestle with a micro hell of AI ships having no programmed checks to stop them from transporting air from just emptied deposits, or from going on secret missions through storms as nodes are discovered.
More over the difficulty seems entirely tied to systems of inconvenience, like forgetting to turn off the power of everything being built, or by not informing you your workers have started planting C4 due to overtime nearing literal 20 seconds.

I will probably continue my quest for efficiency, but if there is something you should take away from this review it is that if you like colony survival you are better off playing LEGO Rock Raiders 1999 which has literal inarguable better ai than this game has and will likely ever have.\t\t\t\t\t
