\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game! If you like grand strategy games like Crusader Kings or Civ this game will share some similarities while being pretty unique on it's own still. There is a learning curve, so be prepared to watch a youtube tutorial or 2 (I recommend PerunGaming). \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game and succeed.

Why? The layers of game play, first on earth, race to the moon, mars, grab resources to fight off the alien invasion is very very tempting and should spin of more games like that in future.

(Or I just want to see Perun to make more videos :) )

The good:

Different factions from exterminate the aliens to welcome our new masters should keep it interesting to play, the player get the whole story, not only the faction "spin".

In the beginning you choose your "agents" and start your little adventure by taking over country by country, use the resources to keep the other factions and the aliens at bay and research the menace.

When the mid game approaches you need space-lift from your countries and hopefully make it either to the moon or mars in time to reserve the space resources you need to build space stations and ships to begin to fight the aliens and the not so friendly factions.

Endgame is when you have enough firepower and knowledge to bring the alien invasion to an halt and bring it to them.

The meh:

The Research tree is not very explanatory, it is split between "global" research and your "faction" research. Global gives you options to research (different drives, weapons, skills) which your "faction" research needs to apply. The idea is good but the implementation is not ideal. Why can you research spaceship drives if you cannot even build an ship. Or you could build theoretically build an ship but cannot fit it and what exactly is missing. Or what drive is better for what kind of ship (later). Or what kind of ship can I build to fight off aliens. This is the Kerbal element. It can be great but it totally sucks if you do not get it right and your investment - due to oversight - gets vaporized or stuck.

To succeed you need to overtake countries but you are limited to a certain amount of countries you can rule. The solution is to unify the countries into one and rule them that way. Idea is great but the current implementation is restrictive and meh.

The bad:

To be successful you need to rule and unify countries, research and built and create stations and ships and fight fleet battles in space.

The rule and unify countries is at the moment not ideal, it cuffs the player to US / EU / Russia / China in the beginning. No I start my uprising in Africa. To unite countries you need to have the research for this specific region. The idea is good but how it is done is not optimal. It restricts and punished players to do their own. This is something that needs to be addressed. And yes, free map painting would be the other extreme.

The missions of your "agents" become repetitive pretty quickly. And the agents themselves need certain skills, the skills you get from organisations you need to buy. Want to detect and kill enemy agents or aliens? Missing kill or detect skill needed? Not enough money? Agents XP - based skills not high enough. Weeeell bad news Frusty McClick - no arms - no cookies.

How to build and use ships is at the moment completely up to explore for the player. So either you save and try and reload or follow some advice from YT step by step. I do not know if that is the fun that is intended.

Want to talk to an other faction to trade / make peace / sell something / steal something? Well first you have to find one of their agents. For stealing it would be acceptable but just to exchange some diplomatic punches? Nah.

Summing it up:

I want this game and it's concepts to be successful. However I do not like that the players starting choices are limited by design (unite countries) and that certain things are difficult to know (the right research for unite *my chosen countries*, how to build ships, what drive is good here for this purpose, what weapons should I use, why is the global research ahead and why is my faction research lagging behind? And why do I want to research this or that.

Certain things in this game - if it wants to have an broad gamer base and an impact - will need to adhere to KISS (Keep It Simple Stupid), intelligently.

Other things in the game need to let the player the freedom to decide. Hey lets start in Cuba and would it not be fun to take over South America and invade the USA? You have to research South American Unification from the tech tree named whatever is either for the chopping block or really really well explained. Seriously if the research for unification would be removed from the tech tree, said tree would become so much clearer and cleaner.

So Hooded Horse should really hard think about this i-KISS thing if the intention is to sell it to more than the the nice hardcore strategic gamer base.

Like the game. Want to enjoy it. Want the game to be successful.

But it is not there. By a few lengths.

And Early Access is open for a long time now. It would be a shame if it falls between the cracks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best game ive played all year

So i was hesitant at first with this game, i wasn't a fan of longwar personally and i expected a game along the same lines, but i was very wrong. This game is compared more accurately to stellaris or other grand strategy titles, with a lot of depth and replay-ability. You are given control over certain spies and activists, with a set number of turns per game, the time is progressive but i would certainly classify this as a turn based game, which i loved to see. They have captured the essence of the agents, you really get attached to them but on the flip side it doesn't kill the game when you lose one, like Xcom.


THE GOOD
-They really hit the sweet spot with flow and progression, and i really got sucked into with that 'one more turn' feeling, till its suddenly 4am.
-Im not one to care for graphics and visuals usually, but wow this game really is beautiful, i love the theme, love the sound effects, love the art, some extra ship skins would be nice though.
-Levelling up and progression with your agents is fun and exciting.
The various ways which you can build up each nation makes the game very repeatable. But Africa is a bit of a joke, they need to fix it.

THE BAD
-The tech tree, like yeah i get it, its a space game and tech is the thing, but theres just too many of them, and its hard to aim for something you really need, when you dont even know it exists yet. The devs cant possibly expect players to read through every single tech just to find what they need. Way too many engine variations, way too many weapon variations etc etc.

THE UGLY
-The random events are mostly terrible, and some of them are way too overpowered. Things like 'oh hey suddenly your capital has been nuked by terrorists' or 'your agents government needs you, now flip a coin to see if he died' all the game with random events ive played in the past, this scores as one of the worst. A few times ive had to reload a save purely due to a stupid event.
-Ship combat, it sucks, they have really made it way too overcomplicated, giving you velocity waypoints and the need to worry about rotation factor and speed etc, all this really should be simplified into a more usable system. Some physics fan went too far. I miss simplistic stellaris type combat. Click, kill this thing, click, move here. The pilots and captains handle the rest.\t\t\t\t\t
