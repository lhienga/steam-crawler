Great game, but not for everyone!

This game is from the same people that made the Long War expansion for XCom, so if you don't have at least 100h to put into it then this game is probably not for you. But if you like the science part in sci-fi, strategy games, and are willing to put in the effort to learn about the game (which will at the moment probably include visiting "external" sites like the wiki) then you can have lots of fun learning how to beat the alien menace.

The game is also in early access and is being actively developed. There are still rough edges to this game with new ones being introduced as the devs introduce new features. But the devs are very active on discord and even more so in pushing out updates that smooth out issues that the community reported.

My expectation is that many of the issues that other reviewers complained about will be fixed by the time that this game is fully released.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI spent over 300 hours (50 in-game years) playing this game, but it was worth it. The pacing could be improved, but it's one of a few games where the mid to late game is not boring. Even with the best technologies and an edge over your rivals, you still need to plan your moves carefully to achieve final victory. And the mid-game was really fun and challenging for me, with multiple times when I thought I will lose, although I was doing pretty well overall.
The biggest drawback in the current version (0.3.x) is the user interface. For instance, you cannot easily sort and filter your ship designs or find ships with the specific desing in your fleets, and loading save games takes 3 minutes on my pretty decent machine. But I definetly recommend this game to anyone who likes hard-core sci-fi, alien invasion genre and realistic space battles. I plan to replay my campaign with different faction once new releases improve the performance.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the moment, I can't recommend the game. But I'd like to. And that hurts.

I hope things improve but here's a short list of my most immediate pains:
- Balance. The game encourages merging regions to larger ones, and it's actually quite possible and satisfying to do so. But i punishes you for doing so in intransparent ways. First, the economic potential is actually lower when you do it. Second, I don't want to spoil but there are adversaries that can use abilities to wrestle control from said large regions and you are stuck with a very limited success chance of gaining it back because the region IS that large.

- Bad documentation. Just one prime example: The gauge of alien awareness/hostility is a critical mechanic in the game. Yet it was decided to make it highly intransparent (why not just a number between 1 and 100?) AND give false information. By design.

- Diplomacy: You may get into non-aggression pacts with factions. Which seems great, because then you'll have to worry about six less operations each cycle that may otherwise be targeted against you. Turns out, after bribing factions into said pacts, they just come around the corner next turn and declare war again. At this point, it's just better to murder everyone. Way better, way more effective and they don't deserve any better anyways. But it does limit gamestyle options severly.
- Orbital maneuvers and ship construction: I admit I might just didn't get it. But it seems that there is little to no way to build actually viable ship designs that are able to fulfill meaningful roles - maybe with the exceptions of very late technologies. The auot-designs certainly don't cut it.

Really hope this improves, because the main gameplay loop is very motivating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst the constructive critic:
-Questionable game design decisions. Prominently the fact that is a sort of real time simulation in where for all the early game of each run you spend hours staring at the spinning earth ball, waiting for the time to pass to assign missions to your councillors. It looks like a patched up solution to the fact that some mechanics would fit better in a turn based game.
-Interface is terrible. It's bad, it's really bad. Many core functionalities hidden in weird sub menus like the ship construction. And you better feel comfy using spreadsheets.
-Steep learning curve. Be prepared to YouTube and Reddit your way into the game mechanics or you won't go very far.
-Game loading times need to be greatly optimised.

Now the good and why I'm leaving a positive review:
-Amazing, really really good game concept. I love the idea to have a grand strategy about an alien invasion and basically you are the men in black.
-So many moving parts, very well arranged. Everything from councillors, research to economy and combat has depth and don't feel like attached minigames, but as a grand whole.
-Overall very engaging game play if you like dense and deep grand strategy. Very immersive and with a great lore.
-So far every time devs update it, it gets better.\t\t\t\t\t
