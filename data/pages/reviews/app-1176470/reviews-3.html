\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really bloody like this game.

I'm terrible at maths/science/physics so the tech tree (specifically the ship engines and reactors) was daunting at first but the ship builder menu is really easy to understand, and the tech tree makes more sense when you get to terms with the idea that you need to compromise and can't instantly make fast ships with massive guns and super armour. I'd argue there are too many useless drives at the start of the tech tree at the moment but equally you could say that's because I don't like to build ships until the mid-game starts.

I'd say there are too many torpedoes/missiles but that could be because I just beeline to the biggest nukes I can get.

Compromise is kind of what the game is about, you have pretty limited resources until the mid-game so you need to tread carefully against the Ayys so they don't send a fleet to Earth before you even have a single ship ready. The same goes for if you're a heretic and you anger too many Humans before you're ready and end up getting dogpiled by Resistance and HF chads and end up having to nuke half of Asia just to keep your money flowing.

Compromising your priorities and conserving your materials mean you need to be a pretty patient game to really get in to this game - Some people can get entire runs done in a few tens of hours (partly by meta-gaming, partly by just being smart) but in my 525 hours of proper gameplay (sometimes i fell asleep with the game running...) I've not finished a run.

Not finishing a run didn't mean I didn't have fun, however - Assassinating other councillors is really fun, trying to maintain peace with other factions while also trying to steal the countries they run while also trying to not ♥♥♥♥ them over so hard they couldn't fight the Ayys themselves meant things stayed interesting.

Actual countries can feel a bit lifeless at times - Running the EU looks and feels the same as running the USA or uniting and running the African Union does, except they give off different amounts of resources. Maybe you Councillors should get tophats if you re-unite the British Empire?

The factions weren't lifeless though - Having quite a lot of voiced dialogue helped keep me immersed in the struggles of my terribly run and ineffecient Resistance.

I have recommended this game to a bunch of my mates but if you don't like buying Early Access titles I don't think you'd miss out by waiting for the game to be released. It's really good but there are a few bugs and weird UI bits that are slowly being ironed out, and there aren't many mods (not that you need any, except for the one that adds the New California Republic!).

I was playing on a Large scale solar system and experienced very little lag at pretty much any time. Loading in to a save took around 60 seconds at times, and I have a 2070S and a 3600x.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really, *really* want to love this game. It's really cool, the underlying gameplay has a ton of potential, and you don't see hard-leaning sci-fi like this often. However, the UI is just, not great. The tutorial is perhaps one of the worst I've played. The way operations and game phases work are more annoying than compelling, even when you know what you're doing (which is rare). I'm of the opinion these need a serious rework or adjustment to make them interesting. Agent actions feel useless a lot of the time, with a few exceptions.

There's something of a skill issue here, but I've played for ~17 hours and still feel like the fundamentals are way out of my reach. Note, however, that this is well under a completion of a single run. It's certainly a very long game, with a whole lot of moving parts. I don't think it'll ever be *easy* to learn, but it could be a lot less painful.

The framerate dips HARD when zooming in and out between levels, which is extremely jarring. Otherwise performance is fine in my experience. I never encountered many if any bugs and the rare updates seem to have quite a lot of fixes in them, so it's already ahead of a lot of very big budget games in that regard.

The game's been in early access for almost two years now, and updates are very rare. Full release doesn't seem in the cards for quite awhile. I'll probably come back to this game in about a year to check up on it, and hopefully it'll be in a better state, but honestly it feels like it's gonna take a lot longer than that. I wouldn't say the game is worth the $40 price tag, but there is some value in supporting the devs so they can make this game the best it can be. It's not a bad game really, there's tons of potential and it's still fun to play for long periods of time. It just needs a lot more work. I genuinely hope this game gets the time, effort, and love it deserves, but I can't say I'm confident that it will.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove this game. It's the Expanse meets XCOM. You start out playing what is essentially a grand strategy game on Earth, competing with 6 other factions to control countries and decide a response to the aliens' arrival, while either interfering with or aiding alien operations on Earth depending on your faction. This part of the game looks simple from the outside, but it's actually a fairly sophisticated little international relations/country management sim, where you're directing the spending of foreign policy of the nations you control via your counselors (read: agents). The council system gives me SCP vibes, feels very much like I'm sending the members of my little 05 council out to advance my interests.

As the game progresses, you get into the space layer. IMO, this is the strongest part of the game. You leapfrog outwards, starting by mining the moon, then Mars etc, all while desperately trying to advance technology to the point that you can challenge the aliens militarily and playing a delicate balancing act where if you build too much too early, the aliens will decide you're a threat and start bombing the hell out of your space infrastructure. Once you have some reasonably good techs, the space combat feels great. One of the more believable space combat systems I've seen, and it's definitely where the expanse comparisons come in. Your ships have to manage heat, fuel, delta-v, and ammunition supplies. Both the quality of your ship designs AND your tactical skill are critical to success here, and when you get things right there are few things quite so satisfying as watching your first alien mothership go up in flames after decades of preparation. Or, I guess, you could be a filthy xeno-lover and play the Servants, in which case your satisfaction comes from STABBING MANKIND IN THE BACK like the COWARDS you are.
On the technical side, I've had a bug here and there, but nothing game-breaking. Graphics are good, space combat and the ship designs are all really cool, the simulation of the solar system is actually really complex, but still runs great on both my old PC and my current machine. UI is a little...clunky? It works, and you can see where everything is, but some of the menus are nested in weird ways, and I kind of think the menu/popup windows just feel kinda dated. The devs are still actively supporting the game, and I've seen marked improvement in AI and performance every time they patch, to the point that the AI actually still really challenges me even after 200 hours in game. I have a feeling this game is someone's labor of love, and I think it shows in the finished product.
Overall, this has become one of my favorite games, I'm nowhere close to doing all the stuff I want to do in it, and I would rate it a solid 9/10 strategy game. Highly recommend if you don't mind a bit of a steep learning curve.
PS: if you're struggling to learn the game, check out PerunGaming on YT. I found the game through his channel, and he has some good guides up that walk through some of the more obtuse parts of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a long time I haven't played such a good, multi-layered and deeply strategic game. It almost feels like touching the Twilight Struggle for the first time, where in place of Soviets you have unknown Aliens.

It's not only a grand strategy game you will soak into. It has also a challengeing learning curve, but extremelly rewarding for a motivated player. It's difficult, yes. It doesn't tell you anything. You're on your own against the alien threat for the globe.

And while the Aliens are coming for sure, sometimes leaving circles in the crops, other time abducting the people, you're also exposed to other uncertainties: human rivals, spies, revanchisms, rougue states politics, nuclear proliferation and finally the growing climate crisis.

The best depiction of the game's atmosphere would be Donald Rumsfeld's quote:

"Reports that say that something hasn't happened are always interesting to me, because as we know, there are known knowns; there are things we know we know. We also know there are known unknowns; that is to say we know there are some things we do not know. But there are also unknown unknowns—the ones we don't know we don't know. And if one looks throughout the history of our country and other free countries, it is the latter category that tends to be the difficult ones."

And since the game is still in development, I simply can't imagine how it's going to be when done.

Kudos to the devs!!!\t\t\t\t\t
