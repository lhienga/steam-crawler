\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was actually very disappointed in this game. I had read the Overwhelmingly Positive reviews on Steam, and heard good things from people that played other games I like, and thus thought I'd be silly not to buy it. However, it is definitely not for me.

Firstly, I DID like how the game's mechanics worked. I really liked how no action was just a single click, but often a continuous motion. An example would be rotating the key inside the music box that appears in-game (not a spoiler, I think) takes multiple rotations of the mouse/joystick. That's cool! The words coming to life as you're walking was really neat, too.

Visuals were fair, in my opinion. I expected better but that's on me.

Audio was good, no complaints. Pretty good music!

The story is what I really did not enjoy. I knew the premise of the game and expected it to be resolved in a way that was meaningful and climactic. It really didn't feel that way when I finished it. At the end of the game, WRoEF felt like it was trying to be a dark story simply for the sake of being a dark story. There are "morals" at the end but they didn't hit home for me. To me, it seemed like there was no point to the despair that was constantly in the story. Thus, I found this game disappointing. I know other people love this game, though, and I'm glad they do! Just not one for me, and I thought I'd say why.\t\t\t\t\t
