Years have passed since then, but the impact of this game has stayed with me, and upon replaying it recently, I've come to appreciate its story in a whole new light.

What Remains of Edith Finch isn't just a game; it's an emotional journey through the lives of the Finch family, each one a chapter in a larger, interconnected narrative. From exploring the mysterious Finch house to uncovering the deeply personal stories of its inhabitants, every moment is meticulously crafted to evoke a range of emotions.

Revisiting the game now, I find myself noticing subtleties and nuances in the narrative that I may have missed before. The way each story is told, through unique gameplay mechanics and stunning visuals, creates an immersive experience unlike any other. Whether it's soaring through the skies as a bird or reliving memories through a comic book, each vignette leaves a lasting impression.

But what truly sets What Remains of Edith Finch apart is its ability to touch the heart. The themes of family, loss, and the passage of time resonate deeply, reminding us of our own mortality and the importance of cherishing every moment we have. It's a reminder that life is fleeting, but our memories endure.

As I reflect on my journey through Edith Finch's world once again, I'm struck by how profoundly moving it is. It's a testament to the power of storytelling in video games, and a reminder of why I fell in love with gaming in the first place.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved this. One of my first walking sims, it definitely has set the tone and the standard for what I would look for in these types of games. I hadn't come across this kind of game (narrative walking sim) when I first played this and it turned out to be an excellent intro which kept me hooked until the end. I enjoyed exploring the family tree and the variety of stories and styles of delivery. Uncovering the mysteries had me nervous and anxious at times and I half expected jump scares many times but there was nothing too bad, there was definitely tension at times but it wasn't scary out of context. I highly recommend this.\t\t\t\t\t
