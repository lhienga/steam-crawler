Still Standing

What Remains of Edith Finch is a game that left me dumb struck staring at the screen while the credits rolled. It's a beautifully written, beautifully designed story that captures your heart in all of its moments, ranging from the smallest quirky moments to the ones that brought the most despair. Excellent voice acting compliment each family member making them seem very real, and thus keeping you immersed in their stories like you're actually there.

A somber tale that will leave you in silent contemplation, and any media that can do that deserves a recommendation in my book.\t\t\t\t\t

The game is honestly a breath of fresh air compared to the games I have been playing recently. The game felt very unique, and the level of interaction was amazing. I loved that the dialogue printed on the screen, and then you could walk into the dialogue, causing it to go flying in different directions. It was awesome to be a part of the action instead of making one click and seeing the action occur. I wish I could forget all about this game and then rediscover and experience it again. Truly a masterpiece of a game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's always interesting to me when you don't agree with the majority. You're not trying to be defiant. You just disagree.

This game reminds me a lot of Gone Home, another short, narrative and story-driven walking simulator. I remember reading the opinions of players at the time where half the people loved it while others disliked it and called it a tech demo. I liked Gone Home at the time but I don't think I would feel the same playing it today.

What Remains of Edith Finch is the superior game of the two, almost mainly because of the stories you play where the game switches up and becomes something different than the average walking simulator. The gameplay and the art direction in some of the stories was good and felt original. The house was refreshing and enjoyable to walk through the first time and made you wish you grew up in a house like that. The narration of Edith by Valerie Rose Lohman was also great and you enjoy listening to her tell her story.

The story and gameplay is also 100% linear. Once you realize this the game will not be able to surprise you excluding maybe where the story goes. While the fate of some of the family members stick with you once you realize what's about to happen to them, not much else does. It makes you wonder what the point of the story was. Shock factor? Just to be depressing for the sake of it? The story hints at supernatural, unexplainable events which get you interested but in the end the game delivers no answers. Just questions open for interpretation.

There's no wonder the game mainly shows off the playable stories in the trailers and screenshots since Edith walking around the house is unsurprisingly bland and even boring. I finished the game in 2 hours and used another 30 minutes to make sure I got all the achievements. Maybe I would have recommended this game if it was more reasonably priced. But 20$ for 2 hours of (mostly) walking is too much. I even grabbed the game at a discount for 4.49$ and still considered refunding it after playing it had I not played it for "so long".

It was, while overpriced, an okay project but in the end it didn't cut it for me.\t\t\t\t\t
