\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never seen games as a medium used for storytelling quite like this. You cannot have a book version of this game. You cannot turn it into a movie and have the same impact. This game, despite being a walking simulator, is very intricately tied to its medium. In a world where most mainstream stories are told in the most simple, pedestrian, and unambiguous way possible to make sure even 3-year olds who've just learned to walk can understand it, this game leaves out just the right amount of information to keep things interesting, and slowly reveals them in a way that feels natural to the story. You'd at times find yourself thinking 'OH!' as you piece the narrative together. It also doesn't feel like exposition is being recited to you--which happens in so many games. It feels like how a normal person would tell a story to someone they already know. And these stories are told in some of the most creative ways I've seen in games. They keep on outdoing themselves with each vignette. In particular, there was this point in the game where (slight spoiler:) mental illness was portrayed in such a creative way that left me impressed.

At 2-2.5 hours, this story is very economical and not one word is wasted in meaningless jabber. Set aside an afternoon for this and it'll hold your attention until the end.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo many people have said their piece on Edith Finch. Its good, I agree its a beautiful bit of interactive story telling. A gothic novel in the southern gothic style, a house and a family haunted by itself in a way. Its good, sure its artsy as its detractors say, but artfully done isn't the negative they seem to make it out to be. Its short, but honestly given its story elements and vibes its as short as it needs to be, and length of art isn't a great measure of value compared to the depth of connection and meaning. It also helps that the game has a stunning art direction and never drops its stylistic edge throughout.

Took me forever to get around to it, barely any time at all to finish it, and I'm grateful for the time I spent. Simple as that.\t\t\t\t\t
