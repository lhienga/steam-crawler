\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSUMMARY: The reason I love gaming is because games can be artful story telling. What Remains of Edith Finch is absolutely beautiful story telling in a unique and interactive way that you wont forget after finishing it.

PROS:
+ Narrative bliss. The narration in this game takes you on a journey. The writing is top notch and it shines as you progress.
+ Never a dull moment. I was engaged throughout and beat this in two sessions (I only stopped the first session so I could go to sleep), but it could easily be done in one session if you've the time.
+ Each character has a completely different story that takes your imagination along for a ride. There's enough told to help you understand, and yet there's plenty untold to let your imagination fill in the blanks - or yearn for more in a good way.
+ The art design, graphics, and sound work perfectly for the narrative.
+ Perfect length. I thought this game was the perfect length for what it was. That said, I enjoyed the story so much that I wouldn't have minded more.

NEUTRAL
= Nit pick, but considering that the house had been "abandoned" for so long, I was amazed at how perfectly preserved everything was both inside and out.

The story showcases themes of life and death in various creative ways that resonate somehow with different characters of different ages from the Finch family. As players progress through the game, we learn that the Finch family is cursed by death, and we get to bare witness to the death of each character mentioned in the game. But with time, players should begin to understand that this curse is metaphorical. While a fictitious video game where one can assume the curse is literal, "What Remains of Edith Finch" definitely seems to have an underlying message about life and death. While I am biased to enjoying the story of Barbara Finch for its comic-like storytelling and horror appeals, I was particularly touched by the story of Lewis. The gameplay for this scene was incredibly creative to me, and I also felt strongly about his story being told by his psychiatrist. With just one person, the game developers were able to hit on themes of substance abuse, mental illness, and death. Nonetheless, all stories were touching and, for the most part, shocking in how they ended. While this game is up for different interpretation, as it truly is a piece of art, I believe that the most basic lesson to take away from the story is that we should live life to the fullest. I don't really think there is anything I would change about the game if I could‚Äî solid 10/10!\t\t\t\t\t
