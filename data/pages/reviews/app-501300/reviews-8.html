\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI saw this game on a 'best games of all time' list, saw some of the gameplay footage and as someone indifferent to 'walking sims', thought it would be one for me to skip over. I now know that the footage used in that video was very careful not to spoil anything.

This game has incredible set design, first and foremost - probably the best I've ever seen in a game. It's extremely likely that, unless you're the type to go into convulsions when you don't shoot something for three seconds - you will be drawn in to this game's environment and world.

The game also has a similar quality of structure to the seminal 'Shadow Of The Colossus' - where there is two gears in which the game's story functions, that juxtapose and play off one another well, Down time, wandering about the real world, to break up the incredible and often surreal set pieces that make up this game... and its depiction of the Finch family.

Sometimes when I read or hear the word wander in a review, i'm like 'that's a nice way of saying the game is going to be mildly disrespectful of my time'. Not here. This game has absolutely no fat on it. I had a big day of work, and finished this game front to back between 4pm and my no-screens cutoff time of 7pm (good for the sleep, good for the mind, can recommend). One of the best gaming experiences I've had. Perfectly paced, and in a great little bite sized chunk at 2 to 2 and a half hours. This is the game that opened me up to walking simulators. Also, movie sized games. I'll definitely be on the lookout for more in the genre because of this one - and my view of videogaming as a whole has been elevated by this.

The set design, the music, the visuals, the experiences you have reliving the tales of the Finches, there's really nothing I can fault this game for (except for the extremely minor detail that the gravestones around the midway point of the game clearly had text just floating on top of them, rather than truly being engraved. But i'm always looking for everything right and wrong a game does, to refine my idea of what makes great in this genre.)

Have this experience. Again, it's done in two hours and change, so it's not a big commitment on your part, you might think if you watch a review on it that it's not for you... that reviewer was doing you a favour by not spoiling the many very very cool experiences you'll have in this game. By the time you've revisited two or three memories of the Finches, you'll start to see what this game is about - and I felt a little lull around the midpoint, but it's almost as if it were implemented there for me to be even further blown away by the latter memories. Lewis at the cannery is a particular standout. Go inside these people's minds. I love greatness in all genres, cutesy, horror, racing, puzzle, whatever. This is a masterpiece in the medium of gaming. Experience it. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
