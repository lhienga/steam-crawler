All in all, a definite recommendation from me!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdith Finch is more of an interactive story than a game, or a walking sim, if you will. While it could have easily been a film or a novella, giving players control during the imaginative portions would not have been as memorable in those medium.

Most walking sims accentuate the inherent linearity in video game narratives, but Edith Finch's ambiguity and non-linear presentation mask that flaw by inviting players to speculate.

Would recommend this game if you're interested in non-action games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA linear walking simulator that is all about story and atmosphere. The gameplay is very simple, with just walking around to find things that can be interacted with, but that is not what the game is about. It is all about exploring the house and the Finch family and their weird history. It is all presented through story segments that are usually unlocked from personal items. These segments are mostly from the perspective of the owner with some being just some short text, or being a fairly long journey. And these segments are usually presented in a very unusual or fanciful way (like controlling a rubber ducky) making them all unique and interesting. My words can't do it justice. The presentation is really great throughout, even the credits.

But if you aren't up for a weird and interesting, non-interactive journey though, the game won't be for you. It is short and sweet though so it is worth playing for anyone who is even slightly interested (like me, not really my normal kind of game). The big negative for me would be the lack of concrete answers on what exactly happened. It is up to the player to interpret and try to make sense of all the stories. Personally I prefer real closure, I have no interest in replaying or looking through every little detail to maybe understand things, if that's even possible.

Still the journey is worthwhile in my opinion. It has got to be one of the best walking simulators out there.\t\t\t\t\t

TL;DR
This is the best emotional walking simulator. Full stop. If you have the chance to play it for the first time, do so without reading any further about the game. Please have tissues ready! I play this game once every year and own it on every platform it's available on.

Story / Character
Please stop reading and play the game first! It will greatly enhance your experience!
This game lets you relive the lives of different members of the Finch family. As the player explores the family home. Each story is told in a completely different way. Every time I showed the game to friends, everyone liked a different story the most.
I feel that there is always at least one story that someone can identify with or feel emotionally touched by.
The story that touched me the most personally was Sam's story. On my first playthrough, I had to put the controller down for ten minutes before I could continue the game.

Gameplay
Each story offers a slightly different kind of running simulation. The game never feels tiring or complicated, but is always fresh enough not to get bored of.

Artstyle / Music
Again, the artstyle and music changes for each story. The screenshots give a pretty good idea of the styles you get to see. The music always felt fitting.

Bugs
I didn't notice any bugs in my numerous playthoughs on different devices.\t\t\t\t\t
