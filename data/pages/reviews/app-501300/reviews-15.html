\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis isn't a game of action, and doesn't pretend to be, so I'm not sure why the negative reviews bring up the calm pacing of the game as the reason why the game isn't entertaining. It plays like a walk through of an interactive movie or a visualization of reading or narrating a story that one might imagine in their head while reading. Takes about 2-3 hours to complete start to finish, and as with any well written movie, the exposition takes it's time, and builds up slowly, but at around the 30 min mark of play through, I was fully entranced within the story line. Each "chapter" of Edith learning about the individual history and connecting it to the broader family story line has both such a creatively different animation style and unique narrative structures. For me, it felt like a short window of rediscovering the magic of being fully lost in the creative imagination of reading a book as a youth, while simultaneously processing the developing themes and broader implications of the story through the eyes of an adult. Buy it and store it in your library to save for a literal rainy day, and instead of watching a movie one evening, give this story a literal walk through.\t\t\t\t\t
