\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those games that don't really worry about being a game, but instead try to give you a new experience. Not to say that this game doesn't qualify as an actual game, it has interactivity, but its main focus is by far its story and the execution of said story.

The narrative is both original and emotionally resonant, with its universal appeal stemming from impeccable execution. Playing as Edith, who returns to her family home after receiving a key from her mother, players unravel the mysteries surrounding the house and their family.

Like I said before, the execution is what truly makes this an unique experience. And that brings me to the visuals, which are one of the most original I've ever seen from a video game, they utilized multiple different styles and all of them are fitting for what they are telling us.

Finally let's talk about the gameplay, it's nothing particularly interesting, the standard that you would expect from a walking simulator type game. Though there are a few segments that have some gameplay outside the norm from these types of games, but nothing to write home about.

For enthusiasts of narrative-driven games with drama and mystery, this short game is definitely worth a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWalking simulator with a great story but very limited gameplay.

It only takes 2 hours to complete so maybe next time you fancy a movie you should experience this, however if you're in the mood for a game don't bother because there isn't much game here. It's probably not fair to compare it to a movie though because it wouldn't work well as a film, the story is definitely enhanced by the fact that it is a game because you do get the feeling you are stepping into the different family members shoes during flashback/story sequences.

Story 9/10
Gameplay 3/10- you can't run or jump so I hope you enjoy walking (however you do have very brief sequences of controlling animals that can jump/fly/swim etc in flashbacks)

Steam only lets you choose thumps up or down for reviews, I'm kind of in the middle as it is a memorable story but if i'm reviewing it as a game then i'd have to lean towards a thumbs down.\t\t\t\t\t
