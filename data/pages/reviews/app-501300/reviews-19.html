\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA tepid recommendation. If Steam implemented a better rating system than the current binary thumb, even a simple star rating, it'd be close to the edge of a non-recommendation, say 6 of 10 stars.

I'm a fan of walking simulators, which this is indubitably. I think atmospherically, the game achieves what it's attempting, an almost gothic horror story of a cursed family, a la the tv shows The Haunting of Hill House or Fall of the House of Usher (both of which are better relative to their medium). The variation in control schemes, tone, and perspective for each vignette adds to the experience.

However, the writing is just not sharp or pointed enough for me to feel what the game wants me to feel. If my interpretation is correct, which seems confirmed by the most popular online analysis, the actual underlying motivations and impetus of the plot are only alluded to. There were plenty of opportunities to flesh out interactions or memories of the actual "main" character (I'm avoiding spoilers, but this is not the primary controlled character). They didn't have to hit you over the head with it. In fact, they could have kept it just as cryptic. We just needed a little more of their perspective, ideally from the horse's mouth. Otherwise, how should we be expected to relate or feel for their conflict? How can we truly mourn the consequences? As is, it all plays too close to melodrama, and I'd far rather point someone towards Kentucky Route Zero or even Gone Home for better examples of the genre's potential.\t\t\t\t\t
