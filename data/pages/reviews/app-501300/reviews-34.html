\r\n\t\t\t\t\tman, it's not often that i cry over a game but this one did it for me.

what remains of edith finch, despite being a "walking simulator", dug so deep into each character and how they perceived the world that it shattered me on impact. lewis's story hit extremely close to home, as someone who deals with maladaptive daydreaming. i think that the portrayal / representation of everything each character dealt with such as maladaptive daydreaming and ptsd was honestly within the best that i've ever seen. the interaction between edith and the world is beautiful in it's own, eerie way, unlike any game i've played before.

getting to know not only their stories, but also their relationships makes each character somehow hard to let go of, even though you know what's coming. and DAMN does it hurt.

if it wouldn't have gone on sale, i probably would never have played it, but damn am i glad i did.

i cannot even put into words how much this is a game that i would do anything to play for the first time again.\t\t\t\t\t
