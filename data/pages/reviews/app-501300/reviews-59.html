\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying What Remains of Edith Finch is a relaxing way to fill a few hours. Visually beautiful, with an atmospheric soundtrack and an intriguing narration, this is a game that draws you into the story and makes you want to learn more. You see the world through different and unusual perspectives and some of the sequences are downright dreamy/trippy.

Some of the themes may be intended for a more mature audience, but there is never anything visually or audibly disturbing that would upset younger players. I spent much of my time marvelling at the beautiful and detailed environment, sometimes forgetting that I was supposed to be looking for clues. I was deeply engrossed in the game and oblivious to everything else until I had finished and sat back from the computer.

I wish more developers would work on games like this. I've never played anything quite like it. The only other game that I know of that has a similar feel is That Dragon, Cancer but that had simpler graphics and a more depressing story line.

What Remains of Edith Finch is not the longest game out there but it's a very welcome break from the usual shooters and horror survival games that appear in my Steam queue. Do yourself a favour and buy this game, then spend a blissful afternoon experiencing something really quite special.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains Of Edith Finch is one of the best games I've ever played, without a doubt. Although a bit pricey at £17.99, I enjoyed following the plot and individual stories of all the family members. None of the deaths felt repeated and they all were equally as interesting as the one before. I really enjoyed Sam's plot, and seeing his specific death shattered me. Calvin has a special place in my heart. I just wish there was more content for the price and I wouldn't have minded seeing a continuation from Christopher (Edith's son's) pov or Edie's pov. The graphics in this game are phenomenal and there is a lot of hidden details and mysterious in the family but there is some glitches and bugs Eg when playing Walter's pov I smashed the bricks but it didn't auto take me through or make a hole. I had to turn down graphics and other details to low just so I could finish the story. The house feels both eerie and welcoming at the same time and at first felt daunting walking around, especially after Barbara's death. This game is a big emotional turmoil and hit me hard, Rated 9/10 :)\t\t\t\t\t
