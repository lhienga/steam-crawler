\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn interesting evolution of the Gone Home style of storytelling. Good voice acting most of the time, even the child actors, which is a coup. A lot of ambition for telling a lot of different stories in a lot of different ways, game design-wise, and they succeed way more than they stumble when they try some pretty out there stuff.

But having just finished it, I'm left feeling a bit less enthused about the whole experience. The stories end, the book closes, everyone who died is dead, and that's it. The game just needed something, one thing, to come together from everything it had shown, one glimmer of hope, or dread foreknowledge, or understanding. And they either didn't know what to say, or thought saying nothing was the better choice. Sometimes that can feel like the right stroke of the pen to make. Here, to me, right now? It just feels empty.

Pretty brilliant little game otherwise. I would not give it a 95% recommendation (where it's sitting at right now), but I would say that the game had 95% effort and ambition put into it, which you could see and respect, all the way to the aforementioned end.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDepression, the game.

What Remains of Edith Finch is a walking simulator that follows the story of Edith, our protagonist who returns to her childhood home whilst recounting her family's tragic history. The Finch family believes they are cursed, and while exploring the various rooms Edith recounts the awful stories that happened to the family members that used to live there.

The game is very simple, with the controls boiling down to movement and a single interact button. But they do a lot with it. With these controls, the game will tell stories in various scenes. Without spoiling too much, scenes will unfold while taking pictures, reading poetry, playing in a bathtub with a bunch of toys, and (my particular favourite story) - working in a fish factory. The controls, while limited, are used in very creative ways to keep the gameplay engaging.

Ultimately you work your way down the Finch family tree, closer and closer to Edith herself. In a way the game unfolds as a mystery as you try to figure out why Edith is reliving this traumatic journey, and wondering if she can overcome this curse.

The story here is fantastic and heavy. Easy thumbs up for this one.\t\t\t\t\t
