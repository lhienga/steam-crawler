\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch: A Quiet Gem

"What Remains of Edith Finch" by Giant Sparrow is a game that surprised and impressed me in a subtle yet memorable way. It's not a flashy blockbuster, but it stands out for a few reasons that I'd like to share without giving away any spoilers.

First, the game's atmosphere is fantastic. The Finch family home, perched on a rugged coastline, feels like a character in its own right. Exploring this house is an adventure in itself, with a rich history and secrets hidden around every corner.

The storytelling is where the game truly shines. It's a collection of short stories, each focusing on a different Finch family member. These stories are told in unique and imaginative ways, making each one feel like a distinct experience. What's impressive is how emotionally engaging these narratives are. They make you care about each character's journey and offer a mix of emotions that leave a mark.

Visually, "What Remains of Edith Finch" is striking and immersive. The game's use of color, lighting, and sound design create the right mood for each story, making the experience more memorable. The music, in particular, is beautifully haunting.

Congratulations to Giant Sparrow for crafting such a distinctive and heartfelt game. "What Remains of Edith Finch" is a quiet gem that might not be for everyone, but if you're looking for a unique, emotionally driven experience, it's well worth your time. It's a game that lingers in your thoughts, and its subtlety is what makes it so remarkable.\t\t\t\t\t

I found the environments really cool to explore (although the details were extremely repetitive, ex., a shelf full of books, but there are only three book titles repeated over and over again, etc.). But tbh, I wish there was more to do! There isn't a lot of choice in this game, you are basically guided step by step through each motion to get to the next story or mini game.
