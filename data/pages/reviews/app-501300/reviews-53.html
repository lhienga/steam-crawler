\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game tells a great story, albeit a compilation of successive tragedies. It's not scary, although the initial ambiance and mystery leads you to that at first. It's definitely more of a "follow the trail and get the story" game so don't expect much input on your part. I'm a slower player and it took me about 3 hours to progress through it all. My only real issue was the controls at times were frustrating, you generally have an idea and some are obvious like "winding a music box" but I also got stuck with one of the first character area entrances. It took me a bit to figure out which way I needed to move it (found out I needed to not just hold, but release and click instead). A few more occasions I found myself frustrated trying to read a file and it just wouldn't flip.

Also, while the stories are great and this is an emotional one - it's extremely tragic. It does have a bittersweet beauty to it and there's definitely love and life in the piece. However, I did stop briefly after a particular character's story. You get an idea early on how each one will go and I was already distressed when one came up, knowing the routine. Personally, if I had known I probably wouldn't have played. It wasn't gruesome, it was just distressing.


Possible spoiler/warning:

This may be a spoiler, but as a hint I would reconsider playing this if you're an anxious parent.\t\t\t\t\t
