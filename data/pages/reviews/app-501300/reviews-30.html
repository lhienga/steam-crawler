\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a first-person exploration game about a seventeen-year-old girl name Edith Finch who’s the last surviving member of the family who decided to go back to the house where she grew up with her multiple family members. She uncovers her family history when exploring the house where she learns a lot about her family’s backstories that she puts in her notebook. While I had heard of this game before, I didn’t know anything about it for me to figure out if I should get it or not. That is until a mutual on TikTok recommended I should play this game. I’m a huge fan of story-based games and find that intriguing when diving into characters that I learn and grow to care about. That was the next game I played after Black Mesa and Inside, and I have to say that I’m on a roll with the streak because this was quite an experience when looking at it from an art form perspective.

The way that the dialogue comes at you from the background and not in a typical fashion is a nice piece of extra effort put into the gameplay where it feels like you’re listening to Edith’s thought process when exploring the area. The art direction in this is amazing that has so much creativity going on in not only the house that holds so much history for the family members but also the stories from each family member that has different gameplay in each. It never felt like a gimmick that was distracting and really enjoyed the gameplay from each family member that’s either fun, hilarious, or sad when knowing about each member. This has the type of story that gets you emotionally sucked in with how the family started it off as that gives you such warm energy, and then can emotionally gut-punch you about how the family ended it off and why Edith became the last surviving member.

It’s like reading a fairy tale book filled with different stories that have a different style, and it’s one of the highlights of the game that always made me look forward to it. Hell, it’s the first game in a long time that I managed to beat 100% because I really like diving into this world to the point where I just want to know everything about it. The ending is just beautifully told that it comes full circle of where it leads which I feel is very fulfilling. I’m also glad that I get to revisit the family members’ stories very easily after finishing the game so that I don’t need to start over and walk to this specific family member’s name. The only issue, and it’s kind of prominent, is the movement of the character. While I don’t mind at times when the game allows the dialogue to build around your movements, the character herself is just too slow and I feel Edith needs to go a little faster to get to her destination. As is, What Remains of Edith Finch is an amazing game with a beautifully told story that dives into the family’s love, grief, and the hardships they went through.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t
