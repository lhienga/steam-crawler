\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a game that I've struggled to write a review for a while now. It's really hard just to put into words what this game means and how well it tells its story. I'll try my best though. Naturally, every person is going to have a different experience with this game, but that's what makes this game beautiful. It is an experience. That is the best way I can describe it, and a very immersive beautiful one at that. This game tells the story of a family in a completely neutral way which makes it excellent. It has such a profound message when it comes to family and mortality, and how your attitude towards death can shape your relationships around you currently. As I get older many of the themes and ideas of this game resonate more and more. This game is very interpretive and symbolic. The game is very satisfying both mechanically and technically as well. The environments and music are lovely and complement the game perfectly. It is a very artistic game but it works so well with this story. It is easily one of the best first-person games I've ever personally played. I really connected it in a way that I cannot fully express despite the fact that Edith has such a different life and life experiences from me. That's what makes this game so special though, and the fact that it's an interactive game really sells the idea that you are experiencing her memories and present through her. It is a story that takes you on a journey and I think it excelled at it. I really cannot recommend this game enough nor gush about it as much as I want to without delving into spoilers, so please try it for yourself and decide how you feel about it <3 And thank you developers!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is undoubtedly the best work among the walking simulation games I've tried. It presents a new direction in the genre that many others have attempted but failed. In the end, what matters in a game is the fun it provides. I have thought of several factors that contributed to the success of this work.

- Level Design -
The meticulously crafted level design keeps the players engaged without feeling bored. Walking is an innate behavior ingrained in human DNA, and following our instincts usually leads to enjoyment.

However, simply walking on a straightforward path with the destination already in sight would be as dull as torture. This can be likened to the difference between running on a treadmill and running outdoors. Looking at it from a neuroscience perspective rather than exercise physiology, walking outdoors is generally more enjoyable than on a treadmill because the brain has to process more information and deal with real-time variables, creating a slight sense of tension.

To apply this concept to a game and make it enjoyable, it is desirable to design paths that make the brain slightly uncomfortable and tense by not being able to predict what will happen next. So, the initial exploration phase, which resembles a typical house, feels more boring than suspenseful as it involves familiar information. However, the game doesn't just make you walk aimlessly. Messages displayed in the direction the player is going provides guidance and maintaining a sense of tension at the same time. Moreover, opening doors or turning levers is done through mouse actions that simulate real-life movements, enhancing immersion.

The pinnacle of level design is reflected in the maze design inside the house. 'Placing dark areas and narrow passages that require crawling' instinctively puts the players on edge and prevents them from predicting the path they need to take, providing constant stimulation to maintain tension.


- Overwhelming Story and Direction -
Despite not including extreme action elements, the mysterious story and corresponding direction overpower everything. I felt a unique blend of tranquility like drifting on calm waters and fear arising from overwhelming awe.

In brief about story, the one revolves around the cursed Finch family, where everyone except one sibling from each generation meets their demise for various reasons. Even if they manage to avoid death, the survivors in the previous generation inexplicably die once they have offspring. Despite such tragic circumstances, the mystery surrounding the Finch family, which does not allow the bloodline to be severed, can literally be called a 'curse'.

The last survivor, Edith Finch, returns to the abandoned family's mansion to investigate the curse that plagues the Finch lineage. Remarkably, the mansion preserves the rooms once used by the deceased family members, along with their wills. In the will, the various family members' processes of accepting death or the circumstances leading to their deaths were written.

When the protagonist reads the will, the perspective shifts from the protagonist to the writer of the will or those around them, and the process of death is portrayed through mini-games. These mini-games are truly intuitive and innovative, adding immersion to the writer's historical background, situation, and emotions.

- Conclusion -
The intriguing storyline that arouses curiosity, the skillful balance between spine-chilling mystery and avoiding true horror, the striking and unconventional mini-games, and the atmospheric background music that sets the dramatic mood can only be described as perfect.

Through this work, I reflect on my own prejudice against walking simulation games. If you are someone who holds biases against walking simulation games like I used to, I strongly recommend playing this game. It will change your perception.\t\t\t\t\t
