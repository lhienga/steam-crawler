Edith Finch is Gone Home done right.

Gone Home was a disappointing game. The premise seemed interesting and the gameplay mechanics had a lot of promise, but the game ended up being a not just a bore, but also poorly written. The big 'mystery' was a dud, the subject matter was terrible, the game committed the biggest sin of bad writing in that it decided to "tell" rather than "show", and it just wasn't fun to play. There was a lot of potential in Gone Home, but it failed at that, and was even more frustrating because the critics praised the game purely over its subject matter rather than its quality (or lack thereof).

That's why What Remains of Edith Finch was such a breath of fresh air.

What Remains of Edith Finch (I'll just shorten it to EF) is essentially the same game as Gone Home, but vastly improved in every conceivable way. You're a character returning to a mysterious house and you spend the next 2-3 hours exploring the house, learning about its unseen characters, and solving the mysteries of what happened there.

And yet EF is so much better than Gone Home was. EF is so much more well written, with interesting characters, amazing backstories, and it's just dripping with creativity everywhere you look. It understands how to "show" rather than just "tell" and it even introduces all sorts of cool gameplay mechanics for all the backstories, which is a vast improvement over Gone Home's endless journals and audio logs.

On top of that, EF is just a beautiful game artistically. There is so much detail everywhere you look, that I can't imagine how much effort the devs must have put into lovingly crafting every single room. Some of the gameplay mechanics work better than others (ie. the Lewis story's mechanics were brilliant while the Gregory story mechanics felt a bit clunky). Make sure you play with a controller.


The whole game is over in about 3 hours (perhaps 2 if you're quick), but it's absolutely worth playing and experiencing. It's a very short game, so I can't give it an A+, but this is just about the best possible score I can give to a short indie game of this nature.

Of the 5 'walking simulators' I've played recently, I'd rank them (from best to worst):

1. SOMA
2. What Remains of Edith Finch
3. Firewatch
4. Dear Esther
5. Gone Home

I absolutely loved SOMA and Edith Finch, I thought Firewatch was a mixed bag with some very strong areas (voice acting) and some weak areas, I thought Dear Esther was a pleasant but unremarkable 90 minutes, and I found Gone Home to be very disappointing.

What Remains of Edith Finch is a must-play 3 hour artistic experience. I'll absolutely be playing through this again in the near future.

8.6/10\t\t\t\t\t
