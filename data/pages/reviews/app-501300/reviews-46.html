\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am conflicted about this game. I liked it a lot, but the ending put me off so much that I've stayed on a negative impression.

The goal is to explore the old family house, and the house is a twisting maze of secret passages, hidden rooms, and all-around chaos. But despite the apparent disorder, the game always points you in the right direction, and usually the place you want to go to is the one you haven't seen yet. I had to do very little backtracking and it was way easier than expected to remember the way.

Each deceased family member you reconnect with in their respective room has a specific and bizarre story to tell. It is never made clear whether the supernatural elements of the stories are real, or whether they're just exaggerations or metaphors. This contribute to a general feeling of uneasiness, but morbid fascination will keep you going. (And it IS morbid : some stories are incredibly sad and dark.) Each story also has specific gameplay elements and mechanics that are unique to it, and sometimes even a specific artistic direction completely different from the actual game. So it's a collection of mini-games, which makes the otherwise uneventful exploration fun and interesting.

There are however several things I disliked. First, in-between the stories about various family member, there are memories of Edith and her grandmother that seem to frame everything else, that seem to be the main conflict to resolve, and at the end... nothing. This story gets completely forgotten, ends up abruptly without answers. It feels unfinished, and unsatisfying.

There's also the reveal. I feel like the game relied a lot on its reveal to conclude the story, except it gives you enough clues to see it coming from halfway through. So the reveal fell flat for me, and when you explore this particular story it's about a teenage girl who is pregnant (so she's probably been raped), and you play an extremely tacky birth sequence from the point of view of the baby only for the girl to die giving birth. That felt incredibly disrespectful, that was unneeded, I felt awful. Because unlike other stories where tragedies were treated as tragedies, here it was all about how happy this literal child was at dying for her baby, which is an incredibly disturbing message to end the game with.

I'm glad I played it, but I won't touch it again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best walking simulator I've ever played. I went in blind and was pleasantly surprised. If you don't like walking simulators then this one probably won't change that, but it has a lot of clever surprises and a surprisingly dark sense of humor that keeps it entertaining throughout.

My only gripe is that certain actions/triggers move you onto the next section of the game and it can be kind of difficult at first to figure out which interactives are these sorts of triggers so I missed a few things early on in the game because I didn't know yet how the game worked. Besides that, though, I recommend going in blind and not spoiling anything.

Also, it's only a couple hours long, but is entertaining throughout. Once I started it I figured I'd play for a little bit then do something else, but I was compelled by the story to keep playing to see where it would go next and ended up finishing it in one sitting. Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
