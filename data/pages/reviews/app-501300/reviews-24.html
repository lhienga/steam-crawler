Eh.
What Remains of Edith Finch is a visually impressive tech demo with a great deal of detail put into it, but a good story or game, it is not. It's like going through a departed family member's belongings after the funeral, but somebody else's family who you don't care about. You very briefly get a glimpse into the life of one of a dozen people, often with fantastical elements thrown in ("I ate a bunch of toxic stuff, then I was a cat, then I was an owl, then I was a shark, then I was a sea monster, then I was a little girl again"), but just as quickly the game shuffles you off to the next one in its very linear path. There is no real overarching story beyond, "gosh, this multi-generational family has a really terrible life expectancy" (largely a product of neglectful parenting and a hereditary lack of common sense, not anything supernatural). I think the ending was supposed to be sad, but the game never gives you a reason to care about the characters, so it just lands flat.

In terms of gameplay, you are locked on to a rail like a Disney dark ride. The player character walks very, very slowly, an intentional move so you can admire the photorealistic environments (and possibly to pad it out just over the two hour refund threshold). Yet the game will often override the camera to make you look at the next checkpoint in the sequence. Everything feels stiff, a chore to get through and there's nothing you will want to return to after playing through once — which you will do in about two to three hours, even if you take your time examining every trinket.

This might make a decent VR demo. As is, though, I really don't understand the glowing reviews or $20 regular price tag. I bought it steeply discounted on sale and I still feel a bit cheated.\t\t\t\t\t
