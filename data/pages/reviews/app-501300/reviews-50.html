\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're in for a really novel experience in immersion and boundary-pushing gameplay, this is a really nice game. It's beautiful, it's always inventing new ways to tell a story and it has quite a few really compelling breathtaking moments that you will remember. It's short, sure, but it has something not many games have: a personality.
Now I'm going to rant a bit, because I'm fairly disappointed with one thing: the story. I don't want to spoil, but the game is making a point of being "mysterious": talking of family curses, old weird house, and so on. But the set up never pays off. Nothing is explained of course, but more so, nothing really ever comes out of it. The mysterious parts are here only to keep you playing without ever having anything to tell. The story in itself would be catastrophic if told as a book or a movie and as such, I can't recommend it much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWalking simulators aren't usually my genre of preference, and I have tried dozens of others, but THIS IS THE ONLY GAME that has caused me to want to explore walking sims more. 'What Remains of Edith Finch' is a story about mysterious circumstances of the lives of members of the Finch family throughout history. You play as each of aforementioned members (in addition to a few other characters), solve puzzles, perform interesting tasks, explore beautifully crafted spaces, and enjoy a well told story.

My thorough enjoyment of this game had less to do with the puzzles themselves (although they were alot of fun and sometimes hilarious). The story, the interactivity of every room / space, and how beautifully those things came together was very rewarding. It's too bad I discovered this game so early in my exploring its genre, because I get the feeling I won't be finding another walking sim that's as beautiful and interesting as "What Remains of Edith Finch".

I really really hope there's a sequel.\t\t\t\t\t
