\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate: WRoEF is FANTASTIC! Beautifully realized visuals, lovely atmospheric music, compelling story. I'd describe it as an "interactive experience," rather than a game, but in the end, I'm glad to have experienced it. So... why the Thumbs down? Because (as I documented in my original review below), the camera motion induces SEVERE motion sickness_and that is something that isn't forgivable. To be fair, I found that (as suggested by others likewise affected) enabling the reticule, taking frequent breaks, and limiting "play" time, enabled me to complete the game--though I'd still feel queasy.

Old review: I _really_want to play this game. Big problem though: after a few minutes of play the head-bobbing/breathing/floating camera leaves me wanting to hurl and as far as I can tell, the devs have not provided any means to disable that "feature." A quick search reveals I am not the only person this game leaves violently motion sick. Devs, if you are reading this, I''ve no doubt you are mighty proud of the camera motion. That said, PLUHLEAZE have pity on us poor (wanna be) players and provide a setting to disable it.\t\t\t\t\t
