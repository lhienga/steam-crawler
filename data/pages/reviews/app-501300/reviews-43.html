\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest story ever, presented in the best way ever = best game ever in its category by miles (and one of the best games ever, period). The story is broken down into about a dozen individual interactive stories, each of which COMPLETELY change the pace into overdrive (and each in a different way). It is very naturally gradual (which is fine because that teetering monolith of a house is beautifully drawn and interesting to explore), but It really starts to pick up towards the end. You'll laugh, you'll cry, and you will be tripped out.

It's incredible (and frustrating) that in six years no game of this genre has come close. Some indie studios with like 4 people (Eastshade - another leader of its genre - was made by ONE person) have tried, and have done good things, but the lack of money flying around often leads to lesser production values et al. This game is polished (the only other game that matches it is Firewatch), and I'm sure it was helpful having a company like Annapurna behind them for it. Oh well, there's obviously way more money in FPS and Madden games, and that ain't gonna change.

Anyway yeah Edith Finch is a must-play. It has the impact of great movie or album, only in a new and totally unique way. I found it not exactly replayable, because by the end the whole thing was just heavy...I was "ok, I got it". Mind blown.\t\t\t\t\t
