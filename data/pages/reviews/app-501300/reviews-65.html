All of my English professors LOVED it, as did fellow graduates, so I think my opinion on the game should be pretty obvious if I was able to prattle on for around 30 pages.

My paper was also centralized around the elements of goth and tragedy through literary and psychoanalytic lenses in order to properly analyze the archived experiences of life, death, and mental health. It was through this game that I was able to cement the efficacy of maintaining positive and communicative relations with people; acknowledge the power and weight of stories; and most importantly, underline the significance of maintaining mental health, no matter the circumstances of the outer world.

Mental health is SO important, and finally getting to play this game for myself really drove that truth home. Don't be like me and ignore your mental health for 2 decades lol-- Otherwise you'll end up resonating with Lewis as hard as I did.

(PS. If you have seen/read my paper, please do not say my birth name in front of millions of steam users XD)

TL;DR Go play this game if you haven't already, it's so worth every precious minute, and an absolute treasure I've finally grown up enough to cherish.
