What Remains of Edith Finch is a story based on the Finchs', a family is believed to be cursed due to all the unfortunate deaths that occur in their family. You play as Edith, the last Finch alive, who returns to her hauntingly beautiful (and absolutely humongous) childhood house and explores the rooms of her dead family members. Each room has its own theme and story as to how that family member died, and none of the representations of their deaths are the same as the last. Each story had it's own unique way of explaining the death of whichever family member's memoir you visited.

These stories are so haunting to hear, but are explained in such poetic, mysterious and beautiful ways. The narrative text captivates your attention by moving around with you as you're exploring the house, and the music is extremely fitting for the atmosphere. The game does a phenomenal job making you feel nostalgic but also creeped out/sad simultaneously, which perfectly captivates the theme of the game in general.

I cannot stress this enough, this game can only be played once, but it's one of those games that just sticks with you. (It also strongly reminds me of the game That Dragon, Cancer for anybody who's played that game, as well.)\t\t\t\t\t
