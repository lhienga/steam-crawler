Perhaps that goes without saying, but I emphasize that because this game is not quite 'gamifying' the skill claw experience, it is literally simulating it, but doing it surprisingly well in that regard.

The Good:
- The physics generally are good, clean, the collision is better than you might imagine it would be and after collecting 50 of the 200 prizes, I very rarely felt like I was missing items due to a collision issue (though it does happen).
- The variety of prizes is very charming, there's a lot of pop culture references to be found among the typical generic prizes you'd see in claw machines today, so plenty of geometry to consider in a way that you can move onto what feels like a different experience after spending a while trying to capture a specifically shaped prize
- The controls are simple and clean, and there are QOL options that don't feel like they're "making it easy for you" rather than just saving your time: laser pointer is helpful so you don't have to keep switching sides, but it won't help you deal with the amount of slack in the claw, so it's a good but minor tool; You can also choose not to reset the claw if you haven't grabbed a prize, again, just a cool QOL option to save you time without making you feel like you're cheating necessarily.
- The subtle difficulty change: two options here in 'Fun' and 'Realistic', but realistic doesn't feel overly brutal, merely that you need to get a little more spot on and the claw strength isn't ridiculous. It can create some teeth-gritting moments, the biggest different being when trying to grab the Emoji balls, as they are essentially the same geometry as the claw itself, and so best exhibit the change in the difficulty, as 'Fun' will have little problem grabbing them on the first try.

THE BAD:
- Only one machine, this probably speaks for itself.
- Lack of 'slack' options; when the claw drops it has far more slack than even the bottom of the cabinet, so with each drop you have to consider how the claw is going to flop all along what you're trying to grab, which feels like an impossible variable to account for often, especially on the Realistic difficulty. Ideally, there would be an option for a claw that stops dropping when it makes collision with anything in the cabinet, or a slider to adjust the amount of slack after collision is first made.
- The collectible items feels imbalanced; as you level up, you get access to more possible items whenever you refill the machine (every 5 prizes collected), the pool of starting items, however, contains several oddly shaped items that *feel* impossible or extremely difficult to collection, like the 'Not Companion Cube from Portal" or a particularly large-headed octopus. What this means is, as you play, these just sit in your machine and you grow to tire of looking at them. Ideally, these would be some of the final prizes you unlock for refill, given their collection difficulty.
- No 're-roll' options, to mix up the prizes in the cabinet; you can re-launch the game however and it will save the prizes in your cabinet but randomly drop them in the machine again, great that the function is there at all but it should be a button in-game.
- Perhaps a little more expensive than it should be for only having one machine and no alternate claw options.

Overall, this really scratched an itch for me, but I feel there are prizes that I would probably never bother trying to collect while playing, I may come back to it every so often to avoid blowing another $100 on claw game sites, but in that regard would LOVE to see UFO and other machine options in this one!\t\t\t\t\t
