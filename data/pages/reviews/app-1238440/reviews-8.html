\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this DLC because it sounds like it should be an improvement over the Arctic pack, and it is, in some ways. However:

1. This pack is still just as overpriced as the Arctic pack, if not moreso because it has no scenarios, and, as I'll explain below, doesn't actually have 250 new pieces. This pack charges you over twice what the basegame does per animal, and that's not taking into account all the things the basegame gives that this DLC doesn't.
2. None of the new foliage is climbable. This sucks because we have all these new trees and roots that should be climable, and take up a lot of space, but the animals can't use them for climbing. To further add insult to injury, two of the animals in the pack need climbing space, so it's like, why even bother using the new foliage when the animals can't even make use of it? It'd be one thing if it was just flowers and shrubs, but six of the new plants in the pack are trees, and then there's a bunch of additional strangler fig roots as well as a few of the trees themselves in the update, and none of them can be used for climbing. Everything climable in this pack is a very basic pole or wood piece that took no effort to make climbable. It feels lazy giving us so many new trees, and animals that need climbing area, and then not letting them use the foliage designed for their pack.
3. The "250 new objects" is only true on a technicality. They fail to mention in that 250 new objects, that most of those are just recolorable versions of other objects in the pack, or, with the case for many of the temple pieces, are golden variants. I didn't go through to count how many actually new pieces there are, but I'd wager it's less than 100, since all of the bamboo pieces have two variants, and many of the temple pieces have three variants. This is practically false advertising. That said, all the actually new pieces are very nice, and I do think I'll be using them. The new thatch pieces and the bamboo poles in particular are going to be useful.
4. The animal's stats and behaviors are just outright wrong and weird. The guests are able to enter the giant anteater exhibit in spite of them being known to be dangerous and having killed people, even in zoos. Anteaters are notorious for being dangerous, and have killed many people, even in captivity, so I'd argue this is even dangerous, as someone may get the wrong idea that it's safe to walk up to an anteater and get seriously hurt. On the opposite end of this, the llamas cannot be used for walkthroughs, even though what other purpose does a domestic llama have in a zoo than for being a petting zoo animal? I'd take my chances with a llama over an anteater any day. Then there's the jaguar, which for some reason needs climbing space, even though lions and tigers don't, and yet despite being the most aquatic of the big cats, they don't need water area. It just seems like stats were thrown at a wall and they decided what stuck, and none of it makes any sense.

tl;dr, the dlc is overpriced, the foliage feels rushed, there isn't actually 250 new pieces, someone is gonna get mauled by an anteater if they follow Frontier's advice. Please, stay away from anteaters. They are dangerous animals.\t\t\t\t\t
