\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautiful pack - I love it! Sure you only get 4 animals, but for the price of a hot drink and cake at a coffee shop (which would take me like 20 minutes to eat as opposed to the hours I'll spend playing this), it's pretty good! The scenery pieces are great too - there are some really lovely items that I know I'll use loads. My only criticism is the South American tropical zoo map has been changed from a flat map to a pre-sculpted map. Don't get me wrong, this is exactly what I wanted - the option to start out a sandbox with a terrain map - but this map is practically unusable. It's the top of a mountain... the top! The terrain is far too steep as it is to do anything. Plus, in real life, who would build a zoo on the peak of a mountain?! It's like Frontier are saying 'You want terrain? Well, here's TERRAIN!'. Would have been much better if it had been a valley with gentle slopes, maybe some cliffs.

TL;DR - beautiful animals, brilliant scenery, definitely worth the money if you've got it, but the new pre-sculpted terrain map for the South American tropical biome is an amazing idea but a bit wacky and could have been executed better\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA bit misleading, the negative comments are less about the quality of the DLC and more about the pricing for such a DLC. Those reviews are abundant, most are well-written and I don't need to repeat it.

What is in this pack is mostly excellent.

Do you want the animals shown? Well, you're definitely going to get them and they're lovely additions, with cute animations. But if you're curious, below follows my nitpicking and what I loved. I tend to go on, but the bulletin points should give you the gist of it.

Positives:

- The Animals

While not many they are beautiful, with lovely animations. There are enough of them are being put out in Franchise so that you won't sit around just waiting to get your new animals into your zoo. Unlike many white animals, a black Jaguar isn't too hard to nab even this early.

I want to add more here, but I can't come up with anything. Keep in mind that I do recommend this DLC, moving on to cons, and that they've yet to really bother me.


Negatives:

- Low quality textures on new items

Unlike the building additions from the Arctic Pack, there are a few items of unforgivably low quality for a DLC. If you look at the base game "log" fences, walls and roofs, then from close up they're shaded nicely enough. If you look at the bamboo walls and roofs from this pack close up, they look like someone put stickers on plaster. I hope and believe they'll polish that up, because despite not being much of a builder this was more disappointing to me than the price vs content. I knew I was walking into that.


- Price vs Quantity

No, there's not a heckload of stuff. I like that I'm funding the great free updates, however, and I wish that maybe Frontier was more outright in saying that that is what these do and vow to combine the packs into larger and less expensive ones in the future.


- Inaccurate animal facts and in-game behaviour.

I'll split this up, because there are a few, and so you can skim the bulletins.

These things have been a thing since base game. I'm still irked about the zebra packs. I have seen those shows where you migrate in the hundreds, Sara - so stop hoofing your mother in the face because there's EIGHT of you! >:c


- Please Don't Pet The Llama, Do Go Right Ahead And Chill With The Anteater

Llamas, that have lived with humans for ages, people cannot enter into exhibits with. I can understand this, as I'm not sure I'd let humans freely walk into any horse or cow pen depending on the breed and individuals.
But it feels as though they definitely wanted an animal that did allow entry, and Llama's would have been the natural choice. Possibly capuchin.

Not the odd choice: Giant Anteaters.

I would rather get spit in the eye, than mauled by those claws - thanks.

(Props to a person in the DLC news comments for hammering this home - my only previous knowledge of giant anteaters is from Steven King, and I don't think his tales are entirely factual. ;D They did instill that anteaters are not to be trifled with, though, so close enough!)


- Jaguars NEED TO CLIMB, DAMMIT

Except outside of Planet Zoo they really don't?

Leopards will even drag their prey into trees in nature, while Jaguars are such apex predators that they'll happily chow down on the ground - five feet from a hungry 'gator. Or crocodile. Or a gathering of hungry caimans. Leopards need climbing areas, when we get them, Jaguars not so much.
This isn't really an issue (unless you build the coolest cliff with a cave that takes up most of the enclosure and- oh, you need to climb. ***ing great. =.= Let me just remodel entirely. (That's totally not on me for not reading up enough on the species. 9.9))

Now - These types of animal inaccuracies have been corrected in the base game (which can be frustrating when you've set up enclosures based on the old rules of interaction) based on feedback.

I hope and believe this gets fixed as well. Until then I will pride myself on my line of hyper-friendly anteaters... who, probably shouldn't be ever released into the wild, now that I think on it. Imagine them getting on their hind-legs to greet someone with a hug... "Huug meee, frieeend~ *Tongue flicker*"

Imagine all the dead anteaters and South American tourists with weak hearts!



All in all, I think this is a solid pack, the contents of which everyone with base game got a good 50% of in the free update. And personally, I'm okay with that. Next time, I can't afford a DLC and get a bunch of neat stuff to cheer me up! :) \t\t\t\t\t
