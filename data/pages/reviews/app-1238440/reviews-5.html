\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanna start out by saying, that i absolutely freaking love this game! It was my most anticipated game of 2019, and it did not disappoint. I liked the Arctic expansion, and i think that 9,99 euro, is pretty fair for the dlc’s, and don't mind paying it to support the developer, they make great games, in my opinion, and i wanna see them expand on Planet Zoo like they did Planet Coaster. BUT, if you have to choose between the Arctic dlc or the South America dlc, i would recommend the Arctic one. So here are my thoughts one the South America dlc:

Negative:
No new scenarios/career
- This is my absolute biggest complaint about the dlc. The Arctic dlc had 2 new scenarios, this one has zero, and i was so freaking disappointed by that. I loved playing through the career mode in the base game, i think it's great, and makes you engaged in what you are doing, and learning the game. In the dlc’s i think that it is a great way to introduce the new animals. This dlc does not have that.
Scenery pieces
- 250 new scenery pieces, let's be honest, it's more like 100 new scenery pieces, and the rest are reskins.

Positive:
The animals
- The new animals that this dlc adds are great, no doubt that they put a lot of work into them.
Scenery pieces
- Wait, wasn't this under negatives? Yes, but as someone who puts a lot of work into the scenery of my parks, it's still very nice to have new scenery to play around with and add to your park, and a lot of the scenery pieces are really nice.

So to sum up, if you have the 9,99 euro to spend, and love the game, get the dlc, and support the developer, if you have to choose between getting the Arctic dlc or the South American one, get the Arctic one.

If the developer where to add a new scenario to this dlc i would definitely give it a positive review.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I know there's a bit of contention about this and the Arctic Pack... First off, if you're interested in the theme, animals, or just want more content, go ahead and give this pack a go. I personally think that the animal variety is better than the arctic pack (which were pretty much all large animals with fairly similar needs) and the scenery pieces are beyond awesome in their look and versatility.

While I do agree that this pack seems very limited animal-wise (especially for an entire continent like South America), we have to realize it is difficult to model, code, and animal new animals, especially when they're unique from the others, like the ones in this pack. If we are patient enough, maybe Frontier might release some extra animals in free updates (personally hoping for an Arctic Fox and Sloth to round out the existing DLC) but again, that's a huge process to get one animal in the game.

And potentially, later down the road, Frontier might start adding purely animal-packs like they did with the Magnificent Rides Pack and Classic Rides Pack in PlanCo. But Frontier shouldn't neglect the other communities in order to add more animals. As a builder, I want to be able to have a theme set that matches my animals, so say, having to pay for an Australian Animal Pack that has like 6-10 new species but no build sets would make me and I'm sure other builders left out. Yes, this is a zoo game, but it's also a Planet game, and some of us came over to Planet Zoo because of the building and creative possibilities PlanCo introduced us to. And yes, technically we can go back, but we want to play with the animals too.

So, TLDR, get the pack if you're interested, it's worth the $10. But if you're worried about the animal number, understand that the Planet Zoo community is diverse, and the devs have to do a lot of work just to add one new species into the game.\t\t\t\t\t
