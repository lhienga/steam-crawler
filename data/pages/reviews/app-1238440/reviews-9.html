\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will preface this by saying I am a builder first and foremost. I bought this pack for the build items, as for me the animals are secondary. While a agree that four animals isn't enough, you've probably heard that enough by now. I'll instead talk about the build items.

PROS:
+ Two very versatile build sets. I have wanted a bamboo set since the game came out. While I was thinking of using it in Asian themed builds, it works wonders for a tropical setting too. The addition of a third -recolourable- stone set is a must have in my opinion. Especially since there is only two other stone build sets in the game without this DLC. The stone ruins are also unique, and allow one to make interesting disheveled buildings and habitat pieces.

+The decals are beautiful. From glowing butterfly lights to gorgeous and colourful wall accessories, this set is both unique and well made. The bench and bins are also particularly detailed and pretty, if a bit tropical-specific.

+ I love love love the new trees and foliage, as well as the fallen tree and root decors. You can make buildings look detailed and overgrown much easier than before. There was a huge lack of tropical flowers in planet zoo before and this pack solves that problem with a whole host of large colourful garden accessories.

MEH
- While I enjoy the palm thatched roof set, its very similar to the African thatched roof. If you don't have this DLC, the base game thatching will work fine in any build requiring such roofing.

CON
- Only two statues and no new fountains. The pack is distinctly lacking here.
-Most of the species enrichment items are re-skins. I would have liked to have seen more enrichment items for the new animals.
-Only four animals and a glorified box with a frog in it.

FINAL THOUGHTS AND IDEAS
Overall, as a builder, this pack is a big plus. You could use all of the new building sets in many different biomes and for many different scenarios. The arctic pack feels limiting to me, because I rarely use its items outside distinctly 'Nordic' themed buildings. The tropical foliage always felt lacking to me, but this pack makes creating a realistic tropical zoo much easier. The only thing that's lacking is that center pieces (statues, fountains) and the themed props. For ten bucks, I feel it's worth the price.

As a small side-note, I would suggest creating 'build' packs and 'animal' packs. The build packs add new themes and build items to the game and the 'animal' packs are focused on adding new animals and habitat items. People play the game for different reasons and therefore should be able to buy packs that suit their wants specifically.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing into this I was unsure wether the review would end up being positive or negative. Given I ranked the arctic pack positive it feels wrong to give this more solid pack anything less, but as a great many reviews have pointed out there are some much needed changes for these DLCS that aren't being met or addressed here.

Simply put, if you like planet zoo you are almost certainly going to purchase this and will enjoy what content you do receive from it. Unless you are really pressed for cash 10 bucks for 4 new animals (About $2.50 per animal) is a pretty fine deal and some of the scenery is quite nice as well.

That said, it suffers from the major issues that everyone else is mentioning that are very worth bringing up. The arctic pack had only four animals and one of those four was a literal reskin of a base game animal (timber wolf into arctic wolf). It was very dissapointing to many, myself included, but given that there were really only one or two other arctic animals that people wanted that didn't make it in, and that we got polar bears which were sorely missed from launch as well as the lovely dall sheep made up for it. The pack came out very shortly after launch and nobody knew wether or not it was representing the future for all DLCS or was simply a smaller pack made relatively quickly to get some nice new animals and scenery out for eager fans. Several months later we now see that it was not a one-time thing and the package deal is 4 animals and a scenery set from a given region.

Literally every single person I know of when the roster was revealed said 'Wait there aren't any capybaras?' South America isn't a small region with a handful of animals, it's an entire continent and one of the most diverse for animal life in the world. Not only lacking one of the most deisred flagship species but countless others like sloths, armadillos, ocelots, bush dogs, maned wolves, the numerous large reptiles that could serve as habitat specimens, coatimundis, almost a dozen unique rodents like agoutis, maras and porcupines, and so many more.

This dlc could have, and should have, been the starting point for more substantial packs. Instead of a mere four animals from a region that doubtlessly will exclude flagship species, major regions should have much larger rosters. A minimum of 8 seems far more reasonable, especially considering that the base game only had 2 south american animals and 0 arctic animals. Fans of the game have to wonder now if we will never see a capybara, a sloth, an armadillo, or any of the other animals native to this region. And that's only considering land animals , which brings me to the second issue going forwards.

I can't help but feel that the dlcs going forwards need to be more, add more, to the game and not just be a few additional items or animals. South America is well known for it's birds (They are even displayed in the decor in this pack). This could have been the perfect place to introduce aviaries and birds. Macaws, Toucans, Harpy Eagles, Ibises and countless others. Aviaries would be an excellent way to add to the gameplay without completely revamping the entire game or needing to change other parts of the game enormously.

To put it simply, for what this dlc is it is a fine price for some lovely animals and nice decor, but it could have and I think should have been so much more, and I hope for the future of PZ going forwards that the developers consider more expensive but more fulfilling packs. 2.50 for an animal is, I think, perfectly reasonable and I think almost all the players who purchase this pack would readily pay double the price for double the animals. A future of more expanded features like aviaries, carousels, or interactive feeding stations (just like what frontier did for Zoo Tycoon) along with more animals would not only make the community happier, but I think bring more people to the game and result in more monetary sucess for PZ's development\t\t\t\t\t
