\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaven't played the original nor have I put in a ton of hours yet, but the game is really fun and well worth the price tag.

Few personal complaints:

- Lockpicking and pickpocketing mini-games feel a bit clunky and unnatural (This might also be due to potato pc).
- NPCs act extremely unnatural, but that's basically signature of this type of game and asking for true to life NPCs under a $20 price tag would be pretty unrealistic.
- I feel like the hiding from cops cooldown could be a bit shorter, but maybe this is an upgradeable skill down the road? Vanilla cooldown feels way too long though. Too many times I've gotten caught, hid in dumpster, walked away from PC to do something else while the chase ended for a couple of minutes.
- It doesn't feel fair to talk about replayability when I've only played like 3 hours, but after robbing the same guy in house 103 a few times, I can see the game becoming a bit repetitive down the road. Again, repetitiveness is kind of signature of the genre and most fans are well aware of this.

Steamdeck:

- Game runs well on Steamdeck on medium settings. High settings WILL hard crash the whole console though.
- Controller support feels smooth and only had to adjust sensitivity settings a bit.\t\t\t\t\t
