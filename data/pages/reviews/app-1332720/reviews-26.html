\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel pretty lukewarm about this game. It honestly feels like if you took games like Skyrim, Deus Ex, Kingdom Come, or any game that has optional thievery - and you just boiled the game down to thievery only. It's not really that in depth for it to be called a simulator and as you play it feels like the challenge gets removed completely in favor of the quick satisfaction of robbing successive houses. You don't really do much planning and the game is begging you to exploit it with respawning loot. The risk is almost non existent as prison can be easily escaped and money can be stored in a bank to avoid losing it (only on hard mode this happens). The first few hours of the game were interesting but a few hours in the game loop becomes exposed and repetitive. It's still kind of fun but I think I have had just as much fun or more being a thief in games like Skyrim. Any of course with any "simulator" game, it lacks A LOT of polish, feeling like more of an overly developed prototype.

But for only 20 dollars on release I think it's hard to criticize too much. It's not an incredible game but it knows what it's worth. If you like thievery and stealth in other games, chances are this is worth the $.\t\t\t\t\t
