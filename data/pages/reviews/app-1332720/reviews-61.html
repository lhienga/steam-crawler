\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright I have to update my review after playing for a while, because this took a turn for the worse

The Good...

1. Music Design- Omg, everytime I hear a soundtrack in this game it just makes me think its better, I dont know what they did, but they managed to make each soundtrack in the game, whether that be chase music, ambient music, radio music, its all top notch and I would say can surpass some triple A game music, its all done so well.

2. The AI- While later in this review I rant about some bad aspects of AI in the game, however its a step up from the first game and when its not extremely OP, its really well done, with a lot of different people doing different things and reacting differently to you, this wasnt done great in the last game, but its much improved now

3. World- This game took the great thief simulator 1 world and upgraded it so much, instead of just making it bigger, which they did, there are now shops, stores, other locations besides houses that you can enter which make the game feel much more realistic and immersive to play.

Unfortunately, now its time to get to the bad


The Bad...

1. AWFUL GRAPHICS- I dont know what happened, but this is the #1 ugliest game ive ever seen, from the clouds, to the outside lighting that is extremely dark at 12pm with max brightness, to the awful inside lighting, the disgusting 2d foliage, and all free low quality assets. I cant bare to look at it. Going back to Thief Simulator 1 was like night and day, i dont know how they made the graphics so bad, and it doesnt even run well at all, being much worse than the first game, while looking like this.

2. Free Assets- While I understand that games should be allowed to use free assets, as a developer I use them myself, however thats pretty much all the games background items are. Characters are from Fuse, Animations are from mixamo, items in the world are from a marketplace or model website such as Sketchfab or CGtrader.

3. Some AI Aspects- Overall this game has amazing AI, however things like AI despawning when leaving houses, AI seeing you instantly through walls, and absolutely each time your detected the cops are called. Overall the perception of the AI is extremely OP, given the results of getting caught, and when getting caught, the AI can catch you through walls, through ceilings, through stairwells, overall the way the AI interacts with the player is overpowered, and should be addressed.\t\t\t\t\t
