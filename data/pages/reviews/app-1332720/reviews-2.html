A Step Backward from the Original Thief Simulator



Thief Simulator 2 promised much with its new additions, but unfortunately, it regresses in several key areas, overshadowing the excitement of its innovations. Here, I delve into the disappointing aspects of the game:



-Lack of a van: While the addition of a sports car is appreciated, the absence of a customizable van feels like a missed opportunity.
-Limited shelter customization: Part 1 allowed for intricate home customization, but here, we're stuck with pre-placed furniture and an inability to add our own touch.
-Inability to place items in the shelter: This restriction is not only frustrating but also diminishes the sense of personalization in our hideout.
-Doors can't be closed at the shelter: It's a small detail, but one that adds to the immersion and was present in the first installment.
-Restricted item carrying: The already limited item ammount from the first game feels even more constrained now, sometimes preventing us from picking up items that should be accessible.
-Reduction in shelters and customization options: Having only one shelter and limited customization options feels like a step back from the previous game's offerings.
-Shorter story and easier gameplay: The diminished difficulty and shorter storyline detract from the overall experience.
-Decreased variety of items and paintings: The lack of diversity in items and paintings within neighborhoods is noticeable and disappointing.
-Interface and storage issues: The storage interface remains clunky, lacking essential features like search functionality and struggling with performance issues.
-Limitations in carrying capacity and item interactions: More flexibility in carrying items and interacting with environments would enhance gameplay.
Unrealistic item genericness: Items feel overly generic, lacking the uniqueness found in real life.
-Underutilization of game mechanics: New systems like the drone feature or the gun feel underdeveloped and could benefit from additional content.
-Lack of challenge in the phone missions: Time missions feel too easy and are doable in one third of the time and fail to provide a satisfying challenge.
-Surplus of in-game currency with limited use: After a certain point, accumulating wealth becomes meaningless due to a lack of meaningful ways to spend it.
-Inconvenience in heists: Requiring multiple trips for heists feels tedious and could be streamlined for a more enjoyable experience.
-Missed opportunities for theft: The inability to steal certain items like furniture diminishes the immersion and potential for creative gameplay.



And a few other small points/wishes:
-The second neighborhood could have its own local police station (jail).
-There's a need for more buyable storage options beyond the current limit of 999 kg.
Introducing a skill or mechanism to carry more than four shots for the gun would enhance gameplay.
-Limited interactivity with items in heists and jail scenarios, such as being unable to open drawers.
-More detailed kitchen interactions are desired, as currently, only one kitchen allows full access to doors.



Overall, Thief Simulator 2 offers some new mechanics but falls short as a sequel due to the absence or downgrade of essential features from its predecessor. While it remains an enjoyable game on its own, those familiar with the first thief simulator may find these regressions disappointing\t\t\t\t\t
