\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game, but if Steam had ratings then I'd give it a lower one than the first Thief Simulator. Some of the added mechanics add some more variety to the gameplay, but others are under utilized. I beat the game having only disassembled like 2 pieces of jewelry. The customization aspect has been stripped bare in that you can't decorate your home with what you steal or modify it much other than a few paintings. Taking apart cars is trivially easy compared to before, which can be good or bad depending on how you felt about it before. Story in the last game was already pretty simple and bland, but this game's is even worse with a wet fart of an ending. The game also has some annoying bugs like people seeing you and noticing stolen items through walls.

Basically, Thief Simulator 2 is more of the same, but less of it. If you wanted more Thief Simulator then I recommend it, but don't go in expecting a vastly improved experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthief sim 2 is a great game that expands on what the first one accomplished in many ways, most importantly with the addition of skills/ abilities and a slew of optional objectives that will test your finesse and how easily you can think on your feet. you start off the game essentially as a bumbling goon with nothing but a crowbar and your limited human eyes, but by the end of the game you can sneak around telepathically marking all nearby cameras and seeing through walls while your invisible associate hauls a 10kg tv back to your truck. that doesn't mean you're invincible tho, npcs will notice certain missing items, and unlocked/ open doors/ windows that weren't that way before, among other intricacies.

its not without its issues and bugs, which are promised to be worked out in addition to the dev still creating new content. however as this is a $20 game created by one guy and not a triple A game studio, one shouldn't expect this to be the next splinter cell with hyper-realistic graphics running smooth as butter. you're playing this game for the fun of (relatively) innocently stealing from folks and racking up those dollar signs with some pretty interesting intricacy.

also there's an alley cat that adopts you and if you buy it a bed you'll get 100xp for petting it each week. WHAT MORE SHOULD YOU WANT???\t\t\t\t\t
