\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a decent sequel to the first game, although it feels more like a remake/remaster than a proper sequel due to many elements being reused.

Pros:
- There's more money sinks than the first game, still not even close enough tho.
- New progression tracks, more ways to encourage staying undetected.
- New event system is okayish, doesn't change the game too much but its a welcome variation.
- Picking up packages is fun.

Cons:
- Game got a lot easier, drone is very strong(I think it wasn't in the first game?).
- Debatable whether this is an issue or a game design decision, but I'd rather NPC facing was more predictable so it was more viable to sneak past them or pickpocket them while they're doing stuff.
- Areas are very similar to the first game. While they have been improvement it's still dissapointing for a proper sequel.
- Disassembling cars got simpler, depends on your preference I guess but I wish the old, more involved dissasembling was behind an option or something.
- The associate is kind of janky, you can't predict whether they will phase through things or open doors...
- I wish there was a way to make the progression much slower in general, after 10-ish hours you'll probably be close to maxing out skills and tool upgrades.

I'm still playing through the game so I'm not sure how much content is there compared to the first game, will update the review once I know.

EDIT: Yeah, there's 2 zones compared to 3 of the first game. And this game has fewer fun collection lists, no famberge eggs or anything. I'd only get this one if you're left wanting more after the first game...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt neat! Especially for being made by 1 person. I wish I could do that. Though I did come up with many ideas for what I think would make it better, they would essentially amount to a different thieving game. So as this one stands, in its dev's direction, it's a good time. However. At times it can get quite annoying, making you have to disarm the whole house again if you get busted while say, leaving the yard and a guard spawns in right in front of you. But that's on me for not paying enough attention to the occupant's routine timer. i haven't run into any glitches of any kind, even minor ones. the game is shockingly flawless in that regard. the achievements are easy/mild and actually mostly fun to get. i got all of them. though you should note that the perception skills become useless on hard mode which does indeed make the game much harder, but more of an annoying way to me. such as you can't see occupants through walls or markers for certain loot won't show up like digging spots.

overall though. despite that i wouldn't call it a simulator exactly (almost), it is indeed quite thiefy, with many sensible thieving elements within. so if you like stealth, upgrades, plotting your own efficient routes, or committing a war crime and gassing the whole place up to knock everyone out so you don't have to stealth, all in the name of stealing stuff for quick cash at a pawn shop, this is a must have for you :P

also you can "pet" a cat for xp each in game week and give it an upgrade for more xp. you can also feed dogs a giant chunk of meat so they don't bark at you when stealing their owner's stuff :p\t\t\t\t\t
