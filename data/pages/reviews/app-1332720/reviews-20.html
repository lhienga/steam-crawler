\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor those that don't know thief simulator, it's actually more burglary simulator. You find out the daily shedule of the tenants by plain old observing, buing that info or hiding a miniature camera at their entrance, then you plan the best time to get in. Mom is out presumably shopping, son has his daily two hours of toilet time? In we go. Get in the house by picking the lock of a door, smashing a window or using the window cutter, or maybe even go through a vent. Then you carefully search the rooms trying to find valuables, and try to transport bigger stuff to your car without being seen. But beware, if you picked a lock, didn't re-lock it behind you and a tenant goes through it, you're busted.

Worthy part two. If you liked part one, you'll like this one.
Sadly some things missing from American Theft 80s (the actual part two, it's like in GTA where VC and SA didn't count for GTA 4 :D), like the variety of cars and such, and the car deconstruction minigame was dumbed down a lot (I may have been on eof the few to actually enjoy the relaxing work of removing screws yourself :D I saw quite a few complaints about this, hence the change, I guess), but other stuff was added. For example, I don't remember any way to dispatch with tenants in the old game(s), here you have sleeping gas and a tranq gun. Both only work a short time and set them on high alert/search mode afterwards, but still, nice to have. You also now have a drone to get a better idea of the situation, and there are new types of alarms.
I'm not quite through with it, but it seems to have just two neighborhoods this time around (The dev, yes singular, is still actively working on the game, so in the future there may be more).

I mentioned in the reviews of the other parts that I play it a bit differently than intended. A hour goes by in a minute, that's hardly time to really look for stuff and each new hour means potentially changes where the tenants go. A hour could be half over just in the time it takes you to open a safe. If you go in when the tenant is on the toilet, you may be able to grab 2 things before you have to go out again.
This is not really realistic and the intended gameplay is probably "grab what you see and get out". but I like to take it slow and methodically. That's why I used time stop trainers in the other games. This means the tenants still walk around in their designated rooms for the time frame, but don't change rooms, letting you plot your path to steal every last thing in the house in one go. And it's not always as easy as it sounds becuase with 5 tenants that block different rooms, you have to do quite some planning if you wanna empty those rooms as well.
So I was dismayed that the wemod trainer crashed the game. I tried reporting the error, waiting for an update, just for the trainer maker to never react (I was not the only one that asked) and a wemod mod going "it works perfectly" (no, it doesn't). Shame on you, wemod, and shame on you, GreenHouse.
Anyway, because the "intended way" was just not fun for me, I paused playing. But Cookie Dev, the dev here, actually added a time freeze cheat in the game itself in v. 1.12, hooray! The cheat is "time freeze" and check the steam community forums on how to use cheats.
One thing that makes it even better: In this game there's a, I think, 10% chance, upgradeable to 5% chance awake tenants change their shedule. I guess it's a chance usually every "hour" but that's not disabled with time freeze so every minute, but that's just a guess. So even if you freeze time, you may notice a guard suddenly deciding he doesn't want to sit in the gard house but check out the back garden instead, with a path that leads through the house. So you better be prepared for that and hide in a side room and always relock doors and such\t\t\t\t\t
