\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinished the main story and most of the side content (missing a few Blackbay deliveries and 1 or 2 upgrades). Yeah, it's good.

This game definitely isn't for everyone. Another reviewer called it "Eurojank" and that is highly accurate. Controls and UI are frequently clunky. Animations jump from one set to another. Graphics are mid and have no stylistic appeal. But the gameplay is unique and not something you can get from any other series.

TS2 improves upon a number of TS1 features, such as now allowing relocking doors, or better insight into resident schedules. Nothing amazing, but plenty of nice little additions. It also has bigger/denser maps. It only has 2 maps right now (a 3rd will apparently be arriving in the next few months), but they feel much larger than the TS1 maps. The only new jank (vs TS2) I've found are 1) scrolling is blocked when a preview modal appears and 2) there are no keyboard shortcuts to exit the sleep menu, you have to click on the button. I'm probably going to alternate between TS2 and AT80 for my yearly thieving playthrough.

If you liked TS1, this is a must-have. If you disliked TS1, or preferred AT80 over TS1, this probably isn't for you. If you've never played those two, but enjoyed the old Thief games, definitely give this a try.

It also has a cat in your hideout, which immediately earns it an extra point on my scale.\t\t\t\t\t
