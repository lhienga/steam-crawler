\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be perfectly blunt this feels like a rush job and more of a side grade than a sequel. Wait for updates. a sale, or just get the original or American Theft instead.

Despite a few notable additions to gameplay, the over all experience is, in a word, crustier than most of PlayWay's endearingly crusty eurojank. Alot could be said for the presentation alone with the way the story mode intro staggers you into existence and the graphical optimization is just flatly bad. I know this is a PlayWay game but it feels like there was hardly any play testing save for making sure tenants ran like clockwork. Hell the issues with windows alone could be an excuse to keep this thing in the oven longer: From not fitting big items through a window (that could actually fit, like a printer or a PC) unless you tilt the camera at obtuse angles, to movement prompts not activating unless you're mid jump to get there, to just being sticky in general. The new mechanics that essentially boil down to having a drone and you can now knock people out are novel, but ultimately near useless with the way the crosshairs work with the drone and the way people just wake up and activate-because call is too generous a word for their response time-the police. The ability to actually use a drone camera to observe and properly mark tenants and, if we really wanna go the home invader route here, atleast tie up tenants for a minuet or two. I will say the Heists are a good idea that needs expanded on immensely.

I want to clarify that I don't hate it, but it does need work to live up as the sequel--especially since Noble Muffins, the devs of the first game, went on to make American Theft 80's and that feels MUCH better than this despite their slight tendency towards making things collectathon side jobs.\t\t\t\t\t
