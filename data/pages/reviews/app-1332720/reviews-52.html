SHORT VERSION
If you loved the first Thief Simulator and are eager to play this one, I suggest you wait until they actually finish it. While the game is not listed as early access, it is not a complete game, which I found to be a bit deceptive. The story is unfinished and will apparently conclude in a major patch later (December 2023 as per the dev's roadmap).


FULL REVIEW
As impressed as I am with the small dev team's ability to create such an engaging game that far exceeds its predecessor in almost every way, I have to take my own side and that of prospective consumers, out of a sense of obligation more than anything.


PROS
  • SIGNIFICANT improvement over gameplay and graphical elements across the board, compared to the first game.

  • NEW mechanics that were sorely lacking in TS1, like being able to knock people out, crawling through ventilation ports, an actually fun talent tree, cooldown-based "ults", a bunch of previously unseen burglary tools like drones and self-defense items, realistic resident schedules, and actually going to jail and then break out of it (which you can opt out of) all made the experience much more grounded and fulfilling.

  • Great QUALITY-OF-LIFE additions made the experience more fun and seamless, such as fast traveling to your car, buying consumables through your smartphone, faster car disassembly minigame, and vendors are found in the neighborhoods themselves. You also are able to tag people whose schedule you know, and it creates holographic dotted lines to show you where they will go in the next few minutes. This helped immensely with making the game less frustrating than its predecessor.

  • Target VARIETY makes for a more fun experience: neighborhoods are not just a line-up of houses anymore. There are junkyards, shops, storage units, and many more places that make each street feel alive.

  • ACTIVE dev team that is constantly updating the game and genuinely listening to feedback.

  • HEIST system introduces a break from the norm, allowing you to explore massive locales and sprawling buildings, netting you tons of loot in the process, some of which you can use yourself.

  • ENGAGING world elements that make immersion a lot easier. For example, you can figure out the jobs and life circumstances of a house's residents simply by taking a look at what they own, the notes they write, and even the notes you are sometimes paid to leave for them (like threatening or creepy letters.)

  • CAR RADIO!!! Too bad it only has like 10 minutes worth of audio, though.

Cons
  • INCOMPLETE STORY mode, as of this review's date. This is currently my biggest gripe with Thief Simulator 2. I don't know if it is a pacing issue or if the game was released in an unfinished state on purpose, but the cutscene that precedes the credits is so abrupt and short that it felt like I was scammed. Apparently the rest of the game is coming out in December, so I hope it is worth the wait. If they make the next "chapter" a paid DLC, I will never buy from this dev again.

  • VOICE ACTING is somehow more jarring and inauthentic than the first game. It feels like the voice actors were rushing to read their lines without breathing in between sentences. Your main ally talks in an unnatural way, and the mafia boss sounds comically bad.

  • BUGS that get in the way occasionally, like getting stuck in a corner behind a locker door or being unable to leave binocular view because you launched your drone while looking through them. While annoying, the bugs in this game are not enough to make it unplayable. It is also fortunate that the dev is actively addressing them with hotfixes right now.

  • Some robbery targets, like the final heist, are UNDERWHELMING. How is there less interesting loot in a BANK than a few cottages at a holiday resort ? It is also very frustrating that there is only one way to leave the bank, when most of the game offered the player at least 2 or more exit strategies.

Final Thoughts
I really enjoyed the game. It is a vast upgrade compared to the first game. The clever game design implementation in TS2 is no doubt a significant milestone in the dev's career, who I hope release more games in the future. However, I cannot recommend this game while it is not finished. I will change my review after I play the December update.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would've played this the day it came out but I was in Florida away from my PC from October 3 to yesterday October 7, I bought and started playing this game pretty much as soon as I got back to Pennsylvania. I was and still am a huge fan of the original thief simulator. I gotta say wow just wow do I love this game. I loved the original thief simulator but I love this one even more. It's just a massive improvement over the original.

First off you can finally knock out tenants (I think it's wrong that it deducts money automatically for doing it though, it should instead add to your time/fine if you get caught by the police.) Whilist that knock out tenants part adds some realism for the burglary gone bad situation as this game really didn't want to stray into GTA territory if you know what I mean, it deducting money from your inventory makes no sense. But as I said I can enjoy the realism.

This game has a natural smooth sensible progression and perk system and the option to freeplay instead of a story which is again a major improvement over the original. Also there are no microtransactions or lootboxes which makes this even better.

Plus in freeplay you are gifted a large hideout plus there are a lot more options on the computer and ways to make money early on and earn xp and perks which again is a huge improvement.

I am gonna continue working at this game in freeplay mode as I enjoy it that much. They made the realism mostly better and things like the perk system, menus, and mechanics are better and updated like I love the drone mechanic.

Now for the negatives
The graphics are probably the only thing that wasn't updated
An unlocked door can still automatically give away a burglary which isn't realistic. Ask yourself if you've ever simply forgotten to lock your door? There should at least be a chance that that doesn't trigger an alarm.
Stop making knocking out tenants deduct money that is wrong. Make it only deduct money and cause you to be penalized if you're caught.


Either way, a solid 7 out of 10 for the updated, better, and expanded mechanics and menus but I can't forgive the horrible system of knocking out tenants deducting money and them still having one unlocked door setting off alarms as that is not realistic at all. If you greatly enjoyed the original thief simulator, like I did, you'll like this game so pick it up.\t\t\t\t\t
