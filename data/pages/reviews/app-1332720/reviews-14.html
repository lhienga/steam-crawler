\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(I don't write reviews often, so please keep in mind these are just the highlights I noticed/enjoyed. If you want to go in blind this mentions a lot of the additions. Might not be the review for you.)

If you liked the first game, this is an excellent second edition. There are SO many new features I would've loved to see in the first game that are implemented here.

Things I loved;
You can hit people without completely ending the game (as it's actually a feature), there are plenty of new tools (blowtorch and sleeping gas for an example), and so many new mechanics to explore (planning routes. UM, YES!!).
The people feel more real too, they talk more, they have variations in schedule which is always a welcomed risk. They have notes that tell you about the characters lives, even if just a little and they're more comical lol. Also, I think they tweaked the hearing and it's so much more clear whether you're going to get detected, which was personally frustrating in the last game since sometimes it felt very touchy.
Heists are a new edition, which I think is a great change since it eliminates the ability to cheese high value areas by cheap saves since you can't save in them. And even better, it adds a ton of variety because it's outside of the normal neighborhoods.
They reworked taking apart cars and its SOOOO MUCH BETTER. SO MUCH. I actually enjoy the process instead of feeling like I have to hunt down each individual screw.
Overall: SO much better with immersion in day to day heists. Just everything about the regular game play has been improved.

Critiques; (take with a grain of salt, I'm only 20 hours in)
The only gripe I have that makes it a 4/5 instead of a 5/5 for me is the characters. Definitely felt bummed to have Vinny's role from the first game replaced with a disembodied voice w/o even a name.
It's very narrator-y and doesn't have half of the character that Vinny did. Of course, adding him back for a sequel might not have been what they wanted to do, and I've yet to see if he's going to get named later on.. but you get the gist. As of what I've seen it's simply a narrator-y guy who's labelled private number.
I'd argue that all of the phone characters struggle with that same problem.\t\t\t\t\t
