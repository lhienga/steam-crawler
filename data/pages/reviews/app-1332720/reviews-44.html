\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn interesting game for sure. Dont expect a lot of polish its as one reviewer called it "Eurojank"
But if you are able to look past that and have a good time then there is alot of fun to be had. some reviewers are saying there arent any changes from the first game which is a flat out lie. You have drones to scope houses, dogs to avoid, a tranq gun, knockout gas, a baton, ATM hacking, payphone missions and a pet cat. There are some other things. Alot of it feels a bit missmatched but its overall a decent experience. Considering it had a single developer we should cut some slack, course the pricing is dubious but we can thank the publisher for that one. If you enjoyed the first game you will enjoy this one, however the story ends in a way I wasnt expecting, I do look forward to seeing what they do with it. Biggest complaint is the broken english and bad voice acting, I feel like I could record better lines for free. Overall, its fun. Dumb at times but its fun, id reccomend trying it out at the very least\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou know that conversation-starter question you hear at parties? There‚Äôs a few but there‚Äôs one that sticks out in my mind, ‚ÄúIf you could commit any crime without any consequences, what would it be?‚Äù or some variation of ‚ÄúIf you could get away with anything‚Ä¶‚Äù and ‚ÄúWhat would you do during The Purge?‚Äù My answer is always the same: Straight up burglary. It‚Äôs been a fantasy of mine that might have spawned, as some other reviewer mentioned, during the Burglary minigame in GTA: San Andreas. Ever since I‚Äôve focused myself on stealthy gameplay and gravitated towards franchises like Splinter Cell, Assassin‚Äôs Creed, and typically focus on rogue types in Elder Scrolls.

This is the same intro I gave to my review of TS1, just to lay the groundwork of how I feel about stealth games.

As the name of the game implies, this is a simulator that explores various facts of being a basic street level thief. There's a story about how you angered some mafia and owe them a half-million in cash. Obviously you won't be earning that kind of dosh right out of the gate but it gives a GTA5-esque framework as to why you are continuing your life of crime. Again, this is just a framework for what generally becomes a stealth sandbox.

You start by just breaking into some houses and casing houses to grab tenant's schedules and eventually graduate to literal grand theft auto and full blown heists. The heists don't really work like in other games but mostly serve as a separate "area" like its own neighborhood, but the difference is you are always trespassing and can't save. I've only experienced the first one so far but the Freeform mode revealed that there's at least three locations for heists, and it's a great framework to add more in the future.

The game is well paced, similar to the first one. It introduces you new tools as you level up (or just make a freeform game and figure it all out on your own). The story mode takes you into places that may have safes within it but you won't be able to do much about it until later. Also new to the sequel is a notepad feature in the notes menu, where you can jot down things like "This house has a safe in it, come back later". A brilliant QoL feature.

I had two complaints from the first game that have been slightly lessened here, to a point. With a brand new complaint to join it. The first was I didn't find the houses to be very 'lived in'. They tend to look wildly clean. That's still a thing here, and is a common style in -Simulator games, as they only have so many assets to pass around. The better graphics here makes this slightly better from the first, as walls and floors look more 'used' than before. Sometimes carpets are a little off-center, giving a sort of 'someone walked on this' vibe to it. I just wish I saw more wrinkles and clutter.

The second complaint was nuance, especially with the AI. It's better here than it was before, albeit they're a bit twitchier than before which is understandable given what you're doing around their house. If memory serves, being on someone's property in the first game gave a suspicious alert, but this game will have people call the cops on you very quickly (though as long as you're still in the yard you can sprint to the sidewalk). For nuance's sake, I wish there was a Hitman-esque disguise system we could utilize to dress up and at least look properly in place just wandering people's yards. Give us a bug sprayer uniform.

My new complaint is the windows of time are incredibly narrow. NPC scheduling is more nuanced here (yay!) and they'll move around a bit more, making your incursions more intense. Most stealth games never incur a time limit on you so you can be methodical, but since every single second is one minute in game time, waiting around too long means you completely burn through your window of opportunity. I honestly wish the time went by much slower, perhaps a possible update to the Easy setting later down the line. (Keep the current speed on Normal and Hard). Sometimes there's a two hour window when a tenant is out of the house and it somehow takes an entire twenty minutes for me to just sprint across the street to get to their windows. That doesn't make much sense to me.

There's more game here at launch than TS1 had on theirs, so I can see myself having dozens of hours of this game that tickles that burglarly itch. As of this posting they've been consistent with updating it, damn near one patch a day including some QoL features like giving tenant's names (to track them around easier), letting us sprint with heavy items (that's something you can do in real life, safety hazards be damned), adding extra keys in the police station, and much more.

Keep it up team, you're doing great.\t\t\t\t\t
