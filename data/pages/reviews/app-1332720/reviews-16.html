\r\n\t\t\t\t\tquite the upgrade from the first game, it felt like more thief simulator!
Although, I am disappointed that the overall scope of the game hasn't grown as much. The heists are a very nice touch, so is the hardcore and survival mode which I played on. I like how punishing the penalty for being caught is, losing half your money is quite a hit. My only issue with this punishment is that there's a bank that you would put your money in anyways since it grows over time, so I only ever kept a small amount of money on myself.

I don't feel that it is much of a challenge in general, the movement upgrades and being well fed/not thirsty/well slept makes it VERY easy to zoom across the skies. Its a bit of an exaggeration, but I can easily jump onto house roofs and skip much of the challenge that comes with breaking into a house

Other than those complaints, I genuinely love the game. I like how the game has a consistent pace that the player doesn't even need to follow, the story is something that the player can easily choose to follow at their own pace without it feeling forced. I like how the houses got more complex and the layouts made it harder for me to break in. It felt like more of a challenge from the first game. I like how overall the game is polished and has Easter eggs to find.

If there is something I'd like in the next installment, it is:
- add the ability to hack cameras to see through the cameras. It adds another element that may pair well with more complex designs of houses, where players may be forced to see through those cameras in order to plan out their route or check on where a person is.

- Increase the penalty for "Hardcore" mode when you get caught, maybe make security more tight at that house for a certain period of time(Although I see why that may be an issue for heists), or maybe reduce speed stat/weight carrying stat for a short amount of time.
Alternatively, "Hardcore" mode may keep the money loss penalty while making the difficulty of the houses higher(maybe on normal mode a certain house has one person that leaves from 9-12 while on Hardcore mode there's two people that leave for less time). Apologys if this is already the case.

- More difficult houses to break into. I find that when I got to Hillsville, I already had all the movement upgrades and it made it very easy to break into even the harder houses since i could easily jump onto a roof(which cops can't arrest you if you are on the roof) and easily escape. I'd like to see houses that aren't as easy to cheese where my speed upgrades don't let me cheese it easily but are needed in order to avoid detection.


Once again, I like the game and what it has to offer, I can't wait to see any DLC for this game and the announcement of the next installment!\t\t\t\t\t
