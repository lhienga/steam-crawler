TL;DR - Less janky than first game. Still janky. Still fun.

I can't say I relate with the sentiment that it's DLC or sidegrade/rushed. Though there are certainly some aspects that need more work, it is very-much so an improvement over the first game. It's important to remember that the game is developed by a single person, so it's to be expected that some things slipped through testing.
Now, onto the actual review.

Positives & Commendations
  • More ways to interact with tenants than before. Gameplay diversity fun.

  • Perk progression that actually feels like some form of progression, rather than only an unlock chain.

  • QoL changes galore! Fast travelling to vehicles, selling loot from your hideout, selling loot when not at your hideout and more.

  • The cops shoot you with something besides a taser this time around. Owie.

  • Drones make for a fantastic information gathering tool, and add a fun dynamic to the game.

  • Driving feels moderately less terrible. You can kinda drift now, which I consider to be a plus.

  • Lots of methods of alternate entry to houses, rather than just having to use your vehicle as a ramp to cheese your way in.

  • Thief.FM (the default car radio) is quite charming, if a bit dry on content. Definitely could use an expansion on voice lines.

  • Increased jump height makes maneuvering a bit more dynamic and intriguing. It's no Dying Light, but it's better than the microhops from TS1.

  • The associate you get is incredibly useful. Perhaps too useful?

  • You can pet the cat(s).

Negatives & Qualms
  • Hiding from the police is still boring. Effectively boils down to; did someone see you go into hiding? If no, then sit still for two minutes doing nothing until they leave. If yes? Move to a different room without line of sight and hide there.

  • Nightvision instantly invalidates the flashlight. Learn to love the color green.

  • Speaking of invalidation, binoculars offer a method of cheapening the gameplay by letting you see every single item through walls, even ones stored in containers. Not sure if you want to risk opening a safe? Don't worry, your handy-dandy x-ray vision binoculars will let you know exactly what's inside.

  • Tenants occasionally have sixth sense, and detect you from impossible angles.

  • It's fine that the game penalizes you for knocking out tenants with a crowbar. However, the fact that it instantly causes the cops to arrive even if there are no other conscious witnesses is not. I understand that this is likely to deter players from smacking and stealing. In my opinion, it would've been much better if the money penalty was higher rather than psychic cops that magically detect assaults.

  • Tenant AI is still pretty poor. You can expect them to always change routines on the hour-- only having to watch for when the clock hits X:00. Elsewise they're stationary. It'd be nice if there was a mild bit of deviation for non "out of the house" routines, it would go a long way to making them somewhat less predictable.

  • Upgrading the Stethoscope (safecracking minigame) makes it harder, not easier for some indiscernible reason. It increases the audio feedback on the numerical range. Though this technically gives you more time to react, it makes it difficult to find where the exact number is, which, slows you down considerably. May just be a me issue. I'll leave that up to comments.

  • Upgrading the T2 Lockpick seems to make them break more often? Perhaps I'm missing something.

  • What does, or does not make noise seems wildly inconsistent. Drop from a 3ft high? No worries! Drop from 4ft? Entire household knows your exact position, and is coming towards you with murderous intent.

  • You can't pet the dogs.

Feature Requests
  • A phone app or wristwatch that you can use as an alarm. I've been using my actual phone to create minute-long timers to keep track of tenant routines or when they'll return home. It'd be neat to have it in-game.

  • The noisy walkie-talkies would make a perfect addition to the quick buy menu.

  • The non-respawning items that you need for Blackbay contracts should not be able to be sold to the pawnshop. Or please allow them to respawn. My inner completionist weeps.

  • Keybinds for the activated skills would be an excellent QoL. Numerical indicators for their cooldowns on the HUD would also be great. It's rather awkward having to open the skill menu to use them.


This review is an active work in progress, and will likely be updated as I progress through the game, or if the game receives noteworthy updates.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is just a better experience than Thief Sim 1 in nearly every aspect. It's not a massive leap, but it's enough. I was worried it would just be the same thing, but it's not. As is, I enjoy it, I'm not sure if it's necessarily worth $20, however perhaps there will be an additional neighborhood in the future which I think it desperately need.

I would say, it's a bit too easy now too. Drones and the companion are kind of cool concepts but when you can get the entire schedule of a household with the micro-camera, both are not so useful. I think a reworking of the stealth system, where you might know their general schedule, but you don't get the silhouettes until you mark them (and they only last for a few in-game hours), or some change like that, would have helped make this game a bit harder, and also helped give these features more attention.

I liked some of the locations you can steal from, like a store, however it's really just a house with a store-front. I think a third location in a city setting, where you can steal from apartments, rob small storefronts, etc -- something along those lines -- would be nice, however with how the game is currently 'optimized' (of which it is most definitely not), I'm not entirely sure it can handle such a busy environment.

The police show up almost immediately again here, however they aren't as omniscient as they were before, so escaping from them is a bit easier, which I'm sort of OK with. They stay around for way too long now, which could make sense and you could reasonably rob other houses, but with how it completely takes over the UI and the way bystanders instantly telepathically communicate to the police, the whole police system feels a bit arcadey. Right now when I get caught, I just run to somewhere safe (most notably, frank's or joe's store), and then go AFK for a few minutes.

I would have liked to see the talent tree break out more. You could try to be a generalist, or specialize in hacking, pick-pocketing, lockpicking, weapons, etc. I would have like to have seen more talents and tools, and be able to specialize in one specific path without the level limit system forcing me to generalize. Even if you take some things and break them apart into multiple talents (drones, spotting camera, all the planning mode stuff, etc).

in short, if you enjoyed the first game you'll enjoy this.\t\t\t\t\t
