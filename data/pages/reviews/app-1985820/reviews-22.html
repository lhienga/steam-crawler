\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Third Reich once brought the world to a heel. They did what many thought was to be impossible, even for a superpower of its time. Germany, once humiliated and defeated thoroughly in the first Great War, had now adopted the predatory eyes of an eagle and set its sights on its neighbors. Nazi Germany is hardly a small bird and its allies have a similar wing span. In North Africa, men from British colonies will come to fight. An educated man of color from Cambridge will find himself on a violent vacation on the beaches of Normandy after a disastrous paradrop. A Soviet lady will inherit a family heirloom and open her heart, not to sorrow, but to the bloodied stitches of hate and a desire for revenge, to close the wounds that have been opened during Germany's invasion of her nation. A few characters just sort of die. Wade is uhh... there I guess and I find it hard to call it a good thing at times. Either way, this Scooby Gang of generic comic book war heroes collide with not just the Germans, but their superior, real historical parts in a way that can only be described as "certainly not good, but not entirely terrible."

I have had the misfortune of playing this game twice, having bought it originally on battlenet along with Cold War and Modern Warfare 2019 out of morbid curiosity for what I had been missing out on, as I was rather preoccupied during the years they came out in Denmark at the time, having all sorts of adventures and some regrets. I wish that Vanguard was one of those experiences that I never had the supposed pleasure of playing, but I insisted upon myself to play it once more for the sake of giving it the review I thought it deserves.

Admittingly, I hate the game a lot less on this second playthrough, if anything I enjoyed some of it more than I did initially. Having some knowledge of the levels meant I was less frustrated and bumbling far less around in it, at an interesting cost. Ultimately, this game was fairly boring even in its best levels and most of my playtime is actually due to procrastination. I would take, at times, hour long breaks from this campaign, its pause menu haunting me, as I partook in otherpast times, such as taking an overly long time cooking a meal or even just laying in my bed and imagining a better game.

After WW2 completely surprised me, I was also deeply spoiled by its spectacular graphics that I think made it one of the most impressive games I've ever visually seen with one of my favourite COD gameplay loops and blood/gore mechanics. I deeply enjoyed the story, how it was able to weave in a distinct and diverse class that were equal in their struggle against the odds of fighting on the Western Front in humanity's greatest and most terrible war, so one must imagine how I feel about having to return to Vanguard, where it is purely the opposite of WW2 in so many ways. Rather than comparing it to any of the other CODs, which I believe is unfair given the fact that this is a very experimental COD campaign and for the worst, I think its immediate older brother WW2 is a proper candidate for comparisons.

Somehow, graphically in game and especially in animations, weapon handling, general model integrity, etc - Vanguard is a graphically excellent game yet still is a full step, a flip and jump backwards from WW2. Even on the highest possible settings, the graphics look like something out of Black Ops 1 when up close, which is odd when the game's otherwise excellent particle effects and lighting effects do present themselves against muddy, melty models with poor animations. Some animations, specially the German half-track MG entering and exiting animation, is straight from WW2 and its a shame that ironically, the rest of the game isn't just assets from WW2 - because then it would actually look good.

There are some impressive set pieces, some truly spectacular presentations of what this engine is capable of doing and yet, it all falls very flat of what came before and after it. This game has the bad kind of restraint and when it should be more sensible, its out right bad.

Many said the MP was fairly decent and Nazi Zombies was... there. I came into this game to play neither and thus will only be discussing the story.

Ironically, if you have mistakenly bought this game in a bundle of COD titles or have simply bought it for a fair price of 20 USD equivalent or less, preferably more like 5-7 at most, I'd say that it is an okay game for one playthrough. Its worth experiencing once, if anything, to laugh at, but there are some really gorgeously animated and rendered cutscenes and the story sometimes is good.

What is remarkable about this game, is that when I try and describe the things that I did like about it, it is like trying to define the symptoms of a bipolar mania wedged between major episodes of lethargy, manic depression and intense apathy. When I first played this game last year, I literally was falling asleep during a few missions. This time around, I really did let myself enjoy the game when it is just shooting, no gimmicks. The gameplay is rather good when it is just that, but still, I get distracted by the absolutely grotesque wasted potential of some interesting concepts.

Also, this game makes Black Ops 1 look like a completely historically accurate milsim game. Germans have obscure Japanese rifles, Japanese troops in Papua New Guinea have time traveling spiderholes and trenches, where you can find oddities from another parallel universe such as a STG44 with a yellow dot sight. I complained about the odd Soviet surplus SVT showing up in WW2, the 74u in Black Ops 1 appearing a full decade before its existence, but this game is by far the most heinous with Waffen SS troops rocking their kit in suspiciously modern ways, where as other units are portrayed remarkably well for the theater they are in. But the game insists on some of the oddest gun choices, like the basically non-existent, one of a kind, revolving chamber shotgun abomination for the germans or some nonsensical proto-AK-47 appearing in Stalingrad.

One of my biggest gripes with the game is that it can come off as genuinely disrespectful at points. I generally really enjoy Polina's missions and her disposition, however many of her lines are blatantly written by the literal white girl who was once interviewed for being "raped on GTA5 virtually" and also has the incredible resume of writing IDW ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ tripe like Rick and Morty comics or cringy DnD graphic novels. Stalingrad is somewhat improperly rendered, it was the largest battle ever fought by humans, with 22,000 people dying an hour at its peak and yet Stalingrad is a nearly vacant ghost town that sees little fighting. Even worse, is that Polina is implied to be the sole partisan that encouraged Stalingrad to push back the German invaders.

Now, there were many Soviet snipers that were ladies. Many of them are very famous and have prestigious kill numbers that simply outmatch many snipers today. They were a significant part of the war effort and Stalingrad's defenders were numerous and immense. There was suicidal sacrifice and resistance from the first day to the last and the game does a serious misjustice to such a devastating chapter in human history by pinning its victory on a fictional character that doesn't fully capture the true picture.

Of course, one cannot expect these games to be so faithful, but the game does have significant examples of dialogue that support narratives that are simply historical revisionism or at times, out right untrue. However, these can be explained merely as the character's perspectives and can be forgiven. Some scenes are just pure ♥♥♥♥, with horrific dialogue and even worse character moments, juxtaposed against otherwise good moments.

The SS in the game, in their self-cannibalizing, internal coup and power struggle have a better plot than the actual game via the gorgeous cutscenes.

Overall, this game is great when it just lets you shoot.\t\t\t\t\t
