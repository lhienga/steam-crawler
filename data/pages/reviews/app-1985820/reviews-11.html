\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished playing this over on my Youtube channel (link at end of review for anyone who wants to see a sample of gameplay before buying). I'll be honest: Vanguard isn't my favourite Call of Duty game. In fact, far from it; but nonetheless, I thought it was alright enough to warrant a thumbs up. Bear in mind here that I'm talking about the single player aspect of the game; I've not touched the multiplayer, so can't speak about that.

The visuals, sound design and gunplay are all fine, and are definitely the game's strong points. I also did enjoy the fact that some of the missions of the single player campaign did take the player to some different theatres of WW2. Sure, there's your popular settings like Stalingrad; but I enjoyed the excursions to locations such as North Africa and the Pacific theatre, as in my honest opinion a lot of these types of WW2 first-person shooters always tend to go for obvious settings like D-Day or Stalingrad. So to have missions set in other locations, ones which perhaps aren't depicted as often in these sorts of games, is always a plus for me.

I'd also like to talk about the characters in this game, as these were a source of contention for many players. Unlike CoD World at War or CoD WWII, which followed characters who were ordinary soldiers fighting to stay alive (thus utilising more gritty, realistic stories), Vanguard's characters instead consist of a group of special forces operatives who are all a bunch of stereotypes/archetypes (if either of those is the right word). I'll be honest; not all of them landed right for me. For some of them (such as the calm, reserved British officer or the stereotypical cold, revenge-seeking Russian sniper girl) I felt kind of... meh, I guess? Can't say I was hugely invested in their stories.

One gripe I did have with this game was that the frame rate really, really stuttered in places. This had been a bit of an issue in CoD WWII as well, though it felt much more prevalent here in Vanguard. I'm still not sure why this was the case; I'm on a beefy PC, so I don't think it's a hardware issue. Either there's an issue in the game's design causing it, or maybe some aspect of my PC's configuration is set up wrong - but I haven't seen any evidence to suggest it's the latter. It's not that the game was unplayable, though; I did manage to beat it. It was just mildly annoying.

As for the zombies mode, it's okay. I had no major issues with it. On a more subjective note, it has a whole cast of characters of its own - and to be honest, the roster of 'demonic' characters is something that I found kind of cheesy.

But, all that aside, I'd still give Vanguard a tentative positive rating. It's not the best CoD WW2 game, in my eyes - but it's alright for what it is.
