\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCall of Duty Vanguard is representative of a game that was simply rushed. I have played the Campaign, a small part of the Multiplayer and its zombies, and it is simply not a good title. Compared to what has been produced by these companies prior, you can tell with this that it was rushed most likely due to Activision simply needing a game asap to publish.

Most people do not like the Call of Duty games primarily developed by Sledgehammer Games. Advanced Warfare and World War 2 were always perceived as just plain titles which either provided nothing for the series or in Advanced Warfare's case caused a long period of futuristic titles which were not appreciated at all by the community. This campaign visually is beautiful, but my main ripe with the game is its appalling writing which is not only just generally lacking in any emotion but could be perceived as offensive considering the games source material. Combining the concept of media such as the A-Team with World War 2 just does not work with a lot of the dialogue generally just being very cringy. There are beautiful set pieces as stated before, but the terrible writing on the characters, as well as the games cliff hanger ending just left a really bad taste in my mouth after finishing. This campaign is generally very weak, and is something I hope is never revisited in any future games or worse a direct sequel.

Zombies and Multiplayer is no better. Multiplayer is no different than any of its previous titles (MW2019 and Cold War) and despite being set in World War 2 does not stop Activision from plaguing this game with operator bundles that just simply DO NOT fit this game aesthetics. A prime example of this is having Snoop Dogg as an operator. It does not fit the setting and just feels wrong. Call of Duty Ghost had a Snoop Dogg announcer pack as well as other goofy packs for the game but was set in a fictional war and therefore made more sense with the games arcade feel. Of course there is no shade towards Snoop Dogg in this situation, and it may be weird talking about how it could be offensive, but when the series was built on creating a Call of Duty around an actual war somewhat respectfully, it just feels odd and out of character for Sledgehammer. Remember, this is from the same company who created Call of Duty World War 2, a title ultimately more respectable towards its source material. We all know of course this is a direct result of Activisions greed, so there is no shade towards Sledgehammer Games, just instead their greedy higher ups.

What they did to Zombies however is just disgusting and would have done better with not having a zombies mode to begin with. Vanguard Zombies is a butchered variant of the games original zombies mode, and similar to every other part of this game, is an example of a rushed product containing the bare minimum, yet again an example of Activisions greed to not give Sledgehammer and other game companies more time to develop a good game. Vanguard zombies contains a grand total of four maps, two of which are objective-based and the other are round-based, only brought in after the complaints brought by the community, and one of the round-based maps is a remake of World at War's Shi No Numa. This remake is the only good notable part of this zombies mode, so if you were to grind for the games camos then you only really have one reliable choice of map to play. Some of the game play elements, such as the hearts system to upgrade your skill ability is unique, but is not enough to justify playing this mode. Camo grinding in zombies is utterly disgusting. Most to all guns require you level them up to level 70. There are over 100 camos per gun needing you to get 1000s of kills to unlock, and is so tedious and grindy that it is simply not worth the hassle. I have played multiple matches with the STG and despite having reached high rounds ranging from 20s to 30s, I have still yet to fully level up this gun just to earn a simple achievement.

Call of Duty Vanguard is indicative of Activisions appalling behaviour towards this franchise, and their inability to not only give its game developers time to create a good quality product as other games they have made prior has showed what they're capable of, but to put aside their greedy practices for one year on a game based around World War 2 is simply not possible. It is offensive towards the original material, it is a half-baked title sold at a full AAA price tag and is a stain on the Call of Duty franchise. It represents a dark time for the Call of Duty community. To think, Call of Duty Vanguard shares the sames three words as it does with Black Ops or the original Modern Warfare titles...

Despite the negative things I have said towards this game, hindsight with Modern Warfare 2023 shows that Activision are even worse than we thought. Simply put, do not waste your money, or your time on this game. Do not give Activision your money for this product. Do not buy any of the in-game skins / cosmetics, Do not fund greed like I have.

Note: If you are an achievement hunter like me, this games campaign is riddled with achievements which are broke as they no not track. A Steam discussion page spanning over a few months now has document any information from Activision and ultimately it seems to be a lost cause. Do not buy this game for achievement hunting sake unless you are willing to SAM achievements which are broken. In the event of broken achievements, myself and many other achievement hunters would certinaly allow the use of SAM.\t\t\t\t\t
