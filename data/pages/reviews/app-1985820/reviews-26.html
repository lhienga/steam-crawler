Mixed feelings & Broken Achievements
Call of Duty for me is World War II and old school Modern Warfare.
I held off in getting this one because I wanted it on Steam.
You can imagine my joy when it suddenly popped up on Steam a year later.
and that's about where the good news ends....

Story
Generic Specialist Unit of Rag Tag I play by my own rule and don't play well with others type of story where everyone has their own story to tell but through their adversity they find purpose and kindship to foil the Secret Nazi plans.
Lots of explosions and crumbling building so feels like watching a Micheal Bay Expendables film.
Guess we've had enough WW2 Stories of Historical relevance.
I did like the Nazi Zombies connection at the end though!

Multiplayer
Run around, Shot enemies, play on a 200ms server because no one plays this game and the ones who do are level 300 but also go 25-13 because they are all console players with $10 headsets.

Zombies
I've played every Nazi Zombies there is, I was not expecting much from this title expect some good old fashion WW2 guns surviving rounds. This time around they've done something different.
Its a sort of open world, play at your own pace area style of most of the levels similar to Cold War Zombies but at a smaller scale.
I actually liked this! because its different and allows you to plan and learn the map at your own pace.
Only problem is... its very easy as long as you know where to escape at the end because you only get 60 seconds to get there. Beat all the levels and moved on! Decent time spent.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecent enough buy (if it's on sale) purely for the campaign, and that's if you can overlook the extreme in-authenticity, and the wokeness, (I don't mind diversity, but perhaps the Developers should have done their research.) The graphics, sound design and gameplay are great. The scale of the battles is great - but the Captain America/Soviet Russia/Australia/England/Rambo characters take away from the battles, the brutality of war, and the authenticity of the time period. For instance, in COD:WAW, you were merely a cog in the war machine, one person whom happened to be in the right place at the right time. This is something I feel COD has been suffering from the last few games *cough* Modern Warfare 1 & 2; Specifically tailoring the campaign towards high speed, low drag 360 degree 420 blaze it no-scope operators.. Rather than sticking to the original formula that made the prior COD campaigns great - a mix of both special operations and large scale warfare, both modern and vintage. Regardless - the campaign is honestly insulting to those whom fought in WW2. It's not a big deal that the story was fictional - but if you're going to create a game based on history, you ought to pay attention to detail, and create an authentic experience in order to give respect to those that fought it.
On another note, being that the game has had a few years - you'd think the technical issues would have been ironed out. The optimization is great, but if you're getting CTD's on the first mission, then verifying the game files, only to be missing 21-27 files each time, (because of the update when booting up.) try booting up in safe mode or turning DLSS off in order to play.
TL;DR
If you're purely a COD player for the campaigns, is it worth $60 to play the campaign?
Wait for a sale. This is a $30 game at best, even with the Zombies and Multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t
