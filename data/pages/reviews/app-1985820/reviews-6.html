\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA real " Call of Duty " game with no lame long time consuming high level progression to unlock basic cool weapons and upgrades !
Main feature of this game is a very efficient hip-firing : This is the first Call of Duty I have ever played with such a nice feature. A lots of fun and satisfaction.
There is a revamped classic Zombie mode : similar to old Black ops and World at War but you are free to choose your loadout + you can progress your weapons level here and upgrade your own level without doing PvP in multiplayer mode !!!
Nice operators and arsenal, Operators can be obtained fairly easily and you can play them anyway even if not unlocked yet ( random assignation of operator ).
Very good graphics ( outstanding actor motion capture ), and optimized. And it takes only 55gb of hard disk space ( COD Cold War uses 170gb !!! ) !
Compared to that game, COD " Cold war " is a lot inferior in every aspect : Graphics, gameplay (Pvp and Zombie), weaponery and operators. I asked a refund for that one as I did not enjoyed it at all.
But in Vanguard, even if you are low skilled, you will be able to put out surprising performance in the matchmakings you play and get a lot of satisfaction doing so. Every player gets enjoyment, from the best to the weaker !
The feedback of the weapons is so good and realistic, that you can do exactly what you want. The hip firing contributes a lot to it ! Unlike in Cold War...

Only complain I have is there are not enough zombies maps + a lot of recycling and there's too much cosmetics and weaponery elements that are " Out of context " of the game ( Non WWII weapons and operator cosmetics with Manga inspiration ) so it's kinda immersion breaking.
I got the game in discount for 30€, it totally worth it but not it's regular 60€ price though just like any Call of Duty as it's almost same for more than 15 years... Game is not huge enough compared to other games to justify a 60 € price.

In conclusion : This is a totally go-to Call of Duty if at a fair price !\t\t\t\t\t
