\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCall of Duty: Vanguard is a remaster of the remaster of Call of Duty: World at War, this is the 57th installment in the series and Sledgehammer games made sure to celebrate this properly.

This remaster is one step at the right direction and 3 steps backwards from the last remaster.
The guys at sledgehammer seemed to have improved the remaster software, while it is not perfect, it is a clear improvement. We still do not get the 2 original campaigns. Instead, we get a combined campaign that gives us one unique mission for each character. Unfortunately, one of the things the remaster software still didn't bring to the remaster is a memorable story, so I can't tell you what happened because I honestly don't remember.

But forget about the campaign, what about the multiplayer?
Once again the shooting mechanics feel more refined than ever, and this time we have a whole lot more guns, but some of the guns are just outright terrible - modern guns or even futuristic guns somehow found their way into this remaster and it feels completely wrong.
Weapon customization is a whole lot more detailed now, with a lot more weapon parts to change and edits, but like the rest of the game, many upgrades do not fit the game's theme.
This time the multiplayer has no actual factions, because of the new operator system, where you can select your character, customize them, level them up and learn some information about them. This feature also comes short due to the minimal customization and the insane monetization, most of the operators can only be unlocked by paying a hefty price and they don't even fit the WW2 theme.

This time the progression is a whole lot more faster, but at the same time after you hit prestige it is completely meaningless, you can only prestige once now, since you could only prestige multiple times during the game's active updates - What a stupid idea. So you are either stuck with the blue ranks forever, or you level up in the latest CoD games (as levels carry over between games now).

Another thing this game fixes is the lack of playerbase on PC and the stupid region lock, nowadays CoD games are cross platform, so not only we almost always have other people to play with, we get to play with console fanboys, and clean house with our superior mouse and keyboard controls. Finally a CoD game where I can get a 100 10 KD.

TLDR: If you want to play the most generic and bland Call of Duty game (which is what I want), then this is the best game for you.

5/10
And don't even get me started on the zombie mode...
