\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I grade COD games on a curve. I only ever buy them on sale, and I only ever bother with the campaign. I know their MP isn't for me. That's fine, not trying to tell folks that their fun is bad and wrong. If you dig MP in COD games, cool. It's not for me.

I've played several of the games over the years. COD1-3, WaW, MW2, BO1, BO2, AW, IW, WWII, etc., etc. I know what I'm gonna get when I play a COD game. These days, it's usually a cinematic affair, scattered in multiple locations, straightforward gunplay, etc. It's not gonna be revolutionary, but it'll be entertaining on a certain level.

But I have to say, this game...was really underwhelming. Small group of SpecOps folks infiltrate German bases and do WWII-ish stuff. The group is made up of stock characters. Ruthless female Russian sniper who wants revenge. Hotshot American flyboy. By-the-book British commander. Not-by-the-book Aussie demo guy. You know the drill. They fight evil Nazi guys who kill bound prisoners with a wooden chair to just to show how evil they are. (I guess because the team didn't have a dog he could kick.) Whatever. I'm not expecting Oscar or BAFTA worthy cinema here. Just keep it fun, right?

But that's where it fails. I played for a little less than an hour and a half. Did the first mission, started the second, and that's where I said "Screw this." You're dropped into enemy territory on a D-Day blow-up-the-guns mission that you've probably played in some form in at least one (or more) of the COD WWII releases set in Europe. Everything goes wrong, so you're on your own, surrounded by Germans everywhere. Oooh. Thrilling. Except it's more just...annoying.

By the 6th time I got killed (sneaking this way or that didn't work; ramboing through didn't work) I just decided "You know what? I don't need to spend my time doing this." Requested a refund.

Hey, maybe the campaign gets better after the start of Mission 2, but from what I've heard...it doesn't. And if you're only here for the campaign in the first place...why bother? Life is too short to waste it faffing about trying to figure out what the "right" path is that the designers had in mind for me to do (which they also didn't bother to indicate at all).

Again, I grade on a curve here. It doesn't have to be revolutionary gameplay. It doesn't have to be great fiction. It just has to be minimally entertaining, but this game didn't feel like it was headed that way, and I decided I'd rather not blow my money trying to "make" the game be entertaining. So, refund it is.

Of course, you get the benefit of my folly, if you read this, and you can save your money AND time on the front end by just not buying it!\t\t\t\t\t
