\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVanguard is one of my least favorite games in the Call of Duty franchise and is a good example for why the series should never return to the World War 2 setting - not because of the setting itself, but because the franchise has shifted too far into the direction of its live service model, with Third-Party crossovers and heavily stylized skins in overpriced bundles, that an immersive World War 2 setting just couldn't work anymore. Real shame since I think a traditional WW2 game in Call of Duty's engine, with a similarly realistic approach as Modern Warfare (2019), would be phenomenal.

Multiplayer can be fun and while I think a lot of the maps & weapons are pretty good, it still suffers greatly from this identity crisis, unable to decide if it wants to be a WW2 game or a fictionalized alt-history version of it. If the later season are any indicator, then it's very clear that Sledgehammer would've much preferred to make an Advanced Warfare sequel instead this game.

As a longtime fan of the mode, Zombies was the biggest letdown in this game. There are things I do like and appreciate about the mode, mainly the game design itself and how it refines a lot of the systems and mechanics from Black Ops Cold War, but all that is undermined by a blatantly rushed development and terrible objective-based loop that doesn't service the gameplay at all. I also really liked the art direction and worldbuilding they did for the mode, expanding on the Dark Aether lore - but again, all that is undermined by a lack of time and resources to even make it. Despite what people say, I firmly believe there are some good aspects to Vanguard Zombies that would work great in a full-fledged Zombies mode that I'd love to see in Treyarch's upcoming game. Other than that, however, Zombies is a disappointment that isn't worth buying the game for.\t\t\t\t\t
