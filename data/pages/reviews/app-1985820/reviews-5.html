\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCall of Duty - Vanguard: Imagine epic WWII movies like 'Saving Private Ryan,' but much shorter, less historically accurate, and with more epic shooting scenes. The creative license taken here goes even further than in Tarantino's 'Inglourious Basterds'.

You play as different soldiers from around the world, blasting your way through bombed cities and deserts. It's fun and loud, but the story lacks depth, and you barely get to know the characters before they're off on another mission and you switch to someone else.

The story tries to be engaging by showing different soldiers' experiences during the war. We see perspectives from different parts of the world, which is a good idea, but the execution is a bit mixed. Some stories are more interesting than others and it feels like they are rushing through the plot.

The gameplay is what you'd expect from Call of Duty: good shooting mechanics and intense action with great cinematics. The missions are varied, but some missions can feel repetitive. You go from sneaking around to big shootouts and all-out assaults on entrenched enemies, which is fun, but it doesn't feel new or different from previous games.

The game's campaign unfolds in a very straightforward manner; it's strictly linear and it only gives an illusion of tactical choice when you're able to direct your troops in combat - and that happens in only one mission. Right from the first mission, you're introduced to an extensive array of Allied and Axis weapons that could belong to a collector in 1950, offering a satisfying and engaging experience both in terms of variety and the actual mechanics of gunplay.

Graphics and sound are top-notch, with great attention to detail in the environments and realistic sound effects. This makes the game more immersive and enjoyable, while not requiring the most powerful hardware. A mid-range GPU will allow you to play Vanguard on Ultra settings at 1440p.

Overall, it offers some thrilling gameplay experiences and impressive visuals, but the brief and inconsistent storyline falls short of expectations. While it's a decent addition to the franchise, it doesn't quite shine as a highlight. Compared to CoD: World at War, this title represents a decline in the series' standards.

I haven't bothered with multiplayer. I'm only in it for the story. Vanguard's fun, but not amazing. It's like a tasty burger, somewhat satisfying but not a gourmet dinner. If you just want to shoot racist bastards (as described by one of the characters) and enjoy pretty explosions, go for it! If you want a deep story and something truly outstanding, maybe look elsewhere.
