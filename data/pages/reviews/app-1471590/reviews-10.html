\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBuy This.

Without a shadow of a doubt this is the best DLC by far and a must-have if you have even the slightest interest in playing Planet Zoo. Three long requested and essential zoo animals, the introduction of a much needed diving mechanic, and some fantastic pieces (probably the best we've seen) really bring this pack together into something super special. Not since the game was announced had I felt the wonder or joy like I did seeing flocks of penguins jetting through the water.

There are very few negatives about this pack. I can't help but feel like the otter animations are much weaker than the seal and penguin ones. They aren't awful but they just don't look very fluid or lively. The penguins jet around rapidly, the seals spin and bump around, the otter default dive animation looks very stiff, when near the top of the water they doggy-paddle, and the only special animation I have seen them play in water is a sort of 'roll over' that almost seems to go in slow-motion. It's definitely not bad at all but when compared to the other two animal animations it sticks out.

I've spoken in previous reviews about the disappointing animal counts, and here I think it is by far the least noticeable. We got three highly requested animals that all have entirely new rigs and animations, along with a major new mechanic, and while the caiman is definitely the 'odd man out' of the pack like we saw in Arctic with the white wolf and Australia with the dingo, the caiman still blasts both of them out of the water when it comes to how unique and interesting it feels. This pack has shown that pure roster size isn't neccesary as long as the pack can bring something new. Arctic and South America felt like they were just doing more of the same, and Australia almost felt like a step backwards in some regards, but this pack is a cannonball forwards bringing so much great stuff to the game. While more animals would have been nice, I am perfectly satisfied with what we got here, which is the first time I can legitimately say that.

One of the only negatives is that there is a hard cap on diving distance; you need a minimum of 4m for animals to dive. This makes perfect sense with the seal, but with the caiman or otter it's odd that they need this distance to dive, especially since if you provide said distance they can actually still dive in the shallower parts, they just won't begin to until minimum 4m has been met.

The pieces are great, the animals are great, the new mechanics are great. This is the first pack where there is no caveat, no bizarre design choice that hampers creativity (perhaps the 4m depth thing but even then not likely), no major disappointment. This is just pure joy, pure success, and it deserves all the positive feedback it receives. I hope that this is a sign of things to come, moving forwards and not stagnation or stepping backwards. It's a beacon of hope for the future of the game. We might just get birds, burrowing animals, and Red Kangaroo walkthroughs yet\t\t\t\t\t
