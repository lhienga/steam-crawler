\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis hurts. It's a nice dose of nostalgia, and for those with modern GPU's it's really the only way to play classic Rome without it being a stutter fest with 18 fps on the campaign map.

With that said... Feral did everything they could to make this legendary and well respected game easy to hate. Even after 70 hours with the new UI I still hate it compared to the original, and it's really obviously ported from mobile. Yes, it is functional once you get used to it, but that doesn't excuse the fact that it is still a downgrade and the ability to fix it with mods is more or less non existent.

Pathfinding is remarkably atrocious. I haven't played the original in a minute, but if there was one thing I was hoping for with the updates it was better pathfinding with more responsive units. Unfortunately, on the battle map this is probably the most unresponsive total war I've ever played. Unit disengagements have never been a strength, but here it is a huge pain point. If your skirmishers are being charged by anything closer than 75m, you can basically just accept that you're going to get slaughtered. Auto skirmish is incredibly unreliable, and I've had skirms that I tried to manually pull back actually run towards the enemy charging them instead. RIP your units if you try to enter a settlement on anything higher than large unit scale. You will frequently take unnecessary losses due to the asinine paths your units choose to take on even the most simple layouts. Cavalry charges compared to something like Rome II or even Empire feel really bad, as cavalry take an incredibly long time to group up, often charges sluggishly, and then gets stuck to whatever unit they charged, often taking very bad losses unless they are charging into tier 1-2 units, relegating them to rout/general sniping duty exclusively. Chariots are the only unit that comes out relatively unscathed since they are very good at breaking lines with move commands through a unit.

The rule changes from classic are a matter of taste, but I'd like to give a shout out to the new AI, which *is* definitely a bit more aggressive than the old AI, but is also equally more neurotic. Reputation and relations don't matter. Strength hardly matters. Being at war with another nation doesn't matter because if you border a country they will suddenly be hyper stable and never fight with their enemies. In several Briton campaigns, Gaul and Julii have become allies and remained so until they are both exterminated. Meanwhile, my allies the Spanish will attack Aquitaine every single time, despite me being twice as strong as them and the Gauls having bordering settlements ripe for the taking. It's fine, the game is about war, not peace, but the changes to AI aggressiveness seem to only apply to disposition towards the player, which serves only to annoy.

Overall, it's very underwhelming. The campaign map and battle map visuals look really good and give me that comfy vibe that I had back when I first played the original. The music is as good as ever. It's just... the changes aside from technical updates are bad across the board. Go back and play Shogun 2 or something, and give a unit a move command or try to reform/disengage from fights. The responsiveness of individual units in those games is so much better than TWR:R. In Rome II, hastati seamlessly throw their pila before closing for melee. In Rome I, the unit stops, takes two seconds to pull out their pila, and then very slowly throws them, making them pretty much only useful for flanking attacks where you already have numerical superiority. It's just dated. I think if pathfinding was better and unit responsiveness was in line with TW games from ten years ago, this would be a positive review. As it stands, I'd only recommend it if you literally can't play Rome classic and want the hit of nostalgia.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first played Rome: Total War sometime in the mid 2000s in middle school. I was a big history nerd(still am lol) and i was just looking around at a small table of games at the book fair at school one day when i spotted this. I very much enjoyed ancient history and this game to a middle schooler was just perfect. I didn't know the treat i was in for when i paid for the box collection of Rome: Total War with all it's DLCs.

I can say i enjoy this game. Do i have some complaints about the remastered edition? Well, yes. It's not perfect, but i can say it runs much better on a modern machine and has allowed me to enjoy the game from my middle school years that made me fall in love with total war games. This has been a treat and i very much plan to play more.

Pros:
----------
-AI has been improved, britons actually use boats and pose a real threat in numbers with late game units, all the factions actually use their late game units and aren't bumbling around using militia and garbage tier units all day. You will be posed with a better challenge, though maybe not to the same extent to later games like the warhammer and shogun era stuff.
-The graphics, while more colorful and not exactly true to the original, are an upgrade(of sorts) and feel pretty sleek and like it will age better than the original graphics thus preserving the game.
-All factions(besides SPQR) have been added to the playable game without modding the files, which is nice.
-Loads of achievements to unlock, giving you small semi goals.
-Lots of graphical options to tune to your playstyle, including the "unit size" option that people love to tune up to huge/experimentally large(yourself included)


Cons:
-----------
-UI does feel a bit meh?
-Some annimations have a huge pause on the map screen where you're waiting for something to happen even when the animations have all been sped up you'll stare at an army who you know is going to attack your city and it takes a solid 5 to 10 seconds before the animation actually happens, which is annoying
-The AI, while improved have taken to walking around your army to attack a city, which may have happened in the original(been a while since i've played the OG copy) but it's quite annoying to chase an army down and it just walks around you to annoyingly attack a city.
-Some of the new balancing(which you can switch to the original) feels like an improvement in some ways and a huge nerf to units that didn't need to be nerfed in other ways.


Overall, i do recommend this remaster pack especially since it includes the great DLC(even if alexander is not anyone's favorite, i enjoy it for what it is). If you enjoyed the original or are looking to try out the total war games, i highly recommend buying this - especially if you can catch it on a sale. I wish i still had my box set, i didn't know at the time that box sets of games would become rare and it'd be nice to look at the cover of it one more time.\t\t\t\t\t
