\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemember when you were a kid and you'd arrange all your toys into these dramatic battle sequences and every time you did it you would just craft the most insane lore imaginable for what was going on?

Yeah - they made a video game for you to recapture that magic with.

As someone who burned several hundred hours on another account with the first game, I'd highly recommend checking that out and exhausting everything that one has to offer. It has lots of maps and units that don't make an appearance in the sequel, including a few features I wish we'd kept from the first entry - but overall; this is all you could want in a video game sequel: genuine improvement on existing mechanics and features with new added ones that are welcome additions. It's just unfortunate we lost some of what the first game had, but for what you get in exchange you aren't too disappointed to impact your enjoyment of the game.

It's all the fun of large-scale conflict and self-generating narrative for when you don't have the mental stamina other titles like Total War that can also give you an experience like this.\t\t\t\t\t
