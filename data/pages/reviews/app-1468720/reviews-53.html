\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI decided to rewrite my review after vising this game once more with the addition of the workshop. On the surface, this game is my dream. I absolutely adore making huge battles and watching them fight to death and games like ARMA editor, X-point or hell, even cossacks or Total war allow you to do this. So when a game called Ultimate epic battle simulator 2 appears on the market offering things like more than a million on screen, tanks, dinosaurs etc of course I'm going to go mental. It's a shame it just seems so shabby and underwhelming.

I played and loved the original. Granted it was janky, but the mod support, animations and overall design of things like the UI seemed far more polished and easier to use. The UI for this game is abysmal and it's a pain setting up huge battles since you can't save battles at all. Even placing units using the map is tedious and you can't even move units prior to starting the battle meaning most of the time your soilders are scattered over the map, stuck in hills or just in places they shouldn't be (the recent DDAY map is a huge sign of this) I really think they should be looking towards the TOTAL WAR franchise for inspiration.

The animations just feel off, you can't have your units walk or stick in tight formations (line battles with muskets are a waste of time) melee units seem like the main focus here meaning ranged units are quite pathetic to watch. WW2 units are something I really want to see worked on. The addition of artillery and tanks was a genuine surprise but they're so lazily done. Tanks literally leak blood and once destroyed don't even change to a broken down version, they simply cease existing. Artillery has no one on it and it seems like they just bought some random asset off the Unreal Engine store and threw it into their game with no care or consideration. Just stick some guys on there! The Artillery also dissapears upon destruction with no effects whatsoever. Ranged units have unlimited ammo, they don't have functions guns mostly due to how the engine works (click once to shoot a bullet then wait a second) there's no reloading system and things like crouching to avoid fire are just not present. It's intense for the first bit but then gets boring incredibly quickly and I feel like extra work to animations could really help this game.

Finally updates are far slower than the previous one. The recently released mod support feels incredibly rushed as they don't allow custom animations or sounds. In fact, EVERY modded unit seems to have issues just standing still as their models glitch into the ground or flail.

Maps are ok but I'm still unsure why you can't make custom maps for the workshop or why some maps from the previous game (which were far better) still aren't present in this game.

I'm sure with time (hopefully) the devs can work and craft this game into something special. Something that will allow HUGE battles with a multitude of different entities ranging form siege weapons to helicopters (maybe) but right now it's incredibly bare bones, feels rushed and just leaves you wanting so much more. It's been a year since it released and it feels barley any different to how it was when it first came out. I hope this does not act as a demotivater the devs as I am fond of this game but right now it's really struggling to live up to its promise of a genuinely good product.\t\t\t\t\t
