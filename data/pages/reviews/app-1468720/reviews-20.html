Ultimate Battle Simulator 2: An Epic Battle Simulation

"Ultimate Battle Simulator 2 - for short UEBS2" promises to push the boundaries of battle simulation games, offering players an epic experience. The sequel to the popular first installment presents itself with improved graphics, expanded features, enhanced AI, and superior gameplay mechanics, making the game smoother and more comprohensive, along with a plethora of options to stage the ultimate battle.
The ability to let millions of units clash against each other, depending on the technical device, adds an epic and chaotic touch even to the smallest skirmishes.


Graphics and Presentation: 4/5

The graphics of "UEBS2" represent a significant leap forward compared to its predecessor.
The battlefields are more detailed, animations appear more realistic, and the variety of available units, expecially with mods, is impressive.
Visual presentation significantly contributes to the overall gaming experience.
Unfortunately, bug fixes or substantial updates have been lacking for some time.
The small development team at Brilliant Game Studios, consisting of four members, may face challanges in maintaining consistency and regularity in updates, expecially when working on other projects.
The question of current activities and plans remain open.


Depth of Play and Customization: 4/5

What truly sets "UEBS2" apart is its incredible depth of play and customization options.
Players can choose from wide range of historical and imaginative units, adjust weather conditions, and even control the time of day. The ability to customize units individually expands the possibilities further.
The game's enormous replay value comes from the freedom to create epic battles according to one's own imagination. A regular update program addressing mod issues and introducing new units and maps would be desirable and undoubtedly pleased by the community.


Game Mechanics: 3,5/5

The mechanics of "UEBS2" are solid and groundbreaking but not tactical.
The intuitive, light, and flexible controls, as well as accessible gameplay, could benefit from improvements in AI and tactical aspects.

Introducing a nuanced tactical AI for units and additional formations would deepen the gaming experiance.
Adjusting unit behaviour based on position and situation would deepen the gaming experience.
Overall, despite some shortcomings, the game provides a captivating experience.


Conclusion

"UEBS2" impresses with stunning graphics, extensive customization options, and epic battles.
Despite significant progress since its predecessor, the absence of recent updates raises questions about the activity of the small development team.
Nonetheless, the passion for detail is evident.

The depth of play and customization options offer tremendous replay value, while improvements in AI and game mechanics indicate potential for an even more challanging gaming experience.
The future of the game remains intriguing, and the team's dedication promises interesting developments.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am just getting started but my initial impression is that the size of the armies on the screen are definitely impressive. You can zoom back until they look like grains of sand fighting. The rest of the experience, however, is really thin. There are different maps to choose... but they don't appear to offer much in the way of tactical uses. For example, I saw no canyons that would create a better defense against large numbers. There is water and they seem to just march through it even when it goes over their heads. Not sure if they drown that way but fighting in water is harder. There are themes for the armies such as Roman or WWII, but no real way to mix the types of units into a single group that works together. Everything is isolated into it's own segregated groups and they just smash into each other or shoot from range... and that's it. I can't even find a way to make them move easily as units or group the units together into a force. It is all done manually using a selection box and clicking. A square is also rarely the shape you need to select the precise units you want. Also, some units, such as tanks, don't seem to want to move at all. I am not sure what I am doing wrong there.

The two scenarios that come with the game are short and a little basic. One of them makes you fight chickens and ducks and the other is the most basic zombie shooters I've seen. Survive the waves... and die trying to use the building stairs to your advantage oof...wow.
The Mods all appear to be mostly different skins for the combatants: Star Wars, Lord of the rings, Warhammer... all the normal suspects. I can't seem to find pre-made scenarios but I am still looking. More scenarios would really help this simulator. If they are in the workshop, then they need a category to find them because they are buried under a mountain of battle droid and storm trooper skins.

Otherwise, you are basically left to place different groups in sandbox mode with no real tactics or goals other than smash two oceans of enemy into each other and move a few of them around using a clumsy selection box and very basic orders. The groups you move have very clumsy controls. One move point at a time. You can't have them move to the next once they get to the first... so you sit there waiting for all 3,000 troops to finally arrive... then you can move them again... going around an enemy line is click... wait... wait... wait... click... wait... wait wait.

There may be more to discover here but my first impression is that this is basically a technical demo that lets you place and move armies. It is not trying very hard to provide entertaining uses for the simulator. You have to figure that out on your own. If I discover more I will provide additional context.

I am on the fence about recommending because of how thin the content is... but the epic size of the battles is definitely impressive... so playing around with that may give some enjoyment. I am choosing to recommend it with some reservations. I got it on sale for $11. It wasn't a complete waste of money... but I hope more content is coming besides a bunch of skins for the combatants. Watch for it on sale... maybe.\t\t\t\t\t
