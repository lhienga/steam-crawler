\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave you ever wanted to recreate the battle of 300? Or the storming of the beaches of Normandy?
You ever thought who would win in a fight between an army of T-Rex's and Sherman Tanks?
Want to see a 4-way battle between a battalion of Laser gun wielding Nicola Tesla's, a horde of Zombies, a militia of duel machine gun equipped Terrorist Puppets and a legion of sentient guns with leg?
Ever wanted to have battles consisting of ten's of thousands, hundred's of thousands or even millions of entities, each having their own individual A.I.?
Then this may be the game for you.
To be able to have this many beings on screen at the same time is almost unbelievable, especially whilst maintaining a decent Frame rate.
Be warned this is not a perfect game, even after the "Full release" I've encountered bugs, floating assets, textures not loading and frame rate drops.
The game doesn't look brilliant up close, even on the highest settings, but this is more of a zoom out and watch the million thing happening king of experience, and when looking at everything happening from afar it's rather spectacular.
It does have a campaign, but at time of writing there are only two missions. However you're really here for the sandbox and all the things you can set up.
So if you have solid PC specs and like the idea of watching insanely massive armies of various shapes and sizes battling it out for your amusement then I'd recommend getting this game.\t\t\t\t\t
