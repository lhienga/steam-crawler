\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I saw the first game, I immediately dismissed it as youtuber bait. "ULTIMATE EPIC BATTLE: CAN 100 KNIGHTS TAKE ON 10,000 CHICKENS"? Stuff like that.

In some ways that energy from the original game exists in the sequel. The big difference however: Scale. You're no longer dealing with 4 digit numbers, you're in the 6-7 digit number territory. Not only this, but bodies are persistent in this game. In battles between 100,000s of units, you will get actual mountains of corpses that advancing units will have to climb up to attack the other side. And astonishingly, in spite of all this, there is enough of a simple AI that self organized swarm intelligence emerges, and you start to see crazy things happen. Riflemen AI will try to stay on elevated ground, and you see clumps on a relatively flat surface where they are standing on the small hills.

All of these factors combine to create insanity. 20,000 shotgun infantry vs 500,000 zombies, as the zombies get mowed down by the infantry, they will advance and climb on top of the corpse hills to maximize their ability to shoot outwards. It's simplistic behavior but on such a massive scale it becomes something else entirely.

We also have hyperoptimization, the game runs almost entirely on the GPU and even with these numbers still runs smoothly.

This is still not a game per se but it's no longer just a toy. We are past Fischer Price and are now in a territory where you can get truly experimental.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough its still very early stages in the game, it is already amazing and quite frankly astonishing.

Have you ever played a game that had 10,000 characters/npc's all on screen at once? yeah !
How about 100,000, or 1 million. or 10 million. Yes you can actually have 10 million people all fighting at the same time.
Premise is quite simple, pit 2 or more teams of characters against each other for a massive fight.
You want to make 500,000 ducks fight 10,000 WW2 german soldiers,? How about 1 million zombies Vs 1 chuck norris, how about re-enacting the movie 300... You can do that..
Choose the map to play on, set up the teams (not limited to just 2 btw) then start the fight and see who wins.
Its not so much about you controlling all of the people, its more (as the title implies) a simulation, you set it all up, then sit back and see the results. Although you can "Posses" individual characters and take control of them.

Just for the hell of it, i did... 1 million zombies Vs 1 million american troops Vs 100,000 ogres Vs 1 chuck norris.
Most of the teams just ran at each other and started fighting, but due to smart path finding and intelligent characters, some of each group broke off to go and try and kill chuck. So you will get masses of people fighting just in a big crowd, but some will go off and sneak around the back way to get at someone else.

Highly recommend. 10/10 would make ducks fight zombies again.\t\t\t\t\t
