Early Access Review
\r\n\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttldr: A sequel that fails to deliver.

Today I purchased Ultimate Epic Battle Simulator 2 (UEBS2). I spent about two hours playing the UEBS1 after not playing it for a while. I'm killing time, just screwing around with it and I was having so much fun. A buddy at work and I are just laughing at some of the stuff from UEBS1, and I was waiting for UEBS2 to come off early access. I've always had reservations with EA games, but I felt that I had so much fun with the first game that the second would be a great pickup. Oh, how was I wrong.

There are some improvements to the game from the original, and then there are a lot of negatives that I have to say about this game. If you are a new player to UEBS, than I would recommend getting the first one. I'll go into the things that I like about this game, and the things that I disliked about this game.

Pro:
1. Amazing graphics. Major step up from the original version. Literally you can have pools of blood on the battlefield. All the character models look amazing. Literally, this game looks gorgeous.
2. Major improvement in the in game music. UEBS1 had that lame but catchy tune that would get stuck in your head, and was fun. The music in this game is epic af.
3. More GOD mode weapons. Some cool new god mode weapons (Noah Flood, Tsunami, and a Shotgun Smite).
4. The game tells you if you're putting too many objects in your sim. The game will give you a heads up if you're trying to load too many objects into a game.
5. Performance Improvement

Cons:
1. Maps are too large. I think that the developers were trying to figure out a way to put more units in the game, and that they decided to make the map larger. That's fine, but there is no way to resize these maps to a smaller setting. There were times where I only wanted to have small battles of 300 Spartans (yea I know) versus 1000 zombies. The problem here is the maps are so large you can't find the units when setting up the sim. I would have to increase the size of the army to 10,000 units just to try and find the location of the unit. This may not sound that bad, but when you are trying to setup a lot of small engagements it becomes a burden to try and locate the units.
2. You can edit values for the units in the game, which is really nice, but you can't edit all the values. The only values you can edit are attack damage, attack speed, and health. There are times when I wanted to edit the speed of a unit, or the defense of a unit. I did a sim of 300 Spartans vs 1000 zombies. With the base default stats the Spartans got decimated. I went into the unit details, and played around with the reach of attacks, health, and damage. Created custom units, and then reloaded it to see that there were minimal changes. I'm sure that I could have spent a lot more time playing around, and trying to create a more perfect unit in by testing small amounts of units but when the maps are so large, it becomes burdensome to try and locate a handful of units.
3. Lack of units for a sequel. Technically there are probably more units than the original, but a lot of these just have bad re-skins of other units. There are a lot of mythical, or comical units which is fine, However, there were unit types in the original game that for some reason or another they decided to not import over. No units on horseback, no type of war elephant, no sci-fi units. Categories for units are limited to just 1 or 2 units. For the fantasy section they have orcs, and trolls. In the original you had ten different types of units for that fantasy setting. Besides, with all the dragon fever everyone has nowadays I'm surprised they didn't try to put in a dragon unit in the game. They introduced tanks in this game, but only gave you a single tank type which is impervious to almost all damage. I mean if you swarm them with ducks eventually they'll get it. Yes, I said you can swarm a tank with ducks and the ducks will destroy a tank.
4. No colorblind mode. Yea I'm colorblind. Oh well. It's 2022. I think even powerwash simulator has a colorblind mode. This makes setting up a custom game irritating and very frustrating.
5. I personally didn't like the controls. It may just be a me thing, so I'm just leaving it at it.
6. It just doesn't feel fun. After ten minutes I got bored. There wasn't a way to try and make new scenarios seem fun with different types of units engaging in combat. Mainly because you're spending more time screwing around with trying to find the units in the setup.
7. With the maps being as large as they are, it would be nice to increase game speed to x4, x8, or even x10 normal speed.
8. The maps look cool, but they're just broken. Loaded up Omaha beach. First off, map size was just frustrating here, but there are pill boxes that units just run through instead of offering any type of cover. It's possible and logical that the AI has problems recognizing this issue, but then just don;'t include them. There is a ww1 theme map with trenches that offer nothing more than just an interesting cosmetic. If you have units in the trench they can't fire out of it.

There are a dozen other issues that I've had with this game, but I feel that I've went over enough.

I rarely have asked for a refund for a game, but this is an instance in which I wanted my money back. I've had a lot of luck with early access games, but I've been burnt recently with a few, and this was the final game that makes me never want to buy an early access game ever again. Developers release games that are half finished with the hopes of cashing in on the popularity of a game quickly, and then they announce the game is no longer supported. This game was supposed to come off early access by the fall of 2022, but here we are at the end of the year and it's still in EA with nothing showing it'll be coming out anytime soon.\t\t\t\t\t
