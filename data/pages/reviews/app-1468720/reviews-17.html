\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly unpolished and unfinished product.

Tough call but if I'm only allowed to do up or down, this is a down based on it's list price.

In all candor, it's fun to play and under $10, I got about two hours of enjoyment which is about what I might spend on a video rental.

My principle beefs are this.

* Very little originality that one couldn't purchase from the Unity asset store. So no real effort on the graphics front. Gets a big yawn and quickly contributes to boredom. But for a few hours, it's OK.

* User interface is College level comp-sci major semester assignment kind of product. As an example, panning around the sandbox map is not obvious.

* Editing navigation keys. It is what it is. Again, indicates that this is unfinished product.

* Settings are not saved between game play. Again, indicates that this is unfinished product.


These are just couple of the obvious glaring issues but if I were to guess, I might believe that this is a one-man effort that is working part time on this. Not bad if that's the case and in fact, if this were a $5.99 game, I would have definitely given it a thumbs up. I would love to know how many copies actually sold at the $19.99 list price.

Who knows, this game might grow over time as refinements are added. I toyed with the idea of getting a refund but I got it cheap enough and it was just OK.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know what I expected, but I definitely thought it would offer more than it did. For your money you'll get a surprisingly small variety of soldiers from different time periods and a modest list of uninspired battlefields for them to fight on. The units don't have any obvious stats or descriptions to help you choose between them, meaning you'll waste a lot of time trying to figure out what each one can do through trial and error. The battlefields aren't terribly special or well designed, but they do serve their purpose as a place for your tiny minions to murder each other. If you want anything else you have to add it on your own time by perusing the list of third party mods available on Steam Workshop. Even with mods I can't really see this being fun for more than an hour, and it certainly isn't worth the price tag.\t\t\t\t\t
