\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a shame this DLC is getting review-bombed, because the content itself is genuinely good, but I do agree it should be less expensive. I only bought it because I have over 1.6K hours in the game, so I got my money's worth a LONG time ago.

Here's what I think of the new content:
- In my opinion, Astral Rifts are generally much more interesting than archaeological sites story-wise. In the span of 3 games, I've only encountered one rift that I thought was boring. The multiple paths also offer some replayability. I do wish some rifts spawned special solar systems that you can explore though. I've only encountered one that does that so far.
- The new faction (the Formless) is cool and interesting, we finally got new crisis lore after so many years!
- The new origin is really fun to play with and allows you to quickly jump into the new content, nice!
- I've also been enjoying the new civics
- Astral actions are fun and unique

I personally don't really get the complaints regarding this DLC being Ancient Relics 2: Electric Boogaloo. Having new "archaeological" sites is a GOOD thing. Especially when they are more indepth like this. This is like complaining your favorite game got a sequel because they're reusing the characters and gameplay mechanics.

And it's not like Ancient Relics was that new either, archaeological sites are literally just a reworked event system, so it technically was also a "reskin" of already existing gameplay.

Anyways, that's my rant over, I genuinely think if the DLC cost less, people would be happy with it
tl.;dr if you like Ancient Relics and want more story content like it, you will enjoy this DLC, but wait for a sale\t\t\t\t\t

Short Summery. Adds good amount of story to the game and address some of older lore within Stellaris itself, however the price to content in this pack is weighted too much. This DLC is too expensive for what it offers. Should be sold for $10-15 USD max
Hello, my perspective would be one of who been playing the game since launch in 2016, I will be mentioning some spoilers in this review. I will only be talking about the story because I havent had much time to mess with the new civics or astral actions to

The Story, warning might be spoilers
It is nice to see the lore of Stellaris to be expanded further and the Unbidden (and their variants) are given more content and context. Their lore is expanded, and I feel somewhat sad with the rise and fall of their multi-Verse civilization. By the time they arrive in the galaxy theyre nothing more then mere scavengers clinging to what remains. There are huge problems with the story but not related to the Unbidden.

I will get into that in a moment.

In the story of Astral Planes, we would eventually obtain contact with a splinter of the unbidden race called The Formless. Initially when hearing about and seeing the price point of this DLC led my expectations that we might either have a mini-crisis or crisis event, though it does have event its more akin to a wave base horde event instead of an actual galaxy changing level event. When it is finished there is a choice between two rewards, one hidden and one shown.
The one that is shown is a legendary admiral who would be a nice choice, but the hidden reward overshadows them to a stupid degree.

The hidden reward is The Throne of the unbidden (which in lore has been talked about before this DLC by the Unbidden) its passive effect makes all of your leaders immortal. This makes any choice between the two rewards mutes. You will always go for the throne for its effects, there is no real choice. It is by far the most powerful relic in the entire game. Fun for fluff purposes but absolutely horrible when making a choice between a legendary paragon leader and that.

Enough with the Unbidden/Formless, onto other things. In the DLC, you will get to explore a place that have been much requested to go, The Galactic Core Region of the Galaxy. This was welcome surprised and wondered what would lie within the region. This is important because Crystalline Empire is supposed to exist here, yet they are not shown at all. This is infuriating. To tell you the importance of this empire, they are the ones who created the Fallen Machine Empire to combat the Contingency before fleeing to the Galactic Core Region. They get no screen time and worse you can only explore one system in the region. If they added several systems and the Crystalline Empire fully to the game, I would be more lenient, but this just feel half-baked at best.

The writers took us to the region that buried in mystery and gives us only one thing and not address other things within that area. We waited for over seven years to explore here, and you give us one system. For the price I paid to play this DLC, I feel like I been slapped.

Gameplay
There are 4 new civics, 8 new relics, and new set of actions called Astral actions.

The civics
  • Sovereign Guardianship Fun civic to play tall but it doesnt really help in playing tall, just helps with defense, should add an effect to make your pops slightly more efficient since you will have fewer worlds then everyone else.

  • Hyperspace Specialty Fun civic for exploration and really good way to get Relays faster.

  • Dark Consortium All around, really like this one, can get Dark Matter level tech without going to war with fallen empires. This effectively puts the last nail in the Fallen Empires coffin, they need a major overhaul.

  • Dimensional Worship, this civic is more gear towards the themes of this DLC but can be thematically fun to play with.

I do feel like stellaris is getting to the point that Empires should start with 3 civics instead of 2 because of all the choices there is now, or when the government system gets fully added, have a path to another civic slot.

Relics
There are more relics to find which is always nice, however can be a pain to find as their scattered across multiple new sites called astral rifts. Honestly, they should add a toggle rule for galaxy for relics being a war goal, its quite hard to find them.

Astral Actions
A lot of the choices within Astral Actions feels kind of meh but still nice to have but might honestly be forgettable after a few games.

Closing thoughts
I really love the story they added but it didnt go far enough with addressing the galactic core region, the Crystalline empire is suppose to be there yet there is no mention. If they were present, then I would find this DLC more acceptable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn one hand, late game exploration is something Stellaris has been severely lacking in since it came out, This DLC barely scratches that itch. It introduces some Astral thread "mechanics"(Yet again archaeology with a different coat of paint) that don't have much impact and by and large are just more things to activate every 10 years for temp modifiers. The actual "threads" themselves don't feel like they hold that much weight or interest behind them, you'd think a DLC themed around late-game exploration of where reality itself starts to unwind would have some more engaging content to go with this new premise, Granted in my current run I've only done 8 of the 30 or so available, but they certainly haven't been memorable in anyway. A handful of new civics are introduced, which I do actually like quite a bit for thematic and play-style variety purposes. You also get another hydraulically pressed, almost AI-generated looking species portrait, so if that's up your alley there's that.

Ive been in favor of PDX DLC releases pretty universally, but cmon guys, This costs as much as other major expansions, with a third of the content ANDDDDD its broken on release *again*. I can't wait to see custodian dev-logs about updating this dlc in 1 year.\t\t\t\t\t
