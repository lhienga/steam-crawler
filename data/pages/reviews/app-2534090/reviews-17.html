\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy problem with this DLC is the impactfulness of Astral Actions. Everything in this DLC is related to Astral Threads, generating them and using them. However, when it comes to the usefulness of it, its very minimal.

I mean, we have fleets with hundreds of thousands and even millions of fleet power by the late game, and yet the End-Game DLC gives us an Unbidden fleet that despawns and has to be respawned every 10 years. And this is one of the "strongests" Astral Actions. By the mid-game we have the Astral Action to instantly create ONE Hyper Relay, with a very long cooldown, so you just have to do that dozens of times, for decades to fill your empire network with only Astral Actions. You can also press a button to get +10% physics research... THE most powerful perk this DLC gives us are the Relics, which were included in another DLC, and their passive/active effects are much more relevant than astral actions.

I get that its supposed to be about space exploration and travel manipulation, but come on, couldn't astral actions be more relevant?

It's like Espionage, where the concept is great but its simply not worth the trouble of getting to know and maximize the mechanics behind it. The benefits of using astral actions are very nimble.

Also, it's counter intuitive with the leader rebalance (which is part of the Free Update), since now its harder than ever to have high level leaders. Then you have the option to use this high level leader to explore an astral rift and maybe get him/her killed just for some astral threads and unlocking some useless astral actions.

Espionage was abandoned, and I'm worried that Astral Actions will also be abandoned. If Paradox improves Astral Actions or give Astral Threads more uses than I think this DLC could be worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

First of all, the general premise of new outliner is good, but the execution is horrible - often times I catch myself going back and forth between government and navy tabs, half a time forgetting what I wanted to check. not to mention shipyard's displacement which makes keeping track of fleet reinforcement remarkably annoying.

Next thing - leaders. Splitting global leader cap into their respective categories was a great idea - now I don't have to limit myself to 1 science ship when building navy in the early game since I can have 3 leaders of each type. unfortunately, 90% of time I have to wait for leader re-roll, because somehow every candidate available for recruitment has strictly governor or council-related starting traits, completely useless for exploration-focused tasks. even worse when your one worthy scientist levels up and instead of useful trait gets planet-related one like Scrapper, completely wasting his potential.

Finally - bugs and balance issues. and there is a lot of them. Although I haven't encountered all of them myself, these are certainly not a good sign:
- wrong shipyard discount calculations
- defense platform limits given by Unyielding tradition tree don't increase
- leaders get the same level trait upon levelling up
- during combat, after losing battle the entire fleet gets destroyed, even if 90% of ships successfully retreated with emergency FTL
- game treats Astral Scars as planets - one guy reported that 42 Years and 3 Days event turned scar into a Gaia world
- AI ignores the fact that system is only partially surveyed by them and build their starbase anyway
- AI perfectly counters your fleet day 1 after making contact (custom Devouring Swarm empire)
- leaders get negative traits far too often and resource-related ones are far too unfair (come on, -5 to -10% energy credits due to a single governor?)\t\t\t\t\t
