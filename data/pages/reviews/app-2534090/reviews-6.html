\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat story, mediocre strategy/mechanics. Medium-High on price. Overall, I really enjoyed this DLC.

The story:
The choices feel like they have impact. You have to constantly weigh the lives of your researches against potentially greater rewards. The rifts also give really creative story lines, from living planets to jelly fish hell. There is a lot of tension and mystery for each one.

Mechanics:
The astral mechanic was meh. Just another mana, with no real impact. I suppose if you rush astral mana you can get a bunch of high level ships early but standard play seems to be much better. Personally, I would be more enthusiastic if you could do some legitimately awesome things like creating extra-dimensional colonies, or a new type of ship specifically meant for stealth attacks (submarines) or ripping a hole into the Dimensional Horror's universe (Hell) to teach them respect.

Price:
Is $20 (current full price) worth it? Unfortunately inflation is a thing, so lets look at the price in terms of purchasing power. In other words, what else could I have used the money for? For $20 right now, you can go to the movie theaters, and watch a blockbuster movie; so two hours of entertainment (worst value). You can also buy one-two months of a streaming service, like Netflix (best value).

I think the amount of content for this update, gives at least 10 hours of value considering I played at least that for this update. So, better value than the movies but far worse than a streaming service. Consequently, I think the price is a little high but not by much.

Overall: Would recommend for people, like myself, who enjoy lore & role play with their strategy games.\t\t\t\t\t
