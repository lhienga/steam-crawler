\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe astral plane (or "the Shroud") was always one of the most interesting aspects of Stellaris lore. At release our interactions with the Shroud were limited. As a spiritualist empire you got to venture into this mysterious realm through text-box interactions at regular intervals. It wasn't great, but it offered a unique playstyle and established the materialist-spiritualist dichotomy. Playing as a psychic species gave you a unique jump drive, ground forces and pop traits which weren't all that different from anything else in the game but at least felt thematic. The crowning feature was a way to ruin your own galaxy which I won't spoil here. The whole thing dripped with potential but felt stapled onto the game at the last minute but I always chalked this up to: "It's a new game. And this is Paradox. Just give them a few years to make a DLC for it".

Things that I hoped for in this potential DLC:
- Jedi/Sith inspired leaders who can use mind tricks on other NPC characters
- A full Shroud-based shipset, cityscape, and maybe an origin or two
- In-game representation of Shroud-space, or at the very least a map layer
- Shroud storms which can be manifested in the real galaxy to be wielded as weapons of mass destruction
- Finishing the base game Shroud features as part of the free update

This expansion has none of that. By now tumble weeds must float through the original Shroud from total neglect because this DLC has not detected it's existence. Instead it adds Astral Rifts, which are just archaeology sites with extra steps. And did it really need to add yet another tab to the already bloated interface? Navigating the astral plane is exactly the same gameplay as archaeology/anomaly hunting. And after 3 playthrough since getting this DLC: what the hell is even an Astral Thread? Can I sew them together to make an astral handkerchief to wipe my tears?

This is easily the least interesting DLC. Buy this only if you run out of ideas during a steam sale.\t\t\t\t\t

In summary; Yes buy this if you are a Stellaris regular and need something new to add new flavour. But don't buy this as your first, second or even third DLC. There are better options.

1) The new content in this DLC is almost purely story based, with a few extra traits and artefacts.

Astral Rifts each have decision chains where you can explore different choices on different play throughs.
Its purpose is to help keep the regulars interested. It's not one of the patches or DLCs designed to attract new customers.

Advertised as something new to explore like the L-Cluster from the Distant Stars DLC, instead the Astral Rifts are only a series of textbox events with arbitrary choices, leading to unpredictable outcomes.

Most of the time, you only gain more of a new resource, the Astral Threads. This resource has no purpose outside of a few unlockables and buffs during the early game; though eventually you find yourself startled by yet another permanent "Resource Storage Full" alert, because this resource cannot be sold or traded.



The DLC also came with a forced "free update" which ruined the few positive elements of the previous Paragon DLC, by making leaders largely useless and frustrating through changing their roles, nerfing or replacing the useful traits with useless ones.

While you can choose to simply not buy this DLC, you cannot opt out of the free update branch.
The lead development of Stellaris is not doing a good job anymore. Avoid.\t\t\t\t\t
