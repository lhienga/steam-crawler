\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have some 1300 hours in Stellaris since 2020. I have all DLC and delayed buying this due to the price point mainly and being out of work :/. I play typical single player, on larger galaxies and don't move the mid or end game around and treat each game as a campaign over several sessions. My games maybe last 6-12 hours.

There's purpose in this DLC, it's not meant to show up in the early game unless you take a set origin and you might feel underwhelmed about that at first, I was. What it does do later on is give you rifts that spawn like a dig site, beautiful purple, blues, reds and pinks and you have to divert resources to know enough about the first rift to actually then do anything with it.

After this, you can allocate a scientist. Unlike dig sites and anomalies there is no right click or automation for this(yet?). One weird aspect about this is, the leader screen to select a scientist will be empty unless a free scientist is crewing a ship. There, they will fly to it and begin exploring it and will unfurl a story and there you make actual choices of how the story goes. These stories are a bit more, in my opinion - deeper, than most most / all other sites and thats something some people will enjoy more than others.

As you finish exploring a rift, you can make use of the Discoveries screen and each successive rift explored enables you to use your growing amount of Astral Threads towards unique activations. They function like relics, some with limited uses, others with long "cool downs". A lot of those activation's are useful or even super-awesome but they are not game-upsetting either.

This DLC also comes with three new music tracks and they are nice but maybe not quite up to the same standard of Stellaris that I'm use to, there is a high bar there though.

This DLC does add more relics to the game but i can't comment on those yet; My purchase was based around more stories, a deeper aspect to the mid game and yeah, that's what I got.

[Post-edit] Seems the AI does use rifts too, so thats cool. Also you can automate rift exploration with science ships, I either didn't notice or maybe its a dynamic menu (probably the former!).

For those that rush the game and bring down those mid / end game years and zerg the AI or even play competitive MP(which are by in large, short games too), you could be forgiven for skipping this DLC as you might find them a chore but for me, I like the flavour this adds and wets my whistle and adds to the mid game where a lot of people complain "not enough happens".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this DLC mostly because I have a compulsion to buy all the DLC for games I enjoy. Paradox and EA own my bank account. With that said, I objectively this DLC is better than people give it credit for. The astral rifts mechanic is a nice addition for your science ships and the perks you get from it are nice. Of these, the best perks are the ability to shut down l-gates and a second, independent jump drive. Pretty handy when the late game war breaks out.

Is this the best DLC? No, that would be Federations. Is it "mostly negative" worthy? No, but I would never pay full price for it.\t\t\t\t\t
