Also, at the time of writing the current patch is riddled with game breaking bugs, such as most cost reducing and cost increasing modifiers not working. For these reasons you shouldn't buy it normally.

This leads into another positive for this DLC, that being the respect it has towards the lore. While I haven't explored all the rifts that are possible to explore, I've gone through about half, and so far they've all added to the existing lore, without any contradictions or retcons. Astral Threads are also a cute new resource to play with, but aren't revolutionary.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo the base price of Astral Planes is the same as Utopia, but it doesn't provide as much value. Thing is though, neither do the other expansions. It's a little unfair to compare this DLC to probably the best one the game ever got. It's fairer to compare it to Overlord and Nemesis and frankly, I think I'll be getting more value out of Astral Planes than I did out of either of those two. Everybody gets rifts, they're non-linear and replayable, and they give your scientists something to do after they've ran out of anomalies and archeological sites. Plus the astral actions like putting a lock on wormholes or activating a second relic are just nice to have.

So in conclusion, Astral Planes is not a must have DLC and there are others you should get first (Utopia, Federations, Synthetic Dawn), but it nicely rounds up an already extensive DLC collection and the complaints about the price are somewhat exaggerated. If you already have all the other DLC and you liked Ancient Relics, then this one will be worth the price too. If you have just the base game, get Utopia instead.\t\t\t\t\t
