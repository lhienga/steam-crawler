\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlatinum Review:

Christmas of 2009, I rushed down the stairs to unbox the game in it's original glory. The unique take on a tower defense game led it to the upper echelons that earned the Game of the Year award. Everything in this game feels well-thought out: The cost of each plant, their unique capabilities, and their synergies.

It's a short game that mostly respects you time. The base campaign takes 5-10 hours and the minigames, puzzles, and survival another 5-10. There are a host of unique challenges in the form of achievements such as storing up 8000 sun that require you to think outside the box and push your plants to their limits.

My biggest gripe is the achievements Cryptozombologist and Towering Wisdom, the first of which requires playing through nearly the whole campaign again (which is just the same thing I already did with very little changes???) and the latter requires storing up a ton of in-game currency to do nothing more than get an achievement. They are time-wasters and a blemish on an otherwise amazing game.

For five dollars, you could do a lot worse. It's painful to see your graphics downgrade so much, but it suits the simpler style. If you missed the PvZ craze or disliked the subsequent games, I still recommend checking this out.\t\t\t\t\t
