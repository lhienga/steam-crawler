\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI realize that I do not exactly have enough time to justify a review, however, i DID buy the game on this platform solely to make a review about the years of experience and memories i have with this game. so, here you go.

Funnily enough, I got introduced to this franchise with PVZ 2. What is nowadays considered a broken mess, filled with unfun mechanics and updates that are unnecessary, was a worthy sequel around the time of release. I was bored and was sitting on my couch, looking at the new games on the App Store (mobile games around that time were actually fun and not like nowadays). I found myself a little game called PVZ 2. The gameplay seemed engaging and intresting, and I got it.
Flash to two months later and I am absolutely obsessed with the first and second games. Like, gushing about how good they are. The sheer amounts of content and replayability, combined with the new approach of "game heavily rng-based but you can shape how the game goes to your desire" would always be one of the reasons as to why I was hooked. This same approach is why I am still hooked on games like Rimworld and The Binding of Isaac.
I tried out the first game also, and had even more of a blast with it. I currently have 300-400 hours(?) on the Xbox version of the game, and these hours are multiplying as I write this review.
After this came what might possibly be my favorite shooter franchise of all time; the PVZ Garden Warfare games. I enjoyed my time with the first one, thought it was unique and charming, and the way they incoporated seamless combat into a hero shooter game where there WASN'T a meta for once and nobody was overly competitive and toxic like a little made this game perfect for me.
And then the second game released
and oh
OH
OHHH
OOOOHHHHH
it was almost near perfection. the ABSOLUTELY MASSIVE amounts of new content and the new improved multiplayer still makes Garden Warfare 2 worth the money. And the third shooter released, and this is when I started losing hope in this franchise, and with PVZ 3 being a Homescapes clone, I don't think it will ever recover. Maybe a good game or two, but still.

Still, the old games and shooters are an absolute blast, and I know this for a fact.. if PVZ still exists in a hundred years, it will be a game remembered by everybody; still in a hundred years.

i love you PopCap.
10/10\t\t\t\t\t
