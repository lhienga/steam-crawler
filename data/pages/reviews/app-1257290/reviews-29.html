\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarly impressions are very positive for me, but do be aware that some players are suffering from performance issues.

So far, I think I enjoy the minor changes they made to the combat system (while I personally would prefer a return to the older battle systems, I do think the changes I seem so far here are positive, like gaining Tactical Level by using skills instead of having to save all AP for it).

Early on the Synthesis system seems to be mostly the same, which is fine - Atelier Ryza had my favorite alchemy system in the series, though I'd prefer it without Item Rebuild. The "recipe tree" is a good addition to re-learn the recipes, too.

Now can someone please go hammer some basic fashion sense into Ryza's head?

Update: After playing for a few dozen more hours, I must say I'm extremely happy with this sequel. The combat system in particular is a fantastic evolution of the first game one, and I say this even though I generally prefer more slow-paced combat in JRPGs. It is a really well designed and fun system.

If you are already used to Atelier's crafting, I strongly recommend downloading a "clear save data" to play on the hardest difficulty on your first playthrough, as the game is otherwise too easy.\t\t\t\t\t
