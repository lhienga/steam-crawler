\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great sequel from Atelier Ryza 1. Recommended to play it if you like Ryza 1. Its better if you play and complete Ryza 1 first before playing this game since the story is direct continuation from Ryza 1, 70% of the characters are also from Ryza 1.

Detailed review:
Story: 3/5
The story is much more simple and straightforward but more relaxing. There is a plot twist but its very predictable. Need to mention here that 90% of the dialogue is voiced, even the side quest & side stories (excluding DLC)

Character: 4/5
Most of the important characters are the same with few additions. If you love the characters at Ryza 1, you will glad to see the continuation of their character development. Ryza 2 is filled with likeable characters.

Battle: 4/5
They tweaked the battle system to become more active battle. You will now have ATB instead of taking turn and can mash buttons to unleash attack combo. Fun, but in the end Atelier series are all about using items. While skills and attack can do 20k damage, items can easily deal 200k damage. Also difficulty is way too easy, I play on Hard the whole playthrough and the enemy barely fights back. Harder difficulty require you to beat the game first.

Alchemy: 5/5
The alchemy now is have even more feature! More ways to discover recipe, combine two items to become stronger items, and for some recipe you can choose which category & which element it want to have. I personally find this as the strongest element in the game.

Music: 3/5
Its mostly relaxing or cheerful flute BGM. Standard for atelier series, nothing stands out.

Graphic: 3/5
Some places are pretty, but the character model and animation really need improvement, which is overdue for the atelier series.



Overall: 3.5/5
Great game, but I recommend to wait for discount. Atelier series are weirdly priced IMO.\t\t\t\t\t

- Constantly getting interrupted by side-story events playing automatically.

- You may get some slight Tomb Raider vibes, what with exploring ruins, finding clues, and Ryza squeezing herself through narrow openings in walls lol

- They've added the feature to customize your Atelier, which is extremely limited! I don't know why they even bothered, but you can get some cute plushies and that made me smile :)
- There is one other thing I am not mentioning, but it was a nice surprise for me, so I leave it a surprise for you!

Took me a bit longer that I was expecting, and started to get a bit tired by the end, but overll, relaxing and wholesome times were had <3 I enjoyed the story and liked the character development.
I look forward to Ryza 3!\t\t\t\t\t

Atelier Ryza 2: Lost Legends and the Secret Fairy is a brilliant sequel that expands upon everything that made the original great, while forging a path forward that can be further expanded upon in the trilogy’s final chapter.

Not only I love how Atelier Ryza 2 show a great growth of character development for the original cast of charactersꓹ it also has a large amount of stories waiting for you to discover – making the character content very engaging.

There is also a huge improvement in its complex alchemy systemꓹ comparing to its predecessor – and its wide expansive world are just too engrossing to explore. It also has very interesting approaches to exploration and combat on par with the fantastic soundtrack.

Neverthelessꓹ the game can be pretty overwhelming due to its massive numbers of mechanics. The alchemy system is complex enough on its own, with the skill tree, item fusion, item reduction, and the aforementioned Essence system to contend with. There is ingredient gathering, of course, which requires a variety of tools such as an axe, bug net and fishing pole. While gathering ingredients in the world, Ryza can also use a grappling-hook like rope to swing around and breathe underwater thanks to Air Drops.

Nextꓹ there is a combat system with real-time elements, action and item points, and a tactical system based on completing characters' in-battle requests. Then there are quests, which Ryza must complete in order to improve her reputation around town. Some quests are multi-part, and progress the character arcs of various party members and NPCs. Ryza is also in charged with helping the town's merchants develop their stores by selling them various items she gathers and makes. Finally, she can earn items and themes to decorate her atelier, as well as raise a pet Puni (a jelly-like creature) who can fetch her ingredients of varying quality depending on how well it is fed.

With all the sheer amount of mechanicsꓹ there were several times during the game when I felt overwhelmed as there are a lot of information to keep track of. I personally didn't feel like I had a good handle on the game and all its various parts at the beginning until I was nearly at the end. However, this abundance of mechanics definitely does not make the game unplayable, as it could be quite refreshing and appeal to players who enjoy having lots of different things to do.

Overall, Atelier Ryza 2: Lost Legends and the Secret Fairy is a thoroughly pleasant game and an excellent entry in a rather unique JRPG franchise. Atelier fans will definitely enjoy it, but newbies to the series are encouraged to try it out as well. While the sheer number of mechanics may feel overwhelming at first, the result is an elaborate game with so much to do - but no real pressure to do them all if you don't want to. Besideꓹ who can refuse to explore the natural world with our adorable little Fi?\t\t\t\t\t

How to run on steam deck
The issue with the PC port is that the game maxes out your gpu each frame which is terrible for performance and battery. For example, the game was maxing out my 3060 on PC and heated my Steam deck to high heaven. Here is how to make the performance better on PC and run amazing on the Steam deck. This fix should also work for other Gust PC ports like Sophie and Blue Reflection, but some might not launch.

1. Google "atelier fix" and look for doitsujin on github. Should be one of the top results.
2. Open local files in the game's properties and extract the fix to where the exe is. (Rest of the guide is for steam deck only)
3. Look up how to get the latest GE Proton, there are several guides and it will help get better performance out of most titles on the deck.
4. Set the in-game settings to medium. The game has an issue with saving the settings, so you may have to enter the game, save, and return to title screen. I eventually got it to work, but it was strangely resistant to saving. It might be a steam cloud issue, so playing the game for a bit after changing the settings might do it.
5. Hit the "..." button and bring up the performance tab. Toggle on per game settings and set the deck to 40hrz. The game runs totally fine at 40fps, it gets you a much longer battery and the vent won't kill you anymore. Stable 40 is also way better than unstable 60 in some of the larger areas.

If you could do me a favor, please hit "no" when steam asks you if "verified" is a good description for the game. Gust/KT really need to improve their PC ports or hire the person who made the fix cause this game does not deserve to be labeled as "great on the deck" without the fix.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood stuff. If you liked the first one, you'll like the second.

It's kind of the perfect sequel in that it doesn't deviate much from the look and feel and style of the first, if at all, but does have enough changes and minor additions to feel like you're doing new stuff. That's really appreciated.

PLOT

One day they'll make an Atelier game with a truly excellent story, just not today. This tale had less stakes than Ryza 1, I felt, but also was a bit less home-y and comfortable (you are literally far from home).

BATTLE

The previous battle system had a reliance on one interesting decision: do you use your AP to execute awesome moves, or do you save your AP so that you can increase your Tactics to execute awesome-er moves down the line?

The Ryza 2 system removes that risk/reward decision entirely. Now, executing awesome moves increases your Tactics to execute awesome-er moves. Hm. Okay, it feels alright, but it lacks a little something.

Using items in battle has never been better, however. You can now equip four items and use as many of them on a single turn as you have the CC to spend. If you can afford it, you can use a buff item, a healing item, and two attack items all in one turn! That's good. That's great, even. Love this system.

MUSIC

I prefer the Ryza 1 OST, but there's nothing wrong with this one. It feels good, some real nice tracks.

REDUNDANCIES

Hmmm, this is a weird category for a game. What does it mean?

Ryza 2 has a lot of what I feel are unnecessary parts. Redundancies. Some of these are vestigial, as in they were part of the last game and no longer serve a purpose. Others are additions that never fully integrate and wear out quickly.

Of the former variety, you still have landmarks. These are special places in the world with special names that add special markers to the map. Finding all the landmarks in Ryza 1 gave you exploration points or something like that, and also allowed you to fill in diaries written by the character of your choice. It wasn't brilliant, but it worked and made the world feel good. But you don't have that same thing in Ryza 2. You just have the landmarks. They make a nice sparkle, say the name, add a marker to your map, and then mean nothing for the rest of the game.

Of the latter variety, you have Popularity. As you do quests for certain groups of people (Children, Students, Scholars, etc.), they like you more, and level up. When they level up, their quests increase in challenge and you get some discounts at the shops. The discounts aren't tracked, so they are hardly noticeable. The upgrade in quest challenge doesn't feel like a reward so much as normal quest progression. And each group of people, of which there are very few, level to max (only 3 levels) pretty easily within a few hours of basic play mid-game. This won't stop the game from reminding you that a quest is giving EXP to each group, but it will stop these notifications from being useful in any way.

SP is also weird.

You have a Skill Tree to unlock basic recipes and skills. As you explore the plot and craft items, you earn SP for the tree. Around mid-game, you should have more than enough SP to unlock everything from the tree, and it's just a matter of moving far enough in the plot for them to allow you to do that. So SP quickly becomes redundant. However, you never stop earning it. Meanwhile, you start unlocking Gems as a currency around mid-game, and also unlock Essences, a currency of which there are five. This means after battle, on top of items you gain, you can gain a variety of currencies:

- EXP
- Coins
- Gold Coins
- Gems
- Fire Essence
- Water Essence
- Lightning Essence
- Wind Essence
- Light Essence

So you're earning those, and also you're earning SP on the side for crafting, and Popularity for doing quests, even though these last two are now redundant. It seems like it could have been streamlined a bit. Make it so Gems work on the Skill Tree, and by the time you are near unlocking everything, you have to really decide if you want to spend Gems on new skills or use them for their inherent crafting purposes. I don't mind juggling 10 weird currencies, but it's a shame when most of these currencies lose all value while others are still being introduced.

ALCHEMY

The crafting is great. I like the attempt to mix up the formula by making the Quality of items accumulative instead of an average, although the freshness is the only thing I really like about it. In the end, getting Quality is easier than it has ever been, and I prefer a little more challenge. Everything else works pretty similarly to the previous installment save the Essence System, which is kinda cool but, like most of the new ideas, not as cleanly integrated as I'd have hoped.

OVERALL

Good game, recommended for Atelier fans. I'll be buying Ryza 3 when it comes out.\t\t\t\t\t
