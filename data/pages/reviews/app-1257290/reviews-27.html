\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechanically superior to the previous game but with the setting not being as clear an improvement.

I'm not far enough along to comment on the plot. The ruins feel a bit too much like a stereotypical JRPG dungeon you can get out of a random dungeon generator. The lore that goes with them is far superior to the layout and implementation. The setting feels a bit more alienated, likely a natural consequence of swapping from a village where the main character grew up to a city the main character is visiting for the first time.

Crafting seems as good if not better than the previous game. Gathering is pretty good as well, with the 'see what you can gather' being a very nice feature for saving bag space.

One of the biggest improvements is how CC work for using items, making them something more than 'boss only'. CC now charges within a battle, so using items in one battle doesn't mean you will have less uses when you get to the boss.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I consider Atelier Ryza 1 probably the worst Atelier game to this date, Ryza 2 improves on literally every bad thing of Ryza 1. I can't say how the entire game is, after just 8 hours, but I can say that this is very unlikely a game I won't be able to recommend. And no, you don't need to play Ryza 1 to play this. Just note that going back from this to Ryza 1 would be absolutely awful.

Gust always did this. After a bad game you'd get a game fixing a whole lot of the previous game. Mana Khemia after Iris 3. Atelier Totori after Atelier Rorona (vanilla, not plus/DX what's on steam). Atelier Shallie after Escha and Logy (which wasn't quite as bad as the other examples, but still the worst of the good Atelier games) And now Ryza 2 after Ryza 1-

-Cutscenes so far improved. No more terribly written characters you hate and randomly become good people without any development. (I don't know what the future of the game brings, but there are no terrible characters so far, period, so it's hard to become like Ryza 1)
-The music, while still Ryza style of sorts, is much closer to what you want in an Atelier game. The main town theme alone is so good, I don't mind randomly walking instead of warping. (!)
-The CC system has been vastly improved. No more item sacrificing. Actually having sustain between battles. Other characters you don't control have their own CC pool (I'm pretty sure), so swapping for items is now a thing.
-Item usage in battles has been improved. You have time to choose them, can use multiple at once and other fun things, with more intuitive controls. CC are built by using skills and the "consumable" items seem, on first glance, very strong. (of course they don't actually get consumed)
-The ranks in battles have been improved. You don't have 1 hit normal attacks on rank 1 anymore, which makes it take forever to upgrade. You also don't need to spend AP to increase the rank anymore, just use them.
-Lot's of comfort functions, like seeing what items an object yields, are already available from the beginning
-The world is bigger, and you can even go to the first Ryza's areas with.. some kind of DLC I'd assume. I don't know if all are available, but quite a few at least are there.

There's more, and the world is this time actually quite beautifully made. But I think this is enough to give a good idea. If you didn't like Ryza 1, this is still worth checking out. If you liked Ryza 1, this game does everything better, provided you are fine with the game's scale being bigger. And you even have auto save now. Took them a while.


edit: After playing a lot more there are mostly two things I'd like to say.
-The writer is probably the same. There are still a lot of the same issues like in Ryza 1, though it's not quite that extreme. Doesn't change though that the cutscenes are extremely uninteresting. They can be skipped though, and probably should be to quite an extend.
-Hard mode is about as hard as easy or even very easy in Ryza 1. You can fix that with cheat engine and set it to legendary, which is maybe a little over Ryza 1's hard mode, but it's a pretty huge issue. I was even thinking changing my recommendation here, because in all honesty, the difficulties the game allows you to choose normally will drag down the game A LOT, add to that the boring and sleep inducing cutscenes, and you have yourself a good game to forget. If you play on Legendary however it can be quite fun.\t\t\t\t\t
