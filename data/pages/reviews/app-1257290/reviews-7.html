\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing through 5 of Gust games throughout the past year (Blue Reflection 1 & 2, Atelier Sophie 1 and Atelier Ryza 1 & 2), I can safely say that Atelier Ryza 2 is the one I enjoyed the most and the one that feels the most complete to me (with Blue Reflection 2 being a close second), as it combines all the good parts of the other Gust titles I experienced.

I felt that Sophie 1 was too focused on the slice of life character scenes and didn't really have any cohesive plot. On the other hand, I felt like Ryza 1 got too focused on the main adventure plot and was lacking when it came to character scenes. Ryza 2 combines the good aspects of both by having many individual character scenes while retaining a central plot, albeit a looser one than its predecessor.

Newly added characters feel a lot more fun to me than the original party.

The combat is also massively improved in design over the previous game, especially when it comes to AP and core item charge generating and management. Exploration and other gameplay systems got a ton of love too with all of them being improved in one way or another.

The art is as good as ever, especially the environments.

The overall game feel is also improved, as the UI feels a lot more slick and modern, less like older Atelier titles and more like Blue Reflection 2, which came out a few months after this game.

Same goes for the PC port. PC ports of Gust games have always been bad, especially when played with mouse and keyboard. Ryza 2 is no exception but there are still significant improvements over Ryza 1. Default key mapping and controls for keyboard are much more intuitive, and the mouse actually works and does stuff like controlling the camera... incredible, I know.

Overall, if you enjoyed Ryza 1, I see absolutely no reason why you wouldn't love Ryza 2 (possibly even more) other than perhaps a less focused main story, which is balanced out by a lot of interesting lore you uncover throughout the game.\t\t\t\t\t

It builds upon the strong points of Ryza 1 very well (the crafting system, the visuals, the music), the combat is much more fluid, the story and character side quests have a more personal tone and the map exploration is much better thanks to the added verticality of climbing vines and the dive mechanic.

It still just reskins monsters all over the place, which gets stale, and the boss fights are still very few and very basic (and also reskin bosses all over the place), but if you liked the first, this one has a similar experience with some details better ironed out.

10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
