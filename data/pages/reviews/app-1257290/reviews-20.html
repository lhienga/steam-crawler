\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRyza 2 was a was pleasure to play. Very pleasant.

I wasn't expecting too many gameplay changes from the first Ryza, but they actually did pretty well in this regard. Beast riding, underwater exploration and the new item rush system kept exploration and battles fresh and interesting. Alchemy is pretty much unchanged, but that's fine, it worked well enough.

The story is pretty much what you'd expect from an Atelier game. Lightweight, not too deep, but plenty of content. It was a treat to see how much these characters have grown and changed since their last adventure together.

There was the odd moment they tried to make the story dark, or poignant, with mixed results. At one point, one of the characters completes his redemption arc and begs the party for forgiveness. I had to laugh. Buddy...You didn't even do anything that bad. Everyone in this game is nice to a fault xD

A few nitpicks. They focused too much on atelier decorations instead of extra costumes, which are almost non-existent. SP gain was too high. I had basically unlocked everything needed with it halfway through the game. Creaminea ingots were a bit too difficult to make. The game effectively locks away the ability to make them until you've explored enough ruins, and by the time I got one, I was able to jump from a tier 2 weapon straight to a tier 5 weapon.

Game difficulty was too easy. Set it to hard if you want any challenge at all. The camera was also a little funky at times. All in all though, there were no issues that were a deal breaker.

If you're in the mood for a new JRPG, I'd check it out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtelier Ryza 2: A Sequel you can Skip.

I absolutely loved the original Atelier Ryza. I was actually about to beat the game for a second time when this game was announced. When I heard it was coming soon, I immediately put down my controller so I could play it with a fresh mindset. Unfortunately, the sequel is a disappointment. Many times, I stopped playing this game, but I had some good times along the way and decided to finish it.

Story (2/10): This sequel requires to you have played the first game to understand it, and it is a very poor story. The party seems to be investigating underground ruins for no real reason, and they seem to stumble through an adventure with a series of coincidences. All the original characters return, but offer no interesting dialogue and no character development. The new characters introduced are one-dimensional, and each of them focus their dialogue on a boring side-quest.

Gameplay (7/10): Much of the gameplay is exactly the same as the original game. The Alchemy system is almost exactly the same, with minor additions. This isn’t so bad since crafting is fun, and perfecting synthesis feels good. Exploration falls flat, the level design now includes many narrow corridors with a small field of view, even when zoomed out. You spend much of your time in underground ruins which seem similar and bland. The new system of finding ‘clues’ through memories make you backtrack through the levels, and matching the ‘clues’ with the ‘puzzle’ is simply tedious. Combat is more fluid, I like the addition of swapping out characters, and the building of ‘CC’ points to reuse your core items. The new challenges are actually fun. Overall gameplay is OK, although the difficulty is too easy even on hard.

Visuals and Audio (5/10): The graphics on this game look worse than the original. You can sometimes see the textures in the level design. Characters movements look stiff and production quality is lacking. Graphical assets look like they needed more polishing, but never really got it. The music is nothing impressive, which is a shame because this series often has some amazing soundtracks.

Reused Assets: Here is one of the biggest issues. This game was produced very quickly, so a lot of assets are reused in this sequel are from the original. Many alchemic recipes and items are exactly the same and it makes it feel like we are solving the same puzzles from the original. The enemies are exactly the same making combat feel repetitive. The music is somewhat similar so no songs really stand out, and they even reuse some of the original storyline which makes this story boring and predictable.

Overall, I would still give this game a passing mark. The core gameplay is quite good, and it is addictive to unlock and create new items. However, I cannot recommend this game. If they made an Atelier Ryza 3, you could skip this game and not miss anything important or enjoyable. You can buy this game and have fun with the core gameplay, but the magic of the original Atelier Ryza just isn’t here. (Review: 6.5/10)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely solid successor to Ryza 1, a step up in every way.

Cute character stories, lots of fun systems that all feed back into the synthesis loop, cool ways to continue to improve your items after synthesis. And while the synthesis itself is kind of dumbed-down compared to other Atelier games still, it does a lot to remove the tedium that may keep out new fans while still keeping enough depth to pay out for those that get really invested in it.

The real-time turn-based combat has been way improved as well. There's a ton you can do to eke out more damage, lots of things to chain together, and having a fourth character you can sub in really lets you be constantly doing something instead of waiting on your next turn. I was hesitant to call Ryza 1 combat better than some of the previous strictly turn-based ones, but Ryza 2's combat is the best in probably the whole Atelier series.

As for some things I didn't like as much:

The exploration diary + skill tree felt like a way to get back somewhat to the Mysterious idea diary thing but kind of fell flat for me. It's not difficult to max out the skill tree a little too early, and just be left with a bunch of skill points and skill point rewards wasting away. If the later DLCs add anything to the skill tree I'll probably be able to just outright buy it out right when it comes and still have leftover.

The overarching story of the game is also a little whatever. I like the individual character stuff a lot (Minor spoilers, opinion on state, no specific story beats. Do not read if you are already getting the game) and was absolutely crying at the ending chapter of the game. But the main story itself has basically no impact on the world itself. No one outside your group even really knows what happened, nothing has really changed. Ryza 1's "save the local area" plot was cliche as all things but it at least meant we had an impact. It's a party JRPG, and the individual member stories were more than enough to keep me going personally, so this did not affect my enjoyment of the game. I can see how this would be a detriment to others though, it's worth mentioning.

Basically, if you played and liked Ryza 1 this is a must-get. This game is my new favorite Atelier game, finally dethroning Sophie -- the first one I played.

Do you need to play Ryza 1 first? Yes, it really is a direct continuation unlike with the previous games' series where the hopping of protagonists meant each was its own contained story. A vast majority of the character story and the foundations of the game story will go right over your head without it. Ryza 1 is a good game I'd recommend as well anyways.\t\t\t\t\t
