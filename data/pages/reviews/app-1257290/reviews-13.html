\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I'm writing this review under the assumption that you've played the first game, and also as someone who as of right now has only played these two Atelier games and certainly enjoyed them quite a bit. I would recommend this game 100% and think its a very good game, but I need to make some notes here towards anyone interested especially if they've only played Ryza 1.

There is virtually no reason a Ryza 1 fan would dislike this game if they liked the first. That's both a good and a bad thing, because while the game does almost everything the first game did but very slightly better mechanically, it does so TOO similarly. The enemy variety is already incredibly small and repetitive in this game, but it's also nearly completely the same as the first game's. Many of the collectible resources are also the same early on. Despite some cool new things added such as ruin exploration and the alchemy skill tree you unlock things from it's going to feel a lot too much like the first game especially if you've very recently finished the first so I'd recommend waiting a bit before jumping into it.

The story is also a bit more aimless in this one for a time. I definitely feel like though the potential for it to be better was there it really never develops the way I hoped it would but it's not "bad."

The music was a strong spot for me, certain areas gave me vibes from games with awesome tracks like Xenoblade. Nothing but positives to say about the ost.

Overall, highly recommended but put some space between this game and ryza 1 if you're interested. Very fun game and I really would like to see a Ryza 3.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - This game is good if you want more of the experience of the first game, but several QOL changes accompany a significantly easier game. Play the first if you haven't played either, pick this up if you enjoyed the first. Alternatively, play this first if you want the Ryza experience without as much grinding.

There are a few issues with the game that are notable considering it is the second PC game in the series:
- Really crappy console style FOV. It can be nauseating at times being so zoomed in.
- Bloom is broken when turned on making everything un-viewable.
- Audio stuttering issues in game and in cut scenes.
- Peripherals may not be correctly detected on the first game launch, requiring a close and relaunch.
- The challenging bosses are only in pirate DLC.

While there are many nice quality of life changes compared to Ryza 1, the difficulty of this game, not including the DLC is significantly reduced in the base game. Not as much grinding and synthesis is required for completion as was for the first game if you just want to rush the main story.

The game plays very similar to its predecessor, with the loop of explore the maps, collect materials, convert them into more powerful weapons and armor almost unaltered from the recipe used in the first. The synthesis system has changed, and a few more combat mechanics are available. While you will have to get used to the new synthesis changes, the new combat stuff can pretty much be ignored unless you want achievements.

Combat is turn based, real time, where you pick from your arsenal of skills, items, or just plain old wacking things. The AI is OK, but I wish there would have been a better way to fine tune them to be more then just opposite spectrums of just slash or use all the AP. Being able to assign roles would have been nice, and setting AP thresholds or item only use would have been a great addition.

The world is OK, similar exploration to Ryza 1 with a new focus on exploration via swimming. There are a pretty good amount of different maps, and you can even visit parts of a few locations from the prior game. There is a good amount of backtracking, but it is limited just below the point of being excessive.

The story, IMO, is very similar to the first, but just not as good. The new story does kind of continue off of the first Ryza game, but it is forced by the introduction of the character Fi, which is the main reason why everything happens the way it does and it makes the story just seem a bit forced. Following Serri's side quest line could just have easily have been turned into the main story, and I think would have been more interesting than cleaning up the mess made by Fi. If there is a Ryza 3, maybe it will expand on the afterwards of Fi, or combine with Serri's story to let you see the outcome of all your work.

At the end, the underwhelming last boss, and the story's completion just made me feel kind of empty. It seems they forgot to add the "Feeling Full L" trait to this one. However, it is still worth some time if you enjoy the Ryza world.\t\t\t\t\t
