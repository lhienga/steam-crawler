\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA reunion of childhood friendships, The warmth of companions in the big city.
As a returning player of the first Ryza, i think this is a great recommendation to anyone who like close knitted personal stories in JRPGS. A common comment is that its generic a JRPG story, but the execution of the personalities in Ryza is what makes it good. Everyone eats fish and chips, it may be a staple, but the crux is in its execution. The way characters interact with each other reminds me of my relationships in real life.

While the first game has a chill, childhood vibe that brings forth a pleasant easy going experience. Ryza is now venturing into the big grand capital. Everything feels bigger and grander, yet like in the city many people feel like faceless passerby...yet the warmth and bond of Ryza's friendships is what makes this game feel so good. The music also has taken on the grand feeling, although I personally much prefer the easy going vibe of the first.

The new stuff:
The 2 new characters patty and clifford, initially put me off. But they grew on me slowly and I started to like their personalities and also clifford is the second best bomber. =)
The combat has been improved, more dynamic then the first and I really liked that you could combine different bombs/tools to do a super attack/buff.

Alchemy:
Its the same as the first, easy for straight forward to grasp. Its always a addicting pleasure to get your item levels to the maximum potential.

In conclusion, if you want to play a chill, good vibes and personal story-centric game. Ryza 2 is for you, highly recommended from me without a doubt and yes, Ryza grew thicker in all the right areas ^^

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtelier Ryza 2 is a beautiful sequel to a nice and relaxing first title. Let's explore the ups and downs of the game (+, +/-, -, ?):

+The game looks and sounds, once again, fantastic! I just need this OST! I also really appreciate the weather and time effects, especially the rain! Ah! Enjoyment!
+It's great to see the original game's characters grow up. Tao is a perfect example. He's grown into a fine young bookworm. Remember how wimpy he was in the first one? A major improvement! Ryza is still lovable and sweet, Lent is still on the path of becoming a warrior, Klaudia is now a boss and Bos is Bos. I wish we'd seen more of Empel and Lila , who are now just NPCs.
+Patty and Serri are great new characters* who fit in perfectly with the old cast. I do wish Serri joined the team earlier... but I guess it's not every day that gorgeous creatures join your team. There's something about Serri that I really, really like; a pleasing character I'd ask out for a coffee. Oh, and don't you forget the new pet character! Lovelyyyy!
+All the quests are simple and relaxing, much like the overall atmosphere of the game. There's no hurry, nothing is that threatening, just take your time and relax with this chill RPG.
+The chillness also applies to the alchemy system, which is deep and addictive. You might end up spending hours in crafting at a time, but unlike in most games, it's so goooood here!
+I loved the ending. I so like these characters and I wanna see them again. Luckily, I know I will!
+/-The story as a whole is not that bad, but the ending feels rushed. The Underworld baddies are here for about an hour before the game ends. Oh, you can't close the gate to Underworld , but then you can, because a character now knows this deus ex thing to do, which we don't see or know anything about. I wish the games would spend more time with the Underworld stuff .
+/-The difficulty could be described as too easy, with the one exception being the last optional boss.
+/-The battle system flaws from the first game return as, once again, the debuffs, or buffs for the most part, don't really matter. Most of the time, you can't almost even see if you've been debuffed or not during a battle.
-*This Clifford character (not big, red or dog) brings nothing to the game. Why is he here? I can't even decide if he looks cool or terrible. Maybe both.
-There are many locations that are just optional, and I somehow felt not impressed with the main hub town. I think I prefer the more alive Kurken from the first game.
?How old is Serri really, and where I can get her number?

Atelier Ryza 2 is a great sequel that feels like it's progressing the story and adding new things to the lore. While still really easy on normal, I did end up loving this a lot. The game is so relaxing and the characters are so nice that the time spent with this game is a pleasure. Ryza, Lila and especially Serri could give me their numbers thou... Can't wait to play the next game! 9/10.

PS. An anime adaptation of the first game is in the works! Hypeeee!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf the first Ryza game was a good enough game, then this one is simply magnificent. The Ryza series is my first ever Atelier game series, and this series definitely made me want to try out some other Atelier game series due to Ryza 2's impeccable quality. They improvised so many things from the first game that made it impossible not to recommend anyone who played (and liked) the first game to try this one out.

Here are some things that I noticed were improved from the previous game

Exploration
Let me start out with this one because I think this is one of the mechanics that has gotten a lot better compared to the first game in the series.

- You can now swim! This adds more depth to the exploration mechanics. Some people might not like underwater levels in video games but I don't find it to be particularly annoying.

- To add to my previous point; you can also dive underwater if the water is not too shallow and if you have the right Adventuring Tool.

- You can now jump down from high places/cliffs without having to go around them. There are fewer invisible walls preventing you from going down from high places. It takes away the slight frustration I've had when playing the first game for not being able to just jump down from an elevated place.

- Spirit Beast allows you to travel through a map faster while keeping you safe from monsters. Spirit Beast can also dig the ground to find rare items/materials.

Combat
The combat mechanics in Ryza 2 is either a hit or miss depending on the players. They changed the combat mechanics by a lot but I personally think there weren't any devastating changes.

- You can't use items immediately at the start of the battle unlike in the previous game. Makes sense since this makes enemy encounters feel like a joke since you can just one-shot them to oblivion with items. Edit: You can actually use item at the start if you've actually upgraded your Core Drive.

- Combat feels more like a hack-n-slash and fast-paced since your Tactics Level doesn't rely on AP anymore. I think this is an improvement since I really am not a big fan of the idea of having to consume AP to raise your Tactics Level.

- You can use more than one item at a time, given that your CC amount is adequate. Especially useful during bossfights.

- Your party formation does not rely on roles anymore; there are no roles to begin with. TBH I really like this one. Having to make a piece of equipment to suit a role is kind of frustrating.

- The enemies' position and your party's position are random throughout the encounter, but you can still switch targets. The position of enemies might impact the effectiveness of your items. Say for example when you're using a Craft. If the target enemy's position is far apart from the others, your Craft will not deal AoE damage. I think this is a bit of a downgrade from the previous game, but maybe not all people found it to be a huge deal.

Synthesis
It's hard to talk about Atelier games without mentioning the Synthesizing mechanics. I don't have much to talk about this. I think that they're just as good as the previous game if not better. I do notice a slight improvement like the quality of your items won't go down no matter how low the quality of the materials you use for the synthesis, but of course, higher quality materials make for some better quality items.

All in all, if you like the first Ryza game, you will probably love this game. I might be trying more Atelier games after finishing the Ryza series. :)\t\t\t\t\t
