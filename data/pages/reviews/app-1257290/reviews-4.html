\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtelier Ryza 2 plays a bit like comfort food. You know it's not the best but it's fun, and if you played the first game then it's familiar. It streamlines some game mechanics, borrows a lot of assets and concepts from the first game, and manages to change very little of the overall milieu while still allowing our good friends from the Ever Darkness to get back together for another adventure. If you liked the first game, then I would recommend this one, but if you haven't played a Ryza game before then you'd be better served by playing Ever Darkness before jumping into this one.

In the beginning of the game, Ryza is given a magical item that she heads to the big city to research. While there she meets up with her old friends; Bos, Klaudia, Tao, Lent, and a few other fun cameos along the way. The game has a bit of dungeon crawling, lots of gathering and crafting, and a good bit more of exploration.

There are several facets to exploration. You craft different items to traverse different areas of the maps throughout the game. Whether it's diving underwater, climbing cliffs, or sniffing out treasure with a mystical pet, there is lots to discover on every map. You take those rare materials back home and use them to craft potions, materials, items, weapons, armor and more. The alchemy/crafting is simple, intuitive, and leaves space for putting in your own personal strategy for how to craft items.

The music is delightful, which is a staple in Atelier games at this point. The graphics were beautiful anime graphics but due to a few changes (particularly the lighting engine which might be a technical improvement but exposes a lot of flaws in the rest of the graphics) I am almost tempted to say they're worse than the first game. Almost all of the monsters were reskins/copies from the first game.

The story is slow paced with lots of slice of life style cut scenes and small stories weaving their way through the game. I love how it slowly builds to the bigger conflicts, it feels very organic and takes some of the edge off typical "chosen one" protagonists in fantasy media. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tWanted to wait until I finished the storyline before reviewing the game.

Three years after the adventure from the first game we rejoin Ryza as she heads to the city after receiving word about ruins relating to alchemy along with a unusual egg shaped stone. Familiar faces rejoin the adventure along with some new characters each with their own unique combat styles.

In Ryza 2 the exploring has been changed up with the addition of climbing, swimming, diving, rope swinging and monster riding which is a welcome change to the gameplay. Alechmy has changed a bit so you'll learn recipes in various ways such as the skill tree, recipe morph, main quests and side quests. Creating and improving items has largely stayed the same from the first game so it's just a matter of having the right materials.

For battles the obvious change in combat is now realtime so being indisicive will allow the enemy to land more attacks or charge up special moves but this does keep the battles moving quickly. Points for special attacks and item usage still remain along with the team calling out for specific moves to be used despite odd requests like physical attacks against golems being a weird one.

After getting used to the new game mechanics I enjoyed Ryza 2 just as much as Ryza 1 even if the ending does hit you right in the feels a bit too much. Fingers crossed to 100% this one as well. It's definitely worth picking up and once again a big thanks to my friend for pointing this one out \t\t\t\t\t
