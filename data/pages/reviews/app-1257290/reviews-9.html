\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis one is going to be a long one....

It's very unfortunate that I am unable to recommend this game, as the developers have clearly fixed and improved on around 80% of all the complaints that I had of the first game. The graphics are better, the combat is better, the systems are better, the environments are better, the controls are better. Even the music I think is better than the first game. There are just 3 major aspects that left me sorely disappointed, and perhaps interestingly, they hit at hardest at the beginning, middle, and end of the game. It's like getting slapped three times across the face. I will briefly talk about what they fixed, and then end with the three disappointments.

[The improvements]
- The maps are bigger and much more interesting to traverse. You will notice you can actually jump on top and over objects and ledges. Ryza can now swim, dive, ride a mount, and you also get access to traversal items in at the beginning of the game rather than at the end (damn you wind shoes)
- The combat is more engaging now. You can combo basic attacks and skills. There are no longer charges for item usage that need to be replenished by returning to your Atelier. Instead you gain item core charges by performing skill combos. Time also pauses for you now while you select items. You can now also now switch/tag-in another character, effectively bringing your party size of 3 to 4.
- There is now a quest board which you can turn in excess materials for money. These also reward gold coins pretty often. You may also sell materials like before, except this time it doubles as a way to improve what NPC vendors sell you.
- The crafting system has gained a Skill Tree, in which you need to spend Skill points to unlock certain recipes or tool upgrades. This works to organise gear/item progression, but should be balanced way better. (I completed the entire skill tree and had so much skill points left over)
- Gear and tool progression is slower, which makes it much better. Instead of making the next tier gear, you benefit tremendously by rebuilding/re-crafting a better version of your current tier gear. You also no longer need to combine tools to make those hybrid tools, thank god.

[3 Major Disappointments]

Slap 1 : HOW did they make the camerawork even worse than the first game. There's no camera tilt settings. The zoom distance constantly resets, and the BIGGEST problem is that every time an object passes between your camera position and Ryza, it automatically zooms in! So the first 3 hours of the game I'm walking around town getting a headache because the camera just goes IN - OUT - IN - OUT - IN - OUT really quickly when rounding corners, or walking up stairs, or passing street lamps. My brain got used it eventually, but please for the love of thick thighs fix that ♥♥♥♥.

Slap 2 : The cutscenes in this game probably make up 50% of the game time. I'm not kidding. There are 6 playable main characters and maybe about 6 side characters. Each character questline has a chain of maybe ~30 cutscenes. At 3 minutes each, we are looking at 18-20 hours of cutscenes total! These cutscenes auto play when you are either in the zone or in the vicinity. This means that there are times where I literally get 5-6 cutscenes in a row. And because these cutscenes happen dependant on you triggering them, the events that occur and the mood is often out of sequence.

Example: Ryza was just yelling at Patricia's dad for wanting to fire Tao as Patricia's tutor. After that cutscene I return to the Atelier where Patricia's dad shows up and sings praises about how he loves me for all the good that I am doing as an alchemist. wat!?

Slap 3 : I never expected Ryza's story to blow my mind or anything, but the ending to Ryza 2 along with some of the character stories are some of the most anti-climatic and explanation-lacking conclusions that I've probably ever seen. I think the plot in Ryza 1 was better?

-Story Ending: So Ryza accidentally dooms her world. We get to the portal, where I was expecting to go into the Underworld, but then the monarch just conveniently pops out. I defeated her in one try, as she was unexpectedly easy. Keep in mind this is THE MONSTER that the ancients toiled and sacrificed themselves to try and defeat. At this point I'm not even using the last tier of gear. Fi just goes home. Somehow Empel is able to find out how to close a 'primordial portal' wtf? We never really find out what Fi is and her significance. Ryza just decides to go back to Kurken island too, ditching all her new found friends for no real reason.

- Ruined old main characters: Tao and Lents' motivations suck. Tao wants to "surpass his ancestors"? That does not make any sense whatsoever? YOU ALREADY HAVE surpassed them. You are the only person on this planet that can decipher ancient texts AND one of the few people who even knows about the true history of the Klint Kingdom, your island's history, and the Underworld. Meanwhile Lent got all depressed for 3 years because he was travelling around and people didn't thank him enough? In what world if you save someone's life from monsters, would they say "if only you came sooner". Bro, just leave them to die then.

- Ruined new characters: Clifford is not that bad of a character imo, but the ending to him finding the treasure was so lackluster. He finds the treasure he has been looking for and then just says "this is too dangerous, toss it in your pot to destroy it Ryza!", and she does exactly that without asking any questions at all. And that's it? I watched 30 cutscenes to not even find out what you were looking for? Then we have Serri, who is looking for a plant to purify her home world. Her personal story actually goes nowhere. We don't find a plant, we don't know anything about her tribe in the Underworld, we know next to nothing about this character really. I would really like to know how her tribe held out against the philusca and how she came to this world, but nope. Nothing.

- Starchild: Kekw. We wake up an ancient, star-faring, alien, fairy, demi-god, who conveniently has lost most of her memories. In particular, the one thing she was tasked to warn us about she has forgotten. We (Ryza) then just leaves her in the ruins where we found her and proceed to never visit her and never talk to her again.

- Ryza's flashbacks: Fi allows Ryza to get flashbacks or memories of past events. But it was never explained how in the dragon village she was essentially able to travel back in time and actually speak with and be seen by the ancient peoples. Why add this when you could have just kept the flashbacks consistent?

So yeah, if you already didn't like Ryza 1's cutscenes and storyline, don't buy Ryza 2. If you never played Ryza 1, just know that this is a visual novel - game hybrid, half the game is dialogue and cutscenes.
