\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInitially, I liked this game. After finishing this game however, I was left with an inexplicable urge to squeeze small white flying animals that squeak. The characters in this game are so uninteresting that I just began to skip cut scenes because I couldn't care less about them. How they managed to ruin the main gang I have no idea, but they are BAD. The new characters are even worse, especially the blacksmith. Gameplay wise, I respect Gust changing how items are used, and the combat is far better than Ryza. Crafting is way better and craft, gather, combat formula persists, though alchemy is still pretty easy. The ruin discovery was neat, I was interested in the beginning, but in the end, I just brute forced through the "puzzles".

The graphics look just as good as Ryza if not better, though there is an obvious shift in style in Ryza 2 that makes me prefer Ryza far more. The style change can also be found in the music, which has devolved into generic RPG GST, especially the boss themes. Not that the music is bad, but it misses a main part that personally attracted me to the series. After completing an atelier game, including the first Ryza, I would always enter the sound gallery and listen. I didn't even look at the extras button this time. I was too busy vomiting from the ending since I couldn't skip it. I couldn't put my finger on it until I reached the end, but throughout my playthrough I felt like something was missing from the game. In all of the atelier games I've played, the atelier theme haunted me for weeks on end. Ryza 2's theme is forgettable and bland.

Even though I absolutely despise this game, it isn't objectively bad. Contrary, I think its an average to decent standard RPG. However, comparing it to the other atelier games I've played, its at the bottom. In my opinion, it has stripped away the music, the art style, and the characters. If you're looking to play a regular RPG, I think you will enjoy this title far more than I did. If you crave an Atelier title to fill your addiction, turn away. Even so, Ryza 2 did relax me like other atelier titles did, just that IT RELAXED ME INTO A VEGETABLE AND I WAS DECLARED BRAIN DEAD BECAUSE I DIDN'T NEED TO THINK.

I enjoyed this game in the beginning. I was excited to learn how the gang changed and I enjoyed Ryza 1. Initially, the combat, characters, and art style were good. It was only after I played a little while longer that I realized that I wasn't playing an atelier game anymore. Somewhere after the first 6 hours of playtime, the writing just dropped, and the game completely inverted. I honestly feel like I've been part of an elaborate prank. I didn't think to change my positive review until the ending, which disappointed me so much I cannot explain. Atelier games are always on the more pricey side, and they are all worth it. This is the first atelier game that I have bought that has not been a worthwhile experience. Hopefully after this rant I have expelled all my hatred for the ending and characters and I can peacefully sleep and forget about this game.\t\t\t\t\t

Crafting system is pretty much the same, which is a good thing because Ryza's crafting system is by far the best one out of all of Atelier entries. Combat system is improved a lot. Ryza is now thicc-er than ever.
