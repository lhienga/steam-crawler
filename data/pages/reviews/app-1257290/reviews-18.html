\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, would like to give a rundown on this game to help others understand what they are buying.

1. Game is subtitles only. Yes, can be annoying having to read everything the characters say, but the voice acting is nice and the dialogue is great even if you don't understand Japanese.
2. This game is story intensive. Majority of gameplay will be split between reading the massive amount of cutscenes, exploring the world and crafting your equipment. Combat doesn't take up much of your game time. Difficulty can be increased, but combat is not exactly the major point of this game. Think of this like the old flash storyboard games but with full 3d environment exploration with combat sprinkled in.
3. This is not a traditional RPG even though this game has the RPG tag on it. There are no choices in this game at all. None of your actions affect the outcome of this game in any way, shape, or form. Everything is set in stone. The only thing your "choices", that is, whether you have decided to view all of the cutscenes and complete side quests or not, only affects whether or not you see additional side stories, character development and in game progression such as unlocking new merchandise at the vendors or unlocking new merchants and recipes for crafting. Hence why this is a JRPG in the most simplest and truest of descriptions. Just sit back and enjoy the story. For comparison sake, this is more closely related to the Final Fantasy series except with a more detailed story.
4. Single Player only. No multiplayer content at all. No Leaderboards. Nothing. Could be a deal breaker for some.
5. Story does not exactly require players to have played the first game of this sub-series to make sense, however, the first game is mentioned and talked about on occasion along with the previous setting. Can leave players slightly confused if they haven't played Atelier Ryza 1. Otherwise, is mostly stand-alone and does well enough to give the players all the major plot points that are relevant to this story (which is very little).

Story: Now, I understand everyone has their own likes and dislikes when it comes to games, even when talking about games of the same genre. Personally, I think the developers did a wonderful job telling the story as a whole. Now, mind you, there are areas that could have been flushed out better, better worded, or planned in general. One such area was overlooked, mainly the lack of acknowledgment by the characters during cutscenes about the memory vestiges you have collected. Story wise the characters need the vestiges in order to progress through the "dungeons", however, respond as if they didn't read the vestiges. They were only for player satisfaction and knowledge apparently, which made the cutscenes slightly agitating to watch when they responded in a dumbfounded manner. Otherwise, no other complaints about the story. Some people will dislike the very stereotypical progress of the story, to the point it becomes predictable if the player has played the first game in this sub-series.

Combat: Fun and engaging. It really rewards the dedicated player and encourages them to attempt to break their characters, especially if playing on higher difficulty modes and/or in the high level DLC zone. Just enough of a change over the last game to keep it fresh and interesting while improving upon/balancing it to give the players a slightly more challenging play through compared to the previous title. Downside, only a handful of new enemy races to fight, however, each enemy has been changed to fit the new combat mechanics and have been changed to provide a new and fresh feel to this sub-series.

Crafting: Balanced and is slightly harder to break early game when compared to the previous title. Many more recipes to play around with and new ways to enhance your gear.

Environment: Breath taking visuals and a great setting for this game. Especially when comparing it to other anime games which tend to have less than palatable visual work such as the Fairy Tail game or early Naruto games. Which to be fair for Naruto, was more of a limitation of the consoles than the developers at that time (possibly both). Some of the level designs didn't necessarily make sense, such as how some of the ruins layouts were designed, but still looked cool and nice regardless.

Sound: Can't accurately say much about the voice actors other than they sound cool and sound like they fit with what the character looks like. You can hear the passion in all of their voices and reflects each of the characters and how they feel/look like they feel at that moment. You can tell they did a wonderful job playing their role. The soundtrack was very well done. Intense boss music, peaceful exploration music, and mood music for each zone of the game that fit with the design of those maps.

Achievements: Nothing too hard, easy to obtain. Most received just by playing normally. Nothing too grindy. Which can also be a downside since none were challenging enough to warrant feeling pride in obtaining any of these achievements. Plus side, leaves more room to actually enjoy playing the game the way you want to and focus on the story.

Character Development: Nothing too outrageous and definitely nothing new. However, was done really well. I enjoyed seeing how each of these characters faced and overcame their own internal conflicts and becoming even better people, including the main NPCs.

Replay Ability: Sadly, this game does lack in that department horrendously. Only options for replay is a New Game + that doesn't add anything new to the game. Allows you to keep all equipped gear and nothing else. Can change to higher difficulty at any point, so, not NG+ exclusive. Doesn't change the story. However, the game does have some massively high difficulty levels, especially the relatively recent addition of Legendary difficulty. Can be fun to play again for the increased challenge. For those that don't like to see the same things over and over again, this can be a massive downside and might not be worth investing in if they want a game that'll last them longer than a few days to a few weeks of play time.

I have only played the previous Atelier Ryza title. So therefore, I can't speak on whether this sub-series holds up to any of the others, but I do give kudos to the developers for making a wonderful game that I enjoyed. Again, not all will feel the way that I do and that is alright. To each their own. Personally I am looking forward to the next title in this series and might actually check out the others I have not played yet. Definitely one of my top 10 JRPG games of all time.

Edit: This game gets my "Game of the Year" award nomination.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a seasoned Atelier player, I enjoyed this installment. The graphics are the most advanced out of every other Atelier game. The Alchemy "crafting" mechanics is similar to Ryza 1 yet unique in it's own version. I didn't completely enjoy the story, because there were too many cut-scenes to wade through (but that is every Atelier game imo). The story was very predictable, but I do not play the Atelier series for the story I just want to battle monsters and craft as a cute JRPG girl. I feel as if a new player into the Atelier series will not fully enjoy this game as it is supposed to be. Most of the story has inside jokes from the first Ryza game. If you are a new player into the Atelier series, I would recommend either playing Ryza 1, the Arland trilogy, or Atelier Lulua. One of the main issues I had with the gameplay was that it was incredibly easy to craft overpowered gear, but that may be due to previous experience with the previous Atelier games. I also wish could select multiple ingredients while crafting, but that is just small detail that I can overlook. A common complaint I have seen is the absence of an English dub, however I thought the Japanese voice actors did an excellent job with the characters. Overall, I loved this game. It has enticing replay-ability, and after you clear the story you unlock higher difficulties which put your alchemy skills & battle strategies to the test. I think every fan of the Atelier series would enjoy this game, unless you prefer the previous alchemy "crafting" methods.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't how it is possible but the sequel is wayyy easier than the 1st game. The standout enemy is the last boss and that's only because I didn't pay enough attention at maximizing all equipment and consumables.

One thing I absolutely hated about this game is the amount of cutscenes. They went wayyy overboard with it. They're not even interesting cutscenes, just unimportant fluff over and over.

I know it's ironic for me to say this but I got crafting fatigue while playing this crafting based game. Some materials becomes obsolete mid game and there's a limited space in your inventory to keep them. Even worse are the gem reduction. Just like in the first game, you have to manually select the materials you want to reduce ONE BY ONE and that gets tiring by the end game. There's also a SP system where you need SP to unlock recipes. I totally get why they went this route, Ryza isn't an amateur anymore and she already knew all of the recipes in the game but by mid game, I've already accumulated enough SP to exceed the maximum amount of SP needed to unlock everything by 10 times. 10 TIMES. This system is clearly wasn't thought out very well.

Story is also not that great. Anyone who says otherwise is lying to themselves. It's basically another retelling of "go to 5 different dungeons to unlock the final dungeon" trope. We've already seen this trope many, many times in other JRPG and this game didn't do anything different with it.

Gameplay is marginally still the same but they knew they f*cked up the core item system in the 1st game because they overhauled it completely in this game. Players can now use core items as long as they can generate CC for it instead of having a limited amount of it.

However, despite my complaints, it's still a competent JRPG. No technical difficulties for me as I was playing and that's rare for PC games nowadays. Since most Atelier series comes in trilogies, I'm kinda excited for the 3rd chapter of Ryza's adventure.\t\t\t\t\t
