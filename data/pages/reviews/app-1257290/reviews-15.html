\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thought the combat in this game was a lot of fun, and very fluid, and the act of discovering hidden lore about the dungeons within the dungeon gave a good reason to fully explore the dungeons. I also really liked the addition of selecting which tool to harvest materials with, in order to better focus on a specific material that you are looking for. Honestly, there's been a LOT of quality of life upgrades in this one compared to the first Ryza game.

That being said, I thought the way you learned recipes (or rather, re-learned), was a little lame, as they admit that Ryza already knew the recipes, so there's no feeling of exploration of alchemy and discovering new items. Additionally, since half the characters were already friends, the relationships between the characters didn't feel fully fleshed out. Also, the lore you learn from the ruins is really really interesting, but they don't really DO anything with the information, other than have it as subtext for what happened to the ruins, and I can understand why a lot of people found exploring the ruins repetitive.

Overall, I think it's in my top 3 for favorite Atelier games, but not my absolute favorite.\t\t\t\t\t
