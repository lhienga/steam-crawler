\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReluctant recommendation, probably neutral.

It's a decent follow up to the first Atelier Ryza, it brings in some quality of life improvements and additions to the crafting process.

This game is playable on its own without having played the first, as any references to the first game are minor and don't affect the enjoyment.

👍The combat is simple and fun, and improved by spending time on the alchemy (crafting) process. Which in turn is improved by exploring new areas and finding new ingredients. So all in all, it's a satisfying and rewarding loop of explore, gather, craft, dominate.

👍The music is pleasant to listen to, catchy and memorable, and some are reminiscent of the first game's soundtrack.

👎Where this entry falls over though is the mundane "story" that is rendered almost to pointlessness. Oh, it has lore, and as the exploration progresses you uncover entries that hint at some grand events behind the scenes. But the actual story is just weak; in fact it's summarized entirely in the game's title. Lost Legends & the Secret Fairy. You uncover lost legends, and there's a secret fairy. There is no purpose to the fairy, other than a blatant attempt at a cheap emotional tug at the heartstrings. As for the lost legends, believe it or not, no purpose either!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing the entire Ryza trilogy, this one definitely stands out as the best of the three. It vastly improves on the systems introduced in Ryza 1 and just overall feels good to play (except the magic compass. That feature can go burn in a fire). Combat especially got the biggest overhaul with new features that makes it a lot more actiony for an ATB game.

Main gripe with the game is just the overall progression of the game, but that applies for the entire trilogy. It just feels bad crafting gear early on when you're limited in gems and item slots, but then in endgame/post game, the stats from a strong but far-from-perfect set is already overkill. There's just a single post game boss (not counting any DLC, I have no idea what's in those) and it really doesn't do much to encourage me to actually learn to play normally instead of just deleting enemy turns. It especially doesn't help that the game has a guard feature, with no perfect taunt feature(at least not an easy one to get), and the AI is trash at guarding! At least me missing a guard is a skill issue. Allies dying because they don't know how to block is just unfair and unfun.

The skill tree was a nice addition, though certain features realistically felt required for certain parts if you don't want to get hit by any hard stat gates, like the core crystal upgrades. I can see missing that feature making the game unfun.

If you enjoyed Ryza 1, this game is definitely worth it. It just does everything better.\t\t\t\t\t
