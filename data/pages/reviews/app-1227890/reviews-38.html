Synopsis

Summer Memories is a dating style resource game where you spend a month with your Aunt, Uncle, and two female cousins because your parents have to work. The graphics vary from 8-bit block style to drawn images. Most of the action is the 8-bit block style. Multiple play throughs are required to unlock all content. After the first play through you have the ability to start with previously unlocked relationship levels, skills, and stats making it easier.

Story

Your parents drop you off at your Aunt and Uncle's house on August 1st. They are coming back to pick you up on August 30th. You spend your summer with your cousins. Being a young guy who gets hard when the wind blows you have difficulty keeping things under control. Maybe your cousins and aunt will help you out.

Gameplay

Days are broken into five "time slices": Morning, Afternoon, Evening, Night, and Late Night. You have Action Points for how many actions you can perform during a specific time slice, you initially start with three and there are various ways to increase them. Other acts will advance to the next time slice. You need to do homework with both of your cousins and your aunt. As you send time with them you increase your relationship with them. You choose who you would like to further your relationship with, either cousin, your aunt, or all three. The three girls in the house require you to advance both their Affection (in 20 level increments) and Homework to advance to the next Affection maximum.

There are also five additional girls in the town that you can have a relationship with. They first appear on different days of the month, so pay attention to the breakfast conversation to find out what's new. The girls in town are available to interact with only during specific time slices. The town girls do not have a Homework meter to fill, they have different tasks you must perform to get the interested in you.

The Gallery

Selecting Memories from the main menu allows you to play a sandboxed version of the game to relive events that you have unlocked.

Final Verdict

I recommend this game on sale or as part of a bundle. I've got 50 hours in the game, but a lot of that is from writing a guide. There is a lot to do and lots of mini games to keep you busy.
