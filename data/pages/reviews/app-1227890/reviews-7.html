\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot bad, but a bit simplistic in how it handles things.

For a pixel-art kind of game, the art is good, and the story is reasonable enough. It's not the rendered photo-realism of BaD, nor the 3D environment of House Party or Koikatsu Party. And its open world is very basic.

What I mean by 'a bit simplistic' though, is that the controls are incredibly basic and rudimentary. The mini-games are controlled by clicking the mouse until a gauge enters the area you want it to be in, even when the mini-game is doing math questions. The controls in something like this should be very easy to get right, and yet, they're kind of messy. Almost everything is LMB, and all you do is hold LMB until the gauge is where you want it to be, rinse, repeat. Which, again, is weird and cumbersome when the objective is to pick the right math answer this way.

I'ts also very simplistic in managing the inventory and H-abilities. You will keep clicking menus, submenus, and/or icons fairly frequently when it would have been nice to be able to assign items to a wheel or hotkeys or something quicker.

For what it is, it's a decent game. Not sure $20 worth, but when it's on sale, it's reasonable, as it does have decent mini-games and a slight bit of exploration. But for not far from the same price, you could get two seasons of BaD and have a game with a phenomenal story and better graphics, so while I recommend this, I do so fully acknowledging that there are other games in the NSFW category that are about the same price and do things better.

Basically, if pixel-graphics H-games are your thing, this isn't bad, but if you just enjoy NSFW in general, it might make sense to check out other games first before giving this a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
