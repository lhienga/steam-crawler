\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played MHW & Iceborn over 600 hours. And I am still not done with the game.
Since this is DLC (expansion) content I am assuming you already played the base game and I don't have to tell you about basic Monster Hunter things.
If the difficulty of the base game was too much for you however, Iceborne Monsters (Master Rank) are faster than normal world. So the game gets harder.
Also I have talk about the big Gammoth in the room: The clutch claw (CC).
In theory the CC is an awesome idea. You can hook onto the Monster and soften parts of the Monster or make it run into a wall for extra damage and topple.
But in actuallity it is mostly frustrating.
Because the monsters are so fast, the time you have available between hooking onto the monster and shooting it into a wall is rather short. So you will get thrown off many times because any attack the monster does throws you off as well as roars (unless you use earplug).
You can use certain mantles to counter that. But you can only shoot a Monster into a wall when it is not enraged.
So you need to have
A. Your mantle up,
B. The monster not enraged
C. A suitable wall and the monster standing next to it.
And D. Slinger ammo equipped. IF you forgot to unequip your flashomb you will only cause nice fireworks.

The CC is by far not a reason to not buy the game. I highly recommend the game. It is so much fun.
But just temper your expectations.

Iceborn brings one more Map. (Spoiler actually 2 more maps.)

Overall the game is great. If you liked MHW then by all means buy it. It is more challanging and can get frustrating here and there.
But for me it is still a 9/10 must have.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComing back from Rise, I can safely say that Iceborne is a superior experience (and not just because of G Rank). World has an amazing way of making you feel like a Hunter despite the innovations of the New World; even though scoutflies help you track a monster, you have to first teach them what the monster smells like to get them on the scent. Not having some Cohoot up in the air permanently giving you a bird's eye view of everything on the map through telepathy or something.

The endgame content for World (and Iceborne) trumps whatever Rise currently has; Rise-Apex Monsters have no real bearing on developing endgame equipment, unlike Tempered Monsters. Hell, even 4U-Apex Monsters had uses in developing stronger Wystones to make future fights easier.

Suffice to say, Iceborne keeps me coming back for more, while Rise...just doesn't. The only thing Rise has an edge on is the quality of the returning monsters, which Iceborne doesn't do as well - most returning monsters feature only in G Rank, making them incredibly fast and punishing for new players who haven't learnt their behavior patterns yet, to the point where they became a joke in Rise in LR/HR (looking at you, Rajang).

Oh, and of course, Brachydios is back in Iceborne. 10/10 Guildmarm approves.

If you're reading this in the future when Rise is on Steam and you're considering which game to go for, stick with World: Iceborne for now. Trust me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6/10 No. Only on a good sale and if you really, REALLY, liked the base game would I recommend this expansion.
The more I played Iceborne, the more I regretted the time spent. Far too much meaningless fluff and not enough done with the actual monster hunting.

They boast adding 30 new monster, but most are just copied from the base with new element types. Example, there were already two fish-type monsters, look and act pretty much the same save for one being water, the other fire (cuz why not?), now there is an ice one too.

Enemies have ri-doodoo-diculous HP and armor to whittle through making hunts feel more like a battle against the timer rather than the monster. Plus a GROSS over reliance on homing and insanely large area of effect attacks is a heavy reminder that you are just playing a numbers game. Rather than relying on bites, claws, and the agility of a massive predator with the occasional ranged attack thrown in, many monsters employ magic that just makes explosions, and he like, happen where your are standing, whether they can even see you or not. This left me to believe that we are not fighting monster, but actually telepathic wizards in disguise.

All in all, the Iceborne expansion feels cheap and lazy. An effort to capitalize on the success of the base game and little more.\t\t\t\t\t
