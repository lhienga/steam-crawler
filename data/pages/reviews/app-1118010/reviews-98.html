\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wasn't super into the base Monster Hunter World game. I adore the MH franchise, but World just wasn't doing it for me. So much so that I even cancelled my pre-order for Icebourne. Well I went and bought it more recently and honestly, I am so glad I did.

The new Hub, the returning monsters and the new sub species really make a difference. I feel like they've tried to return to how Monster Hunter games used to feel. It's almost as World was the biggest tutorial in any MH game ever and now that they've eased the newer players in they can start introducing the more challenging monsters.

In the base game I didn't feel like I wanted to try and make any sets of armor or even really go beyond upgrading one or two weapon trees. The designs were pretty dull and a lot of the fights never really made me feel that I needed to upgrade to complete them, it was just going to take longer if I didn't. Most quests, once they were done were left to sit in the pile untouched. In Icebourne I actually want to do quests over and over and collect mats and make all the things, both because the designs are pretty neat and the gear is actually needed. World has been brought closer to the old feel of what a Monster Hunter game was, by this expansion.

There are some new mechanics for weapons which are fun to learn to incorporate and the clutch claw is... interesting. (I do understand why some veterans aren't hyped for this addition). The steam works mini game is a nice way to farm some more essential (and sometimes rare) items in a pretty interesting way. Decorating your room isn't new to MH but it is somewhat improved this time round. Also the snow, I was literally fascinated by the fact that the SNOW MOVES AROUND WHEN YOU WALK. Small things n all.

I think it is a bit of a pity that the new event monsters + end game elders do follow the same sort of look, as other people have pointed out, they use the same skeletons etc and this is clearly a time save thing to bring content out a little quicker. I think maybe where the game has to appeal to a western audience where we are used to game content being chugged out quick (but not always with quality). Monster Hunter would previously usually add one or two new and very different Elders in each game generation and include some older ones for good measure and this meant the quality of the fights were good and the mechanics would vary considerably. This isn't happening here but at least we get a little bit of variance within one game.

Tl;dr: SO much content that pushes this instalment of Monster Hunter back toward older generation games whilst still keeping it fresh. Worth the money.

Rating
9/10 (S-tier)
Iceborne succeeds in adding more quality content to the game. It's mostly more of the same plus a better endgame and some cool new moves for the player to perform.

Details
It's pretty much a whole sequel seamlessly attached to the end of the base game. It might even be bigger than the base game!

I really like most of the new monsters although there are also some annoying and boring ones. But as a whole the bigger pool of cool ones definitely helps the game in its overall variety when you reach the endgame (the real meat of the game).

As for the elephant in the room: The Clutch Claw... I'm not a big fan of it. Sending the monster into a wall to knock it down, thus getting in some free damage in exchange for making it enraged is fine, although it feels a bit cheap. Being able to get special slinger ammo by using the claw is also nice (since they made slinger ammo more useful). But what kind of annoys me is the wounding mechanic.
Basically, you hook onto the monster and perform a special claw attack to wound it so you can deal more damage (especially when using a specific meta skill). You will have to keep repeating this every few minutes if you want to keep doing as much damage as possible which is a bit of a pain.

Another issue I have is with the grind. Although the new augmenting system and the way you get the materials for it is an improvement over the old one in my eyes, it's still quite grindy. A downside of moving away from investigations is that you'll spend most of your endgame grind on the same map.
Siege monsters like Kulve are also returning to suck the life out of you. I haven't fought the new Kulve herself yet but Safi'jiiva becomes old pretty quickly. It's much better than High Rank Kulve though!

As for other things that might be worth mentioning:
- The new town and hub are great
- Room customization is pretty neat
- Many more layered armor sets and also layered weapons allow for more customisation while still letting you deal efficient damage
- The story is kinda cheesy and takes itself too seriously just like the base game but not terrible
- Performance was supposedly quite bad back when the DLC launched. I also got it at launch but apparently I was lucky to have had no technical issues besides a couple of stutters in specific locations (even though my PC isn't exactly a heavy lifter). Can't really say anything else on that front but the other reviews suggest that the launch issues have been fixed by now.

Gotta say, I was pretty surprised seeing the reviews being "Mixed" even after all this time. But don't let the review average deceive you. If you enjoyed the base game and are fine with mostly just getting more of the same kind of content, some minor improvements and a new endgame then I'm pretty confident you'll also be able to enjoy this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an improvement on the base game in every regard. Much much more than just a new area added. I have a couple of gripes that I think are minor, but overall this is one of the best DLC I have played for a game.

1. The performance seems to be very good. I had no trouble with save data and the game runs super smoothly. You can choose to prioritize framerate or visuals. I choose framerate personally and go for 120fps. Haven't seen it stutter at all. Although the graphics can sometimes look a bit jank on occasion it's quite beautiful (and I'm not even using the free high res texture pack they have.)

2. The mechanics have been cleaned up and expanded on in good ways. They've changed some skills in ways that are fair and many have been improved. Balancing is quite nice of armor sets. If there's a set you want to make, you can probably make it now. Even if it has a crazy combination of skills.

3. The endgame is much, much more interesting than the base game. Still not perfect, but there's plenty of stuff to do and not simply grinding tempered elder dragons to infinity. Instead most of your goals have to do with fighting specific monsters in the guiding lands, and (hurray!) they don't have to all be tempered elder dragons. You will have to fight a bunch to augment your weapons, but this is just one aspect of the endgame.

4. Layered armor is great, now you can choose whatever armor appearance you like without having to mess up your armor set. I just wish it was slightly simpler to obtain the layered armor sets. They require quite a bit for just something cosmetic.

5. Many, many monsters added from the base game. A crazy amount.

6. More endemic life and exploration content. More "decorate your room" content. It's still fun to explore areas and find new things there, they've added a handful of new endemic life to catch and some "treasures" to discover while exploring the hunting areas. They've added quite a detailed room decoration system where you can personalize your hunter's quarters. It's surprisingly fun. You can collect new stuff to display in your room.

7. You can play the game within your own restraints. If you don't like a mechanic of the game, you can tweak it with mods. Don't like busywork like the cultivation fertilizer mechanic for farming materials? You can mod it so you don't have to pay attention to it. Don't like decorations being random drops? You can make them purchasable instead with a mod.

Excellent expansion on the base game and worth playing.

Gripes:
1. End game "superbosses" like Furious Rajang, Raging Brachy, and Alatreon, might be fun to beat as a challenge. But they are never fun to grind a bunch of times like you typically would in Monster Hunter games if you wanted their armor or weapons. This is typically the case with the "G-Rank" versions of MH games. Their endgame is so challenging that repeatedly doing the same ridiculous monster becomes rather miserable. They should reduce the grindiness for these crazy bosses.

2. Alatreon has a broken MMO dps check mechanic that shouldn't be in a MH game. Weird design choice, and I don't like it.

3. There is artificial gating of content in the endgame up to around MR100 for random stuff that is nowhere near as hard as something like Alatreon that you can face at MR24. Not sure why they do this. You'll end up getting to MR100 just by doing all the extra quests, but having to get there just to be able to reach final weapon augments and armor upgrades to have nothing left to use them in is an annoying design choice.

4. There is an achievement for reaching MR200, which is far, far away from the final MR requirement of MR100 for the last level of obtaining materials. Who wants to grind just for that?\t\t\t\t\t
