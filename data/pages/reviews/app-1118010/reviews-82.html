\r\n\t\t\t\t\t===UPDATE===

After 12-15-ish hours with "Iceborne," it's easy to validate some of the concerns noted in critical commentary about the game. Specifically, other reviewers have noted the game's tendency to promote/over rely on the Clutch Claw (which is still cool, btw), and how same-y some of the monsters are (new ones, at that).

I still absolutely recommend "Iceborne." It was important to confirm the observations made by other reviewers, however. This expansion/DLC might be better taken in smaller doses; that's how I'll try to avoid washing out of it.

We now return you to the original review, posted below for posterity.

More intense monster battles. Gorgeous new area. Same great Monster Hunter.

Though the game says it plainly and other reviewers have repeated it, I'll post it again for posterity: "Iceborne" contains Master-rank challenges. "Monster Hunter: World" effectively had an easy/entry mode in Low Rank hunts, then a medium/normal level of difficulty in High Rank. The Master-rank hunts offered in "Iceborne" will try your skill. And sometimes your patience. ;-)

Recommended for: players who loved their "Monster Hunter: World" experience and want to see the next big thing; hunters looking to push/test their skills; gamers that enjoy high-action encounters whilst taking in scenic views of winter wonderlands.
