\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA culmination of desperately removing game mechanics to say "this is really difficult."

Enemies are generally stun immune due to extremely raised stats. Even once you get expansion gear you will simply find that even simple base-game enemies on the new difficulty are virtually never stunned. The threshold for stunning has been raised so high that even Greatswords will almost never stun.

If you do manage to get a weapon that breaks their stun threshold, almost every enemy in this expansion is built to recover in 0.0 seconds from all their moves. Take the Barioth, for instance. A flying wyvern that causes guaranteed debuffs to your stamina if he ever makes contact with any move, even if you block or dodge it in some way. He gets instant teleportation moves that track you, and almost all his moves are long combos that nullify all the mechanics related to dodging and guarding. Should you break his wings, he becomes more reckless and stumbles. When stumbling and falling during attacks, he gets up immediately, defeating the entire point of doing so.

To top this off, enemies now build up a resistance to your quite limited set of options for dealing with them. For instance, flashpods are your only real way for melee builds (i.e. 85% of the game's options) to take down flyers without just waiting for a few minutes for them to land again. At first, you just need one, which is great cause you can only hold 3 unless you craft more on the spot mid-combat. Now? You need two on the 2nd try, so you're already out of them and need to craft more. Then the 3rd time you need 3. Another full batch gone. This assumes you shoot them perfectly as if the monster instantly turns around (Which most of them do) then they'll be unaffected. You can only hold 10 of the bugs to craft them, and many of the expansion monsters can fly 6+ times during a typical combat encounter with ease, some can do many more. In short, you're only real option to deal with some situations has been rendered pointless.

Capcom have a constant obsession with punishing you for doing well. If you stun enemies, they get knocked back so they're safely out of melee range and even out of the effective range of most ranged attacks, making stuns and knockback useful only in organized co-op. In singleplayer, it gives the enemy a chance to recover and punishes you for finding the opportunity to exploit their weakness.

This game already had a gigantic issue of "we turn off game mechanics to make things more difficult" in the base game, and it's been cranked up way past 100 on this expansion. Being appropriately geared means nothing, some weapon types are entirely useless such as Greatsword which is now too slow for almost any monster in the entire expansion pack. This expansion is so obsessed with just setting all stats into overdrive except for the player, and has left half the game itself behind in the process.

And don't worry, all those "new monsters"? 50% reskins.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC IS AWESOME

This is by far my fav entry in the series and favorite expansion by far. I have been playing since MH4U and just went back to play MH3U recently. The challenge this DLC offers is awesome. I finished it on ps4 and got it on here to play with friends. This DLC also gives use to the clutch claw. All that say it has to be used to much, I argue no, and that its perfect. Every monster hunter has had some unique function that was constant in every fight, to the fatigue system of MH3 to the wire bugs of Rise to even the magnet system way back in Monster Hunter Frontier Z Zenith. The clutch claw in base world was never really that useful and was not bringing anything new to the table till Iceborne. When it did it got used in every fight. For those who complain I wish you to refer back to these games who did the same types of things that changed the focus of all fights. Thats what clutch claw did and nailed it perfectly. No doubt moving from MHGU to MHW was one of the coolest experiences but not only does this game take a step forward in a new direction but the right one. This DLC goes back to what monster hunter veterans remember. GRIND!!! If you go into a fight not slightly prepared you are now making a risk reward fight that heavily wagers against you. For those of you who complain. A lot of this content was added for us previous monster hunter players to enjoy the difficulty once again. So if a gripe you have is the clutch claw, difficulty, and grind then i dont think this DLC is for you. Otherwise i believe this is the best content the Capcom team has released in recent years. Its a challenge and absolutely fun with fights that will make you actually remember the fight and enjoy the difficulty curve. I also strongly encourage newcomers to try this DLC, its worth every penny.

P.S. for those having issues about not playing after a long time and losing their saves, my condolences. I understand your frustration.\t\t\t\t\t
