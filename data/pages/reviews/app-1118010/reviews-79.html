\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who really got into playing monster hunter as a whole in MHW, This game became so much more special to me in Iceborne. With its beautiful monsters and locales, it adds to what was already an amazing experience. World and Iceborne have even led to me playing and loving older generations of the game. After beating this I felt that there was still so much to do and while my time on the game has diminished over the past few months, one thing stays the same. I love this game. It has helped me fall in love with the series and I could not be more excited for Monster Hunter Rise in March. The variety is also outstanding. Some may say, "oh but you are fighting the same monster again and again, that can't be fun." To that I say, yes some hunts can get repetitive and the grind is not for everyone, however, with the different weapons a hunt you have done 100 times can feel like a new challenge. I still have yet to become that good with several weapons and love the challenge each one brings. Most recent was switch axe which I have just recently been playing. It is so much fun grabbing a monster and unleashing an explosion into its head to kill or knock it down. I was cautious when I tried the demo for this game but when i bought it and really got into it, it blew me away. Even what I consider to be the worst monsters in the game, i.e the Fish bois, have their own aesthetic that works for the areas they are in. These are not just bosses that you fight to progress. They live in these environments. You can see how monsters have adapted and become accustomed to fighting in their locales and how territorial they become.
Enough rambling though. If you enjoy an action rpg style game that, yes may be a grind, but it is fun and can vary vastly by each hunt. Heck it may even be a gateway to trying other games in the series as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIceborne in a nutshell is an annoying version of World.

The Good
New Weapons, New moves, New Armour, Decos etc..
Some new monsters are fun.
More Multiplayer fun, like MHW.

The Meh
Monster's high bloated HP.
Re skinned monsters of MHW.

The Bad
Monsters are too fast & run around way too much, more than my 2 month old Kitten.

The Clutch Claw. This is used to create a wound on the monster, which creates a weak spot on the monster for about 90secs. Your weapon would then to crit attacks on that wounded spot.
Now the problem here is, to counter the claw attack, the new monsters have fewer weak spots, so now you're pretty much forced to used the claw every time. Given the high HP, without using the claw you'd be wasting your time.
And also given how fast these monsters are prancing around, you'll fall off whilst using the clutch claw, taking damage. This is fine at start, but as you're forced to use the claw again and again, it becomes very annoying, very fast.
Another thing with the monsters movements,if you're aiming for it's head, you'll end up using the claw on it's hind limbs. Again, it gets really annoying.

The Ugly
They re-skinned some of the monsters from World.
They're okay as I mentioned above.
BUT, they went full WWE on them. Every one should have a gimmick.
Lightning, poison, dragon, ice.
And 7 out of 10 of their attacks would do some form of elemental damage, leaving a status effect on you. It's like their primary goal.
And as if that wasn't enough, they do TWO elemental attacks, so they can poison & Stun, or Lightning & Ice.
You'll end up eating truckloads of nulberries.

Hot Drink. So with Iceborne, obviously you get to explore & fight in a new ice covered map.
But even with layered clothing, you can't handle the cold & your stamina starts to deplete.(not a specific area, but the whole freaking map!)
To counter it, just like cool drink, you consume a hot drink.
You may have already deduced the issue here.
High HP, fast & furious, extremely gimmicky monsters with less weak spots.
Of course it'll take time to slay them & Of course your Hot drink will run out mid battle.
Another annoyance, it was completely unnecessary.

I just cannot recommend Iceborne for solo play. Especially given the high price.

IF you plan on playing MP for pretty much every monster, then its fine I guess.
Get it on sale.
