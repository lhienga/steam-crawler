\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the single greatest game I've ever played. If you're looking at this, odds are you either have the base game, or are wondering if you should buy the DLC pack. Don't let the "mixed" reviews fool you, this is a truly astounding amount of quality content for the price. I should know, I've spent 950+ hours in it as of writing this (which apparently doesn't show up, since this is a DLC)

Iceborne takes the mechanics and quality of life changes World set up, and extends them to a whole 'nother level. The new gathering hub is extremely convenient, with all the shops and vendors placed incredibly close together. All weapons get new, incredibly satisfying moves to power them up. Many fan-favorite monsters make comebacks, alongside new monsters and subspecies. The new raid monster is single-handedly the coolest ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ experience I've ever had in a video game (though folks say that it's "not monster hunter design". So what? It's dope!) Not to mention all the other monsters they've added post-release that are beautiful, challenging, and incredibly satisfying to hunt.

There are two major things people tend to complain about with this expansion: the CPU usage, and the Clutch Claw.

For CPU usage, they've since patched the game to fix it. So long as you have a 1080 or better, you'll be able to run it on high settings no problem.

As for the Clutch Claw, it comes down to a problematic part of the Monster Hunter community, the meta-gamers. Many hunters, like myself, play the game to hunt monsters and to feel immersed. Some people aren't content with that, and then mod the game to find out the absolute most damage they could do if they played perfectly. Which they don't. And are then upset about. "Why the hell are you running stun resistance? That could be three more attack jewels!" they say, as they get stunned and then killed. "Why aren't you wounding the monster!? That's lost DPS!" they say as I cut off the tail anyways. "Pffft, who runs elemental? Raw damage is obviously meta" they say as the monster's DPS check denotes me as the highest-damaging hunter. It comes down to these people believing that if they build for a perfect hunt, they'll *have* a perfect hunt no matter how aggressive they play. When mechanics like the clutch claw demand them to play carefully, they get killed, get upset, and leave a negative review for what is really an amazingly cool mechanic.

Monster Hunter was explicitly built *not* to be meta gamed. There is an astounding amount of work put into obscuring monster health, hunter damage, etc. It bewilders me how these people come in, break down all that work obviously meant to stop them, and then have a miserable time and go on to gripe about it. Besides, grappling hooks are dope, why wouldn't it be cool to grapple *onto* a monster and beat the tar out of them!? Or to interrupt a huge attack and send them hurtling into a wall? The clutch claw is definitively optional, and hunt times don't really change based on its use, so why can't we just enjoy a cool mechanic? Speedrunners may disagree, but they also know how to manipulate a monster to do the same attack patterns, which lets them have a perfect hunt. For most hunters, it's just a dope add-on, complimented by a huge roster of monsters, dope new attacks, and beautiful new locales.\t\t\t\t\t
