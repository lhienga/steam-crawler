\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo the main complaint I see from Vanilla to Iceborne is that the health buff for the monsters is insane, and while I agree to an extent, it doesn't bother me because that was one of the main moves from High Rank to G-Rank. Typically, it was more health, more attack power, more aggressive monsters, and MAYBE a few new moves. There are a few more subtleties to classic G-Rank, but that was the general idea.

Really the only thing that the health buff does for the game in terms of "difficulty" is that it really just extends the amount of time spent in a hunt. I like it, but I can see why people don't. Sometimes I don't feel like sitting and whopping on a monster for the entire 50 minutes that game gives out. I just choose to work on High Rank tempered monsters if I don't.

Iceborne adds a ton of new value in the gameplay loop with new decorations, new monsters (obviously), new hub mechanics, new investigation and expedition mechanics, and newer ways to approach building your character. It's a fantastic addition to an already fantastic game, and is well worth the money at full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:

- Tonnes of content, you're getting insane value for money if you enjoy what you're offered.

- Added weapon moves like sword and shield's perfect rush or longsword's sheath attacks are really cool.

Cons:

- Common complaint but for good reason; clutch claw is stupid. Even if you don't wallbang (which is often handicapping yourself), you're HUGELY handicapping yourself by not tenderising unless you have a specific build for avoiding it. This is increasingly problematic for light weapons, which have to go through a ridiculous ritual of 3 clutch strikes before tenderising just to avoid having to claw on twice.

- Hilariously, monsters then do frequent small attacks that knock you off and punish you for trying to do this thing you didn't actually want to do in the first place. Not to mention the aiming is so finicky it's ridiculous. Can't count the amount of times I've tried to latch onto Teostra's head only to fly through it onto its tail.

- Endgame monsters are hyperjuiced with insane speed, damage, and annoying artificial difficulty mechanics like Kirin and Rajang's deflecting auras and Teostra/Lunastra/Kushala's stagger auras.

- Even now, loads of monsters will bug out and spam the same move 5-8 times in a row, often at several occasions on a hunt. This can range from comically reducing the difficulty (e.g. Rathian spamming its run attack), to comically increasing it (Gold Rathian spamming its aerial tail spin), to simply dragging things out in incredibly annoying fashion (e.g. Glavenus/Anjanath jump spam).

- All of the above adds up to a huge middle finger to melee, to the point that I wonder why anyone would suffer through it when range exists. The gap between the two types of weapons just grew massively from Vanilla, when it was already disparate. It's just absurd struggling against something like Furious Rajang with a melee weapon for hours, only to bring out LBG Sticky and watch him thrash around uselessly for 10 minutes until he dies. I loved several melee weapon types in Vanilla, but playing them this xpac just feels like a pointless uphill battle.

- Iceborne's idea of content is sadly disconnected from what gave vanilla compelling replayability. Ideally, the game would frontload essential items and then progressively add levels of farming for progressively increasing luxury; e.g. you would receive all your equipment upfront, then upgrades, then augments and perfect decos. Instead, the game gives you scraps of equipment and then locks augments AND upgrades behind its highest level of farming, whilst giving you deco farming as the path there. I'm meant to farm for Rare 11 jewels when I haven't even gotten my charm past level 2 of 5 yet?

- This ordering of farming is then gated behind the artificial time bloat of the guiding lands. It's not enough to simply farm the monster you want for its parts. You have to level up regions AND hope you get tracks for the monsters you need, whilst other regions get levelled DOWN. In a game all about incremental increases and permanent improvement through farming, Capcom is taking AWAY from the player to make sure you always need to go through unnecessary blockades in the guiding lands before you can even reach the point of farming (AKA farming so that you can farm - genius).

TL;DR:

Iceborne adds a bunch of interesting stuff and potentially endless replayability to Vanilla MHW, but brings with it irritatingly unpolished and nigh mandatory mechanics that muddy the gameplay experience. The new game philosophy ruthlessly punishes melee and layers farming in a way that adds a lot of gametime, but in an arguably pointless and unhealthy manner.

This isn't a bad expansion, it just requires concessions on the part of the player. If the postgame farming design doesn't bother you and you enjoy the added challenge of being melee (or didn't plan to play melee anyway), this game could become your favourite of all time. But if those drawbacks bother you, maybe don't waste your time, and go play MHGU instead.\t\t\t\t\t
