\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: Steam seems to say I have 0 hours in this game. I started Iceborne at about 100 hours in World and, after having gone through just about all the content in Iceborne, now have 270 hours.

Keep in mind that this DLC is mostly an addition of Master Rank (G-rank in earlier games), AKA a step above High Rank, which includes a new map and many monsters new to the base game. It will be harder than base game. The monsters will do relatively more damage and require better equipment, more time, and better execution on your end to hunt.

Some reviews say that monsters become HP sponges. I have thousands of hours across multiple Monster Hunter games, so take this with a grain of salt, but in my experience Iceborne's G/Master Rank had the best singleplayer balancing across any Monster Hunter game I've played. Hunt times were consistently within 15 minutes, as opposed to 20-25 for other games.

Other criticisms land on the clutch claw. The clutch claw shoves the ceiling of possible DPS upward by temporarily increasing the damage dealt to the part of the monster you tenderize. The DLC introduces new openings on monsters (for Master Rank as well as retroactively) explicitly made for you to clutch claw during, giving you plenty of consistent opportunity to do so in a hunt. Even so, the clutch claw is simply not necessary for most hunts if you know what you're doing, and has regular openings for its use. I really didn't get the hate about the clutch claw; it played fine for me.

The worst I can say about Iceborne is the very, very ends of it. Guiding Lands are a bit of a sandbox-y hunting environment where you can get special parts to augment your Master Rank weapons. The grind is very tedious without slight QoL modding, which I would suggest if you want to spend any significant time in the GL.

The last 3 or 4 main fights are also all quite a bit more difficult than the rest of the game. Two of these ( Kulve Tarroth and Safi Jiva ) are special fights that essentially mandate playing in a full party with others to win. The last two ( Alatreon and Fatalis ), ironically, almost require you play SOLO lest your teammates cart for you. The last two are very difficult, even if using sets and weapons designed just for them, even to someone with with as many hours in the franchise as me, but are very satisfying to finally beat, and their gear is stupid good.

If you haven't bought the base game, wait until after beating it before thinking about buying Iceborne. If you decide you liked the base game, and want to play more with some more advanced mechanics, expanded weapon movesets, armor skills, and difficulty, I'd highly recommend it. Iceborne was a blast for me.\t\t\t\t\t
