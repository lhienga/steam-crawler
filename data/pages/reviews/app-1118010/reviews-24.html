\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith almost 1000 hours as of writing and responsibilities to procrastinate I think this is a prime time to review Iceborne, especially for anyone who's looking to get the DLC after the negative reviews from the start.
Short version: Iceborne will be more difficult and also very fun. I 100% recommend it to anyone who enjoyed the base game.
Almost all performance issues have been fixed and patched, the game runs smoothly for me without any issues and many recent reviews seem to have stated the same. As for the anticheat and modding, I have yet to meet a cheater (considering that most my playtime is solo that is not surprising I suppose) and I have never modded the game, so I can't comment much there.
The difficulty: Yes, Iceborne is hard. Not Dark Souls difficult, but you might get stuck on a few monsters in the early stages, since the transition between ranks is sort of painful and you'll also run into issues with some of the very endgame bosses since those are tailor made to kick your ass. You will be tested for your skill and you'll need some advanced skills to get through some of the tougher monsters.
The clutch claw: With some new mechanics and decorations being added it really doesn't feel bad? Although I never actually understood all the hate surrounding it. Softening is a great way to get more damage out of your weapons but in casual hunts it won't really matter all that much if you spend an extra 3 minutes beating up whatever it is you are beating up. I personally never really had any beef with it anyways.
The Grinding Lands: The grind isn't that bad, unless you really really want to min-max. The layered armour and weapons it provides is a huge plus for me, since I care about fashion. The leveling mechanics can be a pain in the ass and if you don't want to go into a lobby where some guy will land you his place for that tempered Namielle than you'll have to grind it out to level 7.
And now something bad: Guardian armour. Gosh I just hate it... If a new player skips the tutorial on it and just sees big numbers for a low price they'll breeze through the base game only to realise that monsters have the means to fight back later on. I'm not sure how many people thought the game was "too easy" or got slapped in the face by Iceborne and said it's "too hard" but if I could, I would remove that armour for first time playthroughs.
Oh yea one more thing: Activity is still very nice. I can still find full lobbies of people grinding Siege monsters and even some of the tougher challenges, so multiplayer is still active until this day.\t\t\t\t\t
