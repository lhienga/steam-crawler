One step forward, two steps back
MHW: Iceborne brings a lot of content for what it's worth. There's a bit of everything for everyone here from the quality of life improvements to the throwback monsters coming from the old games. The gameplay has been improved upon as well, the new moves look great and gives the combat a new layer of depth.

Unfortunately, some changes feel a bit too much and lead to a frustrating experience. Some of the most common complaints are regarding the clutch claw, artificial difficulty, and the added grind.

It's not a bad expansion, there's plenty of replayability and a lot of interesting stuff. But I can't recommend it simply because I feel like it strays too much from what I expect from a Monster Hunter game.

Pros:
  • More powerful gear for you to play around with. There are new decorations to farm and a new augment system as well.
  • A new and better village. Unlike the old village, NPCs have all been put in the same area. More importantly the gathering hall has been improved similarly as well, you can even get to the workshop directly from the gathering hall now.
  • The monster lineup is interesting enough. It's a mix of some new designs, variations of vanilla monsters, and classics.
  • Fashion hunting is better than ever. Most armor sets now have a layered version you can make. Even weapons can have layered customization now.
  • The new end game area is fun enough. You have the option of hunting the random monsters that appear or lure out the ones you want. If you level up the area enough, you can then lure out the tempered variants. There is no time limit so you can hunt to your heart's content.
  • Performance is still pretty good but now with added features such as DLSS and DX12 support. Maintaining 75FPS with no drops on an RTX3070 (21:9).
Cons:
  • Monsters have way too much mobility and damage. They will chain multiple attacks in quick succession and stun lock you to death. If you solo the game, you'll spend most of your time running around waiting for a tiny opening just to get a few measly hits in. I've actually abandoned melee like many players have as it's much easier to just shoot and KO the monster over and over.
  • You're basically forced to use the clutch claw because if you don't then you're missing out on a huge amount of damage. It's janky to use and interrupts the flow of combat.
  • Flyers are way more tedious to fight now since they nerfed the flash pods.
  • Decorations are still a chore to farm. The drop rates are still garbage and you could spend weeks grinding trying to get the one you need.
  • Late game monster difficulty amounts to passing a DPS check or get obliterated in one hit. Am I playing FFXIV here?
  • The cutscenes are still unskippable. No, I don't want to hear about some BS the handler spews out of her mouth or watch some monster do another roar. Also, why not have the option to disable NPC dialogue. I don't need to read the same freaking dialogue every time I need to use the shop or forge a weapon.
  • Almost half of the "new" monsters are just the vanilla ones but with a new color and element.
  • Still very much lacking in the social aspect of the game. There is no need or reason for anyone to talk to another hunter.
