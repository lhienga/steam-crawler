\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNew monsters, old monsters, great DLC, expands so much from the game and the new mechanics add so much to it that it changes the approach you take on the fights.

The endgame introduced is something else. It's a great idea, and in my opinion, it was really well implemented. Basically, you can stay there for hours, just killing monsters without ever going back to town, especially if you are in a group. And I really like the idea that you have to level up a certain area to to get a monster you need an item from, or even level down if you need something for a weaker monster.

The only downside is the way the clutch claw was introduced. The monsters of the new rank have a crazy resistance, and the clutch claw is the only way to soften them. It is interesting, but as it is in the game, it is not a new alternative to help you deal with them, it is an obligation to use it, and with some weapons, it is kinda anticlimactic to have to use it. Especially because it's annoying to waste the first opening the monster gives softening them instead of using some exciting damage dealing combo.

Another thing that is kinda annoying: the clutch claw basically gives you a free stun that you can use on demand as long as the monster is not raging. It is nice to stun monsters, but when you play with the hunting horn (my main), hammer, or even the great sword, the gratification you feel from stunning the old-fashioned way (bonk on the head) slowly diminishes. Those stuns become almost meaningless (especially when you stun them without the part you are targeting being softened, so you have to choose between doing non optimal damage or wasting the stun with a clutch claw attack, hoping that you'll have another opportunity to unleash everything you got).

So yeah, I still think the clutch claw is nice, especially the combos it brings with some weapons, but the obligation to soften monsters parts is a distraction that was really unnecessary.\t\t\t\t\t
