\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a monster hunter game, iceborne has phenomenal graphics and does a great job immersing the player in the world of Monster Hunter. That being said, it has some drawbacks that personally ruin the fight for me. The fighting system revolves around the clutch claw mechanic that can't be ignored. You need to use it in order to tenderise some monster parts, or to yeet the monster into a wall in order to do a lot of damage to it. Despite the large amount of damage that the vault does, it can potentially ruin the flow of your fighting as you have to go out of your way to do it, or at the very least use the clutch claw to tenderise some parts of the monster you're fighting. In order to counter that damage, the monsters are BLOATED in HP, which essentially forces you to use this mechanic that is extremely unpleasant. Monster Hunter has always been a franchise in which you could experiment with different builds and has always felt like you were able to do different approaches to a fight. That being said, in some fights for this game *cough* alatreon *cough*, you're essentially forced to use certain weapon types and to bring a specific playstyle to tackle it in order to win (that mixed with the horrendous clutch claw mechanic) makes for a very boring and unpleasant experience. It also has a lot of artificial difficulty, in it when compared to other titles of the franchise, and a lot of the equipment is locked behind ''raid'' content which you have to do with other people. There's a lot of interesting things in this game, and they do a great job with the immersion, but the combat leaves a lot to be desired. For me this is the worst game in the franchise, and I'm very disappointed in the Iceborne DLC. If you're looking for an immersive experience, then this is the monster hunter game for you. However if the combat is what suits you, then I'd recommend Rise/sunbreak, or older games in the franchise. 4/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't listen to people saying this game is unplayable without a good CPU, I played this game on release with a 10-year old CPU (Xeon X5675) at the time, and had no performance issue. To be fair I also had a really good GPU, but that shows that having a good GPU is more important than a killer CPU for this game. Anyways from what I've heard those performance issues are now long gone.

For those who who don't know, every MonsterHunter game always gets released twice: a first version with "Low Rank + High Rank missions" and an Ultimate version that adds a whole lot of end-game content (called G-Rank). Iceborn is basically MonsterHunter World: Ultimate. It adds a new icy biome, lots of new monsters (with all that comes with it: weapon, armors, etc), a new slinger mechanic, and a new game mode called "guiding lands" (basically expeditions done right). Don't get fooled by the fact that it only adds "one zone" and "one rank", the G rank content is much longer that the low-high rank content combined. Yeah the story goes fast, but the actual G-rank stuff starts in the guiding lands, where you can min-max your way up to G-rank level 999!

This is a solid DLC, for the best MH game in my eye. Get it, you won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t
