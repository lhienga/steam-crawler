\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as expansions go, Iceborne is truly something else. Hundreds upon hundreds of hours of new content makes this very much worth it. Iceborne adds in Master Rank which is Worlds version of grank. I have mixed feelings on some of the gameplay but we'll get to that later. Two new locales have been added, the Hoarfrost reach being the main one. This massive locale features massive mountains, hotsprings, and large underground cave system, tons of new endemic life, and the return of hot drinks! The other new locale is the Guiding lands. World finally has a proper endgame system as this locale features every single locale in the game in one. Almost every monster can show up here and the locale has a unique levelling system which changes the monsters that appear. Many fan favourite monsters return, such as the 3 brute wyverns that everyone knows and loves, Glavenus the 1000 degree glowing knife dinosaur, and of course the thundering beast, Zinogre! Each weapon has an expanded moveset which only adds to their depth and fun. Now for some complaints. The clutch claw sucks. Its clunky, annoying, and is almost require to do good damage due to the tenderising mechanic. Thankfully I didn't have too much trouble as I used the Insect Glaive and Hammer which have built in tenderising moves. Monster AI in master rank can be quite annoying as some monsters never stop moving and they have way more health which makes it much harder to take them down. Its not too bad, but sometimes it can be problematic. The endgame monsters have mechanics that make them feel artificially hard, however due to PC we have ways to Nerf them and make them more fun. The game is still loads of fun and has gotten tons of support from capcom with loads of new content updates, finishing the game with one of most hype updates of all! I think Iceborne is very much worth it, but be warned it is very challenging.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIceborne is the blood sweat and tears tryhard addon for Monster Hunter: World. Almost doubling the content by adding two new layers of difficulty it is well worth the money for Monster Hunter fans.

From all the goodies added with this the clutch claw is a love-hate relationship. When trying to evolve your game you gonna have to take a leap every now and then and just add some new mechanics. The clutch claw lets you clutch on to a monster to do a variety of attacks. Skillfully using the clutch claw can be the game deciding factor which is also where it starts to feel weird. It is so out of the line good using the clutch claw that when you do not use it it feels like you are playing the game wrong.

Like I said Iceborne is all about the top shelf difficulty. Bloated HP pools and hyper aggressive AI is what that means for the average Monster. Sounds horrible but it actually isnt. Many people would complain that it feels like a quick and dirty imbalanced pile of garbage but truth is this isnt chess. There is no other method of increasing the difficulty other than making monsters bigger stronger and faster. They tried adding raid like mechanics to some monsters which were implemented decently but are mostly reserved for special events.

The Grind: The guiding lands added with this is lovingly called the grinding lands by many because it is just that. An endless spawn of monsters for you to grind it out. For solo players this mechanic is absolutely horrible since leveling the areas is a balancing act and you cant top off all areas without an unholy amount of grind. In a group however you can just keep each area on max level between you and the boys and half the grind doesnt exist anymore.

If you are up for the fight and looking for the extra juicy tryhard content this is where its at.\t\t\t\t\t

Clutch claw is a unique mechanic that changes the pace of combat to be a bit more fast paced in my opinion.

Only criticisms with Iceborne is despite the larger roster of monsters most of them are returning monsters from older titles rather than completely new monsters, some of the endgame monsters are a little too BS, and the guiding lands are a little too grindy even to me! Only reason Iceborne seems to have mixed reviews on here seems to be from people salty over Alatreon or Fatalis being too hard and sorry to say, but they should be the most difficult fights with how good their gear is as well as how difficult they were in the older titles. MHW was an 8/10 to me, but Iceborne bumps it up to a 9/10. Not better than MH4U, but still a fantastic addition to the Monster Hunter series!\t\t\t\t\t
