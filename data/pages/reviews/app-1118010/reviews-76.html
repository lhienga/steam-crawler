There's things to like and things to hate about it.

I like how it treats weapons with new moves, and the clutch claw is pretty neat, and how the slinger is utilizedmore.

I don't like however that some of the new things are there but either have two mindsets to them, either "Oh this is broken, i'll use it all the time" Or "That move is cool and all, but this thing that already was in the base game is still better to do"

I like some of the new monsters that were added.

Nightshade can go to hell with how the devs practically thought "You know, we need more things that induce sleep. Let's make a monster that'll put you to sleep every five seconds." Yes you could have sleep resistance and just say no to it, but it just feels like a monster that wastes your time. ..Like Tigrex, or the final one in the story.

Story was.. alright? I like the concept they tried but it's hard to take it seriously at times with small things getting in the way. I tried to be immersed with it and while the cutscenes still looked cool, there's still cracks. ..But then again, Monster Hunter isn't known for story. Sure you have to have a motive for this, the build up Iceborne lead us to having big expectations. Then we get a variant of Nergigante which in hindsight is.. very meh. And then we have the actual final story monster that, ultimately, once again with hindsight, isn't so cool as I once thought, and can just.. kinda ♥♥♥♥ off.

To condense how I feel about Iceborne in one sentence. ..Get it on sale, as problems from the base game persist in Iceborne like performance issues, but it comes with it's own problems. If you want more Monster Hunter, then it's here. But think twice about getting it at it's original price (At the time $40)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMonster Hunter World's expansion, Iceborne, adds literal hours of content if you like a good grind. I have over 800 hours and I still find this game fun to play. If you enjoy grinding, boss rushes, or optimizing your gear this game probably has everything you like. If this game was lacking in anything it would be story, but nobody really plays monster hunter for the story. The game does have some good lore, but it takes a lot of digging into to discover.

Stuff I like: Pets (collect dozens of pets ranging from common carrion crows to rare glowing relicanth), 14 different weapon types that have completely different play styles and ways to play depending on your gear (HH main *doot* *doot*), 50+ monsters to fight as well as special event quests with hilarious or extreme variety (like absolutely massive monsters or tiny ones), huge areas to explore with secrets smattered about, at least a thousand hours worth of content (worth every cent), free updates to the game from the developers (very nice), and an amazing community that is always ready to lend a hand.

Things I don't like: Unskippable cutscenes (gross), friends can't join quests until the cutscene for the quest is over (I'm looking at you story quests), and the story is meh (albeit you can still enjoy the role-playing aspects a bit).

Disclaimer: This game is difficult. Like, I have friends who say it is harder than Dark Souls. It's not like you die a lot or it doesn't tell you how to do anything. In fact, the game literally has a guidebook built into the menu that let's you read literal essay's on combat, item management, hunting, etc. etc. It's just a lot of information to take in and unless you are actively trying to expand your experience through experimentation or in-game research (which the game literally has a research mechanic), you will probably have a bad time; as the difficulty curve spikes pretty hard after low rank quests. But that shouldn't dissuade you to not play this actual gem of a game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this after the Fatalis update, and I wholeheartedly recommend this expansion. It's not that the base game was incomplete, but this expansion is so brimming with content that it makes it so you wonder how the base game ever existed without it. I got over two hundred hours of content for that price. It is truly the gold standard for an expansion. Oodles of quality content, and no microtransaction nonsense.

A lot of it is endgame farming, but this game knows how to make it fun, having you build yourself up with clear and rewarding improvements until you finally face off against the Master Rank 100 monsters (I'll count Alatreon and Fatalis among them because why did Capcom even have them available at MR 24, they'll utterly clap you at that rank). It's not easy, but incredibly satisfying. Beating Fatalis and the Arch-Tempered monsters doubled the size of my penis.

Bugs-wise, I did not see a single bug that I didn't bring upon myself via mods. They've been quite ironed out. The new monsters are incredible (though there are reskinned variants), and the clutch claw gives you a ton more freedom with combat (the aim is sometimes jank). The new tiers of equipment allow for incredible build diversity (if you want to truly reach peak meta though, jewel farming is going through fire and brimstone).

Gameplay wise, this ties for the best game I've ever played, tying with Total War Warhammer 2. Story wise, Iceborne's story is very much still of the same B-rated film quality that the base game had. It's not bad-there are no plot holes and the story doesn't actively suck-but treat it more as a vessel for getting you from monster to monster. Effort has definitely been placed into it though, as the dozens of cutscenes can attest to. Still, it's no Bioshock.

In conclusion, buy this ♥♥♥♥♥♥ thing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love monster hunter as a franchise and have been playing since freedom unite. This might be the worst version of the game I've ever played.

Mounting was stupid OP in 4U when it came out, They toned it down for world and then added a new way to mount monsters in this expansion that operates on a completely different set of rules. People will say "don't use it if you don't like it" doing so is objectively handicapping yourself for the sake of your sanity.

The increased speed of monsters also causes some of the weapons to feel laughably out of date. The base game was better without the changes added by this expansion.
