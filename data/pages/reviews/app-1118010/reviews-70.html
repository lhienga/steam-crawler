+ Basically doubles content. A lot of new things to do and a whole new region to explore

- Some questionable design choices that also affect the non-DLC owners in negative ways

Optimization has been crap since the launch of the base game, so if you've been having bad performance, things won't really change in the DLC. This still holds in 2023. The only solid way to not deal with the low performance it is to have a strong GPU. For reference, I saw a big difference after upgrading my ol' 1060 6 GB to an RTX 3060 Ti. My CPU is Ryzen 5 2600x.

The game is practically incomplete without this DLC. So I think it's safe to say it deserves a higher average rating than "Mixed". Don't listen to 1+ year old reviews, and definitely don't listen to any of the recent one-liner ones that boil down to "I have skill issues and prefer to blame the game for my shortcomings.". If you liked the base game and want more gameplay out of the game, buy the expansion without any doubts.

Chances are, I won't need to update my review ever again since there won't be any updates to the game, so it's not like what I said will ever change unless Capcom decides to update their best-selling game of all time.

So I haven't read that many reviews but if you are reading this and the thought of many reviews being negative makes it difficult to buy this game even in 2022, then hopefully this will help a little.
I'm guessing most of the negativity stems around these 3 problems.

  • The first issue is the messy launch or for players that didn't have computers that were powerful enough.
    That's not really an issue anymore, and hasn't been for anyone I know. If it is, then there are performance fixes with mods that are not that difficult to install. Its a bit of a slap on fix but shouldn't really be a problem anyways.

  • Another issue I see is lot of people really think it just takes too long to hunt now.
    Which somewhat boils down to how long you have played monster hunter, or how much experience you have. It's not intended to be a walk in the park like base game was. That being said I feel like players still found Iceborne easy compared to last games up until Alatreon or Fatalis, but that does not mean all of the comments are just people who haven't "gotten good." This is because some of the monsters are not fun or well designed, whether you are good or not, they have annoying mechanics, even the easiest of these will not be fun to fight for many players. So, your millage will vary with monsters like Barioth, Barroth, Lunastra, Kushala, Blackveil, Alatreon, etc... These are a few that I have seen the community complain about. There are also some others that people either love or hate, of course, every monster cannot be great or even good unfortunately.

  • One of the last criticisms I can absolutely not get behind or even understand is why people feel like the clutch claw is necessary to fight monsters.
    Yeah its annoying at times to use, but I can get by pretty quickly even if I don't touch the clutch at all. Although, Iceborne did bring back some of the difficulty I wanted and missed in world with monsters giving consistent pressure and on longer drawn out hunts. I didn't feel like any of the hunts in base game were quite up to difficulty, unless tempered but even then it just felt gimmicky. By the time arch tempered came out I lost too much interest to farm.
    In Iceborne If I use clutch claw it can be about 1-2 minutes faster (not co-op), if well timed, but that is by no means necessary.
    If most hunts are lasting for longer than 25 minutes without clutch claw use I bet good wall slams would then be saving you a lot more time than a player who could do a hunt in 10 minutes, saving roughly only a minute or two. however relying on that damage doesn't make you better at your weapon of choice. That is not a problem with the clutch tool usage, it just means you are not doing that much damage and have quite literally not gotten good, or made appropriate armor with some thought out decos, and taken a proper weapon element. You either haven't farmed enough for the bare minimum materials or haven't farmed enough to be good.
    Albeit some fights still could feel quite long even then, but If you don't like farming so much, that is absolutely fair and you will not like Iceborne all that much.

A lot of this was imo and yours will probably very by a lot, but from my perspective although Iceborne has had some flaws, it is much more than world had to offer.
You can still see people not recommending the game because of one monster, and launch issues that are no longer a thing.
However, I definitely recommend buying if you want more monsters and the thought of longer hunts don't bother you. If you get easily tilted then Iceborne may takeaway from the initial world experience that drew you in.


A final comment and mini rant, in relation to the clutch claw that I can get behind is Iceborne seems to have shifted the focus and style of hunts drastically. Walking while drinking, to debuffs, buffs and clutch claw, it overall feels like you are meant to be managing too many systems. It also feels like you are meant to take damage, trip, get roared at over and over, no matter if you are good or very skilled at the game (Coming from someone who probably isn't very good), many monster can come out of nowhere so that you really can't predict everything. All of this feels like the game is more balanced to intentionally damage you over time, almost like you are not meant to play perfect, sometimes like a turn based game, you can expect to take damage when its not your turn, it can take away from the monster hunter experience, because it doesn't quite feel like past games.

In conclusion, I feel like clutch claw could be a little more consistent along with the fact menus and actions being super unresponsive after you pick up an item or sniff a footprint, it will make the game feel unnecessarily slow. I however have still enjoyed most of my time with Iceborne, and much preferred over the limited world. Besides some of the issues with design philosophy, I think the game has so much to do and so many details, its paced in a way that you can put it down and pick it up somewhat easily, considering you have probably played a lot to get here.
If you are still on the fence, wait for sales. I have been told that rise just feels a lot easier than this game. I have only tried rise for a short amount of time, and although I feel like the changes in some weapons are more fun, I would rather play world at least until the dlc for rise comes out.
Update: DLC came out, a long while ago now. Both games are very different I'm not going to compare them much more here. If you want immersion probably go with iceborne, unless you really like the way weapons work in sunbreak which is fair they are mostly more fun to use. For me I'm having trouble with muscle memory and input feeling slightly different, I now spam right trigger to load phials in cb, if I hold button early it won't load them. I also accidentally press left trigger often when trying to guard point attempting to charging my axe but that of course doesn't work and is probably my fault for playing too much world right?\t\t\t\t\t
