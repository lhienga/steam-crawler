Overview
You hunt monsters with unique weapons with it's own mechanics. You fight these large or small monsters with your own skill and intelligence of your items, environments and monster's info. You can go with a squad of 4-player, or alone. You can even SOS if you can't hunt the monster on your own. The game is best played multiplayer. The game is great for single-player as well. Graphics is amazing even in low quality. Good gameplay every single time you went out to hunt. On the downside, the game is grindy for the certain materials you need. You will need to depend on the drops on the materials only, not the weapons themselves except for a certain event item which there are only 2. Those are sieges. Everything else is obtainable. No microtransactions ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ and no lootboxes.

There isn't much of a story. Most of the time you follow what the Handler does. In my opinion and experiences, I can say, the story sucks. However, the story isn't the selling point of the game. If you're looking for a story-driven game, this is not it. This is a heavy-gameplay-driven game. Involving real-time reflex and strategy. There isn't much of the story and mostly the story only serves to progress through the next monsters that you will be encountering as well as hunting or slaying.

Gameplay
☐ Journalist
☐ Below-Average
☐ Gamer
☒ Above-Average
☐ Dark Souls

Every hunter will pick one of the weapons. Every weapon is also unique to their own with their own set of moves and special abilities. Mastering a weapon is not as easy, but every weapon will reveal itself, the way of the blades, to you. The map is an immersive layered floor with each map is different. There's no single map like each other. You, as the hunter, will have to adapt and learn the environment and use it to fight. Either in your favor, or against you. You'll have to think things through to fight and take down the beasts. Use the elements to your advantage and victory is within your grasp. Knowledge will help you. Every little hard work that you put in, into hunting a monster, either research or build-testing or item hunting. Does not matter. Victory is not far from your hands.

Sound designs are a bit clunky as they use the same style as their previous installments or series but with some improvements to fit in with PC/PS4. The sounds aren't nothing to write home about. However, they are unique since they are exactly what monsters are represented to sound like. With that said, I think you'll enjoy the experiences the game provides you with as you hear the monsters shook you with rumble and roar.

☐ Below-Average
☐ Normal Loop
☐ Above-Average
☒ Infinite-Possibilities

Every hunt will not be the same. You will spend time hunting the monsters again and again. However, every hunt will never be the same as you have different weapons with different playstyles as well as different builds suited for different occasion, playstyle of each of playstyles of the weapons and mechanics as well as the environments. Safe to say, the gameplay's replayability is nothing short but always excitement once you get into the hunt. Prepare yourselves as you fight through the challenges ahead. Happy hunting.

I love Monster Hunter Games. I will be biased. However, I will say, the price for the BASE game itself is quite expensive so it is better to wait for a discount. The Expansion DLC which is Iceborne, is definitely worth the price as the Expansion DLC provides better coverage, bugfixes and gameplay fixes as well as improvements upon the base game. If you plan to buy the base game only, wait for the sale. The Expansion DLC, is definitely worth the price but it is still very expensive. Do I recommend the game? ONLY if you can sink in at least 100 hours. The tutorial alone is 1-3 hours depending on how fast you read or not at all as well as how well you understand the game. So money, will go down the drain if you choose to spend it on this game. It's worth it though. 500 hours of gameplay is average for most players for this game. Be prepared.\t\t\t\t\t
