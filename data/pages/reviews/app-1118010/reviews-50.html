\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMassive expansion aptly describes this DLC. Iceborne is essentially World's G-rank update, providing at least double the playtime the original game had on offer.

It introduces the clutch claw, which I am frankly not a fan of. It breaks the pace of the hunts, and the game design is sometimes unsure if it wants to encourage you to use it, or punish you for it. The encouragement stems from suboptimal damage since they buffed the hitzone defenses of all Master Rank monsters, and heavily nerfs them if you manage to tenderize them with a clutch claw weapon attack.
However, getting the timing down for a successful claw attack is pretty tedius since monsters contextually react to when you're attached, often just attacking with the body part you are on. This is alleviated by using mantles like rocksteady and temporal, but post-launch update hunts directly counter this by sometimes just killing you instantly.

That said, the rest of the update holds up very well. The story still sucks, but the new monsters attributed to it are very welcome and interesting additions. There is also a ton of nostalgia bait for veterans who had hoped to see monsters like Tigrex and Glavenus in the base game.
Old monsters in the base game are also provided a new moveset to make the refights against them not too redundant. Though the design on some monsters, mainly the original elder dragons, leaves a lot to be desired in Iceborne. Generally in terms of tedium moreso than difficulty.

The endgame grind can be considered rather excessive for some people, though I was very engaged given my playtime with this one. There are rooms for improvements in most places really, given the systems haven't been formidably designed, but at least modding for the PC version can alleviate... some of it.
Of course, at this point in time, almost none of it matters as long as you can get the gear from the post-launch true final boss of the game, Fatalis. End-game augment his gear, and you've officially won the game, essentially. Again, not a perfect design choice, but it's nice to have a goal to strive for at least.

Overall, recommended experience. A lot of things to appreciate, a lot of things to hope they will do better in their next generation of Monster Hunter. Some of which I know won't be an issue in Rise, at least.\t\t\t\t\t
