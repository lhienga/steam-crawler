\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright! Now that the final content update is out, I can give a good review. There is a lot of things to cover, so an obligatory TLDR in the beginning.
Pros:
-plenty of new monsters to enjoy
-new slinger burst mechanics are useful
-slinger ammo has a purpose
-there is a halfway decent story
-the endgame bosses are fantastic! (more below)
-plenty of content to enjoy for months.
-a wonderful and helpful community
Cons:
-Monster AI will occasionally abuse the most annoying move
-Lots of time and effort is required to get to the end game prepared
-the Endgame is punishing and will make unprepared players cry
-RNG is back for materials, somewhat more far but still annoying
-Guiding lands is much more livable, but still annoying if you need a bunch of materials
-The end game bosses make me cry
So now that is out of the way, let’s dig into it. First, the addition of Master Rank is where all the culmination of progress, skills, loadout preparation, and game mechanics are utilized to the fullest. The monsters are tougher, the moves are harder to avoid, and you are a paper doll when you start. Get into a fight you aren’t ready for, get ready to take a ride on the cart a few times. Personally, I loved it. You go form being the top of the food chain to suddenly having to fight your way back up again, which is the main appeal to me for monster hunter. You start with a rock or bone on a stick, then you work up to an ethereal and intimidating looking set of armor that makes you look like a god.
Iceborn then laughs at your glowing armor and tells you to get good all over again. there were a few fights that did cause some frustration before the storyline boss, but nothing that can really be called a game breaker. The story boss is a satisfying fight and will make you mad If you are not willing to grind for better armor and gear, or send out an SOS for help. But the payoff for beating the boss is amazing and the armor is a good STARTING POINT for the true end game.
Once you beat the story, you get access to the guiding lands, a hunter’s dream where you can fight to your hearts content, act like that wild man and do a no-item run, or just mess around and see who you can throw off the tallest cliff. When the Guiding Lands were first introduced, it was a nightmare, but things have gotten better. It is faster to Level regions, monsters drop better loot, and a few dedicated days of play will give you plenty of materials to augment anything.
Also the augment system was overhauled an no longer needs streamstones, which is nice.
And with the Augment system, Capcom Gave us the first big monster that broke the game, Safi’Jiva. Safi was a magnificent fight for a siege monster, the music was amazing, the feeling of finally being the ones to slay the beast was the best feeling, and the armor wrecked everything. Wearing Safi’s Armor made a literal berserker out of anyone, making a majority of the past armors almost irrelevant for damage and the quickest times. The upgrade system for the weapons became extensively grindy and the limited avalibuilty of the siege means that many players will miss out on the materials they desperately need for the Next Big Monster, but it seems that the Siege between Kulve Taroth and Safi’Jivva rotate on a weekly/monthly basis now.
Now we get to the two most Recent Endgame Bosses.

ALATREON. The Single monster that Tanked the Reviews for MHW:Iceborn so bad, I was having flashback to No-man’s Sky. There is a plethora of reasons for this.
The first is that you can jump into the fight right after beating the main story. If you do this, you are going to die. Alatreon was made for the people who have spent months poring time into getting the best possible build, making themselves a god among monsters. There are literally hundreds of resources online and hundreds of videos on what works best against him. I recommend giving them a look and putting in the time to get the gear. Now for the fight itself.

The fight on it’s own is difficult and punishing, the mechanics that were originally abused, such as clutchclaw and farcaster, were nerfed and removed respectively. To this day, even though I have beaten him dozens of times for each weapon and a full set of armor, I have difficulty fighting him with no carts.
The reason for this is two words: Escaton Judgment.
The mechanic on paper is fine, give players extra pressure to use a different playstyle and weapons, or they will not be able to complete the mission. It is possible to be Alatreon without using Elemental weapons, but it is so much more difficult. It is so much more difficult that a casual player would likely never manage it on their own, leaving it a much better move to switch to Elemental weaponry anyway. Unfortunately, this all sounds good on PAPER. In practice, Altreon acted like a meatgrinder that would simply discard hunters that were not running the new elemental meta builds.
Essentially, if you were not running a max elemental Safijivva Long Sword with a full set of fully upgraded SafiJivva armor, as well as having a health augment on your long sword, then you were going to have a bad day. There are other weapons that CAN beat Alatreon, but the Long sword seems to excel in evening the playing field when it comes to DPS and landing hits.
FATALIS
whoo boy, it made me smile like a madman when Fatalis was announced. As a long time fan of Monster Hunter as a franchise, Fatalis wasn’t a ‘new’ monster to me. He looked gorgeous and I already knew he was going to be a royal pain to fight and I was looking forward to it. I was pumped, I was ready, I dusted off my old weapons, practiced with my Great sword, Long sword, even tired to learnt he Duel Blades again. I farmed; I built the best set I could think of. I bullied Alatreon till I got his stuff, then augmented the crap out of it. I could solo most monsters in under ten minuets on average. I was the sapphire star hunter, conqueror of Safijivva and Alatreon!
Then Fatalis picked me up, slammed me to the ground a few times, then set me on fire for good measure. I loved every bit of it because FINALY they could do what they always wanted to with Fatalis. He truly felt like a monster that could and would end the world. To the time of writing this review, I’ve yet to beat him, and I’m alright with that. Each fight, I’m getting better, getting closer to that sweet quest clear.
So, a few things about Fatalis. First and most obvious, He hurts. A lot. As in, he moves forward and you are missing half your health bar. The second is that he punishes people who try to play it safe or take cover. The Third is that it is a fight without moves like Escaton Judgment, but still managed to make hunters cry…and that is a good thing. There are videos on how to deal with him, a few tips and tricks here and there, but I won’t spoil them.
For a monster that is the last great fight of the game, Fatalis was perfect. He takes your confidence, crushes it, then sets it on fire. He really likes setting things on fire. The equipment he drops is equally as terrifying and powerful as he is, making Safi’s look like the scraps of leather armor you started this game with.
FINAL THOUGHTS
Iceborn is fantastic, some minor Grinding Lands aside. The fights are tough, the rewards are worth it, and the music is amazing as always. The sense of accomplishment when you beat a monsters or finally take down that dragon, no other game I’ve played has ever come close. Monster hunter has always been about a Gladiator style boss fight between a puny hunter and a larger-than-life threat, truly a sense of David vs Goliath, where skill and preparation mean more than simply hitting a button and getting a big number.
Welcome to Master rank, hunters. We hope you enjoy your stay~

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(I know iceborne just had its last major update and probably no one would consider buying it now, but I'm still motivated to write this review because iceborne has been treated pretty unfairly imo)
First of all, this DLC is way better than what it's rated now with arguably more contents than the base game with new monsters and weapon moves. It deserves a better rating. (and maybe better players)

There are flaws indeed. I consider the Guiding Land one of the worst design ever with Guiding Land exclusive materials that you have to farm for, with high rng dropping and long grinding (yes, i'm talking about fxxking slumbering greatbone). They also need to fix the drop rate for some extremely rare decos. I know how frustrating farming specific decos can be. 600hrs in game and not a single shield up deco...
However, Capcom has been updating their designs. Lures and extremely rare decos can be created through melding and the time taken to upgrade a region in the Guiding Land is much shorter now.

But most of the time you see a thumb down it's because some players have taken their rage too far. There are challenging mechanisms for some monsters, namely alatreon and fatalis. For alatreon you have to deal damage with elemental weapons to pass an elemental damage check or else the whole team would be wiped out. The fight itself is also challenging with alatreon's high damage combos and few places to wall slam. Fatalis, on the other hand, has stupid oneshot damages and frequent wide flame AOEs. There are counters but they are hard to execute by inexperienced players. If they made the level requirements for alatreon and fatalis 100 instead of 24 (or something in the 20s) I think the DLC would be rated a lot higher.
FYI, I made 3 different lbg builds for alatreon and had to make a db build because farcaster is disabled during alatreon fight (and I've tried 10+ times before getting the first slay). I tried out 3 different builds and spent roughly 5hrs for the first fatalis slay. Yes, the game is challenging, and if you have a review with logical argument why the design of a single monster is bad enough to ruin your whole gameplay experience, I'm totally fine and might even gladly agree with your points (yes, I hate fatalis rng). But if you haven't attempted to even try out new builds and the only way to channel your rage is to spam irrational negative reviews... I feel sorry for you and the game.\t\t\t\t\t
