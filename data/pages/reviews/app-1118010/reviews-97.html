\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith MHWorld getting its final title update in a month, here are my closing thoughts about this wonderful game.

Base World was a blast to play. The environments, monster designs, addictive grind, really fun combat, amazing multiplayer, and armor/weapon aesthetics all meshed together in harmony to become one of my favorite iterations of monster hunter to date. It had its flaws, but ultimately was a big step in the right direction.

Then ICEBORNE came along and doubled down on everything that made the original World so great.

+ The new monsters have awesome designs moveset revamps that make them exciting and fresh. There's ALOT of new monsters.
+ The Hoarfrost Tundra area is vast and a pleasure to explore. The entire map evokes a strong sense of environment design.
+ Master Rank is certainly difficult and a welcome challenge for newcomers and experts alike.
+ The armor and weapon designs look beautiful and I am happy that all of these armors will be able to be layered
(worn on top of your "normal" equipment without affecting any skills/stats)
+ All weapons have gotten moveset changes, some significant and others not that much. Nevertheless, the combat remains
the crown jewel of this game.
+ Just like Base World, it remains as one of my favorite co-op multiplayer experiences ever. Exploring environments and
taking down monsters with others, be it friends or strangers, gives a sense of togetherness and accomplishment that no other game has been able to achieve for me.

Thank you CAPCOM for making and completing this awesome game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't let the price put you off. This "expansion" is essentially a whole new game added onto the original worlds.
I had a few problems with the original game, and most, if not all, have been dealt with now. This includes returning monsters and a hell of a lot more content.

Now, no game is perfect, and I do still have some problems, including:
A lot of weapons still don't have unique designs, which doesn't matter anyway because...

Most of the weapons will never be touched by you. All the "best" weapons can be gotten by fighting 2 or 3 monsters for their weapons. And while you're not forced to use these weapons...

The endgame content assumes you have the best gear. If you don't, you will struggle. This isn't a problem in itself (it's endgame after all), but this endgame is available right after the final boss, so players will be inclined to go and do that instead of maxing out their gear.

I would go into more detail, and I would happily write a 5000 word essay on how much I hate the clutch claw mechanic (to sum up: broken, basically required to use and annoying to use), but this review is getting a bit long, so let's sum up...

If you liked the base game and have the money to spare, this is certainly worth the price. If you didn't like the base game because "it didn't feel like monster hunter", this might've fixed what you didn't like about it. If you just didn't like it, then why would you even consider buying this?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIceborne is a solid 8.5-9.5 out of 10 as an expansion. The reason why the score fluctuates is because sometimes the patches screw things up, but when a patch is good, this expansion never feels better to play (Except the connection... you get disconnected... a lot in all patches). You may see that Iceborne gets a lot of hate suddenly when a patch drops, rarely is it because a patch actually breaks something, that was the case when Iceborne first launched, almost never happens anymore. The reason why Iceborne gets bad reviews when a patch drops is because people get mad that they add a new monster that actually gives them a challenge. That's it, I wish I was kidding. The community is Iceborne either complains that a monster is WAY too hard (See Alatreon and Rajang), and usually they're not hard at all, players just aren't used to fighting them but because they can't win in one try, they get whiny, or the second case which is the new monster was "too easy" and therefore disappointing (See Frostfang Barioth). Other than that, the community is great.

Now for the actual content of Iceborne, they literally more than double the base game's content. If you click my profile and see my past review of the base game, it sounds like I give base game a bad rep, and I kind of do, and it's because I'm so used to how amazing Iceborne is now. Everything was improved upon, even less grinding for small things like herbs and honey are needed, the steamworks lets you get essential hunting items just for playing the game and the new central hub is MILES better than base game's.

As for the monsters: Every monster is just incredible, they took a ton of base game monsters that seemed incomplete or just boring and spiced them up by giving them subspecies, or with the addition of Master Rank, gave every single monster new moves. A ton of fan favorites from the past, new monsters are very well done, and they're still adding more constantly for free.

Armor/Weapons, the meta is SO much better in Iceborne, you can use basically anything you want, you could in base game as well, but you could just tell that you were using a non-meta set, in Iceborne, you rarely feel like hunts take long because of your equipment. Weapons still suffer from terrible designs, but the new unique ones are just amazing, and plus, for armor and weapons both, they introduced layered settings so you can customize how they look fixing that old problem. Armor design as a whole is a massive step up from base game.

Difficulty: This is the big one that people complain about. They're so mad that they can't just stomp every monster in the game now. You still can, but it's not nearly as mindless as base game was where you literally NEVER had to worry about the risk of fainting, low tier monsters in Iceborne can still take you out if you're not careful (I'm talking about monsters from Iceborne's story such as the Tobi Kadachi subspecies, not just normal Tobi who you're not forced to fight during the story and is still a pushover). End game monsters are actually difficult to a degree, as I said in my previous review, the only challenge in base game was Arch tempered Elders, now even non tempered monsters can take you out without any sort of gimmicks.

Story: So they tried hard to actually do a story this time around... and to be frank, while it was better than base game, it still felt like, "there's a new monster, go kill it". They did focus more of the characters which was nice and they're no longer just glorified NPCs that we are told are important just because they have a unique name. It was fine for what it was.

Endgame: It's... an actual endgame. You actually have to fight all the monsters in the game rather than just like 5 of the 40 from base game. There's, I believe, 70 monsters in the game as I write this and Frostfang Barioth is the newest, and there's no reason not to fight all of them. They all contribute something to the endgame grind through the new endgame area. I'll shorten it down to GL as to not spoil the actual name, the GL has levels and as you increase it's levels, harder monsters appear, but by increasing it's levels, you decrease other levels in the GL, so you have to constantly fight the weaker monsters to get to the stronger monsters and get their special materials and rinse and repeat, it's a simple cycle but it allows all monsters to included in the grind in some way (or almost all, there's like 8 monsters they can't put in the GL for varying reasons).

Weapons: The weapons in MHW were solid as is, but then they made them better. So much more freedom and creativity with their movesets with super small simple changes, or additions of new moves that flow extremely well without changing the weapon's original playstyle or use much at all.

A big problem that people run into is optimization. When Iceborne first launched, it was nearly unplayable, even with a good PC, I constantly froze for up to a minute at a time during hunts and pretty frequently at that. They "patched" it a month later, only fixed it for a portion of the community, not including me, it helped a lot, but I still froze every now and again, they again attempted to fix it, and this time, it worked. I have not seen many people complain since and the game runs perfectly. It might be a little demanding of your PC, but it's such a big game, that's to be expected imo.

So with all that, I can safely say that Iceborne is 100% worth the investment, it takes an extremely good base game and fixes a large portion of all the major problems, the only ones that are still constant are poor connections online leading to disconnects and an improved upon, but still poor system for farming important endgame materials called Decorations (different from the GL stuff from before).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't get the negative reviews of Iceborne. The DLC actually offers more than the base game! If you've had a hard time finishing quests in the base game, then prepare to suffer even more in Iceborne, but that doesn't mean the quests are unbeatable, they're just challenging. Those who comment about difficult monsters are either solo players or just plain lazy to put up a fight against the new monsters. Take note that this is a multiplayer game, if you're under geared, you can fire an SOS and other players can join you in finishing the quests.

Let's go with the negative side of the game. CONNECTION. The sessions are not stable and will disconnect you from the game randomly. It get annoying at times but we got used to it. I hope Capcom is working on this to improve the gameplay.

Lastly, one of the best bang of the buck for this DLC is the content. Not only will you get to go on new quests and maps or battle new monsters, Iceborne is filled with events that are updated most twice a month and they're all FREE. Among all the DLCs from other games, this IMO is worth every money spent. Cheers!
