\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI sadly can't recommend this dlc, UNLESS you have friends to play it with or use the long sword/bow/heavy cannon. I say this because from my experience of doing Iceborne hunts solo they feel like a chore because of the length. One hunt usually lasts for 30-40 minutes against new monsters and you'll often have to deal with monsters with roughly 20k-30k health. Although the monsters themselves aren't difficult it's really just the length being the problem for solo hunts, with friends however although the monster's health does scale it is much faster. Every weapon also gets new moves which is nice, but because of this it makes some of the already strong weapons now ridiculously overpowered in the right hands such as the long sword. So tldr unless you have a group or enjoy using the weapons that got major buffs you probably shouldn't get this but instead get rise when it comes to pc next year.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis isn't honestly something you can just faceroll right into. You can't sit there and be like "I defeated all the tempered monsters and the kirin 20 times! Let's do this! YEET!" These new monsters will stomp you into the ground like leftover cheeseburgers. The new world itself is awesome and you really have to know what to take with you in order to take on the new beasts gear wise. Don't be fooled by these fluffballs. Health and stamina are so critical in Iceborne, and you have to rely on your consumables to help out. You'll hit Master Rank almost right away but that doesn't mean squat if you can't own up to it. You have to know what you're doing. I favor the Charge Blade and it's saved my butt multiple times, especially with how I mastered the combos, but if you prefer speed over strength, the Lance, Insect Glaive and Long Sword help in the long run. Up your Palico's gear too and hope for the best. These monsters are smarter, faster and meaner. Best of luck, hunters!\t\t\t\t\t
