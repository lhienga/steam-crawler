\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI write this review as someone who has gotten all achievements to this game, and spent more hours in this game than I have in Skyrim and Skyrim SE combined. The Monster Hunter franchise is like no other game out there, and Monster Hunter World has been the brightest shining star of them all (my opinion for Monster Hunter Rise is pending, as I haven't played it yet). The dynamic of gameplay may be be a little jarring at first, but there's reason behind every mechanic put in that game, and further you get in the game, the better it gets!

In terms of multiplayer, I'd always recommend playing with others when you can. The dynamics of the hunts blossom the more players that hop in to help, and teamwork is a must. My entire experience has been with strangers, and while voice chat is optional, I can count only on one hand that I ever hoped into a party where we used it; most communication is actually through simple chat stickers or emotes.

The Iceborne DLC itself doubles the thickness to the game by doubling the roster of monsters from new variants to new species entirely (some of which are returning monsters from previous games). New areas are added, slews of new armors and weapons, a honestly feels way more than an optional add-on. If Monster Hunter World is like meeting a new, outgoing friend, the Iceborne DLC is like meeting their siblings and parents who welcome you in like part of their family. Character, sound, monster, armor, weapon, and world design are on a whole other level, graphics and animations are candy for the eyes, and altogether worth every penny for both Monster Hunter World and the Iceborne expansion at full price.

Thank you, Capcom, for this sapphire star of a game.\t\t\t\t\t
