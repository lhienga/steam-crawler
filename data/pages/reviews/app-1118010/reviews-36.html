The boredom this game put me through is unmatched (only elden ring was worse). A hunt can easily take more than 10 minutes, and basically consists of you chasing the Monster for this whole time, fight a little and chase again. That’s it. That’s this game.
By the end of the main quest my hunter fainted only once, probably because I fell asleep… and I only used weapon and gear you got from farming, so no guardian(?) gear.
And don’t give me that that’s when it becomes fun, because the monsters get stronger. No they don’t, because so do you with the game throwing even more and more gear your way which you relay on, and makes it even clumsier to use.

Yes, I don’t like it but I have to use it if I want to get it done with the hunt. So basically during the hunt you’re busy shuffling through your inventory bar (sorry, couldn’t care what it’s called) for buffs, mantles, potions etc. Oh yes, there’s the radial menu… god forbid its clumsiness. Never seen speed runners us it, so it has to be crappy.
In this game you start as a weakling and you end as one. Your success entirely depends on your choice for the right gear. What a sad game...
So in the end: You like boring, bad, clumsy, unresponsive combat system like souls/elden ring, you might like this game. You’re more for fast and responsive combat like Dragon’s Dogma? Do not touch!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am going to review Monster Hunter World and its expansion Iceborn.

This is my first entry in the Monster Hunter Franchise. I must i was immediately grabbed by it. I had heard all sorts of horrible comparisons which kept me away. I wish i didn't listen.

The Gameplay
The core elements of MHW, comprise of Tracking a Monster, Hunting and Killing that Monster, and getting its parts to create better armor to hunt with. This is executed very well, with a wide roster of monsters and their signature attacks and patterns.
The lesser talked about things which are still core are Equipment, Weapon Choice, Gearing you palico, and getting decorations for your equipment. These all fill in the gaps left by actual hunting of monsters.
As the game gets further, and especially into Iceborn, the monster get harder. More challenging, and the fights become a real challenge. Where you have to bring the best of all the tools, items, and equipment you have gathered to fight some truly fearsome foes. If you love Souls-Esque bosses, there is one at the very end that made me want to replay the whole game at that moment.

The Style
Graphics and Audio is on point. I would say MHW is the best looking Monster Hunter game i have played. They use plenty of visual and audio cues to help you fight the battle. Not to mention all the different tools and traps. Visually all the armors are mix and match. You will almost never have a pure fullset of armor. That asthetic is really appealing, and makes you want to work towards you next piece or even Layer a different armor over it to look completely different.

9/10 even in 2022
