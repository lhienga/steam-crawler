\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good expansion that, though I have gripes with it, I generally recommend to get alongside base World. The start of the expansion is a lot of fun and offers good challenge, however the addition of the Clutch Claw has to be one of the most obnoxious gimmicks in a Monster Hunter game and some of the late game monsters felt more frustrating than challenging or fun.

Clutch claw is decent on some weapons, such as hammer since you can mix the grapple into the charge attacks fairly smoothly and since you mostly want to focus the head with hammer you usually only really need to worry about keeping one part tenderised, but on other weapons such as greatsword I felt like it was obnoxious. especially with the new stagger animation that monsters do which causes them to reposition away from you. I've had a good amount of occasions where I hit a charge slash and the damage triggers the monsters to stagger away from me just as I start getting my true charge ready. You can try to ignore the tenderise gimmick but there is a noticeable difference in damage and how long the hunt can take.

The late game difficulty is also something to prepare heavily for - it very much feels like the game expects you to fully utilise launching monsters into walls, tenderising consistently and using mantles. Rocksteady felt practically mandatory for me since the late-game monsters are so fast I personally had trouble taking advantage of some openings. Of course, you'll get better as it as you learn monster moves but it can still be challenging.

Generally I felt like the gimmicks soured the experience for me quite a bit. I felt like Rise was a bit too reliant on wirebug moves but at the very least they felt fun to use and the wirebug mobility felt like I was more on an even ground with the monsters, plus Sunbreak added in a lot of really fun skills that could be a game changer for some weapons.

Late-game Iceborne monsters are very fast and aggressive and you will need to learn to adapt. Playing with others makes the tenderising aspect a bit less troublesome as multiple hunters will (hopefully) be taking advantage of openings and tenderising multiple parts or launching monsters into walls to create openings for everyone.

Still, I think this is still something that if you like World is worth getting because of the amount of new content it adds - I enjoyed most of the new monster additions and the new area is one of my favourites in the game. Just be prepared for a difficulty spike and if you feel like you're struggling, take your time to make sure you got the right gear for your current challenge.\t\t\t\t\t
