\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs fantastic as it is, this game is not for the impatient or weak of mind.

The developers really do not care if you figure out how to play their game or not, and provide only the most heavy-handed and unappealing type of tutorial. It's a wonder that the game sold as well in the West as it did. Happily, these problems are improved (though not fixed) in Rise for the Switch. Some examples:
1. If a player hasn't seen certain cut scenes, his multiplayer friends are locked out of the quest. WHY?!
2. When you buy Iceborne, it is not necessarily that version that will load up when you click the desktop icon for the game. You'll love not being able to use the clutch claw and wonder why you're the only person who can't seem to do so.
3. You cannot see your friends in the hub world, except in the Gathering Hub. Thankfully, this was fixed in Rise.
4. Cannot just fast travel from the Start Menu to different parts of the main hub. This also was fixed in Rise.
5. The added DLC armor they give players makes the beginning sets of armor totally pointless, which detracts from the fun of progression in the early part of the game.
6. You cannot repeat Assigned Quests, which means that a lower-level multiplayer friend is a drag on the experience. WHY?!
7. The Training Area is hidden in your room, and might require a Google or YouTube search, when it should be out in the open and easy to access right at the onset of the game.
8. Nothing in the game tells you at the beginning just how crucial your choice of weapon is to your experience of the game, when it's probably the defining element of the series, though they spend lots of time force-feeding boring "story narrative" and forcing veteran players to learn basics that they would already know. WHY?! This is NOT solved in Rise, though there is less of it.
9. Etc., I've mentioned enough.

Anyway, I'm sure you'll actually truly enjoy it, if you're willing to study/bang your head against it to get at the fun. If you must have a rating, it's an 85/100 in my book, but probably should be rated higher for having the kind of multiplayer experience that's hard to get from anything aside from the Borderlands series. I just can't award any higher due to the above issues.
