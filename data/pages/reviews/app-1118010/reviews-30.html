\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIceborne brought a slew of additions worthy of the price tag. The new Master rank offers new Monsters and the return of old favorites from previous titles all revamped and arguably their best iterations. The recent update fixed prior issues and by taking advantage of the DirectX 12 API you could easily improve your performance from barely hitting 50 frames to reaching cap without changing your settings.

The new areas are amazing, the hunts ever challenging, and the grind even more rewarding.

The negative reviews are mostly from it's initial release that caused performance issues but has now been all addressed. I was willing to replay this game again after completing it in PS4 and Rise honestly felt a bit of a downgrade for me (Don't get me wrong, Rise is stellar) and this feels like the definitive way to experience the game with all of it's QoL additions while still retaining all the charm and nuances.

Graphics 9/10 - Monster Hunter had always been a system seller for me and the graphics always never lagged behind the gameplay aspects which is this games most powerful element. Animations are amazing, the biomes are stellar and the environments feel alive. The skybox is amazing, the weather effects dynamic, and the local fauna feels organic. Compared to Rise which feels arcade-y (The hit effects are absurt and the UI is almost atrocious, albeit still servicable) this one feels grounded the same way it did when playing MH 1-4

Sound 8/10 - Music kicks in once fights start and are otherwise absent in normal exploration. It is dynamic enough to adjust to the type of combat and the ambience is what you'll hear in general exploration. Weapon hits sounds impactful, monsters sound amazing and the wildlife makes it all feel alive. Drawback is the small amount of tracks but are still otherwise amazing compositions. (The Rotten Vale combat theme is amazing)

Gameplay 10/10 - Weapon moveset, monster windups, spacing, loot tables? Item interactions? All stellar. It's like playing a fighting game with the way you learn your moves and spacing and learning when and where to use them. Monsters are either fast, slow and tanky, and a combination of both. You will need all the tools the game has to offer to optimize your loadouts and builds. Learning the environment is key and you can play as casually as you want or you can play like TDM doing all the speedrun strats. There is no button mashing here folks, this isn't Kingdom Hearts or DMC.

Replayability 8/10 - There is so much replay value here, in fact it's too replayable you might end up with a burnout. The gameplay loop of repeating fights is dynamic you'll never experience a hunt the same way twice. It is also key to learning how a monster fights. And oh my god the multiplayer aspect could be atrocious sometimes but it has been somewhat fixed.

TLDR get it, the expansion is ridiculously more expensive than the base game but what you get is basically MHW2.
If you've seen SkillUp's review of this game you will know that what he said is true: There is no other game like this. This is peak Monster Hunter.\t\t\t\t\t
