\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlso writing a review since overall the reviews are still 'mixed' when I think they do not deserve to be. Iceborne had a lot of issues at launch which I knew about through the r/monsterhunter subreddit while not having the game myself, but since picking it up last November-ish I can guarantee I have had almost no problems whatsoever with CPU usage or what not on my 2017 gaming laptop. The MOST I have ever had as far as issues is desyncing when someone faints which I think is a server side issue. [SEE EDIT BELOW]

That being said, while Iceborne is technically a DLC it is very much essentially a new game. The MH series usually re-releases old games and adds G rank, more monsters, and end game content in entirely new games as a kind of 'tour de force'. These are usually associated with the previous monster hunter game in the series which experimented more with new mechanics, entirely new locales, etc.

Iceborne is very much that 'tour de force' after World, IMO, and hits the nail on the head. The new fights and G rank is amazing, the loot drops are so much more bearable than it was back in the World HR days since you can actually get good charms and gear easily by just playing through all the games content. The final monsters in Safi Jiva, Alateron, Fatalis, Rajang, etc. are some of the most fun I have had playing Monster Hunter in a long time as a long time fan of the series.

This is especially true for Safi Jiva. The soundtrack, the siege style mechanics of the fight itself working together with other hunters, and the monsters moveset all work together to make a near-perfect close to the main storyline of Iceborne and I have had fun grinding his gear for over a week and am still not bored of it. If you are on the fence, it might be worth your while to watch a video of the fight on Youtube (if you are okay with spoiling the fight a bit) to get a sense of what the game has to offer - it is nothing like the god awful Zorah Magdaros fight.

EDIT: Apparently lot's of people have seen and liked this review, so I figured I ought to update my review with some new info. After I had a particularly bad session with desyncing after each faint, at the end of each hunt, I figured I should do some research to figure out what the heck was happening. I found some threads discussing that, unlike what I previously thought, CPU usage is actually an issue on some lower performing machines like my own. While the game might usually run fine (I can run the game at a steady 30 FPS) when the game needs to update or save mid-hunt the game will stall out causing de-sync issues and disconnections.

This can be pretty serious, especially if you are not a veteran of the series like myself and thus carting each hunt is probably a regular occurrence. I have found that more than once I had a good party but an unfortunate faint meant that I got disconnected and had to either fight the monster OMO or leave the hunt which can get very frustrating for some of the end game monsters. I think my previous review was a bit too rosy and made too light of the issue.

Don't get me wrong - the game is FANTASTIC and I still stand by that - but if you have a lower end machine you might do well to do some research before you splurge. I find it bearable, even if frustrating sometimes, but others may not.\t\t\t\t\t
