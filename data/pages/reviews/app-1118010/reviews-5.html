While you could consider it a continuation of the progression of Monster Hunter World, when the second monster, a big herbivore mammoth-deer thing, has more health than the raid boss of the main game, upon which defeating it gave you an unskippable credits sequence, it just kinda feels like nothing you've already done actually mattered.

From an in-universe perspective, there was a hierarchy to the monster progression of World that sort of made sense. There were increasingly powerful predators that culminated in the Elder Dragons, monstrous beings that defied the ecology of the setting. But then you sail a bit across some water and everything is not only somehow more powerful than a creature that was dangerous enough to redefine the understanding of the setting at the culmination of the story, but that's just a big deer.

Everything else just gets worse from there, and it's all just for "difficulty".

Everything hits a little bit harder but has obnoxious amounts of health, and the rhythm of fights just kinda stays the same but now it takes longer. It's artificially inflated difficulty instead of actually being interesting or making sense in the game, and it's just not fun anymore. I went in with the highest-grade equipment I had access to, and it felt like I may as well have just started a new game for all the progress it felt like I was making. Maybe some people want that experience without losing their character, but for me, after almost 100 hours of playing and grinding out equipment and helping friends do the same, it honestly just feels insulting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Iceborne" - "Monster Hunter World" went and got itself a winter coat. I dug the stuff from the base game more, but the new monsters, weapons, armor and biome are (mostly) great and are worth your time. For a new player, I don't see why you wouldn't get this straight up when starting a new game.

"Iceborne" expands on the essence of "Monster Hunter World," a game that, despite its awesomeness, is in an awkward place with its user interface, item management, and the very mechanics that define its gameplay experience. The jankiness of the UI, the absence of a monster's health bar, oddly enough, has become part of the adventure for me. Like the quick cycling through items to find a healing potion in the nick of time or deploying a shock trap to capture a monster before it gets away.

Yet, the game's UI often feels like it's against you. Navigating through its menus and enduring multiple unnecessary confirmations can take away from the awesome game-play. Item management, while somewhat mitigated by the introduction of loadouts, remains a mediocre at best, often feeling like a battle against the game's design.

"Iceborne" attempts to streamline some of these elements by automating certain tasks, such as allowing players to purchase rather than painstakingly craft every item, and also craft items automatically. This change, while welcome, brings its own set of frustrations, especially when compared to the items that still require manual crafting. Maintenance work is still required - eating, managing the Steamworks, tending to botany - which, while it felt essential in preparing for a hunt in the base game, now steer on the side of tediousness. With the announced "Monster Hunter World: Wilds," I'm curious how these elements will evolve and which direction will the devs take.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIceborne is a really great expansion to a great game with some weird gameplay decisions thrown in the mix.

Pros

  • The new Monster: Iceborne brings in a lot of new monsters and variants. These include some fan favourites like Nargacuga, Tigrex and Zinogre (not my cup of tea, but many love him, so good for them I guess).
    There are a lot of creative subspecies like coral pukei (who is the single best subspecies imo) and Velum Vaal Hazak. Even a new elder dragon makes it appearance in the form of Namielle, which quickly became my favorite monster with its great design and fun fight, which incorporates a big importance in postioning yourself and area control using the slinger without it being in any way tedious.
  • Deco Armour: Iceborne adds decorative armour you can wear above your main armor just for looks. Sadly you only gain access to it late in the game and cannot colour it freely. Most of the time the colour slider changes just a tiny amount of the armour itself.
  • Seliana Gathering Hub: This Hub is a straight upgrade form base Worlds. It has everything you need: Argosy, farm, ressource center and smithy. It also is really gorgeous and cozy.
  • Endgame Hunts: The engame hunts in this game can be quite a challenge to beat, but also really fun. This includes hunts of variants like Furious Rajang and Raging Brachydios. Later on there is a really good siege fight in the form of safi'jiiva, which sees you bringing down a giant dragon as team of 16 hunters, although you fight him in groups of 4. Each hunt you weaken him more and more and drive him deeper into its nest until you can kill him finally. Its loot allows you to make a fully custom weapon where you can fill a number of slots with upgrades like element attack, crit or sharpness and in the case of e.g. gunlance adjust the shelling type and so on.
  • Guiding Lands: MHW:I's engame loop sees you hunting monsters in the guiding lands. A landmass with all the biomes in one map. For each monster you hunt in a zone you level them up. This gives you access to harder monsters and eventually tempered elder dragons. Just hunting in the guiding lands is really fun and diverse.
  • Final Boss: Fatalis in Iceborne is one of the single best end bosses I have fought yet. It was an extremely hard fight, which me and a friend brought down in multiple sittings. Each try saw us getting closer to victory bit by bit. As the monster fell to the ground I just couldn't not smile. This single boss fight will be an experience I think will not be surpassed so soon.

Cons

  • Clutch Claw: The DLC's main gimmick is the clutch claw, which you use to grapple onto the monster. This added some fun moves to a lot of weapons like Lances Clutch Claw Counter and Gunlances Wyrmstake Blast Stake (a stake, which is stuck on a monster and deals extra damage when you hit it with a shell).
    Sadly it also introduces the tenderizing loop. With the clutch claw you can qeaken monster parts so you can deal more damage. The thing is that the presence of this mechanic led the monsters to have ♥♥♥♥ hitzones unless you tenderize them. This makes tenderizing almost mandatory and becomes really tedious after a while.
  • Power Creep: With the updates every new weapon and armour became stronger than all monsters before it. This makes you effectively use like 10% of all the games various weapons at most as the differences in power are not even small.
  • Weapon Desings: The weapon designs in the DLC are still mostly slap on. Even iconic returning weapons like the Glavenus Greatsword, which is a blade like tail on a stick became slap on. It is not as bad as in the base game, but still pretty sad to see.

Overall the Iceborne DLC brings a lot to the table and I would really recommend buying it, if you liked the base game just a little. It easily doubles or triples your playtime, so the price tag is absolutely worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's ignore all the DRM nonsense Capcom is doing for the sake of this review (they're still scum for punishing paying customers and adding DRM to older games we've already paid for specifically because they don't have DRM) and look solely at whether Iceborne adds to MHW in a positive way. All I can say is no it does not. While more monsters, locales, and the guiding lands are nice the new clutch claw mechanic is absolutely terrible. It's essentially mandatory and makes fights so lame as you either have to try and find openings to use it, or wait for the stagger which is designed to let you use the clutch claw. On top of that it's relatively inaccurate and you can often attach to places you didn't want to or didn't mean to. Overall a terrible mechanic and whoever thought of this and approved it at Capcom should never be allowed to design another mechanic because if they understood what makes a mechanic fun they would have scrapped this as soon as the first iteration was done. It sucks.

There are still not enough unique looking weapon and armor sets. Too many sets still have the issue of being slight modifications of low rank looking sets, or the weapons have that same issue of it being a base weapon with some sort of flair added on for the specific monster. Is it really too much to ask to design a cool and unique looking weapon for each monster? It feels lazy and lame.

This could have been a great expansion were it not for the clutch claw but that alone ruins it. I thought people might have been exaggerating how bad it is but they aren't. I thought maybe I don't have to use it and I can still break monsters the old way? Nope not really. It's just terrible and makes the game a lot less fun. Shame on whoever approved this addition.\t\t\t\t\t
