\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: I just realized months later that this doesn't show the 800+ hours I have in the base game and shows zero hours played, so the first sentence seems disingenuous. Sorry lol
And thanks for all the awards everyone :)

After so many hours I can safely say without an ounce of regret that the time I spent with this incredible title was a journey I'll always remember fondly, but there are a few things that made me get sick of it entirely.

--The Clutch Claw ruined the MHW formula and turned what were fun fights into monotonous annoying encounters. Adding the need to 'soften' monster parts really put the game at it's absolute worst due to it being a necessity rather than a gameplay or playstyle choice. This leads to the next problem..

--Most armor sets in the game are useless other than for transmog / vanity purposes. The only good sets were those that do their best to do as much damage to a monster's weakened part as possible or negate the need entirely. Any sets that negated the need for damaging parts were swiftly nerfed, leading players back to the same Crit / Exploit builds and never changing. This leads to another issue...

--Monster health, damage, and speed seem to have been designed post-launch as a direct response to the insane damage output by certain builds / sets / weapons. In turn this then made those meta damage builds almost completely necessary which caused the lack of diverse builds to be even more apparent. The speed and mobility of monsters wasn't outrageous but needing to do consistent damage made some weapons much less desired. I mained Lance, Gunlance, Hammer, and Insect Glaive all the time and the tediousness required for everything mentioned above was only amplified by flying enemies and the need to hit specific parts of a monster. This then leads to my final gripe with MHW:IB and why I eventually lost interest entirely...

--Ranged weapons (Heavy Bowgun, Light Bowgun and Bow) are so much easier to use and play with it really made me feel like I was playing a 3rd person Civilization game with weapons / tech from the information age while my allies are still using swords. All the of the issues presented by the clutch claw could simply be ignored entirely. You don't need to hit specific parts, take risks, be flashy and have fun, or even really play Monster Hunter. It just turned into Horizon: Zero Dawn with other people flailing around big swords as you melt the monster without breaking a sweat. Not saying it was the only or best meta but holy crap it was easy. THEN..

--Event or big monsters that required specific elements was and is a terrible idea. Can't build or play how you want! No sir! Gotta follow a specific mechanical path in a boss fight like it's World of Warcraft. Many players loved the change as something new, but I viewed it as another tedious and flat out stupid design choice that only solidified my choice in quitting for good. Needing to coordinate different elements at different times with random players adds one more layer of unnecessary bloat (not difficulty) that made the fights less appealing.

Tl;dr
Base game was great, but the new mechanics and additions influenced very terrible decision making in the post-launch content that turned a fun and unique experience into a bland and unintuitive hammer-and-nail scenario.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNEUTRAL REVIEW (chose thumbs down because no one reads for positive reviews LOL)

Pros:
-More Monster Hunter Content
-New Locations
-New and New-ish monsters/Armors/Weapons
-The story missions almost match base-game's length
-Tougher fights

Cons:
-Monsters are HP sponges
(Base World hunts last 5-15 minutes max, Iceborne hunts for NORMAL people are MINIMUM 15-30 minutes long)

-You fight monsters in the middle of their Crack High (they spam moves and jump around a LOT more than base game monsters. Think triple dashes across the arena followed by an AOE attack, rinse repeat)

-Base game armors/weapons completely useless.
While I like grinding for new gear and understand this is probably the only way to get people to ditch their old gear and move on, it does suck at first getting one-shot by some low level monsters.

-Some fights are legit hair-pulling and annoying (Crack Kitty family when you FIRST meet them,
Black Veil, and some others later on)

-Clutch Claw... Cool new mechanic but its not optional. Its MANDATORY to spam it and ram monsters into walls to speed up fights or maintain damage on tenderized parts.

*************TL;DR**********
Great expansion if you love everything Monster Hunter related
Great if you love being a Macho Dark Souls MLG Pro
Great if you like the MH grind
Great if you like new Areas

Terrible if you can't stand spongy Monsters
Terrible if you hate abusing the Clutch Claw
Terrible if you hate potentially unfair fights that mayyyy require multiplayer to beat (depending on skill level)

And thus concludes my neutral review.\t\t\t\t\t
