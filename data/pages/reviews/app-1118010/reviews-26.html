\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMonster Hunter: World - 9/10
Iceborne DLC - 6/10

Cons:
* Many Re-Skinned Monsters
* Monsters have 6x more HP than High Rank counterparts from the Base Game. Despite minimal damage upgrades.
* Monsters are EXTREMELY aggressive. They're constantly moving and attacking. There is no downtime to attack, you just need to trade damage and shove potions down your throat.
* Monster Hunts are extremely lengthy. You will never again finish a hunt prior to the 20 minute mark.

Pros:
* Lots of content, even after the Main Story Questline is over, there's ALOT to grind for.
* New Mechanics to obtain loot.
* Two new maps.
* Many new gadgets and upgrades.
* Weapon and Armor Tiers up to Rank 12. (Base game stops at Rank 8)
* Clutch Claw - which can be used to grab onto monsters at anytime. (Only available to Iceborne owners)

Closing thoughts: Don't buy Iceborne unless you plan to play it with friends who can share aggro with you. Otherwise it's a bad time. The monsters are frustratingly aggressive & constantly move. You'll find yourself on your ass, stun locked, or otherwise whiff your attacks. Iceborne is more catered to the Hardcore Monster Hunter audience. Don't ruin your Monster Hunter: World experience over the Icebourne DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA more fitting name would be "Stun&Status Effects Simulator": the developers tried to artificially increase the difficulty of Iceborne by adding random elements and status effects to every monster while keeping the slow and stiff gameplay of World. A poor design choice that makes the monsters extremely frustrating when playing solo and barely acceptable when playing co-op, basically forcing you to either:
- have friends to play with
- summon random gigachads that will one shot everything and ruin your experience
- grind for hours for better gear (let's not forget that drops are random and based on luck, especially streamstones and decorations)
- cheat
The game does have many positive aspects like huge replayability, the design of the mosters is phenomenal and the progression is actually felt, but I just can't recommend it based on how frustrating a lot of the monsters have been for me.\t\t\t\t\t
