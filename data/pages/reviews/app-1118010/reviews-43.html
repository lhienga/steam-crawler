\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIceborne is definitely a good experience for me - it's a high-quality, massive expansion that changes World completely, adding whole new mechanics like the clutch claw and a new region. I now have more than 150 hours on World, and 100 of those were spent on Iceborne. You cannot understate the absolute size of Iceborne - although it's smaller to some extent, it's also got much more of a time investment needed due to the high amount of grinding, especially after the main story. Many other AAA studios would probably only release an expansion the size of Iceborne as a standalone spin-off game instead.

I will say that Iceborne varies in quality much more than base World does - I had much more fun in hunts in the base game, really, and Iceborne has a few extremely tedious hunts (I'm referring to Shrieking Legiana here - I think it is one of the worst monsters in the entire game due to its mechanics and just annoyment factor) that really bring it down. A lot of the monsters in Iceborne, too, are just reskinned variants of the monsters in base World that do different types of damage. Although some of them do change up the gameplay significantly, some of them just feel like the same monster with a different coat of paint splashed on it. There are some great new additions, though, like the pseudo-wyverns and especially the early monsters. Velkhana, the elder dragon at the focus of the main story, is also a major step up from Zorah Magdaros.

Iceborne also adds two new maps and a few very small other maps that act as boss arenas. These two big maps are Hoarfrost Reach (the snow area) and the Guiding Lands. Hoarfrost Reach is a cool area, but all the snow and ice effects tend to tank my framerate. The other map, the Guiding Lands, is kind of cool, but it's just a remix of all the existing zones mashed into one zone. This is part of why I think Iceborne integrates itself really well into World but also to its detriment - I would have liked to see another new zone with completely new assets, but Iceborne sends you into all the parts of World's base game. The other new map introduced is not really a completely new map but a new hub zone: Seliana. I think Seliana is absolutely wonderful. You didn't have to sit through loading screens or run a long way up stairs to get to places - it's all very compact. And everything's there, too, as the compact hub zone of the Third Fleet Base was missing a lot of services. You even have a new room (which looks amazing) you don't need to enter if you want to use the Tailraider Safari. The Gathering Hub, too, is beautiful, and it connects directly to the Smithy, giving some much needed utility.

I definitely think base World has much higher quality and quantity - more monsters, more maps, more everything, but I don't think that justifies base World being less expensive than Iceborne. At this point, Iceborne is pretty much 50% of the game and more because of the endgame. But it's much more grindy, and I think that's kind of a problem. The monsters are also much, much harder - it feels like the developers just slapped on ludicrously high HP and damage to some of the monsters, and that's not really much fun. There's also generally much more grinding for gear, at least in the endgame - it really wasn't that bad in the main story, but the endgame grind is real, even though I haven't been in the endgame for that long.

I will say that in terms of the main story, don't go in expecting that much. I mean, Monster Hunter isn't really known for good story, and that's fine, and I appreciate that the developer team seems to have actively tried to make the narrative better. The main story has some great hunts, especially towards the beginning, but some hunts in the middle are either really tedious or a huge step up from the early monsters. I will admit that I was pretty disappointed that High Rank gear didn't work for Iceborne, since I farmed the hell out of Xeno'jiiva and other elder dragons in High Rank.

The additions to basic mechanics that Iceborne adds also does a great job at making the game more interesting. The clutch claw in particular makes fights much more dynamic and fun, while also giving an actual use to slinger ammo regardless of type. It feels kind of weird to see something like Redpit or Stone actually have use, and not just slinger ammo like Slinger Bombs. Some new stuff too includes some new combos, a bunch of new mechanics when it comes to gear, and some things ike the Steamworks that have a lot of utility to them. Overall, it's just very nice stuff that improves the quality of life in the game.

On a technical level, Iceborne is horrendous. I experienced tons of issues with the game while playing, with high CPU and GPU loads, which was worse because it really amplified the bad experience of some of the worst fights. The game is just not well-optimized, especially with the ice and snow effects.

I'd say that Iceborne, while not deserving of a full game due to its integration into World, is a pretty massive and fun expansion for the most part. It doesn't hit the same quality of World and has a lot of rough edges, especially with the changes to monsters as well as grinding, but it's still decent if you like World and want to have some more.\t\t\t\t\t
