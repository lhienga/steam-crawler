\r\n\t\t\t\t\t!!!NOT RECOMMENDED - NOT RECOMMENDED - NOT RECOMMENDED - NOT RECOMMENDED!!!

Terrible game. Combat is the most convoluted mess I've encountered in years. Your party consistently gets disabled in the first round, always by gas cloud and then you sit there and spectate as the AI plays against itself. By the time the effects wear off (your party members will NEVER make the saving throw), the battle is nearly over and you might get in a couple of token hits. Then there's the issue of the gimped attack rolls. I don't know if it's a bug or deliberately gimped, but the attack rolls for my entire party were so bad for so long that I started keeping track. Attacks rolls higher than 5 (out of 20) happened 48% of the time (with no adjustments, it should be roughly 75%). Attack rolls higher than 10 were a dismal 32% of the time (should be roughly 50%). The kicker is comparing natural 1s to natural 20s, where a natural 1 occurred 4.1 times as often as a natural 20 (should be roughly even). Clearly something is not right, and I suspect it's deliberate. Be prepared to die often. And I do mean often. If you like mostly spectating and being highly frustrated in the few instances you actually get to fight, then this game is for you. Basic spells, ones that are typically standard for casters, like magic missile, for some reason don't work about half the time. You click on it and it just says UNAVAILABLE with no explanation. This occurs regardless of whether or not you have spells cast on you, whether it's the beginning of the round, the end, or anywhere in between. It just randomly won't work, and it's always with offensive spells. It's not a matter of choosing the correct target - you can't even select the spell. Yes, the spell has been memorized and the UI shows it is available, but if you try to use it, it's a flip of the coin as to whether or not the game will let you. Be prepared to die often. And I do mean often. Oh, and all this is with playing a vanilla install - no mods whatsoever.

I'm not even going to get into the poorly written story, obtuse quests, and absolutely horrible voice acting. The game mechanics alone are bad enough it put this game into the "hard no" category. This game is an epic fail on all fronts except for the graphics (big shock there - I mean, surely we've never encountered a game with stunning graphics that sucked in every other way possible - this is one of those games). If the publisher had an ounce of integrity, they'd refund everyone that spend $$$ on this pos game. I highly recommend playing Baldur's Gate 3 instead. It has nearly identical gameplay except they did everything right and BG3 also has better graphics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's 50/50 - everything I liked about KM is twice as good in WotR, but the same applies to what I didn't like. There are moments you just can't stop playing, and there are moments you'd rather bang your head against the wall.

Pros :
The main story and narrative is great. So are companions (story-wise, definitely NOT building-wise) and most of the quests. Kingdom management is easier and not so time restrictive. "HoMaM battles" ... sure, why not. Beautiful music, graphics, massive replayability potential due to enormous class/skill/mythic path choices and of course, endings.
Everything a great CRPG needs is here.

Cons :
If you think KM had a lot of difficulty spikes and "gotcha" boss fight moments, you're in for a treat. Owlcat are downright sadistic in this regard - and the sad part is, it's often for absolutely no reason. You need to be constantly buffed otherwise any encounter could be fatal. And it's not even about the difficulty itself, but the execution - ever present ability debuffs, level drains, exhaustions and crippling effects (which are easily treatable later in the game with mass heals and GRs, but are a REAL pain during early phases), overbloated stats on common mobs and sometimes ridiculously overblown on bosses are the spikes many people spoke against in KM. Only to have Owlcat implement it even more in the next game. It's fairly manageable with custom party, but the game that so vehemently demands min/maxing and proper powerbuilding gives you the most useless companions to work with (with maybe two exceptions). And finally the honorable mentions - Act IV (Abyss) - I almost quit there. No spoilers - the idea around the place might be great, the execution is in my opinion just catastrophic. Second - The Enigma. If you ever get there, you'll know.

TL;DR
It's beautiful, long, complex and detailed, albeit flawed, piece of art I will surely play again... one day.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoving this game. Super nerdy TTRPG style details make this a deep game though this can be somewhat daunting at times.

Love the character creation and leveling up process plus the myriad classes and sub-classes. You can pretty much create any kind of class you can think of. The magic system is really cool also. Take your time choosing spells. It's not just a matter of choosing combat spells like fireball and all that. There are many other approaches to consider.

Occasionally the game can be very frustrating - for example when some mini boss wipes the floor with your party time after time, leaving you scratching your head and possibly quite annoyed. This pretty much means you have to rethink your approach. Ultimately this is a good thing as it stops gameplay from getting stale.

A final closing point, this is not a game to dip in and out of. It's a huge game with loads of detail and several sub-games. To get the most enjoyment out of it you need to be patient and diligent and take the time to understand the many, varied facets of the game. For someone like me, who struggles with concentration and patience, this can make it hard going at times.

Overall though I love this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have enjoyed this game both story and gameplay as well as amount of contents including with the price point.
Remind me of older games like Neverwinter nights and Dragon Age. Of course, people will not compare with Baldur Gate since now BG3 is huge by the time, this review is written.

I will give insight from non-D&D perspective. The story is straight forward, yet there are multiple layers and of course different ending. Your role as a classic RPG troupe as an Nobody to Commander of Crusader army. You will be fighting a lot and a lot of Demons but occasionally human and other races as well.

After 1-2 act, you will start overseeing a base and start to conquer to expand your territory. Although it sounds tiring, actually its not that bad and you can ignore completely to focus on party adventuring.

RPG games will not be completed without companions and this game gives you access to plenty. Sometime, I have to choose between my favourite companions to Story/Quest specific in order to proceed.

Overall, I love this game. One play through is definitely but if you enjoy a good story and not so punishing gameplay (unless of course you play in higher difficulty like Unfair), you will love you game.\t\t\t\t\t
