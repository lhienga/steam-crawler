\r\n\t\t\t\t\tso this game is a real mixed bag. story is good, music great, very high customization.

the problem here is the difficulty. if you like elden ring or love dark souls then please ignore anything further and buy the game you will love it. however if you are a long time pathfinder player or feel that normal difficulty should stand for normal... well keep reading.

so lets talk to those who are not pathfinder players. normal is not normal, you will need to play around with the difficulty slide bars. or look up guides to fights, or die enough to learn how that battle puzzle works to then deploy the proper strategy. this of course assumes you do not wish to just flick on story mode and feel like the battles have no challenge for you just to see the story. Lastly you will need to pre buff drastically before a good amount of the fights or else pure and simple. Owl Cat did give you options to deal with the issues but i feel this was not the right way to do this and for me feels more like a broke game catering to only those who like the difficulty of souls like games. in short they said here's normal and this is how you make it easier for your selves instead of saying here's normal and this is how you make it more challenging. which to me is the larger problem.


now to my fellow pathfinder players. you do have an advantage of knowing how classes mix together. HOWEVER if you are like me and was not a min maxing murder hobo who played things other than how to be the games best dps cannon your going to have a problem. the game is full of half builds. it has one or two in the chain of what you would want to see for a non dps build but then leaves out a few very crucial or mandatory feats to really bring the builds together. next lets talk mobs. YOU CANNOT USE YOUR NORMAL KNOWLEDGE OF PATHFINDER. well you can use some but be aware that owlcat decided to hyper buff a good chunk of the mobs. and they did it in ways that are not balanced for parties of it's proper CR level. things like just handing out +10 natural ac bonus just because. literally Owlcat would be the very definition of abusive DM on a pnp session of the game. like this guy would not have anyone willing to be a player at his or hers table. so do NOT go into this thinking oh i get to play pathfinder like i used to. not saying that this is good or bad for a video game but from the technical stand point of table top its just bad DMing. so do not go into the game thinking your going to get a true pathfinder experience because you will be sorely mistaken.

i will end by saying look i know the game has custom tools to adjust the difficulty. and that their are lower difficulty options. but if your games "normal" wants you to save scum and almost feels like you have to know a lot of fights even before you engage in them before hand or get your rear handed to you in a bag. To then be told to just go and debuff the mobs in the sliders difficulty menu just to play on "normal" then maybe juuuust MAYBE YOUR GAME HAS SOME DIFFICULTY ISSUES TO WORK OUT! unless of course you were indeed going for a souls like game then fine but perhaps you could have oh i dont know actually told the consumer base thats what you did? i mean you'd get a lot more people buying the game who actually WANT that kind of a game!\t\t\t\t\t
