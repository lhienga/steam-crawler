\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"I really liked it." - 3.5 rating, with a round up to a 4. (1-5 scale)

There are certainly highs and lows to this one, but all in all this is a fantastic CRPG that is not for the feint of heart. This is a pretty intense experience when it comes to having to learn the combat system/mechanics, being able to stick it out through a very long game, and also dealing with the Crusader Management mini-game that you will either love or hate.

Acts 1 and 2 had me really questioning this game and I was definitely in a state of "mixed feelings" to say the least. Act 3 however, fixed all of that and was absolutely amazing. I could have ended my play through there and had been greatly satisfied.

Act 4 is a tough pill to swallow at first and if you can accept what has been given to you and go for it, it turns out to be quite rewarding and comes together as you move on to the final Act 5.

Unfortunately by the time you get here you are ready to just end the damn game, as it is an extremely long play through. You are hard walled here with forced Crusader Management in order to proceed, which I wholeheartedly expect a lot of people are not very thrilled with.

Alas, if you finally back it through all of these difficult checkpoints, you earn you reward of 'The End' after all of that.

I picked Angel as my Mythic Path as I was told it was the best. It was an okay Lawful Good experience, but I feel like I would have had more fun and the story would have fit better if I went Demon instead. I will just end my Review at that.

Awesome job Owlcat.

I am super tempted to do a 2nd play through on an Evil run... we shall see...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish this game was better. Unfortunately the implementation of the rule set is really unpleasant and requires "buff-O-rama" prior to most of the fights in the game; otherwise none of your characters will land any abilities. Spell DC is also really hard to raise making much of the spell casting a game of watching fortitude saves etc. Being a buff tender for Prayer, Haste, Bless and Magic weapon is BORING.

Crossing the map is a SLOG. Not only are there repetitive random encounters, but if you travel too far or for too long your party turns into wet noodles. Make an unlucky roll on a camp site and corruption nukes your caster's abilities to cast spells. Punishing exploration a stupid design decision.

All this could be forgiven if the writing was better and the party members were interesting in the slightest. They're not.

The amount of classes is astonishing. Too bad 70% are incredibly hard to make viable.

"Evil" and "good" choices are Saturday morning cartoon level of depth.

This is by far the most half baked CRPG i've ever played. Every moment had me pining to return to Tyranny, Pillars of Eternity 1 & 2, Divinity 1 +2, Baldur's Gate 1 2 & 3, Neverwinter Nights 1 &2... OR even Under Rail.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed my terrible time with this game. It's a big clod hopping fantasy rpg with some domain management where you can spend an entire session looking at your character builds and managing your inventory. As a member of the 0.4 percent that beat the game on core with the settings for "sadistic gamer" I would say that any normal person should look up what custom settings make the game close to the pnp version. If I ever replay it, that's certainly what I'm going to do. Otherwise, you'll run into some hugely gobsmaking AC inflation. Which requires you to stack a bunch of buffs that take a long time to cast over and over. Getting a mod that that lets you load up a spell list for buffs is also something you should do if you value your time and sanity.

Overall a good game, and depending on how Owlcat moves forward it may the last computer game to experience the full decadence of Dnd 3.5 style dumpster diving character builds.\t\t\t\t\t
