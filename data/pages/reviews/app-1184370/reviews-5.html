\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI *almost* really liked this game, but it's not going to appeal to everyone. The gameplay is overly complicated for the average gamer...you get six people in your party, each with like 20 different skills, and 100+ usable inventory items. I'm usually someone who likes to crank the difficulty but OMG I'd be playing this game til I die if I actually took the mechanics seriously. As is, partway through I just put it on easiest difficulty because I got sick of how painfully long battles were. Even then the game took forever to beat.

I find this disappointing because it strikes me as a game that would otherwise have amazing replay value, with plenty of interesting story choices and real consequences, but I'm just not willing to dedicate that kind of time to it yet again.

I imagine this would appeal to some very hardcore gamers or people already used to Pathfinder mechanics, but it feels like it'd be wasted on a huge chunk of potential fans.

**WOKE-OMETOR**
Gayness: 10/10 (multiple big name gay/bi characters, including romanceable options)

Trans presence: 2/10 (if I remember right there is a non-binary god mentioned or something like that, but I didn't notice much else)

Feminism: 8/10 (majority of the strongest, most capable characters are women, though feminism isn't an outright theme)

Racial awareness: ???/10
I'm not sure if this would qualify as racist or woke by today's standards, but I found the Aasimar and Tiefling drama interesting, two races that are respectively angel and demon spawn. In this case stereotypes are kinda well earned but there's always outliers...there's an interesting moral question of what to do in this situation\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn excellent RPG with some light Heroes of Might and Magic elements in terms of the army mechanics which was unexpected as a newcomer to the Pathfinder series.

As a player relatively new to D&D rulesets I came into Pathfinder WotR off the back of Baldurs Gate 3. For any others just note that this game is substantially harder and will require use of various CC and debuff abilities as the game progresses into Act 3 and beyond in order to deal with high AC enemies.

Overall I would recommend the game to any RPG fan who enjoys a bit of build crafting and doesn't mind reading a decent amount of text to keep up with the plot lines (only some key conversations are voiced).

TL;DR - Fun RPG, quite text heavy, harder than BG3\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe content of the game is great. It does a good job of making you feel like a legend. However, I think this game suffers from a core system which is too convoluted to be fun. It's real time, but is also turn based. Players are strong but bosses are just unfairly powerful. Thankfully, one's experience can be tweaked through the custom difficulty settings.

The difficulty is hard do describe since I feel any level of difficulty is just not enjoyable. One stage too high and suddenly you cannot hurt anything, too low and you are untouchable. Combat feels like it should be RTS, but it more or less devolves to an uncontrolled brawl and you have a better time with just everyone using basic attacks rather than micromanaging everything like your HR at the office checking your clicks per minute. Bosses suffer as a result, with boss fights more everyone tickling a boss to death but just reskinned.

Ultimately, I just gave up and dropped the difficulty all the way down so I can just focus on roleplaying as opposed to wasting too much time figuring out builds and compositions. I feel like this game cannot be enjoyed if you take any aspect of it half-heartedly. This may not be an issue with the skill of the development team or to say this is a bad game. I just feel that it's much better enjoyed as a roleplaying adventure where the combat is just eye candy seeing people explode and all the flashy effects, as opposed to any strategic depth. I think this is entirely due to the ttrpg system it is trying to incorporate.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe TLDR is that the source material is good enough to make up for the adaptation's many problems. Playing this game gave me a very positive appreciation for Pathfinder, and an extremely negative view of Owlcat Games.

This is not an easy game to rate. It has a lot going for it, but it also has some very serious flaws. For some people (like myself), adaptations like this game might be the best way for them to experience the Pathfinder system and one of the most popular Pathfinder campaigns, and I personally am glad I was able to experience both the world and the game mechanics.
Adapting tabletop game is not an easy task, and the developers clearly had to make some compromises and changes. However, some of the decisions they made are questionable at best. Players familiar with Pathfinder may be disappointed by some of the rule changes and cut content.
On the other hand, new players will likely find the game daunting to get into. While the basic rules are explained, many of the more technical aspects of the game are not explained at all--in fact, some of the wording for a few spells and feats actually contradicts the way they actually function in the game. New players can play on lower difficulties, use premade builds, or even have the AI play for them, but if they actually want to learn the game, both the information available in game and the UI often impede the player rather than help.
All that being said, however, the options for building a character are still far more interesting and diverse than any similar game I've played. If you do manage to learn how things work, the game offers near infinite content if you just want to play around with different builds and playstyles.
My biggest grievance with the game is how horrendously buggy it is. This is a naturally buggy game genre, but even so, I've never seen bugs like this. They are especially pervasive in the late game, and can cause some pretty major issues with every aspect of gameplay. Combat is so buggy I've even had to alter the way I build my characters to accommodate some of the more common bugs, and based on my experience owning this game for over a year, I don't expect the developers to fix them anytime soon. There's even a recently added dungeon that is completely inaccessible--upon entering, the game tries to play a scene but just gets stuck forever--and the devs have had months to fix this.

Anyways, I don't want this review to turn into a rant. It's a good game, but just be aware that it definitely isn't for everyone, and know what you're getting into before you buy it.\t\t\t\t\t
