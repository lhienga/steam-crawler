\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredible game with a rich story, really meaningful choices with impact and so much character design choice. It's the sign of a sold game where you're half way through it and you're already thinking about what you'll do the next time you play it. I wanted to play a necromancer build and boy was I happy with how the game was able to facilitate that choice. With mounted play also available, which is a nice improvement from the previous pathfinder, I think there's plenty of choice to enjoy.

I have to admit, I found the ending to be a little lacklustre in terms of the story - it was a little anticlimactic as a final fight but maybe I'd just spent too much time getting there, so was a little overpowered...? That's my one issue though, everything else leading up to it was great.

The army management game seemed to be divisive as it was, reportedly, not as in depth as the reference it was based on but as someone who has never played the OG game in question, I thought it was an interesting addition to the game which served to break up other forms of combat well and added nice flavour and gravity to my role as commander.

All in all, I loved this game and I wouldn't be surprised if I end up passing the 1,000 hour mark by the time I'm done, especially once I try all of the expansion DLC.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has lots of content, allows you tons of customization and is definetaly worth your money, especially on sale. However, make no mistake, this game is some serious CBT.

Personally I despise this games idea of difficulty. Enemies die in one or two attacks but are simply next to impossible to hit. Aka every enemy has ridiculously high AC and patheticly low HP. This means the game is heavily reliant on RNG which is my least favourite type of difficulty.
There are ways to counteract this ofcourse, it just means making a spreadsheet on everything you have to do to minmax your characters and then spending atleast 5+ minutes buffing your entire squad before EVERY SINGLE COMBAT ENCOUNTER.

Lower the difficulty: instant hits, one shot enemies. Higher the difficulty: spend the better part of a lifetime buffing your squad if you ever want to hit anything.
This cant even be fixed in custom difficulty since enemy HP and AC is tied together, and I'm not downloading a mod to make the enemies fun to fight. Maybe I would if it had Steam workshop support, but I'm not going to nexus mods.\t\t\t\t\t
