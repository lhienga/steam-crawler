\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going out of my way to like this game, because ♥♥♥♥ WotC for real, man, but this is just a poorly done game all around. I'm trying to play it to learn Pathfinder as opposed to D&D but, I just can't do it. It reminds me of the old TSR games, to be honest, the kind I'd run back in Windows 95.

No matter what armor I put on my main char he has clothes on based on his class and ranger just looks like Grizzly Adams with Ronald McDonald shoes on. Why can't I change his clothes, man?

Why do party members not follow the main char? Why is there not a follow button?

Why doesn't combat go into turn based mode? It's neat that combat can happen in real time mode if you want, but why not go into turn based instead of pausing the game? Wouldn't turn based have the same effect as pause but eliminate my need for interaction?

The voice acting is meh, but the subtitles mixed with non-spoken narration during the voice acting is off-putting. Use an actual narrator.

The animation pacing is weird, the character doesn't seem to walk at a natural pace, the menus feel laggy, the game just seems to react a microsecond too late to anything, the walking doesn't seem to quite line up with the ground movement. Everything about the animations feels slightly uncomfortable.

How do you manage to get graphical clipping and glitching in a top down isometric game? They fixed that in the 90s.

You start out dead and saved by another character, find yourself under the watchful gaze of a dragon in the first 10 seconds of exposition, and then the big-bad promptly kills that dragon just to prove how insurmountable the odds are. There is no escalation of plot here. The story could be summed up as "Once upon a time the world ended and you tried to survive it."

I knew it was never going to be a BG3, but I did want a passable Pathfinder game to at least make a conscious effort to play something other that D&D, but this game is just souless.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLook, I'll be 100% honest with everyone here:

The combat in this game is just so tedious. And what I mean by that is that there's 2 different styles of play. You can play in Automatic Mode or Turn Based mode. At first glance, it is obvious this game was made for automatic mode, and turn based mode was a later development. And that wouldn't be an issue, if automatic mode wasn't so inefficient and bad, that in the later portions of this game, you pretty much need to swap to Turn Based mode. Your characters pathing can result in them pacing back and forth, taking MULTIPLE attacks of opportunities, and you can only assign ONE skill to be automated (which is mainly so that your casters don't run into melee range). Turn Based mode is alright... Most of the fights (because of pathfinder calculation logic) feel very luck based, which isn't necessarily the game's fault.

The character customization in the game is great. You have access to a lot of features, and it all feels really expansive and clean. But the combat is just so taxing, and combat is a huge part of this game.

Overall, I would pass on this game unless you're really struggling to fill that RPG mood. Go check out Divinity: Original Sin 2 or Pillars of Eternity 2 (I haven't played PoE2, but I hear it's wonderful).\t\t\t\t\t

But in reality you're screwed even if you're an adult with a full time job and other interests and hobbies outside computer games. I like complicated games with serious strategic elements, but this one requires way, way, way too much from the player.

2. Pathfinder: wotr was designed with rtwp combat and that's the only way it must be played. Turn based is a trap here. I chose to play it with tbc and what I got was unexciting, easy combats which also came in great amounts because....they are supposed to last a few seconds and not a few minutes. So apparent did it become that the game would take me 200+ hours to beat if I kept playing it in turn-based, and so boring all those trivial combats were, that I switched into rtwp a couple of hours before I quit

So i quit. The core of the game, that is the characters, the story and the world looked amazing for as long as I played it, but it ended up being unplayable for the reasons I mentioned. An extremely hardcore game to be played strictly with rtwp.\t\t\t\t\t
