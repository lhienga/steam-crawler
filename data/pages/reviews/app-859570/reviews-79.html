\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSecret Neighbor is basically a mix of Among us, Dead by Daylight, and Granny, so if you like any of those games, you'll probably have a good time with this one, I'll give a rundown of the core gameplay as well as what the steam version has to offer. You and five other players load into a map and your goal is to find keys scattered around a large home that will unlock the basement/exit and escape. One of the six players is secretly tasked with preventing the other five from escaping. As of writing this review, there are six kid classes/survivor classes, and five neighbor classes/killer classes each with their own unique playstyles and abilities. There's also some additional PVP gamemodes, but they're pretty basic, so I wont go into much depth. The steam version of the game has a level editor which, as of now, is exclusive to steam. The steam version also DOES NOT have crossplay, so keep that in mind.

The game is a lot of fun, but if you plan on playing the game with friends, I'd definitely recommend waiting for a steam sale, where the game usually goes on sale for 5$.\t\t\t\t\t
