\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I absolutely love the Hello Neighbor franchise, and this game is exactly what it advertises itself as, Hello Neighbor multiplayer! This game is also good outside of the context of Hello Neighbor as it is one of the few
asymmetrical multiplayer horror games that isn't just Dead by Daylight with a twist. The game has six players, five of which are kids and one of which is the Neighbor. The kids have to unlock the basement door and get in to win (much like the objective of the original Hello Neighbor) and the Neighbor has to catch all of the kids before they can reach his basement.

The Neighbor doesn't just chase the kids around, he is instead disguised as one of the kids, the kids have to figure out which one of them is a traitor and avoid them as trusting the wrong person in this game can get you cornered real quick. The kids have to look around the map and find keycards to unlock new rooms and sections of the map so that they can find the keys they need to unlock the basement door. There are three different colors of key that correspond to the three different colors of locks on the door. A red key can only be used on a red lock, a blue key can only be used on a blue lock and a yellow key can only be used on a yellow lock. The combination of lock colors will change between games. There will be some keys that won't be useful sometimes as all of the locks of that color have already been unlocked. In a standard game there are six locks on the Neighbors door.

There are also different classes. There are six different kid classes and five different Neighbor classes. They all have different abilities and their own gimmicks which means that each class has to play the game quite different from the others outside of the core concepts of each role that are carried between all classes for that role.

The best part is that despite this game being in a (for the most part) family friendly franchise, this game can be truly scary. I think the scariest Neighbor is by far the Guest as he can charge up huge leaps that launch himself towards you across the cramped spaces of the Neighbors home at scary speeds which makes him absolutely terrifying to encounter in the cramped hallways that some maps have. The game isn't insanely terrifying as it carries over the cartoon like art-style that every Hello Neighbor game has and it doesn't have super terrifying lighting, but it will still scare you! Sometimes you never know when that friend you trusted will walk up behind you and grab you by the neck.

There is also some story to help place this in the Hello Neighbor universe but it is really just a little tiny bit of story. Pretty much every event, cutscene and trailer for this game is non canon. The only thing that's canon is that this game takes place between Act 1 and 2 of the original game and follow Nicky's friends as they attempt to rescue him from the Neighbor's basement.

Overall this game is great for longtime fans of the series and newcomers as it is really fun and has it's own unique style of gameplay that differs from your traditional multiplayer horror game.\t\t\t\t\t
