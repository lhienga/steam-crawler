\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGraphics and Art Style:
The game looks pretty good, with its cartoonish style and colorful environments. The characters are cute, and the world is well-designed, which adds to the fun.

Gameplay:
The gameplay revolves around the multiplayer mode, where you either play as the kids or the neighbor. The kids need to work together to find keys and save their friend, while the neighbor tries to stop them by pretending to be one of them. It's a cool concept, but it can get repetitive, and the balance between the two sides feels a bit off at times.

**Controls:**
The controls are okay, but they can be a bit clunky, especially when you're trying to do something quickly. It's not a dealbreaker, but it can be frustrating.

**Sound Design:**
The sound design is pretty good, with eerie music and sounds that add to the creepy atmosphere. However, some of the sounds can get annoying after a while.

Replayability:
The game has some replay value, especially if you're playing with friends. But it can get old fast, as the gameplay doesn't offer a lot of variety.

Community and Support:
The community is active, and the developers are regularly updating the game. However, there are some bugs and glitches that can be annoying.

Conclusion:
Secret Neighbor is a fun game, especially if you're playing with friends. The graphics are nice, and the concept is cool. However, it does have its flaws, including repetitive gameplay and clunky controls. If you're a fan of the Hello Neighbor series or enjoy multiplayer games, it's worth checking out. Just don't expect it to keep you entertained for too long.

Score: 6/10\t\t\t\t\t
