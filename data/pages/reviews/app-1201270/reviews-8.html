\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a wonderful game, I’ve never been more proud of a masterpiece created by a developer from my very own home country of Indonesia🥹 From the amazing storytelling elements and fun puzzles, down to the beautiful pixel art and sound design, everything was spectacular! They even had nods to other well-known video game titles as well as cultural Indonesian references which I found so delightful.

If you’re a fan of stories that provide a positive message but still remain grounded in reality in terms of how harsh life can be, as well as having pretty fleshed out 3-dimensional characters whose good and bad traits you can relate to, this is definitely the game for you! I was on the verge of tears by the end of it by how it tells such a beautiful story that I can very much relate to, and the imaginative and gorgeous pixel art added even more depth to that.

Gameplay was also great with how it mostly plays like a point-and-click adventure game with your character roaming around a town running errands and solving puzzles with items you’ve picked up along the way, and a very easy to understand map layout and menu interface. The puzzles aren’t too tough to figure out, but still provided enough of a challenge for me to satisfy my point-and-click adventure game cravings (the last I played one was Return to Monkey Island back in 2022🥲).

The side-quests were also really enjoyable and included solving puzzles and collecting items with fun li’l descriptions and upon completing them, will provide you bonus scenes of the main characters which I think is a great reward that provides better context of the relationship between the main characters. However, be warned that you might miss a collectable/puzzle to completing these side-quests so I suggest saving on each chapter before it gets to a “point of no return”.

My only nitpick is probably just of how the background can sometimes become jittery and a blur during the running animation which caused me a bit of a headache. I’m not really sure if it had to do with my resolution and graphical settings especially since I’m not very familiar with these things, but I’d just like to give a heads-up to people that tend to get motion sickness.

All in all, I recommend this game with a massively huge thumbs up with both its story and gameplay and is 1 of the best games I have played from last year. Definitely a MUST-PLAY in my book for adventure game enthusiasts!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Space for the Unbound is an adventure game with an exceptionally strong narrative and pixel art developed by an Indonesian team. Experience 90's rural Indonesia, as both high school and the world ends while two individuals uncover mysterious powers among themselves and deal with themes such as abuse, bullying, loss, heartbreak, and anxiety about growing up and not knowing who you want to be.

Relationships! Trauma! Repairing and/or making peace with them and moving on! You play as Atma, a teenager that has the ability to dive into people and help them make peace with their troubles. Also your girlfriend seems to have her own thing going on; what's up with her? How is she? Why is she? How and Why are you? You remember what happened yesterday, right?

Backgrounds range from pretty to jawdroppingly immaculate. From beautiful vistas to dilapidated buildings, it's super apparent a lot of love and personality was put into the art direction in every single area. Loka City is dripping with a sense of homeyness about it. I've never been to Indonesia in my life, and yet the game managed to instill this sense of nostalgia in me for it.

The story in this game completely moved in a way that no other game ever has. It had me feeling emotions all over the place, had me shed a tear here and there, and maybe broke me into pieces and reassembled me a few times. I haven't experienced anything as impressive as this in a really, really long time.

I'm so unbelievably happy I was able to experience this story during my lifetime, and, if you play it, I hope you feel the same way. This is an incredible masterpiece of a coming-of-age story.

Please play this game. You won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really, really special. It gets a lot of recognition for it's '90's Indonesia setting, and that certainly adds to it's charm, but this is way more than just a game set in Indonesia. It tells a beautiful, meaningful story with perfect pacing and storytelling. The characters and the writing are really fantastic, and the music perfectly complements it. It plays like an old-school point-and-click adventure game, but it completely avoids the weird moon-logic puzzles that sometimes happen in that style of game. It has a deep narrative that will keep you thinking about it, but it avoids being the common problem of being so convoluted that it's hard to follow or makes no sense. It has a good number of side-quests and optional content, but it's set up in such a way that if you choose to, you can easily do it all without resorting to using a guide or spending hours completing collections. It's rich in symbolism and touches on many important topics including bullying, anxiety, depression, abuse, trauma, and loss. The game is very emotional and can make players cry at points, but I wouldn't call it a sad game - it's uplifting and bittersweet. Combine all that with it's simple pleasures: a cute art style, catchy music, unique cultural references, petting and naming all the stray cats in town - it's just an all around wonderful and special game.\t\t\t\t\t

Slowly, the world starts to fall apart as Atma searches through the town for his girlfriend, Raya, who has the power to manipulate reality. A narrative puzzle adventure where you help heal the emotional pains of the townsfolk to progress the story.
Set in late '90s Indonesia in a pixel art style, there are so many nostalgia-provoking references that call back to a simpler time. Armed with his determination to solve the mystery (and his magical book that allows him to 'space dive' into the minds of others), Atma must save his girlfriend and save the world.
