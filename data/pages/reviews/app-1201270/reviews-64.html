\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Space for the Unbound caught me by complete surprise. Especially because I didn't think it deserved the hype even half way through the game. I was wrong, and I highly recommend this experience. Even the price of 20 Dollars is worth it.

Reviews for ASftU are Overwhelmingly Positive on Steam, which - along with its promise of a good story -prompted me to purchase this game. I had never bought anything by Mojiken before and from the looks of the trailer and screenshots, it seemed to pay homage to classic arcade style games and point and click gameplay, which it does.

Now, being perfectly clear. I don't think Space Unbound is a great game, or at least, does not have great gameplay. It is a good game, but not great. The puzzles are a bit too easy at times, the pacing is off at various points, and the back tracking didn't really settle me in the atmosphere like it seems to for others.

The options in the main menu seemed a bit limited to me as well. No chapter jumps after completion, no artwork unlocked for finding hidden achievements... Little things like this would improve the game a bit in my opinion.

Half way through I thought, "Yeah this is a good game. But a masterpiece? No, I don't see that."

...

I was wrong. I finished the second half.

Despite my nitpicking of gameplay and my doubts, I can confirm A Space for the Unbound tells a beautiful, masterful story. It is obvious the creators were careful in their research of depicting the emotions of the depressed, the anxious and anger, that comes from someone suffering. What's even more impressive is that anyone can relate, to some level, what these characters are going through, and how they feel.

I was very impressed.

And that ending, that incredibly moving, gosh darn ending had me re-watching on youtube many times.

Well worth everything, the hours and money spent.

Is A Space for the Unbound a great game? No. But I don't believe it could be told correctly in another form of media either.

Does A Space for the Unbound tell one of the most beautiful, down to earth stories about depression that I have ever played? Yes. And it is done masterfully.

I look forward to your next game Mojiken. We need more video games like these. Highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

When it comes to storytelling, A Space for the Unbound sets a new high bar for others to follow. It also holds back no emotional punches as you come to empathise with other people in surprising ways (not spoiling any of that here!)

Amidst all the other positive reviews, the only other unique thing I can probably contribute is the Southeast Asian flavour of the game, it's really true to the culture and themes, and made me feel nostalgic for the simpler past too, but it also has the childishness wonder and imagination thrown in.
