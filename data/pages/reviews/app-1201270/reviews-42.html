\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Space for the Unbound is a very, very beautiful game.

The pixel art is outstandingly gorgeous, and manages to shine both in countryside landscapes and more surreal environments. The colors are both vibrant and nostalgic, and I particularly liked that the shading was done with pink instead of black. The sound design is also really good, and the music excellent.

The story is good, yet not exceptional. It truly kicks in a quite late, and sometimes felt a little """"""easy"""""" if that makes sense, but what was there was done well -especially with the side-characters. The whole supernatural part is very good, if not very present in the early game. Special applause for the dev team who hired a psychology consultant. Real cool!

The gameplay however‚Ä¶
So much, way too much of this game is just running back and forth to trigger events so Atma can pick the damn things off the ground, or open a door, or a lock. Yes the world is beautiful, but I wish it was more linear or the map clearer because I got lost so often. The last three acts seldom have this problem, but the first three are a slog to go through

Overall, this game should accept to be slightly shorter. We would all be better for it.

A Space for the Unbound is, before all, unpolished. Still a bit rough, a bit jagged around the edges, but the jankiness almost makes it all the more endearing. It was made with love, for the characters and the obviously real people who inspired them, for the craft, and for Indonesia about which I learned a lot playing this game. If not a perfect time, it was a lovely one.\t\t\t\t\t
