\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are looking for a game with good storytelling and whimsical, bittersweet themes, A Space for the Unbound could be appropriate. Gameplay is not particularly original, and there are very few parts that could be qualified as "challenging": It's more of a mellow experience, and the main focus is the story. If you are looking for a more challenging point-and-click adventure, stuff like Machinarium or the Phoenix Wright series might be more up your alley.

Still, the storytelling is delicate and touching, and the aesthetics are definitely interesting: Probably designed by/for someone that was young in the nineties, sometimes it plays lightly on nostalgia. It is still somehow has a unique flavor, at least for people like me who are not familiar with Indonesian culture.

I enjoyed it enough to play it through a second time. A recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
