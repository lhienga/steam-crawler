\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMojiken's New Game: Hype High, Delivery...Not So Much

Okay, Mojiken fans, gather 'round. Let's talk about their latest release, shall we? I jumped on this bandwagon the week it dropped (i think), fueled by Mojiken magic from past games. I was hyped, picture me bouncing off the walls like a caffeinated chihuahua. The art? Gorgeous. The music? Banging. The whole vibe? Perfection personified. I was ready to devour this game whole.

And devour I did...the first few minutes, anyway. But then, I hit a snag. It wasn't the gameplay (though let's be honest, it wasn't exactly groundbreaking). It was the dialogue. Oh, the dialogue. Longwinded monologues that felt like reading Tolstoy after chugging five espressos. The structure? Wonky in both English and Bahasa, like a sentence got lost in Google Translate and returned wearing clown shoes.

Look, I tried. I switched languages like a frantic tourist, hoping one would click. I pushed through, fueled by my initial excitement and a desperate hope for redemption. But alas, the dialogue was a dragon I couldn't slay. It just kept droning on, sucking the joy out of the game like a Dementor with a thesaurus.

So, I tapped out right around the time Atma woke up in the school and started chatting with Raya. Dialogue coma, I tell you. Maybe someday, when the Baldur's Gate itch is scratched, the Valorant dailies are done, and Skyrim's frozen wastes have lost their allure, and the lure of taming cute yet deadly beasties in Palworld has faded, I'll give this Mojiken gem another go. Who knows, maybe with fresh eyes and a pot of strong tea, I'll unlock the secrets of its wordy world. Until then, it slumbers on my backlog shelf, a beautiful mystery waiting to be unraveled. And mark my words, if that day ever comes and the game redeems itself in my eyes, I promise I'll be back to rewrite this review with a chorus of praise. Until then, consider this a placeholder, a testament to my ever-growing backlog and the ever-present hope that hidden gems still await discovery.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Space for the Unbound is a beautiful story about loss; the way we cope with it, and self acceptance; the journey we take to feel at one with ourselves.
The game takes us through this journey, and the steps we encounter along the way.
The graphic style is beautifully rendered, really making a good effort of making you feel in Indonesia.
The characters are believable and make you feel part of the story.
Gameplay sets you on a 2D adventure, with puzzles, collectibles, and some fight encounters.
The music was beautiful, helping set the scene.
The puzzles were on the whole well realised, with the solutions in the environment to be found.
The fight scenes revolved around a sequence of QTEs. Although infrequent, I would say these were the weaker elements of the game.
Exploration takes you across the map, however there are occasions where you are restricted on where you can go, due to story beats. It can also take a bit of time to work out where each section leads, but gets easier over time.
Overall, a lovely story to experience.\t\t\t\t\t
