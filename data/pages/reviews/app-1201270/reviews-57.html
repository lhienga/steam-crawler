i-I'M NOT CRYING, YOU ARE!
｡ﾟ(*´□`)ﾟ｡ Trust me when I say some people might be brutally crying after playing this game.

GENERAL THOUGHTS & OPINIONS
GAMEPLAY Puzzle solving and exploration. The puzzles aren't too difficult but it does require a bit of back-and-forth sometimes. There's a fighting mechanic and I remember there's a mini-game you can play for fun in there. Bonus points, if you love cats, then you're in luck cause YOU GET TO PET AND NAME EVERY. SINGLE. CAT.

I advise you to take your time with this one. Explore every nook-and-cranny folks!
  • Can you 100% the achievements in one playthrough? Yes.

GRAPHICS/VISUALS Very cute pixel artstyle. Shouldn't strain your device at all.

AUDIO/MUSIC The music is also super well-done! Just listen to it for yourself when you play!

STORY Amazing story that will (probably) make you cry. I find it relatable and very touching. Please go into this one blind and enjoy the ride.

It does explore some heavy themes so this is your trigger warning.

PRICE On my end, it's a bit pricy but if you can get it on sale that's great! Please support the devs!

OTHER THOUGHTS & OPINIONS
  • As someone who is born and raised in Indonesia (the setting of the game), I find this game nostalgic even though I didn't live through the era the game is set in. Some of the leftovers of that time got to my childhood so that's a personal treat for me.
  • If you're not Indonesian (or you didn't grow up there), but you have a friend who is, please drag them along to play this game if you want to experience more depth & context to the references in the game.
  • Fun fact, there are cameos and references of the developer's other games in this game!
  • I got to meet the Creator/Director at a convention and got some merchandise signed. He's a chill and nice dood!

I don't know what else to say about this fantastic game but I need you to know, as an Indonesian, that you need to play it. 9/10 ⭐\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Space for The Unbound is an adventure, point-clicked puzzle with a great storyline. You play as Atma, a high school student who goes on a adventure with his girlfriend, Raya. Both tend to complete their wish list to achieve what they call their goals in life.

The game has great pixel art, colorful and cute. I think people will judge this game as a normal puzzle game but it's not. The puzzle on this game are quite complicated but fun. When you solve it, it's makes you feel like completing something big, that's what I say. The mechanic of the gameplay is great. Here you can dive into people mind and help them from inside their heart. There are lot things to do in this game, such as collecting bottle can, peting a cat and even more. Overall it's great experience.

Perhaps the downside of this game, for me, is how it doesn't tell you what to do sooner or later. Like there are some items that you can't get if you miss one item in a certain chapter. When you don't complete any collectibles, the game doesn't feel 100% complete. You can therefore go back and play it again, but it's a linear game, so playing it again or creating a new save file can be tedious and a waste of time. I know it's only for perfectionists but for me keeping every linear 100% complete is important.

The game is more than 10 hours of gameplay, it's quite short, but worth your money. Give it a try if you want to experience a great story, and for fellow Indonesians, you have to play this game. Trust me it's worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't review games often, but I found myself smitten with this game by hour 4.

I feel the need to write this now; If you have any interest in mystery slice of life games, play this game. I went into it expecting a fun little experience with charming characters.

What I got? A deep dive into the human psyche as Atma's world falls apart around him. A lot of the TWs were things I've dealt with personally, so I was intrigued by how they'd pop up. Every single one hit me hard, and it's a miracle I didn't start crying. I teared up several times near the end, but that's as far as I got.

Many of the plot points are very well hinted through out, too a point where I had inklings of what the truth might be the whole way, but the atmosphere and side characters distracted me enough that I was slow to pinpoint every single twist.

After both Coffee Talk games and A Space for the Unbound, Toge Productions is quickly becoming a favorite indie developer of mine. Looking forward to whatever comes next from them.\t\t\t\t\t
