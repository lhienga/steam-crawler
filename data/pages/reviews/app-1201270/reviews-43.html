\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYep, this one got me.

The gameplay is your standard "logic puzzle mind dive" affair, nothing terrible but somewhat bland. Objectives necessary to complete the bucket list are very easily missable... There was one objective that I missed by one (1) interaction and it was quite frustrating. The combat is super-bland but I can't see any other way they could have done it without ruining the game's vibe.

But that's where my complaints end. This game - no, this story - is an obvious labor of love of those who put it together, and while it is set in Indonesia, you can make the connection of being a high school student pressured by your family and peers regardless of where you are in the world. The expectations that society puts on children and teenagers are very real, even in the 2020s. The characters' struggles with their life choices and finding their identities are also very real.

Structurally, the narrative is "formulaic" but engaging throughout most of the game's run until the very end, when it grabs you by the arm and never lets you go. It's equal parts optimistic and sad, and if you have a shred of emotion in your psyche, you will absolutely feel it.

The art direction is fantastic as well, deliberately limiting color palettes and making spritework just crunchy enough. Again, a labor of love here.

I put A Space for the Unbound alongside other short, cozy story-based games I love like Gris, OneShot, and OPUS Echo of Starsong. Will I play it again myself? Probably not, at least for a while. But I am very glad I played it at all.\t\t\t\t\t
