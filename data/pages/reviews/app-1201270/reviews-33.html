\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a nice and well-written game whose premise has been seen before and you can make out in the prologue, but it's still handled very well. A typical adventure game fare of doing things in the right order with specific items, but the story and its pacing is the driving force to completion and the world(s) is(/are) charming that it's a nice escape into modern era Indonesia with very well done music. The art direction itself as well as the subtle detailing within the pixel art with animations and impacts add nice touches of life which can look very simplified even for pixel art at points but enough detail is there to place emphasis on the emotional points of the game that the game shines from.

It can take until the second or third chapter for things to start rolling, especially if you're aware of the type of 'game based on depression and anxiety in a cutesy aesthetic' genre of adventure games, but it's a charming and well-written indie title that's worth a playthrough or two.
At the point of whether you'd want to play it would come down to how well you like slice-of-life, supernatural vibe-y adventure games before the story can hook you in.\t\t\t\t\t
