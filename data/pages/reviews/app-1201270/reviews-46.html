\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis isint really a game it's more like a side scrolling-ish hyper story driven game with very light fighting mechanics. And honestly I am all about it. I can't really relate to the very dire issues that the characters faced, despite that I had a lot of empathy for the characters. I think Raya's back story is one that almost everyone faces. There was this meme going around in the early 2010s it was like "young people act like spongebob but end up growing up to be squidward" the whole premise behind Raya's character reminded me of that.

Honestly looking back on it, its a very depressing story despite it appearing very upbeat and high note for the majority of its run time. Even if it ends on a high note you cant help but question human nature for the lack of benevolence, community, empathy that was required to end up with the story we have here. Hell, id argue that maybe we should return to the rural village life, it seems so nice to live somewhere where everyone knows you and it didn't make me help but wonder if someone who went though Raya turmoil lived in a western society they'd probably be either beyond broken or dead.

Maybe Dr. Theodore Kaczynski was right

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow... as a person who had similar family dynamics to Raya, and had that one dear, kind, patient friend who changed the trajectory of my life... I'm blown away. This game hit me HARD towards the end when everything becomes clear. Gameplay, sure, it was fun to collect things, pet animals, figure out puzzles, get achievements. Yes, sometimes the fights do feel a little bit long, but not so much that it feels like I'm wasting my time, and besides, the game makes you forget about the gameplay, because the story is just that engaging. Was it confusing at the beginning? Maybe, but it's very obvious from the beginning that reality and your perception of it is... off, and that mystery is what drives the plot forward. It's weird that some reviewers state this as if it's a detriment to the game when it's literally just... the story/plot that was already established. Like most games of this genre, all becomes clear the more you progress.

If you enjoy adventure games and are seeking an emotional, heartfelt story with a little bit of a mystery, play this game. It deserves all the good reviews.\t\t\t\t\t
