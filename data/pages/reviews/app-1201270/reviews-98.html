\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played the game in Bahasa. A lot of references are geared more towards Indonesian audience, and I personally like it. I like the overall idea of the concept, the world, the characters, and I respect the fact that no one is a "true protagonist": every one is eerily human, with all their complex background and personality. The story also doesn't end/close in a typical trope, but rather, choose to have a very "mature" conclusion with the "villains".

Gameplay wise, this game is a fetch quest game through and through, with mini games in between. If you're looking for a story heavy game with complex, difficult gameplay (maybe like Undertale), this game is not it. But if you want to experience a more relaxed gameplay, with beautiful pixel arts, good musics, and follow a touching story of trying to understand people and their past, this game is definitely it.

Kudos for the creators, this is a very beautiful game\t\t\t\t\t

5/5 "Fun puzzles, mini game, and a real tearjerker story"

Pros
(+) A Space for the Unbound is a game with beautiful yet heartbreaking story. The game showed us a lot of emotion that all people have. The game thought us about fear, regret, loneliness, envy, jealousy, sadness, and emptiness, but despite all that, the game also thought us about love, bravery, overcoming, moving on, and most importantly, acceptance. At least that's what i learned after playing this game.

(+) I really love how the story turns out. The game itself is like roller coaster of emotions and twists.
I totally recommend this if you really like story rich game! I literally cried while playing the last two chapters. And i gotta say, this game will become memorable. Especially for Indonesian.

(+) I also like how it really captures "pixelated" Indonesia's town in that time (90s) and what culture does people have during that time for example collecting bottle caps, playing arcade games or trading for YOMAN collectibles.

(+) Characters in this game is also well written, all of them are loveable and a fantastic addition in the game especially in story.

(+) Mini games is really enjoyable

Cons
(-) However there is still minor cons that i would like to point out. For instance, i really wished that there is chapter select or free roam to collect missing collectibles. I'm aware that in every chapter the game gave us a chance to do so but, looking for collectibles using wikis or walkthrough video is kind of ruining the experience for me especially for the first run. This comes from completionist player like me so, it is just some minor cons that kind of annoy me as a completionist. Because, if you miss 1 collectibles then you have to start over to get extra cutscene.

(-) Don't get me wrong, the puzzle in this game is really fun and suits the theme based on a person we solve the puzzle to. However, it's a really fun and games until you meet chapter 4 puzzles. It's a damned algebra and to be honest i force myself to cheat by looking answer online because i can't stand math.
