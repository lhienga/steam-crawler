\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf a studio ghibli movie as a pixel game.
...
At least that is what it felt like to me.
------------------------------------------
Anyways onto an actual review for this game. The game itself was a great experiences that was quite stunning to behold. There were a few parts that I would consider to be rather tedious or simple for what they are, but that's likely just me preferring games that have a higher difficulty ceiling than this game and the core of this game is it's story anyways.

The Art
So the art of this game is your like many other indie games where it's some form of pixel art, but unlike some other pixel art styles the pixel art here is spectacular and extravagant in it's design. Almost everything has an attention to detail to it that makes them feel almost alive. There is also quite a few full screen stills and animations that are also pretty neat to see. I never really had any problems with most of the arts or sprites, but there is one thing that always did bother me the entire while playing the game and that was the sprite of the one of the protagonists, Atma.
Now the actual sprite work and animation for him is great, but he always looks mad with his eyebrows and my friend said he reminded them of a Muppet and now I can't unsee that.
But aside from that minor qualm everything was basically eye-candy.

The Music
I feel like there is less to say about the music, primarily because I don't fully resonate with music in general, but the music was quite atmospheric and tended to be pretty on brand for what was needed at that time. The music that stood out the most two me we the ending music in chapter 5 and the pianist song piece. This is mostly due to theses two song lining up with my personal presences tho.
Overall the music appealing to list too and played a great supporting role in the game.

The Game-play
The game-play here is kinda interesting primarily due to it's presentation in my opinion, but the game-play consists of fairly simple puzzles, things that you have to press at the right time, and timed sequences of moves. Overall there is a very low ceiling to it's difficulty and the most difficult any of theses get is when you're in the arcade and attempting to get a score over 1700. After that most of theses mini-games are fairly trivial.
I do understand where why this was done and I can respect it as they wanted to tell a story and having a high difficulty ceiling would go against that goal.

The Story
It can be a little predictable imo, but it's overall a great story and still left me in tears even-thought I already had guessed almost everything that had happened. There really isn't much else to say other than go watch someone else play it or play it yourself.

Overall
I don't really think I need to say much else especially since I kinda already said how I feel about this game, but it was a very moving experiencing and I emplore more people to play this game and try it out. This game is worth the money and if it's on sale then there is almost no reason to not get it imo.
Overall it gets a 93/100\t\t\t\t\t
