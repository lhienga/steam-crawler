Do all video games catch the strings of your pathetic soul like that? Or are video games just a way to pump out money? This is a unique game, an example of real game art, not ♥♥♥♥ from Ubisoft, EA and others. It makes me extremely sad that the culture that I have lived in for so many years, and that has literally created me myself, is now just a fast-growing business. Every time a game like this comes along, I start to believe again. Into yourself, into people, into culture as a whole, and that's wonderful. Art should evoke emotions, real emotions, should make you feel something... to feel the echo of your soul. A space for the Unbound is a game that evoked a huge range of emotions in me, from sadness, worry and mild depression to joy, faith and hope. A game that tells a story as if you're there, as if it's all with you. A game that shows a believable outcome and development of a person, under the pressure of society, family, his inner demons and the struggle with these same demons. This is the merit of many talented people, just like the author of this review, who love games and respect them as art. It pains me to admit that over time such games will clearly lose popularity and lose all status in the world, but as long as they exist, I believe that large companies will have their own little garage geeks who create great worlds and unforgettable stories, worlds that evoke sincere emotions. Thank you for this piece of art, I will never forget it. I wish peace to all, because we are citizens of the same world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Come for the unique 80s Indonesian setting, stay for the feels. And OMG the feels, like WTF am I crying... AGAIN?

Hopefully without spoiling the game, let me wholeheartedly recommend Space for the Unbound. This is first and foremost a very well written and poignant story. A story about growing up; there's school, and friendship, and trauma, and fear, and regret, and courage and... everything. Some parts WILL hit hard, especially if you're from SEA and grew up in the 80s. The prolonged narrative tension followed by the catharsis of the ending is *muak* (chef's kiss).

Gameplay wise its very chill, mostly RPG puzzles with very lenient timing/reflex based 'combat'. All of this frees up your attention to be immersed in the characters' stories and taking stock of the beautifully crafted environment. This is a 11/10 narrative experience not to be missed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBlew my expectations away

I gotta be honest. I played the demo of the upcoming “Until Then” by Polychroma and through my hype train I searched comments about it on the trailers and a lot of people are commenting how similar it is to a game I don’t know about, “A space for the unbound”. Weird title but I still gave it a shot.

Art style 🎨 : is simplistic at first glance but it really grows on you fast with its charm and dynamic animations and constant facial expressions

The writing ✍️ pace is simple & fast that you’re going through an episode fast with the vast characters that you can literally deep dive through their personalities to get to know why they are acting like this in the story which brings to...

The Gameplay🎮: you help everyone that needs it through a vivid psychoanalysis (in a fun way) & try to fix them from the inside for them to overcome their past traumas in order to move on & face their present predicaments which lastly brings us to the story.

The story📖: is a masterclass. Everything is normal at first until things get a bit wild. I don’t want to spoil things here as I want players to experience this masterpiece from start till finish as the pay off is worth it. It’s all about healing ❤️‍🩹. That’s all I can say. Not to mention…

The Music 🎶: I love a game that is recognizable by their songs. Almost every song in the soundtrack is very memorable that goes along the lines of the narrative and the scenarios you’re currently seeing that puts a flurry of emotions in every scene. You will feel Happy, Angry, Sad, Confused, Scared, & a whole lot more with the original composition they embedded on each scene with so much soul and expression that’ll make every moment memorable. Mix this with the fast pace and simple story and you got a formula for a timeless game.

Currently on my 2nd playthrough and I was blown away when I realized the intro foreshadows the final scenes that I have just scene recently 🤯.

Well done, Mojiken & Toge Productions👏. “It was perfect. PURRfect🐈 ! Everything down to the last minute details.” I hope Until Then will be as good as this game. Fingers crossed 🤞.\t\t\t\t\t
