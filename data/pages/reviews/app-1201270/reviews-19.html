\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was fooled by the overwhelmingly positive reviews. I should have read the few negative ones.
The art is serviceable and the sound is OK (I can't remember the music.)

How shall I explain the first 30 minutes.

It's an story with some interactive element in it.
I was so much crushed by the complete void of anything interesting to do that I was gasping for air, drowned in boredom.
The character moves incredibly slowly by default, you have to double tap a direction to make him move at an acceptable pace.
Most of what he can do is to "speak" to people, pet a cat and do "puzzles" a.k.a interacting with whatever is on screen. (Yes, that one will not tire your brain.)
After finishing the prologue, I'm pretty sure I got the gist of what it will be about : a tragedy. The hints aren't exactly subtle. I've played a bit further because, being impervious to sob stories, I was still hoping for something interesting to do.
I had to give up and cut my losses, my time pointlessly wasted.

Reading the reviews, I believe people who recommended this like it solely for the obviously depressing story and the feelings it gave them. (The are comments about tears.) To each his own. This is absolutely not for me.

If you want tears, just watch Grave of the Fireflies : it's a guarantee and you'll not have to mindlessly touch a gamepad to progress the story.
