\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMemories are like, a million shards of shattered glass lying unanimated in the bottomless pit that we call inner space. You pick one up, it slices your finger and reminds you why you never recalled it in the first place. So you drown it out and make up a facade of remedial mental wellness. "Facade". I like that word. At some point, the facade no longer exists as an abstract mental blockage, but becomes a definitive reality, the reality that doesn't seem quite right from the outside eyes but does it matter if it's significant to its creator?

Well yes, actually. As someone who suffered from high school bullying in high frequency and taught myself to block out bad memories to the point that the majority of my past is missing from my head, I don't think I've ever experienced any media with a "your defense mechanism isn't effective if you don't actively fight back the anguish" message as poignant as A Space for the Unbound. It comes in the form of coming to terms with your identities, reshaping your perspective to those around you, making amend to the rage monster inside, seeing grief as the companion to bring you to the next journey, and Street Fighter-style input sequence and QTE blocking even. The air you breathe is the sadness you bear. Dealing with turmoils is a fight in motion, and fighting yourself is the biggest challenge that any individual can see yourself to overcome.

Bake a cake, pet some cats, roleplay as Phoenix Wright, getting lost in the wind of hometown nostalgia. Anguish existentialism is inevitable and messy, but nobody promises you the answer, you have to look for it. What also is inevitable is by the time the storm is over and the sky is clear, the hand that let you go is no longer a faint memory cutting deep in your skin, but rather the glass shard that shows you the future ahead.

While I appreciate Lost Judgment's more realist and societal approach to bullying as subject matter, I give my utmost appreciation to A Space for the Unbound, for giving us not just a space, but mercy and tenderness to who we are. Open the door, fill your bucket list, be somewhat troublesome (or not), and maybe the next day will shine brighter than the last.\t\t\t\t\t
