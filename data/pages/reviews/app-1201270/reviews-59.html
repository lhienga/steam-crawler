A heavy yet beautiful narrative-driven adventure about facing your fears and growing up, set in a rather unique setting of 90s rural Indonesia.

Inspired by the works of Makoto Shinkai ( Your Name, Weathering with You ), A Space for the Unbound tells the story of two high school students with seemingly supernatural powers in Loka City, a town based on Surabaya, East Java. What could possibly happen?

The gameplay involves you exploring Loka City. Most of the chapters are spent free roaming the town, solving puzzles, and petting cats (and other animals). There will be quick time events to do certain things like fighting or doing certain puzzles and minigames. You can fail these, but you can retry as much as you want. Near the end of a chapter, the game will ask you if you want to proceed to a “point of no return” of that chapter. Save before this, since there are missable things.

The pixel art direction looks better than I expected. The characters are expressive, and the backgrounds in particular look gorgeous too, parts of the game is basically wallpaper material.

The story is the best part, and quite difficult to explain without spoiling it. Go experience it and try not to get spoiled!

Also, so many cats to pet. PET THEM ALL.

A must play for those who love story-driven adventures.

In every storm, you'll find your sunrise. \t\t\t\t\t

This is the first step in a gripping plot that zigzags around every corner and lets its mystery simmer before bubbling up to some eye-opening revelations. The gameplay here is a simple graphic adventure fair, where you walk around the Indonesian village solving puzzles and talking to the locals to progress your way. Atma holds a magic book that allows him to “space dive” into the hearts and minds of the residents of his town and solve their internal conflicts. This predictably leads to a series of heartfelt vignettes of those characters’ lives to serve the greater plot between Atma and his girlfriend. My biggest issue with A Space for the Unbound is how much of the moment-to-moment gameplay can feel like a basic fetch quest to pad out some of the lengths but of course, the focus of this type of game is the characters and story, which A Space for the Unbound nails.\t\t\t\t\t
