\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery cute, nostalgic sidescroller with a message about processing trauma that just... stays with you for a while. I knew going into this game that it would be a bit feelsy, and I saw the "big" twist coming, but I wasn't prepared for the suckerpunch of the game's final chapter. Woof.

Lots of references to Indonesian culture that I didn't quite get, being an uncultured American, but these gave the game and its characters lots of color and life. I love the pixel art style used in both the sprites and cut scenes, and the way that the world moves in general. The story is well told and each of the characters (even the NPCs) are given a lot of personality that makes you want to root for each of them. An atmospheric and fitting OST also helps bring this vibrant world to life.

The only bummer is that I missed two items on the bucket list, and I'd have to replay the whole game to get those achievements, because you can't travel between chapters to get them. Navigating between areas is also a bit of a headache, and because of that, I missed out on a couple of areas where there were additional cats to pet, and the music side quest hence the missed achievements. Because the pace of the game is a bit slow, I'm not sure I want to replay it any time soon, but I'd like to finish all the achievements for story purposes, to get the final post-credits ending.

I also really disliked the fighting video game lmao... I think it would've sucked less on keyboard, but it was a real stressful workout on Steam Deck.

Overall, though, I really enjoyed A Space for the Unbound and I'd highly recommend it if you, like me, enjoy emotional, story-driven games that try to rip your heart out with their bare hands.\t\t\t\t\t
