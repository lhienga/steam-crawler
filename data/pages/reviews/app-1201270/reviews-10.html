\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI became aware of this game when it was nominated for "Games for Impact" at the game awards. Initially I was not sure what the fuss was about, it seemed like a fairly ordinary (though pretty) pixel art game. Trust me when i say that you should not let the game's slower start put you off. My initial hesitation was very very wrong. This is a game that is beautiful, sweet, heart-wrenching and ultimately very satisfying. I can issue it my whole-hearted recommendation and assure you that it absolutely deserved the nomination.

Fair warnings: this game does require you to actually solve an algebra problem to progress the story at one point, so just keep that in mind. Also, I'm not normally a completionist, but i would recommend trying to complete every side-quest in this game for a little extra content. It'll be worth it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDislcaimer: I played this on my Steam Deck offline, so it says I've only played it for like two hours despite the fact that I'm in the final section of the game.

Actual review: I bought this game on a whim based on the fact that it had such positive reviews and knowing absolutely nothing about it. It was a great decision.

This is a charming indie game that's part old-school pixel art RPG, part old school point and click adventure, and part something else uniquely its own.

It has a bit of everything. Engaging characters, a good story, humor, drama, magic (or at least superpowers), teenage angst, mental health issues, Inception-style dreamwalking, and a ton of cats you can pet and name. Oh, and ambiance. So much ambiance.

I was wondering why I felt so attached to these characters and why the setting felt so familiar given that I'm not remotely Indonesian, when I found out that the main story is supposed to be set during the '90's. That was it.

What I felt was familiarity and nostalgia because I'm roughly the same age as Atma and Raya and what I was recognizing was the very authentic feeling of what it was like to be a teenager in a small town in those long gone days of the late 20th century before the internet was everywhere and cellphones were even a thing.

I don't think I had ever felt like that except when reading a good book or watching a good movie, but never while playing a video game. What an unexpected and interesting experience.\t\t\t\t\t
