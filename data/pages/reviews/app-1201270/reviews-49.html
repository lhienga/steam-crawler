\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is hard for me to overstate how beautiful this game is.

I am at the end game and just finished chapter 5 on my lunch break. That was a mistake because A Space for the Unbound had this grown ass man crying in my office (luckily behind closed doors).

The pixel art is stunning. The characters are beautifully written. The gameplay ranges from fun to frustrating. I admit that I am not a puzzle lover. But of the late game activities going back and forth to collect/do what you need to advance the story seemed as if they would frustrate even the most zealous puzzle chasers. But WOW, when that story did advance it was amazing.

The central themes hit very close to home for me in more ways that one. Maybe that contributed to the fact that I believe this is one of the most emotionally impactful games I have ever played. I've been gaming for a LONG time, and VERY few games touch me the way this one has.

DO NOT PASS UP this opportunity to experience video game and story telling greatness!

The major attractions here are the story, pixel art, and the music which create a enchanting experience. I won't discuss the story because I think you should experience it blind.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm leaving this to give a bit of a counter to the other reviews here which I think are a bit more positive than the game deserves. It's not a bad game, but it really wasn't worth my time. It may be worth it for other people though. It starts off strong, but unfortunately ends up losing a lot of it's steam past the prologue/first chapter.

One of the bigger issues is just that it's a visual novel trying to justify why it's not a visual novel...even though it would have probably been better had it just stuck with that. This means for a 10 hour game nearly half of it is essentially fetch quests that may unlock a couple lines of dialogue relevant to the characters and the story which makes it feel like it drag on and by the time you get to the unlocks you kind of don't care anymore. It also has a really forced combat system I don't really understand why they included as it felt like it took you more out of the story just to add more time to the game without any real challenge. While I don't think it'd be a great game either way, It'd definitely be a 7/10 for me had they cut out the fluff or at least made it a lot more meaningful.

The story is okay, it's nothing we haven't seen many times before. It was clear what the surprise reveal was early in chapter 1. It's major problem is being too on the nose, I love surrealism and weirdness but it needs to have a purpose and not beat me over the head. For example good weirdness might be you dealing with a bull character that does bull-like things that certain types of people would do to that symbolizes a real character and maybe even eventually showing them turn back if you really want to make sure the player gets it, it ends up feeling more meaningful and natural...this game does the full reverse, instead of revealing the bull was a symbol all along at the end of the bull characters arch the person is just a human, acts like a human, and then randomly turns into a bull at the end for only very tenuous symbolism (because they didn't tie the person to the bull persona all along.) It also tends to be randomly weird without much purpose, there honestly wasn't much reason to dive into people, it was rare that it felt more impact than a gimmick to get you into a puzzle. In something like Psychonauts it's a bit on the nose with the symbolism inside the persons head, but you do feel like you naturally get to know them better...in this game you basically enter in and immediately the character monologues about why their there and then you solve a puzzle and a few minutes later you forget about that character entirely.

The art suffers from the same problem, I love pixel art and this is passable, but especially in the weirder parts it just feels like they drew random artifacts to seem weird, there's not a lot of cohesiveness to it. That being said, some of their higher detailed still images had very expressive characters.

That all being said, about a third of players complete the game, if you like games like this enough to be in that third I'm sure you'll enjoy it a lot. I personally feel every story beat was done significantly better in other games. It ends up being like one of those movies where you don't hate it, but it's just mimicking things that were better and in a week you'll forget everything about it.\t\t\t\t\t
