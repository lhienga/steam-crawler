\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMasterfully done narrative and engaging puzzles. This game, along with classics like "To the moon" reminds me once again that often times, we don't need to embellish pixel-styled games with innovative graphics just to create a wonderful experience--having a good story and well designed mechanics is more than enough.

The people from Mojiken have hit all of the marks with this amazing side-scrolling adventure. The music, the art, and the story all merges into this engaging game that expertly tackles depression, grief, and social relations. I think what I enjoyed the most was the exposure they gave to Indonesian culture and how it was presented. If you're someone who enjoys puzzles, figuring things out, and also appreciates a well-done narrative, this game is for you.

I'm definitely a fan of the developers and will be anticipating more games from them!\t\t\t\t\t

MASTERPIECE

THE STORY

I have to say this is probably one of the best psychological adventure game I've ever played. The part that always amazes me is the story. This is not just a good game with good story; this is A STORY that portrayed beautifully into a game.

There are more than just story. It was so sad that I'm in tears, but I'm also relieved that I was able to finish the story. It was heartwarming even if it was very hard and we had to go through many traumatic events. It's just like in real life where we always have to move on, no matter how hard it is or how painful it's gonna get. I could relate to the psychological side of the story that basically told us that even if everything is a mess, we could always be brave, sort out our minds, and move on. I could say for sure that the story is the best part of this game.

THE MECHANICS

I really love the fact that this game is not just a simple 2D sidescroller. It has a lot of cool mechanics that make this game unique in its own way.

The first thing I'd like to mention is The Rift. I like how they implement a time-travel mechanic into a rift that could be opened by a Magic Wand. It allowed us to travel through dimensions and timelines. I remember playing a game that had this advanced time-travel mechanic just like this. It's called The Silent Age and it was a very good game. Since then, I can never get enough of this amazing mechanic that actually has influence on what would happen in the future if we change the past. This is one of the best time-travel mechanic I encountered in a game, and this game has managed to implement it perfectly.

Spacedive is also a great mechanic. I like how they portrayed the inside of people's mind in the spacedive world. It looked so random but it's actually a great representation of how the thoughts conflicting in their own heads. It subtly explained the depression and the desperation that the people experienced through the stories and mission we should accomplish as the player that go through their minds.

There are more interesting mechanics in the game, but The Rift and Spacedive definitely are unique in the game and I really like those.

THE ART AND SOUND

I'm not an art person. I can't draw, I can't sing, I can't play music. However, I can say for sure that this pixel-art with calming music is a perfect combination that blends sweetly with the story. I really LOVE the environment in the game! The setting of this game is in a city called Loka. Loka City is a very beautiful small town with neatly lined local houses; stalls; shops that always stock all the necessities; bridge; beautiful trees and flowers; a community center with a mosque, internet cafe, arcade, etc. For those of you who live in Indonesia, this Loka City is set to be located in Indonesia. So it was a very familiar environment that brings us to nostalgia. I also recommend to play in Indonesian Language if you speak that language, it feels much more authentic.

THE CATS

Cats are always the winner. The fact that they chose to put cats in this game is such a great decision. It's a perfect addition to the game! Animals are always the best, cats especially. They also put dog, rooster, hen, rat, goose, duck, and finally the legend Kebo Bule and Barong. We can pet them, name them, and they also played a big part in the game.

THE PUZZLES

I love puzzles. Puzzles are always one of the best elements in a game. I always like to solve a puzzle. Unfortunately, no matter how much I like puzzles, this is a bit tiring for me. There are no multiple ways to solve the puzzle, which means most puzzles only has a single solution. And this becomes tiring because completing an objective takes a long time and needs to go through so many steps. Those are actually good puzzles. But one chapter for one puzzle? I ended up got tired that I almost didn't want to do the objectives anymore. There's this objective that needs to be done in a very long way and took a surprisingly very long steps: the single objective in Chapter 2. The rest is still fine, I guess. But this puzzle in Chapter 2 took too long to solve, because it went to another objective, and onto the other objectives, again and again to the point it became tiring.

Nevertheless, I still like how the story turned out, and it blends well with the objectives and puzzles. So, I guess this will only be a small criticism for this game. I still like it though, don't get me wrong. But yeah, that Chapter 2 puzzle is something else for me.

FINAL THOUGHTS

This game is a masterpiece, I could say for sure. I encountered a crucial bug that wouldn't let me finish this game because I really couldn't progress. However, the Dev Team contacted me and give a really helpful solution to install a beta patch in order to fix the bug. I sincerely appreciate the Dev Team for responding to me and actually give a proper solution. Apart from that bug (and maybe the puzzles I mentioned), this is a very good game. The story is told beautifully. It has a perfect execution of how a story-rich adventure game should be. And the cats! Those are the great "addition" which made the game flawless.\t\t\t\t\t
