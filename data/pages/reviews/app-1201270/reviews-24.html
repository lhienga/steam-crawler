\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who played the To the Moon series, I had a very high expectation of this game when I saw the trailer. And boy, it met my expectations magnificently and beyond!

The game did a great job at what it wanted to do: tell you a good story. Its narration is easy to follow and enjoyable. As you follow Atma through his adventure, you can't help but be invested in him and the characters around him. The more the story unfolds, the more you are intrigued. It is one of the stories that you want to keep reading and see through to the end.

Accompanying the beautifully woven story are lovely pixel art and soundtrack. Just like the To the Moon series, pixel art doesn't affect the delivery at all. Rather, its simplicity lets you focus more on the story. The beautifully composed soundtrack significantly amplifies your experience and helps with the immersion as you go through the game.

The setting, which is Indonesia, might be a little bit foreign to many but fear not! You will get used to it in no time. I am sure that you can relate to many situations that occur in the game. Trust me.

But none of this would matter if you can't relate to the characters. As I said above, you will get invested in Atma and the people around him. The characters are well-written and well-thought-out. Each of them has a unique character and is memorable.

The only flaw I could say about the game is how it handle its fetch quest in the late game. Having layers of those is nice. But it can break immersion and the continuation of storytelling sometimes.

Overall, I can recommend this game to anyone who loves narrative adventure. Its story and characters are well-written. The art and the soundtrack are perfect. It will be one of the games you will never forget.

PS. I might be overthinking about this but the reference to the platypus in the game might be a reference to the To the Moon series.\t\t\t\t\t
