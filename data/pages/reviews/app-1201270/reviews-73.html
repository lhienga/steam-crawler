\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love adventure games, and I love it more when indie developers are the one who creates them because they usually have some of the freshest and goofball ideas. So seeing a game created by an Indonesian developer, a country that seldomly heard of creating games like this, I'm pretty excited to see what they have to offer.

Pros

  • I very much like the prologue of the game. It introduces our main characters dynamics and motivation naturally. The introduction to the world is also nice, you can feel the idealized version of living in an Indonesia small city.
  • Some of the pixel art stuff are nice

Cons

  • For the gameplay, there's not a lot of creative or out-of-the-box thinking to do here. Progress always consists of just fetching stuff that the game tells you where to go, so no searching for items or combining X with Y to get Z. It makes the game feels like it's more a glorified visual novel rather than an adventure game.
  • The pixel art while can be nice at times, doesn't have a lot of animations. One of the fun stuff of pixel art is seeing unique animations on big events, but in this game, there are not a lot of them. Mostly in those events, the pixel art will remain static. Poses may change, but usually, it has little to no animation
  • The story got worse after the prologue. I think the main problem is that it becomes less subtle than the prologue. Every information is described and not "shown" which makes it feels like lazy writing. I have a problem with the twist too, in that it's very predictable even at the end of chapter 1, it really deflates any tension on the problem of the story. Do note I personally don't like "Young Adult" stuff, and this really feels like one. So fans of the genre maybe will like it better than me.
  • Meh story can be elevated with fun characters. Sadly, this game does not. The main character is bland, he doesn't have any spunk for a high schooler nor interesting thoughts for himself. All he does is just follow the heroine or the other characters direction. Usually in this game, interaction with items or characters will slowly reveal what kind of personality the main character has. An example is Halligan from Mystery of the Druids, when he interacts with a cigarette, he'll comment with something along the line of "Smoking is a death of me", this indicates the character is a heavy smoker and kind of cynical. In this game, the main character never has fun things to say. When interacting with something, he just usually goes "Hmm, that is a [SOMETHING]", what does the player suppose to pick from that? Other characters also don't really have anything notable to say. More important characters usually exist only for the story can progress, infodump the theme of the game, but other than that it's just...nothing.
  • The "Indonesian" theme of this game feels very off. As someone who had lived extensively for a long time in Indonesia, the overall feeling of this game feels very fake. For example, An internet cafe that looks very clean, which I assure you will never be the case (those places are damn noisy and full of people smoking), there's a cafe with a bakery that had a chef with a full-on white chef attire (I never see a bakery staff using that), or a kondangan (a party reception that usually uses a public street) that doesn't make any traffic jam and not blasting "dangdut" like everyone in the vicinity is deaf. There's a lot more stuff like that, but I'm not going to nitpick. The point is, it has many references to Indonesia stuff, but it took it safe and clean, hence why I said it's an "idealized" version of an Indonesian small city. For people who already finish this game. SPOILER: Yes, I know this is the world inside of the heroine's head, but it still doesn't excuse the bad execution of this particular theme.
  • Last and the part where it irks me the most. The native translation (Indonesian Language). Why is it so stiff? It feels like they write this in English first and then ask ChatGPT to translate it into Indonesian. No sane Indonesia person will tell someone to hurry by using "Mulailah menggerakkan kaki-kaki gemukmu!". They should've leveraged this so people who can read Indonesian have an enhanced experience. Add some fun pun or some reference that only makes sense if it's written in Indonesia, who cares if the international people don't get it, it adds souls to the game. STALKER and Yakuza have good localization, but the experience is better if you enjoyed it in their native language AND understand it. It really is disappointing.

Closing thoughts

If I compare this with something, playing this game feels like drinking stale water while being thirsty. A bit refreshing, taste weird, and makes your stomach hurt a bit after some hours, but then you just forgot about it. I don't regret purchasing it, though. It is a unique game in that there's nothing really like it for now (An adventure game in Indonesia setting). Hopefully, the next one will be a better game.\t\t\t\t\t
