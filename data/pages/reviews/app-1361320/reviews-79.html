\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"The Room 4" continues the series' tradition keeping the same clever puzzles' logic, great graphics, pleasant smooth animations, fantastic sound design and mysterious atmosphere. The biggest drawbacks I found is that it introduces nothing new to the gameplay compared to the previous episode, while I felt it to be the easiest among its predecessors. Nevertheless, the game is so well built that you will feel like you are examining real artifacts as you play with their mechanisms.


Manometer: 8/10


The good
- Great visuals. Detailed models and high resolution aged textures. Nice, atmospheric lighting too. It gives you the impression that most of the objects could be real.
- Fantastic design. All these mechanical objects are so imaginative. They transform, wrap and unwrap and change their shape in so many creative ways. The rooms are nicely designed too.
- Awesome sound design. The sounds of the mechanisms are so convincing. Hearing them clicking and ticking while you move mechanical parts is so satisfying.
- Fantastic, smooth and extremely pleasant animations
- Gameplay is simple but effective. Wise choice not to highlight what is clickable but let you find it by yourself as you would examine a real object.
- Good puzzles' structure. Each one drives you to the next one and all are connected together via the doll house. Great flow and pace.
- Cool background music.
- Great, dark atmosphere.
- A puzzle game like this doesn't really need a plot, but it's nice that it has a pretty decent background story. It enhances the atmosphere for sure.
- Nice direction, camera's movement and cut scenes between key points of the story.
- The hints system has levels so it doesn't tell you the solution at once. It just give you a little push.
- It is so relaxing.

The bad
- There is no real innovation to the gameplay. For example its predecessor added the lens along with some new gameplay mechanisms. It is more of "The Room 3 - Part II" than an actual new episode.
- Although puzzles' design is great, I found it pretty straight forward and a lot easier than the previous games of the series. It could be more challenging.
- Few objects here and there are a bit small to notice especially if you consider that compare to the first game you have to examine whole rooms instead of a single object. You may get stuck sometimes because you didn't notice something small lying in a corner.\t\t\t\t\t
