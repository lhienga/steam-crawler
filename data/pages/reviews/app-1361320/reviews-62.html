\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like The Room, this is more The Room. Like the previous game in the series, there's a frame puzzle that unlocks the individual escape room puzzles. Unlike previous titles, some components in other rooms are needed to solve other rooms, instead of solving one room at a time. Sometimes the various rooms interact in cool ways, and I really liked this feature at first.

That said, the last couple of rooms are pretty quick solves and pad themselves out by making you jump rooms repeatedly. The art room is especially egregious with almost nothing to solve in the room itself. The flow in the early parts of the game made me think this would be the longest The Room yet, but it ended up being shorter than Three.

I guess complaining about the fourth The Room game not containing enough The Room is a testament to the appeal of the series. I just hope The Room 5 creates more densely packed rooms and more of them, with less jumping between them.\t\t\t\t\t

The puzzles themselves are not that difficult, but the scavenging aspect of it can be challenging at times. Adding to the difficulty: items you need to progress can be hidden in rooms you're not currently working on in the dollhouse. I got hung up for a while because I didn't notice the one panel on the thing in The Japanese Room that gave me the thing that I needed to progress and do the other things. Yeah, I know. That was a pretty big hint, wasn't it? That's why I used the spoiler blackout. Maybe don't hover over the spoiler blackout to read the spoiler if you don't want the entire experience ruined for you. Basically, you're looking for the hidden panel that has the key that will let you find the code to open the next latch to open the box. In the scenery and little details around the rooms are little clues (small details being off, geometric anomalies, out of place symbols and the such) that will give you indicators as to where you should look next or what code to use to progress.

Atmospherically, it succeeds in creating an aura of intrigue and mysticism through lighting, sound effects, music, ambiance, and the detail put into the rooms and puzzles.

It has pretty much no replay value, but it is a good game and the price isn't bad.
