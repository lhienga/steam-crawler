\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably my favorite in the series. Occasionally, a chapter here and there seemed too easy or quick, but we learned in an earlier installment that sometimes that's just how it is. That it's intentionally that way. After spending a while puzzling out a particularly difficult puzzle, it's kind of nice to have an easy 'gimme' puzzle. Sometimes it happens too many times and it's disappointing that there was little to no challenge in something. This installment might've put their toe a wee bit over that line into 'too many easy/quick chapters'.

I'm a little disappointed that the eyepiece was mostly only used to enter rooms rather than reveal clues to puzzles. Not that it didn't reveal a few things, but I liked how it was used in previous games to reveal hidden combinations or words or whatever considerably more often. The hidden things it did reveal were kind of pointless, like merely highlighting a puzzle that's plainly a puzzle and doesn't need highlighting. It's like they forgot it existed for that purpose after the first couple of chapters and just used it as a 'doorknob' more often than not, so to speak. I had hoped that they'd find another new use for the eyepiece, as they did with each other installment.

Other than these minor gripes, though, I really enjoyed this game. I liked this part of the story/lore more than the first three and actually paid attention to it rather than just kinda skimming the notes like 'blah blah blah, gimme puzzles'. I wish that the notes served a purpose other than lore, but that's okay. It helped majorly that the notes weren't rambling on and on. Short, sweet, to the point notes are good. The majority of the puzzles were a decent enough challenge. I liked that solving puzzles in one section led to solving puzzles in different sections and going back and forth, rather than a more linear approach of solving one section completely before moving on to the next. While I didn't mind the 'extras' of a previous game, and it was kind of neat that there was an overarching puzzle to get different endings, I'm glad that sort of thing wasn't in this one. Redoing the final puzzle over and over was not fun for me, so thanks for not doing that to us, again.\t\t\t\t\t
