\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's just say all 'The Room' games are great games. I highly recommend just getting the Bundle.

Now that said, the last part is definitely one of the weaker ones. I really liked the third part. The atmosphere, the aesthetic, the difficulty level, the story and the multiple endings, without the option to get help.

In contrast to that, this game feels almost insignificant. The story is much weaker, the whole setting feels just less significant, the riddles are much less challenging. A lot of the time it doesn't feel like solving riddles and more like just working through a step by step manual to get to the end.
And in the end, it gets easier and easier. The last two levels don't take more than 10 minutes each and don't pose any challenge whatsoever.

I really hope there is a 5th part coming, because if this was the end, it would be kinda disappointing.

So just to recap: a great game, but worse than the other parts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t★★★★★ - 5 Stars

I've played all 4 of the Room games now and they honestly just keep getting better and better. 3 and 4 are my favorites, and this one was so clever, with using the dollhouse and going back and forth between the rooms. It feels like 3 was longer, but maybe it just took me longer to figure out. I think I'm getting better at playing these games, too, as I only required one small hint for this game (so proud!). I love the horror/historical feel to these games, and the puzzles are so fun.

I'm also thankful for the one ending. Although multiple endings can be cool, I like one good, interesting ending that helps me know I actually found everything and resolved the entire game. The lore is so interesting, and I really hope, if there are future games, that they will give us more lore on the Null.

It took me about 5 hours to finish the game, and that was being very thorough. I highly recommend to anyone who likes escape rooms and games with lots of puzzles to figure out.\t\t\t\t\t
