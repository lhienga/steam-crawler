\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe second best game in The Room series after The Room 3 in my opinion. Compared to the others the player doesn't go through rooms in a linear fashion, completing one after the other, but rather has rooms of a whole house available to them at the same time. It is even required by the game to start a room, jump to another halfway through, finish a puzzle there, and come back to the previous one in order to finish the first room.

The house setting, however, also means that there is less thematic diversity than there was in The Room 3.

Nevertheless the puzzles are still cleverly designed, the presentation is still top notch and the atmosphere of the game still manages to evoke that unmistakable Lovecraftian horror vibe.\t\t\t\t\t
