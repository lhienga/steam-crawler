\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played each of the room games and, in my opinion, this is the best one by a far margin. The story, while secondary to the point and click puzzles/shape memorization, was more engaging for this release and I found myself more interested in reading the lore pages than in the past 3 games.

Additionally, the game's solutions felt a bit more intuitive in this release. Some of the past games felt frustrating as I rarely found myself stuck on a "puzzle" and more frequently was stuck on "click this random thing/rotate the camera to see that there was actually another switch on this night stand." This type of solution seemed less common in this game. However, I did play this game as the last in the series, so I may have gotten used to where to look/click to resolve problems more quickly. . .

Overall, you can pick up The Room series as a bundle and it seems to frequently go on sale. I picked the bundle up on sale and found it worth that price point. It was a fairly pleasant experience. If for some reason you only plan to play one of the games I would recommend the Room 4 as I had the most fun with it, but I think it's probably worth playing them all in order and they are all fairly short games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Room 4: Old Sins preserves the captivating essence of the series and immerses players in the enigmatic realm of Waldegrave Manor. This mesmerizing variation on escape-the-room experiences centers around the discovery of a highly detailed dollhouse, an intricate web of interconnected puzzles and mysteries. Players navigate this richly detailed world by manipulating objects, solving intricate puzzles and delving into the mysteries of the Null, a mysterious material with the power to warp reality. Equipped with a special eyepiece, players can unveil concealed objects and puzzles within the Null, adding a layer of depth to the gameplay. The collection, examination and manipulation of items are fundamental to the progression of the game and often lead to the discovery of hidden puzzles or objects. A user-friendly hint system is available for those moments when players get stuck.

What sets The Room 4 apart from its predecessors is its shift towards interconnected puzzles that spread across multiple rooms within the manor. There are up to three rooms and the dollhouse's exterior to explore, each contributing to a multifaceted puzzle-solving experience. As additional rooms unlock, the challenge becomes more intricate, requiring critical thinking and the ability to connect the dots between different areas of the dollhouse. Each room features a unique artifact that serves as a thematic centerpiece for the puzzles, enriching the game's narrative and sense of intrigue from the outset.
