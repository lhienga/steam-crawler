The actual puzzles, however, are a bit on the easy side with the challenge lying way too often in noticing an object or remembering where in the model house you have seen the obvious place of usage for a new item. There's rarely any thinking involved, just move the next lever/button etc. to see what happens, which, granted, has its allure, but it also gets easier as the game progresses, so it starts to feel more like busy work towards the end, Most of the contraptions don't make much sense from a mechanical point of view, either. Why can you not move this piece unless you attached that handle that doesn't really do anything? Because videogame. So this review complains a lot about what the game isn't and doesn't, but it's still an entertaining experience; a toy, basically. Just don't expect anything too deep.\t\t\t\t\t
