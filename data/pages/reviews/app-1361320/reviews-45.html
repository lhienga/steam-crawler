\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was hoping it would be longer than the previous game but I won't complain as there is no shortage of detail. The devs continue to impress me with their attention on everything. The mechanisms, cinematics, scenery, atmosphere, all such incredible detail that really makes it immersive.

The puzzles were fun but maybe a little too easy. Sometimes the game can hold your hand a little too much even with hints off; turning on the scanner when entering a new area gives you basically every point of interest. That also might have influenced my opinion on it feeling shorter even though it might have more content.

The story really grabbed me in this one. The stories in these games have generally been vague and/or lacking, though have gotten progressively better. This one was very intriguing and I couldn't wait to get to the next page in the book to see what happened. I also think the way they told the story was very well done.

I think this was great fun and definitely worth the time. I played these games in order and the devs have continued to improve time and time again. The first one was great and they keep growing in complexity and detail, so I'm really looking forward to the next one!\t\t\t\t\t
