The same attention to detail and polished graphics as Room 1, with beautifully designed box puzzles, all integrated in a magical doll's house which combines the movement between rooms and magic eye investigation elements of Room 3. So much to explore as you gradually open up and eliminate 9 rooms, along with a developing story which hints back to earlier games. Puzzles within puzzles within puzzles! The only slight drawback is sometimes you can't work out which room an item belongs in and how to zero down to the specific place, but hints lead there eventually. Also not sure who we are - surely not the same character as in Room 3 who was either freed or lost? - but this is a minor plot element. I bought all four games in a bundle on sale and they have been well worth the price, hours of entertainment at just the right level of challenge. Turn your brightness up high though! If you have my goldfish memory, replayability should be high.\t\t\t\t\t
