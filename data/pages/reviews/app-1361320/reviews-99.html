\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOf all the excellent chapters of The Room series, I have enjoyed this one the most. Kudos to the team at Fireproof Games for their wonderful imagination and beautiful artwork. The puzzles are a good level of difficulty – challenging but not ridiculous, and the graded hint system was welcome, especially when you had no idea where to go next.

I found this episode easier to move from room to room as it was a “doll-house”. In previous episodes, I would tend to get lost trying to find a particular room.
There were only a couple of puzzles that I needed extra help from the internet for, and it was so satisfying to work out the vast majority of them with no help at all.

The various mechanisms were a delight to observe, and I loved the way that, when you completed the puzzle, the particular contraption would close up and recede in such an elegant fashion.

I also appreciated the absence of silly gimmicks such as time-outs, having to continually save the game, or making a wrong move and having to start over.

In closing, I would highly recommend this game to puzzle fans, and I am eagerly looking forward to the next episode of The Room.

Thank you!
