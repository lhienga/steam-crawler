\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved this game!

In some ways, it's quite simple. You basically just have to solve a bunch of mechanical puzzles by manipulating 3D models in real time. But the intricacy and the quality of the puzzles and the incredible presentation really sets it apart from anything else like it I've ever played!

Solving a puzzle can be incredibly satisfying, often triggering a 'cascade' of cool events, with gear wheels spinning, steam powered pistons pumping and water driving paddle wheels etc etc. It can be quite mesmerising watching the various gadgets and gizmos unfold themselves in front of your eyes (and frequently put themselves back together!).

There's also a cool steampunk vibe to everything and a nicely spooky atmosphere with good sound effects.

I guess you could complain about the limited movement system - you basically just teleport between fixed points and have limited freedom of vision the whole time - and maybe the fact that only certain things are interactive without there being any real clues of what they are (although it's usually pretty obvious). But IMO these are minor complaints that don't affect the puzzle solving based game play, and likely allowed the authors to polish things to the N+1'th degree - and this is probably the most polished game I have played for a long time!

All in all, an excellent way to spend 6-ish hours for puzzle/adventure fiends IMO.

Bye,
Mark
