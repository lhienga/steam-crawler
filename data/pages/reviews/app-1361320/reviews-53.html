\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRating: 9/10

Strengths:
Engaging Puzzles: The Room 4: Old Sins offers an ideal balance of difficulty, ensuring that players are constantly challenged, but not frustrated. Staggered multi-stage puzzles add depth and complexity to the gameplay, requiring problem-solving and lateral thinking.

Visual Excellence: The game features beautiful mechanical contraptions that are well animated, and distinct visual indicators (such as pipes running through different rooms) aid in the puzzle-solving experience. The art and object design, featuring ornate brass and polished wood, further contribute to the game's mesmerizing visuals.

Narrative and Atmosphere: A subtle yet engaging story ties in with the ludonarrative and setting, evoking a sense of mystery and wonder reminiscent of Lovecraftian fiction. The game's theme, which is mysterious and archaic without being explicitly scary or creepy, is one of the best I have experienced.

Refreshing Gameplay Elements: The less frequent use of the eyepiece is a refreshing change that allows players to appreciate the game's intricate design more fully.

Weaknesses:

Technical Issues: Aliasing on high settings is noticeable, which could detract from the otherwise excellent graphics. Additionally, the absence of borderless windowed mode may inconvenience some players.

Controls: Clicking in and out of each room can become tiresome, affecting the overall gameplay experience.

Game Length: The game's relatively short length (roughly 4 hours of playtime) might leave players craving for more.\t\t\t\t\t
