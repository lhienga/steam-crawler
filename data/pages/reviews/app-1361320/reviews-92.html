\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite a bit shorter than The Room 3. While it's easy to say that it's "More of the same" and have that sound like a bad thing, with this series it is absolutely not. Devious and genius puzzle design as always. My biggest complaint with this entry over the first three games is that there was tons of constant backtracking. I get the appeal of having puzzle elements found in one room solve something in another room, but sometimes you were going from one room to get an item, to a second room to use it and get another item, back to the first room to use it, then back to the second immediately again. It wouldn't be so bad if the transitions between rooms was a bit quicker. A minor complaint for a game in a series where I've had practically no complaints.

It's also worth pointing out yet again that you would never know these were originally mobile games if you weren't told. The PC overhaul is more well done than anything else you're ever likely to find. It's not just a simple recompile with a mouse interface hastily thrown on top. It was done with care.

I sure hope there are more The Room games that aren't VR exclusive. I'll buy every one of them.\t\t\t\t\t
