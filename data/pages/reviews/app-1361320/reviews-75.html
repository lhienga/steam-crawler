\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike its predecessors, The Room 4 is full of beautifully crafted intricate puzzle boxes, connected with a story that you uncover as you make your way through the game. The graphics and sound are perfect for this type of game and as the story unfolds, there are some genuinely creepy moments too. The puzzles themselves seemed to me to be a tad easier than the previous installments, but that is a good thing as it made figuring them out a bit more enjoyable. There's even a built-in hint system that can dispense a quick hint when needed. I found that I only needed it in some cases to prompt me on where to go next or where to focus my attention. Highly recommended to people who like tinkering with puzzles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMuch like the previous games it has a rewarding mix of difficulty and gorgeously made puzzle boxes.

My favorite room in terms of both the puzzles and the aesthetics would have to be the Japanese Gallery. I loved how this room looked and played and the red herrings were well done.

But my favorite feature of this iteration of The Room, was the hidden menu that saved me when one of my items went missing due to lacking hardware(my problem) causing a glitch. That menu allowed me to resume at a recently opened room and I only had to redo 1 puzzle, that since I had just completed it recently did not pose any difficulty to do so again. Had that feature not been there, I fear I would have had to begin again from the beginning and despite the short length of the games(4-6 hours for myself,) I'm not sure I would have anytime soon.\t\t\t\t\t
