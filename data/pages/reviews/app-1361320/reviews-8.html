\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTh Room 4 gives you more of what you would expect, but advances all aspects of the game to make for the best one of the series. Room 4 is a escape room type of game where you need to figure out puzzles, by finding switches, gears, buttons and solving small but well designed puzzles.

With a special eyepiece you can see different things in the room compared to what you would see with the naked eye. This was the best game of the series to utilize the eyepiece, I feel. The graphics weren't too much different than previous version, but the graphics are beautifully done. The sound and music is haunting, but not a major part of the experience, but there to fit in with the atmosphere, without distracting from the puzzles and the hunt for switches.

I also felt that this was a much more streamlined puzzle experience, the puzzles were a bit more obvious, but still needed some thinking to get you to the answer. It just felt like I was flying through the game, never stuck for too long. Previous iterations had some really hard to find switches and buttons that sometimes I just didn't click right, but I never had that experience here.

It took me under 10 hours to beat this game, and it's a perfect length. Since the atmosphere and scenery do not change all that much other than various renders of different rooms, the motif of the Victorian house is continuous throughout the majority of the game. That's not a bad thing though as it's beautifully rendered, but when the game finishes you've pretty much explored the entire house. I do like the game design where different rooms offer items and solutions to other rooms, made for good connections.

Overall, best in the series and the devs have really set a standard for this type of game. No bugs were experienced during my playthrough and the achievements are all story based and none can be missed, which makes for an easy 100%. Definitely worth the play through.\t\t\t\t\t
