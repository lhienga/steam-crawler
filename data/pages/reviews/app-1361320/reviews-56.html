\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: thank heavens for series like The Room.

Any series that makes it to the 4th installment is inherently suspicious. The case is most often that the creators found a comfortable formula that a dedicated fan base loyally buys every time, and even if the early games were great, installments starting from the 3rd start to get stale, then go straight into boring. I'm sure everyone can think of series like that - while exceptions, where later installments are as good, or even better than the original game is very few and far between. The Room is such a series, though.
Most of The Room 4: Old Sins plays inside a dollhouse, yet it's the most complex game in the series location-wise. How insane does that sound? Through the mechanics of the mysterious eyepiece, the protagonist can enter various rooms of the dollhouse - not one by one, but generally, 3 or 4 at the same time. This logic enables location-spanning puzzles - not only the boring kind, where you find an item in room A which you can use in room B, but also the kind where you put mechanics in motion that have effects in other rooms in the house.
The other thing that's new in The Room 4 is that the puzzle difficulty went up a couple of notches. That's usually a mixed bag - there are some annoyingly opaque puzzles, true, but there are some wonderfully complex things as well that make you think in terms of the entire house, and those are brilliant.
What isn't new is the core mechanic of tinkering with puzzle boxes / puzzle rooms. The best thing about a Room game is always the carefully designed, beautifully animated (and sounded) boxes and other mechanisms, which are both aesthetically pleasing and intellectually satisfying. So much so that I would even love the game without any puzzles - operating all these intricate little things feels like a Christmas gift waiting on every corner. And there's such a diversity of them! The game never feels repetitive or old, despite the huge number of things to do.

In summary, The Room series is a relentless glory ride of video game making craftsmanship, and the 4th installment may just be the best of them. I've loved every episode so far, and I'm looking forward to the provisional next ones.
