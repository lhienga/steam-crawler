\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust a superb series of puzzle games and this entry is no exception. This one feels much more tightly contained then the last 2 much like the first game as it involve just a single Dollhouse, but it still feels expansive like 2 and 3 by having you explore each room of the dollhouse and uncover the secrets within. The inclusion of FMV, although scarce, is really a nice treat. It harkens back to the FMV games of the 90's, like Tex Murphy or Phatasmagoria with the integration of pre-rendered CGI on top of the live action footage (I honestly wish there were more). if you like the other games this is more of the same and I mean that in the best way possible.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe fourth installment of the series, Old Sins delivers us the unique experience that the 3rd game offered us. Which was to navigate through different rooms for a more challenging experience. However, I believe that developers noticed it was a burden to wait for animations while switching rooms in 3rd game so they made it way more unique and appealing. The way unnecessary rooms are closed is just cherry on top.

As a The Room game I can certainly say that game lives up to its name. Even better design, more challenges comparatively "new" mechanics (I know it is basically a click and slide simulator but they tried new things). My favourite level was Japanese Gallery which was quite well designed.

In conclusion, as a cool looking puzzle game The Room 4 checks all marks that is needed to deliver a good experience but falls short in the game's ending. Last level was just a recursion of move this thing to here which got boring way too fast. As many have mentioned, puzzles in this one a bit more easier to solve compared to the other three but I don't think it is the case here. We get used to the design so that we notice things quicker.\t\t\t\t\t
