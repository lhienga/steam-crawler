\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot a bit less amazing than the previous three, I can honestly say that playing through this quadrilogy was a huge enjoyment, and solving puzzles by manipulating beautifully designed objects was extremely satisfying. I am glad that the developers did not go crazy with new additions and mechanics as the series went on, instead they stuck with the saying: "If it ain't broke, don't fix it." , which is something that I wish other developers followed as well.

From these four games I've played, I cannot pick a favorite. They are all worth playing, so if you played one of them and liked it, you will like the others as well.

The only con of these games is that they are a one time experience, meaning that if you decide to play the game again, you will not have the same amount of joy and amazement as you will probably remember how some of the puzzles go and all the things that happen. Maybe some voice acting would've been nice.

All in all, 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just have no love for this one.

No alternate endings to uncover like The Room 3, and the puzzles are more a matter of finding something rather than figuring something out. The constant need to transition between areas makes the game feel tiny and limited, which is annoying because it's still so linear. The amount of glowing sigils and diagrams hidden about are frustratingly pointless, because it emphasises the fact that the game is really shallow.

The story elements feel disconnected from the previous games, and just fall flat. There was nothing that piqued my curiosity and made me want to find more. Instead, it was more or less just a series of cringe updates about a marriage devolving into paranoid violence; made all the worse by having to solve a puzzle by literally helping the doll of a psycho chase down the doll of his innocent wife. Also, having every single story update be fed to us through a copy/paste book animation sequence just made me want to skip looking at any more of the books.

But the graphics were fairly good, I guess. All the intricate mechanisms seem lifeless and excessive compared to the previous games though. Like, I know all of the mechanisms in all of the games are "impossibly" intricate, but the ones in TR4 just seem the most unbelievable, pointless or uninspired. It's less like watching some fantastical machine, and more like watching a PowerPoint presentation with a different transition animation on every slide...\t\t\t\t\t
