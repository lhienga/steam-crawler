\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Room 4 fixes the problem of repetition from Room 3 while also doing well at feeling less linear than the first two room games (travelling between different locations to progress puzzles more often than just staying in one area).

Overall, if you enjoyed the previous room, games there is more of the same fun to be had here.

Mostly the same points as previous Room games:

The Room can be completed fairly quickly and is great for a few evening of (slightly spooky) fun or an introduction to puzzle games / escape rooms.

If you are a seasoned puzzle game player, The Room's puzzles can feel a little simple, but if you enjoy escape rooms, it can still be enjoyable as the (simulation of) pressing of buttons and pulling of levers to slide open hidden panels, revealing the next piece in the sequence, is very satisfying.

Although very linear, the use of environment and atmosphere is interesting, giving your actions a place in a wider story and a charming sense of mystery.
I'd recommend picking the Room 1-4 together, where possible, so they can be played close together in an episodic manner.\t\t\t\t\t
