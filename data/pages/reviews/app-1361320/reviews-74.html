There is no theme to the items, they are neither Dollhouse components nor personal mementos or even "shards of the McGuffin". So why do they need this box and why is the box so hermeticly sealed and triggers ... a thing.
-no real challenge, sure one can spend some time twisting nobs or overlooking a hotspot, but never is there a point where you really need to think about anything other than "now where could this thingy go?". You can't even lose. I guess this is a + for some players but not for me.
- bad usage of the occulus feature. like it's predecessors R4 uses an eyepiece to show "mysterious markings" and "the otherrealm" Unlike those there really isn't a point to it here. Sure it's heavily used because every room requires you to break a seal only visible with it to enter(every time!!) and there are plenty of "strangespace puzzles". But those are clearly marked so even without the piece you see where you need it and putting it on is reduced to an inconvenience. In previous parts it revealed secret knowledge that you needed to solve a puzzle and even then the gimmick had more novelty to it so the "strangespace puzzles" stil felt interesting.
-Inadequate usage of the dollhouse/house and illogical Mechanisms. While there are some examples of the opposite(like shooting down an Item from the detail layer to retrieve on the meta layer), most puzzles are simply shoved into the setting with little context. Put thing on socket -> unrealted doors open. My issue here is not with realism but with puzzle association: If I want to get to a McGuffin I look around where it could be, analyse the locking mechanism and find associated objects/hints, repeat for multi stage mechanisms. here I mostly just wiggle on the one thing I can manipulate until it gives me an item I can then locate the socket for which unlocks a wiggleable thing, repeat for multi stage mechanisms. This has always been a thing in the Room but I feel it got worse which is strange considering all the opportunities.

Overall its nice but the core gamedesign leaves a lot to be desired and considering how much of the house remains untouched it really feels rushed/unfinished.

PS: yes I gave a negative review to a "good game" but that's just what you get when limited to two options.\t\t\t\t\t
