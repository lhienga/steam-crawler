\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good puzzle game with a nice, simple but mysterious story surrounding it. I liked the slow reveal of events as well as the style and mechanics of the world and puzzles. Nothing was too hard to figure out and most of the solutions were fairly logical in nature.

I did have trouble with realizing there are certain mechanics that I didn't think would be part of the game. For example, when you have to clean a smudged piece of glass to see what's inside. The way the game plays, I did not get a single hint that I could just wipe it with the mouse, I was waiting to discover a cloth or something because the game trained me to always look for an item to use on an object, with the only exceptions being for pushing, pulling and flipping lids or switches. I was like "oh, I had not a hint that I could do that." Maybe that's a me thing, but really it's a tiny issue for me.

Another point is that the long camera moves can slow the experience down when I want to just put an object in a thing but I have to wait for the minicutscene to complete. Again, a tiny thing, but this permeated through much of the game so I noticed this small annoyance alot more.

I appreciate the hint system, it doesn't force you to look up how to continue, it gives enough of a hint to steer you in the right direction without giving you the solution straight up. I did turn the hints off through most of the game because I really didn't like clicking on something and the game telling me something I could have figured out myself. The big question mark hints where you are actively asking for a hint is nice, not the ones where you are just clicking on stuff and the game tells you what to do. I think 2 seperate settings to separate the two ideas would be nice.

I really like the slow build near the end when you start to see that you've nearly completed the game, it feels very satisfying and exciting to lead to the end from there. Every time you finish a room, it felt like "yeah, progress." That feeling when you notice something but know you don't have the information or tools to complete the puzzle, and then you find that thing an hour later, is exciting and this game had a lot of moments like that for me.

I loved the dollhouse aesthetic and the slow deterioration of said doll house, it made for the creepy atmosphere to sit with you as you played without ever outright scaring you.

I appreciate the game subtly letting you know you're done with a segment by simply not letting you get closer to it anymore, to inspect it. It saves time and you don't feel like the information is overwhelming as you wonder what is done and what only seems like is done.

It's a pretty good game that I'd recommend if you've played the previous "The Room" games or are just looking for a nice, aesthetically engaging puzzle game to spend 5-7 hours on.\t\t\t\t\t
