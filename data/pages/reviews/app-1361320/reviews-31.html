\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is specially made for those who like to get all the achievements in games (minor gameplay spoilers ahead).

Description:
The Room 4: Old Sins is an indie 3D puzzle and exploration game that blends point and click with hidden object gameplay and is set in a supernatural/mystic universe of horror.
It’s the last game in the series and doesn’t really change the game formula much, but this time, the game takes place inside an attic and you have to solve a very intricate puzzle “house” with several different rooms inside it.

Like the other games before it, the graphics were remastered, and now the game uses modern textures and lighting effects that look amazing. The game areas are very realistic, with lots of details like hidden buttons and elaborate patterns and textures, and each room has a different theme or function. The few cutscenes the game offers look nostalgic and remind me of the games of the early 00’s.

The gameplay stayed almost the same, the puzzles are well designed, logical and intuitive and you can figure out where most objects go easily most of the time, if you pay attention to the details. Like The Room 3, it has a game mechanic where you go inside some of the models, creating a system of puzzles within puzzles you’ll have to solve.
This time, the game seems a bit less linear because you’ll have to solve puzzles while 2 or 3 rooms are active at the same time.

Each time I got stuck was because an item or mechanism was very well hidden, and I didn’t notice it. The game still has a hint system that starts with general advice and gets more specific each time you ask for more hints. This feature should only be used as a last resort to keep the game interesting.

Unlike The Room 3, there aren’t different endings depending on what you do, just a single canon one.


Achievements:
How many: 9 Achievements.
How much time: Around 3 hours
Game difficulty: Easy, most of the time.
Achievements’ Difficulty: Easy, completing the game grants all the achievements.


Overall opinion:
The Room 4 is a 3D puzzle game with P&C and HOG gameplay. The story is set in a supernatural horror universe and is interesting and very well written. It has good graphics and very well designed and elaborate rooms and puzzles.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Room 4: Old Sins manages to deliver a title that is fully realised, intricate, suspenseful and most of all, personal. The 4th adaptation of the series tells a whole new story set in the pre-established universe that expands the lore and uses its environmental storyteling to analyze how the Null affects humanity, and the people that go after it, like the protagonist of the trilogy. It has minimalistic yet powerful narration and writing that entice you to explore further for answers and gateways to even more questions. For the gameplay, the developers fell back on the mechanics the trilogy had set out to perfect, only implementing some rather small innovations but focusing more on the presentation and pacing of the title. The length doesnt overstay its welcome, and leaves you content with the amount of gametime you spent traversing the elaborate and scenic rooms of the family dollhouse. The narrative presents clever twists and exciting revelations whilst accompanied with a puzzle playground that questions your observation and intellect. It's my favorite title in the series which is impressive for an indie project that revolves around the same premise, albeit each crack at the formula is deeper and better than the one that came before it!\t\t\t\t\t
