\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough the other games were great as well, this one is definitely the best of the four. The first one had the effect of being the first, and for that it's great. It didn't try to meddle around lore too much, and it stuck to the "stuck in a room" premise very well. But it gets minus points for being short, it was one of those games you could play to completion and refund it. Although for its quality, you probably wouldn't do it.

The second one deviated a little bit by including other areas as well, and while it was good to see new sceneries it felt like it strayed away from the original premise. The third game I think is the worst one, although still brilliant mind you, because it leaned too heavily on the story of it all. It even gave you multiple endings, but at the end you couldn't even make any sense of it. The third one also had a lot of backtracking, and traversing between the different areas was slow enough to deem it an unnecessary use of time.

But this fourth one though, it took all the good bits of the other games and executed it the best way it could. Like the first game, you're stuck in a single room, but at the same time using clever lore bits you are also exploring entirely different areas. The story in this game doesn't feel dragged out either, but rather compelling. The puzzles themselves are good as well, and I was never stuck like in some of the other games. The hints did help me when I was having problems, but using them wasn't a necessity. And best of all, I feel like this could be played without knowledge of the other games. It's just plain old puzzle fun. Rest assured, I have no qualms.\t\t\t\t\t
