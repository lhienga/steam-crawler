\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been quite some time since I played any of the "The Room" series games, so I won't really be able to draw any meaningful comparison to the previous chapters, but I must admit that I thourughly enjoyed playing this fourth installment. It felt considerably longer than the previous episodes a around 5/6 hours of gameplay. As always, the artistic and techincal side of the game are really top notch with very inspiring visuals and great interaction mechanics with the in-game objects. What I felt that was a bit lacking this time is a bit of complexity in the riddles, I was able to solve them quickly without resorting to any walkthrough video and just a couple of in-game hints. I won't say that this is a bad thing per se, but for sure it didn't feel particularly rewarding. It seems to me as well as the game had to be a bit rushed at the end, so that the last rooms (yes, there's not just *a* room) are quite fast to complete. Finally, the ending didn't impress me gameplay-wise as much as it did with its visuals.
Overall I'd give this game a solid 8.5/10 and I'll be very happy to replay it again in a year or so when I will have forgotten how to solve the riddles.\t\t\t\t\t
