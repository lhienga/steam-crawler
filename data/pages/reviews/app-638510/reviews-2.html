\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot a fan.

I like the city builder/manager concept.

but I HATE that the game randomizes the requirements for buildings each run AND randomizes the benefits that you get from buildings. It also randomizes the tech tree.

This just feels like an awful combination of input AND output randomization. Randomizing the events and maps would be enough to make each run feel interesting and unique, but randomizing the materials needed to build a specific building, the tech tree, and the benefit of a building--makes "learning" the game impossible.

Basically, the game includes SO MUCH randomization that it makes the use of words and English moot.

For example, an apothecary should always be built with ## number of X materials; it should always provide Y benefit; and it should always be unlocked by previously knowing Z and Q. By having this be reliable, player learning occurs and strategies can be built. Randomize 1 of these 3 things is frustrating and arbitrary (not fun). Randomizing ALL of these qualities of an apothecary, and the "apothecary" building is now just a name attached to a picture and is otherwise meaningless.

You basically have to relearn the entire game every time you load it because everything about the game will change. It's anti-fun, anti-strategy, and anti-learning.

I feel like the Devs here just tried something random and new, and they didn't actually look at what has worked/didnt work in other games. They also don't seem to know a thing about input vs. output randomness in game design.

TLDR: I find the game unplayable. There is no ability to strategize and plan when literally everything you learned last run is now reset and randomized to something new.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, a relevant and unique pun for a title is so refreshing. Second, I love a game that keeps you coming back to try different strategies and learn more. Third, when you "finish", a turn, a prophecy or even a game, there are still elements left that you want to explore. New memories, new buildings, new protagonist hero city builders. Just finished my first game on normal difficulty after 5 or 6 tries and had a great time. dotAGE feels like a city builder, sort of, and also a "card" game, sort of (imagine unique buildings with interdependent stats and requirements as "cards"), and maybe also a chess-like board game, sort of, where the pieces (buildings) fit together on a limited board in a myriad of rule governed ways. I'm having a great time with it. Kudos to the developer also, who seems to be very attentive on the discussion forums and ended up fixing a problem I reported within a few days. Looking forward to playing this game some more!\t\t\t\t\t
