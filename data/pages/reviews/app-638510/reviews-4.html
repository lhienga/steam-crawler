\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's great. Scratches a civ itch and city builder itch while still being turn-based (so it doesnt have that hectic RTS feel of most city builders) Some complaints I read mentioned having some things locked behind progression - I've only had that happen once in my first 4 runs (I needed wood planks), and in two more runs I will have unlocked almost everything. I'm sure the dev's will address this as they refine the game.

Each run you feel you are getting better and smarter with your choices. Each run requires making hard choices and potentially pivoting your strategy to the "next tidal wave threat". Makes for an interesting experience.

The game has more potential as and if they add more "leaders" and/or ways to alter game play (e.g., akin to the skull system in halo where you can make things harder).

improvement thoughts:
1) I don't know if one exists, though I'd be curious if a high cost trading post were available (eg, trade 7 wood for 1 berry), which might help save some runs from utter failure.
2) rewind a turn (or two or three) button to see if you can save a dire situation -> this might also quiet some of the complainers who feel the game can be unfair (though I argue its just bad luck or bad planning on their part!)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, but.... also no, at least for me because there is no replay-ability (again, for me) as I have zero interest in re-doing the same thing to achieve better stats, and there is a significant time-sink required.

Pros:
* Super creative game, very enjoyable, both graphics and written content - great job.
* I honestly had no freaking clue what was going on the first couple play-throughs, but I kept going back without looking it up. For me, it was an OK aspect (figuring things out as you go) -- although I did have to eventually look up a few things or I would never have finished a single run. I did NOT know you could change jobs for your people, and that ended up killing me over and over. I looked up the basics after failing several times, and finished the first run.
* Love the sense of humor

Cons:
This is a game that requires a lot of paying attention to many things. Like, you need to be focused on this or else armageddon. And with that said, the playthrough is TOO long for an average person. Only reason I could spend the time on this game is because I'm down with a hip injury at the moment, and I was genuinely curious about this game (as in, what exactly IS this, lol). For a normal person, there is no way in hell they can dedicate that much time and focus to finish the game in one sitting. And if you pick it up few days later, you won't remember what the hell you were doing in the first place. I have elephant's memory and I enjoy games like this one, however, for someone like my partner this would be a nightmare on wheels, especially with breaks. By making the playthrough this long it alienates gamers who would thoroughly enjoy this IF it was shorter. I probably would have not played beyond the first 8 hours if I wasn't couching it. Too much investment, too repetitive, not enough rewards. The achievement "win a game with Matus" is only at 19% of all players - meaning 4 out of 5 people playing dropped off before actually experiencing the game. That's a lot of people who lost interest and couldn't/wouldn't finish even the initial playthrough.

* End game content: really underwhelming. Boo. Expected a lot more, judging by the writing and creativity in general. I was extremely disappointed, especially after running a "perfect" playthrough (all good events) that took a while.

Suggestions:

Create a tutorial (optional) for people who don't have the time to fail endless times because they don't know about some basic in-game mechanic. I'm all for exploration and learning on your own, but there are some things in the game that I still didn't know about, even after playing for 20 hours. I got to armageddon on my own, killed one god on my own, killed the second (by luck), third one and last one got me. After that, I went to look things up as it was getting way too time consuming. And after looking things up, there was no way in hell I would have figured it out on my own, as it wasn't intuitive or obvious in any way, even with endless clicking and reading everything.

Create shorter playthrough options. I know that's easier said than done, but I honestly will not be playing this again knowing the overall timesink. If I knew I can finish the game in three hours (without having to join the PRO team first), then all the unlocked techs and new ways to figure things out would be very appealing.

Please add some end-game content. I was super disappointed with the ending.

Overall, this is one of the most bizarre, fun, creative games I've ever played. It reminded me of good old days when I played games on Atari, there were a lot of "gems" back in the day when individuals could create an entire experience for the platform, and it showed. These days non-rpg games really bore me to tears for the most part as I've seen and played them all, and it's all the same crap with different candy wrap. So, kudos to you for developing this. I did show this game to my partner, he basically said WTFLOL am I looking at (same initial reaction as I had), and then stated it's obviously a perfect game for me (and definitely not for him, way too much going on). If the playthrough was shorter, I would definitely come back for some brain workout.

Edit: played after the last patch. So many things way better! But it still takes forever to play through, and it gets super tedious.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is pretty hard, but not as hard as I found some others in the genre. I won my first playthrough on Hard but it was close.

Overall it was very fun. I love the art style and theme. The town planning elements were alright and I can imagine how different runs would play differently. One thing that nearly lost me the game is that you can retrain your pips!!! right clicking them with shift allows you to give up their job. I was way over population because I thought the only way to get a new basic pip was to make a pippin.

The fact that I was still able to win the run without this really important feature until the last few turns is very generous of the developer! Some technologies were actually not useful which was a little confusing. The game suggests you make use of the seasons which was quite nice :) And the ability to teardown and rebuild my town pretty quickly if I saw some issue forthcoming was nice. Don't want to give too many spoilers but highly recommend!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall I really like the game--it has a fun gameplay loop and very inventive way of handling a civilization/colony type game in a new way!

The only thing I'm not a huge fan of is how long the games go on. It feels /excessively/ long, especially when you consider that if you fail you have to start all over again. My longest run was about 8 hours, and I got maybe 1/3 of the way through the prophecy, before something catastrophic happened and I had to start over. I don't mind games where failure means starting over (I have like 300 hours in Spelunky alone), but it does feel discouraging in dotAGE to start again after sinking an entire workday of time into something, only to have it ruined after /one/ failed event. It'd be nice if there was an option to set a game length at the beginning and choose to have a shorter run or a longer one, although I'm guessing there'd have to be a lot of rebalancing of numbers and events to accommodate this. I understand I can change the difficulty level to something easier, but I don't /want/ to change the difficulty level, I like the normal difficulty level--I just want a shorter run time over all.\t\t\t\t\t
