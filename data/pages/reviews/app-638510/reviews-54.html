Always Obey Your Elders.

Some days I'm willing to struggle against the RNG, punished for every small mistake, and other days, I just want a chill experience. I appreciate that Dotage allows you to experience either thanks to its difficulty settings, transforming from a claw-your-way-up tale of survival into a cutesy village simulator with some hilarious and bizarre random events. Either way you decide to play, it's a great time. I'm playing on Steam Deck and, though there's no controller support, Dotage works just fine right out of the box; the text is completely legible and I'm not having to fiddle with anything to make it less awkward to control. Woot.

Dotage is adorable and the writing is hilarious, but beneath its silly and charming exterior lurks a challenging survival game. Your small tribe consists of the Elder, who has lived through many crisis before and is trying his hand at surviving this one, and the Pips he commands. The premise of the game is that this Elder has visions of the apocalypse and with his guidance and careful attention to your growing population's resources, you can prevail. Your Pips can build, gather resources, and operate increasingly complex buildings. The longer you survive in game, the more your Elder remembers and therefore more tech and random events you unlock. You can also research new technology, which is fairly standard to the genre. I liked that this is turn-based, meaning you can assign your Pips to tasks at your leisure rather than feeling like you're racing against the clock to preemptively deal with a series of small fires.

The needs of your village grow as your technology advances, meaning your Pips will no longer be satisfied with simple huts and will begin to crave leisure activities. Plus you'll need to specialize their jobs in order to create more complex items like cooked food and tools. The steady flow of upgrading from a ragtag group of buildings and foraging for food to a bustling, self-sufficient metropolis is very satisfying.

Food and building materials are your main resources to worry about though, depending on the Domain threat which corresponds with the apocalyptic events that play our during the course of your run, you'll also have to contend with creating heat, hope, and nature. You get plenty of warning ahead of Doom events and can even change the outcome of some of them by having your Pips produce resources to combat them. For example: if a cold event is going to savage your town, you would create heat. This removes some of the RNG reliance, but that isn't to say you're guaranteed a good outcome. Sometimes the game will throw a wrench in your plans by escalating the threat level, meaning the negative resources will build up much more quickly. If your buildings aren't up to snuff in terms of production, you can easily be overrun and get a negative outcome.

Things can—and will—go wrong. Buildings will catch fire, Pips will get sick, and the seasons will be unkind (particularly winter where you can no longer farm and hunted animals no longer replenish), but it never feels unfair or like you don't have a way to at least bounce back from a tragedy. This is the sort of game where you'll say "I'll just finish up research for this tech and then I'll head to bed" and then look up and realize it's 3AM—and I mean that in the best of ways. If you're looking for a simulation/strategy game with roguelike elements, Dotage is well worth the asking price. Support this solo dev!

dotAGE a board game video game

I don't usually review games on Steam, but to support the superb work of this lone developer, here's one.

dotAGE is literally a board game where you don't need to spend time setting up the board as it's a video game.
You play both with and against the rules of the world because the management of the colony aims at its survival against all odds caused by the changing seasons and various prophetic events. Well managed they will benefit you, on the contrary if you fail to take charge of them they will punish you. The game's really good idea is the use of turn-based time, because it allows you to handle any of these situations in a calm manner, and there are plenty of them.
It also brilliantly handles the management dimension, in that it's more a question of day-to-day (or rather turn-to-turn) stock management, with great flexibility and a strong planning dimension in which the player has to think about the colony's future development needs. Sprinkle it all with a roguelite dimension that unlocks mechanics as your games progress and you get a little nugget of a game that while being fun ultimately turns out to be more complicated than it would appear at first glance and first hour of play as well as high replayability. Go for it!

PS: It just seems that the translation for the French language (and maybe the orthers) needs to be revised in places but it is surely only a matter of time and in no way prevents us from understanding how the game works.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a lot of fun! I really like village/city builder games, but a lot of them don't strike the right balance for me. Either they are too intense like RTS games, or they are too open ended with no real goals. Dotage has the perfect balance. There's a lot of resources to manage, optimize, and grow, but you also have explicit objectives to work towards that justify all that work.

I played a lot of the old demo and already fell in love with the game. With the full release, the systems got a lot more fleshed out and look a lot more interesting to dive into. With each playthrough I'm finding more ways to optimize and improve my small village, and hopefully turn into a thriving city!

If you like city builders and especially like the strategy in designing your city to get the most out of it, then I definitely recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't remember last time I've got so addicted with a game so quickly. I play very little city builders and this game has excellent tutorial build in for people like me. I could feel pretty quickly that this game was made with love and care. The dev is also very active on the game's Discord and fix important bugs quickly and discuss everything with players.

The game is really polished and the price is perfect for the amount of content available. Almost 30 hours in and I just finished my first run. Not because it required that much time, but I was managing more than 120 people(pips) and tried to make the best city with well placed buildings and not just win the game ASAP because it could be achieved with half of the people and way faster. I loved the turn-based aspect so I could think as long as I wanted and was not forced at all to make decisions quickly so I was taking my time with planning buildings placement.

I managed to win the game with my first playthrough on Normal difficulty, but I saw a lot of people saying that they had problems with winning the game because it was too hard to them. And you know what? There are 2 more difficulty levels waiting for you in case the game would be too easy for you. There is also option to play on very easy mode if you just want to focus on city building part without thinking that much about losing.

I was constantly unlocking new buildings / events / improvements / etc through my 30 hours playthrough and after that time I've unlocked maybe 50-60% of the stuff available. Think about how much time I would need to play to test all the strategies / see all events myself if just unlocking it takes so much time! It didn't feel grindy btw and I was unlocking new stuff pretty often during that time. Even 100 hours would not be enough to test all possible routes, especially that currently there are 4 Elders and each of them makes you play in a different way!

I can definitelly recommend this game!

Of course it still has some minor bugs like for example text going outside the boxes if you have too many resources, but they didn't break the game experience for me at all.

There are just 3 things that I didn't like:
1) When you end a day (turn) there is an animaiton of all resources floating towards / from all buildings, pips, etc and with bigger village(60-70+ pips) it takes 10 seconds or even more to complete because there is so much stuff using these resources. Currently there is no way of skipping it, but the dev said that he is working on it.
2) When you produce resources over your current limit, then there will be an animation of these extra resources getting destroyed one by one. The problem is even more problematic when you have for example 6-7 resources over the limit, because you will watch every single one of these resources getting destroyed one after another (and different resources will be waiting to be destroyed before previous resources are destroyed). Please add option to speed this up ^^
3) Some events makes your pips sick, mouring, stunned, etc (give them negative status). This is not a problem at all with smaller village, but when I've had 100+ pips then this event was causing 20 of my pips to get sick which is not a problem on it's own. The problem is that the game highlight every single one of these pips one by one, taking like 10-15 seconds to complete this highlighting animation. I wish there was a way of speeding this up.

With having the way of skipping / speeding up the 3 issues mentioned above, the game would be perfect for me <3\t\t\t\t\t
