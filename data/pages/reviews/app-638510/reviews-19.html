\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery neat little gem of a game that I had no idea existed. If you like Against the Storm you will definitely like this. The intense balancing act of managing your villagers needs and the needs of the village itself are done very well here. Surprisingly in depth and a large amount of buildings/materials/jobs to work with. The fact that it's turn based instead of an RTS is perfect for this game too.

It is very challenging, but my only real complaints would be that you have to unlock so many buildings/jobs/traits etc before the whole of the game really opens up for you. Also although the gameplay loop is incredibly fun and addicting, it is a little bit of a long haul as far as the time required to complete a single run takes. This might not bother you at all, I like most of my city builder games to be in depth and time consuming for the most part anyways, worth mentioning though.

Definitely going to be playing a lot more of this though. If you're looking for a new strategy/city builder game that gives you the "oh just one more turn.." feeling like Civ, you'll definitely like this.\t\t\t\t\t

Where did this come from?, meanwhile some children in another village just died of starvation. The above would be the ideal outcome, yet in this game you could be on either side of that "gift". There's just enough humor and squeaking by options to keep one from rage quitting, this game has all the psychological tricks at play to keep you playing one more turn. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an excellent game! (And by a solo dev!)

It has all of the elements of a simple turn based city builder type game, but a well thought out eventing and progression system. It reminds me of a turn-based version of Rimworld, but the cool part is that you can strategize to influence the events in your favor.

There's also a decent amount of replay value with different starting characters, randomized tech tree that changes what you can build, and a roguelite element that unlocks "memories" that further expand the game.

It is very well executed and I could see this being a huge game if more people catch on to it.

tl;dr It's as if rimworld and civilization had a low-poly mutant baby. It's delightfully brutal and strategic.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel I should add my voice to the chorus and enthusiastically recommend this game. If you've played Against the Storm and are hankering for more, look no further. dotAge is a roguelike colony manager with endless replayability. Each elder (staring character/faction) has a fairly unique play style and the tech tree is randomized each run, keeping each game fresh and exciting. Having beat the game once, I feel that the difficulty is very well balanced--quite a challenge, but fair and rewarding. The omen mechanic ensures that poor planning is punished harshly, which also serves the purpose of keeping bad runs short.

A few complaints:
- I've encountered some bugs, mostly due to certain screens becoming unresponsive, but nothing game breaking. Disappointing and interruptive when they appear, but nothing a quick restart won't fix.
- After a few hours in a single session, the game starts to stutter pretty noticeably, especially once you start getting to 50 pips or so. Restarting the game seems to fix this, albeit temporarily.
- The Agepedia (the in-game almanac/glossary) is fairly opaque when it comes to certain mechanics. You're honestly better off finding out through experience what any particular thing does (and probably lose as a result). It could do with some additional detail.

All said, an excellent addition to the burgeoning genre of roguelike city builders. Recommend!\t\t\t\t\t
