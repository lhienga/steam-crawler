\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredible. The runs are long, but the mix of progression, worker placement, proactive planning vs reactive gameplay and absolutely charming visuals creates such a fun and engaging experience. I love to hate the feeling of the planning getting away from me as we get to later acts in the scroll and my town begins to sprawl more and more.

There are lots of opportunities to micromanage your pips here, but none of it feels tedious to me, personally. I'm a gamer with a really low patience for tedium in games and this game somehow makes even the smallest of management tasks enjoyable.

One thing I particularly enjoyed is the art style, the game is just oozing with character. I especially love the little prophet and how he receives visions and goes all wonky-eyed while the screen flashes and the entire village rushes to him. It brings a huge smile to my face every time!

Welcoming but strategically deep, this game is amazing.

The demo is free, and I would encourage everyone to at least try it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDotage is a long and merciless game...

Dotage is a rougelite life sim where you have to guide an elder and 'pips', the name of the little villagers, to make crucial decisions to dodge randomized chaotic events caused by the 'domains'. Think you can play god well enough to bring everyone to safety and make it through the ever evolving events of cataclysm, sickness, fear, and the weather and finally live in peace?

The beauty of Dotage is that the game starts out fairly simple. You resource gather to build more structure that in return, provide better production of resources both tangible, like food, wood, and rock, and intangible, like hope and nature. Perhaps you need to have more hands at work or train them in the ways of a paper maker, or maybe they just need to gather some herbs or seeds to plant to have food for the altar, or maybe they need to hunt before the winter strikes.

Each and every adventure is randomized in some way. From the layout of the board, to the the creatures romping about, to some of the items you research; there is variety to be had with every run. The roguelite aspect comes from retrieving the elder's memories that unlocks even more buildings and resources but also more events and quirks that can make or break a run, so the game over time continuously evolves even within the current run, and those memories stay as unlocked for future apocalypses.

You already have someone who can see the future threats ahead. Easy, right? Not so fast. The complexity rise, the threats become more vicious, and the omens come about regardless, with no way to bend it's outcome, so adapting on the fly, is crucial to success. Dotage takes proper planning, and with the elder's prophecy, you can work together finally achieve peace.

The game's aesthetic is that of classic pixelated games, and it's got some charming animations; simple yet effective. The pips are charming, albeit a little cute and even hilarious. They're goofy little messes sometimes, like you're taking care of children. The voice lines are amazing, and I have them on repeat in my head all the time. The music, from what I heard, is based off of classic medieval tunes, and honestly, the OST, only consisting of 8 pieces, are amazing and really get you in the zone.

So overall, this might be one of my first life sim games, but it's so good, and I'm upset I didn't find this sooner. I would highly recommend to anyone fans of life sims. Give this a spot in your library.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like civ style games without war?
Are you running on integrated graphics?

This is the game for you.

EDIT: After continuing to play this game for another 10 hours or so, I have to give it a thumbs down at least for the time being. The game is very buggy and contains many, many inconsistencies:

- The Seed Field makes reference to seeds being available "in every season!", but the seed fields are restricted to summer and autumn.
- All fields have the description "Buildable: Fertile". When you hover your mouse over it, it says "Can only be built on fertile land." This is not true. It CAN be built on fertile land. It's not required.
- Being able to remove specialties from pips is never covered in the tutorial. I had to find it in the forums to figure out how to do it.
- Things are inconsistently named. For example, some buildings which are built on silver terrain (stone pavement according to the research menu) is called "cobblestone" by the buildings. Why do buildings call it cobblestone and the research menu calls it stone?
- The game is improperly balanced. The beginning of the game is very hard, but once you get enough research, buildings, and memories - the last year of the game is totally pointless because you can generate tons of the required resources needed to autopilot everything with little management.
- Some buildings are extremely underpowered. For example, one of the totems for the shaman which generates research gives 1 research + 1 for each animal next to it. Given that it's very rare to have more than 2 animals nearby and this costing 12 research to get makes it wildly unusable.
- Some buildings require "cobblestone pathways to dwelling". That's not... what it really means. You can build dwellings that are on cobblestone but will not work for the building. It's also easy to misinterpret. I thought it meant that it had to be built next to a dwelling built on cobblestone (for bougie people). Also NOT TRUE. What it's REALLY saying is that your town center has to be built on cobblestone and it doesn't matter where your town center is. Very poor wording. "Requires cobblestone town center" - fewer characters and easier to understand. Please fix it.

And on and on and on... the game is in sore need of balancing and fixing many typos and inconsistencies. After a while of dealing with what the UI says and what it actually means, I gave up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is not as absurdly difficult as some of the reviews make it out to be. The game does punish you harshly for your mistakes but I don't necessarily believe that is a bad thing. It's not really RNG-based, you can completely avoid any RNG if you prepare well enough. The only RNG present is in the omens which are uncontrollable events but usually their effects aren't very severe. You can see every single event that is going to happen in the near future and what threat it relates to using the prophecy page, so the game doesn't just throw random ♥♥♥♥ at you. If you pay attention to the prophecy then you can easily beat the game on normal difficulty. However, on higher difficulties it can be a bit tough because you have to plan many many turns in advance and the prophecy only reveals the events up until the next doom event, but I might just be bad idk I haven't played quite enough to master everything.

Other than difficulty I love that the game tries to keep itself refreshing in between runs and doesn't throw all the content at you at once. You unlock all of the content gradually using the "memories" which I think is a fantastic way to introduce the player to all of the mechanics of the game. The tech tree also shuffles a bit between each run which keeps the game pretty interesting.

Overall definitely a great game! It's comparable to Against the Storm which is also a roguelite city builder, except dotAGE is turn-based while Against the Storm is real-time.\t\t\t\t\t
