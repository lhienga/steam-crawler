\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's definitely a must buy if you love resource management games. It has an incredible depth that has continued to suprise me even after year 1. The complexity thankfully ramps up at an even pace so you don't feel overwhelmed at the beginning. No promises about a few seasons onwards though! The turn based nature of the game allows you to relax and enjoy the game at a casual pace but at the same time the "events" add great tension and stress for the needed challenge of how you will plan to manage your resources and build your little village. The only complaint that I have is that the technology tree is very unclear on how immediately useful a building is, such as the barley being completely useless until you unlock the next two techs down the line. That's only a minor complaint and, without it, this game would be as perfect as a game can be.

Tip: Boost population early. Having the production of basic foundational resources and food locked down by having enough people to handle it makes a huge difference in how you can tackle the game's challenges.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHE best colony survival game, in my opinion. The interface is perfect. The designer did not forget QOL stuff like easy-to-read fonts and keyboard remapping. (SO MANY designers do forget these things!) It has an amazing mix of sandbox freedom and directed play (in the form of frequent challenges you must overcome). Surviving those challenges helps you unlock the roguelike meta progression, which is a brilliant way to ease you into the vast complexity of the game. And, like all good colony games, it has the most evil one-more-turn syndrome you can possibly imagine! Dot Age is very easy to pick up and very, very hard to put down.

I have noticed that some starting techs (which are random) seem a lot easier to work with than others. That's probably a mix of my own failure to pay attention and unlucky map generation. Just be aware that sometimes it really is best to cut your losses if you're clearly getting off to a bad start. Remember where you went wrong, and you're bound to have a better run next time! (Fail to learn from your mistakes, and you're doomed -- which is very much the plainly stated theme of the whole game.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter the tutorial and a wipe or two you will generally get the hang of all the mechanics needed for a longer play through. It's a relatively straightforward worker placement, turn based game. While it may feel like a colony type game at first with the aspect of a random map generally dictating how you will go about your resource gen layout, it quickly becomes a bit of a spreadsheet game without the spreadsheets. You will find yourself staring at your resource income/decline each turn and using that info to dictate shifts in worker placement and figuring out what to build next. So far I like the roguelite unlocking of mechanics and rng aspects. 16 hours in, most of that on my 2nd play on normal difficulty, and it's never felt overly punishing. Perhaps a little bit too much, by the 4th prophecy page it's starting to feel a little repetitive, as if I'm just waiting for bad stuff to happen to make it more interesting.

I would love to see a bit more personality to your pips for RP fun, the trait system feels a little clunky and unintuitive as it stands now. Also once you start hitting higher numbers of pips, the traits are even more worthless as they just become generic workers, their usefulness and importance completely dictated by their job specialization. That being said, I still have a lot of unlocks to go, so that maybe change as I get more stuff. So far it seems like money well spent for me, mad props to the Dev for their work. You can tell a lot of love went into this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn absolute gem of a game. If you enjoy worker-placement type boardgames but either wish there were a solo version with automated "upkeep", or you enjoy doing stuff like straining resources to the absolute limit, greeding until your entire village is about to starve before you hit a crucial tech tripling your food production efficiency - then, finally, "allow" your pips to start producing food, this game is absolutely for you. The absolute chill vibes of this game means that it's also possible to just tune down the difficulty and play it as a peaceful cute village builder, too.

I really enjoyed the way the randomized tech tree worked and how different elements interact with each other. For my first run, I relied on wells surrounded by tomatoes (huge production bonus when next to water production buildings) as my main source of food. For the second run, I discovered I did not have the option to build water wells, but instead had to rely on water springs built in mountains. Without easily placed water production buildings, my tomatoes now had to compete with Heat-mitigating buildings (puddles, double production next to water production) AND stoneworks (needs to be placed next to mountains). The elder I was playing as had a special effect that killing animals increased fear, so I am forced to choose between efficient farmland, efficient heat-mitigation, fear-mitigation + meat, or rush other farming options that was not as water-reliant. A single difference in building options meant I had to rethink my entire approach and tech path to the map.

The domain management mechanic was also very well implemented, it starts off fairly simple with pip-labor converting straight up into mitigation, then later in the game you start having buildings with cross-effects, like metal smelters producing heat. This extra heat is helpful during winter, but becomes an additional strain on your heat-mitigation during summer. Combine this with all the stuff happening during season changes, events and threat strength increases, the stuff you have to juggle around constant changes during a run.

The vibes are great as well, I love watching the pips run around doing their stuff, and the wild animals hopping around and going to sleep. The animation and how events are presented is great, and whimsical but not over the top. You can watch the awkward looks on your pips when someone's joke falls flat, or the sickly pip you have in your village gets hit by disease for the third time this game, throwing up all over the place, or just everyone running back home at days end. It's a game that's very nice to look at.

One thing I would particularly like to mention is how nice and polished the first playthrough felt. New mechanics are revealed one by one, tutorial is not too hand-holdy to the point where you have no agency but you know generally what to do next, and how the domains are revealed and handled along with the backstory of the game. You also unlock "memories" (meta-progression unlocking new buildings, traits, mechanics, stuff) during the playthrough, some cool stuff you can use immediately, some you have to wait till the next run.

There were a couple of issues but nothing too significant: Sometimes the tooltips glitched and displayed hints for the previous message, it can appear that nothing is happening while the game waits for pips to run to their destination for an event, profession management can be a little finicky at times (Cauldron requires cook, cook is picking berries (doable by any job), watcher is idle. You need to manually click the cook off the berry, click the cook onto the cauldron, then click the watcher onto the berry.) Also, sometimes runs can feel "won" and stabilized a long while before the games technically supposed to end, but the new doomsday mechanic in development is supposed to handle this. Basically, I was a bit surprised by how well the game just worked so soon after release.

In general, it's obvious a lot of love and thought went into crafting both the mechanics and the visuals. I highly enjoyed my time with this game, and am looking forward to where the dev takes this game in the future. Absolutely recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a big boardgamer this game scratches many itches for me.
It's a worker placement game combined with a city builder, many good roguelike aspects, good diversity of challenges.
At its price and also considering the generous demo that also seamlessly transitions into the real game if you decide to buy it, it is definitly a steal.
Also know that this is a solo project of love, reminiscent of stardew valley and it is of the same quality.

There are some issues but they are small and far between, like the turn end calculation slowing down once you are at ~70+ pips or onions beeing plantable next to each other even though they shouldn't be able to and then they rot away.
Those are small things that will be fixed and don't take away from the enjoyment of the game.

If you like city builders, worker placement boardgames or both do yourself a favour and at least try the demo\t\t\t\t\t
