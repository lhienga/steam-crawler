\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's turn-based Against the Storm, but if it were more akin to a tile placement game. The spritework is charming, the amount of depth and possibilities make it well-replayable, and it's still constantly in development and balancing.

The audio is buggy as all hell, and admittedly as nice as the soundtracks are, it'd be nicer if they weren't intermittently cutting out or switching to a random one completely out of mood of the situation. I had my second run ended prematurely due to an error skipping the final Doom day and missing resolving it entirely, locking me into an infinite Doom cycle.

Despite the bugs, all I can say actually of issues is that it doesn't really respect your time - a full winning run can take days if you're only doing an hour or two at a time. Sometimes it's a lot of "hurry up and wait" between 3-5 turns because you're waiting for an event to hit, especially at the beginning.

Really enjoy it, well done by just a single dev and a couple supports.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile initially fun, the game (or a single run) lasts 2-4 times as long as it should. After ~5 hours I usually have a very solid foundation, and am unlikely to encounter real problems unless they're completely unpredictable. While unpredictable problems are good in some game genres, they are a sign of poor design in management games, so even that wouldn't really help. Winter is a bit troubling because it's not easy to predict which food sources are disabled during it, but once you've survived it, your food chain is bulletproof and you should have so much surplus productive pips that there's no real challenge left.

Sure, you can finish expanding up the tech tree (although you'll run out of things to research before the end of the game if you're running 2-3 tech buildings consistently). But there's no real benefit to doing that. All of the 2nd tier professions, the ones that require special pips to train into them are "balanced" to be only marginally better than the base buildings. Which would be fine if they didn't require 10+ hours of game play to unlock and didn't require more thought from the player to even break even. In management games, one of the elements that needs to be balanced is the complexity or cognitive load on a player. If one building is standalone and the other building requires a three deep production chain and multiple adjacencies, then the other building needs to much better than the standalone building, or else there's no payoff for all the extra work the player is doing, not to mention all the extra work the pips are doing to maintain the production chain.

Ultimately, the early game is much more fun and engaging than the mid & late game in the current state of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is really really cool but could use some polish:
- The music seems to bug out alot from time to time, especially after doom events and day/night transitions.
- Some event cutscenes locks your camera in it's position while it's waiting for pips to get from place to place, making it feel kinda awkward at times (especially if they have to walk a big distance).
- Gravedigger seems kinda bugged in terms of visuals, one time I had him walk out of bounds to bury a pip (like really far) and in another run I had the tools of a dead pip (hunter) hover his gravestone (felt really out of place and a few days later when I got back into the game, another grave was also far off from the graveyard and also had another tool hovering it's gravestone, effectively combining both bugs I just mentioned).
- The knowledge points/meta progression system could use a better menu, It's annoying to have to scroll side ways a ton just to get to the later portions of the tree.
- Lastly, the worst thing that has happened to me so far is after the latest patch, my current and most succesfull run seems to be softlocked. The game is about to play out an event, I have 100% success rate, it rewards me with the knowledge points and when the cutscene is about to play out, nothing happens. The game just stays there, unable to move the camera (can still access menus) Restarting the game didn't fix this issue and I hope future patches can adress something like this because I really don't wanna reset my best run so far (which is already around 4 hours deep) This same issue ended up rewarding me an achievement that was not intended (produce 100 sick resistance in 1 day). Issue fixed within a couple of hours

I see that the game still has alot of active development, but in my opinion there should be a big patch that adresses bug fixing/QoL before introducing even more mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t
