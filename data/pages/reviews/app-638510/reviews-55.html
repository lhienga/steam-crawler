\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimply and utterly brilliant.
Tried the demo for thirty minutes, bought the game. No regrets at all.
It has kept me totally focussed for the past five hours, which for a game of this style and price is a really great experience to have. The whole thing appears very simple, but the way that several systems interact mean that every turn I am having to make at least one trade off of the things that I 'could' do versus the things that I 'should' do, and occassionally the challenges create a demand for things that absolutely have to be done and then I have to figure out what to set back to free up enough toons. That's exactly the sort of system that I was hoping for.
I have no idea if the game is balanced. I would be surprised if there weren't a few change to make, but right now it doesn't worry me at all. Things that I think look a little overpowered often turn out to be deceptively mild in their benefit, taking up more toons to feed the basics into what looks like an OP production chain suddenly makes you sit back and admire how nothing comes for free.
Absolutely worth a try. The demo is so limited in its scope that I suspect it doesn't actually do the game justice, because the moves you can make are relatively obvious and lack that trade-off calculation because you are still stuck with really basic structures. Don't let that fool you, when I pressed the purchase and started again, I suddenly found that I had more freedom and at the same time more management problems to riddle with. Again, what a pleasure to discover.
Recommended without hesitation.\t\t\t\t\t
