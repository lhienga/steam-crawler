\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe short form is for a single-dev citybuilder, especially in early beta, this is incredible.

It's pretty minimalist in style, but beneath the retro veneer is a lot of depth. There's a lot of building styles, a lot of resources and usually at least two ways to get them. There's a ton of unlocks that not only give you more options, but also alternate paths that get randomized in future playthroughs. There's even different elders that come with completely different playstyles. Tired of wood houses? Try leather ones. Too easy? Here, have a malus that procs whenever you kill an animal. Even after you've played through it a few times, the ever-changing tech tree should keep you on your toes for awhile longer.

There are a couple downsides to it, however. The dev purposely built the game to be snowballed. If you fail events it gets harder. If you're on top of everything, it gets easier. This can make things a total slog at the end, where you just mash 'end turn' 50 times because you've already won in every aspect except storyline. It should get better with updates, patricularly in endgame, but there's work to be done.

By their admission they also havn't beaten the hardest modes. In practice this means they're untested, and challenge mode feels like that too. It's still beatable, and very satisfying when you succeed against all odds, but it also means you're subject to some hardcore RNG-screw. Maybe everything lines up perfect and you have the buildings and tech to race through it lightning-fast. Maybe that one unpreventable event just stuns a single pip for a few turns. Or maybe it one-shots several vital buildings, breaks some major bottleneck synergies that you literally need even to rebuild them, and you're set back a season while the damage procs even more events you have to fight off. If you've played Dwarf Fortress, this sequence is Fun, and not in a fair way.

That said, that's almost never a problem up on easy, normal, maybe even 'harder'.

And moreso, for a single developer they are incredibly responsive, particularly on their Discord. This is a game that is fun to start, a little jank in charming ways akin to games from 20 years ago, and poised to get better with time.\t\t\t\t\t
