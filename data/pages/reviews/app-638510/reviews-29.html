\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBig fan of city builders/roguelike/turn based/strategy games so naturally I was interested.
As I was playing the demo I wasn't 100% sold but I went back and saw the reviews so I decided just to buy it and stick with it.
I'm glad I did this game is incredible. Really rewarding and engaging gameplay. It reminds me of Against the Storm but with a turn-based element that makes me love games like Fire Emblem. You can go at a slower pace than Against the Storm so it's not as hectic, but the stakes are just as high.

Each turn needs to be planned carefully, and an eye needs to be kept 50 turns into the future to make sure everything goes as planned. It's rewarding when things go well and the challenge slowly increases throughout a play. The games are long, one can easily last 5+ hours for me.

I think the game is a little buggy in parts but it hasnt affected my gameplay really. Given it's only developed by one person (which is amazing) I would 100% excuse those.\t\t\t\t\t

It's a city builder where you have to manage chain productions. Harvest ressources, transform them in another ressources. Feed everybody,specialise your people, oops Pips, for doing specific tasks like a good old settlers game.
What is original in this game ?
It's a turn by turn game where almost the citybuilder chain production are real time. It gave a board game feeling (and i realy enjoy that point)
Segondly, the disasters system. You know they where comming. You know when they occure, you can anticipate them and counter them (put your Pips on the right building to earn points every turn. If you have more points "good points" than disaste point, you will earn a reward instead of the disaster). It's rewarding when you can manage to avoid these bad events.
Last thing, you will unlock contain by playing trough the game. You spend memory points to access some ressource or some building. It will be available for your next game ( you unlock the fish, they don't appears on the maps, there will be fish on your next map). This will annoy some poeple because it feels like you have to grind the game. Personnaly i enjoy it. It have help me to learn the mecanics. This made me want to play again for trying the unlocked buildings.

The game won't revolutionize the genre but it's fresh and well made. The dev listen the feedback and there is plenty of humorous in the game.\t\t\t\t\t
