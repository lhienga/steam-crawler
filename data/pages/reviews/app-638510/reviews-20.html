\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played a lot of simulation games that range from RTS-style, to turn-based, to grand strategy and old classics like ActRaiser and early Tycoon games and I love strategy games like anything -Tactics, so this game was a great find to me.

Real-time strategy (RTS) style simulations like Factorio and Satisfactory are fun purely in the mania of it all, but the problem stems from A) threats not being threatening enough, or B) you're a factory wizard, but now it's time to megamind everything but there's no opposition, so it feels empty.

Or turn-based strategy games where poor choices/choices made without existing knowledge of the entire game that punishes you the entire way for not already knowing the game means the developers failed to teach you how to play.
This game isn't like either. I've only completed one playthrough at writing this review, but this game has a great New Game+ feature, with full mechanic shifts so buildings, and redrawing plans feels good.

dotAGE focuses more on individuality of your villagers in a way that's both thorough enough to get a warm and fuzzy for your pips, but not overly in depth (like maybe Rimworld, or Dwarf Fortress) where it becomes a nuisance that you have so much control over each individual character.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't have the heart to give this a negative review, but I feel like I should.

tl;dr - to me, the game is definitely not a rogue-anything and created expectations that it didn't fulfill. Here's why:

Reason 1 - run length. My first run is still ongoing, after 15+H (got some idle time clocked in, so my hours played are different). To me, what's fun and a fundamental part in rogue-something games is the unburdened, non-devastating losing and trying again with new strategies. This is absent here.

Reason 2 - difficulty. Game warned me not to play on hard mode for 1st playthrough. Since it advertises as roguelite AND still warns me that it's super punishing, I decided to go with normal difficulty. A mistake. On normal, even without any unlocks, the game is a breeze. Me and my guys are bored to tears with nothing meaningful to do. I'm building stuff for no reason and/or fun just to see what kind of ridiculous amounts of resources can I amass and to see how new buildings work (which I don't need, but what the hell). I could probably just skip turns for another hour and win.

Because of that, I'm kinda disappointed and bored with my run. It's going to be hard to complete it. And it kinda puts me in a deadlock for future runs. If the harder modes are super hard, then I'll be pissed when I lose my 10H run. If they're easy, I'll be bored.

There's a pretty good game here though, charming and with an interesting take on some mechanics (at least on the surface). I just think some fundamental game loop design decisions went wrong here. That or the game should lose the "rogue" tag as to not create expectations for that kind of experience.
