Usually it's easy to lose interest when you lose quickly. But in this game you actually unlock new game content by playing. Thus your next playthrough might be easier because now you can fish. Next time you get new animals which give more resources. Next time you unlock terraforming. The game really has depth and you can only get to it by playing and eventually losing. Obviously you also unlock content that makes it harder again.

But fighting against the four domains is a great concept. By planning ahead as you know which domain strikes next and when you can make your way through. But there's still a lot of randomness so you might get obliterated anyway :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCute building and worker placement game. Had a similar feel to Stacklands for me initially, but with a great deal of depth and unlockable features. This game does involve a fair amount of micromanaging worker placement, be aware.

On your first run you will start with basic buildings and creatures in the environment. By playing a bit, you'll unlock fish and boar and other features, but many of these won't appear (unless you get a lucky event) until you start a new playthrough. It was frustrating to unlock fishing but not have any fish! But the gradual unlocks were generally a nice idea, resulting in new events and interactions. There is also a randomised research tree, with different techs appearing in different playthroughs, which I love the idea of.

I had a lot of fun playing this game, although a full run through on normal difficulty must have taken me 12+ hours, which started to get a bit long and repetitive. Still, when I'm ready to play again, I've unlocked a new elder which should add a different flavour and challenges.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote for the later game: if you go into settings, you can select "quick turns" to speed up the animations. Essential in the late game.

First of all, this game is all about preparation. You know what is coming, and you have the means to prevent it. I found it very rewarding in the early game.

This game is cute. The early game is challenging, but the reward/punishment system is a positive feedback. That means if you do well, the game gets easier (almost to the point of boring). If you start to mess up, very hard to recover. My first play through, I died terribly. My second (normal), I didn't lose to a single event.

I think the game could benefit from some quality of life updates (ability to search through pips, some "smart switching" of pips if you need a handyman to be trained as a new professional). But most importantly I think it needs tuning on its feedback system, especially for mid and late game. It stopped being a challenge. Maybe if there was also a button for "fast forward to next event" because it got to the point where I was just clicking through days.

That was all on normal difficulty. Maybe the harder difficulties amend that.\t\t\t\t\t
