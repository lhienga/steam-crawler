\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr, it's like if Cult of the Lamb and Stacklands had a baby and both agreed to raise the child without the toxicity from their own families. Meanwhile Frostpunk is named as the child's godfather.

- Great resource management sim + city builder with a dash of puzzle (in the form of efficiency) that includes an original system of applying pressure to the player which feels...idk, not natural but really fluid.

- The game is turnbased, you decide what to construct, which jobs are worked and what professions are trained with the goal being survival - Think Frostpunk but not just cold.

- UI is okay but could be cleaned up a bit, took awhile to fully grasp the mechanics. That said once you do understand the basics the rest clicks into play pretty well

- I recommend playing it on normal difficulty till your first fall or winter and then promptly restarting at a higher difficulty. The pace of the game can be a tad slow meaning you won't want to sink a lot of game time into your first run when trying to understand mechanics because it can bite you later. That said, once you do understand the mechanics normal is a bit too easy for the long haul.

- Surprisingly good story
-----------------

Dev, great game, please in the future add a warfare domain and warfare tree that requires the training of guards/soldiers, events can be raids, there can be night attacks for watchmen bonuses to offset, so on. I love what you did here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis here is a gem buried away, deserves to become much bigger than it is at time of writing this.

It's a sort of Civ/dwarf fortress-ish/rimworld-like village builder game, but with the twist you are building up your village whilst tackling ever growing challenges from weather, sickness and so on - with this all taking place with a turn based system were you decide what tasks your villagers (known as pips) should do to balance survival.
Do you use your pips to solely gather food to store for winter? Or do you balance with some researching to help you get healers for your sick pips? Or do you try and avert a doomsday event by getting your pips to socialise? The genius of the loop comes with it being turn based so you have plenty of time to think about it, and you are limited by the number of pips available to do tasks, which inevitably becomes to the key mechanic for the game

The gameplay loop is addictive and challenging as a result, the developers behind the game have clearly put a lot of thought into the gameplay flow and it works so well, with the game getting more and more complex with time at a good curve to keep you wanting to get to the next stage of growth. To top it off, the presentation is a joy, a combination of a decent soundtrack, charming "tamagotchi"-like pixel art and some really nice UI work with a medieval style of typeface.

I am having a blast so far and I look forward to seeing how far I can get my first village - I'm also hoping with time there will be expansions/additions to the game as my one complaint so far is im anticipating the map might start to feel a bit small - I could be wrong though!\t\t\t\t\t

I do, however, have some gripes:
1. The difficulty. The game warns you when starting on Normal that it's a very hard game and to expect to lose. I found normal to be very easy and I never once found myself in even a pinch of trouble.
2. Pacing of unlocks and tech feels off. Again, too easy and quick.
3. The game spends too much time on animations when you have a lot going on. Animations and downtime in between turns needs to be sped up a lot when you have a lot going on. I spend most of my time doing nothing but watching things fly around the screen.
4. There are a lot of little bugs. Nothing terrible or game-breaking.\t\t\t\t\t
