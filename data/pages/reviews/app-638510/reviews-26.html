\r\n\t\t\t\t\tdotAGE is very similar to civ games and is an easy timesink. However I found it quite frustrating, too...

The early game is great, micro-managing, making sure the resources are properly managed, researching, it all works really well. I found myself restarting many games before turn 100 just to get a more smooth progression. Satisfying gameplay.

However the game punishes you extremely hard if you let things down to luck. On turn 220 I let one of the domains have a 90% chance of succeeding, only to fail the luck check, have 26 pips freeze and basically lose the run on that 10% chance. it felt extremely bad to be punished after hours into the playthrough.

After getting past the turn 231 doom event, the game is a breeze. If you made it this far, you've probably got the facilities to handle any threat, and every turn is just a "quickly check numbers are good, ok, skip turn" which because of all of the mini-event animations and day/night cycle animations can take hours to skip through. The endgame is by far the worst part of the game, but I'm sure that has a good chance of being improved down the line. The performance issues weren't that big of a deal to me - I was okay with a bit of stuttering during the huge animations at the end of each turn.

Overall I'd say go play the game, complete it once or twice, and come back to it once the endgame has been balanced.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to enjoy this game, but I just couldn't engage with it. I had tried the demo and felt as though I wanted to try it with the freedom to unlock more of the tech tree options and maybe experience some of the meta-progression before making a decision. Ultimately, I may come back later and try the game again...

For now though, I think I'll give it a pass. For starters it felt like the pacing was slow. Much of the difficulty is based around not knowing what is coming or what the progression is like. Having never played before, I made my best guess of where to start a village without really knowing what would be good later, or how the village boundary expands, or how the villagers don't know how to cook meat...

That was my biggest gripe with the game. It seemed as though the tech tree never quite made sense. I had researched the animal pens and lured chickens into them... they even had chicks on their own! I thought this would help with my food supplies... but the pen just holds the chickens. It doesn't actually allow pips to collect anything from them. I get that it is a game and some kind of progression is needed, but chickens lay eggs, and eggs can be picked up with your bare hands and boiled in water. The villagers seem to have means of collecting water in vessels and making fire (if you research a well and a bonfire)... so boiling an egg seems simple. Well, to get eggs you then need to research an egg farm and then build an egg farm. Then you learn that not just anyone can go to a chicken coop and pick up eggs! You need a farmer for that. You go to train a farmer and it turns out he needs tools, which means researching another building, creating it, and then presumably finding out you need to have fulfilled some other requirement to make tools.

It seems insane to go through so many hoops to consume eggs from chickens you've already captured.

I destroyed that village... I had spent too much time and resources going down dead ends and thought I'd make a new one. In the new village I went with the hunting hut. That'd show those chickens! I didn't read every detail of every page of encyclopedia... so I guess that makes me a bad gamer.... but I was astonished to realize after I researched it that this only generates raw meat and that you need to research and build a barbecue to then cook the meat!

When I think of survival, I try to secure food sources right after shelter and water. The pips come with shelter, they don't seem to drink water, so I tried to find new ways to access food. Berry bushes don't last all year and foraging isn't great. Still I guess I should have focused on just plowing ahead with more people to do more research because ultimately that's what slows your progress down the most. Someone has to think, in isolation, for six full days to consider how to cook meat... I guess they left a totally vegan society to settle here? You have to learn how to eat while also learning to build bonfires to keep warm because no one in this village had thought of that before...
