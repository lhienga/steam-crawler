\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFull of promise this game definitely occupies its own niche. Whilst drawing on many mechanics from other city builders, and resource management games, the closest might be "Against the Storm". Lacking combat, your job is to help your villagers succeed at life, and help them to be strong enough to overcome the many prophecies of doom and woe. Deal with unique challenges that change your playthroughs. Unlock a lot of tech, with different buildings, shrines and other cool fixtures giving you several strategies to thrive.

Really crisp, well executed pixel art lays the foundation for tactile and snappy menus. The UI is good and the overall looking pleasing. It's rounded out by a surprisingly good soundtrack, almost reminiscent of "Stardew Valley". While a little on the 'pricey' side, beauty is in the eye of the beholder and I for one find it still worth the money. It's got deep gameplay and a great amount of content. The developer is friendly, engaged, and active on the project. Expect updates, fixes, and new additions.

Why not give the demo a try?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the replay value of the game.
The basic premise is simple. You build a village and the difficulty starts to ramp up as YOUR VILLAGE IS CURSED.
Essentially the game will throw fail conditions as you progress and you need to progress your tech to counter the effect. It is turn based so you can take your own sweet time to think what you would like to do.
The fail conditions fall under 4 categories mainly.
Fear (which cause your Pips to run away or murder each other etc)
Sickness (which throws ailments at your Pips, such as poison, sickness etc)
Cataclysm (earthquakes, avalanches etc)
Heat or Cold (causes burning or freezing depending on the seasons)
As you counter the events, you earn Memory points which unlock a wide variety of technology. At the end of your run, you gain a score based on the number of buildings you have the number of survivors etc.
In your first run, you may just have berries as food on your map, but as you unlock the Memory, you can get other sources of food in your next run.
Hence the player experience differs from run to run and every time you restart, you get different resources and even different tech to counter the fail conditions.
I also enjoy the cute sprites and funny dialogue. It is fun to read descriptions in the Agepedia.


Not to mention, you can play with different leaders each with different conditions.
For example the Captain has a map with lots of water and your Pips have a higher chance of getting wet.

There is also a great variety of buildings to build and so many different types of animals to rear.\t\t\t\t\t
