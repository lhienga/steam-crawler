\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Very* narrow recommendation, there are some staggering points of frustration but it's also very unique: it's a lighter weight, rogue-y turn based town builder a la Against the Storm. The 'but' is that the game seems to have a fixation on "difficulty" in the sense of "extreme death spirals", and in a way that just feels below the belt. Riding the razor's edge doesn't feel like a thing. Either I succeed at every event, or I die. Binary in a way that's just frustrating.

I'll narrate through an example. It'll show the trade offs you have to think through, and the death spiral fixation. It's flawed, but "flawed" doesn't mean "bad". I still got my money's worth from it, but be aware what you are getting into.
--
You can provide the town with Healthcare via a regular plant, or a special medicinal plant. A doctor's hut every turn will take some of the store of one plant and convert it to the Healthcare score. Regular plant converts to healthcare score at 1-to-1 or 2-to-3 ratios, while medicinal plant is closer to 1-to-7 initially, and later on it's north of 1-to-20. The medicinal plant by default can only be gathered in the fall (some exception, but not many). You've got limited warehousing capacity, so whatever you collect in the fall needs to last you the year. Also the medicinal plants are additionally consumed to cure the specific pips that are poisoned/sick, every turn a pip isn't treated is a malus on your Healthcare score. The regular plants suffice for background care and building a score buffer, but in an acute infection you need to break out the good stuff, clear out the sick fast before they crater the score.

So, event has a third of the population get sick. Break out the good stuff, thread the needle to barely meet the score check to assure the good outcome. But over the course of the year there are too many events, trying to cure everyone empties my stores before next fall. Investing in more warehouses for year 1 is tricky, I'm already constrained preparing for all the other types of events I might run into. Going deeper instead of wide tends to get me blindsided. So try aiming for lower score? Risk a bad outcome? Well if you risk it and get the bad outcome *you can find yourself with half your town given 10 turns to live, and an immediate follow up event more severe than the original.* Half the town is dying, and half are reinfected. And if you fail that event? Well they keep chaining until you hit a doom event that ends the run..... or fall comes around and suddenly it's all trivial again so you succeed at one.

I'm already being punished: losing pawns means I can't generate as many supplies. Future events *are already harder because of that*. Doubling down on things and immediately running a more severe event as punishment is absolutely insane. "Hard" doesn't have to imply "death spiral or bust". I love the turn-based structure, and it'll certainly appeal to some people, but personally I'm just going to play Against the Storm more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played Dota for over 4000 hours. Now, with Dotage (which is totally different game than Dota...), I am at the edge of something new and special.

The game is very addictive. The game makes you to react with random events - if you have planned something in advance, you can be quite sure you need a plan B soon.

You can expect anything to happen. Sometimes very brutal, sometimes less brutal. Sometimes brutal just happens and you can't do anything about it - be ready for it! ;)

You can tackle the following domain (say, Heat) in advance: If you produce enough cold to tackle Heat, then you achieve a Good Event. Which is good, obviously.

Every playthrough is different because the map, events and tech tree change in every playthrough.

The game has simple mechanics and it's basically all about managing resources. Something similar with Oxygen Not Included, but turn-based. You might get addicted with "one-more-turn" because you are so eager to seeing what next event is hiding behind it...

The late game *might* become micromanage-heavy when you have dozens of Pips (the workers). You need to move them accordingly and if you are min-maxing it, it can take time for you to end your turn. I try to adjust workers with a feeling instead so I would avoid min-max pressure-burnout but it's up to you what you do.

In general, very nice game. I like its simple mechanics yet a complex layer how to manage your resources. Randomizations give a good replayability. And when you are Doomed for good, you can't do anything else but restart trying to satisfy the Elder...\t\t\t\t\t
