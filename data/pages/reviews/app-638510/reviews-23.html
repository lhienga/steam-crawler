---

After a lot of thinking and a bit of lovemaking, the village had enough people and technology to herd chickens & farm their eggs all day long. We were invincible. We'd never run out of food again, we could scale our industrial chicken operations to heights that no village peasant had ever dreamed possible.

Then came the Fear. It started slowly--an omen of crows, a superstitious dream. That was fine: We could cure the people of their worries. We started growing hemp and trained a spiritual guide, and soon the village was smokin' the good stuff, their fears melting away.

But that only lasted so long. The bad dreams persisted. We started running out of hemp. We needed more people to handle the quickly ballooning farm's needs, so we repurposed as many nonessential personnel as we could, for our not-so-clandestine grow op.

That was the beginning of the end, though we didn't know it at the time.

Our greatest thinkers were now weed farmers, tilling the soil day in and day out. The town's creative juices ran dry, and the villagers couldn't research more efficient ways to feed themselves & smoke their worries away.

The town's fear kept building, and the population stagnated. Nobody had time for lovemaking--they needed to feed the chickens and tend to the farm. The stress and fear came to a head. A young woman, Sbaba, snapped during a Fear event. She killed 5 of her fellow villagers.

That was the beginning of the end. We didn't know how to build a cemetery yet. Disease spread from the bodies of Sbaba's murdered comrades, and control was quickly slipping from the village elder's grasp.

One by one, the remaining townsfolk fell sick. They couldn't work. The few remaining workers, were staring down the barrel of their fate--desperately searching for seeds and collecting eggs, in a futile effort to feed their ill, to wait out the plague that befell the town.

Then, the Fear struck again. The people, healthy and sick alike, were overwhelmed with panic. One by one, they fled the town, getting lost in the forest beyond our fences.

The poor village elder's heart couldn't continue--consumed with guilt at his failures of leadership, reminded by the stench of death that enveloped him. He keeled over, the last body to fall into the sea of corpses scattered amongst his once-bustling hippie farm town.
