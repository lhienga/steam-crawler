\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed this game. It takes a bit of a bit of getting used to but it is a meaty and satisfying city builder.

Every turn you have a certain amount of people and you determine which action they should take, but that's not really where the game is happening. If you have to think a lot about who should do what, then the answer is get more people. So really, the game is about building a settlement that can provide enough food and resources for you do all the things you need to. This is a fun puzzle, as each map and each set of available technologies will present will present you with a different solution to the puzzle of having a town that can generate the resources you need to face the upcoming trials.

Its a ton of fun and I couldn't put it down. That said I am all done for now. Games take a bajillion years to finish because when your town is big every turn takes a long time, both between finding something for all of your people to, but also for the between turn animations to and resource calculations to take place.

There's not a lot of tension in game once you figure it out. You are either well ahead at all times or you are going to lose. The way the event system works is that small setbacks, unless completely absorbed by your lead, will ruin you. But if you can maintain a proper lead, none of the small setbacks can slow you down. Doing well early, and what knowing what type of situations you need to prepare for will win the game in a few hours. You just won't be officially declared the winner for another 10 hours.

Really really cool game, I had great time, but the last 3rd of a winning run really takes the steam our of your desire to play again. Fortunately, losing runs are lost well before it becomes a slog so you will have a blast right up until you win once.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tdotAGE is, at its core, a game where you place workers to get resources, hit the end turn button, and get your resources. You play through spring - summer - autumn - winter twice, followed by a final "season". Buildings need to be researched, and most of them have some kind of adjacency or vicinity bonus when placed near specific other types of buildings, while others have requirements and not a bonus.

As you progress your little pip village, you get visions of the four "domains", and have to generate four specific resources in order to counter them, or else. If you're successful, you get buffs or resources. If you fail, you take some sort of malus early on, and potentially risk a game over later on in the game.

The gameplay loop is quite good. Addictive even. Right up there with the likes of Civilization and Football Manager in terms of invoking "just one more turn".


But dotAGE does have its issues.

At the time of writing, the game can can be a little buggy sometimes. There are a number of visual bugs that can occur when loading a save, like in the screenshot. Herding buildings also seem to have visual bugs when interacting with slaughterhouses, regardless of loading the save.

Framerates tank to single digits consistently in the second half of the game. I'm playing this on slightly old hardware (i7-6600, GTX1660Ti), as well as playing most games in single sessions, so YMMV. Going to the title screen and loading the game again temporarily resolves this issue but it'll drop again within the season.

I'm writing this review after having beaten the four scenarios, on normal. At this difficulty, if you make it past the second winter, the final stretch of the game ends up being a victory lap and it feels a bit jarring; anticlimactic even. I may give a higher difficulty a go, but will wait to see if the performance issues get fixed first.


Ultimately, the first playthrough and unlocking all the meta-progression unlocks was well worth the price of admission. For repeated playthroughs beyond that, I'm not so sure. I'm pleased to see that a very super fast mode and a cutscene skip option have been added post-launch, and if the other issues are resolved, I expect this to be a top recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
