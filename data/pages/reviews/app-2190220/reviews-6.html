\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly, I do like this game, I would generally recommend it to any tuhu and rhythm game fans... Maybe not outside of that though. The art and music selection is especially great!

There are more than a few things I think the game could have done better though, so in no particular order:
  • The groove gauge (sort of a life/win condition meter) drains way to fast in my opinion. More than any other rhythm game, I could be on track for a B or A rank, but suddenly at the end I miss a few notes, and the game considers I failed the stage, it's honestly really frustrating (yes I know, "skill issue").
  • Normal difficulty is also only played with 4 keys instead of the 6 keys you play in hard difficulty. This isn't necessarily bad... but the difficulties can be soo vastly different. For me, a lot of the normal difficulty can be boring, but the hard difficulty can also be too difficult (even though there is also a lunatic difficulty). So really, I think there should have been 5 difficulties, because the difficulty spike for many of them seem a bit much.
  • Note speed is also at a static amount unless you manually adjust it. In most rhythm games, the note speed would be a dynamic amount that scales with the song's bpm. Because it is static, on a high bpm song, all the notes will be very close together, very overwhelming and almost unreadable, unless you adjust that speed yourself. In general the notes can also be hard to read.
  • The danmaku stages are fun, interesting, but messy. The thing that sticks out the most though is that if you kill the boss early, they sort of just sit there in the middle of the screen not doing anything for the rest of the song... meaning you will also be sitting there doing very minimal as well. I think it would be better if the boss kept fighting until the end of the song, where they explode if their health is zero, and any damage dealt after their health is drained would go towards an "overkill" bonus. Or something like that, really anything would be better than just sitting there doing nothing lol.
  • The hitbox for your playable character can be very vague during danmaku stages, would love a way to see the hitbox.
  • Some other things in the game can also be quite unclear, such as unlocking characters, certain missions with quotas you can't track...
  • I wish there was a quicker/automatic way to select an optimal mitama card for whatever stage you're playing. Especially since some stages penalise you for using certain types of cards or have gimmicks you might want to reduce the effects of, you are incentivised to switch, but having to scroll through all the cards is a bit of a tiresome.
  • The story is very repetitive, intentionally so, and it still has some great moments, but after a while I just start skimming over anything to do with the ephemerals in the story.

There are some other minor things as well, but the game is still getting updates, including some that should solve some of these issues, and overall if you're a big touhou or rhythm game fan, I'd still recommend the game lmao\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm giving this a tentative recommendation made with the trust that the devs are going to polish up the rough edges of the UI, settings, and rhythm game results detail as mentioned in other reviews. The devs promised many good fixes less than a day after release — I don't think what they're promised is enough to get the game up to a real high standard, but I'm willing to believe that they will address feedback in good faith based on this first step.

Anyway, it's a Touhou rhythm game. I have several hundred hours in Spell Bubble. If you like Touhou arrangements, you cannot go wrong with this one. There's a good lineup and the DLC is much more fairly priced than the other aforementioned licensed rhythm game option. I take this genre seriously, but if you are a casual, you won't notice most things (other than the skewed difficulty) that other reviews complain about. You can tell what this is from the outside looking in — if you think you'd enjoy a Touhou rhythm game in this style, it's a great time!\t\t\t\t\t
