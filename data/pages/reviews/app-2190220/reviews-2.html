tl;dr:
  • If you are someone who has a good history of rhythm games, especially with 6K (6-key), you may like this game! If you're not, you may feel like 50% of the game is locked for you.
  • OK as a standalone Touhou rhythm game + great Touhou music choices, I do not recommend it for story mode.
  • I do not recommend this game for beginners or casuals to rhythm games, hence the negative review. The devs have been actively trying to fix the issues with this in their patch notes, with the new 6K support for Easy/Normal (v1.0.3), and Booster Items will be added in the future. Review will be remade once these items are added, thank you!

Review:
Your enjoyment of this game will depend on your experience with rhythm games.
I will be writing this as a Touhou fan + casual rhythm game player (Mostly played Hard/Extra on old Danmaku Kagura)

Unless you purchased the unlock DLC, you will most likely be playing through story mode to unlock everything. In this case, just playing Story Mode in Easy/Normal is OK.

However, if you are trying to 100% Story Mode and unlock all the cards and characters, you HAVE to play Hard difficulty or Lunatic, and Hard/Lunatic is 6K instead of 4K. For people who can only hit 4K, this can be a big hit to motivation, as it may not be possible to unlock your favorite character/card.

Huge difficulty difference between Normal -> Hard (4K -> 6K):
Easy and Normal is 4K (4-key, or 4 lanes), while Hard/Lunatic is 6K (6-key, or 6 lanes)

If there is an Easy/Normal version of 6K for beginners to get started and practice or Hard/Lunatic versions of 4K, that would be great, but as of this writing (25-2-2024), there is none.

Update for Patch v1.0.3 (1-3-2024): A new mode called 'Outer Random' has been added which has been added for people who wish to practice 6K in Easy and Normal! Thank you, devs!

As mentioned before, there are characters/cards which can only be unlocked at Hard (at minimum, so 6K is required, no compromise), so please take this into consideration if you're a casual player.

Danmaku Stages:
This mechanic is very unique, where you will use your left hand to play the rhythm game (A, S, D, F), and your right hand (Arrow Keys) to move your character for danmaku battles.

It's very fun at the simpler stages where there is only two lanes, but on stages where there are 4 lanes, there are reviews saying people will just randomly push all 4 keys instead, which I do not think is very enjoyable.

Also, the scoring in danmaku stages are extremely strict, and requires lots of min-maxing due to the lack of polish.

Overall:
If you love Touhou and just want to play the music as a standalone rhythm game, then you can get this, even if it feels like around 50% of the content will be locked due to skill level.

However, at present, I cannot recommend this game to my friends or other players unless they are huge fans of Touhou and/or are extremely good in rhythm games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a relatively hardcore rhythm game player and casual touhou fan, this was a genuinely very fun experience.

It's definitely not the best designed game, for example I find the descision to make normal and easy 4k quite baffling. I played the game mostly on hard, and while I struggled reading some stuff due to skill issue, it was always my fault.

Overall, I would recommend this game to anyone who enjoys both rhythm games and touhou, however it's harder to recommend this game, especially compared to something like DJ max respect, It's still a 6k game after all (with pretty tight timing windows).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo to preface this, Rhythm Games are probably the genre I have the most experience with. My main game currently is SDVX, but I've played mobile rhythm games, desktop, and arcade. So I'm going into this game with a lot of experience.

I'd also like to say I pretty much exclusively play this game on Lunatic and exclusively the rhythm game, I'm not a fan of the danmaku mode, so I'll refrain from talking about that.

The Good
- The songlist is phenomenal. Truly some amazing bangers in here. Would like to see more underground circles though, like SEVENTH HEAVEN MAXION and such. But still I love the songs chosen here.
- The charts. Genuinely, I probably like 90% of the charts in this game, even Unknown X. It seems like they got actually experienced charters to work on this game, so seriously love it there.
- The timing window. I am a fan of timing windows like this. Not insanely strict (looking at you, IIDX), but strict enough to where you need to actually get the hang of the timing window. After some hours playing, my accuracies went from 84% all the way to 91+, it just took some practice.
- The ability to not need to interact with the danmaku mode if you don't want to. I am a huuuuuge fan of this! I'm not into the danmaku mode, so I just don't play it. I don't know of the extent of this in the story mode, but if you don't like the danmaku mode you don't need to interface with it.

The cons may seem like a lot, but they're all minor and not really too bad for me. Just a forewarning.

The Bad (There might be some biases as there are some issues I am experiencing I am putting here. So take those specific issues with a grain of salt)
- I run this game through a Focusrite Scarlett 2i2, and I am getting some weird moments where the audio stutters, and in bad cases the game does as well. I'm not sure if this is just me (as I use an interface, these have been known to cause issues with rhythm game audio drivers), but maybe it's worth the team look into this.
- Map offsets. This one's a minor gripe, as I never really noticed my accuracy shifting from map to map often. But I do notice in some maps the offsets are not as precise as they are in other maps. I have seen other people talk about this, it hasn't affected me much but worth pointing out.
- The UI, primarily around the story. When there is text discussing the situation, it looks a bit unpolished and could use improvement there.
- Actually I'd also like to say the story itself feels a bit boring because it's primarily text dump with a few art pieces here and there. If the entire game was in the style of small text with the characters (like after you perform a prayer), I would rate this higher.
- Score and Grades. I don't understand ... what the heck the grades mean? I know they're based on the score but what's the score based on? I feel like grades should be entirely based on my accuracy. Make me work for an SS. I have been getting SS's on nearly every song and I'm not that good of a player. I feel like SS should be for like 99%+, or maybe 95% since the accuracy is tight.

I'm in love with this game, and the good outweigh the cons heavily since the good is all of the core of a rhythm game (minus the score, but there IS an accuracy result so that nearly makes up for it)

I haven't purchased any of the packs yet, but I will have to do that. I hope more music will come out in the future!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis is a good game, the music and art are excellent though, gameplay wise, it could get better, here are some small issues that i found during my experience:
small issues:
-i didnt find a way to have a fixed scrollspeed for every single beatmap, making it so that i have to change the speed for each beatmap independently,
-if you play a map that has a mission locking the speed of a map at a certain speed, this locked speed will be saved for the next time you play that map.
-Some people like the story aspect in a rythm game, some people dont, i first was reading it but after a while found it a bit repetitive and wanted to skip it but there is no skip button so it ends in me spam clicking for multiple minutes.

bug category:
-the pause menu in danmaku mode is far from perfect, blue score and red power pickups are not affected by pausing the game, same as some projectiles, i suspect that if the enemy that shot the projectile is dead then the projectile wont be affected by the pause anymore (and yes it causes damage to the player even in pause).
-the achievement asking for you to have all of the mitama cards didnt complete for me despite having a 100% collection, i suspect it is because the last one i got are from the shop in the village and not earned in battle, there is therefore no way for me to get this achievement anymore except resetting my save.

Aside of that, the game is very good, musics/arts are excellent and the gameplay feels good and responsive which are the most important parts of a rythm game. The art especially is something that was put a lot of effort into with some Live2D models that were implemented and almost every card having animation in the gallery mode.
enjoyed it a lot though i would say to wait for fixes/update or also wait for the game to be at a lower price if i had to fully recommend it in it's current state.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's very rough in some parts especially the UI and the story mode that has some cute animations but also in which 90% is useless and inconsistent story telling.

It's also very inconsistent on the beatmap qualities, some songs are just boring to play and seem very badly calibrated.

The "RPG" elements are more annoying than anything, especially since picking up your characters can be a pain since the filters are bad (I have to read 10-20 character abilities before finally finding the "anti earthquake" or "anti fog" ones).

Kudos for the cards and characters art which are great to look at.

Some very popular songs and characters are missing though, hope to see them in DLC. (where is miracle hinacle ? border of death ?... ) I wish there would be more original songs made from ZUN, thank god "reach for the moon, immortal smoke" was here.

I'm not even gonna mention the danmaku mode as it's a fun idea on paper that is just impossible in the end. If I wanna play that i'll just pop on touhou... This should have been a challenge mode, not something in the main story.

I'll wait that this game get patched and updated with the dlc before finishing it.\t\t\t\t\t
