\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to give this a 'no', unfortunately. If you play for the hour I did and have the experience of rhythm games behind you, you'll understand the frustration of getting bad timing windows that come with poor game design. Went through one of the 15-rated hard songs, can actually get a full combo or near it, and get nothing but 'FAST' timing windows which end up screwing up the score, your timing and your patience.

Looking at the reviews and discussions, I'm not the only one. They've appeared to put a timing window out and supposedly have a way of adjusting it in the game where + is supposedly for those getting a 'fast' timing, but none of this seems to work and in fact seem to inhibit gameplay further.

Considering I have a low latency, high refresh monitor setup and have messed around with rhythm games for the past 20 years or more, I struggle to believe this is on me with evidence of multiple people. There's no greater killer to one's energy than playing a game like this that is timed incorrectly. If you're similarly minded, I would stay away for now until they fix this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn very broad terms, I'd say I'm having a good time with this game. I have a lot of things to point out, but first I'll describe the things I enjoy:

- NO MORE GACHA/LIVE SERVICE LET'S GOOOOO.

- The songs selection is fantastic, some of the best song variety in any rhythm game. I thought this of the mobile version too.

- The art for the cards and jackets is fantastic. This, too, is something I thought with the mobile game.

- The gallery feature is something I adore. The simple fact we have a dedicated place for all the assets implemented in both the mobile game and the pc version for the future to come is super awesome.

- I really enjoy seeing so many characters and their little in-game animations. Renko and Maribel's animations are particularly adorable.

- A lot of the challenges are rather simplistic, but looking for the right card to counter the gimmicks and the right attribute to score high is kinda fun.

- The charting, at least in my experience playing Hard and low difficulty Lunatic charts, is really solid.
To name a few examples:

~ My Awaited won't Come's Lunatic chart is really fun to play, it has a lot of unique sections and rhythms, plus the last thrills are a very unique speed test.

~ The Murasa song's Lunatic chart is funky and bouncy, it fits the songs well.

~ snow amber's Lunatic chart masterfully uses the differently colored lanes to represent each vocalist/character, ending in a sequence of double taps that's both really entertaining to pull off and thematically fitting.

~ Hanipaganda's Lunatic chart is really unique for its constant use of triplets.

I've heard some complaints that some of the higher level Lunatic charts are overcharted, but I'm not yet at that level of skill to properly judge.

With all of that said, there are also a few things I'm not the biggest fan of, for example:

- I dislike how 3 of the 4 DLC do not have a revealed song list.

- The Danmaku Stages are a multitasking mess, and there are a lot of repeated Stages just with only a few more notes. The execution is really rough.

- I also share the opinion a lot of people have that this game has a problem of big difficulty spikes, mostly between Normal and Hard, but sometimes you see it in Hard and Lunatic (see: U.N. Owen was Hero?, Braking The Silence or an stoirm). 2 difficulties being 4k while the other 2 are 6k certainly doesn't help.

- I am not a fan of how this game relies on grinding to unlock songs (including DLC!!!). The game also follows the steps of DJMAX and Project Diva by adding an "unlock everything" DLC, which is kind of a ♥♥♥♥ move tbh.

- The narrative is beyond formulaic, you could even say you have to read the exact same scenario like 12 times. There's barely any new info each time, if any, so most of it feels pointless. The story is rather childish as well.

- The ending was kinda anticlimactic.

Now, moving on to:

Things I want to see addressed in future updates

There are a few things that were promised for the future, so I won't include them (like note skins or customizable UI...):

- Scrolling speed being dependant on BPM is one of the dumbest thing I've ever seen in any rhythm game. I don't know how it is coded in the game, but, judging by the in-game UI, it seems that it uses a formula that uses the bpm and a multiplier to generate the scrolling speed value. I'm not sure how easy it'd be to implement, but it'd be nice if we could just pick the final speed value manually or if the bpm number in the formula was fixed (i.e. 150).

- It'd be helpful if we got a message for when scrolling speeds are going to change. A simple "speed up/down" message would suffice, similar to Cytus 2.

- Night Flight Believer scrolling speed changes are terrible. Please remove/rechart that section.

- It'd be nice if we could see our accuracy on the song selection screen alongside Score and Rank (outside of details).

- It'd help if we could label Mitama Cards as favorites and then filter them.

- It'd be nice if we had the option to make the lanes less wide, or even move it to the sides instead of being stuck in the center.

- I would want to be able to pick any heroine for Free Mode. I am aware not all of them can be used in the Danmaku Stages, but I really wish there was a way to pick any heroine of your choosing there.

- I would like the Groove Gauge to be adjusted. It increases and decreases very quickly, which means that you can fill it up just by playing a quarter of the song and missing a few notes means that you're already failing the stage. It should be more like Taiko no Tatsujin's or Groove Coaster's, where you have to fight to fill the gauge and missing a few notes doesn't put you in the red immediately.

- The judgement timings are currently very awkward. I don't know if it's a skill issue on my part, but sometimes I think I'm doing well only to be hit by a random Great all of a sudden. I think making the windows a little more lax should make the experience more consistent.

- It'd be nice if bullets were above your sprite instead of the other way around, because, as it currently stands, when bullets are very close to you you can't see them.

- I'm not a fan of the gradient effect whenever you hit a normal note. A more pronounced effect on the judgement line (i.e. like a sparkling circle) would look better imo. Let it be customizable, please.

- The translation is serviceable for the most part, however it needs to be revised because there are a few mistranslations here and there.

- Speaking of translations, PLEASE make an attempt to localize the title of several of the songs included in the game. A lot of the currently available songs have generally accepted translations in the fanbase and there are a lot of people willing to provide their own translations if necessary. Anything would be better than simply romanizing them.

- I'd like the Gallery to be expanded so we can see the backgrounds in more detail. Since the lanes aren't transparent anymore, we can't really appreciate them while playing.

- It'd be nice if the charter for each song could be credited in the song presentantion screen.

Nico's Strange Wishlist

These are things that the game doesn't *need*, but I still personally wish to see (if possible):

- I hope all of the characters that didn't make it into the original game get at least 1 song in Phantasia Lost. I know a lot of them show up in the Story Mode, but I wish this doesn't mean they're discarded for the future.

- A 4k mode!!!
I am aware that the charting team isn't very big, but I still wish there will eventually be a 4k mode alongside the standard 6k. It'd be good for variety and pleasing all kinds of players.

- WHERE ARE THE CHIBIS!!! AND THE LIVE2D SPRITES!!!
The Gallery is one of my favorite inclusions in this version of the game, so I hope there will eventually be a way to see all of the chibis and Live2D sprites in the Heroine screen.

- Speaking of chibis, I know that this game focuses more on the Story Mode rather than the pet simulator aspect of the phone game, but I still would like to see the return of the locations and decorations where we could see the chibis hanging out. Maybe in the form of an optional post-game feature.

- It'd be cool if we could get thematic layouts/note skins. I know customization and note skins are on the way, but I'm talking about, for example, a layout themed around EoSD, or a note skin themed around the animal spirits, stuff like that. Similar to how DJMAX does it.

- Online vs mode!!!
This is the most insane request, and I will not complain if it never happens, however the whole being able to pick a character thing and the fact Danmaku Kagura is presented as a sort of rhythmic battle makes me think a simple scoring Vs Mode would work well.

That is all. I'm really enjoying this game and I want the best for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really loved this game when it was on mobile, bar the gacha section of course, and I was excited to hear that the mobile game was finally going to be able to be played in English with this, but I lost hyped for it around the week of it's release and it ended up being some kind of sign.

When I booted up the game there was no audio at all, and I looked over it thinking "Oh this is like background information, I guess it doesn't NEED music or anything, then the first song started up and I found out that no audio was working for me, though it seemed to work for most other people, I saw other people bringing it up in the discussion forum, but I noticed they didn't mention a bug about the audio not working for some people in their bug fixing announcement, and after the update, and the audio still doesn't seem to work, which is pretty much makes this game unplayable, especially since it's a rhythm game.

Other than that major bug, I really hate that the game has locked some of the songs that were available on the mobile version behind DLC 1. Meaning without discount you'd be spending $30 USD for a game that less songs than it had. If the DLC was just new songs to be added to the list, I wouldn't be complaining since that's reasonable. Additionally about the DLC, I am also not the biggest fan of the fact people who bought the Deluxe version pretty much ended up overpaying for what was the original mobile game for the next few months because of the DLC's coming in waves, which luckily for me I chose to stick to just the base version.

I don't mind DLC waves shown that Mystia's Izakaya did that and I really love that game, but I just think that the deluxe pack that including the DLC's that wouldn't drop til months in the future just made people overspend for the game.

Additionally, I haven't had a chance to try out the Danmaku stages yet, but according to some of my other friends who were able to play it, the controls for it is "wonky".

Additionally additionally, i'm very confused on the fact that the game on start up has a "New Game" and "Continue" option, it feels like a very weird design choice that only makes me think more that this game is just a visual novel game with a mandatory Rhythm game section that was half assed and a Touhou coat of paint to get that sort of "niche community" hype, which also doesn't have voice acting at all, which IIRC the original version had voice acting, if not full voice acting then it at least gave the 2hu's SOME voice.

It just feels like a failed successor to a game that I enjoyed playing to just kill some time on. This just isn't the rhythm game for me.

2/10 -
If you're a Touhou fan that just wants to play a game with great Touhou covers, i'd recommend Touhou Mystia's Izakaya. it even has a pseudo rhythm section in the game that's expanded in a DLC. Additionally in terms of money, you can get the game AND all DLC's for pretty close price to the base game of this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've put a lot of time into this game (25 hours at the time of this review) and it's now easily one of my favorite Touhou Fangames. It's cool to have a Touhou Rhythm Game in its entirety that's not locked to a phone. The Touhou aspects of the game are very charming. It's cool to have a card of say, Dream Sumireko as she appears in Violet Detector. I love the song choices, and am also having fun with the charts. This is the game that's finally getting me to learn six lane play. I also liked the story mode and campaign a lot, and found myself going back to complete missions. BUT, there are a lot of problems with this game that prevent it from truly being one of the all time greats. Surprisingly, it's not a lack of content. I was actually satisfied with that. Instead, it's this:

-This game has HORRIBLE tutorials. Players don't know that there's a rock paper scissors system for cards, or that you aren't supposed to do all the missions of the campaign at once because this information is either not taught, or is hidden behind an overly long help screen that gives too much information at once. I think this is why a lot of people don't like the campaign: the game does a horrible job teaching its mechanics.
-There is no constant BPM setting or average (although the BPM of a song itself and the speed you change it to is listed). You can set a modifier for speed, but this is relative to the speed of every song in the game rather than a BPM for notes. So, you have to go through and modify the BPM for every note individually.
-The game has quite a few bugs. For example, I unlocked some achievements, but the game only checks for achievements when you hit the quit button. So when my game crashed, some of my achievements didn't save.
-The UI feels very mobile oriented still, so it's easiest to navigate with a mouse. This isn't non-functional, but it does feel very amateurish.
-There's quite a few translation quirks. The story revolves around the original Gacha Game's end of service, but this isn't translated over as the original talking point for it was a pun. There's also a few instances where objectives are reversed due to the shaky translation.
-While not a problem itself, I really hope the Bond Conversations from the mobile game get added in (those little convos between Touhou characters ala Fire Emblem supports).
-Not within the game itself, but I personally dislike the "Unlock All Songs" DLC a ton. You unlock songs through story mode already, so you're just playing to pay less of the game. This should've been a cheat code rather than something players have to pay for.

The devs have put out a roadmap of things they're going to fix, and they did put out a pretty significant patch Day 2. I'm happy this game will get better, but I also think it's completely understandable if you want to wait for a patch to iron out all the issues. That being said though, I still really enjoyed the game, and was happy with the $27 I spent on it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnce upon a time, Touhou Danmaku Kagura came around as a rhythm game for smartphones. It was supposedly fairly well received at first (I wouldn't know firsthand, as I don't think it came to European playstores where I could have gotten it) but unlucrative in the end, so it died like that other one, Touhou Cannonball. It's somewhat amusing that the Touhou mobile game with the lowest production value is the only one able to survive. Lost Word is not the game we need, but what we deserve...
Touhou Danmaku Kagura Phantasia Lost is somewhat of a reimagining of that original mobile game with a new storyline and thankfully: Not a live-service that can die unless Steam itself died I guess. The scenario is very fitting and eerily meta, as it seems like Gensokyo had already crumbled into a sandy apocalypse, like it's the Gensokyo of a discontinued mobile game. The story is fairly appealing at first and not badly written, but falls into the trap of being very formulaic in its 10 or so episodes. The payoff that I won't reveal is also a little lacking, though the final boss's level was quite good.
Gameplay wise, there's Kagura stages and Danmaku stages. Kagura are notesheets that you play like Guitar Hero, etc. Since I have been a superfan and collector of Doujin music, I had a lot of fun playing these. You'll have many of the evergreens, ranging from joke songs like Cirno's Perfect Math Class to serious pieces of rock like Kishida Kyoudan & The Akeboshi Rockets - Brilliant Star Rocket. Even if I didn't care about the gameplay, the title would be worth having for the songs alone. The best discovery that I had never even heard of before playing this was Breaking the Silence by Get in the Ring.
Danmaku stages are a scrolling shooter that's very simplistic compared to the official Touhou games. You have to survive over the song's duration and complete missions for additional rewards. Defeating the boss in time may not actually be among those missions, which feels like a failure at first, but one must look past and adapt. While dodging bullets, there's still the occasional note to hit for points as you would in the Kagura stages. The blend of these gameplay elements is a little strange and overwhelming, but you'll get over it once you figure out that you hit the notes only for points and fanciness. you won't lose if you miss them. Overall, I enjoyed that mode too.
There are also RPG elements to the game in the form of Mitama Cards. This is actually the aspect of this title that feels the most influenced by mobile games, but it's not executed badly. I've yet to max out a card's level, but I wouldn't say it's especially grindy. Even a card that you haven't greatly leveled up can be helpful in passing a level if its trait matches the situation and side missions. Plus, it seems in free play mode, your current card will always be considered max level.\t\t\t\t\t
