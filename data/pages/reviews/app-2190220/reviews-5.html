\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a fan of Touhou and/or Rhythm Games, then this is a must have, though there are still some nitpicks to be had with the game in its current state.

Firstly, the English translation is a bit rough. While this isn't a game killer, it leads to some aspects of the main campaign not being super clear, both in the story and in the missions required to fully clear different levels (for instance, sometimes a mission will say "fewer" when it means "greater.")

Secondly, if you played the original Danmaku Kagura on Mobile Devices, you need to be aware that this is fundamentally a very different game despite using a lot of the same assets.

-The game has no voice acting, unlike its predecessor, though this is easily forgiven when you consider budgeting. A bit disappointing, but again not game breaking.

-A fair chunk of the original Dankagu's song list is currently missing, and will likely only come later in the paid DLC. This sucks for obvious reasons, but given I paid 60 USD for the base game plus all the DLC packs, I'm more or less fine with it given that's industry standard price. Others may NOT be fine with that however, especially if you are a longtime Touhou fan, as Touhou games have a tendency to run fairly cheap comparatively.

-The gameplay itself is considerably different in a number of ways. Overall, I personally see this as mostly positive with a few major drawbacks. The main Kagura mode is a lot more standardized now, and requires less extreme movements from the player compared to the mobile version, (which is good given when playing mobile, it felt like my phone would slide all over if I didn't put it on something to keep it in place.) The Danmaku mode has seen a total overhaul as well, and feels a lot less tacked on than it was in the mobile version imo, having gameplay more reminiscent of an actual bullet hell with rhythmic elements. These all sound like huge wins over the mobile version on paper, and in practice they sort of are...but also sort of aren't. For one thing, there's only four difficulties for each song in this game compared to the original Dankagu's five. This shouldn't be too bad, but the jump between Normal mode and Hard mode is simply too jarring, as the game suddenly goes from being a 4 key game to being a 6 key game, and there aren't really any precautions taken to help ease the player into the more complicated gameplay. If you're experienced with 6 key games already then it's all good, but if you're not then Easy and Normal are kinda hard to break away from, and it doesn't help that some of the stages in the main story mode outright FORCE you to play on Hard or higher. I have also heard from some other reviews that the game as it is doesn't take well to higher difficulties anyway, not being the best at handling a high volume of inputs. I have no expertise on this matter, as when I get to the higher difficulties here I frankly just tend to just mash until I find a winning strategy, but it's very concerning when a lot of the more challenging stages and difficulties in this game fundamentally rely on perfection. As for the Danmaku mode, while the gameplay is generally a very large step up from what it was in the mobile game, the rhythm part of the game being off to the far sides of the screen will absolutely trip you up if you can't adjust to recognizing color through your peripherals and reacting in turn. There's also less characters to play with general, which is disappointing, but also understandable given this game puts a lot more emphasis on the individual abilities of each character, compared to its mobile-bound predecessor where every character more or less equated to a skin of a basic template. Overall there's positives and negatives to be found here, but absolutely do NOT go into this expecting it to be 1 to 1 with original Dankagu, or even close to it for that matter, because it's not.

-The game doesn't blend the main story mode and the free play mode super well together. You can either play through the main story to unlock songs, or use dream drops that you gain from clearing songs/stages to unlock songs without the need for Story mode (or you can pop an extra three bucks for the unlock dlc but lmao no.) The Story Mode may also not appeal to everyone, though I enjoyed it quite a bit myself, seeing it takes a much more dramatic, drawn-out VN-styled approach compared to your usual simple Touhou plot, and having it feel more or less necessary to really get into the actual rhythm parts of this rhythm game isn't fantastic. Additionally, the way in which you're meant to fill out your collections of unlocked characters and song jackets is incredibly unclear. Any character or song you come across in the main Story Mode unlocks in your collections pretty much immediately upon giving em a go, but that doesn't account for DLC, which does NOT appear in the Story Mode as far as I can tell and only appears in Free Play. So while the DLC characters and jackets evidently ARE included in the in-game gallery/collections, there's no discernable way of actually UNLOCKING them for viewing, and the game does not clear this up at all.

With how specific and long-winded I've been with my criticisms here, you might think "wow he really doesn't like this game," but no, I LOVE this game, and that's exactly why I'm so critical. Despite everything, it still has the style and music that's won my heart over for so many years, it still has a very solid structure as a game both in gameplay and general progression, and it's still a game that I can easily pick up and revisit any time I want some rhythm fun after having cleared the main story. There's some very glaring issues here, and I don't want to understate that, but it's also not enough to dissuade me from calling this a must have for Touhou fans and Rhythm gamers. Just be aware of what it is you're purchasing when you purchase this game, and hopefully you'll enjoy it just as much as I have.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTouhou Danmaku Kagura is a good mobile game, back then. But for Touhou Danmaku Kagura: Phantasia Lost, I feel like this game has like half of everything the original got, for better or for worse.

- The voice acting (that the original got) is gone here.
- There are no online leaderboards.
- The Danamku mechanic is more complicated than it was originally. Not only that you have to move left and right, dodging the opponent's danmaku, and shoot your opponent. But you also have to do some Kagura, WHILE you're doing all of that.
- Unlike the original version, there isn't an option to customize and decorate the locations... Customizing and Decorating the locations in Gensokyo was one of my favorite things to do in Danmaku Kagura, and it's a shame that it didn't return here.
- That low chance of making the characters have a party somewhere and got drunk, and you'll be able to get free stuff from them at that point from the original game? I believe that will never happen here...

If they manage to add the missing features that the original have, I would enjoy it more.

If you played the original version on mobile and enjoyed it, you'll probably won't have that same amount of enjoyment from here. But if you haven't played the original version, give this game a go and see what you think of it.\t\t\t\t\t
