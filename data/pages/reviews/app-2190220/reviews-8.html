\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good! The musical variety is strong, the varying gameplay styles provide an interesting change of pace and novel twist on the basic six key formula, the presentation is surprisingly elaborate in ways it didn't really have to be and there's plenty of Content, I enjoyed it quite a bit. I think the charting expresses the character of its songs fairly well, in terms of difficulty I would say it peaks a fair bit lower than something like DJMax and certainly EZ2On, but that's also fine since the story mode hurls a bunch of gimmicks at you to increase the challenge. This will probably be divisive because I love my gimmick readings whereas others apparently find them to be an instant turnoff because they are cowards, but whatever floats ones boat.

probably my biggest two issues are that the UI for team management is cluttered and awful due to being borne of gacha games and gacha game UI design is made to be bad on purpose and it's clear the team haven't really kicked the habit since leaving that space and the fact the scroll speed adopts the DDR system where it's based on a multiplier of the song's BPM so you need to perform mental math for every single song individually to figure out your multiplier. I'll live, but it's burdensome and kind of bizarre to see in a modern music game, the last game I saw that did that was DJMax Respect's initial Steam release which was like four years ago or something. Still, that was eventually patched to something more Normal so there's hope for this here too.

other than those two things idk man I really like it. the music games are good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty decent.

Two different types of stages:
Kagura stages which are typical music game stages on a 6 button layout, easy/normal only use 4, hard/lunatic use 6
Danmaku stages which are either 2 or 4 lanes you hit notes with one hand and use arrow keys to move a heroine while dodging. they have no difficulty options.

You have a neat story mode with various stages you can unlock songs for play in a free play mode. songs can also be unlocked via ingame currency. danmaku stages are not playable via free play.

57 songs on release for base game I believe.

short pro's/cons
+ decent variety of songs / variety of genre's
+ marasy/lily-an
+ good difficulty spread. hardest songs feel about the same/easier than harder DJMax songs.
+ fun beatmaps

+/- its nice to see official songs in here but they seem weirdly out of place next to so many arranges.
+/- note speed etc needs to be changed from main menu's for a baseline.

- Danmaku mode is neat but damn hard to divert attention to.
- no T.Stebbins.
- no options for beatmap augmenting, hidden/sudden/random notes etc
- no display in song or in results for early / lates\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE 2024-02-10: I hear the 1.0.1.1 patch released just now addresses some of the scroll speed issues AND added a fast/slow display. Haven't tested it personally, but I do have to commend them for being quick with their fixes.

Glad this game rose from the ashes of its fallen mobile predecessor, but... It's rough around the edges, especially for an experienced rhythm gamer. I also wasn't expecting 3 of the 4 DLC songpacks to be "coming soon!" placeholders. (I'm fine with songpack DLC for a rhythm game, but this is basically an a la carte season pass, which feels... awkward?)

tl;dr
Check back in a year a few months from now for QoL improvements.

Issues
- Lanes feel way too wide in fullscreen, and the default noteskin certainly doesn't help! (more are supposedly on the way, though)
- Scroll speed is based on song BPM, not a fixed rate. This means you'll be setting a different speed modifier for every song (or at least, a different one for 130 BPM songs vs 200 BPM songs). At least there's a live preview on the global/song options screen! ...But the preview is a lot smaller than the actual gameplay screen, so it's not terribly useful for gauging whether something is too fast or too slow.
- Cannot adjust scroll speed mid-song, which especially hurts when you consider that you have to fine-tune it on a per-song basis.
- No way to preview/test your sync offset aside from manually playing, and you don't really get any units of measurement to work with like frames or milliseconds, just "number feel good" vs "number feel bad".
- No early/late (or fast/slow) indicators, which makes the previous offset adjustment complaint hurt that much more. (The dev roadmap does say there'll be tap accuracy info coming later on, which helps, but this only applies to the results screen, not mid-song.) (they added this a couple of days after release, hell yeah)
- ☢ CAUTION!! ☢ Charting is NOT controller friendly on Lunatic! Lots of heavy usage of 1+3/4+6 chords, which would require holding dpad left+right (or square+circle) at the same time. Not a problem for keyboard players, but worth keeping in mind if you're a controller main.

Small Tech Nitpicks
(these don't make the game entirely unusable, but they're still kinda annoying)
- When the game loses focus, it basically becomes suspended in time (no animations play/progress, the "are you sure you want to erase your save?" prompt doesn't count down, etc)
- Also, when the game loses focus, it doesn't seem to update its location in the alt-tab list, so it gets buried a few slots back?
- The resolution list is pretty barebones. For example, it doesn't have 2560x1440, it just jumps right from 1080p to 4K. (...and resolution doesn't even matter for borderless windowed, but it's still configurable)
- Keyboard icons are WAY too close to their text labels in the UI, while controller button icons seem to be spaced okay.
- Some awkward English word choices in the UI ("Item 1" and "Item 2" for the sub-menus underneath Kagura and Danmaku settings, when "Page 1" and "Page 2" would sound much more natural)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate: Lots of issues have been fixed, game is even better now.

It's Touhou. It's a rhythm game. It has its charm, and it's fun, which to me is all that matters.

I wouldn't feel bad about recommending this to anyone who enjoys Touhou as a series and wants to enjoy it in rhythm game form. It's a bit rough around the edges, and it's missing a few standard rhythm game features, like an early/late hit display, which can certainly be frustrating to veterans of this genre. The developer seems to be aware of this and is apparently planning on implementing those features in the future.

Nevertheless, it's about what I was expecting, and I am absolutely fine with this being the case right after release. I still consider this to be a great game at its core that will (hopefully) only get better with time and effort put into improving it.\t\t\t\t\t
