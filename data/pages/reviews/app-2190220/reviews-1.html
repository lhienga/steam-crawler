\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been eagerly awaiting this game to come out for quite some time, and it honestly exceeded my expectations. Now, I've never played the mobile version as it was unfortunately never released in my country, so this may be a little bias as I can't really compare to that, however it's still an amazing and fun game to play. I also really like the idea of the danmaku stages as well, where you're having play a mini bullet hell stage alongside the actual rhythm part of the game, and it works quite well and still manageable to do both things at the same time if you have the skill set (which judging by other reviews, not a lot of people have apparently, which makes me think if they've even played a Rhythm game before since they struggle to do that 💀) and yes, I've had my fair share of rhythm games as I grew up playing them, so maybe this is a little bias since I'm much more familiar with them, so keep that in mind. It also introduced me to a lot of previously unheard of touhou fan songs that I've never heard before, which are all great and fluid to play.

Now yes, this does have a few cons that you should consider before you get it.
1. The price:
Yes it isn't the cheapest rhythm game on the market, and if you're after a more, robust i suppose, rhythm game, there are plenty others out there that you could argue are better than this one for around the same price, so I'd just do what I did and wait for it to come on sale. While we're on the topic, this game also has some pretty expensive DLC if you want some particular songs, so keep that in mind as well.

2. The story/progression:
Now most of the time this doesn't annoy me, as I like progression and tbh, the story isn't the worst thing in the world, but I can see how it may get annoying for some people who just want the classic rhythm game play style of loading up any song without having to unlock it through the story, but again, not a massive issue for me.

3. Bugs/Glitches:
Like any game, specially newer ones, this does come with it's fair share of bugs. For instance the first song I got thrown into was...unplayable to say the least. as the frame rate and lag was beyond horrendous. However, after I painfully completed that song, it has never happened again, and all the songs play perfectly now. I've encountered some other minor bugs, but nothing really to note.

4. Better games on the market:
Like I said in my first con about the game, if you're simply after the best rhythm/music game money can buy, this isn't the game for you, as it is, in it's current state, lacklustre with what it offers when put side by side with other ones on the market.

Conclusion:

As a Touhou fan and someone who loves the fan made musics, I adore this game and it's a nice change from the other rhythm games out there. Is it the best rhythm game? No. Is it the worst? absolutely not, but if you are considering buying it, at least in it's current form, I'd wait for it to go on sale. None the less, it seems to get pretty frequent updates which is a very good strong start to the game, so i personally look forward to whats yet to come (assuming it isn't locked behind ridiculously overpriced pay walls), but I have good faith for it and already developed a liking to it, and if you're a Touhou and rhythm game fan, this is certainly a must try.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnly recommend for Tou Hou fans or Rhythm game fans... even for the later you can probably find a better six button rhythm game than this there are plenty.

There are plenty of cons with this game. All of the DLC can get really pricey really fast. The Story is so uninteresting I can't bring myself to read it all. The songs are locked behind story mode. Story mode has you play Danmaku stages that try to mix SHMUP with Rhythm game and it kinda just feels like being distracted from a too simple to be interesting SHMUP with a too simple to be interesting Rhythm game which overall isn't a great experience.

That said it's still a solid rhythm game. The charts are hard yet fun. You have most of the regular features like controlling the speed the notes come down. If you played Bang! Dream Girls or that Miku Hatsune rhythm game you know exactly what to expect as what you get here is very similar. Due to this being a standalone version of a gatcha game though you don't need to worry about banners, pulls, and currency which is nice. You will be getting C rankings even with perfect score at the start of Story mode due to leveling though. On the opposite end in free mode where builds and levels don't matter you can fail a song with a S rank score if you screw up the ending of the song. Scoring issues aside, some of these charts are the funnest I've played in a long while granted they feel harder than your average rhythm game to me.


To wrap things up, if you like TouHou this is a solid recommend. Otherwise I would say no, it's too expensive and you can get something similar but more for the same price or less elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

Still i love it and it is definitely the most polished and pleasant game of all I tried so far (notably Musynx and DJMax)\t\t\t\t\t
