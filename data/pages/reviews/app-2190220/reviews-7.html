Gameplay
  • Difficulty section with different keys putting together
    4K are easy and normal, while 6K are hard and lunatic.
    This can be a great discourage for players who play 4K as main, and have no idea how to deal with 6K charts

  • Note speed is based on BPM, instead of fixed ones
    I noticed this thing after playing few songs, even though I have a global setting at 1.7

  • No early/late indicators and bizarre judgement
    Excludes the cases of skill issue, I have no idea how many notes I've hit too early or too late, and it can be difficult for players who aim for AB (All Brilliant) even FP (Frame perfect)

  • Unknown amount of score to reach certain score rank
    For just reach score rank B as an example, I have achieved like 2~3 million in single song, and still not getting the edge of it.

Story
  • Stories are not able to skip, for players who focus on gameplays

Settings
  • Circled numbers?
    I am a bit confused when seeing circled numbers (such as ①②③) in the setting, until I realized it is meant for track (lane in the play field) 1, track 2, and so on

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as I enjoyed my time with the mobile version, I cannot wholeheartedly recommend Phantasia Lost to quite frankly any audience. The new pc version takes aspects from the original mobile one, but also leaves some out. The mobile version had 5 difficulties per song while this version now only has 4. This wouldn't be so much of a problem except for the fact that with this change, the jump between difficulties has also changed. Easy and Normal mode feature 4 key maps while the Hard and Lunatic feature 6, which changes the game entirely. This also has the adverse effect of making the difficulty ratings misleading because in almost all cases, a song with a Normal difficulty of 17 is easier than a song with a Hard difficulty of 16. The scoring system is the same as it was in the mobile version where you would start out only able to achieve a C rating at best but you would slowly build up your cards and characters to allow you to reach SS ranks. However, this system worked in the mobile version due to the nature of gacha in it. You would get on, do a bit of daily grinding, maybe do some pulls with what you've saved up and get that feeling of progression. Phantasia Lost forgoes the gacha system in exchange for story challenge stages. The game is thus in a weird spot where you are incentivised to grind in order to reach high scores and complete challenges, but also lacks the incentive to do just that. The gacha system works because you are brought back day after day with a feeling of accomplishment from getting just ever slightly stronger but when the progress is just vomited out at you with no restraint and no daily pull, it feels more like a chore even though the gacha grind is designed to be a chore itself. Moving on to the challenge stages, they tend to be either one of two things: a mild difficulty increase through requiring you to be a bit more precise and absolute hellish torture that prevents you from seeing anything or forces your notes to fall so fast you're basically playing it with them fully invisible. Speaking of that, The game also suffers from another problem not present in the former mobile version: the note fall speed. As with most rhythm games like this, you are allowed to change the fall speed of the notes, however in Phantasia Lost, this speed is inconsistent between songs as it appears to base itself off of the current bpm of the song. Additionally, when the bpm changes mid-song, so does the fall speed. This was a feature of the original Danmaku Kagura, however it is more pronounced here in Phantasia Lost and is ultimately more jarring due to the previously stated fall speed issue. Phantasia Lost seems to be designed for more hardcore rhythm game players with little in store for the more casual market the original aimed for. In some more positive light, the danmaku mode for Phantasia Lost feels better than it did on the original mobile version, however it comes with the downside of adding a mini rhythm game to it that keeps shifting your focus from the attacks on the screen to the poorly placed note lanes on the outer sides of the screen. Don't get me wrong, I like this addition, it's jus unfortunate that with the current interface for it, its more of a distraction than a fun gameplay feature. All in all, I think the most pressing issue is the fact that due to the difficulty jump added in with the shift to pc, there isn't very much room or incentive for people to grow and play the harder difficulties, which is a shame considering they all tend to have well designed and fun beatmaps. If you don't have the prior skills to start this game playing from Hard mode at the beginning, you probably will never play anything other than Normal mode. The difficulty curve was well balanced on mobile, but with the new Phantasia Lost system, it appears to have become a difficulty wall, with hardcore players on one side and new, casual, and experienced ones on the other.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI mainly played the rhythm game only (Kagura) mode, so this review excludes the danmaku mode.

As it is, the current game have major flaws and it greatly impacts the gameplay experience.

Even though it is rhythm game, the offset ever slowly drifts into fasts and snaps into its original timings after it drifts enough. This causes the player to get less than perfect judgements like GREATs or even combo break.

In addition to this, the game also cannot handle fast inputs, and this is especially evident on very high level charts. The game would delay any inputs it could not handle, and it would then affect the player's judgement in-game. PERFECTs can be downgraded into 1 or more judgement down even when timed well and with proper hardware. In the most difficult chart of the game, proper timings can result in MISSes due to this delay.

Outside these, the gameplay can still be an ok experience, and listening to Touhou musics on itself is a treat.\t\t\t\t\t
