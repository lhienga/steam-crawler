\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr Only buy the game on sale if you're dying for a 2hu-only rhythm game and have 6K skills. If you really want to suffer through story mode, please keep the JP wiki open. Tenshi (sub Marisa) will be your oshi with deleting the poorly-designed Danmaku mode stages.

I'll preface the review with this: I've sunk hundreds of hours into Respect V spread out into its four modes over the past few years and spent an eye-watering amount of money on its DLCs alongside other rhythm games present on Steam. Call me a hopeless whale if you want; there are standards that can't be ignored on a $30 rhythm game when the Steam competition includes Respect V's regular $10 sale prices, EZ2ON hitting DanKagu's price range on sale, Muse Dash's ridiculously skewed content:price ratio, and Sixtar Gate, a similarly budget indie rhythm game that tries its best to carve its own identity. MD and Sixtar include a minority of Touhou songs nowadays while also staying within DanKagu's price range. If you only want to occasionally play a Touhou song in your rhythm game, please give some thought as to whether you still want to pay for DanKagu over other options on Steam.

DanKagu had a crowdfunding campaign that hit 193m yen compared to its 15m yen goal. Yet, it doesn't feel like this funding was put to good use. Although DanKagu has received steady patching since its release, the game still has an overall feeling of cheapness, highly questionable story mode progression, and is not a great experience without 6K expertise. At best, DanKagu is a passable VSRG; at worst, DanKagu's only draw compared to other Steam rhythm games is a strictly Touhou songlist.

If DanKagu raked in 193m yen through crowdfunding, where did all of it go? It certainly wasn't spent on making the planned 102 DLC songs free at launch with the base game's 68 (+ U.N. Owen Was Hero?) given the original mobage already had those songs, and this Steam release of DanKagu is clearly pulling the original's Mitama card art and cheap character Live2D. The idea of needing an extra 178m yen to fix the game, get new song licenses, and add nigh-redundant story stages that don't address story mode's issues at all is still baffling to me.
$30 is also not cheap with the base game's paltry ~69 songs. This price tag feels like it was meant to pay for licensing and re-used Mitama card art. At least the DLCs have better value, being $14 for around 25-26 songs each, or potentially less through Digital Deluxe; and while there's effort made to include new licenses, going straight for DD blind is still questionable at best.

The offsync is a Timing slider ranging from -50 and +50. Not ±50ms, just "-50 and +50". What does this actually mean? I have no idea given I haven't needed to adjust it on my 144hz monitor; but judging from other reviews, even a few ticks have a drastic effect on the game's broken sync.

DanKagu has a ±16ms Perfect timing window on par with IIDX in strictness, so that means DanKagu is a difficult game that still tries to capture casual players into the rhythm game sphere, right? Nope. See, while DanKagu songs consist of Easy, Normal, Hard, and Lunatic difficulties, Easy and Normal charts are 4K while Hard and Lunatic are 6K. This means the easiest 6K charts have ratings that would be in-line with the "hardest" 4K charts...except even Re: Unknown X's Normal 21 doesn't come close to matching some songs' Hard charts in density or complexity. This difficulty design rears its head in certain story mode stages that outright lock you to only Hard or Lunatic excepting, again, Re: Unknown X, which is magically given an exception because you'd otherwise have to contend with its boss-level charts. A recent update alleviates the 6K learning curve by having an "outer random" toggle for Easy/Normal charts to "convert" them into beginner-friendly 6K charts. It's not an ideal solution, but still better than outright buying another game to learn 6K then deal with difficulty lock stages.
But even leaving aside Re: Unknown X Lunatic, many Lunatic charts also have questionable charting (e.g. Machibito wa Kozu) when coupled with the game's timing windows and sync issues. Failing that, many charts stick so close to fundamentals without spicing things up in any interesting way that even low-end Respect V SCs and mid-range EZ2ON charts do. At least the strict timing window can help with accuracy training on the less offensive charts...

This also ties into story mode's broken balance: there is little to no practical incentive to play a Lunatic chart to satisfy Kagura missions as all Hard charts can fulfill their conditions just fine (or Normal if not locked; again, see above) and do not run into nearly as much risk of failing judge count missions or nuking your rank from a few misses. There's only self-satisfaction to be found with using a Lunatic chart here, but if story results don't count for Free Mode records and can't be reasonably compared with others doing the same thing, what's the point of risking another retry on the same annoying gimmicks? This also doesn't account for the constant Mitama card menuing and astronomical grind to counter later story stages using the same song you've played 5 times already...
Danmaku stages are a complete failure in execution. Sure, it has been done with ONGEKI, but that game also finds a reasonable compromise in mixing rhythm and shmup gameplay; and when this is ONGEKI's only gameplan, it better play well. In contrast, Danmaku stages are a great way to become cross-eyed as you try to focus on both the shmup proper and 2/4 lanes of rhythm game at the extreme opposite sides of the screen. You'll end up re-binding all 2/4 lanes to press all of them at once if you see a note, grind out damage up Mitama cards that contribute way more to score (hope you don't have a toaster because of a scoring bug), and keep Tenshi (sub Marisa) on forever to nuke the bosses ASAP and nullify the rest of the shmup part...except when you don't want the boss downed while you're trying to generate point items from spamming bombs! Thanks a lot Hijiri.

The unvoiced story proper is slightly charming at best, patronizing at worst. Mommy Reimu goes to an Ephemeral village in Gensokyo's wasteland that's fixated on a bad lesson, forcefully lectures them on how that's wrong after getting a Sand Sorrow Sickness flashback, then leaves. Later, Marisa goes to that location to look for Reimu in a restored Gensokyo but only comes across Gensokyo residents inheriting Reimu's lesson to an extreme before leaving with a gift.
Lather, rinse, repeat 12 more times.
Most of the charm in these VN segments come from the CGs on Marisa's side or the scenarios themselves, ranging from Aunn breakdancing to break away from old traditions, Marisa ordering around the SDM residents who inexplicably converted the mansion into a hotel and slap her with a 1-star review as she leaves for being an awful guest, or Murasa preparing an apology/reflection letter in advance to give to Hijiri when the former crashes into Marisa to minimize wasting time on a lecture for both parties. Otherwise, Reimu's sections outright read like a book on social-emotional learning for children.

Lastly, the English translation is shoddy at parts despite being otherwise serviceable. "Yuuhei Sateraito"? "Hades" for the Netherworld? I would just write off the translation if all of it was blatantly machine translated, then move on. I can't help but feel this is awfully in-line with the game's lack of polish.

Unknown X (aka AQUA STYLE) is notorious for releasing glorified prototypes before refining them overtime; DanKagu is no different with its first month feeling like EA. Even for Touhou fanatics with 6K expertise, do not buy DanKagu unless it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes. I recommend this game. It's an engaging Touhou Rhythm game, if you'll look past its flaws. (Many of which might be completely absent by the time you read this review) I'd give it an 8/10, could potentially become a 9 or even 10 with enough polish. If you really like touhou and like rhythm games, I'd definitely recommend it. But if you're a hardcore rhythm person who absolutely cant stand when the implementation isn't completely spotless, maybe not.

This review is going to be long, since a lot of it is responding directly to other criticism I've seen.

Since gameplay is the most contentious part of this game, I'll start with that first. To put it simply: the game is fun to play. The songs are engaging and have good charts, and I've spent 70 hours so far doing my best to master them, despite not being the biggest fan of the genre.

The game launched in a... Somewhat rough state, As a novice to the genre, there were features lacking that I didn't miss, because I wasn't aware they were something necessary. But, people are right that things like a fast/slow indicator and automatic note timing adjustment were things that should've been in the game to begin with.

But, those things are in the game now. Saw what you will about what should've been included to begin with, I firmly believe that if Devs make necessary adjustments and listen to feedback, they deserve more chances- which is why I think most of the negative reviews that focus around these issues should really be reconsidered (Not that I think that'll happen...) And, there's still a laundry list of other fixes they have in the works- on a game that I honestly thought was fine to begin with!

If you don't want developers to abandon projects the moment they launch in a rough state, then you owe it to give this game another chance after a couple months of fixes, if you've already written it off.

Some of the decisions in the story mode are... Unusual, owing themselves to this game's equally unusual development out of a mobile gacha game. But, I don't think they're entirely without merit. I think a lot of the issues people have with the systems in story mode is owed to a more general lack of explanation about the game's systems, which is another issue that steps have already been taken to help. The story mode focuses around clearing missions with challenges to unlock cards featuring beautiful touhou art, with effects that modify the missions and help make those challenges easier.

This system itself isn't bad, and I felt compelled to try and collect most of them just by the art alone. Admittedly a lot of the card effects aren't super useful, but I've heard this system being compared to Smash Bros Ultimate world of light, and I truthfully think it's better implemented than that. Some of the challenges are poorly implemented or frustrating, and I wish some of the cards had less generic effects. But those are minor criticisms. I'd much prefer this implementation, where the art still has gameplay effects, instead of having them tucked away only in a gallery somewhere or not implemented at all.

Difficulty balancing can be an issue- the jump from Normal to Hard (4 lanes vs 6) and Hard to Lunatic (Tons more inputs at the same time with one hand) are massive jumps in difficulty, so if you struggle with overcoming big walls in difficulty that might be worth considering. But, when I increased my scroll speed and practiced for a bit, I was able to overcome the normal-hard barrier, so it's definitely possible- and was very satisfying when I did. It's worth noting that lunatic tracks feature some inputs that may be legitimately impossible on certain controllers, so if you're both a high level player and a controller player, that might be worth considering.

As for the story, it's... Serviceable. This game can't compare to the knockout home run something like Mystia's Izakaya offers in that department, but to be honest after experiencing touhou fanwork after touhou fanwork that uses horrible characterization. even a story that only does an alright job starts seeming great. I don't think any characters are terribly misrepresented, and although the story isn't super compelling, it does have fun beats that are enjoyable, especially if you're a fan of the two central characters (Reimu and Marisa)

The story actually does a really good job in an aspect many touhou fangames don't, and that's character representation. Touhou has an absolutely massive cast, which makes it a little disappointing when games instead choose to focus entirely on the same characters every time. Even if they aren't key players, just seeing a lot of more niche characters in story CGs or represented with songs and sprites makes me happy.

I do think that the story should be completely skippable for people who don't care for it, especially since a lot of songs are locked behind encountering them in story mode. So, if having to mash enter for a bit to skip past it if you really don't care for it is a dealbreaker, there's that.

For presentation, the UI is definitely clunky. I'm of the mindset that UI is important, and a great UI can actually offer a lot to a game, and should be applauded. But I also think that so long as UI is functional, its not too big of an issue- and it is functional.

Beyond that, the art is fantastic (Although the live 2d implementation can be a bit weird in the song sprites) and the music choice is superb. An excellent balance of different genres, artists, characters... And, there's the original touhou tracks in here which I really appreciate, since not having them in something like spell bubble really bummed me out.

For price versus content, I think it's fine. Not exactly a steal like some other games (Mystia's Izakaya, my beloved...) But, if you complete the story mode and practice some songs, you'll get your money's worth. It's not egregious like some people make it out to be, especially in a genre that gouges your money sometimes. Compared to spell bubble, a 60$ full price game that arguably had less content... It's amazing.

The DLC is another issue, but I don't mind the day 1 song pack too much. Licensed music is... Expensive, which is why this genre, and especially its DLC often tends to be so overpriced. If you don't want this entire game to just be songs from the touhou games themselves, I don't think 1 piece of day 1 DLC is really an issue. And the DLC is priced well enough... Comparing it to song packs from most other rhythm games, its actually better value than most.

So yeah, I'd recommend this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't recommend Touhou Danmaku Kagura Phantasia Lost (from now on referred to as Dankagu), despite having had a decent enough time with the game.

Let me preface this by saying that I'm a bit of a rhythm game enthusiast and have drawn my line in the sand on what I look for in games like these and things I can or cannot overlook.

First of all a good rhythm game should have good rhythm game features. Dankagu simply does not. For a VSRG with relatively tight judgement windows, setting up the timing is an abysmal experience. The game does not give specific information on how early/late your hits are aside from showing up the number of how many hits were early/late. All you can do is then adjust your timing by some arbitrary amount and then see how you compare to your previous attempt. Key word is "arbitrary amount" as the timing configuration is a slider that goes from -50 to +50. What does this mean? Nobody knows. It doesn't seem like it's milliseconds as even changing it by 1-2 units feels way more significant than it should if you were simply adjusting it by 1-2ms. So it turns into this trial and error as you try and land yourself on that sweetspot.

The ABSOLUTE worst part of this is that the songs themselves do not seem to be consistently timed from the get go. What this means is that you might need -5 for one song, but +5 for another to hit somewhat consistently on beat. What the actual heck. It feels like whoever timed these charts just kinda eyeballed to be somewhere in the ballpark of "on beat" but for a game that, again, has somewhat strict timing judgements, this is simply not good enough. Having a per-song override would resolve this to some extent as the player could eventually time all of their songs themselves, but even that is missing from this game. This is by far the biggest painpoint of this game to me personally and it's really disappointing to see this game come out at this, somewhat steep pricetag, alongside with four day 1 DLCs and fail such a fundamental aspect of being a bare minimum rhythm game.

Another pain-in-the-ass genius idea that this game had is to make scroll speed BPM synced, and while you're able to change the multiplier and make it faster or slower according to your needs, you always need to do it on a song-by-song basis (since the BPM differs between songs = different scroll speed). Thankfully, for this, there IS a per-song override where it seems to save your setting, so once you configure it for any given song, you shouldn't have to change it every time you play that song again, but still, why can't we just have a toggle that lets us choose a single scroll speed value that applies to all the songs in the game? Ridiculous.

Other than that, the game just feels very cheap in its presentation, UI design and graphical assets. The little live2d illustrations of the characters that show up during gameplay are super jaggy and painful to look at. Story mode is super stretched out with massive amounts of text with a lack of consistency in how the story is told, with certain parts being just pure text, full-on book novel mode where character lines are marked by "" and the rest is some kind of narrator providing additional flavor to the situation, but it's inconsistent and sometimes what feels like it should be character lines aren't properly marked so you may think it's the narrator speaking, but then it doesn't make sense, so it has to be the character, so it becomes a headache-inducing stream of words that is hard to make sense of. Not to mention there seems to be a bug where some of the text lines get shown multiple times so you just end up reading the same thing over and over again until you realize the game has bugged out and UGH this is so painful. Some parts are acted by little live2d sprites of the various characters, which admittedly are somewhat charming and serve way better in providing clarity in the writing and story telling, but even these scenes aren't immunte to bugs. It seems that everytime some kind of effect gets added for additional impact, like screenshake or the character sprite jumping up in joy, these effects get stuck and just start looping until the scene changes. So you end up with a constantly shaking screen with character spazzing out. It's impossible to take this story seriously with that kind of garbage happening all the time and again, looking at the pricetag of this product, it's incredibly disappointing that this is the level of quality and polish that we get.

Danmaku mode is hilariously bad, I'm not even gonna go into detail, because it has to be some kind of sick joke that it exists in the first place, well played devs, good one.

I did mention that I managed to have a "decent enough" time and that was when I went into the Free Play mode and just started playing the songs ignoring the story progression and all the other crap. There's a great variety of Touhou arrangements to be had here and I really cannot complain much aside from the fact that over half of these songs came packaged in four 14 Euro DLCs in a product that is already priced at a steep base price of 29 euros. Songs are nice and some of the charts are genuinely fun. Another thing that pleasantly surprised me is that hitsounds feel very responsive and tactile making this probably the only VSRG where I can actually leave them on and don't mind them.

That being said, there is no way this game is worth the price of admission at its time. Developers did release a development roadmap where it seems like they're looking to address some of the issues I've outlined, but we'll see if they make good on their word. Right now this is a ridiculously bad offering and unless you're a complete Touhou junkie like I am, you're better off playing pretty much any other VSRG on Steam over this catastrophe.\t\t\t\t\t
