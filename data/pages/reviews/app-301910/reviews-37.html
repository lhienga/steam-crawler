\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is about 15 hours of SR4 DLC gameplay -- a whole lot less if you make the right/wrong choices -- in a $15 stand-alone game. Positioned that way, it's a natural buy. Otherwise, it is kind of a lame game with good bones. If you played and enjoyed SR4, then you'll enjoy this game as DLC.

As a Saints Row game, it continues the theme and style of their recent games in good and bad way, and avoids some mechanics in somewhat bad way. You play as either Johnny or Kinzie through a hell-themed map eerily reminiscent of Steelport, but everything is on fire and it never becomes day time. Once you're in, its the same old diversions and tasks that have been with us since at least SR2. Meanwhile, you are bulking up your characters' traits (shared -- there is no real difference between playing J or K.) and upgrading the silly weapons exactly like SR4. The number of vehicles on the road seems to have shrunk, but with your characters becoming stupidly-overpowered so early in the game -- again like SR4 -- you never really desire a vehicle.

One of the sub-goals of the game is to collect a set of weapons that represents the seven deadly sins. Some are worth it, but most are so ridiculously slow that they're just not worth keeping around in your arsenal.

There's not a lot of actual game *content*. You have four (plus or minus) generals that you have to rescue, each with their own little story, but their story has no bearing on your own story, so ... so what? Most of the story is told in painted storybook cutscenes, and read by a narrator. It is cheese on the level of Far Cry: Blood Dragon, but instead of beating you with the jokes, there are instead little self-aware moments that made me laugh out-loud. One of the storybook scenes shows the character negotiating with the publisher over the story flow. Kinzie mentions early on, "Hell looks a lot like Steelport..." and Johnny mentioning "Collectibles... I'm probably going to have to pick up a hundred of these things or something..."

So, in the end, it is more of the same irreverent humor and the same grindy mechanics that Saints Row has been known for ... and that's just fine.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery lukewarm title. Feels underdeveloped and short. Story doesn't fit in well, and is just an excuse to fly around as Gat. Don't use mods if you want this game to work correctly. The game won't start properly with them installed, and I wasn't able to play the last level as it wouldn't activate the mission, which I assume is also due to the modding, but after removing the mods I still can't open it, and I've seen at least one other user with this issue online so it may not be limited to modded games. The activities don't hold the same charm as they have up until this point and the audio seems really lacking in terms of quality, and what to listen to. It barely feels like a saints row game. If you play as Gat, some of the commentary you can find is pretty funny and that's probably the best thing about this game. Kenzie's commentary is pretty meh, like they whoever wrote it didn't actually understand her character fully. If you like saints row, this one may dissapoint, but it was fun for what it was standalone. Really wish I could have played that last level though.\t\t\t\t\t
