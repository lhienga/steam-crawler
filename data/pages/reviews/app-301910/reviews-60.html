\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo Gat Outta Hell, essentially an expansion of sr4, if that seemed fun, this might be worth picking up; however, this game relies on save data and files from sr4, so if you have a mod such as sandbox+ on your copy of sr4, Gat Outta Hell will not run correctly (often will crash Windows for some reason). Also customization basically doesn't exist, so far less personalized character design which really seems to go against the primary principle of the saints row series (since now you can't be the boss of the saints, but I guess being gat is alright).

On the bright side, the game really does have a nice flying mechanic, feels better than sr4's "floating" system of movement, which the game world takes full advantage of. Is this game worth full price? NO, buy it on discount, just do a clean install of sr4 before you install this, and be warned, the game is less about story (even though the story feels like a disney movie set in hell) and more about minigames such as insurance fraud, taking over points, etc...

Overall, despite the better mechanics, sr4 has more bang for the buck (and also seems to run way better on somewhat underpowered hardware), so this really isn't worth it.

CORRECTION: To fix the random save problems, you'll need to wipe a file in Steam's roaming files. This will delete all SR4 savegames unfortunately but will make this product run so much better, look on the web and you'll see what's needed. Still not worth buying till it goes on sale (and having played this on a PS4 it does feel more mechanically stable and enjoyable).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaints Row: Gat Out of Hell is an expansion for Saints Row IV, a game that ended the series about as decisively and satisfyingly as humanly possible. Given that, you'd think that Gat Out of Hell would be an annoying and unwelcome extension of the story past its logical conclusion, but it manages to avoid that issue by 1. not being a full installment in the series, so it kinda feels like it doesn't count; 2. going to pretty much the only place Saints Row could go after IV: Hell; and 3. putting in another decisive and satisfying ending (well, at least the one I picked). Anyway, besides not ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ up the whole "decisive ending" thing, Gat Out of Hell manages to be a fun game in its own right.

Gameplay-wise, it's just Saints Row IV again, but with a new flight mechanic (replacing IV's dumb air dash things) and some new superpowers. It's got mostly the same map, a lot of the same weapons, the same basic sprinting and jumping powers, and most of the same things to do. I don't mind that, because all of those things are a lot of fun, but be warned that this isn't in any way a novel experience.

The main deviation from IV is that there's a lot less structure. Instead of a series of missions in a sandbox, it's just the sandbox, with a total of two actual missions and two story cutscenes. You're not so much going through a linear campaign as doing all the activites until you win; this has a side effect of making the game quite a bit easier, as well. That's a bit of a shame, but the sandbox is varied and interesting enough that I don't mind too much.

You also only can play as Johnny Gat or Kinzie Kensington, instead of your personal Boss (although they do show up if you still have IV installed, which is an excellent touch). Again, I don't mind too much, but it's kind of a letdown (albeit one given away by the title, so at least you know what you're getting).

That's not to say that it doesn't have some improvements over IV. Dane Vogel makes a reappearance as a Hell businessman, which is just about perfect. You also get the ability to carry absurd amounts of ammo right off the bat, which I appreciate. The aforementioned flying is also a lot of fun, and the game makes good use of it.

At the end of the day, Gat Out of Hell feels like a budget version of Saints Row IV. The devs clearly didn't have nearly enough time or money to make anything close to the size of previous games, so they settled for a small, really stripped-down version with a couple of new mechanics and a skeleton story. Because of that, it's not as good as prior installments. It is still fun, though; if nothing else, the Saints Row world and characters are just nice to be around, and more time with them is never not pleasant. For that reason, I recommend Gat Out of Hell.\t\t\t\t\t
