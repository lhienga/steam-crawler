\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGat out of Hell is just... No. For players new and old to the Saints Row franchise. Yes, Gat Out of Hell can be fun in some areas, but overall it leaves a lot to be desired, especially when compared to the older installments like Saints Row III and IV.

- The majority of the game is centered around grinding challenges, rather than story. The story is absolutely tiny and feels incredibly basic and rushed, with it barely being 10% of the game. Seriously, it's like 6 short missions total. The rest of the game consists of repetitive and/or grindy challenges and achievements. Replayability is also practically non-existent because of this.
- There are no cool battles or interesting events. Even the final boss is a pushover. Again, this game is just all about repetitive challenges.
- Ragdolled, ignited/stunned, all powers disabled, imps latched onto you, and more. There are just too many enemies with easy, spammable crowd control in my opinion. It's like I barely get to play the game during some battles.
- The AI is both incredibly stupid and clunky, yet at the same time sometimes pretty unfair in their ability to auto-aim.
- The entire game is mostly just Saints Row IV with a hell skin applied over everything. This game would've felt much more fair as a DLC for Saints Row IV.
- Achievements that force the player to play coop/multiplayer are incredibly dumb. Not everyone is capable of getting it, especially not in older games like this where the multiplayer is completely dead and empty. Oh yeah, and there's also an achievement that requires you to be in-game (pause/menus don't count) for 20 hours, even though the entire game can easily be finished in half that time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's start from the top: should you get Gat out of Hell at full price? If you've never played a Saints Row game, buy The Third or IV to get way more bang for your buck. If you have played either of the last two Saints Row games, you'll largely get the same old but less, and certain draws of the franchise are entirely missing. And if you're looking for a faithful follow-up to Saints Row 2, I'm sorry, but not only are you looking in the wrong place, you should probably give up anyway.

Now let's get to the real question: you bought Gat out of Hell as part of a Saints Row bundle and it's sitting in your library, and you're wondering if you should play it? Oh, absolutely!

It is a glorified DLC for Saints Row IV, but there's an all-new unique map with lots of nice scenery, a new set of enemies, revamped powers, a flying mechanic that is a ton of fun as long as you upgrade it enough straight from the start and just enough of the (later installment style) Saints Row characters and humor. Even if you're a Saints purist that only counts 1 and 2 as canonical, it's still a fun standalone game about kicking ass and doing familiar and - for the most part - amusing activities in hell with an angel-winged Johnny Gat.

My biggest gripe is actually that there is an entire radio station full of fitting music in the game but the on-foot radio from IV is disabled. You need a mod to get it working, and then it can drown out dialogue or end up playing on top of cutscene music. What even is the point of including all those songs in the game if you don't let us listen to them while using the intended method of travel?

Also they gave my Boss the anime haircut for no reason. WTF was up with that?\t\t\t\t\t
