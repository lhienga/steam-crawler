\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7/10 I personally enjoyed it as I have enjoyed the whole Saints Row franchise BUT the story is short, like, extremely short. I think i finished the game in about 2 hours at a leisurely pace.

Would I recommend this game to someone else? No, not really, especially at full price. I dont think it is worth the 10 bucks which is why I got it during the summer sale where it was 75% off. This comes after SR4 in the storyline and as all SR games, there are references to previous games so you might want to play the previous games just so you arent lost when Zinyak is mentioned. I personally enjoyed the game and i think it is a good spin off game in the franchise but it left a bit to be desired. You get the whole saints row feel to the game with the character lines etc. but i felt that the game feels a bit rushed almost. The story concept is great but the story execution was quite poor. You have to charge up a meter in order to play the game and end and there are no story story missions like the previous games. The UI, HUD and Game Layout is very similar to SR4 with the new style but the game feels a little bit cramped

Overall a decent game for any saints row fan. If this is your first saints row game, you need to play SR3 and SR4 instead as they are way better and the best in the series. (I have not played SR2 but I hear good things about it and I plan to play it in the next few days). I dont think leaving this as "positive" or "negative" really does much, more of a neutral review as it really depends on if you are a saints row fan or not.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe humor and content of 3 murdered the franchise, replacing all the M-rated stuff with "dildo bats are funny lol." 4 came along and redeemed it a little by leaning completely in the direction of "no reason" even if it still reeked of corporate interference. However, bringing back the only character people wanted and making it about him shooting Satan in the face is kind of endearing. Let's face it, when they replaced gangs and drugs with Hulk Hogan and Shaundi being a screaming Tumblr blog, there was never any going back. At least now you can experience the gameplay that improves on the past couple of ventures (gunplay is less clunky thankfully) with a story that dares you to care one iota about it and you get way less of the modern video game narrative arcs taking up your time with cutscenes you end up skipping anyway. The result is a game where they only expect you to have fun and won't charge you a premium for it (even if they still made cashgrab DLC for this anyway). If you can get and run SR2 with the Gentlemen of the Row mod, do that, it's the best entry in the series. Then maybe try this one out, fly around in Hell for a few hours, relax a little.\t\t\t\t\t
