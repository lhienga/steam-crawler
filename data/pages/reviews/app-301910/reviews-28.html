\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHell, Ain't a Bad Place to Be.... Well, maybe it could be an interesting place to play Saints Row but then game developers said NO.
Let's take a few textures and tell people this is THE hell. We can save some money on the story, characters, game graphics, actually on everything.

The plot is simple. During a party on Saints' spaceship, they unwittingly contact Devil who takes the Boss to hell as the future husband for his daughter Jezebel. And you, as Johnny Gat or Kinzie Kensington, are trying to stop him. You're actually trying to upset Devil, To flood him in that hell. Destroy this one, take that one. The main story is quite short. Around 4 to 5 hours. At least some cut scenes are funny.

There are also 4 side stories with Twins (Viola and Kiki Wynter), Blackbeard, William Shakespeare, and Vlad the Impaler. However, these side missions are all practically the same. The tasks are repetitive and in a while, they will bore you to death. Save Vlad, save William, etc. The game developers took all the game mechanics we know from previous parts and made one cocktail out of it. In the end, they multiplied it 4 times. When I finished the game I was not sure if this is a full game or just DLC. Maybe if game developers turned it into DLC, it would be better.

Pros:
* some jokes
* flying in the hell

Cons:
* the main story is short
* boring uninteresting story
* boring repetitive tasks
* uninteresting scenery of hell\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed the game. The idea of going to h-e-double-hockey-sticks and face Satan himself is something only the the Saint Rows games could pull off without seeming utterly stupid or pointless (as stupid humor is something these games excel at). However, it is still not convincing enough for me to buy this as a totally new game different from Saints Row IV, even if the launch price was lower. This game could have easily be sold as DLC for SRIV. Why? My point are as follows:

- The main story is incredibly short. I am at 97% completion and I have only played for 8 hours. The only things remaining are the optional challenges. I finished the story in about 6 hours (took some time making side stuff and getting the collectibles). If you only do the main quest, you could probably finish the story in about 4-5 hours.
- The mechanics are almost completely the same as they were in SRIV. The "additions" are the ability to fly and some new powers. Although these new powers are fun, you can see the developers mostly rehashed what was done on the previous game. Maybe the interesting twist is that you are able to play as Gat and Kinzie, however I see this also as a drawback as you lose character customization, something I personally enjoy a lot.
- The new world is quite cool, however it is comparatively smaller than the map in Saints Row IV and III. Although it is fun to fly around the first couple of hours, it can get somewhat boring after a while.
- Upgrade mechanics are the same as SRIV, you collects orbs and then buy the upgrades. The only difference is the physical shape. Flight makes it funner to collect them at the beginning, however it gets old real fast.
- There are very cool new weapons, nonetheless some of them (the demonic generic ones) look and work very similarly to the alien ones in SRIV.
- Its seems that there was a plan to make the story longer originally. After you gain control of all the city, some characters from previous games are mentioned as challengers of the new "Rising Saints Power" in the underworld. However, you never interact with them, and the story is only told through panels. This quite upset me as I was looking forward to face the previous enemies with the new allies and use the new powers against them.

In conclusion, the games has a new world, new weapons, new characters, new powers and new activities to gain control of H-E-DOUBLE HOCKEY STICKS. (it is fun to say it, don't judge). However, the obvious rehashed assets and mechanics from SRIV, the very short campaign, the lack of character customization and the small new world leave a lot to be desired. This could have easily be sold as an add-on to SRIV without the necessity of buying a completely new game, even if the launching price was not as high as SRIV. My recommendation is that, if you're a fan of the Saints Row series, buy it when it's on sale (as I did) since even now paying full price for the game for me is not worth it.
