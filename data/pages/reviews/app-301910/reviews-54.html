\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaints Row: Gat out of Hell, much like its predecessors, is an open-world sandbox. This game, however, lacks some of the features we've come to love about the series over the years.
The main one is the lack of customisation that's been a standard for the franchise since the beginning. Vehicle customisation is gone - however the superpowers accessible through playing the game virtually replace the need for travelling in style, with sprinting and flight being much faster than any other vehicle.
On the other hand, I find the loss of character customisation to take some personality from the game. In Saints Row 3 and 4, the character customisation was quite in-depth and allowed for tons of combinations - but now, instead of playing as your own custom Saints Row boss, you play as (in my opinion) two of the greatest characters in the series. Johnny Gat and Kinzie Kensington act as the protagonists this time and you can freely switch between them at any point in the game. While the character customisation is a big loss, the chance to play as Johnny freakin' Gat makes up for it - at least somewhat.

Despite some significant losses, the game's writing still holds up and might be my favourite Saints Row game both setting-wise and story-wise.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off: If you haven't played Saint's Row 3 and 4, start there (I actually recommend going all the way back to the original, if you are willing to play a game that old). Gat Out of Hell is essentially a reskin of SR4 (which was largely a reskin of SR3), but it's still got the same Saint's Row wackiness and enjoyable gameplay that has made the series such a hit. Also, the game spends zero time on exposition. You won't have any trouble following the plot (such as it is), but a lot of the jokes won't make any sense if you aren't familiar with the series up to this point.

Playing as either Johnny or Kinzie, the player rapidly acquires powers similar to those in SR4, but with a demonic twist. Flying (not just gliding) is a new addition to the game, and adds some enjoyable new dimensions to the environment, as well as providing the basis for two new mini-games: barnstorming (flying through a course against a clock) and salvation (a game that challenges you to fly around catching falling objects before they touch the ground).

I'm not crazy about the choice to make Johnnie Gat the protagonist. While Kinzie's "ridiculously smart" schtick is still fun, Johnnie's "Solves every problem with a headshot and/or shoving a gun in someone's face" persona is a bit flat (despite a strong voice performance). I found myself missing the "puckish rogue" characters of previous ganes (such as Pierce and the Boss).

The game is unfortunately quite short, though running through all the optional sidequests stretches the game out without feeling like padding (my time on record is for a complete runthrough including grinding all challenges), and is a great deal of fun while it lasts. Overall, if it's been a while since you finished Saint's Row 4 and you need a fresh dose of Saint's Row madcap wackiness, definitely give this game a playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t
