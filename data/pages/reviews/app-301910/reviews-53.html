\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaints Row: Gat out of Hell is the epilogue of the popular Saints Row franchise and was released as a standalone game in 2015. Personally, while I thought it was fun it isn’t as good as the previous games and feels padded with lots of collectable quests and killing achievements to fill out its gameplay. I think it would have been better served if it was released as an expansion pack to Saints Row 4 as it uses the same engine as 4 and there are a lot less primary missions. All of the superpowers from Saints Row 4 reappear, with the unique items being guns based on the 7 deadly sins and the ability of flight which adds vertical exploration to the game.

Story wise that Saints are celebrating Kinzie’s birthday on the spaceship they commandeered from Zinyak when they accidently contact Satan through a Ouija board and the boss is kidnapped to marry Satan daughter. Johnny and Kenzie go to hell to recover the boss and find the entrance to Satan tower closed. You are tasked with helping 4 agitators in hell (including some ex members and previous bosses) incite Satan to open his doors so you can take him on. Unlike previous games other than the DeWynter sisters and Dane I found the other 3 agitators boring and uninspired, however their cut scenes were done in a musical format, which was new to the series and funny to watch. Maybe it’s because in both 3 and 4 many of your “crew” are actively helping in in the missions, where as in this you just report back. While the story is short, the Satan battle was really good (who doesn’t like beating the crap out of Satan), and each of the 5 endings were very funny. Having Nathan Fillion voicing God was a very good choice (shame his is only a small role).

Gameplay is a little different than previous games. Instead of capturing areas by taking a certain structures and then completing side quests for cash and fighting a boss to progress you are left to cause as much disruption to Satan, filling a Satan’s Wrath Bar which will gain you access to the King of hell once full. The activities that gain you control are the side missions from previous games, and its good to see that the fraud side missions are still included. Cash and XP are still used to upgrades weapons and your character stats, but because the weapon list is very small there is a good chance that you will be swimming in cash by the end of the game. The new guns are just as crazy as before, but somehow aren’t as fun to use against daemons as they were against people. Many of the challenges artificially inflate the game time, like killing 500 enemies with a set power, or using weapon to get x amount of kills, in a normal play through you wouldn’t get anywhere near completing these. Collecting the upgrade spheres for power upgrades are includes and the map/minimap is still missing their location making getting them an absolute pain in the backside.

Graphically the game looks exactly like Saints Row 4, which was released 2 years before it. Visually the game is very dark (being set in hell), with the structures having a mix of gothic and modern design, if you can ignore the fire coming from the windows and holes through buildings. I found that because everything visually similar (either its on fire, looks like concrete or mud) the game is missing that visual pop that the previous games had. Also there is no character customization (Kenzie and Gat wear the same clothes throughout the game though you can switch between them) or crew customization which were big parts of the previous game. The closest you get is importing a previous boss, but that is very buggy and takes a lot of messing around to get it working. The Game is also missing the radio stations that the previous game had, which makes driving the few cars that are available a drag. The voice work of Satan, God, the Twins, Dane and Jezebel only just offsets Blackbeard, Vlad and Shakespeare and doesn’t makeup for the loss of the Boss or the rest of the crew for majority of the game.

If you are aiming for 100% achievements then it’s possible but involves a fair bit of grinding, as the challenges are even more padded this time around.
