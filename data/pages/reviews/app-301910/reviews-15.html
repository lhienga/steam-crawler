\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(98% Completion, 100% Takeover, Casual)
Do not recommend to play as someone new to franchise. Recommend starting with 1 (if you can, I haven't played it) or 2, which is widely accepted as the best game in the franchise (also my personal favourite), despite being buggy (apparently it works better on console), as subsequent games have references to these.

Interesting story, cool powers, new map, maybe a good game to sate thirst of Saints Row players waiting for the new game to come out, however...

[Potential spoilers]
-A lot of aspects are very similar to Saints Row IV - sin orbs are just re-skinned golden CIDs, legionnaires and arch-dukes basically re-skinned wardens, most of the powers very similar to SRIV, takeover missions very similar etc.
-Only took me 4.5 hours to finish the story, and even then between that I was finding collectables.
-The 'challenges' are dull and take more time than the actual game. e.g. killing 'x' enemies with 'y' power or weapon is not fun at all, especially if you've already done all the city takeover missions. Some of the challenges aren't really clear - e.g. the P.O.S. Comet "balls of fire" one.
-Lacks customisation.
-Random weird singing bit.
-Doesn't really have any music, from what I've noticed there's regular background music and then a jarring change to menu music.
-No real challenge in getting the 7 deadly weapons (but that might be because of the difficulty I played on), don't even really notice getting them.
-Final decision doesn't really do anything.
-Occasional random frame rate drop.

While I have a lot of criticisms, that's just because the franchise has set a high bar, and I would still recommend Saints Row: Gat out of Hell, overall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd recommend this game with the caveat that it's not entirely perfect. It's a spin off to Saint's Row 4, which also means that it's short as... well, short as hell, the weapons are highly unbalanced, I mean, Death's Gallows has the voice of Matthew Mercer, explosive rounds AND a rapid fire infinite ammo mode after hitting a certain amount of shots... kind of makes fights trivial in the long run. And the lack of licensed music is a drag, despite hearing some music in cars that drive past you, then go silent once you steal it.

But, saying this... the voice acting in this is pretty much A*, Matthew Mercer, Liam O'Brien, and Travis Willingham from Critical Role all voice characters (and gun in Matt Mercer's case) in this spin off, which is an enormous amount of vocal starpower, even though they talk about tormenting poor Trinket the Bear.

The flight system works surprisingly well, once you've upgraded it, and if you want a laugh, drive in a Rambulance. Yeah, you read that right, a Rambulance. Drive face first into pedestrians (or Husks as they're called in this), and get rewards for it.

Like I said, I recommend as long as you don't mind the negatives. I'd suggest getting it on sale like I did.\t\t\t\t\t
