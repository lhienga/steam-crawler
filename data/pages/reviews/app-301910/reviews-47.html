\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs soon as I was finished with Saints Row IV, I was eager for more. Saints Row: Gat out of Hell is a 4-6 hour standalone expansion to Saints Row IV and takes place right after the main game. Something goes terribly wrong when the Saints decided to play a mysterious board game and The Boss is sent to Hell! Turns out Satan has kidnapped the Boss to marry his daughter, Jezebel. This time you play as Johnny Gat who goes on to rescue the Boss along with Kinzie (who is another optional female player) if you're not interested in playing as Johnny Gat.

Pros
+Saints Row is best known for its sense of humor and satire and Gat Out of Hell is no exception.
+Pretty straight forward game. Doesn't drag itself too long with the story.
+Main Story can be completed in a single sitting. 100% completion takes no more than 20 hours.
+You can easily swap between Johnny Gat and Kinzie Kensington anytime after tutorial.
+Map is half the size of Steelport, but built entirely from scratch, and vastly more imaginative in its design.
+Exciting new weapons and abilities.
+Tons of side activities and challenges to keep you busy for a day or two.
+Flying is much much better than the previous game thanks to Lucifer's wings.
+Cutscenes look much better due to cell shading. All the ugly and low res textures are nice hidden. Nothing's else changed.
+New characters that are surprisingly interesting this time. [see con]
+There's a musical score for those who like that kind of stuff. It was kinda weird to see that in a Saints Row game but whatever!
+Multiple choice based endings (no karma system)
+Boss character model can be imported through SRIV save files by placing them to the save directory but not really necessary.
+No game breaking bugs and glitches encountered.
+7 special guns in the game based on the 7 deadly sins will be rewarded to only those who chose to complete all side activities and challenges.

Cons
-Although up-to the point, Gat Out of Hell fails to tell a satisfying story or imaginative missions.
-Much like SRIV dlcs, the story is explained with Kinzie narrating the whole story from a picture book. This makes the game look really cheap as 60-70% cutscenes are just low budget slideshow.
-Volition had much so potential with supporting characters but they're just there for the sake of forcing you to complete side activities and fetch quests. A little extra effort on more missions and character development would have been appreciated. Even Dane is underutilized character, let alone other supporting cast.
-I admit playing as Johnny Gat was fun but where are the costumes and the customization options we know and love?
-Pretty much everything is recycled from Saints Row IV except for a bunch of new weapons and side activities.
-Despite featuring a new map with unique, Hell is boring as hell!!
-No human NPCs!! I get it, its hell but why replace humans with ugly undead growling zombies everywhere on the streets???
-No licensed music (seriously what were they thinking?)
-The main villain got worse with every new game. Don't get me wrong, Satan could make a really epic 'big bad' but the execution really fails.
-Driving is literally the worst. Seriously just forget it even exists. Flying eliminates the need of flying vehicles so okay....
-Lackluster visuals......graphics pretty much look the same since 2011's Saints Row: The Third.
-Playing as Kinzie was quite annoying but hey! That's just my opinion. You need to swap for at least 2 hours for an achievement.
-Co-Op feels unnecessary when the game is so easy.
-Final boss battle barely lasts 3-4 minutes. It's just there for the sake of ending the plot.
-Anti-climatic ending(s).

Should you buy Saints Row: Gat out of Hell???
It's really important to remember what it is - a standalone expansion. Do not buy this if you've never played any of the Saints Row games before. Saints Row: The Third would be the best starting point. Saints Row Gat out of Hell is absolutely a must buy title if you've played any of the previous title. It's at least worth a shot! Buy it on a sale if you can. This game would have gotten way better average review score if Deep Silver included it in Saints Row IV main menu. Avoid the workshops pre-order bonus "exclusive" DLC. Definitely not worth paying extra $2.

When Can we expect Saints Row V???
By keeping a positive hope, anytime soon. Volition's attempt to make Saints Row spin-off clearly failed miserably dead with Agent of Mayhem's launch in 2017. This doesn't necessarily mean that we're never getting another fully fledged SR. THQ Nordic acquired Deep Silver and currently owns the rights to Saints Row universe (Agents of Mayhem included). I would say 2022 would be the year we get next big Saints Row game. Thank you for reading.

I give Saints Row: Gat out of Hell 6.5/10

From The Playa (the name of the default protagonist) who became a street gang member who became leader of the 3rd Street Saints to becoming an actor/actress in the third game and now the only United States President alive who suddenly got kidnapped by Satan who came from actual Hell.

As crazier this franchise gets. Saints Row Gat Out Of Hell let us play two of the playable characters that are Johnny Gat the returning fan favorite Saints Row character who just loves kill a ton of his enemies and Kinzie Kinzington the former FBI agent who is now with the 3rd Street Saints which became more than just a street gang.

The map inside Hell felt a bit very small. Meaning that I expect time map of hell felt bigger. Clothing shops and garages were removed in the game due I believed it has no use in Saints Row IV, the soul pedestrians felt like it looked like it has been copy pasted but Hell is still Hell, Calling homies have no use in this game and the missions and the story in this game is just a little too short which explains on why the max level this game is only level 20 not level 50. Must have been the budget concerns in this game or something to move on to new Volition projects.

The things that I love about in the game are the weapons that are absolutely fun to use like the the Armchair-A-Geddon which is more like an electric wheelchair that is a shape of a couch that has mini guns on both sides, and the take downs I just like Saints Row but better when playing as Johnny Gat because most of his take downs specializes on knives while for Kinzie is almost the same unarmed take downs but one of her take downs contains rapid punching which I remember about taking down the main controller in Hotspot side activities.

Saints Row Gat Out Of Hell Does Not Feel Like The Grand Finale Of Classic Era of Saints Row but its still a great game if you wanted to play as mainly Johnny Gat.\t\t\t\t\t
