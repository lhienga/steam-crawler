\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn preparation for Saints Row "5" I've decided to go through all the Saints Row titles that still exist (barring the mobile phone games, and Total Control since they both don't exist anymore, and are basically filler) So for context, originally I started the series back in 2011 with Saints Row 2, my cousin owned the game, and basically I played it for him, and even finished it twice...lol, so naturally I have a preference for the older "classic" SR games (mostly due to also playing GTA SA when I was young as well, but I'm not going to use the "gTa ClOnE!!1" meme) However I can still enjoy the newer titles that turned more into a schizophrenic bipolar fever dream for what they are (even if I'm a SR Boomer that prefers the good old days of realistically grounded mayhem with a semi-serious storyline revolving around gang warfare)

Gat Out of Hell is a spin-off and epilogue to the original Saints Row story, essentially put: Steve Jaros himself said that SRIV is the end of Saints Row's story, and GOOH is the epilogue, which means that the old story is over and SR5 is essentially a reboot.

GOOH doesn't focus on that beloved Saints Row storyline formula, at the most there's cutscenes and that's about it, a lot of times they use this hand-drawn "olde style" picture book aesthetic which is pretty cool. So instead GOOH focuses on activities, and diversions to pad out it's length, this is not a bad thing, especially if known first hand, and much like SRTT/SRIV a lot of these can be outright ignored if you just want to fill the "Satan's Attention" meter, and get on with it, but otherwise you must complete all of them listed on the map if you want expanded powers/abilities/weapons/etc and the full ending. Not much to talk about story wise, it's basically Steve Jaros did Manatee Idea balls..so yeah

The activities can be pretty fun, but more or less easy, it's newer Saint's Row...it's catered more towards the casual fans, still Mayhem, Torment Fraud, and Hellblazing are pretty fun and cool. However there are some issues in the line of tedious playing with Altars, and Survival, best thing to do is just to max out stats and the RPG to make it easier, and slightly less tedious. The Dex diversion is okay, but well...it doesn't close his arc properly, and as a "SR OG" it is kinda whack, but whatever.

In terms of weapons, stats, and powers? Well like every game since SRTT there's been this shift towards pseudo-RPG elements, with upgradable weapons...unfortunately weapons start weak, and turn overpowered, also the lack of downgrading also shoots the game in the foot to an extent. A lot of the new weapons can range from useless to pretty rad, such as the Armchair Gun. Most powers felt okay, but pretty weak, they are just to aid combat and debuff, more than replace it. However the movement powers are fantastic, Flying is definitely a high point, and is just perfect, especially when fully upgraded.

I do like a lot of the references and callbacks to the earlier games, especially the classic era, it seems to imply that Volition really wants to go back to the old days, though it could just be book ends, and to provide some closure. Interesting enough, this game doesn't particularly feel like nu-SR, more or less a mix of it's own thing, old saints row and new saints row.

The map is a massive improvement, no more Steelport, though it does share some of it's aesthetics, and styling, however unlike Steelport, it actually makes sense, plus they didn't copy and paste buildings and areas everywhere, every district is it's own thing and has plenty of unique buildings, art, etc, but much like SR1 it's the case of a great city let down by dead npcs.

If you're a Saints Row fanatic, have a hard-on for New Saints Row, you're waiting for SR5 and going through the series again, or completely bored, and just want balls to the wall fun with a lack of interest in storyline I say get the game, it's extremely cheap on sales. Otherwise if you want that tightly crafted story, casual or you're a boomer that despises new Saints Row, you are best not worrying about this game.

I had fun with it, even if it lacks a lot of things. 7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
