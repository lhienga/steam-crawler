Saints Row: Gat Out of Hell is a standalone DLC that serves as an epilogue to the events of Saints Row 4. It has very similar game mechanics, such as super powers, upgrades, and the same style of missions and side activites. What is different is the story, and the setting (you're now stuck in Hell, DOES have a different map).

The DLC's main content is fairly short, enough to finish in a few hours. There are plenty of side activities and collectibles, achievements and such to collect though. I spent maybe 20-25 hours completing everything the game had to offer.

What I liked:

-The same goofy sense of humor I loved from Saints Row 4.
-The multiple choose-your-own endings. They had some really cool ones! (and one that is a total troll)
-The new map. Different this time, instead of reusing the steelport map.
-The flying. So fun!
-Fun variety of weapons, with really cool upgrades!
-This title has the least amount of bugs in the whole series (in my experience). Volition games are notoriously buggy. It DOES have that really awful freeze/crashing issue that was prevalent in 4 and the Third, but I found I could fix it by running the game in compatibility mode for Windows 7 as an administrator.

Here's what I did not like:

-The grind. Unlike every previous Saints Row title, there is no way to earn money passively in this game. Which wouldn't be a problem except that one of the challenges is to purchase every upgrade for every gun, which must be done if you are going for 100% completion. Because of this, you must repeat side activities over and over again, or farm demon kills with a special gun that grants you extra money dropped from killed enemies. I didn't see the point of this at all, and it got old very quickly, as this took 4+ hours of pure grinding. I feel like this was simply a way to pad out gameplay, and it shows.
-The full piece musical number. Don't get me wrong, it was amusing as stink, but sooooooo cringey. xD
-Killing Dex. He looks exactly the same as the other souls, only difference is he has a purple bandana, and has an crew of body guards protecting him. While he does have a few voice-lines, he isn't at all satisfying to kill. You don't really feel like you got revenge. It isn't hard, there is no cool cutscene, and he isn't really dead, because he appears again sometime later so that you can kill him all over again. Pretty disappointing.

So, what is my recommendation? This is a fun game for the most part, and definitely worth playing. If you are big fan of the Saints Row series, I think you will enjoy it and have a fun time with it, but if you aren't, then it probably isn't up your alley. In either case, I recommend waiting on a sale for this.
