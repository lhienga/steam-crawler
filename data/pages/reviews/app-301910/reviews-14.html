STORY:
After saving humanity from the mighty Zinyak, Saints (Johnny Gat & Kinzie) were tasked with saving the President of The United States, formerly Saints' Boss, from the darkest place of all places... HELL.

WHAT CAN YOU EXPECT:
-Medium-sized sandbox.
-Continuation of the SR4 story.
-New flight mechanics.
-Less side activities compared to SR3 or 4.
-Short, 2-4 hours mission structure.
-Dark comedy and musicals.
-Johnny Gat.

Unless you like grinding achievements and going for the 100%'er... this game will give you about 5 hours of playtime.

It's worth a buy, but only during sale.

NOTE:
If you are worried about stability issues that are present in SR4, I can assure you that none of that is present in this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an easy recommendation as long as you know what to expect. This is nothing more than stand-alone expansion content, just a bunch of sidequests packaged and repainted. It is neither deep, nor must-play but it is absolutely fun to play even at its full price value.

Story-wise, The Boss is kidnapped by Satan to be marry his daughter. Johnny and Kinzie hop of the first portal to hell to shoot the devil in the face. No epic storyline, no major plot twists, that's about it. As you fill a progression meter 3 story cutscenes play out and you meet Satan and shoot him in the face. The entire story can be completed in 4-5 hours easily and is enjoyable enough.

Progression is tied ENTIRELY to a score meter and completing activities. I actually beat the game prior to even meeting the four main story characters or unlocking all the powers, so I can confirm its purely score based. Even the majority of the "Story Missions" are characters asking you to complete a specific set of the side activities rather than anything unique. In most cases for me, I'd already completed the side jobs and just got rewarded each time I talked to a story character.

I will say that I had more fun with Gat out of Hell than I did Saints Row 4, in major part because it was much smaller and less epic. It was good dumb fun and I especially enjoyed just flying around hell. The views were distinctive, the flight controls smooth and overall it was just a great experience.

This game is far from must-play, but if you enjoy Saint's Row there's enough here to love. As long as you don't go in expecting a full-game with lots of content, you'll get your moneys worth.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe next part of the Saints Row series is definitely worth playing.

In this part, we are in hell, and instead of creating a character ourselves, we play with Gat and Kinzie.

In the game, there are very similar things as in the third and IV, such as souls, which are nothing like the clusters from the previous part. Most side missions are also very similar to those of the previous part.

The plot is by far the second best of all the games in the Saints Row series, but unfortunately, also the shortest. We are in hell and our task is to kill the devil. While doing quests, we'll get a few interesting allies such as Shakespeare or Blackbeard, but by far the best character we have to deal with in the whole game is Jezebel, the daughter of Satan who wants to break free from her father and helps us defeat him. Jezebel totally won my heart during the game.

The storyline of the game itself is fairytale-like and written in poems and songs.

The atmosphere is very good and goes quite well. The cutscenes definitely give the whole atmosphere of the game.

Of the things that are definitely on the plus side,

-the available weapons are very interesting to use and each one is quite different, just like in the previous parts (although the big pink friend from the previous parts is missing here).

The graphics and special effects are very nice This is without a doubt the best-looking part of Saints Row (which is not surprising given that it is the newest part at the moment).

-cutscenes don't have 30 fps anymore and it's possible to watch them (and they add a lot to the whole gameplay and I definitely recommend watching instead of skipping)
Findings are plentiful and collecting and searching for them is very easy, making it fun to do 100% of the game.

-challenges (i.e. additional tasks like upgrading all weapons in the game to the max) are much better than in previous parts, although there will be similar ones as well.

But there are also things that are definitely downsides to the game and very much detract from the gameplay itself:

-the storyline is terribly short, it is impossible to enjoy it.

-Tragically small map, it should be much bigger.

-Little DLC, or more precisely just one DLC, which does not introduce much content to the game, or rather does not introduce it at all, it's just one new weapon and a reskin of wings. 

-Definitely too low difficulty level compared to available upgrades.

-very weak or rather horrible economy; at the beginning you earn way too much and at the end of the game and doing 100% achievement, way too little ( this is the biggest minus I have to face and while maxing out the game at 100%, I had to play annoyed for a good 6 hours)

-only a few vehicles, a total embarrassment. I was disappointed considering the previous parts. There is no garage, no customization of vehicles. For good measure, there is not even a garage where you could potentially park the car.

-There is a lack of real estate. In the third and IV, you could buy any store, and here we can not buy anything. Literally nothing. Even weapons stores are available in advance, or more accurately, they are automated and scattered around the map, so we do not have any passive income that would greatly facilitate the late game .

I miss the development of Jezebel's plot here. The storyline could have been taken much further.

The game itself gives the impression of being lacking due to the aforementioned drawbacks and could really have been released as DLC for IV.

In general, I recommend this part, which, in a way, ends the story of the "good old saints." It is really atmospheric, and the endings, of which there are a couple on average, end this chapter of the saints. Unfortunately, we have to reckon with huge downsides.\t\t\t\t\t
