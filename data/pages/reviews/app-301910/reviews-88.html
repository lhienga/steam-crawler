\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by saying, I'm not recommending this game at full price.

If this is your first Saints Row game, don't start here. The game was incomplete because of the closure of the studio, so the game is not a complete experience. If you're already a fan of the franchise, read on.

The game's humor is so good. I got it for $3.75 or so, and it was well worth it. The depiction of Satan is wonderfully done, and I can only imagine how much cooler it could have been if the game had been finished.

If you didn't like Saints Row 4, you might not bother with this one. Personally, the addition of super-powers hurt the franchise for me. Suddenly, I was dis-incentivised to try out the myriad vehicles in the game, or the cool weapons. There are a lot fewer vehicles in this one, and the sensation of being able to run at lightning speeds and flying becomes a stale sandbox experience after a while, especially if you played #4.

The quests are nearly non-existent. The quest-givers were in place already, so the developers opted to make their quests hinge on completing challenges instead. (Fly through the bubbles, capture this control point, defeat 3 waves, etc.)

In summary, if you don't look at it as a full game, you'll enjoy it if you like the others. The way I see it, for three dollars, I got to play a game that would have just been shelved or lost to time. The bits that I got to play of the game that might have been were fun.\t\t\t\t\t
