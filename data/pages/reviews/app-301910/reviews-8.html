\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for a review on this game you've come to the wrong place, but if you're looking for an outstanding pie recipe you've come to the right place.

Ingredients:

6 tablespoons unsalted butter

1/2 cup white sugar

1/2 cup brown sugar

1/4 cup water

1/4 teaspoon cinnamon

1 pinch salt

5 apples - peeled, cored and sliced

1 pastry for double-crust pie

Directions:

Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).

Combine butter, white sugar, brown sugar, water, cinnamon, and salt in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil, remove from heat and set aside.

Roll out half the pastry to fit a 9-inch pie plate. Place bottom crust in pie plate; pour in apple slices.

Roll out top crust into a 10-inch circle. Cut into 8 (1-inch) wide strips with a sharp paring knife or pastry wheel. Weave the pastry strips, one at a time, into a lattice pattern. Fold the ends of the lattice strips under the edge of the bottom crust and crimp to seal.

Pour butter-sugar mixture over top of pie, coating the lattice, and allowing any remaining sauce to drizzle through the crust.

Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes. Reduce heat to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C), and bake until the crust is golden brown, the caramel on the top crust is set, and the apple filling is bubbling, 35 to 40 more minutes. Allow to cool completely before slicing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWelcome to Hell, where good franchises go to die!

I think by now everyone knows the truth about this game. It's a standalone that feels like an expansion, it's painfully short, and a lot of things which made the success of the serie are gone. No customisation of any kind. You're limited to 2 characters, most of Hell feels like it's just an other adaptation of Steelport yet again, ennemies globally use the same "types" and AI, powers are back with a vengeance, and instead of super jump/glide/speed, you can actually fly this time. It's overpriced for what it is, and most people will tell you even at -50%, it's arguable if you could get it or not and get your money's worth.

Out of those things, let me tell you why I can't recommend the game. First, it tried to make something different. A kind of open story akin to what Just Cause 2 did. Just Cause 2 had a system where by creating chaos in a country, you progressively opened new missions, encouraging you to just roam around and blow stuff up, collect supplies and do side missions. Panau, the country in which Just Cause 2 took place was HUGE. You had a lot of content. So this formula worked. In Gat out of Hell, the map is even smaller than Steelport was. You have more collectibles than actual real content. And the way the game traces your progression to unlock new parts of the story is very very wonky. As such the game suffers from a big problem of pacing as far as story goes. For example, you're supposed get the help of 5 characters at the beginning of the game. Let's say you don't prioritise that to do side activities or just challenges, or have fun roaming around and causing mayhem. Well, you can get in a situation where you will be asked to rescue your allies... Despite you having never met them. That thing also force you to wait until after the rescue to have access to the tutorial for the powers(not that you really need them because let's face it, they are very similar to the ones you had in SR IV).
This, is a very big no-no as far as game design goes. It doesn't help that the cut-scenes tend to occur at very weird points as a result of this way of progressing due to yet again a pacing oversight. There are way to put your cut-scenes without breaking the flow of the action, by having just short call telling you to go to a certain place, then launch the scene, for example. Placing them abruptly after a side activity or a brutal gunfight is a poor choice, especially if you get teleported afterward.

This game also continues in poor design with the questionable choice of putting some vehicles in the game, and forcing you to use them for challenges, despite the fact the superpowers again kills all the meaning of having vehicles, due to the fact you go faster by flying. SR 2 and 3 were nice because they remained in the boundaries of action movies and human limits to some extent. Meaning vehicles were the way to move around, so using them was normal. With the additions of superpowers, Saints Row is no longer Saints Row... Vehicles are pretty much useless if they aren't tanks due to the huge protection they provide and the firepower.

The scenario is painfully short, and even the small amount of comedy there is doesn't manage to save it. The choice of making a lot of characters voiced by Liam O'Brian is also stupid. Liam has no voice range, and everybody knows it because his voice is all over the place... As such, you have Shakespeare and Vlad the Impaler who sound pretty much the same. I will let that sink for a moment. You do not make 2 main characters of the game have the same voice actor, except if there is a very good reason for that. But Liam O'Brian is a bad voice actor in general, who gets by due to the fact he pretty much rules the voice casting in the US. They have a nickname for him and a few others, and the ones in the know knows what I'm talking about. At least they could have given us Steve Blum for Vlad, that would have made things better.

The game doesn't even have music proper, probably to save money on rights for said musics.

As such, Gat out of Hell places itself at the spot of "game you play to spend time because you're bored and can't play anything else". And it's expensive even for that spot because it's "oh-so-short" and doesn't even delivers quality wise. It's a cheap product made with low quality materials in the hope of scrounging a few bucks\t\t\t\t\t
