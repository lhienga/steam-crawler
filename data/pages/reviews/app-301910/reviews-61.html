\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR:
Cool at first, then gets repetitive. Hellish, even.

Saints Row: Gat out of Hell really let me down. When you first turn it on, it gives you a really good impression, with an awesome view of Hell, and you can play as Gat and so on and on. But after you get past the first 2 or 3 introduction missions, the quality starts to slope down. The missions are just side missions from Saints Row IV with slight changes. I must admit some of them were cool at first, like the new twist on Fraud, but after a while it got old. The only way to progress through the game was to keep doing activities again and again until it got tedious. The story also lacked, pretty much the only thing to do being "do stuff to make satan mad". There was also no music, and little to no dialogue, the characters having only a couple of voice lines that ended up looping quite quickly, and the character having little to no involvement in the overall story. The gameplay was good, with the wings being quite fun to use. But overall I'd say that you shouldn't get this, and that if you liked Saints Row IV, then get the previous games instead. There was also a musical number. I think that speaks for itself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rate the open-world, third-person, action/adventure (mostly action) game Saints Row: Gat Out of Hell 3.5/5. Pretty good but it really should have been a lot longer so get it for cheap during a steam sale. It's not really long enough to be a true, full game in itself.

This game feels as though its developer may have pushed it out to be sold as soon as its most basic development was completed but well before a truly detailed plot was ever created. Either that, or it really was meant to be a mere expansion for Saints Row IV. Still, I really think this had the potential to be a true sequel in its own right and this game's extreme brevity is a missed opportunity for the Saints Row franchise.

+What little story this game has is very well done and there's some good humor and dialogue to be found (smetimes literally in the form of collectible items). The truly bizarre Disney/Broadway musical sequence which occurs roughly half-way through was particularly funny and unexpected.

+This game features the same good controls, adequate sound and pretty good graphics that are on par with Saints Row 3 and 4 and has the same general lack of significant bugs. The game runs fairly well also.

+Fairly detailed, fairly random stuff plays out (mostly random lightning strikes and police cars crushing vehicles and ambulances hitting people) to the passers by as you go around the city. Havoc you cause also affects the game's general public nicely as well. As in previous Saints Row games, the world feels fairly interactive even if that feeling doesn't truly reach great depths.

+/-Good background music but no vehicle radio stations (though, bizarrely, vehicles driven by NPCs (non-player characters (aka: computer-controlled characters)) occasionally drive by listening to music which you don't have access to). To not feature even an attempt at funny, hellish fake radio stations is a frustratingly obvious missed opportunity. Your character should also have a portable radio (as many people have now, listening to pod casts and so forth) to listen to music as he runs/flies around the city.

+/- Great, fairly well-realized hell-scape setting which perhaps most closely resembles the hell-scapes in the movie Constantine. However, despite how the setting is wonderfully realized and detailed, the game's main plot mostly fails to take advantage of it. There are large areas and numerous locales in the game world which practically beg for some story mission to occur there. It really seems as though whoever created the game's landscape really expected the game's plot to be a lot longer. With the exception of the various repetitive side quests/mini-missions and fairly uninteresting collectables, there is a lot to see but not so much to do. (Much like how it was in Just Cause 2). It feels like a shame for so many interesting settings and locations to go largely unused.

-Numerous missed opportunities, in terms of gameplay, humor and plot. This game could have parodied so many different other games and movies. This game could have actually done a parody of Constantine, Devil's Advocate, or think about it, any other movie which mentions hell. Hellraiser. Anything along those lines. Missions could have been parodies of the entire Doom game series. But No. This game features so many missed opportunities (perhaps due mostly to how short the main plot is) that the entire game could itself be viewed as one single giant missed opportunity. ...And the game could have had some Heaven areas too for added comedic potential.

-This game's main plot is way too short and the side-quests get repetitive fairly quickly, particularly as they are usually so similar to side-quests found in previous Saints Row games. This causes the entire game to feel more like a mere expansion of Saints Row IV rather than a true sequel (which it really wasn't meant to be but should have been).

-No character customization. You can play as Gat or Kinsey. You cannot create your own character. Not being able to customize a character is one of my main dislikes about playing those 3rd person games which do not have good character customization; having to look at whatever boring character the game designers decide to give me for hours and hours. Front and center, what is on the screen throughout most of Gat out of Hell? Either Kinsey's Jean-wearing ass or Gat's jean-wearing ass. Sorry, but that gets old pretty fast. Neither one is really all that hot, particularly in mere blue jeans. At least give me a coat to watch sway side to side (like in Max Payne). But really, I want some choice in deciding which ass I get to look at as I make the character run along, or at least, what to dress that ass up in.

-The game does not keep track of how good/evil you act beyond temporarily alerting enemies to your presence. Your actions do not affect the plot beyond some last-minute, half-baked choose-your-own-adventure book styled options at the main plot's conclusion. And honestly, those text-only "Choice-of" book-games usually do a lot better than most actual 3D games (including this one) when it comes to giving you honest input into what ending you wind up getting based on your character's actions throughout. The multiple-choice ending of this game does nearly nothing to increase replayability. I chose one ending and then, of course, watched all the others on youtube. Big deal. It would be far better if your actions, from beginning to end, determined the ending. In that situation, you could at least possibly have a motive to try playing the game a different way at some future time, if only to see what you have to do to get a different ending. As it stands though, Gat Out of Hell has very little replay-value. So buy it for CHEAP.\t\t\t\t\t

Gat out of hell is legitimately a better game than saints row 4, to the point where I wonder why wasn't gat out of hell a full game with sr4 being the joke expansion. There are so many things better about this game than sr4 I don't know where to start.

For one, the developers actually bothered to make a new map this time instead of copypasting the one from saints row 3 again, and it's a really good map as well. It's amazing well designed and it clashes the fire and brimstone with urban hell EXTREMELY well, to the point where I want to see a complete seperate game in the same setting.

The superpowers this time around are FAR more fun and creative than the ones in saints row 4, as they actually change functionality instead of just being a reskin with different effects, ranging from holy blasts, to summoning imps, to a massive vacuum, to moving arc pylon, ect.

My personal favourite is the worship aura, an ability that you activate and then it passively makes everyone around you bow down and worship you even as you shoot them in the face.

The movement system here is also FAR more fun than the one in prototype or saints row 4. Instead of just super running, superjumping and gliding, the new movement system is super running, superjumping and FLYING.

The flight mechanics in this game are incredible. They are so polished and so fun to experience I wish that a full game about hell using the same mechanics will be made one day. Another reason I wish this was a full game seperate from saints row is because of the lore.

The lore of the characters here is also 10 times more intresting than anything presented in sr4 and even this world's hell and satan have intresting lore, that makes me feel like volition had much bigger ambitions for this game. With just 3 new characters, Vlad Tepes (Better known as Dracula or Vlad The Imapler or "The original kebab remover") Shakespeare and blackbeard, in litterally only 5 paragraphs they develop these characters more than any character old and new in the entirety of sr4.

I want a full game with this lore and mechanics.

The gameplay is amazing and the lore makes me hunger for more.

Honestly? If you have to chose between this and saints row 3 or 4, buy this.

It's much more polished, much more finished, much more self contained, and is overally an amazing game that was constrained by budget (Due to begin a wacky one off expansion) and even in spite of those constraints ended up having mindblowingly amazing movement and very fun combat.\t\t\t\t\t
