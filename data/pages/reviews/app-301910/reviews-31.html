\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst and foremost, I want to say that this game in the Saints Row series is very fun and is a bit more on the serious side compared to Saints Row 4. Assuming the person who plays this game is already familiar with the series, playing as Gat and Kinzie and experiencing their personalities first hand already provides more enjoyable characters to play as unlike your custom "Boss" in SR3 and SR4 who is unbelievably corny compared to how they were portrayed in SR2 and SR1. How Gat handles Vogel and the other supporting characters still shows that he hasn't changed one bit... unlike the series itself. Causing mayhem and exploring the city is very fun and gets even more fun once you unlock the Titan. While the series has unfortunately gotten corny, the gun play and game play features still are quality from Volition. You may now be asking why I'm not recommending the game when I'm saying pretty good things about it, the answers are below:

This game (just like Saints Row 4) is a MASSIVE slap in the face to old school Saints Row fans. Although this game is a bit more serious than 4, how do you go from being gangsters in the street trying to clean up the city and then becoming the leader of the gang after the 1st leader betrays you, to having your best friend go into Hell itself to save you? Like I said in my review of Saints Row 4, SR3 is where the series started getting childishly corny but 4 and this game is where the entire series just goes downhill. At least Gat and Kinzie along with Vogel keeps the tone of this game more direct and serious unlike 4. There are exceptions obviously like the Disney musical inspired cutscene with Jezebel. It is an awesome cutscene and everyone did an amazing job getting their characters into the musical but come on... from a series that started off serious? Come on Volition... If I wanted to have a cheesy musical cutscene, I'd go and play the Kingdom Hearts games. As a fan who started with SR2, I'm still very disappointed with Volition. You don't "NEED" to make this series differ from the GTA series because there are other open world games out there that are also seen as "GTA clones" but they have their own differences to not only make them still stand out from GTA, but also still make the game(s) successful (Watch Dogs series, Sleeping Dogs, Cyberpunk 2077, etc.) Volition please, the new generation of gaming is already here so it would be very appreciated if you guys make a Saints Row game that makes SR3 - Agents of Mayhem take place in an alternate timeline and thus that new SR game acts as the proper SR3 taking place directly after SR2: Corporate Warfare with a more realistic plot of the Boss taking aim at Dex.

The only other reason I wouldn't recommend this game is because it is very short. Not only is it very short but the endgame is extremely shallow once you've completed everything and got all of the achievements. Causing mayhem is fun but the demons are bland and too easy to fight unlike the mix of gangs and cops from SR1, SR2, and SR3. Speaking of challenges, Volition deliberately made them a grind fest in order to make this game feel like a "full game". I went through this once already when I first played this game on my PS4 but this time on PC, I simply used a mod that makes all of the challenges more reasonable to get. If I wanted a grind fest I would play Destiny 1 & 2 and believe me I would because I miss those games so much... After 20 hours playing this game, I have 100% it and got all the achievements except "Twenty-Twenty Vision". Somehow according to my stats, I've only been in Hell for 11 hours. Not sure how that's possible but I'm not going to idle my PC for 9 hours just for one achievement.

Anyways with all of that being said, I also must point out that at least Volition took the time to design an entirely new city which while it is small, it is MUCH more lively than Steelport. Still not even close to how amazing Stilwater was however. Oh and I also must give props to Volition for the satisfying takedown animations Gat has with his dual knives. Lastly, THIS GAME DIDN'T CRASH AT ALL! I played this with a few mods on and it never crashed! This is a big positive factor compared to SR4 which crashed often and for no reason. Overall, I don't recommend this game however if you happen to catch it on sale, definitely get it as the fun factor does justify a discounted purchase unlike SR4.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaints Row, Gat out of Hell is a spinoff game of Saints Row 4 where you play as either Johnny Gat or Kinzie Kensington and must rescue the leader of the Third Street Saints from Satan himself and the armies of Hell. Now since this is a spinoff game, I wasn’t expecting any major gameplay changes or a huge story and this game still managed to disappoint me. It honestly just feels like Saints Row 4 with a new coat of paint. Almost everything about it is the same as Saints Row 4 except instead of aliens and space themed its demon and Hell themed. You get the same superpowers with slight variation, instead of gliding you fly but they function the same way, most of the side activities are the same aside from maybe one or two. My biggest complaint is the story, there isn’t one. Instead you are given a very brief tutorial, then dropped into the hub world and told to go do side activities. You’re introduced to 4 characters who you team up with but they don’t really add anything other than context for doing the side activities, which is pointless because you’re overall goal is to fight Satan and save the boss. After doing enough side activities you’re treated to a cutscene that shows what Satan is up too and then you have a boss fight, then the game just ends.

The game itself only took me about 3 hours to finish and afterwards I tried completing it just to justify my purchase but none of it was fun. All of the achievements and challenges are such a grind because they’re made like it’s a full game like Saints Row 4 where you can do them as you play, but since they’re the only thing to do it all just feels like grinding.

Normally I’d say if you like the last game, you’ll like this, but I don’t believe that’s the case here. It doesn’t offer much as a game, but rather feels like a giant DLC expansion pack. It feels like a very bare bones Saints Row game and I’d honestly just look up the cutescenes on YouTube since they’re the only things that really tell the story.
