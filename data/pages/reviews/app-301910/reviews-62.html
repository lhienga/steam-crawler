Cons: it does get rather grind focused at the end. The story is shorter than the amount of time it takes you to get all the achievements and complete 100% of all challenges.
Need a partner to get one of the achievements, but that's just my personal preference.
Actually hard to achieve some of the achievements if you play on hardcore mode. Personally I knew this would be a one and done for me so I went hard mode first time around. But that's less of a con and more of an observation.
After about hour 3-4 with enemies you come to a point of been there done that, but it is a short game.
Overall I would recommend it's a good (short) game.\t\t\t\t\t

Saints Row: Gat out of Hell

Saints Row: Gat out of Hell is a Stand-Alone Expansion (DLC) with its own world and story mode. The story takes place as an epilogue to Saints Row IV.

The Story

I'm not going to spoil anything much, so I'll try my best to explain the story here of what's important.

You're able to play as Kenzie or Johnny Gat, and I have to say that's very great to pick and choose between the two!

The story is short, like very as I have only 5.6 hours and have 80% of the collectibles completed already and finished the story.

The Cutscenes for the DLC are actually decent. It had that silliness of the good Saints Row quality here and there. Some parts had me going with some chuckles specifically with the reference of some type of Broadway Musicals that they put in part of the game. Not going to spoil it on what it's about though, that's up to you to find out.

You'll also be able to meet some new characters that you like or may not like. Dex is one of the people you get to beat the Hell off. Literally.

The Similarities with Saints Row IV and Gat Out of Hell.

You can tell this game is very similar to Saints Row 3 and mostly IV specifically.

You have your collectibles, like the Data Clusters to collect. But in Saints Row: Gat out of Hell, these Data collectibles are now considered Soul Clusters. In my opinion, that's kind of lazy in a way and it feels like a copy and pastes to me. Either way, it still had me hooked to try and find every single one to collect. You also use the Soul Clusters to upgrade yourself, such as flying. Yes, you have wings and can fly.

You have your takeovers again, you complete everything for the District then you own it.

You also have your powers back. You have your Super Spring, Flight, Stomp, and Blast. These are also Upgradable as I mentioned already. There are two others to mention that are kind of new to the DLC:
Summoning: Titan/Tower/ Imps.
Aura: with Coldfire/Vamp/Worship.
(The game will explain this more to you, trying my best not to spoil it.)

The Guns are crazy, just like how Saints Row IV became. The amount they give you is a nice as I do believe there's 20 to pick and choose from in this DLC, some are just copy and paste though.

My Final Thoughts

The price of this DLC remains neutral for me, It's really how you look at this DLC as a cash grab of a Copy and Paste from Saints Row IV. $15 is the price that's being asked, but for a sale for sure will be even better. If your patient enough, usually the whole Saints Row series is on sale for $7 altogether, including the DLC's and this Expansion. It's worth it!

If your a fan of the Saints Row, or just want to play a game that just makes no damn sense, then this game will be a good choice for you. The game works the same by how Saints Row IV was. You become a sheep and grinding to find all the Collectables for the upgrades you can get for your weapons and powers.

For a rating scale of 1-10, I would give this a 7.9/10

It's alright for the time I have been playing this, but by how short it is is just bleh in my opinion. Still, though, it's not completely bad of a DLC. I just feel like more could've been done, but oh well, at least we got something that was acceptable.\t\t\t\t\t
