\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who wanted to replay the series but dreaded doing so because of how repetitive Saints Row III and IV were (they legitimately had the same map,) expectations were pretty low for Gat. It actually turned out to be a short and sweet taste of bizarre Gat-focused DLC that I never knew I wanted - and absolutely loved.

Without getting into specifics, you end up in hell and can play either Gat or Kinzie from prior games. I didn't play as Kinzie, but Gat was still fitted with a good bit of weaponry and abilities to keep the short play time in hell entertaining. Since you are basically the fastest mode of transportation in the world, I had a lot of fun maneuvering around the city without any clunkiness in movement. Challenges and side quests fill a lot of the game up, and without it the story would probably be less than 20 minutes long. If this is something you've enjoyed since SR2, it is manageable. If it isn't, then this is a very optional game that is self-aware of its weak position in the Saints Row storyline, with an ending that could possibly undermine the entirety of the series. That's the honest level of absolute absurdity that I have come to cherish with the series that Volition has developed, and I will always see it as the last great game that they've come out with.

If you are looking to play through the series again and don't have the time, I think this will satisfy a craving for older SR gameplay with entertaining combat and wild story. Just don't buy it at its current price - wait for a seasonal sale or weekly deal.\t\t\t\t\t
