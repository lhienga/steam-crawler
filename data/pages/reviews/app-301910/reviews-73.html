As much as I want to recommend this game I simply cannot, SR:GooH has this overwhelming feeling, from the get go, that this should've been a DLC for SR IV, not a completely new game. It's a reskin on SR: IV with an underwhelming amount of content (Like no real customisation for weapons/characters). I was upset how SR:IV was essentially SR: III, but I gave it credence that the super powers and new game mechanics made it worth the buy, this, however, just feels like laziness on the producers part and greediness, if this had been DLC I could easily find myself recommending this, it definitely shouldn't be a stand alone game.

The storyline, I do admit, seems interesting and quite fun, typical of any SR game which I've come to enjoy. This is something only a good SR fan would enjoy but even then, I wouldn't pay over $10 for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a game, not a DLC as some claim it should have been. It runs about 4 hours(or roughly the same length as the DLC for SR3 or 4), and was never more than 20 bucks(I am talking Canadian funds here), same as the season passes for the earlier games.

The game itself is pretty much what anyone who has played SR in the past would expect. There is a lot of humour, and there is much much much much violence. It is similar to SR4 in that you are able to move great distances quickly without need for a vehicle, which is disappointing as the driving and the radio were among the positives of the series.

I have yet to hear anyone say this is their favourite SR game, nor do I ever expect to. If you accept it for what it is, then this is a worthwhile purchase, readily available on sale. While it is far from the best game in the series, if you have the other games then there is a lot here for you....just not as much as SR 2 or 3 or 4(whichever was your fave)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat you need to know about SR:GooH

Pros:
+ Superpowers and Wings!
+ Fun weapons to use, but the fun ones are not too practical
+ Contains some good humors that actually gives me chuckles a few times
+ New maps; although some part are recycled straight from Steelport (from SR3 and SR4)
+ Musical
+ If you like edgy atmosphere, this is for you I guess

Cons:
- Keybinding issues; I can't bind to 'G', 'Y', 'N', etc. This is an old issue that has been reported to Volition and no action has ever been taken
- Game is too(, I mean REALLY) short (finished the campaign in 4 hours, even then I spend too much time collecting cluster and upgrades). For comparison, the maximum level you can attain in this game is 20, whilst in SR3 and SR4 it's 50; you would reach near those levels as you progresses on the main storyline naturally
- No 'real mission' here; you are only offered mostly mini games/side mission called 'activities' and 'diversion' as your true objective is to piss of Satan enough that you lure him out and kill him
- No character customisation as you play as Gat or Kinzie (not the 'boss')
- Superpower is OP and makes the game even easier (and faster)
- No sweet sweet radio stations (or maybe I'm too dumbass to realise it and beat the game while only listening to generic hell background sounds)
+/- You can choose 5+1 endings to the game. Unless you made a copy of save file prior to final mission, you gonna need to redo the game from beginning again to watch all 5 endings

Overall, this should have been an SR4 DLC (if this is a DLC, it would be a hell of a DLC he he, hell). I had fun playing this game, but it's too short and honestly it's only worth it if it's on sale and you REALLY like Saints Row franchise (or enjoy Saints Row 4 thoroughly)
