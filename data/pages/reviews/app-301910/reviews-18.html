\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is somewhat fun. But it's like Saints Row IV with only 60% fun stuffs left. And 40% boring or downright annoying stuff. Also it always crashes after finishing 1 "Hard" Torment Fraud challenge I don't know why.

Recommended only for BIG Saints Row IV fan. SR collectors. Or else try it out when on Sale

"Flying is fun and relaxing!"
To be honest the most fun thing is this game is the mode of travelling. Flying around downtown hellscape collecting "Clusters" power-up is the most fun and relaxing thing in this game. It's like Lo-Fi to Sky-surfing chill. But this could be fun only when you have upgraded enough Flying abilities the very first few hours it's limitation will make flying frustrating more than fun and take sometime to get used to.

Sloth Rating : 9/10

"Combat is still fun but not as much as IV"
But when it come to combat mechanic. It's almost like Saints Row IV.... minus some combat features. So your option to bash, shoot, slash, bonk, torch, freeze... etc. are still here but feel limited compared to more selection of combat method of IV. (Also you have less option for special powers and some of them feel useless even with upgrade.) Also while enemies vary to a certain extend you will be more likely to face the same types of enemies from the beginning to the end. It's fun to find out how to deal with them in the early game but 2-3 hours later they are just basically things that get in your way.

Wrath Rating : 7/10


"You play as premade characters without any customization... at all."
If you like creating characters or customizing your char with some weird fun things on them this game is absulutely not for you. You'll play as "Gat" or "Kinzie" I don't mind playing as Kinzie but you stuck with her same tank top outfit from the beginning to the end of the game. I mean cosmetic sense of progression is like some extra flavors to the game. Now it's more bland. Talking about no creation of the main character. The game even represent the main character from previous installation in a bland, vanilla, default character no matter how colorful you created your character back in those installation.

Envy/Pride Rating : 2/10


"Story is too short. Characters need more screentime to be relatable."
Meanwhile the story isn't anything much but still have some cheeky humours like SR usually have plus the musical drama... in hell setting... is quite good parody. But it's extremely short, like only 1-2 hours of story mission. While Gat and Kins is somewhat likeable characters I wish the Satan and his daughter (I even forget her name) have some more screen time and more role in the story.

Lust (Characters charm) Rating : 5/10


"Collect-a-thon is still fun."
Many collectibles are still here in this game to almost the same level of IV. Except that there are less weapons and less special powers to collect. But there are still numerous things for you find and some secret to unlock while flying around. In short, exploration and collect-a-thon is still fun but with less sense of growth and progression compared to older games in the franchise.
Greed Rating : 9/10
Gluttony (growth) Rating : 6/10

This would be my own 8th deadly sin. I still recommend this game even though I have been frustrated dealing with some aspect of the games and feel like good SR IV's features is missing. It's still okay-ish game on it own. It's might just leave you wishing for more.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood fun to be had. Great way to kill a couple hours, if you have 'em on hand.

GAMEPLAY
"Gat out of Hell" follows the same style of gameplay as SR4 but significantly more streamlined. You'll find the player and vehicle customization options are absent among other things, but there's still a broad arsenal of weapons to collect and upgrade. The "7 Deadly Weapons" based on the seven cardinal sins were particularly amusing to collect, and well worth it, because they outclass everything else in their respective categories. One thing I found particularly praiseworthy is the new revised flight mechanics - Johnny flies more like a bird or a plane than superman a la the Boss in SR4. There's a bit more of a challenge to it, but once mastered, it is so damn satisfying!

STORY
Though paced more rapidly than previous SR installments, the story in GooH is not of lower quality. It fits perfectly with the goofy, wacky, over-the-top style we've gotten used to by now with this franchise. GooH also touches on plot points and characters from all the way back as far as the first Saint's Row, which I loved - it was great getting to see and hear from these fan favorites again. There are also 5 endings to choose from, each one absolutely worth watching. Luckily you don't have to play through the whole thing again to view them! There's also a bunch of collectible audio journals from the characters, my favorite being Satan's own commentary and guidebook as to how Hell in the Saints Row universe works.

VISUALS
A dark, hellish recreation of Steelport, the same city from SR3/SR4, but with its own spicy demonic flavor. Floating spires and islands, tons of lava, giant bones, castles, and much, much more. Soaring over the city on your angelic wings is a consistent visual treat. In a way it reminds me of Todd McFarlane's "Spawn" comics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaints Row: Gat out of Hell seemed to be an interesting seperated game from Saints Row IV.
You can play Kinzie or Johnny Gat who both got locked in hell and try to find a way out by fighting the devil! The cinematic cutscenes were done quite well and I had a good laugh as soon as the devil started performing like in a musical for example. The voice acting was well done and I really enjoyed the way the story was told. Guest appearances of personalities like Shakespeare or Blackbeard surprised me for good. The size of the city is quite small and once you step outside into the city and check one block, you already know how the rest of the city is gonna look like. If you played Saints Row IV before, you will recognize the similarities and be familiar with the gameplay of different powers and weapons. Both characters now have wings to make flying look cooler! (I spent most of the time just flying around)

Now why do I still give a bad raiting to this game? First of all the game could be finished in 2 to 3 hours max. if you only focus on the story. Due to many outside activities you can spend more than 10 hours easily like I did. And that leads me to the answer to the question. This game feels like a "compilation" of mini games instead of a real game experience. There are tons of mini games or "challenges" you can do and they take a lot more time than the actual story. I felt like an intern who was doing all the jobs noone else wanted to do and I became really dissapointed by the end of the game.
If you liked Saints Row IV, I am sure you might like this one, too, but the price is way too high for this game "experience" so you better check it out on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough my online play says I only have 24 minutes of play in, Its really more like 15 or more hours. I have finished the game and made all of the different ending choices, so I think I'm qualified to offer a few words on this game.

First of all, I will say this: I am a Christian. And though life has challenged my faith, this game has not. Through out my life my faith has grown stronger. So, I take God seriously, but I do not take this game seriously. Glad we got that out of the way.

What this game is NOT: Saints Row 4 re-skinned. The world is new. The physical abilities are new. Playable Characters are "new". By that I mean you get to play as Kinzie and Gat, and get to know them better. They both have surprisingly funny commentaries. I don't think I'd want to meet them in real life...but it added some chuckles to the game. The level design is actually pretty cool. The sky-haze effects make it annoying when flying into and through some areas.

I initially didn't want to play this game when it came out for two reasons, 1) I knew I couldn't play as my SR 4 character and didn't really care about any of the other characters. 2) Its in "Hell".

Well, number 1 is still an issue, but not as big; Kinzie and Gat are both somewhat entertaining to play as.
Number 2 turned out to be unfounded. This version of "Hell" is pretty far removed from anything depicted in Biblical or other religious sources. GOOH's version of Hell seems like Disney bought the rights to Dante's Inferno and made a theme park.

Moving on - Remember gliding in SR 4? Well you can literally fly in this game. The other Halo powers are an addition, but the real attraction to this game for me is the flying. Who doesn't want to fly? This game is probably the best representation I've seen in my long but limited gaming experience. I used to do flying sims and driving games and FPS exclusively. Dropped the frst two and expanded my gaming to be much more pedestrain. This game feels like the flight sim should feel. It has a decent blend of reality while enveloping iyou in a sort of invisible nerf bubble you so you don't Lara Croft yourself into a street sign or pointy bits while you're zooming around. Making a landing transition to Super-Run would be an improvement. Maybe it is, but my landings were never smooth transitions.

Incidentally, according to my testing around the 3.17 mile highway loop - my test course. I can super-run at 85mph, fly at 110mph. Average. It feels faster in the game, but the numbers don't lie.

That about covers it for me. The story was sort of lame and cute, if you want to play through a heavily fantasized and farsical Catholic version of the afterlife for 10 hours, destroying minions of the Devil, creating havoc, and blowing stuff up...and flying...then this is the game for you. Did I mention the flying?

Suggested upgrade: merging SR4 and GOOH so both worlds are available to fly in, as the Boss.
