\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite the fact that this one was marketized as a stand-alone game, it feels more like yet another DLC for Saints Row IV. It begins on the Saints’ spaceship, it’s narrated by Jane Austen and, well, it has the same mood with Enter the Dominatrix and How the Saints Save Christmas. Only it’s much bigger than those. Not as big as a proper stand-alone game (you'll be able to finish the story in only the few hours), but still bigger than those short story DLC. Actually, for the first time in the series it includes the brand new city. Which is, as you can guess from the title, isn’t actually a city, but… Hell. It’s pretty small, but, well, it’s a new city.

Don’t expect playing as your old character from Saints Row IV, though. Even though this game supports your old saves, you’ll only need them to see your old character in the cutscenes. In Gat Out of Hell we’ll play either as Johnny Gat, or as Kinzie Kensington. No objections here, since everybody loves these two. I mean literally, those two are like the most popular characters in the whole series, so, seeing them kicking butts in the stand-alone project feels nice. Also, the Hell is filled with more stuff to do and collect, so, if you liked Saints Row IV, here’s your chance to get even more fun with it. There are also some nice surprises. Like you’ll meet some old characters from the previous games, will take your revenge on some of them, ally with the others, etc. The humor is still there too. How would you like a mobile armchair with the guns as a weapon, for example? In other words, we have a new city full of fun here.

Unfortunately, there are some really serious downsides too this time. First of all, there are no more story missions. At all. The whole game revolves around so-called “wrath meter” and all you’ll need to do is to fill it to get to the final boss battle. How to fill it? Simple - do stuff in the city. And when I say “do stuff” I mean exactly that - do anything. Complete challenges, collect stuff, do activities, kill demons, explore… Since there are wa-a-ay too many things to do in Hell, you’ll probably finish the game long before you’ll finish with the city. Thankfully, as usual, you’ll be able to continue playing after the credits will end. There are also multiple endings this time, but you won’t need to do anything to get them. You’ll just select the one you want after the final battle.

Another downside is flying. Remember how fun it was in Saints Row IV? Well, forget about that. Now it feels much more realistic. Which makes sense, since it isn’t simulation this time, but, well, it isn’t that fun. Actually, before you’ll fully power up your flying power (again, you’re doing that by collecting clusters), the whole thing will be a royal pain in the neck and you’ll hate it with all of your heart. Here’s a hint - don’t even think about doing flying activities before that, because you’ll suffer hard. And controls aren’t making it any easier for you. I played the game with the Xbox One gamepad and both me and my gamepad hated it. Just think about it - you’re supposed to hold the LB to fly and control your direction with the left stick. It was something like that with the flying vehicles in previous games, but back then we used to press B once to switch into the flying mode and then it was all up to sticks. Having a combo of LB and left stick is like the worst idea ever. So, flying isn’t fun to begin with and thanks to poor controls it feels even worse. It'll get better, but it'll never grow on you. There’s also no radio in this game. At all. Which makes exploration to feel a bit boring.

So, I can’t really say that I recommend this game to everybody. Quite the opposite, if you’re not a big fan of Saints Row series (specifically, Saints Row IV), I’d say, avoid this one. Just because there’s almost no story, it’s pretty small and flying isn’t as fun as it supposed to be. But at the same time it isn’t that bad and certainly doesn’t deserve to be downvoted. It certainly doesn’t worth the price of the full game, but if you liked Saints Row IV and will be able to pick it up with 50% (or more) discount, go for it. It isn’t as fun as Saints Row IV, but it’s somewhat close to it and includes a lot of new things to do.\t\t\t\t\t

Saints Row: Gat out of Hell
(Scroll down for a tl;dr version.)

I started my Saints Row experience with Saints Row: The Third and I quickly fell in love with it. Everything in this game was so much over the top, but in doing so it was funny, and, moreover, incredibly entertaining. Still I was sceptical towards Saints Row IV; I read some stuff about it that I did not like. But I was wrong. Having superpowers was a great addition to the game series. So it was no question whether I would want to play Saints Row: Gat out of Hell.

Despite this game has really no story, it is even more badass than its prequel. I mean, now you can now really fly and **** it, you can punch Satan in the face! Nice.
And when I say "no story", I mean it. Before starting the game, I as usual took a look at some reviews. Many of them stated that this game has no story, but I could not believe it. But now, after finishing it I can tell that it really does not have any story.
Why did I still like it? Because it exactly focuses on what I loved about its prequel: The superpowers and the weapons. Gat out of Hell is absolutely only about mastering challenges, which were, at least in my opinion, only a side-thing in Saints Row IV. But this is not even bad, because many of them are fun. I disliked only some of the challenges, e.g. the ones that were related to some guns that felt strange...

To sum this up:
Pros:
+ Focus on entertainment: Blow the **** out of hell and its citizens
+ Related to the previous point: Be a badass superpowered hero
+ ~20 hours of fun, iff you do the challenges
+ Good gameplay; actually the same as Saints Row IV

Cons:
- No story, just side-quests
- Some of the challenges are really annoying. I still don't know what the hell this Last Supper weapon is good for. For me this gun is completely useless.
- Compared to the sequels: No customization: Neither character, nor cars (but the car customizations already started to be useless in Saints Row IV)

TL;DR
If you like what the Saints Row series has become with Saints Row IV, you will also like this part. And if you liked Prototype, you will also like this game. But do not expect too much. Saints Row: Gat out of Hell is rather a DLC to Saints Row IV than a stand-alone game. In addition, I would not spend 15 bucks for it.

If you liked the story of the previous Saints Row games and want to know how the Saints' story continues, then pray that there will be another sequel, because this game has no story at all.\t\t\t\t\t
