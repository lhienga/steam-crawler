\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaints Row: Gat out of Hell
-----------------------------------
Played it on release, never reviewed it
-----------------------------------
Saints Row: Gat out of Hell is a prequel to Saints Row 4.
And just like that game, its over the top, weird and crazy.
You cant customize your character, you play as Gat.
A huge character in the franchise and he has gone to hell.....

The gameplay is almost the exact same as it was in SR4.
You can fly, have powers and more.
But instead of a Matrix Style world, you are in actual hell.
And you fight demons, instead of aliens.

If you enjoyed SR4, then this is the game for you.
If you like the gangster style of the OG games, then i would not get this.

All in all, this is a really fun title.
Enjoy the game !\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery, very sadly, I have now finished all five endings of "Saints Row: Gat out of Hell" which is pretty close to being the ideal Saints Row game. After starting where Rockstar refused to continue -- preferring to make movies than the fun and funny games they started out with -- Saints Row became, IMHO and I know many disagree, "Stupidity, The Game", which I thought had culminated in the perfect Saints Row IV. No, in truth, it culminated in the even better Gat out of Hell.

Not that it IS perfect. There is but ONE thing wrong with this: brevity!! It is not a full game, it's only a barely ten hour (for most people, I'd wager, twenty hour for we who are having a blast) DLC.

This DEE-EE-EESERVED to be a far longer game because it had such potential. Yes, it's a (standalone) DLC rebadge of IV, in a way -- they recycled the city, again, and yes the gameplay is a rehash of IV -- but they clearly invested a lot of effort in making it fresh and new. Hell feels like a completely different city, the flying is simply perfected that collecting the 930 "souls" (orbs) is giddyingly fun, it just lacks more story, which is embarrassingly short and mostly just "do all these side missions", while the writing and worldbuilding is perhaps the best that there has ever been in a Saints Row title. Sadly, the ending makes very clear that there was much more that they could have done.

It's been eight years since this game came out, but it still felt great to play. And given the sh-tshow that the reboot apparently is, maybe you're thinking this is worth a look. Yes, I thoroughly commend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaints Row: Gat Out of Hell is a lackluster spin-off to the popular Saints Row franchise. It offers very little in terms of new content, relying instead on rehashing elements from previous games and delivering a shallow and uninspired story.

The game is set in the afterlife, with protagonist Johnny Gat and kinzie Kensington venturing to Hell to rescue the leader of the Third Street Saints from Satan himself. The setting and storyline are both intriguing, but the execution is lacking. The characters are one-dimensional and the plot is predictable and unengaging.

The gameplay is also underwhelming, with repetitive missions and limited options for customization. The controls are clunky, and the weapons and abilities feel watered down compared to previous games in the series. The open-world aspect of the game is also lacking, with a small and uninspired map that fails to capture the sense of freedom and exploration that was present in previous games.

Overall, if you're a fan of the Saints Row franchise, you may find some enjoyment in Gat Out of Hell, but it's unlikely to meet your expectations. The game feels like a lazy cash grab rather than a proper addition to the series. If you're looking for a good time in the world of Saints Row, it's better to stick with previous entries in the franchise.\t\t\t\t\t

The last desperate gasp from the corpse of Voilition Software

At its full $15 price tag, it's slightly less offensive than the $60 they wanted for Saints Row III.1 IV.

It's even buggier than the previous games, and while it won't really hurt your gameplay experience, it says something about the lack of care put into the product. I had the game crash quite a bit, but the game is always generously auto-saving.

The story line is a little cheeky and fun, yet even at its brisk and light pace can't sustain itself. Not that I think anyone holds the story in these games in high regard.

It's full of mundane, uninspired check lists of every combination of weapon, enemy, and vehicle.

Some of the activities are infuriating and mind-numbing tedious to complete, and there's endless amounts of pointless crap to collect.

The weapons are wonderfully outlandish, but aren't very different in execution because there's no strategy to the enemies, who are mere fodder no matter how much of a bullet sponge they become.

As usual, the balance breaking level progression in the previous games will quickly turn you borderline invincible.

The flying is fun and is the only major difference between the previous games. The city is at least not a carbon copy like IV. It's still enjoyable to run, jump, and explore, as is collecting the orbs with your winged powers.. Everything else is really poor to awful and forgettable.

I'd only recommend this to hardcore Saints Row fans or anyone looking for a lot of poor content for a few bucks. It's more over the top stupid fun that's really not worth your time unless you're really bored. There's maybe 2 hours of actual decent content in this, but they expect you to play it for at least 20 hours to get everything. It's always on sale for $3.74 USD or less. Make of that as you will.

There is no Steam Cloud support, so you will lose all your progress.

It's more Saints Row III and IV but held together with sticks and glue in polish and artistic integrity. Its existence was a way to squeeze one last use out of the game engine and franchise before destroying it with Saints Row (2022); in which squeezing one out was given a different context.\t\t\t\t\t
