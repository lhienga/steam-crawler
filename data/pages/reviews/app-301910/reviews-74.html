\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good expansion overall.
After upping the craziness even further in Saints Row IV, it would seem the only logical follow-up other than more aliens and interstellar and/or time travel would be interdimensional travel and going to Hell itself as seen here in Saints Row: Gat out of Hell. The game plays very similar to Saints Row IV with the basic powers (in fact, it really could have been DLC) but with a bit of added emphasis on the flying ability. The powers and weapons are pretty fun to use but aren't quite too effective until upgraded a decent amount. The weapon challenge values are a little high and take a bit longer to achieve if you've already completed a lot of activities so your best bet is to actively go after them while you do ones like survival or extraction facility takeover. There are a lot of great jokes and references to both previous games and pop culture just as you'd expect and the story is pretty quick, simple and enjoyable with a nice selection of endings. Seeing as this is just an expansion and pretty short (and the fact it takes place in Hell and you play as Johnny Gat or Kinzie), the limited selection of cars and lack of additional outfits are both forgiveable. However, there are absolutely no radio stations or music to listen to while you conduct your mayhem which is quite disappointing and a missed opportunity for some good heavy metal. According to Volition, budget restrictions and licensing were the reasons behind no radio but what is especially odd is that while you get no music, you can actually hear some other cars playing some metal as they pass by! My only real annoyance was that you initially earn wages quickly but it levels off abruptly once activities are all completed and requires you to do a good amount of grinding in order to finish upgrading everything. Unfortunately, diversions don't earn too much. The only one that seems practical for profit is stealing APCs and I cannot tell you how many times I accidentally ran into one and had it fly into some lava and explode. Thankfully, the Diamond Sting and Vacuum Stomp really help for getting money while killing demons before getting interrupted by the Arch Duke despawning everything in the middle of a huge firefight and having to defeat it and build up more notoriety all over again. Despite its flaws, the game overall is definitely fun and nice for some good laughs. I recommend getting it while on sale if you're really into the franchise.\t\t\t\t\t
