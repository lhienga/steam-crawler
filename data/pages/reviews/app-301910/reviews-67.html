\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter the insane fun of Third and IV I was eager for more, and Gat Out of Hell delivered. Granted it's a much shorter game where a lot of the lifespan comes from the open world activities, but I still found all of it addictive gameplay. The big new thing - that being able to fly instead of merely gliding - is difficult to get to grips with at first but extremely rewarding once you do. Upgrading the ability and then using it to fly around the city is tremendous fun and it's great to see a number of activities focused around it. There are other fun special powers to play around with too like petrifying enemies with stone blasts or summoning a fleshy tower that impales foes. I don't think much of the aura abilities but the rest are awesome to unlock and use.

Screwing around in the open world is also more fun to me than in IV, although still not quite to the extent of Third. Thankfully there's no enemy as annoying as the sphere tank thing from IV, and causing enough trouble causes an elite enemy to appear that's fun to take on without any weird button mashing minigame to deliver the final blow. Resetting notoriety is also more straightforward once you've had enough of causing chaos.

The humour you expect from a Saints Row is indeed here. After all, one of the main overall goals is to go shoot Satan in the face. There isn't quite as much focus on the story as in other Saints Row games but you're still given some amusing scenes and solid backstory behind the happenings in Hell back and during your own visit.\t\t\t\t\t
