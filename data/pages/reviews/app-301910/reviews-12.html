\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith a heavy heart I have to give a negative review to this game. I've played and completed Saints Row The Third and Saints Row IV and I loved both of them (co-op issues and bugs aside), but this one unfortunately disappointed me for multiple reasons. Keep in mind that I'm a completionist and I played this game mostly in co-op, so I'm reviewing this game from those points of view.

1. This is just a personal opinion, but the game was a bit too short for me. I get that it's just a spin-off, but I found it a bit too short for my liking.
2. Some cutscenes interrupt the gameplay, which I found extremely annoying, especially if you're working on getting challenges done. To be quite honest, I'm not exactly sure what triggered them (I think it was Satan's wrath level increase, but I'm honestly not 100% sure cause they'd get triggered while I was doing something else entirely and not paying attention), but it was quite disruptive to the gameplay.
3. There is way too much grinding for the challenges, which made the game quite tedious at times. I've completed all challenges on both SR The Third and SR IV, where I didn't mind the grind, but on this game it was a bit too much and too repetitive.
4. A lot of these challenges literally just consisted on waiting for the AI to do its job (example, the titan power where you spawn a titan and you have to wait for it to kill demons). If I'm playing a game, I want to actively play it, not sit back and watch the AI do stuff (badly) while I eat a snack.
5. The AI is dumb as hell. The challenges I mentioned above were truly painful cause the AI is slooooow and just plain dumb. I get that this is an old game, but holy hell what a pain.
6. Co-op was still somewhat broken, even if thankfully not to the disastrous levels of SR IV. Either the host or the guest would sometimes crash or freeze, and sometimes missions would not sync or count (example, marshalling grounds would not count for the guest, and torment fraud would crash constantly when played in co-op).

I don't have much to say regarding the plot, it was neither terrible nor great. I love the usual "stupidity" and eccentricity of Saints Row, so overall I enjoyed it, but it wasn't an exceptional plot. The humor however was great as always, especially Gat's.

(Despite my negative review, I am still recommending this game to people who are not completionists, who play in single player, and/or who want to experience the SR IV eccentricity a bit more. It's overall an okay game, I feel like it would've been more enjoyable if taken lightly and chilled. From my completionist point of view it was a bit rough (as I explained above), but if you're not that kind of player I think it might be fun for you.)\t\t\t\t\t
