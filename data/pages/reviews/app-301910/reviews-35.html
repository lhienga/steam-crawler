\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood for a few hours, but don't expect a full Saints Row game here. Gat out of Hell is basically an expansion pack. There are very few proper story missions, so the majority of the game will just have you complete side objectives. Most of these are just reskins of the same old diversions from Saints Row The Third and Saints Row IV (i.e. Insurance Fraud, Mayhem, Survival, etc.), and the one brand new diversion is... not particularly great.

There is one particular moment around a third of the way through the story that stands out, but aside from that, Gat out of Hell is nothing to write home about. The best word to describe it would probably be mediocre. If you must play it, wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWithout being a completionist for the collectables, the game only took 10 hours. On normal, never died, only had to do one challenge twice to get gold on them all.... that's on Normal, but still. Super powers again made it easy. It's fun to bounce around and blow things up, though the world doesn't feel very alive. No outfits, crib, or interactions in this one, and only a few different enemies. This is probably the only Saints Row you shouldn't feel bad for obliterating the people walking down the street. :) I like that it has a mix of old and new characters, though there aren't many in total. I don't remember what I paid for this, so I'm fine with it. Wouldn't pay over 15 for a 10 hour game unless you reeeealy need your Saints Row fix. The main Saints Row games would be a much better place to start.\t\t\t\t\t

- Lots of references to past and present Saints Row characters
- Lots of action
- Multitude of challenges to do
- Cheeky yet funny dialogue
- Excellent flight mechanism and concept

Cons:
- Playing Saints Row 4 before playing SR:G.O.H I didn't see my creation of the Playa during the intro and ending cut scene and was instead met with the default Male 1 Avatar (This issue never occurred in the Xbox 360 version of the game)
- Ridiculously fast notoriety gain for some of the Aura / Weapon Challenges making the completion of the challenges agonizingly time consuming for no reason
- Lack of Co Op activities despite it being Single Player focused yet Co Op compatible

The Pros in my opinion however far outweigh the cons as it is a spin-off from Saints Row 4 and only big time fans would be excited just to play as Johnny Gat considering he is a fan favorite since the first Saints Row.\t\t\t\t\t

I still remember with my other account, how I turned my thumb down to Saint's Row IV not realizing that it was always supposed to be the less-serious, more mayhem-y version of GTA. Saint's Row has grown on me since then, after I understood that it's not a GTA-clone and it doesn't want to be. Saint's Row Gat out of Hell is a continuation to the fourth game. After the boss (your custom character from IV) and his gang (Pierce, Shaundy, Kenzie, Gat and some others nobody gives a damn about) beat the Alien Overlord, they were left with nowhere to call home. The Alien invasion has really taken a toll on earth, and the Saints are now stranded in space, aboard their spaceship, waiting for somebody to actually figure out a plot for the next game (Ha Ha Ha). They were celebrating something and then one of them gets the brilliant idea to use a Ouija Board (because it's always fun to celebrate by communicating with demons). In a twist of fate, they get in contact with Satan, who wants his daughter, Jezebel, to get married to the most powerful human in existence... So he pulls the boss into a warpgate, to... *sigh*, yup, HELL. Gat and Kenzie decide to follow after the boss to save him and here begins our story (you play as one of them). I personally did not pay attention much to it while playing, but let me tell you, there's a lot of fun to be had in here. The activities from the other games make a return to form; you've got the insurance fraud, you've got Mayhem and a couple of new ones. One I particularly enjoyed was "Hellblazing" where you get to fly around the underworld before time runs out. There are also a couple of new powers for you to use and a lot of demonic weapons to upgrade and go on a murder spree with. The denizens of hell are also out to get you and with them comes the very famous Notoriety Bar. Graphically this is not the best looking game ever (I thought Saint's Row IV and III looked much better) and this flame-condensed environment is really, really boring. The best part, in my opinion, was the grinding: There are 101 challenges within the game and there are only a couple ways to advance the story; so you've got all the time in the world to grind them out before squaring off against Satan.

Alas, this is indeed a mixed review (with more qualities than defects, I guess, hence the thumbs up) as the sad part about this game remains that this "Steel-port wannabe Hell" is an environmentally boring nightmare; the AI is extremely dull, the cars look out of place and the weapon design was cool, but really lacking. This is a 5.1/10 at best game, and if given choice between this or ANY other Saint's Row game, I'd go with literally ANY Saint's Row game. However; if you're looking for a Saint's Row experience that you can complete in 20 hours flat, then this game is worth trying out.

Nothing else to add, except that I really hope the real life's afterlife is much less boring than this game's bad graphic design LOL.\t\t\t\t\t
