\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have loved the saints row series as I had been introduced about a year ago. I have been wanting to play this game for a long time and was very disappointed with it. Not only did this game feel like a re-skin of saints row IV. But it also felt very empty and boring with little to no character development or much to keep my attention. I only got to 10 hours of playtime by spending about four hours trying to get all the collectibles. Honestly after playing this game, this is nothing in comparison to the others, this feels more like an unfinished DLC for the last game. I am glad I got this on sale because this game wasn't worth much to me. The graphics and world looked amazing. The characters and their back stories were funny and entertaining while they lasted. Missions were repetitive and were just copies of missions from the last game or simple wave clear missions. Many missions felt off like the save your friends mission which popped up after I met only two of the people. The enemies were fun and interesting as well as well crafted. I feel that the bosses had no differences between one another though which led to boring repetitive battles. There were little to no weapon choices and getting the seven sins were way too easy. It didn't feel like much of a challenge and wasn't very rewarding as even fully upgraded most of the weapons were worse than the basic weapons you get. The abilities and upgrades were fun and interesting but I feel like they could have been developed better and had better themed missions to go along with them. In saints row IV we saw many funny and interesting dialogue options of which kept me entertained and laughing throughout. This had very repetitive and bland lines that got old fast. This game also gave you the choice to choose various endings which all just seemed so bland and pushed onto the player randomly. This skeleton of a game had so much potential in my opinion but failed to provide much more than a momentary burst of glee to fly around with angel wings, which seemed like the best part of the game. I was so excited to learn more about Jezebel and see more singing as well as she was so interesting and the singing was just plain hilarious. I would give this game a 3/10 as a solo game and a 5/10 as a DLC for four. Honestly just play Saints Row IV as it has so much more to offer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGat Out of Hell takes place after the events of Saints Row 4. You became the president, killed not-Thanos. What could top this? Oh yeah, going to hell and punching Satan in the face!

You do not play as the Boss in Gat Out of Hell, you play as the legend himself, Jonny Gat, and Kinzie. I spent time with both playable characters and Jonny Gat has the most comedic material and had me laughing at some of the one-liners. Gat feels right at home in Hell, where he can be free to murder again and again.

The story is short, over-the-top and funny at times. Some people may not find the humor in the game very funny, but I'm easy to please I guess. There's even a funny musical number in the game that had me laughing the whole time! This game knows it's story is stupid, and isn't attempting to take itself seriously. This is just stupid fun!

The game is rather short, lasting about six hours or so. I took the time to complete all the diversions, targets and activities. After I had %100 control over New Hades, the city in Hell and punched Satan in the face I found myself wanting more.

This game is mainly a sort of stand-alone DLC and I got it while it was on sale. For the price I paid I was satisfied with what I got overall.

*I will mention that I experience random freezing while playing the game. At random times the game may completely lock up forcing me to terminate the game. I have searched online for a resolution to this issue but I have yet to find one. It did ruined my experience with the game a little bit.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGames I have wanted to stop playing right at the beginning: Blazblue Centralfiction, Carnival Games Minigolf, Lego Star Wars 3 the Clone Wars on 3DS and now, This game, Saints Row: Gat out of Hell.

I wasn't even going in with high expectations. I'd heard all about how it killed the franchise and I bought it when it was on sale because that's the only time you should buy something with a reputation like this. But good grief, I wasn't expecting something THIS bad.

The first and likely only positive I can give is they fixed the &*$!ed up controller support Saints Row 4 has. When gameplay started, I was relieved to find my controller worked perfectly. No more suddenly being ignored in gameplay.

And then it went all downhill from there. I heard the game reads the Saints Row 4 save data to bring your custom protagonist from that game into this one. Well, that didn't happen! Instead what I saw was just a default unaltered character representing the boss. Come on! It can't be that hard to read save data on a PC port, especially with Steam.

So here comes my biggest problem with the game. The story is the boss has been kidnapped by Satan during Kinzie's birthday party and Johnny and Kinzie (Just them. Pierce, Shaundi, Matt they all stay behind) both chase after him into hell.
So you play as Johnny Gat, Kinzie Kinsington, AND NOT THE BOSS!!!!

What the &*%$! Who thought that was a good idea?! How brain-dead do you have to be to think this was okay?

Okay sure, Johnny Gat is like the face of Saints Row, and is so popular, Saints Row 4 brought him back from the dead. While Kinzie on the other hand, is my personal favourite among the Saints.
But when I play a Saints Row game, I expect to play as a character I can customise however I like ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥! Not to mention the character whose story, players have been following for the past four games!

A customisable character was something GTA never provided. You could change their clothes in those games but you always played as a specific character (or three). Because of this, character customisation became something unique to Saints Row and helped it stand out from its overshadowing rival. To throw all that away just baffles me. What were they thinking?

Even if you push that aside, what you get is a dull and directionless reprisal of SR4's gameplay. While I was glad to see they didn't recycle Steelport again, the new city is boring as well... hell. The pedestrians are all zombies and not the zombies from SR3. They all use the same character model.

Weapon shops are vending machines so you can't rob them, there are no clothing shops and Cars just don't feel worth stealing since not only is super speed back, but most of the cars themselves are half wrecked before you steal them and you can't even keep or customise them.

Even the super powers feel disappointing since they're just the powers from SR4 reskinned. The only thing different is flying controls have changed.

Then there's the story, or what little the game has. Rather than follow a main series of missions, you're tasked with clearing side missions over and over again until a meter fills and you can go straight to the final boss.

You can make alliances with the locals to unlock new missions and powers. But the only characters I bothered to join with before walking out on the game were Viola and Kiki for nostalgia's sake.
And not only was the mission where you team up with them lazily put together, (Just shoot the demons attacking them. That's it.) But their story made no sense whatsoever. "It was better to own a 5th of something than be dead." Aren't they dead already? Kilbane snapped Kiki's neck for crying out loud!

To put it simply. Gat out of hell lives up to its setting. Because playing this game is hell. I went in with low expectations and honestly, it deserves a place alongside games like Devil May Cry 2 and Dynasty Warriors 9. It's not bad from a technical standpoint, but the game is so boring, you literally want to just stop and play anything else.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you liked 4 you'll love gat outta hell. The story has the same sense of humor we're used to and I can see why this game came out so fast. It's not a gigantic world and for the on sale price more than fair. Hold on let me buy a character for tekken 7 for 5-10$, or 5$ for tomb raider (2013) where you pay 3-5$ for an extra cave or two you'll speed run through in minutes .. yeah. It's not a very long game unless you're shooting for 100% if so than expect to put about 18-20 hours into it as they give you a lot of weapon challenges some of which take minutes, others will take about a half hour or so depending on how annoying the weapon is. Cough cough 500 electric arc challenge. There is a ton more than something DLC worthy to add onto saints row 4. How they constructed SR4 though they could have just put another simulation tube or something. But how insane and with very little logic SR4 had anything went and that was the charm.

As usual what made open world games like this fun. Missions? Sure they help but rampages. Now just like the worlds most over rated video game GTA5 the issue with both franchises your wanted level sky rockets WAY too quickly. Unlike GTA5 your wanted level will eventually go away if you kill what is called an arch duke. If you've got a fully upgraded say boom chika (pink shotgun that represents lust) you can take him out in 2-3 shots. Just like the bouncing rifle or the black hole gun you can get to see an arch duke in under a minute if you do it right.

most of the guns you'll find get extremely useful with their added on powers. Some of which I would never have given a chance without the challenges absolutely. The cars no one cares about. The flight takes some time to get used to as you can fly up and down instead of this big glide your hero did in the game before so it gives different mini games and how they did the story as well in my opinion. Not sure why everyone asks for this big story like final fantasy 7 when one of the most useless characters in the game gets stabbed by the most over rated character or metal gear solid 5 when quiet wanders off into the desert. As usual you take over a part of the city but should do the missions as quickly as possible otherwise you'll do a lot of back tracking as you gain new powers or activities to do. I miss the old british voice I had in the games before as Johnny always sounds like he has food in his mouth but then again it would not be gat out of hell without johnny. The co-star is someone no one wanted.. Kinzie .. why is she there other than fan service. and a hilarious opening. I would even play as Shaundi ... then again I wouldn't.

After getting king of hell achievement (100% the game). I found myself spending WAY too much time getting all the weapons fully upgraded and the challenges do not hold you over as you'd think ok I'll kill 100 demons with this pistol and I'll have enough cash left over to buy a shotgun upgrade.. yeah... not going to happen. After killing professor Genki who is an absolute chore he gives you 2$ where as the arch duke who is like the warden bad guys in SR4 he pays you whatever and whenever he feels like. Sometimes you'll get a few hundred other times up to 10-20,000$. I was minutes from getting my last upgrade and the bastard didnt pay 2 or 3 times. It makes you wonder how aware the developers are of this. I say the weapons cost too much to upgrade. After the first upgrade everything is practically 5000 , 10,000. I think after all the weapon challenges I was grinding for another 2 hours so get out your phone or whatever and blast your two or three favorite albums as mush mouth I mean johnny gats one liners have probably gotten old and no one picks Kinzie.

the collection items to get the powers luckily they are shared between the two characters. I'm surprised this game doesn't have a workshop option honestly... or cheats. It's a good game i'll play it twice. Don't let the third party developer know someone actually enjoyed the game enough to play it twice.

some tips to get more money
kill civilians with a fully upgraded diamond stinger (bling gun style uzi) which you get a bit later in the game, go after dexter the husk with the purple hat on as much as possible with any other firearms he pays well. The arch duke pays the best but only when he feels like it. The club with shakespear a lot of people recommend is a chore unless you're playing on hardcore don't bother. It just keeps the cops from going in. The only way to really get money or do a lot of weapon challenges easier is simple. Get Professor Genki to appear and then fly to a floating island making sure he doesn't bother you. Eventually two of those floating wizards will appear and just keep shooting the imps until you get the challenges you're after. The only challenge Genki is really handy to have around is the grenade launcher with the frogs as Genki's life bar is stupidly high. It's quite easy to lose Genki too if you want to get the driving challenge as you'll be doing laps around empty roads.

things i didnt like or ways to improve
the fraud mission is unpredictable in this game, fun when you're trying to get a high score but the achievement is just sadistic to get where you've got to bounce on 5 cars.

as stated before your wanted level sky rockets wayy to quickly
the epic flight quest challenge they could have been more clear
the assassins creed homage achievement is too specific
the arch duke should pay consistently not how quickly or whatever you can
the car surf challenge is evil or dumb luck and I used to love the tony hawk games
the 5 car bounce challenge without touching the ground is the worst challenge of the game
the omega omega omega paddle (tri omega) weapon challenge could have been more clear
weapon challenges should also count on husks (the other guys besides demons)
there are too many weapons most of which you'll never use for more than 30 minutes
the voice clips of johnny and kinzie should be able to collect by either
customization is non-existent. This game came out fast and was cheap for a reason

all in all i don't understand why people are complaining get this on sale if you're a fan of 4. Which I think is the best in the series so far playing each twice mind you. If not go play grand theft auto and die tumbling down a hill or taking ONE shotgun blast to the chest go ahead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis game is insanely easy, even on the hardest difficulty. this game is also incredibly short. most people will finish the game in 4 hours. this seems like it was meant to be a saints row 4 dlc, and they just made it its own game for god knows what reason.

this game is pretty playable for the most part, some minor bugs. there is a major bug that hasnt been fixed in 7 years regarding saints row 4 cloud saves. if you have saints row 4 installed this game will NOT work unless you go through a semi-lengthy process to reset steams cloud save of your saints row 4 save. so i cant even recommend it to those who enjoyed saints row 4 because you get punished for playing that game by not being able to launch this one.

the story in this game is more so just trying to make you laugh, which it does pretty well, but overall it is not worth it for the price tag. just play other saints row games or an entirely different game.

this is coming from someone who loves the saints row series, i even have all the achievements int this game! but it should say something when you can 100% the game before you are halfway done with the achievement for spending 20 hours in hell. there is very little value in this game.

i miss this series, and i love it dearly. play the original saints row 3 (remaster is overpriced) and saints row 4.\t\t\t\t\t
