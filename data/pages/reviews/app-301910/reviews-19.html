Rather Lazy and Boring. Forgettable.

I'll start off by saying that I know it's a spin-off, but the game itself is nothing to write home about. Lazy writing, annoying characters and (even more) insane plot.

For fans of the older Saints Row games (1, 2), there's no enjoyment to be found here. GOOH is essentially a reskin of many of Saints Row 4's features on a new map with some new characters mixed in. You can play as either Johnny Gat, or Kinzie Kensington — both of which are transported to "hell" via a portal the gang opened up on their ship during the Boss' birthday (I'd go on but the story is so insane I can't be asked to try and describe it).

On the new map, you're tasked with doing very repetitive missions to "save" the Boss. The gameplay is very boring, though there is a new "flying" mechanic which is quite similar to the glide superpower in SR4. Some new animations here and there. Did I mention this is also a musical?

You meet some old characters, from Saints Row 2 mainly, which is why this title is quite insulting to new players. In one side-mission, you kill a character from Saints Row 1 that you've been hunting ever since, but he dies like any other NPC, there's no dialogue, no reasoning, you just end the arc like that. I should also mention that all the generic NPCs in the game are weird ghoul-type creatures, so he looks like one of them with a hat on.

Honestly, it's overall just a disappointment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game, I really did. Its got some good call backs to previous entries in the series and the flying is actually really enjoyable compared to Saints Row 4. But the story, even for a Saints game, is not good. The level cap is only 20, and I was getting prompted to do the final mission before I even met everyone. It honestly probably would have been better if there wasn't story cutscenes (not really missions minus the last) and instead just let the player do the side missions/collectables. Side note, there were times where voices were missing (really only the president) and parts where there was no music (not even just generic overworld music), both were really odd to experience along with how...odd the final boss fight was (not sure if it went a bit screwy because I was OP during it, or if its just that wonky).

I will say that if you're looking for a really quick game and you enjoyed the side challenges ONLY, this game isn't too bad. But if you wanted to get a feeling of a traditional Saints game, this isn't what you're looking for.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSR4.5? Gat out of Hell should probably have been a DLC for SR4 (and it seems like it very much began life that way before being made into its own title), but setting that aside for a moment, does it stand up as its own game?

Well, its got the characteristic saints row outlandishness, open world with story and side missions, assortment of weapons, and superpowers are back. So, in a word, yes. That being said, although there is just about enough content here for it to be its own title, its origin-as-a-dlc means there isn't as much, and it feels a bit stretched in places (there a far fewer weapons, vehicles, character customisation is gone), there's nothing that a dealbreaker but it does take off a couple of points.

In terms of gameplay it's basically the same as SR3 and SR4, which isn't surprising. And while there's nothing really new or groundbreaking there's some different flavours of powers and weapons to make it a little bit different. Oh and you get to punch Satan; +1 there.

So on the whole, it doesn't score as highly as it would were it a part of SR4, but it's still a positive review as its own title (helped by the price being in a sensible place).\t\t\t\t\t
