\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaints Row: Gat Out of Hell is a standalone expansion for Saints Row IV starring everyone's favorite Saint, Johnny Gat. It's short and recycles a lot of previous Saints Row content, but can still be recommended to Saints Row fans. People who have never played a Saints Row game should try one of the earlier games in the series before buying Gat Out of Hell.

Gat Out of Hell has an amusing setup. The Devil kidnaps the leader of the Saints and drags them down to a Hellish version of Steelport to force a marriage with the Devil's rebellious daughter Jezebel. Johnny Gat won't stand for this, and goes to Hell to rescue the Boss. Kinzie Kensington comes along, solely for the purpose of being a playable character in co-op. The player can switch between Johnny and Kinzie at any time, although they both play the same. There are achievements tied to each character to provide motivation to use both.

To dance with the Devil, Johnny will have to annoy him by doing tasks assigned by the denizens of Hell. Completing these tasks adds to a meter, and when the meter is full Johnny gets to fight Old Scratch. These tasks are mostly re-skinned "activities" from other Saints Row games, like Mayhem, Chop Shop, and Fraud. There are no story missions; Gat Out of Hell is a collection of mini-games. Activities are given out by famous historical figures like Shakespeare and Vlad the Impaler, as well as characters from other Saints Row games like Dane Vogel from Saints Row 2 and the DeWynter sisters from the third game.

To get around Hell, Johnny has a pair of angel wings. The flyiing in Gat Out of Hell is a lot of fun once you get the hang of it. Swooping and soaring around the city either gathering power-up orbs or just seeing how long you can stay aloft is very enjoyable. Two activiites are specifically designed around flying: Hell Blazing and Salvation. Hell Blazing is checkpoint races where you fly through checkpoints as fast as you can without touching the ground. Salvation has you trying to keep descending souls from touching the ground by flying into them and sending them to Heaven. Both are fun.

These acitivites will keep you busy, but not for very long. Gat Out of Hell can be completed in about three or four hours, depending on how much time you spend hunting down the loads of collectibles or doing all the activities. Power-up orbs, hidden weapons based on the Seven Deadly Sins and audio logs are yours for the taking, and there are hundreds of them. Completionists and achievement hunters will like this; other players may not.

Gat Out of Hell's plot generates humor, and there are some funny lines and sight gags. The biggest laughs come from a Disney-styled musical number about halfway through the game; it's random, but very amusing. The weapons based on the Seven Deadly Sins are inspired, and you'll probably want to find them all just to see what they can do.

Saints Row: Gat Out of Hell is all right and can be recommended to fans who can't get enough of Saints Row. Picked up in a sale, it will provide an afternoon of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
