\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhatever the developers says, this is just a short DLC for SR4, nothing more. It can be enjoyable for a few hours, if you have nothing better to do with your life, but buy it only on sale, because there are several problems with it.
On one hand, there are some funny moments, and I enjoyed the flying, the story is nothing special, but it's okay.
However, it takes away many things, that makes SR fun. You cannot tell how your Boss actually looks like, or if they are a male or a female, you are going to have an average looking guy, whether you want it or not. There is no customization anymore, because you have to play as Johnny or Kinzie, which I do not understand. If they wanted you to play as one of the companions, why didn't they let you choose anybody from the gang? It would be a lot of fun to choose whoever your favorite character is, if it's not Gat and Kinzie. Also, I actually played as Kinzie, but all the cutscenes acted like I was Johnny, and I was also forced to play as him during the last boss fight, and the endings were all about him. Which makes playing as Kinzie kind of pointless, doesn't it?
The game takes place in a city called New Hades, but to be honest, it felt like I was in Steelport again, just an ugly and a bit boring version of it. All "citizens" look exactly the same. I felt like the developers were lazy again.
There were also some problems with the quests, because basically, there are no quests. The game says, now go and do some stuff, something might happen eventually. The said stuff you can do, is almost the same objectives as they were in the previous games, like ragdolling and mayhem. Oh, and gang fight. Now, with demons. Such creativity. What I liked previously, is that you could choose, when you wanted to deal with all the bosses, when you wanted to complete a quest, when you wanted to continue the story. Here, you are forced the see the cutscenes and go on with the story, whenever the game says so. I was basically half way through the objectives, when I got to the ending, fighting with the main boss, and sadly, I don't feel like I want to go back to continue them, because they were not as entertaining as before. There are too few of them, it got a bit boring in the end. There are no loyalty quests anymore, you don't get to know your current allies, which is also sad.
It can be enjoyable for a few hours and it made me laugh sometimes. It's also fun, if your favorite Saint is either Johnny or Kinzie, but is not worth the full price. Maybe half, or less. Especially if you want to have the purple Saint wings, because you have to pay for that additionally. For shame.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is a complete letdown. I completed the main story in 2 hours, and the side quests in another 2, which were just "complete x, y, and z activies". The game was extremely easy as well even while playing on hardcore. I went through the final boss within a minute just by spamming Satan into a rock. The story was incredibly rushed and missed out on a ton of potential. They could've easily added the 7 deadly sins and comically exaggerated them but all we got was a "Satan's daughter is gonna marry the boss and you have to stop it". Even the jokes were bad. The entirety of the game is Gat saying "I'm going to shoot Satan in the face" until he actually shoots Satan in the face.

Compared to the previous installments the game is more like DLC than an actual standalone game, yet it's marketed at the same price which forces me to judge it with it's predacessors. There's no customization of your player, cars, or penthouses. There's no wide variety of questionable weapon types. Even the graphics look worse than it's previous installments. If you're going to buy a Saints Row game, buy any one but this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really liked Saints row 4. Tried very hard to like Gat Out Of Hell. Spent several hours of my life trying to like it but I can't. This game takes some of the bad aspects of Saints Row 4 and amplifies them. In other cases it takes decent aspects of Saints row 4 and makes them terrible.

For one thing the game loves to make your character ragdoll around everywhere. In previous Saints Row games you could hit space bar to regain control while ragdolling around. They removed that for some strange reason. So, now you just have to sit there and watch, wondering where the "recover" game mechanic went to. The game goes out of it's way to ragdoll your character, too. When combat is going on, cars drive out of their way to hit you, which means ragdoll. There's a brute-like demon that ragdolls you if it so much as touches you. Any little explosion means ragdoll. There can be times when you are being ragdoll juggled around, wondering if you'll ever regain control of your character. If you don't like games that create false difficulty by forcing you to lose control of your character then you will not like this game, at all.

Another thing that's screwed up is notoriety. Notoriety as in "the cops are coming for you" except since it's Hell this time it's demons coming after you. This time, you can gain a couple of bars of notoriety when you're not even doing anything. You can literally be walking down the street and have a couple of whole bars of notoriety appear out of nowhere. I am not even sure I understand how that comes about, but it does. Suddenly you have have seveal brute style demons ragdolling you around. And they can jump to anywhere you are on the world map so there's no "getting away from the cops" this time, either.

And then there are the scripted events. These take a lot out of that "open world do what you want" aspect which was a key aspect to the Saints Row series. Even if we went from serious to silly, from gangster to saints in space, we always had that open word, do what you want, when you want. But here? Heading to a shop to upgrade weapons and suddenly a NPC contacts you saying someone wants to meet you. Suddenly, you're whisked away to a scripted event that you didn't want to do. When that's done you think, ok now I can go back to doing what I wanted. Nope. Here's yet more scripted events demanding your attention.

And if you honestly didn't like how the Saints Row series went from the somewhat silly but halfway serious "gang land" theme to the matrix, saints in space theme of Saints Row 4, then it's unlikely you'll enjoy seeing Johnny Gat sing and dance his way around as if he were auditioning for Disney. You think I'm kidding, don't you? It's in there. Someone probably thought it was a hillarious idea. Just goes to show that you have to take a care with comedy. One person's idea of "funny" can be four people's idea of "stupid".

I could go on. But, you get the idea. It's a garbage game in Saints Row wrapping. And, as Zinyak once said "A rose by any other name....."

Whoever designed this game seriously needs to consider a career in the fast food industry and forget game design.\t\t\t\t\t
