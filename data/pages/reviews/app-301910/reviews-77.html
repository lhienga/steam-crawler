\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: this is going to turn into a slighly long and waffly review about what I think of the Saints Row games in general (since I've got most of them on XBox rather than Steam so I can't review them here).

Saints Row 1&2 were basically decent Grand Theft Auto clones. I felt they were probably trying a little too hard to be gritty and it didn't entirely seem to ring true. 2 was better than 1, largely because they'd ironed out the minor issues, and the gameplay was fine although nothing original.

Saints Row 3 was the definite high point of the series for me. It had dropped the attempts to be gritty in favour of silliness (and the silliness was a sort that appealed to me) but still felt grounded in a sort of reality. With all the various leveling up it ends up pretty easy at the end, but there's a challenge at the start, so it kind of feels earned.

Saints Row 4 went a bit far for me. Nothing feels remotely related to reality and the superpowers remove the driving element and make it all rather too easy. It's silly enough to be fun though, so still worthwhile.

Gat out of Hell has most of the same issues as Saints Row 4 (no real link to reality, overpowered superpowers). I kind of prefered the Hell setting to the SR4 virtual reality setting, so it has that in it's favour. It's obviously a shorter game (but is cheaper) and is missing some of the fun free-form aspects of the previous games, like character customization. It's still fun though, and provides a good run of mindless OTT violence. I'm just not convinced it's the place to start with the series.

On a side note, the Linux port wasn't phenomenally good - it was a little unstable, the process needed to be killed manually after quitting, and it occassionally ran into sound issues where it would start stuttering and never stop. This was in early 2016, but I don't think it's been updated since. I appreciate the fact that it exists and it was playable, but it could have been better,\t\t\t\t\t
