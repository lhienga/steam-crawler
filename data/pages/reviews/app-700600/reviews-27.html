\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven with the DLC this game becomes very unpleasant in the endgame, everything catches fire, agents bypass my automatic target on sigth protocol and sabotage your base constantly unless I manually target them for elimination which is very repetitive.

The TEMPERATURE system is retarded and makes the game way worse, I have no idea who tought this would be a good idea but it is neither hard nor fun, just tedious. Also it creates a massive problem In the generator rooms when one unit catches fire, it creates a chain reaction that destabilizes the temperature, as the loss of generators makes the room way to cold and damages the remaining generators way faster. It took me half an hour to stabilize it and i have the AI voice imprinted in my head now because of the constant warning (an inferno is raging in your lair!!!yes i know stop telling me dammit!). I really wanted to like this game but once your tech is researched and the base is built, well there aint much to do. Also they over utilize female minions in all cutscenes for some reason and the writing in the cutscene is quite bad. Compare this to all cutscenes from dungeon keeper 2 which were hilarious and added a lot of personality to the minions these one are always = minions dumb/ genius soooo smart / good guys soo good and genius are pantomine cackling villains with no personalities.

I tought the DLC would change up things but turns out the engineering (which is the best content by far) is not really exciting its just another tech tree that dosent do much but give you turrets and subs wich are somewhat usefull but I barely had use for them and even when i used them the JAWS team would end up killing most minions waiting at the rally point soo mehhh. Also I tought I would get new minions with the cabal DLC but no its just a skin for henchmen and Espectro is a werid mix of bane and spectro but the cool gas head is never explained he has 0 back story, he just wants to join the good guys and you beat him up in 5 missions and he joins you. thats it. All in all pretty Im dissapointed as there was real potential with this game but there isnt much depth to it. Maybe if they made the genius have a radiacally different playstyle as it was done in War for the overworld campaign where one keeper is limited to traps, the other with beasts, the other with undead and another wich focuses on gold the game would have more replayability. I really feel like apart from certain bonuses it dosent matter which genius you pick as it changes almost nothing.

Also the devs really need to implement a lair-wide item rebuild button because going to each item manually and clicking it is one of the worst time waster ive had in a game so far.

Only go for the oceans campaing pack if you really want a DLC and even then I got bored after just 1 playthrough.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvil Genius 2 is an interesting game to review. Having played through to world domination I don't find myself wanting to go back and do it again, at least not the story mode. The resource management is a unique take with minions running in and out regularly. The designs are cool, little cutscenes/interactions are amusing and characters are iconic.

This is not a game to play quickly, if you're looking for a quick hour long session you won't feel like you've done anything in the game apart from setting up for the next session. Everything takes much longer than you expect, especially if you're trying to balance the Heat generation with every mission. The down side for me was that there never felt like there was any reason to complete any side stories. it was only after finishing the game that I thought maybe I could have done a few, though doing none didn't affect the game.

The base building is constantly evolving, especially as you expand the base and find that the 100+ power generators you built aren't enough anymore and you have to carefully switch them out for better ones, or build even more.

To sum up, this wasn't the game for me. It was too slow, annoying rare bugs, some missions weren't explained well enough and nothing I built, researched or planned seemed to have any affect. If you like taking your time, setting up elaborate traps and testing them a lot, and gradually building power, then this is for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a huge fan of the first Evil Genius, this game doesn't make it easy for me.
On one hand, it looks pretty good and is filled with useful game of life improvements over the older game...but on the other hand it's exactly those improvements that are the games' weakpoint. Personally, I feel like it's much easier and a lot more suited for casual games - the OG game was brutally difficult and frustrating at times, which was where much of the excitement was hiding.
I could probably write an essay about this game but to make it short(ish): Yes, it is a decent game overall but no, it doesn't feel like a true "part 2", more of a remaster that is aimed at today's audience. It kinda fails to build up long-term motivation too as while there are tons of quests, most are "meh" at best. The story is...alright. Strangely enough, they didn't bother programming good interrogations, which was always fun to watch in the OG game.
Overall, my personal opinion is that if they were to hand over this game to the developers of the first EG and told them "work on this for another year", it would've been a masterpiece...but as it is right now, it's a decent game but not the gem us EG 1 fans were hoping for.\t\t\t\t\t
