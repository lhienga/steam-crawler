\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe mind numbing repetitiveness of the quest designs ruin this game for me. I really enjoy planning and building out my base, but when you constantly have to rework everything just to get past whatever ♥♥♥♥♥♥ idea for a quest the developers came up with, there's nothing left to like.

The last straw that made me really hate this game is that now I have to let agents place listening devices inside my lair. Like, nevermind that I have spent the last 30 hours trying to build a lair that, if nothing else, keeps agents the ♥♥♥♥ out of it. But fine, I'll go through and deactivate every single trap in my maze of doom and stare at nothing while I wait for another wave of agents to enter my base.

The next wave of agents come and one by one gets led out by my workers. Oh ♥♥♥♥, I forgot to turn off that zone-thing that tells my workers how to handle intruders. I turn that off and wait for another wave.

The next wave comes and, once again, every agent gets led out by my workers. I have no idea why.

Fine, I'll just make a hole and the end of my casino that gets direct access to my base. I go through and deactivate all casino things and again, I sit and wait for another wave of agents.

The next wave comes. I sit patiently and watch as a wave of super agents attack just at the same time as the regular agents. All out war starts and my minions kill both groups of agents.

I wait for another wave. This happens AGAIN and i realize that I ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hate this game. The base building IS fun, but the game is not. Every single quest consists of this formula.

X. Wait for your minions to defeat agents in the lair
Y. Research something
Z. Start mission on the map

Toss X,Y and Z around and you have created every quest the game has. At least as far as 40 hours in.
Sometimes the game tries to mix things up and have you do things like "let agents place listening devices" or "restart the lair" because nothing is as fun as restarting something or reworking your lair to be less secure.

Rant over. I ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hate this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo as a lover of the first EG - this has that and more. It does many things right. Great base building abilities and can make huge differences on effectiveness. Minion management (in several ways) becomes very important as you progress. Diverse henchmen that they are still adding/allowing more in.... some DLC's and addittions..

I got the season pass right off the bat so I can't comment on "worthiness" of DLC vs cost but plenty others will have comments there.

My biggest point is actually a negative one - though I love the game as a whole. The addition of the ice fortress / ice genius was poorly done in my opinion. The original three options are all very similar but with some important differences and make it feel like you can do as good in any of them. Same with the geniuses.

However the newest fortress is much larger and has so many benefits over any of the other three you're basically stupid for not using it - including the fact it's already better at lowering temperature which becomes a hassle very quickly on the other maps. The leader is also focused on your entire crew not losing stats as quickly to further reinforce that will simply be a better experience all around than choosing any other leader/fortress.

It feels a lot like they had rock/paper/scissor which were all pretty equal and then they added a shotgun which is just better.... so can you pick the others? Sure. Are they different? Sure.... will using the shotgun make the others feel heavily inferior? Yes.

All in - all - great game. Tons of time to be wasted and fun to be had - they just made one very poor unbalanced mistake - in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis feels like a Minimum Viable Product. All of the base building is there, but the game is boring as paste.

Research is a chore, with every step forward feeling incredibly small. The geniuses feel almost entirely generic. The henchmen are forgettable. The traps to your lair are not only ineffective, but are sometimes completely circumvented by the events going on, and they can only be placed in hallways. Sometimes this makes sense, like when you attract the attention of a Super Agent. Sometimes its just because the game said so.

What really kills it is the minion cap at 300. I understand that there can be engine limitations, but if there are, you should be working to balance your game around that. Otherwise you end up needing to train literally dozens of minions to complete some of the story missions.

The Vault is also incredibly stingy, which is odd to me.

A waste of money in my opinion. Its not even really fun enough to justify on a steep discount.

You can be a much more evil bastard in Rimworld, and that game is cheaper, has better DLC, and has an incredibly active mod community that the developer sometimes buys the code off of because their mods are either that good or add great functionality.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI definitely would recommend EG2.
As I am an old-time fan of the franchise, I had almost no difficulties diving back into lair management. A lot of features are recognisable, but are just a bit more polished than they used to. For newer players, there is the tutorial feature, which is fine to get you startedl

Agents are more difficult to spot by eye, and the "tag group" feature of the original game is missing. This was quite an annoyance, until I unlocked the way nicer upgrade during gameplay.
Super Agents are also back, but although they are still a thorn in your side, they are way less crippling than their original game counterpart.

I also like the new main objective / side story campain progression. You have a lot more control over when to trigger the next sequence compared to the original. Related to this was the minor disappointment in Acts of Infamy. Though easier to manage, they lack a portion of the humour on which the original game relied.

Overall, I score this game 8/10. I am currently in the final stages of the Emma-campaing (though the game keeps on springing extra chapters on me everytime I think I have finished) and am curious to experience gameplay as the other geniuses.\t\t\t\t\t

Cons:

This game is worth recommending. It scratched the Evil Dungeon Master itch. I'm not going to queue up another playthrough immediately, but it will be going into the "I'll probably play this again" list.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original Evil Genius was one of the first PC games that I ever played. I picked it up on GOG back in the early 2010s and played it on integrated graphics on an old Vista laptop... and I would not give up that experience for anything in the world! Evil Genius is a fan-favorite classic that I would argue has aged better than many of its contemporaries. Even graphically, the original game holds up very well to the years since its release. Suffice to say that I am intimately familiar with the OG Evil Genius.

That leaves me in a unique position to comment on the sequel. Evil Genius 2, while simpler than the original game, has brought the Evil Genius franchise to modern gamers. If you came to the game expecting anything more than that, I can understand your negative reviews and disappointment. Evil Genius 2 is arguably just the original game with a fresh coat of paint, some modern features, and general improvements in usability and friendliness for new players. The sequel is simpler and arguably easier than the original in every way while maintaining the charm of the original. The kitschy 60's spy film aesthetic that was similarly tapped by Austin Powers is still present and just as goofy and comical now as it was in the original. The music score is AWESOME, because it mostly just remastered or remixed the hits of the original game. The base building is enjoyable and carries the game well. All the systems - minion hunger, sleep, smarts, etc, - are well designed and do not hinder the gameplay in any meaningful way. The over world section where you send minions out to make money in a timed manner is not pulled from mobile games like some people believe, that was a central mechanic of the original game which came out before the original iPhone.

Put simply, if you were a fan of the original, this game is a worthy successor if for no other reason than the fact that you can replace cave walls that you mined accidentally. If you liked the original, you will enjoy this one. If you are a Rimworld or Dwarf Fortress giga-sweaty, this is not the game for you. Evil Genius is not the best base-building/minion-management-sim game available, but it certainly stands alone in its presentation - era, theme, gameplay, etc.\t\t\t\t\t
