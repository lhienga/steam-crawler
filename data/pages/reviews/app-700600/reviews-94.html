\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've put in about 125 hours into EG2 as of the time of this review. I was a big fan of EG1, and played it for hours long before joining Steam. Because i loved EG1 so much, I bought EG2 pre-release with a slight discount along with the season pass. It was the most I've spent on a game in quite a long time.

While there is a lot that looks like EG1, EG2 is a very different game. Graphics have improved, but the humor and game mechanics have changed. Patches continue to be released to fix initial issues, so I won't get into that. My basic complaints about EG2 at the moment are 1) the grind it takes to acquire loot and deal with crime lords/henchmen and 2) the hidden tricks you have to know about in order to make some of the achievements.

The grind - In additional to the main story, you can only have one side objective open. Each side story has 3-4 tasks. If it is loot, complete those tasks, and you have it. However, if it is acquiring henchman or defeating a super agent, it may be 3-4 side objectives long, each with 3-4 tasks. its just tedious and gets old quickly.

The achievements - If you are achievement hunting, there are bugs in timeline that will just block you from obtaining some of the achievements such as stealing 20 loot, all loot, or all henchmen. You'll spend hours thinking your are making progress only to find out something was missed along the way. Very frustrating.

If the game is on sale later, it would be worth the buy. It was not worth the full price I paid for it. If you are an achievement hunter, be prepared for some long hours doing repetitive actions and hoping you didn't miss something.

I do thank Rebellion for recognizing EG1 as a great game to re-invent. There were some positive changes in EG2, but the grind just really took the fun of it for me..\t\t\t\t\t
