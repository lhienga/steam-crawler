\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow being someone who had played the original 2004 game, it is hard to look at the sequel while remaining unbiased as possible and, fair play to the negative reviews that heavily write about those very differences, not focus on the differences but nonetheless if you can ignore the faults, EG2 is Evil Genius for the modern age. As lately the original game has not been liking my more modern PC.

Much like the original, you are once again on a quest to take over a spy tech, super spy world with an army of henchmen and minions building an underground lair to house your doomsday device. All taking place after the events of the first bout. With returning characters such as Max and Ivan along with newcomer geniuses. Each genius has their own doomsday device, with their look and function reflecting the genius, for you to find out.

Being met with an updated rendition of the Evil Genius theme was enough to feel confident that this was a sequel to a game I love dear. More or less the same rooms and minion types return, hell even the same five Forces of Justice return! Just with different territories from reclaiming the world from the previous genius.

You can build up and down floors, (thank blinking god for this) not having to move to a second island and start all over, and you can fire the doomsday device you've built up as many times as you like. As long as you're prepared to deal with the collateral on your island as you do though however.

As mentioned earlier, I do agree with the reviews that heavily write about noticeable issues. Such as the simplified research leading to your labs just sitting there idling with nothing to do once you've completed the base game research, not-as-engaging traps compared to the first game's offerings, the global stage feeling hands-off, and body bags upon body bags of minions that had been taken out by just one, lone agent. Hey, at least this time around you don't need to build a dedicated freezer to house them all.

Do I ultimately recommend Evil Genius 2? Yes. Can I guarantee you'll have fun? No but it is possible if you enjoy base management games or willing to give it a chance. Otherwise my best offering would be of course to wait for a sale.

Some may say for you to go play the original (that I also heavily recommend you do if you like Evil Genius as a theme) but be aware of its age. As mentioned before and especially if you play EG2 first, the original does show its age by modern gameplay and technical standards and you may begin to appreciate the changes.\t\t\t\t\t

There's no significant choices. Your henchmen are no longer the special cool champions you can level up and controlling them feels worse than the orignal.
There's no minion management as your income is passive on the world map, and any minions you send out will never come back anyway.
Heat doesn't matter, since super agents are very easy, and agents don't pose a real threat against basic stuff like 10 minions on a guard post.
If you wanna collect loot, it'll delay your progress in side quests for no gain at all.
Research is very basic, and there's no fun exploration or experimentations.
Base building is boring because you have close to infinite space, so no need to plan or optimize. You'll always have more room.

On the bright side, the art style is quite neat.\t\t\t\t\t
