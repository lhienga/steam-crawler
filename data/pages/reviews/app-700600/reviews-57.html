\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finally managed to sit this game through.

Why would I spend 50 hrs in a game, that makes it hard to not fall asleep?
(of my recorded 80hrs 30 are from a canceled first try right after release, and the rest of an actual playthrough, this review is based on)
Because I wanted to give the game a chance. I wanted to see, what the devs expected this game-experience to be like.
I wanted to see, if there is something likeable, something that would want me to come back again for another playthrough.

To make it short: It didn't work out. I was happy, when the game finally was over.

Which is a sad thing to say about this game, as one can see, that the devs really tried to make a worthwhile successor to Evil Genius.
But it simply doesn't work out.
Everything is unnecessary lengthy and boring.

To give you an indication: There is an achievment you get roughly a third into the campaing ("A tool for every Job").
You can't progress further than 30% without earning it.
Roughly 27% of the buyers of this game actually have this achievment - go figure.

Get it on a steep discount (not more than 10€) if you want to give it a try, and play it as long as it entertains you.
But don't expect to make it through the campaing, before getting completly bored out.

The main-quest alone is boring and long, but then there are the sidequest, where things really show how loveless an unimaginative this game really is.

The sidequests are all the same:
- click some icons on the map, wait 10 seconds
- start one research, wait for it to finish
- click some icons again on the map (several times)
- *sometimes* play "Where is waldo" in the lair, to find some items, and click "destroy"

And you do that again and again and again and again.

In between wait for the special minions to be retrained. (You can cut time pretty down here, by just spaming training rooms, and klicking "buy worker" to fill up your yellow-ranks again; so no really issue, just an illustration how no mechanic in this game has any impact at all, except idling you, and costing you real-life-time.)

But as they are sidequest, you could easily skip them, and shave off 25hrs of "gameplay".
You won't miss out anything, except for the super-agent side-missions which will make you endgame much harder, if not done prior.

In EG1 I was eager to collect most of the loot-thingies.
In EG2 I ticked off the list, without any investment.
I easily could have skipped most of them - as being an Evil Genius obviously doesn't include doing evil deeds anymore in the 2020s.

Speaking of difficulty-level:
The game plays itself if left alone, as long as you learn how to manage the heat (which means clicking the right icon on the map every 90 minutes)

A more casual gaming experinece is not negative per-se, but in this case it means that basically every gaming mechinc superflous, as none of them actually kicks in or interacts with eachother in any way.

The system requirement really should mention "multi-monitor-setup", as you will need a second monitor to surf the internet, watch some movies or doing some studies on whatever might interest you.

Building a lair is fun though.
For roughly ten hours.
Then it becomes meaningless.
As the whole gameplay loop.

So as mentioned before:
If you want to try it, wait for a steep discount, and don't expect the game to entertain you for long enough to even beat one campaign.\t\t\t\t\t
