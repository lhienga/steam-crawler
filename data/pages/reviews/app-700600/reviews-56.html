Lighthearted base-building game setup as a 60s-90s James Bond villain spoof, like Dr. Evil in Austin Powers.

What I liked:
  • The base building part of the game.
  • Graphics/Art Design.
  • 3 different islands + 1 with the Oceans DLC.
  • Voice Acting (Emma is voiced by Samantha Bond (Moneypenny from the Brosnan Bond movies)).
  • Interaction screens between genius and minions when starting/ending main or side missions.
  • Maximillian is back!
  • Rare cosmetic tiles/graphics which gets randomly placed.
  • Liked some of the DLCs (Rise of the Valkyrie, Oceania (except the Polar genius), TF2 Pyro)).
  • Sound effects are good, but sometimes overshadowed by the music. Some subtle sound effects you can only hear by turning off the music altogether.

What I thought was alright:
  • Animation / Humor / Writing: The quality varies a lot in these areas, but there are definitely some funny/good ones.
  • Music: EG1 composer James Hannigan is back, I adore his EG1 soundtrack! My personal favorites in EG2 are "The Darkest of Plans", "Stealth and Wealth" and "From Maximilian with Love". However, some of his themes sound now a bit overproduced. In some cases, as a background music, it becomes a bit too distracting for me, so I sometimes turned them off in mid/late game.
  • Henchmans are again pretty good/fun in this one. However, their actual usefulness seemed to be very limited.
  • The "World Domination" mission screen was already the weaker part of the original game and still is with this game. I appreciate the efforts of the writers though, who come up with fun mission notes.

What I didn't like:
  • Often overlong Cookie Cutter missions, which feel like a drag once your base is basically done.
  • Reuse of the same genius lines for side-missions. Mocking Dr. Ming with the same lines as the other geniuses felt a bit out of place for Polar.
  • Traps work as debuffs, but once you have unlocked most of them in the last third of the game, they have become pointless as most agent/saboteurs can avoid them.

What I missed:
  • Earned money/gold fill/decrease automatically in your safe room with nearly no interaction from the minions (execpt when they want to desert the base). I miss the way EG1 treated these things.
  • Same again, loosing all minions you send on a mission unconditionally. Liked that in EG1 better.
  • What I missed in the first EG and still do in EG2 is a randomized map generator or editor.

Overall, I enjoyed most of my 150+ hours with the game. While it's not a must-have for every gamer out there, for people who are considering it, want more of a fun lighthearted base building game than an ultra complex one, give it a try!\t\t\t\t\t
