\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was really looking forward to playing a game in which i could be the bad guy. I mean, like Blofeld or Goldfinger in the Bond-movies. But in those movies those badguys weren't just over-the-top and sometimes laughable villains (which they often were), but they were also notably EVIL. Goldfinger was a chemical gas-spraying, laserwielding maniac and Blofeld a cat-stroking, knifeshoe wearing mass-murderer. But the bad guys in Evil Genius are anything but evil, now and then they might tickle an agent into giving him a spontaneous heart-attack, but that's about it. The 'traps' you can place in your lair all seem to have been placed by Wile E. Coyote. We're talking about giant boxinggloves on springs here. This is not an Evil Genius-simulator, it's a cartoon simulator. I wanted to be a bad guy, not a Looney Toon. The game also doesn't have the option to create your own villain (a missed opportunity i think), you need to make do with one of the poorly voiced premade ones. The game seems to be targeting a really, really young audience, we're talking under 14 here or something. Which is fine ofcourse, but not what you expect from a game about Evil Geniuses, so if you're not looking for a kids game, you shouldn't buy this one.
On the upside, lairbuilding is pretty fun. Art & animations are top notch, and the whole casino vs secret base dynamic is a nice idea. Could've been fleshed out more. Agents are a nuisance and don't always seem to be following the rules (sometimes they just appear inside my base, even though i painstakingly barricaded all the entrances). If you want a nice lair-builder, buy this at a discount price. If you want to play the bad guy, steer clear of this one.\t\t\t\t\t

Research is straight forward so you can see and plan out your next upgrade, I preferred when my science minions walked around my base looking at stuff before having a eureka moment and rushing back to give me a research option. That in itself isn't a deal breaker, my biggest problem is the genius dialogue. Who in the hell thought it was a good idea for all of the leaders to say the exact same words!? For instance, I fully expected to see max have a different story narrative than Ivan but they literally say the same lines (aside from some super weapon lines). Completely unacceptable, there is no real replay value.
Overall, this game looks like Evil Genius, but it's the Wish or Facebook marketplace version. Buy the original instead, or if you already have it, just replay that one.\t\t\t\t\t
