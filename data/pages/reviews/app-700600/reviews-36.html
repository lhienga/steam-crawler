\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA slight thumbs up for me. I played the original which was great fun although prone to crashes. It's the same basic premise of a base builder for the purpose of being an evil lair for your genius to complete their masterplan.

It's unbalanced in that you find yourself with huge generator rooms but can get away with a minimal staff room with 2 machines to maintain minion smarts. A massive operations centre but tiny archive and so on.

Multi-level bases are interesting, but largely inaccessible due to them having variations of rock you need to reach at least mid-campaign to get through.

If you enjoyed the original, you will probably like this. I wish they had worked more on the UI, as the minion screen is a step backwards from the clarity of the first game. And sadly lacking is a lot of the humour from the first, such as the world domination missions are quite generic, most have no narrative at all.

Ultimately I think the drive for a massive base just isn't there with the mechanics, as long as you have well defended entrances and plenty of fire extinguishers close, theres not much real incentive to rescale your base to hide away your more precious rooms and I found myself just enlarging the same rooms on the first level to get very deep into the campaign (and I have still to finish one yet so maybe I will be proved wrong further into the game).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn terms of an upgrade to evil genius 1, I would say that objectively, yes it is better. It adds new mechanics like multiple floors and allows for more creativity with the designs of your base.

But gameplay wise if you're used to evil genius 1 it's going to feel very different. And I wouldn't say they are necessarily "bad". I just think they're implemented a little poorly.

For example I think the power consumption scaling gets a little too much with the scale of your base. Most of the time in order to maintain your base you need to have a power room that is basically the entire size of the basement floor. I also feel like compared to evil genius 1, EG2 puts less emphasis on traps, sure they're there but they just aren't as efficient or economical as they were in EG1. One reason is because agents if they're a high level will just disable any trap they come across, I think lowering their skill with the cover op can make them not do that but that still doesn't help the agents that come in through the side entrances. I found just using the newly added security checkpoints is enough if not better.

I see a lot of people comparing it to a mobile game, but I wouldn't say that it really plays like a mobile game. A lot of the complaints come from the idea of the schemes taking hours to complete but that's not how they work.

The idea is that you'd have criminal networks initially idle in specific regions then you'd select a scheme to run, typically you'd choose between, a scheme that will continually gain you money for an hour, or keep the heat in region low for an hour. The idea is that you'd want schemes to be running all the time and have as little of your networks idle at a time.

The evil genius 1 equivalent would be like sending a bunch of minions to a region and setting them to "stealing".

The problem is that it becomes a little bit of a micro managing nightmare when you have like 30 regions that you have to decide what to do in. Because when your networks are idle they continually gain heat. So most of the time you'd want to make sure that you're running a heat reduction scheme at all times in all regions to keep the FOJ off your back.

Which means that you're going to be spending a lot more time in the world stage compared to EG1. Which I guess makes sense from a logical standpoint. (You being an evil genius should be plotting and scheming most of the time rather than developing your base) But from a gameplay standpoint it kinda just gets a little overwhelming at times.

The other things I can mainly complain about is that I feel like all of the geniuses don't really feel all that unique from one another. Like sure they have their own personalities and what not but, the designs of their base is basically the same (Besides the inner sanctums and doomsday device). I would've loved to see specific minion designs for each of the geniuses or have different types of cover operations to better suit their personalities. It doesn't even really need to be anything major tbh, just something to make each one stand out from the other.

And recently the new addition of the hot and cold elements adds even more to the micro, having to make sure certain machines (Specifically mainly generators and radio repeaters) are cooled down since they generate heat and when machines are too hot or too cold they break down faster.

Idk I'd definitely say it's fun and addicting, I know I myself like playing it despite my gripes with it, but I know it certainly wouldn't be a game for everyone so take this more as a "mixed review" rather than a full on "Yes" or "no", and decide for yourself if this would be a game you'd enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish there was a middle option between Yes and No on recommending. Objectively, this is a pretty fun game, and I've gotten two pretty good runs out of it and intend to go back for more and play the other geniuses. However, there were points at which I encountered pretty game-breaking bugs and had to spend a good chunk of time trying to fix them because it was either that or starting over. Also, there were multiple points in which the game didn't explain very well what it wanted you to do and I was totally lost until I looked it up.
However, that being said, still pretty fun. If you like base building type games, you'll probably like this. The characters are entertaining and a lot of them have really cool concepts.

I'd say buy it on sale so you don't feel as bad if you have the same problems as I did.\t\t\t\t\t
