\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original was better, but if you liked the original you will like this one too.

It's essentially the same game with updated graphics, although there are a few aspects of the newer game that are less well thought out and implemented than the other game.

1 - There is no exterior to build, no outbuildings, etc. Everything starts at the casino \\ cover operation, which is a part of your lair
2 - Managing the Forces of Justice is less complex. This may be a good thing or a bad thing depending on your perspective, but your options for not killing the forces of justice are limited. There's only a few items you can build that will drain FoJ stats, and those items get completely ignored by higher level agents.
3 - Traps are much more fundamental, you can't chain and trigger them like the first E.G. There also isn't an option for traps that drain all non-health stats, traps are pretty much lethal or just to buy time.
4 - There isn't a way to drain agents suspicion after you catch them. You pretty much have ti kill them, catch and release isn't a plausible option.

Higher level agents will pretty much walk past whatever maze of casino devices you have, then deactivate all your traps as they get further into your base (you're supposed to drain their "skill" in the casino first, but that doesn't really work). Your only real option is to keep armed guards stationed at a choke point, and kill anything that comes in.

It's a bit disappointing because building the impenetrable maze of traps is what made the first one so much fun. Other than that, it is still very much the same game, with the same off-color cartoonish animations and humor.\t\t\t\t\t
