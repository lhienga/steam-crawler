\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, people tend to be divided on this game (for good reason in some cases), but honestly, this is one of my favorite base builders I've ever played.
It has some pretty rough edges, sure; the design of schemes is a little weird, having only five henchman still feels weird when there are eight seats at the inner sanctum desks (and Full Metal Jackie's recruitment quest is still bugged), I would've appreciated more options for island layouts, I wish the units from the different world agencies felt more unique from each other, and I won't even try to defend the DLC. It just feels like they cut some corners.
However, it stays committed to a theme I find really endearing, the character designs are really solid, and the core gameplay loop is enough to make me keep playing until I lose track of time. Your mileage may vary on this, but if you're looking for a good management sim and this game is ever on sale, I'd say give it an honest try. Or at least look up honest reviews beforehand, Steam reviews tend to be a cesspool.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo I recommend the game? - Yes on sale.

If you have played EG1 and like EG1, then this may or may not be the game for you.

It is hard to explain what this game gets wrong, because to be fair to the devs, they did get a lot of things right.
Here are some features that I find are not as fun in EG2, as they were in EG1.

- The art style is even more comedic than EG1. I feel like EG1 had a better mix of realism & comedy, whereas EG2 threw "realism" out the window and just gave some minions superpowers.

- Balancing. The game has 0 balancing, you should get used to 6 soldiers or 8 saboteurs wiping out your 300 strong minion base. There's nothing you as a player can do to stop you from getting your minions wiped out if a fight breaks out in a hallway.

- Construction and furniture placing is waaaay more restrictive than in EG1. EG1 furniture worked in the following way:
-- Say you needed to build a food counter. The build blueprint will show a bunch of footprints (similar to sims) of where your minions will need to "go" in order to sit on a chair, or work on the counter. In EG1, only the chef's "footprints" were required to build it. You could block off 2/5 chairs and it would still let you place the bar, the bar would never allow more than 2 people to sit down.
This issue persists in ALL furniture in EG2. You want to place a bed down? Well your minion will need to access both sides of the bed, why? Who knows. List goes on. But it makes compact base building impossible.

- Enemy art variety? and animation variety? NOPE. HAMMER, PATRIOT, JAWS, etc. all have the SAME models, just different colour. Super lame imo. I loved that in EG1, the different factions LOOKED, WALKED and were ANIMATED differently.

- Sometimes things are OVERLY animated. Does guardposts really need a red blinking light when its manned? If you build a few of them it gets really annoying really quickly. Same goes with the guard post that has cameras that are supposed to reveal enemy agents using a disguise (it doesnt work but heyo). It's sound effect is literally "WAH WAH WAH WAH" on repeat. Its cool if you have 1 built, but once you have 2, 3 or 4 in your base. You will eventually not be able to escape the sound.

The devs really did a great job on many things in this game. It just COULD be so much better.

Overall 5/10, I will keep playing it. I am 52 hours in, I still haven't finished a single campaign.. maybe eventually.

If it is on sale, go for it. You will probably enjoy yourself. But you might also not.

Just beware EG1, by and far. Is still a superior game and that is disappointing if you're returning to the franchise like I am.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game, but it is definitely much more a sandbox base-building game than a strategy game.

My enjoyment of this game comes from designing and redesigning my base, obtaining new loot, minions, and henchmen from missions, designing trap systems and testing them out, and then watching all the little minions, tourists, and do-gooders run about my base like ants in an ant farm. The campaign for each genius is basically just a set of objectives you can complete, if you want, for a little variety in the gameplay. There's nothing really engaging about that aspect of the game, though.

There is no real strategy, though. The enemies aren't that difficult and the only fail condition is the death of the genius character, something easily avoided by just retreating to the farthest parts of your base. You just throw minions at the enemy until you win. Missions given to you through the story have no chance of failure. The biggest challenge you can face is a fire.

If building an evil little sandbox sounds appealing then this game is probably a good buy. If you want to have to really struggle to outwit your enemies and take over the world (particularly like how things were done in Evil Genius 1), then this game's gonna be pretty disappointing.\t\t\t\t\t

I re-downloaded it again and continued playing (though it was unstable as hell thanks to compatibility issues), and then heard the release of EG2.

How wrong I was. We were all duped. The shine and polish and charm from Elixir and Sierra are long gone. Leaving everything in the hands of Rebellion, a publisher/developer who's claims to fame are... Portable console releases, was a huge mistake as they can only do Sniper Elite (and copy + paste it a dozen times) and AVP. Other comments are 100% accurate. It plays like a mobile game, just without MTXs spitting in your face to speed up your base building. There is the issue of the gated content within a DLC pack, which never should have been a thing for EG.

If it's on sale at 80%, sure, you'll get your money's worth. But not a penny more.\t\t\t\t\t
