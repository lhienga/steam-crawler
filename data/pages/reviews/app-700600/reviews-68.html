\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: The devs have made a game they clearly put effort into but either didn't play test it enough or simply don't think the issues it has are issues. There's also still bugs (primarily related to henchmen recruitment).

I've played almost 200 hours of this game and eventually it just becomes completely frustrating. There's still unfixed bugs but that's the least of the issues. The game is very unbalanced and even if you do everything right it will blow up in your face simply because things are programmed poorly.

There's no real gameplay reason Soldiers and Super Agents should attack your social and science minions, but they do anyways and turn your trap halls into meat grinders as an endless supply of helpless minions rush to pick up the corpses of their friends, only to get effortlessly murdered by Agents. This invalidates trap hallways as an effective way to deal with agents, since you end up getting more minions killed than you do just having your casino end in a armory full of guards.

I don't know if there was a stealth nerf to henchmen but I watched Eli fight an investigator for two minutes real-time before he killed it. 5 damage per punch is not enough damage. Not to mention Jubei who went from dealing almost 100 damage to roughly 40 during Flow. Agent armor is currently scaled way too high so all attacks seem to cap out at 5 damage in most case. Henchmen badly need a buff, like, ASAP. This MAY have been a programming oversight during a patch, but give them armor penetration or something.

The back doors to bases will ruin your gameplay. I tried everything from positioning my guard rooms in front of them to attaching the casino to them to hopefully distract agents. After a certain point Investigators (not just soldiers) will exclusively go through your back entrance, skipping the casino as a mechanic entirely. No sir, I don't like it. I really hope the upcoming map lacks a back entrance because this is, above everything else, a bad idea from the developers. I don't think a lot of real play testing went into the game and the back entrance is proof of that.

In terms of suggestions to make the game more fun:
- Make Soldiers ignore Science/Deception minions. Maybe even workers too. Only make workers fight if agents are tagged.
- Buff henchmen. Increase their armor penetration. Let Eli use his gun permanently; it doesn't even deal that much damage anymore (not even six shots can kill a Soldier, much less a Super Agent). Make Janet Bombe's abilities affect a squad, or even every agent on the map to make her worth using. Give the Pyro voice lines. Redesign Carl Caffard to make him worth using as well. Maybe put a gun in his cane, I don't know? Was this stuff playtested or was it just random ideas?
- Remove the back entrances in maps, or give an option to disable agents using them. It is single-handedly the most disruptive thing. It's viable to link them to the casino in Crown Gold, but Montenas Gemelas is destroyed by it.
- Add more maps. At the moment the only good map is Caine Key, with Montenas Gemalas being awful and Crown Gold fine but too small.

I haven't had that much trouble with bugs like others have, but the game itself is built on flawed or outright bad ideas. If this game was moddable these issues could've already been fixed, but who knows it mod support will ever come.

Honestly, I'd say buy it if you liked the original. It's not as charming as that one, nor is the writing as amusing, but there's more effort in EG2 than you might expect. The devs have done a great job with patching it as fast as they did but things have definitely slowed down and there's still huge issues (bugs, balance, fun vs challenge) to be addressed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI probably should have listened to the reviews a bit more.

I started the game on the highest difficulty looking for an interesting system-builder/resource-management style game. While some of those elements are there, it's really feeling more like a casual game than anything I'll have to spend an ounce of effort challenging myself with.

Seems like the game is more about making an nice looking lair than any of the mechanics. Your main source of money seems to be sending henchmen out on assignments and then just... waiting for them to bring in the money (they start at an hour long!).

The UI and building system seems a little jank to me as well. Why does the map not show me what areas I don't have assignments running in, along with a list of available assignments? Why are the rooms all in separate tabs, and then sub-tabbed away from the furniture that goes it in, and then tabbed again from another page with essentially nonfunctional furniture?

Hot take, but if this was an incremental mobile game it would be much BETTER than its current form. The mechanics seem to lean towards a kind of game when you open it up, assign some henchmen, maybe order a dozen more electric generators, and send the prisoners to be interrogated, before turning it off and doing something else while you wait for things to get completed.

TLDR; Not a strategy game really, just a casual mobilesque that feels kind of empty.\t\t\t\t\t
