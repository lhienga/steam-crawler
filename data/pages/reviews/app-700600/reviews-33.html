I gave it so much hope and I tried so hard to like it but the core game mechanics are just so meh to me. The base building is alright but the combat is so much more to be desired and I almost see zero point to building base defense at all. The story is also kind of repetitive and I feel like some type of cutscenes or comics or something more than those lame talking sections before and after quests would just add more to this spy thriller-ish setting. This is all on top of the horrible bugs on release and the way I encountered a game-breaking bug that didn't allow me to progress the story just killed all interest I had for this game soon after release. Overall, could have been better and it's a pretty disappointing start for a new person introduced to this series.

Would never recommend this game unless it's plainly free or more than 70% or 80% on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

Some new features are added (world map is made exponetially better IMO), some good features are removed for some reason (you can't plan out a room without starting a build order right away is the biggest one), but it's the shift in the overall tone from Pink Panther meets James Bond movie to a more generic, British, Worms campaign-like humor that firmly cements this game as distinct enough from the original to be subjectiely separate (sort of Heroes 3, Heroes 5 situtation).

My first playthrough (Max) [Hard Mode]
Compared to like 2 years ago there have been a lot of QOL improvements. The missions last much longer on the world map so it becomes less microheavy. I remember constantly doing quests, luckily that has been fixed. Also the saving is much better, I remember turning of cloud saving, but now it worked much better. Max is a great way to start the game and feel like an evil genius. Pro tip: build a lot of different training buildings; so you can train like 5-10 specialised agents all at once. Also lvl 2 criminal networks can reduce heat with valets. Else you keep getting strong agents. Remember you might need to reduce heat on the 'whole world map' but luckily the missions last 1.5 hours. I love the gold gun. I did finish Super Agent X in this playthrough. It was pretty fun and you get a nice loot at the end for defeating him. Also if you want to do multiple playhroughs like me but dont want it become a 'chore' I recommend choosing 1 super agent and killing that in your playthrough. Also you can now also recruit super agents so having 1 super agent is nice to have.

My second playthrough (Polar) (medium / traps and all DLC)
This time I went back with all the DLCs. Polar is a much more fun Genius than I expected with great charisma. She cant really fight so dont expect too much of her in that department. She has the best execution when upgraded. Also some really nice genius boosts. I know there is some backlash about the temperature feature and even though it does make the game harder. Remember to plan accordingly and expect each room needing some airco/heaters. I recommend making engineers so you can start upgrading pretty fast. Making everything a bit easier for you. There are a lot of temperature upgrades that can help. it matches the content. See the feature as flavor. I really like the 'divers' and think they did a really great job at making the game feel fresh. Also the super agent that comes with the oceans campaign is really cool. I loved recruiting him. But his suit would make a nice decoration. I love submarines. Divers are hard to pull away from areas so a deception route can best be seen as buying time, they do walk slow at their flippers. Also the pace on this DLC felt better for some reason. Might also be the medium mode.

[Oceans campaign pack]
My advice is to use this once for the Polar campaign and when starting new games to not use it. it might also be best not to use it for your first playthrough. Its still really great but fits most with the ice map and Polar. It really changes the game a lot.

[Mechinical Minions DLC]
These guys look really great. Especially the guards. I know some people are complaining they explode, but with some clever engineering you can work around this. This guys are really good, they are free. If you play the game, you know money is really important and you can pretty easily get the 300 max minion threshold. So in the early game you can make a lot of mechinical minions. You can let them be created automatically. They also respond to cameras and can use clubs and guns. This works pretty good in the begin and in the middle but at the endgame you gonna need the specialised minions (mercenaries, hitman, martial artist) to pack more damage. Also they are easy to unlock with 1 mission in the beginning of the game. This is a great choice for doing a new playthrough focused on 1 DLC. With guard tables and some clubs and pistols these guys are very strong. Pro Tip: Also recruit Iris; as her security network link, can pull mechanical guards from guard posts and guard tables from the entire map! This means she can be used as a scout and coordinate your minions. This is really handy! Normal Tagging will only work with cameras.

[Abomination pack]
These can be seen as the ghouls from Evil Genius 1. But also like a roaming zombies. They are like tanks but don't react to alarms. If they see the enemy in sight they will swarm them if they are close enough. They do look great and have great animation when walking around. Also easy to unlock with 1 mission in the beginning of the game. I recommend having at least a couple of guys walking around because they are great support in fights. This is also great for a new playthrough and having like 30-50% of your minions robots.

[Pyro DLC]
I really love this guy. Yes he burns everything if not carefully but he carries around a flamehrower. Its best to fight in hallways. Now his skill is a bit weird to use. He puts somebody on fire, then you need to wait 2-3 seconds in which you have a small window to 'boost' the agent which is on flame so he goes down faster. Because people on fire will run away, even super agents, this henchman is much better than people think. He also does a lot of damage. But be careful of focused fire. Fight in hallways or near the water places.

[Portal Pack]
This was a nice DLC with some cool missions with test subjects. It explores the traps more. Now I really have tried with playing with traps, but with the Polar campaign the divers disable all the traps. Making it much harder to use it. So in the end I never really used traps. Also the 'portals' go both way when moving. Now people who have played Portal, know it should be 1 way. This is a shame because it could lead to a trap room in which you cant go back. You can have 1 blue portal and 1 orange portal making for some nice pathways.

[Doomhilda / Rise of the Valkyrie]
She is a nice henchman with some nice skills. She is a pretty good tank. Her missions are nice. I used her to also boost the mechnical minions in the fight. Her missions are pretty funny.

[Cabal Pack]
This is a really great DLC. I love the new looks of the henchman with the items and this henchman has a lot of flavor. He is a great tank. Feels a bit like Anonio Banderas meets Mysterio. I recommend to try it out. He is one of my favorite henchman.

Fun tip: your genius can go to prison cells and 'laugh' at captured agents. This is great for feeling evil. Remember this game is a lot about flavor, dont think too much about finishing the game but enjoying the game.

Really important tip. Now this late in the game you are experimenting with lots of things and it goes about all the different things you can think about. You can make rooms consisting of different 'parts'. Also think outside the box! Why would you want to walk around a corrider when you can walk around a castle? Thats right the inner sanctum of Emma can be used as corriders too. This also works great with the abominations as they dont use that a lot. Mostly corriders and armoury and prison. So you can centralise where they walk.
I went even further the whole covert operations; the walls were from the inner sanctum of Emma. So you had some nice knight statues, bookcases, chandeliers and pretty nice paintings. If you love decorating your base, use Emma! You can even change the doors which you use. Lets admit it, the corridor door sucks. Why dont replace it for a nice inner sanctum door, or lab door or some vault door. You need 2x2 tiles for it, which kinda sucks but its doable.

This game is really a diamond in the rough. Yes its rough sometimes with the micro managing but the art and overall feel and music and sound is really great. Also with the all the DLCs and QOL improvements this has become a great game. A playthrough can be like 20-60 depending on how much missions you want to do. Also you dont have to do all the missions. This game does miss some explanations at certain things so its really good to check wikis to learn more about it. I advise.

Contains spoilers; but you can read around it and use it as guideline to get some things
