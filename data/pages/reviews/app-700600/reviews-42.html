Just What I Was Hoping For

I don't know what's up with the "mixed" reviews. Having played Evil Genius [1] back in the day, this game is exactly what I expected it to be.

EG2 is pretty much an updated EG1. They didn't change things, it's essentially the same game, but modernized. So, if you liked the first one and want more of it, there is a 100% chance you'll enjoy this one.


For those who didn't play EG1:
Evil Genius is a base-building/management/world-domination game.

You give orders for rooms, and then items inside them, to be built, and your minions make it happen on their own.

Your base can have up to 5 stories, and there are some 15 different types of rooms to be built.

You'll start the game with Workers only, and then you have to train your minions to upgrade them to more advanced classes. There are some 13 different minion classes, divided into 3 groups: Muscle, Deception and... Scientific? I don't remember what word the game uses. Anyway, Muscle minions are fighters, who will defend your base when it's attacked; Deception ones will handle the tourists that constantly visit your lair, scamming money out of them and keeping them away from your main operations; Scientists will do research. That's mainly it, but some of them do other, specific things, like using particular items in your base.

Besides your Lair, you also have a World window. In it, tasks to be performed show up all the time, and they usually require you to send a group of specific minions to execute them. These tasks can be to generate gold, reduce Heat (which builds up with time, causing more enemies to attack your Lair), complete Main or Side-Story missions, and a few other things.

Every now and then, your base will be attacked by a group of enemies. These can be soldiers, who are though to kill; Rogues, who'll try to steal your gold; or Saboteurs, who will set your Lair on fire, destroying stuff. Sometimes, a Super Agent -- a stronger enemy -- will lead the assailants, and there are some 5 different Super Agents in the game (which are also involved in side quests).

There is a number of tactics you can use to defend your Lair: traps, guard posts, base-design, Henchmen and an alarm you can activate to order all your minions to halt their current tasks and focus on dealing with the current threat.

You can have up to 5 Henchmen, Like your Evil Genius (your main character), each of them has 2 unique active skills. You can order your Henchmen to move around and manually activate their skills. Otherwise, they'll move around and handle enemies automatically. Your Evil Genius, on the other hand, exclusively follows your orders. If left alone, he stands still. And, if he dies, the game is over.

Hiring new Henchmen requires you to complete their Side-Stories, which are comprised of a sequence of 3 or 4 missions, each one with up to 3 steps.

That's basically it. You make money via the World Map and scamming Tourists, build up a base, unlock stronger minions and extra Henchmen, keep the global Heat low while doing Side-Stories and other world-tasks and, eventually, you manage to finish the main story line -- which, by the way, took me 40 hours to achieve (and that is twice as long as I was expecting to be kept entertained by this game).

The challenge mostly comes from keeping the enemies invading your base at bay. If you build your defenses poorly, all hell breaks loose, and you can't get anything done. I had to revisit my defense strategy a few times before getting it right, and that was fun.

After you manage to build everything in your Lair and make it safe, the game gets a bit stale, though. Because, then, all you have left to do is to keep training minions until you have enough to send on whatever task/mission you need to do next on the World Map.

The dialogue is somewhat funny, there is a good number of different things to research, you have Henchmen to unlock, a bunch of special Loot to chase after in the world map, and a few other things to do, and all that manages to keep you going after enemies are no longer a serious problem. I didn't bother doing all side-quests, though, because I eventually got tired and just decided to finish the game.


TL;DR:
Reading this whole review or not, you can get a fair idea of what kind of game this is just by looking at the screenshots up there. If it interests you, just go ahead and buy it, because the negative reviews are nonsense. In this very particular genre, Evil Genius 2 is the best game out there, in my opinion.

Judging by the negative reviews, I expected this to be buggy, poorly done, with little content and somehow worse than the first game. It is none if those things. It's a good game, and I have no idea whatsoever why so many people didn't seem to like it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. I have heard the criticisms and respect peoples opinions but I find them to be game mechanics that are just design choices. I will address common criticisms first. All perceptions are from medium difficulty with Maximillian.

Minions sent out to the world don't come back - Yes, it is true, but it really doesn't affect you. You train right back up behind them and you can get your training room dialed in with duplicate machines for needed minions so they can train quickly. Remember this is a evil genius game not an automation game. You can change your base to accomodate demand and are rewarded for it. If you have a single guard trainer and you need 20 guards, you need to rethink your strategy.

Agents come in from the same directions - Yes, this makes them somewhat predictable, but with all of your other genius stuff you have to do, you WILL be surprised by teams of investigators disguised and making it past your guards. Sneaking in to your labs!! Alarm! apprehend!

The game is uninspired and grindy - I do not find this to be the case. I am constantly progressing on my story objectives and find plenty of time for odds and ends research and a few side stories. I think completionists may get frustrated as there is a lot of side stories to complete. I prefer to play the main story and what side stories I am interested in, which so far (about 80% playthrough) has been about 3. I think once I beat this game on Max, I am going to want to get more achievements and may do some completionist stuff, but I am really happy with the pace of the game.

The traps suck - I don't find this to be true. The folks saying this should try the higher level traps. They will trigger way more often. My level 1 punching glove gets tricked and disabled all the time while my level 3ish shark tank is dunking people left and right. But here's the awesome part. A lot of the agents that get past me are disguised investigators. When they trigger the shark tank it drops their disguise, and all of my guard minions come a running filling the hall with hot lead.

Now on to the good stuff. The graphics are top notch, the music is top notch, the sound is top notch. I am starting to get some super agents coming to my lair. One hailed from Central America and was like a luchador saboteur chucking bombs everywhere. Super cool. Once I defeated him I took revenge and targeted Central America with the M.I.D.A.S. device, which turned one of the incan temples to solid gold! Which I then stole. How cool is that! I totally feel like an Evil Genius! And that is the point of this whole game. It delivers. For me. I played the original, and loved it. This is different, but not so much that it sucks like people are saying. I picked it up while it was on sale for $11. It is easily worth 3 times that to me. But, I got the deluxe for like $21 which came with all the DLC's and I couldn't be happier.\t\t\t\t\t
