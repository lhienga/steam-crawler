\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe updates make the game less grating, but a polished turd is still a turd. There's an old Flash game called "Mastermind: World Conqueror", save yourself $40 and play that instead.

In the incredibly niche library of being an Evil Genius that weaponizes super science, this is easily one of the worst. The biggest rage trigger is the invasions, there's no slow escalation for the frequency and strength of your enemies.

At first its the eye rolling annoyance of manually telling your forces to distract investigators, there is a tech that does it automatically for a zone, which is nice for when the stars align and the crap shoot minion AI decides to do what its told. But then it gets better when the enemy sends units that just start setting your base on fire, and putting out those fires is a real treat. Extinguishing fires is a very slow process, which again only works when the AI decides it should be putting out fires.

It's not that the devs aren't attempting to make this game fun, you can feel the love for this classic series, but here's a slice of their efforts... Fire, it destroys objects obviously and rebuilding ♥♥♥♥ item by item is tedious, whats the developer solution? Selecting each item INDIVIDUALLY, and clicking "Automatic Rebuild".

This game should have had the Steam Workshop in mind from day 1, what an absolute missed opportunity.
Just surf through the Steam Discussions for this game, there are so many posts regarding "How does X feature work?", there's your sign post for why this game is subpar.

Also most of the humor isn't even remotely funny. You can make Super Villains funny, watch Venture Bros or any other source. I've been playing Rebellion games since "Rogue Trooper" came out, I know this company can do better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel like I played a different game than everybody else. The TL;DR here is that Evil Genius 2 is wonderful. It captures everything that made the original so unique while improving on it in almost every way.

Let's start at the beginning. The first Evil Genius was a great game. It blended a spy thriller style with unique base building mechanics and a witty sense of humor. That being said, it seems to me that whenever people discuss this game, they've got their nostalgia goggles on. Because while EG1 has its charm, its also riddled with jank and bugs. The game is filled to the brim with frustrating, tedious nonsense that artificially lengthens the game and wastes your time. Remember how if you wanted to buy something, minions had to manually fill a briefcase with gold and then run it all the way across the island to a purchase depot? Remember how you had to individually locate and tag every single intruder that entered your island or your minions would just ignore them? Remember how halfway through the game all your work is undone and you get moved to a brand new island to start from scratch for absolutely no reason? Yeah, I think people kinda forgot about all that. But I didn't! All of those little frustrations add up. I tried to get into EG1, but I couldn't because of all these things. EG1 is a game with a lot of great ideas and a lot of dumb mistakes. Unless you're piling on a few dozen mods, I don't recommend playing it nowadays. I understand that's an unpopular opinion, but once you've seen your base burn to the ground because a valet decided to take his fire extinguisher to the other side of the island for no reason, you'll see what I mean.

Which brings us to the present. I'm happy to say that Evil Genius 2 is the game that I wanted the first time around. It's basically Evil Genius 1, but better. Which is exactly what I needed in order to get into this. First and foremost, all of the tedious nonsense has been deliberately removed from the game. Guards carry their weapons around at all times instead of needing to run and grab them every time you trigger high alert. You no longer need specialized minions in order to train more; they help hurry it along, but if you lose every mercenary to a pack of S.M.A.S.H. soldiers, you're not screwed. No more individually tagging every single do-gooder on the island; you can still do that if you want to, but now you have the option of just placing standing orders in different parts of your base so all those expensive guards and valets actually have something to do. It's clear that someone on the development team saw all of the frustrating crap in EG1 and went out of their way to either remove it or streamline it, and I love that. It gives the player far more control over their villainous lair than before. It lets you stop worrying about miscellaneous ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, and start worrying about Blue Saint and his pack of bomb-slinging saboteurs who are here to wreck house. Furthermore, the revamped base building mechanics allow the player far more freedom and creativity in how they approach their base. Line each hallway with lockers and beds if you want. Put down guard posts wherever you need them so guards will automatically station themselves there instead of wandering around aimlessly. Hell, knock down half the casino and use it for generators if you want. It's up to you. There's a lot of room for the player to go crazy with a little creativity. It's great. I love it.

Aside from the revamped and improved mechanics, the real standout winner here for me are the visuals. EG2 really brings the style and flair of the original to new heights. The animation work on each minion is so well done. I will never get tired of watching Eli Barracuda strut around the base. Everyone is so loaded with color and character, lovingly crafted and fully realized. Hats off to the arts team for making each minion visually distinct. Couple that with another stellar soundtrack and it's easy to lose 6 hours in this game without even realizing it.

As for the accusations that this is somehow a mobile game, I honestly have no idea how you can come to that conclusion. Did people forget that half of the first game was sitting on your ass waiting for things to happen? Things are a lot snappier in EG2. Most schemes only take a few seconds to finish, and the ones that take longer are only there so you can get a passive benefit over a long period of time. You can cancel them whenever you're done with them and want to move on. The world stage in general is much easier to manage. In EG1 you had to send certain minions into an area and have them sit around for a while until they eventually thought up schemes to do, and during that time it's still possible for them to just die anyway. Here, once you set up your criminal network, the schemes are just there for you when you want to do them. And hey, if you really are that impatient, there's a built in feature to speed up time if you want, another new feature that wasn't in EG1 but really should have been.

I know that this review will probably get buried under a sea of "game bad, give upvote," but if for whatever reason you're discouraged and you stumble upon this, please give this game a try. It's filled with so much wonderful style and character that I cannot recommend it enough. If you're truly still on the fence about it, then wait for a sale and get it half off. I'm someone who wanted to like EG1, but couldn't. Now, I'm hooked on EG2. It's the game I wanted in the first place. Also, someone please give Red Ivan's voice actor some kind of award. He is hamming it up so hard and I love it.\t\t\t\t\t
