\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a good game, but I understand why it has mixed reviews.
Now that the initial season pass is done, I hope we get more DLC for the game to patch it up, or just patches in general.
It feels extremely unpolished. Like the first iteration of an Early Access title waiting for community feedback to become a great game.

I wish we could tell minions to prioritize certain things. Like telling my muscle minions to absolutely man a security post over sitting on a guard table playing Poker.
I wish the game told us what minions can and can't do. A merc is seemingly just a better Guard, yet a Socialite can't man the Casino tables. Biologists can't do what Scientists can do either. Yet both Engineers and Technicians can repair things?
Also, the Cover operation as a whole is incredibly shallow. I hope it gets a full revamp to be honest. Specialized items to scam tourists and specialized items to trick agents would be great.
Why does the "Conference Table" have seats for 8 Henchmen and the Genius, yet its a hardcap of 5 Henchmen?
Why is there a hardcap on 300 minions?

I see minions with perks telling me they want to be a Technician or a Scientist, so why can't I specifically choose them to be next in line? Instead its just seemingly random whether they get to become what they want to be or not.

I also wish we could turn off the robot voice, or at the very least specific voice errors. Why is there 5 different messages for a fire when I can do literally nothing to fix it? I have fire extinguishers where the fire is, I see technicians running to kill the fire. I don't need to be told about it constantly.

Despite my criticisms, I find the game to be quite enjoyable and charming. Incredibly addictive, too.
I recommend the game, but I'd also recommend not using the Oceans DLC; the temperature management system is incredibly unfun. While I'll admit it might be hypocritical of me to both fault the game for being shallow in its base building, then recommend not getting a DLC that makes base building more complex, but the temperature management is just a stick without a carrot to me. There's nothing complex about surrounding your base in air conditioners, and all it really adds is visual clutter.\t\t\t\t\t
