\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved EG1. EG2 isnt EG1. It's its own thing. Now thats not saying its an amazing game, it has its flaws. But I just wanted to start this with that.

A lot of reviews say this is just like a mobile game. I worked in mobile games for a decade as a Product Manager and I honestly dont see how this is like a mobile game. At all. Mobile games have gatchas, multiple currencies some of which you can buy with real money. There are none of those in this. Yea, you have to wait for research to complete or missions to end, but you cant pay real money to speed them up like in a mobile game. EG1 had wait-to-complete missions and research too. I dont get the comparison.

The Good:

Fun lazy base building. If you like micromanagement games, this is for you. I for one love games like Rimworld and prison architect and there are very much elements of those games here. Simplified, but elements still.

The Ok:

Im frustrated that the conference tables have so many chairs but I can only recruit a few henchmen. The henchmen mission stories are a great change from EG1 (where in EG1 you;d just run an off-screen mission to recruit). Overall henchmen and minions are varied and interetsting and fun.

The bad:

The traps ughhhh. I remember spending an hour in EG1 playing with linking traps. Linking of traps is still here, kinda, but not really. You get an indicator when one trap will trigger another, but there seems to be few traps that will interact with one another. I seem to remember in EG1 being able to link the Sleeping Gas trap and the Bee trap, so agents would get stung to death while sleeping. I also remember the Crash Test Dummy, which allowed you to test traps.

The biggest loss from EG1 is that scientists dont wander the base and suggest improvements. I LOVED this feature. A scientist would look at a fire extingiuisher and come up with an Ice Trap you could research. They would also look at Loot you'd steal and come up with traps based on the Loot. It gave you a reason to hunt Loot. Loot now just gives passive buffs, which yay, but the passive buffs are tiny and its hard to tell what minions have what buffs.

Overall I like the game. Its fun to get a little st*ned and play. Its not going to win any awards but not every game need to be a critical darling. Some games are good just being okay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSubpare story and game experience. A third through the storyline the gameplay stagnates and it becomes a waiting game to get the necessary funds (money, henchmen or intelligence points) to progress. The main story and side missions quickly lose their excitement and don’t differ enough in content. Only using the chair and traps as interrogation devices is boring. Using the training equipment and equipment from other rooms as interrogation devices would be much more fun.

A bug stopped the main story. so that I could not complete the story.
I really wanted to like this game, but it felt more like a chore to try to complete.
