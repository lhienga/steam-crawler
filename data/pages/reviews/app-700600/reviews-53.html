\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really hate to say this but you are significantly better off playing the original. This game in its present state does not respect your time AT ALL. I don't know if the developers were stuck in some terrible mobile game mindset but there are some decisions that have no place in a game like this. Were the decisions fixed, the game would actually be a worthy sequel.

For example, there are far, FAR too many side missions confronting the crime lords/super agents that straight up... don't do anything. Just meaningless trivial rewards and a brief conversation when you want to actually deal with them permanently.

Too many objectives require a ridiculous amount of different schemes on the world map. Beyond obviously padding. I get that we're trying to take over the world here but don't waste my damn time. Every time a new set of x/5 requirements appeared I groaned tremendously.

Adding to the annoyance is the fact that schemes now consume all sent minions. I get what they were going for in terms of making the minion losses in schemes more predictable but given the increasing demands of how many minions are required for schemes it just consumes a ridiculous amount of minions, and it becomes a game of waiting for the next set of minions to be trained. Riveting.

Another bad aspect is the fact that heat now passively builds up from your criminal networks set up in the various territories. Unlike EG1 where you could pull everybody from a given region and let things cool down, here you don't get that choice. It's just a never-ending cycle of heat increasing, being maxed out, locked down, and falling again. Making the optimal play to manage heat wasting a bunch of time and resources for heat that generally isn't buying you anything, as money becomes all but worthless the more built up you get.

A significant amount of my time spent in this game was just waiting on max speed for something to happen. Just an absolutely terrible ratio of meaningful actions to waiting around. Playing this felt like drowning in quicksand towards the end of the campaign.

There is a good game buried under all these bad decisions. It is my hope that the developer will fix these and let the game live up to its full potential, but given how long we are post-release I'm going to guess that's not going to happen. I'd love to play the other campaigns to try out other islands and geniuses but I'm just not prepared to waste that much of my life under the current gameplay.

Just play the original instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA faithful sequel to the original, perfectly adequate with no major flaws but there's absolutely nothing remarkable enough to set it apart from any other base builder aside from PG rated supervillian writing. The campaign will take you about 15 hours and there's not much in the way of replay, it feels like a slog after the first hour or two.

I made the mistake of buying all DLC, which adds maybe 1 cumulative hour of gameplay.

Competent enough for die-hard fans of the genre, but casual fans of the genre such as myself will be left wanting much more both in terms of gameplay and writing. It honestly feels like it was once a good game, but was surgically altered by an evil corporation for a kind of mass appeal that ultimately has the same level of appeal as a melted bowl of vanilla ice cream. Not the worst thing in the world, but "disappointment" is an understatement.

5/10, wait for this unenthusiastic handy of a game to go on sale. There are much better games that deserve your attention first.\t\t\t\t\t
