\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first glance the game seems great, a faithfull sequel and upgrade to the original game. It runs well on Windows and Linux, and it has a great sense of style and humor. Where the game ends up falling short is the gameplay, which is an frustrating and unbalanced mess.

The game's premise is about building an evil lair, and managing the many layers of security protecting it from the outside. In truth there is only one layer of security; military force. Between agents reacting to distraction by immediatly opening fire, traps that rarely work and kill more of your own minions, and the sheer power of even the most basic agents.

Enemies are just as bland and non-functional as your defenses, rogues and saboteurs, which are meant to steal your gold and sabotage your base, will never actually make it in your base because of how the game decides to handle confrontation;
they can either be escorted out or start a gunfight. This leads to the entire gameplay loop of defending your power station and vault be completely pointless.

Because of all these flaws, the game essentially turns into Rimworld, where the objective is to build an effective killbox and ignore all other mechanics of the game.

If only the game had more interactions between your minions and agents. If only the game had been better balanced. I would have been able to recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

Rating: 7.5 / 10

Once again you reclaim your authority as an evil genius bent on world domination. Follow your evil genius' story to success while battling the forces of justice.

Choose your island with a preview of buildable areas and stats is what a evil genius looks for in real estate. Another handy feature is to restore walls that you've dug out that either have no use for the space or just doing some renovation.

Desiring success in quick progression can cause a ripple effect of waves of soldiers so best to balance reward over risk.

Henchmen are no longer plagued with lives like EG1, they just need to rest up before being back into action.
Disposing of corpses is much easier and efficient this time round. Simply a minion collects one and puts it into the Incinerator and POOF! Gone. No more freezer rooms for storage.


Some negatives however:
  • You are restricted in only doing one main and one side mission making the game slow paced. In the original you could start multiple side missions simultaneously (like acquiring new henchmen types) which could still mean progress if you were temporarily stuck waiting to progress in one mission.

  • At times it can be boring while you wait for certain things to complete (requiring enough minions for missions, completing side missions so can move onto another side mission, etc).

  • Minions used in missions on the World Map are just....gone. Once the mission succeeds or fails, all these minions are lost and need to retrain replacements adding more of a grind.

  • Some minions prefer a one-on-one fight while your other minions just stand around watching those two punch up each other in your base. The minion eventually loses their life so another takes the spot restarting another one-on-one.
    You NEED minions with ranged weapons for these situations otherwise be prepared to lose ALOT of minions.

  • Missable Loot (missions) - Some loot missions just disappears if you either don't start them when they appear and others just seem to disappear through progression.

  • Lost Missions - while the game does state at times that progressing in the main story mission results in some side missions disappearing, it would be nice to know exactly which ones.

Overall:
It's fun to be evil for the second time around, balancing operations and resources while striving for success.
Slowly expanding your base to become a super power is satisfying while having to adapt to stronger forces undermining your efforts.
At times you need to work hard but no one said world domination was easy!\t\t\t\t\t
