\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never played Evil Genius 1 so can't compare it to that. What I can compare this game to is the original Dungeon Keeper or Theme Hospital and I think this game is one of the best spiritual successors to that style of game that I've played. I despise this graphical style in general as it's everywhere and usually done badly however in EG2 the theme and the style match perfectly, like cute cartoon horror. You can pace the game how you like, storm through and complete each character ASAP or settle in for the longhaul and beat all the objective. The game play is classic in like with DK/TH with quite a lot of added twists.

The game is split between two simultaneous modes, the world network and your Genius' lair. Both of these need attention and a lot of it. Add to this that there are a huge number of spanners being thrown in the works even if you think you're on top of all the action. The world networks is where you sacrifice your minions, time, money, and intel for something you need, and every single node is gathering heat that will trigger an attack. There's a fair bit of strategy and with a DLC like the ice one new strategies completely open up completely. The lair is the bread and button colony sim style, get tokens, spend tokens making your lair better in every way. You'll have to balance defences, budgets, power, heat, number of minions, again it's classic style. The villians are all quirky in their own way with questionable voice acting for some and great for others. I also haven't faced a single technical issue playing the game and very few bugs.

The downsides for me in this game is the QoL. There are so many basic things missing that you'd see in a small indie game I'm amazed they aren't in this as it adds so many extra clicks and wastes time in general. Overall the QoL gives a feel of a game the devs didn't intend for people to play long.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game unlike anything I've ever played before. Building the dungeon stronghold of an evil genius??? Who would have imagined. I was not expecting the depth in which this game pulls you in and a storyline that is both creative and humorous. The graphics are excellent, the music quite pleasant and fitting, and the game-play itself (aside from one or two bits of "not enough info" in the tutorial) is easy to learn yet still proves challenging. To make this game perfect would be to have a sandbox mode where you could pick and chose which enemies to fight, and have more than a few mountains (maps) to transform since 3 is just not enough. Plus if the game were open to mods and more items / furniture / etc. could be added would be even more wonderful. Regardless, the game (as is) is completely addicting and I'm happy I took a chance on playing something different.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh my god, the problems this game has.
First of all I got the game for $3 including all DLC
I still can't recomend it, even were you to purchase it.
First of all, sometimes workers (Of any type) just won't work. They'll get assigned a job, and just not do it, meaning you have tasks eternally not done. Furthermore, both your ability to gain minions, and your total minions are capped pretty heavily. While I understand the performance reasons for this, you have the ability to use 4 floors in your lair and it's hard to use 2. So you can't even really just kill them off easily to replace.
Secondly, so many aspects of the mid and late game are poorly explained, I still haven't figured out how to train the 2 most powerful muscle minions, or how to get more henchmen, or how to permanentely deal with the super agents. What certain minion and agent stats and how to manipulate them are mid game technologies. Unlocking new technologies need new quests completed, but you won't be told which ones.
It's hard to zone minions, so my muscle minions are never near the places I need them. My suspicion minions are constantly being killed by high level agents, who shoot first and get schmoozed late. Adding onto annoying agents, a lot of late game ones are immune to distraction, making them also effectively immune to late game traps, so your only option is minions, who half the time don't even recognize disguises or equip weapons.
On the oceans DLC specifically, their agents are so insanely powerful, and so poorly explained that dozens of minions and hundreds of thousands of dollars have been lost trying to deal with them, and failing.
There's more but honestly, a lot of my issues could just be solved by a tutorials/explanations journal and a fix on the worker assignment bug. Maybe add some tips to the journal and the game works, but currently... it just doesn't.
