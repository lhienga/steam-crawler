\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI won't beat around the bush too much: it's a game I'm enjoying the heck of, but I regularly wish it could get out of its own way. So many of the things I loved from the previous game are here and better; the building, the schemes, the comedy and characters and aesthetic are just absolutely wonderful. The level of management on the World Stage is . . . different, but similar. You're not losing/risking minions just by sticking them there, but boy will you lose 'em by trying to do anything. I actually kind of love how disposable that makes your minions; really leans into the wickedness and apathy about their lives for comic effect.

I played a fair bit near launch, and aside from some bugs that made me reload some earlier saves and some cascade failures that were my own fault for not realizing what saboteurs do and how ineffective my cover op set-up was at avoiding suspicion, I had fun . . . though I burned out on the slog it took to do sidequests and manage so many things simultaneously. You can easily get to a spot in main and sidequests where the consequences are a LOT to deal with, and your tools to mitigate them can't keep up easily.

But I just replayed it with a different villain, and have to admit it's a wonderfully fun and different experience. Not sure how much is the differing genius and how much is patches and additional content that's come out since then, but I recommend it far more now. Emma is a fantastic genius if you like a solid cover operation, and TF2's Pyro is a great henchman who offers some powerful and unsubtle oomph.\t\t\t\t\t
