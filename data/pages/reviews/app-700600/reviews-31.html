\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game could be great. Cool design, cool traps, interesting game systems and neat characters. Doesn't take itself seriously, which it certainly shouldn't - so that's a plus...
Pretty much everything I'd like to see in a Bond-villain lair simulator.

This isn't a game though. This is a chore. So much managing. For no good reason. Concisely listing all the avenues of time-wasting and wildly unnecessary micromanagement this game implements would be nearly impossible, and many other reviews already do so. I will, however, the most gross offender in my opinion:

The tech-tree, pacing, and quests. (these are all, unfortunately, very interconnected)
The tech-tree is designed to hide so many QoL buildings, straight-up upgrades, and some must-have technologies behind many hours of painstakingly slow Main Story Line(MSL) quests.
MSL quests that, if rushed through, will start to remove some secondary quests completely. You can only work on on secondary quest at a time. and there are a lot. To systematically cover all the content is unfathomably laborious.

I am convinced a core part of the game's design is inflating gameplay hours. Sadly can't recommend something so wasteful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is little more than a cash grab based on nostalgia for an old and well liked game.

Much of the game play is boring and repetitive. The entire system of traps is pointless, guards perform the same function but do it better. The casino is useless, agents will never actually be stopped by it and will enter your base where the best option is always to immediately kill them. The agents and super agents can also completely bypass your casino because they can spawn inside your base at various points including the helipad.

It is trivial to reach the point where you no longer need to modify the base before you get half way through the game, which turns the rest of it into sitting around and waiting for schemes and science to complete.

Most of the DLC adds stuff that is either detrimental to the gameplay experience (Temperature mechanics from "Oceans Campaign"), is completely inferior to things that existed in the base game (Robots & Abominations), or adds cosmetic content that should have been in the game when it released (In-Genius Items Pack)

Lastly for this review, they supported the game for precisely one year after launch and have abandoned it since.\t\t\t\t\t

EG 1 was a grind and repetitive, number two however is an incredibly dull, tedious, convoluted and repetitive boring AF strategy. There is very little mission depth and the rewards for completing side quests are just not worth the effort.

Basically it is more of a base defence game as that is what you spend most of your time doing. Except unlike the first game, you can't chain traps and defences together to make elaborate traps for a laugh. EG 2 makes traps basically useless as even the lowest level of investigator etc can bypass them and shut them down. You can't link them, you can't really do anything of value with them and they just seem like a waste of resources as your minions are more likely to set them off.

Saboteurs will get in and destroy so much so often that most of your gameplay will be spent replacing the same stuff over and over. But why not set it up to auto-replace if damaged I hear you say, good ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ point, you can, but you have to build the item first and then select the toggle for auto replace and you have to do that individually for every single ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ item in the game. There is no multi select for anything in this game. So if you are being raided, you have to click on each bad guy individually to set capture, kill etc. If you want to retrofit a room your best bet is to fill in the room using the build tool and it will delete everything in it. Other wise you are forced to click on each item and delete them individually.

The main missions get ridiculous, boring and very very convoluted very quickly. You have to do way to many steps to complete each tier and they get more and more added to it the closer you get to the end. Have x amount of hitmen and valets and technicians, plus do 50000 missions on the main map and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat and repeat.

I tried really hard to love this game but to be honest, I think while it has some great features it has actually taken away from the depth of the first and gave us a very shallow, very dull sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

The game is a sequel to the original Evil Genius, and it definitely lives up to its predecessor. The graphics are top-notch, with a stylized cartoonish look that's both engaging and fun to look at.
