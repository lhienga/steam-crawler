\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMost of the other reviews here have covered the basics of this game and its problems/highlights -

The art style/graphics are excellent - they fit the cheesey setting, they're vibrant and well executed, and the animations are stylized to make the game's charm and aesthetic half the reason to play it. The base building aspect is solid enough, build different rooms to do different things that you need done - vaults to store money, barracks to let your minions rest, labs to research new tech, etc.

The game fails on a few fundamental flaws that are..just the game itself, and they make the entire 'gameplay' experience overtly tedious.

The one thing that this game should've executed well is the most boring of all - agent/super agent infiltrations into your base are just mind-numbingly repetitive. Unless you're willing to turn the entire first floor into nothing but traps/trap rooms then they're absolutely worthless to build. You'll be manually hunting for/always on the lookout for infiltrators so you can tag them, go on high alert, wait for your guards to clean them up, and then going back to the business of the rest of the repetitive aspects of the game. You can either stack guard upon guard upon guard next to the two main entrances to your lair or..spend most of your time hunting agents before they wreck your base and then leave. Traps should be these interactive/fun things that, you know, actually work, instead of just being bypassed wholesale. There is almost zero interplay with this - one of the only main 'fun' - aspect of the game.

Otherwise the main portion of the game is also rinse and repeat. Go to the world map, select a scheme to execute (if an available one is worthwhile - about a quarter of the time you'll be looking at schemes you can't execute or are just non-sensical to execute, regardless of how updated the networks are), press the execute button, and then just...wait and do nothing while it happens.

I wanted to like this game. I really did. I tried to give it a shot and give it a fair amount of time invested. It just..wasn't worth it. After the dozenth time having to hunt down agents, having traps do nothing, having to go on/come off high alert, having to stare at meaningless jobs...it just isn't a fun experience.\t\t\t\t\t

You spend the whole game beefing up security getting ready to make sure no one can get into your base only to find out it’s all useless because the super agents don’t come in on a boat or a helicopter or something realistic.
Instead they pop into existence already in the middle of your vault or somewhere random in your base making all of your defenses pointless. It really ruins the flow of the game and the internal consistency.
I wouldn’t mind if the drive up in their special boat, unloaded their minions and then broke through my defenses because then at least it would feel like there was a purpose to building it but instead they spawn in the middle of the base already past all the defenses. It takes all the fun out of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
