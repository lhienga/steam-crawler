\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has a ton of potential but it's bad for all the wrong reasons. They playstyle is incredibly frustrating due to poor decissions. Everything is designed to slow you down and to stretch the playtime because the story is tiny.

For starters, there's the maintenance mechanic. Everything progressively breaks down so you need staff to repair it. The more things you have, the more staff members you need to assign to maintenance, and since you have a limit on how many staff you can have, it feels like a tax. The more you expand the less you can expand, which limits base potential.

The cold-heat mechanic is another big issue. Normally, for most things, it's inconsequential, except for the two main ways you have to progress: electricity and comms. The more reactors you have, the more the room heats up so the more cooling units you need, cooling units that take space and electricity, so you need bigger rooms and more generators, which need more maintenance, so you need more personnel.

You can go about this two ways, you can either do it the hard way and devise a floor plan with multiple rooms and properly designed cooling patters, which takes a lot of time and money, or you can do what most people do and reserve one entire floor of your base for generators only, ignore the heat mechanic and pay the price by assigning more maintenance staff to fix the rapidly-degrading generators.

Speaking of generators, the electricity mechanic is terrible. This is a common complaint. You need hundreds and hundreds of generators to power your base. The most advanced and powerful generator, the fussion generator can power 40 cameras. Literally everything except decorations and standard beds/lockers require electricity. A painted square on the floor so guards know where to stand? Requires electricity. A wooden counter for a receptionist or a welcoming table with pamflets? Requires electricity. A bioengineered venus flytrap plant? Requires electricity to ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥! And because it'd be to flagrant to make weapon racks require electricity to ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, they turned them into high-tech weapon dispensers with holograms and ♥♥♥♥. Their only purpose is to literally hold guns.

Traps. Oh wow, a fun gameplay mechanic that rewards clever base building and proper placement of- ahahaha! No. Traps are useless. There comes a point when enemy agents simply disable all the traps in their path and they have so much HP and morale that you need a maze with a couple dozen traps one after another to stop them. You need to exploit AI errors to make traps work. Basically, you need a door, a trap right after the door, and a ranged trap that can reach the door. The idea is that the agent will need to cross the door to try to disarm the closest trap after the door, so it'll be hit by the second trap. Repeat this combo ad nauseam.

Dealing with agents is very frustrating. Even at 0 heat, the frequency and strenght of the agents depend a lot on your progress, but there comes a point when waves of investigators are coming every two minutes (at 0 heat!). You have plenty of ways to deal with them, but the game counters all of them.

They give you a casino, which is supposed to be a fake front to hide your lair, and it might work at first, but lategame it's pointless. enemy agents just ignore it.

You can post guards at the entrance of your lair and order them to turn intruders back, but at some point investigators just shoot at them.

You can have special cameras and trainers to spot disguised agents, but at some point they all get "flawless disguises" or are outright invisible with cloaking technology, so you have to manually send your hechmen to kill them.

Again, you have traps, but at some point all agents can disable them at range unless you exploit their IA.

Oh, and of course, you could always order them to capture or kill the enemy agents right? Wrong! If a single agent fails to report, they'll send soldiers to kill you. Soldiers can one or two-shot your minions and sometimes they carry rocket launchers tha obliterate everything in a large area in one hit, including your stuff, traps, etc. and cause fires that quickly spread. So you have to use your henchmen to deal with them.

Then there's the "world". You're supposed to install secret operations in various parts of the world so you can run schemes, which are missions for your minions to steal money and engage in general villainous stuff. This mechanic has a lot of potential. Running schemes gives you money among other things, but it also raises "heat", attracting the forces of justice the more heat you have, and makingthem more powerful. This mechanic soon become spointless. You gain money passively, and waves of enemies are frequent enough, so you don't want to make it worse. Most of the time you simply run schemes to keep the heat at 0.

Ah, but the game has a way to counter this, you see! There are super agents, and they don't care that you are a master of misdirection and money-laundering; they'll attack your base at random. On top of that, they instantly pop-up in territories that don't have a heat-reduction scheme running, and start increasing heat and at the same time prevent the heat-reducing scheme from showing up. And after finishing a heat-reduction scheme, it takes a couple of minutes for the next to show up, which gives plenty of time for those super-agents to move to those territories. If you run any scheme while they are there, they'll start investigating, and if you run a second scheme, they'll attack your base, otherwise, they'll stay there, increasing heat rapadly until they decide to attack your base.

Most of the complaints about this game could be easily resolved by tweaking numbers. It's a matter of balance, but hell, they won't even allow mods because they've installed an anti-cheater third party program in a single player game. It's insane.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEG2 is a mixed bag. There are a few improvements over the original EG, but there are a lot of things the original did better.

The Good
Nice graphics
Choose your starting island
Bigger starting base
Moving objects and changing your room layouts is much easier
Researching is now done through a soft tech tree, rather than trial and error
More than 100 minion cap
Guard posts

The Bad
Enemy agents are harder to identify among the tourists
You can't tag an entire squad at once
You can't place an object in a location where it would block access to a corner of a room, even if there's nothing in that corner
You can't place an object if even one of its access points would be blocked
You can't tell how much money you're making at a glance
You can't just send minions wherever you want to steal (you have to choose specific schemes that require specific minions)
Apparently evil doesn't know what stairs are (needs to be researched before you can access other floors of your base)
You can't place triggers and traps independently and traps can't be linked in sequences
There's no island minimap
There doesn't seem to be any way to torture prisoners with any devices other than the interrogation chair
Loot no longer appears on the world map to be stolen whenever (you have to select a specific sidequest if it's available, then the scheme to steal the loot becomes available on the map)
When building your base, there are a number of objects that actually restore minion stats, or reduce the rate those stats decay, but they are listed under Decor, and if you're like me, you might ignore the Decor tab altogether, then wonder why so many minions are trying to desert

Unintuitive Design
This one isn't really a good or bad, but I wanted to include it. There are some research projects that allow you to select an area of your base and tell your minions to automatically tag enemy agents in that area with a specific tag. For example, you can set it so that all agents who enter the casino are automatically tagged for distraction, or all agents who enter the control room are automatically tagged for capture. After researching the first of these, I was trying to figure out how to use it. I tried the build menu, but there was nothing there. So, I thought maybe it was accessed through security, so I tried the security desk, thinking maybe I could set it to tag enemies who are seen by the cameras, but no. Maybe the guard posts? No, why would it be there? It wasn't. I eventually found it in the menu where you find objectives and research. Oh, btw, you know how in the original EG, after you complete research, you can read a description of the new items, and some of them even have tutorial video? I haven't seen anything like that yet.\t\t\t\t\t
