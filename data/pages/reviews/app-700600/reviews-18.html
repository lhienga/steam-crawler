\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved playing the original Evil Genius, and this game has lots of potential but falls short in many ways.

The pacing of this game is really dreadful. It takes so long to accomplish anything of substance. You can only attempt one 'side story' mission at a time, which usually involves throwing minions at a multitude of missions and waiting for horribly long moments for agents or goons or whoever to show up on your island and then finally make their way to your base where you can get rid of them... just to have to research something to unlock the next step. If that's not bad enough, you compare it to the first iteration of Evil Genius where you could steal as much loot at the same time as you wanted - and the loot actually gives you benefits.

The loot you can steal in the first game had a multitude of uses - it regenerated stats on minions, allowed research to unlock better items in the base, and it even sort of made sense what items could be researched from each loot item, within the context of the absurdity of the game. The loot in #2 is something the minions look at, it has some random minor benefit, it unlocks a mission, maybe.

The world domination screen in the first game wasn't the best, it took your attention away from what was happening in your base... it's not really any better in this one. It's nice you can perform missions to reduce your heat, but all your regions generate heat automatically, and as you upgrade the criminal networks, you need better minions to accomplish the same heat-reducing missions regardless of how much heat you have. The domination screen is still a distraction from your base, but everything is so slow paced I guess that's not much of a problem. The game also fails to mention you're going to attract all the super agents by virtue of establishing criminal networks vs actually doing anything of notoriety, so I ended up with all of them bouncing around the world map almost immediately.

The covert operation doesn't make any sense. Tourists come to the base, are entertained for a little bit, and then leave. Agents come to the base, your deception minions attempt to keep them distracted, the agents have way too high stats to be distracted, and they usually end up coming inside your base anyway.

The henchmen are largely useless in this game. Their only saving graces are they are indestructible and you can get rid of them if you realize how useless they are. Some of them give you hopes you may be able to distract agents because they have abilities that have greater distraction power and/or they are better at distracting in the first place, but even then they usually can't do it. The only henchmen I found remotely useful were Jubei and IRIS. Jubei continues his tradition of usefulness because he can teleport, and IRIS is helpful by virtue of her camera ability which kind of acts like the summon minion ability all the henchmen had in the previous game. One henchmen can't be recruited unless you defeat all of the super agents... too bad I did that before I started that quest line, so that one got locked away by bad quest line design. Eli only uses his gun when you activate an ability - and by the time I recruited him, no agent could be defeated even with all six shots.

The base building... is better. It's nice that you have multiple levels to play around with... annoying so much of the levels and islands are locked away by research options. It's nice you can change one room type to another, or even part of it or all of it back to dirt - the original game was pretty limited with that. I wasn't able to find a way to plan out the base ahead of time and develop the design for a room but not have it built until later, have it paused, until I wanted it built - everything was ordered at the time it was confirmed. Not fun. That makes it hard to know if everything is going to fit where you want it, or to pace yourself with your cash flow. It's also dumb you can only place cameras and traps in corridors. I ended up trying to put pieces of corridors inside of other rooms to get around that.

I never bothered to use traps except for specific mission-related ones. There were never enough agents to feel like I needed them.. and I couldn't suspend my disbelief at agents having lethal traps activate on them and then go on with their business like nothing happened.

I initially had some cash flow issues, but eventually got to the point where the tourist income on the slot machines and the passive income from the criminal networks were so great I couldn't keep up with them or build a big enough vault for all the money.

This game is just boring. It's too slow-paced, and that's for someone who typically turtles games. This game is so boring it makes me want to play the original. This game is so boring I kept playing only because I wanted to see what played out with the story, but there wasn't any significant story to keep my attention. This game is so boring I'm going to stop here instead of going on.\t\t\t\t\t
