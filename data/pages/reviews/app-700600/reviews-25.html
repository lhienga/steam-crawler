\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, this is a great game. It is 100% worth picking up if you like base building games like Dungeon Keeper or the original Evil Genius.

However, there are some flaw that keep it from reaching its potential.

Firstly, the Casino / Traps are poorly implemented. On lower difficulty levels, investigators can be drained of their resolve with just a few valets and some well placed advanced guard posts. It doesn’t matter if there are not enough alcohol and gambling to do anything, because as soon as the agent tries to leave the casino and get past a guard at their post, they are led away and some of their resolve disarmed. It’s trivial to deal with and traps are a waste of time. On the other hand, with higher difficulty levels or much later in the game, the investigators ignore the casino almost entirely and too often if they are stopped by a guard, they will just start shooting. It’s a casino, there are places the public isn’t allowed to go. Shooting makes no sense. Likewise, traps are ignored at this point, because every agent is skilled enough to shut them all down.

Secondly, you can’t select multiple items at a time. I can’t tell you how many times I needed to mass delete objects, because I researched the upgrade. Or there are quests where you have to have a power outage, but I had just upgraded my power room to Fusion Generators, so I had to turn off 20 of them individually. OR, and this is frustrating, playing as Ivan and trying to combat a fire while turning the “Auto Replace” on over 1000 safes / generators that kept catching on fire. (Ivan’s super weapon causes fires to breakout in his lair. Easily the worst super weapon.) I should be able to drag the mouse and select every item and either sell it, turn on auto replace, or turn it off. Those are the three commands that you need to do to a ton of items all at once.\t\t\t\t\t
