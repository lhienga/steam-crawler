\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played, and loved, Evil Genius 1 so my opinions have a lot to do with what was in EG1 compared to EG2

Graphics and animations are much improved. Everything looked and moved great

The evil lair, arguably the most important aspect of the game, is improved in some capacities and worse in in others. The islands are not terribly constrained for space, so its possible to build how you want. This also has a drawback of a lot of waiting if something needs to be moved across the lair. Using stairs to other levels took minions a long time as well. The art for the lair is very well done
In EG1 a cover operation, a hotel and casino, could be built to waste the time of agents and keeps tourists occupied. A well run cover operation had a massive effect on who wandered into the lair. Not so in EG2. Its practically useless. Tourists don't wander into the lair and all but the worst agents will find their way to the door
There is a "back door" in EG2 lairs where enemy forces land on a beach and pretty much walk directly into your lair. This is a terrible design. It necessitates stationing a huge guard force at the doors
Power requirements are ridiculous. To keep everything running I had dozens of power plants.

Traps were a miss with me. In EG1 agents scoured the island looking for your lair, so setting up traps to damage or kill agents in unused areas was a good plan to thin the herd a little. In EG2 the agents start in the hotel, use the back door, or teleport in. There are no unused areas for traps to safely damage agents. Agents can trigger traps which damages minions as often as agents. Most agents had little difficulty disabling traps, so what few traps that did trigger barely made a difference.

The minions were largely carried over from EG1. Two of the groups, Science and Deception, were barely touched. Higher level science minions could research more stuff. Deception just mingle with guests and their ability to get rid of agents weakened considerably. At least muscle minions got a few more weapons, but higher level muscle minions just did higher damage and could absorb more. Minions do not come back from missions anymore. I was on the fence about minions not returning from any mission ever, decided I don't like it because by the endgame it just adds more training time that means sitting around and waiting

The research in EG2 was simplified down considerably. EG1 went through a process where science minions would take notes on whatever was in the lair then see if it could be used to research something better. This made stealing unique artifacts appealing since anything in the lair could spark future research. EG2 has a research tree. Research enough tier 1 stuff, tier 2 opens. There is not a lot to do here, like stuff was missing... (Side Note: I took a look at some of the DLC for sale. Stuff that was in the base game of EG1 is now DLC for EG2. In some cases, items that were endgame in EG1 are available in EG2 but sold separately from the base game. This offended me)

The world map, by the end of the game, was simply annoying. In EG1 if you wanted money or to complete missions you sent a crew out with the right guys for the job. The longer they were in country the more "heat" they generated and the higher strength agents came to the island. In EG2 criminal operations are set up in each territory which automatically generate heat and money. To run missions that advance the plot these networks must be in place. The automatic heat growth necessitates sending out deception minions to lower heat or face high level agents in the lair. Only one mission can be run in each territory so the deception mission ends if any other are run. The deception mission also has a time expiration. The deception mission doesn't always appear immediately either. I spent far too much time playing "whack-a-mole" trying to keep 20-ish territory's deception mission going. If heat gets too high the territory locks down too, no missions can be run. So if a mission important to the story gets locked down you get to wait

Super agents had interesting stories, but ultimately mostly require the same strategy. A huge mass of guards by the front and back door along with the Evil Genius himself. Most of the Super Agents can turn invisible to all minions. Henchmen and the Evil Genius can unmask them, but henchman wander around the base and cannot be ordered to stay anywhere. Super Agents who make it into the lair can do a lot of damage, so the Evil Genius can be parked by the doors, surrounded by guards to prevent that.

I played through the whole campaign, but suspect I won't again. Maybe with Evil Genius 3, and even then I will wait for the reviews and probably a price drop\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10/10 - would take over the world again

Review - First off I loved the original so was very excited for this game. Although the roll out of additional DLC right after launch was controversial I got the season pass and have enjoyed the additions as they've come along. You start by picking your evil genius and where you want to build your secret lair. You then recruit minions and henchman and build your base. You can send minions out to conduct missions across the globe. Agencies will then start sending agents to investigate your base, and will eventually send their super agents. The aspect I enjoyed most was the base building, making a front for it to keep up appearances of a tropical casino to fool tourists and dim witted agents, and then building a hefty set of defences to combat more competent foes who aren't as easily distracted. There is so much content, from unique loot missions, recruiting henchmen, taking down the super agents, and researching new rooms and traps for your base. And of course there's the main story mission which see's you fulfil your evil geniuses plan.

Achievements - Most achievements are quite easy to pick up. There were some buggy ones on release but these have all been resolved. Hard difficulty is very hard, though I believe they've made it easier through patches. At launch it was too easy for your base to spiral out of control with agent killing your minions one by one as they arrive, but once you hit a certain point it becomes relatively easy to defend if you focus on having a constant supply of muscle minions. The addition of DLC content also really helps. The hardest achievements seem to be the collector achievements as for example some of the loot missions are no longer accessible if you move the story too fast.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very solid sequel to the original. The voice acting is actually pretty great, with the little stories especially when it comes to the super agents I think adding a great deal of menacing character to the game. Graphics are great while still staying true to the original, and the campaign has significantly less frustrations in my opinion. Good time to get now as I've found no real bugs compared to how it was at launch.

DLC is probably not worth it, except for the Oceans pack which plays like a mini expansion with a number of game play changes (I liked Polar too). Other DLC are just a bit of character - I got them with the season pass, but would only enable one or two per playthrough to spice things up.

Overall, very happy with this game.\t\t\t\t\t
