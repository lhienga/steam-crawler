\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuick tip: If this game seems appealing at all, you should consider playing the original Evil Genius first. This game is just a polished and updated version of that one.

While this game is pretty easy, rather slow paced, a bit unbalanced, and a bit bugged at times, it's a game with a lot of charm and character. Don't let the bad reviews stop you. This game had a very rough launch, and it took a lot of updates to get this game to the state it's in.

Now with that out of the way, let me ask you this: You like bond villains? You wanna own an island lair, fill it with dangerous and outrageous traps, and beat up would-be goody two shoes? This game has that in spades. We got all kinds of traps. Shark pits. Laser walls. Poison gas. Poison darts. Freeze rays. Man-eating plants. Exploding dogs? Fake vaults? Boxing gloves? Pay walls?!? All this and more.

Of course, keeping this death trap running are your minions, who will run your lair to the best of their abilities. Yellow worker minions who carve out your rooms, red muscle minions who beat and/or shoot anyone you don't like, purple deception minions so you can better keep agents out of your lair, and green science minions who fix anything that breaks, and help you unlock bigger and better toys. And don't forget henchmen. Specialty characters who are (hopefully) tougher than your average minion, and come with unique and fun abilities (hopefully). Unfortunately, there's only 5 that you can keep under your employ at a time, and some are clearly and obviously better than others. Kind of disappointing considering some of the nonsense you have to do to recruit some of them. Consider using the sandbox mode to see what each one does. And of course, lowly worker or dignified henchman, if you're not liking their performance, you can personally kill them, removing the waste of space, and even providing a useful stat boost for anyone who witnessed it.

And of course, the Evil Geniuses themselves. There are a total of 4 in the base game, with a 5th one as DLC. There's a Billionaire weapons tycoon, a militant Stalin impersonator, a scientist with a weird helmet, a sinister former spymaster, and an eco-terrorist Ms. Freeze. While some are better gameplay wise (Maximillian's passive income and higher starting minion cap is great for beginners, while Red Ivan can just throw muscle minions at nearly every problem he faces), they all have their own unique abilities, campaign, look for their inner sanctum, and doomsday device, which each have unique effects when used on the world stage.

I would suggest seeing some actual gameplay footage for yourself before you buy the game, just to get a chance to see how the game plays. It's one of those "love it or hate it" kind of games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would say it's much better than the original, and I liked the original.

The Bad:
In my first playthrough, I came up against miss-able content (IE I missed it), despite trying very hard not to miss anything. It warns you a couple times that things are missable, but it doesn't provide all the possible warnings. Not terrible but frustrating. I will probably have to follow a guide if I want to 100% achievements. More annoyingly, the game is artificially inflated by making you beat it once for every main character, except Polar (who I chose first), so again I wasted time there. I don't think the individual stories will really pay-off compared to the time consumed by replaying from scratch so many times. (1 down...)

The Good:
This is probably one of the coolest base building, play-the-bad-guy games I've ever played. And unlike other games of the genre, the mechanics are both pretty straightforward, and the mechanics of the base defense are believable. Story wise, it's fun and silly. Cartoon villainy humor and as a rarity: the bad guy wins.

I would generally recommend this game, but I think you would be more interested in the mechanics of the game, than the laughably over the top voice acting or story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is decent enough. I didn't play the original, so I have no point of reference. This is a sort-of resource management colony-survival builder with an Austin Powers meets Two-Point Hospital vibe except without the camp of the former and without the refinement of the latter.

Your "Evil Genius" runs a lair full of minions (you don't directly control) that have needs met by various equipment and rooms you build on the various levels of and island you dig & build out. You research new & improved ways of doing this while training & specializing your minions into various roles (better researchers, guards, technicians, etc.). You eventually get henchmen you can directly control who help defend your lair & provide their own abilities to bolster your other minions in combat or around your base.

Your base has a casino - a cover operation - to distract & slow down the investigations, and you can specialize minions and activities to work the cover operation and reduce the effectiveness of agents that will eventually penetrate your lair. And you can make some money scamming tourists through this cover op, but ultimately your revenue is from criminal schemes out in "the world map."

You have a helipad to send minions out into various regions of the world and establish criminal networks to run schemes to keep the money coming in to pay for all of this. And those regions have their own "Justice" groups which, as you engage in schemes, will have heat levels slowly rising. The higher the heat, the more frequently they will send agents, investigators, and even combat troops to your lair to hassle you & disrupt your operations, and the higher your overall heat the tougher these opponents will be.

Managing this "criminal network, make money to build more & better traps and minions to defend against the inevitable increasingly tougher incursions to make more criminal networks and more money..." is the basic gameplay loop. And it works well enough. It's simple in design, basic in implementation, but the comic graphics & animations, nafarious and humorous context, and the nostalgia of old-school games that didn't take themselves too seriously help make this a fun, casual romp.

For the price, it's worth the entertainment budget cost to me, and I'm still playing. There a few different 'geniuses' to choose from, several island maps, and customizable difficulty. Main objective missions, side missions, various henchmen, plenty of DLC options. The audio's fine, voice-overs for the brief cutcenes involving the objectives are fine, plenty of UI options to customize & keys to rebind... It's all there.

And yet, there's room for so much more - it isn't one of those games that feels incomplete, but it does feel 'just barely' complete. Workshop & mod support would add a lot, if only for different art options with repetitive equipment, the ability to paint surfaces, rooms, floors, not to mention player-created islands & maps. Algorithms could be improved to prevent little annoyances like that one minion low on morale from distracting and escorting the same infiltrator out of the casino, then casually walking near them back in to your base only to 'rediscover' them and do it all over again in a broken loop until the morale breaks and they desert you...

The UI could provide much more information about most everything, daily (not weekly) salaries, so you don't spent too much before the day rolls over half your minions lose all morale from not getting paid. Selecting minions could show you their pathing lines and WHAT machine they're "on their way" to operate, not just that they're "operating a machine" halfway across the base. A simple report screen listing all your criminal networks and their statuses, schemes and heat - in text - instead of having to wiggle around the world map checking icons...

And on and on. A complete game, a decent game, and fun - but so much room for improvement. And the devs are still working on it, regular patches, updates, & just recently a new DLC with a new genius & island. For me well worth the entertainment dollar, but if you're concerned about reviews you may have read about the "illusion of difficulty" or "not much under the hood" then be aware those reviews have valid points. If you're looking for a serious simulation colony-building base-defense engine with a humorous overtone, this isn't it; but you want to relax and enjoy the evil genius idea without taking the game or yourself too seriously, this will do.\t\t\t\t\t
