\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell I like it, quite a lot. It took me a long time to decide to buy it, because I saw all the initial negative reviews that I was doubting the quality of the game. I saw there was a discount so I bought the game and after only 5-6 hours, I bought all DLCs except 1 or two. So no regrets, here's what I think:

It's fun organizing your evil lair, sending your minions on missions. The game-play is satisfying, especially the building aspect. Everything feels snappy and polished. Changing the layout of your base is easy and feels smooth. The dialog is funny and well done for most characters, but there's a character that I find very annoying and that's Atomic Olga. Which is supposed to be a eastern-European/Russian archetype but is clearly voiced over by a black woman with a very poor eastern-European accent and voice temper.

The biggest issues for me are that you cannot group select items and you cannot exclude certain type of minions in certain type of areas. I don't need like some people the ability to control individuals, but if I can forbid my guardsmen from going to the trap maze that would be great, or at least with a press of a button have everyone go back in the actual lair. Red alert kinda does that but it isn't perfect.

I saw a lot of complaints of this being money grabbing DLC trap. The only DLC that I consider important is the Oceans Campaign Pack (a new evil genius and her campaign, new island base, submarines system and a new tech tree, new items and so on). When I was a kid this was considered an expansion and no-one complained. All the others are not mandatory, they'd add a henchman or a minion type here or there, but you can skip them, they are purely cosmetic, unless you consider a henchman and robot minions game-play.

I've played both games, I remember liking the first one, but not much else. However I remember not being as glued to the game as I am to this one. So yeah if my opinion is to be taken into consideration, I recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fun, but it falls short on some things. The campaign writing is not nearly as good as the first one. The game has a lot of mundane clicking and the world map is a huge game of "where is waldo" trying to spot how to make money, how to lower heat, and how to do other stuff on each area. The first one had better world map and was way more intuitive.

Traps are kind of fun, but ultimately useless. First you don't need them and then when you think you would need them they are not powerful enough to do anything.

I think the henchmen and agent quest lines are unnecessarily long with 4 missions per destroying/recruiting with mundane feeling tasks.

What I do like is that the base building has improved a lot, the characters have more personality, and the game feels a lot more polished game play wise. I loved the first one, and this is an ok sequel/relaunch. Without the first one I am not sure if I would think this game is better or worse than I do now.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI believe this game had a fair share of criticism, but you know what - it took me 42 hours to complete a campaign and I did enjoy it pretty much. Now, I will bring down what the fans of the good ol' prequel should know.

Pros:
- Great graphics with a lot of attention to details
- More unique heroes and different Islands to choose from, meaning much more replayability comparing to the prequel
- DLCs not obligatory, but decently fun and worthy
- The atmosphere is all there, for sure. They increased the amount of soundwork (dialogues specifically) which was really fun to hear
- Minions are not going rogue for no reason every minute like the prequel
- AI overall is much better for the minions
- You don't need to bombard the world map with scientists to find a new maid at Antarctica
- Research tree is really good, you don't need to study a basic door for 500 times to invent a vending machine
- Interesting henchman hiring system

Cons:
- You cannot build anything outside the base, so the beach and the outside territory is useless
- AI is still not the best (minions are choosing some dumb A to B path to complete sometimes)
- You cannot have multiple side quests running at a time. It is horrible, honestly. I believe it could well save me around 8 hours if I could do some of them simultaneously
- Trap system is not really interesting to experiment with, just gives you new options to torture goodies
- Some in-game events and situations take too much real life time (global map heat removal, minions getting to some map ares etc.)
- Money display. I mean, a block of gold in prequel was worth 40k. Now it's 2k. Just looks kinda dumb when you have 5 huge piles of gold worth 10k. It would be much better to add a few 0's to the prices and incomes so it would look normal

People also mentioned strange bulding limitations like "no corners should be unaccesable" or "every piece of equip should be always accesed from all the sides, not partially". Honestly, after playing the game it made sence - agents can leave some undesirable stuff around or things can be caught on fire. Minions should have access to everything, so I find it plausible.

Overall, I am happy I finally had my hands on this long-awaited game. Wish the Rebellion best of luck and financing, so we can face the EG3 one day which will have all the perks from both games and something new on top.\t\t\t\t\t
