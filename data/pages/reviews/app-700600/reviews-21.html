\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvil Genius 2 is a game brimming with creativity and whimsy, where you're tasked with constructing a base filled with quirky, entertaining traps. While it offers a unique gameplay experience with wacky traps, I've found that it can be a bit challenging to lure enemies into triggering these traps effectively. Most enemy waves manage to sneak in, often fooling our security systems into mistaking them for our own henchmen. Additionally, there are moments when these foes disable our carefully planned traps with ease or just seem to spawn in middle of the base and ruin everything.

In my experience, relying on henchmen to deal with the intruders is often the most reliable strategy, especially when you've meticulously set up an intricate network of traps, only to see it foiled when henchmen inadvertently put themselves in harm's way. Unfortunately, this can lead to a situation where the traps become ineffective due to obstacles like dead bodies blocking their mechanisms.

While Evil Genius 2 is a game with immense potential and a wonderfully imaginative premise, it could truly shine with some refinements to address these issues. Despite these challenges, there's no denying the game's charm and unique appeal, making it worth a try for fans of base-building and strategy games but I cannot recommend it in the current state.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEver wanted to play as a scenery chewing villain? Maybe you know you could do better than every Bond villain when it comes to taking over the world? Or maybe you just really want an army of minions to go do evil but subtle things for you. If this sounds like fun then Evil Genius 2 is worth a go.
Cons include: A camera that can be a pain to move around. If you want to tilt your view or circle to see better, it can be difficult to get right. Moving items has to be done individually (at least I've never found a way to mass move things. But more experienced player may have solutions so check to be sure. It's not clear if there is a way to take a load of things from one place to another) which means any major changes to the lair layout as the game progresses can be a pain to sort. Messages appear thick and fast which can make keeping up with things hard. This isn't helped by messages which won't tell you where to find the problem. For example: A minion has turned against you. That's it. You won't get anything more. Which minion, where they are, etc. Only way I find out is when they set off the traps.
Pros include: There are some really inventive traps. Lasers, shark tanks, killer plants, my personal favourite is the Puppy of Doom. Intruders agree that it's an adorable greeting for any lair. (We think. They don't say much after the explosion.)
Got a minion that's turned against you? Have the rest hunt them down and you can either dispose of them or give them a time out in a holding cell to make them change their mind. Loyalty fix, guaranteed! Make your lair as weird as you like and take over the world one region at a time. Yes there are pesky spies but they're fun to play with.
The writing is funny and the cast are having a great time with the whole idea. There are a wide range of characters with suitably bonkers backstories and weaknesses. Defeating an enemy with a figure skating display? Yes, it's possible. The story is played for laughs and some of the missions are just fun ideas.
If you're looking for something replace Hitman or Golden eye 007, this isn't a game you'll enjoy. It's a resource management game with an increasing level of difficulty, a sense of humour and fun visuals. While the lack of guidance can be frustrating, it is refreshing to have your planning skills tested. I've had other games do that but Evil Genius 2 made me smile while doing it.
And dealing harshly with a minion for setting off the ice trap again is a good stress reliever.\t\t\t\t\t

It is not an RTS and enemies are not very chalenging (at least at easy setting) so expect more of a base building game than anything else. I installed and played the game for 10 hours straight, no crashes, and the only place where i struggle was with the number of Generators needed to power my entire base.

It is a relaxing game more than anything and i enjoyed it for what it is. If you enjoyed building your Dungeon and your Hospital in the games (Keeper and Tycoon) you will probably like it and enjoy building it exactly as you want.

Personaly i think 2 things are lacking;

1- A first person view attached to your main Vilain character and the possibility to use your abilities like you could in Dungeon Keeper through possession spell (if you remember that funny ability!)

2- A more elaborated trap system where you could make the enemies go through a series of tunnel that would lead to a central place (it could be lave pit, hidden sharks lake, hidden secret monster lair, what else...) After all, every villain should have a fetish creature it feeds off from victims, right? lol

This game have TONS of potential to be super fun. Sadly, it is like the game was not pushed with much conviction and passion... it feels the devs played it safe. I seriously believe they should get back to it and push it further! it could become a super hit!

With all DLC's, on sale under 30$, that is worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cried out for a sequel to the original game for years. I thought we would never see anything like it again. And honestly, we haven't. For all its flaws, the original game is better.

- Item hotspots (i.e. places where they're 'used from') are always required to be clear, and never optional. This breaks from established 2004 gameplay when it comes to placing bunk beds in the earliest barracks - both sides of the bed must now be clear, so each end of the room has to be an extra tile long - and so on for other objects, a lot of which take up an obnoxious amount of space. Some objects have a vast number of hotspots I've never seen in use, which makes me wonder why they are there.

- For whatever ungodly reason, you cannot build security cameras in rooms that are not internal corridors, so if you want a live circuit prison, you have to build a corridor through the center of it which looks awful. Additionally, instead of building loudspeakers to alert all minions to a security intrusion, only military minions sitting at guard tables will respond to security alerts, which is wonderful when they're all away from the table because they were responding to an alert in another part of the base.

- Speaking of security cameras, all cameras are now on one network instead of having multiple assigned desks and networks with dedicated security responses. Also, if one desk supports three cameras, and you have three desks, and nine cameras set up, and one minion manning a desk gets up to attend to their morale or whatever, instead of losing three cameras, you lose all nine cameras until that desk is manned again.

- If some of your stuff gets destroyed or stolen in an invasion, it's not getting built again by default unless you remembered to switch on auto-replace. Cut to your vault, stacked with hundreds of safe deposit boxes, each of which has to be individually clicked and enabled for auto-replace... This should have been a defaulted opt-out only setting.

- You can't strap enemy agents to the laboratory laser, impact stress analyser, centrifuge, or in fact any non-standard object that isn't a dedicated corridor trap. (Oh, and once again, traps can only be built in corridors.) What the hell.

- There's a DLC object that gives your henchmen a new outfit and aesthetic. Except it only works on specific henchmen with no clear reasoning as to why / which ones won't have an outfit change.

- Please, please, please let us turn off I.R.I.S. base alerts separate to the rest of the vocal audio. They're constant, they're loud, they're sometimes completely inaccurate, and they interrupt whatever's happening with some inane comment in a vaguely racially insensitive accent. Evil Genius 1 had Shen Yu's white-man-performing-insulting-Chinese-stereotype-voice, and I really thought nearly 20 years passing would have removed this nonsense, but apparently we're stuck with it.

All these points are secondary to the one biggest pressing problem which is that the game simply isn't fun to play. The game is constantly pressuring you to run schemes in the world map (IRIS will yell at you constantly if you're not) and then actively punishing you for doing so, with an incoherent super agent alertness system that crushes you instantly if you so much as blink without a license. Everything is constantly deteriorating - both minion morale and object durability - and it's worse if you play with the global warming genius because instead of simply providing stat boosts in rooms, air conditioning and heating now has an active temperature effect which has to be managed on top of everything else, and can't be turned off in the custom game settings.

I've tried to play this multiple times (8+ save files) and it just gets tedious in no time at all. I don't see myself ever finishing it. I'm glad we got a sequel to an underrated classic. I'm sad that what we got was this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLook, I played 100 hours before putting the game down so I had fun.

The premise is the same as the original game, but they did change a number of things. Some I think for the better, some mechanics I miss.

  • I like the new distract/capture/kill zones
  • I like the different maps alongside different evil geniuses
  • I like how there is less maintenance of getting income, because you can now get it through passive network setup
  • I notice that enemies still work largely the same way to the first game, in that they will go through the first high security door that isn't a hotel door that they find, allowing you to build winding corridors of traps that lead nowhere (insert maniacal evil laughter here)
  • Except that they don't pop through walls and stuff like that which happened in the first game, which makes them easier to manage

  • You can't prevent minions going through certain doors, leading them to stand around in areas they shouldn't be in
  • You can't place cameras in anything other than corridors
  • The damned overhead minion status icons, make me not want to use loot items at all... just let me turn them off!
  • I miss just passive impact from boss and loot items
  • The cover operation is basically useless, but I thought that of original evil genius too
  • Trying to manage heat is pointless unlike in the first one, let them come and die in my corridors of death
  • I wish there was an easier way to prioritise tasks, rather than individually on minions that will probably die soon
