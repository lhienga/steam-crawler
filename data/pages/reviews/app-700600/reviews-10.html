The Goods:
Thematic: Great!
Art Style: Great!
Goofy Voice Acting: Great!
Minions being Minions: Great!
Managing the heat footprint: Phenominal!
Saboteurs breaking in to snatch your gold: Great!

The Bads:
Tutorial: Aweful. Felt like stepping into a dark room and bumping into things as an NPC says. "Open your eyes to see incoming walls." Draw arrows to where you want stuff instead of putting blocks that look like walls there.
After finishing or starting a new playthrough, the tutorial starts over due to the player missing a tiny check box at the bottom to turn it off. (likely why you see so much nerd rage in the reviews)

Mechanics: The rooms are janky. Some buildings snap to the walls, others don't. Attack orders from your character work, sometimes. Canceling dig orders? Nope, everything needs to be in 2s.

Conclusion: If you can ignore/get through the tutorial and the mechanical jank, it would be a 10/10 game. Great design, thematic and progression. I wish more games had this scope.

Note to the Devs: You've made a great game! Make short Youtube tutorials explaining the mechanics of the game like temperature management, morale, vitality, Smarts. Name things like the world stage as "World Stage" with the correct icon so people can cookie crumb their way to solving the problem intuitively instead of bumbling around in the dark.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who played the first game as a child/teenager, this one falls short in almost every aspect, even graphics.

The graphics, while updated feel more generic and not as stylized as the original. Animations seem a bit smoother, but it just seems like its almost a parody of the first one, not its successor.

The gameplay takes the biggest hit from the original. The menus and interface are not great. The original seems to have a better layout and function over this new one. As others have stated, the tutorial seems overbearing and takes forever. There's also a bunch of completely useless items and menus and info that we could do without. For example, there's a tab of decoration items for each room. All they do is take up space, they have no other effect on the game and don't even affect minion morale, they are there just to add fluff. There's minion traits, which seem useless as well and its just random info that isn't needed for the game play. There's more bosses to play as, however, they took away Alexis who was my favorite from the original.

Music seems to be pretty on point, actually good sound track. The voice acting is absolutely horrible and if I am honest, sounds extremely amateurish, like this was these people's first voice acting gig and it was recorded on a cell phone in a closet.

All in all, we wanted more minion types, more evil geniuses, more locations for secret bases and maybe different ways of getting money, infamy. What we ended up getting was some useless decoration items, generic forgettable geniuses and leaving out 2/3 of the ones we got attached to in the first game, and a more clunky menu and an overbearing slow tutorial. Everything we didn't want and not a whole lot of what would have made this game better.\t\t\t\t\t
