\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I was a kid, my brother and I played Evil Genius and I had a ton of fun watching. Now I'm an adult and have a game to share with my niece or nephew once they can say something other than googoogaga. So, some nostalgia for me when playing this.

The main story is short and relies on side stories and progress gates to lengthen it, and since it's so side story reliant, once you've played through once you've already experienced most of the game and have nothing left to surprise you. This kinda sucks, but not the worst thing I suppose.

I wish the gameplay was better and less repetitive, every side story is a combination of "do this on the world stage, research this, kill these guys in your lair, interrogate this dude". But where the gameplay is repetitive, the writing and artstyle are so charming that I think it makes up for it, and the voice acting is really good imo that it stays fun and charming.

Some parts are really unintuitive, like the heat system. It took me too long to realize the best way to manage heat is to just destroy criminal networks when I don't have use for them, and instead camp one continent for schemes. Another complaint I have is that I can't target squads of agents like in the first game, and instead have to mark them one by one which kinda sucks.

Overall though I think this is a great game if a bit repetitive, with low replayability value. I would love to see some sort of branching story system, like way less henchmen and loot schemes so that I can't just experience almost all of them in a single playthrough and instead have to have a unique lair "build" each save, would help with the replayability value for sure\t\t\t\t\t
