\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE: In addition to my original comments below, this game is robust, technically stable (no blips, crashes, artifacts, etc.) and is extremelywell-constructed and thought through. This game stands on its own merit and shouldn't be compared to other games. There's a lot of missions, side quests and a decent main story thread. The start of the campaign gets you started quickly in what you need to do and discovering what needs to be done next is most intuitive.

Many of the negative comments here are from players who appear to expect the next evolution of the orginal game and that's to be expected when you name a game "2." The developer/publisher appears to have erred by naming it a sequel to the first game. They presumably thought they could capture the original buyers and hopefully attract an additional new following but that tactic appears to have backfired.

If you aren't "tainted" by the expectation of a sequel then this game stands alone on its own merits. As a kid I often drew volcano-based lairs in my schoolbooks as a past time, perhaps inspired by early James Bond movies. EG 2 is exctaly that in spades. It is well-thought-out, graphically pleasing, crammed with all kinds of missions and things to do, thoughtfully designed for things one would expect to be able to do like expanding a room that's already been created (although placing security cameras was exhaustively unintuitive).

It's a fun game filled with parody, good natured humour and classic stereotypes. The game is worthy of my valuable game-playing time. It's not in the league of AAA titles but it's certainly worth perhaps $30 or less.\t\t\t\t\t
