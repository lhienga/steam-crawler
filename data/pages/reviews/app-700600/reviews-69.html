\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great attempt of continuing the legacy of the Original Evil Genius game and bringing the evil lair building into the modern era of video gaming. Sadly, annoying bugs that break the various missions hold it back from being amazing.

Evil Genius (original game) started the franchise off strong, but had a very linear progression for the villain scheme and was unforgiving if you laid out your lair poorly. If you dug out earth, there was no reversing it. Evil Genius 2 adds a lot of variety to the original formula with multiple levels for your lair and the ability to fill dirt back in. The map and the cover hotel feel a little less interesting than it did in the original game, but the dialog and cutscenes help offset it a little bit.

The game looks amazing with 300 minions running around my lair performing their jobs. No slowdown or stutter on ultra settings for a Geforce 2060RTX. Sound is good, with Lalo Schifrin-like music playing in the background and the minions make various noises in the lair. The inner circle members and the villains are well voiced though some of the idle lines are limited and get repetitive after ten hours of play.

Bugs amounted to missions not becoming available, or certain sub objectives not appearing in the cover hotel or map or couldn't be built. Generally, saving my game, exiting, and then launching Evil Genius 2 fixed the majority of bugs I encountered. Considering that they have moved to a console release, I'm worried that these bugs would appear in the console version as well.

I recommend this game cautiously. Buy at full price with season pass? Only if you are desperate to build your villain lair. Buy on sale with Season pass? YES! The idea is sound and Rebellion just needs to work out the kinks for it to be a solid game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvil Genius 2 is a colossal disappointment, a sequel that squanders the potential of its predecessor and delivers a lackluster experience that falls flat on every front. From its tedious gameplay to its lack of innovation, this game fails to capture the charm and excitement that made the original Evil Genius so beloved.

First and foremost, the gameplay mechanics are mind-numbingly repetitive and devoid of depth. The game quickly devolves into a monotonous cycle of mundane tasks, with little variation or strategic decision-making. The lack of meaningful choices or consequences makes each action feel inconsequential, robbing the game of any sense of excitement or challenge.

The AI of the game is abysmal, with minions exhibiting baffling behavior and often getting stuck in illogical patterns. This not only breaks immersion but also leads to frustrating moments of micromanagement, as you constantly have to correct their senseless actions. It's evident that the developers prioritized style over substance, resulting in an experience that feels half-baked and unfinished.

Furthermore, the visual and audio design of Evil Genius 2 are underwhelming. The graphics are outdated and lack detail, failing to create a visually captivating world. The sound effects and music are forgettable, doing little to enhance the atmosphere or immerse players in the game. It feels like a missed opportunity to create a truly immersive and engaging experience.

The lack of innovation in Evil Genius 2 is perhaps its most glaring flaw. Rather than building upon the strengths of the original game and introducing new and exciting features, the sequel feels like a watered-down rehash of its predecessor. It fails to deliver any meaningful advancements or innovations that would justify its existence as a standalone title. It's a missed opportunity to take the franchise in bold new directions and provide players with a fresh and engaging experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Evil Genius franchise is very dear to me, and I have played Evil Genius (1) many many times. With that being said...

1. The good

A lot of love went into this game, so thank you Rebellion! The visuals are amazing, the music is totally James Bond, the gameplay is solid.

2. The bad-ish

Some of the side missions feel too long and/or slow moving.
At one point, once you have a solid lair, everything runs on autopilot, so the challenge disappears. The game becomes mostly timing missions and waiting for your minions to replenish/train.

3. Suggestions

All loot should have some positive effect, otherwise there is no incentive to spend the time and resources to acquire it.
The evil genius should be able to assist "interrogations" and gloat. Gloating should increase the morale of the minions near by.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game, a lot.
I loved the first game and I was hoping that it would be the first game but with more options for controlling base building and nicer graphics. The game is not as straight forward as it used to be. There are tons of info screens that just clutter up the whole experience. In the first game it told me what I needed and just let me get on with it, now I spend half the time digging around menus. The graphics and sound effects are wonderful--but the overall experience feels more like a chore than a game. The first game is buggy but is fun, the second game is sleeker but overwrought. If you don't mind tons of information thrown at you and you enjoyed the first game, this game might be for you. Otherwise skip and mourn the loss of great potential.
