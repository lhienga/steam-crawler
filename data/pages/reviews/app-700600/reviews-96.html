\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a huge fan of the original Evil Genius, I REALLY wanted to love this game. Unfortunately, it's just mediocre at best. It has gotten better with some bug fixes that should have really been fixed prior to launch, but there are definitely still issues and Rebellion is too busy cranking out mostly useless DLC to listen to the community and fix them.

The game's theme and art direction are great. They've really improved the base-building quality of life considerably. The problem is that it's just artificially padded to the point where it just gets boring. I have this huge base, but most of my equipment just sits idle because the minion cap is so low. Traps are only reliable in a few select combos, which forces you to choose between reusing the same trap combo over and over to be efficient or having a little fun setting up your own wacky combos only to watch them get disabled one by one.

I could almost forgive the rest of the Evil Genius 2's flaws and still have said it was worth a play-through if the game wasn't so packed with artificially long side quests that can only be completed ONE AT A TIME. Either you rush through the game ignoring the side quests or you postpone the main story so long that you forget what you were doing to actually take over the world 40 hours of uncompelling side quests later. You still may miss some side quests due to bugs or the game not warning you that completing certain chapters will clear away half your side quests. The best side quests are the ones to defeat the super agents, but I didn't even get to finish all those because the last Blue Saint one just never showed up for some reason.

I'd love to come back fresh in a year or so and finish the other campaigns if they ever fix what needs to be fixed, but as it stands I regret sinking as much time as I have into EG2 hoping it would get better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe world map and enemies completely destroy the game.

1. Enemies are a joke and can't come close to defeat basic defenses such as a single camera at the entrance with a bunch of minions. They can't make a dent into anything so agents don't matter.

2. Heat doesn't matter at all. All it does is lock you out of doing schemes. Agents can't do anything so them spawning is useless.

3. The cover operation is worthless. Tourists can't do anything but stand around in the cover operation. Unlike the first game, tourists leaving panicked or dead won't produce heat in EG2. In fact, tourists can't even die nor can they walk into the base if you have a door. So they don't matter. Even if they did, heat relies on the agents being worth something

4. On the hardest difficulty, you can keep the game at 2x speed. Whenever a super agent comes, send the henchman and evil genius to kill them.

5. The base gets way too big way too fast. There is no strategy as to what you need to place. You just make giant rooms and fill it. It feels like cheating when you build 40 lockers and now have 9000 minions and laugh as the Forces of Justice send 4 single target soldiers who take two shots to kill a minion. They can't when based on numbers alone.

There is nothing to do in the game besides build a base with the limited about of funds the game decides to hand to you through schemes (and then take away from through payday). There is no challenge. The waves of agents cannot even overcome basic defense. Compared to the first game, this is extremely disappointing.\t\t\t\t\t
