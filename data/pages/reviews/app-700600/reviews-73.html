\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe big problem with Evil Genius 2 is that the tutorial isn't as comprehensive as it should be. There are a lot of things that aren't communicated very well that make the game much easier and enjoyable, like how firing the doomsday weapon reduces heat in the territory it hits and (eventually) spawns a mission that removes heat on that continent, which makes managing heat much easier in the late game. I think a lot of the negative reviews are from players who, through no fault of their own, just didn't know there were solutions to the problems they had.

Also destroyed items aren't automatically rebuilt so it's easy to have small but important items like security cameras and fire extinguishers get destroyed by some aoe attack or fire, and you don't realise there is a problem until after a horde of agents have killed half your minions while the guards stayed in the armoury playing cards.

Other than that the game is great, but the minion cap really needs to be raised up from 300. I was struggling to find a good reason to expand to the third floor once I hit the cap and had built everything I needed to breeze through the rest of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe graphics alone caught my attention. Sims like, clean, round edged and smooth cartoony looking.
I have read about the reviews and got the impression the game has a rough launch.

In my opinion, this is very fun.
I picked Maximilion, the villain from the first game as my first run. I thought its a good 9 hours game for one run. But after 18 hours of gameplay, I still havent complete the main storyline yet. I only managed to unlock 70% of the research, and there are still tooooo much side stories (the side quests) I havent gotten to. There is a lot of side stories, Crime Lords, Super Agents, unlocks and special loots. And at current stage I only defeated 2 super agents, out of probably 5 and met 2 crime lords out of 9 (probably).

In comparison to EG 1. I actually prefer this one. Its more playable in my opinion.

But I do have some suggestion.
1. The capture / eliminate can be automated by tagging later based on zone. Which save a lot of repetition because your base is constantly being invade. But can the interrogation be automated as well? Like a auto-timer option. When resolved reaches 0, do this on Convert or Interrogate based on Agent class.
2. Can we have the enemy listening device being auto -sweeped as well? They tend to become over whelming.
3. Can the enemy tag, capture/kill be given hotkeys?

Of course, if there is a reason the game is designed not to have these functions because of a certain experience, I am cool with it.\t\t\t\t\t
