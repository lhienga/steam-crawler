\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished my first world takeover, and I quite enjoyed myself. The game has it's flaws and it's rough edges and areas where I feel they could have done more, but I feel it was also a fun experience and did do some things better then the first game and I fully intend to reinstall the game another day to tackle one of the remaining four genius's.

Flaws:
* The whole temperature mechanic was badly done, and there should have been a toggle option to turn it off without needing to also sacrifice the rest of the Ocean DLC.
* The henchman limit should have been higher, only being able to fill half my conference table when there were so many cool options was a bit of a let-down.
* The recruitable super-agents were somewhat disappointing. Particularly the fact that they had to lose a potential cool ability in order to have a stat recovery one due to not being able to sit at the table.
* Not having any fun bonus interrogation devices (like the mixer or the shelves in the first game). Was quite excited to get the R.A.Y.G.U.N. since it was at least something new and also sort of a classic spy genre type thing.
* I really wished there were separate red alerts for the lair and the casino. Really annoying to drive off the tourists and alert the agents being distracted out in the casino just because Agent X popped up in the middle of the lair.

Things Done Better:
* I liked how much more interaction you had with both the world stage and with objectives, especially the ones that required multiple schemes to build up to a goal.
* The cover operation was MUCH better, actually felt like it had a purpose rather then just existing and it was great fun seeing agents come sneaking in, only to get drunk, lose some money on the games and then sneak out again with nothing.
* The build-up of the Doomsday Device through the three levels of the cavern over the course of the game and also each genius having their own unique device.
* All the conversations between the Genius and minions, henchmen and super-agents. Felt like it really brought more to the story getting all those deeper looks into the characters.
* Incinerators were SO much better then freezers for dealing with body-bags.
* The multi-floor lair was such a fun idea, Loved feeling like I actually had room to be creative about how I built without worrying about not having enough space for everything I would need.

Could Have Done More
* It felt like they missed a ton of opportunities with loot, were quite a few schemes where I was asking myself why I couldn't get a cool loot item for the lair instead of just more gold for the vault. Would also have been nice to see trophy type loot from the henchmen/super-agents we killed.
* Would have been nice to be able to have a 'Casino Guard Post' or something in order to keep some muscle closer to where chaos might arise (especially on Caine Key). Just makes sense that a remote and fancy resort casino would have security on the floor.
* A return of the disguised items from the first game would have been cool. Hidden doors to make it easier for minions to slip out and harder for agents to slip in, hidden cameras that could be placed in the casino or other less suspicion generating areas, that sort of thing.
* Modding. Would have been amazing to see what new genius's, henchmen, loot and other things the community might have come up with to make the game even more fun.

Overall though, a fun game that I will certainly return to for another campaign in the future. Already have ideas about how I might build differently to plug gaps in my defenses I discovered during my first campaign.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not a terrible experience, but ultimately this game never fully channeled the charm of its predecessor nor did it introduce any particularly impressive new mechanics.

The best improvement on Evil Genius 1, outside of fewer bugs, is the addition of multiple visually impressive maps. The superweapon chamber is vertically integrated into the 5 floors of your base and gets built up throughout the storyline, which is cool. Also, the islands look and feel more alive than the original. Unfortunately, the game doesn't have enough depth for you to ever need to use close to your full base (also, it's way too hard to properly place staircases). With a halfway decent economy, you can manage investigators with a mob of social minions and tank the casualties randomly rampaging soldiers will cause to your front hallway. No agent will ever threaten your genius, unless you leave him in the cafeteria. The game should have had more creative and subtle ways for agents to interact with your base, like for example an air duct system where high-level enemies could slip in and out of your grasp.

The writing isn't ever quite as funny as it thinks it is. It's not 100% unbearable, but there's a certain saccharine quality that I can only describe as "millennial." The dopey, cheerful way your teenagey, multiethnic minions address you gets grating fast. As another reviewer mentioned, EG1's radio broadcasts were both funny and showed your notoriety growing across the globe, but this game lacks the style and villainous grandeur of the original. I kind of see why people compare it to the Sims, because between the cutscenes, writing and model design EG2 just feels cartoony. The story is really long but there's no particular developments between your genius and any other characters, just more random obstacles placed in your way.

Your evil criminal and military organization is also apparently subject to 21st-century hiring laws, because 50% of your guards will spawn as female, but cartoonishly bloated-up to evenly match the size of your male mercs. I found this humorous at first but eventually just disturbing to look at.

Traps and research are less glitchy but also less charming. Most traps aren't useful. Guards can't be told what weapons to use. Really annoyingly, the electroshock weapons are useless-- you can capture agents, but can't mind-wipe them to return them to the casino, so it's either killing or brainwashing-- which means missing agents, which means more heat. You can no longer use research items as hilarious vehicles of torture either.

Over all, this game just needed more creativity. Sad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLots of wasted potential. By the time you've played through the firs half of the tutorial you realise how badly you've built your base, because you have to follow the prompts and the base ends up laid out badly. Then the investigators never stop coming, aren't put off by anything in the lobby (the gambling front) which is fine, but they put on disguises, walk straight ... whatever, I've put enough time into this than it deserves. Nowhere near enough murder, traps or world domination, far too much "The Sims"-ing your workforce and filling half your mountain lair with power generators.\t\t\t\t\t
