\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, this is a sad review for me. Namely because I so enjoy the style of games like Evil Genius and Dungeon Keeper. It's fun to be part of this stylish, silly, and enjoyable world.

However, the fact that you can run into game breaking bugs over 8 months after launch is inexcusable. So, I've run into this three time across that many play thrus. At some point, usually with a side mission, the game becomes stuck. My minions are unable to deploy to complete whatever mission I need for the side quest. Done, no movement, stuck at the chopper. And since this game has the annoying quirk of not letting you drop side quests, you're stuck. No more loot. No more henchman. Just the main story, forget all about the other wonderful world building and lore the side quests provide.

This last one broke me. A little under 20 hours of game time. Recruited four of the 5 henchman I needed. Each selected based on my personal choice to better forward my organizations schemes. Go to recruit the 5th and finish the inner circle. Nope, game bugged. Now you're stuck without your fifth henchman. And yes, i could finish the play through, but that empty seat will mock me in the darkest hours of the night with 'what could have been'.

What's the use of taking that photo standing over the bodies of all the Super Agents when you know Clara Jones should be standing between Barracuda and Espectro?

Just sucks that a simple and easy bug fix, IN A SINGLE PLAYER GAME, is still beyond the devs abilities almost a year after launch.

Love the game, hate the execution issues.\t\t\t\t\t
