\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKinda hard to like this game.
It lacks basic options like any dungeon game out there.

EDIT: After some playing, kinda very disappointed about the "WORLD DOMINATION" part. Whole gameplay is within your base, you never leave it. The whole conquering is passive, on a picture, paper. You open map, click map .. done. Thats pretty sad. Its brutally simplified, it kinda fits my first impression about UI, setting, options below.

Camera inputs has lags, like move or rotate, feels like its reacting 0.5s later after your input. When you stop doing the movement, there is like "slide movement" still occuring. You stop moving the camera and it still continues to move for 1-2 seconds.
Placing something is nightmare. The axis of your mouse are not aligned properly. I noticed when I want to place something like "here" .. I need to move mouse over 1.5 squares away from the place I want them to actually place it.
Rooms and items has zero explanation. Games 25 years ago told you the minimum size of the room you need for it to work, this game wont tell you nothing. You must experiment, make a room, too small, so expand and go back again.
Editing rooms is nightmare. Wont allow you to place doors if there is no wall, so you go back to building, destruction, back and forth. Its planning and it wont allow you to plan.

Game has blurred top side of the view, cannot be turned off. Makes no sense to have this feature and not allow us to disable it.
Many more useless and annoying features in the game that cannot be disabled.

There is absolutely zero details for demolition and construction. Games 25 years ago had better details. Here you just delete and spawn everything with ugly sound. Kinda sad.

There is absolutely zero quick opening room info, additional buildings etc. You must go back to building menu, select room etc. I mean, games 25 years ago could be just "click the room and see EVERYTHING". Here? Absolutely nothing.

There is no auto placement, auto rotation for decoration. Some things can be placed only next to a wall but you need to manually rotate it. Makes no sense. 90% of the time it wants to place everything on second floor. Is there even a second floor? Just started playing and just playing the tutorial so makes zero sense that everything goes to second floor.

Why cant I move the camera with mouse on World Map? Makes no sense.

Can open World Map and then open building menu without closing WM, but cannot open building menu and then WM without closing building menu? Makes no sense.

Games has "click to start" feature, I hate it. Makes no sense to have this feature in the game, EVER! Just load the menu like any normal sane person.

So weird when one developer is trying to copy another developers game, but refuses to use intelligent and good features of that game. Makes no sense.

After a while it gets super annoying trying to find quests on the map. Map is super bad, hard to tell what is what. Quests could have say at least a continent its happening on.

But I give this game a credit for full dabing. There are way much bigger games who has way too little dabing, just plain text with "ugh" and "huh" sounds.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's bad.
The UI is one of the worst I've ever seen in a game like this. Notifications are unhelpful (like "there is a fire", but you can't go there by clicking on the notification. Dungeon Keeper 2 did stuff like this better and that game is 25 years old now). Traps are useless for the most part because most of the special agents will just disable them or teleport straight into your vault (doors are even more useless). You have to mark enemies before your minions start engaging them, and for that you have to find them first. It's a constant needle in a haystack game. Late game you basically get invaded every few minutes by the same enemies. Like seriously, these "special" agents are so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ annoying. You defeat them, they vanish instead of dying, and come back a few minutes later. How is this fun? Who designed this garbage?
Base-building kinda sucks too because you need research to unlock more areas first, research is painfully slow.
Didn't even finish my first playthrough because lategame is so damn tedious and annoying in every single aspect. Early game is just ok, you still see the flaws (like terrible UI and navigation), but the endgame is where it becomes an utter ♥♥♥♥ show. Don't waste your time on this trash. Bought the Deluxe Edition for 3 bucks and I still regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike many reviewers, I avoided this one after seeing all the negative player reviews. I also played the first one maybe 10+ years ago. I saw EG2 was $2.99 for the deluxe edition, so I tried it. Needless to say, it must have had a bad release, because I played it for over 5 hours and was completely engaged in it. I didn't experience any bugs at all and had a lot of fun.
----
Some may not like the campy 60's theme, but it reminds me of the old Get Smart, Minions and Austin Powers shows that spoof off James Bond. It's like a mix of Dungeon Master with city/base building elements. There are several mechanics of play, beautiful graphics, tech trees (science) and minions with upgrades. It has a slow burn, where it takes time go get some projects and activities to complete, but I am used to that from city builders like Tropico.
----
I know I got a lot of entertainment for $3. I still haven't looked at the season pass extras, but I saw an overview and they range from useful to cosmetic. If you are still on the fence, watch someone play it on YT, even though most of them are from 2022, and see if it is for you. Overall, if you like the genre and have patience, you should give this one a try.\t\t\t\t\t
