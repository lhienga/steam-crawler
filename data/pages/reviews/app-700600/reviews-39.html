Minions not returning from missions make it so improving their stats is not really worth the effort, the tech tree can be completed easily way before even the middle of the game and doing side missions is a pain in the ass. They are basically always the same "research something", "do something in the world map", "kill something in your base" and get really tiring for what little benefits they provide.

Also, control room stuff, it can be completely empty (with no workers in the stations) and you still get the benefits of the stations, so its like it doesnt matter.
I feel like with some minor changes it could become great, but it is the game we got :)
Ambiance and music are great, but they cannot carry the whole game on their backs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Note that I've never played the first evil genius, so that isn't influencing my takes here)

The game has a fun premise, and a somewhat charming ascetic. It walks this fine line between goofy and serious, which sometimes works and sometimes feels a bit cringe. The game is larger than I anticipated, with lots of side missions and multiple campaigns. I want to do all of them but some got very tedious and I eventually resorted to just prioritizing the main objectives and finished up the campaign as Max I was playing.

The first night I was playing this I was hooked and stayed up kinda stupid late. It's fun enough as you are introduced to new features and its fun to curve out rooms and reiterate on the layout with newly researched items... but over time as it all dragged on and I've realized how shallow most of the game mechanics are. (Actually, I'm not sure how much of it is shallow, or how much is poorly explained and then assumed to be shallow?) Seems like most consequences don't matter and there's no real challenge. My money is sitting at maximum all the time with little reason to build a bigger vault, and occasionally a super agent comes in and kills half my guys, but they are all replaceable anyway so its just an annoyance. There doesn't seem to be a fail condition at all, actually. Nothing seems to be actual threat and so its hard to feel like anything matters. I'm just checking boxes and waiting for quest objectives to complete, basically.

That said, despite it being shallow... it feels like it just almost isn't. It feels like its close to something good, but its not quite there. It feels unfinished, and its a shame because it might have been great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI honestly don't understand "mixed" reviews this game received. It's not a huge AAA title and there are some things I would change, but in overall it gave me 25 hours of fun, out of which maybe 2-3 were a grind or waiting, the rest was engaging and interesting. I didn't play 1st version, so maybe there was something not re-made properly, but it doesn't impact the fun of 2nd one. I am considering replay with other evil genius later, we'll see.

As for gameplay - it's well made, with nice looking post-James-Bond graphics and corresponding music. Most of mechanics behind it is balanced and keeps pushing us to conquer another obstacle. Story is nice, something You would expect from evil geniuses to do. Side stories lacking a bit, but they are fun too. Resources bottlenecks are a bit annoying, especially Intel, and those are the main reason of waiting time. Grand finale keeps up the pace and gives a feeling of rush, again lack of specific minions or Intel slows it down a bit. Generally mechanics is 90% good and 10% a bit annoying.

All things considered I do not regret buying this game and spending the time with it - and to be honest I was strongly considering not buying it because of mixed reviews. Now I know it would be a mistake.\t\t\t\t\t
