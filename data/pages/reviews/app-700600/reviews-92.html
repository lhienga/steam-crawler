\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not a terrible game (I've realized about a dozen of my reviews start with this phrase btw), but it's boring. There just isn't anything to do.

I'm some nine hours into the game, not sure how far I am from the ultimate goal, but I have to say I've spent most of the game waiting. Waiting for minions to be trained, waiting for research to be finished. There's almost nothing to manage, because the game plays itself. My vault is overflowing with money, investigators do no harm. Game constantly reminds me I have idle criminal networks, which is true, because there's nothing to do with them. I can reduce "heat" (danger level) in countries, which is low everywhere and I can steal money which I have excess of.

So I'm waiting for something I need for main objective mission. Right at the moment I've got an objective to dig my way to the bottom of the doombase, which seems like it will require another endless research and I've just quit the game and am debating if I want to ever start it again.

Bottom line - great theme, tedious, boring. Buy the original, it's almost the same, cheaper and (a bit) better balanced.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Warning Spoilers Ahead!!!!!! * I really love the evil genius games , they are fun to play and not too heavy. A few thing though with Evil Genius 2. Firstly, continuity, when you play some later missions some henchmen are mentioned as enemies that you already have on your team, also the henchmen's henchmen (ahem) attack you at the end even if you have beaten them. This is a pretty silly oversight and could be easily rectified. Secondly, there is no leveling up of traps which is a huge disappointment as later in the game they pretty much become obsolete. I get that the game is supposed to be harder as it progresses but perhaps a new, very expensive tree of research could be added in the later game so that we can enjoy watching the annoying investigators be eaten and burned and punched etc. Thirdly, is the absence of multi-place, turn on/off hotkeys, placing lots of the same item make the gameplay repetitive and detracts from the enjoyment of taking over the world. The last gripe I have (except for the bee guy just being a tourist, come on lazy!) is that the amount of gold that you could accumulate would, potentially, be awesome but the time it would take to plant all those safes is just so tedious that it sucks the fun out of the game. I do love the game, like I said which is why I am putting this up as constructive criticism, not a diss, about the game. I would recommend it to all, even with the stuff that I have mentioned. Now I am off to continue my world domination MWAH HA HA HA!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis for me is a 6 out of 10

where 1 is "this wasted my life - oh god why did they make this disaster"
Where 5 is "i enjoyed some stuff and hated some stuff but i dont feel robed"
and a 10 is "my wife left me. I can't say when nor do i remeber if we had any kids"

The good:

I liked the obvious love they put in the art. The design is nice and clean. The Music could be in the next James Bond movie.

Compared to EG1 it looks better, flows smoother and in the early game it feels like an overall improvement.

The bad:

But then i wondered why they implemented a system, where you generate a passive income, wich in return generates passiv heat, that demands you to send out some vallets to counter, so you can build or rearange the base, or just simply keep your passiv income.

It does not feel like i am a evil Genius, more a fire fighter in a very slow whack a mole game.
I kind of expected that i will send out my minions to rob banks and wreck havock to finance my evil deeds. That the forces of justice will react to that and invade my base in various ways.

There is some of that in the side and story missions but you mostly get your income passively.

There are 3-4 floors on each island. I planed the base in my head using all the floors, where to put the rooms, the stairs and the traps but soon realized that doing that would make my base ineficcent, the distance my minions had to walk to long, taking them forever to do anything. So there is no real point in using up all the space and floors.

And i felt like i waited more then i played the game.
Waiting for the minions to run to the landing pad, waiting for the mission to end, waiting for a justice forces attack, waiting for a reasearch projekt to finish so i can wait some more to wait some more.

In my playtrough nothing ever entered my base and escaped with any "suspision" allive and i didn't even put any effort in it or used any of the traps. I could have used the traps but they demand repairs wich raises the amount of technicians you need with every trap you place.

This game could use some challanges, like not beeing able to kill intruders, or not have guards at all. Some stuff that pushed you to think out of the box and use the assets in a creative way.I guess this is where the one island systems works against the game.

It is a ok game. I finished it with a good feeling but dont feel like playing it for a long time.\t\t\t\t\t
