\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan if I feel conflicted about this game. I'm a big fan of the original, played the demo of it when it came out, got the original title through piracy as it was impossible to get it otherwise on physical format over here, purchased it on Steam as soon as it came out to play it more, I was excited when this one was announced - I even pre-ordered it, which I rarely do. And when it released? Played it for around 12 hours and dropped it out of sheer boredom. Picked it up a month or so ago now that all DLCs have come out and all, and honestly? Game is alright, but flawed to hell. I will start with the negatives first:

First of all, playing on hard difficulty is a waste of time. I'm serious, don't do it. I'm always looking forward when a game has difficulty options to see if those difficulties will actually change how one would play and oh boy nah, all difficulty does is spawn harder enemies to the point you will NOT see the weaker versions at any point... Except on set spawns. The game will occasionally send the forces of justice your way, and all that Hard difficulty does is replace those by more bullet-sponge versions way earlier than it normally would. They also deal increasingly more damage and are way better equipped. Result? By the point you end the tutorial, you'll lose plenty of minions every five minutes because for SOME DUMB REASON, you're getting assaulted by max rank soldier dudes, half of them equipped with rocket launchers. All it does is force you to waste resources all the time and constantly lose personnel in an extremely forced way. It sucks.

Fires. Fires on this game are unmanageable and at least on hard difficulty, became so tiresome I just gave up and left everything burn so I could replace it by when those flames ended. It doesn't matter one bit if you put fire extinguishers nearby, the minions are just too ineffective at putting them down - by the time they extinguish one item, all other items around it catch fire. When they go and try to put down the flames on that one, the original item has caught fire again. Its a cycle that NEVER ends until everything on that room has gone to ♥♥♥♥, and everytime the game forced me to use the Superweapon of the base to advance a sub-plot it would cause ANOTHER fire on all the bank rooms on the base, along with occassionally also starting fires on most power rooms. It forces the player to not only lose a lot of time, but waste resources left and right NON-STOP.

The devs have done their best to replicate the charm from the original title, but man if it feels lacking. Dialogue is alright, but graphically? Its nowhere as appealing and timeless as the old game - and being outdone by a game that doesn't even run well on modern systems doesn't fill me with much confidence writting this review.

Something I've HATED about this game from start to finish - only being able to do one side-story at a time. They DRAG for so long, some of them asking more and more minions to be sent out there to the point you feel like this is a Facebook game trying to shake you off of your time and cash to sell ya cheap microtransactions. Except here all you can do is just wait, and wait, and wait. So much of the game is just waiting, looking around your base because you have nothing to do, observing NPCs do their thing - and it happens WAY more often than in the original. By the end I had the game running at fast-speed and just forcing my way through the side-stories I had left to leave nothing behind. Wanna know how many I had to still clear by the point I reached the point of no return to the end game? Not five, not ten, not fiveteen, NINETEEN. That's how slow progress is with those, limiting the player to one at a time is a BIG mistake.

The spawns of agents on your crib is also kind of an issue - after a couple hours, you'll learn exactly where they will all appear, and all risk is gone. I've played this last playthrough on the DLC map and f#ck me dude, 90% of the enemies come from THE SAME PLACE. ALL THE TIME. Why have maps so big with so many different ways to go in if they will just use the same? The old game sure didn't suffer from that, they could just parachute into any point of the island. Again, being outdone by a game that's around 18 years old? Not a good luck.

Minion capacity. Many people complained on the original that a limit of 100 was low. So in this one, you have a limit of 300! Problem is, lairs on this game are about four to five times as big - an increase of 3x to crew doesn't get close to what you'll need, and with how many missions ask you to send troops all over the globe (which might I remind, those don't come back and are lost forever), you'll be bleeding personnel and feel understaffed by the end of the game when you've pushed your lair to the max. Doesn't help one bit that hard difficulty makes you bleed personnel due to max level agents invading you so often.

Lastly, some of those side-stories were bug to hell. Many times I've had the same issue: enemies need to be captured. Problem there? My minions, henchmen, EVEN THE EVIL GENIUS refuses to capture them for reasons unknown despite me having two different jails with plenty of cells empty. And somehow, the fix was to build a third one? Wasted around four hours on that specific issue, and it wasn't the only one. Enemies taking 10 minutes to spawn, heat on regions increasing stupidly fast and those regions locking down before the team can be sent there because they take forever to get to the chopper, its a pain.

Now, does the game have good stuff tho? Yeah. Not all of my time on it was me being bored out of my mind, that mostly happened past the half-point on the campaign.

The DLCs? They're actually kinda fun. The cross-over ones with TF2 and Portal? I did NOT expect them to be there, and they were alright! The DLC map also felt better than the one I had played on my first playthrough, only DLC I didn't like was the temperature stuff. That one was just a pain to manage.

In terms of soundtrack and voice work? I liked them both! Sure, they sound cheesy as hell and all over the place... But it does fit on a game that's supposed to be cheesy and all over the place. The devs somehow turned Ivan from the worst possible henchmen you could get in the original game into my favorite evil genius, he's so idiotic and stupid that it actually gets pretty entertaining!

Designing your own lair is possibly the most fun part of this game, just like it was on the original. I did find that traps are nothing more than a small obstacle in hard difficulty as 90% of those traps are spotted instantly and get disabled by the enemy, but that's actually still kinda useful as it gives personnel time to get there and kill them. Having multiple floors on the base only helps with making it feel your own place, making it feel like you've earend it.

Henchmen are alright. Being honest I liked the ones on the original game as their skills feel way more limited in here, but some of them like Jubei can still be absurdly useful. Also, you're able to recruit some of the bosses in the game to your own organization, which I approve of!

Researching things works WAAAAY better in here. The original game really overcomplicated it, and here you have a tech tree in which you properly select what you wanna investigate. With DLCs, there's actually two different tech trees as there'll be both Research and Engineering, both with around four different branches of stuff.

Lastly, in terms of length, the game is GOOD for the price! Its a shame that many of those hours are just you waiting for things to happen however. My suggestion? If you have another screen, watch youtube on that screen as you play. You'll thank me later.

In the end, do I recommend this game? Yes, but with a big IF the size of Texas. If you liked the original game and have played it a lot, wait for a sale. You'll find more of what you liked, but the quality of it will be all over the place. Wait for a sale, seriously.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of the negative comments put me off buying the game for a long time, even though I was a big fan of the original and super happy about it getting reborn. But the stuff reviewers said just seemed like the game was a disaster.

I bought it on sale, expecting the worst, and to my surprise NONE of the gripes people had were true to the game. I don't know why people feel the need to whine like that.

The strategic map got a lot of complaints, but I think it is much improved and makes more logical sense than the original. Managing heat requires constant monitoring and juggling, kind of like a real life global criminal under empire would. Minions don't return after missions but who cares, they get replaced while they're gone!

I don't see many people mention it, but in the original game tourists would wander up off the beach or casino into your lair CONSTANTLY. It was stupid and annoying and made no sense. Now they rarely ever do, which makes a lot more sense! A revamped security system and guard behavior makes it also more sensible to deal with intruders in a logical way.

There are at least a dozen more quality of life improvements the sequel has made over the original, all while keeping and VASTLY enhancing the spirit of the original's gameplay. The graphics are excellent, the sound FFX is even more excellent (and the music is good but there could be more tracks)...what's not to like?

If you truly liked the original you should love the sequel. If you don't, I don't know what to tell you. Maybe chill out and get outside more.\t\t\t\t\t
