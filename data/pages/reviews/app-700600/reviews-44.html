\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvil Genius 2 is a wonderful ant farm, but it's not a game. Even after hours at it I struggled to find what is the actual gameplay part of the game compared to just more 'Pretty Things to Look At', and timers for things that come and go nearly as meaningless.


I have an issue with how interchangeable minions are. Lowly that they are, they are killed or passively lost at an extreme churn rate. The problem here is they bothered to give names, brief personality/buff/debuff traits (that may do something or may do nothing, it's difficult to determine) and stats to ants that will almost certainly be gone in an hour or so. Besides Henchmen as 'soldier ants' (and no I don't count 'muscle' minions dedicated to that purpose here) it feels utterly impossible to grow attachment to or care for things that are SO disposable even if it'd not be your intention. It's literally impossible not to lose all minions sent out on one of many frequent missions, regardless of how long they've been with you or how hard fought their acquisition, training, whatever, was. It all contributes to making me just not care, about any of this. It's like picking a random ant in a colony and calling them Bob, but in 5 minutes you've lost track of them because there is zero reason to spend more than a few seconds thinking about something you are bound to lose. At one point in the tutorial you are ordered to execute a minion (or two). The problem is, while witnesses got a little work harder buff or fear debuff (?), I genuinely could not tell whether it did ANYTHING, good or bad. Life is so worthless here and it all so 'evil' (read: stupid evil) that cold-blooded murder of friends and foes is swept away into an incinerator and memory hole to be utterly forgotten, fitting the running theme of most everything else.


To me there is nothing about this simulator that makes one feel 'powerful' or 'in control' of basically anything besides creating the space for your ants to inhabit like some megalomaniacal, yet impotent, interior decorator, which if that's what they were going for then they nailed it. There is almost nothing that comes close to power fantasy or meaningful progression (with the unlocking of stairs being a bizarre exception), it just feels like simulating the height of futility of middle management, where if you're lucky maybe something good happens, but you won't be able to really appreciate it before more drudgery washes it all away, like all the ants before and to come.


There is some redemption, something to like here through cheeky and irreverent humor as well as at times genuinely enjoyable cutscenes, but that's all I felt I have to look forward to. Most of the rest of the interaction is so thoroughly shallow that it's frustrating for how graphically pretty and slick it all seems, like some trust fund baby executive failed upward enough to focus so hard on selling presentation. But that's just it. It's ALL flash-in-the-pan style over substance. It's maddening.


For me this is the worst kind of sequel / remake. Where you take an idea that has promise but questionable execution, and then miss the point entirely by amplifying and neglecting all the wrong parts. It's shocking how thinly veiled the 'Cover Operation' is for trying (?) to be different from countless timewasting and predatory mobile games such as that modern iteration of Dungeon Keeper (which I suspect more than a little influenced the creation of this). Impressively, EG2 manages to sometimes feel even worse somehow by not even having the option to purchase crystals or whatever to 'expedite progress', outside of limited and vague instructions that your Genius avatar can provide as well as one optional DLC that feels wink-wink for unlocking superior stat-boosting furniture for your ants to be more efficient.


I want to love this concept and style so much and yet I think I utterly, thoroughly hate near everything it's become. Everything is upside down and opposite. Everything is evil. Bravo.\t\t\t\t\t
