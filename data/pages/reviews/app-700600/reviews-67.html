\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried, I really tried...

1. Starting with technical difficulties
I spent 30+ minutes in game options just to play it properly. I have two monitors, and game didn't run on my 2k monitor, always tried to run on second HD monitor. There is no option in game to change monitor, and resolution changing always fits smaller monitor. I tried to run it windowed and drag it to the 2K monitor... Crash. Again? Crash. :) I thought maybe it's my hardware... Well my pc is in RECOMMENDED - i7 10400, nvidia 1660Ti, 32GB RAM. I updated drivers, downgrade all graphics to low. Still can't play on my main 2k monitor... I just disconnected my smaller monitor. Now game runs on 2K monitor, but jumps... So I changed resolution to 1920x1080... Now it works. :/

2. Tutorial
After more than hour I had enough, It's slow, so slow... I understand this is a complex game, but adviser during normal game after basic tutorial would be enough (in Total War it is enough, in Civilization is enough).

3. Gameplay
Well it's not a strategy, and as a base sim it's pretty tiring (controls + UI). To be honest this is just a mediocre managment game, with everything on cooldowns... For me this is like a mobile game, that was to big for android. :/\t\t\t\t\t

I loved Evil Genius 1 and from announcement to release. I kept with all the news and it comes across as an unfinished product behind a paywall. They've released like 6 DLC's which add almost nothing to the game and in their "New Year Update". They praised all the work they did. Where as basic stuff in the game doesn't seem to even have a real function.

Doors? Walls? They actually make your lair worse.

Traps? Once you hit mid game the agents just disable them all and on the off chance that they hit one. It barely tickles them.

Minions? They seem to have two conflicting designs here. Expendable and not. They come with traits that affect gameplay. They also die INCREDIBLY easily. So I'm not sure what's the idea here.

The only good things are the map designs are kinda cool and the geniuses are kinda cool but even the campaigns are really just a 10 sentence "mini-clip" where you say some generic lines.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA supervillain lair management sim where you try to take over the world while fending off interference from superspies and other do-gooders. You build different lairs to meet your minions' needs, train them for the specialized skillsets that you need, and amass the wealth and supertechnology you will need to take over the world. I feel like once you pass the early game, you are only ever almost having fun, spending all your time fast forwarding and watching your minions' training and research progress gradually fill up so that you can meet the next quest objective. And then you run into a bug like I did where it wasn't counting my progress towards a quest objective, and I was on the fence about whether I was enjoying the game or not anyway, and I gave up. Fun premise, but the execution is very fast-forwardy.\t\t\t\t\t
