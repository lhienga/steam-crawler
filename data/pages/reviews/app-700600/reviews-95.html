\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvil Genius 2 - Failing at Order VS Chaos

Sorry for the long read, this is mostly something I write for my own sanity and therapy after playing this game for 100 hours.

I feel a little bad leaving a negative review, I do not think Evil Genius 2 is a bad game. But I dont recommend it, and the reason for me is the Devs dont seem to understand how to balance order vs chaos. In all games, but very apparent in management / base building games, there need to be a balance between order and chaos. The player usually is trying to create order and the game tests that order by creating chaos. Rim World is a perfect example of this since you literally choose the chaos level you want. This usually leads to more challenge.

Evil Genius 2 does a great job letting the player create an evil lair. The building, graphics, art style and the minions are mostly an improvement on the first game, mostly! The game gives you great tools to create order, but then never challenge that order in any real way. Why dont I just say the game lack challenge then? Because I feel the problem goes deeper than just buffing the agents or something like that. I think the Devs fundamentally did not understand this aspect of the game. challenge and chaos is not the same thing.

The beautiful chaos of Evil genius 1:

In the first Evil Genius you were surrounded by chaos. Agents and turists were constantly harassing you. Arriving without warning. Roaming freely on the island, sometimes entering your base at the same time just due to bad luck. You had limited space for the lair, making you think harder about the design and how to use the space. Like how many freezing racks did you want to get ride of the body´s. The world stage was not a safe heaven for farming gold. Minions could fail missions, they could die from agents roaming the map. You had to plan who to send and when. You had a very limited minion pool, making you think about what kind of minions you wanted and what to use them for. Even the gold coming back from the world map had to be carried into your vault. Just these examples from many others shows the chaos in Evil Genius 1. They may not be challenging for all, since that depends on players skill and exp, but it all create chaos leading to interesting and unpredictable situations that may lead to real challenge.

Evil Genius 2 - The game of Order

This game took all that chaos and threw it in the bin. The World map is now only a safe space for farming gold. The building space will never force you to priorities or plan. The island is set up to create order. Agents and turists arrive on a schedule and the game is so kind to tell you who and when, and they go straight for your lair and casino. If only people in the real world were that polite and orderly. You can have 300 minions (I know more minions is something many in the community wanted), but it tips the scale too far. Since building space is no problem, you can just spam lockers. No need to plan how many of each. Body bags was in the first game to create a gameplay feature, if you kill everything you need more racks -> limited building space -> Challenge from chaos. Now its just clean up work for your minions.

I wont go on, but I hope you get the idea what im trying to say. This game needs more chaos. It gets SOOO boring really fast because there is no chaos to challenge the player in interesting and unpredictable ways. Setting the game to hard dosent do anything to fix this. Imagine Rim World giving you a timer for the next event and what is was. Its just misunderstanding the core appeal of the game in my opinion.

I hope they can do better in Evil Genius 3.\t\t\t\t\t
