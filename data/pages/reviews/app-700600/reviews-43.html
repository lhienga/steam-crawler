\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort version:
+Solid gameplay
+Great visuals and sound
+Expanded content over the original
+Improved quality of life stuff from the original: Evil Genius can actually fight now, Can set up posts on the world for passive income, Guard stations, even bigger base

-Features that were present in the original were not included. Multi-tag enemies, doors that cannot be set to 4/Forbidden to minions, tourists/civilians that can be targeted just like any agent, construction being slower with minions having to mine everything instead of the demolition job and having to place furniture afterwards. Game can be slow at times.
-Hard minion cap of 300
-Henchmen cap to 5 instead of 7 in the original

Would recommend if you enjoyed the original and don't mind the imperfections.

Long version:
Bought this game after waiting for it to go on sale. So how does it rack against the OG Evil Genius? One step forward, two steps back. There were parts that got streamlined that I appreciated. Such as making the deceit minions somewhat useful with the addition of the casino. Your henchman will only die if your Evil genius executed them. The fact they can sit on the meeting table to recover health and keep them from wandering into fights they cannot win. So no more locked room isolation trick. Everyone can use the fire extinguisher. A guard post. And your Evil Genius can actually fight. Even Maximillian! It also helps that each Henchman have clear defined goals in combat and the proper kit that makes sense. A tag system that auto-tags any enemy that wanders in certain parts of your base. Lots of nifty new enemies and challenges that would make any fan of the original would try to beat.

Now for the bad parts. Henchmen have lost their ability to call minions. Which is why I ended up going for Iris at every game start as she has a souped up version of it and I have to watch in horror as other henchmen would get beaten down all the while the combat/muscle minions are at earshot. The fact you cannot set doors to 4 levels. As this game is all about managing your base and making sure it flows correctly, what was helpful in the 1st game is the fact you can make forbidden doors. So you can properly control the flow of the base and which set of weapons your minions will grab. Lethal or non-lethal. Also it keeps minions from wandering everywhere. Had a technician who decided to be at the far side of the base when there was a reactor on fire that needed immediate attention. The fact you cannot group tag enemies. This was a feature of the original and it made controlling the fight easier and its not here. The 300 minion hard-cap. There came a moment where I had all these resources to expand the base with only to find certain utilities sparsely populated. Would have been nice to have extra hands. Not like the Evil Genius has to follow arbitrary laws of the universe. Also, the whole "Stuck with 5 henchmen" really bothers me. The fun part about being an Evil Genius is assembling your own Legion of Doom. That was present in the original, but not here. Having 7 was great as it made keeping the base secure and beating down do-gooders extra fun.

So, do I recommend this game? Only if you like base management and world domination. Its a solid game but there are a few noticeable cracks on it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompared to the original, EG2 is a strict upgrade in almost every regard. The world map functions so much better than the original with the caveat that minions are gone once they're sent out. That might sound harsh, but with the original EG you had to spread your population cap between the base and the world map. With a limit of only 100 minions in the original this was a gigantic bottleneck to your progression. EG2 not only fixes this by making world minions not count toward your popcap, it extends that cap past 100 so that you can get more speciality minions to cater to your gameplay style.

Another example is the casino, an area that has been vastly improved. Tourists don't wander into your base, and the starter base has a single point of entry that forces all the investigators through your casino (that's right, your front business is actually out front!). This means you can actually focus on Valets and be rewarded by creating a base that is almost immune to investigators.

The graphics are also great. The addition of randomised minions adds a little life where it was otherwise missing, and the performance is buttery smooth even with a lot happening on-screen.

Audio is my one gripe so far. The music is *fine*, but I feel like it lacks the bombast and presence of the original soundtrack. Even the remake of the original title lacks *something.* The voice acting also falls into the "servicable" category, it's fine, but don't expect oscar-winning stuff here.

Overall, easy recommend at the sale price I picked it up at (70 percent off). A goodly amount of fun that scratches an itch I haven't scratched in a very long time.\t\t\t\t\t
