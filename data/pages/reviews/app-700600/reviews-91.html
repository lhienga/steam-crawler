\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would not recommend this game at this time. It is not production ready. My system exceeds the recommended specification but cannot display the graphics at Ultra for even a small base with only 1 level.

It seems to cope with medium graphics but even then it uses a ton of CPU and GPU. I have a played a lot of graphics intensive games and this is not the most. Yet it seems to seems to use way more computing resources. My computer has never run as hot as it does playing this poorly coded resource hog. This game is probably best played during the winter if you want to turn your computer into a space heater.

Meanwhile, I have had workers, scientists, and technicians standing around doing nothing, even though they have been assigned a job. They just stand where ever they are doing nothing. They can get to their destination, but I found it was just easier to kill them to force a different minion on to the job.

Another problem with frozen employees, who have not been turned to gold / statues, is that they can block equipment being placed. I had a flying Organic Samples workstation because the scientist was stuck shaking nervously as the equipment floated in the air. Trying to tag the employee for capture or termination does not work. Only killing the employee with the boss works,

Another bug that I found was a scientist standing in a wall. Needless to say my research stalled till I realised that the minion was trapped in the wall. On realising that the minion was trapped in the wall, I was lucky enough that I could dig the buggy minion out.

Unfortunately, having played a bit longer, I now have two minions trapped deeper in the wall and no way to dig them out They are near the edge of the base. I believe that I need to advance to the most powerful mining level in the game to dig those buggy minions out.

These kinds of basic BUGS makes me believe that this game is not production ready. It could be worth the money I spent on it if and only if they fix the resource hogging bugs and other basic logic bugs.

When you combine the resource hog with buggy elements of this game, I cannot recommend this game. I did pay for it so I intend to play the game a bit more. I do hope that they fix the bugs and improve the performance of the game so that I can have a large base without massive amounts of lag and a giant electricity bill.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a big fan of the original game so was pretty stoked to see this come about after almost 2 decades since the first. I hesitated at first as the reception was lukewarm here and from some of the reviewers I follow, and it didn't seem to improve over time. I've read some claims this is a mobile game which are pretty far from true. There are some problems here but this isn't one of them.

I finally took the plunge on a fairly deep sale, and I'm glad I did. In my opinion this game is pretty much what i wanted from a sequel; it is essentially the same formula as 1 with lots of quality of life improvements and with the biggest problem fixed.

In the original, past a certain point earning money was pretty much impossible due to the amount of heat you got and not enough ways to reduce it besides waiting or doing an exploit in the early game. In this one you have many objects which can reduce heat and there are a handful of options on the global map to reduce it as well. Money is also easier to come by which could be a negative, but i think gives the game a better pace in the long run since there's only so much you can do without it.

Besides that, the art style is fantastic, the OST is from the same excellent composer as the first (and is much more prominently featured instead of just menus) and the game runs well for me. The way minions are used as a resource in this game also makes the training rooms more important than before. Overall, everything that was good in the OG is improved here imo.

Some of the negatives for me are negatives the original had as well;
- Super agents just teleport directly into your base. They always seem to enter at the same spot (at least in my based they do)
- Traps feel kind of useless in a lot of ways. They're great for flavor but i find just using minions more efficient. They aren't as essential as in say a dungeon keeper game (not yet anyways).
- You can only research 1 thing at a time. From a balance perspective, fine, but this means all the expensive equipment/science minions just sit around doing nothing while the 1 research takes place.
- Laying out rooms can be a bit fiddly due to how the entry points of objects work and the general shapes of them vs the available space. Generally it works pretty well though, and reorganizing is pretty easy (even the rooms can be reorganized easily)
- There are more lulls than i'd like, but at least there's swanky spy-fi music to pass the time and nice art to look at. I think there's a decent balance between peaks and troughs but I'd prefer a more evenly-interesting experience. Maybe I'm just playing too conservatively.
- The perks of geniuses feel a bit underwhelming.
- Paradox DLC model

Overall, i like this quite a lot.\t\t\t\t\t
