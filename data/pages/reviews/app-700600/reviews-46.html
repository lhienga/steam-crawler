\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvil Genius 2 is a decent game, let down by some significant design decisions, and annoying (though not game-breaking) bugs. It feels decidedly undercooked, with many unimaginative or tedious tasks, recycled content and a very disappointing campaign ending. As such, it's not a strong recommendation, but it might be worth buying on sale and playing for 10-20 hours.

The game's main draw is the fantastic art direction: it looks exactly like a 2021-2022 sequel to Evil Genius should look. Everything from the minions to the decor absolutely oozes with charm. There are countless jokes, pop culture references and fantastic animations. The voice acting deserves a special mention with Brian Blessed's Ivan, and his indescribable Eurotrash accent, definitely being one of the highlights of the game for me.

If only this were a more directed effort: I hope Rebellion learns from their mistakes and finances an Evil Genius 3 which addresses all the fumbles while expands on the late game. Plenty of people have criticized the map/scheme system and for good reason: it feels like a contrived way to extend play-time while minimizing development costs. Were the campaigns less padded, I would've played all of them. As such, I'll leave my playtime at what it currently shows and move on to better builders. Too bad, as this could've been a better game.\t\t\t\t\t
