\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you enjoy being a Dungeon Masters in Dungeons or War for the Overlord, it's likely you'll enjoy Evil Genius 2.

The campaign slowly builds and is great a teaching you how to slowly build your Lair thru research and minion missions.

I read a lot of the negative posts and most of them seem to be in comparison to the previous Evil Genius game, which the graphics for look like a Nintendo 64 game, completely unplayable for me, bc I'm used to Starcraft 2 at full 4k running overclocked dual GTX3000 GPUS.

You're able to speed up the rate at which agents enter your lair by letting Heat build in each region, or you can reduce Agents by sending minions to run Covert Operation Cooling down the area.

As the game progresses you'll start encountering super agents, some who show up inside your base and can be very annoying.

The Patriot Agent is the most annoying for me as he leaves trash everywhere he goes labelled as "Evidence." This evidence doesn't seem to have any effect tho even when you leave it around, other than being extremely annoying.

Symmetry is a thief who constantly steals your gold, however she is not difficult to deal with as long as your have one of your Henchmen attack her near your guard posts.

The one glaring flaw in the game is almost ALL of the super agents are able to disable your traps, effectively making it so you're better off just hiring tons of Guards, ranking them to hitmen or Martial artists and then setting up choke points.

SPOILER: The one trap that is consistently effective is the dance floor, because the disable point is at the CENTER of the trap, but a single foot on the dance floor sets it off.


Another GLARING flaw, is that when you change the difficulty from Easy to Normal, suddenly you get almost no Minions. I don't mind the strength of the agents, in fact, my lair can tank almost any amount of agents and super agents, most die or are distracted before they even reach my choke points, I have not yet figured out how to deal with the lack of incoming minions.

The Minions themselves are universally stupid, walking right into teams of Super Agents and instead of running back into the base, the minions will often run DIRECTLY at the Super Agents and then cower in the corner as the agents unloads a machine gun into them.

Another huge flaw is much of the equipment does not seem to work in some of the rooms. The Minion mind room for example, instead of the guards using the upgrade intelligence equipment that is provided, often they simply stand around doing nothing.

About 80% of the room equipment does work tho, you'll need to experiment with it yourself to see which works and which doesn't.

TLDR: If you like being a Dungeon Manager, you'll love Evil Genius 2. I'm going to buy all of the DLC on the next steam sale.

Portal DLC: The Portal DLC is free and it should be. It's incredibly frustrating to get it to work and your reward is a single portal, some cameras that are ok, and some super traps.

I still need to experiment to see if the Portal Traps can be disabled, they are quite complicated to setup tho and it's likely the agents will simply disable them.\t\t\t\t\t

4: Higher minion cap.
5: Still nice soundtrack.
6: Always-manned guard posts and auto tagging of certain enemies.

But there are serious backward steps from the original game.

1: The art style is, for me at least, cartoonish in an entire different way. The original managed to be a caricature but also somewhat took itself seriously (sort of like NOLF), whereas this is just way too over the top and childish with the exaggerated expressions of every character. It's missing the deadpan serious humor of the original like this.

2: While bases are multi level, you can't put entrances wherever you want, and generally have less freedom to build out your island because of this. I guess this came with the territory and making the island layout itself interesting but eh, I much preferred the freedom, and the fact that you could put up cameras and hidden stuff outside the mountain itself.

3: There is way too much space in the base to accommodate all sorts of designs, and this is a flaw, because it kinda doesn't force you to think creatively. They certainly could've made a ton of different islands and just slapped a 'difficulty' scale on and limited the shape and scale of the base by un-excavatable parts and wacky island formations.

4: Traps are basically dumbed down to a point where they're useless and unnecessary. This is a big letdown, because part of the fun was making a base that had various possible entrances, dead-ends, corridors filled with sensors traps and perpetual motion machines or "uber traps" that took a lot of space to make, but was a great sort of endgame hilarity.

5: Research is now a couple of trees, and nothing special. I just loved the old one where you had to experiment with stuff to unlock other stuff, and sampling a lot of things took ages. They could've just stuck to that and kept a ledger on each type of item in the base that has been experimented with to make tracking it more simple.

6: As far as I can tell, actually manning the computers in the computer room is no longer required to have the broadcast strength to do schemes on the world stage... so the only requirement is basically power and maintenance.

7: The guard alert/camera system is so much more inferior that it barely resembles the original. You can't do dedicated response units to certain places, which is a dang shame, it would've played so well with the auto tagging system and multi level bases.

8: The world stage and how super dumbed-down the schemes are in this version is such a heartache. It was so nice, that you weren't told you need X kind of minions for a scheme to succeed, it all just increased the probability. You could mix and match and while the scheme was running they all just kept dying randomly and either succeeded or not, but all in all it was a great fun gamble, and a way superior implementation than the one we got with this one, which is "add x minions, they die, scheme complete, gg". Also, I really don't know why on earth didn't they just do the very same discovery of quests like in the predecessor... now you have a list of tasks, one task gives one mission on the world stage, and all you have to do is find it, click start, or wait because the FOJ are in the area (and they move way too slowly out) and then boom if you've got the minions, you're good to go. I'ts just completing tasks and pushing buttons for all the side quests, of which you can at one time do only one, which is absolutely ridiculous.

I may have left out some things, but realistically, from this you can probably already see, that this game was developed for a much MUCH less sophisticated audience, which is a damn shame.
It's like getting a cover shooter as a sequel to an RPG.
Sure, it's got the same title, and some of the artwork and humor, but I imagine this is a game that's been designed by someone who took a glance at the original and just took some quick notes and said "eh, close enough", or was just sanded down to a frigging ball thanks to overdesign by committee.

If you haven't played the first, well then, go ahead, give it a go, it's okay. But die hard fans of the original will definitely be un-impressed.
I myself just got the urge to reinstall and play the original instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is a cash grab looking to capitalize on the success of EG1.

TLDR:

The meat and potatoes of the game consists of doing the same thing over and over again, reissuing the same tasks on the world map, expanding your vault or worse yet waiting or a mission related research item to complete with nothing to do while you wait. In EG1 you would have to manage your defense, but in EG2 with a decent base configuration and about a third of your minions as muscle minions and you dont even need to do that.

All the missions consist of either researching something, sending minions to the world map, or occasionally interacting with agents sent to your lair. This might be fine if missions did not consist of about five stages of this, that generally all need to be done one at a time. It gets really old really fast.

This game was designed to make money for Rebellion, as the game is okay during the steam return window, but once you realize that thats all there is to the game your stuck with the game and cant return it. I doubt that i will get any more games from this company.

Review:

*Missions*

The way missions are carried implemented means you can only work on one primary mission and one side mission at a time. This makes the game very grindy as side missions outnumber main missions by a large margin, perhaps greater then 10 to 1. Some of these side missions must be completed before moving forward with the primary missions, which leads you to grind through all the side missions before proceeding with the main ones. This is not fun. If you could do multiple side missions at a time this would not be an issue. But you cant. This leads to game play where you are just waiting for these side missions to complete without anything else to do ( I will expand on this in the game play section).
All missions consist of two different tasks, either send minions to the world stage or research something. Missions generally consist of multiple tasks like these some times they can be completed in parallel and sometimes they can not.

*Game play*

The balance and structure of EG2 is very different from EG1.

- The forces of justice are much weaker in EG2. A decent base configuration, no traps, just minions for defense and i vary rarely need to lift a finger when i am under attack. In other words you dont need to manage your defense.

- In EG1 your vault consisted of a room with briefcase racks, from which minions would take your money and use it to pay for the items you have ordered and gold was stored in pallets on the floor. In EG2 You actually have to build a variety of storage containers for your gold. All of which have durability and the best of which also needs power.
What this leads to is a situation where you will need to have a literal army of technicians to repair both your gold storage and your generators. So much so that the limiting factor for how much gold you can store is not space, as it once was but how large a percentage of your workforce you are willing to dedicate to keeping your gold storage and generators from spontaneously combusting, which is what happens if you dont repair an object.
Worse yet, placing all this gold storage and generators is very grindy and unpleasant. Just plain no fun at all. In EG1 all you had to do is make a larger room which you could then watch fill up with gold,

- The tasks on the world map now consist of a set list of repeating ones, all timed and all active for a duration of time. You will have to keep coming back to the world map and re ordering the same missions over and over again all to do mostly one of two things, reduce heat (due to poor game design, you can actually ignore this one, as the consequences of high heat are minor such as stronger agents attacking you, that are still too weak to really be an issue) or to make money (of which, once you get your economy balanced, you dont actually need to do. As the bases that you have to setup to be able to use the world map generate all the money you need once you level them up enough. AND because expanding your vault is such a pain your are not going to bother beyond a certain point). There are two more types of tasks, one lets you effect how many tourists are on your island and the other gives you some minions. The latter can be useful at time and the prior i see no point to as tourists have negligible effect on your game.

*Conclusion*

The game is okay at first. You build your base, you have some things to manage. But once you get set up none of the things you have to do in the game are fun. Its just grindy. Doing the same thing over and over again and lots of waiting. In EG1 it was fun to collect the stat boosting loot in the game and it could really help your game play, but in EG2 doing side missions is grindy and time consuming and while some of the loot is useful, most of the loot is just incredibly weak and has little to no impact on game play. This leads to a game that is not rewarding to play. Its just not fun.

This could have been a fun game but the creators felt the need to make the game long instead of fun. If the mission and task system were thrown out and replaced with the simpler system of EG1 it would make the game much much much faster, but the rewards you get through out the game would be more in tune with the effort you have to put in to the game. Where as right now, its a lot of time and effort for practically no reward.

To my understanding the development of this game is now basically over, so this is all its going to be. The Oceans campaign pack adds a Temperature System. This adds another feature for you to manage, but its so bad, many people are refusing to play the game with it, worse yet, if you buy the dlc, there is no way to disable this feature.
