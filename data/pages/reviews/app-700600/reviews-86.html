\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is too arcade-ish/basic in comparison to the first game.
-There is no Island fighting or building choices such as stopping the forces of justice from even being able to get to your base. No build options on the Island to advance your research or "trick" the enemy that there is no evil base here and to move on.
- Research in the last game required trial and error that was fun...Example... Your loot and ALL build choices could be researched to make new items and those items could make more items and so on. Sometimes you had to make an outside gun turret (which was researched to get), for example, just so they could research it to make something else you needed, but in doing so the gun turrets get noticed easily until you could research them successfully which caused heat increase and more forces of Justice to arrive. You had to design work arounds and those clever ways to hack the situation was part of the game and what make Evil Genius 1 so good. That is lacking so much in this one that this just feels like a watered down version of the first one.
- There is a lack of huge battles in evil genius 2 like there was in the first one. You only deal with small squads in the second game.
- Forces of Justice sometimes just appear in your base with no option to have stopped them from even finding your base.
- You are forced to use a cover operation and are forced to have that cover operation connect to your main entrance. The first game allowed you to have the option to use a cover base and also provided a choice to place that cover operation away from your base entrance.

A complete lack of options, strategies, tactics, and workarounds in this game makes this one a huge disappointment compared to the first evil genius. Play the first one! Forget evil genius 2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never played the original, but I liked the idea. So I came in without hopes or preconceptions.

Noooope. It's like a melting pot of bad design decisions. That's all you really need to know, the game's not fun and there are no redeeming qualities. I'm going to go ahead and explain myself a bit, so stick around if that's not good enough of a warning.

Horrible Humour
So the game wants to be a comedy. Yes, cool, we need more of this. But it manages to be so unfathomably unfunny, it's something to marvel at. It's hard to even see what was meant to be funny in the first place. Scheme descriptions often are incredibly silly, on the level of "Why don't we try to sell fridges to Eskimos?", but they fail to be funny. If nothing else, you're left bewildered that someone actually wrote this. Then there are terrible pop culture references - "how many wild hogs? 30-50? Okay". Reminded me of HOLY POTATOES: A WEAPON SHOP?. If you thought that game is funny, then maybe you can find some humour in this, if you skip those bewildering moments of failed silliness.

The character animations are cartoonish, and this also leans well into humorous territory, but the game does -nothing- with it. It's just, look, cartoony villain man and feeble minion. Funny, yes? Hahaha? Ha. Haha. Yeah, no.

Mobile Malady
There was a time when we had to act and burn our current economic system to the ground. Sadly, we have waited too long to do this, that now there are people thinking that the abominable exploitative practices of mobile gaming are legit design. And those people made this game.

-Everything- is timer based. You wait for the scheme to finish. You wait to get new randomly generated bonus objectives. You wait for the heat to decrease. You wait for the new workers to arrive. You wait, you wait, you wait. It's not even that you watch stuff happen: you WAIT. Yes, you can advance time, but here's an idea: how about giving stuff to do? So that I don't want to skip time? How about I skip playing the game instead? You know what, yes, I will skip straight to writing this review.

Oh, and you're BOMBARDED with notifications. All. The. Time.

Pointless Progression
You can research new technologies, which grant you access to new rooms, new goons, new levels of stuff. But it doesn't take long to realise: none of those things are real game changers. They're more like upgrades, almost numeric ones. What I'm saying is that it doesn't really change the way you play the game. In a good game research and upgrades usually give you some new angle to consider, new type of unit, new style of play. Here there's no such thing, at least not as far as I've noticed, and I got to Tier 2 (out of 4) of research. Like, wow, you can equip your minions with batons! Oh, only your guards. So that the minions who are already good at fighting can be even gooder at fighting. I mean, yes, upgrading unit weapons is a normal thing in games, but here I never really felt like I'm under any threat anyway.

Abysmal Agent Attacks
In the top right corner, you have a warning about an upcoming agent attack. It's signified by, you guessed it, a timer. When it reaches zero, agents appear and try to infiltrate your base. Then it becomes a game of hide and seek where you try to find them as they enter, tag them for extermination, and wait until your minions deal with this. I'm not entirely sure what happens if a non-disguised minion enters your base, from what I've seen you need to micromanage combat. There is a way to place guards, but in order for guards to actually guard, you need to research a better guard post, otherwise you'll have to trigger a red alert every time agents arrive.

Long story short, the agents are an annoyance in the purest sense of the word. You need to clickity click click, or else you'll have to live with flies buzzing next to your ear.

Boring Base Building
You'd think building a supervillain base would be the coolest thing you can do. But no! Somehow even the building is uninteresting. You mine out the room, you place room tiles (ok, those things can be done at once, at least), you place doodads in the room... and that's it. Okay, yes, this is how Dungeon Keeper did it and it was great back then. But here... I don't know, it just doesn't feel meaningful.

Hard to even say anything about this. Only that it was just not very fun. I didn't like doing it, but considering this is the only real thing you can do while waiting for timers to finish... I occasionally did it, if nothing else to complete optional objectives.

Cynical Corporate Cacophony
The design of this game makes me frown in a very specific, existential way. It feels like nobody had any fun, any insight while working on this. There probably was some bigwig executive who said "I read in Forbes that these games are the new craze, so how about we do it like this". And all design decisions came to be made like this. I mean, HoW tO mAkE a GaMe FoR tHe InTeRnEt GeNeRaTiOn? ChEcKlIsT:
- pop culture references? Check!
- timers? Check!
- inoffensive, milquetoast version of villany, devoid of politics? Check!
- funny 50s aesthetic? Check!
- bunch of cosmetic DLCs? CHECK $$$ !

In one word: Uninspired.

I think I had more fun writing this review than playing this game. And I paid 20 freaking EUR for it. Some of the best games I've ever played cost me less than half of this. Stay away. Far, far away. It's too late for the world, games like this will probably keep appearing, but that doesn't mean you have to support this vile industry. Go play some indies instead.\t\t\t\t\t
