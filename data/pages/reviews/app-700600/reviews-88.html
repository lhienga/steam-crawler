\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMegalomaniacal fun. Thwart the forces of good. Build superweapons. Take over the world.

Having played both the original and this sequel I would say there some things I miss, and a few balance issues. The mini map from the original game that allowed you to keep tabs on enemy forces on your island is sorely missed. Threat messages and the presence of enemy forces are out of sync, so you'll spend an inordinate amount of time scrutinizing your base for undetected intruders. The original also allowed you to put nodes of your external network into sleep mode to let heat dissipate. Not so in this version. You either have to expend a significant amount of intel points or workers to damp things down. Both of these changes result in a steady trickle of opposition forces breaching your defenses. Gone are the days of quietly building up your forces before making overt moves.

That said, the humorous spirit of the original is still there. Brian Blessed's voicing of Red Ivan is priceless. The cartoon-ish, bright, hippy cold war art is just the ticket to set the mood. The designers continue to issue updates to strike a balance in play, and we're already seeing improvements in the world stage versus lair management dynamic. If you enjoy spy movies and tongue-in-cheek humor then definitely give this a try.\t\t\t\t\t
