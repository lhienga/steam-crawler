\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow sadly disappointing. As a long time fan of the first Evil Genius, I had looked forward to a 2nd one for a long time, and bought it without much consideration, though the reviews I read were certainly favorable. Bought it months ago, I don't actually know how long it has sat in my library unplayed till I had time to give it a go. Turns out I should have tried it immediately so I could still ask for a refund.

Just a few minutes into the initial game and base building and the tutorial is informing me that I need to setup space heaters and air conditioning units, to maintain both the happiness of my workforce and the functional integrity of my furniture and equipment. Seriously? Who in their right mind thought a game about building an evil organization and base, and trying to "take over the world", imagined that it would be fun to intersperse that with such tedium as managing the air temperature of your base? Yea, I suppose that in reality, this would be a concern. I play games to escape a bit from the boredom and tedium that often comes with many aspects of reality, not so that I can micromanage it in a more analytical and concise fashion.

Perhaps there is some way to turn off this stupidity in the myriad options and settings. Honestly can't be bothered to dig into it all and find out at this point. Better off just playing the original.

In a nut shell, if you haven't already wasted your money on this, don't. Just play the first one and leave this garbage on the shelf. That's what I will be doing, and if I thought I could get a refund at this point, I certainly would.\t\t\t\t\t
