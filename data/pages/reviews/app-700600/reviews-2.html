\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttldr Not really worth your time, even for 3 dollars.

Starting with things I did like
I thought the theme was cool and the voiced dialog and visuals were enjoyable.
They put a lot of flavor text in
A weird thing to say but I enjoy a game I can press pause in.

Why I gave it a negative rating
Steam doesn't allow for mixed reviews in individual reviews, although I think this game deserved the 60% or whatever it got.
I also think that 60% is a D and not really something you'd want, 50% and I'd consider the game possibly non-functional.
The theme/visual/dialog was cool but the gameplay doesn't really do a good job of delivering the theme.
The dialog was also partially shared among the other characters and probably didn't vary that much(I got like 3 lines in a 2nd character before I was like "oh this is pretty much the same even if it shouldn't be") so it didn't feel like I was actually playing anyone different.
The theme is meant to be that you're an evil genius(2) who is supposed to be making this secret base defending yourself with traps and long hallways to stop intruders and well like.. an evil base you know? like the kind of thing an action hero in a cheesy movie would sneak through. Or a dungeon in a different video game.
Except how it works even with the tutorial is that you end up playing an evil casino attached to an evil powerplant. Its basically a huge mess in how it actually works and you're better off just training a ton of troops and just fighting them face up.

Also that you never have enough gold storage to actually hold anything, the amount it holds is so small that you need to make gigantic gold rooms (mine took literally 1/3 of my base.) Same for power consumption, power rooms were 1/3 to 1/2 my entire base even using the max research power unit.

The traps don't really work well on the enemies either, they're basically 1 use, don't kill anything or just a single target on a long cooldown and they don't effect half the invaders anyways.
Also that they take more power to run(forcing even more power rooms for your evil powerplant casino) and most of them can't overlap and only work in 4 tile wide hallways.

Everything also needs to have technician units to repair because they slowly degrade, meaning you need to get at least like 20-30 technicians to have everything up and running and need to keep everything closer together.
Because of this I ended up just.. getting rid of doors and directly connecting most of everything past the entryways because there was no reason to close things off to attempt to place traps that didn't work regardless. The only units that would be able to sneak in are the ones immune to the traps/doors and the rest just fight your army at the front door.

Theme aside the game just took absolutely forever.
I did one game from the tutorial quickstart game and it took me apparently 44 hours to finish.
I even swapped to just keeping fast forward on halfway through and skipping the last 30 optional sidequests.
Everything is slow waiting and every quest is like Train 10 of this troop, send them to a location in the world and wait for them to get on the helicopter and fly out and then do it again with newly trained 10 more dudes(more waiting for the training) another 4 times.
Or its "wait for the enemy to attack your base in waves" and that too takes minutes of waiting for them actually to arrive which.. I guess in theory is so you have time to prepare for them but its almost every time(not even always apparently, sometimes they do just appear immediately regardless of what other stuff is going on) and so you just.. need to wait for them and then wait for them to die to your minions.
Or the third kind is "research a random node we added" which again, more waiting but kind of whatever.
It just adds up and every sidequest has like 6 parts of that which take forever, especially the "on the world map wait for the helicopter" thing. You also get randomly attacked by heros during it and you might have to deal with that depending on how well off you are, and they attack through your helicopter pad delaying your progress because they sometimes just kill the guys trying to get on the helicopter to advance the sidequest/mainplot.

Oh by the way you only have a single helicopter and a hard cap of 300 minions. Meaning "build more things and get more guys" isn't really something you can do after a certain point and makes the other floors past like.. 2-3 floors kind of entirely useless.
Along with the fact that it'd make your sidekicks/guards/technicians wander off into the mostly empty far floors and would decrease their effectiveness(meaning you'd need more but you can't get more..) and make it take longer for them to respond to threats making your base weaker.

There's some other smaller things like weird build constraints on single tile wide rooms not being allowed, things that can't be next to each other like what space a trap takes or that it has to have the full reach to be allowed to be placed. Or that you're not allowed to have spaces in rooms that are blocked off(probably because engine/AI problems). A lot of it just kind of feels like "no fun allowed" with how it works though. You also run into weird problems with money in the game where you need a lot but your gold storage is absolutely atrocious, very costly and takes a ton of space. But you also need said gold to be able to do anything and its just in the end you need to have enough income to overcome that and it basically ends up you have infinite money and your coffers are always full and the game is always warning you about needing more gold storage.
Its Frustrating and poorly designed.
The higher difficulty setting wouldn't help this, it would just change where the point is that you reach this and also make a failure state more possible.. which does not help this game with how long it takes play.

Basically the game kind of fails on delivering the theme of the game and is just an incredibly slow waiting game that is actually a really low thought mostly brainless game which is ironic being called "Evil Genius 2".\t\t\t\t\t
