\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to be honest, with over 500 hours in I have to say I really love this game. I'd still be playing, were it not for a single issue that the at-times overly abundant DLCs for this game never addressed. The maps are huge, with plenty of building space and space for trying out new ideas, but they only ever look half-busy because of the horrendous unit cap of 300. It's fine for early game, but once you get to mid-game on hard-mode and one Olga visit can almost completely wipe your guard, it's easy to be stuck in a rut of constantly retraining personnel.

All I can say about this is COME ON Rebellion! The hard cap of 300 feels awkward and unnatural even within the contexts of the game itself. Maybe it was a conscious design choice, but whoever advocated for it chose poorly. You guys can give me a crazy moonbase dlc and ten new evil mastermind choices and it's not going to rope me back in. Until y'all do something about the wimpily low minion count, my time played is staying at 537.5 hours.

Because of this weird limitation, I'd say early game is GREAT, mid game is PRETTY GOOD, and late game is BAD. This game is stuck at a solid 5/10 for me until the developers stop basking in the smell of their own farts and give the people what they want. Fix your code and make it work guys.

EDIT: I've put about a hundred more hours into this game and find my mid to end game stymied again by the 300 minion cap limitation. After the expansion pack came out, the new map and leader are both pretty good and all, but Rebellion studios seems to have all but abandoned this game at this point. Nothing stopping them from releasing a patch or allowing modders to work on the game, but it's kinda lame how all love for this game seems to have poofed from the company's end. There's only an active community of about 150 players or so at the time I'm writing this, but I bet they'd see a renewed interest in the game by changing this one arbitrarily capped number.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off the hours I played are somewhat deceptive. I have a habit of pausing a game and leaving it running before I go to work. Or perhaps when company comes over for the weekend. So, for the majority of the so called hours played I was away from the computer.

This game seems on the surface to be a decent sequel to the fantastic original game made in 2004. Unfortunately once you scratch the surface you will find there isn't much to it.

The good.

The henchmen now have their own backstories and are unkillable, so investing in them is now more worthwhile.

The casinos actually distract the forces of justice. In the original game you would just dump money into it, and the forces of justice would either ignore it or worse yet shoot up the place and all your social minions are dead. In the end, it just wasted money and your minions more vulnerable by being outside the base. You were better off never making it.

So the primary fault of the original game is corrected. But sadly, too many things draw the game back for that to matter.

The neutral:

The research is much more streamlined, which can be good or bad depending on your view point. Personally, I could toss a coin.

Base building is the same as the original. Some rooms are unlocked via missions in the tutorial as you would expect.
And parts of the base are inaccessible due to the need to do research first. Which in turn is unlocked via the main story.
Not my cup of tea, but whatever.

Minion management is the same as the original. Except they train faster.

The Bad.

The minions are literally expendable. They are used up in missions which means they never come back. Sadly, when it comes to the forces of justice attacking, same thing. Your guys drop like flies and you don't care. In the original you would send muscle minions to protect them on missions because you need them alive to come home. Because if you lost your minions it hurt, a lot, a heck of a lot. It took time and effort to train them, so you cared if they died. If the forces of justice attack and kill half your guys, your base is out of commission for several hours, so take care of them.

Speaking of the forced of justice. In the original game you had to handle the heat effectively, too much heat and they send a troop of soldiers after you that kill half your minions. So you had to lay low and not do any missions until the heat wore off. After time the heat went down and you went back to work. So balancing the heat with the missions you needed to do, or the money you needed to steal was important. Too much heat was bad for business.

Here it is the opposite, it slowly builds up. And what happens if you get too much? Nothing much, they send some troops your way. Ones that are easy to kill. The best way to handle heat in this game is to not handle it at all. Build 30-40 traps between the casino and main base. Then let the heat go wild and the troops they send all end up dead before they get to you.

As for money, in the original you had to send minions to steal it, which gained heat. And you had to balance that with the heat management. In this one, it comes in automatically so you have basically and endless supply. Throughout 3/4 of the game or more my vault is always full.

All of the loot items in the game are now side stories. Which seems good on the surface, until you realize that if something is not associated with side story, it doesn't appear on the map. So no missions appear on the map at all unless their is a sides story active. In which case it pops up.

In the original game you would send scientists to do research and gather intel. As they gathered the intel more and more missions showed up, depending on your notoriety rating. Finding these missions was an interesting idea that I sorely miss.

But the sad part is outside of side missions the only missions there are either reduce heat, which is meaningless, and gathering money which is also meaningless. This means the game consists of doing side missions repeatedly.

This is bad because all the side missions all follow the same formula.

A. Do research.
B. Do missions on the world map.
C. Kill bad guys attacking your base.
D. Repeat one or more of the above.
E. Collect rewards (loot items, money, etc.)

Worse yet it is not just the loot items that fall subject to this formula. So does the main story, so do the henchmen missions, so do the super agents missions. In fact all missions do. Not only that, but some have multiple parts where you repeat the formula four or five times over and over and over again. And it plays just as monotonous as it sounds.

And here is the worst part. All of the campaigns are carbon copies of each other with different dialogue.

As for this dialogue. It is written and sound like a Saturday morning cartoon. Which is to say, juvenile, stupid and hammy.

In summary:

If you come home after a hard day of work with your head hurting to the point you can't think then this game might help you unwind as you sit back and relax. Or watch reruns of Gilligan's island.

But if you want a challenge, or something that will sink its hooks into you and not let go. This game is not for you. Get the original at Gog, with all of its flaws it is still a better game.

Oh and don't get me started on the DLC. They literally cut content to sell it separately. Most of the things in the in-genius pack were things that should have been in the research tree and unlocked their. But they decided to cut it and sell it separately. And it is pretty obvious too if you look at it.
Basically they are pissing on you without the decency to tell you it's rain.\t\t\t\t\t
