\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think the game delivers on what it is aiming for: an evil lair simulator. Designing your base is a breeze and I think the tutorial was enough to guide new players. Trying to immediately play the game with no tutorial will screw over some players but I think it offers a "sense of achievement" on your second run since you already know how to start a thriving base with limited resources. I think it has a solid base for a great game, however:

I don't have time to play games that takes a long time to get gratification especially ones that are filled with distractions and tedium that it feels like a second job.

This game just throws crisis after crisis and managing the world screen seems so.. tedious that I wish there is an auto governor or something that will auto manage heat and missions for me. Also, why isn't there an instant purchase for items that burned out? I'm so busy doing menial stuff like replacing burned items and choosing which schemes instead of actually managing or delegating tasks to my minions. It's too much busywork and the payback: cheesy/childish cut scenes. Good writing can appeal to both the young and the young at heart but the attempt on this game just falls flat.

Maybe if I'm not so busy and not swamped by lots of games I might finally finish my first run. I wish there is a your "mileage may vary" button.

If you're a kid with lots of time, you'll definitely enjoy this game. At least I've narrowed down the games that'll appeal more to me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give this game a neutral review ... as there's more to this than just a thumbs up. Also have played an additional 30 hrs on game pass.

Overall, this can be fun to play. It has a lot of content, the DLC isn't too bad (except for some of the latest one which I'll talk about later), and the story is good. After this however, there are a few things that keep this game from being truly great mainly being:

1. Repetitive/Tedious
This biggest problem with this game is the above. At the beginning, this is probably the best part of the game. Building up from nothing, getting new minion types, creating your base, the first time you set down rooms. This is all good and fun ...

Until you realise everything beyond this point (besides story) is essentially a grind (not always a fun one). The world map is 5 (6 with DLC) regions of the exact same thing, doing the exact same thing at every tier of the game (schemes are the same except sending your higher tier units and getting higher tier rewards). The units are all the same, except they're upgraded and better.

I've seen some instances of people struggling with combat, but on the highest difficulty it's easy ... but again a grind. You end up having 20-30 mercs + hitmen that just nuke anything that comes into your base, and constant waves coming in unless you manage your world management screen and keep consistent schemes going on at all times (another grind). Late game I just have the game on permanent fast forward while intruders die and I wait for tech to complete and quests, leading into another thing that's tedious - some of the quests. They are just TOO long.

For ex. there's one side quest that's from a free DLC I think, that allows you to set up 1 portal. I almost stopped doing this quest because it just became so boring and tedious to repeat these same objectives over and over for 5+ times to get 1 two way portal set up. This is the same case with recruiting henchmen. The same quests over and over 5 + times, doing the same thing on the world map, to get 1 henchmen. Then do it all over again and again. What I end up doing even on the harder difficulty is just stick with my 1 henchmen I get for free at the beginning and that's it.

2. Anti player mechanics

What I mean by these, are systems in the game that penalise the player, but do not affect the AI what so ever - main one being the new temperature mechanic that was introduced in the latest DLC. This should've never made it into the game ... it penalises you if you don't keep temperatures decent, and messes over your minions, but agents don't care about this at all. Also - even with upgrades from engineers, it still isn't enough. You also need so many power buildings that you have to build things super compact and close together, but it's impossible in this state to keep things proper temp - too hot, or too cold no in between. There's probably some other things I'm missing but this one is most recent in my mind.

3. The entire trap line/deception minions

Throughout my entire time I've played this game, on any difficulty, I've never used any of the traps or deception minions heavily. Used some for achievements, sometimes for fun, but you cannot rely on these at all to deal with people coming into your base. Agents either disable them, or just take minimal damage.

I know this is quite a long thing about things I don't enjoy - but I have gotten some good fun hours out of this game and do enjoy it overall. Again, story and visuals are great, there's some good moments, but be prepared to deal with some grindy and tedious mechanics on your way to world domination.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvil Genius 2 is what happens when the Sims and a Lex Luthor's fantasies come to light. In this game, you control super villains such as discount Dr Evil, some Soviet General from WWII, Hilary Clinton with a spider chair, and Black Marge Simpson.

The first thing I need to go over is the idea. I love it. It's fun to play as the bad guys for once. The humor for such stuff is pretty light roasted, but others claim the prequel had dark humor, which I would have loved to see in this game, though it doesn't affect the score.

The execution is exactly that. The devs pretty much executed my enjoyment with horrendous DLCs with thoughtless content as well as a complete lack and total disregard for efficiency.

Building is too restrictive. Spaces must be a set amount or the game will refuse to let you build. Items also decay in durability too quickly. Temperature got added in a paid DLC which you can't turn off, which causes morale loss and in many cases, is indiscriminately unbalanced. In many cases, you spend most of your time tirelessly clicking auto replace in case your fusion generator spoils itself in the power room and burns everything down because you built the room drunk and realized every fire extinguisher is on floor one. As a result, your NPCs scramble with simplistic fly against window pathfinding.

Pathfinding is miserable. I built two doors from the cover operation to the lair. One of them has a scanner and the other is trap hell. If the chose the scanner, route, they SHOULD be scanned, and sent back the way they came. However, the pathfinding is just terrible. The guards either randomly beat the intruders for no reason or take them on a tour through the entire base along the oversized corridor as if the devs did it in a sense of complete irony. Evil game design in a game about being evil? How despicable. My brilliant cherry on top was this story and the reason I'm writing this review. My genius got low on health, and I ordered him to rest in the lair to regen health. He sped away and then turned around to fight the agent I ordered him to retreat from. I then micromanaged him around the oversized corridor my guards use to give intruders a tour of the base. Then I clicked the chair, but Ivan went back towards the fight, so I clicked him halfway around the corridor next to the HAVOC machine. Since he was closer to the sanctum, I ordered him to take it easy. Ivan decided the best way was go back where he came from using his potato pathfinding built from Soviet quality labor. I actually lost that game because the devs have no idea how to program.

Design is just trash. The money scale is too small to feel immersive. The map is very bland with Japan and North Africa being the only regions which stands out in a plethora of copypaste icons, New York, London have one building each if you look hard enough, which really makes each region underwhelming aside from their super agents and respective color. The characters, however, are designed fairly well, but the minions and agents have repetitive design. Minions could use more customization, especially guards since they lazily tend to use the same character model at least from what I do notice. Agents could use more design other than reskinned uniforms. They missed an opportunity to give HAMMER soldiers Ushankas, which would have gave them character. Agents, I find too bland and uninspiring compared to super agents. Super agents and geniuses are fine, they can stay, as they are well designed characters.

If you choose the DLCs, however, you get even more trash designs such as uninspiring robots which cause more damage then they deal and a Frankenstein monster with the most lame design I've seen in any game.

Gameplay is pretty lackluster. Schemes are practically just subtracting resources for money to fill a base for more resources. Most of the time is spent on max speed and repairing damage from each invasion. Heat builds up for no reason on the world stage regardless of what you do or if you let it sit. There's no planning for schemes, just spending forever to research and train the right henchmen. Traps in hallways are completely useless save for the one time it decides to work. I can't imagine why every agent can hack every trap and every door. Even rogues can hack laser doors as fast as reinforced doors. Security is a massive slap to the face when it's just too easy to train guards and get rid of every agent with brute force. Bases aren't spared from game play since it seems you can't even think about security when there's a choke point on one end of one of the bases which everyone uses to bypass the casino.

In total, despite not finishing the story, which I usually do before writing reviews, I give this game a rating of 58/100. This game could have had potential, but cut content DLCs, poor design, terrible annoyances, and artificially increased difficulty ruined a lot of the fun and made this game more of a miserable job.\t\t\t\t\t
