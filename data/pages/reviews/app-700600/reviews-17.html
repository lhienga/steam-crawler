\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Not bad, but inherently flawed. (Buy on sale, if at all.)
While this game honestly looks great, has good dialogue, and feels nice to play and build with, it's gameplay loop is simply broken and encourages you to play in the least complex and fun way possible.
In short, Evil Genius advertises itself as a power fantasy where you get to play as an over the top 90's villain, committing crimes and defending your lair from agents. Supposedly you can counteract agents in several ways, by distracting, trapping, or fighting them outright, however the first two methods simply do not work.

For some ungodly reason, the developers decided to give agents a small chance to straight up kill your minions when distraction is attempted, then take photos of the people they've just killed and gain suspicion off of it. Thus, this can lead to cover operations quickly becoming slaughterhouses through no fault of your own where agents can get to max suspicion off the murders they committed, and call in bigger threats on you without ever even walking into your lair. (This renders deception completely pointless, as many bigger threats are outright immune to distraction.)

Then there's traps, one of the most fun ideas, which is unfortunately completely ruined the moment you exit the early game. Regardless of the complexity of your lair's layout, with very few exceptions, agents can disable each and every single trap between them and your lair, acting as if they simply weren't there at all. While agents are meant to have a "chance" to disarm, this chance rapidly becomes almost 90% past the early game, and skyrockets even further, to the point that even using tactics that directly hinder their "disarm chance" aren't effective enough to stop them from turning your entire defensive trap network off 100% of the time.

This just leaves fighting, which to the game's credit works fine. However, it is also incredibly easy to stack guards and turn every single threat the game can throw at you, even at max difficulty, into a cakewalk. It does not matter how strong your opponent is when you can turn every fight into a 1v50 in your favor with zero downsides.

Thus, this means that despite Evil Genius 2 having tons of options and interesting ways to build your base, the only actually effective way to defend it is to simply hire tons of guards and build one large armory. To nobody's surprise this is both extremely boring and removes all sense of tension or difficulty from the game, only highlighting it's other flaws.

This game is still fun for what it is, and has quite a lot of content on offer, but please keep in mind that it is inherently unbalanced, and so folks expecting an interesting challenge or trying to create interesting solutions for the game's problems will find nothing of worth in here.

This resulted in breaking my fifth metacarpal in my right hand—the hand I worked with, played games with, and used to carry my children to bed—the hand I desperately needed to ensure I could continue providing.

I could experience the joy and happiness of other people. I relaxed for 5 ♥♥♥♥ minutes listening to this music, long enough to realize that I would be okay.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvil Genius 2 is fun, but not as good as the original. I'm still salty about Shen Yu and Lord Kane being absent in EG2. They were my favorites. =( Apart from that, the graphics are updated, and the game got a new style/pain job. But I prefer the mechanics of the first game. The world domination screen in EG1 was much better. EG2 has some improvements that cut out some of the grinding. But the game just isn't as detailed and the story railroads you a bit more. There isn't as much freedom to do things the way you want to. As a guy who prioritizes mechanically fun games over fancy graphics, I get more out of the first Evil Genius. Plus the mods that work with the first game add a lot of replay-ability. EG2 is a money-grab that uses the nostalgia of the first game. That said, EG2 is still fun, and the new story is good. Emma is really fun, I love the idea of a disgruntled spymaster seeking vengeance on the world. I also like the new henchmen, but the DLC is still to darn expensive for what you get. Still, I recommend it if you got the time and cash to burn.\t\t\t\t\t
