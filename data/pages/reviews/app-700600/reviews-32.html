\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't recommend buying this game. It has many improvements over the first, but for every 2 improvements that were made, it has a downgrade. I think they tried to change too much. I can't point to one single thing that makes this game bad, but I just kept having this feeling that I should go and play the original instead. I don't want Rebellion to look at the response that this game got and cancel any further sequel. I believe the series is in capable hands, this game made very good improvements over the original, like the fast forwarding that you can use to skip the time when you are just training troops, and the new system for how henchmen work. But Rebellion also sabotaged the world map, now any minion that goes on a heist will never return, which works against the traits system they added, and the heists don't feel like you are taking any risk (something the original did perfectly). I don't think this is a bad game it just has many systems that are dead weight and work against each other. I tried very hard to love this game, but failed.

If you are a fan of the Game Dungeon series I would give this game the "Try Again" award. It does so many things right, but so many others so wrong. I don't hate this game, the problem is I see so much potential that has been buried under tedious mechanics. For that it also deserves the "Fizzled Potential" award. This game is a gold mine on the wrong mountain.

This is my opinion: This game would be great if the researching was faster (the research system was better than the original), had the original world map and heist system, and the progression overall was more like the original (which felt more rewarding).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvil Genius 2 is a fairly faithful adaptation of the first game. Perhaps too much so. Overall it's a good game, though it is held back by trying to emulate the first game to the point of carrying over some of the original's weaknesses. Additionally the game lacks some qualify of life improvements that I feel would have been easy to add. I do think you'll get your money's worth, especially if you get it on sale like I did. However the game's shortcomings can be frustrating at times, and the game kind of lacks replay value.

Building your base, dealing with agents, and much of the humor of the game are quite good. All of the islands are big enough that you'll have plenty of room to get creative with your base layout. The traps are fairly intuitive to set up and play off each other well (Using a fan to blow an agent into a pool of sharks for example). Sadly, the traps seem to take a backseat in this game, as you'll be relying on your muscle minions to do most of the killing. This is one aspect that I think EG1 did better, although this does avoid the headache of having minions getting caught in your own traps (which was a constant irritation in EG1).

The tagging system is alright, though I do wish the player could take a more direct role in base defense. Right now the most you can do beyond tagging targets, is to activate your henchmen's abilities. Overall I do wish there was more to do in regards to base interaction, as there are long stretches of time in which you won't have much to do really. This is especially true if you pursue many of the side stories, as all of them require researching 1 or 2 things, which just means que up the research and wait 5 minutes or so. I definitely feel there should be more the player could be doing in this time.

The world screen is a bit of an improvement compared to EG1 in my opinion. Some people have expressed irritation that minions don't return from missions even if they are successful, though I greatly prefer this approach to the random deaths mid mission that could cause a mission to fail that EG1 had. Minions can be a pain to replace, especially once you get up to tier 3 minions, but this can be mitigated simply by building a larger training room to train more at a time.

I really enjoyed the Super Agents, though I feel their endings were a bit lackluster. They all just give up after a point and let you take them prisoner. Then you can for no explained reason, recruit them as henchmen. There is no dialogue explaining why they are suddenly willing to switch sides. I also find it annoying that they all survive being executed (except Blue Saint, he never showed up again after I "killed" him with knockout gas).

The biggest annoyance I encountered was the lack of a mass sell option. For instance you'll need to upgrade your gold vault a few times in the game. Even if you don't relocate it to a more secure place in the base, you'll need to replace the gold trays with lockers and then the electronic safes later on. Odds are good you'll have dozens or in my case ~200 gold lockers suddenly in need of replacing when upgrading. I had to manually click on each one and then click sell... This is an issue when mass upgrading anything: beds, lockers, generators, etc. A way to sell items in bulk is kind of standard in most management games, and I don't think it would have taken that long to program one.

Finally, the dlc is fairly dull. The temperature system it introduces is a nightmare to manage as the heating/cooling devices do not function in a way that makes sense. The ocean regions are annoying to manage as every mission in them results in a lockdown if you don't cancel them early. The missions aren't even that good. Instead of getting you cash or items directly, like in normal regions, ocean missions provide buffs to the regions they are connected to (increasing passive money generation, reducing heat buildup). The buff isn't that strong, and managing these regions is more of a hassle than it's worth. Odds are you'll just ignore them aside from lowering their crazy heat build up. Robot minions sound cool on paper, but the fact that they all explode on death makes them garbage. I built the 10 robot guards as part of the side story, and had half my base burn down when a soldier killed one. Seriously never use these guys.

I played through the game twice, first time I disabled the dlc to get the vanilla experience, and I'm glad I did. The dlc adds more hassle than fun.

Overall, I'd say the game is worth a play through despite its issues. Though I would highly encourage you to buy it on sale. I would also recommend you NOT enable the dlc, at least on an initial play through.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile this game does scratch the itch of the first game (and has more polish) and also a bit of dungeon keeper, particularly initially when unlocking rooms and minions, that does turn into a bit of a repetitive grind. This is most evident when you are developing the dooms day weapon. There are just endless “do this and then do this and then this”, with tasks there only to prolong the experience and hardly connected to a cohesive logic and with no way of being anticipated. It’s like getting to the top of a mountain only to see another taller one and only guessing if there is one beyond that. I had to google how long to beat because I had no idea what stage of the game I was even in.
The major frustration (without spoilers) was when my perfectly balances minion force was just decimated and I had to throw basic minions at the problem until it died and left me wondering what the point of balancing my minions was. Luckily this came late in the game and I was finished shortly thereafter.
So much potential but the tasks/”story” means it get a meh at best.\t\t\t\t\t
