\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you enjoyed the first Evil Genius, you'd definitely enjoy this one. It is a super fun and addictive base building and management game. If you decide to pick it up, here are some quick veteran tips to enhance your experience:

-Cameras don't do anything unless you designate it with a Distract/Capture/Kill zone. You unlock these zones through research in the same order as above. To designate a zone, click on your System Menu (Bottom Left Corner) -> Security Zone.

-Check in on your Optional Objectives frequently. (System Menu -> Optional). Collecting the rewards for them can give you gold when you really need it.

-Don't forget about the Side Stories, especially the special loot ones. They are super useful and fun to have in your base. There are also a ton of them and it's easy to get lost in base building and the main quest (System Menu -> Side Stories -> Choose one and activate it.)

-Next research tiers are unlocked by doing the mission for the next tier of science minion.

-Don't be afraid to build temporary rooms. Gold is not a finite resource and you get more than enough space to expand. Start by building tiny rooms that have what you need and then just rebuild it more to your liking as you become more flush with wealth.

-Intel is an important resource that is generated by your control room or by interrogating prisoners. I recommend doing the latter a lot as it generates Intel faster and doesn't require a bunch of minions active in your control room.

-Lastly, if you own the Oceans Campaign Pack, do the Side Mission: Nuts and Bolts as early as you can. It unlocks a new minion and new type of research that is best to have from the beginning.

That's it. Buy a swivel chair, get a cat and channel your inner villain.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've hit my last straw in this game when I lost several hours of work and progress to yet another soft-lock/bug. From the game sending enemies you are required to interrogate in through back entrances into hostile zones and dying to enemies starting fires and the people who should be putting them out getting stuck on nothing, the game has a lot that can make you lose progress.

The first main problem you run into is tedium and repetition. The gameplay gets utterly repetitive and with the way furniture and items are placed the base looks barren with limited options for creativity, decoration, or customization. Lacking both gameplay variety and the lack of decoration combined with clunky and weird functional item setups, there isn't a lot to do.

The second issue is the constant maintenance you need to run on the world map. Unlike the last game, minions are not pieces on the global board. Instead you send minions off for one-off missions that need to be replaced. You have to constantly check back over and over again in available areas for missions you actually want to take (earn money or reduce heat). If it is an insta-complete intel mission or something you don't want clogging the way then you are simply screwed and have to deal with the red notification staying on screen.

The third issue is bugs, lack of polish and balance, and soft-locks. There are quite a few points in side missions where you can become gimped or lose out on rewards due to bugs. For one, capturing and converting henchmen doesn't always work. Sometimes they merely evaporate despite you following everything you were supposed to do to recruit them step-by-step sometimes they do not. Reloading to a previous save does not help, some henchmen simply seem to be unrecruitable in random games though this clearly isn't intended. Additionally, sometimes you are required to interrogate someone who arrived on your island in the step right after it tells you to kill them, this can soft-lock you requiring you to reload an older save. God help you if the extremely limited autosave slots have already been overwritten (happens a lot). Capturing everyone regardless is severely detrimental to progress and the abrupt, unannounced arrival of mission-critical enemies makes it a pain in the ass to manually gimp the security to capture them. Your only choice is to nonlethally capture everyone to avoid this or pay intense attention to the game's arrivals constantly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
Visually the games look really good,
Nice voice acting
Nice story/theme in general.
Imprisoning enemies and turning them to your side is brilliant.
Everything I wanted in terms of resource management was there (Expending my base, units, upgrades, exploring).


Cons:
Traps feel like a big part of the game, but the agent (Enemies) sabotage them, so they are useless (Please prove me wrong so I can go back and keep playing).
The side stories are repetitive, (I saw this game had many paid DLCs, not sure if this will help, and if so then it should be included in the main product).
The units variation between the Builders / Scientists / Guards / Entertainers is good, but each of them has its own variations (4 types of each), feels like the game could not find good enough reasons to do this extra separation, it made the game longer, and more repetitive, let alone the science tree which was not a fun experience thanks to that.

I purchased this game at a 70% off deal and it was worth it.
After 10- 15 hours, I just wanted to win the main story, so I can move to another game, once the game crashed and I lost some progress I quit.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is straight up bloated to increase play time. Everything is more expensive, minions have salaries now, you're lair is bigger but so are the items, everything requires more power, and electric generators are bigger and provide less electricity. In the first game, a million gold is a good amount that will last you a while, but the second game has me burn through that in 30 minutes in just minion salary. Research takes forever, you can only research a single thing at once, and nearly every objective needs to research something. Agents are meaningless with the way that you can set up defenses and place guards. You have 4 floors to build on, but I only needed the first floor for everything and the second floor was power generation. Seriously, you need so much power. The guard trainer costs the same electricity as a computer in the control room, and the highest container for your gold costs 5 electricity and only holds $10,000 (remember that salaries cost me $1,000,000 every 30 minutes). The highest power generator gives you 48 power when so many things need 20 (which seems to be the highest). Power generators and other things generate heat, so you need AC units which cost electricity. If things are too hot or too cold, minion stats are affected but not agents thus removing any trade off with that (I also had at least one minion complain it was too hot and too cold at the same time, but that may have been a glitch). No minions come back from the world map, and completing jobs on the world map has changed. You can't just send all the minions you need for a mission, but rather you need to set up a criminal network. The problem is sometimes you need different levels of criminal networks which requires you to send another wave of minions to raise the level of the network, so a mission requiring a level 4 network requires you to manually send 5 waves of minions and waiting for them to arrive each time. I had fun when I was doing things in the game, but there was just so much down time. I definitely went to make dinner or shower multiple times so a research would finish while I was away.

tl;dr Game is fun when you play, but there is a lot of down time to either build up cash or wait for research.\t\t\t\t\t
