\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat purchase on sale.

The good: Models are fantastic, fun little animations. The jokes and cutscenes are corny but cute. Multiple levels to your base! Building is easy and fun. Can unlock things to automate micro. Feels very Evil Genius.

The bad: With all the DLCs it feels a little overloaded - too many mechanics that its unclear why you'd want to do them initially (submarines - neat? loot - okay?). It feels simultaneously like it goes slow on the highest speed, but also too fast to pay attention to minor details and like you're maybe losign control.. World map is worse than EG1 (seriously its really confusing, little tiny icons!). The automated stealing is nice but it could have done with some more refinement (an option to change how much heat vs money is generated for example). The UI has a lot of menus and submenus and its kind of hard to navigate cleanly - feels like a bit of a chore.

At $4.24 AUD for the deluxe edition with all the DLC its a steal. All up, I'd probably be disappointed if I paid full price for this. Its a worthy successor to EG1 - EG1 was a fun but a bit of a slog to complete later game and held up by its charm. Its pretty much the same here. A lot of fun but some tedium.

Still, $4.24! You can't go wrong.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had a good time!
This is Dungeon Keeper with a Superhero twist, but thankfully unlike the first Evil Genius the game has been streamlined so you can focus more on the fun than the finance.
The DLC being so thick and fast is annoying, but the other complaints people had about this game seem polished out: no real bugs I could find. The Evil Geniuses themselves are full of character, but they play very similarly, basically just adding bonuses and making some aspects of your lair aesthetically different. This is not the kind of game you play for the 'story'. The writing is amusing but it all boils down to silly puns and a little movie at the end showing how you took over the world if you win.

BUT you will love this game if you like emergent gameplay. All the little characters (minions, tourists, agents...etc) all have routines and behaviors that you can effect but not control. Some people find this difficult to enjoy, but I like making the orders and seeing them carried out with your allies bustling around like living people rather than pawns. Minions get tired, bored, hungry. They take a seat if they've been standing a long time. They talk to other minions. It's very lively!
It's also silly, but with an edge. People bounce around and have goofy expressions, but there is a LOT of death in this game. No blood (it's like a classic 60s spy caper that way) but people do get eaten by sharks, disintegrated, shot, exploded and so forth. Nothing explicit but thankfully it does lean into the wackiness of the power fantasy. You can execute a minion at any time for any reason. It even improves the speed at which the others work!

And there's strategy involved but not obscure strategy. You always know how much money you have, how much you're spending, how much power you have and need, how many minions, which minion is operating what...etc.
So you are basically finding the 'fires' and putting them out. You need power to keep your worldwide operation going so you can steal the money to keep the whole program moving. You need space for your stuff, but space costs money too. You need to keep capturing people to learn their secrets and advance.
So your continuing job is to refine and expand, and it is rewarding. You can move anything at any time (thank goodness) and your seldom boxed into a complete corner unless you really do fly off the handle without thinking.
This is the kind of game if everything goes downhill it's usually your fault. You didn't build in an order than made sense (and ton of stuff that needs power before a power plant, things that cost money before things that make money) or you didn't pay enough attention to the do-gooders that will come knocking on your door as soon as you start to pull schemes throughout the world.

So overall, a nice looking, well optimized game! Don't worry about the DLC and dive in if you don't mind what amounts to the same long-form game in several different places. Each genius is amusing and does have so distinctions (each has their own inner sanctum and method of execution) and each location, although you are always making a casino and a lair, is still different enough to be an intriguing new challenge.\t\t\t\t\t
