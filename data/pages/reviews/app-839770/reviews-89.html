\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a hesitant yes from me. This is one of these good news bad news games.

Good news:

The free aiming system adds a lot. At first I thought it was going to be a gimmick, but once you learn to read how the system displays information and learn the value in targeting different body parts you wind up making a lot of meaningful choices and getting a lot of gameplay out of it.

The class combination idea is excellent. The game doesn't have that many classes, but every character gets two. In addition they get three unique masteries which can include the ability to grab proficiencies form other classes. Want someone who uses the heavies jetpack to fly on top of a building where they can deploy an engineers turret while taking shots with a sniper rifle? You got it. There's a lot of uniqueness to the performance of different soldiers.

The geoscape level gives you good reason to run several squads. They need recovery time between missions, even if just to rest when they're not injured. The aircraft are only so fast and missions can pop up worldwide, there's no guarantee of them being in range of any one squad. Juggling a bunch of different squads keeps the gameplay fresher than having one A-team that does everything.

The mission variety feels decent. While every mission boils down to one of essentially three things (Go there, grab that, kill that) there's enough difference between mission types that they feel like they reward different approaches. Some feature infinite reinforcements and are best served by a fast squad that does the job and gets out. Others require you to clear the map and benefit more from something slow and careful. They also offer decent trade offs between grabbing extra loot and winning that can make you question if it's reaaaally worth going for that last box or if you should retreat with what you have.

A lot of thought has gone into giving the aliens unique powers that go beyond "Have more hitpoints, do more damage". There's already a lot of this in existing titles with psionic and melee enemies doing special stuff, but this at least doubles the sorts of weird attack you could be subjected to compared to other games in its genre. I'd rather not spoil them but it's nice to see.

The willpower mechanic works pretty well. All special abilities use willpower. Seeing allies die uses willpower. Some attacks drain willpower. Having a body part take severe damage drains willpower (especially the head). If you hit 0 then you miss a turn fleeing and taking cover. Also all psionics are based on "Is my willpower higher than my targets" rather than a percentage chance which makes critical moments like mind control feel less RNG. Conversely you can skip a turn to regain willpower and some abilities offer other ways to restore it.

Bad news:

There is a lot of not fun micromanagement to get to the good bits. At the end of a mission you get a button to buy back all of the ammo you fired during the level, but if you don't have the resources there and then you'll have to do it manually weapon by weapon once you do. Doing things requires the right balance of tech, materials, and food - trading one for the other means flying to an outpost and trading things they don't have for things they do - but each outpost has limited supplies and replenishes every so often so you'll need to do a circuit of them every now and again to do basic building and supply.

The documentation in the game is so-so. Some abilities, equipment and modifications won't work exactly as advertised. Some critical features of them aren't mentioned and you discover them by trial and (mostly) error.

The game doesn't autosave each turn, which is for the best since it'd be tempting to save scum every death given how tedious hiring and equipping a new recuit is (trade resources for the right types, queue up their leg armour, body armour, head armour, weapon, and equipment to be built seperately) and it seems like the odd misfortune is part of the game rather than intended to be avoided. The thing is: Sometimes the game crashes, or gets stuck on an infinite enemy turn. It's not a lot, you'll only see it once every ten hours of play or so, but if it happens near the end of a long level it's frustrating.

The interface lets itself down in places and there are a few times that the game seems to trip over its own feet. The first of these you'll probably encounter is that its realistic bullet physics are based on where the character holds the gun, so the heavy weapons users who start with jetpacks are also the ones who can't take advantage of high ground because holding their gun at waist height makes them most likely to shoot their own cover if they're angling downwards. Also just little things like the game automatically switching off a character every time they run out of action points, even if they can overwatch for 0 cost or the last thing you did was activate an ability that reduced the cost of their next shot to 0.

Overall:

There's more pros than cons and it's taken some risks doing a lot of worthwhile and interesting things. It's a shame that they've failed to nail some of the core stuff, but the bells and whistles? Very ringy, very whistley.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really good game. Good concept, mechanics, and storyline. As someone who had played XCOM series before, I found this game to be excitingly challenging to play. Aiming mechanic makes me felt more justified when it come to missed shots. Increased in management such as ammunitions and base (both personnel, construction, and defense) also makes the game more challenging, making you to think more carefully of what to do and spend on.

Although, I would admit that this game still had some flaws, such as the lack of mission rewards, adaptive difficulty seemed to develop too quick, and little to no passive generation of resources. Late game can turn into a struggle if things did not goes well in the middle. DLC contents requires these resources and extra time to explore, time with the campaign will not provide more since there is no mechanics to buy more time and the adaptive difficulty. Soundtrack, while it is atmospheric for the geoscope part, it isn't in combat, perhaps makes the combat tad too dreadful and stressful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros: Good plot, Class combinations for tactical combat, every choice matters which leads to difficult but rewarding decision making, visuals are great, fairly balanced tactical combat mechanics.

Cons: Time limited gameplay with no way to gain any time back is stressful especially with how long the campaign can be, lacks modding support, the variety of enemies/map types get a little old even with mutative enemies, AI hangs at some points causing game reloading, Alien capturing is fun but can be tedious as ♥♥♥♥ for the larger targets and there should be some sort of prisoner system for paralyzing human targets. Ramping up enemies makes creating new groups a tad difficult in the late game.

Overall I'd say its a great game to sink a lot of hours in but there needs to be a way to increase the time you have before losing the game, at least make it possible to have a near never ending campaign just for fun. Also be nice to have the per-turn in combat auto-saves that Xcom has. I will almost always restart a mission if a squad member dies to the point I get frustrated and say nah screw that mission. 8/10 More please.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has the potential to be excellent for the right player. I thought I might be the right player because I loved the XCOM games and this is very similar; however, it misses the mark. While XCOM allowed genuine progression towards stronger and more effective weapons relative to enemy strength, PP does not. Enemy strength seems to grow proportionally, or maybe even a bit in the favor of the Pandorans, with regard to weapons research and development. Stronger forms of Pandorans pop up far faster than new weapon types can be researched and acquired. Even the pentultimate weapons of the Ancients are a letdown and still do not have the killing power they should.
Phoenix Point is also too forceful in its storyline. There are many things the player may wish to do, such as visit havens, conduct raids, and acquire new soldiers from different havens but there is scarcely time to do so with all the Pandoran and other faction attacks. The idea of putting a time limit on the game in the form of the humanity survival probability clock is a horrible idea. XCOM did it too but at least in that game there was a way of setting the clock back. It just makes everything so busy you can't really take the time to play the game 'freestyle'. Also no berserker class for the PP operatives!? So if you're not friends with DoA faction you have no real berserkers... that just sucks.\t\t\t\t\t
