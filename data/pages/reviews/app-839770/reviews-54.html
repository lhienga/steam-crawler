\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSomebody called this game "soulless" and I think this sums it up nicely. And it's not because the game is difficult (and oh my, it really is challenging).

Can't really say why I didn't like it, because I really wanted to like it. I loved original UFO games back in the day, liked the reboots too. But here...

The visuals are great. The mechanics of the game are interesting, better than those in X-Coms in many ways. The faction system is an interesting addition too, although it's not very enticing.

Maybe it's the story and the setting? There's something missing in that area, it's just not good enough to draw you in and want to continue. The world is basically lost and you're trying to reclaim it, but there isn't much to motivate you.

After the initial good impression, the game quickly turns into a grindy experience and encounters quickly get boring, if not irritating. It's not a bad game, but yeah... "soulless".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLong time gaming Vet here - Boredom comes easily from many games with "amazing plots" because, I've been there done that, so I tend to look for substance and something different.

Anyway, that said, why the thumbs up for Phoenix Point?
(Comparing to X-com too)

At first, it feels stale - The sound effects and combat feel boring, compared to X-com. There I said it.
But
After leveling up characters
and
Getting other types of Characters from factions + research things get cray cray.

Also, it's the opposite feeling to X-com since the enemies don't feel threatening at first but keep "evolving" and becoming more brutal and annoying AF. So you get this feeling of dread every time the new evolved batch comes out and start to wonder if you need to adapt your team.

Creating "new character classes" is no joke. It's awesome and combat TOTALLY transforms.
I won't give it away, because I prefer surprises, so just hold out if you want interesting combat options and team formation.
There are times when you feel like you have too many options, nahhh I love it. I hate being rail roaded into "charge in" or "this alien is too dangerous up close" - Screw that, I got a guy just for that.

Aim - This is actually awesome. You truly appreciate it when you've bitten off more than you can chew and suddenly feel outmatched by a beast with a large weapon standing a few feet away. You shoot the arm and suddenly, it can't use the 2 handed weapon (beware, this can happen to you!- Pistols people!)

World Map - Far superior to X-Com and also tiring if you're trying to be efficient. There are SO many options and strategies.
Essentially you unlock bases rather than place them (which was annoying at first , but now I get how it adds to planning)
The various 'currencies' and food you have to juggle and manage because opening new bases is expensive and kitting them out to serve your needs is too.
There are different ships to ferry you around (again, you gotta wait for research and factions) but it's quite exciting finding a ship that transports 8 people rather than 5 or 6...but then finding it's so slow, meaning you have to place them in good locations ready to defend the Havens.

Havens - You lose the game if too many die, so base unlocking is paramount.
Diplomacy is ok if you like that sort of thing and there are lots of decisions to make which effects what soldiers you get and what research you can share etc.

So I'm playing slow and not completed a run through yet. It's the sort of game you can play for a week, then stop and pick up a bit later to scratch the TBS itch. Otherwise I think it will feel "samey" - That might just be my subjective bs though.

If you are a patient person and want some interesting combat, this is for you.

Resources management may be described as a constant crysis. You never have enough, you always should keep some dough in the pot just for a case of emergency, and all the possible toys in your arsenal are just lying as unrealised projects literally for dosens of hours - till the arrival of some lucky star (meaning - big bunch of craft materials).

So yea, I do love a good game challenge and I enjoyed this game a looooot. Worth noticing, I met only one significant bug in my 44 hours of playing it (unsaved mission results ---> no rewards). But it's just no fun to be banged against the wall over and over again, with no chance nor ability to capitalize on your in-game achievements and feel satisfied at least at some moments.
