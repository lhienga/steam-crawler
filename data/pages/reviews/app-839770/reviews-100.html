\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a fan of X-Com and when I saw Phoenix Point, I was so excited about the new tactical mechanics. But after finishing the game, I can't recommend it. It's long, grindy, choices don't matter, soldiers are impersonal, and it lacks a sense of epicness.
The lore is really good. But that's about it.
To me, Phoenix Point took the fun out of X-Com.

After 20h of playing the game I was bored: a lot of missions with little impact on the story, allegiance mechanics that lead to no real benefits (it s basically some lore), and the combats seemed repetitive.

After 40h of playing I had found a way, mixing classes, to deal unlimited damages in close combat and I was desperate to end as fast as possible all those useless battles. There are no meaningful upgrades on the weapons and no upgrades at all on the armors. My teams kept the basic armor they had at the beginning through the whole game (while monsters get huge upgrades in heath and/or armor), and for the weapons just get some shotguns and the 2 unique weapons that have no need of ammo through the DLC's missions. It's enough to kill the boss.

There are limitations like you cannot move people from a base to another one without using a airplane. Why not. The idea is that you have to manage your soldiers to defend your bases appropriately. But. Your gear for exemple has no such restriction. So before a mission, I removed the gear from the last team that went on mission to put the gear on the next team that was about to go hot... even if they were 2 continents apart.
Why would I be doing that? Because the game depends only on your Research, I would prefer to invest everything in it then to buy a pair of pant for my heavy (which is about the same price as building a Research Center, go figure).

Took me 68h to finish the game. The last 20h were spent waiting to finish Researches that would unlock the end of the game (with no impact on the gameplay whatesoever). I am a completionist (so I thought) but I got so bored with the game I didn't want to explore anymore, didnt want to look for the shiny weapons of the DLC which upgrade is basically to have no need of ammo. I just wanted out of this game. Finish it as quickly as possible.

So if you are looking for a X-Com 2 type of experience, sorry but as of now, Phoenix Point is not it.
This game is for people who enjoy tactical games, and are not bored by doing impactless missions, over and over again.
For some satisfying management or sense of accomplishment, just go back to X-Com 2 :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWriting this, 13 hours into the game. Restarted once, and I feel like I barely scratch the surface.

While the game itself is a mixed feeling and I understand why some people leave a negative review, my experience is more positive than the negative one. Here is my thought in no particular order.

First of all, this is like a modern XCOM but without the XCOM hassles on the tactical layer. The realistic bullet really lives up to its name. No more 95% chance at point-blank and still miss. That itself already made that this is not an XCOM game. If my shot is missed, it was not because the RNG is rigged, but I accept the fact that the aiming angle was difficult or it was too far that the bullet spreads. However, if it actually hits the target, it feels satisfying.

The game has a "doomsday clock" that keeps ticking the longer you spend time in the Geoscape. And I could not seems to reverse or halt it. This itself is not bad, but it gives me a little bit of anxiety. I have no knowledge about the game, I don't know the meta, I'm not sure if I'm doing it right. What if I already messed up at the beginning? Just thinking about it makes me feel tired. I wish if there is a "practice mode" or mod to disable the clock, I would be grateful for learning purpose before actually jump into the real game.

The game is a little bit repetitive compared to XCOM at the beginning, You started with 5 squads (or 6 if you already recruited one more soldier) and that will be your whole team for the first game (or maybe it will be your team for the entire game, I don't know). Compared to XCOM that you have to send different squads into a different mission.

The game has more management than the modern XCOM. You actually need to bring a magazine to use a reload. And you need to manufacture one or you will run out of them in the field and become useless. New recruit usually does not have anything. Yes, they are 'naked', no armor, no guns. You have to build the equipment for them manually which may take time. Except if you recruit it directly from the faction's haven. Although it is more expensive than if you recruit a plain soldier.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaven't completed a campaign, but I enjoyed the experience so far.
What I like:
- Ballistic system is certainly better system compared to traditional dice roll in XCOM.
- Geoscape is more complex with multiple base, squad, and aircraft which you need to micromanage
- Classes and inventory system are flexible and push you to be more creative, although the skills are not really flashy
- I love the overall thematic of the story and how they presented the cutscenes. Good way to present the story in budget

What I don't like:
- Lack of level variations. Most places feel similar
- Lack of enemy variations. The variations are only presence in what weapon they bring, or armor in some part.
- Lack of excitement. Due to lack of variations I mentioned above, combined with mediocre VA, BGM, and SFX

Overall, it's not as bad as most people said IMO. Sure it has flaws, but I think you should give it a try if you're fan of the genre. There are 2 more DLCs that will be released, with addition of more QoL improvement in the future so I can only see the game keep getting better

edit: Finished a campaign, finally! Do I change my mind about the game? not really, I still think it's a fun game. Late game can be quite grindy and boring, but I will still recommend it to the fan of the genre\t\t\t\t\t
