\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhoenix Point is the premier tactical game. The realistic ballistic model they talk about in their promotions is no joke, and the free aim system is remarkable. This is not to say it is without fault, on rare occasions shots that seemed to have a 100% chance to hit ended up missing, and non standard projectiles like grenades are much more likely to hit something stupid on their path. Given that, this system is far superior to % hit chances. When I make a shot in Phoenix Point I am much more aware of the odds. If I have a sniper at point blank range, there is a 100% chance that I can hit not only the enemy, but the specific body part I need to disable for my guy to live. My favorite part is how this works with the destructible terrain. I can spot an enemy on the other side of the wall, then blow up the wall to make the shot. better still, I can use a high powered cannon or sniper to go through both wall and enemy, or multiple enemies.
My second favorite thing has to be the 4AP/turn with partial AP mechanic allowing your soldiers (and the enemy) to do much more intelligent moves. For example, take a step out of cover, use 3AP to shoot, move back with the other half of the first AP. Or use a full AP to move into the open, 2AP to shoot, then the last to continue moving forward towards the enemy. The downsides here are that Heavies will not lift their weapons over obstacles so them having the ability to jet pack to roofs is kind of dumb if they cant shoot at people below them.

10 JUL 2022 Update

The Infested Skies (IS) DLC ruined the game for me. I guess you can just play without it which is what I will have to do now. I have had a few re-playthroughs with the IS DLC now. Each time, no matter how many aircraft I team up with, the sudden emergence of BADDONS and insane enemy sky weapons just completely obliterates your game, instantly.

So there is that, it is unbalanced. A broader point is that, unlike other really good strategy games, there is no attempt to build the DLC and especially the IS DLC into the story arc as you progress into the later game. It is guesswork trying to prioritise your builds in order to prepare for the late game IS DLC.

All in all, I still think this is a great game. BUT it seems to need a Campaign Mode with a much better story arc and then a separate Challenge Mode. So hardcore elite gamers can muck about with impossible bloody air attacks and the rest of us can enjoy a balanced DLC that is sensibly integrated into the story arc and late game progression.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPROS
Phoenix Point is a quality tactical management game allowing you to make decision about basic resourcing and tactical control of the battle field. There are a variety of unit types that can be further specified for unique combat capabilities or made into a well rounded unit for all occasions. The DLC add lots of new missions and options that affect your relationships, resources, and science. The difficulties in this game does require you to change tactics and become more punishing. So far this game also has a lot of replay value. I'm on my third play through and each one has different challenges based on my decisions to help or raid. You can easily put a few hundred hours into this game.

CONS
It's not without issues. For me there are three
1. Heavies need to be rebalanced, they don't have anywhere near the usefulness of the other classes until late game grenadiers.
2. The camera is a bit frustrating on the combat map (inverse controls when you use the mouse, until you click on a unit. Then it controls as expected. This can make selecting units and setting overwatch frustrating. The free camera option doesn't fix this.
3. Menus block you setting overwatch. This issue is amplified by the camera controls.
4. Enemies to have mist seem to take a long time (sometimes up to 2 minutes) to decide how to move/act on their turn. (This might be an issues with my computer specs)

OVERALL
8/10 - A good value for a game, with high replay-ability, and only a few issues.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith over 3000 hours in XCOM 2 and countless mods played to make it harder, I still end up following the same linear path to the game end using the same tactics of overwatch and pathing my soldiers behind each other. So I loaded up PP with no great expectation that it would be radically different and oops, did I just get my backside handed back to me, on the lowly vetran level? Oh yes, this game is hands down a better tactical experience. It will keep getting better too.

Yes PP still has issues, like the strategy layer where it's easy to get stuck in mid game wondering what to do next, unlike in XCOM 2 (Firaxis version) which more or less holds your hand and walks you through (Yes, I know Bradford, you tell me this every single time I get here). And yes they still have bugs and are still building a library of DLC to meet many a players expectations. Where as I'd prefer them to firstly improve the soldier customisation options, bring in more voice actors and link the information up on screen, rather than clicking through various menus to find that bit of info that makes the difference. Yet for all the little and missing bits this game is a gem that is quietly getting polished. The back story is rich , really rich with some nice comical touches, and oh my Lord, when the modders get their hands on this...

On a slightly different note, this game loads faster and runs cooler on my ageing laptop then XCOM 2 does. This wasn't something I expected and it's a nice little bonus.

So I recommend PP, even though it's still being developed, but let's not forget that XCOM (Firaxis version) wasn't without it's issues too; like the dreaded 95% chance to hit with a shotgun to the face, meaning it's a guaranteed miss, or the sniper shot fired at 90 degrees to the target (What the what!), or the poison spit that hits your grouped units after passing through a wall and the flooring from below (Oh you are taking the ...). I've not had any BS from the tactical layer in PP after 322 hours of play. My shots go where they are aimed, even if it's a miss, so my expectations are not dashed on the rocks of RNG.

Now if I can only remember that line of sight doesn't mean line of shot and that my sniper can shoot that Arthron through a 1cm gap between cover half the map away. Screw it let's take the shot. Holy crap it worked... Oh yeah, whose da man. Whoop whoop!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good game. 90% of this game is masterfully done, 10% was mostly ok.

Comparing the tactical combat to the more recent X-com games, this game fixed everything that bugged me about X-com combat. No grappling hooks though.

I found the global management / base building interesting. It did a good job of making me feel pressured.

The team management was an improvement over similar x-com games. Their choices on character development and global management options made it feel actually necessary, desirable, and not unmanageable to have multiple functional tactical teams.

What would I change? The lead-in from the tutorial into the main game was a little lacking. Could do with a bit more hand-holding into the main operations. And the ramp from the mid-game into the end-game also was a bit lacking. Neither ruins the game, but it makes it very unlikely to be able to make correct decisions without multiple play-thru experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed through the game once without mods with the DLC and it is at best a 6/10. Too many ambushes, way too many broken/boring AF base defenses (they either need a total rework or removed). With Sheep-y's Modnix mod manager and Mad234269's "Assorted Adjustments" mod, the game is a lot more fun and it is a 7.5/10.

I like:
- Have to steal/ally with other factions to get better tech
- One wrong move with a couple mind controllers on the field and there goes your team.
- Free Aim
- Body Part damage makes a difference
- If you don't expand and research you are going to get railroaded
- Have to change tactics when fighting vs humans
- Its a great game to have running when sitting on a Keepstar in Eve Online (now you see why I have so many hours).

I don't like:
- All the ambushes (esp outside the mist) - MOD Fixes
- UI needs help - MOD Fixes
- Resource conversions (buy/sell) is horrible - MOD Fixes
- The amount of base defense missions - Mod kinda Fixes (they still exist)
- Ground vehicles are ridiculously priced, not much use by mid game, and can't be salvaged - MOD Fixes the latter
- The Aliens ramp up so fast that In my couple run throughs and RNG of Ancient Resources, I still haven't been able to build any DLC Ancient Alien tech.

Is the game worth $60.00? Sure if you are a millionaire, or feeling overly generous or want to invest into Phoenix Point 2. I would wait for the next sale and get it, play it then cuss like crazy on your first unmodded run-through and then (mostly) enjoy it as you tweak it via Mod's.... Thanks Sheep-y for the mod manager, you rock!\t\t\t\t\t
