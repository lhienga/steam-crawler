\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith a heavy heart, i have refunded the game and wrote a negative review.

+gets rid of the underwhelming XCOM pod system
+decent graphics, far less taxing on your system than XCOM
+great voice acting
+many "cinematics"
+i like the free aim mechanic, BUT it forces you to wear helmets, so no faces visible

But the cons are so many, this game feels like work, not like a game:

-You have to end every single soldiers turn manually most of the time --> clicks
-After every turn, a dialogue asks you if you are really sure (n turns --> n useless click)
-if you spot an enemy, your movement is automatically interrupted --> many more clicks
-equipment manufactoring and item management is a clickfest
-missions get repetetive very fast (see above), especially the "supply raid"
-base building is lackluster
-soldier skill tree is lackluster
-Game is extremely easy at start (cant talk about mid/end game as i refunded)
-"aliens"/Pandorans are kinda meh from concept, at least for me


%%%%%%
%verdict%
%%%%%%

I can see what Julian Gollop tried to do here. PP improves some clunky XCOM mechanics, but i simply cannot bring myself to play this clickfest game ~60hours+ to reach the end.

I refunded it after 2h, while it only cost 15€ on sale.

I wish Firaxis and Gollop would team up for XCOM3.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is in fact one of the best games I have EVER played. (Top 10 for sure)

+ fantastic turn based mechanics that feel like an evolution to the genre
+ worldspace tactics, lots to cover here so lets just say that it's just heaps better than what recent XCOM games had.
+ epic soundtrack, seriously
+ choices in diplomacy. Get allied with a faction, or ignore them. Or attack them and steal their stuff. Or let the factions kill each other. Or let the aliens kill off your least favorite? The choice is yours, but so are the consequences.
+ even the lore is interesting. I actually read every message etc. I came across. Very rare for me.
+ lots of depth all around
+ tons of gear. I didn't even get to see all of it on my first run.
+ not casual. eventually you're going to need a pen and paper to be able to keep track of things
- somewhat repetitive, this is the only (and I mean only) issue I had with this game. For example: you're going to see some maps twice.

(9.5/10)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the game that the Firaxis XCom games should have been; a new modernized version of the original XCom classics. Firaxis simplified the formula too much by eliminating much of the base micromanagement, making the soldiers classes too boardgamey and restricted and removing the bullet physics etc..

Phoenix Point is so much better. This has all the nice in depth features of the original classic XCom-games but with a modern twist! Realistic bullet physics system is back also where the bullet is its own physical object traveling in the battlefield instead of a weird random chance to hit garbage system like in the Firaxis games.

In conclusion if you loved the original XCom games but want something more flashy when compared to for instance Xenonauts and hated the simplified game that Firaxis made, then I advise giving this a try.\t\t\t\t\t
