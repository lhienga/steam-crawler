\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhoenix Point(PP) nails its shooting mechanics at the cost of everything else. I like our new crab overlords, the Pandorans, but they don't have the same aura of malice XCOM baddies like sectoids or thin men have. But neither thematics or XCOM are PP's real problem.

The game is just a mess. Basic, perfunctory features you expect from other video games are wrong in PP. Hell, the game's own symbol, phoenix wings -used on the game's official twitter- is upside-down in the geoscape, in the diplomacy screen, and in cutscenes. This is emblematic of the mess.

There's the small potatoes: Sounds on the geoscape can stutter. You can't disable the opening cutscene which plays every time. Units interrupted by the "Return Fire" ability will spasm a few times before letting the camera move on. The English voice acting for your soldiers is bad. But none of these are deal-breakers.

There are more serious flaws that seem harder to cut out. PP feels like it was intended to be hardcore but had to make concessions down the pipeline, a feeling that this is a compromised dream. Crafting takes real time; except ammo which is instant. You determine every item you make and bring on missions; except your APC's ammo which you can't bring extra of. Transit happens in realtime and aircraft carry limited crew; except new recruits who teleport to base if your plane is full. These mechanics circumvent otherwise tedious hardcore gameplay but they stick out like a sore thumb.

Diving deeper we reach the sloppy mechanics. To compare gun stats you click, drag, and hold weapons over each other to bring up the comparison window. The same thing is accomplished by a mouseover in other games. The comparison isn't even much help. There are a LOT of weapon stats. There's: damage, burst, range, shock, bleed, poison, virus, fire, shred, piercing, capacity, weight, hands, and more. But if a weapon you're comparing has 0 in a statistic, the tooltip arrows won't recognize it. I don't mind doing mental math but the window could at least meet me halfway and spot the difference for me -all while I maintain my mouseclick of course. Base navigation in the geoscape and personnel menus has pointless clicks too.

Then we dig into the Manufacturing menu. You can't compare items in Manufacturing at all. If you want to know how a new unlock compares to your current gear you'll have to scroll back and forth. You can't filter weapons by type in the Manufacturing menu either. You have to filter by soldier classes. Nevermind that some soldiers can use weapons outside their class. Want to find shotguns? Deselect every class but Assault. That will narrow it down to only assault rifles, assault rifle ammo, shotguns, shotgun ammo, riot shields, grenades, medkits, and other universal gear. Universal gear never leaves the list, even if you deselect every class. The universals wouldn't be so bad if everything wasn't mixed together in a (mostly) alphabetical list. Navigating the catalog of grenades only to realize the gun I wanted is a "Virus Rifle" instead of an "Assault Rifle" is tedious.

Then there's the stuff PP just lies about. There's the "Trooper" perk which said it granted assault rifle proficiency and didn't. Sort of like the mainline missions which say they'll piss off the factions and don't. The last official patch dropped. It's up to the modders from here on out.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a lot of great things going for it. Unfortunately, there are a few very problematic design decisions that ruined it for me.

The Good:
-Compelling lore
-Interesting alien species
-Clever combat mechanics
-Fun vehicles
-THE AMAZING FREE-AIM MECHANIC (XCOM should adopt this!)
-Excellent projectile physics.
-Ability to build multiple bases
-Fun exploration where you can meet factions, gather resources, learn lore, etc.
-Interesting faction mechanics where you can steal, trade, ally, fight, etc.
-Alien species evolve in interesting and logical ways, making you change how you have to fight them.

The Bad
-TERRIBLE IN-GAME ECONOMY (deal-breaker for me): you can't have monthly income without going out of your way to exploit a very specific and very tedious game element.
-You usually can't explore outside your starting continent without specialized vehicles that cost more money than you're likely to have, and take forever to build.
-Those fun exploration sites where you can get money? Every time you go to one, enemy strength increases, and you start to need more and more tech/money to win missions. If you want to take the time to research things, you basically can't explore.
-RESEARCH HITS A WALL TOO SOON (deal-breaker for me): by the time you've explored everything you can, you'll have run out of things to research, and still not have effective equipment. The tech tree is way too limited.
-It's hard to find your bases on the map. The icons are tiny.
-FACTION TRADING IS BROKEN (deal-breaker for me): Most faction locations will never want to sell you the most important resources, and those few that do will only sell you around 100 per day (which is a pittance). So, you basically have to have one of your precious air vehicles camped out on a single location's doorstep and trade food for something more useful every day in the hope that you might eventually be able to build something useful.
-EVEN RAIDING FACTIONS DOESN'T GET YOU MANY RESOURCES (deal-breaker for me): So, you decided that trading is broken, and chose to raid a faction for their useful resources. You fight a grueling mission that lasts an hour or more, costs a lot of ammo, and damages all your units. At the end of it, you get...84 tech. WTF?
-THE IN-GAME STARVATION MECHANIC MAKES NO SENSE (deal-breaker for me): The game has this mechanic where the aliens are basically starving humanity into oblivion. OK, that's fair. However, food is, for some strange reason, the one resource that everyone has more than enough of, and nobody wants to accept your food in exchange for other resources. WTF? It feels like the game designers decided to ignore the lore, just to make the game harder in a way that doesn't make sense.
-There are only a half-dozen alien species. They do evolve in interesting ways, but fighting the same six (or so) aliens gets old, after a while.

In conclusion, this is an almost-amazing game with a small number of fatal flaws. I get that it isn't XCOM with a funding council, but having some reasonable way to get more resources is a basic element of this type of game, and regardless of how it happens, it needs to be possible without completely screwing yourself over. Were it not for these few utterly perplexing design decisions, I would be giving this game a glowing review.\t\t\t\t\t
