\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhoenix Point is a turn-based strategy game developed by Snapshot Games and released in December 2019. As a fan of XCOM and other similar games, I was excited to dive into this one and see what it had to offer.

First off, the graphics are impressive. The environments are richly detailed and the character models look great. The game takes place in a post-apocalyptic world where humanity has been devastated by a virus that mutates the creatures around them. The design of these mutated monsters is really cool and they make for formidable enemies.

The gameplay is solid as well. The turn-based combat is engaging and requires strategic planning and good decision-making. The game offers a good variety of weapons and abilities for your soldiers, allowing you to customize your squad to fit your playstyle.

One aspect of the game that sets it apart from similar titles is the ability to target specific body parts on enemies. This adds an extra layer of strategy and can be really satisfying when you manage to take down a particularly tough enemy by targeting its weak spot.

There is also a base-building element to the game where you manage resources and research new technologies. This adds some depth to the overall experience and gives you something to work towards outside of the combat.

One downside to the game is that it can be quite difficult at times. The enemies can be tough and the permadeath feature means that losing a soldier can be a devastating blow. However, this also makes the victories all the more satisfying.

Overall, Phoenix Point is a solid turn-based strategy game that offers a lot of depth and challenge. The graphics are great, the gameplay is engaging, and the ability to target specific body parts adds a unique twist. If you're a fan of the genre, I would definitely recommend giving this one a try.\t\t\t\t\t
