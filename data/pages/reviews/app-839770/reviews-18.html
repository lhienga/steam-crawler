\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhoenix Point is only what I can describe as one part masochistic chess marathon and one part extremely janky unpolished Xcom/Xcom2. This is not a game for the faint of heart and has a cynicism to it and its mechanics. It assumes that you have an innate knowledge of the game even for their tutorial mission and the game isn't intuitive enough to point you in a particular direction of what you should be doing.

However, once you do grasp the game's unforgiving mechanics and get a handle on what exactly you should be doing. This game is a rhapsody in precise turn-based tactical combat. If you a hardcore tactician always looking to further optimize every move in these kinds of game then P.P. is a solid pickup.

But it is unfair and be ready.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis has to be one of those games that I really hoped would bring something new or fresh. While the whole concept of pandora virus is good and the overall ideas for missions (defending havens, clearing out nests, raiding and stealing, defending bases) this game falls in so many ways that I could never fathom without playing the game. Which is quite sad.

The cover system is so useless and frustrating at times that I honestly started playing the game without using it in combat, but to get out of the sight from the enemy, then get back to him, hit and then run again behind the high obstacle. Most of times you should be able to hit something, but the cover blocks your vision. Having experience with XCOM series, this generated only frustration, seeing how good this system works in XCOMs and how badly implemented it is in Phoenix Point.

The decision to use the different aiming mechanic than in XCOM (comparing them a lot I know, but they stand very close to each other in terms of mechanics) - which is two-circled sight, was pretty good and nice idea. But as I said, the cover system in this game is broken, and even though you can use the free aim, this just doesn't work properly. You cannot peak out from the full cover. The only cover this seems to work with is by the low one.

The overall map designs are awful. There were so many moments that I had my whole squad out of cover, just because the enemy spawned in an area where they could hit my guys from behind of flanks, and I could do little to counter that. I had to make them dash to the covers without the sight on the targets, so it was either leaving them to get hit or let the enemy get close. The second option almost always ended up with at least one team member getting hit to oblivion.

The devs implemented quite an interesting possibility to hit the enemy in some particular part of the body (like arms, legs or heads). Each of the limb/part of the body had it's own HP pool, and disabling them was quite fun. But later on in the campaign you will face so many enemies, that there is almost zero possibility your squadmates will not get any part of their limbs disabled. What is so important about it? Well you cannot heal these parts, except when you finally obtain an engineer from the NJ faction. So if you have a guy with wounded arm, he cannot use any two handed weapons, such as assault rifles, or heavy weapons. That makes sense, and it brings some nice tactical depth into the game. But as I said, you will face so many enemies, and due to the cover system being as useless as it can only be, you will be in a lot of pain to keep these guys combat ready throughout the entirety of the missions.

There are a lot of enemy types, so that is a plus, but sometimes the balance in the game is quite... miscalculated. I fell like I was snowballing from the very beginning of the campaing, just to later realize the enemies (and especially those from the DLCs) are becoming quite overpowered with a lot of HP. The only thing that mitigates this problem, is due to AI being sooo dumb. Most notably allied AI. It gives such a repulsing feeling to the game.

The biggest nail in the coffin is probably the debuffs that are being put on the units. I mean the paralisis, and Virus. If any of your units have been attacked with any of these, and multiple times, then you can count this unit as dead/useless. The debuffs wear down so slow it practically renders them as liability. 1 per turn... and if your unit got hit that it reached around 40 and he had strenght of 26, you can't use him until 15 turns pass. It is a lot of time for a mission. And the enemies will be coming at you indefinitely. The further in the campaign you are the faster they spawn. So having to wait so long for one guy to get rid of the paralisis (and you cannot counter that) makes the pacing so .

And getting to pacing, at the beginning of the game you litteraly fly around, getting know of your surrounding area, scavenge locations, trade with factions there is almost no action. Just few missions. But try and get 2 or even one ship more and create more squads. You will be jumping from mission, to mission, doing the same objectives over and over. As I said, the idea for these was good, but their variety and execution leaves much to be desired. It's just too boring to bear after a time, and destroys the pacing.

The soundtrack is... well, terrible. It eventually became a lullaby for me. While in XCOM, the music was so dope it kept me hyped during encounters/missions. This is killing the mood.

Graphics could have been a bit better, but then again I do not consider graphics to be the most important asset of the game. It is just a thought, but does not influence the rating of the game.

Where there's cones, there's pros as well.
I have to admit, the game had pretty interesting story about the pandoravirus, and felt really cool having the control over the organization in the post apocaliptic world, and trying to save the humankind.
Weapon variety, as well as the technological inventions were very nice, although a bit too much at the beginning, when you try to come to terms with how everything goes.

The addition of combat vehicles is nice, but I think they're too overpowered.

To sum it all up, the game had (probably still has) a potential, but it should be improved to the state where it functions as a good and enjoyable game. It is a shame, that this game did not suceed due to it being so... mediocre, and unpolished.

Word of advice:
Get it on some nice sale. Otherwise, try something else.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe negative first: 1 It took a couple of determined starts before I started enjoying the game. 2 Missions by the end became a bit repetitive and the graphics, while generally quite good and acceptable, still felt just a little "old". 3 Soldier customisation could benefit from lots of mods - I really did not like those ridiculous mohawk helmets, and the limited looks of the various armour types. Related to that, the lack of back stories and individual behavioural quirks (I know they can become reckless) makes them very two-dimensional. 4 It is difficult to see where the game is going and the first playthrough seems like you just go from mission to mission and hope it eventually ends. 5 Equipping: The volume of weapons is overwhelming at first, and while this became part of the fun it was not easy to select items when equipping the troops. It would be nice to be able to separate weapons from ammo on the equipping screen. Also be careful not to waste 90% of a clip of ammo manufacturing more from this screen.
The positive: These two take getting used to, but once you do they are great: shooting and cover. Not creeping from cover to cover works very nicely and so does being able to aim at just a bit of the enemy peeping out from concealment . The aiming system is the best. In combination with cover, the burst, shred and penetration mechanics make the tactical map so much more fun. The independent body parts do make for some gamesman type of shooting, (and healing) but overall, also makes for more fun. The fact that the weapons do not increase in power astronomically, as research happens, and that early weapons are still usable in the end game, gets a nod. The opportunity cost management and balancing of limited resources, with the option to trade or to alienate the factions, makes the strategy management portion also much better. Choices matter in the strategy layer, and in the interplay with the factions, the pure and independents, and in how you spend resources. This is what makes the game immersive and replayable. Sound was not awesome, but passable. The soldier multi-class combos make developing a soldier more interesting., and effective use of training facilities is welcome for that. There are many more positives besides these which stood out to me. While there are definite nods, contributions and learnings from other games, Phoenix Point stands alone in how it feels. Oh, and it played like a dream on Linux. It gets a solid 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved this game and finished it in the 2nd run ><
If you are the player of this genre you would understand that our 1st run is for learning only.
When you know what you need to do and how you should manage your resource then you will have really enjoyed moment.
Ok, let that aside let talk about the game.

Beginning of the game really great! lot of new monster and lot of thing you want to do. Set up the squad, planing on level up your soldier, planing the research and building your base. For the research you must make a hard choice like you want the powerful gun or gadget. You must make a good plan in the beginning otherwise you gonna have the really have time in fight.

Middle of the game it's time for a powerful enemy start to show up and obviously the are more powerful than you no matter how good you made in the beginning. At this point you will start thinking about what is the mistake you made in the beginning, why your technology was um-comparable to your enemy. Here is my opinion: You didn't do anything wrong, this is the moment that you learned enough. Those powerful enemy shared the same behavior with every previous enemy you have fought. You will know where would they go next, what did they do if you destroy their bunker. It's a full strategy time! don't focus on just dealing damage but shoot their leg if you didn't want them to come after you, shoot their arm if they holding a powerful gun. Destroy bunker between you and them to prevent them to come closer, I could said that this is the best moment of the game"

Late game, It quite boring for me but I don't think it would be like that for most of people. I doing some stupid thing like launch the Probe to scan for the ancient location for more than 80% area of the world lol. After that I have a have to fight those strong but always the same many times. And I didn't do the important research cause I want to complete all of another research first. Then it quite a boring time cause nothing new just waiting for the research to complete and fight the similar fight a lot.
Beside that it's still fun, especially at this point I already have super soldier ( Maxed level, maxed skill and potential born talent like sniper soldier that have talent about + accuracy ) In late game if you feel like the fight is too hard for you I think you must make something wrong, most of the battle can end with in less than 5 turns. Some only take 1 - 2 turns. Super soldier alone can kill all enemy by himself in 1 turn. It make a game feel very easy but still enjoyed able cause doing that you must calculate the movement to do that good move.

So if you don't like this game cause you feel enemy too strong then take my advice.
-In squad only 1-3 soldier are potential to do damage, other just for supporting them like destroy enemy bunker, open enemy position, destroy enemy part, use support skill or doing the objective only.
-Recommend team 1 close combat specialist Assault+Heavy, 1 good sniper + assault/infiltrator, 1 good supporter priest+technician. And the rest you can put anything, more sniper more assault or heavy is up to you.
-Each soldier should have 2 weapon, 2 grenade and 1 medic.
-Should have 2 - 3 main squad, while main squad is 3 strong soldier + other soldier.
-Don't save everyone, you won't lost the game from that ( Maybe? ) I was saving all of them till late game and I found out that even each faction was killing each other it took very long time until you lost. That mean during the play you can ignore more than 30% of invasion.\t\t\t\t\t
