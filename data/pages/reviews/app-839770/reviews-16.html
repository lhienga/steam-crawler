Were you never truly happy with the Firaxis reboots? They had the name but not really the game. Does Xenonauts lack the flare you want in a reboot? Look no further! Phoenix Point is the game you have been looking for. this game was made by Julian Gollop. The man himself who started it all. And i do believe he got it right this time. The game has in its DNA the core feel of the X-com series but with alot more Quality Of Life updates as well as a multitude of options to diversify your playstyle. Some of these wonderful Options only available if you have the proper standing with the right factions in the game. the setting feels to me like a reboot of Terror from the deep but this time the world has gone to hell. The factions Are Really interesting and different in both philosophy and playstyle. They can end up hating and going to war with each other, or liking each other and working together as well. i am really enjoying the story they bring to the table. the Combat is great. Of course, Turn based tactical. And NO RNG's!! all aiming and firing is done with a VATS like firing rectical wit two circles in which all bullets will hit within the outer circle and the inner circle having a 50% chance to hit. no more missing at point blank range. there is so much more to talk about here but I'm going to cut it off. I'm only 25 or so hours in right now . I'm sure ill find some flaws here. But so far its all I've ever wanted.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe previous XCOM that I played was XCOM: Enemy Unknown (and old ancestors too but that dosn't make sense).
And what I really want to highlight: the combat mechanics became really better. The action points and movement system, the shooting cone system, abilities that bring different tactics and let you handle many situations. Also I faced minimum bugs (except the Destiny III always missing Artron's elbow with the pincer... its burning pain...) and mutation and cyborg mechanics looked promising.
If the combat is so good (and it will be the main thing that the time is spent on) so why then I dislike?
Let's see.

First of all it has the same problem as the XCOM: Enemy Unknow had - after some point it becomes boring. Nothing significantly change: you have to fly-n-fight here and there and the plot is usually more or less the same. Maybe I was not attentive enough... hints have highlighted some points several times but the one about the aircraft flying destination limit got out of my view, so I got stuck for some time on the first continent with several bases on another one (and they were pillaged of course, a considerable waste of money because you can't refuel anywhere while you can do that on an "empty site"). Perhaps this moment resulted is some staleness in the game, I don't know. Anyway it happened.

The second point. Balance. That is the thing that ruins the battle. Again, maybe I miss something, but I find most of weapons useless. Some of them were usefull but... Just sniper rifles and Destiny III could do something (because those creatures got armor bigger than a heavy soldier of a technically developed humanity). And of course I talk about playing to minimize a probability to loose a soldier, from some point of time in the game that would cost too much. And the Destiny III itself looks like an unbalanced workaround for the overarmored enemies (1 action point, no range limit, absolutely precise, rather cheap ammo). But at some point even that became too low. I tried something else and that was not really effective. A vice-versa unbalanced workaround was the umbra enemies. They suddenly added a lot of pain in the @ss. From time to time I got some new researches, but they didn't change anything significantly (I found most of the armor-peercing weapons useless, explosion weapons are too nerfed compared to the XCOM and too expensive - 90 damage for 3 action points while a "common" enemy might have 400 HP, some less common up to 1000). Ancient weapon I could not afford - I didn't know what to do with those shields and even had no interrest to try what could help. Yes, I could try taking bigger team (on the heaviest aircraft) trying something here and there but those constant reports with enemy becoming more and more tough and deadly just was too much for me. Maybe I'm just too old for such marathons but after 70 hours of rather tought fights I find myself in less and less promising situation. I got tied when I tried not to fire-from-long and engaged with heavy, but from a marksman that had twice the HP of my heavy an umbra was born and it would have 3 strikes on his turn - enought to kill the heavy - and I could do nothing since you can't affect it before its turn, no overwatch for that amount of HP I could arrange. And I couldn't know the umbra will be there.
A game can be difficult, but it should give hope in some kind. Here you can find yourself stuck in tight fights with some plot missions that you already don't want to take because you are exhausted. I was.

I need to mention that I played on the difficulty level before the last. I didn't want a simple walk but was not ready for the nightmare. As a result I winned mostly all the battles and had a very rare loading because of losses, but ended tired. 70 hours is not a small amount for such a game. And I didn't saw any signs of the end. If you are ready to spent so much time - feel free. If your gate is usual 30-50 hours, then don't start. You may feel sad one moment. Maybe I took that too personaly but the game started really very promissing sucking me and my time in, but left empty.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6/10

I suppose i've enjoyed playing it overall, so I am obliged to give it a thumbs up. However, some questionable design choices made me really start to dislike it in the end.

The tactical play is very like the old X-Com games, but with nicer graphics now and an aiming system similar to Fallout 3 where you can shoot limbs/heads/tentacles and disable them, which is a lot of fun.

Most of the tactical map is destructible with the right weapons, I tend to now just use a lot of rockets, makes the game too easy but also very satisfying flattening the map, along with annoying Pandorans that previously caused a lot of frustration with their cheesing.


In my opinion though, Phoenix Point suffers from quite a few bad design choices:

- Infinite enemies in missions that makes it feel like you didn't accomplish anything when you complete a mission, this is compounded by the fact you get very little loot from Pandorans and no experience points for killing them. Missions also feel like a slog when they are just constantly spawning in at the edge of the mission map. Completing infinite enemy missions is basically you running away from the battle...

- Simplistic economic model, basically you just fly around and mindlessly trade with havens for guaranteed profit... you don't even need to check prices, there is no supply/demand and your trading has no effect on the havens. In the early X-Coms you could manufacture or capture equipment from the aliens then sell it for $$$, which was a lot more enjoyable than this.

- Some combinations of Soldier perks and some weapons make the game very unbalanced and can make it stupidly easy. However, expect to be trolled hard by the enemies if you want to play fair, to the point where you almost have to cheese back to win.

- Not many maps, its mostly the 3 factions locations and Pandoran bases, with some wilderness and PP bases thrown in. No fighting on ships, beach resorts, UFO sites, cities, underwater locations, etc.. gets very repetitive, especially considering the frequency of haven attacks later in the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhy I would not recommend:

Missions where the player needs to run to the other side of the map to collect parcels or complete an objective and then they need to run back to escape again.

Missions that include endless enemy reinforcements.

Missions that include running around the map looking for hidden objectives.

Lair missions which include all three of these features are a horrible.

This is 90% of the missions in the game and its tedious to run them over and over.

Missions take too long in general.

More traversal abilities could alleviate the issue maybe.

Good things:
Combat is good
Good variety in classes
Good variety in weapons
Blend your favourites together to make interesting characters
Some missions that stick to shooting aliens until they are all dead are fun (the ones that dont include travelling so much or have endless enemy spawn)
Good variety of enemies
Evolving enemy design so they never feel the same and the threat feels like its growing
Interesting faction design however towards the end they become a nuisance when you are trying to save the world and they just want to kill each other.

There is a lot of good things in the game and you should try it out for yourself. However for me it started to feel very grindy towards the end as enemies had more health and the amount of time it took to run each mission was wearing me down.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe experience you will get from this game can be accurately described in one sentence: "What the ♥♥♥♥, why did they do THAT?!?!?!?!"

If you play this game, you will have this exact line of thought several times throughout playing, without exception, I guarantee you.
I played this game when it first came out and it was an awful experience through and through. I played it again recently as I heard they fixed a lot of the issues the game had when it first came out, and I was surprised at first. The early game was much more enjoyable, things seemed a lot better, but as the game progresses, you notice all the cracks in the game that just make you think "what the ♥♥♥♥, why did they do that?".

The XP system, how you get SP, the unfun enemies, the LACK OF ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ PROGRESSION, the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ horizonal sidegrade system, overchoice, the irritating factions and alliances, the ungodly amount of jank, the poorly balanced economy, the *incredibly* poorly balanced gameplay, just everything will start to get to you by the late game; especially if you didn't look up the broken ass class combos online beforehand, which the game almost feels balanced around.
The game doesn't know what it wants to be. A weird mix of new X-com, and old X-com, somehow taking the worst aspects of both, and it all just feels so half baked.

The gameplay actively punishes you for playing instead of rewarding you. Ammo costs way too much, killing enemies gives you no rewards, destroying nests and lairs is long, tedious, and unrewarding, rescuing soldiers, haven defense, etc... You NEVER feel rewarded for doing these things, and you shouldn't because the game gives you ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ reason to care.
So why should you?
Your only goal is to get to the end of the game. You won't enjoy the process of getting there.

Do yourself a favor, and just wait for Xenonauts 2.
Unless modders do some gigantic game overhauls to fix all the bizarre design and balance choices, this game is not worth it at all. You will leave it feeling irritated and unfulfilled.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecent enough, essentially x-com 3. I have played 2 and its the same sort of game, with some different character classes, weapons, abilities etc. You can get multiple ships flying around with 5-8 soldiers in each, or alternatively some of the vehicles, though these take up 3 of the soldier slots in the ship, and don't feel especially equal to three soldiers. NB I haven't dl'd the KAOS dlc which contains some extra vehicles and weapons. There are three main rival factions which can be enemies or allies, and give some missions, and an ending for each, with unique research from them. You can also raid their bases, for resources (food, tech, and materials), ships or research. The antediluvian weapons and missions were also very cool, but finding all three of the ancient sites required to make most of the weapons was a chore, I was already at the end mission for a long time before finally finding them. Multi-classing is also a decent touch with the soldiers, thus being able to mix abilities etc. Overall, decent game, but similar to x-com2 I finished and don't feel excited or interested enough to replay for a few years. That's just me though. Enjoyed the random extra like mini factions too, could change my mind to explore aligning differently I guess. NB: 97 hours is fairly misleading, probably around 25hrs of direct playtime for a single play-through on veteran difficulty.\t\t\t\t\t
