\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think Phoenix Point is a valiant attempt to spice up the X-Com formula but there are many little kinks in the foundation that threaten to bring the whole thing crashing down. I've played through the game twice, on Legend and on Veteran. I'd like to point out some of the more glaring negatives here with the disclaimer that PP is a full fledged X-Com-like you can definitely get enjoyment out of. My point is mostly that it doesn't have the kind of design to make it a timeless classic. Playing PP you'll have to put up with some rough edges and unless you're deep into tactical strategy you might be better off looking at some of the contemporary games e.g. X-COM 2 and Xenonauts.

- The game balance is fairly whack. From what I've seen It has definitely improved since the backer/epic builds but still the scaling curve is way out of whack. The early game is passable providing a moderate challenge that rewards tactical play but in the mid-end game one of 2 things happen. Either you're way behind the curve so you struggle to stay alive or you completely stomp by exploiting certain wombo combos to the point where you beat most missions on the first turn. Ideally the challenge would reflect the difficulty you're playing on across the whole campaign, to me this dichotomy indicates that the action point/ will point system is extremely exploitable to the point that it makes tactical play obsolete. As a sidenote, I feel that some game elements are also way too punishing in a way that provides no options for counterplay (E.g. Virus, Paralysis). Across the board there seems to be this philosophy of "when it rains it pours" baked into the game and I can't say I'm a huge fan.

- There are too many missions. If you're played Xenonauts its the same concept here, after a certain point you're doing missions just to do missions because you're just waiting for research or some other progression trigger. Many missions don't feel impactful to your cause especially when you can just blaze through them as mentioned before. Of course you can ignore everything if you're already set but either way it's not a good look when you can't be bothered to play the game so you just try to get it over with asap. In Firaxis's X-COM 2 the game is more or less balanced so that a typical campaign will span 40-60 missions, In hindsight that is about the perfect amount so that you always feel like you're progressing.

-There are too few enemy types especially with the amount of missions the game expects you to do.

-There are a fair number of rough edges and missing quality of life functionality. E.g. No unequip all on your soldiers so you have to go through every piece of equipment manually everytime you re-equip, the free aim step out bugs out fairly regularly so you can't take the shot you want or you lose vision when you should clearly have vision.

Overall despite all these problems it's a thumbs up from me but be aware you can probably only get 1-2 playthorughs out of PP before the cracks start overwhelming the experience.

Btw, my unsolicited advice is that you play on the highest difficulty you can manage, my blind playthrough on legend was by far the most fun I had with the game. Somehow when the game is about overcoming the odds it alleviates a lot of the problems I've listed e.g. the too many missions point (When you can't respond to everything because you're behind you've gotta pick your battles and plan for survival instead).\t\t\t\t\t
