\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn almost direct copy of the XCOM and XCOM 2 games, which should come as no surprise given the history of this development team. If you liked XCOM and XCOM 2, it's a safe bet that you'll like this game. I had the first 3 DLCs when I played.

The Living Weapons pack was good as it added a decent AR with Acid and no ammo required and a nice set of armor. Legacy of the Ancients comes with a caveat in that it adds a ton of combat to the game to get materials necessary to build the LotA weapons. The weapons are definitely an upgrade to the normal weapons and none of them require ammunition, which is also very nice. However, I would say grinding for those weapons, which you won't get until the last 1/2 to 1/3 of the game adds between 50% to 100% more combat. If you like extra combat and are willing to grind for better weapons buy LotA, if not don't. Blood & Titanium adds Cybernetic augmentations, and some of them are very good.

Comparing PP to XCOM 2, here's how it stacks up

Combat (most important since you'll spend 70% of time doing this) - Tie (both are good, but both can become very grindy by the end of the game and as you add more DLC)

Inventory Management - XCOM 2
Squad Management - XCOM 2
Classes / Skills - PP
Graphics - XCOM2
Sound - Tie
Story - Tie

Overall rating 8/10 - Recommended\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got Phoenix Point as I wanted a game that was apparently being made by the same people as the original and the remake. Now, the good parts of this game:

-Different world stage. Unlike XCOM 2 where Aliens have plunged the Earth into a dictatorship, here its an alien threat out to terraform the planet to their ends.
-An intricate class system where you are able to mix and match classes to suit your team build.
-Vehicles. With each of their ups and downs.
-Base management and defense
-Interesting enemy variety

Downsides:

-Feels incomplete. There are parts that break in terms of graphics and UI. I assume this is due to Unity or developer oversight.
-Music variety is lacking. Maybe its the XCOM experience spoiling me, but the music gets droning after awhile.
-Resource, gathering. Everything gets extra expensive after a certain point in the game. After awhile, every bullet you shoot becomes more and more expensive. Every medkit you use, and that doesn't get into the trouble of acquiring gear and weapons for your troops. This is due to no steady income of materials. The only times you can get materials are by helping in city defenses, scrapping items taken from the enemy (And its too few) , cache hunting and exploration.

As a result, I found myself looking for ways to save resources as I do missions. Besides the obvious of keeping troops out of harms way and learning to aim, I opted to make most of my troops get into melee classes to compensate which in turn drove me to support the side that helps me save resources. The Disciples of Anu. Repairing damaged cyborgs take money, healing flesh does not. They also have the berserker class which is the only melee centered class in the game. Unless you mutated your troops to have pre-built melee but even then, that requires mutagen which also falls squarely on that faction's specialty.

Despite all that, the game is great, but rough. 7.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tnow that ive played over 100 hours, and come over from my 550 hours in xcom2 two i will give my thoughts, I have totally fallen in love with Phoenix point as it is now, from what i can tell they had some balance and bug issues, i havent encountered any of that in my 100+ hours so its safe to say it has been remedied (some very minor bugs that were solved with reloading save)

being a fan of the genre id say Phoenix point is on its way if not the best turn based strategy tactics game i have ever played. Its unique fist person aim engine associated with every shot made creates a whole new level of strategy that is very rewarding once you get a good understanding of it, also the armour system is fantastic once you understand the mechanics, you can be fairly aggressive with you armoured forces and not get punished.

The challenge level of the game is on a whole new level and coming from xcom2 (legend) Phoenix point was very overwhelming, almost daunting, and the corrupted horizons DLC ramps it up again adding a support/status effect/aoe/bullet sponge enemy. All that aside even outnumbered surrounded and out gunned you can pull threw and this is where IMO this game really shines compared to Xcom2 where in this situation you'll find yourself coin flipping with your troops lives and its not really a result of any tactical genius just the way shot mechanics work in Xcom, however in phoenix point you can take a control of the situation, use some limited special skills points (WP) to eliminate the key threats that turn establish some good cover for your squad to weather the next turn, after a few rounds of good strategy you'll see the tide turning in your favor and the rewarding feeling that comes with it. That said there are some story missions that you can shove your squad into that they just are not ready for and there is no real way to tell other then go in, fail, load save and do the mission later very frustrating.

Now the base/resource management is a part of the game i see a lot of complaints and i understand it took me about 50+ hours to realise that scanning question marks is NOT the primary source of income, haven defence IS, and i will say once i understood that the game became so much more enjoyable getting your fist local defence team together is a grind but once they have defended 4 havens they are basically paid off and are now generating resources with every successful haven defence, rinse and repeat for the next mist covered area and your squads will be chromed out, and the game does hint at this soon as you get on the geoscape but its a little vague, basically it says something like "scan area around mist to find havens to defend" leaves out the, they pay generously for your services, part. Once you get a steady flow of decent income you can start to enjoy managing your income to build better gear (and there's a lot of it) for your squads or create new bases and new squads, or make facilities that make irreversable changes to your soldiers increasing their effectiveness on the battlefield. the upgrades and equipment you can get for your soldiers are vast and only slight upgrades by themselves putting to effective use is another challenge and takes a lot of trial and error to learn who, what, when, and where this equipment is worth and how it synergises with skills is IMO another rewarding aspect of the game.

Soldiers individual skills and level ups seemed insignificant at first especially coming from Xcom2 where your dudes become gods of war able to one tap everything, Phoenix point it seems tries its best to not ascend your soldiers quite that high individually but instead just take them from "squishy" to "reliable" or at least keep the cost high on some of the more "OP" skills, example i was overjoyed to find out my heavy could lob 2 grenades and a fury rocket in one turn with the "boom blast" skill nearly anywhere on the map and was generously delivering a fat amount of explosive pain on every mission until realised i was spending a quarter to half my rewards on reloads every mission, it added up quick i had to start using this skill tactically or lose the economic game.

In conclusion i wanted to share my positive feedback because i found myself thoroughly enjoying the game and just delighted often when unlocking new skills and equipment or creating fancy hybrid classes, or just using my understanding of the game mechanics to play out a grand strategy and come out bruised but not beaten from a good challenge, im sitting at about 130 hours right now and can see probably another 100 more in content and challenge i like to do, i am playing the hardest difficulty however its a bit masochistic and the middle difficulty is hard enough even for a seasoned xcom2 player, i also wanted to share the positive feedback because i read a lot of the negative reviews and some are warrented however a lot of them look like either rage or a failier to understand the mechanics, also i understand the game has come a long way and some of the comments might be from a less balanced, or less optimized version of the game and if thats the case id encourage those gamers to give it another shot.

feel free to comment if there's some other aspect of the game i can elaborate on, hopfully this review was helpful if your deciding on weather or not to purchase :)\t\t\t\t\t
