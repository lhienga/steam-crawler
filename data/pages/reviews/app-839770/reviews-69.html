\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst impressions: Just... just no. It's a busy, hysterical mess. It tries to do far too much and the end result is confusing blob of a game. We didn't need factions at all, and we certainly didn't need the tiresome chore of diplomacy with them. We didn't need a frankly bewildering array of enemies that come in about a billion different almost identical 'variations'. We certainly didn't need the frankly hilariously bad concept of a lovecraftian deep one who has found a gun somewhere. We didn't need an asinine resource management system that appears to have been designed specifically to keep you broke and desperate. If you want your game to be difficult, make it difficult. Don't just design it in a way that makes it not fun to play. That's not 'making it tough on the player'. That is making a design choice to make the game feel like a frustrating chore. I mean, I guess it's AN approach to game design, but it doesn't seem like the optimal one.

Take, for example, the 'Pure'. Bloke contacts me, asks for help, I help him and later get attacked by nigh-unkillable robot men? Is THAT the story you want to tell me? One that punishes me for engaging with it? Come on, Julian. You know better than this. And some of the enemies - specifically those that you can fight in your aircraft - do not die. You are hopelessly outmatched. You can't even use skill, because just about the only influence you have over the combat is pressing 'e' to dodge projectiles for a short time until you run out of ammunition.


Oh, and before anyone says 'oh but the game gets better' or 'the game just needs you to persevere and get used to it' - you have sort of missed the point of what a game actually is, there. This was hyped solely on Gollop's involvement. 'The creator of X-Com!'. It is not unreasonable, as a consumer, for someone to expect a similar experience. A game that can ONLY be enjoyed after you play it for 400 hours or whatever isn't actually a good game, is it? Would you watch a trilogy of three-hour long movies all the way through if you though that the first movie was bad after an hour or so? Time, and Life itself, is far too short. Sorry, folks, but no. I am not convinced by that argument. It's explicitly 'game-ist' and I'm not interested in racking up a thousand hours on Darksouls or whatever - I play games to have fun and relax. And this game is just annoying, and the sources of that annoyance are numerous and disappointing. It's hard to have fun when the game keeps pissing me off with it's tedious nonsense.

To be brutally honest, this also feels like a naked attempt to sabotage future Firaxis games with the X-Com franchise, considering how 'Terror From The Deep' was teased at the end of that game. That's genuinely how I feel about it. It feels somehow... mean-spirited. Spiteful.

Having played the original X-Com for possibly thousands of hours over the last nearly three decades, I had expected more from it's creator. It's a pity that what I got was something I can't wait to stop playing almost every time I am asked by the game to do something. It's a pity because the game is beautiful. I don't mean the derpy monsters or the baffling misama that is the world map, they look like absolute garbage apart from a small number of them - I mean the cutscenes that are shown as the tutorial progresses, the interactions (wholly unwelcome ones, mind) that you have with the other factions. The battlefields. Even the soldiers themselves. Lovely, and the voice acting is pretty good too.

So, in summary: a confusing swamp of badly handled ideas, presumably designed by committee and badged up as 'X-Com, but better!' with the aesthetics of a far superior product drybrushed over the top of it.

###EDIT###: So, don't let it be said that I don't give something a fair crack. I have now played an additional 110 hours. Do you know what that 110 hours consisted of?

Shields. Shieldy-Shield-Shields. Shields with Shields on, with loads of Shields coming out of them. I wanted to see if the DLC did anything to really spruce up the miserable, grinding tedium of playing this game. They added two pieces of DLC that introduced a new enemy type, one of which I have already mentioned. Now... let me just say that Phoenix Point features Shields. A lot. There is a type of ubiquitous crab-man who has a shield. He appears a LOT. You will be dealing with shields a lot in this game. 'Hahaha!' Said the developer. 'I have designed an enemy that will force the player to adapt their strategy! THIS GUY HAS A SHIELD!'

No, mate. What happens is, you force me to waste expensive ammo trying to chip the guy to death, because I cannot move my soldiers because if I do, I get shot immediately to death by a resident from Innsmouth wielding a sniper rifle in a way that would make anyone who ever fought in any modern war feel triggered. It's not challenging. It is tedious. I've already covered this. When you want a game to be challenging, you design it in such a way as to ensure that the player has to adapt their tactics on the fly and not rely on one Alpha-Team setup with a SOP. That's literally what combined arms is. That's literally how battles are fought. You don't just glue eighty pounds of calcium to someone's left arm and say 'Hah! Now they can't be shot at all!' and furthermore, if you DO do that, you don't make that type of enemy more common in the game than hydrogen is in the universe. This. Is. LAZY.

'Never mind all that!' cried the developer. 'I'm not lazy and I'm most certainly not putting almost no effort whatsoever into this product that I am charging AAA prices for! I'll add two new types of enemies! And you know what? These enemies will be ROBOTS! Hahaha! You can't touch me now, I've added something with a bit of variety behind it, a bit of finesse and difference, and a new aesthetic!'

Yes, mate. You certainly have. The Pure and the Ancient Defenders are indeed not crab-fish-cephalopod people.

You know how you managed to balls it all up though? These new factions, that could have been so cool, so unlike anything else in the game?

YOU GLUED SHIELDS ON THEM AGAIN.

I swear to god, I am so BORED of dealing with enemies who stand still, lower their shield, fire a volley of godlike accurate machinegun fire that makes my maxed-out, hyper equipped and over-optimised snipers go green with envy, jiggle about with a weird animation and then put the shield back up in place.

The other two bits of DLC are one where you can dress as a crab and you get a reasonable assault rifle and an absolutely dire machine gun, and one where you can lose a dogfight every few hours. Not going to lie, I am surprised that anyone would send an aircraft up to a dogfight with only a few rounds of ammunition; apparently that is exactly what the megaminds at Phoenix Point do. No wonder they lost the war.

Finally, in this game you can build shields for your team. I have never done this because I find it almost offensive as an offer, considering the almost sexual fetish the developer seems to have for shields and I don't want to enable someone's addiction, because it's clearly impacting them.

I also have no doubt that, based on my experiences of every other item in this game that you can build, it will be hugely unrewarding to use and massively overpriced for what it does.

Rather like Phoenix Point itself, really.\t\t\t\t\t

On the other side you can barely get some improvement as you barely have some materials just to cover new suits, ammo, grenades etc. Being aligned to a faction you have access to their research which is not useful anyway as you gain just a little advantage (and you don't have materials to produce much of the research outcome). My base was placed in South America and I was limited just to it for a long time because the more distant places were too far (so I could not progress in faction's tasks). If you are more to the story than to waste weeks of game time trying over and over again to find the VERY thin line of success between failure scenarios, you should look somewhere else.\t\t\t\t\t
