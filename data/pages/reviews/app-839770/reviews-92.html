\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE 24th January 2022
So, it has been over a year since the last time I played this game. Some DLC got released as well so I asked myself if it had improved.

TLDR Answer: Not really. Still cannot recommend this game, still unfinished, don't purchase.

The points I mentioned below are still there and the new DLC doesn't seem to add much.
Mind you, I can only speak about Corrupted Horizons. From what I hear, Festering Skies is pretty much broken and unbalanced - not really a surprise to be honest, but take this with a grain of salt, I didn't play it because everything I have read about it was just terrible.
I will talk a bit about Corrupted Horizons now because really I didn't feel any changes or improvements from the vanilla content.

TLDR: Corrupted Horizons doesn't really add much value.
The Mutoids feel inferior to ordinary soldiers in spite of the new Pandoran abilities they bring to the table.
They cannot multiclass, cannot equip helmets, can only pick one ability per level and cost mutagen for recruiting, levelling up and healing. Mutagen is not a rare resource by any means, it is quite easy to get huge amounts of but I would rather invest it in other things than these soldiers.
Yes it can spit poion and do other pandoran stuff (how does New Jericho not declare war on you for using these things by the way? They didn't even comment on me getting access to them like they did with the research that lets you turn Pandorans into food) but that doesn't make up for their shortcomings.

The only reason to recruit Mutoids (apart from recruit shortage since you can just craft Mutoids out of thin air with mutagen) is to counter Acherons, the new enemy type added with the DLC. The DLC adds a corruption mechanic that decreases the will of your soldiers when fighting in Pandoran areas. It is also used by the Acheron, which serves as some sort of mini boss - always fun btw when two of those spawn at the same time btw, I recall something like this with the Scylla/Queen/Whatever in the past as well.
I have read about the Acheron being unbalanced but personally I feel like its AI is just very very dumb. I have seen it just jump about and use its fog abilities without anything, friend or foe, nearby to be affected by it, while being shot to death by my soldiers.

The corruption mechanic also isn't adding anything of value - at all. Your soldiers lose will and get more damage over time but you can spend mutagen to stop it, rinse and repeat. It is just another thing to spend mutagen on, gotta wonder if they add this stuff only to counter mutagen abundance. Not an effective way to do that.

Final words:
Instead of adding fun and engaging new game mechanics, they just added a new enemy, a new but just annoying and not engaging game mechanic and a soldier type that can ignore said game mechanic. This DLC just feels like it is there to give you a reason to dump mutagen but while that in itself isn't wrong, the reason for dumping that mutagen is just to undo a poor mechanic instead of doing something even remotely cool with it.

============================================================
Two words describe this game perfectly: Wasted potential.
Unfinished game. Just annoying to play because of the issues it has even after one year at Epic - I will say that it is better now than it was from what I have seen originally but that is a low bar.

TLDR: Some good ideas but bad design. I hope the REAL XCOM takes some ideas from this with actually GOOD execution. Not sure how much of this bad design is the fault of Gollop, I like to think that he had the nice ideas but the execution was just bad. If he is actually satisfied with this result, then I am sorry, but then he has lost his vision.
The game needed a few more years and was rushed. Maybe they were lacking funds despite the Epic deal, maybe they really didn't know how to program a fun game.

Just a few points instead of a full review because I already spent too much time with this game:
- Customization is terrible/non-existant. You can change faces & colors and that is it. And you don't really see the faces most of the time anyway because of the gear - if you use mutations or augmentations you don't even get any customization anyway apart from the color & pattern of your armor. Not at all like they originally presented it in the trailers. You cannot customize your armor apart from what I mentioned.
- The system that makes every single bullet be calculated that they are so proud of that they can't stop telling you about it in the loading screens? It is just annoying. Yeah more realistic and hardcore yadda yadda, doesn't make it any good.
- Pandoran battles (especially from mid-game on) make the dev failure quite obvious. When fighting the Forgotten, the Pure and the three factions is more fun than fighting the Pandorans, the actual enemies, you must see there is something wrong there.
- Blood and Titanium added some nice stuff though I still think the limitation that prevents you from going full augmentations/mutations is just a bad decision. Yes, story, the ones who went in all the way got crazy, but this is hardly a reason. I think it is balancing instead but the game has balancing issues anyway so I do not see an issue there. Awakening of the Ancients seems just a waste of time to me, you keep investing resources for little to no benefit. Yes you get some items at the end I guess but nothing that is worth the massive resources you had to invest into getting so far in the first place.
- The factions are not bad in design but there is just so much wasted potential (which the game is full of). Why aren't the Pure, Forgotten or hell even some large Mafia/Bandit group factions to cooperate with? Why not have the OPTION to work with them instead of the three factions? It is also quite annoying that you cannot really affect relations between the factions at all, you just watch it unfold. Yes I get the metaphor but that doesn't make it good.

Buy only at a discount and even then, I wouldn't recommend it. I want to like the game because of its ideas but the execution is just terrible. I am uninstalling the game without looking back.
I expected an indie rival for XCOM but it really is not no matter how much the devs wanted it to be. Guess we have to wait for XCOM 3.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Good foundation. Mostly lacking in content and polish. Would recommend.

I've played all of the reboot XCOMs extensively. I've also played a bit of the OGs, but I never completed them.

Phoenix Point comes along as a kind of middle-ground between the old and the new. Where the new (reboot XCOM) focuses mainly on tactical combat with a brief strategy layer getting you between battles and old is mostly about having the right number of men with the right gear in the right place at the right time, Phoenix Point straddles a fine middle line. Whether or not it does so successfully is up to the player. In my opinion it does well enough.

The good:
  • The combat system lends itself to actually interesting gear choices. One of the problems I've always had with reboot XCOM is that there weren't any real choices for gear at any point in the game. You use the best stuff. All your weapons upgrade at once. Plasma weapons are always the best, etc, etc. In this game, there's 5 different types of shotguns, 5 different heavy weapons, 4 sniper rifles, and while there sometimes is a best option, almost all of the weapons remain situationally useful even as you acquire other weapons. This, along with the enhanced strategy layer, is the most important thing that other games like this should try to take away from Phoenix Point.
  • Like OG XCOMs, it's not too punishing to lose soldiers, so save-scumming never really felt justified.
  • Although tactical takes a back seat in comparison to reboot, the multiclass system allows for a surprising level of soldier customization. However, the way that faction classes are pretty much strictly better than PP classes meant that I had to wait to upgrade soldiers for a while after they hit max rank.
  • Strategy is good. It's not better than the OG, but it's MILES ahead of reboot. There's multiple ways of getting the stuff you need to beat the game, and it's balanced enough. There's interesting decisions to make concerning your forces size, composition, and logistics. The research tree kept giving out interesting new gear and items.
  • Campaign was decently paced. The only time where I experienced fatigue was in the extreme lategame when uh... every haven was under siege. I wasn't going to do all of those defenses.
The mediocre:
  • The combat is okay. It's not better than the reboot. It honestly might not even be better than OG, because at least in OG it was pretty damn fast-paced. But it's passable.
Some criticisms:
  • There are not enough enemies types, straight up. All enemy types should get mutations, at the very least, and there needs to be more base types. The only time I ever felt scared was the first time I got wacked by a tar shadow.
  • Atmosphere is lacking. Just felt a bit bland/unimmersive.
  • Polish. This game really makes it clear as to the level of polish and production that goes into Firaxis XCOM. Things like the pervasiveness of voice acting, 2D graphics for UI, multiple skills just not doing what their description say they do, the way all cover gets destroyed in the exact same way, where everything including rocks and walls get turned into super-blatantly-video-gamey polygons with no weight when the get destroyed--- yeah. It's not the greatest.
  • Not enough/Not interesting enough exploration targets. Fixing this is easier said than done, but I liked the haven discovery events a lot. A quick and easy way to start would be to give all revealed havens an event (when you hit 25 rep).
  • Part-based disabling means that almost all combat comes down to shooting arms or weapons. Turns out aiming for center mass or the head was a lie. Just feels really wrong that disabling an arm means you can't use 2-handed weapons but disabling your head doesn't mean you can't use your brain. This means that free-aiming is rarely a decision. You either aim at the shredded part or the arm/weapon.
  • Free aiming should always be available and you shouldn't have to have an enemy present to utilize step-out firing positions.
  • Maps, simply put, suck. Maps are one of the most important parts of a game like this, but there aren't any interesting levels in the game, straight up. This was also a problem in the new XCOM games to a much lesser extent. EU/EW had interesting levels, but not enough of them. XCOM2 had a couple interesting levels, alongside randomized tilesets that could sustain some interest due to their nature (and several dedicated modders, credit to them). But the levels ARE interesting. In Phoenix Point, the levels are repetitive and the levels are bland. Random forest. Random house. Nothing feels lived-in. Often times there's no meaningful elevation, just a flat game board with cover dispersed all about; this is especially bad with Synedrion haven defenses, for example. I noticed it on the first glance and when it became apparent that the campaign was going to be ~120 missions long I knew it was going to be rough. It all comes back to polish.
  • Mod support. Games in the genre cannot compete with XCOM fairly without it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like the X-Com games you will love Phoenix Point! It's a delightful blend of the original 1990s Xcom and Xcom 2 as well as the rebooted Xcom Enemy Unknown and Xcom 2 with a lot of stuff of its own thrown in to boot. This is not an Xcom game (though done by one of the original Xcom designers) but takes place in a post-apocalyptic world nearly destroyed by a mysterious mist rising from the sea that spreads the mutation generating Pandora virus. Those infected mutate into horrible mindless(?) creatures from Lovecraftian nightmares bent on destroying humanity. There are many secrets to be learned as you navigate the demands of the three surviving factions of humanity to throw back the Pandoran hordes and put a stop to whatever is causing this all out assault against all life on Earth. The game is quite challenging even on Veteran difficulty (suggested for those who are already experienced at this type of game) and took me over 150 hours to complete so unless you are VERY good at the modern Xcom games I would play it on easy your first time through. There are a few bugs here and there but nothing major so I'm glad I waited for the Steam version to come out as it contains numerous bug fixes and comes with the 4 already released DLCs too. I can see myself logging thousand of hours playing this one in the coming years, it's just that good!\t\t\t\t\t
