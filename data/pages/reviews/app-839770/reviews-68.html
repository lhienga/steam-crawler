\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played around 20 hours of this game when it first came to Steam and thought that it had a lot of really weird design choices, so I left it for a while. I even took the time to fill out one of the surveys the devs put out. When the devs released some patches and all that, I decided to go back. Unfortunately, despite a few neat additions, they didn't fix a damn thing that pushed me away in the first place.

Aside from the occasional slowdown, the fact that enemies that go into stealth upon being hit once are allowed to immediately move, overwatch being capable of murdering your own dudes as soon as the overwatcher can see the gleam of carapace between the legs of his friends, and the absolute deluge of autopsy research you have to do for every single type of enemy, my biggest gripe is with equipment management.

Each soldier has a pretty sizeable inventory: 3 main slots for whatever you want, plus 6 more slots in a backpack, which can be filled or perused during missions. This is fine. Armor also comes piecemeal: a helmet slot, a body slot, and a legs slot. They even let you mix and match armor sets if you want. This is also fine. I actually enjoy this part. However, something that is not fine is running out of ammunition for a gun you haven't researched yet.

"But why don't you just research it?" you might ask. Putting aside the aforementioned deluge of Lovecraftian autopsies and faction research projects that take up the vast majority of your time, you aren't always allowed to research an on-hand weapon immediately. If you, for example, hire a soldier from a settlement early in your game, and he comes with a shiny new faction-specific rifle, you can't research it unless the faction likes you enough, even though the only mechanical requirement for reverse-engineering it is to have 1-2 of the weapons on-hand. Then you sacrifice them to the research gods and you're able to manufacture them afterwards. Of course, you cannot manufacture the gun's magazines until you've researched the gun. So he's living on the edge with a single magazine of irreplaceable bullets for a while.

This weirdly composed system acts as an active deterrent to ever hiring new soldiers from the faction settlements early on since they'll just run out of ammo after a few missions unless you already meet the nebulous requirements to reverse-engineer their equipment. The game has a post-mission resupply screen that lets you spend a small amount of resources to refill your soldiers' magazines, but the magazines of un-researched guns are grayed out. Maybe I've missed something, but the game is tight-lipped about what technologies each research project unlocks, making it difficult for new players to choose which to spend time on.

However, weapons aren't the only issue. On top of having to manufacture new and better weapons for your main crew of 4-6 soldiers, you'll also have to get your new recruits set up with equipment. Since they arrive ready and raring to go with exactly zero equipment to their name. It takes around 1 in-game day to craft a single of armor. So, each soldier requires 3 days of dedicated manufacturing to suitably protect them. Or you could give them one of your un-researched weapons, toss them into the next mission, and use them as a meat shield. You COULD do that, but keep in mind that the game has a morale mechanic, so your soldiers might not be too happy about watching the bright, smiling new recruit get horribly murdered by not-aliens.

One final gripe that doesn't have to do with equipment is the music. It's utterly silent half the time, with the occasional swelling of background noise. I get that this is supposed to be a grim, serious story about the last dregs of humanity trying its best to survive the only ways they know how, but lighten the ♥♥♥♥ up and put some tunes on when we start shooting at least!\t\t\t\t\t
