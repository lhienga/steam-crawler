It's like XCOM: but not.

Obvious, I know. It's made by the same people. But honestly? It's fun, despite its flaws. Aiming feels better than in XCOM, you now have some control over what you hit. Body damage is now a thing. A rifle takes two arms to hold, so if your sniper takes a burst to the shoulder, they're not sniping anymore.

The different factions are neat too. Anarchists, radical militarists, and these new age hippy types. They're fun, if a bit flat.

Game plays the same as xcom, there's a strategy and a tactics layer. In the tactics layer you control up to 6 soldiers, with the option for land vehicles. They take up 3 soldier slots on the dropship, so your options are a rover for 3 guys, and some of the land vehicles are quite nice. Rockets, healing, and a few other abilities.

Something I don't like is how slow the snipers and heavies feel. They move noticably slower than assault classes, and their weapons require 3 actions to use, not the normal 2, making them even slower if you want to move and shoot. You can up their speed a little bit by changing their armor, but then you lose out on the sniper's extra accuracy, or the heavy's jump jets.

Yeah, the heavy has a jetpack. It costs 3 actions to use, but lets you jump a decent distance. I've been using it in combination with assaults to pull the enemies into a frontline, then jumping over them to high ground.

The strategy layer is a bit interesting too. You can have multiple bases you upgrade and fortify, which means you always have a safe place to heal nearby. Most of the time you're flying around in your dropship investigating unknown signals, which turn out to be events, havens, or missions.

Overall I like it. It's not amazing or anything, but I got year 1 edition on sale for $25 and I'm happy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't normally write reviews this long, but as a massive fan of XCOM and take interest in games that try to emulate it, this had potential. The short version of my thoughts about this game is this, it has neat bells and whistles but is charmless in many aspects that don't make it hold up well. If it seems like I'm being harsh towards this game, its because by the get go, it was compared to XCOM.

This game is not a worthy successor to XCOM, it's just not. The biggest, and most prominent, problem this game faces is that there is nothing, NOTHING that it does better than XCOM in any major aspects that really matter. This is to also say that many little details and such add up for a less than enjoyable experience. Especially when any DLC is involved, I had a hell of a time trying to get through the game with said enemies active so I shut them off for good reason. If it was just a few small aspects that made this not so fun, that'd be one bump on its road, but SO many things just don't work.

Let's just go over at least some things it at least gets right to just about ok:
- There's some actually game affecting choices you can make that will alter what you're doing in a particular playthrough. What faction you side does matter as each faction has their own way of trying to gouge out your eyeballs for pissing them off.
- The art design and enemy ideas are at least quite interesting, I love that the reason the many many mutants you face are basically people at some point. Even more cool was the ability to get proper humanoid mutants on your side, I think that's at least interesting.
- The beginning of the game is quite enthralling, not too bogged down by being overly difficult, probably the ONLY thing it has over XCOM is that the early is not a colossal slog of fragile solider biting the dust constantly. (But we'll be getting back to this.)
- The damage system and how location based damage affects the game's combat is very interesting, allowing for very technical moves as you disable arms, or someone's head to prevent them from devastating your forces.
- The story is pretty interesting, and actually has quite a bit of weight to it. Many events happen and the setting it sold pretty well. You get the feeling that they're trying to do something interesting with it.
- The tactical combat is a lot more tantalizing than your typical XCOM where you just shoot and hope you hit, instead you can free aim and a lot of freedom in how you wish to handle enemies. Plus the addition of vehicles with space to carry troops is really cool, I like that aspect.

All of that sounds great... Until you get into the big stuff, and as I said before, the little stuff adds up:
- The interface and map system needs work, it's a little bit of a mess to navigate sometimes when you just want to see what does what at a glance.
- The soldier customization is a bit lacking when compared to its competition, sure its not as high budget, but it's visibly clear that some corners were cut here.
- There isn't a lot to do back at base, there isn't really any long term effects that happen to your soldiers or your base if things got broken. Sure its nice to have a base attack, but I've rarely seen it happen.
- The maps are kinda bland and of somewhat poor design, there's been plenty of times where there's just a bunch of turns of dead air with nothing happening because either enemies spawned too far away or are screwing around in buildings.
- The music is just bad... I don't know why they couldn't do better with it, sometimes it doesn't fit whats going on, or it's just not enjoyable to listen to. I shut it off most of the time to play something better.
- Some of the cut scenes are a bit on the jarring side, and the transition from cut scenes to gameplay can feel like total whiplash.
- Keeping factions under control is a bit too cumbersome to deal with, even with fast aircraft, you're not going to stop all invasions from harassing a faction you want to support. New Jerico being a notable pain.
- The weapon balance is AWFUL, there virtually no reason to use many of the other faction's weapons outside of New Jerico, as armor is king in resisting attacks, and their weapons can pierce said armor easily. The others are either gimmicky, just worse, or do something that just isn't useful. Oh and explosives are just as powerful as you might expect from the original lead from XCOM. This wouldn't be such a major point if it wasn't so prevalent, I want to be able to use interesting weapons, but if they don't do anything effective, then there's no reason to be using them. It basically requires you to at the very least steal or capture effective weapons, too limiting.
- The abilities and skill are ridiculously breakable on both sides, on one hand, you can have the potential to spam grenades and just break all the bones of a unit, or wall units using vehicles. An enemy I DESPISE is the cyborgs from one of the DLCs, it has no reason coming as early as it does and being a gigantic thorn in your side from what is *Supposed to be* a minor faction. Psionics especially are not balanced (and can even be buggy) as all it takes in one wrong move in a turn for a Siren (who can come fairly early in a playthrough) can instantly mind control units with not even a chance to resist it. Sure if you get the jump on them and cripple their head it's not a problem, but other people have reported a similar frustration to fighting this enemy as psionic defense is not something you come by very early. This leads us into...
- Mid to End game difficulty is WAY out of line, I understand that one of the biggest problems with XCOM is that endgame is a cakewalk, by the time you've gotten everything you need, you can wipe the floor with all the aliens. Ok, that's fair, but this is extreme, enemies (even on lower difficulties) have extreme amounts of health and armor, have a tendency to just one shot your soldiers, and respawn too quickly. A lot of this game's missions are escapes after you finish the objective towards the end. This might work if the philosophy is of UFO Defence, where that ALL of your soldiers are expendable and should be treated as such, but no, you NEED elite soldier more than ever in this game and replacing them takes long enough that by the time you've lost them, it's too late, your operations are over.
- Research in this game either is odd ball and un-intuitive to nonsensical, where just because you didn't research that random crab mutant that you didn't think you needed, suddenly, you're without shotguns. Or, hey do you want the best sniper rifle in the game? Well, you better sit down and wait because despite you being a technological and financial superpower, until one faction catches up with you, you can't make it yourself! It's at times like this that the pacing starts to fall apart cause you might need to find that ONE specific mutant lair that needs to be destroyed and researched, only to find out that it's refusing to spawn for you and now continuing the story isn't possible.
- This game is REALLY buggy, even after a while it still hasn't gotten much better, some architecture just doesn't make any sense with what's going on, sometimes you fire a grenade at a window only to find that for some reason the game thinks its a wall, this is a mess. My soldier's gotten stuck in a spot that I couldn't get him out of so I had to just do a mission with one missing guy. A car I built got stuck and blown up one time because apparently some box broke and now it's just doomed. It's stuff like this that gets me really mad.

As I said before, I know all of this seems harsh (and maybe a little unfair in some cases) but when a game has problems, it has problems, and this one suffers from issues big and small making it underwhelming

EDIT: Mod support's coming so I'd be prepared for that.\t\t\t\t\t
