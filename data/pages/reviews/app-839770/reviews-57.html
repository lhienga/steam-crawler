\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game does one thing right. The tactical layer is really good, involving and more strategic than most in its class. Combat is pleasingly difficult, in-depth and mid to late game has a few fun and interesting actions to spend your AP on. There is a 45 degree parabolic learning curve to be able to somewhat accurately predict what enemies might do next. They are very unpredictable and evolve faster than your team. This is really cool at first, but its not long at all before they simply start using guns along with all their other powers and abilities. That feels like cheap and artificial buffing instead of an actual planned or creative progression.

It's not inherently bad in a strategy game that the enemy levels faster than you, as long as the player has options that are reasonable and achievable to counter the growing threat. In this game, they really don't. And that's the problem. Options to keep up with the clock are insufficient to do so. Game content is mainly focused on combat and enemies over any other factor. Again, not necessarily a bad thing. But that means that meaningful options and overall game content for the player's team are very limited compared to other games in its class. There is very little customization of any kind, cosmetic or tangible. Rolls for classes and abilities make no sense at all, they are just random rolls. You could end up with a Heavy tank that rolls Sniper abilities. There are initially only three classes (Assault, Sniper, Heavy) and a couple others have to be unlocked through diplomacy with one of only three factions (Warlike, Scientific, Religious). But that happens late game, too late to matter really. You may adopt a new class and achieve the 2 or 3 abilities from both, but again this can only happen in late game when it's nearly a foregone conclusion.

This isn't the only game where you will always be outclassed, outnumbered and outgunned, where the enemy will always be two or three steps ahead of you with more AP, faster and far more devastating abilities, with better armor and better weapons. Armor and weapons that are extremely expensive and time consuming to first reverse engineer, then to manufacture. With a doomsday clock ticking away, progression is crippled by the amount of time and resources it requires to keep up. Better games in the genre offer ways to overcome the initial difficulty curve rather than simply steepening the curve throughout.

The only way to beat this game is to save-scum. The whole game. Continuously. But that's not fun for me, not an achievement of any kind. It's rather closer to work than fun. Work that costs money instead of being paid. If that sounds fun to you, then at least wait for it to be half off. $9.99 to $12.99 might be reasonable to try out the innovative aiming mechanic if you don't care about building a team and keeping them alive. But there are far better games to scratch that tactical turn-based itch.

The lead developer on this team is a very talented and experienced individual who helped to bring us the original X-Com. He applied what he learned then and made a cool innovation with the aiming mechanic. I do hope that the team expands and grows and may yet bring us an amazing game worthy of them. And no, this is not what we got instead of X-Com 3. For that, you will have to look at Marvel's Midnight Suns, out in March 2022, by Firaxis and 2K. Perhaps the bell has tolled for X-Com, but hopefully we can look to smaller studios like Snapshot Games Inc. to keep the genre alive.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you expect XCOM 3 or anything XCOM-like then you will hate it. They are both tactical strategy games, but that's where the similarities end. I myself am a huge fan of the XCOM franchise and though I feel like Phoenix Point didn't manage to capture the same feeling of grandeur and coolness that XCOM has, it undoubtedly does a lot of things better. Things Phoenix Point does better:
-Chance to hit mechanic. You will never stand right in front of an enemy and miss.
-It allows you to aim at and hit individual parts of an enemy, targeting weak spots and disabling limbs. In the words of a wise drill sergeant training recruits against the bugs, “The enemy can't press a button if you incapacitate their hand.”
-Destructible environment. It exists in XCOM 2, but it is just done better here and enemies never see through walls to shoot at you.
-It adds vehicles to the battlefield and it’s great fun.
-It has minimal bugs.

Things that might annoy XCOM players:
-It has far less individual cosmetic customization of soldiers.
-The game is slow paced. Every playthrough is like playing a Long War campaign.
-In Phoenix Point you have multiple bases and it demands a bit of micro management.
-Turns function as follows: you move all your soldiers, then they move all their soldiers.

All in all I'd definitely recommend playing it. It has its flaws, but the developers have actually been awesome in adding additional content for free and even re-balancing and fixing things the community pointed out. If you start it up expecting it to be XCOM you will hate it, because it's not. It's Phoenix Point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo paraphrase Ned Flanders, "Yes, with an if... No, with a but..."

The tactical layer is really well done. Knowing your lines of fire before you move is key, altho the pure definition of "having a shot" at something is a little strained. I like the 4-AP style better than the 2 AP XCOM one (and it's closer to the originals), and the Free Fire is kind of cool, even tho it seems to miss when I thought that that would be impossible.

But the strategic layer is paper thin. The tech progression in Chimera Squad was more interesting. WAY too much time is spent flying from one dot to another, burning time there, then moving to the next dot. Actual missions are weirdly rare. The factions are interesting, but they don't save the game. They may have interesting stories if you favour each one, but I don't think I'll be playing this thru even once.

Fun for a while, then the classic mid- to late-game problems that all to many of this sort of game have arise, and can't rise above. Worth a fiver, for sure, tho.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a good game, a solid 7.5/10. If you like XCOM, Xenonauts or UFO:Aftermath/Afterlight, this should be right down your alley.

Pros:
- Tactical combat is challenging, but never unfair. If it feels unfair, you're lagging behind on technology. It could use a bit more map variety.
- The lovecraftian storytelling is interesting.
- Global map exploration is something, that I really like, but I would love a broader variety of events. It helps to build the setting so much.
- Managing the Diplomacy between Pheonix and the other factions is a new touch compared to XCOM or Xenonauts. (however there was a lot more potential than what was used. Looking at you FFA-Endgame Faction War.)

Neutral:
- Graphics are okay.
- Air combat in Festering Skies is as mediocre as it's in XCOM 1. It is serviceable, but it's a far cry from Xenonauts excellent air combat.

Cons:
- Can get a bit repetitive due to the limited map pool.
- Festering Skies DLC converts previously 'unlocked' technology upgrades for the fliers into modules that need to be crafted and are limited to 1 per flier and as such are a bit of a pain in the ass.

However the biggest detrimental factor for me is:
The Developers took that sweet sweet EPIC Store money and thus crippled a potential modding scene. So, no workshop support. This game could be on par with XCOM, mod support adds so much replayability. I would love a Pheonix Point 2 thats developed with an active modding scene in mind.

TLDR: Good game, has a few flaws. Could have been better. Give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo upgrade system:

In a general sense, there ARE no upgrades. The way the gearing system works in this game is that the Factions provide you with basically sidegrades…one faction may be better in ACC but lower in everything else, etc. Beside the fact that basically everything that Phoenix Project makes is complete trash, the Faction gear is more tailoring to how you want to play. There isn’t a research progression like the other X-COM games.

I actually find the gearing system overly complicated for no reason. My soldiers always carry extra guns in case I need one that just does lots of raw damage, or one with pierce, or one with acid, etc. I find it rather annoying. And as far as armor goes, it’s basically a Venn Diagram with no happy middle - You can have Armor, Accuracy or Speed, any combination of the two, but the other will suffer. Or in some cases, you can have one and the rest suffer. If you want high Accuracy go Sniper gear, but have low armor and lowered speed. If you want Speed go Assault, but have so-so armor and small boosts to Accuracy, based on which faction’s gear you’re using. If you want Armor, go Heavy, but suffer massive drops to Accuracy and Speed.

This game needs a very good mod that adds better weapon/arnor/vehicle/aircraft etc... techs and alter the magazine system.

Ammunition System:
As for magazine: Its unnecessarily complicated. I shouldn worry with the magazines for each different weapon rather, there should be fixed amount of magazines per battle. That can be increased via research, gear and perks. To balance this out, there can be slightly more expensive weapon crafting system.

As of now, i wouldn't bought the game if I'd know this.
