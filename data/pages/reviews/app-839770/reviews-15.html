\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI did enjoy the game for a while until I hit a difficulty wall I couldn't beat without a mod to help me cheat; dead serious about this. The game has difficulty problems; and I can understand why the active XCOM community put it behind them. I'll go over what I see wrong with it; how the Devs could and should have fixed it, and My hope is that they don't make another Phoenix Point game...

1. It has a steep learning curve even after the patching; this is a game where a mistake can actually cost you the game and it isn't forgiving. No the devs won't fix this either; they stated to me in an email the game is this hard on purpose. If you make any mistakes that cost you too many resources; too many soldiers or even lose a base defense mission, you can lose the game because you just can't fight anymore. Making matters worse with the pandorans is sometimes it isn't your fault a mistake happens or you lose something; sometimes the game will throw a fastball at you and you have no power to stop it period.

2. There is no Haven diplomacy options to get the Havens to stop fighting and hating each other. If the factions get mad there is no chance of stopping them from killing each other off; and this has cost me games because too many people die and I lose every single time... It's a game reset every time the factions go to war. There could have been a lot of options and choices for haven diplomacy but because you can't make havens play nice and you can't interfere with them fighting without all of them getting irreparably angry with you, you have to let them fight each other into ruin and it removes a chunk of your population trying. If the pandorans are particularly aggressive this can make things worse with their already hard evolutions.

3. The Pandoran Evolutions will outrun your ability to fight them; and in many instances even with enhanced units and somewhat better gear the pandorans will kill your squads and even with good unit combos there is a limit. If you have any DLC that adds new unit types; they will get harder and harder and will wipe your forces out. The Trident Varients are one of the worst because while the devs claimed they had the same number of actions as the player units, they have more than that and will use six or seven actions a turn instead of four.

4. The Tech tree is complicated and linear in all the wrong ways and you have to go through the factions to get their gear and options. And if you come to blows with a faction they won't give you any of it and you have to steal it yourself... coupling this with the fact the game has scarce resources you risk spending resources to gain resources and a mistake will cost you and outweigh the benefits of what you stole or gained. Because there are no other research choices or options like XCOM or similar games; some of the equipment and gear you make is linear and predictable. While this can be great because you always know which directions to go to get certain things; you won't outrun the pandorans and you will rarely have enough resources to take advantage of these research options.

5. Resources in the game are scarce and you have to spend resources to gain resources. You can trade resources; scavenge and gather things from battles but beyond that there is hardly any other way to gain additional materials and resources; this slows the game pace down a lot and if you are unlucky a bad mission can cost you and punish you badly even if it isn't your fault. (As mentioned the game has an unforgiving learning curve.) If you spend resources wrong or are trying to save resources the game will find a way to take them from you or force you to risk things to get more. For example ammo and throwables cost resources to make; and if you're low on anything, you're spending resources to make more ammo and that makes it hard to save enough to better things or trades or recruitment. Confounding this further is the fact that scavenger sites do not regenerate and there are no additional options to gain large volumes of resources without angering a faction. I know this game is post apocalyptic but explain to me how scientists with guns can't figure out farming; recycling and technology generation using the items on hand...

(Spoiler Alert)
6. There is no good ending; all of them are bad, if you somehow make it far enough to actually take on and beat the final bossm you will be rewarded with a bad ending of different severity depending on who you allied with and what choices you made to get as far as you did. Of all the endings the Phoenix Point ending is the worst; and so is the Disciples Ending. There is no option for a hopeful future and it's a terrible excuse to set the plot for a Phoenix Point II... Because in the end with how badly written the endings are and the choice paths leading to an end; there is literally no way Phoenix Point can stop a second invasion from Yuggoth without getting their rear ends kicked inside out again... Human Cooperation is how XCOM defeated Advent and in Phoenix Point there is no option for a human cooperation ending that actually suggests a good possible future outcome.

While I love this game from time to time; I have my fair share of issues with it and ran into so many painful challenges in the short time i played it; that I can see why people didn't like it. A major downside to this is that the factions can go to war with each other in as little as two hours of game-play... and you can lose in less time than that.

My recommendation to the devs to fix this issue was to tone down the difficulty and add more options for the players, they blew off my letter of recommendation and refused to address some of the major issues I had outlined from my game-play time.

I hope there isn't going to be a Phoenix Point II because the shortcomings of this one and the fact the devs actively acknowledge the problems outlined and refuse to fix them; refuse to add additional content that could help ease and remedy the game's considerable difficulty challenges, refuse to address the difficulty curve and a myriad of other problems... I do not recommend this game to people looking for an XCOM style title.

The game could literally be the way it is now; if it had more soldier classes, more tech trees and options on par with the original UFO defense, like customization options and maybe even weapon add-ons like XCOM II had to offer, additional vehicles (Come on... XCOM Enemy Unknown had a designated fast mover aircraft class... you're telling me New Jericho can't build fighter jets or VTOLs like the F-35?... or tanks?) The game could have had more random things like more pandoran structure types and challenges; more faction locations, like New Jericho refurbished and Aircraft Carrier or something... better diplomacy options, more research options that are linear and less confusing to use, and a learning curve that isn't so unforgiving that you can lose the game within two hours of playing it. It could even have randomly generated resource sites that have a timer like XCOM II and maybe just enough diplomacy to stop the factions from fighting even if they don't cooperate... It could remain the same game with so many additional fixes and add-ons; even with the high difficulty curve, but the devs in an email i sent them just said no...

If you like a hard and unforgiving game this is for you; If you like an XCOM style game, play XCOM.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you somewhat liked XCOM, either the originals or the reboots, but didn't like the combat or the geoscape, you might like this game I guess. If you're like me and you just wanted more XCOM after the thousands of hours you've sunk into the franchise, just stick to playing XCOM.

The game's setting is neat and interesting for a while, but it quickly devolves into something that's in the end trying to be a "unique spin" on a zombie formula while attempting to constantly flash in front of your face "we're like XCOM we promise" with vague similarities here and there. The faction system seems at first fleshed out, but it's super barebones, basic, linear, and not engaging at all. It is on-par with just clicking yes or no on various pop-ups throughout the game, and in return you get some weapon upgrades that won't matter for a long time, and even when they do you can usually just use brute force + good placements that you've probably built up if you've played any good XCOM games for a good amount of time. The Geoscape is 90% filler, fluff, and unimportant locations you keep going to in the desperate attempt to find anything engaging or find resources. Mission structures and mission types are somehow more lacking than vanilla XCOM 2. Difficulty? The "difficulty" is non-existent until the game forces it to be there. Slow, monotonous, and boring placement as the enemy "not-aliens-and-not-zombies" slowly creep up to you and you blast them with Overwatches set up. Boom. Now, reinforcements show up en masse and you cannot handle them as is without taking casualties. Did you take damage or lose units because you placed yourself badly? No. You played perfectly and the angry GM didn't like that, so they force you to get swarmed so you DO take damage. Don't even get me started on any mission involving human enemies, this game cannot do them right at all. The developers of this game clearly should have focused on just Pandorans as enemies that could pop up, and make any human enemy faction related when you gain enough negative relations with them (which would take time to do unless you bum-rushed aggression at them for some reason). For too long in the game, they have too much armor, too much health, too many numbers, have multiple medkits, are already by default in good spread-out placements at the start of the mission, have some knowledge where you are, and are kitted with ways to destroy your cover and just destroy you. Then you upgrade some stuff in the mid-game and they're a joke and you don't want to even bother with them. You can either ram your head into them early and take losses to do so, or you can force yourself to play the game as intended TM and have no engaging fun. Tactics almost never matter, and whenever you do them well enough you are just forced by the game to endure a wave of reinforcements or to just take a hit because you have to so the tactics get undone and you're back to playing scrappy.

I casually speedrun UFO Defense now and then, and I've played countless times on the harder difficulties in XCOM 2 with added difficulty mods on top of all the nonsense that game can have. I can play those games drunk as a skunk and still do missions flawlessly, and I'm not saying this to just toot my own horn, I genuinely know what I'm talking about and I bring this up to help elaborate on that point. This isn't just some "oh he's a scrub ignore him" take I see all the time, and this game has had criticism leveled at it that absolutely are those types of takes. But, no, this one? Not one bit.

XCOM is a game that prides itself on kicking your ass if you can't figure out how to play it. You make close calls, lose soldiers, take risks, and in the end you try your best to return the favor in incredible scraps and wonderful set-pieces. But, if you're skilled and tactical about things, you can usually destroy missions without taking a single loss or even a single hit. Phoenix Point seems to ignore that last sentence, and thinks that because the majority of casual players only experienced or remember the first two sentences, that THAT is the essence of XCOM by itself and forces the game to feel that way no matter what.

I've played this game back when it came out in the EGS and it sucked massive balls back then. It's improved since then but still keeps the big main issues I've explained in this review, but nonetheless is improved than its original conception. All the DLC also sucks beyond reason, and I am very against anyone thinking this game is worth trying from also purchasing the DLC, because even fans of this game think the DLC all sucks. And I really mean ALL the DLC. I'm currently going to see the rest of this game through on a vanilla playthrough to see if anything changes this review but I doubt it. I am however interested in the mods for this game now that modding support is officially implemented, and will edit this review to reflect my thoughts on the game modded. But the vanilla Steam experience, while better than the EGS version at launch, kinda sucks still!

At the very least, this game runs. It sure does.

UPDATE: Yeah.... no I can't do it. This game is too tedious and repetitive while being incredibly boring at the same time. Don't purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got the game with all DLCs when it was on sale. Mainly because it is Steam Deck verified and I wanted a game similar to XCOM2 WotC. I do like the turn based combat. Love the Free Aim. Graphics are also pretty good. That is really all the positive I can say.

Is it Steam Deck verified but the controller integration is terrible. You often have to fumble around to get to certain menu points. I would have been completely lost if the Deck didn't have the track pads. I configured one of them as mouse. Some menu items aren't reachable at all, via controller. There are older reports about it. Messaged the publisher and they told me that they stopped development and that there won't be any more patches. Good thing I can dock my Steam Deck and then at least play it with mouse and keyboard.

The thing that bothers me most about the game, is the equipment management. You have 3 factions plus your own, that have more or less the same equipment. Which means you have the same item 4 times, just from the different factions. On top of that, you have the ammo as equipment items as well. I wouldn't say anything if it was special ammo, like in XCOM. This way you could specialize your weapons. Nope, it's just the standard ammo. Your gear comes in separate pants, torso and helmet. Again, more or less the same from each faction. There is no good way to compare items. They are all over the place. If there was a way to group for instance all grenades it would make selecting things so much easier. The worst part is that even the better equipment seems barely improved.

I really miss the load out screen from XCOM. Seeing all your soldiers and their equipment in one overview helps to decide if you are missing an ability. You can step through them all, but you don't have the overview. Oh yes, an unequip all would be SO nice as well.

The clutter doesn't stop there. I find the geoscape becomes cluttered quickly as well.

As the game progresses, I find myself more and more busy with managing the equipment and resources than anything else.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably best to skip this one if you like completing stuff fairly. The game has a lot of problems, but I will concede that at the right combination of discounts, mods and a maybe trainer you could extract some entertainment for your money out of it.

The best part of the game is definitely the tactical combat. It's competent and reasonably fun once you assemble a functional team. Manual aiming at body parts is quite satisfying. You can cripple enemies or disable some of their abilities when killing them entirely is not yet an option.

The lore and worldbuilding are also pretty cool and they could definitely serve as a solid platform for future installments. The plot on the other hand is fairly shallow.

Then the problems start. The first one is all the DLCs. DLCs can be integrated into things very well, but that's not the impression I got from this. The first sign that you've got them enabled is the confusing amount of gear dumped into your storage and a ton of quests all over the map. It probably works great if you played the base game and then added them one at a time, but the amount of content and spiking difficulty they bring is quite overwhelming. Crucially, you just don't know if you're following the main plot line or starting some crap that'll increase the difficulty without any real benefit.

Assuming you found some guides or tips on what your priorities are, the next big problem is progression. It really feels like there isn't any. Sure, you get some really crappy items to start with and there's a lot of new gear to unlock, but most of the time it's really not upgrades. There's variety, but it's all about trade-offs. You can get a gun that's a lot more powerful, but the aim is terrible. Or the range is vastly diminished. Or it takes more action points to use. You can change armour, but you're trading between more defense, or stealth, or accuracy. I really wish there was something to research that was a clear, across the board improvement over something you had in the early game, but there just isn't.

Similar stuff on the globe. You have airships and you can have multiple, but it's all just a set of tradeoffs again. You have your base plane. You eventually research a slightly faster one with fewer personnel slots and a slower one with more.

The economy is a real mess. And it doesn't help you have to spend resources to restock your ammo after every mission. There's three types of resource, Tech, Materials and Food. You will eventually research something that lets you grow food, so you can trade that for the other too. But that's something you work out while failing your first campaign and plan for on the retry. Getting resources otherwise is a real gamble. You are just as likely to come out of a mission with fewer resources than you went into it because of all the ammo and gear restocks you'll need.

Then the character skills are a gamble. Finding a sniper with a skill to shoot back when shot at - amazing. Finding a sniper who does extra melee damage - not so much. Some skill sets are vastly superior to others and with the very few recruitment opportunities you get, building a competent team can be a struggle. And you really feel the pain if you let someone die.

Enemies can become a real pain. It feels like many were specifically designed to be frustrating. A lot of them can run into the middle of your team straight out of nowhere and either explode, mind control or at the very least hit you just right to disable your weapon arm, rendering the soldier useless for the rest of the mission. And as a side note, there aren't even that many enemies. Most of the are just re-skins of each other with a bit more armour and an elemental damage twist.

A key thing to navigating this game is figuring out how key missions and technologies you research move the difficulty forward. If you don't do a certain side mission or skip a tech, you can literally prevent a new kind of problem or a class of enemies from entering gameplay. It certainly is a mechanic, but I can't say that it feels right.

Some of the UI and game mechanics are still a mystery to me. Took far too long to figure out how to purchase ammo when equipping soldiers pre-mission without jumping out into the manufacturing menu... Never managed to figure out how to produce artefact class resources at all.

Towards the end of the game things just get overwhelming. You have constant attacks, don't have enough people to handle them all. A giant space crab is destroying all settlements and you're still running with basically the same gear you had at the start, while everything gets harder and more annoying.

Finally, a few tips you might want to consider if you decided to give this a try anyway. Get the mod that make ammo free. You'll thank yourself later. You just need to get the mod, make the game launch with mods, then in the mods section of the game you need to enable the mod, and then configure it to make the ammo free. Nothing is allowed to simple.

Keep an eye on the size of your save folder. If you have too many saves, it can take minutes for the game to open the Save/Load menu.

Consider a trainer. If you already restarted the game and maybe even took the easier difficulty, but it's still getting time consuming, repetitive and overwhelming, just give up. Enable infinite action point, perfect aim, blast through the available missions to see what you're missing on. Then call it quits and play something with a better balance. The XCom remakes are pretty great and you should definitely try Xenonauts for some of the more authentic experience in the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
