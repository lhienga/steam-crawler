\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, It's like X Com - but deeper, with more side tech trees. More troops, more bases, more resource management, more options. You basically get the whole Earth. The DLC's that give you Biotech and Bionics add nice flavor. The Air Game is super simple, but adds to the strategic layers.

I cheated and waited until 2022 for all the bugs to get worked out. And at this point the game is pretty stable without many issues.

Plays on Steam deck, but plays better on a big screen, keyboard, mouse. This game interface isn't set up for Steam Deck. But it is playable, which is pretty nice.

The mutants changing tactics and weapons some does add variety, and the DLC's and side missions are really helpful for fun in the storytelling to get you away from the grind of constant war.

As turn based strategy goes, this one is really good. and when you consider you can actually go to war with multiple factions of mankind and destroy the human race, this game is actually pretty huge on variety and replay value. Basically 6 different factions, you can acquire anyones technology, make them allies or enemies. And the stories, characters, polotics are interesting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like Phoenix Point.
To me, the sighting mechanism of the game gives the same feeling as looking thru the scope of a real weapon.
I did a lot of save-scumming later in the game on my first play-thru. (Vanilla Phoenix Point, no DLC, no mods, veteran difficulty).
I recently purchased all of the DLCs and downloaded the Terror From The Void (TFTV) mod, the PRM Better Enemies Mod, and the PRM better Vehicles mod. I am playing my second play-thru with all DLCs, the above 3 Steam mods on veteran difficulty. The TFTV mod allows all kinds of adjustments by the user to enable or disable game and DLC content. Zig Zag Zog has multiple play-thrus on YouTube that got me to go this direction. I am having a lot of fun playing this, and because a lot of my settings and adjustments are not of the highest level, I anticipate much more fun with this game in the future as I get better. Incidently, with my current game settings, I haven't done any save scumming this play-thru which helps flow and immersion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game, it does things that I like, but as a whole it's actively bad. A dream sequel would mix the free aim and (promised) enemy adaptiveness of Phoenix Point with the polish and presentation of XCOM, while taking notes on ability design from elsewhere. XCOM got the little things right; encounter a freaky new alien and 1 of your dudes shouts 'what the heck is that' as the camera gives the newcomer a bit of fanfare to play them up. Phoenix Point just tells you about 5 new aliens at once in text form outside of missions and when you could care less and explains all its gimmicks up front that would have been interesting to discover first hand. That I think is the most concise criticism of Phoenix Point; it doesn't understand what made its genre engaging. 9/10 times it's just another crabman but this one has meatier buttocks. You have to laugh when the game tells you 'there's a new and deadly strain of the pandora virus' and it turns out to be the exact same triton as before but this one is carrying a shotgun instead of a rifle...the 'enemy variety' is an insult to your intelligence. The game encourages exploitation and is so exploitable that the go to strategy is to make a single soldier that can win any mission solo on the first turn through unlimited actions, if you're principled and don't use that build, then you get to see rest of the game. Hint; the side with more actions typically wins, this holds true across wildly different games and can be concidered a universal soft rule of the genre. As such, don't go handing out unlimited actions, it's just bad. The economy is busted, half the weapons don't get used because they would bankrupt you to deploy on even a single mission. Tactical options that give meaning to this genre's name--things as simple as grenades--are never carried for this reason. Stealth non issue until it's broken with 100% stealth being undetectible because common enemies have such high perception right from the start and once seen you're always seen. Limb health is interesting, but it's broken; once a limb is broken there are only 3 things you can do about it, have a technician with the right skill to heal you, or have the right mutation on that soldier, have the right berserker skill on that soldier, all of these negate the condition entirely but are the only solutions to the problem, only 2 classes have 1-handed backup weapons and 1 of them is already immune to limb loss, also because your soldier's limb health doesn't scale with their general health so they break easier over time no matter how much strength you have.

Of all the DLCs the only one I would keep on is Blood and Titanium, and maybe living weapons. All the others make the game worse.

The Ancients are unfair, unfun, the best part about them is they're finite and you soon won't have to fight them anymore, except for the new pandorans, umbras are about as unfair as it can get; after you kill the guy, a carbon copy will spawn right on top of you and do unpleasant things to your butt, the only possible counterplay is to have 1 guy kill the original and then set all your other dudes around them in a circle facing outward on overwatch and pray that works. Great enemy design. But then the weapons break the game balance over their knee; these gaudy guns are on average 50% better than every other option, in every stat, and completely break the inventory system by not requiring ammo, ammo isn't just free it's unlimited, what was the point of limiting it in the first place then? I thought that was a core mechanic managing limited resources that had alternative uses, I thought wrong appearently. This also breaks the faction system, no longer do you need to interact with them, you can have these super weapons up and running before the factions get their own inferior versions off the drawing boards, so that's another aspect of the game entirely broken by this DLC. Why do I criticize this but not living weapons? Well when your power armor is maybe 20% better but you get only 1 of them ever to share among 8 dudes (minimum) then at least you've got some interesting decisions as to how you should use it, but when your super weapon is at least 50% better than alternatives and can be given out like candy, then the 'no upkeep costs' are just added insult to injury. High pierce negates differing limb armor values, shoot center of mass every time. In every way it's braindead. I wouldn't call Living Weapons a particularly good DLC, but it's at least not offensive.

Corruption adds a bunch of nuicances, useless soldiers that are resource sinks for a useless resource, and useless enemies that bounce around being tanky and annoying but not threatening, they're more a resource drain than anything, it's kind of the theme of this DLC.

Air combat that is gimmicky and weighted against you without any proper tools to make it any kind of challenge, you either show up with the right weapon equipped or you lose yet another haven, even if you do show up properly equipped you'll be losing havens to random dice rolls no matter what you do, and that's not the worst of it; Introducing the worst designed unit in the game, a unit small and low to the ground making it very hard to hit, tanky for it's size, give it a 1 ap fly anywhere ability, and an strong melee (read 'auto-hitting') attack that cannot be disabled and does ludicrous damage also for 1 ap and probably acid to boot, oh and on death they aloha snack-bar for even more acid so if your dude survives the attack he can't capitalize on the bugger's blunder by killing it because he now has to run out of the blast zone on the broken leg the bug just gave him, and broken arm so no attacking either. Now tell me, what's the counterplay to such an enemy unit? If you said end every turn on overwatch and pray it works, then you've also noticed the striking resemblence to umbra, the abuse of defensive and turtling maneuvers, the random chance and hope that your maneuvers do anything, and the required attention of your whole squad for 1 non-boss unit.

Finally cars which is full of more distractions, if you don't mind gambling whether your guns will do anything at all this turn, then you've got another source of overpowered unlimited use weapons that is available even sooner, however; I don't appreciate that kind of gamble, its entirely random and does not reward good play or punish bad. Oh you also get a new car or something, never saw the point of cars, they take up limited soldier slots in missions and soldiers need all the experience they can get, even after hitting max level they need more ability points, so I only ever used cars in base defense where they're a freebie.

Rock 'em sock 'em robots is the best because it adds a difficult yet manageable opponent to the game (though only ever on their own which is disappointing, and they all spam riot shields which is annoying), and then lets you use those same strengths and weaknesses yourself, though some are a bit too powerful and all are much too costly. It also fits with the lore, unlike most other DLCs; a 'the flesh is weak' approach to this mutating virus, to juxtapose with embracing the mutation? I dig it. Vat grown programmable mutated humans (with all the ethical quandaries hand-waved for once), which aren't related to regular soldiers that are mutated, which aren't related to vat grown mutated living armor warn by regular soldiers...ya we're losing the distinction aren't we. Why are these each arbitrarily unrelated? Also there's this egg from outerspace, nice that it showed up now during our 'the ice caps melted releasing an ancient cosmic horror' vibe we had going on, also ancient aliens with rayguns but we never make those, and crossbows it seems..? and clubs, this ancient alien super advanced tech is great for clubs. How does this relate to the pandora virus, the themes of faltering humanity and cosmic horror? Tangentially.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\toriginal review: this is a tough one to review. I'm just going to list off my feelings, here. Does the game disambiguate itself from the newer XCOM games? Sometimes, but at others feels like a cheaper & sluggish knock-off. Is the game world interesting? Definitely, I think the post-apocalyptic scenario a la The Mist is a lot more interesting than XCOM. Are you jonesing to keep playing it? Not really... overall it's a decent game, but the gameplay honestly feels like original XCOM: old- and worse, unlike old XCOM, here a lot of the rpg elements just seem weak & unnecessary.. like the skill trees & research & base building. Really underdeveloped. I would have cut all that & just focused on making a strong diplomacy driven squad action tactics game without the bloat.. because the combat is the best thing, here. It's difficult & bloody & random & the geopolitical stuff is interesting... but after 9+ hrs, no, it's just not grabbing me. ***edit after putting in another 12 hrs: I thought I'd give it another go & this time I'll say I have some new appreciation for the faction mechanics, however- the challenge is just too unforgiving for my taste. the fog of war is at times plain ridiculous and I never feel like I'm making satisfactory progression. it's just all too damn random feeling. another thing is the load times, which drag. this just isn't fun enough, imo.\t\t\t\t\t
