\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent game for fans of X-COM. It's long, difficult, with lots of customization options and multiple endings.

The campaign feels much longer than the newer X-COM games, but there are a few quality of life features missing. For example: soldier don't break glass before shooting through, so if you shoot through glass, the first bullet (or in the case of shotguns all of the pellets) won't make it through. At the same time, the diplomacy features are a nice addition, though the faction ideologies are a bit shallow.

Some features, like secret weapons you can obtain, feel a bit undercooked (you need specific resources and processing sites, which can be frustrating because they very difficult encounters, but you don't have an easy way to tell what type of site you're assaulting beforehand). The mutation and augmentation features are great and add a lot of customization, but most of the options are basically slightly better versions of armour. I found a few combinations that worked really well for specific builds and basically never deviated. That said, between mutations, augmentations, combination classes, gear and unique items, and character-specific abilities, the character build variety is pretty good.

Overall, it's really good! The tone feels more bleak, more desperate, than the X-COM games. It feels like the stakes are higher. It's a bit jankier, but ambitious games usually are.\t\t\t\t\t

What do I like the most about Phoenix Point?
It captures the sense of a desperate fight to save the world from the Pandorans. You mostly feel like you are one step behind, and the game throws something at you when you least expect it. The AI also adapts to your play style, meaning players should change their combat strategy as often as possible.
