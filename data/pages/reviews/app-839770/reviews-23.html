\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs an avid fan of the "X-COM"-Genre i really tried to like this game. And i did find a lot to like about it: The atmosphere (especially in the tutorial), some of the combat- and class mechanics. The voiceover.

But, sadly, overall i cannot recommend it. There are just far too many aspects in which the game is insultingly bare-bones and - frankly - unfinished:
- The UI outside of combat is 1 or 2 steps below minimalistic.
- Finding and maintaining your bases+vehicles on the globe is a chore. If you have multiple squads ready for deployment and just want to find the closest one to a new incident on the globe, you have to visually look for them. Turning the globe near the poles is insultingly bad.
- No way to trash vehicles or get rid of thm in ANY way. Cannot even rename them. There is simply a complete section of the UI just plain MISSING for them. And that despite there being a PAID DLC which focuses entirely on vehicles. WTF.
- Filters for equipment/research are unreliable. Sometimes they work, sometimes they don't. Even if they were working 100% of the time they would be insufficient. Want to see a list of all the Handguns you know about? Though luck. Try to see a list of only head armor? Lol, who would even need THAT?!
- The above makes comparing items/augments almost impossible inside the game. You are better off starting wordpad and wrinting the stats down yourself. This is just not good enough.
- Loading a game inside a mission COMPLETELY breaks the AI. If you ever load a game during a mission you are automatically cheating. No way to avoid it. If there was an Ironman-Mode you could argue that's by design since the developers anticipated that anybody not using it would be a save-scummer automatically....but there is no Ironman-Mode!!!!

Then there are some design dessisions which i simply don#t like but could be considered subjective:
- I love to have more control over my soldiers and their equipment. Being able to chose Perks+Classes and enhance particular weapons/armor would have been nice.
- The game simply assumes that everybody wants to mix+match different armor for stats and look like garbage. Sure, i like to min/max, too....but i would also like my Squad to have a certain visual appeal. In this game you can either look like the faction NPCs, look like your own faction but have bad stats (because the PP armor is simply worse than faction armor) or you can use augments but look like trash because the 2/3 limit means there is no "cohesive" look for them. Not my cup of choices, really.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an excellent tactical turn-based strategy game. The developers poured a lot of love in it to polish it to what it is now.
Pros:
- Best tactical mechanics of its age. Destructible environment done right, cover that actually helps (nothing as satisfying as the enemy hitting the massive stone pillar you are hiding behind), no crazy hit-miss RNG. You point a shotgun in someone's face up close and you will repaint the walls, being able to free aim, the list goes on.
- Beautiful graphics. I loved how things looked from the environment where you fight down to your base.
- You really struggle with resources. I had to sacrifice a lot to get the resources I needed.
- I didnt encounter bugs. Yes, unbelievable I know.
- The campaign is lengthy, it is a long war.

Cons:
- The campaign is lengthy, it is a long war.
- I cant put my finger on it but there is a certain 'je ne sais quoi' that is missing from making this the best game of the genre :(
- There is no detailed character information on your personnel. This irritated me to no end.

All in all I highly recommend this game to anyone looking for a tactical strat\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had high hopes for the game, but overall I'd say it doesn't beat XCOM. Here's the summary:

Pros:
1. Aiming system
2. Evolution of monsters
3. Human factions
4. Overall combat AI is decent

Cons:
1. Easy even at the hardest level
2. Very little variation between battle maps, some of them even seem identical.
3. Most locations on the map are 'havens' belonging to other human factions, and nearly 100% of missions there are to retrieve some objects from (highlighted) containers. Boring.
4. Base building is a lot less exciting than at XCOM. After a couple hours of gaming I still haven't added an upgrade to the one I have, as it was unnecessary.
5. Clunky interface and a lot of micromanagement at equipment level. For example, there are 4 types of armor I can equip an operative, similar in all but one attribute. I have to go through each of them to find the one I need. No combat minimap.
6. Battle maps do not reflect biomes. For example, I get some sort of rainforest in Greenland.

Verdict: 6.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t
