\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried for 35 hours to have fun with this game, but I just gave up.
Too many issues with progression and balancing. The capture system makes no sense when you look at the numbers. You can't capture without accidentally killing most of the time. Enemies too strong for regular soldiers to fight, and veteran soldiers strong enough to clear those enemies out. Snipers are too imbalanced and overpowered. I never even used a priest or technician or berzerker....
while we're on the subject of classes... I HAVE NO IDEA HOW I EVEN UNLOCKED THEM?!
the abilities don't actually do much. everything is far too accuracy and damage number driven. Cover is worthless as a mechanic/system. Abilities themselves don't do much besides "dash-dash-shoot-repeat."
The UI is incredibly bad with a pointless cluttered layout in manufacturing, to just bad design for personnel, to a lack of being able to sort things in containment. The base management screens are hopelessly messy and they don't need to be.
Research gives me resources and a damage boost... so my pitful 10 becomes an 11 damage... yay for +10% damage....
Spelling errors, typographic errors are everywhere.
The Geo-sphere is a mess to navigate: I can't tell what is moving to where at all.
the music is lack luster wannabe EDM techno-esque nonsense that doesn't fit the atmosphere, mood or setting.
the fact that all 3 factions just seem to want to outright murder each other... why? why? it makes zero sense in the context of the game world that they have created. The Diplomacy system, the interactions between factions, none of that makes any kind of sense, gameplay or narrative wise. Don't get me started on the Pure and Forsaken? What the heck was that all about??? I still don't get it.
What you do with each faction doesn't seem to matter: I robbed, beat up, and used NJ as my punching bag... and was still able to make nice with them after leaving them to die.... there is no agency. my decisions have zero consequence/impact.
Free aim... nice idea, poor execution. How my guys are missing targets at point black range I don't get it. The grenade launchers can MISS the bad guy next to me, but somehow HIT MYSELF?!

In one sentence "Phoenix Point is a great idea, that was ineptly written, poorly designed, and incompetently executed."

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCool game with great concept for turnbased missions. Unfortunately this game has winning strategies that when using them, it becomes litterly too easy. There exist builds that can kill all enemies on the map in 1 or 2 turn because of endless actions. All this a long known and never got fixed.

The gameplay itself is amazing. If you ignore the winning strategies, the game is challenging and rewarding. Be prepared to be tested on learning about the strength and weaknesses of the tools (weapons, skills, vehicles, building, research, allies, ...) you are given. Be ready to take a risk and take the initiative. Understand that the war is won on the geoscape level and not on the battleground. If you are loosing, you have missed something important.
The game can be unforgiving when making mistakes triggering a spiral of certain failure.
Unfortunately the game is not always clear on telegraphing what actually was your mistake.

Another weakness of the game is that it feels cumbersome and repetitive too the end. You will know if you are winning but you still have to play it to the very end.

I had a lot of fun playing it and can recommend this game to anyone enjoying complex strategic management games with turnbased combat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately while it's an interesting premise, the absolute lack of content is kind of amazing. You'll mostly just be fighting iterations of the same handful of enemies the entire game. As well, I have played 15 hours so far and unlocked pretty much no upgrades. It's all just side-grades. And what's worse, it's pretty much all just reverse engineering what other factions are already using, so it's stuff you've already seen before.

And soldier upgrades are quite boring with no baseline stat progression -- they don't get faster, better shots, or etc.. over time naturally like in X-Com or games like it. Instead you spend points on ability/passive upgrades. Yet somehow they've managed to make these upgrades much less interesting than XCOM. You don't have the variety or impact of XCOM upgrades or the long-term, generalized passive progression of X-Com. What you have is simply a mediocre split-the-difference.

Overall, it just gets old very fast. Fighting the same old enemies, with the same old weapons, with what feels like the same old soldiers over and over again.\t\t\t\t\t

[Edit Adding:

When you start, you think, this is the same old thing, been there done that...
After you get things going, you realize, this is what XCOM 2 should have been, bad azz!
Issues people gripe about:

Bugs - So rarely saw a bug, cant really say it has any.

New aiming reticle system - SUPERB! Any deaths or failures that occur in battle are almost always my fault. RNG is the past. I am in complete control of my success or failures.

Monsters lacking creativity and seem boring - All games play off the same types of creatures, it is diffult to be original when everything has been played or created already. Sad reason to to gripe about.

Story - There is a reason they are carrying weapons and develop, gotta play to actually hear the stories. THey are pretty good story lines. The more I played the more Phoenix Point shined!!!

Line of sight complaints - Aiming is all based off of line of sight. If you cant see it, you cant hit it. Nothing wrong with that! Honestly, I love the way this plays out compared to XCOM. So much better than the cover in XCOM (I loved and still love XCOM).

Multi class issues - What issues? All of the classes are so unique and bring their own benifit to the table. Crossing them gives you SO MANY combinations/strategies. Love the heavies, Technicians, Assault etc. They are all amazing and add to the gameplay.

Action point complaints? The way the balanced out all of the skills and aciton points, Will Power and Stamina all play perfect together. It forces you to think about each move, and you get some amazing battles, some where you never would have won if you had not made wise choices.


World map confusing and overwhelming - It can be at times late game. Most of the icons on the map are old and useless, you have to keep focused on what matters and not let them bother you.

Faction Storylines arent good - Your on crack, they have great storylines... what are you expecting The Lord of the Rings storyline? They are good stories if you allow yourself to get involved.

Not enough Resources? Your not managing them properly or over spending. I had hardly any issues getting resources and saving them. Learn how to manage them and how to get them, you would be ok.

Worse than XCOM? NO WAY. NOT a chance. This is what XCOM 2 should have been, in every way. All these negative reviews are nuts. This is hands down the best XCOM type game I ever played. My friend also 100% agrees that all these negative reviews are nuts.

Invisible enemies? WTF I never experienced that.
Glitches? Hardly any...
Repair costs? Hardly a concern, never screwed me up.

Comparing gear, yes a little lame, but nothing compared to how amazing the gameplay is.

DLC - I finished the game without DLC, then played again with DLC. DLC bring SO MUCH more to the game, it is amazing! DLC is very worth every dollar.

Conclusion, Phoenix Point is hands down what XCOM 2 should have been. It is not perfect, but it definately amazing and it feels closer to the original XCOM than any game that has come out. The combat is very awesome, you have control over your success and failures, just comes down to you as a commander.

This is a MUST BUY!\t\t\t\t\t
