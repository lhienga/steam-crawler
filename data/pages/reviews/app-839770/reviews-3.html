\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary: XCOM-like that has building issues and the DLC makes it worse.
- Increasingly frustrating combat scenarios.
- Dragged out AI response.
- Game bugs, including an unavoidable soft-lock at the very end of the game on the final mission (for me).

I tried to complete this game just to be able to be done w/ it, but a soft-lock issue kept popping up when I would hurt the final boss in a particular (and necessary) way. I tried the usual troubleshooting but gave up after 5-6 tries cause the game isn't worth it. Let's start from the top.

This is obviously like XCOM but more of a civilization-management aspect w/ faction-diplomacy. Initially it was fun, and a certain level of brutality or hard-learned lessons are par for the course for these types of games. But certain mechanics and combat limitations really get grating after awhile. The most notable thing for me is the arc of grenades. They can only move in a very specific arc, meaning that if you have your unit stacking up against wall, then they can't throw straight up over it. Not the biggest objective problem, but it compounded for me in the late game.

The other annoyance are the enemies, where certain (mind-controlling) varieties will appear, and you have to shift your entire strategy around to getting rid of them as soon possible. Or when the game generates 2 mortar enemies at the same time w/ armor, meaning not only are you being bombarded every turn, but it's not a problem you can even manage w/ all the other enemies on the field.

The DLC makes almost everything more frustrating. Enemy units that can continuously summon reinforcements and reduce your abilities (even after the battle is over). Bugs that have ridiculous movement and melee damage. New types of variants to base enemies that make them more of a pain in the butt. The DLC missions also bait you into attacking the other factions w/ poorly-communicated mission descriptions. The archaeology missions are just tedious that pisses through ammo and risks your best units for small rewards. The vehicles and cybernetic augmentations were actually very cool.

And finally, the moment that got me to write this review. I got a repeating soft-lock on the final mission where hurting the boss in a specific way would cause a never-ending slow-mo effect, locking me out of interacting w/ the game anymore. It was a lot of tedium getting to that point because it's a long mission, and frankly I'm starting all that over again on the hope it won't glitch again.

If you want another XCOM game, just replay XCOM.\t\t\t\t\t
