\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn case you did not know, this piece of art was created by the X-Com creator himself, Julian Gollop!!
Man and how it feels like a modern X-Com successor!
It's awesome. It's fun to play, it's addictive, it has interesting enemies and lore, it’s just great! The story is newly invented but absolutely fitting, every turn counts and the reinvented idea just plays out extremely well altogether.

GAMEPLAY:
The campaign wants you to lose your nerves but it is not as extreme as in X-Com 2, e.g. where you would have to have constantly an eye on the 'AVATAR'-meter.
Instead, the game takes a more chilled and subtle route, at some point though, it starts giving you hard choices with diplomatically relevant choices and sometimes these missions come with a timer, but it is all very well balanced. (at least up to where I am now, and I'm not nearly through the campaign at all...)
To be perfectly honest though: what I personally like AND hate at the same time about this game is its inventory system:
At the positive side: It tries to recreate the original 1994-feeling of UFO. (Watch some Kikoskia LP's if you're completely unfamiliar with it.) Meaning you would have to equip and re-supply your units with just the stuff they need, manually.
This works out for the most part.
But when compared to modern games in the genre it lacks some quality-of-life functions that start getting on your nerves as the game progresses. Examples are included in the PRO / CONTRA section at the end of my review.
Two of my greatest wishes for the title would be the addition of an ‘AMMO’ slot in the manufacturing menu, as well as the possibility of a real alphabetical sorting of stuff to create. Often it feels very random how items are sorted in the manufacturing menu, and as it is one of the core parts of the game, I really think it would deserve an ‘upgrade’.
Currently it is cool for most of the items, but for some exceptions it’s just tedious as hell to find e.g. the right ammunition to produce for the right weapon. (I’m looking at you, TYR-1 magazine! Why do you prefer not to appear directly under the corresponding weapon like your buddies do but instead hide in some place completely unrelated to anything in the manufacturing screen?! )

COMBAT / ACTION:
This one will make your head hurt. In a good way, though. Remember X-Com: Enemy Within where you had exactly 2 actions in your soldier’s turn? Well, forget about that because in PHOENIX POINT all of your soldiers have a fixed amount of AP which they can spend as the like. E.g.: moving your soldier to an elevated spot with sight of the enemy, shooting and then returning to cover is entirely possible, and this adds so much diversity and tactical depth to the gameplay! I really love this system!!

PERFORMANCE:
I did not have any issues running the game, did not encounter any bugs or glitches apart from rarely occurring short-time freezes after being hit with status-changing weapons.
The game is made with UNITY and runs very well also on older machines.
You might consider turning off shadows, though.

AMBIENCE/STORY:
Man this game sucks you in! The lore is incredibly well written, it’s including twists plus it has voice actors which excel at every level! I was amazed by the quality of the voice acting altogether! (Especially subject 24 made me go ‘wow’!)
Music and sound during missions and the ambient theme during ‘GEOSCAPE’ and base-view are okay, nothing special but also nothing to get upset about. Sometimes music is entirely absent in missions which is kinda weird but in tense moments, you most likely get your kick. (I personally really enjoy the Geoscape theme above all.)

ALL-IN-ALL-RATING:
I’d give this one a solid 9/10 because of above given criteria; For someone who’s been part of the X-Com phenomena since the very beginning and is used to it’s kind of tedious inventory management, it is definitely a ‘must-buy’!
PRO:
-\tWhat a great X-Com experience altogether!
-\tBasically the original X-Com with new lore
-\tChallenging but not unfair turn-based combat
- Diverse possibilities of character improvement
- Gameplay during missions is HARD but also FAIR, just like an X-Com successor is
supposed to be!
- Several vehicles to obtain! Not only SHIVs!

CONTRA/Possibilities for improvement and further patches:
-\tConfusing inventory/crafting system – needs REAL alphabetical sorting
-\tInventory system lacks the option to directly compare weaponstats
EDIT: The function to compare WPN/ARM stats is actually in the game, but I didn't find it for a long time... For some incomprehensible reason it is very well hidden... You'd have to equip the alternative weapon/armor and only then the game grants you with a compare window. Pretty stupid imho.
- Base building is very simple and boring. Also it has no visually appealing base-view with
bustling soldiers all around like FIRAXIS' XCOM had.
-\tAmmo needs a separate slot in the manufacturing-menu like:
WPNS / ARMOR / AMMO in order to sort the chaos.
EDIT: You can actually craft the needed ammo for any wpn directly from the soldier equipment window. Using that method eradicates the flaw mostly entirely.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhoenix Point is a direct update of the 1994 XCOM made for the 2020 era by the creator of XCOM Julian Gollop, with a couple things changed around.

Pros:

- You are not constrained by artificial limits like in XCOM, if you have the time and equipment you can respond to 100% of incidents

- Very interesting story, wide variety of enemy types

- XCOM apocalypse style diplomacy

Cons:

- Lacks modern quality of life improvements like removing the need to manufacture ammo, this is a 90s era XCOM game with modern graphics.

- Harder than any classic XCOM game, including terror from the deep

- Julian Gollop has still not learned how to make a balanced game, because all guns are side-grades that doesn't scale with the enemies you encounter

- the DLCs make this dark souls-esque game even harder than the base game

If you are looking for an old school XCOM experience from the creator, or just like Dark Souls style games, this is a must buy. Otherwise, get it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this game as I was in the mood for some kinda X-Com type action...and whilst it is similar...it is a winner in its own right and has some unique quirks and standout points of its own.

I have only played it in its Vanilla form [i.e. without the DLC, even though I own them too] and I have put in over 100hrs into the game. I went in expecting it to be a X-com clone, and initially was disappointed that I cant just apply my knowledge from that game into this one. In the same way when I learnt to ride a motorbike after driving cars for years, I thought it would be the same...but they are not - and both can be enjoyed in their own way.

Phoenix Point has some good stand out moments, like the base attacks and Citadels and the way that the enemy is constantly evolving and adapting forcing you to adjust and change your approach and desperately keep in touch with the technology tree to try to give you the edge [or at least survive the next encounter].

I'm looking forward to trying the game again with some of the DLC enables - from what I can see, they change the dynamics of the game in many new ways giving me something else to get to grips with.

Overall a very good game that gets overlooked sometimes...give it a go...have some patience...and then discover the little gem that it is made by a company that cares abouts its product with great DLC constantly being brought out.\t\t\t\t\t
