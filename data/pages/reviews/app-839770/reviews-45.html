\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm giving this game a thumbs down, not because I hate it but because I love it but it doesn't love me back. This is very nearly the TBS that I've always dreamed of but while it's big on ideas, it has design flaws and minor issues that make it a drag to play.

The biggest issue by far is that I feel like I'm constantly resource constrained in a not fun way. The game has THE BEST resource gathering I've ever seen in the scavenging missions. I really wish it detected when you are low on resources and generated those missions. I'm in starvation mode in my current play through where it costs more, just in ammunition, to do a mission that any mission is worth. I didn't pick up a Sci-fi game to have all my troops run around with swords but that's kinda what I have to do. I was super excited when the factions declared war because haven defenses appear to be the best way to get resources in the game.

Troops are incredibly resource heavy, so much so that after a while it simply is not worth losing a unit. X-Com Ironman Mode is my favorite because of the tension it creates when making choices. I had wanted to play this game in a similar fashion but it simply is not worth losing any units. I found it especially ironic that it costs more to replace a lost troop in a scavenging mission than you are likely to make from it. A strategy game with save and load is really just a puzzle game and this is a great puzzle game but I want it to be a great strategy game.

The biggest tease is watching research project after research project complete and seeing all kinds of new toys become available that I can't afford. A very simple tweak that would solve some of the resource constraint problems and open up all kinds of experimentation in game play is to add one of anything researched to your inventory to try out. I would love the chance to determine if I want to manufacture more or mulch it for a small amount of resources. As it stands now, I feel like I'm struggling to pay for ammo, armor and basic base functionality and can't afford to try out many of the cool things the game has to offer. Or, I do but can't afford the ammo upkeep. The game needs serious economy work.

Ammo is Clutter. Each specific weapon has specific ammo and they all show up in your inventory. In the early game it's not terrible which is why I think the developers thought it was ok but as your troops multi-class and gain proficiencies sifting through your inventory becomes a pain. The "ammo" button feels like a bandaid for a bad design decision and only partially solves the problem.

I kinda hate the skill points and wished they had stuck with perks. Speed, which allows your troops to get in better position and find cover, is a golden path. When you are staring at a pool of skill points it becomes very difficult to not want to dump it into speed. The perks which give you +2 speed along with some game changing ability just feels like a better design and I would be happier not having the option shock of that in addition to stats. Stats are for boomers :P

Various game breaking bugs are also a downer. The only reason why I'm on steam for this one is because the XBone version crashes constantly. I really wanted to play it so I picked it up on steam. Still, the game does some weird things from time to time. I am writing this now instead of playing because I just had a mission fail at great cost to me in game resources (and IRL time) when an enemy's wayward missile blew up the tile I was trying to get to to activate the objective. I would love to say that the game's AI got very meta with that one but it's just another bug.

Would I recommend this game? Yes, and I've encouraged folks to pick it up. I do love the game. It's almost perfect which makes it much more painful to play than if it sucked and I could put it down entirely. However, in the absence of a star rating, in which I would give this a 3/5, the only fair thing I can do is give it a thumbs down and hope that a Phoenix Point 2 smooths out some rough edges and makes this the best TBS ever made.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI agree with most of the review of this game, so I won't rehash too much of what has been previously said. This game has the potential to be a good game, but several things hold it back -- both in design and implementation. For a game of this length it suffers from way too much repetition. There is only about a dozen map looks - pandoran nests, lairs, and citadels all look the same. Each faction map looks the same. The random scavenge maps have little variation to them as well.

Because you have to run so many missions of the same type, they tend to get tedious. Missions basically fall into two types -- ones with fixed forces where you have to clear the map and ones with continuous reinforcements. It is the latter that tends to get old very quickly.

After you have cleared your fifth pandoran nest, lair, citadel they lose their luster.

The game also escalates the difficulty level poorly. A given mission can be incredibly easy or impossibly difficult depending on what random forces show up and how the player's forces start out. Some of the starting positions the player gets put into are just silly.

The AI for the game is reasonable, but the design relies too much on ambushing the player to make it difficult. If compared to X-Com 2: WotC this game feels more dicey. Not because of the combat mechanics, but because of how enemy forces show up. X-Com 2 feels like more of a tactical puzzle game compared to this game which seems more random.

Also, pandorans become tougher as the game progresses to the point of being bullet sponges. However, player weapons and armor do not increase significantly. Because each weapon and armor has to be crafted for each Phoenix recruit, it takes a lot of resources to outfit a team with even mediocre gear. To keep a constant flow of resources one has to engage in a tedious trading game with the faction havens. It is a puzzling design decision to have to use combat aircraft and forces to engage in trading.

Finally, there are still obvious bugs that seem trivial to fix that are still floating around in the game. The most annoying ones are certain Steam achievements failing to pop even when the conditions are met.

The DLC don't really add anything to the game. The living weapons aren't really worth using. Blood and Titanium adds a tough foe and storyline which just kind of peters out without adding to the main game. Legacy of the Ancients does add some useful weapons, but then a tedious method of building them. Festering Skies adds another ambush pandoran that can just pop up out of nowhere and kill a soldier without anything the player can do to prevent it. The aerial combat is uninspired at best. It is arguably worse than XCom's aerial combats. The final boss battle in Festering Skies is also dull.

Speaking of which the game has 5 endings. All of them play out the same. I don't know why they bothered making five of them.

So overall, I would give this game a 5 out of 10 -- not good, not bad, just middle of the road. Pick it up on sale as some of the game is interesting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuch a disappointment

The game wants to reach too far for the level of polish it has. Many good ideas on the paper become terrible burdens once put in practice.

I will pass entirely on the story, it's forgettable and really I could not care less.

Character customization is bare bones compared to XCOM2 and mostly all options available make ugly characters. Seriously who came up with these haircuts???

Micro Management hell:
You have to make sure you craft the proper weapon(s), items, Armor sets( yeah, armor is divided between helmet, torso and legs for whatever reason) and ammo for every single operative you have. You recruit a new guy? he comes in naked and with bare hands (!). Now this would not be so bad if like in XCOM you would have a single base, a single drop ship and manage a single team, + some extra as replacements if something horrible happens to one of them. Here it's different. You have over 10 bases in endgame, which requires building management, dropships and personnel. So you actually need to manage 20 maybe 30+ troops, to the last single detail for each of them. Got yourself a recruit coming form one of the 3 factions? Tough luck, enjoy it while it last, because it's weapons are using ammunition you most likely are incapable to craft for a while. In the meantime, he will be sitting here useless. Don't forget you need to manage your dropships too (and their specific setup). It gets annoying really fast.

Vehicles are useless. Quite strong in early game, they never evolve. This means, they take 3 to 4 slots in the squad that could be filled with soldiers. Vehicles don't get xp. That means they will always be the same fire power. Once they get weak, you will have a hard time replacing them with troopers because the needed ones will be late on the XP for the level of the mission they are supposed to face.

Huge lack of balance between weapons. Heavy machine guns fire 3 bullets per shooting action? really? with poor accuracy of course, and a regular assault rifle fires 6? where is the logic???

Double class system with only 3 classes... What can I say, it's stupid. You basically combine Assault + Heavy or Assault + Sniper. And that's it. Heavy + sniper make very little sense.

And i'm talking only about the working stuff , not the bugs.

I really regret my purchase here, even at a heavy discounted price.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhoenix Point is XCOM2 with a slightly different story. You know, I know, and the developers were probably tired of the XCOM copyrights being controlled or owned by another entity and decided to start their own intellectual property based on a slightly different idea. What idea you might ask? How about if H.P. Lovecraft was obsessed with the Ancient Aliens History Channel series and wrote a story about it. You have weird sea creatures coming from the briny depths to wreak havoc on the world of men, a mist comes from the world's oceans and turns people into sea monsters, and antediluvian civilizations with technology far more advanced than modern equivalent existing in some alternate version of history. What this adds up to is a kind of bland story with enemies that seem kind of out of place. This should have felt interesting and fresh with such a crazy story in the background but I was put off by it. The story isn't explored in the kind of detail needed to flesh it out and you don't feel the stakes of this game as strongly as you do in XCOM2. The aliens seemed terrible and brimming with sinister intent and you never know what they are plotting until the end of the story when it is nearly too late to stop them but here it's a virus that only infects some people when it feels like it and a bunch of sort of intelligent sea creatures that get progressively harder for reasons that aren't clearly explained other than they are mutating. The stakes don't feel nearly as oppressive as any XCOM. I still recommend it because the trappings of XCOM are all here. The tight tactical game play with added the benefit of vehicles (which to be honest aren't much of a benefit) and it is genuinely fun to play through the campaign. I wish they would double down on the vehicles. Make them a part of the game or get rid of them. Put enemy vehicles in the game and add a slot in your drop ship for carrying one vehicle separate from the unit slots so that you don't have to remove units in order to make room for one vehicle.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very unique take on the XCOM like games it brings both some very fun and unique mechanics, and some annoying ones. I do the precision aiming as it lets you get more accurate shots, and blasting body parts off to weaken enemies is fun. However the opposite is true. Break an arm and most soldiers become useless for the rest of the mission, and break both legs, and you won't be going anywhere. I'm also not as big of a fan of all the inventory management as it can be really annoying to run out of ammo near the end of a mission while XCOM just gives u infinite ammo (excluding grenades). Classes are also a pain to unlock as to get all three not only requires getting the research option but spending in game time to unlock it while in XCOM 2 you have all classes available at the start, and it hardly takes any time to unlock the four classes in the expansions. Though I will admit being able to unlock a secondary class for each unit does allow for some great combinations. Vehicles are my favorite units in the game (aside from one useless one) I used the Phoenix and new Jericho vehicles a lot especially for scavaging. The Beast you can create are great for long missions. As for the DLC They do add useful new mechanics and enemies however the fourth one that add corruption makes the game more a pain to play as the only units immune to corrupion are muttons which are glass cannon units that can't be healed like normal units. After 2 play attempts I decided not to play with the 4th expansion, and honestly I had a much better time without it. While I do recommend the game I suggest not getting the 4th expansion. Is it better than XCOM 2? Nope, but it comes very close. What really sets them apart is unit customization and progression. Your customization options are less than half of what u can do in XCOM 2, and u can't even add backstory to units either which helped you make a better connection to your units. As for progression I hardly felt like my equipment or even the enemies where getting much stronger. In XCOM when you upgrade your armor and weapons they get stronger and you have more health points, but in this game u can't do either, so your units will be using slightly better guns with the same armor all game, and while cybernetics and mutations can potentially make units better they are permanent changes and both come with drawbacks that made me reserve them for only a few units. As for enemies you'll be killing basically the same crab men as fish men with different body parts all game. sure they do get a few unique abilities as the game progresses, but it doesn't take long for it to reach a cap the leaves only armor upgrades for them which only somewhat make them harder. XCOM has far more unique enemies that each bring something different and threatening to the fight. I give this game a solid 8.5 out of 10 as a great game for XCOM fans while we wait for XCOM 3\t\t\t\t\t
