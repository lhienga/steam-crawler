\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor players of the Xcoms (like me, who played them all from day one, including ironman): PP is suppier to X when it comes to combat. Aimed shots at different body parts can really be interesting and strategic; there is also stealth; armor and shredding; poison, virus, paralyzing, stunning, mind, control, vehicles and more.

Recourse management and soldier equipping (weapons;consumables;armor;bionics; mutations; multiclassing and tweaks galore) also make this part of the game better than the Xs for me.

You will also meet 3 other factions, who have pretty decent personalities and diverse goals and technologies. Depending on how you handle your diplomacy, 1, 2, or all of them can give your some pretty amazing help and tech. Although, their fighting among themselves can get kind'a annoying.

Sadly, unlike the 3 NPCs, you and your bases are just, well, "there". Barely. Bases are pretty much just paper-doll cut-outs, where you place generic "pictures" of what you construct. And you will have to build more than one around to world in order to help you in your goals in the game. I felt almost no emotional attachment to my bases, or to a lesser extent, my soldiers, except they are very interesting icons and strategic tools to use in the game.

The game balance is what almost had me giving thumbs down. I got slaughtered on my first playthrough on Normal difficulty (I should have started on Rookie, like others suggested). Only made it half way through the game on my second try, and am currently barley holding on by my fingernails on my 3rd attempt. The game can be downright brutal, and even if you have played Ironman Xcoms (like me), get ready for some hair pulling moments. It can be a frustrating game, and unless I am mistaken, it almost seemed the AI got better units and abilities the better I got (which would remove any sense of progression you achieve).

Ironman has always been my 3rd playthrough setting on every Xcom (Normal, then Hard, then Ironman Normal), but to finish my review with a warning, I will NEVER play PP on Ironman. Only the ability to reload my saved games kept me from quitting this game and giving thumbs down. But I can see how some hard core fans would totally love this aspect of the game.

Still, it gets a thumbs up from me due to all the great new innovations and additions to the genre, and a definite buy if you can get it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

A lot of the reviews are from people who haven't even played the released game, or simply attacked the developers because of some drama that happened during a kickstarter for the game (purchase, play for 15 minutes, review, refund). Some are complaining that they got bored after SIXTY HOURS?? Some people think the privacy policy is suspicious. If you don't like it, then block the game executable in your firewall, easy, done. It's not like it's a multiplayer game.

The game has some glitches and issues, like enemies melee attacking characters way above them vertically, and sirens being able to panic an entire team for 5 turns in a row. Enemies could be more diverse, as after a while, you see the same enemies doing the same things. However, the game itself is good, and worth the $30 I paid for on sale. (opposed to the $60 I paid for Xcom). You can expect your first campaign to last around 60-70 hours, which is more than fair for that price.

So yes, if you enjoyed Xcom, you will enjoy this one. Don't judge the final product based on anything else other than the final product.\t\t\t\t\t
