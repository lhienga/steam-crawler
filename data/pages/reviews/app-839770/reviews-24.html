\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe biggest problem I have with this game entirely revolves around the convoluted Tech Trees. You can spend a day(s) researching a way to improve your weapons/gear and only getting ie; a new Pistol and that's it. Research often doesn't feel meaningful, just required and tedious to move the thin plot forward.
Speaking of Weapons, the Devs could have included more flavor text for each device as the first play through you're left wondering wtf are Mounts? You'd have no idea until you physically built every device to confirm their slot. A very simple thing they could have done that would be highly beneficial to new players. Not sure if omission was due to laziness or doing the bare minimum to meet project goals.

Then there is the inventory/manufacturing system. Once you've played for awhile it gets filled with tons of clutter. You can filter by character class, but there's no way to filter out basic gear. Having a button to hide Basic Gear / Ammo would go a long ways to reduce screen clutter.
There should also be a breaking point in the tech trees that makes some of that basic gear instant to produce. Unnecessary tedium. I saw someone created a Mod to make this happen, will be sure to pick that up.

As to the combat, there's plenty of it. Apparently there's no real need for plot if there's just an endless string of combat and side missions. The differing factions at first sounded interesting, but near the end of the game I was thinking maybe the earth would be better off if the Aliens killed them all.

There's a lot to like about Phoenix Point, but there's constant failure among the small details that make it painful to play sometimes. Recommended with Reservations.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhoenix Point is a decent addition to the tactical turn based genre from the creator of the original X-COM: UFO Defense. Just like that 1994 classic, this game is brutally hard at higher difficulty settings. The action point system in this game gives you a lot of freedom to employ different tactics and combinations of soldier types. The graphics portray the world and the horrors of the pandorans very well, however I personally wasn't a fan of the art style of the soldier types and factions, they seemed kind of generic.

Completing the game at higher difficulties is a tedious task, with nearly every shot on your soldiers causing a body part to be disabled and bleeding to occur. The pandorans also evolve quickly to become almost overwhelming, you have to rush to research the end game and miss most of the content, so it is recommended to play on normal if you want to experience as much as you can in the game and enjoy the play through.

Some of the mission types use repetitive strategies, such as the base invasion missions, there is no variation in the approach as you can just set up your squad to destroy the enemies as they come through the entrance.

There are also a few annoying things in the game, such as the overwatch system, which triggers even if your unit only spots a small portion of the enemy. If you have multiple units on overwatch at the same point, the target that triggers overwatch will keep moving while the full animations play, which means some overwatch units don't trigger, or end up shooting parts of the environment as the target continues to move. Very irritating. It is also recommended never to just press the shoot button, as some times even though it says you have a shot, you can end up just shooting a piece of cover in front of you (or squadmates), as the shot success only requires a tiny piece of the enemy to be visible. Always use free aim.

The game gave me vibes of terror from the deep (please someone do a new take on that game!) with the pandorans looking like crustaceans and invertebrates mixed with humanoids. Overall the game is enjoyable and I recommend anyone who enjoys squad based tactics games to play it.\t\t\t\t\t

Balancing reasons I assume, but at that point why bother having these vehicles in combat in the first place.

The ideologies of the factions make no sense. Synedrion’s approach is most absurd, “we’re faced with a natural force that is actively hunting humanity to extinction, so let’s try to find a way to coexist with it” .... what? Imagine this happening with the Flood in Halo, if the UNSC and The Covenant instead of joining forces to exterminate the threat, just sent The Arbiter and Johnson to The Gravemind to say “Can’t we all just get along?” Then there’s the Disciples of Anu, they seem to think that if they convert themselves into Pandoran-Human hybrids the encroaching threat will leave them alone. Even though this is clearly not the case because the Pandorans continue to attack and destroy Anu havens. New Jericho makes the most sense, but given their competition this isn’t saying much. Jericho rallied behind a resolute commander to fight back against the Pandoran threat. How do they do this? Well, they created a special unit you see, a unit with big mechanical arms… that… is a healer. It goes around, applying med kits to wounded soldiers and and repair kits for soldiers with augments. A faction whose goal is to beat back an enemy force with military might had a healer for a special unit instead of say, oh I don’t know, tanks?! Or even a flame thrower unit makes more sense. I know this doesn't affect the game play that much, but it is difficult to get invested in the story when your "allies" are not very smart.

The UI is overall a mess. When in the manufacturing tab, sometimes the class filters for items won’t change colors to indicate whether that filter is active or not. Also, when scrolling to view the items to craft it won’t show the bottom 2 slots until you scroll ~3/4 of the way down, then they will re-appear. The sight line indicator for when previewing a soldier’s movement can sometimes be a lie. Move to a square to get an angle on an enemy only to make the move and not be able to target said enemy. This is partially an issue with Snipers and Heavies, since they have limited movement they can execute before they lose the ability to take a shot that round.

Speaking of shots, once Pandorans get their guns, your soldiers are basically done. Even if they do not die, it only takes one bullet of the six round burst to incapacitate one of your soldier’s arms, making them unable to use their rifles. To add insult to injury, enemies simply DO NOT MISS. Half cover, full cover, does not matter, your soldier is taking damage along with a bleed or a disabled limb, generally both in my experience. It does not help there seems to be no way to increase the accuracy of your soldiers outside of getting closer. There are perks and helmets that give “+perception” and “+accuracy” but these boosts do not translate clearly into the gameplay (assuming they actually affect anything and are not bugged). Unless your soldier is in an adjacent tile to whatever they are shooting at, the percentage to hit does not seem to rise above 40%.

The operative extraction and objective defense missions are the ultimate “get bent” to the player. Both are pretty straight forward: go in, escort target out, or defend structures from enemy attacks. The catch is you will spawn 2-3 turns away from the already active enemies. So, by the time you do get to where the action is, your objective is either already dead or is about to die. For example, a mission where you need to clear out a base with Pandorans inside and keep structural integrity from hitting zero. You load in and the enemies already have the key structures surrounded. Those 2-3 turns to get to the enemies is enough to allow the enemies to attack and destroy as much upwards of 90% of all structural integrity. My worst case of this was when I had 11% left by the time my soldiers saw the first enemies, and the slowest unit had 17/20 speed.

The final straw that broke the camel's back for me was this last extraction mission. I was tasked by an independent haven to find and escort a VIP out of a Disciple of Anu haven. Again, my soldiers spawn 2-3 turns away from being able to engage the enemy. Meanwhile the VIP is already active, surrounded by 6 soldiers, unable to defend himself, and with nowhere to hide. Mind you this was the third mission of a fresh play through. I cannot tell whether this is intentional or was an over site during development that never got fixed. This is not just a small issue, these rescue missions given to you by factions can determine how your relationship with said faction develops. If the VIP dies, the relationship can sour, causing that faction to hate you and not trade resources or research with you. These faction relationships can also influence which ending you get from what I understand. Meaning, you have a chance to get locked out of one of the potential ending to the game because of an unlucky (or potentially bugged) designed mission.

In summary, this game gives off the impression of not being finished and will never get finished. It has an interesting premise for a story but fails to make it compelling in the beginning. The Epic Games store should have kept this for itself.\t\t\t\t\t
