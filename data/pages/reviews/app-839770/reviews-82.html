Veteran XCOM player and i've played almost every single one from the very first ones.
Absolutely love these alien invasion games. (XCOM. UFO. Xenonauts etc) New and old!
Even bought this game a second time as i don't want to play it on epic games store.

Even though the original XCOM creator made Phoenix Point there is something lacking with it.
The maps are super dull. Everything from base defence. Lair assaults to resource missions. They don't really feel 'alive' or 'used' in the same way XCOM does. It feels like a movie set from the 70's.
The assault missions feel like a chore instead of a daunting task.

Lackluster customization. Research don't offer much feel of progression (Unlike XCOM:EU / XCOM 2 it offers no customisation other than colour change of gear and hair. The research is super lame as you don't feel the power increase of weaponry. Or atleast in the same sense as XCOM does when you get Laser / Plasma upgrades:)

It feels very clunky aswell. You are not allowed to move multiple units at the same time and the move animation will take up alot of time. (Yet again; In XCOM EU/XCOM 2 you can order another move order once someone is running. It's a small issue for some. A big one for me.)

To finish it off i'd like to say this game is kinda crazy on the higher difficulties.
I've finished XCOM 2 Impossible Ironman fairly recently. It took a ton of tries but i did it.
Here i'm unable to play on veteran without save-scumming. The opposition gets some crazy tools at their disposal: Pretty tanky mindcontrol units (that love to run and hide once they mindcontrol somone btw) acid artillery. You name it!
Even the 'fodder enemies' become quite dangerous later on as they get a resurrection ability. So even if you kill it it comes right back to cut you down the next turn.
The upgraded armours tend to have a negative accuracy penalty to them aswel and it's just annoying.


TL;DR: I *really* wish i enjoyed this. The concept is fantastic but the core gameplay just feels like work.
Every time i booted it up both on the steam and epic version one thing ran through my mind: Boy i wish i was playing XCOM 2 right now.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's like Xcom 2 but with less polish and way more content. Xcom vets should really check this out as it has many features your used to and a decent amount of curve balls such as bases, havens and resource management. Down the road you get to mutate/cyborg enhance your soldiers along with choosing a second class for them like Sniper/Infiltrator or Heavy/Engineer and all the while your main enemy is mutating its own forces to counter your research (but only up to a certain point).

Only a few minor annoyances but nothing really to complain about. Small things like "free aim" sometimes did not work when standing behind obstacles so you had to rely on your soldier picking a body part and shooting from cover.

I'd honestly keep playing this game after beating it as it can look rather bleak once you feel ready to take on the end game mission. It would have been nice to watch the "bleakness" recede while you still help havens defend themselves.

Anyway, two thumbs up.

Edit:

About towards the end of my second play through and once you know how to manipulate the trade system and can raid your allies for supplies (that also trains up your squad) then your pretty much just waiting for your choice of end mission to pop up. I've got 5 squads, 4 on haven guard duty around the world and my 5th is the cream of the crop that does the hard missions and can wander the earth at will. (Uses the slowest ship but allows max of 8 capacity)

I also tried to get into the melee unit of this game but found it underwhelming even with max skills and stats. Sure, they can chain kill (way better after cyber upgrade) and take more damage but pretty much die just as easily when going toe to toe with a melee spec'd baddie. Priest units sound fun but still can't bring myself to build one up. I need the squad slots for 2 snipers, 2 assault, 1 heavy, 1 engi, and 1 stealth/scout.

Side note: On second play through found a new mission type where you can "rescue" a vehicle instead of solders. Spiffy but I never use vehicles since they take up two slots on transports.

Side Note 2: Screw you ambushes. Enjoy killing my one guy I sent to scout with no armor or weapons. I'll just replace him with a new "volunteer".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing seventy hours of this game, I decided to delete it after the game famous imbalance start to show up. I get it, the game is basically x-com survival mode, with enemy that will eventually win, and you are racing to come up with a solution before that happens. The problem getting to that point is a long tedious slog and if you f-up it can be a major blow which one cannot recover from. God help those that would play the game in ironman mode and not do some serious research beforehand, you could wind up playing a defense mission that is simply too hard and wreck your squad that is simply unreplaceable.

The mission that made me quit was an allied based defense mission (which is a mainstay of the game, it is basically a terror mission from x-com but you will do dozens of times.) The numbers made it look like it was doable. Until I do the mission and find out it is mini kaiju boss pandoran and its retinue of snipers and mind controllers. My squad gets recked by turn three as one my guys are dead two mind controlled and suck allies on the other side of the map who were crushed by turn two. It is doable with the right squad but since the right squad was on the other side of the planet. Let us just say it is infuriating to get near total party killed by a killer cpu cheap move. Did I mention that it takes about two to three guys to generally kill a regular pandoran and the whole squad to kill the boss monster? Gun magic can help but you need the right gun magic and that can only be gained by xp not gear.

Outside of my scrub complaint other things that I didn’t like.

The setting takes place after the end, but you have three major factions in the entire world which weird ideologies that make it feel like you’re playing Alpha Centauri game (only worse since you play mercenaries who is doing all the work and getting ♥♥♥♥ pay in the process) instead of x-com. Hell, I wish I can play one those factions instead of Phoenix Point since at least they can get their own resources.

Another complaint I have is the game is really freaking stingy with it’s rewards. It take about two terror missions to kit out one soldier, while also having demands on base and vehicle constructions and recruitment costs. Granted you can trade which helps a little bit or steal which is difficult.

To sum up it’s like Firaxis X-COM but worse, with flexibility that you wished had in X-COM but the punishment you get for it make just appreciate the causal mode that you got with X-COM

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe inventory management system is a real pain in the ass and a definite step down from XCOM2. Units are recruited without weapons or armor, and because resources are scarce you probably won't be able to build a full kit for every soldier. This means you have to constantly unequip armor, weapons, and ammunition from one soldier so you can give it to another, which you must do manually for every damn piece of equipment. Moving a full kit from one soldier to another is 3 pieces of armor + 1-2 weapons + ammunition + grenades/healthpacks so at least 6 actions, and then another 6 to reequip them on your other soldier. That's presuming you want them to have the exact same kit, if you need to rotate equipment between three or more soldiers it's a total headache.

To make this worse, units can wear any armor and multiclass to use a lot of different weapons, and the only way to see which unit is equipped with which gear is to manually check them one by one. I can only play the game for an hour or two at a time before getting sick of playing inventory manager.\t\t\t\t\t
