I've been thoroughly enjoying Phoenix Point. I come from a background of hundreds of hours in every modern tactical XCOM, with and without dlc, iron man on and off, mods disabled and enabled. I've tried other XCOM-likes such as Xenonauts, Othercide and Mechanicus (not a complete list). Phoenix Point is my favorite non-XCOM on the list so far.

The tactical elements here are awesome, just about the right level of depth without getting too complicated. The difficulty feels brutal but largely fair. It's worth noting I'm using saves for this run. The content is pretty engaging and stays mostly fresh, though my campaign has been on the long side (close to 60 hours and I'm not done with a run yet).

I came in with low expectations due to all the poor reviews and I've been happily surprised by basically everything. Beyond all other elements I was surprised by how engaged I became with the story and the worldbuilding, there's a lot of interesting stuff going on here. I am a bigger fan than most of XCOM's light lore and its characters, but its admittedly pretty sparse. Phoenix point is not, it seems there was genuine thought and care behind the world they put together here and the events that will transpire dynamically over a run of the campaign.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf XCOM is paying for a 9.99$ item using two 5$ bills, then Phoenix Point is buying that same item with exact change. While the plethora of choices for every single action you take gives the user freedom to make unique decisions, in my experience it bogs down the game and feels like a chore. The game is very very slow if you want to play well. I'm talking slower than OW crawling in XCOM EU slow. Realistic ballistics is cool, having individual ammo types for guns is immersive, and choosing where to exactly fire your weapon is interesting, but all these added choices quickly cause choice anxiety and start to feel unfun rather quickly. This game, in my opinion, fails because it over complicates the XCOM formula, which it is clearly based on. For those who enjoy the Long War mod, maybe this is more your speed, but I personally think it's just too complicated for the wrong reasons.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA modern Xcom UFO Defence, but terribly unpolished. With boring atmosphere.
Get it if you need to scratch the itch. Don't do DLCs ( so I've heard ).

Excellent combat gameplay design. ( Ballistic sim, real aim, armour, health, shock, daze, etc )
I have had hours of fun puzzle solving battles. Much room for tactical manuverability.

Get the mod "Assorted Adjustments" for significant Quality Of Life improvements.

Lack of quality-of-life features and information readouts. ( Unequip all, available vs owned equipment )
Poor interface design in key areas. ( No ability to Sorting, ordering, only limited filtering. )
Unresponcive Geoscape UI.
Poor performance in menus / glitches.
Poor/underwhelming level execution. ( eg. special missions are just normal missions with a slightly tougher hat. )


In short:
Agh, I'm disappointed by how... unfinished and shallow it feels. But I like this game a lot in some ways.
  • Combat and soldiers are extremely cool, customisation, multi-weapons, multi-class, ballistics. Lovely looking game. Fascinating world and story. "Real-World" style Xcom narrative and setting.
  • But the soldiers feel dead, gameplay is complex, skills/perks are mediocre or 100% necissary. Maps never look "relevant" in that freaking HAVEN SIEGES are just a few bad guys placed randomly around a map that is otherwise totally untouched. No fire, no obvious path of enemy attack, no sandbags, no defences, nothing, not even "battle" ambience. Unit spawning and map detailing is utterly pathetic. Music is sad and tiring, never gives me any will to play. Crucial UI like MANUFACTURING / SOLDIERS / RESEARCH is awkward, hard to navigate, hard to search, barely sortable.
Over all, it's terribly dissapointing and bland. Despite it's moments of colour and excitement.


In Long:
I've been playing this game for hours, and have gone over the hill and into the valley of loving then being very dissapointed this game.

Phoenix Point has an awesome combat system with an actual physical world. Soldiers actually carry their inventory, so they can have MULTIPLE GUNS. You can also have double-classed soldiers who are both Heavies and Berserkers, Snipers and Technicians, Priests and Assaults. Allowing some insane weapon combos and abilities. I love that about it so much. But.

So much damn promise. So much it could be.

The bloody SPECIAL BATTLE MISSIONS, where these massive havens are "under assault" and need your help to defend a special facility or plot item, just has some bad guys spawned in random places across a generic map and UTTERLY NO indication that there is combat or even that anyone even gives a damn about defending these places. No fortifications, no sandbags, no allies, no damage, no distant sounds of battle, no walls, just a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ normal map with some tough bad guys just randomly spawned around the place. Utter lazy emotionless crap.

It just feels like it's been left at 50%. I'm so dissapointed.
Just don't get this. It's not fun once you realise how much they don't do. Just how fake the maps feel. Just how incomplete and feature-deficient a lot of the menus and systems are. And how utterly half-assed the atmosphere and believability of the maps and missions feel. I am so sad this game is in this state.

BUT it has the same damn issue ALL OVER ITSELF. It is INCOMPLETE. Every last environmental and story detail just feels like it got to 80% then got left as is.

Equipment, as one example, you can equip soldiers. So it's VERY damn important. But is there some way to sort equipment? Alphabetically? Weight? DPS? NO. Do items STAY in the slots you put them in? NO. The order isn't preserved and is instead decided by some internal per-item values.

You also have to manufacture your own equipment, but when it comes to picking what to manifacture, again. There are no Quality Of Life sorting options. No actual DESCRIPTIONS. The F&&&ing things have a massive middle space for item descriptions that is 98% blank. I'm not joking.

It is just so LAME. So limp and bland. Like they try so much with so many things, but never complete it.
It's visually nice I guess, has interesting weapons and armours and the unit's actually being able to use multiple weapons is great. But It's all so INCOMPLETE. They go far enough for it to be almost good enough, and then just stop.

It's got NO story worth playing for. I'm sorry, for ALL the story that they try to show, and narrate, and do stuff with, there's just nothing of immediate substance. No characters with you on your journey, XCOM 2 does it right.

Just please, go play Xcom.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game. I loved both XCOMs, and this game does a lot of things well in a way that could have made it a decent successor. Unfortunately, it just fails to be fun to play, in a way that makes the experience drag a lot.

On the tactical layer, I prefer this game's action point system to XCOM's, and while there are lots of issues with the 'true line of sight' system, I think it's genuinely novel and innovative in a way that I can overlook it's failures. Vehicles are also an interesting and welcome addition to the formula, as they fill the role they're meant to well and give new tactical options. Overall, the tactical layer is actually quite comparable to XCOM's, where it should be almost just as fun.

The strategy layer in Phoenix Point is where the game really excels, and it actually blows anything in XCOM out of the water. Instead of one worldwide base, you have a number of bases throughout the world, and you manage aircraft and troop deployments individually. You actually have to make real strategic decisions as well; it's almost certain you'll reach a point where you want to protect a haven or complete some other mission, but all your troops in the area are injured or there's just nobody nearby. Resource management was also done well, the only real change I felt would help a lot is some idea of what resources you might get from missions before you take them. The exploration mechanics are neat, and the minor world events they can reveal add a lot to the worldbuilding, even if most of your choices don't matter too much. While the diplomacy system is a bit underfeatured (you can't use your influence with factions to really do anything other than obtain more quests and eventually techs from them) it's cool that it exists, and the factions are more interesting and fleshed out than I'd expected.

This game had all the right ingredients to be genuinely good, which is why it kills me to have to leave a negative review. But I feel the need to not because the game was particularly bad, but because it was so close to being good. What Phoenix Point really lacks is polish, and while it seems lame to hate it for this, the UI/UX was so horrendous that playing any portion of the game becomes a colossal slog. I beat the game once, and while I'm a bit curious how the other endings go, it's absolutely not worth the fight to finish it again. I'm going to list the biggest problems I noticed, because there were so many that substantially interfered with potential enjoyment.

- The worst UX decision, by far, is the fact that you can't pause during the enemy turn. If you need to urgently step away for a moment, hope you don't care what the enemies are doing because you won't get to see it. Far worse though, if one of your soldiers that you absolutely need dies or you've made some other unaffordable error during the enemy turn, you have to wait for the entire enemy turn to finish before you can restart the mission (or even exit to the menu and quit if you're hardcore). Enemy turns take a long time to finish. It depends on which enemies you're fighting of course, but you have to wait for each unit to move, play a fairly long attack animation, sometimes attack multiple times, and repeat for every enemy on the field. A specific type of enemy does all this, but is also invisible (so you don't even get to see the attack animation play), and can attack up to 4 times if it doesn't move, playing the full animation that you can't see each time. Add in the fact that there can be a few of these at once and you're waiting multiple minutes just to exit a battle you know you can't win. If they just fixed this, I could probably make this review positive, but it makes the game so painful to play I can't justify doing it any more. And maybe I'm just bad needing to restart missions so much, but it's a feature included in the game, so it should be more usable.
- The tactical layer forcefully pauses any time you reveal something new, even in the middle of a move. This is a good idea because it lets you react to things as they happen. However, if you already generally know or don't care where something is, the camera still gets recentered and you have to make the move command again. If you're trying to go a long distance (especially with vehicles) and there are a lot of enemies outside your line of sight (often happens with worm launchers), you might have to drag to the same point and click many times in a row. A simple hotkey to continue a movie after a pause would easily fix this.
- On the strategy layer, the game pauses when something happens or a ship reaches its destination, and unpauses automatically when you give a ship orders. You often want to give multiple ships orders at the same time though, as the starting aircraft can't carry a full squad worth of troops. You can't do any sort of multiselect though, and you can't make the game wait for both orders unless you set it to unpause manually, so you have to let one lag behind the other, cancel the mission start when the first plane gets there, then wait for the second plane before starting it. I'm not sure what the easiest fix would be but this gets very grating over time.
- Unlike XCOM, this game has a technology queue, which is nice. However, you can't really change the queue order manually; all you can do is add new research to the back, remove items from the queue altogether, or move an item in the queue to the top so it's active. If you want to order three or more technologies to be up next, you have to shuffle them around a bit and it's unintuitive. It absolutely needs a click and drag interface, but honestly even XCOM's approach of only considering one tech at a time and selecting a new one when it finishes would be better.
- Manufacturing specific items is a pain, because all the items are jumbled together on the manufacturing list. You can filter the list by class that uses it, but by default all class filters are on every time you open the menu, so you have to click on every other class filter to disable it every time, and there's no check/uncheck all option. Even when filtered though, you still see all items that aren't class specific and all the items you don't want to build because they're below your tech level, so it's super cluttered. Even that wouldn't be so bad except there is no sorting at all. Items are not in alphabetical order or even in the order in which they've been unlocked, you'll just have to find wherever the devs randomly decided to stick that weapon or ammo amongst all the other items.
- Clearing exclamation marks from the 'Phoenixpedia' is a huge pain. Any time you find a new weapon, a new enemy type appears, or you research technology, the alert pops up showing you there's something new, even if you just read the full text when it first popped up. If you're like me, this annoys you, so you go in to clear the exclamation marks, but again they're not neatly sorted, so you have to just scroll down the appropriate column until you find where it is to hover over. This is amplified if you research a new weapon technology; clearing all exclamation marks means you need to do this for the technology, for the new weapon, and again for the weapon within the manufacturing section. Maybe this is just a first playthrough issue, but going by achievements only about 5% of players even fully beat the game once, and I don't intend to play a second time.

I could go on a bit, but the point is the overall experience of using the software ends up just not being fun. Some of these may just sound like gripes, but they really do add up over time. The biggest problem though, I think, is that the game seems to still be in development, yet the developers are continuing to add new content and DLCs rather than fixing their messy UI. I really think the team needs to step back and fix the user experience, because there is a genuinely decent game under all that, I just can't enjoy the process of getting to it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished my '1st run, Veteran (Normal) difficulty. 90h of gameplay. I think this deserves a review.

My background: non hardcore gamer, aficcionado for tactical combat games (I grew up playing UFO: Enemy Unknown)

If you like tactical combat games (XCOM style, mainly), and can afford to buy this with all the DLC's, go for it. The DLC's ramp up the difficulty even more, but add some (much needed) extra layers to the game. Even in normal mode, it is a difficult game. I recommend splitting your progress into 4-5 save files, because the game eventually likes to checkmate you, and if something like this happens, you'll have an older save file just some hours behind, instead of having to start from scratch again. Use only one save file and you're pretty much doomed (if you 're masochist, or enjoy a hardcore experience try using only 1 save file). What is checkmate looks like in this game? Well, if you somehow get stuck with very low tech resources, and have few vehicles/spare ammunition, and happens to be at war with 1-2 factions and having to deal with pandoran invasions also, you'll have not enough tech to replenish you ammunitions after each fight, and even worse, will have no tech to activate more bases, recruiting and producing (much needed) additional vehicles. If you're playing with the Festering Skies DLC and you only have 1 aircraft and it is blown up when you don't have enough resources to produce another, it's game over.

Although the exploration sites add an enjoyable layer of roulette to the game, there are very few kinds of missions in the game, so it may drag forever from half to end. Basically you have scavenging missions (retrieve boxes with resources and evacuate), extermination missions (killing everyone on the map), rescue missions (rescuing something/someone then evacuating), and activating objectives missions (simply getting to a certain point and interacting with the object). The size limit of the map makes the rescue and objective activation missions not very much thrilling, because with enough speed your soldiers can quickly scan the whole map, locate themselves and trace a quick escape route. Almost never you'll need to split your party to search for anything (the exception would be scavenging missions, to finish the more quickly).

From mid game to late game the enemies start to have A LOT of HP, so extermination missions drag forever. This, combined to the amount of missions of the same kind you'll have to complete in the game makes the second half of it drags on forever. Also, as the game progresses the factions start wars between themselves, so you'll frequently have to intervene to save their havens, and this add a lot more extermination missions in the second half of the game.

I'm not a hardcore gamer, and as such I do not frequently come up with ways to cheese the games I play, but if you want to finish this game below the 100 hour mark I feel you'll have to cheese. There are a lot of class combinations and equipment/abilities interactions that are pure cheese. Also, in this game meelee damage is king. Your meelee fighters are your biggest DPS source (even with the most basic weapons they top most advanced ranged weapons), and they also save you A LOT of ammunition, so protect them well. I didn't like the Stealth mechanic (at least not for the player, bc the computer units always have high Perception values so they are able to detect your stealthed units easily)

The A.I in the only difficulty I've played (Veteran - Normal) felt pretty good and not buggy at all. The enemies knew how to position themselves to enhance their DPS and hinder my advance. Maybe in higher difficulties they behave even better.

The storyline is good, and the endgame is hard and sactisfatory. I cheesed hard in the last battle and I don't know how can someone can win it without cheesing

In my opinion this game looks like a more streamlined/simpler version of XCOM, which focuses more on combat itself than in managing resources/bases, so as such, I'll recommend it only for players that are already familiarized with XCOM style games.\t\t\t\t\t
