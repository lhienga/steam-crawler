\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have actually bought the game when it is in Alpha all these years ago, but had not enjoyed it a bit and requested for a refund. There after I have not followed the game much. But after the Year one edition comes out in steam and with a good price reduction, I decided to try the game again. At this price point this is a great game to have with all those DLCs. My views about the game in general are below,

Pros:

1. Very much XCOM like but with some CRPG vibe.

2. Great value for money at current price point and with 3 DLC. You may at least get 20-30 hours of play time even if you are sped through all the quests. If someone want to experience everything then it may take 50+ hours in single playthrough.

3. Realistic ballistics mechanic is very hyped and although some time very annoying it may well be fun when you grasped the basic.

4. Three factions and their different tech tree provide good variations and tactical intricacies which many player will love.

5. Good story, its not perfect but did not hinder any player in any way

6. The devs at Snapshot games are enthusiastic and always try to improve the game by means of patches. This is a very good sign.

CONS:

1. Even if stated otherwise this game is actually a survival strategy game with some XCOM like RPG mechanics. You have to find ways of survival in every minute. It takes away some enjoyment at the beginning.

2. There has been very little hand holding at the geoscape / strategy layer. You have to take diplomatic and tactical decisions on your own. It may pose difficulty to understand at first.

3. The game is not very much beginner friendly, you have to understand the core mechanics and need to read the game wiki at first.

4. There has been some weird difficulty spike even in lowest difficulty level. Unlike other games, here Early game is pretty much cakewalk, Mid game is very difficult and late game is some what less difficult than mid game. The devs are constantly patching the game and hopefully it will be balanced more in the future.


Overall verdict:

A go-to game for RPG and XCOM lovers if you enjoy hard survival strategy games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is taken from a point of view of someone who recently bought the game (April 2021) and from someone looking for something to scratch the XCom itch.

Like XCom, the game consists of both a strategic layer - or Geoscape - and the tactical layer. I will cover both and then mention some overall game play elements.

The strategic game is, in my opinion, superior to the recent XCom reboots in every way. It is not simply and random RNG excuse for supplies or a few story mission placements but there is actual game play value in the layer. Later in the game, it can take a bit of getting used to since so many things are going on, but I feel the game did a very good job on this aspect of the game.

The tactical layer, however, is where the game finds itself lacking a bit. First off, the maps are very limited and not randomly generated. As the game is much longer than an XCom play through, this can make things a bit tedious and feeling representative as you go through the missions. I do like that the aliens are spread over the map, active since the beginning of the mission and not sitting around in 'pods' waiting for you to discover and pick apart at your whim.

Overall though, the game lacks any kind of progression curve like you find in XCom. Most of the research is rather useless and makes no sense for many items (why do I need to research an alien to unlock shotguns in the year 2047?). Furthermore, there really is no progression tech wise. Most weapons are similar in the game with slight variation in their stats (one offers higher damage and range, while one may offer lower damage, shorter range and armor penetration but neither is necessarily better than the other). The lack of progression in weapons is rather peculiar considering all the aliens in the game have a progression as the game goes on (most aliens progressing through five levels). This does keep the game a challenge throughout, but I feel they could have had some kind of progression to at least keep up with the alien progression throughout the game.

I do like that the game has a bit more complexity than XCom but some of it is there for the sake of being there. For example, in 2021, there is really no need in a tactical game of soldiers to have to manage ammo inventory. It adds nothing to the game and is nothing more than a tedious step for the sake of being there. In a RPG fine, but not a game where you are managing 12-24 soldiers.

To conclude, the game does offer an alternative XCom experience, though with many enough difference you should not play like XCom. Is the game bad? No. Like all games, including XCom, it has it's places it does well and others that could use improvement and therefore, if what I mentioned above would be okay with you in a game, than I would recommend purchasing it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game. While the reflexive response of most people seems to be comparing it to XCOM (which isn't a bad thing per say) there are plenty of things setting it apart from being an out and out XCOM clone.

One of the things that stick out the most to me is the fact that you only have to make sacrifices/trade-offs regarding what missions you take and which you don't if you don't have enough soldiers/aircraft built up, rather than a static pick 1 of 3 like in XCOM. While this is balanced by the fact that travel times exist and you aren't guaranteed to be able to reach a mission in time (if your aircraft is on the other side of the world or something, for example) as well as the potential for a ton of active missions popping up at once, I really like the fact that my ability to respond at any given time is something dependent on the effort I've put in developing and such rather than a static value.

This also means that just because you have a capped out A or B team, developing more units and squads has actual value as even half a dozen or more teams can be genuinely useful rather than just more units you developed just because. It really feels like your developing into more and more of a powerhouse as the game goes on and your ranks swell to dozens of soldiers that actively see combat rather than just a rotation of the same 6-10 doing everything.

Something else I really like is that weapons don't have a real linear development tree, which makes building up a varied arsenal both viable and important. Instead of the blunt this is better than that of XCOM different versions of the same weapon class excel against different enemies and in different situations. Accuracy, Armoring shredding potential, firing AP cost, Damage per shot, type of damage (out of conventional, virus, paralyze), number of shots per burst, and even the cost of ammunition are all things to consider. This is something that extends to armor as well, seeing as different types give different bonuses with focuses on protection, accuracy bonuses, stealth bonuses, movement, etc.

Leveling up an individual soldier is also quite a developed and in depth process, with things like hybrid classes giving a tremendous amount of a possible variations regarding a units skill set. With assignable points both from level ups and as flat gains for every mission a unit takes part in, its possible to unlock every skill (for a unit's given main/secondary class) and max all 3 stats given enough time, and although that is a very long winded process, the difference between a recruit and a fully improved soldier is tremendous and (given how long it takes) something that feels well and truly earned when you finally work your way up to it.

While i could go into other things ranging from the shooting system to the almost dead space style of killing your enemies limb by limb, I've already ranted enough and i feel like plenty of other people have touched on those things.
