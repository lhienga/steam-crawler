\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great.

I like the 3 major factions. I can pick Chaotic Evil, Neutral Good, or Lawful Evil. Suggestion: the start of each new play-through should force you into war with a random faction that utterly hates your presence. This is how StarDrive 1 handled politics(RTS/4X Space game), by making a few factions become "Middle School" bullies and declare war if your army stays weak.

I love the VATS style aiming, I think it's bigger than I thought, initially. The only problem I have is I cannot peek the corner or tilt at all when I'm manually free-aiming. Would love more options to shot anything.

The Pandorans are such a cool threat, if only I received the UFO-Pedia reports after encountering them. I would of loved the mystery and surprise, Classic XCOM style, but I'm sure the devs want us to see all the new enemies as the difficulty curves up so quick that you probably miss out on the 1st few evolutions.

Speaking on difficulty. It seems to be a common topic. I believe what JG has done here was recreate his Classic Unmodded X-COM experience where you're always going to be "under-equipped and undermanned". The game does need more balance, but I am pleased with your 2020 vision.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game takes a simulationist approach to the classic tactics genre from the creator of the original X-Com series. As a fan of the classic X-Com Apocalypse I enjoyed Phoenix Point. However players should expect to save and reload a lot for tactical battles in the early game.

Lots of effort was put into the tactical component of the game, the trajectory of each bullet is simulated. Being able to aim for different limbs is satisfying. Lots of thought was put in by the developers into balancing equipment. Guns aren't strictly better than others, with different trade-offs between them.

However there are still quite bugs related to tactical combat, passive statuses aren't updated correctly in battle and modifications to stats granted by abilities aren't displayed properly in the personnel screen.

The UI could do with more polish, filtering and selection is unintuitive. As your teams grow and more items are available to manufacture scrolling through endless menus is tedious. There ought to be quick save and quick load buttons.

Melee and psychic abilities are not really well thought out. The beserker class is pretty useless because each melee attack with a melee weapon costs 2 AP and they do not get any bonuses to allow them to move faster or do more damage. I found the most effective melee build was an Assault/Infiltrator. Priests don't get any more willpower than plebians

The end game can be tedious. the game doesn't scale well strategically: it's annoying to have to defend many places around the world with a team of fresh recruits.

Synedrion are a faction of Mary Sue communists. The ancient civilization DLC quest line is long and tedious. The living items DLC was bugged and I could not complete the story line as the quest location did not appear.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really loved this game for 10 hours. But its shows that they dont want you to love it any more beyond that. The game is a SLOG when you get out of the early game. Tech trees suck and everything new that you get is rarely an upgrade, more of it does some stat better but another stat worse. There are very few tech tree items that are straight up upgrades. It feels horrible. The DLC adds some stuff that is pretty strong, but with the pacing of the game its feels so bad trying to do the DLC content while you run around the map like a chicken with its head cut off trying to babysit havens, nests, bases...

This game does some stuff that X-COM could take note of, the removal of an % based RNG system is nice, the only RNG is the spread of the bullets. The movement system where soldiers stop if they spot something is nice... but thats about it. What it does not do is it does not have good pacing, the player can barely make it through an in game day late game without having to consider fighting at least 7 times. When at that point in the game certain research projects take 3 days to complete. So if you are trying to save havens and your bases especially, you have to fight so so so so much just trying to wait out research.

The biggest flaws I have for this game are as follows
- The overworld squad management system is poorly done, and at least some kind of auto resolve needs to be created for haven defense
- The base defense is a disaster. Random generation that sometimes locks your vehicles in hallways that they cannot get out of. And enemies always spawn in a room that end in a choke point. This joke of a mission is a waste of time and flamethrower fuel.
- The tech tree is pathetic. You never feel stronger with your weapons, the skills your people get make you feel marginally stronger, but the weapons barely get stronger.
- Overall damage values. This game does not promote a lot of the weaponry that it has in place, this game forces one turn (or close to) wipes of enemies, or you get rammed in by the aliens. Alien damage numbers scale harder than yours do by a long shot
- The final mission. What a cancer mission.

The good:
+ The story and general narrative is nice
+ Vehicles are generally a good addition
+ Good variety of classes with some moderately interesting skills, only moderately though
+ Looks good

Overall: 6/10, only because I really enjoyed the first 10 to 15 hours\t\t\t\t\t
