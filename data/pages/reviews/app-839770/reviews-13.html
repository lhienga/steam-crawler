\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried really hard to like this game, and honestly there is a lot to like.

My issue was that, unlike XCOM and similar titles, there was no significant feeling of progression. The late-game weapons and armour felt like the barest upgrade over the starting kit. There was never a feeling of progressing through a story, just an endless slog of disjointed missions until getting enough research to complete one of the 5 endings (spoiler alert: it's the same mission for all 5, all that changes is which colour doodad you insert into the final objective).

Which brings me to the missions and maps: there was just very little diversity. The maps were all pretty much the same, with the same layouts repeated over and over. Even special operations rarely had unique maps, which was disappointing.

Overall, it wasn't a *bad* game in spite of these issues, it has a lot to like. The class-blending was a fun touch that helped each operative feel unique. Having the ability to field multiple teams and aircraft definitely added an extra layer of strategy, as positioning became very important. But the reason I can't recommend the game is because everything started to feel like a chore about 1/3 of the way through. It might be worth checking out if you really like this kind of game, but I can't bring myself to give it a wholehearted recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game now that it's finished and with all the DLC.


One thing to note that all other reviews have neglected for XCOM and old X-COM returners: Recruitment Mechanics.

Human solider recruitment resembles the X-COM Apocalypse "HR department" system. You'll get notified every so often to look over the new applicants for hire or scan the world map to go direct headhunting premium from your rival factions.

The Corrupted Horizons DLC changes recruitment up by making it more "just in time manufacturing" with the addition of instant grown hybrid replicant soldiers at the cost of a resource sink called mutagens. They are not as individually strong as human soldiers but they make better teams.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think the dwindling playerbase, mediocre dlc reviews at best and lack of mods that may have helped to explain this mess shows something with this game, that so few people actually have stayed around and come back to this game and its for a reason. This game is dead or dying and its entirely because of how it was handled. I first heard of this game from its PR nightmare where people backed it under a promise it was going to be on steam only for the creators to instead go to the Epic game store apparently approaching them not Epic approaching them which is pretty bad and actively betraying their backers with it being likely this game wouldn't have existed without the original promise that people gave money to in the first place. There is no beating around the bush, this was unprecedented and was bad all around, not even mentioning AMA's with questions not being answered and other terrible PR.

I can say I didn't buy the game until much after the games release date when it was on sale (I mean I don't remember but I hope I didn't buy this full price) and when you start its new and its interesting but you quickly hit a wall because of this games mechanics and how little you know while being introduced to the game and how early you get introduced to absurd mission and enemies. I mention the Epic games store because I feel its relevant to the lack of playerbase and mods, had this game not lost so many players and got more support as a smaller game with mods then things would be better but they took that money as if saying they were gonna do it themselves and that was gonna go back into the game, but now its still left unpolished and unplayed by many for good reason. A new player looks up what they are doing wrong in this game because of its strict and boring way to play only to be met with answers like "don't research because the enemy will grow much faster if you advance like the pace of the game suggests" This game is not only strict but new players are met with mixed answers on how to play and at times bad direction on how to actually play by the game itself. Players are told to use the wiki and look up guides, I am sorry but thats a sign something is wrong with how this game is handled.

I would like to just leave a comment someone had on their AMA here "Julian and David, are you worried about running out of feet to shoot yourselves in?" I just think that's funny.

I re-download it and remember after 5 seconds in and one level done why I stopped playing this game, it wasn't just the terrible mid game but the unremarkable character skills (and honestly a lack of skills in the first place I can't believe they didn't add more with updates or dlc). The game itself has really is really bad with a replay experience, the same tutorial missions and if you have the dlc worrying about when you are going to have to deal with the Pure which are armored up and have a shield for the end of their turn which ... like wow what interesting enemy design its like there is a pattern in bad design choices in this game. This game gets boring fast, for what good it has its repetitive and surrounded by not fun stiff and boring gameplay. It's weird I have to ask if this game benefited from its soldier class system, the soldiers feel expendable given the bad trait system and poor customization and I see no clear reason why they went with the system they used other than it was Xcom like. This shallow result is why I think this game isn't doing well, its mechanics are often as deep as a puddle and don't fit with what the game probably should have been. Maybe soldiers could have been more simple and more focused on strategy or gained more individuality, ability and diversity in weapons as the game went on.

I will say that I feel this game is lacking in areas like gameplay options, clear class differentiation (as well as balancing which plays into that), the factions aren't interesting and rewards can be often underwhelming as well as overall upgrades. If someone complains about the game they might be met with "you just don't use strategy" even when strategy in this game ends up playing out repetitively in each respective mission type but unlike Xcom I am not ambushing anyone, I am not exploding roofs to drop enemies down I am just getting barely anything from research to add to how I play. This game is not an upgrade to Xcom it is a step back in so many ways especially tactics wise. Xcom has mods to help but it has a strong core to start from but Phoenix point is lacking a strong core (which isn't to say there isn't close), its missing features and polish that could have made this shine even years after its release as if the game we have now is close to the incomplete version we should have started with and upgraded from. For example the character customization being lackluster, the factions being interesting but underdeveloped and interaction feeling limited, there's a bunch of this game that feels like an afterthought and just never got changed or balanced or added to. The game had the opportunity to be better but it sort of feels like its getting to how it should have started at the end of its life, with how poorly some of those dlc were received maybe they should have worked on the base game more.

I would talk about mutations or cybernetics but who cares? Like the Mutant priest organization is the most unlikable factions in its entirety, almost every quest is like "oh they killed thirty people" they are the the post virus apocalypse anti vaxer society, so instead of getting the poorly implemented priest class or poorly implemented melee class I get the sort of useful but also poorly implemented mechanic class and expensive hard to supply and use Infiltrator class from the other factions that don't seem brain dead. Sort of shame what should be the cool psionics of the game just don't even feel worth it, enjoy the other two factions going to war for no reason as well though. Honestly I almost wish they would all just destroy each other at that point, feels like babysitting children.

However I should add parts of the game like the free aim is clunky and is often sigh worthy when the game tells you an enemy could be hit only for an inch of their arm to be seen a mile away. The free aim often feels a bit off or stiff but the most fun I have had in this game especially early on is using the limb system which works well with that type of aim. But the farther in you get the more you are presented with less fun enemies to use that on, it starts to feel like hitting way too much armor and way too little payoff and large health bars on later enemies often tied to absurd abilities. Have fun with your tiny skill tree and characters whose abilities seem half useless unless you save scummed twenty times, good thing the enemy has 1000 forms which aren't really that different to be honest, not like that time could have spent on the players soldiers mechanics. This plays back into balance, its just not good that's all that really needs to be said the game goes from fun when you start out into a slog and it doesn't even tie into the story well.

So years after the virus basically fractured humanity they suddenly advance and learn to use guns and be tougher right when like 5 people try to revive the phoenix project (who handles all the stuff at the base and computers or pilots their ships? Who knows because this is a half baked story where your organization to fight back for humanity might just be 20 people with guns). Characters that look stupid often with random bright hair colors and poor textures on the hair styles I never felt attached to any of my characters besides one in the very first game I played to which I have not gotten a sniper with as good skills and a cool name since, real shame it just makes me want to play Xcom.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn X-Com style game, from the creator of the original X-Com. Basically a tactics game with a strategic world map layer that generates missions based on the simulation and your actions. You control Phoenix Point and must defend the earth from an invasion of weird creatures coming from the sea, so very X-Com: Terror from the Deep for those that have played that game. I won't say much about them as finding out is a lot of the fun of the game. What sets this game apart, is that there are three factions in addition to you own, that are fighting the creatures and potentially each other as well (maybe even you but I made friends so not sure). Each has it's own goals, techs and ideals and you can choose to try and ally with them them, steal from them or leave them to their own devices. In addition, the creatures you face will evolve over time getting more armor, shields, weapons, abilities and new body types. There are really only a handful on basic types, but the evolutions make for a good amount of variety.

This is a well made X-Com style game with some nice innovations. It is more like the older X-Com games, in that the world feels more like a true simulation instead of a series of forced crisis you have to deal with. Much more to my liking. The interactions with the factions was fun and made the game feel different from other X-Com games. On the tactics layer, the aiming system is nice with the ability to aim where you want (optional) and a two circles denoting where you could hit. More accuracy makes for small circles. This gives you the option of shooting off an arm holding a dangerous weapon, or hitting the head of a unit that can mind-control and freeing it's slaves. Another nice thing is the movement system where you have completely free movement. You still have action points, but you can divide your movement up how you like. Move 1 square 5 times or 5 at once, doesn't matter for instance. Also the tactics has no weird gimmicks like free movement when they first see you which was refreshing. The squad system is nice too with many classes (maybe around 8) and with each soldier being able to get a second class. Each of these has it's own skills, armor and weapons it can use so there are many options to try out.

On the downside, you cannot build bases, only take over existing ones with resources, and perhaps a battle. An odd decision. Also there are no air battles, except I think through DLC which I didn't try out. As the enemies are already on the planet, it does make sense though so not that big a deal. There are some annoying bugs with the camera too, with you rarely being told you can hit an enemy and then going into free mode and just seeing a wall and no sign of the target. Also sometime the first shot will be different than the second shot with the target moving a little, sometimes making the shot almost impossible. Maybe that is intended, but if you exit the shot and start over, the original shot will be there again. The faction system felt a bit off, in that they are just too hostile to each other. This is a threat to the whole world, they can't stop going to war with each other to fight some weird abominations that are taking over the world? Just seems a bit forced... but people and nations can be dumb in real life too, hopefully not that dumb though. The mutation system, which is cool, could have been better. Seems like they just get new options at certain intervals instead of them evolving to what the player is doing. If it was doing this, I didn't notice it. Maybe a lot to ask but that was the original idea pitched by them. The game can also be grindy and long if you try and get all the techs and do all the missions as well, which is how I like to play. This game will take much longer than the new X-Com games if you play like I do (double at least). After a while the missions will start feeling kind of samey and get annoying because of this.

An excellent X-Com style game, more in the vain of the older X-Com games then the newer ones. It has some minor bugs, isn't as balanced, is more grindy and doesn't have as good of presentation and story as the newer X-Coms but makes up for it in other ways ending up being a nice alternative for the right kind of player. Definitely worth a go if you want a more simulation and epic feeling X-Com experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs of 2023 I recommend the year one edition (no additional DLC bought). Note that this is still a vastly inferior game to XCom 2 War of the Chosen. I would recommend playing XCom instead.

The tactical combat game loop is good. I especially love the first few turns, where I choose my deployment strategy. You get to see the whole map without any fog of war, where the enemies might mostly come from, and the location of the objectives. The probabilistic aspect of "I need to cover all the bases but also should probably invest in covering ground more in this direction" is great, and unique to this game.

The game is however far from perfect, as many other reviewers have mentioned:

- There still are a bunch of small, superficial bugs such as the camera movement getting stuck, or animations of being hurt played too late. When it's the enemies' turn it sometimes spends a few second stuck on one enemy's turn.
- The game starts very easy and suddenly becomes way more difficult. That was not an issue for me because that's also the time we get new, way more powerful weapons/classes. I only lost two soldiers during my first play-through and never restarted a mission from an old save.
- Some classes and skills are definitely over powered, for example I strongly suspect that the reason I didn't struggle too much was me over-using the spider gun skill which allows you to deploy 3 explosive spider drones with 2 Action Points.
- I wish there were more map templates. Each environment/city has one map template (I think there are around 15 templates) and playing the same template more than twice gets annoying.
- The game does get a bit grindy towards the end game, and I did lose interest then.
- The writing is mediocre and fairly bland, I found myself skipping all of the story exposition that you get when researching various topics and not caring about any of it.\t\t\t\t\t
