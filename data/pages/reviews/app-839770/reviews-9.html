Hmm, is Phoenix Point a "bad" game?
No, and if I could I would've given a neutral rating.
*Might be up to edits, who knows, maybe I'll go back to the game eventually*


tl;dr: the game just feels like it did not have the budget to compete with XCOm, but tries to do so at every corner.
However, if you feel disenfranchised with Firaxis XCOM Phoenix Point offers ablend of Nu-XCOm paired with Xenonauts in terms of presentation, game flow and difficulty


However, druing my 8 hours into the game I constantly wished I was playing XCOM 2 instead, be it QoL features (i.e., not actually having to build ammo fr your weapons, I get the appeal, but it is misplaced imo in these kind of games, at least on the not ultra + difficulties), GUI or character/flair.

PP is just too similar to Nu-XCOM (and 2) to not warrant this comparison and quite frankly PP loses in this fight.

Sure, PP has some neat ideas and "unique" features, e,g, shooting of/targeting specific body parts, but during the early game, it did not feel useful at all, mostly, because you aren't told your hit-chance whatsover (or if you are, it is done so in an abyssmaly obvious fashion) and disabling the venom gland of an enemy does not trump outright killing sad enemy if you autoaim them.

Vehicles are another point of,,,,meeh-ism.
In principle the idea is cool, get a combat buggy into missions to waltz through the terrain....and whilst your vehicles certainly stand up to enemy fire and pack a punch themself when firing, their movement feels like a hot wheel car driven on rails, as a lot of seemingly destructable obstacles are not desctrutable by your base vehicles (or ever by vehicle?) whilst other obstacles get plowed through in a not satisfactory manner.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI find this game better than Xcom - but I have specific reasoning as to why:
1. The game is faster than xcom , watching granades getting lobbed in xcom makes my blood boil , xcom animations are infuriating.

Phoenix point is much more agile assuming you understand the basic concepts of the game.

2. Once you understand the game systems , which it is heavily based on(a rather difficult game)..
It's very straight forward to get the outcomes you are looking for.
The RNG factor is much less and relies more on positioning and placement of your aimed attacks.

3. The difficulty of X Com feels artificial , Once you trigger an enemy pod it really can mess up the whole situation especially when it just roams into active battle.

In Phoenix point the randomness is largely eliminated , stealth is preserved on unseen pawns , map is marked with clear reinforcement points.

The worst issue with the game is that it does not feel like you're progressing.

First your resources are very thin , you won't be able to buy new weapons for your new soldiers oftentimes, they will be running around with scrap.

All in All:
I think the Negative reviews really misunderstand the game, this isn't Xcom.

This games is as if Xcom were a survival game. Where your're really trying to make it to the next day by thin margins.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the combat mechanics. There's just nothing like it, and it's quite unique.
But that's it. That's the only thing I think is better here compared to xcom for example.
1 year late to steam and 4 years late to get workshop running killed the modding scene. Most platform disscusion is quite dead. Right this moment the latest reddit post is 13 days ago and the latest chat in modding section on discord is more than a month ago.
Mods itself divided into 2 platform, one with official modding support and the other on nexus with community made tool. Both is incompatible with eachother, and neither is active anymore. There's only one major mod and it's Terror from the void, which pretty much carry the entire modding scene right now.
DLC is disappointing. 2 of the 3 paid dlc give minimal content to the base game, and mostly just solve problems they created themselves.
Bottom line, I love the game, but I just can't recommend it to anyone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a solid start but it feels wildly un-finished.

There's a lot of balancing that needs to happen. Even on the lowest difficulty setting it is very likely that you're going to get blindsided by an enemy you didn't see who takes out one or more of your soldiers. Total squad wipes are common and enemies have a number of moves that can just down a soldier with no problem and no way to recover them. Enemies in general seem to have more movement and more actions than you which is extra galling when your soldiers have maybe five to eight tiles where they can move and perform an action.

Overwatch is of very limited utility because of the tendency of your soldiers to shoot window sills, door frames, and anything else that's between them and the target once overwatch gets tripped. Mindfraggers are extremely difficult to deal with because they're so low to the ground that any bush or piece of junk between the soldier and the creature means you're going to be shooting that instead. This problem isn't so bad with Free Aim but with overwatch you don't have that ability.

There's also a chronic lack of explanation for things. What is Corruption? What does it do? How do I get rid of it? Why does everyone seem to be picking it up? Why is there Mist spreading on the geoscape map? Can I stop it?

The item management system is also fairly clunky. The idea of having ammo that takes up space in people's inventory is interesting but there's too much fiddling with it. I have to juggle ammo for a primary and a secondary *and* items *and* any loot and ammo takes resources to make and I have to pay attention to the type. It feels like busywork and while I appreciate the button to auto rearm everyone at the end of a mission, there's still a lot of inventory management that I'm just not here for in this kind of game.

Character options are also pretty limited. Even with expansions, you can't change your soldiers that much. The upgrades are interesting but not super meaningful and there's not a lot of them. Soldiers also never really reach that point where you've had them long enough that they develop into their own being the way they did in X-COM. Once a soldier levels up enough, it generally can survive quite a bit if you aren't completely silly but in Phoniex Point even your long haulers can get dropped by an enemy that appeared out of nowhere with no ceremony.

Mods might fix some of these issues but I wouldn't know because the version of the game that can play mods just crashes instantly on starting so I couldn't tell you.

Overall, if you can get it on *deep* discount and are willing to cheat like crazy, it's a fun time but prepare for a lot of those "are you *serious!?*" moments.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe following is one person's opinion. Yours may differ. And brace yourself cos this ain't going to be no thirty words. Also minor-ish SPOILER WARNINGS.
First the big stuff.

DO NOT BUY THE DLC. It is designed to turn the game into a frustrating slog. And it totally unbalances the game. There are some cool things in there, but they are NOT worth the price of admission. Cool air combat is just not worth dealing with corrupted haven missions. DO NOT BUY.

DO NOT DO SCAVENGING MISSIONS. Scavenge supplies/Rescue soldiers/Retrieve vehicle. They are just a frustrating slog which only get more and more frustrating as the game progresses. Every time you discover a scavenging mission you will feel an overwhelming sense of despair. Don't do it. Seriously, leave them to rot.

At some point in your play-through you will discover a new enemy type which will make you want to quit the game. It can one hit kill your soldiers (unless you invest in strength, more on that later). It spews mist all over the battlefield and it spawns enemies. And it is a tank having a crap-ton of armour and and even bigger crap-ton of health requiring multiple-multiple-multiple turns to kill (or capture). The solution: virus weapons and biochemist perk. Shoot the bugger full of virus and it will spend it's turn panicking or recovering will points and it can't attack you. You're welcome.

Right onto more general advice.

Phoenix Point is the king of "side-grades". What does that mean? Well that new gun you spent all that time researching won't do any more damage, but it will Shred more armour. Or it will have less range but do more damage, you get the idea. Long story short you will have to spend a lot of time in manufacturing comparing stats of all the different types armour and weapons to figure out which ones are "good". Once you figure out what you like it's fine, but it will be a bit of a pain on your first play-through.

This game suffers from some really overpowered builds (LOOKING AT YOU TERMINATORS). My advice: Don't go for the most OP stuff (cough assaults). Instead go crazy figuring out different cross-classing combinations and how all the random perks can work with them. Try using melee weapons and mind control and stealth, just for the hell of it.

Armour-piercing weapons bypass armour leaving the armour completely undamaged. Armour shredding weapons do exactly what it says on the tin i.e. remove/shred armour (and are much more useful IMO).

You cannot afford to lose soldiers. Seriously. It takes sooooo long to level up soldiers, even on normal difficulty. They die you lose all that progress. Invest in strength ASAP (1 point of strength = 10 hp). Trust me you NEED that extra hp.

The only way to improve accuracy is with armour (and one sniper perk). This one kind of threw me coming from XCOM where your rookies slowly learn how to shoot straight. So yeah give every soldier a sniper headpiece (Accuracy is more valuable then armour IMO).

All in all if you like XCOM or Xenonauts you will probably like this game. It doesn't have anywhere near the level of polish or balance but you can still get a lot out of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr review: Snapshot Games Inc. created the perfect engine to make an X-Com game and used it to make an imperfect, slightly buggy, somewhat unbalanced X-Com wannabe. In spite of that, it's actually pretty good. Recommended if you like X-com!

-----------non tl;dr stuff--------------

I'll say this about Phoenix Point: if Snapshot Games Inc. polished the game up a little bit by getting rid of some of the common bugs, rebalanced the game a little bit, and handled story/DLC missions a little better where they didn't feel like they all happen at once, this would be 100% be as good as 2K's X-com games.

The biggest problem Phoenix Point has is how peculiar it is. Its definitely not set up like X-com which has a distinct projectile-laser-plasma weapon tiers and monthly income. Phoenix Point is the first game where I've ever heard the term 'sidegrade' in reference to how researched weapons have a tendency to not look or be any better than your starter gear. There also isn't an income system per-se where its easy to screw yourself over having overspent resources. Story progression is also unusual where instead of having it progress point-by-point as you complete specific tasks, Phoenix Point seemingly vomits up requirements and story missions on the global map all at once. Some tasks and missions, once completed, will add and change things to the game where you can and will absolutely mess up your game if they're done too early and you will start facing enemies you simply just aren't ready for yet.

Speaking personally, I restarted the game 3 times before I got the hang of Phoenix Point's do's and don'ts. If you plan on buying the game or had trouble with it, here's a series of tips on what to do early game so it doesn't give you such a rough time (some of these are specific to DLCs):

- you want to research New Jericho weapons and replace all your basic rifles with Bulldogs ASAP. The 4 bullet burst looks like a downgrade, but the 2 shred the rifles have offer a distinct advantage over the Ares and Deimos. If you don't do this, you will quickly run into enemies with armor so high, you'll have to shoot them a billion times to even start to damage them.

- steal airships from colonies. you're encouraged to do this. your relations will take a big hit, but there are so many opportunities to make your reputation go up it winds up being not an issue.

- research trade ASAP and have 1 aircraft whose only use is to travel between colonies buying out all their salable resource. I advise stealing a Helios for this ASAP. the trade exchanges are always in your favor. example: 2 science for 10 food and 8 food for 2 science. The 2 gain in food isn't much, but it adds up when trading a lot. the only resource you'll be able to produce is food, and that facility won't be unlocked until mid to late game.

- when 'the gift' mission appears, wait until there's a day left on the count down to do it. The moment this mission is completed the behemoth flying enemy will be added to the game. this can completely ruin your game if done too early.

- after getting 'the gift' manticore, research Synedrian Sky Defense Ops and manufacture the Prometheus ASAP. this is the *BEST* anti-air weapon early in the game. you can usually hit an enemy flyer with it and wait for it to burn to death. don't be afraid to disengage if your ship is in danger of being destroyed.

- I would not recommend doing the 'an unexpected emergency' story mission. ever. it will introduce the annoying corruption mechanic and the beyond irritating bullet spongey acheron enemies will begin turning up in missions.

- shielded pandorians have a tiny right arm connected to their shields that, if destroyed, will make their shield disappear.

Once I got these things figured out, Phoenix Point got less frustrating for me. Not catching onto the armor/shred mechanic seems to be the biggest reason people nope out of this game.

So, yeah, I think this game is actually pretty good. I do recommend. I think if it got a little TLC, it could be better than 2K's X-com games. Unfortunately it appears the developers have moved on so the game is as good/bad as its going to get.\t\t\t\t\t
