\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStrongly recommended if you like X-COM like games.

The Hypos update added much needed stability, i have not seen any game breaking bugs, except for rare instances when i load a save file.

Combat is amazing, and i argue superior to the XCOM games, however it does tend to get repetitive late game. The special missions intended to advance the plot are very generic, no voice overs and very simplistic objectives. Compared to the games tutorial which is very well made, the rest of the important missions are very dry.

Soldier customization is very good and engaging, multi-classing is great and allows truly broken combos.
I do however find the Cost of multi-classing to be too high in terms of Skill Points.
In terms of looks, i wish the Helmets available for soldiers allowed their faces to be shown, is kinda of a waste of time customizing a Soldiers face and looks only to be hidden all the time by a full face helmets.

I Wish the game delved deeper into the Lovecraftian elements in the story. Also the final boss is very disapointing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played the big update yet, but I hope it fixes the totally broken economy which did not make any coherent sense.

I wrote a long review and lost it, but hopefully it is relevant anymore.

What I will say is that the core gameplay is the best turn based tactics you're going to find anywhere. It is way better than Xcom 2. It is unfortunate that they got mission creep developing this 4X style economy, which last time I played it was so broken I couldn't even imagine a way of fixing it.

Basically, you're supposed to be heroes saving people, but the only way to get resources is to save bases from being attacked, so you end up benefiting from aliens attacking, which means you don't want to clear out the aliens. This turns into some kind of Alex Jones CIA ISIS type thing where in terms of role-playing, you're working with the aliens to extort the havens. (Like when AJ says that the CIA funds ISIS to come in and rescue civilians and play hero.)

I guess if you start role playing as a kind of clandestine terrorist group exploiting an alien invasion for profit then it becomes kind of funny. But seriously, that's not what the game was supposed to be.

I get that the other part was that you were supposed to go to war with one or two of the factions, and then rob them for resources, but the impulse, and the way most people seem to be playing, is that they try to keep a balance between the factions while extorting all of them using false flag terrorism. Maybe making the wars less easy to avoid would fix this. I don't know.

Anyway, I'm excited to play the new update and maybe it all makes sense now.

But the point is: the combat is fantastic. Honestly, I would consider it a big update if they just cut down resource scarcity and just focused on tactics, plus some map control.

I'm hopeful and will update when I play the new release.

I still recommend. Best tactics.\t\t\t\t\t
