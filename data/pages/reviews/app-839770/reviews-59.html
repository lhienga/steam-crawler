\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm loving Phoenix Point's stripped down XCOM trappings. It's a game that understands exactly what it is that makes the genre good, and goes straight for that with a minimal amount of extra flash or unnecessary fat. That leaves less room for the graphical kinks that come when modern XCOM really gets rolling, which is hugely welcome.

There are a lot of little things I really love, but the biggest and best of the bunch is the easy-to-read UI. My vision is crap so tiny UI font choices in games like this tend to hinder my enjoyment, but Phoenix Point not only has a clean, uncluttered UI, but smart font and color choices that help everything pop. It really helps that the game works well with the built-in Windows Magnifier. Having item descriptions pop next to the actual item would be handy but that's such a minor nitpick as to be negligible.

It could use some more variety in class and mission types, but I think that about most tactical turn-based squad games. The story is pretty generic but there's a good backbone here for sequels, particularly given the splits within the factions themselves and the well-designed characters involved in each.

This is a remarkable self-assured strategy game with a huge heritage, and it doesn't disappoint. Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy two biggest frustrations with xcom1/2 was 1) that classic 99% hit chance shot missing, and 2) running into a group of aliens at a bad moment.

1) Phoenix point fixes this. Basically each individual bullet is now considered one at a time. In xcom I would hardly ever consider taking a shot at <55% accuracy. For example, in xcom if an enemy is at very low health, but you have low accuracy, your turn is better spent repositioning or doing something else because most likely you will miss your shot. But with Phoenix Point, I would take that shot, because even though I have low accuracy, I just need one or two of my 9 bullets to hit the low HP target to kill it. It makes combat feel much more consistent than xcom.

2) Your characters have 4 action points instead of 2. 2 action points of movement is comparable to one action point of movement in xcom in terms of distance, and shooting (for most weapons) takes 2 action points. What this means is that I can run out a short distance, shoot, then get back to cover. Running a small distance and shooting will leave you one additional action point to do something, which is nice since xcom would consider a movement of a few tiles and a full action points worth equivalent to each other. Even heavier weapons that cost three action points to move will allow a soldier some wiggle room to line up that shot.

Movement does not fully consume an action point. That means I can move twice with a single action point. Ex: I have my sniper move one tile consuming ~.2 AP, take a shot consuming three AP, then move back into cover using another ~.2 AP. This used 3.4 of her 4 AP, which would not have been possible if movement consumed an entire action point (it would have cost 5 AP.) However actions such as melee, shooting, ect, all are measured in FULL action points, only movement can take advantage of fractions of an AP.

Troops will stop moving if they spot a new enemy. So what does this mean? It means that if I tell a soldier to run into a building, but there's an alien in there, he will stop and let me decide what to do. In xcom the solider would just keep charging into the building until his order was finished and he was standing next to an alien. This small change compliments the other changes in the previous two paragraphs very well.

TL;DR: combat feels much more consistent and flexible.\t\t\t\t\t

Bought because on sale because i'm a hardcore XCOM 1 fan and I am gutted by what the franchise has become, stuck with its lore that limits what it can be. I hated the way every new iteration of XCOM was just a series of puzzle maps where you had to carefully remember that heavy A will take this square while sniper B will take that one, rinse wash repeat until you found the 'solution'. I miss the old days of random carnage and overall ticking clock, naming and decorating my soldiers with great care (until they get all wiped out in a single explosion, of course :))

New high level strategic elements that incorporates a complex faction system, and a 'damn if you do, damn if you don't' branching storyline options. This elevates the gameplay to brand new heights, while engaging you deep into a complex (seriously, it's not easy) lore that will captivate you.

I think XCOM per say is dead, the same way sim city, civ and other pillar of gaming are dead. These titles don't need sequels, they need a prequel, reboots. AND after 8 hour i feel this appear to be it. This is the reboot we wanted. However, two warnings are in order

a) Being true to the xcom roots, it's likely the game has 'hardlocks'. If you played thousands of hours of xcom you know what i mean by that. This is countered by the infinite save system, which is a welcome relief as i can't see myself replaying the first 8 hours over and over again like XCOM ironman mode made me do.

b) it's fairly evident from the get go that the game has one flaw in common with XCOM: rather than favoritizing explosives and demolishing building (it's possible, but it's not as faithful to the genre as the very first edition that allowed you to not take any chance and just flatten a house with a rocket launcher), it's instead pushing you to use your troops very carefully to prevent being overrun by powerful (and i mean POWERFUL) enemies.

At 10 quid with 2 dlcs and a year worth of patches, this is a steal and i'm sure you can get 100 or 200 hours worth. In an industry where 70 pounds title that will last you 5 hours are the norm, I'm not sure I even have a reason to really complain.

UPDATE: The game however, didn't 'do it' for me. I felt quickly the need for something more fresh 13 short hours in, and I might revisit it in the future. But in a world where new options are around the corner and on sale, it's hard to stick to something that requires so much engagement with so little dopamine release. Still a thumbs up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has so much potential. I backed it on Fig. I have the game (with all its expansion DLCs) on Epic and on Steam.

But between the game play feeling a little "empty" (like something is missing but I cant quite put my finger on what) and the bugs - its a thumbs down for me unfortunately. First thumbs down I've ever given - and i own nearly 1200 games on Steam alone...

Played it when it first came out but stopped playing it because it lacked soul. Then got on Steam again and all its expansions. Figured new content, patches and game play improved - lets try it again. So tried to play it again. But after random hours of playing the game just stops working. After I complete a mission (no specific mission in particular), the game refuses to load back to the geoscope and becomes unplayable. I scrubbed the game. Started fresh. Three times. Same thing each time at different place in the story.

after all that I finally got the game to progress further and discovered things just happen. There is no real cohesion, or motivation, or even just clear direction. Stuff just happens. Narrative cut scenes play, and more stuff to do just piles onto your to do list. Just soooooo much. It just piles it on. All the time.

More and more bases to unlock and build and repair, more and more and more things to research (most of which do not really matter). More repairs to be done. A never-ending queue of monotonous gear and items to be built. Mission after mission to do to collect more resources to continue to research more pointless things and build more stuff and recruit more soldiers. Soldiers whose ability and skill progression are pretty much all exactly the same. A heavy is a heavy is a heavy is a heavy. A sniper is a sniper is a sniper. Etc. So then you build more gear in hopes to try to make them different, which just take and more and more of go here, now go there, now there, now here, back to there

And things just happen. All the time. Most of the time there you have no idea what is going on or what to do. There is a big pink thing flying across the screen. What do I do with that? What is it. Can interact with it? How do I interact with it? Oh, look its destroying havens so what? Still have no idea how to stop it. Cant even target it.

My soldiers have corruption. What now? Is that bad? Can I get rid of it? Is there anything to be done about it? Does anyone care? Is there a scientist somewhere trying find a cure? Can we help???? Does it even matter? What happens when they are completed corrupted???

I have list of 500 things I need to research. Which one do I prioritise? Will researching the random worm thing help me oh look it didn't. Well, that was a waste of time and effort.

Stuff just happens. Factions and peoples spewing things on screen to just add to the clutter that try to create a story by forcing you to make choices which ultimately just give +4 to one faction rating and -4 to another

Thats all you do. No soul. just do stuff. There are no characters to connect with or care about. No personalities you love to hate, or to make your feel like the world matters or even what you do matters.

So, while tactical mission are initially fun, the constant grind just basically turns the game into a glorified farming sim. Its one off those that tries to do everything - and achieves that. But at the cost everything simply being more things for you do with no sense of achievement or meaning for doing them...
