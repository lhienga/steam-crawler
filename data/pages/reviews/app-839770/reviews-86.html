Another frustration is how long combat takes.

I really enjoy this game, but I stop every play through because Chirons are just too exhausting to play against.

On the flip side, I will say that every other enemy is enjoyable to play against and requires different tactics.



If Chirons ever get addressed and reworked, I'll change this review to a positive, despite the bugs and glitches you'll see everyone else talking about.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, you've seen the screenshots and you've probably watched some gameplay on Youtube so you know this is game is <cough> very heavily inspired by XCOM2. So, if you already like XCOM2 and you're looking for a new fix to keep you hooked until XCOM3 comes along whenever that might be, is this game for you? Let's have a quick look at what's different.

Character customisation is not as detailed as XCOM2 but it is more than good enough for me. You're not going to be running cute girls around the battlefield in hot pink shorts and a t-shirt, all the characters are military-style, like they were in XCOM1. But you can name them and set up the colour and pattern schemes for their kit.

You don't have Bradford shouting at you telling you not to let the Berseker Queen get away when you're letting out a huge sigh of relief that the beast is exiting the field. And, after 13 hours in, I have yet to have my in-game performance assessed by some shadowy, bald geezer with a gravelly voice. Don't miss that at all. I know how well I'm performing. (Badly!)

The tactical maps are gorgeous and you really feel like you're playing XCOM2. However, IMO, tactical combat appears to be deeper than XCOM2's combat. You have four action points instead of two and your characters also have willpower which you 'spend' to activate their special abilities. I really like this. I'd have prefered to have XCOM: Chimera Squad's impulse turns but this is a slight improvement over XCOM2.

This is also a difficult game. You might feel like you're walking it your first couple of hours in the game but the difficulty ramps up quite savagely a few hours in and you'll need to form whole new strategies for playing the strategic map. You will find it hard, if not impossible to keep all the factions happy so you'll have to pick one aover the others and go with that so that gives the game more replayability as you'll want to see what all the factions have to offer.

There is so much more to talk about but if you're one of those folks who gets bent out of shape when someone reimagines an existing title, like 'Iratus: Lord of the Dead' did with 'Darkest Dungeon', or 'Knock on the coffin lid' did with 'Slay the Spire', this will cause you a world of pain. However, if you're like me and am happy to have something fresh and interesting to play, you'll probably be fine with this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoes this game have better gameplay than XCOM 2? Yes.
Does it still have BS probability? Yes but it's no longer an easily visible number so people notice less.
Is it more fun than XCOM 2? No. The gameplay is excellent but the enemies are dull, the maps repetitive, the faction bases and people are randomly generated and thus lack any personality, everything takes too long to do and makes the game feel a drag, the player has no actual presence in the world (unlike XCOM where you play as The Commander, the player in Phoenix Point does not actually exist, despite somehow being in charge). All in all, if you want a game with a high quality of gameplay, play this. If you want a fun game, play XCOM 2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a big fan of turn based strategy games, especially sci fi ones that are similar to fir axis' XCOM. During early quarantine (2020) I played through both XCOM2 and WotC and had an absolute blast! And I was so excited to finally play this game. What Phoenix Point does differently from XCOM2 is it is frustratingly obtuse and feels like it was designed to intentionally upset me. You've got some unbalanced nonsense even in the early to mid portion of the game and this sense of dread where you don't even know if the decisions you are making are valid and worth the time or if you are just wasting everyone's time. Cover feels pretty useless, the game flat out lies about how the free aim mechanic works, and a whole litany of nitpicks I have. But you know what? Every time I think I've had enough, I jump right back in, I discover a new research technology to give my boys a better edge in the field. I kill the Crab menace and win the day, I move onto the next headache. This game isn't nearly as long term playable as XCOM, I have tried to veg out and just play it for a whole night and I often get discouraged by lack of any indication of progress, but I always go back. IF you have played SHOCK TACTICS and you thought to yourself "if only they finished this game, it seems like some good ideas here." well I have good news for you. Phoenix Point is the finished product, and it is worth the 30 bucks I payed for it.

I also rec BATTLETECH, and GEARS TACTICS because those are just fun games that you can find on sale semi frequently.\t\t\t\t\t
