\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game isn't done yet. There are glaring issues with it. If you are game developer and you are making a game that is very close to another on the market (XCOM in this case). Then you need to at least meet the same quality of that game. This doesn't happen here.

- Customization is terrible. All you can do is change 2 colors and they are either drab or neon in tone. Where XCOM has different helmets, goggles, armor etc.. Not. When I played XCOM I used to color code and choose my accessories in a way that I would know what soldier is capable of. Example: Red & White = Medic. You can't do that here. Choose a color to represent your soldiers, that's it.

- Overwatch is soooo bad. The game touts that each bullet has a separate trajectory and that each bullet has a calculation behind it. I think that is great. It's really good watching this in action as 100% of your bullets from an overwatch shot go straight into a solid object instead of the alien crossing your path. This is because the overwatch zone is a cone of view centered off your soldiers current position. Not like the floor tile system in XCOM which after 60 hours I now know is far better. In real life, if I see a bad guy cross my overwatch zone, I don't unload all my bullets into the crate in front of him. I wait for him to come from behind it, then begin shooting.

- Speaking of shooting into objects. The AI can shoot through solid objects, and you cannot. I've had every one of my bullets eaten by a railing, but the AI can shoot through a 4x4 bar support marked as hard cover with every bullet going through. My other favorite sold object effect is with grenades. You can have 3 aliens standing around tree planter, throw a nade into them, have the nade land dead center in the half height planter, aliens take NO damage. Maybe the developers should watch a video on Youtube of what a grenade looks like when it blows up and then apply it to game mechanics.

- Just played a game where I was supposed to evac a VIP off the map. Did this and all that was left was to evac my squad. The VIP died off map. MIssion failed.

- Killed alien, respawns as dark alien, runs through entire squad of overwatch, doesn't trigger, 2 shots my sniper dead.

I have to give this game a NO recommendation primarily list for the above. There are many other issues with the game. Heavy class is practically useless for anything else than jet packing around to loot crates and shooting grenades. What is the use of a class that can only move 4 squares and shoot, when the enemy can move 10-20 and then shoot. Better to have a squad of all Assault troops who can do the same and fire farther, more accurately and more often. Serious balance issues.

The game needs attention in basic mechanics, if this is not addressed, do not buy it.

Uninstalled. DO NOT BUY!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhoenix Point is one of those games that makes me curse the binary nature of the steam review. It is a classic 6/10 game and the thumbs up it receives from me is so half-hearted that it barely makes it above the fingerline. I want you to know that this is a deeply flawed game, that you might enjoy for at least a little while.

Standard opening for context. Who the hell am I? I'm the guy who;

-Got X-COM: Terror from the Deep as one of the two first games I owned on my PC in 1996
-Have since played UFO: EU, Apocalypse, XCOM (2012) and XCOM2, in addition to one of the open source UFO games.
-Of those games, the one that stands out for me as the series at its best is XCOM2 (vanilla). The others are, to my mind, all similarly flawed.

But enough about me, let's talk about Julian Gallop, the man on whose name this game was sold. See Julian was responsible for the original XCOM (and Apocalypse), and since XCOM is strategy royalty and the series has been in a bit of a rut since XCOM2, who better to reinvigorate the genre?

Let's be clear. Phoenix point is XCOM. It's a cover-based, turn-based shooter where your team of marine-types fight of scores of alien creatures, whilst also managing bases and researching the alien menace to progress. I would assume that if you're reading this, you know XCOM and are simply asking where this sits in the list of best XCOM games.

Phoenix Point continues the tradition of XCOM games being b-movie stories. If the modern XCOM games choose the ass-kicking scientists trope, Phoenix point prefers a slightly more nerdy approach. The plot is there, but after a bit I started skipping the new plot sections. I doubt anyone would be glued to their seat in awe of the badly scripted and acted scenes, but they are there to provide some context to the carnage. What I will say is that following WotC's assertion that Alien's are basically Orcs who got lost on the way back from the last Shadow of Mordor design, it is pleasant to see aliens that once again look alien.

So what does Phoenix point bring to the table that XCOM doesn't? The key differential is the "realistic ballistic" mechanic. Realistic is probably a stretch, but rather than the simple percentage chance to hit, you now have a crosshair based system where all shots land inside the outer circle and 50% will land inside the inner. I suspect the inspiration for this all the times your soldier in XCOM had been stood next to an enemy and inexplicably decided to fire straight up into the sky. The other idea is that with each bullet in a burst calculated individually, you are less prone to XCOM's bullshitometer. Phoenix point is a world where you work on 50% of the potential damage output and anything else is a bonus. By and large the above system actually works very well, and though the bullshitometer is very much present, at least I'm missing by a hair rather than a point on the compass.

The other thing that has changed is the tactical map. XCOM's floating base - which a lot of long-term fans found quick reductionist - is gone, and we are back to the Terror From the Deep world of multiple bases to cover multiple areas. Although even by TFtD's standards this has been cranked up heavily. I suspect you would need something like 30 different bases to cover the globe. That isn't a requirement necessarily, but if you are the completionist those are the figures that you're looking at.

This is a major theme in the design of Phoenix Point; the idea that "less is more" is wholly untrue, and in fact "more is more." How would you like five different types of shotgun, all with their own strengths and weaknesses? 10 alien unit types? Pfft. Phoenix point has well over 40! Three factions (more with dlc) to either ally or antagonise. Several different quest lines. A huge research tree. The works. For the people who were disappointed that the modern XCOM's scaled back so many of these features it reads list a wishlist.

As the adage goes, though, be careful what you wish for.

My first issue with Phoenix point is that the interface has a clunkiness to it that really seems like bad design. It is epitomised by the manufacturing screen, where you can sort items by the soldier's class, but not by item type. So to go back to those five shotguns you will need to memorise each detail and scroll up and down through the rest of the items to view each one. It makes comparison a pain in the arse, and I'm not sure why it was excluded.

The next issue is that for all the choice to have 40 odd aliens, the reality is that most are copy-paste reskins with boosted stats. The simple reality is that there are very few that create a true sense of fear when they arrive on the map. I didn't always like the art design in XCOM2 but there was no doubt that when a Muton/Crysalid/Gatekeeper showed up for the first time it was always a brown pants moment. Phoenix Point only really has one of those, the mind controlling, will-sapping Zerg-like . The game also tells you at the start of each month that you'll be facing new enemies and gives you a breakdown on each one. Call me a traditionalist, but would it be more dramatic to discover these threats in battle rather than in a small pamphlet?

There are technically more variation in the classes in Phoenix point, including the option to multi-class (which most soldiers will inevitably do). Yet when I compare the choices on offer to that of XCOM:EU or XCOM2, most powers feel a lot less dramatic. They have also done away with the rank system, and instead gone for simple character levels, which is a bit disappointing. The de facto best way to level up those characters is to buy training centres and just leave them in the HQ, which isn't really in the spirit of things. Customisation is very much a tertiary concern, but Phoenix Point is much more limited than XCOM and also seems to have some very, very odd ideas about what hair looks like. I'm also still scratching my head why I can't rename and customise the two characters from the prologue, who have contributed absolutely nothing to the story at any point since.

Yet these issues are but minor ones, and Phoenix Point could still be a great game in spite of them. It isn't, of course, and that is because of the next issue.

It is exactly the same problem that UFO:EU had, that Terror from the Deep had, and that XCOM Apocalypse had, but it's somehow even worse here. See all those games were characterised by Julian's approach to game design: Take a good idea, make the game expansive and then slowly drive the player off by making them engage in the same process an ungodly amount of times. The XCOM games I mentioned at the start - only the modern games I completed. I didn't fail to complete the early ones because I couldn't, I chose not to complete them because after a while they became repetitive and dull. XCOM:EU and XCOM2 don't get everything perfect but they have gone to great lengths to make ensure that you're stuck in these traps that plagued the old games.

Eventually in Phoenix point you will expand to have bases on 3-4 continents. At this point the game falls apart on itself because it feels like that should be headway, but at that point - the point I am at right now - the Haven and base attacks are so frequent that I don't have time to do the main quest. Yet these haven attacks are directly tied to a failstate of the world's population, so I have to get involved. Even more frustratingly, the transport aircraft presumably have an elaborate holographic device that creates the illusion of jet propulsion instead of the propellers they must actually be using in order to get anywhere that slowly. My troops were in my base in the western US. My base in China got attacked, but I had 24 hours notice. The craft could not get there in time.

Phoenix point quickly descends into the standard Gallop slog. If you liked the original XCOM games, carry on. If it was just the reboots, you might want to steer clear.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBit of a rant here.

I picked up the game when it released on steam. I would have bought it the day it came out but they pulled the 1 year epic exclusive crap. I refuse to put that launcher on my pc. I've done about 2 play throughs but I have only beat it once on veteran. First one I lost bad because I was still learning. I am a die hard XCOM fan. I've played the original xcom on PS1 for gods sake. I had to download an emulator for terror from the deep because this was before steam had it. I have apoc but never got into it. I beat xcom reboot and put over 1k hours into XCOM 2+WOTC because of the awesome modding community. I have mixed feelings about Phoenix Point. I for one hated how the creator back stabbed the donators by selling his soul to EPIC. I'm glad I waited the year for the release because I heard the game was very buggy at launch. Which makes me question "Hey. All those millions of dollars EPIC paid you to release your game on their platform. If it's this broken at release just how much of that money went into the game?"

The geoscape. Feels like it's more play your way instead of playing how the game wants you too. Keeping nations happy for supplies, no thanks. In PP(lol) your mostly left to your own devices. Which can feel overwhelming at first but is nice once you get the hang of it. The pit stop mechanic feels wrong though. There have been times where I didn't do missions because I couldn't get there. The Pure's fortress was in Australia for me and I had nothing near there. By the time I got a ship with a far enough range to raid the place the entire country was wasted.

I love how you can aim at specific body parts instead of a hit %. I really hated in XCOM how you can fire a shotgun point blank into a muton with 98% and miss! I love shooting things in the arms to disable their weapons ect. I also love that I don't need a target to fire. If someone is hiding behind a wall I can try to punch a hole in the wall. I could do that in the original XCOM and I missed that feature.

Their version of overwatch is better than XCOM and worse at the same time. It was good to be able to point where I wanted the soldier to shoot. However that wasn't enough. Several enemies crawl. Your soldiers will take shots at them even if it is impossible to connect a shot. My biggest issue with overwatch was the fact the enemies are still moving when a shot is taken. I've had 5 people pointing at one guy. When the first 2 finished their shots the little bastard had ran past the other 3 and killed someone. Forcing me to restart the mission. It would be nice if the enemy moved a bit slower when being shot at or my troops fired a bit faster.

Not enough mission types. Mostly I'm protecting havens or doing a scavenging missions. Those got old real quick. I like the base defense missions but it would have been nice to actually have facilities to help you with those defenses. Eventually I ran out of points of interest and it felt like I was only doing haven defense missions majority of the game. When I completed the game I checked my stats and I had done over 6 scavenging missions, 2 soldier rescues, 6 base defense missions, 4 ancient ruin defense, 19 nests, 12 lairs, 5 citadels and over 60 haven defense missions. That gives you an idea of what you will be doing most of this game.

Base layout was dull. There was no real reason to think about how you build your bases. No thought process on what to build or where to put it. Except for 1 base that was basically a farm, all my other bases where exactly the same. There is a reason to build multiple bases, which reminds me of the old xcom. However each base feels so dull and like it's there so you can reach other areas. In the old XCOM I had several defense facilities I could construct. If I had enough a UFO was destroyed before it landed. Preventing the need to defend the base at all.

Not enough enemies. I do love how they evolve and adapt to what your doing but it just felt like there wasn't enough types. Their version of a heavy, assault and berserker was the exact same enemy. Just with different armor and weapons. Some enemies had really cheesy mechanics. I hated Umbras. I really disliked how a siren can walk a country mile, mind control someone then walk another mile to get behind a structure. Making it impossible to hit her.

Classes. I like and hate the fact you can mix classes. Some combinations are amazing. Like snipers with rage burst or techs who can toss turrets straight from their inventory. However I felt the classes alone were not enough. It's like they knew you would combine 2 classes so they gave us 2 halves to make 1 whole. Some classes flat out need a specific sub class to function. Like the berserker. I felt I needed to train them in assault for dash so they could get into melee range. The Priest class was cool but it feels very weak compared to the Psi Operatives I'm used too from WOTC. Once a squad got maxed out I lost a little interest in battles because there was nothing more to learn.

I like the 3 factions. Their backgrounds, the fact we can trade with them, share research, steal stuff from them. A nice touch. I did feel a little overwhelmed at times when multiple havens were being attacked at once. I like how we can appease to all of them but once they start fighting each other, it felt like being ashamed to be a human. Couldn't we at least wait till after we wiped out Pandora before turning on each other? If I start another play through I plan to raid them more so I can get a better start.

The aircraft. I felt like the generic manticore is the best aircraft. Fits enough people and goes pretty fast. The New Jericho helicopter plane had more room but it's slower than manticore. The synedrion plane was quick but could only fit 5 people. The Anu blimp has the most space but it was so damn slow I regret stealing it. When I found out how slow it was I wanted to give it back. I had a starting position in South America. When I expanded to North America I moved my blimp up north. Some missions ended before it even got there. I had to send it back south and move the manticore up there.

I liked the various research trees. Different weapons for different play styles. Certain factions offer different research. It didn't feel like a linear path compared to xcom. Unlock laser/gauss weapons first, get armor then go straight to plasma weapons.

The story is gripping me. Similar to XCOM but different enough to be it's own thing and I love the lovecraftian take on it. I would have enjoyed some actual CG cut scenes instead of still pictures. The voice actors were average but they didn't have any names I recognized. My biggest issue with the story is multiple endings. I don't have a problem with that but how to get them. Making max research with a faction unlocks their final mission. Which is essentially the same as the final battle except the ending is different. Researching the Phoenix solution forces you to take their ending.

I despise the ancients DLC. The hoplites are annoying as #@$% with their shields. I have multiple sites and I still can't find the one that lets me craft the weapons and it's more crap to protect. I couldn't find all the manufacturing sites.

The cover system feels crappy. I felt despite being in cover enemies could still hit me pretty regularly. I had a heavy with a robotic augment that gave him a shield. They had zero trouble shooting him in the head -.- It almost reminded me of playing Gears of War 5. When playing horde on Master difficulty. Even if you are in cover they can still hit you. It's just flat out better to block line of sight then to be in actual cover.

Last the final mission. First half is fun but the 2nd is really bad. You will know when you see it.
Overall if you like xcom this game is alright. Has some things I wish xcom had and vice versa. Get it when it's on sale though.
