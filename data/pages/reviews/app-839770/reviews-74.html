\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's really just not good. Repetitive missions, broken mechanics, unfair status effects, same-y enemies, obtuse strategic layer, characterless soldiers, too much trial and error, it goes on and on.

The only redeeming factor is the manual aim system, which is novel and could've helped to differentiate the game from other games like it, though I'm not sure if I really prefer it over chance-to-hit based systems.

Even the narrative doesn't really make sense. The game seems to push you towards stealing from factions (it doesn't exactly fit for phoenix point, the supposed saviors of humanity, to resort to banditry) and eventually having them fight one another despite the fact that humanity is being systematically genocided. I also don't like Synedrion, as a communal anarchist utopian faction who wants to be friendly to the environment and combat all forms of discrimination feels a little out of place on an Earth which is already a wasteland by the time the game takes place. It feels like it's trying to be grimdark too hard which just harms the game in the end.

I got it on sale, and it still feels like a waste of money. I doubt that any additional content added onto this game will redeem it unless it's accompanied by major reworks.

Just go play XCOM or Xenonauts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts similar to games like the XCOM series and doesn't really add anything new to the genre. The world map is very large but the mission maps seemed smaller. The world map is spherical and rotates which I found difficult to navigate. Its hard to tell how far a path is when you can't see it all and it doesn't show the travel time. Its also hard to see what has changed in the world when you can't see it all at once. I would much prefer a flat map with some wraparound instead. So it says I have 275 hours but my game time to win was only 21 hours. Some of this is from just leaving it on all day but the majority is having to constantly restart missions because one of my dudes got wrecked from full health to death in one round. This game has no downed state and your dudes die a lot. It takes a lot of resources and game time to outfit a new soldier so you cannot accept much loss as resources are tight. It only auto-saves before the start of each mission so if you don't save it yourself often you will have to repeat a lot of the missions. The game works with both mouse and controller but it certainly is not optimized for controller. A lot of the U/I navigation is just much quicker with the mouse. There is a huge bug in the controls where the cursor randomly decides to home to the top corner of the map and gets stuck there and disables the controller. You have to move the mouse to get things to work again. There are a lot of times where the cursor stays on the wrong part of the map when you are moving between soldiers, if you are using the controller you have to move it all the way back but if you use the mouse it will jump to where it is supposed to be. It locked up on me a couple of times and I had to kill the task to exit. There was a big patch since I bought this, but it did not fix any of the issues I was having and broke some of the animation, especially deaths. The missions are very repetitive, with your standard kill, steal, protect, destroy variations and the maps themselves are reused a lot. I got somewhat bored with it long before the game finished but managed to stick with it to the end but I will have to have a long break from it before I try another round. Overall, if you like XCOM type games then you should try this one if you can get it on sale, if you don't then you should just skip it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I wish there was a maybe category, I love turn base strategy games, I love everything that is in the xcom style of genre. I hate xcom because of random chance, theres a lot less of that here which is nice.

The game offers a fairly steep learning curve with an enemy that is better than you in everyway save its controlled by an AI that can be outwitted. The way shooting works is a lot better here, with it actually being physics based as opposed to other game's Your dice roll veruss theirs. The DLC adds new tools for you to use and new enemies that are terrifying to face.

However, there are bugs and some balancing issues. Some weapons in classes are clearly better than others, the three major factions are not as balanced as they seem and often times you will face down right unfair fights that you either need to flee, be lucky or use genuis strategic moves.

Also corrupted horizons adds a new enemy that is complete and utter ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. You can kill it but it will be tough.\t\t\t\t\t
