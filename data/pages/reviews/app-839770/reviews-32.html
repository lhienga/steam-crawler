\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am pretty torn about this game (disclaimer: I'm an avid fan of Gollop's original X-Coms, the first one I literally played it for many summers during my childhood). And this really and truly feels like the spiritual of successor of those games in many areas. So why is the negative rating? As much as I enjoy it, I can't really recommend it. It's just simply... too ambivalent on so many levels.

Every amazing feature comes with a huge blowback. Every DLC is a mixture of joy and pain. The quality of life is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ abysmal in this game. And last, but not least, there are still glitches and bugs in the game. Not major ones, but for the love of god, this stuff is on the market LITERALLY FOR YEARS.

In it's core, it's really a modern X-Com, seems and feels that Gollop tried to pack everything in a neat package he ever wanted. On the surface looks and plays amazing, huge and wide range of customization, along with a vast sandbox world to play with. Then you start hitting the issues. The enemies scale up pretty fast and while your equipment improves, nowhere near to the enemies' capability. For example there is a single tier of armor upgrade and that's it. Which is manageable (larger teams, you learn the ins and outs of the game), but makes the long series of fights a tedious grind and takes away the joy of the original X-Coms of sending your troops in battle with new gear almost constantly.

Then you suddenly start to realize that some of the essential gameplay stuff is locked behind DLCs (I'm talking about you, hibernation pods). Which aforementioned DLCs either bring frustation or (and) represent a serious sink of resources with barely any or minimal return. Talking about resources, it's super-essential here how you manage them. Then you suddenly realize that the game ♥♥♥♥♥ you over in many aspects. For example you can't scrap vehicles. God knows why. Just imagine that in the original X-Com you couldn't sell your Interceptors, Skyrangers, tanks and no, you can't manufacture stuff here to sell with profit (hello Laser Rifles from the original X-Com) to mitigate the dent on your budget.

Then comes the quality of life. The overwatch feature seems nice at the beginning, then you realize that even your veteran soldiers will happily shoot their comrades in overwatch, because you didn't set up the reaction cone in a pixel-perfect way. Or, the grenade launcher trajectory and UI shows that you can hit something, but no, that grenade will explode by hitting something mid-air. Was it your last grande or last action in the turn? Tough luck!

Or I could talk about the geoscape. Soon, you will have around a half dozen aircrafts flying around, which -if Festering Skies is enabled- will get even more bloated, as you will need aircrafts for intercepting enemy flyers and troop transporting ones to react to POIs, missions, haven defenses, etc. And transporting troops and vehicles between bases. Yes, the old system is gone, where you gave the transport order, now, you have to manually taxi the aforementioned troops and vehicles by aircraft. A ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ abysmal ♥♥♥♥, no idea how it went through the playtesting. To add injury to the insult, you can deploy 8 soldiers for a mission (have no illusions, you will need each and every one of them) and the single aircraft capable of transporting 8 troopers is so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ slow that the Pandorans can kill a half continent until it gets from A to B. Which means, that each of your teams (you need at least 2 separate task force, ideally 3) are loaded up in 2 separate aircrafts. Wait, did I mention that one of the DLCs require you to park a manned aircraft at POIs? So, have fun with managing 8-10 flying ♥♥♥♥ moving around and keeping track each one of them, responding to crisises, handling missions, intercepting flying ♥♥♥♥, trade runs, troop hiring, haven defense, clearing pandoran bases, etc, etc, etc.

In all fairness and bottom line is... if you were a fan of the original X-Coms, go for it. Otherwise there is a fair chance you will be disappointed. It not only replicates the feeling of the original X-Coms, but also feels like -quality of life and difficulty- a game from the 90s. Not in a good sense.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would only recommend this game to players that played original UFO series. It definitely has modernized UFO gameplay and vibe. However, the game is plagued with some tedious and bad design decisions, especially in DLC, even I would recommend playing it without DLC.

If they fix game design mistakes from this game, I would definitely look for the sequel.

The good:
1. Cover and ballistics mechanics are solid and do differ from Firaxis X-Com series
2. Lore and setting is well done, while retaining some vibes from original series
3. Fractions and their agendas add diversity and allow for different play styles plus replayability
4. Geoscape is much alive with events
5. Combat part really gets your brain working
6. Aliens adapt and mutate bodyparts which you can shoot off to disable them and that is really good
7. Tech tree is mostly horizontal with a ton of sidegrades which adds to the diversity of playstyles

The bad:
1. DLC add OP enemies and various penalties to the player without providing adequate or downright bad tools to deal with them. This is actually a serious problem on higher difficulties
2. A lot of missions and level types can drag tediously long with constant spawn mechanics which really kills desire to finish the game
3. A lot can happen without player being even able to react in combat - like killing of NPC's on first turn or spawning shades that can kill a soldier or two without proper activation delay
4. Graphics are 2005
5. UI is clunky, especially on geoscape\t\t\t\t\t
