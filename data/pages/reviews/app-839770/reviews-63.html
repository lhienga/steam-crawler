\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlease don't buy this game, I've tried and tried to like it, but it has nothing on X-Com. All I'm trying to do is finish it and I'm really struggling, not because it's particularly hard, because it just so tedious. It's disappointing this game has so many positive reviews and there are so many examples of positive reviews with more con's than pro's.
1) It's okay for the first hour or two, then just gets tedious, the missions are very repetitive and take a long time.
Examples:
* Most of the time it takes multiple rounds to take one enemy, sounds good? Not really it's just tedious. This actually gets worse as the game progresses. They will often hit yours guy in the head, they miss maybe 10% of the time. This leads to you not having that guy not doing anything while he heals himself, meaning you take longer to kill the other guy. This seems to be a plus on the surface, like ha! the mission must be hard, but after you've done this twenty times in a row (even though they are all different missions), you really won't feel anything. Since you will do so many missions just to try and progress the story.
* A creature shoots other creatures at you from across the map, again tedious you shoot the 4 critters it flings, then you get to shoot it a little, then you do it again for 3 more turns. Why because they gave it a huge amount of hit points. This isn't a fun creature to fight and in the endlessly spawning missions, by the time one is dead the next shows up, so you get a turn of moving to the objective. No game designer should design it so that artificially padding the time is meant to make it more "game" like. it doesn't perfect example of that.
2) Story is incredibly bland, it's just not engaging. They just roll out trope after trope. The factions really are a best a facade of pretending to have game play depth. But in reality they don't.
3) Incredibly poor balancing.
Example:
Half way through the game I got a "better" sniper rifle through research, it was worse in all respects.
I built a new ship that was meant to house more squad members. I was slower, so due to the design of the missions that require you to save something on a time limit, you can't use it. As your squad will never arrive.
Due to making resources a premium, you can't really equip and have another squad. So the above is made worse, as you can't have a roaming squad and an response squad.
Shields, they just start giving them enemy shields. Sure they are now harder to kill, but their shields allow them to shoot multiple times, then shield up. You have no weapon that just busts shields, so the counter is .. shoot at their head, then their arm. I suspect they think assaulting them at close range is a thing, it's not, shot guns miss way to much at close range. They enemy just execute you after you wander up close.
Close quarters fighting, again super poorly balanced. if you get in close it will often take 4 shots to even paralyse the enemy. Which you mostly don't get.
Heavy weapons are utterly useless and seem to be have been completed designed to be a failures.
Example:
If you stand behind the half wall and shoot it will just hit the wall. Which means you can't jump up onto buildings, because they literally stop you shooting things. As in the guy doesn't rest it on the wall, he just sticks the barrel into the wall and shoots. They seem to proudly display that as a tip, like how awesome. Mind you they do less damage than a sniper rifle and hit a lot less. So my heavy's just turned into snipers, like my entire squad is doing, I only had the assaults to try and get the live aliens, but I've steadily given up on that, as it's just so tedious to try and get live aliens.
4) The games design seems to be anti fun. It would prefer to make it just unfair, clap its hands and say are you bored yet?
5) Let's not even talk about the way the auto save works and the lack of options to add additional auto saves.
6) Bugs lots of them, the mission will just stop working, sit there and the AI never completes their turn. This is the most glaring example of the worst kind of bug, coupled with the lack of auto saves, you must start the entire mission again. So if you just spent 30 mins on this mission, then thanks for your time, rather than detecting our AI can't figure a path to try and get a free shot on you will we just loop endlessly and never return. Again, they prefer the approach to screw the player, it's more important our AI pretends to be good. It's not.
7) The AI takes a long time to complete their turns. This is the worst, I've gone to make tea while the AI gets stuck trying to figure out what to do. Again seems to be fine to bore the player rather than just getting on with the game and allowing it to be enjoyed even if they do something stupid.
8) Talent trees, most of the talents seem meaningless and have no discernible way to see if they work, and you can't reset them if you think maybe this could be different. Go level another squad member if you want to do that I guess, except just one problem some of the talents are random. Again .. why allow some flexibility, that might actually be enjoyable.

I will leave it there, please consider not buying this game. It really isn't worth your time or money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played a ton of XCOM, this game just doesn't feel right. This game strays a lot from other XCOM or XCOM like games. It gets some things right and does those things really well, but as the game progresses it gets more frustrating to play.

Good
- Enemy variety is amazing. All the mutations are super cool and you don't fight the same 5 enemies the whole game
- Videos are really well done
- Mist expansion and map discovery are really well done

Bad
- All the varieties of weapons are pretty useless and all the same DPS.
- No upgrades to weapons while enemies constantly scale up.
- Tech mostly feels useless. Intro techs give you resources instead of new weapons/armor.
- Armor and weapons are pretty expensive especially relatively early.
- Ancient tech is so expensive that it's absolutely worthless.
- The animations are SO SLOW even on very fast.
- Aimcone is a great idea, but all of my soldiers are so inaccurate that the system is absolutely pointless. At no time does it seem there a benefit to aiming for yourself.
- Enemy armor scales so high but you don't really get new tech to shred it. 2 AR bursts close range and it still won't kill Jericho or the Pure.
- Cover feels so worthless. The enemy will place itself so you can't fire through their cover from any angles. You'd think you're fine taking cover behind full cover walls then get hit from across the map.\t\t\t\t\t

You have to bring your big boys, the best tech, and the hammer of god himself to any fight I guess, just in case there are 12 gigantic monsters in a fight that looks like it should be a small force.

The other bugs, clipping, and general unbalance aside, the fact that you can't trust your global information makes Xcom a superior game wholesale.\t\t\t\t\t

Con, the "RNG" is utter useless trash. That same physical model is massively biased. If there's a tiny metal bar filling a bit of the reticle, every single shot will hit it. If a slight deviation means your bomb hits one of your team, guaranteed it will.

In short, the physical model lies to you. Your shots are far more likely to hit any obstacle than it seems based on how much of the circle is covered, and any time it's majorly to your disadvantage, you're going to miss. It's the same bad faith crap underneath the X-COM games. The AI can't win fair, so everything is twisted against you behind the scenes.

I've played this game a lot because I am stubborn and I refuse to let the bad-faith BS beat me, but make no mistake, this game is full of infuriating garbage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA spiritual successor to XCOM 2, it seems like the team took a better game and tried to differentiate from it in a bunch of different ways. Unfortunately, the basic thread behind all of these new mechanics is "add complexity!" which, in a turn-based shooter, does not bode well. This is compounded by the fact that the devs HEAVILY constrict what you're able to pull off via resource restrictions which, in all honestly, is a move.

Pros:
-It looks, and sort-of feels, like XCOM.
-The shooting targeting mechanic is interesting (but not that interesting).

Cons:
-The Geoscape sucks. They took the central ship from XCOM 2, but decided to multiply it by a crapton so theoretically, you are managing a ton of different squads in different ships. Sounds great, except that it takes a MASSIVE amount of resources to build new ships and set squads up, to the point where it's basically impossible.
-The economy in this game is absolute trash. There is no way to generate resources so you're constantly scrounging and actively trading for materials (a very tiring mechanic; I'm here for the fighting, guys!), all so that you can repair bases or equip your squad. Good luck doing both though, as it somehow costs as much to build a pistol as it does to build a generator in a base. I get that they were probably trying to simulate scarcity, but it basically makes the game completely unplayable.
-As if this game is not annoying enough, just as you start to turn your squad into something viable, your weapons become peashooters compared to the cannons the enemies are packing. Pair that with the insanity happening on the Geoscape and severe resource restriction, and you've just got a mess of a game.
-Compared to the rest of these, I'd call this a nitpick (as I'd play the game if the other things were resolved) but the story is SUPER meh. Not very compelling as the main enemy is a bunch of dumb faceless crabs.

TLDR: I'm super disappointed in this one. While XCOM is known for being punishing, the central tenet of the game is that it's tough but fair--you can usually work your way out of a bind with a solid strategy and quick thinking. Somehow the team completely missed that here, and instead made complexity and resource restrictions be your main fight. It sucks when dumb simple things torpedo what could be a great game.\t\t\t\t\t
