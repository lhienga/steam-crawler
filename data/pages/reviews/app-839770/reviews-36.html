- By the end game your faction Phoenix is more or less a glorified PMC not a global protector and that is looking back at the beginning up til now.
- Secondly the gear is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ the only meta that exists is heavy spam more on that.
- Finally combat is plain and boring, there aren't any dynamic shots the camera changes to when firing at someone so the customization more or less ends up being SPARTAN-II'ed/early Halo everyone looks the same but has different colors.

Does it still have good points?
Yes such as making you feel like you are running an armed organization which is where it excels at actually.

But game bad why?
If you're like me and just prefer jumping into 12ft deep water with no knowledge of swimming then you skip the tutorial, like no ♥♥♥♥ that tutorial was SO BORING I actually fell asleep, NOT EVEN MID-WAY THROUGH! But take away don't do it 1st if you're an achievement hunter do that LAST.

But your faction Phoenix why are we a PMC? You start with 1000 material, tech and food these resources are utilized in building bases and base facilities, creating arms and armor PLUS ammo BUT ALSO 'Skyrangers'/aerial Troop transports and straight up IFVs/APCs and their biological counterpart the Mutdogs who instead use mutagen.

I think you can see where this can go you shoot your own soldier mission complete now pay the tithe for using this 1 burst in a 36 round magazine, or with grenades which are considered WEAPONS oooohh god, did I mention that it takes about 5 hours to build one grenade AND they aren't replenish-able? It's pretty bad I feel cause at the beginning the only way to increase resources is to go scavenging but here's the next issue with that, you WILL start campaigns with 4 soldiers and everyone no matter their strength can only carry 9 items with at LEAST 3 slots being occupied by weapons or a med-kit, THEN you have to hope RNGesus lets you find a scavenging mission. Did I also mention enemies keep spawning in?

Or a better view of it all is that you start a campaign red shirt 1 dies and you can no longer reacquire his equipment, this ends up netting you with losing more than you gained because to recruit soldiers you need to research (really?) how to recruit soldiers from havens/settlements BEFORE you can recruit random soldiers from your dormitories and these guys aren't cheap until you get that research LATE game but no only that recruitment pools refresh every 3 days. There are alternative ways to recruit soldiers such as random events or rescue missions but again this type of mission suffers from the issue of losing more than you gained unless you have an APC.

Later in game you start having bases active all over the world which means you start to be swarmed with 'another settlement needs your help' issue thus multiple teams need to be raised and by this point you'll most likely be allied with 1 or 2 more factions who will be paying your bills in sums of 500 to 750 for all resources. I actually like this part of the game minus Preston Garvey but that is the legit BULK of the game. And then I realized the game loop wait for 3 days (7-14 minutes real time) while your 7 day equipment builds itself then 'Another settlement needs your help' end up fighting and on a Hard play through lose more than you can ever gain or Standard lose around 64-100 units of resources. Alternatively you can take missions from the Diplomacy screen but they replenish too damn slow.

- Grab some 'Power Armor' aka heavy armor and make that soldier a heavy plus sniper rifle proficient. Then pull an Ewan McGregor and take the highest point with the jet pack that ALL power armor has and pluck away at everything.
- Grab some Power Armor, again and make that soldier a heavy plus assault, hand them a shotgun or NJ Gauss assault rifle and break some arms because EVERY REAL WEAPON requires 2 hands to use.
- Grab some Power Armor for a THIRD time and equip any grenade launcher or mounted missile launcher and level the city you are suppose to protect.
- Grab some Power Armor for a uh... FOURTH TIME!? and make them a strong (wo)man and jump next to the enemy and beat them to death with a heavy gun or melee weapon because this is Compton?

I think you can see where this is going ok and that is with all major DLCs that add something new to the game minus Kaos Engine.

Heavy armor feels like it should be the standard value for medium armor and medium armor is the standard for snipers. Simply put all armor values should be upped more because robotics are just plain broken by having THE MOST armor value in-game, now you can't jet pack but if you equip the Rhino body you can equip mounted weapons and be the tankest ♥♥♥♥♥♥ on the field which makes up for no jet pack but there is one leg that let's you accomplish some abilites close to the jet pack called the leap, it it's pretty bad just use normal Rhino legs.

You may have realized I only talked about snipers, shotguns, melee and heavy armor why not the other stuff? Well they work but blowing ♥♥♥♥ up like an AMERICAN was just so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ easy why would I switch it up? Even the bigger ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ were becoming wastes of spaces on the field because of how many 4th of July shows I was putting on.


Combat is boring simply because A LOT of the SFX don't feel good.
The melee weapons sound laughable especially since the first and best one is a freaking WARHAMMER with a 40k power field! I'm joking about that last part but it's the only good melee, yes you can bleed with the sword or shock with the Phoenix police baton but the amount of heavy armor you run into on a 'natural selection' run is ridiculous so it ends up being the best since it also dazes.

ARs sounds are the epitome of boring so skip it over fun to use though PDWs have the same issue and are the Sniper's alternate to the Pistol, Pistols ... work but more or less use the same SFX. Shotguns aren't loud just like in real life. SNIPERS are AMAZING probably the only thing that redeems the weapons they also feel fun to shoot trespassers with at CLOSE range.

Speaking of snipers I also realized this is the weapon with THE MOST dynamic angles when firing the weapon. Also about firing weapons, it uses the same combat style of Battletech meaning shooting a guys head will straight up 'kill' him but knee capping him will cause him to have half his movement and breaking arms cause them to become Kira Yamato's victims when using DRAGOONS.

It's actually quite fun to see it on a human scale but lacks the cool visuals it has on Mechs. Speaking of visuals the customization is (look at the big letters dumb gamers) Animal Super Squad. PIGMENT too close to each other! Gears Tactics did it better and it's GEARS the game without much in-depth customization!

I think it did but it promised too much such as a ever evolving enemy that would use lion parts from AFRICA, I met the same Arthron and Triton through natural selection 3 times over. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tImagine you played Xcom and changed all the little things you didn't like about it; the narrow strategic scope, the dice-roll 'that's X-Com, baby!' moments that drove us all crazy. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played the old Xcom games (1-3) and the remakes Xcom1 and Xcom2 I had high hopes for this game. Unfortunately the game feels like a weird mashup of old school Xcom and the newer Xcoms, but repeats some old design mistakes that makes it feel like the devs learned nothing along the way.

For starters, I will admit the start of the game is interesting. The game is fun for a while. Then it turns into a boring crawl as you hope to just happen to get the missions you need to advance. To illustrate what I mean if you want to win on the Phoenix Path you need a lair to spawn. I never got one the entire game. It was always nests. Never a lair. So the RNG on what we found held me up on that route. So I decided to try another route, went down the Anu path and was softlocked by needing a live Siren. You'd think the devs would have learned from the Deep One folly of Terror of the Deep to not make the plot depend on a live capture of an alien that may never spawn or was never spawning anymore but nope. Days of trying to get one to spawn I finally cheated and used the console to automatically research it as I was bored of running around with nothing to do until I caught that Siren, but none were spawning in missions.

Then other issues started to show. There are no good weapon upgrades. I was using starting weapons for the most part in the last mission. However aliens had like triple the HP of what they used to have, and combined with armor meant every fight was a slog now. Apparently these devs didn't pay attention to the outcry over the boss in FF12 that had 50 million HP. Higher HP =/= challenge. Oh and the final boss on the Anu route has 9600 HP and I was still doing 30 x 6 with assault rifles and 110 with sniper rifles and 50 x 3 with my heavy. I'll let you do the math on how long that took.

Another issue I had though was how soiliders upgraded, or didn't. You get points to upgrade in skills and in a couple stats, but you can never improve a very vital stat, and that is your to hit. What you got for your ability to hit enemies is what you will have all game. If you get a sniper that can't hit the broadside of a barn at 5 paces, they are never going to get better. First Xcom type game I played where you couldn't improve your accuracy with training. Even the remakes you got some improvement with level ups at least.

And finally, the ballistics system needs to be scrapped. I heard a lot of bragging about there's no random to hits and its all ballistics. Bull. What you get instead is a circle you still got a 50% chance to hit in the first and 100% to hit in the second. That's still random though, and is a random probability on your to hit. I found over the game I actually missed more than I did in the Xcom reboots. I know we may have all seen the meme of the point blank shot with 68% to hit from Xcom1 remake, but here you can do the exact same still, its just hidden and you don't see the numbers. I missed a point blank shot. So did aliens. And the free aim system is terrible. Fallout 4 has a better aim at body parts system, take a lesson from that please. Let me at least select which body part to aim at directly.

Overall I feel the devs need to go back and really look hard at the older games. What they got here is not a fun overall mix. Make sure they learn what made the old games fun. Make sure they learn what people like about the reboots too. And also realize that players want to get more powerful. Gimping the players like they did here with no good weapon upgrades but aliens get 3x the hit points over the game is just not fun. Overall all playing this did was make me want to play Xcom2 again, and if I spend my entire playtime wishing I was playing a different type of game of this type, you failed somewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recently returned to this game and vhoose this over Xcom and I am really enjoying myself.

It is an Xcom game but with a bit more twists to it.

Management.
I see a lot of negative comments about the management of the game and for me it's a bit more immersive. You have sort of a economy to balance. The bigger you get the more economy you gonna need. Most economy comes from completing missions and trade.

Just to master this creates its replays or very careful eye to detail or you can end up, like some of the complaints, with no ammo on a mission.

And I like that to an extent. You need to interact with the environment more than just combat.

You need to keep your forces prepped and ready for battle.


More sandbox than Xcom.
I'm Xcom you have one ship, can go to one place with at a time. Here you can have as many ships and operatives as you can manage and go to all locations more or less at once.
The game is more alive and the path to the end isn't super linear.
You have these factions that you interact with that can change how you approach the end game.
You have sidequests that pop up that you can manage if you have a ship and some troops over.

Shure the setting in Xcom2 and Phoenixpoint are different giving Phoenix point more freedom. But it is a really good freedom.


More choices on the battlefield.
With all special factions that can mix into the combat as well as the different classes phoenixpoint gives you A LOT of classes to mix with. Keeping the game pretty fresh all the way through.

I love the aim system. This really makes it more superior to Xcom games. I literally hate "87% hit chance" and he fires into the sky (if its close)

No more missing point blank shots!!
Also if an enemy takes cover you can aim for an arm, leg or back if it pops out from the cover giving you these chances to damage enemies behind half covers.


Phoenix point is an underrated game. It's focused more on managing your potential army with a lot of freedom and sandbox experience. Balancing politics between the three different factions, who to befriend and who to not. There is a timedial on the game but much more subtle than in Xcom. At the same time as I enjoy Phoenix Point a lot I always find myself feeling that it missing something that Xcom has even if I enjoy the features in Phoenix point more than in Xcom. One thing I guess is the "visual epicness" xcom brings.
It has very little to do with the gaming features but it brings a lot of atmosphere. When monsters become alerted there is this small clip of them reacting. Sometimes when your guy does an epic sprint or take aims and shoots is really nice. Phoenix point would have benefited from that. And that all abilities in Xcom feels quite powerful while in Phoenixpoint some abilities feel more generic and does not do as much.

At the same time as I miss some features from Xcom while playing Phoenix point I miss other features from Phoenix Point while playing Xcom.

It is a good game that challenge you in a different way.
It got a lot of replayability because you are going to fail that management first.
Then you have the different factions to play around giving you different choices and ways to play the game.\t\t\t\t\t
