\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really just a mess. The original Epic release was unbelievably broken, and now over a year in the game has at least improved significantly but still has just so much more work to be done. As far as a game in the X-COM/XCOM genre goes, it's decently solid and I think is worth playing for dedicated fans of the series, but it can really be an exercise in tedium as the game goes on. Balance has been a consistent issue, and as the developers keep releasing patches to nerf certain abilities or equipment, or fix various exploits, the game just keeps getting less fun. A lot of abilities are just really weak and useless now, and so many weapons just have no place in the game. Snipers are just simply too inaccurate to be useful, and in general builds centered around mid-to-close range tend to work best. Enemies don't tend to have particularly varied strategies, they just ultimately stack armor as the game progresses, generally making fights against them more tedious than challenging. The three factions in the game will all hate each other no matter what, kinda making "diplomacy" pointless, but are at least capable enough at defending themselves that you don't really need to worry about protecting every single haven. Bugs, freezes, and crashes are everywhere: enemies will constantly loop animations for some reason, death animations don't play sometimes and an enemy's model will just be stuck after death, abilities will sometimes just be unusable even if you have the AP and WP for them, many bugged and broken achievements, the list goes on. Many bugs have been fixed since day one, so it does at least feel like the devs are continuing to work on the game, perhaps by "year two" we'll have more of a cohesive and finished product.

Bonus round, so far the DLCs are all pretty bad. Granted, you get them for free with this version of the game, but they're just not good.
Blood and Titanium adds yet another enemy type that just has absurd amounts of armor and little else in the way of strategy, and the augmentations it adds for your soldiers are mediocre at best, and generally not worth the cost to manufacture and repair.
Living Weapons was originally a pre-order bonus, just adds a set of armor, a rifle, and an LMG. The armor is very durable but doesn't do much else for you, and the weapons, while better than your starting equipment, will fall out of use pretty quickly. Use them or don't, they don't really add or detract anything from the game.
Legacy of the Ancients is pretty awful. Everything about it comes so late in the game that you'll have had your soldiers' builds and equipment locked in long ago, the new enemy types it adds, once again, just stack lots of armor and are also immune to almost every type of damage, leaving you with the strategy of "shoot it until it dies". The new equipment it adds is admittedly very powerful, but is just largely unnecessary, and since the enemies are just a chore to fight to even reach those weapons, I don't think it's really worth it.
Festering Skies is not out yet, will be releasing in about a week as of writing this, and I believe it will require an additional purchase. Enemy UFOs and interceptions was something that was promised initially so this expansion screams of cut content, also bizarre to me that it took so long to get this one out, but from what I've been reading about it, it doesn't sound very promising. Instead of adding a detailed air game, it just seems to add weapons to the four transport aircraft that are in the game, and UFOs that will occasionally interfere with your craft. There's a large roaming base that you need to deal with, but it feels like the UFOs and interceptions, what should be the focus of the expansion, are more of an afterthought. I'll gladly eat my words on this, but I am just not impressed with what they've shown.

I wanted this game to be Julian Gollop's prodigal return to the genre, and it came out as more of a wet fart. It really didn't break any ground in the genre, and while I usually don't care much about Epic v Steam drama, I do believe Snapshot Games taking the Epic deal for that first year of exclusivity really hurt this game, having it come out unfinished and then finally release to Steam a year later with very little fanfare. Perhaps a better mod scene could help this game, perhaps it just needs to cut its losses and start work on a sequel, but as it stands, I think this game turned out as a failure. I can only hope the team learned a lot from the work they've put into the game over the last few years and can improve for next time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt says it's "from one of the creators of XCOM", and that certainly shows. The broad strokes of the game are very similar to XCOM: You have a global strategy map, you face an implacable global threat, you train and equip squads of troops, and you go on daring high-risk missions with intricate tactical combat. Even most of the details of how the tactical combat proceeds feel very XCOM, from the satisfaction of getting a sniper headshot to the despair of seeing your squad leader get one-shot.

But there are some important differences, some obviously good, some more ambiguous. The realistic ballistics simulation, the addition of land vehicles, and the option to build new bases and aircraft to deploy more squads are all excellent improvements on the formula. But some of the more realistic details require more micromanagement, such as needing to keep track of ammo magazines, replacing used medkits and grenades, and equipping each soldier with their own specific armor. Your mileage may vary on whether this increases your immersion or just feels frustrating. The way that enemies continually mutate and improve over time makes for a compelling challenge, but it can also be annoying to try to keep track of so many different enemy types at once. There are like twelve different types of Arthron alone. You will receive periodic intel reports on the new enemies, which are so long and detailed you will want to TLDR them.

The biggest differences from XCOM are actually in terms of setting and plot of Phoenix Point. The Pandorans are not a alien race with advanced technology; they're more like a virus that mutates and spreads itself through infesting and dominating humans. The situation is considerably bleaker than XCOM: Human civilization is already on the brink of ruin, and the few remaining factions are either naively idealistic (Synedrion), dangerously purist (New Jericho) or just plain bizarre (Disciples of Anu).

If you love XCOM, you'll love Phoenix Point. At least you know what you're getting, eh?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed at launch with Epic, then came back for the Steam version. Completed both versions. Inevitable comparisons with XCOM aside, PP does a few things well, and others not so well.

POSITIVES:

> Shooting is arguably better than XCOM, as there is no % to hit. So no frustrating misses due to that 99% which whiffs. Positioning and weapon choice are rewarded, along with being able to shoot specific areas (like the head of a mind control enemy, or shielded crab etc)

> Action Points are more flexible, with a move, shoot, and move again. Or shoot twice. There are option here that I miss when going back to XCOM.

> Steal stuff. Be it vehicles or research. Again this is more flexibility, that lets you adjust what you want and when.

> Factions. Stereotypical yes, but it's nice to have them around so it feels like the world is also doing it's part combat the enemy. Trade with them, do missions, and also double cross them if you so choose. They need work though..

> Soldiers. Lots of play here for soldier builds, with RNG skills mixed in with multi-class options. Customization is ok, but XCOM trumps it here in a big way. Still, soldiers look quite nice.

MIXED:

> Story. Eh, it's ok. Multiple ways to win though, but you wont exactly be gripped by the narrative.

> DLC Content. A real mixed bag here. The main one, Ancients, throws in new enemies and powerful gear. But it is super grindy and confusing. The smaller DLC gives you augmentations which are good, and gives you more soldier customization. The last one is a small one, with an armor set, and couple of guns that mostly not worth the missions.

NEGATIVES:

> Mid to End Game is quite frankly a mess. Haven defense needs a full rework as you are just spammed with random defense missions as the mist covers more of the globe. And those missions lack interest and meaningful reward. There is no percentage to see if the haven might successfully defend (most times they don't, NJ faction sometimes does win). No option to help them set up defenses, or lend them some troops to bolster future attacks. They are just awful, and keep coming...and coming.

> Doomsday clock. Sigh, does anyone like these? As the lil bar depletes you are supposed to be filled with urgency. But instead you get spammed with haven defense missions as described above. This needs a rework, as the pacing is just so off.

> Character. This is where the big budget clout of Firaxis beats PP hands down. The game lacks a Bradford, and anyone interesting to breath life into the story and tactical missions.

> Enemy design. You like seafood? We got crabs. Big tanks ones, skinny sneaky ones, and slithering mind control ones. Even giant building crushing ones. But still, nothing really memorable that makes you love to hate them. The goo ones just feel cheap and frustrating, while the mind control ones teach you to never leave home without a headshot specialist. Faction enemies are more interesting though, as are the ancients. But the Pandorans are largely forgettable.

>Sound and Music. There is a fine line between atmospheric and depressing. PP often opts for the latter. While combat music just lacks any excitement and bite. Strategy layer music I end up turning off as it is so gloomy. Sound effects are hit and miss, with a few larger ones sounding low budget. Yet the crack of a rail gun is satisfying.

OVERALL

Despite the cracks, there is a good game here and the core is solid. A few mods could easily sort out the issues and add polish. Plus the year one edition is vastly improved over what was released. DLC is ok, but the next one is supposed to be biggest yet.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrankly it's too hard. The research tree is a mess, if you can call it a tree. Most research doesn't seem to lead to anything else. Researching the aliens themselves does almost nothing other than a small buff in damage against them. The base Phoenix Point weapons are junk and they never improve. Soldiers very quickly max out in level and then that's it, they don't improve anymore either. Perhaps I haven't played enough (though you would think 49 hours would qualify), but at this point, it is no longer fun.\t\t\t\t\t

This game while having the same layout and feel as xcom has some major differences that I consider neither good nor bad; they create a unique game that stands on it own. The time commitment is much higher as even 18 hours in I feel like I have barely scratched the surface. The lack of % chances and instead free aim mechanic is bother enfuriating and unique. This game isn't for everyone and even after playing for so long I am not sure if I am enjoying it yet keep thinking about it after I stop.\t\t\t\t\t
