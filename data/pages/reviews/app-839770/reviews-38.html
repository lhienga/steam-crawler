\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh, you want to skip that animation? Too bad
Oh, you want to compare two different weapons to select the best layout for your troops? Too bad
You want the game to stop selecting units that have 0,1 action points left when you cycle with tab? Too bad
Oh, you want to interact with the keyboard in general? Too bad

A workable game in theory with some improvements over it's main competitor (XCOM) but god almighty is the User Experience bad. Playing it involves some much ludicrous micro-management that when you combine everything makes this game more of a burden than a fun experience.

They even managed to ruin exceptionally simple things. For instance when you open the menu and select "Exit Game" or whatever, you get presented with a "Loading..." screen. Who's idea was that!? Like, seriously. First time I thought "omg, did I just start a mission by accident?"

So many rookie mistakes have been made in this game's design (or execution) that I'm not sure whether I'll continue playing. I was really eager (I can't emphasize this enough) to finally have a modern alternative to XCOM2 but I better stick to XCOM2 I and re-play it for the 10th time. Even with all it's flaws XCOM2 is a far better option. Because it's more streamlined and doesn't try to do all the things at once. This game in comparison tries too much (and fails).

Note: I'm well aware that this game was released almost two years ago. It was originally an exclusive for a different platform and I intentionally quite a long time to play it, assuming that the game would be polished by now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom the first moments of launching I felt something was off. I couldn't and still cannot exactly put my finger on it. The UI is quite plain and lacking proper "user friendly design" and looks and feels from the last century. Tooltips are lacking. Choosing weapons to manufacture gives you a dummy list of EVERYTHING, no sorting, no clues to see what is more recently discovered or might have higher value in combat. Combat is tediously slow, no simultaneous moving. In the end I think it is low production value. There is no polish. In clone of a game from 1994 there should be a damn lot of polishing!

I do realize the game is a successor to original XCOM by the original creator and wants to follow in its footsteps, but come on, the market is so saturated in the genre. Something outside of the box thinking should've been in place! Or if you do repeat the same formula, make it really polished. Me, coming from the recent XCOM games as well as Wasteland, this just feels like a sub-par experience.

If you want XCOM-like turn-based strategy & tactical experience, get Wasteland 3 or the recent XCOMs. If you have already played those, then be prepared that this game does not live up them.

It's not all bad though. It's decent turn based strategy and tactics. It has new elements and great mechanics and core gameplay (duh), but in this market, it is not worthy of a recommendation.

I'll change this review if getting further in the game changes my mind.\t\t\t\t\t
