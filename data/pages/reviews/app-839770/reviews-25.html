\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is well developed and story is also quite interesting. Comparing with X-COM, I would say Phoenix is a bit harder (or different). Had to restart 3 times (on veteran) before I could get more or less how everything is working. Main difference is the science probably, in X-COM it was quite critical to push tech asap, here not so much. I researched almost 100% (only advanced medkit left afaik) and most "battle" tech came almost near to the end - just to fill the queue. What I did early - reversed the laser sniper rifle and that was pretty much the only "weapon" based tech that I had like 80% of the game. The main difference - when phoenix or any faction researches something, enemies will evolve almost right after that (I suppose evolution based on overall "battle" tech in game, doesn't matter who contributed into it). So no reason to expedite it. Just reverse engineering some weapons, open classes based armours and that's it. Most time of the game I had only 4 science facilities at all, at the end when it was boring, I had extra resources and only final useless researches left or final factions techs - built extra 6 facilities to expedite a bit. Most time 4 factories and 4 sci labs was totally enough.

The only concern was ending - I researched all factions techs and was going to try New Jericho one ending, but its not possible to choose - faction that you will research everything first will be the only way to finish the game, doesn't matter if you would decide to try different ending (expect Phoenix way, ofc).\t\t\t\t\t
