\r\n\t\t\t\t\tXCOM game with a good take of what it's successor could have been, although with some short comings. 7/10

Its very nice to see an enemy that changes/adapts with you depending on which continent you are on. The game allows you to re-think your move when you just spotted an enemy and gives you the opportunity to aim the weapon (no RNG).

The story could be more dynamic, more customisation should be available. Battles can take 10min to 30min sometimes even 1h, so beware! Mid battle save is available in case you need to do something else.

With me, the loading times of battles and the game itself took a long time, around 5min.

I lost a bit of patience since i have been playing this game for 70h and still have to see something of an endgame - missions started to look a bit repetitive, hence my query of the story needing to be a bit more dynamic.

In all a good game that needs patience but has new quirks to offer if you have an XCOM itch to satisfy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my first written review of any game on Steam. I felt the need to write this.

This review is for players of XCOM and those who like strategy games with tactics elements. If you don't like either, I can't imagine you would like this game at all. But even if you do like these kinds of games, I cannot recommend PP.

The game clearly has been given tons of thought. So I'm not going to criticize it for bugs or whatever. Rather, I still cannot recommend this game due to its design choices.

My main gripe is this game is not FUN. You clearly have to think a lot. You clearly have to make some choices. But everything you do feels like a chore. If you win, it's relief and still mainly a chore. If you lose, you just throw up your hands in frustration. Why is it not fun?

I think there are several issues that make this game not fun. First, I felt like I had to fight against the UI, which is really just an impediment to playing the game. The "realistic ballistics" model doesn't actually seem that realistic to me. "laser" guns have recoil such that there is the same sized reticle? Really? Like why should a "laser" gun EVER miss due to randomness? Have you ever missed playing laser tag due to recoil effects? But then you actually have a mounted laser weapon that ACTUALLY never misses? So what's "realistic" about that? This is just a design choice, but it's inconsistent and overall doesn't make a lot of sense.

As another example, as this game relies on line of sight rather than needing to actually get into cover, finding those lines of sight to an enemy, by relying on one single small line from your square to the enemy, is incredibly misleading. It could be the case that the square that purportedly gave you a direct line of sight (LoS) just gives you a TINY sliver of LoS, passing one tree on the left, passing one street lamp on the right, just above some boxes, and barely enough to show the top of the enemy's head. So you can't trust those lines to truly show you how much LoS you actually have. A more helpful design choice might be to give differently colored lines, or differently sized lines, to indicate roughly how "good" that LoS actually is. Now you might try to estimate how good your LoS is by doing your own eyeball test. The problem is, another poor design choice is you only have 4 isometric angles to choose from to view your entire map. A better design choice would be that because of all the variations in available LoS, why not similarly allow for complete rotation of a map? Too many times I literally couldn't tell what's blocking my LoS or not, because I couldn't see straight up and down to the enemy. Terribly frustrating, but see these are just design choices, nothing fundamental about how the game has to be made. A game can be challenging, but it should not be challenging because you have to fight the GAME itself.

Speaking of LoS, did you know that turrets don't have the same LoS as your troops? When you can shoot on a rooftop, where normally that is always good, it could be terrible for your turret because it can't see over the tiny lip over the edge of the rooftop! So at this point, all helper features in the UI are useless when it comes to placing turrets at reasonable spots. Is this "realistic"? Maybe. Is the lack of any assistance from the UI helpful or fun? Nope.

A second reason I just didn't find this game fun is the music, or lack thereof. I never fully appreciated how much the music can help the mood and experience of the game until I found it so utterly lacking in this game. Seriously, no win of a mission has felt so joyless as these, when all you get is a tiny snare drum riff and some synthesized quirky noise after you win. Or the utter lack of driving dramatic music when you encounter enemies. With basically no music whatsoever, the game feels incredibly spartan, dry. And I'm sure this was actually a design choice. The devs probably wanted the game to feel apocalyptic and desolate, with the lack of any ambiance. They surely achieved that if that's their goal, but that doesn't make the game FUN. It makes it devoid of joy, and completing tasks just feels like an utter chore.

Third - and I'll make this my final point out of other points I could talk about - I thought the pacing of the game was just so off. Now I only beat the game on Veteran, but once I discovered the source of the pandoravirus, there wasn't a single objective left to work on anymore for like... 20 hours of me playing the game! That's 20 real time hours. It might have been more. I just kept researching and trading, and just kept.... waiting. The triggers to make the game progress just didn't seem well paced. This seems like a design choice to me as well. Could they have accelerated certain elements artificially for the sake of game pace, if you reached a certain milestone? I say why not; it's a game after all, not "real life." So while waiting to reach end game conditions, I proceeded to pointlessly activate all PP bases, build every kind of weapon and armor, fly around with my troops aimlessly to do the chore of trading with all the havens to increase my resources, and just kept waiting and waiting.

Actually, I finished the game once - only activated the base game before trying the DLCs - but didn't even see a single pandoran citadel. Is that supposed to happen? Am I not supposed to more readily obtain the final PP research before I can just finish the game? I could keep playing to figure out how the rest of the game should work/end, but I just can't bring myself to keep going. I wanted to at least trudge through the game one time and just see how it plays out. Unfortunately, it hasn't really changed my view of the game, and I just don't have the will to bring myself to play the game on harder difficulty or with the DLCs to see if that improves pacing and the experience overall.

In short, I'm sure this game was given tons of thought, but too much of it was executed in a kind of clunky way that I just can't really recommend this for XCOM-type fans.\t\t\t\t\t

Unfortunately I was glad to finish phoenix point , and wont ever play it again. Its tedious , and I know it says expect to lose people, but its brutal, and unless you want to spam save every turn, you can forget having any emotional attachment to anyone.

Its just dumb.
