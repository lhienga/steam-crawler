\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
+ The aiming system is much more realistic, but still relies on RNG, but allows you to control it, do you want to try a risky a headshot or a guaranteed torso shot, but less damage.
+ You can have multiple teams to cover multiple sectors of the world.
+ Different factions with different technology focuses and classes with different play styles to recruit from.
+ You gain "global" skillpoints that can be used on newer recruits that you acquire later on that you like that have better abilities/traits.
+ There a vehicles in the game, but they come at a cost of taking two soldier positions, and cannot reload ammo. But it is a nice addition.

Cons:
- Story is bare bones non-existent, the story line missions are almost no different from the typical gather/protect missions.
- Inventory management is a nightmare, and immersion breaking, why can you take a gun/armor from a soldier and equip it to another solder half way across the world?
- No point in having storage if there is not enough resources to build enough equipment to equip all your teams.
- Repetitive missions.
- Tech research is needed to move the storyline, but overall lacks an impact.
- Weapons max out near the mid game.
- Mist animation graphically intensive for how little impact it has.

Overall:
Get this game if you already played XCOM and want to scratch that itch again with a different setting. But I would I recommend getting this game when it on a discount. I could not get myself to finish the game due to the repetitiveness of the gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

UPDATE - MARCH 2023

2 years have passed, some new unbalanced DLCs released, which only make the game harder. No bugs fixed, new bugs added - the game is as unpolished as it was. And this includes broken Steam achievements...

ORIGINAL REVIEW - JANUARY 2021

The Game is nice, it took the best of classic X-COM: UFO Defense and the modern and dynamic XCOM: Enemy Unknown. It offers decent amount of playtime (it took me about 150h to complete everything). However, I feel that the game is quite unpolished, which is really disappointing given that they agreed to Epic Games' exclusive to get money upfront "to polish the game". There is a lot of balancing problems, serious bugs and other minor issues. I think Phantom Doctrine was better.

Good things

  • More classic action point system that gives you more freedom of moving and shooting. Every unit has 4 action points, attacking requires 1-3 AP depending on the weapon size and that can even be decreased to 0 with certain abilities. Each unit has speed parameter (usually 15-30) which defines how many tiles the unit can move in a single turn. However, moving one tile consumes only a fraction of the action point.
  • Free aim. You don't just order to shoot at enemy, you can actually aim with your soldier which allows you to see the bullets spread of the weapon and target specific body parts.
  • Targeting and destroying body parts. You can cut off alien's arm or destroy their weapon, you can damage their legs so that they bleed and walk slower, and so on.
  • Realistic bullet trajectory - no more bullets hitting an enemy and seeing “missed” pop up. Bullets damage what they really hit.
  • Multiple bases and aircrafts. You have to actually make tough decisions on the strategic screen, manage multiple teams, consider the speed of aircrafts to be able to respond to attacks and so on.
  • A very interesting choice of skills and classes. Together with dual class it opens a way to some very powerful combos.
  • Soldiers in overwatch shoot one-by-one rather than all-at-once.
  • Soldier's movement is stopped when they spot an enemy (although you will lose some AP).
  • The story is interesting - rather than classic alien invasion and UFOs, the humanity has to deal with a deadly virus that mutates living organisms.
  • Equipment pool is global - this might not be a good thing, however if I had to transfer equipment and ammo between bases, it would add a lot of overhaul. Also, ammo crafting is instant and you can automatically replenish soldiers ammo at the end of the mission (at the regular crafting cost).

Bad things

  • Tradeoffs. There is no clearly better weapons/armors/vehicles (excluding DLCs), all items are just different and you have to decide what parameters are most important for you.
    • For example, basic helmet sensor mod gives motion detection, but if you complete advanced sensors research, you will be able to craft a mod that gives thermovision instead of motion detection.
    • I have spent first 30-40 hours using the basic assault rifle, because nothing else I discovered was better. Take for example the end game assault rifle Piranha AR-51 - although it has 15 armor piercing and 40 damage, it fires only 4 projectiles. Starting Ares AR-1 deals 30 damage and 1 armor shredding per each of 6 bullets. So if enemy has no armor, AR-1 will deal 180 damage, but AR-51 only 160. If enemy has more than 55 armor, AR-51 will do nothing to the enemy, but AR-1 will shred the armor eventually. Also, AR-51 has lower effective range.
    • Same goes for aircrafts - if something is faster, it has lower capacity (but luckily you can send two aircrafts for the mission and pick the soldiers from both). So all the hard worked research doesn't give you anything better, it gives you something different.
  • There is a lot of tactical missions. Really a lot. And no auto-resolve. If you send an aircraft to respond to an attack, by the time it arrives, you are likely to complete 3-4 other tactical missions elsewhere. So if it turns out to be too slow, you won't be able to load an earlier save. There is no time in strategic view when you are just waiting for a mission to pop up. There is always dozens of things to do.
  • Unclear and clunky stealth mechanics. Even if soldier has 100% stealth, enemies will still shoot at them. It still makes the game easier as you don't have enemies from the entire map rushing towards you, but forget about running between enemies undected.
  • Inability to reorder, rename or scrap aircrafts.
  • You cannot skip movement animations or issue move order while the previous soldier is still moving.
  • No quick save/load and although saving is quick, loading takes ages (30+ seconds).
  • Game in borderless window minimizes on focus lost.
  • There are some long scrollable lists of equipments with very limited (or none) filtering options and no search.
  • List of soldiers auto-scrolls whenever you move soldier between base and aircraft.
  • Not much customisation, neither functional (like weapon mods in UFO: Afterlight), nor cosmetic. Your soldiers will rarely wear the whole set of armor, making the soldiers look rather ugly.
  • Low replayability - no randomness in regards to map objects - all Phoenix bases and havens are always in the same places.

Lots of bugs (these are still bad things)

  • Loading a game in tactical mission is pretty bugged and doesn't save the state correctly. You won't be able to use certain abilities, claiming that they were already used this turn (even though it was actually the previous turn), enemies will do nothing on the following turn (nice exploit if mission is too difficult...). There is also another heavy exploit using neutral soldiers as once they are under Phoenix control, save-loading the game resets their AP back to 4...
  • Sniper's Marked for Death skill doesn't do what its description says and can be exploited easily. I read that it was nerfed from giving +50% multiplicative stackable bonus to +10 damage per bullet, however other than description, nothing has changed. Use it a few times on and you can kill everything (including the final boss) with one hit.
  • Passing items between multiple soldiers standing next to each other is pretty bugged. Items don't get moved despite consuming action points, another time items get cloned (yet another exploit).
  • 5 unobtainable Steam achievements.
  • Repeller Module helmet mod doesn't restore will points in contrary to what its description says. A bug that exists for over a year...
  • Sometimes equipment filters disappear.
  • Sometimes spider drones disappear after deployment next to some obstacle.
  • Sometimes there is no line-of-sight indicator even though the target can actually be hit.
  • Units are automatically put in standby when they run out of action points, even though they still have free abilities available.
  • Fog of war in Pandoran Nest often doesn't reveal correctly - be ready to navigate through the darkness.
  • A lot of statistics on the game over screen is bluntly incorrect.
  • Although you can search encyclopedia, if you do so, first few lines of the entry's description will be truncated, making the search useless.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt took me a while to figure what exactly about this game didn't catch my attention the way the rebooted X-COM games have; I think I finally figured it out. On paper, there's a lot to like here if you're a fan of X-COM. The strategic map is well, more strategic in Phoenix Point as you have to carefully decide how you are going to allocate resources to best fight the mutant menace. You also have to pick and choose which faction(s) you'll back, which you'll raid and when since after all the best way to get resources is to help a faction against their attackers, but if you piss off one faction too soon you'll lose any hope of getting resources. Your soldiers are also fairly unique, each soldier gets a random assortment of abilities they can unlock from their class and you can choose what stats you want to improve as they gain experience. Units can also eventually learn a secondary class granting them access to even more abilities. You can give soldiers a different helm, chest piece and leg armor, any weapon you have the ability to make (though they do best if they have proficiency) and more. In the tactical combat the idea of being able to see what your digital soldiers are shooting at is a great idea, although I will note it does not fix the issue of frustration when a bullet just happens to go into the one tiny spot where it could miss.

Here's where Phoenix Point falls apart though, most of the action that you can take in combat are not interesting. The abilities you can unlock for each character class are either passive buffs of minor importance or abilities you can use to do something like moving or shooting at a reduced action point cost. What's more, the vast majority of enemies you face won't have anything more interesting to do either. Most of the mutants in the game come from two "base" mutants that over the course of the game gain new mutations making dozens of different variants, but pretty much all of these variants end up with "I have a gun"; the end result is that pretty much every fight is very samey. I'm fighting fascists in power armor? Get in cover and shoot them. I'm fighting humanoid robots? Get in cover and shoot them. I'm fighting mutants? You guessed it, get in cover and shoot them. In X-COM there are different reasons to shoot different enemies at different times, forcing you to prioritize certain enemies and attack them in different ways...but by and large that doesn't apply to Phoenix Point. With the exception of a handful of late game enemies, every enemy pretty much fights the same and you in turn pretty much fight them in the exact same way too; an endless cascade of bullet casings that don't really add up to much. If there is a Phoenix Two (or whatever they might call a potential sequel) the developers need to focus on creating varied and interesting tactical battles first and doing all the other stuff second.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeriously this game......like the only attraction of this game is that it's not XCOM.

Yeah you have different mechanics like faction, realistic targeting, and body parts. But most mechanics are either super boring or super unintuitive. The AIs, unbalanced weapon, boring research, unintuitive mechanics.

Faction, super boring, the special mission faction mission feels like normal mission, the map are boring. Like you have a special mission where you assault one of the evil faction MAIN BASE and delete them off the map, and the map is super bland. You feel like just another normal mission. Remember when you assault Exalt HQ, you fight in a big building with lots of guy inside, the theme of the building fits the mission. Yeah this game just use the same reused assets.

The realistic targeting. "Hey you can see the enemy from here." Oh it turns out you can just see their legs or there is annoying barrel in the way. So sometimes the game became you inching your soldier to make sure there is nothing annoying in the way.

The body parts mechanic. It is interesting and it's how you win gun fight, like if you target the arms that is holding the shield, it would make them drop their shield. But they make some weapon really strong and the whole mechanics unintuitive. You know how most guns needs two arms to use? Yeah so if you destroyed one of their arms, now they can't use most weapon........and this makes sniper really scary. You and the enemy can target the arms and that unit is pretty much out of the game.

Sometimes the AI are good, but sometimes they're horrible. And they're really predictable, this unit is always going to do this and that. Friendly AIs most of the time are useless, they don't use cover. Also don't know why but sometimes the enemy doesn't move? like you end your turn and they would do nothing.

Weapons, most weapon I would say are balanced but some weapon are utterly broken. What do you want? Inaccurate rocket laucher that only have 2 shots and deal small amount of damage, or super accurate laser that can kill most small enemy or disable important body parts.

Research, the cooler stuff are hidden behind faction, capturing live enemy which you expect give you cool stuff, nah, here is 10% extra damage...........

The enemies are absolutely broken. Super tanky mind control unit that have insane movement, pretty hard to hit, loves to hide, and have deadly melee weapon. Sniper that can go invis when shot. OH you just killed an enemy? let's make them spawn a new enemy when they die and make them twice as tanky as the original host, give them insane movement range and deadly melee weapon.

And there is lot of mechanics that they don't really explain to you. Like the accuracy/chance to hit is not tied to the soldiers and their experience but their weapons. What Stealth, Perception, Hearing stats means. What's the different between Day time mission and Night time mission. How you have different damage types, Bleed, Poison, Acid, Virus, Daze, Shock, etc. Some of them do similar thing but they have different calculation. How Heavy weapon are hold on waist level so they have a hard time aiming something below them. Or how the shape of the cover can affect how much protection you get or block/provide line of sight.
I basically have a lot "Oh you can do that since the beginning!?" or "Wait why I can't do X from here?"

Oh also there is no feel of progression on the research side and the soldier side. Normally you have early game tier, mid game tier, and late game tier weapon and armor. Nope, you don't have any mid game stuff, and the late game stuff are locked behind faction, so most of the game you're going to use the same old weapon. So the late game doesn't feel a lot different than mid game.

TLDR; This game is pretty unique but really unpolished and unintuitive. I really want to love this game but man.........\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclosure: i have not beat the game yet and I'm currently playing 2 campaigns, one on easy and the other on normal. this review is not covering late game stuff. i have also not played this game's earlier versions where it might have been worse.

rating: 8.5/10

Despite having mostly positive rating here on Steam, i saw so many negative reviews of this game on the Steam store page, that i decided not to buy it while it was 50% in the first month it was on Steam.... and to be honest even though i paid the $60 price i do think this is a good game. I have no idea why so many people gave this game a bad review, or perhaps that Steam is showing all the negative ones.

The new AP system and ballistics in this game are like a breath of fresh air and future XCOM games need to have this same system. I hated that feeling in Xcom when you missed and 90% shot. In this game you will miss as well but at least if you are shooting a burst a portion of your shots can still hit, this system is much better. The voice acting is pretty good, i love the art stills that you see in the game, and the story is okay.

The game is built in the Unity engine i think, and although its pretty good there are some janky graphics that come up from time to time. Some debris will go flying to the moon when hit by a vehicle that is traveling at a moderate speed. Or when the nearby environment is destroyed in combat, it looks ridiculous and silly sometimes, but to be honest there were issues like that in Xcom as well i think. Some other people mentioned that the overwatch feature in this game is broken af and that your troops will shoot at the wall that enemies are passing through. i have experienced that once or twice but i think the system does work pretty well for the most part and i think that complaint was also blown out proportion.

Otherwise than that i like this game. There is a decent variety of weapons, soldier classes, etc. Missions are pretty basic (kill all enemies, save civilians) but i don't mind. My main complaint is that for some reason the base management feels kind of weak here. in Xcom when you add a new section in the base you get a significant bonus to something or you unlock new tech... here you can unlock new tech as well but the bonus feels small: my science research went from 1 to 2 and i just spent a bunch of resources building that lab.\t\t\t\t\t
