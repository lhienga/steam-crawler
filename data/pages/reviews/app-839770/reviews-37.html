\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI held off on giving this one a review for a long time because I couldn't press thumbs up and not be a liar.
I can now press that button and feel GOOD about it.

First off, it's very similar to XCOM 2 at first glance. However, this game is far closer to the ORIGINAL XCOM or "UFO" in feel than the remake or sequel. This is of course, because of the shared designer, and it is very interesting to see how the ideas evolved in a different direction than those of Firaxis, who took it in their own standard ways.

Let's get the eclectic parts out of the way first: Limited Maps, slower gameplay, and a system that is prioritizing being out of line of sight rather than being in cover. These may not be downsides to you. For me, these are selling points, as they remind me much more fully of my military experience than chest high walls. You do NOT want to be getting shot at, and this game manages to hammer that one home pretty well. Fortunately, in this game you can do things that you would never be able to pull off in XCOM2, like step out, shoot, and then step back completely out of line of sight.

However, LINE OF SIGHT is one of the major game mechanics. If the Enemy can see you, they can shoot you. Don't care how good your armor is, you're gonna take a hit. Don't get too attached to any soldier, as you can get 100-0%ed in a 2-3 character combo easily. Some people, once again will take this difficulty as a major downside, but I quite enjoy it, as it forces me to plan my moves and flanks more carefully, and pay actual attention to the game.

Finally, there's a trade off. More world map, and less hand holding, vs Xcom's linear "Choose your Own Adventure" style story. Definitely a difficulty spike for those who thought XCOM2 on Normal was hard. We're back to the good old days of being outnumbered, underarmed, and the only thing between you and alien vivisection is whether or not you remembered to pack ammo. Some people will HATE that. Others will find those charming moments as a reminder to next time "It Sure would be Nice if we had some GRENADES, wouldn't it?"

I would say this is a lovely little Divergent Evolution from the XCOM mainline. While one version evolved into the fast paced cinematic superhero experience, this one seems to have gone more for the Heroic Resistance that wins despite ACTUAL overwhelming odds.

It all comes down, In my opinion, to whether you wanna play Tony Stark or John Connor. Phoenix Point is for the John Connors.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long time player of Gollop games, going back as far as Chaos and Rebelstar, and of course X-Com and it's descendants, I was warily excited about Phoenix Point. I put off buying it until DLC4, to give the game time to polish out some of the bugs and build on the content base.

I'm giving this game a qualified recommendation. If you're a particular fan of the genre, you might well be interested in giving Phoenix Point a burl to see what it does differently. If you are a fan of XCOM (2012) specifically, you might find that PP suffers somewhat in comparison with a certain lack of polish and accessibility. If you don't like the genre, this game is probably not going to change your mind.

While it perhaps wasn't the amazing new reinvention of the genre that XCOM (2012) proved, I found Phoenix Point still generally enjoyable. The 'direct aiming' model worked better than I was expecting, and I generally found the geoscape and metagame detailed enough, even if some features seem redundant due to balancing. The inclusion of multiple different victory paths is a nice touch, and I found gives some replayability.

I enjoyed the range of missions, ability to interact with multiple, very distinct factions, and some of the tactical features such as requiring ammo. I also found the general scavenge/fight/research/expand gameplay loop generally engaging, with lots of choices and options available at any time on the strategic screen. I mostly enjoyed the geoscape, but definitely felt that it could integrate far more with the tactical component of the game. Aerial combat - added by the Festering Skies DLC - definitely feels excessively basic and extraneous, but in that sense it isn't much worse than classic X-Com or XCOM2012.

The game does suffer from very repetitive, small maps, a number of bugs (some of them game-breaking; I did have a corrupted savegame at one point requiring a restart) and some mechanics being very unbalanced in relation to others, leading to 'no brainer' choices and exploits. The gameplay does allow a number of different playstyles, but due to being unbalanced, several of them are pretty counterproductive. Explanation of some mechanics can be vague at times, and occasionally some useful information can be difficult to find in-game. The UI isn't the worst I've seen, but it is far from the best. I found dealing with inventory particularly irksome.

Time pressure is quite intense in Phoenix Point, even more so than XCOM2. It is relatively easy to end up in a soft defeat - to find yourself in a position where it's virtually impossible to win. This isn't a deal-breaker for me, and as a rule I haven't found PP too much harder than vanilla XCOM. The game DOES feel much longer than a standard game of vanilla XCOM, and you will likely do many more missions in a full game of PP.

I've only played with DLC switched on, so I cannot comment on how the game plays without DLC. Like the game itself, DLC is a mixed bag, with the aerial combat of Festering Skies being pretty basic and unimmersive. Corrupted Horizons has some interesting ideas, but again the balance is messy, which is part of the problem of having central game mechanics included in small DLCs - it becomes quite difficult to balance both the vanilla and different combinations of DLCs. Hopefully, this model will be reconsidered in any future games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm writing this review with 100 hours of XCOM 2 in mind...

The limited amount of resources, to me, is the main thing that kinda kills Phoenix Point. They give the player the ability to have multiple bases, teams, vehicles, and ships to manage however, the amount of resources that you need to maintain all of that is pretty high. Armor, weapons, and ammo, for fully equipping all of your soldiers will take a crapton of materials to produce and at times it seems there just isn't enough to go around, even after scavenging missions and raiding other factions, which both get kind of old having to do them over and over again anyway.

I gave up my current playthrough with only two fully equipped teams and three ships because my resources were already starting to run dry and I just didn't feel like grinding out any missions doing solely resource gathering.

Difficulty wise, it's pretty comparable to xcom 2. Like xcom, your soldiers are still your most valuable resource. If even one of them dies it could cripple your progress forcing you to start a completely new game depending on how far you've gotten in your current playthrough.

Overall it's not a bad game, you get alot more tactical options in Phoenix Point than you do in xcom which is what initially drew me to this game. I like this game alot but I'm not in love with it yet, just think it needs a little more polish. Would definitely recommend though.

-Lovecraft/eldritch horrors inspired ennemies
-a gigantic pool of weapons,combat abilities,class perks and team customization
-challenging turn based strategic combat
-Superb artwork and graphic choices,dark and futuristic
-Amazing lore
-canpaign replayability is enormous from rng based events,weapon drops,faction alignement choices and combat teams choices
-Constant lack of ressources forces you to make difficult choices
-great atmospheric/ambient soundtrack and sound design in general is amazing
-free aim mechanic and body part injuries is great and innovative
-Campaign is suprisingly long when you don't know the game yet and don't know what to prioritise
-You can easily loose a campaign you have put 40 hours in and have to start over due to lack of ressources,team wipes and ennemy progression on the map becoming impossible to counter quick enough gives a crazy edge to masochist player like myself (and you perhaps)

Negatives
-Bugs,mostly save related,will sometimes force you to uninstal/reinstal or lose acces to your campaign (make backup saves,like A LOT of them,manually save constantly)
-extremely repetitive maps,to the point of rendering the exellent combat boring from seeing the same maps layout over and over again
-Some campaign mechanics are tiedious and outright infuriating,such as taking out ennemie lairs and citadels wich you can't ignore otherwise you loose faction reputation points,theses missions are long and always the same
-Game punishes you hard if you choose to raid/steal from factions,locking out reasearchs and factions related bonuses VERY quickly
-Mid to late game will start feeling like a grind due to the repetitive maps layout,similar missions and mandatory enemy base destruction
-due to the complex mechanics and the gigantic pool of options available to you,you are almost garantee to fail your first campaign,maybe a couple of them
-campaign is suprisingly long,wich i put as a positive but due to the grind like feeling of certain part of the campaign,can be a deal breaker
-Just like xcom 2 there is a loosing timer,this time its a earth popultation countdown,unlike the avatar project in xcom,you can't reverse your lost percentages,only slightly slow down the process,meaning in the mid to late game you will have to hurry finishing the game (although rather forgiving and slow,this countdown accelerate at some point in the game)
-You can easily lose a campaign you have put 40+ hours in and have to start over due to lack of ressources,team wipes and ennemy progression on the map becoming impossible to counter quick enough wich a lot of player will find unacceptable and i understand completely

I recommend the game overall and really got caught in the story,artwork and combat,just know that it catters to a very specific audience
