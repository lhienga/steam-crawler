\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played XCOM: EW and finished an Impossible run and I have to say that the tactical and strategical gameplay is worlds better than in XCOM:
-You find it annoying that your Heavy in XCOM can only use a light MG? Here, you can give him a Handgun, but unless he has a skill giving him proficiency, he is just not as skilled as your sniper with it. So this game has a lot of costumisation and you can give your units full individual gear. I usually multiclass my assults into snipers a give them a sniper rifle and a shotgut. Deadly on a distance and up close.
- You hate that taking a shot usually ends your turn in XCOM. Well here you have the option of taking one or two shots in a turn and still run away if you have enought action points. You can also split action points while moving for more dynamic gameplay. And have I talked about free aim?
- You hate that the fight in XCOM is not really a global fight and the amout of Units you can use in a fight is just random. Well here you can and will have multiple aircrafts with each aircraft having different flying speeds, durability and space for soldier allowing you to costumize your battle plan and have multiple troops enter the same battle.
- You found the factions in XCOM 2 really underwhelming. Well here they are distinct and actually interact with each other. Pheonix Point is not the center of all interaction, but still a powerfull player able to work with one faction or attack it.
- More on a technical level. You can report bugs ingame and thus help the developer fix them, even with the ability of uploading the savegame.
- You hate that you hit the Thin Man in XCOM twice but he is still as precise at sniping your soldiers as if you havent done anything? Well here you can cripple his arm or outright destroy his weapon rendering him mostly harmless.
- You hate that your soldiers can miss shots up close even when its super unlikely. Well here your soldiers aren't morons and always aim in the direction of the target. The Weapon sprays In a circle making unrealistic misses a thing of the past. Strey bullets also interact with the environment, beware Friendly Fire!
- Your soldiers die too quickly inXCOM. Well here they can take at least a bit of a beating, but still die more predictably.
All In all, this game fixes a lot of my issuse I have with XCOM making it a realy enjoyable game to play.
Is it perfect? No, assult weapons are not strong enough and some abilities are strictly stronger than others. But I still recommend the game especially since they are still improving it on a regular basis.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game and find it very fun however it's also very repetitive. If you do not enjoy turn based combat games this is not for you. I love how diverse the roles each squaddie can take based on a few skills or their loadout. This game is not without flaws but it is also very fun. You are constantly resource starved as you strive to outfit your squad(s) to protect an ever growing sphere of influence before the enemies wipe out humanity.

There are definitely some balance issues for instance the opportunity cost / time it takes to field more aircraft to the ease with which you can just steal one from your potential allies for an incredibly minor drop in reputation. The good and novel interactions outweigh the bad though.

With all of that being said, the game is worth taking a look at in my opinion for any strategy game fans.\t\t\t\t\t
