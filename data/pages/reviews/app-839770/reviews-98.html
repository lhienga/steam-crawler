\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI came to this game from Xcom 1 and 2 wanting more of the same, but essentially there's just so much more to deal with that make this game more a pain.

Some things that annoyed me:
-You must manage inventory space of each trooper. (9 slots)- not so bad unless you're on a mission where you need to raid and horde items.

-You must manage ammo for each trooper. If they don't have ammo, you must produce them or they're useless. If you have low supplies, you're SoL and must postpone and trade for supplies. Not great if your mission is time sensitive.

-You can't just go down the research tree like you could with Xcom. At some point in time you'll hit a dead end and need to steal research from another group or ally. (While each of the other three factions is killing each other and whittling down the total human population) your weapons and ammo are still the same sets you started with.

-Free aim, with the ability to target body parts sounds incredibly interesting. What it really means is that you're shooting with the accuracy of a spit ball across a football field unless you're really close. Why not get closer? Because enemies will punish you if you let them get close with double or triple attacks, knocking your guys to 20% health unless you waste an action to heal them. You might get a chance to shoot if you didn't move. But guess who else is there? The monster that double/tripper attacked you in the first place.

-When another settlement is under attack, they don't tell you until the settlement is at least 50% dead, which means unless you have a local base on that continent, you're probably screwed.

All of these make missions an incredible struggle, which honestly makes things more difficult than they are fun.
