\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a very weird attempt to succeed XCOM. It has really cool ideas for combat that are great fun, as far as I am concerned. I like the manual targeting and the evolution of the enemy in more ways than just "new colour = stronger" or something similar. But that's where Phoenix Point stops being a good game, as far as I am concerned. Some of the things that irk me:

- The UI design as atrocious. It feels like the entire strategic interface, the inventory management and the base management was entirely an afterthought. I think 90% of the budget went into combat, the rest...not sure.
- there are nearly TWENTY bases you can/should manage. Lots of clicks for very little reward.
- Movement in combat: Most maps are very small. Which is good, because quite a few of your units can't more further than THREE TILES per action point before they can no longer shoot.
- Cover doesn't seem to work reliably. I've had my guys in full cover get hit A LOT. Breaking line of sight entirely appears to be the only sure way to not get one-shot in some (way too many) circumstances
- resource management: Not sure why there are two material resources (blue "tech", orange material). You need both for nearly everything, could have been condensed. Same with all the other additional resources, most of which seemed to be added for sake of complexity, not gameplay. It just feels tacked on.
- WHY DO I NEED AMMO PACK MANAGEMENT AS WELL?! It CLUTTERS the inventory. And so many armor parts!
- Equipment progression: I feel like there is very little feeling of progress with equipment. I have only played for around 15 hours before losing all interest, had 2 of 3 factions allied and as far as I know went relatively far in the campaign. Yet there seem to be only 3 levels of equipment: Basic stuff, slightly better basic stuff from the factions, and endgame weapons that appear LATE. Maybe I did something wrong, but I never felt any proper improvement.

EDIT because I just remembered and got instantly angry again: THE AERIAL COMBAT! Whoever allowed that abomination is hopefully no longer working in game design. Just wow.

I haven't checked, but I believe the devs ran out of money. I can't find any other reason to explain why some parts of the game look and feel so CHEAP and without any proper design philosophy.

Maybe the interface was meant to be played on tablets. With just thumbs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was quite hyped for this game, but turned out to be a real disappointment:


-Gameplay is clunky, plays out more like some kind of "simulation" than as a game per se.

-Tactical aspect is very lacking, at least at the beginning. Cover appears to have little importance, as you are just shooting the brains out of creatures that are coming towards you in a very linear manner (I don't mean geometrically, but tactically/strategically). One of the marketing slogans for the game was about the "freedom" it adds to the genre with its continuous rather than discrete action system, but the lack of meaningful level design (cover importance, etc) makes it almost trivial. You don't have to make difficult-but-meaningful decisions as often as in a game like XCOM 2. Also, skills for the different classes are boring. You don't get that "OP but also fair tool 'cause we are figthing pretty OP enemies" adrenaline rush XCOM 2 nailed so well.

-Interface is not clear at all, nor streamlined, nor comfortable.

-Artistically ugly. I donÂ´t find a better word for putting it. And this ugliness is not just in the GUI/menus. The music is boring. The writing is so so lacking. All this contributes to the "plain-unfun-simulation" feeling.

-The strategic layer seemed interesting at first, with so many points of interes to explore; but it gets repetitive very quick.

-More about the writing: such a bad plot. The premise looks like it was taken from a brainstorm made in a military barrack. "We military men always knew our budget should have been bigger, and now a pandemic has proven us right. We will save the world because that's what military men do, saving everybody's a***s without any political context whatsoever" (the factions mechanic does NOT manage to add a narrative weight that could make it look as something more than just a game mechanic).


5/10

(XCOM 2 WotC is a much much much better turn-based tactical game imo, in every aspect I mentioned).\t\t\t\t\t

Simply - the game is great.

Reading all of these reviews here I was a little bit confused, but then I have to admit I was exceptionally well impressed with Phoenix Point.

The only thing I'm missing now is just like more vehicles, few more tanks or somethin like this.
I would buy such game expansion if it was released.

I will gonna buy the Kaos Engines for sure.

Why I say no to Festering Skies - cause the game is very deep already and adding additional layer of managing air fights would be to overloading.

I also consider the vehicles have too much armor - scarab is almost immortal ( maybe I'm on the too early stage of the game, but this is my impression ). ( edit. yes - that's the early stage of the game )

The only thing that irritates me - personel UI is a little too raw, there should be a base switch, to see only soldiers awailable in such base, otherwise it becomes messy, when you have a lot soldiers and aircrafts - that's my worst pain in the game.

But the combat, all of these evolving creatures, fatcions. This is amazing.

The only problem I see is that the game gets too long and too difficult over time, at some point it becomes an ordeal, and catching Scylla alive is bordering on a miracle. There should be a maximum of 200 points of paralysis for Scylla, not 350. The constant repetition of missions in the final stage of the game is very tiring. These are the same problems that Terror from the Deep had (too difficult, too long).

Anyway, Mr Gallop, You and Your team has done an excellent work on this production!

8/10!\t\t\t\t\t
