\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: This game needs several mods to be even borderline playable. And even then, I really just wanted to go back and play Xcom 2 with the Long War 2 mod.

Game starts out fine, but ends up getting worse and worse as you progress.

Unlike Xcom, soldiers don't really progress stat wise as they level, and plateau hard. Their power is almost entirely gated by equipment and/or augmentations, and even then, you will hit the performance cap relatively quickly as there aren't really any god tier capstone abilities when hitting the level cap. Hell, their accuracy doesn't even improve! Meanwhile, the enemy is constantly researching better tech that can take out troops (by surprise) in a single turn.

Classes are oversimplified and lack a lot of major unique abilities, and each soldier you recruit gets 3 random passives that may either be really strong for their class, or outright useless. (Needs to be modded.)

Enemies will spawn out of LOS almost randomly around the outside of the map during most missions, and may even be completely invisible unless you rush them thanks to humans having ridiculously low perception, even in sniper gear. Some enemies can call in reinforcements once every other turn. This is only compounded further by almost all enemies having nightvision, and therefore can see twice as far as you can at night. Apparently, nightvision is not built in to hyper advanced suits of armor??? (Really needs to be a mod for built in nightvision.)

Vehicles cannot be reloaded in combat, so they are straight up useless after a few turns. Oh, and they take up 3 soldier's worth of space on dropships. (Needs to be modded.)

Advanced soldier customization like mechanical and biological augmentations are only slightly better (or outright worse at times) than just having a suit of armor, which is incredibly disappointing as they cost a lot of resources. You are also only able to modify 2 out of 3 body components (head/torso/legs) for some reason? Meanwhile, exoskeletons from Xcom2 let you mount advanced weapons on top of a heavy mobility/defense boost. (Needs to be modded.)

Bugs:
Here we are, years after release and there are still major gamebreaking bugs. Some items like backback mounted weapons and some abilities like "Dash" and "Overwatch" can bug out if you use them and reload the save. This bug prevents you from using them for the next turn.

Enemies and allies also regularly don't get a turn for some reason.

DLC:
It's honestly kind of pathetic that I felt the DLCs subtracted from/did not add anything to the game. Festering Skies sucks (seriously, don't enable that DLC), Legacy of the Ancients had incredibly repetitive missions all with the same reskinned and tedious enemies, Kaos Engines may as well not even be a DLC with how little impact it has on your gameplay thanks to how expensive its items are, the new "Muton" soldiers from Corrupted Horizons are only slightly better than base soldiers in specific areas, and straight up WORSE in others (Similar to the advanced customization options mentioned earlier) Corrupted Horizons also adds one of the most infuriating enemies that will show up in every single mission and call reinforcements until you kill it. Since it can cross half the map in a single turn, sometimes you can't deal with them at all.

My advice:
Avoid unless on sale, and browse the mods on NexusMods before you start your playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs I am writing this, I am trying to get a feel for what I would say...
I didn't think I had so much to complain about, but once I started with one thing, it lead to another, and another, and another...
I genuinly thought I was in the 50/50 weather to recommend it or not, but the more I think about game what it does good, what it does bad... I feel it does more bad than good.

So I think the short answer for now is... No, I would not recommend you buy this.

If you want to go into why... join me in the wall of text that follows.
---------------------------------------
Wasted time and directions:

A general theme for this text will be, the amount of time they have had to improve the game and... and feel like they did the bare minimum with it, and how it almost feels like all they did for the game was add some DLC which... more or less are complete washes...
After all of the time they had before the game was released, with the backing that they got, and then sidestepping their promises to their backers by accepting an Epic exclusivity deal for a year to get an even better cash-out, and then an additional 2 years later, they have sort of made a complete game... but it is still barebones when it comes to QoL fixes wich would have made it a better game.
And this game needs it's QoL fixes.
(Now, I wanna be clear, while I was one of the people dissapointed with the Epic exclusivity, I tried to not take it to hard. In my eyes, it was just 1 year more till the game released which would give it time to get bugs fixed and general improvment... Jokes on us I guess...)

Unless you have a high tolerance for a game that does not respect your time - and I do not mean disrespect as in your entire squad got killed and you lost hours or days on a campaign, because that is Xcom - no I mean in sheer micromanagment of the systems that are not thought out correctly, or have been improved upon with how long this game was in the making, and with how long it has been since the game has been released.
That said, there are elements that makes the game compeling, but to the point I do not wish to play it because it is so tedious in all the other areas...

My hope would be that there would be a Phoenix Point 2 where they would improve on a lot of these areas, but since they didn't bother to do so to until this point. I wonder if a sequal would improve...

For example, as of this review, they not to long ago just announced that they are only now - NOW THREEE DAMN YEARS LATER - and only now are they working on making mod support for the game...
(I know I am harping the time since this game released, but that is a lot of damn time for a game to have had to improve itself and not done that.)
Just... wow... most people have played and forgotten this game - and you expect that people will only now want to get in on the action and mod it? *facepalm*
As is, there are only a few mods that have been kept up to date over the years, some more seems to work despite not being updated. And then there are some that would have been lovely if they did work despite not being updated but do not.
Like so many things with this game, things that should have been ironed out as the game came out, or at the very least within the first year of its release, then garnering a healthy mod community should have been on the top of your priority.

I do HOPE that giving this game the proper workshop support will cause a resurgance dedicated fans that release that have enough modders fix more of the areas that is wrong with this game.
Sadly, I do not think this game has enough for that, judging by the not so impresive presence this game has on the modding scene as it is.
-------------
Design decisions:

On one hand, it does away with one of the most infuriating aspect of an Xcom game - point blank chances to miss are ALMOST eliminated with its intuative reticle-system for aiming.
Which is funny, because it is still a decent amout of "chance" based, depending on the size of the reticle/accuracy your soldier have. But it atleast make you feel more in control - it gives you a much better visual representation as to how you could miss or hit something.
So maybe why it feels so good is more of an illusion of control?
Nevertheless - the reticle system is absolutely the best design decision this game could come up with.

On the other hand... EVERY OTHER SYSTEM really feels like a mess, with oversights and wierd design decisions. And I think there is almost atleast one "back breaking" visual glitch on atleast one of my soldiers EVERY SINGLE DAMN FIGHT!
Again... 3 years and not fixed.

For a game that is really trying to encurage you to have atleast 2 teams ready to patrol at any time - and more as you get later and want to cover more ground - it does an absolute piss poor job of minimizing clicks needed to do what you want to do!
And having more than 1 airship to navigate the globe, just ends up being a PAIN as you constantly tell one ship to move, and because you asked it to move, it will activate the globe progression... never mind that you have another ship sitting and doing nothing while the clock starts ticking because you asked one thing to move...
Thankfully there is a mod that tries to fix this issue the best it can. And it is a god send... I would have stopped playing this game way before if this little gem of a mod didn't exist!

FURTHERMORE
Equiping more than 1 team is nothing other than a chore you tell your parents you forgot about because you didn't want to do it... well here, it is a giant barrier for trying to have fun in the game!
Save loadout! 3 years, and still no way to set up loadout saves so you can more easily set up atleast some core eqipment sets you want to field.
Now, ofcourse, there is also the suspension of disbelief that you can remove the gear of your team on one side of the planet, and equip it on the other side of the planet... If anything, I wish the game was made so this was not a thing you could do - but the balancing and price of making your equipment makes it really hard to build enough gear to properly equip multiple squads of soldiers without having to juggle their inventory around.

Speaking of inventory... THE INVENTORY MANAGMENT IS HORRENDOUS!!!
Holy guaccamole the inventory system sucks!
First of, it still bugs out where it does not want to properly update whenever you try to select which class you want to filter out. 3 years people!
Never mind that filtering by class is THE DUMBEST way to sort out at! It still leaves you with an absolute crap ton of items to filter through. Not to mention the screen leaves a lot of real-estate that it could use to help filter out the items better.

Some DLC's does not meld well with the game in my opinion.
Addon like the Festering skies is thematicly fitting and interesting, but it feels like what it adds is almost just some variations of what is already there, it feels somewhat low effort.
Except for the flight combat... with is the dullest, most boring, most unintuative flight combat I have ever seen!!! Holy hell it is SO BORING AND BLAND!!!!! Like, you could just have done the original Xcom flight combat, and that would have been 10 times better. Sure, many people would have called that lazy, but it would at the very least be more fun and interesting than whatever this is... and Original Xcom was made 28 years ago... and their air combat was better.
And sure, it wants to add this one custom made mission to kick start the ordeal, but it just ends up making the thing feel like it doesn't belong more than naturally intergrate in your campaign.


That will have to do as there is no more space to write.
Again, at first I was having a hard time recommending it, but after thinking about it, I would clearly not recommend it.
You can not make a game on ONE good change alone... Which this game tried to do.\t\t\t\t\t
