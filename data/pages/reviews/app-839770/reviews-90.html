\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhoenix Point at first glance does not seem to have recognizable similarities to XCOM, you have to give it a chance. The story line is based on the fall of XCOM, the reactivation of XCOM Bases and man kinds struggle to survive. I do like the game but liked it better when you could train operatives in skills you needed, not random selection as it is. Training adds points to a pool that allows activation of a skill as well as character development. I also liked being able to select a starting Country, game randomly selects it now. I believe that many modern games rely to much on random generation, lazy I guess. Random generation takes away from the story line and is a disappointing distraction. Example, you would think you would find books in a library but a random generator will put ammo there instead, sloppy. Sorry about that, Phoenix Point incorporates elements of the XCOM series but they are bundled so options are limited but it is still a great game but not as good as what XCOM fans are accustomed to, needs more story line and focus.\t\t\t\t\t
