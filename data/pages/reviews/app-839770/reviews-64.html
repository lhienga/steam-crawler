\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who is obsessed with ufo-like games and having completed all games I could find, I find myself in the position to review phoenix point.
Pros:
+ Copied fallout's targeting system, which works surprisingly well. it gives single shot weapons the edge over armor, and assault/heavy weapons and edge over light armored enemies
+ Copied Ufo: Afterlight's faction system. which works well with the ability to share faction research and specialized areas for each faction
+ Nice distribution of different enemies, from small suicidal worms, to building sized queen
+ Challenging resource scarcity
+ Gradual increase in difficulty with evolution of enemies

Cons
- Resource scarcity leads to inability to hire soldiers, equip them let alone build aircraft to carry them. You will have to accept the fact you will not be able to do all missions
- If you loose a single soldier, it will have significant affect on your resources to replace them. which forces you to play very defensively (or save/load a LOT)
- Due to random placement of story/side/diplo missions, you can for a VERY long time, can't progress those because you just can't reach with any of your planes there. stupid, right? but it happens.
- weapons/equipment you research have very marginal difference. you could finish the game with beginner equipment, it makes the whole "Research" somewhat useless, especially faction tech. just an example beginner sniper rifle deals 110 damage, the best sniper outside ancient tech (which is not worth it) deals 130 damage + 50 piercing.
- Whole ancient tech is broken. by the time i started finding excavation sites, i was already almost finished with the game. my first excavation mission never finished (outstanding bug about ancients spawning under floor) which killed the buzz immediately for me. which was also pretty waste of time to do all the missions and researches which in the end were not used at all, because you need special material which you can't get due to buggy maps.
- frequency of havens and bases to protect increases when you explore more regions of the world. but you can't build airplanes and teams with the same frequency. the game makes sure of that.
- doing a mission has a cost, this cost increases if you don't have a superior team, which you can't have due to above reasons. so most of the game you are broke
- there is no income. no seriously, you have to WAIT for enemy to attack your allies so you can protect them be the hero and beg for some rewards, because apparently building factories or labs were never possible. you find a way to harvest mutant organs for food though. of course you wouldn't do mundane stuff like farms...

Other bugs:
- when you load previous turn, most of the time, enemies don't play and you play 2 turns one after the other. this happens almost always. although i didn't want to abuse it whenever i load, i can't stop this bug from happening.
- when you click fire, 30% or so of the time it double clicks which results in shooting the first target in torso, which sucks, because usually that's not what you want to hit.

Overall, the game gets frustrating with no sense of improvements with new equipment or research and the lack of resources to even use them on mass scale and open bugs which are very frequent. but it is worth a try if you're into ufo-like games like me.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best word I can use to describe this game is: Tedious.

Remember that Legendary Ironman X-Com walkthrough where losing was fun and every choice was difficult yet fair? This game isn't it.

Phoenix Point isn't hard like X-Com where you make difficult choices between two options with both their benefits and drawbacks.
It's not versatile like Battletech where you can decide how you want to play the game.
It's not varied like Divinity where you have a lot of valid options and can make your own combos.
It's not a strategy like Chess where your skill determines the outcome and a loss is your fault.

Phoenix Point is more like Wheel of Fortune ... from hell.

Phoenix Point has poorly explained mechanics and UI that let you stumble between options.
You'll slowly understand the choices you made dozens of hours ago were suboptimal and your game is in a bottleneck because you just shouldn't have played a certain way but couldn't have guessed it.
It's having to grind through dozens and dozens of meaningless missions where you alternate between 2 enemy types on repeat and no better objective than "kill everything".
It's saving up on resources but lose them all on a technicality.
It's micromanaging equipment and finding loopholes to abuse the poor interface.
It's luck where you did everything to improve your chances but fail anyways, and failure is so costly save scumming becomes normal.
You don't win and adapt; you just follow an instruction manual and roll dice.

The game feels like the best way to win is by cheesing the AI before it cheeses you and abusing poorly balanced mechanics. A mistake (read: a choice which seemed valid) often means the end of the game completely. "Learning from your mistakes" is not an option here.

The guides and walkthrough out there (other than the fact there's just too much material to cover) all focus on finding cheesy loops and abusing the AI, because those are the only way you can survive.
What equipment to ignore completely (most of it) and in what order you can do things since any other option is bad / you will die.

It may seem like it's a huge game with a lot of replay value while it's actually straightforward and the alternatives are simply defeat. It's not hard, it's work, a lot of it.

Normal gameplay is frustrating and what could have been a good game for some concepts and story becomes infuriating because of the mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t

Super hard but the difficulty must NOT deter you! Its part of what makes it so much fun.

You do need to learn a solid strategy to survive and have to keep up to date with research, its definately doable and worth it to play. I had to restart many times but each restart i knew a little more and lasted a little longer. The challenge keeps me playing and thinking about strategy improvements even long after Ive shut my pc down! This kind of game fuels my passion to play games, it makes me think so hard and feel so rewarded when I get further. Technical aspects are all very well executed theres no weird out of place mechanics that are justthere to make it seem like progress. Turned based strategy must be exactly like it is in Phoneix Point! Graphics are very cool, maps are lively and enriched!

Games often get boring when you get overleveled but not this one, the challege is real and worth every second!

If you thinking about buying this DO IT!!! Stick through the tough learning curve you will emerge a master!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t2/5 - not worth it unless on super sale

I played this game enough to get my money's worth but it isn't very good. It's interesting but not very good. It asks for a lot of micromanagement and throws a ton of problems at the player at all times. Some faction always need help or you're starved for resources. It works on a story level because you are earth's poorly funded best hope but maybe you should actually get help when you're allied with a faction.

The most interesting part of the game is it's combat. Every bullet having it's own travel path is neat and at least feels better than a simple percentile to determine your shot. The issue is that your units cannot aim down and consider seeing the left eyelash of an enemy as having line of sight. Much like the rest of the game, there's something interesting here but it's flawed and really needs to be refined.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was excited to know the original creator of xcom was making a new strategy game. I have been playing strategy games for over 25 years. I have played all xcom games from the original on. I am use to playing games that make you pay dearly for making a strategic mistake. I went into phoenix point knowing this. The story is interesting and the global scale events keep you involved from the politics to the random discoveries. The graphics are enjoyable and you will get many great and very very frustrating hours of play out of this game.
Now for the bad news... As a true veteran of strategy games, I find this game to be more than a little unbalanced tactically speaking. I am playing on veteran level (normal level for most games). The lack of supplies to build new bases and upgrade your equipment only gets worse the longer you play. Even when trading regularly with other cities. You really can't let the other factions get mad at you and raid them often because you need their research and the ability to recruit troops from them. The battles start off easy enough but become pitched in the enemies favor very quickly and doesn't relent. Every battle you fight cost you supplies and the amount you receive from victories isn't enough to keep restocking and building up and reserves. The enemies upgrade at a quicker rate than your troops can because of the above. Even when you max out your troops exp and weapon upgrades, the battles are still uphill and most late enemies are so hard to penetrate their armor its almost pointless to enter into battle with them because you spend more in supplies than its worth. This game lost alot of the fun when you realize that you can't afford to lose a single troop because it sets you back so much. You will have to save scum this game if you want to stand a chance. I highly recommend playing on easy level or not at all. Only true die hard strategy players will find joy in this clearly masochistic pursuit. Most will just find frustration. I like the game but to say its a little unbalanced would be kind. I like the game but not sure I would tell others to play it,\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall the game is above average, thus a decent tactical strategy game.
Gameplay, gfx, sfx are above average, something one would actualy expect from former xcom people. The story is ok.
But why not recommend? Well, its because of the frustration moments this game generates.
1) you can not realy "slowly" learn the game, even at rookie level. you just cant take your time and explore this and that and look how this or that works. there is a virtual time limit and when you do not win by that time point, its game over, and it is much much much worse then xcom. such limits to make a player loose are the worst design idea ever and firaxis learnd from it in xcom 2 where you can counter the timelimit. here, nothing. and its even worse, there are few havens with a few thousand inhabitans each, and 100 of them die in every heaven due to starvation per day. Because that wouldnt cause any trouble, The civilians are so disciplined, that they die by the thousand due to incompetence of the leadership and they still worship and adore them. Ok. So playing rookie in the "geoscape" means nothing. One could think it makes the world a bit easier, less starvation, shorter exploration times etc pp. Nothing, the difficulty level only affects the tactical missions and there... well, just dont bother with rookie, it is as if Ai is completely disabled. Your hit chances are not better. The chances of getting status effects is not lowered compared to the higher difficulties.
2) no quicksave, no quickload ... ey sorry devs, where do we live here, 1980?
3) when I press ESC I expect the menu to come up, either to quit the game or load or whatever. Not in this game, when its enemy turn, you have to wait, sometimes up to 15min. Also something designed by a 3year old. I would be damned if I knew why that was designed that way, as if it would make the game more challenging and not just more frustrating and annoying. Usualy it is faster to kill the task to quit the game or just restart it to load a game.

So final words: if you are addicted to this type of game like me, get the game and play it, will be enjoyable enough, but be warned you will also curse a lot (or not). And if you are just looking for a game, skip this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like XCom style tactical combat games, you will enjoy Phoenix Point. Probably.

The good
*Fantastic map, weapon, and enemy design
*Great lore, from the main plots and factions through to the incidental details/encounters in the world
*Lots of improvements on the XCom model - equipment, actions, etc. Vehicles are cool, but almost never better than just having more soldiers.
*Real depth and breadth.

The bad
*A lot of things are nonintuitive. Understanding how to play the Geoscape is really opaque - be prepared to watch lots of YT videos to learn it.
*It's hard. Very, very hard. You will lose a *lot*. I don't mind a challenge, of course, but I am a pretty seasoned gamer, felt I was pretty good at XCom/EU/2 and this is a step beyond. I restarted on Rookie mode (lowest difficulty) and even that is hard as hell. Just be prepared for that - if you want chill casual gaming, if you like winning, this is not that. I find myself playing a bit, making some progress, getting frustrated and giving up, then going back to it later.
*I'm not sure the Festering Skies DLC is actually fun. Aerial combat feels rushed/tacked on, but is also hard as hell and so feels a brutal drain rather than adding fun. I'm mostly rushing trying to kill the Behemoth at the moment just so I don't have to deal with it any more.\t\t\t\t\t
