As Good as XCOM2, if Not Better

I have absolutely no idea why this game has 72% positive reviews on Steam. It may get the first place on my "most unfairly rated games" list (and there are quite a few in there).


You'll find so much bullsh*t on negative reviews, like:

- It's buggy. Except it's not. The game got stuck, for me, during the enemy's turn twice, forcing me to Alt+F4. After 80 hours playing, that was it. Nothing else.

- It's unbalanced/unfair. Not really. If you force yourself to play ironman-style, on Hero or Legendary difficulty, then OK, you're going to suffer. But playing normally, with the eventual save-scum (which is not good, but XCOM also required it, as do many other games), it's fine. The game's economy does not make things easy for you and, at a certain point during mid-game, things do get tough. But that only makes the game challenging, which is a good part of the fun. If it were too easy, it would suck, hard.

- The AI takes too long to think. It doesn't. The enemy's turn does take a while, but you can speed up combat animations to the point where it's really not an issue. The "thinking" itself is pretty fast.

- Combat is sluggish. Huh? It's as sluggish (or not) as any other turn-based game. It feels exactly like XCOM's. It's how any turn-based combat fan would expect.


Let me just make things simple for you: if you like XCOM, you'll love this game.

Phoenix Point is essentially the same thing (and made by the guy[s] who originally created XCOM) but with more freedom.

In this game, you can have several bases, several aircrafts, several parties, you can ingratiate yourself to 1, 2, all the 3 factions in the game or none of them, you can trade for resources, collect items dropped by enemies during missions, wear armor with augments on your characters, mutate their limbs or make them cybernetic, characters can be dual-class, you have a "global pool" of skillpoints to use on those you didn't take to as many missions as others, building your characters requires some thinking, you have to spend your resources carefully, the game's story is actually interesting, etc., etc., etc.

I played this for the first time on Epic, some 3 years ago. I had a blast with it but, by then, it was somewhat buggy. I got it on Steam because I felt like playing it again (now with the [minor] DLCs included) and also to write a review to balance a little the ridiculous injustice that has been done to this game, here.


In short:
Just completely ignore the negative reviews. Then see how you feel about this game, and trust yourself.

If you think you'll like it, you will.

This is one of the only two games I ever bought twice, and I'm glad I did so.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a great game here somewhere, and I really hope they make a sequel to iron out some of the issues that are plaguing this game. It is insanely buggy first and foremost, not in the way of hard crashes but visual and lesser game play bugs happen every 30 minutes when you play. Secondly it just feels so incredibly unfair, which the other XCOM games never did, enemies which I have a estimated 5-10% chance to hit will hit me back every single time, which makes the rules of the world hard to read as seemingly they play by a different ruleset. The difficulity spikes are also so whiplash inducing; where one mission is a cake walk and the next it feels like your squad need new weapons, armor and 3 levels to even have a chance. Like XCOM there's a big overworld map which you need to traverse, the problems I found was that your ship is incredibly slow, and unless you have multiple ships out you wont have time to react to havens under attack (which is also where you get most of your resources). So if you, like me, wanted to focus on the A-squad on a single ship. You are just ping-ponged between havens that are under attack to get those precious resources since you likely have 0 at this point. The crux is that you'll never make it, and all the while the enemies just get stronger and stronger for each trip you do. Maybe I played it wrong, but I think that is part of the problem as well, why can't I play it "my" way? The fact that the road to victory is so thin makes it, for me, an incredibly unenjoyable experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDid not want to play the game initially, because of the Epic debacle. But. picked it up recently, now have a hundred or so hours on record.

Late mover advantage: it's an excellent game at this point. There are still the occasional buglets; no more than XCOM had. Characterization makes XCOM special (this is what is disappointing with Chimera Squad; it took it away) - and for those wanting top hats & monocles there will be disappointment.

However; it does make up for it in terms of strategic depth. It feels like a bigger, free-er game; and what it lacks in characterization of individual squad members, it makes up for in squad dynamics.

The cinematic quality that marks out the main XCOM titles is - not quite there. The landscapes are still beautifully rendered, and destructible; there are more squad members; more action points; and a broader tech tree. And there is plenty of strategic depth.

So: give it a try. It will be years till the next XCOM release, thanks to the crappy new Marvel game FIRAXIS are working on, anyway.
