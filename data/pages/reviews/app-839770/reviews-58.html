\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis one is so in between a YES and a NO on recommendation. These critiques are in comparison to XCOM 2 and its DLC's.

Tactically the game is actually a lot of fun. The aiming and movement mechanics are much improved over XCOM. MOST of the time if you miss its more or less your fault for taking a risky shot (Sometimes the environment does glitch into the way, which is frustrating but very rarely). You aren't going to miss your point blank 99% percent shots. You really can set up cross fires and the way the game handles movement really is superior, no more walking into enemy squads because of a misclick. It really respects the player there.

There are really a lot of different ways to play as well, a bunch of different classes, a bunch of different items to mess with. I like that a lot, and am always excited for the Next Big Thing....which is actually the problem.

The game is such a grind you'll likely not get a chance to experience all of that cool stuff. Granted, a lot of the weapons and armors are side grades, they do give you a lot of specialization in how you want to play. You just won't get a chance to do it all. I hadn't lost a single mission and had done every exploration thing, trade, and even raid available and I STILL wasn't able to properly field a full squad. The resources just aren't there and there isn't really a way to get more in a reliable way. This isn't an issue with the games difficulty so much as it is with the long grindy method by which they piece out progress. I find myself just looking around the map for SOMETHING to do while I watch the enemy counter just tick up up up.

Which is the next big criticism. The combat is mostly fun, there is just too much of it. I get it, the bad guys are coming, but endless respawning baddies with massive hit point sinks isn't really fun. I feel less like I'm outwitting a smart enemy as I am grinding down an RPG boss with auto attacks and hoping I don't get an unlucky hit or some such. Its to the point where I dread the fights because i know its going to be like an hour just to clear the area, and thats with hitting every shot and finding every enemy.

Its just too much that it slows the game down to this just massive grind. The game itself is fun, but it gets caught up in itself too much to be a really exciting and reactive experience. If they cut down the amount of enemies a bit, rewarded players a bit more for successful actions (don't worry it punishes bad ones readily enough, which is GOOD), and allowed for a more dynamic resource gathering experience, I think you'd have a great game here.

As it is, its fun, but just too much of a grind to really recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPHOENIX POINT IS BETTER

they said. Are people playing Phoenix Point, and thinks it's better than XCOM, high on cocaine? Or they just crazy?

I mean seriously, I grabbed PP since it's dirt cheap, and immediately you can tell how inferior the game is compared to XCOM.


Sure on PP you got free aim, four movement points, vehicles, fliers, no infinite reloads, proper boss fights, enemy evolution; and no damn-game-breaking-xcom-super-soldiers-who-can-take-groups-of-enemies-alone;

Despite all those bells and whistles, PP got no cinematic kill cams, no compelling story, a lackluster strategic layer, graphics sucks (AND YES GRAPHICS IS AS IMPORTANT TO A GAME AS GAMEPLAY AND MECHANICS, IF IT WASN'T, GAME DEVS WOULD HAVE STUCK TO TEXT BASED GAMES OF THE 1990s), most importantly it's painfully obvious that it lacks the polish and dynamism that's only present on XCOM which makes XCOM feel a joy to play.

Heck even PP's turn system is stupid. Oh spotted an enemy? Pause. You and the AI take turns on moving---WTF? XCOM Chimera already showed us that asymmetric turns does work too.

I love Tactical RPGs like XCOM, Phoenix Point, Valkyria, Fire Emblem, etc; but professing Phoenix Point is the best there is to ever come for Tactical RPGs is just nonsense-it's still inferior to XCOM and that's a fact.

You want a fight? Fight me. Heck even DM me. I play Tactical RPGs all the time, and Phoenix Point is shiㄒ.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t1. Random crashing
You can play for many hours without any crashing then you find on a certain mission you get to a certain point and it keeps crashing (not even just to desktop). Sometimes it appears sensitive to particular actions such as free aim and if you work it out (after several reboots) you can find a way to limp through to the end of the mission. After that it may be fine for many hours again. Worse though is when you reach a certain point in a mission and no matter what you try and do it just crashes. Restarting or going back to geoscape then relaunching does not seem to help.
2. It is so bloody slowwwww
Load times are slow. They got slower with the most recent patch which was then 'fixed' with a 2.5gb hotfix.
The AI can sometimes take ages to make its moves and if there are many units each turn can go on a long time sitting twiddling your thumbs.
The actual game is also slow to progress through. Lots of grind through repetitive missions, often with little sense of where it's all going.
3. No buttons to unequip units!!!
This makes everything slower and much more of a grind. Every mission you have to unequip the other units you used to equip the current units but you have to do this item by item! It's not much to ask for a button to unequip a unit or a whole team etc. Also to have some sort of grid to make it easy to compare units as in xcom 2 .
4. Maps
You end up playing on the same few maps over and over, come on . They are all small. A richer variety of terrain, buildings and map sizes would make it all more interesting
5. Uninspiring sound and music
6. Modding
There seems to be little support for modders. Some of the above complaints would be less if there was a thriving community as for xcom 2

I like this type of game, and have persisted with it despite all of the above because there is a good game in there. However I have had enough after 8 crashes all at (roughly) the same point on a mission.
If they would fix point 1 and 3 I would come back and even recommend it. But for now I would say give it a miss. I suspect they are now only interested in pushing out DLC to hardcore fans who will tolerate this and pay the high prices for content which just makes the whole thing drag even more (I actually don't have last 2 dlc's, this is based on comments I've read).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as I do have some problems with Phoenix Point - the busywork of maintenance, the comedy of mistakes from Festering Skies, small number of distinct enemies and the seeming absence of sustainable resource gathering (at least that I've seen so far) - the gameplay and the storyline are really quite enjoyable.

Basically: everything I have an issue with is stuff that can be easily added to the game over time and in some cases has been. The base game itself is excellent.

Accuracy is based on a deviation from the central target and true LOS which, while annoying at times, ensures that every time you take a shot you're not relying on a percentage chance of hitting it. There's no 99% chance whiffs at point blank: if you shove your muzzle into a Pandoran head that head is going to quickly disappear.
Destroying specific parts of the enemies can be a godsend as it will buy you more time, remove their best defenses/weapons/special abilities. Some enemies are even specifically designed to encourage you to flank them which I really enjoy.
All enemies are on the map to begin with so you don't just run into a mob and they go "lol oh noes we got spotted" and scatter. Instead you have to hunt them down one by one or all at once as they try to swarm you.
The factions you're trying to align with are, well, actually there on the map. You have to help keep those settlements safe and, y'know, protect humanity. There's no random mission generator to just create sporadic attacks - the enemies just invade the existing settlements and hopefully you get there in time to stop them (or the settlement's defenders can handle them... though they'll end up far weaker against the next attack if you do this) - creating the sense that you are actually the super secret organization dedicated to keeping humanity from getting wiped off the map.
Finally a small part that is just cozy AF: You're the super secret not-quite government organization with cutting edge tech and the stuff you start with shows it. You kick off the game with probably the best tech available and your potential allies don't offer you dramatically better stuff - just different stuff. Overall the default equipment is really, really good and it further entrenches the feeling that you are, in fact, the secret organization that has been hoarding awesome tech all these years in preparation for saving humanity.

... 'course it's also pretty expensive, but still. It really helps to reinforce the theme.


All in all I give it a tentative recommendation. It's a flawed but fun game marred by a couple poor design choices that can be readily fixed with some attention. If the developers choose to fix them, then it would be truly great, IMO.\t\t\t\t\t
