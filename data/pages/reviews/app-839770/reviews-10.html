\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe X-com we deserve. ©

Yeah, we don't deserve much, it seems. And I really, really want to recommend this game, but it's just... underwhelming. Sadly, Steam does not allow me to post a neutral review.

The good:
- Lore. Lore is freaking awesome, especially if you read the short stories on the game's website. Lovecraftian eldritch horrors that come from the sea.
- Damage model: you totally can (and should) snipe the pincers off enemies, rendering them harmless
- SIMULATED ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ BULLETS. Hell, this would make this game the best in its genre, but...

The meh
- It plays almost like the Firaxis' X-COM: base management, soldier classes, with its own twist like vehicles (they take 3 slots in the transport, so almost useless), willpoints/panic, and
- It's plagued by minor UI issues, like scrollwheel not properly scrolling the list, etc
- Its bullet simulation fails to work sometimes, and bullets pass through enemies or you. I can live with that, frankly
- UI is lacking. Not that it's missing something, it's just not that good IMO
- This may be a matter of taste, but the voice acting is meh: the soldiers sound lifeless, and sounds for enemies, frankly, is weird: almost all the enemies sound almost the same (not human-like), except for the Scylla, that sounds almost human. Dunno, maybe it should be a part of the "horror", but it's very meh

The ugly
- The game is shallow: there's research, sure, but you're not really progressing. The new guns are mostly situational, apart from some notable exceptions. Nothing game-changing, almost nothing exciting. You want to equip some new and cool armor? Nope, you only can cosplay another faction, whose armor is generally the same, with some stats shifted around.
- There are about 15 maps total, or so it seems. It gets old pretty quick.
- At some point you're sent to Antarctica, which is a... tropical rainforest, the environment is a copy of another mission that took place on the equator. -_-
- The game was originally kickstartered, reaching some additional goals, then the management sold its soul to Epic for additional cash, throwing a part of its backers under the bus, yet still failed to deliver the "evolution" mechanic: currently the progression is tied to some obscure checkpoints, with enemy variants appearing in a sequence, instead of the promised "evolution", when the AI would adapt and evolve its minions to adapt to your playstyle
- The game is lacking some features that should be in the game, yet present in, you guessed it!, DLCs. You want to actually distinguish between different ground vehicles? Pay up!

All in all, the game is kinda cool, something to pass time until Xenonauts 2 comes out of the EA. I'd say it's worth buying only heavily discounted, and don't bother with DLCs, they don't add much substance, only clutter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you buy this to scratch the itch of playing XCOM, but like me wanted something a little different as I've played through that series many a time; then I CANNOT recommend this game any less. So I did not play this on release and have only played it with all the DLC installed and it is possibly one of the worst strategy games I have ever played. The game starts tbh a little too easy imo. Many enemies are melee only and you can kind of just gun them down from range. But, after the introduction of corruption, acherons, chirons and the flyer events things start to change.

1) Corruption is a mechanic that reduces your max will, but gives you a small buff to DMG. Now I assume this was added as a DLC because the will point (WP) mechanic is core to the soldiers gameplay loop. I would say unlike XCOM, upgrading guns and equipment plays a lesser role to gaining new abilities via levelling (if you're more experience in PP you may disagree). This means your WP are crucial to a soldiers viability especially on later missions. To tell you the truth though, some of the WP mechanics are simply broken and allow you on soldier to permanently lockdown a troublesome enemy or give you immense mobility. So what did the devs decide to do to rebalance this? They decided to give you a condition that is at times unavoidable that hinders your WP. This means if you levelled into WP, well you're bang out of luck! You want to go deal with a lair, well that's going to corrupt you. Save these Synedrions who are in the mist, well that's going to corrupt you. There are then enemies that also can corrupt you as well as enemies that make all enemies corrupt you. Basically, you are going to be covered in vimto at some point. The worse part about it. THERE IS NO CURE BY DEFAULT WHEN YOU GET THIS! It's actually hidden behind a research of a Pandoran, with no clue really that, that is the case. Even when you do pick this up, it's so expensive that you probably cannot cure everyone. Overall, the mechanic just sucks. To tell you the truth though, this is because the WP mechanic is extremely ill conceived.

2) Acherons and chirons - completely BS enemy. So these critters evolve to have a plethora of abilities, but I want to focus on two mainly here (I already talk about corruption which is meant to be their main gimmick, but it isn't). So the default acherons have a couple hundred HP and maybe like 50 WP. They have an ability that allows them to call reinforcements (like 2 units I think). I'm not sure exactly off the top of my head how much WP it costs them to do this, but I think it's about 5. This means that every turn, unless you hinder them in some way, or kill them; they are going to call for reinforcements EVERY SINGLE TURN! Comparing this to XCOM, I would say they are most like gatekeepers. But, unlike a gatekeeper, their abilities have no cooldowns. If you have the WP to pay for your ability, you can use it every turn. At least with gatekeepers, if they res, you can deal with the added enemy tempo, then kill the gatekeeper before it's ability comes up again. (i.d. also like to add, killing a gatekeeper in a single turn is much more reasonable than an acheron - especially later evolved forms). These big f'ers therefore call for reinforcements, then run away! If you finally exhaust them of their WP, they just use 1 recover and get back to business. The worst thing though and really what makes me think the Devs frankly didn't really care about the balance too much. These creatures in an early evolution cycle evolve to gain a resurrect ability. This now not only means they can call in 2 units. They can also resurrect in an AOE units you killed. Ofc it's a WP ability, hence they can do this every turn until they need to recover. This basically means, you need disruption, that being heavies with war cry or virus rifles or disorientation. However, 2 of these require you to have WP and the game mass corrupts you so you don't have them. See the issue? Chirons I have also included here are they artillery shell 3 worms from across the map basically every turn. If you have 2 acherons or an acheron plus a chiron on a mission, i hope your squad has some real cheddar of it's own; otherwise you are going to be ground down until you die. At least when chirons run out of juice though, they just leave the mission...

3) The whack-a-mole behemoth flying mechanic. Honestly, completely stupidly incorporated into the game. I will say I actually like the aerial battles. They break up the monotony of fighting wave after wave of Pandorans. My main criticism is firstly, I didn't find it was particularly well explained how to initiate them. But I will concede, I think I was just dumb about this to start with. My main issue though is that they just go around the globe immediately destroying everything. This is another DLC FYI. I think it was introduced to stop people just loitering slowly in one continent and forcing you to expand. If you do this now, the behemoth will destroy settlements across the globe and you will lose the game. However, it is so hard to explosively expand to keep up with these flying flips. I started in central americas. The fliers are there. I bonk them. They move to east asia. I can see them destroying stuff. However, without a scan of the location, YOU CANNOT FIGHT THEM! Even though I know they are their. I flew past and pointed at them through the Gift's window. Look commander, the behemoth and it's flying entourage is heading to shanghai. Sorry private pleb, we have no scan data so we cannot just shoot some of the flippers down. Before I can finally complete getting my foothold there, which requires me to restore a PP base, power and fix its satellite system so it can scan. They move to europe. Ok, I will go to europe. They go to africa... etc... I'm always one step behind and honestly I am spending materials that I could otherwise use to augment my soldiers basically just unlocking satellite dishes across the globe and spreading my forces out thinly. There might be a better way of dealing with this, but I just don't know why I cannot fly to unseen locations to fight these guys when I know they are there. It's extremely frustratingly incorporated mechanic!

4) the game is buggy as hell. It's around 6 years old. The developers are clearly not able or have any desire to fix it. The amount of times my preview shows I can shoot from a spot, i move there, and then I cannot see anything. Happens about once a mission. The aim for the body part system. The whole games major gimmick and actually one of the things I really like the concept of, at times simply does not function. Yes you can see this Triton Poachers. I go to manual view and its just a flat wall. Thanks game. I have my reticle 100% on an enemies head with a sniper. I fire. I miss (I assume even though it showed my a still during them aiming minigame, when I took the shot they passively bobbed their head, so between me clicking shoot and the shot going off, the model changed so I missed - I have no control over this). This unit has return fire. It cannot see me from this position though. I lob a grenade over. It shoot me back? How game?

Overall it's a shame. I like the idea of the vehicles in the game. I like the idea of factions and the gameplay changing upon who you are helping and the vats style shoot a limb system. The balance though is appalling and it feels like the DLC have been introduced to punish peoples build for winning them game, rather than them being fun, which feels truly bizarre. If you want to scratch the XCOM itch. Don't play this. Play modded XCOM instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would love to recommend this game as it's a lot of fun. I'm not that invested in the story, but the mechanics is good and well thought out. It does however CRASH a lot. During loading, during saving, after completing a mission, you name it and it quits on you, while autosaves are not done frequently (and never during battles). Some battles can take quite a while and if you don't save manually, then you lose progress as it quits on you. Or it quits on you while trying to save. Really too bad they haven't fixed it. Otherwise, if you can live with this major flaw, it's great, especially if you're a fan of the genre and XCom in particular.

Edit: after upgrading to Win11 just for the sake of this game, it's more stable. Not without the occasional crash, and in one particular mission it brings BSOD several times. Still, the game is very difficult even on rookie level so they could've used some adjustments there. I also changed my recommendation to Yes after the more stable experience.\t\t\t\t\t
