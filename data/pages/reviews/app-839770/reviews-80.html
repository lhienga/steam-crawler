I'd advise frequent save points, then rolling back to earlier ones if you accidentally get stuck on a dead-end mission.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, yet again, I find myself in a difficult position dealing with the creator of the beloved XCOM series. I REALLY wanted to like this game. But the problems are just too big to ignore.
First and foremost, the story hardly holds up to XCOM which isn't saying all that much since that story is rather simple too.
The combat seems to be an improvement as the aiming system is night and day better than what XCOM uses, and at least if you completely miss a shot here, it was likely your own fault.
So many bugs that you'd think they were the ones you're supposed to be fighting. Terrible CTDs left and right to the point that I even had to unplug my computer once or twice just to reset the mess it caused.

At 44 hours in, I'm almost finished with the end of my first campaign, and I've now encountered such terrible loading times that eventually my game locks to where I can't even get my mouse to click on anything in game. I can't save. Can't load. Just stuck with the story at like 90% complete. That's the specific kind of nonsense that kills a franchise like this.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTook me a while to finish my first campaign, but I can say it just clicked for me in a way XCOM2 never did.
My biggest gripe with XCOM2 is that everything runs on countdowns which eventually put me off so much that I've never finished a campaign on that one.
In PP however, countdowns are used sparingly and make more sense.

The free aiming system, which some people dismiss as 'gimmick', is quite fun to use and has its uses beyond the 'cover as large an area as possible on the bad guy'. E.g if an enemy has no means to hurt you anymore, he's basically taken off the board. So if someone has just a 2-handed weapon and you shoot off one of his arms (or destroy the weapon), he's no threat anymore.
It also gives more immersive feedback on your hitchance than a simple number; and since a lot of the weapons fire in bursts and each projectile is rolled seperately, it doesn't need to artificially roll a 'glance' or similar for you, you just hit with 3 out of 6 bullets on a bad shot and that's it.

The 7 classes can be combined into 21 different 'cross-classes' (since Sniper/Heavy is the same as Heavy/Sniper) and while some combinations are more obvious than others, each ability has at the very least their niche where it comes handy. Each person also has up to 3 random perks which can make a difference in what class-combination and what combat role they'd be best suited for.
Mutations and augments add additional variety and further abilities - I was quite surprised how much room for theorycrafting and experimentation is in this game.

However, the UI is adequate at best:
- there are no standard gear layouts or 'unequip all' buttons
- the manufacture and equip screens lack sorting options (the default order of items is just a mess) ...
- ... and are using not enough space on the screen which leads to way too much scrolling, especially if you don't know exactly what you're looking for while you haven't memorized the names or looks of items (and even if you know the names, there's no search function).

All in all, 'twas fun and well worth the time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
Free aim allows you to set your own terms for firing
Frankly I'll take any decent game in the genre

Cons:
Not "any decent game"
Little to no active abilities. Not even frags.
Little to no enemy variety
Aiming makes no sense because idle animation and flinching animation cause your shots to miss
All enemies are either brainless melee units that get killed in a single shot, or brainless ranged units with infinite return fire
Speaking of return fire, this is some high BS. Because of how the game uses the characters' actual in-game model for aiming, if you shoot at anything with return fire, youre getting shot at while out of cover
Balance makes zero sense. The sharpshooter class that needs a high vantage point with long lines of sight gets no movement abilities. Meanwhile the melee class that needs to get in to the thick of the action never sees any action because he's the slowest member of the squad
Who does get movement abilities? The generalist AR class. Who doesnt need movement abilities since they'll be shoot-and-scooting the whole game. Because, again, no special abilities.
Vehicles dont make sense as a stand-in for movement abilities dont make sense either since theyre slower than a AR class soldier using their movement ability

Slight inconveniences:
Tutorial tells you to melee the headcrab on the friendly soldier but fails to tell you the headcrab has its own targeting icon
Game starts when done loading, without waiting for you to click or anything. Wanted to get a drink during the load time? Hope you dont mind missing the cinematics then

Score:
4/10 below average
As much as I'm a fan of the genre, I cant say im a fan of this particular take on the genre\t\t\t\t\t
