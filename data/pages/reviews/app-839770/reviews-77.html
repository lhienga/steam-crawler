Played all the X-COM related games including the X-COM: Interceptor and thought that this would be a very interesting take from one of the creators of the original series but was quite disappointed.

Some of my complains:
- The game still have bugs that a released edition should not have, like the dropship cutscene bug.
- Should have workshop integration for mods.
- The classes are not well thought, the mix of skills range from useless to useful but against the "spirit" of the class, for example, a very good specialization is to make a Heavy into a melee fighter while the Berzerker is mostly cannon fodder in melee combat unless you spend a lot of points in him and then is semi decent.
- The PhoenixPedia lack all the flavor text and context, at the moment looks more like a placeholder or WIP.
- A lot of missed oportunity on the reputation with factions. The extra missions given by the factions are always attack another heaven, so if you dont want to make war to a faction then you are stuck waiting to defend a heaven or be lucky enough to discover a sidequest (that could also hinder progress with some faction) to gain favor.
- The research tree is mostly about weapons that are not very different one from the other, the only way to develop new armor is to salvage 2 pieces and enemies never drop armor and you can reach a point where you cannot research anything and im not talking about endgame, very shallow research tree.
- Armor and weapons are quite lacking and offer little to no choice.
- Movement through the geoscape can be sometimes impossible and you will not be able to defend a heaven or base.\t\t\t\t\t
