\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember playing the original XCom (UFO Defense and then Terror from the Deep) nearly 20 years ago now and being blown away by it. It was just fantastic. Then the new XCom series came out and they were brilliant. So when I heard of Phoenix Point and Julian Gallop and so on, I was very excited. I had bought this and sank hours into it on another platform. Got it again on Steam as I saw a lot of DLC had been added hoping for an improvement, but was left disappointed.

However, while the game is 'ok' as a turn based game, it is very poorly laid out.

-It consists of countless and endless meaningless/repetitive grind missions over and over and over again. Missions become the exact same so fast
-The diplomacy/loyalty mechanic between the three factions had huge potential, however its just boring and more grinding of repetitive missions
-Weapon upgrades are non-existent
-Personnel are completely impersonal; losing a soldier Id levelled up and invested in in XCom was nearly reason to start a new game. In this game I got no such connection. Soldier dies and you just carry on grinding
-Rather than 'challenging', some enemies are just flat out annoying. Having half my squad mind controlled, or overwatch not triggering (overwatch mechanic becomes tediously annoying nearly immediately)


Look, its not a bad game, but its not great either; 5.5/10. And not worth â‚¬50 (another thing the developers did was launch the game and immediately announce 5 DLCs that would be released over the year after release, as if the game wasnt finished when launched and thats the feeling you get playing it). The story behind it sounded so cool and gripping, but the narrative and story in game is rubbish. Doesnt grip or capture the imagination, literally just turn the speed timer to maximum and complete research/grind/level soldiers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: there are better games out there.

I bought it 50 % off and i regret that purchase a little bit. The game starts of promising and isn`t bad per se, but there are many things to throw a spanner in the works.

To get the positive out first. I had one moment, that sticks with me. I had to defend an outpost on extreme difficulty the first time. Never had been to a citadel either. So i spawned in a little villiage full of fog. Than i moved my squad a few fight and saw IT for the first time. My jaw dropped and i regretted the life choices of my boys and girls in the field. Great moment.

A few of the bad things:
1. The game doesn`T explain many of its mechanics in game. You`ll start with no real idea, what to do and how to win. You can reconstruct outposts, but the game doesn`t explain to you, why you should do that. And you get no feedback that you are overextending, which might bite you in the behind hours in the game, since you need an aircraft to get an tradeloop going and generate resources without waiting for outposts of the factions to get attacked. There a a few damage types you can use, but if the enemy isn`t shooting you with damage type X, the game won`t tell, what it does. You can dual-class, but the screen won`T show you the ability of the dual class. With the DLC you can explore archiological sites, but you will be confused as what to do, if you get one, since the game doesn`t tell you, that you`ll need 2 of them in specific pairs. The list goes on.

2. The combat system is not that fun. The game doesn`t tell you when you are going to be spotted. (Its a skill check perception vs stealth). So you can get spotted across the map in round 1 and get bombed by artillery you can`t see and that doesn`t see you but hits you dead center. Mind control enemies cross half the map, and have enough points to MC your soldier and get back into cover. So you can set your soldiers up, but you can`t really prepare for anything. In a bad way.

The game also talks about the realistic bullet physics. "No hit percentages!" True, but you might be suprised how often your soldiers will just shoot at the pixel of air that your free aim didn`t cover, while the enemy gets headshots across the map.

Not to mention, you can`t move soldiers at the same time.

The worst part for me, the classes just don`t feel that good. In X-Com, you got that feeling for each class and their abilites. The dual classes in PP are necessary but take away from that. The abilities also are often forgettable.

3. Just 2 % of the player-base have finished the campaign. I can see why you wouldn`t.
Endgame ist boring. Just boring. About halfway through a campaign, you`ll just sit there, waiting for research, while you play the same defense mission in the same map over and over or attack the same 3 lairs over and over for the next hours. Its not fun. The only time the difficulty ramps up is the last mission, and at least for the Anu-faction, its quite balls to the walls. The enemy will pin you down and bomb you away shooting perfectly over a huge wall.

I`m tired of writing about it, so just play XCom, JA2 or even Gears of War (at least the combat is faster and the plot isn`t worse).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the game is not really bad, and adds a bunch of innovations to the genre, it cannot compete with XCom/XCom2 in terms of pure fun IMO. While the combat is entertaining, and in many ways an improvement over XCom, there is just too much micro-management needed on the strategic layer for my taste.

The Good:
* Ballistic targeting & disabling body parts is great and extremely satisfying.
* Interesting combat mechanics all around that allow great tactical flexibility.

The Bad:
* You need to maintain multiple aircrafts & crews to respond to events in time. Realistic, but just adds more things to look after.
* The resource squeeze forces you to trade with other factions. Which means managing aircrafts to fly around between them. Something that could easily be replaced by an automated trading system that just generates resources over time.
* With the DLCs come ancient sites. Once conquered, you have to manage aircrafts flying between them to collect mined resources. Something else that could be automated easily.
* Combat equipment is far too expensive. A hammer costs about 1/4 of an aircraft. Troops go into combat with half-empty magazines because they cannot afford full ones.
* Weapon balance is off. I'm at the point where I rather walk up to aliens and hit them with a big hammer, because (a) it deals more damage than my best gun, and (b) doesn't use up ammunition.
* The "realistic" approach of requiring multiple teams in multiple places is somewhat undermined by equipment just sitting in a common pool, meaning you end up moving equipment between teams on opposite sites of the world.\t\t\t\t\t
