\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhoenix Point is, not without an incident, a masterpiece, — a REAL and only successor of the X-COM: Enemy Unknown, though not that good as X-COM: Apocalypse... Yeah, I'm that old.

Apart from the inevitable arcadisation it sustained along with all the gaming industry which started treating the Player as an imbecile, who can't keep its inventory in check between bases, — it still musters to deliver joy from challenge (on Legend difficulty) in a consistent and replay-able manner.

Though lacking much of potential the genre has already developed (like
- TOTALLY destructible multi-stored environment,
- or global economy and faction relations,
- or extensive combat mechanics where your soldiers can go prone or float,
- and choose between aimed, snap or barrage shots)
it still delivers some substantial improvements, most important of which are realistic ballistics model with no "chance to hit", and armor degradation (though only in combat).

Aerial combat remained rudimentary, yes. But the essence of the turn-based encounters is simply very good (not superb, nonono). There's also some cool storytelling and voice acting in store.

All in all the game is not ideal, but none of the masterpieces are. Masterpiece — is a little something you get back to. Over and over, despite anything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWas looking forward to this, since I'm a big fan of the original X-Com series and this is made by one of the original guys who worked on it, but it's a fairly mid game. Not terrible but not great either. The updated shooting system is awesome, when it works, and really hope it gets adopted into the newer X-Com games. Unfortunately, that's about all it does well and even that doesn't work all the time. Instead of just giving you a % chance to hit, you actual aim at an enemy yourself and fire. Missing enemies that are right next to you, due to crap rng, is a thing of the past. It has now been replaced by "I can clearly see the target but, when I go to free aim, my guy is just looking at a wall or something instead of the target". Doesn't happen all the time, just enough to annoy you and I still like it better. Too bad the rest of the game is "meh" to mediocre.

The research tree is bogged down with way too much junk and half of it could be cut out without missing out on anything important. You need to research basic stuff like shotguns, grenade launcher, and even freaking melee weapons. It gets even worse when the faction research gets involved. Not only do you have to balance faction rep but 90% of the stuff they give are more like side upgrades and preference rather than flat out better gear/vehicles to make. Oh and you have to make everything yourself...even little stuff like grenades, ammo, medkits, etc. It takes way too long to get any real upgrades and it makes the game feel stale and boring until then. Annoying, not hard, seems to be the common theme of the game.

Enemies aren't any better. Majority of enemies are just buffer palette swaps of older enemies and half of them just seems to exist to annoy you. Either they're bullet sponges, spawn other enemies, go invisible, kamikaze, mind controllers, or my favorite...spawn mist that makes all of them invisible and lasts 5+ turns. The ones that aren't any of the listed die or get crippled in one shot because the a.i. is dumb as a brick. The enemies do look cool though. The classes are nice and multi-classing makes for some awesome combos. Too bad 6 of the 9 classes are locked behind factions. I could honestly do without the 3 factions at all.

It's not horrible game it's just kinda half baked. If you really like turn based squad games, maybe pick it up on sale. Skip the DLC though, the reviews on them are pretty bad. Maybe if they make a 2nd game, fix a few things, cut down on the BS, remove/redo factions, better a.i., and just add some more meat to it, it'll actually stand up to current X-Com. Not a hard pass but wouldn't spend more than $15-$20 for it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed visiting places on the world map and turning question marks into knowns. That, and the art and story might be this game's best features. Gear offers some interesting choices. Squad cosmetics are real basic. Base building is a snooze. Probably the game's worst feature is the way global logistics are handled. Having to activate base after base in order to be able to travel from one continent to another is a huge drain on the very limited resources the game provides, such that it fully prevents you from being able to upgrade your squads to be able to handle the more advanced threats that pop up relatively early in the game's progression. Air transports should have 2 to 3 times the range they currently have. Exploration should more frequently result in a combat encounter and result in a haven discovery far less often. Combat is full of bad design and poorly implemented features. Cover doesn't work, unless enemies are using it. A super-size creature can use a lamppost as cover. Enemies trick shot you consistently from long distances with short-range weapons. I always thought it was annoying in X-COM when half your squad is on overwatch, and one enemy runs in and triggers everything, even though it dies after the first squad member shoots. That doesn't happen in this game, which is nice, except enemies don't trigger overwatch about 30% of the time when they pass through overwatch zones. They also know exactly where your overwatch zone ends, and avoid it if they can. Heavy gunners are dead weight. They are too slow for frontline and don't have the range for backline. Give them pistol proficiency and they can at least jet-boost/pistol until they run out of will. Heavy armor is effective when enemies have it. When you have it, not so much. Enemy with 30 armor = nigh-invincible super-soldier, you with 35 armor = still squishy. High ground is useless most of the time because a lot of action takes place too close to buildings for enemies to be targetable. Apparently nobody knows how to aim down past 45 degrees. Also, like cover, high ground does not provide a defensive advantage. I've only seen the AI use high ground once thus far. It seems to strongly prefer low ground, even when high ground is an option. It doesn't even chase you up to high ground, it just shoots from below, because it can. Overwatch is at its least consistent when trying to overwatch an area that is not at the same elevation as your unit.

The game does not lack for content, and I think the content is generally well-made, but that content is trying to come together around a framework that has a number of impactful flaws. It always bums me out when developers splurge on extras but don't fully consider the basics of their game.

*Edit*

Despite misgivings about the game, I decided I was invested enough to try to see it through to the end. Festering Skies was a bad idea. It requires you to spend already stretched-thin resources on developing air vehicles, which are hugely expensive. The threats you are developing said vehicles to combat cannot be safely ignored. You must commit both research and materials to said development, which means you spend more time spinning the clock waiting for research to complete rather than doing anything meaningful and fun. Building air units means building fewer research facilities, so the time drain this DLC creates is compounded. Also, air combat is a dull minigame that realizes 2% of the concept's potential.

Long story short, past mid-game, everything turns into a sloggy grind, you have no resources to build anything, and you are just advancing time waiting for enemies to attack so that your scanners can locate their bases (important because progression towards every victory condition seems to bottleneck at a particular objective, in which you must destroy a particular type of enemy base). Then you have to research a bunch of stuff, which means advancing time even more while there's nothing to do. There are no hard choices in this game. You don't have to sacrifice anything in order to advance your objectives. You just don't have the resources to do anything fun.

After all this, I finally reached the last mission, only to discover a bug wherein a siren mind-controls one of your units, then wallhacks into terrain. Normally the solution to being mind-controlled is to head-shot the controlling unit, a thing that is often easy to accomplish. However, this task becomes infinitely more complicated after said unit has wallhacked. I would probably have found this hilarious were it not the last straw on top of a pile of frustrations. I have uninstalled this game. I do not care to finish it.\t\t\t\t\t
