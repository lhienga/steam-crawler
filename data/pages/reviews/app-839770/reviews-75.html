\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like Phoenix Point, I waited for the YOE and even retried 2 months later but the game is strange in the sense that always when it's about to scratch the itch, it just reminds you why it doesn't (so far).

It works fine, never crashes and few bugs or glitches. However it constantly leaves you confused or frustrated when it doesn't have to. Things feel different and changed only for the sake of difference and change. You get many choices that don't matter and then few or no choices when they would matter the most.

Individual perks, multiple classes and a dual class option... well that's great! But the first class is pre-selected for you and the perks are completely random, and are often irrelivant since a few missions would net you the same result. Hmm... alright tell me about second class? Well that is important... so good luck. Oh and we hid some of the class options, better jump through some hoops to see them. Now that that's done, the new options aren't very good.

Alright I found some recruits and can fill my 6 soldier aircraft... but missions allow 8 soldiers, and very soon you need all you can bring. So now you need a bigger slower aircraft or multiple ones, but they vary in size and speed so now you are stuck organizing a time-distance multi vehicle carpool. Not exactly action packed gun blasting alien exploding combat.

Better give my soldiers pistols, pistols shoot bullets. Warning: 'Not proficient with weapon'. Ahh the majority of my soldiers doesn't know how to use a handgun.

We want the player to be on the offense and engage - so soldiers lose more stamina the longer missions go on! Great I get to play aggressive instead of an overwatch competition. Well... about that, see the enemies get stronger faster than your squad and there are more of them so you are at a considerable stat and action point disadvantage. Not to worry though, we put the most powerful action point leveler on the overwatch skill... so welcome back to the overwatch competition.

My soldiers are running low on stamina so I'll swap some out for fresh ones. Recruits are plentiful but base located, whereas equipment is global and takes time to be made, even with plenty resources. So now starts the ever tedious deequip and requip. Join this with your pre-selected soldiers... and your squad members pretty much become numbers.

Base building is there, still for anyone capable of solving a 4 piece puzzle it is a non concern.

The tactical combat
Step 1. Get full cover. Step 2. Fire from full cover. Step 3. Soldier steps completely out of full cover to ensure maximum enemy overwatch activation and return fire damage received.

Haven defense. Ah, defenders advantage! Nope random spawns. You and faction soldiers spawn far away, enemies spawn at their objective. You can try hard offense and rush it... that load or restart button isn't there for decorative purposes.

Your rifle won't dent a wall, your pistol one shots it, but only works on some walls and you don't get to know which. Other times you dash your squad for that line of sight to kill that crucial target... but the cruel forest monster and his many friends have set up quite a few indestructible walls of invisibility which block your bullets with magic.

This continues onward and you send your best squad half around the globe only to find out the mission was a dialog popup. You get better armor and defense against explosions, which is then bypassed by poison, and viruses, and there is fire, also acid, then all armor then gets nullified by armor penetration. So it's pretty much a full blown 'ADD' - Rock, Paper, Scissors, Lizard, Spock, Old MacDonald had a farm, Hey Macarena!

So it's strange. It has all the core ingredients, it's stable and with neat mechanics... yet comes out as a story of many good things that were or could have been, but are not now and can't be seen on the horizon, which I don't understand. The combination of current state + DLC's + console direction + no real workshop/mod/community unfortunately indicates the game will continue on a linear path towards simple and quite guided playthroughs, whereas I'd champion variety and replayability. And it appears even if they developer doesn't want to play around with such things, they seem to take steps so others can't either. Which unfortunately puts it in a box of non-recommend.

Still I'm fine with putting down around $40 for it, it is way beyond many others and whatever my capabilities could create... but I'd much rather be at $60 and have a full potential and recommendable version.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried to like this game, I really did. The strategic gameplay, though, was fairly uninspired and didn't show much improvement after 20 hours, and the battles were interesting for a little while until whatever math is on in the background just decided Balance was cool in theory but not in practice. Nothing more fun than fighting multiple enemies that constantly spawn walking explosions 3 at a time every turn, each with hundreds of health and dozens of armor on each body part and can nail you from across the map.

Meanwhile your own vehicles can fire *maybe* three or four times.

Couple that with no real weapon upgrades (just side-grades, sometimes) until dozens of hours into it and a soldier level cap you'll hit way too quickly and then just stagnate on, and this reads like someone who was a big fan of the classic XCOM games but forgot what progression or proper game balance is (though original XCOM games had no balance either, to be fair).

Just...I tried to like it. Really did. But I couldn't.

EDIT: COVID and a touch of poverrty have forced me to take up this game to eat the days, and I will say that there are some mods on nexusmods that make this a lot more enjoyable, with quality of life improvements and some balance/mechanical tweaks. That said, still not going to recommend this game, since in its base state it's painful after a few hours.\t\t\t\t\t

The tactical layer isn't as tight either, but it's based around setting up equal encounters (in the missions where you are attacking a real faction they have soldiers equal to, and sometimes better equipped than you do). Don't go into it expecting XCOM, cause it's not, but it is a game that is close enough to XCOM, while still doing it's own thing.
