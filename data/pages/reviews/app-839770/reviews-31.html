\r\n\t\t\t\t\t- this game's geoscape is really nice, you have multiple ships each carrying around its own squad of soldiers/vehicles around and checking points of interest, visiting havens, fighting back alien lairs, etc. feels very dynamic compared to xcom's skyranger
- game has a setting for Very Fast animations, god bless
- the Free Aim system is actually p dope, you can choose to slice off limbs to reduce mobility/stop attacks/prevent mind control abilities and the like
- instead of the modern xcom games having 2 action points total, PP has 4AP split freely between moves and actions and it actually works pretty great!
- multiclassing is fun

unfortunately, the good points stop here.

- thanks to RNG you can have story missions end up on the other side of the world without a way to access it. i spawned in eastern europe and had the second mission spawn in Latin America, and I didn't have the range to cross over Alaska because the points of interest didn't spawn close enough to one another, so I had to take the long way around which was a pain
- enemies evolve over time, but realistically you're fighting the same 3-4 enemy types all game (the melee crabs with their shields, the stealth triton boys who snipe you and go invisible, the chirons who just rain down artillery at you from across the entire ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ map (which is fair, since you can do the same with your vehicle), and the sirens who can mind control like 3 of your soldiers at once (incredible). now this SOUNDS cool because you start out with grunts who slowly add shields and weapons to their kit, but their armor rating QUICKLY becomes too beefy for weapons like assault rifles or shotguns to scratch them. sure, each faction has a research to tackle those problems (new jericho gets armor piercing, synedrion gets poison bullets, etc), but if you dawdle too long it quickly snowballs into a problem where you can't do the faction missions to unlock those techs, you start losing boys and having recruits catch up is a PAIN and ugh (whoops! guess you should have reloaded your campaign when your second mission was too far away for you to get there in a couple days!)
- you have to engage with factions to unlock their research and techs, which is fine, but the result isn't really worth it. in xcom 2, your later technology is straight up better than what you start with: this is good. in PP, the upgrades feel more like sidegrades: sniper rifles either get a little more damage or a little more range, assault rifles do a little more damage but shoot less bullets, etc. let's not even get into how little armor upgrades for the player.
- inventory management is tedious. if you don't recruit from a haven, your fresh boys don't even have armor or weapons (what) and manufacturing things takes geoscape time and resources, which you have very little of. equipping the individual BULLETS for your characters (because you have limited reloads in this) is a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hassle what were they thinking
- This has to be the most unreliable Overwatch in any strategy game i've played. i've had enemies walk right in front of my guys and trigger jack. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.
- i'm starting to see what people mean by bullet sponge enemies
- no indicator for whether a unit is still stealthed or not
- personal skill issue, some maps the game just runs hella choppy even on minimal settings (restarting the game fixes this)
- no list feature to see what recruits are available at each haven (you have to go through them one by one and there's like a hundred on the map what the heck

The game's wack; it has some pretty great things going for it but the whole package around it is just not it, chief. go read a book\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Highly recommended for anyone who enjoys a nice, profound strategy game.

Turn-based combat with a very cool line-of-sight/physics based aiming system for combat. Beyond the missions, which revolve around different combat scenarios, Phoenix Point offers some very interesting strategic choices. Those choices involve where (and when) to build new bases to better fight the alien onslaught, what vehicles and equipment to favor, trading with various factions, and maintaining (or not) diplomatic ties with 3 major factions and a few sub-factions.

Phoenix Point also offers a non-linear path to victory - and has the courtesy to say so from the start, as the tutorial concludes.

There is a very easy, very obvious comparison to be made between the X-Com series of games, and Phoenix Point. I used to be a hardcore fan of X-Com, but here is why I think you may prefer Phoenix Point:

1. Unlike X-Com 2, Phoenix Point won't require that you install a 3rd person launcher (That's total BS, 3K Interactive!)
2. Phoenix Point has a vastly superior aiming system to X-Com: where you shoot an enemy matters in a big way, and can lead to either great success or constant mediocrity (that's a plus, if you can play smart)
3. Phoenix Point takes the global-monitoring, base-building approach that is flagrantly lacking from modern X-Com games. Phoenix Point will "scratch that itch" for anyone who has played the old-school 80s and 90s X-Com games. (i.e. Build many ships, fend off base invasions, watch over the entire planet with multiple bases)

---

One caveat: Phoenix Point is noticeably less polished than X-Com, when it comes to visual and auditory "trim"... It's doesn't offer the atmospheric and creepy audio of X-Com, nor are the animations and visual character customization as detailed and satisfying as they are in X-Com. Personally, I couldn't care less about that, since Phoenix Point offers a much better overall experience, but I thought I should mention it\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played xcom to death ... this game gave me an amazing breath of fresh air
Excellent game so far. At 40+ hours I'm still in my first playthrough

Pros:
+ story is less linear than xcom/2
+ the game has great story telling, try not to spoil it for yourself by reading guides and forums and jump into normal difficulty (disable all LotA DLC)

Suggestions:
- wish to pitch the camera in a way that I can put the camera in more of a 3rd person shooter view. (Edit:) allow the option to not center the camera during any turn. Or at least freely rotate and zoom while centered on a unit. Regardless of movement/action
- wish for more cosmetic options
- steam workshop so the community can help mod / fix (devs announced workshop coming 2022)

Edit:
get it on sale, it could use some polish.
If you are not familiar with all the mechanics the game can be punishing. Having a solid strategy is key in this game. If you want to be mentally stimulated to achieve victory, this is the game for you. If you want a laid back game that holds your hand, try the easiest difficulty or git gud
~ some of the voice acting is great, some of it it's terrible
~ The geoscape is beautiful. Still, I wish I could scroll to see what is available in list form to save time
+ the enemy composition / strength mechanic is cool. It can be abused, but it can be fun if you are open to a challenge
+ free aim is cool
+ inventory and ammo system is cool
- wish the game would autosave on tactical missions. Not to savescum but to not waste time if the game crashes
- wish spyder drones would not disappear on a good throw. Sometimes a wall or window catches the throw but it still disappears
- wish reinforcement points were not so predictable to the point I can spawn camp only a problem on veteran/normal\t\t\t\t\t
