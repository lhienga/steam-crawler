\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhoenix Point is an evolution of the turn-based tactics genre. The free-aim system allows the player the control to make meaningful decisions in placement and in utilizing the soldiers rather than praying to RNG-esus to give us a good roll. Snipers don't miss shots right in-front of their faces, there are no '99% but whoops it missed!' moments. For this alone it's a worthwhile and substantial upgrade to classic tactics games.

The over-world is on par with X-Com 2 with the player moving from point to point looking for cities and supplies in addition to trading opportunities. The great bulk of the player's time in the over-world should be spent either moving to a mission or moving between havens to trade resources. One of the major missed elements of Phoenix Point, and what I see giving players an endless amount of consternation is in finding enough resources to make it through the game. I've also noticed the achievement for 'Trading 10,000 Resources' is also under 10%.

If you are having trouble with insufficient resources, trading between havens is almost always a win-win

It's not even necessary to check the trade ratios, they don't change over time with diplomacy (as of this writing, anyway). The only thing you shouldn't trade is tech into Synedrion. Trading for tech with Synedrion is by far the easiest way to get it.

The factions are an idealized version of a few distinct philosophies: New Jericho the epitome of a meritocracy built upon the unwavering value of the human spirit. Synedrion a communist/hyper-egalitarian society that somehow manages to be the most technologically advanced. With the last being the most abysmal Disciples of Anu, half-mutated humans basically worshiping the harbingers of their demise and celebrating humanity's extinction.

While it's a bit odd the entire political spectrum is composed of a moderate meritocracy and two liberal, if competing, philosophies, to have anything resembling the actual nature of human societies is refreshing. While I wish there was a more substantial way to get involved with these factions I can appreciate them for what they are.

The base game is exquisite but the expansions are terrible. I wouldn't recommend them to anyone, the effort to create a more challenging play-through is more an exercise in frustration than anything fun.

The corruption mechanic reduces your soldier's willpower which makes it difficult if not impossible to use their abilities to any great degree and essentially reducing them to rookies.

The air fighter mini-game is less a mini-game and more an auto-battler. I didn't pick up the DLC so I could watch the computer play for me...

Blood and Titanium looked like a good one at first but I quickly soured on it for the same reason Legacy of the Ancients missed the mark: Too much time and resources invested in marginal upgrades to produce slightly above-average armor or weapons, respectively. The trouble in both of their stories is about the same as well. Whomever was tasked with writing for the DLC really really despises New Jericho.

In both instances the head of the faction is depicted as being either monstrous, irrational, or outright evil. 'Well, I know these soldiers gave their lives over to us for experimentation and they are neigh incorruptible super-soldiers we could task with some difficult or dangerous missions....but why not just shoot them all instead!'

'What did you say doctor, a proto-civilization with incredible technology and information on the threat destroying the world and corrupting all of humanity? Pffffft...yeah whatever, crazy lobster men are one thing but your research is just too extreme. Okay boys, time to lock her away for in a jail cell for some reason.'

Simply brilliant writing which somehow fails to involve the other factions in any meaningful way whatsoever.

As it stands I think it's clear Phoenix Point desperately needs modding support. There's so many things about this game which are excellent and just as many that are a little off. This has quickly become my favorite turn-based tactics game and I really hope it has the staying power of X-Com but if the DLC isn't improved substantially and if modding is never implemented, I can't see this being much more than a one and done series.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, I am a huge fan of the original X-COM: UFO Defense and X-COM: Terror from the Deep. There was several X-COM game variants since the original one, such as UFO:After series, Xenonauts, or the official X-COM (2012) what I played in the past. The base setting is same here, aliens invading the planet, and we are racing against to clock to find a way how to defeat them. As the time goes, aliens evolving, new species appear, and the game becaome harder, however on the other hand, we can acquire new technologies to shift the balance on our side.

The official X-COM (2012) was a huge disappointment for when I first played. It had a lot of missing oppurtinities in the game, compared to the original one. It looked to me an cellphone app version of the X-COM: Ufo Defense...but with every DLC the game become better and better and started to resembles to the old game.

Well for Phoenix Point I feel the inverse. I bought all DLC and play through the entire story and I have to say it was harsh, but not enjoyable.

The base game has lot of content and good ideas to be a fair contender to the X-COM (2012) and the X-COM2. What I like especially in this game is the character management, you have more freedom to specialize them, and you can give them unique equipment. This is a big thumbs up for me compared to the X-COM (2012). The pace of the game is okay, the monsters and the fighting mechanics also great, you can control your caracter where to aim the enemy, for example you can aim their legs, and if you manmage to damage it, the enemy character movement points greatly reduced. There is a political part of the game which has interesting idea to have contact another three factions of the world and according to your influence with them you can get unique technoligies from them which grants you different benefits, and you can get a unique game ending as well. Story is okay too. So I could recommend the base game!

However I have to say a few word about the DLCs as well. Two of them worth to buy, and two of them is only for gamers who like to suffer. :)

Phoenix Point - Living Weapons Pack: You get some missions to obtain a unique armor and a weapon which don't need ammo. This is okay.

Phoenix Point - Blood and Titanium DLC: A small faction appeared in the game nd some new technoligies. This DLC also okay.

And here comes the other two:

Phoenix Point - Legacy of the Ancients DLC: The good thing of the DLC is, you can get new technologies which gives you the most powerful weapons of the game, and they don't need ammo. Sound good right? But there is a catch. You need to find the technological informations about this weapons (usually this is a special mission site) which are the farest points of the world. So to acquire that infos, takes you a lot of time. After that in order to produce these weapons, you need special resources whic you don't have at the start of the game. To get these resources you have to build scanner droids and sent them to different points of the world. When they find a site, you need to fight with the guardians of the site which missions are very challangable. Then you can get to extract the resources, but time after time aliens start to attack that locations, so you need to defend them if you want the resource extraction continuity. To manufacture the weapons one site is not enough, you need much more, so this part of the DLC is very time consuming, and the "reward" I think is not worth it. And least but not last this DLC has a new monster, the Umbra. I mentioned at the first part of the review, about fighting mechanics where you can aim parts of the enemy. Well for the Umbra this is not valid. Because these monsters rise from the dead of the previously fallen enemy as a "zombie" and the game don't offer weakpoints about on them. So in order to prevent their menace you need to overwatch the dead bodies, or use flamethrower, to prevent their resurrection. In the last mission the alliens contantly spawning to defeat you, and most of them is Umbra, which makes the endgame very very hard...especially if you don't have flamethrower and start over the last mission four time to get the right team to beat the mission. So this DLC is bad. Makes the end game real hard, but offers a limited benefit.

Phoenix Point - Festering Skies DLC: The DLC introduces the Air combats! Yaaay! I loved them in the old X-COM:Ufo Defense. Well the developers not manages to implement this a good way. There is no oppurtinity to initiate air combat with alien air units when they are flying between landing points. What? But why? So you can only fight with Air units when they are in their base, or they are actually in your base (or any other human faction base) and bombarding it. That's kinda stupid. The combat system is very limited, the air untis don't have much unique ability. The good thing of this DLC is the best aircraft what you could get in the game. But I have to say, this is a late-midgame, or endgame technology. The bad thing is in this DLC is a lot of enemy air unit which could bombard the human bases (which will speed up the game over), and a new unique alien flying monster which could only defeatable if you have the ultimate endgame aircraft. So my recommendation is: No Festering Skies DLC, No Best Aircraft, But no alien ones as well, so you can play longer.

Summary: I have some concerns about this game. It has a lot of good ideas and game concepts, but as evolves it has more flaws than good things. I gave thumbs up for the base game and a HUGE thumbs down for the DLC!\t\t\t\t\t
