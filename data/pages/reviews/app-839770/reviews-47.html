Reading the reviews, it's clear that this game isn't that good, but I decided to give it a try due to sales and the turn-based itch. Unfortunately, the reviewers weren't wrong.

I never expect PP to be like Xcom, in fact I embrace whatever difference it provides, just like how I like Othercide(another game) offers a different take. The best thing about PP is that it isn't Xcom, it has different skills and ideas, but that's about it. The worst thing about PP is it isn't Xcom, it is everything Xcom but worse.
While some say it's difficult, difficulty is not the problem, the entire game is a SLOG.

If there is a category for the WORST ost, PP will take the cake absolutely. There is barely any music, with extremely slow ambience sound dropped here and there, Twinkle Twinkle Little Star has a better tempo than all the music in game. Look at Youtube playlist, it has 600+ views, because it's absolute trash, nobody will remember it's music after finishing the game. Whoever approves this music direction should be fired and never touch music again.

Mechanics: 5/10
I get the idea of having resource management, but PP just makes EVERYTHING more tedious. In PP, your bullets & bombs is FINITE. Your lvl 1 recruits come with 0 armors and weapons, and you must make them anew. If you get another tech weapons from battle, you cannot manufacture the ammo for it, because you haven't research it yet. Then your weapons can get destroyed, so yes, you can end up in a battle with no weapons. Then you need "store rooms" for those weapons. You can get higher level recruits on some faction's havens, but there are 70+ havens, and you have to select and find one by one. Your recruits are sent to the multiple 10+ bases across the world, and they cannot travel anywhere without airships... which you must also manufacture individually, while also building the bases, and making sure every recruits are equipped.

"Overwatch" now has to be targetted manually, yes MANUALLY. You have a directional cone that you must set, and only anything within the cone will be fired. Unfortunately, implementation is terrible, ans the smaller cone starts from the soldier and expands outwards, any enemy, even if it's in front of the soldier but 1mm outside the cone will not be fired upon. Soldiers also won't crouch and move away when you shoot on the same directions, which means if your overwatch cones overlapped, you might shoot each other.

Gameplay: 5/10
PP uses "parts targeting" which means you can target specific body parts to disable certain effects. This feature is actually nice as you can employ different tactics to dangerous situations, unfortunately, free-aiming is also a must, because the ai is so bloody stupid, that a point blank shot will be fully missed constantly even if your chances in free-aim was actually 100%. If your soldier is hiding behind a lamp post and you just use auto-fire, they will shoot that lamp post out of everything else. Free-aim also reduce all your accuracy to 50/50 no matter the percentage, unless the target is very close.

PP also uses a real ballistic system, which means every bullet is calculated. On paper it sounds nice, but in practice it is also terrible. First, a successful shot might just mean 1 bullet hits the enemy. Then it also render covers kinda useless. In X-com cover lowers your percentage in getting shot, in PP, due to free-aim, it just obscure you. So you can technically side step a bit to the side, free-aim their legs etc and it will still hit even in full cover, & enemies also do the same. So in order to be really safe, your cover needs to be out of line of sight.

Evolving enemies is a farce, it's just splitting enemies' skills and introducing them later so that you can keep fighting the same enemies again.

The map design is alright, but sometimes it's ridiculous. In a mission where you have to protect the civilians, in turn 1, enemies fly all the way across the map where you cannot reach in 1 turn, and 1 hit kill the civilian. So sometimes you have to restart the mission just to get a different placement because it can mean winnable and impossible.

The real difficulty here is not the combat, but the intense resource management and micromanagement. Remember that you manufacture every single armor to ammo, then you build facilities for every single base, and every single base attacked needs repairs etc. There is a certain way to play for victory, doing otherwise will definitely lead to loss. Why, because it's wholly unbalanced, play meta or die.

UI is garbage.

SUMMARY:
I could write another page for all the points but I won't bore you with it. TLDR: If there is a mechanic, PP will make it more tedious, nothing is streamlined. Every single feature you find in Xcom, you will find it here but inferior, there is not a single thing that PP does better than Xcom. If you have played Xcom 4 times and want to try something new like PP, play Xcom 5 times.
