\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly? One of the best games I've ever played. It's not very expansive, it doesn't have a lot of direction, and there isn't much to explore, especially without mods. And yet, I've never played a game as committed to its physics and mechanics, that allowed you to have so much control over what you build. It is nothing short of remarkable. If they gave a little more direction - not even missions or anything like that, but better scenarios with developed factions and more places to explore, then this game would be near perfect. And most anything that the game lacks is present in the incredible modding community. Mods sure don't make up for a company's mistakes in a game, but it absolutely makes the game that much more enjoyable, and Keen have been pretty helpful and encouraging with the modding community, especially compared to a lot of games of similar caliber. Overall, I don't think I've played a game with such great flaws yet such incredible strengths. Definitely worth the $20.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love/hate this game! I love it's features and the ability to build nearly anything you can imagine. That it has a creative mode that allows you to work out your designs before spending many hours building them in survival mode, which mode is great to have all on it's own (survival mode that is). There is so much to do in this game that I found myself spending several hours doing things when it felt like just a few minutes had passed. Which brings me to the part I hate about this game - the learning curve! Wow. It has a significant learning curve that in some areas seems like the "curve" is a straight line going up! I just want to take a moment and recognize the many players who have helped me get a handle on this game - even though they are not aware of doing so. Players like Beeblebum who made some tutorial videos you can access on YouTube. Also Splitsie who's tutorial videos that are now some 5 years old are still solid examples to follow and get into this game. Kanajashi has some very good example videos as well. There are MANY others who spend great effort and time to do this for new players, with great cost to themselves in time and effort (spending all that time in the game specifically to record demonstrations and then more time and effort in editing it all down so a new player can digest it) - I don't think these people get enough credit for all that they give of themselves, and that it's all for free on YouTube!

Once you get a handle on things, especially the simple things like knowing to hold the Control key while clicking the left mouse button in order to get the text input for things like specifying the number or percentage of the movement of a piston for example, I think it was Splitsie whom mentioned this in one of his videos. That was a game changer for me at that time! THANK YOU SPLITSIE! Where was I, oh yea, once you get a handle on things this game can really become an environment to get lost in. It's beautifully done, when you are immersed in the game time fly's by, as I said before, hours seem like minutes.

I have found one thing that I just don't get, maybe it's just me, but sometimes I get real heavy lag in my game, but only when I'm connected to the internet. Once I've disconnected the internet and play "offline" I never get that lag. Even though I tend to play in the games setting of "offline" as long as I'm actually connected to the internet at all it will eventually cause a lag in my game that requires me to exit and come back in, only to get that lag again in a few minutes. Never seems to happen if I'm physically disconnected from the internet.

I tend to write books when commenting - even when commenting in a YouTube channel, so I apologize for going on and on ... and on. Just get this game, find some tutorials on YouTube (the people I've mentioned have great and simple to follow ones) and get to becoming a great Space Engineer! (I'm still working on that last part myself - a long, long way yet to go, but that's just me.)\t\t\t\t\t
