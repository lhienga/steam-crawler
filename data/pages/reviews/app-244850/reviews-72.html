\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIve spent years looking for an open world game that lets me be freely creative with vehicles and bases...

Finally...

I Found it...

The controls are easy to remember and there is easy to follow tutorials that the game encourages you to watch when opening the game (this can be disabled).

It follows the same trend as most other survival games (spawn, gather basic resources, make crafting station, gather higher tier resources, build better crafting station, expand base) but this one is different, how you go around navigating around a planet or through space is decided by you, use your suit backpack or make a rover or even a spacecraft.

And that is where it gets better, rather than having premade vehicle models that you have to use, you get to build the vehicles yourself (or of course buy some basic ships from stations). this game lets you design and build whatever you want in a 'Scrap Mechanic' esque way, whatever idea you are inspired by can be brought to reality in space engineers.

There is some neat scenarios the game offers aswell as an open solar system in either Sandbox or Survial where you can do what you want.

Unlike most games there is no set 'border' to remain in, you can easily stay planetbound or go off into the deep depths of space far from contact.

Space Engineers supports multiplayer and has many servers that you can play on or even create a private session for your friends to join you on.

There is workshop support and a blueprinting system which allows you to share your creations with others and download the creations of other players.

Relationship status can be increased with factions by completing contracts for discounts in their store, or relationship status can be decreased by attacking ships belonging to the faction.

161 hours into this game (as of the time of this review) and I still picture me having hundreds more hours of entertainment

The possibilities this game offers are endless.\t\t\t\t\t
