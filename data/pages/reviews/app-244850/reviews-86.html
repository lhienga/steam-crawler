\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game knows what it is and is good at what it does.

If you're looking for a Minecraft-like game where you can sink thousands of hours into building colossal vehicles and heavy machinery block-by-block and then using it all to demolish the environment, this game will definitely scratch that itch.

This game also had way more opportunities for vehicle-to-vehicle combat than I was expecting, which was a pleasant surprise.

There is certainly a learning curve to the UI and controls, but the game points you to some good tutorial videos and there are two tutorial scenarios that give you a firm grasp of the basics.

It goes on sale pretty often, so if you see it 75% or even 50% off, I would recommend snagging it - even without the DLC, you'll definitely get your money's worth in terms of content.

EDIT:
Some tips for beginners doing their first single-player custom game on Survival mode:

Go to the Advanced Options:
1. Disable 'Block Destruction' (enabled by default) - collision damage is pretty nutty in this game, so if you leave this turned on, expect to crash and burn... a lot.
2. Set 'Meteor Impacts' to 'Armageddon' - meteor storms are actually a GOOD thing because they leave random ore deposits behind, some of which are normally extremely difficult to find such as Uranium and Platinum.
3. Do NOT disable 'Voxel Destruction!' Turning it off will prevent you from being able to mine resources!
4. Enable 'Spawn Wolves' and 'Spawn Spiders' if you want to be attacked by wild animals :D
(both are disabled by default)
5. Max out inventory size and the speed & efficiency settings for tools and equipment if you want the game to go faster.\t\t\t\t\t
