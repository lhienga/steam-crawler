\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: At its core this is a mix of Minecraft and TerraTech, except ridiculously grindy, much less fun than either and with one of the worst UIs somebody could have come up with. It's been around forever, has tons of paid DLC available but feels like a freshly released early access title with very little meaningful content.

- The survival aspect is less fun than cleaning your room, and a lot less scary.
- The combat system and AI in this game make Wolfenstein 3D look good.
- By far the most dangerous enemy in the game is Klang - a mysterious force emanating from the bugs in the physics engine.
- The exploration aspect is reminiscent of Desert Bus.
- The UI was specifically designed to cultivate patience, resolve and the ability to memorize meaningless recipes.
- One thing the game has going for it is realism: ever wanted Minecraft to limit your inventory to a single block of dirt instead of ridiculously allowing you to carry hundreds of them around in your pocket? Wish granted!
- I'd love to make a joke about the story, but nothing I could potentially come up with could beat simply playing the "action packed" "campaign"; which btw is still unfinished; and I'm not even talking "not finished", we're talking 5 "levels" that would be the start of the first part of the campaign and even those are buggy and incomplete. They take maybe 1h to play through and I feel I could have had more fun watching grass grow.

The basic gameplay loop is this:
1) Mine dirt for maybe 1h until you can make wind turbines, a forge and an assembler.
2) Wander the landscape aimlessly while holding your digging tool, until you get lucky and randomly find some colored dirt you need to make a bigger forge and a bigger assembler.
3) Mine even more colored dirt until maybe 10h later you can build a rig to help automate mining the dirt.
4) Unless you're an experienced player, your new rig will likely take 5 minutes to break and end up in an unrecoverable state where you'll need to spend a few hours more dismantling it, replacing any broken parts and putting it back together again; rinse and repeat.

I've only spent this many hours with this game because on paper it promises a lot of awesome things, and I'm a hopeless completionist. Sadly, in practice the game has so far failed to deliver on all fronts, and at that, it has failed miserably. Will probably try playing this again with mods, since that's supposedly a lot better, but I have serious doubts even fully modding the game can make it any reasonable amount of fun compared to the alternatives.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanna love this game, I REALLY do. I love the premise. I love the possibilities. However, I do not love the buggy physics engine. Every so often my rover goes flying away like it's team rocket with no rhyme or reason for it. I mean Bethesda level "Going going GONE!" as it tumbles and flies through the air with the greatest of ease. Going over completely flat terrain at 20 kmph? Roll dice to see if you're tail end suddenly shoots up into the air like that semi on Dark Knight. One time I was easing down a hillside and suddenly my vehicle went soaring away like a giant just punted it. Tumbling on in all 3 axis. And, of course, vehicle ended up upside down with my storage destroyed AGAIN. The first time it happened my vehicle was falling for a good two minutes.

No mods, solo game. Straight Vanilla. So it's not even that.

I don't mind spending lengthy amounts of time mining and gathering resources. I actually kinda enjoy it, especially as things start to come together. But having it just YANKED away needlessly and senselessly is too much. At least creepers have the common decency of hissing before blowing you off the planet. If I wanted to be brutalized I'd go play Dark Souls.\t\t\t\t\t
