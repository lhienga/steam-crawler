\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis is a love/hate game - at times I love it, and other times I hate it. It is confusing and buggy as ♥♥♥♥ - sometimes you just need to reboot the game for things to work, other times you'll go crazy trying to find that "needle in a haystack" that is preventing things from working. Survival mode is WAY too monotonous, and Creative mode is way too cheese. There's no real automation, and what automation there is is WAY too confusing - if you work really hard, you might be able to get your miner vehicle to automate going from Point A to Point B, but resources at Point B are limited. There is a 100 m/s velocity which is WAY too slow - you'll easily spend an hour (real time) doing nothing but flying from 1 planet to another - and you cant do anything else during that time, because the velocity will kill you if you get out of the chair. There are random, strange sounds like your ship being destroyed despite no enemies being around (bug?). The color-designation of an "off grid" piece ("burnt orange") is WAY too similar to a broken/incomplete piece ("crimson red"). NOTHING in this game is well described. There is no way to "hide" the massive number of pieces that require DLC, so if you havent bought dlc then scrolling through the "available" pieces is highly annoying as most are not actually available, but because the descriptions are so poor, you dont know what any of them do anyhow. For example, there is "light armor" block and "heavy armor" block, "Large Thrusters" and "Thrusters"... and they want you to "engineer" stuff ("ships") without knowing the weight difference between light or heavy, or thrust power between thrusters, and that's not even getting into the whole large vs small block discussion, which doesn't always seem to work right.

There are parts of this game that are truly amazing. Building a ship and flying into space, with the sun and other planets, is really a good feeling. Getting something new to work, and constantly trying to make working things better is also really cool. I've heard, but haven't done, that you can actually import C code and run it in the game - but given how difficult even the most basic thing is in this game, I will probably never get there.\t\t\t\t\t
