\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou have to enjoy problem solving. Very steep learning curve.

Did I mention it has a very steep learning curve. Not hard, just not obvious. Once you learn what to look for, it is easy to stay alive, but it isn't always obvious what you need to keep things running to stay alive.

The "Help" (F1) files are almost worthless, nothing personal developers, but having to watch 30 minutes of help video to learn what you key word searched, is not helpful. They need to develop 30 sec videos with links to bigger context videos. Key word videos, 30 seconds long telling you how to i.e. "recharge", "repair", "deconstruct", "mine", "how to label a build", "How to create a GPS point, and why", Explanation video of each item in the game that has mechanics (short - videos) explaining what each control does, or create better mouse overs.

As you become more familiar with the game, the game goes from very difficult, to hard, and then to challenging. I feel it will always remain challenging, because you need to think like an engineer to thrive in the game. You have to enjoy problem solving. You have to enjoy overcoming problems, and if you enjoy pushing the limits of things -- you will love this game.

Organization skills optional, but highly beneficial.

This is a challenging game, but very enjoyable for the builder, problem solver. If you enjoy survival games, you might enjoy this one, just keep in mind, it has a very steep learning curve -- even if you don't like problem solving, you might enjoy helping your more technical friends play the game, and then enjoy breaking the toys they build for you in the game. =D

Developers, Good Job (I have owned the game a couple of weeks tops, and I have 100 hours of actual game play.)

You might find it less painful to learn in creative mode, then blue print your builds to take them to the scenarios you want to explore.\t\t\t\t\t
