\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game, and I would recommend it if you enjoy RTS and space-based games. It's very themeic, endless customisation, and overall a pretty cool game.

One major gripe I have with it is the Warscore system. It's essentially this: you have two 'civs', Civ A versus Civ B. Civ A is a colossal, multiplanetary, robot-driven, hivemind empire. It hates organics, and wants to purge the galaxy. Civ B is a smaller, democracy-based, pacifist empire. Easy game, really.

But in the declaration of war that Civ A has to sign, they have to state the terms of this war. I'm not sure why (aside from game-balance), but this purging, killer robot has to state which planets he wants to take and strip of life. So he does: all of them.

However, that's too much! See, each planet has a certain currency of "warscore" behind it. You can only afford small amounts. So, the killer-genocide machine says "Ok, I'll take...5 planets." he can afford 5.

He does it with ease, and at the end of the war, Civ B accepts surrender, and cedes the planets. Now, it'd be even easier to take Civ B, but the war system has pulled another neat trick: A PEACE TREATY FOR 10 YEARS PRIOR TO THE WAR!

So, while the game lets you create a hivemind, genocide machine, who's mission it is to purge organic life, and can't even hold diplomacy with them, it also tells you that while this machine has no respect for organic life, it does have respect for:
-How many planets they "should" take at a time- wouldn't want to let this end too quickly now, right?
-Peace treaties, because while we are purging their population on other planets, it'd be barbaric to declare war again!

It's a very silly system for war, but also a big system in the game. You can get perks to make things cheaper, but even with all that, it still takes too much warscore for you to Blitzkreig an entire civilization off the galaxy.

7/10- good but the warscore system is quite silly\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe main issue to overcome here is literally just user-error, I promise, this will become more apparent the longer you play, be it construction of a vehicle, or construction of a mod list and so-on, always ponder: am I doing this **** right?

Currently running 305ish mods in conjunction with each other, it all be functioning smoothly for single-player/multi-player, just so people get a rough idea of how far you can push it, the star system still loads up in 2-3 minutes and has barely any errors.

Takes the term "sandbox game" to a new level, scripts are your friend and there are a lot of user-created ones that work and will make your lives 1000% easier.

This is NOT a game to play without using mods, you are just reducing the overall experience you could be having by leaving out countless features that enhance the game.

If it doesn't exist then you can make it yourself and add it, there are a plethora of tools available to make this easier, as well.

The modding community for this is unlike any other, truly out of this world, endless support and tutorials for everything you could ever need; modelling, textures, in-game scripting help, ect... All available through discord servers steam guides and github, where people are usually available to answer any questions.

I'll save you some time and effort and just say, if something requires another mod to run, it needs to be ABOVE that mod in your mod list for singleplayer/multiplayer.
As an example, if something requires weaponcore to run, you need to put that mod ABOVE weaponcore in the list. Not everyone gets that memo and I feel like it is genuinely one of the most important things that I learned far too late.
