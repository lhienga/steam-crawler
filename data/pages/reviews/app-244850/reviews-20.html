\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile it might say have almost 60 hours in this game, that is just on PC, but together I have over 200+ hours in this game on all platforms. This game is the game I have been playing for over 7 months I think, but the most of it is the last 3 months. I LOVE THIS GAME BUT I do have a few things that I have a problem with. First I think the PCU on consoles is too low, even at its max, but I understand that the processing power on consoles is not the same as a computer, and that Space Engineers was made for PC at first and was not ORIGINALLY made for console.

My second issue is that the Turret AI is dumb in this game. I cannot tell you how many times my ships have gotten holes and damage from my own guns. Not only that, but another problem is with the turrets aiming distance. It is currently capped at 800 meters, which is not a bad distance, but in capitol ship battles that I think most people would love to do, it is pretty pathetic, especially like Artillery and Assault turrets have a maximum distance of 2000 and 1200 meters!

My third and final problem is the weird physics engine. Like when I made a extending crane using pistons, it EXPLODED and did a lot of damage to nearby grids. I realize today that is due to Clang, the Space Engineers god, but in all seriousness, there are a lot of times that I have made stuff just for it to blow up, crunch itself, or damage nearby grids. So I would LOVE to see Keen fix these glitches, but that will probably is not going to happen any time soon.

But there are plenty of things I love about this game. In fact, there are so many things if I were to list them, this review would take 30 minutes to read, so I will just list a few. First I love the ability to play with others. This game would be empty and boring within 6 hours of play. Second I love the way they make the game set in the near distance future, which it is set in 2077, but I don't think will get to where they think we will get in time, but it makes it fun without fancy technology (Looks at Jump Drive in a annoyed way). Finally, the sandbox part makes it feel like playing with Lego ships and building them with ease. Then you can blow them up in the end! Just make to sure blueprint it before you do that though.

Anyway just get this game if you like space, creativity, and fun.\t\t\t\t\t
