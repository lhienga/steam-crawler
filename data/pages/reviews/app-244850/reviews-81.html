\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts really, really bad. If you only ever play games in creative mode you might like it. I thought with a survival mode there would be some structure, but it just feels like an empty sandbox. I like space settings, I like building games, but I also like having something to do, some sense of purpose. This game just doesn't have that. I hesitate to even call it a game.

Getting started is a complete mess. The tutorial videos are tedious and not very helpful. Far too long on camera and movement, then jumping to quick select, but I don't need to know how to setup my toolbar if you haven't covered what blocks or tools are yet. Survival mode feels like an afterthought so don't expect much help there. If you try jumping right into the game, maybe learning as you play, just expect disappointment.

If you try to jump right into the intro to survival scenario, they do give you some tasks to help you learn. Unfortunately about half will get stuck on the first task. This happens if they go to oxygen first (closest task on the left). You will be tasked to refill your oxygen, but you cannot. See the base starts with an incomplete conveyor block and until that block is built you cannot refill oxygen. Instructions on your first block repairs are a task slightly further away. The specific block build needed isn't even covered at all. At least if you go to the closet task on the right first to refill health and energy it mentions that it isn't connected to oxygen.

Its really annoying because it doesn't take much thought to come up with a better intro scenario. Let me arrive on a ship bringing the parts for the base, build a starter base to learn about basic construction, power, oxygen, mining, refining, while the ship provides a safe starting environment and supplies. Then teach me about ship construction with a little mining vessel. If you imagine anything like this is in the game you are mistaken.

If you instead play First Jump you will realize how they things you might build, might be incorporated into some kind of really light scenario. Its a really short and light scenario and serves as a crude intro to controls. It will at least actually explain how to repair a block when you need to. It will also introduce you to combat and just how bad that is. Though don't be surprised when it asks you to fight or run early on and you realize it hasn't even introduced fighting yet. How can you be teaching at the level of how to move and interact, turn on lights, and then forget you never mentioned picking up, equipping or firing a gun.\t\t\t\t\t
