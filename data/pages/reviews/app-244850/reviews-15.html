\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly let me say, Space Engineers is a great game, if you like builder style games. I've been playing it since before planets were added, and things have changed a lot. It was a great game in the pre-release days, and its a great game now, but it is a different game. Keen, not unreasonably has been focusing itself on making money of late, which is not a bad thing, but it means rather than making a gamers game, as was, its now the sort of thing that gets published and sells well, which is different. Gone is the wild west approach where the priority was modding and getting the best, most functional, whilst stable gameplay (mods and scripts using the programming block, used to really make the game, now they're 'experimental' and a considered fringe). Keen's priority is following the money, X-Box and Playstation, with multi-player servers, rather than come as you are PCs with massive GPUs and lots of RAM. Keen's regular return to the market place, to sell more DLC is understandable, and some of the new stuff is great, but it adds fluff, not substance. Hence they game now has limitations to make it reliable and playable on lesser platforms, and that to my mind makes it less of the game it was. Its not difficult, if x2 reasonable productive people are playing in the same world, and making decent sized bases. to max out the PCU/Drill count and hit limits. Those limits can be extended, but then you're into 'experimental' mode. Similarly There are a lot of game functions missing in the vanilla game, automatic solar panel / station orientation is trivial with a programming block, but not in vanilla. Guidance controls, trivial with a PB, not not in vanilla. Using those LCDs to actually display useful info about cargo capacity or station status, a simple script, but not without PBs. Better control of the Conv system... you get the idea. Still a fantastic game, but starting again having played it before it left early access, its a lesser game than it was (and Experimental mode just feels a LOT less stable than it was).

So in short, buy the game, its good. Buy the DLCs they keep Keen in the game, but also keep fingers crossed that the VRAGE rewrite thats coming, will address the issues with how to do Ore scanning in multi player, and how to allow a PB script that can't kill the servers, such that more of the cool stuff, that has been considered detrimental to the marketability of the game, can come back into vanilla, as that WAS the best bit.\t\t\t\t\t
