\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpace Engineers offers an enthralling open universe where the possibilities seem as endless as space itself. This game, with its boundless opportunities for exploration and creation, has become a favourite of mine. Here's my detailed review, delving into what makes it a remarkable experience, as well as some of its downsides.

The Good:

Exploration and Creativity: Space Engineers thrives on the open world it presents. With a vast universe to explore, you'll never run out of places to go or things to do. Whether you're flying through space, building bases on moons or ice asteroids, or crafting colossal space stations to house your fleet of smaller ships, the game encourages unbridled creativity and adventure.

Multiplayer Delight: The multiplayer experience is where Space Engineers truly shines. Teaming up with friends to create, explore, and even indulge in comical antics is a joy. Watching your friends' characters flop around in zero-gravity while using jet-packs adds a whimsical element that keeps the game lighthearted and entertaining.

Robust Game Creation Settings: Space Engineers offers an extensive suite of customisation options, making it perfect for those who prefer a non-combat focus. You can tailor the game to your liking, enhancing the creation and expansion aspects of your journey.

Optimisation: Despite years of development, Space Engineers remains impressively stable and polished. Even on a mid-range PC, the game runs smoothly. It's a testament to the developers' commitment to optimising the experience for a wide range of systems. Notably, the game also runs flawlessly on Linux, even on unsupported systems, thanks to compatibility with Proton 8.

The Not-So-Good:

Price and DLCs: The game's initial price of £15.50 is reasonable given its calibre. However, the main issue arises with the plethora of DLCs available. While these add cosmetic items and support the developers, the cost can quickly add up. Some players may prefer purchasing the complete game with all DLCs or none at all. With the extensive list of DLCs, it can result in an additional cost of £55.86, potentially even more if more DLCs are introduced. This might be a deterrent for some players, even those deeply enamoured with the game.

Conclusion:

In summary, Space Engineers is a game I would wholeheartedly recommend to anyone looking for an exciting, creative, and multiplayer-friendly experience. With over 100 hours played, I still find myself captivated by its universe. If you have friends to share this journey with, it elevates the experience even further.

When it comes to DLCs, they are optional, and your enjoyment of the game can be complete without them. Personally, I'm selective about the DLCs, choosing those that offer functional items. However, the choice of whether to purchase them or not is entirely yours.

Ultimately, if you decide to embark on this space-faring adventure, I hope you have as much fun as I have had with this game. Game on and explore the expanses of space to your heart's desire.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpace is ok, the planetary side of things is a total mess.

Features that should be baked in are left up to the community to fix by scripting.

Want to make a rover with an adjustable height? Sorry the wheels no longer talk to the ship when on pistons. There's a script for that but braking isn't possible. Not to mention that attaching one wheel suspension to a piston if fine, then trying to attach another one further along the craft just will not allow you to orient it properly.

Nix the extending suspension, and just build a non adjustable height rover? You better avoid slopes because the rover then loves to runaway going downhill, then flip over, or alternatively rip out the under carriage and explode. Going up hill is no better.

Do an atmospheric flying miner you say? Good luck staying level. Trying to mine while flying is frustrating. Bump the wrong pixel, and flip over tumble and explode.

If you like spending hours researching why things don't work the way you expect maybe give it a try. I regret not only the purchase, but the time spent trying to make sense of the mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to review this at 100 hours of game but it felt I knew too little.
At 200 hours I can say I explored most of the immediate game potential.
I say immediate because if you want, it can last thousand hours if you get into timers, programmable and AI blocks and so on.

Basically the game is pointless, there is raw materials and tools and stuff to refine it. Once you get that in the first hours of play, i'ts up to you where you want to go.
There is no story beside some short pre-scripted missions in single player only.

You can build whatever you want, then go in space and colonize asteroids or build flying space stations or battle fortresses.

They put into it some REALLY CLEVER buid blocks like hinges, rotors, timers, merging blocks, blueprints of the stuff you build and printers to build it back and others that allow you to build machines yourself like automatic drilling platforms or whatever. The use of these blocks really makes this games for those who want to actually engineer solutions to problems and invent weird stuff.

That said, the game it's pretty fun, expecially with friends, if you like the pointless building of stuff after you grasp all the game mechanics and stuff to get there (which for me, even it's a steep learning curve, it was major part of the fun).

Now to the dark side.
The devs are a little lazy about community bug feedback and there are some nasty bugs around since years and reported multiple times which aren't getting solved. Lot of requests by the community doesn't get attention and the game suffers from crashes from time to time and you will soon or later resort to the modding community, which is the real strength after the dev work, because it's enormous and well done. lot of people liked this game and added a ton of mods for it to do almost anything.

For the discounted price it's almost a thievery, this game is really good and bearing the developer problems it's annoying but the game is so good that you will keep playing anyway\t\t\t\t\t
