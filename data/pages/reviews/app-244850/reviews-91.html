\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game but the lack of content makes it hard to keep playing. Sure I get to make any kinds of vehicles or contraptions I want with a wide range of blocks to flex my creativity and problem solving, but SE does not provide many obstacles for me to tackle with my builds. There's no reason for me to build a battleship or program an auto missile. There's no reason for me to build an orbital satellite or explore endlessly into space with an exploration cruiser. There's no goal, no enemy to fight - really there's nothing much to do BUT design and build.

If that's your cup of tea, SE will provide endless fun with many many opportunities to spend long hours fretting over minor details and the function of certain blocks. You can do the math and figure out how much thrust you need to launch a certain load weight into orbit, and you can build and design anything you can dream up of. But if you want to apply these things - well that's where this game falls short.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't really play the game a lot back when I got it in 2016, but as time has gone on I've really become more interested and invested into this game by it's incredible modding community. There are so many core mods that fundamentally change and add so much to this game, and thinking about it now, Space Engineers is really only kept active by the hard work of people tackling poorly optimized systems to turn this game into what it likely should have been. The base game itself would be okay if this game was marketed as a building simulator, but it is almost entirely just building. Nothing else. It's shown as an exploration and problem solving game, but the only problems are what you create for yourself and the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ server lag. There's no real depth or excitement beyond building things. It would be a long dead pile of abandonware by now without the Workshop.

Modders have given this game a full fledged AI rewrite with dynamic difficulty and unique encounters with hundreds of custom made ships and stations, a much needed combat system overhaul, custom worlds and much more three dimensional challenges. Not to mention the insurmountable quantity of custom blocks, items, scripts, scenarios, and downright awesome builds. SE is MADE by mod makers at this point, and has been for years now.

And now most of the largest core systems that the modding community needs has been turned off arbitrarily, with no warning, betas, or any other kind of assistance. I know its entirely within Keen's right to make such changes, however it really is a fat spit in the face to all the mod makers and enjoyers to make a rushed, half baked pile of garbage "celebration" in the face of all the effort the community has gone to realize the game Keen couldn't, or wouldn't provide. These people are the reason you are still turning a profit for ♥♥♥♥ sakes! Sorry, you can't have your community-made content that generates no direct revenue to us, but you can have two blocks (one of which is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ bugged as I'm writing this of course) and a flare gun. Also you can spend $2 to buy two skins they gave for free years ago and one random new one. Cosmetics. Asking for money as an anniversary. Honestly, modders should be asking them for money for the amount of stuff they put up with, especially when it comes to arbitrary changes.

The route Keen has taken on this unfinished game has been like a mobile game. "Here, a semi-working product, now lets add more paid cosmetic blocks than there are in the base game." This is what they've done, and it's getting old. There is still so little optimization for the kind of gameplay that was envisioned for this game, that modded singleplayer is really the only way to get good performance and large ship battles together.

There has been videos and demonstrations of that a Space Engineers 2 would look like, and while it seems interesting and has some improvements over things like voxels in game, I honestly don't think I'll be picking it up if something like it ever releases from Keen. Much to how other people predict, I too believe they would likely squander it like they have done this game.

There was a lot of potential in SE's vision, and I truly do enjoy this game, but it really is getting worse and worse as time goes on.

Keen, please fix. -Mynxellon\t\t\t\t\t
