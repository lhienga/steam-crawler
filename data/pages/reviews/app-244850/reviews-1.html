\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 1887 hours spread out over almost six months, I think I finally got to a point where I can take a break from Space Engineers. I have never been this engrossed in a game, and I have been gaming since Pong.

I don't generally enjoy the combat in this game, I am more interested in building. My first game after the tutorials was Solar System Survival, where I started on one of the non-earthlike planets with a tiny pod to sustain me, and then gradually mined, built up my base, built a small grid mining ship, a couple rovers and finally a large grid ship that would take me to space, and then to the earth-like planet, where I would build a more permanent base and recover from my harrowing survival experience.

I enjoyed this so much I did it again, but after the second landing on earth I turned around and build a space base as well. Thinking I was done, I was reading about doing contracts, and found that it was possible to buy ships from NPC bases. So I put together a Atmos/Ion ship designed to fly while doing contracts, and pretty soon I have a collection of rovers and ships, and spent a lot of time ferrying these new vehicles from the other side of the planet to either my ground base or my space base.

Then I was thinking, this game would be more fun if there was a way to find wrecked ships as I wandered around, and sure enough, I found an MES Wrecks mod that would spawn random wrecks. So I started a new survival game with this mod enabled, and got myself to earth with a few interesting rebuilt ships.

Then I went back to my second game and enabled the mod, and proceeded to collect even more ships and rovers, fixing the wrecked vehicles I found along the way.

Finally, I hit a point where I had so many ships clustered around my space base (more than 100), that the game started to lag, hence, break time.

I love this game, it's amazing. So many different ways to play, different ways to build ships. I am not done yet, this I know, but I've got to play a few other games before I come back and figure out where my second space base is going to be.

The only cons (really minor cons):

Very little character customization, I know these sort of games don't always have much of that, but it kind of lessens the immersion for me

Most of the blocks can be painted, but a lot of the blocks that have uses other than armor also have yellow bits, I'd like to be able to change that yellow to something else. There might even be a mod for this, couldn't stop playing long enough to look it up.

HIghly recommended.
