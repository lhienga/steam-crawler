\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have a lot of hours in the game, most of which was prior to the Planets update. The game is really fun, however trying to play online is a troublesome experience. Even the official servers have a problem where when they restart (every 6 hours) they don't say anything, and you lose all progress 30 minutes prior to the restart.

Also, don't even try building underground. It works fine, up until you and your friends re load the world and everytime it takes more and more time to load, waiting as long as 20 minutes for it to stop streaming each time

There is also a huge lack of actual things to do, and since building underground or any sizeable ship just creates server lag for anyone near it, it doesn't leave you with much to do


Me and my group of about 5 people, spent about a month and a half playing on a server for a week, and then the server just is slowly riddled with long load times, randomly losing ships, desyncing, crashing and more.

We also ended up just renting our own server, which was working great initially! However again, building underground even just a little bit creates a ton of issues, and it seems the longer the server is up, the more random bugs, player disconnects and more that happen. Essentially tricking you into investing a bunch of time into the game, only to be essentially unplayable.

Its cool for spawning big stuff in on solo and crashing stuff though. It has its place, but you end up losing literally days and days of progress from super common bugs. Games like 7 days to die you can build underground no problem, never have any of these types of issues. Space Engineers is just sort of an empty game, which seems like they left it up to the modding community to add real content to the game

The developer certainly has put some effort into things, however it still seems like an early access game. And sadly enough, the game seemed to have less game breaking issues 10 years ago, prior to adding planets\t\t\t\t\t
