The game was great for building. KSWH was, at first, brilliant. They cared, and did their best.
The game is still that great creative building game... but it has been abandoned. In 10 years of ongoing updates since release, more if you include early acces, and even with all DLC, the game still feels dead.
The recent AI update feels like a barebones tech demo at best.

For anything other than a creative building mode, you need mods, or a server environment, which is horribly imbalanced because...

Originally, the first two DLCs were purely cosmetic. This was, at the time, declared to be intentional, designed start to finish with no multiplayer imbalance to ever come from owning the DLCs. The problem is that the rest have all had functional architecture, so called "cosmetic" blocks that are in fact extremely functional in PVP or server environments, giving more compact designs, sleek and more easy to hide, and more defensive due to smaller hitboxes, with less emissives or light sources to identify with in dark spaces (everywhere but dayside surface on planets.)
Worse, they were specifically catered to that exact environment, the combat and pvp updates in particular had the worst of these blocks despite being targeted toward those groups, including defensive firing positions that fully defend the shooter. There's no way it was designed to keep balance with the non-dlc players in any form.

In addition to the lack of updates in general, when they do release "free" updates, they're alongside DLC and only done in attempt to get people coming back and buying.

For reference, I play PDX games. I love my DLC-adjacent updates, and in case of PDX I agree they have quite aggressive DLC policies, but their updates.. well, they actually have updates at all, and both normal and dlc-adjacent updates are extremely solid. Plus, any content owned by the host can be used by all players.

SE has no changes except for DLC-adjacent 'free' updates, and compared to the beginning, they're barely a shadow and a laugh at what the developers once were, while server environments require every individual to own each DLC to utilise them.\t\t\t\t\t
