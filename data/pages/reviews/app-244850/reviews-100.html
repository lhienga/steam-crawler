\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSE is a game that doesn't really have an end goal. It's like Minecraft in space with voxels.
The ability to create is incredible, and more gets added constantly.
Much like Minecraft and maybe Skyrim though, this game is elevated by its mod community.
SE will give you some hilarious moments, and a way to create.

However, there are some downsides.
The base game doesn't have all the content (mainly parts and skins) that the DLC has, and if you want to design anything worth a look, you will want the DLCs. They are fairly priced though, not P2W, and most importantly, will not stop you from playing from someone who does / not have them.

The physics engine is pretty good, though weird clangy stuff still happens, so save often.

PVE still doesn't really exist without mods. It's coming, I think. But until then I suggest running mods like MES, and don't expect the world. Mods will give enough to tide you over, but we're all waiting for Keen implemented PVE.

Brings me to Keen - one of the few dev teams I've seen who actually listen to what their playerbase says about their game. How far this game has come in a few years is partially thanks to the listening ear Keen gives us. I know they gotta add stuff that makes them money, I have zero issues with it, but the adding requested things and general bug fixing is great.

It's still a bit difficult to get into the game at first. There's a bit of a wall you have to climb before you know enough to make what you want happen. It's not inherently bad, but the game could do with a "New players start here, we got a guided walkthrough" section like many other games have.

All in all, worth my 1200 hours. I bought before planets were even released and there was enough to keep me interested. Nowadays there's just more and more to do and make with all the new parts they add. You get some for free every update, and if you don't mind $5 here or there every couple months, you get a lot more.\t\t\t\t\t
