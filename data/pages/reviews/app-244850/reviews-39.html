Utilizing mods on PC is a great way to expand the game an add.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt still feels like the buggy early access it was 10 years ago.

A TON of the basic content is locked behind paid DLC. The kind of stuff that would be included in any other base builder. I really resent how much you're advertised to while playing a paid-for game. You'll be reminded of all the features you're missing every time you choose a block to build and see that 90% of them are faded out and say "buy our DLC". It takes much longer to find anything because you have to wade through all of these advertisements to find the blocks you actually can build. I would expect this from a free-to-play game.

The "survival" mode is extremely lacking compared to any other survival game, if you come for that aspect you will be disappointed.

Be prepared to scour online forums every time something isn't working. If the shapes of blocks aren't sufficiently connecting, even if it looked like they snapped together, it will create a whole different "grid" and nothing will be connected. You can only fix this by building a specialized merge block to dock them back together.

Inventory is incredibly small, which makes the game tedious until you have a small vessel for every purpose, mining, refining, manufacturing, ground transport, air transport, etc. Managing inventories is tedious also.

Basically the game becomes fun once you've beaten the game, and have conveyors between everything and it's all automated. I would suggest it only if you want to play in creative and build stuff without worrying about resources, and even then you really have to buy some DLC for the interesting stuff.

In 2024 there are simply lots of games that do this better. Satisfactory, Factorio, Empyrion, 7 Days to Die, etc.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; There is a ton to do if you like building machinery/vehicles. However, once the sheen of sci-fi lego game has worn off you are stuck with a bunch of machines with no solid purpose.

This review is a Yes, BUT*. This means that if I had to say yes or no, I would recommend it. I have had tons of fun with trial and error building space ships, ground vehicles, weapon systems, and even entire building complexes. There are a ton of block variations, affordable DLC, community support, and so many possibilities for building the sci-fi space scene of your dreams. Playing with friends is highly recommended due to reasons listed below, but even building mining rigs to gather materials is a good time as you make and break a handful of designs before landing on "the one".
However, aside from the base AI ships there is not much life to the universe. Your vehicles serve no purpose other than that which you give them. They have guns in the game, but I can't say I've encountered any PvE purpose to use them. The in game economy seemed built with player-trading in mind, but that goes out the window when anything and everything can be built/harvested.
In the future, I hope Space Engineers comes along with a Mission Board that gives transport missions which require you to build a vehicle to complete them. Maybe you only have so many in game hours to transport 200k of Iron Ore to make contract. Or bounty hunting contracts. Just some filler so the world doesn't fill so empty.
