\r\n\t\t\t\t\tConvoluted.
The interface and workstations are unclear for refinement and assembly.
I suspect that with MANY hours, I could muck through the game's intentions and learn how to do things.
I do see the community is very helpful, and super supportive of new players. That gave me hope!

However, even with nice people around, a game should not feel like working a second job with mandatory study hall and research time on the weekends.

This is just a sandbox style builder game with some physics.
Speaking of physics, flight controls and navigation are also not intuitive, and less than responsive.
Again, just a very sloppy game. Not fun, hard to learn, goes no where.
My leisure time was more like frustration.
If what you like to do in your free time is get frustrated by non-intuitive games while friendly people tell you it just takes time and cheer you on in social media? Give this a try.

Whatever your preference, I highly recommend watching some gameplay content on Yewt Oob to see some of the cool vehicles the community has built! It shows you the 'end game' perspective, which helped inform my decision to NOT dedicate much time into learning this game.

Sorry, the game markets well but game play is awful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo many newbie traps it makes me want to refund it, but by the time I got that mad I'd already played well over 2 hours. If you do buy it, do so to play with friends to teach you the ropes. If you are going in solo do yourself a favor, don't select the first scenario for your first game unless you like having "access denied" spammed at you every time you try to do something, effectively trapping you in the first room because presumably you don't know how to hack doors because you just started playing. First Jump for a tutorial on basic stuff but you still might want a guide on how to actually build things or to tool around in creative mode.

Do a custom game. Turn off progression unless you want to build a bunch things then grind them down just to farm up 'research'. You can't change game settings on a saved game unless it is creative mode, be aware of this. Save frequently, save so many back ups, do not try to ironman it. I built a lot without saving, 95% built my first vehicle that I fussed over for about an hour and a half, then when I left to go check something out it disappeared. There's a trash cleaning system that will do this if it is unpowered or whatever, you can change that in the admin settings, I haven't gotten around to trying it out because I just lost several hours of progress reloading my last save hoping that at least some of my admitted overly ambitious first vehicle would still be there and am sick of this.

I'll probably delete this review later when I try again FROM SCRATCH but I am so blindingly mad at the moment.\t\t\t\t\t
