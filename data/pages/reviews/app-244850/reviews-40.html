\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI may not have the most hours out there but just these couple hundreds have been enough to completely hook me. I've always been a fan of sandboxes - sure, but this one just clicked for me.
The game is buggy, the devs are doing odd things with the game, the building system is objectively hard to get used to - but the game is extremely fun. I put in the hours and now i'm genuinely enjoying playing on my own little world, expanding my frontiers having started from nothing.

Look this game is not for everyone, but i must admit i'm happy i was introduced to it and i wanna see what else i can come up with.

For those considering buying the game, a small list of pros and cons i've encountered so far:
Pros:
- art direction is really cool, i love the near-future aesthetic, the weird industrial and blocky look, something you'd see on real missions, something tough and easy to manufacture.
- vehicles are extremely fun to both build and drive, yes the speed can be limiting - (you can mod it to be higher tho don't go too crazy or physics die lmao) - but there's something so sattisfying to driving around on planets or cruising through space.
- it can genuinely be immersive. it puts you in the shoes of some engineer out there being deployed into a barren planet to settle and expand to their heart's content, but how you achieve it and the genuine depth there is to it, it's super fun (for me). driving a truck around, hauling materials from point a to point b, crusing through space in your giant ship, checking for fuel consuption, seeing that everything is alright.. it's fun for me.
- sound design is honestly really good. some sounds can and will become annoying but i genuinely enjoy the sounds of menus and most of all ships, all the clicks and clacks, the rumble and the almost sloppy sounding ships just make it feel more real.
- it's super moddable. there's obviously limits but all the sorta stuff you might want there's probably a mod for it.

as cons:
- it's not so well optimised, you may need to crank the graphics way lower than your PC might be able to handle. which can make it loose a lot of what makes it so good looking.
- it's very bugy at times, some things might ♥♥♥♥ you up *big time* and completely break an otherwise fantastic immersion.
- DLC bloat, a lot of the great looking assets are locked behind a paywall, you can grab a selection of them while on sale but it can be a bit predatory. I personally bit the bullet, i can't live without my pretty blocks.
- steep learning curve. as much as i want to apreciate the depth and sheer expansiveness of the game and what it lets you do, i don't feel it's *as well* presented. there is a learning to survive scenario but the fact it requires a step by step guide probably tells you it's not good, if you really need it splitsie has some GREAT guides to get you started on most of the game's basic mechanics.
- air leaks. god i hate how inconsistent the game can be with them. not much to say just do something about it keen lol.\t\t\t\t\t
