\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt breaks my heart to rate this DLC negatively, but imho there's just not enough here to warrant a 15 buck purchase. Once again, I wanted to support developers of a great game and pay full price for this DLC. Once again, I was supremely disappointed.

Let's see:

There's a new mission: You need to find some guy who threatens everyone with vines, but of course there's more.
- You have around 7 or 8 new maps, although only 3 or 4 provide actual new story content. The rest are just some scrap collecting maps. There's also about 5 or 6 rehashed old maps populated with new enemies and new "loot".
- The new loot is pretty boring. I only got 1 hat and 2 weapons or so out of the whole DLC, the rest is junk. By the end of the main game you already have the most powerful weapons completely upgraded, so there's really not much you can do here. I got a new silent automatic rifle, a needle gun and some equipment. There's no new weapon types, the new weapons aren't that much better, the new equipment is okay-ish. Problem is, that you get almost all of that stuff via the rehashed maps where you have to kill 1 or 2 named NPCs to get to the stash, as well as their entire entourage in many cases.
- The new character has 2 or so unique mutations. That's pretty much it. No real reason to use him. My Dux-Bormin-Farrow squad was more than adequate.
- You get the "new" mutations pretty much after 30 minutes of gameplay in this DLC (that takes around 5 or 6 hours, depending on how much you actually explore. If you only follow the story missions, because you don't need to upgrade anything anyway then it's maybe 2 hours. These "new" mutations are just very few upgrades of existing abilities. You have so many DNA points, but nothing to do with them. I think my guys were level 100 or so at the end and each had like 30 points left...but were fully upgraded.
- There's not really any new upgrade items in the shops.
- A few new artifacts with which you can again upgrade existing traits, but other than the grenade upgrade I found all of them to be extremely bland.
- No new enemies really. Even those just got "upgraded" to plant versions, but they pretty much do the same thing...maybe got a few hp more or so...not sure. The only really new "enemy" is the spawner pod that spawns new and ever more dangerous enemies every 2 rounds of combat, but if you play the game as is intended you won't have to deal with them much other than in the final mission and some fight here or there.
- The final fight is just a massive difficulty spike, since you HAVE to engage the enemy head-on pretty much. Not a lot of chances for ambushes. And then you have to deal with 2 spawners as well as 2 tanks and around half a dozen or more other smaller enemies at the same time. Prepare your medkits.

To sum this DLC up: It's just more of the same, a rather bland extension of the main game that wasn't really thought through all that much. And it's red. Yeah. Red plants. That's the theme. It doesn't advance the story in any meaningful way and doesn't add anything interesting to the game. What would have been interesting:
- More varied enemies that require new tactics
- New items and weapons that revamp gameplay or give new options or simply make your characters stronger
- New traits and mutations that give you more options, instead of just marginal upgrades
- A story that actually continues the main game, instead of introducing a completely new and ultimately irrelevant threat and nothing gets resolved in the end

Disappointed, sadly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough Mutant Year Zero (the main game) is one of the best games i have played (which i have explained my feelings here: https://steamcommunity.com/id/leylifer/recommended/760060/ ), the DLC: Seed of Evil is mainly a disappointment for me.

More of the same of base game could be enough to justify buying a DLC. But the story of Seed of Evil looks very dull and boring and it imitates the main game too much. Even the ending video of Seed of Evil (which i stopped playing at the last fight and watched from Youtube) is nearly the same of the main game.

While playing the original game, the skill tree began losing its meaning at late game and at Seed of Evil, it's just obsolete. You have many skill points that don't add anything to the game. The enemy gets stronger but you don't, because there are few new weapons and no new skills. Even though game doesn't become extremely hard, the fights become longer longer and enemies become bullet sponges.

Seed of Evil doesn't force you to think before the fights because unlike the first game, it throws so many enemies in the same area (seeing each other).

I can't say the game is bad, but it isn't necessary. It is just more of the same (not a bad thing) but with dullness added and the mystery removed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA nice extension to the base game. The DLC's plot continues where the base game left off, and it feels like a well told mini "Season 2" of the original game's story. At the end it feels like a well packaged story, but still leaves room for more DLCs / sequel. Well done on that front.

In terms of gameplay, there's a new playable character, a few new skills, and a few new hats. However, there are no new weapons, weapon upgrades, attachments, or consumables. It feels like a continuation of the base game's end-game gameplay with all skills/weapons maxed out, and the DLC doesn't really add room to grow further.

Main story + clearing new optional areas took me around 5 hours. This time doesn't include a few optional backtracking side missions that would probably take a few more hours.

Overall, if you enjoyed the base game then you'll enjoy this DLC. Recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, I love Mutant Year Zero: Road To Eden. I played >200 hours before the Seed Of Evil DLC.
So I was really psyched about Seed Of Evil.
And what a disappointment it was!

The new weapons/armours/hats
Pretty useless all of them compared to the weapons from Road To Eden. With one exception: the silent Wraith. NO NEW ARMOURS! The new hats are one of few redeeming features in Seed Of Evil. Some of them are pretty cool and useful.

Iridia's Shop
You get the "Irida has new items in shop" message from time to time but the "new" items are some more grenades (and not the once you want) and some medkits. Zero new exciting items.

Leveling
Together with the "offerings" in Iridia's shop the second most disappointing thing in Seed Of Evil (I'll save numero uno to last).
The leveling continue to increase after every fight after you have unlocked all the abilities of your characters and you end up with a frustrating number of useless skill points in the end. Would have been nice with some new mutations to the old characters.

The Story
The single most disappointing thing regarding Seed Of Evil.
The main story isn't in the neighbourhood of Road To Eden. Doesn't add anything significant to the lore of the game. And don't get me started on the side quests to rid some of the Road To Eden locations of the new enemies. They are just fillers to prolong the playing time.

Haven't encountered any significant bugs, just some anoying things like the "Quicker than he looks!" achievement doesn't seem to work.

I'll continue to play Road To Eden (still haven't reached Eden in IRON MUTANT mode on VERY HARD) but I'm not tempted at all to do the same with Seed Of Evil.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved Mutant Year Zero: Road to Eden. One of my favourite games of 2018. It was short (21 hours and I completely everything there was to do) but outstanding. For me, this is a wonderful expansion. There's a new set of enemies but they are pretty much the same enemies from the base game with a new skin and one new mechanic. There are a few new weapons and headgear, which is nice. There's a good amount of interesting new areas to explore and some side missions in old areas with good rewards (a 4th silenced weapon!). The new character has a good mix of skills (tanky like Bormin with some sneak capability like Farrow). He was a mainstay of my party as soon as I got him.

Despite what others have said, this is not a short expansion if you are playing on harder difficulties. I do not play on ironman mode and tend to save and load a lot. Seed of Evil took me 16 hours to complete. Well worth the price to nearly double my play time.

As for story, it feels like a "scouring of the shire" or epilogue. It quickly wraps up the loose end of the base game, introduces a new conflict, then leaves with potential for new ideas for a sequel. It's a great content for the game and I hope it does well for them. Excited for the next game, hopefully, in 3 or so years.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt would have been so much better if they hadn't just added the content that was obviously missing from the first release, but had also taken the time to properly fix some of the glaring issues: lack of quick save, items not stacking, lack-luster skill trees, UI grievances, etc. But instead they fixed nothing. The content that is added is more of the same, which isn't bad because despite its flaws I loved the base game. But I can't help but feel a little disappointed that so little effort has been made to iron out some of the issues, particularly some of the ones that would have taken very little effort.

I'm still recommending this because the mood and setting in this game is awesome, and I love poking around the zone for scrap, setting up ambushes with my overpowered energy sniper rifle, and listening to Dux and Bormin and the others banter. But I thoroughly hope that all the positive attention this game has gotten means we'll be seeing a more polished sequel, with a more in-depth story, more interesting skill trees (instead of the same few skills repeated in arbitrary patterns), and better UI.\t\t\t\t\t
