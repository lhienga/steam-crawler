\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's okay. The enemy AI is better than in Ready or Not, but that's its only leg up gameplay wise. This game's issues are primarily with bugs and lighting. 3 of my failed runs were from dying on different ladders. I climbed up them maybe 10 times total, and 9 out of those 10 times, I fell straight back down once reaching the top. I only successfully made it up once. I died 3 of those times. They simply do not work in the slightest, which is really bad since you sometimes are required to traverse them. I'm not sure how a bug so easily replicated, persistent, and crippling can still be in a game that has been in EA this long.

The other issue is one I personally do not know how to fix, but know can be fixed. The lighting on night maps is simply awful, and is some of the most headache inducing stuff I've come across. Your flashlight is like a miniature sun centralized entirely in a single spot in front of you. It reflects brighter than should be possible off surfaces that shouldn't even be reflective to begin with. I've played a lot of games that solve this issue, and a handful that have it. Ready or Not and even SCP Alpha are both games that lack this problem despite being very similar with their low light environments.

I think this game is better than both the aforementioned titles, but still fails to be worth buying. A fundamental and outright simple environmental tool should not kill me more than enemies with guns. To be frank, it's dumb. It's dumb that such a genuinely game breaking bug exists. Add to that the way in which night maps promise more headache than spent brass and it's just not worth the price. This game refuses to drop to 15$, and despite having a very respectable selection of maps, I just can't abide its pricing. This is still way too choppy for how long it has been on the market.

The first person in this has a bit of rust to it that is hard to quantify in a shooter, but can best be simply described as 'stiff'. The aiming just feels bad. It's like going from Battlefield 5 to 2042. In BF5, everything just feels right. You can snap to targets perfectly, your aim feels smooth, and everything just works. But in 2042, it's stiff. Things feel disjointed from what you actually want to have happen. That's the case here. No matter how I adjust the FOV or sensitivity, it just never feels right. Some games nail it, some don't. This feels a lot like playing a mobile FPS on an emulator than a finely tuned, PC based FPS. By no means is it unplayable, but it's worth noting that it just feels...amateurish.

It plays fine. Not great, but not bad. The customization is absolutely amazing. No micro-transactions, so that's always a plus. But somehow, the game fails at two of the basics. One makes certain scenarios unplayable, and the other makes any night mission dreadful.\t\t\t\t\t
