Edit:
Movement is pretty similar to basically any Ubis**t (it's not an o or f that is missing), tacs-mecs shooter. But it is overall very intuitive and easy to acess, props for that.
There is no really indepth to the customization (YET), some parts are simply just cosmetic (e.g. bipods) and you can't switch barrles, trigger group etc. or change pic-rails to quad or octa. It also feels odd that I can have a grip on the bottom, but on the the side or in case of an octa rail on the eight I can't put a grip on, despite having stub grips, to make pointshooting more comfortable and also why is the pic adapter right-side only?
It feels more like back in the days when you would dress your "action man" or "g.i. joe".
Shooting feels like Rainbow Six. Nothing more to add here, feels more arcade like to me, but what really broke the deal here for me was when I finished creating my weapon set-up was when I started zooming in on the targets. What in aod without any magnification just looks like the dot/holo/crosshair is moving slightly bc character is breathing, micromovements, basically just a living creature, is magnified just a dot randomly jumping around the target, like that test you have to go through at the ophtalmologist.

I hope I could make things more clear, I don't see much in this game, but I hope they succeed.\t\t\t\t\t
