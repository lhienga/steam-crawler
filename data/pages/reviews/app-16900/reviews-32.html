\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me preface this review with the fact that I am an old-school tactical shooter fan. I grew up playing all of the Rainbow Six games up to Vegas 2, most of the Ghost Recons, and WWII games like Red Orchestra. That being said, this is the game I have been waiting for as a proper successor to the two former.

I've had this in my wishlist since it was first available on steam, but just couldn't pull the trigger on it, due to all the terrible early-access experiences I've had. Well this year, I decided to give it a shot. All I can say is that I'm kicking myself for not getting this game sooner. It has everything I want from a tactical shooter: AI for solo and coop, dedicated server files for hosting independently of rental companies, great game modes for PVP, and a wide range of map types, from small to large, flat to variable elevation, urban to forest.

Gameplay is awesome. They've placed a lot of attention on player inputs for character and weapon control. You have two sprint speeds, crouch, lean, free-look, three weapon stances, variable zoom optics, lasers (including IR), flashlights, IR illuminators, multiple versions of night-vision to try out, and the weapon customization is on par with Tarkov. The controls for all of this are very intelligently mapped and I found myself only having to customize three or four binds to get me running smoothly.

Here are my critiques as a fan of this game and games like it:

1) Limited camo patterns for gear. The only complete camo seems to be multi-cam.
2) Small selection of base weapons.
3) Weird artifacting in scopes with certain video settings. This seems to be a known issue, particularly with Nvidia cards.
4) AI could use some refining. The difficulty levels are tolerable, but regardless of difficulty, they seem to be either brain-dead or Godlike.
5) Weapon sway, once your character is fatigued, is too robotic.

Despite those critiques, this game does too many things right to count. I'm more than happy with the content provided for $30 USD and I can't wait to see what the final product looks like. Unfortunately, like all of these other early-access titles, we're at the mercy of a terribly slow development cycle (this game has been early-access since 2018).

I normally wouldn't say this for an early-access game, but I'm willing to make an exception in this case: Pay full-price for this game. Give these developers the money they deserve for this already well-made experience. No AAA is making games like this anymore.\t\t\t\t\t
