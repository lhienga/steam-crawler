\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far pretty good, although I did notice a few issues. The issues are listed in the order in which I personally believe they should be addressed.

1. Inconsistent enemy AI - Sometimes enemies will just pile up in one doorway and get shot one by one (as seen here), while other times they'll see me even if I'm far behind them and basically one-tap me. So that needs some balancing.

2. Movement inside specific buildings (as seen on Compound) can be quite jittery, especially in hallways. This would either require some player movement and animation tweaking or map adjustments.

3. Enemies seem to sometimes continuously run into a wall, door, fence, or some other object.

3. Enemy voices - All they say is "Zhey killed one of ours!", and "Get ready, I hear zhem!".. After further testing, there are a few others similar to "Is it clear?" and such, but even then it still feels a little repetitive, especially when they start repeating "I hear zhem, get ready! I hear zhem, get ready! I hear zhem, get ready!" and so on.

4. Texture settings - I really dislike how everything below the highest Texture Quality just looks blurry.

5. Optimization - This is partially my ♥♥♥♥♥♥ PC's fault, but I do feel some aspects could be worked on to improve overall performance.

SIDE NOTE: I've noticed that enemies tend to be dumber the more of them there are. I tested by setting the enemy count to 15, and out of those 15, 9 came running straight through the doorway one by one after I killed the first guy (as seen in this screenshot), while on the other hand, when I put anywhere between 1 and 8/9 they tend to approach the situation more strategically. I think it may have something to do with the AI's higher confidence the more of them there are, which in turn leads them to do stupider things, while when there's almost none of them left, they tend to be more campy-like, although those observations could very well just be coincidences and that feature may not even be in the game itself.\t\t\t\t\t
