\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing in advance - I played the pirate version of this game a long time ago and then decided to buy the game, supporting the developers, respecting their principles, which will be discussed later, however I will compare ALL about not from the beginning of the project, but conditionally 2018-19, when the game was played differently.

The game, starting back in 2012-13 has gone through dramatic changes. The developers do not treat this creation like their "peers in the field" - they love GROUNDBRANCH, they listen to what players tell them, they understand how to do what others will not - love for their brainchild. They put their soul and real labor into the game.

A little bit about the development studio - just a little bit. BlackFoot is a group of people who have been involved in some really good games - starting with Ghost Recon, which I'm excited about, and ending with the second Special Forces simulator after SWAT4 - Ready or Not. This aggregate allows them to do what they want to do - it's a highlight of the developers. Over the years, the studio has sought out a publisher, setting their own terms, but also listening to the publisher's view of the product - if the changes the publisher wants to make don't suit the team - they, I repeat, THEY refuse, not the publisher.

GROUNDBRANCH is not a DayZ-like game that lost its potential after 13 years of development, nor is it a game that will lure you in and then sink itself for money.
Yes, now most of the comments will write that the AI is broken, buggy and so on, but the question is - did they read the DETAILED news from the developers? Were they involved when the new AI system was integrated into the design? I think the answer is obvious since such comments. Especially in order to continue to do something - you need to do basic things well, and here it is the emphasis on realism, that is:
- Character behavior
- FULL control over the movement of the character
- Detailed control of weapons

Here you play for the operative, not for a bag, who was taken into the army, and in the past he was, for example, a basketball player (EXAMPLE). Tarkov, SKVAD and other "realistic" games have some simplifications for various reasons - somewhere a conditional balance for the game, somewhere engine limitations, and somewhere developers don't care about the game and the meanings of the word "realistic".

As it was written in some comment - GB is not a game, but a simulation - this is indeed true. Just running around and killing BEFORE THAT dummies (Yes, at the time of writing the V1033 update where there was a new AI system, which made it break the AI a bit, but it's not that bad, really) - will be boring in any shooter, but add music and it's still not the same - go for games that require you to just "kill everything alive on the map". GB is primarily a tactical game, where the word "tactics" actually carries weight. I'm not saying that if you take a bunch of friends and come up with scenarios, you'll have a very good time. PvP in this game is a special thing. It's one thing to be able to communicate only with your team, watching the epicness around due to equipment and stuff, and another to be in dead silence, knowing that any conversation can be heard everywhere. Your weapon makes a sound, the wrong action and death.

Regarding scenarios - the company is on the way out. Already showed a small clip with allied AI. And as I said above - first the base, the platform, the mechanics basic and necessary are born, and then the rest.

I don't know exactly who will read or get to here the comment where I got a bit "burned" by people who don't quite know how to assess the reality of games adequately and sensibly, but one thing I will say for sure - GROUNDBRANCH is literally an investment in an ambitious project that is worth your attention. The main thing is patience - and any creation will turn into the best version of itself.

P.S - those who complained that the game will not be able to lie down - wait for the 1034 update, there you will be happy with a lot of things.\t\t\t\t\t
