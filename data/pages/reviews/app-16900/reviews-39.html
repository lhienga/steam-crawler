TL;DR

This review was made for V1033, while beginning to test and move onto V1034.

In it's current state, I would not recommend this game over its competitors. For the positives it offers, the negatives are too great.

Expanded

Positives

Starting with the positives, Ground Branch's simulation of tactical movement and character and weapon customisation is fantastic. The flow of movement and switching different weapon positions (i.e. high ready, on target, low ready) is smooth. Customising your operator is simple, easy, flexible, and offers great variety compared to the competition. The feel and sound of weapons are powerful, punchy, and satisfying, the weapons and their output provide a kick that none of the competition gives. Another note is that, when playing with other players, Ground Branch can really emulate the feeling of being a Tier One Operator when playing with the right people.

Negatives

But the positives do not outweigh the negatives. Where Ground Branch could be better or needs a massive overhaul are the maps, enemy designs, enemy AI, and the relatively poor prioritisation. The maps feel extremely dead and silent, bland or otherwise lacking atmosphere. While the enemy design is not as important as the other issues, the enemy designs are simple and recognisable. If done right, this can be a positive, but the lack of variety and some of the nonsensical models for specific maps make them feel as bland as their atmosphere. The enemy AI is also dreadful. It is customisable, but even when customised, a perfectly balanced AI is difficult to achieve, and so the AI tends to be either inhumanly challenging to fight or are Hollywood-henchman levels of incompetent; it's rare to get an AI that's in between.

Even if the development team is small and has less than ten staff working on the game, it has also been a few years since the initial version was released to the public, and so it's irritating that the developers are not prioritising where they should be. Helmets and plate carriers exist in the game but do not protect the player, the enemy AI needs to be improved, and various bugs and glitches need fixing, yet the developers decided to add prone and new visual effects in the latest update (i.e. V1034) instead of addressing most of these issues. I am aware they are trying to fix the AI, but I believe the effort being made is not there as much as it should be, given their priorities.

Additionally, the game is not well optimised, as many players, including me, have difficulty maintaining, getting up to, and/or past 60 FPS. In my situation, this should especially not be the case since I have an RTX 3080 and a 12th Gen Intel Core i9-12900K, but I have difficulty reaching or getting past 60 FPS. For a modern game, and for the content this game provides, this optimisation is unacceptable compared to its competition.

Another large flaw is that, while this part is not the fault of the developers, but the player base, the public multiplayer community leaves something to be desired. Oftentimes, even for some seasoned players, players are non-communicative and incompetent in combat. There are too many incidents of friendly fire, many instances of players not communicating their location, and do not communicate friendly nor enemy movement. These incidents occur both in PVE and PVP, and so I would be cautious and careful if playing in public lobbies.\t\t\t\t\t
