\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBasically Zero Hour

PROS
- Weapon Customization
- Gear Customization
- Controls
- Gunplay
- Map Design
- Very Realistic Night Vision/IR

CONS
- AI
- Graphics
- Optimization
- Suppressor Sound Effects

The AI is absolutely horrendous in this game, which is the only reason Ready or Not maintains an edge over this game.
I like to play Lone-wolf when I'm bored, killing time. Unfortunately the AI are functionally retarded. If you kill one AI, typically every AI on the map will hunt you down and know your exact location. I've finished entire games by standing in a corner and waiting for the enemy to come to me. The game is mundane and boring. Once you try all of the weapons and customization, you'll soon realize that was the only refined aspect of the game. If you have no one to play with, this game doesn't stand a chance.

The graphics are similar to Zero Hour. You can definitely tell you're playing an older game, although it looks more aged than something released in 2018. The lighting is what excels and tricks your brain into thinking the game looks good. It really doesn't. Objects further away typically lose all detail. It's hard to run an optic that doesn't have magnification due to enemies becoming a blur. The Iraq/Afghanistan map is the worst in terms of graphics, detail, and lighting.

The optimization is lacking but is understandable due to the current development stage of this game. That being said I think the price tag is steep for the game in its current state. I think the customization is what players have been wanting for a long time in a mil-sim. The gun-play is great, but the gore is lacking. Kills don't feel satisfying due the lack of perceived kinetic energy transferred from rounds impacting a body. I like the grenade explosions but they tend to immediately impact performance. The suppressor sounds are completely unacceptable for a Mil-Sim and heavily take away from the realism.\t\t\t\t\t
