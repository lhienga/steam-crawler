\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would like to say its a very bad game from multiple aspects.
First and foremost, the shooting mechanics feel like a step backward rather than an improvement. The developers claim to have introduced a more realistic approach, but what we got is a convoluted mess. Timing is everything, they say, but in practice, it's more like a guessing game. The inconsistency in shot success is baffling, with seemingly identical releases yielding drastically different results. This unpredictability undermines the skill-based aspect of the game, leaving players feeling helpless and robbed of agency.

The players' basketball IQ, or lack thereof, is another glaring issue. It's disheartening to witness AI-controlled teammates making baffling decisions that defy the fundamentals of the sport. Whether it's a lack of spatial awareness, questionable shot selection, or failing to recognize open teammates, the overall basketball IQ of the players is abysmal. This not only ruins the immersion but also makes it nearly impossible to execute strategic plays or enjoy a cohesive team experience.

Moreover, the supposed improvements in player animations and realism are overshadowed by the glaring flaws in the game's core mechanics. Clunky movements, awkward transitions, and unrealistic player reactions further contribute to the overall disappointment. It's as if the developers sacrificed fundamental gameplay for the sake of flashy graphics, leaving players with a visually appealing but fundamentally flawed gaming experience.

In conclusion, NBA 2K23 is a regrettable step backward for the franchise. The shooting system's unpredictability and the abysmal basketball IQ of players make for a frustrating and unrewarding gaming experience. Until these issues are addressed and the core gameplay is refined, it's hard to recommend this installment to anyone who values a true-to-life and enjoyable basketball simulation. Save your money and stick to earlier, more polished entries in the NBA 2K series.
