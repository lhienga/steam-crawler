\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hopped on 2k23 late in the game, so this review is with the context of not having to deal with any launch issues, first season bugs, or exploits / toxicity in the early, more populated community.

Pros:
-Good graphics and animations.
-Fun online play with friends.
-Lots of customization options for your MyPlayer.
-A pretty solid story line for the MyCareer.
-MyGM and MyLeague are still incredibly fun, especially with the retro rosters.

Cons:
-Monotony sets in fast in the game play loop.
-The good animations sometimes bug out and look stoppy and awkward.
-Keyboard smashing Hall of Fame difficulty.
-The crowds are still cardboard and the announcers usually aren't even talking about whats actually happening.
-Random performance dips that happen without any rhyme or reason.
-Can you even make 3 pointers without a 99 3pt shot?

**Lots of TL/DR below.**

The last 2K game that I played was 2k18 and I'd played almost all of them since 2k13, with a pause between when the originals were first coming out. Immediately, I was impressed by this game and that's probably just because it was the first one I'd picked up in years. For people who play this game every year, it makes sense how they'd be frustrated with only small changes but if you're gonna pick this game up on sale (which I did and recommend) then it is absolutely night and day. Graphics, animations, features, and the feel of the game on both keyboard and controller has absolutely evolved.

There are some big problems though with 2k23 and most of it stems from progression in this game. Having your progression constantly stall out while you grind points till your next overall is a feature I'm pretty sure isn't new, but it really does add to the monotony of playing an 82 game season especially when you play it on Hall of Fame where every single game is a DRAG to complete with how insane the cheese from the CPU and computer is. Missing wide open layups with a 95 overall slashing forward is INSANITY. This creates a really frustrating and daunting game play loop of watching your bars and badges progress climb barely a millimetre and as someone who didn't immediately grind this game in 2022, I'm unfortunately going to have to restart this entire process over for 2k24 which I will be purchasing on launch because I'm excited to see if they'll repair some of the shortcomings of this franchise.\t\t\t\t\t
