\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI dont write reviews usually, mainly because I am just to lazy to do it. But for this game I had to do it. It is sea dogs, basically, with some facelifting. And it is a good thing. I can understand bad reviews that some younger games put up here, but I love it just the way it is.It does not hold your hand, you discover things along the way, the sense of success is great, immersion is awesome, even in mid and late gameplay. It is a game for gamers who still remember the days when games where challenging and rewarded you for being persistant, make you think and generally enjoy and feel it. It is a game made by people who have dignity and desire to deliver good game, despite the limitation of finances or engine that they have and barrage of kid gamers that write bad reviews so eagerly.

Now, having that said, I do have some big things that I dont like in this game. Main one is that it is not an AAAA game so when you damage enemy ship, you can actually see the damage on that ship visually, witch is apsurde and I cant imagine why would anyone want that.
It is also obvious that this game is not AAAA sailing/pirate game because there is a boarding combat that actually happens, where you have to fight through enemy ship, or defend your own in case you get boarded. It is such a stupid idea to put that into pirate and ship overtaking game.
Also, another abysmall thing that these guys did, they actually read and studied about the ships in that historical period so you actually have the feeling its ships you are controling, you have to be avare of its weight, strength of the wind, direction of the wind. They lack the perfetion of AAAA game where nobody bothered about that and your ships just move as need for speed cars.
Also, crew menagment is implemented as well, not like AAAA games, you actually have to hire crew, during battles they die, their experiance matters, all sorts of things that sadly add to immersion and provides enjoyment to the game unlike AAAA title.

Sarcasm to the side, it is a beutifull game that has its downsides, like old engine and its limitations, but it is made by awesome people who work on and I enjoy and support them. Just dont change guys. Eventually, when you are able, change the engine but never the feel of the game. It is, without a doubt, best and most immersive singleplayer pirate or naval game in the world.

Oh, almost forgot. You can swim in the game. You wont have to, but you can, since it is happening on the sea. So, if you ever want to be AAAA game, you should disable that.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere's a bit of a review of the new-old game Caribbean Lagends.

Personally I really enjoy it. The most beautiful part of it is the storyline which is easily one of the best out there. It's exciting, it's unpredictable and in many places it's not linear either. What I particularly like about it is that the characters (including the main protagonist) are not black or white, they are all shades of grey like in real life generous and honest on the outside but with their dark little secrets which no-one knows about and that's the beauty of it.

The other advantage of it is the atmosphere. It really makes you believe you are a part of a nid-XVII century pirate saga and that's amazing. The individual components of the game such as sea battles, fort sieges, land fights, duels, crafting and exmploration are barely average but all of them together make the gameplay really enjoyable. The storylines is perfectly blended into the sandbox mode which creates a perfect balance of the sense of freedom but with a story to follow so it is not boring as majority of other sandbox games.

Now there are cons as well. The game is essentially a reissued and upgraded 10 years old Sea Dogs: To Each His Own. This means engine with its graphics and effects is terribly outdated. It was old 10 years ago but now it looks like stone age really so it is natural that there are so many negative reviews. To me personally it's not a big deal. The most annoying part is terrible low-poly ship models, especially those ones which migrated from the old Age of Pirates. Newer models added in the To Each His Own are slightly better and only some models added recently as a part of this particular release of Caribbean Legends are OK so here's a tip for the developers: please update ALL ship models to have 80,000 polygons at least, we are not in 2003 anymore.

The effects are terrible too. Empire Total War had better effects with recoiling guns, proper sailor animations and ships falling apart when exploding. What we have now is old for 2010, not mentioning 2024.
Land fights could really use some motion-capture. They look like a fight of puppets, not even close to real fencing.
And lastly, the sea fight mode... Was it to difficult to add separate damage bars for port (left) and starboad (right) side of the ship with some crew damage bonus for stern? Empire total war has it, Ultimate Admiral: Age of Sail has it, an old MMORPG Pirates of the burning seas had it, even the mobile game Pirate: Caribbean Hunt has it! A line formation for ships in the squadron? All these could have improved the gameplay so much!

To summarise: for any die hard fan of the Sea Dogs series the game is a must. It's 100% the top of the evolution and it is better than any othe Sea Dogs / Age of Pirates game. Unfortunately, the engine is super old and clunky so please leave it to rest and don't use it for any future projects as the number of gamers who respects it is diminishing. For someone who began their acquintance with pirate games with Assassin's Creed Black Flag, this game will only cause revulsion as it simply doesn't look nice enough to give it a try. The number of negative comments is your confirmation of that.

So overall, thanks for the nice upgrade of the good old Age of Pirates / Sea Dogs, I am enjoying it right now and I'm sure will enjoy for many more hours straight, especially after swapping the ship models with high-poly ones both created by myself and also taken from various mod packs, but please consider moving it to a decent engine otherwise it will probably be your last project which has any degree of success in the gaming industry.\t\t\t\t\t
