\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the game and it has promise but currently it needs a lot of work.

The main mechanics are great, a factory game in this 2D side scroller style works really well and is really fun, it comes with new challenges when thinking out your build. The game also has some unique additions with adding plants and animals as part of the factory too.

I've played a lot of factory games and this one really needs some more thought and planning when it comes to the pacing and flow of the research/factory building. I've recently reached the mid to late game with the third science pack and it feels like suddenly I have to do everything all at once having not needed to automate anything for ages. There's nothing to do until there's everything to do.

The controls regarding the taskbar are a pain in the ass! You have to make sure you don't have anything equipped (by pressing Q) when you select something or it replaces the thing on the taskbar. It sounds like a small thing but it has been my biggest frustration with the game

The similarities to Terraria end at the 2D graphics and exploration and I think that's a shame. I would love if the game had bosses, weapons and upgrades like Terraria. I also believe adding these things could help the flow and pacing previously mentioned. Having to build a factory to make a weapon to fight a boss not only gives you something to do in the downtime but also makes it so that third science pack isn't so overwhelming.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFour hours in and I'm still having mixed feelings. Part of the issue is due to the somewhat flaky tutorial/quest system that sometimes fails to explain what exactly you need to do, however, this is only an issue because some of the mechanics aren't easily identifiable.

One example: You are told to feed mana to a mana conduit, but you can't manually do it because that doesn't work. The way to do it is to run their version of a conveyor/pipe over to it and have the mana drop onto it. However, you may or may not have previously discovered that this is even possible, because with most machines, the conveyor/pipe connects directly to them.

It isn't that you'd want everything spoon-fed to you, just that you would expect game mechanics to be a bit more obvious.

Then, there's the combat - it just doesn't feel all that satisfying. However, I could accept the combat as it is except for the fact that there's so few mobs below ground. At least in the starting area - maybe that changes later though.

Next - the pacing of everything just seems a bit slow. If RNJeebus smiles on you, you might find a silver node really quickly - I didn't. I found one after digging tunnels all over the place - and only one. Found multiple iron, mana, and wood nodes, and a few others, but only the one silver. Silver is a bit of a choke-point early on BTW.

Now, I do like the concept and how some of it is implemented, despite it being 2D - I mean, crafting, exploring, building. All things I love in a game. 2D automation isn't even bad in this, but overall it just feels like something is lacking.

It is Early Access though, so we'll see how it progresses. If you like games like this, my preview-review shouldn't stop you from buying it. If you don't like such games - it shouldn't coax you into buying it. If you're on the fence - wait a bit.

I'll give it the benefit of the doubt (curious phrase that) for now and revisit this again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts a fantastic game, especially at the current early access stage the game is in.

I'm a massive fan of games like Factorio, Satisfactory, Dyson Sphere Program, Astroneer etc. And this is definitely scratching an itch I've had for the longest time with this genre of games.

I can definitely see where people are coming from in terms of comparing it to Terraria and Factorio but AutoForge definitely holds its own and is pretty unique, and incredibly addictive... From what i've seen and read its looking like its a 50+ hour game to get through in its current state.

Jason the dev, is incredibly active and friendly. Issues that are being reported on the Discord are consistantly being worked on which is incredibly nice to see. For example I reported a save bug and within half an hour they had began working on a fix.

There are plenty of things I'd like to see in the future such as multiplayer, customisation, blueprints and more. But in its current state the game is very meaty in terms of content. Definitely one to watch\t\t\t\t\t
