\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy first impressions on AutoForge:

Aesthetics: A - the visual style is appealing, the theme is nice, you can generally tell what's going on.
Movement: B - for the most part stuff works pretty well, but movement can get a bit janky at times, and air control works in some pretty unexpected ways (without a visual tell that would justify it; specifically I'm thinking, if you keep holding the Jump key while you're against the ceiling, and then slip past said ceiling, you might still go upwards).
Building: C - a promising framework, but it doesn't keep building when the character moves, only when the mouse does, making it awkward to lay scaffold or ladder as you're moving along. Also, while it's nice that some things can be layered over one another, there's insufficient control over which layer you're affecting when you try to remove some.
Factory/Crafting: B - it could use a bit of early explaining on how machines pull from transport tubes, but in general, things work pretty well together. Would be higher except that I ran into a few cases where things just failed to work - furnaces just didn't consume fuel items to gain energy, so they did nothing, until I reloaded the game.
Worldgen: C - the patches of map you work with are too small for more than a few resource nodes to show up, and in particular, you can get absolutely screwed on at least one of them (sand) with no way to get enough to crack open more zones. A way to get pointed to undiscovered resources would help somewhat.

Overall, it's promising, but definitely not ready for primetime just yet. Also, if they don't have multiplayer in the works, I will be sad. Tentatively recommending in the hopes that active development will fix some of the more glaring problems; if it sits idle for a while, that will change.\t\t\t\t\t

If I opt for local storage then that is a base building design choice, but this game simply does not allow it. So I end up feeding the machines 50 pieces of stuff every few minutes or so, instead of the 2000 pieces that are inside that chest right next to the machine... because a chest, for some weird reason, has 4 arrows leading into the chest but none out of the chest??? Seriously? A belt does not even need to connect. It only needs to be within the machine's reach to dump its content, but any chest in reach won't.

But overall, this is a very, very good game. Even though some of the design choices do feel like there is a serious lack of experience with game loop theories. (where you ought to tease and please players... not annoy them)

Undoubtedly these choices will be corrected during future iterations. But until then, the game might feel a tad grindy. Especially during the early game. It's not a Factorio game with it's very organic learning curve and tons of freedom of choice. It's more like Techtonica. You need to get your teeth into this one to slowly discover (and appreciate) the solid gameplay underneath.

A solid game, deserving its place in the market of automation games by being original in many ways.
