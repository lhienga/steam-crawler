\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, I recommend this game!

Why?
I absolutely love the art style, combined with factory building and exploring. And it's super chill but also makes you realy think about how to build your factory.

For it's price you get a ton of hours out of it. Absolutely worth it. While writing this review I am 16.5 hours into the game and having a blast. Still discovering new things and this also changes how you have to setup your factory.

I had to get used to the keybinds but you can just rebind them, no worries. The tutorial is well done for getting the basics down.

The way you unlock areas is also pretty cool, it's not a huge grind wich I also love. Might get harder the further I get but we'll see. With every area you unlock there's a new adventure waiting for you and a new way to add to your factory.

Does it have problems?
Yes, but it's early access and the developer is streaming live on twitch where you can see him activly fixing bugs. There's alot of QOL stuff that could be added and I know the dev is activly working on added these too.

While playing I encountered some bugs and at the start that required me to restart my world but that was just after 2 hours of gameplay. The dev fixed the bug and after that I could continue and found another bug that didn't alow me to progress. Guess what, during his live stream he helped me and saved me from having to restart the world. They're also very active on discord.

This kind of engagement shows that they care and have passion for a game like this.
I love that!

This game is early access but it's just so good.
Overall I can see the potential and it's very promising.
I'm sure there are tons of content updates they could bring out.
I can't wait to see what updates bring after the major bugs are fixed and QOL is added.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI say yes, but with caution. Crazy high reviews will bring backlash by some who are upset by the game.

1. Definitely Early Access. Feels very under-polished. So go into it with patience, or be patient and wait for some updates before buying in.

2. So far its got the Terraria (more like Starbound really) vibes in the exploration/presentation aspect. I love that resources can be mined from background tiles.

3. The automation, menus, and personal crafting feel most akin to DSP mixed with ONI network management. I think this aspect is what is driving the high level reviews already.

I see this game hitting on so many phases its really feels like there's a lot of potential here. And not the 'lot of potential' that never gets fulfilled. A lot of it appears to be fulfilled already and this EA release will generate the funds to expand, improve, and do all the necessary polishing. This is going to be a hit, buy in immediately if you know its your thing and watch it improve. Be patient and wait if you don't like the game feeling too raw.\t\t\t\t\t
