\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is definitely an oldie but a goodie, it was first released in the 2010s on the PS3 and has been re-released on PC with most if not all the original DLC content included...and it's A LOT! First up, there are almost 150 playable characters including crossovers from the Dead Or Alive series and others, plus a surprise appearance from, of all series, Soul Calibur!?! (Not a Koei Tecmo franchise, which was a huge surprise to me) there are also hundreds if not thousands of different weapons and costumes you can unlock to customize your characters as you unlock them (costumes are where maybe some DLC content wasn't included based on numbering) and 5 different play modes including a 3-vs-3 PVP fighting mode (only local multiplayer though, which at least keeps it fair because we all know online play is riddled with hackers, cheaters & modders who will do anything to win unfairly). There's also an online mode where you can customize stages you've completed, upload them to share with others and play stages others have uploaded.
The main story mode and secondary Gauntlet mode are very deep and, if you're going to grind for all the achievements, promise hundreds of hours of gameplay-I'm at nearly 200 hours playtime as of this review just trying to finish all the Gauntlet mode achievements-and with nearly 150 characters and 4 difficulty levels in the story there's almost limitless replay value. The soundtrack once again is a strength as it has been throughout the Warriors franchise-the music is a fantastic mix of rock, techno and fusion or rock/metal with classical Asian elements (the flute/piccolo solos throughout are brilliant and a real treat for the ears).
The only real complaints I have are with initial setup-on first load the graphics defaulted to 640x360 windowed resolution, which on my monitor (1080p Full HD) made text blurry and impossible to read...once I managed to switch it to my preferred settings and make it stick no issues-and also the game has some minor stability issues. If you switch tabs/windows while playing and come back to the game that will cause random crashing and there is one particular stage that, if you make certain things happen during the stage, it will always crash.
If you're trying to decide between this and Warriors Orochi 4, I recommend this one just because there is no additional DLC (you just have to play through the game to unlock everything) and the gameplay is more suited to pick-up-and-play although character development is not nearly as complex as in WO4. This one is also just more fun as it better fits into the time-tested Warriors formula of 1 vs. 1000s...and you actually control 3 characters at a time here (except in Gauntlet mode, where it's 5). But regardless, this one is a must-buy for Warriors fans.\t\t\t\t\t
