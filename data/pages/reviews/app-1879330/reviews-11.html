\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to continue updating this review as I play.

Warriors Orochi 3 Hyper on Wii U was what got me into Musou games. I had Samurai Warriors 3 on Wii but never got into it.

I put 100s of hours into this game originally, so I was excited when I heard a definitive edition was on Steam with all DLC.

I just bought this to play with my wife who has beat Warriors Orochi 4 Ultimate, Hyrule Warriors, and Fire Emblem Warriors with me.

I was expecting it to be polished to perfection since it is an old game, but damn there seems to be a lot of issues early on.

1. Controller support is hit and miss. I use my Switch Pro controller for steam games and have never had issues playing other Musou games like Dynasty Warriors 9 or Warriors All-Stars. With this game, I am unable to remap my controls for jumping and Musou abilities. Even when I go into the options and switch them around, it doesn't change in game. My wife uses a PS4 Pro controller and she had no issues with her controls.

2. A lot of visual glitches already and I've only played the first level. Ma Chao's Musou turns all enemies into black blobs for example, and had a lot of jagginess during some cutscenes.

3. Input lag is bad. Several times I would try to do combos and it didn't register my charge attack so my character would just sit and stop attacking. This is really glaring in a game about big combos and I don't recall ever having that issue on the Wii U version.

4. During split screen play, characters talking during battle completely cover up player 2's screen. With 100s of enemies, tons of flashy attacks, having people talk take up the screen is problematic. There is no way to turn these visuals off either.

It's unfortunate because this is literally the game that got me into the entire franchise. I don't know if newer games are just more polished or if Koei didn't care about polishing this port, but it really is a let down and right now I cannot recommend it over Warriors Orochi 4, whose gameplay is just much better and more polished. This is not a game I would recommend playing with split screen either.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOrochi Warriors 3 Ultimate Definitive Edition is a warriors/musou game. It's a hack & slash game, where you control a character (here, 3) on a gigantic map, in which you have to defeat a lot of soldiers and officers to progress, by (mostly) spamming a lot of light attacks.

Now, a good question is: Can I play WO3UDE without having played the other games in the series? Definitely. Even though the game is full of characters (145) and the third installment in the series, it can be played as a standalone. Besides, there are recaps for the two previous entries in the Gallery mode.

WO3, and especially Warriors Orochi as a series can be viewed as a sort of "best of" kind of game. It contains characters from Dynasty Warriors 7, Samurai Warriors 3 and also some guest characters from other games, like Ryu Hayabusa from Ninja Gaiden or Sophitia from Soul Edge/Calibur.

First of all, there are 4 types of characters in this game: power, speed, wonder and technique. Power characters won't get hit stunned when hit by a normal attack; speed characters can jump mid-combo and do an Aerial Rush (air dash); wonder characters can do a short dash (on ground and in the air) after each attack by consuming a bit of their musou (=energy) that allows them to breaks enemies' guard; and finally, technique characters can do a sidestep by pressing jump when blocking, they can also do it when being hit and that sidestep also allows them to use the running attack without having to run.

Each character has a level and different stats. Finishing a stage will net you gems and growth points, XP that are stored and can be used to level up the characters you have never played with. Each character also has bonds with some other characters. The stronger the bonds, the stronger your True Triple Attack with them. Besides, when you activate your musou, if there's someone with a strong bond close to you, they will also use theirs at the same time. And that's not all, if your partners have a strong bond with the characters you're actually controlling, they will sometimes automatically activate a support attack and even give you 1 or 2 random bonuses (2x attack, 2x defense or 2x speed).

You can customize each character's weapons with attributes. Some of them are elemental attributes, Bolt, Fire, Ice, Lightning and Wind. Some others attributes will have a more practical effect like Reach, that extends the range of your attacks, or Agility, that will make your characters attack faster. Most of the attributes can be stacked up to reach +10. It is important to know that the harder the difficulty is, the better the attributes you obtain are. And in the later stages, you'll be able to obtain some unique and powerful attributes.

This game offers a lot of different systems. First of all, you will mostly spam a lot of normal attacks followed by a charge attack, which can be translated by X, X, X, X, Y. Each character has 2 special attacks (one on the ground, one in the air) and a musou attack, their "ultimate move"; all these moves use your musou gauge, make your character invincible for a short amount of time and can deal a huge amount of damage. There's also the True Triple Attack that you can activate by pressing the two triggers on a controller at the same time. Your two partners will join you on the battlefield and you will be able to hit a huge amount of enemies while being invincible. And those are the main tools you will probably use during your playthrough.

One mechanic that is quite powerful is the ability to switch characters mid-combo by pressing a trigger. And if you do it during a charge attack, you will trigger a Switch Combo and by pressing the trigger three times you will be able to deal a huge amount of damage and fill your True Triple Attack gauge quickly. Some characters also have a unique Switch Combo, depending on the partners used, that will basically works like a mini-special-attack. One very powerful move when you're under attack is the Support Attack: you call one of your partners when you're hit by pressing a trigger. You can also do a counter attack by pressing the normal attack button when you block an enemy attack, it will leave them vulnerable for 3 or 4 seconds. With most of the characters, it’s also possible to hit a downed enemy by using a jump charge (and you can even regenerate musou by holding circle/B).

Here's how everything works. You start on a huge map with allied soldiers, officers and a lot of enemies to defeat. During the stage, you will have to fulfill some conditions to progress. In the heat of the action, it can be hard to know exactly what to do but there's a battle log in the pause menu to see where to go and what to do. However, you have to keep alive 2 or 3 particular allied officers; if one of them is defeated, that means a defeat for you. And that's why it's always useful to save while in a stage. And this is one of the flaws of WO3. Sometimes, you will feel like a baby sitter that have to look after X or Y. A lot of times, you will see the message "X is in trouble. Go assist at once!" That means, press up on the d-pad, ride your horse, and save them before it's too late.

WO3 offers 3 main modes to play through. There's the story mode, the duel mode and the gauntlet mode. Apart from that, you can also use the musou battlefields to edit a stage. The main dish of the game, the story mode, is made of 8 chapters. When the original game was released, there were only 4 chapters; the last 4 ones were added with the ultimate version. To be honest, the first 4 chapters are quite long and offer you a good chunk of playtime, especially if you want to use the recommended team for each stage.

The duel mode is where you can play in a 3 vs 3 mode against the CPU or another player. You can also use 4 cards to create different useful effects. Unless you want to obtain the trophy associated with it (win 50 fights in the survival mode), you won't have to bother with it. But I quite like it. It's nothing special however it is a fun mode. When you win a match, you gain a bit of growth points and gems.

And then, there's the Gauntlet. Like the Duel, it is also optional. But by doing it you can obtain some useful resources and items. In fact, the gauntlet is where you can find the most powerful weapons in the game (and one last special character). It was added with the Ultimate version, which means that even if you don't do it, you will still be able to obtain what was originally there. Personally, I don't like it. Or rather, I find it boring. The gauntlet mode is like a second game in the game. If WO3 is a hack & slash then the gauntlet is a dungeon crawler. You will have to go through different battlefields that are randomly filled with different enemies in search of the exit. The longer you stay and the more enemies you defeat, the stronger those enemies become. The stronger the enemies are, the better the rewards you can obtain from them. While it is not a bad premise, sometimes you have to do up to 5 battlefields in a row and there's also the fact you have to finish that mode 4 times if you want to unlock all the battlefields, which is indigestible for me. One important point though is that playing the gauntlet mode “normally” will certainly ends up in a failure, unless you have a good weapon and you know exactly what you do.

Warriors Orochi 3 is one my favorite game. So do I recommend it? Of course I do. It's a fun game, choke full of content, with almost of the DLCs that were released on PS4 for only 39,99€. With all the DLCs, that game would cost at least 100€ on PS4. It is no perfect game, it is old, it has old graphics, it can be repetitive, it can also be quite grindy. But the sums of its good points far outweigh its flaws in my mind.

By the way, the most precious character is Kaguya and the funniest trio is Sima Yi, Masamune Date and Gracia.\t\t\t\t\t
