\r\n\t\t\t\t\tArguably the best Musou, and in a package that's an obscenely good deal by K-T standards, with all of the normally nickel-and-dime DLC included. While it may feel a bit clunky if you're coming from something newer like Pirate Warriors or Three Hopes, it doesn't take too long to get used to, just keep in mind that it's a game made with early 2000s design philosophies, despite its original 2011 release date. (K-T seems to be permanently stuck in the year 2004, in a good way.)

Essentially, the amount of content in this game blows basically every other musou out of the water, and this game has been the standard that fans of the genre compare new entries to. Over 100 playable characters with almost no cloned movesets, a long campaign with tons of side missions (which often unlock variants of missions you've completed before), and robust mechanics that never interfere with the moment-to-moment 1 vs 100 gameplay.

Does it get repetitive? Yes. But, if you're familiar with the Warriors/Musou franchise, it'll deliver exactly what you're expecting, in a complete and comprehensive package.

As far as the porting job itself, I have no complaints. The graphics have aged fairly well, and scale nicely to 4k resolution, and I haven't experienced any glitches, lag, or crashes thus far. It would have been nice to have some of the more modern QoL features, but as an enhanced port/remaster of one of the classics, you can't go wrong, especially with the asking price.\t\t\t\t\t
