\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've always liked the Orochi series because it combines the characters from both of the main series games (Dynasty Warriors and Samurai Warriors) as well as a smattering of characters made specifically for the Orochi storylines. There's also so many beautiful maps with great views to find between the base game and DLC stages that it's really on you if you get tired of a map because you keep spamming it. Top it off with more than enough modes to fit how you feel like playing the game any given day (quick mission completion, randomly beat some stuff up, slaughter thousands of peons, etc.) and the sheer character roster, and you get a lot of replay value.

Once you've played your first character for a bit and unlocked some high difficulty stages while accumulating some decent items growth points you are set. Just use that character on a high difficulty stage and keep swapping out the others so they collect 4-star weapons, feed them some growth points so they're around level 20, and you never have to feel the level 1 with basic crap weapon play for any other character! At that point you never have to play the game any lower than Hard again, which also means if you randomly meet requirements for certain missions and weapons while running around a map killing people you get them.

The game isn't for everyone. It's a basic hack 'n slash, and it's designed for you to be the hero not be some 1337 bragging rights FPS game. You want to play Counter-Strike then play Counter-Strike, don't come into this game and expect it to be Counter-Strike and get all huffy because of it. For my part I love the stress relief. Had a bad day dealing with moronic co-workers and idiot customers? Drop into an Orochi slaughterfest and feel your blood pressure lower as you end hundreds of peons in a few combos. Popular clique at school got you down and the teachers assigned too much homework again? That's when you notice an officer may look like one of them and you can put your boot in their face with satisfaction.

So give it a try if it sounds like it might be your cup of tea from time to time. Play a bunch of different maps, admire the officers you can play as you unlock more, take some gorgeous screen shots, and listen to the digital lamentations of the masses as you wrack up an absolutely horrific body count!\t\t\t\t\t
