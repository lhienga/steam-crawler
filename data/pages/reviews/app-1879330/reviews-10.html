\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor me Warriors Orochi 3 Ultimate is Tecmo Koei's perfect creation. It's The Infinity War of historical and mythological Asian heroes from both Japan and China with a smattering of other cameos from such well known franchises such as Dead Or Alive,Ninja Gaiden,SoulCalibur,and Blade-Storm. While the game may seem dense with many systems those systems are very easy to understand and the tag-team hack n slash "chaos" still holds up very well for this genre.

Matter of fact,this might be the best musou title to ever exist. Of course you could argue that is a bias stance propped up via rose-tinted nostalgia goggles! Admittedly Senran Kagura Burst Renewal and Fate Extella Link have evolved certain systems within the same genre whilst making the moment to moment combat more intricate yet simultaneously more fluid. They deserve praise for that. My point is if you remember being a kid collecting action figures or cards of all your favorite heroes and favorite villains Warriors Orochi 3 Ultimate is like dusting off an old toy box or opening up a spider-web covered collection binder in gaming form. One should always have a place in their heart for the classics!

So,what's the overall gyst you ask? Eventually you can assemble adventuring parties of three from well over 100 epic combatants whilst cutting your way through weird snake cultists,boar headed yoma,kaiju sized hydras,and terrifying calamity causing deities bent on nothing less than the destruction of all humanity. You get help from guardian mystics to change the time line so you can save the souls of destiny whilst going full "Suikoden" versus Orochi and his legions of goons. (King of fighters,Senran Kagura,etc.....This slippery cosmic eldritch serpent is everywhere isn't he? Pretty much Japan's very own Cthulhu!)

As you can tell the story has the vibe of a young exuberant Dungeon Master's most epic campaign using all his favorite npcs and villains or something a bunch of excited childhood friends would concoct as they play with with their lego sets near a creek bed. It's both utter nonsense and surprisingly very good,striking the balance between over the top wish fulfillment and a plot that would make for an entertaining wuxia movie or a fun manga.

Initially I was hesitant to give my full recommendation. Why? Because Tecmo Koei still has rather dubious business practices. In the past they would make three versions of the same game (Empires,etc) and their dlc catalogs became so bloated that the total "additional content" would often cost more than the core games themselves.

Yet I paid attention when they released the best version of Dynasty Warriors 7 with all its bells and whistles included. It showed me once in a blue moon Tecmo Koei could be generous,albeit to make up for a few more recent blunders!

Put simply, this version of Warriors Of Orochi 3 "is" the ultimate definitive version. Perhaps a few very obscure and rare "Limited time" or "Console exclusive" items are missing in this port but it is a minuscule amount compared to the heaps of content that still got bundled into the same package. As someone whom was limited to the vanilla version back in the day this was a must buy for me. (I'd say 39.99 is a very reasonable price-point but due to financial budget concerns I waited for a sale that made it retail at 29.99.)

Beyond that? Supposedly some special attacks have graphical glitches but from what I've read,they're easily remedied by tweaking the refresh rate.

Overall the game looks gorgeous to this day though yes,you can tell it was made during the Xbox 360/PS3 generation. Still,sometimes a very particular aesthetic style ages better than attempts at uncanny valley. In the realm of fantasy escapism "more realistic" does not always equate to a better product!

Additionally there's different modes (From a fighting game like tournament mode to a challenging labyrinth gauntlet mode allowing you to compose a squad of 5 instead of 3.) and a collection of many of the best soundtracks throughout both Dynasty Warriors and Samurai Warriors lustrous histories.

There's so much to manage from leveling up everyone with growth points,fusing together weapons to create more powerful variants,playing through missions on harder difficulties,maxing out relationship bonds,and perusing all your favorite characters robust wardrobes. This is the one musou game that "almost" doubles as a collect-a-thon action rpg so if that sounds appealing you are in fantastic company.

Thus it makes me simultaneously happy and sad to review this retro banger. It was the beginning of the ending of an era in many respects. It hails from when games were made to be enjoyable as opposed to pushing narratives,being sold as continuing services,or being watered down in the hopes of reaching broader demographics. (Which always alienates the core fanbase)

Pros

+One of the best most robust rosters in a mainline musou title

+Plethora of different modes

+Almost all the dlc included.

+Tag-team slap downs between three switchable characters (Five in gauntlet mode)

+Story-line is "ridiculous" yet earnestly sincere and well thought out.

+This version of Da-Ji is pretty much a younger Rita Repulsa from Power-Rangers. (And that's wonderful!)

+Potentially hundreds of hours of content!

+Addictive hybrid "collect-a-thon rpg" and "weapon forging" mechanics

+Multiple difficulties (Rewards are ramped up as you challenge yourself)

+Enough unlockables and secrets to have the appeal of a gacha game without ripping you off!

+A good port overall.

+The default control scheme out of the gate is somewhat different compared to DW7 Extreme Legends but overall I found it to be better with no need to reassign buttons. (Though it took me awhile to "accidentally" use my whistle function to summon a mount. Yeah,I feel dumb too!)

+I'm glad even Orochi was given more of a back story. Aka,there's now more to him than just being a scary mysterious force to go up against. Kinda fitting as this is officially the last entry in which he is a main antagonist. WO4 supposedly introduces greek and norse gods. Nothing wrong with that per se but it is kind "odd". Orochi was the perfect adversary for the series as he already had ties to Asian-centric folklore. (Same with Da Ji,Tamamo,and Hun Dun)

+I would also highly recommend this game to gamers missing titles such as Marvel Ultimate Alliance as it also has that "Heroes and villains uniting together to face even greater foes" appeal. The high stakes in WO3 Ultimate make for an epic adventure!

+If you like games like "Vampire survivors" this has much of the same appeal as you cut or shoot your way thru thousands of oncoming enemies! The key difference is sometimes there are additional objectives such as helping an ally,killing off messengers,protecting engineers as they construct siege engines,rescuing hostages,or making your way towards an escape point.

Cons

-Gameplay may seem rote and repetitive for those whom have gotten into more sophisticated and challenging musou-like games in the meantime.

-Reported graphical glitches that arise during certain special attacks. (Luckily this can be fixed by changing a couple of settings including the refresh rate.)

-Voices out of sync during certain cutscenes. For me it didn't effect the overall fun factor but it is worth mentioning. A tech guru probably knows how to resolve the issue but I'm just a simple man! (Try to understand!)

-I do wish there were more enemy types. I like how WO3 Ultimate has a darker somewhat scarier look to it compared to other mainline warriors games and this could be complimented even further by adding more mythological monster types in future entries. (Such as those found in Nioh)

-For many this was Tecmo Koei's magnum opus. Aka,it's very hard to catch that lightning in a bottle a second time even with innovations in game developing technology.\t\t\t\t\t
