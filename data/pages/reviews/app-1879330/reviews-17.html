\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best Orochi game finally on PC! I loved this game on ps4 and was considering getting it set back up to play it but now I don't need to since this game made a surprise release.

The good:

- Visuals are great and gameplay is crisp. Runs at 60fps in game while some cutscenes are at 30fps but bear in mind this is a port of an enhanced version of a ps3/xbox 360 game.
- 145 characters to play as. I've not played WO4U but from what I gathered it has less characters than this game. WO3U has the cast of DW7, SW3 as well as a few characters from Koei/Tecmo franchises such as Ninja Gaiden, Dead or Alive, Atelier and a personal niche favourite of mine Warriors: Legends of Troy. As well as this Sophitia from Soul Calibur is a guest character in this game.
- A huge story and extra missions from all previous DLC packs are included easily over 100 hours of content and more if you're a completionist. A gauntlet mode which is almost its own game and an option to make your own custom levels too
- Most of the costume DLC's are included as well as the unlockables for characters that are mostly costumes from earlier DW and SW games.
- Controller support I had to tweak in the settings but once I did it was absolutely fine and had no issues

The bad (there isn't much, #biased)

- Some slight animation glitches but nothing game breaking.
- This is probably controversial but its a personal thing. No English VO. I know they're usually bad but I personally struggle to keep up with the action and then read the translated text. Though from what I've gathered the translation doesn't match anyway.
- No online support which was there in other versions. Though you can remote play

Overall really happy with this port and I hope koei/tecmo keeps it up. Come ooooon Fatal Frame 1, 2 and 3 and DW3 ;-)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far:

60 fps, which is nice.
No online multiplayer. Haven't tried remote play.
All the pre-rendered cutscenes are much, much darker than usual. You can barely see anything.
Ma Chao's musou causes black, pill-shaped artifacts on all enemies that come into contact.
Wang Yuanji's EX attack and 4th Y animation effects do not operate at 60 FPS, so there is a delay in the depiction. Rocks and Light basically animate at slow motion and follow the character until the animation is completed.

Another edit: To the comments below:

I'll try to clarify further since at the time of writing, it was on the day of release:

The gamplay is still solid since it is essentially a port of Warriors Orochi 3 Ultimate from Switch. I used to play this game a lot (and Dynasty Warriors 2-8, Orochi entries) back when it was on Xbox 360. If you don't know what Warriors Orochi 3 brings, it's "tweaked/decloned" movesets from Dynasty Warriors 7 XL and Samurai Warriors Pre-4, so there's a lot to enjoy and appreciate when at least playing as the Dynasty Warriors and Guest cast since their "tweaks" will usually include unique EX (your R1/RB attack) moves and unique strong attacks (Triangle/Y) that, sometimes, will callback to games as early as DW3. It's a ton of fun mega-boosting your favorite team from the Warriors franchises to endgame strength. Samurai Warriors have had mostly the same movesets since Warriors Orochi 1/the Samurai games they originated from. I personally have a fondness for this specific Warriors game since I think the movesets and all the levels/costumes/endgame are fun and unique enough for its large cast.

Does it work properly?

One complaint I had was a very distracting graphical glitch that you can fix by adjusting your refresh rate to 60 hz - there are plenty of threads repeating this solution in the Discussion section, but when there are *many* PC ports of existing games like Monster Hunter Rise, DMC5, etc. that don't suffer from animation glitches due to refresh rate, then yes I think it should be worth mentioning that half of your screen will turn to black on each Musou attack (basically your super move) from your starting character - an attack that you do a lot! This was literally 1 hour into the game and there's like 145 characters in this game - you don't have them all unlocked from the start, so you can't test Musou attacks on other characters that function like Ma Chao's. Sorry!

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZQqmrKxNgAk

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Tctg4OFDsg0

The same complaint persists with Wang Yuanji's attacks. The "omg this rock isn't properly animated" is multiple rock effects from a ground slam that covers a 4th of your screen that moves like molasses you see every on every 4th Strong Attack you do, so if you're literally playing the game normally fighting enemies, that can get distracting. There will be arrays of light arrows from her 3rd Strong attack that animate in slow motion. By the time you regain control and move around, these light arrows are still animating and will follow her like a spotlight. These animations are supposed to be ~ 0.2 second, snappy and flashy animations, but they last about four times as long and literally move around with the character.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jd96_tUQCaE

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4w5lP3AHj7g

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZMCllcaju9Y

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rJv6jJMANk8

This is not an issue in any of the original releases or ports onto PS4 or Switch, so yeah, it's worth pointing out. It's a distracting difference. It's still fun to play, but I don't know how many other characters' animations/effects don't work like they normally did.

It's not just me "giving a hot take." Look in the discussion forum: there's people reporting other issues too like characters turning metallic-black during in-game cutscenes, dialogue going out of sync, and pre-rendered cutscenes crashing their game.

I'm aware that there's a bug report discord from Koei Tecmo, so I hope that whatever laundry list of bugs get reported there will get fixed in an upcoming patch. So no, I'm not going to stop giving a bad review.\t\t\t\t\t
