\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's Dynasty Warriors with a time loop twist. You get what you expect.

While I thoroughly enjoyed the experience I expected and what I got, I'm going to go an off topic tangent a bit. My game score as a Dynasty Warriors game? 8/10. As a game not related to Dynasty Warriors? 7/10

Now here is my tangent: Since the days of old, this game has always been more of the same, and when it follows that formula that is its strength. A few times it has diverted into something else and been awful. My complaint is that it seems like this game has gone backward in a few ways, such as graphics, presentation, and the game engine.

Dynasty Warriors has been coming off as a budget title for a while now. I loved the games growing up and the simple mindless fun. When my brother got a PS3 and I got whatever DW game on there, it was so cool how when you fought an officer that the surrounding soldiers would gather around to watch and form an arena, and how if you were to take out an officer before he could complete a plan then you would change the overall battle. It was cool how your troops would have morale and that they could mop up the battlefield for you if motivated.

I think the first DW game I hated was Empires, or DW 9, I really forget, but I bought it almost 10 years ago and was like.. what the hell? These graphics are BARELY PS3 and it was horrifically translated from Japanese. There really was no love or attention for that game. That was 10 years ago, and the graphics in this game, while better, are still PS3 era. I wouldn't really be surprised if they were still using the exact game engine, but if so, then why on Garfields green earth have there been seemingly no improvements?

Is DW a budget series now? Am I simply playing really old games that are just new on Steam? Even if they kept the gameplay the same and just boosted the graphics and returned to where the gameplay was from a while ago I'd still buy it and be happy but gee whiz.

Ah well. Still a better developer than Bethesda.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed Warriors Orochi 4 Ultimate deluxe edition before this, and while I did enjoy my experience with it, Warriors Orochi 3 Ultimate is just way more fun for me. Like going from 4 to 3 was an upgrade in most regards. 4 does have some positives like smoother combat and quality of life changes such as mass weapon salvage/sell, but 3 feels better when it comes to content. Some comparisons here:

- 4 has no branching path scenarios, meaning everything is set on a linear story path. 3 has branching paths and more multiple endings. The branching paths can be at times small changes, but some are pretty big and having branching scenarios to begin with is more than nothing.

- 4 has sacred treasures, elements being back and stronger, and magic makes it feel like the player outpaces the game toward the mid to end game.

- 4 has almost no different costumes unless you get specific dlc for the characters, compared to 3 ultimate which gives a ton of costumes in one package. Also 3 gives edit colors to certain costumes which is great. 3 ultimate is better than 4 ultimate deluxe costume wise.

- 4 has better combat generally and 3 did at first feel clunky, but I got over it after finding my go-to characters and playing for a few hours. 4 is better here but 3 is fine.

- 4's infinite mode is not as good as 3's gauntlet. Being forced to unlock Zeus's tower after doing each
small tower with the different goals was not enjoyable. Gauntlet mode has it's problems, but I enjoyed going through it far more than infinite.

- 4 has no musou editor thing, 3 does. Just the fact that 3 has something like that is insane and really shows a lot of care that went into 3, besides the other stuff 3 offers.

3 is imo a better experience. 4 isn't bad and I did enjoy it, but 3 is just so good. There are problems like the black chunks when doing musous, but changing your pc framerate or whatever the solution was will likely fix it, even though it is annoying having to switch back and forth. And the clunkier combat doesn't feel good either, but looking past those issues just leaves 3 a way more enjoyable experience.

Would give it an 9/10 or so. Has problems but I am having quite a lot of fun. Would say get 3 ultimate over 4 ultimate.

Edit: Have had a few crashes so it is a decently bad port but I still enjoy nonetheless.\t\t\t\t\t
