\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood Musou game for its price, considering the DLCs it includes. I've played the first 4 chapters, so far the gameplay is great. If you are choosing between this and Orochi 4, this game is better in most aspects.
Pro:
-Interesting story with a lot of time travel.
-No performance issue for modern PC. If you can run DW8 Extreme Legend smoothly, you'll have no problem running this (do expect audio lag in some cutscene tho)
-A large cast of playable characters, including guest characters from other KT games. A lot of these guys are from console-exclusive games, which used to be a major reason why Orochi 3 did not have a PC version. But now KT is moving a lot of its IPs to PC platform, which is a good thing. You can also play as Kasumi, Ryu Hayabusa etc. in another Musou game called "Musou All-Stars", but Orochi 3 is no doubt much better than that game.
-Various fun modes to play with.
Con:
-Can sometimes be tedious due to the time travel nature of the story. You play a map, see some characters die, and have to play the same mission (almost) again to save them.
-Characters are a bit outdated as they are from DW7 and SW3. As a result, ppl playing later Musou games might find their fav characters missing awesome moves. For example, no EX2 special moves for DW characters, no Hyper attack for SW characters.
-Extremely grindy. You can download a 100% save file, but you miss a lot of dialogues due to how the story mode is organized. As later missions become increasingly difficult, you can't breeze through the story in a few hours. Thus, do expect hundreds of hours of game progress.
Overall, I consider this one of the 2 most worthwhile Musou games on Steam (the other is DW8 Extreme Legends, of course).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredible game. Fun story that you can enjoy without needing to be familiar with the source material or other games in the series. Great campaign format that is all about traveling through time and building up a giant roster of characters to play with.

Level up your guys to at least level 15-20 and play on Hard mode for a challenging and fair experience. You earn growth points while playing the game that you can use to give other characters an XP boost, a smart idea that encourages the player to try the game's many characters while advancing the campaign. This game seems to handle difficulty and challenge way better than DW8 does without resorting to enemies with health bloat.

Mouse controls are a plus, though the camera is a bit twitchy when it comes to vertical positioning (it constantly fights your input and resets vertical position). Optimization is good. Make sure to enable borderless mode in video settings to avoid issues with ALT+TAB.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's ridiculous, utterly stupid, devoid of anything approaching meaningful challenge. The enemy officers for some indefensible reason can trigger their own invincible musou attacks which you cannot dodge, escape from, or tech out of. When you lose, it's because you got one shot, or a dumb scripted morale meter went to zero on an AI who wasn't even fighting anyone. It doesn't have the QOL improvements of WO4 or that game's added characters or decloned movesets (Though it has a couple crossover chars that did not make it to 4); the main menu is a stupid ugly base camp you have to walk around. Horses do not work, period, whereas in WO4 you could just teleport onto the horse. Half the maps are either enormous amounts of walking or dumb waiting for talking to finish and doors to open or even more silly gimmick segments.

But most of this is baked into the cake already; if you're looking at a musou game, you know all this in advance. Despite all this absolute garbage, it's still uniquely fun. People oversell it out of nostalgia and undercut WO4 out of disappointment, but WO3U was the king of the genre for a reason and, warts and all, it is one of the strongest products Koei has produced, oozing with love, charm, and effort. While I prefer 4 for the gameplay improvements and the DW8/SW4 added characters (I can't have a musou without Zhang Chunhua or Hisahide any more.), 3U does deserve the hype it has as one of the finest musous ever created, enduring across 3 console generations at this point. To finally have it on PC is a delight.

But seriously don't sleep on WO4U either guys.

EDIT: I am going to make a statement nobody will like: WO3U's postgame gauntlet mode is incredibly overcomplicated and overtuned to the point of becoming an overwrought spamfest of one-shots and infinite juggles and systems upon systems upon systems and you can't even tag combo any more. I don't understand what people see in this mode apart from them being completely bedazzled by the dozens of meters and killing 30,000 dudes per map as opposed to 1500

EDIT2: I will also add that the Ultimate campaign is absolute garbage. Dozens of padded recycled maps with evil clones the excuse for fights drawn out of a mad libs hat and half of these maps I am certain were not playtested at all for AI babysitting or pointless walking across empty terrain.

Plus the villain Tamamo spends the entire campaign not being there and when you do see her she just whines about how much you are bullying her.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery solid and fluid combat. Not a pushover by any means. Game runs flawlessly and is quite stable. As a HUGE DW3-4 fan, I was beyond elated to see all of the DW characters have their old costumes, and remastered as well. Only issues as someone trying this game out for the first time is the lack of autosave. It will only save on its own after you win a fight. And if you lose, you are kicked back to the main menu and need to redo all of your pre battle and team management prep. Also the power creep. You can be cruising along in normal difficulty, then get hit by a single attack from a non-npc general, and flat out die from 3/4 of your health. This alone wouldn't be bad, but when you get to the end of a 25 minute long battle, and that happens out of nowhere, it is enough to make you turn the game off and walk away.

Edit: Get into the habit of saving mid battle, usually before you encounter a strong PC. I've played a good 50-60 characters so far, and every single one feels different, and has weight to them. So far I am loving Kanetsugu Naoe's moveset. If there is one other criticism, its that a lot of things aren't explained well, like Gauntlet Mode. and it's slow to level up characters without EXP buffing weapon attributes.\t\t\t\t\t
