\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Dominion Biosyn expansion is the first big DLC for Jurassic World Evolution 2, and it does not disappoint.
Coming with a new campaign, tons of content, and highly requested species from the infamous film.


Heres' some positives;

1. The Biosyn building style comes with 3 big unique features, an observation deck, a Hyperloop system that can connect to multiple buildings instead of path (including the observation deck) and the highly requested invisible fences.

It also comes with some copper decoration pieces.

2. The two new maps are gorgeous with the Biosyn Valley being an absolute highlight with the rolling fog mechanic.

3. The Chaos Theory mission is probably the second best in the game, the rustler mechanic is good but underused



Now onto the meat and potatoes of this DLC in my opinion, the creatures;

Dimetrodon is an unprecedented addition, the first non Dinosaur land animal and the first animal that hails from before the Dinosaur era. This lumbering sailback is highly unique and adds much variety needed to the games' roster.

Pyroraptor is also a first, being a feathered Raptor it immediately gains respect for that alone, the animations are amazing and the skins range from sleek to vibrant.

Therizinosaurus, hands down the most requested Dinosaur for either game, it finally makes it debut in this DLC.
Whilst the skins are middling, the sound design and animations are immaculate, a must have for any Dino lover.

Quetzalcoatlus, the Atzec Serpent God itself, the sheer presence of this animal almost excuses the lackluster interactions it has with the rest of the cast. With some amazing skins, insane animations and outright insane size, this Pterosaur is sure to leave an impact.

The 2022 Dreadnoughtus variant is the longest creature in the game. Its design, whilst not amazing, is definitely better than Frontiers' original Dreadnought.

The 2022 Giganotosaurus variant leaves a lot to be desired, through the pretty bad movie design, spindly unanimated arms and occasional broken fingers. The only real upside are the movie accurate roars and newly added update 5 skins.



Now we get to the skins, there are 6 in this DLC;

The 2022 Dilophosaurus sadly still has Frontiers' crap model but at least the skin looks screen accurate.

The 2022 Parasaurolophus comes with a completely new sound set and 3 skins to choose from, overall they're pretty good.

The 2022 Tyrannosaurus Rex skin looks amazingly movie accurate, sadly still lacking the proper ILM model though.

And finally the Feathered Tyrannosaurus Rex skin, besides the feathers, looks pretty bad.



This DLC is worth it for the creatures alone, but the added maps and building options are the cherry ontop.
If I have to say, this is probably one of the best DLC for this game, only ones getting close are the Malta expansion and the recently released "Feathered Species Pack"

8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
