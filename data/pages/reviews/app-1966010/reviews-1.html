- Finishes the Chaos Theory which was seriously needed, though the mechanics were a little whack.
- Adds a second campaign, which overall is pretty good, it is flawed, but so are all the campaigns in this game.

- Two new very large maps with unique themes that make them stand out.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI did not like this pack at launch. I heavily disliked the designs from Dominion, especially the feathered animals. But, the pack did come with a new building palette, a new gorgeous map, a so-so story based off a so-bad movie, and a new Chaos Theory map as well.

My focus will be on the Animals (ignoring skins and variants), however, as that is why I buy these packs in the first place.

The Pyroraptor is ugly. I do not like it, I think it is kinda overblown. The colors you can give it are nice, but the dorito shaped head with the shrinkwrapping just does not do it for me.

Therizinosaurus suffered the same problem, but I have grown to like it. Its belligerent, its got some nice colors to help its kinda bland model, but I wish it had more feathers. On the whole its ok.

Dimetrodon is great. A sleeper hit in my opinion, especially considering its rather small role in the movie. No real notes though as it does not do a whole lot of note. It's just a Dimetrodon

Quetzocoatlus is probably my favorite addition, ignoring its ridiculous size. I wish it was able to walk around, as the Aviaries just seem far too small for it to be flying, and unfortunately the Aviaries are the only way to contain it. That said, I'm happy we finally got an Azdharchid to help pad out the relatively bare-bones Pterosaur roster.

On the whole, its an alright pack. made better in recent months by the ability to recolor the variants. I feel that Dreadnoughtus benefited especially from that change, as its old model and movie colors just did not do it for me. On the whole,. however, Id say its still a bit much in price range for me. Wait for a sale, or grab it if the above animals really do it for you.\t\t\t\t\t
