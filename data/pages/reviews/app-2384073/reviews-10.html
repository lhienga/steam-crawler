\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI liked it but I want to start off by admitting, $10 is a bit much for what it adds.
You get 1 new campaign/act for Losomn and its as good, if not better, than the current ones.
You also get the ritualist class in that campaign which is fun and a nice addition to the game.
Comparing this to the base game (ignoring sales) you are getting about 1/6 of the base content as more bonus content for a 1/5 of the price of the base game. Which all and all its not that bad.

So my recommendation, buy this if you have played through the main campaign and want more or are currently playing through the game and really like it. You can only access the content after you beat the main game anyway (I am not a huge fan of that) but after than it gets shuffled in. I would say its worth it, if you get a bundle for both its still not a base price but then you are sitting closer to 60$ for everything.

If you love the base game, this is just more of it so its a welcome addition.

I want to mention other things I saw in reviews.
Weird green suck enemies - Yeah I hated them too but they have since be balanced and although still bullet sponges they aren't a big issue. Although they are still far to frequent for being basically mini-elites.
Confusing lay out - Yeah... thats is a big issue but in my opinion its not any worse than over world N'rud. This is mainly just the main area (which is quite large so it doesn't help). I think the main issue is how vertical it is. It makes reading the map annoying and I hate ladders.
Optimization - It feels like it is better than it was on launch but look out for this one, I died twice due to the game stuttering and me falling into a whole.
Story - Its not mind blowing but its alright. I like Losomn and it feel right with the region and ties in more blood-borne/lovecraft vibes which I am a sucker for. Overall its on par with the other stuff in the region and the boss is not the most fun to fight.

Overall, I liked it and the pain point come no where close to out weighing my enjoyment of it. I doubt a single living soul has read to this point but I am mainly writing this review to pad the percentage. Its an alright dlc, I would of liked a new region and would of paid more for it; but all and all not bad, especially compared to other stuff in gaming this year.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay this DLC has a lot of good things with it but with the caveat that it's more quality than quantity compared to the base game. Personally I really enjoyed playing through the one shot adventure and it was a nice breather while going through my apocalypse campaign play through, and I think I got my moneys worth for it.

*The one thing I feel most mixed over is the fact it isn't a new location but in Lonsom. I love Lonsom don't get me wrong, it was the first place I went to after Ne'rud and is what solidified my love of the game, but I was really looking forward to a change of scenery even if I liked the aesthetics of the new scenario. I like what they're going for, but I would have loved to see them do something new too.

*That said, I was really engaged with the new content, it sucks there's some overlap with level layouts but for the most part it wasn't a majority of the side areas. The new bosses were a blast to fight on nightmare and felt on par with what was in the base game. Also loved the main boss too, felt fun to fight and while having a turret did help eliviate some of the challenge I can see myself happily fighting him again. Those ritual totems in the sewer can die though, those were painful just because of how difficult one of the new enemy types is.

*The new class is really fun! The ritualist is based on applying negative status effects and it's been really fun tinkering with other classes. Probably the highlight of the dlc overall for me due to the

*New weapons are all really fun despite there only being 7 tied to the dlc (4 being melee and 3 being guns). Something to note is about 3 weeks before the release of this dlc they did add 5 "corrupted" weapons that you can obtain that are variants of ones in the base game for free. While the lower number of gun weapons is a little disappointing, the fact they are adding content for free in a non live-service game is appreciated when they could have made the dlc specific. Overall it's a net gain, but I'll likely have more to say when the next dlc comes out depending on how that turns out.

*New relics, rings, amulets and mutators are pretty good overall. I still haven't gotten them all as they're also world drops in the base game locations but form looking them up online there are a lot that get my head going with build ideas.

If you're playing this from the deluxe edition of the game, I think the content is worth the price (since each dlc will average to being about 7 dollars compared to buying it for 10 alone). If you're getting it by itself, if you really, really love the base game I'd say go for it, if you liked it but are on the fence, I'd recommend looking up everything on the wiki that's listed as new or from the dlc to see if anything catches your attention. It was worth it for me but I can see why a person wouldn't buy it.\t\t\t\t\t
