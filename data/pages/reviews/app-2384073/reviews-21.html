\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I've been playing for about 3-4 hours now, and its pretty fun. New areas, new monsters, and new items, pretty standard stuff, and we're having a great time with the lore-bits, story, and new NPC interactions.

Ran through the sewer portion, and I've encountered the acid-spitting crawler that attacks you; this seems to be a big complaint for most players. Now, I am playing a level 20 Challenger/Engineer build, so maybe that's why these guys aren't so tough for me. Yes, the content and gameplay is a bit tougher, but I think thats a good thing, its definitely keeping me on my toes, and going back the basics and mentality of the Dark Soul Shooter.

I would 100% recommend this DLC if you want more story, more challenge, and item variety, while keeping the same formula as the base game.

EDIT: This is DLC is 10$; And I still think its worth it. Games like Call of Duty, Overwatch and Fortnite will charge you 5-10-15$ for less content, such as a skin. But they are also big time companies, so they able to give away free content, like events. But Remnant did that too with the Halloween event, it just wasnt as flashy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat presentation and story. Loved the combat, the items, weapons, and armor, and especially the characters. Barely had any performance issues, and the story and environment was enratpuring and amazing! I loved the new enemies too, it felt like I was playing in an actually changed Losomn, and seeing the new enemies pop up out of nowhere with all the old ones definitely made combat fun.

That said, there were a few things that felt a bit disappointing to me. I do feel like some of the enemy's moves were recycled - the big aristocratic Fae with the axe, for instance, felt as beefy as they looked, but the moveset they had, to me, felt just like the executioner Fae. I was hoping for them to have a whip or an oversized rapier, maybe even a shortsword they threw at you, or to just have a butt slam attack. I still enjoyed fighting them, but it did still feel a little flat aside from the appearance they had. The Witches I really enjoyed though, and their movesets felt much more original to me.

Aside from the elites and regular enemies though, I feel like the DLC was lacking in bosses. Maybe it was just the run I had, but most enemies I fought at the end of dungeons were Aberrations. Those I did fight, while still fun, felt a bit sparse to me. Off the top of my memory, I think I fought three or so original bosses. The Sea Witch, the Idol/Ritualist thing, Bruin, The Blade of the King, and the King himself. And the latter, I feel, was even more disappointing. Fighting him felt more like watching a movie of a ruthless tyrant god king than fighting a ruthless tyrant god king, to me.

Putting those aside though, I really enjoyed the DLC and am definitely going to roll it again until I get everything I can from it, and I would definitely recommend it if you want more story for Losomn and kickass weapons ^^\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview is updated from Negative -> Positive post-DLC patch. Read on for more info.

While it was clear the devs didn't test the new DLC, and I couldn't recommend it while it was in a broken state, it is now officially patched and I can tell you after playing it extensively in Apocalypse Coop, that it's a ton of fun.



First and foremost going into this, you should be aware that all existing assets in the game are re-used for the DLC content, but they've just added more randomized options and enemies into the mix. Also, the first time you play the DLC, you'll be given a "One-Shot" option, where if you choose it all of the content in your first roll of The Awakened King will be new content.

This doesn't mean you won't see existing content in the new side dungeons, though. We entered a new area that had a new end-of-dungeon area attached to it, but the dungeon it was attached to was a dungeon we'd already completed in Vanilla Losomn. I think this is actually a really genius reuse of existing tilesets, to extend the content in the game but you may not agree.

I've been generally-impressed with this content. There's a TON of new areas to explore, new bosses, enemies, boss affixes, loot, and secret areas to discover, on top of the Ritualist class you get with the DLC.

As for as performance, I'm on a 1080ti running Low/High/High/Medium/High with expensive shadows disabled, and in most areas sustain 60-80 fps, but the Forlorn Coast, which is the humongous hub area for the DLC, I drop to around 40 fps. There's just a ton of special effects going on at all times, given the lore of the situation.

I dislike that the DLC released in such a broken state, but they patched it fast and now it's one of the easiest recommendations I can make. Go out and buy this DLC and if you don't own Remnant 2 yet, buy it too.\t\t\t\t\t
