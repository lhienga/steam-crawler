\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very enjoyable DLC. Instead of pure victorian Losomn, with the Nightweaver, or the Fantasy Losomn of Faerin/Faelin, this one goes for a Lovecraftian tone for the One True King. In the base game, he's sleeping, now this god is awake, furious, and on the rampage. His imposing order is certainly evident when you arrive. His presence is everywhere. From the lowest Dran speaker, to the most proud bridge guard.

A decent archetype with it, not one I'd go out of my way to use, I'm not keen on status builds, but it's good to throw those players a bone. More gameplay is more gameplay.

Still think that 85 trait points is a little too low compared to the virtually nonexistant trait cap of Remnant: From the Ashes, but that's my opinion. Others like the trait cap. I think it limits build variety. Then again, the loadout system allows you to change how traits are allocated in a build, so maybe I need to invest some more time in that before complaining.

Performance is a bit iffy due to the rain & reflective surfaces, but all in all, it's stable. Haven't had a crash on it.

The only part I do not like is the Purple infected enemies. They are way too tough for their speed, with a ranged attack. They're a true jack of all trades that is nasty on higher difficulties. Beware these.

Just got the One True Ending, and it ties every part together. The Fae council and their deciet, the Red Prince, the new Fae Scribe you meet, everything in the end works out well... but there is one thing (SPOILERS BELOW)...




Why is there no way to free Nimue? You either kill her or leave her bound down there. Considering how much good loot you can get from her (Especially the ones for the Joint Ribbon and Joint Faerin/Faelin item) in other world stories... I'm surprised the Player Character doesn't at least try to help. Especially since she's on the Base game cover, and is important in every version of Losomn. Kinda hope she helps with other worlds too, but... it's unlikely.

Other than that, This is a great experience. Would recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros: Beautiful map, fun expanding of the story in Losomn, shake up of the in game campaigns with the much larger world map. Combat is tougher given the lack of room in a lot of fights and areas. Forced me to play smarter than I usually do. The green guys suck, but you can tweak your builds and melt them once you get the hang of them. New Archetype is a blast, love the Sparkfire shotgun. Nice continuation of what I liked in the main game.

Cons: The either/or choices felt really abundant in this DLC. Open that door, can't get this one on this playthrough. This is part of the main game too, but feels like the hard cut offs were way more common in this DLC to force replays. If the current meta to get the Anguish is the only way to get it, that's just absurd. RNG, campaign reroll, and no deaths once you start the quest?

Overall I would still recommend it. I loved the game, and the DLC brought more of what I liked, with some little tweaks to add challenge and make me try new things in the way I played. I have 100+ hours and will still jump once the other DLC's drop.\t\t\t\t\t
