\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think the DLC is good. Limited, but good. What you're getting is a new alternate version of Losomn. Entails new maps, dungeons, bosses, and weapons.
Theres 2 bigger problems with the DLC, one is performance, the other, my bigger issue, is that most new weapons are only available through a very long grind/replaying the entire game.

Gunfire Games, for future DLCs, dont waste your hard work on weapons the long time players wont ever see. Please make content more available, and be more generous with scrap, so players like me, who play a lot, can actually enjoy and use the wonderful content you put out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, it's pretty decent, and I'm having fun with it. I do feel like it's a bit light for the price tag - I was really hoping for DLC like the Subject 2923 DLC from the first game (whole new world with multiple bosses, items, and whatnot). But they have done a solid job in supporting/improving the game with updates outside the DLC, so I don't feel awful for continuing to support the game.

I have a couple gripes:

3 new melee weapons and only 2 new primaries feel kind of lame (and only 1 new secondary, which is a pain to get).

And the design of these new green glob enemies is painful. Somehow they seem to always pre-fire their shots before I even walk through doorways so that I get hit before I even know enemies are there. And those staggering, floating mines that they shoot, burst out when they charge, and expel when they die are so annoying. They also seem surprisingly beefy for how quickly they get right on top of you.

The expansion also seems to have introduced some performance issues that I never saw in the base game.

Now for the good stuff:
New archetype is cool. It's nice to have an ability that actually does damage, instead of just a buff or CC effect.

The new major map is pretty well-made (if maybe a bit too big). They heavily lean into the "working through the map lets you open doors to make traversal back to previous areas easier" idea.

Some of the new equipment allows for very cool builds and synergies.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you enjoyed the base game this DLC is more of the same.

The new class is a lot of fun if you like inflicting status effects and a lot of the new equipment added synergizes heavily with status effects and applying them. The new shotgun is a real treat.

The devs did a considerate thing by making your first run-through of the DLC a "one shot" which is a curated map that has most of the new content in the DLC (including the class and the shotgun). There are still more new accessories to find beyond what's present in the one shot though so if you've been meaning to replay the campaign there's new loot to hunt.

The launch has been a little rocky so you may wish to give the devs more time to iron things out. Outside of a few crashes in one specific place due to the new class's perk destroying too many objects at once the game has been running great for me at 1080p/60FPS.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompleted first playthrough of the dlc with a friend today in about 6 hours. We ran through on Nightmare difficulty for an opening run (since we plan on doing more) and thought it was extraordinarily easy.

The world is incredibly well designed, both the most detailed and well structured in the entire game in both our opinions. I loved every second of exploring, and stopped to screenshot the beautiful views (or even an extremely cinematic death to one of the bosses) several times. Although it is still Losomn, featuring mostly the same exact enemies as Losomn, we actually both found it to be not repetitive at all. The level design for the dungeons was remarkably better than any in the base game, as well as refreshing as they differed from the very copy pasted general layout of the base game dungeons with how they progressed. While exploring dungeons and the "open world" we found several hidden puzzles that were super fun to solve. We both really appreciated how uniquely the predominantly Dran map fit in with the game, with almost no tiles resembling anything in the base Losomn, which really built on the lore of the Dran people.

We did have a few gripes, however.
SPOILERS: We assumed most of the dlc would actually have to do with The One True King, but he plays very little role in the dlc. In fact, his castle, looming atop the hill the entire dlc is probably an hour and a half total, and that was with us exploring every corner. It was annoyingly short for the Fae side of Losomn being the most aesthetic area in the game. Once inside the castle, there were only two paths to travel. One lead to a smaller dungeon which included an minion reskinned as a boss (which we personally didn't mind, since the minion itself was added in the dlc and was not overused at all). The boss was interesting in that you had to open its weakspot up, but it was a very straightforward fight that went down too easily. As for the other path, it leads directly to the final boss with no lead up, who basically fights you immediately unless you turn on an old friend from Losomn. The One True King is hyped up to be the supreme overlord of the Fae, who which the gods stand no chance against, and yet he is a single phase fight (I guess a second phase could be debated for a very slight change in action and an addition of one attack which happens pretty early on tbh). He was not difficult in the slightest, and when he died we both thought that there had to be some second phase where he'd get back up and be super powered, but no, his body just disintegrated.

Despite the easy difficultly and poor boss scaling, we both thoroughly enjoyed this dlc, and both believe it to be better than any area in the base game despite its limitations. Even if the bosses were not hard or had terribly easily telegraphed attacks, we still found them to be fun in a very non souls-like overcoming adversity way, if that makes sense. The world was super well designed and map and enemy placement structure way better than I expected, and I have to say I loved the entire experience.

TLDR: I heavily recommend. The encounter designs are way better than the base game and the map structure and dungeon level design far less predictable. There is an obvious level of care and detail put into this dlc that can't be ignored (we found entire short stories explaining the lore of Losumn, the Fae, and the Dran scattered around, even one in the form of a fable!). My only wishes were that there was more depth to the castle, the final boss was drastically more difficult, and that Faerin/Faelin would make some sort of appearance or what became of them was told somewhere.\t\t\t\t\t
