\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love Remnant and Remnant 2, and they both have rightfully earned a place in my top 20 games of all time. Gunfire has developed, and with R2 improved on, an incredible formula. Everything about these games -- the replayability, sense of discovery, the art direction, the diversity of worlds and enemies, the complexity of systems and the challenge of the experience -- is just incredible.

But sadly, this DLC, isn't doing it for me. And I think that's for a number of reasons, which I've listed below. Instead of buying this DLC, skip past my gripes for my recommendation.

--

What's wrong with The Awakened King:

1) The new enemies are either dead-boring (including 3/4 of the new boss fights) or horribly annoying (the new Dran Fiends, with exploding green blobs that shoot out everywhere, including on death, give Dark Souls's basilisks a run for their money for my most hated mob of all time).

2) The new area, which you'll experience in the "One Shot" version of the DLC campaign upon rolling a new The Awakened King adventure, is quite cool. Instead of the more labyrinthine Losomn city mazes, it's much more open and expansive and cinematic. It also runs like crap, doesn't have any terribly interesting engagements, feels overly-huge and (despite its scale and the care that obviously went into it) quite dull.

3) The overarching story isn't super strong, and meeting the big bad was...well, I don't think we were supposed to laugh at him but he's just a big goofy blowhard. Personal preference, maybe more than any of my other points, but I didn't get as much enjoyment out of The Awakened King as the very-similar storytelling in The Swamps of Corsus R1 DLC.

4) The new archetype is rad, and I'm happy to have it. But the new rings and other things to collect? Honestly, after being away from the game for a few months, it's really really hard for me -- someone who collected almost everything in the base game and loved every minute of it -- to get back into making (or even thinking about) builds. It sounds crazy to complain about too much content in a game: the dozens and dozens of mutators, mods, rings, amulets, archetypes, weapons, abilities, armors, relics and relic fragments (did I miss anything?). But there are now over 160 rings...and the UI of the game just isn't equipped with any tools (like search/filter) to make build-crafting anything but hard, painful work.

--

MY RECOMMENDATION INSTEAD OF BUYING THIS DLC: buy the "Remnant II - DLC Bundle" instead, which includes this DLC. Despite my complaints, there's a lot of content here, and it's worth supporting Gunfire so they keep making these amazing games!\t\t\t\t\t
