\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinished in less than 3 hours (on veteran, 2 player co-op). Love the game, but this DLC was quite disappointing, especially when compared to the quality of the DLC in From the Ashes.
An amazing choice was allowing people who do not own the DLC to play with people that do own it. I had my friend who doesn't own this DLC play through with me and I still find that incredible, but the items they pick up and mutators are locked behind DLC ownership which would be a good trade off if the story was a little stronger in this DLC.

That said, this DLC is worth grabbing on sale just for the items and new Archetype (which is pretty awesome) because the story was the most lacking area in this DLC. Second place goes to the level design, lots of reused areas which isn't too surprising, but it was a little too much (sewers haunt my dreams still). The Ethereal Manor really threw me off seeing the Asylum be reused like that but the way they make use of the liquid escape was pretty sweet. Favourite boss in this DLC was definitely the Blade of the King, loved the vibes, looks, and the attacks were super fun and watching him grab and impale my friend on a spike was pretty funny (sorry bro lol).

TLDR: 5/10, buy the DLC bundle when it's discounted and play it then.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot recommend this DLC in my right mind. Like many of the other reviews state, this DLC feels heavily uninspired, almost as if most of the content here is just cut base game content they decided to finish and slap a price tag on.
My main problems with the DLC are the level designs, reused boss enemies, and enemy concentration/design.
Level Design: While visually appealing, a lot of the level design in the DLC is either reused assets (which isnt a huge issue but they could make it look a little less reused) or straight-up confusing design choices. For example, the bridge leading to the castle. It being broken and having to run around the area just feels like extra bloat, especially considering the area leading up to the bridge felt long enough.
Reused Boss Enemies: As a huge fan of Remnant 1, I'm used to some bosses being reused for boss designs. However, these bosses usually have at least SOME difference to the base enemy. The bosses like this in the DLC do not. The Witch boss, for example, is literally just a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ reimagined version of the Butcher from Remnant 1. It spins, breaks walls in a terribly narrow area, and then summons unbelievably weak ads so it has an excuse to not get immediately bursted.
Enemy Concentration/Design: This section is mainly dedicated to the green and purple multi-armed enemies. I just feel like they're once again, uninspired and terribly designed. They often attack in multiples, and spawn a bunch of the slowly falling blobs that explode, so not only are you splitting your attention between enemies but also damaging projectiles. I just don't think it's that good.
All in all, the DLC isn't TERRIBLE, but I would not recommend buying it if you expect something like the Reisum DLC from the first game. It is FAR from that, and I'd say its also worse than the Swamps of Corsus, as at least the Swamps of Corsus added Survival mode.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC might be a bit controversial regarding amount of content for the price of admission. It gives another "version" of one of already existing worlds adding both new "open" location and instances, there are obviously new "quests", weapons, armors etc. and neat class focusing on status effects (as someone who uses at least 2 in each fight, was very welcoming of it). While it may be questionable to have current price for at least half re-used assets from previous versions of Losomn people who like Remnant 2 would most likely enjoy this. Issue is with the cost and maybe some unfulfilled expectations of ultimate edition customers, who wanted more.

In the end of the day - this is more of same game, which is welcome, it does not drastically change game, neither improving or degrading it (there are few qol upgrades that came with dlc patch that do not require dlc). Price could be a bit lower or amount of content could be a bit higher, but at least it's not CA Total War treatment.

Personally the thing that bothers me most is how this is non-consequential to main storyline and vice versa. NPCs in main storyline have nothing to say about you completing DLC and NPCs in DLC has no reaction to what happened in main storyline, which is a pity.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not the best DLC I've ever played, but it is a really solid expansion to one of my favorite areas in Remnant 2. Some really cool new "Bloodborne-y" fishing docks areas with lots of ladders and verticality, more fae castles, and some REALLY good loot that compliments a lot of builds from the base game.
