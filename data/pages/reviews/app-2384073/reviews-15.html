\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother garbage release before it's actually playable.
Apparently it introduces a memory leak, had the game take up 57GB of RAM and obviously this makes it crash. This after ~2h of gameplay.

Quality assurance has no place in today's releases.

As for the DLC itself, I've come about halfway through it (If relying on the rest of the game's formula), and it's so-so. Exploration feels unrewarding compared to the base game, one of the dungeons feels like I'm playing Dark Souls 2: Scholar of the First Sin again, cheap ♥♥♥♥ that makes you crawl at the pace of a crippled baby just to look around corners everywhere before approaching anything whatsoever.

Feels like a step down in every way possible, but maybe the second half is better once they've patched the issues.


Edit: Finished DLC and gathered most of the new items.
This is total, utter, complete destruction of the base game if you want to try playing as the new archetype - All from the ritualist neutering itself with its trait, to bosses being immune to the Miasma skill (Abomination in N'erud being a prime example) which seems a bug, to performance being shot into pieces when it finally started looking up, to memory leaks that makes the game unplayable after a bit if you dare use the game's menus/loadouts too much.

Huge disappointment, I love the concept of the game, I wanted more to it, but jesus christ I regret buying the ultimate edition when basic issues from release STILL aren't fixed. (Effects disappearing requiring you to restart the game as a prime example - Makes some enemy effects just not visible, like lightning)


TL;DR: Disappointment and garbage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC (at release) is quite lackluster, with less polish than the mainline campaign (and I can't help but compare it to Remnant, FtA's Swamp of Corsus, which is done way better).

The new class is great but not so much everything else. In a world where Resident Evil 4's DLC ($10) Separate Ways exists, this DLC is overpriced for one good class and a map that does not adopt the RNG the rest of the game does. The 'Forlorn Coast' is the same layout everytime and without the spectacle of good level design (or gorgeous vistas) like FromSoft titles, the DLC map is a one-run pony in a game where 'replayability is infinite'.

That's not to say it's downright ugly (all the way through), but the castle and the coast vistas does not save the look of a Fae-slain city; the issues of the main game (RNG-events, low enemy spawn rates, poor variety in enemies, optimization, etc.) are highlighted through this DLC.

Not to mention the titular Awakened King himself which dulls in comparison to Iskal Queen (from Swamp of Corsus) both in boss fight and story. I suppose the DLC is 'fine' if you have "modern" standards but you should expect more in your games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow! A good DLC!

What I like so far:

- More non-boss enemies that can actually present a significant difficulty.
Thus far I have often found myself in co-op, on apocalypse difficulty, running through
most enemies easily, while many bosses were very difficult. This DLC presents
enemies(non-bosses) that can move very quickly and tank at the same time, quite
refreshing. This is one of my favorite aspects of this DLC.

- Bigger groups of enemies that can overwhelm you.
- More Interesting layered level design.
- Fun theme! Great visuals!
- Improved co-op connectivity (regional)! wooo hooo!
- Boosted trait count from 65 to 85 yaay!
- $10 for all this extra content, wow!


What I dislike:

- Even worse optimization than the main game. Often continuous 5-10 frames less than other world levels. At this point Remnant 2's biggest flaw in my opinion is optimization. Otherwise I think the development team is making improvement after improvement in general. Thank-you so much for making this game Gunfire!

As it stands the DLC
and main game combined
are a 9/10 rating from me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompleted the DLC story within 24hrs of its release on Apoc and I must say I like it, the only major gripe that I have are performance stuff.

Over world:
The Static over world brings both positive and negative aspects to the table, the positives is that obviously everything is more detailed and we can assume some "lore" things thanks to environmental story telling, the other 2 good things at least for me is that once you know the map layout it makes it much faster to traverse it to farm what you need and the final one being the amount of shortcuts, its always good to have a easy way to go from one place to another without having to use the world stones granted I actually only felt the need to use about 20% of them but its still something that's welcome in my book.
The negatives is that you always know what to expect, what dangers are in each zone and you won't get surprised at all, the randomly generated over worlds while they can be bland for the most part they offer some randomisation that forces you to explore around and this dlc doesn't do that.
Maybe a way to somewhat fix this in a static over world would be random enemy placements, you guys already kinda do it with the fae knights/ gargoyle things that may or may not be on top of pillars.

The bosses:

There is not much to say about them if im completely honest they are perfectly serviceable bosses, I however would have liked to see the final boss to be harder in some way granted his importance in the Fae/Dran worlds.

The trinkets and weapons:

They offer a good amount of rings and weapons and I'm astonished at how far you have to go to get some of the stuff (Speaking in relation to a certain handgun) its not hard but can be annoying and time consuming.
One thing that I would like to have seen is more "trinkets" in the other worlds but I suppose we will get those in a good amount when they release the dlc for those specific areas, if that's their plan.

This is a bit out of place as it was a afterthought but I really got surprised how they tied base game "events/set pieces" to the new content bringing new fresh air into those.

Anyway I enjoyed the dlc and I do recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall pretty cool DLC. Probably the best "world" in the game aside from having too many of the worst enemy in the game (green guys).

If you're doing a casual playthrough (playing the game once, didn't bother with adventure mode rerolls), this DLC effectively expands the game by 25%. It is a new world gen for Losomn, so expect different variations of familiarly themed areas (sewers, burning towns, and Fae castle areas). Expect more items/armor/weapons, more secrets, and a new archetype. Spent about 7+ hours going through it all on the first go around.

It's also probably larger than the other world variations of Losomn, as it adds 10 new sublocations (Losomn has a total combined from either roll of 13 in the base game).

The final boss for this DLC / world variation is also pretty fun.\t\t\t\t\t
