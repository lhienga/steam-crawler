\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEh, I don't think the harsh reviews for this DLC are entirely warranted. The new area, while based in Losomn has a very cool aesthetic with the seaside town. On top of that the new enemies/elites are very enjoyable to fight. The new boss fights are all pretty great. Was a huge fan of Bruin in particular. Yeah, the Sunken Witch was just a beefed up elite but they made a really unique arena for it and altered the move-set somewhat.

Overall, I think for the price of the DLC, this is pretty good. All I really want out of Remnant DLC is more enemies, bosses, weapons/mods etc.

Now with that said... is this DLC as beefy and amazing as the Subject 2923 DLC from the first game that was the same price? No, not even close. That had an entirely new area with a ton of completely new enemies and like 6+ completely unique bosses that were almost all amazing.

So hopefully we'll eventually get a "final" DLC that wraps up things and adds a completely new area after I assume they make one for each area.

But still, if you like Remnant 2, this is more of it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't feel all the negative reviews are fully justified so I will leave my opinion here:

For context I played through the one shot adventure(only has dlc rolls) on nightmare.

The dire Fiend enemy was bugged on day one of the dlc but was fixed a day later. Its ranged projectile is fairly easy to dodge once you get used to it and just don't melee it and you'll be fine.

For the price you get a fair chunk of content, the new archetype is great fun. Spreading status effects across the map never gets old. The 2 new ranged weapons while at a glance are nothing special but with the right build are S tier contenders.
Some nice melee weapons too plus some great rings and amulets to spice up the game. Not to mention some interesting mutators and weapon mods.

The overall map design is an extension of Losomn hence why there are similar assets it retains the same eerie atmosphere that Losomn has. A bit of environmental story telling is present its fairly subtle but does illustrate the mad king and the conflict between the different races
I found the dungeons to be fun as well as challenging.
The final boss isn't very difficult tbh but feels kinda well balanced. Telegraphed attacks and plenty of counter opportunities.

It will likely take a few playthroughs of the dlc to find everything as there a loads of secrets.

I think it's worth the price. If you want extra value get the dlc bundle its about a 17% saving ( can't comment on the other dlc's yet of course).

It has plenty of new items and content with an added layer of challenge and enjoyment.
