The TL;DR
This DLC is honestly fantastic and I don't regret a single penny I spent on it. It adds tons of new content and was largely negatively received due to the fact one of the common enemies in it was overtuned at launch, making it easily able to one-shot builds on high difficulty levels. This has been ironed out now.

What does it add, though?
  • A new Archetype (Ritualist) themed around status effects and spellcasting.
  • A new, self contained mini-campaign (with no randomization) set in a new region of Losomn.
  • 12 new amulets spread across all worlds
  • 7 new weapons
  • 2 new armor sets
  • 1 new trait
  • 3 new mods (8 if you include the mods bound to boss weapons)
  • 8 new mutators
  • 26 new rings spread across all worlds
  • 2 new relics
  • Several secret questlines with unique rewards
  • Several new dungeons, events, and injectables (more than fit into the Losomn mini-campaign), all of which are added to the Losomn randomization pool.

This added another 50-60 hours to my playthrough and I've loved every second of it. If you like Remnant 2, you will like this DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

I think everyone can agree that the biggest sin of this DLC was the green and purple crawling dudes, they're cool in theory but cancer to actually fight especially when an area throws like 5 of them at you all at once. Wasn't exactly a fan of the Shotgun Dran either but to their credit they are a lot more fair to fight than the crawlies.

You may safely ignore 70% of the negative reviews because most of them are saying there wasn't enough content. This is because the DLC needs to be played multiple times in order to get all of the endings, weapons, items, secrets, etc. "It just didn't seem like there was much content when I went through" isn't valid because one run through equates to MAYBE 50% of the content available if you managed to find literally every single little thing loaded in on the One Shot mechanic. One secret even demands traversing all of the available worlds in the game to complete, so again, the "there's no content" claims are from people who one and done'd, which is baffling because the whole point of Remnant is replay-ability, to go back in and see what changed. But hey, whatever floats some people's boats I guess.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt seems weird like overall the DLC is well worth the price and fine, it adds more loot, things to play for, and new enemies etc., but it leaves a bad taste in my mouth. I couldn't stop playing this game -- until I started playing this DLC.

The frog enemies are straight up just un-fun, and there are a LOT of them. No crit spot, a 1 shot ability, jump scare cheap shot hits, and a CoD martyrdom that follows you. Nothing about fighting them is fun.
And its lame that in the new area, old Lossom dungeons are still in it. So you could play an Adventure on Lossom, get certain random dungeons, then switch over to an Adventure on the DLC, and end up getting the same dungeons in your adventure. That's weird.
I had no expectations, I just wanted more loot and more maps and more bosses and whatever, just more of the game, yet I feel kinda meh about it.
Its almost like, this team always puts out nothing but straight HEAT and always impresses, and then they release something just decent idk. Just a weird taste. Its not bad, its worth the cheap price, the game doesn't have in game purchases or battle passes, i wouldve even paid more for this, yet im still kinda just meh.


If you have been grinding the main game, and just want some new bosses, guns, enemies etc., to deal with, then get the DLC 100%.
But, if you haven't played the game much, I'd wait to buy this until you get to a point where you just want more Remnant 2 to explore.

I hope future DLC includes a roguelike mode like in the first Remnant, cuz that mode was AMAZING.\t\t\t\t\t
