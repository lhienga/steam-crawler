\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC is a 5.5 out of 10, which isn't a bad thing at all (or else why would I be recommending it?) It's a very standard 10 dollar dlc that you can expect, a new location with a few new weapons and items, some new enemies and about 4 extra hours of playtime to complete the first time.

The good thing about this DLC is that it gives you a "one shot adventure" where you can get the new area and experience everything all up front at once. After that, it becomes randomly added into the Losom world storyline where you can get some replay ability and rng out of it. This is good because it let's you get a lot of the content without needing to endlessly farm it, while also still giving you a reason to go back and replay it (also because you cannot 100% the dlc in just one playthrough due to some of the weapons and items being locked behind choices you make).

The neutral is that the one new class you get is great. It's fun playing around with it, but after a while it becomes just a regular class that you either main, or put aside for you're main classes. The weapons and gear are also pretty nice, with some new much needed rings and amulets that help bring out more status effects and some new power boosts. The increase to 85 trait points is also amazing because now you can really get more out of various builds, and get some extra fun out of just experimenting with weaker weapons and gear on harder difficulties. However this also makes apocalypse difficulty even easier with your meta "op" builds (which you can solve by not using those extra 20 points I guess)

Now the bad. The new balance scaling is terrible even for a souls like game. The new gremlin blob monsters are annoying and really horrible to fight in groups. Obviously this can all be fixed later, but it's still annoying.

So overall is this DLC worth 10 dollars? Yes. Should you get it? Yes. Is it this ground breaking, incredible DLC? No. It's ok, and that's fine.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed this DLC, the new weapons, the new bosses, everything - quite a lot actually. I will say though, Gunfire - you guy do use a bit of recycled assets quite a bit. Now I like Vanilla Losomn alot, but this is supposed to be a good opportunity to flex your muscles a bit with a new spin on a familiar area.

However, this DLC is only 10 dollars. So I can't be too harsh. It's very fun and I am still getting through content. It's worth it in my eyes, especially since I got the Ultimate Edition. Not disappointed, but there could be more.

Satisfied, but needs more seasoning I think is the best way to put it.
7/10. Solid. Enjoyable. Flavorful. Powerade. Gatorade

EDIT: Finished the DLC and now working on getting every new achievement. It's a solid 8/10 for me. Some of the new boss designs, without getting to spoiler-y, is fantastic.

The only 2 big things keeping this from being a 10/10 is the recycled content. It does rely heavily on it in some places and it's a real shame, Losomn is a amazingly designed world with a very intriguing web of connections that you'll pick up on. Things that will make you go "OOOHH- OH HE'S DOING THIS BECAUSE OF-" type beat.

BUT it goes up for me by 1 to a 8/10 because it adds ALOT of new content to help the new Archetype, the Ritualist, get a grasp on the game with BUNCH of new Mutators and Mods. Also... WORLD BOSSES! :D Go visit N'Erud or Yaesha again, you can now fight world bosses and mid dungeon bosses that have a chance of spawning either in a good fight location or a bad one! Spices the game up in a very fun way! Trust me - there is nothing scarier then walking through the Void Vessel Facility and seeing that ♥♥♥♥' flying slug beast poke its damn head around the corner and a boss health bar appears lmao.


8/10. It's fun and adds a bunch of new (albeit recycled) content. And it's also just 10 dollars. Which in my eyes is pretty fair given everything I just said. Cool new area, 5-6 new weapons, a STRONG new archetype, new bosses, new enemies, more lore, it's just a bit more. Though that 'more' could use a bit more seasoning, it's worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very rushed and lazy DLC, but it's more remnant so I did enjoy the new parts.

It's not that pricey and it was out reasonably soon so I do feel like I can't complain that much. I'd give it a neutral review if it was possible.

Items:
On the playthrough we did it took 8 hours (co-op), we did not get a single one of the 2 new guns, but did find 3 useless melee weapons!
Had to look up how to get the other two and make 2 new worlds to get them, although both of them are really cool.
In comparison, we went back to main game after this and even though we'd found them before, there were loads of items to find and cool encounters.

Areas:
Most areas are just re-used assets from the Fae area in the base game, I don't mind all the bits and bobs being reused of course, but in some regions there were huge chunks of the map which were just copy paste rooms, enemies and barrels. Really stuck out.

Bosses:
There are TWO bosses in this entire DLC. The middle of the area one was really cool, great design and an interesting fight.
The end boss was the easiest and dullest fight I've had in the game so far, I think we killed him second try without having to learn anything. Come on, this was supposed to be the god of the fae, I was expecting someone to conquer the Red King which was a fantastic fight.



TL:DR: It's more remnant, so get it. But don't expect to be wowed.\t\t\t\t\t

On the one hand, I think that it's legitimately hard to recommend a single "Level" with a runtime of about an hour (Or two if you're not already geared and ready to shred) at the price of $10.

Yes, it does come with 6-ish new weapons, a new class, and a decent chunk of new gear between rings, mutators, mods, armor and amulets but I every time I ask myself if I can recommend it, there's this internal "Well, yeah BUUUUUT--".
