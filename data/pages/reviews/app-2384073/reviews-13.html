\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA solid DLC, I enjoyed my time. I've beat the new route twice so far, will probably do it a few more times to try and find more secrets (I try to avoid just googling stuff unless I've tried my best). I think it's worth it if you liked the base game and just like playing it, but it's not going to bring you back for a long time if you just want to beat it by itself and bounce.

For the good:
The new areas look cool, there's a couple secrets I suspect exists but I have to go try and figure out how to get to em. The new sewer level in particular is the coolest sewer area by a mile, and I wish all the new tilesets had that level of detail.

I like the new class a lot, as it addresses my main issue with a lot of the existing classes. Most of the classes "abilities" are actually just timed passives, which isn't super interesting. It has 2 active damage abilities that do their own damage, change your gameplay, and can be built around. That's what I want out of my classes. I like picking classes for what they actually do, rather than what stats they add.

I like how it slots into the current lore of Losomn. One of the early abberations has a cool lore setup and I wish they did that more here.

I think the way they present options for dealing with the final boss is cool and I wish there was more of that. I've found 3 for 3 different rewards so far, might be more will have to see.

For the bad/unfortunate parts:

The new one of the new enemy's definitely a bit of a pain and probably do a bit too much damage for just bumping into you, but I just adapted the build to deal with them. Namely using higher stagger weapons to stop their perma rushdown. They also have an on death effect that is a bit too disruptive. I got used to it, but they're unironically harder than a lot of the elites in the game.

I think the final boss is very, very lackluster. I don't know if there's a "true form" I just haven't unlocked or something. But he's easier and mechanically less interesting than one of the new mini-boss they added, which is a real shame cuz presentation wise it's one of the best bosses in the game, it's just mechanically uninteresting and very, very easy. It's one of those fights that look epic but doesn't feel epic, even when compared to the other world bosses on this very world.

Performance should be better, but that applies to the game at large as well. I hope they do another general performance pass, I don't think this reliance on DLSS only to still get spotty performance is good.

I wish there were a few more guns added. Maybe there's more, but the only one I know of is from the final boss. Most of what I found are melee weapons and I'm never going to use melee weapons that don't offer good utility to things besides melee builds. They really should've allowed you to dodge cancel melee imo, it just gets you killed on higher difficulties with how many enemies have ranged attacks. I personally have no reason to ever use anything besides the Krell axe for its ranged elemental procs and the lightsaber for clearing pots and stuff for scrap.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWorth more than the asking price.

The DLC is good.
It is more Remnant 2, and plays similarly to how the other worlds did.

I especially like the verticality of some of the puzzles. It lets you see your goals before reaching them, and you can try and figure out a way to get to them, or leave them for later. Most puzzles I've seen are far less obtuse than some of the ones in the base game, and can be solved without looking things up. There is one exception, which has a misleading puzzle clue, but that one can be brute forced with the correct combination of skills, or with a good eye for detail.
Of course, it may just be the case that I didn't notice the really obtuse ones.

Much of the gear you find is focused on status effects, which is something the base game didn't touch on all that much. You can come up with some neat new builds and item combinations by using certain pieces from the DLC.
From what I've seen, this DLC also doesn't introduce power creep.

If you get careless, some of the enemies can easily take players out with a self-detonation attack. They are sufficiently tanky to be able to reach the player, too. Fights are interesting, but not too frantic, so long as the player counters the new enemies' mechanics.

The new basic enemies feature blunderbusses, which mixes things up just enough to feel fresh, without breaking the design idea of the game.

The new elite enemies are quite entertaining to fight.

Most of the new boss encounters are interesting, and give the player some amount of control over how the fight progresses. However, some of them can feel unfun, if they spawn with the wrong modifiers. Fighting an enemy with a large weapon in narrow hallways with breakable walls is fun. Fighting that same enemy in the same arena while every non-neutral dodge creates a lingering damage AoE is not.

The new character class has interesting combo potential. It comes with many strong effects. Depending on the active skill one chooses, it also has some severe downsides, which a well-adjusted build can play around with, or even benefit from.

The story is good enough. It is interesting, and turns some prior concepts on their heads, but you can still see how this is just one potential outcome of many for the realm of Losomn.

The voice acting is superbe, and deserves immense praise.

All in all, this DLC is worth more than what the devs are asking for it. I'd say that, to me, it'd be worth ~15€, maybe slightly more.\t\t\t\t\t
