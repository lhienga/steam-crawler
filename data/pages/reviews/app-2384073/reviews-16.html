\r\n\t\t\t\t\tupon completion I am... underwhelmed and slightly frustrated by this small expansion,

While I didn't expect it to be very long at all considering we're getting 4 DLC's (presumably one per area), It still feels way too short even after scouring the map for every collectible and secret.

What my biggest issue with this expansion is, is how ludicrously overtuned it is. The new enemies that spit and crawl on walls are terribly designed and do way, way too much damage in comparison to their speed and their health pool. I played this on veteran and found myself getting killed in two hits from these ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. Definitely a fun ruiner for sure.

Also, every enemy in this zone just seems to be way too strong in general. Like as if Gunfire Games expects us to have an endgame apocalypse build ready to go. No, that's not the case. People like myself who are just jumping back in after a long while away to experience the new content are getting the ♥♥♥♥ stomped out of them. The dlc could be a fun short adventure if it wasn't so horribly unbalanced.

Hopefully they patch this into better conditions and then don't repeat the same mistakes for the next one. Very disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant 2 is my personal GOTY and knowing the dlcs of the first game i expected something great. well i wasnt disappointed entirely but unfortunately the dlc was a letdown.
I like the new area albeit its not a new setting - both parts (town and castle) are just reskinned parts of the main campaign.
The new class feels underwhelming after they nerfed the buggy neck into the ground, rendering it unuseable. Dont get me wrong a nerf was necessary cause it broke the game but it felt pretty cool. maybe nerf in numbers would have been more appropriate rather than killing the item with a panic patch.
So there is 1 new trashmob enemy and 1 reskin trashmob enemy. the new enemy spawns bubbles that explode while its alive and after its death. it comes in 2 colour variants and both are very unfun to play against. the reskinned mob is the sniper losomn enemy but with a shotgun. and because they thought it would be fun they positioned them under ladders, behind walls, you get the idea. the problem is that 2 out of 5(?) pellets are enough to kill you on apocalypse. I think that wasnt very well thought through.
there is 2 new minibosses that also serve as mainbosses. Not going into heavy spoiler terrain here but one of them has a pretty cool bossfight while the other is legit a trashmob with extra hp.
I have yet to find all the new rings but im set on amulets and weapons and i have to say im disappointed - very underwhelming what is a shame.
Leading up to the next point. No other build than HUGS feels somewhat competitive. Ive tried plenty of builds but for goofing around mostly. I completed apo on most of them. But HUGS is just broken and i find myself in a situation similar to Eldenring (where nothing but greatsword jump attack spam with bleed is even close as good) where once you played it, nothing else feels rewarding. The new weapons unfortunately arent close to replacing crescent moon, heros sword etc.
I fully recommend this dlc to tryhard remnant fans like myself, but to everyone else i would not, hence the thumbs down.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall good, 6/10.
The positives outweigh the negatives in my case, might not be the case for you.

Class is both good and fun, nice crowd control with more focus on debuffing and the likes.
Weapons as far as I know aren't anything to write home about, though the shotgun is the exception to this.
The visual design is amazing, both for the new enemies and especially the areas.
The frog enemies suck major ass and are way overtuned, health pool too high, density too high and damage dealt is WAY too high for the amount of clusters they leave around.

Bosses are fun, though someone at Gunfire really needs to playtest their difficulty effects (i.e. Vicious, Hearty, etc) on the bosses, as "trapper", a prefix that create nuking damage areas when you dodge isn't ideal for a boss with no open areas in their arena.

I mean, I'd pay the asking price for this, but it isn't gonna be lingering in my mind for any extended time after I finish it, also, where did the clever puzzles go? Yeah, I'm looking at you "open 5 doors in order during a dream cycle".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very budget, recycled excursion of content; feels more like a large patch than a monetized release.


This content was put out only a few months after the initial game, which indisputably means it either was created within just a few months; or resources were diverted towards it prior to the game even launching. Either possibility is made even worse by the fact that this game launched with an obscene amount of glitches which took an obscene amount of time to very gradually patch. Although that has not stopped new glitches from emerging recently. It begs the question of how releasing this first instance of monetized bonus content so quickly may have impacted the main game launch and or its post-launch support.

Not only does the new content take place in a pre-existing area type but the new map is largely static, unlike iterations of this same area type in the main game. So not only did they recycle the setting but they also downgraded much of the map design from being compositely randomized to linear and consistent. Even worse they chose the most uncreative and mundane setting in the entire game to do this with. At least sixty percent of the new content takes place in the world of the Dran; a place defined by its grey cobblestone streets, rectangular wooden board architecture and human-identical inhabitants. Although a decent amount of new environmental fixtures exist to freshen things up a bit; they still all reside within the same monotonous, uncreative setting.

The story is interesting at least but also feels somewhat incongruent with the main game, particularly due to the seemingly extreme transformation in the One True King's behaviour. In general the way this world transitions from one state to another between the main game and this new content is abrasively drastic to me. Which only makes revisiting this world feel even more unnecessary and forced.

There are some new enemies and some recycled enemies, some of these new foes are obnoxious while others are not. There is one new armour set and one recycled armour set. There are two new decently interesting guns and a third which supposedly requires the player to replay through most of the main game to unlock it. All the new melee weapons are extremely generic and have no significant special abilities to compete with the existing melee weapons that do. The new archetype stands out at least, as it creates more fully fledged build options for damage-over-time kits and the ability animations look reasonably stylish. Many new rings, amulets and mutators of varying usefulness have also been added as usual.


Overall this content does not feel explicitly bad and serves the most basic purpose of expanding on the game experience to some extent, but it is steeped in an atmosphere of scant cheapness that does not inspire much hope for the future. And players would really just not miss out on much if they chose to pass up this content. There are some interesting new things and decent looking map sections but otherwise it is just a generic slog across a going-through-the-motions gameplay gauntlet of so-so sameness, with an extremely minimal amount of halfway decent item rewards plus only one unique boss fight sprinkled about to actually reward the player for bothering.

Between how miserably broken the game was at launch and how arbitrarily rushed its first monetized addition is, I am really left wondering why these things keep happening in a game series that has attracted so much revenue. Although considering these developers took the time to add in two new items that actively mock players who criticized a part of the game balance, perhaps it is not surprising that their work is a mess.
