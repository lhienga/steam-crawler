\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for the enjoyment of optimizing systems in a clicker format... keep looking.

What you WILL do is relentlessly click on blocks until they get broken down into cubes. And you'll do that for a long time. If you don't have RSD, this might be the solution! Eventually you'll be able to build a couple buildings that make it require fewer clicks. And eventually you'll get buildings that do the clicking for you. Slowly. Very slowly. More buildings? Nope, massive exponential cost increases for the units eliminate this option, so hope you'll got lots of free time on your hands.

Meanwhile these helper buildings need to be fuelled. Manually. So you'll start carefully looking at your few buildings to spot if they've run out of fuel. Many which have their fuel indicators partially hidden behind other buildings. And you can't fuel until the machine is completely empty, not a second earlier. So you'll have to mouse-over to see if the refuel option exists. For each machine. At which point you'll click on it to top it back up again. Repeat. Failure to do so means things start grinding to a halt in only a few minutes.

Perhaps you think you'll have fun optimizing arrangements for better production? Nope. Besides the exponential cost increases that make laying down even a couple of extra buildings completely unfeasible, building deletion is not lossless, with a 50% resource penalty. There are buildings you can build later on that eliminate this penalty, which leads to the question - Why? If you're just going to get rid of the penalty anyway, why would you bother inflicting it when the person is the least experienced with the game?

Anyway, that's my take. I rolled the dice, didn't win on this roll. But I wish a demo had been made available. At the very least, make sure you watch a few Let's Play videos. And make sure you watch them properly - no fast forwarding - you sure as hell can't in the game itself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile this looks fantastic, it's just not quite polished enough. It feels like they didn't playtest it. It does *some* interesting things, but mostly it's not really all that interesting.

Some points of tension:
* Every component has the same shape, so after four hours and about 40% of the achievements I've made two designs that are significantly different from each-other, and it's only because there's a single component so far with a different shape. That's the only reason the designs are substantially different.
* Rebuilding things close to the miner is a nightmare because it will place a cube that you then have to manually mine.
* The game teaches you that as you dig deeper, there's a linear progression of what materials you dig up. It breaks this rule as soon as it matters for your build, no longer giving you any green cubes shortly after you realise you no longer need to accommodate for a mix of green and non-green cubes and change your layout.
* The game lags the entire computer when you minimise it, making it not really playable as an idle.
* The story triggers can happen kind of whenever and start without the player's intervention, so sometimes when you come back you need to start scrolling through a chat history, try to figure out what you've already read, and then go through the history instead of seeing it play out in real-time as I assume the devs intended.

It's not really all *that* far away from a good game, but right now I'm not interested in playing it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game where you go from clicker to resource management and automatization. Worth the price and time invested. If you like progression focused games, factory games or incremental/idle games you will find something interesting here.

PROS
  • I loved the game.
  • I loved the gameplay shifts that changed the game mechanics completely.
  • I love that there is an ending.
  • Overall progression feels rewarding and satisfying.
  • Graphics looks good which is rare in incremental/idle.
  • I loved the cryptic story and you can see in the videos that at some moments things caught me offguard and I was curious on whats happening

CONS
  • The weak points are early grind for first silos, I used autoclicker and was annoyed, cant imagine doing it without autoclicker (in the latest patch autoclicker mechanics was introduced when you build specific building).
  • I want to play more so Prestige mechanics would be nice to make the game last for months, but it might be out of the game's concept.
  • Managing base layout when you have some tall buildings is a bit annoying
  • Cant go wide, so its a tall progression.

Playthrough and guides playlist: https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLpaOv0KSQPmpDfHC-jSspG8wWQu-EEG2s

Overall great game, especially for this price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA complete waste of time.

It's like Factorio or Satisfactory, only without any action or conveyor belts.
You can click on blocks and you can build structures that either produce, gather, or convert resources.
That's it.

Every building you place increases in cost, so you never even get the cool mega structures other building games have. This also means that most of the game is just waiting around doing nothing waiting for resources to gather, since unlike the better games you'll never enough resources to expand beyond one or two extractors.

There's also a story that plays out in the corner of the screen.
It's dumb and meta. The main character (you) is a nameless nobody that is playing this game rather than hanging out with friends. You get text messages from your friend, and tell them about this stupid game you're playing rather than doing something useful with your time.

Really feels like the devs made this game as a cruel joke or commentary on clicker/resource gathering games, but the game still sucks so... whatever.

I feel bad for wasting a day with this.\t\t\t\t\t
