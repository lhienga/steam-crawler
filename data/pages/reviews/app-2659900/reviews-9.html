\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game - it looks great, it isn't the exact same thing as every other game out there, and it started off promising. Unfortunately it didn't continue that way. To be clear, this is not a sim or a factory builder in any way - it's 100% an idle game/clicker. That's fine if, like me, you enjoy that genre, but unfortunately it's not at all well tuned for being one.

The game centers around digging up blocks and breaking them for resources. The more machines you build to do this, the more expensive those machines become. Fine, limits make sense, but the scaling is such that these are effectively hard limits on how much you can build, and it's not that much. Further, occasionally you'll get some new tech that basically requires you to completely rebuild. Again, fine, prestige-like mechanics are OK, as long as they promote new gameplay or at least open new avenues in the game. Unfortunately, here is where this game just falls short. Rebuilding doesn't make anything faster. It just wastes your time. It feels like the farther I get into this game, the more egregious the time wasting has become.

Let's dig up a new resource - oh no, it's going to completely destroy your stock of existing resources. How can you prevent that? An expensive new building that requires the resources that were just destroyed. Guess you'd better rebuild and set up the new building this time through. Will it go faster this time? Nope, just literally watching the exact same events unfold, with a slightly different plan of attack this time. Whee.\t\t\t\t\t
