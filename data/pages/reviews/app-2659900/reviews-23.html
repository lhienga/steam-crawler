\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've not finished the game yet (I only get to play an hour or so in the evening) but I felt compelled to write this review because of my general disagreement with the negative ones.

This is not an idle game in the way that they’re generally presented, it’s an incremental game. This means that it’s not so much about idling but about slow grindy progress to an ever more complex goal. You chip away at a mountain in order to eventually reduce it to rubble. But you have to be there to make sure your machines run and can do the chipping. It’s fair to get confused because the dev did specifically mention “idle elements” in the store description and some people assume (understandably) it means total idling.

I personally enjoy the various bits of optimization you can do and think the game is generally fun and enjoyable. Some aspects of it can definitely be improved but as of day 3 the dev is making daily patches based on community feedback.

The closest game I could compare it to is Kittens Game (so called dark souls of incrementals) that also allows for some idling but can only realistically be progressed with active play (or “automation” cheats).

With my current play time I’m only up to the 6th tier of blocks but here are some things I personally would like altered/improved

1.The descriptions of some buildings are too vague. The broadcast tower comes to mind. Once placed I didn’t notice any difference and only later realized that it makes the transfer of resources faster… or so I think anyway?

2.The rate of cost increase for buildings is too high. The game is very heavily tied to playing “tall” and converting newer blocks to lower tier ones. So initially you might think you can create a repeatable line of extractors with some support buildings, but because of the prohibitive costs of extra buildings it’s an absolute waste of time. I think that’s a mistake, as it makes you end up with just one “clump” of buildings and to be honest in the vast white void that acts as your play field it just feels like a waste.

3. The conversion buildings (so far as I’ve seen this might change later in the game) don’t seem to interact with anything other than silos. They’re just set and forget. That’s lame, some way of enhancing or semi-automating their production would be appreciated because they feel “detached” from the extractor gameplay.

4. This also might become available later (not seen in the 5 hours) but some “blocks per second/minute” counters would be appreciated for individual blocks. since they’re not present you just kinda have to guess how long you’ll have to wait for the next structure.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tin it's original form, i couldn't recommend this due to all the rough edges (hard to read text, some clumsy UI issues, sometimes ear-and-eye-melting sounds and visuals, uneven pacing/grind, some parts too grindy).

but, in just a few days, just enough things have been tweaked in the UI and the progression curve that i can now recommend it. it's an interesting blend of factory game, clicker, and idle game (although for much of the game you can't actually idle much or at all).

however, it might still be best to wait a patch or two before jumping in, especially for the accessibility issues like text size, if you think those will bother you. like all such games, some parts of the grind and such are core parts of the game and, while polarizing, can't really be 'fixed' without significantly altering the experience (which will please some and displease others). i'm curious to see where the dev goes with this and if they can walk the line!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat interesting looking game.

If you can stomach getting addicted to micromanaging refill buildings and waiting for resources to fill up for you to build the next mining factory area or perhaps experiment with a more efficient design to completely rebuild your entire setup. Here you go.

There is automation, but it becomes available hours into the game. For me, maybe 18 hours in. I had to rebuild my entire setup as I never knew this could be automated. And I didn't even fully automate everything as I gave up.
There are next game stage walls with steep resources cost that you have to wait hours for resources to fill up to unlock.
Building cost goes up by either twice the amount or exponentially. The cost goes down if you remove the buildings.
Meaning, you will have to either wait a couple hours, micromanaging the game or build a efficient design that you still have to micromanage.

I'm sure the gradient wells or next game stage would be well rewarding for my time spent, increasing my resources to the point I can perhaps build even more. Or be waiting for my resources to accumulate to afford the next building again.\t\t\t\t\t
