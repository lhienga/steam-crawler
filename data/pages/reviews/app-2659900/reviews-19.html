\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: The game is not what you expect it to be. In its own way its quite fun and I do love myself a factory optimization game but it just doesn't add up together. And the worst of it - I cannot think of a way to make it better for myself while keeping the unique ideas intact.

First of all, this is not an idle game - there is no full automation at all and something you would call a semi-automation rolls up after about 5 hours. Maybe I haven't progressed to where it unlocks but it seems I never will. This just sucks and is definitely a bad trait for an idle/clicker game. When you grow your factory at least to some extent manually refilling everything becomes a nightmare. Fixed camera angle also makes certain objects completely invisible behind others so you can't see if something ran out of fuel. Pure pain

The quirk of the game is that the same quarry gives you different elements based on depth. Rather than expanding the factory to new sites you need to keep digging and maintain the efficiency of it as it goes. And while it is a nice idea the delivery is a lost cause. At some point every resource gets a quirk. One of them requires a very specific building that does absolutely NOTHING except allow you to mine it. I wouldn't spoil the rest but every one after that is a lot more frustrating. These quirks really make certain parts of the game into an RTS.

The pricing of expansions ids just crazy. It is a x1.5 exponent for each copy for most buildings. With the basic resources it is sort of ok but as soon as you get to the quirk resources you get into a completely stagnant position where you basically redo all the production from scratch because of course the current setup is not relevant and the new resource is gathered painfully slowly.

The core is there, the concept is somewhat decent but it just isn't an enjoyable experience all together.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game. Cool visuals, interesting interactions between machines, creative and unique mechanics. The problem is that it's kind-of a puzzle game, kind-of an idle game, and kind-of a factory builder, but isn't particularly good at any of them.

How it might be improved: greatly reduce the resource cost scaling and add more actual automation. Unlocking new machines, building out the factory, and finding the optimal machine placements/combinations was the most entertaining part for me, but you're very restricted on how large the factory can get because it simply costs too much to expand. Even at 100x speed with the largest factory I could make I would need days of idling to afford the next machine. But I can't actually do something like that even if I wanted to because machines take resources that must be refilled to continue running. There are machines that refill other machines automatically, but you must refill those machines manually every few minutes anyway. In other words, it can never be fully automated (maybe there's some end-game content I haven't reached yet. I'm past the "trees").

Even with cheats this game is slow because the unlocks are random and progression is often arbitrary and unpredictable. Am I making progress? I don't know. Do I have to do something specific to progress? I don't know. You could call it a "puzzle" to figure out how to progress, but given the time-scales involved it's really off-putting that "I may-or-may not have done the special thing that at some point in the next few hours will trigger the event".

Overall, would I recommend the game? Nope. I sped it up and gave myself resources and it was still too grindy. I don't know what the dev's vision for the game was, but I hope they are willing to make more updates to improve it.\t\t\t\t\t
