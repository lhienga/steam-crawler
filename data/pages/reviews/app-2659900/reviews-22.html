\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have read some reviews that made me hesitant to try this game. BUT, I am glad that my curiosity and "itch" was stronger than the repellent from the reviews.

I love factory games. I like the challenge of building and maximising output while managing resources to advance production and options. The "clicker" portion of the description was a real negative factor for me. I was very worried about having to continuously click to enjoy this game. How relieved and surprised I was to find out how untrue those reviews were. You CAN automate to a certain extent. Yes, you still have to actively manage and pay attention, but it isn't constant clicking all the time once you advance a little.

NOTE: You cannot "MOVE" machines once you place them. However, you eventually unlock the ability to destroy machines. At first, you get some of the cost back, then a bit more, and eventually ALL of the cost back for no penalty at all. I spent a lot of time researching how to move/delete machines. Hopefully this helps someone.

TIP: READ carefully what the machines do. Plan ahead and get rewarded.

At the time of writing this review, I have just reached past 40km down. I am no where close to feeling done with this game, and the "itch" has definitely been scratched; ESPECIALLY for the price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've fully completed the game and I've got to say that it's a gem in the rough. There's something really special about this game that even though it's a little rough around the edges and at points frustrating, I really enjoyed my time with it. There's enough unique ideas within the design of the game that I can overlook some of the more frustrating aspects of the game. The dev is also incredibly fast at implementing some of the QoL changes which is greatly appreciated. At the moment there's some performance issues around mid game, so turn off the sound and destroy the fill director if the game starts to lag.

One mistake I'm seeing people make is the perception that this is an idle incremental game. This game is not meant to be idled, it requires constant engagement. At first, it feels tedious having to click so much. But once the game opens up and you gain access to a variety of machines, I found the task of optimization and planning took over as a priority as you try to balance your resources. The constantly shifting task priorities and new machines requires you to constantly rethink your setup which kept me busy.

Once you get past the initial start of the game, it really makes you think about the choices that you have to make instead of throwing the newest, shiny machine in haphazardly into your setup. The steep cost increases for duplicate machines, refueling machines manually, time required to setup a new extraction point, limited range of machines, steep resource costs to hit the next unlock all require you to make challenging choices to optimize output. The more I played, the more time I had to tinker with the layout of my machines as the next unlock took longer and longer to reach. There was a point where it would take 5 hours of manual labor to unlock the next thing, but by optimizing my setup I cut the time down to an hour to gain the required bottleneck resource, and then while waiting I used that hour to optimize other aspects of my setup which helped create a better base for the next task.

Another thing is that this game does is it does not hold your hand, at all. You are on your own except for very vague hints here and there. There were a few places where I felt there could be a bit more direction to reduce player frustration, but overall I quite liked the feeling of "WHAT IS GOING ON HERE" and trying to puzzle out the mysteries and intricacies of the game.

Overall I'd say all the game really needs is some more QoL changes to tweak some numbers and some interface elements to really make it shine. The core of the game is really solid and has made a deep impression on me. Kudos to the dev!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game more than I did - it felt promising for a while, and I tried to stick with it, but I feel like it never quite comes together as a satisfying experience.

I found the narrative component compelling, and it was certainly fun at first puzzling out how to optimize my little factories so that I could progress through each "stage" and see where things were going. Alas, this is a game that really tests your patience, and the rewards of unlocking new machines, dialogue, and mysteries come along far too slowly and far too tediously for my liking. Like a lot of other reviews point out, calling this an "idle" game is a misnomer - you are forced to micromanage constantly in order to make meaningful progress, otherwise your machines stop operating entirely. Which might not feel so bad if it didn't take literal hours of this micromanaging to unlock each tier of new upgrades. After going through multiple cycles of spending hours at a time refilling the same batteries/silos over and over again, just to discover that it would take several more hours of battery refilling in order to afford the next slate of upgrades, I eventually decided the juice wasn't worth the squeeze. (For reference, I unlocked eight types of resources before finally tapping out).

To sum up: it's got a clean aesthetic and some interesting elements in the narration and gameplay, but the tedium makes it very difficult to enjoy. Unfortunately I find it hard to recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
