\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSixty four, as I am sure you can guess based on the tags, is a clicker based game which focuses on cubes. While this game is far from perfect, it is a fun challenge (especially from people who enjoy 100%ing games) that slowly grows over time. You start with a single extractor and will slowly build a few more. There is some automation in the game, via buildings that you will unlock that will allow for minor automation at the beginning, medium automation in the middle stage, and full automation near end game.
My first run through the game would have taken around 35 hours if I had not gone for an achievement. This was with some minor help from a friend that had a bit more playtime than myself. There is a lot of small scale optimizing and some room for testing your own creations to find the most optimal layout. If you are not one for that particular function, you can always find setups online to assist in progressing through the game. Overall, it took around 55 hours to get all the achievements (minus a 64 hour playtime achievement), but I was forced to look up a guide for two achievements that would have otherwise been very difficult.
There are a lot of people being quite harsh with their reviews by complaining about the lack of proper automation, but I would like to point out that the tags are "clicker" and "strategy" (alongside a few other irrelevant ones). I am not entirely sure why they thought a game without the "automation" tag would be heavily influenced by automation, but it seems rather moronic to make that complaint when most clickers are "grindy" and time consuming. This game's scale isn't very large, so having full automation in the very beginning would ruin the whole point of it. For those with patience, there will be automation that evolves while you play, getting better as you progress.
To put it plainly, if you enjoy clicker games with some optimization mixed in, then I would recommend this game. It will provide you with dozens of hours of content. Overall, I would give this game a 7/10. It was fun and challenging, but left some to be desired story and mechanic wise (plus the sounds can get a little irritating so I recommend using the "sound on/off" button when you hit the escape key).\t\t\t\t\t
