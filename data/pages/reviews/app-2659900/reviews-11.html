\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel very mean giving a not recommended to this, as it's a very worthy attempt from an indie developer and is great in many ways, but falls just short. I feel particularly mean as I've put quite a few hours into this (currently I'm on the end game grind) and it's really cheap, so do feel free to try it for yourself.

It's gorgeously presented, has many good ideas and is technically excellent.

Sadly there are a few areas where it's not quite there and tips things towards frustration rather than enjoyment. The progression is confusing and there are times where it's not at all clear what comes next. This is made worse in that there are balance problems and certain bits are easy and others a painful grind. Many of the descriptions are unclear and don't tell you crucial information about exactly what the machines do. It's also possible at points to accidentally go down a path which makes things much harder.

The plot as it is sort-of interesting but almost non-existent.

Many of these things are things which could be improved on by further tuning (and indeed there have been a few patches that have helped). So hopefully this will eventually be in a better place.

Again, I feel mean for being rather negative, so do try it out if it's your sort of game. Just be prepared for some frustrating parts.\t\t\t\t\t
