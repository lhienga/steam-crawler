\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStellar and unique incremental/factory game. Dev is very responsive in the game's Discord and has hit the ground running since release iterating on feedback from the playerbase.

Requires pretty active play, which I wouldn't consider inherently good or bad. Lots of unfolding mechanics that incentivize you to refactor your builds multiple times, and it doesn't feel punishing to do so. Cool little story to give it a weird, interesting vibe.

It also seems to be deliberately somewhat opaque in the mechanics, aiming for that "discover for yourself" experience.

This game might be for you if you like tweaking optimal builds and coming up with solutions in incremental or factory games. It might not be if you just want to see Number Go Up.\t\t\t\t\t

Revised Review:
The new improvements are in a very nice direction. While most are relatively minor, they have had a significant impact on the gameplay experience.

The main issue with 64 currently is that it can’t decide if it wants to be an idler or a factory builder. The combination of these two game genres really doesn’t work due to the exponential cost increases of a clicker preventing the entire point of a factory builder, which is actually building a factory. The blank, empty world is specifically designed to make people want to fill it, but that simply isn’t possible due to the exponential costs. I would say that it feels more like an idler that is pretending to be a factory builder, but the placement of machines does matter and wouldn’t work as a 2d idler.

After reviewing all of the machines, hell gems disappearing from mines doesn’t seem like a problem as I originally believed. The gem cost for most machines is minor, and the only continued cost currently is for silos, which only use two per refueling. You get gems from chromalits degradation, so you don’t actually need them from mines. Honestly, it seems like a positive overall as gem destabilizers are no longer needed. I’m not sure why I was worried about this previously.

64 manages to be something pretty unique despite its flaws. While the combination of mechanics that the dev has chosen don’t fit together very well, they are actively implementing feedback and resolving issues. Beyond the gameplay is where 64 excels. All of the individual pieces, such as who the player is, who the person we’re communicating with is, what happened to both of us, what the surreal, dream-like world world is, what the cubes are, and what lays at the end of the game/story create a giant interconnected mystery that is compelling enough that the player wants to solve it despite the gameplay issues.\t\t\t\t\t

It also needs more polish, even though it's pretty. The UI is not the best, with things like dark mode being a tiny little torch on the bottom left of the escape menu. There are essentially no settings to tweak. Dark mode is also just slightly less bright mode.

In its current state it has a lot of potential, but feels like an early access game and needs a whole lot more love.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are looking for a chill make numbers go higher idle game, this is definitely not that, keep looking.

This game bills itself as an idle game, but it's not. Zero progress happens when you're not actively playing. I like the idle game Clicker Heroes by Playsaurus, so I thought I'd try this, but it's awful. The only progress I made came because of a comment on Reddit describing how to set up to grind rock, and using an auto-clicker, and even then progress is glacially slow. Good graphics, the text thread/hints is the skeleton of a clever idea, but you have to have enjoyable game play to succeed and this doesn't.

I give the developer credit for being so responsive to feedback, and having released 3 patches already with plans for more. However, the patches lower the cost of several buildings, and future ones are planned to address quality of life issues. It sure sounds to me like the game wasn't sufficiently playtested before being released.\t\t\t\t\t

Avoid, there are better ways to spend your time.\t\t\t\t\t
