\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm gonna say "yes" to recommend because overall its an ok automation game.

It has some major drawbacks though. You're gonna do a lot of clicking to start off, until you can automate that task. Then, you're gonna do a lot of waiting around. The speed of the machines is only so fast, and upgrades/new equipment increase in price every time you place something. So you'll end up making a machine which is as efficient as you figured out how to do, and then... wait. You can't build many more of that same machine yet because your resources are coming in at a fraction of what you need. If you re-build your setup, depending on where you place it, its gonna start all over with the basic resources and you have to wait (again) for them to "dig" deep enough to get the advanced resources again.

The game gives you access to machines that are way outside your ability to use them, way too early. At first, each new thing is relevant to what you're doing, and then it just starts unlocking new stuff, some of which has terrible descriptions of what they do (favoring vague nonsense fake technical descriptions of what they do instead of a functional description).

I *want* to really like this game, the visual are sharp and the concept is good. But the pacing is terrible, and if you get it wrong you're gonna either be clicking until you have carpal tunnel or you're gonna be waiting around unsure if what you made is efficient enough or if you should scrap the whole thing and make something else.

Overall its... ok. It sort of gets in its own way a lot, and prevents you from doing the fun part (designing machines) in order to wait around for things, which experimenting with is difficult because of the various ways rebuilding can slow you down even more. Most automation games let you keep building and building to your heart's content, making things better and better over time, whereas this game expects you to figure out maximum efficiency from the beginning and punishes you for not knowing what to do.

I would recommend Shapez over this for anyone who enjoys the designing part of these automation games. The machines cost no resources so you can build as much as you want, go crazy, delete and rebuild, eventually copy and paste complex machines, etc etc. Its goal is to make X of each shape it asks for, and every minute of the game is spent making things.\t\t\t\t\t

Jokes aside this game is great!

While simple in depth, it is fantastic and surprisingly addictive for a quick and pretty looking incremental game. The devs really nailed the aesthetic, both satisfying to look at yet plays well into the ongoing mystery playing in the background.

I do wish there was more, after reaching the end I wanted to keep playing, however whats cool was I loaded back into the game and can pick up from where I left off before the end mechanic. I believe that that's about as far as you can go though which is a shame, the scaling of progression makes it so you'll probably hit a point where you cant to much else, I don't know that for a fact but it feels that way.

Otherwise this was fun while it lasted!\t\t\t\t\t
