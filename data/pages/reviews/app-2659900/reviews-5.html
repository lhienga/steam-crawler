\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's good. I'm glad to have read reviews before buying, as it correctly set my expectations to enjoy the game to the fullest. This is not a Factorio-like, it's an incremental game. Just happens to have a factory type gameplay loop, but make no mistake, incremental mechanics are the priority here. It is also a short and limited game, with a set objective. For me was about 20 hours.

Most of the game is tinkering with your setup to get the most out of it. The factory does not grow, it stays the same size most of the game and just gets more efficient over time.

Aesthetically game is great, really distinct style. The machines are very intriguing, in form and function. Sound and what little music there is, all serves it's purpose.

The 'story' didn't add anything for me, the text message delivery is ok but the conversation read really robotically and not how people talk over text at all. Doesn't matter, what they were saying doesn't add anything other than providing tutorials. I'm not sure how it would be improved though.

Overall good game. I can recommend it at the full asking price, just make sure you know what you're buying here. Played on patch 1.0.6\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very fun game if you know what you're getting into. It's a semi-idle incremental game with some factory-building mechanics on top of it. Think of it like Cookie Clicker meets Factorio, but not as complex as either. People who enjoy those two types of game will likely find joy in this one as well.

For most of the game, you will be manually doing something most of the time, and it doesn't really become idle until several hours into the game. It does become more and more automated as you go on, though.

There are some small issues with some of the late game mechanics but it looks like recent patches are addressing them. If you really get stuck, there is a fairly active community on discord and Reddit that can help you out.

Overall if you've found this game you probably already enjoy incremental games and should give this one a try. I had a great time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is not an idle clicker, so if that's what you expect going in you will be disappointed. What it is is a scaling production strategy game, with a touch of mystery and a dash of lore. Some earlier reviews mention that the scaling is brutal, but the developer has been actively tuning and balancing the gameplay, and I did not have any major issues with the cost scaling, overall. I thought it was good enough to provide challenge, and every time the game started to become a little bit tedious, I unlocked something that shifted the paradigm and made it interesting again.

The main downside of the game is the build/shop menu; scrolling up and down all the time to find things gets old quickly, and only gets worse the more buildable items you unlock. The game could use a few general UI quality-of-life improvements, like editable keybinds and better save/load features. It would also be nice to have a slightly more robust way to see behind rows of tall structures; the ability see through individual buildings is helpful, but perhaps there could be a way to toggle multiple buildings see-through at once, or something. Overall, other than the shop menu the UI is minimal but aesthetically pleasing and functional.

One fairly minor criticism is that I wish the narrative was just a little bit more fleshed out, and interacted with the gameplay a bit more. I don't need to know every detail of the universe, but I'm curious about the nature of the game world, and how it relates to the universe the two characters come from. I'm sure I could probably glean a few more details if I check the wiki or something, but I prefer the game to tell me its story on its own.

The visual presentation and art are excellent, I recommend taking a moment every once in a while to zoom in and appreciate the surprisingly detailed art and animation of the individual structures. The game does not go far with it's minimalistic narrative, but what is there is well-written and adds some stakes to otherwise emotionless game mechanics. The gameplay loop itself is addictive and satisfying, and just as it is pleasant to view at the close-up level, it also feels great to zoom out and appreciate the complex, interconnected system you have built piece-by-piece running smoothly and efficiently.\t\t\t\t\t
