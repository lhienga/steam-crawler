\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen you play simulation games there is an aspect of busy work that is associated with them and it's all part of the process.
However, This game takes busy work to an extreme and it feels like everything is designed to be an inconvenience.
-Gas delivery truck is set up on the same road as the cars leaving so those cars get trapped behind it
-cannot order more then 1 delivery at a time and store items and workshop parts cannot be ordered together
-cars will get stuck on the obnoxious sand piles left by dust storms
-Rudy is a clunky nightmare to use and runs out of a fuel super quickly
-customers buy items quicker then you can restock them at times and I feel like I'm playing shelf stocker sim not gas station sim
- profit margins are so tight sometimes that you feel like you aren't going anywhere fast
- people will throw trash on the ground despite having empty cans, and cans fill up after 4 people use them
- even the paint on the store deteriorates over time so if you thought that wouldn't need management too you would be mistaken.

That's just the gameplay. Graphically and sound design wise the game is kind of a mess too as christmas stuff was on by default despite it being October which is strange even if there is an option to turn it off why was it on by default? as for the sound. However on the dev team decided it was a good idea to both make the fuel truck block the main road and make every stopped car honk over and over and over again needs to not do sound dev design anymore. I had to turn off the horns 5 minutes in because it was just constant. Which sucks because those horns are designed to tell you traffic is blocked. Like if a car gets stuck on a sand pile because they aren't programmed to drive around the piles like a normal person.

In short I wouldn't recommend this game, and that's not even mentioning the issues I've read in other reviews around the DLC for this game.\t\t\t\t\t
