To put it simply: This game is pretty solid.

I've been bored and refunded on several simulator games. This game is staying in the library, if not simply because the time I'm planning to spend playing it would disqualify me from refunding it.

Pros:
-Interesting, if not nostalgic mini-games that "Nintendo-Arcade-Minigames" lovers will enjoy thoroughly.
-Random event(s) that will shake up the hum-drum of the (few) grindy-ish parts of the game.
-Simple to grasp, it's not intimidating to jump right in. (This game would be excellent for a new PC gamer, or for existing gamers who are trying to migrate a spouse or family member.)
-Lots, and lots of tutorials & information, of which you can return to at any point throughout the game.
-Realistic (I use this word liberally). It has a good repetition to it, with customers noticing if their favorite items are missing from your shelves.

Cons:

- Information for shelving content isn't available in-game, from what I could find. The game doesn't tell you an exact amount of items per shelf, but it matters! If you want to be able to
calculate exact amounts for what you'll need to buy for your store's inventory, you'll have to utilize the wiki.
(I decided to use my real-life notepad and write down these values, which further suggests the need for such a feature in-game. Additionally, the wiki can be annoying to locate due to the Roblox-knockoff's success.)
-The paint DLC allows you to paint the buildings using a paint roller. This feature is fantastic in every way except one: once you click a wall to start painting, you cannot change the color or exit the process. The only way to 'move forward', so to speak, is to paint the wall whatever color you had selected, go back, and paint it again to your desired color.
-This 'con' contains direct game spoilers. You have been warned.
This con is more of a complaint than a real con, but it still counts because it affects gameplay, in my opinion;
The skidsteer/excavator has a turbo function that overheats the engine, which you then have to cool? I understand the need for a nerf to the skidsteer because the designed usage wouldn't take very long to finish up, but if the overheating feature was removed and the fuel was depleted a bit faster, the same goal would be accomplished. Besides being unnecessary to gameplay, it's just downright unrealistic. Real turbo diesel machinery is far more reliable than any other, and this is very well known about.
