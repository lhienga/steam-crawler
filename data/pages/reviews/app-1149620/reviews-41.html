As noted by another reviewer DO NOT get the airstrip DLC until you have completed the basic game. If you do, your progress on basic gets mucked up beyond explanation. I theoretically have the "Can Touch This" (Hammer would be embarrassed) add on, but no indication how to activate it, while the airstrip DLC auto installs and (unfortunately) takes over the Station game. The Store page for Drago shows many, many spin off simulations but, like so many games now, the basic game seems to be more a beta at best and Steam is mostly a mechanism to raise money to develop more incomplete, glitchy games. If this game is offered at a deep discount then OK for awhile but DON'T buy the airstrip DLC at any price until you have the station of your dreams cuz it frags basic game. AND basic game is very glitchy (the highway out front ties up with accidents that are best cleared up with... the BROOM? Look at some of the screenshots) . It appears to actually be a proving ground for other projects. WAAAY TOO MANY games like this around. Spend your money wisely.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t**SPOILERS**

It's fun, if not a little dry after a bit. I would have liked to see more in terms of designing the look of your store, managing the "official" part of the station (bills, paperwork, maybe a few health inspections), and striving to turn your station into maybe a chain wouldn't go amiss. I would have also liked to decorate, paint, and design my sleeping quarters above the warehouse. Unfortunately, you can't sleep in the bed to progress time or anything like that, so you're always awake and expected to do something-- even if there's nothing to do. The moment you get staff, the game almost goes stagnant; not much to engage in beyond washing a few cars, watching for sandstorms, and refreshing peeling paint-- oh, and also throwing trash at Dennis (throwing garbage bags at a naughty child is a pleasant aside).

There are cute little nuggets sprinkled across the game. You got a rock n' roll ghost, Santa flies across the sky at the stroke of midnight, and aliens play an integral part in fixing traffic build ups (an excellent, creative "reset" for little issues that happen when you have free range on Ruth and idiotic customers that don't obey common sense traffic laws). The game tries to make up for your lack of things to do by giving you little mini-games such as RC racing and a game that's a mix between mini-golf and shuffle board (I think?), but it all falls a little flat after a while. There's only so many times you can race the same course before you get bored.

The graphics are a lot better than I expected and your movements are smooth. You can pick trunk locks right in front of your customers (who are sometimes police officers) and they just look at you. Also, if you stand on top of them, you can see the inside of their head. Which is wild. And kind of unsettling. But damn, if the developers don't design a realistic tongue.

There are a bunch of cogs in the game that make it a good experience, but it ins't really for long-term play once you pay Uncle his last loan. You would open it up if you ever feel like, I don't know, using a cash register or changing the oil in a car, but, so far, I'm not seeing how it can progress beyond that point.

Still, for the first 10 hours or so, it's fun and reasonably paced. It has updates to the different services offered at your store, and, honestly, it itches a part of my brain that relishes in tedious, day-to-day management.\t\t\t\t\t
