\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to give this a thumbs up but there are A LOT of major issues in the game that DESTROY the game and need to be mentioned before someone buy. Let's get straight to the point, I'll just say some things that ruined the game for me.

- Game is unbalanced, this is a MAJOR issue. When I bought the game I thought let's just chill and put some gas and stuff, but no. I could literally write a whole essay about this. You CANNOT relax in this game. It IS (BARELY) possible to manage the tasks and the customers but you will be always anxious running here and there.

- The airstrip DLC. What is this? a parallel whole business to manage even tho you can't manage the gas station? This is an absolute mess, nice concept but TERRIBLE execution. If you want to focus on the Gas station you are forced to JUST IGNORE the Airstrip AND VICE VERSA. Every time that old dude pays you a visit he ALWAYS crash his airplane and you have to go like an idiot and close down the station and pick him up with some junk ahh tow truck and don't even get paid. This DLC is TERRIBLE. You don't even have the time (in-game) to check it out. Or forget the gas station while you are at the airstrip.

- Trash. 1 customer is enough to make a mess in your store. Customers throw litter on the floor and if they throw it in the trash can it goes halfway full BY 1 CUSTOMER, so not only you have to run here and there to service customers but every 2 minutes you have to empty the friggin trash cans so they don't give you a thumbs down for stinkyness. SUPER annoying and unrealistic.

- Gameplay. Little things here and there make it even harder to enjoy this game. Having to manage every employee. Who's resting, who's working, who need payment, who's gonna clean, fuel, fix cars, cash register etc. and all these while customers are waiting. This whole system needs to be rewrote and make management easier. Also the total number of 5 employees are not even close to enough if you thought about going to check the airstrip or chill a bit and decorate or something. If their work time is over and they have to rest that's it. You have to handle the whole business by yourself. Also you are the ONLY one who have to buy stock, restock shelves, buy stock for the garage, restock garage shelves, get the delivery from the truck and put it in the warehouse that the driver couldn't do, order fuel, go open some valve when the truck arrives like the driver couldn't do either. clean garbage buy upgrades get the bulldozer and clean, go refill it every half a minute cuz it rans out of fuel and much more stuff. It's nice for a game to keep you busy but this is unbearable. Really annoying that ALL these responsibilities are yours and only. Employees should AT LEAST order stuff that are out of stock and restock shelves, mabye manage some other things here and there.

- Lack of upgrades. Your station reaches level 6 or 7 and that's it. Tasks are over, your grinding is over and now you just run what you made. It took me 9 hours to reach the maximum upgrades of the game. Should have more stuff and more upgrades to make your business run better and without all this running from you.

- Storage, Warehouse seems like a big building. But the storage capacity is SUPER LOW and all the time you have to buy stuff get the delivery and restock while all your employees might be resting. Gasoline. You start with like what? 300L? What gas station on earth has so little gasoline capacity? Yes you can upgrade it but it's not much. Super unrealistic and another reason to be anxious. And for god shake make the driver open that valve and do his job and also the warehouse delivery driver to do the same!

CONCLUSION:

Although the game has nice features and tons of stuff, it doesn't feel to be complete. Some games on early access are much more enjoyable and fulfilling. This feels like it's made for you to just throw your money away, with the Airstrip DLC you become SURE about this feeling. I really like the concept, but I did not enjoy the game at all because it didn't let me. It's a shame because a lot of work have been done in this and I really liked the environment. I'm dissapointed, bad work, SUPER unbalanced. I wish I could refund it and get my money back.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWelcome to our excursion into the charming world of Gas Station Simulator, a game that stands as proof that not all exciting journeys begin in far-off lands or epic fantasy realms. Developed by DRAGO Entertainment, this intriguing simulator offers players the chance to experience the ins and outs of running their very own gas station.

Gameplay:
Gas Station Simulator delivers an experience that is as quirky as it is immersive. You begin with a dilapidated gas station in the middle of a desert, and it is up to you to bring it back to life. From refueling cars and repairing engines to restocking shelves and cleaning restrooms, the game challenges you to manage multiple tasks while continually expanding and improving your station. Its in-depth management mechanics and the variety of tasks keep the gameplay fresh and engaging.

Audio and Music:
The sound design in Gas Station Simulator is surprisingly detailed. From the clinking of cash registers and the hum of car engines to the chit-chat of customers, the game effectively recreates the bustling environment of a busy gas station. The soundtrack, while not particularly memorable, serves as a pleasant background that doesn’t intrude on the gameplay.

Story:
While Gas Station Simulator lacks a formal storyline, the game does an excellent job of storytelling through gameplay. Your progression serves as your narrative as you turn a run-down gas station into a thriving business, creating a satisfying sense of achievement.

Difficulty:
The difficulty in Gas Station Simulator lies in the juggling of tasks and keeping your customers happy. As your station grows, so does the complexity of your responsibilities. The game also features a day-night cycle and random events which can add unexpected challenges.

Price:
Considering the depth of gameplay and the amount of content, Gas Station Simulator is priced reasonably. It offers good value for money, particularly for fans of simulation games.

Critique:
While Gas Station Simulator offers an engaging experience, it isn't without flaws. The game could benefit from more diverse customer interactions and tasks. Additionally, some players might find the early game a bit slow before the gas station starts to expand.

Conclusion:
In conclusion, Gas Station Simulator is a quirky and immersive simulator that successfully makes the ordinary task of running a gas station fun and engaging. It offers an in-depth management system and a variety of tasks that ensure the gameplay remains fresh. Although it has room for improvement, the overall experience is a satisfying and enjoyable one. So, roll up your sleeves, grab that fuel pump, and get ready to jump into the extraordinary world of ordinary life with Gas Station Simulator.\t\t\t\t\t
