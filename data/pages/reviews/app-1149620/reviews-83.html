\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game more than I would have expected. I never wanted to own a gas station or live in the desert but here we are. A lot of reviews talk about the many glitches but the vast majority of them are just silly, like this one customer who got stuck standing on the soda shelf. She stayed there until I closed the station. A few times a delivery truck got stuck and I had to reload from an earlier save since neither the car reset button or restarting the game helped, but it's customary to save often since no game is glitch-free. A lot of people don't like the party bus but it is one of my favourite things! Being able to shut down the repair shop, the car wash , and even the fueling stations from the PC is extremely useful when the onslaught of customers arrives. I don't want to give any spoilers but there are moments of zaniness and unexpected occurrences which add a delightful quality to the game. I play on PC and the controls all work perfectly. The devs thoughtfully added some in-game fixes for when certain things go wrong, like reset buttons for cars, people, and some of the equipment.

I have severe PTSD and doing repetitive tasks to build up the station helps alleviate my ever-present anxiety, and this game serves that purpose for me extremely well. The feeling of accomplishment when completing an upgrade and redecorating is gratifying and keeps me coming back to the game. The graphics are gorgeous too. The desert environment is stunning, and it's obvious the devs took a lot of time creating it. The whole atmosphere takes on a liminal quality during the twilight hours, and sometimes it's a bit spooky, which I love. I don't have any significant complaints, but I do wish there was more of an immersive story, like perhaps getting to know some of the clientele. Aside from that, the game is highly playable and I recommend it, and all of the DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to stress that I enjoy this game and I want to see it do well. I also want to stress that because you or I enjoy something, that doesnt make it automatically good. I love Payday 2, for example, but its a disaster.

This game is walking a fine line between utter brilliance and complete incompetence. It is filled with great ideas but there's something off about all of them. The best way I have to describe this game is; you wished for a gas station simulator, and the monkey's paw curled.

Easily the best parts of the game are in the early stages. Building up your station from a smelly shack and turning it into a profitable business is well done, engaging and paced really well. Once you hit mid to late game, you start to notice some weird ticks and the unholy amount of bugs.

The way cars arrive to the fuel pumps is scripted. If I fill all three up at the same time, they will arrive a few minutes later, all together, at the same time again. When a car arrives for repairs in your garage, the front right tire will ALWAYS be flat. The game tells you that you have to keep the bathrooms clean or customers will complain. This only amounts to sweeping the floor free of dust as you never actually have to clean the bathrooms themselves. Theres an RC track located outside the gas station. Im not sure why its here because once the station gets going, you will never have time to use it. On top of that, even with employees, the game starts to get frantic the more you add to it, because employees cant do everything and once you open all possible parking spaces, the cashier job is essentially endless and youll be ordering more product multiple times a day to keep up with every customer buying a weeks worth of snacks over and over.

These are just some of the things Ive noticed. I notice more every time I play.

I really want to stress that I am in Drago's corner. I want to see them do well. I think they have some great ideas but it is very evident that not much care went into making this game. Several years on and it is still very rough. It is full of pre purchased assets, some of which don't really go together. For example, on your newspaper stand, you can sell crisp fresh newspapers right next to a crumpled magazine that looks like it was dug out of the trash. Im also not sure what year it is as every single person drives a classic car from the 50s and 60s from both Europe and America, but Im also surrounded by relatively new tech such as computers and automatic car washes while my uncle wears a suit from the 40s, while I sell modern day styled snacks and drinks. I know Ive seen this circuit box before. Ive seen it everywhere, Ive turned it on countless times in phasmophobia.

The biggest show of lackadaisical devving is the Christmas event. At the time of writing it is currently March and despite a Valentines day event coming and going, the Christmas event is still in full swing. Santa has flown over head more times than I can count and Ive gotten so used to the customers wearing festive hats that Ive stopped seeing them. Its March, Drago. I know we are playing a country hick and they typically dont take down the christmas lights but can we please put santa out of his misery?

My biggest concern with Drago is that they dont seem to focus on one game and making it good. If you look at their store page they are working on about NINE games at once. While they appear to be using the same asset bundles between them, theres no way there can be any quality between that many projects at once. Even AAA companies only work on one or two games at once. Not NINE. This is on top of the games that they have already released, like this one, that still needs polish and boast about upcoming features. Drago, please just slow down and focus on one at a time. Releasing half baked games, like food truck sim, is not going to hold a fanbase for very long.

Edit: After writing this review, Drago announced that they are "proud" to be working on even more projects. If you look at their store page there is now a whopping 12 upcoming games. This is on top of the 3 they have already released. These games are sitting at mixed except for Gas Station Simulator and feel more like early beta to even alpha stages instead of finished games. Now they are working on 12 at once. Remember that scene from Spongebob where Neptune challenges Spongebob to a cook off and Neptune pumps out a pyramid of burgers but they are all nasty, meanwhile Spongebob cooks a single one and its so amazing it moves Neptune to turn him into a god?

Go watch Spongebob, Drago.\t\t\t\t\t
