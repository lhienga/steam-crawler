Ideally, it should depend only on your ability to generate income and reinvest in the business. Instead, the quest line forces you to acquire new upgrades and additions to your station in a specific order that doesn't make a lot of sense. I found myself in a rut where I didn't have enough money to continue running the station because the quest line was focusing all my money on buying aviation fuel for planes that bring in next to no income. Why are there planes in a game called Gas Station Simulator?

Overall, this game feels more like a proof of concept than a finished title.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBrief: I like the game. At 33.1 hours I reached a point I where I completed all the missions, bought a majority of the upgrades, and started to get bored. (And, yes, I bought all the DLC as a bundle. I have completed the DLC stuff also.)

Pro's:
* The method the story unfolds conveniently unlocks more features and stuff to do.
* The over-all theme was well done.
* There is generally no bad choice, making the game very relaxing to play.

Con's:
* CON and warning... keep many saves files. There are game bugs that will absolutely not let you continue with the same save file.
* The mini-games get annoying. I say this about all games with mini-games. The player should only have to play the same mini-game a couple dozen times, not over and over and over, ad nauseam.
* The game is too short. Once the story missions are done, the game isn't really open to end-less play.
* I personally would have preferred more of a manager game. The overwhelming number of tasks and mini-games, make the game start to become tedious and less enjoyable. Having more employees and having the employees do more tasks would have been better. (Yes, I have the Employee DLC. The Employee DLC still doesn't do enough.)

Summary:
It is a good, slow game. It is not a great game.\t\t\t\t\t
