\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThank you again "Let's Game It Out" Youtube channel. I never would've tried this otherwise. While I didn't play it like a goof, I enjoyed the 30 hour journey it took me to "complete the story." I still have a few more things I can do in the game (popularity levels). I will come back to mess around in it. I had a lot of fun, but I'll preface that I don't mind hopping in a "simulator" game to zone out and chill.

While this game can get hectic and has it's flaws, it was definitely worth picking up during a Steam sale.

Yes, the game has a good amount of jank to it. Yes, it crashes. No, it doesn't have a ton of progression depth. Yes, vehicles and customers get stuck pretty frequently. Attention: There are a few reset buttons around. Every in-game day or so when I had a lull in customers, I'd press the reset button. Not only does it fix stuck vehicles, it corrects a memory-leak issue that causes crashes. It was crashing frequently until I searched for this solution.

This game also has the quirkiness and charm of the "did we really need to simulate this" vibe like Barn Finders, but that presentation is far more polished and gets sillier. BF is "better" but GSS created more laughs in the jank dept. This concludes my TED Talk. If you'd like to read some gameplay mechanic spoilers and rant, continue reading.

Rant:

The register checkout minigame can be nightmarish to operate and I found myself avoiding it fully when I could hire more help. The item physics and incredibly small workspace made it annoying. You WILL accidentally knock something off the conveyor belt or into the basket before it can be scanned. I've never seen a customer get mad IRL when they got an item or two for free... it would have been nice if you could boost your popularity by purposefully giving items away for free to the detriment of monetary loss and no tip. Also, the NPC help is much, much quicker at completing each task with the trade-off of less money made. The math doesn't lie: it would take me about 3 seconds per item scanned. It's not uncommon to scan 10+ items. It would take me at least 30 seconds compared to the NPC's 10 seconds or less. They clear out crowds quickly and still make a good profit.

However, the hirelings are slower at cleaning and don't do car repairs well. Pathing issues, glitches, and doing the bare minimum repairs even at higher stats. I found myself putting NPCs on register, fueling, and cleaning duties (later, when I had more help).

Words of caution: DON'T move your shelves with product on them! The items can disappear and you'll lose what money you paid for the stock. This will likely come into play as you upgrade since you'll have more floor space to work with. So either let the shelves sell out, manually move EVERY item, or eat the cost. I've done all three.

Also I'm very grateful that the devs allow disabling of certain features. The car horns let you know if you have a delivery waiting, but OH MY GOD there is far too much honking. I couldn't be sure, but it seems like they honk at you if all spots are full or if there is a traffic jam too. It's quasi-useful early on as you get a feel for the layout, but of course you're not going to have everything opened up early on. Seriously folks, I'd estimate at least 1 honk per minute...

I also recommend disabling a few "events" once you've experienced them a few times. It doesn't hurt achievements. I turned off the holiday content and some detrimental features. The detrimental stuff is pretty frequent and I had a sandstorm, the graffiti brat, AND the party bus all arrive at the same time. After experiencing each event dozens of times, I realized they brought no fun anymore and only adds more monotonous play time.

I also ended up turning off "warehouse theft" about halfway through bc I realized it was a time-sink feature. This game would have benefited from better progression. AKA, don't like having to manually unload the truck (NPC can't do this), how about an upgrade that unloads for you. The items could have had more depth and stats too, ie a way to track and stock popular items.

So, there it is. I enjoyed this game enough to write a review, but am surprised I had notes on improvements. That means I liked it enough I had feedback to make it better. If you buy, I hope you have fun with it too!\t\t\t\t\t

If you like Simulation/Management games, this is a solid purchase. It's got a nice amount of character, and not too many bugs.* DLC is worth it if you've got a hankerin' for more decorative control of your Dust Bowl. -Model Animations are a little clunky.
-Party Bus is still pretty heavy on rig.
-Items on conveyor bug out sometimes.
-Trash bags fall beneath map, (but loading save despawns them iirc)
-One vehicle is a bit more tedious to wash than the rest (I think it's a 50s Chevy?)

All-in-all, glad I got it!\t\t\t\t\t
