\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot begin to describe how this game has changed my life. I am a 65 year old retired writer, and there is something about that game that goes deep below the surface level. When you first experience the game, it seems fairly shallow with a bit of gimmicks here and there, and npcs that stare at you blankly, almost like you're living in hell. But if you truly get past the basic functionally and delve into the lore of the game, you realize you aren't running just a gas station, you're running the only business for 200 miles any direction. You are the "buckeys" of this world, with no competition. The jingle of santa flying over is a nod to humanities love and affection for holidays, the ghost is a nod to jonny sins, who is an artist well know for his song "Ring of Fire". What truly tears apart your heart strings is the interaction with your uncle, who at first seems like a philanthropist helping you, but as you delve into it... you realize the truth... the turmoil you've ended up in. You are not casually running a business, you are a slave, an indentured servant, to a man you only get to see once. You will never be top dog, even when you have a fleet of peons below you, you are a pawn in the grand game of chess, you just don't realize it. When you get to the part where your character wakes up out of his oppressive position and the absolute best firefight I've ever had in a video game ensues, you feel truly like you're liberating yourself. When you get cornered, run down into the sewers for your final stand, much like osama bin laden, and grab that SVD, you feel as if the weight of the world is crashing down upon you. In those final moments, where you line up the scope on your uncles head, it's a do or die situation. The man who helped you all this way, should I really pull the trigger? A moral quandary to say the least. When you finally come to, and realize he is full sprint running at your with two golden 1911s, you know he is no longer a friend. He is an enemy. You take the shot, and the game fades to black. While not youtube worthy, if you can allow yourself to imagine a real situation rather than the cheap unity default models, you see nothing less than a masterpiece, an orchestra of betrayal and labor, a tale of a simple man going against his oppressor. A beautiful much needed story in these trying times in America.

Thank you for reading,
Benny\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played my fair share of these type of sim games. While i did not have the chance to get as deep into this one, i can tell you, its not that great. I wish i could leave a meh rating for this.

The game doesnt have amazing graphics, but im honestly not expecting the best graphics anymore for indie games (its an imposed limitation that hinders style). Regardless, it looks like a game that is brown and grey at first until you fix it up. That is a plus.

The music is idk, it didn't entice me within the first 10 minutes so i muted it and played my own music.

Now the game play is just kind boring. Imagine you could have car mechanic simulator, house flipper, and some others in one base game? No what if i told you the developers bought the random 99 cent store with sketchy products version and put it in the game. I just wanted to sell people gas and snacks. Now I'm a jiffy lube balancing car repairs, pumping gas (wtf is this new jersey? they can pump and pay themselves), and selling food, while also having to balance all the stock orders and cleaning on top of that. yes you can close for a bit to be able to do stuff like clean and restock, but the time that can take is immense, feeling as if you are closed as long as you are open.

Also the way you balance you funds in this game is not that great. Car mechanic simulator has a good style that still has some form of punishment. This game felt as if i could circumvent that quite easily. There is not much challenge from decisions, rather it is just stressful doing everything yourself.

This game is good, not bad. It just wasn't for me. If you like focused sims like house flipper, power wash sim, car mechanic sim, this game won't scratch that itch. If you like mini games with a sim front end on it, then yeah get it! the minigames can be fun, they just feel exactly like minigames.\t\t\t\t\t
