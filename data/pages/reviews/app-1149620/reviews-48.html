You slowly upgrade everything about your station and the sense of progression is really there from adding new gas pumps to adding new employees to help manage your workload. However, at a point, that feeling of progression comes to an absolute halt and starts to feel like nothing but tedium. You would think the employees would be more beneficial than being money sinks, that upgrades would feature a bit more automation to them like the car wash, that deliveries would be able to unload without assistance, and everything would give you more time to tackle things at your own pace, but that's not the case. You only slowly turn a profit and keeping employees almost feels useless and you could effectively make better profits by closing a majority of the station. It's just disappointing that once I reached the highest levels for my station, I didn't feel nearly as progressed as I did with the first few levels in my first few hours of play.
