\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7.5/10 - Never thought i'd enjoy a game where you are in charge of a gas station.
But here we are.
Graphics 7/10 - Ran this game on all ultra graphics (AMD Radeon 6600XT Mech 2x GPU) And it ran amazing and clean- Until the party bus arrives, then it goes from 51 FPS down to about 15 FPS. But other than that, I rate it a 7/10. Great style to the game.
Gameplay 7/10 - It gets repetitive after you get a handle on things. The incremental change with each upgrade makes it a bit tougher but it also makes it a little bit more boring once you get employees. However, It kept me busy for 14 hours. Between the stocking groceries and ordering gas, and then executing shifts between employees and maximizing profits through a changing price market I think it's one of the most entertaining Sims I've played recently. Plus the Garage is nice touch. It's like Car Mech. Simulator. 7/10
Story 6/10 - It's a fairly straight forward single player story. You play a guy who sold a car to buy a gas station. And Apparently, works for or with the mob. Not too many twists that throw the story off but nothing that sets you on edge. Just focus on your business. Simulators don't really NEED a big story-line to work with, But this one had more potential I feel. 6/10
Conclusion 7.5/10 - Gas Station Simulator DEFINITELY is worth a try if you enjoy Simulators that don't involve 3rd person top down view. It has a fair bit of Logistics involved. If you like to deviate from the story and run an absolute hole-in-the-wall station, or run a clean little desert jewel. You can do anything you want to your station and the future is completely in your hands. It's definitely for an audience of +10 years old and beyond, and it's a great game to get lost in for a little while. Might even be worth the 2nd playthrough.
7.5/10 Worth the $20. Thank you DRAGO for the game.\t\t\t\t\t
