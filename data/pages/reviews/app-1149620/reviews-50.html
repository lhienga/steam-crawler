\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTldr: It's a maybe from me. The high price, and semi-broken DLC (Can Touch This) deducts several points in my non-existent system.


---------------------------------------------------
Story:
I was not a fan. I don't think simulator games need a campaign as such. I understand that the game needs a tutorial, but the forced handholding was a bit much. One minute I could clean stuff, but now I can't because I have to answer the phone? It was very inconsistent.

Simulator:
The game did decently at simulating a gas station. However, as earlier mentioned, it is very inconsistent in what I can clean up and repair. A car wreck right at the back of your fully upgraded gas station is impossible to remove.
I had also purchased the "Can Touch This" DLC because I wanted extra decorations - Only to find out at least 40% was not able to be placed anywhere on the property (chair, gas bottle lamp, etc).

Endgame:
at the end, I only had to stock shelves. Being a stock-boy is not what I would consider owning a successful business, but that's just my opinion - we should have been able to hire someone to manage the warehouse/stocking.

Atmosphere:
I also did not enjoy the gimmicky scene - Cars visiting the gas station would frequently flip for no reason, blocking traffic completely. Your only way to solve this, is to hit a reset button that have aliens come flying by and swoop everyone up. Cool the first time, but getting whited by an alien ship because of bad traffic AI is not good gameplay. There is also mini golf, but only one lane, even though multiple lanes are buillt. No guest can use this for your profit though.

The same applies to the RC cars located in the junk yard - fun the first time, but I bought the game to run a gas station.

The good stuff:

I loved the fluctuating prices in everything, making you rethink to maybe wait on buying fuel a little later, because it might be cheaper.

The decorations that worked, were pretty cool.

I liked the employee system, it was nice working up to a point where I almost had to do nothing.

Some mini-games were applied correctly in my opinion (painting walls, repairing cars, scanning items, for example).

I also appreciate being able to disable most of the annoyances in settings (Graffiti, sand storm).
The graphics were great, although demanding for a mid-tier system.

-----------------------------------------------------------------

Final conclusion:

It's very addictive because you are always busy, however, get it on sale, or wait for the developers to fix bugs & expand the game. In my opinion, it is not worth full price + DLC's at the moment.\t\t\t\t\t
