\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is a lot to do, all of which is shallow and done better by other games. The polished look and sense of freedom to make your station what you will, as presented in the trailer, ends up being much less so: everything is locked behind "challenges" and grind. You end up rushing around a lot while also being completely tied to the quest progression system - even when common sense would suggest you don't HAVE to in order to do various things. Well, the game sez otherwise, so snap to it, drudge.

This sense of drudgery has no shortcut, either, despite a "loan shark" mechanic. Which I didn't appreciate being forcibly introduced to. You don't have a choice: your first loan is imposed on you, impossible to pay back in time, and ends with a fairly vicious, unavoidable beatdown. But even if you go ahead and take out the biggest loan you can after that, you're only slightly accelerating your ability to make more money than you spend on the business. It's really only useful for the occasional emergency bailout, so I ended up avoiding it entirely.

Another annoying mechanic is the kid who will periodically come around to deface the place. Welcome to a new layer of pointless drudgework, which serves only to interrupt the actually useful drudgework. You're supposed to hit them with a random object, usually a bag of garbage or something, so he runs away. Well, he doesn't. He just goes to the next target point to keep on defacing, so you have to hit him multiple times with garbage that will bounce FAR away after you hit him with it. You are NOT allowed to just use the damn broom that you use to sweep the floor and which is on you at all times, for no apparent reason. All of this means that you will end up re-painting your station quite a bit - oh, and that's also a boring, shallow minigame about keeping a marker "in the green" (supposedly representing paint roller pressure).

I think what decided me against playing this any further was that when it came time to make improvements to the grounds, there was zero choice involved. I could buy, or not buy, the single improvement for a given area, like "tombstone car displays" and palm trees. The big lovely sign out front? You don't even say yea or nay; the game AUTOMATICALLY renovates that for you, like it or not.

After six hours of gameplay, this just seems like a chore, with no indication of a breakout point where it starts being fun (or at least open-ended). If I'm going to take on what amounts to an actual job of pumping gas, scanning groceries, and slinging tires, then I at least want a SEMBLANCE of creative freedom regarding the place I bought expressly to renovate.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t**This game is a straight 5/10. It has a lot going for it but it's let down by the developers**

If you've played Weed Shop, you'll have this game down within a few minutes, however, the more you play, the more you notice features missing. Yes, you grind your way to automation and employees, but damn, you have to micro manage every little aspect! More automation please!

A warning for new players. The AI in this game is dumb and you cannot schedule shifts. Each time someone finishes a shift you'll be dipping into the PC to find someone who's not resting. You'll also have to chase the cleaner around as they are more than useless, even detrimental. If you're not already fed up of cleaning up after the cleaner, your shop will be sprayed with 'NASA rockets' by the local kid, which adds nothing to the game... and when you've really had enough, a sandstorm blows through every 60ish minutes and all the cars get stuck... In a buggy, non comical way!! How do you fix it? Using a awful skid steer that has enough fuel to last 2 minutes. You'll be spending most of your time pushing cars out of the way with your broom, to hit the pile 'Just right' to get the button to scoop said sand pile.

The game is good... It could be up their with Weed Shop, if only the devs added quality of life features, like scheduling, more employees, automatic product purchasing, a warehouse employee to unload the truck, automatic shelf stacking... and got rid of stupid things like clearing up after spray paint, sand storms, and having to dip into the sewer to feed the alligator every 30 minutes. The devs think it's a 'fun way' to slow you down. It's not fun, it's repetitive and annoying.

I understand why they added these 'annoying' features. I get they are there to slow the player down. But, it's only take me 15 hours to complete the game. That's it, 15 hours... done. Is there more content? Yes. Is it DLCs? Yes. Should it be base game content? Absolutely!

If you get this game on offer, it's worth it. If you're looking for a game that kills a weekend, this game is for you. If you want a game that you can happily manage, semi-AFK while watching TV... This is not the game for you. What you need, is Weed Shop.

Devs, stop trying to 'slow us down' by adding annoying features. Give us automation and remove the astonishingly 'over
tuned' brutal and repetitive tasks. The whole point in a business simulator is eventually being the boss and watching your employees work while you adjust the plant pot in your office!\t\t\t\t\t
