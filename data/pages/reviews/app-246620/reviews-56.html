A book which remaps US Marines doctrine from battle to communications...

and the world-famous Clausewitz quote: "NO Plan survives 1st-contact with the enemy."

No communications does, either.

Plague, Inc. is actually a damn-good simulation for real-world communications campaigns:

Sometimes enemy-action is direct, sometimes it is indirect, sometimes it is slower, sometimes it is more-aggressive, etc...

So, if you think of the "infection" as being meanings, and you think-of the opponent-force as being counter-meaning, you get tastes of different scenarios in emergency-communications...

Like the information-campaigns & disinformation-campaigns that happened with Covid, e.g. or the gaslighting of Russia with its slaughterous violation of Ukraine, or the gaslighting by various corporations, while they "obey" the law that they lobbied/rigged...

The whole world is somewhat rigged:
there are oceans of evidence that those with privilege are unconsciously-certain that the law doesn't, and shouldn't, apply to them,
whether "royalty" or corporate, or political, or otherwise...

It seems to be an intrinsic self-dishonesty in humankind, so deeply-rooted that it requires active countering, in order to even limit it...

Comically, people miss sooo much of the evidence:

Conspiracists go on about this or that conspiracy, but .. won't notice that prejudice, itself, is a kind of "class conspiracy"??

Like the class of male-culture conspiring to stomp/sabotage/gut/undermine female-validity/worth/potential/opportunity, while gaslighting about "protecting" women's worth?

Is prejudice not a kind of unconscious conspiracy?

Now, consider how one would counter prejudice, with communications...

Consider also, however, that when one attacks dishonestly-leveraged-privilege, whether gender, or race, or class, that those who benefit from the entrenched-abuse, are going to hit with disinformation, as quickly and as hard as they can, to protect their privilege/unearned-wealth...

Consider that there will be dishonest people within one's own side, whose dishonesty will sabotage one's message/credibility/etc,

consider that there will be enemy-agents within one's own side, who will deliberately make-evil what one is doing, specifically so that one loses...

And so-on...

You see, communication, on issues where prejudice is the enemy ( or the "god" to be protected, from the other-side's perspective, from violation ), it is war.

Not only is it war, but different kinds of communication work different ways...

I've had-it with evil's dominion among our world, so I hold that visual-spacial Issue-Diagrams are the proper way to debate issues:

Let anybody suggest an item to add to the Issue-Diagram,
hold to correctness,
break disinformation,
and make it so that all the valid points are seen by all, immediately, readily, easily, with no bamboozling through words-manipulation...

IOW, I want disinformation having zero traction, in ruling our world: I want it gutted & killed.

But, of course, it will never tolerate such change, will it?

You see how this game simulates, in a simplistic way, wars-of-meanings?

I've learned, through this simulation, that in project-management, one sometimes need *completely* hold-back particular kinds of resources, until some tipping-point has happened...

I've learned that it is required for one to try at-least simulations of a particular kind of opponent, before one commits in a real fight/context/battle,
so that one understands how to deal with that kind of opponent...

Many "games" are actually project-management simulations, in disguise...

This particular one maps well to communications, is all...

I do find the endings, when one has "won" by eliminating humanity, to be depressing,
but my reason for doing this thing,
is to learn more competence in understanding the difference between kinds-of-interactions of published-meanings...

You can work-out, for yourself, how "viral memes" and "official communications" and "evidence/fact" map to different aspects of one's "plagues", with actually convincing people being mapped to "lethality", for instance...

As Feynman told people: sometimes you find the SAME law, underlying *very different* processes...

This is true.

Sometimes the rules underlying a sim are sooo similar to the rules underlying a sim of a completely-different-domain, that one can use it to simulate that other domain, with just a little bit of imagining...

And that means that one now has access to simulation of many things for which nobody has made dedicated-simulations for, see?
