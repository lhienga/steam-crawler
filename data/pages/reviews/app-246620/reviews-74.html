\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStrategic pandemic simulator, passable for board game considering its structure. With the Cure DLC around now we can either control a pathogen to take over the world or stand as humanity to stop it introducing an interesting duality and expanding the variety. It surely has been lovely to see this title evolve over the years even if I never truly could stick with it for too long at any time.
Basic gameplay formula is to on a world-map board select a country to infect, pop bubbles that appear for upgrade points, buy upgrades to facilitate spread, detect-ability and lethality then observe how events unfold and buying further upgrades accordingly while enjoying various message pop-ups commenting on what's happening in the world in the meantime; both humorous and serious with great variety thus it's not just a blunt play.
More advanced pathogens that can be selected additionally introduce different mechanics and win conditions transforming the game into something far more strategic and, for instance, requiring moving the infected from country to country manually, account for things like established military bases...it is so because there is a playable zombie plague, Ape virus from its respective movie and more.
Last thing worth mentioning is that the game feature online co-op with expanded gameplay options, PvP and custom scenarios connected to steam workshop so if you, say, want a virus that will play out a tale of transforming earth life into dragons...such a mod exists :3

I think that's all worth noting. If it looks interesting then it should be worth a try. I can't recommend it for Completion though, a lot of RNG required to do so + sheer quantity. The difficulty also varies a lot making it very easy to get into but very difficult to fully understand and master. Perhaps this is what was holding me back to stay with it for longer.\t\t\t\t\t
