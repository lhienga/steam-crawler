\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have very mixed feelings about this game.

OK, let me sum it up ASAP for those who don't care for my reasoning: Plague Inc Evolved is NOT a bad game per-se. It has very high-highs and very low-lows, but in my opinion the game has some flaws that prevent me from suggesting it to people for anything more than a casual playthrough. You can read further if you care why.


To be blunt: I believe that Ndemic created Plague Inc by accident. Plague Inc has a somewhat flawed design. If you boil it down, it's not a innovative masterclass in strategy gameplay; It's a resource-management centered strategy game where the game decides whether it's in the mood to give you resources or not. There's an argument to be made that it's "earned" - And to an extent it is - But where the flaws of limited resources come into play is the lows I mentioned. There will be times when you have done everything right, using what you consider to be masterclass strategy that works with the strengths of your plague and avoids the weaknesses well, only to be stopped just short of securing victory because you ran out of points. That's not fun, that's frustrating, and in my opinion, it's also just bad design. Speaking of bad design: Plague Inc. The Cure. *Shivers*

Ndemic has only created one other strategy game to note - Rebel Inc, and though I had yet to play it what I've seen seems to suggest that game is just as flawed as this one is, only being more monotonous. So why does Plague Inc get a pass? Honestly, I'm not sure. I think it's just because the base concept is so interesting; A game where you semi-directly control a plague.


Mostly unrelated, but there's also one point I wanna skim over quickly: Plague Inc. can sometimes feel like straight up propaganda. For starters: Any ideas that the Ndemic doesn't agree with get discredited and slandered in their portrayal here. To their credit, most of these ideas are wildly considered ridiculous, such as Flat Earthers or Anti-vaxxers, but then there's the game's background obsession with "Fake News" - which shouldn't even be in a game themed around plagues - where it *really* can feel like Ndemic is just indirectly attacking anyone they don't agree with. Not to mention the "Popular" tab in the custom scenario browser that's very obviously curated by Ndemic. Which wouldn't be a problem if they didn't also bump Trumpitus (a highly political scenario) to nearly the top of the popular tab even though it's almost no-where to be found if you browse by popular of all time on Steam. Not to mention Plague Inc. The Cure. *Shivers*

There's nothing wrong with making a game that takes a political stance, but it's Ndemic's lack of respect or neutral or even *balanced* portrayal for what they disagree with that can cause even more neutral people like me to just feel like they're playing interactive propaganda. A good political message should be baked into the themes of the game (Which as I said; The theme is clearly intended to be plagues and science), and should be countered every step of the way. Unironically see George Orwell's book 1984. Regardless: I don't believe Plague Inc even tries.


I don't know, I'm probably just ranting at this point. But my message is this: Plague Inc Evolved is a flawed game made by indie developers who got lucky. If you're really itching for a strategy game, flawed or not, then be my guest and buy it, but when Steam asks me "Would you recommend this game to other players?" I can't honestly pick "Yes"\t\t\t\t\t
