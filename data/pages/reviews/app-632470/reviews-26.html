\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't in good conscience recommend this game. I would rather know that the money goes towards the original creators than a studio stealing everything from them due to a chaotic fraud case that everyone agrees was the worst outcome.

The game itself is something I can only describe as the perfect RPG. Beautiful concept art with a painterly style that puts you in a whimsical realist fantasy of a cop in a blank slate. The freedom to build his character as corrupt as possible, with hours of dialogue to listen to while you walk around the decaying town. The music sets the dramatic tension in different scenarios. It's a tragic RPG that I enjoyed every minute of trying to figure out a murder mystery while also finding out more about the lore of the district as well. It's even an inspiration for me to design a game of my own with similar beats someday.

However, I just can't recommend it under a studio with malicious behavior. As an artist, one of my biggest fears is losing the rights to creations. I would much rather follow Robert Kurvitz if he ever does decide on making his own studio than continue supporting ZA/UM. I play Disco Elysium since I already own a copy, but again, I would not recommend spending the money on it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisco Elysium isn't just a game; it's modern art, a masterpiece that transcends the boundaries of traditional storytelling and gaming. From the moment you step into the worn-out shoes of the nameless detective suffering from amnesia, you're thrust into a world that's as bleak as it is mesmerizing.

First and foremost, the writing in Disco Elysium is unparalleled. Every line of dialogue, every description, every internal monologue is crafted with such care and precision that it feels like you're reading a gritty noir novel set in a surreal, dystopian world. The game's narrative is rich, complex, and hauntingly beautiful, tackling themes of identity, morality, politics, and existentialism with grace and depth.

But what truly sets Disco Elysium apart is its gameplay mechanics. The role-playing elements are deep and immersive, allowing you to mold the detective's personality and moral compass through your choices and actions. Whether you want to be a hard-nosed cop, a charming rogue, or a broken shell of a man, the game accommodates your decisions, shaping the story and world around you in profound ways.

Moreover, the game's open-ended nature and non-linear storytelling give you unparalleled freedom to explore and interact with the world of Revachol. Every corner you turn, every person you meet, every clue you uncover adds layers to the overarching mystery, keeping you hooked and invested until the very end.

Visually, Disco Elysium is a feast for the eyes. The hand-painted art style, combined with the atmospheric music and sound design, creates a mesmerizing ambiance that sucks you into its world and refuses to let go.

In conclusion, Disco Elysium isn't just one of the best RPGs of recent years; it's a landmark achievement in interactive storytelling. With its gripping narrative, deep gameplay mechanics, and stunning presentation, it's a game that will stay with you long after you've put down the controller. Simply put, Disco Elysium is a masterpiece that every gamer owes it to themselves to experience. Though dramatic, Disco Elysium has changed my life.

It's such a shame with everything that is happening with Za/um. Though unlike most I will not be petty and downvote the game. Your're only spitting on the devs work, and not the companies. Be better
