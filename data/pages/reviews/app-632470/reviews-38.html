\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is built different. The mechanics are fresh, and it does inner dialogue like I have not seen in any other game.
It also does world building very well; graphics, music, story, dialogue, voicing, etc. all fit very well together to create a unique atmosphere.

Even though the game includes a lot of randomness - through skill-based dice rolls - I do not feel the need for save scumming, because in this game, there appear to by many, many paths to achieve progress, where one is not really "better" than another. Just, different, depending on how you build your character. One aspect that plays a role in this, is that experience points feel useful, but not a goal in and of itself. They are used to build the character in depth, rather than simply just improve HP, DPS, etc. It feels more like expanding a tool belt, in stead of simply making the individual tools bigger and better.

I would highly recommend this game to anyone that has the time to be absorbed in a new world and experience in-depth character development, a thoroughly built world with a lot of flavor.
