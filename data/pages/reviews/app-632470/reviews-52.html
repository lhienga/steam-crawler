\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisco Elysium is not just a video game; it's a transcendent experience that redefines the boundaries of interactive storytelling. Developed by ZA/UM, this isometric role-playing game stands out as a shining beacon in the gaming industry, delivering a unique blend of gripping narrative, deep character development, and a meticulously crafted world.

At its core, Disco Elysium is a detective story set in the fictional city of Revachol. The protagonist, a troubled and amnesiac detective, navigates a complex narrative filled with political intrigue, moral dilemmas, and existential ponderings. The brilliance of the game lies in its unparalleled writing, which effortlessly weaves together dark humor, philosophical musings, and hard-boiled detective tropes into a cohesive and immersive narrative tapestry.

The standout feature of Disco Elysium is the innovative dialogue system. Every conversation is a labyrinth of choices, allowing players to shape the detective's personality and worldview. The protagonist's internal thoughts, represented by various aspects of his psyche, add a layer of complexity to decision-making. This results in a dynamic and organic progression of the story, ensuring that each playthrough offers a genuinely unique experience.

Visually, Disco Elysium captivates with its hand-painted art style that beautifully complements the gritty and noir-inspired atmosphere. The isometric perspective showcases the diverse districts of Revachol, each with its own distinct personality and visual identity. The attention to detail is commendable, making exploration a delight as players uncover the city's secrets.

The sound design and music deserve special mention for their atmospheric contributions. The haunting score, composed by British Sea Power, perfectly encapsulates the melancholy and mystery of Revachol. The voice acting is exceptional, breathing life into a diverse cast of characters, each with their own quirks and idiosyncrasies.

While Disco Elysium may not cater to those seeking traditional action-oriented gameplay, its strength lies in its narrative depth and player agency. It challenges conventional gaming norms and elevates the medium to new heights as a storytelling platform. The game demands attention and thoughtful engagement, rewarding players with a thought-provoking and emotionally resonant experience.

In conclusion, Disco Elysium is a masterpiece that sets a new standard for narrative-driven games. Its gripping story, rich characters, and innovative design make it a must-play for anyone who appreciates the art of storytelling in gaming. ZA/UM has crafted a truly groundbreaking experience that will be remembered as a landmark achievement in the history of video games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you have the time and money to give it a try just to see for yourself, it is worth it just to make up your own mind. This game is very different than most games that come out, and it deserves credit for that.

That said, I do not think it is a good game.

The Good:
  • Setting: The only other game that comes to mind being similar in setting/tone is Papers, Please (which is a better game, imo).
  • Art Direction: While not necessarily "beautiful", it is a distinct style that fits the game and stands out.
  • Voice Acting: The voice acting in the Final Cut is mostly quite good.
  • Inner Monologue: The different aspects of your character's psyche arguing in your head is by far the most entertaining aspect of the game.
  • Kim: Your partner is the best character.
The Bad:
  • UI/UX: Almost impressively bad. I can't think of a single positive aspect to it. Clunky, inconsistent, and confusing. Both m&kb and controller are terrible. Just climb the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ stairs!
  • RNG Skill Checks: Having high skill in something and then having to retry a skill check 4+ times (but not all at once, come back later after you level in between each try) because you have to roll dice is not compelling gameplay. And despite what others say, few of those checks are "funny" or "rewarding" to fail. Most simply halt your progress and force you to retry later. Swapping clothes/items around using the terrible UI to try and improve your odds is also not compelling gameplay.
  • Role Playing: Here is where my opinion probably gets controversial. The rest of the gameplay could be overlooked if it offered a robust roleplaying experience, but... the role playing in this game is not very good. Sure, you get to make some dialogue choices, maybe pick between different approaches to a situation, but not in nearly as many as I wanted, and rarely with the nuance I was hoping for. It's not a simple binary option like lazier RPGs, but it's not nearly as many as the glowing praise made me anticipate. I don't care about skill trees or anything like that, "role playing" can be entirely about choices and behavior, but there just isn't enough of it here. I felt more like a tourist who could choose who to offend with my dialogue choices. I had a character type in mind when I started playing, and the game repeatedly told me "no, that's not one of the choices".
  • Writing: This is where I really disagree with most people. This game has better writing than a lot of video games, but that is a bar so low you would struggle to trip on it. The good writing is absolutely buried beneath hours of exposition dumps and NPCs droning on and on about hyper-specific fictional history and politics. Speaking of politics, I don't mind that the game is highly political. I like political games. But I do mind that it feels lazy, shallow, and particularly unsubtle about it. It makes no sense for some random working stiff to suddenly give you, a stranger, a college lecture on their personal politics, then demand that you also espouse your own. And when you do, you must choose from a small list of hardline positions, or be mocked by the game for "not picking a side" (another example of sub-par roleplaying options).
Ultimately, I just did not find this game fun or compelling. I would rather spend my time learning about and discussing actual history and politics than playing this.\t\t\t\t\t
