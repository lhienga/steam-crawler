\r\n\t\t\t\t\tArguably the Greatest Game ever made. No game has touched and given me as many deep thoughts as this profound Masterpiece.

Very few games have elevated video games as an art form as Disco Elysium has. Such supreme mixture of visual art, literature, music, and CRPG mechanics. I believe in the future, this game will be taught in Universities at a relatively consistent basis.

Even though much of the game is reading/listening and shares similarities to a Visual Novel, Disco Elysium does the CRPG genre as well as any CRPG since it accomplishes the three most important elements of CRPG very well:

1. Unique character builds resulting in significantly different playthroughs - By focusing on different attributes and skills you can have different dominant voices in your dialogues along with the less featured skills making appearances here and there based on skill point allocation and RNG. This can result in VERY different playthroughs. Also passing skill checks, especially the ones that can't be retried, adds even more to the replay value.

2. Reactivity - Characters, events, and dialogue can interact with you differently depending on things you are wearing, order you do quests, events that pass, and actions pertaining to stuff seemingly unrelated. There were many examples of the city of Revachol feeling alive and malleable.

3. Choices and Consequences - Lots of choices and lots of consequences. For the first playthrough at the minimum, please don't save scum, it will be a better experience. Trust me.

Thank you devs for this Monument to Hope, History, and Humanity.

We are the miracle. ONE DAY WE WILL RETURN TO YOUR SIDE.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally deep storytelling while being very easy to interface with. This game really excels at mixing character writing with world building. It has given me a really good benchmark for what I want to achieve as a writer and what it'll take to get there: using storytelling to express real world views, ideas, concepts, etc. to leave behind the baggage that prevents people from really thinking about these ideas critically by detaching them from the real world. This game manages to comment on and critic so many different ideas, including an honest look at communism, which is really really refreshing for me as a westerner. It manages to be really fair without providing one idea as the ultimate ideal. The narrative never really lays its cards out, so to speak, there's no perfect way to handle the issues we face as a person in this world.

So naturally, sail the hell out of those seven seas, for reasons many other reviews have stated at length.\t\t\t\t\t
