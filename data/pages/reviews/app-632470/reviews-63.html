\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDISCO BABY!

Beat the game for the first time hours before writing this review.

Played this on the steam deck. Will say - this game plays alright on the deck, but it was definitely meant to be played on mouse and keyboard. It's more of a point and click type exploration that's a little awkward using a controller setup. The steam deck makes up for this with the touchpad, making up for any difficulties you would face in moving or interacting with the world, so I would've had a much worse time if I played this using a regular controller.

OF NOTE FOR STEAMDECK USERS - Gallery mode shows the wrong icons on the steam deck. So don't bother with it. That's not really a big deal for the gameplay itself, but I thought I'd mention it if this is something you're into.

The story and gameplay itself is great. You're a detective, but the case itself feels almost secondary to the mystery of the fictional world you're in and the things you're presented with. Your character has amnesia and has to figure out who they are, and you have plenty of opportunities to ask that question, while also asking "what is "this" (extends arms and gestures at the space around them)."

There's also a lot of political and social commentary in this game that was very interesting and can be approached in different ways.

The game also doesn't shy away from certain types of mysticism that's very freaky. Without revealing too much, you can speak to your own spine later in the game - which is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ wild.

The game is both fun and interesting, with really funny dialogue on top of that. I do recommend playing it on mouse and keyboard as your first choice though.\t\t\t\t\t
