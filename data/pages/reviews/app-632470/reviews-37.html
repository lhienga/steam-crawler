Genuinely one of the best games I've ever played. I wish I never played it. My standards are now too high.

If you don't like reading or dialogue heavy games, this isn't the game for you. It is A LOT of reading, A LOT of lore, and A LOT of dialogue. This is not a very fast-paced game, and I recommend you take your time and absorb yourself in the world. Not being a reader is the only reason I'd say not to get the game. I'd recommend this to literally anyone else.

Amazing voice acting, music, humor, nihilism, satire, dialogue, and world building. From someone who generally plays fast action, combat heavy boom shooters and roguelike games, this game has had me desperately searching for anything like it. The replayability is insane due to the internal dialogue and skill checks offering different results. Take the risks if you can. I've failed a 97%, and I've succeeded a 3%. I've played for as many possibilities and dialogue prompts as possible, and there is something I've never seen before every time.

Every single character is interesting - to me anyway. Even the people you aren't supposed to like, even the one-off side characters that are optional to interact with: you want to know more. And Kim is probably the best character in any game I've ever played. His demeanor and the way Raphaël Ambrosius Costeau interacts with him are so captivating and demand you feel the same way as the MC. He is respectable, complex, and you want to impress him.

If an assault were launched on a building Kim was in right now — if the windows came crashing down and the whole world descended upon him— I would hurl myself in death's way to save him. I am sure of this.
