Disco Elysium - The Final Cut

My very first time hearing of Disco Elysium, like most people, was during The Game Award 2019, where it managed to win four awards -- Best Narrative, Best RPG, Fresh Indie Game, and Best Independent Game -- shocking the world in the process. I knew at that moment, I had to check it out. Eventually, five years and more than a handful of world-altering events later, I got around to it. Now hailed by many as quite possibly the best-written video game ever made, it's suffice to say that the game's reputation has only gotten more legendary since -- especially with the release of The Final Cut. With a word count surpassing even that of The Lord of the Rings trilogy, the prospect of playing is an enormous undertaking that had me more than a little intimidated. But, as someone relatively new to the genre, I loved every second of it. A hardcore point-and-click narratively driven role-playing game through and through, Disco Elysium won me over with its sheer creativity, imagination, and raw storytelling prowess. Never before have I played a game such as this.

The Positives ▼

1. Exceptionally Written
Over the course of decades and hundreds of games, the ongoing list of what I'd consider to be the best-written games is constantly growing and changing. Titles such as Bioshock, Yakuza 0, Nier Automata, Persona 5, and Soma, to name a few, are kings of that list and have been for several years. Now, allow me to dethrone them all in one fell swoop of the pen. Ladies & gentlemen, there is now a new reigning monarch in the kingdom, and his majesty's name is Harry motherf#cking Du Bois. Clever, creative, highly imaginative, nuanced, mature, and funny as all hell, Disco Elysium set a new benchmark for writers across the industry for how to write compelling characters, conflicts, and dialogues. Frankly, it's an achievement for writing as a medium in general. Without a hint of hyperbole, Disco Elysium is the greatest written work I've seen in a video game, and I can't praise it enough.

2. Incredible Vocal Performances
The Final Cut brought a heap of additional content and improvements, one of which is fully voice acting. I cannot even begin to stress how phenomenal and life-changing this is for the game. Besides making it more digestible for readers of all skill levels, the vocal performances themselves are world-class. As a matter of fact, I would go as far as to say it's the best vocal performance I have ever seen in an indie title, surpassing even that of Hades. I tried to look up the actors - expecting to see the usual credits and faces, but to my surprise, it was nobody I knew. Just a bunch of relatively unknown actors and musicians with strong theatre backgrounds that I am only now hearing of for the first time. Genuinely, it's some of the best voice acting in any video game.

3. Strong Emphasis On Player's Choice
Much like its other core endeavors, Disco Elysium is unrivaled when it comes to giving players agency, freedom, and choices. Want to take the beaten road and become a good cop? It's yours. How about becoming the most corrupt bastard in town? Done. Wanna become a raging communist? Go for it. There are myriads of different ways to tackle the murder case at the heart of the story. There's no such thing as the "right" path, and players are encouraged to role-play and experiment to their heart's content. And this isn't just some veneer thrown in for good measure, either. What you do and say will have real consequences for the characters and plots of Disco Elysium, and the world will change in accordance with your choices. As a result, Martainise feels like a living breathing district, and it's refreshing to see how reactive the writing is when pushed to its absolute limit.

4. Failure Is Not The End
What I love the most about Disco Elysium's gameplay is that it is equally as rewarding and fun failing a skill check as much as it passing one. When I see a game implement these kinds of mechanics, it's often to the detriment of the players. Luck-based mechanics almost always lead to frustration and save scumming, but Disco Elysium found a way. By making it so that white checks are retriable so long as one invests a skill point into it and by making failing a check equally as hilarious, satisfying, and interesting as succeeding one, the game sorely feels cheap and unfair when you do inevitably fail. I adore this about Disco Elysium and found it to be one of the game's most innovative aspects.

5. Great Soundtrack
When it's commonplace to spend upward of thirty minutes to an hour engaging in an average conversation, it's not just important to have a great soundtrack -- it's an existential imperative. Disco Elysium not only survived -- but thrived. As players, we can explore and see the poverty-stricken district of Martainise firsthand and read all about the political limbo of Revachol. But, to feel and understand it through the power of music, now that's powerful. The heart and soul of Disco Elysium wouldn't be the same without the beautiful music composed by Sea Power that made it so nostalgic and memorable.

6. Excellent Art Style
While not the most technically and graphically impressive game out there, what Disco Elysium does have is a unique and beautiful-looking oil painting art style that sets it apart from its contemporaries. Drawn by Aleksander Rostov, it's hard to overstate how crucial his unique art direction is for visually communicating the tone and feel of the game. Everything from the skill and character portraits to the environments and the surreal thought backgrounds -- the game has a certain indescribable feel to it that makes it so distinctive and beautiful to look at.

The Negatives ▼

1. Technically Unpolished
No game is without fault, and in the case of Disco Elysium, that has to be for its lack of polish. For all of its incredible achievements, the game has a habit of feeling janky and unpolished more often than I would've liked. Technical bugs, voice lines not matching with what's written on the screen, poor animation, mechanics not working consistently like fast traveling, and more. And if you're truly unlucky, some bugs can even be game-breaking. As much as I love the game, this list of technical issues is impossible to ignore and frankly -- unacceptable.

2. No Sorting System?
One of the core gameplay loops of Disco Elysium is collecting articles of clothing that, when equipped, can raise particular stats rendering skill checks easier to pass. By the end of the game, it's not uncommon for the average player to amass anywhere from a few to a couple of dozen, and going through all of them every time you want to increase a stat is a pain. Why is there not a simple sorting system that allows players to choose a stat they're looking for and filter out the rest? Having this kind of system would've greatly reduced time players spend in the inventory equipping gears. I can't believe such a simple and obvious solution somehow got overlooked.

Final Thoughts
The success of Disco Elysium lies in its focus. The writers and designers had a strong vision for the kind of game that they were trying to make and the audience that would play it. No compromise, no surrendering. Five years later, the result continues to speak for itself. Celebrated by fans worldwide for its narrative brilliance and as a generation and genre-defining game, Disco Elysium continues to be a game that fans and developers point to as a glowing beacon in the industry. A standard for all to aspire to and, most importantly, dare to surpass. It's been one hell of a ride, but all good things must come to an end.
