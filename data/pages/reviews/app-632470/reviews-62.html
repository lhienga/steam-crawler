\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisco Elysium is an open-world dialogue based RPG where you are tasked with solving a murder case, the mystery of your own identity and any other conundrums you encounter on your journey.

90% percent of your playthrough will consist of reading (or listening) to dialogue with some exploration and puzzle solving in between. The writing is some of the best I’ve seen in a video game, it manages to be both hilarious and emotional when appropriate, is full of fun twists and surprises and overall very creative. Disco Elysium will mock you, and it will mock itself. As someone who usually prefers more intense and dynamic gameplay it’s incredible that this book-game managed to keep me fully engaged throughout its entire 50+ hour runtime through text alone.

It’s somewhat difficult to describe the game for a review since your playthrough could look entirely different to mine, and that’s what great about it. Your choices do matter and you feel that they matter. If you behave like a deranged drunken communist, the game will acknowledge it, if you act like a corrupt hustler who will do anything for profit the game will acknowledge it, if you flip flop between the two the game will recognise that too. You’re not just picking between a few generic paths like other games of this category will have you do, Disco Elysium gives you the liberty to fully express yourself as you wish.

I have very few minor complaints. Some of the ambience and exposition descriptions are a bit excessive, I came across a bug or two, some insignificant inconsistencies and the death/failure mechanic was a bit annoying if you haven’t saved in a while. But again, these issues are mostly negligible. Also, I have to mention the beautiful, timeless artstyle and soundtrack.

Overall, Disco Elysium is a phenomenal narrative experience that I recommend that you go into as blind as possible. 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuffice it to say, what can be said that already has not by other players turned reviewers?

TLDR: it's rad as hell; language is fun and cool, especially when developers/writers use linguistics to have free reign of their creation and world; voice acting adds top marks to game play; and you're really bombarded with rather adjacent, yet fictional, history and political ideals colliding in ways that feel almost too familiar, but in a good(?) way...or, at least, a let-me-reexamine-my-own-thoughts-on-this, potentially.

Not only is the game addicting, due to its story-rich and unique artistic quality, but the writing and voice acting is, bar none, some of the best I have ever encountered in a game, if not the best at times. As a polyglot and linguist, the imagination and creativity displayed in the conceptualization of the Disco-Elysium-world alone is leagues beyond what so many games even try to do and just barely scratch the surface doing. To combine decades, if not centuries, of history, language, culture, and all other aspects of our world (more Euro-centric than anything, I can admit, so from a very colonizer viewpoint... but how fitting, in the Disco world), and then to distill it down, not into some flat mockery of what struggles people have historically encountered and continuously do encounter today, but distilled into a bright and effervescent take that is equally dark and congealed, showing us new ways to look at the world we're living in. How does Capitalism really make you feel? How about Communism? Socialism? What about Authority, with a capital 'A'? Greedy conglomerates? Nations that commit atrocities in plain sight and won't admit to their true actions? Recognizing even in a game...ACAB.?

Will you admit to being brought into direct contact with your own political thoughts and ideals while playing this? Or will you try to shy away from that truth, and pretend this is simply a game, nothing more than a fun, dark, and welcome distraction from your day-to-day? Because if you're willing to bring a little Harrier into it, and into yourself, maybe you can use this ART, this game, to do what art is meant to do, besides just be entertainment and distraction, and that is, let the art shed light on how history continues to repeat itself because humanity, at the end of the day, is both greedy and stupid, while also being resilient and generous and kind.

I will admit, I stopped playing for several months after beginning the game, partially because I became impatient with having to wait to pull the lever for the water lock and not knowing what to do or where to go to gain more Experience to level up different qualities or attributes. (Perhaps, though, I can be kind to myself and know that the other partial reason was because I started back in uni and there was know way I would have started the term off as strongly if I was hyper-focused on Disco. haha) Please note, me stopping play has nothing to do with the quality of the game in any way, and has much more to do with feeling stuck and frustrated in a mere moment that catapulted me to where I am now: writing a review after resurfacing from a game-play time of ... 9+ hours? If that doesn't instill some sense of a ringing endorsement from a fellow Steamer, I don't know what would.

13/10, would hype this up at a party just to see who else has played and who we could rope into playing\t\t\t\t\t
