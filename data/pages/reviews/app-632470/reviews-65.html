\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisco Elysium is a fantastic achievement in video game writing, and is one of the best games I've ever played. That being said, not everyone will enjoy it. Hopefully this will help figure out if it appeals to you:

The story is the main focus of the game, so the mechanics provide just enough structure to add tension and reward problem solving with your character's skills. It's comparable to tabletop RPGs, but of a different genre than Dungeons & Dragons. While you do roll dice for checks to see if you succeed, it's at a much more minimal level than something like D&D or Pathfinder. Narrative RPGs like Fate, Ten Candles, City of Mist, Dungeon World, Blades in the Dark, etc. are closer to this game.

If what I described above doesn't mean anything to you, think of it like a choose your own adventure book where your character's skills (what they're good at) make it more or less likely to get good outcomes from the scenes that you play though. You could get more information to interrogate a suspect with, notice a clue to a puzzle, or just enjoy some funny banter between characters.

So it's like an interactive book/audiobook. There's lots of reading/listening, and the accompanying painterly art style, atmospheric music, and emotive voice acting are all lovely.

Is this game for you? It depends on how much you enjoy lengthy choice-based narratives that contain a lot of social commentary. I found it insightful and humorous, especially when the game pigeonholes you into a political ideology, and then makes fun of you for it.

There's not much I want to say about the story itself, as it contains so many fun moments and twists that I don't want to even allude to. It's so much fun to experience the story and become immersed. This is one of those games that I wish I could erase from my memory to experience it all over again.

Also, don't buy it here. Obtain it through "other means". The money is going to the wrong people.\t\t\t\t\t

- Interesting characters
- Rich story

Don't like:
- Backtracking
- Fast travel is very uncomfortable
- Stuffy flow of gameplay
- Probabilities (save scam after 20 hours)
- Gamepad controls (character movement)
- Poor animations
- Weak ending

Settings also has troubles, you can't limit your FPS. Natively it runs on FPS of your monitor, so I started to play in 144 fps, this is too much for reading game.
Finally I've found a solution by manual limiting via Nvidia settings in Windows.
On my Steam Deck it was easier, I just set screen refresh rate to 30 fps.

Generally I will recommend this game as a book, not as the game. Be prepared for hours of reading while you sit still.\t\t\t\t\t
