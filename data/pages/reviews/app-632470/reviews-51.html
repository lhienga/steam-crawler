\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDISCLAIMER: I think it's worth $10, not $40. I bought it on sale. Also, apparently the devs got ousted from their own game, so maybe it's not even worth $10 until they can get their IP back.

Pros:
- Good music
- Good setting
- VERY good characters
- VERY good voice acting
- Murder mystery is well-paced for a solid 80% of the game

Cons:
- Slows down at the end
- A little too dependent on pass-fail mechanics to make the story/narrative compelling. I found myself quicksaving/quickloading because I thought "given my stats, <main character> would not say that"
- Political messaging is disappointingly cynical and broad. I was honestly hoping to have my political views challenged by this game, but the critique wasn't good enough to make me think about anything new. I'm sure the message works better if you're from the Eastern European region, where this game was made.

Overall, pretty good. 6.5/10. Well worth $10.\t\t\t\t\t

The best reading simulator known to men

I tried playing this twice and failed within the first couple of minutes, being completely overwhelmed by the inner voices - and dying pretty much on the spot in my first round.
And then I thought this game maybe just wasn't for me.
Boy, was I wrong.

Picked this back up because I wanted something with a Pen & Paper feel, and found out that this delivers very hard on that front.

This game shouldn't work as well as it does.
I call myself an avid reader, but even I felt overwhelmed by the sheer amount of text the game throws at you. The gameplay feels very minimalistic at first. the art style is unusual, and there is next to no tutorial.
It shouldn't have the pull it has.
But it does.

The narrative, dialogue, voice acting, subtle animations and CHARACTERS (<3) in this are incredible.
Full of rich social commentary, stacked to the brim with humor and an unexpected gentleness, this might be the best game about the human psyche that has ever been developed.
Dicso Elysium, is, at its core, a game about the sense of self.
But it's also incredibly fun.

It's so full of absurd, hilarious possibilities, interesting puzzzles and well fleshed out story-bits, I find myself wanting to replay this branching mess right after finishing my first succesfull round.

Don't get discouraged by the start feeling weird and overwhelming. It pays off. You're in for a great ride. And yes, you can skipread a lot. The narrative still holds up.
And you will start doing pretty much everything to please Kim Kitsuragi after a while.
That much is guaranteed.

(Seriously, Kim is one of the best video game characters I have ever come across).

All in all: A must play for everyone who likes story-focused games and / or is a sucker for amazing dialogue.
If you just want gameplay-focus and action, this is not for you.
If you want something that will make you roar with laughter and rip your heart out at the same time, it absolutely is.
And once you cracked the gameplay, it's also tons of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
