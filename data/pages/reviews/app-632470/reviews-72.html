\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't like to review until I get all the achievements, because then I feel as if I have done everything the developers have to offer, so after 74 drug and drink filled hours I feel firm on my belief that this is a masterpiece. While it is more or less an interactive storybook with how it is 75% reading, 25% gambling, and 100% HARDCORE. Every single person, other than the ultra-rich light bending guy, feel like a real person with real emotion. This game has got me in it's apricot scented hands and the community behind it gives so much more to enjoy.

The Politics:
This game is full of it, but I don't agree with calling it a fascist, communist, or liberal agenda type game. I feel like it shows what good and what bad it can for these political ideologies. Having you interact with multiple people of each belief and having yourself express these views helps it all stand grounded, it never felt bizarre having people talk about what they feel about in the world, especially in the semi-restored ruins of a battle zone.

The Offensive
This game isn't scared to call you, or to have you call someone, a slur. The f slur is censored, but occurs multiple times. You can be a misogynist while in a conversation with a high standing woman. Comment on a persons skin color. Or bare witness to in game universe racial slurs. This game isn't scared to throw punches when it comes to expressing the depravity of people.

The Achievements
I has able to get roughly half of the achievements blind, but there are so many specific skill checks, time frames, and builds needed to get some of them that a walkthrough is practically required, I doubt anyone is going to know how to teleport without help.

I don't want to say what everyone else has said so I'm ending this off with a goofy review I thought of work shopping this.

Disco Elysium is a never-ending character customization game.\t\t\t\t\t
