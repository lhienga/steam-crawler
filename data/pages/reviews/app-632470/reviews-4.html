Disco Elysium: a gloomy detective book game with excellent writing and voice acting.

So what's it about?
You wake up in a hostel and can't remember anything. You are a mess, and apparently you are a police officer. You stayed in the hostel for the last three days, drunk and barely alive. Someone got murdered and it seems you need to solve this mystery. But who are you?

Disco Elysium is a journey about discovering yourself while solving the case. It is a dark, detective interactive book where success is unlikely and failure almost certain. It has many adult, political and heavy topics and yet it will make you smile and laugh.

You can fail the game if your morale or health falls too low but otherwise there are no real bad choices, even if you fail a check this may still lead to something that you wouldnâ€™t have uncovered otherwise. In a way Disco Elysium reminded me also of the older Lucas Arts adventure games.

The good
The humour is dark but good and entertaining. I did feel you have to be in the right mood to play the game. Sometimes it became a little too gloomy and depressing - I do put this as a good thing because it illustrates how well the story is told and how good the soundtrack and the voice acting supports the game. It's not a happy story and that's fine not everything is great in life. But that's also how we can appreciate the good things in life: "Take the bitter with the sweet".

There is a lot of replay-ability: the way how you behave will form your character and open new ways or keeps them closed. This is also affected by your skills and how you build your character.

I love the music, the characters and the voice overs; it sets the mood really well.

It's impressive to realise the amount of research that have gone into a wide variety of topics and how the real world is mixed with fiction - including various political views.

The not so good
"Take the bitter with the sweet" forced me to be in in the right mood for it and ultimately, I didnâ€™t finish the game because of this as I was just playing to end the game not because I really wanted to at that point. Eventually I burned out on all the dialogues with NPCs and found myself not reading any of it any more.

Even though you have a notepad that keeps track of your progress there are so many events, people and objects intertwined of which you have no knowledge unless you make extensive notes or continue playing that it may be hard to return to the game if you haven't played for a couple of days. I would recommend to play this until you have finished it otherwise you will feel lost.

It's a dark, point and click adventure that is more an interactive book than a game. If you are looking to play an action game: you will not find it here.

Sometimes the path finding is a little clunky. Partly this has to do that the whole environment is 2d while sometimes you move into 3d: like climbing up the stairs. It is difficult to see what is higher and lower than you at times and how to navigate.

To use consumables, you first need to put it in your hand. Then you need to move your mouse cursor slightly below one of the boxes on the main screen until it changes colour. Clicking on the boxes will not do anything. You need to be quite precise as one pixel lower and you can't use the consumable.

Bugs and glitches
There was one bug (once) where I loaded a save game and the main menu stayed on screen. The only way to get rid off it was to restart the game. Other than that the game is very stable.

The DLCs
There are two DLCs but they only give some extras like a soundtrack and an art booklet. I didn't get that so I can't rate it.

Summary
This is one of the best story driven games I have played in a long time. It is fascinating to experience how the game wraps around you and your character and how much choice you get. It's dark but has a good sense of humour. Many times even in the darkest moments it will give you a smile. I loved the voice acting, it's done so well.

In the end I burned out on all of the dialogue. It's more like a book than a game and I really had to be in the right mood to play it as it's dark. I understand many give it praise and you may well like it and think what am I talking about. For me it was still a good experience but perhaps my taste in games has changed.

Should you buy it?
If you are looking for a strong narrative, point and click RPG/adventure detective interactive then this is really good. Just be aware that it is more like a book than a game and you need to appreciate the dark nature of it.
