\r\n\t\t\t\t\t8.5 out of 10

Having completed Disco Elysium a month ago, the game has lingered in my thoughts unlike any other. Attempting to find a comparable rush and excitement in other games proved futile. The immersive world, initially a wow factor, introduced me to a unique experience. The distinct names, locations, and beautiful art style added to the allure. However, the setting's quality dipped, especially in the second map section, which, despite a strong introduction, felt underwhelming and largely empty compared to the original part. Spending significant time in the initial area made the drastic change less positively impactful, though it wasn't devoid of great places.

Characters, a highlight of the game, left a lasting impression. Starting with a dislike for Kim, my opinion transformed by the end, leading to an emotional response when he considered joining my police district. The memorable cast made the conclusion bittersweet, creating a sense of leaving behind a town and its inhabitants.

Disco Elysium's gameplay, reminiscent of a paper-and-pen game, boasts exceptional role-playing elements. Its rich story and numerous choices, influencing various endings and basic conversations, offer a refreshing RPG experience. In a gaming landscape where titles like Fallout 4 and Starfield fall short in true RPG essence, Disco Elysium stands as a testament to what an authentic RPG should be. Despite my writing limitations, I can't emphasize enough the love I have for this game, warranting a solid rating of 8.5 out of 10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has the most realistic writing I've seen in my entire life, in fact it was so realistic I had to stop playing on several occasions to avoid having an existential crisis, everything is just so well written. I do not recommend this game if you're looking for something to cheer you up, it will remind you of real life traumatic events. I have never played any game before that had this much realism in story and dialogue that I had to actually stop playing and take a break.

I'll go on to say this is one of the most unique games I've ever played on PC because of the gameplay, which is MOSTLY reading and interacting, so you're basically reading a interactive book with amazing artwork and animations and making decisions. You will reload saves a lot in your play through to get the options you want because sometimes you may pick something and something else happens, highly recommend save scumming, its worth it.

In conclusion, you need to be in a certain type mood to play this game and you will experience one of the most well written and designed story-rich games ever made. I really wish there was a sequel but it ended perfectly so it's alright. Don't expect call of duty gameplay if you want to buy this, its an amazing slow-paced detective story rich game with exploration set in an amazing world.

11/10, Masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t
