I felt really strongly for the main character and so much of what he felt about himself and the state of the world around him really resonated with me. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't seen any other reviews yet that seem to accurately describe what you're getting into here, beyond confirming that it is good:

The game has a painted isometric theme, set in a retrofuturistic dystopia, and plays like the original Fallout, or Shadowrun Returns (colors/graphics are closer to the latter), and you play a protagonist who's basically Hunter S. Thompson as a police officer/inspector, and the game-play features mostly reading and dialogue, with the option for voiced dialogue and narration, the quality of which just feels a bit "meh". To be honest, I got kinda sick of the voice acting very early on. I appreciate the intent, but just doesn't hit the mark or add flow to the game, it's still a HELLA lot of clicking dialogues.

All that to say, DON'T get this game unless you want to basically read a Hunter S. Thompson dystopian sci-fi one paragraph at a time. Being honest, there was a good amount of reading in Shadowrun Returns as well, but that game was FAR more engaging in every way. This one is a slow ride to grandma's compared to most other story-driven isometric crpgs I've played.

BUT, the art direction is wonderful, the writing is pretty silly and fun, and you rarely get to step into the shoes of such a living wreck in a game, so you might enjoy this if you want something like a story in an off-tempo arthouse film, particularly, as I said, if you're a Hunter S. Thompson fan and enjoyed films like Fear And Loathing In Los Vegas, The Rum Diary or the general silliness of Monty Python skits, or even films and series featuring absolutely terrible police detectives such as Brooklyn 911 or Super Troopers, or about the dredges of society such as Breaking Bad. All this to try to give you an idea of the game's humor and story.

That said, the strength of the game is it's comedic aspect and features plenty of drug and alcohol references, among other crudeness, none of which faze me personally as they seem to have found a home in my daily affairs and social circles.

Just be warned, the game is QUITE slow, but at a mere 10gb install, and able to run on a potato, I give the game huge marks for overall entertainment value and accessibility.

8/10
