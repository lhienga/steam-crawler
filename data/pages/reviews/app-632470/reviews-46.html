Prove them wrong, or lose your mind
- Disco Elysium - The Final Cut - (a very Disco review)

Well, where do i start? this game is one of the *best* RPG game novel storytelling i ever played, the dialogues, the characters the protagonists it's all very compelling with countless of choices for the player do, i would say that in art, style, the direction of the game it is in itself *timeless*.

Disco Elysium is that game that regardless the era it was released or the time it has passed, the graphics and art style, it will be still looking influentially refreshing and *wunderbar*. Loved every second in it, the characters are very unique and has a lot going on with them(someof).

Though through the complexities of the game, it make you think about unreal things that can be real.. like *radiocomputers* or *cryptozoology *(that it is in fact really a thing) like how does this doesn't exist? it sound so realistic.

That type of stuff it really fits well with the game and your story thematics of concepts and choices of the world of Disco Elysium, it's political ideas also in the game, though i am not here to do a review about politics and ideology in the game, but rather i am here to do a review about the *aesthetics* of the game.

I would say that play Disco Elysium is kinda(imo) - Unsettling sometimes, because it really sets off with your reality of who you *are*?, and why are *you* here? aspect, in a navel-gazing level of every decision causing a felling of *stray*, sometimes you just want to go wild in the game(which is funny), and make decisions that maybe set your head off or maybe a comic relief or even further a Communist Party in Revachol.

In another words *Disco Elysium* is pretty much of a darn complex novel and its universe it's full of mystery to unfold, cases to solve and unlimited(*limited*) choices and *conditions* that you can put yourself into it, thus resulting in a different *resultat* of what are gonna led you through the end, thus resulting to a totally different percussion of the game and it's ending...

Thank you for your time and for reading my review. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA mediocre adventure game with embarrassingly juvenile politics.

Don't believe the hype that this game is brilliant and life-changing. It's a tolerable and competent little adventure game, something like "Tex Murphy: Under A Killing Moon". The RPG stats are basically window-dressing; there are very few problems in the game that have multiple approaches (meaning if your stat is low in something you just have to brute force your way through with rerolls) and nothing you do or fail to do really impacts the plot at all.

The game presents a pretty compelling world, but only lets you explore a tiny little corner of it. Compare that to Grim Fandango, which constantly switches up the setting, and pays off every location it mentions by eventually letting you go there.

There's only one mystery to solve, and it's very dragged out and underwhelming (to say the least). It seems the writers were more concerned with extolling communism than crafting an interesting mystery. The writing for this game gets a lot of praise, and it's true that the lines themselves are mostly well-written, and very well delivered -- the voice acting in this game is absolutely top-notch and truly elevates the material. But the content of the script is sophomoric in the extreme. It feels like it was written by a 15 year old whose first girlfriend just dumped him.

If you like adventure games, you can do worse than play this one. But don't expect the magnum opus that it's hailed as.\t\t\t\t\t
