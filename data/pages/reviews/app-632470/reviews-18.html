\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know this game gets a lot of hate because it's not a "real game" and a bunch of retarded communists glommed onto it, but I actually rather enjoyed it, at least enough to sink about 30 hours into it.

I thought it was really well written and the main mystery kept me invested. I opted to go the Neoliberal route because it was funny and you are financially rewarded for doing so. I initially wanted to do a fascist run, but decided against it because I couldn't bring myself to be racist to Kim, he's just too likable. I think in a "good" route you're supposed to sober up, but I continued to use drugs and alcohol for the stat modifiers and roleplay reasons. The dice rolls on different skill checks could be annoying at times especially when you fail a roll you had a 97% chance of success on, but conversely it was cool to roll box cars and pass a check you only had a 3% chance of success on. The main takeaway is quick-save is your friend.

I don't really get the left wing affinity for this game other than it let's you larp as a commie. It doesn't really portray communism in a positive light. It really doesn't paint any of the political ideologies you can choose from in a positive light. I guess because the head dev was a self-proclaimed communist (who's now in a mental institution lmao) and the first version of the game being voice acted by lefty podcasters (who have thankfully been removed from the final cut).

Still once you get past all the controversy, it was a legitimately entertaining experience that I'd recommend to anyone who likes RPGs\t\t\t\t\t
