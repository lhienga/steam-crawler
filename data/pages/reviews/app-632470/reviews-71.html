\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's true; we finally have a worthy spiritual successor to Planescape Torment. Though, I hear Tides of Numenera is also worth a run, for some of that same experience. But that's where Disco Elysium excels, in that it also manages to distinguish itself sufficiently.

What Disco Elysium is, is a great murder-mystery and very thorough detective story, surrounded by slight political satire and critique, touching on everything from ultra capitalism, racialism, neo-liberalism to socialism and communism. While also managing to expose the player to some very thoughtful, mature and heartfelt stories involving just regular people and the drama of the human condition.

It ranges and very easily shifts from absolute heart-wrenching stories, to grim and depressing, to the most wacky and out-there experiences that almost feel like the writers were on acid. At least that's what I'd imagine being on acid is like.

The amount of physical and mental skills used in the game, and because of that the amount of skill checks for various context and information, is astounding and really encourages experimentation and replays. Though, one area that I really did not dabble in very much is the cabinet of thoughts. It looked cool in theory, and I love the flavor text, but it forcing me to choose between skills and thoughts kind of made it too complicated. So I just stuck to min-maxing the main skills. Maybe on a replay.

The music is also wonderful, as is the pastel art style.

About the only negative I could find was the voice actor of one character, Ruby, that for some reason was very monotone and really stuck out like a sore thumb. And the very final 1-2h of play feel very railroaded. But even so I enjoyed the ending and the explanation of what started the whole story. Very mature and subdued.

A 9.5/10 from me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIT'S 10/10 A VERY GOOD GAME. A MASTERPIECE STORY.
it's the third game i found myself immerse to the story and gameplay, after the last of us and the witcher.
I finished it in 8 days in-game time and 38 hours, i rarely want to discover a game a lot or being as a completionist, but Disco Elysium makes me do it, and i enjoyed it! 38 hours worth of my time. Not just any detective mystery game, it has politics, ideologies, fantasy with inland empire, comedy and i even found myself so tense with the great voice over and dialogue. One i didn't expect to experience in a text RPG game. The journey with Lieutenant Kim Kitsuragi and the people of Martinaise itself that makes the adventure and overall experience in the game really enjoyable. There are so much i want to talk about especially the end game one, i want to give spoiler warning about the plot review.

SPOILER WARNING!

Plot Review :
In my 38 hours gameplay, i think i almost finished all the task and side quest and discover almost everything. I love how first the Lieutenant Kim doesn't seem to respect us at all, but there is a character development of myself and him through the game and he finally trust me. All the people of Martinaise also have so much interesting story and quest to complete. Like, EVERYONE. From Titus Hardy, Cuno, The Gardener, Egg Head, Joyce, Rene even Klassje. No, EVERYONE have their own story to tell. The Union Story or Doomed Commercial Area to Phasmid even so interesting to uncover.

The Tribunal is the peak for me. A lot of skill point that i pour in that tense full of Red Check task. YES, even the Horrific Necktie is memorable and it helped me survive the Tribunal. For the last minute in Sea Fotress, i think it's pretty odd to find new suspect. I've been running around with theories for Titus at first, then to Ruby, even the coolest thing i get in the game is when i left the cafeteria but suddenly a thought pops-up and suggest me that Klassje is behind all of this. I get to upstair again to ask her, but ended-up to face an impossible Shivers Red Check. (i didn't arrest Klassje and Ruby in the end). But still it was unexpected for the The Deserter to be the suspect, but i still enjoyed it nonetheless. YET again, i amazed by the last task, even the Lena and all phasmid things paid off in the end. Kinda think to waste my time for that, but it changed my mind after we encountered the Insulindian Phasmid. I like the way Lieutenant Kim also can see it, it makes me think it was a real thing, not just some imagination again.

Everything was a masterpiece experience for me. Finished it in 15th January 2024 and will obviously coming back to Disco Elysium again with 2nd or 3rd playthrough even. No wonder the review is Overwhelmingly Positive.\t\t\t\t\t
