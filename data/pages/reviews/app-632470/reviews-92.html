\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game takes itself very seriously - sometimes too seriously - but it pretends to not take itself seriously. That's a pretty grating mix at times but the writing overall is competent and entertaining enough. The mystery is also pretty entertaining and has many threads that converge in unexpected ways (into a "stereo investigation", if you will) - definitely a positive.

On the other hand, I have NEVER, in my entire life, identified LESS with a video game protagonist than with this game's. Maybe that's what the game wants. Maybe that means I'm a pretty ok person lol. Anyway, a lot of the drama and emotional impact many people seem to be getting from the protagonist and his story is just... not landing with me. At all. But I may just be the completely wrong demographic, so *shrug* - I personally am not enjoying a lot of the "depth" of the narrative, but if you're a middle aged late stage alcoholic who loves to pity himself, you may have a different experience.

Also, be prepared for endless info-dumping and exposition. I understand what the writers were trying to do and I can appreciate the effort (especially with all the different accents for the voice actors) but it does get tiring after a while. I don't care about 56 other countries and governments and history facts etc. - I'm trying to solve a crime. Again, I understand why all the "Encyclopedia" stuff is there, it just got pretty dry after a while.

Overall, the game is impressive enough in sheer scope, determination, and style to make it work and to make it worth playing at least once. Even if it does sometimes feel like a chore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game based on the recommendations, greatest game ever made seems to be banded around a lot. Well, i wholeheartedly disagree. You'd have to be on crack to call this the greatest game ever made.

The dialogue seems to have the primary objective of using 50 words where 5 are called for, so much meaningless uninteresting ramble with every interaction. None of it is even remotely relatable - it's all just fictional nonsense that tries to loosely mirror some of the darkest times in human history. I get it. I can see what they're trying to do, but fictionalising it just makes it mindless drivel with no real impact.

The 'Choices' you make in the game are firmly backed with pure chance. Well... when i say pure chance, i mean a roll of the dice with the game deciding whether or not you get another try, unless of course you back out of the dialogue and save it first, then you can have as many retries as you want with the old load fail reload retry until successful loop, regardless of whether its white or red.

On top of that, it plays like it was made 30 years ago. Click to go here. Click to talk..

After 5 hours, I just got sick of playing the worlds most useless detective who repeatedly and reliably fails pretty much every roll of the dice that the game throws at him despite that hes supposed to be intelligent. About 4 hours in, after my idiot detective somehow managed to balls up shooting a buckle on a cargo strap so badly that he hit the torso of the dead body about 3 feet away at close range, i took the dialogue option of 'I give up, everyone hates detectives anyway' and was presented with game over, he became some sort of writer or something.. I wasn't really paying attention, i was a long way past being even remotely interested in the story at this point so this seemed like a good spot to abandon this game and go find something more interesting to do.

An entirely forgettable experience, i really hope i never encounter anything similar to this again.\t\t\t\t\t
