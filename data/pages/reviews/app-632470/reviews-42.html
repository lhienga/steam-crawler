\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBear with me as I mention Dark Souls in a game review, but you know how some folks will say that From Software has balls for creating these grandiose stages, environments, music and bosses only to put them behind an optional invisible wall, never to be seen by some players?

This game manifests that notion with some of if not the best prose and world building in a video game. It's so dense I want to say it's almost too much even when you are going to miss dialogue due to your RPG stats and RNG, but too much of a REALLY good thing is a great problem to have. The Voice Acting that comes with the Final Cut is straight up perfect, likely due to clear direction of the writing. Food for thought while we are on the eve of AI Voice Acting. My only complaints is that the game can be a little jank so you might want to quicksave fairly frequently, and the clothes-for-stats system sucks but to be honest I'm not sure what a good alternative would be.

Between this and Outer Wilds, 2019 was the year of incredible indie narrative experiences that you REALLY don't want to spoil yourself before playing.\t\t\t\t\t
