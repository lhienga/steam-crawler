\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisco Elysium is quite different from other CRPGs I've played in that it's so much more abstract. The skill tree doesn't necessarily play into what kind of build you want to run, it's more about what kind of person you want to be. Each skill factors into the dialogue and occasional observations your character makes, and they come into play mostly passively. There are quite a lot of skill checks, but they're often written into the dialogue as you probing deeper into the conversation.

I would highly recommend this to someone who enjoys the narrative in games more than the gameplay. As the developers advertised, it's not a video game with good writing, it's the result of a group of writers wanting to tell their story through a video game. The writing is immediately intriguing; it's thought-provoking, hilarious, and at times really vulgar. The story is not necessarily linear and you are not limited to exploring any specific path. You play as a detective, and your objective is to follow leads you discover through exploration and dialogue in order to figure out the mystery of a murder and also the mystery of who you are.

Disco Elysium is fantastic. It'll make you laugh, it'll have you on the edge of your seat, and it might make you feel things.\t\t\t\t\t
