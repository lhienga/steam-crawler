1. A Novel, Not a Game
Well, I think it is overrated as a "game".
It is magnificent and diligent-writing as an interactive novel,
but the process is not entertaining. It's not fun.


2. So Exhausting to Pick up and Learn All These Background Settings
It takes so much effort to conceptualize the world, but the world itself just feel surreal.
All the NPCs have stories to tell indeed. A LOT of stories to tell.
But how could a city only has so few people, while most of them more or less
chat you with some ideology or philosophy?
By the way, I believe the "Pale" in the game, implying this world, this story is not real. It's an imagination only existing in Harry's brain. The pale itself implies the dementia syndromes, and some pale starts to impact harry's brain. This would also make sense why that Phasmid would tell Harry "to forget that woman, even a three-meter tall creature can tell she is ruining "the world" (means Harry's brain damage for over sorrow on drinking, this imagination is about to collapse). Also, that's why in the church Harry says the strange no-sound comes from within Harry himself.
Too bad the ending just gives some lame trials by three not-so-important characters.
Perhaps the originated story is shortened due to budget or due issue.


3. An "adventure" type, not a "detective" or a "RPG" game
You control your man to move around,
click the dialogue options, find things on map,
but actually it doesn't need you to deduct anything.

Except for some scenes may be critical to determine some characters' destinies,
most of the time, you just try to MAXimize clicking all the options.
Some dialogs are pretty awesome as a novel, but as mentioned above,
it could be too tiresome to read one info after another after another after another...

Why this is not a detective type?
When you get on the island, a can't-move culprit who you never met before is there waiting for you to counter. How convenient.
If so Then why Klaasje doesn't tell Harry the bullet comes from the island in the very beginning, but point to Ruby? On detective story level Ruby works as a red Herring, but the Ruby line itself, doesn't make progress in the main story either. She says Harry is a corrupted lackey under some mob's boss, but in the end Jean tells Harry he isn't. This part just not make sense. And the culprit on the island just make all these investigations effort in vain. The purpose for these investigation only helps the player get to know fascism, communism, liberalism, so we could resonate more about the culprit's feelings.


4. Not an Open World, just Limited Option Lists.
This is not an open world game, at least not the sand box like Kenshi.
What you can do is actually quite limited.
Yes, you have tons of options to choose in the dialog lists, but what you can do are still within these words.
It's not like "Yes I am a cop so I can use my well-chosen-feature-skill to do things such as convincing this NPC to bribe me, or give me discount" or do any things on players' will.
Players still get stuck in the novel's frame with limited freedom.

Another thing, those 24 skills actually only influence pass or not on your "dice checking."
If locked, you just have to talk to more people, to increase your xp to unlock.
Dialogues may differ according to which skill you are good at, but it doesn't influence much.


5. In the story climax, anti-climax, or the final ending,
the emotional-rewarding is not strong and purifying enough to countervail all these pains or heaviness the story gives all the time.

The tone of the game has always been pale, painful, and heavy. The inner thoughts' taunting in the protagonist's dream always give me the creeps.

The ending try to cheer up the atmosphere and be bright and optimistic, but it just doesn't make sense.
Three cops who belong to protagonist's team try to judge his behavior while not helping solve the case at all. Really? I mean, Jean is not even Harry's superior!
There aren't even options for me to choose "Fine, I quit!" or "smack my ledger on Jean's face". It's almost like Harry is begging these partners for return. How shameful!

And Harry couldn't just get over his ex-girlfriend for six years...? and they are not even married...
I am sorry but the whole elaboration from the beginning of the story, it implies Harry is in a kind of huge PAIN and AGONY, wanting to kill himself or in dementia forever, which make the player REALLY feel it.
And in the end it seems all these pain, all these emotions become an absurd comedy.
In the game, Harry can drink, can smoke, and take drugs like eat candy. The only thing this game Harry can't do is to find prostitutes, I guess that's why he is in such great pain to get over his ex even 6 years have passed.
(I am not saying it's not painful, it's just so misleading and lacking in consistency by its story elaboration.)

Below are some personal feelings, just personal.
Some people get touched when finally meeting the Phasmid, but this creature for me just not that appealing, kind of creepy...maybe I am just not open-minded enough.
Some people think the church music is so funny and hardcore! But this strong-beat just makes me feel so repressed.
In addition to the Reptile brain and limbic system's uncomfortable taunting mentioned above, yes I also hate that squeaky talking necktie!

Not until I put so many comments did I realize that this game just doesn't resonate me...
even though I really immerse myself and spend quite a time on this game.
I really want to like this game, but somehow it just doesn't click.

Some people say it's not a game but a journey to seek oneself. But even in the end, even when I have finished all these long and deep text-roads, only some bitterness and hollowness like pale left in me.\t\t\t\t\t
