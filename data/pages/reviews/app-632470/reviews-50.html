\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA wonderfully written adventure (not really an RPG) that doesn't respect your time.

The writing is impeccable, well paced, the dialogue is interesting, layered and funny. Sometimes it's utterly dark and depressing, the political discussions are honestly a bit pointless and on the nose. The fantastical stuff is gripping and interesting and I hoped they would make more use of it. The character dynamics are really interesting.

What I disliked about the game was the setting. Sure it's fun to dive into a deep, new world, but this is not a new world. It's mostly a spin on our world and that with an incredible amount of depth and history. The game doesn't respect your time. It info dumps HARD and you have to read through endless amounts of history, that has no relevance to the game itself, the story itself, it's there as "world building". That would be great if you could expect this to be the first in a series, but we all know that it's not. This is a wonderfull indie title, a magical blitz in a bottle, not a franchise. So I felt sometimes a bit bored reading all that history, all that military actions, the general names, the country names, the places you haven't and will never visit etc. etc. that is the biggest and only real weakness of the game.

The writing and interesting aspect to have conflicting, internal dialogue from the actual "attributes" of your character is such a good idea though, that I think anyone who loves a good story should play this game. Though rest assured: This is more of a "point and click" adventure, than a traditional RPG. Which is not a minus for me, I grew up playing adventures, so it was a welcome deviation from the norm. Go play it.\t\t\t\t\t
