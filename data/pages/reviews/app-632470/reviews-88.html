\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't even beaten this game yet, but I absolutely adore it! (I keep taking hiatuses from finishing due to factors completely unrelated to the game)

This game is a wellspring of excellent narrative, exploratory, and choice-based game play. Over the course of the game you can easily see how your choices affect your character, those around you, and the case.

You'll investigate something seemingly unrelated to the case and hours later use that knowledge to find or learn something incredibly useful. Throughout my first play through I have made several major advances in the main case and in my side quests that left me pondering how I would have made that particular discovery if I hadn't done X, or how I would have continued on in the case without my new insight(s).

This game's primary game play loop gives me a very similar sense of advancement and discovery that Outer Wilds gave me (which is one of my favorite games).

The writing is an absolute joy to read and voice acting is incredible, especially given the fact that this game has a total word count exceeding ONE MILLION (More words than the entire bible) (It is important to note that minor dialogue variations are likely a huge contributor to the total word count).
The voice actor for the player character's thoughts (Lenval Brown) voices over 300 THOUSAND words of dialogue. (Source: https://www.pcgamer.com/we-talk-to-disco-elysiums-incredible-narrator-who-recorded-350000-words-of-dialogue-and-has-never-acted-before/)\t\t\t\t\t

I am glad I finally played this after having it in my library for years, but I think it was the right time as it really takes some focus to get through the game. But it only took me about 30 hours to get through my first play through, and while I def missed some stuff, I feel like I did quite a bit. The writing and voice acting is just at such an insane quality that it rivals some of the best games in the medium, and it being this studios first game makes it even more impressive. The art and music are also second to none. My only issue is that the ending felt a bit abrupt, like a setup for a sequel that we may unfortunately never get. Otherwise this game is a masterpiece and worth every second I spent with it.\t\t\t\t\t
