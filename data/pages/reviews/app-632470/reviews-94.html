\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not sure why this game is categorized as an RPG. It's really nothing like most CRPGs. It's not even an adventure game as there really aren't puzzles or anything like that. It's basically interactive fiction. There's not much in terms of gameplay. There are dice rolls and items but they really only affect what story details and dialog options you get. Also it has one of my least favorite game tropes, the old "chose an option and hope nothing bad happens". You can't really lose the game. You just keep exploring and talking to people until you solve the mystery.

In spite of the weak gameplay, I would still highly recommend this game due to the strong writing and great story. It has the best writing I've ever seen in a game. I'm the type of gamer that normally just clicks through dialogues and doesn't interact with non-quest-giving NPCs but I read most of the text in this game. I say "most" because there are a few places where the pseudo-intellectual babble gets to be too much.\t\t\t\t\t
