\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like narrative based RPG, then this game is for you. I would even say that it feels like a quest with RPG added on top.

It's fully narrated (with minor bugs) and has enough written content to keep you occupied for quite a while (I think it took me around 40 hours to complete it). Usually I don't like long games, but it's mostly because long games are repetitive, while Disco Elysium can be repetitive, it's mostly in a good sense.

Positive moments:
- Hero has amnesia, so you discover the world with him, through dialogues, questions and learning about the world intuitively
- Skill checks are everywhere, there're no useless skills in this game
- A lot of ways to actually role-play, choosing sides, ideologies and outcomes
- It feels like you're time-pressed. Dialogues pass time and some things are time sensitive (or feel like it). At some point in the game you start to feel like you really need to act quickly.
- Rich world with it's lore and history
- Art is an art :)
- Great voiceover
- It doesn't try to hide controversial topics, it uses them to drive the narrative.

Negative moments:
- Some "paths" are strange. Like when you contact the Archer and then it just extract you and that's it. Feels quite underwhelming.
- At several occasions in the game it feels like you just need to extinguish all dialogue options, like your answers don't matter for that parts of the game.
- It feels like there's a room for replayability, but after 40 hours spent in the game you end up quite exhausted, and not ready to do it again (though some parts can be fast-forwarded).
- There's an ironmode in the game, but with no way to retry white checks unless you level you skill it feels like you'll be stuck in some parts of the game. I wish the game had a way to "resource manage" this stuff. Take some strain, or damage, but try it again.

Overall I feel like this is already a great game and it should be experienced by any RPG/Quest lovers.\t\t\t\t\t
