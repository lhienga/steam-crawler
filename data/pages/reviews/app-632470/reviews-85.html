\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not for everyone. It demands a lot of time and patience from the player. It's a very story driven game. There isn't much gameplay and all you really do is read. It's more like a novel disguised as a CRPG. If the premise of a drunken pathetic waste of skin cop trying to solve a murder after losing all his memories from a week long bender doesn't sound appealing, it might be hard to get through the several hours of reading required to finish the game.

If the premise does seem interesting to you and you go in with an open mind, I think most people will fall in love with Disco Elysium. The prose in this is the best I've ever seen in a video game. Absolutely brilliant. It's also absolutely hilarious. I can't think of a game that has made me laugh as much as this one. I wish I could discuss the political and philosophical themes of this game, but I'm not nearly educated enough on the subject. Either way, what I loved most about this game was the overall plot and how heartbreaking and beautiful it was. The game goes to some dark places and you meet characters who are struggling with grief and self-destruction. It gut punches you over and over. then by the end of the game it quietly affirms that life is beautiful and that its worth living. Disco Elysium sets a very high bar for storytelling in video games. I honestly think it might be a very long time until another game comes close to matching the level of technical writing seen in Disco Elysium.

The only other thing I will say is that if you decide to get this game, make sure to do the cryptid hunter guy's side quest.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a lot of things, perhaps it is well made, but I did not enjoy my time with it. I know I am in the minority here, and I understand why it has received universal acclaim.

The first thing I want to note is that this game is incredibly dense. Doing the smallest of tasks elicits pages of dialogue describing how you feel about it. This is not necessarily a bad thing, and its clearly what the developers were shooting for, but for me personally it grows very tiring after a while.

The world is also very alien, with many nations and historical events to be educated on. I expected a dark murder mystery where you get to make decisions, not having to read 10 pages about the politics about various nations around the world. Some people wish to immerse themselves in a fictional world and spend hours learning about the political turmoil in this strange land. I do not, and I don't think the advertisement made it clear what playing this game is like. I am not very interested in politics, and I disagree with some of the political assertions this game makes.

One unquestionably positive thing I can say is that I was intrigued by the idea of your many 'skills' having their own voice and input on things. I've never seen a system quite like this, and it was the primary thing that strung me along to learn more. At first glance it seems like you could play this game dozens of different ways and have a new experience each time by allocating your points differently. You make decisions constantly, and have different outcomes to contend with. Perhaps this is the ultimate replayable game, you might think.

Unfortunately a lot of this is an illusion, and this game is very linear. There is one true path and you can't really fail it. Your skills affect some flavor dialogue, but make very little meaningful change to the direction of the story. Mostly they change your skill checks, most of which are repeatable until you succeed. The only penalty for failing most skill checks is losing time or money. What this basically means is a diceroll, influenced by your skill points, determines not the progression of the story but how much grinding you have to do.

Speaking of grind, you can grind skill points in this game that is basically a visual novel. Keep talking to people and you'll start to see your stats go up a bit. Also you earn money by picking up trash, at the realistic rate of about 5 cents per bottle. That's just what I want to do, run around picking up 'tare' just so I can spend my money to buy some consumable item and attempt some skill check again. Yes, you can savescum any skill check but then that's not fixing the game, its avoiding playing it.

Is this game worth playing? It 100% depends on if you enjoy the dense dialogue, snarky internal monologues, and reading extensively about the history of this fictional world. If you find these characters charming and the world interesting, then you probably will enjoy it as most others have. But for me, I didn't. Between the grinding, the fairly meaningless skills, the random skillchecks, and the ultimate illusion of choice due to the linear narrative, I got annoyed playing this game and felt like I was wasting my time.

I just wanted to run around as a psychopath and be a dirty cop, making all the wrong choices. Instead I felt completely powerless and hamstrung at every turn by the game telling me I wasn't doing what it wanted me to do. If you ever wanted to feel like a cop with zero authority, respect, or power then here you go. The cop that can't do anything (I know you eventually do but it feels like you can't for a very long time).\t\t\t\t\t
