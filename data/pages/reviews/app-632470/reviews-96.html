This is a text heavy game, essentially a light novel with some point and clicking. There's no combat, no reflex checks (QTEs) or any of such sort. The only gamey thing is basically skill rolls (if you play tabletop games like DnD or pathfinder you'll recognize the system), with increased and decreased modifiers depending on the action, your correlated skill and how good you are at that and things you did prior to the skillcheck that ended up being relevant to help or unhelp you.

But what it does it does immensely well. All dialogue is voiced, whether its other characters or your inner thoughts (divided into different 'skills'). There are multiple ways to get through the game which is important as our means of interactions are only what we're allowed to do through dialogue.

It's a murder mystery with an amnesiac cop. Who killed the hanging man? Who are you? Did you do it? You are a blank slate with a past and only through what you ask and explore will you decide who you are now. Are you the man you used to be? Or a completely new butterfly hatched out of his cocoon?

The characters in the game are well written (as are your different skills, depicted as specific ways of thinking or reacting to situations who will talk to you about it when they feel prompted), with their own quirks both good and bad. It is a game about political aspects after all and all political views have their good and bad. You don't need to toot the horn of your favourite political agenda. By merely answering or handling situations and people the way you do you'll fill out your political spectrum naturally.

If you enjoying an engaging read, mystery unravelling and character growth (for many characters can be persuaded by Mr Du Bois to change their way of thinking) this is a golden standard for a narrative, text-based adventure.

It's a dirty, gritty, ♥♥♥♥♥♥ post-war world. And you gotta either suck it up and do your job, or be the sorry cop.\t\t\t\t\t
