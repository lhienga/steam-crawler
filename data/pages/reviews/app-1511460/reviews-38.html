\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*large edit - it seems most (if not all) my gripes have been addressed since I left this review, but I'll keep it up for posterity. Great job devs!

InfraSpace is an interesting one. The city builder aspect is the side dish to the main course. It took me a few hours to realize this.

You're not so much building a city as much as building the most efficient logistics to send products to your citizens. This is done via putting certain buildings next to each other for bonuses, balancing distance between ore clusters, assigning districts so vehicles aren't crossing the entire map to deliver things, and, most important, the roads.

Logistics is what this game is focused on and with the variety of roads it is fun as hell to create complex districts connected by highways which then connect to super highways to make your pathing as smooth as possible.

As for negatives, tooltips are inconsistent and lacking, and more importantly, connecting roads can sometimes be a pain in the ass. If you connect a road to a highway, but it makes the highway a bit too short, you have to destroy every connecting aspect of those roads. Not horrible, just annoying.

*edit - it seems this was fixed in a recent update.

Additionally the snapping 90 degrees is really inconsistent, sometimes it snaps, sometimes it doesn't. There's also the aspect of the habitat buildings upgrading and getting bigger, sometimes making you have to destroy roads and rebuild them or just destroy a couple buildings in the middle b/c there's not enough room.

Honestly, small gripes for what is a very fun logistics game.

Citizens never leave their habitats. Bizarre, but makes sense considering the main purpose of the game (and makes it a bit easier since you need no roads coming out of habitat districts).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLots of reviewers are throwing out comparable games. The first thing that came to mind for me was Anno 2070 but with actual traffic and less frustration. I loved that game, so this is a plus. I also got a huge whiff of Surviving Mars, again with actual traffic and less frustration. I was not a fan of that game, so I'm happy to say I enjoy InfraSpace more. If you liked either game, you'll very much like InfraSpace.

If you enjoy solving traffic problems or even just watching traffic in Cities Skyline, then this will appeal to you. If you enjoyed solving logistic problems in Shapez.io, this is a must have. If you strive for logistical efficiency and optimization, well that's most of the game.

I'm early on, but it seems to me that traffic replaces what would be a conveyor belt in other factory/colony games. I fall asleep playing Mini Motorways in bed all the time, so I can jive.

The game has great potential. I'm looking forward to seeing the roadmap go live and opening the game up to modders.
