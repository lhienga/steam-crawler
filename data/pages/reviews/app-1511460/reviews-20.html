\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you keep in mind that it's Early Access, this is a very good city building game.

You'll run into some unexpected issues, but solving them and figuring out what's happening is something I actually like.
Example: I had a 4 lane T-junction, and connected a park directly to it. Suddenly on one leg of the junction cars would despawn right after the junction.

I'm hoping that they'll add more of a stimulus to optimize or work towards an end goal. During one playthrough I got tired of optimizing for a bit, and set up a single yellow science producer with a chain of 1-1-1-1 buildings leading to it. Didn't work well, but after a nap and dinner, I was on to red science.

My point being: You can get away with not optimizing by taking a nap. After comparing this to other city/colony games I've played, (I'm putting this game on a potential level of Rimworld, sim city since 199x (okay, not the latest), Banished)I feel like there should be something you can't plan for or predict that requires an urgent response.

It's a work in progress, and it's a working early access game (so it beat expectations). Looking forward to the end of the roadmap.\t\t\t\t\t
