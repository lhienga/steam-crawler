\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInfraspace hits a really nice spot for me, in terms of complexity, to be a really relaxing city builder/logistics game. It does such a nice job of managing the fine details of logistics in your city, that you can focus on the bigger picture when building. The recent train update exemplifies this, automatically managing train signals and loading the trains as part of the broader logistics network, making them feel like an equal and hassle-free way to deliver goods between regions. I'm also very fond of the small efficiency bonuses given for adjacency of related buildings. It means that you need to put thought into how you arrange your buildings, but is not such a strong bonus that you agonize over optimal placement. As a result of all of these factors, the game falls into a nice, natural flow of building that I often find missing in other games, either because they're too simple, or have spikes of complexity that throw me out of the game.

The one thing I really have on my wishlist for this game is for it to give better data to the player to let you optimize a bit more carefully. The game will tell you if you have a resource shortfall or surplus across the whole map, but I wish that the user-defined districts would let you check their individual production and consumption levels as well, so it's easier to tell whether your outpost is well-balanced, or if it has to ship in a resource from the other side of the map to supplement it.\t\t\t\t\t
