\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've seen people compare this to a combination of Satisfactory and Cities Skylines (two games I love), and having played it I think it more fairly is Factorio and Cities Skylines with a little bit of idle game thrown in. It certainly scratched my itch for factory/city games, and I do heartily recommend. Below are more details of why:

DA GOOD:
-- I love that the resource patches are all infinite (like Satisfactory), which means you don't have to uproot and change every 2-3 hours.
-- The models and animations are very cute.
-- The ambient music is easy to listen to for hours.
-- Being able to switch between orthogonal and diagonal building grids at will allows great flexibility.
-- Working on the orbiting ship provides fun long-term goals that are satisfying to watch complete.
-- The ability to move buildings/roads after placement is a MUST!
-- Each different pipe system (water, O2, methane) work on different depths, so you don't have to worry about crossing one another with complicated messes (like Factorio).
-- I LOVE that you can upgrade road-types on the fly, and that the largest roads still fit within the established grid. Certain other games would make large roads significantly wider than small ones, making nearby buildings invalid, which totally screws-up my layout.
-- The mechanic where habitats expand with needs provides very organic and satisfying growth.

DA UN-GOOD:
-- Building connections to roads are weirdly finnicky, and have a tendency to disconnect if changing roads nearby.
-- Train-lines are very strange with inclines and terrain. I spent an hour trying to make a smooth ramp up a hill only to have the game imagine some error which invalidated the circuit. So instead I allowed it to auto-populate and it is the ugliest and most strange wiggly ramp. Why?!
-- WHY DIDN'T ANYONE TELL ME YOU COULD PLACE POWER POLES ON ROADS?! I only discovered this 10+ hours in and it changed EVERYTHING!
-- Placing roads at different heights over one-another is very difficult to do because the game wants to make everything connect to everything else, even with road-snapping turned off.
-- I had extreme difficulty learning the District and exportation ruleset. For instance I would build 12 big aluminum drills, but they would only occasionally put-out products. I was confused since my production grid said I was making butt-loads. Turns out they only put-out as much as is consumed by other buildings.

DA REQUESTS:
-- I would love an additional column on the production grid that shows percentage of Produced v. Consumed (something like Produced 150, Consumed 100, Consumption Rate 66%). It would certainly simplify and make clear which production line needs prioritized.
-- A traffic overview (like Cities Skylines) would be very helpful for identifying bottlenecks before I have a 100+ car gridlock.
-- In the same way that groundwater pumps replace early-game water generators, I would love larger versions for generating O2, methane, and carbon (the individual O2 and carbon generators are a little underwhelming).
-- Why do all train stations (minus the smallest one) need 8 road connections? 2 each side is plenty for all except maybe the gigantic one.
-- Maybe introduce weather disasters? I never once had difficulty doubling my energy needs with a combination solar/wind farm.\t\t\t\t\t
