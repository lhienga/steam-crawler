\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I thought the premise of the game was interesting and was thought out enough for me to give it a try. Here I go, energy drink in hand, slam the record button and off I go to record a new series of a game that I was quite interested in seeing what it could do.

I was actually enjoying playing with city layouts and transportation networks until two things bit me terribly. Trains and districts. I spent 2 hours 'off camera' to determine why a train route that was working very well for about 4 hours stopped working completely after I added more rail. I couldn't figure it out for the life of me and completely tearing down all my rail infrastructure to get an addition working was 0% fun.

Building layouts and districts also seem much more irritating than they are worth. Why are circular districts used in a game that uses a square grid for building placement? Furthermore, why do buildings change what orientation they are facing after I add other buildings. This was another aspect that took a lot of extra faffing about to get something that looked amazing and balanced to 'welp, now this side isn't getting any sand because the building changed position on me'.

This game could be amazing, but I wish the devs worked on fundamental errors rather than adding new content. I haven't tried anything past the Pipes update because I was so turned off at how basic things operated. I do look forward to trying this game again, but maybe in a few years and some fixes. Till then, I can't honestly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
