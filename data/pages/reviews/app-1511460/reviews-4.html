\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn attempt to make logistics simulation game in Anno-like game.

The game is effectively production chain management mixed with logistics simulation. Your colony ship crashlands on some remote planet, rather than designated destination, and you'd need to either colonize this one, or repair a ship and continue on your journey.
The production chain iteself is very good, with lot of different resources and factories available to build and expand your realm. Most of those resources and factories you, of cause, need to research - this being another resource sink. However, Infraspace is not a production automation game. Ultimately, your main resource, just as in Anno series, is the population, and you'd want to keep them satisfied to has more workforce. To do so, you supply residential buildings with luxury goods, so that they will upgrade eventually to houses and further to skyrises.
The game attempts to be a logistics simulator - I guess this is where most people get their comparison to Skylines. Fortunately, this is not Skylines. Pathfinding is much better, and delivery trucks wouldn't think you only have a single road in the world. Anyway, may be it is just me, but I just brute-forced the logistics component. If you more or less cluster your production, which game really incentivise to do, you will never have your roads even nearly cognested.
Graphics is very decent; even though it is a bit disappointing to look at a desert surface in the very beginning, it is understandable. Buildings are well drawn and animated, making it quite a nice eyesight.
The music is a bit disappointing, but sound effects are okay.

Still, at least for me, the game was not really fun. Unline old SimCity (e.g., 2000 and the like), it does not feel rewarding to just grow your city.
Researching advanced technologies is a resource sink, as I have mentioned, which can be circumvented by exponentionally growing your production (just like in DSP - this is where this comparison come from), however, population does not grow exponentially. The game says, population grow speed depends on the residential building level. I am not sure about it, it seems to be more dependant on amount of free housing.
This turns later stages of Infraspace into a waiting game, not really rewarding at it, unlike DSP.

Initially, I wanted to not recommend this game, but after giving it a thought, I'm going to suggest you take your shot, if you have a time and will. Even though I believe that it is the formula which is not working, not small details, but, for some reason unknown, I keep returning to Infraspace. May be still, later on, some additions could be made to make it more interesting?

Be wary this is by no means Skylines, luckily. This is not the Railroad tycoon of eld, unfortunately, no-one managed to make a proper infrastructure simulator since. And obviously, it is not a factoroid., This is more or less Anno/SimCity game, and if you like them, probably it is worth checking on it.\t\t\t\t\t
