\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCities Skylines partly meets Dyson Sphere Program (and maybe a little bit of Factorio), this is InfraSpace. A low colony mostly logistic type game that has a pretty solid core, but lacks little details that could take it to the next level. I'll recommend this one with a caution to read reviews.

InfraSpace has very straightforward gameplay. The player looks over the colony from above and can modify the camera as necessary for a better building or viewing angle. Building is an instant process, be it buildings, mines, or roads. The premise of the game heavily focuses on logistic management, or in this specific case: traffic. The goal is to keep roads as organised as possible while ensuring a steady flow of resources to sustain colonists. The ultimate goal in the game is to rebuild a damaged spaceship. The cool concept here is the more resources the player sends to their launching pad equates to a better gameplay experience. For example, the player can fix the weapons on the ship to eliminate obstacles blocking resources or potential build areas whereas some other features may produce additional resources. The more the player researches means more buildings, and later in the game, terraforming. Intersection and road control are most likely going to be the logistic gamer's favourite feature.

The visuals and graphics are okay... not sure what other word to use here. The art style of the game is completely fine, but there are minor details in animations or other little snippets that make the game come short. Construction sites do have stages and change but are limited in their animation. Traffic randomly sometimes disappears or clips through roads, or clip through roads and do a massive u-turn. The user-interface can be enlarged in addition to a lot of in-game UI that helps the player keep a better eye on what is going on. An encyclopedia is also included - super useful in the beginning of the game. The sounds and music are also.... okay. I'll be honest this is probably the worst part about the game. I turned down the music as it was very repetitive and slowly driving me crazy. The sounds are alright, but putting down a bunch of gas based storage will have you thinking there is a gas leak in the house - or at least your neighbours.

So, glowing-ish review, but where is the issue? The game is simply okay. I bought it out of boredom (and on sale - which I sort of feel like it was a little steep still) and had my fun with it. Personally, I'd rather go back to Cities Skylines now that I've beaten the game. It can be slow and sluggish at times to get to certain points (or resources) in the game. There is a fast forward button, but it's not really that fast (which could be due to a buffering issue having so much on the screen). There are small glitches here and there which don't ruin the game, but hinder experience. Distribution of resources isn't always straight forward and buildings next to one another don't seem to deliver goods. Depending on what it is could frustrate the player. It's fun and entertaining while it's new. The polish does wear off a little quicker than it should have. I like the the concept of the game mixing resources with civilian sustainability, there is a missing level of engagement to keep the player interested.


RATING : 7.2/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEnjoyable city-builder, which focuses on transportation route efficiency as much as resource gathering. You can have resources gathered beyond your needs, but still fail in providing them to your factories if you do not understand and use the transportation mechanics efficiently.

Not much hand-holding in the game -- if you cannot take the time to study and understand and use the mechanics as designed, and get frustrated when there is no "EASY" button, then move on...perhaps go back to playing WOW. This game requires you to be able to study, plan and implement an efficient transportation network...NOT just plop down a bunch of roads and expect it to work. Storage and Distribution can be challenging to get your head around in this game, but once you understand how it works things get a lot easier...but expect to go through a fair bit of trial/error in early play-throughs. Game has a few QoL mechanics which make this trial/error period a slightly less frustrating path: for example, you can move buildings/factories, etc., without having to destroy and completely rebuild them.

Pacing is slow, especially in early-game when you've not researched the ability to build faster roads and highways -- so you will spend time waiting for resources to arrive, and for products made from said resources to then be shipped to next stage of production-chain...again if you're the type who cannot understand such systems and expect everything to magically appear instantly then move on to 'easy' games.

Approx. 31-hours into the game at time of writing this review and I am just barely getting a grasp of how to optimize my transportation system (some organizational-wizard-types could most likely learn it a lot faster; but I enjoy the journey, not just rushing to 'end-game' like most); so this game is one you can sink a LOT of time into if you're the type who enjoys 'tinkering' with systems in building games like this. If you're the type who thinks the point is to rush to 'end-game' as fast as possible, you can go else-where and join the group who're leaving negative reviews, as most of those negative reviews are left by people with low play-time who failed to try to learn to use the systems "AS DESIGNED", but rather want game developers to change the systems to suit their instant gratification syndrome.

If you're a true 'builder/tinkerer' you'll enjoy this game. It has some unique mechanics which make it a refreshing change of pace from other 'cookie-cutter-copies' of city-builders, but which can be frustrating to understand at first because they are different (different does not equal wrong)...I got the game for less than $20-USD (sale) and do not regret buying it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImagine Cities: Skylines but on Mars, with the importance put mostly on the industry rather than urban planning, and a few essential quality of life mods built in. Since I spent embarrasingly large amount of time in C:S, I assumed I'll like it, but it's very disappointing. My best guess is that this game is just too slow and anticlimactic. This game cerrtainly gives the superficial appearance of being a decent city builder, but that's all - it looks like one, but it doesn't feel like one.

The first problem is that this game is just too slow. In C:S, you are almost constantly in control of the pace. At the beginning, you progress fast and you get rewarded almost immediately. Later on, the game makes you work harder for bigger rewards, but by that point, you have enough of a city already built to keep yourself busy, and you're already invested enough that you're willing to work for more. Sometimes I actually postponed progressing just to fiddle with the aesthetics of my town. Here, I'm constantly forced to wait - for the resources to be delivered, for the research to be done, for the upgrades to finish. I have nothing to do, I'm getting bored, and I can't speed the game enough to skip to the interesting part. And I don't even know when or if there will be an interesting part, because all I've seen so far are locked items in every menu.

The second problem is that once I achieve anything, it doesn't feel like a success. The fanfare on level up in C:S could be a little silly, but it was something I've actively worked on and it actually progressed the game, so it did feel like a reward. Here, I was informed that my city achieved level 3 with less ceremony than the tutorial messages - I never even noticed reaching level 2, I had no idea what I need to do to reach level 4, or even what that level meant. Unlocking new buildings merely meant adding more cars to the road, in slightly different colors. Unlocking pipes was a bit interesting, but their uses were limited, and I gave up before I reached the trains, because even done perfectly, they had no chance of making me invested in the game again.

The game seems like a good platform that could, over time, evolve into something engaging, so I'm willing to wait for that. And it's good for Cities: Skylines to have some real competition. But it's not there yet, and I hope that it didn't even give me a taste of the final product, because that would be seriously disappointing.\t\t\t\t\t
