\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn one hand, this is the first colony builder that has captured my interest. But after 140 hours of play, cannot stomach the gridlock aspect any more. So yes, I'm recommending it, but with reservations. So what is InfraSpace?

It's a colony builder with an emphasis on the supply chain. Satisfaction of the colonists is reflected by whether they are emigrating or immigrating to/from your colony. Underemployment is very bad thing, because it undermines your industrial capacity. But unemployment is a good thing --> Pool of available workers, to support your industrial expansion, and there is no penalty for it.

4 parts of the economy,
1) Building materials (concrete & steel),
2) Products for your colonists (oxygen, home appliances, various forms of food, schools, etc, etc. ... but no water),
3) Research, and
4) Electricity. Other 3 parts can be unconnected by roads to each other, but everything requires power.

Flow of the game (order is approximate),

1) Create mines & factories for the basics, connecting them with roads. Sulfur & sand for concrete, concrete factory, spaceport, atmosphere processing, housing, etc., etc.
2) Create research buildings, more mining (Fe, Cu, Al), farms, meat processing, and more factories. Leave plenty of space for roads --> You start with gravel, and through research, eventually gain super highways.
3) More advanced factories, and support services for the colonists (parks, stadiums, & schools). In the games that I played to end, Electronics / Microchips / Nanotubes dominated my factory makeup.
4) More mining (uranium & iridium), and more advanced factories to support more advanced research labs & colonists product demands (there are 6 levels of housing, and each level adds more products they require more complex manufacturing).
5) Adamantine mining. Once you research this & start mining, you have probably researched the entire tech tree, and there is nothing left to do.

Pros:

* - Great graphics.
* - Enjoyable supply chain management.
* - Each map is different.
* - 2 sorts of difficulty levels; game & transport. Transport is very important --> At the easy level, each truck carries 4 units, while at hard each truck transports 1 unit. (Think what would happen to traffic in your area if FedEx, UPS, and Amazon used 4x as many trucks to deliver the amount of packages.)
* - Playing from start to finish is good for over 50 hours.
* - Non-combative; no enemies to deal with, so you focus on your economy.
* - Grid system for laying out your roads. This was just added in the March update, and was a much welcome addition to the game. And its optional, in case you don't like it.
* - Job prioritization system: You can control how many colonists work at each industry. (BTW, colonists don't commute. They mysteriously appear at the job sites.)

Cons:

* - Gridlock. Minor annoyance at the easy transport difficulty level, but at the medium level (2 units per truck), this becomes something that must be dealt with. Found traffic lights to be useless.
* - Parks, stadiums, and schools have a radius of influence. So you can't just build all of them in their own little corner of the map. They have to be scattered in the residential areas.
* - Lack of info. Learn the game by playing. (Hey, it's early access.) But it improved in the March update, with the ability to see where goods are being delivered to from a given factory. On the bright side, there are lots of helpful people on both the Steam discussion board & developer's forum ... mostly the former.
* - Tutorial sucks. Again, it's early access.
* - Some stability decrease since the March update. Had 2 or 3 crashes in a 1 hour period, but that was it. And autosave alleviates this.

Bottom Line: Got my money's worth out of this game. Hoping the developer either removes or makes optional the regional effect of schools & other services. But killer is the gridlock problem --> Takes fun out of the game. Hopefully, the developer will add some tool to help players resolve this problem ... but if not, and never play it again, it proved that colony builders can be fun (if find one that's a good fit). If this & other reviews don't persuade you to buy this game, watch the video reviews to get a better idea of what the game is about.\t\t\t\t\t
