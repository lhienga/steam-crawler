\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has an absolute ton of potential but is held down by some simple issues and it definitely needs some (production) balancing.
For example
- Rail - introduced later in the game - can bend in perfect curves, on purpose or by suggestion (even S-curves) of the game. Roads on the other hand require a ton of extra clicks to end nowhere near how perfect rail can look.
(but maybe this is done on purpose...)
- Production rates require some math or an excel spreadsheet to get anywhere near balanced. That's over the top for me. Just make everything per 60sec or something
- The game is missing a map overview while the map is HUGE... It's also littered with rocks and plants (?) that can only be removed a whole lot later in the game, which makes planning / efficient lay outs difficult especially early on (FFS this is the future... we can mine, build etc but not remove rocks and plants from the start?)

- Inconsistency: roads and rails can somehow suddenly break (on conncection points), making a prduct undeliverable which can obliterate your city. There's no indication whatsoever nor a reason that causes it.
- One load of a save game can litterally differ from a 2nd of the same file where you fix unexpected issues from the 1st quickly

For now a thumbs up cause developpers are active, weekly if not daily. It's up to them to get consistancy and better (math wise) playability. Then it can be a top game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI admit that I waited some time before purchasing this game. It languished on my wish list for some time, and I was intrigued by the reviews and by the description. But having now purchased it, and played it for a few hours my verdict is that this is just another low-grade city builder, with no complex mechanics. The fact that it is set in a future environment, colonizing another world, is immaterial to what is just a Sim City clone with an Anno 2XXX feel. This game could just as easily have the story "You inherit a large plot of land and you decide to construct a large industrial city". If you're desperate for another city builder, fill your boots. If you're looking for something vastly different to what has gone before, well, you might be disappointed. I never like giving "No" recommendations, and it could be argued that I'm being rather harsh. If there was a "maybe" button, then maybe I would give it a maybe. But personally, I can't see myself revisiting this game that often.\t\t\t\t\t

Good is, that this game is still actively developed. Other Early Access Games are dead.
