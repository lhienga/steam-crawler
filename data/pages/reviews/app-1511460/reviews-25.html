\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know it's early access but unless workshop adds more depth, there just isn't anything intriguing or exciting about this game.

First off, its definitely a unique smash up as other's have said, Cities: Skylines meets factory/city builder.

So this game is about logistics, vehicles and roads which gets you hooked however, there just isn't enough variables in the logistics part to make it interesting. Your logistics is about as good as your creativity with roads and layout. This game really needs layers to the Logistic part to make it interesting, think freight centers, resource depots that disperse to a certain area, airports, variation of vehicles.... you could go on with ideas which makes this game feel hollow and so much was missed.

They have their upcoming future releases noted and it appears that will be it to finish the game to 1.0, workshop isn't even given yet.

Lastly, when I said this game is about logistics, that's it. There's no fun in building, you have the resources, click on a new building to build and it instantly appears. There's no distractions of any kind while you build this logistic planet. I can't even suggest sale at the moment, economy/city builders is all I love to play but I put this game down after a few hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thought I'd give this game a shot despite the plausible bad reviews but I am a sucker for factory games and I am waiting for DSP to leave early access. The reviews were largely right. It is not bad per se and it's clear that it is work in progress but it's just one more game in the supply chain genre and brings nothing new to the table. The cities skylines "inspiration" on the building mechanics is a bit too obvious, IMO. There's nothing wrong in principle since they work but there's not much there otherwise. The supply chain stuff is not well fleshed out. There also seems to be nothing gripping or motivating about the progression. Finally, I get trying to use some of the traffic managing principles of cities skylines but ultimately it doesn't seem like roads would be a big part of a planetary colony. Loads of empty space, smooth terrain, no vegetation, seems like ATVs would be the way to transport things. That or flying drones if there's an atmosphere. I mean there might be a way to make the roads seem essential not just for the game mechanic but it is not there yet.

Early access and all that so this could end up being great but it is definitely not worth 25 bucks at this point.\t\t\t\t\t
