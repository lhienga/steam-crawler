\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've only just began getting deep into this game but I can already tell you its a solid buy. Its a transportation / automation game with a city building supply / demand addition. My preference would be an automated citizen growth factor where homes pop up by themselves, and the roads into them are built automatically (similar to Transport Fever), as the city building side of this game isn't the focus. There certainly could be some minor tweaks, and I noticed the Dev's have been putting those in as quickly as they can.

My single complaint about this game is the resource collection and distribution centers. Simply put, I don't like them at all. They feel very one dimensional and very limited on their use. I've seen some videos on great use of them, but they just don't seem practical in the current way they're implemented. It really should just be a singular building.

For a team of 6 Developer's they move their updates pretty quickly and have made a great game with tons of potential.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like games of this type and they are usually very addictive for me. In this case it was different. Although I have "played" 58 hours, a large part of that time is leaving the game in the background. I just wanted to see what was at the end.
What I don't like:
- Traffic has very poor AI and is unnatural. For example, cars enter the extreme left lane on a three-lane road and follow it until they reach an intersection where they want to turn right. Just before the intersection they stop trying to change lane to the right, creating traffic jams. It was possibe to take right line and go straight all the time or change line earlier.
- Poor balance of resources and production
- A moment of inattention, a traffic jam caused by poor AI causes problems with deliveries and suddenly the buildings level degrades, eliminating a large part of the inhabitants. Then you have to lose a lot of time to restore all back.
- Poorly developed part of production statistics
- Lack of clear instructions and description of game mechanics
What is good:
- Possibility of laying structures behind a straight, diagonal or with no mesh
- possibility of creating multi-level roads, although it is a pity that it is not possible to build parallel roads one above the other.
- big space for city development
In general, the game completely failed to use its potential and devs did not learn anything from the lessons learned by Cities Skylines. The interesting idea of compiling factories, a citybuilder and a traffic simulator was implemented so ineptly that the game is not any of them.\t\t\t\t\t
