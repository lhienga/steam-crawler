\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLooks good on the surface; does not hold up to sustained scrutiny.

Progress is blocked by buildings that don't do what they say they do, but are required by other buildings to progress.
Traffic is ridiculous, with the in-game components not acting as the game's settings dictate.
Developers don't seem to be actively developing; updates are practically non-existent despite numerous bugs and outright broken things in-game, and there doesn't appear to be much (if any) developer interaction on the forums.
Not sure this should actually have been released to EA yet; as I understand it, Steam's rules suggest the product is actually a playable game at release, and this... is not.

Might be worth an afternoon of goofing around, but wait for a deep discount (ie, 80% or more) before throwing your money away on this title.\t\t\t\t\t

However, when compared to Factorio, a veteran in the genre, it doesn't quite measure up. It's worth noting that Factorio has been around longer, which has allowed its developers to refine and enhance the game over time. In contrast, Infraspace still has room for growth and development.

It falls short in comparison to Factorio, a factory and infrastructure simulation game. While it's true that Factorio has been around longer, the developers have had time to learn and improve upon the game's design. In comparison, Infraspace suffers from clunky and difficult-to-use UI.

While the game's graphics are decent, the gameplay itself is not as engaging. The lack of innovation and poor UI make it difficult to recommend Infraspace over other similar games in the market.
