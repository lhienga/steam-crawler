\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is too early in development for me to really recommend it at the moment, but that does'nt justify a negative review for an early access title. I'd hold off for a few months/years for more features to be added. The cadence of updates has been pretty solid so far and there seems to be a weekly devblog like Factorio used to have. I always appreciate developer transparency and listening to community.

I did have a game-breaking bug, which was fixed literally 3 days after the broken patch came out and two of those days were weekend, so that's definitely good news for the future of the game and one of the main reasons for my positive review.

The gameplay is interesting and definitely fun since I've spent 40 hours in it already, but at the moment it's nothing super revolutionary for somebody like me, who has played Factorio, Satisfactory, DSP, FactoryTown, Timberborn, Kubifaktorium and a bunch of other more or less similar games. Infraspace brings in a little bit of Cities:Skylines into the mix, but I've played a lot of that too, so again, the whole game is a bit derivative, but I like Cities, so that part is definitely a plus.

I had to turn off the traffic collisions, because the vehicles were just too dumb and would gridlock everything and I really didn't feel like messing around with one-way streets. Maybe I'll do that in my next playthrough if some interesting updates come out down the line.

I do like the whole terraforming idea, but I feel like there are some balancing issues with it. I'm now in late game with all research unlocked and now I'd just have to keep my game running in the background for the planet to get automatically terraformed, while I'd be checking the Adamantium Drill and Magnetic Field Generator buildings for their requirements to see if I need to boost parts of my production line to satisfy those constructions' needs. I also have some unfinished ship upgrades, which seem completely pointless now in late game, when I have 20k+ citizens and the upgrades can add hundreds to my colony, but I probably should have paid more attention to my ship earlier in the game, but the game didn't really bring too much attention to it, which brings me to the main problem it has in its current state. The vagueness off all information it's trying to convey.

There really needs to be a better tooltip/tutorial system. The tutorial ends pretty quickly and a lot of things remain completely unexplained and you just have to build them and see what exactly they do, which makes prioritizing your progress frustrating. All buildings should have proper explanation of what exactly they do. Sometimes you can find some explanations in the research section and you can also see what the building upgrades do after they become active (so after you've already built the building or a whole production line required for them), but there are a lot of buildings that are completely unexplained, so you become dependent on a user-powered wiki, which at the moment is severely outdated, because a new patch just came out. At least the basic stuff should be in the tooltips of all buildings, even if it's just one simple sentence explaining what the building does. There are 6 buildings in the terraforming section that I had absolutely no idea what they do, when I unlocked them. I did end up building all of them and I see the sand in their radius becoming green and some trees are popping up, but is that it? Just cosmetic stuff? Does it increase the colony rating to have more immigrants? Does it improve the production in its surroundings like some of the buildings from the miscellaneous section do? Does it make your citizens happier? I have no idea. The game doesn't tell me. The last buildings in Mining and Terraforming sections don't say anything at all and take forever to get built and I assume they also do nothing or very little. Is it the victory condition? Is there one at all? Or is it just something unfinished that the next patch will explore? What about the Coil Factory? Is there any purpose for those coils? I don't know. The game just doesn't tell me much.

The game also suffers from the typical performance creep that many of these city builders bump into late game and after about 40 hours of gameplay, which is about the time when I've unlocked all research, I'm struggling to have more than 15FPS on double speed, a bit over 25FPS on single speed. That's with a 12-core 24-thread CPU (R93900X) and an RTX3080, although GPU is probably not the issue, because that's at about 20-30% usage, when the CPU is at around 60%.

Overall a promising game, but there's a lot of work to be done to make it great, which it totally has potential to become in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
