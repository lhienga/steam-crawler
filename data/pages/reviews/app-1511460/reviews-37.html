\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBasically Cities skyline + DSP.

C:S, but more focus on the industry DLC aspect. There's traffic management, but not as detailed. Like there's lane mathematics, but with no Traffic manager mod.
DSP, with it's different color-coded research and resource production ratios.

Overall I'd say it's a good mix of city building and resource management.

Some aspects that stand out from other games of similar genres:
- One of the better Mars colony/Space sci-fi city building games tbh as compared to some others which I shall not name.
- No spaghetti belts!
- easy and free readjusting of assets.
- I'll add more to this list as I play on.

It's still early access, so definitely there's a few small areas that lacks and I really hope they get implemented in the future.
- Readjusting power lines is a hassle, the length doesn't seem to be congruent when moving it around.
- No map when starting, so need a bit more work to plan ahead early on, although it's very forgiving when adjusting assets.
- lack of options when placing down roads,i.e no angle snapping sometimes and no managing of how the curves are. I don't even know if the roads are perpendicular. It's a little frustrating if you care about aesthetics.
- Vehicles spawn out of thin air. They don't return to original building. A little off if you care about realism. That does help with minimizing traffic tho.
- No grid. So not easy to plan.
- Storage doesn't show the amount of resources stored.
- Can't manipulate height of roads. No underground.
- Most buildings can't upgrade, so when you reach late game, it's going to be huge mess.

Similar games:
C:S
Banished
DSP
Satisfactory
Factorio

Overall, this is pretty solid for an Early Access game. There's huge potential in this game as time goes on and more features are added.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt this time, I DO recommend this game, but, there are still lingering issues that I've found. See bottom of post for my "rant".

[EDIT-Seems as though the #48 news corrected the issues I was experiencing. Everything else looks to be "my" problem with the routing and design of the roads]

Things I like:
- I like having a system that I have to continuously replenish, such as the residential zones.
- I like how in order to grow the residential, you need different components to create more dense areas
- I like that you can upgrade the mining areas.
- I like the infinite resources.
- I like how the traffic is actually managed (Redirect traffic into different lanes)
- I like that your city has to be at a certain size before you can do particular research on techs, and if your city gets smaller than the required size for that tech study, that tech study STOPs, and you have to manually continue on that research once you reach that required city size again.
- I like how every vehicle has a start and stop point and has to deal with traffic wherever its at.
- I like how the game itself "talks" to the forum so you can see recent titles of posts by the community without having to go to the site to see updates.

This I don't like:
- I don't like the seemingly random stop-and-go traffic at non-intersection points (Bug), as mentioned below.
- I'm not seeing a lot of actual feedback from the Devs on comments left by the community in the forum, but I see a lot of decent and active suggestions posted by the community.

Rant:
I LIKE the game, but, traffic flow (not management) is a significant problem. Between reloads, I find that traffic gets "stuck" and stops at a straight through roads. This isn't at an intersection (I get why stop and go traffic would happen), there are merging traffic lanes. I find that at a straight or slight bends in the road, at random points, traffic stops for a moment and continues on, which backs things up. EVERY time I reload my game at the end of my (literal) work day, I end up spending a couple of hours trying to coax traffic to start flowing again. I find major traffic jams, I find that my 3-lane highway is bogged down and has come to a COMPLETE stop from one side of the map to the other, I find vehicles stopping and going, and so on. This causes problems where basic essentials aren't getting to my population centres, which causes people to migrate out, which brings down production. I'll go from a population of 3500 down to 1000 (Or under) and have to re-prioritise EVERY industry every time I load into the game

For this reason, I find it frustrating to play, so I don't. I STILL do recommend the game as I'm sure this will get fixed at some point.\t\t\t\t\t
