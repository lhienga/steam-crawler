\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is enjoyable and well worth the $30+ asking rpice. I was dubious at first because the first thing you see is a very uninteresting and unappealing desert landscape. However, the imagination that has gone into the design of the buildings and other objects more than make up for this. The game is well-balanced EXCEPT that it takes far too long to send materiels up to repair the orbiting spacecraft. More tutorial would have helped as well, especially in explaining why highway upgrades removes road access for buildings. Nevertheless, the animations are great. A tremendous amount of effort went into the design and planning of how buildings are to be upgraded and especially the "adjacency" bonus system for placing buildings next to other complementary buildings and resources. The one-way roads also introduced a very interesting and fun dynamic. The addition of trains and cargo gondolas took some figuring out but they also turned out to be thoughtful additions. If you like city building without dealing with all kinds of enemies that want to see you fail then this game is for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had this game for about two years and have put in a bit over 30 hours of game play. I almost enjoy playing. Almost but not quite. Why is that? After the last major update the old maps went away and it now has a single rather bland and boring map. The Devs plan to add some element of terraforming to spice up the map but that makes no sense. The object of the game is to get off the planet not terraform it. Gameplay is extremely grindy. Getting the final stage of upgrades in order to complete the stages required to leave the planet takes the grindy portion to a further extreme. But grindy games might be another player's forte - it's just not mine. Some of the game mechanics, like laying and connecting water, air and



methane pipes, can be clitchy at times. A lot of the game play mechanics are not intuitive. They require hunting around the menus and/or online assistance. Which takes us to the final review:

Those are annoyances but not the bottom line. The bottom line is the game doesn't appear to be popular. It is the sort of game that needs an active player base that includes online content providers for tutorials, tips and game play. That aspect of the game is almost completely absent. The Steam discussion forum is mostly a ghost town and there are about a dozen videos on YouTube. A few major YouTubers like Katherine of Sky have done a single short play thru and have never gone back to the game. For me that's telling. Coupling that with my personal experience I can't recommend the game at this time.\t\t\t\t\t
