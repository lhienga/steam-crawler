\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, first, the Good bits. As well as the obligatory "It shows promise", I really like the traffic tools. Even at the very start of the game, you can manage intersections, setting them to be a free-for-all, patrolled by a traffic light, or set a priority with yield signs. You can even dictate, to some degree, which lane turns into which, a feature most useful once you get four lane roads. Any traffic jams I had, I felt was my own error, and not the game's engine being screwy. If you had issues with City Skyline's traffic, this game is everything you wanted. There also appears to be a meta component in repairing your ship. While I do not know this for sure, I would assume repairs to the ship carry over saves, as you can see "Zone 1" and "Zone 2" if you have multiple save files.

Now for the bad. Alongside the obligatory "It's early access, and isn't done yet", there are some things that are missing. For starters, the Cheap and Normal roads don't say they offer a speed boost when you hover over them in the construction menu. It DOES say that in the encyclopedia, and you DO notice the difference at an intersection between the two, but all the tool-tip says is an increased cost. Also, the aforementioned encyclopedia is missing some topics. I wanted to look up some finer points of research, but there isn't even a tab for how that works. I notice that all of my science goes only to one of my two Research Labs. Does that mean I only need one? Is there a cap of how many I can have? The tool-tip over the lab just reads "Resources in storage: Green Science Pack: 0." Even with a steady supply of green science visibly flowing in. A similar issue occurs with the Construction Material Storage, but even stranger, it DOES show me how many of the resources are in the building, but does NOT list a cap. If I did not turn off Concrete production (via setting the priority to zero), I suspect it would hoard a theoretically infinite supply of concrete forever.

In short, I recommend the devs look into these issues, and players check out this game after their polish. If you need a game like this right now, I recommend checking out Factory Town, or just the good ol' Factoiro.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExtremely buggy, unintuitive interface.

Currently trying to build a train loop. One of the stations is red. No explanation why, track is hooked up, all directions are correct (note: there's a button for "reversing track direction" that does nothing). The fact I can't even slowly trace the tracks to find the problem spot is shocking.

Up to this point, the resource system looked underwhelming. There's power and minerals and processors and workforce... but it's all so bland and easy. Once I discovered I can get 40% production speed bonuses from just arranging my food and water production correctly all possible challenge of managing workforce/keeping peeps happy went out the window.

Soundtrack and art style look good from a distance. Models are very optimized and don't stand up to close scrutiny, which is probably why the game has camera blurs that can't be turned off. The music is fine, but un-immersive and I'd rather put on my own music to zone out.

Philosophically... eh, yeah, why not, philosophically I don't like this game's core concept. The future of infrastructure shouldn't be some bleak stroad and highway monstrosity of automatic cars whipping through intersections at 100 mph. Energy, People, and Resources are not things that you just build more and more of whenever you want them. Trains are not conveyor belts for delivering from trucks to trucks.

I guess I'm just upset that someone thought that the most important skill in civil engineering was how to build more lanes and good highway ramps. But in a pure gameplay sense I got a little over an hour in and I don't think there's any more gameplay to be had. It's not a good factory game, it's not a good city builder, and it's not a good survival game. So what is it?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal update: The initial 1.0 launch had numerous issues but the devs worked hard to put out multiple patches and have brought the game to a point where I am happy to recommend it.

The game plays like a simpler version of Anno where you also need to manage traffic flows - set up extraction and factories and deliver the goods to houses.

The pacing feels a bit off due to lots of waiting in the early game while you research all the fundamental transportation and resource/factory technologies. If you are a seasoned Anno or Factorio player you might also find the game very easy as there are no upkeep costs. This leads a lack of a long term strategic challenge as you can always just put down more mines or more energy plants etc to solve whatever issue you're facing (as long as you have the work force for it).

Regardless, this is still a fun game if you want something slightly easier and enjoy this genre.

For posterity, I have left my original review before I changed my rating to positive below. It had 129 helpfuls, 3 funnies and 6 awards as of 2023-11-19.



Update: The devs responded and I talked to them. It seems like the game is moving in the right direction with multiple patches released but I still believe in its current state this game is a do not recommend. Come back after a few more patches and things may very well be different. I have added updates in italics in the body of my review to address what has changed. Current as of Patch 1.10.396.

Infraspace feels more like a 0.9 than a 1.0. I haven't played the game or followed its early access development prior to full release. I have multiple hundreds of hours in Factorio, Satisfactory, Cities Skylines, and the Anno series which is where I am coming from.

The basic premise of the game is solid - a mix of Anno and Cities Skylines where you need to manage your traffic while getting the right products to the right factories. Unfortunately, there is a pretty big lack of polish.

Things I'd like to see added or changed:
  • The ability to restrict certain houses from receiving upgrade goods. I am producing enough to support only a certain amount of upgraded houses but production seems to get delivered evenly across all houses so now I have a bunch of houses all getting half the amount of resources they need to upgrade.
    The devs mentioned ways around this but the current implementations are rather clunky. You can set export bans but not import bans. You essentially need to give orders to all your factories to not export to certain houses rather than just telling a specific house to not import. They mentioned they would consider adding import bans in the future.

  • There is no clear information on how delivery trucks work. A build will produce resources and you can see which other buildings will accept this resource but you have no idea how many trucks are currently out delivering and how many trucks a building supports.
    After talking to the devs, I better understand this system. Imo, a few additional tooltips in game would easily clarify how things work.

  • Trains suck. There is no other way of phrasing it. They are nonsensical in their pathing, you have no control over their length or signaling or what items they load/unload. Completely unacceptable for a game that is based around the transportation of goods.
    Pathfinding was fixed in a patch. Controlling the items is not an easy task but you can consider it a part of the challenge of this game.

  • Eyedropper tool, a button to quickly construct whatever your mouse is currently on.
    Tool was added in a patch.

  • More graphics options than just low/medium/high. There are plenty of odd graphical glitches that I'm encountering while playing on high. Poor LOD, shadows popping in, weird ghosting left behind moving objects that you normally see if you have DLSS on (which this game does not have)
    Proper graphics options were added in a patch. I still am getting some odd graphical issues, the biggest one being obvious shadow pop in/out as you zoom and mild ghosting behind moving objects. However, I don't consider this anything more than mildly annoying.

  • Camera movement with mouse (ie right click and hold to move camera)
    Camera mouse pan was added in a patch.

  • Pipes are incredibly clunky to work with. There are multiple types of pipes and they can overlap unless its a corner or an end piece which makes no sense.
    Pipes were fixed in a patch.

  • Better tool tips, some items like roads do not tell you all the information you need to know before building
    Tool tips for every building (function, size etc) have been added in a patch.

The pacing also feels a bit off, the early game feels really empty and slow. I can see how things will get more interesting as you go up the tiers but early on I feel like I am just waiting for long periods of time for my research to produce so that I can unlock all the basic infrastructure tech that will allow me to properly build out my city. The game also seems to be quite easy if you're coming from something like Anno or Factorio. There are no monetary upkeep costs so as long as you can keep your workforce supplied, any supply problems can be solved by just adding more factories - there are no significant efficiency or strategic concerns to worry about.

Luckily none of these things are fundamental issues with the game. A patch or two with the right changes will make this an easy recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
