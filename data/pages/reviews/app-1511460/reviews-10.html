\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very curious game, it's as if city skylines and per aspera had an child with cute graphics. It has taken me about 40 hours to beat the game on medium difficulty, (as in repair the ship) and only needed about 5k population to do it. By this time I found myself very disinterested in the late game(I did the most minimum amount of terraforming). All in all, this is a game I can maybe see myself getting a hundred hours out of over time, but it won't become a new addiction like city skylines or per aspera. Before I get into why, I will say that this game is worth playing and is a lot of fun in parts, mostly in the mid game. Perhaps I will change this review after I come back on a hard play-through on a new map. If you are someone reading wondering if you should buy this game, stop reading here! What follows is mostly criticism that I hope to see addressed by the devs or the mod community in the future.
My single greatest problem with the game is how research functions. The supply chain for tech is difficult, I'd prefer a more simple system so that my research was more gradual. The token system is more difficult to explain in my headcannon as well. Maybe the colonists should have more to do with research and less with factory workers. It just makes more sense story wise. I was often tabing out and just letting the game run to get research done particularly towards the end. And that leads to my second problem. Buildings that are right next to each other should not demand trucking. The system for pipping is already in the game, if buildings are right next to each other and producing something the other requires there should be a conveyor system. Get rid of the efficiency buff which only creates more traffic in smart layouts and replace it with buildings in a set range get conveyors. I did not feel like building up past 5k population because the supply chain got so convoluted that adding a single colony would require 10 minutes of redesigning the supporting structure. Alot of this could be solved by reducing the traffic buildings in proximity generate. I often would have enough production but it would get trapped in local traffic and not get into the highways leading to the main colony. That leads into my final criticism which is vehicle behavior. The construction material storage is always prioritized over buildings that require steel this meant I need to build it incredibly far away so that my trucks would at least on occasion head to the factories first. This resulted in me having a large amount of unnecessary traffic in my factory areas, and is not something I could address. Please either make the vehicles prioritize factories and building projects first over the storage, or stop making steel a requirement and only make it a building material. The same is true for concrete but it was just less egregious.
In terms of issue that are my personal preference, this map is worse than the old one. The road system does not work very well with mountains and thus exposes all the issues of having to press r 40 times and the twichyness of the system. I would prefer more general elevation changes, with a few mountains rather then straight up cliffs. All in all though, still a very polished product with few bugs, loading times were reasonable even in the late game. Still recommend and look forward to future updates.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat I encountered in my 7 hrs of gameplay.

Too broken to be playable.
Logistics are not working!!!!

When everyone moves out, you'e left with your initial 20 population and it takes time to get more people to move in. I had to restart 3 times due to game breaking bugs. The account below is just from my 3rd attempted play thru.

Magically my water supply disappears (2k storage) and everybody moves out.
Magically my oxygen supply disappears (all 2k of it in storage) and everybody moves out.
Then the food stops being delivered and everybody moves out.
Then some habs don't recognize the park right next to them and and won't upgrade and some habs just don't get any food delivered and also can't upgrade and people move out.
After I rebuild everything. My Spaceship factory stops getting deliveries. It needs concrete and steel. I have massive amounts of both and a storage facility right next to the factory. After waiting for an hour, nothing gets delivered.
Then magically all my oxygen disappears again and everybody moves out.

At which point I get frustrated with this and quit.

Some buildings are hard to find as they aren't in expected menus. Like the methane drill, why is it not in the same menu as all the other drills? This just doesn't make any sense.
Pipes are a mess, hard to tell if you actually are connected to a building or not. The game won't let you cross same type pipes at different elevations, but often times won't let you connect them either. I struggled a lot with this.

Buildings only connect on certain side so are often not connected to a road.
The large storage just refuses to connect to any road unless you place it and then run a road up it.

Game is all about logistics that simply don't work.
Supposedly these types of issues were fixed in the last patch. Obviously not.
Maybe in a few patches they'll figure out their own game.
Can't say if it's a good game otherwise, was too frustrated with current game play.
I'd refund it if I could and let it sit another year on my wishlist.\t\t\t\t\t
