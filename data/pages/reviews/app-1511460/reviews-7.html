\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first started playing InfraSpace I had no clue what I was doing. There wasn’t an in game encyclopedia, there were no building descriptions, we had less features, we had one map, we didn’t have an intro video (at least I don’t remember it if we did lol) and it was clunky (actually clunky and not the new users 1.0 clunky, lol).

The fact that I downloaded this game in the first place was a miracle within itself. I randomly stumbled on to a YouTube video from Real Civil Engineer. He was building this absolutely insane highway system on top of a mountain. As he was explaining the game and how he’s building his highway, I started thinking of the old Sim City days and how much fun those games were. So after a few more videos I ended up getting InfraSpace.

230 Hours later and I’m absolutely loving the game. I’ve started playing long before the 1.0 update. So dealt with bugs, seen a LOT of amazing updates, built a lot of cities, and have read every single Friday update the team has put out. InfraSpace isn’t a game you can just pick up and be good at it within a few minutes. It takes time to learn the build recipes, people management (production menu), traffic flow, and so much more. Once you get to higher into the tech tree, that’s when the real fun begins. To be honest, I haven’t made it to Terraforming just yet because I tend to screw up my traffic flow but I’m getting close. I love the Colossal Adamantine Drill. I won’t spoil anything but when you get to it and you start using it, you know you’ve made it a LONG way. Honestly probably one of the best feelings in the world to make it to that level.

To those who are skeptical or new to the game, give it a shot and keep playing. Don’t be afraid to restart, play with the cheats on, or watch videos on YouTube. The devs have done so much for this game in a small amount of time. I can’t imagine where this game is going to be in a few years.

TLDR: 5/5 AMAZING GAME – 230 HOURS CURRENTLY\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCool concept, poor execution. One of my favorite parts of Cities Skylines was the traffic management. So when I saw a factory-like game where logistics happen primarily through roads, I was quite interested. For the first 10 hours or so I had a lot of fun scaling things up, designing better (or at least bigger) road networks, ensuring I was at ~100% traffic flow, and watching the cars go zoom. But before too long it just got repetitive and boring. It was definitely being dragged down by increasingly painful bugs and missing QoL features. Building anything is tedious and an exercise in frustration, fighting the jank that permeates this game. Optimizing logistics chains is somehow both uninteresting and frustrating. All the while, the space of interesting problems to solve rapidly dries up, leaving you to wonder what reason there is to continue scaling up.

The devs seem engaged and like they're trying their best, so I feel bad leaving a negative review - but man, this game currently just doesn't deliver. Maybe it'll improve... but it's got a long way to go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tClearly not a finish product!
So many bugs to fix and features to add to make this game really enjoyable.
Be clear, it took me 15 hours to reach a state where I don't want to play on this game.

1 - The map generator is crappy, and the only proposed map has some advantages and some inconvenient. It's your best choice to have fun.
2 - Press the fast forward button as soon as you can, but be careful, the game has a bug that loves to put it back to slow motion.
3 - Do never build 3 lane roads, yes you can control the flow, but most of the cars will go in the fast lane without considering any other options. I've seen delivery cars doing half of the map to deliver something next door!
4 - you don't have colour codes to identify electric network, having multiple network happens, and you can search few times.
5 - BUG Nuclear power station will take few second each time it reach 100% to reset, be prepared to see multiple messages that you lack electricity (it feels like a third country electricity)
6 - Terraforming, NO I don't want to terraform the whole planet, DEVS please put an ending when you build the shield.
7 - The shield always tells you that it's not connected.
8 - Build your food apart of the rest of the network, and build a lot, I mean a freaking lot of house electronics. It's clear that it's a bug. The citizen eats less than the number of house electronic!
9- All citizen looks unhappy of their jobs
10- One music, yes, only one music, if you remove it, there's nothing to hear.
11- Useless items: Gondolas, Display, Pixels, those are nice features to make the game looks nice, but who would continue and rebuild their city to make it look neat?

It's not a bad game, but for the price, what I got, I'm not impressed.
For the DEVS: good job on fixing some annoying bugs and listening to the community.\t\t\t\t\t
