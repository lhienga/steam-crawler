\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPeople are saying they are having issues with trains not transporting raw materials. I currently have 2 train stations and they are transporting both parts, goods and raw materials just fine, I have the train stations and factories inside zones with the relevant materials being told to go inbetween the zones. It does seem raw materials do not need a zone as I didnt tell it to move iron ore and sand but it does it anyway.

This game does feel like it needs a little more in terms of challenge, at the moment it is quite relaxing to just slowly build up a city with fulfilling the citizens growing needs for communism, but theres nothing here that I can see atm which would make you feel like your city could either be destroyed either by self destructing from lacking resources or outside sources.

Roads feel nice, would like to see intersections with more than 4 connections.

Trains can be a little weird at the beginning but once you play around with it a little its not bad.

Carbon is a pain in the ass until mid to late game once you get the carbon processor, I feel it would be better if other buildings had carbon as a byproduct.

The game feels very City Skylines without money, would like to see more of the following:
Economy.
Outside and internal threats.
Environmental threats like natural disasters (meteor showers ect).
Interplanet/ long range inter settlement transportation.
Areas with different resources.
Teraforming.
A tool tip on buildings showing production per min including the % increase from surrounding buildings.
I could probably think of a few more just not atm (any change to review will be about potential things to be added).



If you want a somewhat relaxing colony building sim with constantly increasing needs from your seemingly communist colonists I would get the game, although depending on your familiarity with both city builders and factory like games your mileage may vary.\t\t\t\t\t

I enjoy Dyson Sphere Program, and have played many different Sim City versions and clones, and this game is like a simplified version of those two things combined, which is fun... for a bit. I'll play it again, but I'm waiting for it to have more features and be more engaging, and for the logic of where goods go to be a bit better. Why put all my steel in storage when multiple factories need it?

It started off fun enough (though really slow at times), and I was really looking forward to using trains to transport goods, so I could separate production areas from each other, but working with trains is absolutely maddening. After over an hour of messing with it, I still can't get anything to go to the train station, and I've seen lots of videos and posts of people having similar issues. Update: After leaving this review I went back into the game and noticed that a very small piece of one-way road was going the wrong way, but there was no error or warning about traffic being blocked. In other places that I've had one-ways going the wrong way, there's a red icon, but it didn't do that near the train station for some reason.

The game is basically just a simplified version of Dyson Sphere Program with roads and houses.

I'll keep an eye on this game to see where it goes. The trains system is only a couple months old, so hopefully they'll do something to get it working, or at the very least add some in-game help that tells you what magic combination of what is needed to get them to work. There are other issues, too. Some graphics bugs that make cars seem to move on the road when you move the camera, and roads sometimes going underground. I also couldn't place some buildings on a corner because they flicker and rotate very quickly, causing me to have to move them one square away from the corner. And placing power poles is odd because if you click and drag when placing them, you can connect multiple, but I tried adding a new one that would connect to an existing one, and it wouldn't. There is also an issue with getting steel to the motor factories. They often sit there with an icon saying they need steel while the amount of steel in storage continues to increase. You can only use materials in storage for construction, and it seems that steel only gets delivered to motor factories after they run out of it, so that causes a lot of issues for anything that needs motors.

I've also been running into performance issues as my city grows. My computer isn't the most powerful, but it far exceeds the minimum requirements, and is able to handle more advanced games with no issues, but in this game, when I upgrade a piece of road, the frame rate basically drops to 0 for a second. There is also a small bit of cursor lag when auto save happens, which has caused me to delete a high level habitat instead of the thing next to it.\t\t\t\t\t
