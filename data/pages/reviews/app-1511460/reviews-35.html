I love city builders but I like for them to be based in current times. I chewed on getting this game for weeks and honestly after I bought it I knew it would probably be a game I would refund after an hour of game play "no harm no foul right?'. I was not sure I could get behind a futuristic city builder. But here we are I have officially played for one hour and to be completely honest I did not think I would even like this game but after just one hour of game play I have fallen head over heals for Infraspace! I have seen no bugs or lag something one would expect with an EA game. I love the detail and the animation put into each individual building. This game is EA but it already has a plethora of in game content which makes game play very enjoyable. The only negative thing I can say about this game is that I am mad at my self for not buying it sooner!
Now don't get me wrong this is no CS in fact I can't think of any other game to compare it to because truthfully Infraspace is in a class of it's own. You will not be disappointed in this game I can promise you that. It scratches both my city building and sci-fi itch at the same time. I love the challenge that goes along with making your city and the supply chain and transportation aspect of the game are so detailed and give you so much freedom to make your city as efficient as possible. Another plus for the game is the poll that is built into the game where us the players can vote on what we would like to see in future updates. They have a detailed road map for the future and the fact that the devs want to implement what we would like to see in game is a MAJOR plus. Some of the upcoming features are going to be awesome and make game play even more fun. So to sum it up buy this game don't wait like I did. It is worth every cent. The devs are very receptive to input and very good about responding to our questions or comments. The updates are regular "something many EA games lack". I am excited for the direction this game is going it has no where to go but up! I honestly did not think I would find a city builder that I would like just as much as City Skylines bloated as that game may be it is the new standard in City builder games. But I absolutely love it ! Keep up the good work devs you have a true gem right here. Now I gotta get back to my game but do your self a big favor and click that add to cart button you will be surprised what an amazing game infraspce is I know I was!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't recommend it and I don't think it is going to get better. The core design is just weird. It is not really a citybuilder in any way, it is logistic chain game with conveyor belt replaced with road and resource sinks that look just like residential buildings[0]. It plays (and looks) similar to "Per Aspera": wait for more research/resources and plop even more same buildings. Nothing in tech tree is "game changing". I finished researching all tech in 12h on "hard" difficulty.
While playing I would recommend splitting whole factory into independent parts (only share power). Placing each building spawns a wave of cars that will deliver input resources to it. Cars waiting to enter it will lock nearby intersections for a minute or two, so you wan't to reduce scale of that issue. I guess districts were supposed to allow logical partition but have weird default(if no rules export everything) and are clunky to use.
The game provides only cycle time for each factory, while residential buildings show only consumption in items/minute. What makes matching ratios even harder are efficiency bonuses for some nearby buildings. Just not worth the effort, even more when fluctuating count for workers, just add more buildings if it looks like they are required.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was sorta worried that this game would have the same problems that cities: skylines had, which was it devolves into a traffic simulator. My concerns turned out to be valid. In the mid and late game this game really just turns into a traffic simulator.

I really love city building games. But, the AI for traffic in these games are just so bad. Having your city come to a grinding halt because production kicks into gear after getting new tech and just coming to grid lock isn't interesting at all. I don't think there's anything interesting at all about a traffic simulator masked as a city builder.

There should be an option to just have the traffic virtualized, or some other solution for traffic aside from having to micro manage and plan for heavy traffic after you get new tech and all of the factories start up again because there is finally some demand. I'm not a game designer, I don't know what would solve this problem. But, as is, I give it a thumbs down.

Can't recommend this game, unless you really enjoy traffic simulators.\t\t\t\t\t
