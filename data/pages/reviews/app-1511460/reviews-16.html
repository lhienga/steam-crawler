\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game. I really do. It has a great premise and a nice tech tree to explore. There's a certain amount of flogging that's expected in factory games, but the lack of tools to run transportation inevitably leads to a point where you have to rip out a lot of what you've done to rework it so you can progress. Even so, I got to a point where nothing was happening because all the traffic was just sitting there. Can't run roads or highways across train lines or other highway lines. Half the resources you need must be retrieved from plateaus or trenches via means of gondolas that are priced at 400 concrete and 500 steel. Except that when you go to build it, it turns out that's the price for one end and doesn't include, apparently, the cables and other stuff needed to build it. Also, there's just not enough information to play the game properly. The graphs aren't standard numbers but appear to be evenly spaced divisions on the graph. Nice for display, but not real helpful when trying to compare Jobs to Population. Finally, some QOL improvements are badly needed. At the very least, an eyedropper is critical in this game: in 26.4 hours of play, I've had to build over 60 electronics factories, and every time I needed to build another one, I had to feed through the menus to find it.

As I said, I really want to like this game and hope that the devs address some of the issues. I realize that it's still Early Access, but there seems to be more depth here at the cost of ease of play. Hopefully, I can revisit this review in the future with better comments!\t\t\t\t\t
