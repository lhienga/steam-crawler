\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI waited until this game was on sale and bought it, glad I did! I enjoy games with resource management and logic problem solving, but sometimes I want to just relax doing it. This game does that. It takes the tedious crafting of Factorio and simplifies it giving you almost a "chill factorio". Simplified intersections to change with a few clicks, automatic traffic, simplified resources and crafting. I run this game on my laptop at the lowest settings to make it doable, but I still enjoy it. Watching other game reviews I can see how a better computer would make the experience even better, since at the lowest settings, nothing moves except the traffic. Turbines don't spin, mines don't move, etc, but those are just extra bits that would improve the alive feel of my colony.

Traffic mechanics can be wonky at times, If I place a long 2 lane road, cars will drive all the way to the far end to perform a u turn to come back to a building at the beginning instead of just turning in when they pass... meaning it takes FOREVER for some resources to actually make it to the destination on time. An obvious solution for this would be to make shorter roads, but I like to plan for expansion right off the bat.

It also needs an upgrade long road option, When you unlock better roads, it gives you a replace road option, except you have to click on every single road section you want to replace. This is fine for smaller areas, but when you have a long motorway running almost the length of the map... it gets to be a LOT of clicking, especially when you replace a one way, and also have to reverse traffic direction,

Overall, love the game. I sat down the first time to play and I got up 3 hours later. Very easy to grasp concepts and figure out the workings and keybindings.\t\t\t\t\t

Ingredients:
1. 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
2. 1/2 cup white sugar
3. 1/2 cup brown sugar
4. 1/4 cup water
5. 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
6. 1 pinch salt
7. 5 apples - peeled, cored and sliced
8. 1 pastry for double-crust pie

Directions:
1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).
2. Combine butter, white sugar, brown sugar, water, cinnamon, and salt in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil, remove from heat and set aside.
3. Roll out half the pastry to fit a 9-inch pie plate. Place bottom crust in pie plate; pour in apple slices.
4. Roll out top crust into a 10-inch circle. Cut into 8 (1-inch) wide strips with a sharp paring knife or pastry wheel. Weave the pastry strips, one at a time, into a lattice pattern. Fold the ends of the lattice strips under the edge of the bottom crust and crimp to seal.
5. Pour butter-sugar mixture over top of pie, coating the lattice, and allowing any remaining sauce to drizzle through the crust.
6. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes. Reduce heat to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C), and bake until the crust is golden brown, the caramel on the top crust is set, and the apple filling is bubbling, 35 to 40 more minutes. Allow to cool completely before slicing.\t\t\t\t\t
