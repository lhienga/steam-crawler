\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell worth playing in its current form.

The start is a little intimidating, especially if you're a perfectionist. Laying out a city without gridlines (and when you can set any angle you please) is intimidating, but you get used to it, and the end result is a city that looks and feels far more organic and real than anything I've created in a competing game.

Developers are very responsive, fixes are being deployed rapidly, and the game is challenging but not punishing. Unlike some other games in the genre, there's a pretty clear sense of "You could do this next" at pretty much every step of the way, but it never tells you that you have to do a particular thing next.

It's not perfectly polished, but it's in very good shape for Early Access, and the bugs (which aren't huge in number) are 'somewhat irritating', nothing worse.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave had two playthroughs of this game now, one till endgame, other to midgame. Here's a bunch of what I have to say:

Summary:
If you're into a game where you have to manage flows of resources from extractors, to factories and onto "consumers", then this is one for you. It has some citybuilder mechanics, but most of it comes down to making sure your infrastructure is equipped to deliver the necessary goods on time. So expect this to be a mix of cities skylines lite, with a mix of factorio lite. That may describe it as best.

The dev is also super responsive to bugs, fixing those I ran into within a day. I have to give some mention of that, because that's some real level of commitment.

Pros:
- Quick to learn, hard to master.
- Not grindy.
- Not as complex as, let's say, cracktorio.
- Dev is super responsive to bugs.

Cons:
- Right now, replayability is not very high. It's the same gameplay with a new map.
- It could do well with extra content, but as it's early access right now, that's definately bound to come.

So all in all, I can give a recommendation for this game. If it continues down it's current path, then this title can definately become very enjoyable to a lot of people in the long run.\t\t\t\t\t
