\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fast-paced platformer roguelite focused on combat. The core gameplay is that you fight enemies platformer-style, usually using melee attacks, and the dodge move which allows you to dash while invulnerable for a short time.

First, the bad and some things not necessarily bad but that may put some players off, which others have also pointed out.
- The smaller enemies tend to overlap each other, making you not able to see the enemies covered behind, even though seeing the visual cues is important to dodge the attacks. This results in some artificial difficulty that could've been avoided, by simply having less enemies and perhaps making them stronger to compensate.
- Luck is a huge factor. Even with the same skill level a boss battle can be very easy or hard depending on your roll on heads, gear, synergy.
- Somewhat small window of sight, which can be a problem if an enemy out of sight is preparing an attack you can't see coming. This is rarely a problem in my experience, you can at least use the minimap on the bottom-right to see the level layout and enemy locations outside of your sight window.
- The particles/effects can be distracting, even from your own attacks (try dodging the attacks of a boss you're fighting for the first time, while your own attacks are 2 giant blackholes in the background... and sometimes I still reflexively jump away from my own passive summons as an Alchemist)
- The difficulty: Skul is on the harder side, and you have to be patient to get familiar with the enemies and their attack patterns to really progress.

The good:
- Tons of content: A diverse array of enemies, and a ton of characters, a good amount of items. The heads/characters are numerous and most are pretty viable, so you don't necessarily tend to gravitate to only the same one or two choices as may happen in some other rogue-lites. They also get various skins as you upgrade them to higher tiers, which is very cool.
- Absolutely beautiful pixel sprites
- Great soundtrack to boot

Now, the good list may end up a lot shorter than the bad one, but that's only the bad ones are quirky bits that need lengthy explanations. Other than these quirks, this game is absolutely epic, you can enjoy intense battles while seeing new content most or every run for a good amount of time, and there also seems to be a considerable amount of unlockable content for replayability, and there is also synergies to figure out, not to mention there is even new content being added through free updates.

I got this game on a 50% off sale, but if you like fast-paced platformer fights, and have the patience or skill to deal with the difficulty, then I absolutely recommend Skul even at full price. Great game!\t\t\t\t\t
