\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played this game for a very long time, because I kind of enjoyed it. But having played many other roguelikes, I have to admit that when I look back on the time spent on Skul, or when I pick it up again, it always feels like wasted time. I usually play at DM10, so I think I know plenty about the game.

Personally, my main gripes come with DM10 and the DLC bosses, because it feels like the devs tried too hard to make enemies look cool rather than actually be fun to fight.

Skul is difficult, but not in a good way. It's not really a challenge that makes you feel good, it's a slog that makes you feel relieved at the end. Enemies at high difficulty levels literally just do more damage and are (significantly) tankier, to the extent that you could actually spend a minute mashing the attack button in the early game.

Enemy design is pretty bad, with lots of attacks feeling like cheap gotcha moments that only nail you because you didn't expect or didn't know that attack existed. With enemies doing a lot more damage at high difficulty, it gets much worse when you have exploding enemies, enemies with huge AOEs, enemies that spam a ton of projectiles. Once again, it is certainly possible to win, but it's just not very enjoyable because it feels like the (very numerous) enemy throws a lot of attacks at you.

Other roguelikes do a better job of ensuring that the screen isn't as visually cluttered as Skul, and they also usually handle higher difficulties better (I honestly think that DM8 is the perfect balance since enemies aren't unfairly tanky or do really absurd damage)

Game is repetitive: Rooms are all fixed. You'll quickly remember exactly what enemies spawn in each room, and sure, that helps in terms of clearing and knowing what to expect, but it's also kinda boring, especially when enemy spawns are also the exact same.

RNG is a definite issue. Not to say that the game is unwinnable with bad RNG, I (and I'm pretty sure other longtime Skul players) have cleared Dark Hero at DM10 with Little Bone and all sorts of scuffed runs. But the real issue is how fun the run actually is, which, with bad RNG, is not fun simply because of how long battles can take.

The story is fantastic, but it doesn't excuse the gameplay being so frustrating.

Only some bosses are actually well designed frankly, Dark Skul, First Hero and Dark Hero.

Everything else is generally a problem of too much stuff on the screen (Chimera, Joan), or have annoying hitboxes that ruin the fight (Sisters, and they'd be a lot more fun if their hitboxes didn't linger longer than necessary). And I'm pretty sure most of us hate adventurer fights since they often devolve into a spam fiesta (not even counting how buggy the enemy heroes can get at times, especially Tank).

Emperor (DLC boss) is exceptionally bad. He's not a difficult boss, so much as he keeps throwing attacks at you, and at higher difficulties, his attacks can sometimes feel pretty cheap. I've seen all his attacks, and they definitely fit into the category of: Cool looking attacks that aren't very fun to deal with because at high DM they hit absurdly hard and cover most of the screen. It's absolutely possible to dodge them, but as for how fun it is? Not very, when you consider that he has effectively 5 health bars to get through (3 phases), and with how spongey bosses are at DM10, I can take about 10 minutes fighting him. Sure, I can usually pull off wins, but when the credits roll, I'll just think to myself: Damn, I really sat there hitting him for that long?

Another issue is that the devs make lots of pretty small changes that don't ever address the really big issues. If anything, when they did make a major change, they ended up making Ghoul the best and easiest skull in the game, and all the weak skulls remained, well, not particularly fun to play. If they at least fixed the hitboxes of enemies, I'd be much more optimistic, but as it stands, they've pretty much never bothered to look into the worst offenders (Thief, Sisters, Transcendent, Arbiters and a few others that I can't quite remember)

Probably the worst thing is that you don't get any iframes when you get hit, so certain attacks or Dark Quartz empowered enemies can actually shred your health bar if they catch you (and they absolutely can even if you're good because their abilities often come out extremely suddenly and without warning), main ones being Conductor and Disaster (the fire one). Sisters are an exceptional offender here, especially in the second phase where single dash skulls are unfairly punished because one slash attack can hit you about three times.

Frankly, I really love this game. And I wish the devs actually bothered to make healthy changes that actually made the game more fun. But right now? I can't in good faith recommend it to anybody. If you want the (genuinely good) story with honestly really nice voice acting, I'd suggest watching it online rather than torturing yourself trying to play this game, especially with how badly balanced the game is at higher difficulties.

Skill is an important factor in roguelikes, but in Skul, even when you do "git gud", it often doesn't feel fulfilling or good even when you win.\t\t\t\t\t
