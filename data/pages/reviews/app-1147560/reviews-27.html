\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is bloody hard but once you beat it it's so satisfying. It's a mashup between a classic roguelite like Rogue Legacy, a metroidvania, and dark souls new game+ elements with the craft and love of something similar to Deadcells. If your into any of those games you should pick it up

STORY
Most roguelite metroidvania games don't have much of a story, but Skul The Hero Slayer definitely has a really well crafted one. You play as a skul, and you later realize how your character fits into the overarching theme of the game as you progress / die over time.

GAMEPLAY
Skul is unique in that there is roguelite and RPG level elements when it comes to acquiring "skulls". You can have up to two skulls at any given time, and they have different active, swap, and passive level abilities. There's a few dozen in the game, each one is randomized on the dungeon rewards you go through in the roguelite genre

So you can have some really interesting combinations between the two skulls you pick. There's three different types - power, speed, balance. And not only that, there's a lot of different items you can pick along the run, with a combination of items providing extra bonuses much like you would armor pieces in a Diablo game for instance

Each run has many infinitely level of possibilities of how you want to approach beating it, and that's what makes this game fun and rewarding. It's like multiple levels of roguelite and RPG elements built into a metroidvania, and it encourages you to experiment with different strategies for beating it

You will die a lot in this game, and every run you do there will be more upgrades - but the game also progressively gets harder as well.

ANIMATION / ART
I would compare this to the same level of deadcells. Every skull, every ability, has a lot of beautiful artwork and animations associated with it. It feels like a AAA production level game for what is normally considered a indie genre

DIFFICULTY
I would pick this games difficulty on the same level as something similar to a really hard metroidvania dark souls. It took a solid 40+ hours to beat it, and every run is only about an hour long. I finished the game on darkmode8 with a ninja/thief build

OVERALL
This game is really addicting and you will want to read a lot of guides and wikis along the way as you play it, there's dozens of classes/skuls to play, and so many ways you can theorycraft to your hearts content
