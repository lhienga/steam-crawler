\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to give this game a boatload of credit for being built in such a manner that it makes action rougelites feel...accessible? for folks such as myself, who may not necessarily have the inherent chops for that sort of game. It feels a lot more possible to come back from bad starts or rough patches during your runs than other games in this sub-genre, which immediately appeals to me.

I'm a humongous sucker for large character rosters in games and though Skul is obviously the lone protagonist here, his selection of heads all play so differently & really pop off the screen as their own individual personalities that you may as well be "choosing" from thirty-nine little dudes. That's in ADDITION to the fact that the way they interact with items and Inscriptions means there are boatloads of builds for each; the amount of variety you have at your disposal is tremendous and reminds very much of the more recent 2D Castlevanias.

The art style for this game is amazing: pixelated, incredibly vibrant and colorful, the amount of animation each model displays is masterful for said pixelation, each environment has such a distinct ambience and palette...of everything I've bought in the past year and change, I think only Young Souls, Cuphead & Shredder's Revenge have it beat visually.

The soundtrack is kinda sneaky I feel: initially it sounds solid but forgettable and only somewhat fits the game it's attached to. It has very quickly grown on me though, mostly because I noticed that it makes nearly EVERYTHING you do sound subtly epic. Going to the Shop and trying to decide what to buy sounds like a heavy weighty decision; going through a couple of the areas sounds like a grand journey despite the fact that you could speedrun through this game in less than an hour; every main boss fight sounds like a massive struggle, and the Adventurer theme just sounds like a bop.

The Broken Mirror has seemingly doubled the size of this game by packing a ton of content into a FREE UPDATE (full disclosure: basing solely on how the beta feels as of this writing) and will easily wring another fifty-one hundred more hours of replay value outta me. I would posit that as of right now, if you're not:
- Supergiant (because you have Hades II making the gaming community salivating)
- Square Enix (because for whatever faults you may have as a whole these days, some branch of your team and Acquire are about to unleash Octopath II upon the world)
- Red Hook (because Darkest Dungeon 2)
- Big Blue Bubble (because Power Chord is poised to be top-tier deckbuilder and really help the team-based deckbuilder rouge take off in popularity)

then you need to step up this year, because as of now, SouthPAW Games has you beat. Massively recommended for those to want to get into Dead Cells and the like, but want to get eased into that type of experience!\t\t\t\t\t
