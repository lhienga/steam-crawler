\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHad so much fun with this game.

In ways the battle isn't complex at all, but it's very involved if you want to play at peak performance. Probably the most unique thing about the combat is that it incentivizes swapping your character/class constantly during combat. That combined with cooldowns and buffs makes it so there's a lot to keep track of while also having to actually jump around and fight. The more times you beat the game, there more it adds small or big changes to mechanics/bosses and so on, a little like in Slay the Spire let's say.

Storytelling sequences is a bit simple but still told tastefully. My only problem is it was sometimes hard to keep track of the whole narrative since it's fed to you slowly between attempts as you reach further parts of the game. If you aren't losing much it's not a big deal, but sometimes it was a couple days before I got to another story segment and it doesn't repeat them if you've seen it on a previous playthrough.

Impressive soundtrack really complements the aesthetic and epic boss fights. Some of the later fights' explosive personality excited me more than most recent gaming memories I can recall.

For downsides, the game doesn't really change each attempt other than what items/upgrades etc show up, so that can feel dull but this can be helpful in crafting a well-balanced experience. Managing the inventory and comparing items while making a build can also be a pain in the ass, but mostly while you're still new and unfamiliar. There's a lot of information to see, though, so I still have some respect for what the UI managed to do. There is occasionally screen diarrhea where you can barely even tell what you're supposed to be trying to dodge which is occasionally infuriating. You can get hit in this game without it destroying you immediately so it's not too bad usually.

If you think you'd like this based on a gameplay vid, you probably will. One of my favorite games in the past year or two.\t\t\t\t\t
