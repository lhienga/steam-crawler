\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's no doubt in my mind that this game is a top tier roguelike. There's a lot to it, with lots of builds and play styles to chose from. Combat is fun and fast paced and each run doesn't feel like a slog to get through.

However, I think the game becomes significantly more frustrating than it is fun once you hit Dark World Level 4. There ends up too many tanky enemies on screen at once and, you get lost in the visual noise of all the chaos on screen. It may be a "mad cause bad" perspective, but I think the difficulty spike hits too hard at this point in the game.

Regardless, the entire reason I am writing this review is to counter the negative reviews that say to avoid it because of the Dark World difficulty. For the 10 bucks I got this for, it was a great way to spend 50 hours. Yes the end game content is mind numbingly frustrating, but that doesn't take away from how much fun the base game and first few levels of Dark World is.

EDIT:

After beating Dark World 10 and 100%ing the game, I still stand by this.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had this game for a long time now and finally decided to review it today after playing the newest content. I started playing (and beat the game in its entirety) before the first big content update, and have picked it up again with every subsequent addition of stuff.

Every single time I am delighted. Even when things don't mesh perfectly at launch (these content updates are so large and ambitious), the team behind Skul always prove that this is a labor of love. Their responsiveness and competent fixes to balance and overall polish are something I wish I saw more often from creators. The controls are simple and intuitive even on a keyboard, the environments and enemies cohesive, readable, and whimsical, the boss fights dynamic and challenging, and the endless options for character builds rewarding. Skul is one of the most charming and addictive roguelites I've ever bought- twenty dollars for this gem, especially with all of the free updated content, is highway robbery.

It's true, Skul might not appeal to everyone, as much as I'd love for everyone to try it. The story is cute and paints a picture of a fully realized world, but isn't so central that one could call it an RPG- the story is all told through gameplay and level progression, which means revealing parts of the world is earned slowly. You have to be in it for the arcade experience first, and then let the story gradually fill in the beautifully pixellated environment it is set in.

Skul's also a challenging title that rewards dedication and experimentation. It isn't impossible, never feeling unfair to me in its enemy patterns or rng. Easy to learn, hard to master, as others have said. In my almost 150 hours of playing I've defeated the final boss maybe twice. Thrice? Either way, not often- you work hard to win this game, and maybe you're not keen on that time investment for completion. But even incomplete runs are a fun experience every time. Whether it be new synergies of items or combinations of skulls I discovered, I never come away from this game feeling frustrated or disappointed as if I've earned nothing towards improving my next run. My choices and skill level always dictate my success rate, and I can see both improve exponentially every time I play. I'd go so far as to say I'd still be playing even if I never beat the final boss once.

Bottom line: Skul is a joy to play. If you like roguelites, if you like action platformers with a classic feel, if you like gameplay that prioritizes fun without sacrificing a fair challenge, this is a shining example of all three, IMO. This is an arcade skill game, both modern and timeless, that has made a place for itself in my heart and future carpal tunnel. I hope to see SouthPAW make more great things in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
