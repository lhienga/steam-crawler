\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo after holding off on this game so long, I thought I'd never play it. I tend to pick up games in bulk during steam sales and then often shelf them without ever attempting to play. I was bored last night and didn't want to play one of my usual games so I started Skul since I saw it in my library. This game is exactly what I thought it would be, a fun roguelike game.

Pros: What's not to love? The art style is kid-friendly not to mention really cool, the combat is overall fluid (as long as you build correctly), and there's a cute light-hearted story with dark tones attached to Skul's journey with every area you progress through and the bosses you beat. This game encourages the player to progress the story / game which many roguelikes fail to do. This is such a unique title, and I can't believe I slept on it for so long

Cons: You'll probably hear this from many other players, there are certain skulls that are for lack of a better word, trash, and will often land you in trouble. Having an idea of what skulls are good and bad is determinant of how long your run will last. There really is a big difference when it comes to certain skulls, and how they perform. Choosing the right one or swapping out your skull for another you found in mid-level and selling it for bones can mean the difference between finishing your playthrough, or ending really early to a what is usually not so difficult boss fight.

Conclusion: Overall I recommend this game, it is quite a fun time, and although I haven't mastered all the game's mechanics yet, I am having fun figuring it out while on my journey to save the Demon King! If anyone has advice they would like to share they are more than welcome. I will definitely be playing more of this title!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI leave this review in hopes that the criticism provided can help improve the game as I wholeheartedly enjoy it. Skul the hero slayer has great visuals, fun ideas and it seems like a very passionate development team behind it. However, major issues with the gameplay creep up on a player as they spend time on the game until it ultimately can destroy a player's experience with it.

The first and biggest issue is the visual clarity, which is something I see many other reviews mention. The problems come not only from the particle effects (which can obscure characters and enemy attacks), but also from the enemy attack windup animations. Generally, a stronger attack will take a longer time to wind up and a weaker attack is unleashed faster to help the player not only react to the incoming danger, but also gauge how much damage they would take from attacks. In this game, the windup animations can be non existent at times, and when there is too much on screen can be difficult to even notice. This becomes a problem especially in boss fights when there are multiple bosses on screen, as each one can unleash their own attacks at any time with little to no warning. Some wind up animations on the same enemy can also look too similar to one another, making it hard to tell what the enemy will do, which can lead to some deaths that are completely uncalled for.

The particle effects help make the game look visually appealing, but only serve to distract the player and can even hurt them in some cases. There is an item in the game - "Flame Dragon" that obscures about a quarter of the screen when used. It creates a wall of fire that damages enemies, but makes everything else in its area very difficult and sometimes even impossible to see. This is made worse by boss attacks being telegraphed by red zones that flash at the incoming attack area. The issue with this seems evident, and makes this item actively harmful in reasons likely not intended by the developers. There are items that would be fun to play with, but I cannot use them because they make certain encounters almost impossible to deal with due to the sheer amount of visual clutter on screen. This problem extends to some sprites including the player sprite. As an example, there is a skull in the game called the frozen soul which is white and light blue in colour. The sprite itself looks very good, but the developers have to consider the environment it is put in and how that might affect the ability for a player to see it. The final boss room has a light blue background. The problem is clear. These are only 2 examples I can think of off the top of my head. The game is filled with this.

The boss design can also be infuriating to deal with. One of my issues is with the adventurer encounters. It is a fun idea in concept, where the adventurers have somewhat simple kits but you have to fight an increasing number of them at the same time as you progress in your game. However, some of their kits can seem too complex to deal with in addition to everything else the other adventurers throw at you, and becomes a major problem when the bosses have badly telegraphed attacks. A possible fix could be to have a unique sound effect for each attack the bosses use, as the sheer amount of visual clutter and things you have to keep track of on the screen becomes unbelievably frustrating to deal with.

The boss I have the biggest issue with are the Leiana sisters. The sisters have individual healthbars, and when one is taken down, the other begins her second phase. The issue comes when you realise that the sisters have identical sprites. Although they appear in the pre-fight cutscene wearing different coloured capes, they take them off before the fight. It is almost impossible to tell them apart unless you apply a status effect on them to differentiate them from one another. There is interesting decision making in the fight as the second sister will start her second phase with less health depending on how much damage you deal to her in her first phase. Will the player try to melt one sister to focus on a harder 1v1 fight or try to juggle damage to both healthbars to gain an advantage in the more difficult second phase? This decision making is severely hurt when you cannot tell who you are fighting at a glance. This boss fight becomes even worse to deal with in the hard mode of the game as the decision making is completely removed. The second phase always starts at full health, so the best option would be to focus one sister before facing the other. And there comes the problem. Every boss has little problems like this that can make some situations infuriating to deal with.

This game can also cause physical discomfort to players. There exists a variety of skulls and items that incentivise using basic attacks to deal the bulk of their damage. Having to continuously press the attack button for a run that can last around 30 minutes becomes not only tiring but also potentially painful with extended play sessions. I would liken the experience to a bullet hell game where the player has to press the shoot button for each bullet they shoot. I saw a review suggesting an option to hold down the button and allow the character to attack without stopping and I believe this to be a good fix.

I have fun with this game. It has a lot of fun gameplay ideas and the huge number of items and skulls makes me excited to experiment with builds and strategies. It is evident from the sprite work and gameplay mechanics that there is a lot of love and effort put into this game, and I appreciate that. I have close to 100 hours on the game, and encountering the previously mentioned issues I have with the game has slowly built up my frustration towards it. But again, I am leaving this review in hopes that it can improve in these areas, as I see the potential it has in becoming an amazing roguelike experience that I can say is my favourite in the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
