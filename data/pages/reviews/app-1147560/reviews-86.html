\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Skul experience went like this for me.

Base game: fun experience, most builds feel great, difficulty feels challenging enough to be fun, but still pretty forgiving. Lots to discover and experiment with.

Dark Mirror Levels 0-3 (included): stale experience, a few skulls feel like they are useless, harder difficulty but still fairly doable. Got a bit boring and repetitive.

Dark Mirror Levels 4-5 (included): unfair experience, a lot of builds feel not viable and RPG dependent, you don't really get enough useful upgrades to keep up with the difficulty scaling. Miserable experience, ended up resetting most runs.

Dark Mirror Levels 6-10 (included): peak roguelite experience, a lot of builds still feel completely useless, but you can customize your build to your liking so it doesn't matter, difficulty is really high and unforgiving but you finally have the tools to adapt to it. A lot of fun

Game has a really beautiful art style and controls feel smooth. I'd say even just the base content is worth the price (if you're not into harder kind of games).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn unconventional recommend but hear me out on this.
I hate Rogue-lites. I don't like progression mechanics. I don't like when the game lets you whittle its difficulty down through a persistent unlock system instead of encouraging you as a player to improve.
It's why I was frustrated with Dead Cells, and why I cannot stand Wizards of Legend.
So the first thing I did when I found out about Skol's Dark Quartz was find its address and crank it up to bypass the hours of mindless grind it would take to max out the skill tree.

Despite this, the game still beat me. It was easier of course, but it wasn't an insultingly dull pushover like so many of its contemporaries become after you've smashed your head into the wall long enough that it softens up and allows you to play as promoted.

So yes, it is a good game, a good action platformer, bypass the grind or not there's still challenge in it. Grab it on sale.
Apologies to the devs if my methods insult or offend you, I just prefer when victories are earned through skill and not time-sink. So I bypassed your timesink and found I still needed skill :)\t\t\t\t\t
