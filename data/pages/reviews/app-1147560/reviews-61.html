\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a fan of the Roguelike genre, this game is for you. The storyline of Skul is very simple: You are a small skeleton with the responsibility to rescue the demon king. Like many other games of the same genre, the gameplay revolves around defeating a series of enemies and bosses to complete the game.
The highlight of Skul is the skulls you can collect throughout the game, which change your skills, stats, and playstyle, making each playthrough completely different. Depending on your preferences, you can become a ninja with fast attacks and stealth abilities, or a mage who attacks enemies from afar, and so on. Overall, the combat in this game is very satisfying and addictive.

Regarding the monsters and bosses in the game, they all have their unique movesets that are diverse but easy to understand. You can grasp most of the basic monster movesets after only two hours of gameplay. The bosses in Skul increase in difficulty from easy to hard, and their mechanics are varied and different. Accompanying this is your own increasing power thanks to the items you earn and the powerful skulls you are carrying, but whether you can defeat them or not depends on how well you play.

In terms of graphics, Skul features pixel art that is well-crafted and beautifully designed (such as the witch), creating an eye-catching world where each map provides a different atmosphere. Additionally, the soundtrack is excellent, ranging from gentle choral music that relaxes you in the deep forest to metal music that gets your heart racing during boss battles.

The downside of the game is that it can get repetitive with the same enemies appearing over and over again, the levels following a linear path, and the game's duration being short. There really isn't a lot to explore. However, the new update adds a new game mode alongside the original one. Dark Mirror introduces new types of monsters, movesets for the bosses, and new features, increasing the game's difficulty and providing a new challenge for those who have completed the original game and are looking for something new and challenging.

In conclusion, Skul is worth having in your game library if you're looking for something to immerse yourself in for hours on end. Let's go rescue the demon king!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot recommend this game enough.

This might be one of my favourite roguelikes ever made, and while it admittedly is not perfect I'd say this game was developed with more love than even Dead Cells.

It's developers have been making a constant effort to polish, re-balance, update, and upgrade the game since launch and have so far released all it's post launch updates FOR FREE. Since it's release in 2021 It's size has easily doubled and the game is showing no signs of slowing down.

Pros
1. Devs make constant revisions on character and item balance to ensure almost every playstyle is viable.
2. Beautiful pixel art, easily some of the best on steam.
3. Powerful Item synergies that can almost rival Binding of Isaac and RoR2.
4. 39 different playable characters, each with several variants and upgraded forms.
5. Quick snappy controls and a well designed dodge roll system.
6. Has had frequent substantial content updates for 2 years now at the time of me writing this review.

Cons
1. Dark Mirror bosses and Dark enemies can be a little unbalanced at times.
2. No auto attack means some builds will require a lot of button mashing.
3. Attack Speed causes some attacks to break if you let it get too high.\t\t\t\t\t
