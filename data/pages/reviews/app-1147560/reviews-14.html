\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gameplay is very fun. It definitely gets very frustrating the further you get though. The issue is that there are many enemies that have attacks that just spring from underneath you. The only way you would know you were going to get hit is a little flash on the floor. The second issue with this is that you have many attacks that do the same thing so you then have to decipher if this is one of your attacks or an enemies, and it sucks. Another bad thing is that many enemies teleport around the map. Nothing better than having an enemy teleport to your face and immediately hit you. Dash has a cooldown so many times you are left without any option but to get hit. The last thing that sucks about this game is that projectiles go through the floor and enemies that you cannot see (literally off the screen) have speedy projectiles that just hit you. It would be fine if the enemy projectiles just didn't go through the floor.
Other than those three things this game is very fun.
One way to kind of counteract this is that you can just quit the game before you die and start the level over again. Which I didn't realize until I got pretty far. I then used this method to clear the mirror 5 stage. At that point there is just crap all over the screen and it feels really cheap when you get hit, which is constantly. So, I am not sure if I will continue with the story at this point. There are 10 mirror stages and 5 was pretty darn annoying. If the enemies didn't have so much health it wouldn't be as big an issue, but you kind of get limited into what you can use in your build the further you progress. I found I had to use magic, and just keep pumping damage and the spirits. The spirits continually damage enemies while you are constantly dodging (later stages), otherwise you get into a loop where you are not doing enough damage and just play forever (bosses have regenerative healing).
Combat is fun when you get the right skull (skulls modify your abilities). However, there are runs where you just wont get anything.
I wish, instead of just making me run over and over again with higher and higher difficulties, it was more like binding of isaac. Having a hard mode but you basically just run that over and over again. Then you can experiment with different builds and such. Right now, at stage 1-5 of the mirror levels, its basically been me waiting for the right skull and right set of skills, not being able to experiment. I have had no success with physical type skulls and 90% of the items.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA wonderful game with items that really impact your play-style that can lead to so many different feeling runs.
The difficulty of the game is a steep learning curve but it is done well as difficulty comes through increasing variety of enemies and traps.

There are two things that make me not recommend this game:

1. One of the three character types "Speed" is not possible to play well unless you are able to mash one button without tiring or hurting yourself. The game would benefit greatly by an option to hold button for continuous attacks.

2.Visual clarity is not this games strong suit. There is some amazing work done with the pixel art and particle effects. Too many effects. Once you stack up bonuses through items, enemies and especially bosses are incredibly hard to see. All or most of these effects should layered behind/around the enemies to allow the player to see incoming attacks and enemy projectiles. Another option would be a toggle to highlight enemy models.

If these two things do not bother you, this is an easy recommend for anyone that loves rouge-like elements, varied player builds, and button mashing action with some platforming elements.\t\t\t\t\t
