\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love roguelites. I've played most of the popular ones and quite a few of the lesser known games. To me, Skul stands out among its competitors.

I have to be honest: I rarely play games for their stories. If I want good stories, I'll read a book. What captivates me, especially with roguelites, is a tight core gameplay loop, a vast array of possible build options and a main character that feels good to control.

Skul, in my opinion, nails all of these aspects. The gameplay loop of smashing through levels, collecting bones, upgrading your skulls and looking for synergies between items and your skull works incredibly well and is super addictive. The various skulls, which act as classes, vary in gameplay and feel. You always feel like you have choices to make, without ever feeling overwhelmed by tedious subsystems.

The build options are fun - items have unique effects and can synergise with each other, both categorically and in effect. There are vast possibilities to combine them with skulls and it never feels like you have to resort to some sort of meta, though you always have the option to. Even after 50+ hours in the game, I end up discovering new and exciting combinations I hadn't thought of before by accident.

Finally, control. I played a lot of Super Smash Bros. Melee and have grown to expect quite a bit from a game when it comes to movement. Skul NAILS this aspect. It feels tight, it feels like you're in control at all times, animations can be cancelled - it's just a good movement system. I truly believe this is one of the things that kept me playing this game even after beating it a couple of times - it just feels GOOOOD to play.

Combine the above with fresh DLC, an active developer that actually cares about their audience and you have a true gem of a roguelite that I certainly will be coming back to over the years.\t\t\t\t\t
