There are a good number of mechanics that just seem counter-intuitive. The skull swapping is the biggest one given that most of the skulls want to a thing one way and trying to itemize, collect bones, and play to 2 skulls' strengths in what amounts to 4 boss chests, 4 adventurer item selections, 8 visits to the store, and navigating cost/benefit of the rooms between seems a little like to really take advantage you'd need to be insanely lucky.

That being said, the actual game play loop is engaging with your standard enemies evolving each level and adding a new feature here and there so that learning the enemy patterns is a pretty smooth process. Upgrading your skulls generally doesn't shift your playstyle wildly but you can feel the weight of the advantage each skull's rarity provides, which coming off playing Dead Cells is pretty nice because it's less numbers get bigger and more features get added. My 2 of my 3 successful runs, with the Werewolf and Genie skull, I really enjoyed how each rarity level tweaked the core concept like Werewolf needing to keep more and more mobile and synergizing well with getting the right skills like the 2 dash attacks or the Genie's skills enhancing a bit and the normal attacks turning into big combos.\t\t\t\t\t
