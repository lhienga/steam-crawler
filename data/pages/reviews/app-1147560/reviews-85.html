The game is quite combat focused but the combat is just not fun. The devs made some great looking sprite animations, put them into the game and applied hitboxes where necessary. There is no weight to any of your attacks nor to the enemy's. This makes the game feel rather underwhelming to play, coming off as a poor man's version of Dead Cells. Not to mention all the missing sound effects.
There is the head swapping mechanic and the typical upgrades. They are fine I guess but couldn't provide that rogue-like hook.

For what I've seen tho, there are some nice monster girls in the game. Sadly they don't have close up dialogue portraits while talking to them but still. That stuff is good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Solid rougelike game; it's got a few flaws that prevent it from being one of the best but it's still very fun and worth a try if you like other games in the genre or 2D platform action games in general.

Although you don't play rougelikes for the story Skul actually has a decently short, charming one that's actually quite good with some decent introspection and commentary on the 'hero' genre especially towards the end.

Gameplay has a good arcadey fun feeling, and it captures the highs and lows of the rougelike genre well, albeit with two possibly major complaints. The clarity takes a big hit as you progress; debris and particles fly around and it's hard to tell what's going on or where your character is. This contributes (although is not the sole cause) to the bigger issue of damage feeling unavoidable at times. The iframes are a bit short on your dash/abilities and the game is a bit too fast and hectic to really 'avoid' damage consistently, and this lowers skill expression a bit and causes some frustration.

The environments do get a little repetitive after a few runs, and the upgrade material for your permanent upgrades are a bit expensive, meaning the game does get a tad grindy at a certain point when compounded with the above mentioned gameplay flaws. The Skulls early game tend to be of lower quality and can feel clunky/weak before they're upgraded, compounding the issue. Toggling on the rookie mode for -50% damage taken was a big helper here.

Overall the game's good. Engaging, solid rougelike fun with a nice charming short story, I'd definitely recommend grabbing it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
