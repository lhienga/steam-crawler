\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the time of writing this review, I have had a little more than 100 hours worth of playtime in Skul: The Hero Slayer. I didn't expect much when I first got in, having only been introduced to it by a good friend, but now... I regret not trying this game out sooner.

If you're looking for a difficult Roguelite with nigh infinite replayability, play this game.

If you're looking for a game with a cute lil buddy as your protagonist and don't care for how difficult it might be, play this game.

If you enjoy the visceral satisfaction of tearing through waves of enemies like a hot knife through butter, and obtaining that unrivalled high of sheer power, play this game.

I cannot overstate how fun this experience has been. I'm probably on the more skilled side of things, seeing as I managed to complete all the content in just about 100 hours, but I can safely call this the most addicting game I have played in a while. Even when it kicked me down, and I felt like my morale was too low to continue playing for the day, the game dragged me back in regardless and I still had a wonderful time.

Is it without flaws? No, not at all- In my experience magic builds basically trivialise most challenges leaving you with the simple problem of being good at the game as opposed to theorycrafting interesting new builds, but that's largely down to my own personal preference. No matter the playstyle you may have, you will inevitably find a skull you enjoy using extensively.

I've gone on for long enough. The point is, this game is a gem and should be enjoyed by far more people. The gate to entry is a little high, but the game never feels unfair. It is one of the best Roguelike experiences I have played, beating out even Dead Cells for myself, personally.

Buy this game and support the developers. You won't regret it, I promise you that.\t\t\t\t\t
