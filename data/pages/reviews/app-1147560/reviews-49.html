\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's good, but not quite great. But it has a lot of problems. Some can be fixed, others are in design. I feel like I'm crawling through tunnels of frustration to see the small slivers of greatness the game could have had more of.

Attacks aren't telegraphed in the usual way, the enemy just takes a pose and you know an attack comes x seconds later. You do not react to attacks, you are told when an attack happens and are to not be there when it does. The tradeoff for this is that attacks are pretty big. You can get trapped if you're unlucky and have to take damage. This makes me sad.

Attack ranges aren't clearly communicated. The actual attack is so fast that it's hard to tell where exactly an attack begins or ends.

Descriptions could use work. They're unclear at times. The tier 1 Mutant set says 'alternating skills have 45% cdr', which I took to mean alternating my skills means they have a lower cooldown. This opened up some cool combos. But then I checked the wiki and it was the skul swapping that is reduced in cooldown. I feel like the game tries very hard to not use Lol terminology, and suffers for it. 'Item cooldown reduction is increased' is needlessly confusing. Wounding and Bleeding is not explained anywhere. You're not given damage numbers either, just whether its physical or magical.

Your basic attacks are very visually noisy and you can entirely miss visual cues because you cannot see the enemies behind your basic attacks. I have to stop attacking to properly know whats happening in attack speed builds. I find myself avoiding rapidity builds because of it. Once they get going I feel my DPS goes down because I have to stop attacking to not get hit.

The set effects are cool, but its a complicated system. It takes a lot of time and thinking to choose what I will keep or toss. After playing a repeatedly this can get tiring. Some set effects are traps, too. The level 6 arms, for example, amplifies the the effect of a random arms in your inventory. Having a kendo stick in arms can mean your 3rd arms proc can be trash. That's ~15 seconds your tier 6 arm procs needs to show its power again.

Also, sometimes items give new skills and sometimes item sets give new skills, and vice versa in way of stats. It makes min-maxing very complicated, which a lot of people like to do. It's very hard to min-max, because you aren't given the information on how much certain effects do. Arms, Artifact, chase items, none of it is clearly defined.

Low tier Skuls are locked away with a massive 140 bone cost for no reason. These Skuls are interesting, and fill intersting niches. But it's a big commitment and I will need to spend 2 to 3 levels with a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ Skul until I get its Legendary, meanwhile when I'm getting these bones I'm passing up Rares and Uniques and sometimes Legendaries to have a worse Skul. And since the Skul upgrader is after a boss or miniboss, then I have to fight a boss with a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ skul.

When I first started I thought legendary Skuls were the final versions of starting Skuls, because that makes sense. I eventually found out they were unique. I still assumed the others were higher levels of starting Skuls, but no they're unique too. They're all unique. You always get the worst possible version of a Skul. I always end up using the same few Skuls because theyre Unique or Legendary and so have two active skills instead of one.

Despite all this, I would recommend this game as good roguelikes are rare. It's just not a great roguelike.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's good roguelite fun that doesn't feel like you're screwed over by RNG, thanks to focus on synergies rather than what items or skulls you get throughout a run. Different skulls make for diverse playstyles, ensuring that every run doesn't feel like a repetitive slogfest. My only issue is that stage layouts are very, very repetitive. Once you've played a 10 runs or so, you've pretty much seen every layout there, so it gets boring at times.

The artstyle is pretty cute and quite distinct from other games that have pixel art graphics. The music is serviceable, albeit gets very, very irritating at times as a result of a lack of diversity in music/sounds. I haven't any encountered any bugs that
have affected the game, so it's pretty coded well.

Overall, 7.5/10 game. The audio and stage design is repetitive, but the core gameplay and diversity of playstyles more than make up for it.

Boned.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat rogue-lite that focuses primarily on player skill and building synergistic items, rather than getting good RNG to be able to win.

For the rogue-lite aspects in the basic game, you get to slowly upgrade your basic stats via dark quartz while freeing a few NPC characters that each give you something at the beginning of your runs.
Once you beat the main game you get access to the "Dark Mirror". Which is essentially a series of 10 extra difficulties that each add something new for you to use in your attempts, at the cost of bringing a lot of new and stronger enemies to the game at the same time.

Mechanically, Skul gives you a lot of freedom and mobility by giving you a double jump plus most skulls having the ability to dash twice in a row if they want to; on top of those there are also many different skills or attacks that move your character around.
Adding on to that even further comes the main gimmick of the game: the ability to quickly switch between 2 skulls in the middle of combat. This effectively triples the amount of skills you get access to later on in the game; as if you have just 1 skull you have access to 2 skills and that's it, but if you have 2 skulls you have access to not just 4 skills but also 2 skills that activate the instant you swap to it.

The main ways the game gets around requiring RNG to win is that it allows you to sell items you no longer want for more money at the shops, while at the same time being generous enough with how much money you normally get to usually be able to buy more than one item at the shop each time; and it allows you to destroy unwanted skulls you find for bones, which you can use to upgrade the power and rarity of your current skull up to legendary rarity. This means that no matter what, if you play well you can always end up with at least 1 legendary rarity skull at the end of every run if not much more; and if you plan out what items you keep and pick up, you can end with a build that massively increases your damage and/or survivability.\t\t\t\t\t
