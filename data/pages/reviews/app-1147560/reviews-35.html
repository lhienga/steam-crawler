\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSkul is a game that grows on you over time. In the beginning I was very skeptical and not very impressed with what was being offered. The maps looked a bit bland, the bosses were either too easy or way too difficult, and I was never a fan of games where you cycle over and over like Dead Cells. But the more I stuck with it the more the game opened up and I began to see a wealth of abilities, item synergies, and builds that kept on giving the more I dived. Once I got over the beginner hump and became familiar with the game's mechanics and how to properly play, things became straight up addicting. You could spend a hundred hours trying out different item builds and different classes and still have barely scratched the surface.

Admittedly some classes are a lot better than others and the same can be said about items. Even getting a run going can be largely dependent on getting good items early on. The post game hard mode mitigates these issues a bit by allowing you to have a small amount of control on starting conditions but these are problems that do persist over the course of the game. The story also leaves a lot to be desired as it's pretty generic and more of a vehicle to get you to the end of the game rather than being an experience.

Despite some of the issues this game has I feel like it has a lot to offer and gives the more you put in. If you want a game that is all about the gameplay and finding unique and interesting synergies and strategies, something you could speedrun, this is the game for you. I myself have put in hundreds of hours and will likely put in several hundred more in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to recommend, it has a lot of charm, but mechanically it's a mess...

If you're familiar with rougelikes, you're familiar with the process of upgrading your characters stats, functionality, or unlocking items from material/currency you find during your runs. Skul functions similarly, except the upgrades are minor and take far too much grinding. There are 12 upgrades with only 3 of them being slightly more than meager ("+6% Magic Damage"...), uninspired pittance to health and damage. There are no discernible item unlocks.

Currently, my greatest frustration is with an NPC that offers "upgrades" to weaponry you find during runs. The currency is so sparse, you'll likely reach the second major boss without having enough to upgrade a single skull (read "weapon"). You also lose all of this currency when you die. These upgrades are also TEMPORARY.

You get the gist. There are many such design decisions that will leave you scratching your head, wondering why the dev thought it was a good idea. At almost 10 hours in, and the gameplay feeling exactly the same as the very first run I did, I feel very comfortable is saying that it wasn't worth the $20 I spent on it.

Do not bother.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPROS
-Game gets hard, extremely so, but challenging in a nice way! (except bosses)
-A huge variety of skulls (classes) that each play a little differently and you can upgrade (evolve) them!
-A decent variety of enemies which sometimes require some strategy
-Plenty of permanent upgrades to get a little stronger every run, to hopefully beat the game (I haven't)
-Some surprises peppered in the runs here and there. Challenges are optional.
-Some pretty visually dazzling cutscenes, ultis and special attack. Bosses look amazing!
-Boss cutscene only has dialogue on the first time!
-Shops have a decent selection of stuff to choose
-Plenty of builds possible with items/class. Typically though the only thing that works is stacking damage bonuses.
-Controls are fantastic. No significant bugs. No crashes. Had zero issues!

CONS
-No branching paths nor alt-bosses à la Dead Cells. You'll be fighting through the same biomes and bosses, over and over.
-Music is nice but gets tiresome after a while. It's a little over-the-top sometimes. Also it mutes when I plug in an Xbox 360 controller, until I change levels.
-Bosses are kinda BS. The telegraphs of some attacks are so small they're pointless. Also some attacks spray the whole screen, forcing you to dodge through big fast projectiles.
-Relies too much on super-reflexes rather than memorisation and tactics. My 33-yr-old brain can't even, it's too fast!
-Some strats with some classes *seem* overpowered at first, until you meet a damage sponge (mini)boss and realise you suck and you'll still have to dodge constantly for a long while. It's really hard to actually feel powerful, even when I'm like, LITERALLY DEATH! ...it's never enough to beat the game unless you have super reflexes and know the patterns.
-It's a bit too hard to actually upgrade your skulls. I get super far into a run, getting almost to the final boss, but I still haven't legendary-ed my guy.
-Story is very cryptic and weird, with cringe dialog, but it was made by a korean dev so it was probably roughly translated. Pretty irrelevant. You can skip it.

BOTTOM LINE
A fairly decent roguelike that's very addicting. Unfortunately it suffers most from lack of variety in terms of levels and bosses, but the skull system and optional challenges slightly make up for it. I recommend getting it on sale. Hopefully we'll have an update in the future with more content to make runs less repetitive.
