\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSkul is a silly game about a tiny skeleton becoming a true hero for all demons and slightly for humans, though they don't like to admit it. Story-wise it does have some minor issues, notably with the usual lack of talking between characters, but also due to some translation issues. There isn't much of it in the actual story itself, but it feels like some translations were a bit rough. This is notable in the items more than anything, but more to that.This game is a solid action-rogue-lite, though they don't really give much reason for it to be that. Characters don't really react to you coming back at all, so it feels less like time is resetting and more like you're just doing everything first-try. Which... is pretty dang hard. This game relies on button mashing the attack button and skills, the latter of which is received by exchanging your skull with some other skull you find on your travels, each with their own set of skills and some with more than just two, but you have to pay to swap them out... which is random rather than a pick screen. But you can wield up to two skulls at once and swapping them in the middle of battle does a signature move, from randomly charging forward or summoning a dark cross in the sky that damages everything around it. Y'know, the usual.The overall theme of each skull is rather interesting and sort of tells a bit of the story in itself with the ones you find; some even being the very skulls of the soldiers you have to fight through. You can also upgrade them by smashing skulls you don't want then getting put into a web by a spider... Don't uh, question that, but upgrading them upgrades the whole skull and even the most basic skulls can win you the game if you're determined enough to upgrade them. There's also just randomly "Legendary" skulls, but... Being random to the bone is more or less this game's gimmick as you also get a lot of item drops which is your main form of gaining power. All items have their own stat to them and 2 specific "Groups" they belong to, each with a rather lengthy paragraph to each. Some are basic like if you have 2 with that group, you get +magic damage... buut then if you have 6, suddenly you can summon meteors randomly. The biggest issue with the game in this regard is that you can only hold 9 items in total, so you really have to pick and choose them carefully. But ... it's random which ones you get, so you can't really commit to a bit unless you're pretty lucky. Even then, you may not get a skull that synergies with those items well either, so it's really up to your own skill and luck to put them all together.But overall, it's fun. While I wish there was really more to it, especially with the "Dark Mirror" update just being a rehash of the main game with a few different features and enemies, the game isn't a bad time to just sit back and beat stuff up.. as a tiny, hero slaying skeleton. Outside of some translation issues, and a bit of a lack of content once you've gone through the game once or twice, the only major complaint I have would be having to mash a button to attack. This really wears down your fingers and some skulls can just hold it down to fire bullets, but all the melee ones /have/ to mash the button and that gets pretty painful.Either way! I'd recommend it if you want to beat stuff up and be a little emotionally driven to do your best. For all the ones you care for... except the Ninja guy. Screw that guy. 7/10\t\t\t\t\t
