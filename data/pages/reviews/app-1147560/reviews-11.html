\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is only one non-rng dependent way to heal in this game and it is beating major bosses. It’s not even a full heal. Bad drop rng? No healing. No money? No healing. Healing shop has an item that only restores 1% of your hp? Too bad, try not getting hit next time.

While health management is often an (annoying) aspect of roguelikes, the rest of the game makes this feel like a last-minute change with unclear telegraphs that force memorization, stage design that forces you choose between taking damage or wasting time and no distinction between player and enemy attacks. All this and more among a general DPS race leads to a gradual wear down of health that makes perseverance through a doomed run to be wasted effort.

Only one difficulty option exists- “Rookie Mode” and it’s a flat 50% reduction to damage taken. This solves none of the issues and just feels like cheating. I would much rather be slightly healed on completing an area or have highlighted enemies and projectiles for a “Rookie Mode”. Beyond this, it is still an enjoyable game, but I cannot recommend it in its current state.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSkul is a real fun, but hard sidescrolling action roguelite.

The controls are solid - it is hard and fair.

When I am writing this - I am 90 hours into playing this game. It took me about 80 hours to beat the boss for the first time and unlock New Game+ or as it is called here the Dark Mirror dimenison. I started a new saveslot though when I came back after not playing for more then a year.

Skul is hard and challenging, but also fair. It almost always feels like your own mistakes lead to your demise.

Skuls main concept are the Skulls of other demons - that are basicly character classes you can find. These can be things like warriors with big swords, alchemists that throw potions, fast rogues and many many more. During a session you will find many of those - but you can only keep two of them. All the others you destroy to improve them over a session.

The other main concept during a session are the items. You use your items to specialise your character. There are many different playstyles: Focusing on strength to deal more damage, speed for attacks, reducing your cooldowns to use your skills more often, collecting magical spirits that grow stronger the more you have - all that is possible.

These two things together blend in a very fun way so that each run will feel unique. The runs go through various levels containing several screens /maps. Each of these maps is fixed - after a while you learn what enemies will spawn where on those levels. You always go through a random selection of those screens, but you will know what to expect on each of the screens.

Each level will have a mid-level boss fight and a boss fight at the end. As you get better at combing items, learning the enemies pattern your runs will be longer.

Also helpfull is of course the meta-progression which makes your stronger after time. Each run you earn bits of a currency to basicly get stronger. At least for me, beating the game without the meta-progression maxed was not possible.

I love coming back to Skul. And so will you.


My main critics are that sometimes so much is happening on Screen, that I have no idea where my character actually is. Also sometimes my double jump just does not work... And some skulls seem pretty useless - but it can be fun to find combinations even for those lower tier heroes that work...\t\t\t\t\t
