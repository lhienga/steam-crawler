\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot to talk about for this game. It has amazing graphics, interesting combat ideas, stellar music, and creative enemy ideas. However, despite my love for this game, it does have flaws that the negative reviews do not blow out of proportion.

Pros
1. The Skuls are incredibly fun, such as Ghoul which has really fun character mechanics. They're all different and Southpaw did a really good job with the ideas for each Skul.
2. The different types of items are all unique with their own sub-mechanics inside them which lets different playstyles be flourished from a small switch of items. Some letting you focus on attacking much faster or have a reliance on dodging to dish out large amounts of damage, making it fun to customize what you want.
3. All the quintessences are even more unique than the items, having really fun playstyles be emerged. I have had a lot of fun using them and they let you switch up your playstyle, just like the items. All of them feel incredibly balanced and fair, the balance of the quintessences are perfect.
4. I love the black mirror update. I know it's not everyone's cup of tea as it's really difficult and so this isn't a pro for everyone but that's why I love it so much. It provides a much harder challenge for the players, one of which happens to be me, who want to play the hardest this game has to offer and it delivers. I'm currently only passed level 4 but I'm going to make it up to level 10.
5. This game is ridiculously atmospheric. The music always fits the mood (like my heart enters overdrive when their boss music kicks in) and is really well made. The art is really appealing, especially to someone who loves pixel art like me, it looks really nice.

Cons
1. There is a defined meta. Some Skuls are outright unplayable, even if they have interesting or fun abilities. There's a clear lean towards physical Skuls, leaving magic Skuls somewhat in the dust.
2. RNG plays a really big part in this. All rougelikes have, to some extent, an aspect of RNG but Skul is heavily reliant on it. Especially if you're attempting to use magic Skuls, it's a roll of the dice if you'll have good enough items to even play the game.
3. No matter what, if you want to win, you can't have both a physical and magic Skul. Okay, that may be a bit of an exaggeration, but seriously. It is almost impossible to play the game by trying to balance having both a physical and magic Skul. This can make some Skuks like Prisoner or Werewolf be really hard to play unless you get really lucky that run.
4. The start of the game is incredibly grind heavy. This has to be my most disliked part of the game. It is so incredibly boring because it is practically unplayable to play the game unless you get all the permanent upgrades. But until then, you need to grind out the first 2 stages because getting past them is a practical miracle. And this is a really bad issue to have as it can take past an hour for a new player to get all of the upgrades which can really turn off new players which sucks because it's really fun. I understand why they made it like this, it was to progress the story at a pace that seemed fit. The issue is they put the story at a much higher priority as the gameplay for getting all the upgrades is like trying to eat sandpaper when you don't have teeth. Or a jaw.
5. This game punishes you extremely hard. This more so applies to collecting bone fragments because the amount of time i have been less than 5 bone shards away from getting a legendary Skul is actually unbelievable. This was somewhat solved by letting bone fragments be bought in the shop and this would be a really amazing fix... if it wasn't for the fact it's only in the black mirror and also you need to beat a few levels of it to even unlock it. All they simply need to do is let it always be available in both normal and black mirror consecutively. But also this applies to boss fights. This is a mechanic I really enjoy, it's a lot like Dark Souls in that it punishes you hard. The difference is that it's fair in Dark Souls, as unless you're good at the game and understand the attack patterns of every boss and enemy, it is really hard to know what they're going to do. This usually ends with the player just spamming the dodge button, hoping they don't get hit, when it shouldn't be played like that.

TL;DR: The game is really fun, with really unique mechanics, but not the best for casual players and really bad for new players who prefer casual games. A defined meta and heavy reliance on RNG leaves several Skuls and items practically unusable but the quintessences are all fun and balanced. This is game needs to be worked on heavily for balance and is grind-heavy at the start but still give it a shot and refund it if it's not your cup of tea.
