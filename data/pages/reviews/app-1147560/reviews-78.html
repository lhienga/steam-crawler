\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't understand the item inscription system. Or maybe I do, it's just that it's as stingy and wasteful as it seems.

Items give bonuses to stats and damage, but they also have two inscriptions on them. There are 35 inscriptions spread over more than hundred items, resulting in lots and lots of combinations. Skul has nine equipment slots, so there are lots of useless items in any run. It's constantly like trying to make an inside straight - theoretically what I need is available, and on a long enough time line I'll get it, but time is linear and I have other things I want to do. Players can refresh the item shop, but it costs money, so refreshing multiple times to fish for the item Skul needs has a good chance to bankrupt the run.

I find I spend a lot of time trying to gather just one inscription type, rarely getting what I need, and the run falling apart. The game play loop is not nearly as satisfying as Dead Cells, where every run feels viable because I'm not hamstrung by a random item shop not giving me what I need. Even on higher difficulties where Dead Cells removes healing potions and enhances enemies, it provides the player with more power and items, not less. The game knows it's asking a lot, so it provides the tools. At times, despite it's cute aesthetic, Skul feels contemptuous of my choices.

I want to enjoy this game - the art and sounds are great - but the game itself gets in the way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are looking for a rougelite that challenges you in every way from thinking about your build to reflexes and strategy then this is the game for you!
In every element of gameplay, Skul excels.

Your limited inventory makes every item you pick up and remove very meaningful.
You only get 9 slots to make or break your run. While each item's base benefits are helpful, each item has 2 inscriptions. Inscriptions are like set bonuses from other games. Most other games require complete sets to get any meaningful benefit from the set. Not Skul. In Skul you will be able to craft entry, mid, or complete level set bonuses to get the exact effect you want (barring bad random drops/selections don't kneecap your intended build).
You want help with your health? Relic.
You want to just smash stuff with one hit? Arms.
You want to rain meteors upon your foes to squash them like the bugs they are? Artifact.
Each inscription set caters to a specific playstyle and build. Careful attention to your Inscriptions will yield VERY satisfying results and likely help carry you to victory.

The ability to swap skulls mid combat is beautiful. It gives you access to a completely different playstyle with the touch of a button.
You want to romp around like a titan? Power Skulls.
You want to zip back and forth across the battlefield? Speed Skulls
You like a more balanced approach? Balance Skulls!
The ability to adjust your approach to any fight instantly is incredibly novel in the roguelite genre. Usually if your build doesn't work for the situation, you are dead and have to restart. Skul allows you to bring two toolsets to each fight with the Skull swapping mechanic. Each skull offers a unique experience and feeling.

The ONLY negative thing I can say about Skul is that the story isnt continuous after restarting/dying. I was spoiled with Hades (the story continues and further develops every time you fail or succeed a run). Skul only progresses with progress. Which is fine. Progress feels meaningful and rewarding, but the story wont enthrall you. Its just the premise for the game mostly. Having said that, I did enjoy the story.

While the story might not captivate you or feel very novel, the gameplay alone is worth EVERY penny you pay for Skul. 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t
