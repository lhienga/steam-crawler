\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter nearly 400 hours and from the release of the game I'm finally writing a review. Skul is amazing. Everything about it just oozes personality and creativity. The visual style of it is incredible and the story is surprisingly good.
Skulls:
Every skull is extremely unique with it's own look and play style. There are some pretty basic ones like sword nut then there's stuff like rockstar and Yaksha. Both with incredibly flashy and crazy moves. While some skulls are much better than others, the average balancing of everything is great. There isn't a single skull in the game that can't beat the hardest difficulty.
Items:
The items used to be very simple; usually giving a boost to damage or health. However, now there are items that blow up the entire map or revive you after you die. There are so many builds that it takes forever to go through everything and that's exactly why I have so much time int the game.
Enemies:
There's a massive variety of enemies but eventually they can get stale by the time you have hundreds of hours in the game. That's where the dark mirror comes in. It adds multiple new enemies to every area and they are much deadlier and usually more visually interesting than those in the base game. All around the enemies are great and mechanically are never to over bearing.
Adventurers:
These are the mini bosses in the middle of the areas, they aren't very difficult until later but all of them fight in a way where they work together well. In chapter 3 you fight 3 adventurers and it never feels like too much.
Bosses: All of the bosses are very unique, they all fight differently and all have a theme to them. In the dark mirror they're all overhauled and made much more difficult and fun. For example, the final boss has an entirely different second phase. (This is excluding the new boss added because spoilers)
Conclusion: This game is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ great. While it's not for everyone, especially due to the immense difficulty, for those who enjoy a challenging roguelite it's perfect.
