\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the better RPGs I've played in a long time. In one sentence; the game is straight-forward.

The Good:
The elemental system isn't so contrived that you'd ignore it.
Equipment has ranks, so upgarding is a breeze.
The internal math isn't so complex as to make stats meaningless.
Enemies don't level-scale so you actually feel the progress you've made.
For the most part the story isn't crap.

The Bad:
It's not very original. Shocking for a game that seems to be a love letter to just about every RPG trope in existance, I know, utterly shocking. At some points though the game just won't let up with the twists, and even the ending is hampered because the creator just doesn't know when to stop and let his own creation breathe.

The Ugly:
Robb needs to ♥♥♥♥ a tire iron with his teeth.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t100% this game a while ago and apparently never wrote a review

very much so a game for turn-based rpg fans, not so much for people new to the genre. it's not super hard or anything nor is it some really deep experience, but to appreciate the heart of this game, i think it's important to play other games in its genre to see its inspiration.

the story is generally pretty good, but certain decisions late in the story (twists and developments) kinda put me off. the ending left me somewhat unsatisfied, although this could be bc it might be setting up for a sequel. some plot points feel like they show up and vanish randomly, and certain twists kylian betraying the party and eventually becoming the final boss, glenn's past life not being lenne's bf but rather her forced marriage partner, etc. this isn't to say all the twists are predictable (i think that big ba'thraz one was really well done), but the huge plot developments are kinda easy to spot.

characters also don't really talk very naturally; regardless of a character's origin, they mostly speak the same. glenn should have a rough manner of speech having grown up in the slums, but he talks identically to lenne and robb, a princess and nobleman.

I don't wanna sound like a hater because i genuinely love this game. I just think that among what it does well, there's still a lot of things it could have done better in its narrative and presentation.

gameplay is really good and difficulty is entirely customizable. as for balance, your party is heavily unbalanced with some characters being utter garbage and others being way better than any of the other options. viktor and (late-game) sienna will carry while robb, magnolia, and tomke will never do anything of worth unless you build your whole party around them. tbh that isn't a huge deal to me given how many party members they give you, but after a certain point, it feels unsatisfying to stray from the objective best party members bc your alternatives are generally just worse.

the way i see it, the best party structure in the early game is viktor, glenn, kylian, and lenne in the front, then when you reach mid/late game, it becomes viktor, sienna, ba'thraz, and the remaining members cycling between glenn and mikah (lenne and amalia as backup members). i really cannot overstate how utterly broken sienna is late in the game. she does the most damage while having so much agility that she's just constantly acting. it's frankly absurd even post-nerfs.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will amend this review to a positive one once these issues are resolved:

1- The game did a great job marking main story objectives on the map, except when it comes to Maddeleine washing ashore on your isle, off screen, in an area of the map the game never gives you an incentive you to check or revisit otherwise. For f's sake, I spent hours scouring every inch of the world trying to find out what the hell the objective "Solve the Nhysa Gates" meant, only to find out I had to talk to someone who has never been introduced before, is never marked on the map, and is in a part of the area that you have no reason to check out. Either add a marker on the map, or update one of the NPCs near the center of Hermit's Isle to mention that they saw someone land on the eastern shore. You did a great job leaving breadcrumbs to guide players to where they need to go next on their main quest. Why stop at Maddeleine's first appearance?

2- If you accidentally press the button to board your airship, and then press it again or land at the same area, you will be taken to the entrance, rather than where you took off. If I were in a part of an area that takes ages to get to and this happened, it's annoying to retrace my steps because of a single misclick.

3- I often find myself stuck with a lot of gems that I have no use for, and a limited gem inventory that deprives me of collecting new ones when full. Many gems are extremely circumstantial and I see no reason to put them on any of my gear, so I try to sell them off, and surprise, you can't sell gems. At least, up to the point in the game where I am, I have found no way to get rid of the gems I got.

4- The Megaloworm's demise is one of the most underwhelming conclusions to a story I've ever seen. You went through so much trouble to describe its significance to the world's lore, but I guess it ended up being cut content. Even the last few lines of dialogue involving it feel extremely rushed. At least add a Sky Armor fight with its heart or something.

I love this game so far, but it has many small annoyances that slowly piled up over time; the Maddeleine incident was the last straw. I almost quit out of sheer frustration when I finally found her.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, I originaly had a huge review for this game trying to cover everything I thought about it in detail, but I found out steam has a character limit on reviews, so here's the abridged version:

- The artwork -
There aren't any major flaws in it. It's creative and easy on the eye.

- The music -
It's ok, but uninspiring. Nothing in praticular stands out, and some tracks, like the goblin village music, are a real pain to listen to.

- The gameplay -
There are some good elements to it, mainly the combat, but a lot of the supporting features are cumbersome and messy, especially the crystals mechanic. The flying armors don't add anything of value to the game either.

- The Story -
This is the weakest part of the game. The narration constantly put me out of the experience. The story is a mess with a terrible pacing that pulls so many twist it start to become funny. The wrost part is the characters' because they say all of their line with the same static expression, which make feel much less alive. By the time I reached the end I was too little invested in the plot to actually are about what happens.

– Summary –
I think the simplest way for me to describe the way I feel about the game is: "You tried." This is a game that tries to make a fresh take on turn-based RPGs, but puts too many limits on its systems for it to engaging. This is a game that tries to tell a complex story with morally grey characters all around, but ends up thumbling on its own plot. This is a game that tries to breath life to a constructed world, but is too afraid to break the mold and stand out.
Despite the many hours that I poured into this title, in the end, I don't feel like I gained anything of value of it. Even the entertainment aspect of is doubtful. There are far better ways for you the spend your time and money than this title. Still, I hope the developer could learn from those faults, so that their next game could really be something worth buying.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly, story wise it's pretty solid as far as JRPG's go, it looks fantastic and rewards you for poking around in everyone's house and quizzing everyone you find in dialog, normal (Not mech) combat is rewarding, allows you to kill things in a variety of ways and lets you take control of your party development to suit how you want to play, it even has a Suikoden style collect characters/companions to flesh out your base, all sounds great right?

So here's the bad news, firstly the mech combat is just not needed and honestly very tedious, it's simplified compared to your normal party combat and just feels like it was crammed in to give a bit of flavor, it wasn't needed and detracted from the combat system. I have no idea what the weapons upgrade/gems system was meant to be for, it seemed hugely redundant and unrewarding, also introducing a character to manage aggro or threat at the end of the game seemed like a weird time to introduce a new concept that we've managed without for the vast majority of the game.

I'd probably have played this again, done all the "hidden" bosses etc had the mech combat not been a thing, instead I quit just before the end because it became the main combat medium and it was such a drag.

If you love JRPG's this may be for you, definitely worth a pick up if you're on the fence when it's on sale but some really weird design choices left me wishing I hadn't bothered.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI grew up playing RPGs from the 90s and early 2000's. I loved Chrono Trigger, Secret of Mana, Final Fantasy, Terranigma, Dragon Quest, Breath of Fire, Shin Megami Tensei, etc; the list goes on. This game made by a single individual captures the magic and nostalgia of those games but also adds some much needed quality of life to out dated mechanics for the modern gamer. It has been some of the most fun I've had playing an RPG in a very long time. It's perfect for newcomers of the genre as well as old veterans like myself.

I didnt think I would appreciate how liberating it feels to play an RPG without a grind. This game has no level ups or EXP in the traditional sense. Instead, you kill bosses or complete side objectives to receive Grimoire Shards. These shards act as points you use for skills/stats/passives instead of a traditional level up system. This gives the player a sense of wonder and motivation for exploration as you want to get as many of these grimoire shards as possible.

The story itself is quite unoriginal but does not take itself too seriously at times. It has a serious tone and dark themes but is also not afraid to relax and make fun of itself. Your party members are not just bystanders in a hero's journey, they're your friends, family and will go through much tribulations together.\t\t\t\t\t
