\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review may offend many, but it is my own take on the game.
Summary: This game is overly hype, it is not as great as many have said. After 100% the game, I regret spending so much time on it.
Game Rating:
Graphic - 7/10 (It is jrpg graphic so you will see boxes)
Music - 8/10 (it is smooth enough to make me fall asleep)
Character Development Plot - 3/10 (It is pretty bad)
Story Development Plot - 4/10 (Something along the line of: "Your stomach build up poop for days, and it is time for you to go. On your way to home, there is a lot missed opportunity to use hotel toilet with AC, and toilet music. The end")
Battle Mechanic - 3/10 (Player need to think about the strategy to use for some enemies, but mainly debuff & buff then poop everywhere)
Non-Battle Mechanic - 2/10 (it is useless because player can complete 100% the game without needing to use it)
Quest Listing System - 1.5/10 (it only track existing quest and completed quest, it would be great to have available quest list)
Save System - 6/10 (cannot delete save files)
Inventory System - 2/10 (it is really buggy, information on the item can be wrong or does not make sense)
Final Rating - "I cannot ask for refund because i realise too late."/10 or "trash game"/10
SPOILER AHEAD:

The character development in this game does not make sense at all.
Character 1: Spoiled brat -> Decent human (Ok. I am digging it.)
Character 2: Depressed -> Give up on live (wdf)
Character 3: Ambitious -> Prostitute -> god (bruh...)
Character 4: Cheerful -> Going mental -> I am fine now. (man...what just happen)
At some point, character development lose its meaning and the flow of concept.

The story development of Chained Echoes did not hit their initial target, then roll the credit.
The story main goal: "Let fight evil."
The end goal: "Yes."
The game introduce "The True King" role where super powerful monster is shown to hype you up. That's about it. It is like Yu Gi Oh mention about the Egyptian gods card, and never use it through out the whole anime.

The number of time where the game force the concept of plot twist on the player is too often which make it less surprising.

The mood of the whole game does not match the ending.
Through out the game, you see:
- people killing people
- people failed to honor the oath, and die
- human experiement
- full metal alchemist
- Family betrayal
But the ending, "yes, you cannot undo your evil, but live". (????)

The battle mechanic is very limited. You are give a team of 8, but you will end up only using 4-6 characters for all battles. 2 healer, 2 buffer/debuffer, 2 dps. Every battle is like tug-o-war, maintaining the pointer at the right position.

The non-battle mechanic aims to boost your character power, but the game is easy enough that it does not see any play.

Throughout the whole game, I didn't use the quest listing system so I don't know why it is there in the first place.

The inventory system is pretty screwed up, where skill does not make sense. Item displayed information is wrong, which make you throw wrong item away.
After finishing this game, I am quite confident that my blood pressure went up.
I will say that this is just an average game, I am pretty sure that there are other existing games which cost the same price, and shine brighter than Chained Echoes in all ways.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is almost perfect, a solid 9. It's a blend of all pixel games from the past like Chrono Trigger, Final Fantasy 6, Secret of Mana and a slew of pop culture references. It's very well priced and if you take your time and don't cheat yourself out of the experience like puzzles and such you are in for a good 80 hours on your first run for a full clear. New Game + is not available yet but when it is you can tack on a second run of 20-30 hours probably. The game is still actively being developed which is a major +. The writing is extremely polished and characters are developed.

Only negatives is that you don't get all characters and classes fast enough for my taste. A lot of stuff is gated from the Acts and the game feels more linear than it should be which is not a terrible issue.

The game Discord server feels somewhat mildly woke, but it doesn't translate too much into the game. LGBT content in game is well integrated so it doesn't distract from enjoyment.

Strong recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t
