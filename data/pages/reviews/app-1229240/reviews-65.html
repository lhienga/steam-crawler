\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty solid game so far. I picked up this game when I saw a video praising it for being very forward thinking in what players want in a game (non-grindy, auto-heal, good exp system).
It has delivered all those things so far!
------
After finishing the whole game, I def recommend this game to people that enjoy turn-based combat. My only problem is that grinding is interesting in this game. In other games, leveling and experience is intuitive. If you're weak just kill lots of enemies and try the boss again. In this game, instead of traditional grinding, you'd grind side quests and explore optional areas. I don't think this is a bad thing, but it just wasn't intuitive that that's how you grind.
Plot was pretty captivating aside from the ending haha. I didn't feel like I had proper closure.\t\t\t\t\t

Good gameplay with strange progression
The gem of this game is definitely the gameplay, it's challenging and strategic in the right ways while allowing the player to go all out in each encounter. But I feel the way exp progression is structured doesn't justify playing this on hard meaning you'll always get 1 SP regardless of what you fight excluding bosses. And you'll probably constantly switch between easy to get through the filler then hard for the bosses. Which is a real shame because the monsters in between are fun to fight as well but it gets hampered by me not wanting to sit down for 10 minutes each fight when i can easily just walk through the place to get to the next boss.

To be honest, my experience after 8 hours has been so much better after the first and the gameplay on it's own should merit a recommend but i just don't want to keep juggling between being pragmatic about grinding and enjoying the difficulty of each new encounter.

First Impression after clearing first dungeon
Game is alright however the encounters although handcrafted feel random. What i mean by this is that usually RPGs reward you with hints to what you'll fight next. This is usually in the form of chest loot (antidote = poison boss, xx res = xx boss) or character acquisition (what skills does this new guy have?) or what is the shop selling? (oh antidote only? must be a poison heavy area). Normally these hints don't affect gameplay much when you can simply grind to get over the boss but in a game where your resources are tight, you'll probably unknowingly choke yourself by getting the wrong skill - in my case i took flying killer and beast killer since they stood out the most but were ultimately useless for boss 1. Honestly hope the next stages are better put together with the player experience in mind cause an easy mode switch to compensate for a lack of thought into your puzzle is plain lazy.Granted i'm being extra harsh because of the good things I've been hearing about this game\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is by far one of the best classic turn-based RPG out there, period.It is definitely not without its faults but the overall experience is so satisfying and entertaining that the flaws end up being barely more than nitpicks.What I think the game excels at is bringing that nostalgic golden era of RPGs into the modern gaming scape, with a series of clever quality of life changes, one above all is the full recovery after every battle, which eliminates the extra management of healing items after every skirmish AND the Grimoire Shards used to power up the characters, which are story related... that in turn eliminate the concept of grinding, unless it's for spoils...!The story is well crafted and while it's clear the inspiration it takes from Final Fantasy 6, Xenogears and Chrono Trigger to name a few (with more than one wink at them during the game xD) it never feels like a parody or a by-product of them: the pacing is great and it most definitely keeps you engaged, throwing a few curve balls here and there that honestly I never saw coming!... (the ending might leave some people skeptical but that goes with personal preference I think...!) in short, Chained Echoes is its own beast and it's really REALLY good!The characters, for the vast majority, are intriguing and interesting, both in relation to the story and in combat: they are all very distinct and bring something to table. Admittedly some come in too late to be fully appreciated or explored but that's true for any RPG I guess xDThere's definitely no lack of side-quest or hunting for secrets which in return grant nice rewards.The game doesn't feel difficult at all, even the toughest fights really only require a good strategy or a different set up if they cause problems, which is also thanks to the battle system, introducing the Overdrive Bar. It boils down to use specific type of skills to keep the bar into a specific range that grants extra defense and attack. Of course the enemies will try to mess up with that xD If anything, I wish more was done with it, a few of the secret bosses mess with it REAL good and that provided quite the challenge!The score is on point, I really really liked it a lot and should not be overlooked as it add quite a lot to the overall experience.The only few stinkers I think are the weapons, armors and crystals upgrade system: it feels clunky and needlessly overcomplicated, I wish it was streamlined a little bit to be honest but it's really not necessary to complete the game...! AND at times I thought the writing in some rare instances could use a second or third review but again, it's all nitpicks.I can hardly recommed this game enough, whether you are a veteran of the genre and you are starving for good ol' turn-based-story-rich RPG or you are looking for a good one to start with, Chained Echoes is definitely the best currently available!(Did I mention this was developed by ONE PERSON??) O.O"\t\t\t\t\t
