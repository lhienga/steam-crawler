\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game. I'd compare it to CrossCode, both games are pure gems, with the difference that Chained Echoes has turn-based combat system, which might be appealing for some and repulsive for others. For a game that takes place in a cliché medieval fantasy land, I was positively surprised with the amount twists the story has. It's a must-play in my books, although one thing the game lacks is replayability value because of the game's turn-based combat nature. As good as the game is, once you finish the game you'll probably will never pick it up again. Matthias Linda (game creator) is working on an New Game+ update for the game, maybe then it'll be worth giving the game another playthrough, we just have to wait and see what the man cooks.




Also, ♥♥♥♥ those sliding orb puzzles

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile some folks out there are technically correct that some things like the gem system are overcomplicated, or that some of the systems of the game aren't that important, I still absolutely and completely loved this game and would recommend it to anyone. It's a love letter to 16 bit rpg's, and succeeds. Early on, it was a little overwhelming, but things settled down, the game opened up, the story improved and improved and I couldn't put it down.

Great on the deck, great on the PC, great docked to the TV. As a 44 year old dude who rarely has the time commit to rpg's, this was *well* worth the time. Don't overanalyze it, just play it, enjoy it, and respect the developer who put his heart and soul into this game.

This ended up being my 2023 GOTY\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've nearly 100% this game and gotta say it's a complete victim to over hype, I don't care if it was made by one person, an un fun game is an unfun game.

-Tries way too hard to be chrono trigger, literally 1:1 scene copy+paste at some points.
-characters are unlikable & you don't connect with them at all
-the opening story is convoluted, the ending is so short lived & anti-dramatic I was kinda left asking "is that it?"
-Battle system is cookie-cutter turn based combat, no need to grind because levels don't exist, only 3 levels per skill which don't really do that much. not only that, it forces you to make decisions based on the overdrive bar, the animations are re used and boring.
-sky armour exploration is cool, but the battles aren't, it's pointless and just forces you to use it against certain enemies that are almost unkillable on foot.
-The crystal system, a common complaint, is so poorly explained and convoluted that most people just skip used it, it just needs removed altogether as you don't even need it.
-REMOVE DIFFICULTY SETTINGS, especially easy mode. add a new game+ for Hard mode...

It's fun for the first 10 hours or so until you realise it's just more and more of the same and never ever gets interesting or changed up in a meaningful way, I'd say get this game for £10 but it's no where near worth the £25 price tag.

tldr: Another 2D RPG Chrono Trigger "Love Letter" wanna be. Go play I am Setsuna or Lost Sphear, they do a much better job if that's what you're after\t\t\t\t\t
