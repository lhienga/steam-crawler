\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWonderful game! I had a blast playing this. It was nice to see a beautiful pixel RPG that is turn based and does not hold your hand. Some sections and enemies can be pretty tough; however, that is the beauty of it! The cast of characters are great and each bring unique strategies to the table, so sometimes you may just need to change up your party formation. It added a little strategy that was fun. Grated, some party members (I'm looking at you Sienna) can just be so OP they never leave your party. I had a blast when I was playing, even doing side quests, exploring, all the little tidbits you get with RPGs. I never once felt like, "Well, I'm a little bored of this. Better turn it off for the night". So.. 100% recommendation.

... But wait, there's more! And this is the negative. Because it is not a fully pleasant experience, the good just happens to outweigh the bad. As mentioned in a few other reviews, the Sky Armor parts can SUCK. The travel function of having them is nice, but the battles can be a pain in the ass. Also, for me, the story was a bit bland. I was just sucked in by the environments and landscapes they created along with the colorful cast of characters (and make sure you get the miss-able ones, too!).

If this was too long and you want some handy-dandy bullet points for reference, here you go:

The Good
  • Beautiful graphics.
  • Unique and colorful characters.
  • Plenty of skills and builds for your characters.
  • Great environments with plenty of areas to explore and discover.
  • Not your standard level up system; uses skill points to enhance skills and stats instead.
  • Sky Armors (transportation).

The Bad
  • Crystals to level up weapons and armor can be a bit of a pain.
  • Requires some serious grinding if you want to get all your character's stats and skills.
  • Story is forgettable.
  • Sky Armors (battling).

So, buy it! Or don't. It's your money, but I definitely recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably one of the biggest disappointments I've ever experienced in gaming.

The game is massively front-loaded. There's a lot that Chained Echoes does to switch up the usual JRPG formula, and it starts to show that off in its early game. Unfortunately by the time you're in the second act you've seen every interesting enemy the game will ever have to offer you and what you're left with is basic elemental/type weaknesses that never change and will be consistently exploited in the same way.

Because you regain all your health and mana after every fight every fight ends up playing out in the exact same way once you've gotten through the early game grind of learning the necessary skills to have a consistent strategy.

Experience points are non-existent with level ups instead being earned through milestones and plot progression, which keeps the game fun and balanced in the early game before ruining all semblance of balance in the late game, where completing side-quests makes your characters so strong that strategy is essentially a non-issue.

The gem system, while cool in concept, is ultimately just a slog. It's tedious to upgrade equipment and I found myself actively avoiding doing so just because I didn't want to go through the hassle of unslotting and re-equipping all of my gems for a new set of equipment.

Sky-armour is just a mess. Take everything good about the game's combat and then remove it. There's very little variety in terms of skill setups, since your skills and stats are tied to a very limited set of equipment. Unlike the regular combat there is actually an experience points system here, which requires you to use each piece of equipment to max out your stats, doing so will mean you have no skills when you initially swap them out, making combat much more difficult and incredibly boring. So you'll want to spend a while grinding the most boring combat system the game has to offer so the late game is less of a slog, because oh-boy does the late game throw a lot of sky-armour battles at you, and no... they don't get any more fun.

The artwork in the game is alright, with the character sprites and especially their portraits looking stiff, and the lack of expressions in the portraits make the already terribly written dialogue feel even more stale.

The story is an absolute joke, with the ending being downright insulting. The game is left open so it can sequel-bait without resolving several of the big setups within the main story.

In short, this game is absolutely terrible. The story and writing are among the worst I've experienced out of any JRPG I've played and the combat has many interesting ideas but fails to execute on any of them beyond the first act. If you're craving modern JRPG action, this ain't it.\t\t\t\t\t
