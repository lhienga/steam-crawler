\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely recommend this game! I'm a big fan of the RPG genre but I'm often less enthusiastic about JRPGs because most of them involve so much time and grinding. This game brings a unique system that strikes a really nice balance. You "level up" and get new skills only by defeating bosses or completing side quests. Grinding random enemies mostly just gets you materials and money. There is a lot of optional stuff to do outside the main quest if you want to grind, but the rewards you get from doing so will always feel like a bonus, not a necessity. If you just stick to the plot and ignore everything else, I don't think you'd ever feel underpowered.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know if anyone's going to read this review, and I'll try and keep this short. To be brief, this game does a lot really really well. Steam says I put 71 hours into this, and I would say it was worth it. However, it is just shy of being perfect and it's a damn shame, too.

The main issues I have with this game, is that there is too much complexity for the sake of complexity. My biggest headaches came whenever I had to upgrade my armor and crystals which honestly was probably a quarter of my play time. It would have been so much easier to design some kind of mechanic that would simply easily transfer the crystals from one piece of armor to another, rather than having to remove them, and then find them again. Also, not being able to sell or dispose of crystals was incredibly annoying. I had so much clutter by the end, I almost gave up entirely on trying to boost my armor at all. Hell, I honestly would've been fine if there simply hadn't been a crystal system to begin with, but if you enjoyed that stuff, then I'm happy you enjoyed this game more than I did.

This game didn't need 12 characters. I'm sorry, but trying to keep everyone balanced felt like such a chore by the end. I don't think I used three characters at all, and I was waiting for that moment in the final tower like in FF6 where you have to split the party into three groups and work together. That moment never happened.

The over complexity also extends to the main narrative, I'm afraid. It clearly wears its influences on its sleeve (90's Final Fantasies, Xenogears, Chrono Trigger, Suikoden 1 & 2), but unfortunately the story for whatever reason seemed to come up short of any of those games. I'm writing this after just having beaten it, so I can't explain why exactly that's the case, but for whatever reason, the emotional moments in this game just didn't have the same impact as those games did. It's also entirely possible that I'm looking at those games with huge nostalgia googles, so YMMV.

Minor gripe, and this is REALLY nitpicky, but I think one thing that would've helped from an artistic design point would've been to use actual illustrations for character dialog boxes, instead of pixel art. I think it would've gone a long way to help give more life to some of these characters, rather than seeing extra sharp pixels that felt more in line with a cozy game like Stardew Valley, than in a sprawling epic.

Ultimately this was a really well made grandiose game. It's hard for me to go into so much of the nitty gritty of what works and what doesn't, but definitely check this out if you're a fan of turn-based JRPG. It's just more a 4/5 score game that fell just short of being the masterpiece I had hoped it would be.\t\t\t\t\t

Dialogue and story is a huge part of a game like this since you can do only so much visually with sprite work on the characters however, most of the dialogue here is only really to describe the world and how a character reacts to information in the most blatant way possible. Dialogue only sets up information we need to know as the story progresses into the next scene probably the only scene of anything not world-building related is the tavern were they are talking about drinking and how they like their drinks. This early part of the story is really tame in what it sets up with it's characters and unable to keep me interested which is a shame as i'm sure it will get better but its main game-play loop keeps me from wanting to explore further.

Leveling in this game is definitely my least favorite part of the game I'm used to having to constantly encounter enemies to grind up levels but with such large values needed to even get skills upgraded or the resources to upgrade weapons and armor it feels a bit discouraged early on. This normally wouldn't be an issue if the enemies didn't scale up so very fast and already be super spongy making more than just a little difficult. Lacking any reason to stick around to continue fight enemies in sections as the side content is pretty spare in the first act making me feel incredibly under-leveled as every fight with normal enemies became a incredibly difficult task even gathering material to upgrade equipment becomes not an option by just how hard encounters are.

All the fights are long very long and with the damage of most enemies able to reduce my party's health to a third i spend most of the fight healing rather trying to do damage. Taking advantage of debuffs while working at the beginning has been a fool's errand after leaving tormund as it just leaves you open to more damage. I do enjoy having 8 party members but being able to only use 4 at a time and if they are defeated locking their switch out of the battle is really unhelpful the only benefit switching has is lowering the overdrive bar.
