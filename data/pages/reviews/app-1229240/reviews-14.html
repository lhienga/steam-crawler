\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game right after Sea of Stars.

Both games were excellent but leaning on quite different strengths.
Chained Echoes looks goodish visually but isn't anywhere near as striking as Sea of Stars, but its musical score is tremendously better. Its story is also deeper, more intricate, but the ending falls abruptly and flat.

The combat in Chained Echoes is equally fun to Sea of Star's combat, loses the real time QTE part that was engaging, but is much deeper and allows for many strategic approach.

However, it is bogged down by terrible balancing issues. About 10 hours into the game, I got stuck because of a fight against random regular enemies, I kept dying over and over and it was clear there was nothing I could do to beat them. Again, I am talking about regular overworld enemies, not bosses or anything like that. This was extremely frustrating, I ended up turning the difficulty settings and then it became a breeze for the rest of the game, effortless almost.

There has to be a balance between "literally impossible to win because you get 2-shotted or even get dumpstered first turn before you can even act" and "you can win while aimlessly facerolling your Steam Deck". This game didn't achieve that and it is its biggest flaw.\t\t\t\t\t

The combat mechanics also have more than enough unique elements to make it feel very different from other turn-based RPGs. If you like turn-based RPGs, I cannot recommend picking up this game enough!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
-A mash up of SNES JRPG games (Chrono Trigger, FF3, FF2)
-Pretty easy to get into, not too many complex battle systems to learn
-Nice music, pixel art, definitely would've been a good game if it was released during the SNES days.
-Game is surprisingly challenging at times.
-If you die, you can retry the battle without having to walk back

Cons:
-Annoying Overdrive bar in the battles. It's unique to this game and I suppose it makes it so that you can't keep spamming the same moves over and over without having to deal with a penalty.
-No characters really stood out and the story didn't draw me in. It was a like combo of stories.
-Game isn't really that good at explaining stuff like crystals or the quest board.
-Battles sometimes relies a lot on RNG, for example a mob can AOE three times in a row and wipe your team and sometimes he won't, just random choice of the available moves they can do.
-there really aren't much useful combos on crystal buffs despite there being so many crystals
-nothing to do after you beat the final boss
-no super secrets to unlock or crazy side quests that yield great rewards

Overall:
I'd still recommend it to fans of good ol days of SNES Rpg's, a good 30-40 hour ride.

Tips:
-the quest board has chain rewards that give you emblems and shards, you have to press another button to redeem those
-do not auto optimize crystals
-crystal buffs don't all stack, (MAG, ATK, MND up) do stack but (Crit dam up, health drain, etc) don't
-managing your overdrive bar for boss battles is essential even if it costs using overdrive reducing items
