\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game when I first started playing it so bad, but I just couldn't. The first few hours of the game were really promising, a simple nostalgia heavy somewhat generic JRPG, but it eventually just fell apart and ended as a complete disaster.

Once the game "starts" to open up, it quickly shows you all the side content that you can get by exploring each map, but after the first zone you just get hit by constant "This isn't the right way!" every time you try to take a side path, which just leads to disappointment and frustration, thanks for making me not want to explore the world I guess.

The combat system is typical turn based RPG combat except with a slight twist of having a bar that fills with skill usage and when it's in the green section it gives you buffs, but when it's in red it gives you debuffs. The only way to decrease the gauge is by using specified skills that are randomly chosen while the gauge is green or red, or by defending or swapping your party member out. When the gauge is red the damage you take goes up, this can either be the difference between dying to an attack or completely negligible because your health and mp fully replenish when a fight ends, either via winning or running away. The problem with that is it's either completely fine because the RNG gives you what you need to keep it in the green, or the fight is short enough that you can ignore it completely, or it makes things much more tedious because you're on a difficult fight and you need to avoid dipping into the red and just have to waste turns by using skills that won't help to push the gauge back, nothing quite like having to use steal twice in a row because it's your only utility skill and that keeps popping up as the chosen skill type. Also there's no traditional EXP leveling system, you get shards that you can use to learn new skills, get new passives, or give you a couple raw stat upgrades, and every few shards you use you get a bit of an upgrade to various stats, but the primary source of shards is boss fights, there are a few you get outside boss fights, so you're basically always as strong as you can be for a section, most of your actual strength progression comes from gear with an upgrade and augment system that results in you not actually using a new piece of gear cause you probably can't upgrade it yet and your upgraded old piece is probably better.

And then there's the writing. I will try to avoid spoilers, but as someone who personally doesn't care about them, I often struggle with recognizing what people consider to be spoilers, so be warned.

The writing in this game starts off as fairly generic fantasy RPG stuff, the continent it's set in is split between 3 kingdoms and they've been warring for complete control for 150 years, one of them acquires a super weapon and uses it to force treaty that it breaks one year later, we shall hereafter refer to them as The Evil Empire™. One of the main characters is the princess of The Evil Empire™ who left it to learn more about The People™ to be a Proper Princess™. This is all fine and dandy for the most part, nothing special but nothing really bad either, but it both to properly use its pieces and just throws them out the window for various twists. There's a complete lack of factionalism in The Evil Empire™ that every JRPG that this game is very clearly inspired by, often just straight up aping, very much has, every member of The Evil Empire™ has collectively decided that the princess has betrayed them without a second thought. You also barely try and stop The Evil Empire™ as a variety of other threats are introduced to be deemed more important than the last, ancient sealed evil of untold proportions? We've got that, but it's quickly shoved aside for something else. You engage with the Empire several times and every single one of them ends unsatisfying. We took their superweapon away from them? Great, except they've got 4 magical elemental generals (archfiends anyone?) who we straight up see annihilate entire armies on their own, we fight exactly one of them near the end after a scene of our characters going "we can't possibly fight them!" another character showing up to hold them, promptly being defeated, and us having to fight the general anyways. In fact I think that's the only actually important member of The Evil Empire™ we actually defeat, the rest are left to unsatisfying cutscenes. I guess it should have been obvious to me when a character tells you, somewhat early on too, that their ENTIRE backstory is "My younger sister is in a coma because the entire slums raped her one day" that the writer really had no clue what they were doing, but I had hoped that it would be an exception to the rule. To sum up, the writing is very clearly a blended mess of various JRPGs, and even manga and anime, the writer really enjoyed, from Xenogears, Final Fantasy 6, Berserk, even Final Fantasy 12. Actually I'm realizing that the story of this game is almost the same as FF12's story, just if the Occuria were more important and if Vayne was even more in the background, and if you added a mess of filler plots along the way. There's even a character whose entire personality can only be summed as as "Written by someone who just really likes anime." In her first actual interaction with the main cast, to show how quirky and how bad at talking she is, she goes "(Oh no, they're staring at me, I should say something to them) Stop staring at me! I understand that I'm incredibly attractive, but I'm only 14 you perverts!" A line that could only be written by someone who knows that JRPGs and anime have a character archetype that says ♥♥♥♥ like this, but doesn't understand that this character archetype is just cringe and annoying and doesn't actually need to exist lmao. She doesn't get better btw, everything that comes out of her mouth is just cringe, somehow made a character just as bad as Robb, a very early character who just hates the poors and doesn't have much depth outside that.

This review is poorly written and worded, I don't write em ever and I mostly just stream of consciousness my thoughts to a friend, but I was so burned by this game I felt like I needed to share that in the off-chance there's someone else who might share my thoughts and save themselves the time and money on this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game started out great: storyline had some good hooks, gameplay and QOL features were nice. After about 9-10 hours, I quickly found progression halting. The overdrive system became cumbersome with RNG: if you don't have the exact right skills or items necessary to manage it, you lose. In this game, you have a maximum potential power for any given point in the game. This becomes an absurd limitation, because regular enemies become like boss fights, and fighting them nets you virtually zero progress, so it becomes a "search around and find treasure chests hidden behind walls" kind of experience in order to get certain rewards that can tip the scales. I find it more frustrating than its worth.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros: Wide cast of diverse characters, a battle system that requires some thinking, many options for customization and strategy, nice big world with a helpful map and many rewards for thorough exploration.
Cons: The story can be a little confusing in the beginning - many place names and political players thrown at you at once. A little like Final Fantasy Tactics in that regard. We all have fond memories of FFT but honestly, the original PS1 translation was ♥♥♥♥ and we were all a little confused whether we admit it or not. This one is likewise a little confusing at first though by mid-game it shouldn't be so bad.

Not everyone will like the overdrive system, there are times when I find it tedious but overall I like having an incentive to avoid mindless button mashing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI generally think the game is a bit overhyped, but it's a recommend for me, since what it does well is good enough to overcome a lot of the flaws.

The game is enormously ambitious in terms of scope. It attempts to cover plots ranging from religious, political, interpersonal, and mythological conflict. The cast of characters is ginormous and the lore behind the world is immense. Characters are consistent, if not a bit flat. The game allows for a lot of exploration with side quests, big open maps, tons of optional content, and various systems players can dive into.

Each individual character has their own skills and party compositions are almost endless with the sheer bulk of different things you can put together. The game avoids a lot of frustrating pitfalls from the older RPGs though--full healing after every battle allows for them to feel more significant, limited restrictions on travel and teleporting allows for less time just running around, and clear map and quest markers, mixed with a lot of unmarked content that can be engaged in, makes deep exploration an option, but not a requirement. On the surface, all of this looks like the components for a fantastic RPG.

But where the game starts to choke a bit is in implementation. The plot is paced very poorly. I'll avoid spoilers here, but what started as a fairly-well paced beginning quickly devolves. Character archs are rushed to the extreme. Dialogue becomes limited to people making dramatic declarations that feel fairly soulless. So many twists are incorporated that each one starts feeling meaningless. The deep conflicts established politically end up grossly oversimplified, and the moral dilemmas the game tries to explore fall flat in favor of moralistic and simplistic statements of "we should do good things!" and "humans will make progress!"

On the surface level, if you look at the combat, it looks like it's going to be complicated. You have different mechanics to pay attention to, different aspects to explore... but it's actually simple. Chokingly simple. The thing is, the ideal choice is blatantly obvious in most circumstances, and making any other choice basically screws you. It's extremely punishing if you do the wrong thing and there's almost never any way to recover from it. Choices become very limited, and I used the exact same strategy, with the same skills, for a vast, vast majority of the game, even if I switched around who was doing it. The game offers optional classes which affect almost nothin and you get so few of them anyways that the actual choices in it are negligable. And the fact that you're so heavily limited on skill slots further means that tactical decisions in battle become extremely linear and one-dimensional.

The sky armor system might appeal to some people, but I found it tedious. The battle system for them becomes even more simplistic and I dreaded every time I had to use them because it was so one-dimensional. The game seems to forget that they exist for a big chunk, and then uses them liberally at the end which ends up dragging things.

The outside systems suffer from bloat and poor design as well. The deals system feels totally pointless since you find plenty of good equipment without even trying. The upgrade system is actively silly, it incentivizes you to ignroe it for most of the game and has so many different options that you rarely end up with that much useful, and even then, it's pretty unnecessary even at hard difficultiy. The rewards board is really, really long and has a lot of stuff in it, but it kind of devolves to having to click a bunch of times to claim things for some reason. Collecting people for your organization later also kind of feels pointless, there's not really much reward for it. And particularly, the game adding random characters at the very end ends up feeling strange.

Over all, the game suffers from a lot of system bloat, from poor pacing of trying to do too many things at once, and a bunch of systems that might sound good on paper, but in practice end up limiting the combat tremendously. Despite this, the scale of the game, the attempt it makes, and the obvious effort that went into it makes it worth a purchase, I think.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery good game. Enjoyable OST, graphics that remind me of amazing games from the past like Chrono Trigger, the story is pretty much your typical JRPG plot, but the character development was good.

However - I do want to list some issues I had with the game:

Crystals - Probably my main issue with the game. I feel like it's poorly explained and it's just a big headache to even try using them since you can finish the game without using crystals at all and can just claim money instead. If you know what you are doing, they are insanely powerful and can easily break the game if you can fit them to certain characters with the way they are meant to be played. I found myself not even trying to touch crystals until the "ultimate side boss" destroyed me, I went to farm crystals for like maybe an hour and beat it very shortly after. Speaking of crystal farming, that's also a point that felt negative to me:
First - It (the crystals) has a lot of randomness and it feels like the respawn is a bit too long for my liking.
Second - Farming them is an absolute waste of time until you basically reach the endgame and are able to target farm specific crystal types.

Puzzles - There were not many and they were pretty easy, Also, not many mechanics were introduced for said puzzles. They were still fun to solve, but I just hoped for a little more.

Gear and upgrades - It felt like an absolute waste to upgrade my weapons and armor because not very long after I spend the resources to upgrade them I instantly run into an upgrade. It basically made me just not bother with upgrading at some point and not changing my setup until I ran into a wall (which never really happened until the final sideboss).

Difficulty - While I am aware that difficulty does not make a game good or bad, I still personally enjoy games that present a challenge, I felt like the entire game was pretty easy to breeze through, the hardest part was basically the start of the game. Once you gain control of a certain support character and the Cleric class emblem which lets the character equipping it cast an AoE heal the game stops being difficult.The hardest side boss was also not very difficult, it was a tiny step above the rest of the game but still not hard.

Characters - The cast is very big, (that's a good thing.) however - I felt like I didn't really need any other character past the first original 7 that are introduced in the main story, so I just never used those. Also, some characters just felt like they have skills that are completely useless or were just completely counter intuitive with how the rest of the skills works.

Menu/controls - There's no option to get to the title screen for whatever reason? The game also does not mention that you can change the battle speed (or maybe I missed that) or skip cutscenes/dialogues. (you can)


Negative points aside, it's time to mention some of the positives:

Battle - Overdrive and overheat were a pretty cool system. Sky armor battles were designed pretty well with the exception of one. ( Maria the Valkyrie because mirror image is kinda bs ). Regular enemies felt pretty powerful, sometimes more than bosses, but it balanced out with being fully healed after every combat.

Clan - The clan system is pretty creative, easy way to get sidetracked and chase loot in areas for hours once you get the option that shows you how many unopened chests there are in the area.


Regardless of the negatives mentioned above (I think it's perfectly fine to like a game and still have issues with it) - I do strongly recommend the game. It gave me a very nostalgic feeling and felt like one of those games that were very dear to me when I was younger.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite a few people were hyping up this game, going so far as to give negative reviews to other RPGs because they aren't this game.

That really seems unfair to both games. Chained Echoes does a lot of unique things and, especially gameplay wise, veers away from tradition in a way that gameplay centric players would surely appreciate. And obviously they do, just look at the reviews.

But the story isn't all that special to me. Sure, for many games the story doesn't necessarily hook people early on, but for a game lauded as the best of 2023 by quite a few, that's supposedly far superior to every other RPG including my personal favorite of 2023, Octopath Traveler II, I don't see it. Sure, no game is perfect and it's important to point out flaws in your favorite games, but this game has been relatively cliche so far. Apparently a party member betrays you later? I have an idea of two candidates, hopefully the game doesn't make it too obvious for them. I'll probably just watch a lets play or something to see what happens next.

There was a really funny moment where we fought a building though. I loved that part. Thanks, Chained Echoes, if nothing else I'll remember that moment.

Otherwise combat, despite healing automatically after battle, manages to actually be tedious for me and the plot is nothing special so far. Those of you who love this game, enjoy, but it definitely is too different from other RPGs to fairly compare them.

Frankly, Steam should give us a maybe option because of how unique this game is and how people interested in different kinds of games should play this just for the experience.

TLDR: Yet another poster child for a "maybe" option, but the story does seem kind of mediocre a few hours in. Combat can be borderline tedious with, as other reviews have put it, each battle feeling like a boss battle. The Overdrive mechanic thing is annoying, but I get how people who enjoy seeing unique gameplay mechanics might appreciate how this game tries to be different. Kudos to the fact that one person developed this game, but this ain't Stardew Valley\t\t\t\t\t
