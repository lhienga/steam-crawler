\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like Chained Echoes because there's clearly a lot of love put into it, but its flaws overshadow what it does well. The art direction is fantastic and the OST is great, but that's about all Chained Echoes does well. Starting with gameplay, the overdrive system is interesting in theory, but in practice, it turns every single encounter into a mini boss fight, thus making regular encounters unnecessarily tedious. The number of gameplay systems is excessive and they're all either poorly implemented, they require you to interact with poorly designed menus, or both. My final issue with the gameplay is just how poorly exploration is implemented. Nothing is more frustrating than trying to explore an area, only to be turned away because one of your party members decides you're going the wrong way. It's like Morgana from P5 telling you to go to bed, but even more annoying, TBH.

However, my biggest complaint has to be the writing. I'm willing to overlook hokey or campy translations, ala FF6 or FF7, because they can add to the character of the game and can result in some really memorable moments (see: Son of a Submariner or Sefer Sephiroth), but this isn't the case with Chained Echoes. The dialogue sounds like the dev took their German script, put it into Google Translate, then edited as needed. As such, the dialogue sounds awkward and stiff at its best, or it's actively detracting from the weight and clarity of the narrative at its worst. Considering this is a game that's released on all major platforms, is streaming on Game Pass, and the dev found the money to pay for a composer to put together an OST, the dialogue being as bad as it is is inexcusable, IMO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is a good JRPG that I would recommend for fans of the genre who want a solid story, complex mechanics, and little need to grind for levels/materials. There is plenty of exploration in the game despite the fairly linear story line. The downside to this game is it is unnecessarily complex. There are too many options for skills, gems, and other spoiler-free things that you will either ignore or quickly realize aren't worth it. The battles are well tuned so there is little room, for example, for your healer to do much more than healing despite a bevy of other skills. Combat is still fun with room for buffs, debuffs, attacks, etc. Expect a solid 40 hour experience out of this game - more if you want to discover everything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is a remarkable indie game that stands out not only for its captivating story but also for its profound exploration of redemption and forgiveness.

At the heart of Chained Echoes lies a story that is nothing short of a masterpiece in the world of video game storytelling. The tale follows the journey of a group of unlikely heroes, each with their own past transgressions and dark secrets. As players embark on this epic adventure, they are gradually introduced to the intricacies of redemption, a theme that is beautifully woven into the fabric of the narrative.

What sets Chained Echoes apart is its ability to explore the theme of redemption in a way that feels both realistic and unexpected. In many games, redemption arcs are often predictable, with characters following a set path towards forgiveness. However, Chained Echoes subverts these expectations and presents redemption as a challenging and nuanced process. Characters in the game are forced to confront their past mistakes, grapple with guilt, and make difficult decisions that may not always lead to immediate absolution.

One of the most commendable aspects of Chained Echoes is its ability to make players reflect on the nature of forgiveness. It challenges players to consider the true meaning of forgiveness and whether it can be granted, earned, or even deserved. The game doesn't provide easy answers, but rather encourages players to think deeply about the moral and emotional complexities involved.

Chained Echoes is a standout RPG that offers a deeply moving and thought-provoking narrative experience. Its exploration of redemption and forgiveness is both refreshing and unpredictable, making it a must-play for anyone looking for a game that goes beyond the traditional tropes of the genre. With its engaging story, memorable characters, and stunning visuals, Chained Echoes is a testament to the power of indie game development and a shining example of storytelling in the medium.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredible. It has a great story with twists I did not see coming, fun characters, loads of optional/side content and secrets to find that you become actively motivated to complete as it helps make you stronger to fight in upcoming boss battles. But most importantly: you can PET the DOGS and they give off a stream of heart emojis as a result. And if you pet the cats, they hiss at you. Just excellent.

The combat system revolves around this "Overdrive" bar that goes up or down depending on actions taken. So rather than spamming your best/hardest hitting abilities over and over, you have to take care to "maintain the balance" or else you do less damage and receive more. I liked this simply because it forces you to not use the same script of actions for each fight, making them more dynamic.

There's also a Hate system for if you wanted to build a more traditional tank+healer+2DPS setup party, which is cool consider the genre loves enemies to just "randomly" attack your squishiest character sometimes lol.

And later on, you get friggin' MECHS! that come with their own twist on the "on foot" combat system. Reminded me of those Gundam animes I watched as a kid on Cartoon Network.\t\t\t\t\t
