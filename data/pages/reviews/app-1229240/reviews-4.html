\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a mess of inconsistent terminology and confusing, contradictory systems.

Overdrive is awful in that an enemy party of four means that you're overheated in two turns and then you're faced with a choice of healing and going into Overheat or using a useless skill to try to get the gauge down. But then it doesn't matter because the enemy forces you into Overheat anyway and you take massive damage and die. Or you're forced into wasting TP trying to match the skill symbol instead of advancing the battle when the game makes a big deal about TP management, so you run out of resources and die. Recovery items recover so little that it doesn't affect the battle outcome, or the enemies hit hard enough that you're bound to die anyway. Attacking and blocking, staples of the genre that this game is trying to be a send-up to, are the least effective things you can do. Overdrive is probably the biggest source of frustration among other death-by-a-thousand-cuts annoyances.

What's "potency"? Why is "defense" referred to as DEF, Def, Defense and defense in different places? Are they the same thing? Can you just sell collectibles to vendors? If so, why are they called "collectibles"? Who's collecting them? Why is crystal combining such a resource consumption nightmare? Why are skill animations so long if you're going to force me to watch them so often? Why bother using traps when you can only set them one target at a time and there's no way to predict or direct who enemies will attack? Why give us six of eight party members at the start of the game in the spaghetti-fest lore dump and force us to only allow swapping with "linked" characters? There's no clear or tangible progression so how do I know when Unique Monsters are ready for fighting? Do I just waste time and slam my face into them and die?

All of this just feels bad, because the game mechanics get in the way of having fun. Overdrive forces you to stop thinking strategically about the combat you're in and forcing you to do things you don't want to do, which isn't fun. Crystal gathering isn't predictable and requires so many before you can slot them in, which isn't fun. Skill leveling requires so much SP that progression feels far, far away, which isn't fun.\t\t\t\t\t
