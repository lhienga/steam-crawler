\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was a delight. :)

I enjoy a good JRPG, and when I uncovered this one, I was quite happy to invest some time and $ into it.

COMBAT
The combat system was quite fun. The overdrive bar adds a nice layer of meta-strategy beyond "Spam my strongest attacks". All character's HP and TP fully recover after a battle, so it also means that you aren't walking around conserving all the good attacks for the inevitable boss. It means you can really experience the full range of your characters abilities, and the enemies are balanced appropriately for it. The random ability type popping up in the overdrive bar also keeps combat fresh, as you have to react to what it changes to on the fly in the longer battles to avoid the extra damage that comes if you don't keep an eye on it.

The Sky Armor combat that unlocks in the latter have of the game was good as well. I didn't enjoy it as much as the ground combat, but I appreciate the customization and the stat bonuses of different weapon types. The gears also make the combat feel distinct from just "normal combat, but with giant robots".

There are also no random battles, and all enemies can be seen as you're walking around. They also don't respawn very often, which is great for exploration. You don't have to fight the same monsters again and again when you're walking around looking for treasure. You can also flee with no chance of failure and no consequences if the battle isn't going your way, giving you a chance to re-evaluate your approach.

They also find a few nice gimmicks in combat to spice things up even more every now and again. I won't spoil any of them, since they are all nice surprises to discover on your own, and they usually only last for a couple of battles, so if you don't like one of them, you'll be back to normal combat soon enough. :)

My 2 biggest complaints with the combat system are:
1. You get more characters than can fit in your 8 member party at once, which I don't like. The out of party members do get more experience, fortunately. I would have preferred having a 3rd row of fighters, though that would make balance difficult, so I understand why the devs didn't go that way.
2. Equipment is kind of boring. Every weapon/armor is a strict upgrade, and there are no interesting effects or abilities unique to any of it. They somewhat make up for this with the crystal system, but removing crystals from any equipment may reduce the utility of the crystal, so I found myself hoarding crystals until the end of the game when I used them to upgrade the end-game gear. I would have preferred to have no penalty, which would have enabled more experimentation.

STORY
The story was also very well written. All the characters have their own motivations, and even if they don't make sense in the beginning, they'll make sense as more of the characters stories and motivations are made clear. Many characters have multiple different levels of depth that make them feel more interesting. Kylian was a particular favorite.

The writing also does a good job of displaying moral ambiguity. Very few decisions are portrayed from only one side. You might see something horrible happen, but then you will often get to hear why the person on the other side did it. Even if you don't agree with their reasoning, it's nice to have antagonists who don't wake up, twirl their evil mustaches, and say to the mirror "I wonder who I can torment today?".

The ending was also satisfying. That's all I'll say to avoid spoilers.

EXPLORATION/SIDE-CONTENT
It was really fun to wander the maps and fill out the reward board. It provides a nice checklist that I can check off as I progress through an area, from finding unique monsters, to buried treasure, to secret caves. It strikes the right balance of providing regular rewards for all your deviations from the main path, while still also having things that require a bit more effort to access, with correspondingly larger rewards.

Minor spoiler for a mid-game mechanic, but nothing story related: I'm a sucker for recruitment side-quests, so I did enjoy the recruitment and base building when it came up halfway through the game. It felt a lot like a mini-Suikoden game. It wasn't as in-depth, but that's fine. It's not really the focus of this game. Just a nice bonus.

ART/SOUND
The game looks and sounds fine. Some of the vistas are quite good, and the music adds to the ambiance of areas quite well. No major songs that are going to be stuck in my head, but still very good music. I don't have much else to say as this isn't really what I look for in a game. I'm generally happy as long as it doesn't make my eyes/ears bleed. :)

TIME REQUIREMENTS/DIFFICULTY
Ignore my 217 hours, as there was quite a bit of down-time in there where I left the game running while I walked away from my computer. I'd say it was a 40-50 hour investment to do /most/ of the game. I'm not a full-completionist, but I made sure to do all the sidequests and that was rewarding. I also unlocked the secret boss, but didn't invest the time to beat it. Maybe someday. :)

Some of the optional content made me think or try a battle multiple times, but generally speaking the games difficulty is middle of the road. It's never brainless, as enemies will usually wreck you if you aren't paying attention and grinding is mostly impossible; however, you'll probably be able to beat most things on the first try. It's a good balance, but if you're looking for something that will really push you to the limit, this isn't it.

TL;DR
Highly recommended game that hits many of my check boxes for an enjoyable experience. It will give you a really solid JRPG fix, especially if you like 2D art and turn-based combat.\t\t\t\t\t

the game, here is my take to CE.

Standard turn-based game, skills per character gave them distinction - near end game characters felt underwhelming, though. In hard mode where every buff, debuff, and overall action matter, giving a character that highly dependent on luck is a bummer. I will recommend Normal Mode run to future casual players only, Hard really is hard. If you want a challenging run that needs mastery of hundreds of skill, then Hard Mode will give you exactly that.

Story is great, indirection and twists lived up to expectations, an easy 9/10. I will not elaborate further to avoid spoilers. Dialogue felt pedantic, this is a personal opinion, some like the ivory-towering speeches, some wants zealous ones.

Overall, albeit technical problems playing it on Steam Deck, this pixel art RPG on hard mode gave a sense of really being role-played.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExtremely overrated. I really don't get the hype. It's fine that it has a %90+ rating but people act like this is the greatest JRPG of our time and the second coming of Chrono Trigger and it's nothing close.

The story and characters are just okay, not great, I don't feel any attachment to any of them or the plotline, They're just walking archetypes going through a rather standard JRPG story.

The gameplay is standard turn based JRPG but with an annoying mechanic that doesn't really have any benefits and will snowball against you if not played around, which is unfun and extremely limiting. The game would be better without the gimmick.

for $25, absolutely not worth it. Buy it at maybe $10. There's no glaring issues with the game (aside from the aforementioned gimmick they shoehorned into combat), but there's not a whole lot to earn it the extremely high pedestal some of these people are putting it on. It's merely a decently executed and somewhat generic JRPG title with good art.

If you're REALLY into JRPGs it probably won't disappoint, but I wouldn't put it before getting through a backlog of other games.\t\t\t\t\t
