\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9/10 Amazing rpg with a lot of charm. The characters are all interesting and mostly unique and fun. The story is quite good. It's outwardly bright, beautiful, and happy with some darker undertones to the plot through events and themes. The music is phenomenal; there hasn't been a track I don't like but there have been multiple that I love.
Then there's the combat. it may seem tedious to some who don't enjoy micromanaging during a battle, but for me it's great. I love all of the systems that exist in the combat. Overdrive is especially fun for me because it gives you an actual reason to use multiple moves rather than just spamming the same attack. And the swapping in and out system allows you to have 8 characters at a time which I really enjoy. Then there is how they deal with health: every time you finish a battle, your health and TP (the mana resource) are fully refilled, and any knocked out characters will be revived. This means that most items can only be used in battle which fixes my issue of never using items in battle because I didn't want to waste them (even though it wouldn't be a waste, I always horde items in games).
All in all, it's a really fun rpg, the visuals are stunning, and the mechanics are numerous but not tedious.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHeavily recommend this game, here is some things I wanted to mention

Combat is very good, the overheat bar puts a decent amount of complexity and layers to your average turn based game. Most of the bosses are interesting and have an interesting mechanic to it. Characters are interesting and each have their own interesting moves. What I dont like about the game is that there is a very obvious power ranking to the characters. Certain characters (like Glenn and Lenne)are just better than other characters. But that said, you can make any character work, some are just better than others, so don't be put down by everyone saying this. Moving onto Sky Armor combat, its ok. Theres different mechanics that come with Sky Armors, which I like, but the moves that sky armors get are just too stale in my opinion. Fighting on foot is just way more fun and the skills are more interesting when you fight on foot.
Another nitpick I also have is that some characters are introduced way too late into the story, to the point where I think that they though of adding these other characters AFTER the story was made. At that point you already have a squad that you run in which you have all the right things for, so the new character usually doesn't end up being used. Endgame content is nice, theres a good amount of things to do after finishing the game, especially if you want to 100% the game(which I did). Overall, game is very good, highly recommend if your into turn based games.\t\t\t\t\t

Nostalgia and love all over the place, happyness and so much fun.
Probably the greatest JRPG of all time honoring+resembling old-school SNES blockbusters like Chrono Trigger or PS1 classics like Final Fantasy.

Music is off the charts - a true masterpiece and sooo charming that I really cannot get enough of every single song. Really loving and digging this <3
EDIT: Just bought the soundtrack - go for it guys, it's amazing!
Combat system is so much fun and rich in variety - even after 200 hours+ I still discover new stuff and combinations.
Had to do 100 % achievements at the end of my first playthrough (which I rarely do), there are many side quests, additional content in endgame and many more secrets to discover.
Very strong story is immersive and mindblowingly hooking. Really stands out and pulls you in.
Player friendliness is written all over the place. Basically nothing annoys you in this game, all is meant to make you feel childlike happy. Only exception may be the crystal system, but I will not spill beans here (embedding weapons+armor with bonus stuff).

Buy this game, no matter what!
It surpasses Octopath Traveler 1 and Astlibra: Revision which were THE most incredible JRPGs in the past few years.
I will delve into Octopath Traveler 2 and Sea of Stars next, already excited, but I cannot let go of Chained Echoes yet.

Replaying on TRUE KING difficulty NG+ at the moment (right after first playthrough), rushing and reliving the best moments, enjoying the wholesome story once more with additional insights from my first playthrough.
True king difficulty really makes you want to go all-out and challenges you greatly.
You need to have all the best stuff from the previous playthrough or you will fail miserably.

I'm German so there's an extra affinity to the developer :)
