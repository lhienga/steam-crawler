\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a Masterpiece. The story is compelling and complex and is supported by a wide array of complex and interesting characters, who each go through their story arcs and grow as characters. Additionally, this takes place alongside an interesting and immersive world, that creates the foundations for the story and characters to really excel. It reflects upon issues that are still relevant today, grappling with questions of sacrifice and the price of peace, in a way that does not make light of the issue, and enables an interesting exploration.
Throughout this game, I thought I knew where the story was going, I thought I knew what tropes where being used. It would have been a decent game if it had, but the way tropes were subverted and characters did more interesting and complex things than the trope, really made this game interesting. But it did so in a way that didn't feel cheap, it did not feel like tropes were being subverted simply for shock value, but because it made sense for the story to go that way.
Gameplay is fantastic. There are a range of player characters, each with interesting and different combat styles, that can be tweaked towards your preferences. You get to develop your gameplay with them throughout the game, and really explore each of the characters. I think the game has a really good balance between forcing you to experiment with new characters and allowing you to choose a kind of core team that you find best. I really enjoyed the kind of levelling up process, and the way that skills were unlocked. Equipment and equipment upgrades were good, and the crafting system was enjoyable. Combat within itself was not always easy, and at time it was really challenging. I would say expect to die a few times (make sure you save), but either with a change in strategy or by progressing your characters will enable you to go forward.
There is a lot to do in this game. There is so much to explore, quests to do, things to collect. I enjoyed the deals, the reward board, and recruitment especially. There are also a range of beautiful locations to explore, from bumbling cities, to wastelands, to forests.
I really loved this game. If you're considering it, I would say that is well worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an awesome game; amazing accomplishment for the developer. Push thru the first 2-3 hours and it comes together; writing is a bit loose and silly at the start but really starts to shine as it progresses. Near 50 hour playtime for me and that's without completing all the side quests and secrets; the 30-40 hours advertised is probably just to keep from scaring folks off. I'd assume if I read a 50-70 hour play time that it probably has a lot of filler. Not the case! Amazing game. If you like the klassik SNES RPGs, this is essential -- absolute tops. 100% recommended!

My only criticism is that the battles were by and large a little easy; I sort of felt a strong strategy pretty early, developed my party with the ideas I fell into in the first chapter, and carried those throughout. Didn't enjoy the second battle type introduced later in the game as much, but it was a super cool effort to mix things up regardless. No grinding required, but lots of exploration and revisiting areas encouraged. Near flawless execution. A friend who went back to Chrono Trigger following a play-thru of this game felt that Chrono Trigger was a little lacking... and I have to agree -- at the end of the day, this production tops any SNES RPG. Must play!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is an outstanding turn-based JRPG. I'm impressed that this is largely a one-man show. Most JRPGs are front weighted, having shot off all of their bullets quickly, perhaps saving one or two for the end (or worse, promising sequels that never follow). This one stays interesting throughout its entirety, with Matthias Linda unloading clip after clip.

+ The plot is wonderful and unpredictable. I had more than a few moments of amazement. Everything flows together like a work of masterpiece; Matthias Linda is truly an artist. If someone tells me they know exactly how this story is going to turn out halfway through, I'm calling them a liar.

+ The turn-based combat is done very well. There aren't traditional levels as per usual. Abilities and stat improvements are acquired as bosses are defeated and with fusion crystals. Grinding was an option, which would lead to maxing out abilities sooner, but largely unnecessary for a normal difficulty run. I'm also very happy that a switch system was implemented, allowing eight characters to be useful instead of just four. I've never understood the point giving players an abundance of controlled characters when most of them end up warming the bench. While many of the passive abilities repeat, there's a great deal of variety and many viable strategies to win.

+ The reward system is neat! I liked that there were tons of little pieces of loot everywhere. Getting a crystal was exciting for me, eliciting a similar feeling I'd get long ago when I played Magic the Gathering and got a booster pack. Sure, many crystals, like the cards, turned out useless, but it was fun to see what would turn up next. The reward board was a sweet idea.

+ Great cast of characters. I caught hints that some of them had hidden motives, and I was made to wonder if some were backstabbers. Very few characters were one-dimensional. I think the only character of the main cast I disliked was Robb, but in the end I had a healthy respect for him.

+ Orb puzzles. If the dev designed a game that was just a whole bunch of orb puzzles I'd buy it.

A few aspects of the game weren't so fun for me.

- Mechs. I don't understand JRPG's love affair with mechs. Mechs are why I'm having trouble continuing Trails of Cold Steel. Having my characters pilot giant metal robots never appealed to me. It fits the plot here, I guess, but this could've easily done without. All too often I was forced into uninteresting mech combat, where I'm given fewer tactical options and the progress I've made with my characters becomes meaningless. Maxing out mech abilities is fast, so mech combat ends up being nearly without reward. Mech abilities are strictly tied to their equipment, so there's little point in learning "cross-class" skills.

- Many rewards for searching off the beaten path were related to each character's final weapon. Well, I never found the trigger that allows for final weapons to be crafted. I think this trigger comes a bit too late and/or is too hidden. The ultimate weapons are not needed for normal difficulty (and obviously they aren't worth spit in mech combat). Still, the feeling of wasted effort stings a bit.

- Multiple scenes end with Glenn going unconscious. Scenes that could've been daring escapes from exploding buildings or collapsing towers end up being a letdown. All hell is breaking loose. Oh, Glenn loses consciousness again? Cut immediately to black screen, reappear to find, yet again, Glenn safe in a bed.

- Sure, there aren't any random encounters. Some have likened this to Saga Frontier, where you could typically dodge encounters. It's not like Saga at all, however; only a select few enemies move around. The encounters in this game are largely unavoidable, especially for players like me who have to open every chest possible. The enemies will reappear as well, if you leave an area and return. Once you have access to the mechs, if you want to revisit a previous area you can fly over these encounters. Until then, you're not going to be avoiding many of them.

- At the end dungeon. Robb (paraphrase): "Do you see those bubbles? I think we can go under water there." No ♥♥♥♥, Sherlock. I'd already encountered them at least twice: first within a giant worm and then at Ograne. It took me a few minutes within that worm and I thought I was stuck. That hint could've come a bit sooner.

- Glenn: "That's not the right way." YES IT IS DAMMIT I WANT TO SEARCH THAT WAY! QUIT THAT!

Chained Echoes was great overall. It's nice to see a video game that was crowdfunded turn out so well. Hopefully, this dev isn't done. I'm happy with the conclusion of Chained Echoes (no need for DLC, in my opinion), and should other worlds be built by this dev, I'd be very interested in checking them out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ta love letter to late 90s, early 00s jrpgs.

takes the best elements from that era and does it own thing with them. the movement is ff6 and fluid, the writing is competent, the lore blends ff7's lifestream with lovercraftian influences. there's a lot of nods to xenogears, some probably even unintentional (a story plot just abruptly ending and being told via on-screen text a la the infamous disc 2.) there's a cute combat tag system that feels like an answer to the frustrations we all had as kids to not being able to play with enough of the characters at once (but then throwing in so many chars anyway you're still slightly annoyed)! the puzzles are serviceable. the way old maps are reused as you unlock new modes of travel is pretty well thought out and enjoyable. the story itself works pretty well. the motivations of the main characters make sense, they themselves being well-rounded. a lot of quality of life things (such as the exit game option right there after you save the game, and the ability to retry a fight from the game over screen but first starting you from the game menu in case you wish to change some gear around). the music is great! there's a system in place to make sure all your characters are the same "level", and "level-ups" are doled out only after reaching certain milestones in the story. this however does not prevent GRINDing, which we all love, since the Skills themselves have levels like ff8's gf abilities. then there's the "materia" system, with crystals that you can combine and slot into your gear. those are nice, but you upgrade your gear so quickly if you rush the main story that it's annoying having to unslot and upgrade and reslot all the "materia" again. luckily, the game doesn't really seem to require you to engage with this system to beat the bosses, so you can limit it to just a few characters with insane damage output (you'll know who).

overall, a great game. still haven't finished it, so i cannot comment on the satisfaction of the plot. everything else feels good though. if you need to scratch that jrpg itch, give this one a whirl.\t\t\t\t\t
