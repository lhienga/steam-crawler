SPOILERS AHEAD - If you don't want spoilers/don't want to read about SA-related topics, I will just say that if you have any SA-related trauma, DO NOT PLAY THIS GAME.

I don't really have much to say about the game- I haven't finished it, and I honestly don't know if I will at this point- but what I played wasn't too bad, though if you're looking for an indie RPG with good gameplay that lacks grinding I'd recommend something like Ara Fell or Rise of the Third Power over this even for those elements- but it was decent, at the very least.

The reason I'm not sure whether I'll even finish the game is because of an event that I believe happens at about the halfway point of the game. Everything was going pretty smoothly story-wise thus far, it was a little bit dark at points but not anything particularly hard to swallow- a bit of a darker tone to your typical JRPG or fantasy-story fare. But at that point, the main character has a discussion with a close friend that eventually comes to a rather indulgently shocking head.

Disclaimer- I don't have any sort of SA-related trauma, and I wouldn't generally say that I'm even *that* squeamish about the topic. But when the main character's friend began to describe how his sister was violently assaulted by a group of men and harping on how alone and powerless she was, I began to feel pretty damn uncomfortable and put off from the game as a whole. I'm not even saying that this topic/event should have necessarily been cut from the game, but it was handled with absolutely no subtlety, and frankly, I don't *think* the game even has a trigger warning or anything that would indicate that it would delve into these sorts of topics. Feel free to correct me if I'm wrong, of course.

I'm not even sure that I would rate this game negatively overall, but this specific part of the story just killed my enthusiasm and enjoyment of it, and I felt that I needed to leave this review in case it might dissuade anyone who might feel similar to me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'ma big fan of RPGS, JRPGS, whatever RPGs (the rest). This game hands down has been one of my favorite to play, and is good enough that it challenges some of my older games that I'm nostalgic for as well.
Every time I've mentioned that this game needs something, it has then showed me within 30-60 minutes, that it does indeed do that. Examples include, sell all bottom for selling loot in stores (I had been doing them line by line before I looked), efficient transition/warp travel, reusing areas, or saving parts of them for later so you are hungry to know more, but have to wait, and numerous other things.

While its not the longest game ( I prefer intensely long RPGS), I think its the perfect lengthen for most people who play, or would be willing to give it a try. The gameplay is fun, easy to learn, and adds new stuff up till about the halfway point in the story (love games that keep giving you new things). the story is very enjoyable, and I don't skip any cut scenes because I'm invested, plus it has some unexpected twists that you don't often see, so I'm still being surprised by the story, which I find is rare these days.

All in all, if you enjoy RPGs/story games, pick this up. I got it on sale for less than $10, but would have been ok if I had paid $40, and not disappointed had I paid $60. Huge hats off to the developer, I know they put in a ton of time, including a full year into the story (most games have half that these days). I love the design too, as a gamer in my 30s, I think going to hard on the graphics ruins games these days, and I prefer the simpler look, with all of the work going into gameplay and story.\t\t\t\t\t

Over all it was interesting to have a jrpg system without experience gains from encounters. Most of the slog that exists in that style game didnt exist. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR:
If you loved all the snes/sega classics and would like to dive into a good story like back then then grab this game. Even for full price its totally worth it.


As many people already wrote this is an absolute homage to all the SNES classics. Even my beloved underrated Secret of Evermore was recognized in the sewers.

But chained echoes is also a great game in itself. I have not finished the story as of writing this review but i really like the writing so far. The dialogues feel mature and the characters have depth. There are also some really goofy and funny characters like goatman that attacks with an anchor and needs to eat enemies to unlock skills. Overall the characters were not too "japanese" for my taste though. I also liked the humor of the writer.
There are a lot of names, especially in the beginning of the game which can be confusing at times but you will get the mainplot nevertheless.

There is no classic leveling system but instead you get upgrades points after boss fights (which there are many). The unlocked skills can be leveled though to rank 3, increasing their strength.

Another really awesome feature is the sky armor. Once unlocked you can always summon the armor. You will have greatly increased stats and damage and you can actually fly across all the maps you used to walk on (with some limitations though). This way you can enter areas on old maps you could not before. You can even fight enemies that were meant for normal fights and completely destroy them. There are also enemies which should only be fought in skyarmor. This system reminds me of phantasy star 4 and the sandworms lol

There is a lot of optional content and challenges like in classic final fantasy games. I liked all of it so far and didn´t have the feeling like doing chores but i really wanted to do that side content. The sidequests are all interesting stories on their own and not just "do x 10 times stuff".

There are som complains of course.
Overdrive system:
The developer tried to reinvent the jrpg fighting system and the idea is interesting but in the end the system feels rather restrictive than tactical.
I think a better solution would be that some kind of "power" skills fuel that bar and then it must be cooled down.

Crystals:
Crystals can be socketed into weapons and armor to boost the characters even more. The crystal can be collected from big crystals on most maps. Finding the one you need is completely luck though since there are like 50 crystaltypes or something and you can always only choose from three.

Character cutoffs:
There are passages where you can only play certain characters for a brief moment for the sake of story telling. Im not a big fan of those parts in rpgs because i usually have my favourite party and focused characters.

But overall the game is absolutely worth it and i will totally 100% it.\t\t\t\t\t
