\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLiterally never reviewed any other game on this platform, but Chained Echoes was such a pleasure and I felt compelled to review. Everything you enjoy from classic JRPG titles with some improvements to make the pacing not feel like a slog. Story was unique and engaging throughout, combat was fun while also being challenging at times, sky armor battles helped switch up the monotony of standard battles, diverse cast of characters with engaging story lines, and dialogue that is actually enjoyable (and not overbearing by any means). Everything about this title was fantastic, even more so knowing this was a one man project(which is insane). There are some negative reviews about bugs, design choices, or other nitpicky items, but those can all be overlooked for the masterpiece that this game truly is. I finished it around 50 hours and could've easily put another 20-30 finishing collectibles and doing side quests . 10/10 would recommend, stop debating and just pick it up for a great adventure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first thing, that captured me, was this gorgeous pixel-art. I can't even comprehend, how long it took to create these detailed, vibrant environments and smooth animations.
Then the interesting story and world made me stay. It starts slow, but then takes exciting turns and makes you care for these characters. Sienna's story was particulary fun to watch, but the others start to grow on me as well.
The combat-system is also unique in the aspect, that you have to keep a balance in the useage of skills. It keep your attention even in small battles.

The only problem I have is the tutorial. I don't like, when a game uses a big text-dump to explain complicated systems.

If you love a classic jrpg with an interesting cast of characters, good story and also happen to love pixel-art, please buy Chained Echoes! It is beautiful, fun and also has some deep wisdom in it's dialogue. I cannot recommend it enough.\t\t\t\t\t
