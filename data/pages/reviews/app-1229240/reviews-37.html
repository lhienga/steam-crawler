\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA few hours in and I have already run out of patience with this game.

At no point have I felt like I am progressing. Not growing stronger even a bit, no matter the gear or skills acquired.

Speaking of skills, each character starts with several of them. You get to add 2 or 3 to each. And somehow skill point acquisition comes to a halt. I don't know, maybe I couldn't figure it out, but I don't have the patience anymore.

You basically get yourself a 6-man strong band of heroes that has to grind the same struggle with every single enemy encounter. It is either the same tedious long fight or the occasional random tough guy that you aren't supposed to fight yet. It's a mystery when or if you will ever get that strong too.

Well, this isn't the first JRPG nostalgia trip failure I have tasted recently. This is one particular genre that requires a lot of effort to put out something solid. Just bare minimum effort simply doesn't cut it.

Have a great time if you have enjoyed it. But I am passing this one. Like someone else wrote, this game can only feel good to a JRPG veteran if you don't want to play the same old games over and over again, and if your expectations are low.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely miraculous JRPG that rivals Chrono Trigger. Anyone who grew up playing the SNES classics really owes it to themselves to play this. The creator has really rejuvenated turn-based combat with the Overdrive system with none of the esoteric nature of older games. The music is phenomenal (shout out to the New Wyrnshire theme for escaping straight out of Persona 5, or the Perpetua theme which uses amazingly otherworldly choir sampling). The plot is wonderfully deep, with charming characters. Some of the QOL aspects are just genius - you heal after each fight, escaping is guaranteed, free battle speed control. It really is just close to a masterpiece - maybe aside from the Sky Armour battles and crystal system which are admittedly a bit clunky. But seriously - please buy this game. The passion from the creator is plain to see.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game takes a bit of inspiration from Chrono Trigger, and doesn't hide it: the first scene of the game is meant to be a direct reference. If you like the SNES era of RPGs, buy this right now. It's one of the best games of its type I have ever played. Some of the unique and overlapping elements of combat might seem confusing and dense at first, but don't sweat it too much at the start: it will all become second nature very fast. Chained Echoes does pretty much everything you could want from an RPG extremely well: the cast of characters and story are fun to experience, the combat system is very intricate and your party is open for lots of layers of customization, there is a large array of side content and exploration to do, the music is phenomenal, and the length of the campaign is much longer than I was expecting from an indie title.

The normal difficulty settings are great, but once you get a feel for the gem customization system and figure out your combat mechanics you might start wanting a bit more challenge. Well... You'll get more than a "bit" more. Go into "System" then "Options," bump enemy stats and aggressiveness both up to high, and get ready to get your ass kicked.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is a good game. I was pleasantly surprised. This game is clearly inspired by many classic RPG titles and anime such as Final Fantasy VI, Chrono Trigger, Final Fantasy Tactics, Full Metal Alchemist and the Mobile Suit Gundam series.

The best part of this game is the battle system. It was brand new to me so it took some time to figure out exactly how it worked. The musical soundtrack is also excellent! The coolest part of the game was when the party acquired the Sky armours (Gundams). They are very cool to use and they have their own variation of the unique battle system. I also like the tag team system and that I could customize how I wanted my formation to look and what abilities I wanted. The only annoying thing was when my formation got shuffled because of story instances.

The weakest part of the game is the story and character development. I found myself not caring about any of the characters besides Lenne and Victor. The story was also not fully explained and there was loose ends that weren't tied up. I imagine this was either the result of a rushed release or/and the developer wants to make a sequel with major villains still at large. The other annoying thing was the crystal system. It is needlessly grindy to give the characters the extra attribute bonuses I wanted. A tip to the developer, either remove the purity altogether or ensure that every crystal takes only 1 slot in the equipment.

All in all, Chained Echoes is a good first installment and I'm looking forward to futures titles from this developer.\t\t\t\t\t
