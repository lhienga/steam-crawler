\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is it. This is the game that does ALL the stuff. It does the old stuff. It does the new stuff. It even does some brand new stuff. I play basically everything in this genre and I have since the early 90s and this has all the goodies: fun AND rewarding exploration, dungeons and zones that ask you to actually think a bit about how you will work through them, a GREAT turn based combat system with meaningful and cool progression for most of the characters and a good story with characters that fit into general types but have unique and often refreshing story beats and characterization. It's even cool. I could probably spend 5000 words on the things that this borrows and then improves on from other turned based RPGs that have come before but instead I just want to point to a few things that I found new and refreshing in my 50ish hours so far:
- Escape is an actual mechanic in the game that you will use and might use all the time (especially on harder fights). You can run from any fight basically including most bosses. You'll figure out yourself all the use cases for it and you'll try a bunch just to see if you can do things in the fight and then escape (or I guess you could look at the up in advance). Bottom line: It's an actual mechanic. Very cool.
- The story is adult in a way that makes it feel really fresh. It's not "edgy" but it has dark moments. It's not too sweet but it is a game where you can pet every animal and little heats appear. It's more that it's not a story for teenagers where people die or bad things happen but the consequences are usually shrugged off. It's a story for adults if you know what I mean. And it has nuance and is cool and interesting and it respects its audience by not hand holding every beat or over explaining every lore bit. I've played plenty of turn based RPGs where there are mature stories, but I haven't played one that was this respectful of it's audience. Which brings me to the last point.
- This game is designed, at it's core, around "what is fun" and then helping you have more fun or rewarding you for that fun. Is being able to run from any fight even if you accidentally run into a wandering world boss fun? Yes, cool put it in. Is backtracking fun? No, ok add like 4 movement options and teleporting. Is collecting and exploring fun? Yes, cool give rewards like 4 different ways including a full overall tracker of basically doing everything in the world. This game respects the hell out of your time (not always but more than most games by a lot) and it wants you to love the time you do spend in it.

The fact that this was a tiny dev team on I'm sure a modest budget is just such an accomplishment. Congratulations to everyone involved. It's only January but this really might end up my GOY anyway. It's just that good. Thank you and I can't wait for Chained Echoes 2 :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is just so much to talk about with this game. Most reviews are comparing this to Chrono Trigger or other era-defining RPGs. While they are not wrong in doing so, I'll simply summarize my thoughts below instead:

-Combat is satisfying. Animations and sounds are clean and crispy. Honestly, playing just to watch my characters use their ultimates is enough for me

-Soundtrack is a yes

-The originality in this game is unparalleled in its non-cliche characters, diverse settings, and MANY (emphasis on the MANY) plot twists

-Theory crafting for your party (mid-late game) is limitless thanks to the many combat-oriented systems such as, for example, squad formation, mix and matching abilities, and unique gear interactions

-The details and effort that goes into every square inch of this game is refreshing. For example, everywhere I traveled to felt alive. No copy/pasted taverns and no empty rooms.
-A lot of quality of life things that I'm sure you'll read in other reviews

The positives mentioned above massively outweigh the negatives but I will mention the cons as though someone who hasn't played it is reading this:

-The original "Overdrive" system for combat is not fun. You have to play matching colors with your abilities and overdrive bar to prevent the meter from reaching the red zone (which buffs your enemies) during your precious turns. It served as only a restriction because it limits you from focusing on a cohesive strategy. Nothing else to say. I played the game fine on higher difficulty. Regardless, it's still restrictive for no reason.

-It takes WAY too long to recruit characters and get to a point in the game where you can say, "okay, for combat, I want to do this rather than that". Throughout most of the game you're forced to play the story characters as your mains which made it feel linear for that portion. You don't even reach a full sized party until way later on. People who play RPGs love to experiment and I didn't get that feeling until late in the game

-The Sky Armors you see in the trailers were simply not fun to use in combat. I dreaded those things. They looked cool and were useful for traveling.

-While the story itself was intriguing. The dialogue alone was either just plain amateur or poorly translated.

That being said, a lot of itches were scratched and I still stand by this recommendation

Go play it\t\t\t\t\t
