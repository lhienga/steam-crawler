\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes delivers an unforgettable RPG experience that blends character depths, an engaging story, and innovated combat system. With it's twist on the traditional turn base combat hearken to old school turn base games and character development, this game shines as a testament to the RPG genre.

One of the game's standout feature I think is its Overdrive Bar. it's inclusion keeps the player on their toes, adding a layer of strategy during combat. By adding this system each combat never feels the same, juggling your abilities to maintain the optimal gauge keeps it fresh. The Overdrive Bar ensures every battle is a thrilling experience.

Beyond combat, Chained echoes respectfully rewards the player for exploring the game and it's world. Completing quests, finding hidden treasure, and a myriad of other tasks, this game ensures players are motivated to push forward and uncover new adventures.

Moreover, this game excels in spotlighting character's uniqueness and development, even though the roster can feel a bit big. I feel like the main cast is enough to enjoy the game itself, and the partner system that allows you to bring multiple characters in combat expands it further. I do feel like some characters are treated a bit better than others, but each character retains their individuality and they do not feel the same in combat.

Speaking of story, this game delivers a well written narrative that captured my attention the whole way through my play through. With its intricate plot twists, compelling themes, and memorable moments, Chained Echoes kept me on the edge of my seat for a majority of my journey. I do feel like the narration falters slightly right at the final stages, but very slightly, and it managed to twist my heartstrings right in the last minute, leaving me a lasting impression that lingered after the credits roll.

This game was a remarkable RPG experience. It's astounding that it was developed mostly by one man. On that note, it earns a near perfect score from me. I rarely finish most games, and this was the first that I really wanted to 100% for the achievements. Innovating from its predecessors in the turn base combat in the past, rewarding players for exploring the world and uncovering hidden treasures, captivating the audience with its characters and their development, Chained Echoes ultimately delivers a heartfelt adventure that resonated with me a profound level.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really good JRPG made by a German guy with serviceable writing, kinda engaging story but with too many plot twists and an ending that sours the story. Still a great experience in the end.
The combat is fun from the beginning to the end, never once have I felt like a certain fight is too tedious which is a rare thing for turn-based games, especially after 60+ hours. Your entire party get fully heal after every battle for free, that alone is already 9/10. Sky armor combat however while not bad, it's not as fun as on foot combat because you can't customize which skills you can have, it depends on your close and long range weapons while on foot, you can choose any skills and put them in the order that you like, each character has 16 active skills and passive skills with more than 1 playstyle.
Character progressions, game pacing, enemy varieties, locations, musics are all great.

Now this is nit picking but the only part I don't like about the game is the crystal system. You slot crystal into your gears to gain passive skills, you need to collect crystals and fuse them together to reach rank X to maximize their effects. My problem with the system is how you get crystals, there's no NPC that can sell you crystals, crystals spawn on fixed spots around the map and you get random crystals every time you harvest a spot, even though you can focus on which type of crystal you wanted, harvesting spots are on a timer, not real world timer, an in game timer, meaning they only respawn every 15-30 minute if your game is running, you don't unlock the ability to focus on the type of crystal that you wanted until Act 4 which is the end of the game, any crystals before that are 90% useless because you only want status crystals and maybe some utility crystals, anything else is just too situational to be good. Unless you keep sidetracking to collect crystals during the story it's near impossible to get 6 rank X crystal for each party memembers because there are too much RNG involve and there isn't much to do at the end of the game to justify the wait time for crystals to respawn. The second problem is that you don't need to use crystals at all, the game is already easy enough, but if the possibility to min-maxing is there, I want to min-max my characters, why put the crystals on a timer like it's an online live-services game? I can farm crystals as early as Act 2, it just take more effort to get a rank X and be OP, why bother to stop people who actually spend more time with the game having fun? I'm already 70 hours in, at the end of Act 4, I just want to min-maxing for fun but this kind of RNG grind combine with a cooldown timer is just too tedious to be bothered with, I ended up with 90% of my playthrough not using any crystals and only have a few rank X crystal at the end because I don't want to postpone the story anymore. This doesn't affect my experience with the game as a whole but it sure damn annoys the hell out of me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother indie RPG that does a lot of things right, but ultimately stumbles near the end. The ending itself felt rushed. Story wise it left a bitter taste with certain characters actions, and passes on making an ultimate resolution of what was talked about most of the latter half of the game. I think the developer was going for some possible future sequel ideas, but that generally makes for an awful feeling JPRG when the conclusion falls flat. There's a reason the Final Fantasy games work, because they are standalone stores that aren't afraid to resolve the entire plot.

Another negative, many of the cool optional end game items can only be obtained basically by fully diverting your attention after already going through the final area and escaping not far from the last boss. Compare that to the more classical 16-bit era RPGs where the build up to the end is where you obtain such items, usually with more story intertwined.

There's some annoying bloat in several systems as well. With the crystal system I felt myself not even wanting to find weapon and armor upgrades because it meant redoing all the enhancements and having to go back to a crafting point to even do so. The shard system has way too many useless skills and not enough slots to assign. Even the jerryrigged class system feels so underwhelming aside from probably the first 2 you receive.

Overall I think the game would have benefited from at least 1 person being able to tell the developer "no" on some ideas. It's like 75% there to being a classic on par with JRPGs of the past, but unfortunately it turned out to be merely an echo.\t\t\t\t\t

  • The exploration in the game is great. It really rewards you for backtracking, checking everything
  • Plenty of characters and the fact that you can use 8 characters in battles
  • Lots of customisation in character builds with the job system
Negatives
  • Story isn't that great
  • Music is mediocre
  • No branching plot, so not much replayability
  • The crystal crafting system is too RNG based

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a throwback masterpiece. I would compare it very favorably to a game like Octopath Traveler. I played this right after Baldurs Gate 3 and it was a relief to play something more simple.

+ Graphics - every locale is different, enemies aren't palette swapped, vibrant
+ Music - really well done soundtrack, one of few games I didn't mute the music and play my own
+ Battle System - fun swapping in and out teammates, skill system is unique, lots to try/strategize for tough battles
+ Story - compelling story from beginning to end about rebirth and how we're tied to our past. Characters are memorable, even the ones you dislike. Lots of people complained about the ending, to me the choice made sense. There are a few loose ends but I think Glenn made the right decision.
+ Length - completed with all achievements in 45 hours, I like this length of game more than 100+ hours

I think my only complaints about the game are inventory management/menus are a bit clunky. Too many button presses to do what you want. And like other reviewers I didn't find the crystal system very engaging, I didn't really care about it until the final battles. I will also mention that although I liked the battle system, I did not like fighting in the Sky Armor, this took away swapping and most of the skills in the game, reducing it to a grind.

I truly hope Matthias Linda makes another game. Thanks for making this one!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just finally got through the game. What an experience! I'm holding back a lump in my throat because of the ending.
It can be challenging, but it's definitely rewarding. The plot is interesting. None of the characters are too heavy handed. But the plot twists and devices are memorable.
I absolutely love the music. You'll have some favorites and everything else is pretty good.
The combat was different but I liked it. Definitely would like to see another game or sequel with the combat style. The mech combat was basic though.
The pixel art was charming. Considering one guy did this, it's pretty impressive.
Overall, an excellent game. And a modern JRPG masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t
