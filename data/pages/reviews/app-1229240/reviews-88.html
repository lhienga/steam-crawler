\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA beautiful JRPG dragged down by terrible writing.

This game looks amazing - It clearly was made with a lote of care. It's pixel art is gorgeous. Character skills are beautifully animated, monsters have creative designs and the scenery is beautiful.

However, for as much as the art is great, Chained Echoes falters in a lot of other areas.
The music is forgettable at best, and sometimes annoying. The writing is incredibly weak. Characters behave erratically and the prose is stilted; they speak as if they were all the same person. The grander story beats are interesting, but the game could have benefited a lot from hiring a writer or an editor for the moment-to-moment dialogue and characterization. This is the game's biggest fault, and it really hurts in a pure turn based RPG where the writing plays such an important role in making your party feel alive.

Combat has ups and downs. The game borrows a lot from late SNES era RPGs; chrono trigger and phantasy star are its most prominent influences, which is always good by my book. Overall the combat system is interesting enough, if not exactly my cup of tea. Turn based rpgs with no timing or positional elements at all are rare nowadays, for good reason. The two big game series in this genre are SMT and pokemon, and it's no coincidence that both offer huge party customization for you to be able to mix it up between fights and to have something to look forward to. Chained Echoes has paltry customization by comparison, and Its overdrive bar is not nearly as swingy as SMTs press turn, even on the highest difficulty. Still, combat has good moments, and planning ahead for the overdrive bar to be where you wanted it to be can be satisfying.

This is a soft negative rec. If you are thirsting for JRPGs, you could do much worse. If you donâ€™t care for bad writing, and want to see some pretty pixel art, go for it. If you are not a huge fan of the genre or are looking for something with more meat to its bones though, skip this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is a JRPG fan's JRPG. Matthias Linda clearly grew up on the SNES-PS2 era of the genre and it shows: the story borrows many concepts from the likes of FF7 and FF12 (with a little bit of Code Geass on the side) while maintaining a unique spin on almost all of them, and the gameplay draws from many sources while refining them to their best points with very few of their flaws.

Off the top of my head, we got

* Reliable, valuable stealing from FF9 (with the flaw of guessing who has what removed - enemies straight up have a steal indicator)
* Locke gameplay from FF6
* A nice little route split homage to FF6
* Quina gameplay from FF9 (similar to stealing, the flaw of guessing what actually has something is addressed with an indicator)
* GBA FF style bestiary
* Mid-battle party switching from FF10
* Chrono Trigger-esque transitions into battle
* "Amalia"
* Hunts a la FF12
* Customizable skills both unique to characters and Jobs from FF5 and the FFT series
* Materia-esque armor and weapon upgrades (that don't get lost when upgrading equipment)

And concepts from the genre as a whole that Matthias adopted and improved upon include

* Reliable status effects that stay relevant through the entire game, with an incentive to maintain upkeep in the form of resistances
* Clan building a la Skies of Arcadia
* Brilliant usage of the 2D map layout for both hidden treasures as well as mobility gating
* Tangible rewards for accomplishing fully transparent achievements, further incentivised with streaks
* Customizable mounted units (with the colors thrown in for FREEEE)
* Auto-healing after battle (which while controversial back in the day, I find very liberating)

And all of this doesn't even touch on the simply masterful writing with extremely believable characters and motivations (almost nothing happens without a reason), seamlessness that permeates the entirety of the gameplay from combat to treasure, or the superb management of map accessibility that accounts for all stages of the game. It also doesn't cover how well balanced the game is (until the endgame where AGI manipulation is a free win) despite its story and achievement progression based leveling system (which also has a high degree of customization), nor does it cover the sheer impact the story leaves. I don't think any plot point in the last 5 years has left as much of an impression on me as entering Farnsport in Act 2 did.

All in all, Chained Echoes is an absolute must for any fan of the JRPG genre, especially those who loved the SquareSoft era. Solid 9/10: an incredible indie RPG that deserves to stand alongside Undertale, Bug Fables, and Omori as one of the greatest.\t\t\t\t\t
