\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnd here we are. Another "love letter" to classic RPG games of the SNES era - well...kinda. The game is good. Its turn based combat system is fun, graphics are great for a pixel art 2D game, music composition is pleasant, and the game offers a wide range of accessibility to any fans. There's a lot of customization to characters and you get to use up to 8 characters in your party in battle out of possible 12, so not everyone in the game is just sitting on the sidelines. I kinda wish they would have dumped 2 of the characters and just let me run with a full roster of 10 characters, but 8 / 12 is still pretty good. There's a lot of character development too which is very important to capturing that feel of older RPGs, instead of just having Jake the Farmer save the world...again.

There are 2 very major issues that harm this game. One is the absolutely terrible pacing. This game is top heavy, and the majority of the best content is found at the very end of the game. The mid game is basically a race to the end so you can do all of the fun stuff. It was the same issue in Final Fantasy X. Once you got the airship, the game finally opened up, but you only got that right before the last few areas, which was silly. The game is riddled with disappointment as areas look like they're open for you to explore, only for your lead character to forcefully turn you around and tell you that's the wrong way. That does finally stop, but that's near the end of the game right before the final dungeon. Again...top heavy. You also still recruit characters up to nearly the end of the game which is dumb and paced horribly. Once you hit the endgame, it's a lot of fun, but I'm not playing World of Warcraft here. I would expect the majority of the recruitment, exploration, and systems to be hashed out in the mid game, not at the very end.

The other issue is the stupid amount of systems this game has. There's the standard party battle system, the crystal system, the sky armor system, equipment upgrade system, skills system; what is this? A FTP mobile game? Having all these systems is the reason the game gets stupid difficult on the normal game setting. You are expected to interact with every system, and not all of them are actually explained. The crystal system could have been done away with all together as it's just a bunch of RNG nonsense, and upgrading your weapons and armor would make sense if the pacing matched the timing of your upgrades. You get most of your stuff near the end and not in the early or mid game, so upgrading weapons and armor can wind up being a complete waste of materials depending on the character since there's not really a good pace of when upgrades come in. Some characters, it's totally worth it as you'll have a piece of gear for nearly 20 hours, and others get upgrades in the span of 1-2 hours.

I still enjoyed this a lot and once I got to the end game I had a hard time putting it down for the last 8-9 hours. Battles are really interesting on foot, and in sky armor and it pretty much is the bulk of what makes this game really good. I'll recommend it, even with it's pacing issues.

You have to manage overheat when in a mech and have to take into account shifting gears to keep the engines all green. When the gauge is leaning left you're more defensive, when it's not doing anything (Gear 0, aka, Nuetral) you get no bonuses, and revving up your mech and making the gauge go right provides a massive bonus to your combat prowess in the mech.

Tons of actually interesting sidequests and sidestories with their own oddyseys.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR:

Modern take on a classic JRPG with some twists. Definitely takes you back but gives you something new to look forward to.

Longer Version:

I won't take too much of your time. I grew up with classic RPG's like Chrono Trigger, FF6, Super Mario RPG, etc. Recent years, it's been hard to really capture the essence of classic JRPG's but this one manages to really grasp on to it. You can tell the developers truly at their heart grew up with the classics.

There's a lot of immaturity that seeps through, yeah. And the gameplay does get a little tedious towards the 50 hour mark. But the game paces you very well so nothing is overly difficult and nothing is overly easy. Sure there's meta builds and certain skills and party set ups to optimize, but every playstyle works.

Music is great, not as catchy as the old stuff, but it's nice. Story works well, seems like they want a sequel.

If you're a fan of classic JRPG, go for it.\t\t\t\t\t
