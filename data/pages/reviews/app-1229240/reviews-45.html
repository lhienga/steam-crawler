\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing. If you love RPGs, do yourself a favor and play it. If you don't really care for RPGs, do yourself a favor and play it. If you hate RPGs, do yourself a dang favor and have this game teach you to love them. There are a lot of things I love about this game, such as:

-The characters: The main cast is great. Like other fantasy games, there are princesses, bards, soldiers, mages, and other classics. Some of them do sort of twist how that archetype generally works, such as Ba'Thraz. Speaking of Ba'Thraz, he's probably who I think is most interesting and cool. All the other members of the main cast are great as well. Sienna and Glenn are my other two favorites that I feel I can say without getting into spoilers.
-Story: I won't spoil anything. Go play it for yourself, but expect to hate some villains, fell sorrow for some characters, and catharsis when certain character arcs are finished.
-Art: It's pixel art, but it's done very well. There wasn't any design I didn't like and some areas in the game were stunning. Especially the swamp area when paired with its music.
-Music: Speaking of music, it's phenomenal. It's the music that will make you feel what the area or moment is like from sound alone. I've never just sat around in a swamp listening to music before this game.
-Combat/Gameplay: It's turn-based, but in a really dynamic way. Like other RPGs, you have your health bar and TP bar. Unlike other RPGs, you have an Overdrive bar that is split into yellow, green, and red. Yellow does nothing. Green makes skills have a cheaper TP cost and raises some stats. Red makes enemies hit harder and nothing else. You can raise it by doing pretty much anything and lower it by using the type of skill shown in the bar. Later on you unlock Sky Armor which has its own combat system with its own differences and nuances.

Again, do yourself a favor and play this game. It is phenomenal\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best JRPGs I've ever played in part because it does away with a lot of the pain points I have with other JRPGs,

Grinding isn't necessary for a normal playthrough, your major power boosts come from the main quest, side quests (which are few, meaningful and well written) and item upgrades. You can grind incremental skill improvements but I never felt the need to do so.

No random battles (ok there's a couple surprises), enemies are visible, they typically stay in place until you get into their aggro radius and in some cases they can be avoided entirely. The game has been clearly developed with momentum in mind, there's no random battle just as you're about to approach some important NPC. It is also used to organically gate off some higher level areas too.

After each battle you recover fully which means there's no tedious out-of-combat healing or item usage. The game is balanced around this which means you have to approach each fight meaningfully and be prepared to make the best use of your full resources. It also means you can go ham and don't need to worry too much if one of your party members die.

Jobs can be easily swapped between characters and you can pretty easily unlock the vast majority of skills for each character without much diversion. This gives you the freedom to play around with jobs and skills and come up with some really interesting synergies without worrying that you're bricking your game.

I really enjoyed the story in which both the backstories of the characters and the universe are revealed to you at an appropriate pace. It has some cliches sure but it uses them to craft a unique story. There are some emotional moments in this game and some parts gave me a real 'A Bird Story' vibe.

One criticism I have is that I felt the party grew too large and some characters played in a very similar style to each other. I'd like to see more focus and more diversity of play styles in the game. That being said there were some characters (e.g. Lizard Wizard) that had a really unique class fantasy so credit where credit is due.
