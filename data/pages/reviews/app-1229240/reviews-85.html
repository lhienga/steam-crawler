\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the perfect situation for me to want steam reviews to have a neutral option.

The game's aesthetic, music, and combat (on foot) is great. However, there are some glaring issues with the difficulty and story that really make it hard for me to recommend.

Broad strokes the story is actually really interesting. However there are some sections of the game that are so poorly written that it just takes you out of the immersion completely. I wish the devs had hired an editor to clean up some of the writing, this game needs it.

That by itself is not a big deal. Chances are the devs didn't have the budget for an editor, but now that it's sold a few copies they might be able to fix it. Worst case, a dedicated modder fixes it down the line.

However, the ending of the story is really, really bad and frustrating. It doesn't tie up the main threat that the game spends hours alluding to. This really makes the overall interesting story and characters fall completely flat and leaves a sour taste in my mouth. I was hoping that a game like this would have a strong, satisfying ending but instead we are left with a weird half-ending that doesn't answer all the questions we have. Maybe the devs wanted to set up for a DLC or sequel, but it seems like a bad choice for a small indie game like this.

Difficulty wise, the beginning is insanely difficult. Act 1 some of the normal enemies felt like straight up bosses. I was retrying many "easy" fights multiple times and had to go find all the equips and play everything perfect. I almost quit playing entirely multiple times, but kept hearing how great the game is so I pushed through. Then after Act 1, the game becomes a complete joke with multiple mechanics allowing you to just straight up steam roll anyone and everyone in your way. I never once felt like the game's difficulty was challenging but fair, instead it was always either completely unfair or a complete joke.

Also, the sky armor seems fun and interesting at first, but quickly becomes one of the most tedious and boring parts of the game. There are only 4 pilots and limited variety in how to play, and for some reason it's insanely easy to get a broken combo that lets you literally skip the enemy's turns for 2 turns straight. This makes every sky armor fight play out the exact same and with the limited combat variety to begin with, I ended up hating whenever a sky armor fight started.

There are many good things about this game. I loved the art style, music, and the worlds. As a final fantasy fan, this game really feels like a love letter to final fantasy in all the right ways but the glaring pitfalls really stand out. This was a world that was worth exploring and mostly fun to get lost in. However, because of the glaring story and difficulty issues, I can't wholeheartedly recommend someone spend their time getting invested here just to fall short.

Overall I would say that this is an okay game, but only play it if you don't have anything else left in the backlog, or are absolutely desperate for a fix of turn based combat gameplay (but be warned because of the balancing issues this game feels okay but never quite as good as legends like Persona 5 or FFX).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was sceptial the first few hours. Felt it was "like any other RPG". But i kept going and quickly realized that wasnt the case at all. The game is not necessarily hard, but its not easy either. The game is fair in its diffculty and if you do mistakes, the game usually punish you. Sometimes the AI script can be really mean. Technically no need for grind since there are no levels, but you might need some reasources for upgrades.

The story is very interesting. It really builds for sequels. You get teased by ALOT of things during the course of the game. But the game did end on a good note and with a finished story. The game is plottwist galore. As soon as you think youve figured something out it turns around. Some obvious, some less so. And i really want more story and game :D

A big cast of characters. Unfortunately 6 are really useful and the other 6 are really more or less worthless. In total outside of my main cast i MIGHT use 1-2 characters for a VERY specific skill then swap them out. Never have a played a game where your first ability (literal starting ability) turns out to be the top 2 skills in the game.

Combat is quick, with every combat being really hard and really easy at the same time. For every new area in the games progression you need to plan out the fights, otherwise the enemies might clean the floor with you. Of course later in game the past areas becomes much easier, but all areas still contains previously unexplored stuff that will keep you on your toes again.

It has some Suikoden elements in it. You get your own base, you recruit characters and part members to it. Very shallow however, but a nice touch. I wont spoil too much about it. I wish it was more fledged out however.

I loved the core theme of political intrigue and i love nation wars in my (J)RPGs. You are in the middle of it, but you are not a part of the main military clashes. Almost every time being a hit squad. I wish more games would make use of army battles like Suikoden did. The game has a more fantasy story focus with a political intrigue shell.

The super bosses had no real buildup except maybe one. Which is a bit of a shame. The game hints at beasts and other secret figures, but only two were actually talked and hinted about.

The biggest negative thing i have to say about this game is the crystal system. It CAN help, but its generally worthless and i kept forgetting about it. Even at very late game preparing for the super bosses i ended up forgetting to equip them to my gear. And the purity/combine system could use more work. That is most likely the most grindy bit of the game and i didnt use it in the end. It was not needed at all. Maybe expand the sharpening system more instead?

All in all a great game i had a VERY hard time letting go of. Luckily i got sick so ive been binging it the whole week.\t\t\t\t\t

For me, this made the story becomes one convoluted big mess where the story is all over the place, little twist here and there but nothing extraordinary. But, credits to the developer for his characters design, battle system, and crystal system (which need more polishing). By the way, battle is challenging even with normal difficulty, geez. With the price as it is and the content as big as Chained Echoes, I'd definitely recommend Chained Echoes\t\t\t\t\t

I suppose if you like JRPG's give this game a try, but if you dislike sporadic characters and random development for the sake of trying to do something different rather than decent story telling do NOT give this game a go, YOU WILL WASTE YOUR TIME. Or if you're playing FF7 and Cloud sacrifices himself to save Rufus Shinra, just because Cloud thinks Rufus deserves another chance to do good in the world.
