\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor me this game was 10/10 for 95% of my playtime, but the ending was written pretty horribly. Very sad, with some better writing in the final act this would've been on the same tier as 90s snes JRPGs. Especially disappointing since a lot of this game was written very well up until the last bit. I understand what the game was trying to go for themetically but it just didn't work, at least not for me. Bummed out I have to leave this game with a sour feeling, man...

For a first game this is incredible though and I still had an insane amount of fun playing through the game. It does A LOT of things right and I thought the ensemble cast were all pretty decent. It seems like Mathias is setting up a sequel but I would actually prefer seeing him work on a different IP for his next game because of my feelings towards the ending.

Either way I'd still definitely recommend a playthrough if you're a fan of JRPGs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's start by saying that this is a great game. It really is a love letter to the classic 90's RPGs. A friend of mine recommended this game by saying it's heavily inspired by Chrono Trigger. Obviously that got me instantly interested.

The story is interesting, the art style is great. The combat system is interesting but has its flaws. The fact that there's no levelling up at all in the game takes some getting used to. I admit that I very much disliked that aspect at first because you couldn't just grind and overpower your way through the content. However, you get used to it after a while and once you truly understand the game's mechanics, it's pretty straight forward. Pro-tip: the AGI stat is broken, focus on that.

The music is very good and the characters are interesting. The dialogue is okay, there are a few mistakes here and there but let's be honest here; this game was (mostly) the work of a single person, and that is absolutely mind-blowing.

Overall, it's a solid 8.5 out of 10. It's one of the rare games I actually 100%'ed and that should speak for itself.\t\t\t\t\t
