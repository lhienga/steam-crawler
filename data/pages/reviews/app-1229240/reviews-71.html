\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Good:
This is a love letter to RPG's that I have been waiting for for a long time.
The classes in this game are varied and unique, along with the skill system replacing leveling.
The characters each feel unique in their motivations without coming across as contrived or out of place.
This is the most fun I have had playing an RPG since Bravely Default.
In an era with so many remakes and disappointing square offerings, this game is a breath of fresh air.
I feel that the way the stories of the characters intertwine is what I expected of games like octopath-traveller and bravely default 2, but those games did not deliver where this game did.


QOL:
Autosave in this game is a blessing, but here is some advice, a party wipe allows you to retry the battle! Collectibles are Separated into 9 categories, Armor, Weapons, Parts, Loot, Gems, Key items, Sky armor, Sky armor weapons, and Sky armor ram.
This game allows you to run from any encounter 100% of the time, allowing you to reassess the situation or turn back.
I really like the parts and loot being separate because it means I can press sell all without feeling guilty, but if you do get extra parts sell them.
Being able to switch characters mid combat; by no means is this the first RPG to do this and by no will it be the last, I just think that it was done well here.
Separate AI and stats difficulty options allow you to pick how aggressive enemies are and how big their health pools are (among other things).
Being able to turn off screenshake (always a plus)
You're able to turn off the white flash when you encounter enemies, however, I wish this was also the case with sky armor attacks (elemental shots)


The Bad:
The Sky Armor battles seem way too bloated for their own good, especially in the early-game.
Finding NPC's who give quests is a nigh impossible task without a guide, I talk to all of the red bubble NPC's and hope they give me one.
Certain class emblems are locked behind cryptic events and slide puzzles that may require a guide.
Field bosses have some cryptic spawn conditions, but at the same time are creative in their execution (one is a building).
There are some party members that are just... garbage. straight trash. throw em in the dumpster E.G. Robb and Base Tomke.
There are sudden and random difficulty spikes in this game, this game is not easy, so do learn the mechanics quickly!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely 100% games, but when i do, i recommend the game in a steam review. The game looks and plays like traditional JRPG games (Chrono Trigger, FF VI, etc.). The first half of the game is linear / story-driven. Then the world opens up and you can explore old areas again. There are side quests, unique monsters to find, hidden chests and optional areas. Combat is challenging, but not too repetitive cause of the overdrive mechanic. Soundtrack and graphics are excellent. The story seems to be very familiar at first glance (political/religous themes), but there are multiple plot twists as the game advances. The ending i think is a bit unsatisfying, but since there are other open end storylines in the game it might be a setup for a sequel.

For everyone who likes turn based JRPGs this is a must-play.\t\t\t\t\t

The reward board system is a good incentive for players to explore the world. Having rewards capped at certain milestones also help for more casual player to not gated for upgrades.
