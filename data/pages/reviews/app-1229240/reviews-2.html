\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI still have a bit to finish, but I feel comfortable recommending this game with some caveats.

Act 1 is a bit of a slow start because the balancing feels awful for the combat. Battles felt way too grueling with not enough opportunities to strengthen your characters. It wasn't until much much later I realized that the SP amount to enhance abilities lessened as a certain level threshold was met. I still do not know where to find that information. This game is the victim of one individual wanting to try a lot of different things in a game. I don't feel they all work together completely.

So when I found out I could just lower aggression and stats on enemies without any consequence I gave that a shot. The game improved 20 fold. Things are a bit too easy now, but there is no middle ground between difficulties for this game. Hard to want to progress in the story when the fights can take forever and suddenly lead to a loss. Not fun.

Fast forward through the Acts and I am impressed at how well the writing and story building progressed. The game started off with dialogue lacking differentiating personalities. Characters would rarely use contractions or slang and were simply monotone. I found out the developer's home language wasn't English and immediately understood; but the writing did improve as the game goes on.

I'm really enjoying the way the world feels as I try to find more of these side quests. If you don't do them, the world can feel extremely hollow as a whole. Of course, right? The game gives you plenty of avenues for mobility to jump around and find these side quests. Major plus.

Music can be dull at times. There are so many opportunities for some extremely heavy music when fighting in certain equipment (limiting spoilers). Missed opportunities.

Really the only qualm I had with this game was the odd balancing for the difficulty. I can't NOT recommend a game for that reason alone. I hope in the future the developer is able to test their games with a broad range of people to see what works best with most. A lot of people will put down a game like this because of the unnecessary difficulty. I can prove that with the percentage of players who have unlocked achievements for act 2, 3, etc. 22.4% have unlocked "finish act 3".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI put in 88 hours into this masterpiece. Initially, I actually almost gave up. I didn't like the battle bar meter, the crystal system I found to be confusing, annoying and unnecessarily time consuming. I changed the setting to make the battle bar set to "Wide" which made the game a lot more enjoyable for me and it was still challenging and there were many times I lost and had to fight battles several times until I was victorious. The characters, storyline, music, items, quests made up for this. I loved being able to pick an extensive lineup of characters each with their own unique skills and abilities. There were so many characters to join your party that I still missed one and there were still so many characters to choose from it didn't even matter, my party was still super stacked. I see people comparing this game to Sea of Stars which I spent about half the time to beat. They're both amazing in their own respects. I loved both for different reasons and they're both masterpieces that left me wanting more as I got towards the end. If you liked these two, you would also like Cross Code which I actually like even more but there are a lot more puzzles in Cross Code. Chained Echoes has an average amount of puzzles I would say but you don't really see them until you reach halfway through the game. All in all I give Chained Echoes an 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

-Clan Upgrades
-Customize characters through armor/gems
-Dialogue box's so you know who you need to speak to
