A. Lazy - they didn't spend enough time in the game to appreciate it.
B. Masochistic - They spent 50+ hours on it, to then not recommend it.

Like, I get it. Some of the mechanics could have been "fine tuned". Which I disagree on, but I understand the point they are making. Or the crystal system complaint. Which again, I disagree. But if we review this game solely based on game play, story, graphics. It is a recommend every time. If we review this game in comparison to the heaps of piles of ♥♥♥♥ out there, it is a major RECOMMEND every time. Now, and I mean this is the last piece that shouldn't even be taken into account. But throw in the fact that one dude built it? Come on. I played the game without knowing any of this. Based only on the algorithm that is used to get me to buy more games on Steam.

Simply put. If you are a fan of JRPG's with turn based combat and story rich details and love Breath of Fire I II, Lufia I II, FFI-VIII, Chrono Trigger types. This game is phenomenal. It is one of those games where you search the guides and walk-through sites just to see if you have anything else you can do before you complete. A game that when I finished, I was buying anything remotely close to it when Steam suggested I try it. Unfortunately, this was a diamond in the rough.

I rarely put reviews on games because typically the reviews capture the essence of how I feel about the game already. But I was looking for a new game that was similar to Chained Echoes, and of course read some of the bad reviews and it inspired me to write this.

Do I think everyone will love this game as much as I do? No. But it is a simple If/then statement. IF this is the genre you like. IF this is the gameplay you like. IF this is the kind of story you like. IF you aren't a POS.

THEN yes. Buy it. Play it. Pray to whatever figure you want to that he will make another one like it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFull completion, 100 percent of achievements acquired without ever touching new game+. From start to finish this game was a blast! I enjoyed both the story and game mechanics, and after finishing everything it's finally time to drop a review.

So let's start off with the story. Personally I'm a strong proponent of making a story that's engaging and crafted from your own hands, and not trying to seek out doing something new that no one's ever done before. I had a great time going through Chained Echoes's story, and it does a good job fleshing out the world and its characters. It is a solid conduit to advance story elements and keep the player engaged with the game mechanics. Is the story mind blowing? No, but that is 100% completely fine (and, of course, your mileage will vary depending on what genres or stories you're looking for). There are some segments where I felt like they could've had more time, and then at other moments the world building really knocked it out of the park. Overall, I enjoyed what the story had to offer.

Speaking of the worldbuilding, same deal. The story offers enough information to understand what's at stake and who the big movers and shakers are. Whatever tidbits the game offered about the world I was interested in learning about, and most of the time you simply get to these tidbits by progressing the story. There's not a huge amount of lore books to comb over and you don't have to really go too far out of the way to find these lore books either. In total there's like... somewhere around 8 lore books, and I was only missing one because I accidentally advanced the plot lmao! Now granted, there are some questions leftover when you finish the game, but they're not vital to the main plot, so everything around the main storyline felt rounded up solidly to the end.

So that's all for the story. Thankfully, the game mechanics also felt pretty well rounded and complimented each other from start to finish. I do have some gripes, but let me start off with what I enjoyed.

There are moments where party members will leave and come back during the story, and there are also some party members you won't get unless you go through some sidequests. Regardless, the game does a good job at playing catch up for them, and will allot whatever skill points they missed to catch them up with your main party. They will also have the appropriate tiered gear on them if they're a party member that shows up late in the story. This mechanic shows up early in the game so you know what will happen when party members come and go, and I definitely appreciated this mechanic to help keep grinding at a minimum.

You can upgrade your gear two times at maximum, and that basically closed the gap if you somehow got unlucky and couldn't find the next tier of equipment throughout the world (this didn't happen to me thankfully lmao). Shops do eventually open up to let you purchase the next tier, and sometimes there were moments that my old gear was much better because they were upgraded and also modded. You can do some really cheesy stuff with the modding system, so if you like seeing numbers go higher or enjoy seeing enemies suffer what is basically permanent blindness, you might also enjoy the modding system too.

There's another layer of combat, and while this layer isn't as cheeseable, it thankfully still involves 4 party members and some simple additional mechanics. This layer is known as sky armor combat. With your party you'll be learning and skilling up abilities with experience they accumulate as the game progresses (known as SP). With sky armor equipment, the skills are tied to the equipment, and skilling them up requires you to: 1) Have them equipped on your sky armor, and 2) Just fight. That's basically it on how you both level up sky armor skills and level up your sky armors lmao

Alright so now the gripes. Overall, I had a great time with this game. It kept me engaged from start to finish, and it's been ages since I ever 100%'d a game but this one just kept luring me through, and before I knew it I was only a few more achievements away from full completion. There are definitely some rough edges with certain things, but my biggest issue was the equipment modification system, specifically its UI.

The modding process will EASILY eat your time up. Whether you're upgrading equipment, or trying to dabble with the modding system, you have to go through a pause with a sound cue before you're able to continue. Upgrading equipment? Removing crystals from a piece of equipment? Slotting in crystals into a new piece of equipment? Combining crystals to make a better one? TINK TINK.

The sound wasn't the issue, it was the constant waiting for the pause and sound cue to finish! Throughout my entire time playing I was constantly going back and forth wondering if this system was worth it because the accumulated time waiting felt awful. Everything throughout the game felt so snappy and quick, and then I get to working with this system and it was a slog to go through. If the devs are able to remove that pause? Honestly the system would be perfect! There's another issue involving the discarding of modding crystals, and that there's no confirmation when you're throwing crystals away (no you can't sell them). Thank GOODNESS I never accidentally threw away a perfectly good, max level crystal, but it definitely scared and concerned me that there was no warning confirmation before throwing crystals away.

So, I mentioned about going back and forth about dealing with the modding system, and I eventually just got used to the waiting, and it was a good thing I did too. Overall, with the right combination of mods to the right party members, you can really cheese through fights! Out of all the superbosses (which are optional) you can fight, there were only 2 that really gave me any trouble... but the issue is, the one that gave me the most trouble I thought flat-out wasn't fair at all, and it was MILES ABOVE more annoying compared to the other superboss I had trouble with.

This one superboss, unless I was extremely lucky, is the only enemy in literally the ENTIRE game, with the ability to one-shot not ONE, but TWO of your party members in a turn. I am not talking about OVER DAMAGE, THIS SUPERBOSS JUST INSTA-KILLS 2 PARTY MEMBERS. I had such a visceral reaction when this happened because nothing else in the ENTIRE game ever telegraphed that this would be possible enemy ability. None. NONE OF THE OTHER SUPERBOSSES, OR THE FINAL BOSS FIGHT IN THE GAME, HAVE THIS ABILITY EITHER.

Thankfully, I was able to beat this one superboss after upgrading and modding my gear and turned them into mince meat within 3-4 turns. But this specific superboss really stands as a bizarre anomaly to me. I genuinely don't think it was fair to make a very special mechanic for literally one enemy like this. It feels extremely cheap when the rest of the game was great at telegraphing and providing expectations to the player, so this specific superboss stands out in a way that's extremely negative for me. if you have trouble trying to beat this boss you're assuredly not alone, but thankfully it's easily doable, and even cheeseable!

Alright, so that basically concludes my review. TLDR: I had a good time with the game, the story is nice, the combat is nice, the modding system can be annoyingly slow, and there's an optional superboss near the end of the game that feels extremely cheap compared to everything else you encounter in the game because of one single ability. And so I'll end with this last note: If you feel like playing a solid RPG, that also provides options with some flexibility on difficulty, and also lets you fast-forward combat, then Chained Echoes may very well be in your scope of interest.
