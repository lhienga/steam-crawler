\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI usually get bored halfway through when playing story driven games, but Chained Echoes had me immersed through the full playthrough. I even got out of my way to complete all side quests and still didn't get bored.

Story is amazing and the pacing is done really well!
I liked everything except the ending, it felt a bit weak to me. Reminded me a lot of the wheel of time ending, but I liked how Chained Echoes did it more.
I expected it to wrap up everything, but the ending felt like a setup for the sequel. It's still a good ending, I just would prefer for it to go differently.

Biggest thing missing for me is that portrait faces don't have emotions like in visual novels. Felt really lacking, especially with the king brother and some parts in the story would be much more impactful if portraits had different emotions in those parts of the text.
This change would make it a 10/10 for me. Without it is 9.5/10.

It's probably my top 3 game, maybe no2. Can't believe it's mostly made by one person.
I can definitely recommend it if you are a fan of jrpgs or turn based combat. Story is good, but not sure if you should get it in case you are not fan of turn based.\t\t\t\t\t

The story is right up there with the best of them.

The gameplay is great, although I think some characters didn't get fully fleshed out. I think they could have trimmed the roster a bit.

The music is very well done.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a beautiful, highly polished game. The graphics are exquisite, despite being clearly "modern retro". The music is likewise of a high professional caliber. The characters are well built and memorable. The story is not the best so far, but it's good.

If you are not a JRPG fan, and simply want a story-centric action adventure (with turn-based combat), you might enjoy it and you can ignore this review.

However, the game has one fatal flaw in that it does not allow you to decide when you should become stronger in order to face the challenges it throws at you. Progression is very much on rails, and none of the choices presented to you (either story or mechanics-wise) are real.

Skill points are given to you when the game decides you should get better. There is no way to grind other than what you can buff with a limited choice of equipment purchases and upgrade options. The game micromanages your party for you to the point of there being a "optimize equipment" option that equips your party the "best" way.

This means there is always an optimal way to pick your skills and use them in battle in order to win. If you deviate too much from the optimal path, you cannot progress. If you deviate just in the right way, the game becomes impossible to beat. You get soft-locked because you didn't play the way the developer intended you to. The frustration this adds to the constant feeling of being babysit by the game really got to me.

This also means there is no point in getting past battles that you can dodge or flee from. You are given a constant trickle of upgrade materials anyway.

I referred to this as a "fatal flaw" because you can come to a point where you will be forced to load a previous save and "play the game right" so that you can continue.

All in all, it looks great, but when you get down to it, it's a very "gamified" experience. A very far cry from what a JRPG is supposed to be. It's a completely on rails experience where you will be punished for deviating from the path the developer had in mind. This also means it has zero replayability.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have mixed feelings about this game.
Even so, I find myself recommending it because I think there's something truly well made here.
Let's get to my issues:
- I have played the game on the hardest settings, because auto attacking my way to victory isn't a strategy and I value my time.
My criticism stems from having experienced this game on those settings. Well, it wasn't balanced. As long as you'll play on normal you won't notice these cracks, but he 2nd part of the game is really, really tough, to a point where there seems to just be a viable strategy for each boss, and if you deviate from that, you're dead.
No problem , we retry the battle and get to turn 10, where I died, hoping the boss won't use one of his attacks to wipe you, like he just did in your previous attempt, so you get a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ heal on your party.
If we do a thought exercise and give an AI or a human control of the enemy, you won't ever be able to win on hard. They have moves in their pool which they could always use and wipe you, you just have to pray to RNJesus the enemy doesn't use X move in a row, else you start again and have to go through 10 turns, then pray he does something different than before.
The "act-twice" or "act-thrice" gimmick some bosses use should be toned the ♥♥♥♥ down, ESPECIALLY in Sky-Armor fights.

-Speaking of that, my second issue is with Sky armor fights. This game has mech battles, which function drastically different from normal encounters. You have to plan ahead and switch gears of your mech so you deal more dmg or have more defense depending on which gear you switch on.
At first, I thought this was a cool mechanic, but then I got to Act 3 and is there when I realized these mech battles truly outstayed their welcome. There's wayyyyyy too many mech sections, which take away from the charm the game built up in its first half, by doing normal encounters.
On hard, even normal encounters are possible to win with some brainstorming and tweaks, maybe you go do a side quest and gain a lvl or something, to make it easier.
Mech battles are borderline impossible on some bosses, in the 2nd half, so dont be ashamed to lower the difficulty to normal. Looking at you Whyatt.
Really smart to combine a mandatory story boss with 'act-thrice' vs your mechs. That's a really good example where this specific boss should've only resumed to 'act-twice' on hard mode.

-Final criticism is about some parts of the story which fall flat due the lack of voice acting. In very pivotal story points, characters take life altering decisions which fail to land and it doesn't click with the player because is just a text on screen. There's not much thought put into it, or emotion. They just do it because is the right thing and I don't buy it.
They do a bad job to also emotionally react to some of these decisions. Is like "meh, I guess".

Let's get to the good parts:

-Game is well written, considering the number of people involved in this project. The writing is mature, and not like your average JRPG. You won't find nakama powers here. This is a story about the brutal horrors of war and people's lives during war-torn times.
Game has sarcasm, good jokes and funny interactions too. Is not all doom and gloom. Is more like there's no ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ here and is more anchored to reality.

-Game takes a healthy inspiration from Suikoden, FF12, Link to the past. There's a lot of optional stuff you can do, and you're rewarded handsomely for tackling it.

-Sienna is the best written character. You'll have to play the game and see it for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game because Asmongold said it is the best game of 2022.
After completing the game and 100%ing the achievements, I can say that this is one of the best JRPGs I have played in quite some time. I have become, in recent years, one of those gamers that just wants to throw myself into the game, have fast action and don't care too much about story but Chained Echoes managed to bring back the old me that loves a gripping storyline, characters and sitting through cutscenes. I highly recommend this game to any fan of the genre, and without going into any spoilers, hope that with all the rich world building and open questions a sequel gets announced.

Detailed Review: 9/10
Art: 10/10
Music: 10/10
Story: 10/10 (honestly a highlight considering how lazy some indie games are when it comes to story and just putting together something boring and predictable. Amazing world building, twists and detail put into this game)
Difficulty: 0/10 Hard mode is too easy makes the game boring. I don't mind ass blasting your ♥♥♥♥♥♥ game through easy mode
Mechanics/Battle system: 7/10 (The overdrive system is a fun and unique system, didnt have any issues with it. The main mechanic that brings the game down for me is the crystal system being complete RNG and the crafting system. Additionally, some characters were cool conceptually but pretty useless in game. You can just use the same 5 or 6 characters for the entire playthrough and never worry about the others).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn amazing JRPG. I don't think I can add to what has already been said? Simply put, you will likely enjoy this if you like SNES-era JRPGs with modern QoL features.

I'd say the only flaw in the game is that the writing can be on the weak side sometimes. There's a few too many anime/other game references for my taste, and although the overall story is great, it lacks emotional impact at certain times.

Although I don't consider it a flaw, the developer does try to streamline many of the traditional mechanics that slow down turn-based combat games, I suppose to appeal to a wider audience. This includes no level grinding, fast travel from anywhere, no healing after battle, and generally fast combat animations.

That all being said, the gameplay, aesthetics, characters, and sound quality are all top notch and easily compensate for any deficiencies. 10/10 recommendation from me.\t\t\t\t\t

Graphics are super basic but not offensive on the eyes. Super unique take on turn based combat that is actually quite challenging as long as you don't fiddle with that difficulty slider and set the enemies stats on "Low" and make them less aggressive with yet another slider for making the game accessible. Trust me on this: if you leave the difficulty exactly where it is at by default you can expect a tough time at different moments in the story. Almost without question be prepared for some wipes even during the games first Act (it's kinda long....).
