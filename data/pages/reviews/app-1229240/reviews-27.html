\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game made me feel things. It has heart and depth that makes it a joy to play and each minute can be gripping.

The structure is very much a classic JRPG; start by running errands, escalate to a conflict with divinity. Sometimes the delivery feels a little clunky, but the story-telling is consistent with itself. Despite encompassing a healthy cast of characters and a "save the world" scope, each moment and party member has personality and motivation. The plot is very much a linear series of chapters that aren't impacted by player choice. It's refreshingly well-constructed though, and a good callback to a time before dialogue trees

The gameplay is classic and mechanically satisfying. The combat is turned-based with depth that's added in that's both easy to learn and fun to master. There are always options for which action to take and risks and rewards with each action. In terms of growth and progression, I felt like I never had to grind to keep up with bosses, and the natural growth of my party was rewarding enough.

Altogether a game I would definitely recommend to any fan of classic RPGs or even to someone trying them out for the first time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn a year where I played both Crystal Project and Octopath Traveler 2 I seriously doubted I would stumble upon another classic JRPG style masterpiece. Yet here it is.

Chained Echoes is of subtle genius. It does not do much on first glance that elevates the genre.
However, its amusing writing, fantastic pacing, improvements to well known points of tedium in RPGs and colorful cast of characters makes it deserve a stand with one of the greats of the genre.

After roughly 60 hours of gameplay time it took to 100% this game the fun never let up and it is definitely an experience I will be cherishing for the following years.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is an homage to old-school SNES JRPG; it combines the best aspects of Chrono Trigger, which is a fantasy-based game and Xenogears, which is a steampunk-based game. The world is beautiful and has a lot of side quests and mini games that you can get into. The story gives you the feeling that you're really part of the world, puts a lot of obstacles in front of you, and shows you that you're not alone and can get stronger by making friends and having them help you along the way. Skills can also be acquired by attaining grimoire shards, which give you and your friends new abilities. You can also traverse the world using mechs by flying them around or even fighting with aerial monsters or enemies in the air. One of the most impressive aspects of this game is the battle system, which fixes every issue I had with older JRPGs that was giving me the feeling that every battle was meaningless. The battle mechanic adds complexity while remaining simplistic.

Anyone who is a fan of JRPGs and wants something fresh, innovative and modern should play this game. It has one of the most intriguing stories out of all the JRPGs that I've played, with a gameplay mechanic and an aerial battle system that I've never seen implemented in a JRPG.

Rating: 9.5/10 (Universally Acclaimed)\t\t\t\t\t
