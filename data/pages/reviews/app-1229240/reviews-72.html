\r\n\t\t\t\t\t8/10. A full zero filler 40+ hour experience for $20. I did not complete some of the optional endgame content, so probably about 50hrs to 100% the game. With that in mind, a $20 price tag on this game feels like stealing. This game is an old school JRPG experience and its easy to see why some people consider this their game of the year. That said if you don't like turn based gameplay you aren't going to magically like it in this game either.

Story: excellent with only a few pacing issues. It gets moving fairly fast and doesn't ever devolve into "collect the four crystals" or whatever story beats are so painfully familiar to anyone whose played an RPG, especially one so obviously inspired to older generations of RPG. The story is dynamic and rapidly changing as you begin to see certain events from different perspectives and piece together whats going on. This is one of those JRPGs that's really fun to speculate about upcoming events as you go through the game because its foreshadowing is generally not clubbing you over the head with the next story beat. Also, this story can be pretty dark at times and it does not shy away from violence. This game got an audible "wtf" out of me a few times which is always good because that only really happens if you are invested in the events.

The games greatest strength is its villains. You can easily understand the motivations of essentially every "bad guy" in the game, most of whom would be heroes in their own right if the story were told from a different perspective. Nobody is being evil just because they are evil. Only one character ever made me think the character was simply a fool. Everyone else was just misguided/working with incomplete information or outright doing evil things for what is clearly and obviously a greater good. Because of that winning didn't always feel like winning. If this were an RPG with branching choices, many players would have at times sided with the villains which is always a good thing in a story like this.

The main party dynamics are weaker than the villains and by a long shot. Some characters are optional and basically don't talk at all once introduced. The game focuses heavily on three of your party members. I didn't really get super attached to anyone, but I also didn't dislike anyone. It still works out pretty well because the game is more about the worlds politics, events, war, and opposing factions than it is about you. MC is good.

Pacing: Generally good. The game alternates big story beats with game play and optional side quests to try and keep things fresh. Sadly its pretty bad at this at the very start of the game where the first open field is very long with little going on and extremely basic combat which drags on.

Combat: I LOATHE turn based combat, yet finished this game. That's probably largely down to this game having relatively few trash mob fights in it compared to your standard RPG. It also has zero grinding unless you count some sky armor stuff mid game. Unlike what some other reviewers are saying I don't think this combat is anything special. You basically do the same routine every battle no matter what you are fighting starting around the mid game where you have most of your tools. The exception is bosses which are almost all solid. There were only two bosses that stood out to me as absolutely awful; one had a tedious mechanic and the other was lacking bad luck protection on a particularly overtuned AOE. The hardest boss in the game is an optional boss shortly after the start of the game which casts an aoe that does 1/2 your hp bar to every party member. It gets to use two moves per turn. Guess what happens if it casts the aoe twice in the same turn.

The start of the game like in any turn based is super basic and there exists no animation skip button that I'm aware of.

Character customization: your characters don't level up and instead get points to spend to either learn a move or increase a base stat. It all works pretty well, though you can easily still "outlevel" things if you explore a lot after the game opens up about 1/4 of the way through the game. You will eventually get everything and just start throwing points in random stuff because you can only equip so many spells and passive abilities. There are of course some major game breakers, but that's ok.

All in all, a great game that is absolutely worth $20 if and only if you like old school JRPGs. Don't discount this just because its turn based.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was initially going to leave a negative review because of my major gripes with this almost-masterpiece, but I think it's worth remembering that this is an almost-masterpiece and you'd be doing a disservice to yourself by not playing it because it is a 9/10 instead of a 10/10.

That being said, let's address some issues:

1. The ending - it leaves SO MANY unresolved plot lines and so many questions unanswered. It truly soured an otherwise amazing experience, and I truly hope a DLC solves that in the future.

2. The crystal system - look, I grinded crystals until I got X level 1 slot ones. For most of my team. First off - removing crystals should not remove potential or increase their size. It made me hoard the good crystals until I got the very best gear in the game, and at that point you get to use it for an amazing number of like 3 battles. Besides, you only get the ability to get only one type of crystal in the last let's say 5% of the time you spend with the game. This is coupled with...

3. The gear problem - not only is the main gear progression (weapons and armor) not interesting at all - just more stats for better gear - it also highlights the major flaws of the crystal system. You will be changing gears with basically each little part of the main quest you do, and you know what that entails? Menuing. Hours and hours of menuing trying to manage which character gets what, which upgrades you can do and all of that for an increase of what, 5-10% of an offensive or defensive stat? And that number gets lower the longer you progress, to the point I wasn't even changing gear in the late game until I got 2 or 3 tiers above my current gear. Besides, since removing crystals is so punishing you are encouraged to keep using the same gear over long periods of time, so getting new gear just makes you think "oh god another 15 minutes of menuing for basically nothing".

4. I wont delve much into this point but Frederik. Oh god what a poorly written character. He is fundamental for the plot of the majority of the game but never gets a deep dive into his personality, he's always being a tyrant without much reason, going against his advisors and for what? Nothing, no end plan, and his plot line just cuts abruptly when the game feels like it's time to move onto the 'more important' issues. His actions felt incredibly random during the whole game and honestly the only way to fix this is to give him more screen time, develop his emotions and character further and make me actually see any reasoning behind the otherwise unreasonable actions he takes.

Other than that the game is very good. Go play it, it's still worth it despite those glaring issues.\t\t\t\t\t

A refreshing retro-like RPG that draws heavy inspiration from PS1 and SNES era JRPGs.

Pros:
Refreshing battle system
Well written characters (Sienna has my favorite character arc.)
New-ish take on plot points
Pacing is okay
Multiple character and party builds are possible and viable
Amount of game time for game price is amazing.
(40 hours for 500php)
Nothing felt like it was included for padding game time.
Graphics are beautiful and stays true to the pixel rpg vibe.
Many easter eggs alluding to multiple classic JRPGs. (The Xenogears easter eggs are my favorites. The one Pokemon one was surprising though hahaha!)

Cons:
-For some, no option to overlevel might be a turn off.
-Crystal system could use a better tutorial and UI improvement.
-Sky armor combat feels like it had less time to cook vs on ground combat.
8.5/10

I'll add a 0.5 to the score since this was made primarily (minus music and art) by 1 person, Matthias Linda, over a span of 3 years.
Final Score is 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t
