\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is a testament to and standalone argument for the creative output generated through appropriation.

An love letter that shamelessly pulls obvious elements from Berserk, Fullmetal Alchemist, Code Geass, Gundam, and (to my surprise and delight) Final Fantasy XII, all while fluidly combining them with the charm and flourish of Chrono Trigger.

While the similarities are there and in your face about it, the story and world of Chained Echoes are all its own. I won't be modest when I say it is hard to surprise me in a narrative nowadays, especially in a TBRPG, but one of the plot twists genuinely shocked me in a supremely pleasant way.

Some of the dialogue in parts of the game seemed to suffer from cliché, but the lore, complexity of the characters, and narrative more than compensate for this.

10/10 Waiting for a sequel or DLC

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes came out in early 2023 and got overshadowed by all the other great games released that year. Even in its niche of classic JRPGs, it was somewhat lost behind the success of Sea of Stars. I have almost missed it, and that would have been sad, because Chained Echoes is amazing. It's a great example of the "steal like an artist" principle. It borrows a lot from the classic JRPGs (Xenogears, Final Fantasy, Chrono Cross, you name it), as well as other media, from Star Wars to Evangelion, it never feels like mere regurgitation of someone else's ideas. Instead it builds upon its inspiration to create an epic adventure capable of standing on its own. What's even better, Chained Echoes learns from the predecessors' mistakes, offering a lot of improvement in the gameplay and quality of life departments. It spares you a lot of little gameplay inconveniences (like having to manually restore health after every combat by drinking 10 of your 999 healing potions) and lets you focus on the fun parts of the gameplay, and, of course, the story. Whether you are an oldschool fan of SNES era games, or just enjoy a good classic-style JRPG, don't miss it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, I finished the game 100% except for a newgame+ file, so that's something. As I've gotten older, this happens less and less as I lose interest in titles at some point.

Story was serviceable, but the ending is a bit divisive. Some will try to accuse the game of being "Woke" but those elements are very minor, mostly just brought up in passing instead of being made the main focus of the game like some developers erroneously do. One of the characters is a lesbian, for instance, so that will probably turn off some who care overly much about such things.

Turn-based combat is pretty good with the overdrive system, but can be a major frustration, especially in some end boss fights where enemy actions push you into overheat and you can barely manage to get out of it.

Overall enjoyable for a single playthrough, though some characters being far more powerful than others made much of the roster pointless as you'll end up using the same characters until the end out of convenience.

I'm looking forward to the developer's next title to see if he can improve on this formula and hash together a better story, but until then, if you like JRPG's, than this should be right up your alley.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who is enjoying jrpg's since the NES days, this game was an easy buy once i found out about it. But lo and behold, it exceeded every expectation i had. The combat is a perfect throwback to the classic JRPG trope, but freshens it up with some mechanics. Music is outstanding and the graphics are faithful to the era. What however tops it all, is the story. The story is an 11/10 without a single doubt. I cannot recommend this game enough. If you like classic turn based jrpg's, this is a game you need to play. At the point of writing i finished the game and 100%ed it in my first go, which took 52.7hours of playtime, so more than generous if you are a completionist at the pricepoint of (currently) 30€. If you dont care for full completion and dont listen to every npc's story, my guess is a solid 30~ish hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely outstanding and shocking that it was made by such a small team. One of the best RPGs I've played in the past few years and more fun than I've had with most recent AAA games. I love the skill system; I actually really enjoyed the updated crystal system, too. Story was engaging, characters were well written. Maps and puzzles were well designed. Not to mention LOTS of secrets and side quests to explore.

I enjoyed the battle system, too. It's definitely easy to settle into a routine of skills and preferred party members but I really didn't mind that. Enemies at times felt a bit spongy and late game you can absolutely demolish enemies. I beat the ultimate secret boss with minimal effort. Not sure he got more than one turn. Love how TP/HP reset each battle so I didn't feel restricted during each battle and could really go all out.

Music also was good. Nothing outrageously memorable, nothing annoying, and fit the locations well. For some reason, one location in particular really reminded me of Nier Automatica, which kept on giving me random jolts of nostalgia.

Don't hesitate to pick this up. And, here's to hoping for a sequel because this game really deserves it.\t\t\t\t\t
