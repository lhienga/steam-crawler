\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen judging this game I like to use 2 different scores: One as a game as a whole and one considering this was made by one developer without the budget of a big studio.

By itself its 7/10 but as a single developer this is 9/10 easy. It has a well developed story, lots of characters, and tons of exploration. Below is a breakdown of my pros and cons.

Pros:
-Story is told well and each character has development and background.
-Music per area is varied and reflects the mood well.
-Fights are challenging and require deep planning.
-Character customization is fun and diverse.

Cons:
-Upgrade system is confusing and not as well explained as it needs to be and the benefit is very marginal considering you can get an upgrade so soon it makes it seem pointless. (Can fix this by allowing continual upgrades based on where you are in the story to make it stay relevant or make unique bonus effects that can make it worth using even if the attack power is slightly less i.e. fire buff, on hit proc, etc. Be creative.
-Some battles are unbalance and ridiculously hard. Now for this one, it isn't that some battles are not beatable it's that the game doesn't ramp up well to train you HOW to approach those battles. It doesn't get incrementally harder in the right way so all of a sudden you really have to micro your character combinations, gem slots and skills to stack % modifiers while considering debuffs and weaknesses. This is good and bad just wish it were slowly instilled from the beginning to MAKE players think about it in depth more.
-Pacing of the story in the second half is WAY TOO FAST and the ending feels super rushed and out of nowhere. The story is good but too heavy upfront and could be spread out over 2 additional acts in my opinion.

Overall this is an easy recommend and its clear this was a passion project. I will definitely be looking out for this developer going forward and their next project will get my support if the quality is anything like this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI 100%ed this game, and while I did enjoy parts of it, I would say the bad outweighed the good.

Let's start with why I liked it:
-Beautiful graphics
-Jamming music
-100% success rate on escaping battles that allow you to escape (keep this phrasing in mind)
-Incredible side quests
-Some character customizing through chosen skills and crystals slotted in equipment
-To successfully apply certain status ailments (paralyze, blind, silence) again, you need to apply it (X + 1) times where X is the number of times that status ailment was already applied, which is both good and bad but balanced. This way, you can't immediately suffer from the same status ailment you cured if the enemy keeps spamming it, but it's bad for you if you wanted to spam that, too. I've never seen another game do something this fair.

Here's what I didn't like:
-Unexplained questions for certain characters, such as Markas, Caspar, Masked Man, and June
-Somewhat unoriginal story
-Some plot twists are only twists because of how long it took for characters to say them
-Some equipment upgrades give diminishing stat returns despite costing more materials
-You can't freely combine crystals, remove/add crystals to gear, or upgrade gear away from an anvil. It's too restrictive for no reason. If I get better gear, it's only potentially better with upgrades and my current gear's crystals, but I can't use the better gear until I get to an anvil who-knows-where in a dungeon unless I want to leave for an anvil, but that would respawn all the enemies I already killed.
-You can't freely scroll up/down the map while picking the fast travel crystals to warp to. The map only moves based on what crystal your cursor selects. This means you need to warp there and then open up the map to scroll up/down before choosing where to really warp to.
-Most negative reviews talk about Overdrive and how much they hate the balancing act or the skill compromise, so I'm not gonna add to that here. Just know that managing Overdrive is much harder in the first half of the game since you don't have that many different skill types to pick from to balance the randomly chosen Overdrive skill type the game wants.
-Making rank 10 crystals is such a pain because removing crystals from gear reduces a crystal's purity to 0. Every point of purity allows that crystal to absorb more crystals to add to its rank, but I can't do that if the purity goes to 0, so that means I have to hoard the crystal I'm raising to rank 10 instead of using it while I'm gathering crystals of the same type. Also, artificial crystals (a crystal that absorbed another crystal) can't be used as fodder, which means you better hope whatever crystal you picked started with a high enough rank, preferably 3 instead of 2 or 1 and that it has enough purity to reach rank 10 because any rank below that is gonna be a rank 5 crystal instead for whatever reason. We couldn't just have an effect that's slightly weaker than rank 10 for people who just aren't lucky enough for the RNG crystal BS?
-If getting the right crystal purity/rank isn't hard enough, there's sorting through all the bloat of useless crystal effects that you'll never use. An aquatic killer crystal lets you do more damage to aquatic type enemies, but you never fight a ton of X type enemy that I would ever want to use this over something more generalized, like an Agi Up crystal for my slower characters. They're always gonna want those because they're so slow.
-Gimmicky enemies can go to hell. Their gimmicks, like Hana Doll's non-physical/non-magical 5,000 damage attack that's reduced only by hitting it for 5,000 damage or silencing/paralyzing to delay it is more annoying than it is fun.
-Skill leveling is either very lackluster in the form of TP usage reduced or amazing, like raising the HP percentage amount upon reviving from 10% to 50%. Where's the consistency?
-Poison ticks off a bigger chunk of your character's HP than it does for the enemy if they're poisoned. So much for status ailment balance outside of application counts.
-Tank characters aren't designed well. They're supposed to draw aggro from enemies, but if most enemies use multi-targeting attacks, there's nothing your tank can do. Unless you use the skill that makes enemies only hit the tank with a single-targeting attack for one turn, your tanks may not always draw enough aggro even with all the skills that add more because another character used a big aggro drawing skill that overwhelmed your tank's aggro.
-You can't customize Sky Armor skills even if you max out the weapon the skills are attached to. A lot of Sky Armor skills on certain weapons suck, such as Quick Heal and Counter Stance. Why would I want a single-targeting healing spell when I could use the multi-targeting one that's only slightly weaker in healing? Why would I spend a turn to apply the buff of countering single-targeting physical attacks with a single-targeting physical attack if I can't pair it with the aggro raising skill? Enemies could bypass it with multi-targeting attacks, single-targeting magic attacks, or attacking someone else instead.
-What's the point of letting me customize the airship if I can't see it in future cutscenes, like I could see before I was able to customize the airship? The little sprite on the overworld isn't worth 75,000 or 100,000 arc, especially when that arc could've been spent on a 50,000 arc Sky Armor. I'm still salty that I spent 100,000 arc for an airship that never saw the light of day in the story only to learn right after saving over my file that a 50,000 arc Sky Armor price tag is attached to completing a certain quest.
-Some optional fights, such as Statue of Leonar fights and superbosses that don't have a cutscene before the fight, don't let you escape to changing your formation or equipment accordingly to better prepare. I know I could throw the fight and format everything after a Game Over, but it's way faster if I could just escape, like any other optional fight.\t\t\t\t\t

It takes clear inspiration from several titles. Let me explain what from where
Encounter system: Chrono Cross
Skill System: FF9 style with its own twist
Combat: Very FFX style but with its own twists
Story: A mix of FFX, Berserk, and its own twists
FFXII Achievement board

Overall Review
The story is good. Most things are explained some won't be its unfortunate but if it is resolved in a follow up game I think could be nice. The end felt slightly rushed. Minor ass-pullish stuff but I wasn't too thrown off by it. The message it attempted to send at the end about redemption is a good message but was not delivered in the best way. Basically I do not believe the character who is a chronic back stabber would be a good legendary hero and would change his ways within a few moments maybe over the course of a lifetime in a different period of time. Gameplay is good. Worth playing at full price. Unfortunately Tomke is a useless character. I nearly 100% this game I did every boss and super boss but only got about 6 of his skills. Some characters just aren't as good as others unintentionally. Also the characters that people paid money on the kick-starter to put into the game are very out of place, unexplained, and suffer from "I don't want my OC to die!". All the paid characters don't die they just flee or escape or disappear and have no relevance to the actual story. They are just there and then they are gone. Some characters story arch's are back stories don't really line up with who they are at the start of the game Sienna is one of my favorite characters but who she is at the start is in a very stark contrast between her back story in a way that is nearly unbelievable. Rob just changes towards the end which is nice but doesn't seem deep since he just changes but has no real motivation to do so. Vincent and Bathraz are by far the most consistent and well written of the main crew. The monk girl is also out of place and having her be a child just doesn't fit with her dialogue. Sometimes its adultish sometimes its childish. Just seemed odd that a child would be keeping pace with the party. Glenn's character seems to forget who he is at the end and all his reasons for what he does. This game is a master piece up until the latter 65%. The story falls off and the gameplay gets samey. Sky armors are cool! However there are some places where they feel inconsistent. For some reason in some forests and some caves you can have them drop from the sky but in others you cannot. There are only 2 seraph class armors which felt sad because you have a party of 4 people. You get weapons and armor way too fast meaning that once ive upgraded the new gear i just got in the next area i will get gear that is already better than it. This means you never get to really put cool gems and combos for your gear in any armor as you'll just be churning through it so fast. It wouldn't be so bad except when you remove gems they degrade in quality meaning that removing them basically means you won't get them anymore. Enemy specific attack modifiers like beast killer are useless. You do not get gems fast enough. I nearly 100% the game. I never got one X level gem after trying to combo them. The furthest I got was VI. Gems are poorly balanced and you'll never use them to their fullest in a standard playthrough and you have no reason to. Its not needed for any bosses or enemies. Sky armor progression is way too slow until the end when all the sudden you get mK 4-6 in an hour or two. There are a few places where tiles are missing or in the wrong layer but its not a big deal. There is a skip you can run to skip Egyl boss fight Its not hard you can basically walk around him. The overdrive system is great. Until its not. Sometimes you get screwed by the move type you need to use and it makes players play in a not always fun way. The class system is great but a lot of them really are just useless. They are good for stat increases but for the most part the really great ones also have the best stat increases. No one and I mean no one in this game needs S Tp scaling. Its worthless. This game was clearly planned to be longer and ran out of budget or time or something else. The polish that was present at the start was not present at the end and that shows in how mechanics work really well at the start of the game and start to be dysfunctional at the end. Also minor nitpick, Lenne is clearly a red head in the game but in the cover art she clearly is blonde. I don't know why this is but its odd. Mathias Linda is clearly talented. He just needs someone to guide his focus and someone with a high attention to detail on his next title. As well as he needs someone to make a doable roadmap. This game is not the complete story. It ends in an odd way and at an odd time. This still is a very good game. One of the the best RPGs I've ever played. It just makes me sad thinking about how with just a tad more time and polish it could have been the best RPG I've ever played. Ideally this is a learning experience or a free update releases to fixe many of these issues. Mathias Linda is someone to watch out for when a game is released.

9/10
Will go down as one of my favorites but could have been my favorite of all time with some more polish.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcuse my writing. I don't write many reviews, but I really just loved this game. It was the best game I played last year. My GOTY. Everything was just perfect and left me wanting for more. There is just so much world building with the small stories and books you find that I wanted to see more of the world.
It felt like there is enough story for another two games.

Every fight on foot was challenging. The later mech fights less so if you had a certain setup, but you could still be wiped out if you made a mistake.

I am just a bit proud that it was mainly solo developed by a guy from Germany and the only thing I regret is that I can't experience it anew and play it again for the first time.
The weakest part is that it is not really replayable unless you choose an entirely different tactic for the whole game because the game is 90% pretty linear.

I only heard good things about Chained Echoes and hope that it gets even more attention so that Matthias Linda might consider a sequel, or just making another game. Even if it takes another seven years.
He really has talent and made one of the best RPGs I ever had the fortune of playing.\t\t\t\t\t
