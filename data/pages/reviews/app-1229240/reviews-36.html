\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a gem for the price. If you like turn-base RPGs this is a MUST play! It basically took a bit from the best turn-based RPGs and combined them into Chained Echoes! I normally can't get into pixel games but it's a beautiful game with lots of character.

70 hours on first play through. I spent a good 10 hours being afk (pause screen).

-The ability to create a team and rotate characters during combat is AWESOME, similar to FFX. It adds more variety than most RPGs "heal when you need" and "attach when healthy". Starting battle with full HP/TP is awesome. No need to heal/cure ailments outside of battle.
-Having a clan and recruiting people across the world that benefit your gameplay is great.
-After completing most of the Reward Board (acquiring the monument for my clan island), getting most ultimate weapons, Seraph & Dayajir sky armors, etc....I want more lol.

What I loved about the game:
-as a musician, the music is good, suits the environments and situations.
-this might be the best turn-based combat I've ever experienced. definitely top 3.
-reward board really encouraged me to sink my teeth into this game. it was fun, not a chore.
-the story got pretty good in Act II and by the time I was in Act III, I was totally into it.
-character/team combat diversity is great. had a blast deciding which combination of gear, skills, and characters to include in my party. it wasn't overwhelming. it felt just right. enough to give me freedom but not to where i needed online guides.

The few small cons of the game, but none of which discouraged me from playing the game.
Cons:
-writing could've been better towards the end, especially Kylian's character. word choice/usage could use some improvement. you can tell this was written by a smaller team.
-sky armor battles are a little underwhelming. could've been better.
-i felt like a few playable characters are pointless for combat.
-when it comes to combining crystals, the system is a bit tedious and i think it could've been explained better.\t\t\t\t\t

Well done, Mr. Linda...

After 42hrs, the fantastical journey full of cultures, people, beasts & monsters, politics and mechs has come to a very moving end. I have not experienced anything like this since in the 90s, and let me just say that my bar is quite high on (rpg-)games (I grew up on the snes and ps1 generation games). Terranigma, FFVI-IX, Secret of Evermore [...] just to name few of them... and Chained Echoes can easily keep up with these. It even comes up with some new fighting twerks, which offer a breath of fresh air in the otherwise familiar genre (e.g. overdrive bar or sky armor fights). The game and it's story is very detailed, has a great way of telling it's lore and history of the countries. Music is incredibly familiar yet new and of high quality.
Only the crystal system could use a revision (even if this upgrade system is not necessary and I have defeated every (optional) enemy even without perfect optimization), but the rng itself is a bit frustrating for people, who want to have "perfect" stat-characters.

Conclusion
The game is a solid 9/10 and a modern classic in my books. The gameplay is deep, and at the same time the quality of life improvements from the 90s hits are so well implemented that your time will go very fast. What a fantastic, and surprisingly good RPG!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was my first JRPG type of game. I liked the pixel art graphics, the colors, animation and music. The story was complex and original, with a vast world to explore. The mechanics were also deep, but not difficult to comprehend. I disliked very minor things: the crystal weapon/armor upgrade system (which is unnecessarily complicated) and the final boss battle whose defeat was very much based on luck and not skill. Still this was a great experience for me and I highly recommend it to anyone who likes RPG games. Also I'm looking forward to the sequel. I hope it will include my favorite character, Victor.\t\t\t\t\t
