\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game play was good for the most part. It wasn’t amazing but there was enough there to keep me playing for a bit, but eventually I quit because the writing is so terrible. It’s like watching modern day SNL or Velma, in that the bulk of the writing consists of ham-fisted social/political commentary fused with cringy and juvenile attempts at humor, and there is absolutely no subtlety in any of it. Furthermore, the creator seems to have taken every trendy talking point he could find on TikTok and tried to cram all of it into the game, so the plot becomes convoluted with tons of different ideas and themes that often don’t connect naturally, and it all feels clunky.

At first it was all whatever and I tried to focus on the gameplay, but after so much terrible dialogue it eventually ceased feeling like a fantasy game and more like an awkward stand-up comedy rant; it was like an Amy Schumer special. Oh and you can’t skip dialogue, because the creator wanted to make sure you listened to his sermonizing. Look, I don’t mind if people want to create a social justice warrior simulator, but please advertise it as such or give us some hint so that those of us who just want to simply play a game about a fantasy world, free of overt politics and social agendas, can avoid your game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere's me going into Chained Echoes:
I've played The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past, The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, Shadowrun Returns and just recently completed my 4th playthrough of Final Fantasy 7. I'm such a clever-clogs that this "Chained Echoes"-game will be a breeze!

I was very wrong. This game is gorgeous, creative and has a whirlwind storyline that you just want to unravel. It is overflowing with game mechanics. I gave up on Chrono Trigger because it lacked depth in mechanics and Chained Echoes is the opposite. It is however quite hard! Not unfairly hard, but you have to leverage all game mechanics and do a lot of side quests and resource gathering to progress.

I'd strongly recommend using online resources to level up because you find parts of quests in the first two hours that you can't complete for another... 20 hours maybe? You don't know that unfortunately so you spend an hour looking for the other entrance to the cave because bosses turn you into floor-polish if you haven't collected enough side quests and extra crystals along the way.

All in all, I think Chained Echoes is a game geared for people more accustomed to hard-as-nails JRPGs. Us "casuals" can still enjoy it even if it might not always be good for one's blood pressure.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredible game. So much emotion and personality come through the characters even though their faces never move. While the world and main story do feel linear at times, I was never able to predict what came next. Endless memorable moments, characters & enemies lead you through a rather dark narrative while you slog your way to reach a fading light of hope. Progress never felt like a grind & kept up with the story fairly well. If you keep your wits about you, taking the beaten path every now and again will certainly be rewarded.

If you're looking for a light-weight turn-based J-RPG style game that doesn't break the bank or trap your soul in Maelstrom, Chained Echoes truly exceeded my expectations. Have fun & be kind out there all :)\t\t\t\t\t
