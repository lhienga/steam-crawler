\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely write more than a sentence or two in a game review but I'll make an exception because I don't know where the high score comes from.

I wish I could write positive things about this game, but the longer I play, the longer I think about it, the less I like the game. At a glance it seems that it has a lot of things going on, but in reality there's not much thought in any of those systems.

Combat seems exciting at the beginning, till you realize every fight is uphill and you'll never feel stronger than the enemies you meet, you will never even feel like you figured something out since you have no say in any order of attacks or enemy focus. Not that you can get stronger. Not that it matters since you can't tell what the enemy is going to do, you just kind of hope for the best. Not that you want to fight anyone because of the loot (I will come back to it later) nor for the experience which becomes obsolete once you level up couple skills.

During combat, you can just get unlucky and enemies focus one guy and decide to kill him in one round, just because, and there's nothing you can do, you just restart since there is literally no downside to it. And since you have only one character that actually revives and does any significant healing, the ressurected character dies right after anyway because...enemies decide to kill him, or not, you can never tell.

You can level up skills, which is nice and seems exciting, but the numbers change by such an insignificant amount, it might as well be not there, it does nothing, the numbers are boring, the gameplay never changes whether you level up a skill or not. There are so many different attack types which feel pointles since most enemies don't even have a weakness that you can exploit. And by exploiting weaknes I mean that only ONE out of 8 characters deals slightly more damage while others kind of stand around pondering their life decisions.

The overdrive system seems like a cool concept but it never feels like it actually balances the scales of difficulty towards you, being in green feels like normal difficulty instead of favorable, while being in red makes the fight harder than it needs to be. Overdrive is not exciting because you have to pick your skills carefuly, It's just tedious because not only skills feel like slapping someone with a dead fish, now you have to use skills you don't even want to, which throws any synergy or combos you tried out of the window. Whoops, you're in the red you shouldn't use that skill now, not that you can, since you need to spam healing at every step. At some point you realize avoding the battle is the way to go, since the experience is actually worthless and you don't need any of the 100 different craftparts you might drop so it's a waste of time (not that you can even tell if the enemy will drop something you want).

Going back to afromentioned loot. Simply put. It's unexciting, boring and lacklulster. There are a lot of chests and pickups scattered around the map, you begin the adventure excited to look everywhere, pick up everywhing, till you realize you're picking up crap you can just buy from a vendor. Nothing you ever find is exciting in any way. Your backpack quickly fills with 1-3 copies of hundered of items that you don't know what to do with, it just kind of is there to sometimes unlock a deal in a shop or upgrade one piece of armor, sometimes. You can never tell either.

Equipment progression is flat which means numbers go slightly up, that's it. No cool effects, no extra synergy, "numbers bigger". And everytime you change a piece of gear (from Sword 1 to Sword 2, or Bow 1 to Bow 2) you have to redo all the crafting with differently named craftparts that you have no idea where to look for, redo all the gems that you have no idea where they drop only to repeat it all over again in couple hours when Sword 2 changes to Sword 3. You never feel like you're actually building towards anything, you just make numbers slightly (and I mean SLIGHTLY) higher and looking in your bags makes you scratch your head wondering what is all this crap.

And along the way there a lot of just weird and odd decisions that make no sense. At one point you have to unlock path forward, but after using a switch, path spawns offscreen so you don't know where it actually is, so you just run around through empty location (since you already picked everything up and killed all 4 enemies) guessing what changed. Some other time you wander around exploring and by chance you trigger a cutscene which blocks you from further exploration since the story moves forward locking you out from the location that you can't come back to.

If the story won't grip you, nothing else will.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is so ambitious. It doesn't pull off everything it wants to but there's so much good stuff here anyway. If you like the sorts of games this draws from it's an easy buy.

Narratively, a lot of what you should expect is laid out in the very first scenes. It begins with essentially the opening of chrono trigger. This is revealed to be a dream when the main character, a mercenary, gets slapped out of it by a workmate: the mission is about to start. In other words, you're not exactly wrong to be thinking this is trying to be chrono trigger, but expect some rude awakenings and genre awareness.

Really, the game blows past that standard. JRPGs are a pretty juvenile genre on the whole, and there's a lot of you against Abstract Divine Evils or whatever, you're still in a generic quasi-steampunk fantasy kingdom, but the characterization and plotting are nonetheless tight, consistent, interesting, impactful, and often moving. A lot of what you see is startlingly dark, but it's in purposeful rather than edgy ways, ones that convey the sense this is a world with consequences and where people have real problems. I cried several times and I'm still thinking about the ending months later. This story feels written by someone who knows how painful responsibility can be.

Sometimes the dialogue is a bit stilted. Part of this is due to the translation. Part of it, I realized halfway through the game, is because certain of the characters come from wealthy backgrounds. They're supposed to sound weird because this is a game where you're supposed to emphasize with the street rats and mostly have contempt for nobles and knights. The writing is more than good enough to overcome this.

(there is one really horrible line that'll stick with me a while tho, "I had to cover my heart's ears with my hands." i don't think that one was a translation issue)

The battle system is very cool and flexible. Other reviews describe it better than I would. The game takes too long getting you to full party membership, which is really important to the flow of the system, but once you're there it all works great. Not all other aspects of the mechanics are well done:

- There's an alternate battle system for mech ("sky armor") fights. It's undeniably cool, but once the novelty wears off you can see there's not much depth to it and the game spends SO much time having you fly around in mechs.

- There's sort of 1/18th of a job system. It doesn't encourage creativity like a fully featured job system, and each job can only go to one character at a time. That means if you miss picking some up (very easy to do) you may have party members stuck with no class features and in particular their base stats, which is frustrating.

- A lot of the party members (and there are so many) are introduced VERY late in the game. You have all these cool new people with exciting abilities running around and you want to learn more about them and have them in fights but the game is practically over by the time you get the chance.

- Character balance is questionable.

- Some of the mechanics are poorly explained. I still don't know what the difference between poison and toxic is. Basic information about what each stat does is scattered across a few NPCs.

- Awful crafting system, one of the worst I've ever seen. Just improving an enchantment on a weapon is a baffling process, Your materials degrade somehow when you use them to enchant but I could never tell how significant the penalty was, Between this and your items constantly being made obsolete trying to improve one just feels like a trap. I made a few good faith efforts and kept my equipment mostly at "++ quality," but otherwise ignored the crafting until just before the end of the game. I was glad I did and this was fine even with the difficulty settings maxed out so, I recommend you do the same.

It seems the game suffered from feature creep. The crafting, the mechs, the quasi job system, perhaps a few characters, they're just more than it can handle. What you've got is still so good that I'm waiting with excitement for the next game from this developer. I just hope they rein in the scope a little bit.\t\t\t\t\t
