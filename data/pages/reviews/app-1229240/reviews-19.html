However, there is one aspect of the game that makes me regret purchasing it. Halfway through the game, you are forced to endure the torment of learning a new form of combat, the sky armor combat. It is, without a doubt, the most annoying, tedious, repetitive, boring, and poorly thought out portion of this game. It is inexcusable that players are forced to endure the torture of sky armor combat with no option of converting the boss battles into "On Foot" format. There is zero reason that this portion of the game should be forced upon players, as it mostly relies on finding chests around the world for vital upgrades that players will have to backtrack for days to collect missing/vital pieces. I'm at the end of the game now, I stopped playing at the very last dungeon because the pain of the sky armor combat was more than I could endure. It is a pathetic excuse for part of the game, and I think it should be removed from the game altogether. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery reminiscent of old school SNES RPGs which are to this day my favorite era of gaming. It captures the look and feel of older titles while bringing something new to the table. The world/lore is a lot of fun to explore. The story, writing, characters and uncovering the mysteries are all great.

My only complaint is that some aspects of the game feel like they should unlock sooner than they do. One optional character in particular unlocks too late to be useful.

The core of combat is focused around a gauge called overdrive, which will increase or decrease based on the action you do. The goal is to keep it in the green and prevent it from reaching red (overheat). This system can be a bit rough at the start as you don't have many tools or characters manage the gauge so you'll find yourself using abilities with no purpose just to maintain overdrive. This will pass a few hours into the game, don't give up on it! It gets significantly better and is such a gem!\t\t\t\t\t
