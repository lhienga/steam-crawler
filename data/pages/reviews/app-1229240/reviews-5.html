\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn excellent modern take on the classic turn-based RPG formula. I can't recommend this game highly enough. The plot, while occasionally fast, is very fulfilling. The music is lovely. The art is colorful and expressive. The exploration is wonderful, and was carefully balanced around the mobility tools you get in Act 2. The combat, particularly starting at about midgame, is where this game shines. Each character brings something different to the table that accommodates a new strategy. Characters I had sidelined for some time suddenly became mainstays in my playstyle. For that matter the "level" system of this game makes units come onboard and almost immediately become viable for your party. The crystal system is a bit difficult to manage initially but once you have a handle on it, it really expands how you treat certain characters (I loaded Sienna with HP Drain and Counter Attack and so she went from a glass cannon to a largely independent crit machine).

Not to completely gloss over the issues of the game. Again, I think the story is occasionally trying to quickly to jump from one major event to another. It's not without twists, but some points don't always feel perfectly resolved. The Class Emblems (this game's take on a job system) are mostly optional, and are more useful for the stat buffs they give than the skills. Expanding on combat/skills, some things feel underexplained (like how Hate works, or what exactly Heroic Tank does). I want to emphasize that these are mostly minor issues that don't meaningfully detriment the player, but they are things you may find a little irksome on your way.

I had this game on my wishlist for the larger part of a year and I am delighted that I picked it up and gave it a firm go. Chained Echoes has been one of the most enjoyable RPG experiences I've had in years, and I'm sure I'll be enjoying it again in the future with the customizable NG+ settings. All in all, great game please play it.\t\t\t\t\t
