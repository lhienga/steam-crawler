LO BUENO:
- Me atrapó por la jugabilidad e historia. Se complementan muy bien, están muy bien planeadas para mantenernos enganchados, bien sea porque la historia nos está dejando intrigados o porque tenemos una nueva mecánica... fluye muy bien y eso no es común en juegos de este estilo.
- Las mejoras de vida, tanto en dificultad, atajos, en el seguimiento de la narrativa, seguimiento de las misiones... muy bien hecho. Tiene el sabor de un juego de rol clásico sin los tedios del género. Se nota que el desarrollador conoce el género y sabe de donde cojea.
- La música y los gráficos son muy agradables, la banda sonora en especial acompaña muy bien y tiene una mezcla ambiental, orquestal y de acción. El arte refleja una gran dedicación y amor, pero sin duda que le llegará más fácilmente a los fanáticos de los juegos clásicos. El pixel art se siente clásico y ya hay varios otros juegos que se ven mucho más bonitos usando el estilo, Sea of Stars por ejemplo que comparte género e inspiración, pero que no he jugado aún.

PUEDE NO GUSTARTE POR
- No está en español.
- La dificultad de base puede ser alta, hay que estar mirando las combinaciones, mantenerse en la sección Overdrive, los enemigos usan cadenas de buffs, debuffs, se curan. A mi eso me gusta … pero si quieres se puede ajustar.

Jugado en Steam Deck ajustando rendimiento para tener la mayor batería posible.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is a gorgeous game I wish I enjoyed more.

The art is gorgeous and I enjoy the story telling. Character movement through the world is fast & smooth.
There are a lot of menus & sub-menus for the characters, like attacks & skills. Some might like complex abilities, with each character having unique abilities to keep track of.

The reason I stopped playing even before getting to the "big feature" mech suits, and for my unfortunate thumbs down, is the combat gimmick.
Every turn-based Chrono Trigger wannabe / turn-based, SNES-era type JRPG seems to want to have its own gimmick in combat to set it apart from the crowd. In Chained Echoes, the gimmick is the overdrive bar.
As you attack, the arrow progresses to the right of the bar, and there's a "sweet spot" where you're in a more powerful, boosted state. While in that state, you attack for more damage and you take less damage, which feels good since extra boosts feel good. Unfortunately, as you keep attacking, that arrow moves farther right, outside of the "sweet spot" to the section where you don't deal extra damage anymore and will take increased damage than normal. This feels bad, punishing you for participating in combat. To get the bar back to the left & into that "sweet spot" (the goal of combat encounters), you have to perform your weaker attacks or do something other than attacking/healing, for example choosing instead of attacking in combat, instead to skip your turn.
Skip your turn.
...for several characters in a row.
That feels BAD.
...then you get wailed on by the enemies again on their turn, where damage taken might push you back out to the right on the bar where you're not boosted powerful & worse taking extra damage again, and need to skip more turns to get that arrow back to the left.
And it makes every combat encounter feel artificially inflated longer than it otherwise would... and this is all combat encounters, including what should be quick overworld randoms.

This situation and the intricacies & synergies of abilities with combat might appeal to some fans of the genre, providing extra "strategy" and complexities in combat; however, it ended up being a literal turn-off (turn the game off) for me.\t\t\t\t\t

I'm simply writing my perspective as a Christian Family for other Christian families that might be sensitive to these topics. I'll also say that if you'r that family pick up Tunic because it's a love letter to those who love the golden age of gaming and happen to be Christian.

This will ushering the Next Renaissance of Indie JRPG
Im gonna keep it short & simple,
This game remind me of:
  • The innovation of gameplay system from Chrono Trigger.

  • The story that have great character, heavy lore & mature theme development as Final Fantasy 6.

  • The sky armor combat that reminiscent mecha combat in Xenogear.

No level grinding, Every after battle HP/TP fully recovered, Reward board to give extra benefit for completing specific task, Team swapping system & more. This game totally respect player time for implement such modern feature.
Soundtrack score by Eddie Marianukroh is nothing short of beauty & mesmerizing, not to compare to the great Nobuo Uematsu but im believe Eddie's work here will inspire other gaming composer in the future.

Cons:
- Personally i think the story is a bit convoluted and feel overlong.
(im an older man so it kinda stress me out when the game is long, but for those avid JRPG player im sure they will love this)
- The crystal crafting system feel undercook.

Lastly I gonna give kudos to the masterful solo developer Matthias Linda who code, do all the character & background pixel design, gameplay system and story writing (according to the internet, if im miss out information, My apologies). Its crazy to think one developer can complete such amazing work in 7 years of time.

Personal Rant:
In this modern age of gaming,
Square enix seems changing direction leading FF16 unsatisfied most of their JRPG fans,
AAA studios put up lazy work like Starfield, Diablo 4, Call of Duty Modern Warfare 3 & etc,
Gaming trend that priorities monetization than provide good gaming experience to player,

This game had put up 100x of effort from a single developer & should be celebrate like this year Larian Studios Baldur's Gate 3!
I urge if you are a Turn Based JRPG fans, You owe it to yourself to play this game!
