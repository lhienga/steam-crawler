\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not one to write reviews but this game had to be an exception.
I bought this game on a whim after seeing a few videos in my recommended saying how good the game was and how under the radar it was flying, and boy were they right.

Chained Echoes is extraordinary from start to finish. If you even remotely like turn based rpgs PLEASE at least give this one a try. IT WAS ALSO DEVELOPED BY 1 GUY WHICH IS INSANE

Every piece of this game combines together to become a masterpiece.

The art is beautiful and detailed, despite being 16 bit everything feels so alive with detail and personality, Enemy design, attack effects, environments, all of it is beautiful.

The music throughout the game does a great job capturing the atmosphere and vibe of the events and surroundings

The story is complex and rich and I was left with shock and desire to learn more as layers and connections between characters and events were unveiled

The gameplay is satisfying and requires genuine strategy for every encounter, even basic enemies will put a hurt on you if you dont properly manager resources and elements. It evolves more as you get deeper into the game, unlocking more members for your party, learning new attacks, encountering new types of enemies

I dont really have any negatives to say about the game other than maybe some parts of the story are kinda hard to follow, but that could very much just be me.

PLEASE give this game your time and money, it deserves every hour and every penny.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay was great.

The story also started off great, but became so disappointing I had to stop playing. It left a horrible feeling in my mouth.

Woke or "current day politics" are hamfisted into this game in the worst ways.

Plantation style diversity is everywhere. Everyone is clearly dark skinned or while skinned with no between (I guess mixed race babies are taken to the butcher or something). This should have been a warning sign, but I foolishly ignored it. After 80 hours, I have no idea where light skin or dark skinned people come from geographically.

A character referred to as "red succubus" and who openly flirts with men (to steal stuff) is suddenly revealed to have a "wife" that she abandoned. First, this doesn't fit her characterization (someone who doesn't sleep with men would not have earned the name "succubus"). Second, the idea of everyone accepting two women marrying in a middle-age setting is extremely immersion breaking (it should have been "lover" instead of "wife"). Don't make your society backwards in certain ways (kings/queens instead of democracy) but extraordinarily enlightened in others.

Lizardmen are revealed to all be male and this is one of the worst narrative decisions I have seen in a long time. It means that lizardmen can never know love and are doomed to be forever alone. It's nightmare fuel. An entire race was turned into incels to support the talking point "men can get pregnant".

I hated what they did to Lenne's character. She started off wonderful, but then got preachy and self-aware (actually calling herself a "damsel in distress" was peak cringe).

Finally, there's the ending where the straight male protagonist chooses to kill himself in the most pathetic way, giving all his power to a traitorous villain. Everyone ends up alone except sienna and her lesbian wife. Peak current day.

It feels like there was a great epic story buried here that was twisted into something foul. Such a waste.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile not perfect, this is most definitely one of the best old-school RPGs I've played in a long time alongside Crystal Project.
I have to admit, I found aspects of base building a bit lacking since I was under the influence of hype which made me expect Suikoden levels of detail.
I also did not care much about the implementation of Sky Armor battles. To me, they felt mostly like a sub-quality addition that you have to use since some enemies outright require to be fought with giant piloted robots...

HOWEVER!
This game has some outright suberb story with a lot of mature themes, unexpected (but not deus ex machina levels) outcomes and a main plot that actually let me want to know more. The humor in the writing is sprinkled sparsely throughout the game so not to seem like a main focus, but for me it usually hits right home. Then again, your mileage may vary with something like humor so take that with a grain of salt.
The wording of some text might seem a bit clumsy at times and I've read the same in other reviews, but for me it was never much of a bother since I think it's at least competent even when it's at its weakest.

The world building and the lore of the world, history, myths, politics and events alike seemed to me like well thought-out, interesting on their own and congruent with the characters.

The music for me is old Squaresoft levels of good. Like seriously Chrono Trigger or Nobuo Uematsu levels of good. Not exactly in the same style, but certainly in quality. I tend to say that if you can actually remember a tune long after you stopped playing, it's good music.

Gameplay-wise, apart from the things I did not enjoy fully and already mentioned, for me the game is very high up in quality as well.

The combat system is fresh and fits very well into the rest of the game. Tactically, the game gives the player a multitude of characters with a multitude of possible set skills to play around with. Fights that are at the point of the game where you should be tend to be mildly challenging but hardly ever boring with some really creative boss fights thrown in to the mix. Especially some optional bosses are quite a doozy.
Although refights in areas you returned to because of exploration or side quests (which are also quite well written and interesting) can have a bit of tedium to them.

To sum it up, to me this was one of the rarer cases of outstanding small team RPGs and I can fully recommend it. Especially for connoisseurs of ye olde SNES era RPGs since this game obviously wants to be compared to them and usually gets full marks for it from me.
I mean seriously, the game starts by slapping you in the face with a Chrono Trigger reference just before going in a wildly different direction. And that's not the only reference by far.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe idea of not having an anti-grind is interesting for someone like me who is in their thirties and doesn't have the time to invest in mindless repetitive actions, The implementation here in Chained Echoes has the unfortunate side effect of taking away any sense of accomplishment or progression since every battle is challenging, but leaves little meta between the large character party to make for any kind of fun gameplay.

The world and story progression is very on-the-rails and I have yet to experience the ability to go and do what i want to do.

So then I am left with is story and characters. The story feels like something I have played before, I really wish JRPGs would stop focusing on some power crystal and do something different.

I'm not invested or interested in any of the characters.

The artwork reminds me of RGP Maker Circa 2005. Its not bad artwork its just borning.

I bought this game because it was the only JRPG I can run on my Mac.
I wasn't expecting anything amazing, it just feels like I'm playing a interactive story and the story is not that all great.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're on the fence, definitely pick this up. It's a brilliant old school JRPG, honestly on par with the likes of Chrono Trigger. I started playing the game, and felt it was maybe a touch too easy (I mean honestly most JRPG's typically are, but I saw the potential of how tactical the battles were), but then I started a new game and went into the settings and turned up the difficulty. The game is now amazing and each battle requires thought and planning.

This is definitely the most interesting take on turn based battles I've ever seen. You've got this gauge that builds up during battle that you want to keep in the green. Most actions build this bar up, but certain types of moves will reduce it to prevent overheating, and the reduction move type changes each round. So you're constantly trying to mind the bar while also dealing with the best way to rout the enemies and also executing FFXIII-esque party member swaps to deal with the dynamic combat.

There's a ton of content, huge incentives to explore, a non-trite story, an S-tier soundtrack, great sprite art, and again and perhaps most importantly, the combat is exquisite and deep and interesting, especially if you turn the difficulty up.

This should be a must purchase for any JRPG fan.\t\t\t\t\t
