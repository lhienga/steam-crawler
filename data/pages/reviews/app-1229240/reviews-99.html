\r\n\t\t\t\t\t** Very minor spoilers on some game systems, nothing story related below **

For anyone craving a JRPG, this game is just amazing, it has a nice world to explore, good world building, a pretty good story and good music. The combat system is very familiar for a JRPG player yet provides some new interesting things and options within a fight. (Overdrive & Character Switching).

At first I wasn't so sure about the Grimoire Shard system (the tokens to unlock skills / level up) but the more I played the more it made sense. Locking those behind boss battles is actually a great thing because it eliminates the need to grind non-stop on trash monsters to keep up and also limits your power up to extent when facing future bosses making them all pretty balanced and challenging enough.

Overall I'm very impressed with this game and came into it with 0 expectations and came out of it with one of my favourite JRPGs I've played in a very long time.

10/10 would recommend to any JRPGs fan, especially at this price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an absolute gem of a game. This game is a treasure. JRPGs are more often a miss than a hit for me, but this game is incredible. It's got all the heart and soul of the classic SNES games like Final Fantasy, Chrono Trigger, or Secret of Mana, but with all the game design improvements that we've made since those games came out. What you end up with is a wildly entertaining game full of heart, challenge, charm, and style. This game is an must buy.

Cons:
A few minor localization issues
Upgrade menus are tedious
Tutorials are only viewable once

Pros:
Very engaging story
Huge cast of unique and loveable characters
Top tier world build. Seriously, the game has such an interesting world I'm dying to see an expansion or something added to it so I can keep exploring
Combat is unique, challenging, and engaging throughout the 50+ hours
Incredible soundtrack

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like just about any JRPG this one will be good, and mind you this takes steps in a direction I like very much like:
- Not having the usual modes of progression.
- Having some unique mechanics in the overclock system that bring some depth to combat (and even is different in one boss)
- Map design is good.

But what could have been a great experience is held back by a few key issues:
-Most fights are really samey, at the start of the game it was acceptable and even had more variety (ghosts became immune to the first damage type they suffered, boxflies dodged everything unless cleansed) but this didnt really translate into fights further down, some enemies healed each other of did buffs but never anything that made you change your approach or your phormula for max dps. Not a single combat with an attack that forces a switch out in my 38 hours.
-Passive skills are uninspired. +10% attack? 10% chance to apply dry? Xslayer how many times? Where is my "attacks apply dry but deal 75% less damage" or "deal 50% more damage but become staggered after every damage skill" "critical hits explode dealing aoe but deal x% less damage)
-Mech combat is uninspired and feels half baked, too much like regular combat and not different enough.
- Not enough clarity of effects of buffs, debuffs and aggro in combat.

Additional nitpicks:
- Crystal auto combine is a noob trap and makes using them more of a hassle
- Traverse animations have to complete for the whole team before you can move again
- Equipment chests seem skewed to just a few chars.
-having equipment be plain stat boost seems like an odd choice since its another power up path tied to story progression
- Too many instances of areas artifically closed with a "I cant go that way" dialogue.

The story itself is better than the usual in games, if very contrived at times and the quality of assets and music is undeniable, the foundations of something fantastic are here. But this is not it, I hope the team's next project is bloder and more creative.\t\t\t\t\t
