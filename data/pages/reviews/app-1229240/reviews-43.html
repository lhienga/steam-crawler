\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith obvious homage references to Chronotrigger, FF7, and Final Fantasy Tactics, you can tell this title was made with care by people who love RPGs. Great pixel art and music score help establish your immersion into this world in a game that respects your time,

They clearly took lessons from ChronoCross in that almost all battles are seen and can sometimes just be avoided. "Levels" come from killing bosses in the form of unlocking stat boosts and skills, making "grinding for levels" the outmoded past time/time-sink that it is.

Dungeons don't overstay their welcome, nor do you need to "stock up" to get through them. Traversing the world comes in flavors of both "air ship" and warp crystals that you can access from your Map at any time.

Overall a lot of quality of life features were made to allow you to actually play the game and enjoy your time, instead of feeling like your grinding you way through an adventure.

The characters and story are enjoyable for the most part. I feel like there could be stronger writing and some of the party members are not-that-interesting, but there are characters who are interesting enough to carry the story, and enough intrigue with the over-all story itself.

My only gripe would be with upgrading weapons and armor. I really wish they had put in a "transfer" system to easily move your materia/crystals from an old piece of equipment to a new one. Currently you have to remove them all, then go an find each individual one you removed and put it back in, one at a time. At the end of the game your list (while organized alphabetically and by type) grows big and it still feels a bit like an egg hunt find the one you just removed. I've literally spent close to an hour upgrading various pieces of gear and moving crystals around for multiple characters, that I almost dread having to do it, and with (currently) 12 playable characters, there's going to be a few that get 'sat' solely so I can minimize this upgrade process as much as possible.

Overall if you're looking for an RPG that will keep you engaged, I highly recommend this title.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a bit hard not to recommend this game, because there's obviously been a lot of work put into it.
But sadly, I still think it's very uneven.

tl:dr my takes if you want to play this game for the JRPG nostalgia trip, without growing tired of it:
- Set the combat speed to the max.
- Save yourself some time and ignore the equipment upgrades until the end.
- Focus on the handful of actually useful skills. Try not to miss the class emblems.
- The best skill grinding spot is in one of the earliest levels, if you need it.

I'll start off with what I like the most in this game: quality of life features. Saving, fast-traveling, looking for chests or specific loot… there are a lot little details that alleviate some of the more tedious aspects of this kind of game.
But there are still a few weird inconsistencies. For example, I understand that story-wise, it might be a little weird to allow escaping from boss battles. But then why not allow opening the menu to load a save? Unless I've missed something, it seems all you can do to exit a boss fight is die or force-quit the game.

Some of the story is nice, but it gets very corny from time to time, especially later in the game.
And… the ending is… not the worst, maybe? At least it provides some actual closure, unlike many other games.

Some of the music is great, but I found some tracks to be too intense for the context, and it gets boring after a few hours anyway, so I ended up disabling it altogether at some point.

The graphics are nice if you're into 16bits, but they feel a bit… flat.
They somewhat lack visual effects like fog, rain, lighting, that kind of stuff.
Effects that you could already find in SNES games like Chrono Trigger or Star Ocean, and that could have greatly contributed to the overall mood.

The combat, loot and equipment system is meh. There are a couple skill combinations that work extremely well, maybe too well, while most of the others are useless.
Buying or upgrading your equipment is pointless until the end of the game, since you very quickly find new weapons and armor, and have to repeat the whole process every time.
The “overdrive” thing is interesting at first, but then it just gets annoying. Obviously, it's there to force you to diversify your gameplay, somewhat like breakable weapons in some games.
But just like breakable weapons, I think it's a cheap trick. No one would miss it if it wasn't there. In this case, all it does is gloss over the fact that battles are extremely repetitive. Enemies have limited skills and barely any AI. They almost never react to what you do but mostly just spam the same stuff in a loop. And as the game progresses, it gets more tedious than it gets difficult. A lot of late game enemies have permanent “act twice” or “act thrice” buffs for event greater spam.
Also, the alarm that plays every time you enter the “overheat” zone is so annoying that I sometimes just gave up and trying to lower the bar.

The addition of a second battle mode mid game, i.e. sky armors, brings nothing of value. It just feels like a worse, trimmed-down version of the same thing.

The description says "No random encounters; enemies can be seen running around". That's nice. On the other hand, a lot of enemies are static and can't be avoided anyway.

You can have up to 12 characters in your party, but as in Chrono Cross or FF6, I'm not sure if that's a good thing. You never really get attached to all of them, you can only play with 8 at the same time anyway, and you'll probably won't use the rest.

In short, this game, like a lot of games, could have been significantly better with less content and more polishing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really, really struggle to recommend this game. It isn't terrible, but it does so much wrong and weird that it's hard to immerse myself in the game.

The story is cliched. In and of itself, that is neither a good nor bad thing, but the way the cliche is handled is pretty hamhanded. A character will announce that she doesn't want you hurting her countrymen and then 5 minutes later say "Well, it's okay now" because there would be no way for you to proceed otherwise. Another character monologues about wanting the MacGuffin to fulfill his dream of influencing the world to be a better place and then everyone else acts surprised when he betrays you an hour after. I was surprised, too, not because of the betrayal, but because he betrays you over BEING BROKE. Way to stick to your convictions there, guy. And I already know about the ending, despite not getting there myself. I'm more than a little disappointed with how it's handled.

The characters, as noted, can be very inconsistent in their portrayals. They're also generally unlikable. One in particular you get saddled with early on has a tendency to make me want to punch him every time he talks. And isn't even that good in the grand scheme of the party. Speaking of, party balance is kind of wonky. The thief you get as one of your starting cast is probably one of the best fighters you could hope to use, while the archer's only good combo is being able to apply poison to the entire enemy team after a couple rounds getting the crap kicked out of him.

Combat is obnoxiously grindy and repetitive. It starts out interesting enough until you discover that every single fight is basically a miniboss, until you find equipment too strong for the monsters in that area. Which brings me to another gripe, your combat ability is basically determined almost exclusively by your equipment. You earn some stat boosts by unlocking skills, but nowhere near enough to actually impact any area beyond the first two. And good luck ever upgrading equipment. You need to grind out ingredients from monsters and enough money to do so. By the time you've done that, you've probably already obtained the next rank up of equipment.

One thing the game teases you with is the presence of mechas you can pilot in combat. You do it exactly twice at the very beginning of the game in a couple of baby training fights and then don't get the ability to do so again for another 12+ hours. Probably longer depending on how lucky you are with monster drops for certain things. A vial of saliva might be "common" in the game's calculations but that didn't make it drop any sooner than my 58th fight against the enemy that drops it. Considering only 2 of them spawn in the entire area they're found in every time you load the map, it took me a while to get just ONE. Of a supposedly common item.

Overall, it isn't a bad game. The art and music are beautiful, but it's hampered by the writing, balance, and wonky systems.\t\t\t\t\t
