\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is all of my favorite classic JRPG elements rolled into one. Fun leveling system that feels rewarding and requires a bit of decision-making, but also avoids the need to stop and grind. The overworld is fun to explore with plenty of secrets to discover and unlock, but not overwhelming or filled with respawning junk. Plenty of playable characters and unlockable, interchangeable classes, but the characters still feel unique and have their own abilities, stories, and roles. Side quests, but not too many.

If you enjoy classic JRPGs, then definitely give this one a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly just a brilliant gem of an RPG and I was not expecting it!
The RPG mechanics were honestly the biggest draw for me, it was a combination of a lot of older systems with some modern freshening up. Some of them were really enjoyable, like the combat?
Loved the push and pull that made combat constantly engaging and made you thing about how you'd perform your next action.
There were definitely some mechanics that fell a bit flat, the Reward Board felt clunky, it was nice to use it as a box to tick things off but it didn't really feel like it added a great deal for me, it was just a mad dash to grab some very, very end game materials that were all a bit useless by the time I got there. Not the biggest fan of the crystal/gem system either.

Nevertheless I still had a great experience, combining the exploration, combat and music it was absolutely delightful.

One of my weirdest complaints would be the main cast themselves and, by extension, the dialogue.
I find it weird to say that I really liked the main cast, but for the most part I really didn't like their dialogue. This is seen throughout the game actually, numerous times I found the dialogue stiff, unnecessarily cruel, completely whiplash or just not at all engaging. It was the designs and stories of each character that really kept me enjoying them, but the things they often said? It was hard to like any of it except for comedy characters like Tomke.

Still, I'd recommend this for anyone that misses classic JRPGs and wants an interesting spin to some of the classic systems.
The story is pretty decent, it's your run of the mill RPG story but it has a few twists here and there to keep you engaged, others...such as Killian, not so much. That guy was really poorly written. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tdefiantly JRPG inspired, some chronotrigger elements, a good story, a neat item/upgrade system - welcome back to socketing.

This takes me to my first criticism, the socketing, upgrading (combining), and equiping screens are all different.. you have to take ALL the skill gems off, then find the ones you were using in the VAST array of gems you have collected (some accidentally that you will never use) then combine them, and then re-equip them to the item.

Its just really easy to forget what your character had that was working well. if you go item by item (thats armor and weapon) across all 12 party members... you can loose an hour just upgrading your skill gems in your current setup).

a non-exhaustive list of skill gems to look for:

-HP Drain
-Counter Heal
-MAG/ATT/HP/DEF/MIND/AGI Up
-all Weapon and Armor gems
-Evasion
-Counter Attack
-Blind Hit

combat is traditional JRPG style - menu-driven turn-based. (AGILITY is how fast/often you act in combat fyi.)
there are a bunch of combinations with the different characters, and by the end there are a few big bad mini-bosses that will absolutely test you.

the story, some elements are honestly down right DARK. it does a pretty good job of sucking you in, as things go from bad to worse for the world and some of your compatriots.

Overall, a good game that wont break the bank.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd heard good things about it, and still loved it way more than expected.

Loved

- This is a MAXIMALIST jrpg. I can't believe how much stuff is in this game. It's overwhelming, in a good way.
- Exploration. You level up by doing stuff, including optional stuff, rather than grinding. It gave me CrossCode vibes, except way more chill. (Aren't they German too? Maybe Germans love exploring.)
- Aesthetics. The character design, the environments, the animations, the music and sound... How the heck did one person do this? (Well, I know the music was a separate contributor, but still.)
- The cast. I didn't care much for their overall plights, but the part they each played in the party -- both mechanially, and the relationships they formed with each other -- was great. I loved all of them, even the latecomers.
- The battle system. It wasn't hard, there's not much to it, but I found it super fun. THANK YOU for letting me switch characters in and out. It's so refreshing!

Liked

- Crafting. This was OK, but if you like staying up to date, there's a bit too much of it. It feels very satisfying to kit out your newest gear, but the pace of getting new gear is a little too fast. I think it would have been fine to cut number of weapons and armor in half.
- Sky armors. Oh man these were fun. BIG NUMBERS, and being able to revisit environments and re-explore them with armors was great. However, it really felt like they had wayyyy too many upgrades. I think they could have done with 2-3 equipment tiers, rather than... 6-7, I think there. Way too many chaff skill rams, as well. They maxed out their entire skill trees so fast as well. If this could be rebalanced, I like to see way less _stuff_ and maybe give us another two Seraph Armors to compensate.
- Classes. I think they were implemented cleverly, but in the end it was a lot of overhead just to learn a bunch of skills that I had to cram into two slots. It also hurt that those slots could "compete" with character skills.

idk

- Crystals. These were kinda fun, but again, way too many, and managing them is not great. My favorite part was just gliding through maps to collect more when farming them, this was surprisingly relaxing and gratifying.
- Tanks. There are two, and it feels like they have a huge mess of abilities for the simple job of "block incoming damage." I'm not sure why this was made so complicated, but it made me ditch one of my favorite personalites Egyl.. I used Raphael religiously (pun intended) once I got them, because their skillset was more straightforward, and they also had great crowd debuffs.
- Skills. This could really use a trim, I think. There are tons of them, they show up repeatedly in many different places (character skill trees, class skill trees, accessories, crystals) and it's hard at any given time to know what your characters even have active. It's also somewhat of a downer to have gobs of skills to choose from, but only 8 skill slots to use in battle -- and at most two can be class skills.
- Storytelling. I like the world, I like the personalities of the characters (both PCs and NPCs.) The actual writing, though, was a bit wobbly. It felt like the script could have used a copywriter pass just to clean up the syntax and grammar in places. This was especially noticeable at high-tension moments like the endgame, where run-on sentences stole from the drama a bit. I didn't mind much, though. This game was much more about atmosphere than plot for me.

Incredible game. If it takes another 10 years for a sequel, I'd happily wait. But honestly, I think subsequent games should be shorter. I couldn't help myself from quasi-100%ing the game (I'd say I 98%ed it) and it took 80 hours. Cut this in half and you'd still have a terrific experience. I'd rather get a much shorter game in two years, rather than one this long again in five. I imagine this is a minority opinion, though.

I'm going to miss the characters and their vibrant world. Hopefully see them again soon. I'm all for June being the main character of the sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

This applies too on seen chest, but still unreachable either by mechanics or simply haven't figured out how to get there yet, but players wanna skip. List goes on.
