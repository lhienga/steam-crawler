\r\n\t\t\t\t\t$40 game with a $25 price tag (as of this review). 8/10 Great game.

I found the story interesting, and the characters developed well for the most part. I will say though, i do not understand the last 10 minutes of the story at all. You obtain A LOT of characters for your group which can get a bit overwhelming, especially towards the end, but also really adds to your strategy pool. Excellently paced progression with new mechanics, characters, locations, and quests being unlocked really keeps the game fresh and engaging. Music was solid. Map/ location/ character art is classic JRPG.

This combat system was unique to me, and I liked it a lot. The battles will be difficult if you're not paying attention. Especially the bosses towards the end of the game. You'll have to adjust your strategy and rethink what characters and skills/equipment to use and really keeps the game interesting. A good variety of enemies and every boss/mini boss is unique.

Another point for pacing is the reward board system which encourages you to explore every nook and cranny of every map. And as a result, i did not find myself grinding for money or exp, and kept the game moving forward smoothly. There are reasons to explore as well, with a bunch of secrets sprinkled all over the place. With my 50hrs of game play, i'm still missing quite a few things.

Three negatives things about the game, more for the dev than the buyer. First, the Sky armor battles got a bit annoying and towards the end and could benefit from more customization. Awesome mechanic though.

Second, i stopped upgrading my weapons and armor about halfway through the game because by the time i fully upgraded a piece of gear, i would find the next level of that gear. It didn't feel worth to upgrade because the piece of gear would be replaced pretty quickly.

Third, and this is coupled with second point, the crystal system is REALLY bad and is easily the worst part of the game. It honestly felt it was slapped on last minute. The UI to manage the crystals at the anvil was infuriating, so much so that i had maybe two crystals equipped the entire game. It was a mechanic i did not want to engage with. A UI overhaul would fix this problem because it IS a cool and fun idea that adds to the game. So finding better weapons and armor at the frequency i was, and the crystal system being terrible to interact with resulted in me never upgrading my gear past mid game. And yeah, I probably made the game more difficult for myself. But i really enjoyed it.

Great game, great ideas, i recommend!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI platinumed this game and reviewed after the fact.

Graphics 10/10
They go very old school with superfluid animations. Only complaint is that the special moves could use a longer animation of some of them.

Music 10/10
The soundtrack is beautifully composed and catchy. This typically is my biggest draw to jrpgs as most can be bland with great music. This is not one.

Story 9/10
As stated below the story can be predicted about 2/3 roughly through. It has some surprises in store up to the very end though.

Mechanics 8/10
The gem system could really use an overhaul. It's clunky and difficult to manage large quantities of them once you reach end game. Luckily that isn't even required to complete. Min max doesn't really need to be done. The supermoves could use some touch up in a sense of be able to maybe up grade or even change them. Give it more reason to use them.

Overall 9.5/10
This game is the hidden gem of 2022. This game has a great story with a very stasfying ending. It can be guessed running about 1/2-3/4 of completion but there are more twists that keep you intrigued.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't do this, I NEVER do this, but I HAVE to do this. I have had a steam account for over 5 years and have yet to write a single review good or bad for anything I've played, but Chained Echoes is SO GOOD that it feels like my ancestral birthright to write one today. First off I'd like to say I'm also a solo indie game developer working on my own RPG of six years now. Back on year two or three I came across the trailer for chained echoes (mostly cinematic) and it looked so amazing back then I thought it was just an awesome pixelated game concept but it inspired me so much I saved the trailer to watch from time to time, I didn't even know the NAME of the game at that point. However, A year later I saw the trailer online again this time with the name attached and I saved that as well to keep in the back of my mind. Then early last year I found their twitter but it was largely quiet....then BOOM the last 8 months their twitter and online activity went crazy, the game released and I grabbed it day one.....and when I began playing it I was simply blown away. Its attention to detail, sprite work, mapping, writing, humor, exposition, battle system, easter eggs, etc. is a master class in Turn Based RPGs.

When I learned the creator was a solo dev who put this together in 7 years it made me take a week off of work and completely look over my current project to see where I could improve, to great success. I say all this to say the game excites and entices me as a player/consumer but also inspires and intimidates me as a developer. So I write this in hopes others see it from any walk of life to say if you enjoy an RPG in any way shape or form, you will enjoy Chained Echoes. Also, if Matthias ever happens to read this I have 7 words for you as the friend in my head: "Your work changed my life, Thank You".\t\t\t\t\t
