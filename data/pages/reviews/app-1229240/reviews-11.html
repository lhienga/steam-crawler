\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would've recommended this game to people if the final quarter of the game didn't mire the experience so badly.

There is lots of fun to be found in this game. The progression system and the combat is genuinely engaging and the exploration brings a sense of nostalgia that I haven't felt since I was in high school. Combat is challenging in the beginning but starts to get a bit too easy towards the end of the game (Which isn't a bad thing). The sidequests and optional objectives you can do are a very fun distraction. There are too many playable characters than I can realistically manage; but that's not really a major issue.

Sadly, the story is what really brings me to say I can't recommend this game to people. It's not an unbearably bad story mind you; it starts off really great actually. But it definitely suffers from a writer who's trying to add too many central story elements into the plot instead of focusing on one grand overarching narrative. It's not too bad throughout the first half of the game; but by the final act I felt like the writer just spat in my face with where the story was heading. That's all I can really say about it without giving anything away.

TL;DR: a solid game that is unfortunately mired by an atrocious final act. There is lots of fun to be had I'll say this. But at the end of the game, I felt like I wasted my time. If you do end up wanting to get it, I would wait for at least 50% on discount.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very solid RPG inspired by some classic JRPGs that wears its heart on its sleeve. This is a very honest and passion-filled game that we need to see more and more of, even if it has its problems.

The real heroes in this game are the absolutely incredible artwork and lovely soundtrack. This game looks and sounds amazing, and that in itself could be half the whole reason to buy this. The story is okay, its nothing groundbreaking or special but its interesting enough to keep you at attention and wanting to play more.

The gameplay, however, is where you will find a big hit or a big miss. The combat system works on a bar-balancing mechanic that ticks the bar up or down based on the type of skill or move you use and if you take damage. There are several nuances to this based on if you're playing on foot or in your Sky Armour (mechs!), but the crux of it is: stay in the sweet spot. Seems simple, but there is a lot that goes into this, and unfortunately there is also a lot of RNG that can go into this. You have tools to manipulate this RNG, but sometimes you can roll a random combination of bar requirements that deadlocks your fight completely. This isn't exactly common, but its not rare either and every time it happens it feels very frustrating. The game is very forgiving though, allowing you to guarantee run away from any non-boss encounter, retry any fight as many times as you like, and being very lenient on upgrades and gear power. Perhaps this is to make up for some of the RNG frustrations.

All in all, it is a very enjoyable experience and a great way to experience inspired JRPG gameplay with a bit more modern QOL.\t\t\t\t\t

I have been told several times that this is a front runner for best RPG of 2023, as it turns the JRPG genre on its head and fixes all the things that have been wrong with the genre since day one.

Apparently what they think has been wrong with the JRPG genre is that there was an RPG in it.

Instead, they prefer this rail battler, where you sit through a scripted cutscene and then walk down The Only Path fighting everything on it until you get to the end, where there will be another scripted cutscene.

It's very well made. The art, the music, the sound design, the writing, they worked very hard and did very well. The problem is that they were making absolute garbage, which I guess is what they wanted to make in the first place, so... good job? I guess? Yay?

But I don't really see myself playing this anymore. I have a dozen cheap RPGMaker asset flips that are better games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never bothered to leave a review in all my years of gaming but I had to for this one, because as a JRPG fan there just hasn't been anything innovative and exciting in over a decade. (More like OctoPASS Traveller amirite??) And I really wanted to provide an additional voice to affirm for original JRPG fans that have felt let down and bored after years of disappointments and schlock, that this game is the real deal and I truly haven't enjoyed a JRPG like this since SNES days.

We're not getting to Sakaguchi level of narrative, but we get damn close. The characters are well written with their own narrative arcs that are actually engaging. The story itself, while a bit hard to keep track of at times with all its politics, is otherwise perfect.

The graphic design is beautiful and easily the best I've seen in a game of this type. Equally paying tribute to games of yore while capitalising on new tech.

And the "overdrive" combat system... well some may hate it, and I understand why, but I think it brings a new element to JRPG battles that is actually challenging, forcing you to strategize your moves, whereas other classic games in the genre were pretty easy once you grinded or got the right loot. And at the same time it doesn't feel so foreign that it's another genre entirely. It's a inventive twist and fun to experience.

If you've been holding out because you think you're getting another late-stage Square-Enix game or a really amateur feeling indie game, this is the game you've been waiting for.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has an amazing and interesting story, the lore is quite wide and keeps you interested and wanting more of the world. Would go more in depth with it but i will refrain from doing so and don't spoil the great experience the game brings.

Combat system is rather unique and fun, the Overdrive gauge keeps you at bay and makes you rethink your options at times. Battles can feel a bit dragged out in the mid game though and the pacing gets pretty slowed down due to sponge difficulty before you start to get real strong in endgame. I am aware its a design choice but not everyone wants to spend minutes per fight just to clear trash along the way in new dungeons. In my personal experience i skipped mob groups in certain dungeons just to keep getting more story since the benefit of actual more battles is rather small since you level up generally by doing story progressions/sidequests.

The big flaw in this game is the itemization and the progress of gear. Whenever you find a piece of weapon/armor in a dungeon/area technically you will get an upgrade, but not just yet. If you spend your time and properly progress your gear and upgrade its stats and crystal slots the newly acquired gear is pretty much useless until you find an anvil to upgrade it with and put in your favoured crystals. This is IF you actually have the new materials to upgrade it otherwise you are better off sticking with your currently equipped gear. It's a lil bit of a kick in the balls at times cause you wont have the materials needed always or even have a clue what drops them without a guide and it lead me to skipping some tier of gear upgrades completely.

Do not get me wrong though, this game is really amazing for what it is and with a improved itemization and less screen tearing when the screen scrolls it would be a 10/10 game. Still damn close and its worth all the hours you put into it if you can handle it being a little bit rough around the edges at times.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is the best JRPG I have played in years, and it isn't even a JRPG. Regardless of its labeling, It has all the charm of a Final Fantasy game while improving on the stale turn based formula that Japanese developers have either abandoned or under-utilized for years. The story is pretty engaging if a little too grandiose, and the characters are well-written and fleshed out. There are side quests or main story events that provide more insights into every party character, adding true depth to these lovable, witty adventurers.
If you enjoy Chrono Trigger, Final Fantasy, Dragon Quest, Earthbound, or any other pixelated JRPG, you will probably enjoy this game.\t\t\t\t\t
