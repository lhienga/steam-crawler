\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI spent two weeks playing this, and loved every moment of it. The fact that it takes inspiration from several well-loved RPGs shows in a way that makes Chained Echoes stand out as something separate and unique rather than derivative. This is one of the most well-polished retro style RPGs I have ever played. Due to the difficulty settings, anyone can enjoy Chained Echoes - whether you want to play casually or whether you're craving a hardcore challenge.

It has...

Great writing:
A lovable cast of characters and a compelling story are at the heart of Chained Echoes. It's a combination of high fantasy, science-fiction, and political intrigue, with 8 main characters and 4 optional ones, each of whom has their place. I can definitely say I wasn't expecting the twists and turns that this game threw at me, but I WAS absolutely hooked from the very beginning. The characters' personalities are endearing and unique amongst each other, and their growth throughout the story really helped me connect to the writing as a whole. (Hell, even the NPCs have great dialogue!)

An engaging battle system:
Battles have an overdrive/overheat meter, which changes based on whether you're fighting on foot or in your sky armours, and sometimes in other circumstances. In overdrive mode you do more and take less damage. In overheat mode you do less and take more damage. The meter raises and lowers based on skills that you use, and manipulating the meter is often as much if not more focus than trying to deal the highest damage to your enemy. I've never seen anything like it, and I loved that it brings an element of tactical gameplay to every fight while not being complex to learn or master.

Maps that are exciting to explore:
There is always loot to be found (often with funny commentary to check in the inventory, for those seeking humour), and always secrets to discover. Towns feel lived in and functional, forests and other wild areas have wildlife (aside from monsters), some of which react to the player's movements while others don't. The breathtakingly beautiful and detailed pixel artwork makes every area feel truly alive, and the musical soundtrack is an enjoyable complement to each area. A lot of thought went into the map design; the attention to detail is astounding.

As well as:
-An in-game reward board/achievement grid
-A home base to develop
-Many sidequests
-New Game+

AND you can pet the dogs and the cats.

Play Chained Echoes. Seriously.\t\t\t\t\t

It's definitely not perfect, for one the dialogue doesn't flow too well, understandable as the developer's mother tongue isn't English. Another negative is the fact that there are some game mechanics and characters in your party that are definitely useless.
Overall this is definitely an easy RPG to recommend despite some flaws, there are plenty enough wonderful components that the game adopted from classic predecessors that its flaws are not too hard to swallow.

Tl;DR: Call this game "Xenogears (Demastered)" or the videogame equivalent of the book "Ready Player One".
This "solo" Kickstarter project is so heckin impressive from every standpoint. While this game does have some low-hanging fruit to poke (e.g. Story / Sprites), everything else is amazing. This developer has a good critical understanding of RPGs and this game is an homage to the entire breadth of the last 3 decades of them.

It is difficult to put this game down because it's just that dang good. There are many different systems to manage in order to strengthen your party. Everything has good tutorials. This is the best turn-based game I've played in awhile. The game is tuned well and they just added a NewGame+.

I love that you can run from any trash battle any time with no consequence (just like in FF13). On that note, wiping in general is pretty low-consequence so it encourages you to treat battles like a solvable puzzle and try often until you solve it. I also love that every single character in your party feels impactful despite it being an 8-person party.

Battle Innovations
Your battle party is 8 characters with only 4 fighting at a time. Each character has an assigned backup character which you can swap in every turn. This system is addictive and has so much potential for different strategies and tactics. Your party has a shared Limit gauge that automatically restores itself to 100% at the start of every boss fight (i.e. you don't have to worry about farming it beforehand). Your HP and MP are auto-restored between battles.

Navigation Innovations
The game shows an icon over any NPC you haven't talked to yet -- This is brilliant. Doors you can use have a subtle green triangle under them which allows the creator to make huge cities that look more alive despite only a few of the houses being enterable. Transportation is both effortless and impactful in this game.

Low-Hanging Fruit
Despite what I list here, I still enthusiastically insist that this game (in its current state) is amazing and worth your time.

The story and dialogue can sometimes vary in quality. Every character only has a single bland sprite so there is nothing to show any emotion in cutscenes except for the dialogue + music. Act I had a killer finale that was diminished by the fact that the Sprite used is so emotionless. There are a lot of plot threads that go unused or underdeveloped. Lastly, about 33%-40% of skills are just plain useless. I'd love to see a remaster or a sequel that addresses these things.

This game makes my list of "Must Play Games"

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite near critical acclaim, why would I not recommend Chained Echoes?

A few things really hampered the experience for me --

1. The story.
Don't expect this to be a cheerful story. Beneath its bright graphics lies a sordid affair which carries out all the way to the end. More bitter for me personally than it is sweet, if you're set for a more philosophical journey than something that takes the load off after a grimy day at work, by all means do give it a shot.

2. The progression system.
I always felt trapped by Chained Echoes' grimoire shard system, which is its equivalent to 'leveling up'. Due to the piecemeal nature of obtaining grimoire shards (which is usually granted after a boss), I felt that I needed to conserve them in the event that I needed to get a particular skill over the other. Not until the last parts of the game did I feel free to experiment with character builds.

3. The crafting system.
It's the same issue I have with the grimoire shard system. I conserved most of my gems until the very end of the game as using them would be more of a waste early on. There's so much potential for this system to be fun, but not having these freely slot out without penalty really hampered the experience.

Was it a good game though? Certainly, it was. But these issues, especially the story, really pulled by my recommendation for this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome game. Best indie turn-based RPG I have played so far and overall 9/10.

Gameplay is fun and very polished 10/10 for its genre. Its different and improves so much on some of the main gripes of turn-based games such as restoring the party's HP and MP after every encounter. This enables you to always go all out in every fight which just feels awesome.

Another thing that separates this game from others is the Overdrive system in battles. It makes battles more interesting in keeping yourself in a favorable situation making you take half damage and use half MP for all skills. But it'll punish you if you go into overheat by making you take double damage and double MP cost of skills.

Story is rich and great with high stakes involved and I personally liked the ending unlike some people. The setting and the world are very cool and illustrated beautifully with its 10/10 pixel art. Plenty of interesting characters you'll meet and some able to be recruited with their own unique abilities and skills so there are a lot of team comps to experiment.

The only thing I didn't like was the crystal system which lacked urgency to use and a bit troublesome to craft. If I wanted crystals, I needed to go around farming them which was alright for me, but if I wanted the very best kind of crystals, I would need to deal with two layers of RNG to get them which I didn't like. Hope it'll improve in the upcoming update soon as of writing this review.

Thank you Mr. Matthias and those involve for this game. I really enjoyed my time with it and can't wait to see more in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is an excellent pastiche of some of my favorite 2D JRPGs - an homage without getting dragged down by the annoying parts of the genre. This love letter to classic titles like Chrono Trigger, Final Fantasy, and Suikoden does more than borrow; it has its own excellent contributions with an amazing soundtrack, cohesive and imaginative world design, and a plot with its own gravity, twists, and turns.

I'm going to go into more detail here but before I do, let me unabashedly recommend this game. Absolutely worth the price if it looks at all appealing, and even if you aren't nostalgic for the genre it's a deep but briskly paced RPG experience.

The back-of-the-box description of Chained Echoes is a familiar one: you'll assemble a motley crew of people that quickly align themselves against a grand threat to their world and fight through an ever-escalating in power series of enemies until they inevitably kill (a) god(s). The game absolutely delivers here, but means justify the ends, and CE delivers here in spades. The presentation is excellent throughout, and the variety of combat stats that can be modified via character choice, equipment, skills, and modular customizable augments is impressive.

The combat in particular avoids a lot of the typical genre issues. Your party enters each fight completely refreshed, eliminating tedious menuing and the optimal strategy going slowly to conserve your resources, extending fights to have access to healing abilities you can't use from the menu, etc. Chained Echoes does not waste your time. Fights are fast and brutal, with status effects giving either strong DOTs or very high swings in damage vulnerability. The overdrive system, something I looked at skeptically at first, gave me big incentives to manipulate the gauge, which has huge effects on its own. It felt good to master how it worked and make it move, and added an additional dimension to my move choices. The stat progression is milestone based (i.e., you gain new skills by defeating bosses rather than by grinding against mobs) which means that for the bulk of the storyline encounters are very well tuned to the rough power levels of your party. Sky Armor fights and the way they tweak your party composition are also excellent, with the attacks gaining massive additional damage and a great, chunky feeling overdrive manipulation scheme. It's a great compliment to the more vulnerable and flexible on-foot fights.

The world design of the game is absolutely genius. From its itemization to optional quests and locations, you're ALWAYS rewarded for going off the beaten path. The reward board is an excellent way to encourage optional objectives and give players side-goals without requiring them, and the fact that it's always available is terrific; a lesser game would require you to plod back to a centralized location. Again, Chained Echoes respects your time. Random NPCs get marked with a dot until you've spoken to them, which both identifies new folks with new stuff to say and also lets you run by them without wondering if they've changed their dialogue for this plot chapter. It's a really great system that in itself shows love for the genre it's now a part of.

The endgame is expansive and elaborate, with new travel options giving you the ability to backtrack to treasure hunt and hunt down Unique Monsters, all of which are marked on your reward board. This game LOVES it when you find its content, and is constantly hinting at secrets. There are no obnoxious hidden mechanics or hidden fail states, if CE shows you an optional path you can go down it when you want, or not at all!

Honestly, my only gripe with the game is that its equipment augment crystal system, which is completely optional (I defeated the super boss without engaging with it much) is a bit finnicky and annoying to use. A shame since it's the system with potentially the most depth and customization potential. That said, even if you barely pay attention to it, it's relatively easy to add a few powerful buffs to your equipment.

Buy Chained Echoes, it's really terrific. A Berserk-inspired classic JRPG-style game with all the modern bells and whistles. Stop reading! Play it!\t\t\t\t\t
