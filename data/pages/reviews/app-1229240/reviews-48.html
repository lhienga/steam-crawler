\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrankly, the game was quite disappointing.
The story is very basic and predictable, essentially recycling the storylines of other JRPGS. This might create a sense of nostalgia for some, but doesn't create a real unique memorable experience.
The difficulty levels are all over the place, where you are either overpowered or unable to defeat anything without the enemies rolling the correct moves at the correct times. This means at several sections of the game there are bottlenecks where, regardless of difficulty setting, the party has to luck out on what moves the enemy uses in what order.
Most of the systems go unexplained, so newcomers to the genre are lost and only veterans can infer how skills function.

Short answer: A game that will be frustrating and unrewarding for newcomers to the genre, not the best suggestion for those new to JRPGs. Not balanced well enough to provide more than nostalgic moments for veteran gamers. Best fit for someone who doesn't want to replay their classic favourite JRPGs, and has low expectations.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played for roughly an hour, but I've come to the conclusion that the game's not for me. A little unexpected, considering that the game very explicitly is made for people who grew up on classic JRPGs, and I can confidently say that I've beaten Chrono Trigger around 15 times, I can (probably) recite some good 30% of Xenogears' script out of memory, and I wore Griever (FF8) pendant on my wedding while dancing to Eyes on Me. Point is, I got the cred.

The first issue I had is perhaps minor, but it's a bit of a pet peeve of mine. There's all this beautiful pixel art, and yet it's not pixel-perfect, and things sometimes get distorted and everything looks flimsy and out of alignment every now and then. Also, the pixel sizes are sometimes inconsistent, which really ruins the look to me.

Second - the references. There are a lot, and I don't like that. To me, it draws attention to the fact that I'm playing a game, that it's a story - made out of pieces of other stories, no less. The opening scene is literally a play on Chrono Trigger's opening scene (down to the location of the furniture!). The main character is called Glenn. The (apparent?) female lead is called Lenne (Leene/Lenneth/Lenna? Take your pick). The second scene takes place in a town during a festival. I understand there's more, including some whole plot references; but I've only played for an hour, so I can only say about what I've seen.

And third, the battle system. It's certainly an attempt at mixing things up, and that's respectable. It's all about choosing specific skills in order to keep your party in the "overdrive" mode, where your ability costs are reduced and you take less damage while dealing more. It sounds pretty cool on paper, but in practice it felt incredibly shallow and just boring; and I can't even really say why.

This is such a shame. For one thing, I have nothing but respect for the dev (es tut mir wirklich leid, Brudi), crafting all of this by yourself is a truly herculean effort, and this is clearly a labour of love. Also, the game's (partially?) funded by the German state, which I believe is the way of the future for indie development - not funding by sales or investors, but by grants like those. So even if I don't like the game, I'm happy that it exists and that it's apparently successful.

What I'm trying to say is: just because JRPGs were a big part of your formative years, don't automatically assume that you'll like this, like I did.

I absolutely abhor mech combat in just about every game; they're almost always handled poorly with the exception of Xenogears. This game is no different, and has changed from me enjoying and loving the game to begrudgingly pressing forward and rolling my eyes with a sigh of defeat every time I'm forced into it.

The Overdrive system is already incredibly unfun to play with, as it seems to disfavor and punish me in combat more often than aid me and help me to succeed. Such is due to its rng nature, so I set the Overdrive system to be in Wide mode. Sky Armor combat seems to widely ignore this setting and even though I'm playing the game with everything but Overdrive set to Hard, I am once again at the Overdrive system's mercy on top of despising combat mech in general.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate at 24.7 hours played: I want to update my review as a warning to other players (like myself) who are looking for a casual JRPG. This ain't it chief!

First things first, I'm no stranger to difficult games. I've played over 170 hours of Slay the Spire and I've made it as far as A17. But the trash mobs in this game are overtuned. Many fights feel like mini boss encounters, and it makes playing the game feel like a slog. HP and damage for all trash mobs need to be nerfed by at least 30% for it to make sense. Why are the trash mobs more difficult than the bosses they precede?

There are also issues with the story, and dialogue. For example, I don't feel like characters need to randomly drop obscenities to advance the plot. It's unnecessary and it feels like the game is trying too hard to seem "adult." It was surprising at first, but then it gets annoying after a while.

It's too bad because I really wanted to love this game. The biggest issue is the tuning for trash pulls. If trash mobs get nerfed 30% or more (across the board) I will update this review.


Original review at 2.5 hours played:
I have only played the game for two and a half hours, but I love what I see so far. I feel like I'm playing Chrono Trigger for the first time, again. That feeling of adventure and excitement is something I've not felt while playing a game, for a long time.

The art and music are great and the characters are believable, which makes the game immersive and cozy. Additionally, the combat system is fun and engaging. I'm not just spamming the strongest spells all the time. Now, I need to manage my overdrive meter to make sure that my party doesn't overheat. And, I appreciate that my party heals fully after combat.

I cannot comment on the overall plot since I have not completed the game. But it seems good thus far.

I was on the fence about buying this game for a while, since (at the time) it didn't have New Game+. As NG+ was added in the latest patch, I immediately purchased the game knowing that I'm going to try to max out my characters, eventually.\t\t\t\t\t
