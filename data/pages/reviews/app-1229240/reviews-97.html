\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a great game. You start every battle at full health which I like, as you don't have to waste time between battles healing up or walking back to a rest area, and it allows you to go all out in battle for fun without having to worry about running out of resources (Unless you do use items). The game is pretty challenging and battles require some thought. It's not uncommon for even normal enemies to give you trouble, and I like that, as they aren't just fodder to just mindlessly kill with normal attacks or auto battle.

The music is pretty good, there's a lot to explore, characters are interesting and it feels like the devs really care about the world they created with the lore, quest, hints, etc.

There are a couple things I would like to see changed:
1 - It can be difficult to tell who's turn it is when its the players turn. Sure I can tell but I would like a character portrait near the command window. I feel that would help a bunch.
2 - This game keeps messing up my party formation. There are many points in the game where it does a major story event, cutscene, etc where once you regain control....your party formation is completely messed up. This happens a LOT in this game and I have to keep reorganizing my party.
3 - The game does a good job in ways in telling you roughly where to go for side events, quest, and the story, but I would like to see a better journal updated more often with what to do and a history of what happened previously. There are plenty of quest where it says something, and you can complete that part and it wants you to do something else but the journal hasn't updated. If I take a week off of playing this game and return, I get lost easy.

*edit*
I have beat the game and I like how they handle things. There isn't anything missable as you can go back and obtain anything needed, and there's a lot of side activities to do either before the final boss or post game (Beating the boss gives you a clear save just before the final boss.) I still have a lot to do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPicked up and played on the recommendation of a friend and I have no regrets in sinking two straight weeks into it. Sincerely one of the most enjoyable games I've played in a long time and a strong rediscovery of my love for the JRPG. I say with no small amount of levity that it's a must-play for fans of the genre (as hilariously cliche a phrase that is nowadays).

Chained Echoes is to JRPGs what J. Kenji Lopez Alt's Food Lab is to cooking: an up to date, modern look that takes traditional techniques and puts them to the test, only keeping what empirically works. By busting the myths that older RPGs were fun because of X or Y and instead just making an experience that would evoke those feelings more directly, it feels like a far more efficient and effective way to hit the way JRPGs made you feel back in the day than even by revisiting the ones you used to play. It cuts all the of the jank that would otherwise wear on you over the course of 40 hours, keeping true to the experience and providing something that no one else out there is.

By taking out random encounters and fully healing you at the end of every battle, dungeons are no longer attrition-fests that rely on minimizing skill and resource usage over long periods of time, hoping you have enough for the boss at the end. Every battle becomes a matter of utilizing all your skills and abilities, and keeping an eye on your overdrive bar, which sincerely makes every battle so much fun. The "milestone" leveling system in the form of grimoire shards means that every battle is relatively well-tuned and balanced and they stay fresh and fun throughout the entire experience.

The world is chock full of secrets and treasure and optional missions that really reward exploring and spending a lot of time in each area and getting to know it. There's lot of required and optional backtracking to areas and always excitement in knowing there's stuff to find lying all around.

The 16-bit graphical style is one of my personal favorites and it was executed so well here. Legitimately beautiful and timeless.

The story writing is sharp and interesting. Clearly written by someone with extensive knowledge of the genre and exactly which annoying pitfalls to avoid, but keeping to the heartfelt sincerity that JRPGs excel at. The game doesn't shy away from darker topics, but also knows that it's not interesting to play them up so much that it exhausts you. The pacing is good and the sidequests and characters are charming and delightful ways to round out a clearly very well-thought-out world. Overall, the amount of text in the game feels perfect. Any more would feel excessive and like it was belaboring the point, and any less would leave it feeling a little hollow. Plus, there are some twists that I genuinely really enjoyed throughout.

Overall, I can't speak highly enough of this game and the fact that it was more or less developed by one person is nothing short of flabbergasting and a little frightening. It punches well above its weight and easily contends with bigger budget games with larger development teams. Chained Echoes has passion in spades and it all comes through in creating one of the most memorable and delightful JRPGs that has every right to stand amongst the venerable giants of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t

It becomes extremely tedious when all the player wants to do is move on to the next story bit. While the leveling system is incredibly novel, the economy comes off as arduous. This game has made me regret every moment I sunk into it as it never gave back with respect to the amount of time I was being forced to sink into it in order to make minuscule amount of progress towards the next story beat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDidn't expect to get a jrpg experience that could rival games as xenogears or chrono trigger for just 25 bucks.

The game is well written and the music is amazing.

The pixelart and sprites are really nice.

Ground combat is really nice but there are some sky armor parts that were a little bit too long. Combat is overall really nice and the bar mechanic and its interactions are a nice idea for a turn based system.

This game is an easy recommendation if you enjoy jrpgs form the golden era.

I really hope to see a sequel. The worldbuilding was well done and there is enough room to continue the story.\t\t\t\t\t
