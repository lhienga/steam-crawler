\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great experience, with a handful of fairly minor nitpicks that just barely keep it from being an absolute masterpiece. If you like games in a classic JRPG style, I would easily label this as a must play, and honestly, unless you absolutely hate turn based RPGs, I feel comfortable recommending this to basically anyone.

First, the good, and wow, there is a lot of good. The game is gorgeous, with beatiful pixel art, just lovingly crafted assets absolutely everywhere. The music is excellent, sound design is incredibly solid. Gameplay is overall very engaging, with a very solid balance at base settings, leading to a challenging, but not overly punishing experience. The story is quite enjoyable, with quite a few twists that, while unexpected, don't feel at all like they're coming out of nowhere, making them satisfying instead of frustrating. Characters are well developed and grow and change throughout the game. There were even a couple of moments that had me tearing up, which doesn't happen too often for me in games.

Now, as I said, the game is not perfect. I do have a handful of issues, but most of them are minor and could really be considered nitpicks, but they do have enough of an effect on my enjoyment of the game to have me label it as just great instead of masterful. Most of these issues relate to the combat. The overdrive system is really cool, but managing overdrive can get very tedious, leading to either stretching out combat trying to stay out of overheat instead of doing damage, or risking taking more and doing less damage by ignoring overdrive management.

Overdrive management is the big issue, but there's also some other problems. The game falls into a trap that a lot of games do, where it's easy to find an effective strategy and just stick with it for the entire game. I found myself using the same party setup about 95% of the time, and really only changed skills for the purpose of mastering them, then switching back to my base setup. You have a lot of characters, and a lot of skills, but little reason to use the characters or skills that fall outside of your one effective strategy. The combat is still fun, but it's much less varied than it could be. It's hard to fault the developer for this overly much, though, as this is an incredibly difficult thing to avoid.

Another issue I had is with the pacing. It's not bad, but it feels a bit unbalanced. The game is split into 4 acts, and it feels incredibly frontloaded. The first act builds the setup for the rest of the game, and it feels like an absolutely huge amount of stuff happens. I think I spent about 40-50% of the game in the first act, and then blazed through the remaining three, even with doing about 90% of the side content. The plot doesn't feel rushed, but it might have benefitted from splitting act 1 into two separate acts, and sprinkling in a bit of side content into the second half of that.

Last minor issue, and this one may not even be noticeable to a lot of people. The dialogue in the game is overall very good, but you can occasionally tell it was written by a non-native English speaker. Some of the dialogue feels a bit... stilted is probably the best word, with a lack of contractions being the most overt thing that pops up from time to time. It never ruins the dialogue, but it does decrease the quality very, very slightly. This is probably the nitpickiest complaint I have though, and the effect on my enjoyment of the game was negligible, if there was any effect at all.

Those are the only real issues I have with the game. I had an amazing time with it, and am truly eager to see what else the developer has in store in the future. Absolutely worth the cost of admission, even if you pay full price.\t\t\t\t\t
