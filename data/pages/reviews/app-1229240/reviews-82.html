\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is a rather unique throwback to old school games from the 16 bit era. That isnt a bad thing, at all however, since the sprite work is incredible and the story is rather unique and interesting.

The biggest thing Echoes has going for it is the combat. You have an eight person party, four of which can stay in the front line and attack and four in the backline. At any time during a characters turn you can swap back between the characters. This is interesting and allows for the player to make a stable party with various characters swapping in and out as the fight sees fit. The other side of combat is something called "Sky Armors." You get these in the middle of the game but they add another unique aspect of the game. You only have four, and cant swap out but they do have "gears" which they can switch into. Starting in first, you can go to second to do more damage but use more TP or then go to 0 and not be able to use any. The final piece of the combat is the overdrive gauge. A gauge up at the top of the screen slowly fills and allows for certain attacks or spells to do more damage or heal more. The draw back is that if you go over into the red your characters will be slower and weaker.

The leveling system isnt the same as other RPGs either. It is similar to Chrono Cross in that you dont have levels, but get a grimoire piece after boss fights to enhance your characters. Each character has their own board of skills, both passive and active, to choose from. You also gain points after fights to level those skills up, up to a max of three. Another piece of the leveling system is the class emblems. These are extra pieces of equipment that give your characters more stats as well as unique abilities; these classes include Warrior and Cleric. These emblems can be equipped to anyone, so you can have a strong magic user have some healing spells if you wish. However, a character can only have 8 active skills equipped at a time, so you better make good use of them. Once a character maxes out the skills of the emblem they get them permanently, so you can keep that ability on a character if you wish. I found it nice to have multiple characters have elemental magic to cover enemy weaknesses,

Combat is very classic turn based, enemies get a turn and so does your party. Agility determines the turn order which can be seen at the top of the screen. Switching out doesn't take away a turn either,which is a positive i think. You have magic, attack skills, healing spells, debuffs, buff and DoTs (damage over time) and each of these is tied to the overdrive bar as mentioned earlier. When the bar hits the green you have so many turns to use that skill to gain a buff to it (Debuffs have an up chevron emblem so using that will make the overdrive bar go down if used) Each character has many different abilities, all of them have at least one of each special skill to use, so you can balance characters around that if you so choose. Enemies can be pilfered or have an elemental weakness, and this is indicated under their name, which is nice to have quick access to. The option "can" was confusing, but you get a party member that is essentially a blue mage (he gains enemy abilities by eating them) so that what that means.

I would recommend Chained Echoes if you enjoy older style RPGs. It isn't too long, honestly. I think you could probably beat the main story in about 20ish hours, but there is plenty of optional content here if you want to try any of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed through this entire game, 70 hours to 100%, on Xbox through Game Pass. Then turned around and bought it on Steam for some reason just to start it all over again the day after completing my first play-through.

No recent game has scratched the classic pixel JRPG itch for me more than this one. If you loved Octopath Traveler, you'll love this game. If you loved FFVI or Chrono Trigger, you'll love this game. If you love JRPGs, you'll likely love this game.

The characters are immediately unique and lovable, with each earning a satisfying growth arc throughout the game. The overall narrative starts delightfully small and gradually expands through twists and turns to a world-saving climax in vintage JRPG fashion. The battle system, level-up mechanics of the world are extremely satisfying. And the developer has shown a clear willingness already to match the intensity of this game's early fanbase, with several patches already out and some significant ones on the way to add a New Game + and revise the imperfect crystal system.

Cannot recommend this one enough for JRPG lovers who are willing to take a chance on a nostalgic classic with all the quality-of-life features of a modern hit. Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t
