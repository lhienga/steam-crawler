\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very faithful RPG of a mid 90s SNES style, similar in veins to "Final Fantasy VI" and "Chrono Trigger".

Before playing this game I went through the reviews here and saw people complaining about the progression system being locked to story battles and this being a bad thing in RPGs, what I say to that is simply: Get good. The game when played on hard + aggressive enemies, in my opinion, is a great challenge and really makes you want to use your full skill set. Not being able to grind much to alleviate this makes it far more satisfying when you do take that victory as it comes down to your skill.

Now for a summary of pros and cons ðŸ‘‡

-------

ðŸŸ¢ Pros ðŸŸ¢

- Really good soundtrack.

- Really good character and level art.

- A unique "Overdrive" system in combat which promotes the use of all abilities.

- A huge character cast with a range of abilities.

- A fairly decent story.

- The map design is complicated enough to reward exploration but not overly convoluted as to require heavy backtracking for goodies. (I had a big problem with this in "CrossCode")

- The game awards you many things through the "Reward Board" giving you different objectives than just the story.

- A good challenge if you set the game to "Hard" and "Aggressive".

- Very fair price considering the 50~ hour gameplay length (I played on hard and completed most side content)

- Game is fully playable on the Steam Deck.

ðŸ”´ Cons ðŸ”´

- Crystal system for fusing weapon/armour buffs is a menu slog and the purity system actively punishes you for trying to put them on early game weapons; might as well late until the endgame before using them.

- Some characters join too late in the story to build them and make them useful among your primary party. I would appreciate a New Game+ and harder difficulties so I could play more with them through the full game.

- There's no archive of the tutorial tool tips and the game often throws a bunch of new info at you at once when teaching you a mechanic so it can be easy to forget. I had to Google a lot of them afterwards when I forgot things.

- The Class system feels somewhat tacked on as the stats given by the classes feel far more important than the abilities for the most part as each character has their niche acutely defined.

- The optional characters seldom show up in story cutscenes which can break the immersion. I hope this could perhaps be fixed up in future updates so they appear and make small comments in said scenes ala the secret characters in "Final Fantasy VI" and "VII".

- A few bugs here and there, but they are being patched.

-------

All in all, I had a blast and it was a beautiful breath of fresh air in this day and age. I eagerly await any new content/sequel in the future and want to thank all those that worked on this game! I recommend everyone to grab it if you even have a slight love of RPGs.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an overall very well polished and impressive game considering it was made mostly by one person. Now despite that I've decided to give it a bad review, and the reason is quite simple. There are way better alternatives.

Story

The story has many interesting idea's many of which are no doubt rather inspired by other titles, so much so it doesn't feel very cohesive. It kinda feels like someone writing a story, getting inspired by something midway through and then just adding said story beat in without really tying it all together in any way. This leads to an endless barrage of plotwists, many contradictions, story beats being revealed only to be thrown away almost immediately. By the end of the game it didn't feel like the same story at all, and most of everything I just went through felt meaningless.

Gameplay
The gameplay has some nice and innovative ideas, and is overall quite enjoyable for the most part. But due to the lack of complexity it grows stale very quickly, becoming a mundane repeat of the same routine over and over. Class Emblems are rather useless by the time you get them, and therefore end up adding close to nothing to the game. Accessories all have rather boring stat boost abilities the only interesting one I found was one that made Robbs traps have a chance to trigger multiple times, the rest were purely stats. The whole crystal system was basically just the passive abilites you already have, but added unto gear which you upgrade every 5 minutes leading to it not only being boring, but also pointless, and a complete waste of time.

Music

There is a really interesting and well made song with this otherworldy feeling which plays mainly during flashbacks, and in the church city. That and the last boss fight theme are the only major standouts here. The rest is not bad by any means, but quite repetitive and forgettable, not that that's a bad thing, they serve their job. There is a definite lack of tracks though, you will be hearing the same 4 themes A LOT.

End

Lastly I would be remiss not to mention that the game has some absolutely beautiful pixel art, most if not all areas are very pleasing to look at, and it's honestly some of the best I have ever seen. But overall this game felt like a waste of time, I would not recommend it if you're looking for a truly unique or interesting experience. That said if you have a lot of free time and nothing interesting to do, I would consider picking it up on sale as a relaxing time killer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, what a journey. I was looking for a game to scratch that 90's nostalgic jrpg itch, and I was not disappointed. Great character development, solid story line, interesting and versatile combat system, excellent soundtrack, and variety of interesting side quests and goals to complete. It always felt like there was something interesting to do, and when I got bored of something there was always a multitude of other tasks to complete.

Some reviewers dislike the leveling mechanic, which leans heavily on obtaining grimoire shards in order to unlock skills and stat bonuses for your party. By and large these are obtained by progressing the story and defeating bosses, or through the rewards board, which gives you bonuses for completing certain tasks. The tasks on the rewards board chain together providing further bonuses and upgrades. Personally I felt like this made the whole experience feel less grindy, as there was limited incentive to repeatedly slaughtering the same basic baddies.

If I had to complain I would argue that a substantial amount of gametime is spent figuring out the best way to upgrade your characters and equipment, combine and set crystals into weapons and armor, and set and upgrade skills to optimize my parties strength. This got a little tedious over time. I felt like every time I would get my party set up how I wanted, I would find some better equipment, or gain a grimoire shard, and I would spend a bunch of time re-optimizing. This wasn't a deal breaker for me, but I could see how some players could get frustrated.

Beyond that I felt like the sky armor combat was a little lackluster, I always preferred the parts of the campaign where I could fight on foot. In practice it just seemed like a simpler version of the basic combat as you don't have access to the full 8 players like you do in ground battles. There is plenty of customization you can do with the sky armor, and it can be fun to do, but it also tended to add to the issue of lengthy optimization sessions.

Keep in mind that this game does not do a lot of hand holding. I feel like completing this game 100% without outside help would be a monumental task, so I do recommend looking up guides and tutorials to reduce playthrough frustration. For a pixel jrpg I felt like the world was huge, and there is stuff hidden in every nook and cranny. It can be easy to miss things, or find what you are looking for, so don't be afraid to seek help.

On the whole this game is a masterpiece, a must play for any fans of the classic 90's jrpg behemoths, and it truly goes above and beyond in many ways. Well worth the full asking price IMO, would love to see this guy make another game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tToday I finished my 2nd playthrough of Chained Echoes, and these are my thoughts:

Pros:
*Mostly good writing: The worldbuilding is good, the plot is fine, and the dialogue is very good.
*Good music, animations and sound effects.
*Very little grinding, compared to typical jRPGs.
*Lots of customization options: You can select your characters' active skills, passive skills, accesory, equipped emblem, and even the gems on your weapon and armor.
*Battle system has a twist that makes it so you don't just use the same commands in the same sequence in every battle, you're rewarded for thinking strategically about which actions to take in order to balance your Overdrive gauge.
*Two alternative, fully-fledged battle systems: one for fighting on foot, and another for piloting mechs.
*The level-up system is the best I've ever seen in a jRPG. You don't gain experience points for defeating enemies; there is no such thing as experience points. Instead, after completing important milestones, such as defeating a boss encounter, everyone gains a level-up. This means the game developer can accurately predict player power for any given dungeon or boss encounter, and design battles with just the right level of difficulty.

Cons:
*The one disappointing aspect in terms of writing is the character work. There's nothing egregiously bad about it, but none of the characters are especially likable; I didn't find myself emotionally invested in, or rooting for, any of the characters.
*Inconsistent quality of character sprites. Some characters like Lenne look great when running around, while others like Sienna look bad.
*Gems were a mistake. They are a large, optional power boost; if you have optimal gem setups, the difficulty level drops dramatically.
*There's a huge power discrepancy between the different characters: Mikah, Sienna and Victor leave everyone else in the dust.

Conclusion:
Chained Echoes is a fantastic jRPG, I wouldn't have played it twice from start to finish otherwise. It blows the pants off of modern AAA jRPGs like the Octopath Traveler and Bravely Default series. There's a few misses, like the Gem system and the character work, but it gets many more things right.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmong the insane amount of JRPGs out there, Chained Echoes stands out above the rest.

A fantastic cast of characters followed by an intriguing plot made me super invested in the game. I'm not going to spoil anything, the game is an experience on its own, honestly.

When it comes to turn based, sometimes fighting might get a tad bit repetitive, but the game mechanics allow you to use a LOTS of abilities (attacks, buffs, debuffs, items,), there isn't a pattern or chain of attacks to follow per se, every time you have to be creative (at least with bosses, long fights and minor bosses.)

Now, the characters... the variety of the cast left me astonished. For good characters to exist there's a core to be followed: Characterization, character interaction and character development. The game NAILS IT, especially when it comes to the interaction.

Great art, and great music too!
The price is also good, grab the game whenever you can, you'll have a blast!\t\t\t\t\t
