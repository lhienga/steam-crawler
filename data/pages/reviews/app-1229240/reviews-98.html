TL;DR
This is one of the best RPGs I've ever played in my life; a definitive tribute to the 90's JRPGs.
_________________________________________

Chained Echoes is 90's JRPG nostalgia as a game. The best way I can describe the whole experience is: what if Chrono Trigger, Xenogears, Suikoden, Octopath Traveler, SaGa Frontier, Final Fantasy Tactics, Breath of Fire and Legend of Zelda were mashed together by this one brilliant German developer?

Easily one of the best RPGs of the year; or rather, of the past decades. It's amazing how nostalgic this game feels, managing to capture the essence of 90's RPG but still giving its own spin to the genre. The story and characters are so well written I've actually cried in a couple of moments that really hit the emotional core.

Character progress is very different in that you don't actually level up in a traditional way. Instead, you get skill points (called Grimoire Shards) upon defeating bosses or completing certain activities, and said points are used to unlock new abilities, passive skills or straight-up improving stats. You get enough points through the whole game to max out all the characters so there is nothing to worry about it. Killing enemies and completing side-quests nets you SP points, which you spend to upgrade your abilities and passive skills.

The Good and the Awesome
✅ Very charming and incredibly detailed pixel art.

✅ The soundtrack is absolutely mesmerizing. I adore its versatility and how themes have this very "PS1 era music" vibe you'd hear from games like Star Ocean 2, Chrono Cross and Xenogears. Eddie Marianukroh is a brilliant composer and deserves all the praise for giving this game's music such immersion.

✅ Combat system is fundamentally simple at its core, yet very engaging and filled with a lot depth. It revolves around the constant use of abilities and swapping between party members.

✅ The story is very reminiscent of Final Fantasy Tactics and Xenogears. There is a lot of political tension, backstabbing and surprising twists and revelations.

✅ The game has a lot of accessibility options that allow the player to tailor their experience. One notable option is that rather than having an actual difficulty setting, you can adjust the enemy stats and their aggression (how often they use abilities) separately.

✅ Exploration feels great, especially after you unlock the Sky Armors (flying giant robots) later down the road. You also get Fast Travel pretty much right off the bat, making sections that require backtracking less draining.

✅ A lot of side activities, such as sidequests, hunting unique monsters, completing specific tasks and farming for materials to upgrade equipment.

✅ As of May 28th, the game got an update that added New Game+, which, on top of giving the player the option to restart the game while retaining all their equipment and skills, also has an option for an additional difficulty setting, as well a randomizer option for treasure chests.


Constructive Feedback

❌ Even with the accessibility options the difficulty is a bit in the extremes: it's either too easy, where enemies hit you like wet noodle, or absurdly hard, where a single hit shaves over half of your HP. There is no in-between.

❌ The lack of a proper level up system can make character progression feel a bit slow. The player cannot refund skill points once spent, also.

❌>✅ The Crystal Crafting system is interesting on paper, but very convoluted in practice, relying too much on RNG. Luckily, you can play the entire game without touching it once and still finish without many issues. Edit: Matthias said he plans on reworking the system on a future patch. Edit 2: he reworked it!

❌>✅ There isn't an option to access past tutorials. Edit: there is now!


Even with the very few weaknesses the game is fantastic as a whole experience. It is outstanding how this was spearheaded by a single individual, aided by some very talented people. Chained Echoes truly is a labor of love and passion for the JRPG genre as a whole, and it shows in every single aspect.

Honestly, I'm hoping this game gets a DLC or even a sequel. It's way too good and I'd love to see more of the lore and unravel more of the mysteries.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a treasure for JRPG fans. It was made with such care and attention to detail that it's hard to believe. It's so fun to explore the world and dungeons, with just the right number of things to look for and make progress on. Furthermore, for turn-based combat fans, it really doesn't get better than this. The overdrive system is like nothing I've seen before (and I've played quite a lot of JRPGs), and it adds such an intriguing dimension to combat that always keeps it fresh. There are very few combat encounters compared to normal JRPGs, but each one is intense and well balanced. I really wish other developers would pay attention. We don't need quantity, we need quality of fights please! I played it on the hardest difficulty and for the first half essentially any combat encounter could mean death, and I loved the challenge. For more casual gamers, normal mode isn't too difficult but still fun as hell.

Of course, this game isn't perfect. I can easily overlook the dated visuals (because it's a classic if unoriginal look, not every game has to be Octopath Traveler) and somewhat awkward dialogue (maybe a localization issue, who knows) since it was made by a single developer who clearly has tremendous love and passion for the genre. My main issues with this game are the crystal crafting system (it's honestly a mess, you are encouraged to spend too much time tinkering with it when it is the least interesting part of the game), and while the sky armor combat is occasionally cool, having so much of it really killed my excitement for the game in certain stretches. If the sky armor was saved for a few combat sequences (similar to say Trails of Cold Steel) it might have been better.

Overall though, my complaints with the game are very minor. I can't overstate how much I enjoyed the challenge each combat encounter presented and exploring the world. Of all the Chrono-Trigger inspired games I've tried, this is by far the best.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an awesome must-have for everyone who've ever played Chrono Trigger or the old 2D Final Fantasy games (4-6).

The story is humorous and light while dipping into darker material with adult language, violent story points and political themes. I like the combination very much. The characters are very good company. I still don't know where some of the story arcs are going.

The gameworld is littered with secret treasures, optional boss fights and easter eggs and it looks great. The turn based combat is a challenge while still being interesting. It is a bit slow paced like all turn based combat games but the fact that most fights can be bypassed/avoided makes it less of a hazzle than other games.

Overall i can't really recommend this game enough.\t\t\t\t\t
