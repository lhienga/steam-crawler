I. Loved. This. Experience.
Even with the negatives that I'll outline below, I struggle with where to begin when recommending this game. It has so much going for it, and I really do look forward to more content, DLC, games, etc. from this developer. And for the love of whatever deity you do or don't pray to, go race some turtles!

Starting with the negatives, I only have a few points that really stand out. There are other minor annoyances but I think they'd read as more nitpicky than valid critique:
-I think the crystal system is overly complicated for a game that's been polished in so many ways. It's about the only mechanic in the game that feels like it's trying to punish the player for using it. You don't have to grind battles to be good, you don't have to hoard or grind loot to be strong, so why is the supplemental system for augmenting weapons set behind so much chance and uncertainty. I know there are mechanics to limit and increase crystal/type acquisition, but they don't really improve the experience.
-Lack of tutorials menu that lets you go back and re-read tips and tricks from earlier on
-The game felt like it ended way too quickly for how much it built up

As for the positives, without gushing:
-Battle Mechanics both in and out of mech suits are so much fun to play around with
-Dying in combat isn't a punishing slog that makes you miserable because you forgot to save and now have to make up 30 minutes of gameplay
-Character choice and synergy are fun to experiment with, but I think most of us probably had 2 - 3 of the same front-line members consistently Sienna, Lenne, Amalia, Glenn, and Mikah got the most mileage in my playthrough
-The story, while having many, many tropes, kept me guessing regularly.
-The soundtrack. It's just a great freakin' soundtrack. I never felt like I was listening to the same track for too long, battle songs were great.

I really hope for more info about The Harbinger because it was left up in the air\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpent two of the twenty hours playing this game trying to beat ten ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ crabs stabbing me relentlessly... and I would do it again, because the game is bloody fun! However, it does have its caveats...

*PROS*

- I seriously don't want to spoil the story AT ALL. It was written with care and love and it has many twists to keep you invested.

- Combat is fun, intuitive and it will keep you on your toes, even though it's tecnically turn-based. The Overdrive mechanic is quite enjoyable and prevents you from spamming attacks and actually has you plan your encounters accordingly if you want to make them easier, especially in higher difficulties. The Sky Armour combat is quite fun as well, which gives an extra depth to combat that is seldom seen in this style of games.

- There's PLENTY challenges to keep you busy, searching and paying attention to your surroundings. Linda and his crew made this game with a lot of passion and it shows, for they try to emulate those feelings we had with old RPGs from the 90's with this gem of a game.

- The music, environments and plenty of the artwork are magnificent too! Plus, some of the challenges are ACTUALLY difficult, so you need to think and plan ahead.

*CONS*

- You get a couple of cool characters WAY too late in the story and they don't precisely bring anything amazing compared to the main ones, who work like clockwork together.

- There's a bit of a skill bloat near the end of the game. There are some skills that are godly, whilst others make the character (Glenn is the best debuffer, for example), but some others are just there, taking up space and/or with another character just doing it better.

*NITPICKS*

- Character portraits don't move nor change expressions and the sprites would benefit a lot from some minor extra animations (like what you would find in FFVI or Chrono Trigger when they're talking to each other). Alas, all the flair went to combat, but I would've loved a bit more emotion or change in their faces when they're talking to each other.

- Some puzzles are great, whilst others are just plain annoying. Be PATIENT; you'll get through them eventually, but a couple do err on the side of bothersome.

*TL;DR*

Buy it! Support it! It was done by ONE guy, Matthias Linda, over the course of 7 long years. You really should just get it right away instead of waiting for a sale. The guy deserves all the praise! <3\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would describe the game as simultaneously very rough around the edges while also being quite polished. Allow me to explain what I mean by that:

Rough around the edges: As I understand it's largely developed and designed by one person, so all the dialogue and story falls on his shoulders. It's a bit of a task for a game of this size, and it does unfortunately show in quite a few areas
-spelling/grammar mistakes which are forgivable but can hurt immersion
-a few overused tropes (everyone in this game loves to suddenly kill themselves a bit too much)
-A very rushed ending, with quite a lot of anticlimactic moments that could have been handled a bit better in my opinion
-some odd moments where you kind of see your sprite jolt around in between cut scenes that could have been cleaned up a bit better

The polish:
-the pixel graphics are quite well done, very beautiful and it always impresses me when a small team (one I believe?) can accomplish something of this scale
-the world building in this game is FANTASTIC! Unique spin on a few races and in general I would love to see more games in this world, he laid the groundfloor for an amazing series.
-The music is incredible and I find a lot of the tracks playing through my head throughout the day, especially some of the quaint town themes. It also can invoke emotion which is hard to do from me.
-he did a good job of making me invested in the story of quite a few of the characters, and made me have characters I both loved and hated
-the battle system is a unique spin on things and I felt it was quite well done, gives a way to char about more than your typical limit of 4 characters, and somewhat forces you to use a larger variety of moves than just spamming the same ones over and over (though once you over-level that does unfortunately not become the case anymore).

Overall I loved the game and was sad when it ended. Pacing-wise I think you really want to push through act 1 because the game truly opens up and becomes something special starting in act 2 in my opinion, act 1 is all just setup for the rest of the game. I do wish a bit more effort was put into the final act as I did felt is was rushed, but to be fair I generally feel that way about most games, it's rare an ending can leave me feeling completely satisfied. That being said without getting into spoilers, it just felt like they built up a lot of characters to be faced at some point and a lot of them you never end up facing, felt a bit underwhelming.

Still though this is such a solid rpg and I am glad I played it, for my play style it took around 50 hours to complete, so plenty of content to be had.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's okay. The writing is what tips it to thumbs down for me.

Summary
I can tell this game has many inspirations from both older 16 bit JRPGs and anime. You can see elements lifted directly from various mechas, Chrono Trigger, Berserk, Final Fantasy, or their derivatives. But from what I can tell on the surface, the game it borrows most from is surprisingly Final Fantasy 12 from 2006. The influences from FF12 are all over with lizardmen and pigmen, teleport crystals, palace heists, the political shenanigans, magical stones of mass destruction, and more. The similarities go on and on. But the best parts of FF12 were it's tight stylized dialogue, the openness of the world (by 2006 standards) and the freeform rpg progression. This game has poor writing and the rpg progression is fairly gated. Despite being inspired by some of the greatest RPGs and anime stories of all time, this game fails to reach their heights.

Writing
The prologue got me engaged and excited about where things were going but the writing took a dive immediately after. Many issues with the writing could be improved with another pass through the script, I think. The script's issues are with diction, cliche phrases, and awkward syntax. These problems are most apparent in the English dialogue, which feels clunky and inorganic. You can clearly feel when a character is saying something not because it makes sense in the situation but because the game needs to inform the audience of some fact about the world or plot. Making the dialogue feel more natural would fix the bulk of this.
The writing unfortunately also has deeper issues. The game has a tell don't show style overall and dumps exposition and character descriptions on you that detract from the experience. The game doesn't trust the player to engage with the story or characters motivations or to comprehend what's happening just from the course of events. Instead it prefers to spell everything out in a way that resolved all curiosity or interest I might've had in the characters or plot at the onset. Exposition appears on screen to tell you exactly who everyone is. Characters explicitly state their motivations in what approaches soliloquy. It throttled whatever interest I started with.

RPG Structure
Chained Echoes starts with a pretty barebones rpg progression. You heal after every battle which is nice in theory but in practice makes the battles often feel kind of pointless. Because you aren't getting worn down over multiple encounters, only the most challenging battles pose any real threat, so why even have the easier battles at all? The game doesn't seem to want you to grind either, limiting both the opportunity and the rewards for doing so. Most progression seems to be gated by story content. The design here feels at odds with itself. I appreciate the anti-power leveling measures, many people who hate grinding will enjoy its absence, and removing the tedium of healing between fights is nice, but these ideas need to be executed in a way that make them compatible with each other.

Combat
The combat itself is decent so far. I wasn't too fond of the overdrive meter which felt somewhat random in what it required of me, but I suspect this improves when you get farther in the game and have more options. I have other nitpicks, but they weren't a huge deal. Overall this stuff was passable.

Conclusion
I don't hate this game. I might even finish it. But it's far from a great RPG. The main thing I look for in a game like this is decent writing (especially if the game is going to spend so much time delivering story). That alone is enough to keep me from recommending it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTl;dr- Buy the game knowing that Tomke shoulda been the main character

Outside of Pokemon or ff14 i think this is the first jrpg I've ever played. The story was what i had imagined a jrpg story to be: overly convoluted with weird and sometimes stupid plot twists but overall endearing. I enjoyed almost the entire cast of characters (except Glenn that guy sucked for the entire game) even if character dialogue felt stunted at times. Considering this was mostly one guys work I think he did an incredible job.

Technically there are a couple bugs i encountered. The game crashed a total of 4 times during my play through. Since the game seems to auto save like every 30 seconds so that wasn't a major inconvenience. At the time of writing the reward board completion achievement on steam doesn't get awarded upon completion but after closing the game and reloading after completion.

Some things didn't make total sense like the crystal system. 100% completed the game without using a single crystal and i don't think many people even understand how they work. Look up a guide online on how to best get tier X crystals and you'll see that the tutorial doesn't explain what you need to know. Some places that could use a map (i.e hideout base) don't have one.

The game play is incredibly fun, both the sky armor (if trivial after leveling up bow gun) and foot battles are entertaining. There's also a surprising amount of side content available both during the story and post game. I had more fun playing this than any triple A game I bought in the last couple years.
