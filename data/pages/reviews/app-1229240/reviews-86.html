\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA charming little RPG with excellent spritework and a fun battle system. The story gets a little strange at times, but it has enough twists and turns to stay interesting.

Because everyone's HP and MP is restored after battle, each battle is more like a puzzle rather than something meant to drain your resources over a long period. The skill system limits you to eight normal skills and two class skills, which adds an interesting layer of strategy as you set up each character for a given role (debuffs, healing, tanking, multitarget attacks, damage over time, targeting certain elemental weaknesses, etc.) and adapt your strategies based on what enemies you're fighting ("Now that I know this enemy can do that, I'm having Glenn equip those skills specifically to deal with it").

It also manages to draw inspiration without being overly derivative. The turn system and character swapping is reminiscent of Final Fantasy X, there's class struggle between nobles and commoners like in FFT (with Robb essentially starting out as an Argath type character), the visual style owes a lot to Chrono Trigger, and it even throws in a little Terranigma influence. It even has a FF9 blue mage style character who learns attacks by eating certain enemies.

Excellent 30-40 hour RPG, more if you want to find all the secrets and complete the reward board.\t\t\t\t\t

Mathias Linda, the sole developer of the game successfully created a love letter for old RPGs.

Story üìñ

In Chained Echoes, you play as Glenn, the main protagonist and de facto leader of a group of mercenaries on a mission to destroy a weapon of mass destruction, restore peace and fight your way through tyrants, monsters, religion and tremendous magic powers.

Without spoiling anything, the game‚Äôs storytelling is extremely well done, and a lot of effort was put into building a cohesive universe. The lore, deities, places, Matthias Linda really thought of everything. Furthermore, this story is exceptionally well-paced and surprised me at times; expecting a classic scenario, some unexpected plot twist drastically changed the course of the story and this down to the very end.

Furthermore, you will be able to play the game with a cast of 12 characters, each with their reason for joining the band and their backstory influencing the main storyline.

Sadly, dialogues sometimes feel forced and extended for no reason. While it is great to have a variety of playable characters for a turn-based RPG such as this, their late arrival in the game and their limited use make the character development extremely uneven. Quite frankly, I didn‚Äôt try most of them in actual fights. I am sure it was also the case for most players.

Gameplay üïπÔ∏è


The Mechanics of the game work well together but can sometimes be frustrating.

Beyond the story and the act of ‚Äúbeating‚Äù the game, there is much optional content. Including and not limited to a reward board that will push the challenge further for full completion. Chained Echoes has an astonishing amount of content for you to explore, loot, fight etc.

In the middle of the story, you will unlock Sky Armour (Mechs), which allows you to fly, discover, and reach new areas of the map previously blocked off. You will also be able to fight with them; it is super fun but eventually becomes a tad tedious as only the same characters can enter mechs. The fighting strategies are extremely limited to a few that shine and many that aren‚Äôt useful.

But the same can be said for combat in general; turn-based and not very innovative until the overdrive system makes its entry. Imagine a bar that buffs you, while you keep it in the green, granting you better defence and less TP (mana) used for skills and pushing it further into the red zone will do the opposite. To raise or lower your overdrive, you need to use specific actions as requested by the game.

I was using the strategy at the right time to facilitate my progression until I completely forgot about this system, ignored it entirely, and wasn‚Äôt punished at all (except at 2 specific boss fights).

Similarly, you are fighting on foot, and agility is the most important stat to grow for your characters. Defence doesn‚Äôt seem to have an impact, and while there are so many variations of builds you can do, some shine, while most of them are not helpful.

On the bright side, all your characters level up simultaneously, and you can max out all their skills and talent tree by simply playing, not limiting you in any way should you have made less desirable decisions early on.

An interesting mechanic is that each character can be assigned a backup that you can switch out at any time in battle to adjust your strategy. Exciting concept, beneficial at times. On top of that, you also have an Ultra Bar, a super skill that builds up with every attack and is available at every boss fight.

Finally, the difficulty will randomly spike, making you wonder what just happened; thankfully, you get to retry battles should you die, which will happen.

Chained Echoes won‚Äôt let you grind levels and overpower the game; you can only acquire new stats or skills each time you kill a boss. Thus, leaving you at the same level as what was first intended by the game. I greatly appreciated that aspect.

Graphics & Score üé®

Chained Echoes has a pretty big map allowing you to explore every little bit of it, and what a beauty. Pixel games are pretty en vogue and have been for quite a couple of years, but this game doesn't pale compared to some bigger names in the genre. Distinguishing itself with magnificent art, locations, buildings, fauna and flora so rich and diverse with such attention to detail that it all truly comes to life.

The Graphics are fantastic, with great animations that are all just contributing to create a general ambience that is to die for.

The score of the game and its different track were also fantastic and reminiscent of some of the classic RPG I played a long time ago. Very well done.

Conclusion
Chained Echoes was a surprise; I wasn't expecting too much and was blown away by the story and general quality of the game. It is an exceptional game to play, even more so considering that only one person made it. This game is a must play.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those times where I wish there was a "mixed" rating.

Pros:
- Story. And this will be a weird one, because I'm putting it on both the pros and cons lists. At the end of the day, it's a fun story that has a few great twists thrown in along the way to keep players guessing - when they feel fresh and original. More on that in a bit.
- Combat system is familiar, yet refreshing. The Overdrive bar keeps boss fights interesting.
- Fun maps, with lots of rewards tucked away in nooks and crannies and an "achievement" system that rewards thorough exploration.
- Soundtrack. Absolutely love every single piece I've heard so far!

Cons:
- Story. Like I said before, the story has its moments, but I also can't overlook the fact that the majority of Chained Echoes lands more on the side of plagiarism than homage. If you're familiar with a good number of jRPGs and anime, you're going to notice a lot of story beats that seem copy-pasted into this game.
- Dialogue. Oh god, the dialogue. I don't know if it's just the lackluster translation from German to English, but much of the game's dialogue feels dull and flat, even when it's trying to be deep and emotional. Typos and grammatical errors are common as well.
- While I'm not a fan of the art style in the character portraits in the first place, it seems silly to only have one per character. Having at least a few different emotions for each character would help bring its main cast to life and give cutscenes some much needed "oomph."

Overall, Chained Echoes is a solid throwback to oldschool RPGs like Chrono Trigger and Final Fantasy Tactics. Its story might be a little *too* influenced by others at times, but still manages to feel new and exciting at certain points.

Where Chained Echoes mainly falls flat is in its poorly written/translated dialogue, which manages to be a large enough barrier to feeling any kind of emotional attachment to the game's story and its characters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to recommend this game, but steam has a "yes" and "no" option, not a rating system. If I had to rate this, I'd say it's a 3.5 out of 5. Chained Echoes is a lot of fun when you get to play it. It's so difficult to keep your party together, it becomes really frustrating to play. Furthermore, you'll finally be able to take control of your party and freely (and I use that term loosely because you don't get to freely navigate the world often) and you'll be bombarded with cut scenes and with TONS of lore dumps. You'll have lore that has lore on that lore, it gets exhausting to the point that I just skip cutscenes altogether so I can just play the game.

Combat is fun, can be frustrating, but I like that. I like the concept that leveling up comes from defeating bosses, but doesn't stagnate combat because you can further bolster your characters combat proficiency with finding and upgrading gear. The idea of unique bosses was a really cool mechanic as well, I love that. The sheer amount of bombarded and forced plot / dialog really killed the experience for me. I just couldn't follow along because it became so long winded, there was just too much to follow. Too much lore dump.

Overall the game has great mechanics with too many cut scenes with far too long dialog dumps. When you do actually get to control all your characters, when you can actually keep your party members from leaving, it's a ton of fun.
