\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is an excellent tribute to JRPGs, yet it is creating its own rules to get out of the pack.
Final Fantasy (Sakaguchi era), Grandia, Suikoden, Xenogears, Chrono Trigger are the fuel franchises that mostly inspired this project and most of its mechanics. Yet, Chained Echoes, is not following "normal codes" of these JRPGs and is pioneering its own systems and that was quite a breath of fresh air in this world of overly-familiar systems, writing and progression. It's price is on point, 100% take it on sale.

TIME
As a single dev project, a full platinum took me 50 hours. The Main story runs for about 30 to 40 hours depending on how much you'll dash through content. This is a generous game and has an end-game chain quest after the main story that will get you to squeeze another 5-10 hours or so.

LORE/STORY
The story beats are nicely paced, but probably not taking "enough time" to set a deep emotional bond with characters or even NPC stories. There are very uncomfortable situations explored here, brutality and violence where strong preys on the weak in very distorted ways. Sadly, I feel it's too much quantity of those shocking writings versus the quality. I think, being critical on a "JRPG-Ã®ke" is the cornerstone of how you can like these kind of text heavy experiences.
As of such, I think it's the weakest part of Chained Echoes.

GAMEPLAY
This "JRPG-like" follows a turn based system based on a speed priority listing. Encounters are visible and are few, but difficult on the map (and can be refreshed by moving from screen to screen, if grinding is what you want).
Yet. levelling is capped by Shards. Shards can only be obtained through completing quests and beating bosses. I think this game gameplay mechanics takes off after the 10 hours-in mark, as there's not enough characters in your line-up to make great chains and combos before that. It is a unique way of making a "JRPG" and pacing it through engaging combat and it pushes you to find "secret bosses" to level up.


ART DIRECTION
Overlooking few sprite art and backgrounds that feel unfinished at very few specific points, the visual quality is very, very good. It's an honest tribute to the Secret of Mana series and Chrono Trigger sprite art. Some
main cast portraits look a bit lifeless (most humans really).

REPLAYABILITY
As most JRPGs, unless you wish to replay the whole game for your own fun, there isn't much to "replay" as there's no new game + here in Chained Echoes.

SOUND/MUSIC
Just a note here that the OST is amazingly good and is on par with many very great JRPGs out there. It's not Nishiki's Octopath Traveler levels, but it's damn close to Yasunori Mitsuda's quality.

PERFORMANCE
On a good rig here:
4K 43" Monitor
AMD RX6800XT
AMD RYZEN 5950X
128GB DDR4 3600Mhz
980Pro Samsung 2tb NVME Drive

Had excellent FPS at over 200 fps at native 4K constantly.

CONCLUSION
It's a 1 dev project.
If you are curious, i'd say give it a go!
If it's on sale just grab it and encourage this dev to pursue his dreams.
I highly recommend!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave you ever seen an obnoxious movie or stage show where every conversation sounds like one person arguing with themselves, using the exact same limited vocabulary and speech patterns and cutesy turns of phrase and empty platitudes over and over for the entire runtime because it's painfully clear it's written by a single smug douchebag who thinks they're some kind of genius? That's Chained Echoes - the biggest pile of self-indulgent slop I've seen in some time. It's not any fun to play either, just lifting design elements from games like Chrono Trigger, Final Fantasy 6 and Xenogears while sparing no thought for their mechanical cohesiveness or pacing whatsoever. There are tons of time wasting click-on-everything-to-find-the-arbitrary-plot-flag segments, obnoxiously boring and drawn-out dungeons, and huge labyrinthine towns with nobody and nothing interesting to interact with; just the same old bland NPCs who wander randomly and repeat inanities forever. Characters are just dropped into the narrative with a 3x5 card of flat explosion laying out their motivations and personality traits in plain text, and that's all the development they ever get. Battles move fast yet still somehow feel drawn-out and boring, with your options constantly restricted at the whim of an incomprehensible meter and every single battle pitting you against damage-sponge enemies that take dozens of special move hits to kill, which is clearly by design since you get healed to full between fights. Basically, this is yet another "homage game" that just uses that label as a free license to waste your time with dated design tropes and steal characterizations and plot points wholesale while contributing nothing worthwhile of its own. And that's the key word - STEAL. There's no layers of depth or fresh twists to anything it does, and none of it is even approached in any sort of self-aware, genre savvy fashion to mock the clichÃ©s; it's just theft. Theft perpetrated not out of any genuine reverence for classic RPGs, but solely to skim a quick buck off gamers nostalgic for an earlier era while propping up the author's own lackluster writing and design skills in the process. So instead of rewarding yet more plagiarism with a topping of personal vanity by someone whose love of classic JRPGs only stretches as far as they can exploit your memories of them for personal gain, just save your money for a few of the games it's blatantly lifting ideas from; nearly all of which are available today for well under this game's $30 price point. Or if you want some fun, strongly written, well-paced indie RPGs that pay tribute to the classics while setting themselves apart too, play Hades, Symphony of War, Ikenfell, Deltarune, Sea of Stars, Kingsvein or Horizon's Gate instead. But hey, at least the title is apt for a game with design sensibilities chained to the worst kind of arrogant mediocrity and a plot that's a faint echo of several much better games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredible, it is nostalgic of the classics (right from the start, there are so many scenes where you can see the developers are paying tributes to Chrono Trigger or Final Fantasy), however Chained Echoes has a style of its own, where aspects of the fights, the gameplay, and the story are all unique. This game isn't afraid to go deep and dark, sometimes I thought I was watching Game of Thrones, sometimes I cried with the NPCs, and I definitely have gasped at some incredible twists.

Initially the fact that there is no levelling up almost turned me away, as I love mindlessly grinding for hours! However, there actually are so many ways to grind and more flexibility of choice that you can make it your own a bit. I suppose we all end up the same at the end, but if you decide to invest in different stats or skills, or upgrade weapons differently, that would make the fights and strategy a bit unique for each player.

I love this game so much that 81 hours in, I still just finished Act II, because I don't want it to end. I kept flying the ship around the entire map to look for places I may not have been, and walking around cities/towns/forests talking to different NPCs in hope of a side quest somewhere. Obviously all of that can be searched online, but the game makes the NPCs interesting enough (and sometimes tragic) to just walk around hearing what their one liner is.

I would definitely recommend it to those who come back to play Chrono Trigger once every year or two! It's clear the developers are fans of the classic JRPGs too, and this game must have been made with so much love. Thanks so much for making such an incredible game, developers :)\t\t\t\t\t
