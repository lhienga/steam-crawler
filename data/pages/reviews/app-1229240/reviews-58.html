\r\n\t\t\t\t\tArt: 10/10
Music/Sound: 10/10
Writing: 10/10
Combat system: 8/10
Can pet the doggos: 10/10

If you have any interest in the genre, play this game. This is easily the best JRPG to have been released in a very, very long time. The story & writing especially is among the best I've seen in any game, period, regardless of genre.

HOWEVER...

There are some smaller issues, like the gear & crafting system just being WAAAAYYY too grind & luck dependent or the characters being really quite uneven in how useful they are and some are introduced so late you'll have a perfectly oiled party machine already and have no use for them or the game mechanics not being explained at all, forcing you to browse wikis to understand what's going on.

And there is one big drawback, at least if you are looking for a fair and balanced challenge:

On "easy" (aka low enemy stats in the options), the game is most likely just story mode. Whatever...

On "normal" (normal enemy stats), the game is still just way too easy and you can make mistake after mistake and will win any combat just fine anyway.

On "hard" (high enemy stats), the majority of the game will actually be adequate in challenge and requires mastery of its systems to a degree.
But every now and again, the game throws non-optional stuff in your way that is far and beyond the rest in challenge and even with perfect equipment will still put you in "reload until RNG goes in your favor" territory - that's not true difficulty, that's just bollocks.
This holds especially true for the first quarter of the game where you just don't have the means yet to adjust strategies at all and later on in quite a lot of sky armor battles where some enemies will suddenly just entirely overpower you unless you get very lucky RNG-wise.\t\t\t\t\t
