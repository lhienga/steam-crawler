\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt definitely feels like it was made by someone who has played and loved many JRPG. Specifically, the older kind such as Chrono Trigger, Xenogears, PS1 Final Fantasy games, etc. Definitely feels like a love letter.

Lot of things to love. Because your health and MP restore after each fight, every fight you have, from regular enemies to bosses, is not afraid to go hard. Keeps things fresh. Locales are spacious and look great. Music is great. The story and characters are surprisingly fantastic. The final boss is up there with some of the best in a JRPG I've played so far.

Thankfully, there's very little to dislike about the game. The Overdrive system is cool, but there are many encounters where you can get bullied into the red with no way out. Especially when fighting hard hitting enemies that can attack 2 to even 4 times in one round. The crystal system was just about useless because of the purity mechanic. Thankfully, you don't need to go deep into that to beat the game.

9/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarly review

it's been a while since i've enjoyed a game like this.
for a traditional jrpg, this game knows how to balance the difficulty in a way that you can lose on regular encounters easily. the boss battles can be tough if you don't equip your characters well, buffs and debuffs are necessary even in normal difficulty, +1/+2 stats are actually meaningful and changes the power level of your units, level grinding without thinking ahead doesn't overlevel (or give infinite amounts of money) make your characters OP unlike in traditional RPGS.

Chained Echoes takes everything good about JRPGS while fine-tuning or fixing the problems of the formula of the old games as the biggest flaw of jrpgs were that the combat is just mindlessly levelling up. I hope to see more of this in the future, i hope for more games, sequels and mostly other franchises copying and learning from the formula this game has.

other positives/ early impressions: Characters are interesting, skills are useful and not merely placeholders, the story is at least decent but you know that there's a lot of thought put into it, the art is at least a 9/10, its easy to see that there's a lot of love put into this game in all aspects.

cons: 1. world-building is just okay, there are 3 kingdoms but i don't know their names, i don't remember them as their faction identity has shown anything interesting yet. this is nitpicking though since its better than most jrpg's.
2. imo the real game starts after you get 6 playable characters together, challenging enemies start at the temple where you see zombies/ ghosts (i hope there will be more of those).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short, this is easily the best JRPG that has come out in over a decade. The music, the gameplay, the systems, the story. All of it works together. If you like turn based JRPGs, you will love this.


The lengthier review. Spoiler Free.

This game is a throwback to the golden age of rpgs (CT, FFVI, Suikoden 2 etc) and has taken the best parts of all of them to create an OUTSTANDING experience. It has the levelling system of Chrono Cross (hold on, it's a good thing!), the greatest battle system ever created (FFX) a world map with an airship like the days of yore, puzzles, great music, epic storytelling, I can go on and on.

In this game, instead of gaining experience points to gain levels, you can "grimoire shards" when defeating most bosses. These allow you to allocate a skill point to either learn a new combat ability, passive ability, or stat boost. Every few shards, you will also get an additional stat boost for your character in addition to what you selected. This means there is no huge power boost for grinding, but that doesn't mean it's pointless if you are the completionist type. The skills you select can be leveled up with points you get for completing a battle. For instance, Glenn starts with cross slash and it can be leveled up to level 3. With each level an ability gets, it will either get stronger, cost less TP, or if its a buff the duration will get longer. All in all, it's a system that rewards you for doing optional side content but if you just want to slam bosses and rush to the end of the game it'll let you do that too.

The battle system. I LOVE IT SO MUCH. It is basically a carbon copy of the FFX system, to where you can switch out characters in battle with no penalty, see the upcoming turn order, and take your time to strategize if you wish. Status ailments are incredibly useful in this game, and it's an interesting system overall. When you apply the inact status on an enemy, their turn is skipped. However, applying a status ailment to an enemy gives them 1 resistance to it the next turn. For example, if you blind an enemy, it will take TWO applications of blind on the next turn if you wish to blind them twice in a row. The best thing to do would just be to wait until the resistance drops, but still. You can keep bleeding or poisoning an enemy if you wish, but there isn't much reason to as the debuffs lasts multiple turns. In conclusion, the combat is fun and the ailment system is strong and easy to understand. No random hidden hit or miss percentages, you'll know beforehand if something will work.

You can learn and set poison resistance or silence resistance on your characters, and you'll know that the first time the enemy tries to apply the status it will fail!

There is vehicle combat in the game, and it persists pretty much throughout. Most sections of the game have normal combat, but there are areas/dungeons where you will be in sky pilot armor. I don't have too much to say, except that it is a fine system. Some battles require a lot of planning, some are more faceroll. It's nice to get a break here and there from the normal system and experience a pretty different way to battle.

I won't go into too much detail about the story, as I definitely think it's best to experience it firsthand for yourselves. It's very good, with a few story elements here and there you may recognize from some of the titans in the genre. There is a base building side quest where you can recruit people you meet out in the world to join your clan and give you passive bonuses and nice QoL. For example, later in the game you can recruit someone who can tell you if there is a hidden item in the zone you missed. Another will tell you if there is a hidden cave you haven't smashed open yet. And some of them may even be playable characters....

Gameplay: 10/10. Perfect battle system, cool ability/levelling system

Worldbuilding: 10/10. Stunning in every way. The story is great, and the dialogue from NPCs really immersed you into the world. I highly recommend taking a few extra seconds to talk to everyone you come across.

Characters: 10/10. Easiest 10 of my life. All of the characters are fully fleshed out by the end and have satisfying development throughout the game. There are a few optional characters you can recruit too, and they all have pretty unique skillsets. One of them is a blue mage in the vein of Strago/Gau, where he can "can" (aka capture) certain monsters to unlock and ability he's forgotten. While I did have certain favorites, I imagine that the characters I didn't use much would still be incredible with the right setup.

Music: 9/10. It's fantastic, and the only reason it's not a 10 is because it feels like there could have been a few more boss tracks to really make them memorable and stand out.

Replayability: ?/10. I imagine the game will have excellent replayability, there are many ways to build each character and tons of characters in general. I'm sure that down the road there will be challenge runs created by the community to only use certain characters, ban skills, etc and that will add unlimited replayability. There is no new game+ from what I can tell, which is perfectly fine.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the best RPG I've played in a long time. It's a love letter to old classics like Chrono Trigger, Final Fantasy on Snes and many others. Everything except of the music is made by one guy, and just for that, it's incredible.

The world in general, the many environments and places to travel are beautiful and well made. The carachter design is good but not great. Don't expect excellent dialogue and an incredible story. For me, those details wasn't important. Do you remember the quality of dialogue in Final Fantasy 6 and it's a mastepiece nevertheless. Chained Echoes fell more like a tribute to the classic area of RPG with its positives and negatives.

The music is generally very good. It's a must for any good RPG and the game didn't miss that point.

A lot of people are complaining about the difficulty. This game is not difficult if you are taking the time to understand the mechanic of the overdrive system and the buff and debuff necessity. It can make a lots of non boss battles unnecessarily long and it can become boring. The boss battle are a delight though and sometime easier than the non-boss battles. I like the fact that the "level progression" is based on the progression of the story. The skill system and equipment are realy simple. It was a big disapointment for me. There's no impressive spells or attacks, everything is a little average. However you can find some awesome synergy, and it's one of the key to be good at this game. About the crystal system, it's just a boring mess.

It's a good game, but not an excellent one. The story felt rushed near the end, with a lots of plotlines and character arc lacking in depth and the antagonists was forgettable. It's nothing in comparision of Kefka or the threat of Lavos.\t\t\t\t\t
