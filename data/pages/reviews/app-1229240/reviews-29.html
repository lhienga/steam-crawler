\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished the game 100% in a lil under 50 Hours (though I am fairly confident it can be done a bit quicker on a casual basis, I'm just slow) and can wholeheartedly recommend this game if you're looking for a reasonably priced RPG experience.

The Story will keep you guessing and on your toes, the Gameplay gives you a lot of options and offsets the need to grind for Levels/Experience unless you are desperate for quick Skill Mastery, some fantastic characters and some optional ones scattered across the realm with their own stories to give you even more variety, with some very good looking Pixel Graphics top things off. There is a lot to like in this game and is something that should give almost any RPG fan plenty to do and keep them engaged from start to finish.

Now this comes with a bit of a caveat. There is a lot of flashing in this game, not just white flashing, but quick strobing effects in general. If you are in any way sensitive to that, I would unfortunately have to suggest giving this one a miss. Even with the games ability to turn White Flashes off, it doesn't turn off all of them, and the quick changes of any colour that the game has at regular intervals will no doubt play havoc on anyone who is adverse to that sort of thing. Please be aware and do your research before buying if this applies to you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an awesome JRPG. Easily top 5 in my library just because of the fantastic story. 9/10


Story: Perfect
The story keep pulling you in since the beginning and never let you go. I always want to know more and play until midnight just to follow up with the story. Although it confuse me toward the end because i thought we have a bigger baddie to kill but the credit rolled, other than that, it is perfect



Exploration: 8/10
The dev fully utilize this 2D graphic with hidden pathway, and door, making each map fun to explore.
The puzzle can be really difficult too, i remember one of the push ball puzzle took me more than 30min to solve.


Battle 7/10
Battle can be split into 2 parts, normal character battle and Sky Armor.
Normal character battle is interesting, although i have to complain about the limited passive slots. I rather they follow FF9 style where you get a lot of passives but you have passive point to equip them, and eventually you can equip more of them, in there, you can only equip 3-5 passives. Each char have like 15-20 passives. Most passive are too situational to justify a slot. So most obvious choice is to pick generic passive that work in all battle, rather than situational passive. This mean by mid point of the game, every skill point you receive going to be useless until end of the game

However it isn't as bad as Sky Armor battle. I loathed this system. It has nothing to do with normal char equipment or passive, it have its own simple system. Too simple and too rigid. Every battle using this mode is despicable. There are so many annoying mechanic, enemy deal too much damage and your skill is limited.

If you are stuck with Sky Armor battle, you just need to grind and max out all weapon/gun to get the extra points. You can't do it while progressing the story because swapping to new weapon mean you missing a few skills. It is really terrible design. It drag down my entire enjoyment of this game.


Overall, I really enjoy this game for the story. 59 hours just flew by in an instant. Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a game inspired by old school RPGs, this game far exceeded my expectations.

Despite being heavily inspired by other games, this game actually stands out in it's originality.

Most mechanics in this game is unique and works very well. From battle, progression, world building, character development, it all feels new and fresh for a type of game that has such a long and established history.

There are problems with this game of course. Class emblem mechanic is problematic. There isn't enough class emblem variety in the game to justify it's implementation imo. Yet, it provides such a significant power up for the characters but there are less number of emblems than playable characters. Also, it's very missable.

I also don't like the motion blur and camera in this game. I'm not extremely sensitive to motion blurs but this game can make me slightly uncomfortable.\t\t\t\t\t
