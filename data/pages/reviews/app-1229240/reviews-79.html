\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI usually don't like turn-based RPGs, mainly because they often feel slow and repetitive. However, this one managed to keep me interested because the "Overdrive" battle mechanic, which encourages changing things around, keeps it engaging, and there aren't any random encounters.
Also, the story is pretty solid and doesn't drag on for too long. The less important parts of the story are told in side quests, so you don't have to sit through details you don't care about. That being said, I personally found even the side quest stories interesting, as they were related to some parts of the main story or world setting.
The idea of recruiting people to help you is neat too. I like the soundtrack as well.

Overall, everything ties together really well, and it's a fresh and enjoyable game that respects your time.

...You can also pet all the cats and dogs within reach of the player character. Amazing.\t\t\t\t\t
