\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first game I ever finished out of spite.

The audio/ visual presentation that most positive reviews seem to rave about, is in my eyes, passable at best.
The music specifically is mostly lacking in character and expression. I listened to a decent bit of jrpg music during the 2 weeks or so, it took me to play through this game and every single piece of music I heard was more enjoyable than this games soundtrack. It could be a matter of taste, I guess... so if almost stocksounding orchestral music is your jam, be my guest, but the stuff of legends this is not.

While the visuals, admittedly, have some very nice pixel art, its usually reserved for backgrounds. The reality is that for the most part you will look at samey monotonously colored dungeon floors, with layouts that are largely devoid of landmarks but if youre part of the dungeoncrawler crowd, this probably won't bother you, I reckon. The silverlining here being that the map is solid and I didn't get lost during any part of the playthrough. Ill just mention here that exploration is for the most part optional, until it isn't because it's the only way to upgrade your mechs.

The combat comes in 2 variations, on foot and in mech. Each presenting a somewhat different version of the overdrive system. Both versions have their own issues. To loosely describe overdrive; its a bar with a green area you essentially have to be and a red area that will have your party take a decent often deadly amount of damage.
On foot the overdrive system comes down to "eventually you have to waste a turn". Thats it. There are different ways to manage(read: lower) the gauge but this either happens naturally by just doing what you would do anyways, or will lead you to do something you don't want to i.e. waste a turn.

In mech combat the problem is that managing the bar takes over the entire gameplay, making encounters feel less unique and more like a wrestling match with the games systems. Difficulties rarely being a result of tough bossfights but because you have to often block with characters who are capable of taking action but would put you in the red part of the gauge.

And finally the story/writing.
I saved the best for last because this games story and writing is without a doubt, undeniably and absolutely terrible. I guess I'll give an obligatory spoiler warning here but quite frankly this game doesn't deserve it. The best example of the atrocious writing and structuring of the game comes at the beginning of act 2 (about 10 hours into the game), where the mc has a bout with depression. A bolder (probably better) game would have taken an hour or two here to give the mc his own arc and solo quest, you know building the character and giving the whole situation emotional impact. Instead the whole situation is resolved in ten, TEN! minutes, with the use of some ghost? children (this plotpoint is picked up here but never resolved or elaborated on), and a peptalk boiling down to "We are all in this together so get in the robot shinji". The entire games writing is like this. Plotpoints are picked up and then never elaborated on, at some point the game contradicts itself, the entire first 2 acts, you are essentially an errant boy going on fetch quests, characters have little motivation, the story constantly tries to get one up on you with nonsensical twists, character moments are presented and shoved aside so the game can move on to the next equally inane bit. At some point an entire village is introduced, you have almost 0 interaction with them, then they all, yes ALL, commit suicide. The party character (sole survivor) you gain out of this nonesense shows little to no interest in these events and then the game just moves on.

Would I say I wasted my time playing through chained echoes? Probably not, but I loved hating the game. If you're looking for a genuinely good time, I wouldn't recommend this to anyone. Even if you want to ironically play the game to laugh at it, wait for a steep sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, took awhile to complete but here is my 1 word review of the game, i.e : Iffy . Make no mistake, it's definitely a good game but there are things in the game that makes me uncertain. Anyway, here is my breakdown:

Music: It's not the best like Octopath Travaller 1 / Nier Automata lvl of music but it's still good enough to catch your attention. Every music theme is fit into its location. If you go to X place, you get Y music. It's as simple as it is.

Art style: Well, its pixel art, not much you can say about it. It is vibrant and colorful so its not like Graveyard Keeper where its color is on the darker side. I do have to say that however there are times where I miss stuffs because of how well the blending is, i.e, missing a cliff because it blends well with the landscape.

Story: I like the entire story about how it all started with a war and develops into something more than a war. Though I wish the story expands more than the war itself, that is, the outer continent or the other gods or something but its good enough.

Characters: This is, imo, the weaker side of the game. I appreciate that the script writing feel very real and authentic ( as how humans would have talk and react ) but the biggest flaw I "think" is that there isnt really anyone to feel attached to . As much as I want to explain more about this part, I dont think its advisable for me to do so since it will spoil quite a lot of the content.

Game mechanics: This is the most iffy part about the game. I do think the dev know what they are doing but for my first time to try this type of game mechanic makes me super uncertain. Let me explain.

For most JRPG in general, there will always be a way to win. If you think you lack DPS / armor, you farm monsters for money to buy armor. If you think you lack xp / skills to win fight, you fight monsters to get xp and if you think you need heals but dont have a healer, you fight monsters to get money to buy heals.

Chained Echoes takes all that away but with a catch. There is no penalty for you running away (100% guaranteed escape), you will always be 100% full HP/Mana after every fight encounter (meaning no need to heal in a inn) and the enemy you encounter will always be more or less the same lvl (meaning you wont be able to fight a dragon that 1 shot you nor be able to travel to places that are not at your lvl). This puts it in a way that when you battle, you aren't guaranteed a definite win (as in outlvl your enemies with xp and armor) and you can lose but you have to think smart on how to beat your enemies. I guess it took me around 40-50 hrs to get used to this gameplay but yeah, its a weird one. Also every HP/Stat/Armor/Defend/DPS counts.

All in all, it's a good game. A solid 8.5-9 / 10. I would prefer if the game expands more smaller stories and banter talks and chit chat about the characters and their life, to further give some attachment to them.

Nitpicks

Nitpick 1: Though I didnt like the concept of the sky armor because it feels out of place. I understand sky armors are needed to fight the bigger threat monsters but then every town is a town and not a megacity. Its equivalent to let say... putting a tank into the year 1500. I know it states that the war is forever ongoing but at least there should be 1 or 2 towns that are mega cities with high rise buildings or something.

Nitpick 2: Honestly, I didnt like Lenne entire backstory.

Nitpick 3: Among all the potraits of the character, I think Glenn portrait should be improve. Its bleh tbh. Just take the steam wallpaper picture of Glenn

Nitpick 4: Towards the end, there is this room where there is 2 secrets, i.e a elevator key and a map . I think that 2 things should be put into the MAIN corridor and not at a side like that. I somehow manage to miss that....

Nitpick 5: There really should be tutorials info or something to explain a lot of the game mechanics in the game. A lot of it is left out. I actually have to read some guide online to explain to me what is the overdrive mechanic as well as some other stuffs, like the deals and stuffs.

Nitpick 6: Along with the unique monsters, the deals should have the option to see what monsters/deals have been done. Because of the deals, I actually have to look up AGAIN to see what I purchase and what I havent done, in order to 100% it

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStory was pretty good at the start. On the one elevator down was some pretty decent worldbuilding , giving your lil continent some scope of the world, was pretty cool, they definitely could have done more with it there, the ending was a bit weak but i was okay with it, and then the real ending happened and it was like, this game is stupid lol. Other then that combat was pretty fresh, and the switch out system was actually pretty fun as i liked that it had actually strategy restrictions to it. The mech fighting was a bit stale. All in all I would probably play it again, it was pretty fun and the character were mostly likeable and there were some good twists. The character growth kinda ended half way through though which was unfortunate. SO led with a strong hand, but ended with a pair of 8's

Pros:
-good switching system for battles
- customizable battle skills for the ultimate strategem (i died in more battles then id like to admit which i liked)
- Lotsa plot twists and some decent storybuilding (elevator scene the best part)
-Materia slots were nice

Cons:
-Bad guy cop out
-battles eventuallly got pretty repetitive, possibly my own fault
-Last half of tthe story felt incredibly forced like they didnt really know what to do with it
-Weapon changes happen SO fast that there almost felt like no reason to upgrade them all, I spent a lot of time removing materia and adding materia lol

I would recommend if your looking for something in the genre

There is no true progression gems might as well not be used til the very end of the game when you can get the ones that actually matter by then you get to use them in very limited parts of the game, the story is very patch work, stumbles leaving blanks in events it expects players to fill out on their own, and retreads it's self on multiple occasions. Combat at first seems interesting til you realize all it's designed to make the battles drag out longer than they need to be with the heat gauge. (Being encouraged to make moves or use characters/skills you wouldn't want to in a turn is not fun)

Overall would not recommend, hopefully the developers learn from this game and their next game if they make one builds off of the mistakes made in Chained Echoes. They were close to having something good here just fumbled hard with the core mechanics and story.\t\t\t\t\t
