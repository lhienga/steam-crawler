\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game fully on the Steam Deck as an FYI.

It's a meh game to me that I enjoyed but I think it's being overrated tbh. People are comparing this game to Chrono Trigger and FFVI and I think that's doing a huge disservice to those games and the legends that made those games. This game simply doesn't compare to those, it merely tries to imitate while modernizing. Don't get me wrong...if you enjoyed those games you'll enjoy this game, but this game sits at 92% rating when it should be closer to 85% lol. And really to me it's like a 6.5/10 game.

I'll just make a general list of what I didn't like, there's a lot to like in this game, some of the characters are actually dope, the mech stuff and some of the puzzles are kinda cool, uh....it's pretty modern in terms of a JRPG style game, the graphics and bosses...are cool. There's like three good songs? Ok there's not that much actually.

- The sound effects are atrocious in this game, walking through water is a never ending sound effect loop that's horrible, same thing with sand and dirt and it's not good...

- In a game like this there's no way to mark the maps, so if you wanted to back track you have to manually remember where everything is, also the way the game handles side quests is horrible, they are locked behind story progression which kills how games like FFVI did these where you were just stumbling into them organically.

- You'll find yourself mashing A through the essays of boring dialog that doesn't really matter, of course randomly there will be dialogue options that can appear and there's no buffer (or at least not enough) of a buffer window to stop you from just mashing the first option randomly.

- When you run into a battle and mess up an option you cannot just manually reload the game, if this is a modern RPG I would've liked the option to quit out of the game or reload my save from a battle and not have to waste time allowing my party to die, this point only applies to boss enemies which you can't run away from. It's pretty much a waste of time and annoying.

- Because of the way the battles are not "random" they are pretty much forced to progress through the map, and they become...tedious, they are not easy fast battles typically in new areas and many of the battles can be very RNG dependent on who the enemies gang pile randomly, or what moves they do, sometimes it can be ez pz, other times you will have to run away and try again lol.

- The edges of the map zones are not well defined at all, I find myself randomly going into other areas of the map without meaning to when flying in the mech, it's annoying.

- The game takes control away from you way too often to show some dialog that doesn't matter. It's annoying because they will do it in areas where there's puzzles or exploration as well and you're in the middle of thinking of how things are gonna go together or this new area and boom you have to watch some 3 mins of dialog only to be like wtf was I doing again?

- The story is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ horrible, I'm not gonna lie this is one of the most boring storys I've seen in an RPG in a long time lmao. The characters are so generic and forgettable for the most part that they really don't matter, I can't even explain most of their stories, oh this girl was part of a church, this dude is like a sage or something, people getting reborn? The ending is like...I don't even know what the ♥♥♥♥ I even killed at the end, it's just bizzare and dumb.

- The game drip feeds you upgrades for weapons which all look the same btw the number is the only thing that changes again the SNES classics did this way better. You get access to ultimate weapons of the game so late in the game that they are literally irrelvant same thing with a lot of charactesr who are pretty much just worse in general than the main cast who are so good that there's almost no point to using any of the other characters. Like the main character and the red haired girl are so good they can literally carry the whole game by themselves I feel with any other characters just being there for magic or support.

- The suit movement is so clunky and weird you can land in certain areas but not take off from that same area, or vice versa where you can lift off from an area in a suit but not land with it. There's other weird things like you can't fast travel from your ship for some reason? Like why? Annoying.

- I ran into several game breaking bugs that had me have to restart the game, with the character sprites disappearing or frame rates so low that I kept thinking the game was freezing, I think many of those were fixed with patches but I can't tell.

Lastly this developer seems to have a thing for bestiality, he had a lot of random places where he had some pig dude behind a woman or on top of a bed with the woman saying stuff like "Good piggy!" or "Oink for me piggy!" it was just weird...and he hates cats? Yeah...no.

It's like the JRPG equivalent of what Chasm was to Metroidvanias, passable but very dull, soulless, and plagued with little issues that ended up just making the game annoying to play. It's still fine if you like JRPG but it's not a benchmark of JRPGS by any metric IMO, it's just that the bar has been so low for these types of games that we are happy to even have a passable game in this genre coming out in 2022.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChrono Trigger aesthetic with a Final Fantasy 6 story and Final Fantasy 10's combat system (with a dash of Xenogears combat). Pretty much sums up what Chained Echos is.

Cute throwback RPG but overall combat is probably the most intuitive and best part of the game. Enemy variety could use a lot more work and the Sky Armor inclusion seems like a fun and interesting idea but the SA combat takes away from the much better regular character combat.

Characters are okay as well I found 2-3 party members really interesting while MC and the others are meh. (Also IMO the more interesting party members are met half way through the story). Story is okay as well. Wish it could of been more compelling or captivating, but it's a RPG inspired by so many other SNES RPG's of it's time it's not surprising the story also is not as inspired or has it's own way of uniquely special.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust 100% the game on normal and oh boy, what a good game.

First of all, the story. A mix if political war between two countries while at the same time, something bigger than anything else lurks above, slowly developing in something much bigger. You could say that's the plot of most JRPGs and you wouldn't be lying, but as long as its well developed, and imo this one does, I think it works.

Loved the gameplay too. The combat plays around managing the overheat bar, which increases or decreases depending of the your actions, so keeping it on the middle maxes out your damage and reduces the received one, while keeping it over the top makes you receive a ton of damage. This changes how a battle develops, since the skills that increase the bar change every time you use one, so you have to both play according to the battle situation and the bar situation.

The game also features auto healing post battle. This decision significantly affect 2 things. First, it makes healing item less common, since you don't need to heal your hp/mana after every combat. Second, it pumps up normal encounters. Since you regenerate after each battle, you can use EVERYTHING in each one. No more AOE that 1S every normal encounter but you have to use mana potions after each encounter, or situations were you don't want to use skills to save mana. Here enemies hit hard and in later areas normal enemies are really strong, so the objective is not to manage your resources thinking in 20 battles ahead, but to win each single one.
Chained echoes doesn't have random encounters either, which isn't exclusive of this game, but i think is something work saying. Most enemies can be seen before fighting them (some are hiding) and some can be dodged by walking around. You can also fly from encounters without a problem, but you will be thrown behind so if a strong enemy is blocking a path, the only answer is to battle.

While the presence of enemies as part of the world and not into random battles is not uncommon, this part really is. In Chained Echoes, you don't level up. An JRPG where you don't get exp? Oh boy yes. Battles give you loot and SP to level up skills (they both level up passively as you use them an with the SP you gather). So, how does progression works? Well, you change your equipment for higher tiers and you level it up, you can also gather and craft gems to put inside your equipment (like Xenoblade Chronicles, if someone has played it) and the BIG part is with skill points.
As you progress through the game and defeat bosses, you are rewarded with skill points that let you unlock Active skills, Passive skills and Stat boosterss. You have a limited set of 6 active skills (that later gets increased to 8 with another system I won't get into here) you can have equipped, and another set of 3 passive ones (increased to 5). After a set number of skill points invested you also get a general stat boost, so there are like little milestones that make you grow when achieved. The difference with the Skill points and EXP is that here you can't grind to be overleveled. If you are in x spot in the campaign, you have 4 skill points and a boss is too strong for you, instead of farming to overpower it you have to play better, change your strategy and learn how to defeat it. This also means that bosses can be designed knowing exactly the strengh the player will have when fighting them, and it feels like it when playing.
This changes a little after completing a point in the story that opens the world, but you don't get that much stronger since there are areas that you can access but are too hard for you to progress through.

About difficulty, the game has 3 little difficulty sliders (I played it default, everything on normal) that can be set to easy, normal or hard. These sliders affect Overdrive size area, making the Overdrive system already mentioned harder or easier to manage, Enemy stats, increasing or decreasing hp, attack etc, and Aggressiveness, that changes enemies behavior.
I haven't tried full hard myself but Im pretty sure its a real challenge.

As a recap, the story and characters are interesting and cool, the combat system, both visually and mechanically is pretty good too so please, if you like the genre, Chained Echoes is such a good game.\t\t\t\t\t
