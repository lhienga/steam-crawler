\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe battle system is great and what you play the game for. Characters have skills that compliment one another, so they have this synergy that makes it fun to build your party and swap characters. Because the total party is so large, you can also build a party that has a lot of different specialties and strategies available. And since your HP and SP are full at the start of every battle, you’re never afraid to go all out on every battle. The Gears are great too, so with all that this game definitely scratches that Xeno itch in 2D.

Unfortunately that doesn’t extend to the writing. The story is good, but I was ready for it to be over long before it was. The dialogue can be kinda not great and it took me out of some key moments.

Also in perfect Xeno fashion, there‘s a gem system that is not very well explained. And some status, buffs, debuffs, etc. just have misleading descriptions.

Great game all around, and considering it’s got a dev team of 1, it’s a remarkable addition to its genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt has the same problems Ikenfell had.

Good art and music and a battle system that seems interesting and fun until you realize every single trash encounter is a mini boss battle. Its refreshing until it gets tedious and only continues to get even more tedious. Managing and finding crystals for your gear (materia) is also pretty tedious, and many of them feel largely arbitrary.

The story seems solid at first, but it develops more and more issues as time goes on and you start to see the hastily stitched seams. At one point even the writers realized that one of the characters had literally no reason to hang around, so they cleverly subverted this with some comedic lamp shading that now feels a lot lazier after the arbitrary writing they used to propel us to the final level of ACT 1.

Overall a game that starts out strong but gets tedious really fast.

Specific complaints with the story

Spoilers ahead.

We get "Nooo, we can't kill them, they're my countrymen, let them capture us" to "okay, now that the writers dilemma of 'how do we get the players onto this set piece for the end of the act' is resolved, I'm totally okay killing my countrymen," within ten minutes of each other.

There's also a pretty lackluster "party member betrays you" at one point that, which on second thought, does not quite come out of nowhere, but their reasoning seems incredibly weak and runs counter to their established character. Especially when much better reasons already exist and were presented within the plot. Like seriously, you can't give us the setup of, "my reasons for doing all this are to benefit the people" then have the payoff be, "so actually I owe these guys money, so I'm betraying my principles."

Literally all you had to do was go, "We can't win a war with these people. We need to cooperate with the aggressors to mitigate the coming war. After hundreds of years of war, our people deserve a quick end so they can get on with their peaceful lives" and boom, rationale that lines up with their ideology of caring about people, even if flawed.\t\t\t\t\t

Chained Echoes

PROS
CONS
Innovative turnbased system
Equipment Upgrades felt weak
Colorful cast of characters
Difficulty pacing was a little off
Writing was engaging…
…until the last bit
Incentivized just playing the main story
Static expression boxes

Bugs and Issues

  • Sound Sync issues
  • PLAYED MAINLY ON STEAM DECK
  • AMD Ryzen 9 3900X
  • ASUS TUF RTX 4070Ti
  • 32 GB 3600MHz RAM
  • 2TB PCIe 4.0 SSD
  • 1440p Settings

\t
Content and Replay Value
\t
The game harkens from old school classic RPGs such as final fantasy and chrono trigger. There is plenty here and took me 68 hours to beat. I find that this game doesn’t have much replay value because there isn’t much to change outside of your cast as most serve a specific role
\t
Should you get it?
\t
This is a love letter to old school RPGs. From the tropes, the style, a little bit of the combat style. Everything is here if you like turn based RPGs such as classic Final Fantasy and Chrono-Trigger
\t
Overall Verdict
\t
8/10. This game was solid with the systems it implies, but the overall difficulty pacing was off. Mostly, it was how the story ended. It wasn’t rushed but felt convoluted and left me saying out loud, “I guess…”.

Setting & Writing:
Set in a war-torn land, Chained Echoes places you in a nation who hasn’t known peace for many generations’ lives. You start out playing as a mercenary Glen who’s supposed to go on a quick in and out mission. This sets in motion the events of the game which involve a weapon of mass destruction, angry petty gods, and reincarnation. This gives way for many points of intrigue in the story. All of this is engaging until the last bits of the game which seem to throw the player for a loop completely. Sometimes instances like these capture the player, I was not one of those players. The game ends with a cliff hanger considering the main issue doesn’t get solved. When credits rolled, I sat back and just thought to myself that it ended weaker than I expected.

Gameplay system & Bosses:
What Chained Echoes does to stray away from the classic turn based is introduce an overdrive gauge. This gauge moves with every action taken. Split into orange, green and red, the overdrive gauge brings strategy into how the player chooses their actions. Each special skill is classified and using a skill that matches your overdrive gauge will move the cursor inside. The objective is to stay in the green for added advantages such as extra damage and reduced skill cost. If the overdrive reaches the red, enemies do more damage and skills cost more. Bosses take advantage of this gauge by using moves that mix out the normal order of the overdrive gauge. Later, the game introduces (badass) mechs that players can pilot that add an extra dimension to area traversal and discovery. These mechs do substantial amount of damage and change the overdrive movement by linking it to “gears” of your mech. Having your mech in a certain gear will move the gauge in a given direction. This change alone keeps the game from just mashing the skill that does the most damage. Sometimes you’ll have to sacrifice a few turns to make sure you don’t go in the red and suffer the penalties.

Quest, Mini-games & Challenges:
Chained echoes side missions are not hard to miss, which I love! At a point in the game, you will be able to go off and adventure in your sky ship straight Cid style. Looking at your map you will be able to discern the side missions that you can do and points where to go next. As a person who doesn’t have as much time as I used to play video games, this streamlines everything for me. The content of each side mission varies, but for the most part they hit. There are some heavy hitting side stories and others are pretty lighthearted. In classic RPG form there are secret boss fights that test the build of your group. There are also special beast hunts that only occur if special conditions to make them appear are met. Both of these task do reward you with special equipment and weapons for your troubles.

Miscellaneous (art, music, etc.):
The game employs the classic 16-bit sprite art concept along with full bodied adventure JRPG soundtrack. There were some definite bangers in there for me such as Rohlan fields. It does a good job of making a high fantasy sound track that tickles your nostalgia bone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; If you like JRPGs just buy this, you will not regret it!

I grew up on JRPG's, and after 7 years or so of being burned out on them, I finally got the itch to play one again. This game was EXACTLY what I was looking for. It's a wonderful ode to the classics of the SNES days while staying fresh and modern enough to not feel outdated, and a few boring tropes of the genre have been tweaked for a smoother, better paced experience. It's got the anime aesthetic. the expected character specification and party management, and the battles manage to stay difficult enough to keep you from mindlessly mashing "attack", yet rarely get frustrating. The story is something special, too. This game's world quickly becomes so alive and rich with detail and history, and the pacing of information and mystery kept me so invested and eager to know what was going to happen, I couldn't put it down. In fact, I really hope the developer is able to give us more games set in the world of Eldrea, because it's a place I would love to continue to explore and experience.
For my fellow Trophy hunters out there, the game is "platinumable" in one playthrough, and to my knowledge nothing significant is permanently missable. I just earned my final achievement an hour ago, and I still feel like I could've happily spent several more hours with Chained Echoes. I'm really glad I gave this one a shot, and I now will be keeping my eye out for whatever this dev has coming in the future. If JRPGs are your jam, check this one out right away, it's a winner!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes, a remarkable title for true JRPG fans, takes us on a captivating adventure reminiscent of the golden era of PSX JRPGs. From the moment I delved into its enchanting world, memories of beloved classics like Chrono Trigger, Xenogears, and Final Fantasy flooded my mind, creating an immersive experience that tugged at my heartstrings.

One of the standout features of Chained Echoes is its innovative and engaging battle system. The strategic depth combined with fluid mechanics kept me enthralled throughout the game. Every encounter felt like a thrilling puzzle to solve, requiring a perfect balance of strategy and quick thinking. It was a delight to experiment with various party compositions and witness the synergistic interactions between characters and their abilities.

Exploring the vast world of Chained Echoes was a joy in itself, thanks to the well-implemented fast travel system. Seamlessly moving between locations not only saved precious time but also allowed me to fully immerse myself in the beautifully crafted environments. The attention to detail in the visuals and the atmospheric soundtrack enhanced the sense of exploration, making each step a delightful discovery.

One aspect that particularly stood out to me was the game's achievement system. Chained Echoes cleverly simplified the board reward system, making it both accessible and rewarding. Completing various in-game achievements felt like an enjoyable and fulfilling task rather than a tedious obligation. The sense of accomplishment as I checked off each achievement evoked a genuine feeling of nostalgia, reminiscent of the rewarding challenges in classic JRPGs.

Chained Echoes masterfully capture the essence of the beloved PSX-era JRPGs, transporting players back to a time of unforgettable adventures and magical storytelling. From its engaging battle system to the seamless exploration and satisfying achievement mechanics, every aspect of the game radiates a nostalgic charm.

For those who yearn for the glory days of JRPGs, Chained Echoes is an absolute gem that will rekindle that special flame within. It's a testament to the developer's dedication and love for the genre, and I wholeheartedly recommend it to any avid JRPG enthusiast seeking a captivating and enchanting journey.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent game that truly recaptures the JRPG SNES magic. It's great on just about every level: exploration, combat, art, story, side quests, and customization. And it evokes numerous classics from Chrono Trigger to Xenogears.

I only had one grip with the game: the ending. It's bad.

ENDING SPOILERS

Essentially the hero, Glenn, has been reincarnated numerous times, and he's a real sad boi about it. So he decides to give his fated responsibility of immortality and ultimate power to protect the world to the Delita/Kain of the game, Kylian, who has basically doubled down on being a nihilistic douche the whole game and we had to kill twice. So now we trust Kylian finally had a change of heart because he got beat down so much and felt bad? And Glenn who is ostensibly sick of sad goodbyes doesn't even say peace out to his party and just dies and is reborn as a bird I guess?

Obviously the ending was supposed to be poetic somehow, but instead it ends up a thematically muddled mess. Basically human life is too difficult for kind and compassionate people who care for others and have to lose them, so just kill yourself and be a bird, and give all your responsibilities to a selfish ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ instead.

Otherwise a phenomenal game though. 🤣🤣🤣\t\t\t\t\t
