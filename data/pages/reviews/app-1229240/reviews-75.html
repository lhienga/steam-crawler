\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually leave reviews.... but holy crap this game was amazing and got me excited to play games again, after a bit of a lull and now searching for more games like this (and hope there are more on the way).

The classic game art style and music are excellent - the bosses are super weird and gross and quite a few hidden areas to explore and discover. For the most part, the story was easy to follow for a JRPG, which added immensely to the game.

There's also a level of difficulty that almost seems a bit extreme but when you finally take down that boss, the feeling is satisfying. Once you start leveling up and gain class emblems things get slightly less difficult.

Overall, really loved this game - well worth the money.
Highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is a near perfect JRPG and without a doubt worth playing. The core combat and systems design is pretty much flawless. It takes aspects and quality of life features that I like about various other JRPGs and combines them so well with each other. Along with an overall enjoyable story, clean UI, and excellent OST, there aren't many negatives I can say about this game besides a personal dislike of certain plot points and character dialogue. Let me explain why the game is 'near-perfect' in more detail.

[Systems Design]
Here is a list of JRPG gameplay elements that exist in other games, that have been combined into Chained Echoes:
- Shared character exp (skill points, since this game has no character levels)
- Active and passive skills for each character that can be mixed and matched
- A job/class system, like in Bravely Default, which can be applied to any character
- Armour upgrades and gem/ability enhancements
- Fast travel and other forms of traversal (mechs/airship)
- Dialogue marker to indicate NPCs you have not talked to

On top of those, the game offers a semi-open world with material gathering, secrets, world bosses, and side quests.

[Gameplay & Core Combat Mechanic]
The unique combat system: 'Overdrive', blew me away by how well it works. There is a bar at the top left with a marker on it. During combat your goal is to try and keep your party within the green-zone by using different ability types. This mechanic introduces combat variety and will force you to plan out your moves and swap out characters to your benefit. Since your characters do not level up, and SP gain is shared, you won't feel bad about swapping to a weaker, under-leveled/geared character like so many other JRPGs.

One potential downside of shared character exp is that the game can be rather challenging since you can't over-level your party. Most of the boss fights are tuned rather well in my opinion, but bad luck on the Overdrive system may put you in the red zone, and recovery is often more difficult than easy. I do want to mention that the developer has done an awesome job on boss design and variety. There are so many bosses, none of them are re-used. Each one is visually interesting and engaging to fight in combat.

[Story/Plot]
As with most JRPGs the game follows a linear main storyline with cutscenes. I enjoyed the overall story, but there are some plot points and character dialogue that does break immersion for me. Clearly the game is inspired by other past JRPGs, but I can't help but feel like some aspects are a bit too similar. Additionally some of the characters just don't quite hit the mark for me. I imagine it is rather difficult to nail every character with such a large cast (8 active party members + unused party members + non-playable characters). There are also quite a few twists and turns in the story that feel forced or unnecessary. But enough of my hyper-criticism, plot points can be rather subjective and it is immensely impressive that all of this is done by 1 guy! Story is better than most JRPGs anyways.

[Minor Annoyances]
1. Weird how I cant fast travel to local map teleport crystals from the initial opening of the local map. Instead the game forces me to open my local map > exit out to world map > find the zone I am currently in (which brings me back to the local map) > then allows me to select a teleport point.
2. Party formation is often scrambled after some events (cutscene, when character leaves party, boss fights). Unsure if intended, bug, or just a something that was not implemented.\t\t\t\t\t
