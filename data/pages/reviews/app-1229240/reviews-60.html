\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't play many JRPGs but Chained Echoes is truly one of the greats and I think will always be in my top 10 JRPG games. Story is absolutely phenomenal and I DIDN'T EXPECT IT TO BE THIS GOOD. The characters are great and unique, they're not as black and white as many other games, they each have their own flaws but each go through their own character developments so that by the end of the game, I love almost all of them. The combat is fun. I like the Overdrive System as it adds another spice to the combat and strategy. Another thing I like is the fact that HP and TP gets restored after every battle so every time I get into another battle I don't have to worry about my HP nor TP because it's full. Also, I love how we can escape from combat anytime we want whenever it's our turn, and it's 100% chance with no drawbacks either. Really good in situations where I want to skip some fights or if I encounter an enemy that is currently much stronger than my team. Speaking of strong enemies, one thing I dislike about the combat is how tanky the enemies are (even small enemies). I feel like no matter how much I progress and upgrade my gear, I never really feel that much more powerful as the enemies gets tankier and tankier in new areas. So I didn't really feel the progress I've made towards my characters that much (unless I explore previous areas). And because small enemies respawn, I usually skip battles if I encounter them because it takes some time to defeat them due to how tanky they are. But other than that, the combat is pretty nice. The gameplay outside the combat mostly consists of exploration which is not bad considering your characters' movement speed is quite fast, so getting everywhere in the map is pretty quick. The map itself gets progressively bigger and introduces new unique biomes and places as the story progresses. The side quests in this game is good because you actually get rewarded with something great, so it pushes you to do them. Plus, they're entertaining so it's fun to finish them regardless of the rewards anyway. Overall, to me what really stands out about this game is the absolutely phenomenal writing, world-building, and character developments. Truly one of the best JRPG game I've ever played. Thank you Mathias! Looking forward to your next project!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a MASTERPIECE! One of the best jrpgs I have ever played. My first adventure game was Zelda: A Link To The Past, which I loved. (I played the original Legend of Zelda on NES but I didn't REALLY get involved in a game until Link to the Past.) Then I played Wild Arms for PS1 when it came out in 1997. I laughed, cried, and it became my favorite game at that time. I instantly fell in love with jrpgs. I followed this with FF7, then FF2 and FF3, Super Mario RPG, FF8, and I stepped into the vast rabbit hole of rpg adventures. Chained Echoes gives me so much nostalgia as it reminds me in ways of all the above mentioned games, as well as Chrono Trigger, Breath of Fire, and many other epic games. The character movement in Chained Echoes is swift and superb. The text is fast paced (adjustable settings) and the story is enthralling. The battle system is fun and unique. There are tons of secrets and I am still discovering and unlocking even more as I am almost finished with my quest. I have put over 35 hours into this game and I could have finished it by now, but I am savoring every moment as I work on secrets before I finish the final area. Matthias Linda is an absolute genius for developing this timeless classic. I would recommend this game to ANYONE who loves SNES, Sega Genesis, PS1, PS2 or PSP jrpgs, or anyone who loves rpgs in general. It took me a few hours of gameplay to get used to crystal and crafting systems, but now I love all the efforts Matthias took to make this game so amazing but not too complex. After I finish it, I plan to start a new game and do it all over again. I have not been this excited about a new game in years, and I am so happy with my decision to give this glorious work of art a chance. Thank you Matthias Linda for your attention to detail and your love that you put into this masterpiece!!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA new gold-standard for JRPGs

Any JRPG fan could agree that games like Chrono Trigger, Final Fantasy VII, Xenogears, etc., are the 'gold-standard' when it comes to making an amazing game that will be fondly remembered by the player for years to come. Many try and play off either nostalgia or homages, but very few manage to hit that perfect sweet spot of creating lightning in a bottle. Chained Echoes is absolutely the exception.

From the very beginning, it's beautifully crafted pixel art and gorgeous orchestral tunes, let you know that you're in for a unique experience. You start the story off with just a small band of heroes working to make a name for themselves, and end up taking on a sprawling story that you really have to experience to believe.

I could not put this game down, and am still in awe of it's amazing gameplay and story. If you are at all a fan of retro JRPGs, or are looking for a game that provides you hours of an encapsulating story, then you absolutely must play Chained Echoes

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been months since I beat this game and I keep thinking about it. This game is a modern classic for JRPG fans.

I genuinely think this game is near perfect, the pacing is amazing most of the game (except for one or two parts imo) no matter how extreme everything gets the game gives the party time to breathe and show how human and vulnerable they are, the stakes are presented in a fantastic way that doesn't feel like it came out of nowhere but keeps gradually escalating for each character. The combat is probably one of the best in the genre, the main mechanics (the overdrive system mainly) are so refreshing and aren't complicated and they never gets too stale and boring, the QoL features make the combat feel so light and fun. The side quests are amazing as well since they went with quality over quantity. The main story in the last third could get too ridiculous and silly to some but I enjoyed overall and was tearing up by the ending\t\t\t\t\t
