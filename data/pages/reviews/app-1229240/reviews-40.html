\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the better, maybe even one of the best JRPGs out there. Lots of vibes from classics like Chrono Trigger and Seiken Densetsu (is the goblin village's music an hommage to Seiken Densetsu 2 dwarf village's music?) but with a real modern touch.
Gameplay experience feels fresh, exploring is worth it and backtracking in the late game is fun. There are a billion improvements compared to other games of that genre that make playing just a lot more comfortable.

I don't like playing with huge groups (8 characters per battle) but the combat system still feels interesting and you'll need some tactical ideas on how to fight certain enemies.

Nothing to complain except the story writing which sometimes feels overcomplicated and even a bit cringe. Especially the German translation (which is supposed to be the original one, right?) has many mistakes and unfitting words in it. But who cares if the gameplay is as great as it is.

9/10, fantastic game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChained Echoes is an ode to the golden age of JRPGs, with many similarities and vibes reminiscent of the titles that shaped the history of the Super Nintendo, especially Final Fantasy 6 and Chrono Trigger. It's not just a "nostalgic mishmash" (because it truly has it all, as you can tell from the early images of the game, from flying ships to mechs to classic sword and cape duels), as it also brings interesting innovations even for genre veterans, especially in the combat system.

The effort to include all the classic JRPG customization features required meticulous work, which perhaps left some elements behind (the weapon and armor upgrade system doesn't offer significant depth) but it's pretty enjoyable anyway. I recommend the game to anyone, as it's undoubtedly enjoyable even for those who have never laid hands on the plasticky SNES joystick. For those familiar with JRPGs, it's a must-buy. The audio department functions well and also provides some gems: one of the first exploration themes begins with a high and sustained note that strongly recalls a track that accompanied the first explorers of the Narshe mines in FF6 nearly 30 years ago.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is absolutely impressive for something made by one person but that does not mean the game itself is good.

I am about 10 hours in and any time I put the game down the only reason I pick it back up is because of the praise it gets and hoping I am close to it getting better. Nothing inherent to the game makes me want to continue playing

The writing rides the line between overly cliche and overly complicated. There keeps being long bouts of exposition about the powers at play but I am not given enough reason to care about them to then also care about their relations. The characters are all boring with Sienna being the only good one so far but it may be the result of the rest being dull or unlikeable. There are times that the writing borders on parody but the context leads me to believe that is unintentional.

The combat system feels unpolished or not well thought out. Having encounters be so fast and everyone recover afterwards makes healing almost always pointless. Also since you always get a full heal after every battle feels the same since there is no reason to not just go all out every time. You may as well ignore the pointless overdrive meter most of the time too since it going up and down is random. I thought at first that certain skills always pushed it in either direction but no its just whatever the game is feeling that round. What's more annoying is when you are in a section where the only magic moves you could have are wind and earth type, but the enemies in the are are resistant to wind and weak to fire. It makes it feel like they forgot to play test that part. Lastly, there is no traditional levelling in this game. You only "level up" from boss fights and random encounters only give you a pittance of SP (to level skills) so again, why bother with the random encounters?

More of a nitpick, the battles starting and ending so quickly makes them feel like something from a cheap mobile game. Like it would be more fun and conclusive to have a brief victory pose at the end but instead its just immediately back to walking around with a small popup of the battle spoils.

The "overworld" map is just bad. Getting around this world feels wholly unnatural, like the only way between any two towns is filled with holes and small rope bridges, cliffs that need climbing, no roads. It just feels tedious and reminds you that you are in a video game world.

The music is pretty impressive but sometimes feels like a bunch of tracks were made first then placed in the game. The music that plays in the sewers and some dungeony areas is very majestic and sounds like what you might hear on some epic mountain climb to a big boss, but instead its a couple hours into the game and you are wallowing in sewer filth.

Lastly is all of the system. It feels like every time you blink there is another system this game introduces. Multiple types of crafting, gem collecting, gem merging, character swapping, overdrive meter, ultra move meter, treasure hunting, mine cart switching. It is overly complicated in a game that seems to be designed to harken back to simpler SNES style games.

This games only drive seems to be to feed off of SNES era nostalgia, but without really doing enough to have its own memorable character. At that point you might as well just play the SNES classics with how easy it is to do that these days. It's certainly not a "bad" game but I struggle to find any way that its the "must play" that so many people keep proclaiming in to be. It is on the lower end of "okay", pick it up on sale and play if you don't have a better game to be playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a great game. The story of this game and the richness of its worldbuilding had me hooked from the beginning. I see many people praising this game's gameplay and combat, which don't get me wrong; are absolutely fantastic. But I really believe this game's strong suit is its amazing story. It puts such a fresh spin on the fantasy genre as a whole and I can't praise Matthias Linda's work on his characters enough. And with an ending that puts an excellent ribbon on things and makes me eagerly hope for more work from this creator.
Compared to other rpgs I've played, it's clear the team behind this one knows their work and knows how to create great new mechanics that make things feel fresh while also not overloading your head. There is definitely an emphasis on quality of life and fast yet strategic combat. Which makes me believe this is definitely a great one for any rpg veterans out there that are looking for something fresh to sink their teeth into.
If you are fans of pixel-art rpgs, especially the classics; I can't recommend this game enough.\t\t\t\t\t
