\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have started to play CoD games only for the Single Player, so this review will only be about that.

This is by far the worst single player CoD game I have ever played, but I have only played all of the CoD games before this one Cod4, MW2, MW3, World at War, and black ops.

Why it is bad:
1. Too easy. On Veteran, I can run around and completely wreck the game with ease, unlike the MW games.
2. Story makes no sense. The WaW story was great, ever since then, it has made no sense. They took Reznov, an amazing character, and made him into a crazy guy's hallucination.
3. Cheesy Gameplay: First mission, running and shooting people in an open field while they run in an open field and shoot at you and miss. I felt like I was playing Star Wars against storm troopers, they looked the same and always missed on veteran. Not to mention the horseback riding vs tanks and helicopters later on and more.
4. Too many unskippable cutscenes. The story sucks, but I want to complete the game, I don't like being forced to watch cut scenes. Many of them are long. Starting some missions requires you to do a lot of waiting before the mission really starts. This would be okay if the story was very engaging, but makes the game far less replayable even if it was.
5. Too many cut scenes that randomly make you participate. You never know when it will happen, sudden you gotta tap F and if you don't you die and have to watch the entire scene again and it sucks. Really sucks if the scene is long.
6. More focus on visuals than gameplay. Every AAA game guilty here nowadays.
7. Futurist gameplay that makes no sense. You can see through walls with some weapons, enemies go invisible. Maybe some like this, I think it is super silly. Technology like this will never exist. I'm okay with future combat, but it has to make sense.

Also the cost, $60 for a game in 2012? A game far inferior to modern $60 or even $20-30 games. I assume the only people that play this are people that grew up playing this one. I grew up during COD4 and even before that, before this franchise was popular. I got this game on sale, I suggest you do the same.

Only buy this if you wanna do multiplayer or the zombies. Just stick to Infinity Ward Single Player, all of their single player modes are +++.

EDIT: Finished the campaign, it gets better toward the end, but the first half was just bad.\t\t\t\t\t

It is the ninth installment in the Call of Duty series and was released in 2012. Black Ops II takes place in the near future and features both a single-player campaign and multiplayer modes.

The single-player campaign is set in two different time periods: the 1980s and the year 2025. Players take on the role of Alex Mason and his son, David, as they fight against a new enemy, Raul Menendez. The story is full of twists and turns and is one of the most engaging and well-written campaigns in the Call of Duty series.

One of the standout features of Black Ops II is the Zombies mode, which returns from previous Call of Duty games. The mode has been expanded upon and includes several maps, each with its own unique challenges and Easter eggs. It is a fun and challenging mode that can be played solo or with friends.

The graphics and sound design in Black Ops II are top-notch, with impressive visuals and immersive sound effects that really draw you into the game. The game runs smoothly and is optimized well for PC, with plenty of options to tweak the graphics settings to suit your system.

Overall, Call of Duty: Black Ops II is an excellent addition to the series and one of the best Call of Duty games to date. The single-player campaign is engaging and well-written, the multiplayer is addictive and highly replayable, and the Zombies mode is a fun and challenging addition. If you're a fan of first-person shooters, this game is definitely worth checking out.
