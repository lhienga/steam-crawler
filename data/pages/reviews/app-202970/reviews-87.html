\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(This is just a review of the campaign itself and not Zombies or Multiplayer)

Call of Duty: Black Ops 2's campaign is the definition of a good time. It has (for a COD game) intriguing characters, a unique story, and the series' best villain in Raul Menendez, who tops both Makarov of the Modern Warfare games and Johnathan Irons of Advanced Warfare. Mason and the gang from Black Ops proper are back, and this time you get to see their actions in the closing days of the Cold War as well as that of Mason's son, David, in the present. What is highly unique (though not a first) for this campaign is the jumping between past and present for missions, letting you see Mason and Woods' actions having dire consequences for David and the world at large in the present. Part of the reason that makes the Black Ops campaigns so great primarily comes down to its characters. There is a reason that Treyarch brought all of these guys back for Black Ops: Cold War, and that is because they well written, acted and believable in their actions and dialogue. However, of course the real star of the show is the villain himself, Menendez. Menendez is a showstopper and the GOAT of all COD villains; he is absolutely ruthless while at the same time being a charismatic and sympathetic force of nature throughout the story. You get to see and experience the actions and consequences the protagonists have on his life, and his motivations are very clear and precise within an hour of the story starting. Also a first for COD, this campaign features multiple endings depending on specific, select choices, decisions and mission objectives that you may or may not complete. While these choices are rather minimal and do not effect the mission structure or overall outcome, they are a welcome addition and I have to give it to Treyarch for trying something new while at the same time adding a good amount of replay value to this game. Also a new addition are the Strike Force missions, which consist of a pseudo-RTS style of 5 missions that can be completed or failed dependant entirely on you. These missions run on a "lives system" and are the only real part of the campaign that I feel are out of place and detract from the moment to moment story. While they are entirely optional to beat, if you do want the best "good" ending/outcome, you will need to successfully beat all 5.

Treyarch definitely nailed it on the head with this one. BO2's campaign is a bombastic action/sci fi story with great gunplay, setpieces, cinematics, great characters and an even greater villain. It really was an awesome time to play this all the way through and it definitely should not be missed for fans, especially those of Black Ops 1.\t\t\t\t\t
