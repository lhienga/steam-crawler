\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCall of Duty: Black Ops II is a game that stands out as an exceptional sequel, building upon the foundation set by its predecessor and delivering a memorable experience. The campaign, in particular, is a shining gem within the franchise, leaving players captivated from start to finish.

One of the most remarkable aspects of the campaign is its brevity. While some might consider a shorter campaign a disadvantage, Black Ops II manages to achieve an incredible level of quality in its condensed timeframe. The storytelling is tight, focused, and filled with memorable moments that keep you engaged throughout the entire journey.

The introduction of choices within the campaign is a notable departure from the linear narratives of previous Call of Duty games. These choices allow players to influence the outcome of the story, adding a layer of replayability and immersion that hadn't been seen before in the series. It's a bold move that pays off, as the decisions you make genuinely impact the narrative, creating a sense of agency and investment in the characters and their fates.

What truly sets Black Ops II apart is its inclusion of RTS-like missions. These sections of the campaign inject a fresh dynamic into the gameplay, allowing players to take a step back from the boots-on-the-ground action and adopt a strategic approach. It's a clever addition that diversifies the gameplay experience and showcases the game's versatility.

Moreover, the campaign's set pieces are nothing short of spectacular. From intense firefight sequences to high-octane vehicle chases, the game constantly throws you into adrenaline-pumping scenarios that keep you on the edge of your seat. The level of detail in these set pieces is astonishing, and they seamlessly blend with the narrative, creating an immersive cinematic experience.

Another commendable aspect of Black Ops II's campaign is its ability to remain grounded while delivering an entertaining and thrilling story. It strikes a delicate balance between the serious, mature themes it tackles and the sheer fun of playing a Call of Duty game. The characters are well-developed, and the voice acting is top-notch, further enhancing the emotional depth and investment in the story.

Moving on to the Zombies mode, while it is undoubtedly enjoyable, it falls slightly short in comparison to previous iterations. The absence of maps that match the quality of fan favorites like "Five" and "Kino der Toten" is disappointing. However, it's important to note that the Zombies mode in Black Ops II still offers a plethora of engaging challenges and Easter eggs to discover. It may not live up to the highest standards set by its predecessors, but it's still a worthy addition to the game.

As for the multiplayer, while it may not cater to everyone's tastes, it undeniably provides an enjoyable experience. The fast-paced action, wealth of customization options, and the competitive nature of the multiplayer make it difficult to put the controller down. Whether you prefer team-based objective modes or intense one-on-one battles, Black Ops II's multiplayer has something for everyone.

In conclusion, Call of Duty: Black Ops II is a masterpiece that exemplifies the potential of the franchise. The campaign's brevity is a strength rather than a weakness, delivering a tightly woven narrative that captivates players throughout. The inclusion of choices and RTS-like missions adds depth and replayability, while the campaign's set pieces and grounded tone create an unforgettable experience. While the Zombies mode may not match up to its predecessors, it still offers plenty of enjoyment. And although the multiplayer might not cater to all tastes, it undeniably provides a fun and addictive multiplayer experience. Black Ops II deserves the highest praise for its exceptional campaign and remains a standout entry in the Call of Duty series. A solid 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
