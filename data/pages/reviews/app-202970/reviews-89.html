\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLook, I know this game's 11 years old today, but I never got around to playing it when it was new and expensive, thank goodness. I've just finished it, and I ... what on earth did I just play?

It's a story that's absurdly silly and poorly written, with pretty bog-standard COD gameplay in about half the missions. The other half are a mixed bag of experiments, some of which worked, some of which felt incredibly clunky. The story and the characters are the big problem throughout; nothing makes sense, and after a while, you can tell that the developers just quit caring whether it did.

But the end's shocking, and not in a good way. At the end they decided to make a music video where all the game's characters show up, breaking the fourth wall, and it's shocking because the people producing obviously thought everybody taking it seriously were cretins.

I thought it was totally amusing that Klobuchar became Madame President in their universe, and David "betray-us" is still seen as a good guy in 2012, at least by liberals working in AAA game-land; all of that political humor aged like fine milk.

The game's anti-hero / villain, on the other hand, was the kind of character that was realized much more fully in Far Cry 5, where they really took the "influencer" concept and made it work for the plot, rather than just be some minor part of the super-genius villain trope. Like, IDK what this game was going for, but I'm pretty sure it was meant to be Dark Comedy, but the joke's on the 15-year-old kid who's heard that Call of Duty stuff is Fun and has plunked down $60.\t\t\t\t\t
