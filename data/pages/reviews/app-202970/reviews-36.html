\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCall of Duty: Black Ops 2 is basically a sequel to Black Ops 1 (obviously for players who played before), with basically the familiar twist we all known for Black Ops, time-travel story telling. Except this time with a futuristic setting in the play as well.

Gameplay wise, Black Ops 2 sure has something new besides shooting and saving the world, which is the branching story narrative, where throughout the campaign, you will have to choose certain choices to proceed the story further, and of course, there is multiple endings which for this review I won't be covering it.

And of course, other than branching narrative, players are able to customize their weapon loadout, attachment, utilities and perks before entering a campaign mission. This definitely encourage versatile ways of playing the game rather than using missions scripted weapons and routes.

And surprise surprise, they did include a controller option for controls instead of keyboard & mouse which is hella needed instead of binding keys for each action from controllers manually and without aim assist (older Call of Duty games like CoD4: MW, CoD: WaW for example).


For the flaw part of the game, very obviously a lot of controls for keyboard & mouse are extremely janky due to made with console (xbox/playstation) in mind. This is very obvious on certain cutscenes and gameplay where your character look controls are like "tank controls" which is very tedious to play. But on certain non-combat gameplay, character movements will bugged out on keyboard & mouse, causing character to move extremely slow for no reason which is not the case for playing on controller.

Other thing that is plaguing the fun storytelling of the game is the strike force (or side-missions) missions. Although you can skip it and play the main mission story until the end, but in order to get a different story ending, the side-mission is a must to do thing. Don't get me wrong, Call of Duty having side-mission is indeed an interesting approach from their regular story telling way, and they did done something interesting, which is having a "base-defense-like-real-time-strategy-gameplay". But the problem is, the controls are all over the place, and it's worst on keyboard. Like trying to control a unit, you have to press a number key that is right-side of your keyboard, which you have to lift-off your hand which temporary loses control in order to done something crucial. (I know I can change the keybind, but like I said, the controls are all over the place, this includes every single action has its own keybind, and no 3 actions are tied into a keybind during top-down view control)

Besides the controls, the entire gameplay of side-missions are tedious at best. The AI in this game are horribly dated, its like having a reskin version of Cod4: MW AI trying to fight you again after 5 freaking years with more bullet sponges while your teammate getting ransacked by enemy like sitting ducks.

Overall, Call of Duty: Black Ops 2 isn't bad, it's just being plagued by its old tech and some rushed work that's been otherwise a fun game and awesome storytelling.

P.s. Can't deny I love when they somehow include a funny scene where all the game characters are goofing around like friends during game ending scene.\t\t\t\t\t
