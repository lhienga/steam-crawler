\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never played this COD until now and I have to say, this is probably the best one in the series, I have ever played so far.

The campaign is fantastic. It continues the previous Black Ops game's story universe, but it's more about the near future. Because of this we got some sci-fi modern warfare equipment, which are pretty fun in my opinion. The story was really good, it gives more history to most of the characters, has some twists too, but the villain's character is probably Treyarch's best work in my opinion. Interestingly, the developers added the option to choose the loadout for every mission, which is a fine choice. I have to mention the strike force missions, in which you have to either protect or take out certain point(s) on the level with multiple units, which you can also control and give orders. It was a bit difficult to get into and the friendly AI is not really useful to you most of the times, but these missions were also a nice additions and add a lot to the campaign. Probably the most weirdest thing from me in this game, it's the multiple endings you can get in the campaign, based on certain events and on your performance. There are eight different endings, which is surprising to see in a COD game, but makes the whole thing even better. I really liked the soundtrack of this game. Also, the graphics looks pretty good, especially for a 2012 game. Zombie mode is still fun, too.

If I want to be honest, there were just a few thing which was a bit annoying in this game. Sometimes enemies walked through certain objects and most enemies can target you through walls, but these only happened 1 or 2 times throughout my playtime. However, what I found annoying is the multiplayer. I can understand the low player number and the close to no activity for a 11 years old game, however there is an exploit which hackers can use to control your PC, if you play with them in a multiplayer match. This issue exist in most of the CODs before Black Ops 3. Because of this reason, I tried to play with a launcher / server browser named Plutonium, which basically cut this problem and there are more players here, than the official mode. Truth to be told, there are indeed more players, but the hacker issue is still a thing and there is also some kind of lag in this matches, which didn't helped in my multiplayer adventure, not even mentioning the constant wall bagging and spawn killing on these servers, which are only plays TDM.

Getting back to the beginning of this review, I still do think this is so far one of the best COD ever made, if I only review the single player part of the game, it's basically perfect, however the game's multiplayer is a slumber, but maybe you can get some fun out of it with the Plutonium laucher, but it didn't worked out for me. Either way, I can definitely recommend this game for the campaign at least, but 50€ is a lot for it, get it on 20€. Also, if you want to try the multiplayer, do not buy the DLCs (they are expensive already, too), because Plutonium got you covered. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think the Black ops series sucks compared to the modern warfare. Lazy writing and the story really drags this game down. Black Ops 1 had a way better story than this game. Also the amount of exploits you can do is ridiculous. The only reason I would recommend this game is it is highly entertaining and does it very well.

-Campaign/Story: It is bad. Continuation of Black ops 1 which was not that great to start with, strings along several characters and tries to fit new narratives with the same characters. Unfortunately several characters have the "America armour" as I like to call it in which they cannot die for "Insert stupid reason here". Such a frustrating story in which I now understand why Call of Duty series gets a bad name. It is truly remarkable how silly and stupid some of the missions are as they try to make everything seem "Epic". Some of them are great missions and very enjoyable to do, others are just annoying and waste of time.

You cannot skip most cut scenes or dialogues which causes you to lose faith in this game and have to watch the same bloody cut scenes over and over again, just because you are trying to get a challenge or achievement. Rewind is probably the best feature because it allows you to rewind the story at a particular mission instead of restarting, saving you valuable time. Also the load outs is a very cool feature to really be able to pick your arsenal and not get stuck with a crappy default weapon.

-Graphics: Quite simply looks better than Black Ops 1. Shocking right? Decently good and really amps it up when it has to, especially the hellish parts of war. I think the futuristic part of the game looks terrible, specially the planes and other vehicles. Some of the levels are magnificently rendered and others poorly done like any jungle mission.

-Bugs: There are actually too many and frustrating. One is enemies gaining 2x speed in which they will run at you and kill you in one melee strike. Another is Rocket Launcher enemies can shoot through walls. Challenges not unlocking even though you are 100% sure it is completed. I had 6 challenges which did not unlock and I could not get the achievement for them, spending hours repeating missions trying to get them. If you try to skip several parts of the map you can bug the entire mission and even if you kill yourself, the checkpoint with the bug will persist rendering the entire mission null. The game almost in its entirely works perfectly but it's the small important details that really causes a lot of frustration.

-Game play: It is bloody good, easy cheap fun to be had. Killing enemies with ease even on veteran and adds different mechanical enemies which have weaknesses to abuse. Overall a very dynamic and fluctuating game play. The only fault it has is the AI which is awful. I hate that every single enemy will try to melee you if you get into 2-3 meter distance. It prefers to melee me instead of maybe spray and pray towards my direction with a fully loaded gun. Another issue is the enemy will just run in front of your fire to run into the most inconvenient and worst spots for him to hide. Basically, they love running into your fire and running in the open space like there are not any bullets flying around trying to hit him.

My biggest gripe about this game is the ability to skip basically everything, causing enemies to disappear and reappear to a different checkpoint. I abused this so much to the point all I was doing is running to cover and sprinting to the next checkpoint. This meant the devaluation of actually fighting and engaging the enemy. Basically, it is safer to run away instead of fighting. The amount of popping in enemies/allies and forced cut scenes made the game into a very tiresome experience and elongated for no reason.

Regenerative health is also a major problem in this series. I would prefer a more realistic game in which I have health points and it does not regenerate throughout the game. Alas, regenerative health points is here to stay and make a game that is really good to be honest, into a quite trivial game in which you can be very reckless without real penalties.

Excellent amount of weapons, skins and upgrades. So many different options to pick from is exactly what you need to give a player in an FPS. It is definitely a joy to try out new load outs and picking different perks. Also, a perk that allows you to access tools to help you in your mission is a really cool mechanic. Sucks that most of the cool stuff you can access are locked into a single mission, for example cloaking shield.

-Voice Acting: It is pretty good. You can tell which actors are playing each character. This is probably the best at the time and it really shows the quality. Even though I did not like the story, Some of the characters had enough charm for me to be rooting for them.\t\t\t\t\t
