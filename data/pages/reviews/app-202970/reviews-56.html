\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey say that nothing beats the original, but that couldn't be more wrong in this situation. Skylanders: Swap Force is the third installment in the Skylanders franchise. The main gimmick of this game is the unique ability to mix and match the upper and lower halves of the new additions to the skylanders roster. This absolutely phenomenal idea gives the game almost infinite replayability, as all the combinations you can make with the skylanders open up new and interesting ways to go about the levels. If not for the fact that you have to buy most of the skylanders separately, I would say the swap mechanic is perfect. This is ,unfortunately, a glaring downside. There are areas in the game where you need a certain element to progress, and not owning all of the characters might force you to use one that you might not enjoy playing as. If the swapping mechanic interests you, and you are willing to spend a pretty penny, I 100% recommend trying this game out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was awesome. There were interesting characters, who were developed and fleshed out throughout the story. There were even returning characters from BO1 that had even more development. Each mission was something new and exciting, and would make you feel totally bad ass. The story was amazing aswell. The antagonist was more than just some terrorist, it was a guy taking revenge on the people who killed his sister, who was the only person he had left. The plot twists and betrayals were done perfectly, and would just leave me in a state of shock. The story took risks. It is the one of the only COD campaigns with a non linear campaign, and it was excecuted perfectly. It didn't have too many times where you could make your own decisions, but when you could, they always were really important and not just trying to show off that the game is non linear. The soundtrack was awesome as well and would kick in at the perfect times, making the game even cooler. There are only 2 issues I have with this game. One is that it can be fairly buggy at times, because one time I was near the end of the mission and I got softlocked, forcing me to replay the mission (it was a really awesome mission anyways so I didn't really care). And the other is the strike team missions. These missions were optional and were an attempt to blend a first person shooter with a strategy game. I think that this is an awesome concept that was way ahead of its time. That was the problem though, the ai sucks. They never would seem to actually respond to the commands, and when they did it was always slow as hell. Again I think they should definitely try this concept again, just with some better ai. Still have no clue what that Avenged Sevenfold thing was at the end tho.\t\t\t\t\t
