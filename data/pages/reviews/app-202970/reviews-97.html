\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSplit between the 1980s and the year 2025, the game puts you in the shoes of returning CIA operative Alex Mason, as well as his son David, a member of the Navy SEALs. Alex is controlled in the 1980s, near the end of the Cold War, as he, along with his buddies, try to find out information about a gun runner/cartel leader from Nicaragua named Raul Menendez, a man who has grown up into despising America because of him witnessing the atrocities done by US-backed forces in Nicaragua in his youth. Meanwhile, in 2025, David is trying to gain information on what it is that Menendez, and a group he leads known as Cordis Die (pretty much an evolution of WikiLeaks, Anonymous, and the Occupy movement), is planning. As the game goes on and the plot thickens, the clock ticks as the US tries to stop Menendez from enacting his plan, along with (optionally, by the player) also trying to stop a rogue Chinese general from spreading the Strategic Defense Coalition across more countries, culminating in a conquest of Russia.

As a CoD game, this one game is especially solid. As always, voice acting, guns, gameplay, and the soundtrack are amazing. Contrary to what would you expect from a Call of Duty game, let alone a military shooter, there are some choices you can make across the campaign that will have an effect on the campaign's story, along with the ending. I think this mostly like adds to replay value, so you can see different outcomes to the game! There is also a sub-campaign you can during the campaign itself known as Strike Force missions, which are essentially RTS-esque missions where you control strike teams as they set out to complete objectives. Its a really cool idea on paper, and when you control an individual unit like you would another CoD protagonist, it really feels like you're in the middle of a war, but the friendly AI is kinda stupid, which can be really frustrating. It did grow on me however. Addtionally, the aforementioned story is legitimately great, aided by Raul Menendez being a rather compelling villain, with him easily joining the ranks of my favorite video game villains of all time. And of course, as is tradition with post-World at War Treyarch CoDs, there is always the Zombies mode.

Overall, I really recommend giving this a game try!\t\t\t\t\t
