\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCOD: Black Ops II was a disappointment for the most part. First, the bad:
My first impression was the graphics seemed set back about five years in development. They seemed more pixelated than BO1 and that came as a surprise as so far there have been gradual improvements as the years have gone by. Next was the controls. Certain missions require you to stand in just the right spot even if it contradicts what your allies are telling you to do (the first instance of this was on the beach as you were carrying your wounded comrade to safety - the mission marker wanted you to go to this rock but your companion is yelling at you to keep going to the boat, and if you didn't hit just the right spot the machine gunner blew you away). There were a few instances like this in the game and it just became frustrating, including trying to figure out how to choose the strike missions - I finally had to go online and look it up on a forum to discover the very un-intuitive scroll the current mission selection to the side to uncover it. My third complaint is with the story - without giving away spoilers for those who haven't played it yet it jumps everything way ahead and leaves huge gaps in the timeline, making this not a great continuation of BO1. I was looking forward to a smooth transition from the end of BO1 but the timeline and the character development just didn't do it for me.
Now for the good: I really liked the choices and outcomes that change the story as you go. This makes this game very replayable despite its flaws, as how well you do and the specific choices you make at certain junctures change the whole scenario and progression. This was a very cool and engaging addition to the COD format and this was the one saving grace to this game.
In short, I recommend it just for those who really want to get the whole COD experience. Just don't expect a neat continuation from the first Black Ops and be prepared for some possible confusion with a few mission controls.\t\t\t\t\t
