\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a zombies kinda guy, and that's why I bought this game. With that in mind, I can confidently give this game a perfect score (kind of).

Bo2 Zombies features a diverse lineup of maps aimed at experienced players, newcomers, and those in between. Players wanting a challenge have Origins and Mob of the Dead whereas someone more casual might choose to play Buried or Town instead.
It is worth noting that this game contains both Die Rise and Tranzit, which are two of the most infamous zombies maps. These maps have some elements that are reliant upon luck to an extent. Because of this, they're generally regarded as being the worst maps the franchise has ever had. With that being said, both maps have the capacity to be quite fun, and I highly recommend you play them with an open mind.

The guns are another strong aspect of this game relative to its successors. There are great guns, so-so guns, and some really ♥♥♥♥ guns. This is in contrast to Bo3 zombies, where every single gun is effectively a wonder weapon when pack-a-punched several times. This variance lends itself to a more interesting early and mid game when buying guns needs to be balanced with other expenses.

The story and characters are fine, with Tranzit, Die Rise, and Buried wrapping up the Bo1 zombies storyline. Mob of the Dead is its own self-contained thing, and Origins begins the story that is continued in Bo3.

One very important thing to note regarding zombies is the small playerbase. You will probably have a difficult time finding other players to join you in public matches, especially if you want to play the less popular maps. This can be entirely remedied by playing with friends, and I highly recommend it.

As for the campaign, I personally had a lot of fun playing it. I especially liked how the story would branch depending on your decisions throughout the game, giving you one of several endings. Also the RTS (Real time strategy) strikeforce missions were an interesting deviation from the norm when it comes to shooter games.

I'm not much of a multiplayer person, but I will acknowledge that it was good back in its day. The same can't be said for now though. Lobbies often have cheaters, and the dwindling playerbase makes it so your gamemode choices are limited. Forget about playing anything other than FFA, TDM, and maybe one or two other modes. It is worth noting that playing with friends can eliminate the issues of game variety and cheaters if you choose to play custom games.

Now the elephant in the room: Pricing. For some reason (money) this game still costs $60 despite being over a decade old. With that in mind, Bo2 is NOT worth the full price. It has been going on sale for $20 as of late, and is somewhat justifiable at that price. I also recommend that you wait for a sale before purchasing DLC for this game as it is also still full price.
