\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike with the first Black Ops, I've put thousands upon thousands of hours (attained max Prestige in multiplayer, too) into this game on PS3, so the hours listed in this Steam review are not entirely accurate.

This game is the god of CoD, the absolute pinnacle of the series; no CoD game made before or after Black Ops II can come anywhere close to touching it in terms of sheer quality and quantity of content. A fantastic campaign featuring the greatest "antagonist" (can't say I really see him as one, his motivations are just too relatable and understandable) in the series' history, Raul Menendez; near-perfect multiplayer, very well-paced and balanced with an excellent progression system and amount of variety regarding strategies; a fully-stocked, multifaceted, and enriching Zombies experience, that could not possibly be more fun with a skilled group of friends to play with (and play we did, I must have spent a couple thousand hours with my buddies on Zombies alone)...yeah, this was the greatest Call of Duty game ever released, bar none.

If only I could go back in time and experience this game for the first time again, back when life was simple and clear-cut, I'd do it happily. I'll always remember coming home from school between (roughly) 2012 and 2015, and jumping right on Black Ops II ASAP, making memories for hours at a time.\t\t\t\t\t
