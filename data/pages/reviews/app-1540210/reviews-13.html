\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA pretty good game that feels more like Arizona Sunshine v2.0 than Arizona Sunshine 2. Aside from the new doggo friend, it's a lot more of the same which isn't really a bad thing since AS1 was pretty great but it doesn't elevate it much over the original, either.

Pros:

- Improved visuals and larger variety of assets.
- Doggo friend. (Yes, you can pet the dog. Shake hands and play fetch, too.)
- New zombie types. Nothing super special, though. One is doggo-resistant and the other is doggo-required so they don't spice up the combat all that much.

Cons:

- The new gun holstering is annoying af. In AS1 you had two holsters and your two hands. You would only drop a gun if you had all 4 slots filled up and the old gun would drop straight to the ground when you picked up a new one. Now you have two hands/holsters and that's it - one gun per hand/holster. You also have a back slot for large weapons but that's irrelevant right now. The problem is, if you pick up a third handgun when you already have two, the one in your holster can ping off in a ridiculous direction so you end up losing it. Sometimes you only pick up a gun so you can eject the magazine, collect the ammo and then go back to your original or sometimes your doggo walks by at the wrong time and you accidentally grab a gun off of him. Myself and my friend both lost weapons this way during our playthrough, including one that went clear over a wall and left player space. If they just dropped straight to the ground, it would be much less of a problem.
- Melee weapon durability is crap. If you have a co-op partner, though, melee durability is client-side so you can swap melee weapons back and forth with your partner to keep them at full durability.

Neutral:

- The campaign length is ok at about 8 hours but it could have gone longer and the ending didn't need to be so abrupt. 10 hours would have been better.
- The arcade-style reloading from AS1 that favored dual wielding is gone. Now you have simulatory reloading mechanics which aren't friendly to dual wielding.\t\t\t\t\t

This game is simply amazing!
Neither genre nor gameplay (Zombie shooter FPS) are unique but the protagonists frequent quibs, along with great graphics, level design and an engaging story make this game my favorite VR game of the year 2023.

Even though it is a zombie game, it isn't overly scary. The pet 'Buddy' is a great and very valuable addition. The campaign took me approximately 9 hours to complete and because the arsenal of weaponry is quite broad, I am certainly going back to play through the game again.

I did not try out the Horde mode or playing Coop but I am looking forward to it.

Downsides
The amount of weapons you can carry is limited. So even though you might find a unique weapon you want to give a try, you often have to leave it in favor of a more practical weapon you already carry with you. Giving Buddy the possibility to carry at least one rifle instead of two pistols would eliminate that issue.

In the heat of the battle, the controls can be a little janky. Instead of reaching for my rifle, I accidentally just removed my hat. Or accidentally stashed my holstered weapon on Buddy instead of me.\t\t\t\t\t
