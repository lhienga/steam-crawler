\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall:
It misses the mark when it comes to living up to it's predecessor. The game is severely lacking in features and function which makes It play like a 2016 VR game. The first Arizona Sunshine was a pretty fun game but had issues in terms of function and user control. That was forgivable at the time because it was 2016 and a fairly early title in terms of VR development. Arizona Sunshine 2 suffers from all the same issue even though it was released 7 years later. In fact, I would say that the 1st game was more fun due to it having a much more fleshed out Hord Mode. Arizona Sunshine has a campaign that's only about 6 hours long and a single hord map that limits your movement to a 12'x6' square. adding new weapons and a NPC companion that is severely under utilized is hardly what I expected for a sequel. You can almost always tell when a hord of zombies is going to show up in the campaign because the game will force you into a tight space beforehand and it happens multiple times a level. At one point the game is trying to portray a sense of urgency and sort of implies to the player that they need to hurry. This is done while the player is pushing a cart, so engaging the zombies seems like a bad idea. However, it blocks your path at one point and you then have to deal with the zombies chasing you as well as dealing with a hord that shows up. This manages to neuter any tension that was being built up to that point and just makes the event a slog to get through. One of the new enemy types is the fat zombie. It acts like a tanker version of the normal zombie. This is a bit odd because the fungus zombie from the first game fills the same role, and also makes a return. The new weapons are fun but the the more advanced ones are removed from the player at one point and the vast majority never show up in the last few levels. I do not know why this was done except maybe to artificially increase the tension but it's just irritating. Especially because the player looses their weapons twice in the campaign. The storyline is a bit anemic but that could just be because of the short runtime. The one major highlight is the protagonist. The game nails how an individual would loose their mind a bit during a zombie Apocalypse. The new dog companion is a good addition but is under utilized. It's primary function is to drive the plot forward. If you got this far and are still on the fence I would recommend waiting until it goes on sale.
TLDR
The Good: It's fun for a few hours with an interesting protagonist
The Bad: Repetitive game play that dose little to challenge the player.
The Ugly: It is a short game with dated mechanics and few new additions that makes it a disappointing sequel.\t\t\t\t\t
