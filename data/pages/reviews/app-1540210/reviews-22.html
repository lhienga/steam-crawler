\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, let’s get started. Beginning with the last two months of marketing, I watched the trailer multiple times, which I usually don’t do for any game except those I have almost zero interest in. And boy, it was the case with AS2… I was convinced that it was a clear cash grab based on the name of the first, like Walking Dead 2. And the marketing was very distasteful, like Ghostbusters (that one was another cheap cash grab).

Let’s say the “we added a dog” gimmick was just a perfect punching bag as a future bad game. The trailer made me focus on the fact that the UI setup hadn’t changed in about 6 years. Ironically, the only thing that almost sold me was the damn dog, but I have a big German Shepherd myself, so I am completely biased about it.

My plan was to not even try it for a fast refund. I was ready to wait a couple of hours to read all the 1-star funny reviews like the Ghostbusters game. But the reviews started getting 5 stars non-stop for the first 6 hours— weird, maybe a marketing thing, I don’t know… but I got curious and spoiled myself the first 20 minutes on YouTube. And damn, it didn’t look that good, but everyone playing it seems to have a good time.

So yeah, I bought it. I know you didn’t see that one coming.

But more seriously, I had to explain my journey so you understand the weight of the comment I am going to make. That supposed bad game with ♥♥♥♥ jokes and a psychotic but great doggo is the closest experience I can compare to when I played Half-Life Alyx for the first time 2 years and 10,000 VR hours ago. I was impressed, alright, it’s not HLA… it’s just not, but the level of “oh alright, I like that” situations that happen one after the other, including laughing and crying (like with tears and all, not good with a headset).

I just loved my experience so far; the dog won me over so fast. The real one on the other side of the headset was getting really jealous. And the main character reminded me so much of Mel Gibson in Lethal Weapon (if you’re old like me), and it’s a winning duo for me.

The graphics on PC are just not comparable to the Quest Version. That game is so much more beautiful than it has the right to be. It’s AS2, not Assassin’s Creed VR, but still, they did the thing, you know when the devs just… give a ♥♥♥♥… Anyway, you can feel that kind of stuff.

The gunplay is fun, fast, and responsive—again, more than it has the right to be. Not perfect, but all the basics are there and work… when they work, yeah, a couple of bugs, a gun disappearing and reappearing at the start of the game made for a funny minute or two running around screaming with Freds on my ass. Also, the two-handed handgun needs to be tweaked because half the time, the second hand goes directly on the slide and not on the grip; you need to be almost under the gun with the Quest Pro controllers.

The belt system is just bad; you cannot work with your guns close to your chest like you’re going to do in real life. It’s very awkward sometimes. Also, when you pick up a new gun, the game automatically drops another one if you are full, and choosing which one you keep and which one you drop is very confusing.

The dog is the best thing in the game and in the VR world of “dogs in VR games.” He is awesome, violent, helpful, and the way your character interacts with it is just perfect. The controls are great, and he will save your life more than one time.

The melee combat is… okay, I guess, but I play on hard and it doesn’t seem to be a very viable option to stay alive. So don’t quote me on that one.

My biggest con is the way the free movement and continuous turning work together. It feels extremely janky if you consider the high level where everything else is; it seems very basic, but somehow in some way, they messed up the walking.

It’s an expensive game too, for a VR game; that’s a rare thing, but the work is there. If I get a long and fulfilling campaign of 6-8hrs+, I will be happy. But still, Vertigo 2 is an almost perfect game where I spent more than 30 hours running around at the end of my grandiose first full playthrough. And that perfect game is half the price of AS2. But if it takes a higher price point to get real games that feel complete and cared about in VR, I am here for it.

Conclusion… I like it.

That’s a good boy 🐕\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun game if you're a fan of the first and can deal with the shortcomings.

Tip before starting game:
Go to video settings ingame and set gamma to -10 or lower, it will increase game brightness.

Pros:
+ It has same tone/vibe as the first game.
+ Good voice acting

Cons:
- Not enough open world areas. Game mostly takes place underground or indoors.
- Only 1 multiplayer map for Horde mode, and it's the one from first game (Grand Canyon)
- Flashlight is weak and can't be turned on/off manually. It's on auto and worst part is sometimes it don't turn on. Again just mess with gamma brightness to alleviate this.
- You can't get close to objects. There's an invisible hit box that keeps you from getting close to anything.
- Trying to reach for ammo or items inside crates can be challenging because of this. You are pushed back when too close.
- Good luck trying to grab items on the ground. You always end up grabbing your gun from your belt that's in the way.
- The game lets you grab items from a distance by teleporting it to your hand, but doesn't always work well.
- Achievements are glitchy, some don't unlock. Light & smoke cigarette and beating it on Apocalyptic didn't unlock.
- Unskippable cutscenes. Understandable for first playthough, but on 2nd playthrough it was annoying being forced to sit through it again.
- Game cheats by spawning zombies behind you. Very annoying getting 1-hit kills on apocalypse difficulty from this.
- Bugs. Oh the bugs and glitches. Man are there plenty. Being stuck when trying to climb ladders. Zombies disappearing, getting stuck in doors so you can't open and progress, the list is extensive.
- Repeated and reused levels. What levels are the most boring in games? Me: Sewers. Devs: Lets reuse it multiple times to pad game time count.
- Let's add even more artificial game time count with backtracking. Wait. Lets make players backtrack while being force to walk at a speed even slower than real life prone crawling.
- How about we make the game feel even more cheap with jump scares, since the game isn't scary at all.
- Game feels more like a story dlc add-on then a sequel. A lot of assets are reused and graphics looks the same as original
- This feels like a game made for Quest and ported for PCVR and not the other way around.
- $50 is asking way to much for a ported mobile game.
- Can't upgrade from Standard Edition to Deluxe.
- If you accidentally bought the Standard Edition or you just change your mind you can't upgrade to Deluxe and have to refund the game then wait for it to go through before buying the Deluxe because Steam says you already own the game.
- If you bought the standard edition and have over 2 hrs playtime forget about returning. You have to buy the full Deluxe Edition if you want to upgrade later.
- Half of the playtime was from me rage quitting and leaving it running overnight.

Wait for price to drop. Worth about $20-$30.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tArizona Sunshine 2 is fantastic! I managed to avoid all the gameplay, reviews & info on the game prior to release so I could experience the game without any outside influence and a fresh mindset. Over 7 hours into the game on the hard difficulty, and it's some of the most fun I have had in VR yet. Co-op is a blast with my friends & helps the game really shine.

As gorgeous as the game looks and feels, it is so many small things that is done well that makes this game an instant classic for VR. Having the doggo companion, Buddy, as not only a friend but as a tool to be used in the game really is unlike anything else in VR. Buddy alone is worth the price of the game but being able to have him fetch items you can't reach, store weapons, attack Freds, alert you for important items/enemies, etc takes the experience to another level. There are "WOW" moments littered all throughout the game. The comedy is as good as it gets for a VR shooter. The story is very well done. The weapons actually have weight and feel like you are firing weapons.

I've had people mention to me that the game is short while I am live streaming, but I'm over 7 hours of playtime into the game and just started another chapter. not to mention Horde mode will get plenty of my time whenever I finish the campaign & I've enjoyed the campaign so much that I'll be playing it again immediately after on the apocalyptic difficulty!

While the game is not perfect and has some minor issues, it's extremely well done for a launch day VR game. There's been numerous times where a VR game has been broken on the first week of release but this game has very minor spots here and there that don't harm the gameplay.

Vertigo Games has done a wonderful job with supplying an extremely fun game and if more VR developers were able to release this quality of content for $50, then I'd be happy to give those companies and games my money. Over 7 hours into the game and I still have some Freds to hang out with before doing it all over again. Can't wait to play again!\t\t\t\t\t
