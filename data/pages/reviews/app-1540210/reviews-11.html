\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game, currently plagued by numerous bugs, leaves me uncertain about the resolution of issues that both I and other players have encountered. Despite its promising start and impressive visuals, positioning itself as possibly the best-looking VR zombie game, the deeper I ventured, the more apparent its flaws became.

One jarring aspect is the inability to grab zombies, a departure from my experiences in The Walking Dead: Saints & Sinners where such interactions were seamless. This departure broke my immersion, forcing me to resort to luring zombies around the map when facing hordes, a less-than-ideal substitute. The introduction of an attacking dog to aid in such scenarios was a welcome addition, but the canine companion's erratic behavior further compounds the game's issues.

The dog, unfortunately, feels less like a reliable ally and more like a hit-or-miss feature. It is frequently found lagging behind me, growling at zombies through walls, or inexplicably fixated on a zombie directly in front of it, even after I commanded it to attack. This inconsistency undermines the intended support the dog is supposed to provide, making it more of a liability than an asset in crucial situations.

Moreover, the necessity to shoot zombies in the head multiple times, especially on higher difficulty levels, consumes excessive ammunition, raising concerns about resource management. The fragility of melee weapons further compounds the issue, with most breaking after a mere four zombie encounters.

The checkpoint system, particularly in the airport, presents another challenge. Sparse lighting and distant checkpoints contribute to a frustrating experience, exacerbated by the need to traverse the entire scenario to collect hidden weapons and scarce ammo. A lack of additional checkpoints, especially near crafting areas, feels like a missed opportunity to enhance player experience.

Weapon handling adds to the frustration, with an unpredictable grabbing mechanism causing items to teleport or behave inconsistently. These issues collectively cast doubt on the game's current playability, prompting skepticism about the likelihood of comprehensive fixes.

Despite these concerns, my desire to explore the game's narrative and appreciation for its concept make me hesitant to seek a refund. Belt system is awful, it's very easy to lose your gun even without grabbing it, you are always unsure if the gun actually went on the belt and not on the floor.
Zombies are copy pasted endlessly without any real variety.

Melee impacts tend to stick to enemies in a very awkward and not VR friendly way.
Weapon balance is bad.
Flamethrower implementation is bad, it's random whether you actually set a zombie on fire, it looks and feels terrible.
