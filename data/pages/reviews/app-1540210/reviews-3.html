\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't in good conscience not recommend the game with the best dog physics I've ever seen in any virtual media. The dog in this game (Buddy) almost makes the game worth buying alone, and is a perfect solution to the loneliness and melancholy that many story VR games get between fighting sequences. The character interactions between the player and Buddy are hilarious, and are genuinely great at filling down time.

The game itself isn't half bad either, the guns are pretty arcade-y but not the worst I've seen, and it is incredibly satisfying to pop zombie heads and loot the various locations. Unfortunately, you'll be spending the vast majority of your time in only one of those locations: the sewers. I don't know if I was just slow at navigating them but wandering through endless stretches of look-a-like pipes and narrow bottlenecks wasn't particularly engaging, especially because of how often you return to them. All in all it feels like they heavily rushed the second half of the game; the locations start repeating, there is no new zombie types after the first few chapters, and the difficulty ramp is literally just spamming more and more zombies at you. Like I said, it's a good game, it just drops the ball towards the end.\t\t\t\t\t
