\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed the campaign. It was.. sort of okay I guess... But not €50 okay. The first few moments were promising, until your character says he's thirsty, suggesting he should drink something. After a few mins of fumbling around with bottles, I concluded that you couldn't drink from them.

There Many bugs and glitches. While some areas look good, many others feel bland and unfinished. Zombies can hit you through walls and closed doors. Standing in the elevator, and hearing it moving, only to see a crack in the door that reveals you are actually not moving. Sometimes weapons would disappear after a level transition.

The physics are a big let down. When swinging an axe for example, upon hitting the zombie, it will snap to a predefined point on it's body and chop that off. There are other things in the leven, like frying pans, but when you try and hit a zombie with it, nothing happens. Buddy sometimes picks up a zombie arm, wanting to play fetch with it. Unfortunately, you can throw the arm only a few centimeters because zombies are seemingly made of lead, so you won't even see the animation of buddy grabbing the arm, walking toward you and giving it back to you. The arm just spawns into Buddy's mouth instead.

There is almost nothing interactive in the environment, except for the countless number of bins and containers of which you can open the lids, and some badly rendered cars you can grab ammo from. And a game of beerpong at some point. That's it.

The story had a lot of potential, but to my surprise it only took me about 10 hours, including dying a lot because sometimes weapons would randomly spazz out of my hands during reloading. And come on man, give me control over my flashlight. Some areas are so dark you can't see the pickups at all, and I'm forced to use my gun as flashlight. Levels feel repetitive pretty quick, but I guess you gotta milk those sewer assets to make a buck.

The game feels like a rush job, as if management was breathing down the dev necks to get it done in a third of the time it should have taken. At times it felt like areas were taken from L4D2, with a sprinkle Dying Light, but badly executed.

On a more positive note, the weapons are good, and were fun to shoot with. Some attachements or upgrades would have been nice, like laserpointers or scopes. I like how you can change clips/magazines by putting the old one back into the inventory to prevent wasting bullets. Buddy is also cute, and I like that you can pet and play fetch with him.

Other than that, I think this review would have been a lot more positive if the game was priced €25\t\t\t\t\t
