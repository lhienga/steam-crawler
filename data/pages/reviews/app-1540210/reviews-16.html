\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:
+ It's a big improvement from Sunshine 1, the graphics and most of the functionality in the game.
+ Joining a friend on steam is much easier now, previously it was very difficult - you still need to share a code to get into a freind's lobby, which is a bit of a faff with a VR headset on.
+ Thrown explosives + Melee is a great addition, and well balanced as not to ruin the game.
+ It's a coop zombie game, always great - glad to see out!
+ Buddy.

The bad / could be better.
- Graphics, while better than AS1 it's nowhere near Alyx - which at this point is a few years old and would love to see more games managing to rival.
- The graphics are straight out of After the Fall, but in a world which is less populated - so personally I think a bit worse - and you're often outside in the daylight, so looks even worse quickly.
- Lots of ammo issues, if you're playing in coop your ammo randomly changes between load screens. Various other issues where you pick up the wrong thing etc, but they haven't annoyed me or put me off TBH.
- It's a very linear game like AS1, I don't think I'll play it again unless another friend wants it and wants somebody to play with. In terms of length of gameplay, I'm not rushing and not completed it in 4 hours, some people have said they have. Well take your time!
-Coop is only 2 player, and the death match is only 3 player.


In summary, if you already have After the fall and want a zombie game to play with friends - get it. If you don't already have After the fall and you want a coop zombie game - get that, it's far better for coop and repeatability.

I'm sure DLC will come along later, but that's not a good reason to buy it now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: Recent update has made some improvements, especially in regard to the red damage vignette. Still too aggressive and a feature which I would prefer to turn off. I find audio queues. Gunplay remains cumbersome.

My intentions were to give this game a more thoughtful review. From a general mixed opinion, I was leaning towards a thumbs up, but one feature continues to prove a tremendous detriment to my enjoyment. Colored Status Vignettes.

Red Status Vignette. When low on health, the edges of the lenses are lit up in red. In this particular instance, it is pulsing in and out towards the center of the lenses. I find this feature exceptionally discomforting, and it is becoming an industry norm. Developers. Stop. If you insist, then let players disable it. Edit: don't rant. I want to rant. The rage of a thousand nerds.

Other than blotting my eyes with a lazy status vignette that reduces my already limited FOV, Arizona Sunshine 2 feels kind of hollow. It is basically walking down a hallway, opening boxes for ammo, and shooting things. Which can be quite fun, but I am expecting more from modern VR games at that price point. It's a pretty game. There were some in-game cinematics that were quite impressive.

The graphics do look nice, and seem well optimized, though the environments are not necessarily rich with detail. The levels are basically a to b, with not much to explore but boxes, trunks, etc. The main shortcoming of the game, come down to mechanics.

Armaments. 2 side slots, 2 wrist slots, and one back slot that can be grabbed from other side. Two storage slots on the dog. Sounds like it should be enough, and it generally is. Only certain guns can be holstered at the hip, back, and dog storage. Pretty standard.

The bigger issue is weapons and belt management. If I grab a gun off the table, it automatically holsters it in its respective slot on the body. Maybe there was an option to disable this? I couldn't find one. Maybe I was having another rage attack triggered by the obnoxious red vignette. This make it so I can't inspect the weapon on the table before holstering it. The belt also gets in the way of looting things beneath it. Never could adjust it to a satisfactory range, though there is a feature to do so as in After the Fall. (add host migration already)

It gets tricky if I want to swap weapons. Not so simple as setting one down and grabbing the other. (discovered you can drop a weapon by long pressing a button). Have had to toss it up in the air towards my other hand and grab. If memory serves (vignette trauma), I can't grab a weapon on the opposite hip. May have to just pull out some yoga.... stuff.

Trying to manage my load-out with the weapons stashed on... the dog, follows the same, clumsy cycle of grab/auto-swapping. You would think swapping around the guns in the dog's holsters would be an easy task. Oh, but no. Probably the best, if not only, puzzle in the game.

The addition of melee weapons is nice, but they break pretty easy. Cheap steel, I guess. You get two storage slots, one in each wrist. A weapon can be stored in either. You also collect various objects like keys, or food. There are crafting components littered about, often in storage boxes that are a pain to open with one hand because they won't stay up. These let you... craft things.

This is all leading up to a lot of options for 2 slots. Can't pack them all, Jimmy. Through maybe 75% of the game, there remains an empty red gas canister on the left side of my belt. Would be nice to click a grenade there. Or my chest. Two. Maybe three. Maybe if I dropped a few of the 30 bottles of glue, I could make room for a slice of moldy pizza.

Can't grab ammunition off the ground and directly insert it into the weapon. Makes sense, to a degree, as multiple guns pull from the same storage. There is a flashlight attached to the chest, but it is basically a mood piece for when the developers wanted to add "tension" or whatever. Just let me use the dang flashlight, Bob. Fortunately there is a gamma slider so you aren't running around blind through half the game.

For the story, it's decent enough. Not an epic tale, but a good one doesn't require such. The protagonist comes off as a bit of a try hard with his campy humor and repeats himself too much.

What?

The dog is done well. Doesn't bark too much, which can be rather grating like the one in Blair Witch VR. Whimpers a lot. Little annoying. Maybe a light petting will shush him up. Mph.

Without the dog? $50? It's already a stretch.

Writing a crappy review can sure deflate the rage. I would like to boot the game back up, but then I remember that red vignette, and all the other otherwise decent games I recently bought with their own horrid version. Seriously. Stop.\t\t\t\t\t
