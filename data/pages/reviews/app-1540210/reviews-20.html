\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy rig: Valve Index, Asus Strix OC RTX 3090, i9 10900K (10cores/20threads), 32GB 3200Mhz, Win10 Pro.

I guess this is the year of the greatly enhanced Quest ports. Like The 7th Guest, Arizona Sunshine 2 is a greatly enhanced Quest port - but so greatly enhanced many may never notice it. The basic geometry was designed for the Quest, so you get the same amount of buildings and basic environments, but textures were greatly enhanced. You'll generally get similar textures res as in Alyx. More items and zombies were added compared to the Quest version, so the overall poly count is much higher than the Quest version - and you got many more different zombies. Lighting was greatly enhanced - so you got realtime lighting and dynamic shadows. The game is 42.2GB installed - compared to just 11.8GB for the Quest version - so great efforts were made to make the PCVR version shine.

https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3109973133
The gameplay is much the same as in Arizona Sunshine 1, so you get to shoot tons of zombies. There are a few very small bosses (or close), mostly the zombies gain strenght in numbers.

https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3106220977
You got many different weapons, like pistols, rifles, machine guns, flame throwers and more. Be careful checking every suitcase or box, or you might miss out on weapons and ammo.

https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3107214284
You got a dog named Buddy in the game, and you can command the dog to kill zombies. You can also pet the dog - and have it go fetch things you throw. And Buddy can help you out opening doors and more. You have a dog too in FallOut4, but Buddy is special - and also provides some company, making the game feel more social :-)

Grabbing when climbing does not work that great, had a few times when climbing over some chasm that suddenly I was thrown back to where I started - so do not let go until you're over all the way. Otherwise controls work perfectly - or much like in Arizona Sunshine 1.

If you played Arizona Sunshine 1, this sequel is more of the same - but I do love to shoot zombies and the game does look beautiful when it shines the most. Some say this game does not hold a candle to Resident Evil 4 VR Mode, but it does. Arizona Sunshine 2 has tons of gore, but it's still not really horror - like RE4 VR. To me Arizona Sunshine 2 is a much more relaxed game, and I'm greatly enjoying it.

For performance I'm getting close to solid 80 fps in 80 Hz with no motion smoothing, Index res 350 % (about 32 million pixel per frame) with 4xMSAA and all other graphics settings maxed. Only the PCVR version works in 90+ fps with all graphics settings enabled, the PSVR2 version is only 60 fps with reprojections, and I bet the PSVR2 version is not running with the many PCVR graphics settings maxed.

In the dark, your flashlight will turn on automatically, and you got full locomotion, snap/smooth turning, perfect support for left-handed person - and basically all you can wish for regaring the controls. Btw, Index finger tracking is fully supported.

Only SteamVR drivers are supported, and Meta users will take a significant performance hit. Meta users should instead get the PCVR version from the Meta PCVR/Rift Store to get optimal performance. The game is cross-buy in the Meta Store, so if you have a Quest hmd, you get both the Quest and PCVR version for the same price in the Meta Store.

My rating 4.6/5 - or 92%. I'd rate Vertigo 2 94% - and would be close to 96% for both Call of the Mountain and Resident Evil 4 VR Mode. And of course Alyx would be like 150% on that scale ;-)

Also do not rush though this game, take it slow and enjoy the scenery, and it will be worth much more than you paid. I've read that some completed the game in 4 hours - which is kinda like having a skilled cook spending a full day making you the most amazing dinner, and then you eat it in 5 minutes. I'd recommend not playing for more than about an hour per session - this will keep the game fresh and from being too repetitive - and increase the difficulty level to fit your skills.\t\t\t\t\t
