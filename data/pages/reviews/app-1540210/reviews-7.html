\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally great graphics, I play everything maxed out, wifi6e dedicated router, Quest 3, Virtual Desktop on Godlike Setting, 3090ti, 64GB , intel i7-12700K . So I can't say what it looks like on medium or high but it is stunning on ultra. Great voice acting, love the canine companion. You should definitely buy this while it is on sale right now. I paid full price I didn't want to wait and I'm glad I did.

The ONLY complaint I have is the controls are a little wonky. I hate how the guns constantly teleport to my hands, when I am trying to interact with stuff. It is totally not necessary. I don't know why Devs keep trying to reinvent the wheel. Play Into The radius for 10 hours and then design your control system. They have a great backpack system too but they want to restrict our carrying capacity slots, I get it. But the wrist inventory used in this game works fine in Half Like Alex because you don't craft things. If you are going to let us craft let us carry more than two items, and at the very least don't make us carry our melee weapons in our wrists. Play Walking Dead Saint in Sinners for a few hours and see how good their inventory system works. Its a backpack with a number of slots that you can define.

Also only carrying one primary is fine but its a bit frustrating when you find a new gun you want to try out and if you end up not liking it you can't go back and trade out your weapon for what you had, you simply aren't going to see it again for the entire play through. The easy fix is let us carry two primaries, I mean sometimes you need a shotgun sometimes you need a rifle let us carry both! Don't make me choose between a grenade launcher with 6 shots and a rifle with 200. I am forced to pick the rifle and won;t get to try the grenade launcher.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn spite of it's shortcomings, I enjoyed my time with the game. It's a shame that I felt like I had to fight with the game in order to have fun.

My gripes could be summed up thus: The first Arizona Sunshine came out when VR was new tech, and was one of the first great titles you could play with a screen on your face. In the time since then, the platform has come a long way, and evolved a great deal. Arizona Sunshine 2 feels like it was made with no regard for the progress of the platform.

Some specific examples: Trying to switch weapons from one holster to another is made complicated by the fact that you have to draw it with the same hand as where it's holstered. It will want to return to holster when you let go, meaning you only have a second to 'throw' it into your other hand.
The gun returning to the holster automatically would be a decent feature if not for the fact that sometimes I would find myself accidentally throwing the gun across the room where it would be impossible to recover, and it would detach itself from the holster, and not return. I don't even know how it's possible to do that on purpose.
Zombie movements are really stiff, making it difficult to judge shots when they switch from one animation to the next.
Your dog companion will occasionally give up on whatever you asked him to do. This usually happens because you've reached a scripted scene that requires the dog to be somewhere. But sometimes it just happens for no apparent reason. And sometimes you really need him for something, and you lose control of him because he's moved into a scripted scene.
Picking up items can be a chore, as there is a lot of junk that you can interact with in the world. And figuring out where your hands need to be to highlight an item is not intuitive.
When interacting with things like door handles, and climbing points, your hand will snap to it, detaching itself from where it should be, so that you can effectively interact with things just beyond your reach. This is a neat feature until you are hand-over-handing your way through a climbing puzzle, and your body is nowhere near your hands.
Reloading break-action weapons is really awkward. You have a mag-release button that you use with every other weapon, I don't know why it wasn't used to open the double barrel shotgun.
Mines are really effective, but about half the time there could be a dozen zombies standing on it and it wont detonate. Sometimes I accidentally swing it into a zombie corpse and it goes off. *shrug*
Wearing a hat can be a liability, because sometimes you grab it instead of the weapon on your back.
This last one is a pet peeve of mine: You have an automatic flashlight. Not only does it *BARELY* produce light, but there's parts of the game where it is LITERALLY night time, and the light never comes on. MAKE THE FLASHLIGHT MANUAL. ALWAYS. There's no excuse for this.

Again, I generally enjoyed my time with the game. But the effort put into the gameplay is really too little too late for this to be remembered as fondly as it's predecessor.\t\t\t\t\t

Rarely experienced any bugs while playing and has an amazing story I wish there was a backpack system where you could store larger weapons like your Machete, Shovel, Shotguns, Rifles or just another large gun slot, but the game was great nonetheless and ran nicely even though my pc has some slightly old parts (RTX-3060 and 12th Gen Core i5-12400f). I hope this game gets some big updates like new modes from other Vertigo projects such as After the Fall's harvest missions and enemy types. DLCs (maybe a continuation on the main story or AS1 DLCs?) would be neat. But overall the game is a 9/10 for me.\t\t\t\t\t
