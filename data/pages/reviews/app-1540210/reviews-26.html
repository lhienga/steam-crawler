\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give this a big thumbs up, but where the game is on release I can't.

These are my personal feelings, people can feel different and I will respect their opinion always, I have been playing VR for about 4 years now? So I am no expert by any means, but I can say I have played around 50-60 VR Titles since starting VR.

I am not sure where to start with this, I enjoyed parts of the game; beat it in about 4.5 hours? Maybe a little less, had some co op issues where it was kicking me or the host every so often. Played on the hardest difficulty, my Q3 died 3 minutes before the ending but my coop partner was able to the last 3 minutes while I watched.

The pros are the game does look pretty dang good on PC, I use a Q3 with VD, the darks in the game are amazing and the flashlight not being super powerful I enjoyed a lot. The gunplay was decent and I enjoyed it for a bit, the gore was great and having buddy being able to just pounce on zombies was fun. But that is pretty much where the pros end.

I think my issues are for a game that is $50 I faced way too much jank, the climbing sections were brutal and just didn't feel great. I could get all the way to the end of a climb and let go thinking I could just drop the last 3 feet, but it would teleport me all the way to the start of the climb again. Climbing ladders, if you don't T-Rex arm your way up the ladder you grab the gun off your back if you have one. Doors would get stuck? Where a zombie arm is in it, and it would cause it to open and close super fast to the point of crashing and having to restart the game. Buddy would just disappear, and your character will keep saying "Buddy I can use your help with this guy" referring to the big armored zombies and when you would go to press the button to give Buddy orders you just couldn't. He would phase through entire walls when you would tell him to grab a key and then would just reappear with the key next to you

And I know for a VR game it is very hard to determine the right length for a game, but for $50 I am not sure I can justify buying it to play for 4 and a half hours, Especially when you have games that are priced around the same or less and gives you more gameplay. I don't feel like I rushed through the game, until the end when my headset was about to die. If people are saying it took them 15 hours or more, I would question that unless they are going for achievements and exploring every single corner of the map, but even then I don't think it would take 15 hours... I think the average VR player will take anywhere from 4-7? hours to beat the game depending on difficulty.

I saw somewhere in the discussions that a dev was saying they are releasing DLC for free? Based off the price of the game... I would wait for that DLC or you will spend $50 on a game that I PERSONALLY don't see it having much replay value atm, and Horde mode just isn't for me.

I think I had more fun in Arizona Sunshine 1 and I recently played that like a month or two ago.

Also I REALLY feel like the Big Armored zombies in this game is almost a direct copy and paste from After the Fall big armored zombie you fight right at the start of it.\t\t\t\t\t

This is one of those cases where I wish I could leave a mixed review, not one or the other! I look forward to updating this guide once I've progressed further to weigh out all the pros and cons! Solo replayability rate: 3/10
Coop replayability rate: 5/10
Bang for your buck rate: 3/10 (Mostly because this game is pretty short)


Pros:

- Considering the genre, the story is simple but entertaining
- The voice acting and the overall sound design is very well done. Probably the best thing about the game
- The dog adds a lot of charme to the otherwise pretty average game
- The game engine is very well optimized. Everything runs smooth without any issues

Cons:

- The game is pretty short. Expect playtime of like 4hrs. Considering the price tag, this is pretty unacceptable
- The Maps are very linear and rather short.
- Grabbing items is a *janky mess*. Items you want to pick up very often aren't highlighted and you end up either grabbing guns from your holster or you simply don't get the item (ammo, interactable objects etc)
- The overall graphics are good on PCVR. But don't expect high-res textures and details. Quest users probably get a blurry mess without shadows and details
- Switching weapons between hands is rather complicated and annoying. More flexibility is required in that regard
- The crafting stations feel kinda pointless as you can deal with most situations using guns. You can only two items at once and crafting stations are rare to find. Due to the grabbing items issue, "looting" for crafting items is absolutely annoying.
Not to mention that picking the right crafting card is also a mess because of the mentioned grabbing issue

Overall it's a short fun ride with a remarkable price tag.\t\t\t\t\t
