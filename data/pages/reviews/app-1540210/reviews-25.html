\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh boy... Where do I begin?

I bought this game to play coop and was really excited after seeing early gameplay. Sadly, after about 2 hours of our own gameplay, I have several issues and will be requesting a refund.

1. Bugs.
To my disappointment, we discovered multiple coop bugs within the first 30 mins of gameplay that should not be present in a fully released title. To name a few important ones:
- Ammo vanishing
- Taking damage randomly
- Weapons not syncing up (namely melee weapons which would break for the user and not for the other player)
- Items on the ground desyncing

2. Optimization/Performance.
Optimization is all over the place. Sometimes the game is super smooth, sometimes it skip lags or freezes entirely. Inconsistent framerates can lead to motion sickness which isn't fun for anyone. My coop partner was having a lot more lag and framerate drops than I was. I was the lobby host so not sure if that makes a difference.

3. Clunk/Counter-intuitiveness.
This is a big one. The entire game feels like it was supposed to be a flat screen game that they ported to vr. Almost everything you would normally do with your body in vr either doesn't work or has an unintended effect.
- You can't cross draw weapons (same side draw/holster only which sucks in a pinch)
- You cannot choose where what type of ammo is stored
- You cannot swap weapons efficiently (you have to toss one in the air and grab it with the other hand. Then the other weapon on your belt will teleport to the other side of your body)
- You cannot get physically close to any lootable items (which makes looting hard because you feel like you have to reach/lean really far to grab anything)
- Grabbing ammo/reloading seems like it should be more consistent (although we got a little more used to it over time)
- Basic features like handing your friend ammo from your belt aren't present
- You can't even drop your ammo on the ground for them to take (dropped ammo won't be present on the other player's screen)
- You cannot load ammo into your gun directly from looting it, you must place it in your belt first
- You cannot give ranged weapons to your friends
- And much more


Fuzzman02 (my coop partner for this game) summed it up perfectly, so I'll quote him here: "The oversimplification of VR mechanics is really what kills this game, which is a huge shame. If I am playing a VR shooter, I expect to be able to control where my guns and ammo are holstered on me, and to have the ability to drop weapons and let my friends pick them up without them just automatically zooming back to the "slot" they were in."
And I agree! Let us use our body intuitively in vr and give us our options! Don't make things so simple, it starts to ruin the immersion.


Bonus: Loot/inventory system.
Oh and not to mention, the loot/inventory system seems almost... Entirely pointless. From what I gathered, you have just 2 inventory slots. Just 2. We didn't find the need to bring much of anything with us because we just found stuff at obvious POIs that you couldn't miss. It felt more like we were on a Disney theme park ride watching the experience more than actually participating in exploring a wasteland with your friend. The gameplay and looting system is all super linear. We didn't find the need or want to really carry anything with us other than our weapons/ammo (which of you can only hold 3. 2 side arms, one primary over the shoulder).


Survival Mode:
The survival mode is pretty laughable. It's basically a carbon copy of the first game (only 1 map and it's the same map from the first game if I remember right). You're bound to a tiny space, progress is extremely linear (every so often you get a fog wave which unlocks one new gun type to use. The progression also seems backwards because you get an uzi before a sawed off which is useless in later waves when it arrives).
The Black Ops Zombies mods for Pavlov blow this thing to shame. The Pavlov BOZ mods even have more intuitive mechanics/gunplay than the entire AZ Sunshine franchise, campaigns, survival mode and all.


I cannot comment on the gameplay length as I did not finish the game, but I am seeing a lot of reports of players finishing the campaign in 4-6 hours which seems pretty short all in all.


Since this review has been negatives so far, here are a few things I liked:
- Shooting feels good
- You run pretty quickly (just watch out for motion sickness!)
- The dog mechanic is fun (albeit a bit wonky at times)
- The ability to interact with a lot of the environment is nice (although mostly pointless)


Overall, I cannot recommend this game in its current state, especially for the price tag. If you REALLY enjoy linearly guided zombie shooters, maybe pick this one up on a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for an immersive and intuitive VR experience DO NOT BUY. I wanted so badly to like this game. Watching pre-release gameplay got me so excited for the game to come out, but I am very disappointed. It is extraordinarily linear, looting is pointless, "inventory" is super basic and not intuitive, and most other features are limited. There was a ton of desync in coop as well. Melee weapons would be broken for one of us but not the other, and items on the ground would be in different places. Basic features like handing your friend some ammo from your belt are nonexistent. I am used to intuitive gunplay and inventory systems from games such as Half-Life Alyx and Into the Radius, and that's kinda the whole point in VR, to make the controls as intuitive as possible in order to provide deeper immersion than flatscreen games. The oversimplification of VR mechanics is really what kills this game, which is a huge shame. If I am playing a VR shooter, I expect to be able to control where my guns and ammo are holstered on me, and to have the ability to drop weapons and let my friends pick them up without them just automatically zooming back to the "slot" they were in.

Overall Arizona Sunshine 2 is a huge disappointment that doesn't take advantage of the advanced control and immersion potential that VR has to offer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSlick Zombie Fest. I felt a great sense of presence in the sewers and streets of Arizona Sunshine II. Mindlessly slaughtering freddies in their hundreds certainly helped. 10/10 would like to go back and experience it all again through fresh eyes.

Only 11.1% of players have completed the game when I edited this. I am one of them. Unknown number of people have completed the campaign at apocalypse level. I am one of them, too.

It's well polished with few bugs, and little jank on my native SteamVR headset, compared to other VR games I've played this year (over 300, AS2 is definitely in my top 50). Here's the things to ignore from the negative reviews that I have found to be user-error, user choices, or minor UX issues which are not as bad as made out to be. The game can be played as-is, with little adjustment required to live out a head-popping playstyle:

1) Trigger activates molotov, no zippo required
2) hold A button, drop weapon
3) lower your belt, keeps in reach & clear of loot grabs
4) carry 4 explosives, 2 with each hand
5) 2-handing weapons is cool, but mostly unneeded. Recoil is minimal (which might upset some purists)
6) Shotgun reloading is not idea;, but ensure to extend your off-hand fully and ignore the SFX, which trigger too early for reload...
7) Headless, walking zombies are a feature... surely....

There are issues

1) Random blackouts during combat. Very frustrating in horde mode particularly.
2) Disappearing ladders
3) Pickup up plasters
4) I got the shopping trolley stuck, once. Restart fixed it.

Graphics are OK. Nothing hugely impressive for PCVR, no real wow moments graphically, but some that come close enough. I write this during a Scottish winter. I feel warm just being in this game's hot environment.

Audio is pretty decent throughout. VA work is good. Character development is good. Pacing is good. The last half of the campaign struck me more interesting than the first. Apocalypse mode is way more fun that the default difficulty.

Melee is satisfying.

Guns work too. Some are very satisfying.

Zombie heads go pop, splat and squelch.

Story is surprisingly decent all things considered. Fred does a lot of work. It's a great story of self-discovery.

Buddy is a tad small for a male GSD, but he has six incisors and knows how to use them. But apparently doesn't know how to piss up a wall. Height matters.

I have enjoyed 20 hours of this game, twice through the campaign, with a few runs in horde mode additionally. I'm looking forward to trying multiplayer, which may yet change my opinion on bugs and janks.

Higher difficulty plays like a different game. When you have little inventory at your disposal (and often when you do), every corner is tense, my imagination helps there. It wasn't like that the first time through on default difficulty. Ramp it up from the get-go and see how you fair.

At least two of the achievements are bugged on Steam. Which isn't great, as it could mean others are too and we'd never know. But in any case, smoking is bad, kids. You can try it in this game without getting sick, but neither will you get the achievement (if it's not broken, it's because I didn't inhale).

Arizona Sunshine 2 is definitely among my favourite new releases in 2023.\t\t\t\t\t
