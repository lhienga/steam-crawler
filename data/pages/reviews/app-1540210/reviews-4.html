The nail in the coffin though is the game forces you down a very linear path into doing exactly what it wants, but I sure can't figure out what it wants half the time, or how to trigger it. However on several occasions, I've not been able to trigger these events for whatever reason, didn't step on the correct pixel I guess? Then worse yet, the game only wants you to kill the the zombies by that event. You will not kill them any other way. Shooting them, not an option. Couple this with the holsters on the dog automatically grabbing my guns it leads to not having the gun you need where you thought it was. I asked for the option to turn this off but have been ignored entirely. I imagine other players who enjoy Into the Radius and other completely manual immersive games would feel the same way. People who like manual reloads prefer manual holstering too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst was great. This is even better! After I finished the campaign, I wanted to play it again. Lot of fun and satisfying to be in first person and shoot a zombie in the face with your own hands (Controller)

Pros for me
Longer campaign than the first (which I enjoy)
Crafting mechanic was cool (Even though it's only explosives, it's still a new and fun concept)
Great story (More depth and even more emotional)
Realistic reloading (I know a few people liked the original, but it makes it even more immersive to me)
Melee weapons! easy to use
New types of zombies. Like fat zombies and some zombies that have some mushroom on their heads (I think) that tank a lot of hits. more challenging
A lot more styles of guns along with two handed weapons and grenades

And the best part is Buddy!! the good dog companion!
He can attack zombies
Hold extra pistols
Keeps your sanity
And can fetch objects that are out of reach.

You can even do dog related stuff like
Play fetch
feed him
shake hands/paws
And pet him!

Cons
Some bugs and glitches (nothing game breaking. Had a moment when I died and it brought me back to the beginning of the map, even though I got a checkpoint. It wouldn't let me continue. Reloading the level worked)
Can't control the flashlight (The game decides when you need the flashlight and will turn it on when you enter the right zones. Going through a dark place missing items because I can't see anything. I had to adjust the Gamma to see anything)
Wish you could carry the lighter in it's own separate pocket (crafted a Molotov and didn't have a lighter. I realize that you would need one, but they're not common and they take what little inventory space you have. If you want to use a Molotov, you'll have to use both inventory spaces for one Molotov and one lighter. Then once you use the Molotov, then there is no need for the lighter. Might as well just make two grenades)

Overall highly recommend. Lots of fun

Suggestions
Maybe a play-through where you can pick any weapons. A weapon selection menu. there are guns that were my favorite that I used a lot.
