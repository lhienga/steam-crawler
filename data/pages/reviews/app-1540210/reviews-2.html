\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed with an Index Headset and Vive wands on survival mode.

Great game.

The proponent is really slipping toward insanity from being alone and you can feel that in this and how it must really feel to be completely and totally cut off from humanity.

The gunplay is tight just like the first one. There are some points where.. you will die. Just remember, having an explosive out and ready to throw in areas that LOOK like there could be an ambush is almost always a good idea.

Ammo... find it. it's everywhere and you're going to use a LOT of it.

Protips:
Your dog has room to hold two pistols and several grenades/tennis balls so make sure you take advantage of that.

Your gloves have an "inner" lining to hold one object each.

Keys are sometimes best held onto for other areas unless they are required for progression

You can REACH some things that appear to need buddy's help

Your arms are like stretch armstrong, use that to your advantage when opening doors.

Shoot locks from a distance... like... a big one.

Freds with growths on their head almost always take less ammo if you aim at unprotected areas even though it's not a headshot.

Big riot gear zombies only take one bullet AFTER you sic buddy on them and their visor opens

Save yourself time and effort and set climbing to "comfort" settings. Then all you have to do is click on things that require climbing puzzles and you'll be on the other side. Great for people with Vertigo.

3/4 through the game you lose all your weapons and ammo so don't hoard for that "final battle". Spend those rounds if you need to.

Cons:

There are parts where you follow the somewhat linear path and end up in a dead end. Don't waste too much time wondering what happened because, most likely you either just need to walk up to a door in the room to get a loading screen or backtrack.

There are several locations where you can't do something simple like step over a small parking lot gate which is aggravating, but you'll find you can lift/move things in those situations that you couldn't otherwise and wouldn't be looking for.

You can only carry one "two handed" weapon on your back.

Sometimes loading an area can put things out of sync (ALA claws and paws area) with the radio and the operating table, but it does seem to eventually catch up after some dialogue.

The "final" battle reminds me of any given level in serious sam. If you understand that reference you know what you're going to be up against.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe shooting is great and the manual reloading they added into this game is also really fun, but like, for an extra $10 you can buy half life alyx which not only has a longer campaign but mod support.

for $50 you get a 4-6 hour long campaign( i beat it in 5 and a half hours) and ONE horde map, horde is also boring because you're literally standing in a box shooting zombies as they slowly walk towards you and you don't even get any good guns in the beginning waves, you're just stuck with a basic pistol and you don't start getting shotguns and two handed weapons until like wave 10.

The story is forgettable, the dialogue is garbage, the dog is more of a hindrance than anything else because it likes to steal your guns when you try to holster them, the game doesn't give you enough ammo at some points throughout, and the enemies kill you in 2-3 hits on normal difficulty so letting them get close to you is pretty much a one-way ticket back to a loading screen.

About the dog, the dog has a harness that you can put guns and explosives on, it has 2 slots for guns and 2 slots for explosives, you can put explosives in the gun slots but not vice versa, so you can strap 4 mines for later use on your dog, cool right? Well, no, if the dog is near your hand when you release a gun, which automatically holster on your body by the way, it might get holstered on the dog, so you could holster your sawn off shotgun after a fight and when you go to pull it out later you don't have it because the dog's harness stole it from you, this happens even when you aren't looking at the dog.

Also also, the game resets your carried weapons and anything the dog is carrying three times throughout the whole game, so that shotgun i mentioned earlier? you will lose that permanently after the first reset because as far as I can tell it doesn't spawn anywhere else in the game. which sucks because the game doesn't give you a ton of ammo to work with so you ideally would have a rifle on your back, a pistol in your dominant hand holster, and a shotgun in the other so you can use every ammo type barring one effectively and stretch your resources as much as possible. The sawn off is the only one handed shotgun in the game, the other shotguns are two handed so you can see the problem here, especially without any one handed rifle-ammo weapons.

The game is fun but man, the campaign is way too short for $50 and having such a bare-bones wave mode that doesn't even use any of the melee weapons present in the campaign is really disappointing. This would've been fine back in 2016 or whenever the first game came out because we didn't have ♥♥♥♥ like HL:A and BONEWORKS but even boneworks has a better horde mode than this, and that game only has like 3 distinct guns. this game at least has 6 guns. Yes, there are more guns in both games but most of them are just reskins with either a higher magazine size or a slightly different reloading mechanism.

You can't even lift the cover on the LMG that goes over the ammo belt fed into the gun, you just drop the box mag and then replace it and pull the charging handle, that's like putting a lever action into the game and having it automatically chamber each round after you fire.

To the credit of the developers, they are adding more horde maps later this year, so we'll have four in total but why weren't those here from the beginning? the mode already sucks, I can't imagine just adding maps is going to change that.

TLDR: wait for a sale, there's no content here worth $50
