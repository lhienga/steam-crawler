\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed using Valve Index controllers and Vive Pro HMD.

Grabbing an item requires completely opening your hands and closing them. There is no button can be bound to use the game's "teleport grab" (like Half-Life: Alyx or Red Matter 2).

Grab "detection" is far too sensitive and loosening/adjusting the grip for comfort will cause you to drop items. There is a setting to make the gun be bound to the hand but not melee weapons. The sensitivity makes it far too easy to drop/holster the gun. This is incredibly frustrating in the middle of a horde as I've unintentionally holstered a weapon or dropped a melee weapon by loosening the grip on the controller.

Reloading needs work and some margin of error. Too many times I was slamming the controllers together because I didn't get close enough to "grab" the pistol slide.

Cupping your hands when using the pistol results in the secondary hand hovering. This looks weird seeing as the reload animation has the cupped hand on the bottom of the pistol.

Above issues aside, I enjoyed the combat and the Buddy dog companion was implemented great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI highly recommend this game, so much fun. The VR graphics are outstanding! As good or better than Half-Life Alyx. It's exactly as advertised, a zombie killing shooter with a dog sidekick. It gets pretty intense, but it's well balanced between chill parts and heart pounding intensity. It follows a simple story line, the main character is trying to find patient zero (not a spoiler) while making funny commentary throughout the game - some of it's pretty funny, other stuff... meh.

Reloading weapons takes some getting used to, but they just added easy reload feature after I figured out how to do the difficult reload reliably. And the default movement is teleport, which is awful, but you can customize the movement which makes the game way better. I almost returned the game before I realized you could change how you move.

It's not an open world survival game, so don't buy it thinking it is. There is crafting but it's not very good. My only complaint is there are a couple of parts that are just impossible to get through and I played on 2nd difficulty level, just above story mode. There were 2 parts I tried 10 different ways to get through and just failed, so I had to switch to story mode just to get past them. But maybe I just suck.

Overall, the game is a lot of fun and I highly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just finished the game, a good 5 hours of gameplay. Just you and your best Buddy trying to find some human companions :)

The inclusion of another named character (Buddy) is great for the main character, talking to himself was very sad in the first game and I could really feel how sad he's been I genuinely felt so happy for him when he got Buddy.

It's hard to convey a character when he's just hands and a voice. But I think this game does a lonely psycho who's lost it all in the apocalypse very well. His quips were funny and having a pet dog to bounce off of helped solidify some small laughs.

I enjoyed the little story and good world building, I liked learning more about the main character like his sister having cancer and him being a construction worker, however the trailer spoiled a few key details, something that happens with the dog. Anyway, had a good ending leaving it open for more (I hope we do some day) The after credits gives you a little play area to. It was good.

Something I haven't seen people talk about is the melee play, it's pretty basic, but holy does a zombie vr game with ANY sort of melee add so much. I do wish there was a way to go melee only without them breaking. It's so fun to slice heads and arms.

I guess it is a bit overpriced... but with vr I can see it being worth if you want a linear experience as vr is generally just a bunch of sandboxes. At least this has a story, but I would recommend getting it on sale anyway.

Also Freddy!\t\t\t\t\t

It's almost just as buggy as well!

I have some minor complaints, though.

The dialogue.
The protagonist is a crazy, lone, corny guy who loves to tell dad jokes, but now for some reason he has a fart fetish and tells sex jokes? I prefered his personality and jokes in the first game.
(The piercing joke made me chuckle though)

The addition of crafting materials feels a little out of place in this franchise.\t\t\t\t\t
