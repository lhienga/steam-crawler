\r\n\t\t\t\t\ton a 10/10 scale I rate it as a 8/10 - however, I don't understand the negative reviews. This is a very competent evolution to the first game Arizona Sunshine (AS1) which in my opinion, was the best Zombie shooter if not one of the best games available in VR at the time of it's release. In fact I am willing to say considering all of the VR games available on Steam when the Vive came out, Arizona Sunshine was the sweet spot of VR shooters and drives the genre much like the Doom/Half-life games were the de facto standard in FPS games from the start. Yes, I think AS1 was the de facto game that helped define how VR shooters are played. AS2 keeps within the spirit of the first game in most ways the game is played in VR. Meaning the interactions with the environment are nearly the same but AS2 takes the game to the next level and opens a new chapter to the story where AS1 leaves off.

One change is the reloading of weapons and flashlight turns on and off automatically but these are very minor things. The story is believable and progression is similar to AS1. The single player game in AS2 is story driven and very linear much like AS1. The AI is upgraded a bit as the zombies are just laying around and react to noise. The less noise you make the less the zombies react. Which brings in the new addition of the dog companion. You can slowly and silently clear a room of zombies by sending the dog at them one at a time. As soon as you shoot all the zombies in the area stand up and attack. It's an small detail to notice but it is a interesting new feature. It has a Dead Island feel to it in that sense.

Another good change is in AS1 the levels followed the same predictable pattern. As you walk along you take out small groups of zombies as you go and at the end of the level you fight a horde. Rinse and repeat. AS2 has unpredictable hordes and the level progression isn't as predictable as AS1. The levels are lot more seamless as you move along the story line. I was feeling a sense as if I was playing a linear/non-open world Dead Island game in VR. I can see the clear upgrades to the AS2 gameplay and visuals. The voice acting is still annoying but the focus is more realistic and they try to focus on the emotional state of someone who has been alone and surviving in a zombie apocalypse.

There are 1 and 2 handed weapons as you progress as well as melee weapons that are decently effective. Another new item is a very basic crafting system. When you find a crafting station, you can use items you can pick up along the way other then ammo that you can craft grenades, firebombs etc. that can be stored on your wrists. There are also the necessary exploding barrels that helps you clear hordes.

The graphics have improved considerably but with a performance hit. I played on a Occulus 2 with Steam on PC. I have a GTX2060 GPU, Intel i9 10k CPU, 32GB of memory and SSD drives. I had to turn the settings down pretty low to get the frame rates to a playable level. The high settings made it unplayable on my setup. The age of my system is starting to show.

If you enjoyed AS1 then you will appreciate the refreshment of AS2 on your journey through the zombie apocalypse. I measure my rating of a game based on how quickly I get bored with it and the immersion that pulls me to keep me playing. Most games I will not spend longer then a half hour or so before I recognize I will not be able to finish the game. I played AS2 for 2 hours straight after installing on the first session and am looking forward to jumping back in and keep going. I will finish this game and the new game play in AS2 makes it a bit more interesting and intriguing to keep going. It's good and a competent sequel to the first and picks the story up where the AS1 left off. It's worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t

Reloading is among the best I've seen in a VR game (second only to the venerable H3VR). The zombies themselves can move quite fast, even on the default skill level - I died several times during the main campaign, and I could see this game becoming extremely difficult for a VR newbie (although there are two easier difficulty options, which is a good accessibility feature). The gore system is impressive, and big showery head-pops are frequent (although it is deeply unsatisfying to shoot one of the boil-encrusted zombies and see your bullet do ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥).

The main campaign has some cool-looking and varied environments (and no less than 3 sewer levels, yippie) and took me about 10 hours to complete. I haven't checked out the horde mode, but that seems pretty cool too. I didn't see many bugs and the game was generally pretty performant, which is a big plus. The humor is quite a bit cruder than the first game, but it works to set the comedy-action-movie tone of the game quite well. No gloom and doom here; we're wise-cracking and poop-joking our way through this apocalypse.

I would recommend this game, especially if it's on sale for around 30-35 bucks. I know it's hard to make money on the VR market right now, so I understand why the game is priced at $60, but it would probably still be a fun game at that price and is, frankly, a steal at anything lower than $40.\t\t\t\t\t
