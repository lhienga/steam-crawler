\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNeed For Speed Hot Pursuit (2011) is by far the worst NFS game.
First of all, this game is a solid proof that the developers simply didn't understand what the fan base wanted.
NFS Underground, NFS Most Wanted, these games perfected the formula several years before this and yet this game lacks many features that those games had.

The open world (known here as "Open Drive") is optional and literally has nothing to do except driving around the map, which is something you wouldn't want to do more than you have to because this game is repetitive as ♥♥♥♥. Races are divided into groups and each group is assigned one area of the map, meaning you will race across the same area around 5 to 8 times, which would feel like 40 because most areas look the same anyway.
Why couldn't they just make an open world game with a unique map and have cops in free roam is beyond me.

The gameplay itself isn't really fun, yes drifting is as fun as always but the game does everything it can to discourage drifting.
We are introduced to "shortcuts" throughout the race, where instead of driving on the smooth roads where you can drift and have fun, you are encouraged to drive on the offroad shortcut where your car is unstable, you can't drift and your controller vibrates more than a deluxe toy for adluts. Keep in mind that sometimes you will have to use those shortcuts to meet the time requirement or pass an OP enemy racer who drives 5 times faster than the fastest car in the game.
In addition to that, sometimes drifting just doesn't work, instead your car just tries to brake, how did they ♥♥♥♥ this feature up after perfecting it so many times?!

The game is divided into 2 "storylines" - Cop and Racer. Calling them storylines is outright a lie since you only have an opening cutscene and that's it, the races or "events" as they're called are completely random and sometimes just stupid. For example, one of the cop events is a time trial where you're racing to help other cops block 2 racers. Why the ♥♥♥♥ do you need me to drive 20 miles... you have 3 cars on 2 Audi cars (yes it's always the same racer cars, which really adds to the laziness of this game), one of the Audis is broken, if you were to just move in closer you could have stopped both of them on your own.
The other events are outright generic, hot pursuits, which as a racer are tedious and unfair because every race it seems like the entire city department is on you while as a cop they are are completely luck based as sometimes racers would drive 5 times faster than you, not letting you hit them once, while other times they would fall for every trap you set and let you easily ram into them.

The car customization is a complete joke, it's only available for racers. You can paint your car with a bland RGB color table and apply some generic shapes. You can't save customization so you have 'recent selections' which never update and only have the random recent selections the game set at the beginning.

After completing both storylines I was ready to review this game but I decided to try the online mode as well, just to get a complete view of the game.
The only good thing I can say about it is that it's cross platform, otherwise it would have died a week after launch.
The online mode is very basic, you have a few gamemodes from the singleplayer and that's it. There is a huge input delay, which is a huge problem in a game like this where timing is crucial since placing spikes and turning around require precision.

Before describing how this remaster is compared to the original version, I would like to explain how I even stumbled upon this game in the first place.
A few years ago I played The Crew, Ubisoft's takes on the racing genre where I played online with a few people. One of them told me about Need For Speed - Another open world AAA racing series. Knowing only of Steam and Uplay at the time, I looked for the series on the Steam Store and this game, NFS HP 2011 was the latest game in the series to be on the store, and also the one with the most positive reviews. So I bought it, just to be completely disappointed by the lack of features compared to what I was told and used to.

Now to compare the 2 versions, as a remaster this is a complete flop as well. It is obvious that the only reason this was remastered was because EA wanted to capitalize on the remastered games hype Created by the Resident Evil 2 remake.
Expecting the same amount of change and improvements here would be a huge mistake. This game only adds a slight graphical upgrade and multiplayer crossplay (Which I wouldn't recommend anyway since future NFS games have a much better online mode).
Hell, if you reinstall the game, your progress wouldn't even be saved. Like c'mon, atleast add cloud saves, you literally save my account in your EA servers.
Now that EA added all of their library to Steam, there really isn't any reason to fall for this game like I did. All of the later installments in this series (except for Unbound maybe, I heard it's terrible) cost just as much, look better and are much more entertaining than this game. If you like the fact you can play both racer and cop, get NFS rivals, if you want a NFS game without any BS offroad driving, get NFS 2016 or NFS Rivals.

2/10
bad game
- Sam Ireland\t\t\t\t\t
