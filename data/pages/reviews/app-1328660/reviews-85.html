\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was super fun and I enjoyed the extra cars and missions. I find online play fun too, but it is sometimes hard to find lobbies with the events that you want to play since there aren't too many players.

A question you may wonder: is this game worth it? Well, it depends:

If you have the original, don't pay $30 for this. While I would say it is a good remaster, I'd get it on sale since it isn't too much different from the original to warrant $30. I got this game on sale for $5.99, so I'd say it's worth it for that price if you want the extra events, cars and online playability. Even if you don't have the original, I'd still wait to see if it's on sale. It is a really fun game so if using $30 for it is okay with you, then I think you'll still have fun. However, since it is still an older game I don't personally see it being worth $30.

Overall, the remaster offers a noticeable bump in graphics (primarily anti-aliasing), new wrap and color system for your cars and new cars & events. I do think that they should've done a little more with the graphics since newer NFS games have rain droplets that appear on cars, glowing brakes, better weather effects, more detailed cars and newer sounds.

Despite the graphics upgrade not having quite what people would expect from a remaster, the game still looks really good and I had a lot of fun with the game.\t\t\t\t\t

Used to play Hot Pursuit on the PS3 when it first released, always enjoyed the ability to play as both police and racers.

A twist upon the usual Need for Speed gameplay, there is no open world but instead 'events' of increasing difficulty as you progress through the game beating the times of and having your times beaten by your 'friends'. Racing gameplay is fun and extremely fast but less realistic than other Need for Speed games.

Achievements are earned naturally apart from a few challenges towards the end, but easy enough for proper gamers like Random Show.

8/10 game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\toh boi my nostalgic hype for this game died real quick lol

short review: you can absolutely buy this game on a sale and have few hours of nostalgic fun with a game that runs decent and rarely has any crashes/bugs and runs on a stable 60 fps (on RTX 3060 tho).

now the long cons of the game as a whole not just remastered component:
-the game is insanely scripted and AI is just annoying. both racers and cops AIs are just very rigged in a very unfun way that they just constantly teleport no matter how far you are, how well u drive, if you are driving a lambo and they have a honda civic, and just enter a "take over" state that will make them catch up to you and always maintain a distance. dont get me wrong this doesnt make the game hard at all you can almost win every race first try but it gives it a very unrewarding and "fake" experience.
- car selection is poorly implemented. the game has a decent variety of car selection but the way the "story" forces you to play with specific cars (even if they are locked) makes the unlocking cars and the progression system almost useless. almost in 90% of the races u are limited to either 1 or 2-3 cars and never actually get to play with the cars u unlock or enjoy driving. on top of that, even though u are forced to drive a 1900s car for a mission all the other AIs have a variety of cars and the pole position drives something like a elemento (again game is not hard to win it just gives a limiting feeling)
- time trial mission are overused! more than half of the missions are always time trial races where u either drive solo or vs few cops and makes you forget the game was supposed to be a racing game vs other racers. plus imo time trial racers are way less fun as its all about being optimised and you cant enjoy the racing component or weapon/fight aspect of the game.
- the "random" traffic is just in the game to make the game annoying and adds nothing positive. the traffic in this game is scripted as you can replay the same sharp turn 10 times and you can guarantee the same traffic is gonna be there everytime to block your turn (which is just annoying in time trial races) and never feels like an open world type of traffic
-the car customisation is non-existence. you can just change the base color or add a generic square decal to a car but some cars are even locked on one color and you cannot change any visuals or parts.

Conclusion: the game is just dated and too scripted to enjoy more than couple hours and doesnt offer much replayability. you can buy it on a sale and enjoy few hours of nostalgic gameplay but the fun ends there. otherwise if you want to play this game for the "pursuit" aspect of it you should try something like NFS PayBack.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPut simply, this game is WAY TOO DIFFICULT. With a lot of practice and trying out different cars, it's possible to finish in the top three sometimes, to attain the Gold medal requires a great deal of determination and repetition.

The time/delay penalties and conditions applied to the player, do not affect the AI racing cars. For example, hitting a wall and crashing gets you a 2 second delay, which in a time trial is crucial. If you notice when an AI racer hits oncoming traffic, they "bounce" right off and keep going, usually at top speed! I realise this is basically an arcade racer, but come on, a little nod toward realism and fair play would not go unappreciated.

Working as a cop, things are a little more forgiving, until you progress a few levels in and have to take a very quick interceptor car through a time trial without damaging it - talk about nerve-wracking!

Once the McLaren F1 appears as a player car, the AI seems to be turbo-boosted beyond all belief and the comparative difficulty climbs vertically. Having overtaken a number of cars and progressed in the race position, you brake for a bend, drift out a little and reapply the throttle coming out of the bend - in which time, the AI cars you previously passed have managed to take said bend at their top speed, without braking, and usually manage to knock into your car on the way past, again throwing you off course and losing a little time as you steer to recover the line you want. As stated previously, racers hitting AI non-race cars or each other has no impact on their speed or progress.

Add to that - the shortcuts are rarely shortcuts and you often cannot see where they're going, especially in the night levels, and high-powered rear-wheel drive cars invariably fare better on asphalt. The night levels can also be extremely dark and with lightning in the skies, visually distracting when trying to keep an eye on the road, your car, the opposition and the road ahead.

All in all, if you have the time and sense of masochism to continually grind away at race after race, attempting to reach a Gold medal, go ahead and buy this game. Like most of my games, I waited till this one was on sale at a very cheap price.\t\t\t\t\t
