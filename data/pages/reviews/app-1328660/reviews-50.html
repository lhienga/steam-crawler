I played this game since forever, I first got it on PS3 and enjoyed the ♥♥♥♥ out of it, later got it for pc and I got really happy when they said they're were gonna remaster this game!


Pros:

- They give you all the DLC (and even included the one pack that was never on pc!).
- Crossplay.
- People are actually playing Hot Pursuit online again to this day (Revived online).
- Fun with friends (up to 8).
- Cheap on sale.
- Has a hidden high skillceiling (I recommend you watch a tutorial for learning how to drift recover)
- One of the better arcade racers on the market where people actually play online


Cons:

- Almost no graphic overhaul (Just some very slight changes, barely noticeable. You can't call this a remaster for crying out loud.)
- Nothing is done against cheaters in online (Thanks EA)
- Pricy when not on sale (30 bucks here, on sale it's 7.50 usually)
- Denuvo (This is what truly killed the game for me, getting random stutters and frame drops is horrible for a racing game where corners are so common and can ♥♥♥♥ up your entire drift by crashing into oncoming traffic or breaking your drift recovery)
- No new content (For a remaster that is a easy cash grab, I wish they at least made another car pack)
- EA launcher
- Didn't get rid of the cinematics for when you spike/EMP/bust/wreck a car, This ruins the game because it puts you in autopilot which is like having a woman behind the wheel for 4 seconds which can lead to you crashing or getting hit by someone's spikestrip etc.
- Too much traffic (The AI for it can be unpredictable too like crashing into other AI at a intersection)
- AI racers and cops are borderline cheating, rubberbanding is common and it's hard to outrun racers and cops because they always keep up with even if you're abusing drift recovery. They also cheat their gadgets and can spam them faster than usual, they also get unlimited ammo for each gadget.
- Got rid of 3 cars, 1 is understandable because the company behind the E7 concept car went bust. but for the Mercedes McLarens they were lazy and didn't bother to get the license for it even though they did get the license for it on the NFS mobile game.

#KillDenuvo

There's no need for Denuvo if in the end the game will get cracked anyway and just ruin the game for the people that actually bought it, So useless and counter productive. I still love this game to death but it's so much wasted potential.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompleted as of writing this review: Both campaigns with Gold medals everywhere.

Since the original was delisted on Steam, I will leave my review for both the original and remaster.


Original version: Played this game since childhood, but never managed to complete it until only now, 10+ years later. What can I say - I’m satisfied with this game. I played only the PS1 classics in my childhood, so I was never fully into the street racing games everybody loves (I still have yet to play MW2005). So playing this was a really good experience. The cars are fast, the driving is smooth (with some goofy moments, but nothing’s perfect) and the graphics are astonishing for their time. I’ve finished both campaigns and I don’t know why, but the Cop campaign was more enjoyable than average Racer campaign. Maybe it’s because I liked the idea of winning by ramming the car over and over (yes, I understand that it’s not NFS and more of Burnout, but that’s how I feel). The driving though, man it’s smooth. I don’t get why some people prefer against this metaphor of “brake to drift”, because that’s probably what the real cars require to push to drift, as far as I know? The extra mechanics for Hot Pursuit aspect aspect are really cool too, but can be annoying sometimes. The challenge here is basically non-existent I would say. Only like 5 times I struggled with some missions before eventually getting the requirement for Gold medal. Yes, the rubber-banding can be ridiculous sometimes, but it was not so often to consider it a minus.

The graphics were top-notch. It still blows my mind how realistic everything here looked, and it was in 2010! Yes, you can argue that the moments where the graphics don’t look as good are really well-hidden (such as people’s faces), but the immersion is definitely here. The music is pretty mid though, was not a fan of most music, except for tracks by Pendulum, the main theme, some tracks the name from which I can’t recall and my personal favorite Big Weekend by Lemonade (yes, I don’t think NFS music should only be DnB or Rock). Also it was annoying to get the radio interrupted every time the pursuit began. Could’ve at least given the option to turn that off.

Anyway, I’m happy that I was finally able to finish the game and say farewell to it, truly an amazing experience that is worth checking out to everyone who likes racing games, that are not strictly located in the city.


Remastered version: This version is an another good example out of hundreds of how an idea to remaster something is only because of money. This remaster is pretty much a scam and a dirty tactic by EA to force you to pay more dollars for less. What do we have? Graphics? - Toned down because they thought it was genius to use PS3/X360 as a base. (can be fixed in PC version) AI? - ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up and can break sometimes to the point of where it was too easy to finish the Race / Pursuit. Cars? - Yes, most are here, but not all of them. We have examples where it’s expected, like Carbon Motors because of company’s closure, but there are also Mercedes-Benz cars’ absence, which is a complicated case. Also for some reason the number plates were changed too…? New features? Well, the DLC that wasn’t in the OG PC version is there, but I didn’t care about that at all, same for Photo Mode.

And lastly, the PC port… Oh, don’t get me started on that one. For some reason when I open the game, I always have to Minimize and Maximize it back so the controls will be recognized. No matter what I tried, nothing fixed this issue. The graphics settings, even if more details, still left me unsatisfied, as there was only a SMAA x1 option for AA, so I still could see some aliasing happening. TXAA was blurry and ugly, so I was against using it. I’m also pretty sure that, because of my 5400rpm HDD, this game was messing up all over the place. Music could suddenly cut out instead of fading out, unpausing the game forced me out of control for 1 second before the HUD reappeared. And I’m pretty sure the AI’s behavior is also dependent on how fast your data transfer is. No, I will not tolerate this, because it was not the issue in the original PC version, do not even try to argue about that with me. Not to mention EA closing the servers for the OG, SO THAT WE COULD’VE BEEN FORCED TO BUY THE REMASTER, so that the online features worked. Personally, I found the Online lackluster, I bought this piece of ♥♥♥♥ only for achievements (yes, I’m that petty).

For people who bought the original I’d suggest to stay the ♥♥♥♥ out of this version. It was released only because of a lame excuse, which was the game’s rerelease for 8th gen consoles with having almost nothing to differentiate it from the already good PC version.\t\t\t\t\t
