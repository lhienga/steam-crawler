I played the 2010 version plenty, this game has not aged well though. People will praise this game like it's the best modern NFS. It's mostly the nostalgia goggles talking though. The reality is the driving and handling is severely flawed and it's because of how prominent and strong the brake to drift mechanic is. It makes all the cars kind of feel the same but with different acceleration and slight handling improvements. This is obviously meant to appeal to the kids that played the classic NFS HP2 and cherished it the most. It does that well, but as far as being a fun arcade driver. It actually is not even better or more fun in terms of driving than the latest NFS Heat. If this game came out stand alone today with none of the nostalgia around it people would call it a colossal failure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis "remaster" is a really confusing game. Original NFS: Hot Pursuit was delightful, if flawed, NFS game.
This new and "improved" version is like a good ice-cream with somewhat bitter aftertaste.
It does a lot of things right and few things very, very wrong.

By far the absolutely biggest improvement is ability to skip damn cutscenes after like 2 seconds.
No more looking at screen for 30-40 seconds after every race, watching all the equipment introduction videos and new car unlocks. So that's great. That's an awesome improvement, and it's beyond me why it wasn't in the original.

Graphically, game improved marginally. It still looks pretty good, cars are detailed enough, environments are also pretty decent.
There are few graphical bugs sometimes, though. Few races, especially at night, sometimes fail to load all lighting. As in,
your car doesn't turn on headlights, and streelamps aren't on.
So you're racing in near pitch-black. It's fun for the first minute or so, but gets really annoying really fast. It doesn't happen too often, but when it does, it sucks.
Some road textures and whatnot are more detailed, but who cares about that when you're driving 350 km/h.
In other ways, game looks pretty unchanged.
Music is a disappointment, though. Wish they added some new tracks. Players that have played original Hot Pursuit 2010 will get bored from the same songs very fast.

However, there are some bizzare and downright bad changes to the game.
Nitrous feels significantly weaker. It's hard to say whether its really weaker, or car acceleration has been changed, or something else happened, but at higher speeds nitrous does basically nothing.
In fact, depending on car, Nitrous actually slows you down above certain speed.
When driving an Agera CCX or Bugatti at like 350km/h , whenever you use nitrous, you can actually see speed on the speedometer drop by like 10 km/h before picking back up. Bizzare.
Car acceleration in general feels changed. Its way harder to get to TOP SPEED now, especially in Exotic, Super and Hyper Cars. In original Hot Pursuit you could often max out Bugatti, Agera CCX or one of the other super fast cars quite easily. Now, going at absolutely max speed is quite rare in fast cars. Above like 300km/h, all the fastest cars with exception of Zonda accelerate very slowly. Unclear if its a problem with overhauled car physics, or acceleration itself was changed.
Pagani Zonda cars are the only cars that feel like they're accelerating properly at high speeds.

Police AI has definitely been overhauled to some extent. Police are brutal now, especially in hyper car pursuit races.
At points its basically insane how much police tries to EMP and ram you car. Sometimes you can get like 6 EMP lock-ons on your car in the span of like 20 seconds, which is purely stupendous.
While police definitely increase intensity of races, their AI sometimes bugs the heck out, and they can easily just drive into wall, oncoming traffic, or just get stuck in road at any time. Doesn't happen super often, but often enough to notice and annoy.
AI in general is buggy, especially on regular traffic cars. Can never know if NPC car will turn left, right, stop in the middle of the road, or start driving in the wrong lane. like 60% of the time NPC cars just stop in the road, but it's never a guarantee.

Car selection is both a good and a bad aspect of this game.
There are more cars, and a lot of them are new and updated models, compared to original Hot Pursuit.
However, there are some really stupid car decisions in the game.
First of all, there are way too many Porsche cars. This version of Hot Pursuit features cars from "Porsche Unleashed", but what it actually means is that there are like 15 Porsche cars, out of which maybe 3 are somewhat unique, and the rest are just 911 versions, that are very marginally different from each other.
There are quite a few Lamborghini cars as well, but those at least are different models, like Sesto Elemento, Gallardo, Murcielago, Reventon etc, with their own stats, handling, looks, design etc.
90% of Porsche cars are almost the exact same in every way, just with very slightly different looks and very slightly different stats.
The stupidest addition is Pagani Zonda, thought. Original game had 2 models of Pagani Zonda, both of them were exactly, 100% the same, with the only difference being slightly different names.
Now there are 4 Pagani Zonda cars that are 100% exactly the same, with same design, same stats, same description, same everything. The only difference is that all of them have slightly different names, like "Roadster, NFS edition" etc, and they have different color options. As in, 2 cars have unlimited colors, 2 have only 1 color design. But they are exactly the same freaking car, so what the hell is the point?
Now there's a severe lack of Mercedes cars. Original game had maybe 5-6 different models, this one has like 2. That's it.

There are some pointless additions to this game as well. There's a slightly richer customization system now. As in, majority of cars can have any color from color palette now, unlike original Hot Pursuit, where most of cars only had few color options.
However, there's now an option to add custom livery to a car. And it's very slow and clunky to use. In a game where most people probably use every car once per track, and every time they play they use different car, this is a pointless addition. Maybe it would be cool in multiplayer, but it's really doubtful.

Multiplayer is another problem. It's basically dead. Thought, it's more because of players than the game, but still, if you want to play multiplayer, its possible, but race selection is very limited, and in pretty much every sense multiplayer in this game is pretty pointless.

TL:DR
Pros:
+Slightly better Graphics
+Slightly more car models available
+More color variations for cars
Cons:
-A lot of cars are basic variations of one model of a car, very very slightly different in case of Porsche, or exactly the same in case of Pagani Zonda with no actual difference between models
-AI that has a tendency to derp out unexpectedly
-Ruined Nitrous
-Changed car acceleration, cars feel slower and rarely reach top speed, especially the fastest cars
-Same music as in the old version, nothing changed.

meh?:
~Car livery customization
~Game in general more buggy that original Hot Pursuit 2010
~Multiplayer dying, to be completely dead in not too distant future
