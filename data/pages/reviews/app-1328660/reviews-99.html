\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't play the original, and haven't played a Need for Speed game since the first Hot Pursuit on the PS1. I bought this for about six quid to play with my brothers. I've had a lot of fun doing so but, mainly, because I'm hanging out with my brothers. I will probably play it more.

At its core the driving can be fun (especially nailing a long drift) but otherwise the handling often feels sluggish. I haven't played online against people I don't know and don't want to: the collision issues, and balancing, mean that I doubt I would have any fun. Perhaps because the genre is under-populated at the moment it has little competition but I feel that the racing/combat driving/thrillseekers deserve something better.

As for its status as a remaster: I think too many opportunities were missed to re-balance the game and expand on its game modes. Some cars just seem to outperform all others in their class, some game modes feel like concepts, or the beginnings of ideas, rather than full-fledged parts of the game. I would have rather seen a remaster of Bizarre Creation's forgotten gem 'blur'.\t\t\t\t\t
