\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, I got the Remastered Hot Pursuit. I have the same opinion as the original one but I must say that this one is for people with newer PCs just to play a good ol' classic game today.

The greatest things are: as a PC player, the DLCs from this NFS are Console-exclusives only, It's a big miracle that these DLCs are all packed in the remaster, so that PC players can experience the DLC cars they've never seen before. Also, the graphics lighting is improved as it is more brighter, and the car Audio is well-improved compared to the original.

The only disadvantages is the removal of Carbon E7 and SLR McLarens (722 Edition and Stirling Moss) due to licensing issues. But I bet they had different reasons behind it.

Man, I really loved the crash and drift physics in this NFS. Way better than Unbound.

UPDATE 19/3/2024: I played Hot Pursuit Online, and it's all fun and games in there! It has different game modes that you can play with 7 other players. And surprisingly, It has a cross-play feature! So you can play with other players playing the game in their specific separate platforms in the same online session together. It's fun, even when it comes to winning or losing. Would recommend trying out the Online multiplayer mode! Even with friends is more fun to play better than ever!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUgh, on the one hand, I don't feel 4ish hours is really giving this game a chance. On the other, I think I've just about had enough of bouncing off the sides of the road in Hot Pursuit, at least for awhile. My thoughts:

The good:
Cars and scenery look great. You're out on the open highway and in small towns for the most part, not in an urban setting.
Sound effects and music are also appropriate for an arcade-y racer. Vroom.
Lots of different sorts of events. Races, chases, etc. (Maybe too many).
Plenty of real cars to drive. No off-brand approximations ala GTA.

The meh:
The game is kind of...hard. At least for me, an average joe playing with an X-box controller. Granted, I *do* suck, though I have enjoyed other casual racers like The Crew, DIRT and NFS Heat. But getting gold in an event requires a lot of luck and timing, and just one minor mistake and you might as well give up. I guess I just don't have what it takes. However, that brings me to the next point: if I did have what it took, what would I get?

Lots of little progression meters go up no matter if you win or lose, but I have no idea what any of it means. Vehicle unlocks happen for reasons I'm still not sure of, and there's just too much going on for me to have any feeling like I'm getting a handle on the rewards I'm getting for progression.

There are quite a few events for both road racers and cops. And there are things like events where you have to trigger EMPs and roadblocks and all sorts of special conditions. I thought I would love this kind of thing - I did in Paradise City back in the day - but exactly what buttons you have to press and when you have to do what you need to do is not explained carefully. When it's frankly rather difficult just to get in position to do what you need to do, and then you're not sure *what* you have to do, it's just frustrating when it all goes to pot.

No open world areas, which is a bit disappointing for me (though others may not care).

I didn't realize how much I should have appreciated the characters and straightforward campaign stories of other racing games. Cause you get none of that here.

Getting ad voiceovers every time you choose your car is kind of tacky and annoying.

Having to log into EA and constantly remake passwords and other BS is of course also super meh.

The verdict:
There's just a lot going on here that just gets thrown at you. I think some may enjoy the challenge, tackle the events one by one and finish this monster. But honestly, I've got a bunch more racing games in my Steam queue, and this one is just not personally sparking joy for me.\t\t\t\t\t
