\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I don't know why this remaster was even made and what it changed compared to the original version. Obviously, for money. But graphically and gameplay-wise it's the same game.

Pros:
+ Sound design. You love to hear the roar of the engines, especially in tunnels, and the tyres screaming for grip.
+ Graphics look good. But as I said, I don't see a difference to the original.
+ World design. I played NFS III Hot Pursuit and NFS Hot Pursuit 2, and this Hot Pursuit really feels like the areas from the first and second Hot Pursuits. You roam around rural areas, villages, forests, rivers and a waterfall, coastlines, mountains, desert. You never enter urban areas, but that's how it was in NFS HP2.
+ Weapon systems.
+ Driving model. You feel the difference between a high downforce exotic and bulky muscle cars.

Cons:
- Game forces you to play a certain way. It railroads you to drive oncoming as a racer and forces you to drive closely to NPC cars to regain your Nitrous. That should not be a thing, as it restricts the freedom to race in whatever way you want. As you remember, this wasn't a thing in the best NFS, the Most Wanted from 2005. There you regained nitrous just by driving, and whatever you did on the track was entirely up to you.
- Cutscenes every time you get totaled or crash. Or when you hit someone with a weapon. Come on, just let me play. I don't care about it, just give me a message and an announcer voice that I hit someone and just let me play. Sometimes when the cutscenes end you are left to hit the wall and lose speed.
- Text popups for doing anything in the game. I don't care, especially if they appear in the middle of the screen. Just let me enjoy the racing, text popups should appear only on the edge of the screen, for example above the speedometer.
- You don't start a race from a standing start, what? That was in the original as well, why can't we start the race ourselves from a standing start in a racing game?
- When you finish some types of races, you see a cutscene where your car stops just behind the finish line. That would be fine if the car was driving at 50kph, but not 300kph when I was crossing the finish line. It's immersion-breaking and just looks cheap.
- Drifting mechanic. It's just a cheap mechanic in modern racing games where you press a button and the game does the drift/powerslide for you, so the drift itself is completely artificial, you just adjust the angle. No, I want my inputs to match what the car does, turning left should always turn the wheels to the left.
- Very visible rubberbanding. Just penalize me with a massive failure if I crash repeatedly and let me win in a commanding fashion if I do everything perfectly. I think only on Seacrest Tour I could win with a big gap to the car behind, doing almost no mistakes so maybe it's the only race without rubberbanding. Sure, it's the longest track, but on every other track the AI cars feel glued to me and stay within touching distance, <2s, no matter what I do.
- Music, just as with the original, is honestly trash. Mostly pop music, I literally just turned it off and played my own in the background. Racing games should be filled with rock, metal and electronic music, just listen to NFS 3 HP soundtrack, Rom di Prisco and Saki Kaskas made great songs for that game I listen to this day.

Overall it's a good game, a good Hot Pursuit game, but most of the negatives outlined above should really be addressed in the remaster and they haven't been. This remaster is released just to sell the exact same game again so I leave a negative review.\t\t\t\t\t
