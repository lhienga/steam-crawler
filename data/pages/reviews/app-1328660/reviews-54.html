TLDR: This isn’t a remaster, it’s a re-release. Mods are required to restore higher resolutions textures from the previous PC release. That aside, NFS:HPR is one of the best arcade racer ever made as well as a great cops vs racers game. Brilliantly designed, endlessly fun, absolutely recommended!

NFS Hot Pursuit 2010 was a return to the roots of the NFS series, harking back to the 3rd entry also named Hot Pursuit. This is a re-release of the 2010 version with Crossplay, a couple cars were removed for licensing reasons and for whatever reason, the textures used for the roads are lower-resolution PS3 assets instead of the PC ones - but you can restore those using a mod (check the PCGW). So calling this release a Remaster is rather disingenuous.

Developed by Criterion of Burnout fame, Hot Pursuit goes for a very simple formula: cops versus racers. There’s two distinct "campaigns", one for each side and they both let you drive cars ranging from sports car to exclusive super-cars.

The gameplay is a mix between the "Black Box Era" NFS games (eg Most Wanted) and Burnout Paradise but a tiny bit more realistic with more weighty and licensed cars. Amongst other similarities, you get nitrous that replenish as you pull-off some cool moves like driving on the oncoming side of the road or drifting, and takedowns.
The driving model is just perfect for an arcade racer. Car handling is different enough from one car to another to make you want to try all of them but you can still use the "tap to drift" technique from Burnout regardless of the car. It's one of those games where you are full throttle almost all the time, rarely braking if ever.
It also features a weapon system similar to Mario Kart. For instance, you can use spike stripes or an EMP charge to slow other racers and cops down. As a cop, you can use spike stripes, call-in for helicopter support or roadblocks.

The sound design deserves a special mention, as every single engine sounds great. Perhaps not 100% accurate to their real-life counterpart, I wouldn't know - but certainly very satisfying.

All of that comes together beautifully in a really fun arcade racer with a fantastic online mode. It still looks great, has a free-roam mode just for the sake of it, a neat photo-mode, an extensive livery editor, and a pretty cool licensed soundtrack. It’s endlessly fun. Not much else to say really, if you like arcade racers with cops then this is one of the very best example of the genre.

100% recommended!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBrings back memories from 12 years ago. But, lets go with negative parts:
- In those memories NFS HP was a little harder game. At least that is how I remember it. Now you can finish all events without almost any replays. Maybe in several races but that is all, so in general it is easy game. I would say that is negative aspect, especially because there is no difficulty setting to change.
- Also, the racing feels a little strange when even if you drive like crap you can get close to NPC racers, like they are intentionally slowing down. Very awkward and bad feeling when you win race later on. But same goes if you are in the heavy lead, opponents can still catch you if you become reckless. So in general its even, but that gameplay experience is somehow weird and artificial.
- There is big delay when you accomplish achievement and when you actually get it registered. Sometimes takes longer then 2 minutes. Don't know what is an issue but it is very bad, you never know if it will unlock. First time I encounter this.

Those cons are not that big to spoil the good parts. Everything else is still nice.
- Graphics is polished and cars are extremely well designed.
- Gadget usage is interesting and creates nice feeling especially when you complete HotPursuit races as a cop. Priceless.
- Feeling of speed is solid but sometimes you think it should be faster.
In general nice game and worth to play. 7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
