\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo... first and foremost - quite possibly my most loved Need For Speed game of all time. I played hours on the original (yes, there was an original that was titled Need For Speed III: Hot Pursuit [PS1] and Need For Speed: Hot Pursuit 2 on the Nintendo Gamecube.) I'm referring to NFSHP2 - I poured hours into the game, thinking it was so cool, to start with a Lotus Elise, and then end up driving the McLaren F1.

As far as the Remaster goes, there are some honorable mentions to missing cars from the original game and events as well. Since the game is highly porsche invovled, we missed out on a few gems. Cars such as the Carbon Motors E7 Concept, the Mercedes Benz Stirling Moss Edition, the Mercedes-Benz SLR McLaren 722 Edition. Events such as "Electrical Storm" Which featured a cop event, with 1 Road block, and 10 EMPs - (yes TEN). It made a few of the most favored events for getting to that Level III EMP fast.

Additionally, the Remastered version, spends WAY TOO MUCH TIME in tutorial mode. Originally, there were "HudsenTech" and "HCM Corporation" cutscenes for all of the Level I equipment, but now it's just a gameplay tutorial, which is kind of a bummer, because (for myself), I enjoy the "movie magic" that comes from cutscenes designed to be purely creative and entertaining, and not another tutorial of how to play the game.

The soundtrack is unchanged, and remains a favored masterpiece.

But....the sound of the cars... that is a different story. In the original game, many of the cars engine types, acceleration, and revving were BAR NONE. They went to great lengths making them sound surreal. In the Remastered version, some cars, are notoriously LOUD (Pagani Zonda, I'm looking at you) and others barely make a peep (Corvette ZR1 and Bentley Continental Coupe Supersports, and literally every British road car). Additionally, some cars are just so sluggish, it's absurd. How does a Maserati Quattroporte GTS and Bentley Continental Coupe rarely get to their top speeds??

Regardless, this game is literally very playable for nailing milestones, finding routes, and good memorization of the layout of the tracks.

Handling 10/10
Driveability 10/10 - from Noob to Expert (any driver, expert or otherwise can handle this game.)
Challenging 5/10 - I really wish you could make the difficulty of the AI racers a bit more fine-tuned. Some races, they are insane to catch up to, others, you might as well be driving a 1 v 7 solo.
Sound 8/10 (only for the reasons mentioned previously)
Collisions 4/10 - very arcade and only meant to be a deterrent, rather than an actual penalty to the driving mechanics

Overall Rating 9/10

This game is still my all time favorite, controller based, arcade driving Need For Speed Title. If they made another, I would play it immediately, although I'd love to see something a bit more simulation, so that I can pull out the steering wheel and pedals (especially for the cop cars :P )\t\t\t\t\t
