\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIronically, menus for many games are now done with consoles in their mind first. Yet, some corporate policy still forces developers to add launchers to PC games, which require a keyboard and mouse. On top of that, games now have long in-game end-user license agreements (Steam supports in-store agreements) that gamers need to accept before they can play it. And obviously, active Internet connection when even playing a single-player game.

Need for Speed: Hot Pursuit Remastered includes all those problems. If you play it on Steam Deck, things are even worse, because the launcher (not the game) can break any time they update something. This is also ironic because developers have managed to polish Proton so much that it can play pretty much any game, but it's now launchers that do some weird nonsense that breaks Linux compatibility.

Why is the launcher needed? So you could be forced to sign in with EA account. Why should you need to do that? I have no idea. I managed to create EA account with a virtual keyboard on Steam Deck, but it seems that the only thing it's needed is multiplayer matchmaking and achievements (Steam has both features too), but UX for adding a friend and inviting a friend are horrible with EA launcher.

On top of that, all recent Need for Speed games have weird gamepad support, which is difficult to get accustomed to when you have been a PC gamer who has been using a keyboard to play racing games. For some reason, the car always auto-corrects steering, any steering you do, it steers back once you lift the analog stick. Maybe it's a Criterion Games thing because other racing games don't do it. But the best example of weird steering is when you are driving slowly, the steering still feels like you are speeding, which is not realistic.

Basically, what I am trying to say is that this game should not be played on Steam or Steam Deck or with a controller. I even tried the keyboard, but the handling was still whack. Level 9 racer and level 5 cop were the best I could do, after that, it became too difficult for me. On top of that, it suddenly stopped working on Steam Deck, the launcher either crashes or hangs forever.\t\t\t\t\t
