\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNeed for Speed™ Hot Pursuit Remastered is an incredibly enjoyable and entertaining arcade racing game!

It takes place in the fictional Seacrest County on the West Coast of the USA. In this game, you have the opportunity to become the most wanted street racer in the county, using 75 different vehicles across 78 events, including races, duels, preview time trials, gauntlets, and hot pursuits. Alternatively, you can become the SCPD's ultimate enforcer cop, using 57 different vehicles across 63 events, including interceptors, rapid response time trials, and hot pursuits. Certain events even allow you to use weapons such as deploying spike strips, using EMP's, calling for road blocks or helicopter support, and using EMP jammers or turbo boost. It's an exhilarating experience that requires some skill, a bit of luck, and a touch of determination. The fun doesn't end there, as the game also offers online modes where you can compete against real drivers from around the world on various platforms. There are 66 achievements to unlock throughout the game, further adding to the excitement. Additionally, there's a free drive mode that allows you to explore Seacrest County limitlessly.

For fans of the Need For Speed series, this game delivers exactly what you'd expect, while also serving as an excellent entry point for newcomers. It runs smoothly on the Steam Deck, and sets itself apart from other EA titles by allowing offline play in single-player game modes. This game is a highly polished upgrade from the original, with a much-improved score compared to my previous review.

Ultimately, this game offers a fantastic racing experience packed with thrills and entertainment, ensuring hours of replay value. It's definitely worth checking out!

During my playthrough of Blast from the Past, I was driving below the posted 35MPH limit. However, as I turned a corner, a SCPD cop car turned on its lights, and then busted me when I pulled over like a good Seacrest citizen might do. So, don't expect to play by the rules in this game!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is fun but your mileage may vary.

Tracks and driving and the overall gameplay is fairly action packed. The hot pursuit events are some of the most racing fun you can have, both as a racer and the police.

The rubber-banding is a necessary evil - but overall it is good here. Keeps you in the action. In some racers it you in first place driving alone, never was it the case here, you're always fighting for the spot.

It can get repetitive, you're doing the same thing throughout the whole game. It isn't even required to get a gold in everything - but of course we are gamers. I would have liked a system to setup our own races with the AI, track selection, car selection, police on/off, etc. For now we can only play through the pre-curated events.

The police side is way more frustrating than the racer side. Police side has these time trails in which you're penalised for touching anything. Also in the police side there are interceptor mission in which you have to chase down a racer in the open world - it is frustrating because the racer AI can erratically take a turn, throw infinite spikes and jammers, to add to this, our cars aren't nimble enough to make those small adjustments at high speeds. I don't hate the police side but I just feel like I have less control over those events.

PC-wise: it doesn't support mouse in the menus. Gotta use the keyboard which is fine. My game was bugged and I had to alt-tab in and out every time I launched it (maybe it was a windows 11 thing).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a mixed bag, but it's far from bad. I can't recommend at full price or without knowing how it plays.

The Good
- Fast high momentum races
- It has some challenging time attack races
- Active multiplayer, it elevates the game a lot

The Bad
- It isn't a perfect blend of racing and combat racing
- Can be repetitive
- Camera often moves to show you some stuffs, it gets annoying very fast.
- Handling isn't perfect, you will definitely notice the steering issues but it's bearable

The Ugly
- EA Launcher
- Hackers are present in this game.

Personal opinion: In multiplayer the game is at it's best when there isn't any combat and abilities, the racing is exciting enough on it's own and it often just feels like a cheat because the handling doesn't feel properly made with combat racing in mind.\t\t\t\t\t
