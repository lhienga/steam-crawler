\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA racing game that offers roundabout 20 hours of fun. No story, just missions, which i did not find to be a problem. Because I did not play the original, I cannot comment on the remaster aspect.
I have some minor problems tough, which made the otherwise fun gameplay more annoying and frustrating then necessary:
1. Genuine Races become somewhat rare, the more you progress through the career, in expanse of some of the other modes which i did not like to the same extend.
2. You can take out rival racer or police car with 300 kmh, no problem. But any other car and you instantly crash. At high speed and with a multitude of cars, this can get very annoying and confusing, if you could not see the one civilian car in the pulk.
3. Also the crash camera lingers on your wreck for longer than necessary. Very annoying if you just want to beat a time trial and unfortunaly not skip-able. Same thing with police chases. The camera shows you the police cars for far to long, all while you wish, you could just speed by.
4. The police-mode interceptor can get downright infuriating. On certain spots your target vehicle will turn around, but if you are driving 200+ you will most likely not hit him. Slowly turning around on these streets and in cars build for max-v isn’t fun. Also, if you crash while he turns around, the game occasionally spawns you in the wrong direction.\t\t\t\t\t

A lazy, but fun remaster
Like most people have stated, it is a lazy remaster that barely changes anything.
  • You still have a 30 or 60fps cap like the original.

  • The graphics look more or less the same apart from the textures that actually use lower resolution version from the Xbox 360 and PS3 port that the remaster is based on.
    But there are texture mods out there for the PC version of the remaster that brings back the higher resolution textures from the original version.

  • The lighting is a bit brighter, whether this is better or not is up to personal preference for most people.

  • It has some new anti-aliasing options to make it look sharper and less jagged than the original. But it's only SMAA, FXAA and TAA. TAA is the most effective, but many people doesn't like it because it can present a very blurry image depending on implementation.

  • Console exclusive DLCs that never came to the PC version of the original is present in full force in the remastered version.

I own the original version through Steam from years ago and bought the remaster at 75% discount, which is a great deal.

If you own the original version, you can probably skip this unless you're gonna play the online multiplayer with friends who own the remasters.
If you don't own or haven't played the original version, then it's definitely worth getting during a sale.
The regular full price here in Norway for the game isn't actually too bad either nowadays.


Be warned though:
  • The game isn't open world except for freedrive which is a seperate mode where you can only drive around freely, no random ecounters, races or stuff like that is happening in this mode.

  • Limited selection of cars, limited color and decal customization and no body kit customization.

  • Limited progression based upgrades on the equipments that cop cars and race cars can use against each other.

  • Game uses Origin integration in terms of DRM and account linking, which many people are not fond of.
