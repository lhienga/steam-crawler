\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an arcade-like, rails-based racer like Mario Kart that adjusts opponent skill and placement depending on your relative position. There are plenty of brands and models in here, some of them quite good. Unfortunately it's clear the developers ignored each car's respective stats, clearly representative of brand agnosticism given various sponsorships, the usual things that ruin realism.

Remastered? No, it isn't worth buying if you own the original. They removed SOME unskippable fluff from the UI that made the original so unbearable, but MUCH of it remains. Marginal difference. I haven't noticed any other distinctions from the original...just another re-release to get your money.

Possible dealbreaker: You CANNOT stream this via Steam Link due to the modern DRM garbage integration requirements from other platforms (EA, etc). Controller input is not recognized between devices. Add onto that the additional difficulty of this game launching in pop-under mode, meaning it requires explicit focus each launch. Those two things rule out remote play in and of themselves.

Overall it's worth ~$10 if you've never played this title before, An engaging arcade racer underneath all the redundant layers from too many cooks.\t\t\t\t\t
