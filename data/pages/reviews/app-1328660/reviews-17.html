\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGrew up playing NFS: Hot Pursuit 2 a lot on the ps2, this is a very good continuation of that legacy with its dual racer and cop campaigns, emphasis on country driving in exotics (to differentiate it from Most Wanted's urban driving in tuners). The country driving is quite breathtaking, the large vistas are amazing looking and this game has an excellent auto-HDR implementation that looks amazing on an OLED.

One thing that a lot of people don't seem to be talking about is that this game is also an excellent spiritual successor to Ridge Racer 7 in its handling model and physics. This is kind of a double edge sword though because NFS from Underground to Carbon had its own very unique car feel and for it to drop that in favour of RR's feel might be disappointing to some. Although I'm very happy when dropping the iconic Underground to Carbon feel they did so in favour of something as niche and interesting as RR and not something more generic and dull. So Ridge Racer fans be sure to check this game out

The main issue I have with this game is its weird PC idiosyncrasies. Now it's not egregious but also not up to par with a good pc port.
-Windows volume has no baring on volume, you have to use the in-game volume sliders (and there is no master volume slider).
-60fps is max, no 120fps
-Playing in Borderless is more stable than Fullscreen (I got the occasional stutter in fullscreen)
-Input lag is kind of bad
-Has open mic on by default, no options to select source (awkward moment in my first multiplayer game)
-Annoying EA drm that makes first time start up a drag with login screens etc.\t\t\t\t\t
