\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, it’s not the fanciest remaster ever made. You might even not see a big difference compared to the original from 2010. On the other hand, this game is very cheap during sales and comes with all the DLC from back then. It works fine on modern hardware, it still looks decent and it is an absolute blast to play to this day. Hot Pursuit gets right to the point of pure arcade racing fun. No open-world busywork, no cringy cutscenes, no story. Just plain racing accompanied by a decent Metal/Rock soundtrack (well, debatable by how much you are into this or not). You can freely choose between being a racer or cop and level up both classes individually to unlock new cars and power-ups like boosters, nail bands and even EMP guns. Several event types gradually unlock. They range from pure racing, time attacks, duels and the main event: The Hot Pursuit where a group of racers gets chased by cops and even helicopters. Especially in these events, Need for Speed Hot Pursuit is a very action-heavy racer where you get rewarded for reckless driving, ramming opponents off the track or damaging or even destroying them with your gadgets. It is Burnout in disguise. No wonder back then Criterion was freshly put onto the NFS franchise and just stuck to their strengths. Unfortunately, we didn’t see that kind of road rage in the following NFS games. So if you are interested in some serious action arcade racing and missed out on this back then or just want to relive the glory days of arcade racing, look no further.\t\t\t\t\t
