\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's easy to understand why Hot Pursuit is the Need For Speed that got a Remastered version. It tried something different from its predecessors, and succeeded with flying colors. After messing up the remake of Most Wanted, a remaster was also probably a safer option for the publisher.

Now you have to know that Nfs Hot Pursuit is a very repetitive game, but the thing is, what you get to do over and over is exactly what a lot of us enjoy. Racing as or against the cops never gets boring and wrecking the other team is always satisfying.
To do so, you have over 140 events split between several categories. Both sides have Preview and Time Trials events, with the only difference being that you have to avoid colliding with anything or anyone as the cops, in order to not get time penalties. You'll take part in Interceptors and Hot Pursuits events to bust racers as the cops, and try to escape in those same events as the racers. The latter team also has classic Race and Duels events, as well as "Gauntlets" that can be seen as survival sprints where everyone is after you. Those can get really intense and I even caught myself leaning physically a few times to avoid a car, which hadn't happened in quite some time. That's a good thing !
The progression is done really well. You do your best in an event, get rewarded with points that level up your racer or cop level, and unlock cars and new equipments. This works in such a way that at the end of pretty much every race, you get a new car or an upgraded equipment that makes you want to keep playing to try it out.

The game looks good, as good as I remember, which means this version has been improved at least slightly. There's a cool short intro before every race to get you into gear, before seamlessly switching to gameplay and I haven't encountered any lag whatsoever.
In the actual races, the gameplay is fluid and doesn't make you feel like the only player since the cops really go for everybody and can even take out each other. This happened on many occasions and really makes the game feel more interesting and lively.

This kind of situation also happens online, and that makes it even more fun. Thanks to crossplay, the game is very much alive and I've found games even at the most unusual hours. You might not find the game mode you want, because the search splits cars into categories, but there is a Quick Race option if you just want to play. You will face really good players and the races can get really hectic, but you'll get the hang of what works and what doesn't quickly.

Despite some strong rubberbanding from the AI, Need for Speed Hot Pursuit Remastered is not a hard game and you'll get through the events quickly. You can count on 20 to 30 hours to do everything, and a lot more if you get caught up in the online experience or try to beat your friends' best times, which is where the real difficulty will lie.
All in all, it's a very fun time and will allow you to enjoy the online action with crossplay, if you missed the original ten years earlier. Easily an 8, as long as you know what you're getting into.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: It will be review of the game itself as I've never played Hot Pursuit 2010 before. I can't review quality of remaster and if is it worth to buy remastered version if you already have original HP from 2010.

I remember playing Hot Pursuit as a child and having a fun playing it. Of course it is more like nostalgia and one of the type of games I don't want to replay to break my memories of it as I am sure, that at least in terms of graphics it looks much worse than memories in my head. If I understand it correctly it is some kind of reboot of Need For Speed Hot Pursuit games - Driving model is a bit strange and feels like cars are glued to the road - first few hours I couldn't get used to it, but generally speaking it is definitely not simulation type of driving model - It's strange kind of arcade racing. Tracks, especially scenery is getting quite repetitve early on - I missed visiting some Cities during races as It was in previous Hot Pursuit games. Cars actually feels like you are driving a car and you can feel it's weight, especially when you colliding with other cars, regardless if it is AI racer car / Cop or just standard traffic one. Game is split into two carrer modes: Cop and Racer - I enjoyed Cop carrer much more than racer one and joy of shutting down racing or busting aggressive AI racer. I don't want to spoil too much, but generally I recommend playing it despite of fact of quite repetetive environment won't discourage you! Sadly couldn't check online mode to see, if playing it online is fun as seems like no one is playing it anymore (empty lobby when I tried to find a quick match)\t\t\t\t\t
