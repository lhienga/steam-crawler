\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStill fun to play and very arcade-like, and if arcade is something you enjoy then this game is for you. If you're looking for a storyline then this game will be very flat, as there is no story whatsoever in the single player ("career") mode. You have nothing to emotionally connect to or fall in love with since they removed the intro girl like Amy, Rachel, Mia, Nikki or Chase. I thought that was one of the best attractions of an NFS game and I preferred to see someone or see a face associated to a specific Need for Speed every time I launched the game!

Now there are no characters except the driver model and a cop model with their sunglasses, that's all the humans you're going to see. I don't miss the cheezy lines, characters or anime effects during races because that could have, ruined it for me personally.

Overall great mechanics, there's a certain traditional cop and racer aspect to it along with a modern twist that we've never seen before in the original Hot Pursuit or other NFS cop and racer games. I think the emphasis is more on the cars and tier of cars that you can unlock. Each of them sound different and feel different that adds tremendous depth to the game. I can recall and connect what automotive journalists have to say about each of the cars over the years, to the look and feel of the cars within the game, which is really impressive!

F*ck EA, f*ck Criterion Games, f*ck this game, Need for Speed is dead as sh*t to me.

I think this is the first time in about 3 years that i write a review for a game before actually finishing it. Because NFS Hot Pursuit Remastered already managed to p*ss me off so thoroughly after not even a handful of hours that it is basically impossible for this game to redeem itself in any way. But let’s start from the beginning:

After the „semi-realistic“ approach of NFS Shift 1 and 2 i was really looking forward to go back to the most barebones arcade-racer-basics possible with NFS Hot Pursuit and just put the pedal to the metal. What i can say about the remaster is: It seriously rivals NFS Undercover in being not only the worst NFS game, but also one of the most awful travesties of the racing game genre. It is stripped back down to the deepest roots of the series (and i really mean the NFS 3 from 1998 kind of roots), which in itself is anything but a bad idea, but it perverts this simplistic principle with a sh*tton of horribly bad design choices and very poor technical performance. There is no car tuning and customizing except for choosing the color of your car, there are no tournaments, knockout races or any other fancy race variations and there is no cheesy storyline. It is just you, your competitors and the police in races for first place or races against the clock and especially the later ones ruin the whole game with one thing: the penalty system.

When you race against the clock and you ram a car or hit the walls, you’ll not only loose precious seconds because of the loss of speed and control, oh no. NFS Hot Pursuit has the glamourous idea to add additional penalty seconds on top: even if you just sliiiiiiighty grace the tunnel wall with your spoiler, you’ll get 2 seconds added to your timer. Even if you just geeeeeeently touch a civilian car with the tip of your driverside mirror, you’ll get 3 seconds added to your timer. Seriously, this game would give you half a minute of penalty just for breathing too loudly if it could. Even if you memorize the race tracks like the f*ckin‘ Google maps algorithm chances are that the randomized traffic will screw it up for you anyway over and over again. Those f*ckers just keep popping up in the most inconvenient spots like in curves or right behind elevations, happy to add literally dozens of seconds to your timer. Needless to say that if you want anything better than bronze, you can basically restart every race against the clock after touching the wall more than once or twice, which will happen quite often in a racing game, especially when it has such horrible technical problems as this one.

Seriously, my system (i7-7700, NVidia GTX 1060, 32 Gigabyte RAM) greatly surpasses the recommended specs. I'm well aware that it's not even average anymore by today's standards, nevertheless it runs recent games like the Resident Evil 4 Remake on almost maxed out settings falwlessly with barely any noticeable framerate drops. Still this f*ckin' piece of sh*t has absolutely awful slow-downs, stuttering and framerate drops, even when i don’t max out the grafics. I tried turning shadows completely off, but the improvement in performance was marginal at best. In the end i turned all the grafics except for the resultion to the lowest setting possible in a desperate attempt to achieve an at least somewhat playable state of the game, but it STILL has infuriatingly frustrating stuttering that constantly keeps f*cking it all up. It’s one thing to set the time limits for a gold medal so strictly that they basically can‘t be reached without driving absolutely flawless plus taking all the available shortcuts possible. It’s another thing to additionally punish the player with penalty seconds if he or she does so much as to scratch his/her tire on the sidewalk. But to impose all of this on the player all while delivering such a literally p*ss-poor technical performance has certainly secured the devs at Criterion Games and especially Stellar Entertainment, who are responsible for this Remaster, a place in hell‘s farthest depths of eternal damnation, and they do nothing else than absolutely deserve it.

NFS Hot Pursuit Remastered is a serious health risk. It will raise your blood pressure to new heights and will make you grind your teeth down to the gums. It’s just downright ridiculous how frustrating, infuriating and generally unfun this game is. I won’t waste another single second with this piece of rotten junk and herewith I banish it to the depths of the purgatory it emerged from. Hear, hear, we have a new worst NFS game, well yes indeed, NFS Hot Pursuit Remastered is even worse than that shameful abomination called NFS Undercover. I’d rather play Autobahnraser 6 or Big Rigs Over the Road Racing for 6 months straight before even just looking at this piece of bullsh*t sitting in my Steam library and staining it like a f*ckin roadkill rotting in front of my garage. F*ck this game, f*ck it to hell, deinstalled it after not even six hours, i‘m going back and try the 2010 version.

EDIT:
Played the first few races of the original. Much smoother framerates and controls, but that was to be expected. Managed to get gold on the first try in races for which i didn’t even manage to get silver after multiple tries in the remaster. Nevertheless, even if it performs technically solid, Need for Speed Hot Pursuit is still a boring, mediocre, simplistic and outdated racer with barely any variety and some infuriatingly frustrating design choices. So yeah, original version = maybe a 3/8 at absolute best if you are Mr. Happy-Clappy who just loves everything and everybody. Seeing how mediocre this game was already in 2010 makes the technically unbearable Remaster appear even more unnecessary and embarrassing. Play Shift, play High Stakes, play Underground 2, play Hot Pursuit 2 from 2002, play any NFS game but this, you‘re gonna be much better off. There really was a time when this franchise was indeed the best racing game series around, but those days have long gone and Hot Pursuit 2010 surely didn't change the trend.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe last NFS game I played was 'Most Wanted', back in 2005. After that I never played a NFS game again. When the autumn sale came along I decided to pick up this title, because I missed the good old time I had with Most Wanted. The game itself is a good NFS game in that arcade style. However I really miss the open world that other titles had and even have and the cops pursuits after a race to shake them off...

The only negative thing I encountered during playing, that I completed all cop events and only was level 16, for racer it was better balanced in my opinion. I had to race some events multiple times to farm up the XP to reach level 20 eventually.

All the achievements can be completed in about 31 hours. The online events are okay, it's necessary to have some friends with this game if you want to complete all achievements.\t\t\t\t\t
