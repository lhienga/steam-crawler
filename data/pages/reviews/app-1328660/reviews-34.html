\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game brings back so many memories. NFS:HP was my first ever Need For Speed game. I remember feeling surprised when trying out other Need For Speed titles and learning that the cop thing was a one-off spin on the franchise and that NFS:HP was regarded as a knock-off NFS game.
The remake brings all of the DLCs together into a complete package (I wasn't even aware
that there were DLCs for the original). I do wish the remake brought more to the table though.
  • Community vehicle skins would be awesome.
  • The game is capped at 60 FPS like the original (wish it could be 144 FPS).
    =====================Uploaded to your wall =======================
Overall though, this title revives a game that I loved way back when and does polish some areas. It also runs extremely well on the Steam Deck with settings mostly on Max to Medium settings with a constant 60 FPS. Loading into the original NFS:HP on the Steam Deck was a pain as it made you login to EA's Autolog system every time and it could never remember your credentials. The remaster fixes this luckily now that your EA and Steam accounts can be bound together.

If you loved this title before, you'll like the remaster for what it is.\t\t\t\t\t

A Worthy Remaster
Honestly I had a blast playing Hot Persuit again!
There's some general EA Remaster jank but .. even the original wasn't perfect so I'm letting it slide.

EA Launcher Controversy!
A lot of reviews mention the EA Launcher being mandatory.
You indeed need it installed and it does have a tendency to sign you out after a few days of not opening it.
While this is annoying, it didn't at any point stop me from enjoying the game.
By no means is this an acceptable business practice though, remove the need for another third party launcher to run in the background and you'll instantly gain consumer satisfaction but .. since when does EA care about that?

Graphics!
While looking great, it's clearly a quicker remaster instead of a complete overhaul.
There's no lack in the feeling of speed, cars handle heavily but feel like they're spaceships once you engage nitrous!

What it "lacks" in graphics it more than makes up for in seriously kicking engine sounds
You feel the engines breathe, every revolution & combustion cycle leading to a howling V12 or roaring V8 putting down amazing power.

Gameplay!
Law enforcement or racer?
I've honestly always preferred playing as a racer in the NFS games when given the choice.
You're mileage may vary, try some of each and if you get bored of being on the dark side you can always switch to the blue side..

Honestly worth the money if you can grab it during any of the Steam sales\t\t\t\t\t
