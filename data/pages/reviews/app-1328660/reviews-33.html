This is the laziest remaster I've ever played. Some cars and music are gone from the original. I think because licenses have lapsed. You wont miss them. However, there are annoying mishaps in this release. Thumbs up because I cant do a sideways thumb.

Cons:

- 60fps in the 2020s. Just a bad oversight. lazy.
- Rubber-banding is the worst I've ever seen. Opponents will instantly catch up or stop at the finish line for you to catch up.
- Wheels work...barely. Fanatec wheels need to be set to 90 degrees to turn quickly. But a 1/3 turn of the wheel will make a 90 degree turn. You have to barely turn the wheel. Additionally, Fanatec pedals only work with the RJ12 attachment. Not the USB. XBox controller works best but there is a large dead zone and the cars hesitate to turn.
- Cars do not leave the road. There are a lot of hilly roads but the cars hug the ground. Environment has not affect on your driving. Cars seem to drive the same on rainy, wet, or dry roads.

Pros:

- It looks good. Graphics are great although, you are on rails and cant really interact with the environment (I guess this can be a con <shrug>).
- Using weapons on cops (and other drivers) is fun. This game has a lot of "cart" aspects to it.

I had fun but I'm glad it's over. I've got a few easy achievement I'll grind out. I wont play it again.

This release helped me realize that the NFS franchise after Shift 2 is made to be played with gamepads. I dont believe the developers beta test with wheels. Using the gamepad was easier and faster than a wheel. This is a shame because from Underground 1 to Shift 2, a wheel made the game much more immersive. Based on this experience, please DO NOT remaster my beloved Underground 1 and 2, Prostreet, and Undercover.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review will focus on the changes made from the original NFS:HP game, not the launcher:
Even though there are zero to no improvements regarding the graphics (still locked on 60fps => unacceptable) the new car customization options make up for it. It is now possible to choose the color and do car wrapping which was the severely lacking in the original game. They also added in a few new cars which is very refreshing but where this remaster really shines is the multiplayer. NFS:HP was always about multiplayer for me – the campaign has no real story and can therefore not be compared story wise with, for example, NFS:MW (2005). In multiplayer they added crossplay (! most important feature to increase player count) and new game modes, including interceptor for 8 players (called “Most Wanted”) and “Arms Race”, which is a normal race but with weapons!
For me, the remaster was totally worth it – but for anyone who plays this game solely for singleplayer, I think there are not enough changes to make up for the price if you already own the original one.\t\t\t\t\t
