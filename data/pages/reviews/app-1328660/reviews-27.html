\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis remastered version of the game is basically the same as the older version and it plays exactly as expected. The single player still holds up even to this day, while the multiplayer leaves much to be desired.

The racing online might have been fun in 2010, but it does not hold up to the standards of 2023. Regardless of what race type you're playing online, the absolutely terrible matchmaker and team balancing is still present from over 13 years ago (at time of review), with little to no consideration of player rank or the level of equipment that they have unlocked. Also in single player, you still cannot turn off the in-game cinematic camera during pursuits after all these years, which is quite disappointing.

The addition of cross-platform play and the new livery editor is nice, but when multiple cars are removed from the game along with the graphics looking about the same compared to the older now de-listed version of the game, it's not enough to justify a $30 USD price tag. Especially since this game now requires the EA launcher to play even if you buy it on Steam. One launcher is enough - thank you very much.

However, it's not a bad game whatsoever, just not well valued given it's age. It's still worth a buy if you can manage to pick it up on sale for $10-15 USD maximum. While it may not be the game it once was back in 2010, it still manages to be fun at times, though that can waiver heavily at any moment when playing online.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Game wants to F* you! By spawning 3 cars in a row, in perfect position to F* you while turning on a corner.

Or the classic of them all, coming out of a corner, just few meters away from the finish line, going pedal to the metal. Having enough time to get the Gold. A car is spawned exactly where you exit the corner, so you fly right into it, with no chance to dodge it. Then you're hoping to cross in-time for the Gold.
*P.S. gets silver*
This coincidental NPC Car spawning at the same spot has 77% chance to happen, after you restarted the race. Especially, the time challenges.

I love challenge, I am used to NFS trying to F* you with NPC cars, since Underground 1.
But in Hot Pursuit is simply ridiculous. So, I have to switch difficulty to medium, but then the time challenges aren't so difficult. Thus not an option for a player like me.
Everything else with the game is satisfactory.

The Handling is a bit clunky with some cars, which gives you the feeling of driving a massive bolder. When you're slightly turning at 120 km/h feels laggy(delayed car response, after key-stroke). Imagine how it feels, when going at 300 km/h. Nonetheless you could master it.
However you definitely have to get used to it. Sudden sharp turns, dodging incoming cars, if you're not prepared. If road is not wide enough. You will feel the wall, worst case scenario
you'll pinball yourself in 2-3 walls, until you're slowed enough to gain control.

Have Fun! ^^

RATING : 6/10
-1 for the Handling.
-3 for the fixed NPC car spawn.\t\t\t\t\t
