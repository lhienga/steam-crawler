\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a difficult review to write. I've always been a fan of the Hot Pursuit games. I've played through the original and bought this remastered because it was so cheap (2,99â‚¬) and had achievements.

PROS:

I like speed. The best part of this game is the feeling and the drivability (the drivability is also the worst aspects of the game but I will come back to that later). Driving considering the difficulty level of this game is for babies. Races, duels and racing against the clock are just fun (Anything that doesn't require contacting other drivers.). The lack of realism is sometimes quite nice, and if you don't own a steering wheel and pedals, you can play this painlessly with a keyboard or controller. Considering how much I've had fun with the game, it feels a little wrong to give a thumbs down, but I'm trying to give as realistic an image of the game as possible to those who haven't played it. The game looks pretty good, but unfortunately it's full of many problems so hear me out.

CONS:

The handling is like a bullet train. Arcade-style driving is kinda fun, but if you're a fan of simulators and realism, skip this game because it can cause mental health and heart problems. One of the biggest problems with this is that this is a "Hot Pursuit". However, the game's physics and drivability are not implemented with collisions and contacts in mind. The game is very buggy and it often feels like the game is working against the player on purpose. Even if an opponent or a police crashes, he may run past you already within the next ten seconds because the respawn happens close to the player and at full speed. Running into an opponent is also like rolling a dice in an RPG; damage occurs to one of the two or not at all, you continue your journey after the crash in the right direction or at full speed in the wrong direction. You never know.

The music doesn't work, but it can be heard for a couple of seconds in some tunnel before the soundtrack cuts off again. It's easier and better to mute the music track completely and leave Spotify playing in the background.

The game was very difficult to get started and many others seem to have the same problems. I finally got the game working when I changed the startup file to "run as administrator". The problems seem to be caused by EA's app.

The game is poorly executed throughout and every race is overshadowed by continuous cutscenes that pop up on the screen about crashes, the police waiting ahead and many other reasons. The first videos at the beginning of the game are still understandable when it comes to the introduction, but the situations they present quickly become pointless. Cutscenes also cause a lot of unnecessary crashes and driving past shortcuts.

The world is great, but maybe it's even too easy to drive. There is practically no need to press the brake in any situation. There are many cars in the game, but not all of them can be unlocked during the actual playthrough. After the last race, it is necessary to run again and again races that have already been won once (or twice).

Online achievements. I don't think any game needs online achievements.\t\t\t\t\t
