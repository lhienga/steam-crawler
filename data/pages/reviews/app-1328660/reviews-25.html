An updated review
If you want to enjoy this game casually, use Zolika patch, it will fix the "delayed" physics and 60FPS rendering. However if you used to original handling and/or want to chase leaderboard, better stick to vanilla behaviour (disable the vehicle response fix).

End of updated note


I am bit of conflicted whether to recommend this "Remastered" edition or not, but since I really enjoyed the gameplay. So a bit of forewarning.

tl;dr:
This Remastered version adds DLCs (events and cars) from Console release, some fixes for car engine sound (like the Cinque), Cross-platform multiplayer. customisable graphics setting And that's it.

The enhanced visual thingy is BS, even they downsampled the textures. even the 4K FMVs are trash given all the original assets are still hold up to this day (You can delete those 4K FMVs to free up spaces without any repercussion). Some bugs in this game to this day are left unfixed (Autolog stuff and the sudden spawn of traffic cars).

---

Hot Pursuit still remain one of the best fast arcade racer NFS game released (Rivals 30fps on PC is sucks) for modern era NFS games. Unlike Ghost Game brake-to-drift implementation, Criterion (before their merger by EA) made tasty brake-to-drift mechanics that satisfying and it is part of the gameplay, most of time you're going to drift (whether it is microdrifting or taking every corners).

Cars are balanced and meta cars are meta if you can handle them. Since this game does not have tuning system, your gameplay skills are the only ones that carry your arse on this game.

Multiplayer lobbies are populated whether it is the cops vs racers (Hot Pursuit mode and Most Wanted) or the racing. If you do not want to race, I recommend to stick to cops vs racers lobby as they're the most populated and always full.

For control, keyboard is almost equal to controller in term of speed unless you're doing Time Attack/grinding record, controller can be faster especially if you can microdrift and finer steering control.

The engine sound is crisp clear and really great. I recommend to lower the sfx level a bit unless you like getting deafened by the loud car sound.

Assuming you like arcade racers, you can have hundreds of gameplay time from this game.

Since this "remastered" also lazy jobs by EA but they also bringing Crossplay to this old game (yes, this game is 10+ yrs old), I'd give 4/10 for the effort, and 8/10 for the actual game.\t\t\t\t\t
