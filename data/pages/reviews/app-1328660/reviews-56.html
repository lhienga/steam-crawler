\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic racing game hidden under forcefully-installed EA middleware and a terrible job of remastering. I agree with everyone else on the front that this is a bad remaster. Though there wasn't a whole lot they did, it seems like everything they did was a downgrade to the original game. The biggest and most redeeming quality in my opinion though is the crossplay support. A lot of my PC friends aren't big on racing games and are against plaguing their computers with the EA App which is completely understandable. I got some console buddies with this game though, so it makes it easier on my end to be able to organize Most Wanted nights on this game, since it's such a fun gamemode.

In terms of gameplay, I think what this game sets out to be is near-perfect. The only bit of story you're given is the backstory of street racing on Seacrest County, which I think a region populated by the fastest police force is such an awesome concept, and all the car & equipment descriptions. The minimal writing aside, it's a very simple game, so simple that even the car customization is limited to just colors and vinyls, which would deter a lot of Black Box NFS fans like myself, except I see so much more quality in the rest of the game itself that it doesn't matter to me. Getting into the action itself, it plays so similarly to Burnout Paradise, down to the control scheme and everything. It's no surprise from a company like Criterion. I don't see anything wrong with it, as the gameplay loop of the Career mode has you levelling up your Racer and your Cop profiles through races, or busting suspects, which might as well just be the tutorial mode or grinding mode for the real meat of the game: Online Multiplayer.

Seriously, Most Wanted mode is one of the most fun modes I've ever played in any racing game. It pits a max of 8 players, one half being Racers, the other half being Cops, in the playground of Seacrest County. The Racers have to help escort a randomly picked Racer, the Most Wanted suspect, to escaping from the police, and that's by getting a far enough distance from them. It's like the car version of VIP from Counter-Strike, even more so when you consider that if one team gets busted or totaled, the match is over and one team wins. There's no time limit either, so you can have comedically short games, or adrenaline-pumping long ones, and you'll need to drive and deploy your equipment creatively to decide you and your team's fate.

That's all I wanted to say with this review. I recommend this game for anyone who doesn't care about their computers being plagued by the cancer that is the EA app (arguably worse than Origin if you ask me). If you hate EA spyware and have an online membership for a console, I'd say get the console version. Although the PC version does have the perk of mods, such as this one that restores the downscaled graphics: https://nfsmods.xyz/mod/2246\t\t\t\t\t
