\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great game from the Yakuza game universe. The sequel to Judgement is just as entertaining and exciting as the first and if you're a Yakuza game fan then this is another one that should be added to our game library. Like with the Yakuza games you have the main protagonist Takayuki Yagami a former lawyer not turned freelance detective who now finds himself in another job assignment that goes far deeper then even he first thought and closer to some people in the government then he suspected. The game allows and awards for exploration though it's not totally open world it still allows enough exploration in the city and other places to keep you entertained.Along the way you can do side missions for clients or help people in the city as well as do a few side games from karaoke to playing some video games.

The graphics are astounding and the sounds and music make you feel like you're actually walking around these cities in real life.The combat mechanics pretty much are the same from before or if you haven't played the first game but some of the Yakuza games then you're in luck as again the mechanics are pretty similar. Overall it's a great game that will give you hours of fun and entertainment as you roam around trying to find the answer to the main story and how much it affects more then just the people around you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs per usual I am writing this review right after completing the main story for Lost Judgement. I have yet to play The Kaito Files DLC, so for now this review will be for the main game and I will update it later for The Kaito Files once I have completed it.

Story:

The story once again was pretty great, it is not my thing to do spoilers so all I'll say is that it is a pretty slow burn all the way up to the end where the title "Lost Judgement" was definitely fitting. The characters were ones that I cared for and there were some genuinely emotional and intense moments. The detective side of the story was also interesting as the player pieces together the entirety of the story little by little.

I really enjoyed the lead up to the final boss fight, it ended up being very impactful and gratifying in the moment.

Gameplay

I will say the gameplay had some ups and downs. I will say for one so far in the Yakuze/Like a Dragon universe this game has the best combat mechanics thus far, greatly improving upon it from the first Judgement game. (I have still yet to play Gaiden and LAD8 but thus far yeah best combat for me). I enjoyed the return of both the Crane and Tiger styles, one for crowd control and one for individuals respectively. I also really liked the introduction of a third fighting style called Snake, which was more focused on parrying and dealing with those with weapons, I used this style most of the game because of how much I enjoyed it. The detective gameplay was alright, I enjoyed being observant when it was needed and being able to use all sorts of new gadgets. The one thing I did not really care for was the introduction to the parkour mechanic, it was just personally not for me, it just seemed like it was too linear and not really something you could be creative with.

Soundtrack:

As with the combat this game has the best soundtrack to date for me, (again I haven't played Gaiden or LA8 yet at this point so of course subject to change). I really enjoyed the music for the Snake style, as well as the music for the boss fights. The final boss music really captured the context and intensity of the moment and gave this spin off series a proper grand conclusion (for now maybe I don't know if they are going to make another one for this series).

These are my initial thoughts for now, I will update this review once I complete The Kaito Files.

EDIT: I have completed The Kaito Files.

I really enjoyed this dlc, it lasted about the appropriate length for a dlc, but the amount of emotional impact and story to it was still there and was just as good as the main story, if not better I think.

The combat was fun, I thought it was really cool that Kaito got his own two fighting styles, and each of them had their own banger soundtracks to them.

The story was truly incredible. Compared to the story of Lost Judgement I felt that The Kaito Files definitely had much more of an emotional impact. We got to see a different side of Kaito as well as some more backstory for him. I really enjoyed the final fights, without getting into spoiler territory I loved the polar opposite contexts and atmospheres they had, with the final fight's soundtrack actually hitting so hard and making the fight itself hit harder, and what was what I believe to be a proper conclusion to Kaito's story in this DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 100+ hours of playing Lost Judgment and 100%'ing it, I can safely say that this is probably the best game RGG has ever done.

Story
The story of Lost Judgment starts off with a seemingly innocent normal RGG game (totally normal) then proceeds to drop a critical point of the story right off the bat and it's Yagami's job to solve it. To refrain from spoiling any story elements, Lost Judgment's story leans toward the emotional side vs. Judgment. Despite that one meme of Yagami screaming, with context it was really heart-wrenching seeing what actually happened. The story revolves around between two beliefs that the protagonists and antagonists strongly and desperately want to believe in which I really like how it was executed, it shows how good and bad each side is but what is truly right or wrong? That's for us to interpret (to judge so to speak). While Judgment felt like a really good roller coaster ride for me (Despite my dumb ass spoiling the final boss), Lost Judgment felt pretty much the same for me but add a bit of emotion into it. I love Judgment's story even though there were some scenes that kind off not made sense for me or I just didn't like how it was executed, but Lost Judgment really kept me hooked despite doing a ton of side content before the really good chapters.

Combat
The combat system is a huge step up compared to Judgment's, everything in Lost Judgment's system is just satisfying to play, watch, even grind whatever you need in your TownGo. You can never go back to Judgment's combat system ever again unless you mod it. The combat system offers 4 styles, 2 returning and 2 new ones with one being a DLC.

Crane
Crane Style has a huge glow-up comparing to Judgment as the combat system removed the annoying guard bounce that no one wants and the combo speed in LJ is the same as the max upgrade in Judgment. With the addition of "Secret Arts", The "Secret Arts" makes it by far the strongest and best styles in the game as it amps up your combo speed (to all styles) which is great for juggling making it by far one of the best styles to fight enemies or even bosses. Crane counter is pretty neat too but once you bought all the +ATK skills, it just one shots any enemy.

Tiger
Tiger also got buffed as the 'Press Y/Triangle' to kill or do big damage is already built in which is great. Tiger style is just the same old style to just deal giga-damage to everyone. A nice addition to Tiger is the super armor it gives when you do the Y/Triangle combo in EX Boost, insanely OP and could really help you in the Amon fight. Tiger did get a somewhat new counter, the 'Dragon Crushing Fist' which is is basically just Tiger Drop but with a different animation. The "Secret Arts" is just more damage as you hit your empowered Y/Triangle attack which is pretty good.

Snake
This style is one of the most fun ones I've ever played. The Snake style combines two of my favorite move sets in Yakuza which is Tanimura and Shinada. Snake style offers a parry window similar to Tanimura's, a rush combo into a grab that kinda feels like Shinada minus the rush combo into tackle. Snake also gives you the ability to disarm weak enemies with weapons by grabbing them and hitting the QTE or doing an EX Action. It also gives you one of the best additions to this game which is EX Surrender. Instead of Yagami just beating them senseless, he can peform a fake-out so hard that the enemy just faints. It can only work on frightened weak enemies as it can literally one shot them. The "Secret Arts" isn't too special but it does help you not get ragdoll'd all around the map. The EX Boost makes you the ultimate defense machine as you can auto parry and not flinch. Parrying makes you hit them after the parry and Y/Triangle lets you tackle + grab whether the opponents standing or ragdoll'd. A defensive stance with very good offensive capabilities.

Boxer (DLC)
This style is okay, it's not as relevant as the other 3 but it is a fun style regardless. The way you unlock skills is to first unlock the 'Todoroki Boxing Gym' for School Stories and then spar opponents to unlock skills and buy them using money. After that you can learn each skill unlocked from the gym for 100 SP which isn't too bad when you compare the unlocked ones. The style reuses Kiryu's style switch to 'Rush' and it's pretty much literally boxing. The thing I love in Boxer is the 1-2-Launch it offers for an easy launch or guard break. The Mortal Reversal on this style is so satisfying to end any encounter or boss with.

Side Content
Lost Judgment's "School Stories" offered a lot of exciting minigames to play and to have fun in (maybe even rage at some). Dancing, Death Racing, Boxing, Mech Battles, Play VF5 and beating up High Schoolers (in-game), the return of Drone Racing, and other classic RGG minigames like Baseball, Mahjong, Shogi, and more. If you get bored or you just wanna complete some TownGo missions while you're in a middle of a chapter, or you just wanna have fun at school then by all means go for it. School Stories is an interesting system compared to the 'Friend' system in Judgment, while the 'Friend' side story was okay but it felt weird where you can just put it as "The power of friendship" sorta thing. "School Stories" in the other hand made feel more immersed as I play and as the story went on because some school stories were pretty long and it actually gets interesting here and there and of course a cameo group from Judgment.

Graphics & Audio
Lost Judgment did a better job with everything in graphics and audio. The graphics in this game really captures what
PS4's Judgment's supposed to be in the PC port. Judgment's blue filter after coming back really feels different comparing it to Lost Judgment. The story cutscenes, dynamic intros were all great. The lighting, the effects are really just better.

The audio in Lost Judgment is pretty much the standard in any RGG game. The OST is an absolute banger. Viper, Dig in your Heels, Unwavering Belief, even the battle style themes are great. Snake being my favorite one.

Final Thoughts
Lost Judgment is a massive upgrade in graphics, a better story (imo), the best combat system overall, OST is fire as usual. There was never a dull moment in playing and it was a fun ride.

Lost Judgment is a 10/10 for me and nothing will change my mind.

Saori is best girl.

SPOILER: My thoughts on the antagonists of Judgment vs. Lost Judgment
I really love Kuwana over Kuroiwa because of how it was shown throughout the game, we saw how he become the person who he is as we talk, drink, argue, and fight with and against him. Simply put, it's like I understand him a lot compared to Kuroiwa where I guess he's a corrupt cop with underground connections blablabla you get the point but he's of course the twist villain which is someone you wouldn't really expect at first while Kuroiwa is something suspicious already. In hindsight, I guess having guest stars is already an indicator that they could be an antagonist/protagonist lol. I guess I just have a thing where the antagonist is someone that has been with you for a while and in the end reveals that they were the villain all along. Also the 2 beliefs that they had, the Lawful Good Yagami vs. the Chaotic Good Kuwana really made sense in the end. Even Kaito mentioned about it, whether if there even is a right answer when both are doing something good in their own way. Kuwana seems to have a very convincing side as it what some of us would want to have in our lives, justice no matter what way its given. Despite Yagami mentioning Sawa-sensei over and over and over and over, it was his only piece that he can play against Kuwana and it goes to show that even though Kuwana is doing something good in his own way, everything he does comes with a sacrifice. \t\t\t\t\t
