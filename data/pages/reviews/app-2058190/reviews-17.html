\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really good ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ game. I've been a fan of RGG's work ever since I played Yakuza 0 back in 2021, which prompted me to chew through the remainder of the Yakuza series over the months thereafter. I had always heard about the Judgment games, being as much of a fan as I am, but I hadn't been able to get at them for a while, being a PC-only gamer. Having them come to Steam some time ago was really exciting, and I picked them up immediately. Both of these games are worthwhile for any Yakuza fan, and I'd really recommend them to just about anyone. They do the beat 'em up style combat better than most of the entries in the Yakuza franchise and with the series being so new, there isn't a lot of games to chew through, making the barrier of entry much lower. This is the second game of the series, and is seriously worth every penny. The story is immaculate, the combat is some of the best I've experienced in a game (aside from my precious Sekiro), and there is just so much to do in this game, as well as all the Yakuza games. I first beat this game some time ago. But recently I decided to take it upon myself to get this game to 100% completion, both for in-game and for Steam. This was the first time I had ever done this for a Yakuza game (aside from Gaiden, which was fairly easy, and I did most of Yakuza 7's trophies; but I had never done a 100% of the in-game completion list before). Safe to say, this is probably the last time I will ever do that. It took me almost 100 hours to get the job done. Going through the main story again, through the entirety of the school stories, the side cases, some of the gauntlets, and all of the different minigames as well as the challenges set forth in the TownGo completion list. All of it. I did all of it. Was it worth it? Yeah, kinda. But I will never do that again. The sheer agony of having to do the school stories (I'm looking at you, biker gang) for twelve hours back-to-back was a pain I never thought I'd experience. Having to learn koi-koi, mahjong, and all the arcade games I suck at was absolute miserable (except koi-koi, that was really fun). This game truly is a gem, alongside all the rest of RGG's games. Please, play this game's predecessor first then come back to this one, even if you've never played Yakuza. It really is that good. But, I will warn you now: only dare to 100% a Yakuza game if you have days upon days of disposable time on your hands. Only dare to do it if you are prepared to do all the worst challenges and minigames in these games because, I promise you, you will have to do them. It is a challenge not for the greatest of gamers, but for the most dedicated of them. That is my warning to you.
9/10. One of RGG's best works, and I really hope they can settle their legal issues with the IP and make a third someday.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: Overall a really solid entry in the series. Story drags a bit towards the end but features some very well written characters.

Gameplay wise it's mostly upgraded compared to Judgment, a lot less following random people around which I'm pretty happy about. The parkour and stealth mechanics feel a bit forced but I don't mind it that much since it's not too frequent. I won't comment too much on the combat system because I'm pretty bad at it but I think it's good enough.

The story is also intriguing. I think it dragged a little bit here and there, and some arguments between characters feel like they are repeating themselves way too much. If I'm being honest I think the previous game had a better story arc. However, I think the villains / antagonists in this one are very well established, probably the best in the entire series imo. Definitely a must-buy if you're a fan of RGG games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start with the good.
The story was interesting. It certainly was the main reason to keep playing, even if it were just the first half (after which I thought the game was over but it just kept going forever).
Another good thing is that something about the environment, the atmosphere or whatever it was had some sort of appeal, which had me launch the game time after time despite the many times I got annoyed.

Now, one of the main reasons I didn't like the game: The constant combat encounters. You know in Pokemon, how, when you walk through bushes, there is a good chance you encounter a pokemon?
Often you don't want to fight, but it just keeps happening. Luckily, you can avoid these bushes and walk out.

Well. Here it is kinda like that except you can't leave these bushes. There is encounter after encounter after encounter. You wanna go to building X? Oh, here is a random group of thugs. What, you dealt with them? Well here you go, a bunch of aggressive high schoolers. Done? Well there ya go, the next group is coming your way.

It really was a choir for me. I just wanted to see the story and move through the main missions, but these constant fights forced me to use the taxi to go everywhere. Which is quite expensive, and still had you move to locations.

Another really bothersome thing was the lack of a challenge. The game held your hand all the way til the very end. Combat was so simple, on the highest difficulty I couldn't be bothered trying to understand it remotely. You have different buttons, combos, stances... well I don't know what the stances were for, what they did, how to get them. I just mashed buttons and I won everything easily. There are many ways to grow stronger... well. I left that all aside. Button mashing and done.

It wasn't just combat. You are a detective. Except you, as a player, do none of that. There is no thinking involved. Trying to find evidence just meant clicking on stuff in a room. A stealth mission was literally: Walk to a square, get a pop up with the button to press, press it, wait, and then walk to the next square. It felt insulting.

The main reason why I wouldn't recommend the game: So the button mashing thing in combat was something I did in the first four hours or so. At that point, I finally encountered an opponent where I'd have to try a little. I died. And then I died again. So, I was like... okay. Not bad, I may have to try to understand the game a little now. And then came the pop up 'do you want to change the difficulty to normal'?

Every time I'd die, the game would ask me that. I got annoyed so much that I just accepted in hopes it'd stop, but no. It did not. It'd then ask to change to simple and during simple it still asks that....\t\t\t\t\t
