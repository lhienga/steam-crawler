\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf people say Yakuza 0 is the best Yakuza game, then this is the greatest game ever conceptualized by humanity. It's got everything.

Gameplay: Easily the best in the entire series. Lost Judgment's combat makes the rest of the series look like a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ tech demo. Each of Yagami's main 3 styles has more depth than any style in the series prior, and Boxer style is an awesome addition on top of them. On top of that, any enemy who has a presence in the story has a unique moveset this time around, unlike the first Judgment which suffered from a lot of copied movesets. The detective-esque minigames from the prior Judgment have also been improved as well, with tailing being actually somewhat enjoyable. The only part of the gameplay I don't like is the stealth missions. ♥♥♥♥ the stealth missions.

Plot: Without saying anything spoilery, the plot is bold. It focuses on actual issues in the real world and handles them with maturity. The characters are also all written extremely well and seem like actual people you could meet or hear about in the news.

Music and Sound: It's a Sega game. The soundtrack is not only phenomenal, but also extremely varied and has something for everyone. The sounds themselves are also great, with big hits having big impacts and small hits not deafening you. In addition, the English dub is incredibly well done and probably the best in the series as of now (though there's only 2 others to really compete with for now). I'd even say I prefer a good chunk of the English voices to the Japanese voices.

Side Content: Being a game in the Yakuza series, of course the game is loaded with tons of enjoyable side content. Though there are only 42 side cases listed in the completion list, this is only because there are 10 whole storylines through the introduction of School Stories, where Yagami becomes an advisor for clubs in the school and tries to solve the mystery of an anonymous individual called "the professor" who has been causing huge amounts of delinquency in Yokohama. Definitely some of the best side content I've ever played in a game, and probably the best in the Yakuza series.

The Kaito Files: An additional paid DLC that takes place after the main game, focusing mainly on Kaito and his past life. Even though it is much less serious and significantly shorter than the main story, it is still a very nice epilogue-esque addition to the game. His combat features two upgraded versions of Kiryu's Brawler and Beast styles that are both very fun to use. Though the DLC only adds 6 new tracks, each of them is just as good as any of the main game's music, if not even better. There is almost no side content with the Kaito Files outside of a superboss and some nice references. Also, Kaito's stealth is actually pretty okay. Still not that good, but it's not bad.

Final review:
Lost Judgment (Base game): 9.5/10, definitely worth the $60 if you're a fan of the Yakuza series. (Although you should play 7/Like a Dragon before this one, because Lost Judgment's plot has a lot to do with a significant event that occurs in it.)

Kaito Files DLC: 8/10, if you really liked the base game and don't want to wait for a sale, it's worth the full $30. However, for anyone else who just plays casually, I'd say wait for at least a 30% sale so you can get it for around $20 or less.\t\t\t\t\t
