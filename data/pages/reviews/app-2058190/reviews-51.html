\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really liked this game. The plot is very strong and the battle system is still fun. I personally like the blending between normal battle gameplay and detective gameplay. THe minigames and side content are great, especially the dance club and robotics club. The side characters and villains are as good as ever. Speaking of the DLC, I enjoyed the story it told, but the memory system for unlocking certain abilities to buy with XP wasn't very fun. I probably ran up and down every street in Kamurocho each chapter to search for them. A couple I had to look up a guide for because they were up high, not on the ground.

I also wish the DLC made more use of the main game's mechanics. It's really weird that we're given the drone mechanic that we only use once, whereas you're using it constantly in the main game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like Lost Judgment more than Judgment 1. I really do. But I don't.

The narrative in Lost Judgment is, in my opinion, much better than Judgment 1 because it doesn't try to make its stakes world-ending. Yes, there are high-level officials involved in Lost Judgment's plot, sure, but it starts off very grounded and generally stays that way. Yagami's story is very much Yagami's story and everything that he's involved in carries personal stakes first. And I am very much into that kind of story.

The combat is stellar too, basically improving on Judgment 1's in every measurable way. Now having three styles a la Like a Dragon instead of two does wonders for how you like to play. Likewise, the expanded "detective" segments serve the game well, with a lot of what would have been tailing and chase segments from Judgment 1 being replaced with stealth and Parkour segments.

But there are flaws, and they're pretty huge.

The first and most egregious of these is the locking of the main side story, the School Stories, behind minigame completion. To complete the School Stories investigations, you have to fully complete each of their respective minigames to their ends. This isn't too much of a problem with something like the Dance minigame, but it's insanely frustrating for the Robotics minigame. When I discovered I had to fully complete the minigames, I just gave up on the School Stories entirely.

Another flaw appears once the RK gang shows up. With every plot point that involves RK, the only fights you ever see are against hordes of RK members, that's it. The only two important characters within RK are the leaders, so there really isn't any variety in antagonists beyond those two and one other individual. One fight with RK in the middle of the game has you fighting the same RK leader boss three times in a row. Deplete their health bar, fight again. And then when you infiltrate a warehouse with them, you likely fight a hundred enemies in total over the course of the infiltration. It becomes incredibly boring.

Finally, the game does not play nice with automatic cutscene triggers. I ran into this at the very end, when the game explicitly told me to prepare before leaving. I set a waypoint for the item shop and proceeded to run there, only to realize that getting close to the cutscene trigger would, with no warning, send me into the final battle arc.

I still like Lost Judgment, and I'll be playing the Kaito Files at a later date. But I find Judgment 1 superior, mainly because it's less annoying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe combat here is unimaginably polished. I already thought the Judgment combat was insanely good, but this took it to another new whole level. I would go out of my way to avoid enemies in the first game, but I looked forward to kicking some ass here. Plus, the minigames! I spent so much time on the minigames alone! The robotics was so fun, including the motorcycle one and the rhythm game. Best minigames ever, man. I also ended up learning how to play Mahjong for the first time and I spent nearly one night just playing it.

While the first game was a pure crime thriller, this one's story is a lot more relatable and personal with some important social commentary about the pressures of growing up and dealing with bullying. This game also has one of the best antagonists I've ever seen. I won't name him to avoid spoilers, but he was a complex character I've come to love and sympathize with. I definitely understand why some people even ended up siding with him than Yagami tbh

Overall, this isn't just a game but an experience. I absolutely recommend it.

It also works great on the Steam Deck, btw!\t\t\t\t\t
