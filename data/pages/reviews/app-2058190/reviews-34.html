Overall I wanna say the combat is great, definitely a step up from the previous title, however a little bit bummed out that we had to wait till the end of the game to get some real combat in. The reason I am giving this game a negative review it is just because of the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ story. I am so glad they toned down the pursuits and everything else in this entry vs the first but man this story sucks.

Yagami is guilt ridden over the fact that he got that innocent dude free'd from that murder charge which ultimately resulted in that dude's GF dying. He feels as if he had somehow either let that innocent dude rot in prison or perhaps if he had found the real culprit he would be a better person. This ties directly into the story which is where I get really annoyed. Sawa this sawa that. She died and apparently if anyone farted in her general direction they had a hand in killing her and are therefore worse than a guy who kills for fun. Yagami's purpose in life at least in this game is to make sure relatively innocent people serve time and get punished for honestly completely justifiable actions. Remember kids what is the difference between a man who kills 1 person in vengeance and one who kills a thousand for pleasure? Nothing apparently.

On a much more minor point man these characters really don't know when to shut the ♥♥♥♥ up. It seems like everything is in the bag and everything is gonna work and then nearly completely unprovoked they go confessing. I'm like dude you just had to keep your mouth shut for literally less than 3 more seconds and you'd have won. Why are you now giving an essay?

Also I ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hate the cutscenes in this game. Specifically when it is recapping. It goes something like this.
Yagami: I believe this happened!
Others: What is your proof?
Yagami: Here is my proof!
Others: I believe you!
Yagami: I believe me too!
Others: We both believe you then!
Yagami: So to recap we both believe in the same thing!
Others: We both believe in the same thing!
Cutscene that restates that entire scene but more cinematic
Another series of dialogues
Cutscene to restate that
Then EnD oF cHaPtEr ReCaP!

Jesus ♥♥♥♥ you don't have to baby feed me all the details.

Listen we all know you're gonna play this ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ game. The fact is you probably already played Judgement, Yakuza 0-6, and Like a dragon. You're already gonna buy this game. Just if I had to rank this it'd be dead last or close to it in the history of this series. Wait for a strong sale so you don't get too mad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is easily the best combat in the Like A Dragon franchise yet. its so fluid and easy to grasp, my only problem with it was the amount of blocking enemies would do (mightve been cause i was playing on hard but still seemed excessive, almost gave me yakuza 3 ptsd)

The stories are interesting and varied and the side missions are as goofy as ever. Seeing Yagami and the gang take on more serious and constantly escalating situations is thrilling in a way the other yakuza games arent especially considering your playing on the side of the law and not against it.

Theres so much to do as well, the side missions in the school are plentiful to a point where its almost a problem (I spent a whole two to three hours doing nothing but side quest specifically at the school (that wasnt even ten percent of what the game offers regarding side missions) This brings up one of my major issues with the game in which a whole fighting style is locked behind a few hours worth of side missions which the side missions themselves arent available till you get a hour or two into the game. This means you have to wait after a hour or two to get to the side missions to even get to start to unlock all the combat this game has to offer. It seems rather unnecessary and could've just had the ability to go to the gym to learn these techniques available from the start instead of winding it so tightly around side missions which some players might not even bother with at all to begin with.

Other then that the character of Yagami himself is a good blend between Ichibans sheer optimism/positivity and Kiryus sterness and and bluntness, the side characters are also something truly great (Kaito in particular is a goofy but loyal partner and brings his own charm and flair while still standing on his own as a great character)

All in all this game is a solid 9/10 from what ive played

So far I'm on Chapter 3, I've forgotten what I'm meant to be investigating, my life is skate boarding now... There's a rhythm game, a boxing game, a fighting game, darts... drunken brawls... this game has everything.

As ever with RGG you will expect wonderful graphics, a great soundtrack, absolutely awesome choreography in the fight scenes (albeit OTT) and the writing! Good lord the writing in these games I've yet to find one release by this company that made me go "Huh... really?" or cause my interest to wane, and if you ever DO get fed up faffing about doing nothing you CAN draw yourself back in with the main story.

Will no doubt edit this when I finish it but as you can see 57 hours in and I'm only on Chapter 3.... SIDE QUESTS FOREVER! <3 I do hope RGG make more of this series. Also I'm not very good at "Action Combat" type games but it's accessible and forgiving enough so don't let that be a barrier for you. The story, side quests alone make up for that.

I cried. All you need to know is that I cried. I know a lot of reviews say the story is messy, aye it is a little, but...
