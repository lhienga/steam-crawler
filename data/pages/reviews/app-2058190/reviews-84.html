\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore saying anything else, if you haven't played the first Judgment before this, I highly recommend you do so.

Lost Judgment is an incredible sequel, improving upon almost everything the first game already did well. It is also, in my opinion, the best RGGS game to date, the Yakuza ones included. The story is amazing, the variety of minigames and side activities that feel compelling to do is astounding, the action-oriented combat is a lot of fun, and the soundtrack is also great.

Even though the price for this game is very high in my region, I feel like it was worth it. I'm not in the mood to elaborate further about the game's details, but it is truly a masterpiece. it has some flaws, but overall, I'm very glad I got to play this. It's almost a crime that it was console-only until recently.

If I had to rate Lost Judgment, it would be a 10/10. This game is truly something else. If you're not in the mood for the Yakuza/Like a Dragon games, at least consider giving Judgment a try, chances are you'll find them great too.

I really hope this series gets a third game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is easily the peak of Yakuza's beat em up combat system. The amount of options, air juggles, ground combos and wallbounce/bound combos are insane not to mention just the sheer amount of moves there are to use. This game takes the gameplay of the Yakuza series is known for and transforms it into something even better which is saying a lot because I've played and loved every game in the series outside of Isshin (Due to there being no english sub).

Story is also very good, presentation is up there with Like a Dragon and Yakuza 0 in terms of direction, characters and writing. On top of that it has a lot more of the goofy charm the Yakuza series is known for, the first Judgment game (which is also very much worth a playthrough) took itself slightly more seriously than the rest of the series but this game goes back to and fully embraces the goofy, wackiness of the mainline Yakuza series.

Side content in this one is super fleshed out and has some of the best presentation in the entire series, this game doesnt really have minigames so much as entirely fleshed out game modes. Theres skateboard competitions, Biker gang races where the goal is to knock the opponents gang off their bikes, and entire Fight Night style boxing game mode that genuinely fun and has some depth to it and thats just the tip of the iceburg when it comes to fun side stuff.

I actually have to give this game my seal as favourite game in the Yakuza franchise, it has the same if not slightly higher level of presentation, writing, characters and story as the other best games in the franchise (Yakuza 0, Like a Dragon and Kiwami 2) but it beats those other entries out with its incredibly deep combat system and genuinely well presented and consistently fun side content.\t\t\t\t\t
