The Combat

Lost Judgement's combat is godly. They improved many things, like the combat, from the Judgement series. Yagami has 4 styles now, and they are the: Tiger, Crane (These 2 are in the original judgement), and the new Snake, and Boxer Style. Tiger style is still an extremely good style against a foe; however, they nerfed flux fissure obviously. Crane style got an extremely helpful buff from Judgement since every style has their own "skill chart", making the once rather universal crane (Most of the skills are universal skills), to a better crane with actual style specific moves. Crane also had received the holy power to juggle enemies by countering, and getting a very BIG speed boost (This speed boost is needed for combos) if you perfect dodge. The Newest Style, Snake Style, is a defensive style focusing on grabbing, disarming, and agony. The other new style is Boxer, and Boxer is obtained by uhh the Boxing Minigame in Lost Judgement. I do not use boxer that much; however, its an actual balanced style. These styles symbolically go along with Chinese Shao-Lin Kung fu, Crane, Tiger, Snake, Leopard (probably boxing), and Dragon (Kiryu). Now for the actual combat, Yagami has the ability to do a Tekken on enemies and Juggle them. Juggling is VERY easy in Lost Judgement, and because of that the RGG devs have added a damage resistance mechanic the longer you juggle them making you do less damage. The particle effects are very exaggerated; however, it looks so look good. Overall, Lost Judgement's Combat is an absolute masterpiece

The Prologue (Spoiler-Free, until the last paragraph)

My personal opinion on Lost Judgement's Story is that it is less good compared to Judgement, but then again Lost Judgement has a giant web. The game starts off with a standard scam case that Yagami, and his partner, Kaito, are working on. The prologue just introduces you to mechanics that are like ditched right afterwards. Yagami tails a man named Kosuke, who is the person who is scamming your cilent, and this Tailing mission is like the only tailing mission in the main story. During the tailing of Kosuke, Yagami gets caught up in trouble by some gangster, and has to fight them in which Yagami wins but loses sight of Kosuke. Yagami does eventually find Kosuke, and tails him into a convenience store where you take your first and only photo ops mission. Afterwards, Kosuke goes into a building with many guards, so Yagami has to do parkour in order to get in the building without being detected. After Yagami gets into the building, he must sneak around avoiding guards, introducing the stealth mechanic to some main story missions. Eventually Yagami reaches the top floor where Kosuke is located, and turns out he was gambling away the Cilent's money. Yagami waits for Kosuke to leave to have a chat with him, which quickly turns into an altercation with Kosuke using Mine's moveset from Yakuza 3... Yagami beats Kosuke up, and Kosuke runs away introducing the chasing mechanic. Eventually Yagami catches Kosuke, and they go meet the boss of Kosuke where they don't have a civil discussion. Once Yagami beats up the crooks, and finishes the scam case the actual story of this game picks up.

Yagami is soon brought back to the dangerous cases, and he is embroiled with a case with so many strands that it had created a web. Kuwana, a former teacher turned handyman, had created this convoluted spider's web, and his story is one of vengeance against the Japanese Justice System since they had let bullies walk free after they pushed student's to suicide . Yagami, and Kuwana are basically like mirrors of each other, one of them seeks justice through the means of the justice system; whilst, the other seeks their own justice through murder. They both rival each other's power with their own techniques. The spider of the web, Kuwana , had entrapped many former bullies to helping him commit murder as a twisted view of revenge... Lost Judgement has one of the best stories in the Yakuza series; however, I still prefer the mysteries, red herrings, and the gruesome case of the Mole in Judgement.

Lost Judgement is seriously one of the best games that RGG had made, and I cant wait to see more of the Dragon Engine combat in Like A Dragon: Gaiden. The story is very good; however, I still do prefer Judgement's story over it since its more life threatening to Yagami though Lost Judgement's story is still really good compared to all the other RGG Games.\t\t\t\t\t
