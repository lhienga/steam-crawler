\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother solid entry from RGG studios. More of what you like, although the detective aspect seems to be less prevalent this time around. Sure, you still tail people (which still sucks), go over evidence and such, but it's not nearly as often as it was in Judgement.

Combat I'd have to say feels like it has improved. It still has that classic Yakuza/Judgement feel, but this outing, Yagami feels like he packs more of a punch. The addition of the Snake style really steals the show in my opinion and really feels like all you need honestly. Crane and Tiger are still viable, but Snake seems to outclass them in every way. The boxing style is ok, but It didn't bring a lot to the table other than really quick dodges and punches. Effective? Sure. Fun? Not really. It gets old quickly, and you'll probably find yourself going back to Snake.

The game is jam packed full of extra stuff to do as per usual. Your favorite mini games are still there, with some new additions as well. The school stories require you to partake in the mini games which aren't all that bad, but they get repetitive very quickly. The robotics mini game is really cool in concept and works OK, but if fleshed out a little bit more, it would have been way more enjoyable. It quickly starts to feel like the school stories mini games feel like a chore to progress though which is a negative in my book. However, you do end up learning some helpful skills from them.

The story is not one of the strongest outings from RGG. Is it good? Not really. It's not really bad either, though. I'd say its about on par with Yakuza 3 or 4 as far as plot. It isn't bad, but it doesn't stand out as special either.

Familiar faces return which is always a welcome sight in these games. While I don't mind Yagami, I don't find him to be nearly as interesting as a main character compared to the other characters we've been introduced to in the Yakuza universe. He can hold his own in a fight, and has character development, but personally, I don't find him as likeable as Kaito.

Speaking of Kaito, the DLC is pretty fun. Again, not an amazing story, but it's decent enough. There's 4 chapters to get though, and maybe adds an additional 6-8 hours of play time if you try to do all the extra stuff. Kaito will bring you back to playing previous Yakuza titles, as he's essentially Kiryu with his brawler and beast styles. Combat feels super fun as Kaito because you're once again a walking tank.

Overall, I recommend. If you're a fan of these games, you know what you're in for and you'll get your money's worth. If you are on the fence, then wait for the sale. I'm confident most people will enjoy the game, just don't expect it to be Yakuza 0 good.\t\t\t\t\t

The gameplay is (somehow) even better than judgments, the fast swapping styles is super satisfiying and they added a third style (snake) and a fourth one if you count the dlc. The side content well... theres A LOT, even for a yakuza game theres a lot, and its mostly quite fun to do.

The characters are the strongest the yakuza series has ever had, having my favourite character in any RGG Game (who I wont spoil) and just generally likeable new characters.

That being said the story can best be described as... confusing. It feels as though they tried to have this huge grand plot setup and it fell flat on his head, and the only thing reviving it are its amazing character writing and villains. not to mention the beggining chapters of the game are pretty boring and extremely slow paced that it made me feel like im playing yakuza 3 again.

I would still recommend this game seeing as how much fun you can have with the gameplay aspects and how much enjoyment you can get from the characters, however the story is not very good.\t\t\t\t\t
