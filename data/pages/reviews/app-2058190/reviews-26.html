\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile a holistically giant leap in quality from Judgment, the game still has that clunky, grindy AA-budget charm that RGG is known for, despite being a very well-renowned and established studio at this point. While I don't necessarily consider this a negative—enough to leave a negative review alone—it feels like this spin-off really exposes just how buggy and unfun RGG's games can actually be, and how much they don't want to deviate from the main series. It also feels as though this series suffers from a lower budget and expectations.

Lost Judgment starts off with a strong story that quickly becomes predictable and boring, and loses its edge due to RGG's ever-present desire to keep everything tethered to the Yakuza story experience. The supporting cast that helps in Yagami's journey barely develop at all as characters and feel hollow and incomplete in the presence of the main character's bland and neutral existence, especially in the presence of Kaito. This series was better off being a single game.\t\t\t\t\t
