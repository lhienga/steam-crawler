\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary:

Better more fluid and faster combat compared to most game made by RGG studios, but the game suffers from too much of a focus on quantity instead of quality, with a story that has terrible pacing and an unsatisfying ending.

Upgrading skills:

1. Too big and drawn out, forcing you to grind a lot to become stronger and to unlock quality of life features.

Combat problems:

1. Getting stunned and knocked to the ground takes the player to gain control.
2. Grabbing an enemy while they're attacking makes it so your grab goes through them. A problem that happened in Yakuza 3 and Yakuza 4.
3. Hitboxes of enemies sometimes would hit you from far away, even hitting you when you're behind them.
4. Badly balanced fighting styles, with Crane being very overpowered, Tiger being pretty good, Snake being alright and Boxing being pretty bad.
5. They literally locked a fighting style (Boxing) behind a DLC.

Characters:

1. Yagami felt more of a stereotypical detective thant an actual character with depth to him like in Judgment.
2. Most of the characters too me felt too uninteresting and bland.
3. They ramble on for too long before finally mentioning information relevant to the story. They also love to bring back past events from the same game or from Judgment multiple times.

Story:

1. Too much expository dialogue.
2. Pacing was too slow, a lot of the interesting parts of the story where just spoon fed to you via dialogue making the whole waiting unrewarding.
3. The main mystery of the story was too obvious.
4. A lot of the reveals revolve around someone giving you paragraphs of them admitting to the truth, while not showing you what happened through the use of cutscenes or flashbacks.
5. Plot and conflict drags on for too long.
6. Constantly being forced to go from point A to point B to progress the story and substories. A lot of the time it just revolves around talking to someone in person instead of through the phone, just to be forced to travel to another location again.

Sub stories:

1. Too serious and it has less of that iconic silly humor.

Mechanics from Judgment:

1. They brought back those unfun and extremely tedious mechanics, like lockpicking, chase, trailing missions, finding clues in first person (Revolves around moving your camera around until you spot something),
parkour (Where it sends you back to the beginning EVERY time you fall, and it's very difficult to not accidentally fall off)
and sneaking where the AI is either too stupid and slow, or they can spot you while having their backs to you.

Robotics:

1. It's just a rat race to on who can connect and place their blocks on the opponent's territory first.
2. Being forced to grind to obtain materials that can only be obtained by specific levels from the robotics minigame.
3. A lot of unskippable cutscenes. Building ONE part? unskippable cutscene. Starting the same level for the 10th+ time to obtain materials? unskippable cutscene.
4. It mostly revolves around building your way as fast as you can into the opponent's territory, which get stale after doing the same thing more than 20 times.
5. Near the end you start running out of funds to upgrade since it only gives you 30k yen every 5 games, which forces you to play the same levesl over and over just so you can finally upgrade after also grinding to obtain the materials.
6. In the final fight, the game forces you to use a robot that is inferior to the ones you can build at that point of the game.
7. Whenever you're tackled by a fortress robot you literally can't do anything until it stops pushing you.

Dancing:

1. More difficult thant Haruka's dance mini game in Yakuza 5, but has less depth and less fun overall.

Boxing:

1. Locks moves from a DLC fighting style behind it's tedious progress system.
2. Annoying stamina system that constantly slows down the fights or makes it very difficult to win unless you grind the same opponents over and over.

Mini-games:

1. Even though there's a lot of minigames, most of them barely have any substance, it's more about quantity instead of quality.

School stories mini-games:

1. To unlock more you have to play the horrible ones like Robotics and Boxing.
2. The game constantly interrupts your progress by forcing you go from point A to point B and back to talk to some random person.
3. You also get interrupted because of the game forcing you to level up a specific stat from the "Yagami's leadership" stat pool which also forces you to play minigames you don't care about to be able to progress the ones you want.
4.

Dating:

1. The dating aspect of the game has improved a little bit, but it's still very bland and it's now locked behind a pay wall (DLC), which is pretty greedy in my opinion since it was free in other games like Judgment and Like a Dragon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJudgment was a pretty good game, but wow, this is such a massive improvement over the first that I can't see myself going back to it after playing this. Other than having a story that is slightly better paced, there is nothing JE does that LJ doesn't do a 100x better.

The biggest improvement, by far, is the combat system. After years of playing through the Yakuza series, I can finally say there is a Yakuza game whose combat system has surpassed Yakuza 0's. So much more fluid, so many options, no more mortal blows, the returning styles got major tweaks and the new styles are fantastic in their own rights, and being able to mix them up and juggle enemies like it was Tekken is exhilarating. From this point forward, every beat-em-up style Yakuza title should strive to reach this level. If you can't juggle enemies in newer titles, it's immediately inferior to this game.

The story is also fantastic, and while it's not quite as compelling as JE's story was, LJ more than makes up for it with who is probably the best antagonist in the entire series. Yakuza games have always had a tendency to adopt a very black and white moral view throughout their storyline, and this is the first time we've gotten such a morally gray character and dilemma in the series, I loved it. All the new characters and antagonists introduced are so memorable.

Technical aspects are also vastly improved. This is, without question, the best looking any game in the Yakuza series has ever been. The Dragon Engine has been perfected so that the prerendered cutscenes look worse than actual in-game cutscenes, and the lighting has also been improved drastically (especially from Y7's awful lighting). The soundtrack is great, and the new QOL improvements are welcome. I can't believe it took this long to be able to fast travel from anywhere.

If I had to point out some flaws in the game though, it would definitely be the school subplot with the MRC, because some of the minigames are definitely better than others. Boxing, robot club, dancing, girl's bar, they were fun and interesting. The others, not so much, and some of them, especially the biker race, just got really repetitive and frustrating. The substories were also pretty lacking for the most part although there were still some wacky and hilarious ones. However, I'm really glad they removed the text messages about the Neo Keihin Gang, that was one of the worst parts of the original JE because it was so annoying.

The flaws overall are minor however, and this is easily one of the best games that RGG has ever put out, and I genuinely hope a third game is in the works somewhere down the line. Powerful story, memorable and interesting characters, a fantastic antagonist that acts as a dark mirror to the protagonist, an extremely fluid combat system that makes me want to engage in random battles instead of running away, and some great minigames, this game has almost everything I could ever want in an RGG title.\t\t\t\t\t
