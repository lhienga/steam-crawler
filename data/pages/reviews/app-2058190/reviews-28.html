\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI HATED Judgment with a passion, but I can say that Lost Judgment does things much much better. Story is extremely interesting, the changes to gameplay is exactly what the game needed. It's extremely satisfying to switch between styles to rack up the combat different bonuses and juggle enemies around. The physics engine works wonders for the combat as well.

Still, the game still really likes to switch to having you just look at things, follow targets, walk a dog, hide from enemies. Basically, things that retract from the amazing core gameplay and story. Yet, it does it to a MUCH lesser extent than the previous game in the series, so it's a minor issue. Much of the side stories and minigames are also moved to a separate linear minigame-plot, meaning that the side-content is more of a grind than a natural distraction. This story is interesting, but there is a lot of build-up that not many have the patience for, especially if there is a certain mini-game you don't like. I pushed through it and liked the pay-off, personally.

The flaws it has are minor. Easily one of RGG Studio's best titles.\t\t\t\t\t
