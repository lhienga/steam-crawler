\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHot take - I actually wasn't a massive fan of Judgment. The story wasn't super gripping (entertaining for sure, just not quite my speed) and, while I loved Crane Style, Tiger Style didn't feel very good to use in my opinion, so the combat didn't have quite as much variety as I was hoping for

That being said, THIS is one of the best RGG games without a doubt! The story is absolutely incredible with great pacing from start to end, the characters are deep and impactful, and every single combat style is so much fun - Tiger Style was actually my favourite in this one!!

I also found myself really enjoying the School Stories side content. Each minigame was a lot of fun (except for the early stages of the Robotics minigame, but once you get the speed upgrades it starts to feel a lot nicer to control)

I even ended up really enjoying the Kaito Files side-story. It was nothing life-changing, just a very engaging story with some really touching emotional beats. I've always really enjoyed Kaito's character so seeing his story explored in a bit more depth was very interesting to me

Overall, this is one of the best experiences that this studio has to offer right now. If you're a fan of the Yakuza games, make sure you don't miss this\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOf all the Yakuza games and their spinoffs, I'd place Lost Judgement in the top 3.
The combat is tight and exciting. The characters are well-developed (many thanks to the previous title, although even the new characters are very well made), and the action is intense. Unlike a lot of the Yakuza titles, the plot makes sense and is relatively easy to follow, even down to the minor details.
In plot context, this takes place following the events of Yakuza 7/Like a Dragon and Judgement, so the former to avoid spoiling that game, and the latter to properly appreciate this one.

The game is set in the Yakuza franchise, but it is more or less a soft reboot. There are slight callbacks to earlier games in the franchise, but you don't need to have played all the Yakuza games or even the first Judgment to enjoy and understand Lost Judgment.

Lost Judgment, shortly
Lost Judgment is, not in particular contrast to other Yakuza games, a beat em up, but this time with detective elements such as tailing and observation to pick out as evidence later on.

Pros & Cons

Pros
-Excellent soundtrack. It feels modern and fitting to the game's period.
-Graphics. The Dragon engine looks good, even if combat physics might leave a bit to be desired. However, performance might be an issue even on lower settings due to how demanding the game is.
-Recycled as Kamurocho may be, it is still well updated.
-New bigger city to roam around in. However, at times it might feel empty or less alive than Kamurocho. Still refreshing, especially having played through all mainline Yakuza games.
-Combat is refreshingly done and felt nice, especially after the heavier combat of the earlier games. Way faster and encourages style-switch comboing.
-The story is actually relatable, yet still manages to even at times feel like well-written horror.
-Characters in the story feel like real characters. A clear antagonist who is obviously a sociopath and almost clearly in the wrong, can still feel like he has ground to stand on to justify his actions and as if he has a heart, haunted by his past who still owns up to his mistakes.
>Two characters simply talking to eachother in a room could never have been better.
-Availability and amount of side content.

Cons
-May be a bit long-winded if you're not feeling patient. The game takes its' time with introductions to side content.
-The detective-spot portions want you to spot everything. Boring and annoying.
-Difficulty spikes at random. Some enemies do ridiculous damage - more damage than the level's actual boss.
-Unlike previous Yakuza games, dying twice and lowering difficulty is permanent for your savefile.
-ClichÃ©s and tropes as with previous Yakuza games.
-At times plain unrealistic, taking leaps of faith (and logic) for the story to proceed as planned. I was purposefully picking the wrong, outlandish or otherwise stupid options in the "present your evidence" sections, and I accidentally picked the correct option. It's... a strange twist, to say the least.
-AI allies, which you actually have in this game as opposed to every Yakuza game, are downright useless.

Gameplay
Hand-to-hand combat based with detective aspects. Leveling system grants points per mobs killed / bosses beaten. However, you get barely anything unless you farm mobs or otherwise grind harder stuff for skill points.

Graphics
Very power-consuming and can make your computer a space heater if tuned too high. Far more realistic graphics with more detailing.
Animations or poses can be clunky, though.

Soundtrack
One of the best I've heard in a game.
It is grandstanding, but it succeeds at it.
The game's main villain has, at first, a dark synth-esque fight track that lets you know there's something more lurking underneath, and the finale track for him is... something you should play the game for.
Bosses in general have fitting tracks. A knife psycho has a track with Psycho (1960) callbacks and an elegant piano to fit his personality. A scrappy brawn-based boss has a suiting electronic track to go along with a Scarface-like personality and upbringing.

Conclusion
Lost Judgment is a wonderful game and an excellent step away from the heavy-to-fill shoes of Yakuza's earlier protagonists. Despite it's story and gameplay shortcomings, it remains a very enjoyable experience you'll want to see through to the end and to see it's contributions to important societal questions as much as be able to do ridiculous heat actions on random Kamurocho inhabitants.\t\t\t\t\t
