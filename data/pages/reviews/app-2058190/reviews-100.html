\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis might be the darkest of the RGG/Yakuza games yet, to the point where it gets somewhat uncomfortable to ignore the main story and go about doing wacky side quests
The main mystery is incredibly intriguing however, and the main side activity, the school stories, adds an extra layer of mystery, which works very well for this game
Other than that, it's classic Yakuza, just in a grand scale
Out of the realtime combat Yakuza games, this is easily the biggest, and is chock full of things to do all over the two towns of Kamurocho and Isezaki Ijincho (A criticism I made on the previous Judgment is that being limited to Kamurocho hampered the game, but this game counters that very well)
The all new Seiryo High School increases this a bit further
As of the time of writing this review, I haven't finished either story yet, but looking forward to finishing sometime soon

Editing after completing main story, school stories, and 28/42 side quests:

The main mystery was incredibly compelling and comes to a believable resolution, but once the core points of the mystery are solved, the main story falters.

Yagami's obsession over BUT WHAT ABOUT SAWA-SENSEI?! turns him into a bona fide hypocrite who criticizes others for trying to invoke their own justice at the cost of others' sacrifices while he himself does the same thing. Ultimately, nothing is really settled, and Yagami's solution is to fall back on the very same broken system that caused everything to happen in the first place, with an assurance that he'll protect those who fall through the cracks, which is downright laughable.

The school stories storyline is the other major story content in this game, and is something fresh in the context of RGG Studio games. This storyline tries to tread the fine line between the craziness of the side stories and the seriousness of the main story, and the results of this are rather mixed. The climax has an anime-ish SEISHUN! POWER OF FRIENDSHIP! type of feel to it, and I'm not sure if that really works. Still, the mystery of it all is very compelling, and made me want to see it through to the end, playing through all the minigames to get to it. Dancing, robotics, street racing, boxing, it all adds up to a massive amount of variety, giving you so much to do in the course of this storyline.

Some of the minigames did feel boring, however, such as the Girls Bite minigame, and it felt like it had to be done too many times to get to the end of that particular questline. Still, it's a minor blemish, and you come across several rather colourful and entertaining characters through it all. It's a shame most of this story is unvoiced. Would've loved to hear Amasawa Kyoko talk a bit more.

Oh, and I missed something important when I first wrote this review: TAILING MISSIONS. This was a huge annoyance in the first game, and luckily, the gameplay itself has been improved slightly, but more than anything, the number and frequency of tailing missions have been reduced drastically. If there was criticism about this from players back then by any chance, the devs clearly listened.

In the end, Yakuza 7/LaD remains the best Yakuza/RGG Studio game to date in my mind.
Lost Judgment does quite a few things right and is an overall improvement over Judgment and older Yakuza games in general, but the main story falters in the end, and the characters of 7 are more entertaining in general, giving 7 an edge over it\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, RGG, you've done it. You've actually developed a game to surpass my favourite game of all time, and that was also by you, in the form of Yakuza 0.

Lost Judgment is the long-awaited sequel to Judgment, two games that I and probably many others thought would never see on Steam. But here they are and damn are they worth the wait. As good as Judgment is, it is a rough-around-the-edges mess with a good story and (in my opinion) an even better story hidden within in the form of School Stories. What do I mean by rough-around-the-edges? A lot of new 'features' from Judgment that makes the story borderline annoying to play through. Tailing missions have improved for the better in theory, allowing you to blend in at any time when your target looks back at you, and you can finally take cover anywhere. However, it is still tedious. Other new features include what I would call basic platform jumping and climbing segments, where you have a stamina meter. Oh, and the stealth missions, which I normally suck at and hate, but the game holds your hand a little too much, forcing you to take cover at certain areas and even where to throw distractions. All these half-baked ideas are mashed together and somehow... work? I can't really explain it, but every moment you have to do these is somehow understandable as to why and never feel like an uneccesary chore.

Combat is, well, like an RGG game that isn't Like a Dragon 7. And there are three battle styles (+1 through dlc) and each feel fantastic, with the kind of crunch you expect from an RGG game. Absolutely terrific.

As for the story, this is probably the best one I've played through. Despite an early warning that it would involve suicide and bullying, two things that went through my head in my teens, turning me off to the story from the start, I was eventually drawn in to the intrigue and plot twists, and my favourite antagonist to date. Trust me, I won't spoil anything because the main story is great.

The best part however, is the School Stories, as you investigate shenanigans involving the high school in Ijincho. Each part unlocks different minigames, and events, as well as a well-written if a bit sappy story.

And now, the negatives, and none of them relate to the game itself.

- Denuvo. Just because I play games with that piece of anticonsumer garbage in them doesn't mean I like it. And I will ALWAYS call out SEGA as long as they continue putting this garbage in their games.

- DLC. Yes the master race gets all bar the Kaito Files with the base game, but it's still ridiculous that items that make minigames pathetically easy as well as locking THREE OF THE FOUR girlfriends behind a paywall for PS4/5 versions is insulting. And if you think PC's getting away with it remember that Ishin is coming out at the same time as consoles. It's coming.

- Price. Come on SEGA, no way both games should cost over $100 SGD. And making the Kaito Files 'free' when it definitely isn't worth forty bucks with the bundle is still stupid.

Still though, I absolutely recommend Lost Judgment. I enjoyed every minute of it and will continue to enjoy it a lot more.

Like a Dragon/10\t\t\t\t\t
