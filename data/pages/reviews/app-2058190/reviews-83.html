In my opinion, the setting of the story in this Lost Judgement was good. It was too good that I feared the next stories could not keep up with the quality of the current ones.
About battle/fighting in Judgement series, especially in Lost judgement, the development team had brought back the multiple battle styles which only existed in Yakuza 0 and 1 (and missed in sequence Yakuza series). Some might argue or prefer that the development team should focus only 1 style only (like Yakuza 2 onward), but for me it made the battle in the game boring, and not much of the thrill in style switching (which also made me hesitated to continue playing the Yakuza 4. 5, 6, and just go with Judgement).
I still wish that the development team could still bring the multiple battle styles to the next series of Judgement / Yakuza.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the best RGG game, or at the very least on the same level as 0 and 7.

The game starts off pretty slow, just to get you familiar with the game's mechanic first ig, but as someone who literally just played its prequel, it's slow as hell. Now, when the it reaches the main plot though, things got very interesting and I couldn't stop playing. But it also repeats one of the point the story is trying to make too many times in the dialogues between Yagami and one of the main character imo.

The chemistry in Yagami's group works a lot better in this game imo, maybe it's because the previous game set it up already. One of the main antagonists are a lot better fleshed out than other games, and the other ones are very charismatic and fun to watch.

The combat gameplay got even better. There are 4 styles, all of them are fun to play (except the DLC one if you don't do side story), there are so many Ex-actions and all are really cool to watch. Dynamic intros and action sequences are arguably the best in the series, the buildup, the music, it's all perfect.

The OSTs are very memorable, especially Viper and the last boss's theme. Most tracks are great, from the menu screen music to minigames ost.

So if you like Judgment, you don't even have to think once, just get this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t+ Fantastic gameplay, especially if you miss Yakuza 0 and Kiwami's 4 style combat. The distinctive styles for Lost Judgment are very fun and incredibly smooth. Snake style is a great new addition.
+Great story that is just as dark as the first game, tackling the subject of bullying. Despite thinking that the first Judgment game still has the best story that RGG has delivered, the story for this game is a close second for me.
+Fantastic new characters, especially the damn good antagonists that rival the first game.
+Along with Side Cases, we're also introduced to School Stories which serve as more centralized Cases with an ongoing story. Great characters and some good feel good moments in these.
+New activities are excellent such as Boxing, Battlebots, and Bike racing are a lot of fun.
+Music. Nothing much to say about this except that the music is always amazing.
+Kaito Files is short but sweet. Playing Kaito who is just a great character with a ton of personality was an enjoyable experience. Kaito's combat similar to Kiryu in Yakuza 0 and Kiwami 1. Introduced to some solid new characters and the story was self contained and pretty satisfying for how short it was.
+Get to pet cats and you get to walk around a doggo

Negatives:
-The detective aspect to the game is still lacking. Thankfully there are less tailing missions but the few that are still on here aren't very much fun. The investigations are pretty cookie cutter and have no real depth to them. Parkour was a new feature for this game but similar to everything else listed on here its pretty shallow.

Overall 9/10
This game excels with pretty much everything over the other beat em up games of the RGG franchise. I would consider this one the best of the bunch. Here's hoping we get a third installment for the Judgment series.\t\t\t\t\t
