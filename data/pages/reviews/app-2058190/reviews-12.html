plot: never have i been so captivated with an rgg game’s plot, so much so that i kept creating theories on how every plot line somehow weaved together, even in my free time when i definitely should’ve been not thinking about this ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ game — truly one of the most refreshing, complex and nuanced takes on the grey zones that arise from the law, and the differences between justice vs punishment. the mystery surrounding the story is easy to grasp and so fun to try to make of every clue that pops up. at times, i felt there were some sequences that felt a little out of place or not necessary to the plot at large, but i ended up having a blast going through the motions with them anyways, regardless of their purpose. i also (as always) really appreciate how rgg tackles grey issues, and how the solution is never just black or white. this game explicitly touched on that sentiment and it was executed so well that even i felt myself switching sides every other chapter and rooting for characters i didn’t think i should’ve been rooting for. the ending left me with a truly hollowed, empty gut feeling that i haven’t experienced since i finished yakuza: like a dragon, and i wouldn’t trade it for the world. i’m so glad this game exists. 9.8/10

characters: i mean cmon what’s not to love?!? i admit i might be a little biased with my love of the found family trope but this ragtag team of losers won over my heart last game, but this just solidified it tenfold. the characters introduced this game were absolutely fascinating to watch whether they were a poor school teacher or an absolute terrifying almost psychopath seeking what he thinks is justice. each character was given time to be explored (except maybe koda, sadly) and their motivations, feelings, and actions all made sense in their own contexts. this game introduces my favorite yakuza antagonist of all time, and some of my favorite protagonists too. yagami is never not a joy to play and his crew is just so heartwarming to watch pal around. i need a beach filler episode. 10/10

side content: oh jesus christ where do i even start man… there is SO much side content. so much. i don’t think it’s as much as other yakuza games but my god it feels like a dauntingly large amount of side content to get through, especially the school stories. not that it’s necessarily a bad thing, i’ve had so much fun with every minigame this game has thrown at me with it seemingly getting more goofy as time goes on. i imagine when i go on to 100% this eventually i’ll grow tired of filling squares with robots or boxing the ♥♥♥♥ out of people but for now i’m having a blast. seriously, there has to be at least one minigame in this game that you’ll become obsessed with and complete the kamurogo in one sitting. OH YEAH SUBSTORIES — they introduced a new way of finding side cases through chatter, where you then find the source of the sub story by either observing, sound amping, dog smelling, or frequency detecting. this has been such a fun mechanic to play around with and hasn’t felt tedious at all as of typing this (could change at the drop of a hat who knows). yagami is so funny in his straight man humor that each side case is a blast to play through. 9.8/10

voice acting (jp): ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. incredible. not a moment went by where i felt taken out of the moment by distracting voice acting and everybody just knocks it out of the park. we got TWO!!! screams this game and both were heart breaking in their raw emotion and delivery that i had to pause and take it in. each voice perfectly fits the character and it’s just such a treat to listen to. 10/10

ost: just listen to viper brah. dig in your heels. unwavering belief. there wasn’t a single track that i wasn’t bopping my head to. even the substory chase music (as goofy as it is) was so endearingly stupid that i swung back around to loving it. the jazz mixed with techno works surprisingly well and perfectly encapsulates how this game feels. will be listening to the ost on repeat until the end of time. 10/10

gameplay: okay i don’t think anybody likes the tailing missions i’ll be honest BUT they majorly improved how ♥♥♥♥♥♥ it was from judgment. they even introduced a stealth mechanic which i honestly felt was coming down the pipe at some point and i’m so glad they implemented it. yagami is such an athletic person that the parkour mechanic was so in character i barely even blinked twice, plus it's so fun to climb buildings to find vantage points on your foes and stuff. i didn’t have any massive complaints about the gameplay, except maybe how frequently they make you go back and forth to sleep at the office and take a taxi and ♥♥♥♥ when they could just let me get on with the story, although i understand why they implemented that. ALSO SKATEBOARDING HELLO??? 9/10

combat: the best i’ve played from any rgg game, beating out ishin. it just felt so fluid, and we get four styles. each punch and kick had an impact and the sound design associated with each style is so mesmerizingly unique and a treat to my ears and eyes. i couldn’t even express to you how much i’ve happy laughed at some EX moves, with how stupidly cool they look and how much pain i’d be in if i were on the other end of yagami’s shoe. tag team moves were a pleasure to watch as always!!! one thing they changed (at least in hard mode?? i think) was adding more difficult qtes and i ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ love it. the stakes have never been higher in these fights cause if you mess up some of these qtes, you either insta die or have to redo the qte while your foe reheals. it’s just such a nice change of pace from other rgg games where i felt the qtes were almost too easy and i never felt challenged enough in boss fights. speaking of boss fights oh my god these dynamic intros are hands down the best i’ve seen period. like we’ve peaked here i don’t need to see any more it’s truly insane. 10/10

enjoyment: i laughed, i cried, i smiled, i died inside, it was all worth it. i wish i got dementia pre AD-9 just so i could play this again with fresh eyes. 10/10

overall: go play it. please. it’s one of those games that come out once a blue moon and have no merit being this ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ good. almost breaking my thumb with how hard i’m giving this thumbs up recommendation. i haven't even played kaito files yet but i'm sure it'll be as great as the main game was.

9.9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is infuriating to me that RGG studios goes on to make one of the best combat systems ever and then does everything it possibly can to divert player's attention and total playtime from that.

Lost Judgment is a game full of varied content to do and it is what this game suffers from the most. It is obvious that this is the product of cultivation of both years of preceding RGG games and a great effort. There are more than 30 minigames in this game and tons of other things to do.
So, what is the problem then?

1- Minigames are hardly play-tested or ever balanced. In any minigame with AI opponents, from classics like Mahjong or Poker to new minigames like drone racing or motorcycle races, AI will cheat %100 of the time and you have to use specific anti-AI tactics to win. It does not matter how good you play the minigames, if you don't specifically play around AI's cheats, you are going to lose.

2- Even if you don't wish to complete everything and it's just that you want to see every cutscene instead, you are going to play a good chunk of those 30 minigames. Now, look, let's say that they were all perfectly made minigames (They are not); no one is going to like every single one of them. If you lock certain content behind those minigames, you have to make sure that every single one of them are as enjoyable as rest of the game. Unfortunately, they are not and although there were some of them I liked to play as much as the base game, there were an equal amount of, perhaps even more, minigames I absolutely detested playing. And that will be the case for a lot of people. You can't expect people to like all of those minigames.

3- After all those complaining about abundance of minigames, this might sound ironic but: Lost Judgment needed another minigame, or I should say, a content where you actually can play the game's brilliantly made fun combat system. RGG studios, in their infinite wisdom, decided to remove Arena from Judgment series albeit it was present in its predecessors. Hell, Like a Dragon: Gaiden has just came out and Arena plays a major role in that game. Why wouldn't they just let us play the game in an environment tailored to test our skills? Currently, if you wish to play the game itself after finishing the main story, there are only 2 options: You either have to go around streets, beating up laughably weak punks or you have to go into Gauntlet mode from main menu, in which you are limited in all aspects (Which items you may use, which combat styles you may use etc.). Ironically, in this game that is full of content to do, there is no content to fight against strong enemies, using everything we have farmed for so far.

I know the first 2 points are present in all RGG studios' games but I have not written a review for them so I'm mentioning them here, since they also apply to Lost Judgment. As for third point, it's truly unfortunate that they withhold such content from us in the game that's considered to have the best combat of the entire series by majority of the RGG fanbase. And rightfully so, Lost Judgment has 4 vastly differing combat styles, all fleshed out and fun-to-play with. Although they could have done a better job with style-switching buttons. It was far better in Yakuza 0 whereas styles were assigned to all different buttons rather than a single button to switch to them in-order, one-by-one.

That all being said, I wrote all these because I truly like this game and I would have loved it if they had added the missing pieces because all the potential was there. Lost Judgment is a great game and worth both your time and money. In case you may want to skip all the side content, you still have 35 hours of main story to play to your heart's content.\t\t\t\t\t
