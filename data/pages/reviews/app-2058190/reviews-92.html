It was a dream to finally play the sequel on PC. I played the first one back on PS4 and it was an awesome experience. The fact that RGG risk in making a new IP to depart from the Yakuza series is just surprising. Turns out, the game was better than expected and I mean a lot better than the main series. This is just a spin off.

Lost Judgment is a direct sequel from the first game but tbh, I enjoyed the first game storyline than this one. Don't get me wrong, the storyline is good but the fact that Yagami keep mentioning 'Sawa Sensei' in every chapter is just tiring. Man could not get away for not mentioning every cutscene. Also, I can say this is the first game I feel kind of bad of the antagonist. The ending does put a smile of my face.

Personally, I enjoyed the side contents a lot than the main story. School stories, sub cases. The school stories are one fine as wine content in the game. You're having fun while investigating. I spend almost 50 hours in completing the whole story. It was worth it. Sub cases are also good and interesting like always.

Combat are too good. The best combat in any Yakuza games except 7 because that's turned based. It's smooth and satisfying. Mortal reversal are a great addition to the game.

In conclusion, this is a worthy sequel and a must have for Yakuza fans out there. I gave a solid 8/10 for everything. Really hoping for a third game now. One of the game I would yolo buy at full price. It just have too many things to do that you could lost count!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I played two games back to back right up to Kaito Files. So which means I can review everything now.

The combat in both games is great, with obvious upgrades in Lost Judgement. However, I think you need to get used to the combat in both games. If you came from Yakuza games, then it'll come as second nature as the gameplay (prior to 7) is almost the same as this game.

Graphics is also great, in my eyes, with once again, upgrades in Lost Judgement. It runs nicely to me as well, with a R5 3600 and a RTX 2060 Super and RTX 3060Ti (because i upgraded along the way).

(((((POTENTIAL SPOILER ALERT))))

tl;dr: I like the second one better because the theme is closer to my heart and the side stories is somewhat better story wise.

Now then, the reason why I write this review, particularly on Lost Judgement is because of the story. I really like both of the stories in Judgement and Lost Judgement. Both do tackle injustice. Without spoiling anything (I hope), Judgement covers theme about how fair can a system be in order to achieve something. Whereas Lost Judgement covers a lot about Bullying and what does it cost. In all honestly, the second one has managed to tucked my heart a lot, because the topic is very close to my heart as a victim of bullying. This could be something that people need to talk about more in society as bullying will actually do something not just to the victim (which is obvious), but also to the rest of society. The side story itself is also very good with an entire story spanning to 70-80% of the entire side story. It is, to me, very good as well.


As for Kaito Files, it is an extra bit of story. Of course, it pales a bit if compared to the main game, but some of the twist and turns can actually make you feel "Oh crap, really???" in a good way.

So overall, I wholeheartedly recommend this game not just because it's absolutely fun, but also it is a very good story that could become a "study case" of what could happen.
So yes, play it!\t\t\t\t\t
