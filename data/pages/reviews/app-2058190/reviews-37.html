\r\n\t\t\t\t\toverall rating: 9/10
ong best real time action combat among all yakuza and its spinoff titles. Tiger and Crane were very unique styles in the first game, but they somehow lacked effectiveness severely against bosses to the fact you kinda had to stick to Tiger (which was the intended way ig, still sucks). They completely overhauled combat with two more styles to the list, and Crane might be my favorite style now lmao, followed by Boxer, Tiger and Snake
Story Rating: I honestly loved the first Judgment's setting and suspense more than this one. Lost Judgment lacks a bit compared to the first for that. Can forgive it a bit tho thanks to the antagonist
Side content: Might be the best part about this game. First game had pretty well side cases, but the Keihin gang 'side story' was extremely lackluster (basically those colored gangs from yakuza 4). School Story is perhaps one of the most diverse bunch of side content rgg ever made. From dancing to skateboard races, robot tetris to biker gang and ofc the boxing club were all extremely enjoyable. Ig some of the 'club' stories ended too fast tbh but half of them took decent amount of time. Tho thanks to school stories ig, most side cases didn't feel that great
Music: Best finale theme, no further elaborations

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo keep this short and sweet, i have to say, when you buy a Ryu ga gotoku game, you know what to expect. In this regard, this game doesn disappoint.

But getting into the bread and butter of this series: The story, compared to the previous Judgment installment, it doesn't hit the spot: Questionable choices (not going in depth because spoilers), some shaky conceptual/moral basis which i personally don't agree, which is higly subjetive i know, characters from the previous game being sideline and wasted, new characters that having 0 CHARISMA; and worst of all: My personal desire of wanting to punch Yagami's face out of his high horse, Ryu ga gotoku can be melodramatic and sometimes omega preachy, but this one is a bit too much, when it wants to preach some ideals about justice, when Japan is the last place i would think these things (take this statement anyway you want).

And my favorite part of this series, the soundtrack, sadly, it doesn't hit as hard, but it does have some bangers.

IGN 7/10\t\t\t\t\t
