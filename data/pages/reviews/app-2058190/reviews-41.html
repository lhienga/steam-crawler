\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis isn't as good as Judgment, story-wise, but mostly an improvement gameplay-wise, which seems to be the general consensus (the climbing and parkour elements they added are tedious at best and detract from the flow of the game, I think).

Bullying as a backdrop and motivation point for the story just makes me roll my eyes. It's far less interesting than what we got in Judgment. But it still has great characters, a hallmark of RGG. I liked Soma in particular as a villain. Really creepy with a cool soundtrack for your fights with him. There were some other standout music pieces here (love the Snake Style battle music); RGG has really stepped it up in that department in their recent games!

My biggest complaint is that this game feels bloated in a bad way. RGG games give you a lot to do if you wish, but here a lot of it feels like a slog. Most of that slog comes from the School Stories. Ugh. They tried to do too many mini games at once; RGG should have cut out half of that "content".

Completionists will feel discouraged by trying to do too much in the beginning at once, like I did. Even without the side stuff, this game starts too slowly. I was thinking this might be the first Yakuza franchise game I give a negative review... But RGG's charm shines through so that I can still recommend it!\t\t\t\t\t
