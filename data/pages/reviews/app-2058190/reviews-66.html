Played Judgment to death then began this. about 200 hours there and then it suddenly gets an official PC port, so imagine the train of thoughts running through my head. PS4 money wasted, the game copy money wasted or perhaps I won't just buy it on PC since I've been doing good on the console. But then something clicked in me today and I just decided to buy this and this only just give it a try, and BOY WAS I TAKEN ABACK!!!!
THIS IS THE BEST DECISION I HAVE EVER MADE!!! Just buy the damn game if you like detective stories/thriller stories or you wanna relive your high school days just from the perspective of an ass-kicking substitute teacher who is allowed to kick some bully-ass and then some. It has its Yakuza flair in it and I am a sucker for the Yakuza series. So all in all, ITS A SOLID 2ND PURCHASE FOR ME IDC WHAT OTHERS FEEL GET IT IF YOU WANNA BE THE JUDGEMAN AND DISH OUT SOME FINE JUDG(E)MENT!!

EDIT- Editing my review after completing the main story and about 60% of the side content done. No, did not reach the infamous AMON but yeah. If you feel that the story is slow and its just dragging on and on then you will probably not reach the end. But if you take your time and understand the stakes and situations of the characters that are involved YOU WILL be rewarded soo much as you will literally have a sense of accomplishment that you have done what you set out to do in the beginning, not all stories have happy endings but in the end truth and justice shall prevail. The last four chapters will literally turn your world upside down if you follow the story properly and keep all the dots connected. The final chapter has such sweet battles with so amazing soundtracks you will literally forget about your health while fighting cuz you will be grooving with the music and the combat, trust me the music and combat flow soo smoothly you will forget the objectives. Also yes Yagami keeps saying Sawa-sensei too much, if that annoys you then boy you have not played Judgment, play that and you will know the reason for it its just too personal for Yagami.
So all in all I stand by my first iteration of the review JUST BUY THE DAMN GAME!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThumbs up for the developers seriously.
I have to say the making and the sequence of the story is just very new and unique to me, it is so deep, realistic and yet very fantastic, the ending felt very real and done so well, things got wrapped up pretty neatly which really happens only in few games these days. Story 10/10 no doubt !
Developers:
The way you implemented "skip" and log reading and so many save slots made this seriously very easy to play and encouraged me to do many things like review some cinematics and important scenes with my wife and she got totally hooked up! The fact that you can skip through almost any scene so I could navigate through events in a flash is a huge bonus.
Gameplay:
Story of side quests was mostly irrelevant, and dialogues were too long, I had to skip through most once I realized they don't related to the main story in any way.
Skill set was very fun, but some skills were hard to use especially ones that needed stun and agony effects, actually I never inflicted a stun during the whole game, but that didn't bother me.
The climax battles in the end were frantic I loved them thanks !
The rewards of minigames didn't encourage me to play most of them, but surely having alot of cash from VR Paradise made it my favorite side activity.

Thanks alot for this game I enjoyed every minute, it did feel like a detective game (Actually felt alot like death note) the mystery was thrilling during the first half of the game, I enjoyed every minute!

Ps: The fact that I could play this without feeling I missed out because of not playing the earlier release "Judgement" is a big bonus!

Thanks Ryu Ga Gotoku Studio

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have spent more time playing Yakuza titles in the last few years than any other game franchise or series by a longshot. Every time I finish one, I tell myself, "that's it, there's no way they can make the next game as good as this." With the way most studios end up burning their own beloved IP into the ground, you would expect it to happen for the Yakuza universe too.

However, instead of becoming an abject failure of a studio like some, RGG somehow manages to publish yet another amazing game in Lost Judgement. A few impressions for those who have yet to invest:


Mechanics: the brawls are as chaotic and showy as ever, retaining and building on the highly enjoyable combat of previous titles. The mini games are more addicting than hard drugs. The character banter is witty, and the amount of explorable space and things to do is truly remarkable. I was just expecting Kamurocho plus maybe a small district to travel to, but they went all out and made it a lot of total playable space without running out of amusements and side tasks to fill it out.


Story: the plot hits all the classic Yakuzaverse high notes--a strong protagonist with deep convictions about justice, and the importance in standing up for your personal code of honor. There's a lot of short, funny moments, juxtaposed against larger story themes that examine some of the really messed up ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• in society. Without spoiling anything, one example that really got me was a depiction of some sociopathic kids that made a habit out of bullying someone. They didn't pull any punches in showing how life-ruining it can be to find yourself trapped in that situation with no one to turn to for help. The writers don't shy away from the harsh reality of it, which makes the story surprisingly emotional.


General Vibe: One last thing I really enjoy--much as mainline Yakuza titles have overtones of the crime film genre, Lost Judgement is likewise wrapped in a veneer of film noir and classic detective movies. It gives the series its own distinct place in the RGG ecosystem and I like the alternate approaches both types of protagonists take to get to the same end result.

Performance: For reference I'm using an RTX3070--at the launch of Yakuza: Like a Dragon it took a while to get the game playing smoothly on my setup The team has really improved the games on PC--not sure about Lost Judgement at launch, but currently it runs flawlessly on my PC at 1440p. Have settings maxed, DLSS running and never see frame drops below 60FPS which is a big improvement over the somewhat variable performance I expected.

Get it now while it's on sale, it's worth every dime in my book.

Cheers and happy New Year, kyodais!\t\t\t\t\t

I have not played it's prequel Judgment but I have played Yakuza 0, Kiwami, Kiwami 2, and Like a Dragon. Overall, the game is okay. As for the story, usual Yakuza stuff where it just escalates into higher ups and police and top gangs and what not. Compared to other Yakuza stories, it was pretty low-tier. Maybe it was because Sawa-sensei was getting a ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• ton of attention and focus despite being such a ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•.
It was weird how some cutscenes allowed you to skip dialogue and some didn't, also it would bug out where you actually had to let them say a couple of words otherwise you don't get to skip the next line of dialogue. I think it was me but when the game introduces the character for the first time, it lasts for 20 seconds for me.

For most of the game, I played on Hard difficulty but the combat was still quite easy. I switched between Simple and Hard and the difference was that enemies had less health, hits you with a feather, and the AI uses combat moves for you such as activating skills and switching between stances. The extracts that you get are extremely overpowered but fits well for how difficult and annoying they are to acquire and store, I never had to use these extracts until the last couple of fights and legit swept them. The EX skills were quite annoying as they take a long time to finish animating. Combat is easily the strong point of this game and carries it pretty hard. The few side missions that I have done were interesting but nothing note-worthy in my opinion, they were better in Yakuza.

The annoying parts of the game:

The ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• school stories, I swear this section took up at least a third of my play time. It was extremely annoying just navigating through the school because you would do a mission for a club and then you have to return to the MCR to talk to the wanna-be detective hoe.

The Dance Club was actually pretty fun because it was just stepmania.

The Robotics Club was interesting but a ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• time sink if you actually play this above Simple difficulty because the enemy AI are nuts and you actually need to have materials to upgrade your robot parts so you have to play practice matches as well as buy mats at other places to win.

The Boxing Gym was cool but repetitive and also hard as ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• on Hard difficulty. I managed to get through most of the opponents on Hard by just spamming buttons but ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• Kenya is a beast who parries and blocks 95% of your ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• and I had to tone it down to Simple.

The Biker Gang is probably the one I hate the most because it doesn't even feel like a race. You have to drive into and hit 10-20 other bikers before you actually get to race and when you're finally racing with the last guy, you can't outpace him, no matter what, he's a set distance away from you so if you happen to make a couple of mistakes at the end, you're done for. So you have around 4 boss races that you have to win and each boss has 3 lackies so you have to race for around 16 times to complete this garbage.

The Photography Club, you just take pictures.

The eSports Club, spam buttons in street fighter, win.

The Casino, you don't actually get to play for real, you auto lose and then auto win.

The Girls' Bar is another grind. You have to drink and talk to 4 girls and complete their side missions in order to complete this section. If you don't have the drunkenness limit, it'll take a long time since the only way to quickly get rid of being drunk is to go into a fight and use the EX drunk skill with 6+ drunk and get rid of the whole meter or drink a bunch of liquids.

That's all for the school stories which is a huge part of this game. I dislike the stealth, climbing, and chasing mechanics because it just feels like they waste time. There's no real skill, it's a pretty linear path for the direction that you're going in, all it does is waste your time.
Because of the physics of this game, you would sometimes just fall off when you're in a climbing section and you also have to investigate the route that you have to take before you start climbing which is just a HUGE waste of time. I don't know if it was buggy or what but several times during chase scenes, I would miss the grab at the end even though I was spamming the button and time was slowing down but the button prompt wouldn't show up at all.

A lot of the previous minigames are still here such as the batting center and darts. They have added a new VR minigame which you can get a bunch of money from and it's basically mario party with more mini games inside.

The investigation tools that you get also feel like a waste of time. You get a sound amplifier, an emp detector, a drone, and a detective dog. I don't think I have ever used the drone and the detective dog was part of a story. They give you these so you can go off and do your side missions which you have to first find based off of another mechanic: buzzwords.
Buzzwords shows you a bunch of tweets around the map and you basically have to run to each relevant tweet and listen to start the quest and you don't know which tweet is relevant until you're there and have listened to their conversation.

The game looks fine, doesn't pop out or anything but runs well.

I only like this game because its reminiscent of Yakuza and I severely disliked Like a Dragon because it was a turn-based game.
