\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I dive into why I gave this a negative review, I'd like to point out that this is my replay of the game. I played it first when it launched in 2021 and actually finished it in one sitting (17 hours) and I wasn't a fan of it then. When the game finally released on PC I thought to myself let's give it another shot and see how I feel. Once again, I felt the same but let me make it clear, there's only one reason I disliked LJ and that is it's writing.

Judgment (LJ's predecessor) was a masterpiece in it's writing, combat, incredible environments and movie-like cinematics. Pretty much just like any Yakuza/RGG game you've played. LJ on the other hand felt like such a huge downgrade in terms of it's writing and story. See I was prepared to accept the fact LJ wasn't going to top JE in it's writing due to how high of a bar the first game set, it was impossible to top it. I knew that, but I expected far better in LJ which to be honest, it wasn't. There was a lot going on, so much that at one point you don't understand what your main goal is, what your actually trying to achieve. It felt overbearing and just added specific parts into the game just for the shock factor. It feels much like Yakuza 5 where you have separate stories happening at one time and when you conjoin them it just feels messy and all over the place. Not to mention how some of the beloved characters from the first game barely get more than 10 minutes of screentime in this one which is such a shame.

I will praise the game for its biggest highlight for me and that is it's combat. Absolute peak combat that the series has seen, so fleshed out and hugely improved on from it's predecessor and not to forget the new styles added are amazing. Style switching is faster, more smooth and solid. It's perfection and it's one of the main reasons why I kept going the first time round and the same again. I really tried my hardest to like this game, to give it a fair second chance but alas my mind has not changed.\t\t\t\t\t
