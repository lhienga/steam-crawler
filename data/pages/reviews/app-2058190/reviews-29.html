\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLost Judgement is an incredible game and it might just be the most significant sign of RGGs growth as a studio so far. While I really liked Judgement, Lost Judgement blows it out of the water in nearly every way. The graphics and presentation are improved, the combat is the best it has ever been in the series, the side content is engrossing and could qualify as a standalone game, the soundtrack is chill-inducing, the story is bold and thought-provoking, and the characters are iconic. Lost Judgment is up there with the best in the series and gives me a lot of hope for the future of the franchise.

COMBAT

Lost Judgements combat is better paced and more liberating than Judgements. Yagami has three combat styles now, Tiger, Crane, and Snake. Unlike Judgement, these styles dont overshadow each other. They all have a near-equal amount of utility. Switching between them is near instantaneous, encouraging constant style switching to meld style-specific buffs together. With all three of these buffs activated, Yagami is fast, strong, and unable to be knocked down, making him incredibly satisfying to play as. The EX actions in this game are better than ever. They are brutal, mesmerizing, hilarious, and sometimes all three. Overall, the combat in Lost Judgement is a massive improvement over its predecessor and it's some of the best Ive seen from the series.

SCHOOL STORIES

The side content in Lost Judgement is among the best Ive seen from an RGG game. Most of the side content can be found in the school stories where Yagami works as a school counselor to solve a string of crimes caused by students in order to find the mastermind behind it all. Nearly all of the school stories are fun and rewarding. The dance club was a hilarious rhythm game where Yagami did a bunch of crazy dance moves to catchy music. The robotics club was the most complicated and frustrating to me but that made it the most satisfying to complete. The boxing gym has a very fleshed-out boxing minigame which has a decent amount of depth and challenge to it. The motorcycle gang was my favorite for being the most funny, endearing, yet intense out of all of the school stories. The skateboarding minigame was very satisfying and addicting to play. Skateboarding is one of the most important additions to this game, and possibly to the series because it is a faster and more fun way of exploration throughout Yokohama. The rest of the school stories are a lot less remarkable. However, they all are worth playing because the final sequence that reveals the person behind it all is a great experience. Whats better is that everyone from all of the school stories come together and help you close the case. Most of the school stories have great character work attached to them and seeing their arcs climax in the finale is incredibly satisfying. Despite their flaws, the school stories might just be the best side content RGG has ever made and it gives me hope they can do even better in the future.

SIDE CASES

Because of how much time the school stories take up, there arent nearly as many side cases as there were in Judgement, nor were they as long, but I see that as a good thing. The game also vastly improved its tailing sequences by making them less linear. I am going to name some of my favorite side cases. Dueling Dojos, The Body Model Walks At Night, and My First Errand were hilarious and sit alongside the best side content in the series. At this point, it is tradition for me to do all of the side cases (or substories) in every RGG game so I can face Amon. Im normally pretty satisfied by the Amon fights in the series, but despite Lost Judgement's amazing combat, its Amon fight rubbed me the wrong way.

SOUNDTRACK

I think the most iconic part of Lost Judgment is its soundtrack. I initially bought Judgement and Lost Judgement because of Lost Judgement's music alone. Im going to list my favorite tracks, starting with Lost In The Rain, the music that plays on the title screen, which is one of my favorite title screens because of its dour mood being flushed away by an optimistic white kitten. Some other great tracks are Vorarephilia, Fog, K.O.G., , Viper, Final Destination, GIRLS, and the two songs that deserve credit for putting Lost Judgement on the map, Dig In Your Heels and Unwavering Belief. Dig In Your Heels is perfect workout music that sounds like the world is at stake, despite the setting its played in. While Dig In Your Heels came out of nowhere, Unwavering Belief perfectly portrays the rising tension up to the beginning of the final fight and it feels as though the song represents a clashing of ideals.

STORY

Finally, we can talk about this games story. Im just going to talk about my favorite parts of the story instead of summarizing the whole thing. In comparison with Judgement, Lost Judgements detective story feels like I can actually follow along and make theories instead of just waiting for a twist that explains everything. It feels like a natural progression of RGGs story structure. I really appreciate how this game went out of its way to criticize Japanese law through the lens of bullying. Ive never seen another game jab at its governments weaknesses while treating it with respect at the same time. That brings me to my favorite part of Lost Judgements story, the dynamic between Yagami and Kuwana. I cant think of another piece of media whose protagonist and antagonist were equally wrong and right. Kuwanas pessimistic outlook on justice proclaims that since the justice system is broken, he has to punish those who are deserving, no matter the damage it causes to innocent bystanders. As a lawyer, Yagamis optimistic view of the law makes him believe no form of self-served justice should take precedence over the law, especially if it affects innocent people. He can only hope that the law can get better if enough trust and care are put into it. RGG took a big risk in making Yagamis character seem rationally flawed because of his morals, but I think it pays off beautifully. No one has the right answer here and I love that. In the end, its about actions, not just ideals, and Kuwana did some horrible things to achieve his version of justice. Yagami just wants the world to be propelled by the truth, which is the fairest thing someone in his position can do. The only loose end is how the law will enact justice based on the truth, still giving strength to Kuwanas argument against Japans imbalanced justice system. I find it pretty bold that Kuwana is let go in the end. Sure, there is no proof to stick him to his crimes, but it also leaves a major crack in Yagamis optimism for the justice system. If a guilty man can never be punished for his crimes in a legal manner, then would self-imposed justice be the next best thing? Kuwana is easily the best antagonist in the Yakuza series. As an antagonist, he asks more out of the protagonist and the player than any other villain has and its difficult not to root for him despite his actions. I really appreciate Lost Judgements story. The characters were easy to love or hate, the discussions about bullying and the justice system were fascinating, and it felt like a real detective story by breaking away from the usual Yakuza story structure. It doesnt have nearly as much emotional weight as Judgement had, but it traded that for being a lot more thought-provoking to the point of being divisive, which only the best stories can accomplish.

CONCLUSION

Lost Judgement is a fantastic sequel to its predecessor. The combat is at its peak in the series, the side content is the most fleshed out it has ever been, the soundtrack is more iconic than the game itself, and its incredibly unique story boasts some of the best characters and themes in the series. This is one of the few games I will definitely come back to. I would love to see Yagami in a future release, but I would also find it fitting for this to be his last hurrah.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy goal in every Yakuza game, and I've played all of them (mostly on PS4, and except for Dead Souls) as of right now, is to fight the secret boss Amon. This game's Amon fight was more funny than he was difficult. He is not the easiest Amon fight, that honor goes to Ishin, but I did beat him in the Gauntlet on my first try. Also, lately, I've been playing on hard mode.

Speaking of, I think the gauntlet in this game is my favorite it's ever been throughout the series and I don't say that lightly. It was adequately challenging and fun without leaving me thinking "oh please, you shouldn't have" at the prospect of the "challenge" it presented. For example, in Yakuza 4, one "challenge" was to beat someone without hitting them with Tanimura, so you needed to put your back to a wall, parry them into the wall so they take damage and spend the next 5 minutes repeating that bored bored bored bored bored until you win. This was as stupid and as boring as it sounds. Also, the difficulty in the gauntlet seems to be averaged for "normal" difficulty, so playing on hard mode made the gauntlet easier to complete even with the constraints.

As for the combat, massive improvements from Judgement. In Judgement, Crane was kinda there. All the upgrades were for Tiger and Tiger did tons of damage and did enough of a job to dominate the entire game. Amon in Judgement was supposed to remind me that Crane exists with his gimmick but I beat him without it anyway because Crane is... just kinda there.
So what does Lost Judgement do? It adds a third combat style and boy howdy how did they do it, I don't know but now we got an amazing balance where each fighting style specializes in something different. It reminds me of Yakuza 0 and it is great all for it. Also, the SP system, which is perhaps the best it has ever been, rewards you for varying your fighting style.
Note: it's a shame that the fourth fighting style does NOT have any unique SP awards so I ended up disabling it until late game (which is also fitting as upgrading it requires you do a side-quest thing). Sure, it was a DLC thing (that comes with the base purchase of this game, I don't own the Kaito Files DLC but I still had it) but come on! Put some SP awards for it!

Late game, I ran around fighting random dudes on the street for around 3,000 SP per fight (around 1,400 from SP gained via the reward system and 1,600 SP for winning the fight. It is incredibly easy and not very grindy to get all skills before you beat the game. Though, if you taxi all over town instead of running around town, then you're probably not the target audience for this game, and also you will feel the grind to get all the skills.

Sure, each style has its unique attacks, finishers, tiger drops, heat actions, and even dodging, but in my opinion, one of the biggest accomplishments is the "secret" of each style. Dodging with Crane increases movement speed while parrying with Snake prevents you from being knocked down (from attacks) and landing a charged finisher (has to be charged despite the game not specifying, I checked) with Tiger increases your damage. All these effects are temporary and until you get an upgrade for each style, they go away if you style switch. When you unlocked these skills to keep the style switch, not only do you get to keep it, but activating the "secret" on another style resets the timer for the secret(s) from the previous style(s) so if you play well, you can be a faster-attacking, stronger-hitting and un-knock-down-able Yagami that tears through the enemies.
Boxing style has this too where if you land a counter hit (like Tiger drop, which I think is a mistake because you may as well Tiger drop late game), the secret ability is your EX gain is increased (more counters = even faster charging).

Even with all this, Tiger is still obscenely strong, so the strongest thing you can do is get crane and snake secrets, swap to tiger, EX Boost and just go ham. My personal favorite is two squares into fully charged triangle attacks. Also, they got rid of the "strong hits break max HP" gimmick from Judgement, that good too. If I have a complaint, it would be why do we swap between the styles with one button instead of using the full D-Pad like in Yakuza 0? Shortcut? I'm aware but I'd delete the thing while delegating the healing to the pause menu, like it has always been and everyone continues to do, and just let us enjoy Y0's style swapping. Someone made a mod for that please and thank you.

While the combat is the center, take a look aside for the side stuff... which I had finished the lion's share of all the side stuff by chapter 4. Every possible thing I could do at that point, I did until it was completed or locked. This is how I enjoy these games and make sure I fight Amon. Arriving to the start of the final chapter, I only had one last side quest to do and Amon. Overall, the humor I like is when funny things happen to characters and they play along. Something that I really do not like it is when these characters, that we've learned about and grown to like, suddenly start acting as if there's a gas leak in the room. Thankfully, the instance where this does happen is fairly brief and works as a contrast to how heavy the plot gets when you first access this side quest, so I'll let it slide.

With characters mentioned, Yagami is better fleshed out compared to how he was in Judgement. Then again, I used to be a teacher so putting him in that context and not screwing up went a long way to making me like him. Still... I'm quite mehhhhhhh on him but what I did early on was mod Mine (read: Mi-Ne, not "mine") from Yakuza 3 via Nexus to replace Yagami and that somehow made me enjoy the game quite a lot more. The only flaw with the mod occurs in Chapter 7 but not in any meaningful way. Also, no "in school shoes" texture of sorts, so invisible feet when in school... which is funny but not impactful.
Trying to avoid any spoilers (except for Amon), I'll just say that the other characters also have their time in the spotlight adequately that I overall enjoyed the story. Watching them play rock paper scissors while the credits rolled was amusing.

Finally, the extract system... It's there and I seldom remember its existence. I imagine it would've made any particular combat section a cake walk, based on the effects I read; like "get the ability to shoot energy balls" or "get a thunder sword that you can use to smite your foes" or whatever; and also the "DLC" that automatically comes with the game gives you a sample to use (a free one for most of them) but still... I never really used them. Even in the gauntlets, I only used one where it is *required* to win.

Overall, amazing entry by RGG that I recommend. I sure hope they don't only make turn-based combat games now and I also hope they never use the Unreal Engine like Ishin. Cheers.\t\t\t\t\t
