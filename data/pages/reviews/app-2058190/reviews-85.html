An overall improvement over the first Judgement, and a solid RGG game overall

If you don't now what an "RGG game" is, it's short for Ryu Ga Gotoku, the name of the main franchise (also known as Yakuza) as well as the studio that makes it, RGG Studios.

These games are beat em up action RPGs with (relatively) small, but extremely dense open world environments, and most famous for managing to use mostly the same maps over multiple games with lots of asset re-use, but still manage to feel fresh and fun each time, though an excellent gameplay loop, tons of minigames and ridiculous sidequests, and corny but still fun action stories.

I love these games as essentially the videogame equivalent of comfort food. Some are better or worse than others, but you broadly know what you are getting and can expect a consistent baseline of quality in each one.

If you are new to these games, I do NOT think Lost Judgement should be your first. If you want to start with the Judgement spinoff franchise please start with the first Judgement, if you want a broader intro to RGG games I recommend Yakuza 0 instead as the ideal game for potential new fans. Both are on Steam!

I played with all DLC enabled, but I'm not reviewing The Kaito Files here, I haven't played it yet.

With that said, the rest of this review will be written for people already familiar with RGG games but wondering how Lost Judgement shapes up. These are my thoughts.

THE GOOD STUFF

  • The combat in this game is a JOY. Base Judgement was a bit iffy in terms of combat, Lost Judgement has improved on it in every single way. Crane and Tiger style have had new features added to make both more appealing and interesting, and the new Snake style is one of THE most fun combat styles RGG have ever done. It adds a timed, omnidirectional parry to your blocking, it allows you to end combos with throws, adds several new throw variations, lets you inflict status effects... It's SUCH a fun way to fight. The EX Surrender moves are also some of the funniest Heat Actions in the entire series. For the first time, every fighting style feels like its own FULLY fleshed out, uniquely fun way to fight enemies. DLC owners also have the Boxer style to look forward to, which is like a better iteration on the old Rush style. It has a ton of defensive and offensive power behind it, but deliberately lacks versatility.

  • I really liked the story, and I feel some aspects of it are better than anything RGG have done in the past. I love the new, more socially relevant direction RGG have been taking their stories in, and in particular the final antagonist of this game has become one of the most interesting characters they've ever made IMO. I believe that a lot of people will take this story in vastly different ways depending on their own life experience, but I don't think that's necessarily a bad thing. There are some people who get hung up on the ending, but IMO most of what they are saying is rooted in misunderstanding the plot. IF you have gotten the ending spoiled by people who don't like it, here's a quick context explanation that won't completely, absolutely, and utterly ruin the ride for you, but still AVOID if you don't already know the ending: In the end, Yagami lets the killer escape. He does NOT do this because he agrees with the killer, even though some of his ex-criminal friends actually do, but because he doesn't have serious evidence that would stick, the killer is going to be in absolutely no position to kill again in the future, and he knows there is corruption in the justice system that will have the killer executed in prison to hide the truth if he was ever arrested. Lost Judgement is a story about trying to do the right thing in a situation where there are only up, down and left things to choose from- You might end up disagreeing with Yagami's choice at the end of the game, but it doesn't make it a bad story

  • This is definitely a Yokohama game primarily, not a Kamurocho game. Kamurocho has the usual activities but absolutely nothing beyond that. In fact, it's got less to do in it than base Judgement, because stuff like Dice & Cube or the Drone Racing League has moved out to Yokohama instead. Having two maps you travel between is definitely pretty cool!

  • The various "detective activities" such as tailing, lockpicking, and observing have been refined, and minimised. Tailing, for example, got REALLY old fast in Judgement. In Lost Judgement, tailing feels more freeform, but is also MUCH less common and takes much less time, so you won't be bored out of your mind. Lockpicking has had thumb turn bypasses removed so you can be speedier on regular lockpicking. There are less Observation points in the story and there's now a hint function if you get stuck, but you can also go into Observation mode in the open world too, and there are actually some mini-puzzles to solve out there using it.

  • There are new "modes" for detective work- Parkour, and stealth. These are both only for scripted story sequences- Kamurocho has not gone full Assassin's Creed. In my opinion, these aren't great. Parkour is harmless, but sadly doesn't add much to the game- I'd love it if it was usable in the open world for shortcuts or back alleys, but that isn't actually the case. Stealth is similar, it's functional but adds nothing to the game as there really aren't multiple ways to approach situations with the highly limited level design.

  • Even by RGG standards, this OST is a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ banger. Some of the best boss/combat themes in the series are in this game. A real highlight of the game.

  • MORTAL WOUNDS ARE GONE! The biggest issue with the original Judgement has been removed. Instead, you have Mortal Attacks, which are charged, highly telegraphed attacks enemies do that you can beat using a timed quickstep, then follow up with a Mortal Reversal that changes based on your equipped Style. It's a simple way to give enemies super attacks and let players respond in an interesting way.

  • Minigames have been enhanced and expanded across the board. From simple things like Drone Racing being shorter 2 lap races instead of 3, to the massive School Stories sidequest chain that spans several different minigames, most completely new, and has its own campaign content related to it. RGG have pulled out all the stops to make up for Judgement not having karaoke, and have mostly managed it.

THE CONS

  • Much of the story won't work as well if you have not played the original Judgement. As per usual with RGG stories, info from previous games is not critically important to understand this one, and they avoid giving any serious spoilers for the first game, so this can be experienced standalone. However, there are a lot of characters that'll make you think "who?" you haven't played the first Judgement.

  • The QTEs, while rare, have become a bit iffy now in terms of your inputs. You need to execute 3-4 different button inputs in a sequence, and boy, that's a BIT too much to ask players to do in a handful of seconds! They should go back to the regular old single inputs.

  • Overall, I don't think Yagami is as good a protagonist as his predecessors. Unlike LiD's Ichiban, who is a very smartly composed character to become extremely likeable and endearing, Yagami feels like a remix of Kiryu without the eccentricities that made Kiryu likeable. He's not horrible but he feels unfortunately too much like a blank slate, sadly.

TL:DR

A very solid RGG game that has left me very excited for RGG 8 and the direction these games are taking. It's fantastic, muddy, cheesy drama and has put many grins on my face. Any RGG game fan should absolutely play this.\t\t\t\t\t
