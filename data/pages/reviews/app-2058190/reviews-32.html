\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very good game and one of the best in the "extended" series (Yakuza+Judgment).

First I am so happy to see the Dragon Engine finally deliver : the game is really beautiful and the fights finally both enjoyable and fair (Y3 to Kiwami system was enjoyable only but broken).
Activities are generally of good quality and I appreciate the gameplay additions to make the game more modern and less clunky (the parkour for instance...yes it feels dated compared to real action/adventure games but this is a step in the right direction).

As always, you need like 2-4 hours to be drawn in the game and after that you cannot stop playing until the end. The story is a bit weak if you look at it coldly (as many Yakuza games to be fair), but they half succeeded to deliver their message with strong characters and emotionnal momentum. I cannot spoil but I would rate this part B, A being the best stories in the series. I cannot put the finger on the reason behind this feeling, maybe because Yagami looped over one event and used it every time in his discussions so that he locked the game instead of developping the numerous interesting characters and leads (the school for instance is abandoned quickly by the plot). To sum up, maybe Yagami and his single argument were too dominating in the plot (compared to other characters, story branches, etc.) and that is why the Kaito files DLC felt like a deliverance.

Aside from that, the game has a huge content, to the point that in almost 40 hours I have never ecountered some. This is certainly here of the best points : we cannot feel bored in this game and the step in modernity for all these old minigames feels good even if the side quests by themselves are not really memorable.

Now I have a huge gripe with this port and I'm tormented to leave a negative review for this alone : the DLC button in the smartophone makes you leave the game (as intended) and then, when you come back to it, the game is FROZEN. So if you did not save, you can lose a lot of playtime. This is even more frustrating when you have already purchased the DLC, so the button is absolutely useless.

Considering the content for the price, the usual free additions and the quality, I will keep my positive review but I hoped that this matter would be fixed.

========

KAITO FILES DLC

My man, this has a bit of drama within but the story was satisfying as hell. It reminded me a bit of Y1 strings+grittiness mixed with the nostalgic/emotionnal vibe of Y3. Again, if I examine it coldly, there are many flaws in the plot but the stream of emotions and the main line are so strong that they are forgotten. In a way, this DLC had the (genuine) humanity lacking in main game which felt colder (despite the forced scenes, because the game was too centered around Yagami and his view).

This is unfortunately not something that memorable because this is a DLC and not a full game but this was a welcomed addition. Mikiko was also one of the first female characters (if not the only one) that feel (and look) like a belivable mature woman and not the "cute/pure/cheesy" girl to protect. I'm not especially feminist but a real woman is a lot more charming (hell she is) and interesting. Plus 30-40 women in the other games acting like 18 princess from movies was a bit frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t
