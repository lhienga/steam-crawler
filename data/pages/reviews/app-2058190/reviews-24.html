\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLost Judgment is another masterpiece in a string of them by RGG Studio. It's the absolute pinnacle of brawler combat, the story is unbelievably engaging, the side content is deep, varied, and almost always incredibly fun, and the total package the game offers is basically unparalleled by anything else the studio has ever made. It's good.

The combat is probably the biggest, brightest selling point - Yagami's three base styles mesh together extremely well, and you can find situations to maximize them all throughout the game. Tiger is as good as ever with the addition of charged moves, Crane is actually very useful now in its intended crowd setting, and Snake is maybe the most satisfying combat style RGG has ever put together. Even with Tiger and Crane being great, I had to force myself to use them from time to time and not just sit in Snake from the moment I unlocked it. The flow of it is just amazing, and you're given fantastic combat situations throughout, too - powerful, tricky bosses that make you feel accomplished for figuring out how to get around them, while you can simply smoke regular encounters on demand, and with mortal wounds gone (thank God), you don't have to worry about putting the game on hold to go restore your full health bar after getting randomly shot by some ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ on the street. Perfect!

The story...easily one of the best, of course, with a great deal of nuance and grey areas for the player to mull over as the story progresses. I'd say it's not quite as satisfying or viscerally gripping as Judgment's, but it's hard to live up to the strongest, most focused story RGG Studio's ever made, and even then, Lost Judgment does an admirable job in keeping me engaged. The returning characters develop, the new characters fascinate, and there are plenty of amazing twists and moments of absolute satisfaction that kept me locked in for hours and hours. The primary antagonist has a ton of layers, and the supplementary antagonists all spiral outwards from them in myriad ways that conveyed a strong, clear message about the story RGG Studio was telling here. Even if I personally find the antagonist a lot more reprehensible than others do, they were written astoundingly well, and the game hinges on that success.

I LOVE the side content this time around - most of it is centered on the School Stories, which basically functions as a second campaign meant to be played through alongside the main story. Amasawa is such a sweet girl, and the stories told through each of the clubs you explore tie pretty well into the game's core themes of the price of bullying and who gets to pass judgment on wrongdoers. Each club has its own minigame associated with it, and while most of these are fun and well-developed, a couple of them were significantly more frustrating and needed better balance. This is about the Robotics Club. It should have been deleted. There's no salvaging it. The rest of the clubs, though? Great. Skateboarding, love it. Boxing, AMAZING. Dancing that's mechanically similar to Haruka's idol minigame? Fantastic. Cooking the CPU in Virtua Fighter? Perfect.

Honestly, there's just SO MUCH to love about Lost Judgment, and its highs are so high that I can barely even remember the lows. I think the way Yagami is written can get a little one-note sometimes, especially in comparison to the antagonist, and as previously mentioned there are minigames necessary for School Stories progression that are absolute torture to get through. Stealth missions suck ass, the parkour segments are interesting but ultimately tedious, tailing is still horrendous even though it was downplayed significantly, and, frustratingly, the female characters get pigeonholed into passive roles more than I'd like (as usual). Even with all that, though? Lost Judgment is one of the best games I've ever played. No qualifiers. Play it.\t\t\t\t\t
