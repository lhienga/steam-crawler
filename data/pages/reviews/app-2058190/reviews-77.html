\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlready having completed the game on PS4, I can say playing Lost Judgment on the PC offers pretty much superior experience, both in gameplay (60 FPS is NEEDED for this game's combat to truly shine) and graphical fidelity, you can even notice much more particles on the screen during combat power-ups compared to the past-gen version on PS4. In comparison to Judgment Remaster this game truly feels like a next-gen title and not just another "cross-gen" title to me.

Only big problem as of now that "visual novel" scenes (there is a lot of these in this game) look very bad on "lower" resolutions as 1080p or when DLSS/FSR upscaling is enabled and I'm hoping for a fix.

Music in Lost Judgment is of my favorite OSTs in whole Yakuza series, probably among the best RGG Studio ever done.

Cons:
Levelling up now feels too grindy compared to first game, good thing that combat is very enjoyable.
Also it's got TOO MUCH side-content, it might be as well a plus to you if you have time and patience to complete it all. I felt some minigames like robot battles or motorcycle "bosozoku" racing game wasn't as enjoyable as other side-content in the game and they took a good chunk of my time compared to others on my first playtrough on PS4.


I hope RGG Studio continues to make more great beat-em up games like Lost Judgment and not commit wholly to new JRPG direction they took, for me personally both Judgment games were way more enjoyable than Yakuza 7's clunky and slow turn-based combat.

This is a must buy if you liked Yakuza games prior to Y7's genre change, Lost Judgment is Yakuza brawler combat at it's peak. But, I highly suggest playing the first game before jumping in LJ to get to know the main returning characters and their story. Without the first game, despite what marketing said at the release, it won't feel right and you probably wouldn't understand much of the references.

8/10, the true GOTY 2021 in my eyes.
Some technical issues still present on PC, unless you have a 3090 and can play at 4K with DLAA enabled to "fix" the jaggies in VN-style dialogues.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is RGG Studio's magnum opus. It's undoubtedly one of their best games and it's not even close.
The amount of love and attention this game has is paralleled by none (save Yakuza 0).
The combat and fluidity RGG has strived to achieve in their dragon engine for so long comes to a staggering peak in this entry. It does everything right. It makes everything feel so nice, so powerful - so fluid.

The main story in and of itself is very thrilling. I fell for the story hook, line and sinker.
But in my honest opinion, the best aspect about this game besides the combat is the school stories. They did extremely well to flesh Yagami out in this game independent from the main story. Yagami tackles so much more than just gloomy detective work, he engages with Ijincho's youth, and cultivates many everlasting relationships with them, but I digress.


Only complaint I have is that the tiger and crane style feel sort of underwhelming in this entry. The boxer and snake style feel so much better, but it might just be a skill issue lol\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecided to play this and the original Judgment since I had a blast with the Yakuza games. A lot of things are fantastic with this game and I believe this is my favorite game that I've played in years. The story all the way through was a suspenseful iceberg that kept uncovering more and more pieces until it eventually turned the original plot on it's head. The gameplay was tight and highly responsive, especially compared to it's predecessor game while simultaneously introducing new styles and movesets to keep things fresh. The characters were developed very well and felt genuine with their beliefs and actions, especially the main antagonist who was a perfect capstone/key player to the story since you as the player can sympathize with their choices even if they aren't the most ethical. The music was downright full of bangers which made encounters more enjoyable and made the settings more ambient assuming you're into EDM. The side content is plentiful and mostly optional though some of it hits while other parts miss in terms of enjoyment. Overall, I'd highly recommend this game if you're a fan of beat em up's, rpgs, and/or story rich games as this one checks all of the above and won't disappoint.\t\t\t\t\t
