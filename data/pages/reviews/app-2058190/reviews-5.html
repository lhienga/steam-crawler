\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros & Cons compared to recent Yakuza & Like a Dragon series
-------------------------------------------------------------------

PROS
- 4 Different types of Martial arts ( including one from School Side Quest )
- School Stories (Different From Side Quests) around 8 different to Master and will provide the best type of fun Challenge such as the best imho :-
Boxing ( its like a totally awesome game of its own where you can spar to upgrade boxing stats to take up like 15 different class of challengers , learn moves from your boxing coach and even can used to apply to one of your fighting style outside the boxing ring) this is much more interesting compared to yakuza series underground fight.

Dancing - Rhythm game but its not as easy at it sounds compared to yakuza karaoke series. you need master the game in order to help your students win national championship.

Racing - again like crash bandicoot where you enter the underground street racing world where you take on around 12 different racing crews while they use weapons like molotov cocktails like fireworks, weapons and such in the same time need to keep up to speed to race the boss

Skateboarding - kinda mini tony pro skater type of game, easier but equally challenging in later levels

Photography - this is kinda lame but whatever

Robot Fighting - kinda like nintendo japanese robot fighting game, while you control robots along with two AI controlled allies battle with other schools in order get more parts to build stronger robots to further advance to harder robots

Drinking - this one is kinda lame unless you like dating sim conversation mini games where you answer bar girls question while getting drunker and drunker and your English have to be at least decent to score.
------------------------------
- Mahjong - is much easier compared to among 5-6 different yakuza games i played.. you tend you draw better tiles in my honest opinion. definitely easiest way to earn money if you are well versed with the rules.

- Stories & Side quests - Totally made more sense and interesting storylines and cases compared to like a dragon series mini games. Yakuza series have some good ones but plenty of bad ones like chasing a person in order to get back a toy for a kid. Some of the side quests in Lost judgement are so smartly crafted that you kinda learn something in real life that you can brag with your non gaming friends such as the quest ( mask of the Chaos author)

- Story is even darker than Yakuza series ( meaning you might even see innocent NPCs you cared for DIE ) compared to Yakuza series where you know that guy should die, he dies, or even when you think he should die but never dies no matter how many times you think he is going to die. Making it even more important to invest in the game to bring justice to bad guys.

- traveling is awesome as you can unlock the fastest skateboard to glide around town in a very fast fashion.

CONS
- stealth quests are kinda lame and one sided sometimes it made no sense. definitely the main factor making the game looks dumb.

- moves have no to very few explanation, such as EX surrender, when you upgrade skills you sometimes don't know what they do because they are meant to enhance a skill that you don't even know how to do at the first place.

- No karaoke, no Pool table

- No load game function, need to go return to title if you wanted to load ( they fixed this in Like a Dragon:Infinite Wealth )

- No fast travel system like Like a Dragon:Infinite Wealth

- No weapon customization\t\t\t\t\t
