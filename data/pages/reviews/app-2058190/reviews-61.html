\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLost Judgment is what a sequel should be. It brings a lot of improvements over the original Judgment and while it has some drawbacks, its strengths far outweighs its blemishes. Lost Judgment is one of RGG's best games to date.

The story and themes about bullying, the flaw in legal system and the act of vengeance when you take justice into your own hands are done expertly in Lost Judgment. You end up understanding and even rooting for the antagonist due to how believable their beliefs and motives. While the original Judgment is much more focused on neo-noir crime action drama, resulting in big climatic battle about what it actually means to sacrifice something for the greater good of society and a tale of redemption, Lost Judgment takes a different approach. It is much more grounded and arguably more low stakes than its predecessor, but it also strikes much more deeper in what it says about society, especially concerning bullying and legal system. This results in a much more emotionally-driven battles of ideologies where neither sides are fully in the right and wrong. Long after the credit rolls, Lost Judgment manages to make me think longer about its themes. Overall, great story told in a beautiful way with some of the best written characters RGG has ever made.

Combat is improved so much to the point that I can safely say that this is RGG's best brawler-type combat. The 4 fighting styles are all unique and balanced. You are encouraged to utilize all the styles' strengths in different scenarios therefore making the combat varied and always exciting to do. The bosses are all great, especially the major boss like Soma or Akutsu or Kuwana. These 3 are top-tier bosses with stellar dynamic intros and soundtrack themes. In short, Lost Judgment perfected Dragon Engine real time combat.

The environment, now the majority of time taking place in Ijincho from Yakuza 7/Like A Dragon, still looks beautiful as ever. With the added skateboard for traversal, making running from one side of the city to the other a breeze and enjoyable. Plus Yagami has a detective dog now which you can use to wander around town collecting items and even skillbooks scattered throughout the city. Activities in Ijincho also can be expanded further through the School Stories side story, ranging from Skateboard minigame, Biker, Casino, and the greatest new addition of all, The Seiryo High School. Built in such detail with every classes, club rooms, gymnasium, etc. Seiryo school is one of the best location RGG has made and it offers a variety of side cases and club activities. Kamurocho is also back, looking great as ever but due to the fewer side cases/things to do in Kamurocho, it ends up not comparable to how much Ijincho offers in terms of things to do.

School Story is a major addition to Lost Judgment, tasking Yagami into Mystery Research Club in Seiryo High School. 10 school clubs, each with different minigames, most of them are addicting and really fun to do. The highlights are the Dance Club, Biker Gang, Skateboard and Boxing Gym. All of them have extensive stories with well-written characters in each of them. Stories about standing up to yourself, friendship, romance are all in School Story, making the lives of many Seiryo students so much more relatable. There are some frustrating moments, though, like in Robotics Club where the RNG in certain match is really bad, but it's just a small annoying thing in a sea of other great activities. Aside from this, side cases this time around have more recurring-type of cases where one cases lead to another, resulting in a series of cases about a certain individuals. I found this to be good and bad. The good part is that it sells the idea that it takes quite a bit of time to solve cases, and it has much more room to build up certain situations or relationship about certain NPC, the bad part is that it lacks variety in the overall side cases. But overall, the side cases are still as great as the previous entries, with some of the most hilarious, wholesome and exciting in the series. Plus some characters from the original Judgment makes comebacks in some of the side cases, and even The School Story which is always a great thing.

That said, there are some questionable changes and just downright worse changes in Lost Judgment over its predecessor. The item shortcut now only has 1 slot. There's only 1 button to change fighting styles even though Yagami has 4 styles to choose. Mortal Wounds are gone now (which is a good thing actually) but it does remove the danger of Mortal Attacks from bosses. The eating animation is gone and replaced by just text pop up and bust image. The friend system is gone, even though it makes the city much more lively with different vendor npc and their short stories. The side cases tied to Girlfriends are awful, the stories and characters in those side cases are actually good but the fact that you need to essentially do the Girlfriend activity to complete the side cases (plus with the added DLC, meaning you "have" to cheat multiple girlfriends) is worse compared to how Girlfriends worked in the original Judgment. You can't reject girlfriends' love confessions is just awful and really weird, considering you can in original Judgment. The message function in Yagami's phone is not used enough for the other activities, this also applies to some of the detective segments that are not used enough in the main story and side cases, like the lockpicking, tailing (though it is improved a lot better now), disguise and drone. In fact, there is no single moment where you need to use drone in any of the main story or side cases which is baffling, considering that drone played quite a big part in some missions in the original Judgment.

Overall though, these blemishes are nothing major. Lost Judgment is still one of the best RGG games to date. An improvement over its predecessor with a much more grounded story and much more grey area the theme goes to explore. RGG instant classic, with lots of things to do, more charms in almost every activities, stellar combat that is always exciting to do and deep complex story about bullying and flawed legal system.\t\t\t\t\t
