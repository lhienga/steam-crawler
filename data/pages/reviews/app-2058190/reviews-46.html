\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother Yakuza game down in the 100% books. While not as annoying to do as a Yakuza 5 this game was tedious in many completion regards like the 50 hour professor side case lmao. Anyway this review will be about the game itself and not as a completionist.

Spoilers below

First of all, the story.
- While not my favourite game due to repetitive tasks, this is my favourite plot line in the yakuza series so far, beating like a dragon, 0 and 2 for me. The continuous theme of bullying and suicide is something that yakuza usually doesn't tackle as its main focus is usually on bizarre evil yakuza plot twists or corrupted police. This game also has my new favourite yakuza villain in Kuwana as he is genuinely the most grey shaded antagonists in terms of motives and desires. The line of good and evil is so blurred that your main bro Kaito even says he doesn't know who is right at the start of the final battle and he leaves you to it. Not to mention that masterpiece of a tear jerker send off where everything he tried to protect falls apart and the characters around him apologise for making him keep such a dark secret for 13 years. Him crying out is heartwrenching as he just balls with tears post Yagami fight.
- Yagami is also here but his character development is not as deep as the previous title and kaito takes much more of a backseat as well, but i think this is what makes Kuwana shine the most. Another massive positive is how they dont do the bad guy turns good then dies trope at the end, bro doesnt even get arrested, he just walks off into the shadows, i loved it.
- Literal Only complaint with the story is how Soma has Yagami and Kaito dead as ♥♥♥♥ in the bar but he just leaves after the bar owner calls and there is just no good defence for that (yes i know its Kuwana dad).
- Also Soma goes on a mad killing spree. Gotta be the most deaths in any yakuza game including the Kaito files. I like it, feels like they took Saejimas advice to Kido from 4 and got their balls out

Content/Gameplay:
- While there is no karoke there are plenty of activities for you to partake in with yagami. A lot of the dumb detective trailing stuff is back but it was toned down from the first game. Some of the questionable observation gimmicks return like "turn on the lights" lol. In my opinion the school professor storyline, while fun, was not worth taking up 40ish hours as it ate up side quests. Of the 42 side quests 13 have to do with one illegal detective agency as well which was very boring. Some quests ended after literally 1 minute, no joke. School def ate development here. While i sound harsh here its because this is all my complaints, i got nothing else besides some of the tedious side content as a completionist and some boring side cases.
- Also, Snake style is the best style in the series, ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. so cool.

Kaito Files:
- Idk how much this dlc cost on release but for the sale bundle i got it was worth it as i enjoyed it more than the yakuza 2 majima story dlc. Nice to see old fighting styles return with kaito and i love how on stealth missions you can get caught and still knock the guy out unlike yamagi because you are a brute. Also, instead of running around crowds kaito pushed them away in chase missions XD. Made me laugh

Conclusion/Summary
- Game is excellent, my fav yakuza story and second fav overall yakuza game after 0. While not my top 3 protagonist, Yagami has the best side cast in the series hands down. I hope they will release a third game down the road or have these characters make a return in a mainline game. If you like judgement 1 this is a must buy since it has improved the formula in every regard.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start this review off by saying I loved the first Judgment, and I had extremely high expectations going into Lost Judgment. It definitely didn't disappoint and excels at every aspect.

Firstly, the story and characters. The story in Judgment was extremely interesting, revolving around government conspiracies and medical trials. Lost Judgment definitely does not disappoint in that aspect, and revolves around the murders of former high school bullies. The best part about Lost Judgment is that, as the tagline says, nothing is black and white. Every character in the story has understandable motivations and goals, and no one is just evil for the sake of being evil. In addition to the story, you have the main cast of characters. Most of which return from the previous game. All of the main cast have amazing chemistry and they're all likable, (or hateable for some of the villains) at some points making you feel like you're a part of Yagami Detective Agency/Yokohama 99.

Secondly, the setting and side content. The first Judgment was set exclusively in Kamurocho, the city which has been a part of the Yakuza series since its debut on the PS2 in 2005. That's not inherently a bad thing, however, but it definitely made the game feel a bit stale if you played through it after playing through Yakuza games where you've explored the ins and outs of Kamurocho already. Lost Judgment expands this to Isezaki Ijincho, Yokohama, which was introduced in Yakuza: Like a Dragon. Ijincho is roughly three times the size of Kamurocho, which gives more opportunities for interesting side cases story beats. Speaking of side cases, the side content in this game is insane. Not only do you have the side cases (or substories as they're referred to in Yakuza) and regular minigames we're used to in the Yakuza/Judgment series, you also have the school stories, which are a set of brand new minigames each with their own storyline attached to them, with an overarching story relating to the Mystery Research Club.

Finally, the combat. I'll start off by saying Lost Judgment has the best combat seen in the Yakuza/Judgment series ever. I can't begin to describe how fluid and fun everything feels but it's definitely the best. Lost Judgment includes 3 main fighting styles, Crane, Tiger, and Snake, with a fourth boxing style which can be progressed through one of the school stories. Crane and Tiger return from Judgment, and they feel so much better in this game. In Judgment, the only upgradable style was Tiger, with any upgrades that weren't explicitly for Tiger applying to both styles. In Lost Judgment each style has its own skill tree and pros and cons for each situation. While you can just use one style for the whole game, the game encourages you to switch it up, with the Hidden Arts buffs and SP bonuses. The Hidden Arts buffs essentially encourage you to use every style in your battle, as they give you specific benefits when you perform specific actions in a style. For example, if you successfully evade your enemy's attack while in Crane style, and have a fully upgraded Hidden Arts skill, you can boost your attack speed for 12 seconds. This might not sound like a huge improvement, but if you switch styles and activate the Hidden Arts for that style, it resets that timer, plus giving you an extra buff. This is extremely helpful, especially in boss fights, and encourages you to switch up your playstyle. Also included are SP bonuses, which return from Yakuza 0, which were money bonuses. They essentially work the same way as they did in that game, giving you SP for performing specific actions in a battle, one of which being to defeat an enemy in each of the three styles, so to level up faster, you'll need to incorporate all 3 main styles into your playstyle.


Overall, Lost Judgment is without a doubt my favourite game in the Like a Dragon series, and I encourage anyone who is on the fence to check it out, as it is an amazing experience throughout the whole game.\t\t\t\t\t
