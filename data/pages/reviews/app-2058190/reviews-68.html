So easy-to-view template out of the way, I never understood the idea of wanting to replay a game for the first time...until this game. I've been obsessed with RGG's work for many years and always wanted to learn more and more about each game so I never felt that even for the rest of the games. I remember watching Cohh play the game on early access and fell in love. But what I wasn't aware of is how different it is when playing.
I fell in love with so many new characters and the writing for each of them is phenomenal. From the antagonists to even the school story students, they all were so compelling.
Speaking of the school subplot, the mini-games were amazing. Even one I had difficulty with for a little while such as the robotics club, once I figured out a proper build for myself, it was incredibly fun. I wanted to complete even the last one and it was so worth it. I love the students like they were my own kids. I went through the boxing mini-game straight through in one night, it is THAT fun. My carpal tunnel issues did not give me any mercy when I went to bed at 3am right after.
The Kaito files were also a joy, with many more well-written characters.
Also, may make some people completely ignore all my other opinions but, play dubs. Just do it. For many games I prefer sub of course but the English VAs really understood how to match the energy that the story provides.
Only gripe is I don't get all the cats to put in the agencies like I did in Judgment. Finally enough space for them but nope, Sad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLost Judg(e)ment has one of the best stories RGG Studio has ever put out. The focus around bullying is very emotionally resonant and I found the climatic final boss to be one of the best in the Yakuza/Like a Dragon universe. LJ improves on the previous game in almost every way, since there are few tailing missions (though the cover system for it has been greatly improved) and the combat styles feel much more fluid and the new styles are really distinct and enjoyable. I found the chase sections tedious since the person you are chasing often gets an arbitrary boost of speed just to run around the same city block until their health bar drains.

Otherwise, there is very little negative to say about this game. I really like the new "School Stories" where each club has their own minigame to master. I am a big fan of a dancing minigame returning to the franchise. All I need is Yagami to do karaoke.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone else said about the first game, stop reading the reviews and play the game yourself! But if you're stubborn like I am, keep reading! If you've played any of the previous Yakuza games, you'll like this one. It's among my favourites already! If you haven't played the others, I'd still recommend it. Depends on how cautious you want to be regarding certain changes in the lore of the Yakuza series. Anyways, be warned of general side content spoilers, as well as an extremely vague overview of the story (no details whatsoever).

Firstly, this game is awesome! Everything about it. If the first Judgment was the Dragon Engine's Kiwami, then in the reverse order of things; this is Dragon Engine's 0. In almost every way possible. From the four fighting styles, to the absurd amount of deep mini-games, to the endlessly entertaining side cases; this game has it all. Great graphics, beautiful music, an amazing presentation overall. You won't want to stop playing!

Secondly, it's a huge game. I have 107 hours at the time of this review. I've only completed around 60% of the game. That's including the main story and all the side cases, some general stuff too. There's so much more to do if you want to be a completionist. It's an addictive game! You can join a robotics club, a biker gang, a boxing gym, a dancing club, etc. There's photography, arcade games in arcades and at home, skateboarding, gambling, darts, batting, VR games, etc. You can even get four different girlfriends! In the game, probably not for real! My favourites are the side cases, dancing, boxing, and robotics. The side cases are a lot of fun. Hilarious, and almost always bigger than they initially seem. A lot of them are interconnected with each other, and are related to the various mini games. That's a trademark of this series, how everything you do is made bigger through the characters you play, and the ones you encounter.

So let's talk of these characters. Overall, a similar cast. But with some remarkable additions that make the story more engaging. Yagami is cool as always, tries to be sensible, he's fairly believable though naive at times in terms of his beliefs. Kaito is hilarious, clumsy, and awesome. Made even better with his own story, the Kaito files were epic! Sugiura is still a nuisance, but he's part of the team. Tsukumo is the ultimate problem solver. Genda law office people are still there, and oh man Saori-san! The antagonists of the game will be made well known to you, and they're among the most memorable ones in the series. You'll just have to see for yourself.

The story itself covers a wide range of sensitive topics in a fair and balanced way. Those being sexual harassment, bullying, suicide, murder, justice, corruption, etc. They give way for nuance rather than sticking to stereotypes, which is what makes the story interesting. How they managed to connect so many different facets of life together in a believable and well put manner is beyond me. Some moments have becomes memes, however serious they may have been intended to be initially. The death of the author, look it up!

How about the main gameplay loop? It's a bit different to the first game. The first one really hammered in the detective side of things, and that was something I enjoyed a lot. But in this game, it's more varied.

As usual, the game weaves together the gameplay with the main story, linking it to side content when it can. Tailing has improved, being much more free flow than it was in the first game. Which makes it more believable. Inspection mode has also been improved thanks to the new gadgets that add more layers to the experience. Makes a lot of sense for a modern detective!

Fighting is contender for the best in the series so far, rivalling 0 easily. Four fighting styles that are all distinct but can be switched between at will, leading to endless creative options for you to explore, with the most insane juggles the games have seen so far. It's ridiculous, in a good way.

The best thing that they did here is they've made Crane useful! Man, it was a meme in the first game. Now it's actually insanely good! Mainly due to its own counter that allows you to combo the opponent. Each style has its own purpose, which I like. Snake allows you to parry everything and forever. Get those weapons outta here! But you can't move for ♥♥♥♥. Tiger has tiger drop, of course. Flux tissure is tough, but still super good. Boxing allows for so many counters, charge moves, launching attacks, stun links, and the ability to sway in and out. Though it's fairly limited in terms of EX moves, and its movement is not as good as Crane or Tiger.

They've also added stealth and parkour, which feel like mechanics that could/should have been here before. Rather basic, but they do the job. A problem is how they're only used when they game wants you to use them, you have no choice of doing them freely. I'd prefer that they weren't so basic, since they appear more often than lockpicking does. Which has also been simplified, no more thumb turn bypasses. No lockpicking jobs either. A little strange.

Contender for best soundtrack. From the songs in each fighting style, to the main menu theme and how dynamic it is, to the boss themes, even the old substory music we've grown to love since ancient times. Man, the boss themes are epic. Viper, Unwavering Belief, Liumang's Chant, K.O.G, even the generic boss theme Vorarephilia is sick! Of course, the Kaito files has awesome stuff too! The Crimson Devil, A Duel for Love, epic stuff! Special mention to Amon's theme Lineage of Clan.

RGG know what they're doing. They know what we want, and they keep giving it to us. Is it perfect? No. But is it as close to that as they can do in almost every game? Pretty much. Still some work to be done to flesh out certain things that are too basic or simply outdated. Some of that is part of the charm, while the rest is a bit annoying. Overall, it's incredible how they managed to make so many good games in this series. And how this spin off feels right at home. As if this is the 10th Judgment game, and not the second one. Fantastic achievement on their part! I hope that there are no more problems, and that they can go ahead and make another one!

9/10 - "Tabo"\t\t\t\t\t
