\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Goes unbelievably hard, buy it and play it now

Having not beaten Lost Judgment on console so I could wait for it on PC, I'm very happy with my choice, as Lost Judgment improves on the gameplay presented in Judgment in almost every single way.

Combat might be at its peak across the whole Yakuza/Like a Dragon/Judgment series, with four styles to choose from, fleshed out upgrades, and amazing animation it feels incredible. I've never looked out for fights as much in other games in the series like I do in Lost Judgment. You also spend a good amount of time early on beating up school kids (amazing).

It's absolutely PACKED full with content, with most returning activities given extra polish and depth and a ton of new activities that are all fun and engaging. Side stories, clubs, school stories, mini games, you can even buy old Sega master system carts and play a variety of games in your office.

Tons of quality of life features like the skateboard especially are very welcome. Alongside that, a lot of the stuff that received criticism from the first game for over staying their welcome like the Tailing missions are surprisingly improved and also a lot less frequent.

The detective features in this game are much more fleshed out in comparison to Judgment. You can start investigation at any time even in the open world and in missions, and you have new tools including a sound amp, a noise detector, and a dog to carry out your detective work.

Ijincho from Yakuza: Like a Dragon returns as the main city this time, however you have the ability to go back to Kamurocho at any time. Both of them are looking better than ever with updated lighting, effects, and density, making this the best looking game in the series bar none.

Audio is great, with both quality English and Japanese voices. The soundtrack in this game is INSANE though, with some of the best music from the entire series. I've played every mainline game in the series to completion except for this one and this game's OST sets a high bar.

PC Port is great, but has some stability issues. Runs great and looks great but for some reason when I play the VR mini game, I've crashed 3 times alone just in that mode, and never crashed outside of that. There's also a weird black flickering only during night time that is rare but noticeable. Both can be easily patched soon I'm sure but just making it known.

The story is the only thing comparable in quality to the first Judgment game, which is a fairly solid bar to reach for. The first Judgment had a lot more moments that stand out as great set pieces with events unfolding through the entire game and constantly evolving until the very last second of the story even if it did have a lot of moments where it was taking a while to get going or it felt like they tried to pad out the story length.

Lost Judgment's story on the other hand feels more emotionally intense. It grabs you from the get go and feels like the developers are making this game to say a lot of things that they truly feel strongly about, which is very refreshing to experience, even if the latter third of the game feels like it might run out of steam after all the secrets and plot points are revealed. I'd say it's very subjective as to which story is better, even if they are both great stories. But I prefer the faster pace, the character arcs, the themes, and the villains in Lost Judgment over the original myself. I wouldn't hold it against anyone for preferring the first though.

I'm a humongous fan of the Yakuza/Like a Dragon/Judgment series, they're all great fun without an outright terrible entry to be found. However playing Lost Judgment makes the original Judgment look like a proof of concept demo in comparison, and even makes some of the other Yakuza games look like ancient relics. Cannot recommend this game enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI did Judgment 2 years ago on PS4, one of my favorites PS4 games, so what can I say about this follow up ?
IT'S EVEN BETTER, THERE IS TOO MUCH CONTENT

+ Honestly pretty much everything is there, but better : 4 fighting style (vs 2 on the first game), A New city to explore alongside Kamurocho, a LOT of minigames and side activities,...
+ The story is great, even if I like more the story from the first game, but this one about school harrassement is really interesting.
+ Music is cool, as always with SEGA
+ This PC version works well, no problems or anything with my high end PC
+ Gameplay is fun and varied : classic Yakuza beat'em up style combat and a LOT of minigames, with some of them that suffise themselve honestly

Maybe the only things I'll say against this sequel is :
- The girlfriend system, less engaging than the first game
- There is a lot of gimmicks having barely any usage (exemple : You don't need to use the drone many time for quests or events)
- Sometimes, and mostly on some activities, the game is too simple (I played on Hard)

Though those lows are nothing really, I'm just quibbling.

Ah and most importantly...
YOU CAN BEAT UP SCHOOL BULLIES IN THIS GAME.

If you're a fan of Yakuza, just go it's one of the best games of the serie (and spin-offs like here)
If not, I recommend the first 'Judgment' before doing this one because both games are great and making it in order is more satisfying since 'Lost Judgment' is kind of a big upgrade to the first one.\t\t\t\t\t
