\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLost Judgment is one of the best RGG studio games I've played - and I've played most of them. My personal favorite is Yakuza 0, but this is the first time another title in this series has come close to dethroning it. The combat, the story, the characters, the sidequests, the minigames, the music - it's all done incredibly well. It's also the first RGG game using the Dragon Engine that has combat I'd actually consider as well made and fun as Y0's combat. 4 Stances (plus 2 for Kaito in the DLC) if you include the secret stance from the side content, and they're all much improved from the original Judgment (which I enjoyed, but not nearly as much as Lost Judgment)

Also, Yagami is great and has really come into his own as a character here. That boy just don't quit, and he's got a great squad with him. Reminds me of Kiryu's glory days in Yakuza 4 with the boys.

tl;dr, Lost Judgment is a fantastic game and one of RGG Studios best games yet. It has the best combat since Y0, and basically great everything else. BUY IT!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSolid improvement over previous entry.
If you liked the previous game, or Ryu Ga Gotoku studio (RGG) games in general this is not a miss.

To speak in broad terms, this game, much like its predecessor is RGG's attempt at making a detective protagonist.
Keyword is attempt, as at the end of the day this is still an RGG game, and as with all RGG games, the protagonist defeats a small army by the end of it.
Final chapter in particular has him (along with 3 basically useless AIs) going against around 100 enemies + 2 tanky bosses.
That's not to say that the story sucks, there is no mystery, or there is no sleuthing taking place.
All of that is definitely present, and is at a more than acceptable level.
There is a well-presented mystery, and what I found an interesting story.
What I am trying to complain about is that this is not so much a "detective" game as it is an action brawler.
At the end of the day, it is not the evidence you gather that brings you closer to the truth, but rather beating basically everyone into submission.
It works as far as RGG games go, but if you are looking for a more "mystery solving" game, this is not it.

I would say that I liked the story of this game more than its predecessor.
As with previous title, I think that the protagonist does not deliver his point across strongly enough.
Once again, I find that the antagonist is very relatable, even more so than Yagami (the protagonist) himself.
Antagonist makes a clear point as to why he does what he does, and why he sees it as necessary.
He has collaborators who agree with him, and the antagonist does his damn hardest to not compromise them.
This is to the point where he stays steadfast in his conviction to not harm his (in his opinion most important) collaborator after being betrayed by them, and even crying when they decide to take Yagami's side and give themselves to the police.
Meanwhile, Yagami's main argument seemed to have revolved around one bystander dying, which kinda begs the question if that's the only problem he had with the antagonist's actions.

I do not have much to say about graphics and gameplay of this title.
It runs on the modern RGG engine, and has gotten the same treatment as all RGG games.
There are a lot of side activities to be had, definitely more than is necessary.
Graphics look nice, game runs smoothly (even on linux).
Compared to previous game, I would say there have been massive improvements.
Combat got better compared to previous title.
There is a new style (technically two), and it is an absolute pleasure to use.
For once, I start to feel that the combat is not just a DPS race slugfest, but actually skill based.
There were still a few annoyances that left it feeling like the dumb slugfest, but overall, an improvement.
SP is much easier to procure in this game, I felt like there was barely enough for anything in previous game, whereas in this one I was swimming in it, and got basically all upgrades I wanted half-way in (though I did grind for a few hours out of personal volition).
This entry also does away with the annoying "mortal wounds" mechanic of the previous game, there is no longer a need to deal with annoying health limitations.
This is also probably the first game since Yakuza 0 that actually got me somewhat interested in its side content.
"School stories" is an interesting take, but ultimately I gave up because I could not be bothered to go through the dancing segments that you are forced to take.

To summarize, this is a solid entry in RGG's lineup.
People who are familiar with RGG's games can expect more of the same goodness.
People who are unfamiliar are in for a pleasant trip to Kamurocho and Ijincho (though, perhaps grab and do the first game beforehand. It is not required, but would provide context)\t\t\t\t\t
