Strong combat, weak story

The combat is undoubtedly one of the best RGGS has to offer, but as fan of yakuza/judgment stories which IMO is still so much more important I have to say Lost Judgment's story is the absolute weakest I've experienced so far.
Whereas in the first game Judgment it was incredibly interesting to learn about the "fallen" lawyer Yagami, his backstory and motivation, and experience his character growth,
the sequel offers nowhere near as much character development time: Yagami stays the same from beginning to the end, his character story incredibly bland, frustratingly boring to watch.
There are some major moments, where the game could have used them to make him more interesting, but no, after some tiny emotional outbursts or occasional brain fogs he returns back to the same old calm, wise character.
The school setting of the main story is refreshing, the horrible problems of school bullying are handled well (shout out to Sawa-Sensei) but it just doesn't help when the protagonist remains so bland for the majority of the game.
So what about the other returning characters?

I liked... Kaito and Shirosaki, I guess, but what about the rest?
You could delete Sugiura and replace his tiny parts with Kaito, absolutely nothing of value would be lost.
Tsukumo aka "Hackerman", the typical boring plot device, to make some occasional discoveries.
Hoshino? Laughable, I don't even want to talk about him.
What they did to Genda hurt me, I liked the old mentor in judgment who never gave up on Yagami, influencing his character growth. Here? Nearly invisible.
Mafuyu, fan favorite, the same treatment.
Lost Judgment massacred so many cool characters from the first game, that I wonder if the writers were suffering from burnouts or were just simply replaced by worse ones.

Oh, and don't get me started with the antagonists.
There are so many repetitive, redundant moments in the story where Yagami and one of the main antagonists meet up, bicker about their motives, over and over again.
Maybe trying to overcompensate for the lack of depths of the other antagonists?

So what about the side stories?
Oh boy. Ignoring the disgusting fact that SEGA or RGGS hid some of the better ones behind locked DLCs, the majority of the stories are a boring mess.
I just finished the game, and for the love of God, I seriously can't remember most of them (besides the school side stuff) because how utterly bland they were.
I liked the mystery club, boxing, and some others, one of the highlights of the side stories. But when you compare them to the rest, it's just... sad.

Ryu Ga Gotoku can do so much better, they showed it over and over again.
But maybe they should take a step back, and invest more time?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am going to recommend this game for the portrayal of bullying and the consequences it has. I was bullied during my school years a lot and it let me a lot of scars and trauma for me to battle them. That aspect of the game was done wonderfully and accurately. I believe that in the rest of the game that it lacks good writing (or at least on-par with the first one).
Gameplay-wise, this is really incredible. Very few slow tailing missions, almost none lockpicking (which is made easier) and more gadgets to play with. The combat is a big step up from Judgement. On the final chapters, when I unlocked many of his abilities, Yagami feels really good to control to. He may not be as powerful as Kiryu or Saejima, but I think he reached the level of fun that Akiyama had in Y4. I really liked how they nerfed Tiger and buffed Crane, as well as the new style Turtle. This is the only game in the whole Yakuza/LAD franchise that I used every style the same and I didn't prefer one.
Story-wise, in the early chapters and the school stories are really good. I didn't really like any of the side-cases, which is a bummer because I play this games to have some laugh with the sub-stories as much as to get sad with the main story. The latter chapters have a lot of bad pacing and a lot of stuff are repeated. I think I read the same dialogue up to five times in one hour. Some story-bits are even taken directly from the first game like Saori infiltrating the cabaret club . Some characters from the first game that returned didn't have the same charm they had, like Siguira and even Yagami. Yagami's whole character arc from the first game is almost forgotten in this one.
That being said, I really enjoyed my time and I may have said that it's the series best brawler if I hadn't played The Man Who Erased His Name mid-way through my play through of this one. I haven't played Kaito's File as of the time of writing this review, but I will write a separate review there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic sequel to a fantastic game part of a fantastic world.

Your role is detective and former Lawyer Takayuki Yagami. Called to Ijincho to investigate a suicide at Seijo High School your case quickly begins to tangle itself with a few other plotlines which I won;t talk about for spoilers.

Lost Judgment is an absolutely gorgeous game. I don't know how RGG does it but they are on top of it in the graphics department. It's easy to see the beautifully crafted world around you but small details such as pants wrinkling as you move creates a highly detailed world.

The combat is excellent and fluid. You are given 2 fighting styles and unlock 2 more for a total of 4 fighting styles. Crane and Tiger return giving hefty upgrades and REAL utility to the crane style while nerfing Tiger so it's not busted easy to beat foes (seriously, that 1-inch punch-like move made the game a cake walk). Snake is a style that has you focusing only effectively catching attacks and and swiftly striking back although most of this is done through heat button prompts. Boxing is a style borrowing moves from the later unlockable boxing side quest. The fact that you can juggle most bosses makes this style head and shoulders above the rest. All are fun to play and have their utility throughout the game.

I won't talk a lot about the story but it's well crafted, detailed and puts you through a rollercoaster of emotions.

As of now I have not completed Kaito Files, will update my review when completed.

This is genuinely one of the best games I've played. Sale or full price, with all of the typical RGG filler of side quests, stories and mini games, or just breezing through the 25 hour story (you'll find at least 1 side activity you'll end up enjoying, trust me), this is another RGG game that offers so much bang for your buck with all the high quality of a Japanese AAA game.\t\t\t\t\t
