\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot as good as the first Judgment, which I thought was excellent, but still a good sequel. I think the story overall was not well done, though it has plenty of great moments. It sort of has two halves to it.

The first half is better and closer to what I expected with the detective aspects and discovering all the elements surrounding the main case. I feel the mystery here is good and on par with the first game.

The latter half feels less natural, as if redeveloped afterwards where they couldn't figure out how to take the story further to make something longer and more climactic. There's some good stuff here, but my biggest issue with it is that it adds little new and very slowly compared to the first half. You're constantly location hopping just to hear the same conversations and names drop in attempts to get slightly new information. The mystery and detective elements feel dead where you're instead either beating around the bush or just pressuring others until someone finally gives you something new or some new event occurs.

Other stuff:
The evidence selection and questioning this time around feels more tacked on than something where you're needing to make meaningful, thought out choices. For me it tended to usually be either completely obvious what to pick where it's the latest piece of evidence you just obtained moments ago (why is there even a choice here?), or sometimes confusingly ambiguous (many of these could technically apply).

The trailing, sneaking, and photography were minimally used as gameplay elements. The climbing stuff which I believe is new felt unwelcome a lot of times since they were long and slowed down the pacing.

The school setting was something new and neat, but I was disappointed with how little it's actually used overall in the main story.

The school stories, which is sort of a primary sidequest not connected to the main story, is way too long, unbalanced in how time is divided between the clubs, heavily padded with repetitive minigame requirements, and does not fit at all chronologically when doing them in the middle of the main story (where some of the activities are likely taking place over at least several weeks). Some of the minigames are pretty fun and had a lot of development put into them, but I was exhausted at the end due to how many times they make you do them to progress.

The game is, for me at least, unwelcomely more violent. Despite being rated M, Judgment and the Yakuza games (though I've only played 2 of all of them) were comparatively tame. It was something I appreciated going into any of them since I knew I'd get some good mature drama and action without anything too unsettling. Overall this was thankfully nearly at the same level, but there is a pretty graphic murder scene and a one time mild threat of sexual assault on a story character.\t\t\t\t\t
