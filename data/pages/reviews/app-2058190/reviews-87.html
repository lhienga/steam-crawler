\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros: Better story in my opinion, I like solving mysteries and finding new stuffs more than flashbacks. The ending feels more impactful. The main theme of the game is bully and the story around it is well crafted. Much better combat than Judgement, got 2 more new styles and the flow of the combat is improved a lot. Most of the side quests are packed into the school stories and it's pretty good. Besides mini games, the open-world now has squire collecting, which is a very interesting puzzle activity that you can use the detective gadgets to solve (for unlocking skills and materials). The school stories has many mini games, some of them are very good and interesting ex: boxing, skating (oh yeah, there is a new way of traversing, skating. But it's a bit limited), racing,... Solving some side cases using keywords in Chatter is pretty fun as well.

Cons: While some of the mini games are great there is one that it's extremely boring and long. That's the robot club activity. There are 4 long quest lines in the school stories and the robot it's easily the worst one. The side quests outside of the school stories are a bit lacking and uninteresting. They somehow butchered some of the old mini games, the biggest one they butchered is the dating game: no chatting, the chatting now is collecting key words in the open-world and then watch an awkward, no sound, no dialogue animation. The quest lines for girlfriend are much shorter. The batting is harder, the darts feel like ♥♥♥♥ and it's my favorite mini game in Judgement, even if you aim properly and the power is correct, sometimes you'll still magically missed. Drone racing and VR feels the same, removing dice limit actually makes VR a bit better. There is also 1 mini game in VR but it's ♥♥♥♥♥♥ and nobody plays it anyway.

Overall, it's still a very good game with a very good story, much more contents than the first one and definitely worth buying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: performance is not that bad but is not up to par with other installments, combat and general gameplay is vastly improved over its predecessor and resurrects some good ideas from other RGG games, plot is marginally more restrained than the typical RGG game and is better for it. there is a major spoiler for yakuza: like a dragon in the game's plot.

TECHNICAL:

I'm pretty sure the performance improved as I was playing the game by virtue of me buying the game on release and important patches being released within a few months while I was actively playing it. That being said the performance was fine but not good. You can obviously tell when a new area is being loaded because the game freezes for a couple seconds, and this was a thing until the very end of the game. Also, whenever you boot the game up, the first fight I would get into would have titanic frame drops. There were also a couple of crashes in the early and mid game but as said before this was probably because of things that were patched.

COMBAT:

The combat is way better than in Judgment. For one, Crane style has a reason to exist now due to various buffs and unique moves that Tiger doesn't have. Tiger was punched up a bit with some new moves, and a new style was added, Snake, which is throw and counter based, akin to how Tanimura would fight in Yakuza 4, except improved. Snake also has the bonus of having a set of "instant-kill" EX actions to compensate for its slower speed and middling damage output (compared to the other styles). This particular port comes with most (not all) DLC pre-packaged so you also get the Boxer style which I used maybe twice in the whole game.

Yagami can equip gear in this game, which is great. He still can't equip weapons or pick up guns, though, which is unfortunate.

SIDE CONTENT/EXPLORING:

You get to go to Ijincho in this game. It's not significantly different than it was when it was first introduced. Same with Kamurocho. The new area is Seiryo High School, which is obviously not a city but a campus you can walk around.

The bulk of the side content is in the "School Stories" which are in Seiryo High. Unlike substories/side cases these have overarching plot lines and unique minigames akin to the side stories in Yakuza 5. They vary in depth and quality: the Robotics Club has a massive slate of parts you need to build and a bunch of robot battles, and the ESports Club is just you playing one of the arcade games against a weak AI.

Minigames mostly are the same. Kamuro of the Dead is now Hama of the Dead and is set in Ijincho, the Drone Race is easier, and Dice and Cube is now a race against an AI rival instead of you wandering around grabbing money until you run out of dice.

The "Celios" in-universe franchise also returns after having been unseen since Yakuza 5. Aircelios is 90% not being able to see what is going on because enemies are indistinguishable for debris. Boxcelios and its spin-offs always were the arcade's weak points.

OTHER GAMEPLAY:

There's some story stuff revolving around tailing, parkour, and stealth. Tailing has some mechanical changes but it is still very boring to follow someone around for a long time with nothing else happening. Parkour and stealth are new. Parkour is not particularly in depth and the sections don't last very long. I wouldn't call it bad but I wouldn't call it fun either. The stealth sections are very restrictive; you have to go to particular spots to hide and throw things in particular spots or else you can't progress. It lacks any degree of choice and feels more like pushing a cart down an aisle than playing a game.

PLOT:

Remarkably, this game does not have a lot of insane out-of-the-blue twists. It's quite grounded even when the conspiracy plot engines start revving up. The plot has school bullying as one of its themes, which is certainly closer to home for many than the majority of RGG games. It seems that Yakuza: Like a Dragon has started a sort of trend within the franchise of more directly discussing and centering around specific societal problems.

Speaking of Yakuza: Like a Dragon, there is a major plot spoiler for that game within the first twenty minutes or so of this one, and mentioned several times after that twenty minute mark. It's an offhand remark but it reveals an integral twist. It's not avoidable, so if you want to dodge that spoiler you may have to hold off this game for a while. Some cameos and organizations also return from Yakuza: LAD but that's not a spoiler.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic title from the Ryu Ga Gotoku. The sequel to Judgment is just as entertaining and fascinating as the first, and if you love Yakuza games, this is another one to add to your collection. As in the Yakuza games, the main protagonist is Takayuki Yagami, a former lawyer turned freelance detective who now finds himself in another job assignment that goes far deeper than he first thought and brings him closer to some people in the government than he suspected. The game encourages and rewards exploration, and while it is not an entirely open world, there is enough exploration in the city and beyond to keep you entertained. Along the way, you can do side missions for clients or help people in the City, as well as play a few side games ranging from Dancing, Boxing, and Drone Racing

The school stories are a lot of fun, with interesting stories and characters, and I've found myself paying more attention to them than the main story, which isn't to say the main story is bad. The main plot is amazing. Once again, I found myself emotionally attached to the characters and eager to find out what happens next. There are parts that are shocking and parts that make me laugh. Overall, it's a Masterpiece game.

===== 2nd RUN on Legend Difficulty =====
I just wanted to let people know that the Legend difficulty now has full access to the checkpoints that other difficulties get, so you don't have to reload your save if you die or fail a section. The game is fairly easy if you play it on Normal Difficultly, thus I quite like this change. NO more Tiger Style Ex/ Crane Style EX and smashing the Y button on your controller.

I'm focused on the main story on this run so I understand better what's happening there are parts that I get more and still mind-blown by the twist in the parts of some chapters, well gonna say it's still one of the best stories in single-player games that I play. Still a Masterpiece for me.

10/10 I wish to see more Judgment Sequels in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
