\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best games that RGG made. They improved the game's combat by reworking the Crane style and refining the juggling system from the first Judgment game and in addition with the two new styles: Snake and Boxer. Boxer is more of an optional style since it's part of the School Stories Expansion DLC and it's toggleable in the options. Nevertheless, it's still a pretty good style for initiating juggle combos.

Compared to Judgment the game is way larger, it has a new city region Ijincho, a separate side story named "School Stories" which consists 10 different clubs with each having their own unique story. The lowlights for School Stories are Biker Gang and Photography Club. Completing Photography Club forces you to be Stealth mode to get to the designated destination before taking a photo and it's not a one time occurrence. Biker Gang takes too much time to complete it and the camera placement in the minigame is such a weird choice.
Speaking of minigames, there are plenty of them that you can do here and it's much easier to get cheat items while completing achievements.

The story overall is not as great as Judgment's but it's still decent compared to other Yakuza games' looking at you 2, 4 and 5 . As mentioned previously, the stealth sections ruin the flow progressing through the story and you can still fail them pretty easily. The boss fights and long battles here are awesome with the game's improved combat. On top of that, dynamic intros and themes for selected fights are memorable and phenomenal.

To summarize, if you liked the previous game or enjoy beat-em up Yakuza, you're going to love this and I recommend you to buy it with a discount or, if you have enough, the Judgment collection.\t\t\t\t\t
