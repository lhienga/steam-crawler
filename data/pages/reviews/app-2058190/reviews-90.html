\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly, if you haven't played the original Judgement, you should go check out the reviews for that and decide if it's for you. Even if you've played every other game from Ryu Ga Gotoku Studio, I can't think of a single reason to recommend playing Lost Judgement first. These games are extremely story heavy and playing this first will give you a severely diminished experience, as context, relationships and callbacks will be totally lost on you.

That makes this review easy. Assuming you've played the original, this is more of that but even more refined. In gameplay terms at least I would say LJ along with Yakuza 0 is the pinnacle of the original style, real-time combat RGG games. The game is just extremely polished from a mechanical standpoint including its combat and vast array of minigames that range from throwaway to quite engaging (golf, gambling, arcades, VR, dating...you know the drill). You can even get about town on a skateboard, Deadly Premonition style, which makes town traversal a lot more fun. On top of that there's the usual boatload of side-quests, collectables and completionist objectives to dabble in (special mention to the School Stories side-quests which are great). The graphics are also very good, with nice models for the main characters and smooth animations. The towns themselves are fantastically detailed pieces of virtual tourism that get better with each instalment, I assume because the studio can focus on improving existing assets instead of creating new ones.

Ok, on to the game's bread and butter, the story. No spoilers here, the story takes places in your old stomping grounds of Kamurocho and, if you've played Like a Dragon, Isezaki Ijincho with a high school being one of the primary locations. Suffice to say LJ delivers an excellent detective mystery, with great writing and performances on par with the original in my opinion. Expect the usual lavish rendered cutscenes for the main story missions. One thing to be wary of; the story features some disturbing scenes and possibly upsetting subject matter (bullying, suicide).

The game took me about 55 hours to finish with a decent amount of optional content done but I would assume at least another potential 25 hours for full completion.

Overall, this is a fantastic entry in the RGG universe. Here's hoping SEGA can sort out their issues with the lead actor so we can get another sequel down the road!\t\t\t\t\t

SPOILER FREE REVIEW

I like to think this game is like the Yakuza 5 of the Judgment games. It might not have 5 characters or 5 cities, but the sheer amount of fantastic content in this game packed on top of one of the best stories in across all the Ryu Ga Gotoku games makes me wholeheartedly say that this is the RGG's best game they've put out to date. I sung the praises of Judgment in my review of that, but Lost Judgment blows that out of the water somehow.

For starters, without any spoilers, the story. It's fantastic. If you liked Judgment you'll get more of the same in this. You have your usual detective work as Yagami Detective Agency working in tandem with Genda Law Office once more with another intricate plot that slowly gives you more pieces to the puzzle, finally letting you put it together in the end. At times it can feel like it's being too complex for the sake of dramatic effect, but it usually quickly pulled away from those moments by giving you a big piece of the puzzle after all that exposition.

The gameplay/combat is the best in the series bar none. The 3 (or 4 depending on your decisions) fighting styles complement each other so well. Although for reasons I won't say, snake was the clear-cut winner for me for a multitude of reasons. Having a skateboard to traverse around Ijincho and Kamurocho was a great addition, albeit kinda finicky with how you'd hop off it if you passed over a pedestrian area for more than a second. I do hope they'll keep it and open up more traversal options in future entries, like being able to do the parkour climbing freely instead of only in instances when it was required by the story/side missions.

As for the actual side content itself, that is where this game shines in ways the other games have failed to, or at least haven't done to this standard. As expected from the previous entry, Drone Races are still here and are more or less unchanged, but have a new set of maps for Ijincho. I was disappointed that I couldn't access the Kamurocho maps in this game, but that's a minor gripe at best. As for the rest of the content present in this game, you have a dance club (this game's karaoke like rhythm mode), A skateboard freestyle competition and races, motorcycle races, a very well fleshed out boxing mode (I think it's the best side content in any of the games so far), robot territory claiming battles, and of course all the returning city activities like darts, batting cages, shogi, blackjack and poker, the arcades, golf, and probably a few other one's I'm forgetting to mention.

In conclusion, play this game if you've ever mildly enjoyed a RGG game. And even if you haven't do yourself a favor and give this game an opportunity. I truly think this is a 10/10 game, and I'll never get over the fact that this game didn't win game of the year for 2021\t\t\t\t\t
