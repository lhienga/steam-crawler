\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally good game, one of the best Yakuza game out there.
Combat system is really good, maybe only second to Kiwami 1 and that's up to debate. As an action game enjoyer (DmC, MGR, Bayonetta, God of War, Monster Hunter, etc) I never got bored of it.
Music wise, there are some bangers like Green vibes, Blue stompin', Unwavering belief and so many more ...
The story is excellent if you are into detective stuff. If you aren't some aspects might get annoying but it's not that big of a deal, the characters are endearing and it's fun seeing them interact with each other.
So all around, pretty much a perfect game, only second to the legendary Yakuza 0, simply because Yakuza 0 will never be surpassed. It's the best modern Yakuza experience you can get right now though that's for sure, better than Yakuza 7 to me personally (I don't enjoy turn based combat) but I understand some people might disagree with me on this and it's ok.

I completed the Kaito files too of course, and honestly from a customer point of view this is not worth it, it costs 30€ where I live and it only lasts a few hours. You could just watch the cinematics on youtube and save your money if you aren't a fan of Yakuza games in the first place. Also Kaito uses Kiryu's brawler and beast styles so nothing new on that level.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredible sequel to Judgment. Game play wise everything is an improvement, especially the enhanced fluidity to combat. I am not the biggest fan of the Dragon Engine beat em ups, but this one is by far my favorite. Story wise I'd say its a little weaker than Judgment due to how certain plot holes were addressed, but its still great. The substories (especially the School stories) are some of the best in the Yakuza series. I have yet to play the Kaito DLC but have only heard great things about it.
Overall I highly recommend this game but you should definitely play Judgment first.
