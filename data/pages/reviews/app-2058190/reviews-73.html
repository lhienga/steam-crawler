\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt seems like Ryu Ga Gotoku took everything they learned from creating Judgment and created this game and removed all problems that Judgment DID have.

My review is basically the same as the Judgment one, however this would have to be an 9, the story is really amazing however it lacks a good villain.

Two things that makes Lost Judgment better is the combat and the side-content.

The combat now gives you way more freedom to do more stuff and introduces more combat styles, personally I thought this made for way more satisfying gameplay, however, even on the highest difficulty, Yagami is just way too OP. Most enemies are just way too easy, ESPECIALLY bosses when you're using the DLC combat style. That thing is just busted to hell.

Side content now introduces way more interesting stories and I honestly believe it was almost at par with the main story. Without spoiling much I will simply say that there's a lot of side content that all wraps up nicely in one big thing, it almost brought tears to my eyes (it did actually.)

Amazing game with good plots and twists with a quite original story, combat is very good but a lot of it is too easy.

9/10!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying as an undercover detective beating up gangsters with random objects in the China Town district is probably the closest thing I’m ever gonna get to a Sleeping Dogs sequel. So..

9/10 Porkbuns

Lost Judgment is crazy fun and you definitely get your money’s worth with this one. While its world map is relatively small compared to other open world games, it’s densely packed with a lot interiors to explore and things to do. It feels a lot more like you’re exploring an actual city. The mini games, arcades games, and collectible playable roms alone make it worth a buy as a retro compilation. The over the top combat system is fun, especially when combined with all the environmental interaction and destruction. I’m still finding new ways to beat down high schoolers.
