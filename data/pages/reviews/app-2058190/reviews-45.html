\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat. A. Game.

Where the previous Judgement game (JUDGE EYES) is THE Yagami's case, this game is more of ONE of Yagami cases. The story in this game introduces a lot of moral grey areas, and the oversights of the law. Where the previous game is a victory lap to finish what's been started, this game is a grim, dark, and quiet affair. The "villain" of the story is insanely well-crafted and can be deeply empathised with. At the end of the game, I was sitting back in my chair going ".....dude this is so f*cked up man...". If you've been bullied before, this game will hit you. HARD.

The beat-em-up combat system in this game is the BEST out of ALL the Ryu Ga Gotoku studios games, period (and I've played them all). It's not even a contest. The different stances all complement each other, encouraging a lot of stance switching during combat. The heat moves are rewarding, flashy, and effective. Combat upgrades feel impactful, and actually upgrades gameplay. The upgrade points (SP) are given generously, but not too much to be considered "OP". The boss fights are seriously really well made. I haven't had this much fun in boss battles since Yakuza Kiwami 1. I played on Hard difficulty and was sweating so damn much in the final few boss fight scenes, but they feel very rewarding.

The sheer number of new mechanics and mini-games introduced in this game is also downright ridiculous. They expanded on the detective aspect of this game a LOT, and also the number of fun minigames are GREATLY increased. In the previous games, the extent of being a detective was quite limited. Tailing missions were done so much it kind of got boring. However, this game actually makes you feel like a PROPER detective. There's parkour, noise amplifiers, radio waves reader, using a dog to sniff out scents, etc. Some minigames they introduced are dancing (not the Yakuza 5 one, this one is better by a long shot), boxing, skateboarding, photography, and so so so many more I can't even name them. This game seriously feels like a damn theme park, I can't play them all!

Finally, the new characters in this game is very well done and well fleshed out. At first, some side characters rubbed me the wrong way, but character development kicked me in the heart, and I love them all. One very minor gripe is that I don't see one of the characters more (Tesso), I wish they could've expanded a little more on him. Hopefully we get to see him in the next Judgement game.

I haven't played the Kaito Files DLC yet, but I've heard good things about it and I'm planning to play it soon. Overall, while the plot is slightly weaker compared to the previous game (but still great overall), the other aspects of the game are 10/10. Highly recommend this game with or without a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
