\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe combat is so good that I haven't upgraded my attack damage in order to spend more time in fights. There is so much room for experimentation.

The combat is seriously up there with Yakuza 5 and 0/Kiwami. Maybe even better than them. If you get this game try incorporating all the styles when you fight. It's so much fun. A more varied approach to fighting awards more sp after battles so you may as well try.

Compared to the previous games, I found only one place that this game regresses significantly: they lock off a lot of incidental places, shortcuts, and back alleys from the over-world maps. Some areas, like in the pre-dragon engine games, have even been blocked off with invisible walls and waist-high obstacles. Stumbling into these pointless places in dragon engine games breathed much life into Kamurocho and Ijincho, so it's sad to see many of them go.

While the combat in this game is incredibly freeing it's too easy. For a sequel to this game I would like to see more difficulty. Either nerf Yagami or make more difficult and varied enemy encounters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as I enjoyed the core part of the game, I absolutely can not recommend it because of just how bad and unacceptable its ending is.

SPOILER ALERT

The game's major antagonist is a former school teacher who got fired after an incident of bullying in his class that led victim to attempted suicide. The teacher preserved video evidence of bullying (in which a bunch of pupils participated) and used it 8 years later to blackmail now adult pupils into massively abducting and murdering bullies over Japan (main case of the game is his 7th killing). Through the course of the game, many major characters voiced sympathy for him, and in the end of the game, the main character just lets the (unremorseful!) serial murderer go due to "lack of evidence".

This is totally beyond my comprehension. There was quite a lot of evidence to begin with, and even if there was not... I just can't accept how the serial murderer and his accomplices (e.g. parents of bullied kids who agreed to murdering bullies) are portrayed as... Kind of not so bad guys? And to think that many fans seem to agree with this notion of the serial killer being in the right. It just makes me quite depressed and sad :(\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, so, I love the Yakuza series, and Judgment/LJ both have their places. They are both great games, Judgment has become my favourite game. However, the one reason why I hate this game, is the same reason why I hated Middle Earth: Shadow Of War, the camera. Your camera constantly moves, automatically, and you cannot turn it off. The only way to prevent this is to hold down R, or L2. It is one of the most annoying things in a video game, when you cannot control your camera. If that doesn't bother you, buy both games and have fun. But for people like me who cannot stand the auto-controlled camera, don't get it.

Alright, a bit of an update since my review. I have been using a strip of duct tape to play properly, after rebinding the "Reset Camera" button. It is fun, a great game, the combat is not as fun as Judgment, but it is still very fun. I still hate being forced to tape down my buttons, but it's still a good game.\t\t\t\t\t
