\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall a great game but if I would compare it with the prequel then the prequel is honestly a much a better game. I'll try to compare both game and mention my biggest gripe, will also avoid talking about major spoilers for this game but I might spoil a little bit of the prequel (if that's a problem, you shouldn't be here anyway. Go buy/finish the first game).

Story:
Since we've learned everything there is to Yagami in the first game, the sequel brings another character that is the polar opposite of Yagami.
The story is not as grand as the first game and halfway point it feels forced because Yagami keep mentioning the part where the antagonist done again and again. It's not a bad story per se, but compared to Judgement the quality went down.

The actual story length:
In Judgement there's a perfect balance between the story and exploring (side cases, mini games, GF, etc), whenever I'm doing a story it feels like the game would indirectly give me a brief time to stop so I could explore the world. The story isn't too long but not too short-- rather each chapter you progress it feels like the story is getting longer.
In Lost Judgement there's barely a stopping point. Like I said, the story is not bad so it's easy to get hooked but the problem is each chapter is too short. I was at chapter 7 when I started to worry if the story is gonna spike up soon and starts bringing the main dish because I barely did anything and was already halfway through the game.
There's also barely a stopping point unlike in the first game so I was blasting through the story quite fast. Well you think it's my fault for not exploring on my own right? Well I did that and guess what? The side cases you did is mostly going to be from school club activity which I'm unsatisfied with (more down below).
It's fine if you think this kinda game is meant to be enjoyed by exploring stuff, do mini games, side cases, etc, you can trust me on this, I am not exaggerating when I said it's really short.

Also side note, this game barely have the option to sleep on sofa which I sometimes need since some GF cases unlocks on certain time.

Side Case:
In the previous game you mostly get Side Cases from the board in your office (and some of them from world encounter). But in this game the side cases came mostly from the school activity club and there's barely cases sent to the detective agency.
I'm not saying that these cases are bad or anything, most of them are great story wise (while some of them I think it's there just because?) but there is no variety in terms of story, the quest you do, the people you met, etc like in Judgement where almost every cases is different / a follow up from the case you've done.

This isn't all of them but it is my biggest gripe, but nonetheless I still really enjoyed it. Also buy the DLC as it's a step up from the base game IMO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo much better than Judgment but not there at the top with Yakuza 0 and 7.
I felt like GTO Onizuka for a part of it, this was so good, i wished Ryu Ga Gotoku woud make such a game where the High school part is the main focus.
----

Lost Judgment does everything better than Judgment. Right from the beginning , fighting feels faster and more responsive, and the change of scenery for Yokohama is more than welcome. No more being stuck in the tiny and oppressive Kamurocho.
Secondly, the game is split between the main story and the optional School story, so there's a good deal of content if you don't skip it.

Before writing about the story, i want to mention some of the other changes, improvements and new things that have been added.
_An entirely optional Story tied to the School that takes a bit of grinding
_Plenty of new side quests, and several new gadget that are needed for them, like a signal detector.
_2 new fighting style, Snake and Boxing (included DLC ); Snake being so strong that i almost only used it past a certain point.
_Lots of new minigames related to the School, some of them being good, some of them very tedious.
_Drone racing has been improved. The drone on the other hand has barely any use during the main story.
_Yokohama being much larger than Kamurocho, you can use a Skateboard to move around faster, with the ability to do some tricks.
_The Keyholder has been completely removed, and one the lockpiciking minigame too.
_There is humor from time to time, but this is still far from Yakuza.
_The events of Judgment are barely mentioned, making it a lot more optional to play it prior.
_There's tons of minigames like in every other games from the same studio that are completely optional !

About the high school/main story ;

_Despite being a bit grindy and tedious, the High School part of the game is what i liked the most ! Some of the clubs were engaging, some of the mini games where actually quite fun, some not and the whole plot surrounding this was enjoyable. I felt very attached to the students near the end, and the last part , to unravel the mystery was very heartwarming !

_The main story is better, but also has a pacing problem. Unlike in Judgment though, here, things are going too fast. Probably not to make a game too long i think, but i felt most of the chapters were very short !

From the beginning, with the informations you are given, you can very quickly find what the end goal for the whole situation is, so it is more about finding all the steps and means that served to create such situation, and there was some good surprises here, which is something the first game is missing a lot.

Near the end, the focus is once more about questioning the player about their perception of Justice. This time though, several events in the game pushes you very hard on one side, which i found a bit disappointing.

The characters implicated in the events felt much move likable and relatable too.


Conclusion : I liked this game to the point i would recommend it without having played Judgment. The story is nice, a bit on the short side, but it is compensated by the optional storyline tied to the High School. Some clutter has been removed, some added, tons of side content and minigames, all that bundled in the quality that this studio is known for. A very good game !\t\t\t\t\t

THE GOOD
+ Continuing from the first game, the story remains excellent. It once again revolves around detective work, complex mysteries, and lots of action...
+ ...But this time, the story deserves extra credit for how unique it is. Much of the game takes place at a school, which comes with all sorts of interesting and HEAVY plot developments that you aren't likely to see in any other game...Even if you've played Bully, from Rockstar Games.
+ Also returning from the first game is its amazing cast of characters, including some new faces I would love to see more of in the future.
+ Many direct improvements upon the first game. Better combat, more fighting styles, a second map, less filler, etc.
+ Tailing missions, which were previously my biggest complaint, are largely removed from the game. They have been replaced with chase, stealth, and climbing sections, which are all a LOT more tolerable.
THE BAD
- Some parts of the story seem like they repeat themselves.
- Less pronounced feeling of mystery compared to the first game.
- Combat is still a bit clunky due to Yagami's flashy fighting style.
- You might feel a little weird beating up high schoolers.
- Disappointing skateboard.
SCORE: 87%
More of the first game, but better.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Before getting into either game I heard that Lost Judgment was just a strict upgrade to the first game. So, going into LJ, my expectations were really high. And while I can't say the game met those expectations, it wasn't bad by any stretch.

A lot of people herald it as having the best combat in the series, but my core gripe with that statement is the difficulty. Yes, the combat is deep, and cool, and flashy, and Yagami has a lot of tools to play with and it's very skill expressive. There's nothing in the game that can stand up to getting the Crane Hidden Art attack speed, and just spamming Tiger charged attacks, or going into Boxer EX Boost. Every enemy in the game except Amon just folds in the face of Yagami's most basic combat options, let alone the laundry list of more obscure mechanics he has as you delve deeper into the combat. Judgment on Legend was WAY harder T_T than Lost Judgment on the same difficulty. And to me, that's a huge disappointment.

In terms of the story, I struggle to articulate exactly how I'd compare the two, but at current the way I'll put it is something to the tune of; Judgment's story is more "solid", generally being higher quality from start to finish and being a great crime thriller, while Lost Judgment's story is more emotional, more compelling, and has a better villain/core ideological struggle. I wouldn't definitively say one is better than the other.

One of my bigger gripes with Lost Judgment outside of the combat is the side content. Some of the side content is really, really good, like the Boxing and Biking questlines (good but get to repetitive IMO). And some of it is really, really , like how 10 of the game's 42 Substories are devoted to the completely "Dastardly Detective" plotline. Actually deplorable that so many Substory slots are taken up by that. I haven't gone back and reviewed all of them to properly form a final opinion, but currently I think the Substory content in the game is very poor, while the School Story content ranged from excellent to okay-ish, depending on the club.

Overall for me this game is solid 7/10 (+1 because they added dating girl from the Judment series (_) )\t\t\t\t\t
