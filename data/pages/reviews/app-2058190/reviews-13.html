\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I played the first Judgment, I thought that it couldn't be topped. The first game compared to the other Yakuza games within the new flashy dragon engine (Kiwami 2, and Yakuza 6) looked like a tech demo compared to Judgment. But I am shocked and astonished to admit that the sequel not only expands on the gameplay in the first game but also makes Judgment look like a demo.

Gameplay - Even without the new and technique heavy snake style which straight up slaps, the two previous styles have been massively fine tuned. Tiger is still bursty as ever, and crane is no longer complete garbage. They gave crane that juicy attack speed buff when you evade that the whole style desperately needed, along with a new mechanic called "Crane Dance" which flips Yagami in the air to either evade or pull off special combos or EX actions. Where in the previous game I would be permanently in tiger style, here you really do have to switch to crane at least once in a fight to get some nice damage in. But onto the snake style, just whoever came up with the combos, and system for snake i love them. If you time your guard just right you can just completely parry an enemies attacks and fear them into a 1 shot EX action just like that. Hell, they even re-did previous reused EX actions in the last game and gave it a brand new fresh coat of paint and im all for it, helps keeps things exciting! Im happy to also see that toned down trailing missions which really wore their welcome in the previous game after the first few, but also reworked it a bit to where you can now hide just about anywhere and you dont have to go to scripted areas to shelter yourself from the enemies site. They also added two new sections you can run into being stealth and parkour. Now stealth is an amazing idea for Yagami on paper. It makes more sense for a detective to sneak around enemy locations than the typical long fight brawl and bashing your way into enemy territory like a yakuza. Dont get me wrong the long fights are still spectacular, but from a logic standpoint its like the . But actually doing stealth is really janky, sometimes enemies just decide they have a sixth sense and can look behind them at random while you're crossing from them and when they spot you, thats it you're toast, and sometimes there are just kill barriers that instantly get you spotted whenever going to a specific spot, and its just overall janky. The parkour segments on the other hand, are handled quite nicely and have different mechanics like wall running and pipe swinging to keep things spiced up. Overall, great gameplay switch ups aside from a few hickups.

Setting - You travel between two cities in this game, Kamurocho which was the main location of the last game, and Isezaki Ijincho of Yokohama! Its great to see the funny rpg game map from Yakuza: Like a Dragon shown to us in real time combat, and im also happy to see both cities get equal enough game time to keep you entertained. Isezaki Ijincho was a little weird tho, with some places like the bottom half of the redlight district, and commercial district being cut off by objects and sometimes invisible walls that was accessible in Yakuza: Like a dragon, but in turn adding a brand new area being the Seriyo School acting as a sort of hub for a bit of the side content of the game.

Mini games - I am was anticipating this game's side content quite a bit because i've heard that there were quite a bit of different things to do within Yokohama, but wow I was just blown away at how much that was just thrown at me. Not talking about the elephant in the room being the "Side Content" which ill explain below this point, the normal mini games we have are still quite a blast. VR paradise and drone racing return from Judgment with a new fresh coat of paint with new coarses or a complete new overhaul to how the game works. Arcelios is brand new and is found within the VR paradise location where you blast different enemy types as fast as you can while surviving with different abilities, and wow was it surprisingly addicting, I swear its always the mini games that im least excited for that catch me off guard and gets me wanting more.

Side Content - Usually these games give you a side content story cultivating with usually a big mini game, like business mini game in Yakuza 7, and 0, or cabaret mini game in Yakuza Kiwami 2, and 0, but this game really took it to the next level and gave us a whole bunch of mini games connected to each other through a story that progresses the more and more you complete different mini games. Going back to the Seriyo School hub that they added to the game, reason I said hub is because this is where most of the mini games to this side story take place, if not start here. Mini games ranging from different school activities like robotics (which was super addicting customizing a robot team and then heading into pve combat arenas) or dance club (with a complete banger ost and dancing combos that hit you with straight dopamine), or to boxing (which the more you progress through the boxing story and more skills you learn within the boxing mini game, the more those moves can be applied into outdoors combat in a whole new 4th style just called "boxer"), and thats just half of it. None of the different mini games within the side story felt like filler or bloat, I found everything enjoyable whether it was instantly or took some growing on to.

Extras - Im happy to see the return of the proving grounds or ultimate battles renamed as "The Gauntlet" in the brand new dragon engine and yep its def. a challenge giving you spins on fights, focusing on a specific technique, or showing off a gameplay switchup like that 10 minute stealth section that can go itself. A new cool thing with the gauntlet is that you can get items from first time completions like either extraction potions to help buff you, gear, or completely cosmetically items, and collectibles such as records. Honestly was just really cool to look forward to after completing a chapter, and acted as a sort of break from the story and tear fest.

This game not only improves on its predecessor in every way possible, but also is the best title in the Ryu Ga Gotoku series, if you're looking for beat em up combat.\t\t\t\t\t
