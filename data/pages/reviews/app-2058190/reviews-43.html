\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen it comes to JRPGs, nobody does it better than Ryu Ga Gotoku Studio and this game may be their best one yet. The story, characters, plot, minigames, side stories, music, and everything are top notch. This is the sequel to Judgment though so I would recommend playing Judgment first if you haven't already to get to know the characters and enjoy the quality of life updates added to this game. This is like Season 2 of a TV Show when they get a higher budget and everything is more polished all around. Sure, you can technically start here but Season 1 was also really good and some of the improvements added to this sequel blow the original out of the water in gameplay.

The combat in this game is fantastic with 5 different fighting styles to master that you can switch to on the fly, the main story is an exceptional drama/mystery, but... my favorite part of this game has to be the side quests. I have never played a game with a single side quest that is just as long and good in terms of narrative as the main story but this game has done exactly that. I seriously spent over 30 hours of gameplay on a single side quest alone. This is the first Ryu Ga Gotoku game that creates an overarching side quest that is interconnected with, and requires you to complete, 8 other overarching side quests that each have their own unique gameplay, stories, characters, plots, and mysteries to solve. The amount of content in this game is staggering and it makes you wonder what other companies like Square Enix are doing when this level of detail and quality exists.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBEST AND DARKEST YAKUZA GAME

This game is fun. The gameplay, combat especially, is more refined than before. Beating enemies in the city never felt better. The past game, Judgment, felt sluggish now because of this game. The combat here is more balanced and fun, rather than just using Tiger Style only in Judgment since Crane Style felt useless. Other than Tiger Style and Crane Style being more balanced and just fun, theres Snake Style that is a much more defensive that is also just fun to use. Changing styles during combo is the bread and butter of combat. Thats my earlier impressions of this game since I'm not really going into this game for the story.

Lost Judgment is kinda spin-off of the Yakuza games but kinda not since the world of Yakuza, which is Kamurocho, always plays a role in the story. But it is a spinoff seeing how the story tells a different group of people rather than criminals, or ex-criminals that is usually in the Yakuza games. The first game, Judgment, have kind off a simple detective story with some Yakuza usual flair. Although I enjoyed it, I'll admit the antagonist kinda didn't do it for me. In Lost Judgment, I really like the story, the events that happen, and also the main villain.

Warning: This story is VERY DARK, dealing with bullying and suicide. Its not a one line or one event kinda thing. This dark element is the problem the game wants to deal with, exploring its issues and even exploring its consequences. I don't want to spoil too much but if you are open to the idea the game wants to discuss, I believe this game handled it very well and in a compelling way. Giving some of the better stories and also villains in a Yakuza game.

As per Yakuza game, the main story, is only 26% of the whole game, atleast for me after reaching the credits. Sidecontent is half of the game and is one of the main reasons why the Yakuza are its own genre. There are loads of minigames you can play, each in a building that are unique and very fun. This also gives an immersive feel to the open world, where even with its small size, the things you can do, and the substories you encounter, gives the world more life than an average Ubisoft open world game. (Ahem, AC Odyssey, ahem)

Soundtrack is the firiest of fire I have ever fire.

Overall, one of the best Yakuza game I've ever played.

5/5\t\t\t\t\t
