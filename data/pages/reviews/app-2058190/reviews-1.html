\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat should've been a sequel of Judgment, with more stories revolving around Yagami and Kaito, more refined combat and more minigame goodness...

Ends up being a... "decent" package but inferior to its predecessor in almost all points. Story is passable but nowhere near the quality of the first game, combat has some really great improvements (really love the Snake style!), but unfortunately feels stagnant in the enemy types (no real threat on the normal encounters, very little on the actual bosses), minigames are decent (boxing was fun, VR is still good), but have a lot of problems due to how they are acquired to begin with, or just how they are (skateboard parks are just terrible, robot fights feel clunky and unbalanced, way too little choice on music dances, lots of "repeat the same thing 3 or 4 times to progress" even if you can't do any better for dance or girls' bar).

The pacing in this game is pretty chaotic, and while there are some good moments, there are some moments that really feel awkward, with Yagami just being overwhelmingly attached to Sawa down to the point where it makes no sense, or him shunning Kuwana from the get go before we get any kind of introduction at the very start of the game, down to be antagonistic from the get go and uncharacteristicly of him, as he tends to be more understanding than.

There's a lot of things that this game introduces way too late, or in a sub story when they should've been part of the main path, or the rewards for completing them are so worthless it makes you feel like you've wasted your time more than anything... And I'm saying this as someone that went through for the 100% of all mainline Yakuza games + Judgment. As an example: Completing all school stories rewards you with a +7%ATK/DEF accessory, which is WORSE THAN AN ITEM YOU CAN JUST BUY IN A STORE (10% ATK+DEF), on top of having an ending that's just... lukewarm, at best.

Again, I usually love the games that RGG Studios put out into the world usually, but... man this one is middling. I'd still say to give it a try, and to actively go for sub content whenever possible instead of separating main story and sub content like I usually do for their games... But don't expect the usual quality of the other Yakuza/Like a Dragon games. Also, get this one on a heavy sale, not full price.\t\t\t\t\t
