\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was kinda hard to down vote this one. I'm a BIG fan of the series. Some of the best Twitch streams were playing this game as a group (and timing out the chatters that made wrong guess xD). But...this just doesn't hit the same as the rest of the series. With the previous games, you could have a 100 pairs of eyes watching the changing cameras and still fail the level. This one? Completion first time on the day of release. Bottom line up front, you might want to wait to play until hard mode is implemented (whatever that means.

Graphics: Detail is great, especially compared to some of the previous games. Often, I had trouble figuring out what an item was supposed to be because of the lack of color or low resolution. In 6, I can read everything (that's in English) and tell almost what all the items are. Unfortunately...that also makes it easier. It's way easier to remember an object to know the name and purpose of. And the maps aren't nearly cluttered enough. They're practically neat and tidy! There is one screen in University that could of been difficult? But if an item disappears, all the items around it do too and leave obvious negative space.

Reporting system: New reporting system is both good and bad. Point and click on the thing that's different? Intuitive and everyone gets it. It also makes it a heck of a lot easier than trying to decide what the anomaly should be when using the old reporting system. The one place where the new reporting system could be used to make things difficult? It's rarely used at all. Clicking items that are missing. The item is usually pretty big and has an obvious vacant space. Would of been better if smaller and more subtle items vanished.

Anomalies: There...just doesn't seem to be a whole lot of them. In the previous games I ran into anomalies I hadn't seen before in replays and made the game more difficult because I wasn't looking for them. In this installment: you play the map once and you've seen most of the different anomalies. And there's no shrinking or enlarging items. Either it's not there any more, it's moved, or it's been replaced with different item. Dropped the ball on that last one. Item replacements are generally wildly different. It would be better of the items were more similar or just changed colors or patterns. And WAY too many obvious anomalies. Items that have no business being in the scene. They make noise and move violently. Or your camera locks until you report the distortion. So much huge blood splatter. We don't want freebies, it really takes away from the satisfaction of finding the anomaly.

So. It's a game. 13 hours and haven't failed a single map. Got an anomaly warning once. Hunting down my last achievement for a specific intruder on a specific map. I'm worried hard mode might not be hard enough if it's just something like you can only send a false report 3 times.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been a big fan of this series, and I like to play with my friends on Discord, where everyone can chime in with their observations. This game was somewhat of a disappointment. It's more expensive than previous titles, but each level has fewer rooms. This makes it much easier to notice when something has changed, as there is less to keep track of, and you naturally return to each camera faster than you did when there were nine rooms to check instead of just six, making it easier to remember.

Also, in most cases the anomalies are much more obvious. There are very few cases of objects swapping places or something subtle disappearing. For most of the previous games it would take several tries to get through a level, but it didn't take more than three attempts on any of the levels, and one we completed first time.

The developer has announced the addition of a hard mode, which is welcome, but I think with the reduction in the number of rooms for each map is going to make it hard to achieve the well-balanced difficulty of previous games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I still enjoyed Observation 6, I can't recommend it when Observation 5 exists at a cheaper price.

I like having to click on the actual anomaly, making it so that you actually need to know exactly what's different rather than going "I think something here has changed" and hail-marrying a whole room. Having no live system and infinite guesses balances this out quite nicely. It would have been frustrating to lose a life because you clicked a little too far to the left of the anomaly.

What doesn't work is the anomalies themselves. They are incredibly obvious. I feel that having infinite guesses should have been the cue to make anomalies harder to spot. You aren't punished for guessing, so subtle anomalies would have been perfect. Instead I was able to complete all but two levels on my first try. One of those failed attempts was only because an intruder killed me, not because I failed to spot the anomalies.

Having color also made everything way easier to spot. This isn't a complaint at the color itself, (even though I prefer black and white for this series) but I feel like having color should have been yet another cue to make anomalies more subtle to compensate.

Overall, it's not a bad game by any means. If you really enjoy the Observation games, you'll still enjoy this. The only reason for my "not recommended" is because it's a step backwards from Observation 5, which is cheaper, and it is way too easy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been a long time fan of the series, but this one just isn't it. The graphics and sound design are great. However, it's a let down everywhere else. It feels so drawn out because very little happens for hours until it picks up at the end. The anomalies aren't as interesting, as most of them are shadows, text, or an intruder skipping around. Yes, that is a common occurrence. There's next to no variation amongst the playthroughs. I got the same anomalies every time, except for maybe 2 or 3. I watched gameplay from other YouTubers and they had the same ones too. With the other games, it would maybe have 2 or 3 the same between plays. It gets boring fast with how little things change. I'd get paranoid that I was missing things, but there was absolutely nothing happening for 1 to 2 minutes at a time. I like that there are a couple special characters per map, but most do not pose much of a threat. In fact, there used to be a lot of threatening anomalies that would take you out if you looked too long. I was able to beat the maps on the first try. There's just no difficulty. I fully support the developers and will continue to, but I really hope we get some updates that can fix the difficulty and occurrence of the anomalies.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played every "I'm on Observation Duty" game thus far, and I am always amazed by the improvements and changes made in each reiteration. IOOD 6 is no exception and I could not wait to start playing.

Pros:
-The intruder anomalies feel more specially geared towards their maps that they appear on. I love the return of old favorite, but the intruders feel less like random people appearing everywhere and more geared towards the maps they are on.

-The new reporting system is way easier to handle. I'll never hate the old reporting system, but not having to frantically look for "object disappearance" or "extra object" frantically when you feel time is running out is always okay.

-The addition of lore to each map is amazing. I've long loved trying to piece together a story line between the games and this made it so much more rich and fulfilling.

-A decent level of challenge. If you've played every game thus far, it may seem less challenging simply because you know what to look for, but newcomers will find it pretty tough.

-The graphics are amazing! The colors were not overwhelming, but added to the effect.

-The "Anomaly Report" when you fail a night is exactly what I needed. No more frustration over trying to figure out what I missed with no aid.

Cons:
-I would love for there to be a difficulty adjuster or more challenge added. It would add a lot more replay-ability and challenge for those more seasoned in IOOD games.

-I would love for the end scene to not just state the number of anomalies found, but also out of how many were present so i could know if there were some I missed.


Overall, super fun game, I love to stream it. Great for chat interaction and lots of laughs!
