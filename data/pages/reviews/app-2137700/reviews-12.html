\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would say 8/10 for this one, compared to the I'm on Observation Duty 5 that is. Overall It is still a very good game and definitely worth trying.
Pros:
The new reporting system is great, it now requires you to point out exactly where the anomalies are, so you can no longer just blind guess like in the previous games but it also simplified the control and makes the game smoother.

In game lore, great.

Different "Boss" type of intruders in every map, which is very interesting.

Cons:
Some anomalies are...a bit “boring". I mean, the ghosts and the stages are much more "normal" than the previous installments, which is a major setback. One of the reasons why the previous game is horrible is because there are many "strangely normal" things in the map e.g. the paintings in the bank (5). And the ghosts, some of them resemble normal people so much, that even finding them is not scary. Also, the anomalies in the previous game give you a sense that they know you are watching them. For example, the chairs will turn around and face the camera, as if some invisible ghosts are staring at you, but in this game, it's more like something moved, something changed position....

The pace is a bit weird. I know the dev is probably trying to make the game gradually harder, giving you time to become familiar with the map and create a kind of tense feeling like, "it is 5 am and the anomalies are launching a final assault", but the 1-3 o'clock is really, really, really really too slow! It's almost like one anomaly at a time! Maybe the hard mode will solve this issue though.\t\t\t\t\t
