\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've really only played (and beat) Train twice so far, so these are just my initial impressions, but...

This game is GREAT so far! It's a big improvement over Observation Duty 5, in many important ways.

For one, the new reporting system is a much appreciated change. It reduces the frustration of not knowing how to define any particular anomaly, while also raising the skill ceiling of reporting. Now, you can't just report "object missing", "object moved", and "extra object" in a row when the vibes are off in a room... now, you have to *point out* what exactly is missing, moved, or unexpected. Beyond that, it also flows much better than clicking through the old menu - though, it does dampen the feeling of being a government agent (the old system felt a lot like filling out form after form for every little thing).

I can't speak to ALL of the level designs yet, but the environment of the Train level, at least, feels much more cohesive than previous entries in the series. The models and textures themselves also feel much more refined; the developers have clearly been improving in skill as the series goes on!

(Minor spoiler for anyone who hasn't beaten a level yet) The addition of creepy ambient music in the last hour MASSIVELY increases the tension, and was an excellent call imo.

I'll update this review later once I've played some more, but I HAD to give this game the praise it deserves.\t\t\t\t\t
