\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all i'm gonna say that overall i enjoyed this game. I have played all the other games of this series (1-5) and even though this game is clearly easier than those i still enjoyed it. It is good to see some innovation with the reporting system instead of repeating what you have already done. It would be interesting to see how this reporting system would work in the other games as in my opinion i think this is a better reporting system. The levels are really good, really well detailed and an improvement from the previous games. It is good that we can play on controller as well as keyboard and mouse.

I will say though that this game is very easy. I still haven't completed the levels on the other games even after playing them a few times, yet the first time i played this one i completed a level on my first go. The anomalies seem very easy to spot compared to the previous games where in those games i may take a few camera rotations before i spot the anomaly, but in this one probably about 95% of the time i will spot them straight away. I will says i am looking forward to the hard mode that you're going to be adding, but if all this does is cause the anomalies to show up faster it is probably not going to make much difference as the anomalies are easy to spot anyway.
The creep factor in this game doesn't seem to match that of the previous games either, especially when it comes to the ghost and intruders. I can't really explain it but with games 1-5 i definitely felt more on edge when the ghosts/intruders show up and there was more of a sense of urgency to get them reported.
While i do recommend this game to others as it is fun to play, it is a downgrade from the previous games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter completing all the levels of the sixth I'm on Observation Duty multiple times, I'm now ready to write the pros and cons of this one.

Pros first:
  • The new reporting system is easier
  • The quality of the game & levels is 10/10
  • If you fail a level you can see a list of anomalies you missed

And then the cons:
  • The levels are way too easy compared to earlier games
  • The anomalies appear too slow in the beginning
  • This game is not worth the 12€ as it is

Honestly, this game feels like what you'd expect from the very first game of the series. It's just too easy and forgiving for the fans of the franchise who expect the challenge to be on par with the previous games. I did not receive a value I was looking for for the 12 Euros I paid, even if I momentarily enjoyed the levels WHEN something was actually happening.

I know the devs also announced that there's going to be a patch bringing us "hard mode", but I can't help feeling that the devs just rushed to get this game out because there are now so many similar games copying the idea. I'd much more prefer to buy a game that would reflect the quality of your earlier games instead of buying an expensive game and then waiting for patches later.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo the hard mode update changed most things I had an issue with besides that I still prefer the art styles of the older games. Especially the game finally is not super easy anymore. I played the first map a couple of times just now and didn't beat it - yay! I'll leave my old review at the bottom for reference.

Old Review:
I have played all Observation Duty games with a friend and I have to say I am quite disappointed by this newest one.

Positives first:
  • The game looks nice and I especially like that the enviroments are more animated than the occasional spinning fan.
  • The new report system feels quite intuitive.

But there are many issues with it:
  • While the graphics are clean and look nice, they really take away from the atmosphere of the game. This is still supposed to be a horror game, right? Previously you often times had maps with mostly greyscales and darker enviroments.
  • Also the maps you can play just seem super clean which firstly takes away from the atmosphere once more and secondly when we were playing quite a few of the maps I was already saying at the start that it would be easy as there are simply so few objects in most maps.
  • The new report system allows you to spam report again, yay! I don't know why you would remove like the best addition in Observation Duty 5 that stopped you from spam reporting.
  • The anomamlies are bad and I mean BAD. There are some weird creatures in every level but those are really just generic horror creatures which is not very interesting at all. The "regular" anomalies are in 95% of cases super easy to spot and my friend and I kept making fun of ways how you could make some interesting and difficult anomalies in the various maps because the ones we actually got were too boring.
  • Besides being easy to spot, most anomalies are plain boring. If you're looking for things like the psychedelic turning walls from Observation Duty 2 (?) or stairways leading somewhere else like in Observation Duty 5 for example or something along those lines, you will be out of luck. Also the anomalies are basically the same in all maps, you won't get to load into a map anymore, read something weird in the report menu and be like "Wtf is that even supposed to mean?".
  • As you can probably guess from the review so far. The game is super easy, we first-tried 4 out of the 5 first maps which is more often than we managed that in Observation Duty 1-5 combined.

All in all I would say, that if you haven't played one of the earlier entries yet, just play that one. This is the worst one by far for now in my opinion (yes, I enjoyed this less than Observation Duty 3). Also this game is really expensive compared to the others. That isn't an issue to me but you just get more bang for you buck with the other installments.
Notivia did post on twitter that there will be an update with a hardmode and more anomalies. If that changes anything about my problems with the game, I'll be happy to update my review.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis new entry in the game is visually stunning, and the new report system is pretty cool. But in every other way, I preferred the previous entry.

Pros:
+Graphics are visually stunning
+The levels are pretty believable as locations
+New report system is solid and makes things more interactive

Cons:
- The new levels are smaller (at least for the two I played) in terms of number of locations to keep track of, which make the game significantly easier
- On the note of difficulty, this entry feels far too easy. I played two levels before I decided to refund it, and had no difficulty. I quit half way through the second level out of boredom to be honest.
- On the note of boredom, the anomalies are mostly boring. I'm not sure if this is due to there being less of them, less variety, or what, but for the most part they are not as interesting as the previous entry. I didn't play enough to see this, but I've seen other reviews that talk about replay-ability being nil because the anomalies repeat frequently between playthroughs, which is not something I experienced in 5.
- Price. Compared to previous entries, it's the most expensive game, which would be fine if the content matched the price. As is, it does not.

I think there is a solid foundation here but overall it's a shallow experience.\t\t\t\t\t
