IMPORTANT: before going further read this - i do not rate this game on basis of the first ACI,II,III Titles. I treat AC Valhalla as an OWorld rpg type game.
IMPORTANT CRASH FIX Windows: Windows 10-11 players !!! READ THIS - if you have any irregular crashes on windows then:
- Navigate to the valhala exe file located in the install folder
- right click properties (ACVAlhalla.exe)
- Compatibility tab
- check the option :"Disable fullscreen optimizations"
0 crashes after that and better performance...

GAME REVIEW (2024)
This game is mind blowing! BEST AC Game hands down. Dont get me wrong i love AC 1 very very much!!! But it got on the list to number 2 right after AC valhalla.
I dont understand all complains. I know you miss AC1-AC2... But this game died over and has been replaced by open world RPG - grow up and deal with it. I am 40 by the way so no kids here.

Some systems are shallow do, and need a bit more love from the developers like fishing.
Quests can be for some who spend 1k hours in WitcherIII: shallow - but i am not a fan of quests overall.
Whats better here if we compare the game to WIII is the fighting and game loop.
WIII combat mechanics ware holding my back... they ware boring. Jump hit jump hit...
Here the combat system is so good. I love it! The gore... I had so many situations where hands and heads ware flying. To this day i cant understand who let this out XD but i love it so much!
Games should learn from this game when it goes to combat and gameplay loop.
I finished for now 3 claimed territories and am 126hours in!!! Love it. Not even bored. I would say i smelled the game and want more more more.
I run around fish, hunt, kill reds, burn villiges. Dont get why you cannot kill to many civilians do.. Your a Norse who is spit on by locals...
Check out my screenshots. You will understand what is so good in this game XD

I can barely make it through a cutscene without a crash and there are a great many of them. I can assure you it isn't hardware related as i have a 7950X and a 7900 XTX with 64GB of DDR5 at 5600 Mhz. If that isn't enough computer to run this game as ubisoft support seems to want to suggest then i don't know what the h3ll is. This game isn't brand new and yet titles made nearly a decade ago run far smoother with a fraction of development time and budget. ubisoft has fallen so far. it's frankly embarrassing that a game from a AAA title can sell a game in this condition and call it complete when it crashes worse than many beta indies i own.

Edit: solved the crashing issue by turning Ubisoft launcher to offline mode. Turning it to online causes crashes to return. not sure why this is the way it is, but it is very frustrating and not acceptable for a AAA title.

Also, to the dev, your game shouldn't be sensitive to multi-monitor setups, using extra drives, and extra peripherals isn't helping your case. That's not a solution, it's a workaround and is indicative of bad design. I won't be wasting my time with a support ticket because based on my search results for solving this issue it is fairly widespread and had been reported before with 0 assistance. It was in one of those forums that i found the the workaround that did the trick. Still, very dumb that this is an issue. If you are going to force your own launcher on people you should make sure it at least works. Otherwise just let steam do it's thing.

Don't believe the dev response, outside of this likely automated message, they have not given me any support. I'd remove it if i could, but sadly i cannot so they get to keep their fake "help" up.\t\t\t\t\t

I will start by saying i like Origins and i enjoyed Valhalla as well BUT the main reason for not recommending it is the fact it kept crashing, comparing with Origins which was flawless (on my moderate system at least).

I really prefer to avoid leaving bad reviews usually, out of respect for the dev. team behind it and the hard work with coding and art, etc but this is on the big sharks from the top, including senior devs and greedy shareholders (Tencent, Guillemot family) which keep pushing micro-transaction , speed up game development (Mirage) when they haven't even polished the newly released ones. The price of their lifeless DLC's are incredible, sometimes reaching the amount of some old GOTY editions games , lasting for 5-20 h max. Of course that no one is forcing us to buy them but at least try to read some reviews before purchasing such expensive useless DLC's , non-story related just some money-grab quick expansions.
I decided long time ago not to purchase any Blizzard game just because i considered that company is stealing money from players with their monthly subscriptions and now i reckon that i should do the same thing with Ubisoft. Just finished the games i have already and stop buying anything from them, including steam. It's funny how many payed reviews for Mirage they have already, tempting you to buy their next "jewel" while their focus will go on the next "new" game, without caring much about the fully development of the actual release, except some overpriced DLC's of course.
I will stop here with being too dark about everything, just tried to open some eyes regarding this game and the fake positive reviews which already has. Just a personal opinion, nothing against Ubisoft, just an old fan getting disappointed with each release.
Here are some pro's and cons which most probably saw it on different other reviews or critics:

PRO:
- awesome graphics (terrain, nature, architecture, landscapes etc)
- amazing world details
- improved AI regarding NPC's compared to different AC's
- improved and unique side-quests
- great skill-tree (complex and simple in the same time, depending on combo with armour and weapons)
- good story-line
- entertaining raid mode and map conquest
- decent weapons design/art and fighting mechanic
- some maps , like Asgard, really nice but too ...big
- good voice acting
- some nice boss fights
for all these reasons, i will definitely recommend this game but wait for the rest...
CON:
- good voice acting, yes, but incredibly repetitive for such a big company which is worth right now around 3,29 billions $
- most of NPC's look the same, just little bit trimming here, some long hair there and of course, different colours (it's not like Ubisoft could afford to make extra 100 different face/body designs, anyway, let's say it was pure laziness or they were focusing on doing that on $$$$$Mirage$$$$$
- still some embarrassing repetitive quests, like we got used to in all AC's
- the same boring crow/eagle/synchronization
- all shop vendors have the same items and quantity with idiotic respawn time, nothing personalised , but why would they complicate with algorithms whatsoever , to make buy and sale system more intriguing, challenging with different array of weapons and armours (hopefully they keep this for next AC' , for the die-hard franchise fans, a new wow-element in the game but wait!!! why would they do that when their SHOP needs to sell all kind of crap weapons and armours for REAL money !? YUP!!! Exactly!)
- first game where i can fish with ...my AXE !!! or not even bother to mention the PREDATOR BOW ! It should be renamed FISHING bow, because you can have 15 nice fat fish if you upgrade that thingy where you keep arrows. No need to waste time on reeling (which is really time consuming) if you have a bow, preferably a predator one for increased dmg and better aiming)
- no legendary ....fish! Yes! I mean it. Legendary cows, sheeps , dogs (except Fenrir which looks sexy) but no legendary Sturgeon for example, they are living fossils at the end of the day, you know!?)
- this game is called Assassins Creed but for me it looked like Butchers Creed more, because it was a lot easier to get into a fort and massacre everybody than wasting precious time going behind the bushes. I was playing on hard mode and somewhere in the mid game, after 50h i was godlike, so overpowered that i was raiding by myself most of the monasteries , calling for crews just for that silly reason i couldn't oped some doors alone. Pathetic. I am not trying to show off or tell everybody how good i am, it's just the truth, and most of the experienced hard-cored AC fans know it! Some good combo's and that's it, you're more like Odin than Eivor. Really regret i did not started with hardest difficulty.
- the spawning of AI/npc warriors on some quests, it's annoing, and pointless
- assaulting fortresses is nice...until you realise that no matter how many enemies you slain, Ubisoft will gladly respawn them again and again ....(unless you follow the quest, which is kind of linear and repetitive)
- weapon upgrade system is lame and old fashioned, rune system even worse. They make so much money, but it's either lack of ideas or interest to implement something unique, original ...
- PRICE is ALIEN !!! I got this on 80% or 85% discount and still feel sorry for paying that much! Normally i buy a game straight away if it's something that is worth the wait and deserves it, specially kickstarters or beta's of new developers, but this one wasn't worth it. I could have bought it with bigger discount on Ubiplay, their platform but nope! And i will tell you why in my next 2 , most important negatives about this game...
- no STEAM achievements. It's not like i do care too much about achievements or i want to fully complete a a game but ...if you ask me 60 for your game, at least offer everything, maybe not some well-evolved DLC's but at least the freaking achievements which most of game dev's offer on most platforms, including STEAM. This is the first disrespect towards customers of VALVe.
- the second disrespect towards fans and STEAM customers was when UBISOFT decided to go on a different platform, just to get a bigger piece of the pie, without giving a damn about us, those which prefer to stay here, and not migrate to Epic Games for the sake of a game. I wanted to play Valhalla from the beginning but the fact they chose not to sell it here, infuriated me! I literally stopped buying any old UBISOFT games from STEAM, discounted or not, removed it from my wishlist , etc. After 2 long years, they finally released here, but my interest in it was faded, almost none , yet still tempted to see if it can match Origins , or even beat it.
As i was saying earlier , i got it very cheap, but even with a low price i still regret it, but i can recommended to you if it;s getting around 5 to 10$ maximum. No more. Not worth it. Just do some quick math: you pay 5$ for the "full" game if you're lucky to get it with 90% discount then another 5$ for their first released DLC , and another 5$ for the second (which is practically the same, 6-17h gameplay) and it repeats itself until you buy Mirage and so on, vicious circle)

PS: you are more than welcome to say your opinion regarding my review but please keep it straight on the subject and don't forget to be polite, as i have cause you personally no harm or tried to insult anyone here.

It's no point to argue about or lie that i tried everything (having some research on how to fix the problem) so i really appreciate someone from Ubisoft bothered to leave a comment here which might be helpful for another players experiencing same problem.
