\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game continues my love-hate relationship with the AC games. But I still recommend it if you enjoy free-roaming games with unexpected encounters, a fairly decent storyline and lots of gear to play around and try.

first a spoiler free part:
What I loved: the exploring, the absolutely stunning landscapes (this game is beautiful to look at), the main story line of Eivor and Sigurd trying to find allies kept me engaged (even if some moments felt against the nature of the character I was playing), the sidequest were fun enough that i finished almost all of the mysteries/artifact hunts/raids etc.

What I hated: Firstly the absolute lack of historical accuracy was laughable. VIkings as well as England are a mishmash of all kind of things from completely different timelines. But I can forgive that, it's a fantasy after all, but compared to some of the other AC settings this was definitely not well researched or a deliberate choice in game design. Add to that the awful responsiveness in gameplay, sometimes you press all the right buttons and nothing happens. This was especially annoying in combat, and happens often enough that I have to mention it here. You press block, nothing happens. You press R to stomp, nothing happens, and some of the abilities just don't work (greyed out all the time). The last thing I hated was the lack of effect in choices you make on the game in general. You get presented with options that sound like they may have grave consequences, but they don't. Some of your choices may slightly change the end game but most of the storyline is set and will not be affected.

Now comes the spoiler section so be warned reader
I'm not a fan of the whole ISU story arc, sorry, it's just all a bit ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ sounding to me. So I don't enjoy these elements and I'm not up to speed with all of that story so part of the endgame was totally lost on me. The Eivor/Sigurd storyline I felt was engaging but ended in a bizarre twist with such a fizzle that it was disappointing. The modern day storyline was creepy, in the final bits where you realise you're Basim playing as Eivor is just not fun. I felt like I was forced into playing a villain (again: not up to date with the ISU stuff so maybe I'm not seeing the whole picture here). I generally don't give a **** about that storyline and felt pushed out of the game I wanted to play (Eivor/SIgurd story) and forced into a game I did not want to play.

In conclusion: I loved AC Valhalla as a totally not historically accurate, but fun game to roam a beautiful landscape and have some fun with making friends and kicking some butt. It kept me playing for 170 hours, the 20 hours or so after that was the part of the story I hated. So all in all, I considered it worth the time, and if on sale I recommend getting it.\t\t\t\t\t

When I bought the game i expected the historical tourism, combat, and exploration to be the soul keeping me playing, the exploration standing out most, though was dampened by a combat system that makes reaching any new destination stale.
Don't buy this game, and keep an eye out for those responsible in creating this travesty, between the world design and ideological need for modern diversity where a very real ethnic and cultural conflict was taking place.
