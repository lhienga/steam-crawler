\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright so. Let me start with this on the outset, I am aware SBI was involved in this game I do not support their behavior nor their regular attacks. If you have a desire to purchase it and that puts you off I will highly recommend looking into the format of AC games and getting a general understanding of what they are. If you have played AC games before this is not too far afield from their work. With that out of the way, let's weigh the pro's and cons.

Pros -
- If you like AC games, this is more of the same dense story relatively well written. Of course there have been moments.
- Combat system is intuitive once you learn the controls, these are explained fairly well.
- Cosmetic system related to found equipment any armor can look like any other armor you've found with minimal investment.
- like-able goofballs mixed in with Dag. I do not like Dag. However the others and even the poet battles have been a source of enjoyment for me
- an astounding time killer, where some may see this as a downside when I want to just piss about there is always something I can go do with a reasonable reward.
- a truly staggering amount of customization for your experience in gameplay.

Cons -
- Micro Transactions tied to Ubisoft
- Macro Transactions IE DLC. I can't totally blame them as of this moment as I have not purchased or played the DLC it's dependent entirely on the quality of the DLC It is very abrasive on how it's shoved into your face though
- UI clutter that's a staple of Ubisoft games but you will see it
- General QoL issues (grain of salt because I've looked but am uncertain) alright this is a nit pick at best, I tend to run into missions that require me to go somewhere at a specific time of day and I do not know how but it's always a ways away I've found myself reading a book waiting for a mission to start because I haven't found a wait function.
- Limited weapon variants. Since I only purchased the base game my usual weapon preference isn't there basically figure Axe, Shield, Big Sword, Shank, Bigger Axe, and Spear while the Ubisoft connect shop can help with that we just kinda run back to Microtransaction land
- Requires a third party application to even access or play

Overall, you have to look at these kinds of games for what they are. Is it a good game? No overall the cons tend to out weigh the pros with the hassle there is on playing a game like this. Is it a good AC game, yeah it is the gameplay is enjoyable the story is tolerable overall. I really enjoy my time with this game and overall if you are used to Ubisoft or just AC games this isn't too far afield from the first AC onward it's more of the same. Mindless enjoyment of sneaky sneaky stab stab.

If you can look past SBI's involvement and their abhorrent actions and targeting of others I would highly encourage you to play the game. While I understand why some people maybe hesitant due to this fact if you like AC games you'll like this one. Buy it on sale if you must but I weighed the game's quality and my desire to play it against other factors and found I wanted to play it more than I needed the fifteen dollars. Do some research on your desired gaming experience and weigh the pro's and cons.

TLDR - Good Assassin's Creed still Ubisoft\t\t\t\t\t
