\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed this game. I had previously played Origins and Odyssey. While I still preferred the other two, I still enjoyed this one a lot. Even though there are no steam achievements, they still exist in UbiConnect. There are also an additional set of goals to go for. So I didn't find the lack of achievements displayed on steam to be a hindrance.
This game is even longer than Odyssey even if you go for all of the side quests in both games.

I will point out that there are some crashing issues. Though I found it wasn't very often and mostly they were within the DLC. I played nearly 200 hours before I got my first crash (despite playing on a machine that far exceeds the recommended requirements). It can get annoying, but I also realise others have this issue worse than I did.

The story-line is fine. But as with Odyssey and Origins, the scenery is beautiful. I am also from England, so some of the scenes and locations were particularly interesting to look at.
I played nothing but Valhalla between July 2023 and October 2023. That is how engrossed I was.

I would recommend this game. It's fun to play and the side quests were interesting.

I can say that Valhalla's number 1 complaint on PC seems to be problems with it running. I've barely had any issues and none of which weren't corrected with some simple graphic tweaking, i've seen a few bugs but nothing really bothersome, sometimes combat feels janky but i realized long ago its just the animations sometimes being a little funky.

Valhalla is a great entry in the creed franchise, its got a great story, good characters and the viking focus is one of the better choices in the series. Combat feels fun and engaging but its where one of our first hiccups comes in.

Basically you the player will get used to 2 types of combat.
Minion combat: many guys, they don't really stand a chance unless you are being dumb or are really out leveled. Its easy and your bread and butter alot of the time.

Named character combat: These guys regularly have immense health pools or other sorts of defenses that puts them on a entirely different strata of difficulty for you, many of which can't be fought directly unless you utilize the various abilities you'll get throughout the game. If you find one of these guys and he wipes the floor with you get creative or come back later with different abilities.

Naval combat has taken a step backward from other entries, its still there but is immensely streamlined, you'll spend your longboat time mostly just traveling with the occasional raid (raids are pretty cool tbh) The additon stories your crew can tell in addition to the songs keeps these segments or exploration.

The world is HUGE, nothing we aren't used to by this point in the series but i always feel like its worth mentioning. of course like previous entries that huge world seems to be sparsely populated save for various areas. Valhalla's world is...probably one of the more boring ones of the franchise which might play a part in why this game is notorious for player retention.

Valhalla is one of the last decent entries in the Creed franchise. Grab it if its on sale but you really shouldn't be paying more than 30 to 40 for the thing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis AC felt better in more ways than not compared to Odyssey. Some things I really appreciated were the varied nature of the side quests and overall open world activities. It does NOT feel like they copy/pasted assets throughout the world like in Odyssey, and you can tell that the people who worked in this game were really putting in a ton of effort into the little things.

The overall quest writing and story was excellent for me, right up there with the writing in Black Flag, AC1, Origins, and the Ezio trilogy. The bosses and mythical creatures were more challenging than Odyssey and Origins.

The combat felt more grounded and crunchy than Odyssey which is a big plus if you're setting a game in this gritty brutal world.

The map was GORGEOUS. This is the most beautiful game I've ever played, hands down. I caught myself fast forwarding time until the sun would rise over the ocean to the East. Such a visual treat.

Some things I think Ubisoft can improve on for future titles:

I understand what they were going for with the gear system in this game, but it didn't really land for me. It's hard to get any real feel for the differences between weapons of the same type. How is flail 1 noticeably different from flail 2? I stopped getting excited about new gear halfway through the game since its value is determined by how many resources you pump into it, not by its own merit. Why do we need 8 different light shields?

The game was TOO EASY. I had trouble with the legendary bear, that was it. I had difficulty set to Drengr the whole game and no aim assist for arrows. I loved the first 40 hours where you don't feel overpowered and there's a real sense of danger in the world. After that, I had zero reason to ever use stealth as I could barge in pretty much anywhere and not have to worry about dying.

Overall, this game is about an 8.5/10 for me. I love it, just wanted a bit more of a challenge !!!\t\t\t\t\t
