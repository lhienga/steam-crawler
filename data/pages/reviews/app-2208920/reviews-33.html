\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI struggle to understand how reviews of this game are mixed! I think it's largely an AC fanbase that want games to be more closely tied to the Assassin storyline, and as someone who has only played Odyssey and Valhalla, I am not one of those people. Taking my perspective into account, I think this is a fantastic game! Yes, there is probably too much content. The sheer number of wealth/mystery/artifact locations in England is somewhat overwhelming and could have been cut back a bit. However, some of the mystery locations in particular are my favorite parts of this game. Much like Odyssey, they often have their own interesting or downright funny stories that add depth to the people outside of the main characters. I think there could have been fewer wealth or artifact locations without losing quality. But the combat in this game is satisfying, the adventuring is super fun, and the main stories are engaging. I have lost many hours in this game. I do wish Eivor had more of a personal investment in getting rid of the Order, like Kassandra did in Odyssey. It feels like Eivor is just doing Hytham a favor and you start to forget why some of the Order members are even that evil. But one of my favorite (albeit confusing) aspects of the game is the tie in with Norse mythology. I love that it isn't even DLC, but just part of the main story. It's incredibly cool to see different realms through Eivor's eyes. Overall, yes, it is not a traditional AC game and I understand that. But it's just a really good combat-heavy open world RPG and I fully recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolute masterpiece of a game.

If you experience random crashing in the game, I'm here to (hopefully) save your day.

I too experienced random crashing with this game, but eventually found the fix after extreme patience and persistence. There are a ton of suggestions online, all of which did not work for me, that is why I feel obligated to share this fix.

My epic random crash fix (for NVIDIA cards):

1. Download and launch the latest version of NVIDIA Profile Inspector
2. In the "Profiles" drop down, select "Assasin's Creed Valhalla"
3. In the main window, scroll down until you see "rBAR - Feature" and change its value to "Disabled"
4. Select "Apply changes" at the top right corner

That's it. You can now close NVIDIA Profile Inspector.

Important: Whenever you update your GPU driver, the game's profile will be reset to default and you will have to repeat the steps above.

I hope my fix saves your day. This game would have been unplayable for me if I couldn't find this solution and had it crashing all the time, which would have been a real shame.

Last comment. Even with my solution above, the game will still sometimes crash. However, it is no where near as frequent as before the fix above. An unfortunate minor annoyance that I had to overlook.\t\t\t\t\t
