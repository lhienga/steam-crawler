\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAssassin's Creed Valhalla is your typical AAA open world RPG game with an overwhelming amount of collectibles and side activities to pad the game time along with a wide scope of gameplay mechanics to attempt to appeal to as many gamers as possible. This game has a Souls-like style of combat in that certain areas/encounters have a recommended player level, but that doesn't mean it can be done at a lower level however you'll have to spend more time in combat as you deal less and cannot take as much damage as your enemies.

There are two main story lines that are active simultaneously. To complete the game you have to obviously take out all of the Knights Templar and kill the leader. However, in this game to do that you have to grind through a series of quests to identify underlings to expose the "Lieutenants" to take them out. Essentially, you have to take out 2 tiers of Templars (something like 15-20 in total) to get to the final boss.

There's a "base building" feature where you plunder churches for their riches that in turn are used to upgrade/build new buildings in your settlement. Your items have a gear score/quality level that can only be upgraded with special/rare materials you get from exploration.

I ultimately got fatigue of playing the game from all the stuff to do and stopped playing it. The game isn't bad, but the story is tedious and there's too much going on that for someone like me who tries to be a completionist it's overwhelming and exhausting to play.\t\t\t\t\t
