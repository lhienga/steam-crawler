\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. This was my longest game, ever, I was really not expecting this.

I loved Origins and Odyssey, and I loved Valhalla, too. It's kind of a mixture of the previous games - the weapon system is more like as in Origins (ie. different weapon types behaving differently), but it misses those irritating upgrades which Odyssey brought in (like the GTA-ish "police"/star system or the levelling system of equipments).

This is far from the classic AC games, but it's a great viking RPG. Graphics is getting old - I've felt both Origins and Odyssey looked better, somehow. Story was engaging enough to keep me playing, loved both the main stories and the ministories. Loved the "choices matter" part, it was definetaly a step-up from Odyssey, but it was limited mostly to the first part of the game. "Assaults" was again a nice step-up from the battles of Odyssey, and loved raids, too. Side activities on the map was also really nice, I've not felt they're just there to increase play time with silly collectibles and fetch quests. The town building part is also a nice touch, I'm glad it's not like in Fallout4.

Overall, it's a great RPG, but there are some minor flaws:
- The prologue and the ending. The prologue is so badly designed and lame, that I've almost gave up after the first few hours. Piece of advise: just follow the main quest, and it will be MUCH better after the AC logo comes up. The ending... is like a non-existent thing. I was definetaly expecting more.

- Equipments. In Odyssey, you could loot something from literally every enemy. Now, you can get gear mainly from hidden places/treasure chests, and their stats are not really important, what matters are their special skills and their type. Which resulted that I've played the game from beginning to end using literally the same equipment and weapons. Not really RPG-ish. For me, Odyssey was too much, this is too little.

- Romancing. Yes, you can romance quite a few people. But it comes as random as it can get and felt out of place most of the time. Also, the intended protagonist is the female one, and most of these romancing options are guys, which results in really awkward situations if you're playing as the male Eivor (like, talking about some random stuff, then out of the blue, "we'll bang, okay?").

+ great story but it will make sense only after you complete all lines
+ enjoyable combat
+ good choice of weapons and various dual-wield combinations
+ impressive combat finishers
+ epic battle music during assaults
+ VERY EPIC song during raids
+ beautiful level design of dungeons and caverns. It was a joy to explore.
+ animal friends may join your settlement
+ male Eivor is exceptionally handsome

Things which I see as positive but some players may find negative:

* very large world, for some maybe too large. For me, perfection.
