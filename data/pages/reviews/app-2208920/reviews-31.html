\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHans, are we the baddies? That's what Eivor should have asked. You raid villages, kill every poor solder trying to defend them, you burn houses, you steal. You work with sadistic maniacs, you let them torture innocents, kill them and mutilate their corpses. All AC games had some pretty questionable morale but now it's getting really sick.

Otherwise, Valhalla is pretty much the same game as Odyssey. Some game mechanics has been changed, but the experience is very similar. The writing is decent, there are few memorable characters, but other questlines are boring and forgettable. Not sure why is the game called Assassin's Creed though; you are not an assassin and you don't act like one. I liked the world events and treasure hunting, some of those were quite fun.

The combat is still ok, although I didn't like the new ability system which felt clunky and in the end I didn't use it much. Itemization is significantly improved. Stealth, on the other hand, is completely broken. Sometimes the enemies won't notice you in bright daylight, sometimes they spot you at night from far away. Enemies get alerted almost immediately and in the end you solve most of the situations with direct combat because it's faster and you eventually need to do it anyway. Actually, you don't have to fight everyone, because you can just run away from enemies; they lose track of you in a few seconds, so just steal their treasure and run, they stop caring the moment you leave the room.

What's was my biggest problem? The game was incredibly unstable. During my 200 hour playthrough I've experienced around 40 crashes. And it didn't help the game takes a very long time to start again.\t\t\t\t\t
