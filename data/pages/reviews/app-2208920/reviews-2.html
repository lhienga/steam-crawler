\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAssassin's Creed Valhalla is a beautiful game that simply feels too big, and too empty.

To start, some good points. Valhalla is stunning. Travelling across viking-age Britain, you get to encounter a number of moments that encourage you to just stop and stare. The combat is also not terrible. Though it doesn't feel as tight as previous entries, it still is pretty fun.

Now for the negatives. Assassin's Creed: Valhalla feels huge, but also empty. Though there are things to do sprinkled about, they all feel the same, and the amount of progression earned feels pitiful. Combine that with a system that necessitates levelling up, and it just feels slow, glacially slow. The perk system while levelling is also horrible. Much like previous entries, levelling up unlocks abilities... sometimes. Between each ability is 5-6 points of stat boosts, that don't feel like they do anything. An example might be +1.5 ranged; what does this mean? Who knows! The game also retains Ubisoft's characteristic jank, with floating characters, and all the rest. Finally, despite the massive world, it feels like their is nothing to collect. While some may have disliked the amount of throw-away armour is Odyssey, Valhalla goes in the opposite direction, with minimal armour or gear to collect, discouraging exploration. The cult, which returns, doesn't even always drop gear, making even defeating them feel pointless.

I have yet to try the DLC, partially due to frequent crashes, but for the main game, it just lacks the charm of the previous entries, making it hard to recommend.

Also, no achievements. Not a deal breaker, but a disappointing omission nonetheless.\t\t\t\t\t
