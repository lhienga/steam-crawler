\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI Don't understand the hate this game gets.. I'm not going to focus on the negatives in this review because plenty of other have already done that and I'm sure you all are quite aware of them by now. Repetitive is the word I see thrown around the most when Valhalla is brought up. It's no more repetitive than any of the other Assassins creed games. I Wasn't a fan of Assassins creed until origins because of the shallow combat and lack of RPG progression and gear, I'm a sucker for a good RPG. Not saying Valhalla is a super deep RPG but as far as Assassins creeds go, It's by far the deepest.

I've been playing this game on and off for the last year with ubiusoft subscription service and i just bought it due to the recent sale. I have to say.. What a steal, the complete edition for half the price of a new tripple A game. I'm glad i picked it back up because I'm enjoying it more than ever this time around. The environments are truly gorgeous with some locations being absolutely awe inspiring. Clever use of fog and lighting, falling leaves and butterflies, how the lighting pours over ruined structures and seeps through tree branches. It really is something else.

Combat in odyssey was one of my biggest letdowns and to say the combat in Valhalla is perfect would be a lie.. but It's much better than odyssey and with access to plenty of abilities (found through the world in hidden locations)and skills gained through the massive skill tree. The skill tree is... Expansive, and filled with active skills and passives. It really allows you to build your character the way you want.

Gear is also a major plus in this game. So many different gear sets and weapons with their own specific traits and upgradeable through the use of runes or by using materials to increase their stats. You can also upgrade their rarity through a blacksmith that allows you to upgrade it's base stats further. It even changes the look of the armor or weapon.

i often see this game bashed for what it does wrong but hardly ever see it praised for what it gets right. If you are looking for a true continuation of the Assassins creed story, you might not find that here. Although you will find a ton of lore that helps set the stage. In conclusion and on its own, Valhalla is a solid open world RPG with decent combat and plenty of interesting locations to explore. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, me myself personally loves the viking setting the game is going for here. It's so fun following the story of Eivor, going from Norway to England along with a crew to build a settlement, do raids, conquer new land and help out people all along the way. The open world is beautiful as well and is actually something I thoroughly enjoy in this game as well, even if I do also like the close quarters parkour settings of other games in this series.

The combat is fine but abilities really spice it up for me as well as some of the skills you can obtain. Heading into a fight knowing that you can shoot the enemies weakspots to catch them off guard and make them stagger, allowing you to get a full on finisher on them is satisfying. If that's not your thing, why not mark 5 enemies at ones and fire a barrage of arrows that will either decimate them or leave them regretting their life decisions as you smack 'em to Niflheim. Or maybe you wanna charge at an enemy, knock them down to the ground, and beat them up with your fisticuffs? The game offers a lot for combat, not just with skills and abilities but also with your arsenal. Axes, spears, hammers, swords, shields and bows, there's a lot of new styles to try.

Gear is also pretty cool. Being able to upgrade your gear or straight up finding super overpowered gear to upgrade as well is nice. The set bonuses are also a nice touch, giving you better stats the more of the set you're wearing at the same time. Such as the Raven Clan armor set giving you increased armor the lower health you have if you have two pieces of the armor set equipped simultaneously and an additional increase to Critical Chance with all five pieces of the armor equipped at the same time. But maybe you wanna mix and match gear to look cooler? Not to worry, you can merge armors to make them have a new appearence but with the stats of another one.
