Gameplay & Mechanics
- Some-what fluid When you use a spear, it is difficult to aim at a specific target, and you often switch to a different one.
- Confirming a Kill Probably the worst and most annoying mechanic in this game. Time stops, and you just hover your axe over the person. It's so tedious and just boring, and then Eivor just doesn't slow down either? I don't know, it makes no sense.
- Raiding Repetitive after the fifth time. The worst part is opening a heavy chest, and then your Jomsviking takes 20 years to help you out and just tip-toes over to you.
- Skill Tree Pretty fun in contrast to Odyssey, I liked it more.
- Armor & Armor Appearance I like that each piece of armor comes with a special power up or whatever in contrast to Odyssey where only specific pieces did, however the armor appearance in this game is subjectively worse. I don't understand why we have to pay to change our appearance of our armor, and then let's say you want to change- oh well let's go back and have a 5 minute loading screen, take 10-15 minutes choosing and then after a while have to repeat the process while in Odyssey you can do it at the click of a couple buttons.
- River Raiding I never got into it. I mean, you really barely gain anything except leveling up your Jomsviking, but it's just so boring.
- Assassin vs Viking I can't speak for how this game differs as a true Assassin's Creed game, but I think it makes sense for Eivor to not be an assassin. I mean, she/he grew up as a viking and all of that tradition, she/he can't be formed into an assassin like that in contrast to Kassandra or Alexios since they were mercenaries.
- Crashing You crash a few times. Oh well. I don't think it's a big deal since you auto-save so often anyways, just more of an inconvenience.
Story Line
- Sigurd At first, I really feel the bond we have with him. Over time, it just disintegrates and so does he. Which makes perfect sense for the storyline he goes through, but it's just done poorly in a way. He explains almost nothing, until we go back to Norway and go through all that and then somehow he just follows Eivor back even though it's his 'destiny' and has been hating us basically. This also can be explained by sibling love, but we can't really feel that love at this point in the story. It would have been better portrayed by a hug AT LEAST. I mean we save his life, how many times? We get nothing in return. I mean we can become Jarl, but that can be seen from a mile away since Eivor singlehandedly built up Ravensthorpe. I wish there was more emotions portrayed other than anger with this storyline. We find his arm, and then just yelling? I don't understand.
- Eivor's Emotions Now this leads into Sigurd and the emotion speal, I really do not understand why Eivor does not show emotion at the end of the game. Hunswald DIES and basically says Eivor you're my #1, and all we get afterwards is a sigh that sounds like a sniffle. But when we listen to Ljufvina pour her heart out, when the person she loves the most dies, we just move past it. It makes no sense whatsoever. Leading up to this, we go to honor our fallen friends, and then we just do it with no emotion. There was no cutscene where Eivor broke down. It would have ended the act perfectly breaking down when she came back to Ravensthorpe, or if she was the last one at their pyre's and did it in front of their bodies. It was so, so disappointing. I cried when Soma and then Hunwald died, but somehow Eivor who is actually their friend, didn't.
- The Ending? There is no end. Literally. Our friends die, and then Guthrum has an extistential crisis about God and then we go home and have Swanburrow cry and it ends. It just ends with no wrap up. The sentiment is that, we shouldn't be killing anymore and we have to find peace and just live with each other- this is completely and utterly stupid. It leaves the player with NO satisfaction, and it feels like a waste to me of 80 hours. I have never played such a more disappointing ending in a game, because there is no end. There is no follow-up with Sigurd; I mean we could come home and cry to him and tie in the sibling sentiment, but no we just stop fighting and Aelfred flees. Like WHAT. I almost struggle to recommend this game just off the basis on that. If you hate endings like this, don't buy the Druid DLC either. Completely similar ending, our friend/love interest just leaves. Worst writing in a game I've ever played.
6.5/10 \t\t\t\t\t
