\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, there is nothing wrong with this game. It's just that after 10 hours, you've done everything you will ever do going forward.

Raids are the same with a different environment.

The ships are the same.

The combat is the same.

The story isn't very engaging. Origins was a revenge story, Odyssey was a story about self, maturity, and the adventure to find these things. Valhalla is a story about... revenge? The challenges of starting a new home? Betraying your sibling when they are an idiot? I'm not sure.

Traversing the English countryside is also boring. It's hills, some forests, and the occasional town / city. London and Winchester are not really all that interesting. Alexandria in Origins is a spectacle, Athens in Odyssey is a marvel, but the cities in Valhalla are empty and pointless. Your colony, Ravensthorpe, has everything you will ever need and while it's cool to watch it grow over time it's annoying that you have to keep going back to get goods, resources, and upgrades.

Oh, and dear lord, the sudden induced coma missions that take you to Asgard are more annoying than anything. Basically we have the present day story with the chick (I forgot her name), the Viking era story with Eivor, and the Asgardian story with Odin. So more names, terms, and places are crammed into a single game.

I think the team at Ubisoft tried to please way too many people and ended up creating a Frankenstein game that pleases no one. If you love Vikings you will probably love this - I know I do. But the gameplay itself is rather tiresome after a few hours and there's nothing else pushing you forward.

Have fun!\t\t\t\t\t
