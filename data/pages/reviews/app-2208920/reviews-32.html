\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd been thinking for a few hours before I finished the game whether to give it a positive or negative review but ultimately I enjoyed it more than I despised it.

The main problem with Valhalla is that it is so. DAMN. Big.
The story parts are mostly fine and yet there are so many different sagas, so many substories, that I had long forgotten about most of them by the time I finished.
And the one issue I have with the main storyline is that it has no ending. It just stops. "You did all we asked and want some closure? ♥♥♥♥ you, free roam instead!"

The game is filled to the brim with side-content and, well, it is the Ubisoft (duh) ♥♥♥♥♥♥ side-content all over the place. The first few hours are fun, later they become these annoying icons on your map that you know you will not bother with since the rewards are not worth it. As always.

On the plus side, the music is absolutely incredible. Hearing the vikings sing on longships, in longhouses, or meadhalls was always a pleasure. The graphics are phenomenal as well albeit there were plenty of visual bugs.

As for the gameplay - well, the fighting was kinda fun but not really. Nothing challenging, sometimes frustrating when a stick on legs with a spear blocks a viking with monumental posture who fights with double-handed swords in both hands.
The longboat gameplay, so all the raids and such, started to bore me to death after four or five times.

I have only beaten the base game and I have no intention to play the DLCs as I am just simply tired of it. Too much unnecessary content with too little meaning. However, I've seen it to the end and cared about the main characters, the main storyline was actually pretty good and engaging, I cannot give
it a negative review; though, if there were ratings here, I would give it a solid 5+/10.\t\t\t\t\t
