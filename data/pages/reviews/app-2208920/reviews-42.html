\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was fun... for the first 30 hours.

My positive and negative feelings are parted like sea before Moses when it comes to Valhalla. Without playing newer, more rpg oriented Assassins Creeds from Ubisoft I've decided to give this one a go. So for me it was a breath of fresh air into the series. But even with that, I've quickly gone to notice how Ubisoft instead of putting more quality into the game, decided to put as much mediocrity as it could.

Talking about the story, it sucks because it has some good points to it. Both beginning and ending segments of the game were great. It’s the whole bloat in the middle that pissed me off in the end. There are many quests and characters in there that should have been side contents but are shoved in as part of the conquering and pledging to the lands of England.

And it doesn’t help how much quests are build to go from point A to B,C,D,E,F and to even later points of the alphabet sometimes. Many of quest threads have mostly set amount of objectives, so you better won’t be going in expecting just 2,3 missions/locations to go, usually it goes around 8/9 per pledge.

Characters again have some good and bad sides to them. There are ones that are really interesting and go through their own story arc’s along with you. And there are some that go through character development off-screen and the next time you see them it feels as if a rock fell on their heads and sadly that is the case for of the main villains of the game. Every time him and Eivor meet it is as if someone was rolling the dice to dictate what is their friendship level.

Hunting the order of the ancients was really interesting at first, as there are set up characters in the game world that you have to hunt down using clues, although if you gather enough of clues the character just becomes gps-trackable, and tracking down the hints itself is a chore. Still one of the best moments in the game was when I hit a monk by mistake during a raid and the lad suddenly started throwing hands giving me a very catholic jumpscare.

Graphically game looks good although nothing special nowadays. I believe Norway looked amazing and we should have much more game time set in there. England is nice but not very assassins creed-like, 80% of the map is either open fields of rivers. The fortresses that exist there are great but they were mostly set up there for main quest missions and aren’t used past that point.

Combat is the best part of this game, bit soulslike’ish even if wonky with bosses. The amount of customizability there is great and set to specific playstyles, with varied equipment and weapons. My main gripe would be that some weapons could have better effects, excalibur for example is a flashbang sword which barely stuns any enemies.

With that said the game is a collectathon with unprecedented scale before in this series. Jesus how much clutter can you get into this game is incredible.

I have finished this game with decent % of completion, synchronized all viewpoints, done all tombs of the fallen, done all the caves for excalibur, done a few river raids, completed side quest of Asgard, completely finished the roguelike mode. And obviously finished the story. It took me about 94 hours. Not much of it I liked. And although I’d like to complete the river raids the game crashed when I finished a very profitable raid, and since the game doesn’t save till you get back from the raid I just had enough.

Oh, and stealth is very meh too. So maybe get the game for the vikings if you want, otherwise, No.\t\t\t\t\t
