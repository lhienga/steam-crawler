\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMore disappointing than not. My biggest complaint is that I encountered a glitch at the very end of the game that is unfixable, and remains unpatched after all these years despite it being very well known. (i.e., Not initiating the final part of the Order of the Ancients story, after all but the last of its members are eliminated.) It's ridiculous that Ubisoft has left this unresolved, yet forces me to wait about a minute every time I launch the game to go look for possible new DLC.

Aside from that, the Assassin-Templar metaplot is too buried in this game. It's there and has some really fascinating plot points, but it takes too long to even start and isn't central enough to the game to tie it to the multi-game story arc in a satisfying way. Part of the problem is that the game is arguably too vast to be able to focus well on anything.

Overall I had fun many times with the game, but all too often it felt directionless, boring, or frustrating. This doesn't give me great faith in the next title in the series.\t\t\t\t\t
