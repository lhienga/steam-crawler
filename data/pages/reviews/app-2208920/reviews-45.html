\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is quite possibly my favorite Assassin's Creed game purely because of my love for anything and everything related to vikings. The world is huge, gorgeous and beautifully crafted; the main character is amazing and lovable, the story is quite good and full of mystery and surprises, the music is out of this world, as always thanks to Jeff Van Dyck. Other than that, the way Ubisoft implemented Norse mythology into the game is great and you get to learn about some really cool Old Norse stories and cultural aspects.

Where this game falls short is the gameplay department. The combat sucks, the animations are janky, most abilities are useless and there's no reason to choose anything aside from the few most powerful/convenient ones. Aside from that, many enemies (especially DLC enemies) have annoying movesets and are a drag to fight, which creates a fake sense of difficulty. The worst part to me is how horrible the stealth mechanics in this game are. Not to mention that the game feels like a total reskin of Odyssey, and Mirage feels like a total reskin of this game. I think Ubisoft should move away from this engine or at least heavily alter it.

All of what I previously said are just some minor to medium-sized gripes I have with the game, but where I started getting annoyed was Dawn of Ragnarok and The Forgotten Saga. The Season Pass DLC does a good job expanding upon the main gameplay elements and loop, but the only thing that Dawn did was add ostensibly neat abilities, most of which you have no reason to use outside of the puzzles you need to solve to acquire the useless loot in this DLC (that there is no reason to ever get anyway because there is no New Game +, by the way) and more of the same gameplay loop that you will have grown tired of by this point in the game. The worst part is, the story of Dawn doesn't even end! Instead, you have to play The Forgotten Saga, which is a needless roguelite annoying game mode, if you want to see what happens. By this point I had already gotten tired of the game and was just praying for it to end already. The amount of content in this game wouldn't be an issue if there was real variety to it.

All of this being said, even though this game annoyed me a lot, I still loved it and I have to recommend it. You're in for a ride. A bumpy one. But I'd say it's worth it.
