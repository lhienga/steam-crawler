\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played this for over 100 hours now, so I think I can safely comment on why I do not like this title like some of the others in the series. First, the story is just not that compelling. The characters are fairly lackluster and you really just do not feel any attachment to any of them, even Eivor the main character. Second, the raids quickly become boring and annoying and there is enough of it that you need to do to quickly make you want to put the controller down and walk away. Third point I'll make, and the thing that broke me down and had me uninstalling before completion: the effing random crashes. Good god this game crashes at the most random and inopportune times. I do not like to do things over, especially when you've taken time to stealth your way through a bunch of baddies to the target only to have the game crash back to Steam. Not cool.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis is a good game but its definitely the worst of the ARPG Assassins Creed games i have tried. and a series of missions i just tried prove my point. While i absolutely love the locations (ive been wanting Medieval England games for ages) this one runs into 3 major problems for me.

Problem 1, the story/pacing/setting, this game is weirdly slow to operate as you have to manually force the story to progress and it doesnt even remind you how to do that so you can forget if a story line was long. as well, its not super compelling due to the characters in this being a bit over competent. and the setting while technically medieval UK, its set at the very beginning of the medieval period, so some of what i most wanted, Knights and castles, arent present yet or are still being developed.

Problem 2, Eivor, they are on paper, a fantastic idea for a character, orphaned but raised by a King/Jarl, capable fighter, established to be smart, but still has several flaws that can be addressed through the story such as overly stubborn and overly confident. BUT, 42 hours in according to my save file, Eivor has learned not a damn thing, and is just not super likable. They are frequently wrong and the player isnt given the choice to act in the correct way when it is obvious Eivor is wrong. this becomes even more apparent when (SPOILERS) Kassandra from AC Odyssey shows up for a major side mission in Isle of Skye, and is both constantly correct, but also very charismatic. Kassandra is just better and letting her appear is both a blessing as i paid more attention to everything when she was around and curse as it made me like Eivor even less.

Problem 3, Ubisoft somehow has not fixed numerous problems with this game despite being out for multiple years now. I have more crashes, bad launches, glitches and odd details than i did with Odyssey or Origins and i played Odyssey from release. these problems can be bad enough that a whole major character wont load in a cutscene and everything else plays out as if they were. so you just completely lose context and have to continually close and reload the save or even the game until it finally loads the character and you can understand what is happening.

Overall, i can say the game is good and fun. but if you loved AC Odyssey or Origins, lower your expectations. If you want a stealthy AC game, its got a little more than Odyssey but not a ton, and if you want a Medieval Knights game, this needed to move ahead a couple hundred years or so for that. but if you find it on sale for like $15 like i did, its worth it. i absolutely dont regret buying but idk if ill finish it. it mostly makes me want to play AC Odyssey again and find another game that better fits the Medieval UK setting i really want.\t\t\t\t\t
