\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAssassin's Creed Valhalla: A Disappointing Departure from the Shadows

Ubisoft, are you serious? Assassin's Creed Valhalla feels like a departure from the franchise's core identity, leaning more towards Witcher-esque gameplay than stealth mechanics. The emphasis on brute close combat proves tedious and lacks the finesse expected from an Assassin's Creed title.

Combat encounters are marred by frustrating mechanics, from struggling to find healing to getting stuck on environmental objects. And beware of fire - your character seems oddly susceptible to spontaneous combustion!

While visually stunning, Valhalla feels barren and devoid of the depth that characterized previous entries. The Norse setting, while appealing in theory, falls flat in execution, lacking the intrigue and depth of past Assassin's Creed narratives.

As a Scandinavian myself, I had high hopes for Valhalla's exploration of Norse mythology. However, the result feels like a shallow imitation, relying too heavily on Viking tropes without delivering meaningful storytelling.

Overall, Assassin's Creed Valhalla is a disappointing entry in the franchise. Compared to the standards set by predecessors like Origins, it falls short in depth and execution. Here's hoping for a return to form in future installments.

Score: 4/10\t\t\t\t\t
