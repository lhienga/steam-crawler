\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like it. Something like "dark souls with guns" - takes a bit of practice to learn the patters, but after some grinding and trying you should be able to beat the final boss.

The best thing about this loop in Remnant is that you lose NOTHING when you die. You died - you got more ammo, go and try again! If you keep dying, go upgrade your gear or look up a strategy for your specific boss. Or go farm more skills and/or gear in Adventure mode (repeatable random chunks of campaign world that co-exist with your campaign).

The game offers a wide selection of unique gear (weapons, armors, trinkets, all have unique effects), large pool of skills (mostly +10-20% to some stat), decent selection of monsters and bosses, and the goal is to go out there and kill stuff. There are several "biomes" (zones, worlds) with different styling, so things are somewhat varied.

The game has a number of difficulties if you are up for a challenge. The game runs well, plays well, is better with teammates (up to 3 players in a single "world").

Not a masterpiece (it gets repetitive after a while and there just aren't enough unique encounters, unique boss/monster mechanics to keep playing), but will keep you occupied for a while. Especially if you have friends to play with.

There's also "survival mode" where you start with a pistol, have a number of shops with VERY limited selection of gear and try your best to kill as many bosses as you can. But the longer you play, the harder the mobs get! And at some point they get too hard for you, so it's not infinite. And it offers 28 bosses in total (there are campaign-only bosses in the game, they're not present in survival). It's pretty hard but manageable. But you need the first DLC for it. Second DLC adds a small campaign and a new map and a few new items. And is required for all achievements. Both DLCs are OK, not awesome, but OK.

Overall, it's quite entertaining if you like souls like games (with large, strong bosses than do a whole ton of damage but who also have slow, well-telegraphed attacks, when you have limited healing items and rely on perfectly dodging the attacks). Except it offers guns on top of melee weapons. Most of the guns have different mechanics, and there are "mods" to further add variety (special effects tied to weapons, like summoning helpers or grenade launchers with area of effect attacks or damage-over-time effects, this kind of stuff).

The game has a good number of "secrets" you will only find by accident, or by looking them up online. I recommend playing on "normal" difficulty "blind" first, and only looking something up if you can't progress. You can study "meta" after you beat the main story, if you're still interested in continuing.

Some limitations: the grind gets boring after a while and despite "procedurally generated" worlds, there's a lot of repeating parts. More than that, in order to play through everything, you need to play the game multiple times. Bosses do not respawn, you need to "re-roll the world" for it. And every time you get a limited selection of those in each world, so you may end up rerolling a lot to get all the items the game has to offer. The second large limitation is that the worlds feel stale. There's just nothing happening. There are almost no NPCs, other than traders and story-related NPCs that... just stand there... you don't make any choices, you don't affect outcomes, the plot is linear, and the bits and pieces of lore, while entertaining, are quite limited.\t\t\t\t\t
