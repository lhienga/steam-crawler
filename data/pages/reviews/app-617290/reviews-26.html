\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall it's a good game. Not long, but I had an enjoyable 20 hours or so playing it. The locations are varied and creative, and the core game-play loop is well-made and enjoyable.

The storytelling however, is godawful. The setup is very good, with an intriguing backstory. The game then goes on to do almost literally nothing with it. You're playing through levels, with no real idea what you're doing or why. Lots of stuff is teased and then not properly explained. Out of 20 hours of game-play, maybe... 2 hours was actually plot relevant? The rest was dungeon crawling and boss fights.

In many ways, it really does feel like half a game. This is a shame because it could have been so much better. I've recommended it, because I did enjoy myself. If you can find it on sale, it is worth the purchase. I doubt I will play it again though, now I've completed it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Co-op Delight in the World of Souls-like Mastery

"Remnant: From the Ashes" isn't just another entry into the souls-like genre; it's a shining example of how to do it right, and it takes the experience to a whole new level with its seamless cooperative play.

At its core, "Remnant" presents a post-apocalyptic world overrun by otherworldly horrors, a backdrop that sets the stage for a challenging and deeply immersive adventure. What sets this game apart is its successful integration of the souls-like formula with a cooperative multiplayer focus, making it not only a thrill to play solo but an absolute blast in co-op.

The souls-like mechanics, characterized by punishing difficulty, strategic combat, and intense boss battles, are executed with finesse. Every encounter is a test of skill, demanding thoughtful approaches and precision in combat. The procedural generation system adds a layer of unpredictability, ensuring that no two playthroughs are the same. This, in turn, significantly enhances the game's replayability, providing a fresh challenge with each new session.

What truly elevates "Remnant" is its cooperative play. Joining forces with up to two friends adds an extra layer of enjoyment to an already gripping experience. The synergy between players, strategic coordination, and the shared triumphs over formidable foes create a camaraderie that few games in the genre can match. It transforms an already intense single-player adventure into a cooperative masterpiece.

The customization options for characters and weapons add depth to the gameplay, allowing players to tailor their experiences to their preferred playstyles. The variety in weaponry, coupled with the ability to adapt and modify, provides a satisfying sense of progression and personalization.

The visuals and design are praiseworthy, painting a vivid and atmospheric post-apocalyptic world that's both haunting and beautiful. The attention to detail in the environments and the menacing design of the otherworldly creatures contribute to the overall immersive experience.

In conclusion, "Remnant: From the Ashes" is a souls-like done right, offering an engaging and challenging adventure that gets even better when shared with friends. The cooperative play elevates the experience, turning an already standout game into a must-play for fans of intense action RPGs. If you're seeking a world to conquer with friends, where every victory is hard-fought and immensely satisfying, "Remnant" beckons you to join the fight. It's a cooperative delight in the challenging realm of souls-like mastery.\t\t\t\t\t
