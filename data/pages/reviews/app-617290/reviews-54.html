\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Remnant is a difficult game to get through. That's not because the game wants to be a souls like, that's because the difficulty isn't natural or rewarding. You can see that they tried to incorporate some tactics with SOME bosses, but a majority of them don't feel special or unique. The game favors hordes instead of tactics while making sure the AI gets a hit on you despite perfectly dodging the enemy that already locked you in. At times your input can feel off or slightly delayed, as well as completely ignored. The game can be fun if you're playing with a friend (I suggest multiple, if you have them), but I would probably advise against playing the game solo. The game is not balanced very well and I've already addressed it's favorite way to engage the player.

Anyways, it's a thumbs up because it's better than most of the games that are released these days, and as I said, with friends the game becomes much more enjoyable. You get what feels like a complete game that isn't entirely riddled with bugs and game breaking moments. I got a few good laughs out of some of the enemies designs and the voice actors. The game has some personality that really lifts it up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an interesting take on the formula and I would definitely recommend picking this game up on sale.

I would call this less of a souls-like and more of a souls-inspired. It honestly feels more like a diet Destiny experience to me than anything else. Though there are a lot of things ripped straight from Fromsoft, even to the point of one whole boss making me wonder if I booted up the wrong game. Fighting in an area that's too small to handle? Check. Boss attacks intentionally designed to knock you off the edge instead of actually fight you? Check. Annoying mechanic where the boss moves away into a position where you can't damage it as much? Check. Unnecessary double boss fight where the second boss adds zero interesting mechanics? Check. I mean come on guys, you just stole Moonlight Butterfly and accidentally hit ctrl+v one too many times.

There are so many interesting concepts and ideas here that are really cool and fun to explore. The traits system is pretty cool, though it is one the slowest and least impactful (per level) leveling systems I've used. Unfortunately my biggest issue with this game is with the way leveling and gearing up works with how the game balances. The developers have confirmed that the game sets the difficulty of the area you are in by the gear you are wearing when you enter the area and how leveled it is. They use a formula that hasn't been made clear to weight pieces of gear differently. This means that the game actively punishes you for grinding and leveling up your weapons and armor. You can inadvertently make the game more difficult at random points by upgrading, which makes the flow of the game feel really strange. I only played on normal, so at no point did it become overwhelmingly difficult, but the difficulty went back and forth from a walking simulator to enemies completely filling the screen and taking entire clips to take down (with a fully upgraded weapon). I fully upgraded myself at the end of the game and all it seemed to do was turn all the enemies (including the most basic ones) into bullet sponges. The game didn't necessarily get more difficult, just more annoying.

The game was very obviously built with co-op in mind, but if you're lonely like me and have no one to play with it can still provide a pretty fun experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant: From the Ashes is a very good game. It lacks certain key features that would make it a great game, but it is fun and well-designed, which is more than I can say for the 90% of the games today.

What it gets right:

The gunplay: The shooting and the movement is tight and precise and it feels like you are in control all of the time. Despite being "Dark Souls with guns", the game is challenging but fair and if you play carefully, you can overcome every obstacle. Yes, the single-player mode feels harder (because all of the mobs focus on you), but it will never make you feel frustrated or angered or rage-quit.

The gameplay: The gameplay is pretty straight-forward: You either clear mobs in a semi-open environment, or you clear mobs in a dungeon or you fight bosses. The mobs are easy but they can be challenging when they swarm on you (so you have to prioritize) or when they combine range and melee attacks. The bosses are a challenge, mostly because you have to switch constantly between the boss and the boss. It's fun and it works. It doesn't do a lot of things, but the one-two things it does, it does perfectly

The traits and the gear: The gear is scarce, so it is not a looter-shooter. Also the gear is upgradable, so you can finish the game with your original gear from level one - if you upgrade it constantly. The same applies to the guns. I finished the game with the sniper rifle I had from the start. There are some interesting weapons with interesting mods (like summoning adds), and you can upgrade them easily by running adventures (random generated content). The traits are plenty and they really help. If you are on single-player, focus on the glutton trait, mostly because every consumable takes 3-4 seconds to activate, so if you consume an item, you'll probably die before you get full health.

What doesn't get right:

The story: While the idea is great (the rot taking over the worlds) and the multiverse is interesting, the story is non-existent as it doesn't have any plot, any characters, any tension or anything at all. The game doesn't need them, to be perfectly honest, but it is such a missed opportunity.

The lack of pause: Yes, even if you are single-player offline, you can pause this game. If something happens and you need to leave, you might as well quit, because the mobs will come and get you eventually.

Generally, I recommend Remnant even to the players that don't play souls-like games in general. It's a good game, but the lack of an engaging story deprives it of being a memorable game into a just enjoyable one.\t\t\t\t\t
