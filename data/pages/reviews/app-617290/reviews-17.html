\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has the 'souls-like' tag in the store and I can't see it anywhere. I'm glad i got it on sale, cause it is not worth the $40 price tag. The difficulty is inflated by throwing more and more enemies at you per encounter instead of any meaningful, mechanical changes like you'd expect.

The gun play, world, and story are all great, but it feels hollow. It feels like there should be more to the game and it just doesn't satisfy that soulsbourne itch. The game is trying to be too many things at once and doesn't have enough meat on the bone to really hook me.

Minuscule amounts of customization. New weapons and armor are rare to come across and your options are very few. Its fun to play with your friends for a few hours but nothing long term. It's designed to be replayed many times over to be able to experience all of the different dungeons and events, but it was difficult going through it once. (It was boring as hell)

The end of the game is abrupt and left me stunlocked. I really wish there was more to it. Maybe Remnant 2 will be better, but i'm going to wait for it to be on sale like its prequel.

Games to play instead!
Outriders for that fun mix of abilities and weapons with a great story and plenty of replayability
Thymesia for that bloodborne itch with a unique weapon system.
CODE VEIN for that souls-like ass kicking you want, you masochist.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI figured that rather than writing a full review, I'm just gonna lay out my thoughts in no particular order.

Having a really hard time with this game, but I'm enjoying it. The combat is responsive and satisfying, the levels are challenging and full of stuff to find, and the story takes a surprisingly cool surreal and interdimensional turn a few bosses in.

I thought this was gonna be another AAA Souls-like that falls short of its premise, but it's actually pretty good. I only played solo so far, and I'm aware the game advertises itself as co-op focused, but I have yet to encounter a boss I can't defeat after trying again and again and again (which I'd say is in the spirit of things for Souls-likes).

The one problem I have with the bosses is that, while they're definitely well designed, my ADHD arse has a hard time focusing on minions and the boss at the same time, so I kinda wish bosses had fewer minions. I mean, the minions are there for two main reasons, I believe: to give you ammo pick ups, and to give a party of cooperators stuff to shoot at. In other words, the reason why the minions annoy me is because I'm not playing the game as intended, so I can't really complain. Beating bosses never stops feeling like an accomplishment, but it does knock the wind out of me and make me feel like stopping for the day.

I also quite like the character writing, especially ol' Mud Tooth, the lovable old man living in the 'helocolopter'. The lore is fascinating, the whole interdimensional stuff is cool, and yeah.

Hmm, what else is there to say? I don't like that the one way to get certain pick ups is to just sweep the same spots in the same maps over and over, but that's also par for the course, so I can't complain too much about it.\t\t\t\t\t
