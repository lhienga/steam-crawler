\r\n\t\t\t\t\tquite the intriguing game...
the most unique feature is that the Map layout is completely randomized everytime you play, from the special events in them to even different final bosses of the areas (minus the final boss of the campaign), meaning what loot you get is also randomized, but the game features what's called "adventure mode" which allows you to replay different area layouts without resetting the campaign progress whether you wanna loot or just experience a certain area/dungeon once again

Story is nothing all too riveting, the whole story as you go from what seems like a simple post-apocalypse world with some eldritch weirdness to uncovering inter-dimensional travel is intriguing but the story goes by really quick and it doesn't feel that fleshed out sadly, but if your a gameplay first story second I suppose it wouldn't be an issue

as for the gameplay loop it feels like a looter shooter but really isn't as something like Borderlands or Destiny where you pick up a large amount of weapons with varying stats that get replaced as soon as you start earning legendary gear you get from bosses, the game instead has it so every weapon you get is special in it's own way from the boss gear to the "standard" gear you get
and I put standard in quotation marks as even they have their own quirks and can become customizable with a wide array of secondary attacks, really does good at giving variety of weapons without having the whole grindy aspect that can draw people away from looter shooters

good solo but most people reccomend you play with friends, which can be hard as the player count isn't the highest and if you get connected and you have bad lag it pretty much means death, as since this is souls-inspired reaction time with your dodging is KEY and lag can really kill it, especially since the lobby browser contains no connection stability notifications

overall an 4/5 story i feel needs more as parts of it do feel really interesting\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried this game coop with my brother. It strikes quite a nice balance between challenging and relaxing. Some of the bosses are extremely surprising - at first blush, they are tanky and hit hard and you have no idea how shooting for longer or dodging 99% instead of 95% of their attacks is interesting - as you understand what mechanics you could use to gain leverage over them, some of them become downright fascinating; intrigues of game design.

Some of the fights you can tell are scaled for three people, and doing them with two is difficult but rewarding.

I played the last 2/3rds and the expansions via online coop with strangers and realized this game is meant to be played exactly like Diablo 2. You join games and quest with randos, but you also often will help them with a quest that you do not have and vice versa. This leads you to discover NPCs and areas in a region (which will be located differently in your game-world, each person's being randomly generate - some NPCs or areas may even be absent in exchange for another) which you may not have found yourself, and you establish some piecemeal comprehensions of future quests which you may endeavor.

The feeling, later on, of working in parties and sometimes knowing (without any language - there is no viable media for written lingual communications implemented in the game) what quests others are on, sometimes realizing that they know what quests you are on (and how they should be done, when they step into a guest leadership mode and rush you along the key events) is wonderful.

When the piecemeal comprehensions come together finally - you spend an hour or two doing a quest, and you see a few familiar items or NPCs or locations, and suddenly you understand what other events were related to those items or NPCs - the sense of satisfaction is very strong. When it begins to happen more frequently, as you have several pieces of several puzzles in your knowledge - It completely changes your opinion on what the game you are playing *is*. You change from a vacationer & a follower into a custodian & a leader as you hit the three objectives you now know were related. You understand the NPC dialogues and cutscenes that you have already seen as they slot into your newly enlightened perspective.

Because of how this works, it is not for those who demand to know everything up front. This is a lot like Diablo 2 - you want to do boss runs for loot, you want to run with groups for safety - and in so doing, you accidentally discover foundational narrative perspectives & significant questlines.

There is also not so much going on that you are not given the opportunity for insight; the size of this game is a perfect one for the Diablo 2-style, experience-minutiae-as-a-follower-first, emergent comprehension of the broadest picture. thirty or forty hours of play, mostly playing coop in regions you are in (and thus often helping people a little ahead in one of your quest lines), with some play with significantly higher level people in unknown regions and some showing people below you how to proceed, results in a total experience which is magical. You have insight into all possible worlds - being RNG, you need to play coop to understand this - and understand leading & following in each region and along each questline.

It is hard to say what the best way to play this is. It's good private co-op with a personal friend, wherein the outstanding part is how fun a shooter it is and how good some boss fights are, but I recommend it foremost as a solo online experience akin to Diablo 2, with a balance around 3:1 guest-host world experience.
