\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant 1 is what you could call a hidden gem.

This game is just simply, fun to play. Thats it all. Inspired from Dark Souls but with Guns, makes it looks like its not a your typical 1:1 clone. Rarely any games make me want to play them everyday once i get back home.

Playing this game, doesn't feel like you have to sit on the chairs with nails. Unlike other souls-like games. Game is not designed to punish you, it was made to give you a "casual" fun.

First, character movement feels very smooth and natural, it does not have a weight of a prisoner with chains.

Boss fights? Are absolutely great, they feel fair, their animations and hitboxes are responsive. You can clearly see what their weakpoints are, their attack patterns are clean and don't try to bait you out ( aka their attack slow down to bait you into post-end roll animation ). Dying to bosses, doesn't feel like a cheap death.. It also applies to all common enemies, great sound cues, fair animations etc.

All bosses usually take 2-5 tries to figure them out, they are not hard but that doesn't mean these are not challenging ( butterfly boss or undying king ). And of course in coop they get bit easier ( tiny bit, cause there is someone to revive you )

This game has RPG choices too, quests have few branching options and each one usually comes with some unique item or reward. Hence why gearing is quite fun, its not a shoot & loot its a shoot & explore to find loot. Hidden in places among the maps, behind events ( that have branching paths and randomized ).

By defeating each boss, you get unique gun , mod or melee weapon. And some bosses have breakable parts that also unlock new stuff or you have to kill them in certain way. Not to mention all weapon feel fun to use, and mix& matching with mods brings out quite a ton of different build setups.

Story, starts slow but it gets quite interested. It does not show you with tons of informations, story is there and with fantastic environment design in later stages. Its gets extremely comfy with loreness.

Co-Op is perfectly done here. If not only Remnant 2 is around the corner, i would simply spend few hundred in this game alone. It offers at minimum 2 playthings if you want to experience all events and bosses that spawn in random patterns.

It was quite fascinating game experience for me, i would put it on the shelf among my Gothic 1, Nioh2 but I will await for Remnant 2 with that. Cause I don't wanna burn myself out with gameplay, especially when sequel is soon to be released.

This game has perfect formula, and now all they need is to improve upon it to reach perfection.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCHANGED MY RECOMMENDATION TO NO AFTER 28 HRS OF GAMEPLAY AND BEATING THE MAIN CAMPAIGN!

My first impression was that the game was a decent 3rd person shooter that I could recommend - provided you're okay with it NOT being a AAA game in terms of graphics, voice acting, and controls. Your character's movement animations are janky and you inexplicably do EVERYTHING slowly (reload, take healing potions, etc) in the middle of combat. This was in keeping with the Dark Souls style of gameplay - which some people dodge and roll simulators.

Well, there is one annoying game design choice that has made me abandon this game: the ENEMY SCALING TO YOUR HIGHEST LEVEL GEAR. Enemy scaling is a lazy idiot's design choice. You see, when you do not know how to properly design and balance a game, so that players feel challenged throughout, or you are too lazy to take the time to design a proper game... lower IQ game devs rely on a simple.. and I MEAN SIMPLE formula that just looks at your gear, and levels up the enemies accordingly.

Enemy scaling renders your gear leveling and crafting system moot. In fact, you should NEVER level up your gear until enemies feel slightly hard. When they do, you know that you're in an area where the minimum level is higher than your gear. This makes the whole point of collecting loot, and crafting resources, absolutely meaningless.

Since the game is linear, it might as well be a 2D side-scroller which, come to think of it, would probably have been more fun. Anytime I see auto-enemy scaling, I will always tell folks to NOT play a game. I wasted my time and money. If you're reading this, don't waste yours, even if you get the game on sale like I did.\t\t\t\t\t
