\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant's a wild ride with a fair few bumps along the road. Here are some do's and don'ts to get the most out of it:

DO:
Play with friends. Yeah, you can solo the game just fine but the real magic happens when you have somebody else shooting up the apocalypse with you. It's honestly something special when you and a buddy make two meme builds and find out none of the enemies are laughing because they're a lot better than you would've thought.

Take your time. This game has a LOT of RNG, so there's going to be a long, hard grind. Take things as they come and enjoy the ride, you'll get what you're looking for eventually.

Read the wiki. Look, it's fine to complete each zone a few times without looking up anything to get the blind experience, but between the many, MANY obscure unlockables and the ungodly amount of RNG this game has you're gonna need to check that wiki.

Remember you have a melee weapon. it may seem stupid in a shooter, but there are a lot of enemies that don't deserve a bullet. Save that ammo, my friends.

DON'T:
Take the story too seriously. Look, the devs tried to make a Dark Souls style "fill in the blanks" story and they clearly didn't realize how hard that is. There are a lot of parts that don't make any sense whatsoever, so just enjoy it for what it is and shrug off the bits that the devs didn't think through.

Be afraid to experiment. The starting kits you get are all solid, but the fun part of this game is cooking up wacky builds and finding out they work a whole lot better than expected

Be discouraged when you fail. Failure's mandatory in this game. You're going to die a lot while you figure things out and when you're working on new builds, so just shake it off and get even with whatever eldritch monstrosity just disemboweled you.

Waste your simulacrum. They're a pain to grind, so spend them on dragon heart upgrades and weapons you 100% know you'll use a lot.\t\t\t\t\t
