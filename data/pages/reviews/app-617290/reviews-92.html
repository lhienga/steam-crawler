\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had never heard about Remnant in any of the gaming media, the only reason I got it was because someone told me it had a decent coop.

Boy, I was in for a treat. Imagine a Souls game with procedurally generated maps. Now add GUNS to it and you're starting to get a feeling what this game is all about.

The coop can be played in a campaign, but only the host will advance in his game. However, this is not a problem, because, the way they did it, you can play a campaign with a friend while keeping yours, bringing all the levels, weapons and mods you get from their game. And even as a guest in the game, the story is still told as if you were the host.

It is not an easy game, but it's not frustratingly hard either. It will challenge even veteran players as you struggle to some mechanics: after that it "clicks" and you feel like a genius, beating bosses with somewhat ease (the twins on the bridge will still serve your rear end on platter, tho).

I highly recommend this game if you're up for some challenging fun, specially with friends.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechanically good. Shooting and headshot feel is 10/10. Not SUPER optimized, had to play with framelock. In one room fps is 300 in the next small corridor it's 90.

Story has potential but badly executed. Bosses are super cool but almost soulless, it's just becomes grind at some point.

But the weird, map/openworld setting is very unconventional. You almost immediately got lost in the game and it's not linear.

We accidently killed the final boss without knowing, turns out you have to explore every inch of the map before going forward. But you don't know which way is forward. So you go in random corners and dungeons and suddenly you are in another map.

It feels very annoying to teleport back to the map you just cleared just because you picked the wrong door. And when you teleport back, all the monsters respawn, so it becomes a chore real quick.

And game won't inform you throughly about stats, mechanics, conditions, and stuff. You have to figure most of them by yourself or googleing. There are skins in this game, which we never unlocked because we didn't know where to find the "Glowing Fragment".

Apparently, you have to play the game on Harder modes to find them. But to survive harder modes you need the weapon and armor upgrades. And to upgrade your gear you have to play the game. And when you upgrade your gear enough to deal with hard mode, you finish the game. When I googled how to get fragments, all of the tutorials I found was using either save-scamming or glitches.

So good job on those skins you just unlocked. The game is over. Cringe.

We bought this game just because of the CO-OP Souls-Like gameplay clickbait . Should've just picked something else.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's clear that the team who made this tried to make a really cool game, and you can see that there are some cool ideas here, but I honestly can't recommend this.

Let's start with the good. The premise is pretty interesting -- apocalypse against eldritch horror trees that you need to traverse different realms to flee. Also, The art department did a good job with some levels (particularly the DLC) where the environment has a cool feel to it.

Now, the bad.

Everything mechanically feels awkward. Throughout the entire time I've played I could never get the sensitivity right and you are forced to engage enemies in a shallow mid-range which doesn't help because the FOV is kinda tight. You feel like you are moving through molasses the whole game because animations are slow, you move slow, and you run out of stamina all the time running from place to place. Later, you can find upgrade perks to speed some things up, but it's pretty much useless to spend points there because there are way more useful skills to put your points into.

As for skills, there are way too many of them that are completely pointless and only serve to clutter up the trait upgrade screen. I beat the whole game essentially only putting points into health and DMG increases, and really that's all you will have points for unless you re-roll the campaign a bunch of times or grind the areas. Though, I have no idea why you would do this as there's no real reason to and most of the perks are minimal improvements at best. You can get much better perks from the armor anyways.

The armor effectively renders class choice pointless as you can just buy the other classes armor sets in the shop once you get enough material. Though, even that is a bit pointless because you will get better armor not long into just going through the campaign and beating bosses. My friend chose the Hunter class and I chose the Cultist, and not far into the game we both just ended up choosing long range weapons and equipping armor that boosted long range damage, effectively making us the same class.

Speaking of the bosses, they are pretty disappointing. Pretty much all of them are just bullet sponges with one gimmick that you have have to work around. You'll probably die a few times figuring out the mechanics (because they are not communicated well to the player), but as soon as you do it will probably take you 1 try to beat any given boss. Many of the bosses can see clear inspiration from Dark Souls bosses or standard boss tropes, which does feel more like homage, but means that none of the mechanics are going to feel very novel.

Overall, every area is the same: you show up, shoot a bunch of mindless ads in a looping map with ease, talk to an NPC, fight 3 bosses, and go onto the next area. The story is non-existent, and during my play through there was even a bug where the game would just cut off character dialogue mid-delivery -- It felt like even the game didn't care. And honestly, it's better that way because none of the parts that I did manage to track made any sense when I thought about it (i.e. why are we killing the guardians of the world if that's just going to facilitate more Root, and our objective is to destroy the root? etc.).

If you can pick this game up dirt cheap with a friend, it might be fun to mess around for a bit. But, I wouldn't buy it for what I bought it for ($20-ish), and certainly not at full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game solo.

Not a terrible game.
Not a great one either.

The level design is terrible : a bunch of corridors and stairs with nooks and crannies everywhere, but with rarely anything it there. But, as there will occasionally be a unique item in there, or an enemy waiting to ambush you, you still have to check every. Single. One of them.
That would be fine if levels were interesting to traverse. But they aren't : there's not verticality to the levels, there's no looping or inter-connectivity to the levels, there's no interesting way to move around. Your character being slow as hell doesn't help either, especially since you don't have infinite stamina outside combat.

The combat itself is fine. The weapons are pretty fun to use, and the mods make things interesting. The enemy design is also pretty solid, even if it sometimes feel like kinda random, lore-wise.

Bosses are... a mixed bag. Some of them are really good. Some of them are just okay. But. Every. Single. Bossfight. Is filled, FILLED with adds. There isn't a single boss fight, not ONE, without a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ of adds. This not only make snipers and revolvers virtually unusable during a boss encounter, but also has the effect of making bosses incredibly similar : hit and run for a minute, transition, kill some adds, rinse and repeat. Bosses usually have 1 or 2 attacks, and 1 ability. And that's it. Incredibly underwhelming and frustrating.

The story and lore are okay. Nothing stellar, nothing horrible. It's just fine.

All in all, as a solo experience it is impossible to recommend this game. Maybe it's fun in multiplayer, but in solo, it's just incredibly tedious and frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPeople keep saying "Dark Souls but with guns" no it isn't! It is nothing like Dark Souls, it is no more like Dark Souls than Tomb Raider is like Mario, they both jump around on platforms....right???

I can't get into this game personally, the world looks very nice and some of the concepts are cool. For me the game play felt too detached, I can't quite put my finger on it but I just didn't feel in control of the player character. I'm not going to down vote but wish I could neutral vote or even better abstain, I haven't played long enough to give a fair critique (although I have played for significantly longer than 1 hour!). All I can offer is my initial impressions.

I'm really confused why people are saying this is a Souls like but with guns, it very much is not, the only connection is that you fight bunches of enemies and sometimes they are hard to beat. Much like every action game you've played, ever! Anyway...

The world looks very nice although AA, but that isn't an issue. The atmosphere and mood is funny, on one hand the world has been ravaged by 'the root' (no ♥♥♥♥ jokes, please) as soon as you step outdoors you are greeted by these weird roid-rage Groot characters, yet indoors people seem to be getting on with things perfectly fine. The world excluding the root reminds me of Nier Automata, it is vastly different but I get the same vibe, make of that what you will.

The gun play feels fine apart of the detached feeling I mentioned earlier, I picked the ex-cult class which had some nice starting weapons but it would have been good to have a fallback melee weapon. Stealth isn't really an option and I'm not sure if it is even meant to be, I was never able to sneak attack a single enemy, again maybe you're not meant to play that way?

The story-line is an acquired taste, the dialogue and scripting is.......sketchy, respectfully. You start the game attempting to rescue a dude then get attacked yourself, these people drag you into their bunker and save you. When you wake you're told to clear out the basement and restore power, then immediately afterwards the head-honcho gives you this special key that her grandfather gave to her. The key opens a big ass teleport crystal..... I often find myself giving away family heirlooms after somebody has fixed the fuse-box.

I can see how some might like this game, it is a good effort for this type of game but it IS NOT A SOULS GAME WITH GUNS!\t\t\t\t\t
