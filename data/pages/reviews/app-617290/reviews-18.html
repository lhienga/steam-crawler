\r\n\t\t\t\t\t TLDR; Story is pretty interesting, but it's not gonna change your life or anything. A fun experience alone or with 1-2 other players. Very replayable with randomly generated map layouts and loot finds/events, and challenging in the RIGHT ways (Friendly to casual/non-souls players, but still offers you a challenge in the right places), but not overly if you don't want it to be. Worth the price in my opinion.

___________

The Full Review:
Solo/Co-op: This game is very fun both alone and with friends (even just one other person). Sorry to your four stack, however, this is a up to three player experience. I went through with my best friend and myself, and we had a BLAST. We loved the lore, the fights, and just exploring together. And don't worry about what class you choose, you can always swap everything around if you want, and if you both want to be the same thing, go for it. You can customize quite a bit of your loadout to compensate for having similar abilities (that you can swap out for others as well as you unlock new ones).

Replayable/Difficulty: It's something you can just dive into and replay over and over as the maps are randomly made and you won't see everything the first time through. Eventually, yes, you'll have seen everything, but that's when you can challenge yourself by upping the difficulty and trying different strats to defeat certain bosses to see if they drop different loot. (And if you're nervous about the souls-like tag, don't be, it's a challenge at some parts, but there's difficulty settings and whatever you face, you can overcome with a little determination and learning about what you're against.) This game is incredibly friendly to those who are not souls-like vets, but it's also a good fit for those vets as well as they can enjoy the game on harder difficulties if it wasn't challenging enough. For more casual players, you might find yourself wanting to challenge yourself down the road and I couldn't recommend it more as it's quite satisfying overcoming that one boss who kept beating you for two days straight because you learned the attack pattern and made a strategy.
*note: I have found, personally, this game is a bit more forgiving in some cases than an official Souls game, so don't stress too hard about dying in this. You get to get back up and try again with no real consequence for your failure than just a few steps back at your last checkpoint. No biggie. It might be less friendly on harder difficulties, but for normal difficulty, it wasn't so bad.

I can't think of much else to say here because I don't normally review games, but this one? This one deserved me at least giving it an attempt. I have very fond memories playing this with my best friend, and we're soon to set out to play the sequel. Neither of us were souls-like gamers, and both of us are picky about what we enjoy, and we came out of this game with smiles on our faces (and a sense of accomplishment after beating one of the last bosses in the game (vets, you know which one)). We fully enjoyed this game and found that this game just got the formula right for casual gamers and souls-like players. Even if you hate Dark Souls (or love it), you'll probably enjoy this.\t\t\t\t\t
