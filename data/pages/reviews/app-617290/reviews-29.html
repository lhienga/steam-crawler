\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is decent enough to play as a co-op, but I would recommend waiting for a sale before picking it up as the content offered is pretty basic. The game took 10 hours to complete on co-op, and could have been even less potentially (my friend kept dying), so it is a pretty small game as well.

Pros:

1) The worlds and creatures seem pretty interesting, and the different biomes we get to see are fairly different from each other.

2) The weapon selection and weapon mods allow for different combinations that can keep things interesting.

3) Mechanics are responsive and feel good, which is important in a game of this genre.

Cons:

1) The story is....there. It seems half-baked, and they definitely ran out of budget towards the second half of the game, leading to the plot being shortened and simplified, just to conclude the game. It definitely felt underwhelming when the credits started to roll.

2) All the characters you meet are flat and boring personality-wise. They have one or two default lines to spout when you interact with them and that's about it. This makes the game feel quite dated.

3) Character classes largely mean nothing and you can just do whatever you want regardless of what initial class you choose.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are for sure some rough design choices in the game, but overall this is a game with tons of secrets, weapons to collect, armors, trinkets and rings, interesting enemy and level designs, unique bosses, and more. But the important thing is that this is all PART OF THE BASE GAME. No seasons passes, no stupid live events, no awful MTX, and the DLCs the game offers actually pack a ton of content.

Sure it's "Dark Souls with guns," but it's also a complete experience where all of the fun stuff is baked in and not broken off and sold back to you as obnoxious and expensive DLC or MTX.

This is also a banger of a coop game, so fun, so challenging, and really lets each player differentiate themselves with the diverse amount of builds you can come up with. The coop mechanics are also decidedly NOT like Dark Souls so you directly join and play with your friends without any goofy summon signs or secret passwords which is also very welcome.

Just one other thing, the narrative in this is not great. I mean it's not awful, but the game is less about a compelling narrative experience and more about slowly accumulating items to make builds and refine them so you can take on increasingly tougher challenges. It's about running the content to get resources or new items that let you increase the effectiveness of your builds or even create new ones based on what you find. People who are disappointed with the game raise the lackluster story as a frequent item of disappointment, but if you can just not let that bother you it's otherwise a fantastic experience.\t\t\t\t\t
