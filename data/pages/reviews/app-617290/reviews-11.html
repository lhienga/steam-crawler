\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI gotta say im really really disappointed by this game.

Everything went well for the first impression. The intro, sorta tutorials and stuff was really decent and kinda immersive even tho i entirely dislike the basic lore. The gameplay is AMAZING. Its honestly like way better than any other soulslike ive played, better than every entry in the Souls Series. You can play this game just perfectly with mouse and keyboard and thats absolutely dope. Movesets, weapon mods and such are fun and theres always lots of weight to hits. Dodge rolling and vaulting is very smooth and reliable. Bosses are unique enough and offer a nice challenge. So i really enjoyed all of that...

Now here is the FUNDAMENTAL issue this game has which is gear variety. 3 bioms deep into the game im pretty much still running around with my tutorial loadout. I found an entirety of 2 boss weapons and 1 armor set in three entire bioms when exploring every little bit of it carefully. This has gotten very frustrating and honestly just boring and dull. Running around with always the same stuff. Also just got a single amulet which was easily missable. Rings drop a ton tho, got like 10 or 15 of those and theyre cool and useful but theyre the only gear type thats in the game for some reason. Bosses only got a single option to forge out of their ressource, a single weapon, no choices. And 75% of bosses only "drop" a weapon mod on top of that. Its hillarious.

That issue basically makes character progression kinda non existent. The "level ups" offer so few impact on your actual char that they dont really matter apart from hp and perhaps endurance. The only thing you actually progress is leveling up weapons and armors with mats in order to higher their stats, which is satisfying ill give you that. So overall there is no char progress at all, no loadout-ing at all in this title. If you combine that with the randomly generated maps and dungeons, which also get repetetive quickly because few assets, the entire thing makes you lose interest quite fast. Literally the only reason that i kept playing for was because the combat was so good and i still had a bit of hopes up that something would change and i could actually get my hands on new gear. Hell even the trader in the hub only sells like 2 or 3 basic weapons and never restocks. Oh man..


Couldve been a really solid, really good game but i dont know for whatever weird reason devs decided to make you play through the game with kind of a single loadout. I definitely expected more from hearsay and ratings tbh. Disappointed..\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely loved this game, and can't believe that I waited this long to play it. RFTA has a lot of things that I love, it has some clear souls-like inspirations, like the ability to rest at check points that will replenish your resources at the cost of re-spawning lesser enemies, but it also has things that make this game its own thing. Combat can be melee or ranged with both working pretty well, the ammo for weapons is not unlimited as you can run out of ammo mid combat, but also not extremely restrictive. This game also has a lot of nice secrets for you to find while exploring, as well as meaningful loot from its really fun, unique boss fights that usually turn into new weapons or mods. Personally, the thing that I loved the most was its world. This game is not an open world, but it for sure rewards the player for their exploration, and that was something I really appreciated as I am a bit tired of huge, open maps. The story pushes you in a certain direction and there isn't much deviating from it, but when you do you usually run into a boss room. I am glad that I jumped into this game, and it has certainly sold me on its sequel. Worth the full price, even more so on sale!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed this game in spite of its many flaws. Despite its foolish and predatory RPG mechanic of always making enemies scale slightly higher than your gear score I was able to use brutal cunning and the power of google to reason that the crossbow which fires 5 bolts is pretty deece when paired with the completely overpowered starting set for hunters (reduces spread, increases ranged + weakpoint damage), and more importantly it is very funny to impale your enemies' skulls and torsos with sharpened fence posts. Somehow a weapon the size of a light crossbow is able to fire these. I am amused.

The game's bosses (except for the Subject DLC) are awash with artificial difficulty (the antichrist of soulslike games), for example random unavoidable roars which can and will stun you into exploding adds, or bosses completely lacking weak points or who may stand afar of the arena in order to hilariously invalidate any weapon that isn't the assault, laser or hunting rifles, the first of which you will never acquire. The developers laugh hysterically at those who bring shotguns and melee builds, which they put into the game as a trap, and you fell for it, you fool, you utter buffoon. The Subject blahdeblah DLC's bosses are more fairly designed but also ridiculously much easier with several of them spending too much time setting up Dance Dance Revolution positioning mechanics and forget that they could clean you up in 5 seconds with a few lazy melee swings. Still pretty fun though.

One less important feature which I nevertheless find interesting is how the game still puts a machine pistol into your hand at the start of one of the boss fights despite the devs being perfectly aware that nobody has ever enjoyed this 'feature' in the last several years. To their credit they repeated this supposed foolishness with great deliberation in the Subject DLC. I intuitively understand this to be a purposeful troll in order to make the thousands of peasants who play their game confused and incredibly angry and so I am therefore 100% behind this impressive work of tomfoolery and wish them luck in future japes. Capable pranksters are they.

If you are wondering how all of this this criticism (or is it?) adds up to an enjoyable game, it may have something to do with having modded the game for double ammo and a first person viewpoint. The first change makes it so that you don't have to ration out your ammo between two weapons which ideally have applications for different situations (I mostly used long range crossbow for long range + dps and the Defiler for personal defence and burst weak point damage), which is a boring and stupid thing to have to do. The second transforms the game into one where situational awareness is necessary rather than automatic, due to no longer having a clownishly large over-the-shoulder POV (which works against you in tight quarters or when fighting large bosses you have to look up at i.e. most of them), and where sound cues are more important - and therefore you actually listen to the game's impressive sound design - and I find it to be much better that way, especially when the sound of launching a dark souls great arrow out of a pistol crossbow triggers right next to your ear. If I'm being honest that probably made up a disproportionate portion of my enjoyment, so your mileage may vary slightly. Uhh... while I'm at it, not all of this game's mods actually explain whether to put their .PAKs in the PAK folder or a ~mods folder in the .paks folder, so have fun figuring that out through trial and error.

That's about it. Enjoy! Or don't.\t\t\t\t\t
