completed it on PS5 and PC (100% trophy completion on PS5)
Tons of replayability through the campaign/adventure mode/achievement or gear hunting,
Great moment to moment gameplay both single player and co-op.
The co-op play is brilliant, and there's just something about it which feels great when you join someone else's session and help them progress or when someone joins you in a public game to help you progress in your campaign or objective. A game that you can just switch on and jump right in, and play for 30mins at a time or 3+ hours and it feels great either way.
The biomes are beautiful. 3rd person gunplay is on point. Bosses, Randomisation and Mob density is great.
A very underated game overall, that not many seem to be aware of.
Can't wait for Remnant 2! Releasing July 2023

If you're interested in the game enough to read this review, get it! (campaign first playthrough takes anywhere between 10-15 hours, I stopped, came back to the game and realised how much more there is you can do, collect, and play in different ways, so I then went for 100% completion due to how fun it was).

Technical summary console vs PC:
- PS5 version runs nicely, but you can't turn off things like motion blur and you get much lower FPS than the PC version (standard for all games). I assume similar will exist for Rem 2, hopefully the console players are given more vid options.
- I bought this game on the PC, to test out the vid quality, PC controls, gameplay and co-op play in prep to decide what platform to buy REM 2 on. It is definitely a much better experience on PC for me personally. I'll be getting Remnant 2 on PC. Personal preference due to playing on PC for years now, used to be a console gamer back in playstation/xbox 360 era but I struggle to play on console these days due to lower FPS and controller use vs what I'm now used to with a mouse and keyboard. It is a great game to sit back on the couch and play though...I just can't play console games for long stretches these days due to low FPS and camera movement giving me vertigo. Whereas, I don't feel these effects from PC gaming.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn interesting take on Dark Souls formula ...now with guns!
It was pretty refreshing. Barebones concept appeared to be functional, however game really lacks some "meat" on the bones. Locations feel pretty generic, monsters feel same. Some guns are WAY better than the others (which leaves almost no choice from efficiency perspective). Difficulty of bosses is all over the place. Replayability is almost absent.
Lore was surprisingly interesting (especially some diaries), however story was pretty weak (although it's OK for such type of games IMO).

Hoping that sequel will UP level and enemy designs, add more guns (that actually differ).

I got it with a great discount (~5$), and for this price it was just great. Not the best game, but a pretty solid foundation that can be built on. Recommend for all DS-style game fans that are OK with some experiments on the well-established formula.\t\t\t\t\t
