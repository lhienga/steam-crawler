Remnant can easily be summarized by "Man, this is really good and really hard. I wish [X] was better designed, though."

There aren't things like classes. You pick a gear loadout in the begging of armor/gun/melee/weaponmod which can easily be bought early as well. This makes a pretty linear experience especially noticeable in multiplayer as there isn't much reason to use much different equipment. There are "traits" you buy as you level which increasing random stats but they are essentially universal with maybe less than a handful of reasonable methods of what to buy into. Range Damage fall off feels a bit more punishing than it should be and the fact there's an item to enhance this seems weird. There's also a variety of items, guns, armor that can easily be be categorized between "cool" and "not useful at all". I could go on, but these are all things worth looking passed as the actual game is very enjoyable and the first playthrough is like no other. There's also a sequel and it looks and I HOPE it remedies all the issues I've personally had with the game.

Also, I hope bosses are better designed than they were in this game. I warn you now, almost ALL bosses except a few are designed to be difficult solely by flooding you with mobs in some kind of way. For the fights themselves as well as the game as a whole being a majority about grand boss fights was probably the most hurtful aspects as throwing droves of enemy types at you while you whittle health bars is not only unfun, but there is a major imbalance of difficulty as some FIRST LEVEL bosses are dramatically harder than some of the end stage bosses.\t\t\t\t\t

Shooting Adds Corridor Sim

If you don't have a decent story with interesting characters and "bear"able storytelling, why bother? Just do it like FromSoftware! (Even though that's because of Miyazaki's childhood trauma but you get what I mean). Don't waste the player's time because Remnant's story certainly didn't help the rest of the torturous experience.

If we get right to it, Remnant has a horrible level design with repetitive dungeons with the same layouts. The gameplay is so bland and nothing new, and it certainly doesn't feel like a souls-like, it feels and plays like an ordinary TPS. It's a shame since the mechanics actually work okay.

Exploration is really weak too, since enemies and environments have generic and unimaginary designs. Boss battles are disgustingly bad, since all of them are dealing with little annoying mobs while fighting the boss. That's Remnant's way of making the player struggle, since, without them, the bosses themselves are, in fact, really easy. I guess they need to learn how to design bosses that are legitimately tough but fair.

This game made me refund my Remnant II pre-order. Even though I was going to play it with my friends this summer. That's how flawed this game is. I wish they focused more on the experience itself rather than focusing on how to make this game longer and longer with looter shooter concepts with absurd level scaling. Such a waste of time and effort. I hope they can fix the issues on their second try (there are so many more, I haven't even mentioned here, so I seriously doubt it)
