\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant: From the Ashes is an exceptional game that seamlessly blends elements of third-person shooting, intense combat, and challenging cooperative gameplay. With its unique blend of post-apocalyptic world-building, deep character customization, and dynamic gameplay, it delivers an exhilarating experience that will keep you engaged from the first bullet fired to the final boss encounter.

The game's atmospheric setting immediately draws you in. As a survivor in a shattered world overrun by menacing creatures known as the Root, you embark on a perilous journey to restore balance and uncover the mysteries of this dark realm. The world-building is masterfully executed, presenting a unique and captivating post-apocalyptic landscape that begs to be explored. The game's attention to detail, from the intricate level design to the haunting ambiance, adds depth and immersion to the overall experience.

The combat in Remnant: From the Ashes is where the game truly shines. With its tight controls, satisfying gunplay, and intense boss battles, every encounter feels exhilarating. The game offers a diverse arsenal of weapons, each with its own distinct feel and playstyle, allowing you to tailor your approach to combat. The challenge is substantial, requiring quick reflexes, strategic decision-making, and teamwork if you choose to embark on the cooperative adventure with friends or online players.

The dynamic nature of Remnant's gameplay is a standout feature. The game's world is procedurally generated, ensuring that no two playthroughs are the same. This not only increases replayability but also keeps you on your toes as you encounter unexpected events, randomized enemy encounters, and hidden areas. The game's generous loot system rewards exploration, providing an abundance of weapons, armor, and enhancements to discover and upgrade, further enhancing your character's abilities.

Character customization is a key aspect of Remnant, allowing you to create a unique playstyle that suits your preferences. With a robust selection of traits, armor sets, and weapon modifications, you can tailor your character's abilities and adapt to different combat situations. The progression system offers a satisfying sense of growth and improvement, as you gain experience and unlock new skills and upgrades.

Visually, Remnant: From the Ashes is a visual treat. The game features detailed environments, stunning particle effects, and atmospheric lighting that add depth and immersion to the world. The hauntingly beautiful art design, combined with a superbly crafted audio landscape, sets the perfect tone for the game's post-apocalyptic atmosphere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant: From the Ashes promises an exhilarating adventure through a post-apocalyptic world filled with challenging enemies and endless surprises. Unfortunately, this game falls far short of its lofty ambitions, leaving players frustrated, bored, and longing for a better experience.

One of the biggest issues with Remnant is its lackluster storytelling. The narrative feels disjointed and uninspired, failing to engage players on any meaningful level. The characters are forgettable, their dialogue uninteresting, and the world-building is shallow. It's as if the developers simply threw together a mishmash of tired post-apocalyptic tropes without bothering to add any depth or originality.

The gameplay mechanics in Remnant are equally disappointing. While it tries to combine elements of third-person shooting, action RPG, and survival gameplay, it fails to excel in any of these areas. The combat lacks impact and finesse, with clunky controls that often leave you feeling disconnected from the action. Enemy encounters quickly become repetitive and predictable, making every battle a dull slog.

Furthermore, the game's progression system is poorly implemented. The RNG loot system is frustrating and unfair, often leaving players with underpowered weapons and gear for extended periods. The reliance on luck rather than skill undermines any sense of accomplishment or meaningful character growth. It feels like an artificial way to extend the game's lifespan, but instead, it only serves to drain the enjoyment from the experience.

The technical aspects of Remnant are riddled with flaws as well. The graphics are unimpressive, lacking the polish and attention to detail that one would expect from a modern title. Frequent frame rate drops and glitches further detract from the overall experience, causing unnecessary frustration and immersion-breaking moments.

Ultimately, Remnant: From the Ashes is a disappointing and forgettable game. It fails to deliver on its promises of an immersive post-apocalyptic adventure, offering instead a lackluster narrative, uninspired gameplay, and technical issues that hinder any enjoyment. There are far better options available in the genre, and I would strongly advise against wasting your time and money on this lackluster title.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA wildly short summary would be Diablo/Dark Souls with guns; or from what little I can remember of it, Hellgate: London with the ranged class.

Overall, it's pretty fun, all gear can be upgraded, dungeons are randomly seeded (so I'm currently stuck looking for one more glowing stick to complete an armor set).

If playing with a controller, the aim assist is pretty much spot-on.

The enemy numbers and AI seem more balanced around multiple players and it can be really annoying with caster types able to create explosions you have to dodge from outside line of sight and some of the bosses can be a right pain since they were really meant to be able to split focus between them and adds on multiple players.

Not impossible, but you'll probably have a few "Really?" moments when playing the game.

The character tends to get hung up on the edge of corners for some reason so be aware if you are closely kiting enemies.

Overall, not exactly my cup of tea, but I'm sure there are others who would really enjoy this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNearly flawless attempt at creating a Third Person Shooter Souls-Like. The game is not perfect, but it is the most perfect version of this style game I have played. Surprised that there are not more games that have borrowed from the souls-like genre to use in first/third person shooters. This game is strong, and I am super excited to see how Gunfire Games improves this formula in Remnant 2. This game is even more fun in a group. It can be punishing, and a bit grindy if you are looking for specific loot, but other than these minor issues, I love this game. I have been playing games on Steam since Left 4 Dead first dropped, and this is my first review on Steam. That is how much I like this game!\t\t\t\t\t
