\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing through and 100%'ing Remnant 2, I decided to revisit the first game and grind out the last achievements I had for it. It was a bit more grindy than I would of wanted, especially the 10 waves on survival and 40 cosmetic ones, but I eventually got it and feel I can now write my review for the game.

I think this game is stellar, I still remember getting it free on the Epic launcher and thinking "why not" only to easily dump 100+ hours into the game. I hadn't even played a Darksouls game before so when my friend told me it was a "souls-like" I had no idea what he meant, but I quickly found out as I almost rage quit on my first boss. I definitely think this is the perfect game to kick start someones souls-like career since it has the punishing aspects of one...but with guns, allowing for someone to deal consistent damage from afar and avoid needing to be up close and personal with the enemies.

I will say though that about half the boss fights weren't as fun as they could've been, in my opinion, since they spammed you with adds on top of the boss attacking you. This meant you needed to try to dispose of the adds as quickly as possible (who can be pretty beefy on higher difficulties) or else try to somehow avoid a half a dozen attacks at once. I am very glad that they toned back the add spam in Remnant 2, as well as made the game less grindy. My only other complaint is that the difficulty scaling is quite sever, going from 165% HP and Damage on hard to 300% on nightmare was way too big of a jump to make me realistically play above hard until I painstakingly maxed most of my offensive and defensive traits. I feel like a difficulty between hard and nightmare is needed for those that want slightly more of a challenge than hard but don't want to be 2 shot on nightmare. Sadly this is also an issue in Remnant 2, but atleast in Remnant 2 I feel like the game is more forgiving both grind-wise and build experimentation/variety wise.

Overall I feel like this game was a hidden gem, and although I am glad it got the recognition it needed for a second game (which I personally enjoy more) I think the first game is still good enough to stand out on its own and is definitely worth a try.\t\t\t\t\t
