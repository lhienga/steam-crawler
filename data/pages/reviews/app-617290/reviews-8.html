\r\n\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOur group of friends were really excited about this, but we ran into random disconnects during maps so frequently that we could barely get into the first map after the tutorial area. And, in this game, if someone disconnects and then reconnects, you need to go to a checkpoint for them to be able to join again. The problem with this is we had to keep backtracking to the beginning of the map each time. Unfortunately, this made the game unplayable. It seems to be a relatively frequent complaint by people from looking online. You would not expect this to happen, especially given the game uses P2P (peer-to-peer) connectivity (so, no servers), but we have played a LOT of P2P games over the last decade and have almost no disconnects ever (like, maybe 1 or 2 times a year?). We were even having disconnects while connecting via LAN. Our computers are extremely high end, so it isn't a resource issue (64 GB of RAM, 7800X3D and RTX 4090).

Single player could still definitely be an option, but that wasn't really something any of us were interested in. If you only care about single player, or are okay gambling that multiplayer works well for you (it apparently works well for some people), then I would otherwise recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisappointment. This is what I retain from Remnant: From the Ashes.

Coming from the Dark Souls series and hearing here and there that Remnant was a souls like but with FPS, I bought the game with hopes high and fell from high.

Bosses are uninteresting, their difficulty only comes from the tremendously high amount of ads they spawn. Areas repeat themselves to the point where you recognise patterns and places, and can even tell that you are at the end of a dungeon just because it's the same tileset repeating.

Had multiple bugs too with immediate death or a restart of the game being required.

Also, after trying here and there weapons I found, I ended up doing the entire game with the starting weapon of the class I chose because nothing really... fit. Boss weapons felt meh, forced to have a mod as well which makes custom mod that you get utterly useless.

Anyway, the game it self isn't that bad, it's just disappointing for me, and not very enjoyable in the end because of ad quantity spawning, as well as uninteresting boss fights (especially last boss which was a HUGE letdown).

I did the entire game in coop with a friend and we had a hard time just JOINING each other. Session wasn't found or appearing, had to restart the game more than once or going back to main menu, it once took us almost half an hour just to be able to play together.

Note: I DON'T own the DLCs, which apparently add quite a few weapons and armours on top of the base game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun shooter, could be summed up as Dark Souls, but with a focus on guns.
Gameplay is fun for the most part, but I really do not think that the game utilises difficulty settings correctly. All they seem to do is inflate enemy damage and health numbers, to the point that even when not on the hardest difficulty you'll be cut down in just a couple of hits, even if you're going for a tank build.
The way the world can be randomized for repeated runs of different worlds is an interesting idea and works well enough, but when you need that one item it can really feel as if you're just running through the world. This could've been implemented better if there was some sort of system that told you the items that you could find for the run, so you can at least know it's there and enjoy the journey a bit more.
Overall, the challenge and gameplay was fun and exciting enough for me to leave satisfied. Though I do hope that the sequel improves some of the issues I did have with this game.\t\t\t\t\t
