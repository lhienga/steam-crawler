\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to preface that this is an incredibly fun game with a lot of innovative ideas and that I will continue to play this game. There is no way to give a "Neutral" review at this point, so I had to be extremely nitpicky and ultimately decided I would not recommend it even though I personally enjoy it. While there are a lot of things the game does well, there are ultimately 2 fuhdamental issues that really hold this back from being an incredible experience.

Let's start with the good:
- Graphics are fine, not outstanding but certainly adequate. The World Design however, is gorgeous.
- Controls are generally responsive, for the most part and animations seem adequate as well.
- Gunplay is satisfying and Weapons (especially Boss Weapons and Mods) are extremely diverse. Hundreds of fun and effective builds to try (though saved loadouts would make this even better!)
- Seamless Co-op Experience is always an objective plus, but the solo experience is (mostly) fine too!
- General Gameplay Loop, Killing Mobs/Elites, Exploring for Loot and Searching out / Killing Bosses is all fun. Adventure Mode is a great way to add replayability to areas you've already explored in campaign without having to re-roll your entire campaign just to hopefully get the combo of loot drops you wanted.

Now, let's talk about the headaches: First and most irritating is Boss Design. I will give credit that many of the bosses pose unique alternative kill prompts to unlock different Loot. This is a very cool idea, sometimes it is executed very meaningfully (such as destroying a body part of a boss prior to killing it) and sometimes it's barely thought about or implemented at all (one boss you literally flick a switch to determine which loot you get). Almost every boss in this game is composed of a handful of attacks, a phase of summoning mobs/elites to swarm you, some more attacking, rinse and repeat. Bosses typically will have some sort of super move or equivalent that does extremely high damage and can either instantly kill you (especially on higher difficulties) or a flurry of attacks that stunlocks you to death due to the swarming mobs they call in. In general, Boss Fights in this game left me extremely unsatisfied, both because of how repetitive they were and how little diversity there is among them, but also because more often than not, deaths don't really teach you anything other than if you get hit by that attack it's an instant death or to manage the swarming mobs better. This was really disappointing because I felt like there was a lot of diversity among regular enemies between worlds, but the boss design really felt like the same boss that pounds on you, then runs away to summon ads to swarm you with a different core mechanic thrown on top. Certain bosses that possess these nasty, one-hit-kill-even-with-maxed-traits-moves honestly didn't even feel fun and left me hoping they'd just be over. All in all, the gameplay experience is great, but the boss fights are low in quality for a Soulslike.

Lack of Content/map diversity: While there is an overabundance of equipment and content you will need to grind for, realistically, there are 5 zones in the game and between 7 and 10 bosses per zone, many of which are elites reskinned as bosses. That's it. While it will take you ages to grind out every piece of equipment, every armour set, every ring and trinket, etc. It's not going to be super satisfying because you'll be repeating the same map layout every time in adventure mode with 3 or 4 of the 10 dungeons/interactions available to the map. The RNG for which events show up is so unforgiving that the commonly accepted meta is to use a save editor to scan your game for events and keep resetting until you have the desired event. This grind started of fun in adventure mode, but quickly becomes a slog when you have collected about 90% of everything and you're waiting on that last weapon, last ring, last questline, etc. to spawn and it never does. This could be really bad luck as well, but almost all of my map generations look the exact same and have extremely similar layouts.

These two issues ultimately lead to a middling experience in moments that should be the climaxes of the game realistically, as well as a general slog through the core gameplay loop as you try to farm out gear and mods and items in an extremely repetitive map generation with extremely unforgiving RNG for random events. It's really a shame because this game is EXTREMELY fun and I thoroughly enjoy it in the overwhelming majority of situations, particularly the open area fights and exploration, whether solo or cooperative . . . except when I do not.\t\t\t\t\t
