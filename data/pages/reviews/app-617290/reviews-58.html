\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[Being "nearly 70" I only do these reviews because I play so many games I forget whether I liked them or not, and I end up re-starting an old game that I really shouldn't.]
So, for my future self (should I be spared, and for anyone who happens to be passing by) this game is quite fun, with a reasonable progression through the first part. The last time I played this I really didn't understand the mechanic, or the nature of the grind, so I cheated by using an invulnerability mod and played all the way to the end.
There's a campaign mode, and an adventure mode. The adventure mode is optional, except that it isn't really.
The adventure mode is a great place to grind, so that you can get all the goodies that you didn't get in the campaign. The campaign is random, and you can replay sections, but if you don't get an item that you know should drop in that level, then you go into adventure mode and grind until you get it.
BUT the grind will also give you lots of currency (scrap & iron) which you can use to upgrade your gear. The only problem with that is that levels are scaled to your gear. That's right, as you enter each new level the game reads your gear, does a calculation and sets the difficulty level. So, levelling your gear early will make the current level easier, but the subsequent level harder, and since you cannot sell or otherwise get rid of your gear even gear you're not using is going to count against you.
Ah, then why not keep the same early gear so that the game remains at the same difficulty level? Good idea superhero in the rubber suit, but then you have bosses. Of course you do!
Bosses. I. Hate. Bosses. Especially these ones, some of which are nearly impossible unless you play in co-op. To beat the bosses, guess what you need to do? Get tougher! How do we get tougher? Upgrade our gear!
Admittedly, there are "traits", which affect your physical abilities, and these are not used for level scaling, but it will still take you an age to get tough enough and traited enough to deal with any particular boss.
And these bosses are really, really unrealistic. Hide behind a pillar and see the boss dead-drop you from the other side, ignoring all those pesky bricks and concrete. Stay at what looks like a safe distance and be amazed as the boss's reach is seemingly invisibly extended beyond its corporeal body. Run away from the boss and go all stealthy and stuff, only to marvel at the ability of the boss to spawn additional enemies that act like homing missiles, heading straight to a location they cannot see!
Is it all worth it in the end?
No, not really. The problem is that there are no big "souls like" rewards, no magical vistas or amazing locations, just more grinding, which means lots of the same thing, which would be OK if the game was like Borderlands, where grinding is fun and colourful, and you get to loot like a woke New Yorker. But this is just more of what you're desperately trying to leave behind you.
So, it's a "no" from me on this one.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game does have potential, it rewards exploration and has fun monster mechanics.
The only big thing that made me write this negative review is how unresponsive the controls are in combat.
For some reason the developers thought that shooting a weapon on lmb only while holding rclick, and using your melee otherwise, is a good idea. Well maybe it would be, if it were more responsive. Very often i click rmb to use my ranged weapon, and the character just stands there doing nothing. Even worse, if i trust the controls and after holding rmb i click lmb, the character would just melee and not switch to ranged weapons. Same goes for melee. You dodge you opponent's attack, and want to immediately punish, mash that lmb, but your guy just stands there looking at the mob, missing the window of opportunity.
This game wants you to study enemy attack patterns, especially considering it has a melee archetype. But then it simply screws you over with these amazingly numb controls. It is extremely frustrating.

P.S.: Not playing multiplayer, only solo, so not a problem with my internet connection (which is a stable 100Mb). KB and mouse are not wireless.\t\t\t\t\t
