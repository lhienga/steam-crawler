\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant is a fantastic AA game, it is the definition of a hidden gem and a true work of passion. It has some set backs sure but hopefully any issues this game had will be remedied in the sequel.

In preparation for said sequel I've been replaying this game in anticipation and I'm so happy to say it still holds up.

So what is remnant? Well it's like if dark souls and resident evil 4 had a baby, sounds like recipe for disaster but not only does it work it works well.

Pros
- fantastic gunplay, this game feels good to play with weapons that feel punchy and control well. This is one of the most important things you need to nail in a 3rd person shooter and I'm happy to say this game delivers.

- weapon and armour variety. We have lots of cool guns and sets to play with and whilst most meta builds demand certain guns and sets the amount we do get are staggering and inventive.

- decent build variety, it's fun to make builds in this game and you can make some pretty powerful stuff. The only issue being you will see the same meta builds endgame and this does weaken build options. Thankfully it seems this has been fixed in the sequel.

- varied biomes which have randomly generated events and quests which can be re-rolled for more replayability. The game is quite short and you can finish it in about 25 hours on your first run but you'd be doing yourself a disservice if you don't re-roll the campaign a few times or mess about in adventure mode, the story is short but the gameplay is long.

- lore, it's not great and a bit cheesy compared to dark souls but it still invites mystery and it is fun to unravel.

- really good dlc offerings and post game support

Cons
- builds are determined by armour sets as skills are tied to sets and the optimal choice is to stack critical damage meaning you will see lots of people running the same sets. Look it might seem silly but not being able to express yourself through fashion is quite a detriment to me. Seeing all these cool sets and knowing I won't use them because the skills they have suck ass is a massive shame, thank god this has been fixed in the sequel.

- graphics and voice acting aren't as polished as you'd expect. They certainly aren't bad but some npcs don't sound amazing and the small amount of voice lines your character has really get old quick, this isn't an AAA game so it's completely understandable but it is something that bothered me a bit

- no hip fire, this isn't in the sequel either and I'm a bit annoyed about that. I really feel it would improve the game but I suppose the devs don't agree, fair enough I'm not a dev and I don't know if it would be op in a game like this but I do miss it's absence.

Conclusion
I really love this game and if you enjoy souls likes, 3rd person shooters or just looters in general then this game is an easy recommend. Is it perfect? No, but it's pretty damn close.

Now bring on the sequel.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game back when it came out on the hardest difficulty available at the time. I got to the final boss and after dying once I stopped playing and never finished it.

Why didn't I try again?

Because I spent the entire game fighting easy bosses that had their HP padded with never ending waves of enemies spawning in to back them up to make up for how on a fundamental level they're poorly designed bosses. It was a tedious game to play all the way through and the final boss was the most tedious one by far. I was sick of the game at the end and the thought of playing any longer no longer appealed to me, so I stopped and uninstalled the game.

I came back to play it in the lead up to Remnant 2, hoping that after years of balance changes the game would be less a slog. But no, it's actually worse now somehow. Or maybe my tolerance for ill thought out design has just lessened.

My advice? If you buy the game don't play it solo, or don't play the harder difficulties. They're doable, you have all the tools to beat the harder difficulties solo. But you will not have fun.

I can see a decent game in there. The controls work well, the guns are nice. It feels like they managed to pull off a double A game on a single A budget. But their commitment to making the enemies unbearably tanky and spawning in endless adds during boss fights makes it real hard to recommend.

Every boss fight I wished the boss itself was more aggressive and dangerous in exchange for not having to deal with the adds. Most of the time the bosses hang around doing the most basic easy to dodge attacks imaginable, they'd be absolutely no threat at all if not for the enemies spawning in constantly. It's like the devs made the bosses, realised they suck and even if they one hit you they're no threat so decided rather than make them more engaging, they could bandaid it with minions instead. So you spend 95 percent of the boss fight dispatching weaklings to buy you breathing room to shoot the boss.

I hope they change tack for Remnant 2.\t\t\t\t\t
