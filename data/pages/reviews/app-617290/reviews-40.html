\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review has been a long time coming. The short version will be first, the longer will be the bottom half for those interested.
For context; I played solo, no co-op experience, and with a controller.

TL;DR
I personally love this game; from it's story and lore entries scattered around the levels, three different main modes of play (campaign, adventure and survival (DLC)), to the satisfying gunplay and combat that are complimented by a solid customization mechanic revolving around numerous weapon mods and a linear upgrade system to give a sense of progression. Traits play a huge role in the combat and gameplay, giving the player the choice of which to upgrade without missing out because once you unlock a trait, you have the minimum of bonus it can give.
Great controls, several weapons and armors to choose from, all with their own benefits and drawbacks. Break things to get more scrap (currency) and materials for upgrades.
Unfortunately, as a solo player, some bosses and encounters can feel unfair and very difficult, like bosses with endless waves of little guys that chip away at your health, making the main boss almost seem like the side piece. That paired with a short story (minus the re-rolling of campaign and adventure mode) can leave one wanting more. Despite it's shortcomings, I find myself coming back to it.
To avoid a decimal rating, I give Remnant: From the Ashes a solid
7/10

Longer Review (minor spoilers)

Story
Without spoiling anything major, the story focuses on a traveler from an island who is tasked with finding a fellow member of the village who set out on a quest to defeat a Dragon (Chronos: Before the Ashes tells his story. Very different game, but good lore tie in). This Dragon is actually the main antagonist; The Root, and it's your quest as the player to destroy The Root and save the world. Still without spoilers, you find Ward 13 and meet the lovely people that reside there. Then your quest really starts.

I really like the story, the writing is great (mostly in the lore journals found throughout the levels) and the world really feels like it's on the brink of extinction. People are scared, hope is hard to find and The Root is everywhere. The story kind of falls flat when interacting with people in the Ward as the game continues, no one really mentions what you've done (except for the last thing on the first map) and post final boss, people say the same things, but the merchants (Mud Tooth and Wud being my favorites) offer more insight into their respective worlds, which is nice. Interacting with the Ward folks though, bears little in the way of lore, and the main story is pretty much all you get. For more lore, be sure to read terminals and journals, all very well written and help add context to the narrative.

Combat

Folks call it a souls-like with guns, and I can see it ( but you lose nothing when you die. Enemies respawn, but you keep all currency, no run back ). You got melee (some variation in there makes it fun to try new weapons), stamina consuming dodging/sprinting (melee doesn't consume stamina, but halts it's regeneration while swinging), and guns, some of which don't have much reserve ammo, and there's no way to increase how much ammo you can have on hand. Guns are the better option for primary engagement, but be mindful of ammo. Some enemies drop ammo (long gun and side arm, so no weapon specific ammo), but ammo boxes will be your friend in the later portions of the game.
Gunplay can be broken down into weak spots and critical hits doing the most damage, while body shots and hitting armor do the least. Melee is pretty straight forward, just be aware, special enemies, as their known (bigger and accompanied by a music cue) rarely stagger when hit with melee, they will hit back and hit back hard. Thankfully, most smaller common enemies will stagger and can be dealt with using melee, saving ammo for the special guys.
Combat can get repetitive, depending on the boss or enemy type, but I personally find it satisfying to land a critical and see the enemy drop. Be wary of groups of enemies, especially in tight spaces and as a solo player, this can be maddening and lead to death.

Bosses (Minor Spoilers)

Like any souls-like, the bosses are often the highlight and this game has it's fair share. The bosses in the first area are good, fun and well thought out. From arenas to movesets, I found myself able to beat most of them after a try or two. But that's not to say they're all good.
*Spoilers (and minor rant)*
Oh my god. The Harrowing on Rhom (second area) is not fun, at all, I hate it. He has an instant kill move, endless runts spawning, a horrible arena and does that "retreat and reappear to ruin your day" crap. This isn't a boss fight, this is padding.
Then, on Reisum (DLC), these damned RATS spill out of the ceiling forever until you frantically rush to the spawn crystals and smash them. That's it, that's the boss fight, that's the whole fight. Yaesha (last area) did something similar in a "random event" in which if you look at the wall, no joke, endless undead will vomit forth, becoming increasingly more powerful, until you destroy the even stronger crystals.
Seriously, listen, if you look at the floor the whole time (except looking up to break the crystals), and use your map as navigation, the undead won't spawn and you can win easy (thank you Wiki, bless you). But I couldn't find the hint that told me that, so I was frustrated for two hours before finally finishing it.

So, aside from some bosses better off being played with another person, they felt good to fight and very satisfying to take down. Kind of a bummer, but you can't skip boss introduction scenes. You only see it once when you fight them, so it's not like you see it several times while dying/trying again, but if you do adventure mode or re-roll campaign, you've already seen the introduction but can't skip it because it's like you're encountering them for the first time, if that makes sense.

General Atmosphere

Graphics, sound, level design, music (Ward 13 and menu tracks hit nicely), all give off the post-apocalyptic vibes you'd expect. Eerie when needed and some actually gorgeous locals, all of which have items you can break for more scarp or items, but Rhom has too many, some breakable pots being on shelves that you have to waste ammo to get to, and even then the scrap isn't guaranteed.

Final Thoughts

I really like this game and the story it tells. I like the combat loop, the looting without weight limit, the traits system, adventure mode, and even survival, to an extent (even though the timer that dictates enemy strength ticks down as you're waiting to return to the main hub, meaning you lose around 12 seconds just getting back from a run). Aside from some things holding it back, and how much more fun it probably is with another person, I still find this to be a great solo experience worth giving a try.
