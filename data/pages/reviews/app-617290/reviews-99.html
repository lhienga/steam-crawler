\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game as a Souls-like with guns sounds like it's right up my alley. I tried the game on Apocalyse which was my own fault. I of course eventually hit a wall hard, and had to restart as I had no idea what I was doing and the difficulty wasn't balanced for a first time player.

It was at this point that I discovered Remnant doesn't have hand-crafted levels but a weird procedural system. Before long I could start seeing the seams of this design everywhere. The areas I repeated, although in theory different levels, had the same building blocks, a side street snapped here when it was there, an exit through a small shop on the left-hand side moved to the right, but it was still the same building, the same shop, the same exit.

But I was sticking with it, the game controls very well, switching weapons is quick and snappy, and the Hunter character I was playing as was fun for the most part...although I was starting to hate the sheer quantity of enemies I was facing.

I then hit my first boss, a sniper who teleported around an area with its own towers trying to get behind me. Perfect, just what I wanted! But no, the fun of a sniper duel was ripped instantly away from me as the game starts spawning infinite regular enemies, about 10 at a time, all sprinting towards me, all causing status ailments; a toxic mist that eventually causes your character to cough intermittently interrupting what you're doing, and a bleed effect that constantly drains your HP...and these statuses don't disappear until you die or rest at a bonfire.

But I grinned and beared it, got past the fight eventually, moved on to the next area and although it was slightly different with some living trees and new enemies with guns, the buildings all looked the exact same and I was getting sick of it.

I hit another boss, the same big enemy with a sword that caused me issues on Apocalypse, but now with the ability to summon 6 exploding one-hit kill enemies chasing you around leaving toxic mists.

Another sort-of boss (has a boss fog at least), defending a person stuck in a tree against an onslaught of the same enemies I've been fighting everywhere. Annoying. I get past this and I'm back in an underground train area that I'd already been in twice. I hated this area because they had so many enemies spawning in from seemingly nowhere but during the previous area I'd noticed some hanging from the ceilings and thought I was being stupid. But no, all a facade. Enemies do fall from the ceilings but they're much more likely just to be behind you because the game decided they were.

All those points I've been putting in to reducing enemy detection range to be sneaky? Utterly pointless since the game arbitrarily decides I'm in a fight. Some enemies just appear and start screaming, spawning more enemies that just chase you endlessly.

But I get past all this crap again and I hit my next boss, The standard tree enemy without a head where they split in two when you enter the room, and then into 4 after the first health bar, then into 8 after the second. During this each enemy decides to do either a heal (shoot twice to stop) or throwing a knife at you, or they'll do a pulsating growing full room AOE that you have to roll into. And they can all do it. There's times when you're trying to avoid this AOE from 4 different enemies whilst 2 of them need to be shot to stop the heal. This means timing your roll, keeping an eye out on the others throwing knives which can stun you causing you to get hit but the AOE, doing huge damage. It was at this point I realised that the healing item is godawful taking like 5 seconds to activate.

I'm not sure what I wanted to see here. There's very few bosses like this that rely on adds in Soulsbourne. I'm trying to think of anyone other than Nito (there must be more) but as an example there's ways around the adds, you can get him to hit them, or you can use a holy weapon or you can kite them whilst you run around the edge since they are melee and fairly slow. But even then, just throwing more enemies at you doesn't make a fight more exciting. Genichiro and Isshin are 10/10 duels that rely on mastering mechanics not dealing with .

But it's hard to have that when you're fighting trees with no personality, and your bosses are the exact same enemy models but with more HP and a bunch of friends. An utter disappointment of a game that I'm just sad I gave up after 6 hours instead of 2 or I'd get my money back.\t\t\t\t\t
