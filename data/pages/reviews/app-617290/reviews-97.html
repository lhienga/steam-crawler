\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely review games, but in this case I feel compelled to do so. It starts out really fun - the core combat flows well, all the weapons seem useful, and swapping between melee and your two guns feels very satisfying. So you start going through the first couple of areas. They look good, there's a nice amount of small-scale exploration for items and upgrade materials (though overall the design is pretty linear with some added side dungeons that put you back to the main path when you are done), the enemies are simple but fine, and hey, these are just the first areas. The red-and-grey color scheme overstays its welcome a bit but it's nothing too glaring.

But then you get to the second world and... it's basically the same. The color scheme has changed a bit, but most areas feel very much like something you've seen before. The overall structure barely changed. And the same goes for the enemies: Yeah, they look different, and they aren't exactly reskins, but the same attack patterns are recognizable: you've got your small enemy that rushes at you and attacks but dies in two shots, you've got your exploding enemy that rushes at you and explodes, you've got your run-of-the-mill enemy with a simple ranged attack, you've got your caster-type enemy that spawns hurty bubbles at your position, and so on. And these enemies are then thrown at you in the same group compositions, with the order of the day being "enemy spam". No enemy is interesting enough on its own, so they just spawn them in in groups of 5 to 10. Carefully exploring to pick off enemies is a no-go since they just spawn in behind you anyway.

In fact, this game is so enemy spammy that even the bosses are enemy spam. Or, more precisely, there are two types of bosses:
1. Total pushovers with nothing interesting to say about them (except that there's some wonky hurtboxes on some attacks).
2. Enemy spam.
I finally quit on a miniboss that was spawning 5+ flying ranged attackers to shoot at me from all angles (in a game that, for all the field of view you are getting, may as well be a 1st person shooter) and then just spam-vomited barely-dodgable fire. It simply wasn't fun. In a game like Dark Souls I would have persevered through such a fight because those games always keep you excited about what will come after. But I realized that I wasn't excited at all anymore about what this game had to offer, because it had already proven to be a bunch of same-y enemy spam. So I stopped.\t\t\t\t\t
