Remnant: From the Ashes is a really good Souls-like game that emphasizes co-op play!
Remnant: From the Ashes is basically Dark Souls, but with heavy focus on ranged, gun-based combat. There is close combat with melee weapons as well, but it is not very useful in every situation or boss fight.

The co-op in this game is amazing. It has a much better co-op system than Dark Souls. You can play up to 3 players in a party. If you plan on playing through the whole game with a couple of friends, you don’t have to replay every area and boss for the other player in their own world in order to progress and keep playing together, like you have to do in Dark Souls. You can do everything in the worlds of your friends that you can do in your own. Like upgrading your gear, rest at checkpoints together, fast travel, you name it. You even share all loot, so if your friend picks up gear and other stuff, you will also get it! You can stay in your friends game as long as you want to, you don’t get automatically thrown out after you have killed a boss together like in Dark Souls.

The environment is beautiful and varied. With the DLCs included, which I recommend you buy, there are 5 very different worlds in total. After you have completed the campaign/story, you have only killed about 1/3 of all the bosses, and I’ll tell you why soon. The story is very bland and uninteresting, so I did not care much about it.

This game does not have New Game+. Instead you can reroll your campaign with new areas and bosses. This will reroll every world in the campaign though. You can also play something called Adventure Mode. Here you can choose which world you want to reroll and play again, with randomized areas and bosses for that world. This way you can easier farm specific items, weapons or traits that you are looking for. You also get to choose which difficulty to play at when rerolling a world; Normal, Hard, Nightmare or Apocalypse.

A few things that I hope they will improve for Remnant 2 that bothered me a tiny bit is the map and the inventory. Every world consists of several areas. On the map you can only see the area that you are currently in. You can not do much with the map except seeing where you have been and where your friends are. If you open up the map between dungeons, and want to know which entrance is which and which exit is which, you can’t see that on the map. You have to go back into the dungeon in order to see which dungeon it is, which is a bit annoying. You can’t really interact much with the map. Your inventory is endless and can’t really become full. This can be a bit annoying as you don’t have a stash where you can put things you don’t need at the moment. Let’s say you want to change rings before a boss fight. You then have to scroll through the whole list of rings that you have before you find it and can equip it. Then after the boss fight you might want to change back to the ring you had before, you have to start scrolling again. Maybe you want to change your amulet too? Start scrolling. It’s just nice to have the option of putting things in a stash that you don’t really want to throw away, because you might wanna use it later on.

All and all, Remnant: From the Ashes is a fantastic game that I strongly recommend. Especially if you are looking for a game to play in co-op with some friends!\t\t\t\t\t
