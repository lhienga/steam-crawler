\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been a lot of effort, I know nobody asked but I finally managed to beat Remnant on apocalypse and felt like sharing with someone. After previously getting humbled on nightmare, I decided to play on normal to farm a few different guns and armor sets to max, or near max level. Then I decided to try apocalypse and see if I can at least finish Earth biome.

EARTH

It was very difficult, but I could see the difference when you have proper gear, full traits and weapons. Even Riphide, that made me give up on my previous attempt, was beatable once I acquired AoE guns. Shroud, Riphide and the Ent were defeated after many attempts (especially Ent, that had way too much unpredictable damage from many sources).

RHOM

Rhom is possibly my favorite world so I decided to try that out too, and somehow I got to the final boss of the area, after cheesing Shade and Shatter by inviting myself in their spirit igloo and donating bullets to their faces and millions of attempts on Scourge. I was really hoping not to get the Harrow, but sadly I did, and after many many attempts, I managed to block him from fleeing by keeping a dangerously close distance to him.

CORSUS

After Ezlan humbled me, I decided to comply with him and go to Corsus. The Unclean One seemed easier downstairs on paper, because of no ads, but I had trouble learning its one-hit-KO ground bash patterns, gave up on the long travel to that point, and tried upstairs. Very easy, to my surprise, probably the easiest fight in the game. If you dodge in areas without vases, you will get almost no ads, and once he starts spinning, he kills the ads too. Barbed Terror was not too bad either, seemed like I finally got lucky with a biome as it was by far the easiest dungeon boss. Not to mention I dodged the hellish dungeon that is like a cave with wooden structures. That one is hard on any mode! Then came my nightmare with Ixillis. Found it impossible to break their focus with two shotgun shots, so I decided to risk it all and wear the admiral's ring to boost that damage, since a hit from them would kill me anyway. All I had to do was to accurately shoot the wisps. Behold, after another way too many attempts, passed that. Decided to fight Ezlan anyway because I got a severe power trip, and managed to do it. It's a surprisingly squishy boss, as you can net a 25% on headshots from a hot shot sniper.

YAESHA

Then moved on to Yaesha. I was dreading the rebellion fight the most, as it gave me a lot of trouble on hard. The ranged dudes just chunk you too much. But I stayed upstairs, and the devastator put in a lot of work. Eventually, with the help of a very good boy, I managed to kill the elite trio that ends the dungeon. The next dungeon was another survival, in which turrets and my newly leveled up Ricochet gun really put in all the work while i was rolling like wild. The next Boss was Onslaught, and he actually had a pretty easy pattern to learn. Most of my deaths were from ads. After that, Stormcaller really humbled me a few times, it was a shockingly difficult match, as my multitasking isn't my best skill. But I gave Ricochet Gun a last chance to shine, and it did put in the work. I needed a bit of luck too, as the ads are doing way too much damage. Finally... Totem Father. I saw it as pretty easy on normal/hard, so I was initially happy. But On apocalypse, the ads are brutal, and the totems take a lot of shots to break. Combining 3xBleed and 3xBurn from Devastator and Spitfire helped me machinegun this boss down, and it took maybe the most attempts of all bosses.

Finaly, The Nightmare was harder than I initially thought, but it's really easy if you do it methodically. Just takes a lot of supplies to play it "safe", kill only 7-8 root in the nightmare realm, then move on. After 5-6 trips it's done. I don't like this boss to be honest and it is neither interesting nor hard, just grindy.

A special thanks to my summons who carried me through the dungeons, nothing stood out as too hard, except the temple dungeons in Rhom. Special thanks to Sniper Rifle, Devastator, Ricochet gun, and Spitfire, that did almost all the work on their own. And Slayer set slays, as they say.

After each boss battle, my heartrate was through the roof, and the feeling of accomplishment was something I almost never experience. It was both a humbling experience, a test on resilience and patience, and a learning road, as I started experimenting more builds, more weapons, and getting some help from the amazing discord community. And special thanks to Esoterikk on youtube for being a light of inspiration whenever I felt hopeless.

Sorry for the long post but this was an amazing journey. This game is now one of my top 3, and I have a way too big collection of games. I am not great at shooters, so if I could do it, anyone else probably can. Keep trying! And thanks for reading.\t\t\t\t\t
