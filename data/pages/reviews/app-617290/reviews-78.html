\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is probably, if not the best souls-like game there is. It has infinitely more polish than Dark Souls or any other game imitating dark souls. All around a very polished and fun to play game regardless of the difficulty you play it on. I've had an absolute blast playing this game, the bosses are well put together and whilst the story isn't out of this world - the gameplay will keep you hooked for many hours. This game is super addicting and the community behind the small but noble modding scene provide content even post-DLC completion. Survival mode is really the bread and butter of the longevity of this game, the main campaign and DLCs can be completed in a day if you were quick about it.

Overall, I wish they would continue to release more content for Remnant 1 instead of making a Remnant 2, but I understand they need their money and tbh, it's well deserved. I cannot fault this game on anything.

It also runs very well on the Steam Deck too, 60fps most of the time from an SD card. What more can I say? 10/10

Oh, finally... Check out Remnant from Hell, I highly recommend that mod.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant is a game with satisfying combat, and a gameplay loop that is good enough to get a decent amount of hours out of you. Probably worth it on sale.

Remnant also lacks a huge amount of identity. A lot of the gameplay systems here make sense mechanically, but the characters, the world, how these systems make sense in the world, most of it isn't exciting or original. I understand drawing inspiration and no doubt there is plenty of original content here but I just can't care enough to delve into it. The god awful dialogue and single personality trait characters don't exactly help reel me in either.

Very excited to see what they do for Remnant 2 very good bones here, but not really for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant is a game of many flaws and many strengths, which in the end comes on top by being its own unique thing.

The souls games comparison couldn't come close to what type of experience this game delivers. While it is a fact that the game does feature souls like mechanics all over the place, Souls games are well know for its 1V1 combat while being extremely flawed when trying to handle multiple enemies at once. For Remnant this design perspective is completely flipped, mostly intentional i would assume. Remnant shines the most when you're getting pressured by fighting multiple enemies at once. Individually most enemies can be dodged simply by side stepping and they easily get stunlocked and killed (playing on hard), but you'll find yourself being constantly overrun by enemies from all directions, some bossfights are almost puzzle like where you have to use the right tools you have to get a better outcome/easier time.

I heard before hand that the game had a bad build variety, but up to this moment i think pretty much the opposite about it, Remnant is not afraid of giving you huge bonuses for using certain pieces of equipment, nor really strong alt fire modes for special guns that cannot be customizable (and they feel reaaally good to use).

The biggest flaw that Remnant suffers from is the randomized loot that is locked to the seed you generated your world on. For an example, there is a Magnum in the game, only 38% of the players got the magnum, which is less than how many people have beat the game, the magnum can be found extremely early on in the first map, but the event it appears on is randomly generated, and even after multiple attempts of re rolling your seed you can just not get what you want. I understand that the system was made like this to encourage replayability, but even while replaying the campaign/adventure mode (shorter campaign) you can still be unlucky and get a very similar seed to the one you've already played, which is bad. The game would benefit way more for having "Sides", like campaign side "A" and "B" and "C" with respectively hard coded loot instead of randomized.

By the way, last but not least, if you're here to play coop with randoms let me tell you that there is no way to sort matches by ping, so it is very likely that you'll end up with an extremely lagged guy from the other side of the planet. To make it worse, your iframes and bullets work on Host side, this mean that you're relying on low ping to dodge and hit attacks, which is really really bad, making so a lot of times you have to dodge at the "wrong times" to evade an attack, and as you guessed, some attacks just wont be dodgeable.\t\t\t\t\t
