\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEveryone says it's "Dark Souls with Guns" (gunsouls) but I honestly don't think it's *only* that. The game does focus on challenging boss fights with interesting mechanics. Many of the bosses have adds that join the fight to distract you from the boss. This is seemingly fixed in the sequel and I love that they pivoted to focus on making the boss fights more immersive.

Where the game differs from Dark Souls by quite a lot is the absence of player messages, the addition of drop in co-op and the focus on traits for builds. The story is told partially through lore and also through interactions with the various NPCs.

I highly recommend checking this game out if you like challenging games with a focus on replayability. I bought this game to prep for Remnant II and was not disappointed at all. I'm looking forward to the continuation of the story and more challenging boss fights. :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI generally have a high tolerance for tedium and poor gameplay in my gaming... but Remnant failed to clear my extremely low bar.

The thing just isn't fun. The combat is clunky. The environments are gross. The enemies spawn regularly behind you. Everything is optimized for co-op so... be prepared to eat a heaping ration of s*** as a solo player.

I'm currently 76 minutes in so I'm hoping and praying that I'll be able to get a refund. In the extremely depressing event that I can't, I'll be back to do a more thorough takedown.

EDIT: I didn't get a refund. Played through exactly once. I've never played a soulslike before and this is apparently one of the crappier soulslikes. There is nothing interesting or redeeming about the plot or dialogue, which is what I usually care about. Very samey enemies. Very dull in general.\t\t\t\t\t
