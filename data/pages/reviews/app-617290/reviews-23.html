\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game. Runs good, is fun with husband. Bosses are very hard.
It feels like a dungeon crawler version of dark souls.

You do not need an Epic Games account unless you plan on playing with friends who have Epic Games.
Just join your steam friends directly through steam instead of using the in-game join feature.

Note: Spoilers, so beware.
We have reached the last boss and decided to stop playing the game.
The entire game we were built up to believe we could make any class combination we want. Husband went for a pure melee build while I went for a shotgun/flame weapon build. We had no issues. Every boss fight was a new challenge that made us think.
Dungeons were fairly easy as they were cramped spaces and with our melee / shotgun builds, we were easily able to get through them.
Upon reaching the final boss - it was unkillable with our builds. After doing research, we discovered that you need a Sniper Build to be able to take out the final boss. As it is to high off the ground for melee or for shotguns to do proper damage.
This completely discouraged us from wanting to continue the game. We don't even want to beat it now. We would have to either grind to get enough materials to upgrade and respect into sniper builds or restart the game.

I am adding this note in as a warning to anyone who plays this game. The final boss is a massive disappointment that ruined our experience with the game. I would still recommend it, but this is your warning.\t\t\t\t\t

Super Disappointed
Honestly, the game isn't fun at all. I came here from Dark Souls and Elden Ring, because this was described as "Dark Souls with guns", which is ironic because it seems to be missing a part - SOUL. This review will be separated according to the aspects that really killed it for me.

Controls
Firstly, I understand this is a gun game on PC, and many people will prefer to use mouse and keyboard. That's great for you. To me - this type of game plays best on a controller, the Dark Souls elements are literally begging for it. Unfortunately for Dark Souls players, the inputs for the controller are not able to be rebound. Don't like your healing flask being left bumper? Too bad. Don't like what is essentially your weapon art to be right bumper? Also, too bad. I don't think its too much to ask to want controller input that can be rebound. If you try to treat this like a shooter and go mouse and keyboard, I think you will find the controls to be... cumbersome.

Character Design
Every enemy is grey/black and red. I can excuse this to an extent, but when enemies are copy/pasted in large packs, it gets really old, really fast. Enemies seem to have one or two moves, but that's it. Most fodder enemies will be perpetually stun locked if you shoot them, so just lay it on and walk on by. The first boss I encountered was a physically larger, bullet sponge version of a slightly uncommon enemy I had already encountered repeatedly. I beat said boss by rolling around him, 2 inches from his face, shooting him until he died. Bullet sponges do not equal difficulty, they are annoying.

Cutscenes
There are way too many. The beginning of the game feels more like a movie with occasional gameplay segments. The tracking on the characters mouths when they are talking is so uncanny it pulls me out of the cutscene entirely. I also didn't feel very compelled by the story, so cutscenes really felt intrusive.

Weapons and Combat Feel
Guns feel and sound nice to shoot. The designs were very awesome looking and evoked a western / WWI vibe. However, melee was painful, especially when compared to Dark Souls. The melee combat can best be summed up as Minecraft "swing a sword until it dies". There is hardly any variation in moves, and it was so excruciatingly boring I found myself only using it on occasion to shake things up, as again, the stun lock means you really don't need to melee. The guns have mod slots, into which you can equip what are essentially power ups. The game equipped me with a heal power up that was so strong I didn't need to use a flask. Ever. It also seems the power up regenerates(?) making flasks useless. The awesome guns could have saved the game, however there are a total of around 28ish guns. Not a whole lot.

Atmosphere
The world that I was able to experience before the end of the refund period was very grey and red, much like the enemies. This is supposed to be a game revolving around ammo conservation, however I found that the only way you could run out of ammo was if you literally kept clicking non stop, even with no enemies on screen. Most fodder dies in 1 to 2 shots, and drop what feels like an entire magazine worth of bullets. You could never touch your melee and be just fine. The audio was okay, but never really hit an air of exploration or even horror that I would have liked, it just felt generic. I thought the camera angle was way too close, and with no option to pull the camera back it became kind of annoying. You can increase the FOV, but it begins to look like you are looking through binoculars really fast.

World Structure
When I was walking around the world, I couldn't help but feel that the levels were poorly laid out, and empty. I kept asking myself, "Why would a dev design a room like this?", "Why is this here?", "This environment is so bland, it needs more details." I only found out afterwards that everything is randomly generated, and then it made so much sense. It looks so bland because they are generic tile sets stitched together randomly by a computer with no sense of atmosphere. Areas looked so similar it was hard to tell where I was going at times. "Oh, this area is all brick" "Oh, this area is all concrete and rusty pipes". You get the deal. Most of the rooms don't feel like there is any story to them. I can't look at a building and say "Oh! This must have been an Office/Hospital/Mechanic! Look at all the Papers/Medical Supplies/Auto parts spread around." The areas just have no story to them. They feel like empty halls designed for a shooting gallery, which really ruins the immersion this game is trying to create.

Multiplayer
This game would have been saved with an invasion feature, and this is coming from someone who doesn't really care for them in the Souls games. The enemies are just too easy, and having an actual player be able to arrive in my world, at any time, gunning for me, would have spiced up a lot of otherwise boring stretches. I can't comment on co-op because I was the only one of my friends to go out on a limb for this game, but I can only wonder how easy the game would be with multiple people to keep enemies suppressed.

So... Who is this game for?
It's hard to say. It almost feels, at least mechanically, like a combination of Dark Souls and Gears of War, which is awkward to say the least. I hate to keep comparing it so heavily to Dark Souls, but the similar elements are just too close. I saw someone compare their enjoyment in this game to the enjoyment they got out of GoldenEye 007 in a different review on google, and I think that helps to paint some of the picture. You would probably enjoy this if you barely had any exposure to games, game only in small intervals after work to cool down with friends, are not very skilled and enjoy cruising through gameplay, or are ancient (when compared to the average age of someone who plays games). If you've ever booted up GTA 5, only to drive in circles while talking to friends, this is the epitome of that atmosphere, except a little more dull. Other games have executed these elements far better. If this isn't your first foray into gaming, I suggest you try one of those instead.

For the sake of full transparency, I have refunded this game, and my experience of it is reflective of what is playable within the refund period of 2 hours. My opinion is just that - an opinion. If you loved this game and purchased it 5 billion times so you could replay it on your Nintoaster, that's great, it just wasn't for me.

To me, it's probably worth about $10
