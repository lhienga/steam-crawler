\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Overall, 9.4/10. After 38 hours in the game, I can say Remnant is worth playing for the well-written story and inventive world most importantly, but also for the co-op fun factor. And everything else. This may be my favorite game of 2022. Note, I got it for about half off, but I would pay full price for this game after playing it (I already prepurchased the second game). You'll get your money's worth.

Story: 10/10
World: 10/10
Character Customization: 8/10
User Interface: 9/10
Gameplay & Controls: 9/10
Graphics & Sound Design: 10/10
NPCs & Mobs: 10/10
Co-op: 10/10
Bug-free: 9/10

STORY: Amazing. So inventive. Very few, if any, writing flaws that really bothered me. I've not had a game story this good in a while! It's rare to find good writing nowadays, so this story was really satisfying. I can happily say I did not skip any dialogue in this game. Ever. There are also some parts of the story that are affected by your choices, which is really fun! If you really wanted to, you could just say "♥♥♥♥ it" and kill the dude you were helping, or help someone you were meant to kill instead. Super fun to have that agency.

WORLD: Again, SO inventive! It's sci-fi and fantasy wrapped up in one. I'd liken it to the creatures & world travel of Star Wars mixed with the apocalyptic atmosphere of Fallout + some unidentifiable magic dust that makes it so unique. It's unimaginably fun and intriguing. I found myself wanting more of the lore and worlds. Gods, if I could come up with this kind of stuff, I'd be a bestselling author.

CHARACTER CUSTOMIZATION: Could use some work. There aren't many options here, either for appearance or for gear. In general though, it's not bad, and I made a character I really liked looking at the whole game through. At least they have curly hair!! Aesthetically, I do like that we wear backpacks - so much more realistic than most post-apocalyptic games out there.

UI: Very clean and clear. Nothing's in my way, though I wish I could see my health a bit easier. The map isn't great, but I usually didn't really need it too much.

GAMEPLAY & CONTROLS: Pretty smooth and intuitive. Feels a little like Outriders, actually. A lot of the weapons feel amazing to use, especially the sniper rifles and crossbow. Some of the gameplay is very difficult, which is super satisfying to beat, though some bosses are just incredibly hard while others are way too easy. However, sometimes dodging is a little slow and you're sitting there spamming the spacebar trying to get out of an impending AoE, to no avail.

GRAPHICS & SOUND DESIGN: Beautiful game! It's not AAA but it looks gorgeous, especially all the alien races.

NPCs & MOBS: Amazing. Never got tired of them. There's a wealth of diversity in this game that is beautiful to see, because you know someone put a ton of effort into designing all of them - not just their appearance, but also their culture. Every race we encounter in the various worlds is unique. The people we meet along the way are also interesting characters (not just the human ones!).

CO-OP: Very fun! I don't think I played the game solo, even though that was fun too - it's just so much more exciting to discover these worlds and vanquish these foes with a friend. It's a little weird to get it running, but once it goes, it's nice and smooth.

BUGS: Not much to say here. There were a couple glitches, but nothing important enough to recall or care about. Definitely nothing game-breaking.

OTHER: I also played this game in French since I'm learning, and it was wonderful. The French voices sound just beautiful, and it's really rather easy to understand. Bravo, Gunfire team!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI admit it. I'm not great at games. I will never "git gud" or whatever. I am casual to the core, insomuch that I rarely set a game above normal difficulty. This is a problem because I like Soulslike games; they are accessible to the casual gamer like myself and I always get my money's worth. The problem is that I never finish any of them because there is always one boss that stands in the way of progress. I spend about 50 times trying to kill one, look up strategies online, try those strategies about 100 more times and then ultimately quit the game, which is sad because it leaves me frustrated and feeling like I wasted time. I know, I know... git gud, nub! But the fact is, I won't, and I don't want to turn into the type of person that does git gud and then repeat that sentiment to others, so I am hearby swearing of Soulslike games.

For those that ARE good or maybe just patient. I recommend this. If you're a casual soloer like me, be prepared to be frustrated by twin bosses, multiple adds, waiting two-seconds to apply health boosts or bandages while praying that you don't get interrupted by the slightest touch, and... yeah. It IS worth the price (got it on the Steam sale), but the frustration of not finishing it makes it not worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
