\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou could describe this as a soulslike shooter. You've got your reusable Estus, Bonfires that respawn non-boss enemies, unlockable shortcuts, etc. Some big differences: there's melee, but this is really a shooter and while melee-focused builds are possible, they're probably for experienced players; you don't loose anything upon dying (you don't even need to recover money/xp from your corpse); maps and bosses aren't fixed like in Dark Souls, but shuffled for each playthrough. If you've played modular boardgames like Zombicide you probably have an idea: each region is made of tiles, with dungeons, bosses and events being drawn from a pool to make a 'unique' playthrough. This is a game to be replayed and you can't get everything in a single playthrough.

To help with this there's Adventure Mode, which let's you roll a specific world in a smaller version compared to the campaign, which is great for farming and getting specific items. There's a roguelike mode called Survival too.

I love this game, especially for co-op. I love the visual design, the music is nice, many weapons/powers, diverse worlds ranging from post-apocalyptic to sci-fi to fantasy...but it has its issues. Bosses tend to rely on too many mobs to make them harder, which can be frustrating and my complaint. It can however be offset by playing with others or just familiarizing yourself with the game.

For a new player the game can feel like it doesn't offer many options or that builds aren't really a thing: your first character will probably be a mess of whatever you found along the way. As you get familiar with the game you'll be able to actually build a build and those can be intensely satisfying. Again, nothing new for soulslikes, they rarely have smooth learning curves.

Speaking of which, this game has a stupid scaling system that it doesn't bother explaining. Just don't upgrade your weapons too fast, keep all your gear balanced and you should be fine.

Remnant is very much worth a try and if you stick with it you might have a jewel on your hands.\t\t\t\t\t
