So, I wanted to play a cooperative game with my friends, that's why I bought it.

It's crashes into the Black Screen with full PC freeze so you need to reboot. After doing that you don't longer have your save. AGAIN: YOU DON'T HAVE YOUR SAVE WORKING. So, I got this 2 times, first after 2 or 4 hours of exploring. Second time after 8 Hours half into the game!!!!!1111 Now I need to play from the begging .
The worst part, they know about that because they have this in FAQ on the website. However, their manual doesn't work for me and not only me....


So AGAIN:
You buy it, then you play it, then game deletes your save with EPIC CRASH, so you could play again. New way to interpret "New game+"
^^^^^^^THE WORST PART HERE^^^^^^

It feels like it's alpha or beta of the real game.
Gameplay-wise:
- Equipment felt the same for me (yes I know you can have different resists and rolls speed but still)
- Weapons are generic and sad, you can just go through the game with the same weapon you had from start, nothing will change
- Skill(trait tree) isn't that great, but okish
- As someone already said "it looks like a bloodborn for poor people"
- Story is weak, it's just there.
- The upgrade system of the game is really weak like from early 2000s or smth

Only pluses:
- you can play with friends
- rings and amulets (the only cool part of the game)
- you can pew-pew in grim environment\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will admit this game is somewhat flawed, so I'll get my complaints out of the way first.

the visuals are weak and uninspired artistically though very pretty. many of the areas are very open and empty. the lack of music while not in combat makes the whole thing feel lifeless. I can understand if they wanted you to feel lonely, but they didn't get the details of that right and instead it just feels empty. that's one reason why playing with a friend improves the experience so much, their voice fills in that void that was perhaps unintentionally created.

there's also an overreliance on breakables being your main source of income. smashing them is a tedious endeavor but if you skip over it you won't be getting much of the game's central currency, scrap.

finally the game's class system is a bit mis-representative. every character you create can eventually have every trait and every weapon, the classes are really just what you start with. it's more "starting gear" than a "class" and it would help assuage some complaints if it had been labeled as such.

now for the good parts:
the gunplay is immensely satisfying and the AI is very good, they occasionally dodge shots and run for cover if they're ranged. some enemies teleport around just to confuse you. the weapon variety is amazing, with most of the standard rifle and shotguns covered, but also including things like a laser sniper that shoots black holes or a bee gun that shoots swarms of insects.
side note: it's not really a looter-shooter per se, because you only get one of each gun and you upgrade them through farming materials. you also don't get drops, you find weapons out in the wild in the same place each time, or craft them from boss parts that you're always gauranteed. that is where the dark souls comparisons come from (well that and the dragon hearts and crystals).

the structure of the campaign is unique, it's not a roguelike but has elements from them (although one of the DLCs contains a classic roguelite mode). instead you are given one of a few modules during your playthrough and you can reroll these at any time, but you have to start from the beginning. this means your second playthrough may be wildly different from your first if you get all different modules. each dimension also has it's own enemy visual variety with earth having the tree inspired Root, and other places having more humanoid obstacles. it really lends an air of quality and polish that the enemies don't stay the same throughout.

all in all I enjoyed my time with a game that put a fresh spin on some concepts that are starting to grow a bit old like the soulslike estus flask and bonfire systems and procedural generation. I look forward to trying the sequel after I've exhausted this one to it's bones. I just hope there's less barrel smashing in it.\t\t\t\t\t
