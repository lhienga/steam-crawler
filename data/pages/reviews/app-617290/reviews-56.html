While Remnant is not a bad game, it is far from being good. It's very rough around the edges and feels empty at times. It might be fun at the start but you begin to find the cracks deeper you go. Certainly not a "Dark Souls with guns", it doesn't even feels like a souls-like.

The Good

+ Some environments look nice.

+ Guns change visuals depending on what mods you slot in them. Thats cool.

+ Shooting feels fine. Can't complain there. Gunplay isn't bad.

+ Finding keys and opening doors with them in the hub is a good idea. Makes it interactive.

The Bad

- Story, in its core, is not that bad but writing is a big question mark. You don't feel anything for the characters. They are just there. Apart from that, Remnant keeps introducing new characters and factions but none of it is explored nor explained. Nothing memorable for me except the mute guy.

- You pick a class in the beginning but it doesn't matter much. They have one class trait and one weapon mod which you can get with other classes too. They have different weapons and armor aswell but you can buy them from the vendor later.

- Progression and gameplay variety are almost non existent. No feeling of improvement other than giving points to basic traits which gives you +5 hp, +%2 crit etc.

- Melee "builds" are usually useless on boss fights. It is not rewarding nor satisfying to play. They are good for killing normal enemies throughout the map IF your teammate is using melee aswell. Otherwise he/she is going to kill them before you even reach them.

- Most bosses are just awful. No exciting designs to see here. They have the same mechanics. They take damage, they spawn mobs, you kill them and damage the boss until another wave spawns.

- For first playthrough, hard difficulty was too easy, we just ran through it except that stupid three orb fight. Nightmare, being designed for later runs, was too hard for a fresh character so there was no middle ground.

Survival Mode

This game mode had potential but it's all wasted.

- "Builds" you make doesn't feel much different at all. You play almost the same everytime. Unique armor skills are not varied enough. Existing ones are not fun nor game changing. Apart from that, despite game having lots of different melee weapons, melee "builds" are mostly useless.

- While you are picking up traits from the ground, you can't see what buff they give. You can only see their assigned names which you have to memorize to know what it does. Very basic QoL to implement imo.

- You can't save while you are playing survival mode. If you quit the game in the middle of a run, you lose all progress. What year is this?\t\t\t\t\t
