\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is mostly going to be a comparison between From The Ashes and Remnant 2 coming from someone who started in Remnant 2 and has over 200 hours in that game.

Remnant FTA is a very good game. On its own, it boasts a ton of replayability, gameplay variety, and endless grind. Here's what I love about it so far:
- Performance on PC is fantastic, very smooth experience on lower end rigs (unlike R2's poor optimization with UE5)
- More lighthearted and "fun" (R2 is very much doom and gloom)
- Main story is solid and interesting to follow along in the campaign
- Every weapon feels great to use and most weapon mods are useful; one of my main gripes with R2 is that there are a select few weapons that are clearly stronger than the rest and only a few weapon mods are actually fun to use
- Scrap and materials are plentiful and allow for much easier leveling of gear
- Unlimited trait points allow for much greater sense of progression and less hesitancy spending trait points
- Armor set bonuses offer unique ways to buildcraft and are much more interesting than DR/weight class in R2
- Co-op system is much more refined and less tedious than R2's awful multiplayer
- Survival mode offers endless replayability after you finish collecting everything
- Armor skins provide somewhat of an endgame for players who enjoy farming

FTA isn't better than R2, however. Here's a list of things I didn't like about FTA:
- Very little buildcrafting with only 2 ring slots and no archetypes
- Melee is just really bad in this game, R2 has much better melee combat
- Constantly running out of stamina while free roaming is one of the worst things about this game
- The worlds and dungeons are much smaller, which made campaign playthroughs feel a bit too short
- The lore of the individual worlds felt a bit lacking and uninteresting (except for the Wards - they have some fantastic storytelling)
- Majority of the boss fights were very underwhelming and lacked interesting mechanics
- Boss fights also tended to spawn a ton of adds which made some fights extremely annoying; it feels like the add spawns were added to compensate for the relatively simple boss patterns
- The cartoon-ish visuals can make important decisions feel less impactful and forgettable
- R2 definitely has better procedural generation, secrets, and hidden items/interactions


Overall, I really enjoyed this game and know that I will be playing it for many more hours. I would not consider R2 to be a direct upgrade to R:FTA, but more of a sidegrade. Both games are fantastic in their own right, and you should definitely pick up both if you enjoy souls-likes.\t\t\t\t\t
