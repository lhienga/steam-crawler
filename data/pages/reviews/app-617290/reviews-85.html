\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is by far THE BEST souls-like I've ever played that is not made by Fromsoft. If I were told that there would be a game that is; souls-like, shooter which I'd opt for playing with KB+M instead of controller, I'd say that would be a bad game. But Remnant proves me wrong so much and I'm happy for it. I'm even amazed of the fact that the Devs were able to sell this idea, because I think a lot of people/publishers would brush it off.

So what is Remnant, well it's a game that copies pretty much every Souls mechanic (bonfires, upgrading items, bosses, anything you can think of except invasions) but being a shooter. I think it gets really fun to play with friends, yes you can play it solo, but I think what the Devs had in mind that this game is sort of supposed to play co-op. On higher difficulties it becomes really necessary unless you're using really optimized setups. In souls games, co-op trivializes the entire game, the levels, the boss fights, but I can't say the same thing for Remnant. I don't know if it scales difficulty if you're playing co-op but I know that my friend and I struggled through Nightmare and Apocalypse difficulty settings (maybe we should git gud at Remnant). So there's challenge if you're looking for it.

Replay value is I'd say high, as it has some kind of weird world creation mechanic that makes the maps and item placements sort of random, and boss placement differently. There are plenty of weapons and builds you can play with, even your starting gear is so good that I finished the game with them (of course upgraded them too).

It doesn't have true co-op really (progress in your world STAYS in your world, your friend's world is different) BUT you can pretty much do co-op in one world and your cooperator would get every item/skill/level and achievement as a part of your world. It's very seamless, if you're planning to play through the game as a duo or trio, go for it, you simply connect to same guy's world and progress. You don't need to progress in each world as if it's DS3 or ER(you can though), one is enough to finish the game.

It has DLC's that I really recommend getting with it (I got all during a sale, it was a bargain), it adds new levels, one of them is pretty long (Subject 2923) and fun.

I found this gem pretty late (although I've heard about it but had prejudice) but there are still people playing it, so when you're done with your world, you can join others to help them and get loot for yourself, which is I think a good initiative to play with others.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant: From the Ashes is, quite simply, an incredibly fun game. Anyone remember fun? From before games became second jobs that you had to pay for? But seriously, Remnant is one of the very few games I've played recently that I actually constantly feel the urge to go back and play again after I beat it, and not even for 100% achievement completion, but just because I LIKE it!

While it's not a perfect game (the story is rather half-baked and a few of the gameplay systems, particularly the way enemies actually OUTSCALE your equipment, could do with a second draft), it's an ideal blend of 3 different genres: soulslike RPG, 3rd person co-op shooter, and roguelike. The procedural generation elements are probably the cleverest innovation, creating a largely linear single-player campaign that always hits the same core points (major story beats, some of the world bosses) but has details that vary in each playthrough- sometimes you have to defeat Singe to get off Earth, sometimes it's the Ent instead, sometimes you get a chance to obtain the Beam Rifle on Rhom, sometimes you don't but you get a chance to get to reactivate the Ancient Construct and acquire the Iron Sentinel mod instead. The ability to replay a segment of the game as a self-contained "adventure" any time you like is the icing on the cake, letting you instantly roll up a new randomised mini-campaign to try and face some of the bosses or find some of the dungeons you never got a chance to before. Honestly, this is probably the #1 source of the replay value I mentioned before- while I wouldn't necessarily like to sign up for another entire campaign run, I can do adventures for days. And if it's more challenge you're after, the multiple difficulty settings (and the popular Apocalypse mod) will keep you occupied for months.

The 3rd person shooting mechanics are perfectly solid, with perfectly balanced dodging and instant melee attacks giving combat that nice soulslike flow, and the vast array of equipment (long guns, small arms, 3-part armour sets and 3 accessories) and the VAST variety of traits give you a huge amount of customisability for your character. Best of all is playing in co-op, which is easy and satisfying, with a small but great community of helpful players.

With Remnant II coming up soon, there's still time for you to get into Remnant and find out what the fuss is all about and fill yourself in on the backstory before a new set of worlds (as well as corrupted old ones) open up before you. You really shouldn't miss this one.\t\t\t\t\t
