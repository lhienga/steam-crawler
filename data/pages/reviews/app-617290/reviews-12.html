\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnmid: For the Masses

I bought it with friends a while ago. But the game didn't land and technical issues convinced us to refund it.

Years later I decided to give Remnant another go since it was free on Gamepass and some YouTubers talked highly of the game.
And boy oh boy. It is just not fun, I felt like I played it to kill time more than anything.

First are foremost, its similarity to Souls starts and ends with a dodge roll and stamina bar. However, nothing but rolls and sprinting use stamina, so there is no pressure to manage it. Beyond that there is nothing souls like to speak of.

The graphics, art design, and areas are well done, I can give it that. Every place looks pretty, and feels like it could have been interesting.

The plot, and characters are one-dimensional. I skipped a lot of spoken dialogs except tree mother, the immortal, bug lady and found logs in Ward 13. Most characters have no clue "what's going on" so you don't really get much from them but small talk. I won't spoil the story but imagine a very cool universe and your job is to explore and inquire nothing of it. Shoot some aliens.

Gameplay-wise wise you travel within several biomes fighting their respective 3 or so enemies. Each biome has unique enemies but all enemies fall into a few archetypes with near-identical move sets, AI & roles. Guys who rush, attack twice, and wait for you to retaliate. Guys who maintain a distance and shoot. Guys who rush, throw exactly two projectiles, then rush more, guys who explode. Different looks but it always feels the same. The maps themselves are randomly generated as I understand but there is no point in exploring save to grind upgrade materials. Don't expect any lore or meaningful experiences off the beaten path. Occasionally special enemies spawn in to fabricate some challenge or variety but they generally don't do much unless they catch you with your pants down. Despite travelling to new areas you always feel like you're doing the same thing, I never needed to adjust how I played with the exception of a handful of boss fights. While there is a variety of bosses you can fight they all follow the same formula. Fight to x% life, spawn wave of enemies, fight to y% life, spawn a wave of enemies, fight to z% life, etc. There are rare exceptions, a moth boss and the last boss which played a little differently. That was it. No boss fight is interesting or memorable.

The game also suffered from some wild difficulty spikes. Obviously it's intended to be played with friends, I know that's a big hook, but I played alone since mine didn't care to try it again.

Mobs are harmless and just make you get from point A to point B slower. Special enemies require your attention before you mop up mobs and if you get an unlucky spawn they can end you quickly. I had a pretty OP aoe healing skill that could out heal most incoming damage but certain seemingly plain attacks can instantly kill you and it feels unfair. Or not telegraphed well enough.

The bosses were hard but manageable even for a solo player- however- there are some bosses who fly. Rip melee builds. I kept pretty balanced in my build but those bosses were very un-fun fights. Imagine if souls popped in a boss you could only hit with bows...


I never needed to change my armor, I went through the entire game with the starter set and the same two guns. You can upgrade everything with iron and because this is about 'achieving big numbers' simply upgraded and stuck with the largest digits throughout. Traits are a cool concept but felt mostly meaningless, I understand they can affect certain bosses or story beats but I don't feel like there was any such boss or beat that mattered. Many traits and skills felt kind of useless but that might just be me.

Remnant is an attempt at a 'soulslike with guns' by people who utterly failed to grasp souls likes in the first place. Enemies are the stars of the show in souls likes. The user changes in order to win. Remnent gives you a tidal wave of the same mob and if you die you just need to grind some iron for bigger numbers. They had a cool idea & world but execution killed it.

I also tried the starting sequence of the sequel Remnant 2 on gamepass but dropped it immediately.

The gameplay is identical. Comically so. Starting Remnant 2 after beating 1 feels like I just booted up a graphics mod and continued. Remnant 2 has nicer models and textures but you start in the same place, same enemies, or close to it. You also get a sidekick who adds what could be called a "Marvel Phase 4" flavor to the game which took the game from just boring to simply annoying.

If you're still interested wait out some huge sale, but the game didn't do it for me.\t\t\t\t\t

That is where my complaint of the game comes in - the 2nd player in survival mode has a lot of glitches such as: equipping rings and having their effects remain even when putting on other gear. I wish they would fix that like don't let latency/lag excuse exist I get it thinks you fell off the world but you just were walking on the stair or down a sloped hill.

Single player campaign also fun but this is a game meant to be played with a couple friends or at least one other for full enjoyment.\t\t\t\t\t
