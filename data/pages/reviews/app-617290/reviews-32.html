\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGrossly imbalanced.
https://youtu.be/twWyRgkxlew

Watch the video. It tells you all you need to know. It is the tl;dr version of this review. What follows below is visceral.

Great campaign! Garbage everything else.
9.2 hours in one day. I can tell you right now, don't bother with Adventure Mode unless your build desperately needs a specific item. Even then, be ready for something that is harder than every FromSoft Souls-Like game combined, with all the random instant deaths and pitfalls of Kingsfield.

Why exactly? Because Adventure Mode is entirely imbalanced. Coming off of the Campaign I had just finished the first World/Chapter. The standard enemy went down in 3 Pistol shots. The Dragon went down in 2 attempts flat, with a tail break for a nifty fire sword. I decided I wanted maybe a little more challenge, so I hopped into Adventure Mode on Hard because I wanted to fight the other bosses.

Enemies took 4 Pistol shots instead of 3, and dealt way more damage. It messed with my flow, so I started a new one on Normal. Immediately, going from Hard mode to Normal mode, enemies took double the amount of shots to kill. Every normal enemy was replaced with torn open tree guys. They dealt normal damage, sure, but they took 8 shots to kill. I know for certain I didn't put it on Apocalypse difficulty because my save says Normal.

I fought my way through the area eventually, and made it to the first boss. Riphide is his name. If you ever come across this boss, pray. It's all you can do. He splits himself into 2 identical copies, which both are the boss. Unlike a Pinwheel or Four Kings, both of these guys take damage to the one health bar. So, fine. Twin boss. Can't kill them one at a time.

And then they split again into 4 guys. Name one other Souls-Like that throws 4 bosses with 1 health bar at you in an enclosed space. Moreover, give those bosses a shockwave attack that has infinite reach, as well as a ranged attack that takes 40% of your health. Oh right! And then let them self-heal. The closest you can find is what happens when you sit around in the Four Kings boss room. Though, not even they as one of the highest damage bosses in Souls, deal as much damage as Riphide.

But it gets worse. They can split once more into 8 guys, and up to 3 of them can heal at a time. One of these guys was a challenging enough boss. They're aggressive, they hit hard, and some of their attacks are genuinely mean. But 8 of them? And it's not even something you can prevent. They do it when you get them down to their last chunk of HP, and then immediately start healing themselves.

Complete and utter crap. Unfun, imbalanced, and above all else... it was an Earth Area 1, Normal Difficulty, Sub-Boss. A random enemy turned boss. Not even a complete unique boss like Singe was.

If you feel so inclined to defend this game from my horrible unfair accusations, I was enjoying the game until what should have been the lowest level, lowest difficulty, starter area became worse than every end game FromSoft boss I've ever fought. The way I finally beat Riphide is 100% cheese, and has left me drained of all satisfaction. Dark Souls from the start has known that the only way you can make something so frustrating, difficult, and draining be enjoyable is to have a satisfying victory. A pop-off. An "OH MY GOD FINALLY! HECK YES!" moment. This moment never came when fighting Riphide.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeat the game for free on EGS and played it a bunch there, probably a hundred hours or so, then bought it here with the DLC and modded it to add some extra zing.
Anyway, I'll review without the modding in mind, of course.

Great game. Flawed, but great. Definitely planning on buying Remnant 2 at some point too just because this one's so cool.
I can never really decide if it's a soulslike or a shooter, or an RPG or whatever, it's some kind of mix of all of them, though the lack of a death penalty might ban it from the "soulslike" label.

I guess it kinda depends on how you build your character, my tank character plays like a Souls character, dodge rolling, whacking stuff with swords and hammers, wearing a chunky suit of armor (side note: the heaviest armor set doesn't let you roll at all, you just kinda "belly flop", and you're so heavy that it actually is a pretty great, high-stagger attack!)
My current character is a... "mage"? A crit-heavy ranged glass cannon dude, and the game plays far more like a shooter as I summon a dog to help me fight and sometimes I'll activate a special that turns my bullets into fire bullets. This build's doing okay, but I'll 100% need more equipment, I don't have much of a build going yet.

Weirdly, something that puts it differently than most other shooters/soulslikes/RPGs is that each run through the game has some randomization. Different bosses, different paths, different side areas, different side quests, heck I had one time on like my seventh run through one zone where I accidentally got a shopkeeper killed and her body was all gross and infected with an ancient evil I let loose, and I haven't seen that event prior to that or since.
Don't like the run you're on, or you're trying to find a specific event? You can redo the whole campaign over at any time, turn the difficulty up or down, then run it again and end up finding some crazy new thing you've never seen before, or find a weird puzzle that requires a key you get from a different run, that kind of stuff.

Anyway, the bad stuff:
-Being later in the game, or on higher difficulties tends to make enemies a bit bullet-spongy
-Intentionally farming to try to get a specific thing is a pain since you're dealing with RNG of RNG of RNG, or rerolling for a boss that might be 4 hours into the game and it's the wrong boss.
-You spend WAY too long in the game without many options for equipment. My first character was using her starting armor and sword for literally the entire first playthrough because I wasn't finding much and what I did find wasn't a good fit for her. DLC/modding helps, but that's still a shame.
-One of the game's shops is run by someone who the devs absolutely wanted you to hate the hell out of, and it works, she's even back in the second game, still insufferable. One of the things she does is scoff like she's annoyed at you as you so much as pass her shop, and basically every word out of her mouth is to insult or berate you, or not believe anything you say. 100% of players who play this hate her, but she's got a very useful shop, so she's unavoidable.
-Basically no plot. The entire plot is that Earth is taken over by interdimensional wooden tree creatures and humanity is 99% dead. You're looking for a guy who might know something. You travel across dimensions. You find him just kinda sitting there. He has a key for a machine back in town. You activate the machine. You fight a boss. Credits.
-Kinda glitchy.
-Weapons with built-in skills make the slottable skills irrelevant, and most of the good weapons have built-in skills, which limits build variety
-Armor sets discourage you from mixing equipment, encouraging you to wear the full set. Mixing-and-matching simply isn't a thing 99.9999% of the time
-Melee weapon selection is varied, but limited. Like, 8 different combat styles, but only 1 or 2 of each, like I think there's one fist weapon in the game?

I typed too much, eh, whatever, you get the idea.\t\t\t\t\t
