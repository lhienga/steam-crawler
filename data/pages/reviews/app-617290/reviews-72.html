\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is a reason this became a classic with a devoted following. It has so much variety and replay-ability. It does things differently than you are used to but does them well. This means it is worth going along for the ride instead of assuming everything it does should be like every other game - It isn’t.

Worth playing in 2023 ahead of remnant 2.
Best with friends. Harder but good alone.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot good for solo. All fights are a massive gank with never-ending adds, 3-6 constantly on-screen with the boss no matter how many you kill. Plowed through on hard and then decided to quit playing solo after reaching Totem Father. Only thing that made it bearable until then was the gun mods that created two ally spawns and a shield for projectiles.

Also highly recommend against choosing the close range class. Initially you will be satisfied, but there are large sections of the game where a shotgun + melee build are heavily punished. There are forced range boss battles and getting in close for a good strike on all other battles will get you two shot. When playing solo, this only added to the frustration, as there was nobody to pull aggro or pick me up. It would be cool if they had a mechanic where you had a companion similar to Bosco in Deep Rock Galactic that could attack, heal, revive, etc.

With that being said, I really wish Steam had a middle ground recommendation, because I have actually had a lot of fun playing with friends. I will be looking into buying Remnant 2 if the PC reviews are positive on launch for this reason. It looks like a solid step forward towards something better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think Remnant is a horrible game, but I can't recommend it either.

The areas and dungeons are incredibly boring to me. This game has no memorable moments. I actually could not believe it when I defeated the final boss, I audibly said to myself "That's it?!". Well, technically, no, because this game is designed to be beaten multiple times on different difficulties and different modes, but that would mean I have to suffer through those worlds and dungeons again. Sometimes it can also be quite confusing where to go next which adds some extra frustration.
I just hated the main hub of this game. The setting is bland, the NPCs are bland, it was just depressing to run through the hub so many times to upgrade or buy stuff. And why do they have to put the "stations" where you can upgrade your gear so far away from the spawn point? I don't know how much time I wasted just running around in that butt ugly hub.

The bosses in this game are perhaps even more disappointing than the level design. I honestly can barely remember any of the bosses anymore. I thought some of the various elites in each world were more interesting. It is just such a massive difference to the Souls series and it makes you appreciate those games even more. The bosses are quite easy throughout, the only difficulty is to avoid the mobs that are spawned in almost all fights. Off the top of my head I only remember one boss that doesn't do this. It's just weak.

The gunplay is fun, you can upgrade your gear of course and add some special abilities to your weapons. I liked playing around with different setups and that is the best part of the game.
It also runs smoothly and I did not experience any bugs.

I wish this game motivated me enough to try other difficulties and experience some other randomly generated enemies and so on, but I just could not bring myself to keep going after I finished the first world for the second time.

And just a warning to those people who have the unstoppable urge to collect ALL the loot: the breakables (pots, crates etc.) have a very high chance to give you some scraps or upgrade materials, so you will often find yourself rolling around for a long time to collect everything in each area. I am one of those people and it was just ... tiring.
