\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant is, all said and done, a good game. I really enjoyed my time in it and felt like I got my money's worth!

To begin, my favorite part of this game is the world building. The worlds you visit outside Earth are varied and fun to explore. The developers made these various places familiar yet alien at the same time. Looking around in the environments can tell you a lot about what kind of aliens/people live there. There is an element of environmental storytelling here that I appreciate and the exploration that pays off in itself rewards you with immersion.

The gameplay is a lot of fun, particularly combat. There are a lot of ways to build your character thanks to weapon mods and traits (essentially attributes and passive bonuses). I ended up running a companion build with a dog and turrets to back me up in combat dealings with adds while I can keep myself occupied to focus on bigger targets with my own weapons. It was an incredibly effective strategy, and Remnants accessory and armor bonuses helped me flesh out the playstyle I wanted just right.

The story itself was engaging, but you do have to explore and dig around to find the finer details, mostly hidden in logs throughout the hub, Ward 13. Having lore hidden in something like computer logs can be a turn off for people, but even for those sorts of people I can say that it isn't the equivalent of reading a Harry Potter book. The logs themselves are short and sweet, they don't overstay their welcome and provide what you want out of them without extra fluff some lore entries in this format tend to have.

My only caveats with Remnant are that, first and foremost, it is quite short. I'm chalking part of this up to the difficulty I chose, that being Normal, and the build I went with through the game ended up making me quite a force to be reckoned with. I breezed through most bosses because of it, and so I'd like to suggest that any first time players who come into this game play on the next step up, Hard. If you are looking for a more relaxed experience and not the souls-like one, go with Normal. Souls fans, go with Hard. My second caveat with Remnant is quite smaller, and that's just enemies being a little samey. While their designs are intriguing, most enemies you run into have the same exact move set. Elite enemies can be fun to fight and I find are more varied.

I'd say Remnant is indeed worth a try picked up on sale. At the time of this review it is $13.99 and that is an absolute steal. I enjoyed my time quite a lot despite its length and I can absolutely see myself doing another playthrough in the future on a higher difficulty. Plaything through Remnant: From The Ashes has made me excited to try Remnant 2 and see what more these devs have to offer!\t\t\t\t\t

One of the worst games I've played
I had to beat this game on the Microsoft Store after buying it here, so I bought this game twice, only because you cannot play with Xbox players in co-op through Steam. First time I've ever heard of this, and absolutely infuriating.

The Good
There are very few positives to this game, but I do want to highlight them. Remnant is well optimized, moving through checkpoints back and forth is a quick process, co-op is well done and meaningful, and I never suffered any disconnects or stutters. The gun play itself is pretty satisfying, hitting crits and getting snipes definitely feels good.

The Bad
BORING!! Level after level of the same poorly designed layouts with a "go-straight ahead" mentality. There's no reason to explore anything, there's barely any chests, and very little of anything you find is of any consequence. I ended the game with the gear I started with, no loot development, no gear additions, no new weapons, a useless repeater pistol and an overpowered sniper rifle is what I started and beat the game with.

Even the gameplay loop just feels old and meaningless. Go through an area beating the same 3 mobs, get to a boss and beat that boss with the same mobs spawning again. Worse yet, each mob in any new area is just a reskin of another mob with a slightly different weapon. I could tell you that going through some areas was mostly asking, "Have we been here before?". Everything feels the same, nothing is unique.

Leveling doesn't really matter. Adding points to anything doesn't have much effect, because I one-shot everything already with a sniper. Outside the bosses, mobs are a total annoyance and not a threat ever. I never even went down once not fighting a boss playing the game on normal.

The story is a joke, I think I skipped every dialogue and cut scene I could, badly voice acted with generic nonsense that doesn't attach you to the world or the characters at all. It's surface level information more than story-telling.

The Ugly
My buddy and I beat the game in 2 sessions, maybe less than 10 hours. We get to the final boss, we figure out what we need to do, and we just don't care, after 20 minutes in the battle and the final boss only at 50% health, we just turned off and uninstalled the game. The final boss isn't difficult, it's tedious.

The worse part is of course micro-transactions and paid DLC to get weapon skins.... what a joke!

Conclusion
You've saved more time reading my review and believing it than wasting your time playing this game. It's not fun, there's no end-game or satisfaction, there's no loot, there's nothing enjoyable expect a sub-par game that is shocking anyone enjoyed.\t\t\t\t\t

The story is okay, but settings take the cake(abandoned overgrown cities, Afghanistan, Florida, and etc)
Remnant is fairly challenging alone when fighting bosses but a well balanced single player game. However it is very fun with friends exploring and labbing against difficult bosses(IT SHOULD BE NOTED THIS IS A 3 PLAYER GAME). there is a comprehensive arsenal to start out with but as you explore and beat bosses you can unlock new weapons to try.
