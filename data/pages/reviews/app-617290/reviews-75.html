\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBy the time you beat this game you will only see 30% of what it has to offer no matter how many side areas you visited. This is because game has a weird rougelike like mechanic that it will randomize areas and bosses from a pool of possible stuff you can see in that specific mission/area. You might ask yourself 'that was it?'' by the end of the game. But it's not and game expects you to use the mission or playthrough reroll feature and search for stuff you have missed. This is in no way shape of form a good game design. Game doesn't prioritize stuff you missed when you reroll and you might end up reroll a mission dozens of times just to fight a boss you haven't fought before. Combat and enemy/boss variety is pretty good and since I had actual fun playing the game I just downloaded a software that shows what you have in your current save so you don't have to reroll, check everything if there is a new boss/side quest area. Instead you can just reroll check the program reroll if there is no new stuff. This actually gave me the desire to keep playing a game I was already enjoying but decided to stop because of the RNG. Also coop is pretty well done. I'm already exited for Remnant 2 and I hope they abandon this experimental rougelike-esque world design.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow, ignore the fact it says I have 17 hours played on this game. I have much, much more on Xbox; I got it on PC because I wanted to play Co-Op with my brother and this was his platform of choice.

Typically, I don't feel good about purchasing a game twice, let alone a game and all of its DLC twice.

This, however, is an exception. Because this game is good. It is a breath of fresh air in a series of grindfest live-service shooters that are all about player retention.

Gameplay-wise, it's great. While it's difficult- especially some of the first bosses, as you won't have the HP or healing to brute-force them- it's more challenging than it is hard. The shooting mechanics are a lot of fun, and build customization is pretty decent. There's lots of variety of weapons, and the aesthetics of every world- while not the best graphics-wise- are jaw-dropping. Each one is distinct, and while they may all have a set of similar types of enemies, there are enough differences to make each of the worlds fun and interesting.

Plus, there's a lot of secret quests and hidden items, as one would expect from a Souls-inspired game. Armor sets, perks, weapons- exploration can be a lot of fun, even if you might need to look up a guide for a handful of the more obscure things.

But when it comes down to it, the best part about this game is that it can be finished. That doesn't sound like high praise, and there are people who likely still keep playing the game even to this day. But it is high praise, because knowing when to end something is the hardest part of the creative process. As much as we all love long-running shows or games, the truth is that gets exhausting because they keep compounding on each other. Remnant isn't that. While there's NG+ and plenty of other ways to keep progressing after beating the final boss, it's not needed to have a satisfying experience.

Now, story-wise, it's not great. Lore and worldbuilding is interesting, especially for the locales that aren't Earth, but it's mostly just "hey there's an ancient evil, go shoot it in the face". And you know what? That's good enough for me.

TL;DR: I highly recommend this game if you're into third-person shooters. it's not exactly the most-polished, but it is a lot of fun, and it's a solid 30-40 hours with DLC, which I'd say is more than enough bang for your buck. In an industry focused on monthly concurrent users and player retention, Remnant is a more back-to-the-basics approach of creating a fun co-op game (that can be done in singleplayer) and letting the sales speak for itself. It's not exactly AAA, but it's not budget game, either. It's a nice, comfortable middle ground. It's great we're finally getting a sequel, and I can't wait to see what else we might encounter. \t\t\t\t\t
