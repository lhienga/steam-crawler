\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDark Souls with guns....except Dark Souls is better.

It's not that Remnant is a bad game, it's just not a great one. The story and characters are just plain weird, the combat is smooth, but lacks any real skill other than aim and shoot fast. I think a cover mechanic (like The Division or Gears of War) would add massively to this game. The environments are about 2 colours per zone which gets dull fast. I spend a lot of time running around rather than fighting, but then when you do finally meet enemies it feels as though it's just a spam of adds.

That said, the RPG element is light but well done, the game runs beautifully, even on an older machine and the power up mechanics of each weapon is good. Plus the RNG of enemies in each playthrough differs, which is a nice touch.

Worth getting if on a good sale (if you like the sound of a dumbed-down Dark Souls with guns), but it's very much not worth full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemnant successfully melds the souls like formula with third person gunplay. Shooting is responsive and impactful, and each weapon has a distinct feel to it. The melee combat is serviceable if not basic, and was definitely intended to take the back seat.

The boss battles are a mixed bag. While some are great (Ixillis, Totem Father, Riphide), a lot of the time their design is basic, and to compensate they throw endless waves of trash mobs at you to divert your attention. It felt like a cheap way of raising difficulty, and is thankfully something they're working on reducing in the upcoming sequel.

The visual presentation is superb, and there were some set pieces in the game that were awe inspiring, namely when you first encounter the labyrinth.

The writing on the other hand lacks the same level of imagination. I completed the game yesterday, and if you put a gun to my head right now, I couldn't name one character, or tell you what the story was about. It's become almost a staple in souls like games, where every character has to be an eccentric loner that speaks in vague, cryptic metaphors. A little bit of mystery is good, but you have to give the player a reason to care enough to uncover it. You need emotional stakes to get people invested, themes of redemption, revenge, love etc. Without it, the story just meanders pointlessly.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is like a little, tasty snack you take at 4 pm. Good gun play, a few RPG elements, rewarding exploration with a few useful trinkets to find scattered in the maps, and souls-like boss battles - including "camp fires/crystals" where you regenerate health and ammo. Though this can be considered carbon copy from Dark Souls, Remnant's take on the game mechanics feels fresh enough.

The main campaign is done rather quickly and is merely a linear story punctuated by the boss battle. These range from rather easy in the lowest difficulty, to unwinnable in difficulty settings above "Difficult" (or I am simply not talented enough). The design of these boss fights is not souls-like at all : whereas Dark Souls relied on good observation and quick reactions while you were fighting ONE or TWO bosses, the Remnant bosses summon ADDS to pester you. In most fights, you will die from the adds... A highly debatable design choice imho.

The so called adventure mode allows for quick and entertaining shooting parties, the procedurally generated maps prevent the game to become stale all to soon.

All in all, decent entertainment.

Still, there is one thing that really bugs me : the background story of the game has MUCH more potential and would definitely deserve a proper, more developped narration. The ideas behind it have the stuff of premium SF/fantasy novels. But obiously, this was not the focus of the game studio.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very fresh take on the souls-like genre, Remnant: From the Ashes reintroduces many of the familiar components like enemies, boss encounters, RPG mechanism and gunplay and adds the amazing aspect of procedural generated over-worlds and dungeons. This makes every maps feel somewhat different every time you play it. Some events and boss fights also trigger in certain map re-rolls, and I really love that.

I played the whole game solo (despite the game very much encouraging co-op gameplay), and I did draw on some conclusions on that. Seeing some reviewers on youtube, I felt that playing this game on co-op has a very different appeal to it, and completely apart from the feeling and sense of achievement you get when playing it solo.

This game is hard, really hard on some cases when u try to take on bosses and Adds at once; as in solo, all the enemy focuses on you. There were times when I was swarmed with Adds- all within close proximity, the boss charging towards me with ranged fire attacks while me burning and my armour corroded, and the surrounding with tar pools ablaze. It was hell.

This game requires heavy space management, and an attention; not only towards the bosses, but towards spawning Adds and environmental hazards. You need proper coordination and a sense of presence in the battle arena when you try to confront a boss. Playing solo in Remnant is tough, and feels very souls-like punishing if not careful.
These, actually becomes easier when you do the same runs on co-op. As in there people can focus on different aspects and manage to dish out damage to boss and adds administratively. The game becomes more organised, as teammates can heal, pull aggro and even revive; which is not possible in solo, as death there means restarting the boss fight entirely. Remnant in co-op actually holds much more resemblance to co-op shooter games than souls-like ones, at least in my opinion seeing people play.

Overall, I really enjoyed my time playing Remnant: From the Ashes, as learning the world generations, boss patterns, environment management and space creation between boss mechanisms was very rewarding. Playing this game solo was tough, not gonna lie, but it was worth it; as it was highly rewarding when I was finally able to fell world bosses alone on hard difficulties, able to make a character I have fun playing with, and a sense of awe every time I re-roll a map event I was not expecting.

We are all dreamers in this world of memories.\t\t\t\t\t
