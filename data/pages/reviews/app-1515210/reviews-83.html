\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll rusty lakes games are great, and this is no exception.
The offline synchronous puzzle approach is super unique, and really really awesome and well executed.
The vibe is slightly different compared to past games, and it maybe felt a bit shorter than some of the previous cube escape installments, but that's bearing in mind that there's roughly double the content.

I also think the reduced run-length is a little bit due to the puzzle loops being a bit tighter by natures of the gameplay loop revolving around communicating information to your partner in order to progress- there was a lot less clicking around & trying to figure out what comes next.

By the same token, the puzzles usually felt pretty straightforward - easier than usual, the core gameplay mechanic seemed to revolve less around lateral thinking and more around communication.

Overall, it's an awesome and unique addition to the series, and as someone who mainly plays with a friend, it felt perfect.

I'm keen to see the format repeated, maybe with a bit more depth of puzzles and a bit more plot.\t\t\t\t\t
