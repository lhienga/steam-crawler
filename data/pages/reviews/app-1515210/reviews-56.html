\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIMO - If you've played similar types of games, the puzzles in this game are pretty simple and not really challenging at all. Most of it is waiting for directions from your partner and very little actually figuring out puzzles or clues yourself. If you're into the Rusty Lake lore/story, it may be worth it, but as a puzzle game I wasn't invested enough to even want to swap the roles to play through again. In this aspect it is much weaker than the We Were Here series for example, or even Tick Tock: A Tale for Two, where on top of cooperation and exchanging information, you still need to use your brain to individually problem solve. This is further exacerbated by the fact that it's a de-synced/offline game where real time actions from your Partner does not affect you, and progress from one side is completely hindered if the other side hasn't caught up. If your Partner is stuck, you can't just go explore some other area to entertain yourself even for a little while, saving is dependent on both of you reaching the end of the chapter, there's no mid-chapter checkpoints, and it's not even clear during first playthrough if your progress will be saved.

It's a good effort from Rusty Lake for their first attempt at a co-op puzzle and I hope they continue to explore and iterate on co-op type games, but overall it was weak in both the co-op and puzzle aspect of gameplay for me.\t\t\t\t\t
