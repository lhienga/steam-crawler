\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Past Within is a point and click co-op only puzzle game. What this game lacks in polish, it more than makes up for in accessibility. Picture this, you have a bestie above all the restie, but said bestie does not have a gaming PC or current gen console. This has led to you either forgoing co-op games altogether or playing them with non-bestie material plebs. Yuck. Well, no more! The Past Within is just the game for you and your bestie! It seems generous to call this game cross-platform only because it is due to the simplicity of the game itself that allows you, a PC or current gen console gamer, to play with anyone who possesses a smart phone or tablet. In fact, all you need to play this game is a device that can run apps and a communication app like discord. I suppose you could also just use your smart phone as a regular ol’ dumb phone and call your bestie while playing, but who talks on the phone anymore? Barf-o-rama!

But that’s what’s key here. The beauty of this game is in its simplicity. The team delivers a game that proves you don’t need all the latest bells and whistles in order to engross players in a unique, thrilling experience. All my partner and I had were cryptic clues and our trust in each other, and that was all we needed to unravel the mystery laid out for us by the ever-elusive Albert Vanderbloom. You see, the main task you are faced with is working with a trusted compadre who is not only distances away, but also years apart. Yes, one of you must tackle the problem from the past while the other approaches it from the future. Throughout the game, you must pass along relevant information to one another as you solve puzzle after puzzle and get closer to activating what seems to be some sort of resurrection machine. But just who on earth are you resurrecting? And should you be doing it?

Questions aside, something important I didn’t realize while playing the game but have come to learn now is that this is Rusty Lake’s first attempt at a co-op game, and I have to say, in a certain regard, they’ve bottled lightning. Is the game limited by such a simplistic format? Sure. You literally have 2 opportunities to play through the game with unique puzzle solutions. They are not randomly generated, so once you’ve played through the game from either timeline, you and your bestie (or someone else, I don’t know your life and the potentially shaky ground your relationships may be founded on) will have to switch roles and opt for the alternate set of puzzle solutions differentiated by bee and butterfly paths. That’s it. So there’s some replayability but not a lot. But I still think Rusty Lake nails something pivotal about the optimal co-op experience that other bigger developers don’t always get right – it really does take both players to beat the game. I can think of a number of co-op experiences where that isn’t necessarily true; where, in fact, one teammate can be carried through the game. And, since my dumb ass is usually the one being carried, I can make this claim with a degree of credibility.

So, if you’re looking for a unique and engrossing co-op experience to try out with your current bestie or perhaps someone who just needed the right bonding event to become your new bestie, look no further. What this game lacks in polish and replayability, it more than makes up for in intrigue, good storytelling, and a solid understanding of what makes a good co-op game. You’re not gonna want to let this one pass you by! But if you still need a little more convincing, check out the free demo first!\t\t\t\t\t
