\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game doesn't have a single puzzle in it.

The gameplay loop consists of:

1. Searching around like a madman for inputs/outputs that sync up with your partner's.
2. Repeating step-by-step basic instructions to your partner or receiving instructions and doing exactly what your partner/the game tells you to do.
3. Repeat for 2 hours

Also, you might want to consider reducing how much you make the players repeat tasks. There's a reason why, in most games, players are tested on new concepts simultaneously with ramping difficulty or complexity until they master it and can integrate it into other game elements.

This game does no such thing. Instead, you're made to repeat chores with your partner over and over again with little variation or challenge. The "challenge" lies in the fact that you cannot see your partner's screen.

For example, there's a chore where you read clock hands to your partner and they set a clock accordingly on their end. I know how to set a clock, so it wasn't even fun the first time. Why then, game, do you make us do it like eight ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ times? WE ALREADY MASTERED THE ART OF SETTING CLOCKS, GAME.

There are many other small tasks that players are made to repeat many times, such as the XYZ puzzles or the dials with symbols on them. This is genuinely upsetting and shows a lack of respect for the player's time. Many tasks are repeated over and over again, and not a single one of them constitutes a puzzle or requires critical thinking.

It almost feels like padding to exceed the refund window. And bravo, you got me, game. I am aware it's only $6, but it feels like I am owed money for playing this to the end.

Arguably, the most critical thinking you'll use in this game is filtering what information is relevant to your partner. However, this mostly devolves into a brute force approach where we just rattle off everything we see, and it quickly becomes annoying.

The initial premise was interesting, and the atmosphere enhanced it, but next to nothing was done with it in this work, leaving a hollow shell of nothing happening. I understand there's a narrative that's expanded upon in your other works that is probably terribly interesting, but if it means playing more games like this, I'll have to pass.

That being said, I would be unfair not to say that my partner really enjoyed it and wants to do another playthrough. Maybe it's simply not to my taste, but this is not a game I ever want to play again.

Anyway. This was a novelty and the 2-player stuff was innovative, so, thanks for making it. I just wish somebody had warned me.\t\t\t\t\t
