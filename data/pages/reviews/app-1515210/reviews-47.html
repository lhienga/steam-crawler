\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA short little co-op experience that mainly involves communication over puzzle solving, some hints at a deeper lore and a great atmosphere make for a great experience that doesn't overstay it's welcome.

The story was quite enjoyable, not in your face and it reminded me of the lovecraft story The Case of Charles Dexter Ward

The main cons are that the initial room feels quite empty and the graphics aren't quite up to par, this is mostly fixed in the latter parts of the game though, and that the co-op is almost entirely about communication over puzzle solving.

The asymmetric multiplayer doesn't really add to the replayability but there is apparently a second campaign available but I have not played it. In addition due to the way the game is made, interaction is limited to scripted exchanges, instead of the real time experience of other games in the genre. For the same reasons the game does progress quite linearly I feel, there is almost never more than one thing to be doing.

I got it on a sale for half off, at that price point it is a steal but it still feels quite fairly price at 6$.

I would wholeheartedly recommend it if you have a friend.\t\t\t\t\t
