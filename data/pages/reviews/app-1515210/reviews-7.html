\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: Before reading this, know that I just learned of a new scam. People are playing this game and then pretending to only have the lite/demo version. If that's true, you shouldn't be able to finish the game with them. I finished the game with someone who still made that claim. They then asked for my Steam info so they could download the game and then give control back to me. I wasn't born yesterday, so I said no. But people should know that this game is being used as part of a scam.

I love this franchise, so I hate leaving this review. For that reason, I'll start by saying this. If you're just a total Rusty Lake junkie, this game does include one story element that you might really enjoy.

By every other metric, however, it's terrible. Since it wants to pretend it's a co-op game, let's start with that.

If you play this game as intended, you're in for a rough time. It's great that Rusty Lake has an international fanbase. You know when that's not great, however? When you're relying on someone who speaks limited English to yell symbols at you on a timed puzzle. One of those players only has to relate basic images. The other has to relate abstract symbols. Between two people who speak the same language, that'll still go over without much drama. But if you have to use the Discord to find another player, you're almost guaranteed to be stuck with someone who struggles to describe these symbols. And this happens about 75% of the way through the game, so you might have communicated fine until this. It's only because this one puzzle is randomly timed that it slows your progress to a halt.

So why won't communication hurt you outside of this one puzzle? Because putting language barriers on a timer is the only thing that makes it challenging. There's no challenge to any other puzzle in the game. You do some basic point and click until you uncover a code. Then you read that code to your partner. They put in that code and then they're given a code to read back to you. This happens repeatedly. There's very little puzzle differentiation in the game. You'll wind up dealing with the same three puzzles a couple dozen times, while reading back the same couple dozen to your partner. I don't mean there are dozens of puzzles. I mean there are dozens of instances where you read off the same 2 or 3. The game might be less than half as long if not for this repetition.

The idea of a co-op Rusty Lake game is nice. But this was not the right execution. It's never difficult and needing help from others never feels like a clever mechanic. It's more of a frustration than anything. The way this plays into the story is kind of neat, but it does so little now that Underground Blossom is behind us that I almost wonder if they shouldn't just kill Rusty Lake and start over with a new story.

Oh, and the one thing I left out. My play time would be shorter if not for the glitches. Looking for the bee? Sometimes it just glitches out and doesn't land where you need for the achievement. Playing with a partner? Sometimes their future cube glitches out and the screen won't move forward when they hit enter. Since your games aren't even technically connected, it'll probably take you quite a while to realize their game is glitched. Then you have to wait for them to back out and come back in so they can tell you some things that don't make sense. I waited for my partner to come back so he could tell me that we needed to click a cube at the same time. Spoiler alert, you don't. Click the cube whenever you want. Your progress does not rely on your partner. That entire aspect of the game is a lie. Not sure why no one realizes this. There's even an achievement guide on this site that doesn't realize how little it matters what your partner is doing.

I wanted to like this game. It's just not very likable. And sorry for breaking away from the fandom, but it feels necessary. If you really care about these games, then allowing them to release a glitchy mess with broken time constraints is exactly what they deserve. Hold them accountable to make better games. A few good ones doesn't justify this kind of backslide.\t\t\t\t\t

It's just like Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes, but bad.


The Past Within showed initial promise for excellence but unfortunately fell short on various aspects, resulting in a sense of disappointment for both myself and my partner.

While the puzzle difficulty initially presented an engaging tutorial-like experience, this positive start quickly devolved into a consistently lackluster gameplay. The so-called puzzles lacked creativity, required zero actual puzzle solving and essentially boiled down to a repetitive exchange of codes. I don't think we had a single thought throughout the gameplay, really. It felt as if the developers were just trying to stretch the gameplay to hit the two-hour mark, appearing more as an attempt to avoid refunds than a commitment to delivering a meaningful gaming experience.

The initially intriguing storyline turned out to be a shallow facade, failing to evolve beyond superficial allure and leaving us with a sense of unfulfilled anticipation for depth.

Graphically, The Past Within falls short. While the 3D elements boast commendable stylization, the 2D aesthetics feel reminiscent of a dated 2008 flash game – a design choice that would have been questionable even in that era.

As the narrative unfolded, the attempt at a profound, inception-like moment felt cringe-worthy rather than thought-provoking. The forced "deep" philosophical twist failed to resonate and left me with a lingering sense of discomfort.

If you do decide to try it, wait for a discount to soften the blow of potential disappointment.

2/10\t\t\t\t\t
