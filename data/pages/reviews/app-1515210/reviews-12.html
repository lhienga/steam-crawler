The Past Within: A Unique Gaming Experience
En extremely short yet entertaining and challenging little game.
This review will be short and concise, just like the game is.
The better you comunicate with your partner the shorter the game gets.
Although the game explores some dark themes and possesses a deeper lore than initially apparent, it is not the primary focus of the game. The central gaming experience is centered on communication between partners and the ability of both players to articulate details about their surroundings, key interactive elements, and the overall situation. Playing this game with my wife, we occasionally found ourselves stuck because one of us overlooked or didn't consider certain information as crucial. These minor details, compounded by communication challenges, led to difficulties in progressing through specific parts—confirming the notion that 'the devil is in the detail.'

Game replayability
The game presents players with four potential paths, categorized into time paths and path versions. In both the past and future timelines, there are two variations: version A featuring the bee and version B featuring the butterfly. This allows players to go through the game four times, each experience distinct from the others.

In conclusion:
It offers a highly enjoyable experience that challenges your ability to express yourself clearly and actively listen to your gaming partner.
The game allows the player to explore it four times without repetition of puzzles.\t\t\t\t\t

We just finished the bee and the butterfly, changing roles of who was in the past and who was in the future and I really didn't see any difference between the two. The puzzles and solutions seemed exactly the same. So it was a bit of a let down the 2nd time through as you know what to expect. My last gripe with the game is that the puzzles aren't really puzzles or very challenging when the game is constantly telling you what to do. There were no AHA moments for me...instead of solving a mystery it just feels like you are reading instructions to the other person instead of putting together clues. It is kind of neat how they manage to make a 2 player game without a direct in-game connection, but the novelty did wear out quickly. In a lot of cases, the sequencing between me and my gaming partner was often broken by one person jumping ahead while the other lagged behind. I think something like this would work better with a direct in-game connection so that the sequences are directly in sync.\t\t\t\t\t
