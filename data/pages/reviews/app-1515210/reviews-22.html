Cons

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying the Past Within is an interesting feeling, it has a lot in common with the We Were Here games but it really captures the escape room feeling. The puzzle box one player is operating feels more like "Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes". And in the middle of it all, there is the strange feeling that no part of the game is networked, which somehow makes the game feel like the candlelit version of couch coop. Asking the other player if they are done already could be seen as a normally unnecessary step, but to me made it feel more intimate.

Definite recommendation for anyone who likes what I mentioned above, or escape room board games. Admittedly, I don't like Rusty Lake that much, but I felt the intensity of the puzzles here was lower than in those games, and therefore perfect for such a game. The vibe is spooky but never scary. All in all, a unique game in its small genre, and I hope we will see more of this!

P.S. Being able to play the 3D part in VR would be incredible!\t\t\t\t\t
