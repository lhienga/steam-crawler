\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is passable to kill an hour or two with someone you like and want an excuse to interact with. That's about it. The "puzzles" basically amount to extremely simple exchanges of code inputs. It's very similar to Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes, except there's no element of garbling the information with tricky ambiguity, so you don't even need to be clever or thoughtful in how you communicate. You and your partner could be extremely drunk and blow through this game. It's intellectually unrewarding.

I've followed Rusty Lake games from the beginning and played all of them. This one disappointed me and I didn't feel it was worth the money. The surrealist horror aspect was weak. I don't mind boring puzzle design if the imagery/events are sufficiently bizarre and exciting, but this was underwhelming. The plot was also quite vague and unsatisfying.

In my opinion, this game just functions as an easy vehicle for engaging with a friend and providing a sense of intrigue, and the experience can be pretty enjoyable in that regard. But the game itself beyond the co-op aspect isn't challenging or memorable. I hate to give a Rusty Lake game a negative review, but this just felt like a subpar entry in the series.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was a fun twist on escape room style puzzle games. We play a lot of them, and usually you work together in a much more straight forward way, seeing the same room and puzzles. This game you don't, thus it involves a lot of communication.

Reading the description, I thought the game would have actual past-present mechanics, but it didn't really have any. Despite that, the game was quite fun and certainly is worth playing. It took me and my wife 105 minutes to beat the game.

You can play again with different answers to the puzzles, but I'm assuming that this means that the puzzles are the same just with different solutions so you can't have them memorized. I don't see this as adding any additional value, and consider this a one play through game. Given that, and having to buy two copies, 12 dollars is probably a bit much for less than two hours. On sale though, I'd say it's worth it if you are looking for a fairly easy experience that mostly involves communication.\t\t\t\t\t
