\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is so beautiful in a Rusty Lake kind of way. The co-op aspect of it makes it so you HAVE to communicate and work together to proceed to the next task. There are parts that make you somewhat stressed because it makes it seem like you're running out of time. Communication, keeping calm and explaining what you see and what you are currently doing is key to this game all together.

The game is so much fun, and if you like Rusty Lake games, and have played the previous ones, you're gonna love this.
If you're not familiar with the previous games, and know nothing of the backstory surrounding the characters, timelines, places or nothing, the game is not going to make a lot of sence.

The ending makes sence and it makes you feel like you want more. Then again, the feeling of wanting more out of it makes it feel like it's not finished. My first reaction was "is that it?", but it honestly doesn't need any greater ending. You get there, you do the objective of the game, and that's it. The feeling of wanting more just makes it so that the developers can add onto the story line, make up for more unsolved mysteries and more content for us to enjoy!

I really do recommend playing through both the past and the future with the same partner.
There are objectives to be found both with bee and with butterfly, and if you want to achievement hunt, settle for playing through it at least 4 times in total. 2 times in the past and 2 times in the future. Once as bee and once as butterfly.

The objectives will stay the same, but the solutions change from each time. You will not be able to write down the solutions from an older play through, and use it to play alone in the next. Either way, this game is so fun with the communication aspect of it, that you would not want to play it alone.

In conclution; it's a great game!\t\t\t\t\t

There's not much else to say here. If you enjoyed previous Rusty Lake games, you'll like this one, and if you're looking for something with more substance, check out the We Were Here series.

4/5 - My fix for surrealism is satisfied.\t\t\t\t\t
