The past or the future?

The Past Within is a co-op game, but with a slightly different twist than a typical co-op game. In the game, we have to uncover the secrets of Albert Vanderboom, a deceased man, and in order to discover them we have to join forces with another player who is in the future or the past (the choice depends on us and our partner). On the other hand, we don't need to connect with the other player to complete further puzzles, but only need text or voice communication to do so.

In general, the game features well-crafted puzzles that require communication with another player to complete. They are often based on the mapping of some patterns. They are not difficult puzzles, but completing each successive one is really satisfying. They are also logical and related to the game's plot. However, over time they can become monotonous.

The graphics of the game are very nice. The game has its own extravagant style, and depending on whether you play in the future or the past, you play in either 2D or 3D mode, which is intuitive and I didn't encounter any bugs in either mode.

Sound-wise it is also very good, we have dubbing and a pleasant soundtrack, on top of that the ambient sounds are equally good.

We can go through the game from two perspectives and, on top of that, by choosing one of the insects, which gives a total of four possible passages of the game, although they are not too different from each other. However, the choice of insects is not very important, it slightly changes some of the puzzles. Therefore, I recommend going through the game twice: in the future and in the past.

Summary

The Past Within is a really good game. It is not a game that is difficult, the subsequent puzzles were not a difficulty for me and my friend, but we had fun playing it, which is what it is all about. Plus the lack of bugs, a good atmosphere and the evening passed quickly and pleasantly.

✔️Specific, nice graphics
✔️The game is even atmospheric
✔️The sound design is really good
