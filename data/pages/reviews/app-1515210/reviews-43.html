\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"The Past Within" is a small co-op puzzle game that will give you some entertaining time when trying to communicate with your friend, explaining what you see and describing strange patterns. It has a nice combination of 2d and 3d graphics, good pacing, eerie atmosphere, cool music and a weird backstory. For sure puzzles could be more creative and challenging, while online co-op with true interactions between players would make it more immersive. Yet, it's a cool way to spend a couple of hours with a friend.


Manometer: 7/10


The good
- Cute visuals. It combines cleverly 2d and 3d graphics. Nice design of the interactive objects and especially those mechanical parts of the cubic machines.
- You'll have some fun time when trying to communicating with your friend, explaining him what you see, describing various codes or symbols until you find the solution.
- Puzzles could be more challenging and creative, but they have pretty good pacing. Each puzzle gives you enough clues to guide you seamlessly to the next one. Each player constantly needs to give information to the other one in order to proceed so there are no big gaps that one of you gets bored by doing nothing.
- Simplistic, but cool and mysterious small story that keeps you interested till the end.
- It has a weird, eerie atmosphere with some creepy moments.
- Nice calm music.

The bad
- Puzzles have a few interesting mechanics, but they are pretty easy and not so creative. They're mostly pattern matches, and codes based on descriptions between players. If they describe things well, puzzles' get solved almost instantly. It doesn't require much though or imagination. It doesn't have clever objects combinations.
- It could be so much better with online co-op where the actions of one player affect automatically his mate's room. It would be much more immersive and would eliminate the need of constantly exchanging codes.
- Small duration and no replay value even if you try to change roles with your friend.\t\t\t\t\t
