\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoved the game, but the price point makes the game a hard pill to swallow.

Don't get me wrong, $6 (or whatever your regional price might be) isn't bad and I've bought worse games for more, but its not REALLY $6, its $12. Sure you can play it with a total stranger but if you plan on playing with a friend you both need to own the game. Theres no real way to play this by yourself. That being said if your buying two copys, one for you and one for a friend, its a must by on sale.

Over-all, I'd recommend it. I've played all the rusty lake games so far in one way or another and each one is just as great as the last. Even the shorter ones like this one is fantastic and while its hard to replay it, it still HAS the ability to replay it. If you plan on doing more of these, it might be worth including a second steam key with purchase or put into practice a similar system as the co-op only It Takes Two.\t\t\t\t\t

Past Blast!
The past within is a very enjoyable coop puzzle game that requires you to find the solution through communication. While you never have to actually connect to your friend you will be prompted at the beginning of the game to start the game together and choose the same path to sync up the solutions. This style of asymmetrical puzzle experience has been done before but this way of setting up the game is quite smart.

The puzzles themselves are rewarding to put together but not never frustrating. I'd imagine this would be a good game to get someone into gaming as it can be played together even if the other person is using their phone. I really enjoyed the look of the game, it gives you a very foreboding feeling. It doesn't look like something you would be able to interact with so its neat when you get to do so. Id love to see more games that use this style in 3D spaces!

Its also a fairly simple 100% aside from the "Squeak" achievement that gave me a little trouble. Going for the full completion is a fun way to see both sides of the game. Its well worth doing so even if your not into achievements.

In conclusion: If you need a fun and easy to run coop game pick this up!\t\t\t\t\t
