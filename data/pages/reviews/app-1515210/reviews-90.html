\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort and Dirty:
-Lots of "find the combination" style puzzles
-Fairly easy (we completed both the Butterfly and Bee versions while exchanging roles, in about 2.5 hours total)
-I would price this at $3.99 considering both parties need to buy a copy.
-Great addition if you like the Rusty Lake series
-Fun hearing the person on the other end get creeped out by what they're seeing

I think this game would be good for those new to these types of games, or wanting a casual experience, and/or are fans of the Rusty Lake series (I personally found some of the previous entries more challenging).

I find a lot of escape room puzzle style games get tedious and over-reliant with the "find the key/combination" to proceed. To an extent there's no 'escaping' this, but I do appreciate when games try to deviate from 'clock' puzzles ("The Inheritance of Crimson Manor" is probably one of the worst offenders for how many clock puzzles they have). The Past Within at least mixes it up a tiny bit in this area, but not too much. As such we went through the game twice, though both modes and switching roles, in about an hour+ for each run. While we found it fun, and we both enjoyed the Rusty Lake series, we both were left a bit "hungry" after. I'm not sure if there's even a save and restore feature to return later if someone has to leave (though given how short it is, I suspect this game was only ever intended to be completed in one sitting)

Things I would improve on is making the Bee and Butterfly modes different from each other, rather than the same thing but with very minor changes (which seems to mostly just be different code sequences). I'm sure this was to allow for people to change roles and experience the story from the other side, but I had hoped for more than just that. I feel the current 5.99 price is a bit high given the experience, very limited replayability, and that both parties need to buy a copy.\t\t\t\t\t
