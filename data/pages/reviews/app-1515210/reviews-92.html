The cooperative nature of gameplay is truly absorbing and fascinating, with the story being somewhat on the same level, as in other games in the series (by "somewhat on the same level" I mean that it's just as puzzling, confusing and mysterious at first glance :3).
And now that I've mentioned "puzzling", sadly I felt a strong lack of actual puzzles in the game. I wouldn't necessarily call the process of constant reporting of certain information to your partner in order to proceed a puzzle element, especially since it's always plain obvious, what information to deliver and how to interpret it. It's still fascinating in the first playthrough, but that fascination slowly fades away after a while, when you realize that it's all there is. And to be fair, I only mind it so much because of how short the game is, too. Completing the game twice, in two different modes, amounted to a bit over 2 hours of playtime, so, combined with inevitable monotony of gameplay, it made the ending feel quite abrupt and not properly earned.
Now, these points are in no way "fails" of the game, but a ground for development in the future: the general concept has firmly proven itself to be great, in my opinion, so hopefully it gets more complex further on. Same for replayability feature too, by the way. Different solutions to same puzzles is nice and welcome, but different puzzles entirely would be even cooler! (Something like in "Cube Escape: Paradox", same environment with different puzzles in different chapters.) With all that said, I definetely recommend this game to fans of the series, it's still the same unique pleasant gaming experience, which gets additionally doubled down due to sharing this experience with another player (even if mandatory :3). As for newcomers, I'd recommend to get yourself familiar with the rest of the series first, that'll give you the comprehensive impression of its tone and style, then get back to "The Past Within" for dessert :)
All in all, I sincerely commend "Rusty Lake" team's passion for not only upgrading their series with each new entry, but also experimenting with it, like with "White Door" and now "The Past Within". This franchise is very dear to my heart, and I'm looking forward to whatever comes next!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is mostly just translating data and telling the other one what is on the screen. I would not call it a real puzzle game per se. It felt mostly mind-numbing 'work' and not taking the player as an intelligent human being. If I would compare it to a game, it would be the Room, but it doesn't reach that level. There really isn't much to think about and I wonder what they could have spend the 3 years development on? The idea to talk with the other player to solve puzzles could have generated very interesting puzzles, but... it just did not happen. As well as the story - there were elements of horror and suspense, but they did not blossom at all (pun intended). I don't know why this got such good reviews, I reckon it's because people are starving for co-op games, and this at least gives some interaction between friends.\t\t\t\t\t
