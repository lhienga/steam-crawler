TL;DR
The Past Within is based on a strong time disparity gimmick which is fun to explore and also does not overstay it’s welcome. A strong contender to enjoy some hours with a buddy, but not an outstanding puzzle game.

Rusty Lake Lore
Having not played the past titles, I was really confused by the presentation of the game. There is lore and a whole world of which you can only catch a glimpse with this game.
Most stuff does not make much sense as one can not see the big picture and only gets the fundamental ideas. Because of that I would recommend checking out more Rusty Lake, if you are interested in actually understanding the plot.
The imagery is disturbing and works well enough in scaring the player without resorting into cheap jump scares, the game is mostly just calm yet menacing.

The Gimmick
One can not talk about this game without mentioning the gimmick. Both players start the game (without necessarily connecting to the internet, except for the purpose of talking with each other) and then solve relatively simple puzzles, to then hand their earned information to the other player.
These are only difficult, if you are having communication problems or do not know what you can interact with. Most of the time the game is smooth sailing, and it can and should be completed in one sitting. Approximately it takes around two to three hours to finish the game.
The gimmick is really the main selling point and otherwise The Past Within is relatively unimpressive for the most part. The puzzles need to stay simple as explaining something without seeing can be too frustrating. Rusty Lake (=the developers) did well in designing like that, but it still lacks puzzles that really ask the player to (excuse the pun) think outside the box. It is not about being insanely smart, more of an:
I wanted more unconventional puzzles than following a step to step guide which leads to one player communicating some information to the other player in a specific order.

Anything Else?
Not really, if I am honest. The visual style is interesting, and the sound design works pretty well for the most part, while it uses certain techniques to flip the script on the player by understanding how to present itself. While it is a masterclass at presentation, it is really difficult to find anything which can be called outstanding (except for the gimmick).
No insane positives, but also no insane negatives.

Conclusion
You got some hours to burn with a buddy of yours and are interested in time-related games?
The Past Within is a great game to enjoy the fun of spending time together while somewhat using your brain cells and inhaling the ambience.
