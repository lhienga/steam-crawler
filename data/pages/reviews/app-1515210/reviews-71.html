\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game fits the narrative and flow of the Rusty Lake universe, which is what initially pulled me into getting it. While it did have their charm and art styles, it felt considerably easier than the other games. As others have said, it is more of a "read this to the other player, wait for them to read next step back" progression. There were a few times of having to search something out, or find where the missing piece went. However, it lacked the deeper mental games and puzzles that RL is known for.

With that said, it was still a fun game for the $5 price point. My buddy and I only played the butterfly side, so I have yet to see how the bee is different (beyond what they tell you is different). I would recommend this game as the puzzles are well constructed and flow nicely, but just don't expect it to have the length or complexity of the previous RL games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game can be summed up as Rusty Lake meets Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes. The problem is it ends up stuck in the middle, being sub-par on both of these ends. The coop tasks almost always consist of simply relaying simple codes to your partner and vice-versa. And the Rusty Lake-y parts (point-and-click escape room) are much easier and simpler than in their other games. While this ease is understandable in a coop game (otherwise one player would be bored to death while waiting for the other to solve a puzzle), it doesn't make for a good game. The story is nice, albeit very simple.

I did have fun playing The Past Within, but I was expecting a deeper, more meaningful coop experience. The fact that you need to buy two copies of the game doesn't help either. I'd only recommend this game to hardcore fans of Rusty Lake; otherwise, you're not missing out on much.\t\t\t\t\t
