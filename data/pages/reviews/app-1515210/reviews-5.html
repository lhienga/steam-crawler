\r\n\t\t\t\t\t My Experience

Although The Past Within didn't end up making my top 10 for 2022, it was definitely a contender. I'm always a sucker for asymmetric co-op, and The Past Within shares the cool design of Tick Tock: A Tale for Two in that two players can play together without actually being connected online. I played the "Past" side, while my co-op partner played the "Future." I'm unsure what difference it ends up making, but we selected the bee instead of the butterfly at the beginning of the game.

I loved the illustrated characters, the items, and the environments in the game, as well as the auditory and visual effects that occur at key shocking moments. The whole thing feels like a creepy voodoo ritual, which does seem to jive with the universe of Rusty Lake from my understanding (I haven't played any other Rusty Lake games). Solving this macabre puzzle box was really unlike anything I've done in a game before, and I had a lovely time doing so.

Another thing I came to appreciate more while watching the "Let's Play" is the way that objects and actions in the past correspond to those in the future, as certain streamers were able to bounce between the perspective of the Past and Future player. At one point, the player in the future starts playing some beat-heavy music. In response, the furniture within the room of the player in the past begins inexplicably (from your perspective) bumping and shaking around (which helps unlock a way forward).

In general, the puzzles are quite well-designed in the game. They're intuitive without being easy, and I felt a sense of satisfaction at each critical story juncture. The only type of player I wouldn't recommend this game to would be a solo gamer since it's inherently a cooperative experience. However, if you have a bud to take on the ride, you should take it. It's also relatively cheap and short, so it's easy to fit in if you have a lot going on.\t\t\t\t\t
