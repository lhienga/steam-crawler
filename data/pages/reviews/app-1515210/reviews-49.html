Genre
  • Co-op communications puzzler in the vein of We Were Here, Tick Tock: A Tale for Two, and The Timeless Child
  • The players are isolated from each other, using voice chat to communicate what they see to help solve each other's puzzles. A basic example is one player must describe a set of symbols so the other can enter them into a keypad.
  • There are elements of "hidden object" and "escape the room" genres, and there's inspiration from games like The Room and Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes

Gameplay
  • While you must choose a unique role when setting up the game, both players are actively puzzling as well as providing clues
  • A minority of the puzzles are timed, requiring you to coordinate their activation, and communicate / input quickly
  • The game is bizarrely half in 2D and half in 3D. The 2D section has you navigate by clicking arrows on the edge of the screen, and the 3D section uses click-and-drag to rotate smoothly. Both parts have you click on areas of interest to focus in on them.

Play time
It took 2.1 hours, but I think I could get at least another 2 hours out of it, as there's the other role, and also the alternative puzzle solution feature.

Why I'm recommending it
  • I enjoyed it. It gave us a few scares and a few laughs
  • The puzzles felt well designed and engaging
  • The Rusty Lake quirkiness makes it unpredictable
