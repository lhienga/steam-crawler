\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is quite good for its price, the atmosphere manages to immerse you in the game, while you cooperate with your partner. This got to the point in which my partner prompted me to "look around, see if anything has changed since he touched something"... to which I reminded him that "this game has no actual multiplayer capabilities".

Any game that manages to fool its players into thinking that it does something it actually doesn't should be highly regarded in my opinion, and this one does just exactly that.

My biggest complains:
- Perhaps we need to play additional games, but we were kinda dissatisfied with the ending, since we couldn't really make any sense of the story, feels like the game was lacking a chapter 3 or maybe as if we need to play other games to understand anything, which is a shame since initially the way the story was going piqued our curiosity really fast.
- (Kind of as expected, since its a simple coop game) There's really no replayability at all, all of the good points this game manages to convey to its players (all the "magical moments" in which two players cooperate as if there was a real multiplayer connection and the likes) just disappear when you try to replay the game. The game kinda makes you think that you can replay it by playing as either the past or the future, which kinda works but takes a lot of the coop magic away really... and that you can replay it again by choosing among two symbols (bee or butterfly) but we have seen little to no changes from playing butterfly or bee? the solutions to most of the puzzles seemed very similar.

All in all, highly recommended in general, just a shame we coouldn't make any sense of the story but the coop puzzles are well worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
