The game is not worth 55 euros, 30 euros would be a fair price.

The combat feels off at times, the enemies are fun to parry. Skeletons shuffle towards you and do long wind-up attacks, the corrupted peasants (zombies to me) are more faster and have more variety in their attacks, but still easily parried.
Goblins are horrible because of their short stature (about waist height), they aggro surrounding goblins when attacked. The goblins basicly attack your legs. Sometimes their weapon drags along the ground (seems to be a physics glitch) which makes their attacks hard to block or parry.
Some special enemies are like ninjas on cocaine, throwing weapons and doing wild sweeps.

Moving around takes ALOT of time. There's run and walk, but even "running" is way too slow for maps this large. There really should be a sprint option.

Magic is quite lackluster at the moment, starter spells don't really have any impact. Spells rarely cause status effects. You essentially "charge" your fist or wand with casts. The spells are very weak. Lvl 1 firebolt does around 1.5 damage per hit, lvl2 firebolt 2 dmg per hit. The enemies at the start have around 14-24 hp. I found a special enemy with 250 hp....... Hitting the enemy with the wand does more damage (5 dmg per hit). Aiming the spells is simple and I haven't really missed my targets. You point towards the target and the spell "auto-aims" a bit, the spells don't home in on enemies. The "bolt" spells (firebolt, icebolt and lightningbolt) inherit momentum from your fist or wand.

Ranged combat is quite nice and the bow is easy to aim. You appear to have unlimited arrows.

Melee combat is weird. Sometimes the enemies attacks slide off the shield and hit you. It seems to be better to "off-hand" a one-handed sword for parrying purposes. 2-handed weapons lack impact. Striking an enemy with a bardiche with full and fast motion rarely staggers the enemy. Parrying with 2-handed weapons is tough, but it might also be a skill issue.

Offensive stats level up by causing damage with their respective weapons/skills.

Each character level up gives you a point to allocate into hp, mana or luck. I have 5 points in luck because I though it would give me better loot, I haven't seen any change in loot quality.

Healing is quite annoying. Either drink a potion or teleport back to town and stand around a fire. Materials for healing potions seems to be quite uncommon.

Crafting is simple, choose the material and the amount of it and do the action.
Strike the bar for metals or turn the lathe for wood products.
You can upgrade a health potion with magic dust.
You can upgrade the quality of whetstones with magic dust.
You can improve the quality of crafted weapons by using a whetstone on it.
Careful though, magic dust is actually crystals which you have to break (squeeze) over an item, I learned this the hard way and sprinkled my only quest given magic dust on the ground.
Magic Dust seems to be quite rare...
You can combine 3 of the same materials at the cauldron to improve the material by 1 "rank".

The game is super dark. The player emits an aura of light, but past that its darkness.
It would be great to get a hip lantern, torch or magic light above you.
The darkness causes eyestrain for me.

Overall the game is ok, but not worth 55 euros.

Old review

Interesting and great foundation for a great game, but the lack of information is very off putting.
There's some bugs, jankyness and overall content that's missing.
I will gladly update my review when/if they actually deliver the game that was promised.
But for now I can't recommend this game to anyone.\t\t\t\t\t
