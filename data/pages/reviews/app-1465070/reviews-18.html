Guess devs really thought it's really fun to walk around with an auto aim pistol that deals little to nothing damage

And it also forces devs to make magic ignore shields (since you can't aim) which makes it EVEN MORE boring. And I would say more - it's even preventing you to hit moving enemies, since it doesn't count their current speed and you absolutely have NOTHING to do with this, because auto aim locks very hard, even when you are trying to anticipate enemies movement.

You won't find out nothing like different buffs/debuffs, intersting combinations, telekinesis, creating obstacles, anything that would give you even a tiny piece of sandbox and fun. Also a big problem - gestures are absolutely useless at the most of the spells, it always come down to a simple trigger pressing with a minor hand movement at some spells, and consider that you don't even need to aim at all. Current state of magic in this game should be reworked entirely to make it fun or at least interesting. It's even have a defensive skill which is basically like "press trigger and you will ignore one next damage event at any time"... And you don't even need to gesture anything lol
  • Why, for example. throwable ice spear can't be used in close combat like a melee weapon? Why fireballs not acting like a light source? why magic don't break pots, barrels etc? (how much it costs now? $55? Really?)
    I was thrilled when I saw that some high level skill which is throwable fire axe had a line in it's description stating that it could be either throwed or be used as a melee weapon. After 10 hours I get it and you know what? When used in melee it's just explodes and disappears yielding the same result as if it would've been thrown... And actually the same result as any other skill does... Meh...
  • Remember I told you that you won't try out different perks? So the devs "gifted" us one single perk reset (btw I didn't get it in my save lol), whilst saying THIS: "We are happy to help players who made mistake. I wish you learned something from your first mistake and don’t make same mistake again. Think enough before you invest points, use it wisely." ARE YOU SERIOUS?! This is just abnormal behaviour. The whole point of skill reset option - is to try out DIFFERENT play styles to make the game less BORING. Especially when you are oftenly fixing game balance now, tweaking out different perks (AND when the skill descriptions might be misleading, but I guess it was my mistake, right?;)). It's absolutely MUST be an option for some amount of in-game money, of course.

After 3 hours of the game I guess I spent most of the time simply walking.

It's like giant line filled with TONS of basic enemies. There are some "secrets" but they are not rewarding. Most of them contain closed chests, so you need a key for them, which are dropping RANDOMLY. And I would say keys are quite rare compared to amount of closed chests, I've opened like 20% of what I found.

  • Inventory system is a big MEH. There are multiple tabs, and to switch them you need to point at the small icons. At least quests could've been moved to some journal on your chest like in the walking dead
  • I'll say again that the game design here is poor: quests stating "go to <dungeon_name>" but not actually telling you where it's located. Since there are no map and your movement is quite slow - it's very frustrating. There should be stated some visual orientirs in the quest log. Or at least direction. Or maybe place some in-world signs.
  • Save&exit option in menu opens up two buttons: save and exit. Why would I want to exit the game without a saving? There should be a button which SAVES AND EXITS at once. Also there are no autosaves.
  • Release game kinda feels like Grimlord, but honestly Grimlord was much more interesting for me in terms of mechanics and level design, even though it's not fully developed yet. The only advantage of Legendary Tales for now - more content
  • If I were a new player I would just refund the game at the first hour, since there is nothing intersting at the start. Tutorial which is followed by walking through the too big landscape for no actual reason. Beta version was fun since you were placed in the intersting dungeon right at the start, where you can see all of the game advantages, and exploring was much more fun imo.

If you are a new player I would wait a bit before the game will be polished (seems like devs are actively doing it now). And I definetly wouldn't buy it at this price for now, considering that you better also find a friend to buy it too. Even knowing that the game will be intersting at some point (UPD The more I play this game - the more doubts I have about it) - I just too bored to actually get there. Can't recommend it for now
