\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't take my "Yes" too seriously. While I have high hopes for the game, and it is kind of fun, it is NOT the best medieval game out there. Now, I have not seen many like it, taking on the mantle of "VR Diablo" is pretty daring. Its being managed by a small dev team, so updates are rare. For what its worth, I think they are doing a pretty fair job.

Whats good about this game? The game is GORGEOUS! The texture, details, lighting, and particle effects are outstanding! Each floor of the dungeon is expansive, multi-leveled, and packed with danger and things to find (even secret rooms). Spell casting is basically easy mode, and you can even mix and match spells to enhance their effects or make new spells! The archery in this game is crisp and responsive. Shields work fairly well, and the parry mechanic is a very nice touch. You can parry with any melee weapon or small shield, and can even upgrade your character to be able to parry with bows or even your bare hands! That said, as someone that prefers a melee play style, my biggest problems are bad melee weapon physics, iffy loot system, and no respec option.

The "physics" of this game are basically governed by two systems - weight and timing. As with all VR games that attempt to correctly simulate weight in things you pick up, this game fails to do so. Unless you are using a dagger or some other really short weapon, you will have the most irritating limp-wrist attacks causing your weapon to flop around as though it were paper taped to a stick and causing your attacks to be largely ineffective when they should be devastating. You will also find yourself "in the bind" a LOT with the enemies as they do not consider space between you, and it is possible that your weapon will get tangled inside of them or even on your own gear. The limp-wristed nature of the game can even cause attacks that you would normally have blocked to still connect with you if they are able to "push past" your guard.

You also have to pay attention to the invisible time limit you have between attacks. Attacking too quickly causes your attacks to do minimal damage, prolonging dangerous fights and adding an unnecessary "skill curve." This can also cause special abilities to trigger, such as bonus damage to parries enemies, if you tip them with a back swing or a wiggle, causing that ability to do basically no extra damage.

I would prefer, however, keeping the timing mechanic while doing away with the weight mechanic, but that is ultimately up to the devs. Have fun swinging around your weapons with rubber arms!

The loot system is not very rewarding. It has some semblance of a scaling system, but nothing special. 99% of the time, you will be getting rid of loot without even considering what is on it because its usually useless anyway. The only weapons that are worth getting are the Legendary gear, but good luck getting any. The rest of the time, you will looking for something better than that level 30 gold item you found three floors ago, but the game keeps giving you you useless common gear.

Oh, and remember that legendary gear I was talking about. Its not really that powerful. They each have unique abilities that you can apparently upgrade, but the game doesn't tell you how. And the damage on the weapons is nothing to be excited for. The most I have been able to hit something for is about 30 damage, and that also includes the skills I invested in and critical hits. This is nothing when faced with enemies that have hundreds of hit points. Nothing really feels worth having.

Speaking of skills, the skill tree is good. In theory. However, a lot of the "upgrades" you get are often fractions of percents, only activate when you are doing something specific such as after hard parries, after taking damage, holding the draw on bows for a certain amount of time before loosing, etc. The descriptions of some skills are vague and not as descriptive as they could/should be, causing some confusion on exactly HOW they work. And you only get so many points during the process of your character build, with some skills requiring more than one point or having tiers that cost progressively more points. The WORST part about this skill tree is you have ZERO room for error as you can't respec if you make a mistake or want to try something different, forcing you to start a new character all over from the beginning of the game.

There is a lot of room for improvement for this game, and I hope the devs are reading these and considering what players think. They recently tried to upgrade to UE5 and things went badly, so they ARE working on the game. But with a lack of any substantive updates, I cannot say for sure what they will be bringing for the future of it.\t\t\t\t\t
