\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was sceptical of Legendary Tales at first. Sure, it had a fantasy theme and scratched the Dungeons & Dragons dungeon exploring in VR itch but Blade and Sorcery sort of did that so it wasn't enough. You couldn't see your body and arms after all. BUT THEN it turned out it had multiplayer. So my friend and I bought it whilst in desperate need of a VR game we could play together. BUT THEN it turned out the dungeons were unique to eachother rather than just feeling like a copy and paste experience with the same enemy and corridor over and over again. BUT THEN it turned out the game had unique legendary items with their own special abilities and stats.
In short, this is a good game. Grab it if you like TTRPGS and wish you could go through one in VR. (just the fantasy ones) You even have skills and stats to increase as you level up, although some of them increase as you use them.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game dropped 1.0 and it's a blast. Weapons have weight like in Blade and Sorcery and every part of the weapon does damage. Whack a dude with the pommel to deal bludgeoning. The skill system gives you options to change your build like a knight, wizard, rogue, or a monk style character where you can pick up enemies and throw them around, box them out.
It's got multiplayer co-op and having a full party is some of the best fun in VR you can have, clearing areas and crafting loot.

Negatives:
Toggle grip is on by default. Which means you press grip to grab something, and press it again to let go. I have an index and prefer this, since always grabbing gets the controllers sweaty and the grip sensors will stick, but the bad part about it is that there is no option to change it.
The price tag: If you read the dev diaries, the early access development was hectic. Months to almost a full year of work had to be scrapped as the devs had to go from Unreal 4 to 5 then back to 4 and downsize everything they worked on. I think the shaky development is what caused the big price jump. Something like $40 is a more reasonable price for this game considering the reused/free animation assets that so many other VR games like this have. (attack animations for example)

If you have the money, this game is a TON of fun and is the VR RPG game you've been looking for. The price is steep so wait for a sale. Unless you can splurge a bit, then try this game!\t\t\t\t\t

Legendary Tales delivers an incredible co-op experience. I dont think words can properly describe the adventure your're in for. several dungeons, weapons, enemies and crafting? This is a VR Title that may not have the best graphical appeal, but the game shines in the combat. Every part of your weapon is fully usable. half sword a weapon, mordhau your great sword, and even find a few legendary items along the way, I intend to also release a video gushing about this game. But that is in the making.

Now £55 is alot to ask for, I understand thats not cheap. Coop play is highly recommended for a great experience. You'll have great moments with your friends, being it slaying a powerful enemy, or having something cool happen. If you've been around since Early Access, it's still a great update! we get a few new legendary items, spears, and armor!

There's also a main quest with some side quests sprinkled in. Whilst they aren't the most 'intuitive', they still serve as a great baseline for what there is. they offer exp, rewards and honor, the third being a secondary currency that (i think) is obtained from defeating bosses and doing quests. Honor unlocks titles, offers Legendary crafting items and buying random ones.

Crafting is also new, letting you put materials into a furnace, or placing wood in a saw, crank, thing? to make a weapon of your choice. whetstones/bowstrings are used to give your weapon a grade/quality. from white, to purple. Legendary weapons are unique.

If your looking for a coop VR RPG experience, this is one of the go to games you should look at. GrimLord being a very close second.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBrilliant roguelite dungeon crawl/looter game.

Pros:
+ Visuals are pretty good, nothing groundbreaking but competently done and the lighting is very nice, especially reflective surfaces
+ Very good combat and movement physics (although spears/long weapons can be a little janky, still delightful to use)
+ Excellent climbing puzzles
+ In-development RPG aspects which are very good but need a little fleshing out; nevertheless an excellent preview of things to come
+ Turning options are very customisable, with snap, smooth, or smooth snap, and customisable degrees of turning; something for everyone there
+ Archery feels "right" - not necessarily accurate to real life, but right for the game.
+ Multiplayer is well-developed, lag-free and an absolute blast with friends.
+ Seeing your friend attempt to put a piece of headgear on their head over and over again - and missing - is highly amusing.
+ Carefully duelling an enemy only to have your friend run in and bludgeon them to death with a large jar. Need I say more?

Cons:
- No matchmaking (not a problem for me but might be for others) - you need friends who have the game to play multiplayer or organise a time/date with random people which is difficult/awkward
- Again, my comment on the RPG elements needing fleshing out:
- Stealth gameplay is planned to be improved but not possible yet - or at least not an option early game.
- Magic is a little limited at the moment, but heading the right direction.
- Accidentally punched a lightshade IRL the other day while climbing a ladder in-game. I feel this is an issue common to VR though. Or just me.

Mixed:
-+ Loot is entirely, and I mean ENTIRELY random. This is both good and bad:
- 90% of the time, new loot isn't great. That strength weapon with +dex on it? yeah...
- 7% of the time new loot is alright, primarily due to sale value or usefulness for a friend.
- 2% of the time, a piece turns up that's a slight improvement on your current gear, so that's fine.
- remaining 1%, you practically punch the lightshade again because you found an AMAZING piece of gear that outclasses anything else you have. This easily makes up for the other 99%.

Overall? It's a solid foundation that is admittedly under development, but very promising. Even as it stands it's a great game to play - and the devs have so far been very committed to player feedback. Genuinely looking forward to every update, and very happy with the price tag as it stands. Best played with friends but not bad solo either.\t\t\t\t\t
