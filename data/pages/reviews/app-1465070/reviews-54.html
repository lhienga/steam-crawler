\r\n\t\t\t\t\tClunky, tiresome, non-intuitive.
Starting from the menu, the game requires such high precision to equip/unequip items, and switching from the inventory screen to the character skills screen is like getting a headshot from across a CSGO map. It also doesn't have a "help" section to remind you of what each skill does, or a character stats screen so you can see a summary of all the effects on your character.

Combat could be interesting but its just goofy, enemies swinging so wildly at you that their skeletons get messed up and now their head is where their ass should be. Archery feels awful, most of the time I couldn't even draw out an arrow, and when I could draw the bow back it would vibrate so much you couldn't hit anything. Magic probably feels the best, but the gestures being different for each spell just means that some spells would take 2-3 attempts to cast, e.g. fireball would more often than not just slowly drop out of my hand and fall at my feet, despite me throwing it like a fastball. Just have all the casting gestures be the same so that people will know how to consistently cast an ability, or don't have the spells be so velocity-dependent.

Platforming is my biggest gripe, it's inconsistent and confusing. Trying to pull yourself up ledges is the worst, the tutorial tells you to grab and pull yourself up, but most of the time your hands will simply phase through the ground and not grab anything. Other times they will grab but you fell slightly so even though you are pulling yourself up its still 5 feet from the ledge so you won't climb over it. Ropes are fine, and most grab-able ledges, except for ones covered in fabric, you have to force your hands into the fabric before it will allow you to grab. Being forced to listen for the "click" before knowing that you can grab the ledge sucks, especially when it appears that my hands are touching the object in question.

TL:DR
Wait until it's out of EA, has potential but just aggravating to play in its current state\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only have 10 hours in the game so use that to gauge this review.

I love this game. It took minimal extra time to get used to how everything feels.
My initial thoughts, being I was going sneaky dex build, was that it was great, but the bow was hard to aim and pin down the accuracy. The enemy numbers seemed to be unbalanced sometimes.
However, the melee was spot on. Everything felt good. The skills attached to weapons were fun.

As of the most recent update we have the skill tree. The skill tree is honestly sized just right and really does touch on every style you could go for with the weapon variety. The bows feel much more controllable. The shots go where I expect them to within a certain range. That's normal of a bow though. You have to adjust for distance at some point and I'm still figuring that out. I run a small shield and dagger with a bow on my back. I love playing this way. With the added stealth skills I can lean in on the stealthy rogue playstyle I'm fond of. But if you want to go heavy 2 handed anything? It's gotchu. You want dual wield slaughter? It's gotchu. You want a mage with multiple spells for multiple elements and skills for fusing magic? It's gotchu.
You want to blend any or all of that into something you're own? It. Has. Got. You.

My current gripes:
- Magic needs work on aiming. Keep working with different gestures. They're really fun. I want to feel the difference in casting an ice arrow and a lighting arrow. Like lightning arrow could be thrown like a javelin while ice arrow could be an open hand forward.
- I wish there was a way to do a crouch by a button or stick direction. I'm trying to be more fit so I can do it myself more comfortably. Having the option would be nice.
- I want more. I crave more. You've done so well with it that I get sad as I complete levels and know I'm going to come to the end of it. 9 floors of dungeon and a boss room. But that's it for now. For now.
The developer has been very open with their updates and process. I know they are going to keep working
They recently stated that they may be increasing the scope.
This excites me.
I want more monsters! More weapon types! More magic fusions! Weapon enchanting via spells you learn! Hand weapons like claws or katars! More origin diversity for weapons! ( I'm a sucker for Japanese Kama)
Scythes!
I haven't even mentioned the legendary weapons you can find.
We don't really have a looter shooter for vr at the moment.
Legendary tales scratches that itch though. Fun loot. Great combat mechanics. Fun skills.
Is worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend that you play this game with a friend, but as of right now the developers are working on server system. (Currently there are join-able servers through the beta build!)

Melee feels great! The parry system works FANTASTICALLY! There are a few kinks to work out, especially if you are over 6 feet or 182 cm tall (those damn goblins!), but this is a minor and user specific issue.

Since it is currently an Early Access game, there is not much enemy variety. However, I never felt bored with this, as bosses, random level design, and enemy density make up for the lack of enemy types.

As I'm writing this review, the developers implemented a skill tree update into the beta-build... This was a WONDERFUL idea. The skill tree makes me want to continue fighting with my favorite weapon to unlock its unique weapon archetype specific ability, with many other perks to build that weapon's strength even further.

All-in-all, Urban Wolf Games has surprised many, many VR users with their take on medieval melee combat. I feel like an actual adventurer in an RPG.

Since it's still in Early Access, I'm giving it an 8/10, only due to lack of enemy type/variety, however I know this will change with time as they continue tirelessly working on improving this fantastic game.

A great start to a fantastic and promising VR melee game!\t\t\t\t\t
