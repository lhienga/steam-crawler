\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVersion 1.0 is out and I have about 7 hours with it so far. I will be leaving my early access review below to show what has changed.
(I haven't gotten very far yet, only two checkpoints deep into the first dungeon.)


So far, I really enjoy many of the changes made for the official launch. I understand complaints about the price increase, but this is an indie team with a very ambitious game for VR. I personally think the game is worth the money when I consider I got over 70 hours out of the comparatively generic playtest this used to be. I expect the game to be over 40 hours with co-op making it very fun.


Gameplay Changes:
The crafting takes a little bit of experimentation to understand, but it's actually super intuitive and useful once you figure it out. (note: some important crafting items are fairly difficult to obtain)
The quests don't really mean anything, but they can give you really helpful rewards just for playing the game.
The dungeons seem interesting, but I've barely touched them yet.
There are many new skills, alongside major changes to some previous skills. Most builds are viable (even more so than before), though some may feel far more challenging solo.
Enemy variety is far more diverse (in terms of how many types and variants there are)

Minor Complaints:
There is only toggle grab, no hold to grab option. The previous version had a toggle, which I preferred, but with the new changes, this actually feels a lot better for some abilities. I still would prefer to have the option to decide for myself.
The enemies hit far harder than they used to, but you also progress a lot slower for getting better abilities. If xp was earned maybe 50% faster and loot drops were about 30% better, this wouldn't bother me at all. As it stands, the game seems to expect you at level 5 to fight a mini boss that was 3-4 shotting me at level 8.
Many things are harder to figure out, as much of it needs to be discovered by the player. This is fine for things like skills, but it took me 3 minutes to figure out how to accept a quest because it didn't specify you had to select the quest title and hit accept before the npc will speak to you. (note: there is no voice acting, but I think that's better for this).


TL;DR: Fun VR dungeon crawler RPG with potential to be a 9.5/10 for me. Must-have for fans of co-op.




(Early Access review from Jan 29, 2022)
I would have recommended the game before the most recent update (at least as long as you had someone to play with), but the new update made the game even better. The new leveling system and newly added skill tree system make you weaker at the beginning, but progression feels much better (less reliant on RNG for viability). Magic is much more usable than it used to be, especially with the inclusion of wands. Overall, a fantastic update and completely worth losing my old characters.

Pros:
Great combat
Physics are nice
Character progression feels good
Different builds are viable
Excellent co-op

Cons:
Enemies aren't very diverse yet
Climbing sections generally feel bad
Two-handed weapons aren't very effective (Seems to have improved a bit with the update)
Fairly resource heavy
Difficulty spikes can be jarring
Updates can potentially delete save data\t\t\t\t\t
