\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCan't recommend this game at the moment due to the following points

- Too repetative
You'll fight skeletons and goblins the entire game, occasionally you'll fight a few bats here and there and at rare points mimics. The enemy variety isn't good at all and unless you enjoy fighting the same thing over and over again this game won't be for you

- Balancing issues
At the start of the game you get some points which you can allocate into any stat you want. Problem with this is there's LUCK which you can't level at all while you're in-game so the only way is to dump all your points into LUCK at the start because you can't get any more points in game in any way.

If you decided to be a wizard and dump all your points into WIS and INT, good luck. You're gonna have to take frequent naps since it takes forever to regenerate mana to be able to use your spells again. While the spells are decent you'll also have to get used to using the same spell over and over again, not because there's a lack of spells, because you'll never find any in the game except for the starting tutorial area, the rest are in the store and cost a fortune (80k gold, what the ♥♥♥♥?).

The only viable "build" seems to be a standard STR knight because everything else sucks damage wise and that brings me to the next point

- Damage sponges
All the enemies except for bats are damage sponges and take forever to kill unless you're very lucky and found a legendary weapon which will carry you for a long time. I understand it's a RPG but come on, it shouldn't take 50 hits to kill a single skeleton in the first few levels unless it's a boss or something. It gets tiring to fight the same skeletons over and over and the fact that there's 5 or more of them in every room just makes it feel tedious to continue to play

- Modding & Devs
Devs have openly stated that they don't want to support modding for the game. Pretty sure they went back on this and said it might happen but the fact that they said it at all more than enough proves it to me that they never had it in mind at all and won't commit to it. Enemy variety is also a thing that gets brought up, devs don't want to include human enemies because they don't want to promote violence or murder or whatever it was, forgot the reason but it's in one of the steam discussion posts they've made. I'd have no problem with no humans if they actually had more enemy variety but if you can't even add some typical fantasy enemy to replace "bandits" or whatever role a human would fill then I just take it as laziness.

- Price
33€. Price would be fine if the developers actually adressed any of these issues and fixed the game. As of now I can see it as a 15€ - 20€ at most and that's being generous.

I'll change my review if they somehow turn their game around but as of now I can't recommend this game as it is very barebones and needs further development and polish. Legendary Tales isn't completely bad, it is just very rough around the edges and it would be nice to see if the developers could turn it into a good game. As of now I'll hold off playing it until they've fixed most of the issues I have with the game.
