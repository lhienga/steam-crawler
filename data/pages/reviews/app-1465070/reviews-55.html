\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best VR games I've ever played. This game is absolutely fantastic in its gameplay and really does allow for some awesome CO-OP. There's only a few issues that the game has:

1. The AI almost completely ignores Parries and will strike you through walls. You can be using a shield, but they don't care.

2. Repetitive in the sense that the aesthetic never changes. I understand that it's supposed to be a dungeon look, but every single floor has a predictable layout with the same aesthetic as the last. I legitimately don't even know what floor I'm on half the time because they all look the same.

3. Enemies are copy and paste. The fighting strategies of each enemy make it practically useless to have anything else but melee as a class. Sure, magic is nice for high DPS and ranged attacks, but a guy with a sword just has to predict what attack the enemy will use from its 3 swings that it knows. Mini-bosses are the exact same way.

I feel this game has huge potential, and it really is a ton of fun to play, but the hefty price tag and limited experience make it difficult to recommend to folks, especially those new to VR. The yellow portals that take you to minigames is an excellent touch ... if the minigames were even possible to beat. Totem hunt (You basically hunt for a totem in a dungeon maze, is one of the most difficult tasks to do in the game, regardless of rank and skill. There's just too many damn enemies.

If you're looking for a nice RPGish game in VR, I recommend looking at Blade and Sorcery. I do really enjoy this game, but it just feels like it's lacking content for the player to enjoy. If you want to have the most fun in a VR game ever, I highly recommend this game, especially with friends.\t\t\t\t\t

TL;DR: Amazing game, one of the BEST Indie VR games out there, but still feels a lil expensive

This is one of the best INDIE VR games out there, I have reccommended the game for years, then the devs have said it's AAA quality and therefore charged AAA price, it needs to be said the game is not AAA quality and not worth AAA price, at which point I gave a negative review, however the game itself is amazing and the devs have been on top of addressing feedback that you wouldn't expect, even if they kinda admitted the PCVR side isn't going to get as much love, this is still one of my favorite VR games and worth it if you have the money, but if you can wait I'd wait until maybe a 30% off sale

Pros
- Great handling of weapons, 2 handers feel really good in this game compared to most similar games
- Really good magic trees that make you think of your build. Caster class 100% has the most replayability when it comes to build diversity, which I think is a nice offset from the fact that you aren't utilizing the amazing parry mechanics as much.
- Good and really fleshed out crafting and item upgrading mechanics. They went about this really nicely. I do however wish this level of polish went into overall atmosphere of the game.
- Really good Weapon and spell variety, really good diversity on rolls for weapons making you always hunting for an upgrade despite the limited slots (only 3 if you use 2-handers: armor, helmet, weapon)
- Really good melee parry mechanics most of the time it feels very satisfying
- Good boss fights, although few they are a lot of fun, the main thing that will keep you playing outside of gear farming for fun and some provide a hell of a challenge in solo
- Loot. We love looting. Wish there was more to explore/more incentive to explore, but the game is built off the loot system. As a hardcore Tarkov, Dark and Darker and Ghosts of Tabor player, being a lil loot goblin is essential and playing this game casually helps satisfy that

Cons (Comparing it to a AAA game as the devs intend)
- Okay graphics (good for quest, not at all good for PCVR, and I'm pretty sure it's not even on Quest)
- Very basic environment design (the world feels very dull and empty compared to any other AAA VR game, port or even mod like Skyrim, Fallout, MoH, Alyx, Resident Evil, HL2VR, etc)
- Lacks scripted events/fights, ambush situations, etc. Things you'd expect in an ARPG (contributing to the point above heavily)
- No real lore or story to the game that keeps you captivated
- Lacks immersion as there's no voice acting, the UI isn't amazing and the quests are.. kinda lackluster
- Combat vs non bosses is fun in bursts, but also starts getting old due to fighting the same 3 enemy types for the first dozen+ hours (skellingtons, zombies and goblins). When they were doing announcements of changing the code base to make adding new enemies easier, I guess I was kinda expecting to fight all kinds of different enemies, not 2 of the same ones from early access for half the game, I know those new enemies come later, but they should have really sprinkled in more variety early to keep players engaged
- No good variety in abilities (while spells have good variety all other abilities are limited to a single path)
- Extremely linear (we're talking walking in a forced straight path, with the only deviations literally leading to eachother within a minute of going down them)
- Nearly no verticality whatsoever to the game, outside of ladders and a couple parkout spots you're running on a flat surface while looking up and seeing all of these towers, castles, structures you'd wish you can go into or climb up but you can't, you can just go down the straight path the devs intended


Notes:
I loved the game for the first couple hours, but it became very clear the next few after that, that the game doesn't offer much beyond that even in the next dungeons.

While the assets look nice, environments feel dead and drull to go through, the new map isn't much of an improvement. They could have done so much more to make you feel immersed, add to the environment, adding scripted events, adding secret areas, just adding "anything" to reward exploring. The whole game is essentially run in this exact linear direction to kill this enemy, loot it, break the box you see in the clear path (since they never have anything to reward exploring, not any good scripted events, everything you can see/destroy is in clear view from miles away) taking away from any spontenaity or excitement

Also there's literally only ONE ability path for each "type" of play you want to do. Want to use 2 handers? You're forced down the same tree to get all of the 2 hander abilities, zero choice. Same goes for any strategy you can think of - you're literally pidgeonholed into a single weapon type for the entire game and can't respec. Only casters get a real variety here with spells which turned out really nice.


I love the gameplay and have since buying a few years ago, but expected a lot out of this update when the dev team compares to a AAA game, it's hard to reccomend when the RPG has literally no exploration, no real quests (other than the quest being run in a straight line to find x or kill x, never anything meaningful) This is pretty far from AAA quality it makes me it hard to reccomend the game at the current price, I'd wait until it's maybe 30% off if you want to be satisfied. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, just wanna mention how much I love the alternative the devs took to simulated weight. In a lot of VR games, I absolutely hated simulated weight which forces you to swing at awkwardly slow speeds or else nothing works. It was an unwanted but somewhat necessary evil to keep players from simply flicking their wrists to do max damage, but in most games the simulated weight feels overtuned. This game has a light/heavy attack system which replaces that evil where heavy attacks give the full benefit of attacks and are performed by pacing your swings while light attacks, performed by attacking too soon between swings, are still viable but are not as powerful. In a game where it's not uncommon to be defending yourself from multiple enemies at once, sometimes surrounded, this works so much better and is more immersive than flailing at an enemy just so see your weapon do absolutely nothing. Big plus.

The selected tutorial and the beginning-of-the-game tutorial are ok for the most part, but some areas... kinda suck. They do explain most things quite well, but they are very long and the stop-motion animation demonstration sometimes doesn't quite help enough to show you what you are expected to do, especially in the climbing tutorial (ledge catching is absolutely awful too so it doesn't help). On the other side of things, some features are just not explained well at all, like Active Skills for weapons. Thankfully, there are options to skip specific parts of the tutorial should you get stuck or if something bugs.

The thematic environment is quite repetitive. I understand you are in a literal dungeon that is randomly generated, but every wall and every floor is grey stone with orange lighting, the exception being of the end-room. Looking at literally anything other than orange stone once in awhile would have been nice. That being said, the levels are randomly generated so it is very possible I just got very unlucky in my environments as the trailers and screenshots I've seen show areas that look like Cathedrals and prisons.

The game looks absolutely beautiful, from the details of the individual pieces of gear to the enemies, to the hub. Absolutely gorgeous for what it is. After you finish adjusting your graphics settings in the in-game menu, just take a look at your hands. You'll see what I mean.

Enemies might seem like they attack pretty slow when you first start out, but I quickly took back that complaint when I turned a corner down a narrow hall just to find myself surrounded by 5 or 6 different enemies with an annoying bat doing drive-by's at me and floating... orb.. trap... thingys spawning randomly around the room. You don't want them to be attack faster at times like that as you can quickly be overwhelmed if not careful, however in smaller scale combat, like 1v1 to maybe 1v3, it does feel a little too slow. For those who start the game and are thrown into the tutorial section, don't worry, that's just THOSE enemies, they're not really THAT slow.

The combat allows for a fair amount of variety with the 3 different damage types for melee (Slash, Pierce, Blunt) and 3 for magic (Fire, Ice, Lightning). The varied loot compliments this and the rng stats can make for some pretty fun playstyles, however I did find myself finding pieces of gear that seemed a little too useless for my liking. For example, in my first hour, I found at least a 3 dozen bladed weapons with blunt-damage modifiers (and yes, you can deal all 3 types of damage with most weapons) or just items with stats that didn't quite fit the weapon a little too often. It was getting a little annoying.

Enemy variety is a little low unfortunately. An overwhelming amount of the time you're going to be fighting skeletons with swords. There's archers, goblins, bats, BIG bois, etc, sometimes they can even be enchanted, but 90% of the time, skeleton with a sword.

The hub is very pretty and very open, but also very empty. You have 4 areas of interest spread out WAY too far away, though I assume more things will be added in the future. None of the npc's have any lines of dialogue, voiced or written sadly but I suppose in the current state of the game, they don't need any. Unrelated to the hub but related to voice and sound, enemies also don't make any sound. There are 4 sounds made by interacting with enemies: the sound of your weapon hitting them, the sound of their bodies hitting the floor, the sound of their attacks/parries and bats. That's it. The game feels very quiet.

If you're not playing multiplayer, looting can sometimes take awhile as there are SO many breakables in a single level. This is a good thing as it supports at least more than enough common loot for that 4 player coop, but when you're doing this solo, it feels like you spend quite a long time looting. You can speed up breaking things though if you're solo by flailing your hands low while moving into things though.

The game is very stable with almost no loading screens at all. I've only experienced 1 bug so far in the tutorial during the archery section where I arrows just wouldn't mount and spaz my arm out if I tried. Archery in game however seems fine.

Lastly, this is more of a nit-pick and it could be just me, but the portal skill feels a little too strong. You start the game with an innate skill which allows you to teleport to the hub at any moment to sell/buy/store. There is no cost or cooldown to this ability and you can head right back in the dungeon exactly where you left off when you're done. Thing is though, it feels a bit too much like a freebie. There is 0 risk or trade-off that I've noticed. Even items you leave on the floor are still there when you get back and you don't even need to go back for them if you previously interacted with them as there is a "lost" tab in your storage for those items. I also never really found myself feeling the need to buy anything so I very quickly amassed a fair pile of gold that I could use at any time to just warp out and buy more affordable potions to heal to full if I ever messed up (and there's already regen skills in the game too so that was pretty rare too). There is a cast-time so it is no ideal to use it in combat, though it is not a very long cast time. The ability just feels a little too... noob-friendly imo.\t\t\t\t\t
