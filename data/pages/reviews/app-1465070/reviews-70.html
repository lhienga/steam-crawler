\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a game.

Other games do what it does better, even in VR, but it's a fun experience. NOT worth $35 dollars, though. If you're trying to best spend your money, this game is not worth more than other VR titles that are available for almost half. If you have money to waste, go for it.

It's janky and glitchy. Frustrating and archaic mechanics that avoid QoL like the plague. The combat is subpar in comparison to a game TWO years older than it (Blade and Sorcery), but it does the job I guess.

If you like dungeon crawling, this is a dungeon crawler in VR. It's not amazing, but it's fine. More fun with friends.

The devs seem to be active, but as far as my experience so far, the game is just 'fine'. I like it, but it's easily not for everyone.

I recommend it barely, but it's an experience worth experiencing, to an extent.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow do I put this?

Blade & Sorcery is currently ruining its wonderfully-built swordplay simulator trying to become a dungeon crawler. I don't like Dungeon Crawlers in VR, and that's not what I bought Blade & Sorcery for. Which is why it annoys me that Blade & Sorcery is trying so hard to become one.

I played this game just long enough to realize it's not a callback to Blade & Sorcery with Dungeon crawler elements-- it's a dungeon crawler first and foremost, with the swordfight physics... kind of a consideration? So it's everything I dislike about what B&S is trying to be without any of the stuff I actually like about B&S

Sidenote- Throwing objects in this game is an unimmersive pain in the ass. Seriously. I had to skip that section of the tutorial because I could not for the life of me figure out how to throw the dagger with any kind of an impact.

With all that considered, the game might be okay if you really enjoy dungeon crawlers in VR. But I don't. And even if you do, it's unimmersive and frustrating to play. The price tag is downright pretentious.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI paid $34.99 and can't recommend it for the reasons below

The Enemies:
-They Proceed to get up in your face, you can't knock them back or away causing your weapon to glitch out and making blocking impossible.
-Most of them can block (almost) all your arrows with their swords, making the bow practically pointless. You can block arrows as well but its much harder to do since your not an AI.

Weapons:
-The bow is basically useless but to make matters worse the arrow is on the opposite side of your eye line making it difficult to aim.
-Arrows feel weightless, no matter if you pull back as far as you can or just a little, it feels like it goes the same distance.
-The melee weapons has a limit on how fast a person can swing their weapon. Example: First hit (12 dmg) Second (5 dmg) Third (7 dmg). It really doesn't work out, they should add a mechanic that depends on the velocity of your swing.

Finally, I say get this on a sale or when it gets out of early access (if it ever does).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fantastic! I want to say right off the bat that in my opinion, it has the most fun VR melee combat system in the business. The combat uses blocks, hard and soft parries, and takes your weapon into consideration when considering whether it is knocked away a lot or a little when an enemy swings and connects with your weapon without you actively parrying them. The enemy attack animations are varied and do a good job of giving you a chance to parry or block. There are faster and slower animations from all different angles. And because it's a stat based ARPG, you have to think about defense at all times because your swing won't insta-kill (which works for a game like this). The legendary weapons and abilities are pretty sweet too.

The magic system is being overhauled (and later down the road, the stealth system will be as well), but they're workable.

I realize the price is high for some, but it actually has quite a bit of content considering it's early access. And the devs are pretty active.

Overall, this is just a really fun game and its still growing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking at this for coop, then buyer beware.

I bought this game to play coop with a friend. It starts off with forcing the tutorial, and this game does the exact opposite of a good tutorial by front-loading all of the information. I spent 45 minutes in the tutorial. There are "skip" portals but then it's unclear what I'm missing, so I ended up slogging through the entire thing.

After that, I was hoping I could hop into coop. Nope, have to beat the entire first area single player before I can play coop. I tried to get it finished before the refund window because I really wanted to see if the coop is good, and I couldn't make it in time. Extremely disappointing.

This game has potential, but several core issues need to be addressed before I would consider re-buying:
- I should be able to play coop with a friend within 5-10 minutes. If the game has a coop tag, I expect to be able to play coop easily.
- Shorten the tutorial. All a player needs to know in order to get started is how to pickup and sheath weapons, and how to hit and defend against enemies. This is pretty intuitive and should only have taken 2 minutes of my time to teach. Why not leave the nuances of the combat system to discover for myself, or teach me after I've had a bit of fun first? Why do I care about the stat progression before I've even gained any stats through playing the game? There are plenty of opportunities to cover information later.
- Simplify item and inventory interaction. Having to smash item crystals and have specific controls for picking up things while wielding weapons is nonsense. All of those mechanics add no fun to the game and made the tutorial 15-20 minutes longer than it needed to be. Instance the loot, make picking it up super easy, and let me enjoy the game instead of fussing over janky controls. This is where a game like Alyx wins for simplicity of design, not letting controls get in the way of fun.
- Improve AI. I feel like every VR game feels an obligation to add a climbing mechanic. The problem here is that the AI is unable to follow you. This trivializes the combat as you can climb a ladder, then pick off AI with a bow as they idle on the ground. Either make the AI climb or make sure they have a route to follow you.\t\t\t\t\t

I'm honestly glad that the developers decided to go through with this rollback to UE4 because I have personally had nothing but constant performance issues with UE5 VR games (And before any of you say "Bad PC lol 🤓", I have an RTX 3080 Ti and a Ryzen 9 5900X, BOTH OF WHICH are overclocked. These games should NOT be running that poorly even on the lowest settings)

The game already looks good enough for me in UE4, and that's because i'm not here for graphics (Which is something that most AAA studios nowadays fail to realize). I'm here for gameplay that will last for a good while (I usually consider a game money well spent if I play the game in question for the same amount of hours as I spent dollars on it, $1 USD = 1 Hour, so 55 hours for this game at it's current default price, though I did buy the game on sale for $30 back when the default price was $35 USD, so that would be 30 hours for me to consider this game money well spent).

I'm looking foward to seeing more from this game, and I think you should give it a shot as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game, Great with friends. A promising start.

So I've only played a few hours and obviously reviews are subjective, but if you like the idea of a dungeon crawler with RPG stats and leveling mechanics this game is definitely worth a try. I'd say its a bit like early DND or Gauntlet in VR. Randomly assembled tileset dungeons filled with Skeletons and Goblins who drop RNG loot. There's even some platforming elements that provide a neat challenge and some variety to the dungeons.

The good:
VR Dungeon crawler
Complex and engaging combat
RPG Stats and leveling might be nothing new, but experiencing them in VR is an entirely new feeling
You can break barrels and urns over the heads of your enemies, or throw your enemies at other enemies. Not super useful, but still fun

The not so good:
There isn't a whole lot of variety at the moment in enemy types or environments
The weapon stats seem to be randomly generated, which can lead to a LOT of useless items. Does make selling easy though.
Multiplayer can introduce input lag making bows and throwing weapons nearly impossible to use.
Town hub is barren. Blacksmith is only good for selling loot and the only other shop is for hp/mana restore and spells.

Overall, my friends and I are having an absolute blast with this game. I think the devs have a solid foundation for a truly amazing game here. Hopefully I'll be back to update this with more good news in the future!\t\t\t\t\t
