\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this game has tons of potential! Some quick points

- I will say its a little pricey for the stage it's in right now.
- I enjoy the dynamic lighting, but otherwise decorations are pretty bland.
- From what I've seen only 2 types of enemies SO FAR
- Multiplayer works very well in my experience, HUGE plus.
- If you want to be a mage or sneak around killing enemies I would suggest waiting to get this game.
- If you want to be a barbarian-gladiator-brute, then get it! So fun for that.
- The combat system is a little different than other VR fighting games you may have played before and it's extremely satisfying - IF you dump all your skill points into Strength.
- It's also VERY IMPORTANT to do the tutorial, even if you are a very intuitive gamer, if you don't understand the game mechanics you will be confused and aggravated. (I did the tutorial in the very beginning otherwise I would have been pissed!)
-Seriously, at this point I do not recommend putting any points into anything else besides Strength if you would like to progress the game, have fun, AND feel like you haven't wasted your time.

Here's why
- It's near impossible to sneak in this game at this point, your enemies will notice you pretty easily. Putting your points into Dex at this stage is a waste.
- Magic system as it is now is frustrating. I spent 7 HOURS using the same fireball spell because no other ones were being sold at the shop nor did i find any through the dungeons (unsure if you can even find them). When new spells were finally available, they were RIDICULOUSLY expensive. I bought the cheapest one available (8.5k for a lightening arrow that stuns but does less damage than fireball, and if you want to upgrade it to level 2 your intellect has to be level 51 - with the way enemies are scaled this is insanity) and after me and my buddy finished the 3rd floor and went the 4th (no exiting of the game) it disappeared. I somehow unlearned the spell and the money I spent on it was not returned to me.
We started a new session where I was the host, worked our money up and bought it AGAIN for me. I wanted to test if it saved it so we went into the dungeon, killed some enemies picked up some loot and exited the game. When we came back, my spell was !GONE AGAIN! as with the money I spent for it. The few new items we picked up were still in our inventories so the game did save. WASTED so much time and potential fun trying to use a completely broken magic system.

Now, in the game it states that the magic system in place is not ideal and will be replaced by a superior idea, but they don't give any clue as to it being completely broken and a waste of time - not an exaggeration. I was furious haha. Like the only somewhat affordable spell (8.5k) disappears when you clear a floor and/or exit the game. The other spells are so expensive 21k, 39k, 81k?!?!?! If you are going to replace them anyways, why not make them affordable so we don't get attached to them by sinking time and effort into them, ESPECIALLY if they're going to disappear? I really enjoyed the lightening arrow spell and I was actually able play as a mage for a short time with the stunning arrow in my left hand a fireball in my right, instead of blocking all my enemies attacks with a shield, back against the wall, blasting the same fireball spell for 7 hours.

Magic is useless right now IMO, unless you hate yourself.

Luckily the actual combat of this game is fun - if you dump all your points into strength, otherwise you will do minimal damage to the enemies.
Blocking, parrying, and the fighting movements are very fluid and make it so you can really get into a flow fighting enemies with a one handed weapon and shield, dual-wielding, or a nice two-handed weapon - This is a short statement but it does have a lot of weight as i really enjoy those last three game mechanics.

Feel free to add your magic (or any other gameplay) experience/opinions underneath, maybe I was seriously unlucky.\t\t\t\t\t

Despite having a nearly-hour-long tutorial, the dungeon itself is poorly explained and can be difficult to navigate. God help you if you're deep in the first level and die, forcing you to retrace your steps all the way back, making the slow movement speed painfully obvious, even when running. This may be fine in normal Diablo-style games, where backtracking takes only a few seconds, but it is slow and annoying in vr. The looting mechanics are nice, and may serve an open-world rpg well, but in it's current state (granted it's early access), I find it frustrating and tedious.\t\t\t\t\t

Unfortunately at the moment it's too janky to be enjoyed.

Attempt was made to bring more "classic Diablo-like ARPG flavour" to combat mechanics but implementation leaves much to be desired. It is basically a direct transfer of ARPG combat, but instead of lazily holding LMB you need to mash your weapon repeatedly. In B&S good swordsmanship skills will allow you to decimate your enemies with deliberate skilful moves. Here you just mash, wait for cooldown timer, mash again, rinse and repeat. It's a design philosophy that usually feels weird in VR without tweaking. (Vanilla Skyrim VR with it's damage sponges for example)

Other issues include:
  • No finger tracking support
  • Questionable pricing
  • Needlessly long unskippable and poorly designed tutorial that ignores more than 20 years of evolution of game tutorial design since original Half-Life
  • Poor interactivity and environmental physics interactions (Can't even push a lever with sword guard, come on!)
  • Weird default UE4 post-processing filter that was definitely designed for flat-screen gaming and for whatever reason was left untouched and can't be disabled in options.
And many others. It all feels small compared to main issue of janky combat and enemy AI, but I have no idea why all this stuff, most of which could easily be fixed in a few days by amateur developer with youtube tutorials for UE4, was left in.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLook, I get it. Another dungeon crawl? Another VR cash grab? Why bother with a game that you'll probably never see completed?

I don't have the answers to these questions. What I do know is that this was the first game that my brother and I could play together for almost 5 hours steady, and still smiling at the end of it. Are there bugs? YES! Are they gamebreaking? NO! (Unless you're a mage but hey, magic be fickle).

The few key items you need to know are this:

- The Region Pricing bit was broken because the lead dev made an honest mistake. He's working on fixing this, but Steam won't let him do it right away. If it's overpriced in your area (more than the equivalent of $35 USD, and you're still curious, check back in a few weeks.)

- The multiplayer works great, as we saw very little latency and no disconnect issues. Physics remained sane throughout the game despite my brother and I both trying to pick up the same vase/box/crate to smash over a goblin's head multiple times.

- Enemy variety is scarce right now, but there are plans to introduce more soon. The team behind this have a very clear and honest roadmap linked on their discord, if you're curious, but think this isn't a "right now!" game, take a look there to see if you need to wishlist this one.

- Bugs are fairly safe and innocent so far. Don't buy spells, as you will lose the purchased spells between dungeon floors, sometimes a magically shocked enemy will do the electric boogaloo, and some looted weapons can go for a spin before you can check them out. None of this is game breaking though, and honestly, most of this is fairly hilarious to experience.

- This is a game that forces you to slow down and use those two brain cells you have leftover from Algebra 2!
Don't swing your sword like a madman! Think like Dark Souls. Parry, Attack, Dodge, Attack.

- Oblivion stats are implemented! This is great and also horrifying! You want to get better at hitting things really hard? Get that big ass sword out and start swinging! Trying to bump up your vitality so a stray swing doesn't send you back to the village on a stretcher? Congrats, you're now a punching bag! You have to get hit to survive hits! For a generation that has all their abilities preened over at every level up, you're in for a shock! Think you don't need to increase strength because you're a rad mad spell casting lad? Congrats! You can only do ONE damage with ANY WEAPON! Magic is a finite resource when you're facing a room full of skeletons and goblins who have no issue getting in close and giving you a new hole to breathe out of!

TL;DR

Game Fun, but Young. Small team with a dream. Buy this, but at the correct price. Tell me how you dumped all your stats into Luck and you're gonna be the world's strongest while hiding behind two tower shields!\t\t\t\t\t

The enemies are suuuper repetitive (yay, more skeletons). You have to grind for 2 hours in a boring setting before finally being able to play with a friend. When you can finally play with a friend, you get to your first dungeon AAAAAND..!
What you saw in the trailer? Killing things and looting and leveling and all the fun things? Lies.

A skeleton with a red glow appeared in the first 1 minute me and my friend were in our first dungeon together. He oneshot me after facehugging, strafing and generally being all up in my face. I then had to walk all the way back, and get killed 5 more times before my friend could down him with fire arrows (this happened while I was walking for the 5th time so I even missed that tiny bit of fun).

Naturally, when I first leveled up, I figured I could improve some skills or maybe get stronger. That's not how this works.
I spent 18 points on Dex to get some damage going - turns out all the weapons that drop are swords and STR based weapons and the dagger is trash. It doesn't appear I can respec into STR or do anything with my levels. Hmm.

DEX builds should promote high risk high reward assassin-playstyle. When I activate my dagger skill and get this tiny circle to stab a skeleton, I want to be rewarded with an insta-kill or at least some massive CRIT DMG BONUS OMG. Instead, I have to do this around 6 times or something to kill it, but there are actually 8 skeletons strafing me and facehugging me so I can't and die.

FYI, I have 1650 hours in Mordhau and platinumed Bloodborne - I love a challenge. This ain't that challenge though.

Don't drop the cash on this game yet, ladies & gentlemen. Save yourself some waste of time and frustration.\t\t\t\t\t
