\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI play lots of VR games, this is one of my favorites, top 5 for sure. BUT:

Currently at $55 I personally would not recommend, but if you can afford go ahead, in case you are short on cash get it on sale or wait until you have spare cash , as of now it feels like a $29.99 AA game, it does have amazing features that are hard to find in other VR games, think of it like a VR Souls-like, enemies hit hard so you must parry or dodge, there are amazing legendary and unique weapons but it`s missing polish and features so the price tag does not match the current product.
1-Graphics are all over the place, the armor and weapons are top-notch, but the enemies and environment aren`t, the floor in most places looks straightout PS1 era.
2-The controls, if you are on Index don`t get this game, it doesn`t work, even after the hold-grip update, you have to almost destroy your controllers to actually recognize the grip, they didn`t test this game for Index and it shows.
3-Holsters, there are a bunch of weirdness happening with this game related to holsters, like I was climbing a hope and suddenly pulled my bow? from my back? while looking up? how does that happen? also the weapons are fixed in a position in your holster, so you can`t position the way you want your weapon to be, like a dagger is always pointing down so when you grab the weapon keep pointing down, while axes are pointing up and in the correct orientation when you grab. On top of that, sometimes I grab 3 arrows from my quiver and none of them shoot, and the bow string gets locked with one of the 3 arrows.
4-Enemies are fun to fight, but you must, I reinforce that, you must upgrade your passives for more damage, there are a bunch of skills locked behind perks, which leads to another issue, there are no respec button, so you character build is final, didn`t like bows and wanna switch to heavy strength weapons? succs to be you, make a new character.
5-Music is fine, nothing great, but it`s serviceable, but dear lord, please add more music.
6-Movement speed, you are a slug, and the map is huge, you walk a lot, all over the place, I`m okay with being somewhat slow in combat, but out of combat? weird to just sit and walk for 5 minutes, also plenty of backtracking so make use of portals as much as you can.
7-Crafting is okay, but is really weird the way its done, you need to take items from your inventory and they come in stacks, sometimes you need 1 and when you use lets say "Right hand to hold the stack" and use the "Left hand on the right hand stack", you just split the stack in half instead of picking a single item from the stack, which leads to another issue.
8-Inventory, I can`t split stack in inventory or select a single of the stack, you equip the whole stack and keep cutting in half until you have a single item left, the inventory is also clunk and not at all intuitive, like, quests, items, skill upgrades, all in one place, so you need to open this menu somewhat constantly, I wish there was shortcuts for "Quests", "Inventory", "Skills" and so on, like right hand up and menu button opens inventory, right hand down and menu button opens skills, that sort of thing.
9-Light, this game is really dark, and there is no light options like torches, wish it was possible to have something like that.
10-Food items, why food heals you so little?Like you have to grab the meat chunk from your inventory, cook in a fire like a site of grace, and that heals like 15 hp? some enemies hit like a truck, taking 40-50 hp per hit, and the food heals only 15? you need to eat like 10 for a full heal on a lvl 20 character.

Currently as is, it`s a good game, but not $55 good, it`s very steep, I bought it a long time ago, I think it was like $20 for the early access and I had tons of fun, at most this game should cost $40, by pricing that high they must provide the same level of polish, it` s good foundation that they can build upon, but it`s not built, they just released 1.0, there are bugs, lacking lots of quality of life features, textures mismatch, sound and music needs improvement, the devs definitely need to sit down, rethink some strategies, and work on this game, it`s up to them to either lower the price or make the game better to deserve said price, why do I say that the devs need to rethink?
It`s a VR game, you use melee weapons, and it got release with toggle-grip only... People on discord asked why? And the community manager said that the reason for toggle-grip was because players fingers will get tired from holding the weapons from longer periods? what? I`m glad they acknowledged this was an issue and got it fixed somewhat quickly, it`s a good step forward, I will be hopeful, and leave a positive review, but I`ll change my review if I don`t see improvements within a few months, keep in mind this is a product, we pay for the experience, we are not obligated to buy something just to "help the devs out", especially when that "help" is $55 USD.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis VR Game is just amazing. It's a perfect fit between an RPG and VR Game, everything is well thought out, and the game keeps the real player in mind.

The gameplay itself is a perfect fit to the motions that you do IRL: close combat, magic casting, crafting and even the pit stops to rest, regain health and your own IRL stamina.

I actually have to really take my hat off to the little rest places you have throughout the levels, its a great place to see what items you looted, to check the next skills or spells you are going to take, to just sit on the floor and chat with your mates, whilst your char regens all Health and Mana back.

Crafting is really fun as well, it made me want to go run back a few dungeons several times, to get more crafting materials and just grind a few more levels before taking on some challenging bosses.

Sure the price might be steep, and you might want to get it on a sale. But I think it's well worth the game and the effort the devs put in.

I think 10/10, looking forward to see more from this team of devs.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was great in early access, amazing dungeon crawler with intense combat and incredible fun with friends, loot galore, diablo eat your heart out.

Full release though? Hoo boy. The absolute scope of the game changes is immense. It's gone from semi random generated dungeon to full-on epic adventure. The diablo loot combined with some old school RPG level design makes for some comforting game-feel, the ability to break down loot into resources to make the weapons you actually want is a lovely touch...but this is a VR game, and it really makes you work for it. The loot may be plentiful but you gotta fight hard if you want to grind, it is a workout, you will sweat, you will struggle but it just makes the rewards seem genuinely earned. The game will force you to learn how to fight if you want to survive, you will memorise the attack patterns and feel like a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ warrior when you clear an area of enemies. It's hard-going, hard-won combat that no VR game I've played has made me feel before. I see a lot of comparisons to blade and sorcery being made but that is an unfair comparison, B&S makes you feel like an unstoppable killing machine, this game humbles you at almost every turn. Legendary tales is the dark fantasy version of into the radius, a game that dares you to conquer it rather than grovel at your feet.

Bringing friends into the mix though, that changes things. Team work, making battle plans, making builds that play off eachother, and just tackling the nightmare gauntlet of this game together...ah man, it's just one hell of an experience that you cant help but still talk about it days later.

Yeah, it's pricey, no more expensive than any other pancake games though so I don't understand the complaints there. That's capitalism baybeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeee!\t\t\t\t\t
