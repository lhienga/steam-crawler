Where it shines with potential: the skill system is surprisingly robust, with some very novel approaches of special passive and active skills used with different weapon types -- example: dagger's first active skill is for weakpoints to be highlighted on your targeted enemy, stab those points (back/neck/whatever) for critical damage -- or for swords: bash an enemy's face with your hilt after an attack to stun. This is a great way to blend the traditional RPG skills we've seen many times with a VR experience. Given that they're trying to incorporate skills and experience like this for magic, melee, and ranged, this may be some of the best RPG-VR hybrid action mechanics out there right now.

It has a great deal of work needed to be something that can be praised without caveat, though. The quality of life issues for VR is rough and rudimentary. You can become accustomed to most matters, but you will likely not be able to play this game without gripes about how some elements feel, such as looting and inventory management. And most glaringly, the enemy/map diversity right now is extremely light. Skeletons for days with a few uninteresting goblins -- though a few more surprises have made it in through development.

Gripes aside, we had a blast in co-op with some dungeon crawling mishaps. Rope climbing accidents are the most memorable. After a good co-op session, I felt less annoyed by the price.

My friends and I look forward to where this title will go in development.\t\t\t\t\t

Visually 'meh', priced high, but god is it still satisfying to play and feels much better than it looks in screenshots/videos! --- Most people probably should wait for sale (as-is) and/or more big content releases (coming soon), but sword/melee phsyics entusiasts should jump in immediate and will likely get your 30-40 hours out of it minimum like me if that's your thing.

Probably better with friends too? I'm still craving more 30+hours in solo.

----- long version ------
This game I had so many mixed feelings for before buying in, ... things like price, dev team (moreso the business/marketing team and ethics) and even now 30 hours in (apparently, barely felt like it).

Yes, visually 'as-is' its super plain (though in-game I found the lighting/textures actually veyr good, you';d neve rknow until you're in it), but much like Blade & Sorcery where you fight the same unity Male/Female enemies over and over for 10s of 100s of hours, this game satisfies just the same 'crave', but actually MORE because there is the added sense of progression, "trying new builds"/skill trees (no respecs so you literally start from lvl1 Diablo2 style -- don't screw up!), and challenge while provides ample motivation to keep experiencing the melee/physics combat (which is what this game is all about right now).

I still argue 'as-is' from a technical stand-point its still a bit 'overpriced', happy they had at least a small sale, but I've honestly gotten my money out of it anyways (which I wasn't sure at first) just from curiosity, replay and the satisfaction of the combat which sort of shocked me!

As long as you 'actually have' the 40hours of your life to play in VR, I think even in it's current state you'll be able to get your moneys worth that if you enjoy more realistic sword-fighting/physic simulation; this is one of the most satisfying and fun so far on that front (I've literally played them all), with skill trees top pair with techniques/styles on top.

Better yet, if you actually have friends (unlike me), the game has 4-player co-op which is literally the only 'full-physics VR melee game' on the market right now.

Devs themselves have been putting out substantial updates and quite active on discord, as long as they don't burn themsleves out the potential for more is huge

Updates coming include (finally) more enemy types, more levels, more difficulties/?game modes?, more spells, etc. with lots of promise.

Might be worth waiting for a sale for most, but definitly worth it, even now, for enthusiasts!
