\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNever before have I seen such a good alpha become such a disaster, but here we are.

To be clear the alpha was peerless, perhaps one of the best games ever made. Procedural generated levels woven together in an impressive way, along with random enemies. It was desperate and glorious and tooth and nail, never knowing what would be waiting for you in the next room. It actually felt like you were progressing deeper into the heart of a cursed otherwordly place, and playing it on Hardcore was a sublime experience.

All of this is gone now replaced with some half baked version of the generic RPG game we have seen a million times before. The randomness is gone and has been replaced with an unchanging an underwhelming world, and somehow the combat just feels worse now too; Where it was tight and reliable it is now loose and floppy. The dark and beautiful atmosphere has been replaced with some cartoon parody of itself, and the brutal difficulty is gone now, along with the literally infinite replayability of the alpha.

This could have been, as the name implied, one of those legendary games people would play in fifty years time. Instead it will be forgotten in months and fade away, never to be mentioned again except perhaps as an example of squandering potential.

This is a rank and categorical failure.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVR is missing some really in depth multiplayer games. This game is the closest I have found. I play it on Max setting through Virtual Desktop on Quest 3 with Bhaptic Vest, Arms and Head (it has native support) and it is freaking amazing. I get a steady 120 FPS about 80% of the time. I play with a group of about 5 and we rotate members in and out. If you are looking for a Diablo Lite to play in Multiplayer kind of game (which lets be honest is about the most in depth you will get in VR currently) this is the game for you. If you are going solo there maybe some slightly more story focused or perhaps slightly more polished games but not many. Remember there are very few almost flawless VR games right now, this one is about 80% flawless and the bugs are not game breaking.

I only have a couple issues, I wish the NPC characters were animated, but Its not a deal breaker I mean Zelda? The story is a little light, a cut scene here or their akin to RE or Diablo with deep story would really elevate your investment in the game. There is currently no map and no ability to toggle a minimap off and on and no map you can look at either via inventory or putting a map in your hand. Some option akin to Diablo (for Pancake equivalent) or Surv1v3 would be a huge help to working your way through dungeons when questing. It doesn't need to be a permanent thing as I know some prefer no map at all but I prefer a Suv1v3 style. I wish you could see your own body, I am not a huge fan of floating hands, especially in a game where you can customize your look. I would love to see some more variety in the NPCs and a little more depth to the game. Better initial explanation of skills, etc would be nice and the ability to respec is currently not supported. The only really frustrating aspect is throwing of magic and climbing can be a bit uneven when in another persons lobby.

None of these things are even close to deal breakers and do not at all detract from a game that VR sorely needed. Is this going to go down as the best Co-Op VR based Dungeon Crawler ever? Probably not, will it hopefully Blaze the way for large gaming studios to see the benefit of VR and give us Baldurs Gate, Diablo etc in VR I hope so. It will hopefully go down as the game that started it all (similar to Everquest and what it grew into, better games came later but without everquests success you don't get those) Buy the game if you want to have a heck of a time with friends. Yes the price is higher but lets be honest for the amount of time you will be spending in the game its not that much.
