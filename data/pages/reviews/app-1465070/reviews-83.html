\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLegendary Tales has promise and a great foundation to build upon.

Pros:
Good physics.
Combat that is not like flailing a weightless sword around.
Performance was really good I never noticed any lag.
Interesting weapons that can have stat bonuses and abilities.
A loot tier system.
Character stats system.
A good inventory system.
4 player co-op that works reliably.
An informative tutorial that is necessarily long.

Cons:
Sometimes I drop my weapon when not intending to do so.
The dungeon rooms can get repetitive.
Enemies sometimes get right on top of you making it nigh impossible to block or parry the hit.
Enemies sometimes can be damage sponges, especially the champions you can encounter in the dungeon.
There is no visual or auditory cue that you are nearly dead and health potions are hard to come by.
Very rarely ragdolls can glitch out.
Two-handed weapon handling sometimes feels weird.
After a while loot feels repetitive.

While the pros may not outweigh the cons for some they definitely do for me. I can see myself sinking a good amount of time into this game. I have high hopes that with time this game will become well-rounded and a very enjoyable VR ARPG. If you can spend money on games and are enthusiastic about VR, then I recommend you give this game a try. Perhaps bring some friends along for the ride.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwful game, especially for $35.

The trailer shows a completely different game then what you're buying. Going into it I was expecting the combat of Blade and Sorcery put into a full game, but the combat is not at all good. Enemies are tanks unless you max strength, the boss enemies have annoying gimmicks that are unfun to play against, the AI is garbage, hit registration is bad, and a lot of the magic may as well be completely unattainable unless you get very late into the game. It seems the game is trying to be "Dark Souls in VR," and part of that is being difficult. The "difficulty" is completely artificial though. The enemies don't outsmart you or anything like that, they just do a lot of damage with stupidly high defense. On a shady note, the game doesn't let you play multiplayer before going through the hour long tutorial and then an hour long solo dungeon, so you can't refund it after finding out the multiplayer doesn't make the game any more fun.

Skip for now, but I really hope this game gets more balanced as time goes on.
