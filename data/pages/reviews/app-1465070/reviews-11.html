\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLots of fun and cool systems, like crafting, talent trees/skills... some very strange decisions for a VR game that ruin it for me.

Edit: Removed the grip stuff until I can test the new patch.

Looks - ok, just ok, there's some strange texture things that happen, like a clover/grass texture map for the ground seeming to follow the hit box of the ground? Not to the actual shape of the terrain/ground, or they just applied it as a flat plane over the ground rather than attached to the ground. Makes it look like a strange 75% transparent grass/texture map is floating 2-6 inches above the actual ground, the distance seems to be dependent on whether the modeled terrain is flat, concave, or convex. Normally this is just a very minor complaint for me, and no reason to say it's a bad game.

Controls on index knuckles - The controls are really really bad right now, and this is after the patch that added the option to change grip type to grip to hold or toggle. One of the best features of the index controls is the grip and finger tracking. This game instead defaults to "Toggle Grip," which is just plain bad.

"Toggle Grip" meaning every time your fingers touch the controller, it grabs or releases. Close my hand on the controller to grab my weapon, take a swing at the target dummy, and the weapon gets thrown across the map because I squeezed a little too much during my swing, and the game thought I was trying to release my grip. In order to make toggle grip work without remapping, you close your hand to grab a weapon, then just leave your hand open, karate-chopping at all the enemies. Honestly, kinda fun for a punch build, but ruins the feel of everything else for me.

"Hold to Grip" this is supposed to be more like the normal way grip works on index, like Alyx/Arizona Sunshine and most other games use. BUT! they set the sensitivity entirely wrong. In order to activate, you have to squeeze the controller all the way til the haptic click/buzz rather than just closing your hand around the controller, and you need to keep that much tension all the time, which requires a decent amount of force and will quickly wear out hands. The fix seems to be, use toggle grip and just remap the grip to the touch pad button, this works ok, but forget about throwing anything.

Controller Orientation. The menu pointer and magic both seem to be angled wrong, so they feel like they are coming out of the heel of my hand and pointed a bit downward and out instead of the top of my hand/index finger following the direction I'm actually pointing. This, added to the grip issues, makes throwing difficult as it's hard to release at the right time, and things tend to go straight down as the release point isn't right because of the point angle being wrong. If I try to offset the hands to actually match the way my hand is in real life and get the point/magic to feel right, then swords and shields are held entirely wrong. If I am attempting to hold a blade straight up and down in game, the blade will be tilted pretty far back at me.

Bow design and orientation. First off, on a cosmetic level, the starter bow is wrong...just straight up wrong. The arrow rest opens and arrows are notched to the palm side. Real recurve bows are on the other side, even on bows without an arrow rest, you are still supposed to rest the arrow on the top/knuckle of the hand, not the fingertip/palm side. This ends up not just a cosmetic issue as you aim down your arm/arrow and now there's a big old arrow rest right in my line of sight rather than the target. Add in the default offset on the controller would require my draw hand to be in my head to actually see and aim at all. The best way I found to actually get a full draw and be able to aim, is to move my draw hand all the way behind my head so I can actually sight down the arrow shaft.

Other weapon orientations, I do find the two-handed sword and axe feel a bit off and can cause some hits to be with the flat of the blade. Some of the shield grips are strange. On a large shield that should be held with your full arm and parallel to my forearm (which is how it's modeled on other characters in multiplayer), instead are angled so that if my wrist is straight like it should be with that shield type, the shield is rotated through my forearm. Once again, if I adjust the hand orientation to make these feel right, then bows, spells, and the menu pointer are way way off.

Stash, kind of small but fine. NO shared stash just seems silly for online characters, as you can just use a friend to transfer items to other chars. Should give us a shared stash tab rather than making me jump through hoops. This, little bit of both thing, is always a bad idea, pick one or the other. Trading and a shared stash, or no trading and no shared stash.

Corpse Runs and move speed. When you die, you get to "run" back to your death location naked to get your stuff, but you also have a town portal that you can drop from time to time to shorten the trip. IF this isn't an mmo why am I doing corpse runs! Again this is that, little bit of both is a bad idea, thing. Either you drop everything on death and you have to get a res or run back from town/way point and town portal are only one way/close on death. OR just make a portal somewhat near my corpse on death. Personally, corpse "runs" in a VR game feel incredibly bad and are just plain boring as nothing respawns, so they aren't even adding a challenge, you're just padding your play time.

Move Speed, there is a reason I put run in quotes, the movement is so slow! Which again could be fine for a slower-paced, timed fighting game, and to be fair in fights, the move speed feels pretty good, a touch slower than I'd like but still good. The issue is the corpse runs, the size of the map, and the dead space in between things, it takes forever to get anywhere. Add this to the fact that this is an active VR game that I play standing up, and now you're just trying to make my back and feet hurt for no good reason. Simple solution if they don't want to change combat movement speed, just make it so whenever I'm in combat, crouched, or have enemy agro, you get the slow speed, but if nothing is around, just let me move faster/teleport freely.

Respec, there is no respec at all. By itself, neither good nor bad, but the extremely bad tooltips (which might just be translation issues), lack of in-game information, and a wiki that's old and very lacking make it so half the time, I have no clue what a talent or ability actually does or how it works. Does an ability crit, does it not, how does it activate, how often, did I just waste stats on luck because the wiki says luck only affects drops but discord says it affects crits too, but wait, spells don't crit at all, what does "other body part" on this talent even mean. I'm all for experimenting and figuring things out, but I have no desire to spend 15-20 hours on a new char just to try things out. Make repec expensive or require some strange drop if you want it to be rare, or just have good tooltips so I know what I'm spending my points on! This also wouldn't matter as much if the end game wasn't as hard to straight up impossible if you mess up your stats/talents/abilities.

If this was still an EA game with the EA price, I'd say go for it, there is a really cool core game and concept here, it just needs bug fixes, polish, and QoL stuff. Most of what I mentioned are not large things and shouldn't be too hard to address. But this being the full release with a price to match, it's a hard pass. I have my doubts there will be many more patches or changes, but who knows. If it does get a major patch that fixes my issues, I will gladly change this annoyingly large wall of text, that definitely got away from me as I was typing, and recommend the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, lets cut to the chase. I adore this game, I will likely continue playing it because there are few like it. However I have some serious issues regarding it that desperately need to change.

Multi stat or Multi classing is completely nonviable due to the devs insistent use of a leveling system countless players, including myself, have stated needs a serious revamp. Firstly, there are limited stat points that can be distributed to your build, this is the norm of course. However, these are not as a cap on the stats themselves, oh no, but as a whole on all 3 primary stats. You have 50 points that can be earned, period. This isn't necessarily the root of the problem though. No, the root of the problem is how those stat points are earned, coupled with the limit. You only gain points for a stat if you use a weapon or skill that relates to that stat, such as a sword for for strength. This means that if you use say magic in your off hand which scales with intelligence and a sword in your main hand that scales with strength you have to be very mindful of what you are using, because you have no control over when a stat levels up, thus no control over how far you lean on one stat or the other. One stray level up can destroy your build due to skills and respective upgrades being stat and level locked.

Now of course, that can be fixed right? you can just respec the point right? HA WRONG.

I don't understand why these devs are so afraid of this very basic and honestly vital quality of life improvement, that has been repeatedly asked for on every forum, but in order for you to "Respec", you have to start an entirely new character, which means any and all progress in this quest driven open world RPG is now lost because you panicked and punched a skeleton instead of casting fireball.

Considering the amount of time and effort your players put into these characters and your game, this blatant disregard of your players wishes is unacceptable Urban Wolf Games.

Secondly, Character movement is a serious problem, especially after the introduction of 1.0 that added a massive amount of new animations, some very quick and unblockable ones too mind you. Your run speed is a fast walk at best and your walking speed is a shuffle basically. With the introduction of 1.0 i will note they added a short dash, but on a 3 second timer for some reason. It is very difficult to avoid attacks from enemies, especially ones like the shaman or berserker that have unrelenting attack patterns.

Finally, Damage and resistance scaling needs a major rebalance. For one, AC is next to useless. The damage reduction is so minimal that you don't even notice it. For two, damage output for weapons needs to be up'd as the weapon level increases. Supposedly the weapon level is meant to give you a better chance at higher sub stats, but the issue with that is the "Chance" part. RNG is nice to no-one, we all know this. Weapon level should reflect a higher quality of that weapon, thus it should have higher stats. A level 1 weapon should not do the same base damage as a level 10 and so on.

Like I said, I do adore this game, but with these glaring problems that really disturb the game-play as a whole, I cannot in good consciousness recommend this game, especially at $55, to other people.\t\t\t\t\t

***NO CROUCH BUTTON, HAVING A BAD KNEE MEANS I CANT PLAY STEALTH? PLEASE ADD A CROUCH BUTTON***

1. Lack of enemy types. I'm 8 hours in and have only fought Bats, Skeletons and Zombies.

2. Bow and arrow bugs out frequently

3. Environmental graphics are pretty outdated - like a game from 1995. Like there is no grass, just a green texture if that makes sense. The entire time playing i question why i dont just play Skyrim VR with mods.

4. Cant jump. Sometimes have to walk around to get over a 6 inch lip on the ground

5. Tooltips and explanations are poorly explained. This includes the skills/abilities and quest log. I dont want my hand held but i also dont want a "find the thing somewhere" with no real direction. There are also some skills i still just dont understand.

6. NPC's are extremely outdated. Reminds me of NPC's from Morrowind lol


What I like:

1. Melee combat feels great. Playing as a Rogue it's really fun when a mob gets close to parry its attacks with my bow and then stab it with my offhand dagger.

2.. Made for VR mechanics, like crafting and combat are decent.

3. Bow and arrow feels great when it works


IT'S A GOOD GAME, Just not worth the asking price. Wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

There are better versions of this game that cost less that you can buy right now.
I put in nearly 100 hours in this game during early access when it had random dungeons, but they took that out.
There is now nothing about this game that is original or worth the asking price.
0/10 would not recommend.
EDIT: If there were a way to downgrade the game to the early access version, I would then recommend this game. I LOVED that version.
EDIT2: There were a whole bunch of updates, so I figured I would give it another try to see if they fixed the controls... nope. I WANT to like this game because early access was so good, but I can't BECAUSE I CAN'T HOLD ANYTHING FOR VERY LONG WITH VALVE INDEX CONTROLLERS. In the early access version of this game you used the trigger to pick up things and that was great; why did they decide to change the basic controls? Do the devs not own an index? What the hell?!? I know that they are never going to bring back the random dungeon aspect of this game (which is extremely unfortunate as that was the part that made me play early access over and over.), but could they at least work on making the controls for index usable? Damn. There is nothing more frustrating than going to swing a sword and... Whoops! you dropped it! Pure ragefuel.

The review asks me, "Do you recommend this game?" I wish I could put " no".
If you want to swing swords in VR, go check out Hellsplit Arena where you can actually hold the weapons.\t\t\t\t\t
