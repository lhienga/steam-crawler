\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA promising VR Swordplay game, reminding us of Blade and Sorcery, with added Co-op and Dungeon Crawling...

Pros :
- Combat is easily the strong suit of this game, parrying and stunning ennemies before you attack them feels great
- Semi RNG Levels, helping mix up the game and making sure you don't learn all the designs that easily
- RNG Loot, pushing you to always get the next upgrade to be even more powerful
- Co-op makes it a blast to specialise in what you love, allowing you to take on specific roles in the party
=> Warrior, Wizard, Ranger or Rogue for example are all viable options.

Cons :
- Price tag is high for what the game currently has to offer in my opinion
- Levels are a bit empty at the moment, filled with the same props, not always interactible
- Loot has a level requirement, sometimes forcing you to wait until you can use that one rare weapon you found
- Loot and gold aren't shared and it's always first come, first served
- Looted items are bound to you by default and cannot be given right away to another party member, this is especially problematic since in this game you find "crystals" before they turn into items.
EDIT: You can turn back items into crystals and trade them using the crystal fountain.
- Moving around the town and dungeon is somewhat tedious, since our character is slow and the levels are huge
- Missed opportunities to make the game more interactable in VR
=> ex : Manually drinking potions instead of pressing a button
- No seated mode

While it isn't the multiplayer dungeon crawling Blade & Sorcery I hear plenty of influencers mention,
Legendary Tales is it's own promising game, hindered by it's somewhat hefty price tag for the content it has to offer at the time of this review.
I would change this to a "Recommendation" if the game was around 20€ or below\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game, engaging combat, needs more enemy variety and a few balance tweaks to the gameplay.

First, I should say I highly recommend this game to anyone who wants a social VR game but is tired of shooters. It has some solid melee combat, competent bows, and usable magic. It does have some balance issues though.

1. Too few enemy types. Basically just skeletons. There are occasional goblins and some mimic chests, but basically just sword swinging skeletons with an occasional archer. Could use more variety.

2. Enemies often get too close and fighting close up enemies can be awkward. They will often swing swords behind you and hit you with their hilt on accident, or the get between you and your shield.

3. Large shields are too overpowered. They basically block all enemy attacks in the general direction of the shield. And you can basically attack through the shield. There needs to be a greater draw back to using these shields. Maybe they can't absorb too many attacks at once. Maybe you shouldn't be able to attack around them so easily. Maybe enemies should be better at flanking large shields. I don't think it needs anything huge, but it needs some rebalancing to make things more interesting, otherwise I might restart without a tank to keep things more fun. Disclosure, I have only played a few hours, and haven't faced any actually armored enemies yet, but I have been playing long enough to feel a little too overpowered.

I haven't tried the new levelling system, but look forward to it. But I really like this game and hope to see it expanded.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCurrently, I do not recommend. The game feels very linear, to which I compare to a movie theater arcade shooter, but just in VR. You walk forward, have mobs slowly creep toward you, they do a *spicy* dodge, then you slap them with a weapon until they die, then walk forward, repeat, etc.

It is early on in development, and these types of clunky mechanics are completely to be expected! The combat is nice, comparable to Blade and Sorcery with their styles, but I do believe that with the parry system in place, this game actually is better than B&S in regards to the combat. The trailer said you could grab anything in the game, but I tried to climb on a window sill and was unable to. I don't believe it's entirely accurate. The game needs more work, but it's getting more work done.

TL;DR: 5/10. Feels like a VR PS2 dungeon crawler, but the devs are working on more content and are very transparent about it. Feels incomplete at its current stage in beta (BUT PLEASE NOTE*** it is in the very early stages of beta).\t\t\t\t\t
