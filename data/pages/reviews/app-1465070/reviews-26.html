\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrankly, the only thing that makes this worthwhile is the multiplayer aspect, and even then, I'm fairly certain there are games that handle it better.

It's the latest in a long slew of VR Sword games where seemingly next to no effort is put into how the most fundamental of gameplay aspects work: Swords, maces, axes and et al all have hundreds of times the wind resistance and weight that they would in real life, making the most basic premise of the game -- the fighting -- feel clunky and sluggish, and generally more tacked on than anything built with 'fun' in mind.

The game is NOT properly represented by either trailer on the steam store page, it should be noted, and the game should be frankly taken off of steam for such misrepresentation.

There's any number of other issues, such as 2-handed weapons not tracking hands properly, the player avatars' arms being too short and therefore limiting reach, no options for seated play, and the lack of enemy variety, but really, those are all far too advanced issues to talk about when the most crucial, most fundamental aspect of a game like this, it's swordplay, is so horribly lacking.

I understand not everyone wants to be Gorn -- which has fantastically silly, albeit responsive physics -- but it really doesn't take a genius to understand that this sluggish, incredibly unresponsive swordplay is not only not how real weapons work, but also isn't any fun.

Devs, maybe you were trying to prevent the game from just becoming something you could waggle your controller through, but did you really never stop to think that in trying to obstruct that form of gameplay, you just made everything worse? Was that so important that you felt the need to sacrifice the quality of the most important aspect of your game?

I think the game would be a lot more salvageable, even despite other issues, if the actual swordplay was fun. If it felt responsive and quick, I don't think I'd care so much that I was fighting a ton of copy-paste'd enemies and about all the esoteric controls and lack of options. I'd still think they were a problem, but it wouldn't be as bad, because the core game would be solid.

But it isn't. And this isn't 2010 anymore: Early Access doesn't mean anything, least of all when the game has been around for years without anything being fixed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe recent mixed reviews for this game are a joke, this game is phenomenal. If you are looking to buy this game, look no further. This game is one of the best VR dungeon crawlers you can buy right now. This game has great combat, and it doesn't make you feel like a outrageous god on a blood lust like blade and sorcery does, but it makes the combat more satisfying, and rewarding. When your fighting, you actually need to block and parry attacks instead of rushing and pushing over people like you would in blade and sorcery, the fighting is something you should actually practice in Legendary Tales, and the combat feels more fun because of it.

There are downsides, as well. Like the repetitive enemies (Skeletons, Bats, and Goblins) but its not all bad because there are different variants, sizes, health of some different ones, but sometimes the buff enemies are another downside. Sometimes you will come across "mini bosses" that are just big skeletons or goblins, they are really strong and I find myself dying to them way too much, and being spawned back into the village for the fourth time after dying from the boss while just trying to grab your dropped stuff is the most annoying thing in this game. That's the only complaints about combat I have, which annoyances should hopefully be fixed in the next update I hope. The rest of the game is great, and there is a lot to come for this game.

For the people who think this game was abandoned by the developers, the discord server's community is relatively active and you can find a little bit of information about the infamous and delayed update. The developers have not said much about progress about the update, nor a release date. Yet they say in the discord that the game is being worked on avidly, even if they do not say anything about it.\t\t\t\t\t
