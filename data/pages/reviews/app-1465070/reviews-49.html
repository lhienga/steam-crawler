\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an absolute blast to play. The weapons are fun, finding new items gives you a little hit of dopamine, and figuring out what you want to invest in the skill tree is interesting. Playing with friends is really fun, and people on the public servers have been friendly and helpful to me as a new player. However, it has some drawbacks that need addressing before it can really shine, I think.

AI

The enemy AI can be very frustrating to deal with, or just plain dumb. They will attack straight into your character, attempt to swing their weapons into walls, or generally not be aware of the space around them. If you are in coop mode, they will target whoever attacked them last, even if you are closer than the other attacking player. This can lead to frustrating moments where you get crowded out or the enemies attack directly on top of you, which really breaks immersion. Also, the goblin enemies do some really dumb things like running into walls when trying to escape you.

Enemy Variety

There are 2 enemy types. That's it. I guess 3 if you count the two varieties of skeleton. This needs expanding.

Level Generation

The procedural level generation is a bit limited. By the time you've gone through a few levels, you will begin to recognise the same zones again and again, which doesn't feel great. There are also lots of dead ends and back tracking. There are also some climbing puzzles which are a little bit janky, but could be really great with a bit more variety.

General

When you take a weapon from a holster, it picks it up from the very top of the weapon rather than the handle, for most weapons. This means you have to adjust your grip down each time you pick up and holster your weapon. Some swords get drawn correctly from the hilt, so this just needs expanding to all weapons.

The skill tree perks could do with a bit more explaining, as do the stats. For example, lots of guides on the community hub discuss whether luck actually does anything, this could be cleared up with some better explanation in game. You also can't respec your skills, as far as I can see.

It would be nice if gold was shared in coop runs, as it can lead to people being loot ninjas.

Overall

If you enjoy being a loot goblin and figuring out fun ways to build your character, you will enjoy this game. Especially so if you have a few people to play with. Don't expect to have a fully fleshed out VR RPG experience, but with a bit more work and polish it could be an absolute banger.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fantastic, especially after the skill tree update. The dungeon crawling adventure experience the devs have created with this game really feels like playing something like diablo but in VR, and the coop works great. There are enough skills and weapon types and magic for experimenting with builds to feel fun. Personally, I have played tank-type characters for years in mmos and have been doing the same in this game. The first time my friends and I made it to the boss, looking up at a 2-3 story boss bearing down on you was just as thrilling as the first few times tanking giant legendary monsters on traditional mmos was, something that had otherwise become routine and stale. The combat physics are done well, and you can't just wiggle your sword like a lightsaber, and the parrying/blocking is responsive with nice physics and responsiveness for an overall awesome feel to the combat.

My only criticism is one of most current VR games, the movement is kind of janky. Especially the boss fight is a very movement intensive fight, but the sprint toggle feels like it constantly switches to walk and your character starts creeping which makes it tough to dodge mechanics when you're moving so slow unless you have a big enough play space to actually move. Looking around while you're trying to walk straight also causes you to change directions, which is a pain for similar reasons.

If you're on the fence about this one, I'd say it is absolutely worth checking out\t\t\t\t\t

For one, gore feels weird. The goblins heads just... pop off. Skeletons are fine. It feels a little weird as stabbing doesn't do much, fights feel both too long and too short, and are usually just "smash sword into it until it dies/goes on a knee so you can finish it"

It's fun with friends, but I don't think it would be very fun alone imo.
A lot of the floors, while its really cool to see them be randomly generated, feel absurdly long, there feels like there is an abundance of "trash" loot that is just low tier garbage that fills up your inventory "oh great, another buckler"
Overall, the game is janky, needs work, but is fun with friends. Some stuff doesn't seem to work (for my friend, teleporting items into his inventory doesn't work at all)
Gripping swords is unnatural and unintuitive (NO, I do not want to grab the sword upside down and by the blade)
It feels like it gets boring and I can't really play for more than a hour, but my friend seems to like it alot.
I don't think it's bad, I think it needs work. I'm not sure its worth 35$ as is, but we'll see if my opinion changes on that.
The climbing areas that are not optional are a pain in the ass, I hate the stupid rope areas with the bats.
The models for the NPC's need work.
One thing that I think is very cool is the seamless transition from the hub world to a dungeon when you go through the church doors. Very well done there.
Longswords, Zweihanders, and nearly any "long" two handed weapon feel really weird and unnatural to use.
Some magic SFX are too loud.
I'll keep updating this review as I think of more, but that's all for now ig.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game needs so much more love! I have only played this for 11 hours at the current moment but this game is my favourite as the developers have done the best they can to make this game REALLY good in early access. Although there are a few problems (due to early access) these are the things that should be fixed:
- Skeletons do WAY to much damage and should be slightly weaker and slower.
- Parrying a enemy should stun them for longer and make it so after they get hit again, they will go into a heavy stun animation, making killing them easier.
- Weapons should have special moves (e.g Greatswords can do larger criticals, swords will get a massive damage boost for a short period, daggers make you dodge enemy attacks when used, bows make you undetectable and detect enemies through walls, spears can skewer and inflict overtime bleed damage etc...)
- Magic spells could be a little nicer animated (nothing wrong with the animations now)
- Greatsword combat should be a little better

But, also in my opinion, if all they did going forward was add variety (new enemy types, new dungeon pallettes, new weapon skins for the weapon types, etc), it would be perfect. Luckily for us, we get that AND more and better mechanics.

Definitely buy it. Great as is, will only get better with time!\t\t\t\t\t
