\r\n\t\t\t\t\twith the content the game currently has, which while decent isn't ginormous or anything, i dont think its quite worth the current pricetag unless you're really itching for a game like this.

the melee combat is very fun, enemies and their attacks are all slightly 'limp physics' as opposed to the more 'rigid' style that B&S has for example...this makes hitting enemies feel a bit better in some ways as they get moved a bit more with swings, plus the game has an impaling system that even lets you pull the weapons out sideways by 'cutting through' them, though afaik it doesnt do more damage, just feels good to do. its certainly a different feel to the flow of combat compared ot B&S, where with that game attacks feel a bit stiff and a bit too 'canned' or over the top sometimes. here everything feels like it leans more into the physics side of it, and generally feels better imo.

personally some of the spell effects are lackluster, im talking how they look/feel...though most of what ive tried is some fire and the first lightning spell, it's basically a taser but you can shoot it, and i like my magic feeling powerful/looking good so that kinda dissapoints me, hopefully they can be improved sometime down the line to look a bit nicer (or maybe the first spells just look lackluster on purpose). the portal spell looks really nice though.

a new update looks like its on the horizon that should add a good # of things, incl more spells!

the way weapons handle feels good, 2 handers work nicely, feels good to try and parry and counter enemies, i find myself roleplaying a bit with it instead of trying to be efficient and get a ton of hits in when i parry or just punching enemies to death (punching is a little OP atm).

goblins are funny, they run away and often slam into walls comically.

either grab the game if you love VR physics based melee combat, or if you're on the fence, wait till it's on sale for 20something or less, i think. great game imo\t\t\t\t\t

Big Improvements
- Crafting
- Armor
- Enemies
- Map
Things That Still Need Love
- Magic
- No support for casters or dexterity characters
- Useless Overpriced skill tree
- Better UI

So far absolutely everything feels better. Explorations is fun, questing gives purpose to the player, and the new enemies and map are a long overdue breath of fresh air. Almost right away my friends and I had a blast trying out different weapons and exploring the environment looking for materials and cool fights.

I have only one gripe that can really overshadow the fun at times. Strength is still way powerful and the other stats simply aren't as useful. Casting is clunky and hardly does enough damage, No mana regen so your constantly having to sit at fires or drink potions to get it back (by the way dodging uses mana which is a horrible way to do that) Rogues dont have any abilities that do anything and their weapons are inherently worse that swords, axes, and hammers. The only way to get stable as a Rogue or Mage is to find broken weapons with perfect stats. whereas strength can pick up just about anything and fight well. I would recommend having move speed work off of your dex stat, I would add a stamina bar that refreshes overtime allowing for a major improvement to the dodge function, And I would add armors and weapons that better represent the Mage, Rogue, and Ranger subtype.

Overall the game feels incredibly well made. I would definitely recommend you pick it up and play if your new. if your a returning player, grab a few buddies and try it out again. I guarantee you will have a good time.

Hope the devs have goodluck in the future, and may the updates be on time this go around.
