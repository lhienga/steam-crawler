\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am target audience of this game & I didn't like it.

I love dungeons, I love physic-based combat, I love Diablo aesthetics, I love challenge.
Yet in current state this game doesn't deserve 30+ euro price. Moreover, I wouldn't give it even 8.

The game has a lot of good ideas. To be honest, the project itself in its current state would be suitable as a "proof of concept" for a potential investor. I am not a potential investor, I am a potential player, so I need the game to work as it should, albeit with a small amount of content.

What I did not like:
A. General "clumsiness".
1. Enemies stick to walls, funny bounce into textures.
2. Collision of weapons. The sword and shield the player is holding, for example, have a vibration impact when they collide, but there is no sound or visual feedback until enough force is applied. It's like appearing in echo room periodically.
3. Incredibly uncomfortable weapon holders on the player. Counter-intuitive, poorly working, too far forward from the player, which makes it sometimes difficult to pick up a new object in your hands.
4. Interface. The way the buttons are pressed. The way the menu behaves. This stupid laser pointer in VR format, where there are so many opportunities to interact with the elements of the environment...
5. Default mapping, default comfort settings, default graphics settings - everything is very bad, but you can't change it. Perhaps I did not see it, but, to be honest, at some point I simply did not want to spend more than a minute in the game.
I'm used to holding the "grip" button for the "grip", instead you need to press the trigger, and in order to release the object, you need to press it again. lol
B. The developers are clearly old school players. Remember how in Diablo 2 you had to carry talismans in your inventory? Making all the looting pain? Fans of Diablo 2 also call it "management" and revel in the inconvenience. Here are such inconveniences that it would be worth getting rid of - the sea & more. Collecting gold is inconvenient. Climbing stairs is uncomfortable. Collecting spheres is inconvenient.

Clumsiness is felt in everything. The whole game is riddled with problems that can't be justified by early access - small, annoying, knocking out of the flow and immersion.

What I liked:

A. The developers are clearly old school players. Iteration of music from Diablo, grimm aesthetic, trip to tristram - it's all very cool.
B. This is a very interesting concept and probably my childhood dream.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game :D
It has it all, almost. It has the weapons variation, the magic casting, the stats and traits and it's even co-op. What it's lacking for me so far is the UI/Menu, it's a bit complicated and not always easy to read.
Something that could also bring more variation are small puzzles or voice acting in some way.
Otherwise, this game captures the combat very well. Depending on your location, your enemies will dash and move. I have also realised that they try cutting you off, attacking your blindspots.

Something the combat also does really well is letting you hold your weapon in any way. I was cut of from both sides so I had to hold my weapon in the middle to quickly bludgeon the enemy to the right so i had time to parry and attack the enemy to the left.

Again, this game lacks a few things and might not be worth over 30 euros (I'd buy it for like 25) but it is good at what it is meant to be good at. A medieval dungeon crawler focused on combat with Magic and swords.\t\t\t\t\t
