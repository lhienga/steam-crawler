\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLots of potential there, but wayyy overpriced for the little content there is. Literally 2 mobs in the whole game, every dungeon is randomly generated, but made of the same rooms, and theres like 8 different room style in the game. By the time you reach floor 2 of 10 you'll pretty much know every room, and every secret by heart. Most of the game is a copy/paste of the ideology of diablo 1 from way back then. Music, town, mechanics and all! For an early game access game with such a shallow pool of content and variety on top of the lack of polish and game update since the beginning I cannot recommend this game. It's a shame because this game as HUGE potential, but all I can notice after an hour of gameplay is just how lazy the design is even for a VR game. There wasn't much need for thought when it comes to concept as it was almost entirely inspired from diablo 1 like I said earlier. Blade and sorcery is miles ahead when it comes to polishing and fighting mechanic. I mean holy crap how can you ask for such a huge pricetag with such a janky mess of repetitive content? Done the first floor? You've seen it all ladies and gentlemen! Even when it comes to the weapon skins i'm not joking! First level and you'll pretty much have seen all the different looks of helmets, gloves and weapons the game has to offer. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game that I've put plenty of hours into already, though haven't beaten. I have my gripes about it, namely the fact that enemies get in your face and then quickstep closer while you're backing away for a proper swing and/or deflect. The deflection system is satisfying. Enemy variation needs some polish, but that's being ironed out in the next big update, I guess. Another gripe is that not all weapons (and shields) are accurate. A small detail, but the scutum (I usually like to play defensive tank) is hard to use properly with the handle as it is. Devs should put it in the center of the shield and turn the handle 90, like a proper Roman Scutum shield. Games like these have encouraged me to really get into swordplay, even though I don't have the time or funds to join an actual club. Youtube is your friend in that scenario :).

All in all, I enjoy the game for what it is. Still haven't got any friends I can play with, but maybe I'll try some public matchmaking soon, test the stability of the servers and my internet myself.\t\t\t\t\t

A Personal Journey of Disappointment with Legendary (Fails) Tales

As an avid fan of Legendary Tales, my journey with this game has been nothing short of frustrating and disheartening. Year after year, the developers have dangled the promise of a new and improved remake, only to snatch it away with each passing deadline.

The disappointment began with the last patch, a mere trickle of content released on 06-July-2022. Since then, the developers have continuously teased the much-anticipated 1.0 update, but each time, the release date has been pushed back, leaving players like myself in a perpetual state of waiting.

Let's walk through the timeline of broken promises and dashed hopes. In 2022, the excitement was palpable as the game was supposed to release that year. However, by the end of the year, the developers had already changed their plans. Halfway through 2023, the community was hit with the bombshell that the entire update needed a remake because of a switch from Unreal 5 to Unreal 4. The reassurance from the discord chat that "it's 101% releasing this year" turned out to be another hollow pledge.

Now, at the close of 2023, we're met with yet another delay, and the once-promising future of Legendary Tales is shrouded in uncertainty. It's disheartening to see a game I love so much become a victim of unfulfilled promises and constant setbacks.

Despite my genuine affection for the game, I find it hard to overlook the issues plaguing Legendary Tales. The high price tag, combined with misleading promotional content showcasing features not yet implemented, only adds to the frustration. The controls have their fair share of jankiness, and the lack of full-body support is a missed opportunity.

I find myself torn between the enjoyment of the current state of the game, with its engaging combat system, skill trees, magic, and looting, and the bitterness of being let down time and time again. I yearn for the day when the 1.0 update finally sees the light of day, but until then, it's challenging to recommend Legendary Tales without reservations.

In conclusion, my personal experience with Legendary Tales has been defined by a rollercoaster of emotions, from excitement to frustration. The game's potential is evident, but the constant delays and unfulfilled promises have cast a shadow over what could have been a fantastic gaming experience. Until the developers deliver on their commitments, I must caution others to approach Legendary Tales with a healthy dose of skepticism and a preparedness for potential disappointment.
