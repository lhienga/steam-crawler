Really good, love the combat. It's more videogamey than purely physics combat but that also makes it more interesting to me. Especially once you're later in the game with abilities and weapons with modifiers, it matters whether you slash or stab or pommel strike with a sword. Parrying isn't required but causing a stun+damage bonus makes it rewarding. And remember to actually parry into the strike, not just limply hold your sword in the path (only shields work that way). When you're facing several enemies a clean fight requires watching animations, juggling parries and distance, striking around enemies' guard/armor, using abilities to the greatest effect, and it all feels great. There's a nice variety of weapons and they're a joy to experiment with.

I'm happy with the playtime I'm getting out of the game in its current state. It adds a few new enemies as you progress but admittedly the content is a bit thin and will start to repeat. However the gameplay is too fun and there is enough progression to keep it entertaining. If the devs deliver anything close to what's in the next update's preview then this game will absolutely be top tier.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is absolutely phenomenal. The combat and difficulty scaling is solid and keeps the game from being too easy and is actually quite a challenge if you plan on soloing through later levels but is entirely possible. The AI itself also improves throughout the game so hitting endgame isn't just an enemy with a massive HP pool, its a relatively big pool with enemies that actively try to out smart you by fainting attacks. The mechanics (for those of you that are like me and are what I like to call "mechanically insane") go very in-depth and all weapons either scale with 'Str', 'Dex', and 'Int'. Leveling your character is amazing fun and the skills in the skill tree are extraordinarily unique and enable *very* different play styles. With one character, you can use a sort of pseudo-stealth 'Dex-tard' capable of dealing increased damage whenever an enemy is hit with 2 weapons at once, or a headstrong two-handed 'Str' monkey that receives damage increases whenever using slashing damage with a sword at an enemies head. It can take anywhere from 10-20 hours to completely max out a character and even longer if you want to find the perfect mythic item for yourself (there are a lot of them and they are AMAZING). It all can be as deep and complex or as superficial as you'd like.

Now for the few issues that I personally have come across. While some are simple gripes, one is a glaring issues that is in the process of being fixed. I will start small and move my way up.
-First, the dungeon corridors are small (Lmao and yes, by small issues I really mean SMALL). With any other weapon type its extraordinarily easy to navigate and kill any enemies you find there but larger weapons and/or shields are almost unusable. This is less so an issue with the game (I love the feeling of the cramped hallways) and is rather more so caution sign to those BEEG weapon monkies like me.
-Secondly, once you hit lvl 50, there is no real *character* progression past that point. There are tons of cool weapons and armors to find and equip but once you hit that point but any form of character leveling is nipped just after your build is starting to come together. Personally, some mastery system would be nice but the in depth stats for the weapons do far more than enough to carry the fun after.
-Next up, enemy variety. There are basically 3 enemies in this game. Goblins, Bats, and the very, VERY diverse assortment of skeletons. Personally, I absolutely love what they did with the skeleton. Its a very good base model, and depending on where in the game they are, they can look old and brittle, or armed to the teeth in cold steel with very different fighting styles and combat patterns between one another. Goblins and bats are more of a nuisance than anything else (believe me when I tell you that being over 6 foot and trying to a 2-3 foot tall gremlin that runs away after every attack is very aggravating XD). While the way they got over the design hurdle is very unique indeed, it still leaves something to be desired. This game uses the same skin for vastly different enemies. Although this VASTLY beats out fighting the same enemy but just larger and re-skined, cosmetically everything just feels really same-y. This leads into the biggest issue I have with the game.
-Lastly, the lack of cosmetic difference. Although the dungeons are so extraordinarily expansive and there are so many different rooms that you may not even come across same room no more twice in 5-10 hours, everything just looks the same. There are like maybe 2-4 different types of flooring and walls that you will see throughout the entire game and just ***looks*** boring. Its all either "generic_dungeon_textile_1" or "generic_dungeon_textile_2". I love this game so much but its what happens when you have 9/10 game mechanics but reuse game assets and use dungeon crawling to carry the game-play loop (although I love and stand with the decision, there just isn't much to it).

"'Arbitrary closing thoughts and misc comments' statement". As you can tell and as I've said several times, I am very adamant about this games success. As a VR game, it does quite literally everything right. Weapons are swift and responsive but have a distinguished weight to them, the UIs are intuitive and easy to use, and its multiplayer. Those three facts alone provide a much better base than many of the VR games out there, but when you throw all the mechanics and leveling and everything else mechanically I've talked about, the game is an absolute gold mine. It just needs that layer of polish to really make it pop out among the rest. And it looks like it should be pretty soon. New world update was teased pretty recently and I am so hyped for it. New dungeon settings, new monsters, crafting/smithing your own weapons, an entire (frankly massive) open world to explore, and so much more. If you are looking for a good, in-depth, melee-centric, game-play cycle that is harsh, rewarding, responsive, and above all fun, this game is for you. If this game isn't where you want it to be cosmetically, trust and believe that you will be back here in a couple months or even a years time, perhaps even reading this same review again. Especially because its still only in early access. Buy it. Buy the game. On-sale, original price, it doesn't matter. This is a gameplay experience everyone should have. You dont have a VR headset? Buy this game, then buy the headset. You will thank me later.
