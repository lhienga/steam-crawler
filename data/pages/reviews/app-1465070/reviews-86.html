\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a no from me chief.

Tutorial is far too long. Already a bad taste from that as it was slowly hand holding you. I don't like games that treat you as if you can't figure anything out by yourself. This needs to be optional. Red flag one.

Combat is boring and slow. Also clunky. You start off pitifully weak so it's a grind from the beginning to build a little endurance. Because everything is physics bound, things bounce off walls etc. Led to annoying issues with the bow where its corners would clip onto wall corners, skewing shots. Selling stuff from your inventory was a pain, too. Body tends to slide off things. Huge QOL issue there. I'm cool with physics based worlds, but I found this one to be intrusive to the gameplay. More annoying than immersive.

Stats system seems cool, but my experience was it heavily favoured STR drops. Not interesting. Itemization needs to be much more dynamic.

Core gameplay loop and idea is good. Nice aesthetic. Potential is big, but right now very janky and not even remotely close to the asking price.

At $15? A solid maybe. $35? Get outta here. That's like 2kg of Turkish Dried Figs. Figs much better (really into figs recently) option atm.

May be solid in the future, but for now a refund. Needs a lot of QOL work. Tutorial needs to go, too. GL to the devs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview Subject to Change

This is a good dungeon crawler.

THE GOOD:

Lots of variety with the weapons be that of sword variants, axes, mauls, maces, clubs. I have not found any polearms as of writing. (they added spears and spears are AMAZING)

Enemy intelligence is fairly good with the AI trying to flank the player as well as changing up their attack patterns on the regular. You will find some enemies to be repetitive in design but they can still be hell in groups.


ISSUES:

Main issue that me and a few friends have had experienced is some dungeon generation does not lead to any exit to the next floor. (Close the game and reopen it and it will generate a new dungeon)

A secondary issue is that the bonus wave based challenge room will lock the 2nd participant in the starting room by not opening the door for the player but the mobs will be able to enter anyway

CONCLUSION:

I want to see this game get some TLC when it comes to dungeon generation. Have your system test the generation to see if it is possible to get from beginning to end and if it isn't have it regenerate the rooms causing the issue until it resolves. (If it doesn't reach an end for now just go to main menu and reload the save)
When it comes to the challenge rooms locking in players simply just dont close the door after each round and it will solve the issue. Most players wont use that room as it is really poor for melee vs melee as you cut off any escape routes to kite the enemies. Cut down on the tutorial I does not need to be as long as it is. Add a few more enemy types to spice up the battles not just subtypes but whole new creatures. Add unique floor bosses that guard the exit, I would make for a challenge and to help you test your build before you move on. MORE DEX WEAPONS use polearms such as the halberd or the pike for this as they do require some technical skill to use well. and Change the broadsword weapon to be called Rapier and make it a DEX weapon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played about 10 hours both single player and coop. This is one of the most fun I have had in a VR game to date. The swordplay is fantastic and the models for them are better. The legendary items feel legendary, they give greater stats and have extremely special abilities that match the weapon. Combat is fluent and intuitive.

Now for the bad, The coop from my experience is laggy and chunky at times. Sometimes when you parry an enemy, the strike still hits you. Goblins are very hard to defend against. The early game is a pain in the ♥♥♥♥ because you do not know anything and are squishy as hell. The only problem I have is the economy, in coop this is impossible, loot is not shared and generally there isn't enough to go around.

I know my cons are just as long as my positives, but those cons sometimes just do not happen at all. Where as the positives shine through very bright.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I was on the fence about this game at first. It definitely has major potential, but there are some kinks to work out and content to add (The Devs seem to be fairly active from what iv seen so im sure with time these will come). After doing the tutorial, i almost wanted to refund it, but i gave it a chance and kept pushing, and once you get the concept down for the combat (it is NOT Blade & Sorcery, although its not as arcade-y as VR Dungeon Knight) Its actually a solid game even in the current state. The thing to know, is it is step for step a replica of Diablo 1 in VR. Which is what drew me in once i saw the town upon completing the tutorial. (Dat Tristram Music Doe XD) If the price point was lower, i would recommend it 10/10 even in EA as it sits. But seeing as it is $35, i can only recommend buying this now if you are:
A. A huge VR buff who plays all the new VR games
B. A massive Diablo 1 fan (me)
C. Buying with friends to play with friends

Its definitely one to watch though, put it on your wishlist for sure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just have to spread the word a bit on how great this title is. Right away I'm damn impressed with how fleshed out all of the game mechanics are. The game has come into Early Access feeling to me like a full on complete Co-op dungeon crawler experience.

The physics based combat is some of the best I've felt yet, really is right up there with Blade & Sorcery, Tales of Glory, and Boneworks. However, in Lengendary Tales you can't just sprint up to an enemy and instantly pick him up and shuck him around like he's nothing. You can still grab and pick enemies up, but it's balanced in a way so that you have to parry an enemy attack 1st to create an opening to grab them.

The actual melee combat itself is awesome. I practice HEMA Longsword in real life so when I found how well some of my techniques actually translated into this game it blew me away. Feels so good getting into a low guard just to up parry the one skeleton away as I turn and stab his friend.

Then you add a friend into your game and slaying enemies gets pretty creative. Nothing better than going up to the skeley that my buddy has pinned and just bashing it's skull across the dungeon in true home run fashion. In my experience the coop players are synced up very very well. It's pretty wild how you can faux duel and manipulate each other's weapons so accurately; I can only hope the Dev will add a PvP or Dueling option as it looks like it could work great!

After the tutorial you start off by clearing your way to the main hub, and once you do you are bound for a hit of some nice nostalgia if you ever played Diablo 1. Once in town you have access to what I call the cathedral of hell, where it appears there could be close to 10 layers to descend upon and explore. So far I cleared 2 layers in co-op and in that time I've found loads of cool and unique weapons that are all a blast to use. I'm actually impressed with all of the variety in this game. Even the dungeon is more varied than I was expecting.

Lastly, one of the best parts for replayability is how important your stats really are. In true RPG fashion if you make an all magic character with only 1 point into strength don't expect to slay much of anything with your sword until you level up that strength, but your magic will chill the bones of your enemies and be quite satisfying to use. With what's out now I'm guessing it'll take me at least ~40hrs with 1 character to make it to the end. Though I have already made 3 characters and have plans for a 4th.

To those of you interested I say dive in now and support the Dev! You won't be disappointed in what's already here I can't wait to keep grinding this game, but my body's a little too sore from slaying for 4hrs straight lastnight haha.

As of right now there is no matchmaking so you have to have a steam friend to play online co-op. Here's the official Legendary Tales Discord if you want to find someone to play with.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a frustrating one. Some neat ideas here, with some good-feeling gameplay when it works. When it works. Which isn't always.

Two handed weapons remain sluggish, imperfect, handling as if they're eighty or so pounds heavier than in reality. The way you swing your controllers never really lines up with how your swing in game comes out. Blade&Sorcery remains the gold standard on two-handed weapon handling.

The game's balance is currently extremely bad. I had a floor spawn two Boss Skellingtons with 600 HP in the same room before I could ever even hope to have a chance to find equipment that's balanced for that floor. One of them also revived with full HP after dying, so that was "fun". The other had the berserk trait causing him to do double damage and attack much faster at half HP. Neither of these skelly-boys had anything to give any indication that this was the case aside from being taller than the mook skeletons.

Sword and board work well, parrying with the buckler in particular is very satisfying.

Your mobility is severely lacking. Most certainly a game design decision. Your movement speed, even while "running" is barely enough to get away from any of the two enemy types. Your backpedal speed is slower than a snails pace.

This itself isn't a bad thing; in theory it means you're meant to stand your ground and fight, but in practice it means when an enemy inevitably gets literally inches away from you it makes it impossible to get away or defend because their swings go through your weapons at that distance and just hit you.

Don't even think of moving back in reality either, because the enemies just slide after you 1:1 with your IRL movement speed; especially that jumping slash, this game does the common trope of enemies being able to do a jumping attack and turn in midair to hit you.

This becomes less of an issue once you finally through luck of the almighty item drop RNG find some decent armor that prevents you from getting killed in two hits.


It's hard to recommend this in it's current state and price point. Regardless of my gripes I do keep coming back to it. I'd say if you don't mind some seriously nasty early game Hell, and are willing to just accept that the RNG can give you a really, really, really lousy floor and enemies, sure, give it a go. It can only get better as it's updated, after all!\t\t\t\t\t
