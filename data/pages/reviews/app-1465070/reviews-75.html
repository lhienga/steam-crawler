\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is insane levels of fun, especially if you have idiot friends to play with. The level of nuance and attention to detail in the combat is seriously impressive for a VR game. Definitely beats any other generic hack and slash VR title out there currently, not only from a mechanical aspect, but also from being able to play with up to 3 other friends. Great visuals, great combat, good times, good COOP, experienced no issues with multiplayer whatsoever. I think only experienced one glitch so far as it is, and it was not at all gamebreaking, only very minor. For minor access titles go, this is a seriously impressive VR game. Think there are some things I would recommend developer to try upgrading and improving in the future regarding some of the animations for the player models and maybe add some custom features and appearances to player models but aside from that, honestly this game comes as close to a perfect 10/10 as a VR game can for me. 9.5/10 definitely would recommend!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDiablo-esque dungeon crawler in VR. Having a ton of fun and not getting bored yet.

* You have to get good. Mobs are challenging and there's a lot of them. Sneaking, running, and actually parrying your way through them feels impossible, and then really rewarding once you manage it. This isn't a game where you're OP and blast through hordes of enemies for fun, this is a game where you sweat to try and get through a room with six mobs and feel like you've really pulled off something great when you've finally done it.
* Co-op dungeon crawling, just great.
* Rogue playstyle is a lot of fun. You find and stab the weak point to crit, Feather them from above. Can't speak to the other play styles.
* Rough around the edges, EA game. Not many enemy types, some inventory interactions and weapon collisions feel off.
* ARPG/Dark Souls tradition of stats being hard to understand and easy to level suboptimally.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLegendary Tales is in desperate need of some rebalancing in the favor of the player, it's easily at its best when assisting a lower level player in floors that have been cleared before. While this can be a decent way of earning some gold it isn't good for progression, XP gain or finding anything that you might want to keep. The fun factor, however, is ramped up when the player is at least on the same level as the enemies they're facing from a health / damage standpoint.

Alternatively, simply nerfing enemy health might be all that's needed. The game will pressure you into decisions; ranging, tanking and dpsing - and having the constant threat of a swift death with the reward of skillful play leading to quick(er) clears would only make the current system more satisfying.

Floors may take you hours to clear on their own. You'll be surprised every now and again with what you find in the labyrinthine dungeon.

Parrying is usually effective and always satisfying though outright dodging is far the greater rush.

Magic is being reworked, and that might make a true mage build a more attractive role to play.\t\t\t\t\t
