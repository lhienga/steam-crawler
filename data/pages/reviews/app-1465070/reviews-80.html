\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm loving the gameplay, this was the sort of experience i always wanted in VR.
Swordplay with physics, magic and archery ( Like Blade and sorcery), stats and progression and actual proper inbuilt coop.

Sadly i did not expect it to be this.. scary.
I have a low fear tolerance, and having almost exclusively well rendered skeletons enemies rush you in herds, with some tiny goblins sneaking up and bats jumping on you in dark rooms and corridors isn't really easy for me to handle. Not to mention sometimes the developer goes full horror when it spawns enemies out of thin air behind you when you open a chest for example.

One main issue i seem to have is being unable to properly swing a bigger sword. I'm not sure if it's the game being buggy or the actual Rift S is having tracking issues with such a large object that goes off center but it's really impossible to use.

Would've love the game far more if the setting was less dark and included fighting more actual humans , in light and open spaces.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game brings Dungeon crawler to VR extremely well.

Out of my entire playtime I've notices *almost* no bugs and the game feels extremely polished, especially for an Early access game released not too long ago.The weight of the weapons are done very well and the position of your hand on the handle matters and affects your swing. Overall the combat and physics are very impressive, even so you can use objects (mainly vases and barrels) to hit enemies, and break them for health, mana, or loot.

There's also a large variety of weapons to choose from and fit a variety of play styles
including a selection of spells and legendary weapons with each of their own unique abilities to help you
hack and slash your way through enemies.

Overall the game play feels very nice and I'm happy, and impressed with it

As for sound design, the game sounds pretty nice, from walking to swords clanging it does a pretty good job, including environmental sounds and BGM.

And Finally Visuals, the game looks very nice especially some of the dungeon rooms with tiles and reflective floors as well as the items such as weapons.

Now for some things i don't like

In this dungeon crawler you go through a variety of floors to get to the final one with a boss. Currently there is only one dungeon and that is Abbey. My only issue with this is these floors can get lengthy and can take a while to complete, and that would be fine but there is no way to save ur progress on that floor, meaning you have to finish that floor in one game session, if you leave even to just the menu, or crash, you have to start that floor over again, on a completely new dungeon (the dungeons are always randomized)

The enemies can also can get really beefy, i dont know if im playing the game wrong but im currently on floor 6, with weapons that do around 10-20 damage, but the enemies have 400 health each. so when fighting hordes of skeletons it becomes quite a challenge, ive used the blacksmith and blackmarket to get better gear, and i also wait the 1.5 seconds before hitting again (doing this gets the most damage out of your weapon) so if im doin something wrong someone please let me know, if this is an intentional game design thats fine its still super fun!




Dont let the price turn you off from the game, its well worth the money and will be worth it event more when its out of Early Access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not big on writing reviews, but I'll make an exception here. I had the pleasure of being one of the closed beta testers and I can rightfully say that this game is phenomenal. It was already good back then and it has only improved since launch. This is my #1 VR purchase of 2021 so far, hands down.

It runs well, looks good, has physics based combat, parkour, tons of replay value, 4 player Co-Op support...what more could you ask for?

The only negative I can say currently would be the lack of enemy variety. Skeletons and Goblins are the bread and butter with two other minor enemies thrown in here and there. There is a new one being worked on as I write this though, so I hope to see many more in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
