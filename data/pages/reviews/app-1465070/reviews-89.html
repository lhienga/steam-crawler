\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't buy this game expecting Blade & Sorcery or Hellsplit, it's not trying to be either of those at all. You will be disappointed if that is what you're looking for. First and foremost it is a multiplayer dungeon crawler, more akin to Karnage Chronicles but with much more substance and much less style and polish.

You really do need to slog out the tutorial, and it is a bit long-winded, AND the first area which must be completed to play multiplayer feels like a redundant tutorial 2.0, however there are a lot of mechanics that you'll miss which are integral to the gameplay. Once you understand these mechanics, the combat is slow and methodical, with a lot of room to mix skills and abilities.

Visually, it is kinda ass. The lighting is weirdly good, but the dungeons are pretty bleak and sameish. The UI is slightly clunky, as are some of the controls. Not super intuitive, but it gives it a bit of an oldschool RPG feel where it gives you the info you need without trying to be too slick or streamlined. The sound design is just as lacking. The game really lacks style.

Grinding through the dungeon is discouraging when death takes you back to the start, but then you realise it's actually kind of a rougelike. And if you play MP, your friend can open a portal to bring you back much sooner.

I feel this game will be very misunderstood, but give it time and patience and it's actually really rewarding and satisfying. Definitely play it MP though, it'd be a bit lonely and boring solo. Also, the netcode is amazing. Other players look janky sometimes, but the physics and interactions work surprisingly well at low ping.

The shortcomings of this game are really inconsequential, the substance of the gameplay mechanics and progression loop more than make up for it. The online works really well amd you can tell it is the primary selling point. That said, I'm surprised they haven't got a server list or matchmaking option. It'd be great if the town acted as a hub world where everyone online can be seen as a ghost, then you can view their character profile and buddy up. I highly recommend this game if you have a few mates to play with. Unsure if I can recommend a solo experience though.

TL;DR: Unforgiving Diablo-esque/rougelike MP dungeon crawler, with a surprising amount of substance despite a lack of style and polish.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWish i could give a neutral review. I can tell love went into the game, but its not there yet. battling is fun, and I like the combat(i've not played multiplayer cant comment on that). Graphics are well done, and i can see repeatable game play for just loot grinding alone.

However, my negs:

I feel forced to play one way, at least starting off. you play either well rounded and do not enough dmg, or go in mostly str and now cant do any dmg with a bow, or vice versa with melee. Dmg is based on attributes, but its not scaled properly i feel. You do too little dmg one vs the other. until you just get way high level i suppose and well rounded.

not all objects are breakable, and you dont know until you swing at everything, thats odd?
aiming with a bow is pretty difficult to say the least.
picking up items can be a chore, they should highlight on the ground when hovered that may help.


I want to like the game and a bit i do, i just cant commit until i see what the developers do with changes down the line. with this price tag, I hope they can for their sake.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I've been eye ballin this game for quite a while. Big fan of melee games and I have several hours in Blade and Sorcery, Karnage, VR Dungeon Knight, Crypt Crawler, Gorn, Tales of Glory and Asgard's Wrath. The closes I can compare this game to, is more like Karnage/VR Dungeon Knight. Now hear me out, everyone is comparing it B&S but that game is a bit different. As of now, B&S is murdering humans and nothing else. There are a lot of plans to add dungeons and loot and such but as of now, it's a Jedi/Sith VR game.

Combat in this game is more like Karnage or VR Dungeon due to the fact that the enemies are a bit meaty and well...you're in a dungeon battling monsters, collecting loot, cash, and gaining levels. Legendary Tales, however, is like the "serious" version of these two games with a heavy Diablo 1 influence. The more you use your skills the more you gain points into those things. Swinging swords and hammers increase your strength while take hits helps increase your vitality.

*So let's break down the main part of this game, Combat:

In LT (Legendary Tales) you can wail on the enemy just as you would in Dungeon Knight, Karnage, or B&S. Just like in the aforementioned games, you can parry to leave the enemy open for a strong opening hit. When you do a strong parry in LT, you can get a good solid hit or just grab them and pummel them to death. I had no issue blocking with a dagger and countering with my sword.

You have pierce, slash, and blunt damage. So poking with a sword is piercing, slashing is...slashing, and smacking them with your hilt is blunt damage. Oh, and there is actual half swording in this game.

There's bows, clay pots, wooden folding chair, ripping off their arms to beat another skeleton down, throw your melee weapons for damage and magic. Though, compared to B&S, magic and bows are a lot easeir to use.

*Second big thing about this game: Dungeon Crawling.

Just like other looter/dungeon games there is random loot. What makes it fun but yet painful, is finding a new weapon that you can't use yet because you are not a high enough level. You have an inventory bag that you always have on you, a stash box to save valuables to share with friends, and a potion system similar to Diablo. When you die, you drop all of your loot and some cash. You can always try and fight your way back to retrieve your loot or pay a little bit of money to have it auto retrieved, minus the cash.

*Third thing, which is on the weaker side (as of now) Enemies:

So far it has been slow skeletons, a fast skeleton, and a little goblin character. Honestly, the goblin is the hardest because he's knee height and you have to get low to fight them. I don't expect the skeleton's to bleed cause...they are skeletons, but I was expecting at least purple blood from the goblins. As of now, it's a bit of a work out fighting because they are a bit spongey. I mean, it could be because I was level 2 with low points in strength and basic weaponry. That's to be seen.

* The game does have 4 person coop but I wasn't able to do it because currently its only playable with friends. No public lobbies.

Conclusion:

So far, the environment is quite interesting and it's a workout fighting these spongey creatures. Would I recommend this game in it's current state? That's a hard one. For an early access game, there's a lot to do, say compared to Blade and Sorcery. At the same time, the asking price of 35 bucks is a bit much. Especially when Boneworks, Saints and Sinners, Karnage/VR Dungeon Knight, and Tales of Glory cost a lot less for a lot more. In the case of Saints and Sinners, it's 5 bucks more for a full complete game. I honestly think, in it's current state, it should be 25 bucks and maybe increase it to its current price when the game fully releases.


*So I guess it comes down to this:


You got extra cash burning a hole in your pocket? Then pick this game up.

If you don't have extra cash, no need to pick this game up right now. BUT, do keep an eye on it.

I'm only refunding it because of the latter mentioned above.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMultiplayer Blade and Sorcery with your friends. Usually that's all I need to say, but it also comes packed with a unique leveling system and a host of RPG elements that I haven't seen included in any other VR game out right now. Some of the reviewers are saying its not worth it, but that's simply not true. If you have friends with VR headsets, this game is a must because the melee combat works so smoothly online. Sure if you play solo, Blade and Sorcery would be the better choice, but this game is more tailored towards co-op play, where one of you defends while another stabs from behind and one more shoots arrows from a distance. Every enemy, no matter the number, felt stressful but was ultimately beatable. My friend had a harder time with the tutorial than I did but once he was able to get into a game with me, we popped off. 3 hours later and we only got through the first level.

If you want to talk about worth here's your diagram:
No friends --> Blade and Sorcery ($25)
Friends --> Legendary Tales ($35)
$10 for multiplayer is not asking all that much, especially for people that have someone they enjoy playing with.\t\t\t\t\t
