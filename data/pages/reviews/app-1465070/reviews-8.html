\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery much a classic RPG. Definitely has some Diablo inspirations. So much fun. Few things to keep on mind. First, read a guide or video and join the discord or reddit community for tips and advice. The tutorial is kinda lacking.
- Fires heal. Use the teleport ability often like we used to do in Diablo.
- Get the dash skill that makes it cost 1 to move more across the map.
- Get the skill that auto-fills mana as well.
- Careful with your character skills. This is more careful, old school rpg - choices matter and you have one respec.
- Look up a guide for crafting.
- There is no map. It’s easy to get lost. That’s ok.

All in all, I’m loving this game and I still have a ton to see.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is, to put it as simply as I can, what happens if Diablo 2 and Blade and Sorcery have a baby. The story isn't very well translated, as this is an independent development team from an Asian country (I want to say Korea), but it provides a clear indication of things to do and try out, places to go, etc. In short, it gives you the guideposts for the game in general. Beyond that, it's much more about exploring and murdering your way through enemies to find sick loot, and you can do it in all kinds of ways! I've played double daggers, spear and buckler, and bow on my dex character (I prefer the spear and buckler combo the most), I've played a lightning mage (super sick calling down lightning bolts on enemy heads), and a 2h-sword wielder. You can also just straight punch everything to death like some kind of fantasy world Saitama. All are fun, all are viable.

The launch was a little rough with controllers and lost save files, but they've patched the problems and the game plays great. The cross play with PSVR2 is seamless, and in general, I can't recommend this game enough for all VR players. It's taken all my gaming time since its release, so ignore my relatively short play time. I have to be very choosy about what I play, and this game has me by the gonads.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, I highly recommend. I've been playing ARPGs since the original Diablo. Has everything you'd want. I'd say more like a classic ARPG, unlike a lot of these newer ones that over complicate everything. Crafting and skill system are simple yet in depth enough to be immersive. The combat is progressively challenging and realistic enough. Block, parry, deflect, strike, cast spells, go total beast mode. Choice is yours. It's has freedom to explore, fight, craft and create your perfect character.

Devs are very active and they listen to us, game launched only 2 weeks ago and already 2 updates to fix the few issues there was. They believe in this masterpiece they've created and want it to succeed.

I agree it's a bit on the pricy side but I still think it's worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
