\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend to play with friends or if you need a new VR adventure. The swordplay is very nice and fighting skellys and parrying their attacks is really unique, but the game is sooo clunky at the moment.

Pros:
For coming out a few weeks ago this game has a lot to offer, lots of different enemies, really cool dungeon design and a ton of weapons with different styles to using them. With all it has to offer, the bugs and glitches that this game has kinda makes it not fun at times.

Multiplayer is a really cool aspect of the game, not only do you get to defeat challenging bosses and complete dungeons with your friends, you get a ton of loot and can share it among multiple people. Games like this don't really exist so having a hack and slash multiplayer VR game gives friend groups and people more options.

Cons:
Attacking on stairs is almost impossible unless you are below your enemy. For some reason if you are being chased up some stairs, the enemies can slash your feet very easily, killing you if there is a swarm. This comes with being incredibly slow when you walk.

There is no sprinting or dodging, only a slow jog and a walking pace. It would be cool to move through tunnels and dungeons a little faster.

The little goblins have such a weird attack style. They shoot in a straight line at you, and a straight line away when they are retreating. More than a couple of those guys surrounding you is almost guaranteed death. They only slash at your legs which makes sense but is very annoying at times and can be a little OP.

There isn't really a storyline or any character customization. Having some story to follow or quests of some sorts would really immerse the player in having a goal, but when it comes to the game, you're just thrown into a dungeon and you have to fight your way through to an end that is nowhere in sight. Character customization would be neat too considering it is a multiplayer game, having different colors or styles of armor presets, different faces and skin tones.


All in all with my experience, I would tell people to get this game and try it out with some friends. It's a good grinding VR game and its a new type of fighting game. It is worth it with all the jank that comes with it. I hope the devs read some of the reviews because this game has a lot of potential and I feel that the community can be a pretty good media for finding out what will improve the game over time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnless you're REALLY looking for Co-op, go get Blade & Sorcery when the dungeons update comes out instead. This game is not worth its current price.

This game rips the melee and magic system straight from Blade & Sorcery. It then sticks it into a tile generated dungeon crawler with an RPG stats system and Item stats rolls.

The problem here is that Blade & Sorcery is also in the process of doing this. For $15 less.

In effect, the only thing this game provides that Blade & Sorcery does not, is multiplayer.
Blade & Sorcery, on the other hand, offers more weapons, and a less grindy combat experience.

Combat in this game is heavily stat dependent, while that might sound interesting to you, in practice, it can be very annoying. Especially when you need to hit a standard enemy upward of 7+ times to kill them when starting out in the game. This is also compounded upon by the fact that the dungeon generation does not take balance into account, so your first dungeon could literally begin with a room containing a dozen enemies that you have trouble killing just one of.

This game will probably be worth $35 later on, but not right now. Not with its current issues and content set.

Just go get Blade & Sorcery for $20 and wait for the dungeons update.\t\t\t\t\t
