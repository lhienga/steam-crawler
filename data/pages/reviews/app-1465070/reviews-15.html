\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat began as a hopeful foray into VR dungeon crawling has ended up as another pitiful reminder of the dangers of scope creep and the unchecked ambition of devs who struggle with fundamental features like grip toggle and hit detection. As Legendary Tales limps across the finish line into full release, I find myself amazed that anyone at Urban Wolf Games believes the decision to increase the price to nearly 60 dollars was a rational one. Legendary Tales costs far more than its competition yet is patently worse than all of it. It isn't entirely bad, but for what you're getting, this pricing is shameful. It might stay afloat for awhile since there aren't too many PCVR games like this and especially not on PSVR2, but if Dungeons of Eternity ever makes it to Steam you can pretty much consider this game done for.

Pros: Okay balance between physics and arcadey fighting. Decent variety of weapons and magic. Multiplayer dungeon crawling and looting is always good. The quick access menus and inventory are handy. Being able to adjust the position of holsters is cool. Being able to half-sword and do pommel strikes is always a welcome feature to these kinds of games. The special abilities on some weapons is cool.
Cons: Quests, crafting, NPCs, pretty much everything about Legendary Tales feels stapled together to make something substantial enough to sell. There's a rough, ugly quality to the visuals and audio. Enemies have a nasty way of clipping through your defenses and getting right up in your face. This problem appears to have gotten worse since the latest update, making the use of shields an even more dubious prospect as many of them are too small to be of use against attacks as wonky as these. Your attacks impart no force save for causing enemies to wiggle a little bit, so the monsters cannot be held at bay with thrusting attacks. This means polearms and two-handed swords actually become a liability to use as they tend to get tangled up. In one instance my zweihander clipped into an enemy and I couldn't free it because the blade was simultaneously colliding with a piece of terrain, so my weapon (with my hands still holding it) got abducted until I dropped it. Also, handling two-handed weapons is just not a good time. It's probably not easy to program that sort of interaction, but many other games do it far better. B&S does it better, Hellsweeper does it better, Hellsplit does it better and it doesn't even incorporate the input from the second hand. You have to hold and move your controllers in unnatural ways to make accurate attacks if you're using a weapon with both hands. Half-swording is the only thing that feels good to perform.
The map is big, empty and dull and you move through it so, so slowly. This game wears it Dark Souls influences proudly, so imagine playing Dark Souls while only being able to walk. You have a little dash that costs lots of mana and that's it. I often had a distinct feeling of having my time wasted while I played this game. That's tragic, because it wasn't like this in Early Access. Again, the price of this game is an egregious display of hubris.

I could go on. Look, if your curiosity simply cannot be satisfied, buy Legendary Tales. You'll have plenty of time to apply for a refund after you're done trying it out. If they reduced the cost to around 20 dollars then I could maybe recommend it to people who want to play a multiplayer dungeon crawler in VR but aren't part of the Meta ecosystem. If you are on Quest, again, just get Dungeons of Eternity. It smokes this game and costs way less. I regret paying into Early Access for this.

Edit: 2/19 : The devs have added a hold grip option, bringing the equipping and handling of items up to the minimum standard that almost every VR game follows. If only those items were fun to handle. Since I forced myself to play a couple more hours I have decided to update the pros and cons to reflect a few more of my thoughts about Legendary Tales.\t\t\t\t\t

"I played this game using the Index
After playing this for about 30 minutes I could already tell that this isn't going to be playable for me. Holding weapons feels so wrong. Attempting to point the tip of the blade at the enemy to hold up my guard resulted in my character holding the sword tip up towards the sky. While this is at best a nit-pick, the fact that it's toggle only makes for a truly awful experience when trying to swing my weapon. I am sure there are some people who can easily figure out how to swing a weapon while your hand is open, but I for one simply cannot. It feels unnatural and like I am going to throw my controller through the wall.

I shall be refunding this for the time being, maybe I'll pick it up again in the future if this is fixed."

After trying the game out a tiny bit more it's now in a playable state for those of us who use an index. It's a hell of a lot better than it was before, but it isn't perfect and needs a tad bit of refining. The controls require you to fully press down on the index controller and activate the button within the grips instead of using finger detection to determine rather or not you are holding your weapon, which sometimes leads to finger strain and awkward releasing of your weapon mid-swing.. As someone who is right handed, two handed weapons felt a tad awkward to swing from left to right, but I anticipate with how quickly they squashed the index control issues before that it'll be fixed in a future update.

Onto the actual game that I was able to play in my currently limited schedule. Unfortunately I was unable to play the multiplayer section of the game as of now. Combat feels quite good. strikes and parrying have great impact and the enemies I have found fall into pieces when you deliver finishing blows to certain parts of their bodies. Items were dropping like candy and I quite enjoyed the style of looting where you need to pick up crystals and bust them open to see what goodies you got. Striking damage change depending on what part of the weapon you hit them with and how frequently you strike instead of how fast and hard you hit which does wonders for me within my small play space. My wall thanks the developers for this, as well as my hands. Overall do I recommend this? Well, sorta. It's definitely got some fun to be had here, but the index controls aren't quite there yet. It's definitely playable and the imperfections could be gotten use to if you're patient with it. As for me I'll definitely be playing more of this regardless if the control issues are fixed as I find the game to be likely worth the adjustments from other hack and slash sword fighting vr games like Battle Talent and Blade and Sorcery.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a rpg with a sprawling open world.
It comes with co-op multiplayer, that works.
Playable solo.
It has a questing system with rewards.
You can customize your character's stats (str, dex, int, wis, vit, lck) as well as their active and passive skills.
The skill trees are completely free and open to any character build so you are not locked into any class.(you can be a dual shield tank or a sword and wand mage it doesn't matter you pick the skills and stats)
The combat is a fully physics based combat system with parrying, dodging, blocking, and special attacks.
There are many types of weapons to master. Some are bow, halberd, gladius, parrying dagger, wand, magic, many more.
There is a crafting system so that you can craft weapons and potions.
You can cook meat on the campfire to restore health.
Mobs do not scale with you, so you can revisit areas and farm exp or items.
There are very large dungeons with bosses that fit with the main quest line.
Diverse mob types with different combat skills. Skelley archers, skelley warriors, goblin mages, goblin spearmen, etc..
You can trade items if you find legendaries and they have a discord with an active trading section to coordinate.
You can save at any time.
There is a waypoint system.
This game was made by a team of 5 people and a clear passion project from the depth and variety of rpg systems available.

The combat is ok at best, but again not worth the price you are paying. The visuals fall flat, the audio is poor, and there is no option for hold to grip. I thought I could look past the toggle grip but it absolutely ruins any type of immersion. Compare the overall feel of this to another VR game that came out in the same month: Underdogs and you will see that this is not worth the money whatsoever.

Also, it seems like a major selling point of the game is that you can mess around with ragdolling enemies with your friends, but they don't ragdoll until you hit them down to 1 HP where they proceed to take a knee and just fall over/die at the slightest touch. This means that the beating a ragdolled skeleton as seen in the trailer isn't how the game would actually work as the enemy is already dead and doesn't need to be hit anymore. Some skills later into the game might change this but I'm not going to stick past the refund window for a poorly polished AAA priced indie game.

If this was a $25 or even $30 game this would probably be a mixed and at best positive review. However the devs have decided that because of their development issues and delays due to a failed port to UE5, they should charge an unreaonsable price to the playerbase. Half life Alyx goes on sale pretty often, and the workshop content is free. Baldurs Gate 3 is $60, Elden Ring is on sale for under $40, Vertigo 2 is $30, Into the Radius is $30. Respect your money and wait until this goes on sale for atleast 50%. Its not up to you to forfeit money because devs make mistakes.\t\t\t\t\t
