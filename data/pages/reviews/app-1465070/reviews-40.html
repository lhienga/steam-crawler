\r\n\t\t\t\t\t1.0 Update. Raised price. Poor game design choices. Jank. Long running problems undressed. Appreciate the work that went into this upgrade, but I don't buy effort, I buy products.

I had previously refunded this game, but decided to purchase it again while on sale. Among other issues, I didn't like the developer’s engagement with the community as they seemed unprofessional, full of hubris, and thin skinned.

I think it is overpriced for its current stage of development, but would be fine suggesting it for $30 if this is your genre. While it has many shortcomings, I am certainly glad I picked it up again, as I love a good loot/skill-based dungeon crawler. The Tristram music in town is a nice touch. Put on some Wardruna and smash faces.

It took a while to adjust to the nontraditional key bindings, but they became more intuitive once acclimated. There are a few issues, though. I too often press down on the left thumbstick, which toggles the walk speed. It is painstakingly slow. The max speed is a bit slow in itself, but I read a charge is coming in future updates.

I would like an option to disable haptics for my holsters. Their detection area is broad and it is annoying to have my controllers constantly vibrating while my hands are at my side.
I often quaff pots unintentionally, as these are bound to the Y and B keys of my motion controllers. While user error, it remains frustrating. Not sure a good solution to this without a considerable rework. Manually having to drink the potions would be a nice touch, but quick access to them is essential to survival.

While another user error issue, It is easy to leave your weapons behind when you thought you sheathed them. Fortunately, they show up in your storage back in town. A nice touch. Requiring to press a button to unbind them would be nice. Equipping items through the inventory is cumbersome. It seems you have to unequip your current gear first, which puts them in your hand. From there, I usually just drop them on the ground, equip the new item, then pick up the one I dropped. I simple swap would be more efficient. This, again, may be a user error issue. A pattern is emerging that indicates an unintuitive system. Inventory management still needs work, like being able to manually sort items. It is nice to have it in VR, though, as I can't recall many games that have used suc.

The combat/gear system is the meat of the game. While not as realistic as B&S, it strikes a nice balance. The parrying system is the most enjoyable I have played and feels rewarding to deflect a strike. There are light and heavy attacks, but I am not too clear when I am doing either. If there is an indicator when a heavy strike is completed, I haven't noticed it yet. Barrels. So many barrels. Smash!

The difference between strength and dexterity based builds feel imbalanced, both in performance and options. You get two side slots and functionally one back slot. The possible combinations for dexterity are dagger/buckler/bow: spear, or dagger/dagger/Bow: spear, excluding buckler/buckler (that’s silly) and str weapons.

Strength has Mace/sword/shield:2 handed, Sword/Axe/Shield:2 handed, Axe/mace/Shield:2 handed, Mace/Mace/shield: 2 handed, Sword/Sword/Shield:2 handed, Axe/Axe/Shield:2 handed.

I may have missed something, but that is 4 dex builds vs 12 strength builds. I haven't tried a hybrid build, but intend to do so. A quick solution is to make the hatchet a dex based weapon. There is also a hilted broadsword that is str based, which is already thin enough to pass as a rapier, a traditionally dex based weapon. Just converting those two weapons could grant a little more build diversity.

Unfortunately, the above would also probably require a rearrangement of the skill tree. I like the current skill system, which has enough variety to play around with for multiple characters. It would be nice if it was changed a bit to offer more options to hybrid builds, as it currently feels like I would be gimping myself for taking that route

I ramble. Personally, mechanics are the foundation of a good game, and Legendary Tales has a solid one to build off of. While limited at this stage of its development, with limited enemy types and dungeon layouts, it is still quite addicting. They game also runs well on my system, though I cannot yet use dlss as it doesn’t recognize my 30 series video card.

Edit: Wanted to add that the footsteps are annoyingly loud. Clanking metal. Even among fully geared knights, it was standard to wear leather footwear. Was damn near unplayable until someone helped me out with a fix.

No seated mode. Climbing is sticky, but climbing over a ledge or surface remains an issue. Common problem in VR. Loot is nice and well designed. Legendary items are well done with unique model design and combat traits.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game.

Negatives: Environment is kind of bland. I feel the audio needs quite a bit of work. I can't hear enemies walking towards me. My own movements make noise that's disorienting. The basic walking speed feels far too slow to me; and you end up in situations where you're just constantly moving backwards to try to get space. There are similar sword games with a dash mechanic; allowing you to quickly get to a position where you can more more use of your physical space (as opposed to punching a wall). I've not gotten very far, but it's just running around in dungeons. That's going to get repetitive very quickly

Positives: The combat mechanics are pretty fantastic. You're not bound to a class. I've been using a small shield and sword and have a bow. I can also use a little magic. I value progression in games and this feels like it will take a long time to progress everything. Not because it's grinding; but because there's so many options. While I think the artstyle is bland; it does look nice. The sense of scale is impressive. The dungeon has a different layout each time you start a new game. That's very cool.

Neutral: Some of the weapons are a bit difficult to use. Two handed weapons feel incredibly awkward to me. I like using the bow, but aiming it feels weird. The menu system feels a bit awkward. You're probably going to want to tweak your controls a bit.

You'll likely enjoy this game as long as you don't mind doing nothing but running through dungeons. I'd like more world building. NPC quests, missions, etc. I'm excited to see where it goes.\t\t\t\t\t

Impressed by the weapon handling in this. The weight of everything feels real, I can flip a dagger into the air and actually catch it consistently (unlike say Blade and Sorcery where it will go flying off into space). I didn't realize there were any enemies outside of skeletons, but so far we've seen a mimic, some nicely animated bats, goblins, and a few special goblins and skeleton bosses. Of course, I'd appreciate more enemy variety, and more environment variety. I've made it 3 levels deep and it's a nice surprise when you come into a room that isn't a torch-lit brown dungeon, I just wish there were more.

Honestly, the only thing that B&S does better than this is the gore. The RPG melee system works pretty nicely, and I haven't come across anything that was annoyingly tanky (yet). Timing of attacks and actually physically blocking is important too, which is great, and I haven't had enemies jump into my physical space and screw up my attacks or hit me when they shouldn't (yet).

So far so good. Only played an hour (and planned to refund it because $35 is asking a lot for a game in this state), but it's surprisingly decent.

My main complaint (so far), aside from the boring dungeons, is that reducing the texture resolution makes the menus almost unreadable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like the concept of this game and would love to play it more, but I cannot recommend this game for the price at this time. I am hopeful that this feedback below is addressed so that I can enjoy the game more because it really is fun when it works.

Do not purchase this game if you are 6ft or above. The game does not accommodate you. The closest thing there is to adjustment is the holster height, but even then, it is poorly implemented. Holstering weapons on your back is impossible. I tried the various configs with no luck. My character drops the weapons when I try to holster a two handed weapon. My character also floats above the ground according to my friend. I don't think the player skeleton was made to handle taller players. Ideally, there would be an option to change player height and have the holster points correctly scale with the player.

In addition, the side holsters get in your way constantly. There were many times where I just drop my side-holstered weapons because I tried to pick something up and grabbed my holstered weapons instead. I ask the devs to look into maybe adding an option where you can't pick up your holstered equipment unless you are looking at it or some other way to make it harder to accidentally yeet your gear on the ground.

The controls in general are poorly thought out (on the Index). Why would you have menu/inventory open by FLICKING THE STICK UP???? The same stick you need for movement???? I would like either the ability to rebind or another pass on the controller layout for Index.

The menus themselves look unreadable with low resolution (specifically in the main menu). I am playing on max settings with a 3080Ti, so something is wrong with the rendering of the text. Aside from that, the game performs very well and looks good.

A nice quality of life feature would be the ability to exchange loot with a friend during the dungeon. I have no need for bows but my friend does. But I can't let him keep the bow because the loot system binds weapons to whomever picks up and cracks the crystal. Adding a toggle for this option to share loot during a dungeon dive would be nice.

Climbing is frustrating. How it works with ladders is you grab each rung with your hand and pull yourself up. That part is fine. The issue is when you reach the top. The idea is you are supposed to pull yourself up and over the edge. You will then be quickly warped to the top of the ladder, standing on the stone. Issue I have is sometimes, the warp doesn't trigger unless I REALLY hoist myself up. I have fallen many times because I tried to pull myself up but fail to trigger the warp. I think the warp hitbox needs to be larger so that it's easier for players to warp up. Or add an option where when you reach the top rung you warp (instead of having to push off the stone).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoy this game enough? It's a hard one to explain, but I'll try in so many words.

I think the TLDR is: The next update they have teased sort of worries me.

The TLWR: The dungeoneering needs work, and with the entire game being that at the moment, it's really hard to recommend this game at this pricepoint. Enemies slice right through your defense, hit detection is weird and not very good, calculated wrist wiggling is sometimes more effective than actual fighting despite the "power attack" system. It would be much better if the swordplay was more similar to the systems in B&S where it's purely physics based. Honestly, it shouldn't matter if it leads to more cheezing or something, because the combat as is just doesn't feel satisfying. I can do a power attack, or smash a skeleton in the skull with my hilt, and do 1 damage. Or I can kinda just tap the skele with the flat of my blade and they explode from the 6 damage it did for some reason. There's a weird mixture of too little physics, and a lot. Blades collide with mine, but don't stop, and hit me anyway. I can swing a two handed sword and hit the very tip of the enemy's blade, so when the blade comes careening into it's skull, it does 0 damage?

It's weird, it's messy, it needs a lot of work to shine. The shining grace of this game is that it works online, however, that doesn't make up for lackluster combat.

The movement is slow when running, feels more like walk speed, and the walk speed is... well... who would even use it? haha. It's like RT in super meat boy, you'll never not be using it. They should make a proper run, because backtracking can be a real slog.

The climbing is BAD. Why would you slide your upper hand down when climbing up a rope? You wouldn't, but it happens every time you put one hand over the other unless you IMMEDIATELY let go when you grab again. Your hands slip off when the very wonky detection doesn't deem your hands low enough. I am average height but my friend who is very tall literally couldn't climb or sheath weapons on his back. It makes the game nearly unplayable, and his character model was floating like a foot and a half off the ground.

If you're going to have weird platforming, you should let us jump, because you can't climb walls unless you're off the ground already. Or let us grab the environment as well, or at least corners.

There's something really off with the menu resolution in this game, because the game looks great though with some performance issues, and my resolution can be at 1.0x or 1.2x but when I open a menu it's like watching a video at 144p.

For balance, they need to let us spend skill points on core stat points, if you want to move into a dex build, you basically have to use the worst stuff all over again, and have almost no chance to change or unlock the better abilities. Why are we doing like skyrim style grinding? It takes SO long, crushing every environmental thing and being thorough makes your playtime for one run like 30-ish minutes. Since the backtracking from dead ends due to procedural generation makes it tedious more than fun.

This game has so much potential, but the update that's coming out makes me think that the devs sorta think the base game is finished as is, and can move on to other things, but it's really quite not. We don't need crafting, we need the weapons in game to be refined, we don't need new areas, we need the areas to be better to navigate. We don't need new enemies, we need the ones in game now to work properly. (Though these things would be nice in the future, I'd rather them refine what there is now) There's a complete game in here somewhere, but we need to find it.\t\t\t\t\t
