\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the grim dark setting. There's definitely a Diablo feel to aspects of the game. I do see promise in the game but....

https://youtu.be/Ru_TuhlOTt4

I wasn't as in love with the combat as I thought I would be. I'm hopeful that higher level enemies will pose more of a challenge and EA updates that add more enemies will bring more diversity to the combat. But specifically the attempt to make weapons feel 'weighty' just adds disconnect to what I'm doing vs what I wanted to do. I swing my arm too fast for the heavy weapon and instead of following the path my arms took it tries to straight line from where it is to where my hands are. This can cause what was supposed to be a hit into a miss. This will likely keep me from using any strength based builds whatsoever. It's also not really my preference anyway but still. Other games have done this better where you see a disconnected ghost hand for where your IRL hands are and your game hands follow that path attempting to keep up. IDK if adding more strength will minimize this but the base mechanic seems to need to be retooled a bit.

Throwing was a bit of a pain. There probably needs to be some assistance here. Throwing in VR in general is typically painful. To throw straight you had to throw at a 45 deg angle up which is harder than it sounds when your brain is telling you that's not how that works...

Archery felt good. Though there may have been some aim assist there. That headshot in the prison area didn't seem like it was my skill at all. Thoroughly expected to miss that not decapitate him.

I really liked the stealth crit hit mechanic but I wished there was more of a specific stealth mechanic. Some indicator that I'm hidden and correctly stealthing. From my point of view I'm just walking and clanking around in heavy armor according to my avatar.

Speaking of Avatars, I'd love to see some customization here. Especially in a MP game I feel this is necessary. I need to distinguish my identity from other individuals. I'd also like it if the gloves I was wearing were reflected in my avatar.
And using that to segue into the equipment screen, some work is needed here. I loved the look and feel. The interaction was a bit clunky and needs some smoothing out. Let me just grab items and put them in other spots. I'm going to want to reorganize stuff. It shouldn't take 2 clicks a grip and a release to sell something that could be a single grip and release. It doesn't sound like much but it adds up with a full inventory. Let me just grip and grab and if I let go while pointing at an inventory spot, put the item in that inventory slot.

I'd also like to be able to stow an item in my hand into my inventory without having to drop it and right stick click it.

The magic system is being reworked so I'll refrain from commenting on that because it did feel clunky and I'm glad they're redoing it. I also appreciate that they're willing to do that.

I did enjoy the puzzles that I saw in the second tutorial. The Indiana Jones pressure plate puzzle was good. Also a good MP mechanic. Hopefully we see more of that in the future. I do think having two tutorials that range an hour and a half is probably a bit overkill. This likely could have been whittled down considerably and approached much more like the second tutorial where it's delivered as your crawling through a dungeon so it feels less like wasted time and more like progress. I actually got something out of the second tutorial. The first was just kinda long and dull.

Overall I really need to see more to give a good recommendation to be honest. I see potential but this is definitely an Early Access title and $35 might be a bit steep for what's here. It does offer everything that it promises on the Steam page and I did spend nearly 2 hours playing and wanting to keep going so I will likely be giving it a thumbs up for now. However if you have issues with Early Access titles, you might want to skip out on this for a while until it gets some polish.

Undead Soldier: Medieval footsoldiers that have been raised from the dead with rotting flesh and are more agile than Skeletons. Slashing attacks work best on them.

Dragons (BOSS): Elemental reptilian beasts that can breathe gases of different types such as Fire, Ice, Lightning, Darkness, Nature, and Earth.
-
Fire will damage you by 10 and engulf you in flames that do 2 damage every second for 10 seconds,
Ice will prevent you from doing heavy attacks and slow you down for 5 seconds,
Lightning will damage you by 20 and prevent you from doing fast attacks for 5 seconds,
Darkness damages you by 5 and makes your vision darker for 10 seconds,
Nature does 1 damage to you every second and prevents you from parrying for 5 seconds,
Earth will enclose you in a wall of dirt for 15 seconds with enemies inside for 10 seconds.
-
Dragons can also do melee attacks by warning for 3 seconds by roaring then stomping you for up to 35 damage.

Mythical Chests: The rarest chest you can find, Mythical Chests can give from Unique to Legendary tier items in a quantity of up to 2. Crossbow: Accurate long range bow that has to be charged to shoot. Has a Legendary rarity variant that shoots three arrows.

Spear: Long piercing melee weapon good for piercing armor and bludgeoning with handle. Has a Legendary variant that is tipped with poison that damages enemies overtime.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLegendary Tales is a dungeon crawling "RPG" with "Blade and Sorcery"-like combat.

The Cons:

To start, I recommend anyone starting off to actually complete the tutorial, unless you want to miss half of the content, such as ability actives and selling items, because without it, I either had to return to the tutorial, or brute-force trying to figure out how things work.

The "RPG" mechanics are a bit lackluster, as they are simply stat points, and likewise to Dark Souls, you usually only put points into damage, whether Dexterity or Strength. There are no quests, and the only objective is to progress through the dungeons, eventually reaching an exit portal.

As others have said, the "gameplay" trailer really doesn't reflect the gameplay. You won't get to do cinematic finishers on the enemies, because in order to reach the "stunned" state for an enemy, they must reach a low health threshold. Another issue is the early game, the enemies, especially when playing with friends, are quite tanky. As you progress, around floor 4 and 5, you will be able to actually fight back, and absolutely decimate them. It is a good feeling to "rise up", and gain strength, but I wish the early game was a little more inviting.

The town is somewhat empty, with only three vendors, who you cannot directly talk to, but rather just grabbing an item you want to purchase from them. There is a portal which leads you to an empty area, which I can only imagine will be filled out in the future.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Pros:

Overlooking these potential downsides, the real glory of this game is shown. If you have friends to play with, it is absolutely fun, and worth every penny. You will fight over drops and engage in glorious skirmishes against the skeleton and goblin hordes. The combat is very satisfying, with the weapons correctly parrying enemy attacks.

The items, excluding the legendary items, don't have a lot of interesting characteristics. The items come in 5 rarities, from memory: common (white), magic (blue), unique (yellow), mystic (purple), legendary. Everything past common usually has randomly rolled attributes, and an active ability. Legendary items of course have a unique active that is interesting and adds fun mechanics. I recommend learning the bind for the active ability as I played with my friends for about 5 hours without realizing the bind.

The levels, especially in later floors have great and fresh variation. I won't spoil too much, but there are puzzles, parkour, and hidden levels accessed through portals.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Do I recommend this game? Yes, infinitely more so if you have friends willing to buy and play with you. This game is still an early release, but it has such great potential. It has the possibility to become a new Diablo, but in VR, adding a much needed push into the future of VR, but in its current state, it is somewhat difficult to see their overall direction, past a "VR dungeon crawler."

What do I want to see? Most of what I want to see from this game in the future is variation. Variation in terms of items, music, enemies, enemy animations, level themes. A revision of the skill tree and potentially quests alone would make this one of the top VR games.

All in all, I recommend and rate this game,
Solo: 7.5/10.
With friends: 8.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

Big Picture: Not Bad But Overpriced

Legendary Tales is essentially Blade & Sorcery combat and climbing applied to Diablo 1. You have a single town with music that feels literally ripped straight from the original, and you go into a cursed monestary to fight skeletons and get loot and level up stats. You descend down through 10 floors, all of which nearly identical. Every enemy in the game is the same skeleton with varying amounts of armor. There's no story of any kind. It's a game that is insultingly thin on content, especially given it's $35 price tag, and has a mountain of minor annoyances. But I have to admit, it gets the hard things mostly right. Combat honestly does feel good and is the best part of the game, and there's a lot of feel and nuance with each weapon, and at least a couple ways to play. Lastly, there is coop, which is also unfortunately rare in the VR world, so it really does occupy a niche for some people that no other game on the market offers. Because of all that, there's enough reason for optimism to wishlist and follow updates. It's not too buggy either, it just DESPERATELY needs more content and QoL. I don't recommend buying this game in its current state unless you really love pretend sword fighting and Diablo and have some cash sitting around. Having a friend also boosts the value of the game a lot, but I only had the fortune of playing singleplayer this time around :( But I imagine it's really fun.

A Note On Difficulty and advice
Just wanted to address this here since I've seen other people complain about it. I found this game to actually be very easy. I got the "500 kills" achievement before the "die once" achievement, and I did it as a strength character who just ran into enemies with a sword and board. I don't think skeletons are too tanky or do that much damage. Any time I was low on health, I just town portaled and bought health potions. Not Hard. I think the reason people have overreacted to the difficulty is them being totally unfamiliar with min/maxing in diablo-style games, plus the fact that the game does literally nothing to explain to you wtf the stats do. It is EXTREMELY IMPORTANT to prioritize one of the damage stats early, like Strength or Dexterity. Each weapon scales with one of the two. It's also super important to have armor early on. I can't think of any other reason people are complaining about the stats of enemies. I have frequently been able to one-hit kill them with regular weapons. You just have to understand that stats give you more damage than the actual damage number on weapons. If you play, make sure you are testing your weapons on the target dummy to see which is better.

Also I have seen people complain about the damage timer. It is kind of a confusing mechanic, it's considered a "heavy attack" if you attack only once every 1.5 seconds. I don't know if it's a mechanic that makes the game better, but it didn't bother me too much, because there are ways around it. 1) the timer is indepent for each weapon, so dual-wielding gives you two timers to work with. This is part of the reason that two-handed weapons just ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ suck in this game, at least early on. Strength is such a big part of my damage that even hitting enemies with my shield does a large amount of damage. 2) there are stats that boost your "light attack", or attacks that are rapid, if that's what you want. The game doesn't "force" you to attack slowly, Light attacks are absolutely viable, they are just harder to win with because it requires you to be more active and parry less. But I absolutely follow my heavy attacks up with a few light attacks when I play, just to maximize each opening. 3) There are ways to RESET the heavy attack and do them rapidly, the tutorial tells you this, but people probably skipped it because it's a dense, atrocious slog. Weapon abilities reset the heavy attack and it's a great way to get a big attack in before you get hit.

A Mountain Of Annoyances
My God is this game from ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ stone ages when it comes to RPG mechanics. Let me just run down the list. Ridiculously slow movement. No item compare. No damage numbers. Tiny inventory, horrible inventory controls. No buyback (sold items aren't even upcharged, they are gone forever). To sell items, you have to drop them into this tiny ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ pit that glitches out constantly. You drop your equipment where you die, which is ABSOLUTELY not good design in an ARPG where you are totally defined by the strength of your gear. The weapon holster positioning is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ stupid. The hip slots are WAY too far forward and constantly get in the way. The back holster is way too low and should be over the shoulder. It just kills the fantasy when I reach behind my shoulder to grab my giant sword, and then realize I actually need to reach awkwardly under my shoulder. The drop rates are OUTRAGEOUSLY bad. I found 2 legendaries in 10 hours and they both sucked. All stats are linearly scaling, and all enemies are linearly scaling, so the gameplay literally doesn't evolve the tiniest bit as you play. You are just getting bigger numbers at the same rate as your enemies. The only salvation here is the unique abilities the legendaries have, I found such an awesome one once. It was a giant anime-style katana who's ability send wind slashes through the air and could hit multiple enemies at a distance. But by time I found it, it was 12 levels behind me and therefore had garbage stats, so I couldn't even enjoy it. In the twenty levels since then, I haven't found a single one. So the only thing in the entire game that makes the gameplay evolve at all might as well not even exist because the game doesn't give you any, ever. There's no more insulting, asinine design philosophy than ♥♥♥♥ drop rates, especially in a game that offers nothing else to look forward to. There's no boss fights as far as I can tell, if there is, there's only 1, on the tenth floor which I haven't been to yet. The graphics are unbelievably bad, every single room is a literally flat square with 90 degree angles and no decorations or lighting.

Biggest annoyance, we have to talk about the parrying. It's one of the best things about this game, which is why hitbox issues are such a large frustration. There is no player body, despite them having the capability and IK already in place to render it. It is impossible to tell what will and will not hit me half the time, seriously. Sometimes enemies lunge through my body behind me, and I take damage from their ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ elbows (which can't be parried) going through me. There is literally nothing you can do to stop this, since parrying is the only way to interrupt the enemy (besides killing them). But you can't do this with enemies, because they have a physically simulated body that you can get stuck on. They NEED to implement a player body, and fix the aiming on the enemies so they are swinging their weapons at you and not their elbows. If an enemy swings their weapon BEHIND you, their arms that bump into you should lose momentum and not do damage. Lastly, there is a very serious issue in this game of attacks that the player DOES perfectly parry do damage to the player anyway. Because the player has no body, you can parry the attack AFTER it hits you, which is just stupid. Please devs, give us a body. It is a core part of the physically simulated realism, check bonework and blade & Sorcery.

PS. I really think this game needs 1) mod support 2) body part damage or disablement so that there's a reason to aim for an enemy's arm or head and not just their feet all the time 3) way more creativity in non-combat interactions in the rooms. So much of the potential of the fully physically based gameplay is untapped, and there's no traps or puzzles or hidden curiosities to pique the player's interest. The game is all combat all the time. There are other components to a good adventure besides just combat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've only played this game co-op with one other player, but the game supports up to four total players. From what I've experienced this game is best played co-op. The loot and the maps are randomly generated. While you may see the same segments reused it's never the same dungeon twice. The weapon loot is varied and has many styles of weapons to use, every weapon beyond the most common rarity has random stats, so if you are a big fan of grinding for that god roll, this is probably the game for you.

I've played through the first 6 floors and repeated some a few times for more practice and better loot. The game can be pretty hard at times, but the combat is so rewarding when you do finally clear a section that gave you trouble. With a great blend of archery, magic, and melee combat plus shared physics of objects, i.e. hitting enemies with chairs or having your friend hold back a weakened enemy allowing you to land the final blow in whatever manner you deem the most entertaining.

With awesome shared physics, numerous combat styles, random weapons rolls, and randomly generated dungeons, I feel this game offers unlimited replayability and an overall amazing experience with friends. While the price I feel is a little steep at $34.99 I do think it can be worth the price if you are someone who likes to grind or someone who overall wants a great co-op VR experience.\t\t\t\t\t
