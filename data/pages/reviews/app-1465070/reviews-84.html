\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is so much potential here but they are not there yet.
This might become the diablo game we all love but in VR.

Lets start the with the positives.
The combat is good but there is room for improvement.
Graphics are great but it looks like its using a sharpening filter instead of really high textures.
Performance is great and I did not notice any frame drops.
Inventory handling is good.
Audio is good as well.
Multiplayer up to 4 players but as usal all my friends refuse to get VR.
Native Bhaptics support which need some fine tuningy.

Cons.
Controlls, when using grip sensor. I kept dropping my weapon even though I firmly squish the grip which made me switch to trigger mode instead which breaks immersion somewhat. You also have to point at the items with 100% accuracy to pickup the items.
Dungeons, There are 10 dungeons in the game and it takes around 1-2 hours to complete each dungeon. This would be fine if it had enough of variety but it does not.
After 4 hours I encountered 3 enemy types. Skeletons, Bigger skeletons, and tiny goblins.
And the dungeon gets old fast ist just brick after brick. The only difference in scenery is at the end of the level where you get a chest.
Now the dungeons and enemy variety might very well change the further down the dungeon you get but I was to bored to continue any further.
Too Expensive. 35 euros is way to much for its current state.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTake this review with a grain of salt because it's early in development, but this game is borderline unplayable in its current build. The second dungeon starts with a handful of bats which shouldn't be a problem, except parrying bats doesn't work at all and parrying is absolutely integral to the combat (the bats killed me in about 10 seconds, most of the time spent with them clipped over my head.

Bats aside, the game forces you to complete the first dungeon totally solo, and while the parries feel good sometimes, quite often you'll get the parry effect and sound but still take damage which, again, wouldn't be so bad if you didn't die in 5 or 6 hits. The combat is definitely geared towards multiplayer, but as of now there is no matchmaking, so if you don't have 3 friends with VR who want to buy this game, good luck finding someone to play with unless you slog through discord chats looking for randoms.

I would wait it out instead of refunding and uninstalling if the trailer wasn't intentionally misleading. The combat in this game looks and controls nothing like Blade and Sorcery (and I saw reviews comparing the two, making me think they're dev bots or just people who don't know any better). Enemies don't react to your attacks at all unless they die. You have about half a second to grab any dead enemy and can only use their limbs as weapons if one so happens to fall off, which I have never successfully pulled off because, again, enemy corpses disappear instantly. This would be a minor complaint if the trailer did not purposefully advertise this feature that doesn't exist. Same for using the environment in combat like smashing pots on skeletons' heads. In the first dungeon, you'll pass what seems like dozens of pots and barrels that you can't interact with at all, then randomly towards the end of the dungeon there are a handful in rooms with no skeletons that are breakable.

Again, I wouldn't care about a clunky and awkward experience in an early access game, and normally I'd give it time, but the misdirection in the trailer seems dishonest and sours the entire experience. Uninstalled and refunded.\t\t\t\t\t
