\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a master-class game in the making.

Emphasis on "in the making", However don't let this hold you back from playing it, in it's current state it still took me and my friend 18 hours to finish the game on our first characters.
If you are looking for an RPG game to play with your friends (or just alone) this game is definitely worth trying out. I'm really glad I picked up the game when I did, before the skill-tree update I might have just felt like this game is too bare-bones.
Right now It's still very much early-access and while there is only a handful of enemies so far, from what I've read this is next up on the to-do list.

What I like about this game the most is not it's current state, but it's potential, the game has an extremely rich foundation, base concept and execution, the combat is great, the terrain, the movement and how you traverse certain terrains (such as climbing ropes, ladders, holding bars, pulling yourself up) is really well designed and fun, the gear system is good, the skills system is fun.
What I'm trying to say is that what this game is lacking is content: more enemies, more areas, more different looking areas, more bosses, more weapons, more weapon types, more skills, (quests?, gamemodes?). This is the only thing that's holding the game back from truly shining, and this is exactly why I said this game has huge potential but it needs time, and seeing the future updates and announcements it seems like that's exactly what we'll get.

TLDR: Very strong foundation and core mechanics, This game will grow into one of the best VR RPGs with more content/time. And currently it is still worth trying out/supporting to make that happen (took me 18hrs to beat the game in its current state)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite being in Early Access, this is bar none, my favorite VR game.
I injured my hand twice while playing and still wanted to play more after. I've since expanded my play space, and I've even bought arm weights just for this game, but if you're looking for a VR game to get really into and put weight behind your swings: This is it.

The leveling system is a nice addition, and it really feels like you're making a build around specific weapons and play styles.
I think what might be nice in the future is some more areas to delve into, so it's no just the Abbey dungeon. The title menu currently has some interesting outdoor landscapes that make me wonder if the devs have any intent in making a forest-like dungeon, and different locales in general.
The developers are already planning on adding more enemies, but what they have now is very enjoyable to fight through.

I look forward to seeing what this game has to offer in the months and years to come!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLets start with the combat system. If you've only ever played VRChat and Rec Room then the combat here will probably feel nice and immersive. However, if you have experienced any somewhat well made physics combat system like in Boneworks, Blade and Sorcery, Hard Bullet, etc., this is gonna feel like a buggy, clunky mess of an attempt at physics based combat. Getting good damage out of swinging your weapons requires 4 NASA scientists, Albert Einstein, and Bill Nye to calculate exactly what inch of the enemies hit box to hit, how many mph exactly to swing your arm and the wind pressure of the current level that you are on. After about halfway through the game you'll realize that the only efficient way to not get 1 shot by any normal enemy type is to become Gandalf himself. Using physical weapons after about the sixth stage in this game is about as useful as putting up anti-cigarette signs in a smoke shop. The combat just becomes *throw fireball*, *block with magic shield*, *throw ice spear*, *get 2 hit by 3 foot tall goblin *. The best part about getting one hit by a baby ass enemy thats damaging you through any block you try to attempt is that you have to run all the way back from the main spawn area to where you died because who thinks to put a portal in a room with one small ass enemy right. So overall, if you want to invest a good 5 hours of the game with the hopes of finally becoming overpowered and fully leveled up, just to realize that all you end up becoming each level is weaker with worse loot dropping for you and 3x the amount of enemies in each room that haven't varied in enemy type since you first loaded the game, I would definitely recommend buying this game. But, if you don't enjoy wasting time and want a solid VR experience, buy Boneworks.\t\t\t\t\t
