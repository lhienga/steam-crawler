\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn its current state I'd say this game is very much worth your time and money at the seemingly high price for a VR game. I recently picked up Blade and Sorcery: Nomad on the quest store and was very disappointed. Not gonna get too into that here as this is about LT and not B&S but, basically there are no RPG elements and the new advertised Dungeon Mode isn't on most maps. The dungeon mode being what I was most excited for.

This game is basically everything I wanted that purchase to be. Dungeon-delving game where you make a character with specific skills, you level it up, find loot, and fight stuff.

The swordplay and general combat feel really good. Your swings are coded so, they have to be deliberate. If you just wail wildly at the enemy you're not gonna be doing much damage and will be open to attack.

I do have a couple of gripes with the weapons but, they mostly can be avoided with skill and practice. Like two-handed weapons just feel terrible to me. You pretty much have to have mastered the parrying mechanics to fully take advantage of it and I personally have not. Main issue is that for the added risk of using a two-handed weapon you don't seem to do that much more damage. Why would I holster my shortsword and shield to pick up a Falchion when I'll be doing only 2-3 more damage while taking 3x as many hits?

There's also the newly added wands which have become my favorite weapon. There's just something really cool and visceral about physically pointing your staff at an enemy and watching arcana be let loose upon it. The problem is though that the controls on them are unlike anything else in the game and afaik there is currently no tutorial teaching you how to use them. I use grip controls rather than trigger so, this is partially on me but, I imagine trigger isn't much better. The process of selecting magic is to bring up the magic menu, select the magic with the grip button, get out of the menu, then cast it by clicking the grip button once again. The wand now just being bound to your VR hand.

It may not sound too complicated but, the process of figuring this all out in-game can be pretty agonizing. There's also the issue of your wand just flying out of your hand for one reason or the other because spell casting and gripping it are the same button.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
From my experience, this is the best multiplayer dungeon crawler on VR at this time.
Melee feels very satisfying, Blade and Sorcery fans would approve.
Random loot with randomized stats feels great (as well as lets you down, but that's the carrot on the stick)
Multiplayer up to 4 players
After opting into the beta, the skill tree is diverse and I've enjoyed making different characters with different builds.
Good foundation for this dungeon crawler to get better, and the devs appear active in updating it.
Large maps with:
-Secrets
-Randomized challenge rooms
-Elite mobs with Enhancements


Cons:
Price tag (intimidating, which I think deters players, but I haven't regretted it yet)
Not enough enemies (Common Complaint.)
Repetitive textures and props.
Bugs (Nothing game breaking as of yet.)

Overall:
Like I said, this is a well designed dungeon crawler that leaves you wanting to further level up your character, and find all the loots, and show off to your friends in multiplayer.
Although the largest complaint appears to be the repetitive enemies, props, and textures, I think they did better than what I expected on the foundation of the game: Combat, Loot, Character development, Movement, AI (which includes a decent sneaking mechanic!), and multiplayer
I don't doubt the potential for this game, and I haven't yet come close to regretting my purchase. (30 hours so far in 2 weeks)
If I find something that changes my opinion on this game, I will edit this review.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDiablo - Dark Souls VR (Early Access)

This is starting to get the title 'the Diablo of VR' and after putting about 40 hours into it with having barely scratched the surface, I can confidently say that this is the game you're looking for.

Weapons spawn with different stats (more stats with higher rarities: white, blue, yellow, purple-orange) and even spell effects or buffs and are dropped from killed enemies, chests, or destructible container items. Legendary (orange) drops can spawn after killing any enemy and offer a full set of bonus stats and a unique ability (think Moonlight Greatsword special attack type of thing from Dark Souls) that feels better than blade and sorcery in some ways. The whole experience is a lot of what we've been waiting for from B & S. The team responsible for Legendary Tales deserve huge credit and support. Looking over their support of the game and roadmap is very exciting. Don't forget to check out the Beta branch!

On the beta branch they are cooking up a skill tree system that already lets you create unique fighting styles based on cool gear or smart play, and they've added some new weapon types and things!

Right now I am working on a dual wield axe build where I can one-shot my way through the higher-floor levels with a strike to the head from each hand at the same time... this game is crazy fun.

10/10 would recommend to anyone who likes this/these genre(s).

Need to knows:
You cannot currently sit and play.
Multiplayer has an easy to use server list on the Beta branch.
The tutorial is long because there are a lot of systems to introduce you to; stick with it!
No story right now whatsoever.
Only a few enemies in the game but they have stated that this is part of how they're optimizing and developing.
Facebook denied this game @ the Oculus Quest 2 store multiple times...

Looking forward to maybe seeing you in game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game and installed the Beta version on friday with my wife. we put 15-16 hours on this game by sunday.

Its fantastic. Sure there are some things that need enhancing. its an early access game.

its a brilliant co-op dungeon crawler that is like playing diablo first person. The skill trees are a great addition. The dynamic dungeon is awesome. its very pretty and the levels are complex and fun. The monsters skins are limited at the moment, Im aware of 3 types of monsters being 4 levels down but there is a lot more for me to explore

you can be a monk like character, or ranger, rogue/assassins, Warrior, or mage (though mage is way OP at the moment) or any combination there of. Want to toss fire magic in your off hand and use a sword? or spear? sure... want to stealth in and backstab guys.. absolutely. Want to pip the ace from 200 yards with a bow.. Yup. got that covered.

There are traps and leavers you have to find, not quite any puzzle like things but a hell of a lot of fun. Disarming traps arent yet a thing but I hope that comes in time.

We have really enjoyed it. I sent a long feedback to the dev team on things we found that are clumsy or rough. I will continue to play this game a lot and will be excited for updates when they come.

This is a brilliant dungeon crawler with character sheets, it could be a really great RPG if they wanted to fix a story to it. the under lying game is a fantastic framework and Im excited to see this grow.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best VR titles out there.

I'll start with the not as good aspects. The aesthetics are a little on the boring side, but technologically the graphics work. It has a sort of Diablo 2 vibe that I enjoy, but it feels like they could push just a little bit more style. Drab, that's the word. It's lacking in color. Goes for sound design/ music as well. It's fine, it's functional, but I do wish it were *more*.

But honestly I can overlook those details because this game is *fun*. The core gameplay loop has you exploring a randomized dungeon, fighting monsters, getting loot, and then returning to town to buy and sell items. If you beat a floor, you unlock the next one, even if you die. You don't permanently lose your equipment when you die, but you do have to go find where you died to pick it back up (if you quit the game, your stuff is sent back to town so you actually can't lose it). Even stuff that glitches through the floor or gets stuck somewhere will be sent back to town, and I appreciate how forgiving that is. There is a permadeath mode though, if that's more your speed (I'll try that at some point, I'm sure).

Like most games in the loot genre, it comes in the form of colored crystals that indicate rarity. White items have no added stats, blue have one, then yellow and purple have more, and orange items have extra special effects. For example, I found an orange one handed axe that does extra damage as a thrown weapon, and comes with an ability that lets you pull it back to your hand. It totally changed up my fighting style and was a lot of fun. True to the genre, finding white items is a bit underwhelming, but unlike other games they actually do come in handy sometimes. For example, there are some puzzles that require holding down a switch, and you may need to leave an item behind. Or if you throw or drop your weapon, you can use one you find to defend yourself while you pick it up.

The melee combat is fun. The enemies have good attack telegraphing as you learn to block and parry, but I've even found as I've advanced up the floors that they do gain slightly more complicated patterns, or do the same ones a bit faster. One of the weaker aspects of the game as it is now is the lack of variety in enemy types; you'll mostly be fighting skeletons. That being said, it is likely there will be more in the future, and aside from skeletons I have seen at least three other enemy types that come with interesting challenges. One thing I appreciate about the enemies so far is they all stay within the acceptable range of clearly monsters. I find killing humans in VR just a little too intimate most of the time, so the RPG aspects are very welcome.

I've only recently started checking out magic. Currently it's a little bit difficult to aim. There is definitely an assist that prefers to target enemies, but occasionally I get shots flying in weird directions. There are three main elemental attacks: fire, ice, and lightning. Their base forms all seem to be identical, but it looks like they grow into more of an identity at higher levels of the skill tree.

I can't really speak to multiplayer as I haven't tried it, but I can't imagine it wouldn't be super fun to be doing all this with a group of friends. There's even a pretty clear space for taking on different roles of tank, ranged dps, and melee dps. As far as I know there isn't currently a system for healing or buffing, but given that some of the enemies can do that I wouldn't be surprised if that came along at some point.

Overall, one of my favorite VR games (the only other contender is Until You Fall, so if you like that you might like this). The gameplay loop is fun in its current state, and it's a great foundation for the added content they say they have planned, and hopefully a whole bunch more they haven't announced yet.\t\t\t\t\t
