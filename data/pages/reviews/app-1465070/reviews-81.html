It's still in early access so there are issues, but the developers are very active on Steam and the game's Discord and regularly release updates. Be aware that when you buy an early access game it's not going to be perfect. That said, this one has far fewer issues than most early access games have at this point in development.

There's also a community already growing around the game, so finding someone to play with shouldn't be an issue like it is in other multiplayer VR games. So far there's no match making and you can only play with people on your friends list, but you can always find someone ready to play on the game's Discord.

If you've dreamed of a Diablo or Dark Souls type game in VR it's finally here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've kept up with this game for a while, and while the high price of the game put me off for a while, the devs have talked about lowering the price, but that it would take a few months to do so, so I just bought it.

While Legendary Tales is super expensive for the average VR game, I can't recommend it enough. I'm sure you've seen the reviews making comparisons to Diablo, but I had no idea that this game isn't just inspired by Diablo, it's full on trying to be Diablo in VR (that's a good thing)!

If you've played Diablo, this is literally Diablo in VR, even the town music resembles Diablo, so much so I wonder how they legally got away with it.

In the town / hub you have a stash for your character, a shared stash to share loot with other characters, shops and all that goodness, and the game itself has you going into a dungeon with deeper and deeper floors providing better and better loot, but stronger and stronger enemies.
You can at any point place down a portal to town, but you'll lose your place in the dungeon and have to travel back in. I assume there's a few checkpoints, like a portal you can unlock on a specific floor, but I haven't gotten too far yet.

While most games you can skip the tutorial, I recommend going through this one, because while the game at surface level is your standard VR RPG, there's a lot of underlying mechanics, like how daggers can get crits on weak points, inventory system, climbing, multiple damage types with one specific weapon (a sword can deal piercing, slashing and blunt damage by stabbing, cutting or smacking with the handle).

There's also a heavy emphasis on stats. The more DEX you have, the more damage you do with bows and knives. Armored enemies might give you a lot of trouble unless you have high enough strength to deal damage through their armor, there's a lot of layers to the combat system that I really appreciate. It makes it feel like the gear I use matters because of the stats I chose to compliment my playstyle, and the way you level up each stat is by using the item that benefits from it (using spells increases magic stat etc).

Legendary Tales is a VR game with a surprising amount of depth, but it IS overpriced and as I mentioned, the developers are going to lower the price, but they just can't right now because Steam won't let them.
I would've waited for the price change (Legendary Tales costs around the save price as Half-Life Alyx for me just as a comparison), but my friend bought it and we plan to play together.. I forgot to mention the biggest appeal is that this game has multiplayer.

Definitely recommend, but you can wait for the price drop if you want. I'll update my review once I've put more hours into it.\t\t\t\t\t
