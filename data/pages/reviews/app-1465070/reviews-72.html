\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to gush about this game for a little while. But here's the TLDR: This game can keep me grinding (and exercising) for long hours the way an ARPG like Diablo 2 can, because of the satisfying loot treadmill and fun combat. You want more details? Read on.

There's a lot of melee fighting VR games out there now. Some have excellent combat, most have okay combat, none have a gameplay loop that keeps you at it for hours. That's what makes this game very unique in my mind. A big part of what's so addictive about games like Diablo, Borderlands, and other looters is seeing those rarity-colored drops that explode out loot piñata enemies and treasure chests. This game has that in spades, and I'd argue it's even more compelling because there's something very special about holding that loot in your hands and being able to bring it close to examine and appreciate it. And you can do all of this in cooperative play with your friends.

At first I was concerned that this would be yet another rogue-lite dungeon crawler, but it's structured a lot like a typical ARPG instead: You have a town hub where you can buy, sell, and gamble on rare items, or even trade with your friends. When you enter the dungeon, its layout is randomly generated and the difficulty is based on which level of the dungeon you've descended to. There are boss and miniboss enemies, who seem to have random special abilities and drop extra loot. Basically, it's everything you'd expect from a "Diablo-clone", but in VR.

There are also magic spells that are fun to use, but currently magic builds aren't very well implemented. The developers have been vocal about plans to make magic builds viable, though. Right now your build is determined by your stat point distribution and gear, and those stats grow based on how you fight. Because magic-focused builds don't work well yet, you basically have the option of Strength-focused builds and Dexterity-focused builds. There are plans to add skill trees and rework how the stat system works, so I'm very excited for that. It will greatly increase build diversity.

The melee combat is very fun. I'd say it's a good mix of entirely physics-based combat and more mechanics-focused combat. Enemy bodies react to your strikes, and you can move weapons and limbs around with your own. If you can stun an enemy, you can also grab them and toss them or punish them. The ragdolling and sending heads and limbs flying from a finishing blow is always entertaining. The combat mechanics come in with timed parries causing knockbacks or stuns, and the concept of light and heavy blows. Heavy blows come from spacing your strikes out by about a second and a half, which makes "vr sword waggling" an ineffective way of fighting. Weapons have a nice weight to them which affects how they move through the air as you swing. Also very interesting (and, as far as I know, unique to this game) is that striking your enemy with different parts of your weapon causes different types of damage. For example: A sword's point can pierce an enemy, the blade will slash, and the pommel will be a blunt strike. I haven't done a lot of archery, but it works well from what I have tried.

Magic spells are already fun to use, because they require arm movement gestures to cast. I am hoping that when magic builds are more usable, the casting motions required will be just as much of a workout as melee fighting. Obviously, spells require magic points/mana to cast. These points are useful to melee, stealth, and ranged fighters as well. Every type of weapon in the game (including the various types of shields) have a special ability that uses mana. For example: Regular swords have a special ability that can be used to cause high damage with a blade strike, or an instant stun with a pommel strike.

I also really enjoy the visuals of this game. Not only does the gear look great, but the town, and dungeon sections themselves look awesome. The game nails the atmosphere pretty well. I've only seen a few enemy types, but they also look good. I assume more enemies are on the way. But for now you'll be fighting skeleton warriors and goblins for the most part. I am also hoping for some kind of transmogrification system, or a way to continually upgrade your favorite weapons if you really fall in love with a particular weapon type. At least the drops are frequent enough, and the weapon types few enough that you can usually get a higher level version of your favorite weapon without too much waiting.

Overall, I'd say if you just really want a game that feels pretty close to a Diablo-clone in VR, go for it. But keep in mind that the game is pretty early in development, and character wipes will probably happen often as the stat and skill systems are overhauled.\t\t\t\t\t

I'm now using a falcion underhanded so the blade is aligned with my forearm and am acting like my forearm is a giant blade. Note: maybe this is intentional so people with daggers can close more easily?

3. The tutorial - it seems to require more patience than most people are used to nowadays. May lose some players here
