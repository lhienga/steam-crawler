\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great VR dungeon crawler. I have no performance issues, though I do have a decent computer for vr. The only complaint I have about visuals so far is everything seems a tiny bit grainy, making it hard to make out arrows when fired at you or firing them. Archery is still fun, at least for me, it's just hard to judge where you are hitting when shooting far away. This hasn't been much of a problem as rooms aren't super big so far. This might be fixed by upping the resolution through steam VR, though I haven't tried that yet.

Combat is fun, though last I played enemies don't really react to getting hit much, but just today an update came out that supposedly changed that. I will have to try it and give my opinion later.

The leveling system is nice, you don't gain levels through defeating enemies normally. You gain stat points and each stat increase is a level, though each stat is gained by doing different things. You gain strength by using strength weapons, dexterity by using dex weapons, and vitality from getting hit. Luck is the only stat you can't level so far aside from what you start with and possibly any items that increase it.

The difference between going solo and multiplayer is big. Solo is definitely possible and easy with certain builds, but multiplayer lets you focus on certain enemies and have someone around to back you up when overwhelmed. I'm not sure what the difference is with enemies when you go in multiplayer, but I've heard that they just get an HP boost.

Don't expect to be one-shotting enemies like they show in the trailer, they take a good few hits which I honestly prefer. I don't want to run in and just instantly beat anything in my way, I like it to be an actual challenge of blocking their attacks to attack myself.

I've started three characters now because I couldn't decide on how I wanted to play. I did a dex build as my first which I learned I didn't like because I barely got any dex weapons except for daggers and bows and I've already said my main problem with bows at the start of this. I'm not sure if I've really seen anything other than daggers, bows, and small shields so far for dex actually, though I may be wrong. I then did a mix build with no dex to see what I liked while not having a super hard time. Now the character I ended on is a wisdom-intelligence build because hell yeah, I love the magic in this game.

Speaking of the magic, as I've only gotten two spells so far, I can't really say much other than it feels great. It isn't super complicated to cast spells, just select the spell and do a basic motion. In the tutorial it lets you upgrade the basic fireball spell you learn once, though that doesn't carry over to actual game-play. Upgrading a spell seems to let you do a combined version using both hands. I'm not sure exactly how strong it is as again, I only got to do it in the tutorial so far and was still figuring things out so wasn't 100% on how to use it yet. The second spell I have gotten so far is a lightning arrow, does the same thing as the fireball but instead of applying a DOT, it applies a stun. This is very useful when trying to support someone about to die in multiplayer or even if you just need a chance to back away from the enemies.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t- This game is an unabashed ripoff of Diablo 1 and That's A Good Thing. In fact it should rip Diablo off even more.
- The trailer is misleading, you CAN showboat and make epic kill compilations but in reality there are 10,000+ skeletons and you won't get anywhere if you sadistically torture each one to death.
- The trailer is misleading, you will be 99% of the time fighting with boring but shiny REALISTIC and HISTORICAL weapons that don't shoot lightning at all. If you get super lucky you'll get a legendary drop that is totally inappropriate for your build maybe after hours of play or maybe after 10 minutes. This is fine by the way and expected of the genre.
- Enemy variety is lacking, quite simply this is a skeleton genocide simulator. Fortunately the skeletons are enjoyable to fight with a number of well-telegraphed attacks (except the ones that aren't) aggressive AI that tries to flank you and are individually competent enough that if you're sloppy, they'll put some dents in your head. They even taunt you. There's also goblins that run into walls inbetween stabbing you in the ♥♥♥♥, bats that will make you jump around and flail wildly and some other surprises. Being Early Access, enemy variety will certainly improve but for now it ain't bad at all. Some of the better melee AI I've seen in VR along with Hellsplit Arena.
- It took me a couple hours to beat the first floor of a 10 floor dungeon. I've seen a number of floors now and while they were all hewn from the same bland stone and packed with (surprise) skeletons, there was more variation than I'm used to with this sort of game: verticality, traps, surprises, even some minigame where I got chased by infinite skeletons and got a crappy reward. All in all this game has some actual meat to it, maybe too much meat if you just want an "experience" or can't cope with killing 10,000+ skeletons. Other VR devs, please take note.
- The game is genuinely challenging. You will have a bad time if you don't: pay attention to the 30 minute long tutorial, use weapon arts, manage your potions, acquire armor quickly, pull single targets when possible, parry, invest in actual damage stats, have basic hand-eye coordination. Each time I hit a new floor, the new skeletons chunk me in a few hits and by the end of it I'm slapping them silly, as it should be.
- The game can be tough at the start, you have no gear and you have to git gud. Sometimes there's a miniboss skeleton camping the entrance. Stick with it.
- Magic is admittedly underdeveloped and more like a preview right now, I'd avoid investing in it.
- Two Handed swords feel kinda bad
- Sword and board, archery are great. Even dual daggers are fun, until I get mobbed by a pack of skeletons.
- The physics and IK are kinda jank but this is VR, you should be used to it by now.

All in all this is easily the best VR dungeon crawler I've played. Gameplay systems that matter, enemies that aren't just punching bags, levels that aren't babby's first procgen maze, tons of weapons and best of all, isn't over in 3-4 hours like the other ones. If the dev is committed to shoring up its weaknesses (enemy variety! everything variety!) this could be truly amazing.\t\t\t\t\t
