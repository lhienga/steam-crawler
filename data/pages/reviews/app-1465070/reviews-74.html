\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDope Game.

Edit: February 11, 2024.

Fantastic. 1.0 dropped. It's great. One minor hiccup that I hear is going to be fixed next week due to Dev's being on vacation that you should know about though; gripping objects is ♥♥♥♥♥♥' toggle only for some reason. Not sure if intended feature or bug or just an oversight but it's REALLY ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ inconvenient if you prefer hold grip to grip and hold items. You kinda get used to it, but I've played like 3-5 hours of the 1.0 in SPITE of that problem which tells you it's that good.

Full disclosure; I bought the game LONG before the price was raised as it left early access just recently. The price might seem a bit steep - and it's not a perfect game - but I'm planning on buying the OST to further support the Devs. For all the problems they've had to deal with and their relative size this game is in my view a MUST ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ HAVE if you like fantasy adventure action games in VR.

The sword play feels amazing. There's now crafting and once you figure it out THAT'S ♥♥♥♥♥♥' cool as ♥♥♥♥ (eventually you can just craft legendary weapons you don't have to hope you find one but it's not cheap!!)

For those returning; you already know probably they added a bunch more enemies no longer just skeletons and goblins. Though there's still plenty of both - there's also plenty of other types of enemies. The open world is really cool - though I do not like how it's practically pitch black. I think that's a design choice and it DOES have it's upsides and sets an atmosphere of caution in the over world, but most importantly it makes what you CAN see pop so much more in terms of the environment. It all looks very cool. So I'm not sure if they should necessarily change it, but I think the immediate light circle around you should either by default be wider or there should be something you can do in game to widen it. Maybe you can have a lantern equipped or something idk.

Also the premade dungeons are really cool. Some amazing set pieces and like...things that can happen only because of the intentional and intelligent design of a dungeon. I know a lot of old hats liked the procedural generated thing before, but give this a shot. It's so much better in my view - at least so far. And because the dungeons have like...bosses at the end - some familiar and "generic" but others like the Goblin Shaman (no spoilers but that fight is...MUCH different than any fight you'd have had pre 1.0) and other named enemies are just...chef's kiss. Also maddening. Still can't beat the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Shaman.

Tl;DR? If you want a really good game that's actually come a LONG ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ way from when it started unlike a lot of other games in the same or adjacent genre that blends RPG elements with dynamic physics based combat - AND ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ MAGIC I FORGOT TO EVEN MENTION MAGIC) with a pretty good crafting system, and as you progress starts to require more and more skill from you, and has a killer atmosphere with gorgeous looking set pieces, that is ACTUALLY a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ game and proper dungeon crawler and not a glorified tech demo or arena sandbox fighter then THIS IS A MUST ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ HAVE GAME.

It's not perfect. There are flaws. The Grip thing will be fixed I'm fairly certain in the next week or something as I mentioned, but also there's some a lot of jank here and there - as is expected of a indie dev team trying to make something GENUINELY unique.

But all of that jank really stops mattering when you find a legendary weapon crystal, crack it open, and you're suddenly holding the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Dragon Slayer sword from berse- I mean Ahem..."Castle Breaker" in your hands.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis started out as something like a reverse Blade&Sorcery roadmap with more Dark Souls elements but I see very much potential and a team of people who are willing to submit great amounts of effort and persistence into this game. First i wanna say thank you to the devs for their great effort in fixing every bug submitted and I look forward to the next updates and will continue to play this already-good and yet-to-become great game.

Magic: I like magic, so it's easy to get a catch on me with someting basic yet already promising. After getting the tutorial on fusion magic and not seeing the five spells combinable it feels a bit 'off' but I like how clearly everything is communicated by the devs so this is no biggy.

Weapons: Due to the nature of the random number generator and the damage calculation it seems a bit like a tradeoff between timing vs. physics based calculations. Blocking is hefty physics-bound, i can't block to close to my body 'cause hits will still pass into me and if I strike an enemy it is completely unimportant where and how i hit the enemy if I don't spaghetti them to death like in Skyrim (it's really stupid there). Thats a bit off but you can still get into the mood if you take a blind eye there. Also I suppose this is just for the moment and will change in the future but it is a big piece of work to get physics-based damage into such a game technically and balancewise. Don't give up on this side.

Scaling: After the recent updates I find the game to be very much playable and you can beat it. Sometime ago like 3 weeks back I've had great problems due to being unable to kill enemies in time and not getting beat to a pulp with 2 hits in any room but now it feels 'alright' on the scaling.

Tileset: As I have played Warframe where a similar system for the generation of levels seems to be used I can understand the system and it looks good and feels right. I'm looking forward on what your imagination and art will bring us.

P.S. you are doing great and I can recommend everyone who likes the Souls series and Soulslike games to buy this, it is great and will bring you everything you miss in B&S.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Legendary tales is well worth the buy even in early access.

First a Legendary tale: One night as I adventured on the 3rd floor with a friend we found a broken ladder that needed to be reached to unlock a secret. We could almost smell the treasure, but the ladder mocked us in our attempts to reach it from ropes and nearby objects. Then, a daring realization came to me. Why not ACTUALLY jump and try to grab it!? Before reconsidering the dangers, I knew my playspace was empty and with an IRL run and jump combined with in game sprint! I barely caught the last rung of the ladder with one hand and just started laughing like a mad man! SO glad my buddy saw the whole thing! AND I'm really glad I didn't break anything IRL.
10/10 would jump again!
(Subtext disclaimer: Don't try this at home kids.)

Now what IS this game where such legendary tales occur?!
For ease of explanation I will compare systems to similar game systems. This is complimentary.
It is a dungeon crawl of 10 floors and each floor and session randomly generates the contents of the dungeon like Diablo dungeons. The loot system is also similar to diablo with randomly magical stats and such. I also believe there are elite enemies with random effect on them much like diablo. Fun fact these are great systems for an Action RPG and they work very well in VR. Great job!

The game starts out nice and easy. then you hit floor 3 and there's a difficulty spike and you'll be glad you got the practice in of the first two floors as blocking and parrying attacks are critical for survival. Or if you have friends with VR or want to make some friends, you can invite up to 3 more players for some fun 4-man co-op dungeon crawling! There are climbing puzzles, mimics and more awaiting in the dungeon!
There's a slew of weapons, and magic to be found with gesture based magic spells to learn. (though magic is getting a revamp atm so subject to change), You can share items between players to help build each other up so playing with your friends is like old times where you share the gear you find among the party like your pathfinder or dnd party.

The presentation is good and stable. The load transitions between town and the dungeon are short, like you blink and you're there. Visually it's nice and feels grounded in the grim medieval theme.

To top this all off, the dev is active on discord and regularly seeks player feedback to changes and ideas! I mean the game has been in early access for a month so far and it's already one of the best co-op experiences I've had since stormlands.

I played this on an oculus quest 2 via virtual desktop, Bhaptics vest (native supported), on a Intel(R) Core(TM) i7-9750H CPU @ 2.60GHz 2.59 GHz, 16.0 GB Ram with an RTX 2070.\t\t\t\t\t
