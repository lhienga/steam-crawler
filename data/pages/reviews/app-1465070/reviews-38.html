\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLevel 1-50 (Once you beat the game you get challenge mode)

When you kill stuff a loot crystal drops of common-legendary rarity and you break it for an item. Full anime.

Open world with different zones/areas you load into, you unlock checkpoints in each zone, which you can teleport to. Depending on the zone/area there might be friendly NPCs there with quests/tasks they want to to accomplish. Enemy types will change with areas and there are elites and mini bosses that drop loot crystals of guaranteed rarity.

If you die you drop your equipped items, (not quick equip slots) and you can either recover by going to your body and pulling the sword out of your grave your corpse left, which auto equips all the stuff you had on, or you can go to the stash in your main base and buy your stuff back for gold.


Primary stats leveled by weapon/skill use
Secondary stats leveled through points

Talent tree is gated behind stat/level requirements, so you'll have to specialize if you want something lower in the trees.

Ex.) I'm currently playing a stealth, melee wizard with the flame punch spell, but I'm leveling dexterity to 19 to get 30% inc move speed when in stealth. That means I'm focusing on getting bow, spear, dagger or buckler kills to level up that stat.


The crafting system is more interactive than anything I've seen from any just about any title.
That being said here's some QoL tips that might help if you're having trouble with it.

- you can't attack with a weapon higher level than you but you can wear / equip it so don't think the cool thing you just made is broken, it might just be higher level than you.

- if you're using the old index control set I wasn't able to crush magic powder with that specific control scheme. So, whenever I craft I swap back to the default controls to do it. (Make sure you're not holding the magic powder when you swap control schemes because it will instantly shatter.

- things thrown in the alchemy pot do not currently give you $change$ make sure you throw in exact amounts if you don't want to lose excess. (Throw in 4 base ore = 1 blue ore, you've lost 1 base ore)\t\t\t\t\t
