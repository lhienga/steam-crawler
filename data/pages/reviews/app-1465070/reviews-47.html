\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaven't played a whole ton yet, but it's quite fun. Definitely in early access, there isn't a whole lot of content yet but it did only just get out on early access a few months ago. Combat is fun, progression is fun, looting is fun. I've been playing with a 2-handed build, with some side weapons for throwing. You have to experiment and get used to some things if you want to use them effectively, 2 handed and throwing were that way at least. Will update this when I have more play time I think.

edit (22 hours in now): It's fun, but if you're on the fence then it's probably worth waiting for an update or two. I'm glad I got the game since I think these devs can do a really good job, and it's not too expensive. I imagine the game is a ton of fun with friends, but I just played solo. The magic was pretty fun, I made a second character that focuses on punching and spells (but I didn't reach the level cap, so I didn't get to see if that build would be severely gimped or not haha). Some mechanics definitely need some minor tweaks (like wands being super easy to accidentally holster). I haven't played blade n' sorcery (since arena battles don't seem interesting to me, I'm waiting for other content), but there's some good youtube videos comparing the two if you're curious on what the difference is.

I had no motion sickness, one of my brothers had a lot of motion sickness (but he slowly got used to it), and my other brother felt weird being high up near cliffs and stuff (but he got used to it too).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe idea behind making you wait to attack adds a bit of tactical depth to the combat that the other games don't have right now, and it makes it easy to feel like you're swinging a heavier weapon.

But beyond that, the game is a mess. The UI is terrible, and there are no remappable keybinds. You're stuck with UP on y our analogue stick being open inventory / menu. And the process of getting items into your inventory / stash / selling is a nightmare. If there is real time crafting in this I don't even wanna know, it seems like it would be a total hassle considering how frequent item drops are.

There are buttons on the controller straight up unused and coudl be used for like a quickbar with ur health potion mana potion etc on it instead it is convoluted and results in frustration. The game didn't seem to let me holster my large shield or two handed sword and the holster positions on the side get in the way of the annoying looting system. There isn't a crouch button, it's all meant to be you doing squats IRL, so looting is laborious and annoying as the axe you're trying to keep hold of is consistently getting in the way of items you want to pick up. The force pull ability is only apparent on equippables, which items don't drop as - they drop as little stupid crystals that you have to crush for the item to pop out.

The music is pretty much identical to Diablo's as well, to the point where it could warrant a copyright claim. There doesn't seem to be much originality here at all. In the time I've played i've seen 4 types of enemies, two of which being a big version of the other two.

The game says the physics is tied to the framerate, but the physics isn't great, anyway. There are some interactive items in the levels, like pots you can pick up, and I think i saw a chair that was interactable, but the game doesn't let you grab torches and there are plenty of static objects that you WANT to pick up, but it doesn't let you, like lanterns and stuff. You can clip through a lot of things at certain angles in this. You wanna tap your weapon on your shield to pointlessly intimidate a skeleton who doesn't even realise what it means? Only right at the exact spot or else you'll phase through your shield. This was with the recommended 90 FPS, too, so it wasn't about the FPS, the game's physics is just bad.

I understand the limitations of VR, but why bother having a dimly lit game that's meant to be prawning off of the atmosphere of Diablo, in VR, if you're not gonna have interactive light sources that can light the dark passages? Bit of a wasted opportunity to be more than what it is.

You also can't see your body, which is a pretty big deal in a game where the main thing is loot. And you can't jump, in addition to not being able to crouch, so if you want to have the extra attack angles, you better have a big enough room to jump around like Jackie Chan in your under pants. I understand you're meant to be a tough, heavily armoured knight, but you can't see your body so the fantasy of that is difficult to immerse yourself in.

I'm sure it's fun with friends, but so is picking up rocks off the ground, and at least when I do that, what's in my pockets doesn't get in the way of the rocks.

I'd recommend it if you're rich, have rich friends, and don't really care about all the jank in this game, or the fact that it's basically an unlicensed knockoff VR Diablo Simulator.

3/10 for the effort in compiling the assets I suppose. But-2 for the fact that there seems to be root and stun mechanics in the game. On the player. So sorry not sorry.
