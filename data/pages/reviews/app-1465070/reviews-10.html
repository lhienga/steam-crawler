Impressive job from just a small number of developers for this indie studio!

There are some cons, aka bugs & things needing updates, but so far nothing game breakings, minor annoyances like parrying not working 100% of the time and crafting items can fall into the cooling barrel and be difficult to pull out, like I said minor nothing a patch can't fix.

I hope they open the game for mod support & while hacking off limbs & a occasional head is dandy it would be nice to see some lovely blood splatter added to the living creatures, also expanding the crafting to armor as well.

TLDR::

Awesome D&D styled adventuring game you can play solo or with a group of friends, Best VR game I experienced so far that gave me that D&D feeling! Must own for any D&D fan!


Edit: I should of mentioned the atmosphere of the game! they did an amazing job giving it that dark Diablo 1 creepy feeling but in VR, Ignore those complaining about the price, This is an amazing experience that should be encouraged & the developer is still working hard polishing the game adding more features and mechanics to enhance the game, your support only encourages them to keep up the great work! Plus they're now setting a new standard to VR melee combat it's vastly better then Blade & Sorcery and pretty much any other melee combat VR game I've played so far...none other keeps drawing me back in for more!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLegendary Tales has a really fun game play loop, and now that it's fully out it has a lot more content too though I'd probably wait for a sale. I've been playing the game off and on for a little while now, and I've always enjoyed my time on it. Melee fighting is fun, archery feels good (though I'm not good at it), and the skill and magic trees are a good addition to let you get that feeling of working towards something and giving more to the replay value. That being said, the magic attacks have a bit of aim assist that doesn't always target the person I want, but it's not that big a problem as you can compensate and it doesn't "mis-target" often. It also added crafting, and I'm always about that.

The biggest down side for me is the price tag. $55 is a bit much in my opinion, and I don't know if there's enough content to justify that price tag as I haven't gotten very far in the new update. I'd suggest waiting for a decent sale unless you're gonna be playing it a lot.

Really fun game, but I'd wait for a sale unless, maybe, you play VR as much as non-VR games.

I should mention at the time of writing some people are having problems with the toggle hold function. I am not, I'm playing on a Quest 2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHard to describe this game.

A lot of people say it is some sort of Dark Souls in VR. I disagree. It is More of Diablo 2 in VR.

Not realistic physics (not for the lack of trying). Laggy Coop. RNGesus, especially to some implementations such as weapon crafting and use of Luck stat (hint: they're useless). At higher levels, feeling like the enemies become Bullet Sponges (well... to their credit, 80% to 90% of them are undead and demons anyway. The rest are goblins and they're technically not human). But man.... this game is addicting. It is probably the first VR title that is comparable to playability and replayability of Fallout: New Vegas (when it came out).

What is so great about this game? - It definitely gives the feel of that Diablo Hero. There isn't really a class to choose, but you build your own, or mix and match in between (BAD IDEA - As the beginner's tip I highly advise not to do it).

Melee combat and archery is done "Well Enough" to make you feel like a Knight or Fighter. You can even become rogue and sneak around with daggers if you wish. There is Magic and specializations in it as well of course, or you can try to create the hybrid build of sorts.

Overall it is not bad VR title. I just wish it had the same physics of Sword and Sorcery for example, instead of trying to make do with limiting mechanics (like cooldowns between the attacks, making the weaker. For example- Two handed weapons have diminishing returns when doing rapid attacks. You get less and less damage for combinations. -> That is simply flawed mechanics since in real life, you want to do just that with two handed weapons, inflictng quick devastating attacks).

Weapons basically have no weight to them and feel like pool noodles (again undead and magic), so you can pull off the VR Wiggle Wobble cheesy mechanics (with two handed weapons flick your wrists as fast you can with those pointy sticks to inflict massive damage, depending on your real life fitness level).

Unsure what to add. The game got a lot of problems, but it is definitely the stand out game at the moment that deserves big yes due to the relatively large amount of content put in it, in comparison to the vast majority of other VR titles.\t\t\t\t\t
