\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played this game for only a short time so far, but I think I can see where this game is going, and I like it.

The easy comparison to make here is of course Blade and Sorcery, and that's understandable but I think its misleading.

The reality is that this is Diablo 1, being lovingly translated into a VR experience. The loot mechanics, the environments, and the enemies all scream Diablo, right down to the music in town being extremely reminiscent of Tristram's signature music, and that we are diving into the depths of some massive labryinthian and evil abbey(church). when I started realizing that the goblins were running away from me when I killed on of their number temporarily, I can practically hear them screaming "Rakanishu!" and half expect to find a goblin shaman reviving them and chucking fireballs at me when I run into them.

So its Diablo 1 translated into VR with Blade & Sorcery esque combat, and honestly I already love it.

Its early, there aren't a lot of enemy types or tilesets, and there is a certain amount of clunkyness with menus and controls and such.
The tutorial is likewise clunky but relatively informative.

Once you get into the swing of things and get used to the controls, (Quest 2) the combat and looting cycle is just about right, though I wish they would add maybe a universal vacuum for gold and health orbs with a small radius, and I hope they will consider private instanced loot, especially if they play on adding any kind of matchmaking in the future.

I have played only solo so far, and I can imagine this being that much more fun with a friend.\t\t\t\t\t
