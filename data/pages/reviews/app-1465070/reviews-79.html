\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI liked Legendary Tales and do recommend it. It has rough spots, but these are also delightful. The things that I liked are the collision mechanics of the weapons and interaction with objects. Other things that I liked are, attack animations of the enemies and realistic feel of inertia, visual depiction of museum weapons and Diablo vibe.

Being a longsword practitioner I was attracted to the two handed weapons. One thing I would like to point out to the developers is the generation of power in the longsword is not strictly moving both hands the same direction, like an axe swing. It also includes hip rotation and opposed movement in the hands. For example, moving the hands together is how one would transition from left guard to block overhead. Now, leave your left hand up and swing your right hand back over your head, causing your arms to cross. You just transitioned from a parry to a low strike. Uncross you arms again and you now have made a second follow up strike to the head or parry to the right, depends on hoe much rotation you add.

In the game, manipulating the blade position by rotation of the handle looks great but generates no power and the collision is not recognized as a hit on the enemies. So many new attack angles would be possible if strikes like this where recognized. Thank you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWoooooow!! This is a BLAST. The swordfighting is excellent, the graphics are great, the game runs well (and supports DLSS, if you need it) and its even multiplayer if any of your lame friends are cool enough to also have VR. Archer is fun and awesome, I haven't gotten far enough to really play with magic much yet though. The stat progress is very cool (usage based progression) and the variety of weapons seems neat.

Index controls are great. The enemies are tricksy, they deliberately circle around you so you get tangled up in your cable. Grumble. This would actually better on a Quest 2 just because of that, but mine MELTED before I could test that theory out.

That said its got some Early Access limitations, yeah. Enemy variety is pretty thin, for example. Your character customization is .. really just not there at all. We'll see how this progresses, but so far this is off to an unbelievably good start.\t\t\t\t\t

With goblins, get lower on your right leg, the shield will cover you on your left completely, and his head will be right under your right arm.
In general, it's better to be in a low right-hand stance all the time.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's great!! But it gets very repetitive. Other complaints about the combat are accurate, sometimes it feels pretty unfair (esp. the goblins who can duck under every block/parry you attempt).
It needs a LOT more variation in enemies/rooms/aesthetic before I can recommend it. Feels like you're doing the exact same thing over and over again for hours, which can be enjoyable if you really like the combat system. The combat system is definitely one of the best out there and I enjoyed it well enough, I just got tired of smacking around the same skeletons over and over, and constantly dying to hits that didn't appear to land on me. The loot and skills seemed cool but need a lot more improvement to keep people interested. At its core it's a very solid early access game with a lot of potential. But that doesn't justify a $35 pricetag yet.\t\t\t\t\t
