\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot super far into the game, but i really like what i have seen so far.
It plays like a mix between diablo 1 and Dark Souls but with the freedom of a fully physic based VR combat system.
you can parry any attack in the game (even projectiles) by physically clashing your weapon with the attack. if done correctly this will open the enemy up for a counter attack. bosses need you to parry combo strings for the same effect. It creates this wonderful combat flow that no other vr game has managed to deliver so far.
Apart from the stellar combat you have a very deep loot and crafting system that even has legendary items with unique effects, like a shield that can be thrown captain america style, or a helmet that allows you to consume unwanted loot for temporary stat bonuses and much more.
The world itself is very well done for a VR game. it invites you to explore and gives you that sense of exploration only a good RPG can deliver. the level design might not be as elegant as a DS1 but it feels close to DS2 where the world spreads out in all directions from a central hub area.
To top it all of you have several skill trees to explore, that allow you to create a very unique an versatile play style. can you be a sneaky rogue, master 5 different magic schools, play a tank with a towering shield that knows down opponents and much more. whats so great about the skill system is that it is tied to the physics based combat and does not need a hotbar or anything like that. one example is a popular build for two handed swords: the skill hard counter will allow you to stun enemies for a brief moment if you counter with a two handed weapon. then there is another skill that knocks enemies down if you attack their feet while in stun state and lastly you use the execute skill that does massive damage by stabbing a downed enemy. and just like that you made a three skill combo just be swinging your two handed sword the right way.

all in all this is the best and most complete RPG experience i ever played in VR. Can't wait to see what else the game has to offer!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, as much as I want to give this game a thumbs up the price is absolute killer for what the game is worth. Yes, the game is fun. One of the best VR games that i've played. The medieval feeling, the physics, the weapons, the physics. Pretty much what most VR games don't have. Unfortunately with the game being $55 (Even after reading why it cost that much from the creator) it doesn't hit the nail for that much:

- One of the most basic features that they've had in the Early Access version (Grip for Index) isn't even in the full game. How did they manage to remove it and mess that up?
- No crouching system/seated in the game, which kind of ruins the immersion of being stealthy (Even if partially worked in Early Access). I
- The game is buggy, but what do you expect these days from games on Day 1 with no patches? My friend lost 2 Legendaries and had to restart because the game said "LOL Nope". At some point he even lost his progress and had to make a new character. Joining friends is a pain because you can't join via Steam or send an invite, my friend had to join my game through the multiplayer menu in order to play. At some points after teleporting the game lags badly unless you restart.
- There's no voice acting at all, I know it's not much but for $55? Even other games have voice acting in them and they're much cheaper.

Now, there are some good parts of the game such as being able to make your own weapons and even legendary weapons through crafting. They took the system from A Township Tale which makes it pretty simple and a nice touch. The game phenomenal, so many new places to explore and a few new enemies, and the boss battles make you actually have to parry/dodge. There are a lot more weapons and magic spells which adds to variety which is awesome! The replay value of the game is pretty high and you can spend hours on end just mindlessly playing it.

TL;DR The game is fun, but not worth $55 Indie company or not. Creator is cool, but this is the most EA move in the VR community this year. Safe to say wait for sale\t\t\t\t\t

I only played a few hour and I really like the game. Some mechanics are a little bit clunky but once you get use to it you can manage. The game just got out of early acces and i feel like it's not totaly fiinish I really hope the dev will add content and polish the game as time goes by. I would really like to see audio implemented when you interact with npc that would greatly improve the immersion. I use a meta quest 3 via airlink with the graphic setting at epic and it's super smooth.

UPDATE:
Bugs:
- Sometime when i launch the game my saved games are not showing i have to restart my computer ( I always start steamvr in airlink before launching the game... the game doesnt run in steamvr since last patch this might be the problem)
- Sometimes my left hand doest track anymore but the button are working (when i go in my airlink menu all is fine).
- Sometimes when you grab a stunned enemy he glitch in place and get stretched out as you move holding him

Annoying stuff:
- When you pickup some weapon from your side holster are picked up the wrong way you have to drop them and repick them to hold them by the handle (mace and 1h axes)
- There is no vocal on the npc dialog
- Not enough variety of enemy
- No Pvp feature
-Crafting no well explained
-Hardcore mode (perma death) not avaible until you complete the game
-The light and heavy attack mechanic is very annoying
-Gold is mostly useless
-Game is very short (6h to finish the game i messed around)

Conclusion
I cannot recommend this game yet for the price point as i feel like its not a finished product. It as a great potential and the coop mode is very fun but the game is way to short for a 72$ canadian dollar and is clearly not worth that much money(at the moment). I really hope this game gonna get improved and get more dungeon more enemy maybe even puzzle would be nice.\t\t\t\t\t
