\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWay before the 1.0 update, I tried the game out and did not really like it at the time, though I can't remember why. I decided to come back and try the game again now that the game has been majorly updated, and I'm liking what I'm seeing so far.
--------------------
First, I'll talk about the combat, easily this game's greatest strength. It is brutal, punishing, and can be demoralizing when you die to the same boss over and over. But sticking it out and figuring out how to fight those bosses and kill them is so very rewarding. The melee combat feels very rewarding, satisfying, and fun to methodically fight enemies in the game, with new enemies having new patterns to learn.

One of the things I noticed early on is that you can lure enemies with your bow by shooting nearby them. This allowed me to take down enemies one at a time for fights with elite or otherwise tougher enemies. It felt rewarding to learn something new about the game.

Speaking of learning new things about the game, this game does not hold your hand really at all. You are given the brief tutorial before being dropped into the game, a few quests to guide you the right way. Other than that, you have to do your learning by reading the journal and tool-tips. Not everyone likes this approach, but I think it really works for this game. It build on exploring the game, finding new things, new loot.

The crafting is also enjoyable, with a few mechanics for complexity but nothing much. Grinding weapons to melt down into materials, upgrading those materials to their maximum, and then crafting a weapon can yield very good items. It's thankfully not hard to craft, just time consuming to get materials. I recommend saving your early crafting materials for when you get level 10+. The early crafting you can do will just give you basically random loot drops you could get from enemies. Better to save those materials to try and craft something truly amazing later.
--------------------
I know many people would complain about the $55 price-tag. And I do get that for VR. But this is a game I can easily see putting hundreds if not thousands of hours into. Building a character to demolish bosses, trying to beat the game as quick as possible. This is NOT a VR game that has only an hour of gameplay for the price like some VR titles. There seems to be a healthy amount of content in the game and lots of things to do like optimizing your build, seeing enemies that were once tough be complete pushovers, etc. Not to mention this game also would probably be very enjoyable with a friend.

I have not played the multiplayer yet. But based on similar multiplayer VR RPG experiences, I can say that playing with up to four people would make this game a blast with friends. The combat also lends well to this, a ranged attacker like an archer does much MUCH more DPS than melee (presumably due to how the game combats melee wiggle spam). But it is hard to implement that strategy in singleplayer due to enemy aggro.
--------------------
There is a lot of skill tree customization in this game. There is no early game respec which is a bit of a con for me personally, but not overwhelmingly so. You will want to choose your build carefully!

I will give one recommendation if you are starting out, especially on a magic user or melee user in the spoiler below.

Your dodge mechanic is tied to your mana, which does not by default regenerate over time. This makes the dodge useless for melee since unless you constantly check mana, you don't know if you are able to dodge again. Same problem for magic builds, run out of mana and you will have to melee the enemy with your wand!

The first skill you should get in any build imo, is +1 mana regen every 3 seconds. Not great of course, but for classes like melee, if you are parrying and using dodge sparingly, you don't have to worry about pressing dodge and nothing happening!
--------------------
Overall, so far I think this game is worth the price gameplay wise, but I get that it feels behind in quality expected from AAA games, but I personally don't mind the price, I've bought games way worse looking and even more janky and still got 1000+ hours out of those titles since the quality of the gameplay was good, jank doesn't really matter to me as long as I find it fun, which I do in this case.

It takes a little bit to get used to of course, and can be pretty frustrating at times. But I'm finding myself coming back again and again after getting frustrated at a boss the last time I played.

I will say, this game is very physically demanding and I don't recommend playing sitting, even though it is supported. Moving with your whole body is rewarding, immersive, and I feel like it gives a slight edge in combat. So make sure you don't push yourself too hard.\t\t\t\t\t
