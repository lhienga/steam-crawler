\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would have recommended this game until I got to parts in levels that are climbing/jumping puzzles. Ones where you need to have the VR precision of a god to pull off. I had to swing from a rope to a narrow beam all while bats are attacking you. Not to mention some things you can grab onto but other things one would think are safe to grab are not. The amount of times I A: failed to hit the beam just right and fell, or B: landed on the beam but was knocked off or moved just in the wrong way was infuriating enough to portal back to town and turn the game off. Now my only hope is that reloading the game will generate a new level seed that does not have that rubbish in it. Overly challenging climbing and jumping puzzles in VR are just not fun. Please for the sake of your game remove these idiotic puzzles for the game or make them less of a hassle.

OTHER THAN THAT.

-Combat is fun an engaging. Parrying and blocking feel satisfying.
-Magic use is a little wonky, having to reselect the spell every time you want to use it sucks.
-The difference in movement speed is a joke. Would love to have a sprint, maybe like how blade and sorcery
handles it.
-The RPG leveling up and random stats is great.
-Random loot is great.
-Random mobs and dungeon generation really makes it feel like a rogue lite.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very fun VR Fantasy RPG Dungeon Crawler that's also fully playable in four player co-op. The super responsive and variable combat keeps the game exciting and fun to play with tons of weapons and builds you could potentially play around with. Despite all these upsides, it's hard to deny just how barren this in level and enemy variety.

_____
SIGHTS AND SOUNDS -

I'm going to sound quite harsh here, but personally I think this game's visuals are pretty unappealing. It's extremely generic visually and lacks any real artstyle to it. What makes the visuals all the more bland is how almost every single room in the game is identical brown bricked squares. Once you've played your first dungeon for 10 minutes, you've seen almost everything this game has to offer visually. At least the UI is typically very nice.

Sound-wise it also lacks quite a bit as well, everything you'd want is there, but there's no flourish or style to any of it. Easily the most grating part of the sound design is how you will be listening to the exact same track for hours on end when exploring a dungeon. Stick on your own music to be honest.

_____
HOW IT PLAYS -

This is where the game gets some serious meat on it. The combat is addicting and REALLY fun. It's like Blade and Sorcery, but with stats and loot progression, and playable with multiple friends. Melee combat has quite a decent chunk of depth to it, especially since you can interact with your weapons in pretty much any way you want, hold them in any way you like. You can block in so many different ways, make actual use of different stances for different situations, whiff punishing enemies, grabbing them and doing anything you want with them, using the environment like chairs, pots, or even your enemy's own limbs against them.

Mix in all that with dozens of different weapons and a good handful of fun spells and the combat is for sure the big seller here. There's few things that give me more dopamine than perfectly parrying an attack, grabbing the enemy by the throat, slamming them to the ground, then executing them in one swift chop.

Honestly it's nice to just go in and have fun. Screw about with weapons or playstyles, find one you really like, then hone it like absolute crazy, feeling like an expertly trained swordsman.

I also have to give praise to just how polished and full of quality of life systems this game has. So many safety nets that stop you from losing your gear from technical problems, and lots of well thought out interactions between players to players with gear. There may not be a whole lot here content wise, but what is here is massively polished, which is especially impressive for an early access VR title.

_____
WHAT COULD BE IMPROVED -

Magic as it stands feels pretty depthless and clunky. Some smoother ways to use it, more variance, more interesting spells, new mechanics, maybe even something like alchemy, could all go a long way into making magic feel like a playstyle you can fully dedicate yourself to without feeling like you're missing out in the excellent sword combat.
_
Enemy variety is actually just abysmal. It's 95% skeletons, 5% small weak goblins. You better get used to fighting the same skeletons, because right now that's literally all you get. This isn't as big of a problem for me since I do enjoy the combat that much, but I can't deny that these enemies are getting super stale already.
_
Location variety suffers the same problems too, but I already mentioned that up in the visuals section. Would be nice to explore more than just brown bricks.
_
A faster way to move would really help the sluggish running speed, especially on huge dungeons. Maybe something like you can run faster if you swing your arms or if you're holding nothing.
_
Dungeons sizes could do with quite a bit of tuning, as sometimes I end up with a dungeon that takes me 15 minutes to explore almost in it's entirety, and sometimes I get one that takes over two hours to find the exit. Maybe let players even choose a dungeon size, tailor the experience to them and what they have time/energy for.
_
While more a suggestion than an improvement, I wouldn't mind being able to customise my character, their armour, hands, etc. Would be a near addition to have.

_____
SUMMARY -

It's an extremely solid foundation with combat and stat building that's already super satisfying to engage with. Being able to play with with friends is a phenomenal cherry on top. There's plenty that is begging out for improvement though, and the lack of enemy and location variety makes much less engaging long term.

I recommend the game regardless, it's a pretty steep price as it stands, but it just about makes up for it. I'm eagerly awaiting to see how the game expands and progresses, because in a year or two's time, this could be one of VR's greatest titles.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing a lot of this lately and spending a lot of time on the discord, and so far it's been a really positive experience.

Pros:
-Gameplay is fun. Sword and board, archery, even their placeholder magic system is fun to play.
-Community has been really solid. People always willing to help on the discord. I talked to one of the devs (BJ) late into the night last week.
-Really good support. I reported a pretty serious bug and one of the devs (Medic) DMed me that night with some possible solutions, spent the whole day trying to replicate and fix it, and DMed me in the morning with his findings.
-Environmentally one of the best dungeon crawlers in VR. It feels like a diablo or kingsfield dungeon.
-Every build seems viable. Someone on discord told me that Vitality was a useless stat. So I made a 100% VIT only build. I can't do damage, but also I'm immortal. I face tanked the boss while chipping him down. I've become so powerful God can't kill me. Needless to say, stupid builds like 100% VIT are still playable.
-Legendary weapons are generally really cool. Every weapon has an ability that uses mana. On normal weapons this is normally something that forces a crit, or stuns the enemy for a second. Cool little feature. Legendary weapon abilities are like "Teleport dash after landing a crit" or "freeze an enemy in place for 7 seconds or until you hit them 5 times with this katana, while anime sound effects happen, then the enemy falls after the final hit and your katana sheaths itself", or "Throw this buckler in the air, where it will then hover. Shooting arrows into the buckler will turn them into homing arrows." Or just Mjolnir which calls down lightning on hit. Legendaries are cool.

Cons:
-Games still in early access and feels like it. It's sparse on content meaning there's only a hand full of enemies, a couple dozen weapons (though the diablo-like weapon perks means you still get lots of random rolls on the same weapons), and you'll start noticing the same rooms after a while. (That being said, there definitely seems to be common and rare rooms. I'm about 50 hours in and I still regularly find a room I haven't seen before.)
-The team is small and have no illusions of this game being massive. This could also be seen as a pro, because they are dedicated to getting this game to a state where it feels solid and complete (and especially they want it to be worth the price) but the game is never going to be massive. We should get at least a few more monsters, a new boss, and at least one more stage, but it's probably not ever going to be huge.
-Dungeons can feel long, especially if you don't know how to explore. When I first stated playing I got lost on an early floor and wandered around for a hour. Now I know you can use the blue handprints in the dungeon to summon a wisp, which will lead you towards the exit, at the cost of health.
-People say the game is hard, and for some people it will be very hard. Expect to actually have to put in effort to win fights. People complain about getting ambushed by half a dozen skeletons and murdered instantly. If you keep your eyes open, actually watch out for traps, and use your surroundings to fight groups carefully it's not bad. I nearly beat the game without dying because I immersed myself in being a dungeon crawling adventurer, parried like I was playing dark souls, and handled group fights like I was in Old Boy. You can too.

Other:
-As of November 2021, the magic system is placeholder. I'm excited to try a mage build, but right now there's only 4 or 5 spells. They're cool spells, if not a little basic, but it's going to get better.
-The attribute/stat system is getting a rework too. There's probably going to be a skill tree system soon and the ability to allocate stats when you level up. Currently it's kind of like a skyrim system where you gain exp by for specific things by doing it. Swing a mace, get Strength experience. Use magic, get Intelligence experience.
-More weapons are coming. They're working on spears, throwing weapons, two handed axes.
-Check out their roadmap link on the discord.

Conclusion:
The games got a lot of promise, and I feel like these devs are in the right minds and skills to get us there.
Is the game perfect? Nah, but it is my favorite dungeon delving VR game so far. Also it's still very early into it's development, so it might get closer to perfect.
Is it worth the price? At the moment it might not be for you, depending on how much you like VR dungeon crawlers. If you've got friends who are willing to pick it up, multiplayer is totally worth it. It's one of those games that's fun solo, but a blast with a party of fellow adventurers. I can also confidently say that even if I get bored with this EA version of the game, I'll just put it down for a couple months and come back when it's further along in development, and then it'll totally be worth the price. Hell, look at my hours played. I've got 50 in this less fleshed out version and I think it's totally worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game had definite promise. Great maps, with hidden rooms, puzzles with buttons and levers and climbing. A canny mob AI that will disengage and maneuver, forcing you to turn, and ambushes from ranged mobs at higher elevation. An interesting combat system that rewards using both hands and even grappling. A solid concept that is well on it's way.

That isn't to say there aren't issues though. The same mechanic that allows you to hold weapons at whatever location and orientation you want tend to reverse swords when you unsheathe them (though this may be intentional realism to force you to cross draw). The tracking on weapons is also a bit wonky, at least on the quest two (something I haven't had an issue with in other games like VR Dungeon Knight). When bending down towards a container (like the throwing weapon tutorial), the container actively moves away from the HMD location. Finally, bows... They feel great and are awesome, right up until you bash your controller into the HMD because of how you have to hold it to aim. This results in you having to do a weird wide draw and then aim with your drawing hand positioned above your head.

All in all I expect good things. The dev team seems to be very communicative and engaged.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe price is kinda high for what it is, but it's pretty fun. Basically an RPG where you decide to raid a dungeon for its precious metal (gold) and enchanted weaponry, and you use your melee/shield/bow skills to take down skeletons, goblins, bats, and also break stuff and get loot from it, along with the occasional chest hidden somewhere. All in VR, too. Also, each multiplayer server is ran off of one of the up-to 4 players' computers in each session, just to add a slight bit of info, since the description doesn't (need to) tell that much.

As far as weaponry goes, there are mainly a bunch of different types of melee weapons, some blunt, bladed, and in-between. For defense, you can parry the enemy's attack, or block with a shield. Bows also exist, but so do Skeleton Archers! There are rarities (Legendary is one) and added effects to weapons (like +5 dexterity, +3% mana gain per second, etc.). In the main town that you start in, you can buy/sell weapons, and even press your luck at buying random items with random effects added onto them without knowing beforehand.

Note that there's a bit involved in learning the game (mostly the controls and stats system), so paying full attention to the tutorial helps a lot for this one. If this sounds overwhelming, just know that I understood everything after completing the tutorial.

Lastly, just know that since the game is in some sort of testing phase, the developer seems to be planning to erase data as large, game-changing updates roll out in the future. Might be a deal-breaker for some.\t\t\t\t\t
