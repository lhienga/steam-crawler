\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere is my launch day experience, I don't review often, but I felt a down-to-earth review was necessary to show how much fun I had.
General Overview:
This game is exactly what I wanted from a VR multiplayer dungeon crawl. It took me and two of my friends 3hrs to fully explore the tutorial and first floor of the main dungeon. Combat is unforgiving, so get used to parrying and the swing-refresh times (no swing randomly or death awaits).
The play-style I went with was a spell-blade, I found a nice spiked mace and used the fire dart spell you start with. So I would parry and then be able to punish with both my mace and a spell.
As far as the magic system goes, there's a couple of gripes, but it was day one so meh. I had to re-select my spell after every use, which was fairly simple but I can see people getting butthurt over the action. Also, from what I've seen, spells are not common. I can see the alchemist shopkeep has a place for books but none are placed.
Enemy types:
Four types on the first floor. Sword skelly, big skelly, bow skelly, and those stupid little goblins (i hate them) (i throw them every time i see one) seriously those little guys are so low to the ground, and with me being 6ft (they are about 2) I am fighting a toddler with a sword.
Music...
Turn it off, put on some DMX and make that first floor bend over.
TL;DR:
Good game for you and the buds, I look forward to seeing how this game grows :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing for a while I cannot recommend it anymore, will update the review after further testing

Improvements:
- The combat seems to be faster overall, less HP sponges
- Some boss enemies that attack constantly, similar experience to dark souls, great improvement
- Enemies react to your hits, which is really immersive, great!
- Crafting, I have not mastered it and for now all weapons are better than whatever I craft, but I like the system, good job
- Story missions, as the game is more open it is good to have clear objectives, rewards are nice
- Better difficulty modes, I did not get to them but supposedly the enemies are not hp sponges but actually more difficult, new attacks, animations and similar.
- Grip has been fixed, also they added seated mode which is great


Biggest flaws:
- The price, it is too high, fortunately I already had the game, would buy at 30€ in its current state
- No enemy diversity, it honestly seems like every dungeon has 1 enemy type and 80% of the dungeon will be that, it is quite tiring to always see the same enemy models, bosses are different and great but normal enemies need more love, as you are killing them 95% of the time, only saw 1 new common enemy.
- Only 1 skill point reset per save file, and only after you beat normal mode, implementing this after all the feedbacks is honestly so terrible, allow me to reset by paying 20.000 coins or a legendary weapon/armor.
- You can't really use hybrid classes, you heavily nerf yourself, don't try STR+INT or whatever combinations, remember no refunds


Flaws:
- Possibility to reroll substats of items, nice to have more options but it is WAY overpriced, can't really use it, not sure how you are supposed to use the system unless it is meant for endgame
- The magic system is still the same, limited spells because of skill points, which means you can't really use multiple elements as you nerf yourself, you also have to be extremely careful in what you unlock as you can't really reset the skillpoints as it is 1 time use, feels more stressing than fun
- Piercing is still janky, but it is not too bad for most weapons
- Extremely slow walk speed, the walk speed should be faster if nothing is near you, you can spam dashes as a workaround
- Anti-mod stance, thinking that allowing mods is being a lazy developer makes no sense, limiting freedom is always a bad idea and believing in the community is always best, obviously we are not expecting levels like B&D as the game was designed by a modder to be modded, I did some simple modding to the game years ago to increase the enemies density and had fun, but having simple tools and a process would be best as the crude method has its limits and those mods cannot be shared
Note: Thanks to modding I was able to report that a weapon had 0% spawn rate, which was fixed, benefits of modding community

Not sure if improvement or flaw:
- Removal of old linear dungeon (?), loved hopping in doing 1-2 runs and stopping, harder now with "open world", maybe I didn't discover the classic "tower" yet\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLegendary Tales is a punishing physics based VR Dungeon Crawler that I am genuinely in love with!

I was lucky enough to be a part of the closed Beta testing, but I did pay full price for my copy of the Early Access release. During Beta I had logged in around 15 hours and still craved more when it was over so this was an easy buying decision for me personally.

A TL:DR Pros/Cons list will be below my personal thoughts about the game and pricing if you want to skip over them.

My Thoughts:

I've played everything the VR Melee Combat market has to offer and mastered them all. Legendary Tales scratches an itch that has been gnawing at me for quite some time. That itch being the desire for an ACTUAL challenge.

This isn't a standard VR sandbox where you love tap the enemies and watch them go flying. It demands the player understand the mechanics and put real effort into the sword play. Every kill feels satisfying because you had to work for it. Creatively dispatching your foes is a side effect of learning how to best handle the threat you're being faced with.

While enemy types and environments feel a little repetitive at times, the gameplay loop has been addicting enough to keep me more than entertained. This is a game I will end up sinking hundreds of hours into. Not because I'm bored and want to hit glorified pinatas, but for the simple fact that I love exploring the ever changing dungeon. Each time a loot crystal drops there's always that anticipation that it might be a Rare or Unique item. Every combat encounter has me thinking before running in to fight. It's like being transported into an oldschool Action RPG and I just can't get enough of it!

Pricing:

The price may seem steep upon first glance, but once you really put some time into the game you understand that it is not another toybox. This really is a VR game that you can immerse yourself in for hours at a time. I think the value for money is there (which will only get better as the game develops further), and I'll be honest this is the closest you're going to get to a Souls-Like experience in VR currently. I will however point out that regional pricing is messed up at the time of writing this review, but the developers have acknowledged this and are already working to get the pricing fixed for affected regions.

If you live in a region where the pricing does not properly align with the suggested $35 USD price, I would say to wait until it is resolved before purchasing.

Pros:
-Solid Visuals.
-Engaging combat.
-Control options (including controller offset adjustment).
-Snap AND Smooth Turning (with speed adjustment).
-An optional 3rd Person Spectator camera.
-Decent level variety.
-Extremely atmospheric.
-Rewarding gameplay.
-Runs great If you disable Volumetric Fog.
-Nice weapon variety.
-Option to play Solo or Co-op.

Cons:
-A tad bit blurry without upping SS in SteamVR.
-Music feels a little placeholder.
-Enemies and environments can feel repetitive.
-Tutorial is a bit long and a little confusing.

Neutral:
-I wish there was a centered wide FoV spectator camera option.
-A single dungeon run can take over an hour. (I personally like this, but felt it should be noted).
-No visual difference when equipping different armor/helmets. Purely a Stat change.
-$35 price point. (In my opinion totally worth it, but I know it's controversial to some).

Conclusion:

Overall I would recommend Legendary Tales to anyone that has been craving a nice challenging VR Dungeon Crawl. There's a lot to love here, and if you absolutely, positively don't like it... there's always the refund button.

With the state of the PCVR software market I feel like the ones who still care about making great content for us deserve the support. From my own experience talking to people from Urban Wolf they are a super passionate team of developers who really do take into account user feedback. Consider purchasing and having a hand in helping them to make one of the best PCVR games on the market! Also, be sure to let me know if the review was helpful. I structured things a bit differently than I normally do.\t\t\t\t\t
