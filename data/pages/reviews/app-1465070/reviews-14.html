Main problem is the horrible balance. The game has 3 main stats and 3 secondary stats that the player can level up. It would be important to make sure that each of the paths is also viable through the entire game. As it is now, Strength build becomes completely viable around 3/4 through the game because the skills in str tree have significant penalty against bosses and do not have the damage benefit of other trees. Vitality stat is completely useless, because the boss can instant kill the player even with full vitality stat. Shield blocking is useless, because the boss hits through shields. There is also no possibility of resetting skills so you are stuck with what you have.

If the game gets a balance update and patch to the boss movements so that playing with any type of character is actually possible, then I will change this review to positive, with the caveat that this game is not worth the full price even with VR premium considered.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn absolute blast! Do need to preface that i played during early access and so far i havent completed most of the main areas yet. review will be updated as i continue through the game and if any patches fix issues stated below.
I recommend purchasing when on sale or waiting for a bit more polish.

Pros
- probably the most satisfying melee combat on the market currently. the parrying and stabbing is just amazing.

- crafting is satisfying, you physically smelt, smith, and quench metal into blades then craft the handle and attach it. although a bit clunky and not thoroughly explained, its still satisfying.

- large areas that scratch the itch for exploration, i will say that some can seem empty or leave you wondering why its there in the first place.

- variety of skills and abilities offering a wide array of play styles.

- The multiplayer is seamless (there is one wonky bug with bows that makes it hard to shoot them if you arent host. its noticeable but playable).

Cons
- some of the larger weapons can phase through enemies and their weapons resulting in missed damage windows and failed parries.

- for now, grip is toggle and you arent able to switch it. breaks the flow of combat if you want to throw weapons and makes it somewhat awkward to say drop a weapon and open a chest, but the biggest issue is that its breaks index controller usability requiring a workaround. oculus controllers work fine.

- the price....at $55 im not sure if i can recommend this game. the devs reason is so they dont upset the indie market on psvr? since their game offers 40 hours of gameplay and indie games offer up to 8. id wait for a sale or wait until theres more polish. $55 is breaching AAA territory and thus requires a completed game (cant speak to the content since im still playing but some of the systems and core gameplay need a bit of elbow grease). If 40 hours of content and some replayability on top of it is true, id say a $40 price tag would be the upper end of fairness for what is offered here.

Close
As an early access title, this game was absolutely incredible and could have stood the test of time with updates to the core systems. the devs revamped the entire game and the progression system and the core gameplay loop is fun, mostly fluent, and some of the best vr hand-to-hand, physics based, combat you can get on the market currently. the devs appear to be extremely passionate about their project shown by their dedication to revamping the game over the course of 1.5-2 years (most other vr indie games would have been abandoned) and i believe with time this could be one of the best games to grace the vr industry given a bit of elbow grease, polish, and of course, listening to community feedback.\t\t\t\t\t
