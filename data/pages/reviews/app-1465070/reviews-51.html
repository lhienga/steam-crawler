\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI very rarely review Steam games, but I couldn't pass up the opportunity to give some praise to this really awesome game. Legendary Tales is the best VR game I've played since I got my Oculus Quest last year and I definitely think it's worth the full asking price. Even though it's still Early Access, this is currently a full-featured action RPG in a cool Gothic world with plenty of content to experience. You will be able to level & build a character from the ground up with a broad set of stats.

You can focus on close-quarter combat, ranged throwing/archery, spell casting, or even a combination of all three. There are a lot of skills to choose from as well as different spells and attack bonuses that all have real effects in how your character performs in combat. Choose from and customize skill trees and discover tons of pieces of equipment with randomized bonuses along the way. Like any good action RPG, finding a cool new RNG weapon that compliments your build never gets old in this game.

The combat feels really good, with each weapon being a completely different experience. The levels are hand-crafted and include some really neat climbing puzzles. Monsters are limited right now (mostly skeletons), but there are already new creatures being developed that will be added to the game at some point. Still, combat is versatile and challenging enough where it doesn't ever seem to get old or mundane. The icing on the cake is the integrated co-op support for you and your friends to dungeon delve together. This game really reminds me of Diablo I, in both game mechanics and the atmosphere and setting.

The only thing I feel it really lacks right now is cosmetic character customization. I think being able to choose a different model, customize your face & head, as well as the ability to see visual changes in equipped armor would go a long way to make you feel connected to your character (and show off to your friends). I have no doubt these things will be coming soon and I am very excited for it.

If you love action RPGs I absolutely recommend Legendary Tales. I will be playing this game a lot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you have not played Blade and Sorcery, just get that. If you have blade and sorcery, dont waste your time and keep playing that.

Less motion sickness in blade and sorcery.
Blade and sorcery is cheaper.
Legendary tales is more complicated than Blade and sorcery, in the worst way possible.
The dungeons are better in Blade and Sorcery.
Legendary Tales maps are huge and have no detail, nor are they fun to explore. It is just go to area -> hit the boxes -> repeat.

The combat overall just feels a million times worse than blade and sorcery.
Better mods in blade and sorcery (which you could say its because blade and sorcery is older, but better mods are better mods).

In blade and sorcery you are fighting real people, easy to slice and kill people. They don't walk around you and are around your size.

In Legendary Tales, its skeletons, goblins, and huge skeletons. Now the normals skeletons are your size, but the goblins and huge skeletons are a pain to reach down or look at. Fighting Goblins are just annoying, they arent different in anyway, just harder to hit. And when you get near a skeleton/goblin they are constantly circling you, so you are always moving either in real life or in game, which makes it way more motion sick inducing.

In Blade and Sorcery, the maps arent the most detailed, but they look different.

In Legendary Tales, it is the same exact brick wall, over and over, very little detail. And the map is so big, its just annoying to traverse. Even the things where you arent just holding foward on a joystick forever. Like the ropes, they are incredible boring, you are just moving your arms over and over and over and over, you cant even hold the button to glide your hand across.

Also, if you are somebody who doesn't like boring and long tutorials, dont get this game. Some of the time the instructions were unclear and i was trying to figure out what to do, or it didnt work as intended. It was also super long, and thats because this game is so complicated, it doesnt make the experience any better, just more boring and a pain to learn.

In blade in sorcery, the only boring parts are the loading screen, they do take a while and just sitting/standing there for a minute can be boring. But once it is over you can start playing and have fun. In Legendary Tales, 95% of the gameplay is boring, because 95% of gameplay is just pushing the joystick forward and hitting boxes.

Just get B&S\t\t\t\t\t
