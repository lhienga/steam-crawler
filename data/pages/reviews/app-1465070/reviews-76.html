\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's diablo 1, in VR, and that's absolutely brilliant.
So, theres a bit of a learning curve to getting into it, and thanks to its RPG nature, you can end up making a 'build' statwise that can make playing it a bit of a slog to start out, but all issues like that are solved in time if you put in enough effort, both physically and through time simply spent learning the in's and outs of the games mechanics.

After playing for almost thirteen hours, I can't say i've quite reached proficiency as other players may have, but I can say i'm starting to find a niche way of playing that I enjoy, that being archery supplemented by magic.

Swordplay is pretty decent, but your damage I think is partially related to how fast you swing your weapon, with bigger weapons requiring two hands to swing effectively, which is a bit weird when you aren't actually grabbing one heavy thing in real life to swing. Similarly, thanks to it not having weight in real life, beyond whatever your controller weighs, you can't use the weapons weight to aid in downward strokes and such. This lead to, for me, a poor impression of two handed weapons, so I stuck to one handed ones and shields when I first got started.

As I explored more of the dungeon in Legendary Tales, I grew stronger, through repetitive use of attacking and simply surviving. Gearing up, I managed to find a bunch of Dexterity granting gear, which lead me to my niche of bow use. Archery doesn't have the problem of needing to swing your controllers at high enough speeds to count as an attack, you simply loose an arrow and, can loose as many arrows, as you can nock and release them.

Magic is magic, with spells being quite expensive. In my perhaps short time compared to other players of playing Legendary Tales, i've only managed to afford two spells at this time, that being the Fire spell, and a Lightning spell. The fire spell does a short DoT which was handy in the early floors but is somewhat falling off in utility as my int is still quite low due to focusing on Archery, which lends itself to benefitting from Dex, and Trains Dex up in use of weapons that benefit from dex. The lightning spells utility however remains, as it provides a cheap, effective stun, that lets one stop an enemy in their tracks. This has saved my bacon many a time from the fire, as I can just stun an enemy giving me trouble and riddle it with arrows in rapid succession.

All of this of course, requires you to move your body. Being a gamer, i'm not exactly the healthiest of individuals, so I grow worn out quickly, but the experience of Legendary Tales still drags my tired and sore body back to its dungeons door steps time and time again, as I seek treasure and glory in its halls.

I give Legendary Tales a thumbs up. Try it if you have VR, it's a great time if you can get into it and understand its nuances. It's not trying to be a VR Dark Souls, it's more like, a VR Diablo 1.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a bit unclear about what makes this game good and the price tag is a bit steep. Basically the game is Diablo 1 but from a VR perspective and with semi-realistic physics based combat. Weapons are nicely detailed albeit generic but with adeal different types of damage depending on how they are used. It has atmosphere and combat pacing akin to Dark Souls and coop multiplayer. In response to some of the criticisms, you start off pretty weak and the stamina system can make fights take a long time if you don't plan your attacks and use the specials. Overall, it's very enjoyable. Give it a try if you want a melee In Death or a grittier VR Dungeon Knight\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a few hours into this game. It took some time to get used to the controls and the combat system, but it's starting to come together and my enjoyment has increased dramatically. The combat system is complex enough to make it interesting and make you feel good about your skill, but simple enough that you can learn the basics pretty quickly. There are a lot of weapons to choose from to suit your preference and play style.
I haven't found a super wide variety of enemies so far, but they are different enough that you have to change your tactics significantly for each one.

A few issues:

The climbing mechanic is clunky

The walking speed is very slow. in combat the enemies move way faster than you, so it's basically impossible to escape, and strategic movement is very hard. Walking forward is casual strolling speed, and walking sideways or backwards is grandma speed.

When you have a weapon/shield in your hand, you can pick up gold or orbs by clicking the thumbstick. This only works like 5% of the time (I'm using an index).

I wish there was an easier way to stash found items.

There are a variety of mechanics that are not explained or are poorly explained.

The town area is really empty. I don't know if this will start to fill up as I get to be higher level.

There are hardly any people around and none of them talk to you.

The weapon throwing mechanic is terrible and completely useless.


Overall this game is very fun and I'm sure a lot of these issues will get sorted out as they continue to work on it. Strongly recommend. This is a must-have game for VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game so far with a very fresh VR challenge. At the start I thought that it was too unfair, but this game is all about gear. If you are playing with a melee strength build for example, focus all your gear arround the attribute strength, even if the equipment peaces are outleveled. Attributes like Darkness Resistance for example are totally useless in the current state of the game, so dont bother with those. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tInitial thoughts:
This game has potential, and with friends I can see this being super fun.
The game is a typically dungeon crawler (even secret puzzles) but in VR. Price of $35 is up there, but it has good bones on it for sure. And if the multiplayer holds up...its going to be a keeper.

Combat:
Combat is physics heavy, if you have played Tales of glory, it feels like that, everything has a weight, and different use.
Swinging your arms wildly also doesn't "cheese" like we are used to in some games, you actually do more damage if you wait 1.5 seconds between strikes (from the tutorial).
Magic at least on index is rough to aim...so don't sign up for Hogwarts just yet...they are making improvements based on the intro message.

Neat Features:
Attributes gain by frequency and do play a big role; I went with low STR and was having a heck of a time killing multiple skeletons in the first level and worked up a sweat...didn't die because of high VIT but was neat to see.
Not sure if Diablo was an inspiration, but the music, and overall vibe I get is they wanted to bring Diablo series to VR (hopefully not he horde of foes...my arms can only flail so much).
Combat feels toe to toe, and engaging. Some VR games just have you flailing or very punishing for not doing something very specific. This game allowed me to take my sword and use the hilt to inflict bludgeoning damage.
MULTIPLAYER, I haven't tried it yet, but that is what can be the hook for me if it’s not horrible.
You can create multiple characters to play around with, so maybe you want to make a mage or an archer, no need to mess with your original character.

If anything changes as I progress with it, I'll update my review (keeping in mind its early access)
