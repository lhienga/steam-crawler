\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a huge fan of the Sword and Sorcery genre; movies, anime, cartoons, comics, games, etc... I am also quite passionate about VR, and have been paying attention to it for years, and when it reemerged with Palmer Luckey, worlds I could only dream about began to become somewhat real.

I've been patiently awaiting an experience that isn't yet here, but I can see the steps to it as VR progresses. Blade & Sorcery was a step towards that experience. Tales of Glory was a step towards that experience, Sairento was a step towards that experience, Skyrim VR was a step towards that experience, and LEGENDARY TALES IS YET ANOTHER STEP TOWARDS THAT EXPERIENCE.
The experience I dream of is still some years away. It is going to require an advanced AI in order to bring NPC's to life, and the ability to craft story itself based on the idea of Joseph Campbell's "Heroes Journey". A game that gives you a new story to play everytime you defeat the Antagonist, and decide to retire the character, which continues to exist and play as you did, while you start another story with another character in the same world, uncovering the new Antagonist to defeat, and overtime, each character played brings you closer to the full story unraveling. Think of how the Marvel Phase Movies system worked out. Using a bunch of smaller stories to lead into something much larger to tell.
The experience I dream of is one of many play styles. Dragon's Dogma had a large impact on me with it's ability to climb large enemies. Sairento made a large impact on me by its daring to go out of the realm of VR comfort and give the player the ability to run on walls, sideflip, backflip, frontflip, and go into slow motion for a bit. These experiences let me explore combat. I only wished Dragon's Dogma had a vr experience in order to climb monsters that felt to scale, and tackling them felt awesome. I don't want to feel contrained in VR. I want to be able to move around, and the character class I choose be able to move accordingly.
There is no game that fits this and more of my criteria for the experience I dream of, but Legendary Tales does feel like a step in that direction in one category; THE DUNGEON! I've seen some games use Random Dungeon Generator's before, and they are ok, but the Dungeon's in Legendary Tales have something just a bit more to it. There is still a lot to be desired, but I've really enjoyed my time going through the Dungeons that are randomly generated in Legendary Tales. The atmosphere feels fun.
There are a ton of weapons to be found in the game, and they range from common, rare, ultra rare, and LEGENDARY! The skills used alongside these LEGENDARY weapons adds a lot of unique fun to the combat.
Magic is something that feels still very lacking to me, as it uses a menu to choose your spell from, and this has always been an issue with me in VR games that use spells because it kills the immersion. Speech Recognition has come a long ways since Dragon Naturally Speaking, and it shouldn't be too hard to add in the ability to speak a spell while holding a button on one hands controller to then use that spell for that hand.
I've yet to play multiplayer, so I can't tell you what that experience is like atm.
As far as enemies goes in Legendary Tales, they are very few, at the moment. The ocassional Bats, Goblin's, and A LOT OF SKELETONS! I mean A LOT. You will be pretty much only be facing skeletons all the time. It does get redundant, but they aren't boring. This is still in early release, and there is a TON OF POTENTIAL for this game.

TLDR:
As this game is in the Early Access stage there isn't a lot yet, but it has a ton of potential, and the Dev Team does seem to be fired up about bringing the best they can. They've shown also to be open to communicating with the fans, and this is always a good sign for a Dev Team! I do think they have full intentions of making this game as great as they can make it, and plan to be reasonable with their roadmap, and what new ideas fans bring to their table, along with trying to fullfill their own ideas set in their roadmap.
You should buy this game if you are a fan of the genre, would like to help offer up ideas in making this game become more, and become a part of its growth by funding those making it. It shows great promise, as does the Dev Team behind it.
You should not buy this game if you want a full game right now, and do not have the patience to be a part of its growth, or to watch it grow.
The difference is like the early adopters of Blade & Sorcery since the beginning, and those that only bought in once so much of the roadmap was finished.
This game will get there. If you don't buy it now, keep an eye on it, you will buy it later. Until then, many of us will be having fun with it!\t\t\t\t\t
