A pitfall that I've seen many people fall into and I fell into was putting points in different attributes thinking that you can be a jack of all trades. unfortunately you will be left feeling under powered if you don't specialize in one strong suit. I really have been enjoying this game, I've gotten to the point where I am kinda clueless on what to do. Very enjoyable when finding rare loot. So eventually I decided to make a punching build since I had gotten an item that boosted punch damage. BEST CHOICE EVER. You definitely feel the weight of your punches. I feel like Senator Armstrong with how hard I hit. You can essentially solo the finial boss if you equip a shield and beat him to death. A few of my disappointment in this game are the stats, which are very important. The only problem is that there is no re spec or anything to change your character. Meaning if you wanted to use magic on your melee character, then better luck next time. You need to make a whole new character to specialize on one thing and start on floor 1.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom what I can tell , the focus on this game at the moment is not the bells and whistles . It is early access and developers have been putting a bad taste in our mouths with early releases , I get that , but I am giving this team a chance . It feels like the primary goal is combat , and it shows . You can swing for the fences if that is your thing . But if you want to actually engage in some good combat , this is your game . It is more than look at those physics and wow I can cut its head off . The parry , deflection and counter attacking are at another level . When you try some tricky maneuver , they counter and quite well . If you block with a shield and try to stab over top of the shield , they will step behind you at an angle and try to remove your head as an example . Yeah , it needs polish and alot of environmental detail is missing . But the heart of this game is coming along nicely and I can already see just from what they have shown us the direction there going .

The legendary 2 handers are all janky and need alot of work , but the normal 2 handers are fine .The legendary ones are just huge and clip on everything they touch . You can use them but the fun factor is not there yet like every other weapon type in game . Magic is ok , but is lacking spells , which the devs have clearly said they are working on right now . It feels satisfying but is a bit under powered in later levels due to lack of mana . You can prolly fix that in the skill tree , I just have not rebuilt a mage yet .

The dungeon is random but you need to play alot to see drastically different levels . They do exist , lol . There is alot of little climbing and jump puzzles built into the levels now . I really appreciate how they feel like they are supposed to be there and not forced like other games just to get the content in . If you have not found them , there are MANY hidden areas ranging from knocking down a wall to free falling from way up and crashing through the floor . There not all obvious .

Certainly there is a lack of enemies , but I am not concerned with this atm , there coming . Honestly , If I had to guess . The reason we have so few now is because they are really going all out on the AI combat . The bats and goblins and mimics are just fodder , but good fodder are they not . The skeletons and the variations on there combat style has alot of depth . It is fine for now .

At first glance , the leveling system is blah . Take a deeper look and you will be rewarded .
All in all , this is a great start and just enough to wet the pallet and make you want more . I do hope this is not another fly by night dev team and they stick with this , it will pay off in the end . Although it is not even close to a Diablo , it is the best your gonna get in VR right now .\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLegendary tales has the foundation to be a much better game than it currently is, but honestly it's kind of your only choice right now when it comes to Coop VR PvE Swordfighting, but I still don't really recommend it in it's current state.

There's only FOUR enemy types currently, all of which are the most boring and uninspired possible enemy designs for a fantasy setting (Bats, Goblins, Mimics and Skeletons). Like seriously? Thats IT? and you want more than the price of something like BONEWORKS or Pavlov?

The levels themselves are all also procedurally generated, and you WILL DEFINITELY NOTICE. Because everything looks exactly the same, which is to be expected to *some* extent for a game like this, but not to this level. There's practically 3 textures in this entire game (Brick, Cobblestone, and Stained Glass) and it goes without saying that it gets really old really fast. There's actually so few prefabricated rooms to piece together that you'll very often see the exact same looking corridor or chapel repeated several times in just a single level.

The music seems like it might be good at first because the hub has a pretty cool theme, but you'll quickly come to realize that every dungeon has the same 1 minute generic combat drum track looped ad nauseam.

& The same can be said for this game's entire art direction. At first it seems kinda cool, a little diablo-eque even, but the second you get into your second dungeon you'll realize every model might as well be a unity asset flip because it all looks so boring and similar to any other generic fantasy games you've already played.

This game definitely has the mechanics in place to be something awesome (Physical swordfighting, spellcasting, and a skill tree with progression), but everything else is SERIOUSLY lacking in both content and polish in its current state and its honestly an insult that they'd ask $35 for something this unfinished. Especially when you compare it to any other VR game in this price bracket.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally like the game, however I'm making this review negative in the hopes of getting the Devs' attention.
Hi Devs! You guys really need to fix your networking, having weapons be handled server side is terrible. I like playing with a friend who lives quite far, so it's not unusual to play with about 200+ ping, which in most games is fine, the issue here is that that latency is so noticeable that it's game spoiling whenever you hit an enemy or shoot an arrow (the arrow won't actually fly until your PC gets a response from the server, so double the ping time, same with melee weapons, the hit won't actually register or animate until the client gets a response from the server). This makes the game feel GIGA unresponsive.
I know you guys are hard working, I know for myself the nightmares of netcode so I understand that issues like this often go overlooked, please get this sorted.
Much love!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverything, so far, is intuitive and relatively polished for it being in early-access. The controls are smartly designed and simple to understand, and the gameplay is smooth, too. You should definitely do the tutorial before going into the first floor - lots of mechanics to learn, but it doesn't take long to master.
I was really surprised by the visuals - the dungeon levels are gorgeous, and the lighting looks great without burning my GPU.
The multiplayer system is what sets this game apart - it can sometimes feel repetitive by yourself, but having others working with you makes it so much fun. You gotta get your friends into this game!
Only criticism I'd have is that the walking/running speed can feel a bit slow in the large rooms, but that's probably stemmed from my eagerness to keep exploring these dungeons!
I definitely recommend it to anyone who loves ARPGs, action-fantasy, or dungeon crawlers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game needs to keep developing to completion. My buddies and I LOVE it, it's an awesome game with tons of potential. It's got a very "Diablo" vibe with realistic and tactical swordplay and combat. There's a bunch of different weapons that change the entire feeling of your play-style. The legendary weapons look and feel cool when you find them, and the perks of gear makes you excited to loot everything in sight. My only gripes so far, and it comes with the "early access" territory (which hopefully they're working on), is the variety in the monsters...there's quite literally only two types of monsters that you'll face...skeletons and goblins... Granted, there are "elite" skeletons and goblins mixed in, and as you decend into deeper levels, they'll gain armor and be harder to kill...but still...two monsters. Also, there's the dungeon itself, it gets pretty repetitive pretty quick. Rinse and repeat scenery and puzzles that kind of take away from the "discovery" aspect of the game pretty quickly. It'd be awesome to see much more in the way of monster diversity, and scenery. Also, more gear slots and cosmetic options would be amazing. All you get is a helmet and gloves for armor...and only the helmet changes the way you look...so quite literally everyone is the same dude wearing the same exact armor, except for the helmets, of which, don't have much variety in their cosmetic. As I said before, it's early access, so hopefully they're working on that. However, that being said, lots of these games never leave early access and never reach "launch". So I'm hopeful but skeptical that this game will reach it's potential within a reasonable amount of time. Regardless, it's worth picking up because what is there is absolutely fun and super fun with buddies you can enjoy it with. Please keep working on this, you've got seriously good framework and huge potential here.\t\t\t\t\t
