\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImagine a cross between Diablo and Hellsplit Arena, and you have Legendary Tales.
A dungeon crawling RPG played in VR with friends is probably something I always wanted but wasn't sure if it could be done. With weapon physics/collision, environment physics, and pleasing combat, the game feels like a classic experience you can physically interact with.

If you ever wanted to feel how playing a specific class/playstyle would play out when YOU are the one in control, you can experience it here. You can be a dual dagger wielding Rogue, or a Ranger, or Paladin/Fighter, the choice is yours. With the physical element to contend with, your playstyle might change. Your greatsword may do a lot of damage, but can it be swung in tight passageways? Your daggers may do high damage, but can you block with such small weapons?
Do you use the LVL 3 Heater shield with base stats or the LVL 5 Buckler that is smaller and harder to block with? Your actual skill may play a factor to your playstyle.

Though it is not perfect currently. Your character models are all the same *Armored Knight* model, equipment is just a helmet and gloves. The game runs fairly well but had a few performance hiccups here and there. Also the resolution even on max settings (Valve Index) were pretty blurry.

DO NOT play this expecting Blade & Sorcery, it is an RPG first and foremost. Enemies have health bars, and while physics are taken into account, you can't just pick up enemies and throw them unless they are stunned/weak.

This game is worth a look, though it's definitely a far better experience with friends.
Anxiously awaiting what updates come for the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAvoid. I haven't seen any significant progress from developers nor any desire to put up with this game again. Bottom line is if you want a swordfighting game go pick up Blade and Sorcery. To paraphrase some of my initial review:

>UI issues. Text tends to be blurry even when right in front of you making the game an eyesore to read (there are significant things to read).
>RPG stats/leveling. If this is your thing, cool, but the way it is handled in this game is not fun for me. The way stats are currently built tend to lock your character into a specific playstyle early on, and trying to "side-level" other styles like DEX when you dumped into STR will mean you do barely any damage. Weapon damage and combat performance is heavily dependent upon stats.
>Combat feels good sometimes, but there is not any real physics simulation going on here like Boneworks or Blade and Sorcery. Trailer is misleading in this regard and only shows finishing moves or special stuns which ragdoll the enemy, majority of your combat is spent slowly chipping away at enemies who do not react to your attacks and continue swing animations despite damage, forcing you to wait for the enemy to GIVE you an opening instead of taking advantage of what you think is one. Speciality moves per weapon are fun to experiment with and there's enough to keep it interesting, but at the end of the day the core of combat is not enjoyable.
>Top movement speed is abysmal and feels like walking through molasses. The starting town area is large and you will idly twiddle your thumbs and have your thoughts wander while you are holding your left stick forward waiting for your character to walk to the dungeon entrance.
>Enemy variety sucks and quickly becomes aggravating past the first few levels. Skeletons, shin-high goblins who are impossible to defend against, and bats who are impossible to hit and also are insanely lethal because of that. Your minibosses consist of an average enemy with a bunch of stats and maybe a gimmick attached meaning they take more hits than normal and kill you in about two.
>Co-op is a large reason why anyone (including myself) got this game in the first place. Co-op is not well fleshed out and weapons have invisible ownership tags when obtained from a crystal, preventing you from saying "Ah man I got a bow and my character uses swords, here you can have this" during a raid. For some reason there seems to be an abundance of protection on "stealing" items from other players despite the fact that public co-op lobbies are very unlikely in this kind of game.
>Grabbed enemies are essentially dead anyways, so unlike it would appear to be displayed in the trailer, grabbing one and having your friend beat the marrow out of it isn't really useful except for screwing around. Also in the trailer, enemies do not die nearly as easily as you think. Lots of weapons just do bumps and scrapes of damage unless you dump everything into STR early on (I dumped everything into luck and was unpleasantly surprised that without putting anything into combat stats killing things quickly is impossible, whereas if I dump all into STR I can punch a skeleton's head off in two punches. Punches actually do more damage than most weapons).
>Inventory management and looting is a complete chore. I spend about as much time walking around breaking pots and barrels as I do actually fighting. Auto-collecting loot should be an option at least for single player. Getting loot in some other manner then having to break open pots all day would be a lot nicer.
>Tutorial is an anti-refund gate that can quite literally take you almost two hours. While well-meaning it attempts to teach the player about every single possible scenario they may encounter and prevents you from progressing if you cannot do it, such as throwing a pugio at a target from a distance away. After you do the tutorial and make a new character, you have to put this character through an opening level which also must be completed prior to doing co-op or the main game at all. This must be done for each new character.
>Combat mechanics dictate a difference between a "heavy" and "light" attack. Delivering an attack with a certain amount of force counts as a heavy and does more damage, but heavies can only be done on a cooldown or after particular moves which reset the cooldown, otherwise you get a light attack that does piddly damage. Heavy attacks are independent of weapon, so two hand weapons are clunky, slow, and don't do anywhere near as much damage as two one-hand weapons throwing out heavy attacks independently. Two hands are also awkward to wield and strike with.
>Large variety of weapons which, besides special ability, have the depth of a puddle. Be it a mace, sword, club, or dagger, they all do the same thing in standard combat and the only difference between them is the level of the weapon which directly influences its stats and the special ability which is often tied to the stat the weapon is for. I found no purpose in seeking out particular weapons or purchasing new ones, I could find any old rusty sword and use it just as well as anything else because there is no difference when attacking besides the damage number.

There's the bare essentials of a good idea going on here which is why it received as much attention as it did, but I simply do not think the developers are equipped to put out an acceptable product with the scant development blogs I've seen and lack of overall change to the main game. Blade and Sorcery will much better allow you to stab and cut the heads off of things if that's what you're after.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fantastic VR game, it has great progression and a nice difficulty curve, the Dev team is incredibly hard working listening to every small issue.

Positives:
+Physics based combat, you can toss your enemies after a good parry or throw a chair at them to stun them, or simply send them into a dark pit and forget.
+Huge loot table from Normal, Magical, Rare, Unique, and Legendary items to find and use you will be hunting down these Items.
+Length I'm 18 Hours in and not finished the game once this is the EA build to so that's incredible Dungeons are very vast for a VR game you can spent upwards to two hours on a floor and so far there are 10.
+For what this game is doing it runs like a dream even in its early access state I've had no issues whatsoever performance wise.

Maybe For You Maybe Not:
+-Difficulty curve is wide for this game it'll beat you down. If you are not the most perceptive and reactive person this could be hard to master.
+-Leveling is based on use; get hit Vitality, use magic Intellect, shoot arrow Dexterity, smash bones Strength, use skills Wisdom. Skills are tied to equipment not the character.

Negatives:
-At time of review the enemy type is extremely small we have Skeletons of different variety IE armor and bow, Goblins, Bats, and Mimics more to come of course.
-Some finicky physics hard to explain, sometimes things just don't work the way you think they do.
-Personal gripe the enemies can really pop my personal space bubble sometime making parrying nigh impossible.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn exciting dungeon crawler. Also a 4 player Co-op dungeon crawler. The dungeon itself has up to 10 levels, plenty of weapon choices with different stats. Some weapons can be legendary with crazy abilities. Eagle shield flies like Captain America’s shield, Thor’s lightning hammer, daggers that summon spikes, etc.

Combat is physics based. Slice, stab, smash your enemies. Learn to parry attacks and grab your opponent and toss them off a ledge. Don’t want to get close? Grab a chair or pot and toss them, unload some fire balls or arrows from safety.

The dungeons themselves are very interesting, sometimes you can come across a floor that has quite some vertically to it, you may need to climb ropes and ladders to get around. You may also get lost, but you can find your way back with the portal spell or by use of the door wisps.

I’ve already spent a number of hours going through a few floors alone. I know I won’t be satisfied until I collect a bunch of legendary treasure. So I’ll be sure to go back in for much longer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLegendary Tales has the potential to be a good IP and I think that the more support this small team gets, the better the end product will be.

Being an early access title, it is lacking in some QoL features and some polish, however what is here shows a lot of promise. The game has immersive and scary pacing and it's pretty difficult if you don't take your time and evaluate each room of each dungeon. It's easy to get lost, and in a dungeon crawler that's a good thing. I would say the gameplay is a combination of any sword fighting game, Diablo, and traditional tabletop RPGs. You have stats and you level as you progress, with items to supplement. You portal back to town and sell your junk and go back in. If this wasn't a VR game, I wouldn't recommend it. I think it is a really good step for VR rpgs and I wish I had friends to play it with.\t\t\t\t\t
