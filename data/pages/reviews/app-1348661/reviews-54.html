\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHo boy...

I can't imagine who came up with the idea for this, but good grief...

This adds unnecessary complexity to an already complicated game. It was hard enough managing logistics when there was just the two things to build and deal with. Now there are depots and trains and railway lines as well as the infrastructure and docks.

Why? Who was playing HOI4 and saying "Gosh, what this WWII grand strategy game needs is more focus on the logistics"?

The tank designer is alright, I guess, but again, I wasn't asking for one. I was happy with light medium and heavy.

I don't like the changes to combat width. Things were FINE the way they were, now I have to relearn every damned thing.

Baltic focus trees are good enough. I don't like the Russian one, and I HATE the paranoia system. It's a pain. Also, The Russian tree isn't balanced properly, too many things to do in very little time.

The changes to the way doctrines are managed as well as military high command are interesting enough, even fun, but they are brought down by the mess logistics has become now. Attrition is everywhere, building railways takes time and resources from other things.

It really is all very unnecessary. I'd have taken the new focus trees, the railway gun and the floating docks; I'd have tolerated the new high command mechanics, I'd even have enjoyed a few new types of tank. I didn't want a complete redesign of a game I already loved and have sunk a truly ridiculous amount of time into learning.

5/10. If you enjoy tedious amounts of micromanagement, then this expansion is for you, though we're all stuck with these mechanics now, whether we like it or not. Thanks a bunch, Paradox, you fixed what wasn't broken and ignored what was. Again.\t\t\t\t\t

It's a fun new additional but I'd be a lot happier if the supply was either more generous or the build time faster.

The tank designer I'm not crazy about and it seems to lack the oomph that I felt with the Naval designer. That being said, I think I'll see myself come to enjoy it more with time as I get the hang of it.

I've been producing railway runs and assigning them to armies but I'm unsure if they were doing anything, again I think this is something I just need to get used to.

With all of the doctrine moving into the Corps the research seems very bare now and you can speed through them. I'm not overly keen on this and would like to see more tech added down the line. While on the subject of the corps itself. I do really like the new XP, perk and doctrine system, they make a real nice addition.

Overall, I'm enjoying it more than LR but I still think MTG is the best.\t\t\t\t\t
