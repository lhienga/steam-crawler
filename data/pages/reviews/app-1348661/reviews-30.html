Expansion - Hearts of Iron IV: No Step Back
This DLC adds new focus trees, a new supply system, and a tank crator to the game. Let's start with the worst parts of this expansion, which are the tank creator and supplies. In theory the supply system with tracks and trains makes more sense, and in theory we should have more control over what we do, but it's completely the opposite, until today I haven't found a good way to supply all units, no matter what the player does, the game always will be against you. As for the tank creator, it's the same as with the fleet creator, i.e. I just don't feel like doing it, and I prefer to use already made templates. Moving on to focus trees, Lithuania/Latvia/Estonia have very similar trees with small changes, and although they are not big, you can still play around with them. The Polish tree got a very nice update, now having a lot of political options, and even special decisions regarding the Polish Underground State if we fall under German occupation, which allows us to continue the fight. And of course, there is the issue of the USSR tree, which went from ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• to one of the best, giving us a lot of options and decisions for industry, army and politics. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, this is the best dlc so far.

The supply system takes some getting used to and should be much better explained by Paradox. YouTube videos help a lot, but it also just takes some getting used to. It slows the game down a bit which may or may not be a good thing depending on your play style. I'd suggest playing a straightforward Germany game before diving in to play the Soviets since Germany has far fewer supply issues at game start and you can take the time to see how changes you make affect supply.

The tank designer is interesting. But like Man the Guns, it add a lot more stuff to research which means you'll either skip most of it or have to wait additional years to make the most of it.

The officer corp, doctrine changes, and what not take getting used to just because it's all different and located in different places. It'll take a few games to overcome muscle memory habits--I still click the wrong buttons for production and logistics and trade just because I think about them from right to left and they are now one spot to the left of where they were.

The focus trees are pretty good and long overdue. I like that there are some 35 day options.

I briefly switched back to the previous version of the game (without the supply system) and it felt like a totally different game and I switched back pretty quickly. But I think the changes are worth learning and future bug fixes and tweaks will help improve the rough edges.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a model for how every DLC for this game should be.

The Soviet rework is amazing. The best part of it (Stalin's path) is free, but the alternative-history paths are quite fun as well. Unfortunately, I think they cut out a Democratic path that would've made my opinion far higher, but it doesn't ruin my opinion of the DLC.

The Poland tree is one of my favorites. Unfortunately, the Monarchist paths all supercede every other path on offer, but all five of them are incredibly fun countries that you can play through multiple times.

The Baltic trees, notably, Fascist Estonia and Monarchist Lithuania are quite fun, but aren't the most replayable. This should've honestly been where they reworked Finland.


And lastly, The new mechanics and Tank Designer are pretty good but I don't have much to say on them.

Overall, I have incredibly good thoughts of the DLC, and the patch that came with it.\t\t\t\t\t
