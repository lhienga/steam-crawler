\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy Dearest Reader,

I have purchased this product, Dear Reader, not for my own benefit but rather for yours.

In February of 2020, in the wake of the last disaster that descended on you and your fellow HoI4 players, La Resistance, I made a prediction that it would take an entire year to release their next expansion. I made a, what turned out to be generous, prediction of February, 2021. I was wrong. So very wrong. Rather than a year to release their own content, the HoI4 team has instead taken a few days more than 21 months. Perhaps they did it in honor of it being the year of our Lord, 2021. Perhaps fittingly, only the Lord knows.

Now you may be thinking, "What about Battle for the Bosporus?" and you would be half right. But that expansion wasn't made in totality by the HoI4 team. In fact, most of its elements were designed and built by modders with support from the HoI4 team. And for this, the comparatively few issues with BftB can be forgiven since the designers can't be expected to be held to a professional standard. Sure, the investment decisions may be broken or ignored by the AI and you get two chances to select a mediator for the Transylvania event, but they did their best and that is honestly the most you can expect from enthusiasts. In other words, the actual paid and presumably self-identified professional HoI4 team has taken the better part of two years to make their next in-house expansion, that being No Step Back.

And that is why it is so disappointing that, for your sake, Dear Reader, that without even installing it, short even of turning it on, I can assure you of several things:
I can assure you there there will be at least one absolutely impassable game-breaking bug that will be hotfixed before the weekend.
I can assure you that all of the irritating minor bugs that have been piling up over the years have not only not been addressed, but the list has grown. Probably significantly.
I can assure you that all of the dead, broken, and clashing focuses have not only not been addressed, but the list has grown.
I can assure you that all of the flaws with the frontline AI have not only not been fixed, but rather exacerbated by the fundamental changes to the underlying supply system that the frontline AI was designed to use.
I can assure you that the new supply system will be an unbalanced, economy killing mess that will turn most games into a WW1 simulator rather than a WW2 simulator if you so much as think about using your civilian factories for anything other than infrastructure improvements.
I can assure you, based on experience from prior expansions and the ship designer in particular, that none of the features introduced by No Step Back will (ever) be fully used by the AI.
And finally I can assure you that it will be at least one to two weeks before a token bugfixing effort is made, the team declares victory, and then goes radio silent for a month of vacation.

By then, it might be eligible for a Christmas discount, however small.
I urge you to wait.

Yours with affection,
Nora~\t\t\t\t\t
