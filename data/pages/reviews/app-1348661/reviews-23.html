I'm looking forward to seeing the best mods get updated to use these new systems, as they've got tons of potential.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo Step back is perhaps the best thing to ever happen to HoI4. It bring a crucial element - logistics - back to WW2 grand strategy. It drastically changes how you plan your offensives, removing some of the arcadey elements and requiring you to focus on key supply hubs, cities and transport routes. As it was historically and as it should be in a war simulator worthy of that title.

You can also build and optimize your own supply networks which is very expensive and time consuming but having supply while your enemy doesn't can be ridiculously powerful!

The tank designer is also ok I guess. The customization options are nice but the added cost and supply restrictions makes armour less useful than before, while making air even more OP. I always focus heavily on airpower and now even more so.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best DLC at launch that Paradox has released for HoI4. Relatively free of bugs and issues and patching is still in-progress ironing out the few issues that do exist.

Overhauls the USSR with an absolute beast of a tree which is incredibly fun for historical games. The ahistorical options though are a little less interesting as they all depend on fighting a civil war, which will basically always handicap you in the long-run. Making the Stalinist route the only way to go if you want to do really well. Also a lot of new unique advisors and generals who 99% of the time you will just never get to use as they will be purged away with no way to save them. This could use some tweaking as the USSR in-theory has a lot of super interesting advisors and officers who you will almost never get to use, even going ahistorical many of these advisors will be killed or not brought back from exile since you're on such a time crunch.

The Baltic trees do a good job of fleshing out their respective nations and making these small countries pack a heavy punch. While perhaps a bit overpowered they sort-of need to be if they're to have any chance of survival.

The Polish tree is a massive improvement over what came before it but somehow still feels a little off. Simply put some paths are just so much better than others. The Sanation Right path is better than the castle and much much better than the Sanation Left. Most of the monarchist options are just straight up better than historical, meanwhile there's precious little reason to go Peasant Union outside of the curiosity factor. Fighting a whole civil war just saw the wheel can turn blue and you can (maybe) get Madagascar? No thanks.

The logistics system is great though it will need to be tweaked a bit to make the scorched earth work as intended and debuff the logistic strike mission which at present seems way too good.

The officer corp system is really cool and adds a lot of flavor, though unfortunately I think it's a case that really benefits the Axis a lot more than the allies since they have a much better ability to grind XP than the allies do, even with the advisor XP generation.

Tank Designer is a really cool feature, shame I can't seem to design a good one to save my life.

Highly recommended, one of the best HoI4 DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC seems great on the surface, until you sink your teeth in.

Mechanics:
Tanks - The new Tanks aren't bad but Heavies are still just the best option by far in Vanilla which is frustrating. This DLC honestly just made them more OP, plus sillier, you can have something as big as the Ferdinand with a Christie suspension going faster than the trucks accompanying it. Light's and Medium's are about the same as before, useful but simply outclassed. Also I find it dumb that the best tank you can build is a Heavy with a cannon, 3 HMG's sticking out of it, a Christie suspension, cast armor, a gasoline engine that you upgrade the engine on to go 10KM/H. Can you even imagine that? I sure as hell can't, it's a damn heavy tank zooming past with so many guns it should belong in 40K.

Supply - The new supply system is alright and much more realistic, however it has a few keys issues. 1. Supply Hubs cost way too much to build and it makes them irrelevant, you just capture ones that existed from the start of the game and that's it. You would never build one while invading a country because by the time you have the front will have moved. 2. Trains are great for majors and easy to build, but for minors it makes the game very tedious as you have to wait long times to build them if you don't want to hurt your already very weak industry, this makes sense but from a gameplay perspective is annoying. 3,. Transport planes negate the whole new supply system entirely, a few hundred will supply any regions that you struggle to supply which is unrealistic and places further emphasis on the air war in a tank focused DLC. 4. The motorization priority on the armies unit tab is a bit silly, this isn't really an issue so much of a pet peeve of mine. You just press the more trucks button, never think about it again and since trucks are so easy to produce you always have it on. It just makes the new supply system less relevant since the range of the maximum trucks setting from supply hubs is quite long.

XP + Doctrines - The shift of doctrines from research to XP is one I don't fully understand, it frees up a research slot (2 for certain countries) in favour having to spend some more PP. But it still doesn't change the fact that countries that are allowed to send volunteers are far ahead when WW2 breaks out. There is now more customisation for each branch which is great but the XP costs behind doctrine make it just as frustrating for minors and more prohibitive to the Allies. Overall I like this new system and I think it's a step in the right direction but I think just the doctrines themselves should go back to research with the ability to speed it up with XP from before.

Combat Width - I really don't understand the new mechanics here well so I'll keep this brief. I see zero reason to not do 20 and 40 width divisions still except for Special Forces. Also, the way damage is spread out now across divs in a battle makes just spamming inf with shovels way too strong. Plus, reinforce memeing is still just the only real way to push in an even match up. Again I really on't know the new stuff here well this is just my impression of it so far.

Focus Trees:
The new Soviet tree is rough, the paranoia mechanic is annoying to deal with and makes you rush down that part of the tree every game you don't want to have a civil war. It also doesn't leave much if any room for even slight alt-history, you could save a general from every random purge event at a high cost just for the scripted events to kill him anyway. I understand that certain figures died IRL and they want it to stay historical but it would be nice to be allowed to save them and sacrifice others who survived. The new industry part of the tree is strangely weak for an industrial behemoth like the USSR, you get few benefits and some focuses are just nerfs with tiny upsides that are just bad to take but you have to. You also just get locked out of it when you enter a war with a major power, even though historically that's when the Soviet industry kicked into high gear (albeit moreso after 42). There is also just a large amount of 70 day focuses with plenty to get through, especially considering the need to rush down the paranoia focuses. It just isn't fun to sit around waiting 70 days over and over again for focuses that aren't really fun to do, personally I don't sit there excited thinking "oh boy in 70 more days I get to purge some random admiral I never would have used anyway".

The alt-history paths are also rough, you fight a low supply civil war from a chosen section of the country which is slow and grindy. Then you get ganked by the Germans (or the Japanese with all of China under them potentially, which is a horrible war because the supply is horrendous) in a year with no time to prep or get things in order. It's just disappointing how boring these paths are and even after the civil war there's not much to do in them really. Not to mention that if you fought the civil war then whatever major attacks you by the time it's over the game is lagging beyond belief and you're unstoppable anyway so why continue.

The focus trees for the Baltic are cool and fun to play, but their very existence is incomprehensible to me. Italy and Finland both had massive amounts more to do with WW2 and they have nothing to their name. Italy's is a joke that you finish a year after the war starts with nothing interesting and no "real" alt-history paths. How can Paradox justify not touching them at all in this DLC but giving the Baltic a massive focus tree. It's just because they want to sell them in another DLC, which is understandable until you see the focus trees that modders make and are free...

Listen, I love HOI4 and play it alot and buy the DLC's because I think they add good content to the game. But it was so disheartening to see this DLC that was supposed to change how we play the game so massively amount to so little good change. This DLC is a strong framework which Paradox could really improve if they spent time and effort doing so, but they'll be stuck fixing the bug and optimisation issues until they start working on the next DLC.

TL:DR Buy it on sale I guess and if you liked playing the Soviets it sucks to suck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHOI4 is the worthy successor to the HOI series. It is the best since HOI2 and is overall an extremely satisfying WW2 4X experience. No Step back brings many cool innovations that make this experience even better.

The problem is that Paradox is too dependent on the DLCs. No Step Back left the base game a complete mess. I ended up purchasing the DLC not for the added content, but to be able to play with at least some degree of optimization.

Paradox - you need to change how you operate. The honest truth is your games are built to have the ENTIRE DLC content (excluding music and image packs). This means that it can take >$100 to be able to play just 1 of your major titles. I own all of these titles (some multiple in a series) many of which I stopped playing after a DLC paywall - Prison Architect, HOI, CK, EU, City Skylines, Surviving Mars, Stelleris.

Suggestion - go all in. Make the DLC mandatory. Focus your development efforts on making the best games possible. In exchange lower the DLC pricing by at least half.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo Step Back is a wonderful addition to the game on most accounts. The new focus tree for the Soviet Union is MASSIVE and the options contained therein are absolutely a ton of fun to mess around with. Poland also has a new tree with some hidden paths that greatly increase the diversity of playthroughs possible.

The new supply system is great in concept; however, it does cause many issues with the pacing and balance of the game. Smaller nations are nigh impossible to supply properly, especially when under your railways are under constant bombardment by the enemy AI nations. This means you have to dedicate the vast majority (or entirety) of your construction queue to railway production and repair which severly curtails the options of what you can build. (Protip: Set your main supply hubs near the front line to fully motorized.)

That all being said, I must address the elephant in the room. That being the performance, or lack thereof, of the game with this DLC. The performance issues that started with the launch of this DLC are beyond unacceptable. Starting in the year 1941/1942, when the world is at war, the sim speed slows to a crawl. Speed 5 reduces to essentially speed 1 (2 if you're lucky.) Even with the previous DLCs the game would slow down significantly when the world went to war; however, the extent of the slowdown pales in comparison to that introduced with No Step Back. I assume this is on account of all the new supply calculations taking place.

Is it worth a purchase? I'd unequivocally say "Yes!" if you love HOI4; however, it may be prudent to wait until the performance issues are addressed if you're content with the free patch. That is, of course, if they address them at all...\t\t\t\t\t
