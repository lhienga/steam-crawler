\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would give this a neutral review if I could.

There are definitely some things to like. The new supply system for one does seem like an upgrade, even if it's still gonna need some tweaks to get it right. The Tank Designer seems nice as well-but again tweaks are needed. In this case even more so because the new way doctrines are unlocked relies exclusively on XP while you also need it to design tanks-and as before-to update divisions. From what i've played there is way too few XP to go around, and countries like the USA can now just blitz through their Naval Doctrine in an incredibly short time.

The USSR focus tree looks good...on paper. In practice you need way too long for most of the alternate History paths. You need at least a year of setup and will lose a ton of Generals, ironically you lose more Generals to purges if you play as Stalins opposition than if you just play Stalin. It seems weird to me that Stalin suffers less from his Paranoia than his opposition if the Alt History path is one where Stalin gets more Pushback and there actually is a Great Conspiracy that tries to overthrow him-you'd think that you can save more officers from the Purge if you are actively working against Stalin. But whatever, it's not a huge proble. The fact that the Civil war takes too long to start is a bigger one. It basically cripples your Economy to such an extent that you are almost worse off than at Game Start in 1939, while not having had the time to do any focuses to improve your army and get ridd of the massive debuffs you have on them. If germany does an early Barbarossa you're in a really bad spot.

Even as Stalin it takes forever to get rid of the Paranoia system that makes him Purge Officers. Your first year and a half are basically reserved for that focus path which restricts your choices a lot.

It's even worse for the Monarchists and fascists because while you can start your Uprising in Eastern Europe or Russia Proper as Trotsky or Bukharin, the Monarchists start in Siberia. The pure walking time for an Infanterist from your Capital in Chita to Moscow is 160 days. In a low supply hellhole during a Civil war you will take forever.

The economy tree of 5 year plans seems terrible as well. Why would I do focuses that increase my Consumer Goods factories usage by 5% when the returns don't seem to be worth it at all. They make your factories more efficient, but if you don't do those focuses you can probably build way more factories anyway, so in the End you barely even profit.

The Baltic trees seem ok, but to be totally honest I don't care about them at all. (I have tried playing as Estonia.) I also don't understand why the Baltics got focus trees instead of Finland and the Post Soviet States in case of a Monarchist takeover. A shared Tree for former Soviet States like the Chinese Warlords have a shared tree. I haven't tried Poland yet so I can't say much about them.

In general there is much new content but it seems very rough around the edges. Especially the needed time for focuses and balancing of the USSR Civil War. If there is an update that rebalances a lot of it I might change this review.\t\t\t\t\t

While I will not touch these topics in detail, it is important to note that all overhaul features are free(except for the officer corp) and are a major step in the right direction which finally puts some strategy into the "grand strategy".

What the DLC actually come with? Nation flavor for the Baltic States, alternative paths for the Soviet Union and Poland, a new officer system, railway guns, mulberry ports, and the tank designer.

Focuses:
The Baltic trees are poorly designed overall and lean far to heavily into alternative history even within their historical paths. They add very little to the region and do not interact with other nations mechanically outside of the Soviet Union. Poland now has a proper historical tree, but it's alternate history paths, particularly the Communist, Socialist, and Monarchist paths, are completely absurd and are poorly researched. Furthermore, the historical tree runs out of content relatively quickly if you intended to do a historical(free) Polish run and survive the blitzkrieg leaving me very frustrated with Paradox's priorities. The Soviet Union tree is, to put it lightly, extremely apologetic to Stalin by justifying his cruel purges. Alternative history paths focused around Trotsky, the exiles, and Bukharin are interesting, but do not reach their full potential. In a sense, it is a reverse of Poland. The Stalin Tree has the most content(and is free), but it completely overshadows all other paths.

Officer System:
The officer system, despite being poorly executed, enhances the doctrine changes. It allows the player to select a favored doctrine for the nation and for individual generals and allows a player to spend army, navy, and air mana to specialize their forces by effectively changing up the culture of a nation's military. The system is a glorified mana dump, but it is genuinely a good attempt to add more character and customization to the player's forces. Hopefully this feature will be expanded. If you enjoy modded hoi4(like most people) this is a must buy.

Railway guns, mulberry ports, and tank designer:
The Railway gun and mulberry ports fall more on the rule of cool side rather than being practical. In my experience, the railway gun usually just sits a province behind your front line and gives a flat bonus to your troops. The mulberry port is a fine addition if you have a large navy like if you play as the UK or US, but is completely unpractical otherwise due to it's relatively high cost as well. The tank designer is neat, but has been nerfed into the ground since the launch of the DLC and turns into more of a hassle after the novelty runs out. All of these features have the potential to be used in mods, especially the railway gun and is a must buy for those interested in the modding scene.

Conclusion: Paradox's content designers are complete trash that don't know how to code, but the meat and potatoes of the DLC: the Officer Corp, tank designer, and railway guns, and the mulberry port will only grow in importance as they are incorporated more and more into mods.
Buy the DLC if KR has a massive release or when OWB 4.0 comes out or if it's on half price.\t\t\t\t\t
