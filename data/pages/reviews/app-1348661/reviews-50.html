\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo Step Back is a mixed bag.

On one hand, many of the additions are solid. The new focus trees, while some may question the relevance of the nations chosen, mostly the Baltics, what we got was fairly solid. The new supply system is fun to play with and while it does take some learning, understanding it can greatly change how you play. Railroads and supply hubs also open up a new aspect of offense, and even a minor can make major contributions to a war by cutting off railroads. And the tank designer is fabulous, with plenty of variety and versatility. As for the new military high command, while it initially feels quite slow, once war actually starts you manage to unlock doctrines at a healthy rate that feels organic.

However, there are some issue as well. For one, the number of bugs. Broken portraits, France not forming Vichy, multiplayer disconnects breaking the AI, it's a bit of a mess. Additionally, most nations start with their armies completely overloading the supply system, and for nations like the Soviets this can be massively damaging to your early game. Speaking of the Soviets, economy is a nightmare. The Five Year Plans are almost useless, particularly due to raising the amount of consumer goods you need. The rest of the tree however is quite good, and feels well spaced out in timing especially for the purges. Plenty of other smaller issues also take away from the experience, like the Egyptian railroad not extending to El Alamein, a railway station.

Overall, I'd say that NSB is a positive experience that dramatically shakes up how you play the game, for better or for worse. With a few bug fixes, rebalances, and small changes, I think the DLC can be really strong.\t\t\t\t\t
