\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA buggy, untested, and over-priced mess.

I was looking forward to the changes to the logistic system-but instead of adding any appreciable depth it has just added more micromanagement.

The naval battle notifications are now broken (on Mac), and they're so obviously and consistently broken that Paradox had to make the decision to release this DLC knowing it's broken. I've had a few CTDs with this DLC, not many, but without No Step Back the game was extremely stable. The UI seems way more sluggish with this DLC; clicking through notifications can take 1-2 seconds for something that used to be near instantaneous, unassigned units for example. It's extremely frustrating that there are still so many glitches after 2 patches/hotfixes since this DLC was released.

The new tank designer is fun, but a bit confusing. The image it picks for a given tank is almost always flat out wrong, the option to manually select an image is broken because NSB just uses whatever misleading image it wants. The ability to compare different designs would be nice.

The changes to Command Power and Political Power feel really unbalanced. After filling all available High Command and Chief of Army/Navy/Air Force slots I don't have enough CP to recruit a new general. I feel like you should be able to unassign an officer from one of these slots, instead of just replacing them.

The changes to Combat Width feel like a big bag of meh. I guess it's more realistic to have to create specific units for specific terrain types, but if this basic game mechanic was so broken then why did it take 5 years to fix it?

There are railway guns, and that's cool. There are floating harbors for naval invasions, but I haven't noticed much difference between these and regular naval invasions. I haven't plumbed the depths of Stalin's paranoia and the Focus Tree changes because I've been trying to get a handle on the changes to the base game.

I'm cranky, in case that wasn't blindingly obvious, and feel that I've wasted my money on this DLC. HOI4 as a whole remains a great game, in spite of and not because of No Step Back.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have good and bad things to say about this expansion.

The good - A wider variety of division setups have become viable, and this is fantastic. 20 and 40 width divisions bored the hell out of me. The tank designer is absolutely wonderful and fun to play around with.

The bad- What were they thinking with this supply system? It's one thing for it to be about complicating the supply situation while invading the Soviet Union. Fine. but herein lies the problem; the supply system is so restrictive that it just just moving ONE AREA causes your divisions to lose out on all their supplies. You simply CANNOT build supply chains fast enough to keep up with advances, creating situations in which you're constantly bogged down, even if you're doing something as simple as invading Romania. Trying to fight your way OUT of the mountains on the border becomes a freaking nightmare, and because supply depots cost more than a civilian factory to build and the decision to speed it up can only be used once in the game, you're inevitably left in a situation in which no matter which decision you make, your supply lines are screwed. No, not just for invading Russia, try invading Romania, try invading Yugoslavia. It's a nightmare scenario. I haven't even tried fighting in Africa, I can't imagine it being any better there. It feels like this entire concept was playtested as Germany blitzkrieging France, and that was it, that was the extent, because anywhere else it is MISERABLE. It is so bad that, until this supply system gets fixed, I am no longer going to play this game, it is THAT stressful to deal with.\t\t\t\t\t
