\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished my first Ironman game as Poland and I have some thoughts. Firstly the supply mechanic feels very unintuitive, and it was nearly impossible to push into Germany, especially with the Soviet army zerg rushing and taking up all the supply. There is a function to not let allies use supply but it still feels like there needs to be some work on the supply mechanics, the supply caps seem to always be lower than they need to be and despite building more railways, supply depots, motorising supply etc, it was never enough to deal with the issues. It also felt very arbitrary when taking supply depots and cutting off enemy railroads actually impacted their supplies. Secondly the peace deals seem to somehow have gotten ever worse than before? I’m genuinely shocked at how bad they were, the borders made no sense, the allies made puppet regimes in places they didn’t even fight etc. I occupied pretty much all of Germany and a lot of their allies in the east such as Hungary and Slovakia. I also occupied all of Czechia. Somehow the US established puppet states on territory I controlled despite them not even being in my faction. Eastern Europe in general is littered with one province minor puppets from the allies despite them not setting foot on that front. The Soviets puppeted all of Germany despite the fact that I was the one controlling the states and I was the one pushing, and I couldn’t even get states I had claims on since the Soviets and Americans made puppets there. Britain puppeted Vichy France rather than returning the land to free France, america just decided to randomly take savoy as well. Ultimately I genuinely can’t say the DLC is worth 20 euro. It ads 2 complete focus trees and 3 small focus trees, and although the focus trees are fun, the army command stuff is pretty meh, it doesn’t really add anything, and the logistics mechanics are needlessly confusing. I also experienced a lot of audio glitches and some leader portraits were broken. It doesn’t fix issues such as peace deals not making any sense, and to me the new supply mechanics just made wars feel unnecessarily long. I couldn’t push in Germany past Berlin for a year pretty much because my troops would randomly lose all their supply. Unless there’s a major patch, wait with buying this DLC until it’s on sale.

Edit: It has been brought to my attention that my review is a little bit misleading, the supply system is in the free patch, my apologies for that. However targeting infrastructure with bombers is a DLC only feature. "Sorry general you do not own the DLC for this type of mission that is crucial for the new logistics system. Can we entice you to get it for 20 euro by making the game noticeably worse otherwise for no good reason?" They should have been honest that the main thing you're paying for is the tank designer and 2 unique and 3 semi unique focus trees. I also forgot to mention the tank designer in my review (mainly because I was playing as Poland and didn't have the industry for tanks), which is neat if you're the type of player who liked building your own ships in man the guns or coming up with your own designs and I could see it being really appealing to people, but sadly I'm not that kind of player.\t\t\t\t\t
