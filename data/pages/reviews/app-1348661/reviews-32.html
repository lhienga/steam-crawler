\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, after about 15 hours with NSB, and around 2,000 hours with the base game (100% achievements before NSB), I have a pretty rough idea of how this DLC functions and contributes to the game.

The verdict? It adds much needed core mechanics to the game that provide another layer of strategy previously ignored. Now you don't wait until your supply situation has gone disastrously wrong, you have to be on top of it, fast, or else the war you are involved in could very well turn against you.

The focus tree for the USSR is comprehensive, bold and absolutely massive. Yes, there are some things that need editing. I think the USSR is too weak at the moment but trying to demonstrate the actual USSR in a video game is quite difficult. If your too weak at the start of the German-Soviet war, you collapse. Too strong, you roll over Germany. A balance is always hard to find.

The AI seems to have undergone a metamorphosis of actually being half-decent. I welcome this change. Some cheeky and clever coding from the devs have ensured this. Check out their dev diaries for more information on the topic.

So I can wholeheartedly recommend NSB. If you want to wait until they Iron out the bugs and clean up the focus trees, please do, but I would still absolutely pick this DLC up down the road at some point. As it stands, I believe it adds more to the game through its additions, than it takes away in its bugs and unbalanced focuses.

Looking forward, this may very well be the magnum opus of Hearts Of Iron IV. I'm going to try and enjoy this period while I can and I hope many others can too!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of interesting changes but again execution lets you down.

The logistical supply system is an interesting change but seems to overly punishing not to mention seems to be lacking a peacetime/wartime modifier. Invading the Soviet Union which was tiresome with then old supply issues before becomes a major pain in the ass (especially with how quickly tanks eat up supplies).

The Soviet Focus Tree is WAY too large and there are some major bugs that need to be ironed out. For example, during two test-games to follow the historical Stalin route I have become a resurrected Trotsky AFTER assassinating him?!? It appears near impossible for the Soviet Union to follow its historical route whether the player or the AI is leading them even with historical focuses path set. As far as the other nations that got focus tree updates, Poland's is nice but the others have little meaning other than to work with the other focus tree updates as they are too small to make any difference in the war.
Finally, as others have mentioned they should have updated Finland's focus tree if they were going to update the Baltic states as Finland was caught up in the fighting between Germany and the Soviet Union like the Baltic states.

Terrain and weather affecting combat width is probably going to be a pain to deal with until somebody (not me) figures out the "best" templates for the different unit types to deal with the modifier.

I will probably update this review after I have played around with it a bit more (and Paradox has corrected some of the more egregious issues) but for now unless you intend to play as Poland or the Soviet Union and want more options than the old focus tree offers I can't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
