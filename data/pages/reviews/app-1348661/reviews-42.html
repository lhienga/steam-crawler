\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat DLC in concept, poor in execution.
There are two main mechanics that this DLC adds: Supply/trains, and focus trees/events.

The supply system does add a cool element of targeting railway networks to effectively 'encircle' divisions. However, too much of your production will be on railways and trains (~50% will be, at least playing as the soviet union). This means it can be incredibly easy to stalemate or result in deathstacking late game. Worse of all, is how quickly units melt the MOMENT you are a tile or two out of a railway line, often leading to the enemy pushing you back multiple tiles (as 0% organisation) until they reach your supply zone. Even when prepping for a war against independent Manchukuo in 1941, your units will melt because of wacky logistic mechanics even if you think your prepared.

Focus tree wise, pretty good. Soviet's are a fun balance of fighting with the AI (in a good way) as Stalin goes off and does his thing while you secretly plot. I've only tried the 'Centre [Stalin]' and the 'Right opposition [but still communist]' trees. Ousting Stalin makes the game vastly more difficult. While fun for singleplayer, it would be a detrimental shoot in the foot for multiplayer, as there are no clear benefits/pros to go anyone other than Stalin. To get ready for a civil war, many of the opposition focuses are solely to gain regional support, no buffs/factories or anything. So 1936-39 will be full of focuses that dont actually benefit you even after the soviet civil war ends. Fun as a challenge, but no real reward other than winning a war (fun for the first time). Oddly, despite kerensky still being alive, there's no democratic tree/focus. Likewise, despite the variety of the russian civil war (green army, black army, white army, menshevik, bolshevik etc). there's only 4-5 main paths to go down (stalin, trostky, moderate communists, empire, fascist).

Overall, the supply system needs to be balanced to not nuke your divisions near instantly, leading to your army 'melting' the moment the enemy pushes. Once fixed, this DLC would be a 6/10 - due to the decent trees overall, but lack of deviation, and no trees for other communist countries or nearby countries (e.g. Mongolia).\t\t\t\t\t
