\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was excited for the new Soviet Focus tree and the tank designer, but, after having played the dlc for a while I am thoroughly disappointed. The supply system is a mess, In Western Europe, where there are less tiles and therefore your armies tend to bunch up, the supply isn't enough to properly invade, leading to factories being wasted to amass enough supply to blitzkrieg France. On the other hand, in Eastern Europe, a few supply hubs are enough for hundreds of divisions. Tanks are unusable now, as they collapse supply networks anywhere unless you build dozens of hubs specifically for offensives, which considering their cost it's not really an option and a lack of supply grinds an offensive to a halt as your divisions slow down dramatically. Blitzkriegs and other offensives involving encirclements and quick invasions through swathes of territory aren't practical anymore, as the instant your units are two states or so away from their supply hubs, they lose all their supply which, as mentioned before, slows them and gives them a heavy attack and defense penalty. All in all, the supply system doesn't know what it wants to be; is supply precious, where only a few hubs exist and you must take care not to over extend yourself while also being supplied when you aren't over extended, or are supply hubs meant to be built every few miles as you push through a country? The soviet focus tree is now full of filler, to the point where it isn't possible to finish most of your tree by the 40's like most nations and the political paranoia system is just irritating. You also need 225 pp to conquer the baltic states which is ridiculous. Army xp gain was reduced which, considering doctrines are now researched with xp and the new officer corps system, makes no sense. Air power is also infuriatingly strong as CAS can shred your supply in days, making playing germany against the allies in '40 or the soviet union against germany cruel and unusual punishment. Finally, keeping your research up to date is incredibly difficult, even if you ignore the navy and without having to deal with doctrines. While there are more problems, this review is already too long so I will refrain from continuing.

TL;DR, The supply system has an identity crisis, offensives play out like it's WWI, tanks are unusable, the soviets suck now, there isn't enough xp to go around, there is too much to research and air power is too strong.\t\t\t\t\t

Every DLC for this game, with the exception of MtG, has been a lackluster content starved mess (TtV and DoD) or an overly convoluted and bugged mess (BotB and now NSB).

The biggest praise I can give this expansion is the new tank designer and officer corps. Huge plus that makes this game still playable for me. Unfortunately they took something I was looking forward to, the new Soviet foci for flipping back to Tsardom or going democratic, is an utter mess of epic proportions and the biggest reason for this is the "Stalin's Paranoia" mechanic. Poland has a new updated tree too. It's also quite broken, especially if you go fascist. You can, get this, surrender Danzig, and then successfully demand Pomerania after. Effectively making the surrender pointless because all you lost was Danzig in the exchange.

The Baltic states are boring. Small, boring, not much to do unless you're good at exploiting the game and gaming the mechanics altogether. No kidding, they try hard to combat exploitative play styles with every patch, all while introducing nation tree's that would require that exact play style in order to have any actual fun with said nations. (I'm again reminded of TtV)

Pass on this until a sale happens, cause they aren't going to fix even half the problems this introduces if past experience is anything to go by.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey tried, they really did, and there are a lot of good features here. Railways are cool, strategic bombing railways are even better, and trucks are actually needed to get supply to your guys. It's also a load of fun to find your divisions stuck on the wrong side of the river, without supply, and launching a counter-attack to bring them home (really, not being sarcastic)

HOWEVER I MUST DIGRESS

I am giving this a negative review because of the sea-supply mechanic. Supply now only comes from the highest level naval base, and there is no way to turn this off. This means as Soviets you will always be trying to supply from Leningrad, to anywhere in the world. Asia? it's coming from Leningrad. Kerch Peninsula? Leningrad. The enemy will stop this.

I am playing a great USSR campaign, and I got excited to be sending sea-supply to my low supplied troops in the Crimea. Nevertheless, the ships did not come, for they cannot, and there is currently no workaround about this. Very frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t
