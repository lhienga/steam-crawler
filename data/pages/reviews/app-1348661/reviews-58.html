\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery pleasantly surprised by this DLC. I think, generally, the HoI4 team does some of the best work in Paradox, but this is certainly one of their best. I'll go over the biggest changes from my few hours of playing so far:

- tank designer/division changes: Tank designer feels so natural, this is a great change and opens up a lot of possibilities. The division changes are welcome too, after like 800 hours and almost every achievement, the meta was stale and I'm glad I'll get to experiment some more.

- focus trees: so far, so good. Poland was fun and the USSR really fits, the sheer scale of the focus tree and how intricate it is allows for some really interesting possibilities. I also think that the paranoia system was implemented quite well and, while micro-y, does make the USSR more dynamic and realistic. It doesn't feel like I'm just clicking the button - it has more flavor.

- supply: THANK GOD, I LOVE this so far. Yeah, it requires more micro, but finally I have some control over supply and know wtf is going on and how to fix it. Implemented very well, though I do think it could be expanded and developed further. This has been a godsend during some of my brief China test runs (I have struggled with the warlord achievement forever because of supply issues, and this has already improved that massively!).

- officer corp/doctrine: Good change, more depth without added complexity, more dynamic and just a straight up solid improvement.

No bugs encountered so far, but some older focus trees probably need updates to reflect the railroads. Hungary's "integrate the railroad" focus should, well, provide railroads, not infrastructure. Playing around with Italy for their achievement was similar, Africa is a major focus for Italy yet the complete lack of railroads or focuses that help with that should be addressed.

All in all, I am very happy with this DLC and recommend it. Great balance of quality of life, content, and updates/changes without adding needless complexity.

Additional Info
=====

You thinbk that will be it. Nut there's more, this expansion set is like a reverse midas, corrupts anything it touches.

One crucial problem of it being the supply system. I've talked about the vanishing supply problem, now I'm about to give you a deeper look into the core of the problem:

The supplies do not flow in this game in a natural way, sure each tile can get supplied from the nodes which is supplied by railways. Yet the supplies in each tile does not flow between tiles, resulting in a hilarious situation where in one tile the troops are starving to death while the supplies in the next tile just sits there.

Another problem concerning the tile is that the supply capability of each tile is determined by a default value, you cannot expand the supply capacity of a given tile save for constructing a hub, which may or may not work.

And imagine you'll have to march all the way from Washington to Tokyo, or worse, from Berlin to Vladivostok. This system simply does not worl.

The second crucial problem is the Tank Designer, where should I start? I got no frigging clue.

How about begin the accusation with the design companies, which now seems to do the exact opposite of what it was meant to do. Say I put army ordnance group as my design company, I'll get a -10 penalty on armor when the modifier is +5%.

And that was the less broken part, the new feature of autoupdate obsoletes your design each time you research something new, sometimes replacing your design with something you didn't ask for.

Furthermore, the entire system is kinda broken, since the cannons are all over the place and the value assigned simple do not bear comprehension.

Finally, the last crucial problem: the military doctrine and construction balance. They basically removed the need to research doctrines but added the need to build supply infrastructures. The devs addressed the former issue with increasing XP gain, which was good for the XP consumption added. However, they did not address the added research capacity once the doctrines are removed from the equation, which is a minor problem since less research options means I get to rush 43-45 techs earlier.

The construction, on the other hand, is completely different. The devs did nothing to address the need to build up the supply network. This means you'll have significantly less free construction queues available to you if you don't want your troops to starve. While the game is still balanced as if you don't have the issue.

I would suggest the devs to add a option to disable all new features for the time being, msince the game is kinda completely broken at this point.\t\t\t\t\t
