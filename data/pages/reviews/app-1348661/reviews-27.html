\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis new DLC is an important step in the development of HOI4. I think it will greatly increase the lifespan of the game for at least a few years. In time, it's innovative additions to the mechanics of the game will be remembered and continue to be used extensively.

The new supply system replaces its simplistic predecessor with a new dimension to strategic play, adding a whole new element of combat and industry to plan around. It now requires more intelligence to ensure that your troops are consistently supplied, and you are given more ways to sabotage the supply of your enemy. The supply system adds even more ability for a small nation that is played well to defeat a larger nation that is not. However it is also important to consider that whenever an awesome new mechanic like this is added it just gives paradox's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ AI a new opportunity to be ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.

The new office corps mechanics are pretty awesome, it adds new value to army experience and allows you to specialize your country a lot more. It can really reward you for planning your campaign well. Even in my short time playing, I have already suffered for choosing an offensive officer corps when I need the other, and vice versa. I have also felt the benefits of making an officer corps and setting doctrines that work well for my playstyle. The new officer corps mechanics also give you new tools to groom the perfect general, and new benefits for doing so. It's an awesome feeling when you use all the these mechanics perfectly and end up having a perfect level 8 tank commander genius advisor with all of the right traits zooming around enemy lines supported by perfectly chosen doctrines.

The new focus trees are also great fun, and it will be interesting to see how they continue to play out in multiplayer. The new trees add a new level of depth to play in Eastern Europe, giving you a bunch of fun new adventures to go through. Poland, rather than just being a speed bump for Germany, can be a power of its own (perhaps a bit too powerful) with various personal unions and alliances that bring together the non-aligned minors of Europe. The Baltic states have interesting focus trees that make them from simply a battleground between Russia and Germany, to important buffer states that can swing between one side or the other, or to their own side. The Soviet Union has been given an epic mega-tree that gives them various options in how to defend their country from the fascists and dominate Europe. While the tree as a whole is stunning, some of the sub-paths are a bit underwhelming (looking at you White Russia path), but it's mostly all groovy. Your massive country is given an array of hefty debuffs at the beginning of the game that you must selectively remove before the Germans attack, forcing you to specialize based on which parts you fix. The new propaganda campaigns are another interesting (and powerful) tool to win the sacred war and "Sweep out the scum". The Soviet Union's new focus tree is a far cry from how it has looked in previous years and it is a change for the better.

The new width system is definitely appreciated in that it has broken up the old meta, but I am doubtful that it will prevent a "best width" from being discovered. Already, nerds with spreadsheets and calculators are threatening to make a new width meta that will make the game less interesting. Regardless, the changing of combat widths will probably make some divisions better at defending cities, some better for marshes and so on. The new changes have also made larger divisions a bit less OP, and coming up with new division templates is a good bit of fun.

The tank designer is also an interesting new addition to the game, adding even more ways to specialize your strategy and even more new uses for army xp. The designer allows you to make armored monsters that punch through enemy lines but struggle to follow up and make encirclements, or fragile little creatures that can zoom from Minsk to Moscow in one day (BTW that needs to be fixed it's super OP), or anything in between. The additional specializations that it adds (like flamethrower tanks and aquatic tanks) make having an armored core even more useful and interesting than it already was.

Overall, I think that the the strong points of this update are great and will continue to be used to make the game better, and the flaws are things that can be fixed in future patches.
