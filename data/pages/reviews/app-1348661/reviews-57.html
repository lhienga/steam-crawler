\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith this DLC and update, Poland has finally become VERY enjoyable to play; with either monarchist, communist, fascist or the "true" historical path. The Baltics have a staggering array of focuses for such small countries, and I will absolutely play as each of them in the coming future. The sheer size of the new Soviet tree matches the size of the USSR itself. The combat system, chain of command and supply mechanics have been completely reworked.

I do rate this DLC incredibly high, but I have a couple of minor nitpicks. The Russian Imperial flag is wrong, it's the St. Andrew's Cross and not the white-blue-red tricolor. Plus I kind of wanted more variety to choose who becomes Tsar, like maybe an older Aleksey or Anastasiya (I know it's not "historically accurate", but it's HOI4. Paradox has done plenty of unhistorical stuff before).

Other than that, excellent addition to the game. 9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

Attrition. Basically, at the start of the game the soviets are suffering attrition everywhere and there is just generally no supply. It's rare to not see a unit attritioning when on a front line. God forbid you want to attack Finland.

You have a bunch of economy stuff to do before Germany attacks, but you also have diplomacy stuff to do so you can get your Molotov-Ribbentrop claims, while also managing the purge (which the whole "wait 175 days" is seriously annoying. ON TOP of military focuses, as well as navy/air/research.

So if you aren't micro'ing all parts of the front at every moment, you could easily Germans flooding through. The Spanish Civil War is also just.. horrible. Using troops that normally could single handedly solo the war, I can barely push anymore. Not to mention supply is absolutely abysmal, so even if I wanted to I couldn't.

Lastly, I really do not like how the purge mechanics work, I much preferred the more simple nature of pre-DLC. By the end, I lost every top general and field marshal, so that sucked.
