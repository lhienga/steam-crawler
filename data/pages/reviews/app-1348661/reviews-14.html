\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:
The new tank designer is pretty good, and encourages you to research technologies that you normally never research, as some of the best tank equipment such as good radio, stabilizers, and literal gas turbines can all be found in technologies that people rarely ever research
The new supply system can result in some pretty cool strategy, however
The bad:
Supply hubs take an absolutely ludicrous amount of time to produce given that you probably want to build them while at war to keep your troops supplied in low supply areas. This makes Europe basically the only battleground where you can use the new tanks and such without getting harassed by supply issues the moment you step out of range of the supply hubs. Naval bases can be used as supply hubs, and they're only 1/4 the price for the exact same effect.
Some strategies have become basically unbeatable. 1 example is:
CAS used to be really good for ground battles, but fall very short against all of the other planes. Now, with one of the new spirits in the new officer corps you can get a fully maxed out CAS design for only 175 exp, plus the spirit itself for 50. This allows you to get a fully maxed out CAS design as Germany in 1937 using the Spanish civil war, which makes practically a destroyer ship in the air. The only real reason to build fighters in any capacity now is if you need to intercept enemy bombers, or you need to get air superiority for a paradrop (and even then you can sometimes still use CAS for air superiority)

Overall, I still like the DLC. It's fun to use, however I do not know how they didn't see the ultra-stuka or supply being really annoying because of the supply hubs being the second highest cost building coming. 7/10\t\t\t\t\t

for the Soviet Union, if you choose the historical path, you get a bunch of cool focus' that mirrors the way the Soviets were in real life in a sort! Focus' for the politburo, the agitprop, things like assasinating Trotsky, improving the collectivisation process, the five year plan, the red army, the purges! all good stuff, cool cool cool.

on a surface level. it isn't until you're deep into several soviet games that you realise how terrible alot of these mechanics are. Purge events are random, so you could be playing trying to be historical, then all of a sudden the game tries to purge Georgy Zhukov, or Konstantin Rokosovossky- several times in some games! You have to spend pp you cant afford to spend and increase paranoia yet again just to prevent these unhistorical things from happening in your historic game. At the very least if the game wanted to be more accurate, sure, you can let the game purge characters like Konstantin (who was targeted by the NKVD irl and tortured) but then have a decision to restore them to command! (just like irl again, with Konstantin) instead of permanently losing your only tank genius until you get a tank commanding general to level 8.

The purge can target political advisors who are already supposed to be purged in one of the great purges from your focus', what do you expect would happen when you reach that focus? You might assume "oh so instead of purging 4 people like it normally does, it will only purge 3 since one of them was purged earlier!" nope, you lose one of your other advisors at random. Permanently locking you out of important advisors like the Captain of Industry, who is invaluable to Centre-path Soviet Union because the other advisors (which there are too few of) are horrible and do so little- like Nikita. +5% PP gain per day? instead of the 15% other advisors for other countries give? WHY?!?!? WHY DOES NIKITA ONLY GET +5% TO TRADEOFF HIS OTHER USELESS BONUS' OF +30 MAXIMUM COMMAND POWER (useless) or +0.10 COMMUNIST POWER (completely useless, the soviet union is already impervious to ideological drift thanks to its national spirits)

Enough about the purges, lets talk about the 5 year plan!
Wait, whats that? the 5 year plan just got disrupted because you got into a major war? oh, well... ok... that makes sense, it happened IRL so its kinda neat the game reflects that by perma-locking those focus- wait a sec, its 1938 and you are playing ahistorically and so got into an ahistorical early war with another major, maybe Japan for instance? too bad! You never even get a chance to do some of those five year focus' because of something outside of your control. If you play a ahistorical game where anything can happen- for instant Japan declaring war on Siankang which is allied to you thus putting you 2 at war, why does the 5 year plan get locked when the fighting between the Soviets and Japan is gonna take place in the East- FAR, FAR away from where the 5 year plan is being implemented in the industrial heartlands of the Soviet Union?

You see- once you start thinking about this stuff, the entire DLC and the way the countries of this game were designed falls apart. For some reason, for your army focus' restoring the cossacks is a mutual choice from getting permanent +5% extra org for any other kind of unit in the game. Why the would you ever want to restore the cossacks then (who hilariously dont even get an org bonus, just a measley +5% attack and +5% defense bonus) and the answer is simple: its a choice the AI makes that makes it much weaker then a player (AI always restores cossacks and so always loses out on a free 5+% org for any kind of unit they can make
