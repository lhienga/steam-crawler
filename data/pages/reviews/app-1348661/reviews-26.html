\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo Step Back almost feels like HOI4 - The Remake. It totally changes the way supply works which, in turn, changes how literally every-single strategy for the game works.

Primarily, the HOI4 meta was whittling down your enemy until they are low-supply and then rushing around and encircling them. You could get away with this rapidly if you have a high-supply region and push in quickly. Not anymore. Like doing that Greece strategy to cheese Turkey? Much-more difficult now. It can be really frustrating.

However, if you look at it on the flipside, this opens WORLDS of depth on supply alone. Now your strategies are to get supply hubs and starve out the enemy. The game now rewards long-term tactics over savvy micro. It's rather exciting.

Oh, and they totally reworked the way generals work, made it so you aren't wasting research slots on doctrines by creating command structure powers, and added incredible bonuses to tanks with the new designer system. Just small things, really.

Highly recommend if you want to breathe new life into this game. Nearly every HOI4 Youtuber has their take on the new meta, but we're really all still learning this together. I'm very excited to see where this goes next!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably one of the best, if not the best, HOI4 DLC out there. From the focus trees, the new supply system, designing your own tanks to the scorched ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ earth tactics this DLC has it all.

+Scorched Earth tactics are incredibly fun to use. You can blow up your own railway and supply systems to make sure the enemy that is capturing that land won't be able to use your own systems against you.
+The new supply system with supply hubs, trains, armored trains, war-time cheaper trains and even railway guns this new supply system is super cool and the fact that we have railroads means there is an actual supply system in the game WITH railway cannons. Just come on. They might be incredibly ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ expensive to build but still.
+The new focus trees are really fun.
+Designing your own tanks is really fun.
+Supplies are actually important now. As Latvia i conquered both Lithuania and Estonia the problem is i forgot about Memel. Germany demended that i give Memel back to them. I refused. They declared war on me and even with my forts i could not hold the border i had to repeatedly fall back. As i was falling back i kept blowing up my own infastructure and railroads to make sure the Germans couldn't supply themselves. At first it didn't help but after the Germans advanced far enough then they started to really lose supplies to the point where i could almost beat them back. Of course the entire plan fell apart when the German AI decided to naval invade me from behind. Stuck between a rock and a hard place my country collapsed hours of progress gone within a few seconds. Still it was one of the most fun wars i ever had in HOI4.
+Maybe this is just me i don't necessarily know if the AI went through any improvements or changes but the AI certainly felt a lot better at the game. With naval invasions especially. The AI always did small naval invasions in some areas and generally they were easily pushed back. And they'd try again and again and again and again and they'd be pushed back all the time without even setting foot on the land they were trying to invade. They'd just lose man all the time. Looks like thats changed now the AI felt better at certain things like again naval invasions. Of course the AI isn't great all of a sudden i've still seen them do stupid ♥♥♥♥ but i feel like they are not as bad as they used to be.

-The price is my only problem with this DLC. Don't get me wrong i'm glad i have the DLC and this is one of those few things that i bought for full price without a discount and i'm really happy with it. But still 32 Turkish lira is a lot. Is the DLC bad ? No. If you can afford it should you get it ? Hell yeah. But still even with all that waiting for a discount might be the best choice.

This DLC is really fun i enjoyed it and i'd recommend it. If you can afford the full price then go for it otherwise wait for a discount. I hope Paradox does a similar thing with aircraft as well. We can design our own ships, our own tanks but we can't design our own planes. I'd love to have a plane designer too. I believe Paradox said they wouldn't do major DLCs anymore but firstly i don't believe them and secondly a plane designer DLC with improvements to planes in general would be incredibly good. I don't think they'll miss that chance. We all know how much Paradox likes money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I enjoy tinkering with tanks in this dlc like I do ships from man the guns, there's a inherit problem that really just castrates my ability to enjoy the game. The game suddenly goes into really, really slow lag right when you hit about 1942, I'm talking going from times 5 speed to literally times 1 speed. You can be at times 5, it'll run at times 1 and ignore what it's supposed to be.

This was with World War II in full gear, just with some factional differences. Europe was split into war, Japan and China, Soviets against the Allies etc. With the world at war, the game just can't handle it anymore with all the mechanics paradox added.

So for me, this means I have to knock out some major powers before 1941 if I wanna try and minimize or even stop that lag from happening. I can't just, roleplay and sit through world war II without the game literally coming to a crawl. I have high grade specs for a CPU and Graphics card along with RAM, so I know it's not that causing this. The game wasn't this laggy in previous dlcs. I dunno what Paradox was thinking releasing this sorta mechanic when it causes this sort of slowdown for so many people but if they don't fix it it's gonna ruin the game for a lotta people especially in multiplayer. So I can't really recommend this dlc until these problems are addressed.

Also as a last thing that bothers me, I dunno why the devs gave Stalin all those buffs like "Father of nations" etc, but if you ask me it's pretty unfair how Stalin gets all these big juicy leader buffs and yet other major world leaders of the time can't even compare with their own buffs to their countries. Unless the devs are Comintern simps, they should seriously consider revisiting things they did if they don't wanna leave out Axis and Allies fans.\t\t\t\t\t
