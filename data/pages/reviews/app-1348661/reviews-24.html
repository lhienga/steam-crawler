Make Artillery Great Again

Love that the new tank designer necessitates artillery research - whereas before I would just research artillery/AA/AT based on if I was going to use them for any divisions, now to get better turrets for your tanks, you need to research these technologies. And of course railway guns. Always railway guns.

Glad that doctrines for land/naval/air are now bought with experience, rather than bogging down your research progress by perpetually researching doctrines - as we all know theories don't always pan out to what experience tells us!

To support your offensives, you also now have the ability to build airports, infrastructure and railways in allied territory! Have not had the chance to try out the floating harbors for naval invasions yet; will need to rectify that deficiency.

The main downside to this expansion is the balance of industrial capacity (IC) and manufacturing. I feel your production is really sluggish due to these new changes and need a buff to restore balance (either by increasing IC or decreasing costs of some items).

You now need to provide trains and trucks for logistics (on top of trucks for all your divisions), so this takes up a decent part of your military production capacity, as well as for fighters to protect all these logistics as you need to protect all these territories.

Also, you now have both infrastructure and railways (fortunately railways are cheaper than airport levels) for buildings to improve and repair, on top of the 20,000-point supply hub (if you need to add any beyond the native ones), which is equivalent to almost 2 civilian factories or 1.5 refineries.


Overall I would recommend this based on the new opportunities it offers (fully customized vehicles) over the negatives, which I feel could easily be improved/fixed by a balance patch (or mod). Also, I'm not off-put by more details/management of details like the logistics; the additional detail to track may be off-putting to some as there's more micromanagement involved.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSoviet Union Focus Tree
I played through as the U.S.S.R. until 1944 with this DLC following the Stalin path, it was very fun. The USSR easily becomes the best faction in terms of depth with this DLC. You have national modifiers that make it feel quite unique, and Operation Barbarossa is now very heavily weighted to be a game of buying time and reforming your army. It took until the Allies were able to draw off the Axis forces for me to pursue army reforms, upon which the game gave me a new subsystem of preparing offensives to capture specific regions; success gave me lots of war support which encouraged using Force Attack, Staff Office Plan, and Last Stand to make sure I got the job done in the one month window it gave.

Tank Designer
The tank designer is pretty cool.

Railway Guns
I tried using them and they barely made a slight difference; it's the kind of thing that is there to give you more to play with, not unlike the new supply system.\t\t\t\t\t
