\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA mixed bag but ultimately a disappointing DLC released in a very buggy state with major balancing issues. 1.11 is a very ambitious update that brings a lot of bug fixes and improvements to the game most notably the new supply system and makes significant changes to combat mechanics which breath new life into the rather stale state land warfare had been in for quite some time now. However, as of version 1.11.2 (I've also tested the 1.11.4 beta) this expansion is crippled by many rather obvious bugs and some egregious mistakes in balancing some of which are unfortunately game breaking and extend to this DLC. I'd give the DLC a 5/10 given the bugs & balance issues but mostly because of the unfortunate state that tanks are now in.

The new focus trees are excessively large in my opinion, continuing the trend since Man the Guns, I much prefer the earlier focus trees which had fewer but more impactful focuses. Some focuses are bugged as of right now but this is being addressed. Perhaps if they had stuck to a more manageable amount they could avoid such issues...
I'm most disappointed by tanks and their designer which are a major part of this update and the DLC. Not only have they been heavily nerfed: with IC & resource costs increased significantly while their combat stats have been heavily reduced which has made offensive infantry or motorized divisions much more competitive but has also made CAS a more viable option to win on the ground. The tank designer feels clunky and unbalanced at best, with medium tanks made obsolete by cheap heavies which are far too cost effective. Some modules are so unbalanced that they allow you to create unbeatable divisions when defending, by stacking entrenchment from the dozer blade module for instance. The designer also requires you to pick the 3D model and icon for your tank or else your heavy assault gun might look like an AA tank for example. The game should be able to match those appropriately by itself. This issue, while only cosmetic, is quite frustrating for people who also paid for the armor packs that aren't even fully compatible yet. To make maters worse, tank conversion doesn't work at all with the DLC.
The logistics strike mission is far too effective and cannot be reasonably countered. Transport planes can be used to effectively supply an entire army group cut off from your supply network and are a much cheaper investment then infrastructure (addressed in beta). With how powerful CAS has become and the nerf to AA, winning the air war is paramount. So design all the fancy armor vehicles you want, at the end of the day the real war is won in the skies.
Army experience gain has been nerfed but has never been as important; even with great army advisors (which are now a source of it) you are unlikely to ever have enough so making specialized templates, variants or tank designs is not much of an option unless you neglect your doctrine & spirits. As usual new technologies and experience (mainly army) requirements are poorly integrated with nations outside of the ones reworked in this DLC.

It is quite frankly absurd that some of these bugs and oversights made it into release, especially with the extended development time. Many of the balancing decisions are questionable given the focus of expansion and don't seem to accomplish what the devs were aiming for.\t\t\t\t\t
