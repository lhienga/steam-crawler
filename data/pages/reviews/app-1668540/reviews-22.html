\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou probably already know whether you want to play this, but as someone who was really disappointed with Trails of Cold Steel 4 I'll note that I enjoyed Reverie more than any of the Cold Steel games. You should definitely play Zero and Azure first if you haven't already though, not just because they're great games but because Reverie very much expects you to have played them - the Crossbell characters and setting are far more prominent than they were in Cold Steel 3 & 4 and parts of the story are a direct follow-up to Azure.

Sadly it has to be said that there are a _lot_ of gameplay-related translation errors, things like ability/item descriptions and mission descriptions (e.g. Laura's self-buff craft which grants completely different buffs than the description says it does). Most of the localization is pretty well done but parts of it were obviously rushed (or just not edited) - the NPC dialogue in the final chapter has typos and grammatical errors galore, plus several instances of characters referring to a location using a different name translation than what the main story dialogue uses. There's also one lategame instance of the "Helmord" soldat being mistranslated as "Hell Mode", which is funny but obviously a mistake.

The translation errors weren't a dealbreaker for me but they did occasionally take me out of the experience; I really hope they clean it up before moving on and then do better with the next game in the series, because Cold Steel 3 & 4 didn't have this many obvious mistakes (at least after CS4's translation got patched).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best way to describe this game is Trails in the Sky: the 3rd done better. This game is brimming with content from the 3 concurrent main stories to the dungeon crawling Reverie Corridor, along with every mini-game and most mechanics from the rest of the series. It's a pleasant family reunion of characters and places from this nearly 20 year old series and I couldn't be happier with it. The only real critique I can muster is that with so much content there is bound to be some low points and occasionally I did notice some plot lines being retreads of the past games, which is a shame because the newest cast and their story is far and away the crown jewel of this game so far. Overall a solid addition to the franchise and a must play for anyone that loves the series!

Edit: Finished the Series at almost 100 hours, game is one of the most content-rich Trails games to date. My feelings are roughly the same as they were when I originally made the review, with the addition that some side characters could have used more love and attention.\t\t\t\t\t
