\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA wonderful conclusion to a series of games of which you must play a solid majority of to enjoy the payoff. That said, these games don't have the beloved reputation among fans that they do for no reason. There are so many satisfying conclusions, and the way that this is handled as both a bridge and a coda is pretty masterful. As someone who loves the typical slow build of the series, I was cautiously optimistic about the shorter main story (stories, really) - but it has not been a concern at any point in my first playthrough and I'm almost at the final chapter (and then a lot more of the extra stuff).

For a JRPG fan with a commitment to the Trails/Kiseki universe, this game will check boxes you didn't know you had. But that commitment is clutch.

Still, for me, this is as near a perfect experience with a game as I've ever had.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had mixed feelings about this series since the Crossbell Arc, and those feelings have trended increasingly negative as the series drags on as Falcom has injected ♥♥♥♥♥♥-knockoff-of-a-bad-disney-movie writing into the series with increasing levels of aggresion. Along with other mechanisms such as the neckbeard harem system which I have torn apart in previous reviews. Some of these extreme flaws have been mitigated - the neckbeard system for example has been relegated to optional side content so it doesn't cripple the dynamics between any character not named Rean Schwarzer. Our MC is still an MC by all accounts, but he's not quite the black hole of a character that just sucks any and all relevance out of everyone and everything else like he did in Cold Steel. Other people and things are allowed to somewhat matter for the first time in....Jesus, I can't even remember how long. As a caveat, this may or may not actually extend to your favorite characters, as the cast is extremely bloated and there is simply not enough time in one game for the writers to share the TLC with everyone. Whoever your favorite characters are unless you're a Kevin fan, they're here, but it's overwhelmingly likely that they've been demoted to extra because of the sheer number of characters by this point.

That said, the character writing is still, all in all, a noticeable improvement over CS4. However, the ♥♥♥♥♥♥-knockoff-of-bad-Disney-writing continues to rear it's ugly head. I get that redemption is a major theme of this series, but we've gotten so far over the line of insulting your intelligence with it at this point that it has me questioning whether or not the series is worth continuing. Going back to the Liberl Arc, you had characters like Renne and Joshua whom, while it might take some suspension of disbelief to buy their rehabilitation/redemption, were still kids when their rehabilitation began and the latter took something almost akin to divine intervention to keep him away from the deep end as he would've killed Estelle if not for Kevin's help. In this game, redemption happens because the cast mouth sharts suicidal levels of battered-wife syndrome all over Rufus until he's magically healed. Falcom should be legally required to add a disclaimer above the aforementioned character's head that acting this way toward anyone who is even a whole digit percent as narcissistic/sociopathic as Rufus in real life is going to get you raped and murdered because that's how that ♥♥♥♥ goes in realityland with someone who is anywhere in the same multiverse of being this far gone. Falcom is still as allergic to death and consequences as ever and it just makes everyone and everything come off as pathetic. How the hell did we get to this point from the days of Kevin being forced to kill a child, then being forced to relive that experience in Phantasma, his mom committing suicide, Loewe actually dying for realzies with no takebacks, Star Door 15, etc?. The series today feels like kindergarten by comparison, the writing in the last several games in a row has just been insufferably childish.

All in all, I would say that just enough of the Cold Steel Arc's extreme flaws have been mitigated just enough for me to give this game a thumbs up. Steam badly needs a mixed reviews option because that's how I've felt about most of the last 7 or so games of the series, but I'd say this one is slightly tilted upwards if I have to be black and white thanks to some long overdue improvements over the Cold Steel Arc.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Good game overall, but more of the same made it feel like an expensive DLC for Cold Steel IV rather than its own game. But the story and dialogue is fairly massive from a text perspective so that probably pushed it into being its own thing. Has some translation/in-game-explanation issues. Certain parts of the game felt underdeveloped or over-expansive to me.

Caveat: this game is 10th in an overarching series and an epilogue to two sub-series, so not the best place to start but ... you do you. I only played the Cold Steel sub-series and used the internet to figure out the relevant info from the other two sub-series. But there's some minor rewards for having save files from the 2 Crossbell arc games as well.

I enjoyed the game but not as much as prior entries. In many ways, I feel this game feels like a full game priced major DLC for Cold Steel IV. It has a enjoyable story that benefits from being able to bounce around between three concurring protagonists. Good stuff! The game's events happen over the span of about a week in game, so I needed to suspend my disbelief that the protagonists solve everything so efficiently, but the characters are largely elite to begin with, so it's not completely preposterous. And with a lot of personal bias, I was more interested in Rean's route, and the newer playable characters in "C"'s route, over Lloyd's route. The SSS and Crossbell characters aren't my faves and the weave some hefty chunks of Class VII into their route but I'm just not as engaged when playing that route.

The combat style is polished and satisfying but at this point you've probably been casting the same arts and crafts for a few games now and I ended up fast forwarding through most combats. There's a reverie dungeon that, before concluding the main story, is somewhat interesting but somewhat repetitive by the time you reach the third strata.

Overall, I enjoyed the game there's a few spots that detracted from walking away from a good feeling upon completion.

1. When the player chooses to make the attack into the final dungeon of the Finale chapter, there's a significant amount of going-ons involving the supporting cast. This going-ons are somewhat lengthy and rendered with the game's normal graphics. It really should have been an animated cut scene. It definitely feels like Nihon Falcom was aiming for epic but it kind of falls flat. It just doesn't feel like it captures the scale of what they are trying to show you, at all. For me, this dropped my vibe and made my interest dip somewhat.

2. There's a ton of typos. I most noticed it in the Combat Orders. Good luck finding an order that does what it's text says it will do; it'll do something like what the order says it does but not what the order actually says. This is usually just percentages being wrong but there were some other ones. Also If the game properly explained how to set tagalongs once they were available I missed it. I figured it out but it was a couple of hours after they became available.

3. The reverie corridor! A place for all your people to come together and bond. It has a bunch of time-sinking content that opens after the main story ends. I like some of it, but it kind of drags and IDK if I'd bother doing it at all a second time. There's a bit of foreshadowing that happens in here, but I bet there's a social media content creator that has/will make a video showing/telling the foreshadowing you need to know going into the next game.

?. It's not a criticism of this game, but it takes too damn long for the games in the Trails series to get an official English translation.

But I did have fun with it, mostly.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTrails into Reverie is a decent step up from Cold Steel 4 in my opinion. CS4 had a couple plot points that kinda turned me off a bit, but I'm a huge fan of the trails series and super invested at this point so I continued on and enjoyed it quite a bit. Reverie had a much more engaging plot without it needing to be "massively epic" just for the sake of being so. Having all the characters that we have came to know since sky 1 in the game and playing a part was awesome.

Upsides:
- Reverie Corridor was ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ great. Loved it... so many cool minigames, dungeons, and characters to play. Great break up from the story too
- Playable characters... all awesome
- Good story
- Zone designs and art were awesome
- Rixia!!!
- Characters feel powerful from the start
- Multiple routes required and stories merge into one. Super cool
- New character additions were solid!

Downsides:
- Still a bit cheesy with the "I wont hold back" and "we can do anything if we are together" speeches. Man its tough to get through some of those after the 4 CS games.
- Plot Armor (mostly still kinda feeling this from CS4)
- Like some of the other CS stories, lacking in well thought out narratives such as Renne's back story in Sky 3. Some are decent, but I havent really felt anything like Joshua and Renne's stories since cold steel one.
- Definite opinion: millium's VA was way better in CS1... wish they kept her lol she just sounds super animated now.

Anyways, super solid, definitely recommend. Can't wait for the next entry and so glad I played all these in order. Trails is probably the best consecutive story in my gaming history.
