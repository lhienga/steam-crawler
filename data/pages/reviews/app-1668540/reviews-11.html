\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTrails Into Reverie truly is the neat little bow to wrap up both the Crossbell and Cold Steel sagas into one majestic and emotional conclusion. But it is one to be opened once all the previous trails have been traversed, something that shouldn't be a problem for most veteran players that have undertaken this odyssey.

THE GOOD:

The gameplay has been perfected, with over 40 playable characters and a nearly limitless amount of possible broken builds to overcome the barriers in your way. The music is still a banger like always, with geniuses like Mitsuo Singa and Yukihiro Jindo on the team. And the story, which spans across 3 routes, is masterfully executed. Trails into Reverie truly is the greatest entry thus far when taking everything I've said into account.

THE BAD:
Unfortunately, I can't end this review without mentioning the several technical screw-ups which fall into NIS America. From the LE preorders having all those delivery issues, the numerous bugs at release (which have been patched for the most part), and the many many typos, item and/or craft description errors, and downright wrong names in certain dialogues (which haven't been patched as of the time I'm writing this review, and I doubt they will.) The Quality Assurance truly left more to be desired, and I hope they can reflect and improve for the Kuro release so this doesn't happen again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, and a good end to the Saga. While not my favorite in the series, it is easily top 3.

After beating Cold Steel 4 I really did feel there was not much else to do with these characters. And while most of the story revolves around the problems in Crossbell, the story keeps it fresh by having the mysteries of why things happened being the more important part of the story. Eventually Crossbell just became another problem to solve, while the mysteries of what were happening is what pushed me forward to finish the story.

The gameplay is largely unchanged from Cold Steel 4, with only United Front and tagalong being good quality of life additions. United Front gives you a field wide heal or attack while putting the assault gauge to good use. Tagalong gives you better options with Brave Orders by letting you use the Order of certain characters who are not on the team. Good additions to the game overall.

The game has introduces a 3 route system and it is completely fine. Don't worry about when you need to change routes. The game will just tell you when to change routes, but you can change routes whenever you want for the most part until the game forces you to change.

Reverie also has an absolute monster of a playable roster. While this makes equipment management a complete hassle early on, it is amazing to have certain characters just show up and join the team that you never had a chance to really use extensively before.

The Reverie Corridor introduces a randomized dungeon that you can just have fun with and really put the huge cast to good use by making a dream team to tackle it. Its also a great way to power up everyone in the game, and I really felt overpowered after each trip. I do feel like the game has a balance problem, with the limited gear early on and huge boss hp pools, making the early game a slog. However, once you get through the Corridor dungeon a few times the game becomes a breeze.

The game thankfully removes side quests and bonding events. This was the first time when I wasn't really worried about missing much and could just leisurely play the game without worrying about missing a hidden quest. Romance is out the window here for the mot part. You can thankfully pick anyone you want for Rean's romance at the start of his route, but the romance was only brought up twice in the main story. The game is really laser focused on presenting the story.

The game also tones down the overuse of nicknames and titles, and the dialogue is generally better here. There are still some cringy moments, and people gasping and the general use of ellipsis, but its much less and no one is constantly interrupted by people saying their name after every other sentence. The characters and story is given time to breath here. Unfortunately, the story is really focused on Rean, Lloyd, and Cs group. Most of the side characters barely have any dialogue after showing up, and some characters just come and go without contributing much. Don't expect much from the cast. However, I was really happy how they managed to give Rean an appropriate end. It started with me not sure if I wanted another game with him after Cold Steel 4, to ending with closure and a perfect end for him.

One thing I really wish you could do is revisit areas from different chapters. A lot of the early game for Rean is areas where he never returns to. Didn't get everything from his first chapter? well, your gonna have to comeback in new game+ because he never returns. Also, the game still has a few noticeable bugs. The amount of times a daydream or minigame just crashed due to someone having a costume on is wild, the worst being never able to finish the magical girl mini game until no one has an costumes equipped. Even the ending crashed because someone had a costume, finished it when I just removed them all.

I also have to mention the final dungeon. By far the most bare bones final dungeon of any in the series, with you able to run to the end of the dungeon with only a easy required fight before the final boss. Largely a forgettable dungeon. However, this is by far the funnest final boss in the series, and also the absolute longest. That fight is really, really long. But it is also obvious they tried really hard to make it fun and varied.

Finally, I really like the use of Daydreams, which are stories mainly for specific characters to shine. I do dislike that when they involve combat its with a fixed team, with fixed gear, bu they weren't to hard. It is lame when you only have the basic spells to sling at boss fights with huge hp though. But I do like that some are just anime episodes to just sit and enjoy.

Overall a great game, and my favorite just to play in the series. A few things really keep it from being my top of the series, but it is a game that I really enjoyed, and a story worth telling.\t\t\t\t\t
