\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a celebration/send-off to this era of Trails, it does a great job. As a stand alone story? Not so much, but I never expected much to be coherent given this is mostly an asset flip jrpg. The appeal imo is just getting to play as most of your favorite Trails characters across 3 different arcs, with a few welcome additions. It's more like Trails in the Sky the 3rd than anything else, but since I knew what I was getting into, it was good. Grinding and equipment management is more of a pain than ever given the bloated cast though, but you can mitigate it somewhat by only focusing on your favorites. This game is pretty easy though, since it still falls into the same pitfall Cold Steel 3 and 4 had of "if you break an enemy they never get another turn" which can get super tiring especially coupled with equipment grinding. The pre rendered cutscenes are a highlight as well, even if there are only a few.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is basically the Crossbell and Cold Steel arc's swan song and feels properly fanservicey and still provides a fun time.

Gameplay wise, it's identical to modern day Cold Steel with an exception being United Fronts, which can break the game even more. Basically they are like even MORE powerful burst attacks that can be launched using the assault gauge mid fight, and can generate free brave points and various other bonuses depending on what you need at that moment. Some caveats is that you need more than 4 people in the party to use them, and you need to build assault gauge during the fight or go into the fight with a full gauge to make use of it. I think this feature basically makes the party immortal, but I appreciate it because it makes early game less of a slog because it can be hard to get consistent unbalances to accumulate brave points for proper orders or bond attacks. Another feature of this game is the True Reverie Corridor, full of fun side activities, goodies, and a place to grind characters up. In my opinion this is the best dungeon in the series not quality wise, but rather the immense roster you are afforded in this dungeon. You can basically access it at any time, and gear up anyone you want and run them together to unlock bonus scenes, upgrades for your team, or fun minigames. Imagine Persona 5's momentos but infinitely better and condensed. Plus, when the credits roll there's still lot's of stuff to do in the True Reverie Corridor that will keep people who love the dungeon busy.

Story wise it feels a bit like 'been there done that' at first. Crossbell has been occupied like... three times now? I get struggling for independence has been a major plotpoint in Crossbell for the last like 8 years of trails games so it's refreshing they finally get a true shot at it now, but I can't help but feel slightly annoyed. However the story builds off of that plotpoint and the three paths system provides an interesting interweaving storyline, which was neat but they force you to switch so often it should've been one path. Once you get past the first major conflict, I grew to like the story more and the fanservice felt appropriate and it felt like the developers basically took it down a notch in a few areas to tell a more character focused finale than CS3 and CS4 were with MASSIVE bloat. It doesn't reach the highs of Azure, but it's a good story all the less.

If I had to make a final score, probably an 8/10. Good end point to the first half of the Trails saga, and a good setup for the adventures to come. The sendoffs for fan favorite characters was good, the gameplay was fun, and the fanservice was cool. I'd recommend this as a purchase to any trails fan caught up with the series at this point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Trails series is incredible. Having played since the start of Trails in the Sky, I've sunk many hours into this series already, so I'm here until the end truly captivated by the world that it's constantly expanding upon.

This game doesn't really miss, but what I was very happy about, was how much I was able to play as the older characters (e.g. the Trails in the Sky gang), which we've only gotten a taste of since Trails in the Sky the 3rd.

New players will have a hard time jumping into this game without knowledge of prior games, and while ordinarily I'd suggest starting at Trails in the Sky (since it is my favourite arc by far), it is a bit dated now & much easier to start with Trails of Cold Steel / Trails from Zero[+ to Azure] (which both play out at roughly the same time in the timeline). Even Trails from Zero is fairly outdated at this point, but there are a lot of important links back to the Crossbell arc.

It is very much a classic turn-based JRPG.
There is a LOT of worldbuilding; the Trails series has an insane amount of text-based dialogue in these games (most of the main story in this game is voice acted though!), which continually builds upon and references its past games.

Graphically it's getting a little better over time. It doesn't really feel like a modern game per se, but the visuals are there. It has decent enough models and such, but exploring the world usually doesn't give you any real "wow" moments. Its story is definitely more of the Trails forte.

I love these games. The only gripe I have is that it takes them so long for the english release to come out, so I've started to really consider learning JP to play the original game. They do such a great job with cleaning up & improving settings and such in the english version though.

Favourite character has always been Estelle, so the fact that I can play as her again is incredible (even though one of her skill ranges seemed bugged for some reason, but oh well, it didn't really affect much).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the 10th game in the series and you're retarded if you played it without at least experiencing the Erebonia arc.
No matter what I tell you, it is within you to play this game if you've come this far in the series.
The combat is without question the best the series has to offer thus far. Funny remark, considering the game has the worst writing of all the others.
There's 100 characters or something, and everyone interacts with each other on screen.
You play 3 routes: Lloyd's, Rean's, C's.
The 3 groups will eventually come together.
When characters interact with one another, initially, they always greet each other and make vague remarks. 25% of the entire dialogue in the game is about various characters greeting each other and making absolutely no contribution to the ongoing story or even past events, or have anything worthwhile to say. Is politeness for the sake of being polite. Is like women complimenting each other for the clothes/accessories they're wearing. Is all fake, all made of plastic just to give characters lines. Is the 10th time you can bear to hear how Sharon's tea is the best. If you've played all the games until now. you should know that she is a culinary genius. Sorry for picking on you, Sharon, you were always my 1st Trails waifu, but I need to convey a message.
That's the kinda deadbeat dialogue you can expect from this game. The main story is better written, but is still bad as there are no risks being taken. They could've made it really special at the end if they killed someone off.
The whole reason I played these games is because the way they're written and how they treat mature themes. With the advent of the Erebonia saga (when the series went mainstream), the writing took a massive, massive punch in the nuts and is just watered down to put children to sleep now. Civil wars where 1 guy dies, a continental war where no one dies, otherworldly events defying logic, time and space, and nothing grim happens in the aftermath. Even those who they killed off, somehow the writers found a way to bring them back with some fartetched explanation.
I miss the days where death meant death. This ain't no Dragon Ball. Are these writers trying to find job opportunities at Disney or what?
Now go play your game now and enjoy it for what it is.\t\t\t\t\t
