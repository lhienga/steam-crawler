\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith Reverie ends the first half of the whole Trails series and in my opinion, it did a great job closing a lot of character arcs. When I started Reverie I was already kind of expecting a lot from the new cast of characters since I had heard a lot of good things about them. Well, turns out that C's route was for me personally the best route from all the 3. What Reverie really shines at is the Reverie Corridor which is kind of like a randomly generated dungeon where you can farm, fight bosses recruit characters, watch episodes that are certain events from characters in their past, and play mini-games. I don’t have that much to say about the story to avoid spoilers but I will say that it does get somewhat ridiculous in its plot twists (not in a bad way) though it may throw some people off. Another problem that doesn't only effect Reverie but the entire series (up until this point because of a certain other game that fixes this issue) is, that in Trails, Falcom very rarely goes through with character deaths. By that, I mean letting one of the heroes or basically important characters (that are not villains) be killed or stay dead. This problem already reached its peak in CS4 and somewhat finds its footing here in Reverie as well. I personally find it to be an annoying thing that they aren't willing to let characters ACTUALLY die but it’s not a huge deal breaker for me. The combat is basically the same as in CS4 but better and with a lot more characters. The soundtrack overall was ight. Had some absolute bangers but also misses a lot with it's battle and dungeon themes IMO.
I'm giving it a 9.5/10. Was planning to 100% this game but good god who thought it was a good idea to make an achievement where you need to win 1500 battles in total???? Because I will NOT do that

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tone of the best parts of the Trails/ Legends of Heroes series is that it takes its time to tell the story, delving into the motivations of many characters - and not just heroes. It also probes deeper questions, inclding what it means to live and what it means to be human as well as the nature of time and possibility. This jrpg tops any other jrpg out there in terms of storytelling, gameplay features, art, heart and execution. The final part is reminiscent of Trails 3rd, but gives a lot more freedom in going into the reverie. I loved Phantasma and I think Reverie delivers and ties many emotional loose ends. At the same time, it continues stories that will still take their time to grow in Kuro, pretty sure. like Renne. The storytelling is very moving and satisfying, with a positive and inspiring message that is very important to me.

Thank you for the story, Falcom, writers, Nis, Xseed, translators... for Olivert, Schera, Lloyd, Rean, Estelle, Joshua, Randy, Mireille, Kevin, Ries, Jusis, and so many other npcs whose fates are tied, who have formed so many strong bonds and relationships. I love pretty much all of them.

and ...the unexpected hidden playable character in Reverie. I did not expect that. Gives amazing depth to the character. Well-done!

Also note: post-game make sure to try talking to characters in the reverie corridor as different characters -you can control who walks around the garden then, and some conversations are worthwhile. Try Jusis and the secret character, for instance. I love Oliver and Schera one. THe side stories in the reverie are also fantastic. It's trulyl a game worth EVERY penny.

This is the best jrpg ever and that's it!

Play it all, from the start/ It's a journey worthwhile unlike many otherx out there, playing Reverie only made me want to go back and replay the entire series so far, to again experience how they all got to this point, how thye grew.... NIS is amazing and I hope Kuro won't disappoint.\t\t\t\t\t
