\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE July 14 2023

I got to a Mishy boss and the game completely froze - and that whole segment was extremely tedious. I refuse to do it again - Rain Code has my attention now anyway.

I can't recommend the game now that I've had a chance to really dig in. It's not that it's bad. It's that it's clear they wanted to make a mobile game but figured they would put it on console. The story isn't that good, there's very little town exploration and the characters are slapped into teams with no real rhyme or reason. Some of the minigames also are unskippable; normally this isn't an issue, but they're just horrible VR experiences rather than something that's actually interesting to do.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Figured out the graphics issue - Borderless is essentially broken/useless. Requires Fullscreen to set the proper refresh rate for the monitor, then rates are between 80-100 on the highest settings. Still feels unoptimized, but I can live with this.

- Music is pretty good, though nothing really beats Cold Steel III (so far).

- It seems like they at least tried to have more voice acting (Cold Steel III and Cold Steel IV were pathetic in this regard). Again, not quite far yet to say for sure; Cold Steel III in particular started strong and then fell off for the second half.

- I don't know what it is but Lloyd's segment so far is extremely dull. It's strange; it's like they purposely copied the feel from Azure and Zero, but that doesn't translate well at all to 3D. It's hard to explain. Like if you look at Cold Steel 1, the first part is meeting everyone; so you expect it to be a bit slower because these are people you haven't met, but with Reverie, it's dog slow yet isn't trying to introduce you really to anyone; it assumes you know who they are, which means it feels slow. It's really hard to describe.

- Are these controls different than IV? I'm having a difficult time with the buttons and I don't know why. It took me forever to figure out how to get speedup going; and I'm thinking I have to customize this back to what I'm used to maybe.


If you can get this on a STEEP discount - I'd say no more than $30 - sure, it's worth that. Not full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA wonderful capstone to the Erobonia and Crossbell arcs of the Trails series. Reverie tells a captivating story combined with refined combat, polished visuals and a stellar soundtrack.

The localisation could be better, as there are still some typos and incorrect descriptions that have yet to be patched, however the English voice performance continues to be an impressive standout among dubbed JRPGs.

There is a wealth of content to explore, both concluding the loose ends of previous entries and foreshadowing for the upcoming Trails into Daybreak. While not a suitable entry point into the series, Reverie is a no-brainer for anyone who has played through Zero/Azure and the Cold Steel games.\t\t\t\t\t
