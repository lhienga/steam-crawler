\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don’t know why I took so long to write something about this game, maybe I just wanted to let it fully sink in, but I think it is finally time to buckle up and get the job done.

No spoilers as always haha.


Trails into Reverie is a game with a lot to live up to, it's a game that was revered as something special in this series since the time of its original japanese release, and did it manage to? In my opinion, yes, above and beyond. This game was a return to form for the series in a lot of ways and really rekindled my love and enthusiasm for the series.

Tying up a lot of loose ends from both Crossbell and Cold Steel (much like how the 3rd was for Sky) while serving as a bridge to the Calvard arc, Reverie stands strong as the 10th entry in the series.

The route system is something I was really interested in and it delivered, it’s a great way to handle a game with such a massive cast while giving the characters natural reasons and motives for being where they are, also allowing for some of the best and most engaging pace I’ve seen in the series.

While the core gameplay is very much the same as Cold Steel, what makes this game shine is the amount of options and characters you have to mess around, the Corridor is a playground of endless possibilities where you can spend hours upon hours just trying builds, reading side stories or playing minigames.

On a technical side, besides some bugs from release that are now fixed, Durante’s port is great as always.

This game was a really special experience, it’s everything I could have wanted from the final chapter of the first half of the series and a send off to both Crossbell and Erebonia, it was a labor of love and it shows in every aspect. The Trails series have been with me thought some of the best and worst times of my life now, it has been almost 3 years since I played FC for the first time, the ride had some of the biggest highs I’ve ever experienced in my life, but also quite a few lows, but even so I can confidently say that this is my favorite series in anything, and I will forever treasure the wonderful experience I had with this series.

Personal feelings aside, the bottom line is that this is a great game haha, one that is definitely worth your time.

Thank you Falcom for creating this series, thank you xseed for pioneering the series in the west, thank you Geofront for all the effort in making it whole and thank you NISA for carrying the torch.

See you all beyond the reverie.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTrails of Cold Steel had let me down.
The way that it ended didn't make any sense to me and then i found out that in order to get the "real" ending, i would have to go through a bunch of very stupid, contrived bullcrap to get it. But i couldn't bear myself to do it. Ever since the ending of Cold Steel 2, something about the series was eating away at me. Gone were the days when the series was willing to dive into darker material, like it had with Trails in the Sky. Furthermore, it seemed like they had fallen into a very formulaic kind of storytelling. The protagonists would goof around doing nothing of consequence until the last minute when they finally get to someone that has answers to what is going on, they would fight, the antagonist would say something cryptic but then gain the upper hand and then the protagonists would get rescued by someone. Rinse and repeat. It got to be so predictable and dumb that i took long periods of time on hiatus midway through the games before finishing Cold Steel 3 and 4. Couple this with many of the people that they try very desperately to make you like betraying you for very stupid reasons and then none of them facing any consequences for that and Cold Steel's story left a VERY bitter taste in my mouth.

I say all this to preface that THIS game helped to fix all this. I still haven't completely forgiven Claire. I never liked Lechter to begin with, and while i still kinda like Sharon, the fact that she was so willing to betray me in the first place still feels wrong. But Rufus? I hated him the most. And now he's one of my favorite characters. I never would have thought that i would be rooting for that arrogant jackass. But here i am. I feel like he is the only one that truly redeemed himself with this installment. But similarly, this game has redeemed the series for me as a whole. I tried to play Trails into zero after Cold Steel, since it came out on steam after Cold Steel did, but i couldn't. The hurt i had from Cold Steel was still fresh and i could see the seeds what what Cold Steel would become starting to form. I started to recognize the same formulaic storytelling that got Cold Steel in trouble.

This game is great and has reignited my passion for the series again.

But of course, if you've played through the series so far and are looking at this game now, you likely already have your answer on if you want this game or not. Let me just say that if you were like me and didn't like where Cold Steel took the series, this game is worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTrails into Reverie is a great The Legend of Heroes title. It's a bit of a weird experience at times, but on a whole, the game is great. Let's explore the ups and downs of the game (+, +/-, -, ?):

+The story is pretty good with occasional confusing segments to it. Following three different groups feels weird, but everything comes together in the end quite nicely.*
+I like how the Reverie Corridor works**. It's like the main hub in Sky the 3rd.
+Having all the mini-games*** be in one place is great. Finally, you get to be Rean and stare at boobs. Nice. But really, the Sailor Moon-like on-rail shooter was the best... and way too short!
+The OST is once again awesome, and these dungeon themes slap so damn hard! This is a top-tier OST among top-tier OSTs! I can't even pick a favorite, there are so many great tracks here.
+It feels like something is always happening. The atmosphere is awesome and you just can't stop playing. Playing 8 hours straight? Oh yes!
+ Rufus was not my choice of being the third main person, but I love how things turned out. I definitely need to mention Lapis . I though she would become a painful character, but I ended up really liking her.
+ Randy proposed ! Yesss! My boy!
+/-The game is way too easy. At first, I thought there might be some challenge to this (on normal), but no. I may have played these games too much and become too good... Nothing could stop my evasion tank Kurt.
+/-*I would have loved to see Rufus actually die . The series has a major problem in presenting death, so maybe this could've been something different.
+/-**While I like The Corridor , it feels a bit off. It's like having a 4th wall breaking element as part of the story. It is both important and not at the same time.
-***Oh god I hated Project Tyrfing. That was both boring and clunky. It felt painful. A rare misstep.
-Too many characters. I like seeing these characters work together, but there's barely any time to focus on most of them. Even some main characters feel totally left out! I honestly forgot Elliot is in the game until the finale when I hear his voice again.
-The main points of the story are too off to me. I feel a bit disappointed with "super AI creates bots and people are causing their own demise with hate..." story. I had high expectations, but I feel a bit let down.
-So, relationship choices mean almost nothing now?
?The finale is super long. Be aware of this!
?Umm...Joerg? Where are you? And where's almost everyone from the Society ?
?Am I supposed to know what the hell happened with The Emperor ? I mean, aside from reading the books?
? Schera is pregnant. It was very smoothly implied. I know who scored! Bring out the party wine!

Trails into Reverie is a great title with some minor flaws to it. The game is awesome and you can easily find yourself spending hours and hours improving your teams and enjoying the story (for the most part). I gotta replay this one on hard and add points to my review, as I always do. 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: They finally patched the game's translation, nearly nine months after release. I went ahead and verified that the item and skill descriptions and game systems are now up to the standard of previous games. (I can't easily check if NPC dialogue has been improved, without replaying the entire game.) At any rate, I can recommend this game now that the TL patch has gone through. Nobody this deep into the series needs me to explain *why* I recommend the game, so I've kept the text of the original "not recommended" review below.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

I don't regret playing the game, but I do regret playing it before they patched the translation.

If you're like me and you read most/all of the NPC dialogue in this series, that process will regularly uncover some utterly mangled sentence structure, lines of dialogue assigned to the wrong NPC entirely, or mistranslated words that change the entire meaning of the sentence (for example, a critical line changing "hours" to "years"). You will regularly have to stop and think "wait, what was that line *supposed* to be?" In fact, you'll run into a good deal of this even if you mostly skip optional dialogue, because the critical path's script has plenty of errors too.

Regardless of how you play this game, several of the item and skill descriptions are also falsely translated - Crafts will say they boost STR when they boost SPD, items will say they give stats that they don't, items will say they *break* when they don't, the entire cooking system is in raw notation as though they didn't finish putting the item descriptions into an easily readable English format, etc.

The game content is fine - I particularly enjoyed C's Route - but I cannot recommend picking Reverie up until they fix the frankly unacceptable volume of text errors. I will gladly change this review once that patch has come through.\t\t\t\t\t
