The ONLY fresh addition is the REAL RUFUS ALBAREA disguising himself as C (yep, C as in Crow Armburst's alias in Cold Steel 1), and getting some new additions to the plot (one of the which is the AI that was supposed to be protecting the ELYSIUM system but got sent to the body of a Rosenberg doll ......... ).

The 3 protagonists : C, Rean and Lloyd try to break the curse again. Rean has a bigger challenge because Ishmelga-Rean is a cooler and sexier version of himself who does not give a about using his full power (self-deprecating and oh-so-righteous REAN SCHWARZER always feels bad for doing so except under extreme circumstances).

Anyways, the plot follows the 3 main characters trying to understand WTF is going on (likely so do the DEVs). Then after understanding the situation they defeat the villains while trying to reverse the brainwash and the curse's effects.

This game is a repetition of the same events in CS4, with some new characters.

The game introduces (and probably discards it from Kuro no kiseki again) a new mechanic called TRAILS TO WALK, which essentially lets you choose a different protagonist and play their side of the story while the same events unfold in other areas. So basically you get 3 SIDES OF THE SAME STORY.


However, I am hopeful I will get more involved into the game and really like the story by the end. Fingers crossed.....


Edit :
Finally finished my first play-through. The story does effectively serve 2 purposes : Close out the arcs of Erebonia, Liberl and Crossbell AND give an introduction to Calvard.

The game runs really smooth and the gameplay is quite fun and the vast array of playable characters makes it much more enjoyable.

There are 2 things, I did not like about the game (even after finishing it) :

1. The story felt like a drag. The game essentially is meant to close out the Great Twilight Arc and to usher in Calvard's arc, but sometimes I felt like it was dragging its feet. However, opinions on the story are completely subjective. This is simply how I felt, others may feel differently and that's upto them.

2. Besides the main story, there is a HUGE GAMEPLAY AREA that is revealed quite early but not fully accessible until the end of the story. THE REVERIE CORRIDOR. Won't go into spoilers, but this area is basically a dungeon crawler + gacha game + some slice-of-life stories. The entire point of this area is to grind and level up your characters. UNTIL THE MAIN STORY FINISHES. Then this becomes much more important. I feel like, Nihon Falcom could have just made the story extension at the end and skipped all the dungeon crawling (again this is because I don't like dungeon crawling).

Anyways, I would really prefer to give this game a NEUTRAL REVIEW but steam does not allow that. And since I want the Trails games to succeed, I will give a THUMBS UP HERE.

Anyone who managed to read this review till the end, you have my respect for your patience.\t\t\t\t\t
