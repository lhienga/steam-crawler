\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I've finally caught up with english Trails releases. This game is not standalone. If you have no prior knowledge of Trails you'd have to start at one of a few points. But aside from that, Reverie definitely serves as a very fitting conclusion to most plot points up to this point in the series. I don't get the massive hate for CS IV but definitely this ending serves as a much more fulfilling one with the 3 main characters getting the screentime they deserve.

First place to start for newcomers is definitely Sky first chapter. It's the oldest game so the pacing might be slow, I did drop the game for a while but I picked it back up after a while of playing Cold Steel 1. This game serves as the chronological first game in Zemuria, while not essential to the other starting points it does build up the world and I do recommend this just for Second and Third chapters and their much better story.

Secondly you could start at Trails to Zero or Trails of Cold Steel. This is part of the split in timelines for the franchise. Eventually if you'd get to Cold Steel 3 the timelines will merge again so having prior knowledge of both is pretty good to understanding the larger story context. But if you'd like to skip those...

Cold Steel 3 can also serve as a starting point but you'd need to be filled in on all the lore points up to that point. It is the middle of the Cold Steel arc being 3/4th games, but it does tell its own story and is still playable but I don't recommend this one.

Reverie is not suitable as a starting point, it serves as a sort of epilogue game alike Sky 3rd, mostly finishing the storyline their respective arcs seek to tell.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, no you can't start with this game. It essentially a capstone to the previous 2 arcs (6 games), and to some extent all 3 prior arcs of the series. Tonally, this is Cold Steel 5; focus-wise it leans a lot more toward being Crossbell 3.

The story I'd have to say is the weakest part. It's not outright terrible, but there were definitely lackluster directions and perplexing ways of handling a couple situations, even when the writing was otherwise brilliant. You can tell this game wasn't originally planned and they had to come back with a plot that doesn't retcon what Cold Steel 4 says happened at the end, but still have things happen in the story. In the end, there aren't as many lows as in the previous game, but it also doesn't hit the same highs scenario-wise. One thing that does deserve credit is that the pacing of the main story is miles better than any other Trails game, although Sky 3rd is pretty similar.

The new characters in this game are easily the standout. They're a breath of fresh air and some of the best additions to the series in a while, and steal every scene focused on them.

In terms of party-making and build-making, this is one of the most fun rpgs I've played. Make your dream team, build them whatever way you want, and do some good old fashioned dungeon crawling. I feel like this is the easiest Trails game, at least once you get past the early game. It's not as blatantly broken as CS3, but you can break it more than that game if you try. Honestly it adds more to the fun of it, though.

The dungeon crawling and other side content is where this game shines. The daydreams are similar to doors in Sky 3rd. I'd say at least 80% of them range from good to outstanding, with not many being lackluster. There's a lot of good sendoffs to characters you know and love.

There is a system that is reminiscent of gacha for the side content, but it's only the good side of gacha. It's not like Xenoblade 2 where good luck pulling a character you want. You will get all the characters by putting in a little bit of extra effort. You will get all the daydreams, and all the minigames. There's no duplicates. It's fun to get the surprise of seeing what they are, and it's not much of a time investment to get them.

The postgame is honestly the best part of the game, so make sure you do it. It's where the best aspects of the game get the most focus: the dungeon crawling, the full extent of endgame gear, a lot of the best side stories, the lore tying everything together, exciting setups for the next arc.

The typos at launch (still awaiting the patch, which they assure is coming) are a bit extra in this game. Definitely way more than is acceptable. I do have some sympathy given that it is now apparent they were juggling the release and translation of FIVE different Trails games at once, in addition to all the other projects they have going on. It's not enough to make the game unplayable either or anything. But it'd be great if they put more than like 1 person on the patch so it would actually get finished.

Oh, and make sure you read Three & Nine from CS4 before starting this game. (You can find it in the title screen of this game and it's also posted on NISA's website.) It's easily the best Trails novel to date anyway, so what have you got to lose?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReverie is a sort of epilogue to the Crosbell (Zero/Azure) and Cold Steel arcs. It's probably the worst possible starting point for the Trails series, and if you're playing this game you should have ideally played at least the games for those two arcs.

The gameplay and narrative intent are both closest to the 3rd Sky game as there is a large slowly expanding dungeon area where you can eventually use all 50+ playable characters. However, that's only half the game since the other half is handled through three intertwining story paths where you play as different groups. I absolutely loved the blend of both of these styles since it permits a nonlinear storytelling of past, present, and future events. It also lets you see POVs of events and stories from characters that you never would have had the chance to with the flow of a linear story.

Overall, it provides satisfying closure to two arcs spanning 6 games while introducing new characters and extending the threads of what's coming next. Onwards east towards Daybreak/Kuro!

Side note: Trails continues its tradition of having the in-game stories from books turn out to be about actual characters / people from the past or present. As such, I recommend reading through at least the main collectible book series in Cold Steel IV before playing, if you haven't already, for an even better experience.\t\t\t\t\t
