\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter finally finishing the main story I think it is safe to say that this was a good conclusion to both Erebonia and Crossbell's arcs. It was nice to finally see the closure of the bid for independence back from Azure as well as finally say goodbye to Erebonia with this 5th and final installment before jumping to Calvard. In terms of story, Lloyd's route felt nice to see although maybe a little uninspired and Rean's just got a little messy all around. C's route however was a wonderful perspective shift that I think also works wonderfully as an appetizer for what will come in Kuro (I did play these two out of order). Gameplay feels like it is in fact at it's peak of what it can be, it's clear that there's little room to grow as this compared to CS4 had very minor differences and it was very easy to build momentum to steamroll anything that came at you. Anyone would tell you that this is not a game to start with so if you are looking to get into the trails series, I absolutely do not recommend this one specifically as your start, pick any of the first games in a saga and go from there. As a long time fan however, I would absolutely recommend. As a side note, in terms on technical issues, as of the time of me writing this, many of the issues seem to have been resolved as the game ran smoothly for me the entire time, however there are still some very obvious translation errors that will hopefully be fixed at some point, as long as it doesn't slow down Kuro's (Daybreak's) release.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRather mixed feelings on this one but overall would recommend if you've played earlier Trails games. Similar to Sky the Third it acts as a wrap up of the first 3 sub-series and a bridge to the Kuro no Kiseki games.

Pros:
-With a wide variety of quartz available and the addition of the United Fronts feature the gameplay is probably the best it's been
-Music is about as good as the Cold Steel games were
-Plenty of great character interactions with most of the casts from the previous 9 games

Cons:
-Cast is too big. Sky the Third had 16 playable characters. Reverie has over 50. Nice to have options but the menu is cluttered and managing builds across 3 routes with characters joining and leaving gets tedious.
-No global exp. You get some exp items but nowhere near enough to keep everyone leveled without extensive grinding
-Spotty localization. Plenty of typos, some of which make quests confusing or describe quartz wrong
-I'm a bit sick of some Trails tropes like friendship speeches, head nodding, saying "Right" in unison, teleporting into a conversation somehow having heard the entire thing

Rating is probably a 7/10. I have some complaints but enjoyed it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been an incredible journey getting to this point.

MINOR SPOILERS

Tbh, my one main complaint was the music itself. I loved the motifs inter-weaved throughout each game. Even CSIV had the "Warmth" motif and though it was only introduced within that game, by them using it again for the end credits and also the new opening title sequence helped solidify the emotional impact of the game.
This one on the other hand had lot of the tracks of this game don't hold well except for a few exceptions. No motifs either :(

That aside, I still genuinely love this series and Trails into Reverie is a true goodbye and cya next time for all of these characters.
The plot and character arcs are wrapped up nicely and though at times I felt like they didn't need to wrap up anything, when finishing the game I understood why and enjoyed every second of it.\t\t\t\t\t
