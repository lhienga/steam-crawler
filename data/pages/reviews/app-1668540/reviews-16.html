\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game ties all the lose ends left in the Erebonia and Crossbell arcs. The story is the epilogue revolving around the aftermath of both arcs and puts them together for heartfelt send of for characters we connected with over MANY games. It also serves as a mini prologue for the Kuro series, introducing characters that are playable in said games. If you are left with more questions after finishing the previous games then playing this game gives you all the answers desired.

THE GOOD:
- Combat has an added mechanic that adds more strategic freedom to the player as well as giving a previous mechanic the attention needed to be worth using often. The system has been refined since the first game into a highly polished experience, any more additions would tip the balance into unnecessary bloat.
- Playable characters are not temporary like they were in previous games, they do swap out in the main story but are still viable for play in other ways. This makes team building and character optimisation very fun if you enjoy theory crafting.
- End game is a first for the series (i think) and is done fairly well for players who want to either watch background story or grind for the best items in the game. Both add a decent amount of time to the unusually short story.
- Cut scenes are slightly improved in terms of camera movement and animation but watching combat scenes instead of playing through them at times was enjoyable.
- Nightmare difficulty has more tactically interesting bosses than CS4 did. I am glad they also added a tougher mode for New game+ because Min Maxed characters are very powerful and the added difficulty makes enemies especially bosses deadly.

THE BAD:
- Pacing in this game is HORRIBLE. It still suffers from the low starts and high ends pacing this franchise repeats for nearly every game. However since there are 3 routes in this game players must endure this pacing trend for 3x longer which made me restless at the start where 80% of the dialogue feels like filler before a short combat section. Even when the story starts to pick up, the completion of one route means starting another route and going through the same pattern.
- Plot in this game strays away from the high fantasy lore the previous games tend to build to. This caught me by surprise and was on the fence if this new direction (being so relatable to our reality) make this world more interesting or not. From the little i have seen from the kuro series, orbal technology is a main presence in the story.\t\t\t\t\t

The continuation of this world building, the story, and the characters are amazing. This feels like the proper End Game with everyone else finally coming together and not just solely focusing on one main protagonist. The gameplay will also scratch that "RPG" itch. There are so many characters to build upon abilities, stats, etc. You may even say that there are too many characters. But every character is welcomed, specially as someone that has gone through the previous games. There are some flaws that anyone could have a take on like the story, characters, power creep, etc. But again. if you've experienced the previous games, then you already know what to expect and with how deep we are in the series, it is what it is. My only hope is for the localization to be caught up with the current game of the series, imagine a world-wide release when a new game of the franchise comes out.

This isn't the game for everyone to start or jump into the series, so please do search the series to know which game to start on; whether it be Trails in the Sky FC, Trails of Cold Steel 1, or Trails from Zero. It's all based on your preference as the art style of the game does differ from each arc.\t\t\t\t\t
