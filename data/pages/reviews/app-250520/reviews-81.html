\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - Amazing game, 9.5/10 - a truly fantastic spiritual successor to Fallout 2 with a lot of its own ideas and mechanics to make it original rather than a clone. I meant to play for 15 minutes and I've been at it for 10 hours

Gameplay: 9/10 - amazing fun game that feels a lot like Fallout 2 (in a good way) - lots of combat options, builds for days, and some fun combos and abilities that I've rarely seen anywhere else in this type of game. One point knocked off because some enemies seem to have an obscene quantity of attacks and it encourages you to prioritise (or indeed power-game) combat for the first few levels.
Story: 8/10 - I'm only 10 hours in and it feels really interesting - I'm engaged to find out more, and the world feels deep and lore-rich in a way that makes me want to keep exploring. It might be a solid 10/10 by the end, but for now I'm going 8 because it has lacked a bit of depth in some of the dialogue options and one or two of the quests had odd resolutions that only became obvious with trial and error rather than investigation
Replayability: 10/10 - definitely will be playing this again with a different class, I'm desperate to try out psionics now I know a bit more about the game and it feels like there's a lot of dialogue I've not seen because I'm going down one path rather than another.
Visual Style: 10/10 - love the aesthetic and the style, new gear is quite visible even though it's an isometric camera angle, and generally the whole theme feels consistent and well utilised.

Probably my biggest recommendation would be to read a build guide for your first level, or play around with a build calculator a bit to see how the synergies work. I built my first character blind and although playable, a guide helped a lot when it came to understanding what each skill meant and how it interacted with the world (as well as a bit of advice on things like what named vendor stocks what items relating to your skills, which you could find out but was a lot easier to have on a reference guide to avoid vising 15 shops in rotation looking for a piece of cloth). Fortunately the community and veteran players seem incredibly engaged and welcoming, and there are several great guide on Steam and/or Google which make the first level a lot less confusing and contextualise what skills go where\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly this game is amazing in everything but the gameplay. The world of this game and art around it really good the lore is really good and this game boosts on of the best and most fun crafting systems ive seen around but its game i really can't honestly recommend. There are two major problems with the game in my prospective and they are combat and replayablity
Ill start with replayability because its the easiest to explain. Imagine skyrim's helegen sequence but its 8 to 8+ hours and you get this games intro before it lets you roam freely around the world, its an insanely bad design decision and one of the reasons i don't see me coming back to this often.
As for gameplay it feels like it takes bad aspects of others games combining them hoping to make something great but ending at a mess making combat mostly feel like a repetitive cycle where you have to grind out a victory instead of being able to deal with things as they come , All enemys are fallout levels of bullet spongy and you fight them in a system similar to Xcom games. Death has no real consequences other then repetition making almost all of combat a boring grind rather then a triumphant stride or a story of avoiding death. and the different ways you have to deal with situations as different types of characters are pretty limited. And Mines, yes mines. Honestly they only make gameplay worse, you can see them through high perception or memorize their location through meta-gameing and they only make the game worse and more repetitive. Its game that really wants to be hardcore but in a misguided way which makes it boring and grindy instead of hard.
I really want to like this game, the world is amazing to explore and the writing is good there are a of places to visit once you are done with the tedious intro mission, but the cost just in time wasted to get there feels too high.

As a hineside message i only played the game in the normal difficulty and ultra difficulty and had a few attempts at perma death that did not go too well.
And i mostly play traditional rougelike and immersive sim games, some strategy games and some rpgs.\t\t\t\t\t
