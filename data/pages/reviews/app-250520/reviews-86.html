\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderrail can be best described as a “guilty pleasure” type of game, this game has some serious issues but somehow I ended up spending well over 400 + hours in this game. For me to give a good review of this game I have to explain what makes this game so bad.
For starters, when you start you first playthrough the first thing your introduced to is a character creation screen, the problem with this is that a first time player won’t know how each Base Stat and Skills affect the combat and gameplay. If you don’t create a character that is sub-optimal, more than likely you won’t be able to defeat the final boss and a majority of combat encounters will require heavy save scumming which is what happened to me on my first playthrough.

Underail has an extremely frustrating early game when you reach the junkyard section of the game because your expected to fight multiple high level enemies at the same time. The only way that the player can get past these combat encounters is by cheesing enemies with bear traps and Molotov cocktails. Junkyard is also very confusing since it is full of locked doors and the game never tells you where you can find the keys for them. The strange thing is that the game gets easier after you finish junkyard so I don’t really know why the developers though it was a good idea to put a giant roadblock near the beginning of the game.

The endgame is just as annoying to play through, when you reach the end of the game the last mission is to open some giant door but to do this you must collect several key items. The key items in question are scattered in this big open area and there are no NPC’s or messages that give you hints on how to find them. I had to look up a walkthrough on where to find the key items because they are incredibly hard to find. If that didn’t sound bad enough you also have a limited number of healing items and enemies constantly respawn so it is possible to soft lock yourself.
If everything I just said made Underrail seem like a terrible game then don’t touch this game at all. However, all of the issues I just mentioned aren’t a problem on subsequent playthroughs. Once you understand what each stat does it is incredibly easy to make overpowered characters that stomp on the games difficulty. There are strategies that make the early game a breeze and the late game is actually my favorite section of the game when you fully memorize the location of each item and what to do.

In my first 40 hrs of playing Underail I stopped playing the game when I reached Junkyard and I thought this was one of the worst games I have ever played. I returned to the game about a year later and finished it. Every playthrough afterward the game got better and better as a played around with different builds. Some of the builds you can achieve are incredibly fun and change how the game is played entirely. So if you want a game that you can play over and over again I would highly recommend Underaill to any CRPG enthusiast.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is quite disappointing to see the same people that usually complain about the "old school RPG" genre being dead and that also constantly complain about newer games not being more like Fallout 1&2 slander this game for being "poorly designed" and "frustrating". You have no reason to believe my word over anybody else's but if you do then listen to me tell you how this game is excellent. The game is poorly desgined, it is frustrating and unforgiving but beneath all that beats the heart of a true CRPG that will provide possibly hundreds of hours of enjoyment to those that are willing to first be broken by the game only to learn, understand it's mechanics and finally thrive in it. This is not a game that you can "pick up and play" the first few hours will feel like forcing yourself to play it as you are confused by the combat, quest objectives and expository dialogue. Almost every enemy encounter will make you shake in fear and for a reason because you will die fast and suddenly. Those first moments are necessary in establishing this world, if you want to survive in it you will need to be resourceful and know your options, sure, you could charge headfirst into the 2 guys armed with fully automatic weaponry while you only have a handgun, or you could place traps before the fight starts, anything from explosive mines to incendary mines that will not only deal damage but also make your enemies run away in fear giving you the perfect time to strike. Not good enough? Skill into stealth and take out one of the guys before his friend even knows what happened, then use your remaining action points to throw a molotov, stun grenade or even use a psionic power to stun them. Don't have that? Thats ok too, just buy/craft some cheap caltrops and retreat behind a wall, his enemy will chase you down only to take damage and lose action points at the same time, effectively wasting his turn. The game always gives you a way to surpass the challenges ahead, your job is finding them by understanding this confusing and hostile world and by the time that you do, you will realize that you don't hate this game, you love it. I encourage anybody with at least some tenacity to at least try the game, the devs appreciate and probably will need the money every purchase of the game gets them.\t\t\t\t\t
