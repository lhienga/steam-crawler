\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderrail is a post-apocalyptic turn-based role-playing game developed by Stygian Software and released in 2015. Set in an underground world where humanity has taken refuge after a global catastrophe, the game offers a unique blend of classic isometric RPG gameplay with tactical combat and intricate character customization.

One of the standout features of Underrail is its deep and complex character system. Players have a wide range of options when it comes to customizing their character's skills, attributes, and feats, allowing for a high degree of specialization. This level of customization provides a great deal of replayability, as different character builds can drastically alter the gameplay experience.

The game's combat mechanics are challenging and strategic, requiring careful planning and tactical decision-making. Positioning, cover, and proper resource management play vital roles in combat encounters. The variety of weapons, traps, and special abilities available to the player allows for different playstyles and approaches to combat.

Underrail's world is rich in lore and atmosphere. The underground setting is detailed and immersive, with various factions, settlements, and unique environments to explore. The game offers a nonlinear storyline with multiple questlines and branching paths, giving players a sense of freedom and agency in shaping their character's journey.

However, it's worth noting that Underrail's difficulty level can be quite high, especially for players new to the genre or those who prefer a more casual gaming experience. The learning curve can be steep, and the game doesn't hold your hand when it comes to understanding its mechanics. This can be both a positive and negative aspect depending on your preferences.

Visually, Underrail is not the most graphically impressive game, but it effectively captures the gritty and desolate atmosphere of its underground setting. The sound design and music complement the game well, further enhancing the immersive experience.

Overall, Underrail is a gem for fans of old-school isometric RPGs and those who enjoy challenging tactical combat and deep character customization. Its rich world, complex mechanics, and high replayability make it a rewarding experience for players willing to invest time and effort into mastering its intricacies.\t\t\t\t\t

If you're into Fallout 1 and 2, don't mind reloading a lot and have patience, then you're in for a bumpy, but overall cool experience.\t\t\t\t\t
