\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA brutally difficult, old-school esque role playing game with a heavy focus on combat, builds (build variety) and the occasional lore and immersion mixed in, that requires game mechanical knowledge, doesn't hold your hand AT ALL and is playing in an underground metro-like system filled with cities, labs, farm areas or several varieties of caves.

Its unforgiving but at the same time extremely satisfying to overcome the next encounter or coming back to a fight with better gear and absolutely destroy your enemies. I will now list pros and cons.

Pros:
+Nice atmosphere, music and graphics that really fit the setting. An underground metro-like setting beneath earth, sounds overdone but its done masterfully.

+A good variety in terms of how you approach certain quests and builds.

+Combat heavy.

+Not TOO text heavy, the right amount to not get bored but stay interested.

+Exploration gets rewarded (or get you brutally killed).

+Crafting is intuitive and strong. Great way to boost your character no matter the build. But also playable without crafting at all (like my first playthrough).

+Lore is interesting and some quests and characters have a lot to say and are intricate enough. There is a lot of "hidden" lore or things you have to clue out for yourself but worth a deep dive.


Cons:
-difficult. Since this is a non handholding game, it requires you to use your own critical thinking skills and understanding of the mechanics to understand the system. (Which is only a problem at the very beginning)

-if you ♥♥♥♥ up a build royally you are about to have a bad time. (If you manage to complete junkyard, your build will get by)

-if you don't wanna waste or have limited time, search for a build or plan beforehand. There is great variety how you play your character but you cant spread your points too thin. A jack of all trades will have a bad time.

-no companions. A real shame. Its always you vs the others. And sometimes it feels like you don't stand a chance. But clever usage of your items, environment, skills etc. WILL make the difference. I think this game would just profit from equipping and skilling another party member but that's just personal preference.

-game could need a tiny bit of more options to approach some quests. Some quests have 1 way to solve and these always feel boring, but there are several ones where your choices and actions do matter.


Of course there are way more pros and cons to be listed up but this is just a general overview of the imo important parts.

If you are interested in a crpg with a heavy focus on atmosphere, combat, a GREAT expansion and building your character you should go grab a copy and play this game. Don't get turned off by the uncommon graphic style or difficulty, be patient and you will overcome. In my eyes a MUST PLAY.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an awesome, super-creative RPG that definitely deserves to be more popular. I love the futuristic setting, the abundant crafting possibilities, the great writing, and the fact that you can customize a home for yourself at Core City. The only thing I dislike about it is that there's a maximum character level and a limited number of base abilities. I would enjoy having more opportunities to grind for XP and loot (with more numerous rather than more difficult enemies and more frequent respawns), also because I'm somewhat of a hoarder and love selling off the loot I recover from each fight. Even better: why not also small customizable homes at every friendly settlement (SGS, Junkyard, Camp Hathor, Rail Crossing, etc.) after completing all the tasks at each of those places? Anyway, a guy can dream. Great game! I definitely recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really fun game and a true spiritual successor to the original fallout series. You wont find a better and more refined isometric turn based rpg experience from the modern era. That being said, the game is not for everyone, it is brutal and quite combat centered, if you dont make your build right, you're pretty much ♥♥♥♥♥♥. Thankfully the game is somewhat forgiving and will push you in the right direction build wise (most of the time).

All in all a really fun yet somewhat time consuming crpg experience. Worth every penny. (dlc is good too btw, and adds new content to the whole game, not only dlc area).\t\t\t\t\t
