\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere to start with this thing?

Here I am sitting at my computer waching some cs with some 420, and I put on
the faceless ost, the music in it self in this game is excellent...
The graphics are great, you would only judge a game based on graphics if it mattered to you.
The world is very very big, and I am sure I will not be able to remember all the places I can backtrack to
when I am stronger. The game tho in it self does not have backtracking at all, atleast in the 40h I played.
Maybe a couple of quests make you go back and forth between bases but on the bright side there is a
implemented fast travel system in the game that actually makes sense for a change and is not just plain teleporting
between sign posts...

I have not yet completed the game but have 40h on record and feel can recommend it to anyone who wants to find something new, hard, and based on old school principles of combat.

The interesting thing in this game is that you can only rely on yourself, there are no companions as I know of
and this makes the game much more interesting, as every encounter is more a game of wits with the enemies trying to lay
traps after scouting their positions, and planing the best way to aproach the situation as the game those not hold any punches back and is really hard and unforgiving. But never never is this game unfair. from the start you have everything you need to pass any encounter you can come up in the surounding area. The difficulty for each playtrough will come also
from the build you chose to opt for. There are a lot of builds, and I advise anyone who starts to pay attention to the feats list, before creating their character. I have not known this and have gimped myself, but in the end I managed to pull trough for now on my first time semi blind playtrough..
I am happy to say that there is a diverce amount of different builds you can go. How many of them are viable I cannot tell you as I am on my first playtrough. But judging by the feat list, it seems really fun. You can be a mindbending fistpunching knife throwing monk that pins people to the floor with his knives and punches them to oblivion with his mind and fists....
So all in all the geam is great, from music, atmosphere, mechanics, leveling, writing, humor everyting is great except one thing...
You have to implement an aditional feature for x-ray vision trough walls as sometimes I have a hard time locating enemy positions, doors, etc... with just the plain higlight feature.

I would recommend this game to anyone who likes crpgs based in post apocaliptic worlds. This is not a fallout clone. This is what fallout should of have become.

Before I finish I have to admit that I am writing my first review, and I felt obligated to do so as the devs are my fellow
Serbs! Shoutout to the team.
But this does not mean I am biased in my love to this. to Me it does not matter who made it, but it does make me proud that we have such talented people in my country in the gaming industry!

Cant wait to get to the expansion at some point in time of my playtrough!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, if you liked Fallout 1 or 2, or isometric turn based RPGs in general, you'll love this... with caveats. See, underrail is a game that expands upon the formula of turn-based isometric RPGs, by giving you a massive wealth of content, using the same gameplay you're used to. The character creation system is deep and varied, allowing you to make many, many builds (most of which are entirely viable), and for most parts, the world is well developed, and there're plenty of things to do in the world. Think of Underrail as an anti-thesis of Bethesda games: you have a deep character system, really really engaging combat, multiple ways to finish quests, and almost every map is filled with meaningful things to do. One other thing Underrail does well is mutually exclusive quests. If you join certain factions, it will lock out quests from opposing factions. All in all, it's fun, realistic, and will bring back a lot of nostalgia.

So why did I give this a thumbs down? Because this is not a game that I will recommend to any other players. It's very apparent that the game developers are extremely inexperienced with RPGs. Many quests are designed in a way that demands you to do things in a very specific way, and it's frustratingly difficult to a point where if you don't save scum, and cheese the hell out of every encounter, you're not playing the game right. Dark souls is difficult but fair; Underrail on the other hand, plays dirty, and you'd be a fool not to do the same.

See, this is a game where it actually rewards you for meta-gaming the hell out of it. The odds are so stacked against you that you need to read online guides, and save/load all the time just so that you can come out with an advantage. To me, this is not a good design; this is simply a grave misunderstanding of what "fun" is in old school games. I played Doom Eternal on Nightmare, and grew up with unforgivingly difficult games all the way back to the DOS era. Trust me when I say I know the difference between difficult but fun, and frustrating.

Finally, while the worldbuilding is great (no seriously, it's amazing), the dialog on the other hand can be downright juvenile. It's a strange dichotomy: the world design is so mind blowingly impressive (no, this is not hyperbole, this is one of the best world designs I've had the honour of experiencing), but the dialogs are so awfully cringeworthy that the two balances out each other into mediocrity.

It's a shame, really. Underrail has so much potential, and does so many things right, but the things it does wrong is more than enough to cancel out all the goodwill it managed to get.

Not a game I would recommend to anybody, but I do forsee myself replaying this every now and then.\t\t\t\t\t
