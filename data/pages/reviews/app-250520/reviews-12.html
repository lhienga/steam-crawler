Pros
  • deep world-building and excellent writing
  • varied play styles for multiple playthroughs
  • large map to explore, even in base game
  • loot hunting galore
  • random events that can just happen

Cons
  • the early game leaves you underpowered, so exploration is discouraged as most things can kill you in an instant, but you need to explore to get more exp. Catch 22. Can be challenging if you don't expect it.
  • a few player skills are practically essential (lockpicking, hacking), so this locks up some skill points in player builds
  • minimal player choice in quest outcomes, even if there are multiple ways to explore a given area. Rather, choice is in the factions you choose to support
  • merchant system takes some getting used to. not every merchant will buy every item you want to sell, so it takes a lot of time backtracking if you want to earn money. Thankfully, you don't need large sums of money to complete the game.
  • Save files are pretty big, nearly 20 megs per file. If you have OneDrive sync'd, it may fill up or notify you that a lot of files were deleted due to the amount of quicksaving you'll be doing.

Conclusion
Well worth your time and money. There's a lot of content in the base game alone and it's just fun to play. The pros are foundational to the game, while the cons are minor nitpicks that do not detract that much from your enjoyment. A lot of time and effort was put into world-building, storytelling, and attention to detail and it shows.\t\t\t\t\t
