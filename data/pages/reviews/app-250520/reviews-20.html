\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never beat any of the games in my backlog, but I managed to beat this one almost entirely out of spite.

This was one of the most difficult games I have ever played.

This game does not like you it also doesn't dislike you, it is completely apathetic to your existence.

In Underrail you are just another schmuck living in underground sh!tworld. You have the reigns to do whatever you want, but the world has the power to put you in your place.

You are not special or unique and cannot take an unlimited number of bullets before dying. You have injectables to heal you, make you faster, take less damage, etc? Cool. The bad guys have those too. You have grenades to explode people and nets to hold them in place? Cool. So do the bad guys.

I am a stubborn jerk of a human being and I completely ignored the suggestions of looking at builds before playing this game and I can neither recommend nor tell people to abstain from this approach.

For me it was impossible to look at a build online and have any idea how it would look/work in game. So I just made a character based on what I wanted to play. A sneaky goober with a crossbow.

Somehow I managed not to get softlocked/hardlocked from progession, which can happen in this game and I eventually beat the boss and the expedition DLC, but it was not a walk in the park.

I save scummed and died a lot. Like a lot alot. I died in horrible ways that I do not want to spoil for other people, but this game has some of the most unfair, repulsive enemies of any game I have other played. The debuffs and poisons that can be placed on you are just cruel. Oh and the poisons stack! Sure your poisons stack as well, but there is only ONE of you. Sometimes there can be up to a DOZEN bad guys on the map. (But don't worry if you've aggroed a dozen bad guys the stacks of poison will be the worst of you issues).

I never felt 'safe' while playing this game, ever encounter felt like a deadly encounter, and often was. This was at times aggravating, but it kept me on my toes and I was always doing my best to play smart. For example, I used a crossbow the don't make much if any noise so if you can pick guys off without line of sight to the other baddies that is a great way to clear a room.

I would say to try you best to figure stuff out without the wiki as some quests don't get resolved until MUCH later in the game, that being said, at the same time some quests are completely impossible to figure out how to complete and there were many I would have never completed without help from the wiki... so look up stuff if you want but be prepared for spoilers.

My overall emotions about this game are so mixed. It would describe it as the greatest worst time I have ever had. If you want to roleplay as someone living in an underground dystopian future where everyone treats you like crap then this is the game for you.

This game leans more simulation than RPG so you have to be ready to accept the premise mentioned previously to enjoy the experience. The best use for this game would probably be to get ideas on how to GM a TTRPG in a dystopian setting.

This game is a unique gem, but I dont reccomend it to my friends, it is a game for a very specific kind of person that enjoys a challenge from their games and is okay with dying. A lot.

This game made me mad on many occasions, but when I finally beat an encounter I was stuck on I would almost jump out of my seat with joy. It's not impossible, but it's not easy. It may be for you, but be prepared to get your teeth kicked in.

@Developers- when you press tab to see interactables would it be possible to have doors be highlighted as well? There were a few times when I was sneaking around where I got stuck because I missed a room. This was usually because a door blended into the walls and I didn't realize it was there. (Once it happened with an automatic door, which wasn't interactable, but I couldn't tell it was there as it blended into the wall. Not sure if there would be a way to make automatic doors more obvious as well).\t\t\t\t\t
