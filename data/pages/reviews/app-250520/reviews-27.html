\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot my kind of game. This is a relentless min-max heaven. The game expects you to use 1 weapon type, dump 2 or more primary attributes, and plan out your character start to finish. The game prescribes relatively obtuse methods of play, and then forces you to play that way (the same way) for the duration of your 100+ hour play-through. In contrast to fallout 1 or 2, you cannot just be "good at guns" and expect to have a good time. Your damage will suck and your character will die. Thorough research of skills, future weapons, and feats to maximize damage output and suitability is the only way to play. I know many people enjoy these complications, but for me, that just means I restart over and over desperately wanting to try different strategies and feeling gimped all the while. This is a very common dynamic in RPGs nowadays, and it sadly just has no appeal to me. I hope we see more games in the future with less of this needless complexity.
Again, i'm sure this game is great for people who like this sort of thing, but it is definitely not for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe idea of taking the CRPG genre to its extremes of specialization is nice on paper but eventually comes a point where you see the actual results and realize this may be a bit ridiculous. THAT is the case with this game. I personally like the way this game is built: it has a nice artwork, it is full of interesting lore bits, and its mechanics work just as they promise you to do so. But the main problem is that the developer cannot distinguish between difficulty as a result of technical specialization and difficulty as just making the game more annoying. This becomes more apparent as you descend into the deep caverns where even on the easy difficulty you have to deal with the annoyance of the same 4 types of enemies spawning every 30 seconds. I understand that this is supposed to be the end game area where you'll meet the final demon of the game but how is the player expected to explore and to learn about the final area (which btw is very interesting and brings the whole story to a great boiling point) when all the player is thinking about is the next nuisance around the corner?! So, on paper this is a great game. It has great design. It has good lore. It has compelling exploration. It has unforgiving combat. It has true roleplaying in character build. But in reality it is an unbalanced game. All this makes the game a great cult attraction but unless the devs learn how to balance their games the next project they're currently working on (which I look forward to) will again fail bring in a wider range of audiences.\t\t\t\t\t
