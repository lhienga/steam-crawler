\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good game with some bad design choices. The exploration and setting are great and it will scratch that old school Fallout/Arcanum isometric RPG fix for old folks like myself. I would recommend using the Classic leveling option for a first runthrough as the Oddity system, while a neat idea intended to reward non-kill based progression, creates more problems than it fixes and leads to big stretches of stunted progression. The loot/economy system is a mixed bag and I appreciate the game tries to mix up the "I sell all my junk" mechanic of other RPGs, and I didn't mind this so much, but I could see it being annoying depending on the play-style.

The biggest weakness of the game is the combat, which leans way too heavily on the "if enemies see you first, then you die" style of difficulty. I would estimate at least 60% of encounters involve a 2-turn CC on the player at some point if you let them get a turn in, and in a game with no companions, that usually means death. Sure, there are ways to mitigate this, but that ends up tunneling players to approach encounters in a very narrow, meta-gamey, way which kills a lot of the immersion.

I found I had the most fun early on, exploring the world and seeing what there was to craft and then the least fun when I had to go back on the main storyline which comes across as a little janky and non-responsive at times. Though I suppose jank is perfectly in the spirit of old school Fallout, so there is that.

I am right at the end of the game and struggling to get the motivation to push through the final slog. Worth it if you have some extra time on your hands and want to revisit the old isometric glory days, but it does get very tedious the longer the game goes on. The thumbs up only barely eeks ahead of the thumbs down for the fun I had in the early game and the satisfaction I felt when I put together my first proper suit of armor with a bug shell shield.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 3 different attempts and over 150 hours I've finally managed to kill the final boss and complete the game. While i very much enjoyed my time with the game, i can see why only about 2% of players have actually managed to make it all the way through the entire story line. The last section of the game is especially frustrating and provides little direction as to where you need to go or what you need to do. The combat can be pretty brutal and you will often have to cheese your way through encounters to win, even on the standard normal difficulty. However that same difficulty also makes the combat extremely fun and satisfying, with every encounter feeling like a new puzzle to be solved. Remember that there is always a way to cheese your way through an encounter, whether it be using mines and traps, flashbangs or tasers to incapacitate enemies, or specific armor sets and shields that block certain types of damage, there is always a way and your options can feel limitless. My biggest problem with this game is that it is very difficult to make a build viable enough to make it through the endgame without any foresight or previous knowledge about the games mechanics and challenges. For example, in my first two attempts at this game, i essentially was unable to know whether or not my build was viable until about 15-20 hours into the game, and with no respec mechanic that essentially means that if your build is crap you have no choice but to start the entire thing over. For a first playthrough i highly recommend using an online build guide, or you likely will have these same headaches(also make a hard save every time you level up for the love of god). Once you learn how to properly build out a viable character and min-max your stats efficiently, simply creating character builds becomes one of the most enjoyable parts of this game. I feel like i can see that damn external character builder in my dreams. There is a wide variety of builds from laser pistols, sniper rifles, sledgehammers, chemical blob pistols, assault rifles, crossbows, and even magic schools with a very wide variety of abilities and playstyles. Remember that in this game you will die, and you will die often. Save scumming and constantly hitting F5 and F9 is a core mechanic of the game. As a noob, I found having a high constitution allowed me more wiggle room to make mistakes and learn from them as opposed to simply getting one shot every 5 seconds. Traps skill is also important, as there are A LOT of traps throughout this game that will wreck you, and unless you just really love to play minesweeper i strongly recommend dumping some points into that skill. Some people are strongly against using wikis or guides in their games which i can understand, however i would say that using wikis and guides (especially for the last section of the game) is practically necessary to complete the main quest line in its entirety. This is largely due to the in game map being pretty worthless until you have basically already memorized the entire layout of every level, and a lack of quest markers which can often make it difficult to figure out where the hell you need to go or what you need to be doing.Having said that, i found the atmosphere, NPC's and general story and lore of the game to be extremely well written and interesting. Many of the quests you partake in and the motivations of various characters may confuse you at first, but at the end of the game it all wraps up into a very beautiful crescendo, and I was left yearning to learn more about the lore of the world and its inhabitants. The pixelated art style was something i grew to truly love, and the music in game was very enjoyable. For the price, the amount of content in just the base game is pretty absurd. Over 150 hours played and i have not even touched the DLC content yet, which apparently adds another 50-60 hours of content. This was really the first CRPG game ive ever dumped any considerable amount of time into and while frustrating, seeing those end credits roll is probably one of the most satisfying gaming experiences ive ever had. While i do highly recommend this game, it isnt for the faint of heart and you just have to accept that Underrail will kick your ass. BUT DO YOU HAVE THE BOULDERS TO GET BACK UP AND KEEP ON TRUDGING, OR ARE YOU GONNA LAY DOWN AND DIE LIKE A DOG!?!?! GET OUT THERE PIPEWORKER, WE'VE GOT ELDRITCH GODS TO PUNCH!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs of writing this I haven't finished the game, but so far but even now this game is incredibly addicting. The game is very difficult, you will die a lot. It ends up forcing you to be very smart about how you play. Raw numbers and just hitting ♥♥♥♥ til it dies will only get you so far. Enemies in this game get increasingly more dangerous and they aren't stupid. They will hide, use traps, set up ambushes, they will push you into corners, split up to close off escapes, and minimize risk on their end and you only really have yourself. And you aren't a hero either, you aren't a jacked up specialty. Most human NPCs will have similar stats to you, and there are enemies with much higher stats than you and stupid abilities such as the mutants. Even things like sentry bots put out enough damage to one turn you and can even stun you. Let alone the fact that most enemies WILL be in groups. In this game, to survive, you have to be incredibly smart and utilize as many different tools as you can. Traps, grenades, special ammo, spare armor sets - ontop of good strategies. My first character ended up being forced to invest in throwing and traps because as the game went on they were physically required of me to actually survive some really difficult encounters and at this point I'm not sure if the game is even playable without some use of both. But at the end of all that, the game is incredibly addicting. I keep finding myself sucked in because despite being a very brutal experience something about it is very alluring. The way it forces you to actually strategize oddly enough makes it a better strategy game than some in the genre, because without them you're gonna die over and over again. There is no brute forcing it, and actually making it is one of the most satisfying feelings I've ever had in a game\t\t\t\t\t

The world that Stygian has made is the most immersive and high detail world I've ever played. I love that you are not some hero from the get go, but instead someone just trying to get by and help if possible, building up more and more until your actions have huge changes on this world (for better or worse).

The farther down you go the story and tension just gets bigger and bigger and I love the pacing it takes. Nothing ever feels dull, nothing is dragged out or copy pasted. Every room, character, song and dialogue text is unique with love and passion put into it.
