\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou get a turn-based, isometric, roleplaying Game with deep customization options regarding combat and a storyrich world to explore at your leisure. Its pretty post-apocalyptic because the whole of humanity has been forced to live underground, mostly in metro tunnels and vast underground complexes. Different Factions exist, some of them employ you for different, well thought out Quests, though you are mostly wandering around solo. The Graphics are very nice: functional and very sharp, conveying information in a precise manner. The Soundtrack fits the Theme nicely, a pretty atmospheric, somewhat dark composition.

I finished the game on normal and have to advise everyone to be prepared for the games difficulty: a spread out build, a jack of all trades so to say, will have problems on the normal difficulty. I had to start a New Game some times until i found the right build. Some Quest Designs also left me stressed, because of the things you can miss or paths you can become locked out of if you make a mistake, so i checked the wiki pretty often.

It was a pleasant Experience for me playing this Game so i leave a Thumbs Up. If you have any Questions, feel free to ask me :D\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA somewhat rough, but very fun CRPG. Underrail is a very deep game that presents you with a lot of choices and a very wide array of builds and styles are viable with good itemization and leveling. That said it doesn't care if you're new if you make wrong choices it will kill you dead in a second. Underrail hates you and wants you to die and if you play poorly you will even on normal.

There are two things that filter out players who can't adapt to the unrelenting difficulty. Depot A and Death Stalkers. If your build and equipment can't handle the wide variety of threats ranging from different damage types, snares, traps, and robots you won't get past Depot A. But if you're clever and use what the game gives you you'll do fine. Death stalkers are bugs that sneak up on you and ambush you with a delayed paralyzing poison and run away. If you blunder around you will die without being able to land a hit. But if you recognize the signs of their presence (Red dream mushrooms, extreme darkness, etc) and carry the right tools (Flares, bear traps, detection boosters) you can stand a fighting chance if you use them well. The game won't tell you to do this. You have to think and consider your options which can be a problem when Underrail discourages you from picking up and hoarding everything. Think and tailor your inventory appropriate to the threats.

In short wonderful game. Read guides, read all the text of skills, feats, and quests, and you can use Cheat Engine to deal with some of the more tedious elements like merchant refresh time and walking speed. With knowledge you can cut your deaths from hundreds to dozens. Good luck. You'll need it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone with hundreds of hours in the Dark Souls franchise, and who generally plays games on their more brutal difficulty levels, Underrail is without a doubt the single most difficult game I have ever played (and I finished it on Normal). It requires an immense amount of patience and learning to drag your way through the endless spiral of chaos, death and intrigue that is this game. However, if you put in the time and effort to push through the painfully unforgiving beginning, (make sure you have trap detection before Depot A and have a good build or else theres a high likelihood you'll have to restart) then you'll be privy to one of the most engaging and unique stories ever told in a video game, as well as the most brutally difficult gameplay I've personally ever experienced. Underrail is a game that will never, at any point in its 150+ hour runtime (incredible DLC included) hold your hand. It gives you a broken world, filled to the brim with nightmares beyond comprehension that will rip you limb from limb, alongside the tools you need to forge your path through it. It is not always an intuitive game; you'll almost certainly need the wiki from time to time for clarification or because you missed a detail in a piece of dialogue. It's certainly not always a fair game. But, it is always, from front to back, an interesting game; a game that forces your mind to its absolute limits, but rewards this effort with a story that never ceases to subvert expectations in the best ways and keep you pondering it in the back of your head for months to come.

P.S. Watch the Sseth video, and make sure you get the game bundled with the DLC. It adds an immense amount of content, a separate but equally driving story that matches the base game's in terms of quality from beginning to end, and a mountain of new options for how to build your character, on top of largely aiding the pacing of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

That said once you are a certain way in the game really shines as a deep and wide CRPG experience with solid mechanics and intersesting narratives. The alternative XP system is also a refreshing change.

Caution: It probably needs 8+ hours gamelay and maybe a few experimental builds to fail before it gets good. It can be frustrating at first.\t\t\t\t\t
