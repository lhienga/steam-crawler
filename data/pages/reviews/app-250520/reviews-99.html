\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderrail is a good idea killed by poor execution.

First, there is gameplay and challenge. The game is a snorefest. On all conventional character builds and difficulty settings, you pretty much click everything until it's dead with occasional inventory management and that's the end of it. The game is very simple at its core for your average playstyle.
There were, however, things to keep me interested before. A combination of pure psionic build (overpowered, but takes actual brain to use, by far the most interactive and engaging playstyle provided by the game, if not the only one) and the highest difficulty setting, stated to be unbalanced and unfair to the point of being untested to be beatable at all. It turned out, it was unbeatable because of how enemy respawn mechanic softlocked you from leaving certain areas near the very end of the game, thus preventing you from advancing the main plot through the last couple areas. It was kinda fun, I was looking for a new update to fix the difficulty to make it jsut not broken enough to be beatable...
How did the new patch address the issue? Psionics = butchered to the point of being unusable. Difficulties and softlock bugs = untouched. Yes, the developer of this game has so little idea about how to keep the player engaged, that the only proactive playstyle gets hardnerfed in a single player game, because it's unfair to the AI.

The game doesn't really show for much without fun and challenge in its gameplay. The novelty factor of oldschool Fallout2-style dialogue trees wears off, and does so relatively quickly, since the game is linear, and all your playthroughs will barely be different from one another. There are a couple interesting moments in writing (for example, a character's presence considering at which point in story you activate the dialogue may change it entirely), but the outcome is always the same; there are a few points where you can bypass some dialogue options, or pick from two optional questlines, but they are so few and far in between that the game can barely even be called an RPG.

Every single side character's story gets cut on a cliffhanger, or just stops at some random point: by the middle of the game, you will probably stop giving a damn about what's goint on anymore. The main plot and all relatable subplots have some mildly interesting twists, but there are no characters to drive the story or make player care to begin with. Fairly competent writing of the game lacks personality.

Visually and technically, it's a mess. The game is going for some "visually functional" minimalist style, but all the character models are ugly, and animations are so hideous you might want to rip your eyes out just after ten minutes of watching at your walk cycle. Add it to the fact that 90% of what you do in this game is walk through empty zone after empty zone, usually backtracking for god knows how many times... yeah, it doesn't win any points with that. As for technical stuff - as I previously said, there are some gamebreaking bugs, and, since you're in for a very long ride, you will start noticing a lot of minor bugs as well, which are usually tied to turn-based system and waste an immense amount of your time, on top of already prominent action scarcity.

To conclude, the game at current state is a huge waste of time. All the fun and challenge is being siphoned out of it with updates that are rare enough to add any variety, and incompetent enough to keep few redeeming qualities of this game untouched. Purchase it only if you have that oldschool itch, want some linear semi-RPG experience, and have a lot of free time on your hands. Otherwise, give Underrail a pass, at least until there is some interactivity being added to its gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed this game. I love the story and the interactions and the (somewhat) free form way you can progress. I say somewhat because there were several instances where I was stuck because of choices I'd made in character evolution which meant (for me, and I realize this is a personal failing) I couldn't solve some of the areas without looking up how. It's very much a Fallout 1 or 2 style game, and if you liked those, and like turn-based tactical combat, then I think you will like Underrail.\t\t\t\t\t
