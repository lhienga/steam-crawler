\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderrail is a proof of concept prototype effort and labor of love that succeeds with flying colors. With time, money and the benefit of hindsight and experience, a sequel could very easily establish Underrail as one of the most influential and instantly-classic intellectual properties of the decade in its genre. There is a clear lineage here, descending from the pedigree of the original Infinity Engine -era Fallout games, but Underrail manages to prove itself distinct while still nailing that particular blend of post-apocalyptic future-punk.

It's a serious game that has taken its genre and performed it "straight" without kitsch, without gimmick, without camp. The difficulty is unforgiving because the developer knows that players know how to use a quicksave feature - and that the learning curve is quite gently sloped thanks to players' access to the internet. That said, reading wikis will only get you so far in Underrail since the array of tasks set before the player still require choices. Decisions made can easily close off alternative routes in Underrail, whether that be your character's repertoire of skills and abilities or their affiliation with factions that inhabit the setting or your path forward through a dungeon. The most impressive thing about Underrail is that these choices always seem to feel so organic and immersive since the game's response isn't to punish you but rather to continue presenting paths forward according to your continuously evolving position within it. If you are a perfectionist packrat who has an attack of anxiety if you leave an unopened chest behind, flee now!

That said, Underrail is not a fast-paced game for those with attention deficits. It requires patience, determination, sometimes good memory, and the obsessive-compulsive interest in inventory management that attracts survival gamers. The setting is brilliantly presented with factoids about its history only provided on an as-needed and as-requested basis rather than as an info-dump. And yet it feels so ominously alive and engaging at all times. It's a title that boldly proves one does not need a studio of a hundred people and ten million dollars creating the most modern photorealistic rendering in order to be profoundly immersive and compelling. This game instantly and consistently evokes the atmosphere of existential dread and suspense of a television tuned to a dead channel...

Underrail recreates and innovates upon a genre that has too long languished under the whip of mainstream profit motive: it's a sincere and sensitive treatment of the very most core kernel of contemporary Punk philosophy while effortlessly avoiding the temptation to veer off into the sophomoric candylands of post-post-modernism.\t\t\t\t\t
