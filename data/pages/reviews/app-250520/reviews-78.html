\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs you can probably tell based on my (as of writing this) 676 hour play time, I love this game. It's got cool lore, it's got plenty of different places to explore, it's got a variety of different ways to handle different situations, it's got different factions to befriend or become enemies with, it's got more science fiction mumbo jumbo than you can shake a stick at, and for me, it scratches that roleplaying itch that the last two fallout games just couldn't.

That said, this game can be tricky to get the hang of. It has a significant difficulty curve fairly early on, there aren't any objective markers to tell you where to go, and how you mold your character will largely determine whether you will end up kicking ass and taking names or end up getting your ass kicked up and down the metro. Lastly, don't crank up the difficulty until you're already familiar with the game mechanics and have played through the game a few times unless you want to be taught the hard way the definition of insanity.

All in all, if you like fallout 1, 2 and new vegas, enjoy a challenge, and like a nice, deep, single player RPG experience, you will probably like Underrail. On the other hand, if you don't like sci fi or RPGs, are too intimidated by the reputation of games like Dark Souls, or just prefer mindless FPS Games or grindy looter shooters and MMOs, Underrail probably isn't for you.\t\t\t\t\t
