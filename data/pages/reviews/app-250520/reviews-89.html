\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a mixed review.

Underrail is a unique game. There are two (2) seemingly intended ways to play it:
  1. Popping open a guide, applying what it says, and having fun murking NPCs using different light shows in some crunchy turn-based combat. However, doing this first will lock you from doing the following;
  2. Blindly crawling down into this horrible, huge, mysterious caved-in universe; a place where everyone is insane, nobody respects or cares about you, and everything else from the architecture to nature simply hates you; a reality wherein you can't possibly predict what will happen around the corner no matter how wild your imagination is, where you'll bow your head in one way or another then do as you are told even if it seems ludicrous, needlessly risky, or down right impossible.

I've gone through the latter, and am here to tell you all about it. Welcome to Underrail, fraggin pipeworker.

Here be spoilers, if you like the idea of checking out #2, close this review page immediately. Simply go play the game right now.

This game is really out there for an indie. My expectations were "hardcore survival universe" and I got something else. The description on the Steam page is accurate however, which is why I can't give it a bad review; there's a huge focus on exploration and combat, and that's all that you will really get out of this game.
  • Do not play Underrail expecting to be immersed in the narrative of it; the story, characters, and roleplaying elements are all absolutely derailed and not worth putting much thought into. Otherwise, when it isn't talking, the world of Underrail is very poetic. Its atmosphere and worldbuilding are very captivating;
  • In the event you do get into the narrative of it, you'll do nothing but consistently hurt yourself from trying to process what messed up turn it is going to take next, and what kind of nonsensical garbage the quest givers will demand you risk your skin for, always rewarding you with a pitiful amount of coins in return.
  • The game loves questions and does mystery well. It just doesn't do a good job answering them. This means exactly what you think it means.
  • This game is about the journey, and not the destination. This means exactly what you think it means.

As for the gameplay, allow me to open this topic with the following paragraph: Embarking into this game blind is a humbling experience in the sense that it feels like you are in a prank show, trying not to get pranked, then getting pranked anyway. It doesn't matter what kinda RPG/Strategy games you've played; original XCOM, Wizardry series, Fallout; what kind of strategy you like doing; what kinda paranoias and precautions you make, this game will grab your head and flush it down a toilet. Multiple times, you will die. Multiple times, you'll feel like a complete idiot in both combat and narrative. Multiple times, the rug will be pulled from under you to get you to fail and give up. The only way to fight this, without using a guide, is by saving the game and reloading. Throughout my playthrough (Main plot + DLC) I've had at least 200 save files amounting to at least 15GB of data. It's actively hostile to you as a player at every front.

Understanding all of that is the first fundamental of gameplay. The game will not give you information; you can right click enemies to get their Health/Energy Shield/Mana Points. That's all you will ever get. You don't get to know Enemy Level, and also won't know their feats/skills, weapons, strategies or gear until they use them on you. And there are a lot of skills, weapons, gear and strategies available for both NPCs and yourself. At first this may sound like a good thing, but it really isn't:

  • You get skill points whenever you level up, and you're forced to assign them. There is no "respec" anywhere from the near 200 hours I played. Picking a way to play is all or nothing, either you make the build already knowing why you're putting points in things or you're playing a guessing game.
  • Even though I managed to be "wise" enough with my guessing, I initially kind of expected any build could sort of work. However as I played the game I could clearly tell the opposite; you can easily make some builds that will lock your character for good and force you to restart.
  • Stealth is a mandatory skill to keep yourself sane, being one of the few skills that always has utility and works during the real time mode.
  • Turn based mode starts the moment you are spotted by something that doesn't like you. Again, if you don't have stealth, you can't prepare or try to shift the odds otherwise.
  • You're not chased by enemies across load zones, easily allowing you to leave and resupply mid combat. However, the zone will be stopped in time, and NPCs will know exactly where you are and have the guaranteed first turn the moment you load back in, even if it's a different entrance and/or is narratively months later.
  • Some quests will send you on a huge tangent from not meeting some crafting point requirements, if you don't have combat down for any sort of imaginable theater you are simply going to be soft locked.
  • The social skill tree is a trap, as most quests barely trigger these. Some huge quest lines will disappear completely if you don't pass certain checks, or call the wrong dude "old". Be a complete suck up to every NPC or the game just becomes harder for free.

The actual combat works as follows: Game rolls initiative for turn order, on your turn you get movement and action points based on your gear and stats, then actions will subtract from one of these point pools. The most narrative you can do with this is checking inventories of things on the ground; now go try to escape by going out of (or into) a hole during combat however, and the game will pretend to allow you to then laugh in your face as you wasted points trying to do something you can't. Again, it's a game focused on RNG/tactics combat. If you don't want that, you don't want anything to do with this game.

Finally, there's the level and quest design. These were clearly done on a complete whim, under a game of darts between the developers every other Friday night.
  • Quests range from being insane, having several ways of finishing them with multiple equally insane dialogue trees, to having just a single way to do them, both in map layout and narrative.
  • There's no real persistent NPC dialogue log, so if you aren't paying attention or click through dialogue you will definitely get stuck in your quests.
  • Quest status is the only note you get, and rarely updates after you complete conditions, only updating after you return with the completed condition and talk to the quest giver; this usually infers in you doing the whole trip back to figure out you didn't complete the condition because the dialogue didn't change.
  • Areas of interest for the player like shops are often sprinkled across different load zones with little regard for convenience. There is no quick travel, and the in-game travel costs a fortune. Pair this with the previous point.
  • The isometric perspective of the game will often put a wall blocking vision on the path, making it impossible to see things without the highlighting option. There is no wall transparency or toggle for this highlight option, so you're effectively holding TAB throughout the whole game.

Conclusion
This review ultimately reads "This game sucks.", there's no other way to put it. Despite that, it all makes for an experience unlike any other.

Through all the torment and garbage you go through, it leaves you feeling the world of Underrail is its own thing. It was not built for you to enjoy it, and it shows. This, on its own, gives it life and independence that I could not find in any other game. Play it and be humbled; or simply pop a guide and lose yourself to some wacky turn based combat.\t\t\t\t\t

Due note i have yet to complete the game as i end up seeing other builds that make me want to play a different character but ive gotten pretty far and will keep getting closer to the end with my current build

1. Play on Easy Classic xp mode with a premade build that has high Consitution the game heavily rely's on knowledge of encounters and the general systems of the game something you aren't going to have a clue about if you make a build yourself you will more than likely end up unable to complete the mid-end game because the enemies are numerous, Well built and capable of killing you in mere turns due to stun locks or just sheer damage [Snipers,Crossbow]

2. You will be quick saving pretty much before every encounter and after along with making hard saves before quest or if you decide to enter a dangerous looking area so you don't get stuck and some inter area loading screens lead to enemies straight away and they get the first turn which is pretty fatal leading on to tip 3

3. Always always try to get the first turn using stealth or fore knowledge of enemies, Enter Key starts combat mode meaning you have the first turn so you can Crowd Control "CC" Dangerous foes and hopefully at least kill a target

4.Use cheat engine's speed hack to speed the game up as it is slow as ♥♥♥♥ even with the inbuilt speed option
the game has tons of loading screens as each area is small so an SSD and high ram is recommended, Also you can use the infinite carry weight cheat [Probably not for the first time] table to deal with the issue of carry weight becoming bothersome and time consuming due to the next tips importance

5. Crafting is essential and a must have being able to make napalm grenades is both fun and practical by the mid game to late or even early game your gear will consist of self crafted items and said ingredients take up weight which is a premium if you don't have high strength most premade builds have the crafting min maxed as in putting just enough points in to be able to craft a certain item with the buffs you can get food+workbench that you get for your home in core city. So with the points they save they can be put else where hence the meaning of min-max

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolute gem of a game, took me long enough to get to actually play it, but it is very much a game to satiate that old crpg hunger that stuff like Fallout 1/2 might've left you with.

It is by no means a pleasant experience, but a very satisfying one. It'll slide it's shlong down your a-hole and initially it will hurt, but after the pain subsides you'll beg for more. Quick save and quick load are your very best friends and you'll learn to appreciate different approaches to nearly every encounter.

So what is it that makes it so special among other crpgs?
- For one, the atmosphere and world building. Dense underground air giving you that heavy Glukhovsky's Metro vibe and the claustrophobic tunnels, where you never know what's actually awaiting you ahead, will likely give you creeps and make you rethink every corner and every step you take since some of them may very well end up being your last. Respect the wilderness, cherish the safety of settlements.
- Diverse approach to handle encounters. You'll quickly learn that your gun isn't the only way to dispatch your enemies. Hell, it might even not be the most cost effective option sometimes(!)! All encounters revolve around exploiting enemy weaknesses in a way that benefits you the most and puts you out of the harm's way if possible. Early game especially, most things can shred you to pieces if you aren't careful. And what do you have to defend yourself? A crafting system, loads of different traps and throwables, armor pieces that you might want to buy/craft for different encounters, terrain or even doors! Every encounter is different, and not everything can be solved by running and gunning everything down, especially if you're heavily outnumbered. And remember, animals don't have thumbs.
- Expansive character building system that will reward game knowledge with immense satisfaction in handling obstacles the way you choose.

The game isn't without it's downsides however.
- Sound design is a bit hit or miss. A lot of it is great, but gunshots makes you feel like holding a paintball gun even if the impact behind your projectiles is there. Generally speaking gun sounds are probably the weakest part of it.
- Very difficult and not new player friendly. The game throws you into this world without any pointers or tutorials and it's insanely easy to screw up your character if you aren't careful, irreparably. Crafting system also isn't explained at all, so you'll likely learn it the hard way.
- A lot of RNG in vendor's stock. It doesn't sound bad and in a way it prevents exploiting some of the more crafting related recipes, but hunting down specific gun/armor parts in a quality you want, when vendors restock every couple in game hours, can be a massive chore.



Overall a very good game for every old school crpg fan. I definitely see myself coming back to it in the future once I'm done with this playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t
