Yesterday I finally passed the tests for the admission to the South Gate Station, it was hard but I could overcome it. Finally, after a life on the run, I'll be able to sleep on a soft bed.

Day 2:
I woke up this morning rested, I _felt_ rested. It feels GREAT!

I have been tasked to fix generators in the underground so I have been given my trusty and rusty crossbow, a few credits, a small amount of bolts and off I went toward the unknown. I lived in caves like this, nothing I could worry about. Here there are dogs, rabid dogs, or rats or both (rabid ratdogs?). They are feral and aggressive but afraid of light, less dangerous than crazed bots in the city. I could find a generator, opened a few hidden hatches and got almost mauled by an abdomesticated rathound (the grumpy guy got the worst ... he didn't like my explosives). I had to sneak in the dark for a while, got lost in the unknown tunnels but luckily enough I found my way back to the station.

Underground life is a bit more dangerous than what I was used to but nothing a good night of sleep can't fix. Tomorrow will be another day in the Underrail.

A great true RPG, it's like diving back to the first steps of the wasteland looking for nuka cola caps. Overall it's a great experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderrail...is a gem. A pure gem. It is the closest thing to Fallout 1 and 2 that you will experience.

Gameplay : Isometric cRPG with a MASSIVE world to explore. The difficulty is brutal. Even on easy mode. You can use guns, fist weapons, knives, swods, hammers and mind powers due to the irradiated world above. So yeah, you live underground. Sort of like Metro. The difficulty will turn alot of people away. My play time at the time of this review is quite alot and I havent even beaten it yet.

You will restart multiple times. I probably restarted 9 times before I found a build that worked. When other folks say your build is everything, they aren't joking. I made one wrong move for one level in my build and completely screwed it up, So , I restarted....yes! Hours of content restarted.

Here's the typical gameplay rotation for a noob. 2 hours in, junkyard, Depot A. That is all that needs to be said. Players get to Depot A and find out their build sucks and rage quit. Depot A is the litmus test for the game for your build. Do yourself a HUGE favor and look up a build before playing. I got to hour 20 before I found a build that somewhat worked. You can make several builds, but if you don't take the correct traits, you won't survive in combat.

Graphics : The UI and graphics are quaint. Very similiar to Fallout 1 and old black isle rpg's. Not much to say here. The graphics are crisp. Fire effects look great. It's quite impressive what a simple engine like this can render.

Sound : The music is some of the best. I am very impressed with such a small studio nailing the music. Think 80's synth music but not the lame kind, the John Carpenter kind. Creepy, synth music playing on loop while you're in a cave exploring in the ass end of the world underground.

Conclusion: Buy this game if you even remotely liked Fallout 1 or 2. Fantasy RPG enthusiasts would appreciate it too. The difficulty of Pillars of Eternity II is here as again, the combat here is brutal. Very much so. You will reload saves and save scumm so many times due to RNG.

I recommend playing past 2 hours and avoiding the refund on this one, The first 2 hours are basically a tutorial and not representative of the game much later on. Underrail just nails the atmosphere with the music and graphics. It just works.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game. Easily 100h worth of quality dungeon exploration and some nice stories with choices\\consequences, great post-apoc setting and lore too. And that's without DLC.

>>> POSITIVES:

>A lot of build variety. Melee weapons, heavy armor tanks, unhittable dodge ninjas, crit energy weapons, stealthy snipers, SMG commandos, cave wizards etc. etc. So many builds, hybrids and variations are possible. Even on the hardest difficulty (and it is very hard) there's a lot of room for experimentation.

>Stealth is very well implemented. By far the best stealth system in an RPG. There is a noise system with different weapons producing varying levels of noise and sound travels through walls and is subsequently dampened by them. There is a light system with different levels of lighting. It actually affects combat in a significant way and there's a Feat - Ambush - that only works if your enemy is lit and you striking from darkness.

>Meaningful character progression. Every level feels like a powerup. Every other level you get a Feat and feats are very strong with some being build enabling or completely changing how you play the game (like the previously mentioned Ambush). Every stat can be useful, every skill can be useful.

>Great setting and lore. Dialogue is well written.

>Music and ambiance is incredible. The main menu theme is one of my favourite menu themes in videogames.

>>> PERSONAL TASTE:

>Underrail is an open-world (kind of?) made from small interconnected (sometimes vertically!) areas. Lots of room for exploration and it is worth exploring all of it, both for experience and loot and for potential quests and fun dungeons\\encounters. There's fast travel by trains and boats as well as another (very convenient) hidden means of transportation.

>Combat is more strategic than tactical, meaning that if you prepare well and strike first (with exception of some builds) you will steamroll through most encounters. However even if your alpha strike fails you can get salvage the fight or reset if you have certain feats or grenades, prepared an escape route with traps etc.

>There's crafting and it's central to making a strong character on higher difficulties. Some builds can work with unique weapons\\armor though (meaning less crafting is required).

>XP system is split into Classic and Oddity. Classic - get xp from quests and killing, as well as finding oddities. Oddity - get xp primarily from finding oddities (those can be old world artifacts, old mutagen canisters, beetle brains, beer cans, chewing gums etc.) and also from quests. You will be overlevelled on Classic if you explore a lot and kill a lot. Oddity is better balanced, but I imagine getting XP mostly from collecting trash is not everyone's cup of tea.

>>> NEGATIVES (these are minor and shouldn't be a dealbreaker but still worth mentioning):

>Your character is very very very slow. There's a speedup option and the game is basically unplayable without it. The downside is that your character movement now looks like benny hill powerwalking and all the ambient animations, like waterfalls and fires, are sped up.

>Final area can be a slog with infinitely respawning enemies and lots of backtracking.

>DLC is essentially on a timer (but you can "pause" it if you leave the DLC area) because of camp raids and the only way to stop these raids is to ignore the main objectives and anakin skywalker the raiders right away. Very unfun mechanic, but otherwise DLC is great.

____

Overall 9\\10, because no game is perfect! And since there's just nothing quite like Underrail it's worth playing no matter the downsides.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery punishing and will most likely need a few restarts to get the hang of but once you start making progress this is a phenomenal game from both a story and gameplay perspective.

World has tons of interesting lore and unique factions to join, as well as a very versatile combat system that allows for just about any build you can think of - my first playthrough had me using psychic powers to turn enemies against each other and zap them with lightning while for my second I donned riot gear and started impaling enemies from across the map with an electric spear, using my shield to beat away anyone that came too close. DLC is also strongly recommended and is probably the highlight of the entire game.\t\t\t\t\t
