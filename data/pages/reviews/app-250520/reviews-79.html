\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would totally recommend Underrail if you enjoy oldschool crpgs a-la Fallout or Wasteland. The atmosphere is great and, along with the music, makes for a lot of memorable places and events. The history and detail of the in-game world kept me exploring for hours, wondering if there were some secret behind every nook and cranny. As for the gameplay, there are many sections where difficulty ramps up and becomes frustrating, but they can all be overcome with patience and planning. The writing is really good, the side quests are fun, and the main story is engaging up until the very end. My only gripe is that, upon reaching the end, I began to feel like a lot of things were left unfinished. Overall, with almost 250 hours in this game, I have to say it has been one of my favourite games in years.\t\t\t\t\t

There is no recourse for making a mistake in this game- you'd better have a hard save already before you steal a spoon or the whole game will break and you will never be able to deaggro anyone or progress through the game, as quest givers are now permanently hostile.

The maps are nothing special. Looting is "eh, alright."
Overall it's just too janky, despite a few interesting ideas.\t\t\t\t\t
