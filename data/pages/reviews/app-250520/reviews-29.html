\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best CRPGs, not for the faint of heart either. Combat is the core of the game and it's incredible. Enemies and the player have roughly the same time to kill, so fights are often quick, with the first turn being the most crucial. The odds against you seem overwhelming, but once you learn how to use all the tools at your disposal, you'll find that the enemy often doesn't stand a chance. Ambushed by ten well-armed cannibals? Light the ground underneath them on fire with napalm, shred their feet with poison-tipped caltrops, and pick off the rest with burst fire from your gun. Few fights are impossible in this game, as long as you are appropriately leveled.

I give this game the highest possible recommendation with a big caveat: use a pre-made build for your first playthrough. There's someone on the forums who made solid SMG and AR builds for beginners with a spoiler-free walkthrough as well, so use that. This game's biggest flaw (along with its brutal endgame area) is that it doesn't sufficiently teach the player on how to utilize the tools at their disposal. Following a build/guide can help with this, which is 100% worth it because these systems are transcendental once you wrap your head around them.

The writing is decent in the base game, but the interesting lore steals the show over the macguffin-centered plot. The DLC, on the other hand, has some of the best writing I've seen in a CRPG, with some truly horrifying segments.

Play this game, follow a guide. Stay free, death to the Protectorate.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played many crpgs, but generally I'd say this is a combat-puzzle game first and foremost. There are a plethora of tools available to you in every combat regardless of build (*cough* grenades, taser, bear trap) and figuring out how to fully utilize every bit you can scrounge up to get through a tough encounter is the main draw of this game for me. The game has an unexpected amount of worldbuilding, especially in the expansion, but doesn't care too much about the immersive aspects. You can find a lot of lore and it's delivered in digestible amounts, but as far as quests go dialogue and reading are minimal for people who like to play faster. Can be a plus or minus depending on if you care to lean on the role-playing side more. There is a tinge of chosen-one stuff going on, but the game is overall not super dramatic in that way for the wide majority of the playthrough. There's an overall focus on grittiness, with resource management and rough encounters discouraging reckless exploration in the dark and dangerous (and sensibly, unrewarding) corners of the world.

The character sheet has a lot going on, accompanied by my favorite crafting system I've come across. On easier difficulties you can get away without investing into crafting, but you'd be missing out on a massive part of the game's customization and powerscaling. A good number of playstyles are very fleshed out, with wiggle room to hybridize or grab comfort skills and feats along the way. There are two XP systems, the Oddity and the Classic systems: the former helps equalize XP gain between combat-hungry and combat-adverse playstyles, but the (deserved) novelty wears off and often is only used for one's first character or two; the Classic system gives a lot more XP from combats, rewarding efficient builds that can safely chew through encounters that you get comfortable approaching as you learn the game.

Difficulty starts off rough and gets crazy at the topmost setting. One thing that I enjoy about this game's difficulty settings is that it changes the layout of some encounters, changing enemy type/enemy count on top of normal stat-slider stuff, even extending to making the puzzles (which, what few there are, can be very in-depth) more complicated. This game does expect a certain mindset towards optimization, and will let you walk into an unbeatable situation if you're not careful and prepared. The wiki and build forums aren't a requirement, but if you get stumped somewhere the time investment of a run is hefty enough that you shouldn't let yourself get stonewalled when you're looking at spending anywhere from 50 to 90 hours on a single character.

There is an area relatively early in the game, within your first 6-10 hours, called Depot A that serves as a filter for if you can handle the rest of the game. If you can get your build through there, the game opens up so much that you can probably figure out how to get through anything else the game can throw at you.

Lots of fun if you like being challenged and exploring a massive, unknown world. The quests are hands-off and usually have at least 2 or 3 ways of getting a desirable result, helping make the most out of the relative few you'll hit in a non-completionist run. Planning ahead, squeezing every bit of usefulness out of whatever you can get, and a good amount of saves ready to pull you back from a bad decision will get you far in this game. For the price, you can barely get off the ground and you'll have gotten your money's worth out it already.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComparisons to Fallout are inevitable, but not entirely fair. Underrail does a lot of things differently than its inspiration did, and it manages to carve out its own identity.

Underrail is mostly about combat and exploration; dialogue is there and it matters (there are even skills dedicated to it), but there's no pretense that it's equally important to the gameplay. The dialogue tends to be dry, but be careful not to skip reading quest directions.

If you thought Fallout 2 was hard (it isn't), be prepared for Underrail to cut you off at the knees. The conventional wisdom is that you'll have to look up a build for your first playthrough. This isn't necessarily true, but you do NEED a build. Trying to wing it, or trying to be a jack of all trades, simply doesn't work.

So why play Underrail? Fantastic atmosphere/music, deep tactical combat systems, a huge and perilous world to explore, and tons of replayability from all the different build possibilities. There's a story, and it's pretty good, but it's not the selling point.\t\t\t\t\t
