\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn incredibly fun and varied game for any fans of old-school CRPGs. The moment I bought it and played it, I literally spent entire days playing it till completion since I was just so hooked. And then I rolled up a new character and beat it AGAIN shortly.

My only warning to new buyers that may be worried about the game's difficulty is that this is NOT a game you can be a pacifist in. You need SOME method of enacting violence, since while you can intimidate or persuade your way past most humans, the mutants and post-apocalyptic wildlife will not be swayed, and you have no companions who can do the fighting for you.

Other than that, just pick a fighting style to be good at and you will be more or less set. This game dominates and gives a surprising amount of leniency in a lot of areas I didn't expect a "hardcore" RPG to give me.

So yeah, great game, highly recommend, would spend week entirely on it just to play it, the DLC is very good too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo get it out of the way; This is a very impressive game for a one-man development. It is far better than any of the old fallout games in regards to the gameplay. The combat, crafting, skill tree, and diversity of enemies is vast and engaging. Now, you ask, why after saying all that would I not recommend this game. The story writing, and to a degree the enemy programming.

What made me lose interest in playing further in the game was the story writing. Two instances in particular. One was during the JKK mission line. You meet a character who is probably the most interesting character you meet up to that point. After doing a few quests with them, they are unceremoniously killed off screen with no further explanation. After looking for answers online I find that this appears to be a quest line that was abandoned in development potentially, or the writer simply didn't know what they were doing. Either way, this really annoyed me. Especially when I see people try to defend the decision by saying "In real life bad things just happen ya know." Yes, but this isn't real life. It’s a game, and in real life I could have grabbed the prick that killed them and had a nice long conversation with them and my knife. As the player you just accept this murder and move on working for the people that murdered them.

The Second instance of missed opportunity was the Rathound King. I will spoil this as there is only one outcome. If you actually make it to him and have a conversation with him you see he is actually an interesting dude. There is so much room there for you to be friends with him and do quest for him. The only quest you do is if you the player recommend that you will go kidnap a woman for the King. He doesn't recommend it; you do, if you choose to. However, even if you choose to do this, you never get any further conversation from him other than one response, and no more opportunities for friendship. You can either do the quest to Kill him, or ignore the kill quest and leave. What a waste of an opportunity in writing.

These two instances of dead-end writing made me wonder just how many more stories in this game were the same. Then I realized, I had zero emotional investment in the game so far. Sure, I had over a hundred hours of play, but I had mostly just been engaged with the combat and travelling. The story itself was just not connecting with me. I didn’t care which side was winning in these skirmishes. I didn’t care or know why I should be supporting any of them other then just getting variety in quest lines. I didn’t actually have any interest in any of the characters in the game because they had given me no reason to be interested. Most of them simply give a quest, a few lines of dialogue, and your done. Obviously, The Dude is an interesting character to a degree, but you have to suspend your immersion when doing all his quest as they really don’t jive with the game, but are needed in order to explain a necessary game mechanic.

I do love several single developer games, but sometimes they take on more then they can handle. This game could have really used at least a secondary writer to make sure the story wasn’t getting stunted in the name of cutting corners in programming. There are many other areas in the game that could have not wasted time on in order to finish out the stories.

On a minor complaint is the enemy AI. Sometimes the AI knows things that it simply shouldn’t know. I was playing a thief focused character and had broken into the big mansion in Core City residential district. I had a blast going through the 3-level mansion not getting caught. Then, when I though I have made it out without getting any alarms, when I stepped outside suddenly every person on the level turned aggressive. None of them witnessed me break in or out of the mansion, but there was no way for me to change this outcome. I had to revert to a save a few hours earlier before I broke into the mansion. That was annoying.

In the end, if you can get this on a good sale, get it. Just don’t expect this to be a game you will care about once the combat has lost its flavor.
