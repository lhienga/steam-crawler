\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe highlight of under rail is the combat system. It's fairly deep, and walks the right line between frustrating and challenging during the game. I particularly enjoyed how effective grenades and area denial weapons are, as you don't see a lot of that outside of tactical rpgs.

I think the writing / story telling is the weaker part of the game. There's some good dialogue, and some humorous one-off lines, but the writing and scenario quality are all over the place. It's very mile wide, inch deep.

Setting wise, I just never could get into the lore or story of under rail. They paint out how the different factions are in conflict with one another, but it never really feels important. Even the central quest line of the game felt pretty disconnected from the character and the universe. For SGS's professed stance of neutrality, you spend a lot of time sticking your fingers in everyone's business.

The game failed to hook me before I got to Core City, and by the end of it, I was just ticking off boxes to play out the game so I wouldn't be tempted to come back to it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I beat the game, not the expansion, just the base game, check my playtime for an idea of what a typical playthrough might take.

This is not a fun game, if you are going into this looking to have a "good time" or "relax" you are wrong. This game isn't relaxing, nor is it a good time, it is a challenge first and foremost. You need to approach the game with that in mind if you want to actually enjoy it.

Unlike many of the other reviewers I would encourage you not to use a pre-made character from a website. However, you should spend some time reading up about the mechanics of the game on the wiki before starting. Outside of certain edge cases the game does not reward "Meme" builds.

You will need the ability to deal lots of damage with a variety of damage types, ways to either absorb or evade damage and a character which can make use of crafting to create items which are superior to what you can buy/loot.

You will die in this game, sometimes repeatedly, for the most part if your build is doing everything I mentioned above, all encounters are winnable with enough attempts. If they are not, reload your save and go do something else, then come back with more levels.

In many ways this game is like life, its difficult, unforgiving and often times just unfair, but there is a path forward, a way to achieve victory, you just have to put in the effort to take that path, and when you do, you might just have a "good time" after all.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderRail is ok. It's not the best of the best, but it's still a very competently done isometric RPG. It encapsulates some of the best and the worst qualities of this genre of games.

On the good side, it lets you roleplay and make very complex character builds. The story and writing are also pretty good. The world of UnderRail feels very fleshed out, making you really feel like you're some deep and dark hellhole with no future and no escape. The main story is only like 30% of the game, while the remaining 70% are the gazillion side quests you can run into in the world, most of which are very well designed and usually lead you on some weird and wacky adventure through this shltpit of a place we call home.

On the bad side, you'll die of old age before you get from point A to point B, as your character moves very slowly through sprawling maze-like maps full of dead ends and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ locked doors that require a very specific key or item to unlock. Your character can only walk. There is no sprinting or anything of that sort. Get ready to speedhack the game with cheat engine if you want to finish the game by the time your kids graduate. There are also no map markers or any sort of UI handholding. You only have the vague directions given to you by the NPCs and a compass, i.e, they'll tell you to go south of homebase, and when you do go south, you'll find three paths that all lead south. Let's hope you're lucky and get the right one on your first try because the other two will welcome you with bandits, raiders, and giant bugs with psychic powers. Also, you know how in CRPGs like Divinity: Original Sin, the combat is difficult but you have like 5 party members to help you out? In UnderRail, the combat is just as difficult but you only have your player character and no one else. Get ready to miss on an 80% chance to hit three times in a row, then get one shot by a sniper from afar. This game will really beat you down, man.

In conclusion, UnderRail is an ok game. I'll give it a 7/10. It has a lot of heart and effort put into it, but the gameplay really wears on you after a while, and in fact, I didn't finish it. I'll leave it there for another day when I feel like sitting through this manufactured hell again. Fans of classic isometric RPGs like Fallout or Baldur's Gate will find a lot to like about this one. Pick it up if you're looking for a meaty, hardcore isometric RPG to tide you over until Baldur's Gate 3 or Wasteland 4 comes out or something.\t\t\t\t\t
