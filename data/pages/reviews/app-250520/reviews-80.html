\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou don´t need 3D graphics or voice acting to make a brilliant game. Underrail is a classic style RPG with old-school elements that make it hard and very rewarding at the same time. Robust character creator, turn-based combat, elaborate dialogues, many quests with various ways of solving and sometimes involving befriending or making enemies with various factions. Crafting is fun. Big part of the game is exploration, that ties to the unique oddity xp system, which is very entertaining and it is also a great anti-grind device.

The learning curve might be a bit steep. I gave up after 3 hours the first time. After a while I tried again and with some patience discovered one of the best games I have ever played. The playtime of 186 hours on my second try speaks for itself. So yes, you might feel overwhelmed at first and find yourself reloading the game repeatedly to get a better dice roll, because your skills at level 1 suck hard. After gaining a few levels though, things start to pick up and you slowly begin dominating the underrail world.

Underrail is essentially a Fallout (the real one, not the 3D BS) and Arcanum reincarnation. If you start liking the game, the Expedition expansion is a must.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFeels like a love child between the classic fallout games and metro 2033.
The pros:
The quest design is one of the best I've seen in a long time. It has a very classic approach on having very little hand holding, It gives only what you have to do and leaves It to the player to figure out how. Also the quests have actually interesting stories in them, you won't find boring quests, you'll be looking for serial killers, killing virtually unkillable monsters, jumping between dimensions and many, many more (and I'm talking about side quests).

The combat for most strategy games is pretty bland and repetitive, but not so much here. That's because stealth is a viable option, that means that you can get past enemies without a scratch (the oddity xp system makes this a viable playstyle, oddity means you don't get xp from killing enemies, but for finding oddities, if you don't like this, you can swith to classic xp) or atleast put traps up before engaging the enemy. This game is hard and it helps the combat, you're going to be using everything at your disposal, to just suffer through the enemy, the difficulty makes the combat more interesting in my opinion, because it becomes like a puzzle. Combat styles also range between melee, guns and psi. Psi (powers) is the most interesting build, because your powers aren't only offensive, you have a lot of support powers, which gives you a lot more opportunities in combat.

This game is long, at 50h I haven't yet finished it. It's long, but it doesn't get repetitive or boring, the world and story keep you engaged. I also recommend buying the dlc, believe me, it's well worth your money.
