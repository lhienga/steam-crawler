\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the prime example for a game where everything positive about it gets ruined by one single factor. In this game's case, the great worldbuilding, gameplay, RPG elements, nice quests etc. all get overshadowed by this game's non existant normal, realistic difficulty and the absolutely horrendeous balance. Or better said: lack of.

Every enemy has 50000 moves and action points per turn. You are constantly outnumbered and outgunned. Enemies never miss anything, can close gaps within a single turn and also attack you on the same one. When you almost kill all the enemies, one of them decides to call for help and spawns in another 4 more when you are already at 10 hp and out of ammo. Your actions cost way too much and have a way too long cooldown, rendering you unable to use any strategy. Instead, you are expected to abuse and exploit the AI and use the same 2 cheese tactics over and over again, leading to this game having nothing to do with an RPG anymore, but rather you just bulldozing your way through the game by hitting your head against the same wall over and over until your head or the wall breaks first.

Oh, and of course, similar to the X-Com fashion: even with the most optimal build you will miss a majority of your shots, have a bad hit percentage or deal absolutely no damage whatsoever, even if you are literally running the most optimal build possible for your character. All while being mauled to death by 2 dogs that eat 50 bullets like it's nothing and deal 50 damage in return per turn, per dog. Have fun!

Your stats apparantly don't work or matter, since there is no difference between a 50 stealth or 0 stealth character, given every enemy appears to have some sort of radar that can detect you the moment you enter their vision fields even when you are 50 miles away and in stealth mode. The majority of the stats are useless anyway, because you are forced to brute force certain builds if you even want to go 5 seconds without constantly dying or being forced to save/reload abuse the entire time until you maybe get lucky after half an hour of wasting your time with the same encounter.

Speaking of dying: I hope you enjoy doing that. You are expected to basically constantly die 10 times per encounter in order to even be able to properly deal with what is being thrown at you and so you even know how many enemies and other factors are against you during the fight thanks to this game's aforementioned lack of any logical balance or difficulty curve.

Need more proof of how unbalanced this game is? I used a trainer to buy up literally every single item from all the merchants available at that given time in order to see if having the best items possible help out with a difficult encounter in a base filled with enemies. Guess what? My character still got decimated while wearing armor 5 times better than without the trainer and using guns 5 times better than what I had at the time before.

I could go on more and more about how this game should be introduced into the museum for catastrophic game balance for future generations, so other potentially great games can learn from it and not repeat the same mistakes that ruined this otherwise fantastic game.\t\t\t\t\t
