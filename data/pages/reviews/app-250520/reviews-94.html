A friend of mine recommended this game to me. I am very glad he did. This feels like Fallout 1 but with some of the best character customization I have seen in awhile. It's pretty close to how you might set up a D&D character.

Pros:
- Diverse character creation and skills.
- Feat system that is extremely relevant to the game and who your character is. (investing in skills unlocks feats)
- A really well done stealth system.
- Perfectly executed turn based gameplay.
- A lot of content.
- Many different ways to play the game.

Cons:
- Some skills/feats are totally useless and can't be used seriously if you want to complete the game.
- The game is also extremely difficult.
- The developers can't support modding by the community on the engine.
- Sometimes you can accidentally preform actions when you are not meaning to.
- Did I mention the game is difficult? Easy is "Baby's first souls-like" level of cushioning and padding. Normal is really tough and sometimes requires remaking a few characters to find something that works. I have not yet played enough to even get through the starting area on Hard difficulty. The devs also make it known that it might not be possible to finish the game on the hardest difficulty.

The graphics are alright. It's a retro style. Dare I say high res osrs?
The music/sound design is really good. I enjoy the music and the sound effects are not too bad. They really feel like they fit in the environment.


tl;dr Very difficult Fallout 1 with D&D level of character customization.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've tried to get into this three times.

The first time (playing offline), I wasted about five hours of my time and my character, because this game is impossible without heavy specialization and I went for a mix of skills. Womp womp.

The second time, I was tempted by the 'easy' spec option - psi. But I persevered, and tried a stealth grenadier instead. That time I got hopelessly lost about two hours in. So I left it for a year or so.

The third time, I sat down and went, okay, this game is clearly asking a lot more of me than I was necessarily prepared to give, so let's try the psi character and we'll be able to get some of the story in at least - only to discover that of all the mechanical changes they could have made over the intervening years, they decided to nerf psi.

I give in. This game doesn't want me to play it. It wants somebody with an obsessive quick-save habit and a second screen with the wiki open. It wants somebody who finds puzzling out obtuse mechanics to be a joy. It wants people who adore getting lost in a pixelated cave. I'm just not that person. Would not recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

Can be a bit of a tough cookie with constant backtracking, unholy amount of loading screens, â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥-level rng and questionable approach to game difficulty but the game is very solid story- and gameplay-wise. RPG system feels like a condensed fallout, with less skills overall but much more impact for each of them. Traits are pretty numerous and useful yet they are mostly for combat or utility. Combat is fine, dividing action points into movement and character actions is an interesting approach that makes perfect sense but you can't really judge if you have enough action points for your next move if you are planning ahead. Action points bar lacks some sort of number indication of how many points you have left which got me killed more than once. UI is a bit of a mess but it's fine once you got used to it. The biggest downside is a lack of companions which is one of those questionable things developers did about game difficulty. Given that enemies are mostly stronger or on par with player stats and come in high numbers it takes a lot of reloads and sometimes backtracking (to get stuff like traps, meds etc.) to deal with them solo. It gets better the more you play but it's usually at least one reload per fight. Design and graphics are mostly fine. You can clearly see where they cut corners (there is no gore, animations are pretty stiff and few, you can't see objects if they are behind the wall so highlighting button must be pressed at all times etc.) but overall design of locations and enemies is pretty distinct and skillfully crafted.

Overall: 7/10. It's a good game but every aspect of it has some sorts of flaws to it. Can recommend if you like turn-based rpgs and aren't afraid of a bit of difficulty. Otherwise you might skip on this one to save your nerves.\t\t\t\t\t
