\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly recommend this game. It does everything you wanted from Fallout 1 and 2 but much more polished. The amount of playtime is insane. I'm 150 hours in as of this review and haven't beaten it. That isn't even including the DLC which I have barely touched on - it's worth getting both at the same time for extra quests. The writing is good, funny, tense, and believable. The lore is fairly unique. As these games do - there are a ton of reloads. An IMPORTANT note is that at the beginning of the game there are two playstyles you can choose, classic or oddity. Classic is much like fallout in that you complete quests and kill things to level up, I chose this. Oddity you collect little artifacts found here and there to level up - this IS how the devs wanted it to be played uniquely. That being said, I feel like it would make the game harder - and it is already pretty tough. Make this choice wisely because if you start a playthrough you will definitely get too far in to want to restart.

This game is a modern hidden gem. Finding it I didn't know if I'd think much of it but it ended up being amazing. Like finding an amazing game off an old blockbuster shelf. Highly recommend for classic RPG players.

UPDATE: A FAIR WARNING: Be very cautious when making your first character, I haven't played a lot of games like this and didn't really know what Min-Maxing was. I managed to get 250 hours in to the final boss and got softlocked hard. If you aren't using a prebuilt character, be sure to min-max carefully so this doesn't happen to you. It is a bit annoying to have a build capable of reaching the final boss and then being softlocked but there's not a lot you can do about it once there. People recommend doing a googled build for 80 hours and then restarting with a personalized one, but literally who has time for that? Be warned/\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty sweet fallout 1 type game. Combat is turn based but since the game gives you a ton of creativity with builds and actions, it never really gets boring. That being said the game is difficult and I would recommend looking up a build to follow or you will get bodied throughout the game. Story and lore is cool but nothing crazy. I look forward to doing another run of this game when I get some free time! Only complaint is deep caverns section of the game which gets pretty repetitive and requires a ton of back-tracking and walkthroughs which will definitely give you a headache.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game world I got completely lost in.

This game isn't for everyone - it took me a few tries to really get into it. I'd say that it is indeed an old-school RPG, a spiritual successor to Fallout 1 and 2. It has it's issues, but the game kept surprising me all the way.

Mind you; It took me about 120 hours to finish my playthrough, where I tried to complete all the content I could. Some of it is mutually exclusive and will have to be experienced in another run.

What I would recommend is to read on already existing builds and see what each of them makes them tick, before deciding to make your own.

Either way, you can feel the dev's love put into the game. You'll learn to love it, even though it might not love you back.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing and you should be playing it right now. Seriously.

We talk about gems in gaming but how often do they actually come along? Well for any gamers looking to play something that made them excited about gaming during the era of titles like Fallout 1 and 2, Arcanum and Baldur's Gate or Planescape: Torment, this is that killer app you wanted and it's beautiful. I know I sound super biased but it's the truth from my perspective, this game is that good and deserves all of our praise and accolades. There are some hard and rigid truths regarding life on the rails, but there are also hard and rigid truths about life in general, right?

As far as gameplay goes, this game is a turn based tactical RPG in the tradition of Fallout. I would put Baldur's Gate in here too for the depth of combat if not for the time delay system present in BG over a turn based system, but the game plays as if it were made by a fan of these classic gaming systems and concepts. That might sound like a nostalgia trip, and it is, along with an inventory management system that can be absolute bananas at times. Honestly half the time I'm playing I feel like it's mostly an inventory management simulator with a "walking around in post-apocalyptic underground while shooting psychotic strangers and dodging violent fauna" minigame attached. But I think that's mostly because I'm obsessed with crafting the best possible gears and this game offers a plethora of opportunities to acquire resources and craft anything you could want. At least in the context of something that could help your adventure to progress, no cocaine and hookers here, sorry uncle Cam.

If you've read this far and aren't already downloading I don't know what else to say. ¯\\_(ツ)_/¯\t\t\t\t\t
