\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game does not respect you or your time. I do not say this because it is challenging, but because it is punishing. The design gates a lot of things behind skill requirements, meaning you need to be assigning your points "correctly" if you want to actually succeed. But how can you assign your points correctly before you have gotten a chance to learn what they really mean in action? You can't.

The answer I've seen in advice threads is that when you get 10 to 20 hours in and find your character just can't do the things you need them to do to advance, you should restart the game and apply the knowledge you got from that failed attempt. To that I say no. Life is too short.

If you want to play a game like Fallout 1, but minus the good writing and with a whole lot of extra demands for system mastery, this is for you. If not, avoid.\t\t\t\t\t
