\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe build variety, story (most of it, particularly the DLC), and sound design are fantastic, but there are definitely issues with the game. I did everything I could, every side quest, talked to every NPC I could find, and slayed every enemy that came across my path. And still, I missed things which I only found out existed by looking at an external wiki page. I was able to get past this, and also the fact this game is very not beginner friendly. But what finally killed my interest in this game was the end. There was no clear indication that I was going to get locked out of THE ENTIRE GAME until I finished it. I tried, but it just became so... boring ... the sheer amount of enemies, the non-stop slough through only semi-difficult foes, the annoying backtracking, and mind-numbing puzzles had just grind down my enthusiasm for this game. Even other reviews, Reddit posts, and Discord users agree there are issues with the ending. It is not my intent to judge the whole game just because of a bad ending (I do have over 80 hours). Rather, I simply wish to warn you that you'll want a second monitor with advice, the wiki, and everything up to see if you even want to EXPERIENCE the game, not just finish it. This is why I have an issue with it. The game simply became boring, through it's own design. I really wish I could recommend this game but I cannot. Plenty of others have, so you can listen to them instead of me, of course. But to those of you interested in accessibility and more holistic approaches to buying games. I hope this review helped a bit.\t\t\t\t\t
