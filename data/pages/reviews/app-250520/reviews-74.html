For a casual player like me, I would recommend using one of the builds listed on fan websites (I would have recommended the stealth Psi build, but it seems a new update on September of 2020 changed that, and now I have to start from square one).

Like most old school isometric RPGs, underrail is hard to start off with, gets easier mid-game, and absolutely dominates you during the final part of the game. It could be for the faint of heart, as long as you follow a guide.\t\t\t\t\t

General
Underrail is a turn-based isometric game focused more on exploration and especially combat. Personally, I'd rather call Underrail a dungeon crawler since the game is packed tight with lots of smaller/bigger dungeons. All in-game areas are cut into smaller ones, which alongside DLC accumulate to a couple of hundred areas in total. It took me 110 hours for my second playthrough to complete 99% of the game's entirety. As for my first one, I, *aham*, "borrowed" it from a friend...

Exploration
The environment and tiles are beautifully designed in most areas while caves are mostly adorned with a monotone grey palate. Personally I think they are immersive and offer lots of variety in total. Bits and pieces of lore are scattered everywhere. Just don't expect novels or walls of texts like in Planescape:Torment or Baldur's Gate for the most part.
There are two ways of leveling, classic and oddity. Classic is something everyone is used to, kill stuff>get XP> level up. Oddity on the other hand gives chunks of XP through finding various objects scattered throughout Underrail, which can also be found/looted/stolen from enemies and NPCs alike. I've completed the game on both ways of leveling and I found Oddity to be a much more interesting choice than the generic XP farm through killing.

Quests
The quests are quite interesting for the most part and oftentimes intriguing. I can't rightly remember a single *dull* quest to be honest. Most of them have multiple ways to be accomplished, be it via dialogue, finding different passages through exploration, or having certain skills. Moreover, there are certain tasks with certain groups that require *good* performance in order to continue their quest line, some of which have big revelations. Although my initial interest in the main story itself wasn't that great in the beginning, it gets more and more interesting as you progress through it.

Skills & Combat
Now, the combat itself is where the game shines as it gives you a HUGE variety of possible builds and combinations of skills. If you're a "build enthusiast" like myself, and love to make your own characters from scratch and min-max them to the fullest, this is absolutely a game for you. On the other hand if you aren't really into such stuff, you might not enjoy the game all that much. There are many builds available online and should be followed if one is rather inexperienced with this type of games.
As for the combat itself, you will be fighting A LOT. It can be brutal at times, but it's difficulty can completely vary from build to build and from a situation to situation. I've completed the game in normal and hard and can say that there isn't a huge leap between the two if you come prepared and have a somewhat optimized build. With that being said, even normal can be overwhelming at times if you don't have an at least ok build.
I will put some general tips on another section down below.
DOMINATING difficulty should be completely avoided for newcomers and should ONLY be tried if you are really familiar with the game and all of it's mechanics.

Pros:
+HUGE maps to explore
+Varied environments
+Lots of dungeons
+Interesting quests
+Tons of skills & builds
+ALL skills are useful
+Well thought out combat

Neutral:
>Lots of skills and options can be overwhelming to newcomers of the genre
>A very long game (80 hours+)

Cons:
-Unforgiving combat if your build is not that great
-Big difficulty spike on roughly 1/5 of the game (Junkyard)
-Takes a bit for interesting stuff/quests to start happening
-RNG can be a beach, prepare to load a lot
-Loading time, loading time everywhere, on each and every small/big location change!!!
-There are only a few real puzzles in the game (which are completely optional), but there is a hard requirement of 9000IQ+ to solve them, even with online guides as their solutions are unique to your playthrough

Tips
1. Always be prepared. That means have max durability items on you or some emergency repair kits, lots of healing items, antidotes, lots of ammo if you use guns, psi inhalants/injectors if you go psi.
2. Speaking of antidotes, if a quest mentions burrowers, immediately take at least a handful of antidotes!
3. When making builds, don't be a jack of all trades since no skill will be that reliable. Instead, focus on one of your main attributes (E.g. dexterity and invest heavily in dex-based skills as melee/throwing).
4. Low HP (Constitution-Con) builds are very much possible, but only if you pair it with some other skills as Electronics, Stealth, Dodge/Evasion, Throwing and knowing when to engage/disengage/kite your enemies.
5. Never completely rely on Dodge/Evasion, they are your complement, not a guarantee. Even with a hefty investment in those skill, you will be hit, a lot, unless you have absurd amounts (700+ late game)
6. (ab)Use line of sight, it can make or break an encounter completely if you position correctly.
7. If you are struggling with packs of enemies, invest in Throwing for better grenade accuracy. It is a game changer in encounters with lots of enemies (which is basically 90%+ of all encounters)
8. Some areas are more difficult than others, even though they are near areas you have just completely annihilated. Don't be disheartened though! Level up a bit, get/craft some better gear and come back later
9. Crafting is a big part of Underrail, make sure to at the very least invest in one or two crafting skills like Tailoring, Mechanics or Electonics (especially Electronics if you go low Con for Energy Shield Emitters)
10. If all else fails, you can also invest in Traps to completely cheese some encounters, especially the earlier ones

TL;DR
Massive game with tons of build and customization options which can be overwhelming if you're not a fan of those. Combat is difficult but rewarding if prepared properly. Story, quests, lore and characters are intriguing and interesting. It's worth every cent if you enjoy these but should probably stay away if you're not a fan of min-maxing.\t\t\t\t\t
