\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: It's dope.


It's been said before, but I'll say it again. If Skyrim is as wide as an ocean and deep as a puddle, then Underrail is the exact opposite - or no, actually Underrail is as wide AND as deep as an ocean.

I can't believe I'm saying this, but I think a CRPG has finally upended my favorite of all time - that being Fallout 2. Underrail is as spiritual successor to Fallout 2 as it gets.

I saw another review that said, "Underrail is the real Fallout 3". That statement is dead-on accurate. If you loved the classic Fallouts, or any older CRPG at all for that matter - buy Underrail immediately. If you weren't a fan of those older games because you were born after them or whatever, then buy Underrail and play a massively expanded and updated version of all that you missed.

I just love the crap out of everything about this game. The graphics, the guns, the factions, the inventory management. I also love the little details all over this game, like how you can fish, or stand by a fire barrel in a city to warm yourself. Those are the moments where you can really get into your character, as you stand around a fire next to other citizens of this underworld. You end up just sitting there staring at the game, and imagining what these people may be talking to each other about. I get sucked in to moments like that all the time in Underrail.

The map is massive and the detail is everywhere. The layout of the cities is masterful. The story is amazing and the characters are awesome. Some of them might not even have a lot to say, but they still feel like they are so fleshed out...again you just look at them and imagine what they are thinking of, or what their day to day must be like. It's a ridiculously immersive world out there.

There possibilities for character builds are limitless. As you are playing you are constantly thinking of ways to improve your character, and you end up thinking up other builds that you want to try out. The replayability is off the charts.

The thing I love most about Underrail is that it's obvious that it was made by someone who understands CRPGs to the point of having a P.H.D. in them, and it shows no matter where or what you look at. It plays like a game that was made by someone who labored to make the dream game that THEY always wanted to play.
