\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 25 hours of playin:

A deeply flawed auteur gem. Generally not recommended, unless you know what you're getting yourself into.This game is a very niche game and even within that space, you must be willing to put up with the BS for the hidden gem that exists within. If you can work through the frustrating elements of this game, the biggest highlights of this game are the stellar level design (truly exceptional stuff), the hardcore design philosophies, and the exceptional world building through the rumors and character accounts as the story slowly paces out the truth.

Underrail is a hardcore turn based crpg that is uncompromising in its vision and execution. Everything from survival, buildcraft, resource management, to combat is all very finely tuned, challenging, and requires you to consider every decision and think tactically. It is immensely satisfying to learn the systems, do well, and be rewarded for your tactical considerations. Every encounter and level is deliberately designed, and offers an interesting puzzle and challenge for each build. This can lead to a lot of trial and error, which is reinforced by the fact that you have 2 autosaves and 2 quicksaves.

The plot and setting is engaging and fun and definitely well served by the hardcore challenge, creating a satisfying ludonarrative. All of this creates a satisfying, albeit niche game.

However, it's not all good. You have to put up with a lot of ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ to enjoy this game.

The game is very foreknowledge dependent. You have to build a character, using the complex buildcraft, before you know how the game works or what anything does, and you can build your charactrer in such a way that you gate yourself out of beating the game. This is easy to avoid, when you know the basics of how the game works, but you should have the wiki handy and do some research before you start. That said, the wiki is limited in its own way and is lacking some features that might make things a bit more frustrating to look up than it should be, but at least it works. And for a game of the size, the wiki is incredibly exhaustive and detailed as a companion to this game.

Interface and UI is hot garbage, clunky, and lacking in features. You can't move windows, resize windows. UI Scaling is limited. You can only have 2 windows open at once. Crafting has very little quality of life, and it's rare that you will know what skill is required for a given crafting recipe until after you use it and find the components. You can't sort inventory by things like weight. You can only assign one key per action, actions could have been simplilfied, and actions don't accept special keys (like mouse buttons 3-5). Combat interface can be abysmal at times, because it's extremely easy to misclick, and there is no way to verify what you are doing before you commit to an action and there is no undo. If you click, the game does it, and that is in conflict without how easy it is to inexplicably do the wrong thing. "No underrail, I wanted to shoot my crossbow at the rat, not run up to the gate and use my entire aciton to close the door the rat was standing next to; now I have to reload".


Stealth is horrendous but also super critical to the game. You don't NEED to invest in stealth, but it's hugely advantageous to do so and many missions are presented as stealth missions. However, stealth sucks to play in this game for a variety of reasons. The game has an extremely restrictive line of sight, which is fundamental to how combat works, but also makes stealthing around incredibly tedious, since there are blind corners everywhere, and you might just accidentally bump into an enemy, even if you are being careful and have high stealth. Stealth requires lots of quicksaving. In addition to general clunkiness of stealth, you don't have any options to distract npcs or any sort of features that you might hope to have in a game which features stealth. Minimal actions you can perform while stealthed. Overall stealth as a mechanic is underdeveloped.

They prioritize balance and cohesiveness of the rpg mechanics over the notion of good design or interesting gameplay at times. In other words, there will inevitably be sections of this game that will run counter to your build in such a way that the game becomes tedious to the degree of becoming outright bad and almost not worth playing, with save scumming becoming an absolute must regardless of how you play or what your build is. This is most prominent if you are running a lower perception build, wherein your gameplay experience will be that of randomly dying every other step in some dungeons, because of hidden mines, and then reloading the save to manually click around the mines. This is about as fun as it sounds and happens frequently enough that it drags the game through the mud. You might think, okay so don't run low perception, but I think you would be missing the point. You'll just have to sacrifice in some other area, and who knows exactly what build is the "most fun blind 1st time run" and at that point, if you're getting that meta with your initial build to try and figure out at what point this game is actually fun, it sort of defeats the purpose and the game has failed in some capacity. This game, is a times, truly an awful game and really collapses under its own ideas in execution extremely often. This is what I mean when I say you have to put up with a lot of BS in order to experience the true excellence this game has to offer.

Overall the game is really good despite itself. The core of this game is a 9 or 10/10, intermixed with 1/10 gameplay experiences. A mixed bag, a hidden gem, a masterpiece, a total mess. I love it, and I hope they consider some of the criticisms for their next game, as I truly think the developer is exceptional in the craft. I want to shake his hand just for the level design.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInhumanly, monstrously unforgiving and difficult, on par with Rain world and Noita for levels of BS. That's what anyone who wants to play this game should know. However that is its only real flaw, Which can be mitigated by just picking an easier difficulty or spamming quicksave and quickload.
The good parts of this game are MUCH more numerous: Incredible worldbuilding and characters, as well as a satisfying story with a lot of sidequests and collectibles. Unique XP system that actually feels amazing to progress in. Inventory and currency management that matters, same with stats. Many different combat encounters, but ways to negotiate and avoid it too (Plus freedom to kill ANYONE you want!). A lot of ways to prepare in combat (or cheese encounters.) And the DLC has so much new content that it feels like a second game. Highest reccomendation. But be prepared to go through some serious BS. Keep the wiki on hand.
9/10\t\t\t\t\t
