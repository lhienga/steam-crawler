\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWriting this after 7 hours of trying to enjoy the game, I can safely say that this game is definitely not for everyone and it is probably not for most CRPG fans. If you loved Fallout 1 and 2 and have played both games and all the other similar games on the market and you crave a challenge, then you might like this game. If you're just looking for a fun CRPG to relax with, you should probably look elsewhere.

The game has a reputation for being difficult, but it also deserves to be called frustrating. Even with build recommendations, a manual, and experience with other CRPGs, it is incredibly easy to find yourself in situations that feel completely unfair without much warning that you many be entering such a situation. Some consider that "hardcore" or "challenging," but I consider having to reload saves constantly to make progress to be annoying.

Many people seem to really like the game, so you might also like it. I haven't enjoyed it so far.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo Over. A strange phrase but one that personifies how Under Rail is so poorly designed that you'll constantly be forced to progress through much of the game by playing and replaying the same moments over and over and over like some twisted form of post-apocalyptic Groundhog day.

The biggest offender here is needlessly blind decision making. Since the game is split into zones this primarily happens as you cross between them. Exit a zone, walk into bandit territory. Dead. Do Over. Unlock a door, guards on the other side. Dead. Do Over. Go into a cave with invisible poisonous scorpions. Dead. Do Over. Worse still is that if NPCs aggro to you within a zone then they stay aggro'd if you return. Backed out of a zone because you had a swarm of Protectorate Soldiers swarmed as soon as you stepped in? Guess what? They'll still be there hours later and at point blank range ready to gank you like some dirty spawn campers.

This brings me to the faction system. Somehow, and I still don't know why, I returned to the Core City after exploring the Expedition DLC area of the game to find that EVERYONE now wanted to kill me. All three factions. Now I don't know if that was the result of a bug, somebody wandering into some caltrops I left laying around (another frustrating feature), if it was due to me not save scumming to fix when I accidentally trespassed or whatever the case was but this makes it impossible to complete the main campaign quest. Why? Because a particular keycard is only obtainable by working for one of the factions. And if they all hate you, then you can't get the quest for it.

How on earth did the devs think that it would be a good idea, in a CRPG none the less, to prevent players from completing the main story arc and cut themselves off from a huge chunk of content? Poor design. Very poor design.\t\t\t\t\t
