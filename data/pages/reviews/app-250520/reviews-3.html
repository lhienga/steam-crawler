\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIF YOU HAVE BAD EYESIGHT, THIS GAME IS NOT FOR YOU

I'm recommending this game because I'm a HUGE fan of these kinda raw and gritty games that have been coming out. I spend more money on indie devs and these older classic style of games. It's absolutely incredible what people have been pushing out lately. This game is no exception. It's deep game with deep lore, a deep combat system, alllllllllllll kinds of ways to build your character. It doesn't hold your hand at all. There's no magical fast travel system. You have to walk everywhere and fight your way through everything.

Quests range from puzzles you need to solve to straight up being a murder factory, carving your way through locations in search for your quest objective. The colour scheme of the maps are really well done, and this game definitely has that post-apocalyptic feel to it. It's honestly what fallout should have been.

My ONLY gripe about this game is it's entirely text based, which is fine, except they pack entire conversations into a single text window. There's no thought in breaking down conversations into smaller bite sized chunks so that the text can be made bigger. So for people like me who are legally blind without the glasses on, having to read massive quantities of dialogue which is found in nearly every single converstaion in the game, makes the game basically unplayable.

I have not been able to play this game since I got past the first area and into the city, spoiler alert, but I"m not really revealing anything important. So when you get there, there's volumes and I mean VOLUMES of text to read through. Massive chunks of backstory and lore, and you are stuck reading a 5000 word essay all packed into a single window. It makes it LITERALLY unplayable for me and I couldn't handle it anymore. I cannot progress further than that, so despite having 44 hours played, that's actually me re-starting the game as soon as I leave the starter area, because it's just too much reading the tiny text, and I can't do it.

So if you're younger and have good eyesight and the patience to read a literal novel's worth of tiny text for several hours while you explore the new area opened up, good on ya. You'll absolutely love the game, hands down. There's no reason to buy it. If you're old and blind like me, either pull up a giant magnifying glass so you can see what's going on, or just skip this title as it's not worth the purchase at that point.\t\t\t\t\t
