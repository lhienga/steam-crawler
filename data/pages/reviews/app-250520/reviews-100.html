\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderrail is what Fallout 2 would be if it were set underground and cheaply made.

The game itself has a poorly made Action Point system, a well defined crafting system; And a very broken leveling system where you gain a Perk every 2 levels and a Stat Point every 4.

Also the combat and rng ranges from abusively fun to abusively unfair and will have you either running through rooms one shotting and stunning every enemy or walking into said room and being randomly attacked by several rng enemies you never saw in that room before that playthrough followed by being stunned, debuffed, and blown up by several mines- Thus making that room impossible to traverse through.

All and All, Underrail is a very *Balanced* and *Enjoyable* game that will slowly descend you into a type of madness we locals call "Derailment". 8/10: Make a better AP system.

But hey, none of that really matters- The game lets you become a Mind Wizard and abuse people with both Elemental and Mental mind magic.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is probably one of the best games I have ever played, up there in my top 10 for sure. The mood and atmosphere are perfectly created. The world is so dark and dirty (literally), with brief hints of what might have been. The characters are all interesting, awesome, or 'de-railed'.

It took me a few characters, quite a lot of characters actually, but eventually I got into the swing of the game. It can take some time to understand what works, and when, but when you get it the game really opens up and becomes a massive playground.

I really can't do anything but praise this game honestly. Well, actually there is one thing. I can understand wanting a realistic economy, but to a degree. It would be nice if stores bought more things, or had more in stock, or had more money on them. It gets very tiring going around from city to city over and over again hoping and praying people will finally take some items off of you, or finally have what you want for sale.

That is the one thing in this game I could do without, the crafting grind. You need good crafting components, the late game makes you depend on that. Other than that though, an almost perfect game. Buy this, play this, lose yourself to it, love it, then hate it and resent it for loving it so much, then forgive it and play some more!\t\t\t\t\t
