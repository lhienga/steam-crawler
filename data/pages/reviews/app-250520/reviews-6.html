\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very difficult for me to evaluate because I really like the ideas here, and its very well made and unique, but I didn't have much fun with the game to be honest. I'm giving the game a recommend score because the creator clearly succeeded in making the game he envisioned, and many people share a love for that vision. but its not for me, I'll try to explain my complaints and experience so you can decide if you would like it or not.

Like many of you, I found out about this game from Sseth's video, and I want to clarify two things his video might give you the wrong impression of.

First is that this game is incredibly linear, you have to complete the main story in a specific order. Yes, there's optional areas and side missions, some of which have multiple different solutions. But you have very little freedom ultimately to affect the story or where you will need to go.

The second thing is that you cannot get caught breaking the law. If you get caught stealing one tiny object in town, that entire faction will hate you and shoot you on sight forever. The NPCs seem to be psychic and will know telepathically if you picked something up out of their line of sight. This obstructs the main story progression permanently, and you might as well re-load your save. The one exception is the pickpocket ability, which allows you to steal certain things without being caught. Same for attacking people of a given faction. You can be a scumbag in neutral zones though for the most part.

Personally I just find this game too long for how simple the gameplay is. The skill tree is fascinating, and crafting builds is really engaging. But then you have to slog around this world slowly for hours and hours at a time, slowly leveling up. Using the same few abilities over and over. You're always outnumbered, so you have to keep using hit-and-run tactics to take out enemies one at a time... it gets old. Maybe if progression was quicker and the world a little smaller it would be more fun.

Seriously, these maps are sprawling huge wastelands. It's great in a sense, but it just makes this game a slog for me. Mostly empty, full of the same crap as everywhere else. There's nothing of value to find it seems (unless you're playing Oddity). Money is almost meaningless since you can easily acquire it and the stores mostly sell crap. Crafting is okay but it takes forever to find what you need and to get the requisite skills.

I just hate feeling like I played this game for like 8 hours straight and barely made any progress. I end up making new characters to try new builds, I think I have 3 main characters at this point. So with my play time that's 20-25 hours apiece, and I have not gotten very far in the story or character progression, maybe halfway or so.

However, I do love the writing and atmosphere this game provides. I think the aesthetic is what keeps me coming back. But every time I came back to this game I remember why I left. The story is actually kind of good though, I do plan to eventually finish it before I die.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI did it. After 90 hours. I beat my first playthrough, and I haven't even touched the DLC.

Underrail is a hardcore -and I mean HARDCORE- turn based RPG. Honestly, it is among the best RPGs I've ever played. Very few games have immersed me so deeply into the setting.

Be warned however, it is not easy. Do not choose anything higher than normal difficulty for your first run. Be prepared to save A LOT. You will die A LOT. It's about figuring out how to fight every situation perfectly. There are so many tools to use in any given encounter; the game rewards clever thinking over brute force.

If you're looking to scratch that Fallout/Metro vibe, definitely give this one a look.\t\t\t\t\t
