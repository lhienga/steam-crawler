\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, but massively long. So beware before attempting to start this epic adventure.
Try not to blow yourself up and save often. You will need it.
10/10
You will regret playing it and love doing it at the same time.

Edit: finally beat Tchort and Expedition after 130 hours, and I still have a lot to explore. Got tired after a while, as the more I played the more focused my build had to become. Deep Caverns were a slog to get through as stated on the Wikis, and the Tchort fight was annoying. I still have the Heavy Duty quest to complete, but after seeing what it is, I somewhat lost interest.

I would still recommend Underrail, highly. My favorite part of the game is exploring the world and trying out different weapons and abilities. However, the game is NOT meant to be played this way, at least without experiencing a lost of frustration at certain parts of the game. It was definitively a different experience, which I probably, never could have finished without the help of guides.
My advice: soak in the atmosphere, as that is the best part of the game in my opinion. Focus on one, or a few main combat skills, as creating a versatile build is not possible and will hinder you later on in the game. Should you not be able to beat a fight or quest, and you are not under leveled or crazy understated, you may try looking at those as puzzles, which will need a lot of try and error to figure out and complete. Everything is solvable, but it may take some time to understand what the game wants from you.
AND SAVE A LOT!!! You will need it.

Best of luck to anyone, both dominating and fresh to the game.

Spoilers:

Hopefully, the game will have a proper story based conclusion some day. As this whole story so far, seems like a prelude to a main quest yet to be released, at least I got that feeling in the end.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSurprisingly excellent.

This is a spiritual successor to Fallout 1 & 2, in the "Isometric Turn-Based RPG" genre. It also has similarities to the Baldur's Gate series in that regard.

Many possible builds, lots of crafting, lots of dialog. The dialog is well-written. The world-building is quite good. The tone is more serious than Fallout for the most part, although it does stray into the light hearted occasionally.

This is a big game, and the Expeditions expansion is also big. The value for money here is exceptional.

This would have been GOTY 15-20 years ago, but nowadays the old-school graphics means that it gets buried beneath thousands of other games, only to be excavated by keen gen-Xers and millennials thirsting for another taste of Fallout 2. You found it, have fun!\t\t\t\t\t

Most of the game is slow, turn-based combat in the style of the first Fallout. Specializing your stats is almost necessary.

Edit: I finally finished this game. A great bang for the buck, given the additional 152 hours (I really hope it wasn't that much and just a lot of AFK). The 'second half' of the game is huge. I haven't even finished the Expedition DLC.

Underrail is best when it's down the last bullet in the magazine and the last hitpoint. Many firefights end with my character passing out and dying right after the last enemy falls. It's a chess game with guns.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game back in 2016. Never really played it in any capacity until recently. If you like Fallout 1 and 2 or the CRPG genre while this game may appear similar its own spin on it to a degree to make it very fascinating and a heavy recommendation.

Game elements are similar to that seen in CRPG but add a little complexity towards the design. The interactions with the map, stat checks, item/stat check, little gimmick quests, and a multi floor interactive areas deviates just enough from whats commonly seen in these games to induce adrenaline or fear. The best way in my experience to explain the interactive dungeon would be how FOEs function in Etrian Odyssey where while in battle the longer you take an enemy can invade, except depending on a multitude of sound generation, cameras, and enemies moving around, you become prone to alerting things a floor above you at times.

This makes Underrail tactical enough to where you may often have to re-approach a battle or rework how you view it. perhaps your first engagement allowed an enemy to pull a switch thus alerting everyone, so you may bait with grenades and stealth around to set up the environment to clean the floor in one go or ultimately ignore it. It is really good in both combat and puzzle solving to turn the tides in your favor or resolve the quests or missions in a way you enjoy.

Aside from the great gameplay elements, the story is great. Some of the descriptions of NPCs are sort of poetic or use novel writing levels of descriptions to just invoke imaginative thoughts for what is visually limited. Aside from that, there are other jokes, gags, and visual gags to complement lore. Aside from the great and joke writing, the story is intriguing, and instead of writing an essay about how quests complement the regional developments or how the story progresses. All i can say is Gameplay great story great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finished the first two Fallout games again somewhat recently, enjoyed them just as much as when I was a kid, and wanted to keep chasing that high. I reinstalled New Vegas with a ton of mods, but an hour in I could tell it just wasn't quite the same as what I was getting out of those charming old isometric games.

So, Underrail was a game that had hovered in my radar for a while now. I figured, if its a modernized, challenging iteration of Fallout with tons of character build depth, well, that sounded pretty much right up my alley.

It was worth every penny I paid for it. The story (at least for the base game experience) is a little bare bones, and the characters populating it aren't the most sharply drawn. But man is the gameplay fun. A ton of depth, many consumables, and many ways to approach a given situation. It scratched my post-Fallout itch pretty hard, and for three hundred hours just on my first playthrough. I enjoyed it so much that I headed straight back in for another playthrough immediately.\t\t\t\t\t
