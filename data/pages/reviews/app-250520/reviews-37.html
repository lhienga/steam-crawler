\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis has been compared to the original Fallout games, but this is really a deceptive description. It resembles a Fallout game about as much as a chimpanzee does a human. I will leave it up to your to decide which of these games is the chimp and which is the human. Having said this, I will be using Fallout as a point of comparison, since many fans of the original Fallout game should like this game too.

Like Fallout this game has stats and perks. Which means the game has builds. Perks matter in what kind of weapons you can use. What kind of items you can make. Some of the dialogue choices.

Like classic Fallout this game shares many of the same game mechanics. For the younger players, this game's mechanics will be intolerable. The game is isometric. All combat is turn based. If you can get past this, you still have to deal with the tedious traveling to and from each area because unlike Fallout there is no fast travel. Also selling your loot will be even harder than it was in Fallout. Merchants won't buy just anything. They are very picky, meaning you will need to go from merchant to merchant to sell all your loot. Sometimes no one will want the junk you are carrying until much later.

Can you get past this? If so, the exploration can be as rewarding as a Fallout game. Lots of areas to explore with lots of loot. Enemies drop loot too. But this game has a far more extensive crafting system than classic Fallout. Just about everything you need can be made by you if you have the right perks and materials.

Where it truly differs from classic Fallout is the tone. Though both are set in post Apoc settings, Fallout seems to be light-hearted and humorous. Actually it can be silly in spots.For me Underrail had none of this. Everything is dreary and serious. Also characters seem to have less personality than in the Fallouts. They are there to hand out assignments and handle your adventure needs. And there don't seem to be any companions for your journey. Which means things can be very lonely in the tunnels of Underrail.

As for the story, I never did much story in either Fallout or this game. For me these games are meant for the four core (loot, shoot, explore, craft). Both games have plenty of all four. Underrail seems to be much tougher. The combat seems more unfair. If you mess up your build you will be stranded in early game because you won't be able to compete against the higher tier enemies. This happened to me more than once.


conclusion

for hardcore classic RPG fans, this is a must play.. For entry level players, you will break your baby teeth on the hard as diamond game play. Fans of classic Fallout will be disappointed by a lot of the game. However, if they can get past the differences, this game has enough of Fallout's genes to make it enjoyable to fans of those games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderrail is one of the most frustrating, tedious and brutally difficult games I've ever played and I love it a lot. However, I understand that most people do not hate themselves as much as I do and may not enjoy Underrail as much as I do. If you are considering purchasing Underrail, please read this review first.

To say that Underrail is clunky would be like saying battery acid is a little sour. Underrail has some of the most obtuse systems I've ever seen in an RPG, and the game demands that you have total respect for them or it'll call you a pipeworker and shoot you in the knee. Expect to die and reload repeatedly.

The story of Underrail is a very slow burn. It is so slow that you probably won't even notice it for the first 15 hours or so. I've heard a lot of people criticise it for this reason, but I think the way important story information is presented actually works well for what the game is going for. It's more in the vein of books like Roadside Picknic and Metro 2033 where you've got very high sci-fi concepts as seen through the eyes of some random idiot. In Underrail, you play as one such idiot, and most of your goals are practical ones. Nobody is going to dump exposition on you unless you ask, and most people don't even remember what happened to make the world this way. The Show-Don't-Tell method of exposition does make the story a bit difficult to get into, but what it does do is hammer home this idea that the world is a complete garbage fire where nobody even remembers what a tree looks like. Story highlights include going on Rick and Morty adventures with a dimension-hopping stoner and helping a band of jet ski pirates steal the GECK.

By far the biggest problem for new players is building a character that doesn't completely suck balls. Underrail basically requires you to already know how the system works before starting, which, like, you couldn't possibly know if you're just starting. Meaning new players are guaranteed to have a bad time. If you're a new player and don't know what you're doing, go to the forums and use someone else's build. Honestly I can't recommend this game to anyone who doesn't have manchild-levels of free time to learn how all the game systems work.

A lot of people compare this game's combat to Fallout 1 and 2. While there are similarities, you can't approach it the same way. In Fallout, you can win just by having the highest stats and the best equipment, so you can just brute force your way through most encounters. Underrail's combat is waaaaay more complicated. All equipment has advantages and trade-offs, and you can craft items to your specific needs. Go into a gunfight wearing a set of melee riot gear and you're gonna get shot to pieces. The enemies are tough, but you have access to all the tools they do, so you've got to out-smart them rather than out-gun them.

TL;DR: If you like complex CRPGs with expansive worlds, challenging turn-based combat, weird deep lore and knife-fighting subterranean crackheads, Underrail is the game for you. If you value your time and don't like strange and dehumanizing classic RPGs, play something fun instead.\t\t\t\t\t
