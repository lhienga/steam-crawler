\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game whilst it was still Early Access and played over 200 hours despite the game not having all the content (maybe about 25%). Something must have drew me in to put that much time into a semi finished product. I like the graphics and the game play has depth... maybe it was just new/fresh to me, having backtracked from Fallout: New Vegas to the original Fallout CRPG releases and ended up really enjoying the different gameplay and 'old school' graphics. So I was enjoying the original Fallout and Arcanum (a bit of Baldurs Gate too, Planescape etc) but UnderRail just really clicked with me for whatever reasons.

UnderRail (in my opinion) doesn't have the dialogue depth or diversity of Arcanum but what dialogue and story it does have is well written and works (it doesn't feel 'off' or awkward). There is also less choices in how to approach tasks and quests but UnderRail excels where Arcanum falls short: Combat. I had never encountered such depth/choice/tactic/engagement before and I loved it. Its a very combat focused game. Combat and exploration are the main gameplay elements that UnderRail provides and there are many options and choices of how to build your character to tackle these things. The game also provides a nice crafting system.

So, yeah... over 200 hours in Early Access and added over 100 more after it fully released I can say that I found this game engaging and fun to explore.\t\t\t\t\t
