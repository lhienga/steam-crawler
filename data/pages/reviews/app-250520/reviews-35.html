\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played this game through one time. Not all of these 358 hours were gameplay, I tend to leave things sitting paused. I can however tell you that most of them were. I am quite thorough with games like this, I want to see all the things that devs have to offer. I have concluded that the stuff on offer in this package is an utter domination of what seems to be intended.

So many interesting tidbits to explore and I felt so satisfied every time I opened up a crack and found something atmospheric and exciting. So many small things that kept me playing to find every little nook with some weirdo or creature or bizarre artifact.

I liked that the game was tough, felt very rewarded and I relied on a pretty wide variety of things to back up the one or two main focuses I put my stats into. I had to get creative on how to even survive and it is pretty rare to feel so fully like I have put brainpower into solving combat. There are a few awesome deliberate puzzles in this game (made me feel like I was doing hack science, very awesome) but my favorite puzzle was combat.

Great atmosphere, I recommend NOT doing an exhaustive playthrough, instead poke around and be investigative, but keep a few things for your next time around. Going completionist in this game will wear you out and drive you mad. I loved the game but by the end of expedition I was tired, so much stuff in this game.

DO EXPEDITION BEFORE JOINING THE INSTITUTE

MAKE UNIQUE SAVES INSTEAD OF SAVING OVER AUTOSAVES AND GETTING THEM OVERWRITTEN, YOU WANT ALL THOSE TIMESTAMPS TRUST ME. SAVE OFTEN.

GIVE UP SOMETIMES AND COME BACK LATER.

MAKE YOUR BUILD EXTREMELY GOOD AT AT LEAST ONE OR TWO THINGS, ONE BUM STAT IS NO BIGGIE BUT ALL MEH STATS SOUNDS LIKE AGONY

And have a dang good time, I really liked exploring this world so thanks to all the work that got put into it. Some of the most thoroughly invested I have ever been. The ratio of awesome to price in this product is phenomenal. Pretty much my first real cRPG and now I love the genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best classic role-playing game experiences on Steam. Highly recommended to anyone who enjoyed the classic Fallout games. It captures the ambiance, feel, and gameplay of Fallout without being derivative.

The writing is excellent with an insane amount of dialog options. Combat is extremely challenging requiring creative solutions. Many character customization options and areas to get lost in. The game also offers a unique character advancement system that encourages exploration over killing, which eliminates grinding. Oh, and there's item crafting, too.

Stygian Software gives you many opportunities to get lost in their world with excellent lore and world building. That being said, it's recommended for those willing to commit and get immersed. This game is mechanically deep and requires an investment in time and focus. The amount of micro-management of inventory space and backtracking add a lot of what feels like busy work. This may be due to my character build as a weak merchant hoarder or a consequence of a complex and open-ended game.

I loved this game and am excited to see what Stygian Software has in store next.\t\t\t\t\t
