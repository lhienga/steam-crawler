\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish there was a "maybe" option in reviews. Whether or not you're going to sink your whole attention span or 5 minutes into the game is going to depend mostly on you. If you like the idea of fallout 1 but want it to be just a tiny bit more accessible, that's a sign you might really like this game.

Not that I'm calling it accessible, mind you--it really lacks a LOT of quality of life features we've come to expect of rpgs (beyond just quest markers). You will very likely need to utilize the wiki and various maps to get to obscure locations that have little to no signposting. You will probably need to save before each encounter just to be sure you're not going to be wiped out, even on the default difficulty. Personally, I'm getting a lot of mileage out of stealth; avoid fights, lay down a million bear traps for when you can't and snipe trapped enemies. Expect to find some method to cheese the game and lean hard into that. Do not generalize stats. Specialize. Become the best of one particular niche.

My biggest gripe is honestly just that the main quest requires you to fight a certain number of fights in the gladiatorial arena, which is not actually told to you at any point and is painfully difficult for people who play stealth or charisma based builds. But this deep, it's a pill I'm willing to swallow to follow the plot through. The writing is a little hit or miss, varying by npc, but on the whole, the plot has me hooked.

If you're still on the fence at this point, you'll probably want to give it a try, since you haven't been scared away by the worst of it. It's been a worthwhile experience, even if it's been a bit of an uphill battle.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI found Underrail an amazing game, it's in the style of classic Fallout 1&2 mixed in with a Metro 2033 setting, topped off with psychic powers if you want your build to have them. Once you get used to the how the game-play handles I think any CRPG fan will fall in love with it. The story requires you to look to look around a bit for hidden details a bit like the Souls like games, but it still interesting to discover all the in-game lore. It's important to note that this game requires you to pick a build and stick with it more or less, if you try to be a jack-of-all-trades and master of none you'll find yourself screwed at later parts of the game. Overall I think the game is amazing and I'm looking forward to the sequel that's in development.

A small final boss/dungeon SPOILER ...

At the end game dungeon, there are certain mutagens to help weaken the final boss, who is very hard to find even on the easiest setting. There's an on online fan made site on the wiki that can help identify how to solve the mutagen puzzle to weaken the final boss; I couldn't beat it without solving this optional puzzle, as your locked into the final dungeon with little in the way of restoring your items outside what you can salvage from the area.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game. While it's incredibly punishing at times, it has this "something" that makes you quickload for 25th time and try to beat "these two ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ that already killed you 24 times". Yes, the games gives you the liberty of creating a build so useless that you will struggle throughout the whole game and EVERY fight with more than 2 enemies would be like a final boss battle. There are some builds that you can find online that are so OP that the game may be too easy on every difficulty level (except Dominating), but I suggest you don't use those, but find out yourself which build is the best for you and gives you most fun. Like I did during my 3rd playthrough, but as it turned out, my character was too weak to defeat the final boss. Well... about 60 hours in with the character and I had to create a new one and start over, but well... That's not a problem, because the atmosphere, some amazing characters that you meet on your way, the multiple ways you can approach some of the quests make you return to this game.
This game reminds me of old Fallouts, but also STALKER games (yup, that's far-fetched, it's a completely different game), in terms of the hopelessness of the world, the feeling of anxiety and that you're alone most of the time and you need to get the best gear, as much ammo/PSI hypos as you can carry to make it to the final stage of the game. Despite dying the milionth time, something makes you reload the game over and over again.
The only thing that pains me is that some quest lines are just too short. For example, Protectorate/Free drones. Yeah, during your your first playthrough it may take you some time to find all the locations/people that are essential to complete certain quests, but if you replay the game and you know where to look for certain things or people, the questlines seem just too short.
And this is a segway to another thing that I love about this game: finding people or places. Instructions that you are given most of the time are extremely vague (e.g. some guy that you are about to kill is south of XYZ base). South means that you may have to travel through 10 locations, killing 50 enemies and maybe meeting some interesting people or finding cool places on your way. Or not. It's a lottery, especially during the first playthrough.

And finally, the endgame. I think you'll know when you are about to get to the final area in the main game. And if you don't then... good luck XD You'll fail miserably, but it's admirable if you try. The last area cuts you off from all the areas that you came to know very well during the playthrough, including vendors and feeling of safety XD. The number of hints and directions comes close to none, with an exception of one character that you may have met before (when you get to Junkyard do what Abram asks you to, this questline is worth it). You need to explore a pretty huge area, and you cannot feel safe almost anywhere (literally, you'll see it when you get there).

To sum up, the game is totally worth playing, winning some of the fights (like random bandits, but there's 10 of them and you have to fight them all at the same time) is extremely satisfying and rewarding. Some quests are just amazing, sometimes hillarious. I strongly recommend this game to everyone who loves oldschool RPG mechanics, has no problem with lack of quest markers and sometimes extremely unfavourable RNG.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is for a subset of a subset of people, it's unlikely most people on average would enjoy it. If you like turn based RPG's, and very specifically like to play those with only a single character through a run, then you might like this. I'd recommend being very aware of the return window if you want to try this game out.

Personally, I just reached a point where I could have kept going out of an addictive need to beat this game, but I'm going to give up as it's a real slog and I'm not enjoying my time with it at this point. I'm playing on normal difficulty and it just doesn't seem to be balanced well. I suspect the excuse is that this is supposed to be a hard game, but I don't personally buy into that. After playing for a while now, there's a few builds I can think of that would skate through likely. (You could probably find a few online also.) To me those builds would be very boring to play through even though they'd get me to the end. I like the build I came up with, but a good portion of the game is just slow going and constant save scumming. Every encounter is you against a large group of enemies, who take and dish a lot of damage. (I'm betting an AR build does pretty well.) Often you'll have to gauge whether the situation is too hard and you need to go elsewhere, or if you can get lucky enough to beat them. A good portion of the enemy attacks have a stun element it seems also, which can be a problem when you only have one player. The second you get stunned it's essentially game over at that point, it's rare you can survive a round after that. Even with high will power I seemed to consistently get stunned every time. I think the main fix here for me personally, would be to allow people to manage a party. It'd also add into the tactical side of this game, managing a group of people with different skills, etc. (This would also help with another factor, the aiming system is straight XCom aiming. Where it can show 70-80% and you can fully expect it to in reality miss most of the time. XCom allowed you to have a team though is the thing.) The benefit of allowing people to manage a party is they can decide the difficulty they want based on the experience they want. If you want a really difficult experience you can go solo, otherwise you could have a party and lean into the RP side a bit more.

Aside from that there's a few other problems in my opinion. There doesn't seem to be too much choice in weapons (even with the expansion), but the game is pretty long, I suspect as I've played awhile and I'm just giving up now. Like I got to Core and Rail Crossing, wondered around a lot of the map. I've spent quite a bit of time on this game so far, and there just isn't a lot of variety to the weapons it seems really. The writing is serviceable but never really hooked me in or made me feel much of anything. I starting putting on podcasts while playing just to keep that part of my brain from getting bored. The UI is pretty bad. Like I get it's trying to be Fallout but it's still pretty bad. Often you'll click on something in the action menu and it'll un-select right after for some reason. Many a time I've had to keep clicking an action till it randomly stayed highlighted. Often you'll miss-click and move when your intending to select an action also. Which is essentially a game over and save scum moment when it happens. I'm also not quite sure about the attribute/skill balancing, I've seen a couple builds where they dumped everything into dexterity and perception and nothing else. Whereas other attributes seems to have a soft cap on their usefulness. It's the same for skills also. There's some skill you want to dump everything into, but other skills that have a soft cap you might not know about unless you research.

There was a lot of great ideas here that I really liked, just they didn't come together very well in implementation, in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t
