\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I found out about this game from a stream a while back and from then until now it has jump up to being in my top 5 favorite games of all time. I personally believe this game has more realistic dialogue, more in depth quests and lore, and more satisfying combat than Skyrim and Fallout 4. This game is clearly a spiritual successor to Fallout 1 and 2. Now the perspective and the turn based combat might put people off but I can attest that the immersion is not lost whatsoever.

One of my favorite parts of this game is it's lack of hand-holding. Of course there's a tutorial to help you figure out the controls but that's about it. After that, you have to figure stuff out for yourself. There are a lot of quests where the person gives you vague instructions and you have to investigate on your own which makes it so much more satisfying to complete a quest. If you get stuck there is almost always an NPC you can talk to who will point you in the right direction but there's no quest marker on the map.

I honestly think the only reason this game isn't Overwhelmingly positive is because of it's difficulty. Sometimes it can feel like enemies just one shot you and there's nothing you can do about it, but if you just look closely there's always a way to defeat an enemy.

A couple points of advice:
- Allocate at least 10 points or so in stealth because otherwise every enemy will spot you from far away even if you stealth walk
- There are two types of exp, oddity exp which encourages exploration and classic exp which encourages killing enemies and allows you to farm. I personally like classic exp better but it shouldn't effect game play too much.
- Get lock picks, a hacking tool, and a multi tool the second you can buy them.
- Don't go on the expedition too early if you get the DLC

Other than that you have complete freedom when it comes to how you build your character and what decide to do. Good luck and have fun in the hostile cesspool of fear and despair that is the world of Underrail :].

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderrail is yet another entry in the proud lineup of "spiritual successors that don't get it." In this case, Underrail very clearly wants to be Fallout. You've got the isometric view, a similar-ish mechanical setup, the same sort of menus, a post-apocalypse(albeit grey rather than sandy), green mutants, barrels everywhere, etc.

The main difference is that Underrail is really bad, and I have a hard time figuring out where to start because everything seems to have a mistake of some sort.

Starting with presentation: Music's good, verging on great in places, but sound effects all sound like they were recorded at the bottom of a well and gunfire sounds like firecrackers on the other side of an apartment building. The world unfortunately tends to look pretty unimpressive outside of one or two areas, instead just being grey, grey, grey, bit of blue, grey, green where there are mutants. The presentation is occasionally even disruptive to gameplay, darkness and foreground elements that serve no direct gameplay purpose often get in the way of seeing what's there(even important stuff like area transitions) and sometimes get in the way of interactibles.

Mechanically... where to even start. I think that what I'd prioritize is the insanely swingy difficulty with very limited guidance. The game will happily start out by giving you a sidequest which you should DE-prioritize until you're a good bit into the first storyline missions. There's no re-speccing, yet you absolutely NEED to figure out what the broken options are and specialize into them. Be prepared to either read build guides or just plain restart several times, and the game does not have enough randomization to make the latter anything but a miserable chore.

Part of the problem is that some skills that stop being useful after you've got enough for everything in the game(hacking, mechanics, persuasion, intimidation) have no clear indication of when you've "capped" them and can stop wasting points on them, while other stuff(psionics, combat abilities) just keep scaling as long as the game goes on(yet at the same time I had no idea what was ever ENOUGH at chargen.).

The crafting system is semi-opaque without the wiki and has a couple of recipes that are absolutely necessary to play the game, and a ton of others you'll never want to interact with.

The biggest issue, though, aside from all the other stuff, like how I think I hit maybe ten Persuasion checks in all my hours playing the game, is all the absolutely empty space and wasted time. Unlike Fallout, there's no world map, you are travelling every single step manually, and it's TIRESOME. Entering areas, the overhead map doesn't show exits until you've used them either, so it's easy to miss that you didn't take a turn-off somewhere later in the game. Enemies also occasionally respawn, adding to the tedium. I just felt like I spent most of the game walking slowly and beating up rats, rather than actually engaging with combats and encounters that required me to engage my brain, pay attention and make decisions. It doesn't help that most of the screens are remarkably tiny, forcing you through a loading screen just to enter a broomcloset with a door on the far side.

One of the worst examples of this was a... I think it was a ten-floor dungeon that turned out to have no payoff unless you were playing on the hardest difficulty(I thought I was missing something and checked the wiki to discover this), but still lets you enter and waste your time, even greeting you with like fifty loot containers that are empty at the very end just to mock you.

Among other tedious things is the "Oddity" XP system. At first it sounds great, you get XP for finding stuff rather than just killing things. And you think "oh boy, that means I won't miss out on XP by dodging encounters and stealthing past stuff!" but you will, because while SOME Oddities are hand-placed(I think about 50% of the ones in containers), Oddities are ALSO random drops from enemies. So it just means you have to BOTH kill everything AND scrounge through every pile of trash and boxes for more XP.

I also hate the cutscenes, they are all SO insanely slow-paced and I usually went to read a book or make a sandwich before they were even halfway done. Similarly the game slows to a murderous crawl during huge fights, sometimes it literally takes two minutes for all the NPC's on screen to take their actions.

Part of what really took me out of it, though, was the lack of payoff in multiple ways. Going through a ruined facility in Fallout usually meant a combination of finding loot and finding lore, both of which made it a rewarding experience. Underrail almost never has any lore in its ruined facilities, which just makes them a pile of rust and corpses, and makes it hard for me to care and be excited to find more of the story somewhere else. Likewise, 90% of the loot is just vendor trash or consumables. Actual gear upgrades and choices are insanely rare once you stumble on a couple of decent things.

Most characters also just appear for one quest line and then not really much again, so I never really felt connected to anyone or cared about their personal stories. Everything in that regard feels kind of abrupt and it rarely felt like my choices had long-term consequences or effects on the world. Even joining factions didn't seem to affect how others in the gameworld reacted to me or bring me much into conflict with the other factions in ways I wouldn't otherwise, I think I encountered ONE example of that.

Oh and the water travel is just rancid. Insanely bad, because once you're on a jet ski, the jet ski's carry capacity determines your movement speed, not your own carry capacity, and it seems to be next to nothing, on top of the basic jet skis being super slow on their own. So you either have to leave 90% of your inventory on the dock or spend half an hour crossing the screen even at 5x speed.

It's also a game where you are only ever one character, but the game LOVES to throw enemies with stun abilities at you, so most fights turn into a battle of rocket tag where whoever stuns the others or takes out their ability to stun first wins, making the "you can't be stunned"-feat pretty mandatory in my mind.

There are also weird decisions like psionic power not regenerating naturally but instead relying on consumables entirely. After a certain point it felt trivial to have a huge stock of them, so it mostly just felt like it added more annoyance rather than actually affecting the gameplay. Everything you wield or wear also has durability, but not low enough to make you be careful about using them, just enough to clutter up your inventory with repair kits and make them get near breaking once every ten hours of gameplay.

Also not sure why recharging and reloading gear has a timer outside of combat. It just makes everything slower, wasting even more of my time.

So yeah this game is bad. Don't play it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t40 hours in and I'm in the final area, and this is the only part of the game that I feel is kinda bad. The rest is absolutely amazing. The soundtrack is brilliant, gameplay was really fun (I quite enjoyed the 'Oddity' XP system, where you level up from finding 'oddities'). I'd probably say that the only thing I'd like to have seen different is for what you're wearing to show up on your character more. Currently, every type of weapon has a set default look which universal for that type of weapon, e.g., every sniper will look the same, even if the icon for it is very different. The only thing that varies is the main armour you wear which does look different, every other piece of equipment you wear is hidden and not seen. Probably the only thing I'd really like to see different in the next game (apparently a sequel was started in 2019, although progress seems slow).

Overall, for £8 with the DLC (which I have not even played yet), fantastic game. Only let itself down in the final stretch and admittedly it has put me off wanting to complete it but either way, the prior 40 hours was incredibly fun. If you like classic isometric turn-based games then you'll love this, feels a lot like Fallout 1/2 to me.\t\t\t\t\t
