\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom the 21 hours that I have played so far, I can tell you that this game is by no means easy. In these 21 hours I've played 3 builds, the first two failing once I hit Depot A (End of the prologueish). The game is quite long from what I've seen, but it's definitely pretty great if you enjoy the old Fallouts since it definitely has similar enemies - seeing as for most of your time in the Underrail you will be fighting either humans/animals, monsters or mechanoids of different varieties.
The game has quite an interesting crafting system which rewards the player ( i.e. crafting yourself a tac vest earlier on and saving yourself a lot of trouble for the first part of the game). The XP systems are also interesting, yet I must admit that I've only chosen oddity, because it rewards exploration and it also provides you with plenty of tiny tidbits of lore from all of the different little trinkets and baubles you'll be collecting in your adventures through the Underrail. There's a variety of weapons and armors that you can try out (and more importantly make a build with) such as: Sledgehammers, pistols, SMGs, Swords, Spears, Assault Rifles, Shotguns, Grenades, Sniper Rifles, Crossbows, Traps, Knives, Punching Gloves, etc.
In terms of Graphics, the game looks quite good if you enjoy its particular style and while the entire game takes place underground, the lighting makes each hallway, compound or tunnel look interesting and unique to an extent.
In terms of Story, the game definitely leaves you wanting more at every step - and I mean that in a good way. The story kicks off a little slow in the start, with you as a regular station boy/girl having to help your station recover after a massive earthquake, yet it quickly picks up as you delve deeper and deeper into the Underrail, uncovering mysteries that might've best been left to rot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell. If you are looking for a great Isometric Roleplay Experience like in the old days (Fallout, Wasteland, old Shadowrun, etc.), than look no further, because Underrail might just be the right game for you. It does not come without its flaws, but if you are willing to accept them, you will be taken on a wonderful journey.


Pros:

1.) Story
2.) Lore
3.) Setting
4.) Immersion (right from the start, until the very end)
5.) Soundtrack
6.) Possiple Builds
7.) Most Gameplay Mechanics and Rules
8.) DLC "Expedition"

Neutral:

1.) The amount of combat and the lack of RP opportunities in a lot of situations.
2.) You might need a few restarts, because it is very easy to ruin your build and with that your gameplay experience.

Skills like Persuasion, Stealth, Mercantile, Intimidation are nice, but ultimately it all comes down to combat. And when you don't have a good build for that, it's better to just restart the game. Really. (I had very high stealth and persuasion, but still needed 10k bullets at least for that one playthrough)


Cons:

1.) Crafting, compared to merchant items. And the lack of item scaling in containers and such.

If you really wich to get the best out of your build, crafting is absolutly essential. Even if you just want to make traps, bullets grenades and consumables. And when you have the skills for the good stuff, like weapons and armor, it is a pain in the ass to find the right resources. Even in endgame areas there is no guarantee. I really wish that it wasn't so random and that your skillls had a little more influence on it. Your other option is to run from merchant to merchant like a total manic. *Urgh*
At that brings us to the second big Con.

2.) Carry Weight

Ok, i get it from a roleplay perspective. But seriously? Where is the fun in spending more time going through the inventrory than being somewhere else? And i'am just talking about the things you absolutly need to survive. Like bullets, grenades, potions, maybe traps, weapons and so on. There is very little room for the rest and that's just poor game design.


Conclusion:

Just make sure of your priorities. The main focus is definitely on exploration, with combat not far behind. And with a little bit of distance the real RPG elements come into play. So, if you accept that, nothing is holding you back. I might have to mention the difficulty of the game without the right build (played on normal and hard, but there is an easy difficulty as well). Gameplay is at least 100 hours, more with the DLC. So if you like the genre, or wanna get into it, don't think twice. Grab the base game, the DLC and begin your journey.
