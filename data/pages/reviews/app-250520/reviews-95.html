\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderrail is an amazing game, although there are some complaints people have. Some of those are related to things like carry weight or the combat being annoying due to things such as enemies kicking your ass unless you know what to bring to fight them. Personally, i don't have a problem with that sort of difficulty since it's mainly reliant on your experience rather than the game throwing ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ enemies at you.
The dialogue and characters in this game are also amazing. Personally i have very little complaints about the game and those are usually when i get ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ CRIPPLING STRIKE stacked on me for 10 turns straight by a mugger, preventing me from doing anything due to the heavily overburdened debuff i get. If you're interested in the game, i'll give you a few tips on how to enjoy it.
1: It's normal to die in this game. Just remember that you should reconsider your strategy, or try something different rather than doing the same thing and hoping for RNG to work in your favor.
2: Put points into crafting. Seriously. Things you can craft are usually better for you since you know how they work.
3: Use Oddity XP. It may be annoying for a bit, but it takes the focus off of enemies and makes you able to skip combat without losing much.
4: Explore, but know when to turn back. There are some great places you can find with good loot in them, but if you start going into 1-turn territory, then turn back and either reconsider your gear or look for oddities and side quests to level up.
5: Don't stay in Depot A longer than you need to. Seriously, when an NPC calls that place Hell, he's being honest. It will suck your enjoyment of the game out of you along with your soul.
6: Explore Core City, but avoid combat. Most enemies you find there will probably 1-turn you if you visit it immediately after it opens up.
7: Don't sell things in SGS past the early-game. SGS credits are like monopoly money, and as such are absolutely ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ useless anywhere outside of SGS, and the exchange rate for them at Junkyard is absolutely terrible.
8: Specialise. Know where to put your points. Lockpicking and Hacking come in handy at any stage of the game, but if you're going for a non-psi playthrough, putting points into any Psi stat is just a smooth brain move.
9: Immerse yourself. Your enjoyment of the game will increase exponentially when you take breaks and chat with the NPCs. Their writing is absolutely amazing and you're simply denying yourself more fun if you ignore side characters and only talk to quest NPCs or merchants.
I've gone on a pretty long tangent. As such i'll be finishing it up here.
The current stacks of Crippled i have on me/10. Amazing game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game and I never even finished it. Yes, 1579 hours in, and haven't completed a single playthrough. That should tell you something about how immersed you can get.

If you liked the best isometric games from the 1990s - Fallouts 1 + 2, Planescape Torment, Arcanum, Baldur's Gate 1+2, then you will probably love this...provided you enjoy challenging gameplay, or are a masochist.

The learning curve is steep and you may find it punishing. If you allocate your character points "incorrectly" it makes it a lot harder than it should be, and you'll have to resort to savescumming.

That said, the gameworld is deep and the dialogue is excellent. Eastern European (well, southeastern European - the developer, Stygian, is Serbian) so you know it won't be laden with all the ideological trappings that compromise most Western games these days.

Definitely get it with the "Expedition" expansion pack, which is some of the best content in the game (the most punishing, but also the most wonderful).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game drives me mad and yet I can't stop thinking about it when I'm not playing it. It's probably the greatest Fallout's spiritual successor out there! The aesthetic, the world itself, the gameplay, it's all here. It's good old Fallout, but more polished. But, much more linear and unforgiving. Your first build will probably suck and softlock you at the so-called 'Depot A'. God knows mine did. You really need to specialize and take all of the game's system into account when playing anything other than a assault rifle tanky boi. It also feels much more linear in terms of it's story, there's not much to roleplay in terms of making choices in the story - though it's still there. It's just more about the strategic gameplay and expressing yourself through your build, really. One thing I really didn't liked though was the fact the game doesn't try as much as it could to teach you it's systems. You're gonna be wiki diving *a lot* and the wiki's not that good either - not that it should be, this one's on the devs. This complaint is also about the way it handles quests - the quest menu uses some very vague directions and there's not dialogue log. That means that you can get youself lost with ease unless you're really paying attention and keeping notes.

The bottom line: A Troika's Fallout-esque hellishly addictive experience.\t\t\t\t\t
