\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game very quickly took over my life. I haven't beaten it yet and I won't gush over it too hard so here's what I like about this game:

- Huge amount of items, equipment, weapons
- Simple but expansive crafting system
- Lots of character options and powers
- Very open game structure - easy to wander off, get lost, make your own adventure
- Combat system can be punishing but greatly rewards good positioning and tactics, clever thinking, and using the right tools for the job - feels great once you get the hang of it and then you're the one doing the punishing :)
- Lots of freedom in how you approach/avoid combat
- Lore and story is there if you want it but focus is on exploration and combat - very few cutscenes or long dialogues to sit through
- Doesn't hold your hand in more ways than one - quests for instance won't have objective markers and often you'll have to remember verbal directions given by an NPC (they usually are NOT written down in your quest log or anything)

A few nitpicks in the interest of fairness:

- The not holding your hand, "remember what the NPC said" mentality might be painful for some
- Haven't encountered too many compelling characters so far, but like I said story and characters take a backseat and are more just there to deliver you to the meat of the game (I also haven't dug into too many dialogues, more about lighting stuff on fire and snatching dudes in bear traps)
- Finding specific components of decent quality can be a real pain - you have the skills and 3/4 of the things you need to craft something and just can't find that last damn piece because of compounding RNG
- Item encumbrance seems right for this kind of game but sometimes breaks up the pace of play while you play inventory Tetris (PROTIP: Be very judicious about looting armor off of enemies, usually not worth the weight)
- Backtracking between towns can take a while depending on where you are. You'll finish a quest or need to go to a specific vendor and then have to spend a few minutes walking back to the fast travel point, then walk to the NPC, then go back and fast travel back... you get the idea
-No voice acting (Personally, I like everything being text - very 90's/early 2000's computer game vibes)

If you're looking for an easily accessible or casual RPG with beautiful graphics and grand storylines, this is not the game for you. If you're looking for a nitty gritty, d20 rolling, melting psychic beetles with acid kind of RPG, then strap on your gas mask and get in here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first played Underrail during the beta. While the game has changed significantly since that time (obviously), my impression of the game remains strongly the same.

Underrail is both a conundrum and a masterpiece. The art and audio are chunky and rich in the best way. Stylistically and atmospherically, Underrail is one of the best games of the last decade. While the writing of the base game struggled at times, the expansion Expedition is a narrative coup, and playing through is a philosophical experience.

Underrail is also deeply flawed from a gameplay perspective. This game is hard, which is not itself a flaw. However, notions of "balance" should be abandoned. Some playstyles work far better than others; an exploitative or "power-gaming" approach yields best results, which is not conducive to maintaining the suspension of disbelief during combat encounters and detracts from the otherwise juicy atmosphere and characterization. And, there is a lot of combat. A tremendous amount - trial and error in discovering which tactics work and which do not is essential, which provides the game with somewhat of a learning curve.

That said, Underrail is still immensely enjoyable, and has a breadth and depth of storytelling that is still remarkable to me. I highly recommend that you buy it, and play it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start this review off with a minor disclaimer: this game is not for everyone.

However if you're patient and stubborn enough you'll have a great time with this.

Underrail (not to be confused with Undertale) is a post-apocalyptic CRPG set in an undeground hellscape after oldworld megacorporations nuked the surface into oblivion, forcing everyone underground.
No major storyline of you acting as some kind of messiah needing to save the world form ruin, as the world is allready ruined and you're just trying to survive and making sure your home does as well.

Combat is absolutely unforgiving and brutal.
While trying to make a jack of all trades might work in other games, here you will absolutely be punished for it.
Accidently put a point in the wrong skill? Hope you got a save before you took said skill, otherwise you're better off starting over (speaking of which, don't make the same mistake I made and use more than just 4 saves).
It isn't just recommended that you plan ahead with your character build, it's mandatory.

Luckily the game's forums has plenty of builds that work perfectly.

Crafting is also a major part of the game, more so than other games where it is mostly a minor aspect or an afterthought.
Sure, you could just use whatever armor, shield or weapon you find in a chest or store somewhere, but it'll never be as good as when you build it yourself.

Also, you can craft all kinds of fun stuff you won't find in stores, such as magnesium bombs, acid traps and so much more.

The expansion adds in a metric ton of content, which should keep you busy for slighly less than a week.

In short, if you like Fallout 1 and 2, but found them lacking in challenge, then this should be right up your alley.\t\t\t\t\t

If you can handle normal rpgs I sincerely recommend playing on easy for the first time. I had a bit of a hard time on my first playthough. This game is difficult, however whenever you do something big you feel accomplished, really accomplished. Getting the drills parts is the first challenge and you will feel great after doing so.

Although I do agree some mechanics and features are not well explained during the game, that is a bit annoying. However besides the lack of hand holding anybody should be able to beat this game if they use their brain enough. Hopefully.

If you are downright terrible at games and find skyrim difficult, do not touch this. You are simply too casual, sorry.

I wish all rpgs were this fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimultaneously this game is great and completely broken and infuriating.

I know its a game for "hardcore gaymers" and I should git gud, but the reality is the combat relies primarily on rng rolls to actually win, and aside from a few limited options that present themselves every now and again to complete a quest alternatively, there is very little tactical planning you can do to intelligently win a battle.
I enjoy hard video games when there's an actual aspect of having to get better at a certain skill to advance past an area, there's hardly any skill involved in this game, it is the video-game equivalent to rolling a dice over and over again until you land a 6, and it isnt very fun to do, no matter how much sseth fans and people on the forums will give you for not liking that style of gameplay, there simply is no actual skill to it aside from reading guides (which negates the entire point of playing a video game).

The game quickly becomes one of reloading the same save over and over again, hoping that either enemy placement will be better, their hit rates will be bad or praying that no crit hits happen.
I've reached a completely impassable point in the game I've spent two hours reloading my save from, trying to get past it because my character lacks the required hacking skill to activate a turret that kills bandits in a corridor.
I get spotted, the one bandit intermediately kneecaps me, taking away my combat turn and then another runs from the entire other side of the map in the same turn and stun-lock slaps my character to death with a sledgehammer.
If im lucky I manage to kill the sledgehammer man before he can instantly stun kill me (he's also the one that doesn't move at all from the door you have to enter if you thought you could try to stealth round him like you can the others) but then his two other buddies poke me and my head explodes from the 1HP i have remaining.

The only way i could see myself passing this point of the game is by starting again and making a build that is able to hack the turret, but i also know it's likely i'll encounter another one of these impassable points again and I dont really feel like spending another 8+ hours reloading saves to get past this one hurdle, let alone encountering one that requires another reset to pass.

It's a shame since it is probably the closest game on the market that comes to recreating the theme and gameplay of the original style fallouts, the characters, quests, world-building and general writing are also very good.

If you like turn based rpgs and getting kicked repeatedly in the balls then you will probably like this game.

If you are also able to see how rng is not equivalent to actual difficulty then you probably will not.

You either love it or hate it, simple as.
