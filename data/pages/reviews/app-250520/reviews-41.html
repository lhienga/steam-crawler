\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo those who find the difficulty unsavory:

1. Find a build. Cannot stress this enough. If you like certain weapons or play styles, look up a good build. Wrong builds cannot progress in this game and can lead you to resetting a 20 hour run. The myriad of options doesn't help to nail down "what's important" either. There's only stats that suit your play style and stats that don't, except the social stats which you can safely ignore outside of Mercantile.

2. The start of the game is very rough. You cannot hit anything, you are p1ss poor, and every single hostile creature is stronger than you. There are human enemies right outside the safe zone that you wouldn't be able to beat until much later. And at level 15 I haven't managed to kill a single robot. THIS IS NORMAL. Until you complete several main quests and open up the rest of the game world, you are a punching bag and there is nothing you can do about it. Don't stray off the beaten path, don't explore places you aren't told to go to, and don't get into fights with any human beings if you can't help it. Do your job and get stronger. And then come back and whoop everyone's @ss.

3. Use everything at your disposal, and prepare for every fight. Don't expect to come up to a group of bandits and start blasting to win. Set up bear traps and lure them out, place mines, use grenade combinations (EMP for shielded opponents, molotov for fleshy targets and CC, flashbang to buy time for cooldown and repositioning, HE and frags to end fights before they start). Throw those caltrops. Throw nets at melee opponents. Exit the combat zone to reset your cooldowns. Eat the food, drink the potions, inject the syringes -- don't "save" them for your marriage.

4. Learn crafting and master the economy. Level up Mercantile, fix up guns and sell them, know which merchants to sell items to, pickpocket everyone (you can pickpocket someone infinitely, just wait a bit for it to reset every time), break every door and cabinets if no one's looking. Level up crafting skills and start making your own nades, traps, bullets, weapons, armor. This will be slow at first, but save up that library of blueprints and hoard the materials, you'll be opening up that crafting menu very often soon enough.

5. If you're stuck, don't be afraid to look up guides, wiki, and ask people in the Discord. The quest design isn't the most intuitive -- you are often told to just "find a way" and then scour the map to find that one NPC that you could never think to be relevant, or told to go to a place that you have no way of getting to without completing other quests. You may get lost, the map is not that helpful on a micro level, so bust out the wiki pages on tunnels and caves so you don't end up where you don't belong. Enjoy the quest and narrative and dungeon crawling and puzzle solving, but never let it become a chore or frustrate you -- you'll miss out on the good parts and get hung up on bad patches.

6. If you're not a hardcore vanilla freak, find out how to get infinite inventory and speed hack. It'll suck out the game's tedium so that you can enjoy a backtrack-free and non-sloth gameplay.

When this game came out 6 years ago, I quit after an hour after dying to a starting enemy. I had the same sentiment as many of the negative reviews here.

Last week I mustered the enthusiasm to play, and 70 hours went by faster than I realized. It's a damn good game, and I hope you'll come to agree with me when you've learned how to maneuver around the game's minor faults.\t\t\t\t\t

A sequel is in the works and I cannot wait. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have a very love-hate relationship with this game's combat. It feels like you're perpetually under-leveled, under-equipped, and outnumbered. It's by design, and when it's not making me pull my hair out and completely re-think how I've been approaching combat, it's something I very much appreciate. Nothing is quite as satisfying as a half-baked plan coming out perfectly.

I've got two examples, one from the early game, and one from the not-as-early game. I went the wrong way out of South Gate Station, into the rails and not the cave. I wanted to progress through an area that had four rathounds, but couldn't dispatch them quickly enough. Instead, I snuck into a nearby, abandoned security station, put a bear trap in the doorway, and shot one through a window. Bottlenecking them made combat much easier, and I felt like a giga-brain for figuring it out on my own.

The other example is one I just dealt with; the four Black Eels gang members beneath the Old Junkyard. I'd fallen into a tactical rut at this point, relying on tanky armor and molotovs to lure enemies together then hit them with DoT and potential fear. It was really great for when enemies bunch together, but I'd gotten pushed back to the map entrance, which was a bottleneck in the wrong direction. I was pushing through a funnel to try and take out one or two guys with grenades, then running away to lick my wounds. Progress was very slow, and the two ranged enemies were giving me trouble. Then I remembered some barbed netting and a flashbang I found, and my half-baked plan came to fruition. I incapacitated them both, tossed the barbed net on the tankier one, and hit him with a burst from my newly-minted rifle. Never underestimate the power of burst fire against an enemy that can't dodge. The other guy was easy pickings afterwards.

TL;DR - The combat is challenging in the right way, and will reward clever thinking with lots of precious, precious dopamine. The atmosphere and music are also perfect.\t\t\t\t\t
