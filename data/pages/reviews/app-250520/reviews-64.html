almost 40 hrs in. the portrait is neat (lit in real time and color)
couple issues.
figuring out the skills( tailor, and mech could ve been 'engineering' while bio and chem could ve been 'science') specially for crafting; it would be nice to know what skills ll be required before downloading the blueprints instead of finding it out when checking each components.
another one on the crafting, sub menus like the inventory (gear, utility, consumable etc...)would ve been perfect instead of the single scroll down list.
no animation for climbing and the character teleports (though that turned out to be a blessing since there is no running; i m not sure i want there to be with all the traps i walked into) .
still very brutal (not a major issue except for 'a bit' of save spam) especially early on. i m still using my first character but here is an example encounter that happened earlier this evening. roll initiative; they get first turn; before i can do anything, i m crippled, kneecapped, poisoned, entangled, bleeding, off balance and stunned with 05/192 hp. luck is a big factor in this game.
it s a cool game. i feel like this first playthrough may take a good long while.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Refreshing Take on Post-Apocalyptic RPGs

Rating: ★★★★★ (5/5)

Underrail is, without a doubt, one of the best post-apocalyptic RPG games out there. What sets it apart from the crowd is that it doesn't merely mimic the Fallout series but offers a refreshing and original experience in terms of both its storyline and RPG mechanics.

The game excels in the realm of spells and character optimization, providing a wide range of possibilities for players to experiment with. I've played through Underrail twice, and each time I ended up with completely different character builds and strategies for engaging enemies. This level of customization and strategic depth is a hallmark of a great RPG.

The story, while not groundbreaking, is solid and engaging. Underrail boasts a diverse array of enemies and presents them in various intriguing locations, each filled with secrets waiting to be uncovered. This world is brimming with enemy variety and hidden surprises, making exploration an absolute joy.

In conclusion, I wholeheartedly recommend Underrail to any RPG enthusiast. It's a game that not only captures the essence of the post-apocalyptic genre but also brings its own unique flair to the table. If you enjoy deep character customization, engaging combat, and a richly detailed world, Underrail is a must-play. Don't miss out on this hidden gem.\t\t\t\t\t

Anyway, Let me start off by ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ about it. There's a bunch of quality of life crap that would have straight up improved the experience. Elitists can sit on it and spin, it's the truth. The midgame is full of rubber banding, in that you'll often just run into enemies that you're absurdly underleveled for with no way of knowing that (outside of dying 5 times and getting the picture). I guess a little red skull above their heads to let you know would make the game too tainted by modern conventions, right?

No respec either. No reason to not have it. 0. Allowing you to have another crack at your build without having to play through 10 or so hours of ♥♥♥♥ you've seen? Yes, please.
It’s super easy to ♥♥♥♥ yourself out of a major questling because of a single choice, etc. It’s just that type of game.

The combat UI is pre-historic. Given that the game was developed by 4 war criminals hiding in a bunker in the outskirts of Kosovo, this is to be expected. Still kinda annoying. Compared to other, more polished recent CRPGs you will definitely feel a little lacking in the information that's given.

That's another problem: information. A lot of the core mechanics of the game are completely obfuscated to you unless you look them up online, which I recommend.

The various ways the game's systems interact make combat, stealth, preparation, crafting and exploration a blast. It's hard as hell to get your build working, but when you do: oh boy is it awesome: leaving traps on patrol roots, special crossbow bolts that can stun, explode and do all sorts of Geneva suggestion violating things, magnesium bombs, acid traps that root enemies in place, throwing nets over dudes with knives and laughing as you riddle them with bullets (that's for the crippling strike spam from the least reload, ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥), walking through mines, and gunfire in a giant metal suit, exploding peoples heats with your fist, exploding their heads with your mind, leading packs of wild animals into corridors and stabbing them to death with spears, I can go on and on.

Story and atmosphere are grim and gritty like a Soviet panel block in late autumn. Good ♥♥♥♥. Roleplaying is honestly not the game's strongest suit, only because of how dominating the combat is. It still benches, like 3 plates aside, so yeah.

Try it out, have fun, realize the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ making the build is playing on 3 con because he knows this game better than his own mother. Try another one. Dominate, kill pipeworkers, drink mushroom booze.
