\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely insane effort from such a microscopic dev team, this is a phenomenal case of worldbuilding and strategic combat that builds out everything I wanted from an isometric fallout successor, while also having the longest playtime of any CRPG I've played (1.8% of players have actually even beat the game according to steam achievements).

But be warned, this game is brutal, certainly the hardest game I've ever played, making something out of yourself in this bleak setting is going to require you to fight for it and choose those fights carefully.

Also, too much of a wiki game in some places, it has that open-ended quest design that relies on picking up very small hints that make you feel like a genius when it does work out, but result in you spending 2+ hours walking around trying everything and ultimately capitulating when the hints don't click. Just go on the wiki if you don't get something, they try to hide spoilers, but make sure to at least give the hard quests a shot on your own, because some of them easily beat classic fallout's best.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR The best fallout game of the 21st century (so far)

Wot I liked
- Good, witty and funny writing, great setting;
- A bunch of feats and skills and stats and gear let you roleplay your own tough post WWIII survivor. Your build has a significant impact on how you interact with the world: there're a bunch of hidden stuff, shortcuts, skill checks, stat checks, feat checks, equipment checks. A strong dude might move a boulder, an agile gal might jump over that chasm, a dextrous person will fish that item from a hard-to-reach place;
- Prepare to READ. Anyone who says that Underrail's plot is boring or uninteresting or mundane or crude is illiterate. Through the volumes of text you're going to see this scary world described in all its eerie glory;
- No stupid pointers directing you to the next objective: you gotta read, and read attentively;
- A huuuge multilevel map. Get lost in the sprawling network of caverns and tunnels, sail the underground sea;
- Exploration is the name of the game: head out into the underground world (the above is unliveable) and see how the mankind might turn out after the A-bombs fall;
- Oddity XP system awards you with XP for finding rare stuff in containers or enemies (basically for exploring). You can also play classical XP system (kills & quests);
- Turn-based combat in Underrail is... Swift and brutal: you can decimate foes in 1-2 turns, they can do pay you in the same coin. Or kite you to death;
- There are skills and special moves instead of VAT system;
- Cool quests, and (secret) quest lines;
- Many quests have randomised properties, ensuring that your playthrough won't be the same;
- Random events;
- Expansion: if the base game is Fallout+Metro, then Expansion is Fallout+Water World;
- The NPCs don't mess around: opened the wrong door - initiate combat, went into the off limits room - initiate combat, said something wrong - initiate combat;
- Barter trading system;
- An extensive crafting system where the enemies/monsters drop most of the materials and you're doing your best to find the highest quality parts;
- OST: it's great, Josh Culler is da man!
- Most indoors maps feature air duct system that you can use to stealthy up your ante. Sometimes ducts are occupied;
- Enemies are quite aware of their surroundings and will not let you take them down one by one easily: they will kite you, they will call for help, they'll heal and use their inventory as any decent AI in an RPG should do;
- The effects of inebriation and intoxication are ingenious;
- If you're ever in Railroad Crossing don't forget to have a long thoughtful convo with Dude the local philosopher-poet with mysterious past;
- Oinko is my soulmate;
- Hello-o, real true dominating Fallout 3 for real pipeworkers!
- Expedition: that's one NFT you can trust!!

Wot I disliked

- Oddity XP system has no XP bar. Ok. There's an XP bar, but it's so inconspicuous that I thought that there is none;
- Crafting system can be easily abused to become an underground Croesus (actually difficulties above Normal fix it);
- If you bump into NPCs your character stops: it might not sound like much but I've spent a lot of time walking while overburdened;
- Tactical combat system. It is great, but kinda barebones. Lotsa things are not there: you cannot flank, target bodyparts, backstab, change position, etc. And the game would only benefit from making combat deeper;
- I have never experienced a corrupted savefile but I have had many times had "failed to load the game" - it is kinda scary. Always keep multiple saves, just in case;
- All powered melee weapons should have an on/off switch - you waste charge fighting cockroaches and then have to recharge in battle vs a serious opponent;
- There's fishing and hunting, but, strangely, no cooking. Also you have rags but you cannot make bandages outta them;
- GUI is pretty rough and unfriendly as well;
- The map... One would argue that Underrail has a map. One would be wrong;
- You get encumbered too easily, packrat in me is indignant!

To sum it all up, Is Underrail "innovative"? Not really. Does it have eyecatchy graphics? No, the graphics is barely manageable. Still, it has everything that made Fallout 1 & 2 cult classics, loved and cherished throughout the world, and Fallout 3, 4, 5, etc. a casual shooter with a subpar kitschy story. I'd go Underrail over Bethesda Fallout any time. This game does not throw explosions in your face to distract you from plot holes or obvious lack of motivation. It's deep, it got social commentaries, it is vast, it lives. It is a long game. My 1st playthrough took over 500 hours. However I cannot say that the game is drawn out, long-winded or filled with minutiae or mindless grinding, cause it’s not. You can grind for better quality items but it’s totally optional - the best items are <~10% better than easy to get items or purplez.

P.S. Can I just have Underrail 2 with more of everything and a deeper combat system?

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an experience. Currently in my first play-through as a fire-resistant, molotov hording, lightning quick, smg-weilding goofball. This game has amazing atmosphere, great writing, and does the absolute most it can with the art style it stuck with. Discovering the local cities, hidden bandit camps, and a new-to-you hostile native species of the world is amazing (...terrifying). I have also been having a pretty good time with enemy level-scaling, but I may have been exploring the map in such an order that, by chance, each combat situation I find myself in are very challenging, but fair (some fights are exceptions as they are incredibly difficult, but usually a change in field tactics and use of combat drugs help turn the tides). Looking back on these interactions, the game will CERTAINLY tell you if you are supposed to be where you are at that point, and kick you into the dirt until you come back, more prepared and more geared to put some Ironheads to sleep.

I have played many games with large, branching, geopolitical landscapes (elder scrolls and fallout series come to mind), and I can generally keep enough info in my brain to have a basic understanding of all the local factions, current happenings, and how each community thinks of one another, but I am no means an expert. This games story and factions are so well written and interesting, that it makes me take an active effort to remember absolutely everything going on around me. Small details about skirmishes in darker days past between paramilitary groups you work with, the history of the rise and fall of the political super powers of the underrail, knowing who knows and trades with who, everything. Usually games absolutely jam-packed with details like this make it very hard for me to remember what exactly is going on with who, and I have to take notes, but I have studied the lore of this game as I play solely due to its quality.

The gear system is great, and assembling new guns, equipment and armor reminds me quite a bit of the same system in Stalker: Anomaly - using many different input materials of varying quality and purpose that determine the usefulness/quality of the end-product. It's satisfyingly complicated, but you get the hang of it very quick. Investing in your mechanics and tailoring skills is 100% the best way to end up with the best gear you can find. If you find that 100+ quality nano-composite armor plate, better take it - it would make a pretty awesome addition to a tac-vest if you find the other materials.

I originally discovered this game from Sseth's video, and took interest from having enjoyed other isometric turn-based action games like Into the Breach, and I had NO IDEA what I was getting myself into. I cannot stop playing it, the story develops in such an interesting way that involves such a great cast of interesting characters. Fine-tuning my kit, upkeeping my weapons, keeping my armory well-stocked, and keeping my wallet fat has been insanely fun. The games map is mind-bendingly gigantic. I feel like every few days I play, I find out that there's an entire new level of the underrail with new factions, traders, quests and enemies to discover - it is truly something to behold how stupidly enormous this world is. It makes exploration and paying attention to your environment a treat.

Get this game. Let it kick your ass. Enjoy a style of game initially popularized in the 90's, but minus all the jank due to it's modern-day release date. I have just under 90 hours of constant exploration, quest-completion and relation building under my belt, and I'm not even close to finishing. On top of that, Expedition, the game's DLC, I have heard is over 50 hours long, and I haven't even started it yet. The longevity in which one play-through can last is truly impressive. I have loved this game thus far, and look forward to finishing it, and playing it again some day.\t\t\t\t\t

The "intro" of the game actually lasts much longer than you think, especially with the difficulty. It can take a LONG time before you are actually free to (and prepared to) branch out and explore the game. Once you beat the first set of quests, you get access to the rest of the world, and the game stops feeling like you're just revisiting the same 3 locations over and over.

If you're willing to put up with the intro to the game, it is a fascinating title, but I wouldn't blame anyone who dropped it after 10 hours of going to the same places over and over.\t\t\t\t\t
