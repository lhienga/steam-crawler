\r\n\t\t\t\t\t+ The lore and side quests are so good that it kept me interested and intrigued all throughout the game. I haven't played any other game with post-apocalyptic and cryptic sci-fi lore so this definitely makes the game unique. Combat makes you feel like you're always on the losing side at the start, but once you get to get more perks for your build and craft your own gear, your character becomes exceptional and makes the time you spend learning the gameplay extremely worth it.

- Main quests for both base game and DLC served as introduction to the lore, factions, and locations of the game. But at the end there's no grand resolution to things and raises more questions than it answers. I'd compare it to the Skyrim's main questline where you'll less likely to tackle it in the next playthroughs since the side quests and the immersion completely trumps it.

If you're looking for a combat-focused game with incredible post-apocalyptic and cryptic sci-fi lore, this game is definitely for you. If you like the CRPG story-focused choices matter with grandiose ending more similar to Divinity or Pathfinder, then this game is not it and is definitely not trying to be one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the perfect example of a 'mixed' rating. I played on easy and followed a build from hearing about the difficulty. My interest was mainly in exploring the world, and I was very satisfied with the game for the first 30 hours.

There are a lot of complaints are about the difficulty, however I appreciated it since exploration was much more intense and satisfying. The issue was that despite hitting the 30 hour mark, the difficulty didn't have any pay off.

Narratively, the final zone slows down to a crawl. I walked around for a couple hours, completely lost while enemies constantly respawned (from an unexplained mechanic), until looking at a guide and quickly finishing. Without a guide, It would have taken upwards of 10 hours, assuming I hadn't run out of supplies.

And so there must be a great narrative payoff for this brutal zone? Nope. The game ends instructing the player to do a quest the next game will start with. I'm sure this wasn't initially meant to be where the game ends, but a 40+ hour game ending like this feels terrible.

Since this is often compared the fallout 1 and 2, it's worth noting that player agency mostly consists in choosing how to get to the next quest. There's nothing comparable to fallout's ending narration describing the player's impact on the world. It didn't feel like any of my decisions had any impact.

Given the constraints around development, a lot of this is understandable, and the game still has a lot to offer to any CRPG fan. Hopefully the sequel can improve upon the first.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExplosive pistol rounds, riot armor with automated health regeneration, and electromagnetic shield capable of stopping high velocity impacts and the game still finds unique and entertaining ways to kill me. 10/10

Amazing game, just be prepared for a challenge. Important advice for first time players:
-Best most broken gear mostly available through crafting
-Choose the feats that you want your character to have and use that to determine your stats
-Plan your build and playstyle carefully. Wanna be an energy sword-wielding ninja that can manipulate time and kill 3 enemies in one turn? Focus your stats and feats correctly and you totally can.

IMO the main detractors I see to this game are people that are just mad that they suck. Rock Paper Shotgun, looking at you my dude. Sorry you guys can't handle that games are hard because you're spoiled by games designed for the masses. Add wrinkles to your smooth brains and try playing again.

I digress.

Are the enemies and situations constantly unfair? Yes, but so is me being able to break the geneva convention and essentially commit war crimes every time combat happens. It's the apocalypse, learn how to play dirty. I really do believe we need more games like Underrail, stop expecting every game to hold your hand like a baby and embrace facing impossible odds.

Stygian Software, amazing work you guys, character progression and customization system is badass. Hope to see more updates over time if possible, either way you've created a cyberpunk dystopian masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t
