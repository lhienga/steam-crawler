\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is what you get when people who love CRPGs decide to put their hearts into something. This game is nothing short of amazing and I'm still playing through as my first character, exploring every nook and cranny, finding out everything I can about the world.

Be warned, this is a true CRPG, you're gonna die, A LOT, but it saves so often you get the chance to fix any mistakes or even just avoid what killed you in the first place. I started playing this game after I finished the masterpiece that is Arcanum: Of Steamworks and Magick Obscura and it just feels like they came out in the same year. The depth of the world and characters alone is amazing, but on top of that the philosophy and deep introspection the game slowly brings to you leads to great moments of existentialism and self realization that I've only ever really felt in a few games.

If you're wanting to get into CRPGs this game is a must play, but not as the first one you play, start with the Fallout games, then something with less depth of philosophy like Baulders Gate, then Arcanum, and finally UnderRail. There's a lot more you can throw in the interim there so never be afraid to try every CRPG you can.

Alright I gotta go figure out if I'm even real at this point, I give this game 10 drunk hippies that can bend the fabric of space-time out of 10.

I'm truly excited to see what they work on next. This is not a hard game, it is a frustrating game, since your win/loss is totally arbitrary. You will constantly load and load and load until you get lucky killing an enemy. Yes you can influence a bit by choosing a loadout, crafting better items, developing your character and using tactics.. however sometimes its just luck, roll of the dice, and nothing is more frustrating than seeing your friggin warmachine get beaten by a dog, since your overpowered melee attack missed it once and it hits you 4 times with a critical cripling bite.

It is fun and I spend quite a lot of time already on the game and I am not done yet, however I would not recommend it based on its broken fight mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlcohol is helluva drug.
Let's start from the good and there is indeed trainload of good in this awesome and artistic game. Visuals are absolutely gorgeous, kinda similar to the classic Crusader: No Regret, and atmosphere just perfect. Dialogue is fantastically written, but never overbearing or pretentious. Story is very fun and captivating and lore of the game is just fantastic, especially in the expansion, and you always yearn to learn every bit of it. Combat mechanics are great. Stealth mechanics are surprisingly good too. No skill or ability is a dump and every point in everything matters. Oddity exp system is simple yet excellent solution. Character building system is great, every build is viable. You always get the feeling that the game rewards brain and planning. I generally find the crafting part in any game boring, but objectively speaking it's good, and if you are "the crafting type" I guess you'll get your kick here too. Besides, you just have to be a crafting type if you live in an underground post apocalypse.
The bad(spoilers ahead):
Deep caverns. It seems it's like completely different game separated from from the first 85%. That's because it literally is. That's the first problem. It lacks any meaningful content story-wise and is badly designed in general. That warehouse block in faceless territory for instance, full of worthless everything, though saved a bit by Al Fabet's post-game dialogue. Eye of Tchort debuff even worse. I made myself go through it by convincing myself it's some kind of intentional weird philosophical test of my will to do mindless chores. Whole area just has this "still in alpha" aura and lacks meaningful choices in the Tchortists vs the Faceless fight. I still don't even get why are the Tchortists the bad guys (well, at least compared with other factions you can freely join in Underrail) and mysterious murderers in scary masks which we practically find nothing about, and are being helped by an immortal lunatic who cuts you in half instantly if you don't treat him like the god, good guys. Only motivation for all those chores I mentioned given in the game was literally that you can't find the elevator unless you slay Tchort. Why no jets in DC? I isto vrlo bitno: daj neki nain da ubijem jebenog Sixa ili Tannera, a da igra dalje progresa. Najvie od svega mrzim besmrtne likove u svakom RPG-u. Why make unnecessary changes of mechanics in 6 years old game instead of adding content and all the content up to DC is beautiful and polished? All this game needs at this stage is more content.Also, is it really that hard to commision like 100 extra portrarits in the game? Would help immersion a lot.

In conclusion, one of the best RPGs ever made. Just as good as the Fallout, Planescepe and Arcanum. Needs one more DLC that adds more content and ties some lose ends to be the best.

Also, if you are experienced in this kind of games I strongly suggest you start your first game on dominating difficulty. I made a huge mistake by listening to bad reviews of this game that pretty much all complained about the game being too hard and I ruined my first playtrough by playing on normal.\t\t\t\t\t

Some builds are king at this.
Stealth Sniper 1 shot 1 Kills , which often lets you kill 2-3 enemies , before you get into close quarters. However most enemies spring across entire map like olympian atheletes. At most you have A turn.

Not to mention enemies are often mixed to cover for each-other weaknesses.

It's a completly different kind of game, which does not let you play , unless you save scum and learn every corner of it. Which for someone who's more "hardcore" will definitely enjoy
