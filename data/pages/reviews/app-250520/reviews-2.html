\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor those who miss the good old Fallout 1 and 2 glory days, Underrail is here for you. Story dense, with loads of exploration, nice combat (No VATS, tho. Sorry, no groin shots for you), solid crafting, and a neat dynamic lighting system that helps with stealth and other things.
Besides guns and meele options, you have the Psi users, which are the "mage class" of the game.

BEWARE though, very minor spoiler, but it is helpful to avoid a situation I found my self in, on my first playthroug:
Taking the pill from the doctor at the begining of the game will enable your character to use Psi habilities, but it will also permanently reduce your maximum hit points, to below what they'd normally be. I didn't know about that, took the piils, but never went the PSI route. So, my sniper/shotgunner had lower health than it should.
Not detrimental in any way but it's annoying to not have known it beforehand.

The Oddity XP system is supposed to be the default mode to play. Through it, you gain experience by finding the titular Oddities. A pierced Rathound ear? Some old smart phone? A clipboard with train schedules? All of those give you XP. This encourages you to explore, as rarer Oddities award more XP, but are harder to find, or to be obtained by their now-dead ex-owners.

For me, the Classic XP system is the way to go. You get XP when succesfully using skills (like, lockpiging, or disarming a mine) and killing whatever is alive in front of you. I mean, Under Rail feels like a modern take on the first 2 Fallouts, why not play it more like them?

The Expedition expansion adds tons of new content, practically a whole new side story, and brings new weapons, like shotguns and spears, alongside new feats to use with them.
Heavy Duty adds LMG and granade launchers, and new feats. I haven't played it much yet, so can't give more details. But both DLC are totaly worth it, TBH.

Heck, just the base game is worth your while!\t\t\t\t\t
