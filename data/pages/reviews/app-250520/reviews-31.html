\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is old school in the sense that it has really ancient values.

Conflicting trial by fire design that does not open up and really let you use the breadth of character build options available to you until you have managed to survive incredibly unkind encounters that escalate suddenly and rapidly beyond a player's capacity to predict or respond to them. You go from killing rats to things with triple digit health, incredibly hard to detect explosive traps, and large groups of well equipped bandits VERY quickly.

I'm sure people will try to tell you that this game's story is good, but the writing feels soul-less to me. Characters are flat, two dimensional, characterized by what they tell you is the case without showing you any substance. And if you stick it out, but made the mistake in the dev's eyes of being a girl, a mission where you are meant to work with an armored disguise, you will instead be oggled at and harassed, failing at what you were sent to do.

There's also absolutely no punk or post-apocalyptic flavor to this world at all. I'm told there is fun existential horror content in the late game, but I've seen so much of Core City at this point that I am wholely convinced that players would be morally in the right to do a genocidal playthrough of this game world. There is just nothing and no one of value to engage with beyond some engaging mechanical rpg systems that DO GENUINELY allow you to build some neat characters.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLong review incoming with strange formatting to separate thoughts. I have many many hours into this game. This is not for everyone. I recommend it for anyone to try, but understand that many will bounce real hard similarly to the dark souls series(without comparing to dark souls).

This game is amazing for anyone who likes to go in with a plan for a character due to the number of options of ways to approach an individual challenge/encounter. As I saw someone else say, this is a min/maxers dream game.

The lore of the game prior to the current events of the game are hidden within logs and in some things that only psionic characters can interact with. The game simultaneously encourages exploration with this, and punishes exploration with creatures that you have to almost spec into seeing and are also capable of teleporting because they don't want to be in front of you anymore. At certain points it seems like the developer(styg) was so tired of peoples complaints he just started making enemies overly difficult because he can. That being said the game is really well done. The art style(minus the explosions that were done by a different artist) is all cohesive and movement all looks similarly done.

The writing is decently well done but the story is not going to win any awards and is more of a string of events happening than an a huge story, easier to look at each quest hub being their own self contained story with lead ins to other quest hubs to give you an excuse to go to those locations, very MMO style with most fights happening just because the things fighting you are hostile, not because you had a driving force or reason/stake to do so. This being most obvious in the last quarter(?) of the game involving the final boss and pretty much everything in the accompanying zone.

The other reviews claim this game is simultaneously hard and frustrating and i agree on both of these. You have to use EVERY tool at your disposal without question, your character WILL be a drugged out mess regardless of what you want, you ARE role playing florida man. There are points in the game that you will not be able to do if your character does not have a cohesive build, and even if your character is cohesive and can make it through all of the filters this game has, some of these filters will still make you want to pull your hair out. Much of this game is a meta knowledge check that you are going to fail. I fail these damn tests and i have hundreds of hours into the game. Your first few characters will fail at these filters.

Some of the frustration of the game comes from the descriptions, it is no longer being worked on from my understanding so this is very likely the final revision of the game, the official being the experimental version so that is unlikely to change. Certain skills impacting things you wouldn't suspect, for instance the mercantile skill impacting both the availability of goods from the shops AND the quality of crafting items in the open world which of course makes some skills very much a requirement to get anywhere and makes no sense.

There are soft locks everywhere, so keeping multiple hard saves is a requirement. At a minimum I hard save before each quest I start and each time the game says something along the lines of "if you have anything else to do, now would be the time to do it." because they will teleport you to a location that you may not be able to actually work your way out of.

TLDR: Game is good with some jank and soft locks. If you like turn based CRPGs you may enjoy this one, just...be patient cause this game isnt messing around and will destroy you at the first opportunity.\t\t\t\t\t
