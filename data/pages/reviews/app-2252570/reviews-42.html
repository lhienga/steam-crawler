Here is a (very long) review from a player who is:
  • Loves playing sim and tycoon games
  • Coming from FM22, and played FM20.
  • Plays only on Indonesian league, where most players attributes at its peak is below decent compared to european standards.
  • Playing create-a-club mode
  • Uses a custom DB (5 level Indonesian Pyramid and much more leagues) with :
    • Starting point at Indonesian 5th division
    • 200k+ players
    • 50+ leagues active (at least top3 nations from each continent is playable for the top 2 level, 80% of available asian nation is top 2 level playable and below that is view only)
  • With PC specs as follows:
    • R9 5950X, 32GB @ 3200Mhz, RX 6800XT

The Good
  • 3D match engine for Players is an absolute banger. Improved significantly compared to FM22.
    • Player animation is smooth, way more realistic (their body actually lean, twists etc depending on their required movement), and expressive (had a CB showing his anger by throwing his hands towards my GK that made a stupid pass and regretfully hold his hands behind his head).
    • GK actually do saves by every single part of their body.
    • Tackles and shooting movement is much better, players getting tackled is (most of the time) no longer like a bugged pedestrian animation in vanilla GTA San Andreas.
    • Ridiculous rubberbanding (when defender with significant pace-related attributes advantages or attackers with "winds up opponents trait") like a Need for Speed's cops is no longer happening.
    • RUNNING IS NO LONGER DRUNK, in FM22 my players (in vertical scroll cam) almost all the time NEVER RUN STRAIGHT, in this edition however, their run is actually straight enough to be called as sober.
  • 3D match engine for THE BALL is an amazing improvement compared to FM22.
    • The ball now has A REALISTIC TRAJECTORY where its movement is based on the last contact. No more ball turning and moving along with the player without making any contact.
    • Swerving ball is now much more realistic, its spinning speed is equivalent to how much swerve the player put into the ball.
  • Simulation engine is way faster and responsive. My FM22 save has 160k players enabled and I would confidently say this edition is ~35% faster despite having much more players included, but take this as a grain of salt, it could also be the custom db that makes this point, but overall, the speed improvement is noticeable and much more enjoyable playing my FM24 save than the FM22.

The Bad
  • Realism outside the squad, staff, tactics, and match management is lacking because there is no physical evidence of the facilities other than our home stadium. For example, a club has a very small capacity stadium with terrible condition field, but its training and youth facilities is superb, corporate facilities graded as top, junior and youth recruitment is exceptional, it just don't make any sense because mostly we can't see the physical evidence of this facilities. Making my club has its own training and/or youth complex different with its stadium (like Real Madrid, including after several long saves in FM20) so far is not possible.
  • The 3D match cameras is basically stagnant for the last few years. Director camera has the MOST VARIETY OF ANGLES, but the chance of it chooses angles other than the default TV-like camera is insulting. If this camera was meant to be "PoV of your club's director/president" then it should not have multi-angle views during highlights. If this is actually a 'directed camera', then make it like the way most in-game esports titles has its own automated action-directed camera. I also don't understand why there is no "Player PoV" camera, or better yet we can control the movement of our character during the match if using 3D view. It would add so much flavor and realism inside the game. I sincerely hope FM in their new engine for future titles have this.
  • The game's managerial aspect is getting deeper and more accurate to its counterparts (for example, the set piece coach, set piece routines, and squad planning feature, which is good), but it is mostly graph and text. We are a text and data, not a character. We don't know how our clubs complex looks like, we can't even roam our club's stadium in our free days and/or during/after the match, interaction with players titled "put hands on his shoulders" but no animation or anything. For me, sometimes this creates a certain level of boredom. I want to see my clubs whole facilities in person you know? Since I've worked hard for most of that.
  • Going from amateur to pros gives me some stupid contract issues (or if it just GM features that still stupid). Two legitimately funny (and frustrating) case is
    • one of my player is on youth contract, I instruct my GM to extend its contract, it was fine, but when the actual contract trigger date happens, the news shows the player as "going professional", with "N/A" contract values, and his status is actually a free agent, what the hell?
    • all of my staffs is in amateur contract except my technical director, I instruct TD to update all my contract staff just to find that 60% of my staff never wanting to make it full contract, while the other 40% getting a new full-time contract, with N/A salary for a few years, which when I checked by "change contract offer", the salary values is actually 0. What is this? Modern slavery?

TL;DR
This one has matured in most of its features and aspects.
If you love or want to try a football club management game, this game is worth to buy, even more when it's on a discount or you can get it below the standard Steam's price, since the next FM will be using a new engine and I would sure it won't be as smooth as this one.\t\t\t\t\t
