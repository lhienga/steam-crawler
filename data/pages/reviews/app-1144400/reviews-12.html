For my part, the story is really captivating, we feel well the universe and the Japanese tradition through Senren Banka even if all the elements of the story are fictional as for example the city "Hoori". Who says Visual Novel, says multiple possible choices after the common story. In this game, 6 different routes bring us a lot of perceptives on the course of the adventure of our hero. Of course, it is possible to do the routes in the order, it will not change the way you perceive the game, the choice will be made according to your attraction on each heroine of the game. But you have to keep in mind that some roads will seem less interesting than others, despite that, I still advise you to do them all, you will have a global vision of the game!

A little advice to unlock all the routes, if you are like me who has the laziness to look for them all, a small Google search and hop, it's settled! :)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to give my review and end it with a hour or minute (depending on the type of game) to USD review.

I'll admit, I haven't really played any visual novels. So this was my first dedicated sit down and boy, am I glad I chose this one. The soundtrack, art, and characters are absolutely lovely. I found myself getting sad towards the end of my last romance route because I didn't want the story and world to end. Each character path provides a unique experience either into the character relationships or the lore of the country town Hoori that you're in. I felt the game was really well polished, though the only thing I would change was maybe get rid of Koharu's romance. I know there is a disclaimer that everyone is 18 or older, but it is really unsettling that everyone is still in high-school and she is obviously the youngest, and your cousin. I did play this game with the patch installed, so I'm not sure how this plays without it. But given including the patch, there is SO much content to explore. At times I skipped bits, especially when the main character was talking since he didn't have a voice and I read it faster than it would have been said. I'm sure if I let this auto-play, I'd probably have upwards of 40 hours or more on the game. Either way, I highly recommend this game.

Ratio time!
My completionist: 32 hours/$34.99
Howlongtobeat completionist: 58.5 hours/$34.99

Is this game worth its money? Yes. I read fast, so I'm sure completing all the routes is likely a higher hourly average time, given the results from howlongtobeat's website. Buying it on sale or not is up to you, but either way, you will get about an hour for every dollar you pay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYuzusoft just doesn't miss with their solid "Pure Love" Comedy Galges. SenrenÔºäBanka is no exception and is just another good entry in their catalog. SenrenÔºäBanka compared to the other available Yuzusoft titles on steam leans more into traditional Japanese culture with the protagonist moving to a small village in a rural part of Japan reminiscent of P4 with Inaba if you've played that. Overall I enjoyed all the routes, and each heroine had an interesting enough character arc beyond the common route to read to the end. I think Mako's was my favorite to read, the writers got her gap moe tuned just right especially with her playful personality. I loved the gimmick with the Murasame sword, and the curse of the village this time around. If you have never played a Yuzusoft title they play out somewhat similarly, but they differentiate themselves with varying designs and character traits for the heroines. And then there's always a specific motif, setting, or theme they go with. It's just really all in the small nuanced details that allow individual Yuzusoft titles to stand on their own.

The artwork is great as always with a signature style that when you see it you know you're reading a Yuzusoft VN. The OST is appropriate for the setting with some fun upbeat tracks thrown in the mix as well. Pacing is also decent, not perfect, but wasn't to the point I felt like things were moving way too slowly or too quickly with not enough narrative development. Also as always vocal performances were great, everyone from main cast to side characters made Hoori feel lively.

I mean what else can you say, you get quality when you get a Yuzusoft VN, at worst they're alright, and at their best they're comfy and entertaining. Although preference and what you're looking for also plays a part, if you have certain expectations that aren't realistic you will probably find yourself disappointed. But as I stated before SenrenÔºäBanka is another solid title in their catalog, not my personal favorite Yuzusoft title, but is worth a read for Moege enthusiasts.\t\t\t\t\t
