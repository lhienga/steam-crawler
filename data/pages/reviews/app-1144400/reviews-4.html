The go-to default for moe lovers?
I am no stranger to the name Yuzusoft, and certainly, this is not my first review of a game by said company either. However, among all the titles I have previously played, SenrenÔºäBanka is the only romance visual novel that manages to catch the attention of the mainstream.

Question: What makes this game the gateway for many foreign fans to the Yuzusoft brand? This is a question I aim to answer in this review.

Production Value
Yuzusoft never disappoints when it comes to presentation. This is especially the case for SenrenÔºäBanka, thanks to its Japanese mythology roots. Hoori is a fictional town that is brimming with cultural integrities. "Historical" is the norm: from old architecture to the traditional garments, shamisen strumming in the background. It has a clear distinct identity that stands out from the rest.

Oh my, to my surprise, the music takes a step even further in nailing the theme of the game close to perfection. Words alone simply cannot describe the beauty of Hoori without mentioning the soundtrack. To further illustrate, it is a modern take on traditional Japanese music, and I highly recommend listening to it in lossless format. Of course, voice acting is superb too (if you are wondering).

Story
Two words: poor pacing. SenrenÔºäBanka is a game that is slow to pick up its momentum whenever necessary, fast to end its highlights for no good reason. More often than not, it severely breaks the flow of the storytelling.

For a game about Japanese folklore, the contents on display are often stretched thin to make room for more slice-of-life and comedy elements, resulting in the story lacking any conciseness in its narrative. To be clear, I do not dislike such elements being included in a visual novel (in fact, I am a big fan of it), as it helps to establish the characters, giving readers a reason to care for them. However, there exists a delicate balance in deciding when and how to incorporate these elements into the narrative. Unfortunately, SenrenÔºäBanka struggles to find that middle ground.

On the flip side, you do get a cast of likeable characters that are, of course, carefully written to represent a certain archetype to distinguish themselves. Comedy, for the most part, is entertaining (albeit clich√©) and the heroines are even more so lovable. At the very least, it hits all the right checkmarks (as a romance visual novel) with their target audience.

To summarise, if story is the least of your concerns, by all means, just go for it. Otherwise, the issues I mentioned above are carried over to the heroine routes as well. It can, at times, feel like fillers after fillers, with the story moving at a snail's pace.


Yoshino:
I don‚Äôt hate it, but I expected more. Especially, after revolving the entire plot around her in the Common route. It is a bit unfortunate that the route ultimately did not provide much closure to the main story itself.

Precisely because the Common route had already reached its conclusion, there isn't much left on the table for content. Still, I would not consider this as a bad route. Underwhelming? Maybe, but not bad. For the most part, the theme of personal growth is the centrepiece of the storytelling. It demands Yoshino to come out of her shell and face her own set of obstacles along the way.

Romance is great. I do enjoy the chemistry between Yoshino and Masaomi. It can be silly at times, due to how socially awkward Yoshino is in expressing herself. It helps to establish that romantic relationship between the two.

Of course, you do get a series of climaxes near the end, in which (finally) the main storyline has once again shown its relevance, tying in with the theme of the route.


Murasame:
One of the things I liked about this route was its heavy involvement with the other cast members. It recaptures the essence of the Common route, as an extension to the main story itself. Displaying the aftermath of the Common route in full.

Simultaneously, it also taps into Murasame's own mental fortitude as the only non-human heroine. A being who has outlived others for many generations is bound to have a backstory to tell and a fair amount of regrets to share.

If I haven't made my point clear by now, protect that smile at all cost! Especially the ending, making it all the more worthwhile. Also yes, Murasame is the best girl.


Mako:
I will say this upfront, it is a mess. Honestly, I lost count of the amount of times I wanted to give up on reading this. "Why" you may ask. Same deal as the criticism I mentioned before, but amplified.

Mako's route suffers the most (when compared) in terms of its pacing. Everything just feels unbearably slow, with seemingly nothing in between. Plotwise, it is also barebones at best, with zero urgency throughout.

There were some funny and heartwarming moments, but "is it justified enough to bear with the cons" is a question I am constantly asking myself. Just to be clear, Mako is not a bad heroine. But, the way how the game handles her character arc is just outright insulting.


Lena:
First and foremost, the game falls short in introducing Lena to the readers during the Common route. She is consistently neglected as a heroine, maintaining very little presence throughout.

Predictably, based on Steam achievements, not many have cleared her route. I wasn‚Äôt even sure what to expect either. However, as I delved deeper, it became evident that this was the spark the story desperately needed.

It offers a whole new dimension of questions to the main plot itself. Creating fresh and new story content that was not possible before. Dare I say, the final missing chapter to conclude the story. Super underrated, in my opinion. Not to mention, it also avoids over-centralising the focus to only Lena, giving others the opportunity to shine as well.

Romance, on the other hand... Masaomi, what happened to you? No matter how assertive Lena is with her approach, my guy is not able to read between the lines. For whatever reason, he became denser than a rock, and I blame the writers for that. It can, at times, feel irritating, to say the least.


Koharu & Roka:
As a side route (dateable supporting characters), the story is self-contained without overstaying its welcome. A short and enjoyable experience. Featuring both Koharu and Roka as heroines also allows for twice the romantic interaction with our main protagonist, as a contest of love.

As expected, the route ends with a choice between the two. Maybe they could, you know, make a third alternate ending... wink


Verdict (7.5/10)
The reasons behind the numerous praises that SenrenÔºäBanka has garnered are evident, thanks to its distinctive setting and remarkably high production value. It is a well-made product that anyone can at least appreciate. Especially when you factor in its low price and frequent discounts. Without a doubt, an easy thumbs up for my recommendation.

‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ Overview ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ
Senren Banka is an interactive visual novel set in the fictional town of Hoori, Japan. You play as Masaomi, a late teen boy who visits the town to help his grandfather run a busy inn, and ends up getting mixed up in a local folk legend involving a magical katana, cursed spirits, and the Yoshino family's legacy of attempting to dispel the grudge that has haunted the town for hundreds of years. Through the process of dealing with the legend, the main character ends up falling in love with one of 6 different girls.

The game is essentially broken up into two sections, the common route (chapters 1-3), and then the individual routes (chapters 4-8). The common route mostly deals with the cursed spirits harassing the town and resolving the curse. In each of the individual routes, the feel of the game changes quite a lot from an action-mystery setting, to more of a slice of life dating sim.

‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ Rating explanations and personal thoughts ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ
‚òÖ Game-play: Being a visual novel there isn't really much to talk about game-play wise. Some decisions you make can affect the ending you receive, so there is at least some minor engagement, but there wasn't any particular time I felt like I recognized myself having fun.
‚òÖ Aesthetics: The aesthetics were quite good, the art was exceptional, the voice acting was anime quality, and the sound and ambiance were appropriate. There was even a few anime-quality cut-scenes that were fully animated.
‚òÖ Story: The story was pretty "meh" overall. Interesting during the common route, but each girls' individual routes were completely hit or miss. There was nothing revolutionary about the story, but it held my attention long enough to want to complete all possible routes. I hated Lena during the common route, but her individual route ended up being my favorite overall. I liked Mako and Murasame in the common route, and then disliked them more in their individual routes. It was somewhat jarring to have my opinion changed on basically every character throughout the full context of the story.
‚òÖ Difficulty: It's a visual novel, there is no difficulty.
‚òÖ Length: It took 10 hours to finish the common route, and 10 hours to finish my first ending (Mako), so 20 hours to get to the credits once, it took 70 hours total to finish all endings.

‚òÖ Overall: I think this is probably one of the better visual novels out there. It does everything well, but nothing in particular was mind blowing. Probably a must play for people who enjoy games like this in general, but not something I would just recommend to anyone.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSenren * Banka is an interesting case in the visual novel space for how to do things well, yet also how to do things badly. The experience within is well worth the price of admission if you're looking for a good storyline in an interesting setting accented by a beautiful aesthetic, but you're going to be sorely disappointed if you come for the characters or the romance.

The common route of this piece is a full arc in its own right. It follows Arichi Masaomi as he assists the shrine maiden of Hoori, Tomotake Yoshino, and her ninja companion, Hitachi Mako, in solving the mystery of the curse casted upon Hoori and attempting to resolve it. It's a neat storyline that progresses at a good pace, then ends in a good spot before branching off into the various character routes. This part of the story is, for the most part, well done. The setting and the conflict fit together well and it's entirely believable that the solution that is used to resolve it isn't discovered before the time of the main story.

The strength of the story carries on through to the routes. Discounting the sub-routes of Roka and Koharu, the other four routes each carry a significant piece of the puzzle regarding how the curse came about and its full nature. I'm a big fan of this type of storytelling in Visual Novels since it grounds the entire story together to be a compelling whole rather than a jumbled set of individual arcs. My one complaint, which is generally a consistent one across this style, is that there is a near required order to the routes that will lessen the impact of the individual routes if you do not follow it. The generally accepted route order that I usually see is Yoshino -> Mako -> Murasame -> Lena, but I strongly believe that doing Murasame's route last is much better for the overall arc of the story, even if it doesn't have the best climax at the end. It's ultimately whichever you prefer for whichever route order you would enjoy more.

The art and music are definitely above average. The characters and set pieces are vibrant and do a great job of setting the right atmosphere for the entirety of the piece. Murasame in particular has a design that directly shows how much effort went into setting the right atmosphere for the story. The voice acting is consistently high quality as well, even among the side characters. Lena and Genjuurou are the two performances that I was particularly impressed by, since their characters felt like the most difficult to properly pull off for how well the performances were nailed. The music isn't top notch by any means, but it was clear that care went into the composition. These two together would comprise around a 7/10 in my eyes; definitely good enough to be worthy of praise, but not so good as to be memorable for months afterwards.

Finally, let's get to the worse parts of Senren * Banka.

While the setting and the story are quite good, the characters are the most glaring weak link. The main character is a jumbled mess, having a very lackluster backstory that doesn't match his personality or actions throughout the story. Each of the heroines have very generic personalities throughout the common route that range from minimal to decent characterization in their routes. Murasame's route features the most characterization of every route, while the other three primary heroines get essentially generic or minimal changes. The characters simply do not make the routes work the way you would hope in a romance story. As a plus, however, the side characters are quite well characterized, which is bizarre since it's rare for them to have a depth of personality when it doesn't feel like the primary characters do.

Speaking of romance, it's very lackluster. This is likely because the characters involved are so weak, but the romance in every single route (including the sub routes) are essentially retreads of the same thing. You're getting the same flowery generic romance story six different times with the differences coming down to the individual way the overall story affects that particular arc. It feels very forced and that they didn't even want to create a romance story in the first place, or that the developers fleshed out the overall storyline of this piece and just shoved romance in to appeal to the masses on release.

In conclusion, Senren * Banka joins the extensive list of Visual Novels that had a lot of potential out of the gate, but simply fall short in an important way. I don't regret buying and playing through this game, and its clear that there was a lot of effort put into it, but this is an experience of driving through a beautiful landscape on a terribly maintained road. Enjoy the aesthetic and the story, but keep in mind that there is a price to pay to experience it.

A short description of Senren * Banka would be "a wholesome eroge". If by that you are intrigued then you should see it for yourself. In this review I'll talk about the setting, cast and specially the gameplay, but I won't actually spoil any story details. If you don't want to know how many options you actually have in the game, stop reading right now.



The actual review

Senren * Banka is a simplistic visual novel, with a simplistic storyline, simplistic characters and simplistic decisions. This does not make the game bad but it doesn't make it that special either. Actually, calling this game a visual novel could be misleading, because you really don't have that many options. Yes, you have different endings and routes, but it's not like other heavy visual novel titles in which you need to make a lot of decisions and explore different scenarios until you reach the end.

This could be almost cataloged as a kinetic novel. The choices are straightforward and you can get into the route of the girl you most want without any issues. For me, this is a turnoff. Altought I really hate when novels make stupid requirements for reaching X or Y ending, I also hate when it's so basic that it makes you wonder why they added them in the first place.

This VN has some good ideas like a Flowchart, saving dialogue lines & so on while also not really using them to the fullest potential. Like, why would I need a Flowchart for my decisions if they don't actually matter in the big scheme of things?

Yes, it's hard to make a branching storyline for a game this long but that does not mean you need to bait me like that. I was expecting much more when I saw just how many options you had at your disposal. However this decision makes sense when you see what the team behind Senren * Banka tried to make.

Story & Characters

Might spoil some minor details about the story.

This game is an eroge. The 'heroines' are basically models with explendid bodies and their personalities are just too pure for this world. All of them are virings who want to experience love, real love, and you are the one to give them that. You also have your all-time favorite cliches like your childhood friend which you haven't seen for a while, your big sister, your 500 year old loli, the princess that is kind but really cold, the servant of that princess that's wife material but doesn't realize it, and your airheaded friend that just too nice to everyone.

Senren * Banka is not reinventing the wheel. Althought the story is fairly original the characters are not and you have seen someone similar somewhere else, specially if you played a lot of VNs & you consumed a lot of manga/anime. Again, this does not make the story or characters bad, but rather expected. It's not boring but you get what you see. They will fall for you the same way you expect.

Don't forget this is an eroge. The MC will sometimes have the luck of Rito from To LOVE-Ru. All the tropes from that series are going to happen in some way or another. This does not make the game less wholesome tho. Even if characters have sexual desires they are kept in check while somewhat still being wholesome. They tend to find their own sexuality and desires in such a pure way that it seems wrong seeing them do it tbh.

This game has no drama at all, no love-triangles, no actual fights between characters. I was expecting some kind of drama but I did not find it here.

Gameplay & Settings

As stated before, this game is far closer to a kinetic novel than an actual visual novel with branching stories. You have decision making but its more like "hey you want to end with girl A or girl B?". Moreover, I think you have a bigger number of decisions in the H! scenes than the others. Those scenes are kinda pointless and if you don't have the R18 patch I think you can't see them. Yes, the game has a patch but to be completely honest I ended up skipping all those H! scenes because I felt bad watching them. For context, I played "Monster Girl Club Bifrost" patched without any issue. The problem for me here is that is like I was watching two lovers have sex, like it's something I shouldn't be seeing.

The game has some nice features. You have a "panic button" that changes whatever you are seeing in game for a photo of your liking, for the folks that don't have any privacy. You also have a lot of options in the configuration menu. I'm a fan of configuration options. I like games to be as configurable as possible. Sometimes is hard for developers to implement these kind of features, but that's mainly because of desing and not because they are actually hard to implement.

You can set the voice of all characters, you can hide some UI elements, you can even change the text size, font and color. The only thing really lacking is the auto-mode because it works like ass, maybe because no one uses it (apart from me T . T). The auto-mode will NOT take into consideration long texts so you will eventually need to pause the game and go back.

There aren't that many effects in the game. Other VNs will play a lot more with the text box. Senren * Banka text box in comparison is too simplistic. This is a minor nit-pick but, for VNs, using the text box in creative ways is a plus and it always amazes me when some developer is creative with it. Just think of Doki Doki Literature Club!. Heck, this VN cant even make pauses between the same text box dialogue so you kinda get spoiled on what the character is going to say next.

Music & Voice Acting

Senren * Banka has actual voice acting for all characters except the MC. That's really cool. Sometimes I find myself waiting for the voice line to finish before pressing space, because I actually want to hear the VA say something silly.

And the music is alright. I have the main theme stuck in my head but that's about it. I'll certainly forget it after a few weeks.



Overall

This is a nice, wholesome game, at least for Japanese media standards. Yes, there is ecchi and xxx scenes, but it's still between two loving and consenting adults.
