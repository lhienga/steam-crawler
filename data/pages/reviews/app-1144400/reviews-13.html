\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just 100% all the routes and I figured I may as well write a review. My "hours played" stat is a little inflated from leaving it on overnight a few times, but I still spent quite a bit of time with it.

I'm no VN expert, as I've only ever really delved into the original Fate Stay Night and Katawa Shoujo. That being said, Senren Banka was a pretty decent Visual Novel, with high production value. A few routes are very good, and I found that my favorite was Murasame's route. All of the main routes (Yoshino, Lena, Murasame, Mako) are well fleshed out- some more than others. Roka/Koharu routes are the least involved, and feels like a side story more than anything.

The H-Scenes- there's plenty of them. I played with the optional uncensored patch. Each route consists of several H-Scenes after the relationships blossom. Personally, I could have done with more character development / character moments than so many H-Scenes, but that's just me! When I say I want more PLOT, I don't mean it ironically.

The main character is dense as hell, and sometimes annoying. The routes are very lengthy, often without real substance. However, despite the flaws, I found that many of the character moments were well executed. The banter between the characters can be very funny, and many of the more dire moments hit with appropriate impact, with many sad emotional moments. Sometimes the feels are there, and that's a good thing. It means I ended up liking the characters, but as a result, I feel a little underwhelmed they weren't developed further.

While I may have many negative things to say about Senren Banka, I wouldn't have 100% it if I didn't enjoy it! It was a good time, and I do recommend giving it a look if you like VNs. Murasame's route had lots of laughs and feels, and I loved it. Playing Senren Banka was worth it for that route alone, as it hit on some interesting subject matter which I found particularly interesting and not often explored. Is Senren Banka worth it? Sure! It's not perfect, but there's no denying that it's a well crafted VN.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my second Yuzusoft game, and by this point, I’m a fan of the company. Why?

Yes, it is a classical tropey tale about a Japanese dude who breaks the town’s Excalibur… er, katana in the stone, and now has to live at the shrine where it was kept and marry the shrine maiden. Of course, there are other girls involved in the mix, such as the loyal ninja girl whose family has served the shrine maiden for generations, the scaredy sword spirit of the katana, a gorgeous foreigner exchange student, the mandatory childhood friend and cousin, a curse hanging over the town they have to deal with… you know, the works.
But in true Yuzusoft manner, they quickly make use of the charming personalities and quirks of each girl, to the point that all grow on you by the end of the common route and you are wondering which heroine’s route to start first.

This is because of how skillfully they weave the tale of Hoori’s curse and how it affects our characters, and how each character represents one part of the solution (my recommended order: Yoshino > Roka and Koharu > Mako > Lena > Murasame) to the whole mystery. And it really is one hell of a tale, delightfully intricate and full to the brim with colorful Japanese folklore. Plus the characters themselves are just as fun to watch in Yuzusoft’s classic “Another View”, a feature I love and wish to see appear more often in VNs, where you get to see the perspective of other characters aside from the MC.

That way, you get to see Yoshino’s seriousness as a shrine maiden and funny innocence as a girl (her achievements are SO. DAMN. HILARIOUS.), Mako’s fierce loyalty and girlish femininity, Lena’s charming naivete and unique cultural mishaps (kudos for the translators for succeeding in properly translating her linguistic blunders, that’s no mean feat), Murasame’s regality and childishness, and Roka and Koharu as the ones to show you the less “arcane” side of Hoori, love the cultural bits they provide.
Plus Masaomi himself, as a good Yuzusoft MC, has real charm, and does not feel constrained by his tropes. He really grows during the story, and has some great dynamics with all the characters, making you cheer for him.

All of this, to the beat of some nice, relaxing music, neatly dressed up with some proper classic Japanese musical instruments that truly give of the vibe a town with its haunted mountain should have.

I seriously wish this game got an anime adaptation. Its tale is easier to bring to anime than Sabbat of the Witch’s (less… risqué scenes to worry about). It has all the right pieces, even if they adapt the story to only follow the “true path”. But if they go the Yosuga no Sora route and adapt all the heroine routes in true Vn fashion… THAT would be interesting.
So yeah, I highly recommend it. Does not matter if you are a fan of VNs or not. Good tale. Accessible for everyone.

What’s that? The +18 patch content? Yeah, get it if you want from Nekonyan. Yuzusoft makes those scenes interesting and have relevance in the story when necessary; though IMO, the story is just fine without the extra spice.\t\t\t\t\t
