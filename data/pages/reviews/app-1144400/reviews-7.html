-This is more translation review than a review about the game itself-
I'm not really sure if I should give positive or negative review. There's no option for in-between.

Senren Banka is an amazing VN, after playing Sanobawitch a few years ago I instantly bought this on its launch day. And yes, the story is amazing, although at the time I writing this review (2023-03-03) I have only play 3 out of 4 main route (Mako, Murasame & Lena), and side stories haven't been touched.
You know I'm glad the title is translated by NekoNyan instead of SP. But I have set my expectation way too high, while the translation is not as fully cringe as SP, I can't help but feel a little disappointed. I drop this a few times, and then back playing it again, over and over again. I'm not sure if I want to continue the rest of the story because of the translation. It's that kind of feeling where I'm still curious about story and I want to see it more and play it again and then when I see the translation, ugh, I can't, I wish I could forget what I see. Yes I am that kind of guy who picky about translation. There was times when I use Textractor and machine-translate it myself directly from Japanese text, but it's not really convenient and it crash or failed sometimes.

So what make me hate the translation?
  • The first one is, SP style when joke/wordplay "converted" (not translated) into western joke. VN are niche game, hardly any normies play this, no need to change Japanese joke or meme into what they think as a western equivalent of the joke, pretty sure majority of the player already understand and familiar with Japanese joke & meme anyway. For example "Chotto matta!" translated to "Stop right there criminal scum!". That's cringe. Also everyone already know who it is, no one force you to translate "Roboneko" as "Doraemon". And there are more stuff like that.
  • And the second one is inconsistency, a lot of it. For example 竹刀 doesn't even have consistent translation as sometimes translated as "bamboo sword", "wooden sword", and sometimes simply as "shinai". And the second one is much more important because it's a character name: 白狛, where in name section it's translated as "White Komainu", but in dialogue it's "Shirokoma". If you have good memory, actually listen to dialogue and read the text, you will notice more things like this.

But other than that, there are also good things that I like from NN translation:
  • Original Japanese name order, they are still "FamilyName GivenName".
  • Original honorific included, they still write -chan, -san, etc. at almost all of them.
  • Using original measurement, they didn't convert it into feet when the dialogue mention it in meter, and also still translating floor originally but with added explanation instead of straight convert it into meter/feet.
Although I have mixed feeling about their choice to leave the princess title not translated.


So what's my opinion about this game?
Game & story: ★★★★★ (only 3 out 4 routes finished so far)
Translation: ★★☆☆☆
I'm not really sure if I should give positive or negative review. There's no option for in between. I wish there's more than Yes or No in Steam review, better if there's separate points for gameplay, bgm, story, etc..\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCould be better, could be worse.

I gotta say that I like the production quality: The UI is great, art is well-made, soundtrack fits well, the voice acting is almost flawless and translation is perfect. It all just looks and feels good. The free and easy to add extra content patch is also nice. At first everything about this VN looks really good.

But there are a few flaws that are worth mentioning. For example, the story. It all seems promising at first. The town of Hoori feels alive and it's traditions play a big part in the story. And up to individual girl paths there isn't a lot to nitpick. However it's only a small part of the story and only makes up like 20% at most and everything after that slowly goes downhill. The first individual route might seem nice, they have a decent plot to them, but every route shares a lot in common. Some events (like canceling an important engagement) definitely happens in at least 4 of the routes, but is only really described in detail in a single one. Not really a bad thing but it is an example of big similarities in the different routes. After some time you start to make connections and after some more time you can kinda guess when major plot twits are gonna happen. And some routes are clearly better. For example Hitachi's route had her own special scenes in the main route and an unexpected story, a big existential struggle, a hobby (something that other girls lacked), an interesting personality, the best h-scenes, and the most interesting story. And it's not a biased opinion, it's fact. Instead like how it is in other story rich VNs, here every ending felt weaker that the previous one. And there wasn't a true ending! I understand that it might be hard to to connect the 7 (or 8) different routes, but not having a final ending just leaves an unpleasant aftertaste you get from the last remaining bad and boring endings. Finishing the last route didn't even give a sense of accomplishment or anything. Oh and the After stories that take place some time after the main story finished. Like 2 or 3 of them really feel needed and in reality all of em are just additional h-scenes. And I gotta say those scenes are kinda lame. Lame as in too vanilla. Out of 20+ scenes that are in the novel, all of it is either a titjob, blowjob or just vanilla action (besides that one foot job with Yoshino). It all is just aimed for the general audience and as a result it's not special. I would have probably enjoyed it more if I had played it as an absolute visual novel beginner.

Nothing really stands out and it all just passes.

It was still fun so I still really recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
