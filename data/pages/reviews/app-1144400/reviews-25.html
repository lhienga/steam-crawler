\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a very lighthearted visual novel, where the most interesting part of the story ends before you get to the routes. It's very slow with filler content that should be fun and humorous but just feels like they're droning because there is no real story to care about. After playing a few of the routes, it feels like any sort of conflict should have been either addressed or happened in all of the routes or be apart of the main story. The character designs are amazing with a good soundtrack to back it up, but the everything does start to sound the same after some time. I'm pretty tired of this incredibly dense protagonist, who doesn't have any real redeeming qualities to make me want to make choices as him. It feels like after the first few hours of the game, the writers didn't really know how to make the routes interesting or have any purpose for the player to want to keep reading except for the cute bits of high school romance. An improvement from their other work, Sabbat of the Witch, but misses the mark for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMINOR SPOILERS AHEAD!

Since this game came out a year after Sanoba Witch (both made by Yuzusoft), I expected some slight improvements and thankfully I felt like there were more than enough improvements.

The most notable was how the main story never lost its focus. It either got resolved during the common route or it expanded on certain character routes, this is a huge plus. Speaking of which, each of the main 4 routes (Yoshino, Mako, Murasame, Lena) were super good. All had something different to offer, all of them had some very memorable moments and genuine surprises. Not to say I didn't enjoy the side routes which felt like a seperate common route, splitting into two choices at the end (Roka and Koharu).

Another big thing is, that while the story was set up in a way that Yoshino is definitely the main choice, the game never forced me to actually pick her (like how in Sanoba Witch it kinda didn't really make a lot of sense to pick anyone else than Nene). Every girl had a fairly even chance, I never felt like the game truly ever showed heavy favoritism towards Yoshino. Truly intimate moments with the girls (which actually had any meaning) only show up on their respective routes. I don't know if character favoritism is a thing in previous Yuzusoft games, but even if it isn't, I feel like this is at least somewhat important.

I really liked the character development, especially with Yoshino and Murasame. It was such a heartwarming experience to see them change throughout the story, becoming much more free and open people. It was well written and well acted.

The MC is very likeable and relatable. Sure he's a bit exaggerated, but I think it was the healthiest thing to do in this game's case.

The music was also great, I liked most of the tracks in the game.

Now for the routes:

Yoshino: When I completed this route, I felt super emotional with my teary eyes and felt like it was one of the most fulfilling story I've ever read. You know the writing is good when all the feels suddenly hit you, and hit you hard. While I felt like some important pieces were missing, the story still felt complete to me.

Mako: While I think her's was the weakest of the 4 routes, I'd stay it was still "good" if not anything else. It was nice to see Mako's viewpoint, her cute quirks and weaknesses and just generally getting to know her better. I think it just simply lacked some of the impact Yoshino's route had if I had to pick on something.

Murasame: For whatever reason, I didn't have a lot of expectations regarding her route, but I ended up liking it a lot more than I thought I would. Since in her case almost everything was resolved during the common route, it lacked some tension... or at least I first thought. While the outcome became fairly obvious soon and lost that impact quickly, I liked it for what it was. Oh BOY Masaomi was an absolute BADASS in this route. I could feel my beard growing from all that testosterone and manliness.

Lena: Seriously, was this the canon route or what? We got to know a LOT more... and I mean A LOT more information about the main story (curse, the past, etc.) in this route than in Yoshino's, which hid a lot of the finer details. As far as the routes themselves goes, there is no competition, this one's the best. It involves the other characters a lot more as well, not like in other routes where most of the other characters become more or less side characters. Here, everyone gets together while Lena still remains in the focus, as she should. Someone mentioned here that Lena might be secretly the main girl and I kinda agree.

As for the characters themselves:

Yoshino: I absolutely loved Yoshino. She goes through the most changes in a positive way, while her charming quirks still remain. Her change felt super genuine and not forced. She was also cute as heck.

Mako: I didn't feel particularly attached to Mako, but she's a more than fine character nonetheless. Her story was interesting and she showed that she's a lot more vulnerable than she lets on.

Murasame: I usually don't like the loli archetype, mostly because I find their personalities and voices annoying, not in her case. She was an absolute sweetheart and her VA did an amazing job so that she'd feel genuine instead of being annoying. Very fun personality.

Lena: Did you say positivity? Again, did I hear you saying POSITIVITY? Sure that's not all there's to it, but I absolutely adored Lena and felt like that she might as well be the main heroine. Of course she wasn't perfect or invulnerable, but she was definitely the most honest and genuine girl out of all of them right from the getgo. She was also the funniest girl, no contest there. I'm pretty sure if you outright hate Lena you're a bad person.

Roka: Like, holy hell she looks hotter than the hottest sun in the universe. I usually don't talk about how characters look, but damn that's DEFINITELY an attractive 2D character. I don't especially like the "Onee-san" archetype, but in this case, it was never overplayed or was overused. Her personality felt unique and very fun, I wish she was one of the main heroines. At least you can romance her so I can't complain too much.

Koharu: With Mako, I didn't feel very attached to Koharu, but at the same time I had no issues with her. She was really cute, but that's all there's to it in my case.

I had an absolute blast playing this. All of the VAs did a fantastic job in general, especially Yoshino's, Roka's, Murasame's and Lena's. I enjoyed even some of the side characters as well, especially Genjuuro. He's pretty complex for just a side character and also likeable. I'm definitely recommending this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR - I've only finished the first route and started a second but I'm running out of steam on this one. A below average VN whose execution is worse than the last title (sabbat) and has a story I don't find enjoyable. Which is ironic as this one hardly touches the school tropes I'm so sick of.

This is my second Yuzusoft VN and next time I won't be so quick to buy another one of their games (afaik there is two and possibly three titles coming). I'm not saying Yuzusoft is bad or anything. I was just hoping for something better and I am cautious now. If I had to choose I'd hope for Dracu-Roit to come out next but I'm not sure the status of that one as the translation exchanged hands iirc.

I found Yoshino's route tiring. Not my fav character to begin with, but grows on you a little. It's mostly the story that turns it into a slog. Nothing all that exciting, no twists I didn't see coming, and ultimately lacking in humor that might make it a bit less wearisome.

Mako's route... made me sad as she was the character I took a liking to in Yoshino's route, but unfortunately her personality undergoes too much of a change imo. That's not to say it's an unbelievable change, just not one I like.

I have yet to play any of murasame's route but I'm not getting my hopes up from reviews I've read.

I'll update this when/if I manage to get through the other routes.\t\t\t\t\t
