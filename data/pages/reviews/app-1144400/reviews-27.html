My perspective on "Senren＊Banka"

Overall: 6.5/10

[Senren＊Banka], in my opinion, has its own place right in the middle of VN spectrum: Not too heavy of a story and not too focused on "special scenes". A leisure read that does not weight down too much on your conscience, but also does not leave that big of a memorable souvenir in your mind either. It could be judged as something 'too average' and 'too cliche', but we all know that sometimes we just want to blast through a feel-good read and enjoy a heartwarming average story. [Senren＊Banka] scratches that of itch pretty well.

Design: 8/10

The strongest part. Nothing much to critique about this, as everything is almost how it should be as Yuzusoft's game. There are some problem with proportion on some of the CGs, but to my non-artistic untrained eyes, I do not see that much of errors. The cast are designed very attractively, as well as the sprites and SD sprites, which constitute the standard charm of most modern VNs.

Story: 5.0/10

In my opinion, the weakest aspect of the whole. I have not read many recent VNs lately, but it seems that the one that I did (post 2015) focused too much on each story segments and neglect to seemlessly connects common route to character routes in a seemless, coherent manner. A lot of stuff happens during the common route and the narrative there is quite enjoyable, while most of the characters' arcs were almost too bland and cliche to the point of being almost a filler. I almost felt like I have finished reading the whole story after the common route ends and there was no need to read any further. There are some routes that are exceptions, in which the narrative from the common route continues to be relevant throughout, but most of them hardly ever answer the questions set in the common route. This really kills the urge to replay the VN after finishing everything.

Another disappointing aspect to the story is that everything feels too "optimistic". The situation dealt a god-like hands to our cast almost all the time, and I felt almost no tension from the scenes in which other VNs with tense situation would keep me on edge (I am talking to you, Kara no Shoujo). I felt that the character does not have things 'at stakes' despite the situation portrayed them to do so, and that made the story felt too bland on the serious side despite having serious tone of lore with Japanese mythical curses, gods, and legend.

Story enjoyment: Lena > Murasame >> Mako >>>>>> Yoshino
- Yes, most of us played the Yoshino arc first, and after finished every arc I was really annoyed by the fact that Yoshino route meagerly, if not least, contributed to the lore of the medium and cursed spirit despite being the priestess route.
- On the other hand, even without a proper elaboration to the curse and the medium, Murasame's arc was the only one who shed light on what eventually happen to Murasame after the end of the curse, while other route completely, or almost completely, forgot about her existence altogether.
- Lena's arc was the reason I gave story score about 5 (average), as I like the symbolism that played there with "The local problem of a conservative, traditional community solved by a pair of outsiders who turned to be the one who care the most". If only there was a ceremony to enshrine Murasamemaru back like in Murasame's arc, Lena's arc would be almost perfect.

Character & Development: 6/10

This pains me the most as there is a lot of potential here, and at one point it almost able to break through the filter of infamous Japanese tropes. What I like about characters in this VNs is that almost all of them started with their own specific archetypes, but later on show several signs that they have other layers in which they could feel like a legit natural character in non-Japanese media. However, those signs quickly dissipates as the common route reaches climax and became almost non-existent during character arcs. Character's portrayal also tends to fluctuate a lot during the latter part, when they either goes in circle or revert back and forth between their developed and undeveloped part. Which makes me grit my teeth in pain for such a missed opportunity to perfection.

All negatives aside, I actually quite enjoyed the (existed) character growth in most of the routes, and the attempt to make several characters cross their archetype, although not 100% succeeded, was charming and refreshing for me. (like in figurative sense, The Coo-dere does not remain only as one, while the annoying small char does not remain annoying or loud throughout.)

Character enjoyment: Murasame = Yoshino >> Lena > Mako > The Childhood Friends
- Murasame and Yoshino received most of the chances to break through their old archetype to become a well-round attractive character with a sense of their former self left as little spice. Although I would slightly rate Murasame a bit higher as her character arc has a much more fulfilling and fitting closure to the story aspect.
- Mako was the one who I felt was the biggest missed opportunity I mentioned above

Music and Voice acting: 7/10

Music: Nothing too fancy, nothing horrible. The most proper term I could say here is "fitting". Everything goes very well with its rural Japanese aspect of the game.

VA: I was prepared to be annoyed by some of the voices judging from the character design, however, everything turned out to be very pleasing. I was actually shocked how different the voice range of Yoshino were portrayed throughout the game, which kind of strange for her character archetype, but all in all, I enjoyed it quite a lot.

Voice enjoyment: Yoshino >>>> Murasame > Mako > Childhood friends > Lena

Extra scenes: 8/10

Yes, we all know why we are here, and I can say with confidence that this is has been done well and upto Yuzusoft's standard.


Summary

Despite talking a lot about its flaws. I am by no mean saying that I do not enjoy this VN. Minus all the negative parts, it was a very comforting, leisure read with cute character design and good atmosphere. There are AAA quality visual novels out there, but sometimes, you just need something easy-reading, decent VN to pass the time. This is one of the top-quality of such type.
