Introduction

The game starts with a main common route. Once it ends, you can choose to finish the game on the route of one of the four main girls, or Roka/Koharu's common route (locked on first playthrough). If you choose to go down Roka/Koharu's common route, once this ends, you can choose once again to go down Roka or Koharu's specific routes. The length of the main common route is around 10 hours, with each route of the main girl taking another 10 hours. Roka and Koharu's common route takes around 6 hours, and their specific route each take around 3 hours.

General review

Story and script-writing

The visual novel intertwines advancing the main storyline whilst promoting the relationship between the MC and the girl. Excellent pacing and content keeps you gripped on the main storyline whilst also keeping your heart throbbing for the interactions between the characters. The script-writing fully captures the charms of the character, and makes each scene feel vibrant and cohesive. It does an especially good job of capturing one of Yuzusoft's best qualities in their VNs: making the girls feel clingy. What I mean by this is that you can really see and feel how the girl falls more and more in love with the MC, with exceptional scenes and scripts along each step of their developing feelings.

Voice acting

The game is fully voice acted save for the MC and narration. I believe this is the best combo as it helps immensely in the world-building whilst also not breaking any immersion. Each character is brought to life by VAs that capture the essence of their character, where emotions are conveyed wholly and impactfully.

H Content

As with most VNs, the quality of the script drops substantially as soon as H content start. Superior to most VNs, despite this fact, it mostly remains a decent quality, with some pleasant surprises with good quality scripting. However, I did find myself fast reading some of the H content. H content is not included on Steam, you can download the patch on Yuzusoft's official website.

Sprite quality

A very high number of poses and facial expressions are used on detailed sprites, which frequently changes during a conversation to keep the characters life-like and natural. Attention to detail is paid in order to get the perfect timing and sprite selection to achievement the aforementioned point, which makes it feel even more fluid than an animation.

CG quality (non H standpoint)

There is a high number of CGs that are vibrant and dynamic. Overall quality is excellent, but it is emphasised by the outstanding timings the CGs are introduced. They serve to enhance the story and moments dramatically, and add to the fact that some details (e.g. facial expressions) can change whilst showing the CG, it serves as a powerful cornerstone for the VN to shine upon.

CG quality (H standpoint)

Due to the nature of a H scene, it may sometimes feel repetitive because ultimately, it is still just one image. The H aspect is mostly decent, but sometimes it fails to successfully capture the balance between the H quality of the girl and the charm of the girl. That said, it still fully inherits all the good points discussed in the non-H standpoint, with the overall quality being better than most other VNs.

Gameplay

You occasionally make choices that impact what route you end up going into (H-Scene choices have no impact).

Translation

I played the game mostly in English and sometimes with Chinese sub-text. I can't speak for the faithfulness to the original (since I haven't seen the original), but both languages flow naturally and don't detract from the game's charm. There is no element of annoying over-localisation present, allowing you to fully enjoy the story.

Settings and misc

The game excels in this aspect. Settings that are crucial for a VN, settings you may want in a VN, settings you didn't even think you will need in a VN, this has it all. Despite the high customizability, the settings are straight forward to navigate and comprehend, with the default being perfect acceptable as well. A small detail I really appreciate is that almost every navigation or options is accompanied by the voice of one of the girls. The game also features the ability to bookmark favourite voice lines, which is quite wonderful (I don't play a lot of VNs, so I'm not sure if this is a common feature).

MC

The game's MC does a good job of filling the role of a character in the world, whilst also not making it feel like you are watching a story from the sidelines. There is no eccentric qualities about him, which thankfully also means there are no annoying qualities about him. He is also not an emotionless insert; he is able to express various emotions appropriate for the scenario, so you won't find yourself cringing or raging over the MC's actions.

Routes

Disclaimer: I may discuss what some may consider minor spoilers, but I promise there won't be any details pertaining to what happens.

Common

The common route is where you are introduced to the main storyline of the curse and the characters, whilst also building up the world of Hoori. The common route intertwines advancing the storyline of solving the curse whilst simultaneously showing off the personality of the characters and the charm of the girls. The former is achieved somewhat dully, with the majority coming from dialogue that feels dragging, but the latter is achieved phenomenally, from enticing dialogue sparkled with colourful CG, to well planned events that serve as a powerful backbone for the script to shine.

The proportion of the main story and dating aspect leans slightly towards the former, so I can't help but think that being a bit more skewed towards the latter, or at least being balanced, would making the common route more enjoyable. However, I'm no scriptwriter and I'm sure the currently balance has been tested and was deemed optimal. This is also more than made up by the fact that during a specific route, the dating aspect (even without considering H), is more pronounced than the main story (or in the case of Roka/Koharu, the main story completely ends).

World-building of Hoori is relatively simple, but that doesn't mean it feels one-dimensional. Details are sprinkled into the story to make the world believable and cohesive, and the balance between subtleness and explicitness leaves no room for confusion, whilst also not overwhelming you with details like a lorebook.

The actual story quality of the main plot (at least in the common route) is detailed, but unexciting. You can clearly see that whilst a detailed storyline is present, the heart of the VN is not here. Whilst you won't be bored or uninterested, you may start feeling indifferent about the events that happen in the main storyline.

The story quality of the dating aspect is where the positives of this VN shine through. You get a taste of each of the girls' charms, with enough details to understand their traits, and sufficient encouragement to select any of their routes. Each scene is carefully crafted to achieve this, with all elements of the VN working in harmony.

(Due to the character limit, I will be discussing each specific routes in the comments.)

Conclusion

Whilst not the first VN I finished, as a relatively new VN player, this showed me the charms and potentials of VN. This VN was a brilliant introduction to the genre for me, and I can easily say its the best out of the limited number of ones I have played. For the current price, it is more than worth it, as the sheer quantity of quality content will easily be proven by the number of willing hours one would inevitably be putting in.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR the VN is decent, if somewhat generic. Be sure that you're the target audience (teenaged weebs) when playing. The trial mode is very representative of what you'll get and a great thing.

I haven't really played a lot of VNs before if JRPG segments don't count, but I thought I'd give it a try and this was the highest rated one around. I'm not really that into the anime tropes anymore and they're pretty ubiquitous here so that kind of hampered my enjoyment a bit but not to the point where I dropped it early. I'm writing this review after having played every route but Lena's.

Technical aspects
The thing that made me buy this to try it out as opposed to simply pirating it was the options menu, funnily enough. The amount of complex customization you can do, from adjusting voice volume of specific characters to binding specific mouse gestures to customizing the color of already seen text, was honestly crazy to me.

Other than that, the game performed totally fine and I didn't notice any problems when playing outside of one somewhat problematic feature.

The auto mode. This feature is somewhat buggy, if you have the text speed at 100% you'll regularly find some unvoiced lines be skipped after voiced lines because of how this feature works. If you're on an unvoiced line of text when a voiced line stops, the unvoiced text is advanced in auto mode, this means that if the text is on the screen too quickly when the voice is less than a second from being done, that text will be skipped. Annoying. This can be fixed by setting text speed to 95%, it's still fast enough to be near indistinguishable.
The issue described above is a general flaw though, if you skip ahead in text and a voice line ends you'll sometimes skip unvoiced lines accidentally, although maybe I should've just stopped using auto mode at that point.

Production value
This was somewhat saddening to me, after the options menu gave me such a good impression. The character images and backgrounds, as well as the music are all good, great even! But there's so little of it, the majority of the time you'll see the same character image with 2 outfits, about 7 different expressions and around 5 custom CGs (including sex scenes) per route. I had expected a few more custom images and was a little disappointed to find out that this is where they mainly saved on costs. Maybe I'm expecting too much of a 30$ game but then again, VNs don't have to care about gameplay at all, so I expect the funds to be put into things that are basically the "gameplay" of VNs.

On the other hand, the voice acting is top-notch and everything but the inner monologue and speech of the protagonist is voiced. No complaints here at all.

Plot and characters
I'll write a bit about the character-specific routes without spoilers below my abstract thoughts on them, which come next.

The plot itself is alright, I was not particularly invested in it, but it didn't bore me to tears and was satisfactorily resolved in the basic route. It's definitely more of a vehicle for character interactions than anything else, outside of the character-specific routes.

The characters are pretty good actually, most of them are decently fleshed out by the time you've played all routes and have multiple motivations for their actions. They're not especially complex or deep, but they have enough going for them that you can emphasize and, in some cases, relate.
Most of the character development of a character is locked behind a character-specific route though, during the basic route they remain rather static, which is fine because that's where you get to know them in the first place. Although, the relationships between the characters could've been expanded upon, it sometimes feels like characters cease to exist if the protagonist doesn't interact with them.

Two final things to note, despite what the game may say, these characters are not 18+ and certainly don't act anywhere near that age. The main character is a very vexing person in the basic route, and only really seems to grow out of his macho phase once you start the character-specific routes. Outside those moments, he's a very passive and "nice" person, probably for ideal self-insertion. Although, he's made dense to the point of self-delusion in some routes.
Furthermore, basically every heroine turns into a blushing innocent lily the moment they're romanced, I suppose this may be a more cultural thing about the Japanese female ideal, but it's noticeable where one second they're confident in themselves and the next they're a blushing stuttering wreck that has never dated anybody. I suppose they didn't want to give an impression of "used goods" which in of itself is a pretty toxic view. In one of the routes a heroine mentions that she's happy the protagonist has never dated somebody either because she didn't want to feel like she'd be compared to a prior experience, which is understandable if a rather immature view.

Time for a spoiler free overview of the character-specific routes, I'll mainly split it up between the romance/relationship and the plot of the route. The number next to their name is the age based on maturity I'd assign the heroine and protagonist in this route. The order is the way I played them in.

Murasame(19): The first route I played, with the most involved plot. That kind of was its downfall though, a lot of focus and words was spent on resolving the plot and the payoff/solution was good, but the way to it was a bit of a slog. It definitely dragged in the middle, and I would recommend using the skip button during some scenes. It gave me true-ending vibes with its resolution though.
The relationship with Murasame was expanded upon in this route, and she gets a satisfying character arc and pay-off, although you don't notice that she's supposed to be 500 years old. They didn't really do anything with the concept and she feels more like a somewhat more mature girl in a young body, not an adult though. They could've definitely done a bit more with the well-spring of experiences Murasame has seen over her time, and her relationships with characters she has ostensibly been watching over for their entire lives.

A decent route but the middle part of the plot really dragged it down, I'd say this was thoroughly mediocre. I probably did Murasame a disservice by comparing her to Sothis from Fire Emblem 3 Houses which does a much better job of working with the 1000 year old loli archetype.

Mako(17): This route had a pretty good plot that kept me interested and was the best one in typing together plot and character development for the heroine. Also has one of the most amusing plot lines, sleepless in Hoori had me chuckling.
This route actually let the characters breathe and get to know each other before falling in love and felt a lot more plausible than some of the others. The characters also do a great job communicating here, something that I was very surprised in an anime-inspired piece of media. Feelings were communicated and explored in a rather mature way. Ended with a very satisfying character-development based pay-off.

My favorite route overall.

Roka(20): Not much to write here, this and Koharu are bother side-character routes and basically just there, so you can romance other notable characters without making them actually plot-relevant. The ending was decent though, the plot while nothing to write home about was well-handled.
The relationship was alright, Roka could've been made a little more mature, but that would've gone against the innocent lily portrayal of all heroines, oh well.

Koharu(14): Ah, the incest route. I played with the 18+ patch, this was the only route that was actually made worse by it, I feel. Very lame resolution overall and all in all not a great time for me.

Character limit reached, oops.

Yoshino(16): This route had the best plot, I'd argue. The relationship was also well-explored. Contender for true-ending route.\t\t\t\t\t
