\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hesitated to write a review right after this game launched on Steam, since I didn't finish Roka and Koharu routes when I played the Japanese version. I did play through the four main routes, though, so after giving it some thought I decided to write a review anyway.

SenrenÔºäBanka is a great game. The art is top-notch (also the first 1080P title by Yuzusoft), the cast beats out just about any other game's in terms of talent and experience, and the tracks include quite a few memorable ones, especially the opening theme song, Koikou Enishi. Each of the four main routes also manages to tell an engaging story, with Lena's route being the closest to the core plot of the game, which comes off as a surprise given her role as a foreign student in the village. On top of all that, Yuzusoft's customized Kirikiri engine famously provides for a highly configurable and enjoyable gaming experience.

In short, this game is well worth its price tag, with or without discount - you won't regret buying it, period. Buying it will also support NekoNyan in their quest to introduce more high quality VNs to the western market - especially given the incredibly exciting news that they have now convinced and partnered with another great VN developer SAGA PLANETS to publish an English version of Kin'iro Loveriche, SAGA PLANETS's 2017 work that was so successful in Japan it prompted them to make their first fan disk in over a decade. I can't wait for the day when all of the great games made by Yuzusoft and SAGA PLANETS receive the renown they deserve by fans all around the world. (Sorry for using this review to blatantly lobby NekoNyan for more SAGA PLANETS titles; as a devout fan of SAGA PLANETS and especially of KarumarukaÔºäCircle, I couldn't resist. Both Yuzusoft and SAGA PLANETS are located in Osaka, and they often ran joint campaigns to promote each other's works, so hopefully this little bit of digression can be forgiven.)\t\t\t\t\t
