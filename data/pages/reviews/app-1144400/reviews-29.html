Senren * Banka
Cutting into the strings of fate.

Preclaimer
- This is an Adult Visual Novel - one with a free R18 Patch (obtainable at NekoNyan's webshop) at that - designed to be R18, which is why I consider the R18 content part of the whole and will include it in this review. If for whatever reason you are not interested in this content, or aren't legally allowed to play it, you should just skip this review.
- Since many Visual Novels are similar in a bunch of ways, I'm gonna review this one on a few common aspects, including heavy reference to the previous title from YuzuSoft as well as independent explanations.


Graphical & Technical Quality
Have you played the previous title, Sabbat of the Witch? If so, this makes it easy. If not - let's just cut it short, you should do so right away. Senren * Banka's story does not require any knowledge from Sanoba Witch, but with it being the next title from the very same developer, it is very similar (and does contain very funny joke references you only understand that way) - and Sanoba Witch is even better.
Anyway, as to be expected from YuzuSoft, Senren * Banka features amazing quality in both graphics and technical features. You get the whole spectrum from Sanoba Witch, alongside some cool newcomers and enhancements.
Such as the KiriKiri engine default features like dozens of saveslots, skipping back and forth throughout the story (as far as you have already seen it once), costumization for menu voices, skip mode behaviour, text colors, fonts and so on.
The cool additions back in Sanoba Witch like saving voicelines to rehear them whenever you wanna hear your favorite "Baka baaaaka!" again, mouse gestures, the option to add even more saveslots and so on.
And finally all the cool new gimmicks Senren * Banka brings to the table, like detailed backwards skipping, quick chapter jumping, customizable keyboard shortcuts, a customizable menu bar, being able to jump to the scene of a saved voiceline, a more user friendly savegame manager and finally - the flowchart diagram, the easiest way ever to keep track of every story segment, find out what you're missing and as easy as ever build your new route for a new playthrough.
Also, with the hidden "Advanced settings" menu you can now feel like a pro, adjusting stuff like render methods, cache sizes and all that juicy expert stuff.

As with Sanoba Witch there are no character sprite animations (like blinking or moving mouths) but the various character sprites are vividly used and changed mid-sentence to bring the scene to life.

Though, also like with the previous game, Senren * Banka features a few textual errors in the translation here and there, but nothing too disturbing.
And Senren * Banka seems to have some technical hicups too, atleast the reaction of the mouse gestures is far worse.

Content & Runtime
Of course not spoiling anything on the actual story, Senren * Banka is much more of a casual, ecchi humor RomCom than it's predecessor focussing more on - stuff that the romance and adult genre entail. So if you are looking for a serious, deep, heartfelt story giving you an emotional journey of your lifetime - I again highly recommend the predecessor, Sabbat of the Witch. Though if you are looking for a good laugh amongst some good looking virtual characters making out, this is one of the best things you could get.

Senren * Banka features the usual 4 main routes (without a real "main" route like Sanoba Witch) with an additional bonus route (shorter in comparison) splitting between the two side characters, which gives a total of 7 ("bad" ending included) endings on 5 routes to enjoy. The Prologue/Common route takes around 10h of time, the main routes around 15h each and the bonus route around 10h whole (as always measured by not rush-skipping standards), totaling to around 80h.
The characters are well developed and distinct, giving lot's of variation to enjoy no matter which route you play, though the male protagonist has been reduced to your everyday anime protagonist again (even though he looks exactly like the one from Sanoba Witch :I).

As this again is a full fledged Visual Novel with multiple routes/endings, you determine your path with the 8(+1) in-dialog-decisions made throughout the story, with some of them having more than the regular 2 options and some of them even leading to different event-paths right on the common route.

If you're not after your personal Waifu I'd recommend playing the routes in the order they are displayed (flowchart menu, shoppage description, ...) for maximum effect.

After clearing your first playthrough you unlock the gallery, where you can enjoy the 93 wallpaper CGs, 30 (somewhat animated) SD Chibi arts, 53 really awesome soundtracks and 7 opening/ending movies (as long as you saw/heared them once before of course), neatly sorted by category and character.
There also are After Stories again, which contain another bonus scene for every character whose route you already cleared.

Upon clearing all routes you unlock the Sprite Viewer too, where you can play around with all the backgrounds and character sprites in all their variations to your heart's content, and even load custom backgrounds for all your photoshop needs.

The R18 content
Considering the content is for free, you can still expect an experience worthy of any regular full price R18 DLC. Each main character comes with 3+1 hentai scenes, each bonus character with 2+1.
Great voice acting alongside missing weird sound effects (you know, those weird "break bones" and "mix cement" sounds you often hear) and CG backgrounds with high variety make up for the previously mentioned lack of animation and the likes.
When it comes to the depictions of bodily fluids and the will and stamina of the participants, it's noteably more than with the predecessor, but nothing out of the ordinary hentai and even varies strongly from route to route.

Senren * Banka does a slight serving on fetish play, with the concept of "turning into sex crazy monsters" and that peeing fetish thingie being a thing to look out for.

You get to choose between coming inside or outside in most occasions, with personal preference (aka. auto choice) to be set in the settings.

And again there is your best buddy, the panic button, customizable in function (minimize, placeholder image, custom image).

Also, as is suitable for having a foreign character on board, you even get a free lesson on "Japanese for Beginners - Hentai Edition".

As usual (sadly) mosaic censorship on the 'good' bits is in place.


https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2011875313


Conclusion
Is this game worth it?
Well, if I'd go and rate it on a simplistic scale from 1 to 10, with 12 being where the predecessor managed to land, I'd give Senren * Banka an overall 9/10, depending on personal taste.
If you played and enjoyed the predecessor (or any other VN of the same caliber) for it's rich, deep and serious story, this here might not be the thing for you. If you don't mind or are actively looking for a nice, lighthearted RomCom with adult content, Senren * Banka is well invested money.
Also, if you're still unsure - there will always be a next sale.
