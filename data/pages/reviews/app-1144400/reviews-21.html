Senren＊Banka is a regular Gal visual novel game that won't let you down. The plot perfectly interprets what is called a bland but not mediocre daily life, and the music, art, and character design are also quite good, which is unique in similar games.

I think this game can be used as an entry game for many players Galgame. Smooth and comfortable gameplay and just the right length of the game make for a relaxing and enjoyable gaming experience. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you liked Sabbat Of The Witch you're going to love this!

Not once did I get bored while reading this, it's just on a completely different level when it comes to the plot. Since every route reveals different aspects of the backstory within this VN you'll actually feel a lot more motivated to go through all of the routes to learn everything you can.
As for the length of this VN... It's not really that different in terms of length when compared to Sabbat Of The Witch, however the hours you spend reading this are a lot more evenly distributed across different routes.

The UI has seen a substantial improvement when compared to Sabbat Of The Witch, with it feeling generally a lot more fluid and I actually found myself using gesture controls in this one. Flowchart makes it easier for you to change your choices made during the common route, allowing you to remold the decision path for a different route without the need to start the story from the beginning.

Soundtrack is great and fits the atmosphere really well. Having classic Japanese music with a modern twist to it was certainly a unique experience and just makes me crave for an option to buy the entire soundtrack so much more.

As for the visuals? Stunning. The art style is the same as before but with another level of detail and deeper, more stunning colours. Also it's all in Full HD and I was comfortable reading this fullscreen on my 27" 2K monitor without any issues.

Joking. Sorta. That aside, I gave the previous title, Sabbat of the Witch, a barely passing grade. Thus my surprise when this game turned out to be absolutely amazing. The story is great, a touch Higurashi-ish, but I'm not complaining. While the previous was an obvious shallow dating sim that happened to have other elements, this game has a strong focus that also happens to have the dating aspect. The music is solid - very well done, and has a distinct eastern feel to it. The little details such as UI, options, etc., are well polished, and it has a convenient flowchart system. It's also supported in multiple languages and doesn't seem too difficult a read, so I look forward to practicing my chinese and japanese by rereading this.

My only real complaint is that there's no 'true route'. Through the various routes you learn a lot of unique aspects about the main focus of the story, but you never get a chance to put them all together and really solve the issue.

Overall: great game. I would even recommend this to people with little interest in visual novels that might want to give one a go. Maybe not as their first VN, but within the first few.\t\t\t\t\t
