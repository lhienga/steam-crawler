\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had my eye on this game for a long time, but due to the early access label I opted to hold off on purchasing it and keep my eye on its development. I checked intermittently over the last year or so, and noticed with this past holiday sale that it was on sale for a few dollars off. Seeing the positive reviews, it being recently updated, and the sale, I decided I would pick it up as it looked like a cool intersection between my interest in ants and enjoyment of RTS/sim games. I am more than glad that I did!

Having put a good 30 hours into it so far (and an accidental 10 hours of idling), I already feel like I've got my money's worth. I'd feel the same if I bought it at full price. The games mechanics are fairly simple and straightforward, very easy to pick up. And there feels like a good variety between each ant species that each feels unique. Most of all though I enjoyed the atta leaf cutter ants, who harvest leaves to grow fungus for food, as opposed to the rest of the ants who hunt & harvest other critters. The gameplay is simple and easy to pick up, and not much of a difficulty on the lower levels, but scales well. The campaign is quite interesting as well, featuring a formicarium hub world being studied by scientists. Each mission in the campaign features a different ant colony in the wild, and completing those missions grants resources to upgrade your hub world (whose strength will eventually be tested by insect attacks.) The campaign encourages returning to other missions and playing on higher difficulties or with challenges to amass more resources for your hub world. This makes the game feel like it's easy to learn but harder to master, and I find myself going back and replaying missions over and over, trying to design your colony more efficiently and better manage resources. Even playing each mission multiple times, coming back to them still feel fresh.

I also feel the need to comment on the narration and atmosphere that this game creates. It makes the interactions between different ant colonies and insects feel grandiose, and as it should. The narrator really nails the "National Geographic" feel. It really emphasizes how something as mundane as a house spider or cricket can be a formidable foe for your ants, and reminds you that the campaign is not just win or lose, and that your queen and the whole of your colony is at stake. Personally, it adds a great deal of "feeling" to the game.

Overall, this game has been a pleasant experience so far. Highly recommend to anyone who enjoys classic RTSs, or ant lovers who aren't opposed to colony sims. Arachnophobes and insect haters beware! (though the game does feature a funny arachnophobia mode)

Thank you to the devs for delivering such a great product, glad to be here now after much of the polish, though I'm sure I still would've enjoyed the game before. Greatly looking forward to future updates from Slug Disco, and any other upcoming projects!\t\t\t\t\t
