They've defocused the dungeon full of monsters aspect of Dungeon Keeper and amped up the control massively, to the point where Micromangement is actually somewhat do-able, but ants are ants and have relatively simple instructions in the real world and will follow what the hive wants/needs, but also go off and do their own thing or take time to respond in force.
basically you can set rally points for multiple control groups, a newish thing for games like this.

second of all, this game says Early Access. It's not vaporware, but it develops on Valve time, in that it will be multiple years before major content releases. The dev team is 3 people as far as I know, and even Pokemon's Gamefreak, a notoriously small and underfunded developer, is something like 150 people, ish, and it's nothing short of a small miracle that they manage a new Pokemon Generation every 1-2 years, plus side games like Legends Arceus and New Snap; and even then the games are picking up a VERY bad reputation for being SUPER buggy and underdone because of this gruelling schedule.
Slug Disco is a fraction of a fraction of Gamefreak's size, resources and budget. the Upside of this is that new additions to the game tend to be well thought out at least, as the ideas and numbers have had a lot of time to mature and be tweaked. the downside is that in the time it takes Gamefreak to release a new Gen, Slug Disco can only kinda manage a new ant species and a handful of new levels, most of which probably look like they can be bashed out in an afternoon to a week assuming they have a decently powerful editor, ala the major RTS names.

As Far as the actual game goes, I've had a lot of fun, and I kinda love ants, bees and wasps, and it's sat there installed on my PC since 2019-ish when I picked it up, occasionally getting a couple replays every so often when I feel like dipping my toes in again. I do wish more updates would happen, and I do get excited when they happen, especially as they tend to be laden with cool ideas, but this game is one where you should temper your expectations. Slug Disco follow the fine British Tradition of "Three Dudes in a Shed", and it shows.
It more or less scratches the itch left by the large Dungeon Keeper shaped hole I've had since Bullfrog got EA'd. Sure there's War for the Overworld, but I'm not sure I like the spiritual successor as much as the original.
Empires of the Undergrowth is very niche, and if it's your niche, that's great, but until they hire a bigger dev team this game's not going to be for much of anybody outside that niche.\t\t\t\t\t
