\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fantastic little game. It plays a little bit like a simplified Dungeon Keeper with a nest that you excavate over time and an overworld. The missions are fun and well designed with interesting challenges, the art is great, the dynamic music is really good as well. You'll learn a bit too about various ant species.

My two complaints: 1.) Though the art is great, the textures seem to be a bit low res so it doesn't look quite as good as it could and 2.) I wish there were more missions.

Overall, this is a fun, relaxing game that is relatively straightforward to learn and appropriate for kids as well. You're not going to play it for 500 hours but I bet I get about 20 or so very enjoyable hours out of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven though this game have a lot of flaws, I would recommend it if you like the concept.
It might feel repetitive for some, but otherwise, it is quite addicting until you played all the missions.

Major flaws being the balance...it is super easy in normal difficulty, super hard/impossible in hard.
Which put you in this very frustrating spot where you will be playing each missions in hard 3 times until you accept that you can't beat it, then switch to normal and destroy it...unsatisfied...

Game is really ugly too, art direction is non-existent, and UI is terrible. Sound design doesn't existe either for some reason. (in the way that 80% of sound effects are just not there at all)


But damn this game have some satisfying moments too.
Launching an attack of 100+ ants on an other colony is exciting.


Overall, this game combine great game design choices with terrible ones, which doesn't make much sense when you think about it...
Ah, and the story is pretty well written and acted. In a few words, you are getting bullied by a mad scientist all game. :D\t\t\t\t\t
