\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, in real life, i absolutely do not like ants, in fact, I'm quite terrified of them and that's despite only having harmless black garden ants where I live.

This game however, is very pleasing to play. The ants here come off as sorta cute even with how realistic their models are, which seem indeed fairly realistic, surely whoever made the models knew what they were doing and did a good job. Sure, some of the squeaks and noises the ants makes are weird, but it is what it is. Games get a bit boring without soundeffects right?

Anyways, do I recommend it? Yes. It's quite intertaining and the levels get increasingly more difficult, even if you're a first time player thats 'testing' the waters on easy (which yes I am lol). I found the leafcutter challenges the most challenging so far, and yes, even those I initially played on easy.

But the graphics are good, the music is good, the voiceacting is good and the narration is good. You get to learn a bit, and you almost feel like you're watching a documentary with the way the narrator speaks, and gives info, and even uses the latin names first to introduce the various different ants you play as and the enemies they encounter. Absolutely love it. It is also kinda great if you have some OCD actually, or a perfectionism kind of trait to you because you decide how your anthills look underground and you can make them SO aesthetically pleasing, which is absolutely amazing.

You have to remember it is still early acces and that things can be added and/or changed in the future, so if you're no fan of that, probably better you wait with getting the game until it is fully done, but if you don't mind the possibility of changes, additions, etc. to the game, then I definitely recommend it, yes, even if bugs and creepy crawlies aren't normally your cup of tea.\t\t\t\t\t
