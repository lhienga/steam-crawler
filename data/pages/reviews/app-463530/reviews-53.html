Huckleberry pancakes.

1 3/4 cups all purpose white flour
4 tablespoons sugar
1 tablespoon baking powder
1/4 teaspoon salt
1 1/2 cups of buttermilk
5 tablespoon butter, melted
2 large eggs
1/2 cup Huckleberries
In medium bowl combine flour, sugar, baking powder and salt, set aside. In small bowl combine buttermilk, butter and eggs. Add buttermilk mixture to the flour mixture, combine until mixed do not over mix (I prefer to use a whisk it does the best job). Fry at 400 degrees. Pour about 1/4 cup of batter on the griddle and sprinkle with the huckleberries.

Cook on first side about 3 minutes until bubbles appear along the edge of the pancake. Flip and cook for 2 minutes more and golden brown. Serve with a big pat of butter, and some pure maple syrup ( get the real stuff it is worth it).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Graphics are great
- Sounds are great
- Campaign is fun and has adequate levels of challenge (certain formicarium challenges are a bit grindy, but that's somewhat to be expected)
- Ant facts are interesting for all ages
- Ant "upgrades" are kind of neat

Cons:
- Although updates are relatively frequent, I would prefer finishing the campaign as opposed to adding one-shot scenario maps. Your mileage may vary.
- No multiplayer (see below)

Conclusion:
- I would recommend this game at an on-sale price, but this game would be worth a AAA price tag with multiplayer. I would love to play co-op with my child; she loves the game, but she is young and it is too challenging for her to really do much on her own.
