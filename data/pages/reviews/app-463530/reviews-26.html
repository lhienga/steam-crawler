\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, as you can see I have experience with this game...

...and I only have a handful of issues.

issues:
for instance, if an ant starts walking a way and it is out of visible range of the opponent, it can still get hit. also ants suck at chasing things that choose to run, like REALLY SUCK. They can catch up, but they still can't attack and just sit there awkwardly. achievements will still not unlock after I do the achievement mission multiple times (I can't tell if this is steam or the game). One time a hermit went in its shell and had never come back out.

Dislikes:
Firstly I don't like how the ant colonies have nearly no numbers, I understand it lags really bad if you have to many (as i've tested in free-play), but still it just bothers me from a realism view, even if the ants grow in like 5 seconds. Mounting creatures is very strong don't get me wrong, BUT, sometimes I feel that not enough ants mount on an enemy (bullfrog specifically, it just looks underwhelming). Also I feel that the Narrow-mouth toads are just plain overpowered, they ate about 50 ants in one hefty gulp which is way too strong, especially due to a lack of ants like I mentioned before. everything else is pretty good apart from a few things I feel I don't need to mention.

likes:
Personally I just really like ants, I even have several species of my own, so this is just a really cool game to me. Also the aspect of being an overseer of this army of ants is just really fun, everything from watching them horde small bugs, to ripping apart half hermit half scorpion bosses. the fight with the Dragonfly is really fun, especially because after a certain point it gets crippled (mentioned later) and can no longer fly and is grounded which then really highlights the combat/mounting aspect of the ants. This is very few of my likes of this game, probably one of the best "command an army" like game i've played.

Recommendations (things that would be cool in the future):
1. Crippling of all medium-large enemies
2. Dismemberment like missing legs, wings, antennae, etc...
3. Venom stages for ants with venom (fire ants, formica rufa).
venom stages explained:
If an enemy gets hit with a venom attack it builds up a venom value which over time increases damage and applies negative status effects
stage 1. 5% venom damage intake
stage 2. 3% melee damage intake + 10% venom damage intake
stage 3. slowed down + 10% melee damage intake + 20% venom damage intake
4. visible damage on enemies, ie holes in wings, scratches, blisters, etc...
5. A burnt down (wildfire) map that is just an FFA arena

Welp, this is it. i'm done yapping on about this game. the recommendations are not at all expected to be listened to, as they are all daunting tasks to program and/or design.

bye! :)\t\t\t\t\t
