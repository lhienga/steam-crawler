\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was instantly taken by the gimmick of Empires of the Undergrowth, although when I played through the tutorial levels I did start to worry it was all style and no substance. The more I play it, however, the more won over I am by it. A surprising variety of ants and enemy types, as well as heavy use of environmental factors in the story missions, keeps things feeling fresh, and optional challenge modes give each story mission replayability.

Is it perfect? No. Some of the new gimmicks introduced in story missions aren't very intuitive (bit of a mistake making the first Leaf Cutter Ant mission a race, since it likely takes most players a good 5 minutes or so to figure out how this new faction works) and some of the challenge modes seem more annoying than challenging (I'm looking at you, beach missions). But as a rounded experience, it definitely lives up to the premise. Once you've got your ant colony built up and you're sending out streams of little critters to new food sources, it feels very unique and ant-like, rather than just being any old RTS with insect skins thrown over it. And the way various enemies move feels so natural that it sometimes makes my skin crawl - seeing the spiders dart around through the ant tunnels, for example.

Overall, I'd definitely recommend this to any RTS fan looking for a new spin on the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
