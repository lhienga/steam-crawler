\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am really interested in arthropods and this game looked super cool to me. I thought the idea was awesome, every looked great, and you get to learn about some of the creatures within the game. My problem is that the story is poorly thought out. Why do you have to play the same levels over and over just to build resources? The way the campaign works is that you have levels to build resources that carry over to your main hive/nest. That nest is treated as a main hub between levels, but also as the nest you use to complete milestones to advance further. When you bet a level, you are given food (used to buy things for your nest or to hatch ants), royal jelly (used to upgrade your ants), and territory (used to expand the nest). You can only build so many tunnels to allow space for units. The less room you have, the less ants you can have. The first milestone level swarms you with waves of ants. The problem is that unless you grind those first 2 levels, you aren't going to stand a chance. This is very poor design. I could understand that design choice if these levels were not randomly generated, but they are not. It is the exact same each time. I really wish this wasn't such an issue as I would have enjoyed the game a lot more if it didn't hinder progress so much. I will say that there is a cool arcade feature if you just want to mess around with ants or make enemies fight each other, but you need to play the story if you want to create your own nest of ants built how you want them. I spent 4 hours playing this and 3 hours of that was grinding the same levels. The AI isn't the greatest ether. I tried many times to have ants focus on different tasks in order to flank enemies, but the ants just seem to scatter the second they start fighting. It's also pretty unclear just how much damage ants do. The game tells you at one point to try and out number enemies in the level, but the same level has 3 larger bugs. You are basically told that you need a strong force to take them down. You can toggle health bars, but it isn't clear just how many ants you need or how much damage each will do. I don't really want to spend 30 minutes of trial and error just to lose and have to start over.

---Pros---
Ants are cool
You can learn about many different creatures
The game looks great
Arcade system you can goof around in
Spiders
Music fits well as you play

---Cons---
Story is a grind
AI isn't the best
Punishes simple mistakes
Levels become repetitive and boring.
Poor design for the story hub.

TLDR - I was disappointed with the story and how long of a drag it became. Arcade is cool, but not sure if that would warrant a purchase.\t\t\t\t\t
