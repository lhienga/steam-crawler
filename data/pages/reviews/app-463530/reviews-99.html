\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly Some of the best RTS fun I've had in a very long time.

I originally, passed this game up multiple times cause it was never on sale and looking at the video it seemed pretty meh.

Dude, was i absolutely wrong. You are the research project of an ant fetishizing researcher and his assistant (or fellow researcher).

At first things seem rather academic with a generic voice acted cast. NO. The voice acting is sheer gold. When this bastard has the chance. he pushes the scope of the project with god tier deep seeded repressed rage that is just foisted on to this ant colony. You will know things are about to get super crappy when the female researcher "has to step away" he just absolutely goes off the rails.

All that aside, the gameplay is pretty straight forward, a bit of base building, a bit of unit selection, minimal micro management other than using scent beacons to essentially flag where you want groups 1-5 to go to do ant stuff.

While being somewhat simple in appearance the game is extremely deep and customizable. The grand campaign is extremely well put together. I had no idea what i was doing on mission 1... turned it off.. went back after an hour.. and really saw an extremely beautifully thought out evolution of how to grow a little ant colony into an empire, challenging larger creatures, finding unexpected scenerios either forced on to me or naturally progressed as the enviornments changed.

Each mission tells a great story, that adds points and progress to the main campaign in a beautiful upgrade as you play system. A game that really makes you appreciate the little dudes you walk over every single day.

I honestly couldn't recommend this enough. i got it at 10% off but even at full price this game is just incredible.

10,000,000 / 10 - for the brood mother.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore this game came out, the one thing I wanted was a robust game about playing as an ant colony. The draw of going out, killing things, gathering resources, and streaming it all back to become bigger and do it all again. This is the only game that even came close to my dreams of a decent ant game, but it also far far outweighed any expectations or hopes I dared to have for such a game. Providing several different ant species and scenarios with fun mechanics I never could've came up with myself. There are a few things here and there I could do without or would preferred changed, but being the only game in this genre to come close to the right formula far out weighs the criticisms I could possibly have for it. Not to mention the witty back and forth between the two scientists that overlook your main colony distract you from the rougher bits and get you excited to continue the game and story. Never before have I wanted a game to exist so bad, to search the internet for something that came close and finding nothing, just to have that same game released a few years later. You're doing a great job, Slug Disco. I've been following the updates since the launch and am looking forward to the 1.0 release.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing. I have played since the second beta came out and have watched this game grow. It has gone from a simple tile based game with only 2 castes of ant (worker and soldier) which couldn't even be told to move on their own, to a campaign with so many species and castes as well as complex ordering systems with pheromone markers and toggles for gathering and fighting. I find it hard to believe that the surface was the biggest update back then and now we have the ability to harvest leaves to make fungus, build pontoons out of ants, and climb the body of titanic creatures.

This game has so much to offer and is so fun, it's team works extremely hard and know what needs to be done for the game to be as good as it is (for example they postponed the fire ant update to refine it and it has been one of their best updates ever). The game and it's team deserve so much love and I hope it brings joy to many more people the way it has for me.\t\t\t\t\t
