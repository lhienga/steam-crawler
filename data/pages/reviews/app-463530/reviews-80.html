\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnt

Although seriously, I don't think Empires of the Undergrowth is a /great/ game. If you're newer to strategy games this honestly isn't a bad place to start, since the game is very low on micro, and heavier into your base setup and economy. It's just kinda shallow. The leaf cutter ants are my favorite since they lean so heavily into the game's strengths of macro; you need to harvest plant matter, have it processed on special tiles, and everytime food is spent it creates necrotic fungus that needs its own decomposer tile. You need to build your base so your chain runs smoothly, since ants move slower on nurseries and econ tiles, and that necrotic fungus gives a massive speed malus and applies a nasty dot.

But most of the time its pretty simple: out number the enemy, secure your food routes, watch health bars tick down. That sounds aweful, but the presentation carries the game pretty hard imo. The music is awesome, and the whole game is presented in the form of a nature documentary; you dont get a "We're under attack!" its "The ants must defend themselves," and giving you lil tid bits of actual bug info, its pretty awesome.

I do recommend the game if that premise sounds good, but if you're looking for in-depth strategy game with an ant theme, this isn't it I'm afraid.\t\t\t\t\t
