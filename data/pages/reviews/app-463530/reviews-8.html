\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really hate to give this a thumbs down - the game is almost perfect in every way:
- Easy to learn
- Dungeon digging
- Excellent campaign missions
- Tons of achievements for every level (forcing you to play VERY differently in the levels, giving huge variety)
- Very clever setting for this type of game (and the narration and voice overs are excellent and well written)

However there is one thing that makes me feel outright bullied by the developers - there is no checkpoints or save/load features in the ~1h long missions! This quickly turns the levels from being an exciting puzzle solving experience (where the fun would be in trying different tactics and strategies) to just being a punishing slog of a chore, making you angrier and angrier the longer you play.

The game is designed that you don't control the units, everything is controlled by the AI, you just set rally points and mark tiles for digging and where to place your infrastructure.The AI will dig/build/harvest/fight and you can sit back and relax. The fun comes from figuring out strategies & worker/military balance to beat the level, since there are so many possibilities on every level (and sometimes multiple win conditions) this is a wet dream for strategy players that enjoy macro gameplay.

So, if you can't micromanage and the AI controls you units (all you do is set rally points), you are bound to encounter stupid AI and RNG things that simply costs you the game, and is out of your control. Some achievements are outright based on luck with the RNG, since the achievement relies on the AI not making a mistake for the ~1 hour the mission is going on. Yeah, it you want to feel how it feels to have 50 minutes of your life & master plan ruined due to the RNG trolling you, this is the game for you.

So, to keep the excellent core chill gameplay AND still have a great time with extreme difficulties for levels, a simple checkpoint to fall back to when the RNG just had a bad day would resolve all frustration with this game. Until that is implemented, I just have to ask you to avoid this game - for your own anger management and sanity sake. The game tricks you into believing you'll be relaxed, but you'll always leave angry and frustrated as you rage quit. Finally completing a difficult achievement dos not even feel good, you just feel pure hatred towards it - you know that feeling when you finally beat a difficult boss that was almost impossible due to a glitch or something, you felt you earned it sooner but that the game bullies you into not giving you your reward. Yeah, that feeling.\t\t\t\t\t
