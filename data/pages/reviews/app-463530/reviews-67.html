\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am DOOM_BOOM_ENDER, and I have been playing this game for a few years. I would like to provide a decent review of one of my current favorite games.

To begin, the graphics are fairly good, the lighting is exquisite and most of the creatures have a unique feel. The designs are made with intent for real life accuracy, which I would like to applaud. The developers took their time with the creatures to truly flesh them out and achieve the proper settings for the levels.

By Far the most important aspect is gameplay. The developers take their time to consider the utilities and attributes of each unit available to the players. Each ant has specific strengths, and taking the time to practice using each can help truly bring out the best strategy.
The creatures also present their own dangers, and by playing with the mechanics of the game and understanding the abilities each opponent possesses, it will reward you for developing strategies. You can also learn to use these creatures to your advantage in other ways than simply beating them. such as using one creature to weaken another or delay an oncoming wave of wood ants.
The levels often provide challenges other than the difficulty settings. The space for your nest, the different elevations of the surface, and the movement patterns of the environmental creatures, all work to manifest an excellent challenge. The missions also provide challenge modes, which add an extra threat to how the level normally operates, causing the player to learn to operate around this obstacle.
I also love the inclusion of extra levels and the secret prizes for freeplay. The extra levels provide players something fresh every so often while we wait for the truly massive updates. Some of these levels can even leak into the freeplay mode, of which I quite enjoy. Sometimes these levels can provide a unique challenge that is not encountered in the campaign.
The freeplay mode is still a work in progress, but has great potential if you have sufficient creativity. This mode grants the ability of challenging yourself against your own colonies, present colossal threats and challenges. Or a good time killer. Freeplay is also a great place to test the different ant colonies to learn their strengths and weaknesses, as well as what battles they thrive in or falter.

To conclude this review, I would like to thank the developers for the work they put into this game. I love indie games and developers. They have taken much time to their player base and they strive to ensure this game is as fun as possible. I am excited to see where they conclude the Early access and to see where they take it. They have many possabilities to continue this game. Thank you and God bless!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game gives me the same feeling I get from watching my many real ant colonies. But if you are not an ant keeper then you won't understand that. So I will review this game in a more traditional way for you.

The visuals are fantastic with outstanding performance on my RTX 2070. I'm talking 80+ fps with around 600+ ants on-screen with seamless transition between above or below ground. (The shadows and light effects in general are some of the best.)

The AI after the recent update is phenomenal and the trailing system perfectly captures how real ants behave.

The update schedule is not the fastest but the team puts out really fun extra levels to hold us over.

The story is not amazing but in the campaign, there are two myrmecologists that have some great lines with equally great voice acting.

If you like RTS games or just like Insects i can't recommend this game enough.\t\t\t\t\t
