\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has been on my wishlist for awhile, and I finally decided to buy it after I saw it had a recent major update. I just finished the story mode last night, so my review will reflect that. There is also a "free play" mode and some additional challenges/maps that I haven't checked out yet.

Overall, I recommend this game for people who enjoy simulation type games. There are a lot of things going for it, and a few areas of improvement.


+ I love the nature documentary style narration when you are doing missions in story mode.
- I wish there were some additional phrases because some of the phrases are repetitive (like when you find an enemy underground when exploring, or other creatures attack your ants as they harvest resources)

+ I love the overall mechanics. It might seem complicated at first, but it's basically just a point and click sort of thing. You point, and the ants go.
- For the leaf cutter ants in particular, I wish there was a way to set multiple points on their route instead of them just going in a straight line to the end point.
- I wish there was a "charge" feature where I can tell ants to stop what they are doing and attack a nearby creature. It's frustrating when ants have to return the food to the nest before they engage in combat. The leaf cutter ants seem somewhat better about this, and will set their leaves down if attacked (and then pick them up when combat ends).

+ I love the idea of having a "base" in the story mode.
- it really annoyed me the first time I had to transfer my ants to a new "base" and lost all the work I had done in the previous one. I understand why it has to work that way in terms of story and balance issues, but I don't feel like I was adequately rewarded for the trouble.

+ Story mode levels are creative and unique. They do a good job showcasing what each type of ant can do.
- the difficulty I would say is pretty high. Even on easy mode, a few mistakes can easily cost you the game.
- the rewards for playing on more difficult levels is not worth the trouble IMHO. I don't know why you'd play higher than medium difficulty unless you hate yourself. Rewards should really reflect the effort and time put in.
- I have way too much Jelly left over after unlocking all the ants and traits that I can and I don't know what to do with it. Wish I could convert this into food or another resource because it feels like a waste.
- after you complete story mode you can drop random creatures on your ants in the "base" to get them to fight. Which is cool and all, but they don't give you food when they die so you can't replenish the ants you might lose in battle. So why would you use this feature if the only way to get food is to replay the previous levels?

+ 'Challenge' mode is a great twist that adds to replay value w/o making the story levels feel grind-y.
- There is no "challenge" mode for the latest fire ant update. You need to grind and redo previously done missions if you want to get enough resources to max out your "base" before the final "test".
- I wish there was more variety to the "challenges". The same 'challenge' applies to both missions in a given level.
