\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; Great game, regular updates and patches, open communication from developer, fun gameplay, great graphics. like a cross between SimAnt and dungeon keeper

As an avid hell diver, Deep Rock Galactic Miner, and fan of everything Starship troopers, I can say I HATE bugs.

However, I love this game.
Reminiscent of the SimAnt game of my young days in the 90s, Empires Of The Undergrowth does a fantastic job of building a world where you can play with ants and run a colony. The campaign is currently a little grindy because if you want to have a strong enough colony with enough resources to beat the "gateway" missions you will have to replay the previous missions a couple times, albeit the challenge mode does help alleviate this by adding small changes to the mission and greater rewards. The story is kinda outlandish and fun, and I really enjoy the different ant species and how they function in game. Free play / sandbox is available and is a cool way to just chill out and vibe while you build your colony and I like that certain creatures become like bosses in the game, like the bullfrog or whatever chimera crackpot creature the mad scientist cooks up.
The games never crashed on me despite running a ten year old rig, and I have never experienced any game breaking bugs.
I think the game is reasonably priced for the current fun to dollar ratio and it's going no where but up.\t\t\t\t\t
