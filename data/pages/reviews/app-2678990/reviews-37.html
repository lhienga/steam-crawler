\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a management game were petting cats is a metric starting mid-game. You get a dashboard that lets you know how many cats you pet. Did you achieve your optimal cat petting ratio today? The game is super casual and does not judge you at all, but I judged myself. Optimal cat petting ratios are key to happiness.

Anyways, its a delightful short and sweet game (2-3 hours missions with an endless mode) that was a neat way to spend a Saturday night for less than 1/3 of what I paid to see Dune II and I liked this more. Difficulty: 3/10 with most of that being the optional objectives, which were actually a helpful nudge towards exploring the options in an organized way. Smile factor: 9/10.

My only two issues: 1)I had to scour the community pages to recycle buildings. I'm used to that option just showing up normally on other sims and it wasn't showing up and I got anxious that I ran into an issue. Didn't occur to me after scouring community section that it was a clickable building option. Might be too much handholding to have some sort of nudging tool tip if everyone else was figuring it out but that was the one place where it would have helped for me. 2) An accessibility mode for the tanooki letters would be neat because they're really small and my autistic brain struggled with the object stimulation on screen, but this was minor and I sucked it up and pressed onward.

Otherwise, I didn't want it to be over so soon! That being said, I'll probably mess around with endless mode.

Devs: you probably have a 50-200 hour game somewhere in here if you expand it out. Really curious how many people mess with endless mode. Seriously, well done.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough Minami Lane is extremely cute and the design and graphics of the game are just wonderful to look at I felt after the 1 hour mark I found that the game itself overall just feels a little too repetitive for me. As someone who really loves "cozy/chill games" I was looking forward to seeing what Minami Lane was like, but sadly can't say this one is for me.
One of the highlights of Minami Lane is trying to find the Tanooki everyday in your street and being able to make your street look beautiful by upgrading your houses/Shops and placing gardens all around but I do really wish the days didn't go by so fast! It can make the progress of the game feel rushed and not at all like a "Casual" Management Sim. Also the repetitiveness of the game stand outs a lot once you reach a certain point. There is just not much you are able to do apart from try to make your "villagers" happy and upgrade your places to progress to the next part of the game. Also it at times was just unnecessarily hard to "please" all your villagers when it came to certain things. Really feel it missed the point of "Management Sim" . Sadly I wouldn't at this point in time recommend Minami lane to "Causal" Gamers, and would more than likely point them towards games such as Cozy grove instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompleted game 3/3/24 - took about 3 hours to 100%.

Really cute, relaxing game! It's a straightforward concept (balance beauty of the street vs. the number of customers) but with a nice twist of - you have 2 kinds of customers, and they disagree about what makes a good shop. You have to work out what they prefer to get the best profit and satisfaction. This is a good challenge in the early levels where you probably need a notepad, but in about mission 4 you can unlock a service that helps with it.

I found the game really lovely to play, and a good length considering the content it has. Would like to see it expanded with more shops etc.

A few things:

1. Music gets a bit repetitive after a while. It's relaxing lift music, and fits the game, but it's the same all the way through.

2. Once you get to about 70% satisfaction, it seems too easy to get to 100%. 50 - 65% is quite challenging in comparison. I'd like to see a third qualifier to get to the 100% mark, so possibly something like an entertainment rating from karaoke/onsen buildings or something.

3. There are 3 kinds of customers, but only 2 of them really count towards gameplay. It'd be fun to have another kind eg. parents, tourists in the later levels to mix up the gameplay a bit.

4. This makes me sound like an evil villain, I know - but a service that auto-pets cats would be nice. When running the game at 4x speed they're hard to grab. They also disappear behind trees sometimes so if you pause to look for them, they hide. Like real cats, mocking me!

But yeah, lovely game!\t\t\t\t\t
