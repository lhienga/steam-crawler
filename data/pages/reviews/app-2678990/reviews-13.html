Minami Lane is a cozy management simulator where you build streets and work to make your residents happy there through various ways. It can be a perfect relaxer, a nice break from other games, etc. The art style is cute and colorful, but simple enough that it is easy on my eyes. In addition, Minami Lane offers a set of different management features to bring their game together. From creating the perfect recipe for ramen/boba, setting the right prices, all the way to two different age groups you'll have to handle. You'll also find small "mini-games", including finding a Tanuki every day, petting the cats that visit your street, picking up trash that was left behind, and checking in on the cyclists that pass through. The game is short, having only 5 missions with a playtime of 2-4 hours but includes a freeplay option for continuous play. Despite this, Minami Lane will leave an impact on the player that makes them wanting more. I would highly recommend picking up this game if you are a fan of management sims, if you enjoy indie games, or if you would like a quick game that brings lots of joy.\t\t\t\t\t
