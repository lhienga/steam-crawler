\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI definitely recommend Minami Lane to any cozy game enjoyers! This is a short experience in terms of "missions" - I completed all 5 of the currently available missions and their optional objectives in 3 hours, going at a very leisurely pace. There is a sandbox mode for additional gameplay if someone wanted to do more, or get to 100% achievements (in the event that you did not reach that during the missions).

The art is very cute and fun. The gameplay loop is simple, easy to pick up and never throws too many things at you. This is definitely a casual game. I found it perfect to play while I was talking on the phone or listening to a podcast, because it's a game with no punishing mechanics.

This was made by essentially 3 people - two people did the artwork and programming and one person did the music and sounds. And they did it all in 6 months! That is, frankly, very impressive. I have no real complaints regarding the game, my only nitpicks would be that the music was very cute and pleasant but I would have liked some variety, another track or two would have been nice. And the changing parameters for each day (at the start you are told that perhaps a certain type of building is on sale, or there will be more cyclists, etc) don't seem to make enough of an impact and aren't frequent enough.

Those are very minor issues and with a game like this, at a price like this, it really is splitting hairs. I only encountered one bug - I was given the start of day notification that I would be seeing double the cyclists and I had zero cyclists that day.

The price point is good - even better if you get it at a discount. You'll get a few hours at least out of this and I think it's the kind of game you could mess with in sandbox mode for quite a long time while doing other things.

Very cute, fun, and a pleasure to play.\t\t\t\t\t
