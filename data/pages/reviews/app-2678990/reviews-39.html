\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a fantastic street development/management game with an eastern flair. Each level becomes more complex as new buildings and villager types are unlocked, which brought a refreshing complexity and the need for strategy in which buildings, upgrades, and villagers you attract to your street.

The pros:
- Ambiance and art style is perfect. The music is lively without being chaotic, and I enjoyed clicking on villagers to hear them say a soft "hi".
- The price. Amazing for what the game provides with multiple levels, and I sincerely hope DLCs are in this game's future. I'd happily pay a couple dollars for more levels or more complex buildings, villagers, etc.
- More strategic and complex than anticipated. This game actually has you sit and think about which building you pick and which upgrade you choose to reach your end game objective.
- Sandbox mode. I love me a good sandbox aspect to a puzzle game.

The crits:
- Please bring back the demo. I know people who need to test out games for accessibility reasons to see whether or not they can even play the game.
- Accessibility. As "simple" as the game mechanics are with point and click, the trash and Tanuki item can be hard to spot with the natural color scheme. The sparkle animation is a nice touch, but maybe add a sound reveal like when the cats enter the street?
- Villagers tend to merge together, making it difficult to tell who said what.\t\t\t\t\t
