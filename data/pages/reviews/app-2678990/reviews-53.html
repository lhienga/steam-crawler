\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a cute little management game. You build a street with shops, relaxing places like parks and houses where your residents/customers live. Each mission has different goals which feel manageable, as far as I can say at his point. During the nights you spend money to add buildings to your street or upgrade the existing ones. You have to play around with the offerings of your shops to satisfy your customers. When you start the day, your street gets lively and the inhabitants stroll around and visit your shops and parks. When they've visited a shop, you get immediate feedback what they liked and what not, so you can vary the offer during night time. In the mean time you can pick up litter to keep your streets clean or pet the cats.

Some minor issues (for me):
- The "Hi" while clicking on people becomes more annoying over time
- The music is quite repetetive. It fits the game but it is nothing special.
- The hitbox of your cutomers feels a little bit off. You have to click exactly on the body to get the feedback. Together with the fact that there are quite a few customers on the streets, it feels a little bit challenging to catch the person you want. But you can always pause the game so this is not a big issue.

I recommend the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is honestly so charming, I just happened to stumble across it about a week ago and instantly fell in love.

The art style is so cute, the colours are soft but vibrant, and the management just scratches an itch I didn't realise I had. The game only has 5 missions and a sandbox mode, and each mission adds a few new mechanics to keep the game feeling fresh. The level of detail put into every aspect makes the experience feel so polished. Each building feels distinctly unique, and with selection of roofs, colours and upgrades, despite them being pre-made, you genuinely feel like your crafting your own little street community.

I really cannot recommend this game enough, if your looking for a cozy stress-free escape for a few hours with satisfying mechanics, Minami Lane is absolutely perfect. For only $5 it's a genuine steal, not to mention these developers absolutely deserve the support. :) <3 I hope they add more to it in the future.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMinami Lane is such an adorable build-your-own management sim where you design your own street. Unlock and customize your buildings, complete quests & strive to make your residents happy & content.

I found this game browsing the store to see what was out there. It always pays to browse the new releases and not just the popular upcoming ones but all of them! You find hidden gems like this game. As a lover of casual management games and being into games that encourage creativity this one was an automatic try at such a great price.

Gameplay & Highlights

The game is divided up into the main story campaign with 5 missions and a sandbox mode. As you build your street you will have 3 tabs to keep an eye on to track your progress. The first tab shows the overall stats such as your money, number of villagers and satisfaction level. This tab also keeps track of your missions and objectives.

The second tab keeps track of your shops. When you buy a shop, for example a Ramen shop, you can customize your menu, set the prices and see your profits.

The third tab tracks 3 things; Beauty satisfaction, Street satisfaction and Bonus satisfaction. Beauty is how pleasant your street is, does it have parks for example. Street satisfaction refers to shops and shop diversity, prices and recipes. What does your street offer your residents to make them happy? And Bonus satisfaction is wildlife. The first objective being adding cats to your street.

Your progress will automatically save at the end of each day. As you complete a mission you will “finish” the objectives of that street. The next mission will start a new street. However you can keep playing the current mission or advance. The missions unlock one at a time as you complete them. However you can always replay missions you’ve already completed.

Tips & Tricks

* The Tanuki (little Raccoon dog) drops gifts every day so remember to pick them up for extra coin
* People drop money and trash (pick them up) to keep the street clean and get some extra coin
* You can pause the day if you need to take a break and plan out your next moves.
* Supports widescreen monitors
* I do NOT believe you can move your buildings once placed (if so I have not seen a way how). So take a moment to consider placement and if your business is set up to attract older people or youths, and their placement next to houses for youths or older people (each have their own type of dwellings).
* In mission 3 to attract more cats, build at least 3-4 markets or Konbini that sell fish (Taiyaki). You can do away with houses on this level and focus on businesses (as some business upgrades increase the number of people that visit vs beauty upgrades).

Overall Experience

This game is adorable and fun and will provide you with about 2-4 hours of game play at a very reasonable price. This game is a complete experience and no future content is planned but it will be supported for bugs and fixes. My experience so far is fun and relaxing with some challenges, and just the right amount of game-play (I am on mission 4 currently). I want to give thanks to Doot & Blibloop for a great little game. It is entertaining, adorable and wholesome. I look forward to any future projects they may release in the future. 🦝
