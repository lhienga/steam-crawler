\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMinami Lane is beyond cute, from the art to the quick mission-based "story" and the chill music. After getting through all the missions, it left me desiring more games like this as breathers from other games and life; we need more intentionally short (2-4 hours) games that you can skip one day's worth of coffee for, just so that there's more wholesome art being put out there for fun.

I love that there's a sandbox mode with two different options, which I promptly went to after completing all the missions. Although a relatively quick game, part of the charm for me is that while you can play around with customizing the little shops and all, it's not overwhelming, so you get around to actually completing your tasks without much of a decision paralysis on things (as I would for things like character creation, where I could easily lose 5+ hours just getting stuck on creating one thing).

Before getting it, I briefly got nostalgic for the old mobile game Happy Street. I'm glad there's an "ending", because it leaves you with such joy and you don't get tired of it. It's just simply perfect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA full review of the game, broken down into The Good, The Bad, and The Ugly.
Plus, wishes for the future.

Total score: 8/10

The Good
For a game made in only 6 months and at such a low price, it is phenomenal. Cute and fun!
If you like a casual, relaxing business/builder sim ... this is for you!

I'm not saying more, because I don't want to spoil any of the good stuff.
Instead, I'll just give you some general scores:

Gameplay: 8/10
Graphics: 9/10
NPC programming: 6/10
Fun & Relaxation: 9/10

The Bad
Don't read this if you're sensitive to spoilers !!!

Apart from a few bugs that the devs may or may not already be aware of, the game also has some ... peculiarities that could be better, I think.

Bugs I encountered (that may or may not have been fixed by the time you're reading this):

* Townies' sprites sometimes switch appearance (and age group!) when interacting with venues (i.e., parks and open shops).
* Townies' sprites pathing (incl. cats) is very glitchy, meaning they frequently clip into each other as they walk over/into each other (which makes them flicker). This not only looks bad but also makes clicking/tracking them to get shop feedback very hard and cumbersome. Villagers will also walk in the streets (why?) and right into the path of cyclists, who will just run them over (meaning they just clip through them too). Same goes for cats. Although they are confined to the other sidewalk, they also walk through each other. Needs TLC. A lot of it. 
* "Daily news" sometimes affects the wrong age group (e.g., news said elders would have less money, but the report showed it affected youths instead).

These mechanics could be better:

* Recipes: No townie ever wanted more than 4 of any given ingredient, but there is no cap on the -/+ selection (I managed to go over 100 and the numbers didn't fit anymore). The visual representation also stops at 3 each (I think), so there is no reason why the number selection doesn't stop at 3 and then says/goes to "Max." (instead of 4).
* Buildings: Instead of just recycling a building, I'd really like to be able to swap/switch existing buildings AND be able to add a building in between existing ones. This is purely for cometics' sake, but to build a visually pleasing street, this is a must. If we can "vanish" a building, deleting the space, then we should also be able to insert one, creating space.
* Report: It would be very much appreciated if the shops were listed by type, instead of by lineup/addition (to better compare recipe satisfaction). Even better if we could sort them by different parameters (age, type, income, etc.).
* Upgrades: Instead of having to choose between upgrades (people/beauty) I'd rather appreciate it if we could buy both (or at least be able to switch upgrades for visuals' sake). I'd be happy with the either-or choice for a challenging mission, but, in general, I'd prefer the addition system. This also makes more sense in irl terms, as shops naturally attract more customers when they become more attractive/beautiful. Right?
* Customization: The colors/swatches that exist are nice and match well, for the most part. Not only, though, do I wish there were more color choices, but also that we could better choose individual parts (e.g. a green fir for the park but also one pink and one yellow deciduous tree—or a different color "beauty upgrade" tree that would belong to another swatch).
* Music: I like it, but it gets too repetitive.

The Ugly
There's none. (Gotchu! Hehe ...) This game is amazing, despite its flaws.

What I wish would come with an update ...
* more music
* more missions
* more customization options
* quality-of-life changes

All in all, a wonderfull game. 100% recommended!
Just needs a bit more polishing to become the best version of itself!

PS: I'd love it if the days didn't stop at 8 p.m. and we saw nighttime too. If that doesn't happen, then I'd really love a "night market" mission that switches day to night where the shops shine in their beautiful glow ... just the way we see them at the end during credits (such ethereal beauty!)
