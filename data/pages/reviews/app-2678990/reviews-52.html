\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually leave reviews, but after watching MadMorph play the demo on Youtube and absolutely having to buy this game as soon as it released - and now having played it for seven hours - I felt compelled! This adorable little game has lovely graphics, and the sandbox modes are an easy way to spend hours in this game without realizing it. I know that the creator has said that they don't intend to release any more levels, but after beating all five within the first two hours of playtime, I would love love LOVE to see more.

The music is very, very repetitive, but can be turned off separately from the sound fx, so it's hardly worth mentioning. If the dev decides to expand the game, a new soundtrack per level (and perhaps those on rotate in sandbox) would be a major improvement. But that's the thing - that's all that I had to bring up negatively.

This game is just so cozy and cute. A great way to unwind and disappear into a fantasy of a Japan-esque street that you're designing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuch a short & sweet cozy game!

If you are looking for a cozy management sim, you've come to the right place. In Minami Lane, you'll manage your own street and make sure your villagers are happy. There are five chapters and each chapter introduces a new mechanic - whether it be a new building type, a new villager type, or a new interaction. Often times to clear a level, you'll have to implement a trial and error method in order to make sure each shop has the perfect selection and price to match. On the fifth chapter, the street is officially your own to make it however you'd like.

Often times, management sims can be a bit tedious to sit and watch your developments unfold, but Minami Lane takes the tediousness away by having little seek and finds with liter, cats, cyclists, and tanuki - keeping you occupied until the end of the day. The game-play is semi-complex where you'll need to complete challenges to progress the game and tackle optional challenges - such as finishing a level with fewer than 9 buildings. Despite having these challenges, you never feel overwhelmed and don't feel punished for not meeting certain criteria.

In addition to the game-play offering an incredibly fun and stress-free environment, the cozy pastel visuals are absolutely beautiful. The game is well worth the cheap price and you can tell a lot of love put into it. I'd highly recommend picking this one up if you are a cozy gamer or enjoy a short and sweet management sim.\t\t\t\t\t
