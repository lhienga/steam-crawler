\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit for clarity: while this review says I've only played 2.9 hours at the time of this review, I have fully completed the game while offline. Sorry for any confusion!

Dragon Age: Origins is a modern classic, that while having flaws, is nonetheless a fantastic RPG that any serious fan of the genre should play. The characters, lore, dialogue, choices, and music of this title are deeply romantic, brutal, and epic. Playing Dragon Age is like if J.R.R. Tolkien wrote Lord of the Rings to be a little more morally grey and sexy.

My largest complaints with this game are the pacing and gameplay during certain questlines. The strategic, slow pace of the gameplay mixed with lengthy levels and tough enemies exhaust my patience. For example, the Deep Roads made me quit for several months. (Sorry, fans of this game!) The gameplay didn't make sense to me at the start, either. Team composition and spell/skill combos were not part of my knowledge going into Dragon Age, so it was a bit of an adjustment. Steep learning curve, but fulfilling once you gain a decent understanding.

Overall, Dragon Age: Origins is one of BioWare's greatest hits and a fantastic RPG. While it may drag on (pun not intended) in places, Ferelden is worthy of your time and investment. I give this game 9 Goth Witch Girlfriend points out of 10.\t\t\t\t\t

A Priceless Gem

There will be a TL;DR at the end.

~

I have fond memories of this game.
During a very dark time in my life, I found this game for sale on the Xbox store.
Booting up my 360 back in 2013 when I first happened upon this title.
I binged it for months.

Bed ridden, in the hospital, between 2013 and 2018 I managed to play through Dragon Age: Origins a total of 5 times!

While the sequels did what they could to put their own unique spin on this lightning-in-a-bottle product, it's clear to see when viewed from the lens of a consumer in 2024 that this title simply can never be replicated.

I could prattle on and on about this game.
It has a great character creator.
Lots of different options for who you can romance.
Lots of ways to start the game with different runs.
Tons of different options!
The world is expansive with actually well written and thought out lore!
You could honestly play this game for well over 60 hours if you take your time with it.
The DLCs are all-included and make accessing the game a lot more fun and interesting.
There's a perfect balance between dark fantasy and human levity.
The story is gripping and GOOD!
The combat varies between simplistic and highly tactical.

There's a reason I've spent well over 320 hours playing this game on my XBOX.

The only reason I stopped after 29 hours is because, honestly, even after so many years having not played it - everything was so familiar that I felt like I was just padding my own time.
I don't need to add another 40 hours of enjoyment to remember Dragon Age; Origins - I already have around 360 cumulative hours in this game and love it to death.

The only shame is knowing that it could never be made again today:
- The world is massive, which is a feat that was accomplished by lowering graphical fidelity.
- The combat style of mixing TBS style movesets with MMORPG style isometric combat is both daring and demanding.
- Lots of sensitive matters are brought up succinctly and humanely (Never gonna happen)
- The game knows when to be visceral, bleak, and dark ; it knows when to be powerful, courageous, and human.

Oh, and P.S.
Boys fall for Morrigan - because she's pretty and interesting.
Men fall for Leliana - because devotion and love is more important than looks and thrills.
Chad's don't fall for either - because you can either be a broken person's first love, or a desperate soul's last hope, but neither will bring you peace.

~

TL;DR
9/10.
Dragon Age: Origins is an Amazing game!
Like a precious pearl found in a treacherous sea, the unique marbling and blemishes on its face do little to devalue it in the face of others, but instead plead for greater exultation for its bespoke majesty and intrinsic beauty.
It is the luster of gold that never tarnishes, the rusted sword that never tires, and the shattered hand-mirror that you can't help but remember with a broken heart and ruminate of days lost to time.\t\t\t\t\t
