On the first time through I didn't care much for this game, but this time around I was really fond of it. I love the combat system and when you play a Mage you really do feel powerful, and they do a great job reminding you that the power often comes at a cost to your party with friendly fire damage. The companions were written rather well and the quest lines you get to participate in do feel important to the direction of the world, most of the time anyway. The only thing that soured this experience was knowing that the direction of the games would culminate to Inquisition which if you desire to play all of them, I would rush through that one. But Origins is absolutely worth playing through and enjoying every second of it, save for those menial miscellaneous quests.\t\t\t\t\t

Good game

  • In terms of story it's very good
  • Graphics are dated naturally, it came out 14 years ago, don't care about graphics myself though
  • The game-play leaves a bit to be desired and can be annoying at times, with some enemies it's just a matter of stun-locking them/killing them quickly or being stun-locked yourself. I still enjoyed it overall though (Throwing a fireball at a group of enemies is always fun)
  • The balancing is a bit odd, the strongest enemy in the game was a random spider mini boss in a cave (♥♥♥♥ the corrupted spider queen)
  • The characters are good, they aren't dull or boring
  • The DA world is pretty cool, some choices you make you can carry over between games which is neat
  • Character creation is a plus for me in games

    (I do have more than 4 hours btw, just wasn't playing through steam at the time)
