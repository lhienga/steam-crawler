\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is simply one of the finest RPGs ever designed. Origins stays true to it's tabletop predecessors by crafting a world with depth and focusing on player agency. The story itself is not all that grand, simply you are the only ones capable of stopping the big evil that brings the apocalypse in it's wake, and its up to you to stop it. While not truly non linear, from the very beginning you are given a level of agency over your place in the story that is very rarely seen. Each of the many different origin options place you immediately in situations where your decisions matter. Even in the small decisions, you will find that your actions have weight. People will react differently to almost all actions you take, and you will find many decisions affect your outcome by the end.

The user interface and controls are quite good. Everything follows the same directionality, top to bottom then left to right which makes navigating each menu easy. The split real time/paused action mechanic is a great translation of classical turn based combat. You get both the faster pace of real time mixed with the more tactical approach found in turn based. Every encounter is designed around this tactical approach, which makes the combat far more rewarding. Discovering new synergies or ways to deal with similar patterns feels great.

This is a must play for anyone who is a fan of RPGS as a genre. It is also best considered as a standalone title in my opinion, as the sequels do not excel at all in the ways that Origins does. They are simply not RPGS, at least not in the same way Origins is. The true disappointment in Origins is the knowledge that you will never be able to play another game like it.

While Baldur's Gate 3 suggests to us that RPGs may return to being a dominant force in the market soon, that doesn't mean we can't go back to older titles. I recommend this especially to anyone who has found themselves enjoying RPGs recently when they previously did not. This is an excellent gateway into the more classical design philosophies of RPGs, especially to those of the 90's and before as this title bridges the gap between the two.

I have played it before on X-box 360, so I know that it's a worthwhile game. It is a traditional rpg set in medieval times, and it handles the storyline well. Basically, you start off as one of 6 different locals for the tutorial. Once completed, you move on to a besieged city, where you discover that a blight of monstrous zombie-type creatures are preparing to wipe out the human race and spread their numbers further across the land. It is up to you to join the grey wardens of the land to fight against their forces and bring peace to the land once and for all. For anyone looking for a modest rpg with a gripping storyline and great combat action, I would highly recommend playing the game at least once.\t\t\t\t\t
