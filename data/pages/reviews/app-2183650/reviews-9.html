\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompleted the game 100% before writing this review.

This is pretty much the version of Dive I wanted to play when I first started. I played the Global version of Online for well over a year, was heavily invested in all the events and raids and could be considered a top player of my server. While the memories are good, playing Offline really put into perspective as to how badly monetised the Gacha was and how it was handled. Progress was arbitrarily slow and low % drops made unlocking new things miserable.

Dive Offline does away with all that, adds a linear progression system and becomes this sort of sandbox playground you can make as easy or as hard as you please. Unlocking everything is an eventuality and it is genuinely fun to play if you don't take it too seriously and realise it's not a true Megaman game. Not all the content from the game is there and of course all PvP and CooP modes are gone, but there is still a considerable amount of campaign, event, race and challenge stages to play.

Sure what is here is a vehicle made up from a game that just wanted to trick players into gambling for pulls, it's very obvious in a lot of places. Nothing stops you from taking the absurdly strong character they give you six levels in and beating the whole campaign in a couple hours, but I don't think this is meant for that at all. It's unlock your favourite characters, toys and then play with them until you get bored or are a completionist. I still don't get why they couldn't just rig a peer to peer connection for CooP and PvP, those were fun with friends. Also the lack of collaboration characters when all properties belonged to Capcom to begin with. But I still think the amount of content is worth the price of admission and hope they go make some actual Megaman games now the Gacha one is done with.\t\t\t\t\t
