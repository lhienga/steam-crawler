\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
+ Great presentation and fanservice. It's hard to not smile when you see Zero cosplaying as Dracula, Iris in a swimsuit, or Sigma cosplaying as a Japanese delinquent. Each character also do these really sick intros when you select them, and believe me, there are a LOT of characters. If you are a Megaman afficonado, the character select screen will make you cream your pants. It is magical.

+ Great music. The soundtrack consists of remixes of iconic tracks from every Megaman game over the past 40 years. Most of them are bangers just like the originals.

Cons:
- This game is not a Megaman game. It is a gacha game wearing a Megaman skin - the kind of game that has numbers attached to literally everything to incentivize players to pull more gacha to see bigger numbers. At higher difficulties, you could spend 10 minutes shooting a regular enemy before they die if you don't upgrade your weapon, character, equip cards, equip chips, etc... It's not about your skill as a player anymore. It's about how long you spend in like 10 different menus before every stage fiddling with your equipment, skill upgrades, card loadout, etc etc... In a regular Megaman game, if you're a skilled enough player, you can play from the start all the way to the final boss without picking up a single upgrade or navigating any menu other than the boss select screen. That is not the case with X DIve. It is a tedious number-cruncher, which is antithetical to what a Megaman game is like. Here's a disclaimer: I don't hate gacha games or RPGs. In fact, I love both. However, I enjoy Megaman games in a completely different way from how I enjoy those two types of games, and mixing them all together was a recipe for disaster.

- Bad gameplay. Everything about the gameplay is just a gimped, watered-down version of regular Megaman gameplay. The movement and the combat - both key ingredients of any Megaman game, feel weak and sluggish. In any other Megaman game with melee combat, your melee hits interrupt enemies, make satisfying sounds, and look quite flashy. In X-Dive, melee weapons feel awful because your attacks just phase through enemies like your Z-saber doesn't even exist. The only feedback you get is a white number that pops up on top of the enemy when your weapon's hitbox connects with their hurtbox. The movement is also really janky. Every other input doesn't register. Half the time, your character won't jump or dash when you want them to. Wall jumping is also really janky. Half the time, your character will not kick off the wall when you want them to. Everything about the gameplay just feels off, like it's something that resembles Megaman gameplay, but is actually not.

- Awful story and translation. The story is this stupid token gacha self-insert story with zero meaningful stakes, characters, or themes. None of the characters you know and love are actually here and none of them are real in this universe. You don't feel attached to anyone and the plot is a padded out, disjointed mess. You get fed literal breadcrumbs of plot for every 5-6 stages you trudge yourself through, and there are hundreds of stages in this game. I find myself not caring for the story at all; I just completed the stages to grind for more currency so I can unlock more characters. Then, I started asking myself: why is a Megaman game such a grindfest? This is clearly not an RPG like Battle Network or Starforce, but why am I still grinding my balls off?

Did I also mention that the translation is incomprehensible? The first few chapters were actually serviceable, but by chapter 5, the dialogue devolved into absolute gibberish as the translators probably thought no one would care about the story past chapter 4. Broken grammar, awkward word choice, typos, weird spacing and syntax - the whole shebang.

TLDR:
X-DIve was fun and fanservicey for the firsts few hours, but the more you play it, the more the cracks start showing, eventually revealing an abomination that Capcom has slapped together out of every Megaman IP they've ever created. The story sucks, the gameplay is the opposite of what a Megaman game should be like, and the translation is awful. Even if you are a giga Megaman fan, I still wouldn't recommend picking this game up at full price. It is not the Megaman you know and love.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCapcom made a solid Megaman game again in Megaman X Dive Offline! From a roster that contains over 100+ playable characters with their own unique abilities, to delivering a fast, action-packed game mechanic that exceeds the earlier titles, this game makes it very easy for players to play for hours non-stop. Every character, including the original Megaman and Roll, can double-jump, air-dash and wall-bounce in this game - a feature that was missing in the classic Megaman titles. A ton of customizations are available; as this was formerly an online pay-real-money-to-win gatcha game, this offline version allows players to use the in-game currency (Zenny), along with tickets and medals, earned after completing missions to purchase all sorts of weapons, skins and armor upgrades for their favorite characters. Players can revisit stages to take on the increasingly difficult missions in Challenge mode to acquire rare items that they couldn't receive in Story mode. And talk about nostalgia; most of the 900+ stages in this game are recycled remakes of the stages that appeared in the previous titles in the entire Megaman universe! Players can dive into the action while listening to a fleet of familiar, mostly remastered, tunes from the past. All-in-all, especially regarding the game mechanics and customizations, this is an amazing example of what the future games in the Megaman franchise should look like. After all, the playable characters are technically robots in the story. Robots can be tweaked/modified in real life. Why not ditch the limitations from the classic games completely and continue to allow players to modify their robotic characters as they see fit? Megaman X Dive Offline, you rock! (A+++)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, for context--I played the original F2P gacha version of this game. And while I really enjoyed the actual gameplay of it, the gacha mechanics ruined it for me. So I played it until I hit the wall and gave up.

Well, now the game is offline, and it is much better for it. There is no more gacha. There are no more microtransactions (or macrotransactions, for that matter). Pay one time and the game is yours, and everything is obtainable via the in-game shop (which ONLY takes in-game currency!) And that in-game currency is much, MUCH easier to get hold of. You're also no longer beholden to RNGesus to get what you want. You can just go to the shop and buy it. Instead, the best weapons and characters are now locked behind story and level progression--as are special events. They've included nearly every special event they ever ran in the game (with the exception of cross-game collaborations, like Street Fighter), and once you hit level 60, you can turn them on and off at will.

The game looks great and plays well. Character models are pretty detailed for what's basically a mobile port, and the controls are surprisingly tight (this is a Mega Man game after all. Tight controls are mandatory here!). The audio is great too, featuring modernized arrangements of classic stages from almost the entire Mega Man history. And there's loads of characters, too, from MMX, ZX, Command Mission, Battle Network, and even Legends! You'll play through many of the classic stages as well, broken up into segments, with both a miniboss and stage boss at the end--but there's a problem. You won't be facing the bosses you expect. The "plot" of the game revolves around you trying to repair the "deep log", a core of memories surrounding all these games. The Deep Log is damaged, and there's all kinds of errors with the games that you, as the player, must fix--by blowing everything to smithereens, of course. It's a little silly, but it serves as a vehicle to move things forward.

When this game was a F2P gacha game, I recommended it, but with several caveats related to the fact that it's basically gambling and it's going to annoy you to death. Those problems are gone now, and I can now recommend it without qualifiers. It's a solid title and it's definitely worth 30 bucks. Is it the next MMX game we've been wanting? No. But it's still fun in its own right, and there's A LOT of content to unlock, so there's plenty to keep you playing for a long, long time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has played about 10 hrs of the online version of X-Dive and over 70 hrs of the Offline version I would absolutely recommend it, with some caveats. I find myself really enjoying the changes made to remove all the gotcha systems in X-Dive Offline. There is no need for an internet connection. This version of the game is 100% DRM/Gotcha Free! The game is still a grind, make no mistake, but the progression feels much better as you unlock more tiers of shop Items/Characters/Weapons/Cards by playing through story mode.

Eventually you will unlock the events mode which you can toggle on and off which events you want to play at will, adding even more levels/environments to enjoy with your favorite characters. In addition there is (Jacob's Elevator): A multi-level Challenge/Wave mode with better rewards as you move up in the stages, (Race Mode): which has you blasting through enemies in remixed stages for the best time and rewards, and of course (Challenge Mode) a harder variant of story mode with more enemies and life pickups and additional star rewards for stage completion.

The card system is a neat time capsule of the series artwork and events and it's fun trying to figure out the best card combinations for a specific character. I really like that they added a soundtrack mode to listen to all the awesome remixed Mega Man Music! (Though I wish you could pick a song to favorite and play in all the menus).

You can remap your button configuration to a game pad and adjust the in game settings to behave more like a classic Mega Man X Game (Toggle: Precision Jumping, Auto Aim, Manual Aim, Double Tap Direction to Dash etc. and Change the Opacity of the mobile icons for weapon stamina off if you like more screen visibility). One caveat, You do need a mouse for navigating the menus properly, though you can use the analog sticks (Not Recommended) and so I agree that it only really has partial controller support. I have heard that the game may also have issues with certain game controllers (X-Input?). I use a wired PDP brand Direct-Input Nintendo Switch pad myself.

One piece of advice for newcomers, If you find yourself behind the power curve for the levels and events, You need to engage with all the power up systems to upgrade your Characters, Weapons, Cards, Chips, Dive Triggers etc. (There is a helpful guide if you click on the power meter.) I was able to buy all my favorite characters and upgrade them with in game currencies relatively quickly. The added end game credits sequence and new song really sells it for me as a complete Mega Man game experience.

One last point of criticism though; The story can be a little grating at times (Don't worry, there is a skip button), with all the bad voice overs, and lack of coherent sentences. It is a passable if a little corny story, (But I mean, What do you expect from a mobile port really?), and I think the lack of voice over options hurts the experience overall, but hey at least you can change the in game text to your language!

Overall I'd say Good job on Capcom for preserving this game for folks to enjoy and Mod in the future. For those of you on the fence about a purchase, or those of you who have invested a lot of time/money in the online version of X-Dive, I'd say maybe wait for a sale. It is a good game for casual, pick up and play sessions, and while it is a little shallow mechanically, it still nails the feels of a Mega Man game (with some adjustments).

Do give this a try if you are nostalgic for the Mega Man series as a whole! It is a fun, shoot'em up, platforming, romp through remixed stages from throughout the series history and with amazing remixed music to boot! All with your favorite Mega Man characters + some original newcomers!

8/10 Would Recommend!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall this is a fun experience. It's easy to pick up and play, and theres a lot of content bundled in. It plays well, considering it's mobile game roots.

The most interesting thing about this is the fact that it exists at all. Taking a gacha game, removing all the f2p micro-transaction nonsense, and bundling it as a premium title is a very interesting move that I hope more studios draw from.

That said, it's still a mobile game with all the mobile game tropes: lots of collectible characters, somewhat watered down gameplay compared to the source material, and a ton of grinding.

My biggest gripe is that this game could REALLY use another localisation pass. A lot of the dialog in this is super stilted and feels very rough. It's understandable, but sprucing up the dialog would go a long way for presentation.\t\t\t\t\t
