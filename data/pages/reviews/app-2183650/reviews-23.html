\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen i found out that Rockman X Dive was getting a version that can be played offline with gacha elements removed I was slightly optimistic but still a bit worried about it. The original game on mobile was quite badly designed, in the sense that it's a fast-paced action platformer that was designed to be played on a touch screen, which meant that levels in it were rather flat in terms of geometry.

Now, those same exact levels are in a game that has controller, unchanged. This might be one of the most boring games i've played, and i didn't even play that much of it. The unlock system changes are a good start, but i would've not locked everyone behind high player level still. It's still a grindfest.

And this isn't even mentioning the port's quality. No controller support for menus, despite having actual controller support. Instead, you either need to use the mouse or just move the cursor with your controller's control stick (or DPAD, if you changed it in the settings). This makes navigating menus take a really bloody long time, and gives a bit of dissonance too, i felt a bit ill sometimes from it, since the cursor with the controller has a weird momentum to it.

I will say though, If this port is getting ported to phones, You'd definitely have a better experience with the GUI than the original game's. The GUI on the online version of X DiVE was a bloody mess at points.

The only reason to play this is to get a little bit of a dopamine hit from seeing little robots explode, which, to be honest, is a fine reason to play this, but don't expect to be able to play it in long bursts and not feel incredibly bored.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt turns out, when you yank the 'gacha' out of 'mobile gacha game'... you actually end up with an okay game? Don't get me wrong, XDiVE was never amazing, but when the game wasn't falling down the usual daily-repetition, FOMO-powered, gambling-addict traps of a Mobile structure, it was okay. And this version actually does a good job of culling out the bad.

All of the characters and weapons from throughout Mega Man's history (all the fun stuff) is unlockable just by playing the game, and the progression has been massively rebalanced in the player's favour; before long you'll be unlocking new characters and weapons almost faster than you can play them. Other tweaks, like culling some unnecessary stat inflated repeats in favour of Challenge Modes where your level doesn't matter, also help suit this game more towards a condensed, finite experience instead of the usual daily attention grabs. You can even access all the event-specific limited time content when you want to.

There's some losses; mainly, the online-specific modes (Co-op, PVP) are gone, since this is Fully Offline. Yet that seems an acceptable loss in return for a permanently available, moddable-in-the-future, actually player friendly version of this collection of Mega Man's history.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm just adding a positive review to make sure it's plainly stated that the game is a single purchase with no additional buy-ins. You can even turn on or off game events to simulate the various festivals they had and such and unlock event characters and weapons. It's great.

As far as an actual review, it's a pretty fun game, it definitely has its mobile oddities and a small amount of jank so far but everything is free and you unlock things at reasonable rates compared to the original gacha. It's fun to collect characters and weapons, especially without having to grind your day away to do so. It also allows you to remap all your controls which is goooooooooooooooooooooood! (What kinda weirdo dashes with B?)

Gameplay-wise it's X8 with slightly different physics, you collect characters who have unique skills and weapons that have different movesets and effects, don't expect the depth of X8 though, but there's a lot to collect. Graphics-wise it looks like X8 did, with a few more lighting and model details. Nothing to write home about but it gets the job done, it's pretty, and it's the first time we see a lot of these characters in 3D, let alone playable (3D Omega... mmmmm...)
The story is.. well a Mega Man story.

If you never played the gacha game it's a decent value if you're into grinding and seeing numbers go up. If you did play the gacha, the game is coming to End of Service soon, so I'm glad it's here but I can see why people who invested in it would be upset even if I can't sympathise.

Lastly, as a side note: I wish more EoS games would get a free version rebalanced like this one. We have lost so many decent to good games through their deaths, lots of MMOs and stuff that vanished off the face of the earth never to be seen again, and that's a shame.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the offline version of Mega Man X Dive, a gacha game which originally held grindy progression, awful monetization, and even worse RNG. This offline version, however, is Mega Man X Dive as it always should have been.

Those bad things about the original X Dive that everyone hates? Grindy progression? Gone. Awful monetization? Gone. RNG? Maybe only present in level completion drops, but otherwise gone and not garbage anymore. In the original game you had to replay previous missions on hard mode in order to unlock your next area's standard difficulty, that seems to be gone now too. You unlock stages normally like any other game.

Pros:
-Simple yet fun
-Lots of content, characters and weapons to collect
-No Microtransactions or super-grindy progression
-All base and seasonal content included
-Could probably still run on a potato rig

Nitpicks:
-Controlling this game's UI, like the original version on PC, just feels weird as Left Stick controls the mouse. Not really bad, just weird.
-Okay, we can all admit, the story sucks. But you don't play this game for the story.

Cons:
-Crossover Content (Monster Hunter, Street Fighter, Devil May Cry) Content is absent in this version
-Multiplayer was gutted entirely in this version

I stopped playing the original version after about 3.4 hours of playtime because of the grind and monetization, but this version... oh, it's wonderful. It's pretty much what I wished X Dive was from the beginning. If you hated X Dive for similar reasons I did but enjoyed its gameplay, you'll love this. If you didn't enjoy any of it anyway, this might not change your mind.\t\t\t\t\t
