【MEGA MAN X DiVE Offline Review】

I begrudgingly tried and fell in love with MEGA MAN X DiVE when it was initially released here on Steam. I was curious as to how it played, and was delighted to see I got addicted to it (never spent a dime on it, tho).

I wasn't so surprised to hear it was sunsetting so soon, but was PLEASANTLY surprised to hear they were going to release an Offline version of it. After my time with the OG and seeing the price, it was a no-brainer, and I'm thankful I got a chance to try it when it was "F2P" prior to it shutting down so I had a better idea of what I was getting into. Otherwise, I may have overlooked this lil' MEGA nugget.

There was still some concern over how this would port over, but I am thankful in my modest time spent that it is literally just as it's pitched - the same game, but offline.

I hope this review helps those that missed the F2P demo :-)

All Switched ON!
+Celebration of ALL things Mega Man!
Wowzers, that's alotta Mega Man! This is one of the Mega Man-est of all Mega Man titles. There is a huge cast of characters (and interesting alts) that you can receive and power up, and you can equip them how you'd like (think Roll Caskett from Mega Man Legends using Zero's Lightsaber and a bazooka... or Sigma using X's buster arm and a huge fiery sword). Or Dr. Light dressed as Ryu? Odd that still made it in, as all the crossover content was removed, but still delicious none the less.

If you love Mega Man, there is at least one character, if not 2-3 different versions of them, for you here!

SO glad to see MegaMan Volnutt in there!

+GOTYA! mechanics entirely purged
It's like the game just got back from an L.A. liposuction - literally all the gristle has been trimmed and it's now beach-ready. What's left is just the game. The pull mechanic is gone, and now you just play the game to earn the mats to unlock the stuff (or via story achievements).

+Mobile Game controls toggleable
I would not have been able to play the original if there weren't ways to make it look less like a cheap mobile game, and I'm thankful those options have totally transferred over.

Turn off auto-aim and charge assist, set precision jumping, put UI transparency up to 100%, and switch the dash button. Turn off health bars above yourself/enemies, as well as floating damage #s.

+Toggable Events
The in-game events have made it over (again, sans anything crossover), and you can toggle them to be active.

+Price tag
Listen - $30 is NOT bad. Did you SEE my review for Bomb Rush Cyberflunk, in which they are asking $40 minimum for a game that foogazy and without proper skyboxes? (This game has proper sky boxes, in case you were wondering)

At the end of the day, I will GLADLY pay $30 for a game with this much Mega Man art/content that has utterly changed it's monetization philosophy so that I may enjoy all it has to offer at a one-time admission fee, and entirely at my own pace.

They coulda tried to charge $40/$50 or have tiered pricing, but it's literally just $30.

+Runs great on Gabe Gear (/Steam Deck)
Yup, 'nuff said. Other than some might prefer it on Gabe Gear due to the menu controls.

Code: Error
-No more co-op of any sort.
It was fun! Sucks that there is no co-op/remote play, but WTH, I don't care enough to really see it as a full detriment.

-No Crossover material
I get it. Sort of. Like, why couldn't Chun-Li/Ryu/Akuma stay? And how come Dr. Light dressed as Ryu is still in? Maybe modders will fix it. Again, not a full detriment, but worth knowing what you're missing.

-Plot makes no sense
Don't come into this expecting some decent plot pulling all the series together. Understand and respect it merely serves as a backdrop to the gameplay and system menus, and just roll with it.

I can't speak to this version fully yet, but there were some questionable translations in the F2P, so if that grates your gears, grind elsewhere. Nothing confusing, just confounding at times.

TL;DuuuuRRRRRRR:
I absolutely recommend MEGA MAN X DiVE Offline for 3 main reasons:
🔩Lots of Mega Man art/content
🔩Supporting Online-only titles getting Offline versions
🔩In a summer of blockbusters, $30 is a nice price point for the content on offer

If you like Mega Man X or the franchise in general, there is something for you here to ^.^ squee ^.^ over. It's enough of an adherence to the old formula (with the right settings) adding the remixes thrown into a pick-up & play anytime/as much as you want Mega Man X experience that I think is well-worth the DiVE.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe closest MMX game we have to an actual X9.
This is not perfect as it is indeed a mobile game in nature.
But XDiVE was already a faithful mobile game in the first place, the only issues it had was the greedy monetization and imbalance level difficulty & grindy progression.

This version actually fixed most of those. 1 hour in I was able to get the S characters that originally worth ~$100 dollars.
It is actually way less grindy than I thought.

The amount of content is also ridiculously rich. The number of source material characters & boss battles combined is already worth the purchase I feel.
Number of stages is actually not as many as advertised since those are mini-stages designed for mobile. But still, they are great remakes of the old games and combining a few of them gives you a full experience of the classic stages.
There are also rebalance here and there to make it feels like a complete console experience instead of those mobile games non-sense.

Recommended for MMX fans and MM fans in general.
Props to Capcom Taiwan / Capcom for not abandoning their hard work in remaking all those characters/stages/bosses.


Edit: I just realize they actually recorded a brand-new theme song just for this release and it is beautiful. One of the best theme songs out of any Capcom games.
I believe one stage is brand new to this version also.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst and foremost just to get it out of the way, I want to make two things clear. The first is that this game is confirmed to work on Steam Deck. The second is that this is a game made for fans of the series first and foremost. While the game is not devoid of content that those new to Mega Man could enjoy, a majority of the enjoyment value here is in reference to characters and locations from the past of the series. You can think of this as a sort of celebration of the series history.

This is actually a re-release of an already existing mobile game. The original game was a free to play title, but as a result of this it featured aggressive monetization through microtransactions. It was designed to make progression without spending real money a complete and total chore, and rewarded spending large sums of money at a time, even if often only in the form of random chance via gacha mechanics.

This is not that. "Offline" takes away the free to play business model from the game, and as a result everything has been restructured around the idea of making a simple up front purchase. Gone are the days of spending hundreds of dollars/pounds/etc. for the chance to maybe get what you want and have a nice steady progression through the game. Instead, the progression has been streamlined to be much more reasonable and characters are simply unlocked via in-game currency in the shop and through story progression.

As part of this restructuring, the characters were rebalanced with this new form of progression in mind, and many are completely locked off from you at the start of the game and become available through progression. The stronger characters seem to have been generally put into the "Limited" tab and in the "Events" section, costing you Element Metals and Event Tickets.

Gone are the days of Element Metals being premium currency, as since the microtransactions are gone the game will shower you with Element Metals as rewards for completing a stage for the first time as well as for completing various other challenges. They are quite easy to come by and it feels very satisfying being able to unlock characters without having to rely on random chance. The whole thing feels surprisingly well thought out and balanced, with the most overpowered characters not being available right at the very start but becoming available as progression rewards later.

I recommend playing this game with a controller. It feels best when played this way in my opinion, and you can even control the mouse cursor with your d-pad and "confirm" button on your controller to interact with menu's.

A small criticism I do have with the game however is that in order to experience the event stages, you do have to progress to the point of becoming Level 60. I was looking forward to hopping right into experiencing these, as I did not play the Online version of the game often enough to play most of them. However, since the progression is streamlined so well, the task of reaching level 60 is achievable in a small fraction of the time it used to be. It will take hours to do it, but that's better than taking months.

Another small criticism is that the option to disable the auto lock-on mechanic appears to have been removed for some reason. I'm not entirely sure about this, but I haven't been able to find the option anywhere in my time playing so far.

Edit: The option was not removed. Click the button that looks like a pause button on the top left of the screen while playing in a stage to access the menu with the option.

I also want to discuss what else was removed from the Online version of the game. For starters, the Online version featured collaboration events with Street Fighter, Monster Hunter, and Devil May Cry. Through these events, a number of characters themed after these franchises were added to the game such as Dante Trigger X and Vergil Trigger Zero. This collab content was not included in this version of the game, and it was stated by the devs that they have no current plans to include DLC for the game. My speculation on the matter is that while this content is all owned by Capcom, the specific Taiwan branch that developed this game was unable to re-secure the rights from the other Capcom series IP holders for including this content in the re-release. Or to put it another way, the devs responsible for the Street Fighter, Monster Hunter, and Devil May Cry series likely backed out, potentially due to fear of this version of the game being a flop.

Also not included are the Co-op and PvP modes. While this makes sense in regards to the game no longer having server based functionality and being playable completely offline, a form of peer to peer connectivity or even local split screen for playing these modes with friends/family would have been appreciated, and I find it odd that no such thing was implemented. I don't personally consider this a deal breaker by any stretch as these modes honestly weren't that good to begin with, but it does make me sad that they could potentially become lost media.

A few additional positives I would like to mention are some much needed QoL tweaks and new features added into the experience for this version. Although accessing Jakobs Orbital Elevator is now locked behind progression, the stamina system within it was removed allowing you to retry individual stages of it to your hearts content, even as the same character over and over. In fact, the "stamina" system in the game in general was also removed, you are no longer limited in how long you can play per day.

Also, as I mentioned previously all the event stages (not including the collab content as far as I am aware) are included and can be toggled on and off as you see fit for as long as you want to complete at your own pace and in whatever order you desire. Once you are high enough level to access these a wealth of content becomes available to you all at once. Even the holiday themed events can be toggled whenever you want and for however long you want.

There's a new sound test feature that allows you to listen to the in-game music and character voices. This is a really neat thing and it's a convenient way to listen to the various music. I just wish there was an option to replace the main menu theme with whatever music you want by using this.

And finally, because the game is no longer server based there's a new save file system. You can save and load manually, and there's also an autosave feature. You have a bunch of save slots, so you can actually have different save data progression on different slots. This could be useful for allowing different family members to have their own save data, for example.

The final thing I want to bring up is in regards to modding. Because this is now a fully single player experience, the extent to which the game can be modded has been cracked wide open. I'm already aware of plans to add back in the removed collab characters through fanmade mods, and that's likely not even close to what could be possible here. You can also use the opportunity to cheat while playing the game if that's your cup of tea. It's not like it's going to hurt anyone else, so you do you.

Overall I find this to be the current definitive way to play the game. Although it doesn't include all the content that was originally in, the content that is here is much more enjoyable than it ever was in previous versions of the game. I definitely recommend giving this one a shot if you were able to get any sort of enjoyment from the Online version, or if it just looks interesting to you in general.\t\t\t\t\t
