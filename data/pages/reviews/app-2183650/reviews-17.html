\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFollow these instructions to make this game 100% more enjoyable.

1. Play through or press select to skip the tutorial.
2. Load the first stage.
3. Press select on your controller after entering the stage.
4. Scroll down on the options and turn Precise Jumps on.
(Optional for steam deck)5. Use a mouse for all menu navigation.

Seriously, I do not know what these developers were thinking. Not only is it stupid that such a thing is an "option" instead of just built in but the fact that they left it off by default is just...I don't even know.

To be clear there are other options to make it a more true experience such as turning off auto aim or making charge shots work via holding down the button instead of tapping it to let it autocharge...but I don't believe any of them will have as much of an impact on anyone's enjoyment of this game as "Precise Jumps" will.

If you gave up on this game because of how clunky the controls feel I implore you to give it another go. And afterwards to bash whatever developer thought it was a good idea to leave that off by default.

Anyways, with that rant out of the way. I will try giving a somewhat proper review.


Story is told in engrish so don't expect much quality.


Music is basically just ripped from the series so if you like Megaman X music you will probably be happy.


Gameplay is Megaman in the most basic sense of the word. You run and jump and shoot.

You get two weapons to swap between with your selection being between a bunch of projectile options like machine guns, launchers and busters with the only true outlier being melee weapons which all function similar to a basic z saber.

Weapons can be upgraded to boost stats and can have a number of passives unique to them. The passives typically just influence weapon stats like damage/energy consumption while at least one either gives the weapon a unique trait or augements a unique trait that the weapon already had. These can be simple things such as adding an extra projectile to attacks or status effects to them and occasionally they can be more unique/potent such as healing or allowing projectiles to pass through terrain.



You can choose between tons of characters as you might expect and if you haven't heard it already the gatcha elements are completely gone from this version. Everything is earned in game at a fairly reasonable pace. (Reasonable for a typical single player game that is.)

Characters each have two active skills and a ton of passive skills. The active skills typically consist of one ranged and one melee/utility. The passive skills almost exclusively augement the active skills in a variety of ways such as giving them healing or barriers or additional uses or status effects or what have you.

Everything else about customization can be seen as just stats. Some people find stats to be fun, I find them to be boring but for those who are interested there are a fair number of sources of stat augmentations in this game.



The levels run the gambit from boring straight roads with basically nothing to really maze like messes that can be a pain to navigate. I was taken aback by the Megaman ZX floating islands and the sheer amount of up and down traveling that occured during them. To be clear your goal is "always" to the right and there will always be a path leading to it so there is no danger of being truely lost. Still, for those looking at the early stages of the game I can assure you they get more interesting.

In the levels you run from left to right killing baddies. Not much else to say about that honestly. Beyond that you have little collectable items that are hidden in each stage. I expected them to be brain dead to find based on the early stages but was surprised to realize I had to go out of my way to locate them in a number of stages. It gives you something to do beyond just taking out enemies and it honestly made me appreciate the scale of some of the later stages a bit more as a result.




Challenge can be summarized as "whatever you want". The game frontloads more than enough stat boosting options at you to just steamroll the entire thing if you want to. Next to no grind needed, especially if you employ some of the more potent characters.

On the other hand the enemies do make a fair effort at trying to take you out. They are aggressive and can come at you in swarms so if you want some challenge then feel free to keep your defensive stats on the lower end.


My favorite time of feeling completely helpless was during a specific stage where you have these odd floating orbs in the area. If one of the orbs comes into direct contact with you they will hold you in place and do a little bit of damage. One of those times when I got caught by one of them I had like 4-5 wheelie guys just come in from above and drop down on me all casually while another guy just tossed missiles at me. I was dead real quick.

Obviously, I play this game with lower stats so your experience may vary but the point is if you want a challenge you can get one. That being said I "DO NOT" recommend lowering your damage too far. Bosses in particular take a fair bit to take down and are too repetitive to be considered enjoyable to fight for long periods. Maybe consider having a spare set of overpowered weapons for times when you want to just burst down a boring boss?


Overall I would call it a decent time waster. Get it for 30$ or get it on sale is up to you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI initially was very unsure as to whether or not I was going to purchase this game because I was in the same mindset as most people were for a little while. I had a hard time deciding if purchasing a port of a game that was once a free to play mobile game was really worth while because on the surface it seemed a little superfluous to purchase a game that I didn't really enjoy much a couple of years ago when it first came out. However my good friend helped me to realize that if I wanted to support my favorite iteration of Mega Man then I should give the offline version a chance and man am I glad that I made the purchase because this is a pretty solid game. It's in no means the greatest of the franchise but it definitely scratches the itch for some MMX action. Gone are the gacha elements and most of the characters that were in the original X Dive are still here and available to purchase with in game currency. The 30$ price tag might sound steep but there really is so much content that makes the price pretty darn justifiable. However I don't blame anybody who would prefer to wait for a sale on the game. Playing this with a controller really helps make this feel like a genuine Mega Man experience as the controls feel incredibly fluid and snappy. For those wondering, the gacha mechanics have been removed and the grind is heavily reduced. It's like playing a gacha game at 20x the speed.

I had played X dive online originally on mobile for a few months before I dropped it and in less than 1 session I was able to surpass where I was in the online version, not to mention I got a plethora of characters I would've never had gotten without whaling. Controller support also has remapping as it should, though I find it odd you have to choose between dpad or stick, not both for some reason.

The cloud saving does work as well however it's not really like relying on normal steam cloud, despite the fact it has cloud support? It's weird but you have to manually upload your save from the main menu and download it on your other device instead of it just being automatic. Menu navigation also well, technically has controller support but it's just using your controller to move your mouse. Not preferable but it works I guess? This however also means steam deck is actually a very convenient way to play as that has a touchscreen for menu navigation.

I don't particularly think those are deal breakers so I personally recommend this. It's also good to note that this is something I'd also like to support for the fact that it's game preservation which seems to be lost on most companies these days sadly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI both love and hate this game, i played it both while it was live and just now, so i can tell every difference there is between both version of the game, i always felt guilty that i didn't support the live game, being a Megaman X fan, i played it and dropped it hundreds of times and playing it now: it pisses me off to no end to finally see that it was a very well made and competent game with plenty of fun and interesting characters, loyal to it's source material and that it can be hype when it wants and that it was all dragged down and drowned by the cancerous Gotcha business model wich i can't wait for it to die, playing this versions makes me see how cheated we were all for the sake of micro transactions.

Even though it has mobile game level graphics, it sill looks good, not even goode enough, just good, it's exciting to see what characters look like in their animated portraits, their especial animation sequences from their unlock screen and on gameplay, it's not 1 to 1 Mega Man X, it implements an auto target and cool down skills systems, partially because it had to acomodate for touch screens and partially to make characters feel different from each other, and to be honest: it's awesome, the screen can get very crowed and chaotic in a good way, both form the enemies coming at you and from you unloading everything you have and since they gave it a few tweaks like adding some of the Legacy Collections OST arranges, it just feels right.

It's still have some problems remaining from being a Gotcha mobile game, namely :there is a huge power creep with the S Rank characters, it's RPG Elements require a bit of grinding for weapons and so to fulfill missions requirements, some missions feel like padding and the biggest and most unfixable problem to me: it relies too hard on "waifus" (Iris has a surprising amount of attention for a character that dies on the same game she was introduced in, and i never though i'd live too see the day i saw Ferham on a Swim Suit).

Aside of that, the other problems that could be, hopefully ironed out are: you can only change the "touch" HUD's transparency and through that turn it off, but not move it or change it's size and you need to see it to know when your cooldowns are ready, but it's too big and too centered, the graphics settings are limited and finally, and this is more a bit of wishful thinking from me: hopefully they restore the co-op in some fashion.

it's hard to say if it's worht the full price, on the pros:
-there are a TON of charachters and weapons with unique assets and animation, a lot of work was put there.
-there is a lot more to play than in your normal ,run on the mill Megaman game, like 20+ times the characters and like 10+ times the levels.
-it's not that expensive compared to the Legacy Collections.

on the cons:
-it's a repackage, those assets were already paid off by micro transactions.
-it looks and feels like a mobile game.
-aside of the gameplay acomodations (wich to be fair are huge) there was little to no changes to things like it's grapchis or settings.

It's up to you if you think it's worth the cost now or wait for a price drop or sale, i PERSONALLY don't think buying it is about "helping the franchise" and am relucant to call it a "right step"... but , and this comming from a Mega Man fan: im not regreting buying it, so even though i can't whole heartedly tell you to buy it now ASAP, i can tell you i think that if you are a Megaman fan, you do get and play this game at some point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: decent game, stripped of online components like PvP and Co-op, things got reworked and changed, and grinding has drastically been reduced. 7/10 would play again. Might not shell for mobile port.

So...X DiVE Offline dropped 3 days ago and I have 14 hours sunk into my main save, completing around 90% of the game's Normal Mode Story with UAX, Doom Blade, and Boomerang Blade after using Gravitational Pulse for a short period. I could have used other stuff, but my main goal was Shadow Armor X unlock, which required Chapter 20-6 to be cleared...very late into the main story. Shadow Armor X will be my cleanup character for my main save, getting missing elements and speed grinding event coupons and what not. I'm gonna finish the UAX run Offline and may start a streaming file some time this month to play the game "normally". UAX dedicated run was a throwback to using the cheat codes back in the day and well...let's just say his short comings become very apparent in the later half of the game...moving on.

My thoughts on the game? A good way to preserve the original Gacha version but with a lot of cut content. Anything related to PvP and player interactions are gone. No co-op stages for solo play and, the biggest disappointment for many, no collaboration characters (i.e. Street Fighter, Monster Hunter, and DmC5). Because of this, a lot of changes were made to replace Player interactions like teleporting, Player Killer/Sentinel skills, etc. Some got buffs like cool down and damage, others got....hp based skills? Some can deal debuffs now. It's hit and miss for many...and some cards became straight up useless. Not cool man.

The Permanent Event Stages (Material stages) have increased rewards and output and makes grinding far less tedious for what it used to be. Speaking of grinding, it's a lot less stressful due to buffed rewards. You can replay any and all stages, and as usual, the first clear yields the most exp from story. Skip DiVE no long exists, so it may be tedious to run the same stage 10 times just to get ahead of the curve.

Side note: All seasonal and limited events are available, except the Collaboration ones. These unlock at level 60, and require a bit of power (~300K), are a fun side quest to do if you want to take a break from the story. Event Coupons from the seasonal/limited Event stages will let you grab some unique characters with a design motif and skill set, as well as cards.

Hard Mode for story is removed in favor of Challenge Mode, with extra health pickups along the way to make up for the difficulty spike, and it's a nice change of pace. Speaking of the story, Droitclair's, Angepitoyeir, and Eratoeir's debut events are now 1 part chapters added to the original 20 chapters. These never got a rerun event, and clearing the normal mode chapters for them gives you 40 memory to unlock them. You also gain access to S-Class Hunter X and Zero at 1-6 and 3-6 respectively. Very good Hunter Programs and are more than enough for the entire game. Some hidden elements have a mix of Normal and Hard Mode placements from the original version, so it was a nice mixup choice to keep the game fresh.

The shop was an interesting choice. You basically have 3 currency to spend: Zenny, Element Medals (Metals? Idk), and Event Coupons, which are universal across all events. You can buy pretty much...anything you want provided you meet the chapter clear requirements and have the resources to do so. Other than the lack of Collab characters and cards, it's pretty much all there. Zenny will be your main source of upgrades for...pretty much everything.

Coming from the original Online Version, I...actually didn't find it boring to grind for my stuff again. While there are some efforts made to transfer saves, I feel you can quickly rebuild your account faster than trying to work your way through various methods and experimental ideas to try and get your old save from online into offline. I won't say anything else about since it takes away from the game, plus it feels better to work for it and have some sense of accomplishment. To each their own I suppose. With the way the game is structured, challenge runs will probably start popping up everywhere now, and I look forward to making my own runs with self-imposed limitations.

Is it worth 30 dollars? Kinda? Maybe 25 or even 20 if you wait for a sale. It's worth every penny in my opinion....even if I did drop 4 figures into the online version hahaha...I love X DiVE and being able to experienced the game again with a fresh coat of paint and reworked systems is great. Plus we get a bonus credits scene which I will not spoil, with a baller vocal song. Still has some flaws and the unfortunate lack of co-op content, let alone PvP (but let's not go there) can leave a bit to be desired for a complete "definitive version" of X DiVE. 7/10, would buy again but not sure if Cross saves are available for mobile and steam but the likelihood is very slim. I'm still on the fence on buying the Mobile port of X DiVE Offline...

I hope to see everyone soon and I can't wait to stream this game again.\t\t\t\t\t
