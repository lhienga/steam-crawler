\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, thank you Capcom for doing us all a win and preserving this game. In a year in which sadly lost a game called School of Dragons around the same time it made me smile to know that one game would at least be saved (even if the global servers are still online for this game at the time of posting, the OG JP and TW server have been shut down.)

Moving on, would I recommend this game? Yes. X DiVE isn't a MM12, MMX9, or even a MML3 but its still good ol megaman and while yes this is a (former) mobile game with its own mechanics its still a really and solid fun title. Of course as with everything it still has its flaws, namely some missing content from the Online version and some Engrish here and there but as there is a will there is a way and this game has a small modding community you can check out.

Overall I would say that if you are a megaman fan and are looking for something new to play and dont want to play the online version then this is a great little title to add to your collection.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMega Man X Dive Offline adds a slew of RPG elements to the traditional Mega Man formula. In the traditional games you and your enemies operated at a fixed power level, but in Dive your power level increases by completing story missions and grinding materials for your gear. This becomes necessary as your enemies become stronger with each new stage or advanced difficulty mode. There are hundreds of different currencies to keep track of, so if you like that sort of thing then this game is for you.

The platforming gameplay is more or less the same. There's a double jump now, which looks really funny when X does it because I've become accustomed to having it on Zero exclusively in the traditional games. Combat is completely different because you can lock on to enemies and shoot in any direction, like Axl does in Mega Man X7. No longer are our heroes limited to the horizontal planes!

Instead of boss weapons you can equip Chips that add their power to your own, such as Shotgun Ice firing randomly while you use your normal Buster.

Give this game a try if the idea of grinding levels appeals to you.

The only thing I really dislike about the game so far is the "In Battle" segments in each regular stage where you cannot progress until you destroy a few waves of enemies. Those segments totally kill the forward momentum and pacing that I am accustomed with in traditional stages.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game can be summed up as: Megaman X but with other characters. More accurately, you are playing through various MM game levels (mostly from Megaman X, but there are levels from other MM games as well), but with the ability to play as other MM characters from the series. You start with Megaman X and can earn characters to play as such as Zero, the original Megaman, and even some characters that appeared as enemies.

A thing to note is that this was originally a mobile game, and as such there are various things in the game that point to that. The main 2 things are that there is an extremely large amount of things to buy and upgrade (which means grinding is still a thing, especially if you want to max out everything), and that the levels are overall very short. In fact, each level is actually an original MM level split into 6 smaller levels; if you've played the stage in the original game you'll notice this very quickly.

In terms of gameplay, you play as your chosen character using Megaman X controls, but also with the character's unique attacks and passive skills, as well as the weapons you equip. Every weapon and character has their own set of skills that can be unlocked, and you can equip any character with any weapon, allowing for all sorts of combinations. The enemies consists of those that appeared in the original stage as well as some that didn't. Every 3rd level contains a mini-boss to fight, and every 6th level a Boss; these Bosses aren't always "in the correct game". The Bosses themselves are usually accurate to the game they appeared in, although sometimes they use attacks that they normally didn't have in their original appearance.

The main issue about this game is that you have a stat called "Power" that determines your overall strength. Each stage has a recommended Power rating that rises a bit after each level, and rises sharply after moving on to the next stage after clearing all 6 levels. If your Power isn't high enough, you'll have a really tough time unless you improve your Power by upgrading your gear, which might require some grinding.

Overall this is a very nice Megaman X-like game with lots of references to other Megaman games (characters, enemies, stages, music, and more), and even gaming in general. Its takes a bit of time to progress through the story, but there are so many things to unlock and other modes to play, its certainly worth its price.\t\t\t\t\t
