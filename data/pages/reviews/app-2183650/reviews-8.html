\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMega Man X DiVE Offline. It's a mobile gacha game that has been rebalanced and reworked to remove the gacha mechanics and to make it playable as a premium title. The game has some issues such as it still being obviously designed for mobile screens and mobile playing.

I'm having to swap between mouse and controller quite frequently to play it comfortably. The game has some localization issues, especially in the Events no doubt due to not giving the Loc team enough time to fix all the text issues. That being said, the game is fun. It's not a full Mega Man X game, since the stages can usually be completed in a minute or so, but I've had a blast playing the game. I'm enjoying unlocking a bunch of different weapons and different characters. It's very similar to how older Mega Man titles would unlock the new armor variants to give Mega Man new powers and new abilities to work and experiment with.

The character designs are very nostalgic as someone who's played a lot of older Mega Man titles and there are the typical gacha designs with bridal, swimsuit, and festive costumes but I'll admit that it's something that I really wish would happen with other Gacha titles that are set to hit their EOS. Though, I will also admit that it's very unlikely as companies won't want to put in extra time and money into making an offline version of their gachas just to sell it at a one time price.

So I've discussed small things, but essentially the game is enjoyable. It's very Mega Man-esque and despite the stages being short, the stages are still very enjoyable with many story stages to get through as well as having access to a lot of the Limited Timed Events that ran in Mega Man X DiVE in order to let you unlock the characters and play as a variety of characters such as Volnutt, Mega Man, Mega Man.exe, Protoman, Roll, Tron, Martial Arts Expert Dr. Light, Servbot, and even the new characters introduced in this game RiCO, ViA, and iCO.

The weapon system is very robust and with a variety of weapons to choose from, you do get to feel a bit of making your own Mega Man character. Each character has their own abilities which each have a different way of using them providing a bit more flexibility in your character and the game is very generous with its' in-game currency that I was able to stay on top of the story beats with very minimal grinding. The only time I felt there was a curve in difficulty was when I hit level 60 and unlocked events. I was at Power Level 150K and was excited to play the events until I went to play one and the events had a recommended Power Level of 300K which made me have to grind a bit and I wasn't able to play my first Event until about Level 80 and wasn't able to beat it until about Level 90 when I had unlocked even better armors to use which boosted my power to a considerable degree. I haven't finished the story, but I can't say it's all that interesting from what I've played. I would bet that the big twist that seems to be obvious will come into play but I'm more here for the gameplay over the story since it's an offline gachas and I don't expect Gachas to have a good story since it's just a vehicle to get people in to spend money on characters and weapons which isn't the case here in the offline version.

All in all, the game is quite enjoyable if you're a Mega Man fan and if you can buy it on a sale. I got it for about 14 USD thanks to already owning the other Mega Man Collections and completing the bundle that it had provided for me. Even at 30 USD, I would say it's worth the price of admission as I spent 60 USD on DMC V and got about the same amount of playtime and I'm still not done with MMXDO and I'm still having a blast playing the game and progressing the story to unlock more characters.

So if you're a Mega Man fan and you want to pick it up on sale, MMXDO is a great game to purchase. Just be aware that the story isn't too great and the UI/menu design is a bit wonky and the localization is kind of all over the place. The main reason you're picking this up is the same reason I picked it up and it's to play a Mega Man game with a bunch of characters I've grown to love over my time with the series with even more characters I didn't know existed until now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMEGA MAN X DiVE was a mobile game and MEGA MAN X DiVE Offline is a port of that mobile game without GACHA mechanics that can be played offline as a single player experience.

A lot of the initial reviews for this game were positive, because most mobile GACHA games that have their servers turned off are just lost forever and can no longer be played. By modifying the game so it can be played offline without the GACHA mechanics, Capcom gave us a way we can still officially play the game. Do I like that Capcom modified this game to be playable offline? Yes. I never played the game while the online servers were live, so this was my first experience with the game and I otherwise would not have been able to experience it. However, Is the game good? No.

First off, the menus are painful. They retain the touch controls from the original mobile version of the game. There is no "back" button. There is no "move selection up/down/left/right" button. You have to move the cursor around the screen with the joystick and press any face button on your controller to click/touch at the current cursor location. If you didn't have to menu much, then this wouldn't be a problem but you will spend a lot of time in the menus of this game. Missions are all extremely short and you will be kicked back to the menus after every mission. There doesn't appear to be a game mode to just play all the levels in order without returning to the menu. So the horrible menu controls really ruin the experience.

The game is horribly balanced. One level you may only lose 5% of your HP from enemy attacks and the next you might lose 50% of your HP per enemy attack. The balancing is simply all over the place. You might kill the boss before it can even attack. Granted, I have not reached the end-game content (if there is an end-game), but at least playing through the mission in order is an inconsistent mess.

There are tons of enemies, bosses, and characters from the other Megaman games, but don't expect the same experience that you have with those other games. The experience here is worse in every way. You don't have to aim your shots in MEGA MAN X DiVE Offline. The aiming is all automatic and shot in a linear direction at whatever enemy the game currently decides to target, which is usually the closest enemy to your character, but the targeting can be inconsistent at times too. Because you don't aim your shots, none of the returning enemies and bosses will ever feel the same as they did in their original games and appearances. They could have leaned into the patterns and really made you focus on dodging all the patterns, but they didn't do that either. Nothing here feels like Megaman. **EDIT**: I found out that there are additional options in the mid-level pause menu that allow you to disable auto aim and enable "precision jumping", and while I'm glad those options exist, the game clearly wasn't designed with those options in mind. Enemies often enter the screen from the top so that the only way to hit them would be to shoot upwards (rather than horizontal). There is the ability to manually aim with the right joystick, but it controls terribly.

The graphics suck. The game uses 3D models instead of sprites, which I would have preferred. There is nothing wrong with mixing sprites and 3D graphics; just look at Star Ocean The Second Story R or how Streets of Rage 4 lets you play with the new versions of the characters but also the old pixel graphics versions of the characters too. Mixing art styles in mash-up games like this can be fun. Instead we got pretty low-detail 3D models and very bland and basic stages and backgrounds; this was originally a mobile game after all, and they made no attempt to update the graphics from the mobile version.

There are a lot of options to try out with all the different characters and weapons, but simply having a lot of options doesn't make a good game. We need content designed around the various options to challenge us. Again, I haven't made it to the end-game yet (if there is an end game), but even if there is an end-game and it is more properly balanced, I shouldn't have to play dozens of hours to get to the good content. The standard missions you play through are just a terrible experience.

I've also been running into issues with my inputs being eaten. I don't know if there is an issue with my game settings or what, but the inputs just seems to mess up a lot in this game. I play a lot of games and don't run into this issue in other games, so at least at the moment I have to assume it is something wrong with the input buffering of this game.

Also, while there is cloud synch for this title, it requires you to manually trigger the synch from the title menu. This is a big problem for this game because it is supposed to be a quick pick-up-and-play-for-a-few-minutes-at-a-time game, but having to remember to go back to the title menu in order to cloud save your progress makes it really annoying. This is especially problematic for myself as I like to go back and forth between my Steam Deck and my desktop PC (PC during the day and Steam Deck at night before bed). Why can't it just automatically cloud synch like literally every single other game? This is just stupid.

This game is only for people who played MEGA MAN X DiVE while the servers were online. I can't even recommend this game to fans of Megaman. Literally every other Megaman game is better than this one. Even then, here are some more games on Steam I would recommend over MEGA MAN X DiVE:
- Gunvolt franchise
- Savage Halloween
- Contra franchise
- Metal Slug Franchise
- Castlevania franchise
- Steel Assault
- Gravity Circuit
- Blazing Chrome
- Huntdown
- Super Cyborg
- 20XX & 30XX
... and many other games too\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll definitely say that this is a "buy it on sale" game. When compared to the mainline games, gameplay is behind: missions are a couple of minutes long, auto aim is on by default, and a lot of the gameplay revolves around stats, about on equal par with skill. For newcomers, that normally isn't the case.

What I WILL say, though, is that if you get this game on sale, it is definitely worth it if you're used to the gacha package. It has the typical dopamine hits from upgrades and rainbows, and being able to play as any character from a massive roster spanning Megaman X and a bit beyond definitely is a selling point. What's even more, this game is one of few gacha games to even HAVE an offline version with altered currencies for the sake of a singleplayer game. For the sake of game preservation, that's a glowing mark.

In short; not perfect, especially for the regular Mega Man crowd, but has something to offer in terms of variety if you give it a chance.

I will repeat, though; BUY ON SALE.\t\t\t\t\t

Use a controller but not really

The short version:

The good:
- Plays just like the original
- Balanced for offline play
- Works on Steam Deck

The bad:
- Menus not optimized for controller play
- Cloud saves are manual

The long version:
I played Mega Man X DiVE when the app was online. It was pretty fun, I got a bit stuck and the game ended up falling to the wayside after a while. I decided to pick up Offline because I wanted to play again.

Right off the bat, the good part is that it's very similar to the Online version. I mapped my 8BitDo SN30 to the Online version because it served the 2D SNES style pretty well. In Offline, I was able to map the same 8BitDo SN30 and the controls felt just as good as they did previously. The controls are sharp, responsive, and easy to remap.

Additionally, there have been balances put in place to offset the lack of gacha elements and microtransactions. It works pretty well and even though some of the balances make the game -really- easy, they work.

Lastly, and this one is really important, the game works on Steam Deck. Hooray!

Now for the bad. The game is a little TOO similar to the Online version, specifically the UI elements. The original game was never optimized to sue a controller on any menu. Which is fine when you are playing on a touch screen device between 5-7 inches. However, on a full size screen, usually without touch capabilities, this becomes problematic. The touch functionality is replaced by an onscreen cursor, which is not nearly as accurate. The existing solution is to use a mouse, or just put up with the cursor scrolling. This is my biggest complaint which would require work to resolve but would fix 90% of what's wrong with the game.

Cloud saves are completely confusing. On the main menu in the game, you have to click the Cloud Up icon to upload the saves to the cloud. On any other device, you have to click the Computer Down icon to bring those saves to the device. The process must be repeated any time you change devices. The process should be like EVERY OTHER STEAM GAME THAT SUPPORTS CLOUD SAVES and require none of that.

Overall, I do recommend if you are looking for that classic Mega Man X experience, which is the same reason I bought the game. At the very least, get it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor those looking for a quick review, this game is a solid 8/10 experience.

Those two points are deducted simply because the English translation of the script was never once read by an english speaking human being. There is no way someone who speaks english could look at some of the typos and awkwardly translated jokes or lines and let those fly.

But when it comes to the story, gameplay, amount of content and the overall feel and vibe of the game, this has nestled itself up in my top 3 quite handily.

Easily 100s+ hours of gameplay to be had, over 800 stages, 120 characters with unique skills and styles, 2 which are just stronger versions of the same character (Megaman X and S-rank Maverick Hunter X are the same, S-rank just has an aura and slightly better stats. Zero and S-rank Zero is the same way.) and tons of weapons that all have their own unique skill trees of their own, this game does NOT lack for content.

The story changed how I see the past games, the gameplay itself is HELLA smooth and feels really good once you get into the swing of it, and the developers clearly had a lot of fun making what is essentially a toybox to goof around in and smash cool figurines in.

And if you're a fan of Megaman X in any capacity, I highly suggest you pick this game up whilst it's on sale. $30 might be a mite much to ask, but $20 feels like a fair package deal, especially since it comes with Legacy collection 1 and X collection 1 for cheaper than the game itself.

My main heartache comes from the fact that.....capcom just doesn't seem to care. This is a port of a mobile MMO that admittedly was given an offline release, which is FANTASTIC and should be applauded that what is essentially a "Backup" version of the game is available for those who don't have steady access to the internet, or for when the MMO itself eventually completely dissipates from the internet.

However....I can imagine a world where this game was given just that little bit of extra polish, because it's so close to being the perfect sandbox-style megaman action RPG.

A little translation work here and there, move it to a real engine and make some truly high-res models, sell it with Peer-to-peer co-op for the $30 they're asking for it now and I'd have bought multiple copies to spread around to friends. It's a buffet of megaman X's history, when it could have been a much nicer, much more targeted and designed seven course meal. Sure, you can stuff your face with all of the cool stuff, and everything tastes pretty good....but you can tell you're not exactly eating Wagyu.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is NOT a Megaman X game.

Megaman X has momentum and velocity. Speed and fluid motion are core components to traversing a level, and movement options expand across the series in new and (usually) creative ways. Dashing is a core component, and finding new ways to use it leads to hidden secrets; rather than feeling like it's only there because another game did it. Momentum and acceleration carry over when you jump, and attacking while moving is clean and fluid. This game has none of that.

Megaman X has natural exploration. The levels are designed in such a way that you can figure out what is happening and what to do without tutorial nonsense stopping gameplay dead to explain it. Secrets aren't obvious, but have onscreen draws and clues. It rewards returning to levels with new powers and abilities to see what hidden interactions might lead to treasure. This game decided to remove all of that. It has gear instead.

Megaman X has strong characterization and story without excessive narration. It establishes everything you need to know instantly, without explanation. It uses dialogue to add flavor rather than lore dump. You see and feel who this character is, and follow along on his journey of growth and self-discovery. It shows you your current limits, gives you new goals to reach, then sets you out to attain them. This game divorces itself from story instantly by telling me I'm playing a video game (no sh*t, game!) and that I'm just rummaging around in a toy box. It introduces new characters who actively ape existing ones, tell me they're doing that (again, no sh*t, game!), and then tell me how I, the player, am feeling about it. Megaman X respects my time and intelligence; this game actively insults it.

This is NOT a Megaman X game. This is a half-baked, lazy money-milking simulator with Megaman X in it. Silver lining: at least they ripped the predatory monetization back out (too they didn't put the quality they removed for it back in). Go play 20XX or Protodroid Delta instead. Or the Megaman X Collection, so you can see for yourselves the violent vandalism this game inflicts upon of the greatest games of all time.

If this is what Capcom is going to do with Megaman X games going forward, maybe it's better the franchise stays dead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSad to say but I'm a bigger fan of the aesthetic of Megaman than the games themselves, which is why this game is perfect for me. I'm not exactly the best at platformers and sure I could beat the originals by save stating near every spike but, I rather get filtered than be brought down to that level.

If you're like me then this is the game for you. This menu simulator lets you play through babified Megaman X stages as your favorite version of the dozen or so characters from the series stretched out into 100+. There is a plot and so far I'm half paying attention. Hopefully it improves over time.

The gameplay is kind of a Trojan horse of Megaman. Less jump and shoot man, and more jump and shoot and use cooldowns man. When you are finished with that after a few stages you enter the menu simulator. I am still discovering new menus as I progress through the game. All of these menus are in service of making different numbers go up. If you love getting your dopamine fix from big numbers going up then this is the game for you.

There are; characters, character stars, character skills, weapon ranks, weapons stats, weapons skills, weapon bonus levels, armor, armor levels, weapon addon powers, leveling and ranking up said powers, enemy cards for buffs and I still think I missed a few. There are a good 2 dozen menus for you to go through when its time for a "number get bigger" session. If that doesn't sound like something you would enjoy then don't bother with this game as it takes up the majority of your time.

One of the most hilarious parts of the game has to be the first level which takes place on a remake of the X1 opening highway stage. Its as if the devs watched Egoraptor's video from way back on how well designed the stage was in the original and... proceeded to specifically add tutorials at the exact spots that they weren't needed in the SNES game.

Game lets me play as Iris using a minigun so I'd say its pretty good overall.\t\t\t\t\t
