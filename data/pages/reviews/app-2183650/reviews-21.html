Does it suck that I have to pay another $30 to access this as someone who dumped money into the online version of the game? Yes. But now everything that would have been a microtransaction or part of the hellish gatcha is now an unlockable--effectively free.

I sincerely hope that this game sets a precedent that many others follow, this needs to be the standard for the industry as opposed to the standard practice. Because the standard practice is "You lose everything when the servers shut down, go suck a fat one if you gave us money." With X-Dive Offline, Capcom basically wrote a love letter to the people that supported their game, and I couldn't be happier with it.

Edit: Apparently there was a form for you to get a full refund when the servers were going down. That's just good business, most companies would take the money and run like a pump and dump. Another W for Capcom.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't let the rating deceive you. I've got a LOT of gripes

for added Context, I've played very few Megaman games including the Online version of this which closed down. this doesn't mean I dislike this game, but there's problems I feel that I am equipped to judge the game for what it's worth. also keep in mind this used to be a mobile game which was also available for PC.

Pros:
-The gameplay is fun and snappy, being pretty dang close to the Megaman X games (that I have played) The music is pleasant to listen to and the overall vibe is interesting.

-The Characters. Tons of picks from the series and spinoffs. from the classic games to Legends, from Star Force to Battle Network, it has a large roster. a personal favorite of mine is Servbot, from Megaman Legends. they may be functionally the same, but they do have some special abilities to each of them

-a simple story, from my experiences. in a cyberspace where Megaman players' memories gather, you must remove the bugs in the system along side RiCO, an "Admin" in said cyberspace. think of the playable characters as a remote control car or drone, which you use to fix the mess that is the code.

Cons:
-The length... of stages I mean. for 100+ stages, they're really short. Sure quick bursts of Gameplay are fun, and I DO enjoy them, I really do, they feel like they give so much for so little. (which is understandable, since Online had a energy meter which limited stage playing)

-The missing Gatcha. now look, random getting things can be annoying when it comes to getting particular items, but I'd debate grinding for the same might be worse. You can easily grind "Zenny" to buy character/weapon/chip memories (the things used to obtain and rank up characters) but there's something so exciting about getting something completely random and unexpected, if done right. something like the Yokai Watch Crank-a-Kai, for lack of better comparison.

-The... Characters? yes, the Characters. despite the roster being rather big, there are quite a lot of repeats. not including different series' Megamen, of course. Things like Thunder Beam Megaman, Rising Fire X, S rank versions of normal characters, Bass with and without Treble being separate characters, tons of characters original to Dive (excluding story important ones) as well as various other characters that should of have realistically been skins. was that painful to read? I agree. this is a problem originating form the online version by the way.

-The crossover characters. not a real problem for me, but a bunch of crossover events with properties Capcom owned were mysteriously taken out. maybe to be later released as DLC? who knows.

In conclusion, would I recommend this? I enjoyed it, but this might be your thing if you're either Megaman crazed or if you enjoy tight platformer/shooters, but 20XX is a good pick for that. It's not a complete waste of your money, but it could be spent elsewhere.

On sale or bail for this one!
