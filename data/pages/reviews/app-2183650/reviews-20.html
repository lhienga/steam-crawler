\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTerrible port… And before anyone says; if its such a terrible port, then why do you have so many hours? Sit down and let me explain, the only reason why I did so, is so that people cannot argue that I was biased, that I didn't have the full experience or some other BS on why my opinion matters less than theirs, all because I am giving this game a bad review after completing it, like all people do when they try to be a "white knight".

Primarily I must declare myself a huge Megaman fan, and when I say that I really mean it, to the point where I have collected consoles just because, of some of the Megaman games that are exclusive to them, and I even have done cosplay before. I also dabble with romhacking and modding, all because unfortunately some of the Megaman games, have not been coded or developed as intended, mostly because they had to meet deadlines, so I really appreciate the communities, that take their time to fix up some of the games, that could unfortunately not have been better because of such limitations, and it has been a joy to play better versions of X5 or X6 or my least played X game which was X3, but with the Zero Project mod that is my most played SNES game now.

Now for my history with X Dive, I played the original in the Asian server, through the use of both Bluestacks and Nox Player, and had a pretty terrible time, mostly due to the insane ping I had because of my location, and on top of that having to emulate Android as well, so I was psyched when Global came out, as I could play it natively in my country, on my Samsung Galaxy Note20 Ultra 5G, and for the most part I had been happy with it, I wasn’t an over-spender as I only got the monthly packs, and I also wasn’t desperate for the META plays, I played with what I got and developed strategies to counter the META, and that’s how I got over average at playing. My best units in Global had to be all the crossover characters, my most used being between Chun Li or Ryu, and even the Monster Hunter sword had been my most used weapon. My favorite part of the game, was the PVP even though it got messy and unfair, as the META always topped itself after each and every season, and by the point SuperBass and the Deicide's Will came out the PVP became unplayable, as you had to either had the same OP loadout, or get extremely lucky by doing cheese strategies with other OP units, but overall it became a toxic experience. I also liked being able to compete with my friends, whether it was the race modes, or to see who could deal the most damage in CoOp, and running a small guild was cool too. I had played the game religiously since Global had arrived, and I only quit shortly after the Asian servers announced the end of service, all because I’m a realist and I know that there is no way, for Nebulajoy to somehow keep the game alive past the Asian server content, and the fact that they are currently trying to milk all they can over the sinking ship they currently have, is a disgusting sight indeed specially how they keep making promises they can’t deliver on, but a lot of people are still recklessly spending.

With all that said, time to get on with the proper Megaman X Dive Offline review. The first thing I have to say is that I hate the menu, the online version also didn't have a user friendly menu, but I feel that it was categorized sort of well, and I liked that with most new updates you would be greeted to a different lobby type, and the screenshots of the games in the background would change as well, but with Offline there is no way to customize the lobby, and it doesn't matter if you have finished the story or "events" or other challenges, the lobby will always stay the same using the same music, and its a super lazy feeling specially when the game freaking has an "Audio Player", which if you go to it and select any music tracks if you go back in the menu, it will revert to the default lobby music instead of playing what you were listening to in the Audio Player, and I just wish that the Audio Player was similar to the one in Smash Bros Ultimate, where you could make a playlist and choose what tracks to play in the menus. Continuing from the menus, I hate that you cannot navigate them using a gamepad, and I understand that they made the Offline version mostly designed for iOS or Android which are usually touch devices, but they should have known that most people use gamepads to play platfomer games, and speaking of which I hate that you no longer have as many options as you once did, in order to setup the functionality for the buttons on your gamepad, as I liked having multiple dash buttons in the online version but now I can only configure one, and some buttons such as Select or Start or L3 or R3 cannot be used for mapping. I do not like having such little options in the settings either, as you can only select from running the game in Low, Medium or High instead of being able to control the specific fidelity you would like to experience for your game, and there's no excuse of this being designed this way because of this also existing on Mobile, because games such as Tower of Fantasy or Genshin Impact, gives you plenty of options so that you can configure your game to run best on what specs you have. I think that whoever did the localization for this game needs to be fired, because some information boxes are vague in their descriptions, there are still typos and grammar problems in the story that still stuck around from when it was a mobile online game, and the same goes for some of the cards you can actually acquire. Its hilarious how some of the bugs from when the game was live, have somehow still stuck around and not been fixed, such as levels breaking and not progressing among a few other things, and the most infuriating problem I had encountered with the game was with the saving, where for the most part the auto save did its job, but a couple of times when I was manual saving the game actually crashed on me, and I lost precious progress as I had upgraded characters and weapons, and I had to do everything again. I did not like that you can acquire OP characters fairly early for just clearing the first few worlds 3 stars, as once you are given S Class X the game is pretty much on EZ mode, even if you do not upgrade him, and once you get S Class Zero the game becomes a breeze, and once you get to World 10 and beat it and then upgrade Zero to 5 stars, that character is so OP that I never had to grind for EXP, as I could take stages even if they were 300-400k combat power above me, and only the ultimate attacks of 23-6 boss could actually break my shield and damage me. I do not like how characters or weapons are separated into, ones that can be acquired with just Zenny, and ones that can only be acquired with EMs, especially because even after 20+ hours in, I have not located a way to earn EMs infinitely like you can with the Zenny Event Stage, where once you are max level for it you can earn 1m Zenny every run, but for EMs you can only get them for first clear rewards, and I think they are a fixed amount which is bogus, because there are actually some EM acquirable weapons that have hidden skills, that you need to reroll over and over again in order to get the skill that you most desire, I will continue playing the game and see if I can unlock everything, but I strongly believe that in the future some people from the community will end up having to create a save editor or something, in order to add more EMs to be able to unlock everything in 1 save, as I really have not seen a challenge or event that gives you EMs for every attempt. I also do not like that this version of the game has no Raids, or Boss Rush event stages despite them having had implemented them through the Event Settings menu, and I despise that all the crossover stuff is not available in the game. Because of this and more I honestly cannot recommend this game to be bought at full price I can only say just wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
