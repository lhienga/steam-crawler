\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was never a fan of mobile games, but since this version came out, I gave it a try. And I like it.

This game feels familiar and unfamiliar at the same time. The familiar part is the "jump, dash, wall-jump" move set, while the unfamiliar part is the way you handle your weapons (which you can obviously feel is designed for a touch screen). It takes some time to get used to the game mechanics, but once you get used to it, the game is pretty fun.

There are quite a lot of content to play with: 23 areas, each with a boss fight at the end, and on top of that, tons of events. You can also skin X and Zero with a large array of characters from all over the franchise, and for each character, you can use two of his/her signature moves. (Em... including a lot of characters who are not supposed to be fighters. But yeah, you can beat Sigma with Ciel if you like.)

Not very difficult if you keep upgrading your characters, weapons, etc throughout your play. If you want some challenge, just don't upgrade things too much.\t\t\t\t\t

Oh :| Well maybe I'll just try a few more levels... there's gotta' be SOMETHING to this ga--

Oh :(

If you're looking for your Mega Man X fix, just fire up the original trilogy. Getting bored? Try one of the randomizers online. Mega Man X Dive has nothing to offer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo... MEGA MAN X DiVE Offline.

Is it a good Mega Man/Rockman game? Not really, though it's similar enough to the late X series games, I guess (that's not really a compliment). The control and movement doesn't feel particularly tight, though it doesn't feel bad either. Otherwise, the game has auto-aim (which X7 also had, which again, not a compliment to really compare it to, but it's there), which you can turn off if you want, but the game was pretty clearly designed with it in mind.

Is X DiVE still fun? Yeah. The large number of characters with different skills and weapons with different properties are fun to play around with. The silly mobile-game style story is fun too.

So what is this game exactly?

This is an offline conversion of the previously mobile/online/gacha game MEGA MAN X DiVE. No PVP. No co-op. No play limits. No crossover events. No gacha (you just buy everything directly with in-game currency). Less grindy, but still kind of grindy.

It's got 23 sets of stages, 19 of which are 5 mini stages, followed by a boss (usually with a mid-boss on stage 3). 3 of which are short single stages with a boss at the end, and one that's designed like a classic Mega Man boss rush, but broken up into 6 stages, so it's not actually a boss rush.

Otherwise there's a bunch of events you can manually activate to play at any time, once you're level 60 (or just uh... wait for the right time of year to play, if you don't want to manually activate them, for some reason - still have to be level 60), most of which are actually complete stages (still not very long) with a boss at the end.

There's also the "Tower" stages, which appear to just be progressively more challenging small arena fights, but I haven't really dug into that area of the game, so I can't really comment on it.

Characters range from X and Zero (obviously), to original characters, to Iris, and even to a servbot, and Dr. Light clearly dressed as Ryu from Street Fighter, using some of Ryu's signature attacks. Being based on a mobile game with different rarities (and I'm assuming time-limited gacha), the characters in this game are not at all balanced. Some of them will give you a relatively classic Mega Man challenge (past the extremely easy early sets of stages - though spikes and pits aren't insta-death here), while others will allow you to actually just mash buttons and win through the entire game.

Speaking of classic Mega Man, as I mentioned previously in this review, this game features auto-aim (and manual aim) options, which you can turn off so you only shoot horizontally in the direction you're facing. While you can turn aiming off, I don't recommend it, because some enemy patterns and bosses are pretty clearly designed knowing you have it. You can probably still manage in most cases, but I'm not actually entirely sure how you would shoot the face of some large bosses without at least manual aim (which I think only works with a controller?).

Maoh the Giant, for example, is much larger than his original X3 counterpart. Not entirely sure his head is in double jump distance (it might be), but it's clearly not in single jump distance (like it was in X3). While you might be able to manage him with just horizontal shots, it would probably be pretty awkward.

Anyway, I've got nearly 30 hours in game already. I'm having a good time. While it might not be the Mega Man/Rockman game you were waiting for, it's still a fun game

Of note: I've had two crashes in my roughly 30 hours. One of which was right before the end credits. It still saved my completion data, but I actually loaded a previous manual save and redid the last boss anyway (and didn't crash that time).

Also, this game features a censored version of Swimsuit Roll, which has a more cutesy swimsuit with a skirt, rather than a school swimsuit, if that bothers you. As far as I understand, this was originally done on mobile due to some rules change or some such on one of the app stores (haven't really looked into it, just what I read in one post on the forums, so take that with a grain of salt), but whatever the reason, we have the new version of Swimsuit Roll rather than the original.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI came into this game wanting to hate it, but I gotta admit, it does what it does well. Capcom made the right choice in deciding to save all the work the dev team put into this game and remove all the microtransactions. So, is it worth $30 instead of F2P? That depends.

1. Do you want to play a LOT of short Mega Man levels and bosses?
2. Do you want to play as a bunch of characters who were never playable in mainline games?
3. Do you really, and I mean REALLY like Mega Man X? Enough to spend hours playing it and never get bored?

If you said yes to all 3 of these, then sure. It is exactly what you expect. A lot of mobile game content that plays like a MMX game.
If you said no to any of them, then probably not. There are much better games than this in terms of structure, fun factor, and playability. I can't bring myself to hate it, and I'm glad that it was preserved for people to enjoy in some form, even with all the crossover and PVP stuff removed from the game.

6/10, if you like Mega Man, you'll probably like this, but no guarantee. Honestly, just go play 30XX or Gravity Circuit. They're both cheaper and way better games.\t\t\t\t\t
