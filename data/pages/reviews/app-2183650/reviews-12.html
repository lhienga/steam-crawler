\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMore gacha games need to do this! Mega Man X DiVE Offline is a complete rebalancing of Mega Man X DiVE, with zero microtransactions or gacha. The game has been completely streamlined from top to bottom, to the point where I never had to grind throughout the main story once as long as I steadily upgraded my character as the game expected me to. Honestly, it feels like a completely different game when you aren't fighting against enemies that can kill you in two hits but it takes forever for you to kill them. I will admit, I do think 30 dollars is a little bit of a steep price for this game, but it also is worth noting X DiVE Offline has more content than any other Mega Man game out there, including the Battle Network & Star Force games (for reference, the previous longest Mega Man game for beginning to story end is Battle Network 5, around 25 hours to completion of the story, whereas this took me around 35 hours). While it is unfortunate that we lost the crossover characters and co-op, I still think the game is vastly better than the online original. The one thing that I still detest about X DiVE is Jakob's Elevator, and unfortunately if you're an achievement hunter, you have to get all the way to the top of the Normal Mode of Jakob which really sucks. Still, if you love Mega Man and are itching for more of the series, X DiVE Offline is a blast, it gets my recommendation for sure.\t\t\t\t\t

Sure, it isn't perfect to control it with a keyboard, but with a controller or better, on a Steam Deck, it just feels... so.. RIGHT for some reason!

Overall, if you're a huge Mega Man fan or just want a 2.5D platforming/battle game with a HUGE roster of different weapons and characters you can mix and match, X DiVE Offline is for you!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a Megaman fan myself, I highly recommend getting this game if with to get your Megaman fix
in an all original story with you as the "Player"; the story's main protagonist who gets isekai'd into
the cyberspace of Megaman games...The Deep Log. Together with the Deep Log's admin, RiCO,
it is your mission to fix all data & preserved, cherished memories of your years that you played
all those Megaman games...as for the enemy, you'll not only be facing foes for the X series, but
also other bosses from other Megaman games.

To fight them, you'll be armed with unlockable Hunter Programs and Weapons too. Farm through
the game to accumulate resources and assets to grind your characters & weapons alike, not to mention
make armor to increase your stats to become more powerful.

The overall base game itself is good, very good...we get all the events, characters, weapons & cards
from the mobile game...the only downside is the Capcom collabs content isn't available. my only hope
is that they'll be released later as DLC with any luck.

So, if you're a fan of Megaman, and have even played X DiVE on the mobile version...then become invested
in Megaman X DiVE Offline. and cherish those memories you have of when you first played it.\t\t\t\t\t
