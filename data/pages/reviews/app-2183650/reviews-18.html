\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Comprehensive Review for Your Money

TL;DR, Get this game. You will have hours of fun and will be good to put doen and come back to time and rime again. The price is not bad compared to how much items would cost players in Online mode.

What Makes it Worth It:
I am one of the original players of Mega Man X Dive. Not Rock Man X Dive. This means I played the beta and the main game, resulting in two restarts. This is the third restart I have done for X Dive and I can say it is my favorite one.

I would have loved to review this earlier but I felt it was right to wait until I had access to all game modes at level 60 to do this. I have provided a comprehensive review of the things I wanted to know before I paid for this game. The price has been a concern but after playing this game as well as still having access to Online (for now), the price is worth it. This feels like a full game and the Online just being a PvP mess with extra characters on it.

Why Buy this Game for $30:
I am not just saying this because Offline is easier, I am stating it as fact. I have almost every S Rank character in Online and have spent $3 on it; I have played almost every day and used the reward and Gacha mechanics based on time to get the characters I wanted. If it wasn't for livestreams giving free codes, I would have stopped playing Online, but I still continue to log in for free stuff. In the last two days playing this game, I get to choose what characters and weapons I want without the need of waiting. The progress is up to me.


No longer did I have to wait for a character I saw in Rock Man X DIve to be released as well as play a gacha system to get them. No longer did I have to wait to replay Event missions or progress through Story Missions. And for the best part, not being forced to play Hard Mode levels to continue playing Normal Mode levels. I feel the need to continue playing Event Stages and play characters I never played in Online. The creators put time and care into this version and it shows. This may not be Mega Man X9, but it sure feels like a Mega Man fan game.

Characters, Weapons, and Farming:
The creators knew how hard X Dive Online was to get through the story because the game forced you to gacha S Rank characters. The game would almost become impossible with A rank characters. With love and thought to the players, they provide an S Rank character at the end of chapter 1. They continue to do this trend until the end of Chapter 3. By then, you will have access to the other game modes as well as copious amount of Zenny and Elemental Metals to pick from a list of characters in the shop to be able to pick one and 5 Star Rank them. This applies to the weapons as well. Doom Blade and Biochemical Buster are there waiting for you to purchase. This raises the question of how long it takes, and honestly, not long at all once you reach level 20 to 25. When you unlock DNA at level 50, instead of having to buy more character memories, they cost Zenny.

Events:
When playing X Dive Online, players only cared about the Seasonal Events (which I will get to later). Now, with how the Offline system plays, the Event stages are worth playing.

Once you reach level 20, you now have access to the Armor Enhancement Event. This is helpful for bringing up your level by buying armor. Once you reach 25, this is when the game becomes your personal farm. You will gain access to the Zenny Event stage where every time you S Rank it, will give you $60000. Doing this 10 times (roughly 11 minutes at most) will nab you 600k.

Later on comes the Dive Trigger Enhancement providing you core skills and then EXP which helps you gain levels faster. If you are stuck in the story, come back to the events and you will come back fulfilled.

When you become level 60, the Seasonal Events unlock. These are additional ways to obtain elemental metals, cards, and event tickets. These are used for unlocking cards and seasonal character memories. It appears each time you beat them, you get additional tickets, so these can be replayed. Just be warned, when you're at level 60, this does not mean you're ready as they require a power level of at least 306,000.

Story:
Story was the most underwhelming aspect of X Dive Online due to it making you play harder version levels before advancing the story. In Onlin3, if you didn't have enough E Tanks, you would have to wait a day for more. This mechanic is gone as well as the need to complete Hard Mode stages. You can progress without the need to go back. Doing this is now an option as Challenge Mode to provide you free characters and cards.

With story, there is the Jakob Orbital Elevator and Race Mode. In the online version, your characters would lose energy if you played or your character died. Here, you can complete them and redo them without the need for waiting 24 hours. This is the best way to earn elemental metals as there are 300 levels.

With Race, it is garbage. There are miniscule rewards and are just there as additional stages. You do not even get progression rewards when you complete them.

Store:
Unlike needing real money to get easier access to characters and weapons, they are all here. Your additions like Reorginzation Programs and Chip exp points are easily purchasable. Some characters are locked down until you progress through the story, but this makes the game feel like a real game to unlock characters through progression. If you had a card you loved in Online that was not an exclusive crossover one, it is here for you. Utilize the Zenny Event stage and purchase till your hearts content. For Elemental Metals, the costs are low so it will only take you about 8500 to get a character and max their level to 5 stars.

The Cons:
With all good things, here comes the bad. Areas where music once were, is now replaced by the X4 player select theme. This song is on the main, character, weapon, chips, skills, cards, and mission select screens. This is one of the most annoying music tracks as it is only 5 seconds long and repeats. In the OG X4 game you were not supposed to be on this screen for longer than 5 seconds. I am unsure with the option for the music player why songs cannot be changed. They could have also utilized select themes from the other 8 X games. Also, there is some music glitching.

Grinding to level 60 to get access to seasonal events is a bit tedious. It's worse the game baits you thinking you can complete them, but they require your power level to be 300k. Also, collaborative events are missing which results in no special characters. If these come out as DLC, it will be ridiculous as there is already a $30 price tag. Hopefully the mods come out soon.

There are some bugs when completing stages. Sometimes it will not load causing you to exit out of the game and doing it again. Make sure you keep autosave on as well as manually saving.

Race is wortheless and not even worth anyone's time. It's a sad addition and is just there for speedrunners on a non-online game.

Replayability may be a factor once someone has beaten all levels and unlocked everything. This was not thr case for Online as there was PVP. Although elemental metals appear plentiful, once you complete everything, there won't be additional ways (that I currently know of) to earn more. So if you accidentally buy too many memories with elemental metals, you may be screwed out of maxing a character or weapon.

Conclusion:
Surprisingly, this is not a clunky mess. It feels like Offline was what the game was supposed to be but then developers wanted a paywall, so they added the gacha mechanics. If you love Mega Man, get this. If you love platformers, get this. If you don't like these but respect fans who want more Mega Man, get this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fun but a few things I don't like about it.

1.) The game definitely feels like it was a mobile game, there are so many things you need to upgrade most of which seem useless and just there because they wanted you to spend money upgrading it or spend hours of your day getting the necessary upgrade materials.
2.) Game does not have full controller support like EVERY Megaman game. Everything must be navigated with the virtual mouse, can't even press up on the D-pad to move a menu cursor.
3.) The game doesn't always accept the input. I've tried with 3 different controllers and the game isn't responsive. There have been many time where I did not double jump when I should have leading me to fall off stage (it's also not like other Megaman games where this leads to death, you simply take dmg and return to the closest platform).
4.) Stages are too short and interrupted frequently by Rico (a character in the game) you can tell this was an on the go game as most stages end in 1-2 minutes. Rico is a very annoying character that just plain stops the game to say some useless dialogue in what should just be an action plat-former.
5.) The main menu music will drive you insane. Also there was a missed opportunity as there is a jukebox in the game and should've been given the option to choose what to play during the main menu.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMega Man X DiVE is something every gacha game should look to, and by that I mean "they should look at what it did after it shut down." Making an offline version of a mobile game after EoS is absolutely what every single game should do, in my opinion. Kudos to Capcom for doing it.

Is the game itself any good, though? Kiiiiiiiinda?

I'm gonna review this from the perspective of "I am very aware this used to be a gacha game." So, going in, you really should know it used to be a mobile game and all.

The gameplay itself is pretty solid. Way better than I'd expect from a phone game, as some who's played the vast majority of Mega Man games I think it feels pretty good. It's not the BEST, but it's certainly not the worst X game they've ever made, not by a longshot.

The roster is, honestly, the biggest pull I feel. My inter child is doing backflips over being able to not only play as Bass.exe, but even as multiple versions of him. Pretty sick tbh, I'm a big fan of that. Unlocking characters is locked behind story progression, but once you're able to unlock a character, it's EXTREMELY easy to do so. The original game had collab characters that aren't in this version, which WOULD be understandable but... every single collab was with a Capcom IP. I really can't say I understand why they decided to get rid of the collab content, unless they're gonna try and be cheeky and sell it as DLC or something. Which I can't say I'd be a fan of but it'd still be cheaper than rolling the gacha AM I RIGHT LOL

I haven't completely gotten through the story yet but, if I had to make one really big criticism about this game: It's the translation. It's bad. REALLY bad. It feels like they threw the script into Google Translate or something. I really hope this game picks up enough steam that someone makes a fixed translation mod, as far as I can tell there's not really THAT much plot. Which is fine, but hoooo boy.

Another big criticism is the UI, which is just awful. People asking for a console port don't realize there isn't one because the UI wouldn't allow for it. You control the UI with your mouse. If you're using a controller, you're moving the mouse cursor with the control stick. It's pretty bad. Just use a mouse in the menus, trust me.

All in all, I'd say it's probably worth $30, especially if people wind up modding in the collab content and a translation done by an actual human. If either of those are deal breakers for you, wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo what if you took the 'gatcha' system out of a gatcha game, and left just 'the game' it was built around as a result. Would it still be fun? Would it still have merit to even play it anymore? X DiVE I feel like is a good answer towards this question coming out with a rather decent 'yes' all around, even if it comes with some caveats as a result.

Outright, if you played X DiVE while it's Online half was still going, this is more or less the same game but a lot of the fat trimmed and experience tuned more for ~20 hour clear time of it's (sudo) extended story mode, it's large swath of Event quests, and huge roster of characters to unlock and play as you see fit with only a few real limits imposed onto the player. The biggest factor is the resource grind, that in Online had a huge assortment of bits and bobs for crafting, enhancing armor, and unlocking skills with DNA's. Here nearly all of it's been simplified towards either a single real resource, along with a Zenny cost making the entire process far more cut and dry. On top of that, the 'Power Requirements', an overall stat number declaring how strong you are for content, for stages has been drastically lowered in turn, removing a fair amount of the grind Online had. Add on some extra small refinements like characters getting a few new extra voice lines, some small detail clean up on a few troubled Live2D animations and 3D models, and altering a fair amount of skills that where made for PVP in mind to better enhance a large amount of the cast for easier PVE clears really does make this entire thing not feel like they just ported the content from online and called it a day.

In turn though, it's not perfect either. The biggest gripe is how unlock progress is weighted and timed, with a few notably OP characters (S Rank X and Zero) being given at the dead start of your experience nearly trivializing most content already, yet then a lot of only sub-decent to outright clunky to use characters doing unlock till well farther, towards nearly the dead end of the story mode experience. As a result the start feels far too easy, and depending on your favorites you might not even get to enjoy them much till 'post-ending' content clean up. Also beyond gallery improvement, there's little real benefit towards unlocking everyone beyond achievement gaining/self satisfying as the one area that would require multiple character use, Jakob's Orbital Elevator, has been altered to let you clear everything with just a single character without limit.

Overall though, as a -game-, X DiVE is overall still 'fun', given it's short burst platforming stages with clear callbacks towards the X series of games design layouts (along with hiding hidden elements in reference towards armor parts placement), and the general 'run and gun' nature and flow of things is still rather snappy with a controller (and passable via touchscreen for mobile players). At worse I think for some they might not feel challenged by the content on offer (Mostly in part due towards said OP units given at the dead start), and for others the more 'bite sized' stage designs will make you wish instead of a stage broken up into 6 chunks, it was more of a single run from start to finish without a break. Yet beyond those issues, I think what is here really shows the Taiwan Capcom team was really trying to make a 'game', but had to also aim for profits doing the time as well, and while I can't say the base $30 bucks is what I'd call a 'desirable' price for the game on offer, I can't help but admit that was is here isn't at least an enjoyable experience including if you're a Megaman fan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf this was a review of a business model, I'd give it a thumbs up. Unfortunately, this is a video game review.

For a mobile gatcha game, this is well above the standard of literally every game out there.
Full control of events, micro-transactions and energy systems removed, full control of your save file... It's a dream; one-time payment for tens of thousands of dollars of contrived value is a steal...

But when you step back and think of it as a normal video game......................................
Well, that's where everything falls apart.

The controls are clunky, even when you're playing on a phone, and the levels are very short with lots of talking in-between.
This wouldn't be bad if the story wasn't all filler, and if the Japanese voice-lines matches with the English. The levels start off easy, then become very difficult, and after activating events, becomes ridiculously easy. Almost like money and grind (previously) dictated the game's difficulty.

Unless you're a fanboy of the various Megaman franchises, or a die-hard free-to-play gatcha game addict looking for a way to curb that addiction, I don't recommend it.

Also, they really should add the gatcha back, but you'd have to farm for tickets (ie. getting one per level competion or something).\t\t\t\t\t
