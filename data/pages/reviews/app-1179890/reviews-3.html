\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a very wacky and surreal action beat-em-up, where you play as a robot buddy who needs to protect his NEET friend from being assaulted from strangers from the outside of his house. As one of those experiences where the player is either going to like it or really dislike it... I do like it, as it requires more skill than Goat Simulator and contains an interesting story with a strong message of personal health.

There are plenty of bugs (the robo protagonist permanently shrinking down for the RC car toy causing wonky grapple interactions until you restart the stage, the dragon costume boss leaping into the air and getting stuck and causing a game softlock, the intro tutorial bugging / freaking out and causing a softlock if you run around and rush into building traps early, etc.)

However, the experience is very compelling when you can cook up a bunch of homing fish rockets, set up a row of deviously innocuous body pillows to consume your friend's foes, set up a bathtub rubber ducky that turns into a giant bulldozing rubber ducky, and set up a grease fryer that turns your enemies into waddling tempuras that can be grabbed and thrown like missiles... there's a lot of fun stuff to try out.

Though, not all traps are created equal. For the game room, the cheap toy boxing glove and RC car don't anything on their own, the game console is very expensive (20 wood, 20 rocks and 20 CRYSTALS is very daunting and inefficient for an RNG active effect) and while the monster Pac Man is fun, it is time consuming to micromanage, so I didn't find those three traps to be very effective. There's also the electric fan for the living room, but it's mostly a downgrade for the fish cooker (which you can prepare ahead of time before a wave, and there is no limit to how many fish missiles you can have on the screen at once, so...)

I've listened to the developer's videos on what he wanted to say with his indie solo game, and I can respect that message. It also helps that despite wonky bugs and occasional soft locks, the game is very fun to play.

For feedback, I think it would be great if there was a stage select screen, so you don't have to start the whole game over if you decide to take a break, exit out of the game and come back later.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf like me, you were a sucker for the oddball Japanese games for the PS1/PS2 that would miraculously see release stateside, then you already know what you're getting into and will not be disappointed. Discovering this game feels like it's 2004 and I just found out in a gaming magazine that not only is Katamari Damacy coming stateside, but it's a budget title.

For everyone else, it's exactly as described on the tin: one-man developer makes a semi-autobiographical game about the life of a shut-in via comedic beat 'em up with light tower defense elements. But the elephant in the room with regard to all the praise it's already getting is... look at it. Its qualities probably aren't immediately apparent. What's more, it's buggy if not outright broken at times, its systems are unclear, the balance is questionable, I wasn't able to save my progress*, and the list goes on. But when I'm playing, I don't care about any of that. It has what's shaping up to be a heartfelt story; it has great humor that keeps getting better; it has loads of little touches including plenty of surprises (try frying a tempura man a 2nd/3rd time). That list goes on, too. Between the design-by-committee AAA titles and the conveyor belt of AI generated schlock that seems to be speeding up like it's the chocolate factory from I Love Lucy, I'm just happy to be playing a game so evidently made by a human.

Apparent quality is not quality. Play this game. And then play Katamari. Bakage for life.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been following the developer of this game for quite a while and I am happy to see that his game is released. It's always really neat when someone makes a game solo because generally those types of games are really special. Pull Stay looked like a really funny and weird Japanese beat em up and it didn't disappoint in that regard. The gameplay itself is an interesting mix between a beat em up and a tower defense game, you defend a shut in (hikikomori) while building rooms and placing traps in them on the opposite sides of the stairs leading up to his room.

One of my complaints with the game is that I think its a bit buggy and janky sometimes, I restarted the game a couple times because I thought the game glitched out on me. Once when fighting the final boss when I knocked him off of a vehicle he vanished and then reappeared a couple minutes later, I thought I bugged him out lol. Whenever I got Robo to do the pile drive move I thought he would jump way too high in the sky and it wasted a lot of time, got kind of annoyed whenever I got him to do it by accident.

My major complaint with the game I guess is that it doesn't really seem to have much replayability, a lot of beat em up games I played have branching paths which encourage you to play through them multiple times. I would have also liked a multiplayer function where I could play coop with a friend, but I guess that would not have worked very well with the games tower defence mechanics and would make the game too easy? Beat em ups are a lot better when you are playing with someone else. Also with the crafting materials I think they might be a bit unbalanced? I got like a bunch of wood planks through my playthrough and barely any crystals. Also when I start a new game I don't think the items reset to zero?

However the game is very impressive with the amount of work that the developer put into it for a 3d game, it looks so good! A lot of detail in the characters and the environment, the game looks so amazing for something made by one person. When I placed a fan trap in one of the rooms I wasn't expecting Robo to shield himself against the wind, but its little animations like that puts some life into a game.

The games humor is its biggest selling point, the beat em up itself is pretty decent. Robo has a good decent amount of moves that he can use on the enemies and using them and the traps in the rooms are satisfying and funny.

I wasn't expecting the game to be very long... most beat em ups aren't very long, Pull Stay is about 2 hours long. The most I could get more out of this game is to play through it a few times trying to die less and try to get a 1cc run I guess. I died a few times and used 1 continue in my first playthrough. Beyond that there isn't much of a reason for me to really return to this game besides the fact that the developer said (I think, it's a bit hard to understand him with his accent lol) that he plans on adding more bosses to the game when I asked him in a live stream. The game is still in early access, so I am looking forward to what else he is going to do with the game. And despite it being in early access the game does have its final boss and ending.

Do I recommend this game? Well yea lol, if you want a funny Japanese otaku game with decent gameplay. Although I might be biased because I like the developer lol. The game is expensive tho and there are much better beat em ups on Steam like Fight'n Rage that you can get really cheap on sale, but Pull Stay is genuinely a decent game imo.\t\t\t\t\t
