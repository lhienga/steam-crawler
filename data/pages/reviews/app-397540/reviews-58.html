\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe key to enjoying this game is A) not taking the base-game story too seriously and B) recognizing the base-game story is not that great. Not the worst thing ever, but also very much a downgrade from BL2's writing. I like that BL3 is now a space-faring, galaxy treading adventure due to the amount of different environments you can AND WILL come across in this game. Lots of planets, lots of main and side quests, and plenty of awesome characters to meet for the first time or see again.

The gunplay and loot system is the best it's ever been. LOTS of weapons and alternate modes for them, and a lot of freedom to create many different loadouts with each character available. My one and only pick in this game will always be FL4K for the amount of cool stuff you can do with him. I mainly switch between his Spiderant and the Ion Loader robot, but there are way more possibilities and combinations that I have yet to discover. The game is made a little more forgiving in that it's easier to get through in the single player mode, but tough enough that you'll still experience challenge when fighting the game's wide variety of enemies and bosses.

The DLC for this game was what made it a much better experience for me. It kinda sucks that the DLC expansions hit a higher mark than the base game, but they retroactively improve the game as a whole, so I'm fine with it. I got the Ultimate Edition of BL3 for $28 bucks, and that's a generous price for the amount of content included.\t\t\t\t\t
