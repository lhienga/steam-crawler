\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBorderlands 3 is a game I’ve been meaning to play for a while now but had to wait a bit to enjoy it on Steam as I refused to buy it in the Epic Games store. I figured to wait until a deluxe edition came out with all the DLC, bugs fixed, etc. before jumping in. I’m glad I waited since it was well worth it for the most part as I loved the original and its sequel and this one wasn’t so bad. It’s nowhere near as good as the original two but it’s a decent experience.

All the usual Borderlands style of things is here from the great graphics, amazing weapons, and the beyond satisfying gunplay. It’s a lot of fun to get all sorts of unique and different types of weapons and plow through enemies. Even the boss encounters were awesome as it’s a tried-and-true formula that has defined this franchise for over 15 years now.

It was great to see the return of many beloved characters in the series from Lilith, Ellie, Tiny Tina, Moxxi, and many others. Besides that, the graphics, soundtrack, and overall loot are all fantastic. I had some incredible weapons while playing which made it even more fun to go through the entire game. Now, the biggest issue I have with this game is its main story in the campaign and all the boring side quests.

The entire story from the campaign is the WORST I’ve ever seen in a Borderlands video game. It’s so terrible, cringe, immature, and flat-out dumb that it took me away from the overall gaming experience. I knew going into this game I wasn’t expecting an amazing or super serious story but this just sucked. The main villains are trash and the entire story is a weird, hot mess.

It wasn’t as funny or as good as Borderlands 2 and yeah, I get that this wouldn’t have Handsome Jack who is a legend in his own right but common now, the main villains could have been a lot better. It feels as if it was written by a bunch of teenagers who looked up #Borderlands on Twitter and used the most immature tweets they could find to write a story around them. The main story gets boring from the halfway point on as I just wanted to finish it already, collect my Steam Achievements, and be done with this hoping the DLC is better.

There’s just way too many fart jokes, characters in underwear, pictures of butts, and a high degree of immaturity and not of the funny kind. Besides that, this game also has a good amount of bugs and issues while playing. Loot will disappear into the ground and it froze a few times. It also crashed numerous times while playing out of nowhere and luckily, everything was saved so I didn’t lose my gear, character, or progress. If that happened, I would have given this a negative review, uninstalled it, and never played it again but thankfully, that wasn’t the case.

I will say though, after completing that abysmal main campaign, the four unique DLCs were a lot of fun to play. I enjoyed “Moxxi's Heist of the Handsome Jackpot” the most since we got to see the return of Handsome Jack. “Guns, Love and Tentacles: The Marriage of Wainwright & Hammerlock” was awesome since it was Lovecraftian-themed and I love horror so that resonated well with me. Both “Bounty of Blood” and “Psycho Krieg and the Fantastic Fustercluck” were fantastic and again, the graphics, new DLC weapons, and bosses were all a ton of fun. The storylines here were a lot better.

I didn’t waste any time trying out the mini-game of “Borderlands Science” as it didn’t interest me at all and the same with 2K Games trying to tap into the battle royale scene with their “Arms Race” mode. I tried it and it’s okay but when it comes to the co-op and multiplayer aspect of Borderlands 3, you’ll find some people playing but not a ton so expect queue times.

Crossplay and cross-platform are possible in Borderlands 3 so if you use its matchmaking you should be fine during optimal days and times depending on where you live. I was able to get into matches pretty quickly for the campaign but nobody was playing the other modes so keep that in mind.

I give Borderlands 3 a 3/5 for being a decent experience and still being one of the best looter shooters ever made. I am hoping 2K Games learns from their mistakes here since if this had a great story as the original and sequel did, this would be a perfect game. Unfortunately, the storyline is terrible and the bugs, glitches, and crashes were a shame. Aside from that, the DLC balances things out, the graphics are great, and the multiplayer works well during primetime. Here’s to hoping Borderlands 4 brings this incredible franchise back to its roots since this one was very close to being a dud and a negative review.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis very quickly became my all-time favorite Shooter, and kept me hook for hundreds of hours. Usually, when something takes more than 60 hours of my life, I get resentful, but I just couldn't get enough of this one. It builds on the foundation of its predecessors, adding in a new elemental, the ability to steal cars and download their parts to make your own custom ride, interplanetary travel, and SO much more. On top of that, you now have four skill trees, and each one lets you unlock new action skills as you go, rather than locking you into a single skill for the entire game, not to mention that you can customize that, too. You also get all kinds of cosmetic options, including gun skins that you can apply to any weapon, and it isn't monetized; you get this stuff by... you know... actually PLAYING THE GAME.

There aren't quite as many expansions as in 2, but what's there is absolutely solid. Moxxi's Heist of the Handsome Jackpot is like the Dead Money expansion for Fallout: New Vegas, if it were fun. Guns, Love, and Tentacles has a very unique setting, and it's a surprise Halloween episode to boot! Bounty of Blood is a cool cowboy expansion with a new vehicle type, and with butchered Japanese instead of Spanish. Psycho Krieg and the Fantastic Fustercluck is a cool journey into the mind, and I give it bonus points for digging my heart out with a claw hammer; I wasn't ready for it.

This all does come with the caveat that it can be pretty buggy; I ran into voice clips playing after the attached event had been long over, expansions capping their first areas' level at 13, and some other little stuff. It's nowhere near as bad as something like a Bethesda game, but I don't remember the other Borderlands games being like that. Any other criticisms I have would be fairly minor, like the occasional weird emphasis on puzzle platforming, and I absolutely loved the experience overall.\t\t\t\t\t
