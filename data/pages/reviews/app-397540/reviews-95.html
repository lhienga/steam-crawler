\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI’m not a video game critic. I don’t write in verbose prose waxing poetically about the accomplishments or shortcomings of the developers. Steam suggested I write a review of a game that is already 4+ years old; so here goes.

The Borderlands franchise is successful for several reasons. Gearbox has created a well written main story line with plenty of events to make for a full and engaging main story arc. There are plenty of side missions and tertiary goals that add to the game time. Also, the DLCs help keep the game fresh and interesting. The artwork and theme of the game are quite unique. In a world where many FPSs are virtually indistinguishable, Borderlands definitely stands out. Additionally, the voice acting is mostly well produced with some very familiar voices such as Ice-T, Penn Jillett and Jamie Marchi. The only complaint I have about the game is the amount of weapons/gear you can find. I sometimes spend as much as 15% - 20% of my game time going through my loot in an effort to make sure I have the most powerful weapon(s) and defenses available. Sometimes the chore feels a little tedious.

In conclusion, I completed the original Borderlands many years ago and had a good time doing it. Currently I am about halfway through the main story line of Borderlands 3 and the game is still fun and engaging. If you enjoy the FPS genre then Borderlands 3 is a definite must-have.
