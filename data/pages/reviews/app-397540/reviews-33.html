\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOver the years, one of the things that's kept me interested in Borderlands is the interesting characters and the world they live in. For the most part this game fumbles both. While the cool artstyle, weapon designs, builds, and gameplay are all good and what I'd expect from a sequel, the character and story writing physically hurt at times and makes me question the future of the series, which so far in 2024 seems to have panned out as poorly as I'd expected.

The humour is bad, but in exactly the way I'd expect and want from a borderlands game. The problem is, In previous games the over-the-top "who cares" attitude of the humour mixed with the devastating wasteland worldbuilding made for a unique feel to the universe and very fun game. When the game has characters and story this bad, the worldbuilding breaks apart and previously tolerable humour becomes really painful. This ends up making me wish the dialogue didn't exist at all.

Hilariously enough that's exactly what I did. The majority of my playtime is with mods that removes all dialogue from the game and replaces the annoying videos that play on every map. Past your first playthrough, this is a genuine and major improvement I can recommend to anyone who plays it. I cannot imagine I would get invested in the series if this was my first experience.\t\t\t\t\t
