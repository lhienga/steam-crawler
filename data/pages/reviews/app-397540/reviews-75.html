\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI sat here thinking... people complaining about the BL3 story are clearly not very smart. It's a Borderlands game, the story was always ♥♥♥♥. ..... And then I played it, and I felt my soul leave my ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ body. The dialogue and story are insufferable. They explain every dumbass joke they make, so even if it had a sliver of amusement to it, they ruin what little they might have had by treating you like a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ moron.

The skill trees are my second biggest complaint. It feels like it could have had potential, but depending on what character you picked, is also strangely restrictive. For example, if you pick Hunter like I did... the skill trees special perks don't affect the skills outside of that specific tree... so If I wanted to have my perks split between two trees, I'd be shooting myself in the foot. That's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ garbage. Apparently not all the characters suffer from that issue, but it still sucks.
-The DLC trees also feel strangely underpowered. I know you could say that's a good thing... but I think it's completely pointless. If you want to play I wouldn't bother with the DLC.

In other news, the AI is somehow even dumber! How the ♥♥♥♥ is that even possible? The pets cannot path to anything, and often times NPCs and pets will just stand in one spot and shoot a wall that's blocking their shots for eternity until you move. Spells will outright fail to go off, and go on cooldown anyways, seemingly due to pathing errors. The new wall grab system doesn't feel good, because it forces parkour in the awkward ass BL physics nightmare, instead of adding optional stuff to reward players for tolerating/abusing the jank.

As far as pros go, I rather liked the changes to the guns. They have some guns with alternate modes, such as a rifle that can swap to shoot rockets. I think that's really satisfying, and can help make you feel better equipped for fight, more versatile is always a bonus in my book.

The cars are also a bit more interesting, customization, and satisfying enough to drive. (At least where you're allowed to drive.)
You can also pick a different car from your multiplayer buddy, which is a really important change. They should have already had this... but I guess they can get props for it.
Strangely, it also feels more like an open world game in multiplayer, despite still being region locked via teleporters/zoning. Whether that's good or bad is up to the individual I suppose. I also could be alone in thinking that.

There isn't really enough to justify the rest of the game, however. I don't think I've ever played a game that made me feel as dead inside as this one did. It drained the life and soul out of me, and I'm not even sure I can describe why. My friend and I often play miserable and bad games together, and can find enjoyment from just about everything we've tried... but both of us became cranky and disinterested in this game. Neither of us was having even a few moments of fun, and we gave it more of a shot than it deserved.

This very well might be the most miserable experience I've ever had playing a video game. And I've played some boring ass ♥♥♥♥. I ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hated this game.\t\t\t\t\t
