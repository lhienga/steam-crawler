\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought BL3 when it first released on Epic Store, so you can add another 200 or so hours to the playtime you see here on Steam; the Pandora Collection sale on Steam was too good to pass up.

Let's get the elephant in the room out of the way: the story - while enjoyable - doesn't hold a candle to BL2's story and humor. While I had a few chuckles along the way in BL3, it's missing that darkness in its humor that we all loved from BL2. I know Handsome Jack was the real star of BL2 (also Scooter, arguably), and while he's pseudo-present in The Handsome Jackpot, you can tell there was a seismic shift in the comedy as they were making this game. It's omnipresent, and while it has its moments, it largely falls flat. However, this is largely just a sign of the last ten years and growing pains within the industry and comedic writing trying to capitalize on social issues and stigma, and while BL3's writing and humor isn't as bad as The Pre-Sequel's pandering, it definitely approaches it. Though it doesn't ruin the game, it definitely detracts from it.

BL3 tried to walk the tightrope of edgy humor with a helping of virtue signaling so as not to get into trouble sales-wise, but I think it's clear by now that backfired. Still, while it approaches gaudy virtue-signaling, it never fully embraces it; it seems as if there were several writers writing against each other politically and socially, and if you pay attention you can see that unfold in some missions, the clearest being the Trashlantis quests in THJ. It's actually done quite well. However, the beauty of BL2 was that it was able to successfully poke fun at hot-button topics with really funny, biting humor that no one was safe from. BL2 seemed to embrace the mantra of, "♥♥♥♥♥♥-up humor is funny, and if you don't like it, Animal Crossing exists." The writers of BL3 gambled on walking that tightrope and thought they could err on the side of caution when it comes to humor, but you have to commit with writing if you want to be taken seriously. You have to be willing to take risks; they weren't willing to take those risks this time and it showed. Sarcasm on its own isn't funny; there has to be a joke there. Making fun of things that are objectively bad isn't funny, it's just stating the obvious. This has been a trend in the last 8 years or so, and it's thankfully coming to a close. That said, it's not that bad in BL3, but it's there.

Where BL3 falls flat writing-wise, it excels beyond all imagination in its gameplay. In every facet but one, it is head and shoulders above BL2 in its mechanics and gunplay. It's just FUN. So what's that one facet where it doesn't hold up? The Anointed mini-bosses in the higher Mayhem levels in the endgame, which is a sort of NG+. Even after the nerf, they still only serve as a bullet sponge, momentum-slowing mechanic that should be optional, or even an RNG Mayhem mechanic like all the others built into the levels themselves you can re-roll. Running around in circles for two minutes trying to kill one random spawn with no purpose other than slowing down your momentum is a terrible mechanic. Still, it's only in the endgame, and only in the higher Mayhem levels at that.

Borderlands has always excelled in its DLC, and BL3 is no exception. From Hammerlock and Wainwright's wedding on a planet paying homage to Lovecraft (Guns, Love, and Tentacles), to the Old West-themed Bounty of Blood, to the surprisingly mature, fascinating, and poignant Psycho Krieg and the Fantastic Fustercluck, Borderlands 3 really shines and makes you all but forget about its missteps in the main campaign. Just like BL2, BL3 is not complete without these DLCs. They are a must-have.

Play through the campaign and take enjoyment where you can, but don't let its maligned story dissuade you from enjoying the rest of what is on offer here. From the Circle of Slaughter horde modes that are designed to be incredibly fun level and loot farms, to the DLC, to the customization and quality of life improvements over BL2, there is so much on offer here that it would be a shame to overlook it all because of the main story's shortcomings, those of which make up only a small part of the game.

I bought it on two different platforms. That should tell you something.\t\t\t\t\t
