\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I have nearly 100 hours in this now. I think i can praise and complain.
A 'gamergate part is coming after a general review.

This game is great and frustrating.
I have only played part 1 when it came out and have not yet touched 2 (though I have heard it is supposed to be the best one). I remember the characters in 1 being fun and interesting, the humour being witty and hilarious and the story, while not groundbreaking, to be engaging and exciting.

So as I now mostly play coop with my brother, we gave part 3 a try.
The game itself is really fun and engaging if you can overcome the terrible pain of leaving loot; there is just too much stuff. I love the dynamic combat and the many different guns and their effects. The weird descriptions are a nice bonus. Though shotguns that need to 'load' before firing a shot suck. No amount of damage will change my mind on that.
Many of the characters are fun to listen to and there were a few moments that really made me laugh.
The world building is interesting and the different locations have awesome designs and details. But i do not think if it would be that hard to put one or two different texts onto open books and letters so that not everything starts with a big headline saying "The Hunters". Seems like little work to be less immersion breaking and there is great potential for hidden jokes.

Now for the part that I do not like: The 'main' characters. And the story that hinges on them.
When Lilith arrived in the beginning, I was like "Awesome, I remember her being pretty badass in part 1!". Then she got her powers stolen while acting like a child that just discovered walking. That was disappointing.
The others weren't much better. Maya also just dies. Tannis is such a b'tch that i was seriously hoping she dies when she got kidnapped.
The villains where fine insofar as that you made them so annoying that i really wanted to punch their face in. But you also made them dumb and as flat as that tiiiiny layer of air that begins to flimmer in rainbow colours when you press two plates of glass together. The girl is daddy issues paired with narcissism and the boy is idiot with sister complex. Really outdid yourself there. Must have taken all but 10 minutes to come up with that.
And then that little girl? I can't even remember her name, just that I sighed in annoyance the moment she finished her first sentence in that graveyard. What a prick! Absolutely annoying from top to bottom! I have not played 'her' DLC, but a PODCAST? With HER? I DO NOT want to hear more of her!
And then the ending of the main story? WHAT THE HELL WAS THAT?

The awesome character that you nerfed into child status now just gets her powers back without doing f all and then is instantly powerful enough to STOP THE F-ING MOON FROM EXPLODING? WHERE THE HELL DID YOU LEARN CHARACTER DEVELOPMENT FROM? NEWS PAPER COMICS? How did she get punted into the ground in the beginning if she is that powerful? And who gets an undeserved powerup? That annoying brat. And she gets handed the spaceship and the 'keys to the kingdom' because what? "You deserve it"?? FOR WHAT? FOR BEING AN ANNOYING ASSWIPE? SHE DID NOTHING WORTHWHILE!

All in all the game is awesome if you just skip the cutscenes with the annoying characters.

Controversial 'Gamergate2' part:

But since I am a gamer and a (as I understand former) developer of this game has raged a bit about us as a whole, I want to add here that the aspect I am complaining about is (imo) the core of the whole problem of 'woke' gaming. The writing is bad in the aspects where it matters. The characters. 'Woke' as a denominator in and of itself has been useless for a while now. So let's use other words. The GOAL of the overarching 'movement' is not bad. It is mainly representation in a fair way. That is what we should want and i do not see most gamers being against that. Here, as happens so often, the loudest ones (who often have the least mainstream perspectives) become the face of the 'scene' to the outside. See: "♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ pronouns!"
But honestly, we make freaky characters and use different races in rpgs because we find that ♥♥♥♥ pretty cool. Most of us would probably even try a different race in the (with todays society hopeful) case of getting isekai'd. We have lapped up strong female characters long before gamergate itself, which was one of the main criticisms that anita sarkesian ignored. And there is a BIG irony in that. Because the people pushing these narratives want to stop their own perceived groups from being seen in a 'wrong' way (e.g. 'women are weak'). But in trying to change that, they merely push a false perception onto another group.
The problem here is again the loudest people being those that want the 'wrong' kind of change. In case of AAA games that means forcing diversity. I find it baffling that those like SBI do not just WRITE AWESOME STORIES WITH DIVERSE CHARACTERS and give those to the studios to use. There are people on the internet who write amazing stories with hundreds of chapters that follow awesome characters. If you can't, at least hire those people! Instead, they shoehorn stuff that doesn't fit into narratives that don't need it. It is a bit like those protests for climate activism that anger other 'normal' people by defacing art or gluin themselves to streets. I always ask myself if those are funded by the oil lobby to ruin their side of the debate, and i am more and more sure that I am right with that. But I don't think that fits in the gamergate2 case. Even though they shoot themselves in the foot all the same.

That the studios are fearful of the potential righteous mob is not strange to me. People who make up their mind without adequate information, e.g. over one POV from a twitter post, are scary (though we can all do that. NOT doing that makes one a decent person). The big studios need to please the all powerful investors and the small ones may even risk everything depending on the circumstances.
That gaming journalists basically disowned us should not matter. A lot of them have long traded honesty for money and career. With the state the 'media' as a whole is in, it's no wonder, though. But don't let them gaslight you.

tl;dr:
Games need GOOD CHARACTERS with GOOD STORIES. It does NOT matter what 'race' (of human), species, colour, shape, wavelength or other features you use for characters. JUST WRITE THEM WELL! (Good story + bad character = bad time)\t\t\t\t\t
